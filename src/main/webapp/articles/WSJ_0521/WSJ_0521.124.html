International Brief -- Montedison S.p.A.: Earnings for 1989 Surged But Operating Profit Fell

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/21/90

Montedison S.p.A. said its 1989 consolidated profit surged 73% to 1.156 trillion lire ($952.1 million), or 427 lire a share, from 668 billion lire, or 247 lire a share, a year earlier, because of a large capital gain from its contribution to the Enimont S.p.A. chemical joint venture with state-owned Ente Nazionale Idrocarburi. Excluding special items, profit fell 26% to 362 billion lire. Gross operating earnings fell 29% to 978 billion lire, and net operating profit plunged 52% to 420 billion lire. Sales fell 58% to 5.861 trillion lire from 14.122 trillion lire; adjusted revenue rose 7%, mostly because of the acquisition of SIR S.p.A., a resins maker.

Montedison, 45%-owned by Gruppo Ferruzzi of Italy, an agri-industrial and chemical concern, blamed the operating results on a "consistent narrowing of margins" from exceptionally good 1988 levels.
