ServiceMaster L.P. Considers Realigning Ownership Structure

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/21/90

DOWNERS GROVE, Ill. -- ServiceMaster L.P. plans to revamp its ownership structure sometime within the 18 months. C. William Pollard, chief executive, said he's considering three alternatives for reshaping the company's ownership: spinning off a segment, re-incorporating the company or selling convertible debentures. Mr. Pollard, who will also become the company's chairman on June 1, didn't indicate whether he presently favors any of the choices.

In a move that would facilitate a possible spinoff, the company earlier this month named two presidents to head its main operating groups, management services and consumer and commercial services.

Analysts indicated they'd like the company to take public the consumer and commercial services division, which is the fastest-growing part of the company. Mr. Pollard declined to say whether a spinoff would likely involve that unit.

ServiceMaster is considering the realignment because its partnership structure is unattractive to institutional investors. Less than 9% of its units are institutionally owned, Mr. Pollard said.
