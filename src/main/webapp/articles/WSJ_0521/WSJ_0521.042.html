Cerner to Buy Back First Chicago Capital's Stake in the Company

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/21/90

KANSAS CITY, Mo. -- Cerner Corp. said it agreed in principle to buy, for $11 each, 505,662 of its common shares held by First Chicago Capital Corp. and its affiliates. The maker of clinical information systems said it expects to complete the repurchase of the 13.8% stake in about two weeks.

The First Chicago Corp. unit will finance one-half of the buy-back over an 18-month period, Cerner said; the remainder will be financed through a combination of operating funds and Cerner's existing credit lines.

Cerner said it has had "a long and outstanding relationship with our venture capital partner," but "Considering our strengthened cash flows and the long-term potential for Cerner's products in the market place, we believe that the opportunity to repurchase our stock at $11 per share represents a sound investment for Cerner."
