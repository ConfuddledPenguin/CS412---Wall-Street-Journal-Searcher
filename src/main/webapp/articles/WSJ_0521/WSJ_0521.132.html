California Court Rejects Fetal-Protection Policy

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/21/90

SAN FRANCISCO -- (AP) Women can't be banned from jobs that could harm a fetus, California's highest court ruled, upholding a challenge to a company policy that affected even women who have no plans to have children. The state Supreme Court on Thursday let stand a lower court ruling that a battery company's fetal-protection policy, keeping fertile women away from jobs that could expose them to low levels of lead, amounts to sex discrimination.

The company, Milwaukee-based Johnson Controls Inc., also has a case pending before the U.S. Supreme Court over the same policy.

The California appellate decision had called the policy "blatant, overt gender-based discrimination" based on "unfounded, unscientific stereotypic notions about women."

"We are in an era of choice. A woman is not required to become a Victorian brood mare," said the 4th District Court of Appeal in its 3-0 ruling Feb. 28 in a case from Fullerton.

But in a case stemming from a Johnson Controls plant in Minnesota, a federal appeals court had upheld the policy. The U.S. Supreme Court is expected to rule on the issue by mid-1991.

However that turns out, Thursday's action, involving state fair-employment law, should settle the issue for California, a state lawyer said.
