Philadelphia Electric Appeals Rate Decision By Utility Commission

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/21/90

PHILADELPHIA -- Philadelphia Electric Co. appealed an April 19 decision by the Pennsylvania Public Utility Commission that granted the utility only $242.2 million of the $548.6 million rate increase it requested. Philadelphia Electric said that by denying the rate request, the commission unconstitutionally denied shareholders any return on their investment in Limerick No. 2 nuclear power station. In denying most of the rate increase, the commission disallowed the company's request to recover the costs of building and operating the plant.

In the appeal, filed with the Commonwealth Court of Pennsylvania, Philadelphia Electric alleged that the commission approved various aspects of the project in the mid-1980s, and then "suddenly reversed course" this year.
