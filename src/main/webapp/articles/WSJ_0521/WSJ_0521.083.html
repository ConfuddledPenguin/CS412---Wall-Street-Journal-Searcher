Kroger Agrees to Buy Meadowdale Assets

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/21/90

CINCINNATI -- Kroger Co. said it agreed in principle to acquire 30 food stores and four others under construction from the Meadowdale Great Scott] division of Meadowdale Foods Inc., Detroit. Terms weren't disclosed. The proposed acquisition would double the number of Kroger supermarkets in southeastern Michigan.

Elizabeth M. Shiels, an analyst at Hilliard Lyons Inc., Louisville, Ky., said the proposal marks the first Kroger would make since its recapitalization in 1988. "Management is very prudent so I would assume the price was right," she said. She declined to estimate how much Kroger would pay for the stores.

Kroger officials couldn't be reached for comment.

In addition, Foodland Distributors said it will acquire the operating assets of Meadowdale's Abner Wolf Distributors wholesale division, as well as the four remaining Great Scott] retail locations.

Foodland, Livonia, Mich., is a joint venture between Kroger and Wetterau Inc., St. Louis. Wetterau is a food processor, distributor and retailer.

Foodland will acquire Meadowdale's headquarters and warehouse building. Foodland is a wholesale supplier to more than 110 chain and independent retail stores in Michigan.

Kroger and Foodland said they will assume certain of Meadowdale's obligations and liabilities, including all trade payables and real estate and equipment leases. The exact amount hasn't been determined, said Greg Gallus, Foodland's president.

Completion of the transaction is subject to a definitive agreement and regulatory approval. The transaction is expected to be completed in the next two to three months, Kroger said.
