Harris Corp. Cites Improved Production With a New System

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/21/90

MELBOURNE, Fla. -- Harris Corp. says it has achieved sharply higher yields in its semiconductor plants by using a production control system developed with Digital Equipment Corp. and a Bolt Beranek & Newman Inc. unit. Manufacturing yield is a major problem in chip making, where typical yields range from 99%, to as low as 35%, for each wafer of silicon, depending on the complexity of the chip. Harris said it has produced yield improvements of between 17% to 50%.

Digital, Maynard, Mass., and BBN, of Cambridge, Mass., are in discussions with Harris to commercialize the jointly developed software. Randy Burdick, director of manufacturing systems at Harris, says that other chip makers have expressed interest in the system, which he said could yield "huge savings for the industry."
