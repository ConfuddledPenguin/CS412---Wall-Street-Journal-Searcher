Marketing Brief -- Cablevision Systems Corp.: Cable Operator Posts Loss Of $74 Million for Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/21/90

Cablevision Systems Corp. said it had a net loss of $74 million, or $3.38 a share, for the first quarter, citing additional interest, depreciation and amortization expenses related to 1989 acquisitions. A year earlier, the Woodbury, N.Y., cable-television company had a net loss of $59.1 million, or $2.73 a share.

Revenues rose 21% to $133.8 million from $110.7 million.

Cablevision said operating profit before depreciation and amortization rose 19% to $54 million. Net interest expense rose $17 million to $64 million, while depreciation and amortization expense rose $10 million to about $54 million.

At March 31, Cablevision had 1.3 million subscribers in 11 states, as well as interests in programming and advertising sales companies.
