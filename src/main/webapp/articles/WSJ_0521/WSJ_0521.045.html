Commodore Wins Subcontract to Supply Computers to U.S.

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/21/90

WEST CHESTER, Pa. -- Commodore International Ltd. said it achieved an early success in its attempt to enter the federal government market for personal computers. Commodore has won a five-year subcontract with the Treasury Department under which it expects to supply more than 3,000 of its Amiga personal computers. The subcontract was awarded as part of a $400 million contract won by Sears Business Systems, a unit of Sears, Roebuck & Co.

The subcontract comes six months after Commodore began trying to enter the market -- even though Lloyd Mahaffey, vice president of marketing, said he hadn't expected to make a sale for a year or more. Because of the long purchase cycles on government contracts, Mr. Mahaffey said, many companies take 18 months or even longer to make their first sale.

While it is obviously unclear whether additional contracts will follow close on the heels of the first one, any success would be welcomed by Commodore. The company's U.S. operations have long been lackluster, and a new management team is looking for improvement by focusing on the federal government market, schools and corporate training departments.
