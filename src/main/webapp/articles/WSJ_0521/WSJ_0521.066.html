Pic 'N' Save In Fight, Offers Itself for Sale

  • Author: Rhonda L. Rundle
  • Date: 05/21/90

Pic 'N' Save Corp., struggling to keep control of its board out of the hands of an investor group led by David H. Batchelder, put itself up for sale but hinted that it didn't expect any bidders would emerge. The investor group, Girard Partners Limited Partnership, holds a 6.6% stake in Pic 'N' Save and is soliciting consents from shareholders to install five of its nominees on the company's seven-member board. If the group succeeds, it has said it would buy the company or auction it off to the highest bidder.

Pic 'N' Save said it would provide nonpublic information beginning today to prospective buyers who sign a confidentiality agreement with several standstill provisions. Girard Partners would be forced to abandon for three years its efforts to seize control of the board under one of the provisions.

Pic 'N' Save's action appears to be a gambit to placate investors who are moving toward Girard's camp. Pic 'N' Save wants "to remove any doubts in the minds of our shareholders," that value will be "maximized" through the company's recently-modified operating plan, Lewis B. Merrifield III, chairman and chief executive officer, said.

Pic 'N' Save, a Dominguez, Calif., retailer of close-out merchandise, has a market value of about $493 million, based on its current stock price. In national over-the-counter trading Friday, the shares climbed 87.5 cents to $13.875. The announcement, which came during the session, fueled trading as more than 1.6 million shares changed hands.

Because it's difficult to borrow money under current credit-market conditions, "the board has serious reservations. . . . as to whether a proposal reflecting the inherent value of the company will be forthcoming," Mr. Merrifield said.

In an interview, he said that prospects for a bid were somewhat better from a "strategic buyer," such as another retailer, who might not need to borrow funds to make an acquisition. In the past year, however, the company hasn't received a single expression of interest from anyone, he added.

Pic 'N' Save said its financial advisers would begin "actively" contacting a "lengthy" list of potential bidders today. A deadline for offers was set for June 20, the day of the annual shareholders' meeting.

Some analysts doubted that the company could attract any offers above its current market value. "The takeover premium is already in the stock," said Sarah Stack, an analyst at Bateman Eichler, Hill Richards Inc. in Los Angeles. On the strength of Pic 'N' Save's business prospects, the shares should be selling at under $10 a share, she thinks.

Ms. Stack also has said that Pic 'N' Save investors are unhappy with the company's financial performance and may be receptive to Girard's proposals. Last week, a former Pic 'N' Save executive who owns about 1.4 million shares, or 4% of the stock, agreed to support Girard.

In a letter mailed to shareholders late last week, Mr. Merrifield disparaged qualifications of some of the Girard nominees. Only one of more than a dozen takeover attempts were completed by T. Boone Pickens while Mr. Batchelder was part of his team, the letter stated. Of five public retail companies run by Robert J. McNulty, only two reported an operating profit for any period while Mr. McNulty was in control, according to the letter.

Mr. Batchelder didn't return telephone calls made to his office in La Jolla, Calif. Mr. McNulty couldn't be reached over the weekend.
