New Stock Listings

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/21/90

NEW YORK -- The New York Stock Exchange added two stocks and the National Market System of the over-the-counter market added four. The Big Board added Nuveen California Municipal Market Opportunity Fund Inc. and Nuveen New York Municipal Market Opportunity Fund Inc. The symbols are NCO and NNO, respectively. Based in Chicago, the closed-end management investment companies invest primarily in municipal obligations exempt from federal income taxes and, respectively, California and New York state income taxes. On the OTC market, Columbia Hospital Corp., a Fort Worth hospital operator, began trading under CHOS.

Gwinnett Bancshares Inc., a Lawrenceville, Ga., bank holding company, began trading under GBSI.

Integrated Waste Services Inc., a Buffalo manager of non-hazardous solid wastes, began trading under IWSI.

Loewen Group Inc., a Burnaby, British Columbia, operator of funeral homes, started trading under LWNGF. The American Stock Exchange and the Chicago Board Options Exchange have begun trading options on the common stock of Cellular Communications Inc., a New York owner and operator of phone systems, under CMQ. The Amex also began trading options on the shares of CoreStates Financial Corp., the Philadelphia bank holding company, under CFQ.
