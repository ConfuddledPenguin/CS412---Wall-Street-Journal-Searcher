Credit Ratings: Wisconsin Power Debt, Preferred Stock Face Moody's Downgrade

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/21/90

NEW YORK -- Moody's Investors Service Inc., citing a recent weakening in Wisconsin Power & Light Co.'s credit ratios, said it placed the utility's long-term debt and preferred stock of about $400 million under review for potential downgrade. The rating concern said the utility's credit measures "have come under pressure as a result of lower allowed returns by regulators, federal tax law changes, and increasing construction requirements."

Moody's said its review will focus on "the extent to which WP&L's current and prospective measures of debt protection are sufficient to support {Moody's} highest rating, especially in view of growing business and operating risks in the electric utility industry."

Under review are the triple-A rating on the company's first mortgage and secured pollution control revenue bonds, and the double-A-1 rating on its preferred stock. Wisconsin Power's Prime-1 rating on its commercial paper isn't under review.

A Wisconsin Power spokesman said Moody's announcement of a review "wasn't unexpected," and added that the company, " would expect, based on what Moody's release said today, that we will see a down rating occur." He noted that much of Moody's comments were concerned "with the state of the industry," as opposed to the company in particular.
