Papua New Guinea Gets Aid

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/21/90

SINGAPORE -- A group of donors including the World Bank have agreed to provide Papua New Guinea with $710 million in concessional loans and grants, Finance Minister Paul Pora said. Concessional loans from the World Bank, Asian Development Bank, the European Community, Australia, Japan and New Zealand would amount to $390 million, while grants for 1990 would amount to $320 million.

The funds would be used for power, roads, education and to help the economy rebound from declining commodities prices and the closure of mines in Bougainville island, Mr. Pora said.
