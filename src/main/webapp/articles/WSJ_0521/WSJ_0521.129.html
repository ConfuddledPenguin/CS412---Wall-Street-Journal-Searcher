Business Brief -- Apple Bancorp: Stahl's Request for Meeting Date Is Denied by Court

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/21/90

A Delaware court denied a request by investor Stanley Stahl seeking to compel New York-based Apple Bancorp to hold its 1990 meeting for shareholders on or before June 16. Mr. Stahl, who controls 31% of Apple's shares, is waging a proxy fight to gain control of Apple's board. A New York real-estate investor, Mr. Stahl has offered $38 a share, or $121.6 million, for all of the bank's shares that he doesn't own.

Apple's board has rejected that offer as inadequate, and investors apparently think either Mr. Stahl will sweeten his offer, or another bidder will emerge. On Friday, Apple shares closed at $44.625 a share, unchanged, in composite New York Stock Exchange trading.

Apple hasn't set a date for its annual meeting in order to allow time to explore alternatives to Mr. Stahl's offer.
