Business Brief -- Fletcher Challenge Ltd.: Canada Unit Plans to Make More Cuts in Pulp Output

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/21/90

Fletcher Challenge Canada Ltd., Vancouver, British Columbia, said it plans further reductions of kraft pulp production at its British Columbia mills, in the face of weakening market demand and growing inventories. A spokesman for the company, a unit of New Zealand-based Fletcher Challenge Ltd., said the curtailments would reduce kraft pulp output a total of about 36,000 metric tons. A metric ton is equal to about 2,204.62 pounds.

Fletcher Challenge Canada said the curtailments include the idling for a week, beginning yesterday, of one of two pulp lines at its Crofton mill on Vancouver Island, and the shutdown of the same line for four weeks in July. The company's Elk Falls mill, also on Vancouver Island, already curtailed output by about 3,000 metric tons a month. The Mackenzie mill, in northern British Columbia, has been operating "considerably below capacity" and is reviewing a plan for idle time in the third quarter, the company said. All three mills closed for varying periods earlier in the year.
