Who's News: Chemex Says Chief Steps Down, Unveils Cost-Cutting Moves

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/21/90

DENVER -- Chemex Pharmaceuticals Inc. said that its president and chief operating officer has resigned and that the company has decided to terminate one of two research agreements it has with Yale University and move into smaller offices in a bid to cut costs. The pharmaceutical concern said that Edward S. Neiss resigned from his executive positions and as a director effective May 1 to pursue other interests. A company spokesman said the decision was Mr. Neiss's choice. The company said that Mr. Neiss's contract with the company was to expire in July.

Chemex also said late Friday that it has notified Yale that it will terminate a general research contract on Sept. 30, cutting its Yale research costs in half. The company declined to disclose the amount of those research costs.

Chemex also said it agreed in principle with its landlord to move into a 5,000 square foot office from a 32,000 square foot office space in the same building. It said it expects to move in a few weeks.

The company, which had a loss of $6.6 million last year, said that it has an agreement for short-term debt financing secured by certain company assets, but it declined to disclose the amount or provide details of the agreement.
