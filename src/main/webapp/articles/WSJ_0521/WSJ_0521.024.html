Who's News: Elders IXL's Elliott Steps Down as Chief, Remains Chairman

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/21/90

MELBOURNE -- Elders IXL Ltd. said John Elliott resigned as chief executive but will remain chairman of the diversified Australian conglomerate. Peter Bartels was named the new chief executive. Mr. Bartels heads the brewing division of the company, which is the world's fourth-largest brewer.

Elders announced in March it plans to sell its natural resources and financial-services divisions and spin off its agribusiness operation to become a single-purpose brewer that will be renamed Foster's Brewing Group Ltd.

Mr. Elliott had said he planned to step down as chief executive at some time as part of the corporate restructuring.

The decision is seen by analysts as helping distance Elders from Harlin Holdings Pty. Ltd., a closely held concern that owns 55% of Elders's shares. Mr. Elliott is a major shareholder of Harlin.

Harlin took on a heavy debt load last year when it acquired its controlling stake in Elders. Stock-market concerns about Harlin's financial health have depressed Elders shares in recent weeks.

A statement from Elders Friday said Alan McGregor, a director of Elders for 10 years, becomes deputy chairman. Mr. McGregor has broken his connections with Harlin, the statement said.

It said that Ken Jarrett and Geoffrey Lord will retire from the Elders board later this year. When the changes are completed early in the fiscal year starting July, the Elders board will comprise four representatives from Harlin, four independent directors, and the chief executive.
