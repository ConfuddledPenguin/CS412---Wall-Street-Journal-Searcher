Letters to the Editor: Threat of Warming Just Makes Me Shiver

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/21/90

In his comments on the "global-warming" hoax, Paul A. Gigot (Potomac Watch, April 20) states that ". . . everyone agrees the earth has gotten slightly warmer in the last 100 years." It may be of interest to cite what Mark Twain had to say in "Life on the Mississippi," published in 1883: "Natchez (Miss.) is the furthest point to the north at which oranges ripen . . . or endure the winter without shelter."

Several pages further along:

". . . for many miles above New Orleans . . . the graceful and luxuriant palmetto . . . and the bright orange, were everywhere to be seen. . . ."

Our winters on the Gulf Coast are now so cold that unprotected orange groves survive only in southernmost Florida. Attempts to grow oranges south of New Orleans are regularly frozen out every few years. Those few Louisiana palmetto trees that had survived previous harsh winters were frozen by the severe cold of December 1989.

Perhaps Mark Twain was secretly employed by the anti-environmentalists?

Willard P. Leutze

Lafayette, La.
