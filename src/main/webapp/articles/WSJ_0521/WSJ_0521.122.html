International Brief -- Axa-Midi Assurances: Insurer Sells Its 92% Stake In Paris Warehouse Firm

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/21/90

Axa-Midi Assurances said it sold its 92% holding in a Paris-based warehouse company to La Societe Magnant S.A. for the equivalent of $731 a share, or $575 million. The warehouse company, Cie. des Entrepots & Magasins Generaux, is traded on the Paris Bourse.

The sale price is almost twice the $296.5 million value estimated by an independent appraiser in Axa-Midi's application earlier this year to purchase Farmers Insurance Group, Axa-Midi said. The sale of Farmers Insurance to Axa-Midi was proposed by Sir James Goldsmith as part of his $21 billion bid for B.A.T Industries PLC. California insurance authorities rejected the sale of Farmers, saying the leverage required would have endangered Farmers.

Axa Midi, Europe's seventh largest insurance company, said it continues to look for a U.S. insurance company to buy.
