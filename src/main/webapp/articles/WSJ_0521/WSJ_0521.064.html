New Securities Issues

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/21/90

The following were among Friday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATES

European Investment Bank -- $300 million issue of 9 1/8% notes, due June 1, 2002, won in competitive bidding by a group led by First Boston Corp., priced at 99.32 to yield 9.22%. The noncallable notes were priced at a spread of 50 basis points above the Treasury's 10-year note. The issue is rated Triple-A by both Moody's Investors Service Inc. and by Standard & Poor's Corp.

Federal Farm Credit Banks -- $150 million of notes, due June 5, 1992, priced at par to float weekly at a spread of 50 basis points above the Treasury's three-month bill. The noncallable notes will be reoffered at various prices and will pay interest quarterly.

SunGard Data Systems Inc. -- $75 million of its 8 1/4% convertible subordinate debentures due 2015 priced at par was declared effective. The total principal amount of debentures being sold was increased to $75 million from the $60 million previously reported. The debentures will be convertible into common stock at a price of $26 per share. Alex. Brown & Sons Inc. and First Boston Corp. are the underwriters.

MORTGAGE-BACKED

Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $400 million of Remic mortgage securities is being offered in nine classes by Prudential-Bache Securities Inc. The offering, Series 177, is backed by Freddie Mac 9% securities, with a weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29.5 years.

Ryland Mortgage Securities Corp. -- $100 million of mortgage participation certificates (Series 1990-3), priced Thursday. The issue, underwritten by Goldman Sachs & Co., will be collateralized by fixed-rate mortgage loans purchased from RAC Mortgage Funding Corp. Series 1990-3 will be collateralized by mortgage loans having an initial aggregate principal balance of approximately $30 million with original and remaining terms to maturity of 15 years or less, and mortgage loans having an initial aggregate principal balance of approximately $70 million with original and remaining terms to maturity of greater than 15 years but not more than 30 years. It is anticipated that Series 1990-3 will be rated triple-A by both Moody's and S&P. Ryland Mortgage Securities Corp. is a limited-purpose finance subsidiary of Ryland Acceptance Corp., which is an indirect, wholly owned subsidiary of The Ryland Group Inc.

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $300 million of Remic mortgage securities, offered in 18 classes by Kidder Peabody & Co. The offering, Series 1990-68, is backed by Fannie Mae 10% securities.

OTHER

Province Of Newfoundland -- $150 million of 9 7/8% debentures, due June 1, 2020, priced at 99.761 to yield 9.90%. The noncallable debentures were priced at a spread of 120 basis points above the Treasury's 30-year bellwether bond. Rated Baa-1 by Moody's and single-A-minus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets.

EUROBONDS

Credit Local de France (France) -- 125 million European currency units of 10 3/8% Eurobonds due June 15, 1993, priced at 101.30 to yield 10.41% less fees, via Paribas Capital Markets. Fees 1 3/8.

Eurofima (agency) -- 125 million European currency units of 10 3/8% Eurobonds due June 13, 1995, priced at 101.65 to yield 10.40% less fees, via Credit Lyonnais. Fees 1 3/4.

Eksportfinans (Norway) -- 100 million European currency units of 10 1/2% five-year Eurobonds due May 29, 1995, priced at 101 7/8, via Merrill Lynch International.

Swedish National Mortgage Corp. (SBAB) (Sweden) -- 10 billion yen of dual-currency Eurobonds due May 29, 1995, priced at 101 7/8, via Mitsui Taiyo Kobe. Coupon of 8 1/2%c payable in Australian dollars, at undisclosed exchange rate. Redemption in yen.

City of Copenhagen (Denmark) -- 100 million Swiss francs of 7 1/2% public bonds due June 15, 2000, priced at 102 via Union Bank of Switzerland. Call from June 15, 1995 at 102 1/2, declining by 1/2 point per year to par.

Mitsubishi Trust Australia Ltd. (Japan parent) -- $50 million of bonds due June 14, 2000 priced at 102, via UBS Phillips & Drew. Interest for first three years 0.45 point above the six-month London Interbank Offered Rate; thereafter 10% annually. Once-only call at par at end of third year. Guaranteed by Mitsubishi Trust & Banking Corp.
