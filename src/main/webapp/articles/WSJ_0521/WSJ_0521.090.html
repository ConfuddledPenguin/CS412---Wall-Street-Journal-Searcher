Who's News: N.V. Philips Names Fine, Levy to Posts At PolyGram Unit

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 05/21/90

EINDHOVEN, the Netherlands -- The management shake-up at N.V. Philips continues as Jan Timmer promoted two PolyGram N.V. officers Friday to fill the vacancy created by his departure in July to assume the post of president of Philips. Filling Mr. Timmer's vacancy at PolyGram as chairman of the supervisory board will be David Fine. Mr. Fine, currently president and chief executive officer of PolyGram, will assume his new post at the end of 1990. Mr. Fine has been president and chief executive officer of PolyGram since 1987.

Alain Levy, currently an executive vicepresident with responsibility for popular music and music publishing in France and the U.S., will assume Mr. Fine's position as president and chief executive officer at PolyGram, which is 80%-owned by Philips.

Mr. Levy, who has been with PolyGram since 1984, oversaw PolyGram's recent acquisitions of A&M Records and Island Records in the U.S.

The two promotions are the latest developments in the management shake-up of the Dutch electronics group following Cornelius Van Der Klugt's announcement earlier this month that he would resign as president of Philips in July 1990, a year earlier than expected.
