Letters to the Editor: We Need Indexation To Control Inflation

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/21/90

Lindley H. Clark Jr.'s April 19 editorial-page column, "Indexation May Be Bad for Us," implies that tax indexing somehow increases the chances of high inflation. That's absurd. An unindexed tax system gives politicians an incentive to continue or increase inflation. At current inflation rates, a repeal of tax indexing would increase taxes by $140 billion over the next four years. Does anyone seriously believe that the hidden tax increases caused by repeal of indexing would end inflation? It certainly didn't work in the 1970s.

The politicians who create inflation would be the biggest beneficiaries. After repealing indexing, politicians could "cut" taxes by $100 billion over the four-year period, spend the $40 billion difference on pet projects, and look like heroes to their constituents.

By taking the political profit out of inflation, tax indexing helps control it. If there's an indexing culprit, it is the indexation of congressional, high-ranking executive branch and Federal Reserve officials' salaries. Consideration should be given to rewarding the Federal Reserve officials for low and steady rates of inflation.

David Keating

Executive Vice President

National Taxpayers Union

Washington
