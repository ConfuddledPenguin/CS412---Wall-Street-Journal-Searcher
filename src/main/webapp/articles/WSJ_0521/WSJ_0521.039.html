Letters to the Editor: Sadly, Chinese Become Yesterday's Headlines

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/21/90

As I read the excerpt from Bette Bao Lord's book "Legacies: A Chinese Mosaic" (editorial page, April 20), I couldn't help wonder why the U.S. government -- and even worse, the people of the U.S. -- continues to ignore the oppression of human rights occurring in China. During the Tiananmen Square incident, America rallied for the students who craved a democratic society and protested the bloody massacre that occurred afterward. It was obvious that as journalists were expelled from the country and stories stopped making the headlines, our concerns for the Chinese people seemed to dwindle. We criticize countries for depriving their citizens freedom of the press, stating they have a right to know what is going on in the world. It is true that the nightly news and newspaper media are a source of this information, but should they have such an enormous control over such an idealistic society as our own? One week it's China, the next South Africa. And then the next week it's Lithuania. Let's not lose sight of the fact that after the headlines are gone, the problems still remain.

Valerie Saye

Senior

Middle Tennessee State University

Murfreesboro, Tenn.
