Who's News: USAir Group Names Schofield President Of Namesake Unit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/21/90

ARLINGTON, Va. -- Seth E. Schofield is being promoted to the post of president of the namesake airline unit of USAir Group Inc., making him the top contender to succeed Chairman Edwin I. Colodny. Industry analysts said the promotion, to take effect June 1, moves Mr. Schofield above his top rival for the chairman's job, Randall Malin, who is executive vice president, marketing, at the airline unit, USAir, and the holding company.

Mr. Schofield, 50 years old, is being promoted from his position as executive vice president, operations, for the unit. He will keep that title at the holding company.

A company spokesman confirmed that Mr. Malin, 53, will report to Mr. Schofield at the unit. Both executives are on equal footing at the holding company.

The question of who will succeed Mr. Colodny has been the subject of much speculation, largely fueled by the fact that Mr. Colodny, 63, will reach the company's retirement age in about 13 months.

As late as two weeks ago, Mr. Colodny stayed quiet on the subject. At the company's annual shareholders meeting, Mr. Colodny wouldn't say whether or not he plans to continue running the company after he turns 65. He said such a move would depend on board approval. "The board is well aware" of its options, he said.

He declined to comment on whether he had pegged someone as a successor. Industry experts said Mr. Colodny might look outside the company for a candidate.

As president of the unit, Mr. Schofield will be succeeding Mr. Colodny, who will retain his posts as chairman and chief executive officer at the airline unit. Mr. Colodny also remains chairman and president of USAir Group.

Messrs. Malin and Schofield are vice chairmen of USAir Group. The two men and Mr. Colodny will continue to make up the office of the chairman at the holding company. The triumvirate was created a year ago in preparation for the merger of USAir and Piedmont Airlines. The merger was completed in August but has been hamstrung by operational and customer service problems.
