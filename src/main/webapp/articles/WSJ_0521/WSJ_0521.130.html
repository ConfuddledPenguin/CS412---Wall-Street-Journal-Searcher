Chrysler Expects Decent 2nd Period, Tough 3rd Quarter

  • Author: Melinda Grenier Guiles
  • Date: 05/21/90

LOS ANGELES -- An aggressive cost-cutting program should help Chrysler Corp. have "a pretty decent second quarter" but temporary closings at the auto maker's two minivan plants will likely mean a "tough" third quarter, according to Vice Chairman Gerald Greenwald. In an interview here Friday, Mr. Greenwald declined to be more specific about the company's earnings expectations.

In the first quarter, Chrysler surprised analysts with a $5 million profit from automotive operations -- a profit the company attributed to the belt-tightening it began last year. Net income dropped 80%, however, to $71 million or 32 cents a share.

Mr. Greenwald said Chrysler will lose roughly 60,000 to 70,000 units of minivan production this summer because of model-changeover closings lasting one month each at the auto maker's Canadian and U.S. assembly plants. This means Chrysler loses revenue it would have booked when it sold these vehicles to dealers.

Chrysler's retail sales might not fall by the same amount, however, because some dealers are stockpiling 1990 model vans to tide them over, Mr. Greenwald said.

He added that Chrysler is "encouraged" by dealer orders for some other Chrysler products and wants to add more third-quarter production for several cars, including Spirit and Acclaim sedans.

In the second quarter of 1989, Chrysler posted net of $341 million, or $1.46 a share. In the third quarter, Chrysler had an operating profit of $22 million, or 10 cents a share. Net was $331 million, or $1.42 a share, after a credit of $309 million, or $1.32 a share, from the sale of shares in Mitsubishi Motors Corp.

At the last stop on the auto maker's six-city promotional tour here Friday, Chairman Lee A. Iacocca confirmed that Chrysler will go ahead with plans to build the high-performance Viper sports car it unveiled at the 1989 Detroit auto show.

Chrysler officials said the budget for Viper was under $50 million -- roughly what Mr. Iacocca says Chrysler spends in one month on advertising. Viper, which will generate more than 400 horsepower with a V-10 aluminum engine, will go on sale in early 1992 with a price tag under $50,000.

Annual output eventually could total 3,000 to 5,000 units, but only about 500 will be available in the first year of sales.
