Merchants Capital Banking Subsidiary Declared Insolvent

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/21/90

BOSTON -- MerchantsBank of Boston, the main subsidiary of Merchants Capital Corp., was declared insolvent and its $350 million in assets placed under control of the Federal Deposit Insurance Corp. The eight branches of MerchantsBank will reopen today as offices of Bank of Boston Corp., the state's largest commercial bank. Bank of Boston said it paid the FDIC $7 million for MerchantsBank's $340 million in deposits.

The failure of MerchantsBank, a Massachusetts-chartered cooperative bank, marks the second insolvency at a Boston-area financial institution in the past three weeks. Late last month, federal thrift regulators took control of Home Owners Savings Bank in Burlington, Mass.

Douglas Clark, the FDIC official put in charge of MerchantsBank after Massachusetts regulators declared the bank insolvent Friday afternoon, said the agency will sell off the bank's assets. Mr. Clark said recent financial statements indicate MerchantsBank had negative net worth of about $11.3 million. But he said agency officials must now re-examine the value of the assets, comprised mainly of real estate loans. "I suspect the {ultimate} loss will be greater than $11.3 million," Mr. Clark said.

Mr. Clark and Andrew Calamare, Massachusetts commissioner of banks, said MerchantsBank represented substantially all of the assets of Merchants Capital.

John Lakian, chairman and largest shareholder of Merchants Capital, couldn't be reached to comment on the government action, which came after the close of stock market trading on Friday.

MerchantsBank stock, which has barely traded in recent weeks, remained at six cents a share Friday on the national over-the-counter market. In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange, Bank of Boston stock closed at $13 a share, up 50 cents.
