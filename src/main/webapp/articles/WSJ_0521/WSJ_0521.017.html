It'd Be a Good Place to Ballyhoo A Redo of `Goodbye, Mr. Chips'

  • Author: Meg Cox
  • Date: 05/21/90

Making book in Las Vegas isn't usually a literary pursuit. But next month, the home of the Gamblers Book Shop will host the annual convention of the American Booksellers Association. Starting June 2, thousands of bookstore owners and managers will arrive in Las Vegas, where several hundred exhibiting publishers will give away books, buttons and canvas bags to promote books coming out in the next year. Such august authors as John Updike, Amy Tan and Isabel Allende will sign autographs. In 90 years of conventions, this will be the first time the booksellers have gathered in Vegas, and frankly, some participants are shocked.

But despite their highbrow image, many publishers are scheduling appropriately tacky promotions. Take "The Encyclopedia of Bad Taste," by Jane and Michael Stern. Harper & Row, the book's publisher, has ordered caterers to serve tuna cassarole and spam sandwiches at its book party. Guests will mingle with Dolly Parton and Pee Wee Herman look-alikes. Naturally, there will be accordian music, plastic flamingos and rubber chickens.

Avon Books, a unit of Hearst Corp., plans a party honoring author Johanna Lindsey, whose best-selling romance novels include "Savage Thunder" and "Defy Not the Heart." Dinner will be served at L'Amour Chapelle, a wedding chapel, and the 50 or so couples getting married that night will all troop past the crowd of dining booksellers.

One of the classiest publishers in the business, Random House, is having a huge party at the only casino with its own erupting volcano, the new Mirage. The book being celebrated is Random House's biggest fall title, with a first printing of 500,000 copies. "Trump: Surviving at the Top," by Donald Trump, is the book.

The list goes on: Viking, an imprint of Penguin USA, is sponsoring a poker clinic with ace British poker player Anthony Holden, author of a book called "Big Deal." St. Martin's Press is holding a party for "The Caesar's Palace Book of Sports Betting Strategies" at, of course, Caesar's Palace. And Penguin, which is publishing the paperback edition of "Liar's Poker," is holding a party where a musical comedy version of the book about greedy, young bond traders will be staged.

"We'd have done something flashy for Liar's Poker anywhere," says a Penguin publicist. "But being in Vegas makes it easier to get in the mood."
