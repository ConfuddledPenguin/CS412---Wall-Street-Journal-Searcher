Business Brief -- Peabody Holding Co.: Amax Inc. Will Not Make Another Takeover Proposal

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/21/90

Amax Inc., New York, said it won't make any takeover proposal for Peabody Holding Co. other than the $1.25 billion bid it made several days ago. Amax's proposal was immediately topped by Hanson PLC, which holds 45% of Peabody, the largest U.S. coal producer. In March, Hanson agreed to buy the 55% it doesn't hold from Newmont Mining Corp. for $715 million.

Amax's bid valued the 55% Peabody stake at $718.5 million, just a bit more than Hanson's plan. After Amax's bid was announced, Hanson boosted its offer to $725.6 million.

"Amax's offer for Peabody was within our range of value for the company," said Allen Born, Amax's chairman, president and chief executive officer. The metals and energy company "will continue to pursue and evaluate opportunities in its energy businesses that have a good strategic fit for us and will enhance the level and balance of future earnings."

Many investors on Wall Street had doubted Amax would enter a bidding war over Peabody. Hanson has interests in tobacco, specialty chemicals, building products and mining, including 49% of Newmont Mining, Denver.
