Business Brief -- Lone Star Industries Inc.: Massachusetts Agency Suit Claims Defective Rail Ties

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/21/90

Lone Star Industries Inc. said the Massachusetts Bay Transportation Authority filed suit against the company in federal court in Massachusetts for $18 million over allegedly defective concrete crossties. The suit stems from MBTA's purchase of about 36,900 ties that MBTA alleges are defective. The MBTA ties are among 500,000 concrete crossties that Lone Star previously said were aging prematurely. The ties were manufactured at Lone Star's SanVel plant in Littleton, Mass.

Other purchasers of the crossties have also filed suit against Lone Star, a producer of cement and other construction materials. Those actions brought in federal court have been consolidated for pretrial purposes in the U.S. District Court of Maryland.

Lone Star has sued the suppliers of the cement used for the crossties and a testing company, which performed work on the aggregates used in the crossties.
