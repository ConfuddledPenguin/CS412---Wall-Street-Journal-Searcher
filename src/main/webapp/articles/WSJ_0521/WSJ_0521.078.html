Norfolk Southern Sells, Subleases Rail Facilities to Wheeling

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/21/90

NORFOLK, Va. -- Norfolk Southern Corp. announced the sale and sublease of major rail facilities in eastern Ohio and western Pennsylvania to Wheeling Acquisition Corp. Norfolk Southern sold 446 miles of track and facilities in Akron, Canton and Brewster, Ohio, to Wheeling for about $40 million. Wheeling, a new corporation created to acquire the properties, will also sublease the 121-mile Pittsburgh and West Virginia Railway, now leased from a real-estate investment trust by Norfolk Southern's Norfolk and Western Railway subsidiary.

The new railroad will extend from Bellevue, Huron and Cleveland south to the Ohio River, and east and south to Connellsville, Pa. It will also have access to existing Norfolk Southern yards in Cleveland and Bellevue, Ohio, and Hagerstown, Md., and interconnections with CSX, Conrail and other rail carriers in the region.

The sale continues Norfolk Southern's program of spinning off rail properties that can be run more economically by smaller lines. Roughly 425 employees now work on the lines involved in the sale; the new railroad expects to have a work force of about 380 in its first year.

As part of the $40 million sale, Norfolk Southern will provide financing to Wheeling of up to $16.7 million for existing rolling stock, including locomotives and freight cars.
