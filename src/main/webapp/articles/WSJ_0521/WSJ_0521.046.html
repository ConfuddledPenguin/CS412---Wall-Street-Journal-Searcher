Dividend News: Zemex Corp.

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/21/90

ZEMEX Corp. directors voted to omit the second-quarter dividend. The New York-based company paid a 2% stock dividend in March and a five-cent dividend in December. Previously, the company had been paying 10 cents quarterly. Don Shanks, vice president, secretary and treasurer, said the company decided to omit the dividend because it is raising funds for capital improvements. A ten-cent quarterly dividend would cost the company $711,000 a year. Zemex mines industrial minerals and tin, and has an iron-powder plant. For the first quarter, the company had a net loss of $101,000, or six cents a share, on revenue of $10.3 million. In New York Stock Exchange composite trading Friday, Zemex closed at $7, unchanged.
