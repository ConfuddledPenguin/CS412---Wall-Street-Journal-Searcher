Letters to the Editor: Sullivan's Predictions Have All Come True

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/21/90

Your April 11 editorial on medical insurance put to mind an incident that occurred 35 years ago, which I thought might be of interest. In the winter of 1955-56, I was traveling back from New Haven with the Riverdale Fencing Team, with my parents as the team chaperons. Usually it would have been a trip made by car and school bus, but there had been a heavy snow overnight and we were consigned to the tender mercies of the New Haven Railroad. My parents shuffled all of us into the dining car for a little sustenance. We were the only people in the dining section of the car, but down at the other end in the club section was a couple enjoying cocktails. It took us about one second to realize that the man was none other than Ed Sullivan, who was certainly one of the most recognizable people in America in those days. There was the usual schoolboy excitement in the presence of a celebrity and the usual banter about who was going to be brave enough to approach him for an autograph, or interview for the school paper, or whatever. Sullivan was perfectly aware of us, as we were not a small group, or especially quiet -- as we had bested Yale in the day's match.

None of us were making too much progress in deciding who was going down to see him, and Sullivan was, of course, innately aware of the subject of our conversation. Finally, he leaned forward and gave us a big sweeping motion with his hand, indicating that if we would all troop down the length of the car we would be welcome. He was charming and we were all captivated.

Then, he treated us to a 15-minute virtual diatribe on the evils of medical insurance and how it was going to inflate the cost of health-care delivery to unimaginable proportions. My parents, who were in the insurance business, were of course the only ones present who had the slightest idea of what he was talking about; but the vehemence with which he expressed his ideas left a deep impression on me, and the fact that all of his predictions have come true has only enhanced the memory of that experience.

Kenyon B. FitzGerald Jr.

McAteer & FitzGerald

Underwriting Managers

New York
