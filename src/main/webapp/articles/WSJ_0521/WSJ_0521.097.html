Business Brief -- Contel Corp.: Ex-Workers Get $8 Million In Defamation-Suit Award

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/21/90

After a four-week civil trial, a jury in U.S. District Court in Baltimore Friday found that Contel Corp. defamed two former employees, and awarded the two men a total of $8 million in punitive and compensatory damages, according to one of the plaintiff's lawyers. William P. Kamachaitis, former president of Contel's Fairfax, Va., federal-systems sector, was awarded $2.5 million in punitive damages and $2 million in compensatory damages. Thomas Hassey, former vice president for human resources of the sector, won $1.5 million in punitive damages and $2 million in compensatory damages, said Mr. Hassey's lawyer, Charles F. Chester, of Rockville, Md.

Contel officials couldn't be reached for comment.

The two men filed suit against the telephone holding company in December 1987, contending they were defamed by corporate officers in statements made to business publications and Contel employees, as well as in internal memos.

Mr. Kamachaitis and Mr. Hassey were dismissed in May 1987.
