Newbridge Networks Gets 2-Year Contract From Bell Atlantic

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/21/90

HERNDON, Va. -- Newbridge Networks Corp. has signed a two-year contract valued at $35 million to provide a line of digital networking equipment to Bell Atlantic Corp., said Peter Madsen, president of Newbridge's U.S. unit. Mr. Madsen said the contract could become a cornerstone for the Ottawa company's new push into public telecommunications networks.

Newbridge's end-user networks are installed in a phone company's central offices rather than customer locations. Mr. Madsen said this approach has several advantages, including lower costs, higher level of service, longer depreciation cycles, and "partitioning" that permits hundreds of network customers to share equipment.

"We are in discussions with most of the other Bell operating companies," Mr. Madsen said. "Some of them are interested in similar applications at phone company central offices, and others for customer-premises networks."

In addition, the company is testing equipment and software programs with government customers in Australia, New Zealand, Chile and Mexico.

Mr. Madsen declined to make an earnings estimate for the year ended April 30. But he indicated that revenue almost doubled from the previous year's level of 67 million Canadian dollars (US$57 million). In fiscal 1989, Newbridge earned C$9.1 million. The company went public in July 1989.
