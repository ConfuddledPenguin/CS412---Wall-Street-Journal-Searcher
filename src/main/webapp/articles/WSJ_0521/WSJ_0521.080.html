Who's News: John Carway Gets Two Top Positions At Verit Industries

  • Author: Earl C. Gottschalk Jr.
  • Date: 05/21/90

PHOENIX, Ariz. -- John Carway, 44-year-old president and 38% owner of Verit Industries, a real-estate concern, was elected chairman and chief executive officer. Mr. Carway, an Irish businessman who acquired his stake in Verit and became its president last November, succeeds Malcolm Stockdale, who resigned last week as chairman and chief executive. Mr. Stockdale, 45, resigned after Verit's acquisition of 56% of Houston-based Iroquois Brands Ltd. was "rescinded."

Mr. Stockdale, along with Mr. Carway, had led Verit on the acquisition trail in recent months in an apparent attempt to build the company into a multi-industry concern with interests in construction equipment, real estate and control of toxic wastes. Many of the proposed transactions involved target companies in need of financial aid, and two of the proposed acquisitions had become unraveled in the past month.

A previous Wall Street Journal story noted that terms of transactions proposed by Messrs. Stockdale and Carway sometimes called for issuance of large numbers of convertible preferred shares and often were bewilderingly complex, sometimes involving companies in the British Isle of Man tax haven. The story also pointed out that Mr. Carway has convictions on his record in Ireland for check fraud and in England for marijuana possession.

Verit also said Arthur Andersen & Co. was appointed auditor of the company. A previous auditor, Touche Ross & Co., now part of Deloitte & Touche, quit auditing Verit's books last November after a dispute with the company over what it said could be inadequate disclosure of consulting fees to a company controlled by Mr. Carway.

Verit also announced that it earned a profit of $316,000, or four cents a share, in its third quarter ended March 31, on sales of $9.3 million. The company said year-earlier figures aren't relevant because the company's structure changed after acquisition of two home-building concerns from Emerald Homes Limited Partnership. The company didn't provide earnings and sales figures for the nine months.

Philip J. Polich, 41, formerly vice president and chief operating officer of Verit, was named to succeed Mr. Carway as president. Jimmy Wood, 58, president of his own Phoenix-based real-estate investment company, was elected executive vice president, chief operating officer and chief financial officer.
