Michaels Stores Sees Earnings Near Estimates

  • Author: Karen Blumenthal
  • Date: 05/21/90

IRVING, Texas -- Michaels Stores Inc. is comfortable with analysts' estimates that it will report net income of about $450,000, or four cents a share, for the first quarter ended April 29, said B.B. Tuley, president and chief executive officer. Michaels, an arts and crafts retailer, had net of $408,000, or four cents a share, in the year-ago quarter. The company previously reported that first-quarter sales rose 27.5% to $71.7 million from $56.2 million.

Mr. Tuley said Michaels is hopeful that it will report record earnings this year after a dismal fiscal 1990.

In fiscal 1990, Michaels had net of $13,000, essentially breaking even after taking about $4 million in pretax charges to reflect a failed acquisition. The retailer also added about $1.5 million to its reserves in case it decides to close some stores. Sales were $289.8 million.

Mr. Tuley said Michaels expects to grow 15% to 20% this year, in part by opening 18 stores. Michaels had 121 stores at the end of 1989.

Last year, Michaels agreed to be acquired for $9.50 a share in cash and $2.50 in new preferred stock by an investment group that included the Robert M. Bass Group of Fort Worth, Texas, and Grauer & Wheat Investments Inc. of Dallas. But the offer fell apart when the group acknowledged that it couldn't sell the junk bonds to finance the deal. Mr. Tuley said the company hadn't been looking for a buyer, but its directors accepted the transaction because it would have offered a premium over the stock price.

Now, he says, "we're not for sale."

This year, Michaels is to open its first stores in Florida and Mississippi, giving it stores in almost every state in the southern half of the country.
