Marketing Brief -- All American Television Inc.: Scotti Brothers to Acquire 42.5% of Shares Outstanding

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/21/90

Scotti Brothers Entertainment Industries Inc., a privately held entertainment concern in Los Angeles, said it reached an option agreement which gives Scotti the right to acquire 42.5% of the shares outstanding of All American Television Inc. All American Television Inc., based in New York, is engaged in the distribution of television programming and the sale of commercial time to advertisers. Scotti said the agreement, reached with certain principal shareholders of All American, gives Scotti the right to acquire all of their shares at a price of $6.25 a share. The agreement further stipulates that not less than $3.50 a share will be paid in cash, and the balance in securities.

The option agreement contemplates a merger, business combination or similar transaction between Scotti and All American, the companies said. Scotti Brothers may not exercise the option unless it offers to acquire all of the stock of All American owned by public shareholders on terms at least as favorable as those given to the company's principal holders, the companies said.

Scotti Brothers' exercise of the option is also conditional, among other things, upon All American's satisfying certain financial conditions. The option agreement expires Aug. 16, unless extended, and if exercised, the closing must occur by Dec. 14, 1990, subject to extension.
