REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: Accounting for Congress?

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/21/90

The Census Bureau can't explain the low rate of census forms that have been filled out and returned; it's about 75% nationwide, down from nearly 90% a decade ago. Some say it's apathy, but we say it was a marketing error. The Census Bureau warned in grave tones that if people failed to return their forms, they could lose congressional representation. What a brier patch. The prospect of fewer congressional seats might seem like an even better alternative than the proposed 12-year-term limitation for Congressmen.
