Sprague Technologies Directors Authorize Spinoff of Chip Unit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/21/90

STAMFORD, Conn. -- Sprague Technologies Inc. said its board authorized the company to spin off its semiconductor business to shareholders. The distribution, whose terms haven't yet been worked out, is expected to occur in the third quarter. A company official said Sprague would be filing documents with the Securities and Exchange Commission in about a month.

The semiconductor business represents about 25% of Sprague's total assets of $387 million, said Steven Meyers, senior vice president, finance and administration. Mr. Meyers said the spinoff doesn't require shareholder approval. Sprague, which has about 18.2 million shares outstanding, closed Friday at $5.625, up 50 cents, in composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange Friday.

Edward F. Kosnik, president and chief executive officer of the capacitor and electronic parts company, said the spinoff would give the semiconductor group its own identity and that the move would enable Sprague holders "to better realize the underlying value" of that group.

Mr. Kosnik said the spinoff is timely because of the semiconductor group's "new generation of product offerings for the disk drive, computer peripherals and automotive markets." The semiconductor group, which Sprague reports as a discontinued operation, had profit of $100,000 on sales of $28 million in the first quarter. Without the semiconductor results, Sprague net income would have been $1.4 million, or eight cents a share, on sales of $84.4 million.
