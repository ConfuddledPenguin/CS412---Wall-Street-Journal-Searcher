International: GEC and Thomson Discuss Work on Fighter-Jet Radar

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/21/90

PARIS -- The defense and space unit of General Electric Co. of Britain, GEC-Marconi Ltd., said late last week an agreement is "very close" on cooperating with one of its archrivals, Thomson-CSF S.A., on the next generation of fighter-aircraft radar. Thomson-CSF, a unit of French state-owned Thomson S.A., declined to comment. GEC isn't related to General Electric Co. of the U.S. GEC-Marconi said this would be the first venture by Europe's two largest defense electronics companies. The talks are the latest indication of the transformation under way in the world's defense business, after revolutionary changes in Eastern Europe, as Western governments are cutting defense spending plans.

A Thomson-GEC radar pact would be a first step toward creating the first truly pan-European fighter aircraft. France is building the Rafale, competing with a British, West German, Italian and Spanish effort to develop the European Fighter Aircraft. Thomson is involved in developing the Rafale radar, and GEC-Marconi is developing the EFA radar. The proposed radar cooperation wouldn't affect those programs but would be aimed at a joint effort for any successor programs.
