South Africa's Trade Surplus

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/21/90

JOHANNESBURG -- South Africa's trade surplus widened to 969 million rand ($368 million) in April from 872 million rand a year earlier, the government's Customs and Excise office said. April's surplus was lower than March's surplus of 1.17 billion rand.

The figures aren't seasonally adjusted, but export and import data are adjusted to bring them in line with the requirements for compiling the nation's balance of payments.

Exports for April totaled about 4.60 billion rand, against 4.55 billion a year earlier, while imports amounted to about 3.63 billion rand, compared with approximaely 3.68 billion in April 1989.
