Lionel Corp.'s Loss Widened to $6 Million In Fiscal First Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/21/90

NEW YORK -- Embattled Lionel Corp. posted a wider fiscal first-quarter loss, citing higher expenses associated with new stores opened late last year and lower existing-store sales. The loss in the quarter ended April 28 was $6 million, or 44 cents a share, vs. $3.6 million, or 27 cents a share, a year earlier. Sales grew 7.5% to $65.9 million from $61.3 million.

Lionel, which currently operates 92 toy stores in 15 states, said it plans to open 10 stores in major metropolitan markets in 1990.

The company has been under pressure from some shareholders to find a buyer. Earlier this year it turned down investor Robert I. Toussie's $8-a-share, or $95.4 million, offer and got him to drop the proposal. The stock since then has been trading well below that level.

Trendex Capital Management Corp., a Fort Lauderdale, Fla., firm representing a dissident group of Lionel shareholders with a 5% stake, has requested that Lionel clear the way for would-be acquirers. It also asked Lionel to empanel an outside committee to examine Lionel's relationship with Isaac Perlmutter and Bernard Marden.

Messrs. Perlmutter and Marden are principals in a media-buying company that has Lionel as a client, and Lionel has purchased toy inventory from companies controlled by them. Mr. Marden provided the cash for Otis Group Inc., a newly formed entity, to buy half of Mr. Toussie's 9.9% Lionel stake for $7 a share the day Mr. Toussie dropped his bid.

Lionel has refused Trendex's requests and has declined to meet with Trendex, calling the firm "a disgruntled, uninformed risk-arbitrager." As for any link with between Otis and "any entities with which Lionel does business," Lionel told Trendex it knows of none, adding that it is cooperating with a Securities and Exchange Commission investigation of the Otis stock purchase.

Trendex, however, has provided Lionel with stock-trade records showing that Mr. Marden provided the capital for Otis. The investment firm said Lionel has agreed to reconsider its requests at a future board meeting.
