Business Brief -- Entergy Corp.: New Orleans Rejects Plan To Purchase Utility Unit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/21/90

Entergy Corp. said the New Orleans City Council voted to reject a buy-out plan under which the city could have acquired the company's New Orleans Public Service Inc. unit and certain other properties for more than $700 million. The utility holding company previously said it would rather not sell the assets. The buy-out plan was made under a longstanding "perpetual right" held by the city allowing it to purchase New Orleans Public Service assets at any time.

Under the proposal, the utility would have become city property, but Entergy management would have continued to operate it for five years. New Orleans Public Service provides electricity to about 192,000 customers and gas to about 159,000 customers in that city.

A proposed financing package called for the issuance of more than $850 million of revenue bonds, on which payments would be about $8 million a month for 20 years. A council advisory committee estimated that consumers would save about $8 a month under the plan.
