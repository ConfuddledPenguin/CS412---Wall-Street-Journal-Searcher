Who's News: Campeau Corp.

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/21/90

CAMPEAU Corp. (Toronto) -- Gerard Coulombe, a Montreal lawyer, was appointed a director of this real estate and retailing concern, to serve as a National Bank of Canada representative, succeeding Claude Ducharme. Harry Macdonnell, an independent director appointed in February, resigned, and a successor on the 12-member board is expected to be named soon. Montreal-based National Bank obtained two Campeau board seats in January after it seized certain Campeau securities from Campeau Chairman and Chief Executive Officer Robert Campeau as the result of defaults on about 153 million Canadian dollars (US$130.4 million) of personal loans he owed the bank; it agreed to return the securities to him on May 31 if he obtains financing to repay C$40 million of the loans. He would then have until Jan. 31, 1992, to pay another C$40 million due to National Bank, or the bank will take back part of the securities.
