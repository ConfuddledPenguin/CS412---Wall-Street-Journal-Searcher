Business Brief -- Corporate Capital Resources Inc.: Venture-Capital Firm Says It's Under Scrutiny by SEC

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/21/90

Corporate Capital Resources Inc., Westlake Village, Calif., said the Securities and Exchange Commission has been investigating the company since February for possibly manipulating its stock price. Corporate Capital, a venture-capital firm, said the probe relates to its lawsuit against Los Angeles-based Securities Settlement Corp., a clearing broker. The suit alleges that Securities Settlement manipulated Corporate Capital's stock price, the company said.

Corporate Capital said the SEC is looking into whether Corporate Capital participated in that alleged manipulation or others since September 1988.

Securities Settlement officials couldn't be reached immediately for comment.

The SEC also is investigating whether Corporate Capital misstated its investment holdings in its financial reports to the agency, the company said.

In an interview, Daniel Weston, Corporate Capital president and chief executive officer, said: "We strongly believe the results of the investigation will be favorable to us and won't have any adverse effects on the company."

In national over-the-counter trading Friday, Corporate Capital's stock closed at 12.5 cents a share, down 3.125 cents.
