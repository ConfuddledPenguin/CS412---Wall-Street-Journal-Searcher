Technology: Johnson & Johnson Unit's Hepatitis C Kit Is Approved by FDA

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/03/90

WASHINGTON -- The Food and Drug Administration approved a hepatitis C test kit developed by Ortho Diagnostics Systems Inc., a unit of Johnson & Johnson. The kit, which is used to detect hepatitis C in blood for transfusions, includes a biotechnologically derived recombinant antigen developed by Chiron Corp. of Ermeyville, Calif. Chiron, whose stock is traded over the counter, has licensed the product to Ortho.

The antigen detects the presence of the antibody to hepatitis C, which is "responsible for the majority of post-transfusion hepatitis cases," according to an FDA spokeswoman. There are 170,000 new infections of hepatitis C each year.

The FDA is recommending the new screening test to all blood banks. A spokesman for Ortho said the company will begin shipping the test kits immediately.
