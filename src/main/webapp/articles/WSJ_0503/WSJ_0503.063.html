R.H. Macy Says Its Loss Narrowed In April 28 Quarter

  • Author: Jeffrey A. Trachtenberg
  • Date: 05/03/90

NEW YORK -- R.H. Macy & Co. said in a letter to shareholders that it narrowed losses in its third quarter ended April 28 on flat sales. Although investors won't receive an audited filing for the company's third quarter before mid-June, Macy management regularly updates shareholders about the company's performance. In the year-ago quarter, Macy had a loss of $64 million on revenue of $1.5 billion.

In the letter, which was signed by Edward Finkelstein, chairman, Mark Handler, president, and Myron Ullman, executive vice president, Macy noted that earnings before interest, taxes and depreciation had exceeded the $154 million the company reported for the same period last year.

Macy executives also said they expect fourth-quarter results will "be similar" to those of the third quarter. However, they predicted that revenue will probably be higher because of a planned July preseason sale. One of Macy's objectives has been to reduce its storewide promotions, a goal management said it is achieving. Retailers who saw their Christmas profits disappear because of aggressive price-cutting are trying to limit such discounting.

"The letter indicates not only that they had a plan, but that the plan is working," said Barre W. Littel, a high yield analyst at First Boston Corp. who recently issued an optimistic report on Macy's junk bonds. "Macy entered spring with low inventories, had fewer promotions, and increased {operating} earnings as a result."

Mr. Littel noted that Macy continued to keep inventories down, and by doing so, reduced both borrowings and interest expense. In its shareholder letter, Macy management said its inventory expenditures during the third quarter were $250 million below that of the 1989 quarter, a strategy that enabled the company to cut its seasonal working capital needs.

"Macy is working on both blades of the scissors, reducing the seasonal borrowings and interest expense while improving margins and operating expenses on the other," added Mr. Littel.

In recent weeks Macy bond prices have dropped as anxiety levels regarding retailer bonds have risen. The Macy 14.5% senior subordinated debentures due 1998 are being quoted at about 70.5 cents on the dollar, while the l4.5% subordinated debentures due 2001 are being quoted at 60 cents on the dollar.

Still, others besides First Boston are upbeat about Macy's bonds. "Assuming a return to a normal retail environment and a slow-growth economy, they are a good buy," said Clayton Rose, managing director at J.P. Morgan Securities Inc. "But even if there is a recession and the company is forced to restructure on a worst-case basis, the two coupon-paying bonds have values only five to seven points below current levels. Given the price level and the yield there is value for the risk."
