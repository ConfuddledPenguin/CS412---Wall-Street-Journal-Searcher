Credit Ratings: Citizens & Southern, Sovran Debt Ratings Are Cut by Moody's

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/03/90

NEW YORK -- Moody's Investors Service Inc. said it downgraded its debt ratings for Citizens & Southern Corp. of Atlanta, and Sovran Financial Corp. of Norfolk, Va. The two companies are in the process of merging to form Avantor Financial Corp. The $4.7 billion transaction, which will create the nation's 14th-largest banking company, is expected to be completed in the third quarter.

Moody's also lowered the ratings on the long-term deposits of the superregional banking company's subsidiary banks.

The rating agency said the actions, which affect about $750 million in securities, reflect the combined $47 billion institution's significant exposure to overbuilt real estate markets. Citizens recently reported first-quarter profit fell 28% to $41.6 million, or 64 cents a share, primarily because of a $54.8 million charge for problem loans.

The company's senior debt was downgraded to single-A-2 from single-A-1; subordinated debt to single-A-3 from single-A-2; shelf registration to (P)single-A-2 from (P)single-A-1, and preferred stock to single-A-2 from single-A-1.

Sovran's senior debt was downgraded to single-A-2 from single-A-1; its medium-term notes to single-A-2 from single-A-1; subordinated debt to single-A-3 from single-A-2; subordinated capital notes to single-A-3 from single-A-2, and shelf registration to (P)single-A-2 from (P)single-A-1.
