Grow Group Settles Felony, Misdemeanor Charges From Fire

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/03/90

NEW YORK -- Grow Group Inc. said the company and two of its executives settled charges stemming from a 1988 chemical fire at its Commerce, Calif., facility. The fire released a cloud of chlorine gas over the company's warehouse, forcing authorities to evacuate some 25,000 residents in surrounding neighborhoods.

The company said it would incur a $3.8 million charge in its fiscal third quarter ended March 31 because of the settlement and other related matters concerning its consumer and professional products group. Grow Group reported a loss of $2,263,000 in the March 1989 quarter.

Under terms of the settlement, the company said it pleaded no contest to one felony and three misdemeanor charges of violations of the California Hazardous Waste Control Act, agreeing to pay a fine of $400,000.

The company also agreed to contribute $350,000 to fund academic scholarships at five high schools in the Commerce area as restitution for the costs incurred by Los Angeles County agencies and municipalities.

Grow Group also agreed to be placed on summary probation for three years.

Two executives in the company's consumer and professional products group pleaded "no contest" to four misdemeanor charges.

The company said it has reached an agreement in principle to settle a related civil action which the California state attorney general had been considering bringing against the company.
