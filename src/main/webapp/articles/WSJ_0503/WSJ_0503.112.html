Business Brief -- EECO Inc.: Parent and 3 Divisions Seek Protection From Creditors

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/03/90

EECO Inc. said it filed for protection from creditors under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code. The filing affects the Santa Ana, Calif., company's keyboard, switch and keypad divisions. Robert Bonney, chairman, said EECO Computer Inc., a financially independent unit, and EECO Ltd., a subsidiary based in Scotland, were not included in the petition. A company spokesman said the filing didn't include a statement of assets and liabilities, and said those figures weren't immediately available.

Previously, the company had said it may seek bankruptcy-law protection if Sanwa Bank Ltd.'s Sanwa Bank of California declared the company in default on its $10 million credit agreement, which expired March 31. EECO said it has been unable to sell its Maxi-Switch division on terms acceptable to the bank, and Sanwa had ceased to fund the company's restructuring plan.

EECO also said George B. DeHuff III, previously president of the switch division, was elected president and chief executive officer of the high-technology products maker.
