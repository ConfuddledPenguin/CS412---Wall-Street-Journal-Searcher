REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: Mexico's Progress

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/03/90

While Mikhail Gorbachev flounders with perestroika, popularly elected Mexican President Carlos Salinas de Gortari speeds forward with the reformation of Mexico's formerly inert economy. Yesterday he submitted to the Mexican Congress a bill to eliminate restrictions on private-sector ownership of banks. Simply put, he plans to privatize Mexico's banking system, nationalized in 1982 by former President Lopez Portillo. To do so, Mr. Salinas will have to change the constitution, and this takes a two-thirds vote by Congress. But with the support of the free-market opposition party, PAN, the President should be able to achieve this necessary step in the continuing economic liberalization of Mexico.
