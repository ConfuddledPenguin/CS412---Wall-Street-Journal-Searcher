Los Angeles Mayor Submits Water Conservation Plan

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/03/90

LOS ANGELES -- Mayor Tom Bradley proposed a mandatory water conservation plan to cut individual water use 10% from 1986 levels as the state faces a fourth consecutive year of drought. The plan would include penalties for excess use and the hiring of additional workers to enforce the program. The proposal, which must be enacted by the City Council, would be the toughest yet in the struggle to curtail use by homes, businesses and corporations.

A voluntary conservation program has been in effect since April 1988, but Mayor Bradley said that "we are facing a crisis." Those who have voluntarily reduced consumption 10% won't be required to make additional cuts. The mayor urged the council to act on the plan "as soon as possible," which could mean the program may be in place in six weeks, but local officials said full implementation could take months.

Preliminary water runoff projections from the eastern Sierra watershed are about half the normal level for the summer months, according to L.A. Department of Water and Power officials. The runoff is even lower than that of the past few drought years.

In Northern California, the San Francisco Public Utilities Commission is scheduled to vote Tuesday on a proposal for mandatory 25% cuts in water use based on 1987 consumption. The rationing, if approved, would be similar to a mandatory program in place from May 1988 to May 1989, said Cheryl Davis, acting manager of the San Francisco Water Department's customer service unit.
