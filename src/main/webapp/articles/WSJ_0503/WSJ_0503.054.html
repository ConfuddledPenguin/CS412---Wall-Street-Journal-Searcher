Salomon Chief Tells Meeting His Views On `Liar's Poker' --- Gutfreund Says Book on Firm Caused Tension but Bears `No Relation to Reality'

  • Author: Michael Siconolfi
  • Date: 05/03/90

NEW YORK -- At Salomon Inc.'s annual meeting yesterday, it was business as usual until somebody mentioned That Book. In an unusual diversion from Wall Street solemnity, Salomon Chairman John Gutfreund treated shareholders to his first comments on the book "Liar's Poker," a bestseller about bond trading at the firm.

"We have been a laughingstock" because of the book, he said. The book apparently caused tensions with some clients. But "we have overcome that by doing our job well."

The bestselling book chronicles the bawdy antics of some Salomon traders, including one known as the "Human Piranha." Written by a former Salomon bond salesman, Michael Lewis, it paints an unflattering picture of Salomon sticking certain clients with bad bonds on occasion, among other things.

At the meeting, Mr. Gutfreund said he never met the author Mr. Lewis, and said the book bears "no relation to reality." The book "systematically attempted to say we ravaged our customers. It didn't make much sense," he said. "We may have been stupid, but we have been honorable in dealing with our customers."

When "Liar's Poker" came out last autumn, Salomon officials discussed the book at a morning meeting, telling employees to read it so they'd be able to defend themselves, industry executives said. Since then, the firm hasn't said much publicly about the matter.

But "Liar's Poker" wasn't the only piece of literature on Salomon's agenda at yesterday's 1 1/4-hour meeting. Mr. Gutfreund called "Barbarians at the Gate" -- the book about the mammoth RJR Nabisco Inc. takeover -- a "pretty good book."

One passage in Barbarians has Salomon pulling out of the deal because its name wouldn't be on the preferred side of a planned advertisement. That passage was "a gross oversimplification," Mr. Gutfreund said. "We had major reservations about other aspects of the deal."
