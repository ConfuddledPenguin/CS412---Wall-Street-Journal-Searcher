Business Brief -- Ply Gem Industries: Quarterly Net Loss Reflects Seasonal Business of SNE

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/03/90

Ply Gem Industries Inc. reported a first-quarter net loss of $1.7 million, or 16 cents a share, reflecting the highly seasonal business of SNE Enterprises, acquired by Ply Gem in March 1989. The New York-based manufacturer of home improvement products earned $2.2 million, or 20 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue increased 23% to $121.3 million, from $98.3 million.

Ply Gem said its financial condition remains strong. The company said SNE normally posts a large first-quarter loss because of its seasonal window-and-door product line. Ply Gem added that an increasingly higher percentage of its sales and earnings will occur during the busy spring and summer remodeling periods.

The company also authorized the purchase of as many as 500,000 shares of Ply Gem stock over an unspecified period. The company currently has 10.5 million common shares outstanding.

In American Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Ply Gem closed at $7.50, up 25 cents.
