Inspiration Resources Loss in First Quarter Grew to $11.7 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/03/90

NEW YORK -- Inspiration Resources Corp. reported its net loss widened in the first quarter, to $11.7 million, or 18 cents a share, from $322,000 a year earlier. At the company's annual meeting here, Reuben F. Richards, chairman, president and chief executive officer, blamed lower base-metals prices at its Hudson Bay Mining & Smelting Co. subsidiary and a decline in volumes and fertilizer prices at its agribusiness subsidiary, Terra International Inc. Mr. Richards said he expects Terra to post second-quarter profit higher than the $43 million of a year ago.

First-quarter revenue declined 8.7%, to $268.2 million from $293.8 million.

Mr. Richards said the company's coal operations were profitable, reversing a year-earlier loss.

Inspiration Resources authorized about $100 million for capital spending this year, an increase from the year-earlier $87 million, Mr. Richards said. He added that Inspiration plans to commit "substantial funds" in the 1990s to exploration at Hudson Bay Mining, and he expects work to begin by year end on a more than $140 million project to reduce sulfur dioxide emissions at its Flin Flon metallurgical complex.

The company plans to open a zinc and copper mine in northern Manitoba, and to complete a shaft development project at Hudson Bay's 44%-owned Trout Lake copper and zinc mine, which are expected to increase overall copper and zinc production for its own account about 10% this year.

Mr. Richards said the company, which recently entered the construction-materials business with the purchase of LTM Inc. and Rogue Aggregates Inc., expects to expand that business through acquisitions so that it eventually will account for 20% to 30% of annual pretax earnings.
