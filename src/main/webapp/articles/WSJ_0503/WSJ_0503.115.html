Business Brief -- Integrated Resources Inc.: Big Board Plans to Suspend Trading in Firm's Securities

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/03/90

Trading in securities of Integrated Resources Inc. on the New York Stock Exchange will be suspended before the opening on May 16, and the exchange said it will seek to delist the issues. The exchange said it is taking the action because New York-based Integrated has fallen below exchange requirements related to aggregate market value of publicly held shares and of shares outstanding; average net income for the past three years; and net tangible assets available to common stock.

Integrated filed for protection from creditors under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code in February. The company, once a high-flying financial-services concern, now manages more than 600 partnerships in diverse businesses. It had a third-quarter loss of $183.8 million, or $24.81 a share.

The exchange said that in addition to common stock, its action affects the following securities: three issues of preferred stock; one issue of senior subordinated debentures; three issues of senior subordinated notes; and three issues of senior notes.
