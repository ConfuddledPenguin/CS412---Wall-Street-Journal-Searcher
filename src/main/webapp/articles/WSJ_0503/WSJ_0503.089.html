Technology: IBM to Buy Minority Stake In Valid Logic Systems

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/03/90

ARMONK N.Y. -- International Business Machines Corp. said it will buy a minority stake in Valid Logic Systems Inc., a San Jose, Calif.-based developer of software that helps engineers design electronic parts. Continuing its recent series of investments in small hardware and software companies, IBM said it will make an initial investment of $11.2 million in Valid's newly issued voting-convertible-preferred stock. That will give it between 5% and 8.3% of Valid, with the per-share price and resultant stake depending on how Valid's common stock performs between May 2 and May 30.

An IBM spokeswoman said the new capital will help Valid develop applications that run on IBM's new RS-6000 workstation.

IBM also said it will buy $1.2 million of warrants to acquire up to 4.5 million Valid common shares at $11 a share. And it agreed to buy up to $90 million more in Valid convertible preferred stock between 1991 and 1994, with the condition that IBM's stake isn't required to exceed 49.99%.

In national over-the-counter trading yesterday, Valid shares closed at $3, up 37.5 cents.
