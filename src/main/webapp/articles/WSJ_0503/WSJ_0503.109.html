International Brief -- Cie. Financiere de Paribas: Bank Unit's Group Earnings Climbed 19% During 1989

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/03/90

Banque Paribas said group profit rose 19% in 1989, helped by a decline in new provisions for loan risks. The bank unit of Cie. Financiere de Paribas said group earnings after payments to minority interests climbed to 1.38 billion francs ($244.3 million) for the year from 1.16 billion francs in 1988.

Banque Paribas noted that its 1989 performance was marked by narrower margins on loan activity, following an inversion of the yield curve in France. It also said that profit from capital market operations was weaker than in previous years, without elaborating. Banque Paribas said merchant banking and fund management grew significantly in 1989, however.

The combination of these elements left operating profit before income taxes and provisions at 3.66 billion francs, down from 4.48 billion francs a year earlier. This erosion was offset by a decline in new loan provisions to 1.34 billion francs from 2.5 billion francs in 1988. Paribas noted that the lower provisions reflected an improvement in the quality of its loan portfolio.
