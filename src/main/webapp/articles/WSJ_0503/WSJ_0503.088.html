Marketing Brief -- TV3 Network Ltd.: Appointment of a Receiver Asked of Westpac Banking

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/03/90

TV3 Network Ltd., a New Zealand television broadcaster 14.7%-owned by General Electric Co.'s National Broadcasting Co. of the U.S., said it has asked Westpac Banking Corp. to appoint a receiver. In a brief statement to the New Zealand Stock Exchange, Auckland-based TV3 said Westpac, its principal banker, "has withdrawn its banking facilities from the company." The statement added, "Accordingly, the directors have resolved to request Westpac to appoint a receiver."

TV3 is New Zealand's first private television network. It began broadcasting in October 1989 in competition with two state-owned television networks, and was floated on the stock exchange in November.
