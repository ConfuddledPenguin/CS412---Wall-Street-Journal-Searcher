Pickens to Disclose Results of Survey On Japan Pricing

  • Author: Paulette Thomas
  • Date: 05/03/90

WASHINGTON -- T. Boone Pickens, the Texas shareholder advocate and corporate raider, said he will disclose the results of a survey of 2,500 Japanese auto parts suppliers, in testimony before the House Judiciary Committee tomorrow, that indicate dissatisfaction with Japan's system of corporate ownership. Mr. Pickens, who owns a 26% stake in Koito Manufacturing Co. through his Boone Co., is fighting Japan's "keiretsu" system, under which parent companies dictate pricing policies of affiliated companies from which they purchase supplies.

Under the system, Toyota Motor Corp., which owns 19% of Koito's stock, may dictate prices of the lights they purchase from Koito for Toyota cars. Mr. Pickens asserts that the keiretsu system gives Toyota "hidden dividends," and benefits at the expense of Koito's other shareholders.

Mr. Pickens said that responses to the survey indicate dissatisfaction with the system. A Japanese executive, whose identity will be hidden by a screen, is also scheduled to testify about the system.

Mr. Pickens, in an interview, said he expects to earn a profit on his Koito's holdings by exerting pressure to broaden the potential buyers of Koito's products. "We believe Koito has great opportunity to expand its business," he said. "They aren't allowed to do it, because they are a Toyota captive."

He added that other parts suppliers with relationships to parent companies similar to Koito's are pleased that Mr. Pickens is attempting to upset the status quo. "Other peer companies to Koito are in communication with us," he said.
