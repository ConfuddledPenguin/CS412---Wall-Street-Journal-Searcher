World Bank Seeking Loans to Poor Nations For the Environment

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/03/90

WASHINGTON -- World Bank President Barber Conable said he hopes to launch a fund to make low-interest loans to poor and middle-income countries for environmental projects. Mr. Conable told reporters he plans to seek endorsement for the fund at a meeting of the bank's development committee next week.

The pilot project would make loans totaling $300 million to $400 million annually for three years. The loans would be targeted to addressing environmental problems, such as deforestation or desertification, that affect the global climate. He said the loans might also be available for projects such as cleaning up pollution in Eastern Europe.

The fund would differ from current World Bank programs by providing subsidized, low-rate loans to middle-income nations, as well as poor nations. Currently, the bank's cut-rate financing is available only to the very poorest nations. Other countries must borrow from the bank at market-based interest rates.

Mr. Conable said the subsidy for middle-income nations is justified because the benefits of environmental projects go beyond national boundaries. He said the project would also result in an increase in the bank's environmental staff.

In answering questions on the program, Mr. Conable refused to join the debate over whether there is a general trend toward global warming. But "if there is global change, it will affect poor people more than rich," he said, and therefore is a worthy concern for the bank.

The environmental fund must be approved by the member nations of the bank, who will also have to provide the money. The fund will be a cooperative effort of the World Bank, the United Nations Development Program and the United Nations Environmental Program.

The establishment of an environmental fund at the World Bank was first advocated last year by French officials. Treasury officials say the U.S. hasn't formulated a position on the proposal.
