U.S. High School Students Fail to Improve Test Scores

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/03/90

WASHINGTON -- Years of increasing efforts to improve America's schools have failed to boost high school student scores on two widely administered tests. In its annual performance review, the Education Department reported that scores have barely changed over the past four years. "We have reached a plateau," Education Secretary Lauro Cavazos complained. "Moving beyond this point will require more comprehensive and even radical changes in our school systems."

The average score on the Scholastic Aptitude Test slipped to 903 in 1989 from 904 in 1988, while the average score on the American College Testing Program fell to 18.6 from 18.8. The report also noted that the proportion of public school ninth-graders who graduate with their class fell to 71.1% last year from 71.7% the year before.

These figures for the 1988-1989 school year are likely to become one of the baselines against which the Bush administration's efforts to improve U.S. education are measured. Early this year, President Bush and the nation's governors detailed an ambitious set of education goals for the year 2000.
