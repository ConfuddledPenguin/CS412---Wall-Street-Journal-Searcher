International Brief -- Tate & Lyle PLC: Pretax Earnings Rose 25% In Fiscal 6-Month Period

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/03/90

Tate & Lyle PLC, Britain's biggest sugar producer, reported a 25% jump in pretax profit for the six months ended March 31, helped by good results at both its U.K. and U.S. operations. The company said pretax profit rose to #87.5 million ($143.9 million) from #70.2 million, surpassing market expectations.

Profit after tax, less minority interests, jumped 35% to #50.3 million from #37.2 million. The company reported an extraordinary gain of #28.8 million from the sales of businesses completed during the half year. It said profit attributable to shareholders more than doubled to #79.1 million from #37.2 million. There were no extraordinary items during the same period a year earlier.

Sales rose 11% to #1.68 billion from #1.52 billion.

Tate & Lyle Chairman Neil Shaw said the company is expecting a continued good performance from all the core businesses during 1990 "to produce another satisfactory result for the full year."
