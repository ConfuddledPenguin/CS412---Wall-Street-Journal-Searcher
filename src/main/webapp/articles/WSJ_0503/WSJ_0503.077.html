Treasury's Brady Voices Mild Concern About Dollar

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/03/90

WASHINGTON -- Treasury Secretary Nicholas Brady expressed mild concern over the rise in the value of the dollar against the yen, saying it "certainly doesn't help" U.S. efforts to narrow the country's massive trade deficit with Japan. Mr. Brady said he doesn't know whether the dollar's recent strength against the Japanese currency will hurt U.S. exports seriously, but he added: "it's something you do worry about."

Speaking at a United States Information Agency conference, Mr. Brady said he was pleased by the way the world financial markets have weathered the recent plunge in the Japanese stock market.

"When you get that huge an adjustment in an individual country, you worry it's going to ripple out and produce dislocation all over the world," he said. "The truth is the system has worked very well.

Although the yen has fallen, he said, "the very, very good news is we've had stability while we've had an enormous sell-off in the Japanese market."
