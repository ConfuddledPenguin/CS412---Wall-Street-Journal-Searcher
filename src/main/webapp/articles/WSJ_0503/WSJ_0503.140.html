Ames Financing Of $25 Million Is Cleared by Court

  • Author: Joseph Pereira
  • Date: 05/03/90

Ames Department Stores Inc. said it received U.S. bankruptcy court clearance to receive $25 million in financing, enabling the discount retailer to purchase badly needed merchandise for its some 600 stores. The loan is part of $250 million in financing Ames negotiated with Chemical Bank, pending court approval. A May 8 hearing in U.S. bankruptcy court in Manhattan has been scheduled to determine if more funds should be released.

Judge Howard C. Buschman approved the initial $25 million after Ames disclosed in court that it expects to collect $268.4 million, including sales receipts, through May 19, but will need $314 million to buy goods and operate the stores in that period.

Some of the merchandise will be purchased to make good on rainchecks issued last week after Ames shoppers discovered that the stores lacked many of the toys, housewares, toiletry and clothing items featured in its April 29 circular. Ames subsequently took out ads in local newspapers apologizing for the missing goods.

Ames filed for protection from its creditors under Chapter 11 of the U.S. Bankruptcy Code last week, after negotiations with its lenders stalled and many of its suppliers stopped shipments. The filing followed an announcement that the retailer expects to post a $228 million loss for the year ended Jan. 27. The loss includes a charge of $150 million taken to close 74 of its unprofitable stores. All but two are part the 389 Zayre store chain Ames acquired for $778 million in November 1988.

In trading on the New York Stock Exchange, Ames closed at $2.875 a share, up 37.5 cents.

Separately, a Chicago investor filed a lawsuit against Ames directors, alleging that the company issued misleading statements regarding its financial condition.

In the suit, filed in federal court in Bridgeport, Conn., debenture holder Avers Wexler charged that Ames directors permitted the firm to sell about $155 million in debentures without fully disclosing the extent of its financial difficulties.

The suit seeks class action status and was filed on behalf of investors who bought Ames's 7.5% convertible subordinated debentures from Oct. 5, 1989, to April 9, 1990, the plaintiff's attorney, Michael J. Freed, said.

Ames officials didn't return phone calls seeking comment.

A preliminary meeting between company representatives, bondholders and trade creditors has been scheduled for May 10 in New York.
