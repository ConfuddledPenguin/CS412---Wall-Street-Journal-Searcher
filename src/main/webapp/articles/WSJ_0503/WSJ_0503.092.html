Technology: Interneuron, NutraSweet To Explore Salt Substitute

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/03/90

LEXINGTON, Mass. -- Interneuron Pharmaceuticals Inc., a small research and development company, said it reached an agreement with Monsanto Co.'s NutraSweet Co. involving research on a salt substitute. Although sugar substitutes have been available for years, and fat substitutes have begun to come on the market, researchers say that salt substitutes have thus far failed to satisfy the human craving for salt. The simple molecular structure of salt makes it difficult to alter chemically.

Charles J. Casamento, president and chief executive officer of Interneuron, said the company will use the NutraSweet funding to pay for research "to look at compounds that will satisfy salt cravings in animals." If it finds such compounds, the company would sign a more extensive research agreement. "We believe the technology is there that will let us come up with something," he added.

Under the agreement, NutraSweet, a maker of sugar substitutes, gets a nine-month option to license from Interneuron the right to develop and market any salt substitute or salt enhancer developed. Terms weren't disclosed.

NutraSweet, based in Deerfield, Ill., said that Interneuron's work is at the "very early stages."
