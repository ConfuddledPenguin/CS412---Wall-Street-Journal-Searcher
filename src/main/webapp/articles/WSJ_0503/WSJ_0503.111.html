International Brief -- Johnson Matthey PLC: Big Cost-Cutting Program Is Planned by British Firm

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/03/90

Johnson Matthey PLC, a major British precious metals and metal-goods manufacturer, said it intends to undergo a major cost-cutting program that will lead to "substantial" charges against earnings for the year ended March 31. The move was triggered by a need to bring production and overhead costs back into line after a period in which they were growing faster than the group's overall gross profit margin.

David Davies, who was named chairman of the company in January following a board room shake-up, said the measures would include a reduction in the company's business units, currently totaling 77; a more decentralized management, and the rationalization of U.K. precious metal refining operations.

"Investment will increasingly be focused on those businesses with potential for good long-term growth," Mr. Davies said.
