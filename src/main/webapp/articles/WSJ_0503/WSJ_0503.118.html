International Brief -- German Wings: One-Year-Old Airline Files For Bankruptcy Proceedings

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/03/90

German Wings Luftfahrtunternehmen G.m.b.H. filed for bankruptcy proceedings and said it has about a "10% chance" of being saved. A spokesman said that in the past week the company "had a seat-load factor of 60%." German Wings, which struggled with a load factor of about 45% for most of its maiden year, has said it needed a load factor of about 55% to break even.

The small, one-year-old airline halted all flights late Monday.

The airline said negotiations with a "large European airline" collapsed at the last minute. German Wings said contracts were ready to be signed with the partner, believed to be British Airways. Reports in the German media have said that British Airways was about to take a 49% stake in German Wings for 70 million Deutsche marks ($41.7 million).

German Wings is owned by Peter and Christian Kimmel, who have a 40% stake, publishing group F&F Burda Gesellschaft fuer Beteiligungen G.m.b.H., with 45%, and Bayerische Unternehmens-Beteiligungsgesellschaft AG, a venture capital company controlled by several Bavarian banks, which holds 15%.
