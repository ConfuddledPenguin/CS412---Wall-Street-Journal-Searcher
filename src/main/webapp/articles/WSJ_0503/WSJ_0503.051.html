Three Broker Firms Fail in Dismissal Bid In West Virginia Suit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/03/90

NEW YORK -- A state court judge denied a motion by three investment firms to dismiss a lawsuit filed by West Virginia, accusing them of partial responsibility for losses on the state's investments. In October, the state filed suit in West Virginia, accusing five brokerage firms with responsibility for much of large losses arising in West Virginia's consolidated investment pool. The firms, Morgan Stanley Group Inc.'s Morgan Stanley & Co. unit, Goldman, Sachs & Co., Greenwich Capital Markets, and subsidiaries of Salomon Inc. and National Westminster Bank PLC, processed investments for the state.

Greenwich and the NatWest subsidiary since have settled with the state. Salomon Brothers Inc., Morgan Stanley and Goldman asked a judge in state Supreme Court, a trial court, in Manhattan to declare that the firms hadn't any liability in the case or to declare that the case should be heard here.

In an opinion, Justice Harold Baer here declined to absolve the firms and he said the case should be heard in West Virginia. The U.S. District Court in Charleston, W.Va., now is weighing whether it or a West Virginia state court should hear the case.

Morgan Stanley and Goldman Sachs declined to comment. A spokesman for Salomon couldn't be reached for comment.
