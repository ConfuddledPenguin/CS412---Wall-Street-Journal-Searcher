World Bank Loans to Poland

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/03/90

WASHINGTON -- The World Bank approved two loans totaling $153 million to Poland for transport-sector projects. World Bank officials said a $145 million credit will help finance equipment needed to modernize the country's state-run railway system. A second loan, for $8 million, will be used to improve Poland's administration of highways, rail lines and other transport facilities.

Both loans will be at variable lending rates, currently 7.75% a year.
