Business Brief -- Derlan Industries Ltd.: Firm Expects to Post Gain For 2nd Period From Sale

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/03/90

Derlan Industries Ltd., Toronto, said it expects to report a second-quarter pretax gain of about nine million Canadian dollars (US$7.7 million) from the sale of a subsidiary. The diversified manufacturing concern reported net income of C$3.5 million for the year-ago quarter.

Derlan said the gain would result from the recently completed sale of its Carte International Ltd. unit to Federal Industries Ltd., Winnipeg, Manitoba, for about C$22 million. Carte makes transformers. Federal Industries is a retailing, transportation and metals distribution concern.

Derlan said first-quarter net income fell to C$1.7 million, or 11 Canadian cents (9.4 U.S. cents) a share, from C$5.8 million, or 34 Canadian cents, a year earlier, when earnings included a one-time gain of C$1.3 million, or 7 Canadian cents. Sales rose to C$102.3 million from C$101.9 million.

In trading on the Toronto Stock Exchange yesterday, Derlan closed at C$7.25, down 25 Canadian cents.
