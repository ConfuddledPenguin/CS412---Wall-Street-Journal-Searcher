Business Brief -- Highland Superstores Inc.: Electronics Retailer Takes Charge Against 4th Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/03/90

Highland Superstores Inc. took an $8.9 million, or 49-cent-a-share charge against fourth-quarter earnings to close stores and consolidate operations. The restructuring charge resulted in a fourth-quarter loss of $2.8 million, or 15 cents a share, compared with net income of $4.3 million, or 24 cents a share, during the year-ago quarter. Revenue for the quarter ended Jan. 31 rose less than 1%, to $316.7 million.

The Plymouth, Mich.-based electronics and appliance retailer said it plans to pull out of Shreveport, La., and Milwaukee and suspend previous plans to open stores in Rochester, N.Y., and Albany, N.Y. It also plans to modify warehousing and distribution operations.

The company said it plans to reduce inventories and cut staffing by 180 jobs in the areas where it is closing stores or consolidating operations. Highland said the restructuring plan was adopted to "maximize the company's long-term operating results."

For the fiscal year ended Jan. 31, Highland reported a loss of $12.4 million, or 68 cents a share, compared with net income of $7.3 million, or 40 cents a share, a year ago. Revenue rose 15% to $925 million.
