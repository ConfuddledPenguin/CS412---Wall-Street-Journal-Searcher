Canadian Pacific Net Fell 43% in 1st Period; Rights Plan Is Cleared

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/03/90

MONTREAL -- Canadian Pacific Ltd. said first-quarter net income fell 43%. The company also said holders approved a shareholder rights plan and the distribution of an 80% interest in the company's real estate subsidiary.

Separately, James F. Hankinson, 46 years old, was named president and chief operating officer and elected to the board of this transportation, resources, telecommunications and real estate concern. He was executive vice president. The chief operating officer post is new. As president, Mr. Hankinson succeeds William W. Stinson, 56, who remains chairman and chief executive. Also elected to the board was consultant Dian Cohen, 57. The newly elected directors fill vacancies.

Net fell to 76.2 million Canadian dollars (US$65.4 million), or 24 Canadian cents (21 U.S. cents) a share, from C$134.1 million, or 42 Canadian cents a share, a year earlier. Year-earlier figures include profit from discontinued operations of C$2.7 million, or one Canadian cent a share. Revenue fell to C$2.59 billion from C$2.62 billion.

Mr. Stinson told the annual meeting that first-quarter results "are far from satisfactory," adding that "earnings this year will be significantly below those of 1989." He didn't elaborate.

Holders approved a shareholder rights plan, with 58% of votes cast in favor of the plan. In a separate ballot, holders approved the distribution of 80% of Canadian Pacific's interest in its Marathon Realty Co. subsidiary, with 90.2% of votes cast in favor of the proposal. Shares in Marathon, a Toronto-based real estate concern, will be distributed to holders on the basis of one Marathon share for every four Canadian Pacific shares held. Canadian Pacific retains a 20% interest in Marathon, which will be listed on the Toronto and Montreal stock exchanges.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Canadian Pacific rose 12.5 cents a share to $18.
