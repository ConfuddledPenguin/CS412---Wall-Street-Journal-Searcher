Republic Health Leaves Chapter 11 As Public Company

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/03/90

DALLAS -- Republic Health Corp. said it completed reorganization under Chapter 11 of the U.S. Bankruptcy Code and emerged as a publicly traded company. The owner of 14 acute-care hospitals and two psychiatric hospitals went private in August 1986 in a $406.2 million leveraged buy-out led by Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette Securities Corp. But changes in Medicare regulations and overly optimistic projections made the debt burden too heavy. The company and its parent, REPH Acquisition Co., sought bankruptcy-court protection last December to restructure total debt of about $645 million.

Under the reorganization plan, which was approved by the majority of the company's creditors before the company filed for Chapter 11 protection, Republic Health and its parent reduced total debt to $379 million and reduced their annual interest obligations to $40 million, from $89 million.

Of the remaining debt, $74 million is owed banks and $86 million is owed under capitalized leases and notes. The remainder consists of new securities issued to creditors, the company said.

As part of the reorganization, REPH Acquisition was merged into the now-publicly traded Republic Health. Debtholders own 88.8% of the new stock; holders of the old preferred stock own 6.1%; and holders of the old common stock own 5.1%.

Under the reorganization plan, the company said, secured debtholders received 81 cents for each dollar owed; senior subordinated debtholders received 67 cents; and subordinated debtholders received 20 cents.
