Eastman Kodak Earnings Fell 6% In First Quarter

  • Author: James S. Hirsch
  • Date: 05/03/90

Eastman Kodak Co., showing the benefits of extensive cost cutting, recorded a 6% drop in first-quarter net income, better than analysts expected. Net fell to $199 million, or 61 cents a share, from $212 million, or 65 cents a share, a year earlier. Sales fell to $4.11 billion from $4.21 billion.

The Rochester, N.Y.-based photographic, chemical and pharmaceutical concern said it restated 1989 results to reflect the company's conversion to a calendar year. Kodak's fiscal year began Dec. 26, and its quarters weren't uniform. The change increased 1989 first-quarter net three cents a share.

In Kodak's core photographic products division, domestic sales fell 15%, but lower costs and higher film prices helped boost operating earnings 50% to $219 million. Colby H. Chandler, chairman and chief executive officer, who retires this month, cautioned that the rate of earnings increase in this division will moderate in the coming months.

Mr. Chandler said, "Barring unexpected developments in the economy, we believe the recovery forecast for Kodak will come about and that this will become increasingly clear in subsequent quarters."

Kodak's results exceeded most analysts' expectations of 50 to 55 cents a share, but analysts said it's uncertain how much the improvement in photographic products comes from one-time cost-cutting benefits derived from a restructuring program announced last year, as opposed to fundamental improvements in operations.

Yet, despite the better-than-expected results, some analysts expressed disappointment with the 3.5% decline in operating profit, as earnings fell in Kodak's information-system, chemical and health divisions.

"Even though the company's on track in terms of Wall Street's earnings expectations, how can you be excited if chemical profits are off 38% and information systems is off 20%?" asked David Nelson, an analyst at Shearson Lehman Hutton. "That's not the stuff you write home about."
