Northeast Savings Posts 4th-Period Loss On Big Write-Down

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/03/90

HARTFORD, Conn. -- Northeast Savings F.A. reported a net loss of $106.9 million, or $19.07 a share, for the fourth quarter ended March 31 because of a write-down of good will but said operations are showing "solid progress." A year earlier, it reported net income of $2.1 million, or a net loss of four cents a share, after payment of preferred dividends.

Northeast said that before the $110.3 million write-down of good will, net was $3.4 million, or 22 cents a share.

George P. Rutland, chairman and president, said the good will represented the value of having a unique two-state thrift business in Connecticut and Massachusetts at a time when interstate banking was restricted in the region. Due to the passage of liberalized interstate banking laws, "that franchise doesn't have the value it used to have," he said.

After the write-down, Northeast still has $90 million in good will on its books. It previously filed a suit challenging the Federal Office of Thrift Supervision's requirement that good will can't be counted toward meeting regulatory requirements.

Mr. Rutland said Northeast has been successfully reducing its size. At the end of fiscal 1990, assets were $4.97 billion, down 37% from $7.94 billion a year earlier. While retail deposits are down only 3% to $3.4 million, brokered deposits are down 69% to $304.4 million.

Mr. Rutland said that over the past year Northeast sold off all but $16.1 million of its $314.1 million junk-bond portfolio, taking a $13 million loss.

For the year, Northeast reported a loss of $107.1 million, or $20.27 a share, after writing down the good will. In fiscal 1989, it had a loss of $12.9 million, or $4.40 a share.
