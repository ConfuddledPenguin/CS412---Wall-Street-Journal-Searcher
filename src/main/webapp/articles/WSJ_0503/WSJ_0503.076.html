Bushes to Pay More Taxes Than Expected for 1989

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/03/90

WASHINGTON -- President and Mrs. Bush paid an additional $7,497 in federal taxes after coming across an additional $26,250 in 1989 income they hadn't reported, the White House said. The additional payment brought the Bushes' total federal tax bill to $108,879, on total income of $483,030, according to amended tax forms released by the White House.

White House spokesman Marlin Fitzwater said the couple's federal tax return had to be amended to reflect deferred income, in the form of a paid-up life-insurance policy from Zapata Off Shore Corp., an oil exploration company Mr. Bush headed until 1966.

Mr. Bush became entitled to the policy when he turned 65 years old last year. But the president didn't receive a form notifying him of the income, and his tax preparer, whose firm manages Mr. Bush's blind trust, didn't become aware of the transaction until April 19. The Bushes' original tax return was filed April 13.

The discovery caused the couple to lose $525 in itemized deductions and to pay $40.97 in interest on the extra payment. But the Bushes shouldn't have to pay a penalty, Mr. Fitzwater said, because the omission was unintentional, due to a "reasonable cause," and corrected in a timely fashion.
