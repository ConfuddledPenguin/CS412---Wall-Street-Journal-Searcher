International Brief -- Bayerische Motoren Werke AG: German Auto Maker's Net Jumped 23% During 1989

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/03/90

Bayerische Motoren Werke AG, citing high customer demand for its entire model range, said group net income in 1989 soared 23% as sales gained 8.4%. The West German luxury car maker said profit gained to 558.1 million marks ($332.2 million) from 454.9 million marks a year ago, as sales grew to 26.52 billion marks from 24.47 billion marks.

BMW said it produced 511,476 passenger cars in 1989, up 5.7% from 484,121 cars in 1988. New car registrations in the U.S., however, fell 12% to 64,900 from 73,400.

This is the first time the company has reported full consolidated world-wide annual group financial data.

Separately, BMW said group sales rose 4.1% to 9.24 billion marks in the first four months of 1990 from 8.87 billion marks a year earlier.

BMW said group car output rose 5.5% to 191,749 from 181,739 in the period.
