Federal Industries Expects Net to Fall In 1990 as Much as 20%

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/03/90

WINNIPEG, Manitoba -- John Fraser, president and chief executive officer of Federal Industries Ltd., said the industrial holding company expects a 15% to 20% decline in net income this year as a result of the "deteriorating economic outlook" in Canada. In 1989, the company had net income of 45.6 million Canadian dollars (US$39.1 million) or C$1.66 a share, on revenue of C$2.28 billion. Mr. Fraser had said earlier this year that the company expected 1990 net to be about the same level.

"The outlook now is worse than it was three, four, six months ago," Mr. Fraser said. "{Interest} rates have stayed surprisingly high; the {strong} Canadian dollar seems to defy gravity; and we have a serious fiscal situation with the deficit."

He said the Canadian manufacturing sector is already in a recession.

Results for the first quarter of the year, due to be released Wednesday, will be "quite dramatically" lower than the year-earlier C$8.9 million, or 31 Canadian cents a share, Mr. Fraser said. He declined to give a specific estimate.

"The first quarter is always our weakest quarter," he said. "Nearly all of our businesses have a seasonal component to them."

The company has four operating divisions: consumer products, metals, industrial products and transportation.

Mr. Fraser said the company's newly acquired bookstore chain, W.H. Smith, had been expected to report a first-quarter operating loss. In addition, the economic slowdown in Ontario and Quebec hurt Federal's trucking operations in those provinces. Earnings at the metals group also declined in the first quarter.

But Mr. Fraser noted that the company's first-quarter earnings drop shouldn't be taken as an indication of the size of the decline it expects for the year as a whole.

Federal Industries generates about 80% of its approximately C$2.2 billion in annual sales from its steel service and distribution centers as well as its trucking division.
