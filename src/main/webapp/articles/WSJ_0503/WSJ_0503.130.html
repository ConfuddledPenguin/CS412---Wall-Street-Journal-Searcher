Former assistant deputy secretary of state Charles H. Fairbanks Jr. writing in the spring issue of the National Interest:

From the communist point of view, Gorbachev is a jinx, a hoodoo, a Jonah. His arrival everywhere presages disaster. Like Jaruzelski and Poszgay, he did not understand the people's deep weariness of communism, nor could he sense the people's mood at a particular crux. Perhaps Gorbachev's soaring ambition, his splendid vision of the future, and his courage are spoiled by his lifetime as a member of an isolated priestly caste, whose pride was its ability to manipulate people.
