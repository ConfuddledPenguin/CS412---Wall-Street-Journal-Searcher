What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/19/90

EASTERN AIRLINES was turned over to a trustee by a bankruptcy judge, who took control of the financially battered carrier away from Texas Air and Chairman Frank Lorenzo. Named as trustee was Martin R. Shugrue, who was ousted last year as president of Continental Airlines, Texas Air's other airline unit. Unsecured creditors sought a trustee because they blamed management for Eastern's ever-growing losses. ---

Treasury bonds sank for the second day in a row, sending stock prices and the dollar lower. The benchmark 30-year Treasury issue fell 1 1/8 points, pushing the yield up to 8.83%, the highest in nearly a year. The Dow Jones industrials slid 32.89, to 2732.88.

Oil prices skidded as much as 70 cents a barrel on world markets, reflecting disappointment that a meeting of three key OPEC members failed to result in any action.

---

The U.S. trade deficit shrank sharply in February, to $6.49 billion, the smallest gap in over six years. Imports slid 7.6%, led by a plunge in oil buying. Analysts said the improvement reflected the weak economy.

---

Brazil plans to resume negotiations on its $115 billion foreign debt and to begin efforts to sell off state companies. The actions reflect the government's belief that its new anti-inflation program is working.

---

AT&T's profit rose 12% in the first quarter, buoyed by continued strength in its long-distance business and slimmer costs. Still, earnings were slightly below expectations.

---

USX may not get anywhere near the $1.5 billion it hoped to raise by selling oil and gas properties. Such a development could fuel Carl Icahn's campaign against USX management.

---

Ford is negotiating to buy the auto finance operations of Marine Midland Banks, which in the 1980s led an unsuccessful rush by commercial banks into the car-loan business.

---

Dayton Hudson is said to be the front-runner to acquire Marshall Field's. The department-store chain is being sold by B.A.T to help fend off a takeover by Sir James Goldsmith.

---

A bill to help discount retailers sue competitors and manufacturers that conspire to fix prices was approved by the House. The legislation has prompted a Bush veto threat.

---

Kidder Peabody isn't liable for damages in an alleged insider-trading case relating to Maxus Energy's 1983 takeover of Natomas, a judge ruled. The case involved Martin Siegel, who worked for Kidder, and Ivan Boesky.

---

Apple Computer's profit jumped 134% in the quarter due to improved profit margins, brisk new-product sales and strong foreign sales.

---

Merrill Lynch's profit rose 11% in the first quarter, while Shearson had a $915 million loss. Both were hurt by the slump in investment banking.

---

Coca-Cola's earnings climbed 9.1% in the first quarter, fueled by a strong 9% growth in international volume for its soft-drink products.

---

A fiscal 1991 budget plan was unveiled by House Budget Chairman Panetta, but Democrats were divided over cuts in military spending.

---

G. Heileman Brewing is negotiating to restructure its huge bank debt, the result of its $1.3 billion buy-out in 1987 by Australian Alan Bond.

---

Occidental Petroleum's profit surged 61% in the quarter, boosted by oil and natural-gas operations.
