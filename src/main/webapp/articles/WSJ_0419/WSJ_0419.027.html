Philip Morris Profit Rose 31% In First Quarter

  • Author: Alix M. Freedman
  • Date: 04/19/90

Philip Morris Cos. earnings rose 31% in the first quarter to $775 million, or 84 cents a share, powered by strong gains in its core food, beer, and tobacco businesses. In the year-ago quarter, the nation's largest packaged-goods maker earned $590 million, or 64 cents a share. Revenue rose 7.3% to $11.38 billion from $10.6 billion.

Analysts reacted favorably to the higher-than-expected earnings, which were fueled by surprisingly robust results at Philip Morris's domestic tobacco and Miller Brewing operations. Many analysts had forecast earnings of around 78 cents a share for the quarter.

"You become numb to Philip Morris's strong earnings gains, but this was awesome," said Emanuel Goldman, an analyst at PaineWebber Inc. "This company wants cash flow to facilitate their acquisition efforts."

Added Marc Cohen of Sanford C. Bernstein & Co.: "People who were doubtful about this company's ability to continue to grow given the industries in which it operates, as well as its size, have reason to rethink their postitions."

In the first quarter, Philip Morris's operating profit rose 18% to $1.9 billion from $1.6 billion. Although the company doesn't break out operating profit by division, Mr. Cohen of Sanford Bernstein estimated that operating profit from the company's U.S. tobacco business rose 18% to about $815 million. At a time when cigarette consumption is declining about 3%, Philip Morris's unit volume grew about 0.4%. Overseas, the No. 1 tobacco maker's unit sales rose 8%.

At Miller Brewing, unit sales grew a buoyant 4%, thanks in large part to the company's recently introduced Genuine Draft and further gains by Milwaukee's Best, a popular-priced beer.

Operating profit at Kraft General Foods rose an estimated 15% to about $600 million, mostly due to cost savings in the aftermath of Kraft's 1988 merger with Philip Morris. But volume at the nation's largest food company was up only 2%.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading, Philip Morris closed at $42.625, down 37.5 cents, on volume of more than 2.3 million shares.
