Business Brief -- SEEQ Technology Inc.: Loss of $4.5 Million Posted For Fiscal Second Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/19/90

SEEQ Technology Inc., San Jose, Calif., said slumping prices for its basic line of erasable programmable chips resulted in a loss of $4.5 million, or 35 cents a share, in its second quarter ended March 31. In the year-earlier quarter, the computer chip maker posted a profit of $722,000, or six cents a share. Revenue for the latest period slumped 25% to $11.5 million from $15.5 million a year earlier.

SEEQ said the declines reflect "continued competitive pricing pressure" on its Eeproms, or electrically erasable programmable read-only memories. These are specialized chips -- with military, telecommunications and industrial applicationsthat can be erased and reprogrammed for varied uses. Despite the poor quarterly showing, SEEQ said it was "encouraged" by a substantial increase in Eeprom orders in the latter part of the quarter.

For the six months, SEEQ posted a loss of $5.6 million, or 43 cents a share, compared with net of $1.8 million, or 14 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue fell 20% to $23.8 million from $30 million.
