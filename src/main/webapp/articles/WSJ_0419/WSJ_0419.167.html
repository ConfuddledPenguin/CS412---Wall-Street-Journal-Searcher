GF Corp., Unit Seek Creditor Protection Of Bankruptcy Code

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/19/90

YOUNGSTOWN, Ohio -- GF Corp. said it and its wholly owned GF Furniture Systems Inc. subsidiary have filed for protection from creditors under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code. The maker of office furniture also said its furniture-systems unit has agreed in principle to sell substantially all of its assets, including manufacturing facilities, trademarks and patents, to a private holding company. The holding company will be formed by Cyrus Tang, the owner of Tang Industries of Chicago, a $700 million steel-service concern.

GF has had trouble obtaining sufficient financing for general working capital and to cover the closing costs of its 88-year-old Youngstown manufacturing plant. That plant had been posting losses of between $9 million and $10 million a year, because it wasn't producing enough to cover operating costs.

In its filing, the company listed total assets of $44 million and total liabilities of $57 million, excluding certain health care and lease liabilities, which are listed off the company's balance sheet.

GF said the agreement to sell the furniture-systems assets calls for the Tang group to provide new capital and pay off about $22 million of bank debt, and make additional payments to GF Furniture Systems during the next six years based on earnings from furniture operations. The agreement doesn't cover the unit's trade debt, liability for shutdown costs, or health and other labor contract obligations.

GF said its directors have approved the immediate conversion of GF's Class B common shares outstanding into an equal number of common shares. The company said the Class B shares had superior voting power; otherwise, they were identical to regular common.
