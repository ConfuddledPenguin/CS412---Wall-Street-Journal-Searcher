Business Brief -- Overmyer Corp.: Firm, Two Subsidiaries File For Chapter 11 Protection

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/19/90

Overmyer Corp. said it filed for Chapter 11 protection under the U.S. Bankruptcy Code for itself and its two subsidiaries, Aquality Inc. and Premier Pump & Pool Products Inc. The Winchester, Ind., maker of glass and swimming pool equipment said the filings were brought about by difficulties in negotiating a continuation of adequate credit lines with its principal lender that would enable the company to conduct normal business operations.

In addition, Overmyer cited a combination of business factors underlying the situation, including losses at Premier Pump, an extremely weak swimming pool market and a "precipitous downturn in the commercial market for glass container mould equipment." For the third quarter ended Oct. 1, 1989, Overmyer had a loss of $39,632, or four cents a share, on sales of $7.8 million.

Overmyer said that at the time of the filings, adequate cash reserves existed and the process of securing "restructured, post-petition financing" was under way.
