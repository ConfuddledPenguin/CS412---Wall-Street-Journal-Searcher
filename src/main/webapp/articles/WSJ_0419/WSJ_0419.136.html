Apple's Profit Spurted by 134% In 2nd Quarter --- Revenue Advanced by 8%; Computer Concern Cites Improved Margins, Sales

  • Author: Ken Wells
  • Date: 04/19/90

CUPERTINO, Calif. -- Apple Computer Inc. said fiscal second-quarter profit surged 134% as a result of improved gross margins, brisk sales of new products and robust foreign sales. The company posted net income of $131.8 million, or $1.04 a share, for the quarter ended March 31, up from $56.4 million, or 44 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue climbed 8% to $1.35 billion from $1.25 billion.

For the six months, Apple said net spurted 30% to $256.7 million, or $2 a share, from $196.8 million, or $1.54 a share, the year earlier. Revenue rose 7% to $2.84 billion from $2.65 billion.

Analysts said they were expecting a strong quarter from Apple but that the results exceeded even those expectations. The key appears to have been Apple's ability to wrench its profit margins up to a "spectacular 55%, which has to be a record or a near record," said Steven Ossad , a computer analyst with Montgomery Securities in San Francisco.

The good news, however, was tempered by Apple's forecast, delivered to analysts during a conference call on Wednesday, that its profit margins would fall from those elevated levels in the second half of fiscal 1990.

The company also told analysts that it expects combined second half domestic and international growth rates to be a modest 7% to 10%. Indeed, despite the second-quarter showing, Apple's stock closed unchanged at $43.25 a share yesterday on volume of 1.7 million shares in national over-the-counter trading.

Apple said its results were greatly aided by record international sales, which totaled 47% of all sales for the quarter, which also set a record. Apple attributed the increase to aggressive overseas marketing efforts coupled with "the relatively higher growth rates the industry is experiencing in markets outside the United States."

Indeed, Apple said that while it remains "cautious" about growth in the U.S. computer industry in coming quarters, "we are optimistic regarding the level of international demand."

Apple said profit also received a boost from improved margins on its computer products, stemming from lower parts costs and a more effective program of managing operating expenses.

The company added that results were aided by brisk sales of upgraded models of its mid- and high-end Macintosh computer introduced last year. The machines, notably the Macintosh FE 30, 2ci and 2cx, retail between about $3,800 to $8,600 depending upon the configuration.
