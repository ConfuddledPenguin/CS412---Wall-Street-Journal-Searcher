From a March 13 speech to the Cato Institute in Washington by Carl Hagen, leader of the Norwegian Progress Party:

As communism is being destroyed in Eastern Europe, social democrats all over the world are urging the new regimes to adopt a Scandinavian welfare-state model, and not a pure free-market system. Having lived most of my life in this utopia, "the most perfect of all systems," as our domestic social democrats love to describe it, I take a somewhat different position. . . . In our countries people are growing disillusioned, and no longer believe the welfare state can deliver what it promised. At the same time, the moral hazards of the system are beginning to show. The Scandinavian welfare state has little to offer either East or West. Do not adopt a model we are moving away from.

From a posthumous book by former university professor W.W. Bartley III, "Unfathomed Knowledge, Unmeasured Wealth," to be published by Open Court Press this year:

For certain kinds of groups, universities are handy places in which to have a strong redoubt. They are handy for groups that are not competitive, that are peddling ideas for which there is little demand, ideas that do not work, that fail to explain, and whose proponents are consciously or unconsciously tempted to turn them into ideologies in order to perpetuate themselves or further the groups to which they belong.
