Quantum Chemical And Ethyl Net Fell; Rohm Profit Rose

  • Author: Randolph B. Smith
  • Date: 04/19/90

The chemical industry continues to be battered by generally weak commodity prices, but other factors contributed to widely varying first-quarter results at three companies. Rohm & Haas Co. reported that net income increased 2%, which was significantly better than expected. The gain came on increased plant operating efficiencies and lower propylene costs.

Quantum Chemical Corp.'s earnings, which dropped 92%, were below expectations as a result of weak commodity prices, a plant shutdown and reduced propane sales caused by warm weather.

Ethyl Corp.'s profit, which dropped 7%, was consistent with expectations, with higher insurance segment income making up for lower chemical profits.

Philadelphia-based Rohm & Haas said first-quarter results reflected modest sales increases, smoother plant operations and a favorable litigation settlement with Rhone-Poulenc S.A. Sales increased 2.2% to $694.4 million from $679.5 million. Analyst James Wilbur of Smith Barney, Harris Upham & Co. said Rohm & Haas earnings were "significantly better" than an expected 65 cents a share, reflecting successful efforts to reduce manufacturing costs. William R. Young of Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette Securities Corp. said he will probably increase by 20 cents a share his 1990 earnings estimate of $2.40 a share.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Rohm & Haas shares closed at $33.75, down 37.5 cents.

Quantum Chemical, based in New York, said first-quarter results were hurt by lower selling prices for polyethylene and polypropylene and by lower propane sales volume. Costs exceeding insurance recoveries for a fire-related plant shutdown reduced earnings by $15 million, or 59 cents a share. Sales dropped 16% to $682.8 million from $814.1 million.

Analyst George Krug of Oppenheimer & Co. said Quantum earnings were "somewhat below" the expected range of 30 cents to 80 cents a share. Charles LoCastro of Donaldson Lufkin said he is considering lowering his current 1990 profit estimate of $2.25 a share because second and third-quarter earnings are likely to be lower than first-quarter net.

Quantum shares closed at $22.25, down 62.5 cents, in Big Board trading.

Ethyl, of Richmond, Va., said income from continuing operations increased 2.6%, reflecting a 24% increase in insurance segment operating profit. Chemical earnings declined 6% as a result of sharply reduced shipments of anti-knock chemicals. Sales were up 6.9% to $609.7 million from $570.5 million. Analysts said earnings were consistent with estimates in the range of 44 to 45 cents a share.

Shares of Ethyl closed on the Big Board at $24.75, down 25 cents.
