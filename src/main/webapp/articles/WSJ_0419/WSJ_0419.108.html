CMS Energy's Profit Slipped 2.8% in Quarter On 5.7% Revenue Drop

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/19/90

JACKSON, Mich. -- CMS Energy Corp. said first-quarter earnings fell 2.8% to $103.3 million, or $1.27 a share, from $106.3 million, or $1.29 a share, a year earlier. Revenue fell 5.7% to $870.1 million from $922.6 million. The Jackson, Mich., utility concern said the earnings decline resulted primarily from lower electric and natural gas sales because of unusually warm winter weather. The first quarter of 1990 was the warmest in lower Michigan in 69 years, the company said.

CMS said the warm weather reduced net income by $7.3 million, or nine cents a share. This was partially offset by benefits from the company's stock buy-back program. CMS said it continues to buy back stock under a previously announced program.

William T. McCormick, Jr., CMS chairman, said the utility company is sticking with a forecast calling for 1990 earnings to outstrip the $3.80 a share achieved in 1989. But 1990 net could be lower in the event of possible non-cash, one-time write-offs associated with regulatory matters arising from CMS's Midland ventures.

In 1984 CMS abandoned a nuclear plant project in Midland, Mich., after spending $4.1 billion on construction. But CMS converted the failed nuclear plant to a gas-fired cogeneration facility, which recently began commercial operation. CMS has a 49% interest in the cogeneration facility.

CMS stock rose 75 cents yesterday to close at $30 in New York Stock Exchange composite trading.
