Canadian Trade Surplus

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/19/90

OTTAWA -- Canada's trade surplus narrowed to 264.2 million Canadian dollars (US$226.8 million) in February from C$669.4 million (US$574.7 million) in January, partly as a result of a rise in imports from the U.S., Statistics Canada, a federal agency, said. Canadian imports from the U.S. rose 4.7% to C$7.81 billion, or 68.2% of total Canadian imports of C$11.44 billion. Canadian exports to the U.S. declined 0.1% to C$8.63 billion, or 73.7% of total Canadian exports of C$11.71 billion.

Auto parts showed the strongest import growth in February, rising C$405.8 million from January as a result of a pickup in Canadian auto production.
