Chase Manhattan To Take Deposits Of Seamen's Bank

  • Author: Robert Guenther
  • Date: 04/19/90

NEW YORK -- Federal regulators closed Seamen's Corp.'s Seamen's Bank for Savings, one of New York's oldest thrifts, and approved the transfer of 13 branches and $2.1 billion in deposits to Chase Manhattan Corp., effective today. Chase, which will operate the branches as part of its Chase Manhattan Bank, agreed to pay the Federal Deposit Insurance Corp. $5 million for 161-year-old Seamen's deposits, which represent 169,800 accounts.

As part of the transaction, the FDIC has agreed to offer a $2.1 billion short-term note to Chase, repay secured borrowings of $450 million and retain assets of the failed bank, chiefly mortgage-backed securities, totaling $2.4 billion.

Late last year, the American Stock Exchange delisted Seamen's, after the thrift reported a negative net worth of $66 million. For its last publicly reported period, the nine months ended Sept. 30, 1989, Seamen's reported a loss of $168 million, or $7.18 a share.

For 1988, Seamen's reported earnings of $16.5 million, or $1.02 a share, down 10% from $18.3 million, or $1.10 a share, in 1987.

In 1984, a group of Texas investors injected $75 million of capital into the troubled mutual savings bank and converted it to public ownership in 1985. The three individuals -- Robert T. Enloe, Robert K. Utley and Jess Hay -- together controlled 69% of the company's stock.

In 1985, Seamen's shifted its strategy from the traditional thrift role of originating residential mortgages to one of investing in mortgage-backed securities, building up a huge $3.5 billion portfolio. As part of that strategy, Seamen's placed complicated hedges using mortgage-related derivative securities on the portfolio to protect its income from rising interest rates.

However, those hedges relied on short-term interest rates keeping their historical relationship to long-term rates. That relationship changed, and Seamen's found itself paying consumers more for their deposits.

In composite trading yesterday on the New York Stock Exchange, Chase Manhattan shares closed at $27.25, down 50 cents.
