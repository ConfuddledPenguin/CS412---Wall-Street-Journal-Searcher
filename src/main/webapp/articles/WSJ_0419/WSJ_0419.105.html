EPA Plans to Reduce Indoor Air Pollution Caused by New Carpets

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/19/90

WASHINGTON -- The Environmental Protection Agency announced that it plans to reduce indoor air pollution caused by chemical emissions from new carpets and during carpet installation. This announcement follows a Jan. 11, 1990, petition filed with the agency by the National Federation of Federal Employees. The petition charges that the health of agency workers and union members has been hurt by carpet emissions at the EPA's headquarters in Washington.

The agency has asked carpet companies to voluntarily test all of their products for total chemical emissions and to provide the public with comparative information on emissions. The carpet industry has already promised to conduct voluntary testing to identify specific chemicals emitted by new carpeting.

There is no scientific evidence on the health effects of carpet emissions, the EPA said, although numerous chemicals are emitted by carpets. The union identified 4-phenylcyclohexene as a cause of respiratory and other illnesses, due to fumes from carpets installed in a poorly ventilated area of the office.

Conclusive evidence on the health hazards posed by carpets won't be available soon, and the agency said it will continue expanding its research into the health effects of complex chemicals from all indoor sources.
