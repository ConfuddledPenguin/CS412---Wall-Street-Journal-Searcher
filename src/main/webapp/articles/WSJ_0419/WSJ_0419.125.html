Business Brief -- Hytek Microsystems Inc.: Auditors Question Firm's Future as `Going Concern'

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/19/90

Hytek Microsystems Inc. said its auditors expressed reservations about the company's survival as a "going concern." Separately, the maker of hybrid microcircuits said it reached an agreement to sell the assets of its commercial hybrid circuit-making operations at Los Gatos, Calif., and to consolidate its remaining company functions at its Carson City, Calif., military site. It declined to disclose terms. About 90 plant workers will be affected by the sale: Some of those workers may be re-employed at the Carson City plant, Hytek said. The company currently has about 145 employees.

Hytek said the consolidation will free the company to concentrate on its core business of manufacturing specialty electronic circuits for military applications. The company said that despite its auditor's opinion, the consolidation and sale of assets should "provide the basis for a viable entity."

For the year ended Dec. 30, 1989, Hytek posted a net loss of $5.4 million, or $1.93 a share, on revenue of $9.5 million. In the year-earlier period, the company had a profit of $518,000, or 18 cents a share, on revenue of $18.5 million.
