Business Brief -- Maxus Energy Corp.: Firm Files Common Shares As Part of Prudential Plan

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/19/90

Maxus Energy Corp. said it registered with the Securities and Exchange Commission to offer nine million common shares as part of a plan to restructure preferred stock held by Prudential Insurance Co. of America. Under its agreement with Prudential, Dallas-based Maxus said it plans to pay Prudential $67.5 million to repurchase 500,000 of the three million $9.75 cumulative convertible preferred shares that Prudential owns and to make 750,000 additional shares non-convertible. The shares that won't be convertible any longer will be entitled to an additional annual dividend of $1 a share, Maxus said.

Prudential also will receive a $1.5 million restructuring fee. Further, the agreement would allow Prudential to elect two directors, instead of the current three, to the Maxus board. However, the insurance concern hasn't exercised its right to elect Maxus directors.

Maxus said the transaction will reduce its preferred dividends and cut Prudential's stake in the firm's fully diluted shares to about 11% from 18.5%. Maxus has about 90.8 million common shares outstanding.
