Georgia-Pacific Earnings Drop 34% in Quarter

  • Author: Glenn Ruffenach
  • Date: 04/19/90

ATLANTA -- Georgia-Pacific Corp. reported that first-quarter earnings fell 34% because of higher interest expense and lower prices for pulp and paper products. The forest-products concern said first-quarter net income dropped to $101 million, or $1.18 a share, from $154 million, or $1.65 a share, a year earlier. Sales rose 8.6% to $2.66 billion from $2.45 billion.

The results were the first to include earnings from Great Northern Kekoosa Corp., which Georgia-Pacific acquired in March for $3.74 billion. Total cost of the purchase -- including fees and expenses related to the acquisition, refinancing certain Georgia-Pacific debt and paying off Nekoosa's debt -- was about $5 billion.

That acquisition pushed Georgia-Pacific's interest expense in the first quarter to $97 million from $66 million a year ago. At the same time, softer prices for pulp and paper helped reduce first-quarter operating profits in that segment to $182 million from $247 million a year earlier.

Analysts said the results were in line with expectations and added that Georgia-Pacific should have little difficulty meeting its new debt payments -- even considering a downturn in pulp and paper. "The earnings from Nekoosa itself should be able to cover the interest costs," said Gary Palmero, paper and forest-products analyst at Oppenheimer & Co. in New York. He still expects Georgia-Pacific to post 1989 earnings of between $5 and $5.30 a share, compared with $7.42 a share in 1989 for Georgia-Pacific alone.

Meanwhile, Georgia-Pacific indicated that it is already considering restructuring its debt -- a move that the company estimated would save about $80 million a year in finance costs. A spokeswoman declined to elaborate on how or when the debt might be refinanced.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Georgia-Pacific closed at $40.75 a share, down 37.5 cents.
