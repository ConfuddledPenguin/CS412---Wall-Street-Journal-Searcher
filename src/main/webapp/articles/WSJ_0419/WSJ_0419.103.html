Chrysler Is Recalling 72,000 of '83 Models To Correct Emissions

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/19/90

WASHINGTON -- Chrysler Corp. agreed to recall 72,000 of its 1983 vehicles, because they fail to meet federal emissions standards for carbon monoxide and hydrocarbons, the Environmental Protection Agency said. The models being recalled have 2.2-liter engines and manual transmissions. They include Plymouth Horizon, Turismo and Reliant; Dodge Omni, Charger, Aries, 600ES and 400; and Chrysler LeBaron.

The agency said Chrysler will modify the cars to increase the amount of air that enters the intake manifold, which is between the combustion chamber and carburetor. This will reduce the amount of carbon monoxide and hydrocarbons formed in the combustion chamber, an EPA official said.

Chrysler also will reset the ignition timing, so that the fuel is ignited earlier, allowing it to burn longer and resulting in more complete combustion and lower emissions, the official said. In addition, he said, the company will adjust the idle speed.

A Chrysler spokesman said notification about the recall has already been mailed. The company declined to say how much the recall will cost.

The spokesman for the auto maker said, "We've got to spiff up the engine, so that we can meet the standards, which are extremely stringent."

The national emission standard for cars, established in 1981, permits the emission of 3.4 grams per mile of carbon monoxide and 0.41 grams per mile of hydrocarbons, said an EPA spokeswoman.
