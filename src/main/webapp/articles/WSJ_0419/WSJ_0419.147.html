Business Brief -- Asarco Inc.: Concern Drops Its Lawsuit Against Freeport-McMoRan

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/19/90

Asarco Inc. said it dropped a lawsuit against Freeport-McMoRan Inc. over their 50-50 joint-venture copper-mining research project. Asarco, which has interests in nonferrous metals and specialty chemicals, filed the suit last month in New York state Supreme Court challenging Freeport's plan to sell its half-interest to Minorco S.A. as part of a broader transaction involving the sale of a 61%-owned Freeport unit. But before that transaction was completed at the end of March, Freeport bought the venture stake from the unit for $15 million, Freeport said.

The Asarco suit alleged that if Minorco became its new partner, it could harm the venture. Asarco was concerned that Luxembourg-based Minorco's South African ties might jeopardize the venture's federal funding.

The purpose of the venture, the Santa Cruz Project near Casa Grande, Ariz., is to test the feasibility of new technology to extract copper from a deep underground deposit.

In late February, Freeport-McMoRan Gold Co., a unit of New Orleans-based Freeport-McMoRan Inc., agreed to be acquired-along with its 50% stake in the Santa Cruz Project-by Minorco for about $705 million in a $17-a-share tender offer.
