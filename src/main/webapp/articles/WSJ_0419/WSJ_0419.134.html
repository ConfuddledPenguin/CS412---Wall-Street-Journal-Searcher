Business Brief -- Ashton-Tate Corp.: Software Maker Posts Loss Of $1 Million for 1st Period

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/19/90

Ashton-Tate Corp., Torrance, Calif., said it posted a first-quarter loss of $1 million, or four cents a share, as revenue plummeted 37% to $57 million. The company cited continuing slumping sales resulting from delays in the introduction of its upgraded version of dBASE IV, a computer spreadsheet program. In the year-earlier quarter, the software maker had a profit of $11.5 million, or 44 cents a share, on revenue of $89.8 million.

The results had been expected. The firm in 1989 had a loss of $28.7 million largely because of glitches in developing the dBASE upgrade. Ashton-Tate said it still can't give a delivery date for the upgrade, which many analysts had hoped would be ready during the 1990 first quarter.

"Many customers have delayed their purchases of dBASE IV in anticipation of the shipment of the new version," Ashton-Tate said. It said it believes "this trend will continue" until shipment of the updated version.
