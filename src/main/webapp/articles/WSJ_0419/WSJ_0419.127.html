International Brief -- Brierley Investments Ltd.: Firm to Seek Majority Role In New Zealand's Telecom

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/19/90

Brierley Investments Ltd. said it applied to New Zealand's Commerce Commission to acquire a majority interest in Telecom Corp., the state-owned monopoly telephone company that the government plans to sell to reduce debt. Bob Matthew, chief executive officer of Brierley, said the company's bid for Telecom may involve foreign partners.

"While Brierley Investments may well seek in its own right to acquire a significant shareholding in Telecom, we are also having discussions with a number of overseas parties who have a genuine interest in investing in Telecom," he said.

Earlier Wednesday the government said it has accepted a number of parties as suitable potential bidders for shares in Telecom, and will be giving them special access to information about Telecom to help in valuations of the company.

But the government said it won't be identifying any of the potential bidders or detailing the timing of the sale process.

Foreign buyers will be restricted to 49.9% of Telecom shares.
