Business Brief -- Tandem Computers Inc.: Fiscal 2nd-Quarter Profit Climbed 59%, Revenue 24%

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/19/90

Tandem Computers Inc. said fiscal second-quarter net income surged 59% on the strength of new product sales and a tight rein on expenses. Revenue rose 24%. The Cupertino, Calif., computer concern posted a profit of $26.8 million, or 25 cents a share, on revenue of $451.3 million for the quarter ended March 31. It earned $16.9 million, or 17 cents a share, on revenue of $363.4 million, in the year-earlier period.

For the six-month period, Tandem said profit grew 20% to $57.6 million, or 53 cents a share, from $48.2 million, or 48 cents a share. Revenue was up 17% to $887.8 million from $755.7 million a year earlier.

Tandem makes computers with automatic backup systems that are used by stock exchanges and banks, among others, to run sophisticated financial systems.

Tandem stock rose $1.125 a share yesterday to close at $24.125 in New York Stock Exchange composite trading.
