East German Output Off

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/19/90

EAST BERLIN -- East Germany's industrial production dropped 4.7% in the first quarter of 1990 from year-earlier levels, mostly reflecting a decline in the number of workers, the president of the nation's Statistics Office, Arno Donda, said. According to East Germany's ADN news agency, Mr. Donda said the total number of industrial workers declined by 124,000 during the period, while the number of workers in the construction sector alone was off 33,000 from year-earlier levels.

Many East Germans started to move permanently to West Germany in search of better living conditions after the breaching of the Berlin Wall on Nov. 9. That trend continued during the first months of 1990, but the stream of resettlers, which included a large number of skilled workers, slowed to a trickle after East Germany held its first free elections on March 18.
