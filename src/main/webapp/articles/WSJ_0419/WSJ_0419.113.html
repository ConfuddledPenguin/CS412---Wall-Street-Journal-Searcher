Business Brief -- J.P. Industries: T&N Extends Bid for Firm As FTC Seeks More Data

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/19/90

T&N PLC said it extended the expiration date of its tender offer to buy all the common stock of J.P. Industries Inc., an Ann Arbour, Mich., automotive engine parts maker, after regulators requested additional information. T&N, an English equipment manufacturer, began its tender offer earlier this month, after J.P. Industries agreed to be acquired by T&N for $190 million, or $17.30 a share, plus the assumption of $185 million of debt.

The companies said the Federal Trade Commission has requested additional information about the transaction under the Hart-Scott-Rodino antitrust law.

T&N said it will extend the offer until May 14. The tender offer had been scheduled to expire April 26. As of Tuesday, shareholders had tendered 505,747 shares, or about 5% of the shares outstanding.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, J.P. Industries stock closed at $16.875 a share, unchanged.
