Jacobs Drops Plan To Wage Proxy Fight Against CML Group

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/19/90

CML Group Inc. said Minneapolis investor Irwin Jacobs, who with Minstar Inc. holds a 14.9% stake in the company, dropped a previously announced plan to wage a proxy fight to replace CML's board. CML, Acton, Mass., said Mr. Jacobs decided against pursuing the proxy fight after a meeting with company officials in which the sportswear and recreation products concern predicted healthy earnings and sales increases for fiscal 1990 and 1991.

Mr. Jacobs, whose earlier $25-a-share bid was rejected by CML, stopped short of ruling out any future action on CML. He said no standstill or other agreements have been reached with CML.

Mr. Jacobs noted that CML's forecast represents "quite a difference from where they've been." He said CML, after selling a number of businesses, "can focus on fewer businesses without stretching beyond their means."

Since Mr. Jacobs and Minstar, a Minneapolis company he runs, bought their stake, CML has sold several businesses as part of a restructuring. CML predicted earnings from continuing operations of $1.90 to $2 a share in the fiscal year ending July 31, although it will post a loss for the year because of losses from discontinued operations. Sales in the year will be about $275 million, up from $234.6 million.

CML also predicted that earnings from operations will increase to about $2.80 a share on increased sales of about $318 million. CML said its growth will come in part from an expansion of its Nature Co. stores and additions to its line of NordicTrack exercise equipment.

In the previous fiscal year, the company had a loss of $5.5 million, or 85 cents a share, from continuing operations. A loss from discontinued operations of $26.4 million resulted in a loss in the year of $31.9 million, or $4.92 a share.

In an agreement earlier this year between Mr. Jacobs and CML and filed with the Securities and Exchange Commission, Mr. Jacobs would have been permitted to solicit consents from CML shareholders from April 18 to May 18, calling for a special meeting to remove CML's board and replace it with a slate committed to selling the company to the highest bidder.

In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange, CML closed at $21.125 a share, down $1.125.
