Swiss Trade Deficit Narrows

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/19/90

BERN, Switzerland -- Switzerland's trade deficit in March narrowed to 775 million francs ($520.3 million) from a revised gap of 827 million francs in February and from a deficit of 993 million francs a year earlier, the government said. For the first three months of this year, the trade gap widened to 2.8 billion francs from 2.5 billion francs a year earlier.

Last month, imports surged to 9.2 billion francs from 8.2 billion francs in February and from 8.2 billion francs in March 1989. Exports expanded strongly to 8.4 billion francs last month from 7.4 billion francs in February and from 7.2 billion francs a year earlier. The continued export boom has worried the Swiss National Bank because of the inflationary implications of an overheated economy.

For the year through March, imports totaled 25.4 billion francs, up from 22.3 billion francs a year earlier, and exports increased to 22.57 billion francs from 19.7 billion francs.
