Thomson McKinnon Plans to Sell Interest In Asset Management

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/19/90

NEW YORK -- Thomson McKinnon Inc. agreed in principle to sell its general partnership interest in its asset management company to a group including executives of the money manager. The money manager, Thomson McKinnon Asset Management Limited Partnership, is Thomson McKinnon's largest remaining operating company. Thomson McKinnon, which is liquidating, last year sold its retail brokerage network to Prudential-Bache Securities Group Inc. The operations that remained of the broker-dealer unit filed for protection from creditors under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code last month.

Greylock Investments and TA Associates, both of Boston, and several investment advisers of Thomson McKinnon Asset Management are proposing to acquire Thomson McKinnon's general partnership interest in the manager of mutual funds and private accounts.

Greylock and TA are closely held equity investment firms with a total of more than $1 billion of committed equity capital. The Thomson unit had $6.7 billion in assets under management as of March 31. The group doesn't plan to buy any of the public limited partnership units.

Under the plan, the group would become the general partner, purchasing 200,000 general partnership units from Thomson McKinnon for $800,000 and paying $5.4 million for an option to acquire the balance of Thomson's total of about 6.2 million units. The option would have to be exercised by 1994, and dividends would go toward reducing the purchase price.

The agreement also includes the sale of the broker dealer's Thomson McKinnon Fund Distributors Inc.
