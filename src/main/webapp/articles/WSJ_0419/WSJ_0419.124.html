MUTUAL FUNDS with sales-related charges called 12b-1 fees generally couldn't charge investors more than 7.25% of sales, under a proposal by the National Association of Securities Dealers. However, investors who hold their shares for a long time may be forced to pay more than the 7.25% level. Yesterday's edition misstated the proposed fee structure. (See: "NASD Proposes Limit on Fees Set by Funds" -- WSJ April 18, 1990)

---

VIACOM Inc. is approximately 84% owned by National Amusements Inc. Yesterday's advertising column misstated the relationship between the two companies.

(See: "Marketing & Media -- Advertising: Warner Bros. Plans an Offensive Against Ads in Movie Theaters" -- WSJ April 18, 1990)

---

BALDWIN TECHNOLOGY Co. produces accessories and controls for printing equipment. In Tuesday's edition it was incorrectly indicated that the company was considering expanding into manufacturing printing presses.

(See: "Baldwin Technology Is Planning to Acquire Dyson-Kissner Units" -- WSJ April 17, 1990)

---

EECO Inc. expects to report a $10.3 million 1989 net loss. The loss was misstated in Tuesday's edition.

(See: "Business Brief -- Eeco Inc.: Net Loss of $1.4 Million Estimated for Jan. 1 Year" -- WSJ April 17, 1990)
