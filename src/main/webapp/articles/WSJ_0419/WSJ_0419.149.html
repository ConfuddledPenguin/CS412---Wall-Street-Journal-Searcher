Business Brief -- Signal Apparel Co.: Walsh Greenwood Will Buy 51% Holding From Trusts

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/19/90

Signal Apparel Co. said Walsh, Greenwood & Co. agreed to buy as much as a 51% stake from trusts for the benefit of George B. Clairmont, a director and former chairman of the apparel company. Signal Apparel, Chattanooga, Tenn., said the New York limited partnership agreed to buy about 25% of the Class A common and 6% of the cumulative convertible preferred shares from the trusts. Walsh Greenwood also has a two-year option to buy 60% of the Class B common held by the trusts, the company said.

The transaction's value wasn't disclosed. The company said, however, that Walsh Greenwood will pay $4.50 for each Class A share, $18 apiece for the preferred, and $6.50 for each Class B share.

Signal said that subject to certain conditions, the transaction is expected to close in 10 days.

It said upon closing of the transaction, Roger Holland, former president and chief executive officer of Champion Products Inc., would be named president and chief executive officer, succeeding Robert B. Colbert Jr., who will either remain chairman or become vice chairman.
