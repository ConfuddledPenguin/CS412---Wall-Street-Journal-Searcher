Letters to the Editor: National I.D. Card Was Never My Idea

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/19/90

Of all the statements that any knowledgeable person could take issue with in your April 3 editorial "A National Identity Card?" none is further from the truth than your assertion that "INS Commissioner Gene McNary didn't shrink from calling it a national I.D." Not only have I never "floated" such an idea, I am strongly opposed to a national identity card. What I have said is that Congress should consider just what the General Accounting Office report called for: an improved verification system to eliminate discrimination in hiring. This could include something as simple as a phone verification using a Social Security number, similar to what occurs every time you or I use a credit card.

Though your longstanding bias against any provisions that forbid employers from hiring illegal aliens makes it difficult for you to acknowledge the fact, that is significantly different from a national identity card.

Gene McNary

Commissioner

Immigration and Naturalization Service

Department of Justice

Washington
