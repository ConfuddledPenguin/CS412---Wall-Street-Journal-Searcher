International Brief -- Hyundai Group: Korean Firm to Take Part In Soviet Petrochemical Plan

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/19/90

Hyundai Group agreed to take part in the Soviet Union's $2 billion petrochemical complex project at Tobolsk in the West Siberian lowland, officials said in Seoul, South Korea. Hyundai said the accord was reached between the Soviet Petrochemical Industry Ministry and Chung Ju-yung, Hyundai's founder-honorary chairman. He returned home Tuesday after a 10-day trip to Moscow.

The Hyundai officials quoted Mr. Chung as saying that Hyundai will be awarded a $600 million contract to build a polypropylene plant for the complex. They didn't say what would be the plant's annual production capacity.

The South Korean business conglomerate also agreed to invest $30 million, or 5% of the capital of a joint venture company to be set up to operate the complex, Mr. Chung said.
