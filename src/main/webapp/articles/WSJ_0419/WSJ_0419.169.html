American Toxxic Cancels Negotiations With Verit Industries

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/19/90

American Toxxic Control Inc. directors said they canceled negotiations with Verit Industries about the proposed acquisition of American Toxxic by a Verit subsidiary. Last Thursday, Verit said its 80%-owned LaPointe Industries Inc. unit, which recently emerged from bankruptcy-court proceedings as a shell company, had agreed in principle to merge in a transaction that would give American Toxxic common stock of LaPointe.

Verit said it expected LaPointe to change its name to American Toxxic Control and apply for a listing on the American Stock Exchange after the merger was completed. Verit also offered to help American Toxxic restructure about $4 million of debt.

American Toxxic, based in Whittier, Calif., is involved in treating chemical-industry wastes.

A statement issued late yesterday by Verit, based in Phoenix, Ariz., described the negotiations as having been "mutually terminated." But Ski Milburn, chief executive officer of American Toxxic, said Verit was "trying to save face" with that wording, which he said was inaccurate. "We discontinued the negotiations. They said, `Oh, please no,'" according to Mr. Milburn.

Mr. Milburn, who said he had succeeded American Toxxic founder and director Louis Purmort as chairman last year, said Mr. Purmort had brought the Verit proposal to the American Toxxic board originally. Mr. Milburn said, however, that after the preliminary agreement had been reached, Mr. Milburn had raised some "real questions about Verit's balance sheet" last week.

He also said a Wall Street Journal article on Verit earlier this week presented information that the board believed Verit should have made available previously to American Toxxic.
