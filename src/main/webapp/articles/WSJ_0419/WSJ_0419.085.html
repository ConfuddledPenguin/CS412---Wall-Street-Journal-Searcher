International: Cuba, Soviet Union Sign $14.7 Billion Trade Pact

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/19/90

HAVANA, Cuba -- Cuba and the Soviet Union signed a one-year trade pact valued at $14.7 billion and set up a special committee to discuss future trade relations. The agreement calls for an exchange of Soviet oil, cereals, food, equipment and other goods for Cuban sugar, nickel, chrome, citrus and, for the first time, Cuban-made medicine and medical equipment. AIN, the domestic Cuban news agency, said the value of the pact was 8.7% higher than last year.

A special committee was set up to study future economic ties, which the Soviets say they want to make more efficient. Soviet officials say a new economic cooperation accord will be shorter than five years, probably two years, and will take into account Soviet economic reforms. About 80% of Cuba's trade is with the Soviet Union.

Western diplomats say the changes could affect the price Moscow is willing to pay for Cuban sugar in the future. The Soviets currently pay well over the world market price for sugar and in return sell Cuba oil at subsidized rates.
