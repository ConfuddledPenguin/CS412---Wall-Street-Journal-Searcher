Reserve Assets Rose in March

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/19/90

WASHINGTON -- U.S. official reserve assets rose $2.13 billion in March to $76.3 billion, the Treasury Department said. Last month's rise followed a $1.33 billion decline in reserve assets in February, the department said.

U.S. reserve assets consist of foreign currencies, gold, Special Drawing Rights at the International Monetary Fund and the U.S. reserve position, or its ability to draw foreign currencies, at the IMF.

The nation's holdings of foreign currencies increased $2.51 billion in March to $46.42 billion, while its gold reserves were virtually unchanged at $11.06 billion.

U.S. holdings of IMF Special Drawing Rights last month fell $124 million to $10.09 billion, and its reserve position at the IMF dropped $258 million to $8.73 billion.
