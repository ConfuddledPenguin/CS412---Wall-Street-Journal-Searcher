Norton Goes to Court Again To Stave Off BTR Takeover

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/19/90

BOSTON -- Norton Co. brought a new legal challenge to a $1.64 billion takeover bid by BTR PLC, arguing that shareholders who have accepted the bid shouldn't be able to vote at Norton's annual meeting. BTR is trying to oust Norton directors at the company's annual meeting next Thursday in order to force Norton, an abrasives company based in Worcester, Mass., to accept the bid. Holders of more than 62% of Norton have already tendered their shares to BTR.

In a claim filed in federal court yesterday, Norton said those shares shouldn't be allowed to be voted at the meeting because BTR has already acquired them under a 1988 Massachusetts anti-takeover law without complying with other provisions of the same law.

A lawyer for BTR called the lawsuit "totally frivolous." Takeover stock traders were also skeptical of the lawsuit. Although Norton stock fell 62.5 cents to $75.375 a share in composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange, its price still remained above the $75 BTR bid.

The stock fell after Massachusetts Governor Michael Dukakis signed a new state law allowing companies to impose staggered terms for directors, with one-third elected every year, which could delay BTR's acquisition. However, a federal appeals court upheld an earlier federal court decision blocking Norton's attempt to delay its annual meeting until June.
