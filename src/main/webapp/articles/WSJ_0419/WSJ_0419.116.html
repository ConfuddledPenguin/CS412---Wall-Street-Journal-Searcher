Business Brief -- Bank of New England Corp.: Connecticut Bank & Trust Is Told to Tighten Policies

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/19/90

The Comptroller of the Currency directed Bank of New England Corp.'s Connecticut Bank & Trust Co. subsidiary to tighten its lending policies and management oversight in a consent cease-and-desist order. The Comptroller also cited a violation of law applicable to the bank, but the Treasury Department office declined to give further details.

Bank of New England is already operating under a similar order, and the Connecticut order was expected. Its provisions include a requirement that the bank maintain an equity capital-to-total assets ratio of at least 3% and develop a timetable through 1991 to achieve and maintain a ratio of at least 5%.

In a statement, Connecticut Bank & Trust called the plans and procedures outlined in the order "sound measures that any bank should follow" and said that "many of the changes called for are already under way."
