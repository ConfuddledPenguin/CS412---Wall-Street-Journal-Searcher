Southmark Creditors Seeking to File Suit Against Ex-Officials

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/19/90

DALLAS -- Southmark Corp.'s creditors' committee has asked a federal bankruptcy judge to allow it to sue the company's former top two executives to recover more than $83 million in assets. In a filing with the court, the committee said it asked to file an adversary proceeding against Gene E. Phillips, former Southmark chairman; William S. Friedman, former vice chairman, and companies they control to recover funds and assets on behalf of Southmark.

The creditors contend that the assets were fraudulently conveyed to Messrs. Phillips and Friedman as part of settlements with Southmark in January and July 1989, when the real estate and thrift concern already was insolvent.

In the proposed complaint, the creditors committee argues that Southmark gave up at least $82 million in actual assets in those two settlements while receiving less than $14 million in return. A hearing on the request is scheduled for May 16.

The settlements stem from an arrangement under which Mr. Phillips and Mr. Friedman would resign their positions at Southmark. Among other things, the agreements called for Southmark to swap its stock in several affiliates and release the executives from personal guarantees on a costly real estate project in exchange for mortgages and release from its own guarantees on other assets.

Messrs. Phillips and Friedman have contended that the agreements were fairly negotiated transactions that should be allowed to stand. In an interview yesterday, Mr. Friedman said he hasn't seen the complaint, but said: "We're very happy to have an opportunity in a fair and unbiased forum to present what actually happened in these transactions."

Separately, Southmark said it reached an agreement with its equity holders committee calling for holders of its preferred and common stock to receive 11% of the stock in reorganized company, up from about 5%.
