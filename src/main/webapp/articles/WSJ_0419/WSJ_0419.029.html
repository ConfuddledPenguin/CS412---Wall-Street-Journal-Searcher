Occidental's Net Advanced 61% In First Quarter

  • Author: David J. Jefferson
  • Date: 04/19/90

LOS ANGELES -- Occidental Petroleum Corp. posted a 61% increase in first-quarter net income to $108 million, or 37 cents a share, from $67 million, or 24 cents a share, a year earlier. Sales rose 2%, to $5.2 billion from $5.1 billion. The results include extraordinary losses of $2 million, or one cent a share, in the latest first quarter, and $9 million, or three cents a share, in the year-earlier period from the early extinguishment of debt.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Occidental closed at $27.125, unchanged.

The company had increased earnings in its oil and gas and natural gas transmission divisions, offset by a $96 million drop in earnings from chemical operations.

Profit from oil and gas operations rose to $100 million in the quarter from $35 million a year earlier. That increase reflected higher world-wide prices for crude oil and higher domestic prices for natural gas and natural gas liquids, and the resumption of production from the North Sea Claymore and Scalpa fields after the explosion of the nearby Piper Alpha oil platform in 1988. These gains were partially offset by reduced domestic crude oil and natural gas volumes.

The latest first-quarter results included a $36 million pretax gain from the settlement of a claim against Venezuela for the 1975 nationalization of Occidental's commercial block, a reduction in costs due to the restructuring last year of domestic oil and gas operations, a $13 million pretax write-off of certain receivables, and a $16 million benefit from the reversal of certain income-tax reserves. Occidental said the reversal of the tax reserves resulted from an Internal Revenue Service audit of the company and certain subsidiaries back to 1985 that determined the reserves are no longer necessary.

Earnings from chemical operations fell to $186 million from $282 million in the year-earlier quarter, reflecting lower profit margins in petrochemicals and polymers and plastics. Earnings in the latest quarter included a $14 million reversal of income tax reserves.

Natural gas transmission earnings rose to $94 million from $61 million a year earlier, reflecting lower operating costs, a rate refund and a $12 million tax benefit in the latest period. Earnings from agribusiness operations, conducted by Occidental's 51%-owned IBP Inc. subsidiary, rose to $14 million in the latest first quarter from $13 million the year before. Earnings from coal operations rose to $4 million from $3 million.

Among the unallocated corporate items in the year-earlier first quarter was a $36 million pretax charge for a charitable contribution to the Armand Hammer Museum of Art and Cultural Center. The $95 million project has sparked shareholder litigation, and the Delaware Chancery Court is considering whether the outlay is appropriate.
