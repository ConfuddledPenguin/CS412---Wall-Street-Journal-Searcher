Marketing & Media: Perrier Will Water Down Claim Made on Its Label

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/19/90

Perrier Group of America Inc. agreed to drop the notation of "naturally sparkling water" from its labels following a ruling by the Food and Drug Administration. The FDA said the carbon dioxide and water are taken out separately from the Perrier spring.

"We have a difference in opinion but the FDA feels consumers will better understand what the product is" with the revised labeling, said a Perrier spokeswoman.

The FDA action comes as claims for bottled water come under increasing scrutiny. Rep. John D. Dingell (D, Mich.) said recently that he might hold hearings into the marketing claims of the bottled water industry.

Perrier Group said its mineral water will be back on store shelves beginning next week. The company said the revised label will be on all bottles by July 31.

In February, Perrier S.A. announced a world-wide recall of its mineral water after minute levels of benzene, a known carcinogen, was found in samples of the water. The incident forced Perrier to disclose that benzene occurs naturally in Perrier water.
