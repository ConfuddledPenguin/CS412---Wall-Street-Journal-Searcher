Business Brief -- CAE Industries Ltd.: Shares Fall on News Report Of Inquiry Into Link Unit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/19/90

Shares of CAE Industries Ltd. fell sharply after a Binghamton, N.Y., newspaper reported that a grand jury had been convened to investigate accusations against a unit of the Toronto-based maker of electronics and aviation equipment. A CAE spokesman said he believes a grand jury has been empaneled, but wouldn't elaborate. CAE shares closed yesterday at 6.375 Canadian dollars (US$5.47), down 87.5 Canadian cents, in trading on the Toronto stock exchange.

CAE's Link Flight Simulation unit in Binghamton is the subject of a previously reported civil suit, alleging that the flight simulator maker submitted more than $77 million in fraudulent and inflated claims against the U.S. government between 1980 and 1988. CAE bought Link in 1988 for $560 million from the former Singer Co., Stamford, Conn., now Bicoastal Corp., Tampa, Fla. CAE declined to comment on details of that suit, but said Link's former owner would have to indemnify it for claims arising from activities before the acquisition.
