U.S.'s Steel Imports Fell 16.8% in February As Exports Soared

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/19/90

WASHINGTON -- U.S. steel imports declined 16.8% in February from a year earlier, while steel exports climbed to one of the highest monthly totals in recent years, according to the American Iron and Steel Institute. The trade group compilation of Commerce Department data showed U.S imports of steel mill products in February were 1,111,000 tons compared with 1,336,000 a year earlier. January imports totaled 1,430,000.

Imported steel in February was 15.8% of the total supply of the U.S. market, compared with 16.8% a year earlier and 17% for the first two months of the year.

February steel exports were 565,000 tons, substantially higher than both the 237,000 tons a year earlier and 374,000 tons in January.

The American Institute for Imported Steel noted that imports for the first five months under the new voluntary restraint agreement were 17.9% of consumption, well below the U.S. target of 19.1%. The agreement, which went into effect in October, allows imports to account for 19.1% of consumption through December 1990 and 20.1% until the program ends in March 1992.
