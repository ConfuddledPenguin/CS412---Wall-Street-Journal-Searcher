Shell Canada Pulls Out Of Altamont Project To Build Gas Pipeline

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/19/90

CALGARY, Alberta -- Shell Canada Ltd. said it withdrew as a 16.66% equity partner in the proposed $580 million Altamont gas pipeline, citing conflicting contractual obligations with Altamont rival Pacific Gas Transmission Co, a unit of Pacific Gas & Electric Co. of San Francisco. Analysts said the loss of Shell Canada strengthens the position of Pacific Gas, which has proposed a competing $1.2 billion expansion of its Alberta-to-California pipeline system.

Pacific Gas has secured conditional contracts covering all of its proposed additional pipeline capacity. The expansion would allow the concern to move each day an additional 755 million cubic feet to California and 148 million cubic feet to the Pacific Northwest.

Altamont still has about 200 million cubic feet a day of uncommitted space on its proposed pipeline, a spokeswoman said. Its sponsors, led by a unit of Houston-based Tenneco Inc. with a 40% interest, plan to transport as many as 700 million cubic feet of gas a day by late 1993 on their system. It would extend from Alberta to Wyoming, where it would connect with a separate Wyoming-to-California pipeline also backed by Tenneco.

Altamont said its remaining sponsors, including Petro-Canada Inc., Amoco Canada Petroleum Co., a unit of Chicago-based Amoco Corp., and Entech Inc., a subsidiary of Montana Power Co., will absorb Shell Canada's interest. Petro-Canada and Amoco Canada are major Calgary-based gas producers.
