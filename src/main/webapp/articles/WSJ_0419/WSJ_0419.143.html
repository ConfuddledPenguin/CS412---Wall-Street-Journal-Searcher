Business Brief -- Consolidated Rail Corp.: Railroad's Net Declines 58%, Sending Stock for a Tumble

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/19/90

Consolidated Rail Corp. blamed lower revenue plus recapitalization costs for a steeper-than-expected 58% drop in first-quarter earnings that sent the Philadelphia company's stock tumbling. Conrail said net dropped to $25 million, or 43 cents a share, from $60 million, or 88 cents a share, a year ago. Revenue fell 5.6% to $823 million from $872 million.

Conrail closed yesterday at $43.25, down $1.625, in composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange.

Wall Street had been expecting a drop in Conrail's profit because of lower traffic and a change in traffic mix. The company had said that it carried higher volumes of lower-revenue freight, such as coal, and lower volumes of higher-revenue freight, such as autos.

But PaineWebber Inc. analyst Anthony Hatch noted that while Conrail's operating profit came in about as expected, the "other income" category showed expenses of $20 million -- far higher than the $4 million or so he had expected. A year ago, the railroad had $7 million in other income.

A railroad spokesman attributed the $27 million swing to a reduction in interest income of $6 million and an increase in interest expenses of $8 million, both related to the recapitalization. The quarter also included about $10 million in litigation settlements, he said.
