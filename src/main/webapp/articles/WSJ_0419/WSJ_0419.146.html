Business Brief -- Heidelberger Zement A.G.: Lehigh Cement Unit Agrees To Labor-Case Settlement

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/19/90

Lehigh Portland Cement Co. agreed to a $5 million settlement of National Labor Relations Board charges that it engaged in unfair labor practices, the NLRB said. The settlement covers back pay for members of the International Brotherhood of Boilermakers, the board said, and stems from a case that was begun in 1984. Lehigh, based in Allentown, Pa., is a subsidiary of Heidelberger Zement A.G., Heidelberg, West Germany.

The NLRB concluded in November 1987 that Lehigh violated labor law by putting contract proposals into effect unilaterally, thereby changing the terms of an expired collective-bargaining agreement without first bargaining to an impasse with the union. The company and the union eventually agreed to a new contract in October 1988.

Under the settlement, the NLRB's Philadelphia regional office will determine how the back wages will be distributed among approximately 1,200 employees at the company's nine plants.
