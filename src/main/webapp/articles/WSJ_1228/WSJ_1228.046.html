Technology & Health: Benzene Is Found In Dental Glues; 3 Brands Recalled

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 12/28/90

WASHINGTON -- Three brands of dental adhesive contain unacceptable levels of benzene, according to the Food and Drug Administration. The three products are Orafix Special and Brace, made by Norcliff Thayer, a Philadelphia unit of Britain's SmithKline Beecham PLC, and Cushion Grip, made by Schering-Plough HealthCare Products, a unit of Schering-Plough Corp., Madison, N.J.

All three products will be voluntarily recalled by their manufacturers, the FDA said in a statement released late yesterday. Meanwhile, the agency is urging consumers not to use the dental adhesives until the manufacturers resume manufacturing them without benzene.

Although benzene causes cancer when ingested in large amounts, James Benson, FDA deputy commissioner said, "We don't see this as a significant health issue at this point." But he added that "any time you have something that's a known or potential carcinogen you want to get rid of it."

While SmithKline called the levels of benzene contamination "minute" and "measured in parts per million," it was sufficient to warrant an immediate recall.

A spokesman for Schering-Plough said that the company "believes the trace concentrations do not pose a health hazard." But he said the company would cooperate with the FDA to revise the manufacturing process.

The amount of benzene found in the three dental adhesive products exceeds levels that were found by the FDA in Perrier earlier this year. The result was a highly publicized recall of Perrier by its French manufacturer, Source Perrier S.A. Perrier returned to grocery shelves this past July after the company altered its production process.

The FDA also found low levels of benzene in Fasteeth and Fixodent, two dental adhesive products manufactured by Richardson-Vicks Inc., Shelton, Conn., and said that it would work with the company to reformulate them. But the agency said the benzene level in these products didn't pose a significant health risk, noting that most people consume more benzene daily in their drinking water. A company spokesman couldn't be reached for comment.
