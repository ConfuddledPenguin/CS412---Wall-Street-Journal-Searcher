Air Canada Will Sell At Least a Majority Of Travel-Card Unit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/28/90

MONTREAL -- Air Canada said it will seek to sell at least a majority interest in its enRoute corporate travel credit card unit. Gary Lynch, president and chief executive of enRoute, said the unit is profitable, but he declined to provide revenue and net income figures.

Mr. Lynch said enRoute has about 300,000 cardholders, mainly in Canada. The enRoute credit card was introduced in the U.S. two years ago. The card is accepted by more than 200 airlines and about 15,000 hotels, restaurants and other concerns.

Air Canada, the country's largest carrier, said it decided to sell at least a majority stake in the enRoute unit to allow it to concentrate on its core airline business. Mr. Lynch said Air Canada hasn't indicated how large a minority stake it would be willing to retain in enRoute.

Air Canada recently cut about 2,900 jobs, or 12.5% of its work force, and suspended service on three international routes. For 1990's first nine months, Air Canada's net income dropped 45% to C$50 million, or 68 Canadian cents a share, from the year-earlier period. Revenue was C$3.04 billion.
