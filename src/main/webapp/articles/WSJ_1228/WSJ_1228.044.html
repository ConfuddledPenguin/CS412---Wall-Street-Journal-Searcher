Letters to the Editor: Nigerians Subjected To Absurd Suspicions

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/28/90

We protest the irresponsible insinuations implicit in your story "Nigerians Smuggling Heroin in Their Stomachs Pose Fast-Growing Problem for Federal Agents" (Nov. 16). With a combination of questionable and historically prejudiced DEA statistics, poor investigation and outright ignorance, you added to the wave of unfair Nigeria-bashing pervasive among certain elements in the U.S. media who are quick to judge a country of 125 million people by the work of a handful.

To suggest that Nigerians supply 25% of the $15 billion U.S. heroin market is absurd; to state that 200 Nigerians bring heroin to the U.S. daily and that Nigerians supply up to 50% of Washington, D.C.'s, heroin has to be patently false.

Pieces like this suggest that the assumption is fine that any Nigerian at any U.S. airport is carrying heroin until the contrary is proven. Many Nigerian dignitaries, including the winner of the Nobel Prize in Literature, Prof. Wole Soyinka, and a former head of state, Olusegun Obasanjo, have been subjected to embarrassing intrusive searches at American airports.

When was the last time someone screamed, "American heroin smugglers . . ." "Italian heroin smugglers . . . etc"? Or are we to assume that other drug dealers have no nationalities? If some Nigerians are bad, why must our country suffer so?

Yinka Adeyemi

Organization for the Advancement of Nigerians

Brooklyn, N.Y.
