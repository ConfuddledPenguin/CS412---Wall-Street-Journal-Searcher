Business Brief -- American International Group: Court Upholds Unit's Fine For Liability Overcharges

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/28/90

The state insurance department said the New York State Court of Appeals upheld a fine against a subsidiary of American International Group for overcharging taxicab owners on liability coverage. As a result, the AIG unit, Insurance Co. of the State of Pennsylvania, based in New York, must pay $425,000, the insurance department said.

In May 1988, the subsidiary was fined for overcharging taxicab mini-fleet owners about $1.4 million over a three-year period, starting in 1981. The state insurance department fined the unit for improperly using a liability conversion factor to increase premiums and increasing premiums without state approval.

AIG said the decision puts an end to the "old" case, but questioned why the Court of Appeals, the state's highest court, came to the decision without conducting a hearing. "It's a shame," said Patrick Foley, AIG's general counsel. "We feel the decision was wrong."
