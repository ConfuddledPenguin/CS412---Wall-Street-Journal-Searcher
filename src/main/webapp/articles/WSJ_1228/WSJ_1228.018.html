Best Buy's Earnings In Quarter Rose 47% On Higher Revenue

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/28/90

MINNEAPOLIS -- Best Buy Co., with its expanded operations and a reduced cost structure more than offsetting a tough selling environment, said net income for its fiscal third quarter ended Dec. 1 jumped 47%, on a 29% increase in revenue. The consumer electronics and appliance retailer warned, however, that it may take a hefty charge against earnings in the near future, to cover changes in an accounting requirement.

Best Buy's third-quarter net climbed in the latest quarter to $1.3 million, or 16 cents a share, from the year-earlier $888,000, or 11 cents a share. Revenue rose to $170.8 million from $132.7 million.

"Competitive conditions," such as heavy discounting in a sluggish economic environment, worked against profit margins, and same-store sales declined by 1%, Best Buy said. In addition, profitability was squeezed in the latest quarter by narrower margins at "new concept" self-service stores the company has recently opened.

Despite those pressures, earnings improved, "mainly due to sales from eight new stores opened this year," as well as lower operating expenses.

But Best Buy cautioned that a recent decision by the Financial Accounting Standards Board could require a charge of $14.3 million, or $1.73 a share, in the current fiscal year.

The FASB's Technical Bulletin 90-I, issued this month, requires companies to defer the initial recognition of revenue from the sale of extended service plans, on plans sold after Dec. 15, 1990. In general, such revenue is to be recognized as income on a straight-line basis over the period of the service contract, which generally runs from three to five years.

Currently, such contacts are recorded in their entirety in the period in which they are sold.

Best Buy said that if it decides to apply the provisions of the new accounting pronouncement to existing contracts, the move would generate the $14.3 million pretax charge; that figure would include an approximately $14 million cumulative effect adjustment for the prior years ended March 4, 1990, and a charge of about $300,000, or four cents a share, for the current year. A company official said, "I would expect that we'll go ahead with the cumulative charge" in the current fourth quarter.

The company stressed that the anticipated accounting change "has no impact on the cash flow aspects of the service plans."

For the nine months, Best Buy's net was $928,000, or 11 cents a share, compared with the year-earlier period's loss of $246,000, or three cents a share. Nine-month revenue was up 26%, to $449.1 million from $355.5 million.

In composite trading yesterday on the New York Stock Exchange, Best Buy shares closed unchanged at $5.125.
