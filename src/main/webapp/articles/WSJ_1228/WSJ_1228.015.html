International: Hitachi Is Building a Chip Plant

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/28/90

TOKYO -- Hitachi Ltd., a maker of computers and semiconductors, said it started building a plant for mass production of 16-megabit dynamic random-access memory, or DRAM, chips. The construction cost and the plant's expected output weren't disclosed. One megabit is equal to one million units of digital information. The plant is next to Hitachi's four-megabit DRAM chip plant in Ibaragi Prefecture northeast of Tokyo. Construction is scheduled to be completed by the end of 1991 and chip production is to start in 1992, a spokesmen said. Hitachi has already begun mass production of four-megabit chips, which are expected to become widely used starting next year.

Earlier this year, NEC Corp. and Toshiba Corp., other major makers of semiconductors in Japan, also announced the start of construction on plants for mass production of 16-megabit DRAM chips.
