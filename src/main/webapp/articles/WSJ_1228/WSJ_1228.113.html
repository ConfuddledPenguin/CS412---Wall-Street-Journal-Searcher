Business Brief -- Southern Co.: Power-Contracts Pact Set With Gulf States Utilities

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/28/90

Southern Co. said its subsidiaries have completed negotiating an agreement with Gulf States Utilities Co. over disputed contracts for the sale of power. The agreement, if approved by the companies' boards, the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission and others, will terminate litigation that began in July 1986 when Gulf States asked Southern Co. to renegotiate contracts to buy approximately $1 billion in electricity from Southern Co.

The agreement calls for Gulf States, of Beaumont, Texas, to give Southern Co., of Atlanta, as much as $300 million in cash, common stock and deferred payments. In a refinement of the initial agreement, Gulf States agreed to pay a penalty to Southern Co. unless the price of Gulf States stock reaches $18.25 a share for five consecutive days before Jan. 1, 1993. In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Gulf States stock closed at $10.125 a share, down 12.5 cents.

Southern Co. said it agreed to exercise voting rights on Gulf States stock only "in very limited circumstances."
