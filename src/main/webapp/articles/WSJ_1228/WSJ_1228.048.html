Money-Fund Assets Dropped $1.11 Billion During Latest Week

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/28/90

WASHINGTON -- Assets of the nation's 533 money-market funds fell $1.11 billion to a total $427.90 billion, in the week ended Wednesday, according to the Investment Company Institute, a Washington-based trade association. Assets of 267 general-purpose funds fell $226.3 million to $150.41 billion and assets of 104 broker/dealer funds fell $251.9 million to $166.02 billion. Assets of 162 institutional funds fell $627.6 million to $111.47 billion.

Assets of money-market funds didn't change significantly in the general-purpose and broker/dealer categories, but institutional assets fell moderately, after five consecutive weeks of large increases, "probably in response to some firming of short-term interest rates," said Jacob Dreyer, vice president and chief economist at the institute.

Money-market mutual fund assets for the previous week ended Dec. 19 were revised to $429.01 billion, and the increase in assets was revised to $1 billion.
