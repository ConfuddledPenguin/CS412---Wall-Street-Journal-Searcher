International: Sweden's Nobel, FFV To Merge Arms Units

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/28/90

STOCKHOLM -- The restructuring of Sweden's defense industry continues, with an announcement that Nobel Industrier Sverige AB and Foerenade Fabriksverken, the state-owned arms maker known as FFV, agreed to pool most of their defense-related operations under a new jointly owned company. The new company, to be called Swedish Ordnance-FFV/Bofors AB, will begin operations Tuesday with 6,000 employees and estimated annual sales of five billion Swedish kronor ($873 million). Nobel's AB Bofors unit has long been Sweden's biggest defense contractor. Swedish Ordnance will control virtually all arms output except the manufacture of military aircraft and engines by Saab-Scania AB and AB Volvo.

The Nobel-FFV accord reflects swift consolidation among Swedish defense contractors in recent years. Sweden's military has cut back spending, and international competition has intensified on the limited number of export markets open to defense contractors under Sweden's tight arms export controls.

During the past 18 months, Nobel has acquired the defense electronics operations of the Swedish unit of N.V. Philips and formed a military electronics venture with Telefon AB L.M. Ericsson. Last month, FFV and Volvo's aircraft engine unit combined their military aircraft maintenance operations under a company 51% controlled by Volvo.

FFV and Bofors have been talking for months about merging their operations.

Anders Carlberg, Nobel's chief executive, cited missile production -- where Bofors competes head on with Saab-Scaniaas virtually the only remaining defense sector where costly duplication could be eliminated.

The Nobel chief said that no talks are under way with Saab-Scania or are likely to begin soon.
