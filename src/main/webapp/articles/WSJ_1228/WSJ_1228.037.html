Letters to the Editor: NAS Didn't Endorse Soviet Doctors' Report

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/28/90

Your Nov. 28 editorial "Scientific Fraud?" discussed a Soviet scientific presentation made at the National Academy of Sciences in April 1987 (not 1986 as the editorial states). It is important to clarify for your readers that the visit of the Soviet doctors was not an event sponsored by the National Academy of Sciences. Rather, it was privately sponsored by Matthew Meselson, who is an NAS member. Dr. Meselson was permitted to hold the Washington session at the NAS, consistent with academy policy to allow its members to use academy facilities for scientific meetings. This does not, however, imply any academy endorsement of the information or viewpoints expressed in the meetings. Neither the academy nor any of its committees have taken a stand on the veracity of the Soviet doctors' explanation of the events in Sverdlovsk in 1979. We do, of course, urge and welcome complete openness from the Soviet Union to address any lingering concerns regarding past and current Soviet compliance with the Biological Weapons Convention to which it is a party.

Frank Press

President

National Academy of Sciences

Washington
