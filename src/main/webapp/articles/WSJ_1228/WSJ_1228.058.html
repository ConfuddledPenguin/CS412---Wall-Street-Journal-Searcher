U S West Group Sets Pact For Moscow Cellular Service

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/28/90

NEW YORK -- A group including U S West Inc., Millicom Inc. and a Russian eye surgeon signed a preliminary agreement with the Soviet Union's Ministry of Communications to provide cellular phone service in Moscow. The companies and Millicom's Soviet partner Svyatoslaz Fyodorov, a Moscow-based eye surgeon and entrepreneur, are still negotiating a definitive pact, including financial terms and a network equipment supply contract, according to a Millicom spokeswoman.

However, she said the companies expect to begin service by the third quarter of 1991 and plan to jointly bid on cellular service projects in other Soviet cities.

U S West, Englewood, Colo., is one of the regional Bell telephone companies. Millicom, New York, is a cellular and paging services company.

Moscow's cellular plan calls for the group, including U S West International Holdings Inc., a U S West unit, and Millicom International Cellular S.A.R.L., a Millicom affiliate, to run one of two networks in the Soviet capital. The group also includes the Moscow Territorial Operational Center for Long Distance and International Circuits, Voronezh Research-Production Association and the Moscow Local Telephone Network.
