Tandy's McDuff Unit Agrees to Pay a Fine In Case on Ad Claims

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/28/90

DALLAS -- McDuff Electronics & Appliances Supercenters, a division of Tandy Corp. of Fort Worth, has agreed to pay a $15,000 fine and refrain from making misleading advertising claims. In a complaint brought by the Texas attorney general's office, McDuff was accused of advertising sales in which products were priced as little as 0.10 of a percentage point below the normal price. "If you advertise a sale, the discount must be at least 10% less than the normal price," said Assistant Attorney General Steve Gardner.

Among other practices, McDuff was also accused of calling certain prices "the lowest of the year" when they were equal to prices before and after the sale.

Although the company didn't admit to any wrongdoing in accepting the final judgment, it accepted a list of restrictions on its advertising practices and agreed to provide proof it is conforming with the judgment until the end of 1992.

In its ads McDuff must make clear all wording that indicates a sale and must include a prominent explanation of its pricing policy when using the legend "Nobody Undersells McDuff." The $15,000 fine is intended to reimburse the attorney general's office for its investigative costs.

McDuff president Victor J. Sholis referred comment to a spokesman, who didn't return telephone calls.
