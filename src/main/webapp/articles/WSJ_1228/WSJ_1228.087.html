Thomas Gamel Quits Position as Director Of Hollywood Park

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/28/90

INGLEWOOD, Calif. -- Thomas W. Gamel said he resigned as a director of Hollywood Park Operating Co., a race track operator that's in the midst of a proxy and legal battle. In a Dec. 26 letter attached to a filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission, Mr. Gamel said he was resigning because he had been "shut out of the board's deliberations and decisions" by Chairwoman and Chief Executive Officer Marjorie Everett and her supporters on the board.

Mr. Gamel became a director last year after threatening his own proxy fight and calling for the removal of Mrs. Everett. He subsequently withdrew his rival slate when he was offered a seat on the board, and agreed he wouldn't acquire more stock while he remained a director. In August, Mr. Gamel reported that he had a 5.6% stake in the company.

According to the SEC filing, Mr. Gamel delivered a proxy and consent forms to investor Randall D. Hubbard, allowing his shares to be voted in favor of Mr. Hubbard's proposal to replace members of the board with Mr. Hubbard's own eleven-person slate. The company has sued Mr. Hubbard, claiming violations of the securities laws.

Mr. Gamel remains a director of Hollywood Park Realty Enterprises Inc., a real estate investment trust that rents out the track site and other property. The realty and operating companies are managed separately, but their shares are paired for trading in the national over-the-counter market.

Spokesmen for Mr. Gamel and the company couldn't be reached for comment.
