International: Isosceles to Receive Up to $446.1 Million To Refinance Debt

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 12/28/90

LONDON -- Isosceles PLC, the takeover vehicle created for the 1989 leveraged buy-out of British grocery chain Gateway Corp. PLC, has secured a debt-refinancing package including as much as #237 million ($446.1 million) in fresh equity capital. The refinancing, which has been under negotiation since mid-1990, calls for the issue of 12.99 million new shares based on units of values at #18.27 each. For every 30 ordinary shares held, 19 units will be issued.

Of the issue, #222 million has been underwritten by U.S. investment boutique Wasserstein Perella & Co. and a consortium of U.K. investors, including Isosceles creditors.

Under the share offering, Wasserstein Perella's stake in Isosceles will rise to 40% from 19.9%, with the U.K. consortium holding as much as 46.5% compared with its current 19.9% holding. The U.S. investment firm's contribution to the refinancing is #115 million against #107 million from the U.K. consortium led by Mercury Asset Management PLC, a fund management unit of S.G. Warburg & Co.

Proceeds will be used to retire company debt, dropping its total indebtedness to #1.2 billion from about #1.4 billion presently.

Besides the fresh capital, creditors have also agreed to reschedule payments on #592 million of senior debt initially due in 1991 and 1992 to August 1996. Also, creditors agreed to defer #48 million in interest due on subordinated debt over the next two years.

Isosceles said it has also agreed to buy back about #87 million of the subordinated, or so-called mezzanine debt, while mezzanine financers have agreed to waive certain equity warrants held against the company.

U.K. merchant bank S.G. Warburg & Co. has also agreed to provide Isosceles with #15 million in working capital once shareholder approval of the refinancing plan is secured.

Other members of the U.K. consortium are: Standard Chartered PLC, a major British commercial bank; 3i PLC, a British investment firm owned by major British banks and the Bank of England; Globe Venture Nominees Ltd., a fund management group; and Murray Johnstone Ltd., a U.K. investment firm.

Great Atlantic & Pacific Tea Co., the U.S. grocery chain that was among the initial Isosceles investors at a 19.9% stake, isn't participating in the fresh equity portion of the refinancing, Isosceles said.
