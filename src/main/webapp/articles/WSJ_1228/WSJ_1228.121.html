Russian Republic's Chief Of Finance Steps Down

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/28/90

MOSCOW (AP) -- Russia's finance minister has resigned, saying the largest and richest Soviet republic has made no progress in the transition to a market economy, the Russian legislature's newspaper reported. Boris Fyodorov, 33 years old, was quoted as telling Rossiiskaya Gazeta that lawmakers in the Russian republic have been unwilling to take responsibility for necessary but unpopular measures, such as lifting artificial price controls.

Mr. Fyodorov told the newspaper that important decisions, such as forming private companies, setting up a stock market and bringing Russian accounting practices in line with international norms, "get bogged down in the depths of the apparatus . . . for months on end."

"This is a shortcut to financial bankruptcy for the republic and for the {Soviet} Union as a whole," he said.

On Nov. 1, the Russian Federation began a program to switch from a centrally planned, command economy to a free market within 500 days. Russian lawmakers said they wanted to move faster than the national government, but critics contend their policies often conflict and create confusion rather than reform.
