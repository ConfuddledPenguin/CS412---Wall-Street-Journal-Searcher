International: Japanese Political Figure Charged With Tax Evasion

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 12/28/90

TOKYO -- Toshiyuki Inamura, a member of Japan's House of Representatives and a cabinet minister for the governing Liberal Democratic Party until he resigned Wednesday, was charged with evading taxes on stock profits. While the incident is yet another blow to the party's credibility, it could help Prime Minister Toshiki Kaifu in a way: The indictment will remind Japanese voters of an earlier political stock scandal -- the notorious Recruit Co. affair of two years ago. Mr. Kaifu is struggling against powerful party faction leaders who want to give ministerial posts back to Recruit-tainted politicians in a coming Cabinet shuffle. Renewed public anger about scandals could provide the ammunition Mr. Kaifu needs to appoint another slate.

The Tokyo Public Prosecutor's Office alleges that the 55-year-old Mr. Inamura failed to pay taxes of 1.72 billion yen, or $12.6 million, on profit of 2.88 billion yen, or $21.1 million, earned in share transactions between 1986 and 1988.

Mr. Inamura's physician, Akiyuki Yamaguchi, also was charged with evading 827.9 million yen in taxes in 1986-87, Chief Prosecutor Yusuke Yoshinaga said. The physician is charged with channeling inside information to Mr. Inamura from a third man who is being tried for allegedly manipulating stock prices.

Mr. Inamura was minister of state and director general of the Environment Agency in 1986-87. If found guilty, he could be fined as much as 3.2 billion yen in back tax and penalties, officials said.
