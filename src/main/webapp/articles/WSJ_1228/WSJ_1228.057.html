Acuson is Ordered To Pay $6.3 Million In Dispute Settlement

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/28/90

MOUNTAIN VIEW, Calif. -- Acuson Corp. said it was ordered to pay a $6.3 million award plus legal fees and costs as the result of an arbitrated settlement over a marketing dispute with Advanced Technology Laboratories, a unit of Westmark International Inc. At the center of the dispute are claims of false and misleading advertising made by both companies. The Acuson ad campaign compared the resolution of its ultrasound imaging with ATL's ultrasound equipment.

The advertisements appeared in brochures and trade magazines, said Robert J. Gallagher, Acuson's chief financial officer.

"We don't think any award is justified, and we certainly can't comprehend an award of this size," Mr. Gallagher said. He said the compay will investigate the arbitration process to make sure the rules were followed. Because both parties agreed to accept the decision of the private arbitrator, the only redress Acuson can seek against the judgment is to find an irregularity in the process.

Dennis C. Fill, the chairman and chief executive officer of Westmark, said he was pleased with the verdict but was legally enjoined from elaborating on the judgment.

Mr. Fill said a federal judge in Seattle who approved the arbitration will review the sales and advertising practices of both companies.

Acuson's stock closed at $27, down $1.625 in composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange. Westmark stock closed at $26.25, unchanged in national over-the-counter trading.
