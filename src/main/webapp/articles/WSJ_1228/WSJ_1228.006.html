Certain AT&T Rates To Drop Dec. 31, Jan. 1

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/28/90

NEW YORK -- American Telephone & Telegraph Co.'s long-distance rates will drop on New Year's Eve. AT&T said it will cut its standard weekday rates on interstate calls made from the continental U.S. by 32% on Dec. 31 and Jan. 1 between 8 a.m. and 11 p.m.

For instance, a 10-minute, direct-dialed daytime call from New York to Los Angeles that normally costs $2.49 will cost only $1.49 on New Year's Eve, said AT&T.

AT&T also said subscribers to its Reach Out America discount calling plans will get additional rate cuts depending on which plan they have.

AT&T's competitors MCI Communications Corp. and US Sprint Communications Co., a unit of United Telecommunications Inc., also will offer holiday discounts. On New Year's Day, Sprint said it will apply its lower evening rates to calls made between 8 a.m. and 5 p.m. Similarly, MCI will apply its lower night-weekend rates to holiday pricing.
