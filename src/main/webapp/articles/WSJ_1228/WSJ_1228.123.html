Czechoslovakia to Auction Small Shops, Businesses

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 12/28/90

PRAGUE -- The first round of auctions to privatize small shops and state-owned businesses is scheduled to begin next month, the state CTK news agency said. The privatization is part of the government's program of economic reforms and is designed to turn over to private hands some 130,000 small shops and enterprises as the nation's centralized economy is transformed into a Western-style economy.

The first auctions are scheduled for the last weekend in January in Prague.

Thousands of vendors across the country staged symbolic strikes in September demanding they be given first crack at buying the shops and a chance to pay as much as half the price in installments over a seven-year period.

However, the law approved by parliament in October excluded any provisions that would give preferential treatment to any potential buyer.

Auctions will be held in two rounds with the first one only for Czechoslovak citizens and those who left the country after 1948, when the Communists came to power.

Shops and enterprises that aren't sold in the first round will proceed to the second, in which some foreigners will also be allowed to bid. CTK said it would take at least a year to sell the shops and businesses.

Privatization of large industrial plants and the agricultural sector is expected to begin later in 1991.
