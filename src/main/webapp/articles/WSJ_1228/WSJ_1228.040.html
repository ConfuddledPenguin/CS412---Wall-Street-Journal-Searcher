Who's News: Procter & Gamble Creates Division For East Europe

  • Author: James S. Hirsch
  • Date: 12/28/90

Procter & Gamble Co., seeking to expand its presence in the potentially lucrative Eastern European market, created a new division to oversee its sales operations there. The Cincinnati consumer products giant said that effective Jan. 1, it would also subdivide its European Health and Beauty Care operation into three divisions, each responsible for different regions within Europe.

In recent months, Procter & Gamble has been laying the groundwork for a more aggressive stance in Eastern Europe by installing sales managers in Hungary, Poland and Czechoslovakia. Procter & Gamble had been cautious about expanding into the region, but that's changed as the Eastern bloc has moved to a more market-oriented economy. The company's ties to the region have been strictly through exports, but analysts speculate the company may also want to eventually build manufacturing plants there. The company won't comment.

Analysts believe Procter & Gamble and other consumer products concerns, such as Colgate-Palmolive Co., Unilever N.V. and Gillette Co., can take advantage of huge untapped markets in Eastern Europe, particularly after currency problems are ironed out.

"A lot of people in those countries don't have access to the type of high quality products that Procter & Gamble makes, and it doesn't take a lot of money to buy things like bath soap, detergent, toothpaste and diapers," said Bonita Austin, an analyst at Wertheim Schroder & Co.

Procter & Gamble named Herbert Schmitz, 54 years old and currently vice president of the company's Central Europe Health and Beauty Care unit, to head the newly created Eastern European division.

The company also named three executives to newly created positions in its European Health and Beauty Care division, effective Jan. 1. They are:

-- Michael A. Thompson, 48, currently division manager, Western Europe Health and Beauty Care, was named division manager, Northern Europe Health and Beauty Care, with responsibility for business in Belgium, the Netherlands, Scandinavia, the U.K. and the European Noxell shaving cream and Shulton personal-care operations.

-- Bernd E. Beetz, 40, currently general manager, Procter & Gamble Germany, was appointed division manager, Southern Europe Health and Beauty Care, with responsibility for business in Italy, France, Spain and Portugal.

-- Hans J. Honigfort, 44, currently general manager, Health and Beauty Care, Germany, was named division manager, Central Europe Health and Beauty Care, with responsibility for business in Austria, Germany and Switzerland.
