Letters to the Editor: Small Talk, Big Victory

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/28/90

Jeffrey Tannenbaum's article (Leisure & Arts, Nov. 21) makes us wish we would be on hand in Lyons for the great Kasparov-Karpov war. But then, upon reflection, we all know this is just another run-of-the-mill chess championship, despite all the media hype. Now a real titanic struggle, I submit, was seen in 1834 between Frenchman La Bourdonnais and Englishman A. McDonnell. What a shame that you did not have a reporter on hand back then. The Frenchman never stopped talking, swearing and joking about in his games, while McDonnell apparently was dullness incarnate as he took up to 90 minutes on a single move. He learned to utterly ignore the antics of his opponent as he planned strategies, sometimes involving queen sacrifices of a kind that Kasparov would admire.

I look forward to Mr. Tannenbaum's next article, when he puts the two Russian Ks in perspective. (Oh, by the way, La Bourdonnais was the victor with 45 wins, 27 losses and 13 draws])

Frank Vogl

Washington
