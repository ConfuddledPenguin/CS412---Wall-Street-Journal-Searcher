Business Brief -- Westpac Banking Corp.: Concern Offers $235 Million To Buy New Zealand Thrift

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/28/90

Westpac Banking Corp. of Wellington, Australia, said it offered to acquire Southland Building & Investment Society, New Zealand's biggest mutually owned savings institution with assets of about 400 million New Zealand dollars (US$235 million). A Westpac spokesman wouldn't disclose financial details of the offer. The 50,000 members of Southland are to hold an extraordinary meeting in February to consider the offer.

The proposed acquisition is the first expansion by Westpac in New Zealand in about seven years. If successful, Westpac intends to maintain Southland as a wholly owned but separate entity. An application to the Reserve Bank of New Zealand for a full banking license for Southland would be made if the bid is successful, Westpac said.
