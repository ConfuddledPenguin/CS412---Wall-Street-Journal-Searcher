Texaco Unit, Partner To Buy Properties From Canadian Firm

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/28/90

CALGARY, Alberta -- Texaco Canada Petroleum Inc. said it and a partner agreed to acquire certain British Columbia oil and gas properties from a unit of Imperial Oil Ltd., for 100 million Canadian dollars (US$86.2 million). Texaco Canada, 78%-owned by Texaco Inc., White Plains, N.Y., said the planned purchase from Imperial's wholly owned Esso Resources Canada Ltd. unit is subject to various conditions and approvals.

Texaco Canada and Mission Energy Co., a unit of SCEcorp of Rosemead, Calif., plan to form a joint venture to acquire and operate the properties, which contain an estimated 300 billion cubic feet of proven or probable natural gas reserves, and six million barrels of oil or gas liquids, Texaco Canada said.

Texaco Canada said the proposed purchase would increase its share of daily natural gas production by about 20 million cubic feet and its oil and gas liquids production by 450 barrels. The company currently produces about 7.5 million cubic feet of gas a day and 1,000 barrels of oil or gas liquids a day.

Separately, Texaco Canada said it agreed to acquire for C$6 million Esso Resources' oil and natural gas rights at 21 exploration properties, covering about 73,000 acres of undeveloped land, along with an option to drill four additional prospects and earn rights to a further 18,800 net acres. All of the exploration properties are in British Columbia, Texaco Canada said.

Texaco Canada Petroleum was formed in September 1988 to hold selected exploration assets that weren't included in the $4.15 billion sale of Texaco's interest in Texaco Canada Inc. to Imperial Oil. That transaction received final regulatory approval in February.

Imperial Oil is 70%-owned by Exxon Corp., New York.
