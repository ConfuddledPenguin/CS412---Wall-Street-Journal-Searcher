Who's News: Harding Is Named A Class A Director Of the New York Fed

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/28/90

NEW YORK -- The Federal Reserve Bank of New York said it elected Barbara Harding a Class A director for a three-year term, beginning Jan. 1. Ms. Harding succeeds J. Kirby Fowler, president and chief executive officer of Flemington National Bank & Trust Co., Flemington, N.J. Ms. Harding is currently chairwoman of Phillipsburg National Bank & Trust Co., Phillipsburg, N.J., and is also president and chief executive of its parent holding company, Vista Bancorp Inc.

Cyrus R. Vance and Ellen V. Futter were reappointed Class C directors and renamed chairman and vice chairwoman of the New York Fed's board. Mr. Vance, a former U.S. secretary of state, is presiding partner at Simpson Thacher & Bartlett, a New York law firm. Mr. Futter is president of Barnard College. Their reappointments are for 1991.

The nine members of this bank's board, as at the other 11 Federal Reserve Banks, are grouped into three classes. Class A and B directors are elected by small and medium-sized member banks, respectively. Class C directors are appointed by the board of governors of the Federal Reserve System in Washington, D.C.
