Marketing & Media: LBJ Co. Radio Stations Are Put on Selling Block

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/28/90

AUSTIN, Texas -- A pair of radio stations owned by the family of former President Lyndon Baines Johnson have been put on the block. But like many Texas properties, they aren't worth as much as they used to be. Austin stations KLBJ-AM and KLBJ-FM have been valued at $13.5 million by the Ted Hepburn Co., which has been retained by the LBJ Co. to entertain offers for the properties.

As recently as 1987, though, the family had received an offer of $27.5 million from the Chase Group, a New York investment company. Chase didn't meet the deadline for closing the transaction, and the deal fell through.

John Barr, executive vice president of LBJ Co. said the stations were profitable. He attributed the apparent steep decline in the station's value to "the lack of capital and borrowing ability of investors across the country." He added, "This isn't a distress sale," although the family would sell if it got the right price. The Johnson family has owned the stations for over 40 years.

The LBJ Co. also has interests in communications, cable television and real estate.
