Business Brief -- Qume Corp.: Computer Peripherals Maker Posts Fiscal 3rd-Period Loss

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/28/90

Qume Corp. said write-downs and other unusual costs totaling $8.4 million resulted in a loss of $12.7 million, or $1.13 a share, for the third quarter ended Nov. 30. In the year-earlier period, the Milpitas, Calif., maker of computer printers and other peripheral equipment earned $1 million, or nine cents a share.

The loss reflects the cost of a failed buy-out transaction, inventory write-downs and other costs, Qume said.

The failed buy-out of the company by David S. Lee, Qume's chairman and chief executive officer, and a group of investors cost $1.4 million, the company said. For the nine months ended Nov. 30, buy-out costs were $2 million. Net inventory declined $10.5 million during the quarter, which included an inventory write-down of $5.7 million.

Quarterly revenue dipped 2.3% to $54.7 million from $55.9 million.
