SCEcorp Says Agency Criticized FERC Order Rejecting Merger Plan

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/28/90

ROSEMEAD, Calif. -- SCEcorp said the Justice Department's antitrust division filed a brief criticizing a Federal Energy Regulatory Commission judge's ruling rejecting SCE's proposed merger with San Diego Gas & Electric Co. FERC administrative law judge George P. Lewnes last month rejected the proposed $2.56 billion merger as "monopolistic" and "anti-competitive." But in its brief filed yesterday, the antitrust division of the Justice Department said Judge Lewnes's decision was based in part on "misperception and misapplication" of Justice Department merger guidelines. It added that the two companies "have shown that the benefits outweigh the costs of the merger."

SCE filed its own brief with the energy commission, citing agreements it has reached with the Justice Department "that would adequately resolve all competition issues posed by the merger."

Judge Lewnes and FERC spokesmen couldn't immediately be reached for comment.
