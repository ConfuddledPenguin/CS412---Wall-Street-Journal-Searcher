Business Brief -- Dynatronics Laser Corp.: Firm Sees Quarterly Results Close to Break-Even Level

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/28/90

Dynatronics Laser Corp. said a shipping delay in two new product lines will cut profit in the second quarter, ending Monday, to near break-even. In the year-ago quarter, the medical devices company earned $92,696, or one cent a share, on sales of $1.6 million.

The delay of the product lines -- medical rehabilitation exercise equipment and software -- until after the start of the new year was caused by extended field tests and the shortage of parts, company officials said in Salt Lake City.

The company cited increased expenses and reduced sales caused by the delay as reasons for lower-than-expected second-quarter financial results.
