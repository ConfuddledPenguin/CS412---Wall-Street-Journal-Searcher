Business Brief -- Alliance New Europe Fund Inc.: Final Vote Is Postponed On Open-End Fund Switch

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/28/90

At its first meeting of shareholders yesterday, Alliance New Europe Fund Inc., New York, postponed until early next year a final vote on whether to convert to an open-end investment company from a closed-end fund. A closed-end fund has a fixed number of shares that trade either at a discount or premium to the value of its assets. An open-end fund issues shares continuously based on investor demand, and redeems the shares directly from investors who want to sell.

The meeting was adjourned until Jan. 10 to allow more time to receive proxies from shareholders, the company said. Management's open-end fund proposal requires the support of 75% or more of the shares outstanding for approval. At the meeting, David H. Dievler, chairman, said 65% of the fund's shares had voted, and of those, 62% favored converting to an openend investment company.
