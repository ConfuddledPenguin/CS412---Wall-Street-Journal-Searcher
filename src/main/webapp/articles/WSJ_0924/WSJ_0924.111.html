Business Brief -- Security Pacific Corp.: Firm Acquires Some Assets, Liabilities of Failed Thrift

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/24/90

Security Pacific Corp. said it paid $44.4 million to assume certain assets and liabilities of Mercury Federal Savings & Loan Association, the Huntington Beach, Calif., thrift that was seized by the federal Resolution Trust Corp. last February. Los Angeles-based Security Pacific said it is acquiring $1.4 billion in total deposits, including $1.3 billion in core deposits. It is also assuming $340 million in loans from the thrift, most of them residential loans.

The purchase price, which is slightly more than 3% of core deposits, appears to be at the lower end of what banks have been paying for core deposits of failed thrifts in recent months.

Mercury Federal had total assets of $1.7 billion and total liabilities of $1.8 billion, as of July 31.

Security Pacific is the parent company of Security Pacific National Bank.
