International Brief -- Chargeurs S.A.: Profit Surged in First Half; Gain From UTA Sale Cited

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/24/90

French industrial holding company Chargeurs S.A. posted a 67% rise in net profit after payments to minority interests in the first half of 1990, largely as a result of gains from the sale of airline UTA to Air France. The company said net profit came to 1.54 billion francs ($292 million), up from 921 million francs in the year-ago half. Chargeurs recorded a one-time capital gain of 1.55 billion francs from the UTA sale.

Disregarding financial gains, the group's operating profit was actually down 48% to 219 million francs from 422 million a year earlier.

Consolidated revenue, meanwhile, fell 41% to 6.11 billion francs from 10.47 billion. The latest figure takes account of a marked decline in textile sector sales.
