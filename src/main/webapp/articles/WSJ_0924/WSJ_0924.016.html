Hilton Hotels, In Change of Heart, Plans Buy-Back

  • Author: Pauline Yoshihashi
  • Date: 09/24/90

BEVERLY HILLS, Calif. -- Hilton Hotels Corp., whose stock has been battered by weak earnings and worries about the travel and tourism industries, announced plans to buy back as many as six million common shares, or about 12.5% of the outstanding stock. The buy-back, part of a previously authorized plan, reflects an apparent change of heart by the hotel and casino operator's board. A week earlier, senior Hilton officials told a group of investors and analysts in Las Vegas, Nev., that the company had no plans for a buy-back despite the drubbing the stock has taken since hitting an all-time high of $115.50 last summer during the company's aborted auction process.

John V. Giovenco, who heads Hilton's casino operations, also told investors at the conference that operating profits for the third quarter ending Sept. 30 will decline about 50% because of increased competition and other problems. Hilton's stock closed at $30.25 Friday, up 87.5 cents in New York Stock Exchange composite trading.

This year, Hilton's board turned down proposals offering about $90 a share or roughly $4.3 billion for the company's profitable casinos and hotel operations. Last month, the company scrapped an offering of liquid-yield option notes with a principal amount of $650 million when the stock's decline made the offering impractical.

Analysts and institutional investors, stung by the recent barrage of bad news, were reserved about the announcement. "It's hard to say how aggressive they'll be on the buy-back, although it appears they'd be buying pretty close to the bottom," said Joseph Doyle, an analyst with Smith Barney, Harris Upham & Co.

"We're not supporting the price of the stock," said Leroy A. Judge, a Hilton vice president. "We simply see it as a good investment." The purchases will be made periodically in the open market or through negotiated purchases, and the current authorization may be extended beyond the current Dec. 31, 1990, expiration date if conditions warrant. Hilton has about 48 million commmon shares outstanding.
