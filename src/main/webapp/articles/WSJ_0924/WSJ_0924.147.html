Business Brief -- Boston Edison Co.: Court Reverses Judgment Towns Won From Firm

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/24/90

Boston Edison Co. said the U.S. Court of Appeals in Boston reversed a $39.3 million antitrust judgment against the company. The company last year was ordered to pay the damages to the towns of Concord and Wellesley, Mass., after a jury found that wholesale rates the utility charged the towns violated antitrust laws. The towns alleged price discrimination, anticompetitive restrictions and price squeezing.

The appeals court, however, noting that the towns could have purchased power from other suppliers, said the towns failed to prove that Boston Edison had a monopoly.

Boston Edison said that the 1989 judgment hadn't been reflected in the company's financial statements, and thus the appeals court decision won't affect the company's earnings.
