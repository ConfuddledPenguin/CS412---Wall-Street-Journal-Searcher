RJR Nabisco Agrees to Sell More Cigarettes to Soviets

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/24/90

WINSTON-SALEM, N.C. -- RJR Nabisco Inc., which already has plans to ship 14 billion cigarettes to the Soviet Union, said it agreed to ship an additional 3.2 billion smokes. The food and tobacco company, controlled by Kohlberg Kravis Roberts & Co., also said it agreed in principle to expanded long-term cooperation with the Soviet tobacco industry. The agreement includes technical assistance to improve manufacturing facilities throughout the Soviet Union.

Analysts estimated RJR's original plan to provide 14 billion cigarettes to the Soviets would generate about $40 million in operating profit. In 1989, RJR's operating profit from tobacco exports totaled $328 million. The company declined to comment on the original estimate, or to project what the added tobacco shipments might contribute to earnings.

RJR said the first air-freight shipments of cigarettes are expected to begin this week, with cigarettes arriving on Soviet shelves before the end of the month. Shipments of the total 17.2 billion cigarettes, the company said, will continue over the next 12 months.
