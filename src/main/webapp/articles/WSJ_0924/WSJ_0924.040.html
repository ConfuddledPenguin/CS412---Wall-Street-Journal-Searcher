Upjohn Sells Rights To Brands to Roberts Pharmaceutical Corp.

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/24/90

EATONTOWN, N.J. -- Roberts Pharmaceutical Corp. said it purchased the rights to several brands in the U.S., Canada, Britain and Ireland from Upjohn Co., a health care company based in Kalamazoo, Mich. Terms weren't disclosed. Roberts, which acquires, develops and markets pharmaceuticals, acquired several respiratory and analgesic brands, including Cheracol and Orthoxicol cough syrups, Pyrroxate cold and allergy medicine, P-A-C aspirin tablets, and Haltran, an ibuprofen product.

It also purchased other nutritional brands such as the multivitamin supplements Zymacap, Sigtab and Super D Perles; the calcium supplements Calcium Lactate tablets and Calcium Gluconate tablets; the dietary supplement Diostsate D; and Lipomul, a product that increases calorie intake. Roberts also bought the brand Clocream, a hand cream.

For the nine months ended March 31, Roberts, which went public last January, had a net loss of $3.3 million, or 65 cents a share, on sales of $972,500.

In national over-the-counter trading Friday, Roberts shares closed at $3.75, up 25 cents.
