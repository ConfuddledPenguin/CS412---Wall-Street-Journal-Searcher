NL Steps Up Effort Against Lockheed Poison-Pill Plan

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/24/90

DALLAS -- Barred from meeting in person with a special committee of the Lockheed Corp. board, NL Industries Inc. sent the group a videotape last week attacking Lockheed's poison-pill plan to deter hostile takeovers. The videotape marks the latest move in Dallas financier Harold Simmons' campaign to increase his stake in the nation's sixth largest Pentagon contractor. Mr. Simmons, who lost a proxy battle for control of Lockheed in April, controls a 19.8% stake in the company through NL Industries, a Houston chemical concern that he controls.

Under the poison-pill provision, acquisition by Mr. Simmons of more than 20% of Lockheed's stock could trigger the issuance of shares that would dilute his stake. Mr. Simmons has asked Lockheed to amend the provision and allow him to buy 10 million Lockheed shares now controlled by the trustee of Lockheed's employee stock-ownership plan.

In a lawsuit filed in federal court in Los Angeles last April, NL attacked the plan as an attempt by Lockheed management to entrench itself. Also included in last week's SEC filing was a previously sealed amended complaint involving that lawsuit.

The amended complaint charged that Lockheed had hidden from shareholders the potential size of the plan, which NL claimed is operating as "a creeping, management-led, employee buy-out of the company."

In Calabasas, Calif., a Lockheed spokesman said it considered the NL complaint "to be without merit and simply more harassment."
