Business Brief -- Salant Corp.: Quarterly Deficit Widens At New York Apparel Firm

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/24/90

Salant Corp., an apparel maker operating under protection of the bankruptcy laws, reported its fiscal second-quarter net loss widened to $33.3 million from nearly $9 million a year earlier. On a per-share basis, Salant's loss for the second period, ended June 26, amounted to $9.64 compared with $2.62. Sales, which were hurt by weakness at the retail level, fell 31% to $77.4 million from $101.4 million. Sales also were interrupted in the two weeks before Salant filed its bankruptcy-law petition, the company said.

For July, the New York company posted nearly $2.4 million in profit on $43.2 million of sales. The profit reflected factors including the elimination of interest expense on Salant's debt; the company neither accrues nor pays such interest while it operates under Chapter 11 of the Bankruptcy Code.

Salant also estimated that in August it earned more than July's profit on some $54 million of sales.
