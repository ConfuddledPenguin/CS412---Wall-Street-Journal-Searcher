Phoenix Technologies Expects to Report Loss For September Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/24/90

NORWOOD, Mass. -- Phoenix Technologies Ltd., continuing to struggle, said it expects to post a loss of about $8.5 million for the fiscal fourth quarter, ending Sept. 30, in part because of a restructuring charge. Phoenix, which makes software for personal computers, has been hurt by diversification moves that haven't worked out and by increased competition. But it said it hopes to return to profitability "before the end of fiscal 1991" next September.

Phoenix said it expects to report a loss before restructuring charges of about $2 million for the fourth quarter, and a loss after a restructuring charge, bad debts and taxes of about $8.5 million. In the year-ago fourth quarter, the company has a loss of $11.9 million after a restructuring charge of $3.6 million. Revenue will rise to about $10 million from $4.5 million a year ago.

The restructuring charges are largely the write-off of fixed assets and related facilities costs.

Ron Fisher, president and chief executive officer, said the company's plan calls for it to become profitable by the end of next fiscal year by becoming more efficient. The company has cut expenses while putting more resources into growing market segments, such as notebook PCs, printer controllers and graphics-oriented PCs, he said.

The fourth-quarter loss means that the company will have a substantial loss for the current year; through the first nine months, it had a loss of $14.2 million, including a restructuring charge of $1 million, on revenue of $25.8 million. For all of fiscal 1989, Phoenix had a loss of $7.7 million, including a charge of $5.5 million, on revenue of $52.6 million.
