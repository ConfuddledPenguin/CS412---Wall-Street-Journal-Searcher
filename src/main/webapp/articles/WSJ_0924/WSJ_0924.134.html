Business Brief -- UCB S.A.: Company Expects '91 Profit Despite Surging Oil Prices

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/24/90

UCB S.A. should be able to produce a 1991 profit before exceptional items that is at least as high as this year's earnings, even with the recent surge in oil prices, the chairman of the Brussels chemical and pharmaceutical company's executive committee said. "We are clearly less vulnerable than other chemical companies," Georges Jacobs said in an interview. Higher oil prices shouldn't have much effect on UCB's specialty chemicals and pharmaceuticals, he said.

The oil surge will raise raw-material costs for UCB units producing special polypropylene films used in food packaging, but the effect will be minor in terms of the entire company's results, he said. UCB's production of cellulose film, also used to wrap food, is partially shielded from higher oil costs because the company has converted its plants to burn coal, Mr. Jacobs said.

For the first half of 1990, UCB reported group net profit of 1.31 billion Belgian francs ($41 million), up 31% from 1 billion francs ($31 million) a year earlier. For the full year, UCB has projected its profit before exceptional items will equal or moderately exceed 1989's earnings of 2.26 billion francs.
