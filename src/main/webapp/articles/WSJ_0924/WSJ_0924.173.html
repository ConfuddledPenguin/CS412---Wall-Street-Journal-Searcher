French Authority To Probe TV Station's Proposed Reshuffling

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/24/90

PARIS -- The top French broadcasting authority will examine proposals this week for a power reshuffle in faltering young television station La Cinq. But the plans, put to the Supreme Audiovisual Council last Friday, could run into problems because they hand control of the station to Hachette, a media group that already owns a hefty chunk of French publishing and broadcasting interests.

The Council warned Hachette earlier this year, when it acquired an initial 22% stake in La Cinq, that it was concerned by the concentration of media power. According to the proposals now on the table, Hachette would raise its stake to 25% and friendly banks would take a further combined 20%, giving it effective majority control.

At the same time publisher Robert Hersant, the controlling shareholder who submitted the reshuffle plans to the Council, would cut his stake to 10% from 25%. Under French law, no shareholder may own more than a quarter of any television channel's capital.

The future of unprofitable La Cinq, which began broadcasting in 1986, has been up in the air since Mr. Hersant successfully fought off last year's bid for control by Italian media magnate Silvio Berlusconi and French businessman Jerome Seydoux. Their failure gave Hachette chairman Jean-Luc Lagardere, who has been seeking a role in French television for several years, his opportunity to move in, acquiring 22% of the station in May.

Mr. Hersant, whose press empire includes Le Figaro, one of France's largest newspapers, was also believed to be disenchanted with the channel's poor financial performance. Despite heavy investment, audience ratings remain low at La Cinq and the station is estimated to be running a 2.2 billion franc ($416.9 million) deficit this year.
