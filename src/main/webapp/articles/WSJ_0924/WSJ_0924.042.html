Analog Devices Inc. Charge of $12 Million To Result in Losses

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/24/90

NORWOOD, Mass. -- Analog Devices Inc. said it will take a pretax charge of about $12 million against its venture-capital portfolio that, with a previously announced charge, will result in losses for the fiscal fourth quarter and year ending Nov. 2. Ray Stata, president and chairman, said the "soft market and economic climate for early-stage, high-technology companies" prompted the company to take a charge against its venture portfolio. The value of the portfolio is about $13 million.

Mr. Stata said "volatile performance" of companies in the portfolio has produced a "random and unpredictable" effect on Analog's earnings. A spokesman added that Analog probably won't make future venture-capital investments because "it has been a tougher deal than we anticipated."

The charge will be in addition to a previously announced pretax charge of $12 million to $15 million that the company expects to take as a result of "planned restructuring and inventory adjustments" following its acquisition of Precision Monolithics.

Joseph Hinchey, senior vice president, finance, said the company remains in "very strong financial condition" with healthy cash flow and debt at 8% of equity.

Analog, which makes integrated circuits and other electronic parts, had net income of $469,000, or one cent a share, on sales of $110.9 million in the fiscal fourth quarter ended Oct. 28, 1989. For all of fiscal 1989, it had net of $27.9 million, or 58 cents a share, on sales of $453.4 million.
