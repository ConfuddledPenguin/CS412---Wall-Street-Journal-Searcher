Credit Ratings: Michigan National's Debt Ratings Reduced

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/24/90

FARMINGTON HILLS, Mich. -- Moody's Investors Service Inc. said it lowered its ratings on about $54 million of long-term debt issued by Michigan National Corp. The rating concern downgraded the bank holding company's subordinated debt to Baa-3 from Baa-2. Moody's also lowered its ratings on the company's lead bank, Michigan National Bank, for long-term and short term deposits, and long-term and short-term issues backed by letters of credit.

Moody's said the soft real estate market in the Midwest has raised the risk of Michigan National's commercial real estate assets. It also cited Michigan National's "mediocre" core earnings. But Moody's said the holding company's liquidity level appeared to be "prudent."

The bank acknowledged the weakness in the real estate market. But it said there is no "specific event" at Michigan National to cause the downgrade. "The company continues to focus on maintaining a strong balance sheet," a spokesman said.

In national over-the-counter trading Friday, Michigan National shares closed at $24.50, down 25 cents.
