Environmentalists to Lobby Yosemite Park Concession

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/24/90

SAN FRANCISCO -- One week before the 100th anniversary of Yosemite National Park, a group of affluent environmentalists are expected to announce today its plan to start a company that might one day bid against the park's sole concessionaire, an entrenched institution that has been accused of rapidly reducing America's most beloved national site into a theme park. The environmentalists said that they would need to raise at least $200 million to launch their company, the Yosemite Restoration Trust, which would press for less construction and fewer cars in the park and, if all else fails, put in a bid for the federal concession contract when it comes up for renewal in 1993.

"This doesn't have anything to do with running a concession," said Bob Maynard, president of the Aspen Skiing Company and one of the environmentalists heading up the group. "The real interest is just to try to get the National Park Service and the concessionaire to try to work together to implement the master plan." This master plan, recommended by the Park Service in 1980, would curtail traffic, reduce lodging and move other buildings to the periphery of the park.

The current concessionaire, MCA Inc.'s Yosemite Park & Curry Co., has held the contract since 1973. In that time, it has made millions while paying the federal government only 0.75% of its annual revenue, among the lowest franchise fee paid by any national park concessionaire.
