International: Britain Lifts Asset Freeze On Kuwait's Biggest Bank

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 09/24/90

LONDON -- The Bank of England lifted its seven-week-old freeze on National Bank of Kuwait assets, agreeing to grant resident status to the bank's operations in Britain. The freeze, which Britain imposed on all Kuwaiti institutions when Iraqi forces invaded Kuwait on Aug. 2, had prevented the largest Kuwaiti bank from transferring any of its assets in Britain outside the country.

By receiving resident status, the Kuwaiti bank, which has been using its London branch as its operational headquarters, is able to conduct business more freely. The bank is still barred from transferring assets back to Kuwait or to Iraq, in line with an international embargo following the Iraqi invasion.

The bank expressed hope that Britain's decision will help "regulatory authorities in those few remaining countries where the operations of National Bank of Kuwait continue to be fettered by the impact of the freezing orders." It declined to specify these countries, though bankers mentioned Canada. The bank also has New York, Singapore and Bahrain branches, and Paris and Geneva financial-services units.
