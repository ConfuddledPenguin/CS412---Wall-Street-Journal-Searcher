Technology: LSI Logic to Unveil Chips It Says Will Cut Technology Costs

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/24/90

MILPITAS, Calif. -- LSI Logic Corp. said it will unveil today a set of chips that will drastically reduce the cost of video conferencing and other "multimedia" technologies. LSI said it will introduce a family of so-called full-motion video compression chips, chips that shrink video images into small amounts of data so that the images can be processed by telephone lines and computers that otherwise couldn't handle the information. Called the L64700 family, LSI's chips will help bring the cost of a typical teleconferencing system down to $5,000 in 1992 from about $50,000 today, said LSI Vice President Brian Halla.

Analysts see teleconferencing, which allows people in different locations to hold meetings by watching each other on TV screens, as a big growth market in the 1990s. And computers of the 1990's are expected to be used increasingly to display and edit movielike images. But such systems cost too much today for broad acceptance, in part because conventional technology can't squeeze moving images enough to fit on cheap telephone lines. LSI said a kit of its L64700 chips that function as a standard "codec," a coder and decoder of video information, will cost under $700.
