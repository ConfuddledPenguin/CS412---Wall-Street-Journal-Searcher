International Brief -- Broken Hill Proprietary Co.: Australian Company's Profit Gained 60% in First Period

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/24/90

Broken Hill Proprietary Co. said after-tax operating profit gained 60% in the fiscal first quarter, as results benefited from profit from the sale of investments. The Australian oil, steel and mining company said profit for the period ended Aug. 31 was 435.1 million Australian dollars (US$359.6 million), up from A$272.6 million a year earlier.

BHP said it had profit from the sale of investments of A$136.3 million, including an "abnormal" profit of A$114 million from selling shares in Woodside Petroleum Ltd. A year ago, profit from such sales was "immaterial."

Excluding these abnormal profits, BHP's earnings rose 18%.

Per-share earnings were 29.1 cents, up from 18.9 cents. BHP has raised its halfyearly dividend to 19.5 cents a share from 17.5 cents.

Sales and other revenue rose 25% to A$4.29 billion from A$3.42 billion.
