Business Brief -- Harley-Davidson Inc.: Recreational-Vehicle Firm Is Laying Off 180 at Unit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/24/90

Harley-Davidson Inc., signaling troubles in the recreational-vehicle market, said it is laying off 180 employees, or 10% of the work force at its Holiday Rambler Corp. subsidiary. Harley, based in Milwaukee, cited softness in demand for recreational vehicles and consumer skittishness prompted by the Middle East crisis. The company said the layoffs involved hourly and salaried employees.

The action doesn't appear to be an indication of wider troubles at the company, which has posted hefty profit increases, largely as a result of its strong motorcycle-manufacturing business.

Harley added that it expects to report third-quarter costs of $1.1 million as a result of closing two operations. The company said it is discontinuing Holiday Rambler's Aviator Van Conversions operations in Milwaukee and closing a Woodland, Calif., facility that produces Utilimaster, a commercial vehicle.

Harley said the two operations had 1989 combined sales of $8 million and only $1.6 million for the first six months of 1990.
