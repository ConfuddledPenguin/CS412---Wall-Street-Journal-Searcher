Technology: Parkinson's Disease Symptoms Cure Seen In New Treatment

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/24/90

ATLANTA -- Researchers at Emory University say a new treatment could lead to a cure for the symptoms of Parkinson's disease, the muscular ailment that affects more than 350,000 Americans. The research, published in the current issue of Science magazine, involves the subthalamic nucleus, a part of the brain that scientists previously regarded as being unconnected to Parkinson's.

By deactivating cells in the nucleus, however, researchers found they could relieve the tremors and loss of muscle control that characterize the disease.

The study indicates this particular part of the brain "is playing an important role in producing the signs and symptoms of Parkinson's disease," said Mahlon DeLong, a physician and chairman of the department of neurology at Emory. "Knowing this, we can design better forms of treatment for the disorder."

The next step will be to perform additional tests on monkeys, Dr. DeLong said, noting that surgical treatment or medicine for humans is still "some years away."

But he characterized the current research as encouraging. "It gives us a better idea of where the problem is," he said.
