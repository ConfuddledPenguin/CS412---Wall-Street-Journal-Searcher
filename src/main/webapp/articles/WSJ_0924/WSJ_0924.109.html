Japanese Economy's Expansion Continues

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 09/24/90

TOKYO -- Japan's economic expansion continues, owing to robust domestic demand led by personal consumption and corporate-equipment investment, the Bank of Japan said in its monthly report. The central bank's report, reviewing the country's monetary and economic trends, also said that mining and industrial production is on the rise, and that corporate business sentiment remains strong.

The Economic Planning Agency also painted a bright picture of the economy in its latest monthly report, making it official that the economy has entered into its 46th consecutive month of growth. The assessment means higher oil prices and rising interest rates as a result of the Middle East crisis have had little significant effect on Japan's economy.

The central bank's report said that "both manufacturers and non-manufacturers are maintaining a buoyant attitude toward investment plans. Personal consumption continues firm against the background of increasing disposable income, and housing starts are also at a high level."

The Bank of Japan believes prices are maintaining stability, even as it noted in its report that "domestic wholesale prices continued to rise, though by a small margin. . . . Consumer prices rose mainly due to higher perishable foodstuff prices."

Despite the price stability, Bank of Japan officials have warned that it is necessary to keep prices under close surveillance, as the effect of the Mideast crisis on the economy may be gradual.
