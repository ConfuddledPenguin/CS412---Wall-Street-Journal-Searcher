International Brief -- Hongkong & Shanghai Banking: Marine Midland Bank Unit Faces Additional Cutbacks

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/24/90

Hongkong & Shanghai Banking Corp., facing profit pressures, intends to continue trimming the operations of its unprofitable Marine Midland Bank operation in the U.S., John Gray, deputy chairman of the Hong Kong-based banking concern, said. The work force at Marine Midland, based in Buffalo, N.Y., has been cut by 1,400, or 10%, in the past year, Mr. Gray said. Its monthly operating costs have fallen to $70 million from $78 million at year end, and he said the figure will be lower by the end of 1990.

Mr. Gray also said profitability at Hongkong & Shanghai Banking, which owns large stakes in four Middle Eastern banks, hasn't been seriously affected by the Persian Gulf crisis. The company doesn't have operations in Kuwait or Iraq, he said, but does have "some exposure" to Kuwaiti banks, which he didn't specify. Hongkong Bank owns British Bank of the Middle East, 40% stakes in Saudi British Bank and Hongkong Egyptian Bank SAE, and 21% of Cyprus Popular Bank Ltd.
