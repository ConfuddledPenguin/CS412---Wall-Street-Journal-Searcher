Reichmanns' Plan For Oil Merger Stalled by Prices

  • Author: Gary Lamphier
  • Date: 09/24/90

Rising oil prices have stalled a proposal by Toronto's Reichmann family to create Canada's biggest domestically owned oil producer by merging Gulf Canada Resources Ltd. and Home Oil Co. The merger process "has been somewhat complicated" by the price surge set off by Iraq's Aug. 2 invasion of Kuwait, said Ronald Watkins, vice president, industry and government relations, for Interhome Energy Inc., Calgary, Alberta, which owns Home Oil. "Everyone has had to sit down and re-examine their oil price forecasts, which form the basis for valuation assumptions," he said.

Spot prices of West Texas Intermediate, a benchmark grade of U.S. crude oil, have risen more than $13 a barrel since the invasion and subsequent trade embargo against oil and other products from Iraq and occupied Kuwait.

The Reichmanns' plan, announced in April, called for the merger of Gulf Canada Resources, a Calgary-based oil and gas producer, and Home Oil this summer. It also sought the spinoff of Interhome's Interprovincial Pipe Line Ltd. unit as a separate public company by year's end.

The Reichmann family owns 74.3% of Gulf Canada through its real estate flagship, Toronto-based Olympia & York Developments Ltd. Companies controlled by the Reichmanns, including Gulf Canada, own a combined 63.5% of Interhome.

Gulf Canada and Home Oil would have joint daily output of 132,000 barrels of oil, more than any other Canadian-owned oil producer, and assets of about five billion Canadian dollars (US$4.3 billion).

But with the Iraqi situation unresolved and the outlook for oil prices unclear, a Gulf Canada spokesman asked, "How do you get an agreement on price?" He added that "the intention now is to see if we can go ahead with this merger before the end of the year." The proposed spinoff of Interprovincial could proceed at the same time, he said.

Financial advisers are expected to submit proposed terms for the merger next month to committees set up by Gulf Canada and Interhome. The committees will then submit the recommendations to their respective boards. The proposed terms should be disclosed shortly thereafter, the Gulf Canada spokesman said.

The delay in the merger won't have any direct impact on the Reichmann family's wealth, and the merger doesn't involve the sale of any assets to raise cash. The Reichmanns' energy assets are held separately from their vast real estate holdings.
