Business Brief -- Newell Co.: Concern Says Strike at Unit Could Cause Earnings Loss

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/24/90

Newell Co. said it "could have some negative impact on earnings" from a strike that has started at a unit's plant in Monaca, Pa. It didn't elaborate on the possible impact. Newell, the maker of home do-it-yourself products based in Freeport, Ill., said 450 employees at the plant have rejected a tentative labor agreement and are now on strike. The company said the employees are members of American Flint & Glass Workers Locals 512, 36, 67 and 544.

The plant is operated by Phoenix Glass, a unit of Newell's Anchor Hocking Industrial Glass division.

Newell said a cooling-off period is now in effect. The company said it's "hopeful" that negotiations will soon resume and an "acceptable" agreement can be reached.

Newell said the plant continues to operate with office and supervisory personnel. It said that "if necessary," the company can shift "some" production to Newell's Anchor Hocking Consumer Glass plant in Lancaster, Ohio.

Net income for the second quarter just ended fell to $21.9 million, or 36 cents a share, from $46.1 million, or 76 cents a share, during the year-ago period.
