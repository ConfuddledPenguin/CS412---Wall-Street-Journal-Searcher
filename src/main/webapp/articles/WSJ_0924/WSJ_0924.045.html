Law: U.S. Hires Cravath Swaine In Drexel Junk-Bond Case

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/24/90

WASHINGTON -- Federal regulators said they retained the New York law firm Cravath, Swaine & Moore to help try to recover millions of dollars of thrift and bank junk-bond losses from Drexel Burnham Lambert Inc. The agreement, which was expected, was sought by a Federal Deposit Insurance Corp. task force. The U.S. Bankruptcy Court in New York has set a Nov. 15 deadline for asserting claims against Drexel, which filed for protection from creditors under Chapter 11 of the U.S. Bankruptcy Code earlier this year. Recoveries could come from Drexel's bankruptcy estate or two restitution funds.

Cravath Swaine has committed itself to provide whatever legal staffing is necessary, including senior trial partners Thomas Barr and David Boies, who are among the country's best-known litigators.

The government contends that Drexel's former junk-bond chief, Michael Milken, helped thrifts that were big Drexel customers fraudulently swap high-risk, high-yield junk bonds and record artificial profits on the sales. No such charges have ever been filed against Mr. Milken or Drexel.
