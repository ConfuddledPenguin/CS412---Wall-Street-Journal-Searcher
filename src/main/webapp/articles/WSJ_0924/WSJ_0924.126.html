International Brief -- Jardine Matheson Holdings Ltd.: Hongkong Land Holdings Reports 32% Rise in Profit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/24/90

Hongkong Land Holdings Ltd. said its interim after-tax profit surged 32% in the first six months of 1990, helped by higher lease renewals despite the softening of the local property market. The Hong Kong landlord, which owns a swatch of the colony's most valuable downtown property, also said it intends to follow in the footsteps of its parent group, Jardine Matheson Holdings Ltd., and sister company, Dairy Farm International Holdings Ltd., by obtaining a listing on the International Stock Exchange in London and by keeping its accounts in U.S. dollars. The moves, which it said are aimed at enhancing its international corporate profile, come at a time when it is seeking to purchase properties overseas.

Hongkong Land's after-tax profit rose to US$129.6 million from $98.3 million in the year-earlier first half, equal to a 32% rise in per-share earnings to 5.10 cents from 3.86 cents. Turnover rose 35% to $176.9 million from $131.4 million.

The group's directors proposed raising its interim dividend to 2.75 cents from 2.05 cents a year earlier.
