William Galvin Sentenced To 32-Month Prison Term

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/24/90

WASHINGTON (AP) -- Defense consultant William Galvin was sentenced to 32 months in prison after pleading guilty to bribing two senior Pentagon officials to influence the awarding of lucrative military contracts. Mr. Galvin, a major figure in the defense procurement scandal, was also fined $10,000 by U.S. District Judge Claude M. Hilton in Alexandra, Va., on his guilty plea to charges of conspiracy, bribery and tax evasion.

Along with another 32-month prison term given to a former Unisys Corp. executive, Mr. Galvin's sentence was the longest imposed in the long-running probe of Pentagon procurement fraud.

Mr. Galvin, 59 years old, faced a possible 40-year prison sentence and fines of as much as $1 million. But prosecutors, citing Mr. Galvin's "extraordinary cooperation" with the investigation, asked Judge Hilton to impose a shorter prison term. Assistant U.S. Attorney Joseph Aronica told Judge Hilton that Mr. Galvin is expected to be "a critical witness in future prosecutions."

Mr. Galvin pleaded guilty in March to arranging payments through a Swiss bank account to then-Assistant Navy Secretary Melvyn R. Paisley for helping an Israeli company sell pilotless intelligence airplanes to the Pentagon. Mr. Galvin also said he paid $123,100 from 1983 to 1985 to a friend of Victor D. Cohen, then a deputy assistant Air Force secretary, knowing that some of the money would be given to Mr. Cohen to influence the awarding of a contract on radar-warning devices. Mr. Paisley and Mr. Cohen haven't been charged but both are under investigation.

Under his plea, Mr. Galvin agreed to pay the Internal Revenue Service $648,548 in back taxes and interest.
