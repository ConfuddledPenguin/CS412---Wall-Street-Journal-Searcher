International Brief -- Assicurazioni Generali S.p.A.: Earnings at Italian Insurer For the 1st Half Edged Up

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/24/90

Assicurazioni Generali S.p.A., Italy's largest insurance concern, said parent-company gross earnings for the first half rose less than 1% to 349.7 billion lire ($297.3 million) from 346.8 billion lire a year earlier. Generali, which has more than 50 units world-wide, cited a fundamental downturn in the insurance sector, especially in foreign sales and the Italian non-life sector. An official said increased inflationary pressure, exchange-rate instability and the plunge in financial markets following Iraq's occupation of Kuwait created concern about second-half results. Nonetheless, he predicted year-end performance to be "in line" with year-earlier profit, which rose 15% to 390.2 billion lire.

First-half gross premium income rose 7.2% to 2.923 trillion lire. Net income from investments rose 14% to 733 billion lire. Income from asset sales totaled 77.8 billion lire. Group aggregate premium income rose 8.4% to 7.311 trillion lire. Premium income from life insurance rose 8.5% to 1.109 trillion lire, and non-life premium income rose 8.6% to 1.814 trillion lire.
