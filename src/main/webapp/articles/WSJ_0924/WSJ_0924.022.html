Figgie Suspends Buy-Back

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/24/90

WILLOUGHBY, Ohio -- Figgie International Inc. suspended its share-repurchase program because of market conditions, the company said. The consumer, industrial and technical concern announced the buy-back program earlier this year to restore the balance between the number of Class A and Class B shares outstanding. Under the program, the company repurchased about 150,000 shares of Class B common, which have greater voting power.

Figgie had 18,909,822 average outstanding shares for the year ended Dec. 31, 1989.
