Tucson Electric Seeks Surcharge, 11.6% Rate Jump

  • Author: Frederick Rose
  • Date: 09/24/90

TUCSON, Ariz -- Tucson Electric Power Co., seeking "an immediate and positive sign to creditors," said it applied to Arizona regulators for an 11.6% rate increase and a further 2% temporary fuel surcharge. The requested increases, which would raise $55 million in permanent new annual revenue and $15.5 million through the temporary surcharge, are substantially lower than analysts have said is eventually needed to put the utility on a sound footing.

The application, filed Friday with the Arizona Corporation Commission, seeks to forestall an important reckoning of rate recovery for Tucson Electric's controversial new, $829 million Springerville 2 coal-fired generating plant.

In its application, the utility asked the commission for permission to defer about $100 million annually of interest and other expenses related to the facility. Tucson Electric said it expects to file for recovery of Springerville 2's costs late next year.

The application for cost recovery, which could mean a rate boost of 15% or more on top of the latest application, faces considerable resistance whenever it is filed. Two members of the three-member Arizona Corporation Commission have questioned whether the plant is needed in a region awash with generating capacity, raising the painful possibility that Tucson Electric might be barred from recovering some or all of its investment.

The wounded electric utility, Arizona's second largest after Arizona Public Service, which also filed for a rate increase Friday, said its requests for rate increases are "the initial step to returning Tucson Electric to a reasonably sound financial condition."

As previously reported, Tucson Electric had a net loss of $87.4 million, or $3.62 a share, on $233.1 million of revenue in the first six months of the year. The total included $27.4 million in losses from continuing operations and $59.9 million of one-time charges and losses from discontinued operations.

The utility said its request for a temporary 2% fuel surcharge is intended to recover about $15.5 million in fuel and purchased power expenses incurred between Jan. 1 and June 30, 1989, adding that it is seeking "expedited consideration" of the surcharge, in hopes that the levy could start in mid-November.

The Arizona Corporation Commission has twice dismissed Tucson Electric's requests for recovery of fuel expenses for the six months in question and the entire issue of fuel and purchased power recovery is the subject of litigation in state and federal courts in Tucson.

However, a company spokesman said an earlier commission order opened the way for the company to reapply for the money. "We are persistent," the spokesman said.

Tucson Electric last was granted a rate increase in October 1989 when the Arizona commission approved an 11.9% boost.
