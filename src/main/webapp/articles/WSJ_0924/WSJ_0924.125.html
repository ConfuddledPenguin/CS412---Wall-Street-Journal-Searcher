Business Brief -- Honda Motor Co.: Acura Integra '91 Models' Price Increases Restrained

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/24/90

Honda Motor Co.'s U.S. sales arm in Gardena, Calif., said base prices for 1991 Acura Integra models will range from $11,950 to $17,675, with some versions of the car showing no price increase from a year ago and others rising $500. The Integra is the Japanese auto maker's third-best selling car line in the U.S., and accounts for about 60% of the volume in its Acura luxury division. The Integra is a sporty car designed to compete with so-called near-luxury European models, such as BMW 3-series sedans. Honda's relatively restrained pricing for the 1991 Integra reflects increasing competition in this segment from European manufacturers and new Japanese entries such as the Infiniti G20, which will have a base price of $17,500.

The price of the least expensive Integra RS hatchback, $11,950, is unchanged from a year ago, as is that of the least expensive Integra RS four-door at $12,850. The better-equipped Integra GS four-door with automatic transmission will start at $17,175, up $500, or 3%. The rise for the GS sedan reflects an added sunroof.
