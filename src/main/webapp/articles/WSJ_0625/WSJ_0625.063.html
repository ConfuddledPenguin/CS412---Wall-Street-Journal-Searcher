Olivetti's Chairman Says Strength of Lira Will Hurt Sales, Profit

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 06/25/90

MILAN, Italy -- Carlo De Benedetti said the effects of the strong lira will cut into Ing. C. Olivetti & C. S.p.A.'s 1990 group sales by between 300 billion and 400 billion lire ($243 million to $324 million) from what they otherwise would have been. The chairman of the Italian computer and business machines group also said the strength of the lira will wipe about 100 billion lire off the group's net profit compared with what it might have been if the lira weren't so strong.

Mr. De Benedetti made the comments at Olivetti's annual meeting in Ivrea.
