Prime Reseources Chairman Refuses To Complete Deal

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/25/90

VANCOUVER, British Columbia -- Stock promoter Murray Pezim refused to complete a transaction that would have raised Corona Corp.'s stake in Prime Resources Group Inc. to 45% from 20%. Prime Resources owns 50% of Canada's hottest gold prospect. Mr. Pezim, Prime's chairman as well as honorary chairman of Corona, earlier vowed to derail the transaction unless Corona altered the terms. Terms call for Mr. Pezim and two other large Prime holders to swap their 10.2 million Prime shares for an equal number of Corona Class A shares.

The parties agreed to exend the closing date to July 5, though Mr. Pezim said he won't close unless Corona revises the pact. Corona's spokesman said it expects Mr. Pezim to stand by the initial agreement.

In Toronto Stock Exchange trading Friday, Corona's Class A shares, which have sagged in recent weeks, closed unchanged at seven Canadian dollars (US$5.95) each. Prime, which is listed on the Vancouver Stock Exchange, closed at C$9.62 a share, up 12.5 Canadian cents.

The private transaction is part of a complex plan under which Corona hopes to secure control of British Columbia's big Eskay Creek gold deposit, now widely acknowledged as the largest gold discovery in Canada since Ontario's Hemlo find in the early 1980s. Corona had hoped the purchase would help cement its control of Prime.

Meanwhile, Corona and rival Placer Dome Inc., Vancouver, are battling for control of Prime's Eskay Creek partner, Stikine Resources Ltd. Placer Dome has so far acquired about 45% of Stikine under a tender offer that was extended to July 4. For its part, Corona agreed to a lock-up pact with a group that owns 42% of Stikine and has offered to acquire the rest through a stock swap.

Mr. Pezim once controlled Corona's predecessor and played a big role in financing its early exploration at Hemlo. A Toronto group, led by Ned Goodman, Corona's chairman, acquired control of Corona in the mid-1980s.
