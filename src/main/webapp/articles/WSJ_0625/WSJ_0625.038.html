Hong Kong Toy Centre

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/25/90

HONG KONG -- Hong Kong Toy Centre International Ltd., a toy maker, said it will raise 92 million Hong Kong dollars (US$11.8 million) by selling 25% of its shares to the public. The 75 million shares in Toy Centre are to be priced at HK$1.23 each, representing a price-earnings ratio of six, based on projected annual profit of HK$50 million. The prospective dividend yield is 8.1%. Analysts said the P-E ratio is below the average of about seven for local industrial stocks.

After the offering is completed, Chairman Victor Lo and his family will hold 52.5% of the company, and Prudential Asset Management Asia Ltd. will own 22.5%.

Of the cash to be raised, HK$33 million is earmarked for debt repayment, HK$30 million for the purchase of plant and equipment, HK$6 million HK for toy development, and the rest for working capital.
