Bush Pledges to Intensify Attack on Financial Crime

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/25/90

WASHINGTON -- President Bush vowed to "leave no stone unturned" in an intensified federal attack on criminal fraud in the savings and loan and banking industries. Firing back at Democrats who have criticized his administration's efforts to prosecute white-collar crime, Mr. Bush told a gathering of hundreds of federal enforcement officials that the Justice Department will form new committees and task forces to coordinate S&L investigations and "throw the crooks in jail."

The president praised a variety of recent legislative proposals to help prosecutors go after financial crime, including one that would allow federal investigators to request court-ordered wiretaps in bank fraud cases. He chastized Congress for moving too slowly to appropriate additional money for criminal investigations of the deeply troubled S&L industry. And he proposed that Congress authorize $100 million for such investigations for fiscal year 1991, a $25 million increase over the amount currently authorized.

Congressional Democrats rejected the Bush assault, pointing out that for fiscal years 1989 and 1990, Mr. Bush didn't even propose spending the full $75 million that Congress had authorized for S&L enforcement. Speaking on behalf of the critics, Rep. Charles Schumer (D., N.Y.) said federal prosecutors have moved slowly in seeking indictments, creating a huge backlog of unaddressed cases.
