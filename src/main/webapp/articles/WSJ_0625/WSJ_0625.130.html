Ex-GM, Raytheon Executives Convicted In Defense-Data Case

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/25/90

WASHINGTON -- A former market-research manager at Raytheon Co.'s largest unit and a onetime marketing official at General Motors Corp.'s Hughes Aircraft subsidiary were convicted of conspiring to swap sensitive Pentagon planning documents. Frank Caso, who worked at Hughes until last year, and John Kiely, formerly a market-research manager for Raytheon's Missile Systems division, were convicted after a weeklong trial in federal district court in Alexandria, Va. Prosecutors asserted that, beginning in the mid-1970s, the two men conspired with employees of other defense contractors to illegally acquire and trade classified Pentagon documents. They face a maximum sentence of five years in prison.

Prosecutors presented new details about how such document-swapping over the years gave major defense contractors access to some of the military's most sensitive long-range planning materials. Six individuals and five companies, including Hughes, Raytheon and Boeing Co., previously were convicted of charges stemming from the investigation code-named "Operation Uncover." The grand jury investigation is continuing.

Mr. Caso was indicted on charges involving using a post office box to receive the documents and placing false titles in Hughes's document-accountability system to try to hide some of his activities. Mr. Kiely, who was an Air Force major before joining Raytheon in 1967, was accused of illegally copying classified documents in the firm's Arlington, Va., office as part of a scheme to share them with other defense contractors.
