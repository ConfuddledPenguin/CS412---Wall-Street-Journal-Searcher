Helionetics to Enter Computer Venture With Chinese Firm

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/25/90

IRVINE, Calif. -- Helionetics Inc., which emerged from bankruptcy-law proceedings in March 1989, said it plans to launch a joint venture with a Chinese company to build computer workstations in Shenyang City, an industrial center north of Beijing. Helionetics Chairman Bernard B. Katz said the venture, Shenyang Shenlong Computer Systems Co., would be financed with equal contributions of $600,000 in cash and equipment from Helionetics and from Northern Computer Application Development Co., a technology concern sponsored by the Chinese government.

Mr. Katz said that Helionetics was attracted to China because of the relatively inexpensive scientific talent there. "Physics is physics the world over, and price is the big factor" in selling computers, he said.

After riding the wave of the defense-industry buildup in the early 1980s, Helionetics' large debt load forced it to seek bankruptcy-law protection in 1986. Now the company is pinning its hopes on its computer business.

Under terms of the agreement, Helionetics said its recently acquired Definicon International Corp. unit will sell to the joint-venture certain advanced technology boards that are about to enter production. Helionetics hasn't yet received an export license from the U.S. Department of Commerce to sell the boards, a Helionetics spokesman said.

Mr. Katz said Helionetics has approval to export a less advanced board. These, or the more advanced board if government approval is granted, will be installed in personal computers made by the new company. The computers would be sold mainly in China, as well as in the U.S. and Europe, for scientific and engineering applications, Mr. Katz said.
