Dallas Semiconductor, SGS-Thomson Settle, Enter Patent Accord

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/25/90

DALLAS -- Dallas Semiconductor Corp. said that all lawsuits between it and SGS Thomson Microelectronics Inc. have been settled and that the two companies have entered into a comprehensive, patent cross-licensing agreement. Dallas Semiconductor, a specialty chip maker whose personnel include several former employees of SGS-Thomson's predecessor, sued SGS-Thomson in state court in January over a 1986 agreement to swap proprietary information. SGS-Thomson responded with a suit in federal court, charging patent infringement.

Under terms of the patent cross-licensing agreement, confirmed by SGS-Thomson, all state court litigation has been dismissed, and a consent judgment has been entered in federal court.

Dallas Semiconductor said the cross-licensing agreement covers virtually every patent owned by both companies.
