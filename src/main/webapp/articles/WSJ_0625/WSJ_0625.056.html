Photronics Sells Out Public Offer, but Price Of Shares Later Drops

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/25/90

BROOKFIELD, Conn. -- Photronics Inc. sold out a public offering of 1,250,000 common shares at $10 apiece, but in over-the-counter trading Friday, the shares fell below the offer price. In national over-the-counter trading, shares of the manufacturer of photomasks fell 37.5 cents to $9.875.

Of the total, Photronics sold 1,050,000 shares and shareholders 200,000. Photronics will use its proceeds from the offering to cut debt and general purposes, said Senior Vice President Michael J. Yomazzo. Photronics now has nearly 4.4 million shares outstanding. Prudential-Bache Capital Funding, a group within Prudential-Bache Securities Inc., led the underwriters.

Photomasks are high-precision glass or quartz plates used to transfer circuit designs onto computer chips.

The selling shareholders were three Photronics executives: Constantine S. Macricostas, chairman, president and chief executive officer, who sold 90,000 shares, leaving him with 44.1%; Mr. Yomazzo, 50,000 shares, leaving 5.9%; and Vincent Santoro, 10,000 shares, leaving less than 1%.

Also selling 25,000 shares apiece were two trusts for Mr. Macricostas's children, leaving each trust with 3.7%.
