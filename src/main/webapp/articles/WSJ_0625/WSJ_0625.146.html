Canada's May Production Of Market Pulp Fell 8%

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/25/90

MONTREAL -- Canadian production of market pulp dropped 8% in May from a year earlier as the industry operated at 85% of capacity. The Canadian Pulp and Paper Association, an industry group, said Canadian mills produced 550,000 metric tons of market pulp in May, compared with 596,000 metric tons a year earlier.

The 85% operating rate in May was up from 76% in April but down from 94% a year earlier.

For the year to date Canadian mills produced 2.751 million metric tons of market pulp compared with 2.990 million metric tons a year earlier, an 8% decline. The industry operated at 87% of capacity in the first five months, down from 97% a year earlier.

Market pulp is wood pulp sold on the open market to producers of paper, sanitary and other products. The statistics exclude pulp consumed at the producing mill or shipped to another mill affiliated with the producing mill. Canada is the world's largest producer of market pulp.

Total market shipments in May dropped 15% to 488,000 metric tons from 573,000 metric tons a year earlier. Shipments to all markets were down.

Market shipments for the first five months dropped 13% to 2.528 million metric tons from 2.917 million metric tons a year earlier.

Canadian producer inventories at the end of May were 583,000 metric tons, a 35-day supply, compared with 521,000 metric tons, a 29-day supply, at the end of April. Producer inventories at the end of May 1989 were 310,000 metric tons, a 16-day supply.
