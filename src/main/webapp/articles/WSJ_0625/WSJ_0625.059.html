La Jolla Bancorp's Holders Back Merger With Security Pacific

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/25/90

SAN DIEGO -- La Jolla Bancorp said shareholders approved its agreement to merge with Security Pacific Corp. in an exchange of stock valued at about $110 million. La Jolla said 64% of its shareholders approved the merger at the company's annual meeting on Friday, while fewer than 2% voted against it. The agreement calls for the conversion, at a rate to be determined later, of each of La Jolla Bancorp's about seven million shares outstanding into the right to receive about $15 in market value of Security Pacific common stock.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading Friday, Security Pacific's stock closed unchanged at $38.50 a share. La Jolla Bancorp's stock closed at $13.875 a share, down 12.5 cents, in American Stock Exchange trading.

Security Pacific is a Los Angeles-based bank holding company. La Jolla Bank & Trust Co., La Jolla Bancorp's principal unit, has 12 branches in San Diego.

The two companies signed a letter of intent to merge last October and ratified a definitive merger pact in December. La Jolla Bancorp said it expects federal regulators to approve the combination in the next two months.
