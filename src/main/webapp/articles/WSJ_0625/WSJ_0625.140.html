International Brief -- Kumagai Gumi Co.: Construction Firm Predicts Drop in Fiscal 1990 Profit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/25/90

Kumagai Gumi Co., a major Japanese construction company, predicted that its profit in the fiscal 1990, ending March 31, would drop mainly because of higher running costs in the U.S. It estimated that pretax earnings would fall 10.3%, to around 41 billion yen ($265 million) from 45.685 billion yen in the past fiscal year, and net profit 9.9% to 14 billion yen from 15.523 billion yen.

Kumagai Gumi officials said their company projected lower profit because of expectations of growing costs to maintain buildings in the U.S. after they are built.

"If we can sell them earlier, that will, of course, make a difference in the running costs," one official said. "Right now, however, we expect the costs to grow and pressure profits down."

Fiscal 1990 sales are estimated at about 1.4 trillion yen, a 9.5% gain from 1.187 trillion yen in the previous fiscal year.

Kumagai Gumi changed its fiscal year to begin April 1. As a result, its fiscal 1988 covers only six months, making comparisons with fiscal 1990 results difficult.

In the truncated fiscal 1988, pretax earnings totaled 14.147 billion yen and net income 4.057 billion yen. Sales in the six months were 483.249 billion yen.
