Business Brief -- Software Toolworks Inc.: Company's Stock Declines Amid Fears of Weak Profit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/25/90

Shares of Software Toolworks Inc. fell $2.875 to $16 on volume of 2.4 million shares in national over-the-counter trading Friday, apparently reflecting investor fears that the company's profit for the quarter that ends this week may be disappointing. The concerns were spurred by a company filing Thursday with the Securities and Exchange Commission indicating that the company had net income of $5,000 on revenue of $2.7 million in the 30 days ended April 30.

In the wake of the stock drop, the Sherman Oaks, Calif., software publisher said that the results "should not be relied upon by investors as indicative of results for the quarter." The filing was made to satisfy pooling-of-interest requirements in the wake of Software Toolworks' March acquisition of Mindscape Inc., a Northbrook, Ill., software concern.

Software Toolworks has seen its stock price triple this year after tripling in 1989 amid the prospect the company will sell Nintendo-related products. For the year ended March 31, the company reported net of $2.6 million, or 14 cents a share, on revenue of $68.7 million.
