Central Mortgage Plans to Write Off $1.1 Million Goodwill

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/25/90

MOUNT CLEMENS, Mich. -- Central Mortgage Corp. plans to write off $1.1 million in non-tax-deductible goodwill associated with the 1987 acquisition of Superior Mortgage Inc. Separately, the bank holding company said it has been ordered to boost its loan-loss reserves $1.7 million, a move that will reduce net income for the quarter ending June 30 about $1.5 million.

Central Mortgage said the Office of Thrift Supervision ordered the increase in loan-loss reserves following a review of its Colonial Central Savings Bank. The majority of the additional reserves considered necessary by the OTS relate to six construction projects that had been identified as problem assets.

Although they agreed to increase reserves, Central Holding executives said they believe the additional reserves may be greater than necessary, considering the collateral value of the properties.

The goodwill write-off comes in connection with Central Mortgage's closing of its loan-origination offices in Topeka and Wichita, Kan. Central Mortgage acquired the offices in 1987. Since this goodwill was already excluded from regulatory capital, the write-off will not affect regulatory capital, Central officials said.
