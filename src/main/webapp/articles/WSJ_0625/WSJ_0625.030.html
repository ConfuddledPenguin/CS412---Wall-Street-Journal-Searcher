International: Malaysia May Privatize Port

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/25/90

KUALA LUMPUR, Malaysia -- The Malaysian cabinet decided to privatize Port Klang, the country's largest port, which is 18.6 miles west of here, by placing all of its operations, with 22 wharves and about 5,000 government employees, under a single commercial entity, local newspaper reports said. The Klang Port Authority, which has operational duties, would be reduced to a regulatory body overseeing the port.

Transport Minister Ling Liong Sik said details of the privatization program will be decided later. Mr. Ling said he couldn't provide details on the equity and privatization schedule, because the matter was beyond the ministry's jurisdiction. He said that port workers would have wages similar to those in the private sector, including bonuses, and that their pay would be determined by the performance of the new company to be set up. Equity of 5% in the company would be allocated to the workers.
