Business Brief -- Mellon Bank Corp.: Staff Cuts Planned, Though Specifics Aren't Disclosed

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/25/90

Mellon Bank Corp., Pittsburgh, said it will reduce staffing this year but declined to indicate how deep the cuts will be. The bank holding company, citing the need to operate more efficiently, said the move stems from a review begun earlier this year of all business lines. Rumors of broad staff reductions had raised concerns among Mellon employees, who fear a repeat of across-the-board layoffs in 1987. After posting an $844 million loss that year, Mellon cut its work force to 16,700 from 19,259.

Mellon said it is evaluating each department individually and won't make an across-the-board cut. It said it has already dismissed trust department employees.

Mellon returned to profitability last year, earning $181 million, or $3.33 a share. But some analysts have voiced concern about how the bank will grow after cutting expenses and shedding bad loans. A Mellon spokeswoman said the impending dismissals don't stem from earnings problems but reflect efforts to "fine tune" operations.
