Marketing & Media: `Tracy' Following Is Strong As Film Leads the Weekend

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/25/90

LOS ANGELES -- "Dick Tracy," the closely watched summer movie from Walt Disney Co., turned in a solid performance in its second weekend, allaying fears that it might be a flop, entertainment industry sources said. "Dick Tracy" was the top-grossing film for the weekend; preliminary estimates put its weekend gross at $15.5 million to $16.7 million. The No. 2 film was the estimated $14.8 million opening of Orion Pictures Corp.'s "RoboCop 2." The No. 3 movie, Carolco Pictures Inc.'s "Total Recall," made an estimated $8.6 million for its fourth weekend.

The estimate for "Dick Tracy" appears to be a modest 26%-31% slide from the movie's $22.5 million take on its opening weekend. "It's a very, very good hold," said Martin Grove, box office analyst with Baseline, an entertainment information service. Some movies have slumped as much as 45% in their second weekend, Mr. Grove noted.

Moreover, the weekend pattern of a sharp 47% drop on Friday, a smoother 5% slide on Saturday from a week earlier, and a projected 18% drop for Sunday suggests that word-of-mouth recommendations are holding up, said Mr. Grove. He projects that the movie will "easily" top $100 million in gross ticket sales at U.S. theaters.
