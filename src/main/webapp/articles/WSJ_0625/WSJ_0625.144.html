International: Lloyds Bank Italian Assets Seized Under Injunction

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/25/90

MILAN -- Just as measures have been announced making it easier for foreign banks to operate in Italy, Britain's fourth-largest bank, Lloyds Bank PLC, has found its assets in the country seized in a mysterious legal move that jeopardizes its business here. Lloyds said it is moving swiftly to safeguard its customers' interests following a private injunction issued by a Pisa court sequestering as much as $796.2 million of its assets.

"We believe the accusation behind the court order is utterly without foundation. . . . We have lawyers taking the appropriate action to have it set aside," a Lloyds spokesman said in London.

While neither Lloyds nor the Pisa court would give information on the accusation or who was behind it, sources close to the case said it related to the seizure by British police of financial documents in 1987.

According to Italian and British news reports and banking sources, the problems apparently stem from an incident in Manchester, England, in 1987, when a Swiss national, two Britons and two Italians tried unsuccessfully to cash several hundred million U.S. dollars of Philippine government securities at a Lloyds branch. While the link between the two actions isn't certain, a Lloyds official said the dispute involves some of the same matters.

The bank described the move as unprecedented and said the court order affected all Lloyds transactions in Italy.
