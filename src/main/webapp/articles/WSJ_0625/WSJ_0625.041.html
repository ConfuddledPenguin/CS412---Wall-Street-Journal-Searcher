International: French Insurer's Capital Boost

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/25/90

PARIS -- Groupement des Assurances Nationales, called GAN, unveiled details of a planned 3.6 billion franc ($639.4 million) capital boost through the issuance of new shares with warrants attached. The capital increase makes GAN the third major French state-owned insurer to take advantage of a law passed in December that relaxes the limits on shareholdings in state insurers by concerns other than the government. Insurers now may issue 25% of their share capital to private investors.

Assurances Generales de France plans to tap the market later this year, and Union des Assurances de Paris has already launched a 10.5 billion-franc capital boost through a share increase.

GAN Chairman Francois Heilbronner said the new funds will help finance the company's acquisitions and investments abroad. "Up until now, GAN has been able to raise the necessary funds for its expansion through internal sources," he said. "But financial deregulation and the opening of European borders has created a new situation, which requires a rapid acceleration of our activities abroad."
