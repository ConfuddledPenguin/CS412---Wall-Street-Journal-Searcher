Business Brief -- Friction Division Products Inc.: Firm to Pay $285,000 Fine To Settle Agency Charges

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/25/90

Friction Division Products Inc. agreed to pay $285,000 in fines as part of a settlement of Labor Department charges of asbestos-related, workplace health violations. The Trenton, N.J., maker of brake pads and linings for cars and trucks also agreed to pay as much as $1.7 million more in fines if it violates a consent order by the federal appeals court in Philadelphia, the department's Occupational Safety and Health Administration said. The company further agreed to take a series of actions aimed at abating serious asbestos-control problems, OSHA said.

Last July, OSHA proposed fines of $2.7 million against the company, the largest ever proposed for health violations.

The company agreed to the settlement without admitting guilt, OSHA said.

Friction Division said that its president was the only person authorized to comment on the settlement and that he wasn't immediately available.
