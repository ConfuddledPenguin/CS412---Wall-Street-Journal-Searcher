Politics & Policy: `Robin HUD' Is Sentenced To 46-Month Prison Term

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/25/90

BALTIMORE (AP) -- A former real estate agent, Marilyn Harrell, who was nicknamed "Robin HUD" after she claimed she stole from the Department of Housing and Urban Development to help the poor, was sentenced to 46 months in prison and ordered to return $110,000 to the government. Ms. Harrell, 46 years old, had acknowledged taking $5.7 million, and pleaded guilty in January to stealing government property and filing a false tax return. She was given the sobriquet "Robin HUD" after claiming she gave most of the money to charity.

But in passing the stiff sentence recommended by prosecutors, federal Judge Herbert Murray said a "substantial amount" of the money went to Ms. Harrell and her family. Ms. Harrell told the judge this wasn't true. Her attorney, Anthony Gallagher, a public defender, said the sentence would be appealed.

As an escrow agent, Ms. Harrell collected money from the sales of property purchased with HUD-guaranteed loans that had been foreclosed. She became a symbol of mismanagement at HUD during the Reagan administration, when the scandal broke.
