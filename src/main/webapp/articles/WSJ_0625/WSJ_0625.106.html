Who's News: Merrill Lynch Merges Financial Service Line

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/25/90

NEW YORK -- Merrill Lynch & Co. said it merged two major units that serve the corporate market. As part of the merger, senior vice president Herbert S. Ruben, the head of one of the units, is retiring at age 60 after 34 years at the big securities firm.

Merrill Lynch said it folded Mr. Ruben's Group Financial Services unit, which provides retirement-plan and related services to large companies, into the firm's Business Financial Services unit, which serves small and midsize companies. The newly combined Business Financial Services operation will have annual revenue of about $550 million.

Senior vice president J. Arthur Urciuoli, 51, remains head of Business Financial Services.

Mr. Ruben is a former Merrill human-resources director, research director and regional sales director who "helped the firm achieve prominence" in retirement-plan services, Merrill said.
