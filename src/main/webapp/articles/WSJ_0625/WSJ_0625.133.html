Business Brief -- Tektronix Inc.: Holders File Suit Alleging Violations of Security Laws

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/25/90

Tektronix Inc. said holders filed a class-action suit against the company and certain of its officers and directors, alleging violations of security laws. The suit, filed in federal district court in Portland, Ore., accuses Tektronix of "withholding material adverse information from the public" from July, 1989, to February, 1990. The electronics equipment maker didn't elaborate on the lawsuit, calling it "entirely without merit."

As reported, Tektronix last month fired about 10% of its 13,000 work force and said it was taking a one-time charge of $70 million against its fiscal fourth-quarter earnings, as a result of slumping sales. It hasn't announced earnings for that quarter, ended May 26.

For the fiscal third quarter, ended March 3, the company posted a loss of $25.5 million, or 88 cents a share, on revenues of $421.8 million. It earned $6.4 million, or 22 cents a share, on revenue of $434.1 million a year earlier.
