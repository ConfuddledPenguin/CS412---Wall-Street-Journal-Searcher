International: Mannesmann Unit Buys Firm

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/25/90

FRANKFURT, West Germany -- Mannesmannroehren-Werke AG of Duesseldorf, a producer of steel pipes that is 75%-owned by Mannesmann AG and 25%-owned by Thyssen AG, said it acquired IWKA Stahlflaschen G.m.b.H., a West German maker of steel cylinders. Terms weren't disclosed. Mannesmannroehren said 90% of IWKA was acquired from Saarstahl AG, a minority-owned affiliate of Luxembourg's big steel combine Arbed S.A., and the remaining 10% from Industrie-Werke Karlsruhe Augsberg AG. Mannesmann is a steel, chemical and engineering concern. Thyssen is a steelmaker.

Mannesmannroehren will merge its steel cylinder and high-pressure gas container businesses with those of IWKA, and change the unit's name to Mannesmann Stahlflaschen G.m.b.H.
