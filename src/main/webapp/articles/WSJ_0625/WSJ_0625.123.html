Business Brief -- McFarland Energy Inc.: Firm Has $3.4 Million Gain On Sale of Stake in Field

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/25/90

McFarland Energy Inc. said it realized a cash gain of about $3.4 million from the $3.8 million sale to Nerco Inc. of a 6% working interest in the Trimble Field in Mississippi. McFarland Energy, based in Santa Fe Springs, Calif., said it probably will use the cash to pay down debt. The gain, however, won't appear as net income because McFarland Energy uses so-called "full-cost" accounting, under which exploration and development expenditures are pooled and depreciated over time, J.C. McFarland, president and chief executive officer, said.

McFarland's stake in the Trimble field was part of a $67 million purchase by Nerco earlier last week that gave Nerco, a unit of PacifiCorp, an 80% working interest in the field.

McFarland's stake in the field equaled 2.3 billion cubic feet of natural gas, about 23% of the company's net proven, developed gas reserves.
