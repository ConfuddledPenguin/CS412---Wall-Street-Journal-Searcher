Business Brief -- Curtice Burns Foods Inc.: Profit Decline, Sales Increase Are Expected for Fiscal '90

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/25/90

Curtice Burns Foods Inc., Rochester, N.Y., said it expects to report a 40% decline in profit for the June 29 fiscal year as sales rose about 10%. In fiscal 1989, the processor of regionally branded food products earned $14.9 million, or $1.78 a share, adjusted for a 3-for-2 split paid Dec. 20. Sales in fiscal 1989 were $807.3 million.

The company said it was hurt by a short crop of canning and freezing vegetables in the Northeast, lower sales in its meatsnack business and reduced sales of oat bran. Other factors included costs related to development activities, and plant and administrative consolidations.

This year will mark only the second time since 1980 that earnings haven't increased over the previous period, the company said.

Curtice Burns said that it considers its problems short-term, and that it expects a return to its historic rate of earnings in the next fiscal year. The company said crop conditions have returned to normal this year, recent acquisitions are producing satisfactory profits, and a major portion of consolidation expenses are behind it.
