SEN. JOHN KERRY (D., Mass.) hasn't accepted contributions from political action committees in any of his campaigns for federal office. Because of an error in Federal Election Commission data, he was listed in Thursday's edition as having received $2,000 in PAC contributions from companies supporting the overhaul of federal product-liability laws. (See: "Politics & Policy: Product-Liability Bill Provides Opportunity for Long-Term Milking of PACs by Congress" -- WSJ June 21, 1990)

---

MITSUBISHI TRUST & BANKING Corp. was incorrectly described in Friday's edition as one of the banks opposed to a $65 million loan to Donald Trump. The bank was Mitsubishi Bank.

(See: "Judge Declines to Drop Claims Against Trump --- Pratt Hotel Corp. Alleges Violations of Antitrust Law; Fall Trial Possible" -- WSJ June 22, 1990)

---

CONTINENTAL AIRLINES HOLDINGS Inc. short interest volume was 7,532,266 shares for the month ended June 15, making it the largest-volume issue and bringing total American Stock Exchange short volume to 75,240,053 shares. Because Continental recently changed its name from Texas Air Corp., some brokerage firms neglected to report some of the company's short positions, the Amex said.

(See: "Short Interest Rose to Highs In Latest Month --- Big Board Figure Up 2.6% And Amex Climbed 0.3% in Period Ended June 15" -- WSJ June 22, 1990)

---

ENERGY SERVICE Co., a Houston energy services concern, said the short interest in its shares for the month ended June 15 rose because investors appear to be arbitraging its common and preferred stock. The company was incorrectly described in Friday's short interest article as being involved in a hazardous-waste facility.

(See: "Short Interest Rose to Highs In Latest Month --- Big Board Figure Up 2.6% And Amex Climbed 0.3% In Period Ended June 15" -- WSJ June 22, 1990)
