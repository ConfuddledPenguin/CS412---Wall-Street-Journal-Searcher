Marketing & Media: Producer of Bond Films Puts Rights Up for Sale

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/25/90

HOLLYWOOD -- Albert R. Broccoli, producer of the James Bond films, has put up for sale his interest in the successful series and rights to the famous British spy's name. Danjaq S.A., Mr. Broccoli's Swiss holding company, owns the Bond library of 16 films and certain rights to any future Bond films, television shows or other projects. MGM/UA Communications Co., Culver City, Calif., holds longterm distribution rights to the films.

MGM/UA is talking with Mr. Broccoli's representatives about acquiring his rights, said Dean Stolber, executive vice president of MGM/UA's entertainment business group. But it is considered more likely that another buyer will be sought because negotiations with MGM/UA aren't going well.

An attorney for Mr. Broccoli and Danjaq declined to discuss the talks and Mr. Broccoli's reasons for selling the company now. Mr. Broccoli and his partners would continue as producers of future Agent 007 films after Danjaq is sold, the attorney said.

While estimates of the library's value weren't available, film distributors have had a big appetite in recent years for big-budget action movies. The 17th film in the series is in development now.

Lazard Freres & Co., the New York investment-banking concern, is handling the sale for Danjaq.
