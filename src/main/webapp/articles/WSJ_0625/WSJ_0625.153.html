Charter Medical Says U.S. Is Probing Reimbursements

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/25/90

MACON, Ga. -- Charter Medical Corp. said federal regulators are investigating the company for possible fraud related to its Medicare and Medicaid reimbursements. C. Michael Ford, chief financial officer of the hospital and psychiatric-care company, said the Office of the Inspector General of the Department of Health and Human Services has interviewed several former reimbursement specialists from Charter in connection with the probe. The interviews, he said, have taken place in the past several months.

Mr. Ford added, however, that he isn't aware of any current employees who have been interviewed.

Mr. Ford said the company expects to make a statement today regarding the investigation.

Charter, which went private in a 1988 buy-out, posted a $67 million loss on revenue of $475.2 million for its second quarter ended March 31. The year earlier, Charter had a $14.6 million loss on revenue of $397.3 million.

The most recent figures, which included a $35 million pretax charge for accounting problems, have raised some concerns on Wall Street about the company's ability to service its debt. Charter has said it will cut capital expenditures and other costs to ensure its ability to meet its payments.
