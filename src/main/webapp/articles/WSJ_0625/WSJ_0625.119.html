Environmental Case Tied to Car Explosion Delayed in California

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/25/90

OAKLAND, Calif. -- A municipal court judge here gave prosecutors and police two more weeks to complete an investigation aimed at determining whether two members of the militant Earth First] environmental group were responsible for a bomb that exploded in their car on May 12. Results of that investigation were due last Friday, but prosecutors said a final report has been delayed because the Federal Bureau of Investigation is taking longer than expected to complete a detailed analysis of the bombing. The blast seriously injured Earth First] member Judi Bari; her companion in the car, Darryl Cherney, suffered minor facial cuts.

Police took the two into custody after the blast on suspicion that they were carrying the bomb for a yet unnamed "ecoterrorist" purpose when it exploded accidentally. Both Ms. Bari and Mr. Cherney have denied the charges and contend they were the objects of a murder plot. Both are the chief organizers of a so-called "Redwood Summer" protest now under way in Northern California against timber companies' harvesting of the state's redwood forests.

The demonstrations have thus far resulted in a few dozen arrests of protesters and vocal clashes with timber and other local interests, but there has been no reported violence.
