Law: Drexel Creditors Urge Court to Bar Asset Liquidations

  • Author: Wade Lambert
  • Date: 06/25/90

NEW YORK -- The Drexel Burnham Lambert Group Inc. creditors' committee asked a U.S. bankruptcy court to prevent the company from liquidating assets until steps are taken to guarantee that the creditors will have a say in reviewing the transactions. In their request, the creditors accused Drexel of backing out of a court-sanctioned oversight process that allowed members of the committee to review liquidation proposals.

Drexel filed under Chapter 11 of the U.S. Bankruptcy Code on Feb. 13. According to the motion, Drexel ended the oversight arrangement on May 27, two days before Drexel's brokerage unit and other subsidiaries joined the parent in bankruptcy proceedings.

The motion asks that a procedure be set up to ensure that all liquidation proposals be reviewed by the creditors and approved by the court. The end result, however, is likely to be an agreement by Drexel and the creditors to reinstate a review process similar to the previous arrangement. Representatives of the creditors and Drexel said they expect an agreement to be reached before July 19, when a hearing on the motion is scheduled before U.S. Bankruptcy Court Judge Howard Buschman III in Manhattan.

"The committee is very eager to have the oversight procedure back in place," said Wilbur Ross of Rothschild Inc., the committee's financial adviser.

A Drexel spokesman said plans for a new review procedure were already being made before the committee filed its motion. He said Drexel wants to include the creditors of several units as well as the parent's creditors in the review process.

The creditors, in their motion, said that since the oversight procedure ended one Drexel subsidiary has tried to liquidate assets without informing the creditors or the court.

The Drexel spokesman, however, said no transactions have been pursued without notifying the committee or the court. He said the company isn't currently liquidating assets and doesn't plan to resume doing so until a new review procedure including the creditors is adopted.

Mr. Ross said the committee also is concerned that Drexel has missed some potential liquidation transactions in the junk-bond market in recent weeks. He said that if the oversight procedure was still in place, the creditors could question Drexel about whether such transactions should be made at this time.

The Drexel spokesman said the company has missed no such opportunities. He said "there has been no compelling reason" for the company to enter into any liquidation transactions since the oversight procedure ended.
