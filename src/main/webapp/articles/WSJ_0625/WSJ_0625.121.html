Business Brief -- General Motors Corp.: Buick Unit Adjusts Prices Of Two Redesigned Models

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/25/90

The Buick division of General Motors Corp., Flint, Mich., said its substantially redesigned 1991 Park Avenue model, now going on sale, will start at $24,385, up 12% from the 1990 Electra Park Avenue model it replaces. A more heavily equipped version, the 1991 Park Avenue Ultra, will start at $27,420, down 1% from the model it replaces.

The new, full-size Park Avenues have improved engines and transmissions and sleeker, more aerodynamic looks than their boxy predecessors. Buick said the new Park Avenue model will cost more in part because such equipment as anti-lock brakes, driver's-side airbag and an anti-theft device are now standard.

The 1991 Park Avenue Ultra's base price is $405 lower in part because Buick took out a seat that adjusted in 20 directions and replaced it with one that adjusted in just eight directions.

Buick also said it will eliminate two other models, the Electra Limited and the Electra T-type, that were built off the same chassis as the Park Avenue in the 1990 model year.
