Troubled Excalibur Halts Production of Fancy Autos

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/25/90

MILWAUKEE (AP) -- Excalibur Automobile Corp., maker of neoclassic automobiles fancied by wealthy collectors, has suspended production after being rocked by layoffs, lawsuits, bounced paychecks and allegations of mileage tampering. "If we could build 120 cars a year, we could be profitable," said Scott Dennison, who became president of the company June 1 after Henry A. Warner resigned.

But the company has been producing just one car a week since January and has a backlog of 76 vehicles. Layoffs this month left only five of Excalibur's 45 workers on staff.

The company has $2.1 million in loans outstanding, owes $97,000 in overdue property taxes and owes thousands more to unpaid workers and vendors.

Mr. Dennison said his restructuring plans include buying Mr. Warner's 59% stake, raising at least $500,000 in additional capital for debt financing and operations and proceeding with plans to find new markets.
