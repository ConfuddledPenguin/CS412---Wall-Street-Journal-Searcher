Henley Group Inc. Seeks to Dispose Of Itel Shares

  • Author: Robert L. Rose
  • Date: 06/25/90

Henley Group Inc., moving forward in its plan to liquidate, said it intends to dispose of as much as its entire 38.5% stake in Itel Corp., a chunk of shares valued at $381 million at current prices. In a filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission, Henley said it plans to sell eight million to 16.7 million of its 18.7 million common shares in the Chicago-based leasing concern through a public offering "or otherwise."

A Henley spokesman, Norman Ritter, said the filing includes the possibility that some of the Itel shares would be swapped with current Henley holders for their Henley shares. But at least some of the Itel stake will be sold, Mr. Ritter said.

Henley, based in Hampton, N.H., said it plans to contribute the remaining two million shares to Fisher International Inc., a Henley subsidiary that Henley plans to spin off.

Henley acquired the Itel shares in 1988 as part of a $1.2 billion cash and stock payment from Itel. In return, Henley sold Itel its stake in Santa Fe Southern Pacific Corp. and leasing operations, among other holdings.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading Friday, Itel shares fell 37.5 cents, to $20.375. Henley stock fell 25 cents to $31 a share in national over-the-counter trading.

Itel officials couldn't be reached for comment, but Henley's holdings weren't considered a threat to Itel. The 1988 transaction included a 10-year standstill agreement that restricted how Henley could vote or trade its Itel stake.

At the time, Itel Chairman Samuel Zell asserted in an interview that the terms of the standstill agreement meant that Henley would be distributing its Itel shares to Henley shareholders. But a Henley spokesman said at the time that Henley planned to keep its Itel shares.

Henley's disposition of Itel shares fits with its current plan to liquidate. Henley has said it intends to spin off its Fisher Scientific Group unit and 35% of Pneumo Abex Corp. The spinoff plan is being reviewed by the SEC.

Proceeds from the sale of any of the rest of the 16.7 million shares will be used to reduce Henley's huge debt load of about $1.5 billion, a Henley spokesman said. Most of that debt, about $1.03 billion, is Pneumo Abex's.

But Henley said its plans don't currently call for using proceeds of the sale to lighten that load before the 35% spinoff. At current market prices, the 16.7 million Itel shares are valued at $340.3 million.

In addition to its railcar and container leasing operations, Itel is involved in dredging, distribution and wire making. It also invests in stocks of other companies.
