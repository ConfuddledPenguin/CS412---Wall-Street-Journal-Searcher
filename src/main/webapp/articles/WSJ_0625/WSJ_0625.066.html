UPS Recommended For Cargo Service Between U.S., Japan

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/25/90

WASHINGTON -- An administrative law judge recommended giving United Parcel Service Co. authority to offer new airline cargo service between the U.S. and Japan. If the recommendation is approved by the Bush administration, UPS would be authorized to make six new round-trip, all-cargo flights weekly between the two countries. The authority would continue for five years.

Acquisition of the route by UPS would be a defeat for Emery Worldwide Airlines, a unit of Consolidated Freightways Inc., which also sought the authority.

The law judge, Burton S. Kolko, said UPS "presented a credible and achievable proposal that will provide greater public benefits and enhance the competitive environment to a greater degree in the U.S.-Japan all-cargo air transportation market" than Emery would.

He recommended giving Emery backup authority for a year to provide the service should UPS fail to do so.

The recommendation now will be considered by top Transportation Department officials. Their decision is subject to review by the White House, which generally goes along with the department.

A final decision is expected by the end of the year.
