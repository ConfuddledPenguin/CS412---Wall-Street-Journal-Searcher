UAL Union Group Will Go to Europe To Seek Investors

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/25/90

CHICAGO -- Representatives of the unions seeking to buy UAL Corp. will travel to Europe this week to talk with airlines and suppliers about possibly investing in the labor group's $4.38 billion buy-out proposal. Gerald Greenwald, former Chrysler Corp. vice chairman who is heading the UAL buy-out effort, is expected to talk with British Airways PLC, Rolls-Royce PLC, a British jet-engine manufacturer, Airbus Industries, a European aircraft maker and Guinness Peat Aviation, a leasing company.

British Airways was an investor in a previous, failed labor-management bid for UAL. In the earlier bid, British Airways agreed to provide $700 million in equity in return for a 15% stake in UAL. The airline's management has refused so far to participate in the new buy-out effort, but apparently has agreed to listen to a pitch from Mr. Greenwald, according to an individual familiar with the situation.

As previously reported, the unions are also talking with Boeing Co, the Seattle aircraft manufacturer; General Electric Co., a Fairfield, Conn., maker of jet engines and electrical products; and Pratt & Whitney, the jet engine maker that is a unit of United Technologies Inc., Hartford, Conn.

The unions are said to be seeking between $500 million and $1 billion in financing from these companies that would be subordinate to any bank financing.

In return for their investment, the suppliers would receive either a note or nonvoting stock in UAL as well as "preferred-supplier" status.

The labor group is seeking to reduce its dependence on bank financing. Commercial lenders have been cool to the transaction, which would result in the largest employee-owned company in the nation.

Banks repeatedly have expressed concern about the absence of an outside equity investor. Currently, the only equity in the deal is the $2 billion of "sweat equity" the unions agreed to provide over five years in the form of wage concessions.
