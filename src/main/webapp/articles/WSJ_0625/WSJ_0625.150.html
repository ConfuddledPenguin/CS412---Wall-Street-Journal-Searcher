Politics & Policy: Thompson Issues an Order On Patronage in Illinois

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/25/90

CHICAGO -- Illinois Gov. James R. Thompson issued an executive order requiring department heads to bring the state in line with last week's Supreme Court decision striking down patronage practices. Five Illinois residents successfully challenged the Republican governor over his party's control of more than 60,000 state jobs. Mr. Thompson, who called the high court's ruling "naive," told reporters that the death of patronage "turns politics on its head."

But the spoils system may still have some life in Illinois. While the governor's order ends a requirement of political loyalty in the state and specifically covers promotions, transfers and firings, Mr. Thompson said that when faced with two job candidates with equal qualifications, "We'll hire the Republican."

Mr. Thompson, the nation's longest-serving governor, will leave office in January after 14 years.
