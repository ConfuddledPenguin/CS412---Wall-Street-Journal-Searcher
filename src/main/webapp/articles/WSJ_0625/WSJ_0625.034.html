International: Hong Kong Auctions Off Land

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/25/90

HONG KONG -- The government's first land auction in the fiscal year that began April 1 raised a total of 357 million Hong Kong dollars (US$45.8 million), far more than the combined minimum of HK$257 million, and in line with most market expectations, though weak bidding interest was viewed by analysts as a sign of continuing caution. The auction covered a site for warehouse construction, one for residential use and a third site for nonindustrial purposes. "There were only a few bidders, which suggests that a number of people are quite conservative," said K.M. Wu, valuation manager for Richard Ellis Ltd., a property consulting group. "The outlook is still not too certain."

Roger Nissim, the government's land agent who conducted the auction, sounded a more optimistic note than most analysts. He said the prices were in line with government expectations. "There was quite spirited bidding for each lot," he said. "The three lots are for different types of users, so that shows quite a healthy sign that various sectors of the property market are still quite active."
