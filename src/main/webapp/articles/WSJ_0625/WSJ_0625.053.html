Jacor Ends Accord With Dalton Over Sale Of Station, Files Suit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/25/90

CINCINNATI -- Jacor Communications Inc. said it terminated its agreement to sell FM radio station WMYU in Knoxville, Tenn., for $11.6 million to Dalton Group Inc. because Dalton breached terms of the agreement. The radio-station operator also filed suit in Hamilton County common pleas court against Dalton seeking a determination of rights to Dalton's $500,000 deposit. Jacor claims Dalton officials failed to show up for the closing of the agreement.

William Dalton, president of Dalton, based in Potomac, Md., said, "I don't have any comment, except that we're still in negotiations" to purchase the station. His company currently owns one radio station in Cincinnati.
