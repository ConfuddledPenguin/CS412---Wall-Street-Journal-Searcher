South Korean Aid to Paraguay

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/25/90

SEOUL, South Korea -- South Korea has agreed to consider Paraguay's request for long-term loans for its economic development, the Foreign Ministry said. Officials said South Korean Foreign Minister Choi Ho-joong made the promise Thursday when Paraguayan Foreign Minister Luis Maria Aragana asked for financial and other support.

The ministry officials said that Choi asked Paraguay to present detailed plans for its development projects to facilitate Korean inter-ministerial considerations. It was not known how much Paraguay was seeking in loans.

Mr. Aragana was here with Paraguayan President Andres Rodriguez who came Wednesday for three days of talks with South Korean President Roh Tae-woo to discuss ways to strengthen economic and other relations.

Mr. Choi told Mr. Aragana that South Korea was studying the possibility of providing communications equipment and other supplies requested by the South American country, the officials said.
