PHILIP MORRIS AGREED to acquire 80% of Swiss coffee and chocolate maker Jacobs Suchard for $3.8 billion. Suchard's trove of brands, including Toblerone chocolate, will significantly strengthen the U.S. firm's European presence before trade barriers fall in 1992 and will help lessen the company's dependence on tobacco sales in the U.S. ---

Orders for durable goods leaped 3.9% in May, suggesting to some that the manufacturing sector is stabilizing. But other parts of the economy may be softening; personal income rose only 0.3% in the month, while personal spending was flat.

Machine tool orders dropped 29% in May from a high April rate but were above a year earlier.

---

The Dow Jones industrials tumbled 44.55 points Friday to 2857.18, the average's lowest level since Memorial Day. The sell-off in stocks pushed bond prices modestly higher.

---

USX has held "exploratory" talks with foreign investors about the possible sale of some of its steel operations. USX's chairman played down the likelihood of such a move but said the firm's future is in energy.

---

First Brands' controlling shareholders are seeking to sell all or part of the consumer-products firm. Its stock jumped on the annnouncement.

---

Zinc and lead prices are expected to rise sharply if new labor contracts aren't reached this week at Canada's two largest producers, Cominco Ltd. and Noranda Inc.'s Brunswick unit.

---

NBC may overhaul its daytime schedule, perhaps expanding the "Today" show to three hours.

---

The EPA is expected to propose today that second-hand smoke be declared a human carcinogen, which could encourage more firms to adopt total workplace bans on smoking.

---

The prison term for former Drexel trading assistant Lisa Jones was reduced to 10 months from 18 months.

---

Trump gained the consent of three Japanese banks for a bailout loan. The proposal's fate now appears to rest with two European banks.

---

Circle K named Cincinnati lawyer Bart Brown to be chairman, succeeding Karl Eller, who quit in May.

---

Bank of New England named a new head of its main unit as part of a continuing shuffling of management.

---

Northrop and McDonnell unveiled their version of the Air Force's next-generation fighter. But the jet is emerging just as lawmakers question how much such a warplane is needed.
