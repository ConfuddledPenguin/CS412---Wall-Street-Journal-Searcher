Panel Calls for Sharp Cut In Worker Radiation Limit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/25/90

WASHINGTON -- An international commission recommended sharply lower radiation exposure limits for workers regularly exposed to high levels of radiation. The International Commission on Radiological Radiation Protection, which conducts research into the effects of radiation on humans, said the risks from ionizing radiation are about three times as high as was estimated a decade ago. The organization, made up of scientists and physicians from the industrialized countries, recommended lowering the acceptable level of exposure to two rems a year, measured as an average over five-year basis, from five rems a year. A rem is a measure of radiation.

The Nuclear Regulatory Commission, which sets standards for worker exposure levels in the U.S., currently sets a limit of five rems a year with a maximum exposure of three rems in any three-month period. An NRC official declined to comment on the new recommendation pending review of the commission's report.

The chairman of the international commission, Dr. Dan Beninson of Argentina, estimated that 1.5 million people in the U.S. were involved in occupations that would put them at risk of exposure beyond the new suggested range. Dr. Beninson said that people working in occupations such as nuclear plant repair, medical radiology and uranium mining could be affected.
