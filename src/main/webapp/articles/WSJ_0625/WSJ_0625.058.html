New Stock Listings

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/25/90

NEW YORK -- The New York Stock Exchange announced four new stock listings, including an initial public offering, the American Stock Exchange announced two and the National Market System of the over-the-counter market announced one new listing.

First of America Bank Corp., a Kalamazoo, Mich., bank holding company, will begin trading Friday on the Big Board under the symbol FOA. It previously traded over the counter.

Nuveen Investment Quality Municipal Fund Inc., a Chicago management investment company, began trading Friday as an initial public offering under NQM.

Omnicom Group Inc., the New York-based parent of DDB Needham Worldwide Inc. and BBDO Worldwide Inc., full-service advertising agencies, opens Wednesday under the symbol OMC. It also has traded over the counter.

RTZ PLC, a London-based natural resources company, will begin trading American Depositary Receipts, representing four ordinary shares, under the symbol RTZ on Thursday. The company continues to trade on the London stock exchange.

Joining the Amex, Heartland Partners L.P., a Chicago limited partnership, began trading last week on a when-issued basis under HTL.WI. Heartland was formed to receive the real estate assets and business of Chicago Milwaukee Corp. in a spin-off to shareholders. The exchange will be completed Sunday when each share of

Chicago Milwaukee will receive a unit of Heartland.

Information Display Technology Inc. began trading last week on a when-issued basis under IDT.WI. The Pittsburgh-based visual information display systems company, will change its name from RT Acquisition Associates on July 9.

Joining the OTC market, Security Financial Holding Co., a Durham, N.C., bank holding company for Security Federal Savings Bank, began trading last week under SECF.

The Pacific Stock Exchange began trading options on Applied Materials Inc., a Santa Clara, Calif., maker of semiconductor wafer processing eqipment, under ANQ; First Wachovia Corp., a Winston-Salem, N.C., banking services firm, under FW; and Oceaneering International Inc., a Houston contractor of underwater services to oil and gas industries, under OEQ.
