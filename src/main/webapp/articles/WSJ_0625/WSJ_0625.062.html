New Securities Issues

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/25/90

The following were among Friday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATES

Chiron Corp. -- $100 million of convertible subordinated debentures, due July 1, 2015, priced at par to yield 7.25%. The offering was increased from an originally planned $75 million. The debentures are convertible into common stock at $47 a share, representing a 23.68% conversion premium over Thursday's closing price. Rated single-B-1 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and single-B-plus by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Morgan Stanley & Co.

Cincinnati Bell Inc. -- $75 million of 9.10% notes, due June 15, 2000, priced at 99.865 to yield 9.12%. The non-callable notes were priced at a spread of 60 basis points above the Treasury's 10-year note. Rated double-A-2 by Moody's and double-A-minus by S&P, the issue will be sold by underwriters at Morgan Stanley & Co. Inc.

MORTGAGE

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $300 million of Remic mortgage securities offered in 14 classes by Prudential-Bache Securities. The offering, Series 1990-86, is backed by Fannie Mae 9 1/2% securities with a weighted average coupon rate of 10.15% and weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29.75 years.

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $300 million of Remic mortgage securities offered in six classes by First Boston Corp. The offering, Series 199-85, is backed by Fannie Mae 9 1/2% securities.

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $300 million of Remic mortgage securities offered in 17 classes by Kidder Peabody & Co. The offering, Series 1990-88, is backed by Fannie Mae 9 1/2% securities. The interest rates for classes FA, SA, F and S are based on one-month London Interbank Offered Rate, reset monthly.

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $200.1 million of Remic mortgage securities offered in 12 classes by PaineWebber Inc. The offering, Series 1990-87, is backed by Fannie Mae 8% securities with a weighted average remaining term to maturity of 26.83 years.

EUROBONDS

Saint Gobain Nederland BV -- 750 million French francs of 10 1/2% Eurobonds due July 27, 1995, priced at 101.80 to yield 10.52% less fees via lead manager Credit Commercial de France. Guaranteed by Compagnie de Saint Gobain. Fees 1 7/8.
