Canadian Regulators Set Payment to Settle Insider-Trading Case

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/20/90

TORONTO -- Securities regulators from Ontario and Manitoba agreed to accept a voluntary payment of about 550,000 Canadian dollars (US$468,270) by James Richardson, a former government minister, in settlement of proceedings brought against him on allegations of insider trading. The settlement agreement didn't attribute any wrongdoing to Mr. Richardson. The agreement was reached between Mr. Richardson's legal counsel and staff members of each of the provincial securities commissions. Both parties said they wanted to avoid the lengthy proceedings that could result if formal charges were brought.

Mr. Richardson, 68 years old, comes from a prominent Winnipeg, Manitoba, family that made its name and fortune in grain trading. Mr. Richardson also served as defense minister in the Liberal Party governments of Pierre Trudeau in the 1970s.

The insider-trading allegations arose from Mr. Richardson's short-selling of shares of Kingston, Ontario-based Plastic Engine Technology Corp., which subsequently went into receivership in April 1989. Regulatory officials alleged that Mr. Richardson initiated the short sales after a discussion with a Plastic Engine director about the company's financial position and the possibility it would file for bankruptcy-law proceedings. A short sale is the sale of borrowed stock by an investor who hopes to make a profit by buying an equal number of shares later at a lower price to replace the borrowed stock.

As part of the settlement, Mr. Richardson is prohibited from dealing in securities in Ontario and Manitoba for three years. He also agreed to pay C$300,000 into a fund that will be used to compensate people who incurred losses as a result of the short sales. Mr. Richardson's gross proceeds from the short sales amounted to C$298,515.

Mr. Richardson also agreed to pay C$100,000 each to the treasuries of the two provinces and as much as C$50,000 to cover costs of the provinces' investigation in the case.
