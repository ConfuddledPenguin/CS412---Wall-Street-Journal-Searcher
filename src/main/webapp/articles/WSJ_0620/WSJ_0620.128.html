Saatchi & Saatchi Files Suit Against an Insurer

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/20/90

LOS ANGELES -- Saatchi & Saatchi Co. sued an insurer for allegedly refusing to pay for its defense against a flurry of investor lawsuits filed this year. In a complaint filed in state court in Los Angeles, Saatchi asserts that a directors' and officers' liability policy issued by New Hampshire Insurance Co. provides for payment of defense costs of the suits now pending in federal court in Los Angeles but that the insurer has declined payment.

The suits, filed by holders of Saatchi's American depositary receipts, allege that Saatchi and its officers artificially inflated the market price of the receipts, which represent shares in London-based Saatchi, and that they failed to disclose the giant advertising firm's difficulties in digesting a series of acquisitions.

Saatchi's suit seeks a declaration that New Hampshire Insurance must pay for defense of the investor suits and asks at least $100,000 in damages.

Officials of New Hampshire Insurance, based in Manchester, N.H., couldn't be reached for comment.
