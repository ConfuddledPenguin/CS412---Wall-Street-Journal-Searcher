Leibler Resigning As Amex President To Join an Insurer

  • Author: William Power
  • Date: 06/20/90

NEW YORK -- Kenneth R. Leibler, president of the American Stock Exchange for the past 4 1/2 years and one of the best-knownfigures in the securities industry, is leaving the Amex for a top job at Liberty Financial Cos. in Boston. Mr. Leibler, 41 years old, was passed over last October when the stock exchange brought in former Congressman James R. Jones to succeed longtime chairman Arthur Levitt Jr.

Mr. Leibler will assume the new position of president and chief operating officer at Liberty Financial, a unit of Liberty Mutual Insurance Co. Among the eight Liberty Financial operating companies he'll run are Stein Roe & Farnham and Liberty Asset Management moneymanagement companies, with combined assets under management of $15 billion, and the insurer Keystone Provident Life. Mr. Leibler will report to Sam Marinella, chief executive officer of Liberty Financial.

Mr. Jones said Mr. Leibler's departure -- which apparently caught the Amex by surprise -- is a "big loss" for the exchange, which is a tiny stock market compared with the New York Stock Exchange, but which has become one of the country's major options markets. Mr. Jones said no decision has been made about a successor or about whether a search committee will be formed.

The youngest president of a securities exchange when he took over as Amex president in 1986, Mr. Leibler has worked at the Amex for most of his career, starting with an entry-level job in the options division 15 years ago. Joseph Hardiman, president of the National Association of Securities Dealers, praised him yesterday for his knowledge of markets.

For his part, Mr. Leibler said yesterday that he is looking forward to the "much broader management capacity" of his new job. "There's no doubt that when I got to this position at age 36, this wasn't something I would do for the next 30 years."

He hopes the move allows him the chance for a short vacation to indulge in his "two major passions" -- vegetable gardening and collecting wine. He also has a passion for business, of course. One day, "I hope to get a vineyard and combine them all," Mr. Leibler said.

It has been an active time in the executive suites of major exchanges. The New York Stock Exchange's chairman, John J. Phelan Jr., also plans to step down later this year.
