U.S. Reserve Assets Rose $745 Million During May

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/20/90

WASHINGTON -- U.S. official reserve assets rose $745 million in May to $77.03 billion, the Treasury Department said. Last month's rise compared with a $20 million decline in April to $76.28 billion, the department said.

U.S. reserve assets consist of foreign currencies, gold, special drawing rights at the International Monetary Fund and the U.S.'s reserve position, or ability to draw foreign currencies, at the IMF.

The nation's holdings of foreign currencies increased $370 million in May to $46.8 billion, while its gold reserves rose $5 million to $11.07 billion.

U.S. holdings of IMF special drawing rights last month rose $293 million to $10.4 billion, and its reserve position at the IMF increased $77 million to $8.76 billion.
