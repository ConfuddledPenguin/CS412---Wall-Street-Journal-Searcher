Corona's Plan to Win Canadian Gold Site Hits Major Barriers

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/20/90

VANCOUVER, British Columbia -- Corona Corp.'s plan to secure control of Canada's hottest gold prospect -- the big Eskay Creek gold deposit in northwestern British Columbia -- has hit a couple of major roadblocks. Placer Dome Inc., a big, cash-rich gold producer that is competing with Corona to acquire Stikine Resources Ltd., half-owner of the Eskay Creek deposit, is expected today to announce that about 40% of Stikine's 3,330,000 shares were tendered under Placer Dome's offer, which expired yesterday.

A spokesman for Placer Dome said final results of its 225.1 million Canadian dollar (US$191.7 million) or C$67.50-a-share tender offer for Stikine won't be released until this morning. But insiders familiar with the bid said Placer Dome is expected to wind up with about 40% of the company.

As a result, Placer Dome will be in a position to thwart a previously proposed plan under which Corona hoped to secure control of Stikine through a stock swap and then merge Stikine and Stikine's Eskay Creek partner, Prime Resources Group Inc., with a newly created unit, Corona Gold Corp. The plan requires approval by 75% of Stikine's holders.

Separately, Corona confirmed that a proposed private transaction under which it planned to immediately raise its stake in Prime to 45% from 20% didn't close as scheduled Friday. Corona's spokesman said the proposed purchase of 10.2 million Prime shares for an equal number of Corona Class A shares from a group including Prime Chairman Murray Pezim, associate Arthur Clemiss and Vancouver financier Jim Pattison is only awaiting regulatory clearance.

But Mr. Pezim disagreed, citing dissatisfaction with the terms of the transaction, partly over the decline in Corona's share price.
