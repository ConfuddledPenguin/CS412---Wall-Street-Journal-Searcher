Who's News: Arlie Lazarus Resigns From No. 2 Position At Jamesway Corp.

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/20/90

SECAUCUS, N.J. -- Jamesway Corp. said Arlie G. Lazarus resigned as president and chief operating officer, effective Aug. 3. The 52-year-old Mr. Lazarus, the discount-store operator's No. 2 officer, said, "Since I have been president of Jamesway for over 12 years, it is now time to move on to something new."

Herbert Fisher, 68 years old, remains chairman and chief executive officer of the company, which operates 132 stores in the mid-Atlantic states. After Mr. Lazarus's departure, Mr. Fisher will assume the duties of president and chief operating officer until a successor is found, Jamesway said.

In the year ended Feb. 3, Mr. Lazarus received cash compensation of $552,590, compared with $569,357 for Mr. Fisher, according to proxy material for the annual meeting held June 4.

"They've been a good team for a long time," Edward F. Johnson, analyst at Prescott Ball & Turben Inc. in New York, said of Messrs. Fisher and Lazarus. Though not a strong performer in recent years, Jamesway did stave off potentially weaker performance in a highly competitive industry with many troubled companies, Mr. Johnson said. But he added that Jamesway might well do better with a new president.

Jamesway's earnings declined in fiscal 1990 for the third year in a row, despite higher sales. But the company said it expects earnings in the current fiscal year, ending next Feb. 2, to exceed the $5.8 million, or 41 cents a share, earned on sales of $855.1 million in fiscal 1990.

Barry Rockland, senior vice president for finance, said sales for stores open more than a year are expected to rise this year, and expense control is tighter.
