Senate Panel Limits Cuts In Dairy Price Supports

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/20/90

WASHINGTON -- The Senate Agriculture Committee voted to spare the nation's dairy farmers from any further cuts in dairy price supports over the next five years. The panel agreed to make the current milk support price -- $10.10 for each 100 pounds -- the five-year floor price for milk producers. They would allow for adjustments in the support price as government-owned dairy surpluses fluctuate in size, but in no case could the support price fall below $10.10.

But committee members rebuffed their chairman, Democratic Sen. Patrick Leahy of Vermont, by voting to prohibit the government from undertaking another massive buy-out of dairy herds to control surpluses of butter and other dairy products.

Disenchantment with the $1.8 billion whole-herd buy-out in the mid-1980s -- also known as the dairy termination program -- was so great on the committee that even Democrats deserted Sen. Leahy in the 16-2 vote yesterday. Only Republican Sen. Rudy Boschwitz of Minnesota, a major dairy-farming state like Vermont, took his side.

The amendment was advanced by Sens. Max Baucus (D., Mont.) and David Boren (D., Okla.), who both represent major beef cattle-raising states, and it was strongly supported by the National Cattlemen's Association and other farm groups.
