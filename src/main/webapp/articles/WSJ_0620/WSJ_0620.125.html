Hunt Brothers' Art, Coins Find Brisk Demand at Sale

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/20/90

NEW YORK -- The first leg of a court-ordered sale of the art and coin collections of William Herbert Hunt and Nelson Bunker Hunt met with considerable success last night. At Sotheby's Holdings Inc.'s New York auction house, there was brisk demand for the one-time Dallas billionaire brothers' collections of Greek and Roman antiquities. Bidding for the roughly 2,000-year-old marble statues, bronzes and vases came from private collectors and museums, and many of the pieces sold for double their pre-sale estimated prices.

The total for the sale was $11.4 million. Sotheby's had expected it to fetch $5 million to $7 million.

It was good news for the Hunt brothers, whose businesses are now in bankruptcy proceedings. In an unusual arrangement with the bankruptcy trustees and creditors, the brothers stood to gain if the sales were a success.

If the total raised exceeds a certain amount yet to be determined by the court, the Hunts will receive a portion of the remainder as compensation, according to R. Carter Pate, trustee for the Nelson Bunker Hunt liquidating trust. The brothers spent time helping to sell the collection through interviews and at receptions Sotheby's has hosted worldwide.

All told, Sotheby's estimates that the three-day series of sales will fetch about $20 million.
