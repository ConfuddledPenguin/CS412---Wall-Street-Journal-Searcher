Pension Fund Completes South African Divestment

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/20/90

NEW YORK -- New York City's largest public employee pension fund said it sold shares valued at $563 million to complete its divestment of stock in companies doing business in South Africa. The $17.6-billion New York City Employees Retirement System said the shares in 31 companies were sold in the final phase of the divestment program begun in 1984. Shares valued at $262 million in 44 companies with South Africa ties had been sold earlier.

The divestment program applied to almost 90% of the system's $8.6 billion in stocks invested "passively" in an index fund. A spokeswoman said the pension system's trustees haven't decided if the South Africa divestiture retirement should also apply to the $895 million in shares invested by so-called active money managers.

The public employee fund is the only one of New York City's five pension systems to complete a South Africa-related divestment program. The other city funds either don't invest in stocks or have approved divestment programs without implementing them.
