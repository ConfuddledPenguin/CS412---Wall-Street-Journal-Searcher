New Jersey Casino Panel Sets Hearing on Stake Rule

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 06/20/90

The New Jersey Casino Control Commission said it will hold a hearing July 24 to consider whether to change its rule limiting "passive" institutional investing in casinos. Currently, anyone owning more than 5% of a publicly traded casino company is subject to the commission's controls. But representatives of the casino industry have said such a rule restricts outside investment in their companies.

In a statement, the commission's acting chairwoman, Valerie H. Armstrong, said she doesn't believe the rule serves as a disincentive to outside investment but said she ordered the hearing "to allow the casino industry, the investment community, the attorney general's office and other interested parties an opportunity to be heard."
