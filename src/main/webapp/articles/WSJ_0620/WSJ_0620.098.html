Business Brief -- Allegheny Ludlum Corp.: Steel Concern Says Strength In Markets Boosts Orders

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/20/90

Allegheny Ludlum Corp. told securities analysts that its markets, except for automotive, are strong and that orders are 25% ahead of its 1990 plan. In the current fiscal second quarter, ending July 2, the company said shipments should be up at least 10% from the year-earlier's 126,419 tons. In the 1989 second quarter the company earned $36.7 million, or $1.63 a share.

However, the Pittsburgh specialty steel producer said it expects sales in the latest quarter to decline from $333.8 million a year ago, citing the absence of raw material surcharges.

"We are surprised at the strength in the market. Our backlog is growing, exports are turning up, and the third quarter is looking much stronger than anticipated," the company said.
