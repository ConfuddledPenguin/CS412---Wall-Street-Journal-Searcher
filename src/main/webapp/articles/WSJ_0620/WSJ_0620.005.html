Eagle-Picher Expects Asbestos-Claim Costs To Hit $110 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/20/90

CINCINNATI -- Eagle-Picher Industries Inc. said that costs related to asbestos claims are expected to total about $110 million this year and that it's concerned about a sudden upturn in the number of such claims being filed. The industrial company also reported that in its second quarter ended May 31 net rose 74% to $22.6 million, or $2.11 a share, from $13 million, or $1.22 a share, a year earlier. The latest quarter included a gain of $11.9 million, or $1.11 a share, from the sale of a division.

Sales slid about 5% to $184.4 million from $193.1 million.

But despite improvements in operations, especially those businesses serving the automotive industry, the company said its asbestos litigation costs remains a "serious problem."

Until the second quarter, the number of new claims had been decreasing, a trend that the company had expected to continue. But in the quarter, about 6,200 new claims were received compared with 4,000 a year earlier and 4,900 in the 1987 quarter, the company said.

In the quarter, the company said it disposed of 3,600 claims, compared with 3,400 a year earlier and 1,500 in the 1988 second quarter. Total costs related to those settlements were about $31 million for the quarter and $62 million for the six months.

To fund litigation costs, the company has said it intends to sell off additional assets, including its trim division and injection-molding division, which make rubber molded parts and trim for the automotive industry. "In order to avoid a severe liquidity problem, the company must complete the sale of these assets on a timely basis," the company said.

The company is a defendant in about 62,100 claims stemming from its manufacture, from 1934 until 1971, of an asbestos-containing insulating cement used principally in shipbuilding and refinery construction.
