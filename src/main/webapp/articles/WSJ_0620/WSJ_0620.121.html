CD Yields at Banks And Brokerage Firms Fell Slightly for Week

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/20/90

NEW YORK -- Yields on certificates of deposit at major banks and brokerage firms fell slighly in the week that ended yesterday. Average yields on small-denomination CDs sold directly by banks were flat to down slightly. The average yield on a one-year consumer CD eased to 7.85% from 7.87%, while the five-year CD average dipped to 8.09% from 8.11%, according to an 18-bank survey by Banxquote Money Markets, an information service here.

While none of the banks in the survey offered a yield as high as 9%, investors can still find consumer CDs at some banks that yield that much or more.

Average yields on small-denomination CDs sold by leading broker dealers were mixed. The average yield on six-month CDs slipped to 8.17% from 8.27%, while the average for two-year CDs remained at 8.30%, according to Banxquote.

Meanwhile, average yields for large-denomination CDs, which typically require deposits of $90,000 or more, were down slightly. The average yield on one-year jumbo CDs dipped to 7.90% from 7.98%, while five-year maturities eased to 8.22% from 8.25%.

"Because banks are now reducing their loan portfolios, they are not hungry for cash in order to make new loans. That's why they can afford to lower their CD rates," said Norberto Mehl, chairman of Banxquote. He said he expects the pattern to continue for the foreseeable future.
