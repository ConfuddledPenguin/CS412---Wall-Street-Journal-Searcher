Marketing & Media: Time Warner Inc. Forms Venture With Quincy Jones

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/20/90

NEW YORK -- Time Warner Inc. and music producer Quincy Jones formed a joint venture for the development of television, film and music projects. Terms weren't disclosed. Time Warner's strategic-development arm, Time Warner Enterprises, will be the financial backer and a 50% owner of Quincy Jones Entertainment Co. Time Warner Enterprises also said it will "support" a second company, Quincy Jones Broadcasting, which will buy television and radio stations. The nature of that relationship couldn't be learned.

Quincy Jones Entertainment is producing a new comedy, "Fresh Prince of Bel Air," for the NBC network for next fall; it is also making a talk show hosted by Jesse Jackson for local stations. While Time Warner will fund the venture, Mr. Jones will run the new company as chairman and chief executive officer, and he will control all creative matters. Kevin Wendle, a former executive with Fox Broadcasting Co., will be president and chief operating officer of Quincy Jones Entertainment.

Mr. Jones is a music producer who has released his own albums as well as produced the records of other artists, including "Thriller" by Michael Jackson. Mr. Jones's album label, Qwest Records, continues to be associated with Time Warner's record company, Warner Bros. Records.
