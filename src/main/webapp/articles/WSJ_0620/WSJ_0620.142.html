Getty Petroleum Says Unit May Be Indicted On Excise-Tax Charge

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/20/90

JERICHO, N.Y. -- Getty Petroleum Corp. said the U.S. Attorney's office in Brooklyn intends to seek indictments against the company's Getty Terminals Corp. unit and an executive. The independent gasoline marketer said the possible indictments are related to an alleged conspiracy with a customer to evade certain federal gasoline excise taxes in 1985.

The office of U.S. Attorney Andrew Maloney advised the company that the government intends to seek the indictments, Getty said. Getty called the allegations without merit and said that if it is indicted, it will vigorously defend itself.

Getty Petroleum, formerly Power Test Corp., was formed out of the purchase of the Getty name, marketing assets, and terminals from Texaco Inc., when the White Plains, N.Y., oil giant bought Getty Oil Co. Getty Petroleum also markets heating oil and is a wholesale distributor of a variety of petroleum products through its East Coast petroleum storage and distribution network.

Spokesmen for Getty and Mr. Maloney's office couldn't be reached for elaboration.
