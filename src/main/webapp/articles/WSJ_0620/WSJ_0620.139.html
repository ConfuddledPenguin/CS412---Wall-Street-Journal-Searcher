Business Brief -- H.J. Heinz Co.: Quarterly Net Climbed 16%; Firm Cites Price Increases

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/20/90

H.J. Heinz Co., citing increased prices of its food products, reported that fiscal fourth-quarter net income increased nearly 16% as sales rose about 4%. In the quarter ended May 2, net was $135.3 million, or 51 cents a share, compared with $117.2 million, or 44 cents a share, a year earlier.

Sales were $1.69 billion, up from $1.63 billion. Sales gains of products such as ketchup and tuna were offset by declines in other products, the company said in Pittsburgh.

For the year, profit jumped nearly 15% to $504.5 million, or $1.90 a share, from $440.2 million, or $1.67 a share. Sales rose nearly 5%, to $6.09 billion from $5.8 billion. Growth came from both price increases and volume gains, especially from Heinz's major brands, the company said.
