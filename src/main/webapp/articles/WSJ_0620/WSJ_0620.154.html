Ernst & Young Receives Soviet Approval for Audits

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/20/90

NEW YORK -- Ernst & Young said it received approval from the Soviet Ministry of Finance to perform official audits for joint ventures of Soviet and Western companies. The approval given to the professional-services firm to audit balance sheets of the ventures follows similar approval given to Arthur Andersen & Co., another major accounting and consulting firm.

In addition, Ernst & Young said Oleg Valerious, director of the official Soviet audit agency, Inaudit, was joining Ernst & Young's joint venture in the Soviet Union, Ernst & Young Vneshaudit. The venture is 50%-owned by Ernst & Young, 45%-owned by Vneshconsult, a Soviet management consulting firm, and 5%-owned by Vneshtechnika, a state technical-services organization.

The Ernst & Young venture in the Soviet Union has offices in Moscow and Kiev; it currently does work in accounting in addition to some tax and consulting. A spokeswoman for Ernst & Young said the approval enables the firm to offer a full range of tax services as well.
