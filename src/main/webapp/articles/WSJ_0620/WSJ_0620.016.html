Bell Atlantic to Buy Simborg, Hospital Data Systems Firm

  • Author: Pamela J. Podger
  • Date: 06/20/90

PHILADELPHIA -- Bell Atlantic Corp. said it signed an agreement to acquire Simborg Systems Corp. in an all-stock transaction but didn't disclose the terms. Closely held Simborg, based in Greenbrae, Calif., develops hospital information management systems and is a pioneer in open architecture systems for the health care industry.

Bell Atlantic is a telecommunications company.

Bell Atlantic said the purchase -- which would involve its first subsidiary in the hospital information industry -- will be closed within 60 days and the venture will be renamed Bell Atlantic Healthcare Systems, providing total patient care solutions for hospitals and other health care facilities in the U.S.and Canada.

Donald W. Simborg, who founded the company in 1984, will become president of Bell Atlantic Healthcare Systems. All of Simborg Systems' 40 employees are expected to join the new company, Bell Atlantic said.

Simborg System's STAT LAN hospital network product, currently used in 25 U.S. hospitals, will complement network and business systems offered by Bell Atlantic units.

"STAT LAN is a backbone to a network to which a customer can plug in applications," a Bell Atlantic spokeswoman said.

"Healthcare is a major thrust for Bell Atlantic," said Jeffrey S. McDermott, president of Bell Atlantic Software and Systems Solutions division, which will oversee the acquisition.

The STAT LAN health care information network uses the latest local area network and microcomputer technology, allowing different hospital departments to run independent systems and to share information over the network.

The company said Bell Atlantic Healthcare Systems will market sales of STAT LAN and will continue joint marketing accords with distributors such as Andersen Consulting and Ameritech Corp., both based in Chicago.

Douglas Ryckman, managing partner of Andersen Consulting, said hospital systems must evolve to include open architecture and networking.

Industry consultant Sheldon Dorenfest said the deep pockets of Bell Atlantic will give Simborg Systems the "resources and staying power to further enhance STAT LAN and become a major player in health care information systems."

Clayton Brown, an analyst for A.G. Edwards in New York, said the venture is another way for Bell Atlantic to diversify. "The deal is different from Bell Atlantic's cellular phone and yellow pages mainstays," he said.

Bell Atlantic shares closed yesterday at $49.75, off 25 cents, in New York Stock Exchange composite trading.
