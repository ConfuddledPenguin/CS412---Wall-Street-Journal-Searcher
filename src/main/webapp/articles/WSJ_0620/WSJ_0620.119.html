General Instrument Says Profit Fell 29% In Fiscal 1st Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/20/90

NEW YORK -- General Instrument Corp. reported that fiscal first-quarter net income fell 29% because of a year-ago gain, but results, excluding the special item, were "ahead of plan." Revenue and earnings grew in General Instrument's biggest business, cable-TV and satellite equipment, and in specialized semiconductors used in microelectronics.

In wagering systems, profit declined because foreign shipments of lottery equipment didn't match year-ago levels. And the defense electronics business, producing radar warning and surveillance equipment, was hurt by lower foreign orders at the government systems unit. Dalmo Victor, another unit of the defense electronics business that produces the same type of equipment, is doing well, a spokesman said.

Net for the first period ended May 27 dropped to $23.2 million, or 84 cents a share, from $32.5 million, or 96 cents a share, a year earlier.

Year-ago results include $10.3 million, or 30 cents a share, of earnings from discontinued operations. In May 1989, General Instrument sold its majority interest in Sytek Inc. to the Hughes Aircraft division of General Motors Corp. Sytek is a Mountain View, Calif., manufacturer of local area networks, used to tie together personal computers.

Also, shares outstanding declined 19% to 27.5 million, from 33.8 million, because of a stock buy-back in August.

Revenue climbed 9.8% to $379.5 million from $345.6 million.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, General Instrument's shares rose 50 cents to close at $35.75.

Separately, General Instrument named Ronald A. Ostertag president of its power-semiconductor division based in Hicksville, N.Y. Effective Sept. 1, Mr. Ostertag, currently senior vice president of operations for the VideoCipher division, succeeds Christopher F. Sutphin, who retires early next year.
