Coca-Cola Enterprises Faces $1 Million Fine In Unit's Conviction

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/20/90

ATLANTA -- Coca-Cola Enterprises Inc. said it could face a fine of as much as $1 million following a conviction on conspiracy and bribery charges related to the sale of Coke to a U.S. Navy base. In February, the Justice Department issued an 11-count indictment against a Coca-Cola bottling unit, Mid-Atlantic Coca-Cola Bottling Co., on charges related to the sale of soft drinks at the Navy operations base in Norfolk, Va. The indictment also charged that illegal gratuities were paid to a former civilian U.S. Navy procurement officer.

Two weeks ago, a federal court in Norfolk found Mid-Atlantic guilty of all 11 counts under the indictment. In a Securities and Exchange Commission filing, Coca-Cola Enterprises said it may appeal. Sentencing is set for Aug. 3.

The indictment, covering sales in 1984 and 1985, also named Michael A. Hedges, a former Mid-Atlantic area vice president, sales, and James J. Valerio, a former Mid-Atlantic area controller.

Before the trial, Mr. Hedges pleaded guilty to two counts of paying illegal gratuities, according to the SEC filing. Mr. Valerio was acquitted of a charge of conspiracy, the filing said. Mr. Hedges couldn't be reached for comment.

Coca-Cola Enterprises, a bottling business formed in 1986, is 49%-owned by Coca-Cola Co. Coca-Cola acquired a controlling interest in Mid-Atlantic in September 1984, subsequently turning over its ownership of the business to Coca-Cola Enterprises.

Coca-Cola officials couldn't be reached for comment.
