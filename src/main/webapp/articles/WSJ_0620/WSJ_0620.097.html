Helene Curtis Posts Loss for First Quarter Despite Sales Increase

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/20/90

CHICAGO -- Helene Curtis Industries Inc., its earnings hurt by new-product spending and a strategy that seeks to boost market share through price cuts, reported a fiscal first-quarter loss, despite a 22% sales increase. For the quarter ended May 31, the maker of personal-care products had a net loss of $1.8 million, or 20 cents a share, compared with year-earlier net income of $2.2 million, or 24 cents a share. Sales rose to $195.1 million from $160.2 million.

The company attributed much of the sales increase to the recent introduction of its Degree antiperspirant/deodorant, as well as "continued gains by our U.S. consumer brands and strong performances by hair-care products in Japan and Canada."

Helene Curtis said its "strong" domestic business reflects increased market share in hair-care products, and said the more than 20% sales rise in its Suave line "underscores the effectiveness of our strategy of lowering this brand's prices by 10% last year to reinforce its position."

The company said that, as it had projected, higher marketing expenses and capital layouts aimed at boosting manufacturing and distribution cut into earnings. And it reiterated that it "will be difficult for us to match our fiscal 1990 earnings performance in 1991."

In composite New York Stock Exchange trading yesterday, Helene Curtis shares dipped 50 cents to $25.125.
