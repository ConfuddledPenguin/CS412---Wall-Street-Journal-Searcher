International Brief -- Mercedes-Benz AG: World-Wide Group Sales Rose 10% by End of May

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/20/90

Mercedes-Benz AG, Stuttgart, said sales of cars and trucks grew firmly in the first five months of 1990, with world-wide group sales increasing 10% from a year earlier to 24 billion marks ($14.32 billion). But officials of the vehicle manufacturing arm of West Germany's largest industrial group, Daimler-Benz AG, were guarded about prospects for 1990 earnings growth.

Mercedes Chairman Werner Niefer projected an increase in group sales to 58 billion marks from 56.37 billion marks in 1989. Mercedes accounts for nearly three-quarters of Daimler's global sales.

In Europe, excluding West Germany, Mercedes boosted unit sales 3% in the five months to an unspecified level, Mr. Niefer said. In West Germany, unit sales rose slightly to 105,000 cars.

The official said Mercedes-Benz sales in Japan were up 60% from a year earlier, making the company Japan's largest importer of foreign cars. Unit sales of cars in the U.S. gained 5% in the five months. World-wide, the company sold 240,000 cars.
