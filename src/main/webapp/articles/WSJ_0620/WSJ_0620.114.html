Marketing & Media: Kansas City Paper Sets Its Last Edition

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/20/90

KANSAS CITY, Mo. (AP) -- The Kansas City Evening News will print its 27th and final edition today even though it was making a profit, Publisher Stephen F. Rose announced. Mr. Rose blamed the newspaper's demise on city officials who were forcing him to take its teen-age sales force off the streets.

"We were forced to make a very painful decision, to go down in history as perhaps the only paper to close while making money," Mr. Rose said. "We simply wouldn't be making any money if we had to convert to home delivery."

Mr. Rose, who is president of a family-owned group of 18 suburban weekly papers called Sun Publications Inc., was the sole investor in the Evening News, an afternoon tabloid.

It was the second unsuccessful attempt to start a metropolitan newspaper in recent months. Ralph Ingersoll II launched the St. Louis Sun in September but closed that afternoon tabloid in April, citing insufficient circulation.

Mr. Rose said Kansas City, Mo., authorities had been cracking down on the street sellers, including issuing a ticket to one and forcing others to move off street medians. City officials cited safety and traffic considerations.

Apart from its street sales, The Evening News was sold in news racks and stores. About 22,800 papers were sold when the paper started May 14. Last week, Mr. Rose said, sales were averaging about 15,000 copies a day.
