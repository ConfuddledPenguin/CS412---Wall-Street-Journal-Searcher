Business Brief -- Topps Co.: Growth in New Lines Fuels Jump in 1st-Period Earnings

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/20/90

Topps Co. reported record results for its first quarter on strong growth in its staple baseball card and Bazooka bubble gum lines but particularly in its newer products featuring Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles and New Kids on the Block. Topps, based in New York, reported net income of $13.9 million, or 44 cents a share, for the quarter ended June 2. In the comparable quarter last year, net was $8.5 million, or 27 cents a share. Sales rose to $86.2 million from $62.8 million.

During the quarter, the company said, it paid $25 million of long-term debt, reducing its total debt to $60 million.

Topps shares closed yesterday at $22, unchanged, in national over-the-counter trading.
