West German Exports Outlook

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/20/90

BONN -- West German exports are expected to rise 7.2% this year, as demand for capital goods continues to be strong, according to a survey by the Association of German Chambers of Commerce and Industry of 45 commercial offices that represent the country abroad. Export growth will slow from last year's 12.5% rate of increase, the survey found, but the organization said this is still good, in view of a general slowdown in world trade growth. The continued rise in exports underlines the strong position West German industry has managed to maintain internationally, the association said.

A regional breakdown shows that an 8.5% increase in exports is likely to other member nations of the European Community, which account for 72% of West Germany's total shipments abroad. Exports to North America are expected to drop 4.3% this year, after a 2.5% increase in 1989; the reversal reflects the strength of the mark against the dollar, which makes German exports relatively expensive.
