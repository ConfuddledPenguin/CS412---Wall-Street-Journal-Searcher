Lufthansa Joins Program To Increase Service to U.S.

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/20/90

WASHINGTON -- The Transportation Department gave Lufthansa German Airlines temporary authority to fly between Charlotte, N.C., and Frankfurt, West Germany. The department granted authority for six months and said it would extend authority further if the German government eases restrictions on fares set by U.S. airlines for flights originating in Germany.

The department granted Lufthansa's application under a new program designed to increase international air service to U.S. cities. Under the program, route authority is granted foreign airlines on an expedited basis if certain conditions are met. That bypasses the lengthy bilateral negotiations that usually are required.

The department said that U.S. airlines have complained that the German government has blocked efforts by U.S. carriers to set fares lower than Lufthansa's on flights originating in Germany.
