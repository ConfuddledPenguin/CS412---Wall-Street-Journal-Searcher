Thomas Industries Says 2nd-Quarter Net Will Fall 20% to 30%

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/20/90

LOUISVILLE, Ky. -- Thomas Industries Inc., citing softer than expected U.S. residential and commercial construction, said it expects second-quarter profit to fall 20% to 30% from 43 cents a share a year earlier. But the producer of lighting products, compressors and pumps said sales are expected to top the year-earlier $112.5 million.

Thomas said that the second-half outlook is uncertain but that earnings for the year are expected to meet or exceed 1989 levels, adjusted for non-recurring gains.

In 1989, Thomas earned $20.6 million, or $2.02 a share, on sales of $436.6 million. Included were non-recurring gains of $7.4 million, or 75 cents a share.

In addition to the U.S. construction slowdown, Thomas said it has been hurt by a construction trade strike in Canada that has weakened demand for its lighting products. Also, it said, it has been hurt by strong competitive pricing pressures.

However, the company said it has been increasing its lighting sales and market share. It added that the compressor and pump markets are buffering it somewhat from the cyclical swings in the construction industry.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Thomas closed at $17, down $1.
