Business Brief -- Freeport McMoRan Inc.: Unit of New Orleans Firm Acquires Chevron Reserves

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/20/90

Freeport McMoRan Resource Partners L.P. said it and joint venture partners acquired from Chevron U.S.A. Inc., for $137 million, reserves estimated at 35 million to 50 million barrels of oil and eight billion cubic feet of natural gas. An additional $13 million will be paid to Chevron for costs already incurred. Chevron U.S.A. is a unit of Chevron Corp., San Francisco. Freeport-McMoRan Resource Partners is a unit of Freeport McMoRan Inc., New Orleans.

The acquired reserves are in an area offshore from Louisiana in the Gulf of Mexico where Freeport Resource Partners has a 67 million-long-ton sulfur reserve with venture partners IMC Fertilizer Group Inc. and Homestake Mining Co.

Freeport Resource Partners said it expects expenditures of close to $100 million to develop the acquired oil and gas reserves. Chevron retains the right, under certain conditions, to a portion of the profits of future oil production if price or volumes exceed certain levels.
