International: Continental AG And Czech Firm Sign Tire Accord

  • Author: Terence Roth
  • Date: 06/20/90

FRANKFURT -- Continental AG of West Germany, having already sealed cooperation links with a major East German tire maker, has now signed a similar pact with Czechoslovakia's largest tire company for a quick start in Eastern Europe. The state-owned Barum Group has three major plants that together produce 3.4 million radial tires for passenger cars and 2.5 million truck tires annually. The Czechoslovak enterprise, which supplies tires to the domestic Skoda auto works and other auto producers in Eastern Europe, had 1989 annual sales of about $441 million and employs 10,000 workers.

Hanover-based Continental, expected to plow fresh investments into the Barum venture, said it is still undecided whether the cooperation will be in the form of a joint venture or a straight equity stake in Barum.

"It could be anything. It may take six months, but our goal is to have the framework set in November," said Continental spokesman Dieter von Herz.

Although committed to a business link, both sides are waiting for the passage of legislation by Czechoslovakia's newly elected parliament that would privatize industry and allow foreign purchases of domestic companies. Prague's newly elected democratic government, eager to attract foreign investment to modernize its sluggish economy, has pledged quick free-market reforms by autumn.

Several Western companies have entered into talks with Czechoslovak companies, but few deals have been signed ahead of the parliamentary green light.

Continental didn't wait, said Mr. von Herz, because "Barum is extremely interesting to us. They have their market, and the technical status of their plants is remarkably good for Eastern Europe."

Continental, the world's fourth-largest tire company, last month reported net group profit of 227.8 million Deutsche marks ($135.9 million) on 1989 sales of 8.38 marks billion. Continental last year produced 50 million passenger car tires and 8.5 million truck tires.

A common thread linking the acquisitions also has been Continental's longstanding business ties with Volkswagen AG, a major client for its tires. Volkswagen is negotiating with Skoda for a joint production agreement.

Continental recently secured an agreement to pursue a business linkup with East Germany's VEB Reifenkombinat Fuerstenwalde, which controls five plants producing 8.5 million tires for cars and trucks annually. Continental also acquired three major makers of other automotive rubber products in East Germany.

A major client of the East German tire and rubber groups has been East German car maker IFA-Kombinats Personnenkraftwagen, with which Volkswagen signed a joint production pact earlier this year as part of Volkswagen's 5 billion mark investment program in East Germany.

While Continental is moving hard into the East, analysts observe that other international tire producers are hard on its heels.

"All the big tire companies are following the auto makers into the East. Most of them are looking for state-owned firms that they can pick up and restructure," said Peter Sedlmeier, an analyst for Bayerische Hypotheken-& Wechsel-Bank AG.
