Data on Australian Economy

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/20/90

SYDNEY, Australia -- May data showed demand in the Australian economy continued to slow, but the effects were uneven among sectors, the Reserve Bank of Australia, the central bank, said. In its latest bulletin, the central bank said it "continued its recent policy of keeping monetary conditions steady" during May. The government and Reserve Bank eased interest rates in three steps between January and April, after nearly two years of pushing rates up, but have declined to lower rates further until they see clear signs of slower imports and inflation.

Arguing that "quarterly figures tend to be volatile and sometimes misleading," the central bank put aside consideration of national accounts issued for the quarter ended March 31, which showed an unexpected 1.8% expansion in gross domestic product. It said private final demand in the economy "was flat and investment {was} falling."

Balance-of-payments figures for March "offered disappointing evidence on the strength of imports and the size of the current-account deficit," the bank said. But "April's results . . . supported the belief that reduced domestic demand would feed into lower demand for imports."
