Jury Indicts Seller Of Securities, Charges He Defrauded Buyers

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 06/20/90

CHICAGO -- A federal grand jury indicted Michael S. Douglas of Antioch, Ill., for allegedly selling unregistered securities and defrauding more than 300 investors of millions of dollars. Mr. Douglas, 37 years old, who the indictment says failed to disclose to investors that he has twice been convicted in Illinois for theft and deceptive practices, collected about $45 million from investors between 1987 and late 1989 by selling securities and other market instruments through three trading companies he controlled.

The limited partnership interests he offered to investors constituted securities under federal law, the indictment says, and thus should have been registered with the Securities and Exchange Commission.

Mr. Douglas is charged with misrepresenting to investors in at least eight states the actual investment strategy of the limited partnerships he offered through his company, D&S Trading Group Ltd.

Mr. Douglas also told investors D&S had historical returns of between 10% and 25% on a monthly basis, but those returns were "invented," according to the indictment. Mr. Douglas also calculated his share of the purported profit and a management fee on the basis of profit that was "frequently nonexistent or grossly inflated," the grand jury said.

When the SEC began investigating D&S Trading, Mr. Douglas established a second company, Analytic Trading Systems Inc., to prevent discovery of the scheme, according to the indictment. He subsequently established a third company, Analytic Trading Service Inc., for the same reason, the indictment says.

Federal prosecutors said they haven't determined how much of the investors' money had been pocketed and how much remains available. Mr. Douglas's companies are currently being liquidated.

Mr. Douglas, who is being held pending a hearing scheduled for tomorrow, didn't enter a plea yesterday.
