Court Rejects Appeal By Mission Reinsurers Over Future Claims

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/20/90

SAN FRANCISCO -- A California appeals court upheld a ruling by the state's insurance commissioner that allows the state's Department of Insurance to obtain lump-sum payments of future policyholder claims from the reinsurers of Mission Insurance Co. Mission and its units were declared insolvent and placed in liquidation by California insurance regulators in February 1987. The appellate court ruling dismissed a challenge by Underwriters Reinsurance Co., one of Mission's reinsurers, to the state's demand that it pay claims for future damages based on an industrywide actuarial method of calculating expected future claims.

Claims costs that are not paid by reinsurers would be passed on to the public through assessments by the California Insurance Guarantee Association. There are no estimates of what future claims might amount to; so far, however, the state has collected more than $250 million on behalf of California consumers.

"This goes a long way towards ensuring that the public will not have to pay for the mistakes of Mission and its reinsurers," Insurance Commissioner Roxani Gillespie said. "The reinsurers will no longer be able to drag their feet and avoid paying balances due on the premise that my demand for all damages, including those which will occur in the future, is premature."
