Marketing & Media: New American Magazine Stake Is Mulled by Firm

  • Author: Patrick M. Reilly
  • Date: 06/20/90

NEW YORK -- New American Magazine Co., the debt-laden publisher of Smart, Psychology Today and Mother Earth News, said it is discussing the sale of a half-interest in the company to a Japanese publisher. Owen Lipstein, president and controlling shareholder of New American, said the Japanese company, which he declined to identify, signed a letter of intent in May to "put substantial equity into the company."

Although Japanese concerns haven't previously made any significant investments in the U.S. publishing industry, Veronis, Suhler & Associates, the New York-based investment banker, linked Mr. Lipstein with an interested Japanese publisher.

Meanwhile, Terry McDonnell, who founded Smart with Mr. Lipstein, said that a U.S. publisher has shown "serious" interest in purchasing the 250,000-circulation men's life-style magazine started last year. He declined to identify the publisher, but industry executives said Playboy Enterprises Inc. and Straight Arrow Publishers Inc., publisher of Rolling Stone, are among possible bidders.

A spokeswoman for Playboy said, "We are talking" to Smart, "but we aren't even close to a deal. We are talking to a lot of other magazines too." Straight Arrow executives said through a spokesman that they are "not interested."

Mr. Lipstein acknowledged in an interview that New American is strapped for cash and last week laid off 30 staffers, or about 50% of the employees, at all three of the magazines. The layoffs follow the departure of a number of top sales and editorial executives from the staffs of the magazines.

A number of smaller publishers, such as New American, have been hurt by an industrywide downturn in advertising sales, and some consumer magazines have folded. But Mr. Lipstein said most of the company staffers laid off will be rehired "when new partners come aboard." And he dismissed industry speculation that New American may soon become another industry fatality. "We're just operating on a tighter schedule," Mr. Lipstein said. "We are still alive."

Earlier this year, Mr. Lipstein kept creditors and investors at bay when he sold his flagship magazine, American Health, to Reader's Digest Association Inc. for $29.1 million. He also halted publication of Psychology Today in December and said it would be redesigned and relaunched this fall.
