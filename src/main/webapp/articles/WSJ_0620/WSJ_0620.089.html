Dividend News: Consolidated Freight Suspends Its Dividend As Part of Credit Pact

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/20/90

MENLO PARK, Calif. -- Consolidated Freightways Inc., buffeted by continuing losses at its Emery Worldwide air-freight unit, said it will suspend its quarterly dividend as part of an agreement with lenders providing a new line of credit of as much as $860 million. Consolidated said the five-year agreement permits it to reinstate its 26.5-cent-a-share payout "subject to meeting certain performance standards." A spokesman declined to say how soon that might be; Consolidated was scheduled to pay its next dividend after the second quarter.

The trucking and air-freight company had gone nine years without a quarterly deficit until its $230 million acquisition of loss-plagued Emery last year; Consolidated also agreed to take on about $240 million in Emery debt.

Hurt by a slump in its own business and higher-than-anticipated losses at Emery, Consolidated reported a fourth-quarter loss of $32.3 million, or $1.01 a share, on revenue of $991.3 million. In the first quarter, its loss widened to $33.9 million, or $1.06 a share, on revenue of $997.7 million.

Consolidated said that although losses at Emery have narrowed in recent months, "Emery's long-term prospects are dependent on substantially increased business levels." At the time of its acquisition, Emery, battered by stiffening competition in the international air-freight market, had three consecutive years of losses. Consolidated viewed the purchase as central to its strategy to compete in the global freight-hauling business.
