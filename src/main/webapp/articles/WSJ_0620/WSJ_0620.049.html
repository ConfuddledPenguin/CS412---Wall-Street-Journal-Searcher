Commodity Funds Fall in May

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/20/90

CHICAGO -- The average managed commodity fund lost 5.8% in May, according to Norwood Securities, which tracks the performance of 133 publicly offered commodity funds. Jay Klopfenstein, Norwood president, said a sharp rally in U.S. Treasury bond futures in the first week of May caused "many fund managers to suffer severe losses." He said many managers were short bond futures, which had been in a steady downtrend since peaking last December.

For the first five months of 1990, the funds showed an average gain of 4.3%.
