Kollmorgen Corp. Says It Won Proxy Contest In Final Ballot Report

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/20/90

SIMSBURY, Conn. -- Kollmorgen Corp. said the final report of the balloting in a proxy fight for control of the board confirmed that the maker of motors and electro-optical systems beat back a challenge by Vernitron Corp. But closely held Vernitron, a Deer Park, N.Y., maker of motors and controls that has been pursuing Kollmorgen for some 18 months, disputed the final report of the inspectors of the election.

Vernitron said it "presented a number of significant challenges to votes affecting hundreds of thousands of shares." Vernitron "believes the inspectors {of the election} erred in ruling with Kollmorgen management on these challenges and is considering" taking legal action.

Late in May, Kollmorgen said preliminary numbers indicated that it won the proxy fight. Vernitron said then and reiterated that its slate of directors won a plurality of the shares voted at the meeting if the 3.1 million shares Kollmorgen sold to an investor group in March are excluded from the total. Vernitron has claimed that Kollmorgen sold the 3.1 million shares to the investor group as a defensive measure against Vernitron.

Separately, the New York money manager Mario Gabelli disclosed in a Securities and Exchange Commission filing in Washington that a company he controls boosted its stake in Kollmorgen to 19.99% from 17.79%. The group to which Kollmorgen sold the 3.1 million shares, which includes Tinicum Enterprises Inc. of Garden City, N.Y., recently boosted its Kollmorgen holdings to 19.5%.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Kollmorgen shares closed unchanged at $11.50.
