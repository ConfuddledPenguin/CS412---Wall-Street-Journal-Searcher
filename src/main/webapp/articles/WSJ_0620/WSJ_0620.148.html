Business Brief -- Traditional Industries Inc.: Loss of $5.5 Million Posted For the Latest Nine Months

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/20/90

Traditional Industries Inc. posted a net loss of $5.5 million, or $1.46 a share, for the nine months ended March 31, reflecting in part the impact of a Federal Trade Commission settlement last year. Nine-month revenue dropped 8% to $40.8 million from $44.3 million. In the year-earlier period, the company had net income of $4.7 million, or $1.31 a share.

Traditional Industries, Agoura Hills, Calif., markets photographic products and services; it didn't report any third-quarter results. Company officials weren't available to provide additional figures or comment.

Last summer, the company reached a settlement with the FTC regarding some of its sales practices. Under the agreement, Traditional was required to set up a $250,000 trust fund to provide refunds to certain customers.

In a statement, Arland D. Dunn, chairman and chief executive officer, said, "We have experienced in the past two years an increase in canceled sales, defaulted payments and slower collections." These problems are due to the FTC settlement and to some bad accounts related to discontinued operations, he said.
