Letters to the Editor: Come On, Pozner, Show Us the Bruises

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/20/90

Andrew Ferguson's June 4 Leisure & Arts review of Vladimir Pozner's "Parting With Illusions" quotes Mr. Pozner as saying: "By 1947, I was getting beaten up at Stuyvesant High for being pro-Soviet." Baloney. I graduated New York City's Stuyvesant High School in 1950. (Question: was Edward Posner, my classmate, his brother?) No one got beaten up at Stuyvesant in those days. Our athletic teams were losers. Our idea of physicality was a rousing chess game.

As for being pro-Soviet . . . who wasn't? This was New York, and we were mostly the children of proletarian Jews. Anybody who got beaten up for his politics was probably a Catholic Republican, and I never heard of them until I entered the service during the Korean War.

Get real. Mr. Pozner and P.T. Barnum are brothers under the skin.

U. Harold Males

University Heights, Ohio
