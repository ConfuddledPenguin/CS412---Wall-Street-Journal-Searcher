What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/20/90

A NEW BUDGET PLAN is expected from a top Bush official in an effort to jump-start stalled talks with Congress. Darman's proposal reportedly includes deficit cuts of $45 billion to $50 billion, including a stiff $17 billion reduction in "entitlement" allocations. Democrats are likely to oppose the plan because it relies mostly on lowering domestic spending. ---

RJR Nabisco started a $5.3 billion refinancing effort that will drastically reshape its balance sheet. The proposal, backed by main shareholder KKR, reportedly seeks to retire more than $4 billion of the food and tobacco firm's poorest-performing junk bonds.

---

Housing starts sank 1.4% in May to their lowest level since 1982, in a further sign of a sluggish economy. The apartment market was the hardest hit.

---

Stock prices rebounded slightly while the bond market drifted lower. The Dow Jones industrials rose 11.38 points to 2893.56. The dollar weakened against most other currencies.

---

Crude oil prices fell to their lowest level in 18 months as signs of a drop in OPEC output largely failed to affect a market plagued by oversupply.

---

Tele-Communications Inc. proposed that its cable systems become NBC affiliates in rural areas that aren't served by broadcasters.

---

Donald Trump's bankers reported no progress in bailout talks. Dozens of Japanese creditors are unwilling to help the developer and casino owner.

---

Motorola is expected to unveil plans next week for a satellite communications system that may handle cellular phone calls in a few years.

---

Ames Department Stores posted a $429.1 million quarterly loss, possibly signaling more store closings. The firm's liabilities now exceed its assets.

---

Japan's economy grew at a 10.4% annual rate in the quarter that ended in March, as the country's exports jumped. Government officials tried to play down the increase in exports.

---

Woolworth plans to open as many as a dozen stores in East Germany by the end of next year. They would be the first U.S.-owned general merchandise outlets in that country.

---

American Stock Exchange President Kenneth Leibler is leaving for a top job at Liberty Financial. He was passed over last fall when the exchange picked a new chairman.

---

PS New Mexico named two officers to succeed Jerry Geist as chairman and chief executive, but critics said the new aides will bring little change to the troubled utility.

---

Goodyear expects to report a second-quarter loss as big as $10 million. The tire maker plans to dismiss 1,100 people in a revamping that will result in a $35 million pretax charge.

---

Bell Atlantic agreed to acquire Simborg Systems, a pioneer in healthcare data-management systems.

---
