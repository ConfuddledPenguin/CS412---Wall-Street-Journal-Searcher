Retired FDA Aide Indicted In Generic-Drug Inquiry

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/20/90

BALTIMORE (AP) -- A federal grand jury indicted the retired head of the Food and Drug Administration's generic-drug division on two counts of perjury in a corruption scandal involving the division. The indictment accuses Marvin Seife of lying to government investigators about meetings with generic-drug company executives and about whether he let the executives buy him meals, said U.S. Attorney Breckinridge Willcox.

The indictment was the latest development in a two-year investigation of corruption in the generic-drug industry and among its regulators.

Mr. Seife, of San Antonio, Texas, retired Dec. 31 after 17 years as director of the generic-drug division, where he was responsible for reviewing manufacturers' applications to market generic drugs.

According to the indictment, Mr. Seife in a sworn statement to an FDA investigator falsely denied meeting with two generic-drug company executives in 1987 to discuss FDA matters. The indictment also alleges that Mr. Seife made a similar false statement last year to a House subcommittee.

The second count of the indictment charges that Mr. Siefe lied to FDA investigators when he said he had never had meals with the executives. The indictment alleges he accepted numerous lunches, dinners and drinks paid for by generic-drug companies.

If convicted on both counts, Mr. Seife would face a maximum of 10 years in prison and a $500,000 fine.
