Inside Track: Two Top Executives at Reader's Digest Sell Large Part of Holdings in Company

  • Author: Alexandra Peers
  • Date: 06/20/90

NEW YORK -- Two top executives at Reader's Digest Association Inc. recently sold large chunks of their holdings in the Pleasantville, N.Y., publishing company, according to filings with the Securities and Exchange Commission. Richard F. McLoughlin, the company's president, sold 81% of his stock last month, receiving $4.1 million for the 175,000 shares he sold, according to the filings.

Kenneth O. Gilmore, editor in chief of Reader's Digest magazine and a company director, sold 72,000 Class A common shares for about $1.9 million, representing 45% of his holdings, according to the filings.

Both men declined to comment. But Bruce Trachtenberg, a spokesman for the company, said Mr. McLoughlin's sale was related to the president's approaching retirement June 30. According to SEC filings, Mr. McLoughlin retains 40,160 Class A common shares and 60 shares of the company's preferred stock.

The company went public Feb. 15 in an offering priced at $20 a share. At the time, the stock that the two officials had accumulated were converted to public shares.

Mr. McLoughlin sold his shares May 15 at an average price of $23.64 a share, while Mr. Gilmore's shares, sold on May 31, fetched an average of $26.13 a share. In New York Stock Exchange composite trading, Reader's Digest stock closed at $25.375, down 25 cents.

BLOCK SELLERS: In several sales in late April and May, four insiders at Blockbuster Entertainment Corp., a large video-rental chain, sold a total of 289,200 shares, SEC filings show.

The bulk of the shares were sold by Vice President Jeffrey Shearer. He sold 275,000 shares in several sales in late May and has 350,081 shares remaining, according to the filings. Mr. Shearer couldn't be reached for comment, but a company spokesman for the Fort Lauderdale, Fla.-based company said Mr. Shearer sold most of the shares "pursuant to a divorce settlement."

Thomas Gruber, senior vice president and chief marketing officer, sold 10,000 shares on May 25, and has 143,332 shares remaining, SEC filings show. He said he sold for the "purpose of paying taxes." The two other insiders, also vice presidents, sold 1,948 and 2,252 shares each, representing 100% and 31% of their holdings, respectively, according to the filings.

The insiders sold their shares at an average price of $22.43 a share. In New York Stock Exchange composite trading, Blockbuster shares closed up 37.5 cents at $23.375.

---

STOCK COMPACTING: Six vice presidents at Compaq Computer Corp. sold a total of $3.7 million of its stock in May, SEC filings show.

The two largest sales at the Houston-based company were by Vice President James M. Eckhart, who sold 10,000 common shares on May 7, and now holds 1,256 shares, and by Ross Cooley, also a vice president. Mr. Cooley sold 8,964 common shares on May 29 and now holds 1,035 shares, according to SEC filings. Both executives declined to comment.

The other four insiders sold between 1,500 and 5,000 shares, SEC filings show, representing between 1% and 39% of their holdings.

Bob Beach, a spokesman for Compaq, said all the insiders sold for personal reasons and added that the company has only four 30-day periods a year during which insiders can sell shares.

The insiders sold at share prices ranging from $104.79 to $120. The stock has since inched up. In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange yesterday, Compaq shares closed at $124.50, up 87.5 cents.
