Marketing & Media: Journal's Tanner Honored For Energy Affairs Career

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/20/90

NEW YORK -- James Tanner, national energy correspondent for The Wall Street Journal, was named winner of the 1989 Journalism Award of the International Association for Energy Economics. The award honored his extended career covering international energy affairs. Mr. Tanner received the award, which includes a $1,500 prize, at the association's annual conference in Copenhagen.

Mr. Tanner joined The Wall Street Journal in 1954 in the Dallas bureau; in the 1970s, he was responsible for natural resources coverage from the New York bureau. He briefly left the Journal in the 1980s, but he returned in 1986 as national energy correspondent based in Houston.
