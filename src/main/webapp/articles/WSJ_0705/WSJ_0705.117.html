Business Brief -- Talman Home Federal Savings: Investor Group Terminates $100 Million Purchase Offer

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/05/90

Talman Home Federal Savings & Loan Association of Chicago said a private investor group terminated a previously disclosed preliminary purchase offer of $10 a share, or $100 million. The investor group is led by Harrison I. Steans, who has headed a number of Chicago-area bank holding companies in recent years.

Talman said Mr. Steans dropped the bid after he had a chance to look at the company's books. The thrift said the figures caused Mr. Steans to revise downward his original earnings projections.

Talman also said it anticipates that second-quarter earnings will be lower than those in the first quarter, primarily because of increased costs of funds. It said earnings will also be hurt by expenses related to the transaction that was terminated.

In the first quarter of 1990, Talman Home had net income of $4.5 million, or 47 cents a share.

Talman's stock closed Tuesday in national over-the-counter trading at $4.75 a share, down $2.875.
