International: Development-Bank Plan For South Africa Advance

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/05/90

NEW YORK -- A proposed South African development bank took a step toward reality when Nelson Mandela met with Peter Goldmark, president of the Rockefeller Foundation. "What began as a germ of an idea is now a seedling," Mr. Goldmark said yesterday after his return to New York.

Mr. Goldmark had suggested during a New York breakfast with business leaders for Mr. Mandela June 22 that corporations and foundations were interested in a mechanism for channeling investments to South Africa that could begin to function "the minute a just government is elected."

Few specifics have been settled, but Mr. Goldmark said that after an hour's discussion in Mr. Mandela's London hotel Tuesday it was tentatively decided that the development bank's objectives for South Africa would be establishment of an equitable economic structure and investment in roads, irrigation systems and other development infrastructure.

Mr. Goldmark said a "core group" was formed to plan the bank's structure, including himself, African National Congress Foreign Secretary Thabo Mbeki, and ANC Treasurer Thomas Nkobi. The Rockefeller Foundation "is only to be of professional and technical assistance," with central leadership coming from the South African participants, he said.
