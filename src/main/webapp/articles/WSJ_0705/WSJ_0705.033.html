United Air's June Traffic Rose

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/05/90

CHICAGO -- UAL Corp. said June traffic at its United Airlines unit climbed 8.4% to 6.91 billion revenue passenger miles from 6.37 billion a year earlier. A revenue passenger mile is one paying passenger flown one mile. The airline's June load factor, or the percentage of available seats occupied, slipped to 70.9% from 72.2%.

Domestic traffic eased less than 1% to 5.02 billion revenue passenger miles from 5.03 billion a year earlier. But overseas traffic rose significantly, the company said, citing recently instituted trans-Atlantic flights and more substantial Pacific routes.

In the first six months, United's traffic rose 7.3% to 35.84 billion revenue passenger miles from 33.41 billion a year earlier. The load factor increased to 65.5% from 64.9%.
