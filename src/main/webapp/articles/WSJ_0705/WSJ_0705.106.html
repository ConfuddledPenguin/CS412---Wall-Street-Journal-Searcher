Business Brief -- Cleveland-Cliffs Inc.: Restructuring Accord Set By Pickands Mather & Co.

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/05/90

Cleveland-Cliffs Inc. said its Pickands Mather & Co. unit agreed to restructure the ownership and operation of the jointly run Savage River iron ore mines in Australia. Terms weren't disclosed. But Cleveland-Cliffs, a Cleveland-based iron ore mining concern, said the transaction would have a "material effect" on its financial position and its current year operating results.

The Savage River properties are a joint venture managed by Pickands Mather in Tasmania. Under the restructuring accord, effective Oct. 1, Pickands Mather will acquire 100% of the interests of the current Australian, U.S. and Japanese participants, including the 36% interest of a company affiliate.

In turn, Pickands Mather said it agreed to reduce iron ore production to 1.3 million tons, from the current capacity of 2.5 million tons. Operations are expected to continue through 1995, with production, mostly iron ore pellets, being marketed to customers in Australia, Japan and Korea.

Continued operation will maintain about 300 jobs, Cleveland-Cliffs said.

Corrections & Amplifications

CLEVELAND-CLIFFS Inc. said a transaction to restructure ownership of the Savage River iron ore mines in Australia would not have a "material effect" on the company's financial position or operating results. An earlier edition misstated the company's position.

(WSJ July 6, 1990)
