Two Men Get Prison Terms For Falsifying Test Results

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/05/90

LOS ANGELES -- Two men were sentenced after pleading guilty in April to a long-running scam involving falsified test results on millions of nuts and bolts designed to fasten parts in aircraft engines. Norman McHaffie, president of the now-defunct defense contractor McHaffie Inc. of Sylmar, Calif., was sentenced to three years in prison and three years' probation and was fined $750,000. James Hicks, an employee, was sentenced to one and a half years in prison and two years' probation, and the company was fined $750,000.

A third defendant, former shop floor manager William Whitham, was sentenced June 18 to 20 weekends in prison, three years' probation and 150 hours of community service after pleading guilty.

The government accused the men of participating in a 10-year scheme beginning in 1979 that involved more than 4,000 government and commercial contracts. According to the government's investigation, the company did not conduct Pentagon-mandated tests on the products, but went to great lengths to falsify results and otherwise mask the fraud.
