Who's News: J.P. Morgan Chooses Lane to Succeed Head Of U.S. Private Bank

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/05/90

NEW YORK -- J.P. Morgan & Co. replaced Earle N. Cutler as head of its U.S. private bank, in one of several personnel changes affecting that unit. John T. Lane, who until May had been a corporate banker at Morgan, will succeed Mr. Cutler, who will become senior marketing executive within the domestic private bank.

In addition, Arthur Sculley, who heads private banking for Latin America and the Middle East, will become head of a new global asset management group within Morgan's private bank, effective Oct. 1.

Also, Harry Barbee will retire Oct. 1 as head of the private bank's investment management unit. He will be succeeded by Jean L.P. Brunel, who currently heads J.P. Morgan Investment Management Australia Ltd.
