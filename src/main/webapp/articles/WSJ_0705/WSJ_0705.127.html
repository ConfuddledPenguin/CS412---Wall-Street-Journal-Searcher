Pratt & Whitney Recalls 38 Engines Used for Jet Planes

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/05/90

EAST HARTFORD, Conn. -- Pratt & Whitney, a unit of United Technologies Corp., said it recalled all 38 of its new PW4000 engines for wide-body planes because of a compressor problem. A Pratt & Whitney spokesman said that the company decided to recall the engines after tests at Boeing Co. in Seattle disclosed the problem that could cause an in-flight engine shutdown. Although a few of the engines had been installed on planes, none of the aircraft were in service, the spokesman added.

The recalled engines are part of a PW4000 "performance improvement" package that Pratt & Whitney introduced in February and shipped to Boeing, McDonnell Douglas Corp., and Airbus Industrie. Boeing plans to use the new PW-4000 engines for its 747-400 and 767-300 jets. McDonnell Douglas and Airbus will use the engines to power their MD-11 and A330 jets respectively.

Pratt & Whitney said the latest PW-4000 engines, which have slight design modifications from its earlier PW-4000 engines, are being replaced by engines with the older design.

The PW-4000, Pratt & Whitney's most powerful engine, accounted for about $4.7 billion in firm orders and options in the year ended February.
