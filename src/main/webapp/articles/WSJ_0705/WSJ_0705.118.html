International Brief -- Siemens AG: Firm Formed From Nixdorf, Unit Will Be 78%-Owned

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/05/90

West Germany's Siemens AG said it will own 78% of the surviving company following the planned merger of its Data & Information Systems division with Nixdorf Computer AG. In April, Siemens acquired 51% of the voting shares of the financially troubled West German computer group and plans to merge it with the Data & Information Systems division.

The merger is scheduled to take effect Oct. 1.

The resulting company, Siemens-Nixdorf Informationssysteme AG, would have world-wide sales of around 12 billion marks ($7.26 billion), making it one of Europe's largest information technology groups.

Under terms of the merger, Siemens will swap the Data & Information Systems unit for 26.3 million newly issued Nixdorf voting shares carrying a total nominal value of 1.32 billion marks. Siemens already owns 11.2 million Nixdorf voting shares.

Siemens added that two independent auditors valued Nixdorf at 3.02 billion marks, while the Siemens division was estimated to be worth 7.1 billion marks.
