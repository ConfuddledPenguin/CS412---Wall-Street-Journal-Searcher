Business Brief -- Hibernia Savings Bank: Dividend Omitted, Deficit To Be Posted for Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/05/90

Hibernia Savings Bank, citing troubled real estate loans, said it will post losses for the second quarter and the first half, and the Quincy, Mass., company omitted its quarterly dividend. In the year-earlier second quarter, Hibernia earned $108,992, or 14 cents a share, and in the 1989 first half, had net income of $514,863, or 63 cents a share. It has been paying a quarterly dividend of nine cents a share, last paid in April.

The bank said "the continuing deterioration in the real estate market, which has resulted in increases in the levels of both nonperforming loans and other real estate owned," prompted it to allocate a special loan-loss provision of $2,150,000 for the latest quarter; Hibernia said it would have been profitable otherwise.

As of Saturday, after recording the special loan-loss provision, the bank's capital will be about 6.55%, while regulatory capital will be 8.05%, and it met all regulatory capital requirements.
