International Brief -- General Electric Co.: Fiscal 1990 Pretax Earnings Rose 9.4% to $1.54 Billion

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/05/90

General Electric Co. of Britain said pretax profit for the year ended March 31 increased 9.4% to #872 million ($1.54 billion) from #797 million a year earlier, boosted by strong overseas sales and by acquisitions and alliances that the electrical company built up during the fiscal year, but still below market forecasts. GEC, which is unrelated to the U.S. company of the same name, said profit after taxes and minority interests but before extraordinary items rose 6.5% to #543 million from #510 million. The latest period included a #139 million gain, representing surplus from the sale of fixed-asset investments to joint ventures, and year-earlier results had a #12 million special provision. Per-share earnings rose 5.8% to 20.2 pence from 19.1 pence. Net profit attributable to shareholders, after taxes and special items, increased 38% to #433 million from #314 million.

Sales jumped 32% to #8.79 billion from #6.66 billion.

GEC is recommending a final dividend of 6.70 pence a share, making a total payout for the year of 9.25 pence, up 19% from 7.80 pence a year earlier.
