International Brief -- Enasa: Spain, West Germany Clear Spanish Truck Firm's Sale

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/05/90

Spain's industry minister, Claudio Aranzadi, reached agreement with West German government officials for the planned sale of Spanish truck manufacturer Enasa to MAN AG and Daimler-Benz AG, the Spanish government television station said. Television de Espana said Enasa, whose full name is Empresa Nacional de Autocamiones S.A., "will be sold" to the two West German firms. The report didn't give any further details, and an industry ministry spokesman said he couldn't elaborate. Mr. Aranzadi is to hold further meetings with MAN and Daimler-Benz officials today.

Under the original sale agreement signed in December, MAN was to take 60% of Enasa and Daimler-Benz 20%. The agreement valued the Spanish truck manufacturer at 36 billion pesetas ($354.9 million).

The agreement ran into opposition from the European Commission and the West German cartel office on the grounds that it infringed competition rules. The Spanish government recently offered new proposals under which Enasa's operations would be split into two divisions, to be run respectively by MAN and Daimler-Benz.
