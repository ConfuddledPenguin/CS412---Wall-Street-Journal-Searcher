Business Brief -- Aztar Corp.: Court Upholds a Judgment Over 1979 Tropicana Deal

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/05/90

Aztar Corp. said the Nevada Supreme Court upheld a judgment ordering the casino company to pay $33 million to settle a contract dispute involving its 1979 purchase of the Las Vegas Tropicana casino-hotel. Aztar, which changed its name from Ramada Inc. last year after selling hotel and restaurant operations, said it may petition for a rehearing. The Phoenix company posted a $33 million bond supported by a bank letter of credit after the initial ruling in May 1989, but said the judgment would be paid out of available company funds if the order stands.

The lower court awarded Hotel Conquistador Inc. and its holders $14,225,000 with 14% interest from January 1982, plus attorney's fees and other costs, in a dispute over the price of the Tropicana.

Harold Gewerter, an attorney for Hotel Conquistador and holders including Mitzi Stauffer Briggs, Ed Doumani and members of his family, said the judgment funds will eventually wend their way into federal Bankruptcy Court, which is overseeing a reorganization of Hotel Conquistador.
