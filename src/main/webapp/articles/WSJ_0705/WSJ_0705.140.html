International Brief -- Enimont S.p.A.: Operating Earnings Plunge On Chemical-Industry Woes

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/05/90

Chemical joint venture Enimont S.p.A. reported a plunge in first-half operating profit as the downturn in the world chemical industry and the Italian company's continuing internal problems took their toll. However the performance was broadly in line with the company's own budget forecasts, and in some respects better than anticipated.

Gross operating income fell to 937 billion lire ($772 million) in the first half of this year from 1.42 trillion lire in the first six months of 1989, although it was only slightly below the budget forecast of 955 billion lire.

Sales slipped to 7.53 trillion lire from 8.21 trillion a year earlier. Operating profit totaled 477 billion, compared with a budget forecast of 491 billion and a first-half 1989 operating profit of 999 billion. Gross pretax income was 182 billion lire in the first six months of this year, above the forecast 61 billion but well below the 693 billion recorded in the first half of 1989.
