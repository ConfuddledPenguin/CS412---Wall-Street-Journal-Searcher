Melville Corp. to Buy The Operating Assets Of Greenman Unit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/05/90

RYE, N.Y. -- Melville Corp. agreed to buy the operating assets of Circus World Toy Stores Inc., a Greenman Bros. Inc. subsidiary, for about $95 million. The transaction, which is subject to customary conditions and regulatory approval, is expected to close in August. Circus World currently operates 330 retail toy stores in 32 states, and its 1989 sales were $177.6 million. It will become part of Melville's Kay-Bee division.

Greenman Bros. said proceeds from the sale would be used for repayment of bank and trade debt and for expansion, as well as for other corporate purposes.

Melville, one of the nation's largest specialty retailers with interests in toys, apparel, prescription drugs and footwear, had 1989 sales of $7.6 billion. Last year, Kay-Bee sales totaled $724 million.

The Circus World acquisition will be financed from operating cash flow and existing lines of credit.

Melville Corp. had earnings of $398 million in 1989. In New York Stock Exchange composite trading Tuesday, Melville shares closed at $53, up 75 cents.
