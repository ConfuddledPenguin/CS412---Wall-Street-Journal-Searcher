Business Brief -- Procordia AB: Swedish Company Acquires Danish Confectionery Maker

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/05/90

Sweden's Procordia AB continued its push into European confectionery by acquiring Danish candy maker Benzon Brands AS. Benzon Brands, a unit of Danish holding concern Monberg & Thorsen Holdings, is the third European candy company snapped up by Procordia in the past 18 months. The acquisition brings Procordia a new brand of product, Sorbits chewing gum, plus bases in Holland and Belgium.

Terms weren't disclosed. Procordia officials said the purchase price was less than Benzon Brands' 1989 sales of 450 million Danish kroner ($71.2 million) but declined to elaborate, citing a pledge of confidentiality given to the Danish seller.

The relatively modest price fetched by Benzon Brands' core candy operations reflect dwindling profit in recent years. According to Procordia officials, Benzon Brands' pretax profit fell 52% last year to 24 million kroner from 50 million kroner the previous year.

Besides confectionery, Benzon Brands also makes skin-care and hair-care products, which account for about a fifth of sales. Those activities will be shifted to a division at Procordia's drug unit.
