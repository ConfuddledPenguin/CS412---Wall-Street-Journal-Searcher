Marketing & Media: Saatchi & Saatchi To Sell Gartner For $70 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/05/90

LONDON -- Saatchi & Saatchi PLC said it agreed to sell its Gartner Group Inc. consultancy business to an investment vehicle owned by Information Partners L.P. of the U.S. for $70 million in a mixture of cash and either a promissory note or shares. The sale of Gartner, a Connecticut market information and product data supplier, forms part of Saatchi's bid to sell off its consulting businesses in favor of concentrating on its core communications operations and reducing debt, the British advertising company said. Last month, Saatchi sold its Peterson Consulting unit -- for which it paid $116 million -- for less than $20 million.

Information Partners, a Boston investment group specializing in communications concerns, is proposing to pay $55 million in cash and $15 million of either a subordinated promissory note or exchangeable preferred stock.

Saatchi, meanwhile, said it is paying an estimated $4 million for an evaluation of Gartner's net asset value.

The sale is expected to go through within the next six weeks, the company said.

Saatchi paid $86 million for Gartner, which it bought in June 1988.
