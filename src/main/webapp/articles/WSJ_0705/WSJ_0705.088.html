Technology: Ousted Defense Aide Gets Posts at Research Group

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/05/90

AUSTIN, Texas -- Microelectronic & Computer Technology Corp., a private research consortium better known as MCC, said Craig Fields will assume the new posts of president, chief technical officer and chief operating officer Monday. Mr. Fields, 43 years old, was ousted in April as director of defense research and engineering with the Defense Applied Research Projects Agency, a research arm of the Defense Department.

While with the defense agency, Mr. Fields touted private industry's involvement in the development of high-definition television, a technological endeavor in which the U.S. is competing against Japan and Europe.

But Mr. Fields rankled the Bush administration and some Pentagon officials by promoting a federal role in the development of HDTV, advanced semiconductor manufacturing and a host of other technologies with potentially big commercial payoffs. He was demoted to a less-visible Defense Department post in April.

MCC, which counts 21 technology companies among its shareholders, said it would release more information about Mr. Fields's duties Monday.
