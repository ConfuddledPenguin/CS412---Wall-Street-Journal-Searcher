International Brief -- Airbus Industrie: Group Signs Pact to Deliver 7 Planes to French Airline

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/05/90

Airbus Industrie said it signed a contract with French airline Air Inter for delivery of seven Airbus Industrie A321s and for options on another eight aircraft. In keeping with company policy, Airbus didn't disclose the value of the contract. The latest published price for an A321 is between $40 million and $42 million.

Airbus said Air Inter also had exercised all of its remaining options for A320s, bringing to 24 the number of A320s Air Inter has ordered.

The A321s will seat 210 passengers in an all-economy layout, Airbus said. Both the A320s and the A321s will be used for short routes.

Airbus said total commitments for the A321 now stand at 185 from 10 customers. Combined firm orders for the A320 and the A321 now total 607 from 28 customers.

Airbus is a consortium of Aerospatiale of France, Messerschmitt-Boelkow-Blohm G.m.b.H of West Germany, British Aerospace PLC and Construcciones Aeronauticas S.A. of Spain.
