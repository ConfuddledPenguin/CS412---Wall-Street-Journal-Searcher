Business Brief -- Price Stern Sloan Inc.: Publishing Concern Drops Plans to Sell the Company

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/05/90

Price Stern Sloan Inc. said it has abandoned the idea of selling the company, and will instead consider asset sales or other steps to reduce debt. In November, the Los Angeles-based publishing company retained Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette Securities Inc. to help evaluate restructuring or merger alternatives. A company spokesman said no decision has been made as to which assets might be sold.

L. Lawrence Sloan, chairman and chief executive officer, said the company is working to control costs and improve gross profit margins despite a slow retail environment. Even so, the company won't report a profit for the first half, usually a weak period.

Price Stern Sloan reported a loss of $11.7 million, or $3.10 a share, on sales of $41 million for 1989. The company's stock closed unchanged at $4.25 in over-the-counter trading.
