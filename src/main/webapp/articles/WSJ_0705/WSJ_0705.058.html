Credit Ratings: Moody's Downgrades General Instrument Debt and Sets Review

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/05/90

NEW YORK -- Moody's Investors Service Inc. said it downgraded its rating on General Instrument Corp.'s commercial paper and put its subordinated debt under review, after a leveraged buy-out offer for the cable-television equipment maker. The rating concern said it lowered the commercial paper rating to not prime from prime-2. It also will consider downgrading the subordinated debt, which is rated Baa-3. About $175 million of debt outstanding is affected.

Moody's said its action reflects the deteriorated financial flexibility in General Instrument that would result from a buyout. It said it expects the deal will be completed at a minimum of $1.42 billion, the amount proposed Monday by Forstmann, Little & Co., a buy-out firm. It said General Instrument's management supports the proposed transaction, and noted that an enhanced offer could be made. The Forstmann bid was valued at $44.50 a share. General Instrument shares closed Tuesday at $45.625, up 50 cents, in New York Stock Exchange composite trading, on volume of more than 1.9 million shares, making it the third most-active issue on the Big Board.

Forstmann Little has said it will use $600 million in subordinated debt in financing the planned acquisition. The remainder will be financed with equity from Forstmann Little investors and syndicated bank loans totaling about $1 billion.
