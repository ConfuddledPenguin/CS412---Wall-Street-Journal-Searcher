Business Brief -- TCF Financial Corp.: Thrift Says It Had a Loss Of $32 Million in Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/05/90

TCF Financial Corp. said it will report a $32 million loss for the second quarter as a result of restructuring that includes the termination of what it called "poorly conceived expansion" efforts. The Minneapolis-based thrift institution said it will take a $13.1 million write-off in closing its Texas unit, TCF Banc Savings. Balance-sheet restructuring also includes the sale of $137 million of mortgage-backed securities at a gain of $10.1 million and the sale of $28 million of collateralized mortgage obligations at a loss of $14.4 million.

"We believe these actions bring to a close TCF's poorly conceived expansion into Texas and the national commercial lending markets, and will reduce interest rate risk," said William A. Cooper, chairman and chief executive officer of the thrift. He said TCF expects to return to profitability in the second half.

In the year-earlier second quarter, TCF earned $5.5 million, or 75 cents a share, which included gains from tax benefits and a pension plan settlement.
