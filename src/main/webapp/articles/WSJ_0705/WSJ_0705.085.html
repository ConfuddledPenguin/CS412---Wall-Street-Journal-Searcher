Business Brief -- Comdata Holdings Corp.: Charge Set for 2nd Quarter, Will Widen Expected Loss

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/05/90

Comdata Holdings Corp., Brentwood, Tenn., said it expects to take a second-quarter charge of $23 million for prepaying a non-compete agreement with American Express Information Services Co. Comdata, which provides electronic data services to the transportation, leisure and retail industries, said the transaction with the unit of New York-based American Express Co., including $15 million in cash and a $5.7 million non-interest-bearing note, will increase the loss that the company expects to report for the second quarter, but will "reduce substantially" its continuing amortization costs in the future. Comdata had a net loss of $1.8 million on revenue of $32.7 million a year earlier.

Comdata said the non-compete obligation relates to the company's acquisition in June 1989 of three American Express Information Services units.
