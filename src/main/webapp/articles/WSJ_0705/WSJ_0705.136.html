Technology Brief -- Alliant Computer Systems Corp.: Company Expects to Post Loss for Second Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/05/90

Alliant Computer Systems Corp., citing disappointing sales of large systems and costs of introducing a new computer, said it expects to report a second-quarter loss of eight to 12 cents a share on revenue of close to $20 million. In the year-ago period, the Littleton, Mass.-based company had net income of $377,000, or three cents a share, on revenue of $17 million. Alliant said analysts had been predicting revenue of $23 million to $24 million.

Revenue was hurt by "longer and more extensive" customer testing of the company's new FX/2800 supercomputer and by "a predominance of upgrade business," Alliant said.

Alliant also said that Ronald Gruner, president and chief executive, was named vice chairman and that Craig J. Mundie, vice president, research and development, was named president and chief operating officer. Mr. Gruner will continue as chief executive officer.

Mr. Mundie will be responsible for day-to-day operations and will report to Mr. Gruner. Richard B. Goldman, recently appointed chief financial officer, will assume the additional position of treasurer, formerly held by Mr. Mundie.
