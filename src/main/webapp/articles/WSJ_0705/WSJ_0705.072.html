Firms Ordered to Deal With Discount Brokers By Ontario's Court

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/05/90

OTTAWA -- The Ontario Supreme Court prohibited four mutual fund management companies from refusing to deal with discount brokers and dealers. The four companies are: A.G.F. Management Ltd., Mackenzie Financial Corp., Noram Capital Management Inc. and Templeton Management Ltd.

The federal Bureau of Competition Policy investigated the companies' sales practices after receiving a complaint from a Toronto discount broker, Gardiner Group Stockbrokers Inc. Gardiner alleged that the four management companies were refusing to do business with it because of its practice of rebating to its clients part of the commission it receives for the sale of securities of mutual funds.

The court orders were requested by the Attorney General's Department with the consent of the four companies.

The management companies all said they weren't admitting to any offense. Templeton said it consented to the order to avoid long and costly litigation. Templeton said it doesn't refuse to supply any broker because of its low pricing policy or commission rebate practices.

The competition bureau said the four companies "have each taken steps to make clear that securities of mutual funds managed by them may be sold in Canada on a negotiated basis."

Since Gardiner Group made its complaint to the competition bureau in 1985, the company has been acquired by Green Line Investor Services Inc., a discount stock brokerage unit of Toronto Dominion Bank. Keith Gray, a Toronto Dominion Bank senior vice president, said Green Line Investor Services deals with all four mutual fund management companies named in the court orders.
