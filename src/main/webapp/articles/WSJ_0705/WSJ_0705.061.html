Who's News: Sudbury Inc.'s Chief And President Resigns With Two Directors

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/05/90

CLEVELAND -- Sudbury Inc.'s president and chief executive officer, Douglas F. Johnston, resigned along with two other board members, in what the company described as a "major difference of opinion" over its management and strategic course. Ross D. Lake, 52 years old, who had held the No. 2 position at the automotive-parts maker, was named by directors to succeed the dismissed Mr. Johnston, 59.

Mr. Johnston had been recruited two years ago to turn around Sudbury and craft a new strategy. But while declining to cite the board's specific disagreements with that strategy, the company's corporate secretary Robert G. Kuhbach said a majority of directors had a different vision of "how to return the company to profitability." The resignations of Mr. Johnston and board members William C. Scott and Thomas C. Sutton leave six remaining directors.

Sudbury, which makes dashboard padding, cast car parts and other components for the automobile industry, has been unprofitable in recent years. It recently said it expects to report net income of about $2 million for the year ended May 31, reflecting a $10 million extraordinary gain. Sales were about $500 million, it said.
