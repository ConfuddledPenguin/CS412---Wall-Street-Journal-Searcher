New Securities Issues

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/05/90

The following were among Tuesday's and yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: MUNICIPAL

Rhode Island and Providence Plantation -- $150 million of 6 1/2% general obligation tax anticipation notes priced by a Shawmut Bank group to yield 6% on Dec. 20, 1990. The notes are rated MIG-2 by Moody's Investors Service Inc.

EUROBONDS

Banque Indosuez Paris (France) -- one billion Luxembourg francs of 9 1/2% bonds due July 31, 1996, priced at 101 1/2 via Banque & Caisse d'Epargne de l'Etat Luxembourg.

Bayerische Landesanstalt fuer Aufbaufinanzierung (West Germany) -- 200 million marks of floating-rate notes at the six-month London interbank offered rate less 0.10 point, priced at par and due July 23, 1996, via Trinkaus & Burkhardt. First Libor fixing July 19.

Unilever Capital Corp. (U.S.) -- 100 million Swiss francs of 6 5/8% privately placed notes due July 31, 1997, priced at 101 3/4 via Union Bank of Switzerland. Joint guarantee by Unilever PLC, Unilever USA and Unilever NV.

Toyota Motor Credit Corp. (Japanese parent) -- $250 million of 9% bonds due July 30, 1993, priced at 101.50 to yield 8.95% after fees, via UBS Phillips & Drew. Fees 1 3/8.

Blue Circle Industries Capital Ltd. (United Kingdom) -- #90 million of 10 1/2% extendible convertible capital bonds due June 21, 2005, via Baring Brothers & Co. Convertible into ordinary shares of parent Blue Circle Industries PLC at #2.70 a share, a 9.76% premium to Tuesday afternoon's share price. Guarantee on subordinated basis by Blue Circle Industries. Callable after three years.

Lonrho Finance PLC (U.K. parent) -- indicated $160 million of bonds due Aug. 9, 1994, with equity-purchase warrants indicating coupon of 6 5/8% to 6 7/8% at par, via Nomura International Ltd. Guarantee by Lonrho PLC. Each $5,000 bond carries one warrant, exercisable Aug. 15, 1990, through July 26, 1994, to buy shares at an indicated premium of 15%. Terms to be fixed by Wednesday.

European Investment Bank (agency) -- 250 billion lire of floating-rate notes due Aug. 6, 2000, via Istituto Bancario San Paolo di Torino. Coupon 11 1/8% at par until Feb. 6, 1991, thereafter six-month London interbank bid rate. Call on any interest payment date from 1991 at par.

Tomen Finance Netherlands B.V. (Japanese parent) -- $56.5 million of zero-coupon bonds due Sept. 30, 1997, priced at 55.414, via Nikko Securities Co. (Europe). Guarantee by Tokai Bank.

Dresdner International Finance PLC (West German parent) -- 75 million Australian dollars of 14 1/2% bonds due Aug. 2, 1994, priced at 101 1/2 to yield 13.99%, via Dresdner Bank. Guarantee by parent Dresdner Bank AG.

Skandinaviska Enskilda Banken (Sweden) -- 100 million Swiss francs of 7 1/8% subordinated public bonds due Aug. 3, 2000, priced at 102 via Credit Suisse.

TopDanmark AS (Denmark) -- 80 million Swiss francs of 7 1/2% privately placed notes due July 20, 1996, priced at 101 1/4, via Swiss Volksbank.
