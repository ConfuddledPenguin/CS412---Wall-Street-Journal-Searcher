GE, Snecma Sign Accord On Developing Jet Engine

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/05/90

FAIRFIELD, Conn. -- General Electric Co. said it signed an agreement with France's Snecma to share in the development of its new jumbo-jet engine, the GE-90, and is negotiating with Italy's Fiat SpA on a similar agreement. A spokesman for GE's Aircraft Engines division in Evandale, Ohio, wouldn't confirm whether the company had reached a definitive agreement with Fiat.

In January, GE said that it planned to offer the GE-90, a new and more powerful engine for Boeing Co.'s planned 777 and other wide-body planes in the mid-1990s. It also said it would seek to form a partnership with several foreign firms to share in developing, manufacturing and marketing the GE-90, which it estimated would cost from $1.2 billion to $2 billion.

Under the terms of its agreement with GE, Snecma will have a 25% stake in the GE-90 program. The French company also will provide 40% of the assembly and test work on the GE-90 engine.

People in the industry said Fiat was considering a 10% stake in the project, but neither Fiat nor GE would provide additional details.
