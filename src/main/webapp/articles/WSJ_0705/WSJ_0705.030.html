REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: Local Taxes

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/05/90

A study by the National League of Cities offers more evidence of the additional tax load any federal tax increase would place on voters: About 41% of surveyed cities in their current budgets have already raised property taxes and 76% have raised various fees. But only half have cut their rate of spending growth. In other words, most taxpayers already know from experiences in their own neighborhoods that, like Washington, the politicians' reflexive answer to their failure to gain control of spending is a tax increase to "cut" the "deficit."
