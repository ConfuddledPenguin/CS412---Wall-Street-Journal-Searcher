Business Brief -- Media General Inc.: Communication Firm's Net Doubled in Second Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/23/90

Media General Inc. said net income more than doubled in the second quarter but would have declined slightly if not for a gain on the sale of the company's West Coast weekly newspapers. Including the $6.4 million gain, net was $12.4 million, or 48 cents a share, up from the year-earlier $6.1 million, or 23 cents a share. Revenue climbed 5.4% to $158 million from $149.9 million.

Media General of Richmond, Va., said its newspaper operations remained sluggish. Broadcast division revenue and earnings rose as the Fairfax, Va., cable operation grew strongly. Revenue dropped at the company's broadcast television stations, although profit rose slightly. Newsprint sales volume increased, and prices continued to rise.

In the first half, net declined 16% to $15.1 million, or 58 cents a share, from the year-earlier $17.9 million, or 69 cents a share. The drop would have been somewhat worse except that the latest period included the $6.4 million gain while the 1989 half included a $5.7 million gain on the sale of a newsprint mill in California. Sales rose 2.3% to $306.1 million from $299.2 million.
