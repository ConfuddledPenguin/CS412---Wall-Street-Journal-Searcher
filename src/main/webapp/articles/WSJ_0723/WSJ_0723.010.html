Four Investors Seek Controlling Interest In Polifly Financial

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/23/90

NEW YORK -- Four investors here are seeking a controlling interest in Polifly Financial Corp., the New Milford, N.J., holding company for Polifly Savings & Loan Association. In a Securities and Exchange Commission filing, the group, which holds 9.99% of Polifly, said it proposed acquiring control by buying new shares from the company. The proposal, outlined in a May 29 letter to the company, is subject to negotiation and discussions and may be modified or abandoned, the filing said.

The group, which hasn't obtained the financing needed to buy a controlling interest, also plans to ask Polifly for representation on the board, the filing said.

The investors are Haralambos S. Kostakopoulos, chairman of Hsky Inc., a bank-advisory firm; Nicola Biase, a financial consultant and U.S. representative of Findim Investments S.A. of Massagno, Switzerland, which has financial and other interests; Emmanuel M. Kontokosta, a general partner and engineer at Kontokosta Associates; and Nikos Mouyiaris, president of Mana Products Inc., a cosmetics manufacturer.

Hsky and Kontokosta are in Manhattan; Mana is in Long Island City, N.Y. Findim, which has an office in Manhattan, is a subsidiary of Findim S.p.A., Milan.

The investors acquired the shares of Polifly between June 27 and July 13 at $1.50 to $2.28 a share. Polifly shares closed Friday at $2.19, up 19 cents, in national over-the-counter trading.

Polifly has had financial problems. This year it signed an agreement with federal thrift regulators substantially restricting its activities. The bank also is bringing in new managers: Last month, it named Joseph S. Paparatto president and chief executive officer, and this month, Frederick T. Muller, who had been acting chief executive, resigned as chairman.

Mr. Paparatto, who assumed the chairmanship as well on July 11, says the filing is under review but declined further comment.
