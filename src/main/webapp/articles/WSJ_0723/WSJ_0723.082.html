Letters to the Editor: Imagine: Gloating Over Poor Old Mikhail

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/23/90

The June 22 letter from Prof. John Manley of Stanford University clarifying his role in Mikhail Gorbachev's cancellation of his scheduled visit to the Hoover Institution was most revealing. Let me get it straight: On the one side are a bunch of researchers from the Hoover Institution, all mild-mannered academics, who, over the years, have done nothing more sinister or violent than to describe how communism at best impoverishes people -- and at worst murders them by the millions -- and to predict communism's eventual demise. On the other side is Mr. Gorbachev, a man whose whole life was built on service to the Soviet Communist Party and who, during that service, most certainly abetted, if not actively engaged in, countless human-rights violations including the imprisonment of peace-loving people such as Andrei Sakharov and the massacre of thousands of civilians in Afghanistan. And here comes Prof. Manley, who would rather deny the poor fellows from Hoover the chance to "gloat" over how right they were all along than see the "proud and self-respecting" Soviet dictator suffer the ignominy of seeming to come "hat in hand to the tower of anti-communism for advice on how to save his failed country."

It's nice to know where the sympathies of some Stanford professors lie.

U.S. Rangan

New Orleans
