Politics & Policy: Bush Plans Daily Sessions On Deficit-Cutting Effort

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/23/90

WASHINGTON -- President Bush is inviting congressional leaders to meet with him daily to speed progress toward a deficit-reduction compromise. Senate Republican leader Bob Dole said yesterday on NBC-TV's "Meet the Press" that Mr. Bush plans to begin the sessions tomorrow.

"You're going to see a more active President Bush," said the Kansas senator, who spoke to Mr. Bush over the weekend. A White House spokesman later confirmed the invitation but couldn't say whether the meetings would continue beyond this week.

Democrats have been clamoring for Mr. Bush to take a more prominent role in the talks between administration officials and a bipartisan contingent of 21 members of Congress. The talks began in mid-May.

Unless the pace picks up considerably, negotiators are unlikely to meet their stated goal of reaching an agreement in the next two or three weeks. Budget Director Richard Darman has argued that a breakthrough will occur only in a smaller group.

Sen. Dole's aides said Mr. Bush's daily meetings will include Senate Majority Leader George Mitchell (D., Maine), House Speaker Thomas Foley (D., Wash.), House Majority Leader Dick Gephardt (D., Mo.), House Minority Leader Bob Michel (R., Ill.) and Sen. Dole.
