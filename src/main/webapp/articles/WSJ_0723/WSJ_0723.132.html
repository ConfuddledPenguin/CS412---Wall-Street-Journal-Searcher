Business Brief -- Hinderliter Industries Inc.: Judgment Entered for Itel's Signal Capital Unit in Suit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/23/90

Hinderliter Industries Inc., Dallas, said a San Antonio, Texas, court entered a summary judgment in favor of the Signal Capital Corp. unit of Itel Corp., Chicago, in a $28 million lender liability lawsuit. Hinderliter, which makes alternative fuel-system parts, said it will appeal. Costs associated with the suit were primarily responsible for two quarterly losses.

The company also said it asked the Securities and Exchange Commission for approval to withdraw its convertible subordinated debenture offering. It has been unable to sell the notes because of conditions in the unsecured bond market, and will seek refinancing alternatives, including a private placement or debt restructuring.

Hinderliter said it will post a charge of about $2 million against earnings for the year ended June 30, related to litigation costs associated with the suit, costs for the company's move from Oklahoma City, the offering's withdrawal, and the May resignation of John L. Robertson, 70 years old, as chairman. Richard H. Hughes, president and chief executive officer, was named to the added post of chairman.
