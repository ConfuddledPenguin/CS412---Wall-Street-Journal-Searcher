New Stock Listings

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/23/90

NEW YORK -- The New York Stock Exchange announced three new listings, the American Stock Exchange one, and the National Market System of the over-the-counter market six. All of these listings are initial public offerings.

B.J. Services Co., a Houston-based concern that does pressure pumping for the petroleum industry, joined the big board Friday, under the symbol BJS.

Compania de Telefonos de Chile S.A., a Santiago, Chile, telecommunications enterprise, began trading American depositary shares under TCH. Each depositary share represents 16 Series A common shares.

The European Warrant Fund Inc., a New York-base closed-end management company, began trading under EWF.

Joining the Amex, Sierra Capital Realty Trust VIII Co., a San Francisco real estate investment trust company, begins trading today, under SZH.

In the over-the-counter market, Alias Research Inc., a Toronto-based maker of industrial design software products, began trading under ADDDF.

Environmental Elements Corp., a Baltimore supplier of air pollution control systems, began trading under NLCO.

In-Store Advertising Inc., a New York-based provider of in-store electronic signs, began trading under ISAN.

MECA Software Inc., a Westport, Conn., maker of software products for managing personal or small business finances, began trading under MECA.

Modtech Inc., a Perris, Calif. maker of modular relocatable classrooms, began trading under MODT.

O'Charley's Inc., a Nashville, Tenn., owner and operator of restaurants and franchises, began trading under CHUX.

Separately, the American Stock Exchange and the Chicago Board Options Exchange began trading options on the over-the-counter stock shares of Cadence Design Systems Inc., a San Jose, Calif., maker of computer-aided design software products, under CDQ.

Also on the Chicago Board Options Exchange, Teradata Corp., a El Segundo, Calif., supplier of relational database computer systems, began trading under TDQ and UJB Financial Corp., a Princeton, N.J. banking company, began trading under UJB.
