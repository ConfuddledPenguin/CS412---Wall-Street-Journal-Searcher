Business Brief -- CrossLand Savings: Firm Posts $40 Million Loss For the Second Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/23/90

CrossLand Savings reported a second-quarter net loss of $40 million, or $3.64 a share, after taking $50 million of charges for, among other things, writing down the value of its real estate assets. For the year-earlier period, the Brooklyn, N.Y., savings bank, the largest in the state and one of the biggest in the U.S., reported net income of $3.8 million, but it posted a loss of 47 cents a share after paying dividends on its preferred stock.

CrossLand, which like many Northeast banks was hit hard by the slump in the region's real estate market, has submitted to regulators a plan to bring itself back into compliance with federal capital standards.

CrossLand said that regulators haven't yet cleared the proposal and that it will update the proposal to reflect the latest results. The plan calls for converting its two classes of preferred stock, which don't qualify as capital, into common stock, which does.
