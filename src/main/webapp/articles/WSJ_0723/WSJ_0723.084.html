Sino-Soviet Accords Signed

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/23/90

BEIJING -- Chinese and Soviet officials signed agreements to protect investment in each other's countries and avoid double taxation for their nationals, the official Xinhua News Agency reported. The pacts were signed at the end of a six-day meeting of the Sino-Soviet Economic, Trade, Scientific and Technological Cooperation Committee. Xinhua said Chinese Vice Premier Yao Yilin and his Soviet counterpart, U.D. Maslukov, signed a memorandum but its contents and details of the other accords weren't disclosed.

Premier Li Peng also met with Mr. Maslukov. He called for growth in Sino-Soviet trade, which totaled about $3.2 billion last year, and for more joint ventures; about 20 ventures have been set up.
