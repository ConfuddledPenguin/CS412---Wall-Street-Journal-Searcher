Kellogg's Net Rose 4.6% in 2nd Quarter On International Sales

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/23/90

BATTLE CREEK, Mich. -- Kellogg Co., citing "substantially" improved sales from international operations, said second-quarter earnings rose 4.6%, reversing a trend of lower earnings experienced in the past three quarters. The cereal maker said it earned $135.7 million, or $1.13 a share, on sales of $1.26 billion. In the year-earlier quarter, it earned $129.7 million, or $1.06 a share, on sales of $1.23 billion.

Earlier, the company's chairman, William E. LaMothe, said the quarter would be one of "very heavy investment" in marketing as well as product development. Therefore, the results weren't expected to match last year's earnings.

In New York Stock Exchange trading Friday, Kellogg shares rose 87.5 cents to close at $69.50.

Kellogg said sales volumes of ready-to-eat cereal in its international operations increased substantially in the quarter. A spokesman for the company said "international is becoming an increasing percentage of our business. Last year, sales from outside the U.S. contributed 37% of the sales of the organization and it is continuing to grow." The company also said the results were helped by cost-containment measures taken throughout the company.

Despite the improvement in those operations, Kellogg said the performance of its U.S. ready-to-eat cereal business was down because of "intense" competition and slowed growth.

In the first six months, earnings fell 19.8% to $232 million, or $1.92 a share, on sales of $2.47 billion.
