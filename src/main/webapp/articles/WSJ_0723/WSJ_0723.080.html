Letters to the Editor: Earth First] -- Madmen Or Valiant Crusaders?

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/23/90

In reference to your June 19 page-one article "Earth First] Group Manages to Offend Nearly Everybody," I find it amusing that everyone is so shocked and sanctimonious about how we mustn't destroy private property, e.g., bulldozers, trucks, etc., while our national property -- the ancient forests -- is being systematically destroyed by those who hide behind those very property rights and free enterprise. Entire civilizations have died because they abused their forests, land and water. Now, our ancient forests are being destroyed. By the time the system gets around to saving the ancient forests, there won't be any left. Earth First] would be rendered unnecessary if we would all join together to save our ancient forests.

Daniel H. Stone

San Francisco

---

Contrary to your story, Earth First] is not a "burden and an embarrassment" to all in the "genteel" environmental movement. Here's Brock Evans, vice president of the mainstream National Audubon Society, as quoted in the Los Angeles Times Magazine: "I honor Earth First] for having the guts to do the things they do. It's not for me, but I understand why they do what they do. And, ultimately, we all help each other."

Mark Rey

Executive Director

American Forest Resource Alliance

Washington

---

Your article wasn't the first on the subject that doesn't excuse militant environmentalists' behavior as a justifiable means to a noble end. While you reported that the Sierra Club and National Wildlife Federation have denounced Earth First] as "the moral equivalent of outlaws," this fact has been minimized or ignored.

Lawlessness, even under the protective umbrella of "saving the planet," must not go unpunished. The destroyers and their tacit consenters do little more than discourage new recruits for mainstream environmental groups. Some of us just might retreat to the closet rather than appear as though we sympathize, through indirect association, with a bunch of madmen.

Norman Jacobson

Los Angeles

---

Doug Bandow's June 20 editorial-page article appropriately criticized the concepts of ecoterrorism currently espoused by Earth First] and obviously practiced widely by irresponsible people. I strongly agree that such ecoterrorism must be denounced by true environmentalists, those of us who use and produce forest products, as well as protectionists who use forest products but wish to deny forest-products producers access to raw materials.

But I do object to Mr. Bandow's assertion that "government-subsidized timbering . . . is both environmentally destructive and wasteful. . . ." That is irresponsible and generally untrue.

I earn my living producing forest products. Much of the timber is purchased from state and federal forests. When properly managed, such renewable resources are grown and harvested as a cyclical crop that benefits wildlife, recreationists, hunters, the atmosphere, the watershed and many other interests as well as the forest-products industry. Such use of public timber doesn't strike me as wasteful, and, if properly managed for multiple-use objectives, it is not subsidized.

Charles R. Pottenger

Cloquet, Minn.
