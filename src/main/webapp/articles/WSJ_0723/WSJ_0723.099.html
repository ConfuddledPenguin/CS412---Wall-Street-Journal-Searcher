Business Brief -- Dexter Corp.: Firm Signs Accord to Sell Unit to British Company

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/23/90

Dexter Corp. said it signed an agreement to sell its Charlotte, N.C., polyvinyl chloride compounding and extrusion business to Evode Group PLC of Britain. The Windsor Locks, Conn., company didn't disclose terms of the agreement, but said it is expected that the cash sale of the business, "which is profitable, will result in a capital gain for Dexter."

Dexter had said it was in talks for the sale of the business, which had 1989 sales of about $50 million, but hadn't named the prospective buyer.

Dexter said the transaction is subject to completion of financing arrangements, approval by Evode shareholders, and various regulatory approvals.

Evode is a specialty chemical concern.
