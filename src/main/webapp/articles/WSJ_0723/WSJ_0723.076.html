BASF Says Workers Accounted For in Blast At Cincinnati Plant

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/23/90

PARSIPPANY, N.J. -- BASF Corp. said it has accounted for all employees at a Cincinnati chemical plant where an explosion and fire killed one person and injured 73 others, mostly BASF employees. But it said it hasn't yet been able to estimate damage to the plant. A spokesman said BASF has set up a center to provide support and counseling for employees and neighbors at a hotel in Cincinnati. The company, a subsidiary of West Germany's BASF AG, said it also is opening a claims office at Xavier University.

The explosion at the coatings and resins facility killed a maintenance worker, Lawrence Krechting, a 55-year-old resident of Norwood, Ohio. Four of the injured were hurt critically, Cincinnati police said. Initial reports said 63 people were injured and at least one person was missing.

The facility makes resins, as well as coatings for paper and for containers such as beverage cans. The company said it is investigating the incident along with the federal Occupational Safety and Health Administration; it suspects the explosion and fire started in the resin building.

Air-quality tests show that environmental hazards to residents are low, BASF said. But "damage to the plant is serious," and production won't resume for "a considerable period," BASF said. Customer orders shouldn't be affected because they will be handled by other BASF plants, BASF said.
