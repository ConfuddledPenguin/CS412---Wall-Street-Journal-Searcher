Spanish Banks' Merger Clears

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/23/90

MADRID -- Spain's Ministry of Economy approved the merger of two of the country's top savings banks, Caixa de Pensions de Catalunya and Caixa de Barcelona, creating the largest financial institution in Spain and one of biggest in Europe. The ministry said the two Barcelona-based banks, with combined deposits estimated at more than four trillion pesetas ($39.76 billion) will be given a tax write-off amounting to 99% of their total tax bill, or 112.6 million pesetas. They initially sought a tax concession of 167.5 million pesetas.

The Spanish government has strongly encouraged a spate of recent bank mergers, fearing that Spain's financial institutions otherwise may be too small to compete when the European Community fully liberalizes its markets in 1992.
