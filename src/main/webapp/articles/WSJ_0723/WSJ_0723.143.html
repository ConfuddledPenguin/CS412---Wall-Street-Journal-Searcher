Business Brief -- Grossman's Inc.: Dissident Shareholder Group Nominates Nine to Board

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/23/90

Grossman's Inc. said that a dissident shareholder group led by real-estate investors Jay H. Shidler and Robert W. Holman Jr. made filings with the Securities and Exchange Commission naming nine nominees for Grossman's board. Grossman's said that the papers indicate that if elected they would name W. Allen Doane, a Hawaii real-estate executive, to be chief executive officer.

According to Grossman's of Braintree, Mass., Messrs. Shidler and Holman announced their plans to gain control of Grossman's after Grossman's rejected a plan by which the building-supply retailer would sell its properties to the men and then lease them back "on terms which Grossman's believed were highly unfavorable."

Grossman's said that once the dissidents send material to shareholders, it will send shareholders its response.
