French Consumption Eases

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/23/90

PARIS -- French household consumption of manufactured goods eased to 55.72 billion francs ($10.12 billion) in June from May's high level of 55.91 billion francs, according to seasonally adjusted figures published by the National Statistics Institute. The state agency said in a communique that the drop was led by leather and textile goods, but was partly offset by a 2.4% rise in consumption of durable goods, especially household electronics. It said auto demand was also down slightly.

The June figures fit into the trend seen since the start of 1990, with monthly consumption bouncing between 55.5 billion francs and 55.9 billion francs. A brief exception was seen in April, however, when consumption fell to 54.07 billion francs.

The relatively steady consumption data coincide with a period of little growth in industrial production and moderation on the import front.
