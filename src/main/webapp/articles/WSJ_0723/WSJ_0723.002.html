MECA's Shares Rise to a Premium After Initial Offer

  • Author: Tania Bien-Lambert
  • Date: 07/23/90

MECA Software Inc. turned out to be investor-friendly in its initial public offering. The Westport, Conn., producer of software aimed at helping individuals and small businesses manage their taxes and legal affairs sold 1,750,000 shares Thursday at $10 apiece. The offering immediately surged to a premium, trading as high as $13.25 the first day on volume of 1,754,700 shares, closing at $12.25.

MECA closed Friday at $11.75 in over-the-counter trading on volume of 427,600 shares, giving the company's 4.68 million shares an indicated market value of about $55 million.

Most of the company's revenue comes from two software packages, Andrew Tobias Managing Your Money, designed in part by financial writer Andrew Tobias, and Andrew Tobias TaxCut, with help from Mr. Tobias and tax attorney Daniel Caine. The company also markets and is developing other products.

The underwriters, Needham & Co., Hambrecht & Quist Inc. and Piper, Jaffray & Hopwood, had estimated in the preliminary prospectus in early June that the offering would be priced at $7.50 to $9.50 a share.

In the offering, the company sold one million shares, saying it will use the funds for marketing, product development and working capital. In addition, two founders and two other investors sold 750,000 shares, with an additional 270,000 overallotment. Prior to the offering, Hambro International Venture Funds and Palmer Organization III L.P. each held a 32.6% interest; Daniel M. Schley, president and chief executive officer, 27.2%; and Garner W. Lester, executive vice president, 7.6%.

If the holders sell all one million shares, Hambro's interest would decline to 18.5%; Palmer, to 19.6%; Mr. Schley, to 15%; and Mr. Lester, to 4.2%.

The company's revenue has surged, from $6.4 million in fiscal 1988 to $11 million in fiscal 1989 and $17.3 million for the nine months ended March 31; the latter period included $5.4 million in revenue from a joint marketing arrangement with International Business Machines Corp., which included copies of MECA software as part of the sale of certain personal computers. Net income totaled $11,000 in fiscal 1988, $468,000 in fiscal 1989, and $1.2 million in the most recent nine months.
