Export Orders Decline For Taiwan's Products

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 07/23/90

TAIPEI, Taiwan -- Export orders for Taiwanese products in June eased to $5.56 billion, down 7.4% from the previous month, a government agency said. The figures also registered a drop of 2.3% from the year-earlier $5.69 billion, according to the Economics Ministry.

Officials attributed the month-to-month drop to a cut in machinery orders as well as those for home appliances and transportation equipment. Machinery orders totaled $468 million, a decrease of 19% from the previous month.

Transportation orders fell to $320 million in June from May's $397 million. Home-appliance orders fell 45% to $100 million.

Export orders from January through June amounted to $33.29 billion, down 0.9% from the year-earlier period.

The U.S. remained the leading destination for Taiwanese exports, accounting for orders valued at $2.04 billion in June, down 14% from May and down 9.5% from June 1989.

European countries ranked second among buyers of Taiwanese goods. Those orders in June stood at $865 million, against May's $864 million and June 1989's $820 million.

Orders from Japan were valued at $786 million, a gain of 4.4% from May but down 6.4% from the year-earlier period.

Hong Kong ranked fourth among buyers, with orders valued at $569 million. The figure was a drop of $60 million from May but a gain of $152 million from June 1989.
