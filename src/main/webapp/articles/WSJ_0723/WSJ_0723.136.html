Wall to Wall Sound Files for Chapter 11 After Loan Talks Fail

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/23/90

PHILADELPHIA -- Wall to Wall Sound & Video Inc. filed for reorganization under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code after it failed to reach an agreement with lenders. Under Chapter 11, a company is protected from creditor suits while it works out a plan to repay its debts.

Wall to Wall, a chain of 88 music and electronics stores, said it filed the bankruptcy-law petition after loan talks broke down and lenders demanded immediate payment of certain term loans and lines of credit. The Cinnaminson, N.J., company reportedly has about $22.4 million in unsecured debt.

Wall to Wall officials could not be reached for comment. In a statement late Friday, the company's president and chief executive officer, Jerome Shulman said, "We believe that the action we are taking today will give the company the breathing room necessary" to complete previously reported strategic changes.

In May, the company said it was phasing out the electronics portion of its business to concentrate on audio and video software.
