Business Brief -- Caterpillar Inc.: Second-Quarter Net Income Declined 67% to $46 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/23/90

Caterpillar Inc. said second-quarter earnings tumbled 67% on a 3.1% decline in sales. Net income dropped to $46 million, or 46 cents a share, from $141 million, or $1.39 a share, in the year-earlier period. Sales slipped to $2.95 billion from $3.04 billion.

The discouraging numbers didn't come as a surprise. The Peoria, Ill., heavy-equipment maker disclosed late last month that its once highly profitable Brazilian operation has been badly hit by the economic disruptions in that nation.

In addition, Caterpillar is feeling the effects of a soft demand in the U.S. and some other markets. And the dollar's recent strengthening means Caterpillar's sales to Japan generate fewer dollars, and render competing Japanese imports relatively cheaper in this country.

In composite New York Stock Exchange trading Friday, Caterpillar shares closed at $52.50, unchanged.

Caterpillar also disclosed Friday that it is the target of a shareholder suit, filed in U.S. District Court in Peoria, which alleges the company violated securities laws by failing to properly disclose its financial situation. The company said it thinks the suit, which seeks class-action status, is "without merit."
