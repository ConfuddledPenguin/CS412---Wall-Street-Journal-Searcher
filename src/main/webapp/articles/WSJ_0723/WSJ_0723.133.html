What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/23/90

EASTERN AIRLINES would be liquidated under a proposal expected to be made today by Alfred Checchi, Northwest Airlines' chairman. Northwest would take over certain assets, including planes, airport gates and maintenance facilities. It would offer to broker the sale of the rest to benefit Eastern's creditors, people familiar with the plan said. ---

A major guarantor of loans in the student-aid program, the Higher Education Assistance Foundation, is facing possible financial collapse, raising doubts about the soundness of its $9.6 billion in loan guarantees.

---

American Brands agreed to buy Moen Group from Forstmann Little for $975 million, sharpening the focus of its home-improvement business. American Brands also posted a 32% rise in second-quarter profit.

---

Lockheed's contract to build P-7 anti-submarine aircraft was terminated by the Navy after the company refused to accept production losses it says could have topped $300 million.

---

Chrysler offered union employees part ownership of the company and incentive payments in return for helping the auto maker slash costs.

---

McDonald's failed to meet Wall Street's earnings expectations and said it is considering new menu strategies to attract customers.

---

Boeing is holding discussions with Aeritalia of Italy and Aerospatiale of France about selling its unprofitable de Havilland commuter airplane unit.

---

Grumman's ex-chairman has drawn the attention of investigators conducting an inquiry into Pentagon contracting fraud. But John O'Brien, who resigned suddenly last week, isn't believed to be a target of the probe.

---

The bidding war heated up for Ford Motor's aerospace subsidiary, as a consortium headed by Westinghouse promised not to dismantle the unit if its bid is successful.

---

Compaq plans to introduce today three powerful personal computers, even though sales of its Systempro 386 personal computer have been slow.

---

Time Warner may convert Life magazine from a monthly to a weekly, overhaul its content and switch to a standard magazine size.

---

Manville's reorganization was placed under the control of federal Judge Jack B. Weinstein, who has criticized the financing of the firm's asbestos personal-injury trust.

---

The Philadelphia Stock Exchange is close to signing a pact with the Frankfurt Stock Exchange to trade warrants on a German stock index.

---

Texas Instruments profit tumbled in the second quarter, largely because of lower prices for semiconductor memory chips. The firm also warned that it might post a third-quarter loss.

---

Citicorp eliminated two management layers in its commercial and investment banking unit for North America, Europe and Japan as part of an effort to trim expenses.

---

Disney settled EPA allegations that the company illegally dumped hazardous paint thinner and other cleaning solvents used at Disneyland.
