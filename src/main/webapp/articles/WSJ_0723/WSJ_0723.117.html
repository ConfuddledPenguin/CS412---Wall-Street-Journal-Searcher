Korea Selects Three Firms As Aircraft Manufacturers

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 07/23/90

SEOUL -- The South Korean government designated three companies as the country's exclusive manufacturers of aircraft, the Ministry of Trade and Industry said. Ministry officials said Samsung Aerospace Co., Korean Air Co. and Daewoo Heavy Industries Co. were selected as those that will be eligible to receive government orders for aircraft, particularly from the military.

The decision was reached in a meeting of government officials and aircraft experts that was to reduce the number of local aircraft manufacturers to reduce excessive and duplicating investments in the industry.

As a result, three other major aircraft makers, Hyundai Precision & Industrial Co., Sammi Aerospace Co. and Daewoo Sikorsky Aerospace Co. were excluded from the lists.

The selection was based on the evaluation on the technical status, development potentials and management capabilities of the companies, officials said.

South Korea's aircraft industry is still in its infancy, but a growing number of companies are entering the business due to its high growth potential, analysts say. The government designated the industry as a strategic sector for the next century and plans to provide various supports for its growth.
