PS of New Hampshire Plan Is Approved by State Panel

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/23/90

CONCORD, N.H. -- The New Hampshire Public Utilities Commission has unanimously approved the bankruptcy reorganization plan for Public Service Co. of New Hampshire under which the utility will be acquired by Northeast Utilities for about $2.3 billion. The proposed reorganization plan has already been approved by shareholders, creditors, and the U.S. Bankruptcy Court in Manchester. Northeast Utilities has been managing PS New Hampshire since April 30.

The reorganization plan still requires the approval of the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission.

The New Hampshire commission said parties to its proceedings have 20 days to request reconsideration of its decision and the commission then has 10 days to respond. If the commission denies requests for reconsideration, the parties then have 30 days to appeal to the state supreme court.
