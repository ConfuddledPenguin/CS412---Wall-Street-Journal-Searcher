Chandler Insurance Will Post Record Net For Second Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/23/90

GRAND CAYMAN, Cayman Islands -- Chandler Insurance Co. expects to report record net income of about $3 million for the second quarter, compared with $2.6 million in the year-earlier period, W. Brent LaGere, chairman and chief executive officer, said. Per-share earnings fell to between 38 cents and 40 cents from 50 cents a year earlier because of a stock offering in March of 2.2 million shares. At the end of the most recent quarter, Chandler had an average of 7.7 million shares outstanding, compared with 5.3 million a year earlier, Mr. LaGere said.

For the first half, Chandler, which primarily reinsures auto and general liability risks for trucking companies, expects to report earnings of about $5.7 million, compared with $5.2 million a year earlier. Per-share earnings fell to about 85 cents from 97 cents. Chandler had an average of 6.7 million shares outstanding at the end of June, compared with 5.3 million a year earlier.

Mr. LaGere, in an interview, attributed the improvement in earnings to strong growth in the company's large-fleet trucking program, which accounts for about 36% of premiums. Chandler expects earned premiums in the segment to total about $10.2 million for the quarter, compared with about $7.8 million in the year-earlier period.

Chandler is also seeing significant growth in its surety bond program, Mr. LaGere said, particularly because the Treasury Department in December authorized the company to write bonds for contractors that perform U.S. government work. In the second quarter, the surety bond program had earned premiums of about $3.7 million, compared with about $1.8 million in the first quarter and about $1.5 million in the 1989 second quarter.

Mr. LaGere declined to project results for the remainder of the year. But he characterized Chandler as being "somewhat immune" from price competition because of the specialty niches that the company occupies.
