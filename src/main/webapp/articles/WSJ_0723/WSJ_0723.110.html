Syntex Reaches Accord Over Dioxin in Missouri

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/23/90

PALO ALTO, Calif. -- Syntex Corp. said it reached a settlement with Missouri and the Environmental Protection Agency over a dispute involving one of the company's subsidiaries and dioxin contamination in the state. The dispute stems from the contamination of a number of Missouri sites in the 1970s by wastes allegedly transported from a Verona, Mo., plant operated by a tenant of Syntex Agribusiness, the subsidiary. The worst hit of the sites is Times Beach, a town about 20 miles southwest of St. Louis that was rocked seven years ago when it was discovered that dioxin in waste oil had been sprayed on the town's streets.

As part of the settlement, Syntex will clean up the biggest site and pay the EPA $2 million a year for five years. The company says it expects reserves and possible reimbursements from insurers to cover the cost of the cleanup, which the company says could take as long as 10 years to complete.

The EPA estimates that cleanup of the sites will cost $118 million. Syntex, which didn't produce the dioxin, is considered a responsible party under federal law.
