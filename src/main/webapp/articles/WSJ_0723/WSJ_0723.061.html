New Securities Issues

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/23/90

The following were among Friday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATES

Student Loan Marketing Association -- $250 million of notes, due Aug. 6, 1995, priced with a coupon of 8.63% at variable prices based on prevailing market conditions at the time of the sale. The notes, which are noncallable for two years, were initially priced at par to yield 8.63%, a spread of 35 basis points above Treasury's five-year note. The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Morgan Stanley & Co. The amount of the offering was increased from an origianl amount of $150 million.

EUROBONDS

Republic of Turkey (sovereign) -- $150 million of 10 3/8% bonds due Aug. 16, 1995, priced at 101.20, to yield 10.55% less fees, via Daiwa Europe Ltd. Fees 1 7/8.

Credito Italiano (Italy) -- via Law Debenture Trust Co., $100 million of floating-rate notes due Aug. 8, 2000, priced at par. Sumitomo Finance International Ltd. lead manager. Coupon is 0.40 point above six-month London Interbank Offered Rate. Call is after fifth year on coupon payment dates. Amortization is from August 1996, at 20% annually.

New South Wales Treasury Corp. (Australia) -- 300 million Australian dollars of zero-coupon Eurobonds due Sept. 5, 2005, at an issue price of 15.65 and a fixed, reoffering price of 15.25, via Merrill Lynch International Ltd. Guarantee by Crown in right of New South Wales. Fees 0.90.

Eurofima (agency) -- One billion French francs of 9 7/8% Eurobonds due Aug. 21, 2000, priced at 101 1/4 to yield 9.98% less fees, via Banque Paribas Capital Markets Ltd. Fees 1 7/8.

Compagnie Bancaire (France) -- 400 million French francs of 10 1/4% Eurobonds due June 10, 1992, priced at 101.025, via Credit Commercial de France. Fungible with existing 600 million francs launched in May.

Council of Europe (agency) -- 200 million marks of bonds due Aug. 14, 2000, and priced at par, via Trinkaus & Burkhardt. Coupon for first two years fixed at 9 1/2%, calculated under the German method, or 30 days per month, divided by 360. Coupon for final eight years is 16% less six-month Libor. Calculated using the Euro method, or the actual number of days per month, divided by 360. Libor is capped at 16%.

Canon Sales Co. (Japan) -- 300 million Swiss francs of privately placed convertible notes due Dec. 31, 1994, indicating a 3 1/2% coupon at par, via Banca della Svizzera Italiana. Put Dec. 31, 1992, at indicated 106.38 to yield 6.01%. Terms to be fixed July 25.

OSG Corp. (Japan) -- 60 million Swiss francs of privately placed notes due Aug. 9, 1994, with equity-purchase warrants. Fixed 4 1/4% coupon at par, via Union Bank of Switzerland. Guaranteed by Industrial Bank of Japan. Warrant exercise period: Aug. 28, 1990, to July 26, 1994. Terms to be fixed July 27. Call at issuer's option from Aug. 9, 1992, at 101 declining by 1/4 point semiannually. Fees 1.38.
