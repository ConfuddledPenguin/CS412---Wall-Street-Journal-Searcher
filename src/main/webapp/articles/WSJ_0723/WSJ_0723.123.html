South Carolina Lawmakers Focus of Inquiry on Votes

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/23/90

COLUMBIA, S.C. -- A federal grand jury here has subpoenaed the financial records of all 170 of South Carolina's state legislators as part of an investigation into a cash-for-votes scheme. In a statement, the Federal Bureau of Investigation and the Justice Department confirmed that they were investigating "allegations of illegal conduct involving certain members of the South Carolina Legislature." They wouldn't elaborate, except to say the investigation would be thorough and would be concluded as quickly as possible.

However, one person familiar with the investigation said that it stems from the FBI's use of an undercover lobbyist to offer legislators money in exchange for their votes. The grand jury subpoenaed financial records from the 124 state representatives Wednesday, the individual said, and subpoenaed records for the 46 state senators Friday.
