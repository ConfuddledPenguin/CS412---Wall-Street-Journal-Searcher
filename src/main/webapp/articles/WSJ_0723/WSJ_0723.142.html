Business Brief -- Eastman Kodak Co.: Unit, French Firm Study Feasibility of Joint Venture

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/23/90

The chemical units of Rhone-Poulenc S.A. and Eastman Kodak Co. plan to study the feasibility of jointly producing cellulose acetate, the French company said. If the project receives a green light, Rhone-Poulenc Chimie and Eastman Chemical Co. will form a joint venture requiring a total investment of about $200 million to $300 million, a Rhone-Poulenc spokesman said. The partners would divide the costs equally.

The site of the venture will be determined in the course of the feasibility study, he said.

Cellulose acetate is used in the production of cigarette filters, textiles and plastics. The companies are targeting a completion date around the end of 1993 for a factory that would produce 60,000 tons of the chemical annually.

The spokesman said this would represent the first cooperative effort between Paris-based Rhone-Poulenc and Kodak of Rochester, N.Y.

A U.S. subsidiary of the French concern bought Kodak's Beta Physics polyester film operation in January 1988.
