Marketing & Media: Murdoch Brand New Bag: Starting a Record Label

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/23/90

NEW YORK -- Starting up a new record label is the chic thing to do these days, and Rupert Murdoch is about to join the party. A New York spokeswoman for Mr. Murdoch's News Corp. confirmed reports that he is investing in the start-up of a new record company, but said the name of the company and other details won't be released until this fall.

Insiders in the record industry said Mr. Murdoch has hired Aart Dalhuisen, a former executive with N.V. Philips's PolyGram subsidiary, to run the company. Mr. Dalhuisen couldn't be reached for comment.

Mr. Murdoch enters the record industry at a time of massive consolidation, but also a very healthy time for independent labels. Even small successful independents have sold in the past year for $300 million and more, including A&M and Island, both bought by PolyGram. Geffen Co.'s record unit was bought by MCA Inc. this year for stock valued at more than $500 million.

Geffen, started just a decade ago, is a demonstration of how quickly the value of a hot label can escalate. On the other hand, entry costs for the record business are small, in the double-digit millions. Walt Disney Co. started its own pop label last year, Hollywood Records, rather than pay a prohibitive price for an existing independent.

News Corp. has interests in newspaper, book and magazine publishing, as well as owning a movie studio and Fox Broadcasting Co.
