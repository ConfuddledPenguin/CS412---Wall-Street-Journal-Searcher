Business Brief -- Nevada Power Co.: Utility Wins State Approval Of Power Purchase Contract

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/23/90

Nevada Power Co. said it received approval from the state Public Service Commission for a $110 million power purchase contract between the utility and SCEcorp's Mission Energy Co. unit to provide more generating capacity for the fast-growing Las Vegas area. An earlier version of the contract was rejected by the commission last month as too expensive, so the companies renegotiated and lowered the price by $50 million. A company spokesman said the 210-megawatt power plant, consisting of three combustion turbines to be built, owned and operated by Mission, would go on line in June 1991, if federal approval is obtained quickly. It is to be built on the property of an existing Nevada Power generating station.

Nevada Power sought to contract out the power plant when its own construction budget was already at $200 million, the spokesman said, adding that the utility is adding customers at a rate of 10% per year. The contract runs for 25 years. SCEcorp. is a Rosemead, Calif.-based utility holding company.
