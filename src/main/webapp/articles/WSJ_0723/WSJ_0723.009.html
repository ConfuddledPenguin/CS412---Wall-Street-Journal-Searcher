Letters to the Editor: To Be or Not to Be a Catholic

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/23/90

In response to Tom Bethell's June 20 editorial-page article, "The Heresy of Cardinal O' Connor": The timidity of Cardinal John O'Connor and other American Catholic Church leaders is amazing to this Catholic. The Catholic leadership in America is not leading; it is posturing. Church teaching on abortion is unequivocal: Abortion is wrong and must be opposed. It is not sufficient to proclaim you are a Catholic to be one. This can be said of any creed. Your claim to Catholicism must be confirmed by your acceptance of and practice of the precepts of the Catholic Church. Rejection of the teachings and precepts of the Catholic Church means you have left the church. Therefore, your claim to Catholicism is false.

It is unfortunate that Cardinal O'Connor and other church officials have not been more public and forceful in their criticism of "Catholicism" as practiced by some public figures. Their failure has allowed public officials to claim "Catholicism" yet support abortion. Catholics in America deserve better leadership.

R.G. Lingenfelser

Ponte Vedra Beach, Fla.

---

I am reminded of an old stratagem: If you can convince your audience that two plus two equal five, you may proceed with impunity to prove any number of equations based on that premise. For instance: Membership in the Catholic Church is not like membership in an exclusive golf club; (the rules of admission are more egalitarian); Mario Cuomo's position relative to the cardinal's is not that of Henry VIII's relative to (St.) Thomas More's. The governor is not given to throw his subjects in the Tower of London for the purpose of putting their heads on the chopping block. Yes, there surely is a "God's Law." The question is whether the cardinal is its sole arbiter in the realm of the state of New York. You try to prove that]

William W. Apt

Manhasset N.Y.

---

Mr. Bethell complains about the role played by the press and the politicians in the recent excommunication brouhaha. It seems to me the Catholic Church is in more danger from prelates like Cardinal O'Connor. After all, he's the one who backed off as soon as the media started giving him a hard time.

What kind of church has it become to place so high a value on avoiding martyrdom?

Woody Woodruff

New York

---

If Cardinal O'Connor and the Catholic Church are opposed to abortion, why are they also unequivocally opposed to birth control?

Why does Cardinal O'Connor shrink from publicly condemning the truly grievous sinners in our society -- many of whom reside in the cardinal's own back yard? (i.e. Mafia leaders, drug dealers, dishonest public officials).

It is easier, and probably a lot safer, to condemn Gov. Cuomo. The cardinal should know that there are millions of non-Catholic women like myself who applaud Gov. Cuomo's defense of our legal right to a medically safe abortion.

Karen M. Dickes

Ellsworth, Maine

---

Hooray for Mr. Bethell for presenting the Catholic Church's side. I'm tired of hearing "I'm a Catholic but I don't believe in: the stance against abortion, celibacy, the sacrament of reconciliation (formerly known as confession); the stance against artificial birth control; the teaching that the pope is the highest authority in the church," etc.

These are all precepts of the Catholic religion today, and unless and until there are any changes made -- if one cannot accept these precepts -- one should search for some other religion by which to live. Of course the Catholic religion is a difficult one by which to live in this age of total permissiveness but there is no one living in the U.S. today who is not free to worship in whatever religion that person finds most comfortable.

I feel our elected officials should keep their religious opinions to themselves. I feel the same about paid performers such as the so-called "superstars." Their opinions are just that -- their opinions, but because of their high profiles, their opinions are being touted constantly.

Cardinal O'Connor has a duty as head of the Catholic Church in New York to remind all Catholics (including Mario Cuomo) about the precept concerning abortion and the consequences of ignoring the Catholic teaching on this subject. I sincerely hope Cardinal O'Connor sticks to his guns on this matter and does not give in to media blackmail, or he will be remiss in his duties.

G.C. Urban

Houston
