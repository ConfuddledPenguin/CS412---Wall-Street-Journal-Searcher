Business Brief -- Lone Star Industries Inc.: Firm Says 2nd-Quarter Net Dropped 77% to $1.5 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/23/90

Lone Star Industries Inc. reported that second-quarter net income dropped 77%, largely because of tax benefits reflected in year-earlier results. Earnings before taxes grew 67%, said the Stamford, Conn., maker of cement and other construction materials.

Second-quarter net dropped to $1.5 million, or two cents a share, from $6.6 million, or 32 cents a share, in the year-earlier period. The 1989 period included $5.6 million, or 34 cents a share, of investment tax credits.

Sales fell 26% to $71.2 million from $96.3 million. The company has been shedding assets under a restructuring program, and the latest quarter's sales figures don't reflect a number of assets that the company is planning to sell.

Lone Star is facing litigation over railroad cross ties that it manufactured; purchasers have claimed the ties are defective.
