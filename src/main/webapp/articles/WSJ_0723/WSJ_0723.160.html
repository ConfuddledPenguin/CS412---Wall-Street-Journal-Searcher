What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/20/90

BUDGET NEGOTIATORS are considering proposals to curb itemized tax deductions, including those for mortgage-interest payments, charitable giving and state and local tax payments. The idea of restricting itemized deductions has emerged because of Democratic demands that any tax package hit the wealthy harder than other groups. ---

AT&T's profit slipped 6% in the second quarter, reflecting lower longdistance revenue and sluggish equipment sales. But the company said it still expects record 1990 earnings.

---

Fujitsu is discussing buying control of International Computers Ltd., Britain's biggest computer maker. It would be the first big move by a Japanese computer firm into Europe.

---

PPG Industries agreed to pay New Jersey $80 million to clean up toxic chromium wastes, as well as a $2.5 million fine. PPG had been threatened with lawsuits over the wastes.

---

American Express earnings rose only 1.1% in the second quarter, weighed down by results at its Shearson brokerage unit. Shearson's revenue slumped 20% in the quarter.

---

The Resolution Trust Corp. said it holds 483 junk-bond issues in the portfolio it inherited in the S&L crisis. Analysts said the portfolio may be worth only 50 cents on the dollar.

---

Grumman said its top executive, John O'Brien, 60, resigned unexpectedly for personal reasons.

---

Iraq's aggressive stance is raising new questions about next week's OPEC meeting in Geneva, and about the organization's future leadership.

---

Unisys' net income plunged 78% in the second quarter, as its international growth slowed while domestic computer sales remained depressed.

---

U.S. Shoe said falling sales at its Lenscrafters unit will result in lower second-quarter earnings. U.S. Shoe's stock fell $4.25, closing at $18.

---

A bill to shift power to the SEC from commodities regulators is in trouble, and backers in the Senate are scrambling to find a compromise.

---

Short interest fell 2.8% on the Big Board in the month ended July 13, but rose 4.2% on the American Exchange.

---

Three Baby Bell companies, Nynex, BellSouth and Ameritech, reported solid gains in second-quarter profits. But Southwestern Bell said its earnings were flat, and Pacific Telesis posted an 8.9% decline.

---

Alan Bond said he will resign as chairman of his flagship company if investors approve a restructuring aimed at saving the Australian entrepreneur's debt-heavy corporate empire.

---

Two big waste-hauling firms reported higher profits for the June quarter. Waste Management's net income increased 26%, while Browning-Ferris posted a 16% gain.
