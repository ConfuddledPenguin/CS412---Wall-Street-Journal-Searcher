Business Brief -- Angeles Corp.: Pact Set to Acquire Option To Buy Back a 22% Stake

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/23/90

Angeles Corp. said it agreed in principle to acquire for $30 million a three-year option to buy 22% of its common stock and other securities from certain former owners of Quinoco Cos., an oil and gas concern. The securities were issued to the former owners as part of a $50 million acquisition of Quinoco in 1984. Angeles sold the Quinoco oil and gas businesses and related businesses to Hallwood Energy Partners of Dallas for about $26 million in September 1989.

Angeles, a Los Angeles investment management concern, said that company officials weren't available to discuss the agreement. It said it expects the agreement to be finalized by early next month.

---

Corrections & Amplifications

ANGELES Corp. said its agreement to acquire a three-year option to buy 22% of its common and other securities from certain former owners of Quinoco Cos. involves an exercise price for the option of $30 million. Monday's edition incorrectly said Angeles agreed to pay $30 million for the option.

(WSJ July 25, 1990)
