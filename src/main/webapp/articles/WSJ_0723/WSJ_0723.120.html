Who's News: Accountants' Group Head Steps Down Unexpectedly

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/23/90

NEW YORK -- Charles Kaiser Jr. abruptly resigned as chairman of the American Institute of Certified Public Accountants, the major professional body for U.S. accountants. Thomas W. Rimerman, managing partner of Frank, Rimerman & Co., Menlo Park, Calif., was designated acting chairman by the AICPA board. Mr. Rimerman, who was vice chairman, will formally assume the chairmanship for a one-year term when the institute holds its annual meeting in October.

The AICPA is the primary professional group of accountants; it has more than 296,000 members, and sets auditing standards for public accountants.

In submitting his resignation as chairman, Mr. Kaiser, a partner in the Los Angeles office of Pannell Kerr Forster, told the AICPA that "other commitments have become so compelling" that he didn't believe he could "continue to do justice" to the position.

The institute wouldn't comment beyond the statement, and Mr. Kaiser was said to be traveling and couldn't be reached for comment.

However, in a statement, Mr. Kaiser indicated the other commitments related to a dispute with Pannell Kerr over his partnership agreement. James Tinney, partner in charge of Pannell Kerr's New York office, said that the dispute was settled after arbitration and that Mr. Kaiser was "absolutely" still a partner.
