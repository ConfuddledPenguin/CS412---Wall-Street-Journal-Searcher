Ferruzzi Profit Fell 45% In '89 on Soybean Trading

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/30/90

MILAN -- Ferruzzi Finanziaria S.p.A. reported that 1989 consolidated net profit after minority interests fell 45% to 311 billion lire ($251.8 million) from 561 billion lire the year earlier. Ferruzzi Finanziaria, Italy's leading agro-industrial group, attributed the decline to the extraordinary loss of $128.7 million incurred by its soybean trading division last summer. Ferruzzi also said the result reflected a higher corporate tax rate and radical changes in the group's structure following the transfer of chemical assets to Enimont S.p.A. last year.

Ferruzzi also announced plans to buy back as much as 150 billion lire of its shares.

Because of the radical changes in the group's structure following the foundation of Enimont last year, Ferruzzi said it restated 1988 results to enable a comparison with last year's earnings figures. Montedison, which is controlled by the Ferruzzi group through Ferruzzi Agricola Finanziaria S.p.A., ceded the bulk of its chemical activities to Enimont last year.

The earnings announcement also was the first time the company revealed the full impact of its clash with the Chicago Board of Trade last summer over its accumulation of soybean contracts, although Ferruzzi had predicted in September the loss would amount to about $100 million. Ferruzzi was forced to sell off futures contracts totaling some 23 million bushels of soybeans.
