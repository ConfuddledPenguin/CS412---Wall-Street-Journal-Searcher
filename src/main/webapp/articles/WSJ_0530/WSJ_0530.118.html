Business Brief -- Hasbro Inc.: Firm No Longer Controls 17.4% Stake Held by Time

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/30/90

Hasbro Inc., Pawtucket, R.I., said in a Securities and Exchange Commission filing that it no longer has voting control over Time Warner Inc.'s 10 million-share stake in the company. But Hasbro said it continues to retain rights of first refusal to buy the shares until 1993, if Time Warner wants to sell them. The filing said a May 1983, shareholder rights agreement with Warner Communications expired, as expected. The agreement had given Alan G. Hassenfeld, chairman, and Barry Alperin, co-chief operating officer, voting control over the Time Warner unit's 17.4% stake.

Analysts said the change in voting control would probably have little effect on Hasbro. "Hasbro is in excellent financial shape and has the resources to buy these shares back if it wants to," said Paul Valentine, an analyst with Standard & Poor's Corp. He added that "there's no indication that Alan Hassenfeld wants to give up control of the company."

A Time Warner spokesman said the company would have no comment on whether it has any plans to dispose of its Hasbro stake.
