Politics & Policy: Canada House of Commons Passes Abortion Legislation

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/30/90

OTTAWA -- The Canadian House of Commons passed abortion legislation that allows doctors to decide whether an abortion should be performed. Canada has been without an abortion law since the Canadian Supreme Court struck down the previous one more than two years ago, because it was deemed to infringe upon women's constitutional rights. Under the former legislation, abortions had to be approved by a hospital abortion committee, which often caused delays in the abortion procedure.

The new bill would allow an abortion if a doctor believes that a woman's "physical, mental or psychological health" would be threatened should the pregnancy continue.

The new law has been criticized by anti-abortion groups, who say it is too lax, and by abortion-rights advocates, who claim it will discourage doctors from performing abortions.

Canadian Justice Minister Kim Campbell said the bill was drafted "to achieve a delicate balance between the conflicting views" on abortion. She said it provides safeguards for Canadian women and reflects "society's interest in the protection of the fetus."

The bill, which was approved by a vote of 140 to 131 in the House of Commons yesterday, must be approved by the Senate, the appointed chamber of Parliament.
