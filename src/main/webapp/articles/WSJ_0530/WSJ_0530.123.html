Business Brief -- Bicoastal Corp.: Sale of Aviation Subsidiary To Thomson Unit Planned

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/30/90

Bicoastal Corp. said it agreed to sell its Link-Miles aviation subsidiary to Thomson-CSF for $100 million. Bicoastal, the troubled successor to Singer Co., filed for creditor protection under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code in November. The Tampa, Fla., company said its agreement with Thomson-CSF -- a unit of Thomson S.A., the French state-controlled defense and electronics group -- is subject to receiving approvals from the U.S. Bankruptcy Court and from British and French governmental and regulatory authorities.

Bicoastal's Link-Miles division, based in Lancing, England, develops and produces simulators for world-wide military and commercial applications.
