International: CGE's Effort to Control Framatome May Be Fading

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/30/90

PARIS -- Cie. Generale d'Electricite's unfriendly effort to control Framatome S.A., France's large builder of nuclear power plants, may be fading. Credit Lyonnais, a large state-owned bank allied with Framatome management, announced that it has begun an effort to restructure Framatome's shareholding. Government and business officials here say the effort has the Finance Ministry's backing, and is aimed at keeping Framatome independent of CGE. Credit Lyonnais said CGE's departure from Framatome is just one working hypothesis.

CGE, an electric-equipment company, specializes in telephone equipment, high-speed trains and electric power equipment. In that last capacity, it works closely with Framatome, and CGE long has viewed Framatome as a logical subsidiary.

CGE, in a surprise announcement, said it is prepared to discuss all proposals, including the sale of its current 40% Framatome stake. Government sources say CGE Chairman Pierre Suard is expected to meet with Finance Minister Pierre Beregovoy to discuss possible terms for CGE to sell some or all of its stake to Credit Lyonnais.

Various government and business officials stressed that a solution hasn't yet been reached. But if CGE were to sell, it would reflect a defeat for the huge company, which for years has sought to control Framatome.
