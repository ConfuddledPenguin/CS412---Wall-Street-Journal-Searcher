Kollmorgen Wins Court Approval On Preferred Stock

  • Author: Robert Daniels
  • Date: 05/30/90

A Connecticut state court judge allowed Kollmorgen Corp.'s issuance of some preferred stock to an investor group, but he ordered the company to pay legal fees for Vernitron Corp.'s attempt to block the transaction. Vernitron, a closely held Deer Park, N.Y., manufacturer of motors and controls, has been trying to gain control of Simsbury, Conn.-based Kollmorgen, which has similar interests. Vernitron, which controls almost 6% of Kollmorgen, has made a $15-a-share takeover proposal for half of Kollmorgen's remaining shares outstanding and is waging a proxy fight for control of its board. The companies are also locked in litigation over a merger they earlier had agreed upon; the plan collapsed last year.

In March, Kollmorgen sold the preferred, which is convertible into 11.9% of the company on a fully diluted basis, for $23.2 million to the investors. Vernitron sued to block the issuance.

Superior Court Judge Nicholas Cioffi said that the investor group could vote the preferred at the annual meeting tomorrow, but he said Kollmorgen should have submitted the stock-issuance plan to the court for review before it was completed. Kollmorgen plans to appeal the award of legal fees.

In allowing the stock issuance to stand, Judge Cioffi followed the recommendations of a panel of special masters he had appointed to help resolve the dispute over the preferred.

The next step in the matter is tomorrow's vote by Kollmorgen's shareholders for a board of directors. One question is how Mario Gabelli, the New York money manager, will vote the 16.29% Kollmorgen stake he controls.

Mr. Gabelli couldn't immediately be reached for comment.

Vernitron said yesterday that if its slate of directors is elected, the new board would allow possible buyers of Kollmorgen to submit takeover proposals either to the board or directly to shareholders.

Vernitron said that Kollmorgen currently requires interested buyers to submit their proposals to the board, but Kollmorgen called the assertion incorrect and said the current board gives potential suitors the same two options.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Kollmorgen shares closed at $10.75, down 25 cents.
