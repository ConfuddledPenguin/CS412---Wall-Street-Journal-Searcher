International Brief -- MAN AG: Bonn Plans to Bar Takeover With Daimler of Enasa

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/30/90

West Germany plans to block the proposed takeover by MAN AG and Daimler-Benz AG of 80% of Empresa Nacional de Autocamiones S.A., or Enasa, the Spanish state-owned truck company, MAN said. The federal anti-cartel office informed MAN, an engineering concern, that it can't approve the acquisition in its current form, MAN said. MAN and Daimler have until next Wednesday to respond to the objections. The agency is expected to make its final ruling by July 15. A denial may be appealed to the economics minister.

The antitrust agency declined to comment, noting that the investigation isn't complete. People in the industry said the agency objects to the cooperation of MAN and Daimler, the two main West German truck makers; MAN agreed to acquire 60% of Enasa and Daimler-Benz 20%, with 20% to remain in Spain's state industrial holding company, Instituto Nacional de Industria, which also is to acquire an 8% interest in MAN. MAN would gain control of the heavy-truck lines and Daimler the light-truck plant, but they want to exercise joint managerial control and take responsibility for all of Enasa.
