Timken Buys Maker Of Precision Bearings

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/30/90

CANTON, Ohio -- Timken Co. purchased MPB Corp. for $185 million, moving the big maker of tapered roller bearings and specialty steels into the precision aircraft bearings market. MPB, which has annual sales of more than $120 million and employs 1,850 people at plants in Keene and Lebanon, N.H., is a major supplier of ball and roller bearings for aircraft engines and aircraft and missile control systems. Its bearings also are used in computer disk drives, X-ray equipment and other precision instruments, but aircraft is the major market, Timken said.

Timken's bearings are used in automobiles, trucks, industrial machinery and other equipment, but the company hasn't previously participated in the precision bearings business, said Joseph F. Toot Jr., president. He said Timken is aware that a defense spending cutback may cut into MPB's business, but he said strong commercial aircraft sales and increased applications in other precision equipment will offset any loss of defense business.

Timken bought MPB for cash, and the price included the cost of retiring the concern's debt, Mr. Toot said. He said the acquisition won't dilute Timken's earnings.

There are no plans to move any of the MPB operations nor to change the company's management, said Peter J. Ashton, Timken executive vice president, bearings. Thomas Uhlig is president and chief executive officer of MPB, Mr. Ashton said.
