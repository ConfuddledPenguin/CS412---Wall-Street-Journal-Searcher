What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/30/90

STOCK PRICES SURGED as the earnings worries that jolted the market Friday vanished. The Dow Jones Industrial Average soared 49.57 points to 2870.49, a record close. The S&P 500 index also set a new high, its first since October. Bond prices rose steadily, while the dollar was mixed. ---

Revlon accepted new terms on a pending $1.8 billion bank loan amid regulatory pressure on banks to limit lending to highly leveraged concerns.

---

Xerox faces new write-offs at its Crum & Forster insurance unit that could approach $400 million, insurance-industry executives and analysts say.

---

Ford told dealers it may nearly double incentives on 1990 autos after next year's models go on sale in October. The disclosure may hurt the auto maker's sales this summer.

---

Harcourt named former Energy Secretary John Herrington as chairman, succeeding William Jovanovich, whose son will follow him as head of the publisher's executive committee.

---

UAL's unions are talking to Chrysler Vice Chairman Gerald Greenwald about taking the top job at United Airlines if an employee buy-out succeeds, sources said.

---

Federal Express overcame an obstacle to efficiency as it won clearance to merge its pilot seniority list with that of Tiger International, which Federal acquired last year.

---

Westinghouse is expected to name Paul Lego chairman and chief executive in a move that could put the company on a growth course.

---

Equimark's Judith Fellheimer resigned her top management position, ending the bank holding company's husband-wife management team.

---

Massachusetts may adopt a 5% sales tax on professional services that are largely untaxed by states.

---

Drexel's brokerage unit filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy-law protection along with 14 Drexel subsidiaries.

---

William Farley's bondholders may seek to wrest from him voting control of West Point-Pepperell. But the creditors may try to avoid a forced liquidation of the firm because of a perception that Farley overpaid for it.

---

The securities industry won a case in Supreme Court. The justices let stand a lower court's finding that Massachusetts can't stop brokerage firms from requiring customers to sign arbitration agreements.

---

Source Perrier agreed to sell most of its soft-drink business to Cadbury Schweppes for $211 million.

---

Chase Manhattan is opening 10 offices nationwide to offer investment advice to well-off people who aren't wealthy enough to qualify for Chase's "private banking" department.

---

Concern about Macy's creditworthiness led an insurer to tighten its policy for firms with links to Macy.

---

Tyco Laboratories agreed to buy the operating assets of an Australian maker of fire-protection gear for about $600 million in cash and stock.

---

DHL International is selling minority stakes to Lufthansa, Japan Air and a Japanese trading company.

---
