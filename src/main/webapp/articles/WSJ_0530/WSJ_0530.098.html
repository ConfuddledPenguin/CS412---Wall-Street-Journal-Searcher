Who's News: Huston of PepsiCo's Pizza Hut Gets Post At Chicken Chain Unit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/30/90

LOUISVILLE, Ky. -- Kentucky Fried Chicken Corp. named Allan S. Huston president of its international business, the largest chain of restaurants abroad. Mr. Huston, 46 years old, was formerly senior vice president, operations, at Pizza Hut, which, like Kentucky Fried Chicken, is a unit of PepsiCo Inc., based in Purchase, N.Y.

Mr. Huston succeeds Donald L. Pierce, 45, who resigned to become president of Pentagram Corp., a restaurant operator in Hawaii. Calling the Pentagram post a "unique opportunity," Mr. Pierce said he wanted to change jobs partly to cut down on the amount of time he has spent traveling.

Since 1988, when Mr. Pierce assumed his post, Kentucky Fried Chicken's international business has had double-digit earnings gains.

Mr. Huston's appointment is the latest in a series of high-level management changes at the chain. Since last summer, the company, which has had a sluggish domestic business, has appointed a new corporate president, a new top marketing executive and new head for its U.S. business.
