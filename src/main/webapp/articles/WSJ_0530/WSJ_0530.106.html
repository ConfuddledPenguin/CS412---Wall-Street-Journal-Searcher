Washington Bancorp. To Issue $45 Million In New Secured Debt

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/30/90

WASHINGTON -- Washington Bancorp. said it plans to issue $45 million in new secured debt to private investors to ease the bank holding company's current liquidity crunch. The new debt will be secured by virtually all the assets of the company, and the investors will receive warrants to acquire a "major" stock interest, the amount of which is still being negotiated. John M. Toups, chairman and chief executive officer, said he was not pleased about the likely dilution of the common stock, but added, "You've got to offer something."

Earlier this month, Washington Bancorp. said it wouldn't be able to repay about $36.7 million of unsecured commercial paper obligations because certain credit lines were cut off.

While purchasers for all of the new bonds have not yet been found, those committed to buying a portion of the notes include Wafic Said, whose family trust and related entities own about 27% of the company, and John J. Mason, who owns a 6.4% stake.

After the immediate liquidity needs of the company are met, Washington Bancorp. plans to raise additional funds by issuing more equity, probably through a rights offering, Mr. Toups said.

Mr. Toups added that the company is scaling back its operations to reduce its capital needs, particularly by getting out of the mortgage banking and venture-capital businesses. "We're going to stay close to home," Mr. Toups said.
