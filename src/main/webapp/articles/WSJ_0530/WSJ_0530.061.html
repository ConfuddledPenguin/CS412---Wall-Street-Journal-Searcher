Japan's Leading Indicator Rises

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/30/90

TOKYO -- Japan's leading indicator of its economy moved well above the so-called boom-or-bust line in March, the latest reporting month, to 68.2 from a revised 41.7 in February, an official at the Economic Planning Agency said. Another agency official said that, barring a sudden change in external factors, Japan's economy should remain in an expanding trend. The agency considers a reading above 50 a sign of economic expansion in the future and a reading below 50 an indication of possible contraction ahead.

Of the leading indicator's 13 parts, those that turned positive in March, contributing to the index's favorable move, included construction starts, private-sector housing starts, and the commodities index.

The coincident indicator, which reflects the current state of the economy, stood at 60 in March, rising from a revised 50 in February. Of the 11 parts in this index, notable changes included the inventory ratio to production, which swung to the plus side in March, after lingering at negative levels since last autumn; positive turns in industrial production and capacity utilization; and improvement in sales by medium-sized businesses.

The lagging indicator, which measures the economy's recent performance, remained unchanged at 85.7.
