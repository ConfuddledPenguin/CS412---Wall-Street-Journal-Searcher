L.M. Ericsson Expects To Boost 1990 Profit By More Than 25%

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/30/90

STOCKHOLM -- LM Ericsson, buoyed by strong growth in its phone-switching equipment and cellular radio-systems businesses, plans to boost its profit by more than 25% this year. However, Lars Ramquist, Ericsson's president and chief executive officer, declined to predict revenue growth. Although revenue rose by 30% in the first quarter, there is increasing pressure from falling prices in the world telecommunications equipment market, the executive explained.

"We're running full speed now," said Mr. Ramquist. But he cautioned, "I'm concerned that we are doing so well that it could go away easily. No tree grows to heaven." He added that the company should split its stock 5-1 by mid-September.

He said Ericsson faces numerous hurdles in the 1990s, chief among them the challenge of remaining a major player in the European market. Ericsson derives 70% of its more than $6 billion in annual revenue from Western Europe. But since Sweden isn't a member of the Common Market, Ericsson could feel a squeeze on its business as Europe moves to lower market barriers after 1992.

"We {come from} such a small country . . . that we will always have to fight uphill" against rivals such as West Germany's Siemens and France's Alcatel, he said.

Mr. Ramquist, 52 years old, rose through Ericsson's ranks from its Radio Systems business, where the company has posted some of its biggest gains in recent years, capturing 40% of the world market for cellular network systems. He took his new position only three weeks ago.

Mr. Ramquist said Ericsson's core business has a strong order book that should enable it to navigate competition from such rivals as Siemens and American Telephone & Telegraph Co.

On the radio systems side, Mr. Ramquist said orders in the U.S. for cellular network systems remain strong. But he's also counting on a new joint venture with General Electric Co. to boost Ericsson's share of the cellular subscriber phone business.
