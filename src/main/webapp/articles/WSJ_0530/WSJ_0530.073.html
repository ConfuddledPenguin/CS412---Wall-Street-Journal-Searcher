Business Brief -- Mark IV Industries: Firm's Dayco Unit Reaches Pact to End 24-Day Strike

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/30/90

Mark IV Industries Inc. said its Dayco subsidiary reached a five-year agreement with the United Rubber Workers to end a 24-day strike at its Waynesville, N.C., plant. Mark IV said it expects first-quarter earnings to be reduced by about $650,000, or four cents per fully diluted share, from the strike. But the quarter's earnings should "compare favorably" to last year, said Sal H. Alfiero, Mark IV chairman.

In the year-ago quarter, the Amherst, N.Y.-based maker of industrial products earned net income of $6.1 million, or 58 cents a share. On a fully diluted basis, the year-ago net was 50 cents a share. The year-ago figures include a gain of $700,000 on the sale of securities.

No terms of the labor pact were disclosed, but company officials called it a "non-inflationary" pact that would make the Waynesville facility more competitive.
