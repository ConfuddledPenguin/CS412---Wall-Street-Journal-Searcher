Letters to the Editor: First Thanksgiving Was April 30, 1598

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/30/90

It was interesting to read "Texas Receives a Counter Offer: It Can Have the Salem Witch Trials" (April 30). Interesting, because on April 28 a few members of the Hispanic Family History Researchers based in Orange County, Calif., presented a dramatic re-enactment of "Thanksgiving 1598." Eight members of the cast are descendants of those original Onate colonizers, with some of us playing the parts of our 13th-great-grandparents. As a native of New Mexico, I have done considerable family research in tracing my roots back four centuries in America. Through my research, I learned about the first Thanksgiving. Mimi Lozano-Holtzman wrote the script for "Thanksgiving 1598," and with the cast in full costume we entertained the audience with the "first act of Thanksgiving recorded in the continental United States by Europeans."

The spokesman whom you quote is correct in saying "The Pilgrims were never there," but could have elaborated and said that they were still in England being persecuted for their religious beliefs. In 1608 they fled to Holland, and it would be an additional 12 years before they could get passage to the East Coast of this continent.

I agree, we don't have a Plymouth Rock in New Mexico, but we have a Mount Robledo, and it was in the shadow of this peak that my 13th-great-grandfather is buried. His name was Pedro Robledo, born in Maqueda, Spain, in 1539.

With the Quintcentennial around the corner, it is my wish that the many contributions of the Spanish finally will be manifested, and I for one will be delighted to celebrate the first Thanksgiving on April 30 and the second one in November.

Pauline Chavez Bent

Huntington Beach, Calif.
