International Brief -- Co op AG: Board Chairman Friderichs Quits in Apparent Dispute

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/30/90

The supervisory board chairman of Co op AG, Hans Friderichs, resigned from his post early yesterday in an apparent dispute with the majority owners of the troubled West German retailing group. In a news release, Co op also said it will continue its cooperation talks with Rewe-Zentral AG, another West German food retailer.

The company didn't give any reasons for Mr. Friderich's resignation, which will take effect tomorrow. Two other supervisory board members, Walter Birkhan and Wolfgang Freiherr von Poelnitz, are also resigning.

According to industry sources, Mr. Friderichs was opposed to the sale of parts or all of the company to another company by two banks that hold about 90% of Co op's shares.

Mr. Friderichs, who negotiated the massive bank bailout that rescued Co op from bankruptcy last autumn, was aiming to keep the group independent and return it to profit through its own means, the sources say.
