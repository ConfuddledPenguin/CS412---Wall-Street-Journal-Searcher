New Securities Issues

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/30/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: MUNICIPALS

Texas Turnpike Authority -- $131.99 million Dallas North Tollway revenue bonds, due Jan. 1, 1995-2005, 2010, 2015 and 2020, tentatively priced by a Dillon Read & Co. group to yield from 6.60% in 1995 to 7.38% in 2015. Current interest serial bonds are priced to yield from 6.60% in 1995 to 7.25% in 2005. There are $23.26 million of 7 1/4% term bonds priced at 99 1/4 to yield 7.32% in 2010. There are $33.01 million of 7 1/8% term bond priced at 97.12 to yield 7.38% in 2015 and $45.68 million of 6% term bonds priced at 84.85 to yield 7.25% in 2020. Ratings of single-A by Moody's and single-A-minus by S&P are expected on serial bonds due 1995-1998. The rest of the issue is AMBAC-insured and rated triple-A by both Moody's and S&P.

MORTGAGE-BACKED

Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $420.1 million of Remic mortgage securities, offered by Goldman Sachs & Co. The offering, Series 179, is backed by 10% stripped giant participation certificates and/or fixed-rate participation certificates with a weighted average coupon rate of 10.63% and weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29.08 years. The weighted average lives assumes prepayments at 180% of the PSA Model.

Ryland Acceptance Corp. -- $315 million of four Remic mortgage securities is being offered in 13 classes by Kidder, Peabody & Co. Inc. The offering, Series 94, is backed by GNMA certificates with a weighted average coupon rate of 10% and weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29 years. It is anticipated that Series 94 will be rated triple-A by S&P, and triple-A by Moody's, according to Ryland. Ryland Acceptance Corp. Four expects to transfer the GNMA certificates and other assets collateralizing the Series 94 bonds to a separate limited-purpose corporation or trust to further protect the ratings.

EUROBONDS

Japan Highway Public Corp. (Japan) -- $200 million of 9 3/8% Eurobonds due June 21, 2000, priced at 101 1/4, via IBJ International. Guaranteed by government of Japan.

Unilever Capital Corp., Delaware, (Anglo-Dutch) -- $150 million of 9 1/4 fungible Eurobonds due March 29, 2000, priced at 98.275, via Deutsche Bank Capital Markets Ltd. Tap on the existing $250 million of 9 1/4% bonds launched late February. Guaranteed by Unilever NV, Unilever Inc. and Unilever PLC. Fungible from payment date. No formal commission structure.

Osterreichische Kontrollbank AG, (Austria) -- 150 million European currency units of 10 3/4% fungible Eurobonds due April 11, 1995, priced at 102 5/8, via Banque Paribas Capital Markets Ltd. Fungible with existing 150 million ECU launched in March. Guaranteed by Republic of Austria.

Republic of Austria (sovereign) -- 150 billion lire of 12 1/2% Eurobonds due June 22, 1993, priced at 101.60 via Banca Commerciale Italiana.

New Zealand (sovereign) -- 300 million European currency units of 10 5/8% Eurobonds due June 26, 1997, at a fixed-reoffered price of 100, via UBS Philips & Drew Ltd.

Shell Australia Ltd. (Australia) -- $100 million of 15 1/4% Eurobonds due July 6, 1993, priced at 101 7/8, via Hambros Bank Ltd.

World Bank (agency) -- One billion Austrian schillings of 8 7/8% Eurobonds priced at 101.70, via Creditanstalt-Bankverein.

Inter-American Development Bank (agency) -- 200 million Dutch guilders of 9 1/4% bonds due July 5, 2000, priced at 101 to yield 9.09%, via Algemene Bank Nederland N.V.

Nacional Financiera Mexico (Mexico) -- 125 million Deutsche marks of 11% bonds due June 27, 1995, priced at par, via Dresdner Bank.
