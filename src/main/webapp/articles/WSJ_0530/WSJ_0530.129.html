CD Yields at Banks And Brokerage Firms Remain Unchanged

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/30/90

NEW YORK -- Yields on certificates of deposit at major banks and brokerage firms remained largely unchanged in the week ended yesterday. Changes in average yields were mixed for small-denomination CDs, while they remained at or above 8% for maturities of six months and longer. The average yield on a one-year consumer CD eased to 8% from 8.02%, while yields on two-year consumer CDs were up to 8.12% from 8.10%, according to figures compiled by Banxquote Money Markets, a New York information service.

None of the banks in Banxquote's 18-bank survey offered a yield as high as 9%. The highest yield for a small-denomination consumer CD was 8.76% for a five-year CD offered by Chase Manhattan Bank in New York.

Small-denomination CDs sold by leading broker-dealers were also little-changed, with average yields remaining above 8% for all maturities. Average yields on six-month CDs eased to 8.30% from 8.32%, while average yields on five-year CDs remained at 8.63%.

Meanwhile, average yields for large-denomination CDs, which typically require deposits of $90,000 or more, were down slightly for all maturities. Average yields on six-month jumbo CDs slid to 7.89% from 7.91%, while one-year maturities fell to 8.06% from 8.11%.
