Business Brief -- Mediq Inc.: Firm Sues Kinetic Concepts, Seeks to Halt Acquisition

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/30/90

Mediq Inc., a New Jersey hospital equipment supplier, sued Kinetic Concepts Inc., claiming that Kinetic obtained confidential information about Mediq during negotiations for a proposed buy-out, and then bought a competing supplier instead. The suit, filed in U.S. District Court in Philadelphia, asks the court to halt Kinetic's acquisition of Salt Lake City, Utah-based Medirec and to enjoin Kinetic from using any Mediq confidential information. It also asks for unspecified damages.

Kinetic, a specialized bed manufacturing and rental concern, agreed earlier this month to buy Medirec for about $26 million in cash and $10 million of preferred stock, which is convertible into common at $10 a share. Medirec, a privately held medical equipment rental concern, competes with Mediq/PRN Life Support Services of Pennsauken, N.J., a health-care services and equipment manufacturing and leasing unit of Mediq.

Mediq claims that during buy-out talks with Kinetic, it disclosed confidential information including marketing methods, cost and pricing information and bidding strategies. Kinetic abruptly terminated the buy-out talks in April, and three weeks later disclosed its purchase of Medirec. Mediq claims Kinetic's possession of the information puts Mediq/PRN at a disadvantage.

Spokesmen for Kinetic and Medirec denied Mediq's charges.
