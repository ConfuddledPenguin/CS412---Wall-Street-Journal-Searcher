Business Brief -- Long-Term Credit Bank of Japan: Fed Clears Underwriting, Dealing Through U.S. Unit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/30/90

The U.S. Federal Reserve System said Tokyo-based Long-Term Credit Bank of Japan Ltd., through a subsidiary, can underwrite and deal in certain one- to four-family mortgage-related securities, municipal revenue bonds, and commercial paper. Through Greenwich Capital Markets Inc., Greenwich, Conn., the bank also can underwrite and deal in consumer receivable-related securities to a limited extent, in futures-commission merchant activities, and the purchase and sale of gold and silver, the Fed said.

Long-Term Credit Bank is the 21st-largest bank in the world and the 12th-largest in Japan, controlling total consolidated assets of about $194 billion, the Fed said.
