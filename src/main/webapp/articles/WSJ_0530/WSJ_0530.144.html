Technology: Software Sales in U.S. Grew at Healthy Rate In the First Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/30/90

WASHINGTON -- The Software Publishers Association said domestic software sales grew at a surprisingly healthy rate in the first quarter. North American sales grew 26%, and international sales by U.S. software companies jumped even higher -- by 50% -- in the same period. The news is a major departure from 1989, when the annual growth rate in domestic software sales slowed to 12% from the previous year; in 1988, by contrast, the annual growth rate was up 40% from 1987.

The bright figures "shocked us," said the association's research director, Ann Stephens, who said that the first quarter is generally the software industry's slowest season.

Ms. Stephens attributed the growth largely to the introduction of a plethora of new products and upgrades and to improved distribution controls. Most encouraging, she said, is evidence that the sales surge is highest in traditional categories like spreadsheets and word processors, "which shows that fundamentally the industry is doing well."

Word processors had the largest and fastest growth for the period, with $117 million in sales, an 88% rise. The SPA data are based on monthly sales information from 150 leading PC software publishers.

"The popular wisdom was that domestic sales have stalled," said SPA executive director Ken Wasch. "But this is the best quarter we've had in years." And preliminary numbers for April, he said, suggest that the sales improvement will continue.

Mr. Wasch partly credited a crackdown on illegal software copying -- an anti-piracy campaign that was spearheaded by his association last year and reinforced by several recent and hefty court settlements on the issue.
