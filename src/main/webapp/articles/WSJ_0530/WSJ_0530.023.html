Treasury Aide Is Watching Credit Situation Closely

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/30/90

WASHINGTON -- Deputy Treasury Secretary John E. Robson said in an interview that he is watching the country's credit situation "very carefully" and has been trying to encourage lenders and regulators to "use reason." So far Treasury officials don't see indications of a nationwide credit crunch. But Mr. Robson, the second-highest Treasury official, said, "There is a clear indication that the credit availability of the real estate market is very low, particularly with respect to commercial real estate."

The evidence of a credit squeeze seems contained to real estate so far, Mr. Robson said. But he cautioned, "Remember that if you look at credit data it is to some extent driven by demand, and it's very hard to separate what loans aren't being made because people aren't asking for loans that they want very badly," but fear may be denied to them.

Recent widespread reports that banks are tightening lending standards have caused concern among some government officials and private economists that credit could become so pinched as to tip the economy into a recession. A number of people believe that federal regulators' increased scrutiny of lending practices, in the wake of the savings and loan fiasco, has led banks to cut back on even prudent lending.
