Letters to the Editor: We're Not Rolling Out Until We Have Proof

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/30/90

Your April 23 Advertising column on Seagram's development of Mount Royal Light, a 54-proof whiskey, requires comment. Seagram is not yet "beginning a national rollout" of this new product. Mount Royal Light, which has been in two test markets in the Northeast since last September with some success, is adding three more test markets, in Southern, Midwestern and Western states. The operative word is "test" and will remain so for the time being.

You are also wrong, as is the beverage consultant Tom Pirko, in stating the consumer can already control the amount of alcohol in a drink by merely adding mixer or water. The typical serving portion of about an ounce and a half of 80-proof liquor contains a half-ounce of alcohol. That half-ounce of alcohol remains in the drink, whether the drink is taken neat, on the rocks or topped off with water, soda or fruit juice. Dilution will alter the taste and make a bigger drink, but it can't and won't reduce the quantity of alcohol (or calories either, for that matter).

By contrast, the unique process by which Mount Royal Light is produced does reduce by one-third the amount of alcohol (and calories) in the serving, but it does not dilute the whiskey or weaken the taste and body of the product. You can't do that at home.

Our extensive research indicates a strong consumer interest in a lower-proof, thus "lighter," spirits product. If Mount Royal Light continues the test well, the Nobel Prize may well elude us, as Mr. Pirko predicts, but so far consumers and distributors, whom you and Mr. Pirko apparently neglected to canvas, are telling us we have developed a very promising product.

Jerome S. Mann

President

The House of Seagram

New York
