International Brief -- Japan Air Lines: Carrier and All Nippon Post Profit Data for Fiscal Year

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/30/90

Japan Air Lines and All Nippon Airways carried 52.2 million passengers in the fiscal year ended March 31, up 11% from year-earlier levels. JAL, Japan's former flagship carrier, maintained its lead over ANA in kilometers flown for each passenger, but the smaller company flew more passengers than any Japanese carrier. ANA's net income more than doubled as a result.

Air Nippon net was 15.02 billion yen ($100.5 million), up from 7.4 billion yen the previous year. Per-share net was 10.9 yen, up from 5.4 yen. Air Nippon carried 30.5 million passengers for the year, up 11% from 27.5 million. Passenger-kilometers totaled 29.82 billion (18.49 billion), up 15% from 25.83 billion.

JAL net fell 4.4% to 16.57 billion yen from 17.330 billion yen because of a 5.12 billion yen provision for future tax payments. Per-share net was 100.75 yen, down from 110.75 yen. JAL carried 21.7 million passengers in the year, up 11%. Passenger-kilometers rose 7.4% to 54.25 billion.
