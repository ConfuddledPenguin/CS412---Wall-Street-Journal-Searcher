Letters to the Editor: Pro-Choicers Oppose State Control

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/30/90

R. Cort Kirkwood, in his review of John Aird's book "Slaughter of the Innocents" (Leisure & Arts, May 14), tries to link "the population control lobby" with the forced abortions and sterilizations of China. It's hard to know if this is sheer ignorance on his part, or the calculated propaganda of someone trying to make points in the anti-abortion war here at home. Let's go through it one more time: The issue is choice, and who chooses. China says the state chooses, and enforces that policy. The anti-choice types here in the U.S. also say the state chooses: They want to make sure women can't choose abortion. Some of them want to make sure women can't even choose birth control. What those of us in the "population control movement" want is for each woman to choose how and when to have a family, of the size that she wants.

While the state may have an interest in the size of its population, we believe the limits of state policy should be to provide incentives to encourage one behavior or another. For example, our tax code provides deductions and credits for children, child care, etc.

Just as the ACLU sometimes defends the rights of free speech of hate groups in order to uphold the rights of all of us to speak freely, we defend the right to abortion in order to uphold women's right to choose. But no one in any pro-choice or population-control group supports the actions described in this review, of forced abortions and state control of women's bodies. That's precisely what we are fighting against.

Mary Poulin

Palo Alto, Calif.

---

It's easy for someone sitting amid the bounty of U.S. food production to criticize China for its family-planning practices, and to imply that vague "economic development objectives" were responsible for such programs. In fact, as the Chinese leaders knew and Mr. Kirkwood should have known, the limiting of births in the late 20th century was done to prevent a greater tragedy -- deaths by starvation of already-born children in the early 21st century.

We are fortunate that our society has not been faced with such a difficult set of options. But our harsh judgment of one that has is unwarranted.

Further unwarranted is the condemnation of coerced family planning and abortion in China by Americans who favor coerced childbearing by limiting access to contraceptives and abortion in the U.S.

In the ultimate irony, U.S. anti-abortionists used the example of China to seek to limit funds for birth-control programs in Third World countries that are trying to avoid reaching the crisis point that forced China to turn to unpalatable solutions.

Carol Jennings

Beavercreek, Ohio
