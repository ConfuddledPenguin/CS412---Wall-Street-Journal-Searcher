Shearson's Chief Of Brokers Seeks To Restore Morale

  • Author: William Power
  • Date: 05/30/90

NEW YORK -- The new head of Shearson Lehman Hutton Inc.'s brokerage and money-management businesses said he aims to restore morale of his 10,000-broker sales force. Jonathan S. Linen, currently a top executive at American Express Co., Shearson's parent, was named to the new position at struggling Shearson; the move officially takes effect next week. The appointment by American Express was expected.

"You've got a great institution here with a lot of great people who've been struggling in an environment {on Wall Street} that's a little bit debilitating, without a great deal of consumer confidence," Mr. Linen said in an interview.

He said it was too early to discuss if Shearson might close more than the 67 brokerage offices it already has this year, leaving 450. But Mr. Linen added: "All bets are open right now."

Mr. Linen, 46 years old, was head of the traveler's checks, direct marketing and publishing group of American Express Travel Related Services Co. At Shearson, he will have the title of president and chief executive officer of the Private Client Group and asset management businesses.

Mr. Linen's appointment anticipates the planned split-up of Shearson into two halves: its brokerage network and money-management businesses that he will head, and its investment banking and trading operations. The split-up is part of American Express's efforts to reorganize and revive Shearson, which had an industry record loss of $915 million in the first quarter because of retrenchment expenses.

After talking with many of Shearson's 10,000 brokers yesterday, Mr. Linen acknowledged that broker morale isn't ideal. "My perception is, based on a day on the job, that things aren't settled down yet," he said. His goal: "Make money and have fun."

Mr. Linen will report to Howard L. Clark Jr., a former American Express chief financial officer, who remains Shearson's chairman. Mr. Clark said in a statement that Mr. Linen's "customer and quality driven management philosophy will find a receptive audience here at Shearson Lehman, as we all work to strengthen our position as a premier securities firm."

Mr. Linen said that he has "no illusions" about his lack of brokerage-industry experience, but that his consumer-marketing experience will help him. Asked if he can hold his own with Shearson's independent-minded brokers. Mr. Linen said he thought he could. "Try dealing with 1,500 travel agents," he joked.

In his new post, Mr. Linen succeeds Hardwick Simmons, head of the Private Client Group, who resigned last week. The asset-management businesses had been reporting to Shearson Vice Chairman Herbert Freiman, who remains head of Shearson's capital-commitment committee.

American Express this year has shaken up Shearson management, pumped more than $1 billion into the brokerage firm to replenish its capital base and announced plans to acquire the Shearson common stock that it doesn't already own.
