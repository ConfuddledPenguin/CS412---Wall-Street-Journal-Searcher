Britain's GDP Rose in Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/30/90

LONDON -- Britain's first-quarter real gross domestic product, measured by the output of goods and services, rose a preliminary 0.4% from the fourth quarter and 1.5% from a year earlier, the Central Statistics Office said. The output-based GDP is considered the most reliable guide to short-term movements in the economy. Britain also calculates GDP based on expenditure and income data, and publishes an average estimate of the three GDP measures, which is considered the best guide to long-term movements in the economy.

Real GDP in the index, which is based on January 1985 equaling 100, was 117.3 in the first quarter, 116.8 in the 1989 fourth quarter, and 115.6 in the 1989 first quarter.
