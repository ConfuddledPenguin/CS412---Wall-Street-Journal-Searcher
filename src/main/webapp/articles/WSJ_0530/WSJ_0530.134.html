Business Brief -- World Bank: Loan of $300 Million Voted To Help Forestry in China

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/30/90

The World Bank in Washington approved a $300 million loan to China to help finance long-term forestry development projects, but it delayed action on two other credits, totaling $150 million, for transportation projects. The bank's executive directors also cleared $402 million in loans to Mexico to support economic reforms in the trucking industry and other sectors of its economy. Part of the money also will be used for telecommunications projects.

The loan to China will finance treeplanting. It will be provided by the International Development Association, a World Bank affiliate.

A Treasury spokesman said the U.S. supported the loan because it meets basic human needs. He said the credit will help China overcome a serious shortage of wood needed for the construction of houses and for cooking and heating in rural areas. The forestry projects also will generate part-time and seasonal employment for workers in poor rural areas, he added.

The U.S., voicing concern about China's repression of pro-democracy groups, opposed the two transportation-sector loans.
