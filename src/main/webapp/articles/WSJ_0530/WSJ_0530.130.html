Tyco Labs to Buy Operating Assets Of Australia Firm

  • Author: John R. Wilke
  • Date: 05/30/90

In a big push into Europe and the Pacific Rim, Tyco Laboratories Inc. said it will buy the operating assets of an Australian maker of fire-protection equipment for about $600 million in cash and stock. Tyco, which has a commanding share of the fast-growing fire-protection market, said it will pay $350 million in cash and five million shares of common stock for Wormald International Ltd. The stock, valued at $242 million at current prices, will represent 10.6% of shares outstanding after closing the deal, a Tyco statement said.

Separately, Irving Gutin, senior vice president, said Tyco will report revenue of $2.1 billion for the fiscal year ending Thursday, up from $1.97 billion in 1989. Mr. Gutin also forecast revenue of $3.45 billion for the next fiscal year, reflecting the Wormald acquisition.

Investors reacted positively. In active New York Stock Exchange composite trading, Tyco closed at $50.75 a share, up $2.50.

"This clearly gives Tyco the No. 1 position in the world as a fire-control provider," said Laurence C. Baker, an analyst with Dillon, Read & Co. Based on Tyco's history with acquisitions, the deal probably will be non-dilutive of its stock, he said. But he added: "The question is what happens in the short term, and we don't have enough information yet to tell."

The Wormald acquisition gives Tyco a strong presence in Europe and the United Kingdom, where fiscal 1991 sales should rise to $600 million from approximately $35 million before the acquisition, Tyco said. In Pacific Rim nations, Tyco projects a sales increase to $340 million from $15 million before the acquisition. Wormald's fiscal-1991 sales in the U.S. and Canada are projected to rise to about $210 million.

Fire protection-making and installing sprinklers, alarms and industrial fire-suppression systems -- is Tyco's largest business, accounting for $1.7 billion of the projected 1991 sales of $3.45 billion. Industrial flow-control system sales are forecast at $900 million, with electronics and packaging accounting for the rest.

L. Dennis Kozlowski, president and chief operating officer and president of Tyco's Grinnell Corp. fire-protection unit, said the fire-detection market "has grown dramatically in the U.S. and Canada" as laws require such systems in public buildings. He projected increased profitability as a result of the acquisition.

Under terms of the Wormald agreement, Tyco also will issue a five-year warrant to buy five million additional Tyco shares, at $70 each. Tyco said the firms agreed to appoint representatives to their respective boards and will enter an agreement whereby Wormald may not acquire additional shares except under specified circumstances. Tyco also said completion of the transaction is subject to government approvals and a vote by Wormald shareholders.
