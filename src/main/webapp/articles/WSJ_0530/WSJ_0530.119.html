Holder Accuses Adobe In Suit of Violations Of Securities Laws

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/30/90

MOUNTAIN VIEW, Calif. -- A class-action shareholder lawsuit was filed against Adobe Systems Inc. in federal court, alleging violations of federal securities laws. The suit was prompted by the software company's disclosure last Thursday that earnings and revenue for the second quarter would be much lower than expected.

Adobe had blamed the revised figures on a 23% decline in the company's sales to customer Apple Computer Inc. But Apple disputed the claim and issued a counter news release a day later, asserting that the sales decline was far less.

The suit, filed by investor Richard Clotier in U.S. District Court in Northern California, charges Adobe with failure to disclose information and insider trading. On the insider trading count, the suit names Adobe's chief executive officer, John Warnock, Charles Geschke, its president, and Bruce Nakaw, chief financial officer.

An Adobe spokeswoman called the lawsuit "completely without merit" and said the software maker intends to contest it. She added that the company "would not be surprised" if several other shareholder suits were filed shortly.

Following disclosure of the revised forecast, Adobe's stock plunged $15.25, or about 30%, to $35.25 a share on Friday. The revised estimate predicted earnings of 29 cents to 37 cents a share, on revenue of $33 million to $36 million. This report was well below analyst estimates for net of 51 cents a share and revenue of about $42 million.

Adobe insiders sold 501,153 shares from Jan. 1 to April 30, compared with 293,032 shares in all of 1989, according to Invest/Net, a North Miami, Fla., firm that tracks insider buying and selling. In April, 90,000 shares were sold by Mr. Warnock at between $37.75 and $38.25, and 40,000 were sold by Mr. Geschke at between $39 and $40 each.

The company spokeswoman said "there was nothing extraordinary" about the sales. "They were all consistent" with the executives' sales in previous years, she said.
