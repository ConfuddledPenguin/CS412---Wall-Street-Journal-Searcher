Politics & Policy: Thornburgh Asks For Expansion Of HUD Inquiry

  • Author: Paul M. Barrett
  • Date: 05/30/90

WASHINGTON -- Attorney General Dick Thornburgh asked the special prosecutor investigating former Housing Secretary Samuel Pierce to examine new allegations of political favoritism at the Department of Housing and Urban Development. The request by Mr. Thornburgh, whose initial recommendation for an investigation was narrowly drawn, came in response to testimony before a House subcommittee by DuBois Gilliam, a former Pierce deputy. Mr. Gilliam, who is serving an 18-month prison sentence for accepting illegal gratuities, told the subcommittee that Mr. Pierce had instructed him several times to award grants to projects tied to powerful Republicans. Mr. Gilliam also testified about alleged kickbacks paid to HUD officials by developers seeking favorable treatment.

Mr. Thornburgh's latest request came in a letter to the court-appointed special prosecutor, Arlin Adams, asking him to expand his inquiry to include the new allegations of favoritism in grants from Mr. Pierce's discretionary fund and from another HUD urban development program.

A number of lawmakers from both parties had urged a broader investigation following Mr. Gilliam's testimony. But Mr. Pierce's lawyers have challenged Mr. Gilliam's credibility, given his criminal conviction.

"Mr. Pierce has done nothing wrong," Paul Perito, a Pierce attorney, said yesterday. "We have nothing to fear by an impartial review of scurrilous allegations of Gilliam by a group of dedicated and responsible prosecutors as opposed to the hysteria and insatiable thirst for publicity of a congressional inquiry gone awry."

Mr. Adams is expected to follow the attorney general's suggestion. In a brief prepared statement yesterday he said that "in light of the Gilliam testimony, it may be appropriate to redefine the mandate of the independent counsel," which is the formal name for Mr. Adams's office. "I shall, of course, carefully consider this letter from the attorney general during the next few days and take action promptly," Mr. Adams added.
