Politics & Policy: Bentsen Seeks to Link Aid Abroad to U.S. Purchases

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/30/90

WASHINGTON -- Chairman Lloyd Bentsen of the Senate Finance Committee has proposed requiring countries to use some of the economic aid they receive from the U.S. to buy U.S. goods. Along with Sen. David Boren, (D., Okla.), the Texas Democrat has drafted a bill that would tie U.S. foreign assistance to increased purchases of U.S. goods. The bill comes at a time the Bush administration is trying to persuade countries including Japan, West Germany, Britain, Canada and France to eliminate such "tied aid."

The bill's outlook is unclear, but Congress currently is under pressure to change the way it dispenses foreign aid and to find ways to increase U.S. exports. It would take effect in the fiscal year beginning October 1991.

"It's time to move more aggressively," the finance chairman said.
