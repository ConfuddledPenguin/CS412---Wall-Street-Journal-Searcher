Taiwan Economic Performance

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/30/90

TAIPEI -- Taiwan's economic performance has shown signs of a slowdown as the coincident-composite index dropped in April for the third consecutive month and more manufacturers became pessimistic toward the local economy. The Council for Economic Planning and Development said the coincident-composite index, which measures economic activity for the current month, fell in April to 130.33, against 131.9 the previous month and 133.96 in April 1989. The month-on-month decline of 1.19% compares with a drop of 0.58% and 0.02% in the March and February data respectively.

Capacity utilization in April reached 76.5%, down from 77.8% in March.

Among manufacturers interviewed by the council, 18% of those surveyed said they expect Taiwan's economy to improve during the next three months, down from 21% in March.
