Letters to the Editor: Country Club Elites Recall Hippier Days

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/30/90

My compliments for the interesting article (page one, April 12) on the subject of former hippies now joining country clubs. Perhaps you are not familiar with the old French saying, "If a man is not a socialist at 20, he has no heart. If he is not a conservative at 40, he has no head."

George G. Bull

Chevy Chase, Md.

---

People quoted in your article about the resurgence of country clubs came across as the shallow, elite yuppies that are so reviled by most of society today. Their reasons for joining the clubs and the importance of being accepted said much about their priorities.

The most absurd quote, however, was by Kelvin Perry when he complained that these overwhelmingly white country club applicants "are shunning the melting pot that promotes racial harmony." What makes this statement so absurd is that Mr. Perry is president of the Black Chamber of Commerce of Kansas City. Not exactly embracing the "melting pot" ourselves, are we, Mr. Perry?

Donald G. Schofield

Wadmalaw Island, S.C.
