Nynex Officials Knew of Gatherings, Attorney Testifies

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 08/17/90

Several top officials at Nynex Corp. knew about parties involving company suppliers and Nynex purchasing officials well before informants prompted the company to investigate the annual gatherings, according to testimony in a New York rate case. Charles Many, head of Nynex Mobile Communications Co. and Joseph Farina, who headed Nynex Properties, were aware of the Florida gatherings because they were friends of Lawrence Friedman, the Nynex Materiel Enterprises vice president who organized them, an attorney for Nynex testified at a rate-case hearing in Albany, N.Y. At issue in the rate case are allegations that suppliers at the gatherings received favored treatment in the granting of Nynex contracts.

He also testified that Raymond Burke, Nynex's executive vice president and general counsel, and John Clarke, New York Telephone's general counsel, knew about the gatherings from a 1985 investigation into Mr. Friedman's partial ownership of a company that had done business with Nynex. That investigation, however, didn't focus on the gatherings, which were believed to be small, private parties held on employees' own time, a Nynex spokesman said.

Guy Miller Struve, a partner at Davis Polk & Wardwell who led the 1988 investigation, testified that interviews in the investigation also indicated that former Nynex Chairman Delbert C. Staley and Robert Eckenrode, Nynex's vice chairman, were also aware of the gatherings. But investigators didn't pursue whether Messrs. Staley or Eckenrode knew about weeklong parties, the spokesman said.

The 1988 investigation led to the firing of Mr. Friedman and another Nynex purchasing official as well as disciplinary action against seven other employees who attended the gatherings. Nynex retained Davis Polk after receiving anonymous allegations about the gatherings.

Nynex's New York Telephone unit, which is seeking a $1.4 billion rate increase over three years, said its president, Fred Salerno, "had no knowledge of the alleged improprieties" at the Florida gatherings. The statement followed testimony Wednesday that Mr. Salerno may have been invited to one of the gatherings and had advised an employee against attending.

Corrections & Amplifications

AN AUG. 17 ARTICLE reported incorrectly that a lawyer for Nynex Corp. testified in a rate case in New York that New York Telephone Co.'s general counsel John Clarke was one of several Nynex officials who knew about bawdy parties in Florida attended by Nynex purchasing personnel and suppliers. In fact, the lawyer testified that company investigators had been told that Mr. Clarke might have received a report of the 1985-86 investigation that mentioned the gatherings, known as the perverts conventions. Mr. Clarke has since stated that he never received a copy of the investigation report, nor was he ever apprised of its contents.

(WSJ Aug. 27, 1990)
