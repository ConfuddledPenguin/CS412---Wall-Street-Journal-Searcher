Marketing & Media: Kellogg Asks Court In Towns to Block Action Against Its Cereal Ads

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/27/90

BATTLE CREEK, Mich. -- Kellogg Co. said it asked a federal court in Iowa to block the Iowa attorney general from acting against the company's health claims in advertisements for Special K, 40 + Bran Flakes and Frosted Flakes cereals. Kellogg said it filed its suit to end what it views as "harassment" by Iowa Attorney General Thomas J. Miller and the attorneys general of seven other states. The officials raised questions two years ago about the Kellogg ads. Since then, Kellogg said it has engaged in "long and fruitless discussions" with the attorneys general and provided thousands of pages of documents substantiating its claims.

"We think it's time to bring this to an end and let the courts decide," said Joseph M. Stewart, senior vice president for corporate affairs. "We stand behind our advertising. . . . They (the attorneys general) continue to say our advertising is misleading."

Mr. Miller, the Iowa attorney general, couldn't be reached for comment. Kellogg said the attorneys general hadn't produced a single consumer complaint or scientific study to support their allegations.

Kellogg said the issue involves its claims that Special K is a high-protein cereal and can help dieters or exercisers "keep the muscle, lose the fat;" that 40 + Bran Flakes is rich in fiber and iron and is beneficial to those 40 and older and that Frosted Flakes is "good for you" and has less sugar than many snack foods.
