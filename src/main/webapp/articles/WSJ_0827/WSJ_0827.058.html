REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: Smugglers `R' Us

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/27/90

Mexico has found a solution to its smugglers problem: free trade. The Associated Press reports that the dropping of Mexico's trade barriers has transformed Tepito, Mexico City's 400-year-old smugglers' market. Electronic goods are now offered at cut-rate prices in stores with names like Super Gringo. A five-story shopping center and parking garage are under construction. The market's president, Miguel Galan, says Tepito went upscale after vendors "realized it was easier to pay taxes than to make payoffs." By bringing the underground economy to the surface, free trade promises to lift many Mexicans out of poverty at the same time the government reaps a revenue windfall.
