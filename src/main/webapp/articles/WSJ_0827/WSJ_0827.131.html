International: London Panel Criticizes Chairman of Polly Peck

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 08/27/90

LONDON -- The Quotations Panel of London's Stock Exchange Friday criticized the chairman of Polly Peck International PLC for the manner in which the chairman attempted to take the company private. In a lengthy statement released after the market closed, the panel said Asil Nadir failed to follow "normal standards" when advising the company's board that he wished to make an offer for the diversified company.

The panel has powers to delist or suspend a company's shares if it finds wrongdoing or it can issue a public statement on its report. The panel has conveyed its findings to the relevant authorities, but didn't elaborate.

The stock exchange also was disapproving of Mr. Nadir's decision to ignore advice from financial advisers as well as his calling a board meeting at such short notice on a Sunday. Additionally, they found that the wording used in announcing that an offer might be made for the company was misleading in that it didn't state discussions were still at a preliminary stage.

The company and chairman said it noted the findings of the panel. It added that Polly Peck is "committed to ensuring the long-term maximization of shareholder value and will continue to work towards this end." It said neither the company nor Mr. Nadir wish to make any further comment on the panel's announcement.
