Business Brief -- Steve's Homemade Ice Cream: Second-Period Loss Totaled $86,000 as Sales Fell 24%

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/27/90

Summer is normally the best time for ice cream sales, but for Steve's Homemade Ice Cream Inc. this year it has been dripping with problems. The Lindenhurst, N.Y., company reported a loss of $86,000, or a penny a share, for the second quarter, compared with net income of $531,000, or four cents a share, a year ago. Revenue fell 24% to $6.8 million from $8.9 million.

The ice cream maker attributed the loss to problems with the inventory control program of a major buyer, a temporary disruption of the company's distribution system in California and Florida, and a dispute with a major New England area developer licensee. Officials declined to identify the customers involved.

Company officials say they are confident that earnings will turn in the third quarter. The company continues to spend heavily on advertising, investing $1.2 million more on promotion in the first six months of this year than it did in the 1989 period. Officials also say they expect to resolve the dispute with the developer and licensee soon.
