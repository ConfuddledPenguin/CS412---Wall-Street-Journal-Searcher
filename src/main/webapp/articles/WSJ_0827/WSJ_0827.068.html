Pioneer Electric, IBM Sign Pact On Laser Disks

  • Author: Yumiko Ono
  • Date: 08/27/90

TOKYO -- Pioneer Electronic Corp. said it signed an agreement with International Business Machines Corp. under which IBM will market Pioneer's laser disk players to corporate clients in the U.S. The Japanese company said its Pioneer Communications of America Inc. subsidiary will provide industrial disk players to hook up with IBM computers. IBM will adapt its graphics software to operate on Pioneer's machines.

The laser disk units can provide programs that interact with the user by sound as well as by screen. Pioneer hopes to provide its players programs for schools and for showrooms.

While Pioneer is the leading maker of disk players, about 95% of its sales until now have been for home use -- mainly for laser-disk movies. With IBM's software technology, Pioneer hopes to make greater inroads in the industrial market.

A Pioneer spokesman declined to estimate how much sales the agreement with IBM might generate. The industrial machines range in price from $1,000 to $2,800, he said.

Pioneer last year sold 100,000 laser-disk players to U.S. consumers, giving it about 70% of the U.S. market, according to the spokesman. The consumer units sell for around $600.

In Japan last year Pioneer sold 700,000 laser-disk players, including household and industrial units, and the company hopes to increase the total to 1.2 million this year. The company has about a 50% share of the market for consumer units in Japan, the spokesman said.
