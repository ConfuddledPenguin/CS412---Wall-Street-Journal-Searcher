Australian Airlines Posts Loss

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/27/90

MELBOURNE, Australia -- Australian Airlines, a government-owned domestic carrier, posted an operating loss of 13.7 million Australian dollars (US$11.4 million) for the fiscal year ended June 30, compared with year-earlier profit of A$70.4 million, citing a 1989 pilots strike that disrupted the industry for eight months. The loss totaled A$79.9 million before an income-tax credit and gains from selling aircraft. Operating revenue fell 18% to A$1.02 billion from A$1.24 billion.

Chairman Ted Harris said he believes Australian Airlines will return to profitability in the current fiscal year. He said the airline is "well prepared" for partial deregulation of Australia's airline industry that is to start in November.
