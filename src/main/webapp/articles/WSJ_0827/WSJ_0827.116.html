Business Brief -- Sun Microsystems Inc.: Computer Maker Withdraws Public Stock Offering Plan

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/27/90

Sun Microsystems Inc. dropped its planned public offering of 5.9 million shares of new common stock. The computer maker, which is based in Mountain View, Calif., had been having second thoughts about the plan to raise $150 million through the offering because of the stock's sharp dive since the proposal was announced 11 days ago. At the time, the stock was trading at $32 a share, and Sun had been seeking $31 a share for the new shares.

In national over-the-counter trading Friday, the stock closed at $27.625, up $1.25.

"We do not believe it is in the best interest of shareholders to sell additional shares under current market conditons," said Scott McNealy, Sun's chairman, president and chief executive officer. Mr. McNealy said the company is studying other options to raise capital, but that Sun doesn't plan another stock offering any time soon.

Some analysts had criticized Sun for proposing the offering just a few days after announcing a separate plan, under a long-running agreement, to issue 4.6 million shares to American Telephone & Telegraph Co.
