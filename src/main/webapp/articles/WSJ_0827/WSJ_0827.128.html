Credit Ratings

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/27/90

SOUTHERN Co. (Atlanta) -- Moody's Investors Serv- ice Inc. said it cut its ratings on $490 million of securities of this electric holding company's Gulf Power Co. unit, Pensacola, Fla. The rating concern cited a "weakening of Gulf Power's financial performance, and further pressure on prospective credit quality measurements, given an unconstructive rate decision recently issued by the Florida Public Service Commission." Earlier this month, the commission granted Gulf Power a rate increase of only $11.8 million; the company had requested a $26.3 million boost. Moody's lowered ratings on Gulf Power's first mortgage and secured pollution control bonds to single-A-2 from single-A-1; unsecured pollution-control bonds to single-A-3 from single-A-2; and preferred stock to single-A-2 from single-A-1. A Gulf Power spokesman said the downgrades are "further evidence that the Florida Public Service Commission decision was counterproductive."
