Business Brief -- Everest & Jennings International: Medical Equipment Maker Posts Loss in 2nd Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/27/90

Everest & Jennings International Ltd. reported a second-quarter loss of $4 million, or 44 cents a share, and said that its ability to continue domestic operations depends on successful negotiation of a new credit agreement. In the year-earlier quarter, the maker of wheelchairs and other medical equipment, posted a loss of $5.5 million, or 68 cents a share. Sales rose 15% to $52.8 million from $45.9 million.

Everest & Jennings, which is based in Camarillo, Calif., said the losses have significantly hurt its financial condition and its ability to obtain long-term financing. The company said it took steps last week to cope with its cash shortages, including laying off about 55 employees at its main domestic plant in Camarillo.

Everest & Jennings said it has been trying to negotiate a long-term financing arrangement with its primary domestic lender. The company's credit agreement expired on Dec. 31. Since then, the company has obtained periodic cash infusions from its controlling shareholder, Industrial Equity (Pacific) Ltd., a Hong Kong investment firm.

Barre L. Rorabaugh, president and chief executive officer, said that Everest & Jennings's foreign subsidiaries are profitable and are exceeding their projected performance for the year.
