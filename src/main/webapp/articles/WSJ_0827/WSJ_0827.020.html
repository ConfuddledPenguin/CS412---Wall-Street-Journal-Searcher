S&P Lowers Ratings On Debt of McDonnell Douglas Finance Unit

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 08/27/90

Standard & Poor's Corp. lowered its ratings on about $3 billion of debt issued by McDonnell Douglas Corp.'s finance unit. The rating concern cited the finance unit's problem real-estate and auto loans in addition to the parent company's "weaker credit-worthiness" and the negative outlook for its aerospace business.

S&P reduced McDonnell Douglas Finance Corp.'s senior debt to single-A-minus from single-A-plus, subordinated debt to triple-B-plus from single-A and commercial paper to A-2 from A-1. S&P removed the issues from its Credit-Watch list, where it had placed the term debt June 11 and the commercial paper July 27.

A spokesman for the St. Louis-based parent company said the finance unit had increased its provisions for loan losses. But the unit "isn't in a net-loss position and has been very profitable for some time."

S&P said that although it has traditionally rated the finance unit's debt separately, "the negative outlook for the parent's business represents a potential drag on the credit-worthiness of the subsidiary."

As previously reported, S&P reduced McDonnell Douglas's senior debt rating July 27 to triple-B-plus from single-A-minus and subordinated debt to triple-B from triple-B-plus and removed those issues from its Credit-Watch list. The company's single-A commercial paper rating was placed on Credit-Watch.
