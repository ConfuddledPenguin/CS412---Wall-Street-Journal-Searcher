Letters to the Editor: Playing by the Rules

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/27/90

Contrary to "S&L Cleanup Law's Measures Weakened by Court Rulings, Relaxed Regulations" (Politics and Policy, July 19), the rules governing the operation of the nation's savings institutions have not been weakened or relaxed. If anything, our institutions are operating under some capital standards that are tighter than those applied to commercial banks. The Office of Thrift Supervision has not relaxed its rules. The loans-to-one-borrower transition period referred to is only available to savings institutions that already have more capital today than the law requires them to hold in 1995. They are hardly a threat to the deposit insurance fund.

Also, there is no requirement in the law that savings institutions hold more capital against certain Veterans Administration loans (actually the servicing of VA loans in Government National Mortgage Association pools of mortgages). The OTS imposed a higher capital requirement on some VA loan servicing, then rescinded its directive so that it could follow a congressional mandate to conform capital requirements for savings institutions with those for national banks.

Fred Webber

President

U.S. League of Savings Institutions

Washington
