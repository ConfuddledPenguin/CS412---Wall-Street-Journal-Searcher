New Securities Issues

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/27/90

The following were among Friday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report. CORPORATES

American Airlines -- $238.9 million of equipment trust certificates through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets. The 25-part issue, with maturities ranging from 2 1/2 to 24 1/2 years, was priced with yields varying from 8.75% to 10.68%. The certificates were priced at spreads ranging from 63 basis points to 160 basis points above comparable Treasury issues. The certificates are secured by three McDonnell Douglas DC-9-82 aircraft and five Boeing 757-223 aircraft. According to the preliminary prospectus, American Airlines, a subsidiary of AMR Corp., leases the aircraft from an owner-trustee, Wilmington Trust Co. Although the certificates are not direct obligations of American Airlines, the lease provides that the payments American makes to lease the aircraft together with certain other payments by the airline will go toward interest and principal payments on the certificates.

MORTGAGES

Merrill Lynch Trust 45 -- $510 million of Remic mortgage securities offered in 10 classes by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets. The offering is backed by Government National Mortgage Association 9 1/2% securities with a weighted average coupon rate of 10% and weighted average remaining term to maturity of 26.67 years. Among classes for which details were available, yields ranged from 8.95%, or 55 basis points above three-year Treasury yields, to 10.09%, or 115 basis points over 30-year Treasurys.

OTHER

Student Loan Marketing Association -- $300 million of floating-rate notes due Sept. 6, 1993, to be sold at various prices in negotiated transactions from time to time. The interest rate will float at 55 basis points above the bond equivalent yield of the 91-day U.S. Treasury bill auction rate. The rate will be reset the day after each weekly Treasury bill auction. Interest will be paid quarterly beginning Dec. 6, 1990. The offering is being managed by Lehman Brothers.
