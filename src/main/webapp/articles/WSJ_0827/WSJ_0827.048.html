France's Foreign Trade Deficit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/27/90

PARIS -- France's seasonally adjusted foreign trade deficit widened to a preliminary 4.64 billion francs ($893.9 million) in July from a revised 877 million francs in June, which previously was estimated at 1.03 billion francs, the National Customs office said. Unadjusted July results showed a deficit of 3.78 billion francs, compared with a revised June gap of 415 million francspreviously estimated at 592 million francs -- and a July 1989 deficit of 3.38 billion francs.

The latest results brought France's adjusted trade deficit for the first seven months of this year to 17.67 billion francs, a narrowing from the year-earlier deficit of 24.73 billion francs. On an unadjusted basis, this deficit narrowed to 23.55 billion francs from a gap of 29.3 billion francs in the 1989 first seven months.
