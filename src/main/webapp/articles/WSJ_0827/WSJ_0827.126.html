Bush Discusses '91 Budget In Light of Events in Gulf

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/27/90

WASHINGTON -- President Bush met with his top economic advisers to discuss the fiscal 1991 budget in light of recent events in the Persian Gulf, administration officials said. The meeting, which included Treasury Secretary Nicholas Brady, Budget Director Richard Darman and White House Chief of Staff John Sununu, focused on the proposed mix of spending cuts for fiscal 1991, when the administration is pledged to shrink the federal deficit by $50 billion. It is widely expected that cuts in defense spending may now be smaller than originally planned because of Iraq's Aug. 2 invasion of Kuwait and the subsequent deployment of U.S. forces in the region.

"It was a planning session, they're going to be getting together later, in September," an administration official said after the meeting, which was held in Kennebunkport, Maine, where the president is spending his summer vacation.

"The administration remains firm and resolute that a budget agreement is critical," this official said, adding that the proposed $50 billion of savings for fiscal 1991, which begins Oct. 1, is "still a fairly firm number."

Meanwhile, President Bush signed the paperwork needed to authorize the Office of Management and Budget to carry out the automatic spending cuts required under the Gramm-Rudman deficit-reduction act. The presidential order specifies that the automatic spending restraints take effect Oct. 1.
