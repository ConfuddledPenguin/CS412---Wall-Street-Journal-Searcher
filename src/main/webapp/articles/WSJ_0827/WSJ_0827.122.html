Top French Horse Auction Is Reined In by Gulf Crisis

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/27/90

DEAUVILLE, France -- France's top horse-flesh market took a big hit from the Persian Gulf crisis. The six-day auction of thoroughbreds here this past week attracted winning bids totaling 112.7 million French francs ($21.7 million), down 16% from last year's total of 134.5 million francs, organizers said.

According to observers, breeders from Kuwait and other Gulf states -- big buyers at past auctions -- were conspicuous by their absence this year. Japanese and other Asian buyers only partly made up the difference, including a bid of 6.5 million francs for one horse by a Japanese shipping magnate.

According to the French Thoroughbred Sales Agency, which organizes the annual sale, 375 horses were sold this year, at an average price of 286,679 francs, compared with 413 horses sold last year for an average of 325,750 francs.
