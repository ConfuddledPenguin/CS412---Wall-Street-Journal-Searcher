Short Interest Drops in OTC's Two Categories --- TW Holdings Inc. Figure Shows Largest Increase In Month to Mid-August

  • Author: Robert J. Brennan
  • Date: 08/27/90

NEW YORK -- Short interest among over-the-counter stocks, both large and small, edged down in the month ended in mid-August after small issues leaped the prior month and larger issues edged up. TW Holdings Inc., Spartanburg, S.C., which owns and operates restaurants, principally 470 Hardee's fast-food outlets, led the issues with the largest increase in short interest. Short position in the company rose more than one million shares, to nearly 2.2 million. Last month it reported second-quarter and six-month losses of $12.7 million and $58.8 million, respectively, against net income of $20.8 million and $29.6 million. The losses were attributed to debt and other expenses in the 1989 acquisition of TW Services Inc.

Acclaim Entertainment Inc., a major licensee of Nintendo computer games based in Oyster Bay, N.Y., saw short interest in its shares soar to 785,281 from 225,643 a month earlier. Acclaim recently lifted restrictions on a block of its stock held by entertainment giant MCA Inc., clearing MCA to sell it. Also, it plans to make a new offering of two million shares; current shares outstanding total 18.7 million.

Puget Sound Bancorp, Tacoma, Wash., had practically no short position in the prior month, but in the recent period short interest shot up to 544,633 shares. It was one of several bank and thrift issues that were among the leaders in short interest. Puget Sound, however, recently reported good quarterly results and last month increased its dividend, raising the possibility that investors were seeking to capture the dividend.

First Executive Corp., a Los Angeles life insurance company that once held a large portfolio of junk bonds, again was the volume leader in short interest, with nearly 8.5 million shares, up from 8.3 million at mid-July.

Among the companies with the largest decreases in short interest, Intel Corp. dropped 80%, to 588,393 shares from the previous month's 4.3 million. The chip maker in July reported substantial increases in profit and revenue for the second quarter and six months.

Furon Co., a Laguna Niguel, Calif.-based maker of polymers, had short interest practically disappear, to 6,700 shares from more than 900,000. The company in June announced a plan to redeem a debenture issue for either cash or stock.

Traders who sell short borrow stock and sell it, betting that the stock's price will decline and that they can buy the shares back later at a lower price for return to the lender.

Short interest is the number of shares that haven't yet been purchased for return to lenders. Although a substantial short position reflects heavy speculation that a stock's price will decline, some investors consider an increase in short interest bullish because the borrowed shares eventually must be bought back.

And some short interest doesn't reflect any opinion on the stock at all but shows up as part of another strategy, such as arbitrage or dividend capture.

The National Association of Securities Dealers, the self-regulatory agency that operates the Nasdaq computerized system on which more than 4,300 OTC issues trade, compiles short interest data in two categories: the approximately two-thirds, and generally biggest, Nasdaq stocks that trade on the National Market System; and the other, generally smaller stocks that don't.

NASD reported that short interest in 2,335 National Market System issues, the largest OTC stocks, totaled 255.5 million shares at mid-August, down from a revised 256.9 million in mid-July among 2,299 issues.

Short interest in 1,232 smaller securities traded on Nasdaq declined to 37.7 million shares in August from a revised 38.5 million in July among 1,212 issues.

The National Market System's short-interest ratio at mid-August fell to 2.19 from a revised 2.58 a month earlier, while the short-interest ratio for other OTC issues climbed to 1.06 from a revised 0.96.

The ratio -- the number of trading days at the market's average daily trading volume that would be required to convert the total short interest position -- is a measure of negative trader sentiment. Some analysts suggest, however, that the ratio has weakened in value as an indicator because options and other products can be used to hedge short positions.
