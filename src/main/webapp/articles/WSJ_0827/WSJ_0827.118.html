July Consumption Of Newsprint Rose Among U.S. Users

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/27/90

WASHINGTON -- The American Newspaper Publishers Association estimated July newsprint consumption by all U.S. users at 989,533 metric tons, up 4% from a year earlier. The trade group said consumption by U.S. daily newspapers was little changed at 718,651 metric tons, compared with 719,080 tons in July 1989. A metric ton is equal to about 2,204.62 pounds.

For the first seven months of 1990, the association estimated consumption by all U.S. users was 7,063,463 metric tons, up 1.6% from a year earlier. It said daily newspapers' consumption rose slightly to 5,246,353 metric tons from 5,223,757 tons.

The association said newsprint inventories of newspapers in its statistical sample averaged a 41-day supply in July, down from 43 days in the year-earlier month. It said stocks of North American mills in July were down about 16% from a year earlier.

The association said Canadian newsprint mills operated at 94% of rated capacity in July and 95% in the first seven months. It said U.S. mills operated at 98.6% and 96.6% in the respective periods.
