Business Brief -- Fidelity Federal Bank: California Bank to Acquire Failed Savings and Loans

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/27/90

Fidelity Federal Bank of Glendale, Calif., will acquire two insolvent thrifts in California, the Resolution Trust Corp. said. Fidelity Federal will acquire the six offices of Westwood Savings & Loan Association, Los Angeles, and assume $437.5 million in deposits and secured liabilities from the failed thrift. The RTC will advance $395 million to Fidelity Federal and retain about $301.1 million of the closed institution's assets. After the sale of assets, the ultimate cost to the RTC is estimated at $259.5 million.

Fidelity Federal also will acquire Investment Federal Savings & Loan Association, Chatsworth, and assume about $218.6 million in deposits and secured liabilities from the failed thrift. The RTC is advancing an estimated $173.8 million to Fidelity Federal, and the ultimate cost to the RTC is put at $10.4 million.

The purchases were among several transactions announced by the RTC Friday. In one of those transactions, the agency was appointed receiver for First Federal Savings & Loan Association, Temple, Texas. The failed thrift had about $350.4 million in assets.
