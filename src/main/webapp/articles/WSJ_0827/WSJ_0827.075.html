  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/27/90

AN AUG. 17 ARTICLE reported incorrectly that a lawyer for Nynex Corp. testified in a rate case in New York that New York Telephone Co.'s general counsel John Clarke was one of several Nynex officials who knew about bawdy parties in Florida attended by Nynex purchasing personnel and suppliers. In fact, the lawyer testified that company investigators had been told that Mr. Clarke might have received a report of the 1985-86 investigation that mentioned the gatherings, known as the perverts conventions. Mr. Clarke has since stated that he never received a copy of the investigation report, nor was he ever apprised of its contents. (See: "Nynex Officials Knew of Gatherings, Attorney Testifies" -- WSJ Aug. 17, 1990)
