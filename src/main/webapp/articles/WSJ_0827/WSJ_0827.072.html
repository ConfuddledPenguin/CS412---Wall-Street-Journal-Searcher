Letters to the Editor: Apartheid Is Not Capitalism

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/27/90

Your July 30 article "In South Africa, Communists' Pact With ANC Comes Under Pressure" quotes Jay Naidoo of the Congress of South African Trade Unions as saying at a South African Communist Party rally, "Apartheid has been proven to be the most brutal and inhuman form of capitalism the world has ever seen." This is an irresponsible and erroneous statement, however emotionally appealing. Prior to the advent of legal apartheid, black South African workers and farmers were competing successfully with their white counterparts in the free-market economy. It was because of this that the white government imposed discriminatory labor and land-owning laws, hardly a definition of "capitalism."

What black workers and budding entrepreneurs in South Africa need is not more government intrusion, but less. To jumble labels in such an emotional way is to prepare the ground for tragedy, as has been proved by the recent plethora of collapsed state-dominated economies, not least on the African continent.

Frederic Wile

New York
