Business Brief -- Fokker N.V.: Profit for First Half Soared, Due Partly to Special Gain

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/27/90

Koninklijke Nederlandse Vliegtuigenfa briek Fokker N.V., a Dutch aerospace concern, said its first-half profit more than doubled to 41.9 million guilders ($24.1 million) -- exceeding analysts' estimates -- from 14.1 million guilders a year earlier. The latest results were boosted by a special gain of 10 million guilders related to disposal of 50% of Fokker's Aircraft Financing & Trading B.V. unit. Total operating income advanced 19% to 1.61 billion guilders from 1.35 billion guilders. But operating costs rose 19% to 1.556 billion guilders from 1.31 billion guilders. Operating profit increased 6.5% to 50.9 million guilders from 47.8 million guilders.

Sales rose 17% to 1.312 billion guilders from 1.12 billion guilders.

Fokker, which had 1989 profit of 42.4 million guilders, said that "if the U.S. dollar remains weak for an extended period . . . this could depress profits in the years ahead." Nevertheless, Ata Kahrel, director of Wesselius & Co., said, "I think they will double their (1990) profit and resume paying a dividend."
