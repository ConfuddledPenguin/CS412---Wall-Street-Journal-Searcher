Business Brief -- Granite Co-operative Bank: A Cease-and-Desist Order Is Proposed by the FDIC

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/27/90

Granite Co-operative Bank, Quincy, Mass., reported a fiscal first-quarter loss of $82,000, or 10 cents a share, and said the Federal Deposit Insurance Corp. has proposed that the bank consent to a cease-and-desist order. The loss for the quarter, ended July 31, came after a provision for possible loan losses of $138,000. Granite had net income of $144,000, or 18 cents a share, in the year-earlier period. In last year's first quarter, the loss provision was $38,000. The bank's assets at July 31 declined to $109,496,000, from $129,643,000 at July 31, 1989.

Granite said the order with the FDIC will outline corrective action designed to improve the bank's condition. It said it expects to negotiate specific terms of the order with the FDIC.

Many New England banks, suffering from economic woes and a downturn in the area real estate market, have entered into similar agreements with various regulatory agencies.

Granite's loss for the first quarter follows the bank's loss of $4,768,000, or $5.87 per share, for the year ended April 30, after a loan loss provision of $2,730,000.
