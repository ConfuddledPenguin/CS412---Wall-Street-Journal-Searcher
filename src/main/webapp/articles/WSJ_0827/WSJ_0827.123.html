Sceptre Resources May Profit From Rise In Crude-Oil Prices

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/27/90

CALGARY -- The sharp jump in crude oil prices could boost Sceptre Resources Ltd.'s 1990 cash flow by about 15 million Canadian dollars (US$13.3 million) and earnings by C$10 million, said Richard Gusella, the company's president and chief executive officer. Gusella said the figures assume an average price for West Texas Intermediate crude of about US$25 a barrel for the rest of 1990, or $5 above Sceptre's previous expected price. At US$30 a barrel, Sceptre's cash flow would jump C$30 million and earnings would rise C$20 million this year, he said.

After a disappointing first half, Gusella said that Sceptre in July scaled down its expectations for the current year, only to see its revised numbers completely changed by the oil price spiral that followed Iraq's invasion of Kuwait. "Our old assumptions are all wrong now," he said in an interview, although Sceptre may "coincidentally" now reach its initial cash flow estimate for 1990 of about C$90 million.

That would represent a sharp gain over Sceptre's 1989 cash flow of C$59 million or 85 cents a share. On the earnings side, Gusella said Sceptre expected a break-even year for 1990, and a small loss on a per-share basis, before the recent jump in crude prices.

In 1989, Sceptre reported a net loss of C$24.2 million or 40 cents, after a C$19 million write-down of foreign assets, on revenue of C$198.3 million.

Gusella said the company continues to whittle down debt from its 1989 acquisition of cash-strapped Oakwood Petroleums Ltd., and sees bank debt shrinking to C$196 million by year end, down from C$305.9 million at the end of 1989.
