Business Brief -- General Electric Co.: Unit Plans More Layoffs, Cites Military Budget Cuts

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/27/90

General Electric Co.'s aerospace division announced additional layoffs, citing cutbacks in the U.S. military budget. GE Aerospace of Valley Forge, Pa., said that as part of a restructuring of its Automated Systems department, it will eliminate 650 out of the 950 jobs at its Burlington, Mass., plant by 1992. The plant makes automatic test equipment for military programs. Some of the operations at the plant will be transferred to other GE Aerospace facilities.

GE Aerospace will also close its Huntsville, Ala., plant by the end of 1991 and move the operations to facilities in Daytona Beach, Fla. The Huntsville plant employs 400 people. A GE spokesman said that half of them will be offered relocation, and the rest of the jobs will be eliminated through early retirement and attrition.

The latest cutbacks follow the division's announcement a week earlier that it would eliminate 100 jobs as part of consolidating its Re-Entry Systems department into one facility. Last April, the division eliminated 4,200 jobs.
