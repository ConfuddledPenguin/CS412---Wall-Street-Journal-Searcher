Credit Ratings: Moody's Cuts Ratings On USG Corp. Debt Valued at $1.5 Billion

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/27/90

NEW YORK -- Moody's Investors Service Inc. cut its ratings on $1.5 billion of USG Corp. long-term debt, saying in part that the crisis in the Middle East is likely to reduce housing starts and hurt the company's gypsum wallboard business. The rating agency also cited "the prospect of continued low prices for gypsum wallboard and the rigor of the company's debt amortization schedule." It noted that USG is currently operating at "about break-even" because of depressed gypsum prices and high interest expenses.

Moody's said it expects that USG will be forced to renegotiate its bank covenants and supplement operating cash flow to meet interest and principal payments. Moody's said it believes the company's $200 million revolving credit facility should enable it to service its debt over the next year. But with previously projected asset sales virtually complete, Moody's said USG may be under pressure to sell additional assets and to come up with alternative financing sources on "an ongoing basis."

The agency cut its rating to single-B-2 from single-B-1 on the senior secured debt of USG's United States Gypsum Co. unit. The agency also reduced its rating on USG senior debt to single-B-3 from single-B-1 and on USG subordinated debentures to single-B-3 from single-B-2.
