International: Rosenthal May Resume Payout

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/27/90

FRANKFURT -- Rosenthal AG of West Germany, a porcelain products maker, said it expects to resume paying annual cash dividends beginning for 1990, after omitting the 1989 payout. It last paid five marks ($3.23) a share for 1988. In February, Rosenthal decided to forgo a dividend for 1989, to channel fresh cash to beef up the capital base of the Johan Haviland China Corp. subsidiary in the U.S. "We are confident that for 1990 we can again pay a satisfactory dividend," the company said, but it didn't mention a sum.

The dividend probably will be declared in early 1991.
