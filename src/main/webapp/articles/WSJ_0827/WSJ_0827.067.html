East German Future Isn't Hopeless, Official At Bundesbank Says

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/27/90

FRANKFURT -- The financial state and future prospects for East German industry are not "hopeless," Claus Koehler, a director of the Bundesbank said on West German radio. Mr. Koehler, who is the central bank's directorate member responsible for credit markets, apparently is optimistic. "We mustn't go on the assumption that we are in a hopeless situation" regarding the health and outlook for East German industry, the official said.

He said the central bank polled 140 enterprises with more than 200,000 employees in East Germany over their expectations, and said the Bundesbank was "astonished" with the favorable nature of the replies.

Mr. Koehler said one-third of the respondents expect to boost production, while another third foresee output trends at current levels. Only one-third anticipate falling production, Mr. Koehler said.

He added that the companies noted that the East German economy is "fully ailing," but asserted that their prospects aren't bad. "We know that we have to pass through a deep valley that will be especially characterized by high unemployment. But expectations are improving," Mr. Koehler said.

Mr. Koehler also said that after Oct. 3, when the two Germanys expect to merge, it would make sense for West Germany to undertake an emergency financial cash infusion to support the economically strapped area that is now East Germany.

He said the West German government shouldn't raise taxes to finance the cost of integrating and overhauling East Germany, but should be willing to broaden its budget deficit if necessary.
