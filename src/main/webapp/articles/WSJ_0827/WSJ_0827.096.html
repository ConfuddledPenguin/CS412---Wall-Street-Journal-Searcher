Who's News: James River Assigns Broader Responsibility To 4 Senior Officials

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/27/90

RICHMOND, Va. -- James River Corp. said four executives were given broader responsibilities as part of an effort to simplify the paper-products company's business structure and boost its resources for expansion. Charles E. Eberle, who had directed the company's U.S. towel and tissue operations, was named executive vice president in charge of all consumer-product businesses. The new grouping accounts for about 50% of James River's domestic sales.

Norman K. Ryan, who had headed paperboard operations, was named executive vice president for packaging. Combined operations for food and consumer packaging contribute about 30% of total company sales.

Ernest S. Leopold, a former senior vice president and group executive, was picked as executive vice president of communications papers, the company's coated printing-papers business.

Ronald B. Estridge, the company's technology executive in charge of research and development, assumed additional duties for directing fiber and energy operations.
