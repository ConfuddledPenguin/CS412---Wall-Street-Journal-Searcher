Laura Ashley PLC Chief James Resigns, Effective Saturday

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 08/27/90

LONDON -- Laura Ashley PLC, a clothing and home furnishings concern, said its chief executive officer, John James, will resign on Saturday. A spokesman said that Mr. James's departure was amicable, and that the executive's plans weren't known.

Mr. James's post will be assumed temporarily by Mike Smith, chairman of Laura Ashley's British retail operations. Laura Ashley said it intends to seek a successor from outside the company.

Laura Ashley, which expanded rapidly overseas in the past decade, had a #4.6 million ($9 million) pretax loss in the year ended Jan. 27, a reversal from year-earlier pretax profit of #20.3 million, despite an 18% rise in sales to #296.6 million.

The company experienced a sharp decline in several sectors of its important domestic and U.S. markets, and introduced a line of furniture in Britain just as the local housing market entered a slump.

Mr. James, who joined Laura Ashley in 1974 when its sales stood at #2 million a year, was widely criticized by analysts as the fortunes of the company soured in the past year. "The business has now outgrown him," said Richard Ratner of Carr Kitcat & Aitken, a London stockbrokerage firm.
