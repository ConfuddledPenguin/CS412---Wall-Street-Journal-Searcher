International: Hong Kong's Outlook for GDP

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/27/90

HONG KONG -- The government lowered its 1990 gross domestic product growth estimate to 2.5% from 3%, and increased its estimate for this year's inflation rate to 9.5% from 8.5%. An official said that the revisions were largely influenced by the crisis in the Persian Gulf, but that the weaker trends were in place even before Iraq seized Kuwait.

He declined to break down GDP growth on a quarterly basis, but indicated that while first-quarter growth was close to zero, GDP in the second quarter was slightly better. Growth is expected to peak in the current quarter, before the full economic effects of higher oil prices work their way through the economy.
