Business Brief -- Wellman Inc.: Plastics Concern Acquires New England CRInc, Unit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/27/90

Wellman Inc. said it completed the acquisition of New England CRInc, and its subsidiary, Materials Recovery Inc. To complete the acquisition, Wellman issued about 300,000 shares of its common stock to CRInc shareholders valued at $7.5 million, and retired about $4.5 million of CRInc's debt. Before issuing the new shares, Wellman had about 32 million common shares outstanding. For the first six months of 1990, Wellman posted net sales of $418 million.

For Wellman, based in Shrewsbury, N.J., a leading manufacturer of fibers and plastic resins from recyclable waste products, the acquisition of CRInc will mean a steady source of raw materials. CRInc, a closely held company formed in 1982 and based in Billerica, Mass., designs, builds and operates recycling facilities. The company also has exclusive North American rights to a West German technology that mechanically separates waste products.

CRInc's shareholders plan to form a new limited partnership to continue the firm's deposit container and curbside pickups in New England, Wellman officials said. Plastics collected by the company will then be sold to Wellman under a five-year purchase agreement.

In New York Stock Exchange trading Friday, Wellman closed at $17.625, up $1 a share.
