Business Brief -- Ford Motor Co.: Closure of Assembly Plant Is Extended for Two Weeks

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/27/90

Ford Motor Co. said it's extending for two weeks a temporary shutdown at its Dearborn, Mich., assembly plant because of a parts shortage. The plant was scheduled to reopen Sept. 4 but now is slated to reopen the week of Sept. 10, the company said. Ford said the temporary shutdown "is necessitated by a parts shortage related to start-up of the new 2.3-liter engine."

The assembly plant builds about 3,700 Ford Mustangs a week and already has been idle for two weeks. Initially, Ford idled the plant for one week in mid-August because of weak demand for the Mustangs. Since then it has been idle because of the parts shortage.

The combined four weeks of no production will result in about 14,000 fewer Mustangs and will idle 2,100 hourly workers.
