Ericsson Profit Rise Before Taxes Slows Quarter-to-Quarter

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 08/27/90

STOCKHOLM -- Telefon AB L.M. Ericsson said its rate of pretax profit growth slowed to 14% in the second quarter from 94% during this year's first three months, and its share prices tumbled on nervous world equity markets. The big Swedish telecommunications concern insisted its biggest divisions are performing above expectations and reaffirmed an earlier forecast for a 25% increase in full-year profit before tax.

Ericsson's American depositary receipts fell $13 to $180.50 yesterday in national over-the-counter trading.

Ericsson management remained optimistic, dealing special accolades to the U.S.-based joint venture for cellular phone equipment formed with General Electric Co. last year. Ericsson's chief executive officer, Lars Ramqvist, said sales at the cellular venture with GE are running between 10% and 15% above the year's budgeted target of $1 billion.

He added that the Ericsson-GE venture has picked up market share in the U.S. at the expense of local competitors and has reached preliminary agreements with several big American network operators to replace their existing cellular systems with new ones from Ericsson. The Ericsson executive declined to name any of the operators but said pending successful completion of financing he expected announcement of several major orders before year end.

Shrugging off the second-quarter profit slowdown, Mr. Ramqvist insisted he was "more optimistic about the quality of earnings now than after the year's first three months. We're seeing a continued positive trends in markets that have been strong the past few years."

Ericsson said profit before tax in the second quarter climbed to 1.2 billion kronor ($208.3 million) from 1.06 billion kronor a year earlier. Sales in the period rose 22% to 12.08 billion kronor from 9.9 billion kronor, the company said.

First-half pretax profit surged 43% to 2.39 billion kronor from 1.67 billion kronor the previous year, mainly due to the strong first quarter. However Ericsson officials noted that the first-quarter year-on-year comparison had been distorted by heavy one-time charges that left last year's profit figure unusually weak.

Installation of a cellular system that was under way in Kuwait has been halted, Ericsson said. But company executives added that operations in the Mideast were marginal, accounting for less than 3% of sales and a smaller chunk of profit in the first quarter.
