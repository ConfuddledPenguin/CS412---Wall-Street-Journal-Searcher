Business Brief -- Lewis Galoob Toys Inc.: Company Reports Net Loss For Quarter as Sales Plunge

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/30/90

Lewis Galoob Toys Inc. reported a second quarter net loss of $8.3 million, or 89 cents a share. Sales plunged 49% to $28.4 million from $56 million a year earlier. In the year-earlier quarter, Lewis Galoob had net income of $4.7 million, or 49 cents a share.

The loss came on the heels of a $1.4 million first-quarter loss and reflects general sluggishness in the toy industry and problems in certain product lines, among other things.

A federal court three weeks ago issued a preliminary injunction halting sales of the San Francisco-based company's new Game Genie video game. The order stemmed from a suit brought by Nintendo of America Inc., a unit of Japan's Nintendo Co., charging Lewis Galoob with copyright infringement.

For the six months, Lewis Galoob had a net loss of $9.8 million, or $1.05 a share, on sales of $61.6 million. In the year-ago period, the company had profit of $7.1 million, or 74 cents a share, on sales of $93.6 million.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading Friday, Lewis Galoob closed unchanged at $4.375.
