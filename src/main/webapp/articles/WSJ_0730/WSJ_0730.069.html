International Brief -- Thorn EMI PLC: Results Seen Weak for Half But Strong for Fiscal Year

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/30/90

Thorn EMI PLC of Britain, a music, home-appliance rental and lighting concern, is being hurt by soft domestic consumer demand and stiff competition in some product areas world-wide and expects weak first-half earnings, Colin Southgate, chairman and chief executive officer, said. But Mr. Southgate told shareholders at the annual meeting that Thorn should show improved earnings for fiscal 1991. For the year ended March 31, Thorn had pretax profit of #318 million ($576.9 million), up 9.8% from a year earlier.

Thorn has been selling interests that it considers to be outside its mainstream businesses. Thorn recently said it is holding talks to sell its Holophane S.A. pressed glass subsidiary and in May said it was negotiating the sale of its lighting unit to GTE Corp., Stamford, Conn.

Both of Thorn's two main businesses, recorded music and appliance rentals, "are performing well on a world-wide basis and in line with plan," Mr. Southgate said.
