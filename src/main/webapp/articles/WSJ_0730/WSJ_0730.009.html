Micrografx Expects Profit for First Period Since Going Public

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/30/90

RICHARDSON, Texas -- Micrografx Inc. is "real comfortable" with analysts' estimates that it will report net income of $430,000, or 11 cents a share, for its first quarter, ended June 30, said J. Paul Grayson, chairman. Micrografx, which designs and sells personal computer graphics software, had profit of $270,000, or seven cents a share, a year earlier. Analysts expect sales to reach $5 million in this quarter, compared with $3.9 million in the 1989 period. The earnings report will be the first since Micrografx went public June 19.

Mr. Grayson said Micrografx owes its success to Microsoft Corp.'s successful Windows 3.0 -- a graphics package that put a friendlier face on the Microsoft's ubiquitous MS-DOS personal computer operating system. Mr. Grayson and his brother George, Micrografx's president, were among the first to design software based on Windows. That was in 1985, when the Microsoft package was scorned as a pale imitation of Apple Computer Corp. graphics.

Now, WorkGroup Technologies, a Hampton, N.H., market research company, expects Microsoft to ship 1.8 million copies of Windows by year's end. "A year ago it was starting to look like we were geniuses," Mr. Grayson joked. "This year people decided we could predict the future."

As users shop for software compatible with Windows, said David Bayer, an analyst with Montgomery Securities in San Francisco, "the strongest single-product category next year will be Windows application software."

Micrografx cut staff and overhead as its annual sales growth fell from 100% to 60% in fiscal 1990, ended March 31, and its margins tightened. Analysts forecast fiscal 1991 sales growth of about 60%.

Even so, investors enthusiastically embraced the company's initial public offering. Underwriters estimated an offering price of $11 to $13, but the stock opened at $16 a share and traded up to its present level of around $23.
