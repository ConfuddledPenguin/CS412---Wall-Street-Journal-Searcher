New Stock Listings

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/30/90

NEW YORK -- The New York Stock Exchange announced seven new listings, the American Stock Exchange one, and the National Market System of the over-the-counter market three. Seven of the new listings are initial public offerings.

On the Big Board, Banner Aerospace Inc., a Cleveland distributor of replacement parts for the aviation industry, began trading as an initial public offering Friday under BAR.

Greenery Rehabilitation Group Inc., a Newton, Mass., provider of rehabilitation services to patients with brain injuries, previously traded over-the-counter, will begin trading Friday under the symbol GRG.

International Recovery Corp., a Miami Springs, Fla., waste management and oil recycling company, previously traded on the Amex, begins trading today under INT.

Latin America Investment Fund Inc., a New York investment company, began trading last Wednesday as an initial public offering under LAM.

Offshore Pipelines Inc., Houston, a marine construction service for offshore oil and gas companies, began trading Wednesday as an initial public offering under OFP.

Patriot Select Dividend Trust, a Boston investment company, began trading last Tuesday under DIV.

Singapore Fund Inc., a New York investment company, began trading Wednesday as an initial public offering under SGF.

Joining the Amex, Belmac Corp., a Tampa, Fla., pharmaceutical research and development company, previously traded on the over-the-counter market, begins trading tomorrow under BLM.

On the OTC market, Coho Resources Inc., Dallas, a subsidiary of Coho Canada, an oil and gas exploration services company, began trading Thursday under COHO.

IKOS Systems Inc., a Sunnyvale, Calif., maker of hardware and software to simulate semiconductors and computer systems, began trading Wednesday under IKOS.

Trimble Navigation Ltd., a Sunnyvale, Calif., maker of precision electronics gear for determining geographic locations, began trading July 20 under TRMB.

Separately, the Amex, the Philadelphia Stock Exchange and the Chicago Board Options Exchange began trading options on the listed stock of Oryx Energy Co., a Dallas oil and gas company, under options symbol ORX.
