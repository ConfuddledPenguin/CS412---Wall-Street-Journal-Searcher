Credit Ratings: Debt of McDonnell, General Dynamics Is Downgraded

  • Author: David J. Jefferson
  • Date: 07/30/90

Standard & Poor's Corp. and Moody's Investors Service Inc. each lowered their ratings on debt of McDonnell Douglas Corp., citing its weakened financial condition and the troubles it is having with military programs. Separately, S&P downgraded debt of General Dynamics Corp., reflecting second-quarter charges related to military jobs. Both aerospace companies are based in St. Louis.

S&P reduced McDonnell's senior debt to triple-B-plus from single-A-minus and subordinated debt to triple-B from triple-B-plus. The rating concern removed the issues from its CreditWatch list, where it had placed them June 18. The single-A-2 rating on commercial paper was affirmed.

The McDonnell Douglas Finance Corp. unit's senior debt, rated single-A-plus, and subordinated debt, rated single-A, remain on CreditWatch with negative implications. The single-A-1 commercial paper rating was placed on CreditWatch.

Total debt of McDonnell and the finance unit is about $5.2 billion, S&P said. It didn't say how much of the debt was affected by its downgrades.

S&P said the reductions reflect potential cost overruns on the Navy's A-12 attack plane that McDonnell is developing with General Dynamics and the T-45 Navy trainer jet program. It noted that McDonnell hasn't taken any charges against earnings for the potential overruns. Instead, the company is filing claims against the government for reimbursements it believes are due on those programs; it included an estimate of the value of these claims in its second-quarter earnings. General Dynamics, however, took a $450 million charge in the second quarter on the delayed A-12 program.

Moody's said it downgraded its ratings on McDonnell's commercial paper to prime-3 from prime-2, senior debt to Baa-3 from Baa-1 and subordinated debt to Ba-2 from Baa-2. It also lowered ratings on the finance unit's senior debt to Baa-1 from single-A-2; subordinated debt to Baa-3 from single-A-3; and commercial paper to prime-2 from prime-1.

About $3 billion of long-term debt is affected by the rating concern's actions.

"McDonnell Douglas Corp.'s financial condition has weakened significantly, despite several major restructuring programs designed to significantly improve its cost structure," Moody's said.

McDonnell said it was "disappointed" by the ratings reductions. "We don't feel that our company's performance, financial condition and our future prospects warrant these measures," a spokeswoman said.

In its action regarding General Dynamics, S&P downgraded senior debt to singleA from single-A-plus and subordinated debt to single-A-minus from single-A.

About $610 million of long-term debt is affected.

S&P affirmed its single-A-1 ratings on commercial paper of the company and its General Dynamics Credit Corp.

S&P said the downgrades reflect the $450 million charge the company took in the second quarter for the delayed A-12 Navy attack plane and a $50 million charge on the Sincgars Army radio program. "As a result," S&P said, "financial parameters will be weaker than previously anticipated."

General Dynamics said of the downgrades, "We expected it, and it is probably no surprise to the financial community, either."

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading Friday, McDonnell shares closed at $42.75, up 12.5 cents, and General Dynamics shares closed at $27.375, down $2.125.
