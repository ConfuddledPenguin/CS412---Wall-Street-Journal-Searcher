New Securities Issues

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/30/90

The following were among Friday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATES

Associates Corp. of North America -- $300 million of 8 7/8% senior notes, due Aug. 1, 1995, priced at 99.94 to yield 8.89%. The noncallable notes were priced at a spread of 67 basis points over the Treasury five-year note. Rated double-A-3 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and double-A-minus by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Kidder Peabody & Co.

Johnson & Johnson -- $250 million of 8 1/2% notes, due Aug. 15, 1995, priced at par to yield 8.5%. The noncallable notes were priced at a spread of 27 basis points over Treasury's five-year note. Rated Triple-A by both Moody's and S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Salomon Brothers Inc. The offering was increased to $250 million from $200 million.

C.I.T. Group Holdings Inc. -- $200 million of 8 7/8% senior notes, due Aug. 15, 1994, priced at par to yield 8.875%. The noncallable notes were priced at a spread of 66 basis points over Treasury's four-year note. Rated single-A-1 by Moody's and single-A-plus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Bear Stearns & Co.

First Chicago Corp. -- $100 million of 9 7/8% subordinated notes, due Aug. 15, 2000, priced at 98.625 to yield 10.097%. The noncallable notes were priced at a spread of 165 basis points over Treasury 10-year note. Rated Baa-1 by Moody's and single-A-minus by S&P., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Salomon Brothers Inc.

MORTGAGE

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $250 million issue of Remic mortgage securities offered in 22 classes by Donaldson Lufkin Jenrette Securities. The offering, Series 1990-100, is backed by Fannie Mae 9 1/2% securities.

EUROBONDS

Province of Quebec (Canada) -- 250 million Canadian dollars of 11% Eurobonds due Aug. 16, 2000, at a fixed reoffering price of 98.97 via Societe Generale.

Deutsche Bank Finance, Curacao, Netherlands Antilles (West German parent) -- 300 million marks of bonds priced at 100.80 and due Aug. 29, 2000, via Deutsche Bank. Coupon is 9 1/2% for first three years, 15 1/8% less the six-month London Interbank Offered Rate for the final seven years. Guaranteed by Deutsche Bank AG in Frankfurt. Fees 1.70.

Dresdner Finance BV (West German parent) -- 150 million Swiss francs of 7% public bonds due Aug. 23, 1995, priced at 101.75 via Dresdner Bank (Schweiz). Guaranteed by Dresdner Bank AG, Frankfurt.

Ask Planning Center (Japan) -- 150 million Swiss francs of privately placed convertible notes due Dec. 31, 1995, with a fixed 5% coupon at par via Union Bank of Switzerland. Notes callable beginning June. 30, 1993 at 104, declining by one point annually. Each 50,000 Swiss franc note convertible from Sept. 3, 1990 to Dec. 20, 1995 at a premium approximately 2 1/2% pc over the closing share price Aug. 2, when terms are fixed. Fees 1.50.
