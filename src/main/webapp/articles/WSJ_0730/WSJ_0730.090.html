Chicago Blackout, Looting Follow Fire at Utility Site

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/30/90

CHICAGO -- A fire at a Commonwealth Edison Co. generating plant knocked out power for about 40,000 customers on the city's west and southwest side late Saturday night, and the blackout sparked a rash of looting incidents that lasted until dawn. By yesterday evening, a spokesman for the utility said, power had been restored to "about half" the affected customers in the area. Returning service to the remaining customers may take until Wednesday, he said, although the work could be completed as early as today.

Police put extra patrols on the streets and said they arrested 49 people on looting-related charges after power was knocked out late Saturday, the Associated Press reported. According to the AP, three people died in a fire that was started by candles those people were using to light their home.

Commonwealth Edison serves three million customers in northern Illinois; the spokesman said the problem at the plant isn't expected to have any significant financial impact on the company.
