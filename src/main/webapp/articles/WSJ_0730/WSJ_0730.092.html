Marketing & Media: Union Rejects Latest Offer From NBC for 4-Year Pact

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/30/90

NEW YORK -- A union representing producers, news writers, engineers and technicians at National Broadcasting Co. overwhelmingly rejected a four-year contract for the second time since April. A spokesman for the union, the National Association of Broadcast Employees and Technicians, said it hoped to have further negotiations with NBC, a unit of General Electric Co. NBC had no immediate comment.

The vote was 1,290 to 516 to reject NBC's latest offer. The previous contract expired March 30. No strike vote has been taken. The union went on a 17-week strike against NBC in 1987. The main issue involves jurisdiction over people hired by the day rather than on a full-time basis.
