Business Brief -- Landsing Pacific Fund: California Investment Trust Adopts Poison-Pill Proposal

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/30/90

Landsing Pacific Fund said its board adopted a stock rights offering plan, in part to "prevent abusive takeover tactics." Landsing, a Menlo Park, Calif., real estate investment trust, said the so-called poison pill plan wasn't in response to any specific takeover effort, but acknowledged that it recently received "an unfinanced expression of interest" in a possibile acquisition.

Landsing didn't elaborate and didn't disclose the interested party; however, a group including Independent Holdings Inc. said in June that it raised its stake in Landsing to 6.25% and that it might seek to enter discussions with management over the direction of the company. The rights offering would make any unwanted takeover bid much more expensive. Independent Holdings couldn't be reached to comment.

In American Stock Exchange trading Friday, Landsing closed at $8.125, unchanged.
