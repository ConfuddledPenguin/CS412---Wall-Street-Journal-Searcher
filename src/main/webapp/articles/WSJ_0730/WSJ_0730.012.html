Western Digital Says Net Doubled In Its 4th Quarter

  • Author: Jim Herron Zamora
  • Date: 07/30/90

IRVINE, Calif. -- Western Digital Corp.'s net income more than doubled in the fourth quarter ended June 30 to $9.5 million, or 32 cents a share, from $4.3 million, or 15 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue rose 21% to $293.9 million from $243.5 million.

Western Digital said the improved results were spurred by a large order for disk drives in its storage products division.

For the year, net fell 29% to $24.2 million, or 82 cents a share, from $34.3 million, or $1.18 a share, in fiscal 1989. Revenue rose 8% to $1.07 billion from $992.1 million.

Roger W. Johnson, Western Digital chairman, president and chief executive officer, attributed the year to year drop to poor first-quarter results. He said results in the quarter were hurt by hurricane damage to a company plant in Puerto Rico, as well as a rapid change in graphics technology that damped sales of the company's graphic boards when accompanying new monitors were in short supply.

The company's storage products unit, which represents about 75% of Western Digital's business, has helped bring earnings back up. The unit, which manufactures storage controllers, chips, and disk drives, secured a $38 million order for intelligent disk drives -- its largest ever for those drives -- in the fourth quarter.

Western Digital also reached development agreements with International Business Machines Corp., Mr. Johnson said. Terms of the agreements weren't disclosed.

Separately, Western Digital named Kathryn A. Braun, formerly senior vice president and general manager of storage products, executive vice president.

In her new post, Ms. Braun, 39 years old, will oversee the company's move toward so-called "interarchitecture" -- designing, developing and marketing complete packages of storage and microprocessor products.
