Business Brief -- Citizens First Bancorp Inc.: Loss Totaling $34.5 Million Is Reported for 2nd Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/30/90

Citizens First Bancorp Inc., Glen Rock, N.J., reported a $34.5 million second-quarter loss and predicted a $15 million to $17 million deficit for the year because of a large increase in its reserve for possible losses on bad loans. The quarterly loss equals $1.61 a share. It had net income of $9.2 million, or 43 cents a share, a year earlier.

For all of 1989, the banking concern earned $37.2 million, or $1.73 a share. Citizens expects to return to profitability in 1991, Chairman Richard G. Kelley said.

The $70 million increase in the loan-loss reserve was required after a routine examination by the comptroller of the currency, Mr. Kelley said.

"The increasingly stringent regulatory banking environment nationwide is now penetrating our region, requiring banks to add to loan-loss reserves because of the widespread downturn in the real estate industry," he said. But Mr. Kelley described Citizens' loan portfolio and cash flows as "very strong."

In American Stock Exchange composite trading Friday, Citizens closed at $8.75, up 12.5 cents.
