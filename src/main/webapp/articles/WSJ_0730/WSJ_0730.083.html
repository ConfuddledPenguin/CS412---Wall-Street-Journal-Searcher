Senate Hearings on Souter Are Set to Begin Sept. 13

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/30/90

WASHINGTON -- The Senate Judiciary Committee set Sept. 13 as the start of the hearings on the nomination of Judge David Souter for the U.S. Supreme Court. That makes it unlikely that Judge Souter, if confirmed by the Senate, would be sworn in before the high court's closed-door conference the week of Sept. 24, when the justices vote on whether to hear or reject around 1,000 cases. And it would take a quick timetable for Judge Souter to be sworn in by Oct. 1, when the court term opens with two weeks of oral arguments.

In announcing the date, Sen. Joseph Biden (D., Del.), the committee chairman, said he doesn't know how long the confirmation process will take.
