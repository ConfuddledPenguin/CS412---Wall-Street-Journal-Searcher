Letters to the Editor: Trump Card Played On Banks, Taxpayers

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/30/90

In your June 27 news article you mention that the banks agreed on the amounts Mr. Trump could spend on charitable contributions and personal/household expenses. The figure published by other papers was about $450,000 a month, to be reduced to $350,000 a month within a number of years. I consider it completely amoral, not to say repugnant, to grant a man $5.4 million for his pocket money, a man who built castles on air bragging that he never used his own money, who has misused these borrowed funds to the detriment of the people involved. Banks will suffer large losses, these losses will be deductible from their taxes, so that ultimately it will be the taxpayer who pays for it all.

Savings-and-loan directors are being tried for fraud; Mr. Trump gets $5.4 million a year instead.

Peter Manasse

Monte Carlo
