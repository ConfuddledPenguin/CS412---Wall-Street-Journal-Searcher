Business Brief -- Royal Dutch/Shell Group: Shell Oil Says 2nd Period Earnings Declined by 63%

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/30/90

Shell Oil Co. reported that second-quarter earnings fell 63% to $161 million from $440 million a year earlier. Revenue declined to $5.17 billion from $5.87 billion.

Shell said a major factor in the profit decline was a decision to write off some of the investment in certain oil and gas properties offshore California because of continuing delays in securing development permits.

Shell Oil, a subsidiary of the Royal Dutch/Shell Group, said the write-offs applying to offshore California and the Beaufort Sea amounted to $76 million. The benefit of a tax adjustment applicable to prior years partly offset the write-offs, the company said.

The company also attributed the earnings decline to lower production volumes. Crude oil prices were significantly lower in the second quarter but slightly improved in the first half.
