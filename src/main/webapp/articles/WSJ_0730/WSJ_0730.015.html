Bay View Capital Withdraws Offering, Cited Poor Market

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/30/90

SAN MATEO, Calif. -- Bay View Capital Corp. said it withdrew a planned offering of $31.3 million of convertible preferred stock, citing the poor market for financial-institution stocks. Bay View joins a growing list of thrifts who have scrapped equity offerings because the market wasn't much interested. Coast Savings Financial Inc. recently gave up on a $150-million offering of preferred shares for the same reasons.

The market's disinterest in S&Ls' equity is making it all the more difficult for the scores of undercapitalized thrifts to raise equity and meet the new, tougher capital requirements that took effect in December. Failure to meet those new standards can lead to regulators' sanctions, which can include being put out of business. Bay View said it already meets all of the new thrift-capital requirements.

Separately, Bay View said it adopted a common-stock rights offering plan to "deter abusive takeover tactics." The thrift said the plan isn't in response to any specific takeover threat. Such so-called "poison pill" plans are designed to discourage unwanted takeover bids by making them much more expensive.
