Who's News: MIPS's Boesenberg Is Named President

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/30/90

SUNNYVALE, Calif. -- MIPS Computer Systems Inc., a computer concern, said it named Charles M. Boesenberg president. Mr. Boesenberg, 42 years old, had been executive vice president, marketing, and succeeds Robert C. Miller, 46, who remains chairman and chief executive officer. Mr. Boesenberg joined MIPS from Apple Computer Inc., where he had been the top sales executive.

MIPS makes microprocessers based on a semiconductor technology known as reduced instruction set computing, or RISC, which allows for fast and powerful computing. The company has been growing rapidly for several years and went public last fall. In the first six months of 1990, it earned $7.5 million, or 30 cents a share, on revenue of $71.8 million.

Mr. Miller said Mr. Boesenberg's appointment will strengthen and broaden MIPS's management structure, which he said is necessary for MIPS to deal with anticipated growth. "This new structure should better position MIPS to become a $1 billion corporation in the 1990s," Mr. Miller said.
