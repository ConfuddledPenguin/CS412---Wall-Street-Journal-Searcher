World Bank's IFC Unit To Join Lending Group For Colombia Project

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/30/90

WASHINGTON -- The International Finance Corp., a World Bank affiliate, said it will join with a group of European banks in providing a $70 million loan for a $321 million crude-oil pipeline project in Colombia. The IFC will provide half the loan.

The borrower, Oleoducto de Colombia, will operate a 295-mile pipeline to transport crude oil from oil fields in the Llanos and Magdalena Valley regions to a coastal terminal.

The IFC said the project will have an initial capacity to move 150,000 barrels a day. The capacity may be increased later.

Other financing will include about $100 million in equity capital from the owners of the pipeline venture and export credits from Argentina, Brazil, France and the U.S.

The European banks participating in the IFC-arranged financing include Societe Generale of France; Deutsche Bank Luxembourg, a subsidiary of Deutsche Bank AG of West Germany; Banque Francaise du Commerce Exterieur; ABN Bank; Banque Nationale de Paris; Credit Lyonnais; and Banque Sudameris.
