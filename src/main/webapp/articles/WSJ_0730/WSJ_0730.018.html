International Brief -- Lloyds Bank PLC: Six-Month Pretax Earnings Rose Sharply From '89 Pace

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/30/90

Lloyds Bank PLC posted first-half pretax profit of #408 million ($740 million), nearly five times that of the year-earlier period, when profit was curbed by extensive Third World loan provisions. Pretax profit for first half of 1989 was #93 million, largely reflecting the British bank's #483 million charge to cover loans to less developed countries.

In the latest half, Lloyds reduced such charges to #66 million. The latest charges, the bank said, were used to cover unpaid interest on loans, primarily to Brazil and Argentina.
