Who's News: Ford Taps Officers In North American Car Lines Revamping

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/30/90

DEARBORN, Mich. -- Ford Motor Co. is shuffling its North American auto operations to realign a variety of engine development, vehicle engineering and manufacturing operations into three units. The moves, which take effect Sept. 1, are aimed at streamlining Ford's North American automotive operations with the company investing heavily in a campaign to develop new vehicles, engines and transmissions, company spokesmen said.

Ford said Max L. Jurosek, 60-year-old vice president, car product development, will become vice president and general manager of powertrain operations. Mr. Jurosek will oversee nearly all Ford's North American engine and transmission operations, including engine and transmission engineering and casting operations. Ford is making a major push to develop new engines and transmissions and bolster one of the weak spots in the auto maker's product lineup.

Mr. Jurosek will be succeeded as vice president, car product development, by Kenneth K. Kohrs, 51, vice president, car programs management and powertrain planning and engineering. In his new role, Mr. Kohrs will oversee planning for products as well as a new vehicle engineering operation. In effect, he will oversee the process of taking a car from a concept to a working product design.

Ford also is creating a vehicle operations unit that will combine engineers who design car bodies for manufacture and the people who design and manage the North American factories that build the vehicles. The general manager of this new unit will be Henry A. Nickol, 58, general manager of Ford's powertrain operations.

Messrs. Jurosek, Kohrs and Nickol will report to Alexander J. Trotman, executive vice president, North American automotive operations, Ford said.
