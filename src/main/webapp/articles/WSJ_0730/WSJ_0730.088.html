International: Ferruzzi Units Will Merge Into New Holding Concern

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/30/90

MILAN -- Montedison S.p.A and Ferruzzi Agricola Finanziaria S.p.A., two companies controlled by Ferruzzi Finanziaria S.p.A., announced plans to merge into a holding company, controlling all the agro-industrial and chemical interests of Italy's Ferruzzi group. The company, to be named Montedison S.p.A., will have annual sales of about 35 trillion lire ($29.5 million).

As a precondition of the merger, Ferruzzi Agricola will raise the value of its shares to 1,000 lire from 800 lire.

The company will also arrange a three-for-seven bonus issue for all categories of its stock. It will then merge and incorporate Montedison through a share swap, offering seven shares of Ferruzzi Agricola Finanziaria for every 10 shares of Montedison.

Ferruzzi Agricola said that, thanks to the bonus issue, the effective share-swap ratio is 10 Ferruzzi Agricola shares for every 10 Montedison shares. No cash is involved in the transaction.

The new company, to be based in Milan, should make it easier for investors to evaluate Ferruzzi's business and help it raise money on international markets.

Ferruzzi said that the deal would benefit Ferruzzi Agricola shareholders because it would strengthen the company's assets. For Montedison shareholders, the merger means the agro-industrial company Erizania S.p.A. will become part of the enlarged group.
