REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: Cuomo vs. a Tax

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/30/90

New York Gov. Mario Cuomo finally met a higher tax he didn't like. Last week, he was part of a press conference to blast the idea of limiting the deductibility of state taxes on the federal form. This would chiefly affect taxpayers in the states with the highest local and state taxes. As the table nearby shows (see related story: "GOP Tax Targets -- State by State" -- WSJ July 30, 1990), New York tops this list. Gov. Cuomo prefers a rise in federal income tax rates for the wealthy. "Raise their taxes," he said. "It hurts nobody." New York Mayor David Dinkins defended deductibility to keep the federal government "a passive indirect partner and an unwilling partner to boot" in the city. We can see why the governor and mayor want taxpayers in other states to subsidize high-tax states, but maybe we could have a national referendum on the question.
