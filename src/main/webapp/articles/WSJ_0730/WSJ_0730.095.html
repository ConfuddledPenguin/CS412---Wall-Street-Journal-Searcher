Business Brief -- Arix Corp.: Minicomputer Firm Expects To Report 4th-Quarter Loss

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/30/90

Arix Corp. said it expects to report a loss of $20 million to $22 million for its fourth quarter, ended June 30, because of charges related to shrinking its main unit. The San Jose, Calif., minicomputer maker also said its deteriorating condition will reduce its ability to borrow under its existing bank line of credit to about $2.9 million. It said it is seeking alternate financing sources but didn't elaborate.

Arix has been hurt by declining revenue from its biggest customer, Unisys Corp.

It said the charges, which will total $12 million to $14 million, relate to employee-termination costs, to costs of terminating leases and to write-downs.

Arix also said it expects to report that fourth-quarter revenue plunged 65% to $7 million from $20 million. For the first nine months, the company had a net loss of $11.2 million, or $1.46 a share, on revenue of $49.6 million.

In over-the-counter trading Friday, Arix closed at $1.9375, down 6.25 cents.
