Marketing & Media: Visa Gets Aggressive In Battle for Users Of American Express --- Advertising Campaign Hopes To Convince Merchants That Bank Card Is Better

  • Author: Thomas R. King
  • Date: 07/30/90

Visa International is going after American Express Co. again -- and this time it's more aggressive than ever. Visa will unveil an ad campaign today, its first-ever directed to merchants, to convince them that American Express's higher fee to merchants is exorbitant. Visa also charges the so-called discount rate -- the fee merchants pay for each sale made with a card. But Visa's fees are about 2% for charges made with its card, while American Express's fee schedule calls for payments of 3.5% of each sale made with an American Express card.

In the latest campaign to woo customers from American Express, a unit of Shearson Lehman Hutton Holdings Inc., Visa is planning a direct-mail package to 250,000 merchants. The mailing will ask the merchants to remove all American Express signs, putting Visa signs on doors and cash registers instead. Visa also urges merchants to ask its customers, "Would you like to put this on your Visa?" when ringing up a sale.

The move on American Express comes as Visa's card ranks are steadily increasing. But the company has been trying to burnish its image by attracting more of the heavy-charging consumers that make up the bulk of American Express's franchise.

"We feel the merchant community doesn't have the real facts on how the two brands compare," said Brad Morgan, executive vice president at Visa. Mr. Morgan said Visa is escalating its effort to overcome "the myth that merchants have no choice but to accept American Express if they want to go after the upscale consumer."

The ad campaign, which makes its debut in many publications this week, pitches a toll-free number that merchants can call to get a "Profit Improvement Calculator." This product shows merchants how much money they would keep if they converted American Express sales to Visa sales.

Experts who watch the credit card industry predicted it isn't likely many merchants will offer Visa cards exclusively as a result of the campaign. But some said Visa's strategy is sound: "This is the first time that anybody has ever presented a real strong case for the merchant to look at the bottom line," said Spencer Nilson, publisher of the Nilson Report, a newsletter. "Most merchants really make more money honoring bank cards."

American Express indicated it will retaliate, but wouldn't provide specifics. "This gives new meaning to the expression `frustration marketing,'" a spokesman said. He said that while the company charges higher fees, it "also delivers a customer that spends nearly three times as much as the customer who comes in and uses a bank card." American Express said average annual spending on its card is $3,000, compared with $1,050 for a bank credit card.
