Business Brief -- Dow Chemical Co.: Concern Plans to Sell Stake In Destec Energy Subsidiary

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/30/90

Dow Chemical Co. said it plans to sell as much as 27.7% of its Destec Energy Inc. subsidiary in an initial public offering for as much as $327.8 million. Dow Chemical, which intends to keep the remaining shares of Destec, said the offering will provide a valuation for Destec and give it access to capital markets as it seeks opportunities in the independent power generation industry, which sells power to large utilities. The Midland, Mich., company has estimated that Destec's sales will reach $500 million this year and $1 billion by 1995.

In a filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission, Destec said it will issue as many as 17,250,000 shares at a proposed maximum price of $19 a share. Goldman, Sachs & Co. is leading the underwriting group.

Dow Chemical formed Destec, based in Houston, in May 1989 by consolidating some of the parent's energy-related assets, including power-generating facilities not dedicated to Dow's own factories. Destec expanded last October through the $115 million acquisition of PSE Inc., a Houston-based designer and operator of energy-cogeneration facilities.
