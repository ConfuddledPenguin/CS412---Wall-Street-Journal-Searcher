Tanker With Double Hull Involved in Oil-Spill Crash

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/30/90

GALVESTON, Texas -- A new, double-hulled tanker and two oil-laden barges collided near here, spilling as much as 50,000 gallons of heavy black oil from the barges into Galveston Bay. One of the barges sank, the other was towed to safety. Both continued to leak, surrounded by oil-containment booms, the U.S. Coast Guard said. The Liberian-registered tanker Shinoussa, outbound with almost 10 million gallons of jet fuel, sustained hull damage and took on water. But the vessel was able to anchor safely and wasn't adding to the three-mile slick spreading from the accident scene, the Coast Guard said.

No one was injured, the Coast Guard said, adding that it is investigating the incident, which forced the closing of the busy Houston Ship Channel, scene of numerous oil spills, for most of the weekend. Late last night the channel remained closed.

The ship, built earlier this year in South Korea, has a design that will be required by such ships in all U.S. waters under new oil-spill legislation pending in Congress. Double-hulled tankers are said to be spill-resistant because they carry oil in an inner steel compartment separated from the outside hull.

The damaged barges were among three containing 2.6 million gallons of oil being moved into the ship channel by the tugboat Chandy N. The tug and barges are owned by a St. Louis-based towing company.
