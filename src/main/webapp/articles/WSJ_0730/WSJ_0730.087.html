Rep. Gonzalez Plans Probe On Treasury Use of Funds

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/30/90

WASHINGTON -- House Banking Committee Chairman Henry B. Gonzalez announced plans to investigate the Treasury Department's use of government funds for intervening in currency markets and for funding its Third World debt plan. The Texas Democrat said he plans a series of hearings starting Aug. 14.

Mr. Gonzalez, in a statement, said, "The American people have the right to know how their hard-earned tax dollars are being spent . . . yet the Treasury and the Federal Reserve apparently believe that Congress and the American taxpayer do not have the right to question certain aspects of their international activities." He said this "intolerable attitude toward accountability to the American people cannot be justified."

At issue is the $25 billion exchange stabilization fund that the Treasury uses to intervene in foreign currency markets. Mr. Gonzalez called it a "slush fund that lacks public scrutiny." He said his committee will investigate whether the Treasury Department should be banned from manipulating the foreign-exchange value of the dollar and that power be given exclusively to the Federal Reserve.

In addition, Mr. Gonzalez plans to look into a Treasury agreement to sell zero-coupon bonds to Mexico at below-market prices, in effect subsidizing the bonds with taxpayers' money.
