Business Brief -- Petrobras: Brazil's Oil Company Posts $466 Million Profit for Half

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/30/90

Petrobras, Brazil's government oil company, reported first-half profit of $466 million, despite a June loss of $49 million. Reynaldo Aloy, financial director, said the June deficit was due in part to government-set fuel prices, which have been frozen since President Fernando Collor de Mello took office in March. Petrobras President Luis Octavio da Motta Veiga said in June that the government needed to raise domestic fuel prices 10% to 15%.

The June deficit also resulted from the creation of a $114 million fund to protect against losses from the privatization of the Petromisa unit, Mr. Aloy said. Petromisa is one of several Petrobras units to be sold under a national campaign to cut government spending. Mr. Motta Veiga has said the company will lay off 16,000 of 80,000 workers at Petrobras and its units.
