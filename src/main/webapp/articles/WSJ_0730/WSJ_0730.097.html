Business Brief -- Franklin Electronic Publishers Inc.: Fiscal First-Quarter Loss Widened to $2.8 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/30/90

Franklin Electronic Publishers Inc., Mount Holly, N.J., said its net loss in its first quarter, ended June 30, more than doubled because of a "sluggish retail environment for electronics" and high inventories. The deficit at the maker of hand-held electronic reference products widened to $2.8 million, or 42 cents a share, from a year-earlier loss of $1.2 million, or 21 cents a share. Sales rose 12% to $12 million from $10.8 million.

Chairman Morton E. David said the company has reduced inventories by almost 40% to $17.6 million, compared with a year earlier. He also said the fiscal first quarter is the company's slowest period. "Inventories should be in line by the end of the September quarter," he said, and the company expects to return to profitability in its second half.
