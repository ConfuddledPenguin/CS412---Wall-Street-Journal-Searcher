Pinnacle West Takes Action To Help Utility

  • Author: David J. Jefferson
  • Date: 07/30/90

PHOENIX, Ariz. -- Pinnacle West Capital Corp. cut the quarterly dividends it takes from its Arizona Public Service unit to $42.5 million from $52.7 million, in an effort to help shore up the finances of the utility unit. The action comes one month after Pinnacle West, burdened by heavy debt, unveiled a plan to reduce sharply capital outlays and operating expenditures at Arizona Public Service. Pinnacle West has said it plans to pay about $600 million of debt by year-end 1993, with $200 million of that to be paid this year. At March 31, Pinnacle West had $1.13 billion of its own long-term debt; the consolidated total, including debt of the utility, was $3.53 billion.

"I think it was prudent to do it, particularly with the reduced earnings levels at APS," Richard Snell, Pinnacle West's chairman and chief executive officer, said of the dividend cut. "At some point, it allows APS to start reducing some of its own debt." Last year, the utility unit paid more to the parent company than it earned, "and it appears the situation would have been the same this year," a company spokesman added.

The dividend cut means that Pinnacle West will now receive only $170 million annually from Arizona Public Service instead of the previous $211 million a year. Still, the utility holding company insists the cut will have little impact on its debt repayment efforts. Pinnacle West plans to make up the difference with proceeds from the pending $38 million sale of its Malapai Resources Co. uranium mining unit, plus positive cash flow from its SunCor Development Co. and Eldorado Investment Co. units. Since March, Pinnacle West has paid down about $115 million of its debt.

"In developing a schedule for debt repayment, we anticipated lower dividends from APS," Mr. Snell said. "For that reason, we do not plan to reduce the rate of our debt repayments, nor will the dividend reduction impact our projections of increased equity in the company or our hope of restoring a dividend to Pinnacle West shareholders by year-end 1993." Pinnacle West suspended its 40-cent-a-share common stock dividend in October in the face of mounting losses from its MeraBank thrift unit, which was seized by federal regulators in January.

Separately, Pinnacle West's board voted to eliminate the annual $10,000 cash retainer paid to each board member. Instead, directors will receive options to buy 3,500 Pinnacle West common shares for $16.25 each, the market price as of July 2. The company said it expects to pay directors with stock options through mid-1993.

PacifiCorp, Portland, Ore., which has made an unsolicited $1.82 billion takeover offer for Pinnacle West, didn't have any comment on Pinnacle West's actions.
