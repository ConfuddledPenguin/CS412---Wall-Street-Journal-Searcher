Japan Urges Boost From Industries To Aid Gulf Force

  • Author: Urban C. Lehner
  • Date: 09/04/90

TOKYO -- The Japanese government called in the leaders of six major manufacturing industries and urged their "utmost cooperation" in supplying U.S. forces in Saudi Arabia. Cooperation would include adding production capacity to meet the needs of U.S. and other forces challenging Iraq, shipping the forces goods intended for other customers and billing the goods at cost, government officials said.

The Japanese government would foot the bill for the goods as part of the $1 billion in assistance to the multinational forces it pledged a week ago, officials said.

The Japanese government will purchase items in the name of U.S. forces, from foreign suppliers as well as Japanese. But the emphasis will be on Japanese suppliers, because the government can more easily persuade Japanese companies to bill at cost.

The industry leaders called in by the Ministry of International Trade and Industry were the heads of industry associations for construction equipment, industrial machinery, automobiles, iron and steel, electronics and plastics.

The government has been stung by international criticism that its contribution in the Persian Gulf crisis was too little, too late. While denying that there will be a second package of measures beyond those announced Wednesday, the officials tried to flesh out details of previously vague promises and explain points that may have been misunderstood overseas.
