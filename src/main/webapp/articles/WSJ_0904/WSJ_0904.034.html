Who's News: Oglebay Norton Co. Gives Green New Post

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/04/90

CLEVELAND -- Oglebay Norton Co. directors named R. Thomas Green Jr. executive vice president, making him the leading candidate to be the next president of the mining and lake-shipping concern. Mr. Green, 53 years old, joined the company in 1965 and has headed both the company's silica and iron ore mining businesses. His latest title was vice president, iron ore operations.

Oglebay's president and chief executive officer, Renold D. Thompson, is 64. Courtney Burton, chairman, is 77.

Oglebay's largest business is lake shipping. It owns 13 Great Lakes ships and has agreed to purchase two others. The company operates an iron ore mine and pellet processing plant at Eveleth, Minn., which it owns jointly with steel company partners. It produces silica for glass, paint and other uses; mines coal, and manufactures products for the iron and steel industry. In 1989, Oglebay earned $5 million, or $1.94 a share, on revenue of $165.3 million.
