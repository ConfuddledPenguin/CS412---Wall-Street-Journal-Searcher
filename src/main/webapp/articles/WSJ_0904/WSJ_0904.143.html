Alberta Natural To Take Control Of Joint Venture

  • Author: Gary Lamphier
  • Date: 09/04/90

CALGARY, Alberta -- Alberta Natural Gas Co. said it plans to secure control of its troubled magnesium production joint venture with Houston-based Magnesium International Corp. Norman Wagner, Alberta Natural's chairman and newly appointed chief executive officer, said the company expects to invest a further 50 million Canadian dollars (US$43.6 million) or so to win an unspecified majority interest in Magnesium Co. of Canada, the joint venture that operates the plant at High River, Alberta.

Mr. Wagner wouldn't say by how much Alberta Natural plans to raise its stake. But he said its effort to secure control of the venture, which has been hurt by construction and engineering snags since modest output began in February, shows "our confidence and our faith" in it. He predicted the plant could be profitable within a year.

The 12,500-metric-ton-a-year magnesium plant, originally conceived as phase one of a three-phase, C$370 million facility ultimately capable of producing 62,500 tons of magnesium annually, is currently producing "exceptionally high-quality product in an appropriate volume, but we are working our way through some start-up problems," Mr. Wagner said.

Magnesium, a silver-white metallic element, is mainly used as an alloy for producing aluminum.

He didn't specify how much of the plant's capacity is currently being used. It was previously expected to reach commercial production levels by the current quarter. "We'll let reality determine whether we have the capability here of developing full-scale production," he said.

Rumors have circulated that the resignation last month of Alberta Natural's former president and chief executive, Douglas Fenton, was tied to start-up problems at the magnesium plant. Mr. Wagner wouldn't comment. Mr. Fenton couldn't be reached.

The plant was built as part of an ongoing diversification strategy by Alberta Natural, whose current core businesses include natural gas pipelines, gas processing and specialty chemicals. The company is 49.2%-owned by Pacific Gas & Electric Co.'s gas pipeline unit, Pacific Gas Transmission Co.

Despite problems with its magnesium arm, Mr. Wagner said Alberta Natural still has "a very good chance" of matching last year's earnings, before special charges, of C$26.9 million, or C$1.26 a share. He didn't offer a specific forecast. Final net last year was C$23.6 million, or C$1.11 a share, on revenue of C$366.8 million.
