International Brief -- Swire Pacific Ltd.: Hong Kong Firm's Profit For First Half Fell 17%

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/04/90

Swire Pacific Ltd., a Hong Kong-based conglomerate, posted a 17% decline in first-half profit to 1.09 billion Hong Kong dollars (US$141.3 million) from HK$1.32 billion a year earlier, citing an expected drop in property section earnings in that operation's transition to a property investment concern from a developer. Declines of 17% also were posted in pershare earnings for Series A stock to 69.1 cents from 82.9 cents, and for Series B to 13.8 cents from 16.6 cents.

Revenue increased 5% to HK$14.03 billion from HK$13.4 billion.

Directors proposed an interim dividend of 23 cents a Series A share and 4.6 cents a Series B share, both unchanged.

Swire's shipping and industries division did well in the first half, but its aviation units, Cathay Pacific Airways and Hong Kong Aircraft Engineering, were hurt by rising operational costs. Cathay had a lower-than-expected 4% growth, and the engineering unit's profit fell 8%. A downturn in retail demand, especially in Taiwan, pared results of trading operations.
