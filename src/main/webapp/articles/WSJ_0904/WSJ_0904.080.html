Arguments Open in Trial Of Hong Kong's Ronald Li

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/04/90

HONG KONG -- Opening arguments began Monday in the closely watched corruption trial of former Hong Kong Stock Exchange chairman Ronald Li, which prosecutors expect to last six weeks. Mr. Li, 61 years old, pleaded innocent last week to two counts of accepting preferential offerings of shares in Cathay Pacific Airways Ltd., and Novel Enterprises Ltd. in 1986 and 1987, in exchange for helping to obtain listings for the companies' shares.

The former official, who is free on HK$12 million bail (US$1.6 million), faces a maximum penalty of seven years in prison and HK$500,000 in fines on each count.

Mr. Li and six other former bourse officials, including his son Alfred, will be jointly tried in February on six similar corruption charges. A string of other counts were dismissed in separate actions earlier this year.
