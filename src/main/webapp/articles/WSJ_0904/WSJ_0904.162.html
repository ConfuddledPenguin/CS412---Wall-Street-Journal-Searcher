What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/31/90

OIL PRICES ROSE on renewed worry that the Mideast crisis might not be resolved soon enough to prevent tight supplies. The price of refined petroleum rose faster than that of crude. Gasoline futures gained 4.35 cents a gallon, while crude jumped 85 cents a barrel to $26.77. ---

Stock and bond prices sank, driven down by oil's rebound. The Dow Jones industrials fell 39.11 points in thin trading to 2593.32, as program trading helped trigger a late sell-off. Long-term Treasury's lost about half a point. The dollar strengthened.

---

Tokyo stocks rose sharply as investors took in stride an increase in the Bank of Japan's discount rate. The Nikkei jumped 775.17 points, or 3.1%, on Thursday and gained a further 244.49 points early Friday.

Japan's discount-rate boost could sour the world economy by forcing higher rates elsewhere, analysts say.

---

Stock fund sales collapsed in August, posting their worst month since the 1987 crash, as many investors shifted into money market funds.

---

Airline reservation systems ground into gridlock Wednesday as a flood of callers tried to book cheap fares before the prices went up.

---

Mideast investors moved as much as $8 billion in capital to the West after Iraq invaded Kuwait. Big U.S. banks are the main beneficiaries.

---

The number of S&Ls described by regulators as well-capitalized and profitable fell 7% in the first quarter.

---

Hanson PLC plans to cut its stake in Newmont Mining to as low as 26% from 49% through a public offering of shares in the U.S. gold-mining firm.

---

Lockheed shareholder Harold Simmons continues to press for an increased stake in the company despite losing a proxy battle this year.

---

Factory orders rose 1.6% in July, nearly making up for a 1.9% drop in June. Aircraft orders were particularly strong, while autos were weak.

---

The Canadian and Hong Kong groups who agreed last month to buy Columbia S&L's $3 billion junk-bond portfolio may back out of the deal if the U.S. economy worsens further.

---

Campeau's Federated unit is challenging $586.8 million in IRS claims. Separately, National Bank of Canada is taking a $70.1 million provision against loans to Robert Campeau.

---

Four big California utilities will be able to profit from savings generated by their conservation efforts under a plan approved by state regulators.

---

The Labor Department launched a program to reduce arm and wrist injuries and other disorders that are common in repeated-motion jobs.

---

Korean officials proposed legislation aimed at reversing the Seoul stock market's 16-month slide. But prices sank on the announcement.

---

Gannett named Gary L. Watson, formerly head of the community newspapers division, to run the firm's newly consolidated newspaper group.
