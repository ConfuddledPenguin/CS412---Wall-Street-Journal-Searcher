Shearson Is Indicted In Ohio Case Alleging Broker Wrongdoing

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/04/90

COLUMBUS, Ohio -- In a case alleging stockbroker wrongdoing before the 1987 stock-market crash, a state grand jury issued 42 indictments each against Shearson Lehman Brothers Inc., a former top Shearson broker in Cleveland and the broker's former branch manager. The indictments charge the broker, Sheldon Strauss, with fraud and false representation in the sale of securities. The state said Mr. Strauss's clients suffered extensive losses in the 1987 crash because of "heavily margined, speculative and unauthorized investments" he made "with the full knowledge of his supervisors at Shearson Lehman."

The former branch manager, Stephen Weinberg, and Shearson were also named in the indictments for their alleged complicity in Mr. Strauss's actions, the state said.

Shearson, a New York-based brokerage concern, has already settled more than $4.9 million of claims with Mr. Strauss's former clients, the state said.

A spokesman for Shearson, a unit of New York-based American Express Co., said allegations that Shearson had complicity are "totally without merit," even though the alleged activity "didn't serve the best interest" of its clients.

The spokesman said the Messrs. Strauss and Weinberg no longer work for Shearson -- Shearson dismissed Mr. Strauss in late 1987 and Mr. Weinberg resigned in early 1988 -- and noted that the alleged activity occured nearly three years ago.

In magazine and newspaper interviews over the past several months, Mr. Strauss has acknowledged wiping out himself and his clients in the 1987 stock crash, but he said Shearson is as much to blame as him. He has said he wanted to "devote whatever meager resources I have" to help investors sue their brokerage firms in cases of improper sales techniques.
