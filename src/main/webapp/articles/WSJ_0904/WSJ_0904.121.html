International Brief -- Tokyu Department Store Co.: First Half Profit Up 14.7%; Firm Expects Competition

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/04/90

Tokyu Department Store Co. reported that net income in the first half, ended July 31, climbed 14.7% to 3.473 billion yen, or 12.65 yen a share, from 3.029 billion yen, or 12.01 yen a share. Reflecting Japan's strong consumer spending in the latest reporting term, overall sales registered 190.336 billion yen, showing a 21.4% increase from 156.816 billion yen a year earlier.

But the Tokyo-based company also is predicting some trying circumstances ahead, such as fierce competition to result from easing of the large-scale retail store regulation, which enables foreign department stores to make inroads to Japan.

For the full year, Tokyu forecasts that sales will reach 387 billion yen from 346.523 billion yen the previous year. Net income is estimated at around 5.7 billion, up from 5.189 billion yen in the prior fiscal year.
