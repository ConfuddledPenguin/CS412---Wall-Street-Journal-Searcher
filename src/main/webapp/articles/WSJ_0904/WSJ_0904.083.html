Axel Springer Profit Rose 7% in First Half

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 09/04/90

FRANKFURT -- Axel Springer Verlag said first-half net income rose 7% to 60 million Deutsche marks ($38.3 million). The Hamburg, West Germany, publisher didn't provide comparable figures for the year-earlier period. The company said it achieved the results despite its cooperative ventures in East Germany, which the company said demanded considerable expenses.

The company said it expects the positive earnings developments to continue in the second half. The company publishes West Germany's largest mass circulation tabloid newspaper, Bild.

Sales rose 14% to 1.67 billion marks, with all of the company's sectors contributing to the increase.

Newspapers sales rose 83 million marks to 369 million marks, while magazine sales climbed 34 million marks to 369 million marks.

Springer, which has about 11,800 employees, invested about 102 million marks in the first half.
