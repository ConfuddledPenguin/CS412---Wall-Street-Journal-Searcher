Marketing & Media: Men and Smart Magazines To Become Smart: for Men

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/04/90

NEW YORK -- Scratch one men's magazine. Men, a magazine that was supposed to launch in early 1991, will instead merge with Smart, a men's magazine published by New American Magazine Co. Smart, which is 18 months old, will carry a new, extended title -- Smart: for Men.

Peter Kaplan, the editor who had helped develop Men for Fairfield, Conn.-based Norris Publishing, will be the new editor in chief of Smart. The men's general-interest magazine recently lost its founding editor Terry McDonell when he was named editor of Hearst Corp.'s Esquire.

Owen Lipstein, chairman of New American Magazine Co., said Christopher Kimball, one of the founders of Norris Publishing, will become publishing director of New American Magazine Co. Besides Smart, Mr. Lipstein's New York-based company also publishes Mother Earth News and Psychology Today.

Mr. Lipstein wouldn't say what he paid to acquire Men; he did say that many of Mr. Kaplan's ideas for his magazine would be incorporated into Smart.
