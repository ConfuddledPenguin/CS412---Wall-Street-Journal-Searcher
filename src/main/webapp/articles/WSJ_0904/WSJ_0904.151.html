Who's News: AB Volvo's Holtback Resigns to Take Post With Swedish Bank

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/04/90

STOCKHOLM -- Roger Holtback, president of AB Volvo's car division since 1984, resigned abruptly to accept a senior executive post at Sweden's biggest bank, Skandinaviska Enskilda Banken. The move comes less than a week after Volvo reported plunging pretax profit for the third consecutive quarter and announced plans to slash roughly 15% of the jobs at its Swedish car plants by the end of next year.

Analysts said the departure of Mr. Holtback, 45 years old, reflected a top managment reorganization last year. At that time, Volvo tapped Stockholm banker Christer Zetterberg as its new chief operating officer and heir apparent to longtime chairman and chief executive officer Pehr Gyllenhammar.

The choice of Mr. Zetterberg was a big surprise as Mr. Gyllenhammar previously pledged his successor would be chosen from inside the company. Mr. Holtback was considered a leading contender for the post.

Other industry analysts suggested that the prospects of diminished autonomy for Volvo's car division under its planned strategic alliance with France's Renault probably hastened Mr. Holtback's exit. "It won't be exactly a junior partnership. But freedom of maneuver for Volvo on the car side will be much more constrained under the Renault alliance," said John Lawson, a London-based auto analyst for Nomura Securities Co.

Volvo said Lennart Jeansson, an executive vice president heading group financial operations, will succeed Mr. Holtback. Mr. Jeansson, 49, is a 24-year Volvo veteran who has spent much of his career at the car division. Most recently, he was the division's vice president for purchasing and materials from 1983 to 1986, before assuming control of group financial operations.

Mr. Jeansson also had strong backing within the company in last year's contest for the chief operating officer post. He has been a member of Volvo's executive committee since 1986.
