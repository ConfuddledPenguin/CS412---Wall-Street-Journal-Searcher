Business Brief -- Bank Building & Equipment Corp.: Most Assets Will Be Sold To First Financial Building

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/04/90

Bank Building & Equipment Corp. of America, St. Louis, which filed for protection under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code in May, said it would sell most of its assets to a new closely held company. Former Bank Building president and chief executive John T. Golitz has resigned to head the new company, First Financial Building Corp. in Arlington Heights, Ill.

Bank Building will sell the consulting, architectural design and construction contracts of its Financial Facilities and Financial Research Associates divisions and its Modular Buildings, Inc. subsidiary. The purchase price wasn't disclosed.

Various other Bank Building executives also will be joining the new company.

"The management, consulting, design, construction and marketing teams will remain intact at the new company," Mr. Golitz said, adding that the company would start with a staff of 88 people at four regional offices in St. Louis; Hartford, Conn.; Atlanta; and Clearwater, Fla. The company also expects to open a western divisional office in California.

Mr. Golitz was unavailable for further comment.
