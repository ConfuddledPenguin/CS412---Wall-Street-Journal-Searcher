California Grape Dealers Under U.S. Investigation

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/04/90

SACRAMENTO, Calif. -- There are grapes, and then there are grapes. And according to the U.S. government, grape dealers better know the differences among varieties.

To make its point, a U.S. attorney in California indicted Michael Licciardi of Stockton, Calif., for misrepresenting to two wineries at least 1,600 tons of "relatively common grapes" as expensive Zinfandel grapes. The indictment, which alleges 11 felony crimes, charges that about 300,000 gallons of wine was produced from these grapes with a value of about $6 million.

David F. Levi, a U.S. attorney, said the indictment was the second in a "continuing investigation . . . of fraud in the California wine industry." A similar indictment was filed last month against two Escalon, Calif., grape dealers for misrepresenting about 6,500 tons of grapes to California wineries.

Mr. Licciardi could not be reached for comment. If convicted of all charges, he faces up to 55 years in prison and as much as $2,750,000 in fines, according to the U.S. Justice Department.
