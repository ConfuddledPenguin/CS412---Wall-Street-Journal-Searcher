Vestron Inc. Reports Narrower Loss, Misses Debenture Payments

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/04/90

STAMFORD, Conn. -- Vestron Inc. reported a narrower loss for the second quarter and said it wasn't able to make interest payments due last Saturday on certain debentures. The video distributor and movie maker, which has agreed to be acquired by Live Entertainment Inc., reported its loss shrank to $12.4 million, or 33 cents a share, from $65 million, or $1.73 a share, in the year-earlier period. Revenue dropped 36% to $33.9 million from $52.7 million.

Vestron also reported that because of insufficient funds and other conditions in its credit facility with Chemical Banking Corp., its agent, it couldn't meet interest payments on its 9% convertible debentures due 2011.

The company said it doesn't expect to satisfy conditions that will allow it to make interest payments "in the foreseeable future." Vestron added that its banks may at any time issue a notice of default, which would prohibit interest payments for 120 days.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading Friday, Vestron closed at 31.25 cents, down 9.375 cents.
