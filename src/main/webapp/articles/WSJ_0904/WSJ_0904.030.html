Letters to the Editor: Needed: More Gas

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/04/90

Despite the encouraging opinion of S. Fred Singer (editorial page, Aug. 8) that the Strategic Petroleum Reserve's 600 million gallons is not as effective as private reserves of oil in a free market, he fails to consider that an increased supply of natural gas in the Northeast would be an important element affecting our energy dependency on OPEC oil. For example, Canadian gas from the Iroquois pipeline would equal the equivalent of 5% of the oil stored in the Strategic Petroleum Reserve. By displacing the energy equivalent of more than 30 million barrels of oil annually, the Iroquois pipeline would help offset the supply imbalance created by the embargo. Unfortunately, just one week before Iraq invaded Kuwait, the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission (FERC) voted, after four years of exhaustive hearings and overwhelmingly favorable evidence, to delay approval of the Iroquois pipeline in favor of additional hearings.

Clearly, FERC's decision could not have come at a worse time. The Iraqi crisis underscores the Northeast's critical need for reliable, secure supplies of energy that the pipeline will deliver. Regardless of whether the government sells oil from the Strategic Petroleum Reserve, the fact is, as long as we depend on imported oil, we will be hostage to hostile regimes and uncertain supplies.

William V. Federici

Corporate Communications

Brooklyn Union Gas

Brooklyn, N.Y.
