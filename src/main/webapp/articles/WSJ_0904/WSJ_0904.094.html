Business Brief -- Lone Star Technologies Inc.: All Preferred Held by U.S. In Thrift Unit Is Redeemed

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/04/90

Lone Star Technologies Inc., Dallas, said its American Federal Bank subsidiary redeemed all of its preferred stock held by federal regulators, giving Lone Star 100% control of the thrift. Lone Star, which also owns a troubled steelmaking company, said American Federal redeemed the $48 million Class A convertible preferred stock issue by canceling a $48 million note that regulators owed the thrift and by paying regulators $3.7 million. Cumulative accrued and unpaid dividends due regulators were canceled, resulting in income to the thrift of about $3.7 million.

The Federal Savings and Loan Insurance Corp. resolution fund purchased the preferred in 1988 as part of a $1.3 billion bailout that combined 12 insolvent thrifts into American Federal. Lone Star paid $48 million for all the common stock of the resulting institution.

A Lone Star spokeswoman said regulators also agreed to give American Federal "more flexibility" to pay dividends.
