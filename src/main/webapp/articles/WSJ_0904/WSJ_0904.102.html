Business Brief -- Outboard Marine Corp.: Concern Plans $33 Million Charge in Its 4th Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/04/90

Outboard Marine Corp., citing an "industrywide slump in marine products sales," plans to take a $33 million restructuring charge in the fourth quarter ending Sept. 30. That will bring the total of such charges in this fiscal year to about $47.5 million.

The actions involve closure of facilities, including three retail outlets. Outboard said it is also in the process of closing three U.S. boat manufacturing plants and an office facility, as well as an Australian outboard motor assembly plant.

The boat-manufacturing plants scheduled for closure will have their production consolidated into other plants, the company said, and Australian dealers will be provided with outboard motors made in the U.S. and other countries.

The Waukegan, Ill.-based company's nautical-fashion apparel, formerly sold through its retail outlets, will now be sold through boat dealerships and a catalog sales operation.

Included in the charges for the current quarter are $16 million in one-time charges and write-offs. Additional charges are under consideration, the company said.
