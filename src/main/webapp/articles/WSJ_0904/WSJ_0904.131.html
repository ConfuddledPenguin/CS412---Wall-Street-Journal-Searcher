International Brief -- ABN-Amro Holding N.V.: Newly Merged Banks Post Rise in Joint Net for Half

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/04/90

The two largest Dutch banks, Algemene Bank Nederland N.V., or ABN, and Amsterdam-Rotterdam Bank N.V., or Amro, which merged Aug. 24 to form ABN-Amro Holding N.V., reported that their joint net income for the first half rose 1.9% to 703 million guilders ($398.3 million) from 690 million guilders a year earlier. The profit was in line with analysts' expectations. Earnings per share fell 5.5% to 2.77 guilders from 2.93 guilders. As part of the merger, shares in ABN and Amro were exchanged for ABN-Amro Holding.

The two banks also declared an interim dividend of 1.40 guilders a share in ABN-Amro Holding. This compares with interim payouts in 1989 of 1.40 guilders from ABN and 2.60 guilders from Amro. ABN-Amro Holding shares were priced on par with ABN shares.

Income, including categories such as interest income and commissions, rose 4.7% to 4.93 billion guilders. Pre-tax operating profit slipped less than 1% to 1.55 billion guilders, but this was offset by a lower tax burden that was reduced to 234 million guilders from 272 million guilders.
