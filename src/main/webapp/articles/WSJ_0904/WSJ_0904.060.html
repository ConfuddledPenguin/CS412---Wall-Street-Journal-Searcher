Ford, Citing Soft Demand, To Idle 2 Plants for a Week

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/04/90

DEARBORN, Mich. -- Ford Motor Co., citing soft demand, said it will halt production for a week at its Kansas City, Mo., car plant and its medium and heavy-duty truck assembly facility in Louisville, Ky. A total of 4,200 workers will be laid off at the two plants during the week of Sept. 10, the auto maker said.

The Kansas City plant is building 1991 versions of the Ford Tempo and Mercury Topaz compact models. Dealers have a relatively lean 54 to 55 days of inventories of these cars. But Ford said dealers aren't ordering enough 1991 models to sustain the planned production, an indication that dealers are skittish about a possible sales slump this fall. The plant has about 2,300 hourly employees who build about 4,000 cars a week.

The truck plant, with about 1,900 hourly workers, produces about 1,100 vehicles a week, Ford said.
