What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/04/90

CHRYSLER PLANS TO EXTEND its cost-cutting efforts and raise the goal to $2.5 billion, $1 billion more than previously disclosed, Iacocca said in an interview. The auto maker needs the savings to cope with a surge in buyer-incentive expenses. The chairman also said the company is committed to maintaining its current common dividend and is looking for a European partner to cooperate on vehicle development and distribution. ---

PacifiCorp agreed to drop its pursuit of Pinnacle West Capital in exchange for a power swap with Pinnacle's Arizona utility unit in a pact potentially worth $260 million.

---

Boeing received $6 billion of firm orders and options from Asiana, a new South Korean airline. Separately, McDonnell Douglas got $2.5 billion in orders and options from airliner-leasing firm GPA Group.

---

World oil supplies will remain adequate until October, but tighter reserves are anticipated as winter approaches, a 21-nation group said.

Alaskan oil output will increase more than first predicted to help offset the losses of Mideast crude, the Energy Department said.

---

Stocks rose Friday in the slowest trading session of the year. The Dow Jones industrials gained 21.04 points to close at 2614.36. The bond market closed mostly lower in light activity.

---

Japanese regulators punished four firms, including a Citicorp unit, for alleged roles in a stock-rigging scheme. The move signals a tougher stance by Tokyo against trading abuses. Each firm will have to close an office briefly and pay a fine.

Tokyo stock prices declined sharply Monday on rumors of a new stock-manipulation investigation.

---

First Fidelity contracted with GM's EDS unit to consolidate the bank's software and data operations. The 10-year pact is valued at about $450 million and may set a pattern for other major banking companies.

---

Advanced Micro Devices is preparing to clone Intel's 80386 microprocessor. The move would break Intel's monopoly on the chip of choice for new personal computers.

---

Brazil and the IMF agreed to a new pact, although the exact amount of money the agency will lend the country hasn't been determined.

---

Shell Canada won the right to develop the Caroline gas field. The company's $719.2 million plan beat out a rival proposal by Husky Oil.

---

Business failures are up 11.7% this year, adding to worries that the U.S. is moving toward a recession.

---

Bristol-Myers agreed to buy a one-third stake in French nonprescription drug maker UPSA Group.

---

Average farm prices fell 1.3% in August from July, as a slump in grain prices more than offset record levels for beef cattle and milk.

---

The Tisch family disclosed it acquired stakes in BayBanks, Continental Bank and Equimark. Shares of the banking firms rose on the news.

---

Canada's gross domestic product fell 0.4% in the second quarter, reversing 7 1/2 years of growth and raising fears that a recession is at hand.

---

The purchasing managers' index due out today is expected to indicate again a weak economy. Friday's August unemployment report is anticipated to be unchanged from July.
