Who's News: Rockwell Completes Group's Realignment And Names Officials

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/04/90

EL SEGUNDO, Calif. -- Rockwell International Corp. said it completed the realignment of its defense-electronics group by combining several businesses and naming new executives to head two reorganized divisions. The realignment, which began in March, is designed to allow Rockwell to bid more effectively on Pentagon contracts and overseas work, the company has said. The latest moves affect about half of the defense-electronic's group's 16,000 employees. Some of them may move, but there aren't any job reductions planned at this time, a Rockwell spokesman said.

John D. Cosgrove, 55 years old, was named president of the newly created Collins Avionics and Communications division with about 5,000 employees in Cedar Rapids, Iowa. He was formerly president of a business that is part of the new division. Robert P. Marovich, 47, formerly vice president and general manager of another business in the division, was named its executive vice president.

Ronald A. Kodimer, 55, was named vice president and general manager of the newly created Command and Control Systems division in Richardson, Texas, a suburb near Dallas. The division employs about 2,400 people, including 600 in San Bernardino, Calif. Mr. Kodimer was executive vice president of another division as well as vice president and director of a business that is part of the new division. Edward J. Stephens, 62, was named executive vice president of the division. He had been a director of another division.
