Holder Group Blames Society for Savings

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/04/90

HARTFORD, Conn. -- A group including Heine Securities Corp. of Short Hills, N.J., and Michael F. Price said it is dissatisfied with what it described as the failure of Society for Savings Bancorp's management to enhance shareholder value. In a Securities and Exchange Commission filing, Heine said the management should actively seek to maximize shareholder value, including the possible sale or spinoff of Society's consumer finance business. William Kosturko, Society's executive vice president, declined to comment on the group's filing.

The group holds 645,339 Society shares, or a 5.4% stake, which was previously reported in an SEC filing as being held for investment reasons.
