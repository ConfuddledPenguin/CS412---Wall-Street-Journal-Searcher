International Brief -- N.V. Philips: Firm Said to End SRAM Chip Project to Cut Losses

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/04/90

N.V. Philips is expected to abandon one of its most cherished semiconductor projects in a bid to reduce losses. A senior executive familiar with the company confirmed Dutch news reports that Philips will stop making static random access memory chips, or SRAMs, after spending more than $200 million in the 1980s to develop the technology.

The Dutch electronics company also is expected to reduce its participation in the Joint European Submicron Silicon project, known as JESSI, under which major European companies are jointly developing semiconductor technology with subsidies from national governments and the European Community.

A Philips spokesman declined to comment.

The moves by Philips, one of the major pillars of technology research in Europe, would be a blow to European efforts to catch up with Japan and the U.S. in semiconductor technology and sales said Bill Coleman, an analyst at the London stockbrokerage James Capel & Co.

At the same time, the cost-cutting measures would demonstrate that Philips's new chairman, Jan Timmer, is willing to sacrifice some of the company's highest technological ambitions in an effort to improve financial results.

SRAMs, which retain their information even when the power is turned off, are memory chips used in telecommunications equipment, defense electronics and portable computers, among other products.
