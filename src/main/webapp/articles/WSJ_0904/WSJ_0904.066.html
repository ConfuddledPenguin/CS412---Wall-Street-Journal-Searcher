Marketing & Media: Gallup Survey: Moms Say `No Way' to Cloth Diapers

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/04/90

NEW YORK -- Mothers will recycle newspapers or car pool to help the environment, but ask them to do without disposable diapers and most say, "No way." That's according to a Gallup Organization survey of more than 800 mothers with children three years of age or younger. The survey was conducted at the request of the Diaper Manufacturers group of the American Paper Institute, a trade group.

The survey, conducted in June, found that only one in four mothers was willing to switch to cloth diapers to help improve the environment, though nearly three of four would recycle newspapers, and about 45% would car pool or build a backyard compost pile to dispose of trash.

Gallup said most mothers cited health reasons for using disposable diapers. Tops on their list were: Disposable diapers prevent leakage (85% of respondents), keeps baby's skin drier (83%) and helps prevent spread of germs and disease (62%).

The findings didn't go over well with the National Association of Diaper Services, proponents of cloth diapers. According to the association, the cloth-diaper business has been growing at an annual rate of 38.5%. "Somebody must be changing," said John Shiffert, the association's executive director.
