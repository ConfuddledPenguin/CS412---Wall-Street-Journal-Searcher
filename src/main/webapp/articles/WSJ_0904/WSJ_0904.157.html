Computer & Communications

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/04/90

SAN DIEGO -- Computer & Communications Technology Corp. said shareholders overwhelmingly approved its merger with closely held Sunward Technologies. Everett Bahre, chairman, president and chief executive officer of Computer & Communications, said 65% of the company's shares were represented in the voting. Some 99% of those shares were cast in favor of giving Sunward holders 55% majority control of Computer & Communications.

To accomplish that, Computer & Communications will issue 14.5 million new shares to Sunward holders. However, the merged company will adopt the Sunward name. Both companies make magnetic recording heads for computer disk drives and are based in San Diego.

Greg Reyes was elected chairman and chief executive officer of the new Sunward Technologies. Mr. Reyes said the new firm had completed the private sale of preferred stock and warrants to purchase about 3.8 million Sunward shares for an aggregate purchase price of $5 million.
