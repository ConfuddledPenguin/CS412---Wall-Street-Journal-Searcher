International: Japanese Vehicle Sales Rose by 12% in August

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/04/90

TOKYO -- Sales of cars, trucks and buses in Japan climbed 12% in August from a year earlier to 356,312 units, a record for the month, the Japan Automobile Dealers' Association said. Car sales rose 10% to 252,033 units. Sales of medium-size cars soared 46% to 25,207 units, maintaining a sharp upward trend that officials said has resulted in part from a growing preference among the Japanese for upscale models. Small-car sales showed a 7.1% gain from the previous year, totaling 226,826 units.

Truck sales rose 15% to 102,532 units, the highest on record for the month. Bus sales grew 18% from the previous year to 1,747 units.

Most car makers registered considerable growth in August sales. Toyota Motor Corp., Japan's biggest car maker, sold 141,872 cars, trucks and buses, up 20% from a year earlier. Sales by Nissan Motor Co., the second biggest manufacturer, rose 5.1% to 82,245 units. Mazda Motor Corp. reported sales of 31,019 units, an 11% gain. Sales by Mitsubishi Motors Corp. jumped 19% to 26,983 units.

On the other hand, Honda Motor Co. posted a 3.8% drop, with sales totaling 27,699 units. Honda officials said the decline was a result of brisk sales the previous year on the introduction of a popular new model.

Separately, Japan's exports of cars, trucks and buses rose 2.9% in July from a year earlier to 489,804 units because of growing car shipments, the Japan Automobile Manufacturers' Association reported.
