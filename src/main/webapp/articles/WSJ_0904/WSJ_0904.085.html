Xerox Unit Is Selling Auto Insurer to GM, Puts Another on Block

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/04/90

BASKING RIDGE, N.J. -- Crum & Forster Inc. said it intends to get out of the automobile insurance business, selling one unit to General Motors Corp. and putting another on the block. The troubled unit of Xerox Corp. said it wants to raise money so it can focus on its commercial insurance business. Xerox's new chief executive, Paul Allaire, has decreed that any such moves must be funded without help from the parent company's more-healthy units.

Crum & Forster said it signed a memorandum of understanding regarding the sale of its Navco Corp. subsidiary to Motors Insurance Corp., a unit of General Motors Acceptance Corp., GM's finance arm. Terms weren't disclosed, and the transaction is subject to definitive agreement and various approvals.

Navco offers auto policies tailored for specific groups and associations. MIC writes auto insurance for GM employees only, though it offers a range of other auto- and mortgage-related lines.

Crum & Forster said it also intends to sell its other auto insurance unit, Viking Insurance Co. of Wisconsin. Viking and Navco each had annual premiums of $147 million in 1989, the company said. Together, the units make up about 9% of Crum & Forster's business.

In April, Crum & Forster stopped writing all insurance lines, including auto policies, because the business was having losses and wouldn't be profitable anytime soon. A GMAC spokesman said he didn't know if Navco is profitable individually; a Crum & Forster spokeswoman didn't return calls.

Corrections & Amplifications
