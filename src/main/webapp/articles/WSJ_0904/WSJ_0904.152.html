Business Brief -- First Union Corp.: Banking Company to Sell 100 Offices for $116 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/04/90

First Union Corp. said it will sell 100 of its bank offices for about $116 million. The Charlotte, N.C., banking company said the offices -- in North Carolina, Georgia and Florida -- will be sold to an affiliate of Pitney Bowes Credit Corp., a unit of Pitney Bowes Inc. in Stamford, Conn. First Union said it will lease back the branches under a 20-year agreement.

First Union, which operates 779 bank offices in the Southeast, said the sale is expected to generate a gain of about $40 million, which will be recognized over the 20-year lease. In 1991, the company said, the sale will translate into net income of about two cents a share. In 1989, First Union earned $256.2 million, or $2.40 a share.
