Toyota Motor Raises Lexus LS 400 Price By 8.8% to $38,000

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/04/90

TORRANCE, Calif. -- Toyota Motor Corp.'s U.S. sales arm is raising the base price of its 1991-model Lexus LS 400 luxury sedan to $38,000, up 8.8% from the introductory price last fall. The increase is the second price rise for the LS 400 since it was introduced. In May, Toyota raised the sedan's price by $1,000 to $36,000. Toyota attributed the latest increase, which takes effect in the middle of this month, in part to new standard equipment.

The 1991 LS 400 will have a new feature that allows drivers to operate the power windows with the ignition turned off, new remote control door locks, and new equipment to ensure that the car will pass emission testing anywhere in the U.S., Toyota said.

Despite the price increase, the LS 400 remains less expensive than several of its European luxury car rivals, such as the Mercedes-Benz 300 E model. Late last month, Mercedes raised the 1991 300 E sedan's price to $47,200, up 2.7% from a year ago.

Toyota also increased the 1991 base price of its smaller and less-popular Lexus ES 250 model to $21,300, a 1.2% increase from the year-ago starting price of $21,050.

Separately, the U.S. sales arm of Hyundai Motor Corp. raised prices of its 1991 model cars as much as 7%.

The Korean auto maker boosted the price of its most expensive model, the V-6 powered Sonata with four-speed automatic transmission to $12,170, up 6.9% from a year ago. The Sonata model equipped with a standard five-speed manual transmission now starts at $10,700, up 7% from $9,999 a year earlier.

Hyundai increased the price of its cheapest subcompact Excel hatchback to $6,275, up 6.4% from $5,899 a year ago. The price of the four-door Excel model rose to $7,410, up 5.9% from $6,999 a year ago.

Hyundai said the price increases reflect in part new standard equipment such as power steering, seat-height adjusters, side molding, and body-color bumpers.
