Hedged-Investment, Founder Both Make Bankruptcy Filings

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/04/90

ENGLEWOOD, Colo. -- Hedged-Investments Associates Inc. and its founder, James D. Donahue, filed for protection from creditors under the federal Bankruptcy Code late Thursday. Hedged-Investments is managing general partner of Hedged Securities Associates Inc., a limited partnership. On Aug. 23, Mr. Donahue told 500 Hedged Securities investors, via videotape, that he had lost more than $100 million of their money through stock-option trading, primarily involving shares of UAL Corp. Attorneys for the firm say Hedged Securities has only about $2 million in assets remaining.

Hedged-Investments filed under Chapter 11 of the Bankruptcy Code, which gives businesses protection from creditors while they formulate a reorganization plan. Mr. Donahue filed under Chapter 13, which grants similar protection to individuals. Aeroturbine Energy Corp., another Hedged Investments subsidiary, also filed for Chapter 11 protection.

The Hedged-Investment filing listed more than 1,000 creditors but provided few details about the company's assets and liabilities. Lawyers for the firm said such information would be provided in future filings.

In a federal lawsuit filed last week, Weyerhaeuser Co. said its pension fund lost an estimated $35 million by investing in Hedged Securities. In separate suits filed in state district court here, Paradigm Partners Inc., Chicago said a pension fund it controls lost more than $15 million through similar investments. Union Texas Petroleum Holdings Inc., Houston, said its pension fund lost about $8 million.
