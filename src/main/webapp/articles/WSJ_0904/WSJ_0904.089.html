GTE Corp. Scraps Negotiations to Buy Unit of Thorn EMI

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/04/90

LONDON -- For the second time in a year, Britain's Thorn EMI PLC was jilted at the altar as talks to sell its big European lighting business to U.S.-based GTE Corp. ended in failure. A Thorn spokeswoman said GTE ended the three-month-old negotiations after being unable to reach agreement on sales terms. The Thorn division, valued by analysts at about #350 million ($671.3 million), is a struggling player in a tough market that Thorn was eager to exit. GTE had coveted the Thorn business, the world's fifth largest light-bulb maker, to expand its own, far-bigger world lighting business.

But the match didn't work out. Thorn declined to specify why the talks failed, but the spokeswoman cited several factors that may have cooled GTE's ardor. They included economic fears arising in the Middle East; GTE's efforts to digest its planned $6.2 billion purchase of U.S.-based Contel Corp.; and a continuing decline in the Thorn lighting business. The Thorn division's operating profit dropped 19% to #32.9 million in the year ended March 31, on revenue of #573.4 million. Since March, market conditions have worsened.

A GTE spokesman confirmed that the talks failed because of inability to agree on terms, but denied that GTE's decision was influenced by its Contel purchase. He added that the company is "committed to the global lighting business" and remains interested in other acquisitions or alliances. GTE's electrical products division, of which lighting is a part, posted operating income of $222 million on revenue of $2.2 billion in 1989.
