Martin Marietta Pays Out $2,565,377 to Settle Case

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/04/90

WASHINGTON -- Martin Marietta Corp. paid an additional $2,565,377 to settle civil charges that its Martin Marietta Electronics & Missiles Group overcharged the government for use of computer facilities, the Justice Department said. The company earlier had paid $898,000 to settle part of the case, which began when the company voluntarily disclosed the problem in September 1986. The Justice Department said an investigation by the Defense Criminal Investigative Service found that the Martin Marietta unit provided inflated cost data and as a result received more money than it should have.

The unit had disclosed that it overcharged the government for use of its Technical Computation Center, and has cooperated with the investigation, the Justice Department said. Since disclosing the problem, Martin Marietta has taken corrective actions, including dismissing employees who were found responsible, improving reporting and billing procedures, and increasing its training and internal audits, a company spokesman said.
