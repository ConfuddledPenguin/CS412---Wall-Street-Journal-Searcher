Marketing Brief -- Times Publishing Co.: Poynter Institute Reassures State on Bass-Pact Terms

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/04/90

Poynter Institute, the St. Petersburg, Fla., nonprofit foundation that owns a majority stake of Times Publishing Co., reassured Florida Attorney General Robert A. Butterworth that its finances can withstand Times Publishing's secret stock agreement with an investor group including Texas billionaire Robert M. Bass. Mr. Butterworth, in a letter to the institute last week, threatened to take the foundation to court to learn more about the Times-Bass agreement, which was ostensibly struck to ensure the publishing company's independence.

Mr. Butterworth, whom the Poynter Institute had previously asked for help in fending off Mr. Bass, wrote that he was concerned "that the fiscal integrity of a charitable educational institute is now in jeopardy."

In responding to Mr. Butterworth's query, Robert J. Haiman, the institute's president and managing director, said that as part of the stock agreement, Poynter is exchanging 300 shares of Times Publishing preferred stock for a $30 million subordinated debenture. The 13% debenture, due 2002, will yield more income than Poynter received from the preferred shares, he wrote.
