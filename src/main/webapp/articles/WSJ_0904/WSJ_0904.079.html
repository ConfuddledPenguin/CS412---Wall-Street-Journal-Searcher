Chrysler Joins GM to Offer Early Rebates on '91 Cars

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/04/90

HIGHLAND PARK, Mich. -- Chrysler Corp., following the lead of General Motors Corp. is offering rebates on most of its 1991 model cars and trucks before the model year begins. Chrysler said it will offer rebates of as much as $1,000 on most 1991 car and truck models, but not on its restyled minivans and certain other models.

GM said last month that it was offering discounts of as much as $1,250 on some of its 1991-model cars now arriving in showrooms. The 1991 model year begins Oct. 1.

Ford Motor Co. hasn't matched GM's and Chrysler's offers. But Ford is likely to follow suit, possibly as soon as today.

Chrysler officials said last month they wanted to reduce their reliance on rebates as a marketing tool. But with the outlook for fall sales already uncertain in the face of a softening U.S. economy, Chrysler had little choice but to match GM's offers. The No. 3 auto maker's share of the car market fell sharply in the first seven months of this year to 8.8% from 10.4%.

Chrysler said it's offering $500 rebates on 19 models, including the Chrysler Imperial, Plymouth Acclaim and Sundance, Dodge Daytona and Spirit and Jeep Comanche. Chrysler set rebates of $1,000 on its Dodge Ramcharger and Jeep Grand Wagoneer.
