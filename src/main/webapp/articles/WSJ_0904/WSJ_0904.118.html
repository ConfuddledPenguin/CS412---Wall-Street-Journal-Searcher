Business Brief -- Pan Am Corp.: Carrier Will Cut Flights, Lease Planes to Lufthansa

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/04/90

Pan American World Airways said it agreed to phase out some of its Berlin flights and provide planes for Lufthansa German Airlines' planned Berlin operations. Lufthansa has applied for permission to serve Berlin beginning in October. Under its "agreement in principle" with Pan Am, a unit of Pan Am Corp., Lufthansa will pay Pan Am $150 million to lease at least nine Pan Am Boeing Co. 727 aircraft, with pilots.

Pan Am, which currently operates 74 daily flights between Berlin and the German cities of Hamburg, Nuremburg, Frankfurt, Stuttgart, Dusseldorf and Munich, will terminate those services in late October. The agreement does not affect Pan Am's non-stop service to Berlin from New York or the operations of Pan Am Express, its regional airline subsidiary, which operates 34 daily flights to 10 European cities and Berlin.

Corrections & Amplifications

PAN AMERICAN WORLD AIRWAYS expects to receive $150 million from Lufthansa German Airlines for certain services and facilities, including the transfer of gate space. Tuesday's edition incorrectly described what Pan American was selling.
