New Securities Issues

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/01/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATES

Consolidated Rail Corp. -- $250 million issue of 9 3/4% notes, due June 1, 2000, priced at 99.775 to yield 9.785%. The noncallable notes were priced at a spread of 115 basis points above the Treasury's 10-year note. The size of the issue was increased from an originally planned $200 million. Rated single-A-3 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and single-A by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Morgan Stanley & Co.

Westinghouse Credit Corp. -- $150 million issue of senior notes, due June 15, 2014, priced late Wednesday at par to yield 8.85%. The noncallable notes were priced at a spread of 27 basis points above the Treasury's four-year note. The notes may be put back to the company in the fourth, ninth and 14th years. Rated single-A-2 by Moody's and single-A by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets.

MUNICIPALS

New Jersey -- $50.9 million certificates of participation, due Dec. 15, and June 15, 1990-1997, apparently won by a Dillon Read & Co. group. The certificates, rated single-A-1 by Moody's and double-A by S&P, were priced to yield from 5.90% for bonds due Dec. 15, 1990 to 6.65% for bonds due June 15, 1997. Bonds maturing in 1991 through 1996 mature on June 15 and Dec. 15 of each year.

MORTGAGE-BACKED

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $300 million of Remic mortgage securities is being offered in 22 classes by Donaldson Lufkin & Jenrette Securities Corp. The offering, Series 1990-73, is backed by 9% Fannie Mae securities, with a weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29.5 years.

EUROBONDS

McDonald's Restaurants of Canada Ltd. (U.S. parent) -- 100 million Australian dollars of 15% Eurobonds due June 28, 1995, priced at 101.65, via Deutsche Bank Capital Markets Ltd. Fees 2. Guaranteed by McDonald's Corp.

Credit d'Equipment de Petites et Moyennes Enterprises (France) -- One billion French francs of 10% Eurobonds due June 28, 1995, priced at 101.60, via lead manager Credit Commercial de France. Guaranteed by the Republic of France.

State Bank of South Australia (Australia) -- 100 million Australian dollars of 5% Eurobonds due June 28, 2000, priced at 102, via Hambros Bank Ltd. Guaranteed by the State of South Australia.

ESOP Finance Trust (Special-purpose vehicle) -- $173.8 million of 9.35% Eurobonds due June 27, 1995, priced at 100.25, via lead manager Goldman Sachs International Ltd. Guaranteed by Exxon Corp., and backed by loans under company's employee stock ownership plan.

Monte Dei Paschi Di Siena, London (Italy) -- 10 billion yen of 6.9% depositary notes due September 27, 1993, priced at 100 7/8, via lead manager Yamaichi International Ltd.

Nippon Credit Bank Curacao Finance NV (Netherlands) $130 million 10% Eurobond, priced at 102 and due July 5, 2000, via lead manager Nippon Credit International Ltd. Call July 5, 1993. Guaranteed by Nippon Credit Bank.

---

Corrections & Amplifications

ESOP FINANCE TRUST's 9.35% Eurobonds due June 27, 1995, are not guaranteed by Exxon Corp. In Friday's list of New Securities Issues, it was incorrectly stated that the bonds are guaranteed by Exxon.

(WSJ June 4, 1990)
