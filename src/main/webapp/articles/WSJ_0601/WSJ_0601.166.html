Marketing Brief -- Carolco Pictures Inc.: Tokyo's Pioneer Electronic To Acquire a Stake of 10%

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/01/90

Carolco Pictures Inc. said a unit of Pioneer Electronic Corp. of Tokyo agreed to buy a 10% stake in the Los Angeles movie company and appoint two members to its board. Pioneer LDCA will pay $60 million for a new Series C convertible preferred stock that will pay a 5% yearly dividend until redeemed. The stock will be convertible into 3.24 million shares of Carolco common stock at $18.50 each, a considerable premium to Carolco's close at $13 in New York Stock Exchange composite trading.

Pioneer LDCA also will buy a warrant entitling it to buy 300,000 Carolco common shares for $18.50 each. The agreement grants the unit the right of first refusal to world-wide rights to Carolco titles on laser disk format.

Peter Hoffman, president and chief executive officer, said the transaction will help pay down some of the debt incurred by Mario Kassar, Carolco's chairman, when he bought out his partner, Andrew Vajna, last fall.

Carolco also said it signed an agreement with another Pioneer unit, under which it will license all Japanese rights to several new Carolco movies including "Terminator II" and "The Doors."
