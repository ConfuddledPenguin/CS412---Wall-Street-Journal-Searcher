OTC Focus: Market Increases Slightly as Investors Await Unemployment Data; Tyco Up 2 3/4

  • Author: Anne Newman
  • Date: 06/01/90

The over-the-counter market stepped timidly toward today's employment figures. The Nasdaq Composite Index edged up 0.20 to 458.97, or 0.04%, as advancing stocks narrowly led decliners, 979 to 941. Volume slowed to 157.3 million shares from 169.4 million Wednesday.

"I imagine some people are sitting back and waiting to see what the unemployment data is," said William Rothe, managing director of OTC trading at Alex. Brown & Sons, Baltimore. The Labor Department will release data on jobs growth and unemployment before trading begins this morning.

"The market seems to be getting tired," Mr. Rothe added, suggesting that OTC stocks might be due for a rest. However, Mr. Rothe said that he thinks there remain a number of money managers on the sidelines with cash to invest in stocks.

One of the OTC market's more bearish market professional shares that opinion. Walter Carucci, vice president of Carr Securities, said, "There's still so much cash on sidelines, you could almost create a bullish scenario if you want to." Mr. Carucci said that last week he began to see buying interest in smaller issues across-the-board "for the first time in a long time," adding that "our volume has picked up quite substantially." However, pointing to such economic troubles as the savings and loan crisis, Mr. Carucci said, "I'm still very bearish, but would like to be proven wrong."

Traders said they were kept busy much of the day by a new issue of Mr. Coffee and a secondary offering of Urcarco.

The most active stock was Mr. Coffee, which sold an initial public offering of 4 million shares at $10 each. Trading under the stock symbol JAVA, more than 4.2 million shares of the Bedford Heights, Ohio, coffeemaker company said changed hands and closed unchanged at 11 1/8. Proceeds from the offering will be used to reduce debt incurred by Mr. Coffee in a leveraged buy-out.

Second on the most-active list was Urcarco, up 3/4 at 20 5/8. A secondary offering of 5 million shares of the Fort Worth, Texas, retailer of used cars and trucks was priced at $19.875. Urcarco went public last November at $11 a share.

Tyco Toys gained 2 3/4 to 27 3/8 on more than 18 times average volume. The Mt. Laurel, N.J., toy maker said late Wednesday it received an unsolicited acquisition offer. Tyco Toys, which didn't identify the possible buyer, said the two parties have had discussions but gave no assurance that an agreement will be reached.

Dow Jones Professional Investor Report said that Hasbro is rumored to be the suitor among Wall Street analysts and arbitragers. Hasbro Chief Financial Officer John O'Neill declined to comment.

SynOptics Communications added 6 1/4 to 45 1/4 after the Mountain View, Calif., maker of computer network systems said late Wednesday that it expects net income for the second quarter ending June 29 will be about 35% higher than first-quarter net income of 43 cents a share. SynOptics earned 26 cents a share in the year-ago second quarter. However, the company added that although product demand is strong, it doesn't expect to repeat that growth rate in future quarters.

Seagate Technology rose 3/4 to 15 5/8. The Scotts Valley, Calif., disk-drive maker unveiled its second-generation disk drive for small computers, such as laptop computers. Shares of Maxtor, a San Jose, Calif., disk-drive maker, rose 3/8 to 16 3/8 on volume of 1.4 million.

Software Toolworks jumped 1 5/8 to 22 3/4 on nearly 1.7 million shares after the Chatsworth, Calif., software developer reported net income for the fourth quarter ended March 31 of 12 cents a share, including extraordinary items, up from five cents a year earlier. The company also said that it signed a letter of intent with Canon Inc. to develop a software program for use in an IBM-compatible Canon product to be released later this year.

Sequent Computer Systems rose 1 1/4 to 31 3/4 and Pyramid Technology dropped 2 1/4 to 32 3/4 following comments by Goldman Sachs computer analyst John C. Levinson, according to DJPIR. Mr. Levinson concluded that American Telephone & Telegraph might choose the Beaverton, Ore.-based Sequent, rather than Pyramid, of Mountain View, Calif., to provide a multiprocessing element for a new version of AT&T's Unix system, which is slated for release late next year, DJPIR said. Mr. Levinson wasn't available for comment, but a Goldman employee confirmed the analyst repeated a long-standing "buy" opinion on Sequent and removed a "trading buy" opinion on Pyramid and now rates it a near-term "hold."

A Sequent official called Mr. Levinson's report "speculation" and declined to comment on AT&T's intentions, DJPIR said. AT&T and Pyramid officials weren't available for comment.

Occupational Urgent Care Health Systems gained 1 1/4 to 24 1/2. David A. Edelstein, chief financial officer of the Sacramento, Calif., provider of cost containment and other health care services, told DJPIR there weren't corporate developments to explain the move. However, the company's chairman, James W. Cameron, was scheduled to appear last night at an informal meeting with portfolio managers arranged by Hambrecht & Quist, DJPIR reported. Occupational Urgent Care also is one of several companies scheduled to appear June 4 at a Morgan Stanley conference on growth stocks.

Life Technologies fell 3/4 to 15 3/4. Late Wednesday, the Gaithersburg, Md., developer of life-science products said it might sell all or part of its diagnostic operations and plans to cut its participation in the molecular diagnostics business.

Chips & Technologies lost 1 1/4 to 19 after the San Jose, Calif., maker of microcomputer chips said it settled a trade secrets lawsuit with Elite Microelectronics Inc. The agreement doesn't involve any payments, but the companies agreed that a jointly appointed inspector will periodically review Elite's operations.

NESB tumbled 7/8 to 1 1/2 after the New London, Conn., thrift said that holders of its $50 million long-term senior notes due June 1, 1995, are seeking immediate repayment following negotiations to amend the terms of the note agreements. The thrift said that it's willing to work out an acceptable agreement, but is uncertain whether negotiations will resume.

Washington Scientific Industries fell 1/2 to 6 5/8. The Minneapolis maker of precision equipment said late Wednesday that it expects to report a loss for the third quarter ending June 3 of about 15 cents a share, compared with restated net income from continuing operations of 18 cents a share a year earlier.
