Big Board Seeks to Tighten Rules on Members' Capital

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/01/90

NEW YORK -- In the wake of Drexel Burnham Lambert Inc.'s collapse, the New York Stock Exchange is tightening up its rules regarding member firms' and floor traders' capital. The Big Board has proposed a rule requiring written notification within 24 hours of any transaction between a broker-dealer and its parent that reduces broker capital by 20% or more.

When Drexel collapsed in February, it was disclosed that its parent company, Drexel Burnham Lambert Group Inc., had been raiding the broker-dealer's capital to stay afloat after the parent became unable to get short-term financing. The Drexel situation disturbed regulators.

The Big Board now also wants its specialists, or dealers on the floor of the exchange who are assigned to make markets in individual stocks, to notify the exchange in writing within 24 hours when their net liquid assets drop by 20% or more.

The rule changes have been filed for approval with the Securities and Exchange Commission.
