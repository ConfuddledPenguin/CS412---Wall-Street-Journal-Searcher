Who's News: Delorme Is Named Chairman of Quebec's Pension Fund Agency

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/01/90

QUEBEC -- The Quebec government, as expected, named Jean-Claude Delorme chairman and general manager of Caisse de Depot et Placement, the provincial government's powerful pension fund agency. Mr. Delorme, 56 years old, is president and chief executive officer of Teleglobe Canada Inc., a unit of Memotec Data Inc., a Montreal telecommunications and communications equipment concern.

Mr. Delorme was appointed to a 10-year term, effective July 1. He succeeds Jean Campeau, 58, chairman and general manager since 1980. Montreal-based Caisse de Depot manages 34.2 billion Canadian dollars (US$29.1 billion) in assets and is one of the biggest players in Canadian financial markets.

The government also named Guy Savard, a Sherbrooke, Quebec, accountant, as a director of Caisse de Depot, filling a vacancy on the board. The government said it intends later this year to create the new positions of president and chief operating officer of Caisse de Depot. A government spokeswoman said Mr. Savard "would be an interesting candidate" for the new posts.
