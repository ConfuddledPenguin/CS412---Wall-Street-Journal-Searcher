Initial Jobless Benefits Claims

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/01/90

WASHINGTON -- Initial claims for regular state unemployment benefits fell to a seasonally adjusted 343,000 in the week ended May 19, from 353,000 the previous week, the Labor Department said. The number of people receiving regular state benefits in the week ended May 12 rose to a seasonally adjusted 2,434,000, or 2.3% of those covered by unemployment insurance, from 2,379,000 the previous week, when the insured unemployment rate was also 2.3%.

Counting all state and federal benefit programs, the number of people receiving unemployment benefits in the week ended May 12 rose to 2,336,500, from 2,328,600 a week earlier. These figures aren't seasonally adjusted.
