AT&T Introduces Device for Messages Via Rotary Phones

  • Author: Robert J. Brennan
  • Date: 06/01/90

NEW YORK -- American Telephone & Telegraph Co. says it's introducing the first answering machines that respond to rotary-dial phones, making it easier for people to call home or office to get their messages. Owners previously were limited to using touch-tone phones or beepers when calling home for messages. The new answering machines now have a human-speech synthesizer that responds to a voice command to retrieve messages, getting around the touch-tone problem.

The machines also are the first to store as many as four different outgoing messages to callers, the company said. Two models of the answering machine have two lines, permitting one caller to leave a message while another is given a signal to wait and then leave a message. AT&T said the two-line models are designed for the growing number of households, home offices and small businesses with two telephone numbers.

AT&T is introducing the machines at the Consumer Electronics Show, which opens tomorrow in Chicago. Among other products, the telecommunications giant is introducing a two-line cordless telephone and a caller identification unit, which can be used with caller identification services provided by local phone companies. Users who have the local service can use the Call Display 64 to view the numbers of incoming calls. The unit stores the numbers and times of the most recent 64 calls, whether or not they were answered.

Separately, AT&T Capital Corp. said it completed its acquisition of Encore International Inc., a computer leasing concern. Encore will operate under the name AT&T Systems Leasing Corp., and current management will remain intact in its Bloomfield Hills, Mich., headquarters, AT&T Capital said.

AT&T earlier this year divided its AT&T Credit Corp. subsidiary into five business units and renamed it AT&T Capital. The unit offers equipment leasing and financing services for AT&T and non-AT&T equipment.
