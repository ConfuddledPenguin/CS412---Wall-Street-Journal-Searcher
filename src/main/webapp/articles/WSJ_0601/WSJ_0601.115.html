U.S. Agency Examines Safety of Rear Seats In Chrysler Minivans

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/01/90

WASHINGTON -- The National Highway Traffic Safety Administration is investigating complaints that rear seats in 1986-89 Chrysler minivans may collapse and eject passengers during accidents. The agency on May 7 began a preliminary evaluation, its lowest-level investigation, of the vans after receiving six complaints alleging that the seats had failed in three accidents, resulting in four injuries and the death of a two-year-old Pennsylvania boy.

The investigation, which was made public by the Center for Auto Safety and confirmed by the NHTSA, covers Dodge Caravans and Plymouth Voyagers. The Center for Auto Safety, a consumer group, said that one million minivans were covered by the inquiry, but NHTSA couldn't confirm the number.

According to the Center for Auto Safety, the two-year boy in Washington County, Pa., was killed when the seat in his mother's 1987 Plymouth Voyager collapsed backward in an accident. The child reportedly was thrown from the van into a creek 35 feet away, where he died of hypothermia.

A Chrysler spokesman said the auto maker is aware of the investigation. "We don't have a problem with our seats, which comply with federal standards. And we are cooperating with NHTSA in its investigation," the spokesman said.
