Letters to the Editor: Bigotry by Implication

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/01/90

Julie Salamon's review of the movie "Longtime Companion" (Leisure & Arts, May 10), while positive in general overview, harbored underlying prejudices that I found disturbing. The implication that homosexuals are other than familiar and likable, and that they live on the fringe, serves only to emphasize the point that society seeks to isolate those largely affected by acquired immune deficiency syndrome. It is precisely this bigoted attitude that promotes superficial judgment and obliterates one of the major health concerns in this country. It is unfortunate that more deliberate attention will not be made toward conquering this fatal disease until it either begins to ravage the heterosexual population or until heterosexuals begin to accept the fact that homosexuals do indeed lead respectable, middle-class lives, no different from their own.

Suzanne Ridge

Boston
