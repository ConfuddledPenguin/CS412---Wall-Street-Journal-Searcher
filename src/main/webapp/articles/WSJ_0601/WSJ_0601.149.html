What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Date: 06/01/90

CHRYSLER NAMED two men to take over the duties of Vice Chairman Gerald Greenwald, who resigned Wednesday to lead a proposed employee buy-out of UAL. The two executives, Robert Miller and Robert Lutz, now appear to be the leading candidates to eventually succeed Chairman Iacocca. ---

Nynex was indicted on a criminal contempt charge of providing information services through a unit, a violation of the 1982 consent decree that broke up the Bell system. It is the first such case against a Bell firm.

---

BankAmerica won the bidding contest to take over insolvent Western S&L, Arizona's second-largest thrift. BankAmerica will pay $81 million, one of the larger premiums federal regulators have received in liquidating their inventory of troubled thrifts.

---

Factory orders plunged 2.3% in April, another sign that manufacturing remains weak. The decline, led by a 4.3% drop in durable-goods orders, follows two months of increases.

---

Boeing received an order from China for 33 commercial jets worth $2 billion with options for 36 more. It is Boeing's second big order in a week.

---

Coca-Cola is scrapping the much-hyped MagiCan promotion after only three weeks. The $100 million campaign, the biggest in Coke's history, was hurt by malfunctioning cans and a barrage of negative publicity.

---

Capital Cities/ABC announced a plan to limit the audience guarantees it usually promises advertisers.

---

---

Commonwealth Edison was ordered to refund over $300 million from a rate increase designed to pay for three nuclear plants. The Chicago utility also has to roll back rates.

---

Stock prices were mixed as investors took profits in blue-chips and then bought other stocks. The Dow Jones industrials closed off 1.90, at 2876.66. Bond prices and the dollar gained.

Oil prices plunged following a report of higher U.S. petroleum inventories. U.S. gasoline prices, which led the recent rally, took the sharpest fall.

---

Corporate buy-backs of junk bonds hit a record $2.5 billion in the first quarter and are expected to set highs for the year. Traders say the trend helped spur the market's modest rally.

---

Economic leaders from major industrial nations committed themselves to reaching a multilateral trade accord this year. But the officials remained divided on cutting farm subsidies.

---

The Bank of Credit & Commerce plans to close most U.S. operations. The Luxembourg bank is the focus of two new investigations and is under growing pressure from the Fed.

---

Saatchi & Saatchi's long-awaited sale of its Hay Group consulting unit finally appears imminent, though at a price far below what the firm wanted.

---

Westinghouse plans to buy back up to 7% of its stock. Directors also voted to exempt the company from Pennsylvania's new anti-takeover law.

---

Agriculture prices rose 2% in May as strong demand for pork and tight supplies sent hog prices soaring to their second-highest level ever.
