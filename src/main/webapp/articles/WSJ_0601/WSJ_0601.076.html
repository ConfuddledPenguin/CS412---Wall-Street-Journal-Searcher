Halsey Taylor Co. To Replace Certain Contaminated Coolers

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/01/90

WASHINGTON -- Halsey Taylor Co. agreed to replace certain water coolers that had lead-contaminated tanks, but only if the owners provide the company with test results from a qualified laboratory. The Consumer Product Safety Commission, which negotiated the consent agreement with the company, said the tests can cost as much as $30.

In January, the Environmental Protection Agency said six models of Halsey Taylor coolers made before 1979 may have lead-lined tanks that could affect the drinking water in them. Ingestion of lead can cause severe health problems, particularly for children. Children who ingest lead can suffer mental retardation and growth impairment. Lead also damages fetuses when it's ingested by pregnant women.

Vernon Hills, Ill.-based Halsey Taylor, a major manufacturer of water coolers, is a division of Scotsman Industries Inc. In agreeing to the order, it didn't acknowledge that its coolers were contaminated.

Under the CPSC program, customers may choose to be repaid a portion of the cost of the machine, based on its age.

Under its agreement with the consumer agency, Halsey Taylor will notify all daycare centers with more than 10 children, as well as public and private schools, colleges, and boys and girls clubs of its replacement program. Water cooler owners who wish to participate in the program can get further information by calling a toll-free number, (800) 635-2358.
