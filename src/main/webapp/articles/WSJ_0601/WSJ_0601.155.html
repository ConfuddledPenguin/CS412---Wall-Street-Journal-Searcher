Child World Posts Sharply Wider Losses For Fiscal 1st Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/01/90

AVON, Mass. -- Child World Inc., citing a post-Christmas "out-of-stock" inventory position, reported sharply larger losses for its fiscal first quarter ended May 5. In the latest period, the operator of toy supermarkets reported a loss of $7.4 million, or 65 cents a share, compared with a year-ago loss of $5.1 million, or 45 cents a share.

Sales in the latest period dropped slightly to $130.1 million from $131.7 million the year earlier. Comparable-stores sales dropped 7% in the latest quarter.

The company said it improved its inventory "in-stock" levels and had a same-store sales gain in April.

Recently, the company announced that it had retained Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette Securities Corp., S.G. Warburg & Co. and Argosy Group "to explore its financial and strategic alternatives including the possible sale of the company."

Previously, it was reported that CNC Holding Corp., owner of about 82% of the company's stock, and Child World were considering the sale of all or part of CNC's interest in Child World. CNC Holding is a private investment group in Cleveland.

Yesterday, in national over-the-counter trading, Child World common closed at $10.375, off 62.5 cents.
