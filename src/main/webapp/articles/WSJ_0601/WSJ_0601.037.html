Japan's Steel Exports Decline

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/01/90

TOKYO -- Japan's steel exports in April dropped 23% to 1.4 million metric tons from a year earlier, the Japan Iron and Steel Federation said. Shipments to all major markets showed substantial year-to-year declines, except for those to the U.S., which fell a modest 1% to 287,000 metric tons. A metric ton is equal to about 2,204.62 pounds. Total exports in April, mostly based on contracts signed last December, were down 7% from the previous month. The exports have declined from year-earlier levels in every month but one since August 1986.

With Japan's economy in its 42nd consecutive month of expansion, steelmakers have been preoccupied with meeting strong demand from domestic industries, such as auto making, shipbuilding and construction.

April's exports were relatively unaffected by the weakness of the yen since the beginning of the year. Lower dollar-denominated prices of Japanese steel may push up exports to the U.S. and Southeast Asian countries, but the gains are likely to be offset by continuing declines in shipments to the Soviet Union and China.
