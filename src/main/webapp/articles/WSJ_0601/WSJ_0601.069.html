International: LVMH Makes Offer to Buy 6.8% More of Guinness

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 06/01/90

LONDON -- LVMH Moet Hennessy Louis Vuitton, a French spirits and luxury products group, said it is offering to buy an additional 6.8% of Guinness PLC through a tender offer. The company said it is offering to pay as much as 825 pence ($13.99) a share to acquire as much as 59.4 million shares in Guinness, a British spirits group. The purchases would lift LVMH's holdings in Guinness to 24.1%.

LVMH's current holdings include 42.8 million shares, which the company acquired yesterday. Guinness shares are currently trading at 762 pence, up from 742 at Wednesday's close.

The intended enlarged holding would draw LVMH's stake in Guinness roughly even with the 24.1% fully diluted stake Guinness holds in the French group. The two companies agreed in 1988 to allow each to hold equivalent stakes in the other.

If LVMH receives more than the required shares under the tender, it said it will drop its offer price to the lowest price at which the full number of shares can be acquired.

Should the tender fail to attract enough shares to lift its stake to the target level, LVMH said it will buy additional shares in the market through U.K. brokers S.G. Warburg & Co.
