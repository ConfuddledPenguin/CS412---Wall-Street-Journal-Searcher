Technology: Slate Signs Up Frankston; Software Creators Reunite

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/01/90

SCOTTSDALE, Ariz. -- Slate Corp., a software start-up concern, said it hired software developer Robert Frankston from Lotus Development Corp., in an action that reunites the creators of VisiCalc, one of the first successful personal-computer software programs. At Slate, Mr. Frankston will be working again with Daniel Bricklin, the co-developer of VisiCalc. The two started Software Arts in Mr. Frankston's house in Arlington, Mass., in 1979, and developed VisiCalc, the first spreadsheet. The program helped get business people to start buying Apple Computer Inc. personal computers, expanding the market beyond hobbyists. It was later superseded by Lotus Development's spreadsheet, 1-2-3.

Slate is developing "pen-based software applications" for a new class of computers that let users enter data by writing on a screen with a stylus, rather than typing with a keyboard. People in the industry say Slate is working on software for note-pad computers that GO Corp., based in Foster City, Calif., is expected to introduce later this year.

Mr. Frankston, 40 years old, had been working on network computer software at Lotus. He said his title at Slate hasn't been determined. He added that he will work with Mr. Bricklin, a Slate co-founder and vice president, in Slate's Watertown, Mass., offices.
