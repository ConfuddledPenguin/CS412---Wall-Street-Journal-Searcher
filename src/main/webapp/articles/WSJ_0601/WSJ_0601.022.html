Australian Banks' Bad Debts

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/01/90

SYDNEY -- The bad debt expenses of banks in Australia more than doubled during the fiscal year ended June 30, 1989, reaching almost three billion Australian dollars (US$2.32 billion), according to a new KPMG Peat Marwick 1990 Financial Institutions Performance Survey. In announcing the survey results, Michael Ullmer, chairman of KPMG Peat Marwick's Banking Industry Group, attributed the increase in bad debt expenses to aggressive lending to entrepreneurs coupled with the government's high interest-rate policy in 1988 and 1989.
