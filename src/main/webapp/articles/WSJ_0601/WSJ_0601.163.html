SEC Approves Pilot Plan For Trading of OTC Stocks

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/01/90

WASHINGTON -- The Securities and Exchange Commission voted to approve a pilot project allowing stock exchanges to trade some over-the-counter stocks. The project, passed by the commission 3-1, is designed to foster competition in stock trading.

The SEC approved the plan as a pilot to run about a year before evaluation.

Under the plan, exchanges would apply to the SEC for the right to trade OTC stocks under "unlisted trading privileges." That would allow the exchanges to trade the shares without having the issues go through the exchanges' standard registration or listing requirements.

"This is one of the steps the commission is taking to create a national market system," an SEC spokeswoman said. She added that the action follows a 1975 congressional action asking the SEC to help create such a system.
