Rembrandt Work Fails To Stir Christie's Bidders

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/01/90

NEW YORK -- Art-buying fever was chilly at Christie's International PLC's auction house, as Rembrandt van Rijn's "Saint Peter Repentant" failed to find a buyer. Bidding stopped at $9.5 million for the 17th-century masterwork, which had, until recently, hung in the Metropolitan Museum of Art on loan from its owner. The piece had been expected to sell for $10 million to $15 million.

Overall, 43% of the 149 works offered did not sell. The auction raised $8.1 million, vs. pre-sale expectations of $23 million to $33 million.

The mediocre results of the Old Masters auction appear to dash hopes that that type of artwork will be the next "hot" market now that prices for Impressionist and Modern paintings seem to be leveling off, based on recent auction results. Art dealers said concerns about authenticity of many Old Master paintings are keeping collectors from that slightly more affordable sector of the market.

The top lot of the Christie's auction was Pieter Brueghel the Younger's, "A Peasant Wedding Feast." The colorful 1603 scene sold for $1.4 million, vs. a pre-sale estimate of $1.5 to $2 million.

Sotheby's Holdings Inc.'s Old Masters paintings sale is scheduled for today. Up for sale are two views of Venice by Il Canaletto. The pair is expected to bring about $10 million.
