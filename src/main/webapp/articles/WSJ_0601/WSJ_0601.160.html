Business Brief -- Kawasaki Heavy Industries Ltd.: Unconsolidated Pretax Profit For Fiscal 1990 Rose 25%

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/01/90

Kawasaki Heavy Industries Ltd., the leading shipbuilder in Japan, posted a 25% increase in unconsolidated pretax profit for the fiscal year ended March 31 to 17.17 billion yen ($113.4 million) from 13.77 billion yen a year earlier. Net income rose 55% to 12.35 billion yen, or 9.28 yen a share, from 7.97 billion yen, or 5.99 yen a share. The improvement allowed the company to resume its dividend, at five yen a share.

Sales grew 5.9% to 812.81 billion yen from 767.07 billion yen, owing to strong growth in the three largest divisions. Sales rose 8.1% to 236.44 billion yen in the aircraft and jets division, 23% to 190.67 billion yen in the machinery unit, and 17% to 151.96 billion yen in consumer products. But steel structure sales declined 12% to 124.25 billion yen. Increased attention to profitability continued to buffet the shipbuilding unit, where sales dropped 19% to 42.17 billion yen. But shipbuilding orders for the year soared 181%, reflecting boosted demand for Japanese ships amid rising prices in competing nations. The company had 153.11 billion yen in orders at the end of March.
