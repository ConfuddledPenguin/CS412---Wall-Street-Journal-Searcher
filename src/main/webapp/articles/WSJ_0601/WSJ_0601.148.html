SEC Seeks Comment On Plan for Holders In Foreign Companies

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/01/90

WASHINGTON -- The Securities and Exchange Commission voted to seek comment on a proposal that would make it easier for U.S. shareholders in foreign companies to take advantage of tender and exchange offers. The proposal was drafted in response to concerns that bidders for shares of foreign issues sometimes deliberately exclude U.S. shareholders to avoid the cost of complying with U.S. disclosure requirements.

The proposal would allow such offers to proceed without complying with U.S. requirements if the U.S. securities holdings constitute a relatively small portion of the entire transaction.

The SEC is seeking comment on what the level of U.S. holdings should be. "This is an area that will become more and more important as there are more foreign-issued securities purchased by U.S. investors," said Linda Quinn, head of the SEC's division of corporation finance. "People are being substantially affected in the value of their investments when they get carved out" of exchange and tender offers, she said. The commission is expected to take public comments on the proposal for about 120 days.

The SEC also approved a rule proposed by the Municipal Securities Rulemaking Board to require underwriters of municipal bonds to provide the board with copies of final official statements on bond issues, and to create a reference room where the public could obtain copies of official documents. The commission approved the MSRB proposal by a vote of 3-1.
