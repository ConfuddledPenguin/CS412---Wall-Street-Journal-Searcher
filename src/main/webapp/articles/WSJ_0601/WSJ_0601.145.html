Hertz Note Issue Gets Triple-B S&P Rating

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/01/90

NEW YORK -- Standard & Poor's Corp. said it rated a $100 million issue of Hertz Corp. medium-term notes triple-B, noting that the car-rental company's U.S. rental operations continue to face competitive pressures. "Although commercial pricing is improving gradually, competition in the leisure market is expected to remain intense this year," S&P said.

The report said Hertz's competitive position should be strengthened over time by fees being imposed on off-airport renters at some locations and state laws forcing elimination or advertising disclosure of separate collision damage waiver charges.

Hertz was acquired by Park Ridge Corp. at the end of 1987; Park Ridge is 49%-owned by Ford Motor Co., 26% by Volvo North America Corp., 20% by Hertz management, and 5% by Commerzbank AG.

S&P said, "Hertz's credit strength is limited by the need to service acquisition debt at Park Ridge, and consolidated financial measures are weaker than those for Hertz alone. However, Ford's strategic interest in preserving the financial health of its largest single customer provides significant credit support. During 1989, Ford bought $150 million of Hertz subordinated notes, allowing repayment of a like amount of Park Ridge acquisition bank loans and increasing Ford's total investment."
