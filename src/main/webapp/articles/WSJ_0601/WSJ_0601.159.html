Business Brief -- Storehouse PLC: Annual Pretax Profit Rose 13%, Despite Retailing Ills

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/01/90

Storehouse PLC of Britain said pretax profit for the year ended March 31 rose 13% to #12.8 million ($21.7 million) from #11.3 million a year earlier, despite widespread setbacks for the retailer as increased costs and competitive pressures hurt profit margins. Still, the results were below market estimates of #15 million to #25 million. Profit after taxes, less an extraordinary loss, or profit attributable to shareholders, plunged to #1.1 million from #58.8 million. Per-share earnings, including special items, rose 40% to 2.1 pence from 1.5 pence. Profit from retail operations fell 38% to #39.4 million from #63.3 million.

An extraordinary loss of #7.4 million in the latest period resulted from disposal of the Storecard credit-card business and closure of the Anonymous fashion chain. Year-earlier results included a #52.8 million one-time gain, mainly from surplus from the sale of an investment. Sales rose 8.3% to #1.3 billion from #1.2 billion.

Storehouse is recommending a final dividend of 2.5 pence a share, making the total payout five pence, down 43% from 8.8 pence a year earlier.
