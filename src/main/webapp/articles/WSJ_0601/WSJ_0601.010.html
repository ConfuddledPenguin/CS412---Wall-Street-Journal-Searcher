Salomon Offers Warrants In Play on Belgian Shares

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/01/90

BRUSSELS -- Salomon Inc. offered a novel way for investors to bet on a rise in Belgian share prices without the complications of dealing on the Brussels Bourse. The New York-based investment banking firm offered 500,000 warrants representing a basket of six blue-chip Belgian shares at 283 Belgian francs ($8.17) each, or a total of 142 million Belgian francs ($4.1 million). Such share warrants have proliferated in several other European countries in recent years, but bankers here said they believed Salomon's issue was the first of its kind in Belgium.

Each 100 warrants represents the current value of two shares of Barco N.V., one of Bekaert N.V., one of S.A. Cimenteries CBR, four of Delhaize "Le Lion" S.A. Belgium, three of Petrofina S.A. and two of Solvay & Cie. Based on yesterday's share prices, the warrants are priced at a premium of 18.6%. That means that, if the warrant is held for its full 18-month life, the value of the underlying shares would have to rise at least 18.6% to make it profitable for holders.

The warrants can be exercised from June 29, 1990, through Nov. 29, 1991. They will be listed on the London and Luxembourg stock exchanges. The issue is being distributed by Salomon's London investment banking unit as well as Dumenil Leble Bank (Belgium) S.A. and Banque Bruxelles Lambert S.A.
