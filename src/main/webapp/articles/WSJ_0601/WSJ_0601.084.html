Canadian Dollar Gains On Move to Resolve Impasse

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/01/90

OTTAWA -- The Canadian dollar strengthened yesterday on news that Prime Minister Brian Mulroney will convene a weekend meeting to address Canada's constitutional impasse. The Canadian currency rose in Toronto to 85.30 U.S. cents from 85 U.S. cents Wednesday.

Mr. Mulroney summoned the country's 10 provincial premiers to Ottawa Sunday for negotiations to overcome the constitutional deadlock. Financial markets interpreted the announcement as a sign of possible progress toward a settlement.

The impasse has renewed demands from some in Quebec for independence for their largely French-speaking province and has unsettled Canada's markets.

The Ottawa meeting follows weeks of intense negotiations between the federal government and individual provinces that have narrowed but failed to settle the differences. The dispute involves proposed constitutional amendments that which would recognize Quebec as a "distinct society" within Canada. The accord would also extend new powers to the provinces.

Edward Neufeld, executive vice president of the Royal Bank of Canada, estimated that political uncertainties have pushed up short-term Canadian interest rates by at least a full percentage point.
