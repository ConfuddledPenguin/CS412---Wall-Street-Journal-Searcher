Who's News: American Home Products Names Canavan President

  • Author: Robert Daniels
  • Date: 06/01/90

NEW YORK -- American Home Products Corp. named Bernard Canavan president, strongly emphasizing its effort to focus on health-care businesses. The 54-year-old Dr. Canavan, who has been executive vice president of the parent and chairman of the Wyeth-Ayerst Laboratories subsidiary, succeeds John R. Stafford, 52, who continues as chairman and chief executive officer of American Home.

"It's a natural progression of events," said Ronald M. Nordmann, analyst at Paine Webber Group Inc. Dr. Canavan "had been there for over 20 years. He'd run the major pharmaceutical operations of the company, and it sends a signal that American Home Products wants to be recognized as a {prescription} drug company rather than an over-the-counter drug or consumer-product company."

American Home took a giant step in that direction in mid-March, when it agreed to sell its Boyle Midway consumer-product operations, which include prominent brands such as Black Flag insect repellent and Woolite cleanser for delicate fabrics, for $1.25 billion to Reckitt & Colman PLC of the U.K. American Home said it expects to close that sale shortly.

Dr. Canavan declined to comment on specific moves he plans to make. But he said he aims to build American Home's strong franchises in prescription and over-the-counter pharmaceuticals and veterinary-care and hospital products.

Even with the focus on health care, American Home has no plans to shed its foods division, which has "good consumer brands and recognition," Dr. Canavan said. The division, with some $900 million in annual sales, includes products such as Chef Boyardee Italian foods and Pam antistick cooking spray.

American Home's 1989 sales were $6.75 billion.

Dr. Canavan's former positions at the parent and Wyeth-Ayerst won't be filled. Fred Hassan, president of Wyeth-Ayerst, will continue to report to Dr. Canavan. The company's prescription-drug research and development activities, which had reported to Dr. Canavan, now will report to Mr. Hassan.
