Storage Technology To Retire $210 Million Of 13.5% Debentures

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/01/90

LOUISVILLE, Colo. -- Storage Technology Corp. said it will retire its $210 million outstanding of 13.5% senior debentures due in 1996, saving about $25 million a year in interest. The maker of data storage devices said it will redeem debentures of record June 11 for 104.48% of par. The company issued about $285 million of the bonds, along with stock and cash, when it emerged from bankruptcy-law proceedings in 1987. It has been trying for about a year to find ways to retire the high-cost debt. This year, it has redeemed $34 million of the debentures and bought about $40 million of them on the open market.

As a result of the planned redemption, Storage Technology said it will record an extraordinary second-quarter charge of about $4.6 million. In the second quarter last year, it had net income of $4.4 million, or 17 cents a share, on revenue of $163.8 million.

The company said it will pay for the redemption with proceeds from a stock offering in March and a recently completed offering of 8% convertible subordinated debentures due 2015.
