Housing Starts Rise in Japan

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/01/90

TOKYO -- Construction of 147,845 houses got under way in Japan in April, a 0.5% increase from a year earlier, the Construction Ministry said. The rise was in line with a general trend upward, sustained by long-lasting economic growth led by domestic demand. Though housing starts fell 2% in March, they have been rising consistently since August 1989.

Meanwhile, a research institute said housing starts in the fiscal year that began in April will total about 1.5 million units, a 10% decline from fiscal 1990, and the same as the Construction Ministry's earlier estimate.
