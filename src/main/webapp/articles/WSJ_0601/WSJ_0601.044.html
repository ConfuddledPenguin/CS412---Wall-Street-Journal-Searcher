Harlyn Sees Net In Period Above 14 Cents a Share

  • Author: David J. Jefferson
  • Date: 06/01/90

LOS ANGELES -- Harlyn Products Inc., encouraged by strong sales for Mother's Day, expects per-share net income of more than 14 cents on sales of about $8 million for the fiscal fourth quarter ending June 30, said Harold Weisbrod, chairman, and Edward Dudziak, president. In an interview, the executives didn't give net income estimates in dollars.

In the year-earlier fourth quarter, the jewelry maker had indicated net income of $569,000, or 16 cents a share, on sales of about $5.8 million. Harlyn doesn't normally break out fourth-quarter results from its year-end numbers. Year-earlier fourth-quarter results were arrived at by subtracting sales and earnings for the first nine months of fiscal 1989 from the year-end numbers.

For fiscal 1990, Harlyn expects earnings of more than 50 cents a share on sales of more than $30 million, Messrs. Weisbrod and Dudziak said. That compares with fiscal 1989 net of $1.5 million, or 40 cents a share, on sales of $24.8 million.

Harlyn is also in talks for a possible acquisition of a jewelry company that the executives declined to identify. "It would fit in very well with our sales, marketing and manufacturing . . . and it wouldn't require any additional costs other than the purchase price," Mr. Weisbrod said.

Last year, Harlyn acquired George Hoffman Jewelry Manufacturing Inc., a Beverly Hills, Calif., maker and marketer of diamond jewelry. "We've controlled the costs there, and sales have increased slightly," Mr. Dudziak said.

Of Harlyn's continued earnings performance, Mr. Weisbrod said, "We're very optimistic. . . . Even though the jewelry business hasn't been so good, ours has been superb." He said sales of family jewelry, adorned with birthstones of various family members, "is stronger than it has ever been." The company experienced unusually strong sales of its rings for Mother's Day.

Mr. Weisbrod said Harlyn has reduced the average delivery time for its rings to under two days, from three or more days last year. (Harlyn now guarantees delivery within three days after a customer places an order.) The company has also implemented various cost-cutting measures to improve the bottom line.

Business for Harlyn's Bangkok manufacturing plant has increased as a result of higher sales in Europe and Japan. The plant's sales are expected to more than double this year from last year's $1.2 million and to double next year, Mr. Dudziak said.
