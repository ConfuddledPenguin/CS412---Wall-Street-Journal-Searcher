Hechinger Views Its Warehouse Unit As Growth Vehicle

  • Author: Anne Nichols Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 06/01/90

LANDOVER, Md. -- Hechinger Co. expects its Home Quarters Warehouse subsidiary to become "the primary growth vehicle for the company," John Hechinger Jr., president and chief executive officer, told Dow Jones. The retailer of home-improvement products and equipment currently operates a total of 108 outlets, including 16 Home Quarters units. Nine more Home Quarters outlets are scheduled to open in the next few months.

"Certainly, opening new stores will have a dampening effect on earnings" for the near term, Mr. Hechinger conceded. He said the company will open four Home Quarters units in Boston and will enter the markets of Columbus, Ga.; Birmingham and Montgomery, Ala.; and Newport News, Va.

Last year, the company opened four Home Quarters outlets and 13 Hechinger stores. This year, it will open only two new Hechinger stores.

Home Quarters stores, which have about 105,000 square feet and are nearly twice the size of traditional Hechinger outlets, carry fewer items and take a more aggressive discounting approach.

"One of the big misconceptions with our business is that it is affected by housing starts," Mr. Hechinger said. The company targets owners of existing, single-family homes for do-it-yourself improvements.

In general, the home-improvement industry is "highly fragmented," Mr. Hechinger said. "The top 25 chains represent a small portion of the total business done in the category." He predicted there will be "fewer and fewer larger players."

For the fiscal year ended Feb. 3, Hechinger reported earnings of $34.2 million, or 95 cents a share, on revenue of $1.23 billion. On Tuesday, Hechinger reported that first-quarter profit rose 15% to $8.4 million, or 23 cents a share, from $7.3 million, or 21 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue rose 20% in the latest quarter.

Mr. Hechinger declined to make a specific projection for fiscal 1991, but he expressed confidence in the outlook. "I feel good about our company's prospects for the future," he said. "We've grown at 25% a year for 20 years, and I expect a continued 20% growth range in new markets."
