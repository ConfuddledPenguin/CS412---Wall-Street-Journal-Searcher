Business Brief -- Agnico-Eagle Mines Ltd.: Canadian Concern Cancels Shares, Warrants Offering

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/01/90

Agnico-Eagle Mines Ltd., Toronto, canceled its plan to offer 40 million Canadian dollars (US$34 million) in common shares and share-purchase warrants, citing recent declines in prices of gold and the company's shares. In national over-the-counter trading yesterday, Agnico shares closed at $7, up 37.5 cents. The offering price wouldn't reflect Agnico's value, the company said, noting it will rely for now on its own resources to finance the early stages of a planned three-year, C$32 million exploration and development program. Agnico said it is considering other means of raising funds.

The offering was opposed by Jacques Forget, a Montreal businessman who owns about 3% of Agnico and had notified it that he intended to ask a court to block the issue. Agnico said its cancellation of the offering, which was to be priced soon, wasn't related to Mr. Forget's actions. Mr. Forget said that the injunction isn't needed now, but that he still opposes Agnico's management and will continue to try to establish a holder group to pursue a takeover bid for Agnico, which wouldn't comment on Mr. Forget's plan to change management.
