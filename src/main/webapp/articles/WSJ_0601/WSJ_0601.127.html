Marketing & Media: Judge Approves Sale Of PTL TV Network To Morris Cerullo

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/01/90

COLUMBIA, S.C. -- A federal bankruptcy judge approved the sale of the PTL television network for $7 million to Morris Cerullo World Evangelism, a San Diego-based ministry. Morris Cerullo's ministry, which has operations in Canada, Mexico, Brazil, Britain and the Philippines, among other sites, also has made a $45 million offer for the balance of PTL's assets, including 2,200 acres of land, and a hotel and theme park.

PTL's founder, Jim Bakker, was convicted of numerous federal fraud charges in connection with the operations.

Morris Cerullo World Evangelism said it intends to operate the complex, known as Heritage Village Church and Missionary Fellowship Inc., as a family resort. The TV network, it said, will be run as a 24-hour-a-day, family-oriented, global satellite network.

Dennis Shedd, a court-appointed trustee for the PTL ministry, said the bankruptcy court is expected to consider the offer for the balance of the estate in about a month, after proper notice is given to the parties.

The sale of the network is subject to the approval of the license transfer by the Federal Communications Commission. The trustee said the buyer placed $7 million in escrow to cover the purchase.

A rival bidder, Oral Roberts University, had offered about $6.3 million to $6.45 million for the TV network, which includes production facilities and a satellite lease, Mr. Shedd said. He added that only Morris Cerullo had made an offer for the balance of the failed religious empire.
