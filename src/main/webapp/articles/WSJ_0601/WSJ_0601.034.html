Letters to the Editor -- Salamander Find: Witnessed, Verified

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/01/90

I appreciate the attention given to our environmental activities in your April 23 article "Salamander Draws Fire From Developers." The salamander pond on the Bridgewood, N.Y., parcel was found without prior knowledge of a proposed subdivision. As to the statement "No one was there to witness the find," I was accompanied by James Ash, a noted Long Island naturalist. The salamander was kept in a refrigerator to duplicate the outdoor temperature at that time of year. Southhampton town officers verified the find the following day.

The issue of balancing the needs of developers and endangered species is an urgent global problem. As a businessman, I understand the need for a responsible balance between economic necessity and environmental protection. However, once a species is extinct, any possibility for balance is lost forever.

Andrew E. Sabin

President

Sabin Metal Corp.

New York
