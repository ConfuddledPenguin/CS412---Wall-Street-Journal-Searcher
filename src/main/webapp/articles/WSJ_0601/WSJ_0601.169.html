Who's News: Washington Bancorp. Names Mason to Be Chairman and Chief

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/01/90

WASHINGTON -- Washington Bancorp. named John J. Mason chairman and chief executive officer and gave preliminary approval to a bailout plan for the cash-strapped bank holding company. Mr. Mason, the 51-year-old former chairman of NS&T Bankshares Inc., owns 6.4% of Washington Bancorp. In addition, he is one of two lead investors in the company's planned issue of $45 million in new secured debt, announced Tuesday.

Washington Bancorp. also named Michael F. Ryan, 51-year-old former president of NS&T Bank, as president and chief operating officer. John M. Toups, who served as interim chairman and chief executive after the January resignation of Luther H. Hodges Jr., said he will go back into retirement and remain a director.

In early May, Washington Bancorp. said it wouldn't be able to repay about $36.7 million of unsecured commercial paper obligations because certain credit lines were cut off. The company has since defaulted on most of that short-term debt.

As part of the financial revamp plan, virtually all of Washington Bancorp.'s assets will be pledged to secure the $45 million in new debt, which will probably have a term of 12 months. In addition, Mr. Toups said the debt buyers will receive warrants, with anti-dilution protection, to acquire 60% or more of the company's common stock at a "nominal" price.

Mr. Mason declined a request for an interview, but in a statement, the new chairman said he has two immediate goals: to end Washington Bancorp.'s liquidity crisis and to restore public confidence in the company's principal subsidiary, National Bank of Washington.

Mr. Mason also resigned as a director of Richmond, Va.-based Crestar Financial Corp. to devote more time to Washington Bancorp. Crestar hasn't yet decided whether it will fill the vacancy.
