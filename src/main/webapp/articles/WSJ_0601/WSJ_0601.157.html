Who's News: Rockwell's Churchill To Quit Presidency Of Avionics Group

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/01/90

EL SEGUNDO, Calif. -- Rockwell International Corp. said that James L. Churchill, president of Rockwell's avionics group since 1981, has decided to retire at the end of the year. Mr. Churchill, 60 years old, has held various executive posts over 21 years at the group, based in Cedar Rapids, Iowa. During his tenure as president, group sales more than doubled to $984 million last year from $455 million, Rockwell said.

Mr. Churchill's duties will be divided between two executives as part of a realignment that spins off the group's government division into Rockwell's defense electronics segment. "This brings all of our defense electronics under one umbrella," a Rockwell spokesman said.

John R. Girotto, 52, who will immediately assume most of Mr. Churchill's duties, was named president of the group's air transport and general aviation divisions. He was formerly vice president and general manager of the air transport division. John A. McLuckey, 50, continues as president of the defense electronics segment, with additional duties.
