Business Brief -- Air Products & Chemicals Inc.: PVC Business to Be Sold To Unit of Pacific Western

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/01/90

Air Products & Chemicals Inc. said it agreed to sell its $125 million-a-year polyvinyl chloride, or PVC, business, where weak pricing has hurt the Allentown, Pa., company's profit recently. Terms weren't disclosed. The company said its earnings this year won't be materially affected by the sale to the Pacific Western Resin Co. unit of privately held Pacific Western Extruded Plastics Co., a Eugene, Ore.-based PVC pipe maker.

Analysts said the divestiture is long overdue and will improve Air Products' earnings prospects starting in 1991, by reducing exposure to cyclical commodity chemicals. The PVC business being sold includes two manufacturing facilities in Kentucky and Florida, with total annual capacity of 400 million pounds. The deal, which Air Products expects to complete within two months, after regulatory review, is part of the company's strategy of shifting resources toward higher-margin specialty chemicals. PVC represents about half of the company's $240 million a year in sales of commodity chemicals. Total chemical sales last year were $976 million.
