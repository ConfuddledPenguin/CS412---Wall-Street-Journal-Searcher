International Brief -- British Gas PLC: Fiscal 1990 Net Rose 3% On an Historic-Cost Basis

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/01/90

British Gas PLC reported that net income on an historic-cost basis for the year ended March 31 rose 3% to #926 million ($1.57 billion) from #899 million a year earlier, as gains from exploration and production activities offset softened demand from the warm European winter. The historic-cost net, which values inventories at their acquisition cost, was slightly below analysts' expectations, ranging from #930 million to #970 million. On a current-cost basis, in which inventories are valued at replacement cost, net rose 9.7% to #680 million from #620 million. Earnings per share increased 2.4% to 21.7 pence from 21.2 pence. Sales rose 6% to #7.98 billion from #7.53 billion.

Chairman Robert Evans reiterated the company's commitment to seeking opportunities beyond its traditional hold on the domestic market. He cited the potential for British Gas to develop natural gas reserves and distribution systems in unserved areas globally. The company is in the midst of a 1.1 billion Canadian dollar (US$935.3 million) friendly takeover bid for Consumers' Gas Co. of Canada, which is being contested by a Canadian investor group.
