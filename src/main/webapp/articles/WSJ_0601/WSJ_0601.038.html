Property, Casualty Insurers' Net

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/01/90

NEW YORK -- Net income of the nation's property and casualty insurers declined 34% in the first quarter. Earnings, including investment income, fell to $2.26 billion from $3.46 billion a year earlier, according to the Insurance Information Institute, a trade group. The figures are compiled from reports of insurers representing about 97% of the nation's property-casualty insurance business.

The decline in profitability reflected "basic economics," with costs rising faster than premiums, the institute said.
