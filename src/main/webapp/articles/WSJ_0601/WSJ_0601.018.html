Net at Software Toolworks Surged During 4th Period

  • Author: Frederick Rose
  • Date: 06/01/90

CHATSWORTH, Calif. -- Software Toolworks Inc. said net income more than tripled in its fourth quarter ended March 31, bolstered by its recent acquisition of Mindscape Inc. and strong sales of computer game products. The maker of entertainment, educational and office software said net rose to $2.3 million, or 12 cents a share, from $740,000, or five cents a share, a year earlier. Per-share amounts are adjusted for a 2-for-1 split paid April 4.

The latest period includes an after-tax charge of $1.2 million related to costs of the Mindscape acquisition and $677,000 in tax credits for previous losses.

Revenue jumped 78% in the quarter, to $21.9 million from $12.3 million. Shares outstanding averaged 20.3 million in the latest period, compared with 16 million a year earlier.

The company acquired Mindscape on March 15 in a stock swap valued at about $40 million. Because the transaction is being accounted for under the pooling-of-interests method, the latest period's results as well as the earlier ones include those of Mindscape, a company official said.

Mindscape, based in Northbrook, Ill., has a license to produce game cartridges for Nintendo Co. computer game system. Software Toolworks said its latest results were boosted by the company's release of an "Infiltrator" game for the Nintendo system as well as sales of older game products including "Paperboy," "Road Blaster" and "The Hunt for Red October."

Software Toolworks said it signed a letter of intent with Canon Inc. of Japan to develop software for the Canon Navigator IBM-compatible personal computer to be released this year.

For the year ended March 31, Software Toolworks' net was $2.5 million, or 14 cents a share. It had a net loss of $4.3 million, or 29 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue swelled 96% to $68.7 million from $35.0 million. Average shares outstanding, adjusted for the 2-for-1 split, rose to 18.3 million from 14.8 million.
