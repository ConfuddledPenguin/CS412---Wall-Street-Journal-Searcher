Avon Suggests Chartwell Not Buy Additional Shares

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/06/90

NEW YORK -- In an unusual move, Avon Products Inc.'s board suggested that its largest shareholder not acquire additional shares in the company. In a letter to Richard Fisher, a leading participant in Chartwell Associates L.P., Avon Chairman James Preston wrote, "Our board views Chartwell's additional purchases of Avon stock as inappropriate." Chartwell owns about 16% of Avon's common, or 12% of voting stock including preferred. On Wednesday, Chartwell bought three million shares of Avon common.

Mr. Preston said Avon's board believes additional Chartwell purchases will unfairly influence Avon's desire to "maximize shareholder value." He also expressed concern that more purchases will be unfair to other holders and harm Avon's door-to-door business.

To underline its point, the board amended Avon's share rights plan. Share rights are attached to each share of common, and in Avon's case allow shareholders to acquire Avon stock at half price if any buyer passes a 20% common stock threshold.

Now, the board has decided that if anybody acquires more than 12.5% of Avon's voting shares, the board will have only 10 days to redeem the share rights for that particular buyer. After that, the poison pill will automatically kick in at the 20% benchmark.

"What we're telling Chartwell is don't try to buy the company on the cheap," said an executive familiar with Avon's strategy.

A Chartwell spokesman said, "We are disappointed by Avon's unilateral change in its rights plan. We haven't read the full amendment, but we are considering all of our options."
