What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/06/90

A BROAD ECONOMIC PACT was reached by the U.S. and Japan that is intended to make it easier for foreigners to do business in Japan. The accord also calls for the U.S. to increase savings and competitiveness. Still, the landmark agreement will require legislative changes in both nations, which may be hard to achieve. ---

A House panel approved clean-air legislation after adopting an acid rain package that would help Midwest utilities facing cleanup costs. The bill may reach the House floor in May.

---

UAL directors began merger talks with United Air's unions following a sweetened union offer of $200 a share, or $4.36 billion. The decision to negotiate signaled that the board may be ready to accept the bid rather than wage a proxy battle with UAL's largest shareholder, Coniston Partners.

---

Ford is holding talks about the possible sale of its New Holland farm-equipment unit to Fiat of Italy, sources said. A sale would mark Ford's second major retreat from diversification in recent months.

---

The thrift-bailout program is now widely expected to cost at least twice as much as the $157.6 billion that Bush officials were estimating last year. The new projection leaves a funding gap of $100 billion to $200 billion.

---

Browning-Ferris plans to sell its hazardous waste-disposal business following problems complying with environmental rules. The move may result in a $295 million charge.

---

Bankruptcy-law filings jumped 34% in the fourth quarter of last year, reflecting the soft economy and heavy debt burdens at many companies. The growth appears likely to continue.

---

Stock exchanges are seeking the authority to charge fees for price quotations they regularly provide for free. The proposal has sparked a major dispute between the Big Board and brokerage and media firms.

---

Japan's securities industry is expected to undergo a major shake-up following the collapse in Tokyo share prices this year. The changes may mean stiffer competition for U.S. and European brokerages in Japan.

---

Vernon S&L ex-chairman Woody Lemons was sentenced to 30 years in prison for fraud and other charges relating to the 1987 collapse of the Dallas thrift. It is one of the stiffest sentences ever in an S&L fraud case.

---

Petroleum prices tumbled on worries about a possible supply glut. OPEC production is now at an eightyear high, while U.S. crude inventories are the biggest in two years.

---

Eight publicly traded funds originally sponsored by VMS Realty Partners are delaying dividend declarations. Three are suspending payouts.

---

Newspaper advertising has been weaker than expected this year, surprising publishers. Ad revenue is flat or down at papers across the country, especially in the Northeast.

---

Major retailers posted weak sales for March, reflecting the later Easter holiday season and continued sluggish demand for some durable goods. Also, many retail chains don't report March sales until next week.
