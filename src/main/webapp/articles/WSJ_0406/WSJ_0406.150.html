Ford Mulls Sale Of New Holland Unit to Fiat --- Discussions Are Being Held With Italian Auto Maker On Farm Gear Subsidiary

  • Author: Paul Ingrassia and Robert L. Rose
  • Date: 04/06/90

DEARBORN, Mich. -- Ford Motor Co. is holding discussions on the possible sale of its Ford New Holland farm-equipment subsidiary to Fiat S.p.A. of Italy, people familiar with the situation said. Such a move would mark the second major retreat from diversification by Ford in recent months. The No. 2 U.S. auto maker already has announced that it wants to sell its Ford Aerospace unit. The company wants to free capital and other resources to concentrate on the increasingly competitive auto industry worldwide, and to expand its financial-services operations.

Just three years ago Ford was aggressively expanding into the agricultural-equipment business. The auto maker bought the New Holland division of Sperry Corp. for $330 million and combined it with the Ford Tractor division to form Ford New Holland in January 1987. Later that year, Ford purchased Versatile Corp., which makes large four-wheel drive tractors.

In all, Ford New Holland had sales of about $3 billion last year. While significant by farm-equipment industry standards, that was just a small portion of Ford's overall revenue of $96.15 billion in 1989.

An announcement of the Ford-Fiat discussions could come as early as today, said one individual familiar with the talks. A Ford official referred inquiries to the Ford New Holland subsidiary, where a spokesman said he wouldn't "comment on speculation."

Ford and Fiat have a mixed record on completing deals. In the mid-1980s they held intense and prolonged discussions about merging their European automotive operations. But the discussions fell apart when each side insisted on having control of the combined business.

However, the two companies are partners in the heavy-truck business in Europe. It's possible, said one industry insider, that Fiat could swap its interest in that operation in return for acquiring Ford New Holland.

Taking over Ford New Holland would make Fiat an instant powerhouse in the U.S. as farmers continue their recent buying spree. Its chief rivals would be Deere & Co. and the JI Case unit of Tenneco Inc., which makes Case International farm equipment.

Fiat's Geotech farm-equipment unit, which has major operations in Europe, already owns Hesston Corp. in the U.S., a relatively small maker of farm implements. It wasn't immediately clear what a Fiat purchase of New Holland would mean for the joint venture that Hesston has with Case to make hay and forage equipment.

However, if a deal is completed, it would "open up an entire new market for exports of small farm tractors Fiat builds in Europe," said John Stark, editor of Stark's Off-Highway Ledger, a Chicagobased trade publication. "It also gives Fiat flexibility for competing with Japanese {farm-equipment} companies in Europe in 1992."

In the U.S., Fiat would face the same challenge that plagues Ford: Ford New Holland's tractors and farm equipment have loyal customers in what industry officials call the "horseshoe" from the East Coast through the South and up the West Coast. But it is relatively weak in the heartland, where Deere and Case are dominant.

Ford New Holland has about 17,000 employees and does business in 120 countries, but most of its sales are in North America and Europe. Besides tractors, it makes combines, forage equipment and hay-making machines.
