Claim Against Newman, His Companies Is Rejected

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/06/90

BRIDGEPORT, Conn. -- A Superior Court jury rejected a Westport delicatessen owner's claim that he was promised a stake in actor Paul Newman's gourmet food companies. The jury found that Mr. Newman never promised Julius Gold an 8% stake in the companies, Newman's Own Inc. and Salad King Inc.

Mr. Gold contended that he was involved in marketing efforts as well as manufacturing and bottling operations since the business began, said his attorney, Richard Albrecht. Mr. Newman first started the business by making salad dressing in the basement of his Westport, Conn., home. The line later expanded to include spaghetti sauce, popcorn and lemonade.

"Mr. Gold naturally is disappointed but is not totally surprised," said Mr. Albrecht. "He recognized that he was going after an extremely popular institution predicated on an oral agreement."

Attorneys for Mr. Newman said they were "delighted" with the results. "Paul Newman has always said Newman's Own is Newman's own and the jury agreed," said W. Patrick Ryan, one of Mr. Newman's attorneys.

All profits from the companies, $28 million to date, have gone to charity, according to the Associated Press. The Westport-based companies had a record $42 million in sales last year, up from $36 million in 1988, the AP reported.

"It's just tragic, all of this energy and time could not have been spent in more charitable pursuits, but that's the nature of the beast," Mr. Newman told the AP after the verdict.
