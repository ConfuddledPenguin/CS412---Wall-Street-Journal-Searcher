QVC Network to Post Loss of $10 Million For Its First Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/06/90

WEST CHESTER, Pa. -- QVC Network Inc. said it expects to report a loss of about $10 million, or about 59 cents a share, for the first quarter ending April 30. The company said the loss reflects the costs of absorbing CVN Cos., acquired last fall. A year earlier, the video-shopping concern posted net income of $1.2 million, or eight cents a share. Last month, QVC suspended its quarterly dividend of 10 cents a share.

QVC also said its agreement to sell its C.O.M.B. mail-order business to Damark International Inc. was terminated because conditions of closing weren't satisfied, specifically with regard to financing the transaction.

QVC said it also expects a loss in the second quarter but is "confident" it will return to profitability in the third quarter. The company added that it hopes to earn $7 million for the year ending Jan. 31, 1991, compared with $6.3 million the previous year.

QVC said it is reopening the bidding for the customer lists, goodwill, and other intangible assets of C.O.M.B. The mail-order business has only $4 million in inventory, and QVC said the final disposition of C.O.M.B. will have no material effect on QVC's financial reports. QVC acquired C.O.M.B. last fall as part of its purchase of CVN. In February, QVC agreed to sell C.O.M.B. to Damark contingent on financing and government approval.
