Cheer Up, You Devils, It Appears The Wages of Sin May Be Rising

  • Author: Kerry Pechter Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 04/06/90

ALLENTOWN, Pa. -- A philandering pizza maker and his homicidal wife may profit from a new movie. And some people here don't think that's right. The movie is a new Hollywood comedy opening today. It's based on the true-life attempted murder of Tony Toto, a former Allentown pizzeria owner and admitted womanizer, by his jealous wife, Frances. Directed by Lawrence ("The Big Chill") Kasdan, the movie stars Kevin Kline and Tracey Ullman as the husband and wife.

If the movie, "I Love You To Death," is a box-office hit, the Totos could reap a tidy sum. But some people here grumble that the Totos should be ashamed of trying to cash in on their wicked ways.

"It's a sin that people can resort to violence and are rewarded by being made rich beyond their wildest dreams," says Lori Donnelly, an Allentown resident. Ms. Donnelly has started a campaign urging a boycott of local theaters that show the movie. She has even sent to President Bush a copy of a protest letter she wrote to Sony Corp.'s Tri-Star Pictures Inc. unit, which made the movie.

Seven years ago, police found Mr. Toto at home, shot in the head and in his cheating heart, but miraculously alive. For a year or so before that, according to a detective who worked on the case, his wife and inept hired killers had tried bombing his car, clubbing him, drugging him and shooting him. But to no avail.

The unsinkable Mr. Toto, who is 44 years old, not only survived, he bailed his wife out of jail before she was sentenced, then waited faithfully while she served four years. Their improbable love-conquers-all story made headlines.

The movie idea was hatched by two Allentown detectives, who found a producer who sold the idea to Tri-Star.

Pennsylvania law restricts convicted felons such as Mrs. Toto from enjoying the fruits of their crimes. But the law won't stop Mr. Toto, who still has a .25-caliber slug in his skull, from sharing any gains with her. He stands to receive an undisclosed share of the proceeds.

Mr. Toto says he just hopes for enough money to send his three younger children to college. But watching clips from the movie and chuckling over Mr. Kline's portrayal of him, Mr. Toto was thinking of another successful black comedy about a warring couple. "`The War of the Roses' has made" more than $80 million, he says, adding that he's been keeping track.
