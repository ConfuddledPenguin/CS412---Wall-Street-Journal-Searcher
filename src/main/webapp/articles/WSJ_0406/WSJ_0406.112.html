Inflation Is Expected to Fall

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/06/90

ZURICH, Switzerland -- A study by Union Bank of Switzerland forecasts lower inflation in most major industrial countries in 1991. The bank's economists estimate that average U.S. inflation in 1991 will ease to 4.3%, after 4.5% this year and 4.8% in 1989.

Japan should see average inflation fall to 2.0% in 1991 from 2.5% this year and 2.3% last year. West Germany, meanwhile, is expected to have inflation of 3.0% next year and this year, after 2.8% in 1989.

The most pronounced improvement is expected for the U.K., where average inflation is seen sinking to 4.0% in 1991 after 6.8% this year and 7.8% in 1989.
