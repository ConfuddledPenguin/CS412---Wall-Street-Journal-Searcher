Technology Brief -- Sanyo Electric Co.: New Battery Cell Developed Using Nickel-Metal Hydride

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/06/90

Sanyo Electric Co., Tokyo, said it developed a new-generation, high-performance, nickel-metal-hydride storage battery cell, which is rechargeable and yet provides a relatively large energy density. The company said that the battery cell provides 1.8 times the energy density of conventional nickel-cadmium battery cells, and that it is suitable for use in equipment such as transceivers, cellular phones and laptop computers.

Sanyo will unveil two types of the new product, HR-AA and HR-4/3A, this autumn. The former weighs 26 grams with a 14.5mm diameter and 55mm length, while the latter weighs 51 grams, and is 17mm in diameter, 67mm in length.

The company plans to churn out 400,000 units of the rechargeable cells a month in the initial production year.
