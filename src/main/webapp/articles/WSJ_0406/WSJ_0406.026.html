CFTC Proposes New Rule To Curb Trading Abuses

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/06/90

WASHINGTON -- The Commodity Futures Trading Commission, in another move aimed at curbing trading abuses, proposed a rule that would order members of brokers' groups to register with commodities exchanges. The groups, known as broker associations, have come under heightened scrutiny in the wake of last year's federal undercover investigation of fraud in the Chicago futures pits. An earlier CFTC study found that relationships among members of broker associations may increase the potential for trading abuses.

The proposed rules would require broker associations to register with futures exchanges and would direct exchanges to set up monitoring programs for the groups. The rules would apply to any group in which brokers work together to execute customer orders and share profits and losses from customer trades.

The commission will take public comments on the proposal for 60 days before making a decision on final rules.
