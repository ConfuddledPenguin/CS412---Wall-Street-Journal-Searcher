Shearson to Transfer Part of Boston Co. To New Joint Venture

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/06/90

NEW YORK -- Shearson Lehman Hutton Holdings Inc. agreed to transfer part of its Boston Co. money-management unit to a new joint venture, a move that will raise more than $10 million for Shearson. In a move that was expected, Shearson, a securities-firm unit of American Express Co., said it would transfer about $6 billion of Boston Co.'s money-management business into a new joint venture with Nippon Life Insurance Co.

The joint venture, in which Nippon will hold a 50% stake, will be called PanAgora Asset Management Inc. and be based in Boston. A related PanAgora joint venture was established last year in London.

Shearson said it will receive compensation from Nippon in exchange for the asset transfer. The amount of the compensation wasn't disclosed, but individuals in the industry said it was probably $10 million to $15 million.

The part of Boston Co. being transferred to the PanAgora venture is its Structured Investment Products Group, which specializes in so-called index funds, asset allocation and research for institutional investors.

Shearson has been trying to cut costs and replenish its capital. The securities firm on Monday said it expects to report a first-quarter loss of $897 million to $917 million, a securities-industry record, mainly because of charges it will take to pay for office closings and other parts of its big retrenchment plan. Included in the expected first-quarter loss, Shearson said, is $25 million in reserves for losses primarily on loans made by Boston Co.

Nippon has had a 13% interest in Shearson since 1987 through its holding of Shearson preferred stock. That interest will decline to approximately 8% when American Express completes a plan in which it will buy the rest of Shearson's common stock outstanding.
