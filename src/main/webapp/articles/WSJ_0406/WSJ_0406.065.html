Home National Corp. Says Principal Unit May Get a Receiver

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/06/90

MILFORD, Mass. -- Home National Corp. said regulators may appoint a receiver for its principal unit, Home National Bank of Milford. The move is possible because it is "very doubtful" the unit can raise $8 million to pad its $22.1 million loan loss reserves, as advised by regulators after an examination of the bank. The company warned that failure to raise capital could cause the Comptroller of the Currency and other regulatory authorities to "take such action as they consider appropriate, including the possible appointment of a receiver for the institution."

Home National also said it expects to report operating losses for the first quarter. In the year-ago period, the bank holding company had a loss of $3 million, or 47 cents a share.

The operating losses and the increase in the loan loss reserves will result in a deficit of shareholder equity of not less than $6 million, the company said.

In addition, Home National said it may restate its 1989 year-end results, which were already revised last month to show a loss of $26.6 million, or $4.18 a share, compared with profit of $4.1 million, or 62 cents a share, in 1988.

Home National Corp. had assets of $484.5 million at Dec. 31.
