Business Brief -- Bethlehem Steel Corp.: Fine of $1 Million Is Set In Air-Pollution Law Case

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/06/90

Bethlehem Steel Corp. agreed to pay a fine of $1 million to New York state for violating state air-pollution laws. The steelmaker also agreed to eliminate a major source of benzene emissions from its Lackawanna, N.Y., plant. Under the agreement, reached after negotiations between Bethlehem and New York state environmental officials, Bethlehem will cease operating one of its three coke oven batteries by July 1, 1990, construct a closed-loop cooling system and upgrade its two remaining coke oven batteries at Lackawanna. That will result in a 95% reduction of the 1.5 million pounds of benzene that the plant emits each year.

Bethlehem also must pay $700,000 of the fine within 30 days. Payment of the remaining $300,000 will be suspended subject to Bethlehem's compliance with the July 1 cease-operation date.
