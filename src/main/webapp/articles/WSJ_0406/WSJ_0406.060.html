Insurance Offered By London Firm In Takeover Fights

  • Author: Tim Carrington
  • Date: 04/06/90

LONDON -- For corporations, being the object of a takeover war is economically similar to being hit by a hurricane or a flood. So it isn't surprising that the insurance industry has hatched a new policy that companies can buy for protection against this modern catastrophe. TOI Corporate Services, a newly formed firm, is pushing the new takeover insurance in collaboration with Robert Fraser Group, a brokerage concern here. According to Sandra Ringsell, one of TOI's founders, "we're the first to market" takeover insurance, though others have sold it here and there on a makeshift basis.

A sales brochure TOI is circulating highlights the dizzying costs of being raided: for a company valued at more than #30 million ($49.2 million), it can cost about #500,000 to fend off an unwanted suitor, according to TOI.

Merchant bankers lay claim on the biggest chunk of these costs, with about #320,000. Lawyers and stockbrokers grab another #110,000. Public-relations people speak for another #5,000, while accountants claim #35,000.

Then there's all the extra time that executives at raided companies have to put in. That can be insured too. For coverage of #500,000 of takeover expenses, an insurance policy costs as much as #25,000 a year. The premium doesn't count as a takeover expense, however.

Ms. Ringsell says she has pieced together takeover-insurance policies through various underwriters for about 35 companies. Now that TOI has been formed to specialize in raider policies, she figures she will bring in about 400 more.

Although takeovers have slowed in the U.S. with the collapse of the junk-bond market, TOI warns companies in Europe that they aren't out of the woods yet. "Takeovers are likely to increase," the company said in sales literature, partly because "companies will be seeking to be better positioned in their respective markets ahead of 1992."

So far, Ms. Ringsell said, only one policyholder has filed a claim. Everything went smoothly: The company got reimbursed for the costs of repulsing the raider, and it came back to renew its policy for another year.
