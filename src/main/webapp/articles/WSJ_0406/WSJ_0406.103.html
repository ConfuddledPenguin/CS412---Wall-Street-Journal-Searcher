New Securities Issues

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/06/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATE

Hydro-Quebec -- $500 million of 9 3/8% debentures, due April 15, 2030, was priced at 99.873 to yield 9.387%. The noncallable notes were priced at a spread of 86 basis points above the Treasury's bellwether bond. Rated double-A3 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and double-A-minus by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets.

General Motors Acceptance Corp. -- $300 million of 9 3/8% notes, due April 1, 2000, was priced at 99.58 to yield 9.44%. The non-callable notes were priced at a spread of 86 basis points above the Treasury's 10-year note. Rated Double-A-3 by Moody's and double-A-minus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by First Boston Corp.

Du Pont Co. -- $300 million of 9.15% notes, due April 15, 2000, was priced at 99.831 to yield 9.176%. The non-callable notes were priced at a spread of 60 basis points above the Treasury's 10-year note. Rated double-A-2 by Moody's and double-A by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by First Boston Corp.

Private Export Funding Corp. -- $200 million of 9% secured notes, due Jan. 31, 1996, was priced at 99.958 to yield 9.004%. The non-callable notes were priced at a spread of 43 basis points above the Treasury's five-year note. Rated triple-A by Moody's and S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Citicorp Securities Markets Inc.

Potomac Electric Power Co. -- $100 million of 9% first mortgage bonds, due April 15, 2000, was won through competitive bidding by Goldman Sachs & Co. and is being reoffered initially at 99.125 to yield 9.135%. The bonds were priced at a spread of 56 basis points above the Treasury's 10-year note. The issue is rated double-A-2 by Moody's and double-A by S&P.

U.S. West Financial Inc. -- $75 million (Australian) of notes, due April 26, 1993, was priced at par to yield 14.25%. Rated single-A-2 by Moody's and double-A-minus by S&P, the non-callable issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets.

MUNICIPALS

Triborough Bridge and Tunnel Authority, New York -- $166.9 million general purpose revenue bonds, Series R, due Jan. 1, 1991-2005, 2010, 2016 and 2020, were tentatively priced by a Dillon Read & Co. group to yield from 6% in 1991 to 7.48% in 2016. Current interest serial bonds are priced to yield from 6% in 1991 to 7.25% in 2005. There are $27.8 million term bonds priced at 99 1/2 to yield 7.42% in 2010. There are $49.46 million term bonds priced at 98 3/4 to yield 7.48% in 2016 and $45.92 million term bonds priced at 83 7/8 to yield 7.34% in 2020. The bonds are rated double-A by Moody's. A single-A-plus rating is expected from S&P.

Cook County, Illinois -- $144.2 million general obligation bonds due Nov. 1, 1994-2004, 2007 and 2010, were priced by a Donaldson Lufkin Jenrette Securities Corp. group to yield from 6.50% in 1994 to 7.35% in 2010. Current interest serial bonds are priced at par to yield from 6.50% in 1994 to 7.30% in 2004. There are $33 million 7 1/4% term bonds priced at par in 2007 and $40.66 million term bonds priced at 96.35 to yield 7.35 pc in 2010. The bonds are MBIA-insured and rated triple-A by both Moody's and S&P.

MORTGAGES

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $300 million of real estate mortage investment conduit securiteis is being offered in eight classes by Kidder Peabody & Co. The offering, trust number 1990-50, is backed by Fannie Mae's 10 1/2% securities.

EUROBONDS

Finnish Export Credit (agency) -- $100 million (Canadian) of 13 3/8% eurobonds, due May 4, 1993, priced at 101.85 to yield 13.17% less fees via Bankers Trust International. Fees 1 3/8.
