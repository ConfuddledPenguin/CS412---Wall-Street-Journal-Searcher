Business Brief -- GLI Holding Co.: Sixty Fired by Greyhound For Strike-Related Violence

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/06/90

Greyhound Lines Inc., Dallas, said it had fired 60 employees for incidents of violence and vandalism associated with the strike by 9,300 members of the Amalgamated Transit Union. Company spokesman George Gravley said the company was processing a "very large number" of complaints that would result in additional terminations. The action came a day after a suspicious fire erupted at a Greyhound terminal in Boston. Since the strike began onMarch 2, 29 Greyhound buses and terminals have been struck by gunfire, and the company has received 81 bomb threats. In addition, one striker driver has been killed and one replacement was seriously injured.

Mr. Gravley described as "talk and not action" a statement issued earlier in the day by ATU President James La Sala, who condemned the violence as "reprehensible." Greyhound, a unit of GLI Holding Co., has said it will not return to the bargaining table until a week passes without violence.

Regarding that stance, Mr. La Sala complained that: "Any crackpot can stop negotiations from resuming by calling in a phone threat or by taking a potshot at a bus."
