Cabot Alters Plans, Will Buy Some Assets Of American Optical

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/06/90

WALTHAM, Mass. -- Cabot Corp. said it will buy all the assets of American Optical Corp.'s AO Safety division in an outright purchase by Cabot, instead of its previous plan to have partial ownership of AO Safety through a joint venture. Terms of the new transaction, which is expected to be completed later this month, weren't disclosed.

Cabot said it will merge AO Safety with Cabot's E-A-R noise control and hearing protection business into a wholly owned subsidiary, which is expected to have combined annual revenue of about $200 million. AO Safety makes safety eyeware, respirators and other safety products.

Previously, Cabot had said it would form a limited partnership with a unit of Manufacturers Hanover Corp. that was going to acquire AO Safety from American Optical. In addition, Cabot was going to contribute its E-A-R business to the partnership. From that transaction, Cabot would have received $100 million in cash and $30 million in securities, plus a "significant" minority interest in the partnership.

Cabot Chairman Samuel Bodman said, "As a result of certain strategic and financial developments, we decided it was in the best interest of Cabot to structure the transaction as an outright purchase. This will have a positive impact on earnings and provide more flexibility for financing in the future."

American Optical is based in Southbridge, Mass.
