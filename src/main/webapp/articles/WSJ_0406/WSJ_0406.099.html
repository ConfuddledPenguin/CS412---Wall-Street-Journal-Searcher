Talks on International Telecharge Sale End; Two Top Posts Filled

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/06/90

DALLAS -- International Telecharge Inc. said that talks about a possible sale of the company have ended and that Allan L. Rayfield, a former telecommunications executive, was named chairman and chief executive officer. The company, which provides operator-assisted long-distance service to hotels, hospitals and pay phones, said that discussions to sell the company to a suitor ended before a formal offer was made. The potential acquirer wasn't disclosed. International Telecharge said last month that it had received an inquiry about a possible acquisition.

The company also said that it named Mr. Rayfield, a company director, to the two top posts, succeeding Edmund F. Tagg, who was chairman, chief executive officer and president. Mr. Tagg, who remains a director, now will serve as president and chief operating officer, succeeding Dennis Geraghty, formerly chief operating officer, who resigned, the company said.

Mr. Rayfield was a senior vice president and director of GTE Corp. until a year ago. Since then, he has been a consultant.

International Telecharge also said that it has agreed to provide operator services to hotel and hospital customers of MCI Communications Corp. as part of an agreement calling for International Telecharge to become a large customer of MCI's long-distance network. International Telecharge said the pact reduces its long-distance line costs, and that it calls for MCI to market its operator, billing and collection services for hotels under the MCI name.

As part of the agreement, MCI returned warrants to purchase 2,986,895 International Telecharge shares to International Telecharge and agreed to provide a working-capital loan of undisclosed size to defray development costs for the hospitality services.

In American Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, International Telecharge rose 12.5 cents to close at $3.125 a share.
