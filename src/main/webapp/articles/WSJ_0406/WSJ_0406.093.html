Thrift Regulators Seize Pennsylvania Savings Firm

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/06/90

WASHINGTON -- After a one-month court hold on the thrift-industry restructuring, government regulators seized Colony Savings Bank, Monaca, Pa., an insolvent thrift with assets of $451 million. It was the first government seizure of an insolvent S&L since Olympic Federal Savings & Loan, Berwyn, Ill., won a round of court cases March 7. The Office of Thrift Supervision said Colony was operating in an unsafe and unsound condition, and suffered losses related to securities trading and commercial real-estate lending. Colony will continue operating under government management.

The court case rested on the fact that the director of the thrift office hadn't been confirmed by the Senate, as required by the Constitution. On Wednesday, the Senate confirmed Timothy Ryan for the post, and he will be sworn in Monday, apparently making the court decision moot.

Separately today, the General Accounting Office, the investigative arm of Congress, is expected to disclose its latest estimate of how much the thrift bailout may cost over 10 years. William Seidman, chairman of the Resolution Trust Corp., the S&L bailout agency, has already estimated that another $25 billion will have to be piled onto the $166 billion estimated by the Bush administration. Congressional sources expect the GAO estimate to be much higher.
