Who's News: Carnegie Mellon Chooses Mehrabian to Be President

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/06/90

PITTSBURGH -- Carnegie Mellon University named as its new president Robert Mehrabian, dean of the College of Engineering at the University of California at Santa Barbara. The 49-year-old Mr. Mehrabian will succeed Richard M. Cyert, who is retiring June 30 after heading the university for 18 years. Mr. Cyert is 68 years old.

Mr. Mehrabian, born in Tehran, Iran, is credited with bringing UCSB's engineering college to national prominence, Carnegie Mellon said. A metallurgist and mechanical engineer, Mr. Mehrabian previously served as director of the Center for Materials Science at the National Bureau of Standards.

He earned his bachelor of science and doctor of science degrees from Massachusetts Institute of Technology, where he then became a professor.
