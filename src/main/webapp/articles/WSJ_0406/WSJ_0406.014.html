International: Lufthansa to Join Venture To Expand Soviet Airport

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 04/06/90

FRANKFURT -- Deutsche Lufthansa AG said it will join four West German companies to invest 750 million marks ($441.3 million) in a 50-50 joint venture it has formed with Aeroflot, the Soviet national airline, to update and expand the Moscow airport, Sheremetjevo I. West Germany's national air carrier, which is 51% government owned, announced a year earlier that it had signed a letter of intent for the project. Feasibility studies were made during the past 11 months.

The Sheremetjevo I airport in Moscow, as well as other Soviet airports in the future, will be modernized, expanded and operated by the joint venture. The 750 million marks will be used to expand and update a passenger terminal in Moscow, to build an international freight terminal, to build a waiting hall and to build an airport hotel.

In addition to Lufthansa, partners in the venture will include electronics concern AEG AG, Flughafen Frankfurt am Main, Salzgitter subsidiary Noell G.m.b.H and Dresdner Bank AG.

Lufthansa also said it received a comprehensive maintenance contract to service the fleet of Airbus planes owned by Aeroflot. Lufthansa didn't disclose financial details of its new Aeroflot service contract.
