Technology & Health: Businessland To Report Loss For 3rd Period

  • Author: Carrie Dolan
  • Date: 04/06/90

SAN JOSE, Calif. -- Businessland Inc. said it expects to report a third-quarter net loss of about $2 million, reflecting soft retail sales and lower profit margins overseas. The news surprised analysts who had been expecting profit of $4 million to $5 million. Analysts said Businessland as late as this past month indicated the quarter would be profitable. In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, the computer retailer fell $1.50 to $8.875.

In the year-earlier quarter, net income was $8.5 million, or 27 cents a share. The company said it expects to report revenue for the latest quarter ended March 31 grew 9% to about $320 million from $293.3 million.

Businessland said new product announcements from Apple Computer Inc. and International Business Machines Corp. in mid-March caused "unanticipated slowing" in the market, as customers delayed purchases in order to evalute the new offerings.

Apple Computer has said it expects to post better-than-expected results for the quarter ended March 30, spurred by sales of its newer personal computers.

Businessland's chairman, David Norman, said that a strategy "to put a cap on discounts" affected sales, and in some cases "we were not competitive with Apple." In addition, he said some "sales people lost their focus" in the quarter, as Businessland geared up to start selling Compaq Computer Corp.'s products once again. However, Compaq products didn't begin shipping until this month.

Early last month, Businessland said it would begin selling Compaq products, after a feud between the companies caused Compaq to halt sales to Businessland for nearly a year. That announcement boosted Businessland's stock 23% in one day, to $10.75.

Mr. Norman said the company's U.K. operations and its Computer Craft unit weren't profitable in the third quarter. He said cost-cutting moves, including firing 15 to 20 of the 270 employees in the U.K. may bring U.K. operations back to profitability this quarter.

Businessland said it would also take an unspecified write-down for inventory of certain service parts. In the second quarter, the company took a $7.5 million charge for the closure of 19 retail centers, and the dismissal of as much as 7% of its work force.

Sales of Next Inc. computers, the company headed by Steve Jobs, co-founder of Apple, were "building momentum" in the third quarter but were "below plan," Mr. Norman said. Last year, Mr. Norman signed on as exclusive dealer for Next, which has been off to a slow start.
