Technology: Tandem Says Earnings in Latest Period Didn't Meet the Company's Expectations

  • Author: Carrie Dolan
  • Date: 04/06/90

CUPERTINO, Calif. -- Tandem Computers Inc. said that earnings and revenue for its second quarter ended March 31 were below its expectations. The announcement, which surprised analysts, sent the company's stock plunging in composite trading yesterday on the New York Stock Exchange. Tandem shares closed at $22.625, down $4.625.

Tandem was the most active stock on the New York Stock Exchange, with 4.8 million shares changing hands. A Tandem spokeswoman declined to comment on the drop in stock price.

The computer maker said that net income in the quarter was between $16.9 million, or 17 cents a share, and $30.7 million, or 29 cents a share. The company had expected earnings to be more than the $30.7 million it posted in the fiscal first quarter.

In the year-ago period, Tandem posted net income of $16.9 million on revenue of $363.4 million.

Revenue in the second quarter was between $437 million and $457 million, the company said, falling below its expectations. A spokeswoman said Tandem expected revenue to be above $457 million, but wouldn't say by how much. Analysts had estimated that revenue would be about $465 million.

Tandem said some orders it expected to close in the quarter were deferred. In addition, the company said higher interest rates in the United Kingdom may have caused financial institutions there to tighten budgets, and also blamed the downsizing of the securities market in the U.S. Tandem makes computers that are widely used by banks, stock exchanges and others who seek to record transactions at once, refreshing their central data bases at the same time.

J. Neil Weintraut, analyst with Hambrecht & Quist, blamed the stock plunge on Tandem's overly optimistic outlook, rather than on changes in its markets, or fundamental problems at the company. "Tandem has unfortunately established a record of disappointments for Wall Street," he said, which may have caused investors to react strongly to the announcement. He now expects Tandem to earn about 22 cents a share for the second quarter.

He said Tandem's high-end computer, Cyclone, introduced last year, and other recent products "are very competitive" with offerings from International Business Machines Corp. and Digital Equipment Corp.
