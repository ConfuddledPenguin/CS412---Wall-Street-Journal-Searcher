Letters to the Editor: Unjustified Implication

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/06/90

Your March 7 Law-column item seems to imply that the State Bar of Michigan had somehow sought to influence the disposition of a now-dismissed complaint against its president-elect. Complaints against lawyers in Michigan are handled by an agency appointed for that purpose by the Michigan Supreme Court, which is funded from state-bar dues. No one outside the discipline agency even knew of the existence of the complaint file. Even our president-elect, who was the target, did not learn of its existence until the incident was disclosed in a newspaper article long after the file had been closed. Consequently, it was not possible for the State Bar of Michigan to seek to influence the disposition of the complaint, the existence of which was not known. Further, the state bar has no control over the operations of the Attorney Grievance Commission. Robert B. Webster

President

State Bar of Michigan

Lansing, Mich.
