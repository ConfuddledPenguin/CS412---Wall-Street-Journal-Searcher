American Air, Eastern And Mesa Post Gains In Traffic for March

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 04/06/90

American Airlines, Eastern Airlines and Mesa Airlines all reported March traffic increases. American, a unit of Fort Worth, Texas-based AMR Corp., said its traffic rose 4.1% to 6.45 billion revenue passenger miles from 6.19 billion last year. A revenue passenger mile is one paying passenger flown one mile. American's load factor, or percentage of seats filled, fell to 63.2% from 65.6%.

For the first quarter, American said it flew 17.5 billion revenue passenger miles, up 7.9% from 16.2 billion. Its year-to-date load factor was 59.9%, down from 60%.

Miami-based Eastern, a unit of Texas Air Corp., said it flew 1.72 billion revenue passenger miles last month, against 299 million a year ago, when Eastern was nearly grounded by a strike. Eastern's load factor was 66.8% last month, compared with 50% a year ago. For the quarter, revenue passenger miles rose 9.2% to 4.75 billion from 4.35 billion. Load factor also was up, to 63.2% from 59.2%.

Mesa said its March traffic soared 74%, to 10.2 million revenue passenger miles from 5.9 million a year ago. Load factor fell to 47.4% from 51.5% last March.

The commuter carrier attributed its increased traffic to its new operations carrying passengers as a United Express carrier out of Denver. The airline continues to operate as an independent carrier out of its Albuquerque, N.M., and Phoenix, Ariz., hubs.

For the first three months of 1990, Mesa said its traffic jumped 62%, to 25.1 million revenue passengers from 15.5 million in 1989. Load factor for the same period declined, to 44.7% from 48.5%.
