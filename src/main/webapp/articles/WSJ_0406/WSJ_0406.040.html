International: Philippines Cut Foreign Debt

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/06/90

MANILA, the Philippines -- In a recent debt buy-back deal with foreign commercial banks, the Philippines reduced its debt stock 3.6% to $26.6 billion as of the end of January. This compares with $27.62 billion at the end of 1989, according to latest figures on the country's foreign debt, released by the Central Bank of the Philippines.

A Central Bank official said January's lower level of foreign debt coincides with a debt purchase plan the Philippines signed with foreign creditors that month.

The plan gave the debt-ridden country a chance to buy $1.34 billion of debt, owed by government agencies to foreign commercial banks, at a 50% discount.

The Philippines used an estimated $668 million in new loans obtained from the International Monetary Fund, the World Bank, and Export-Import Bank of Japan to buy its debt.
