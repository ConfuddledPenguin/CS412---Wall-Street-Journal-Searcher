Kodak Pays $2 Million In Environmental Fines

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/06/90

ROCHESTER, N.Y. -- Eastman Kodak Co. pleaded guilty to two misdemeanor violations of New York environmental law and paid the state a $2 million fine. Kodak, which had been accused of contaminating ground water at Kodak Park, its core manufacturing facility, also signed a consent order to do extensive cleanup work at the site. Company officials didn't have an immediate estimate on how much that would cost.

Kodak has already agreed to spend $100 million to replace or remove 1,013 chemical storage tanks in the wake of hometown furor over toxic spills. It also agreed to reduce emissions of methylene chloride, a suspected cancer-causing chemical, by 70% by 1995. But New York State Attorney General Robert Abrams yesterday charged the company must still "remedy decades of environmental abuse."

Kodak pleaded guilty to unlawful removal of hazardous waste in connection with a July 1, 1986, shipment of 48,900 pounds of contaminated soil to a silver-recovery plant. State officials said Kodak failed to file proper hazardous waste documents and ensure that the waste hauler and reclamation plant were licensed to handle the hazardous soil.
