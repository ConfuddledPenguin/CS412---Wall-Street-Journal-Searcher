Business Brief -- Durham Corp.: Group's $315 Million Offer Called `Highly Speculative'

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/05/90

Durham Corp. turned a cold shoulder to an investor group's offer to buy the company for $37.50 a share, or an indicated total of about $315 million. Durham, based in Raleigh, N.C., said its lawyers and financial advisers were reviewing what it called the "highly speculative" cash-and-debt proposal, made by a group including Bailey Financial Group Inc., a Rocky Mount, N.C., investment concern.

In a statement, George M. Womble, Durham's chairman and president, called the proposal "both grossly inadequate and highly unfavorable to Durham's shareholders." Mr. Womble said those were his preliminary reactions to the bid, which is subject to financing, and that he planned to discuss the proposal with the board.

Bailey Financial said it could remove the contingencies on its offer -- and possibly even raise the bid -- once Durham allows it to review financial and actuarial information. "We are serious about our offer, and we look forward to a friendly, negotiated transaction being consummated," Bailey Financial said.
