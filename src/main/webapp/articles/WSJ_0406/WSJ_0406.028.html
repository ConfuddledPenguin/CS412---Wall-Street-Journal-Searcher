Letters to the Editor: A TV-Jaded Critic Shouldn't Scorn Papa

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/06/90

I find it incredible that your television critic Martha Bayles would call Ernest Hemingway's "The Old Man and the Sea" a "pretentious, mediocre novella" (Leisure & Arts, March 15). I would like to remind her that Hemingway won the Nobel Prize for literature for this work in 1954. Ms. Bayles's judgment undoubtedly has been altered by watching too much TV. I suggest that in the future she forgo literary criticism and stick to reviewing such masterpieces as "The Gong Show." Gregory F. Farrell

Basking Ridge, N.J.

---

"The Old Man and the Sea" re-established Hemingway as one of the significant writers of the 20th century. Even Hemingway's most virulent literary critics would hesitate to describe "The Old Man" in such negative terms.

Ms. Bayles, who must spend much of her working life watching television, has obviously had her tastes jaded by that most pretentious, mediocre media.

As a bookseller, I am pleased that she is not a book reviewer and hope she continues to work in a field to which she is so nicely suited.

Bill Dalton

Owner, Andover Bookstore

Andover, Mass.
