Who's News: Spectrum Control Taps Johnston as President

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/06/90

ERIE, Pa. -- J.L. Johnston was named president and chief executive officer of Spectrum Control Inc., an ailing maker of electromagnetic products. The 47-year-old executive succeeds Thomas L. Venable, 55, who remains chairman. Mr. Johnston previously was vice president and general manager of the Electronic Components division, Allen Bradley Co., a unit of Rockwell International Corp.

Noting that Mr. Johnston has more than 20 years experience in Spectrum's industry, Mr. Venable said: "He knows the customers we serve and the product categories in which we participate. We're confident that he will help return Spectrum to profitability."

In the nine months ended Aug. 31, Spectrum posted a net loss of $665,000 on sales of $23.2 million.
