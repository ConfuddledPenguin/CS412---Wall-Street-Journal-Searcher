Business Brief -- American Telephone & Telegraph: Giant Communications Firm Resumes Talks With Unions

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/06/90

American Telephone & Telegraph Co. resumed talks on an informal basis with its two unions, the Communications Workers of America and the International Brotherhood of Electrical Workers on an early retirement program for non-management employees. Formal negotiations between the New York-based telecommunications giant and the unions broke off on March 27 in Washington after the unions rejected AT&T's proposal, which was designed to attract twice as many workers as are eligible for retirement.

AT&T, which trimmed its work force in 1989 by 25,500 jobs to a current total of 281,400, has firm plans to cut as many as 12,000 jobs this year. The new retirement program, if successful, could trim an additional 20,000 positions, an AT&T spokesman said.
