Mediation Board Sets Cooling Off Period For Railroad Talks

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/06/90

WASHINGTON -- The National Mediation Board ordered a 30-day cooling-off period in the deadlocked railroad labor negotiations. The order blocks any strike at least until midnight May 5. It is another step in the strike-stopping procedures of the Railway Labor Act, under which the White House would eventually name an emergency panel that would develop recommendations for solving the health-cost issues and other matters in dispute. White House officials have said that President Bush is willing to name the emergency panel.

The president may name the panel as soon as the Mediation Board notifies the White House that the negotiating impasse threatens to disrupt interstate commerce. That notification is likely to occur soon.

An emergency panel usually has 30 days to come up with settlement recommendations, but the railroads and the unions say they would allow until Sept. 15, and longer if necessary. The parties also agreed they wouldn't take any action until 30 days after the emergency board reports, and in any event won't act if Congress isn't in session. Congress typically intervenes if the Railway Labor Act fails to prevent a strike.
