Imagine Films Says Its Annual Meeting Is Postponed Again

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 04/06/90

LOS ANGELES -- Imagine Films Entertainment Inc. said it postponed its annual meeting for the second time because it is still negotiating the termination of Robert Harris, president of motion pictures and television. The meeting was scheduled for 10 a.m. PDT and the company's explanation wasn't released until after the stock market closed. Meanwhile, Imagine shares climbed sharply to $16.25 each, up $1.50, in over-the-counter trading amid speculation the company was negotiating a merger.

The company's meeting originally was scheduled for March 21. Imagine Films, which was formed by producers Ron Howard and Brian Grazer five years ago and went public in 1986, has produced such movies as "Parenthood," "The Dream Team" and "The Burbs."

Mike Meltzer, Imagine's chief financial officer, said, "We aren't negotiating with anyone."

Mr. Meltzer said the stock may have climbed because the company's latest movie, "Cry-Baby," opens today and "the reviews have been good." He also noted that shares in New Line Cinema Corp. climbed before the release of the hit movie "Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles."

Mr. Meltzer said the postponement of the annual meeting was necessary since Mr. Harris is on the company's board. Mr. Harris wasn't available for comment.

Christopher Dixon, an analyst at Kidder Peabody & Co, said, "Many of the smaller film companies tend to trade up on the potential opening of movies."
