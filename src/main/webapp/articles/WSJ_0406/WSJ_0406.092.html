Ford Mustang Plant Shutdown Extended Due to Slow Sales

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/06/90

DETROIT -- Ford Motor Co. said it is extending for one week the shutdown of its Mustang car assembly plant because of slow sales. The Dearborn, Mich., production facility, which has been closed this week, is now scheduled to resume output April 16. Good Friday, April 13, is a holiday for virtually all U.S. auto workers.

Mustang sales so far this year are down 35% from year-ago levels. Some 2,100 hourly employees work at the Dearborn plant.

Chrysler Corp. closed all but one of its four U.S. car-assembly plants this week, idling a total of about 12,700 workers. The auto maker said all the plants are scheduled to reopen April 9. The only car plant that worked this week is a St. Louis facility that builds LeBaron and Daytona cars.

General Motors Corp. said it has scheduled daily and weekend overtime at its Janesville, Wis., and Lansing, Mich., assembly plants. Janesville builds the Chevrolet Cavalier and Lansing manufactures the Buick Skylark and Cutlass Calais. Overall, the 10 major U.S. auto makers plan to build 110,245 cars this week, down 30.7% from 159,001 a year ago.

In March, the manufacturers built 992,012 cars and trucks, down 3.6% from 1,028,993 vehicles in the same month a year ago. Car production fell 4.9% and truck output was off 1.2%.

Total U.S. car and truck output for the first quarter fell 21.4% compared with a year ago.
