International: De Benedetti May Be Near Sale of Belgium Firm Stake

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/06/90

PARIS -- Cerus SA, a holding company headed by Italian financier Carlo De Benedetti, declined to comment on a news report that it is near a sale of its 15.4% stake in Societe Generale de Belgique S.A. In the past, Cerus has sometimes scoffed at such reports. Meanwhile, a financial executive familiar with the situation suggested Mr. De Benedetti might be willing to take a loss on the sale of the shares under certain circumstances.

The report, in the Belgian daily Echo de la Bourse, suggested Cerus might sell the shares in three blocks, perhaps to Japanese and German buyers.

Cerus bought the shares during a failed 1988 battle to win control of SGB, a sprawling holding company for Belgian industrial and financial interests. The battle ended with Cie. Financiere de Suez of France gaining 51% of SGB. Since then, Cerus has said it was interested in selling its stake eventually. It has assumed, however, that Cerus would be very hesitant to sell for less than the average of about 3,500 Belgian francs ($99) a share that it paid in 1988.

On the Brussels Bourse yesterday, SGB closed at 3,320 Belgian francs a share, down 20. At the latest closing price, Cerus's stake is valued at the equivalent of about $935 million.
