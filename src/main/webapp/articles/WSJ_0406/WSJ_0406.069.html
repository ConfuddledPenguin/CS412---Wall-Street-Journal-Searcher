Help-Wanted Ad Volume May Stabilize, Board Says

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/06/90

NEW YORK -- The Conference Board, reporting that help-wanted ad volume continued to fall in February, said that it may begin to stabilize over the next few months. The Help-Wanted Advertising Index, a seasonally adjusted indicator of hiring plans, fell six points from January's 147 and eight points from 149 in December. The index was 14 points lower than in February 1989.

Labor demand was measurably lower in February 1990 from a year ago because of the slower pace of economic activity over the past 12 months, said Ken Goldstein, a Conference Board economist. "However, economic growth is not expected to erode from its present level," he said, "suggesting that recent declines in help-wanted advertising are unlikely to accelerate."

Ad volume, measured in 51 major U.S. newspapers, rose in three of nine regions. Leading was the West North Central region with a 6.8% increase. The East South Central and West South Central regions showed increases of 1.7% and 1.5%, respectively. Declines ranged from 6.9% in the East North Central region to 3.9% in the Pacific region.
