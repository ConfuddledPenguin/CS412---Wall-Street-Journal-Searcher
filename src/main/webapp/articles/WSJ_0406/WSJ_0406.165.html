Who's News: Unitrode's President Will Resign Posts, Chairman to Retire

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/06/90

LEXINGTON, Mass. -- Unitrode Corp., plagued with quality-control problems that prompted the Pentagon to ban taking shipments of some semiconductor products, said its president is resigning and its chairman will retire. The electronics company said Howard F. Wasserman, 45, president and chief executive officer, is resigning to "pursue other interests" and George M. Berman, 64, chairman and founder, is retiring. Both moves take effect June 5.

Robert L. Gable, 59, an outside director who headed a special committee overseeing an investigation into the quality-control problems, will become chairman and chief executive. Franklin P. Weigold, 51, who is a Unitrode vice president, will become president and chief operating officer.

Although Unitrode said the departures of Messrs. Wasserman and Berman are voluntary, analysts said the quality-control problems may have led to the change.

Mr. Wasserman said he was "frustrated by the lack of results I had been able to bring about in the last three years. The potential for the company to succeed is still very high and . . . some other people in the organization may be able to achieve it."

Mr. Gable is the former president of Computervision Corp., a Bedford, Mass., computer-aided design and manufacturing systems concern that was acquired by Prime Computer Inc. in 1988.

"Our immediate task is to restore {Unitrode} to profitability as soon as possible and to enhance its position as a dependable supplier of quality products," Mr. Gable said.

Partly because of the shipment ban, Unitrode posted a $20.6 million loss in the fiscal year ended Jan. 30.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Unitrode closed at $4.625, up 25 cents.
