Business Brief -- Oracle Systems Corp.: Shareholders File Lawsuits Over Decline in Stock Price

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/06/90

A dozen shareholder lawsuits have been filed against Belmont, Calif.-based software maker Oracle Systems Corp. after last week's 31% plunge in the company's stock price. The lead suit by stockholder John Coleman alleges that Oracle made false and misleading forecasts of its third-quarter earnings and that the company failed to disclose that two large contracts wouldn't be completed. Oracle called the suit "meritless."

In record one-day volume for an over-the-counter issue, Oracle stock tumbled March 28, the day after the company reported flat third-quarter earnings of 18 cents a share. Analysts had expected earnings would be between 25 cents and 29 cents a share.

The stock dropped $7.825 to close at $17.50 with nearly 21 million of the company's 129 million shares changing hands. The stock price peaked March 19, at $28.125.
