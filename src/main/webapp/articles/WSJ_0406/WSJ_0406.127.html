American General Is Granted Injunction Against Torchmark

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/06/90

HOUSTON -- U.S. District Judge David Hittner barred Torchmark Corp. from nominating five directors for American General Corp.'s board, a move that could stifle Torchmark's current bid to win influence with the insurance concern. Torchmark had proposed nominating candidates for five of the six American General seats that are up for election on the 15-member board at American General's annual meeting May 2.

However, American General argued that Torchmark failed to notify American General of its plans 60 days in advance of the meeting, as required by American General's bylaws.

In granting the preliminary injunction, the judge said that American General had shown that it had a substantial likelihood of proving its case, that the company would be injured if the injunction weren't granted and that the injunction wouldn't harm the public interest.

Torchmark immediately said it plans to appeal the preliminary injunction and said it still plans to solicit proxies for a resolution calling for American General to appoint a special committee to evaluate acquisition proposals. The resolution also calls for American General's board to hire an investment banking firm to solicit offers for American General.

Earlier this week, Torchmark, a Birmingham, Ala., insurer, dropped a $50-a-share, or $6.3 billion, unsolicited bid for American General after American General didn't respond to its offer within 48 hours. American General had said it would consider the offer at its next regularly scheduled board meeting.
