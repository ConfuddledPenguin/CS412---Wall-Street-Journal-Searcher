Business Brief -- Lionel Corp.: Trendex Capital Is Boosting Pressure on Concern to Sell

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/06/90

Trendex Capital Management Corp., representing a dissident group of Lionel Corp. shareholders, increased pressure on the toy retailer to find a buyer. The Fort Lauderdale, Fla.-based group represents 5% of Lionel's 13.5 million shares outstanding. It requested that New York-based Lionel remove any impediments to would-be acquirers, including cancellation of a standstill agreement with former suitor Robert I. Toussie, of New York. The group, in a filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission, also asked Lionel to empanel an independent committee to examine Lionel's relationship with Isaac Perlmutter and Bernard Marden.

In American Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Lionel closed at $4.25, unchanged.

Lionel officials couldn't be reached for comment. Nor could Messrs. Perlmutter and Marden be reached.

James DeBolt, of Trendex, said the group has no intention of launching its own takeover bid for Lionel but will do whatever is necessary to maximize shareholder value.
