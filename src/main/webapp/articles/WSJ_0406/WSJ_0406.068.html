Conditions That Cut Nordson's Earnings Continue This Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/06/90

WESTLAKE, Ohio -- Nordson Corp. said conditions that reduced profit in the first quarter ended Jan. 28 are continuing in the second quarter. William P. Madar, president and chief executive officer, said weaker demand for Nordson's core products, greater sales of new products that carry lower profit margins and the effects of the stronger U.S. dollar continue to affect earnings.

Nordson, which produces equipment to apply adhesives and coatings, declined to make a specific earnings forecast for the quarter ending this month.

In the 1990 first quarter, net income fell 17% to $6.7 million, or 70 cents a share, from $8.1 million, or 84 cents a share, a year earlier. Sales increased 19% to $77.5 million from $65 million.

For the second quarter of fiscal 1989, Nordson earned $9.7 million, or 99 cents a share, on sales of $73.5 million. More than half of the company's sales are overseas, but production is mainly in the U.S.; that makes the company vulnerable when the value of the dollar rises against foreign currencies. The dollar has risen sharply against the yen this year, and Nordson does a sizable business in Japan.
