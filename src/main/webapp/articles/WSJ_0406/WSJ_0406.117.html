Westinghouse to Sell Soft-Drink Business

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/06/90

PITTSBURGH -- Westinghouse Electric Corp. said it agreed to sell its soft-drink beverage business to a group led by management of the unit. In addition, Westinghouse said it will purchase a 15% stake in the new company. Terms weren't disclosed. A Westinghouse spokesman said the company expects to record a gain from the sale, which is expected to close in the current quarter. However, the spokesman declined to be more specific.

Financing will be provided by Citicorp Venture Capital Ltd., a unit of Citibank; Hong Capital Investors Limited Partnership, managed by Hong & Co., of Richmond, Va.; and other institutional investors, Westinghouse said.

In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange, Westinghouse closed at $74.875, down 25 cents.

The divestiture of the beverage group, a leading bottler of brands such as 7-Up and Royal Crown, had been expected. Sale of the unit, which has estimated annual sales of about $400 million, marks the last major divestiture expected to be made during the tenure of John C. Marous, chairman and chief executive officer. Mr. Marous will retire later this year, and will be succeeded by Paul E. Lego, president and chief operating officer.

The beverage group, based in Vernon, Calif., also bottles and distributes A&W Root Beer, Sunkist Orange, Hawaiian Punch and Seagram's Wine Coolers. It also distributes Perrier and Evian bottled water, the company said.

Last year, Mr. Marous signaled that the beverage group was on the block because it had been hurt by pricing competition within the soft-drink industry.

In the past three years, Westinghouse has shed businesses representing $2.64 billion in annual revenue while acquiring more than 70 smaller businesses with revenue of about $1.5 billion.

Westinghouse has interests in broadcasting, defense electronics, community development, financial services and over-the-road refrigeration.
