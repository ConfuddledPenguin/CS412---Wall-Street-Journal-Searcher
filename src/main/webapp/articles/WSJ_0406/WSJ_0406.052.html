Technology: Genesis Is a Partner With Soviet Institutes To Develop Inventions

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/06/90

CAMBRIDGE, Mass. -- Genesis Technology Group Inc. said it formed a partnership with a group of Soviet biological institutes to commercialize the institutes' technology in the West and Japan. Genesis, a closely held concern, said it formed the partnership, BioGenesis International, with Biogen, a group of 26 Soviet biological institutes. Biogen, formed in 1988, is unrelated to a biotechnology concern with the same name based here.

The partnership will seek to raise $10 million from drug companies and other concerns outside the Soviet Union to fund development of Soviet inventions in medicine, biotechnology and agriculture, Genesis said. The partnership, which will have offices here, in Moscow and in Kiev, U.S.S.R., also will seek to arrange for the companies to pay for research at the Soviet institutes in return for rights to resulting inventions.
