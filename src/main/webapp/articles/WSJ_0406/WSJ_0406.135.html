Business Brief -- BFS Bankorp Inc.: Share Price Jumps as Rift With Shareholder Is Settled

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/06/90

BFS Bankorp Inc. shares jumped 11% as the New York-based holding company for Bankers Federal Savings settled litigation with a dissident shareholder. In national over-the-counter trading yesterday, the shares rose $1 to close at $10.

Under terms of the agreement, the holder, First Capital Group Inc., a Redondo Beach, Calif., investment firm, will relinquish claim to the BFS board seat it won in a proxy fight. BFS had filed suit to void the proxies that First Capital solicited in trying to gain a directorship. Eldon C. Hanes continues as chairman at BFS.

BFS now will drop the lawsuit, agreeing to reimburse some of First Capital's expenses related to the proxy solicitation. First Capital, in turn, won't acquire any BFS stock for five years.

First Capital also will sell its shares to Fredric H. Gould, who is to be named BFS's sixth director. BFS has backed a plan under which Mr. Gould, Gould Investors L.P. and BRT Realty Trust would seek clearance to boost their stake in the thrift to as much as 24.9% from 9.9%. Mr. Gould is chairman of BRT and a general partner of Gould Investors.
