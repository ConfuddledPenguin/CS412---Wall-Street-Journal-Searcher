Marketing & Media: Dawn Steele Will Produce Diverse Projects for Disney

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/06/90

BURBANK, Calif. -- Dawn Steele, who resigned as president of Columbia Pictures earlier this year, has landed at Walt Disney Co., which said she will produce movies and television shows and develop other projects. Ms. Steele, in her early 40s, had been rumored for several months to be leaning toward a relationship with Disney that might include the establishment of combination day-care and family-entertainment centers.

"She wants to do a lot of things in her life; this is a place where she can fulfill any dreams she has," said David Hoberman, president of Touchstone Pictures, part of Disney's Walt Disney Studios unit. There haven't been any discussions about specific films or other projects, he said.

Ms. Steel's "exclusive" arrangement with Disney includes TV production, which she hasn't done before, Mr. Hoberman said. She left Columbia last fall after the company was purchased by Sony Corp. Ms. Steele was the second woman after Sherry Lansing, who oversaw what was then Twentieth Century-Fox Film Corp.'s movie division, to head a Hollywood studio.
