Ex-Texscan Officers Are Sued by the SEC

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/24/90

The Securities and Exchange Commission said it filed motions for judgment of civil contempt against Robert J. Palle Jr., the former president of Texscan Corp., and James A. Luksch, a former Texscan vice president, for failure to pay fines associated with their 1989 settlement of insider trading charges. In 1987, the Securities and Exchange Commission charged Texscan and six of its former officials with insider trading in connection with the sale of 40,200 Texscan shares ahead of negative news about the company's financial health. The civil suit, filed in federal court in Phoenix, Ariz., alleged that the defendants materially overstated the company's financial results for its fiscal years ending April 30, 1984 and 1985, and that certain officers used nearly $750,000 in advances from the company for their personal use.

Texscan, which in 1987 was a Scottsdale, Ariz., maker of cable television equipment operating under the protection of the federal Bankruptcy Code, has since moved to El Paso, Texas.

Mr. Palle, who hasn't paid $12,416 owed, and Mr. Luksch, who has failed to pay $14,287, were ordered to pay the amounts by last year, the SEC said. Messrs. Palle and Luksch couldn't be reached to comment.
