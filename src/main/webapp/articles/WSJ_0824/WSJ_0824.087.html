Retailers, Nike Say Boycott Isn't Harming Firm's Sales

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/24/90

Operation PUSH's call for a nationwide boycott of Nike Inc. athletic shoes and other products apparently hasn't had much impact so far. Several retailers who sell Nike items report that consumers for the most part are ignoring the civil rights group's boycott call. Many customers don't even seem aware of it, they say.

Nike's stock has taken a pounding since the boycott was announced, dropping $2 yesterday in over-the-counter trading to close at $62. But analysts said the declines had more to do with investor nervousness over the possibility of war in the Middle East and fears that a recession is imminent.

Ray Cabillot, an analyst with Piper Jaffray & Hopwood, said he hasn't altered his forecast that Nike will earn $7.25 a share on revenue of $2.7 billion for the year ending next May. Last fiscal year, Nike earned $243 million, or $6.42 a share, on revenue of $2.2 billion.

A spokeswoman for the athletic apparel maker said sales in Chicago, where Operation PUSH is based, have increased since the boycott call.

PUSH called for the boycott because it says Nike gets about 30% of its more than $2 billion in annual revenue from black customers but invests little in black communities. Last week, Nike announced a series of affirmative-action programs that PUSH officials then denounced as inadequate.

A spokeswoman for PUSH said its boycott is working and that the impact on sales probably won't show up until Nike's next quarter.
