Itoh, Chisso Get Job for Plant

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/24/90

TOKYO -- Two Japanese companies, the trading concern C. Itoh & Co. and the chemical plant engineering firm Chisso Engineering Co., received an order valued at about 2.3 billion yen ($15.7 million) from Slovnaft State Enterprise of Czechoslovakia to build a chemical plant in Bratislava, officials of the companies said. Itoh and Chisso said the plant, to be completed in the beginning of 1992, will produce isoparaffin, an additive for use in unleaded, high-octane gasoline. With the new plant, officials said, Czechoslovakia is expected to switch as much as 60% of its domestic demand to unleaded gasoline from heavy dependence on leaded gas.

Coinciding with a move in Europe to prevent environmental pollution, as well as with Czechoslovakia's auto production project, the new plant will process 250,000 metric tons of isoparaffin annually. A metric ton is equal to about 2,204.62 pounds.
