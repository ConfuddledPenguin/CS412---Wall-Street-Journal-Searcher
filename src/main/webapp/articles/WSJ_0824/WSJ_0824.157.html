Marketing & Media: New York Post's Salembier Quits as Paper's President

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/24/90

NEW YORK (AP) -- The owner of the New York Post announced the resignation of Valerie Salembier as president of the newspaper. Ms. Salembier, the former publisher of TV Guide, was named to the post in April 1989.

Peter Kalikow, the Post's owner and publisher, said he would reassume the title of president.

Ms. Salembier, who supervised business, advertising and circulation, said she felt it was time "to move forward to new challenges and opportunities."

"She has successfully structured a business operation which has effectively positioned the New York Post for sales and profit growth in the '90s," Mr. Kalikow said.

Last November, the Post discontinued its eight-month-old Sunday edition, which failed to attract sufficient advertisers or readers and lost millions of dollars.
