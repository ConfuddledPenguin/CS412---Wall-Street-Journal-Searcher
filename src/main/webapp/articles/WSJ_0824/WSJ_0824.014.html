Reebok's Avia Unit Picks Mitsui & Co. For Japan Market

  • Author: Joseph Pereira
  • Date: 08/24/90

PORTLAND, Ore. -- Avia Group International Inc., a unit of Reebok International Ltd., said it named Mitsui & Co. as the exclusive distributor of its athletic shoes and apparel in Japan. Under terms of the agreement, the shoes, shirts, shorts and other apparel items, will be marketed under the Avia name by Mitsui, a $126 billion trading company that owns a number of health clubs and markets lines of skis, golf clubs and bowling equipment.

Mitsui will market Avia's products through its Bussan Sports Academy subsidiary.

Approximately $9 million of Avia's $200 million in sales last year came from Japan, analysts said. Backed by the name of a big Japanese firm, Avia's Japan sales are expected to grow by about 50% each year, reaching about $30 million in three years, analysts estimate.

Avia is also negotiating deals with Yugoslavia and Czechoslovakia, said Harry de Boer, Avia's international vice president. Agreements are expected to be signed soon. Avia's international division, which last year had sales of $35 million, is expecting growth of about 40% this year, and better than 50% in each of the next two years, Mr. de Boer added. Currently, it does 75% of its business in Canada, the U.K., Germany and Spain.

Avia's international growth comes at a time of retrenchment in its domestic division. The company's overall sales last year of about $200 million were flat, though at one time it had visions of selling $250 million of products.

Avia shoes sell on the higher end of the retail scale -- between $50 and $90 a pair. Competition in that league is stiff, especially since it must rival companies, including its parent Reebok, that have advertising budgets two to three times in size. Avia spent about $20 million in advertising and promotions in 1989.

As a result, the company has scaled back its overall growth expectations for the next couple of years, taking the time to reposition itself on the marketing front. For example, next year it will stop making sneakers for tiny tots and younger children, focusing instead on making greater inroads in the adult market. As part of the regrouping effort, the company trimmed the size of its staff by 30% earlier this year.

Its "For Athletic Use Only" advertising campaign will also be "less exclusive and elitist," a company spokeswoman said.

Reebok International is based in Stoughton, Mass.
