Eastern Airlines Launches Frequent-Flier Promotion

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/24/90

MIAMI -- Eastern Airlines, in another attempt to lure business travelers, launched a frequent-flier promotion designed to award free tickets more quickly than competitors' programs. Under "Business Break," Eastern passengers will receive two free round-trip tickets to Honolulu, Cancun, St. Thomas or St. Croix after seven non-excursion round trips on Eastern. Other airlines' programs generally require more travel before awarding free tickets.

Eastern, which is being operated by a trustee appointed by the federal Bankruptcy Court, also said it will schedule special flights exclusively for "Business Break" award recipients and that bonus travel will be available between Dec. 14 and April 14, generally a peak period for those Hawaiian, Mexican and Caribbean destinations. A spokeswoman said the chartered planes will make it possible for Eastern to fly to Honolulu, the most popular destination for frequent fliers, even though the airline doesn't offer scheduled service to that city.

Eastern entered Chapter 11 bankruptcy-law protection last year after a strike grounded nearly all its flights and has been trying to rebuild its operations.
