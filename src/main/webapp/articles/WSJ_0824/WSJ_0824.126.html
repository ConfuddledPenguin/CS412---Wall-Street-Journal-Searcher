Business Brief -- Justin Industries Inc.: Holder Group Seeks Vote On Planned Lama Purchase

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/24/90

A group of disgruntled investors asked Justin Industries Inc. to put to a shareholder vote the company's proposed acquisition of Tony Lama Co. In a letter to the Justin board, Perry H. Sutherland of Kansas City, Mo., and Barry S. Rosenstein of New York, leaders of a group that owns about 11.8% of Justin, called the proposed transaction "astonishing" and said they would "review all of our options to protect our 1,000,000-plus share investment in the company and that of our fellow shareholders." An offer by the group to purchase the shares of Justin it didn't already own was rejected this year by the Justin board.

In Dallas, Justin declined to comment on the request for a vote.

Justin has agreed to acquire Tony Lama, based in El Paso, Texas, for $9 a share, or about $18 million, plus the assumption of about $34 million in debt. In the first half of 1990, Tony Lama had a loss of $901,000, or 47 cents a share, after a loss of $1.8 million, or 95 cents a share, in 1989. Both companies make Western boots; Justin also makes building supplies and industrial equipment.
