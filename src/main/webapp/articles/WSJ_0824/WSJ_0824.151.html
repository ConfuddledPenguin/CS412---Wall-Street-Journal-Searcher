International Brief -- Hongkong & Shanghai Banking: Hang Seng Bank Unit's Net Increased 17% in First Half

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/24/90

Hang Seng Bank Ltd. reported that consolidated profit after taxes and transfers to inner reserves spurted 17% to 651 million Hong Kong dollars (US$83.7 million) in the first half from HK$556 million (US$71 million) a year earlier, helped by the modest rebound of the local economy. The bank, a unit of Hongkong & Shanghai Banking Corp., said share earnings rose 17% to 65.7 cents from 56.2 cents. Its directors proposed raising the interim dividend to 27 cents from 22.5 cents.

Hang Seng, based in Hong Kong, cited improved deposits and loans, driven by the local economy's improvement over the course of the half. It expects its full-year dividend to reach at least HK$1.20 following 1989's HK$1 payout.

Keith Wu, senior research manager at Citicorp Scrimgeour Vickers Hong Kong Ltd., termed the results "extremely encouraging," and reflective of the group's success in capitalizing on high interest rates.

Mr. Wu said the bank generally manipulates its figures so it can report a stronger performance in the second half than the interim period. But he quickly dismissed the notion that the results portend well for its parent, which reports Tuesday and is expected to be hard hit by its operations in the U.S. and Australia.
