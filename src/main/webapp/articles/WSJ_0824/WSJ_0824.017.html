Initial State Jobless Claims Increased in Aug. 11 Week

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/24/90

WASHINGTON -- Initial claims for regular state unemployment benefits rose to a seasonally adjusted 366,000 in the week ended Aug. 11 from 341,000 in the previous week, the Labor Department said. The number of people receiving regular state benefits in the week ended Aug. 4 was a seasonally adjusted 2,445,000, or 2.3% of those covered by unemployment insurance, down from 2,455,000 in the previous week, when the insured unemployment rate also was 2.3%.

Counting all state and federal benefit programs, the number of people receiving unemployment benefits in the week ended Aug. 4 fell to 2,369,900 from 2,387,500 a week earlier. These figures aren't seasonally adjusted.
