Who's News: Noranda Names Kerr To Additional Post Of Chief Executive

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/24/90

TORONTO -- Noranda Inc. named its president, David Kerr, to the additional position of chief executive officer, succeeding Alfred Powis, who remains chairman. Mr. Powis, 59 years old, said the executive change was made last month, but wasn't made public at the time because "it's not regarded by the board or anybody else as being a big deal. I don't think it will change anything particularly."

An internal notice about the change had been circulated to the staff of Noranda, which is a diversified resource concern.

Mr. Powis said the executive change was initiated by Noranda's major shareholder, Brascan Ltd., a holding company controlled by Toronto's Bronfman family. Mr. Powis said the move is consistent with changes taking place throughout the Bronfman group of companies to make the chairmen non-executive officers.

Mr. Powis, who served as chief executive at Noranda for 22 years, said that as chairman, he will continue to focus on long-term strategic issues. He said Mr. Kerr will continue to look after the company's day-to-day operations.

Mr. Kerr, 46, has served as Noranda president since 1987. He previously served in executive positions at Brascan and other Bronfman group companies.
