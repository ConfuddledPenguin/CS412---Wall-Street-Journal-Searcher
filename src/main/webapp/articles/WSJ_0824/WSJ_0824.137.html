Business Brief -- Hutchison Whampoa Ltd.: Hong Kong Firm's Profit For First Half Rose 16%

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/24/90

Hutchison Whampoa Ltd., Hong Kong, reported that first-half profit after taxes climbed 16% to 1.12 billion Hong Kong dollars (US$143.9 million) from HK$964 million a year earlier, buoyed by the strength of its core businesses. The conglomerate, controlled by the local tycoon Li Ka Shing, said per-share earnings also grew 16% to 37 cents from 32 cents. But profit after extraordinary items fell 56% to HK$1.49 billion, or 49 Hong Kong cents a share, from HK$3.42 billion, or HK$1.13 a share. The company recorded a one-time gain of HK$368 million from the sale of its engineering units, which was dwarfed by the HK$2.45 billion gain a year earlier from the sale of shipping terminal interests. Operating profit grew 11% to HK$1.25 billion from HK$1.13 billion.

Revenue rose slightly to HK$6.4 billion from HK$6.34 billion.

Mr. Li, Hutchison's chairman, praised the company's performance and said profit before special items for the full year should show an improvement, compared with the year-earlier HK$3.03 billion.
