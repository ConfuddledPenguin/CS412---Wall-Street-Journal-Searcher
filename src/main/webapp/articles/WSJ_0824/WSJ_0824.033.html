Letters to the Editor: The Business Of Selling Businesses

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/24/90

Bryan Jamison's distorted views of the business brokerage industry (Manager's Journal, editorial page, July 30) require comment. As the president of a business sales firm, I am truly amazed that a part-time president of a small manufacturing company felt he was so well versed in the art of business acquisitions that he would proceed to paint everyone in the business sales industry with the same narrow-minded brush. It is possible that the brokers Mr. Jamison dealt with were well aware he was looking to get "great mileage at cocktail parties" by mentioning his plans to acquire another company. What a professional business broker has to offer to a serious buyer is invaluable. Please, Mr. Jamison, next time you're looking for party chatter, stick to the repaving of your parking lot. With any luck you won't be satisfied with the job the paving company does for you and in your next column you can bad-mouth every paving contractor in the country.

John J. Halvorsen

President

Business Options Inc.

South Windsor, Conn.
