Business Brief -- Boeing Co.: First of 2 Jets to Replace Air Force One Is Delivered

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/24/90

The first of two 747-200B jetliners built by Seattle-based Boeing Co. to replace the aging Air Force One presidential plane was delivered yesterday to Andrews Air Force Base, Md. The planes have taken five years to produce at a cost of $262 million, and will replace the Boeing 707-320B used for nearly 30 years. The aerospace concern said it hopes to deliver the second, identical presidential transport before year end.

Much larger than the old "Flying Oval Office" first used by the late President John F. Kennedy, the new planes can carry as many as 70 passengers and 23 crew members, require 35% shorter runways, have a range of more than 7,140 miles, are equipped for air-to-air refueling, and have an enhanced communications system for sending or receiving data through normal or secured channels world-wide. They also have a conference/dining room, quarters for the president and first lady, an office area for senior staff, and another office that can be converted into a medical facility.
