  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/24/90

HARTFORD, Conn. -- United Technologies Corp.'s Pratt & Whitney Canada unit said the American Eagle commuter arm of American Airlines ordered its PW100 turboprop engines to power 100 new aircraft. Terms of the order weren't disclosed. The airplanes are a combination of ATR 42s and ATR 72s, two versions of a regional aircraft built by Aerospatiale of France and Aeritalia of Italy.

Pratt & Whitney Canada produces gas turbine engines for general aviation and regional markets.
