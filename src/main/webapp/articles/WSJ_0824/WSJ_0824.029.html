International: Britain Breaks Off Talks With Hanson on Powergen

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/24/90

LONDON -- The British government broke off talks with Hanson PLC about the sale of state-owned PowerGen and said it will sell the country's second-largest electricity generator through a public offering instead. The government's abrupt flip-flop came one month after it said it might shelve the long-planned flotation -- part of the Thatcher government's #11 billion ($21.19 billion) electric-utility privatization -- because of Hanson's "firm interest" in bidding for PowerGen. Since then, Hanson executives have pored over the PowerGen books while the government has endured criticism that Hanson, a longtime government contributor, was being given unfair advantage in the sale.

Energy Secretary John Wakeham indicated yesterday that the government's decision to proceed with a sale to the public was based on price. The Energy Department said Mr. Wakeham concluded the terms of any sale to Hanson or another single bidder "now offer insufficient benefit compared with flotation," scheduled for next February. Analysts have said the PowerGen sale could raise #1.1 billion or more.

The department's statement indicated the government relied on new information from a recent PowerGen board report that boosted profit projections. The board has been hostile to the idea of a Hanson takeover.

Hanson declined to say what it had been prepared to pay or whether it had submitted a formal bid.
