New Securities Issues

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/24/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report. CORPORATES

Merrill Lynch & Co. -- $100 million of senior notes, due Sept. 4, 1993, priced at par to yield 9.30%. The noncallable notes were priced at a spread of 88 basis points above the Treasury's three-year note. Rated single-A-2 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and single-A by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets.

Rohm & Haas Co. -- $100 million of 9 7/8% notes, due Sept. 1, 2000, priced at 99.53 to yield 9.95%. The non-callable notes were priced at a spread of 95 basis points above the Treasury's 10-year note. Rated single-A-1 by Moody's and single-A by S&P, the issue will be sold through Goldman Sachs & Co.

MUNICIPAL

Texas -- $550 million of 12-month tax and revenue anticipation notes, Series 1990, due Aug. 30, 1991, won by seven municipal underwriting firms. Final pricing information as received from each of the underwriters is as follows: $200 million of notes purchased by a Merrill Lynch Capital Markets group. These notes carry a 6 1/2% coupon and are priced to yield 6.18%; $150 million notes were won by a BT Securities Corp. group. These carry a 6 1/2% coupon and are priced to yield 6.20%.; $100 million of 6 1/2% notes were won by a Morgan Stanley & Co. group, and are priced to yield 6.20%; $40 million of 6 1/2% notes were won by a J.P. Morgan Securities Inc., and are priced to yield 6.18%; $25 million of 6 1/2% notes were won by a Prudential-Bache Capital Funding group, and are priced to yield 6.20%; $25 million of 6 1/2% notes also were won by a Smith Barney Harris Upham & Co. group, and are priced to yield 6.20.%; $10 million of 6 1/2% notes were won by a Bears Stearns & Co. group, and aren't formally reoffered. The notes are rated MIG-1 by Moody's and SP-1-plus by S&P.

State of New York Mortgage Agency Homeowner -- $123 million of mortgage revenue bonds, Series RR, due Oct. 1, 2010 and 2017 and Series SS, due April 1, and Oct. 1, 1992-2000, and due Oct. 1, 2019 and 2022. The bonds are priced to yield from 6.20% in 1992 to 7.95% in 2022. The final pricing reflects a repricing that raised the yields on some issues by five to 10 basis points.

MORTGAGE-BACKED

American Housing Trust VII -- $293 million of mortgage pass-through certificates, backed by Vendee Loans, offered by the Department of Veterans Affairs through First Boston Corp. Vendee loans are issued to finance the sale of VA-owned properties acquired as a result of VA loan defaults. The offering has been structured to yield from 9.337%, a spread of 95 basis points over the three-year Treasury note, to a yield of 10.67%%, a spread of 155 basis points over the 30-year Treasury bond. The collateral consists of Vendee loans with a weighted average coupon rate of 9.462% and weighted average remaining term to maturity of 28.92 years. The issue is rated triple-A by S&P and Moody's.

OTHER

Federal Farm Credit Banks -- $200 million of notes, due Sept. 7, 1995, priced at par for an initial yield of 8.94%. The notes, which are non-callable for two years, will be offered at various prices. The size of the FFCB issue was increased to $200 million from $150 million after pricing through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets. The notes were priced at a spread of 31 basis points above the Treasury's when-issued five-year note.

Student Loan Marketing Association -- $150 million of notes, due Sept. 13, 1993, priced at par to yield 8.65%. The notes, which are non-callable for one year, were priced at a spread of 23 basis points above the Treasury's one-year note through underwriters led by Morgan Stanley & Co.

EUROBONDS

Best Denki Co. (Japan) -- $190 million four-year bonds due Sept. 14, 1994, with equity warrants, via Nikko Europe. Interest 4 7/8% annually, issue price at par. Each $5,000 bond carries one warrant exercisable to buy shares at expected 2.5% premium over closing share price when exercise terms Aug. 28.

Fuji Electric Co. (Japan) -- 350 million marks of bonds with equity-purchase warrants with a fixed 5 1/4% coupon due Sept. 6, 1994 and priced at par, via Deutsche Bank. Each 5,000 mark bond carries five warrants, exercisable from Oct. 2, 1990 to Aug. 30, 1994, to buy company shares at expected 2 1/2% premium above the closing share price when prices are fixed Aug. 29. Each 50,000 mark bond carries 50 warrants attached exercisable under the same terms.

Copyer Co. (Japan) -- 70 million marks of bonds with equity-purchase warrants with a fixed 5 1/4% coupon due Sept. 20, 1994 and priced at par, via Bayerische Vereinsbank. Guaranteed by Sumitomo Bank Ltd. Each 5,000 mark bond carries one warrant and one certificate with four warrants attached, exercisable from Oct. 4, 1990 to Sept. 6, 1994, to buy company shares at an expected premium of 2 1/2% above the closing share price when prices are fixed Aug. 30.
