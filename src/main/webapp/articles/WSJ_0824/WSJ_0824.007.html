Letters to the Editor: The Perils of ERM

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/24/90

George Melloan paints a distorted picture of the exchange-rate mechanism (ERM) of the European Monetary System (Global View, op-ed page, July 16). This mechanism makes many European currencies artificially strong by tying them to the deutsche mark. But Mr. Melloan states, "Strong currencies have certainly done no harm to the industries of France, the Netherlands, Ireland and Austria." As evidence, he cites their impressive economic growth rates. He then argues that Britain, which has remained outside the ERM, has no compelling reason not to join: "If having a strong currency did none of these countries any harm, what exactly is England's problem?" The track record of strong-currency ERM nations is not as nice as Mr. Melloan suggests. For example, the average annual growth rate of the economy in France was lower from 1979-87 than for the pre-ERM years 1970-78. The same was true for Italy. Evidence also suggests that the average rate of unemployment in ERM countries has been higher and has declined more slowly than in non-ERM countries.

Moreover, the clearest supposed benefit of ERM membership -- price stability -- may not be so clear at all. While inflation has been reduced dramatically in ERM nations, it has fallen by a similar amount in the rest of the industrialized world. Inflation rates in ERM nations are now somewhat lower than in many non-ERM nations, but the disinflation process was much slower for ERM members.

Mr. Melloan concludes by wondering why Prime Minister Margaret Thatcher has been so reluctant to place the pound in the ERM, ". . . what is England waiting for . . . given the good track record of the other ERM nations? . . . Presumably, Mrs. Thatcher knows the answer, but she isn't telling." Yet, perhaps Mrs. Thatcher has been hesitant precisely because of the track record of other ERM nations. Existing evidence suggests that this reluctance merits praise, not derision.

Christopher L. Culp

Associate Policy Analyst

Competitive Enterprise Institute

Washington
