Business Brief -- Comfed Bancorp Inc.: Firm Hires Goldman Sachs To Help Restructure Unit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/24/90

Comfed Bancorp Inc. said it retained Goldman, Sachs & Co. to assist in the restructuring of its Comfed Savings Bank unit. The company also said it signed a consent agreement with the government's Office of Thrift Supervision. Comfed, Lowell, Mass., said it hired Goldman as part of its plan to apply for open thrift assistance through the Federal Deposit Insurance Corp. Goldman will seek new sources of equity capital through a private investment offering begun this week.

The agreement with regulators places certain limitations on the bank and establishes the government office's authority to appoint a receiver or conservator if deemed appropriate. The agreement allows the company to conduct retail banking, residential mortgage lending and loan servicing, which Comfed said was "evidence of the regulators' desire to avoid such conservatorship at this time."

Comfed, with $1.4 billion in assets, also announced that it named Jack Zoeller, president and chief executive, to the added post of chairman. He succeeds Jefferson Allen, who resigned June 30.
