Business Brief -- Flagship Financial Corp.: Unit Asset Sale, Write-Off, Faster Reserve Boost Set

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/24/90

Flagship Financial Corp., Jenkintown, Pa., said it plans to close or sell the five retail offices of its First American Savings unit in New Jersey, write off $10 million of good will, and accelerate the buildup of its loan-loss reserves. Flagship estimated the effect of these actions would be a charge of $13 million against third-quarter earnings that would be partly offset by future improvements in earnings, lower quarterly expenses and reduced taxes. In the 1989 third quarter, Flagship earned $1.5 million, or 42 cents a share, including a gain of $312,000, or nine cents a share, on the sale of assets.

Flagship said it decided that First American couldn't meet profit targets in the New Jersey market "in a reasonable time," so the company will concentrate instead on expanding in suburban Pennsylvania markets.
