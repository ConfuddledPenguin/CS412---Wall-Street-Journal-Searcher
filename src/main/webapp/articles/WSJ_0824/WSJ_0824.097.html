Who's News: Liz Claiborne and Husband Leave Firm They Founded

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/24/90

NEW YORK -- Liz Claiborne Inc. said two of the company's founders, designer Elizabeth Claiborne Ortenberg and her husband, Arthur Ortenberg, resigned from its board. Walter Krieger, vice president of financial operations at the women's apparel maker, said directors haven't yet considered who will succeed the couple on the nine-member board. He wouldn't comment on whether the resignations had been expected.

The couple retired from active management roles in May 1989. Before that, Ms. Claiborne Ortenberg had been president and chairman, and Mr. Ortenberg, vice chairman.

In a statement, the couple said they were retiring from the board because of "growing pressures" on their time from their charitable foundation, which is active on environmental and social issues.

The couple expressed confidence in the strategy planned by the company's chairman, Jerome A. Chazen, another founder and the company's largest inside shareholder.

In the statement, Mr. Chazen said the company respected the couple's decision, which he described as "obviously a difficult one." He said the couple's values will remain at the core of the company's culture.

In national over-the-counter trading, Liz Claiborne shares fell 25 cents yesterday to close at $23.50.
