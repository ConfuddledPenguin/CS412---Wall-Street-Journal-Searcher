REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: Environmental Hazard

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/24/90

If some environmentalists have their way, the decision to have a tooth filled may soon require people to grapple with decisions of a much larger order, such as their burial plans. According to a recent report in Nature magazine, the increasing use of cremation poses environmental problems related to fillings. It seems the crematoria heat causes the dental amalgams to release harmful mercury vapor in the air. Getting yourself and your fillings buried in the ground, on the other hand, would be relatively harmless, argues Dr. Allan Mills, the author of this study. While investigators have been preoccupied with the dangers that amalgam materials might pose to patients, Dr. Mills notes, "none of the investigators seems to have considered the nature of the final disposal of the teeth and their owners."

We'd bet that a whole lot of people haven't been thinking much about this particular disposal problem, but concerned environmentalists needn't worry. They can either opt for burial in the ground or, if they insist on cremation, they can consider Dr. Mills's suggestion that their filled teeth be extracted and safely disposed of. How and when these extractions are to take place, Dr. Mills does not say. Will dentists be rushed to the deathbed to remove fillings, as family members gather round for this final gesture toward the environment? Will Medicare pick up the costs? These questions have yet to be answered.
