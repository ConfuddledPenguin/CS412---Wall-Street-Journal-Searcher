Cedar Group Expects Gardenbolt Purchase Will Pay Off Soon

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/24/90

PHILADELPHIA -- Cedar Group Inc. expects to realize marketing and management benefits "almost immediately" from its acquisition of Gardenbolt International Corp., said Lawrence Rutstein, chairman. Earlier this week, Cedar Group announced its agreement to acquire Gardenbolt, a Sayerville, N.J., distributor of imported fasteners, for $5.5 million. Gardenbolt had 1989 sales of about $26 million. The transaction is expected to be completed next month.

Mr. Rutstein said Cedar Group believes the move will help the company expand its market share significantly at a time when the industrial-fasteners industry is consolidating and sales growth is generally "stagnant or flat." In the U.S., industry sales of imported nuts, bolts and other fasteners exceeded $1 billion in 1989.

"Gardenbolt is a major step for us," Mr. Rutstein said in an interview. "It puts us into the top tier of distributors in the country."

Cedar Group would also gain an experienced management team "that can help us as we make additional acquisitions," Mr. Rutstein said. Under terms of the agreement, Steven Schonholtz, president and chief executive of Gardenbolt, will become executive vice president and chief operating officer of Cedar Group.

Mr. Rutstein said Gardenbolt, with more than 50 years of experience in the fasteners industry, has "an excellent reputation that can help us attract other companies to our nest."

Moreover, the product-line mixes of the two companies complement each other. Cedar Group, which anticipates sales in the range of $18 million to $20 million in fiscal 1991 ending Jan. 31, concentrates on bulk marketing of internally threaded products, such as nuts and washers.

In contrast, Gardenbolt handles a broad line of screws and externally threaded bolts. It generally sells them in smaller quantities at higher margins and to different categories of customers. "We'll have a wider product line for a wider customer base," Mr. Rutstein said. As another advantage, he cited Cedar Group's ability to expand beyond its traditional distribution centers in Philadlephia, Chicago and Houston by tapping into the Gardenbolt network.

Mr. Rutstein declined to make any specific forecasts for fiscal 1991 sales or earnings, but he indicated that Gardenbolt would contribute immediately to profit.

Combined annualized sales would reach about $45 million, he said. But depending on the timing of the transaction's close, Gardenbolt may add sales of only about $10 million in the current fiscal year.
