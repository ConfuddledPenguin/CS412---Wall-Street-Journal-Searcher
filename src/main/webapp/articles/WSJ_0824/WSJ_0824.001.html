Florida Seeks Details Of Times Publishing's Stock Pact With Bass

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/24/90

TALLAHASSEE, Fla. -- Florida Attorney General Robert A. Butterworth is seeking details of a secret stock agreement between Times Publishing Co. and a group including Texas billionaire Robert M. Bass. Mr. Butterworth said he is concerned the agreement, announced last week, may have harmful effects on the Poynter Institute, a St. Petersburg educational foundation that owns a majority stake in Times Publishing. And Mr. Butterworth, in a letter to the institute, said he may take the matter to court, if necessary.

Under the stock agreement, Times Publishing, the parent company of the St. Petersburg Times, is to buy back a 40% stake owned by the Bass group. The parties agreed not to disclose certain terms of the transaction, including the purchase price. As part of the accord, the Bass group also agreed to drop a lawsuit seeking to force the redemption of a preferred stock issue.

However, the parties did disclose that under the pact, preferred stock would be redeemed. And that's what concerns Mr. Butterworth. He said the Poynter Institute petitioned his office earlier this year for help in litigation, claiming that any redemption of the preferred stock would severely limit the institute's future charitable educational activities. The Poynter Institute had been receiving dividends from the preferred stock.

In requesting additional information on the stock deal, Mr. Butterworth's letter said, "In the absence of any verifiable explanation to the contrary, the earlier statements by the Institute . . . can only lead us to conclude that the fiscal integrity of a charitable educational institute is now in jeopardy."

A spokesman for the Bass group hadn't any comment. A prepared statement from Andrew Barnes, chairman of both Times Publishing and the institute, said, "We are comfortable . . . that the settlement is fair and assures significant on-going financial support for the institute."
