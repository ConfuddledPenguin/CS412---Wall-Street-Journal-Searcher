International Brief -- Royal Nedlloyd Group N.V.: Company Cancels Dividend As First-Half Profit Plunges

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/24/90

Royal Nedlloyd Group N.V. reported a 76% plunge in its first-half profit and said it wouldn't pay an interim dividend. The transport giant, which is based in Rotterdam, the Netherlands, said profit collapsed to 26.8 million guilders ($15.3 million), or 1.40 guilders (80 cents) a share, from 112.7 million guilders, or 6.47 guilders a share, a year earlier. The latest half's net included 95.6 million guilders of profit from sales of assets, including a tanker and Japanese real estate. Without those asset sales, Nedlloyd would have reported a substantial loss.

"These results are horrible," said Jan Stoop, a securities salesman at Barclays de Zoete Wedd Nederland N.V. in Amsterdam. "The outlook does not look particularly good." The profit drop was bigger than most analysts expected, despite Nedlloyd's warning this month that its performance was deteriorating.

The results were announced after the close of trading on the Amsterdam Stock Exchange, where Nedlloyd shares closed at 54.3 guilders, down from 56.5 guilders, in a generally weak market.

Nedlloyd said it expects that its net for the full year will be "considerably below" the 146 million guilders forecast this year. For 1989, the company reported net of 252 million guilders, including about 106 million guilders of exceptional items.
