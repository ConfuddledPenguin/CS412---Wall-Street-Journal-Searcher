Ex-Enfield Pension Officers Are Charged In Investment Case

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/24/90

TORONTO -- The Pension Commission of Ontario said it charged four former officials of Enfield Corp. and its former subsidiary, Federal Pioneer Ltd., with a breach of fiduciary duties in administering the companies' pension funds. The individuals charged are Michael Blair, former president of Enfield and Federal Pioneer; Jacques Lavergne, Enfield's former chief financial officer; Donald Webster, a former Enfield director; and Carole Penhale, Enfield's former corporate investment manager.

The commission said the charges arose from the purchase in August 1989 by the Enfield Master Trust pension fund of nearly two million Enfield shares for 17.9 million Canadian dollars (US$15.8 million), or 49.7% of the trust's estimated C$36 million in total assets at the time.

The Master Trust was an umbrella fund covering various participating pension plans within Enfield and Federal Pioneer.

The commission alleged the share purchases violated Ontario rules that prohibit more than 10% of a pension plan's assets from being invested in the securities of any single issuer. The commission said Mr. Blair also has been charged with a conflict of interest. At the time of the share purchases, Mr. Blair was vying for control of Enfield. Hees International Bancorp Inc., Enfield's major shareholder, was attempting to remove Mr. Blair from the Enfield board.

Mr. Blair said he hadn't yet seen the commission's charges. He denied he had any knowledge of the share purchase. The other defendants or their representatives couldn't be reached for comment. A total of six charges have been filed against each of the defendants, the commission said. The maximum fine for each conviction is C$25,000.

Enfield is a holding company based in Toronto. Federal Pioneer, an electrical equipment maker, has since been sold.
