Business Brief -- Continental Airlines Holdings: Company's Unit Receives Fine in Pollution Law Case

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/24/90

Continental Airlines pleaded no contest to charges of criminal violations of California's air pollution laws and was fined $101,050, the Los Angeles city attorney's office said. A spokesman for the unit of Continental Airlines Holdings Corp. wasn't available for comment.

The charges arose from inspections in April and May of a Los Angeles facility the company uses for aircraft painting and stripping operations. The airline was charged with failure to obtain proper permits and with improper handling of various hazardous chemicals and a known carcinogen, the office said.
