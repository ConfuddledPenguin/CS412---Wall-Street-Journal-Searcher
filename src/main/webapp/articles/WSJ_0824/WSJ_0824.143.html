Business Brief -- British Airways PLC: Seven Boeing 767s Grounded After Discovery of Cracks

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/24/90

British Airways PLC grounded seven Boeing 767s in London after routine maintenance revealed cracks in six of the jetliners in the pylons connecting the engines to the wings. The planes only began flying with British Airways last February. The airline had used them on routes to Europe and the Middle East, a Boeing Co. spokesman said. They are powered by Rolls-Royce RB211524E engines.

British Airways has 10 more 767s on order, Boeing said.

The design of the grounded planes is "unique to that engine and airframe construction," said the Boeing spokesman. "There isn't any reason to expect this problem would show up in any other Boeing airplane."

Boeing and British Airways have developed a possible solution to the problem, the Boeing spokesman said. The Seattle-based company sent a team of engineers and mechanics to London last night to begin implementing the changes.

The Boeing spokesman said he didn't know how long it might be before the planes are back in service.
