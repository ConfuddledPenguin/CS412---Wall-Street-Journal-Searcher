Business Brief -- Castle & Cooke Inc.: Company Expects to Report An Increase in Net for 1990

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/24/90

Castle & Cooke Inc. said it expects to report an increase in 1990 net income. David H. Murdock, chairman and chief executive officer, didn't give an estimate for the year, but said he expects a continuation of Castle & Cooke's first-half performance. For the first half, the company's net rose 19%, to $57 million, or 96 cents a share, on a 15% increase in revenue, to $1.5 billion.

Last year, the Los Angeles-based company earned $94.9 million, or $1.60 a share, on revenue of $2.7 billion.

Mr. Murdock, speaking at the company's annual meeting, also said Castle & Cooke hasn't set a time limit on the possible sale or spin-off of its Dole Food Co. unit. Castle & Cooke has said it has been contacted by several major companies interested in acquiring Dole Food. It has retained Goldman, Sachs & Co. to help evaluate a possible sale of the unit.

Castle & Cooke had first planned to spin off Dole Food into a new publicly traded company. That plan has been on hold since May, however, because of financing concerns and uncertainty over the tax treatment of the transaction.
