Pacific Exchange Plans To Set Up Formal Link With Taiwan's Market

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/24/90

SAN FRANCISCO -- The Pacific Stock Exchange and the Taiwan Stock Exchange said they plan a sister-exchange agreement aimed at promoting global investment. Lee Korins, chairman of the Pacific Stock Exchange, said he hopes the new, more formal ties will eventually make it easier for American investors to tap into the lucrative yet volatile Taiwanese stock market, and for Taiwanese investors to buy U.S. equities.

Currently, American investors interested in investing in Taiwanese companies can only do so by buying a few mutual funds. "We are trying to build some bridges, and to explore opportunities to trade each other's products," said Mr. Korins.

For the Taipei-based exchange, the sister-exchange agreement offers a way to improve its settlement and regulatory capabilities, Mr. Korins said.
