Ex-Drexel Executive, Others Hold 7% Stake In Fruit of the Loom

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/24/90

NEW YORK -- Drexel Burnham Lambert Inc.'s former mergers chief, Leon Black, is part of a group that bought 3,750,000 shares of Class A stock in Fruit of the Loom Inc. from Farley Inc. Mr. Black's role was disclosed in a Securities and Exchange Commission filing by FL Management, an investment advisory firm headed by Mr. Black and three other former Drexel employees, John Hannan, Arthur Bilger and Craig Cogut. The group's holding amounts to 7% of Fruit of the Loom's stock.

The Black group is investing money on behalf of several French institutions, which put up the cash to buy the Fruit of the Loom stock, Mr. Hannan said in an interview. The French institutions formally made their investment as Land Free Investment Ltd.

Farley previously entered into a put option agreement that gives Land Free Investment the right to require Farley to purchase its Fruit of the Loom stock, under certain conditions, at an exercise price of $15.54 between July 12 and July 15, 1991.

The filing said the purchases were made for investment reasons, and Mr. Hannan said the group didn't have any present intention to increase its holdings. Mr. Black, while at Drexel, was a regular adviser to Farley Inc.'s chairman, William Farley.

The filing also disclosed that Messrs. Bilger, Black and Cogut have an indirect interest in 3,660,000 Class A Fruit of the Loom shares which were previously acquired through partnerships that are partners in Champlain Associates. In addition, Messrs. Black and Cogut have an indirect interest in 141,153 shares of Class A stock owned by Wicklow Investment Associates, in which Mr. Cogut is a general partner.

Mr. Black also directly owns 124,000 Class A shares and Mr. Cogut owns 34,264 Class A shares.
