Arco Ends Freeze, Raises Gas Prices in Some Markets

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/24/90

LOS ANGELES -- Atlantic Richfield Co. said it is boosting gasoline prices in some markets, ending a two-week-old price freeze that began when President Bush requested that oil companies set prices responsibly following Iraq's invasion of Kuwait. Arco said it would make increases averaging 2.5 cents per gallon in certain markets due to dwindling gasoline supplies at its service stations. In the past two weeks, Arco's regular unleaded gasoline has been selling for as much as 13 cents below the general market, sales have increased over 19%, and more than 160 stations a day are running out of gas.

Earlier this week, Arco said it was raising the price of its diesel and jet fuel.

The company said it will limit gasoline price adjustments to crude-oil price changes occuring since Iraq's invasion of Kuwait. "The spread between Arco's wholesale gasoline prices and crude-oil market prices will not exceed the spread in place during July, the month before the Iraqi invasion," said George H. Babikian, president of Arco Products Co., the company's marketing and refining division.
