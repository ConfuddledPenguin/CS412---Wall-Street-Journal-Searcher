Failed Thrift's Head, Moers, Is Charged With 4 Felony Counts

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/24/90

LOS ANGELES -- U.S. Attorney Lourdes G. Baird charged Michael S. Moers, the former chairman of the board and coowner of the now-defunct Brookside Savings & Loan Association, with four federal felony counts. The lawsuit charges Mr. Moers, 39 years old, once a close associate of the also-defunct Westwood Savings & Loan Association, with criminal conduct in three separate transactions.

Westwood Savings and Brookside Savings, both of Los Angeles, were taken over by federal regulators in 1986 and 1989, respectively, and are among the failed thrifts being investigated by the Department of Justice and Office of Thrift Supervision.

The charges allege that Mr. Moers secretly sold his interest in a San Antonio, Texas, apartment complex to Brookside Savings. They allege that Mr. Moers concealed his interest in the property from savings and loan regulators by using a straw man to pose as the seller of the property and funnel proceeds to Mr. Moers.

The lawsuit also alleges that Mr. Moers, who resides in Los Angeles, together with his associates at Westwood Savings, owned an option to buy a large piece of prime real estate in West Los Angeles. It alleges he sold it to the two savings and loan associations and concealed his interest in the property from federal regulators by using a straw man.

And, the lawsuit alleges that Mr. Moers secretly received consulting fees that Westwood Savings owed him through another straw man.

An attorney for Mr. Moers, Robert Silverman, declined to comment on the charges.

The four charges carry a maximum sentence of 20 years in prison and a $265,000 fine. Mr. Moers will be arraigned Monday.
