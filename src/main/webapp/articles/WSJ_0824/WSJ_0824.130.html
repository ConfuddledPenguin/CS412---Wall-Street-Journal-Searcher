U.S. Auto Makers Set To Build 106,425 Cars And Trucks in Week

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/24/90

DETROIT -- The 10 major U.S. auto makers planned to build 106,425 cars and trucks this week, down 18.7% from 130,980 a year ago. General Motors Corp. said two of its U.S. car assembly plants will remain idled until Sept. 4. The Ypsilanti, Mich., plant has been closed since Aug. 13, initially because the Persian Gulf conflict derailed an order for Chevrolet Caprices and Buick Estate Wagons bound for the Mideast, but now because of a lack of U.S. dealer orders.

The plant that builds the Buick Reatta has been closed since the end of July to retool for the 1991 model.

Ford Motor Co. said it's planning Saturday overtime this week at its Wayne, Mich., car assembly plant and daily overtime at three truck assembly plants.

As previously reported, Ford has four car assembly plants idled this week but has scheduled to reopen the plants next week.

Chrysler Corp. said this week it's planning Saturday overtime for its Newark, Del., plant. The company said it plans to reopen Monday its St. Louis II assembly plant that builds its long wheelbase minivans. That plant has been closed since the beginning of the month for retooling.
