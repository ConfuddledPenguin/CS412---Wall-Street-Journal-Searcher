Business Brief -- Triton Energy Corp.: Exploration Concern Reports $34.6 Million Quarterly Loss

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/24/90

Triton Energy Corp. reported a fiscal fourth-quarter loss of $34.6 million, or $1.77 a share, largely reflecting asset writedowns. The loss in the quarter ended May 31 included a gain of $439,000, or two cents a share, from tax-loss carryforward. The Dallas oil and gas exploration concern said it took a charge of $31.1 million for the write-down of certain assets in connection with a restructuring plan.

Vice President Michael P. McInerney said the restructuring was unrelated to a possible buy-out bid by New Zealand investor Sir Ronald Brierly, who has accumulated 10.9% of Triton.

Fourth-quarter revenue fell 15% to $67.7 million from $79.8 million in the year-earlier period, when Triton had net income of $2.2 million, or five cents a share.

For the year, Triton recorded a net loss of $54.2 million, or $2.93 a share, on revenue of $260.1 million after recording a $593,000, or three cent-a-share, extraordinary gain from tax-loss carry-forward. A year earlier, Triton had a net loss of $12.5 million, or 87 cents a share, on revenue of $242.3 million. Those figures included a $534,000, or three cent-a-share, extraordinary gain from tax-loss carry-forwards.
