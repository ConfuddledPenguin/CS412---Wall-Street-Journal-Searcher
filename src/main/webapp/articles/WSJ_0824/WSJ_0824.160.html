Business Brief -- Western Union Corp.: Company Extends Deadline For Its Recapitalization Plan

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/24/90

Western Union Corp. again extended the deadline of its recapitalization plan in which it is seeking to exchange $530 million of secured notes for new notes and common stock. The financial services and communications company, which is based in Upper Saddle River, N.J., extended the deadline to midnight Monday; the deadline had been last Monday and then was extended a day, to Tuesday. Western Union said it wants at least 95% of $500 million in 19.25% senior secured notes exchanged, and at least 80% of $30 million in 16% notes. As of Tuesday, $322 million and $1.2 million, respectively, had been tendered by holders, the company said.

Last week, holders of Western Union preferred stock approved reclassification of the shares to common stock, an important part of the recapitalization plan. Western Union has said it could face a bankruptcy-court filing without the recapitalization.
