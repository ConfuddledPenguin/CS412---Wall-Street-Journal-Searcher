BSN Says Subsidiary Of Artal Will Acquire U.S. Biscuit Operations

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/24/90

PARIS -- BSN S.A. said Invus Group Ltd. of the U.S. agreed to buy the U.S. biscuit operations it put up for sale in May. A spokesman for the French food group said he couldn't disclose the price for the transaction, beyond saying that it would generate a "substantial capital gain."

Terms of the sale will see Invus Group, a unit of the closely held Belgian company Artal, take control of BSN's U.S. biscuit units Mother's and Salerno. The units have combined annual revenue of about $200 million for a 5.7% stake of the U.S. market.

Invus Group will also take over distribution of BSN's Lu brand in the U.S., but BSN will retain ownership.

BSN's move out of the U.S. biscuit market reflects its decision to focus on faster-growing, less-expensive markets in Asia and Europe, where it has recently been on an acquisition binge.

The U.S. pullout doesn't extend to BSN's other U.S. operations, which include products with higher market shares, such as Dannon yogurt, Lea & Perrins condiments and Evian mineral water.

BSN is France's biggest food group, with consolidated 1989 revenue of 48.7 billion francs ($9.3 billion) and profit of 2.7 billion francs. BSN said Invus Group generates annual revenue of about $1.3 billion in the U.S., with a large portion coming from baked goods. It didn't provide further details.
