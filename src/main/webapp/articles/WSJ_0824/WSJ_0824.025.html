Letters to the Editor: Degree of Futility

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/24/90

James W. Schmotter (Manager's Journal, editorial page, July 23) writes that Japanese graduates of American M.B.A. programs resent the fact that they "use virtually none of the training they received in the U.S." upon returning home. Based on my experience in M.B.A. programs, both as a Berkeley student and a teacher at Golden Gate University, I believe many U.S. business graduates feel the same way. Rather than blaming "the companies that sponsored their educations," as Mr. Schmotter does, one might also re-examine the usefulness of the current curriculum. Ellen O'Connor

Lecturer

School of Business

San Francisco State University

Los Altos, Calif.
