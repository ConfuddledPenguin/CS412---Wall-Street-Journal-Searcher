Oil Price Jump May Slash Japanese Trade Surplus

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 08/24/90

TOKYO -- A rise in the price of oil to $40 a barrel could shave $20 billion a year off Japan's trade surplus, the Tokai Bank Ltd. reported. Oil prices have climbed sharply since Iraq invaded Kuwait Aug. 2, with the price of a barrel of West Texas Intermediate oil for October delivery rising above $30 in Tokyo yesterday.

The latest statistics show that rising oil prices could trigger higher interest rates, lowering Japan's real economic growth by 0.8 percentage point.

The impact of higher oil on Japan's economy will be relatively light, compared with the blows Japan suffered in the two oil crises of the 1970s, a bank official said.
