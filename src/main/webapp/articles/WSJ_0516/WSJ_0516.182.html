Ford Canada Expects Fewer Vehicle Sales, Cites Slow Economies

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/16/90

OAKVILLE, Ontario -- Ford Motor Co. of Canada said it expects to report Canadian car and truck sales of 1,390,000 vehicles in the 12 months ending March 31, 1991, down about 6% from calendar 1989. Ford Canada said it expects Canadawide car sales of 920,000 in the 12 months and truck sales of 470,000. It said its target for the period is to have 20% of the car market and 30% of the truck market. The company attributed the decline in sales to slowdowns in the Canadian and U.S. economies and high interest rates.

Kenneth W. Harrigan, president and chief executive officer of Ford Canada, added that Canada's proposed goods and services tax, to take effect Jan. 1, would result in "abnormally lower volumes" in the fourth quarter of this year, which is why the sales estimate was made for the year ending March 1991.

"But what we lose in the fourth quarter of this year we will gain in the first quarter of next year," he said.

The company also said it intends to build a new paint facility here, pending final approval of funding by the boards of Ford Canada and Ford Motor Co., which holds 94% of the Canadian company. The company said the facility would paint the Ford Tempo and Mercury Topaz models that are produced at the Oakville plant.

Ford Canada said such a plant "would represent the largest amount spent on a single project in our 86-year history." A company spokesman said the facility would cost approximately C$500 million (US$424.8 million).

Preliminary work on the plant has begun and the project would be completed in May 1992, the company said.

The proposed 7% consumption levy is designed to replace the current 13.5% manufacturers sales tax. Consumers are expected to put off purchases of manufactured items until the new year when they can take advantage of the saving.
