More Companies Starting Drug Testing, Poll Finds

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/16/90

NEW YORK -- An increasing number of companies are instituting or plan to implement programs to test job applicants or employees for substance abuse, a survey by the Conference Board found. The one-year survey, results of which were released today, was based on information collected in 1988. The survey found that 55% of 681 human resources executives polled have implemented or were about to implement the testing programs. About nine out of 10 of the companies engaged in drug testing have issued formal policies on drug and alcohol use in the workplace.

The executives polled come from a broad range of industries, and most frequently cited the need to counter drug problems in the workplace as the reason for the testing programs. Among other reasons were improving safety, providing a deterrent to nonusers, preventing legal liability in drug-related accidents and reducing costs.

A previous survey reported in 1986 in a trade publication, Human Resource Management News, found only about 3% of companies polled were testing job applicants or employees for drug use before 1984, the current survey report said. The Conference Board is a nonprofit business research organization.

The survey found that testing programs are most prevalent in manufacturing, mining, public utilities and transportation sectors, and least prevalent among financial services companies.
