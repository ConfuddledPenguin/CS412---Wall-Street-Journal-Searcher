Who's News: Seegal Leaves Shearson's Media Group For Broader Banking Role at Salomon

  • Author: Michael Siconolfi
  • Date: 05/16/90

NEW YORK -- Frederic Seegal, head of Shearson Lehman Hutton Inc.'s investment-banking group for media companies, jumped ship to become co-head of Salomon Inc.'s corporate finance operations. The move, considered a coup for Salomon, is a sign of that firm's determination to build its presence in investment banking for the media industry. And analysts said it underscores the problems Shearson faces in retaining key talent amid its much-publicized financial and management troubles.

Mr. Seegal, a 16-year veteran at Shearson, will become a managing director at Salomon. Mr. Seegal, 41 years old, will work with Denis A. Bovin, co-head of corporate finance, and report to Salomon Vice Chairmen Jay F. Higgins and Leo I. Higdon Jr.

Salomon has been seeking to position its investment-banking operation in several areas -- including media, energy and industrial companies -- that it has identified as having long-term profit potential. For example, Salomon represented GTE Corp. in its recently announced $710 million purchase of the Providence Journal's ownership interest in cellular franchises in four states.

For Shearson, the loss of Mr. Seegal may be blunted somewhat by the slowdown in transactions among media companies. But Mr. Seegal and Salomon nevertheless expect the media business, which in their broad definition includes telephone, cellular and entertainment companies, to heat up again.

"If there's ever been an industry populated with aggressive people who want to do deals, it's the media business," Mr. Seegal said yesterday.

Shearson hasn't yet named a successor to Mr. Seegal, who joined the firm as an associate in 1974 from Harvard University, where he studied law and business. In 1981, Mr. Seegal was named managing director at Lehman Brothers Kuhn Loeb Inc., a predecessor firm, and he also was named head of the firm's media and communications team.

Shearson's media group has 11 professionals, including three analysts. The No. 2 professional in Shearson's media group, senior vice president Jill Greenthal, recently moved to the firm's London office, reflecting the increasingly international bent of media banking.

While Mr. Seegal's departure suggests that Shearson is having trouble keeping some of its senior professionals, Shearson officials privately say they believe the firm has done a good job at keeping most of its top people. And Mr. Seegal said his move was no reflection on Shearson.

"This shouldn't be viewed as a vote of no confidence at Shearson; it was just a great opportunity," Mr. Seegal said. "If (Salomon) had called a year and a half ago, I'd have taken it."

Indeed, Mr. Seegal will have far greater responsibilities at Salomon than he ever had at Shearson. Along with Mr. Bovin, he will oversee Salomon's coverage of corporate clients in most of the U.S., Canada and Latin America.

Salomon, for its part, is expected to add to its corporate finance area, which currently has a staff of about 150. The firm has about a dozen professionals in its media group.
