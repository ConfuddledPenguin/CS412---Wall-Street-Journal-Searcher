Senate Panel Approves Federal Reserve Board Nomination of Mullins

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/16/90

WASHINGTON -- The Senate Banking Committee unanimously approved the nomination of David Mullins to the Federal Reserve Board. The nomination of Mr. Mullins, who is the assistant Treasury secretary for domestic finance, now goes to the full Senate.

Mr. Mullins, a former Harvard Business School professor, was a principal author of the Bush administration's plan to bail out the savings-and-loan industry. Before coming to the Treasury, he was a top staffer on a task force set up by former President Reagan to investigate the 1987 stock market crash.

If approved by the Senate, Mr. Mullins would serve out the term vacated by Robert Heller, who left the Fed last year to become an executive vice president of Visa International, the credit card company. The term expires in 1996.
