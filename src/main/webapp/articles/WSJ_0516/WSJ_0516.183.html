Cineplex Odeon Posts Loss of $11.9 Million For Its First Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/16/90

TORONTO -- Losses at Cineplex Odeon Corp. continued in the first quarter of 1990, with the beleagured movie theater chain reporting a net loss of $11.9 million, or 25 cents a share, on revenue of $139.7 million. In the first quarter of 1989, Cineplex reported net income of $10.7 million, or 23 cents a share, on revenue of $128.5 million.

While the loss narrowed from Cineplex's fourth-quarter loss of 91 cents a share, analysts note that the loss from continuing operations of $32.1 million, or 67 cents a share, was considerably higher than the market had been expecting.

The biggest blow to Cineplex's first-quarter results came from a $15 million charge, a provision for estimated losses on asset sales now under negotiation. The company said those sales are expected to close during the second quarter. That charge offset a $15 million gain on the sale of Cineplex's residual interest in Universal Studios Florida.

Lisbeth Barron, vice president of research at S.G. Warburg & Co. in New York, said that the size of Cineplex's loss wasn't surprising.

"It takes a while to turn around operations like this; it can't be done quickly," she said.

The interest payable on Cineplex's bank debt rose from $12.2 million in the first quarter of 1989, to $14.3 million in the first quarter of 1990, the company said, despite a decrease in long-term debt of $46.1 million in the same period. Cineplex said it currently has $531.9 million in long-term debt.

Cineplex reported an 18% increase in box office revenue for the first quarter, compared to a U.S. industry average gain of 2%. Allen Karp, president and chief operating officer of Cineplex, said he is "particularly encouraged" by the surge.
