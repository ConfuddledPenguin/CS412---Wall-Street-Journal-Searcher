Letters to the Editor: Semiconductor Lobby Represents Many Firms

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/16/90

I take great exception to the mention in your April 24 editorial "A Trade Deficit Primer" that "the Semiconductor Industry Association, {is} in effect a lobby for only two companies. . . ." At present, we represent 35 U.S.-based manufacturers of semiconductors, including small and large, merchant and captive companies. Additionally, SIA has 27 associate members that are either foreign-owned semiconductor makers or firms that support the industry (i.e. equipment and materials producers, law firms, etc.). The SIA board of directors is made up of CEOs, sector presidents and vice presidents of 14 different U.S. semiconductor makers.

The SIA prides itself on having the ability to build consensus among our members or to reach compromise when necessary. We evaluate and appreciate input from all of our members, whether it be one of the board-member companies or an associate member. We have up to 60 recommendations and opinions to consider, not two.

Andrew A. Procassini

President

Semiconductor Industry Association

Cupertino, Calif.
