Marketing & Media: Time Warner Unit to Close Its Southpoint Magazine

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/16/90

Southern Progress Corp., a subsidiary of Time Warner Inc., said it will close down its Southpoint magazine, a regional magazine for southern states. Southpoint, a general interest monthly magazine, will cease publication with its June issue. Don Logan, chief executive officer of Southern Progress Corp., said the magazine is being folded because it wasn't drawing enough advertising or new paid circulation.

Staff members of Southpoint, based in Atlanta, were stunned by the news. "Obviously I am deeply disappointed that the commitment to Southpoint proved to be so fleeting," said John Huey, editor of Southpoint.

A publisher of several profitable magazines such as Southern Living and Southern Accents, the Birmingham, Ala.-based Southern Progress said it will shift focus from Southpoint to new advertising packages that include Sunset magazine, another regional magazine. Sunset, a large-circulation life style magazine in the western states, was acquired by Time Warner in March for $225 million.

Hoping to expand its franchise in the South, Southern Progress acquired 70% of Southpoint, then known as Southern magazine, in March 1989. The remaining 30% of the magazine is owned by Stephens Inc., the Little Rock, Ark.-based investment banking firm, which originally bankrolled the magazine when it was launched in 1986.
