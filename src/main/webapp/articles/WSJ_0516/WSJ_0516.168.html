Trump's Casinos Had A Loss of $12 Million During First Quarter

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 05/16/90

Donald Trump's casinos, traditionally among the real estate mogul's biggest winners, lost a combined $12 million during the first quarter of 1990. In the year-earlier quarter the casinos had a gain of $2.2 million. The unaudited figures, released yesterday by the Casino Association of New Jersey, indicate that Mr. Trump's properties may have started suffering from a slow Atlantic City, N.J.-market even before his $1 billion Taj Mahal opened in April.

Citywide, the industry lost $4.3 million on gross revenue of $729.9 million compared with a first-quarter loss of $3.8 million last year on revenue of $730.4 million.

Trump Plaza lost $3.7 million and Trump's Castle lost $8.3 million.

The first quarter results are significant because they reveal for the first time what effect Mr. Trump's intense marketing strategies, which raised revenue for the Trump's Castle, have had on the bottom line.

Mr. Trump could not be reached for comment.

Analysts said the Trump Plaza results, while poor, were distorted by a loss of $6 million to a single baccarat player in January.
