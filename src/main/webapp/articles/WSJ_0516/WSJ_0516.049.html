Japan Machinery Orders Rose

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/16/90

TOKYO -- Japanese manufacturers received machinery orders totaling 3.034 trillion yen ($19.9 billion) in March, up an unadjusted 7.9% from the previous year, the Economic Planning Agency said. The gain followed a 29% year-on-year increase in February.

An agency official said machinery orders remain in an upward trend, and added that March's rise was in line with expectations. Orders have been rising since October 1988 with the exception of December 1989.

After seasonal adjustment, orders in March fell 14% from the previous month to 1.758 trillion yen.

Machinery orders to Japanese companies rose 16% to 21.863 trillion yen in fiscal 1989 from the previous fiscal year, according to the agency.
