Farm Credit System's Profit Climbed 8.5% In the First Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/16/90

NEW YORK -- The Farm Credit System's first-quarter profit rose 8.5% as the big agricultural lender's fundamental financial condition continued to improve. Net income for the quarter was $191 million, up from $176 million a year earlier. More indicative of the borrower-owned system's health, however, was a 34% rise in net interest income -- basically, interest collected on loans minus interest paid out to bondholders -- to $321 million from $239 million in the 1989 quarter.

The improvement was due to lower borrowing costs as high-interest-rate bonds retired, and an improvement in new loan volume and in the condition of existing loans.

Loans rose about $500 million during the quarter to $49.63 billion. Nonperforming assets, meanwhile, declined by about $87 million during the period to $2.93 billion. And so-called other high-risk loans fell about $100 million to $4.44 billion.

The system, which received a federal bailout in the mid-1980s after the farm economy's slump drove it to the brink of insolvency, also continued to lessen its use of an unusual accounting technique that boosted profits substantially in prior years. The so-called negative provision for loan losses -- meaning money is taken out of the reserve for losses and recorded as profit -- was $18 million in the first quarter, compared with $75 million a year ago.

The technique is allowable under accounting rules, but its use is rare.
