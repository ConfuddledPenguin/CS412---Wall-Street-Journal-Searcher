Technology: Sony Text Display System, `Data Discman,' Due in July

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/16/90

TOKYO (AP) -- Sony Corp. said it will introduce an "electronic book" system that uses a palm-sized player for reading books recorded on three-inch compact disks. Sony said its "Data Discman" player has a screen that displays text recorded on CDs, called Electronic Books, that can store about 100,000 pages of text each, or more than 300 paperback books.

"The purpose of this product is to create an entirely new market. Instead of having to go to a library or bookshelf for information, people can have access to it anytime and anywhere," said Sunobu Horigome, head of Sony's General Audio Group.

He said Sony hopes the product will create a "new culture" and approach to information access, with an impact similar to its Walkman portable stereos.

Sony said it plans to begin marketing the machine in Japan in July for about $380. Overseas sales are expected to begin in less than a year, after arrangements with local publishers are made, officials said.
