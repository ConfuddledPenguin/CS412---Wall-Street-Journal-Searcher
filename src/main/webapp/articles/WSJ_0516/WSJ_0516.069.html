Galveston-Houston's Advisers

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/16/90

HOUSTON -- Galveston-Houston Co. said it hired Goldman, Sachs & Co. and Simmons & Co. International to formulate a plan to enhance shareholder value. In April, the company said its chairman, president and chief executive officer, Nathan M. Avery, would resign to assume a post with the Commerce Department, and that it would hire advisers to help determine the best course of action. Galveston-Houston manufactures oil and natural gas drilling and production equipment.

A spokesman said options include hiring a new chief executive, possible mergers and acquisitions and the sale of all or parts of the company. He stressed that no decisions have been made and that the investment bankers haven't yet completed a review of the company.
