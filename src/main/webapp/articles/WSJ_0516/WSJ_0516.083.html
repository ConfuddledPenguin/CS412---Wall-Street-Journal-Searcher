REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: Sandinista History

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/16/90

While strikes by Sandinista unions yesterday paralyzed Violetta Chamorro's newly elected government, unsettling news of past Sandinista acts was emerging. The May 5 issue of Barricada, the Sandinista Party organ, reports that 20,289 Contra rebels were killed during the war. Additionally, they claim to have captured 3,365 Contras. This far exceeds all known figures for Contra combatants, so it seems likely that unarmed opponents of the Sandinistas were also being killed and counted. These Sandinista figures raise questions about the work of the human-rights groups chartered to monitor abuses in that beleaguered country. In their rush to condemn Contra abuses, these groups apparently missed the real level of Sandinista killing.
