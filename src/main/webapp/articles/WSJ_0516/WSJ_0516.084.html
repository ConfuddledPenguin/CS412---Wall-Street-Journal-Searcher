Who's News: ITT Names Comey To Corporate Post Directing 2 Units

  • Author: Beatrice E. Garcia
  • Date: 05/16/90

NEW YORK -- ITT Corp. named Dale Comey as executive vice president and gave him responsibility for the day-to-day operations of two key units. Mr. Comey, 49 years old, has been president and chief operating officer of ITT Hartford Fire Insurance Co. since 1988. ITT Hartford is ITT's largest operating unit, generating nearly 21% of the company's total operating profit last year.

People at the company said that with Mr. Comey's appointment to executive vice president, he has been clearly identified as a possible successor to DeRoy Thomas, ITT president and chief operating officer. Mr. Thomas plans to retire next February. In moving up from the insurance unit to the corporate staff, Mr. Comey has skipped several rungs on the ITT corporate ladder.

"Given this slot and the amount of responsibility they have given him, they could be grooming him for a bigger role within the company," said Jack Kelly, who follows ITT at Goldman, Sachs & Co.

"The appointment of Dale Comey to this senior executive position is an expression of my confidence . . . that Dale will play a significant role in the management of our corporation over the long term," said Rand Araskog, ITT chairman.

Mr. Comey, who began his business career as an actuarial trainee at ITT Hartford in 1965, will be responsible for ITT Rayonier, a forest products operation, and ITT Coins, which involves a yellow-pages directory outside the U.S. and 38 technical schools in this country.

ITT Rayonier was ITT's second biggest income generator in 1989, contributing 16% of operating net. Like insurance, forest products is a highly cylical business. ITT Coins is one of the company's smaller units.

Mr. Comey said his first task is to develop a full understanding of what opportunities lie ahead for these two units. Then, his focus will be on developing their competitive advantages, which is what "yields a better-than-average return for shareholders," he said.

In his new position, Mr. Comey reports to Mr. Thomas. Before becoming president of ITT in 1988, Mr. Thomas headed the Hartford insurance group. Mr. Comey worked as his executive assistant during the mid-1970s.
