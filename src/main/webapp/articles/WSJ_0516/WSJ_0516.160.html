Business Brief -- Flexible Bond Trust Inc.: Board Proposes Converting To Open-End Investment

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/16/90

Flexible Bond Trust Inc. said its board plans to take steps to convert the New York-based closed-end fund to an open-end investment company. The proposed conversion and related matters are expected to be submitted to shareholders for a vote at a special meeting scheduled for Sept. 6. If approved, the conversion would become effective in the 1991 first quarter. The fund now pays monthly income dividends; it expects to continue this policy after the conversion. As of March 31, the fund's assets were valued at $66 million.

Open-end mutual funds issue and redeem shares on demand and these trade at a price based on their asset value. Closed-end funds issue a fixed number of shares that trade publicly at a premium to or a discount from their asset value. Since it went public, Flexible Bond Trust has been traded at varying discounts; the conversion would eliminate these discounts.

In American Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Flexible Bond Trust shares closed at $7.375.
