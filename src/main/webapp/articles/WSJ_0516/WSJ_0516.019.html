International: Degussa and E. Merck End Plan for Merging Divisions

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/16/90

FRANKFURT, West Germany -- Degussa AG and E. Merck said they have abandoned their plan to merge their pharmaceutical divisions, citing a lack of common interests. The two West German chemicals groups said a study by a joint expert commission has shown that "the common interests for a cooperation weren't sufficient."

"The talks were subsequently terminated," Degussa and Merck said in a statement.

In late 1989, Degussa approached Merck with the proposal to merge its Asta Pharma AG drug subsidiary with the much larger pharmaceutical division of Merck and take a minority stake in the joint venture.

Merck, which is closely held, isn't affiliated with Merck & Co. of the U.S.

Details on the talks were first disclosed in February, and at a news conference in March, Degussa Chairman Gert Becker still expressed optimism that the negotations would succeed.

But Hans-Werner Hauck, Merck's finance chief, said the concept proposed by Degussa wasn't in the best interest of Merck. "Degussa wanted to sell its division and still have a say in it, but this is a difficult construction," he said.

A Degussa spokesman declined to give any reasons for the failure of the talks.
