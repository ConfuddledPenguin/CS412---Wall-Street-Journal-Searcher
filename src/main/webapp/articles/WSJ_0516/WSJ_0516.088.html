Quixote's Purchase Of an Ex-Unit Isn't Tilting at Windmills --- CD Firm Now Profitable; Company Down Under Gives Up Stake for $1,000

  • Author: James P. Miller
  • Date: 05/16/90

CHICAGO -- Quixote Corp.'s sale of its compact-disk unit to an Australian concern has boomeranged, and Quixote says it couldn't be happier. The holding company yesterday announced that it had reacquired -- for $1,000 -- the remaining 51% of an operation it sold for $55.5 million two years ago. What's more, the CD unit, which was losing money when Quixote sold it, is now profitable, says a spokeswoman. The unit makes laser video disks, audio compact disks and CDs for use in computers.

Quixote sold the unit, then called LaserVideo Inc., to Disctronics Ltd. of Melbourne in January 1988. The Australian company paid Quixote $29 million cash and issued a note for $26.5 million, payable in January 1989.

Disctronics didn't make good on the note. Quixote initially sued, but late last year it entered into a new agreement under which Quixote received 49% stake in its former unit, now called Disctronics Manufacturing Inc.

As part of the revised agreement, Disctronics Ltd. also issued Quixote preferred shares and set up a schedule of repayments which, if Disctronics didn't meet, allowed Quixote to buy the remainder of the unit for a "nominal" $1,000.

Disctronics Ltd. didn't meet the first payment, scheduled for April 30, Quixote says, and so the Chicago company "regained total ownership."

The unit, renamed yet again Disc Manufacturing Inc., seems none the worse for its round trip: Quixote said Disc's sales climbed 70% in the year ended March 31, to $38 million, and are expected to climb to $55 million in the current 12 months. Operating profit was $3.8 million in the latest year, and is also expected to increase "at a good clip," Quixote said, thanks in part to $10 million its former owners spent to boost capacity.

Disc, which manufactures CDs at plants in Alabama and California, is also benefiting from trends favorable to domestic CD manufacturers, Quixote said.

Quixote is a holding company with interests in highway-safety products, computer-aided transcription services and diagnostic equipment.
