Consumer Prices Rose in March

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/16/90

PARIS -- Consumer prices among the world's industrial countries rose an average 0.7% in March after a 0.6% increase in February, despite slower growth in food prices and declining energy costs, the Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development said. For the 12 months to March, OECD consumer prices rose 6.5%, up from February's year-on-year rise of 6.4%.

The organization said food prices grew 0.4% in the latest reporting month compared with a 0.9% gain in February.

Meanwhile, March energy prices fell 0.2% after declining 0.5% in February.

The "underlying" inflation rate, excluding food and energy costs, edged up to 0.6% from 0.5%. The underlying rate during the 12 months to March was 4.7%, up from 4.5% in February.
