Letters to the Editor: Diaper Services, Sir, Are Not to Be Mocked

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/16/90

Regarding Tim W. Ferguson's April 17 Business World column, "Where the Greens Risk Soiling Themselves": As members of the "thirtysomething generation" with two kids in diapers, we take issue with Mr. Ferguson's implication that disposable diapers are not a legitimate environmental concern. Using them creates non-recyclable trash and lots of it. One can argue whether there are worse offenders, but that does not solve the problem. As Mr. Ferguson notes, disposables are popular, and rightly so, because they are convenient. No one wants to spend his or her days washing diapers like mom used to do. But the diaper services he mocks offer a recyclable alternative. We discovered to our surprise that for less than the monthly cost of disposables, we can keep our two kids in 100% reusable cloth diapers that are delivered and picked up at the door. And one does not need to be "affluent young in urban centers" as he suggests to take advantage of a diaper service. We have it here in a little Oregon town. You may, too. Just look in your phone book.

Mark Becker

Susan Albright

Corvallis, Ore.
