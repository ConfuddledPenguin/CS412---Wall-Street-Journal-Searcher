Warnaco Managers Lead the Acquisition Of Activewear Lines

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/16/90

NEW YORK -- Warnaco Inc. said a management group bought out 95% of the apparel company's Activewear division, which includes the Speedo swimwear line, for $85 million plus assumption of about $5 million of debt. The move marks the first major asset sale for Warnaco, which was taken private in a leveraged buy-out in 1986, said Linda J. Wachner, the parent's president and chief executive officer.

The $18 million gain on the sale ensures that for 1990, Warnaco will post its first full-year profit, Ms. Wachner said, adding that the parent also expects to operate profitably for the rest of 1990.

The 44-year-old Ms. Wachner also becomes chairman and chief executive officer of the new company, which is expected to be named Speedo/Activewear Inc. and based in Van Nuys, Calif.

Also taking an equity stake as part of the investor group is a subsidiary of Pentland Group PLC of London. Warnaco will retain 5% of the new company.

The Activewear unit accounted for $85 million, or 13%, of Warnaco's $650 million of sales in 1989.

For 1989, the company posted a $20 million loss, reflecting $13 million of noncash write-offs of certain apparel lines and amortization of $20 million of intangible assets.

The company is current on interest payments and in November will begin paying down about $390 million in subordinated debt from the original leveraged buy-out, Ms. Wachner said.

In 1989, Warnaco reduced bank debt to $94 million from $133 million; bank debt now sits at about $50 million, Ms. Wachner said.
