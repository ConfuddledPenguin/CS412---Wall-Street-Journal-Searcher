LOS ANGELES -- Natural Gas Pipeline Co. of America said it is seeking federal regulators' approval to expand the capacity of the central and eastern segments of its recently completed Amarillo/Gulf Coast natural gas pipeline in Oklahoma and Texas. Natural Gas is the interstate transmission unit of MidCon Corp., a unit of Los Angeles-based Occidental Petroleum Corp.

The company has applied to the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission to increase the capacity of each segment by 300 million cubic feet of gas a day. The central segment currently carries 500 million cubic feet a day, while the eastern segment carries 350 million.
