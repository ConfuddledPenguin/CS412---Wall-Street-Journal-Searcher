Sweden's Consumer Prices Rise

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/16/90

STOCKHOLM -- Sweden's consumer prices rose 0.4% in April from March and were up 10.1% from a year earlier, according to Statistics Sweden, the national statistics agency. The agency said consumer prices have shot up 6.4% this year. It said the rise was mainly a result of rent increases and higher prices on clothes and shoes.

The 0.4% increase was in line with the 0.5% average prediction of analysts.

Sweden's annualized rate of inflation fell to 10.1% in April from 11.2% in March but was up from 6.5% in April last year.

The national Price and Competition office predicted consumer prices would increase at a rate of 8.6% this year. Sweden's consumer price index was measured at 205.2 in April, down from 205.4 in March. The index reflects a 1980 base of 100.
