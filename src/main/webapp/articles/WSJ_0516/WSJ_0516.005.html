Yields on CDs Fell Across the Board In the Latest Week

  • Author: Ellen E. Schultz
  • Date: 05/16/90

NEW YORK -- Yields on certificates of deposit at major banks and brokerage companies declined across the board in the week ended yesterday. "Treasury bill rates are coming down, so banks are eager to lower their CD rates," said Norberto Mehl, chairman of Banxquote Money Markets, a New York information service.

Average yields on small-denomination, consumer CDs slipped the least, according to an 18-bank survey by Banxquote. Average yields remained above 8% for longer-term maturities. Yields fell to 8.02% from 8.10% on one-year CDs, and to 8.25% from 8.32% on five-year CDs.

Small-denomination CDs sold through leading brokerage companies showed a slightly greater decline in average yields, but were still higher across the board than those offered directly by banks. Average yields on six-month CDs dropped to 8.22% from 8.34%, while average yields on two-year CDs slid to 8.60% from 8.80%, according to figures compiled by Banxquote.

Brokerage firms are typically able to offer higher-yielding CDs because they shop for competitive yields at banks across the country. They then buy CDs in bulk and resell them to their customers as a service, without fees.

Meanwhile, average yields for large-denomination CDs, which generally require deposits of $90,000 or more, were also down. Average yields on six-month jumbo CDs declined to 7.93% from 8.07%, while one-year maturities dropped to 8.10% from 8.25%. The only bank in the survey that offered a CD with a yield of at least 9% was Continental Bank in Chicago, which offered a 9% five-year jumbo.

Mr. Mehl expects CD yields to waffle for the next few weeks, but cautions that when change comes, it may come quickly. "If banks have an opportunity to lower the rates," he said, "they'll do it as fast as they can."
