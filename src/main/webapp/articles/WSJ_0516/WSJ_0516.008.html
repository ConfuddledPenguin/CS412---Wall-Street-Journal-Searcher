Technology: IBM Unveils Details Of Major Expansion Of CallPath Software

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/16/90

NEW YORK -- International Business Machines Corp. unveiled, as expected, a major expansion of CallPath, a software product that links telephone switches to computers. IBM said a new CallPath product will allow its minicomputers to talk to switches made by four leading telecommunications companies -- American Telephone & Telegraph Co., Northern Telecom Inc., Rolm Co. and Siemens AG. Previously, CallPath had worked only with switches made by Rolm, which IBM owns in partnership with Siemens.

Northern Telecom, Rolm and Siemens announced that their links to CallPath would be available immediately. AT&T said it will work with IBM to enable its switches to talk to CallPath, though neither company announced a schedule.

IBM, based in Armonk, N.Y., also announced new CallPath capabilities and a set of protocols for companies that write software that works with the product. And it promised that CallPath will soon work with other switches and IBM mainframes and personal computers, too.

Taken together, the announcements suggest that CallPath will emerge as a standard in the burgeoning field of "voice-integrated applications."

"This indicates an eagerness {on IBM's part} to connect to the broad range of communications vendors' switches," said Carl Pitasi of the Gartner Group, market researchers based in Stamford, Conn. "The old CallPath product is a teeny shadow of what this will become."

CallPath identifies the origin or destination of calls made through a telephone switch known as a PBX, for private branch exchange. Then it automatically pulls information about callers from an IBM computer. Telephone operators -- customer service representatives or brokers, for example -- can see callers' files displayed on a screen as soon as they pick up the phone, which saves time and opens up new marketing opportunities.
