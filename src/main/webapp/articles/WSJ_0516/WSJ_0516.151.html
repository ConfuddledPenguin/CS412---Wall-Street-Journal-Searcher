Mellon Bank to Sell Finance Unit to Ford, Widens Buy-Back

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/16/90

PITTSBURGH -- Mellon Bank Corp. said it agreed to sell its consumer finance subsidiary, Mellon Financial Services Corp., to Associates Corp. of North America, a unit of Ford Motor Co. Terms weren't disclosed. Separately, the bank holding company said its board authorized it to purchase as many as 250,000 of its common shares, as part of a plan announced last October.

The transaction involving the consumer finance unit, which is subject to regulatory approval, is expected to close by June 30 and to result in a one-time $75 million gain, Mellon said. In 1989, Mellon earned $210 million, or $4.01 a share, including an extraordinary credit of $29 million from retirement of debt.

Assets covered by the transaction include 96 consumer finance offices in 17 states, mainly in the Midwest and western U.S., as well as $460 million in secured and unsecured consumer finance loans. Mellon said it will retain about $90 million of its commercial finance portfolio.

Mellon noted that the sale reflects its strategy to concentrate on retail banking in the Central Atlantic region. It said proceeds from the sale will help finance Mellon's recent acquisition of Meritor Savings Bank.

Associates Corp., based in Dallas, is one of the nation's largest finance companies, with assets of more than $15 billion.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Mellon shares closed at $27.50, down 25 cents, giving the new buy-back authorization a current indicated value of about $6.9 million.

Under the buy-back program, Mellon said it previously purchased 250,000 of its common shares in connection with benefit plan requirements. As of March 31, the company had 43.9 million common shares outstanding.

Under the latest buy-back authorization, Mellon said it can make purchases from time to time in the open market or privately negotiated transactions. Shares bought back will be added to the corporation's treasury shares and used to meet Mellon's current and near-term common stock requirements for its benefit plans.
