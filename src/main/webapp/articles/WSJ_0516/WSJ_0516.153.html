United Research Sells A Majority Stake To Information Firm

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/16/90

MORRISTOWN, N.J. -- The management consultant firm United Research said it has sold a majority interest in itself to Cap Gemini Sogeti, the Paris-based information technology service company. The transaction is part of a pattern of mergers between consulting firms and information technology firms, said industry analysts.

"This is the first time in the world that a major management consulting firm and a major information technology firm have joined forces," said David Teiger, United's chairman. Mr. Teiger declined to disclose terms of the transaction.

United Research, which specializes in strategy and operations consulting, had combined domestic and overseas revenue last year of $80 million, according to David Lord, managing editor of Consultant News, a Fitzwilliam, N.H., newsletter.

Cap Gemini had world-wide revenue of $1.3 billion, including $213 million in this country.

Mr. Lord said the sale is consistent with similar marriages of consultants and information technology companies, including the purchase by Computer Sciences Corp., El Segundo, Calif., of the Index Group.
