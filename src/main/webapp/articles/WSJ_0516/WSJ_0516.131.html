Business Brief -- Live Entertainment Inc.: First-Period Net Rose 17%; Sales to Burger King Cited

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/16/90

Live Entertainment Inc. said first-quarter net income rose 17%, largely because of about $17 million in Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles videocassette tape sales to Burger King. Yesterday, Live shares rose $1.25, to close at $21 a share in national over-the-counter trading. Live's stock price had dropped to $13.25 a share following news last August that the company's chairman and chief executive officer, Jose Menendez, was slain along with his wife Kitty.

The Los Angeles-based company, which distributes videocassettes and sells entertainment software, reported net income for the first quarter of $1.8 million, or 15 cents a share, compared with $1.6 million, or 12 cents a share, a year ago. Sales increased 99% to $148.3 million from $74.6 million last year. The company's weighted average number of shares outstanding increased to 11.9 million from 10.2 million a year earlier.

The company said that the quarter's results were reduced because of a $3.7 million reserve taken on amounts owed its Lieberman Enterprises unit from Ames Department Stores Inc., which has filed for bankruptcy law protection.
