Dillard, Limited, Penney Post Gains In 1st-Quarter Net

  • Author: Karen Blumenthal
  • Date: 05/16/90

Dillard Department Stores Inc., Limited Inc. and J.C. Penney Co. posted double-digit earnings gains for the first quarter, although Penney's increase was largely the result of a one-time write-down in the year-earlier period. Dillard, a department store chain based in Little Rock, Ark., reported a 50% increase in net income, and Limited, a specialty apparel retailer based in Columbus, Ohio, reported a 38% increase in profit for the quarter. Both cited strong sales as a primary reason behind the increases.

Penney, however, said its first-quarter sales were softer than it had expected, a problem analysts have attributed to continued disappointing sales of women's apparel. Penney said net income rose 20.5%, to $154 million, or $1.21 a share, from $128 million, or 97 cents a share. But the year-earlier results included a $20 million charge to discontinue an interactive home television shopping service.

Penney's sales rose just 5.7%, to $3.53 billion from $3.34 billion. Total revenue, which was reclassified to include finance-charge revenue from the company's credit-card accounts, increased 6.3%, to $3.8 billion from $3.58 billion.

Dallas-based Penney said its department store margins increased in the quarter, but its overall gross margin fell to 35.6% from 36% last year because of an unusually high level of markdowns in its catalog business.

Daniel Barry, a senior vice president at Kidder, Peabody & Co., said the company's catalog profits have been hurt for much of the last year by a lack of fashionable clothing offerings. But he said the company is expected to improve its selection in its June book, and catalog margins are expected to improve later this year. The company also is trying to improve its women's apparel sales in its department stores by upgrading fashions and bringing in more brand names.

Still, the market didn't like Penney's results, and its stock fell $1.375 to close at $65.25. Dillard, which released its results after the market closed, was up $3.50 to close at $86.75, and Limited closed at $45.625, unchanged. All three trade on the New York Stock Exchange.

Dillard's stock is up more than $15 since May 1 on expectations of stronger-than-projected earnings.

Limited, citing a "solid customer response," said its net income rose to $62.3 million, or 34 cents a share, from $45.2 million, or 25 cents a share.

The retailer attributed the increases to good performances by Lane Bryant, Express and Victoria's Secret stores and catalogs.

Sales for Limited rose 15% to $1.11 billion from $964.6 million. Leslie H. Wexner, chairman and chief executive officer, said the company will increase store selling space by 2.8 million square feet in 1990, or 19%, from the 14.4 million square feet the company had at the start of the year.

Dillard said its net increased to $29.4 million, or 82 cents a share, from $19.6 million, or 61 cents a share. Analysts had been expecting results of about 72 cents a share. The company had 35.9 million shares outstanding this year, up from 32.3 million last year. Sales rose 24.1% to $742.4 million from $598.1 million.
