Blind Trust Held by Bush Totals Nearly $1,280,000

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/16/90

WASHINGTON -- The blind trust containing President Bush's personal investments has risen in value to nearly $1,280,000, the White House disclosed. A year ago, the blind trust had a value of $998,000. As previously disclosed in Mr. Bush's tax return, the trust earned $217,000 in taxable and tax-exempt income in 1989, and had capital gains of $36,000.

The latest information about Mr. Bush's assets was contained in his annual financial-disclosure statement. The statement indicates that the blind trust contains the bulk of Mr. Bush's personal investments, with the exception of his vacation home in Maine. That home is valued at $892,000, the disclosure reports. A piece of real estate Mr. Bush owns in Houston is valued at $81,000.
