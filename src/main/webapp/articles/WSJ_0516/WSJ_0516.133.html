International Brief -- Banque Nationale de Paris: Institution to Take Control Of Troubled Banque Arabe

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/16/90

Banque Nationale de Paris announced yesterday that it will take control of troubled Banque Arabe et Internationale D'Investissement, which was hobbled by the steep decline in world crude oil prices during the latter half of the 1980s and bad loans in developing countries. BAII, based in Paris, was created in 1973 with 50% of its shares held by Arab banks and 50% held by non-Arab financial institutions. It was one of several banks founded during the 1970s to act as a conduit for the recycling of petrodollars amassed by the Arab members of the Organization of Petroleum Exporting Countries.

BNP will buy all shares outstanding of the bank it doesn't already own at a price to be set later. The French state-owned bank will then inject 600 million francs ($108.4 million) of fresh funds into BAII.

Following the capital boost, BAII's capital structure will be modified once again, such that BNP will cede a portion of the bank's shares to other unnamed future shareholders while retaining a majority stake for itself.

The operations of BAII will be reorganized, BNP said, but the focus of its business will remain within the countries of the Maghreb and the Middle East.
