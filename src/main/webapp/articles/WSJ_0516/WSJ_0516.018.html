Marketing & Media: CBS Posts Ratings Victory In Prime-Time Viewing

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/16/90

NEW YORK -- CBS won the prime-time ratings last week for the first time since June 1988. According to the A.C. Nielsen Co., CBS Inc.'s TV network finished first with 11.7% of the audience, followed by NBC, a unit of General Electric Co., with 11.5% and ABC, a division of Capital Cities/ABC Inc., with 11%. Nielsen is a unit of New York-based Dun & Bradstreet Corp.

CBS's victory was attributed to its strong ratings on Sunday, with "60 Minutes" and "Shattered Dreams" a made-for-TV movie, and on Monday with two hours of comedies and a one-hour special hosted by Connie Chung.

ABC had the top three shows, including "America's Funniest Home Videos," which was No. 1. A companion show, "America's Funniest . . . Part II" was rated No. 3. "Roseanne" was No. 2.

CBS also had the lowest-rated show, the Saturday night comedy series, "City," which attracted only 3.3% of the viewers. That was the lowest-rated network show in history tying "Live From Studio 8H" on NBC in January 1982.
