Polish Bank Submits Bankruptcy Petition Against Drexel Unit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/16/90

NEW YORK -- Bank Polska Kasa Opieki, a Polish bank, filed an involuntary Chapter 7 bankruptcy petition against Drexel Burnham Lambert Trade Finance Inc., an attorney for the bank said. Under Chapter 7, a creditor -- such as Bank Polska -- can force a debtor into bankruptcy.

A spokesman for the parent company, Drexel Burnham Lambert Group Inc., said its attorneys had been notified of the filing in federal bankruptcy court in Manhattan but hadn't yet seen the petition.

Jon Arnason, an attorney for Bank Polska, said that the Drexel unit's total assets are $94 million and its total liabilities are $98 million. He said he expects the case to be converted from Chapter 7 to Chapter 11 under the U.S. Bankruptcy Code. Under Chapter 11, the debtor would continue to manage the company.

The Drexel spokesman said he also expects the case to be converted to Chapter 11. The parent company filed under Chapter 11 of the U.S. Bankruptcy Code on Feb. 13.

Bank Polska said it is owed $5 million by Trade Finance for a short-term loan that was to be repaid around the time of the parent company's Chapter 11 filing. Mr. Arnason said the other creditors include the National Bank of Poland, which is owed $12 million.

Mr. Arnason said the largest creditor is DBL Finance Ltd., a Drexel unit that is in receivership in England. He said that unit is owed $28 million. According to the parent company's previous filings, it owed DBL Finance $141.3 million on Feb. 12.

The parent company has disclosed that it owed DBL Trade Finance $28 million on Feb. 12. Mr. Arnason said Drexel Burnham Lambert Trading Corp., which was pushed into bankruptcy proceedings last seek, is owed $20.5 million by DBL Trade Finance.

Mr. Arnason said many of the creditors' transactions with DBL Trade Finance involved foreign-exchange transactions.
