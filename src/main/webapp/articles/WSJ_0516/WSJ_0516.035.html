Australia Interest Rate Forecast

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/16/90

MELBOURNE, Australia -- Australian interest rates are likely to stay high next fiscal year and the nation's current-account deficit will deteriorate further, two economists forecast. Philip Adams and Peter Dixon from the Institute of Applied Economic and Social Research at Melbourne University predict the current-account deficit will widen to 19.7 billion Australian dollars (US$15.12 billion) for the fiscal year ending June 30 from A$17.41 billion last fiscal year, and deteriorate to $A20.53 billion next year.

This will push the current-account deficit to 5.4% of gross domestic product this fiscal year from 5.2% last year, and back to 5.2% next fiscal year.

The economists say in the latest issue of the Institute's Australian Economic Review that the current-account deficit will deteriorate because interest payments overseas will keep rising and prices for Australia's exports are likely to fall.
