Letters to the Editor: Editorial Trivializes Iran-Contra Misdeeds

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/16/90

I take serious exception to your efforts to trivialize the serious misdeeds of the principals in the Iran-Contra scandal. Your April 9 "After Poindexter" editorial is built on a mighty thin reed. Blaming Congress and the Democrats for "criminalizing of policy differences with the executive branch" begs a much larger issue here: Should members of the executive branch be allowed to withhold vital information from those members of Congress charged by law to monitor specific actions of the president? A lifelong Republican, Special Prosecutor Lawrence Walsh, didn't think so. An impartial grand jury that voted to indict Adm. John Poindexter didn't think so, nor did the 12 jurors who found him guilty on all five charges.

Your Iran-Contra editorials send the wrong message both to those who believe they can hide behind so-called patriotic acts without punishment, and also to those who cynically view such selective "law-and-order" posturing as further evidence that there indeed exist two standards of justice in our country.

John M. Burns

New York
