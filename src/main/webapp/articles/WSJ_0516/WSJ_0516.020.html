Who's News: Hoover Institution Names John Raisian as Director

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/16/90

PALO ALTO, Calif. -- The Hoover Institution, the conservative think tank at Stanford University, named John Raisian as its new director. Mr. Raisian, 40 years old, succeeds W. Glenn Campbell, who held the post from 1960 to 1989. Mr. Raisian is an economist who has served as Hoover's acting director since September and became the front-runner for the post after Michael Boskin, the first choice, withdrew himself from consideration in February. Mr. Boskin heads President Bush's Council of Economic Advisers.

During his stint as acting director, Mr. Raisian has been credited with defusing tensions -- years in the making -- between Hoover and Stanford's faculty over what some say are Hoover's ideologically driven research activities.

Despite the institution's widely viewed reputation for a conservative bias, Mr. Raisian said, "I have tried to take my case to the university: We do not have a mission or a point of view." He added that his efforts "are paying dividends" among Stanford faculty and that in the future he expects Hoover's resident fellows, who include such influential conservatives as economist Milton Friedman and physicist Edward Teller, to have more involvement with Stanford students.
