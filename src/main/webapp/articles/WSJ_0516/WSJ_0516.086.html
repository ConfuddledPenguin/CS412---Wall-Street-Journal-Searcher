Ames Stores Reports Sharp Decline in Sales During Recent Weeks

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/16/90

NEW YORK -- Ames Department Stores Inc., Rocky Hill, Conn., which filed for bankruptcy court protection last month, disclosed that its sales have plummeted in recent weeks. The discount department store chain, which reported a $228 million loss for the fiscal year ended Jan. 27, disclosed the recent sales slump at a bankruptcy court hearing yesterday to determine whether it can receive $250 million in interim financing from Chemical Bank.

Attorneys for various parties indicated they have reached an agreement resolving differences between Ames and pre-bankruptcy lenders, including Citibank, that had led those lenders to object to the new financing. The court indicated a decision on the financing would be made today.

According to testimony at the hearing, sales at Ames stores dropped 30%, to $67.9 million for the week ended May 5; and sales declined 42%, to $53.4 million for the week ended May 12.

Ames, which filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy-law protection on April 25, has had trouble keeping its shelves fully stocked as many vendors stopped shipping goods because they hadn't been paid for past shipments. Ames previously received bankruptcy court clearance for $25 million in financing. It has said it needs the additional $225 million in financing to continue its operation.

"If it doesn't have (additional) financing, it cannot survive, because they're not going to get goods shipped to them," said Wilbur Ross Jr. of Rothschild Inc., an adviser to certain creditors of Ames. He said he expects the financing to be approved.

Separately, Ames reported a net loss of $228 million, or $6.41 a share last year, compared with net income of $42 million, or $1.07 a share, in fiscal 1989. The latest year's loss includes a restructuring charge of $137 million to cover previously announced plans to close certain unprofitable stores.
