Business Brief -- Kent Electronics Corp.: Holder Rights Plan Adopted To Deter Hostile Takeovers

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/16/90

Kent Electronics Corp., Houston, said it adopted a shareholder rights plan intended to deter a hostile takeover. In connection with that plan, the company declared a distribution of one preferred stock purchase right on each Kent common share outstanding. The distribution will be made on May 24, 1990, to holders of record on that date, the company said. The rights are exercisable if any person or group acquires 20% or more of Kent's common stock, or announces an offer to acquire 30% or more of the common stock. Each right entitles shareholders to buy one one-hundredth of a share of Kent's Series A preferred stock, $1 par value per share, for $40. Each right also entitles shareholders to purchase Kent commmon stock, or securities of an acquiring entity, at half of market value.

The company said the actions were designed to assure that all shareholders receive fair treatment if any person or group attempts a takeover of the company. The company said it was not aware of any takeover plans.
