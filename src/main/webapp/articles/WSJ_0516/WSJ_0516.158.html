Ex-Im Bank and AID Plan $500 Million Pool To Help Exporters

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/16/90

WASHINGTON -- The U.S. Export Import Bank said it and the U.S. Agency for International Development will jointly create a $500 million pool to assist sales by U.S. exporters in highly competitive Third World markets. The funds will back U.S. exports in markets where other governments engage in "extensive, trade-distorting tied-aid activities," the Ex-Im Bank said. "Tied-aid" is credit given on the condition that the recipient country uses it to purchase goods and services from the country supplying the credit. The new U.S. lending pool will operate on the same principle.

The U.S. generally opposes use of tied-aid. Eugene Lawson, the Ex-Im Bank's vice chairman, said the kitty will be used to protect U.S. commercial interests while Washington is attempting to negotiate a comprehensive international accord restricting the use of tied-aid.

The Ex-Im Bank said the markets where the funds may be used include Indonesia, Pakistan, the Philippines and Thailand. Among the projects to be assisted are telecommunications, transportation, power and construction equipment.

The pool will be drawn from existing Ex-Im Bank and AID programs. Pool applicants have to meet AID's standards and country priorities, the bank said.
