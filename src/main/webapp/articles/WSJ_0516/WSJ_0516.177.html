What's News -- Business and finance

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/16/90

THE BUDGET DEFICIT is now expected to reach $128 billion to $140 billion for fiscal 1991, even if the cost of the thrift bailout is excluded, the White House said. The latest forecast, which came as Bush opened deficit talks with congressional leaders, suggests negotiators need to find up to $66 billion to avoid automatic spending cuts under Gramm-Rudman. ---

Circle K filed for Chapter 11 protection after failing to get creditor agreement on a debt restructuring. The nation's No. 2 convenience store chain said it is trying to get vendor cooperation to keep stores stocked.

---

A key bank-regulatory policy was struck down by a federal appeals court, which said the Fed doesn't have the power to force bank holding companies to help ailing units. The case involved Dallas-based MCorp.

---

First Executive said it paid over $1.33 billion in the first quarter to holders surrending insurance policies and annuities, creating a vast cash outflow for the Los Angeles-based insurance holding company. But officials said the pace of policy surrenders slowed sharply this quarter.

---

The Justice Department is pushing a bill to expand prosecutors' ability to freeze assets of defendants in fraud cases before trial and permanently seize their assets after conviction.

---

Manville's trust fund for asbestos victims came under fire as two judges ordered the trust to account for a huge shortfall in funds. The judges also indicated they may order the fund to pay damages more quickly.

---

Perkin-Elmer sold to a U.S. group a semiconductor-equipment unit that was the focus of concerns about Japanese inroads in the chip market.

---

Blue-chip stocks edged higher, pushing the the Dow Jones industrials up 0.92, to a record 2822.45. But the broader market declined due to profittaking. Bonds fell modestly, while the dollar was mixed.

---

Former law partners of the defunct firm of Finley Kumble can be sued by a bankruptcy trustee over debt payments, a judge ruled.

---

Industrial output fell 0.4% in April, reflecting a sharp drop in the auto industry and declines in most other sectors. Factories, utilities and mines operated at 83% of capacity, off from 83.5% in March. The reports were a further sign the economy is weak.

Car and truck sales fell 8.4% in early May, damping hopes the industry's year-long slump will end soon. Many dealers have cut new-car orders amid uncertainty that sales will start to rebound anytime soon.

---

Manufacturers Hanover's head of investment banking, Douglas Ebert, resigned after failing to win the top spot in a newly combined commercial and investment banking unit.

---

Nobel Industries agreed to acquire Spectra-Physics, a major U.S. producer of lasers, from Ciba-Geigy for an estimated $325 million.

---

Corn futures prices declined on unfounded rumors about a worsening political upheaval in the Soviet Union, a major grain buyer, and broad selling by commodity fund managers.
