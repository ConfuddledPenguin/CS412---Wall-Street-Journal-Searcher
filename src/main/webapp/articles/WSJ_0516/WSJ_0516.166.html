Business Brief -- Munsingwear Inc.: Firm Plans to Sell 2 Units, Ends Its Talks With Jacobs

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/16/90

Munsingwear Inc., saying talks broke off with Irwin Jacobs about the investor's proposal to take control of the struggling apparel maker, announced plans to seek buyers for two of its three units and to focus on menswear. Minneapolis-based Munsingwear, which got its start in the men's underwear business 104 years ago, also said Charles J. Campbell was appointed president and chief executive officer. Mr. Campbell, 45 years old, joined the company last year as president of the men's division and was responsible for the introduction of its "Grand Slam" line of golf clothes.

Mr. Campbell succeeds Lawrence L. Horsch as chief executive. Mr. Horsch, 55, will continue as chairman while the company seeks buyers for its LeRoi Princeton and Munsingwear Canada divisions, which make infants' clothes and women's sportswear, respectively. But Mr. Horsch's role will be greatly reduced.

Munsingwear and Mr. Jacobs said talks were terminated by mutual agreement.
