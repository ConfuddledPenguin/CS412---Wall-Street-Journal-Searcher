South Korean Trade With U.S.

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/16/90

SEOUL, South Korea -- Declining exports forced South Korea's trade surplus with the U.S. to narrow to $216 million between January and March from $1.17 billion a year earlier, the Trade and Industry Ministry reported. It was South Korea's smallest first-quarter trade surplus with its No. 1 trading partner since 1982, when Korea began to show bilateral annual trade surpluses on a sustained basis.

The plunge in bilateral surpluses was attributed to a fall in exports coupled with a rapid rise in imports.

South Korean exports to the U.S. in the first quarter decreased 7% from the same period last year to $4.19 billion, while imports from the U.S. jumped 19% to $3.97 billion.
