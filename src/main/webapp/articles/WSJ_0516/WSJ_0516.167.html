Business Brief -- Trans World Airlines: Loss Widened Significantly During the First Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/16/90

Trans World Airlines reported a first-quarter net loss of $143 million, significantly wider than the $83.1 million net loss a year earlier. The financially troubled company, which Chairman Carl Icahn took private in 1988, said that revenue rose 2.5% to $1.01 billion from $989.7 million a year earlier.

It said operating expenses rose more sharply. Costs rose by 9.3% to $1.1 billion from $1.02 billion during the year-earlier quarter.

The Mount Kisco, N.Y., company attributed most of the jump to a 35.7% rise in the price of jet fuel. It said the average jet fuel price rose to 73.7 cents a gallon from 54.3 cents a gallon during the first quarter of 1989. TWA said the increase cost the company an additional $52.9 million in fuel expenses compared with the year-earlier period.

It said the remaining $41.9 million jump in expenses was related to increased capacity and service changes.
