Cost of Fighter Is Put At More Than $63 Billion

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/16/90

WASHINGTON -- The Air Force disclosed that the radar-eluding fighter it's planning for the 21st century has a price tag of more than $63 billion. The new cost estimate, based on 750 planes, is at least $5 billion higher than the public estimates previously used by top Pentagon officials.

The plane, called the Advanced Tactical Fighter, is designed to be the first U.S. dog fighter to carry missiles entirely inside its fuselage, the Air Force said. Officials also confirmed speculation that the plane will have powerful twin engines intended to push it to supersonic speeds without using afterburners, making it more difficult to hit with heat-seeking missiles.

The Pentagon apparently hopes that releasing sketches of the sleek fighters and making more information public about their capabilities will help deflect congressional criticism of the program. The Pentagon said it already has spent about $2.8 billion to develop a pair of ATF prototypes. Two teams of contractors competing to build the plane have invested another $1.2 billion.

One of the teams, led by Lockheed Corp., includes Boeing Co. and General Dynamics Corp. The other team consists of Northrop Corp. and McDonnell Douglas Corp.

But many lawmakers increasingly question the huge price tag and, just last month, Defense Secretary Dick Cheney proposed to delay development of the aircraft for two years and to reduce production rates once the assembly line gets going.
