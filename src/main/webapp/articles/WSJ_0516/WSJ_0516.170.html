Who's News: McDonnell Douglas Helicopter Co. Unit's President Quits Post

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/16/90

LOS ANGELES -- William P. Brown resigned as president of McDonnell Douglas Helicopter Co. in the wake of the company's continuing poor financial performance. The decision was announced by the company's St. Louis-based parent, McDonnell Douglas Corp. Spokesmen there and at the helicopter company in Mesa, Ariz., refused to speculate on the reason for the resignation, which is effective Friday. Mr. Brown wasn't available to comment.

McDonnell Douglas said a successor will be named in a few weeks. Until then, William S. Ross, president of the St. Louis-based McDonnell Aircraft Co. unit, will take on the additional duty of overseeing the helicopter company.

Mr. Brown, 57 years old, will become a consultant to the parent company and will work out of an office in the Phoenix, Ariz., area, said a spokesman for the helicopter unit. The executive joined the company in 1980 as chief engineer on the Army's Apache helicopter program before the company was acquired by McDonnell Douglas.

Mr. Brown played an important role in smoothing the company's move in 1986 to Mesa from Culver City, Calif., McDonnell Douglas said. During his tenure, he also launched three helicopter programs and implemented a reorganization plan.

Last year, the helicopter company had a $137 million write-off, mainly related to the Apache program, which accounted for about 80% of the company's revenue. In February, the company said it was trimming its work force and making other spending cuts to improve its financial performance and lessen the effect of reductions in defense spending.
