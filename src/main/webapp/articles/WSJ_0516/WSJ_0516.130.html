New Securities Issues

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/16/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATES

Delta Air Lines Inc. -- $300 million two-part offering of debt securities, priced through Goldman Sachs & Co. The first part, which consists of $175 million of 9 7/8% notes, due May 15, 2000, priced at 99.48 to yield 9.958%. The noncallable notes were priced at a spread of 130 basis points above the Treasury's 10-year note. The issue was increased in size from an originally planned $125 million. The second part, which consists of $125 million of debentures, due May 15, 2010, was priced at par to yield 10.125%. The noncallable debentures were priced at a spread of 150 basis points above the Treasury's 30-year bond. The issue is rated single-A-2 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and single-A by Standard & Poor's Corp.

Fleet/Norstar Financial Group Corp -- $150 million issue of notes, due May 15, 1993, priced at par to yield 9.95%. The noncallable notes were priced at a spread of 135 basis points above the Treasury's three-year note. Rated Baa-1 by Moody's and single-A-minus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets.

Golden West Financial Corp. -- $115 million of 10 1/4% subordinated notes, due May 15, 1997, priced at 99.815 to yield 10.287%. The noncallable notes were priced at a spread of 160 basis points above the Treasury's seven-year note. Rated Baa-1 by Moody's and single-A-minus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets.

MUNICIPALS

Texas National Research Laboratory Commission -- $250 million superconducting super collider project general obligation bonds, Series 1990, due April 1, 1993-2008, 2011 and 2020, apparently won by a Chase Securities Inc. group. The bonds, rated double-A by both Moody's and S&P, were priced to yield from 6.30% in 1993 to 7.25% in 2020. Current interest serial bonds are priced to yield from 6.30% in 1993 to 7.15% in 2007 and 2008. There is approximately $29 million of 7 1/8% term bonds priced at 99 1/4 to yield 7.20% in 2011 and $130 million of 7 1/8% term bonds priced at 98 1/2 to yield 7.25% in 2020.

New York State Environmental Facilities Corp. -- $166.5 million state water pollution control revolving revenue bonds, Series 1990 A, New York City Municipal Water Finance Authority Project, due June 15, 1993-2004, and 2012, were repriced by a Goldman Sachs & Co. group to lower yields by nine to 10 basis points. The bonds are now priced to yield from 6.60% in 1993 to 7.63% in 2012, changed from priced to yield from 6.70% in 1993 to 7.72% in 2012. Current interest serial bonds are now priced to yield from 6.60% in 1993 to 7.45% in 2004, changed from priced to yield from 6.70% in 1993 to 7.55% in 2004. There is $95.49 million of 7 1/2% term bonds priced at 98.67 to yield 7.63% in 2012, changed from 7.63% securities priced at 99 to yield 7.72% in 2012. The bonds are rated double-A by Moody's and single-A by S&P.

Hawaii -- $100 million airport system revenue bonds, due July 1, 1993-2003, 2010 and 2020, tentatively priced by a Goldman Sachs & Co. group to yield from 6.45% in 1993 to 7.50% in 2020. Current interest serial bonds are priced to yield from 6.45% in 1993 to 7.25% in 2003. There is $22.21 million of 7 3/8% term bonds priced at 99.21 to yield 7.45% in 2010 and $58.77 million of 7.30% term bonds priced at 97.61 to yield 7.50% in 2020. The bonds are AMBAC-insured and rated triple-A by both Moody's and S&P. Interest on the bonds will be treated as a preference item in calculating the federal alternative minimum tax that may be imposed on certain investors.

Los Angeles Department of Water and Power, Calif. -- $100 million electric plant revenue bonds, due May 15, 1996-2010, and 2030, apparently won by a Merrill Lynch Capital Markets group. The bonds, rated Double-A by Moody's and S&P, were priced to yield from 6.50% in 1996 to 7.16% in 2030. Current interest serial bonds are priced to yield from 6.50% in 1996 to 7.05% in 2008-2010. There is $66 million of 7 1/8% term bonds priced at 99 1/2 to yield 7.16% in 2030.

Washington State Housing Finance Commission -- $99.4 million single-family mortgage revenue refunding bonds, Series 1990 A, due July 1, 2016, and revenue bonds, Series 1990 B, due Jan. 1 and July 1, 1994-2000, July 1, 2010 and 2023 and Jan. 1, 2012, with capital appreciation bonds due Jan. 1 and July 1, 2001-2005, tentatively priced by a Shearson Lehman Hutton Inc. group to yield from 6.65% for AMT bonds due in 1994 to 7.95% for AMT bonds due in 2023. Series A consists of a single portion of $19.37 million 7.70% bonds priced at par in 2016. Series B consists of current interest serial bonds priced at par to yield from 6.65% in 1994 to 7.25% in 2000. There is $12.63 million of 7.55% bonds priced at par due in 2010. There is $4.93 million 7.85% term bonds priced at par due in 2012 and $47.58 million 7.95% term bonds priced at par due in 2023. Capital appreciation bonds are priced to yield to maturity from 7.40% in 2001 to 7.75% in 2005. The bonds are rated triple-A by S&P. Interest on the Series B bonds will be treated as a preference item in calculating the Federal alternative minimum tax that may be imposed on certain investors.

Hillsborough County School District, Fla. -- $98.6 million general obligation refunding bonds, due Aug. 15, 1990-2000, 2003 and 2005, tentatively priced by a Merrill Lynch Capital Markets group to yield from 6% in 1990 to 7.15% in 2005. Current interest serial bonds are priced to yield from 6% in 1990 to 7% in 2000. There is $23.71 million of 7.05% term bonds priced at par in 2003 and $18.54 million of 7.15% term bonds priced at par in 2005. The bonds are rated single-A-1 by Moody's and single-A-plus by S&P.

Berks County, Pa. -- $88.03 million general obligation bonds, Series 1990, due Nov. 15, 1993-2005, 2010, 2015 and 2020, tentatively priced by a Meridian Capital Markets group to yield from 6.10% in 1993 to 7.30% in 2020. Current interest serial bonds are priced to yield from 6.10% in 1993 to 7.10% in 2005. There are $5.95 million 7.10% term bonds priced at 99 3/8 to yield 7.16% in 2010. There is $33.33 million of7 1/8% term bonds priced at 98.55 to yield 7.25% in 2015 and $47.72 million of 7 1/8% term bonds priced at 97.87 to yield 7.30% in 2020. The bonds are FGIC-insured and rated tripleA by both Moody's and S&P.

Baltimore, Md. -- $85.4 million refunding certificates of participation, due April 1, 1991-2005, 2007, 2010 and 2016, entatively priced by a Legg Mason Wood Walker Inc. group to yield from 6.10% in 1991 to 7.34% in 2016. Current interest serial bonds are priced at par to yield from 6.10% in 1991 to 7.15% in 2005. Term bonds due in 2007 are priced as 7.15% securities at par and term bonds due in 2010 are priced as 7.20% securities at 99 1/4 to yield 7.27%. Term bonds due in 2016 are priced as 7 1/4% securities at 99 to yield 7.34%. The bonds are rated MBIA-insured and rated triple-A by both Moody's and S&P.

Los Angeles, California -- $72 million improvement election revenue bonds, 1989 Series A, due Sept. 1, 1991-2010, were won by a Prudential-Bache Capital Funding group. The bonds, rated triple-A by Moody's and double-A, were priced to yield from 5.90% in 1991 to 6.88% in 2007-2010.

MORTGAGE-BACKED

Ryland Acceptance Corp. -- $315 million of Remic mortgage securities, offered in 13 classes by Kidder Peabody & Co. The offering, Series 94, is backed by Ginnie Mae 10% securities, with a weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29 years.

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $190 million of Remic mortgage securities, offered in five classes by First Boston Corp. The offering, Series 1990-66, is backed by principal-only strips from Fannie Mae 9 1/2% securities from existing Fannie Mae's strips trusts 1 and 43.
