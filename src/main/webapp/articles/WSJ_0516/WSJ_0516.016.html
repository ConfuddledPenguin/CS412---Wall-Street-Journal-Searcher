Who's News: Investment-Management Director at SEC Resigns

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/16/90

WASHINGTON -- Kathryn McGrath, director of investment management at the Securities and Exchange Commission, resigned effective early June to enter private practice. Ms. McGrath, a 16-year SEC veteran who has headed the division for nearly seven years, will become a partner in the Washington office of the law firm Morgan, Lewis & Bockius.

The investment-management division regulates the mutual-fund industry, which has grown furiously in the past decade to $1 trillion, as well as other investment companies and utility holding companies. Under Ms. McGrath, 45 years old, the division has pushed for better mutual-fund disclosure while at the same time trying to scale back the size of prospectuses sent to investors.

It is unclear who will succeed Ms. McGrath. Her one-time most likely successor, Marianne Smythe, recently moved from a division associate director slot to executive assistant to the chairman. The mutual-fund industry, which has often battled Ms. McGrath in the past, is likely to push its own candidates for the job.
