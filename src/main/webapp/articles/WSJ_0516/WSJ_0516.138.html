Business Brief -- Bombardier Inc.: Unit of Lufthansa Places First Firm Order for Jet

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/16/90

Bombardier Inc. said Lufthansa German Airlines' DLT Deutsche Luftverkehrs G.m.b.H. unit placed the first firm order for the Montreal-based transportation equipment and aerospace concern's 50-seat Regional Jet. DLT, a West German regional airline, ordered 13 RJs, valued at about US$250 million, including spare parts and training, and took options for 12 additional RJs, subject to Lufthansa board approval. The passenger plane is a stretched version of the Challenger jet developed and made by Bombardier's Canadair unit. RJ deliveries are to begin in 1992. The DLT purchase contract brings to 139 Bombardier's orders and options commitments for the RJ. Since the RJ program was begun in November 1988, eight other airlines have signed order commitments and taken options.

Analysts expect the market for small commercial jets such as the RJ to explode in the next few years. Airlines are expected to increase so-called long thin routes bypassing hub airports as they reach maximum capacity; the RJ is the first jet designed for such routes.
