Van Gogh Work Draws Record Sum Of $82.5 Million

  • Author: Alexandra Peers
  • Date: 05/16/90

NEW YORK -- The world's record for a work of art was shattered last night at Christie's International PLC's major spring art auction as Vincent van Gogh's "Portrait of Dr. Gachet," fetched $82.5 million. The heartbreakingly beautiful oil-on-canvas of the troubled painter's physician and friend, which formerly hung in the Metropolitan Museum of Art on loan from its owner, was reportedly purchased by Kobayashi Gallery of Tokyo. It had been expected to sell for $40 million to $50 million.

At Christie's, the packed saleroom erupted in applause as the fevered bidding broke $50 million, appearing to put to rest concerns that the art boom, at least regarding the great works, is history. However, while the sale was by-and-large successful, with the majority of the artworks selling, the prices fetched tended to be at or below Christie's lowest presale estimate. The prices indicated a cooling of the recent art market frenzy. But while bidding wasn't quite as frantic as in recent years, it proceeded at a fast clip. About 19% of the art works went unsold.

The record price for the van Gogh eclipsed the previous record for van Gogh's "Irises," which was set at Sotheby's auction house in November 1987 and was later tainted by the disclosure that the purchaser, Australian businessman, Alan Bond, had never paid for it. "Irises" was later sold to the Getty Museum in Los Angeles, for a price that wasn't disclosed.

The attention now turns to Sotheby's Holdings Inc.'s auction Thursday evening. The centerpiece of the Sotheby's auction is Pierre-Auguste Renoir's "Au Moulin de la Gallette." It is expected to sell for $40 million to $50 million.
