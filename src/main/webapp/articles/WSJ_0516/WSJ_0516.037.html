Letters to the Editor: Inflation Impoverishes Core of Middle Class

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/16/90

Your April 16 page-one story "Steady Inflation: Does Anyone Care?" suggests that "perhaps most Americans are correct not to care about inflation." In a highly intensive and largely mature economy, with 80% of our GNP comprising services, inflation of 4.5% is not manageable, it is pernicious. It causes a small percentage of our people to get temporarily rich and a big percentage to become permanently poor. It has impoverished and greatly eroded the core of our middle class and established a propensity among us to spend rather than to save, to speculate rather than to invest and to lose respect for the hard-earned dollar.

Inflation is the worst form of taxation. As our Fed appears, at least, to be committed to fight inflation, we should see to it that it has many allies.

Emil Sherer Finley

President and CEO

International Commodities Export Corp.

Purchase, N.Y.
