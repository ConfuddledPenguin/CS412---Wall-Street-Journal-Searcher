Business Brief -- Tiffany & Co.: Retailer Reports 29% Rise In Net for Its First Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/16/90

Tiffany & Co., a New York-based retailer of fine gifts and jewelry, reported net income for its first quarter ended April 30 rose 29% to $5.4 million, or 35 cents a share, from $4.2 million, a 27 cents a share, a year earlier. Sales increased 27% to $93.2 million from $73.2 million. Year-earlier share net is adjusted to reflect a 3-for-2 stock split paid in July 1989.

Tiffany's international retail sales more than doubled, mainly because of strong demand in Asia and Europe, the company said. But U.S. retail sales remained roughly flat, "which we believe reflected consumer caution in certain regions of the U.S.," Tiffany Chairman William R. Chaney said.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Tiffany shares closed at $43.25, up $1.
