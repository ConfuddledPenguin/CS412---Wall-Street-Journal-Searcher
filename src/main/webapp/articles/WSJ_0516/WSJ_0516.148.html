Business Brief -- J.P. Morgan & Co.: Primary-Dealer Status Won For Italian Bond Market

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/16/90

J.P. Morgan & Co. of New York is the first non-Italian bank to be named a primary dealer in Italian government securities. Morgan received final approval from the Bank of Italy and will begin quoting prices today on the screen-based trading system for the secondary bond market. Daily turnover on the regulated market, in which liquidity is estimated to have doubled in the past year, is about 1.4 trillion lire ($1.16 billion), according to banking sources, while over-the-counter volume has been estimated around four trillion lire, though exact figures aren't available.

Hundreds of banks, including other foreign banks, deal in the secondary market, but Morgan is one of 20 authorized by the Bank of Italy to quote bid and offer prices continuously for Italy's government paper. These banks meet minimum capital requirements set by the central bank.

Morgan said it believes the Italian market holds unrecognized potential for international investors. It cited high yields, new confidence in the lira, and Italy's liberalization of foreign exchange barriers.
