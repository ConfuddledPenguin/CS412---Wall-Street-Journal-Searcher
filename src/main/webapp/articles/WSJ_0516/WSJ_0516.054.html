Hong Kong Business Survey

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/16/90

HONG KONG -- Hong Kong manufacturers' business was hurt during the first quarter amid diminished orders, stiffer competition and increased costs, but improvement is expected in the current quarter, according to a government survey. The study, based on interviews with approximately 1,000 concerns, found that companies in the textile, plastics and clothing industries experienced the downturn most markedly. But improved orders are expected for the April-June period, particularly in the plastics, metals and appliances industries.

Construction companies, buoyed by the colony's continuing building boom, reported better business conditions in the first half and anticipate further improvement during the current period.

Banks and real-estate concerns reported stable business for the first period and expect conditions to remain in place for the remainder of the first half.
