Business Brief -- Boeing Co.: A Swedish Carrier Converts Jet Options to Firm Orders

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/05/90

Boeing Co. said that Linjeflyg, a Swedish domestic airline, converted options on four 737-500 jetliners into firm orders that Boeing valued at $130 million, including spare parts. The initial order, placed in August 1988, included six 737-500s and options on six more. All of the planes ordered by Linjeflyg will be powered by engines produced by a joint venture of General Electric Co., Fairfield, Conn., and France's state-owned Snecma.

Seattle-based Boeing said the latest order brings to 215 the total orders for the advanced-technology twinjet. The 737-500s can be configured to seat between 108 and 132 passengers. Boeing said that initial deliveries of the 737-500 to other customers began in February.
