Trump's New Book Is Likely to Read Like a Mystery Tale --- Entrepreneur Asks Publisher To Wait Until He Finds Out How the Story Ends

  • Author: Meg Cox
  • Date: 06/05/90

LAS VEGAS -- Donald Trump's personal and financial tribulations are giving him cold feet over a second trip to the bookstore. "Trump: Surviving at the Top," is due for publication by Random House in October, a sequel to the wildly successful "Trump: The Art of the Deal," which sold between 900,000 and one million copies in 1987. But speaking at a breakfast at the American Booksellers Association convention here yesterday, Mr. Trump said he called Random House associate publisher Peter Osnos several days ago and "tried to put the book off, postpone it for a year."

In a jocular speech, Mr. Trump told the ABA crowd that he was persuaded to go ahead in spite of his reservations by Random House executives. But, he said, "We may have to end certain chapters with a question mark. We may have to end the whole book with a question mark."

Mr. Trump, usually not shy about publicity, said he told Random House's Mr. Osnos, "This really isn't good timing for this book: There are so many unknowns. I'm getting the worst press of my life. How can we bring this book out now? I mean, the marriage, the this, the that. I named 10 other small things" that are unresolved. Mr. Trump was referring to the much-publicized rift with his wife, Ivana, and to reports of concerns that he won't be able to meet interest payments due in the next few months. Yesterday, Trump Organization said it is "meeting with major banks and having cooperative and constructive discussions" regarding the company's assets.

Mr. Trump, who said he is still finishing "Surviving at the Top," added that he still thinks it might be better to postpone the book until everything is resolved. "Then I'll know how the marriage turned out, whether my victory over Merv Griffin was really devastating." Mr. Griffin's Resorts International Inc. is under bankruptcy protection following a well-publicized battle for control with Mr. Trump. "I told him {Mr. Osnos} that Chapter 11 has to be the Merv Griffin chapter," Mr. Trump joked at the booksellers meeting.

Mr. Trump was persuaded to write a second book by his friend S.I. Newhouse Jr., whose family's Advance Publications Inc. owns Random House.

While he didn't specifically comment on his financial situation, Mr. Trump did read a paragraph from his new book that could be taken as a response to those skeptical of his chances. "Toughness is being a gracious winner and bouncing back when you lose," he read. ". . . Some say I'm at the beginning of the end, but I say this is just the beginning of the beginning."
