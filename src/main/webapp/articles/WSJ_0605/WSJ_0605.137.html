National Bancshares Says It Plans to File For Chapter 11 Soon

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/05/90

SAN ANTONIO, Texas -- National Bancshares Corp. of Texas, which continues to own three Texas banks after nine others were declared insolvent, expects to seek protection under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code in "the next day or so," an attorney for the company said. The bank holding company said Friday it would file immediately for bankruptcy-law protection; however, the attorney said the company is still preparing papers for the filing.

Regulators declared the nine banks insolvent Friday and sold their deposits and some assets to NCNB Texas National Bank, a unit of NCNB Corp. The action left National Bancshares with banks in Rockdale, Laredo and Eagle Pass.

The attorney said that National Bancshares has an estimated $8 million to $9 million in non-bank assets, such as real estate, and that the three banks' combined asset value is estimated at $9 million to $12 million. However, he said, the company's true asset value could be $5 million to $20 million, depending on regulators' claims.

National Bancshares has estimated liabilities of $35 million to $40 million, the attorney said, including $25 million in convertible subordinated debentures and about $4.9 million in debt owed to Federal Asset Management Corp., a company liquidating assets of the former Texas American Bancshares Inc. of Fort Worth.
