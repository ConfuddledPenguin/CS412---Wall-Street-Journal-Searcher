Washington Lawyer Faces Securities-Fraud Charges

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/05/90

NASHVILLE, N.C. -- A Washington, D.C., lawyer who drew national attention last summer when his private plane crashed into the Atlantic Ocean has been indicted on 455 counts of securities fraud and related charges. A grand jury here returned a total of 2,695 counts of securities fraud and other charges against Sonrise Management Services of Columbus, Ga., Telemedia Inc. of Landover, Md., several officers and owners of the two firms and Thomas Root, the Washington lawyer who allegedly provided legal services for Sonrise clients.

Mr. Root, in a July flight from Washington to Rocky Mount, N.C., reported blacking out in the cockpit of his single-engine plane. The aircraft, followed by military planes, eventually crashed in the Caribbean. Mr. Root survived the crash, but was pulled from the water with a gunshot wound in his stomach.

The charges arise from Sonrise's claims that individuals who invested in the company would receive help in applying for licenses for radio stations from the Federal Communications Commission. The district attorney in Nashville said only a handful of Sonrise's more than 1,600 investors -- who put down more than $16 million -- ever received an FCC license.

Officials at Sonrise and Telemedia, an engineering-consulting firm allegedly involved with Sonrise, couldn't be reached for comment. Mr. Root was also unavailable for comment.
