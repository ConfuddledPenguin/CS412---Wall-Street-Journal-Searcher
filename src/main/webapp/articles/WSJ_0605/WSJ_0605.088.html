Marketing & Media: Holder of Playboy Enterprises Objects To Plan for 2 Stocks

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/05/90

CHICAGO -- Playboy Enterprises Inc.'s plan to convert its thinly traded common stock into separate voting and nonvoting classes is under fire from a major shareholder, Industrial Equity (Pacific) Ltd. The plan, announced last month by the publishing and entertainment concern, would allow it to issue more shares while preserving founder Hugh Hefner's control of the company. The 64-year-old former chairman and chief executive, who owns a 71% stake, could retain voting control of Playboy while disposing of up to three-quarters of his holdings.

But Industrial Equity, an investment firm based in Hong Kong, whose 7.6% stake makes it Playboy's second largest holder, objects. In a May 31 letter to Playboy, IEP Consultants (USA) Inc., an affiliate of Industrial Equity, said the stock conversion proposal is unfair to holders who have purchased their shares on a one-share, one-vote structure.

The letter, which was attached to a Securities and Exchange Commission filing, specifically objected to the idea of Mr. Hefner retaining voting control if he sells his majority interest.

"The proposed plan gives all stockholders the same proportionate voting rights and the same flexibility to determine their continuing ownership of voting and nonvoting stock," a Playboy spokesman said. "The plan contains an unusual minority protection provision that is designed to help ensure price parity between the two classes of stock, and we plan to go forward with the plan as described in the company's consent solicitation statement."

Industrial Equity officials didn't return calls seeking further comment.

Dual-class arrangements aren't rare in the publishing business: founding families of New York Times Co., Dow Jones & Co. and Washington Post Co., for example, all maintain voting control through special classes of stock.

Playboy's plan calls for it to carry out a 1-for-2 reverse split of its current shares, reducing the number of common shares outstanding to 4.7 million from 9.4 million. Those shares will then be reclassified as Class A stock, each having one vote. The Class A shares will then each pay a dividend of three shares of nonvoting Class B Playboy shares, which the company will seek to have traded on the New York Stock Exchange.

Playboy has said the plan will provide management with additional financial flexibility by permitting it to use equity for possible future acquisitions, financings and employee benefit plans -- without diluting Mr. Hefner's control.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Playboy closed at $12.75, up 12.5 cents.
