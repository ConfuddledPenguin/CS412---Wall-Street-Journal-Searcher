Intel Warrants Expiration

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/05/90

SANTA CLARA, Calif. -- Intel Corp. said it accelerated the expiration of its 1992 warrants. The company said that any warrants not exercised will expire at the close of business on July 25. Under the company's warrant agreement, Intel can accelerate the expiration whenever the stock price closes at $42 or higher for any 20 trading days within a period of 30 trading days. Intel said it met those conditions yesterday. Intel's stock closed at $48.50 a share, up $1, in national over-the-counter trading yesterday.

Each warrant entitles the holder to buy one and one-half shares of Intel's common stock for $45 a warrant exercised. The microcomputer maker currently has 3.5 million warrants outstanding and expects to raise about $157 million.
