Business Brief -- Franklin Savings Bank: Michigan Thrift Files Papers To Be State-Chartered Bank

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/05/90

Franklin Savings Bank said it applied to the Michigan Financial Institutions Bureau and the Office of Thrift Supervision to convert to a state-chartered commercial bank from its current status as a federally chartered savings bank. The conversion is subject to shareholder and regulatory approvals.

Franklin, based in Southfield, Mich., was the first savings institution to apply for a national commercial bank charter after enactment of the Financial Institutions Reform, Recovery and Enforcement Act of 1989. However, Franklin officials said they are now seeking the state charter because of delays in promulgating the regulations and uncertainties over the interpretation of such conversions under the federal act.
