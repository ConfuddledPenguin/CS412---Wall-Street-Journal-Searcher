Monday's Markets: Industrial Average Sets Record at 2935.19

  • Author: Christopher Winans and Craig Torres
  • Date: 06/05/90

NEW YORK -- Stock prices jumped to record levels on brisk volume, with investors snapping up issues that are sensitive to the monthlong drop in interest rates. Bond prices rose slightly in slow trading, adding fuel to the stock-market rally. Helping nudge bonds higher were falling commodities prices, especially oil and gold, which signal lower inflation. The dollar was mixed.

The Dow Jones Industrial Average surged 34.22 points to close at a record 2935.19 in active trading. Records also were set by Standard & Poor's 500-stock index and, for the first time in this rally, by the New York Stock Exchange composite index. The S&P 500 closed at 367.40, up 4.24 points, and the Big Board composite closed at 200.21, up 2.18 points, breaking a record set Oct. 9. The Big Board index measures all the more than 1,700 common stocks traded on the exchange.

"Money managers are really feeling the pressure," said James Solloway, chief economist at Argus Research. "As of April, we saw some of the highest cash levels ever recorded -- even higher than in 1982. With that much cash, managers are very vulnerable to underperforming the market."

With each record high, money managers with cash on their books become more nervous, traders said. Many institutional stockholders are showing little inclination to sell, so money managers are paying more for stocks they want to buy.

"A lot of these funds are supposed to be 100% invested, but they have some cash," says Dudley Eppel, head of equity trading at Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette. "They are trying to buy intelligently, but there are no sellers."

Mr. Eppel says money managers and asset allocators with cash levels of 30% or more are "sitting there with sweaty palms; they've turned ashen white."

Anxious investors looking for cheap stocks to buy turned to many financial issues, which have lagged the market much of this year. These stocks are trading at much lower multiples of their per-share earnings than the overall market, making them attractive to many investors, especially mutual-fund managers with large stockpiles of uninvested cash. American Express, for example, which closed $1.875 higher at $31.625 a share, is trading at 12.5 times estimated 1990 earnings. Currently, the comparable price-earnings ratio of the industrial average is 14.

Other investors went after airline stocks, which also have been trading at low price-earnings ratios and are benefiting from the recent and continuing decline in oil prices. Their buying spree pushed the Dow Jones Transportation Average 18.43 higher to close at 1207.85.

Edward Shopkorn, director of Mabon Nugent's equity department says: "The market acts incredibly well. People want to buy stocks; any further downward movement in interest rates and the Dow Jones Industrial Average could hit 3000."

Ronald Sloan, executive vice president with Siebel Capital Management Inc., said "the magnet of 3000 in the Dow industrials is proving irresistible. It's like a flame and the moths are drawn to it. I don't know what happens when we get there, but obviously we are getting there."

However, Mr. Sloan worries that the stock market may be overvalued relative to bonds. "Earnings have to build the story from here, and I have been disappointed by earnings so far," he says.

Investors are justified in expecting earnings growth in certain sectors, said Mr. Solloway, "but the approach we're seeing now is a bit indiscriminate."

In major market action:

Stock prices climbed in active trading, with 175.5 million shares changing hands on the Big Board. Advancing issues outnumbered decliners, 1,105 to 477.

Bond prices rose slightly. The benchmark 30-year U.S. Treasury bond gained more than an eighth of a point, or more than $1.25 for each $1,000 of face amount, in slow trading. The yield was 8.42%.

The dollar was mixed, rising against the yen partly because of the strong stock market. In late afternoon New York trading, the currency was at 1.6855 marks and 152.40 yen, compared with 1.6980 marks and 151.85 yen Friday.
