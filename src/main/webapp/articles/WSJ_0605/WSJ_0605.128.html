Accel and Exchange To Merge by Year End

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/05/90

SEATTLE -- Two electronic banking networks in the Pacific Northwest, the Exchange and Accel, said they agreed to merge by the end of the year. The merger would create a network of 2,892 automated teller machines, or ATMs, in Washington, Oregon, Idaho, Montana, Alaska and Canada's western provinces, representatives for the networks said. The details of the merger will be worked out over the next 120 days, they said.

The Exchange, which provides electronic-banking and data-processing services, operates a network of 2,000 ATMs representing over 250 financial institutions. Accel, a network of 50 financial institutions, has about 2000 ATMs.
