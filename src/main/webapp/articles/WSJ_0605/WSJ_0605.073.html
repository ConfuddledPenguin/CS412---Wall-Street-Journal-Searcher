Alaska Air's May Traffic Rose

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/05/90

SEATTLE -- Alaska Airlines, a unit of Alaska Air Group Inc., said that its May traffic rose 14% to 340.4 million revenue passenger miles from 299 million flown a year earlier. A revenue passenger mile is one paying passenger flown one mile. The month's passenger load factor, or the percentage of available seats occupied by fare-paying passengers, rose slightly to 50.2% from 50.9% in May 1989.

For the five months ended in May, revenue passenger miles totaled 1.62 billion, up 18% from 1.38 billion the year earlier. The five-month load factor was 49.4%, down slightly from 50% the year before.
