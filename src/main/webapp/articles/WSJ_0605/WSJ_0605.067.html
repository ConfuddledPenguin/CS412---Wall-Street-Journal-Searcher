Nova Corp. Agrees To Sell Valve Unit To Big Italian Bank

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/05/90

CALGARY, Alberta -- Nova Corp. of Alberta said it agreed to sell its valve-making subsidiary, Grove Italia S.p.A., to an affiliate of one of Italy's biggest banks, for 121 billion lire (US$96.8 million). The planned sale, to an investment group one-third owned by Sanpaolo Finance S.p.A., a unit of Instituto Bancario San Paolo di Torino, is expected to generate a net gain to Nova of about 30 million Canadian dollars (US$25.6 million), or 10 cents a share. Nova had first-quarter net income of C$36 million, or 11 cents a share, on revenue of C$1.2 billion.

The sale, the second announced by Nova in the past two weeks, was greeted with enthusiasm by investors. In heavy Toronto Stock Exchange trading yesterday, Nova closed at C$9.125, up 50 cents on volume of about 6.8 million shares.

Nova announced late last month a pact to sell its rubber operations to Bayer AG of West Germany, for C$1.25 billion. Proceeds from both transactions will be used to reduce Nova's long-term non-utility debt, which swelled with its C$1.3 billion purchase of Polysar Energy & Chemical Corp. in 1988.

Nova, which has interests in pipelines, oil and gas, and petrochemicals, expects long-term debt from its unregulated businesses to fall to C$450 million to C$500 million once the transactions are completed, down from C$2.01 billion at March 31. The shrunken debt figure includes the impact of debt and other liabilities to be assumed by Bayer.

"Nova would be very well capitalized" once the transactions are completed, "and in a good position to grow its polyethylene business" when prices for that commodity rebound, said John McKimm, an analyst in Toronto with Prudential-Bache Securities Canada Ltd.

Grove Italia is one of four non-core units that Nova put up for sale nearly a year ago. It estimated the value of the group at C$500 million. A spokesman said Nova is continuing to pursue the sale of the other units, though he didn't offer details.
