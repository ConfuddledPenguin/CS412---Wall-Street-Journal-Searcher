Business Brief -- Builders Transport Inc.: Agreement for Acquisition By Comcar Industries Ends

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/05/90

Builders Transport Inc.. Camden, S.C., said a tentative agreement to be acquired by Comcar Industries Inc. has collapsed. But the trucking company added that a second takeover proposal from Trism Inc. is still being pursued.

In February, Builders Transport signed a non-binding letter of intent to be acquired for $15 a share, or $73.6 million, by closely held Comcar, an Auburndale, Fla., trucking company.

Yesterday, however, Builders Transport said that Comcar isn't willing to proceed with the transaction and that the letter of intent is "no longer operative."

In national over-the-counter trading yesterday, Builders Transport closed at $10, down 75 cents.

Builders Transport added that Trism, the Joplin, Mo., parent of trucking firm Tri-State Motor Transit Inc. of Delaware, has completed its due diligence and is working to secure financing. Trism has proposed to pay $14 cash plus $3 of preferred stock for each Builders Transport share.

Builders Transport said it is still hopeful that a transaction with Trism, Comcar or some other party may be completed. But it said it is also considering other alternatives, including an internal restructuring.

Meanwhile, the company said its annual meeting will be held on Sept. 18, adding that it had waited to set the date so that all options could be considered at a single meeting.
