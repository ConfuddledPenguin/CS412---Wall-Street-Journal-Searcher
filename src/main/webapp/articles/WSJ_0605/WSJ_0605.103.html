Business Brief -- Columbia Hospital Corp.: Definitive Pact Set to Buy HEI for $22 Million Total

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/05/90

Columbia Hospital Corp. of Fort Worth, Texas, said it definitively agreed to acquire HEI Corp. of Houston, also a hospital company, for $4.50 a share, or about $22 million. Columbia said it plans to begin by next Monday a tender offer for HEI that will be contingent on at least 90% of HEI's shares being tendered. In national over-the-counter trading yesterday, HEI shares jumped $1.46875, to close at $4.125.

HEI, which owns three Texas hospitals, repeatedly rebuffed offers last year from Hospital Partners Ltd., a San Antonio limited partnership, saying it wasn't for sale. But Robert Hux, HEI president and chief executive officer, said Columbia provided a commitment letter from Citibank, showing it could finance the transaction, while Hospital Partners didn't have financing.

Columbia, founded in 1987 by Richard L. Scott and a former Bass family adviser, Richard E. Rainwater, operates seven hospitals in Texas and Florida. It recently became publicly traded when it merged with Smith Laboratories Inc., a San Diego-based medical equipment concern.
