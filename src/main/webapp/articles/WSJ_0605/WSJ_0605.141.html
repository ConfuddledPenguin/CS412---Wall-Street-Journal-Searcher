NASD Mulls Plan To Require Brokers To Requalify in Test

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/05/90

WASHINGTON -- The self-regulatory agency for the over-the-counter market is considering a plan to require securities brokers to requalify by examination every three years. The National Association of Securities Dealers is calling for public comment on the plan, which would exempt brokers who already are registered when the program begins.

"We don't envision this as a back-to-the-basics exam," said Ronald E. Buesinger, vice chairman of the NASD and director of the securities firm A.G. Edwards Inc.'s division that handles personnel and training. "This is kind of an update test, focusing on new" rules, regulations, securities products and tax laws.

NASD has been working on the plan with the North American Securities Administrators Association, which includes the securities agencies of all 50 states. And the states have been cracking down on incompetence and wrongdoing in the securities field, particularly in the penny-stock industry.

Lee Polson, executive director of the administrators group, said that the states "have been increasingly requiring" professionals "to demonstrate professional competence." He says, "If it's good for the doctors and lawyers, why isn't it good for the . . . broker-dealers" as well.

He noted that a number of states were developing their own requirements specifically for the securities industry. "It would be better to have a single across-the-board proposal," allowing for more-uniform measurement of competence, Mr. Polson said.

The plan proposes that if a broker fails a recertification test four consecutive times, the broker's registration would be revoked and he or she would have to pass the required entry-level exams for new brokers.

Mr. Buesinger said comments will be taken for 60 days. If the NASD board and Securities and Exchange Commission approve the plan, the agency hopes to implement the plan beginning Jan. 1, 1991, he said. If that happens, the first exam would be administered in calendar year 1993, he said.
