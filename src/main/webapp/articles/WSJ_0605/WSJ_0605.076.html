Marketing & Media: Top Louisville, Ky., Station Leaves CBS, Joins ABC Network

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/05/90

A switch to the ABC network from CBS by the top TV station in Louisville, Ky., and ABC's decision to drop its longtime affiliate there may leave the two rejected partners with no choice but to join forces. After 40 years of affiliation with New York-based CBS Inc.'s network, WHAS, the No. 1 station in Louisville, last week terminated that contract and signed on instead with rival ABC, a unit of New York-based Capital Cities/ABC Inc. ABC, in turn, is dropping its longtime affiliate, WLKY, a unit of St. Louis-based Pulitzer Publishing Co.'s broadcasting subsidiary.

Both CBS and WLKY said the switch was unexpected. "We were really surprised and hadn't expected this," said Lyn Stoyer, general manager of WLKY. Although he said his station has had no formal talks with CBS, the station hopes it can strike an affiliation agreement with that network. CBS said in a statement that it, too, was surprised by the switch, and that its plans for Louisville are under consideration.

Although stations rarely switch affiliations, the last-place network is always at risk. Last-place CBS has lost two affiliates in the past three years; Fairfield, Conn.-based General Electric Co.'s NBC network lost some affiliates a few years ago when it was in last place. The three major broadcast networks each have about 200 affiliates nationwide. Sydney, Australia-based News Corp.'s Fox Broadcasting Co. network has about 127 affiliates.

George Newi, executive vice president of affiliate relations for ABC, said the company decided to make the switch because WHAS is the top news station in the market and has a greater viewership that will allow his network to "significantly increase our audience." WHAS is owned by the Providence Journal Broadcasting Corp. In a statement, Neil Kuvin, president of the station, said the "local competitive and business environment in Louisville is so challenging and volatile that we feel it essential to seize every opportunity available to move forward." Affiliating with ABC "represents the boldest and potentially most productive enhancement of our station imaginable," he added.
