Business Brief -- Pacific Gas & Electric Co.: Quarter Results to Include Charge Totaling $68 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/05/90

Pacific Gas & Electric Co. said in a filing that its second-quarter results will include a $68 million, or 16 cent-a-share, after-tax charge related to a recent legal settlement with USX Corp. Last month, San Francisco-based Pacific Gas paid USX $14 million to settle a dispute over the cause of a water conduit rupture in 1982 that delayed the start-up of operations at Pacific Gas's Helms hydroelectric power facility. USX's predecessor company filed suit in 1983. Of the $68 million charge, $8 million represents the after-tax cost of the settlement. The remainder is a reserve related to costs for the plant repairs, said a company spokesman.

In a Securities and Exchange Commission filing, Pacific Gas said the pact came after a jury finding that the conduit failure was caused by Pacific Gas's negligence. In addition, "the jury found that . . . {Pacific Gas's} conduct in bidding the rebuild of the water conduit constituted a fraud on USX, entitling USX to punitive damages." Pacific Gas said the pact was a "compromise" and "does not constitute an admission of liability by either company."
