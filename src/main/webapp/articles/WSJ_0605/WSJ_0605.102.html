Business Brief -- Boise Cascade Corp.: Nonrecurring Mill Problems Will Depress 2nd-Period Net

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/05/90

Boise Cascade Corp., Boise, Idaho, said that nonrecurring operating problems at four of its pulp and paper mills will depress second-quarter earnings by about $15 million before taxes, or 20 cents a share on a fully diluted basis. Analysts had estimated earlier that the forest-products company's second-quarter results would be flat or even slightly down from the first quarter of 1990, when the company reported fully diluted earnings of 73 cents a share. Unanticipated complications with maintenance and repair at several of its pulp and paper mills led to lost production time and higher costs, a company spokesman said. The mills are located in Maine, Washington, Louisiana and Alabama.

The company expects to report second-quarter results July 16.
