New Consumer Credit in U.K.

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/05/90

LONDON -- New consumer credit extended by finance houses, building societies, other specialist credit grantors and retailers totaled a seasonally adjusted #3.69 billion ($6.21 billion) in April, compared with #3.72 billion in March and #3.37 billion in April 1989, Britain's Central Statistical Office said. Consumer credit totaled #27.79 billion in April, #27.65 billion in March and #25.07 billion in April 1989.

In the three months through April, new consumer credit extended rose 0.4% to #11.13 billion from #11.09 billion in the previous three months.
