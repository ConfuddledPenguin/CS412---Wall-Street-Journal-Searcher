Sturm Ruger Files Anew With SEC For Stock Offer

  • Author: Robert Daniels
  • Date: 06/05/90

SOUTHPORT, Conn. -- Sturm, Ruger & Co., which withdrew a planned secondary offering of shares in March because its stock price was too low, registered a similar offering with its shares down a bit more. The manufacturer of firearms filed with the Securities and Exchange Commission to make a secondary offering of 1,830,000 shares. Sturm Ruger has more than 6.7 million shares outstanding.

Selling the shares are Chairman William B. Ruger Sr., one million shares; Joanna Sturm, daughter of founder Alexander Sturm, 750,000 shares; J. Thompson Ruger, who is Mr. Ruger's son, 40,000 shares; and Claudia Ruger, wife of the younger Mr. Ruger, 40,000 shares.

After the offering, the holding of the senior Mr. Ruger will drop to 39% from 54% and that of Ms. Sturm to 4.6% from nearly 16%. J. Thompson Ruger and his wife would hold about 0.8% each, compared with 1.4% each currently. William B. Ruger Jr., who isn't selling shares in the offering, holds nearly 3%.

The company granted the underwriters, led by Kidder, Peabody & Co., an option on a total 274,500 shares to cover over-allotments. Those shares would be sold by the senior Mr. Ruger and Ms. Sturm.

In January, with its shares trading at around $28, Sturm Ruger registered a secondary offering of 1,850,000 shares, including one million shares offered by the senior Mr. Ruger. The offering was to be underwritten by Drexel Burnham Lambert Inc.

In mid-February, the company reported that earnings for 1989 jumped 43% to $18.1 million, or $2.70 a share, as sales grew 19% to $133.7 million. But on March 8, with the shares at around $24.50, the company announced it had withdrawn the offering. A spokesman said then that the share price was too low and market conditions made such an offering inadvisable.

Yesterday, Sturm Ruger shares fell 50 cents to $23.50 in national over-the-counter trading. In 1990, the company's shares have traded as high as $29 and as low as $23.

Christian F. Baiz, a Kidder vice president of corporate finance who is handling the underwriting, said Sturm Ruger "simply wanted to bring the offering back fresh," having recently named a new president, Gary W. French, and boosted the quarterly dividend 20%, to 30 cents a share from 25 cents.

Separately, the company said its stock has been approved for listing on the New York Stock Exchange under the symbol RGR. The exchange couldn't estimate when it would list the shares.

For the first quarter, Sturm Ruger reported that net income dipped 4.3% to $6.2 million, or 92 cents a share, as sales grew 11% to $42.7 million.
