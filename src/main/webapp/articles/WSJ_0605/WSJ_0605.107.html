Business Brief -- Dress Barn Inc.: Retailer Reports 7% Gain In Fiscal Third-Period Net

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/05/90

Dress Barn Inc., Stamford, Conn., reported a 7% increase in earnings for its third quarter but a 26% decline for the nine months ended April 28. The operator of a chain of off-price women's apparel retail stores said earnings for the quarter rose to $4 million, or 18 cents a share, from $3.7 million, or 17 cents a share, a year earlier. Sales rose 18% to $70.6 million from $59.8 million.

For the nine months, earnings fell to $10.1 million, or 45 cents a share, from $13.7 million, or 61 cents a share, a year earlier. Sales rose 13% to $208.3 million from $183.6 million.

Dress Barn said it believes the quarter's results "signify the end of the promotional retail turmoil of the prior six months." It said it expects a return to its traditional growth pattern.

Off-price apparel chains recently have been pressed as department stores and other retailers undertook big promotions and widespread discounting.

Dress Barn said as of April 28 it had 456 stores operating, a rise of 54 from a year earlier. It said it planned to have 470 stores operating by the end of fiscal 1990.
