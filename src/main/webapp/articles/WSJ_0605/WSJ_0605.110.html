Business Brief -- Vernitron Corp.: Holding in Kollmorgen Cut In Wake of Proxy Fight

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/05/90

Vernitron Corp., which had been pursuing a takeover of Kollmorgen Corp. for 17 months, lowered its holding in the Simsbury, Conn., maker of motors and electro-optical instruments to 4.8% from around 5.5%. Separately, a group led by the New York money manager Mario Gabelli boosted its stake in Kollmorgen. Closely held Vernitron, a Deere Park, N.Y., maker of motors and controls, said in a Securities and Exchange Commission filing it sold 77,000 shares of Kollmorgen.

Last week, Kollmorgen appeared to have won a proxy fight that Vernitron waged for control of Kollmorgen's board. Despite the election results, Vernitron said it reserves the right to institute further proposals and to boost or cut its holdings.

A spokesman for Kollmorgen declined comment. In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Kollmorgen shares rose 37.5 cents to $10.75.

The Gabelli group said it boosted its stake in Kollmorgen to 17.79% from 16.29%. It said in an SEC filing it now controls 1,948,869 shares, including certain shares bought May 21 to May 31 for $10.50 to $11 each.
