Corona to Sweeten Bid for Joint Owners Of New Gold Deposit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/05/90

TORONTO -- Corona Corp. said it agreed to make an improved offer to acquire the joint owners of a new gold property in a move to head off a takeover bid by Placer Dome Inc. Corona also said it raised its holdings in the two concerns, Stikine Resources Ltd. and Prime Resources Group Inc., both of Vancouver, British Columbia.

Toronto-based Corona said it plans to merge the two companies into a new subsidiary to be created through the spinoff of Corona's gold assets. Corona will retain a 50.2% interest in the new company, to be known as Corona Gold Corp. Corona will remain as a holding company with interests in oil and gas, industrial minerals, gold and financial services.

Corona said it advanced its restructuring plans by as much as 18 months in response to a takeover bid for Stikine by Placer. The Vancouver-based mining concern has offered 67.50 Canadian dollars (US$57.50) a share, valuing all of Stikine at 229.5 million Canadian dollars.

The takeover battle is being fought over the Eskay Creek gold deposit located in northwestern British Columbia. The property's estimated reserves to date total about 4.8 million ounces of gold or equivalent, valued at about US$1.71 billion.

Ned Goodman, Corona's chairman, said the company last week bought more shares in Prime Resources, raising its stake to 45% from 21%. He said Corona obtained an option from Stikine's principal holders that would boost its stake there to 45%.

Corona will offer 6.5 of its shares and two shares of Corona Gold for each Stikine share. Stikine holders will be eligible to receive, for each Corona share, 0.38 share of Corona Gold as part of a special dividend payment to be made to Corona holders.

For each Prime share, holders will be entitled to receive one Corona Gold share and one share of a successor company to be known as Prime Equities Inc., which will comprise Prime Resources' holdings in junior exploration concerns.
