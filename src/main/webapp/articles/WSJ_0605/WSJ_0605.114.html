Technology Brief -- Hewlett-Packard Co.: Company Agrees to Develop Computers With Hughes

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/05/90

Hewlett-Packard Co., Palo Alto, Calif., agreed with Hughes Aircraft Co. jointly to develop and manufacture computers for government, military and aerospace uses. The agreement will create $200 million in revenue over the first three years, the companies estimated. H-P and Hughes said they will be modifying current H-P equipment to produce new machines that meet U.S. military standards for prevention of eavesdropping into computers. They will also develop a high-resolution workstation and "ruggedized" versions of H-P's commercial workstations that meet the military's demands for reliability.

Los Angeles-based Hughes, a unit of General Motors Corp., plans to create a special operating unit to deal with the H-P alliance. H-P will transfer certain related technology to Hughes and will provide sales support and service.
