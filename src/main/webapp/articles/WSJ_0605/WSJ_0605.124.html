Law: Hispanics' Rights Violated In Los Angeles, Judge Rules

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/05/90

LOS ANGELES -- A federal judge here ruled that the Los Angeles County Board of Supervisors violated the constitutional rights of the area's large Hispanic population when it drew voting boundaries in 1981. The suit was filed in 1988 by the U.S. Justice Department, the American Civil Liberties Union and the Mexican American Legal Defense and Educational Fund. It alleged that the Board of Supervisors illegally drew boundaries to separate the county's Hispanic community in Central and East Los Angeles into three separate supervisoral districts, thus diluting Hispanic voting strength and maintaining an all-white governing board.

Federal Judge David Kenyon said in his ruling that the County Board of Supervisors "knew that by adopting the 1981 redistricting plan, they were further impairing the ability of Hispanics to gain representation on the board."

Officials of the Board of Supervisors were in meetings yesterday afternoon, and couldn't immediately be reached for comment.

It wasn't clear what effect the ruling would have on the voting boundaries. Judge Kenyon called a hearing for Thursday to discuss the ruling with the parties involved.
