Business Brief -- Trans World Music Corp.: Peaches Entertainment Suit Is Settled for $1.5 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/05/90

Trans World Music Corp., Albany, N.Y., settled a trademark-infringement suit brought by rival record and tape retailer Peaches Entertainment Corp. for $1.5 million. Trans World, which has been rapidly expanding its chain of outlets, had taken a $2.5 million charge against earnings in the first quarter of 1989 to cover the original $2 million judgment in Peaches' favor and related expenses. Peaches is based in Hialeah Gardens, Fla.

However, Albany-based Trans World appealed the court decision and late Friday the two companies settled for $1.5 million. Trans World expects the adjustment to boost its second-quarter earnings by about $300,000 or three cents per share.

Meanwhile, at Trans World's annual meeting on Friday, Chairman Robert J. Higgins predicted that the company's net income in 1990 could top $18 million or about $2 per share, a 35% increase over 1989's $13.3 million or $1.48 per share, and that revenues could hit $400 million, about a 28% rise over 1989's $313 million.
