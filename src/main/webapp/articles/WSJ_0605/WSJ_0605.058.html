OTC Focus: Market Ends Higher as Financial Stocks Spur Big Gains; Ohio Casualty Up 2 1/2

  • Author: Craig S. Smith Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 06/05/90

NEW YORK -- Over-the-counter stocks surged to their highest levels so far this year, with financial stocks leading the advance. Traders say investors turned to the long-dormant financial sector in hopes that an easing interest-rate environment will fatten profits there.

Weak non-farm employment growth in May, evident in the Labor Department's report on Friday, and a subsequent spike in bond prices has encouraged hopes that interest rates will decline from current levels.

"If rates are coming down then the financials are certainly going to look more attractive," says Claudia Mott, small-stock analyst at Prudential-Bache Securities. She notes that the stocks have performed miserably so far this year.

Insurance stocks performed particularly well, sending the Nasdaq Insurance Index up 7.72, or more than 1.5%, to 514.86. As of Friday's close, the index was down about 7% for the year.

Mary-Alice Dennehy, OTC trader at insurance-stock specialist Conning & Co., says there may be some follow-through in insurance stocks today.

She notes that trading activity in St. Paul Cos., the OTC market's largest insurance stock by market capitalization, was strong both Friday and yesterday. The stock gained 3/4 to 59 1/2 on volume of about 594,000 shares, almost three times its average daily volume.

Insurance companies earn higher margins with lower interest rates, and their bond investments surge in value when rates come down.

Among the OTC market's other big insurance stocks that gained were Ohio Casualty, which rose 2 1/2 to 46 1/2, and Safeco, which gained 1 5/8 to 39 1/4.

The buying helped push the Nasdaq Financial Index up 6.25, or 1.6%, to 398.65. The Nasdaq 100 Index, which includes the OTC market's largest nonfinancial stocks, gained only about 1%.

But the rotation into financial stocks allowed the OTC market to extend its string of gains, with the Nasdaq Composite Index adding 3.48, or 0.75%, to 465.61 -- its highest level so far this year.

The New York Stock Exchange Composite Index, meanwhile, hit a new all-time high, breaking above the 200 level with a 1.1% gain.

OTC advancers led decliners, 1,082 to 907. National Market System volume moderated to 107,262,500 shares from Friday's robust 120,669,600 shares.

Intel led the most-active list with a 1 point gain to 48 1/2. The company moved the expiration of its 1992 warrants up to July 25. Each warrant entitles the holder to buy 1.5 shares of Intel for $45.

Also on the list, Oracle Systems added 5/8 to 20 1/2. Adobe Systems, however, fell 1 1/2 to 33 1/4.

HEI added nearly 39%, up 1 5/32 to 4 1/8.

Columbia Hospital has agreed to buy the company for $4.50 a share.

Timberline Software has gained 2 to 12 1/2, after surging 16.7% on Friday. The company's executives tell Dow Jones Professional Investor Report that a newsletter may be responsible for the sudden interest in the stock. The newsletter estimates that Timberline will earn 45 to 50 cents a share in 1990. Timberline's chief financial officer called the estimates "reasonable."

Prospect Financial lost 1 to 2, after falling 20% on Friday. The company announced Friday that it expects to record a charge in the fourth quarter ended in April, and may not be in compliance with certain regulatory capital requirements as of July 1.

Players International surged 1 5/8 to 8. The company began test marketing 900-number telephone games based on the hit television syndicated game shows "Jeopardy" and "Wheel of Fortune."

Acclaim Entertainment slid 1 1/8 to 13 1/8. Profit-taking has hit the stock after it surged to a 52-week high last week. Acclaim rose to 16 last Thursday, largely on the prospects for its video game based on the television show "The Simpsons."
