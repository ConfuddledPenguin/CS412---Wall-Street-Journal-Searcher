New Securities Issues

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/05/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATES

Champion International Corp. -- $200 million of 9 7/8% notes due June 1, 2000, priced at par. The non-callable notes were priced at a spread of 143.5 basis points above the Treasury's 10-year note. The issue was increased from an originally planned $150 million. Rated Baa-1 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and triple-B by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Goldman Sachs & Co.

Times Mirror Co. -- $100 million of 8.55% notes due June 1, 2000, priced at 99.866 for an initial yield of 8.60%. The notes, which may be put back to the company in three years, were priced at 26 basis points above the Treasury's three-year note. Rated single-A-1 by Moody's and double-A-minus by S&P, the issue will be sold through Morgan Stanley & Co.

MORTGAGE-BACKED

Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $300 million of Remic mortgage securities offered in 11 classes by Morgan Stanley & Co. The offering, Series 180, is backed by Freddie Mac 10% securities with a weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29.7 years. Information on yields wasn't immediately available from the underwriter.

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $300 million of Remic mortgage securities offered in six classes by Kidder, Peabody & Co. The offering, Series 1990-75, is backed by Fannie Mae 10 1/2% securities. In the two classes for which information was available, prices were set at 9.5%, and yield spreads were 97 basis points above three-year Treasury securities for a class with an average life of 3.5 years and 120 basis points above seven-year Treasurys for a class with an average life of 7.9 years.

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $200 million of Remic mortgage securities offered in nine classes by Prudential-Bache Securities Inc. The offering, Series 1990-74, is backed by Fannie Mae 15-year 9 1/2% mortgage securities. Price and yield information wasn't immediately available from the underwriter.

EUROBONDS

The new-issue Eurobond market was closed for a holiday.
