Marketing & Media: Maxwell Says He's Seeking To Buy Major U.S. Paper

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/05/90

NEW YORK -- Robert Maxwell, chairman of Maxwell Communication Corp. PLC, said he negotiating to buy a major U.S. daily newspaper that he refused to identify. Mr. Maxwell, who owns newspapers in the Britain and who recently bought three U.S. supermarket tabloids, said the acquisition, could involve "hundreds of millions" of dollars.

Rumors of Mr. Maxwell's interest in a U.S. daily newspaper have circulated in recent months. However, yesterday was the first time he acknowledged that he is actively seeking a U.S. paper.

Industry executives say Mr. Maxwell has focused his interest on the Chicago Sun-Times, the country's 11th largest newspaper with daily circulation of 532,678. Mr. Maxwell said yesterday he "wouldn't deny or confirm" that he is negotiating for the Sun-Times.

The Sun-Times is currently owned by an investment group led by the New York investment firm of Adler & Shaykin, which bought the newspaper from Rupert Murdoch in 1986 for $145 million. Sun-Times Chairman Leonard P. Shaykin, a managing partner of Adler & Shaykin, said "we aren't in discussion with Maxwell about the purchase of the Sun-Times." Mr. Shaykin wouldn't comment on whether previous negotiations had taken place.

Mr. Maxwell also acknowledged he is interested in Tribune Co.'s New York Daily News, currently in tough contract negotiations with its union. Although Tribune executives say it isn't for sale, Mr. Maxwell said yesterday, "We would be very interested in the Daily News if the deadlock becomes impossible. We would be a very competent party to buy it, as a way out for the Tribune."
