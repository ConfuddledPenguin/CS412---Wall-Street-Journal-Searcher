World Bank, Affiliate Approve New Loans To Developing Nations

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/05/90

WASHINGTON -- The World Bank and its affiliate, the International Development Association, approved $440.6 million in loans to seven developing nations. The largest credit, $180.4 million from the IDA, will help finance extensive road construction in Tanzania.

Algeria will get a $99.5 million loan to help restructure various industries, while Indonesia will receive an $86 million credit to expand natural-gas lines. Both of these loans will be provided by the World Bank.

Other loans, all from the IDA, included $50 million to Guinea to support private-sector enterprises and revamp the country's banking system; $15 million to Yemen for public health facilities; $6 million to Kenya to restructure the country's industrial-development bank and its industrial and commercial development corporation; and $3.7 million to Madagascar to support agricultural research.

The World Bank loans will be at variable interest rates, currently 7.75% a year. The IDA credits will be interest-free except for administrative fees.
