Business Brief -- Justin Industries Inc.: Filing With SEC Alleges Misrepresentation by Holders

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/05/90

Justin Industries Inc. sued a shareholder group led by investors Perry H. Sutherland and Barry S. Rosenstein, alleging the group violated securities laws, according to a filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission. Justin alleged that, among other things, the group misrepresented itself by failing to disclose adequately in its initial 13D filing in November its intentions to acquire the company.

The complaint, filed in federal court in Fort Worth, Texas, seeks to prevent the group, which owns about 11% of Justin, from acquiring additional shares or from taking steps to acquire control.

Earlier this year, the group's $18.50-a-share proposal to acquire Justin was rejected. The group said last month it is still interested in acquiring Justin and is continuing to meet with financing sources.

The group said in the SEC filing that the allegations are without merit and that it plans to contest them.

Justin, based in Fort Worth, makes building materials and leather goods.
