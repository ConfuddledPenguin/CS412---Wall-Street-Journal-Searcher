Business Brief -- Washington Public Power Supply: U.S. Judge Approves Plan To Pay Settlement Funds

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/17/90

A federal judge in Arizona approved a plan that allocates money paid in the settlement of litigation stemming from Washington Public Power Supply System's $2.25 billion bond default in 1983. U.S. District Judge William Browning of Tucson approved an allocation plan proposed by New York-based Chemical Bank and plaintiffs' lawyers earlier this year. According to the plan, $93.5 million will be put into a fund to be distributed by Chemical Bank to all bondholders. The rest will be used for attorney's fees and reimbursement of litigation expenses and then distributed to plaintiffs who bought bonds before June 1983.

Plaintiffs who bought WPPSS bonds before June 1983 and Chemical Bank, a unit of Chemical Banking Corp. and trustee for the estimated 24,000 bondholders, reached approximately $700 million in settlements with the defendants last year. The defendants include WPPSS, several utilities in six states in the Northwest, the Bonneville Power Administration, the state of Washington and various professional firms.

Bondholders will be reimbursed based on their reported losses and proportions will vary depending on when they purchased the bonds. Actual losses will be reduced in many cases by a deemed "salvage" value of 22 cents on the dollar assigned to the bonds. The approved plan, which included speculators who bought after the default, appears to offer current holders at least nine cents on the dollar.
