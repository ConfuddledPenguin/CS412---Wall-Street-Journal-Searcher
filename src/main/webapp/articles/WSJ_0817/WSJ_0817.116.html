U.S. Imported Over Half Of Oil Consumed in July

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 08/17/90

Imports accounted for more than half of the nation's oil consumption in July for the third month in a row. An average of just under nine million barrels a day of crude oil and petroleum products were imported last month, the American Petroleum Institute reported, up 8.4% from the same month last year. Foreign oil accounted for 51.3% of total July deliveries; for the first seven months of the year it averaged 50.0%. The API said the increase in part reflected buying in advance of widely anticipated price increases last month. This month, prices have risen even more because of the Middle East crisis.

Nonetheless, the July figures are "a harbinger of the future," said Charles DiBona, API president. He noted that the recent string of 50%-plus import months have been coupled with a continuing decline in domestic oil output. API said domestic crude production last month dropped 5.3% to just over seven million barrels a day compared with the year-ago month.

"The decline, which has persisted for more than four years . . . has reduced domestic crude oil output by roughly two million barrels a day or 20% since early 1986," the API said.
