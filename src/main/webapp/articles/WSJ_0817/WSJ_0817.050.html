Turkish Banker Buys Two-Thirds Interest In Park Avenue Bank

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/17/90

NEW YORK -- Erol Aksoy, managing director and principal stockholder of Turkey's Iktisat Bank, paid $8 million for a two-thirds interest in Park Avenue Bank, which caters to wealthy individuals. The 43-year-old Mr. Aksoy, who also is chairman of Banque Internationale de Commerce of Paris, plans to expand Park Avenue Bank's operations to include financing trade.

Mr. Aksoy, a native of Istanbul, named Huseyin Unver, 41, president of Park Avenue Bank. Mr. Unver has been Iktisat Bank's representative here for the past year; prior to that, he was a vice president and area manager responsible for lending activity in several Middle East countries for Irving Trust Co., now part of Bank of New York Co.

Park Avenue Bank, a private bank, has one branch and $15 million in assets. The bank has posted a loss of about $600,000 so far this year, Mr. Aksoy said. Iktisat Bank, with about $1 billion of assets, earned about $20 million in the fiscal year ended June 30, he said.

Banking services to the wealthy is a very competitive business and "New York is an expensive place," Mr. Aksoy said. But in trade finance, a small bank can be more flexible than the majors, Mr. Aksoy said, adding that requests for letters of credit, which are principal trade financing instruments, generally require a response in no more than 24 or 48 hours.
