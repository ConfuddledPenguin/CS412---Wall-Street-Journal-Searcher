REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: Friend of Science

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/17/90

With some justification, the scientific community laments that U.S. basic research grants haven't kept pace with the state of inquiry, such that whereas 20 years ago most applications were accepted, nowadays less than a fifth are funded. Before you think that science has no political constituency, consider the success of senator Mark Hatfield (R., Ore.) in using his powerful Appropriations Committee seat to rack up construction money for the Oregon Health Sciences University. With the latest $12.5 million catch for a Neurosensory Research Center this month, the Oregonian newspaper reports, Mr. Hatfield has now funneled $91 million into bricks and mortar for the 1,300-student school over the last decade -- more than for any other campus in the country. Scientists might well conclude that Congress's main interest is not really research, but ribbon cuttings.
