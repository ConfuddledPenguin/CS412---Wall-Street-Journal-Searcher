Ex-Owner of Thrift In Texas Pleads Guilty To Charges of Fraud

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/17/90

DALLAS -- The former owner of a Texas thrift that failed under the weight of fraudulent condominium-development loans pleaded guilty to conspiring to defraud the federal deposit insurance fund. Welba Lee "Bubba" Keetch Jr., who owned the defunct First Savings & Loan Association of Burkburnett, Texas, also pleaded guilty to charges of mail fraud, misapplication of thrift funds and illegally participating in the proceeds of a loan.

The Burkburnett thrift was one of six Texas savings associations that poured at least $750 million into land acquisitions and condominium developments along Interstate 30, east of Dallas, in 1982 and 1983. Some 2,000 condominiums were built, glutting the Dallas real estate market.

The loans, which were inflated by fraudulent land appraisals, also provided millions of dollars in profits for the financiers and developers of the condominiums, according to court records and the testimony of appraisers and promoters who participated in the scheme.

Mr. Keetch is one of 102 people who have been convicted of federal crimes in connection with the condominium lending scheme. He faces a maximum possible sentence of 13 years in federal prison and a $275,000 fine. Mr. Keetch is scheduled for sentencing Oct. 25 before Judge Barefoot Sanders of Dallas.
