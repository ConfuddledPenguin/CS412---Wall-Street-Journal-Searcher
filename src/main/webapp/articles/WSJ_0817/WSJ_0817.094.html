Reuters Holdings' Stock Hurt by Report On Currency Network

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/17/90

LONDON -- Shares in Reuters Holdings PLC were hit by reports of snags in the upgraded version of the company's Dealing 2000 system, a communications network for foreign-exchange markets. On London's Stock Exchange, Reuters stock fell 7.9%, while American depositary receipts plunged 11% to $51.375 in national over-the-counter trading in New York. The sell-off was prompted by a negative research report by Wertheim Schroder, a New York investment firm. The firm reportedly took Reuters off its list of recommended stocks, citing problems with Dealing 2000-2.

Wertheim refused to provide copies of its report or to discuss its message.

Also, Trading Systems Technology, an industry newsletter, said in a recent edition that Reuters was having testing problems with the new system.

The system, which represents a major investment and a potentially significant source of profits, will allow foreign-exchange dealers to communicate with as many as four parties via computer terminals. Dealers could also enter bids and offers, which the system would match up.

In London, the company dismissed reports that the Dealing 2000-2 project is having problems, though it said that "further testing is required on peripheral systems and overall system integration."

Dow Jones & Co., publisher of The Wall Street Journal, owns Telerate Inc., which, among other things, competes with Reuters in the electronic currency trading market.
