Technology: U.S. Arrests Boy, 5 Others In Computer Hacker Cases

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 08/17/90

New York state and federal investigators arrested a 14-year-old boy who was suspected of using a home computer to break into a Pentagon computer used by the office of the Secretary of the Air Force. Investigators also searched 17 locations yesterday in New York, Westchester County and Long Island, seizing computer equipment and data storage devices. Five men, ranging in age from 17 to 32, were arrested in the sweep and charged with computer tampering and trespass, unauthorized entry and theft of services. They were suspected of breaking into a computer at the City University of Bellevue, Wash. Investigators said the 14-year-old was also linked to the Bellevue intrusion.

The arrests were part of a two-month investigation by the New York state police, U.S. Secret Service, Air Force Office of Special Investigations, the New York attorney general and other agencies.

Major Steve Headley of the Air Force investigations office said the Pentagon computer that was penetrated was part of an administrative network and did not contain any classified information. "There was no breach of national security," he said.

Investigators did not release the name of the 14-year-old, who will be charged as a juvenile delinquent.
