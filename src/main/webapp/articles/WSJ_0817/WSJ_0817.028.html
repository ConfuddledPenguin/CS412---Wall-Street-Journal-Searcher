GE Capital Unit Buys Empire Federal Savings Auto-Loan Portfolio

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/17/90

FAIRFIELD, Conn. -- GE Capital Auto Financial Services said it acquired all of the auto installment loans held by Empire Federal Savings of America, Buffalo, N.Y. A spokesman for GE Capital Auto, a unit of General Electric Co.'s General Electric Capital Corp., said the company jumped at the chance to make such a bulk purchase of auto loans. Empire is currently held in conservatorship by the federal Resolution Trust Corp.

GE Capital Auto, with over $5.7 billion of auto-loan assets, is a major servicer of auto loans for banks. A spokesman said the portfolio was bought at a discount, but he would not disclose the price. He said Empire's auto-loan assets were about $900 million.

GE Capital Auto said it plans to retain the 100 or so Empire employees who were managing the portfolio. GE Capital Auto is based in Barrington, Ill.

General Electric's businesses, in addition to financial services, include consumer and industrial products, broadcasting and aerospace.
