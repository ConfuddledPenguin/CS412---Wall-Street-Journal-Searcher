Who's News: Philip Morris Picks Viault to Be Chief Of Jacobs Suchard

  • Author: Kathleen Deveny
  • Date: 08/17/90

NEW YORK -- Philip Morris Cos. moved closer to its planned $3.8 billion acquisition of Swiss chocolate and coffee company Jacobs Suchard AG, and appointed the architect of a turnaround at its Maxwell House Coffee Co. to run the European company. The U.S. tobacco and consumer products company said it completed its acquisition of Colima Holding AG, a closely held company that controls 62% of the voting rights of Suchard. Through Colima, Philip Morris is making a tender offer for the balance of Suchard's shares outstanding.

At the same time, the Suchard board named Raymond Viault as chief executive officer of Jacobs Suchard, which is now an operating unit of Philip Morris's Kraft General Foods subsidiary.

Mr. Viault, 46 years old, had previously served as president of Kraft General Foods' Maxwell House Coffee Co. While at Maxwell House, which accounts for 22.2% of Kraft General Foods' U.S. business, Mr. Viault helped engineer a turnaround of the unit's lagging domestic coffee business.

By boosting the quality of the beans used in its Maxwell House brand, and by rolling out such new products as Maxwell House Rich French Roast, Maxwell House Colombian Supreme and Maxwell House 1882, the company saw its market share begin to climb in the past year. Together, its brands now account for about 34.6% of the domestic coffee market, making Maxwell House the nation's largest coffee manufacturer.

At Suchard, Mr. Viault will oversee a trove of brands that includes the Jacobs Cafe, and Night and Day coffees, and the Toblerone and Milka brands of chocolate. When Philip Morris announced the acquisition in June, it said it hoped Suchard would allow it to build Pan-European brands.

To succeed Mr. Viault, Kraft General Foods named Charles Phillips as president of Maxwell House. Mr. Phillips, 45, has spent his entire career at General Foods, climbing through financial and marketing positions at Maxwell House and other Kraft General Foods' units. Most recently, he served as Kraft General Foods' president for Latin America, Eastern Europe, the Middle East and Africa.

To maintain Maxwell House's momentum in the U.S. market, Mr. Phillips vows to step up the company's new product initiatives. He said the company is already testing a liquid-coffee concentrate and a fully prepared liquid coffee, each designed to be heated in microwave ovens. The company is also testing both canned and bottled versions of an iced-coffee product called Cappio.

Industry experts say further gains in the U.S. coffee business may be difficult, however. Consumption of coffee in the U.S. has dropped for more than a decade, as younger consumers have turned to sweet drinks for their morning beverage.

But Mr. Phillips maintains Maxwell House's new initiatives will lure back soda drinkers. "We just have to find new ways to deliver the benefits of coffee to young people," Mr. Phillips said. "We've given that market away without a fight."
