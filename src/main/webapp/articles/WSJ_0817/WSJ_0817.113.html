What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/17/90

STOCKS AND BONDS sank on rising Mideast tension and more signs of recession and inflation. The Dow Jones industrials fell 66.83 points to close at 2681.44. Long-term Treasury bonds dropped 1 5/8 points. The bad economic news pushed the dollar to a new low against the mark. Gold futures prices surged $6.60 an ounce. ---

Consumer prices rose 0.4% in July, putting inflation at a 5.8% annual rate for 1990's first seven months, before oil prices soared. Meanwhile, housing starts fell for the sixth straight month, dropping 2.6% to their lowest level since 1982. The data show non-energy inflation persisting despite economic weakness.

---

Saudi Arabia and Venezuela intend to gradually boost their oil output, by as much as 2.5 million barrels a day, whether or not they win OPEC's approval at an emergency meeting that the two nations are seeking.

---

Sears Roebuck plans to eliminate thousands of jobs in the next several months, in an effort to cut annual expenses by more than $100 million.

---

James River plans to sell or close units with $1.3 billion in annual sales, nearly a fourth of the paper company. The firm will take a charge against earnings of about $150 million.

---

Cetus Corp.'s Robert Fildes quit as chief executive and president, two weeks after an FDA panel rebuffed the firm's cancer drug. Cetus also plans to lay off about 10% of its staff.

---

CBS Records' Walter Yetnikoff agreed to step down as chief executive of the Sony unit in two years.

---

Exxon sued the IRS, challenging the agency's claim that the oil company owes $6.5 billion in back taxes. Exxon said it expects to win but has set aside "adequate" reserves.

---

Merrill Lynch and other firms are promoting a new technique for companies to cut their capital-gains taxes that is the subject of IRS scrutiny.

---

Saatchi agreed to sell McCaffrey & McCall back to the unit's management for much less than the ad giant paid, people close to the deal said. The lower price reflects Saatchi's desire to streamline itself, as well as bad times for ad agencies.

---

Navistar posted a 79% drop in profit and a 10% decline in revenue for the quarter that ended in July.

---

Shearson agreed to accept less interest on a $500 million loan to Prime Computer's parent in exchange for a bigger equity stake in the company.

---

Ameritrust plans to cut 780 jobs, 11.5% of its work force. The bank company hopes to stem loan losses by reining in its growth strategy.

---

The New York Fed's backup power failed, delaying some bank transactions and securities trading.

---

The FTC accused the nation's largest coin-assessment firm of making false and misleading claims.

---

Canadian interest rates fell on the strength of the nation's dollar. The Bank of Canada's bank rate reached its lowest level since February.

---

Austria will get nearly the same privileges in high-technology trade that the U.S. accords to allies.

---
