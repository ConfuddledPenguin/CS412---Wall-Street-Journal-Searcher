Leonard Green Agrees to Purchase Carr-Gottstein Inc.

  • Author: Sonia L. Nazario
  • Date: 08/17/90

LOS ANGELES -- Leonard Green & Partners said it agreed to buy Carr-Gottstein Inc., Alaska's largest food-and-drug retailer and wholesaler, for about $300 million. This is the first acquisition by Leonard Green & Partners, a merchant banking firm specializing in leveraged buy-outs that was founded late last year by Leonard Green, formerly a partner of New York-based Gibbons, Green, van Amerongen Ltd. Leveraged buy-out activity in general has dropped since last year's collapse of the junk-bond market.

A spokeswoman for Leonard Green & Partners said the acquisition of Carr-Gottenstein, which should be completed in the fall, wouldn't involve junk bonds. She wouldn't elaborate on the nature of the financing.

Mr. Green, who was credited with much of the success that Gibbons, Green had with arranging LBOs in the 1980s, said he left that firm because of "philosophical differences" with his partners. Since setting up his own shop, he has been struggling to match his prior track record; he has said his goal is to complete between six and nine buy-outs, valued at $100 million to $500 million each, in the next three years.

Carr-Gottstein includes Carr's food and drug stores, the J.B. Gottstein Co. wholesaling operation, the Oaken Keg liquor stores, several shopping centers in Anchorage, Eagle River, and Fairbanks and several other distribution-related concerns.

The acquisition provides for new stores and remodeling of existing stores. Carr's General Manager John Cairns and its vice president of operations, Mark Williams, will retain their management positions, Leonard Green & Partners said.
