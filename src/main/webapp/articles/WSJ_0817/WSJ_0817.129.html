International Brief -- Michelin: Revenue for the First Half Declined 4% to $5.21 Billion

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/17/90

Cie. Generale des Etablissements Michelin of France reported that its first-half consolidated revenue fell 4% to 27.37 billion francs ($5.21 billion) from 28.47 billion francs a year earlier. The tire and rubber concern, which announced the results in a mandatory legal filing, declined to provide additional details or comparable first-half profit data. For all of 1989, Michelin had consolidated profit of 2.45 billion francs, after payments to minority interests, on revenue of 55.26 billion francs.

The first-half sales decline came as little surprise to the markets, as analysts had anticipated weaker 1990 results in the wake of price-cutting by major manufacturers in the sector, in an effort to protect market share.

Michelin executives in June told shareholders that the company is likely to face three or four difficult years soon, because of tight market conditions and plans for heavy investment outlays. The executives also declined to rule out the possibility of losses in the years ahead, and unveiled previously reported job cuts aimed at holding down costs.
