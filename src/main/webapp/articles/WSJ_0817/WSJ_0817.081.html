Security Pacific Sees Problems For 2nd-Half Net

  • Author: Charles McCoy
  • Date: 08/17/90

LOS ANGELES -- Security Pacific Corp. said overall weakness in the economy would crimp its earnings for the remainder of the year. The bank holding company wouldn't make specific estimates. But John Kooken, chief financial officer, said Security Pacific at best expects net income for the second half to grow less than the 5% increase recorded in profit for the first six months. Mr. Kooken also said profit for the second half could be below year-earlier levels.

For the first six months, Security Pacific earned $195.2 million, or $1.59 a share. In last year's second half, the company earned $376.8 million, or $3.14 a share. For all of 1989, profit was $740.6 million, or $6.26 a share.

The announcement rocked Security Pacific's stock and prompted several analysts to lower their 1990 earnings forecasts for the company. In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Security Pacific closed at $28.75, off $3.

Livia Asher, an analyst with Merrill Lynch Research/U.S., trimmed her 1990 estimate by 65 cents a share, to $5.70. For 1991, she cut her estimate by 60 cents, to $6.20 a share.

Mr. Kooken and other Security Pacific officials were dismayed by the stock's plunge. "We certainly feel that the market has overreacted," Mr. Kooken said. "The reaction is not borne out by the facts."

He said the market may have interpreted the disclosure as evidence of a swoon in California real estate. Many investors have been dreading such a downturn because California is about the only regional real estate market in the country that hasn't tanked. Recently, several large California thrifts and some banks have reported losses because of trouble in California. But Mr. Kooken said the California real estate market, as well as the local economy, remain relatively strong.

He attributed the projected slow second half to weakness in the general economy and said that first-half profit was pumped up by unusually high non-interest income. He said loan charge-offs in each of the third and fourth quarters are likely to match the $160 million Security Pacific reported in the second quarter. By comparison, the company reported only $115 million of loan losses in the first quarter.

Separately, Security Pacific said it agreed to sell $100 million of 8.85% notes, maturing Aug. 15, 1993, and non-callable over the life of the issue. The notes will be underwritten by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets, Lehman Brothers and Salomon Brothers Inc. Proceeds will be used for general corporate purposes.
