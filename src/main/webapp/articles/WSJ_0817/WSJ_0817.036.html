Notable & Quotable

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/17/90

British financier Sir James Goldsmith speaking to the Institute of Directors in London on June 12:

Let me quote the Nobel prize laureate, Maurice Allais: ". . . Europe is already overpopulated. Therefore, it is imperative to stop immigration. . . ."

I would add that not only are we overpopulated, but also we have a multitribal and unproven community. To add large quantities of immigrants of different cultures would produce an explosive mix.

There is a free movement of peoples in Europe, so the whole of Europe is affected by the immigration policies of each nation. Immigration must be determined by the European constitution and, "inter alia," the real meaning of the surviving arrangements between all former colonial powers and their ex-colonies must be reassessed unflinchingly.
