Business Brief -- Dress Barn Inc.: Women's Apparel Retailer Authorizes Stock Buy-Back

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/17/90

Dress Barn Inc. said directors authorized a buy-back of about 10% of shares outstanding in an attempt to boost the share price of the discount women's apparel retailer. The company, which is based in Stamford, Conn., said the board authorized the purchase of as many as two million shares. "We believe the stock had gone far below where it should be," said Elliot Jaffe, Dress Barn's chairman and chief executive officer.

In national over-the-counter trading yesterday, Dress Barn shares closed at $8.125, up 12.5 cents. The company previously stepped in to buy back shares after the October 1987 market crash.

Contrary to market expectations that higher gasoline prices will dampen customers' willingness to drive to shopping centers, Mr. Jaffe argues that women will continue to flock to discount department stores. Dress Barn operates 463 discount apparel stores nationwide and plans to increase the number of outlets to 500 by the end of the year.
