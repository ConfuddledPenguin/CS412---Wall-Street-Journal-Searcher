Plunging Earnings Turn a Big Party Into a Little Bash --- N.V. Philips Trims Plans For Its 100th Anniversary As Cost Savings Measure

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 08/17/90

EINDHOVEN, The Netherlands -- Faced with plunging profits in the first half of 1990 and an expected loss of 2 billion guilders ($1.13 billion) for the year, N.V. Philips said it has extensively cut back on lavish plans for the troubled Dutch electronics giant's 100th anniversary celebration in 1991. Rather, Philips spokesman Jan van Schagen said the company now plans to "commemorate the centenary in an unostentatious and modest manner."

Although plans hadn't yet been finalized, at one time they included a street carnival in Eindhoven as well as two major company exhibits, Mr. Van Schagen said.

"Those are definitely off," said Mr. Van Schagen. "We feel the situation in which we now are and the way we celebrate these festivities ought to be in some proportion."

Originally, Philips had been planning to spend "tens of millions" of guilders on the anniversary, Mr. Van Schagen said but added he had no estimate for the costs of the currently planned celebrations.

On Aug. 9, Philips reported that its net income from ordinary activities plummeted to 43 million guilders in the first half of 1990 from 420 million guilders in the year-earlier period. Including extraordinary items, Philips had a total net income of 373 million guilders in the first half of 1990 compared with 458 million guilders in the first half of 1989.

Philips is maintaining an earlier forecast that it will have to spend 2.7 billion guilders on restructuring efforts and lay off close to 10,000 workers, mostly in its Western European operations. The restructuring charge will result in a loss of about 2 billion guilders for the year, according to Philips' forecast.
