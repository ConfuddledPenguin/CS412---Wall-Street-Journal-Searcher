AmBase Lawsuit Says Firm Misled Investors To Inflate Stock Price

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/17/90

NEW YORK -- A shareholder filed suit, charging AmBase Corp. with disseminating false information about a special dividend to inflate its stock price in recent weeks. The lawsuit, filed in federal court in Manhattan, seeks class-action status. The suit claims that investors were misled from May 14 until Tuesday, when the company announced details of an agreement to sell its Home Insurance Co. unit.

On May 14, AmBase said that it was suspending its quarterly common stock dividend but that it expected to pay a special dividend to shareholders when it closed the sale of Home Insurance. But Tuesday, when AmBase announced the sale of Home Insurance to Vik Brothers International, it also said it no longer expects to pay the special dividend.

AmBase, a financial-services company, said this week that it will use proceeds from the $544 million sale of Home Insurance to help pay down debt.

The lawsuit claims that AmBase should have disclosed earlier that it wouldn't be able to pay the special dividend. The lawsuit, filed on behalf of all shareholders who bought stock between May 14 and Tuesday, claims that AmBase kept the information secret to entice investors. The lawsuit seeks unspecified damages.

A company spokesman couldn't be reached for comment.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Ambase shares fell 87.5 cents to $1.875. On Wednesday, the stock plummeted $4.75 to close at $2.75.
