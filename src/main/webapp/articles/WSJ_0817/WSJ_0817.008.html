Noranda Unit Wins Ruling in Dispute On a Metal Deposit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/17/90

MONTREAL -- A Noranda Inc. subsidiary succeeded in overturning an agreement that would have settled a legal dispute regarding ownership of a large copper and zinc deposit in northern Quebec, in which Noranda has an indirect interest. The Supreme Court of Quebec late Wednesday ordered the owners of the Louvicourt deposit, Aur Resources Inc. and Louvem Mines Inc., and Louvem's parent, St. Genevieve Resources Ltd., to cancel the settlement proposal. Lance Tigert, treasurer of Noranda Minerals Inc., said the court found that the agreement violated the company's rights under a shareholder pact with St. Genevieve.

Mr. Tigert said Noranda already has begun negotiations with the other parties to arrive at a new settlement of the legal dispute. He criticized the earlier accord, to which Noranda wasn't a party, as being "lopsided."

The settlement would have given Toronto-based Aur 60% of Louvicourt, which has been hailed as one of Canada's largest base-metal discoveries in recent years. Louvem, based in Montreal, would have kept 40% of the deposit. Louvem is owned 21% by Toronto-based Noranda and 50% by Montreal-based St. Genevieve.

Negotiations could bog down on the important issue of who will operate the mine, with both Noranda and Aur determined to retain this role.

Pierre Gauthier, chairman and chief executive officer of St. Genevieve, said the court also instructed the company to cancel a notice of sale of 23% of Louvem's shares for a total of 32.8 million Canadian dollars (US$28.7 million). Noranda has the right of first refusal on any Louvem shares sold by St. Genevieve.

But Mr. Gauthier said St. Genevieve might still seek to sell part of its Louvem shares -- a move that could pressure Noranda to agree to a settlement of the legal dispute. Mr. Gauthier said Noranda would lose the right to veto any settlement agreement if the combined interests of Noranda and St. Genevieve in Louvem fell below 50%.
