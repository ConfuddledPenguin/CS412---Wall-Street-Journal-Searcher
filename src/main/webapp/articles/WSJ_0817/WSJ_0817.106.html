GE Aircraft Engine To Lose 1,500 Jobs As Military Cuts Back

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/17/90

EVENDALE, Ohio -- General Electric Co.'s aircraft engine division will eliminate 1,500 jobs, mostly through attrition, in response to cuts in military spending. GE Aircraft Engine said that even though its commercial business is up significantly from a year earlier, the backlog of orders "will not be enough" to offset the predicted cuts. The backlog currently is about $20 billion, with orders for delivery over the next 10 years, it said. The backlog two years ago was $12.4 billion.

A GE spokesman said that since 1987 the commercial part of the aircraft engine business has absorbed about 5,000 employees from the military side. He said 47% of the aircraft engine's $6.8 billion in revenue last year came from military contracts. That percentage is expected to drop to 35% by 1992.

The cutbacks will be shared equally by the unit's Lynn, Mass., and Evendale, Ohio, operations. Attrition will account for about 1,000 jobs over the next 18 months, and the remaining 500 people will be laid off. GE Aircraft Engine employs 46,170 people in the U.S.

The layoffs reflect the cancellation of the Navy's P-7 anti-submarine attack aircraft, a reduction in the Air Force's program for B-2 bombers, and a delayed production schedule for the Navy's A-12 fighter plane, among other cutbacks.

These are the first significant layoffs at the aircraft engine division in response to defense spending cuts. Last April, GE's aerospace division announced that it would eliminate 4,200 jobs, farm out production of standard component parts, and consolidate several plants.
