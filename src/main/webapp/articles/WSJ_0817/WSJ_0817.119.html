Business Brief -- Western Union Corp.: Preferred Holders Approve Reclassification to Common

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/17/90

Western Union Corp. holders of its preferred shares approved the company's plan to reclassify the preferred as common, at a reconvened annual meeting in Upper Saddle River, N.J. The reclassification, which required approval by holders of at least two-thirds of the preferred, is a critical part of the financial services and communications company's recapitalization plan. Western Union has said it might have to make a bankruptcy filing unless it can recapitalize.

The company also is offering to swap new notes and common shares for two issues of notes outstanding totaling $530 million. The issues are $500 million of 19.25% notes, of which the company is seeking at least 95%, and $30 million of 16% notes, of which it is seeking at least 80%.

The exchange offer was extended to next Monday from last Wednesday.
