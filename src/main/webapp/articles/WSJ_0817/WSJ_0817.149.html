Raven Industries To Boost Payout 20%

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/17/90

SIOUX FALLS, S.D. -- Raven Industries Inc. plans to increase its quarterly dividend 20%, and expects to report that net income for the second quarter ended July 31 rose about 8%, David Christensen, president and chief executive officer said. The company attributed the earnings increase to a high level of defense-equipment sales for the quarter.

Mr. Christensen said Raven will soon announce a dividend increase to nine cents a share from 7.5 cents a share, payable Oct. 15 to stock of record Sept. 21.

He estimated that net in the second quarter rose to about $1.3 million, or 42 cents a share, compared with $1.2 million, or 38 cents, a year ago. Sales fell roughly 2% to $21.4 million from $21.8 million.

In the six months, Mr. Christensen estimated that net rose 9% to $2.3 million, or 74 cents a share, from $2.1 million, or 66 cents. Sales fell about 3% to $40.9 million from $42.3 million.

The company's backlog of orders as of July 31 rose 18% to $43.2 million from $36.7 million a year ago.

Raven's electronics division will report that operating profit more than doubled, largely because of increases in orders for defense-related equipment. Sales of agricultural controls for herbicide sprayers were also strong, said Mr. Christensen.

The plastics division, which makes glass fiber tops for pickup trucks and tanks for storing industrial chemicals, expects a 76% increase in operating profit from last year's second quarter.

The clothing division expects to report that operating profit fell 39%, partly from the loss of a contract with the Defense Department to make uniforms.

Mr. Christensen said he expects to report "record" sales and profit for the year ending Jan. 31, but he declined to elaborate. Last year the company earned $4.2 million, or $1.31, on sales of $91 million.
