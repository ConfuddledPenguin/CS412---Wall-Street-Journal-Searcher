House Panel Head Cites Abuse in Fund For Railroad Workers

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/17/90

WASHINGTON -- A federal agency that provides retirement and unemployment benefits for railroad workers is vulnerable "to fraud and abuse involving hundreds of millions of dollars," the chairman of a House committee said. Rep. John Conyers (D., Mich.), in a letter to Budget Director Richard Darman, said that auditors for the Railroad Retirement Board's inspector general reviewed 19 railroads and found "$73 million in nonpayment of taxes, including interest and penalties." The payroll taxes fund the benefits for the nation's railroad workers. More than 1,100 railroads weren't audited, Rep. Conyers noted.

In addition, Rep. Conyers, who heads the Government Operations Committee, said that the Treasury Department didn't transfer $152.6 million in tax receipts to the railroad board for four months, costing the retirement trust funds about $4.4 million in interest.

The findings "show an alarming lack of oversight by the Treasury Department and significant under-reporting of payroll taxes by railroad employers," Rep. Conyers said. He cited an inspector general's report that concluded that the Internal Revenue Service hadn't adequately monitored railroad compliance with tax laws. The IRS and a Treasury Department spokesman said they hadn't seen Rep. Conyers's letter and couldn't comment.

A senior Bush administration official, who asked not to be named, said Rep. Conyers was piggybacking on the administration's work. He said a White House budget office "swat team" from various federal agencies uncovered serious problems in the railroad retirement program earlier this year. The administration, he said, notified Rep. Conyers and others in Congress. He said the administration is preparing corrective legislation.
