Money-Fund Assets Rose $2.39 Billion In Weekly Measure

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/17/90

WASHINGTON -- Assets of the nation's 525 money market funds rose $2.39 billion to $404.48 billion, in the week ended Wednesday, according to the Investment Company Institute, a Washington-based trade association. Assets of 252 general purpose funds rose $469.9 million to $140.04 billion and assets of 104 broker-dealer funds rose $1.09 billion to $163.32 billion. Assets of 169 institutional funds rose $829.3 million to $101.11 billion.

Assets of money-market funds rose for the seventh consecutive week as investors leaned toward shorter-term instruments. "Despite a slight easing of yields offered by money-market funds, events in the Persian Gulf have enhanced their attraction as safe and liquid investment vehicles," said Dr. Jacob Dreyer, vice president and chief economist at the institute.

Mutual-fund assets were revised for the week ended Aug. 8, to $402.09 billion, with a revised increase of $6.18 billion.
