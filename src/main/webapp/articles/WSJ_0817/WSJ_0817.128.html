Marketing & Media: Union Reverses Its Stance On Actor in `Miss Saigon'

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/17/90

NEW YORK -- The Actors Equity union will permit a white actor to play a Vietnamese man of mixed race in a Broadway production of the hit London musical "Miss Saigon." The vote by the executive council reverses a stand it took on behalf of the union's members of color last week. In June, the union's committee on racial equality asked producer Cameron Mackintosh to consider auditioning Asian actors here for the starring role created in London by Jonathan Pryce. Mr. Mackintosh refused. Last week union leaders voted to deny Mr. Pryce permission to play the role. Mr. Mackintosh canceled the show the next day.

"Miss Saigon," about the American withdrawal from Vietnam in 1975, had a record New York advance ticket sale of $25 million.

The original union stand put a national spotlight on an Asian-American civil rights issue. But the position was quickly assailed by arguments over economic consequences, artistic freedom and racial equality.

Tisa Chang, director of the Pan Asian Repertory Theatre here, said Asian-Americans are offended by the physical posturing and makeup Mr. Pryce uses to portray an Asian. Ms. Chang vowed to continue to fight "yellow-face" portrayals of Asian characters.
