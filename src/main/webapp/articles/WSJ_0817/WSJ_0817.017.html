Switzerland's Trade Deficit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/17/90

BERN, Switzerland -- Switzerland's trade gap widened in July to 594.8 million francs ($455.6 million) from a revised deficit of 505.5 million francs in June but narrowed from the July 1989 deficit of 662 million francs, the government said. For this year through July, the trade deficit narrowed to 5.97 billion francs from the year-earlier gap of 6.57 billion francs.

In July, imports slowed to 8.08 billion francs from 8.19 billion francs in June but increased from 7.64 billion francs in July 1989. For the first seven months, imports totaled 58.42 billion francs, up from 55 billion francs a year earlier. Exports eased to 7.48 billion francs in July from 7.68 billion francs in June, but grew from 6.97 billion francs in July 1989. Through July, exports totaled 52.45 billion francs, up from 48.43 billion francs a year earlier.
