Letters to the Editor: Nintendo Quality: It's All in the Game

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/17/90

At Nintendo, customer feedback helps us deliver top-quality entertainment products. We were particularly distressed, therefore, when our good customer, the Honorable Alex Kozinski, offered his perspective in a July 27 Leisure & Arts page article. We share with Judge Kozinski an abiding concern for game quality. We actively search world-wide for the best software developers. Contrary to the judge's impression, we do not control these developers. We work with them intensively to unleash the creative talents that are so apparent in games designed for the Nintendo Entertainment System. Our intensive game-evaluation system, which includes input from players such as Judge Kozinski, ensures that our developers are constantly one step ahead in creating games that will pique consumer interest.

Our customer services have expanded since 1985 and have become even more comprehensive. Game counselors now answer over 50,000 consumer game inquiries on game play per week. The Judge apparently had an unsatisfactory experience trying to return a defective cartridge to an unnamed retailer. He should be assured that Nintendo retailers will accept a defective product for immediate exchange. Retailers receive full credit for any defective products returned to Nintendo. Customers who choose to send products directly to Nintendo for repair can expect to receive their items back in just three to five days on average. By the way, we pride ourselves on a software defect rate of less than one percent.

So we say to Judge Kozinski that we have not become "fat and flabby." We do not take you and your son for granted as Nintendo players. We know that, apart from your backgammon board, there are other entertainment options available to you inside and outside the video game business. Whatever your doubts, we have the perfect prescription for your apparent game burnout, an exciting new brainteaser, "Dr. Mario," which comes to market this fall. It is sure to be another of the Nintendo mega-hits.

Howard C. Lincoln

Senior Vice President

Nintendo of America Inc.

Redmond, Wash.
