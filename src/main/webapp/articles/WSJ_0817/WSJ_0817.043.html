Marketing & Media: Reader's Digest Net About Doubled In Fiscal 4th Period

  • Author: Patrick M. Reilly
  • Date: 08/17/90

Reader's Digest Association Inc. said fiscal fourth-quarter earnings nearly doubled from a year earlier, despite mounting losses at its specialized magazine operations. But the Pleasantville, N.Y., media company attributed the jump in earnings largely to the negative effect of charges taken in the 1989 fourth quarter. Excluding the effect of the 1989 charges, per-share earnings rose about 16% in the latest period.

For the quarter ended June 30, net income rose to $20 million, or 16 cents a share, from $10.3 million, or nine cents a share, a year earlier. The 1989 quarter included costs from a program of awarding stock to senior management and relocation of a U.K. subsidiary.

For fiscal 1990 -- the first full year of operations since the company went public on Feb. 15 -- net rose to $176 million, or $1.48 a share, from $151.5 million, or $1.28 a share, a year earlier.

Revenue reached a record $2.01 billion, up 17% from $1.8 billion a year earlier.

Reader's Digest said its largest contributor to earnings was its book publishing and home entertainment unit, which had a 19% rise in operating profit, to $229 million. Reader's Digest magazine, including its domestic and numerous international editions, had operating profit of $75.4 million, a 22% increase from 1989.

A growing area of weakness was the company's special interest magazines, which include New Choices, Family Handyman, Travel Holiday, American Health and the recently acquired Moneywise, a U.K. personal finance magazine. Losses at the unit grew to $26 million from $17.2 million in fiscal 1989.

Reader's Digest chairman George V. Grune said the losses were due to "investment spending" on magazines to improve their editorial quality. He said soft advertising for consumer magazines also contributed to the unit's losses.
