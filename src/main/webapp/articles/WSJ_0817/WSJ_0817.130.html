Indians in Ontario Block Main East-West Rail Lines

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/17/90

MONTREAL -- Canada's main East-West railroad lines were blocked by two groups of Indians in northern Ontario, halting freight and passenger traffic between Toronto and Winnipeg, Manitoba. Taking their cue from Mohawk Indians near Montreal who have blocked a bridge into the city for the past six weeks, the Indians are seeking provincial and federal government action on land claims and other issues, government officials said. Leaders of the Indian groups couldn't be reached for comment.

CP Rail, a unit of Montreal-based Canadian Pacific Ltd., said the line was blocked at about noon yesterday at Mobert, Ontario, by Indians from a reservation there.

CP Rail spokesman Paul Thurston said the railroad is studying ways to reroute rail traffic.

Another blockade began Monday when Indians at Long Lac, Ontario, blocked government-owned Canadian National Railways' main East-West lines.

Indian and Native Affairs Minister Thomas Siddon said the federal government won't negotiate land claims with the Indians as long as the blockades remain in place.

Industry officials said the two railroads carry about 20% to 30% of East-West traffic in Canada, with bulk commodities accounting for most of the railroads' business.
