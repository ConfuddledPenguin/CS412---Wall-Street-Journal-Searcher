REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: Lifestyle Changes

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/17/90

When Multnomah County, Oregon, officials two months ago began rousting those urban outdoorsmen known as the homeless from their encampments under bridges and along railroad tracks, they expected the load at official shelters to swell. It didn't. Either there weren't as many homeless as was thought, or they couldn't face the "zero-tolerance" policy of some of the shelters toward intoxicants. The liberal Oregonian newspaper, under the headline "Boozers get boot," quotes a Salvation Army official saying of transients who won't straighten up: "Portland may not be willing to tolerate their lifestyle any longer."
