General Motors Idles Plant in California

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/17/90

DETROIT -- General Motors Corp. said the six-day strike at a parts plant in Flint, Mich., forced the company to idle the Van Nuys, Calif., assembly plant that builds the Pontiac Firebird and Chevrolet Camaro. The California facility was closed yesterday, putting about 3,500 employees out of work. It will reopen Monday.

The Flint walkout, which ended Aug. 13, caused GM to shut all or part of nine U.S. assembly plants. All but the Van Nuys plant and a truck plant in Ohio are scheduled to reopen by today.

Meanwhile, the 10 major domestic auto makers planned to build 111,263 cars this week, down 12.2% from 126,654 cars manufactured a year earlier.
