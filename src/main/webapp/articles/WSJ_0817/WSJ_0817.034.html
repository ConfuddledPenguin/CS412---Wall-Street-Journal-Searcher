Marketing & Media: Dow Jones to Stop Using Bloomberg's Distribution Service

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/17/90

NEW YORK -- Dow Jones & Co. said it would stop distributing its newswires through Bloomberg L.P. starting in September 1991. Bloomberg distributes prices of financial instruments, including stocks and bonds, and provides analytic features through its terminals. About three months ago, Bloomberg began a newswire, Bloomberg Business News, that carries corporate news announcements, bond market coverage and foreign exchange reports, among other features.

A Dow Jones spokesman said it acted under its longstanding policy against distributing its news services through competitors. "This decision has no bearing on our relationship with other vendors," he added.

Dow Jones also publishes The Wall Street Journal, Barron's magazine, and community newspapers, and operates the Telerate Inc. electronic financial information system.
