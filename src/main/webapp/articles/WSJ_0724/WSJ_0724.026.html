Who's News: General Dynamics Assigns Its President To A-12 Plane Project

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/24/90

ST. LOUIS -- General Dynamics Corp., in an effort to shore up its troubled A-12 Navy attack-plane program, said it placed several senior executives on special assignment, including Herbert F. Rogers, president and chief operating officer. Mr. Rogers will relocate to Fort Worth, Texas, where the plane program is located, and assume the duties of general manager of the Fort Worth division. Mr. Rogers will continue to report directly to Stanley C. Pace, chairman and chief executive officer.

William A. Anders, General Dynamics' vice chairman, will assume Mr. Rogers's corporate management duties. The new assignments "will not change the timetable" of Mr. Anders's scheduled appointment as chairman and chief executive officer of the company Jan. 1, a spokesman said.

Charles A. Anderson, corporate vice president and general manager of the Fort Worth division, will become program director of the A-12. John A. Lamers, vice president of the A-12 program, will become deputy program director.

Mr. Pace said the new assignments will help focus necessary corporate and division resources on the A-12. The company last week took a $450 million charge against second-quarter earnings for the delayed program, resulting in a net loss of $240 million. General Dynamics' partner in the program, McDonnell Douglas Corp., didn't take any such charge.

In addition to improving performance on the A-12 contract, Mr. Rogers's special assignment "will assure that the other important aircraft and business programs of the division receive continued management focus," Mr. Pace said.
