International Brief -- Telefonica de Espana S.A.: Phone Company's Earnings Jump 28.5% in First Half

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/24/90

Telefonica de Espana S.A. said net income surged 28.5% in the first half of 1990 to 42.01 billion pesetas ($417.8 million) from 32.69 billion pesetas a year earlier. The company said that a 7.3% rise in service charges helped bolster profits in the first half of 1990.

Telefonica's general director, German Ancochea, called the company's first-half performance "reasonable" in light of a "sluggish" first quarter, before higher service charges took effect.

Mr. Ancochea noted that the company is negotiating with the government to establish a pricing framework designed to guarantee Telefonica a stable income. The formula, pegged to Spain's inflation rate, would enable the company to finance its investment plans through earnings.

The telecommunications monopoly reported a 38.6% jump in financing costs, reflecting Bank of Spain's tight credit restrictions.

Financing costs stood at 68.47 billion pesetas in the first six months of the year, compared to 49.40 billion pesetas a year earlier.
