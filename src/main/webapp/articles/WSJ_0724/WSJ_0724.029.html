Japan's Vehicle Production

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/24/90

TOKYO -- Helped by strong domestic demand for passenger cars, Japan's production of cars, trucks and buses edged up 0.9% in the first half of 1990 from a year earlier to a record 6.7 million units, the Japan Automobile Manufacturers' Association reported. In June alone, however, production dropped 1.0% from a year earlier to 1.2 million units, the first year-to-year contraction in three months. In May, production grew 10.8%.

Vehicle production in the first half of the year as well as June was supported strongly by domestic demand, the association said. According to the report, domestic demand grew 10.1% in the first half from a year earlier, largely pushed up by a sharp increase in passenger car sales.
