Business Brief -- Mycogen Corp.: Biological Pesticide Maker Has First Profitable Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/24/90

Mycogen Corp., San Diego, posted its first profitable quarter, with net income in the second quarter of $209,000, or two cents a share, compared with a net loss of $184,000, or three cents a share, in the second quarter of last year. Revenue for the quarter rose 48% to $4.3 million from $2.9 million last year.

For the first half, the maker of biological pesticides said its net loss narrowed to $466,000, or five cents a share, from a net loss of $919,000, or 13 cents a share, last year. Revenue for the first six months was $7.4 million, up 55% from $4.7 million in the same period last year.

The profitable quarter follows several recent moves by the company, including receiving federal government approval in March to do large-scale commercial tests on a genetically engineered pesticide called MVP. Mycogen also entered into two separate joint research pacts; with Monsanto Co. in June to develop plants genetically resistant to pests, and with S.C. Johnson & Son Inc. in May to develop biological pesticides for common household pests.
