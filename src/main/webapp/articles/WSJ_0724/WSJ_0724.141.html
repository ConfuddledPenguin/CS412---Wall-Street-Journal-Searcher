International Brief -- Hawker Siddeley Group PLC: British Firm's Plan Fizzles To Buy Two UNC Units

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/24/90

Hawker Siddeley Group PLC, a British electrical and mechanical engineering concern, said discussions to buy two subsidiaries of UNC Inc., Annapolis, Md., were terminated after failure to agree on a price. A Hawker Siddeley spokeswoman declined to say what price range was discussed. She said that if successful, the move would have been a "major acquisition" for the group.

The two subsidiaries, Airwork Corp. and Pacific Airmotive Corp., are airplane engine overhaul and repair concerns.

The spokeswoman said the company wants to increase its presence in this industry. The purchase would have offered Hawker Siddeley a foothold in the U.S. industry. It acquired Standard Aero Ltd., a Canadian concern, last August for #53 million ($96.3 million).
