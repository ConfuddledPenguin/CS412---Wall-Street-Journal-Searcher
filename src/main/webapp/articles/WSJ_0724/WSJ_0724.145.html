Nestle Unit Plans to Sell Stouffer Restaurant Line

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/24/90

CLEVELAND (AP) -- Nestle Enterprises Inc. won't proceed with an expansion of its Stouffer Restaurant Co. and instead will try to sell the subsidiary, the Swiss-owned conglomerate announced. "The decision follows an extensive evaluation of the long-range business strategy of Nestle in the United States," Nestle Enterprises said.

Nestle, one of three U.S. subsidiaries of Swiss-based Nestle S.A., said it would begin "an orderly process of preparing the restaurant group for sale."

Stouffer operates 65 restaurants, including outlets under the names of J.B. Winberie, James Tavern, Rusty Scupper and Parkers' Lighthouse as well as the "Top" restaurants in New York, Boston, Pittsburgh, Philadelphia, Columbus, Ohio, and Cleveland.

James M. Biggar, Nestle Enterprises chairman, said operating restaurants is better suited to specialty chains and entrepreneurs than a large corporation.

He said Nestle will focus its efforts on its Nestle Foods Corp., Nestle Hills Coffee Co., Stouffer Foods Corp., Stouffer Hotel Co., L.J. Minor Corp., Wine World Estates, Sunmark Inc., and FreshNes Foods Corp.
