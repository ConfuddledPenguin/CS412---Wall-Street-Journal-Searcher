LEISURE & ARTS: Time Off: Diversions and Excursions July 24-Aug. 6

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/24/90

ART

National Gallery of Art, Washington, D.C.: "George Caleb Bingham" -- Nearly 30 paintings by the 19th-century American genre and landscape artist, including the well-known "The Jolly Flatboatmen" and "Fur Traders Descending the Missouri."

Fourth Street at Constitution Avenue NW, through Sept. 30. Chrysler Museum, Norfolk, Va.: "A Golden Age: Art and Society in Hungary, 1896-1914" -- More than 300 works -- including paintings, drawings, photographs, textiles, ceramics, stained glass and furniture -- document the search for a national style and the development of art nouveau in Hungary. Olney Road and Mowbray Arch, through Sept. 30. The show then travels to the San Diego Museum of Art, Nov. 10, 1990-Jan.5, 1991.

Museum of Fine Arts, Boston: "Unlocking the Hidden Museum: Riches From the Storeroom" -- Nearly 200 works of art from the museum's "backstage," some of which have not been on display for decades. Included are etchings by Rembrandt, paintings by Millet and Fragonard, shawls from Kashmir and Japanese netsuke. Also look for a display of fake artworks, and a section on art conservation. 465 Huntington Ave., through Sept. 16.

DANCE

Australian Ballet: Australia's national classical troupe tours the U.S. for the first time in 14 years with a new generation of dancers, a new artistic director and some new works. The dates at New York's Metropolitan Opera House (through July 29, 212-362-6000) and at Washington's Kennedy Center (July 31-Aug. 5, 202-467-4600) feature the U.S. premiere of Laszlo Seregi's "Spartacus" and director Maina Gielgud's production of "Giselle." The Costa Mesa, Calif., run (Orange County Performing Arts Center, Aug. 7-12, 714-556-ARTS) includes the premiere of "Catalyst," a piece by Stephen Baynes set to Poulenc's double piano concerto.

SPECTACLE

"Moscow on Ice": The thaw in U.S.-Soviet relations brings this musically eclectic company to the U.S. for the first time. Sixty performers, many from the Moscow State Ballet on Ice, will skate choreography inspired by sources as disparate as Russian folk dances, classical ballet and Janet Jackson's "Rhythm Nation." Scheduled dates on the lengthy tour include: Atlantic City, N.J. (Convention Hall), through July 29; West Palm Beach, Fla. (West Palm Auditorium), Sept. 7-8; Biloxi, Miss. (Mississippi Coliseum), Sept. 9; Atlanta (Omni), Sept. 11; Miami (James L. Knight Center), Sept. 23; Reno, Nev. (Lawlor Events Center), Sept. 26-29; Boulder, Colo. (University of Colorado), Oct. 9-10; Oklahoma City, Okla. (Civic Music Hall), Oct. 29-31; Seattle (Seattle Coliseum), Nov. 20-21; Great Falls, Mont. (Four Seasons Arena), Dec. 19-20; New York City (City Center), Dec. 31, 1990-Jan. 13 1991.

MUSIC

New York City Opera offers a new production of Stephen Sondheim's "A Little Night Music," in its first major New York revival since its Tony Award-winning Broadway run in the early '70s. The show -- to be televised on PBS Nov. 7 -- stars Sally Ann Howes as Desiree and Regina Resnik as Madame Armfeldt. Paul Gemignani conducts. New York State Theater, Lincoln Center, Aug. 3, 10, 12, 16, 25; and returning Oct. 16, 20, 27, Nov. 1, 4 and 7. (212) 870-5570.

Festival of Contemporary Music at Tanglewood, Lenox, Mass., opens with Copland's "Ceremonial Fanfare" (1969). The program also includes works written in the past decade by distinguished alumni from the period of Copland's tenure at Tanglewood -- George Crumb, Jacob Druckman, Lukas Foss and Luciano Berio. Theatre-Concert Hall, Aug. 4. Other events run through Aug. 9. (413) 637-1600.

New World Symphony performs four outdoor concerts as part of Montana's Big Sky Arts Festival. Highlights include guest appearances by Californian Wendy Chen, winner of the 1990 National Piano Competition (July 25); Free Flight, a flute, keyboard, bass and drum quartet (July 28); and by classical guitarist Christopher Parkening (July 30). Leif Bjaland and John Nelson share the conducting duties. Arabesque Stage, Meadow Village. (406) 9954211, ext. 2400.
