Bankers Trust Reports Unchanged Earnings For Second Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/24/90

NEW YORK -- Bankers Trust New York Corp. reported second-quarter earnings of $174 million, or $2.06 a share, flat with the year-ago figure of $175 million, or $2.14 a share. Excluding tax benefits and a gain on the sale of its factoring business, operating earnings were off 8.6% to $147 million, or $1.73 a share, compared with $161 million, or $1.97, a year ago, according to analysts.

Chairman Charles S. Sanford Jr. said: "We again saw superior results in our trading and risk management and trust businesses, as well as a slight improvement in corporate finance" from the preceding quarters.

For the first six months, Bankers Trust's earnings rose 10% to $372 million, or $4.42 a share, from $339 million, or $4.16, a year ago.

Trading revenue posted a strong $50 million, or 26%, rise to $243 million.

Net interest revenue dropped 21.3% to $191 million during the quarter, partly because of the continuing absence of Brazilian interest payments and an $11 million reduction for the recently completed Mexican debt accord and the pending Venezuelan debt pact.

Non-performing real estate loans, presumably including $100 million owed by developer Donald Trump, rose $101 million during the period to $219 million. Problem loans to highly leveraged transactions increased a modest $10 million to $221 million.

The provision to Bankers Trust's reserves for possible loan losses was $28 million, the same amount that was charged off against its reserve for non-developing country debt. Also charged off were $129 million in developing country loans, including $40 million owed by Brazil.

In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange yesterday, Bankers Trust closed at $39.50, down 37.5 cents.
