Business Brief -- Cyclops Industries Inc.: Losses Set for 2nd Quarter, Half, on Revamping Charge

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/24/90

Cyclops Industries Inc., a Pittsburgh-based producer of stainless and carbon steel products, said it will report losses for the second quarter and the first half. It cited a pretax charge of as much as $16 million for restructuring its specialty steel division and realigning certain carbon strip operations.

The company said it will close a Pittsburgh plant that produces high-temperature steel sheets, idling 85 hourly and 10 salaried employees. It said high-temperature sheet has been a low-volume product for some time.

In the 1989 second quarter, Cyclops earned $12.1 million, or $1.69 a share, on sales of $301.5 million. In the 1989 first half, the company had net income of $21.3 million, or $2.98 a share, on sales of $608.3 million. Net for the 1990 first quarter fell to $3.6 million, or 49 cents a share, from $10.1 million, or $1.42 a share, a year earlier, on sales that slipped to $299.3 million from $364.7 million.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Cyclops shares closed at $24, unchanged.
