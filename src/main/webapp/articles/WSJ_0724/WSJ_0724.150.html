Chevron Revamping Resources Line, Sets 2nd-Quarter Charge

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/24/90

SAN FRANCISCO -- Chevron Corp. said it is restructuring its non-coal minerals business, Chevron Resources Co., and taking a $30 million charge against second-quarter earnings for anticipated losses on the sales of assets. The charge will be included in the oil company's earnings report, scheduled for today. Chevron said it expects the asset sales could total more than $70 million. In the year-earlier second quarter, Chevron had net income of $404 million, or $1.18 a share, on revenue of $8.2 billion.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Chevron shares closed at $76.875, down 62.5 cents.

Assets to be sold in the restructuring include several gold properties, and American Gilsonite Co., a Salt Lake City-based producer of solid hydrocarbon. Chevron said it is still reviewing long-range plans for its uranium operations in New Mexico and South Texas.

The restructuring will result in the elimination or transfer of 210 jobs, of which 150 are held by exploration and other field employees. About 10 employees will be transferred to a new strategic-minerals business unit that will manage the three major minerals projects that Chevron intends to retain.

Projects to be retained are the Stillwater platinum and palladium operations in Nye and Columbus, Mont., where Chevron is the managing partner and owns a 50% stake in Stillwater Mining Co.; the Collahuasi copper and silver property in Chile; and the Lisheen zinc and lead property in Ireland.

Minerals operations to be retained will report to Chevron's Pittsburg & Midway Coal Mining Co. unit based in Englewood, Colo. Chevron Resources will manage the disposition of its remaining assets.
