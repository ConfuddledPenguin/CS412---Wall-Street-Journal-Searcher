Who's News: Atlantic Richfield Prepares Company For Succession Needs

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/24/90

LOS ANGELES -- Atlantic Richfield Co. shuffled responsibilities for three executive vice presidents in preparation for the eventual retirement of its chairman and chief executive officer, Lodwrick M. Cook. "Our primary goal in shifting responsibilities of a number of our senior managers is to provide broader experiences in order to appropriately prepare for the company's management succession needs," said Mr. Cook, 62 years old. Mr. Cook had previously said he would remain in the top posts of the oil company until he reached 65.

Ronald J. Arnault, 47, formerly chief financial officer, was named executive vice president in charge of the company's exploration and production overseas and in the 48 contiguous states domestically. He also will oversee Arco Coal Co.

James S. Morrison, 60, was named chief financial officer succeeding Mr. Arnault. Mr. Morrison formerly was executive vice president in charge of Arco's West Coast petroleum operations, which include Arco Alaska Inc., Arco Transportation Co. and Arco Products Co.

James A. Middleton, 54, who formerly oversaw exploration and production in the 48 contiguous states, will assume Mr. Morrison's former duties overseeing West Coast petroleum operations.

Messrs. Arnault and Middleton will report to Robert E. Wycoff, 60, Arco's president and chief operating officer. Mr. Morrison will report to Mr. Cook.

Analysts said the shift in responsibilities is a particular boost for Mr. Arnault, who was a principal architect behind the company's huge restructuring program started in 1985. "It tells me that Arco is going to move aggressively in the international area," said Bernard J. Picchi, an oil analyst with Salomon Bros. in New York. "I think this is a way that (chairman) Lod Cook is saying, `We believe in international, and we want a man who was successful in restructuring the company to oversee that effort.'"
