Curtiss-Wright Says Net Advanced 6.7% In the Second Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/24/90

LYNDHURST, N.J. -- Curtiss-Wright Corp. said second-quarter net income climbed 6.7% to $7.2 million, or $1.45 a share, reflecting higher margins in the company's aerospace segment. Revenue rose 8.9% to $56.5 million from $51.9 million a year earlier. The company, which makes precision parts used in aerospace, flow-control and other industrial applications, had net in the year-earlier quarter of $6.8 million, or $1.36 a share. The year-earlier figures were restated to reflect the cumulative effect of an accounting change.

The profit would have been greater except for losses at the company's flow control and marine segment after a "modest" profit in the first quarter, Curtiss-Wright said. "The short-term situation in the area of highly engineered valves remains unsettled," the company said. Despite winning a number of Navy orders, the available number of orders has shrunk to their lowest level in a a decade or more, the company said.

In the first half, net fell 26% to $11.6 million, or $2.32 a share, from $15.8 million, or $3.16 a share. The decline reflects recognition of losses in short-term equity securities, accelerated amortization of nonrecurring non-cash charges in the first quarter and an upward restatement of 1989 first-quarter earnings. Revenue rose 5.1% to $109.5 million from $104.2 million.
