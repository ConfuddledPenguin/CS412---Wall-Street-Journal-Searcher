Eastern Air Creditors Get Checchi Liquidation Plan

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 07/24/90

A proposal by Alfred A. Checchi, chairman of Northwest Airlines parent NWA Inc., suggesting a "controlled liquidation" of Eastern Airlines, was presented to financial advisers for Eastern's creditors, but no action was taken. Under the proposal, Northwest, St. Paul, Minn., would take over certain Eastern planes, airport gates and maintenance facilities, and arrange the sale of the rest of Eastern's assets to benefit its creditors, people familiar with the proposal said. If such an arrangement materializes, Northwest could strengthen its position on the East Coast with little risk, while offering Eastern's creditors a resolution of the carrier's 16-month-old proceedings under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code.

Advisers to Eastern's creditors are expected to report on the plan soon to the full creditors committee. That panel's endorsement would probably speed the bankruptcy court approval needed for such a proposal to take effect. Committee representatives couldn't be reached for comment.

Eastern reiterated it hasn't received any offer from Northwest to acquire any part of the airline. It said that Martin R. Shugrue Jr., the court-appointed trustee running the airline, a unit of Houston-based Continental Airlines Holdings Inc., has said there is no intention to liquidate Eastern, and that the carrier is interested only in offers for the complete operation and its employees.
