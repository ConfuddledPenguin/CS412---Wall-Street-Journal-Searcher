International: U.K. May Decide To Sell PowerGen To Hanson PLC

  • Author: Barbara Toman
  • Date: 07/24/90

LONDON -- The British government said it may shelve plans to float PowerGen, Britain's second largest electricity-generating concern, and instead sell it to Hanson PLC or another company. Hanson, a conglomerate with interests in consumer goods, building products, foods and a variety of manufacturing industries, confirmed yesterday that it has indicated to the government "a firm interest" in bidding for PowerGen, a state-owned concern scheduled to be privatized in February.

The sale is part of Prime Minister Margaret Thatcher's electric-utility privatization plan that is expected to raise #11 billion ($20 billion). Analysts say PowerGen alone could fetch #1 billion.

Energy Secretary John Wakeham told the House of Commons that if Hanson decides to bid for PowerGen, he will invite other companies to make offers. Analysts suggested that companies from continental Europe and perhaps the U.S. would join any eventual auction.

But Mr. Wakeham stressed that the government hasn't ruled out floating the generating concern. "I therefore propose to pursue discussions with Hanson in parallel with pushing forward preparations for the flotation of PowerGen," he said.

Yesterday's announcement, which sparked angry debate in Parliament, marked the second major shift in the government's strategy for privatizing the electricity industry. In November, the government withdrew all of Britain's nuclear power plants from the planned sale after the plants were publicly criticized as unreliable and uneconomic.

Recently, critics of the plan have suggested there isn't sufficient investor interest to successfully float both PowerGen and National Power, Britain's largest generating concern. Mr. Wakeham called Hanson's overture "a vote of confidence" in PowerGen and its management.

Hanson's deputy chairman, Martin Taylor, called PowerGen "a strong, good basic industry supplying continuing needs." He declined to say how much Hanson would be willing to pay.
