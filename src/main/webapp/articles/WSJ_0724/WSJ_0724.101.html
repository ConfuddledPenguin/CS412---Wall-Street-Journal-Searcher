Letters to the Editor: A Lonely Martyr In Hitler's Reich

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/24/90

I submit the following footnote to Peter Keresztes's review of "Letters to Freya, 1939-1945" (Leisure & Art, June 25), the correspondence of the German resistance leader Count Helmuth James von Moltke to his wife, Freya. Readers of the review will be interested in George F. Kennan's tribute to Moltke found on pages 119-122 of his "Memoirs: 1925-1950" (Little, Brown, 1967).

Mr. Kennan writes: "I consider him, in fact, to have been the greatest person, morally, and the largest and most enlightened in his concepts, that I met on either side of the battle lines in the Second World War."

He concludes: "I record all this because the image of this lonely, struggling man, one of the few genuine Protestant-Christian martyrs of our time, has remained for me over the intervening years a pillar of moral conscience and an unfailing source of political and intellectual inspiration."

J. Frederick Stevenson

Blairstown, N.J.

---

My compliments for running a review of "Letters to Freya" by the remarkable Count von Moltke. He was a great hero. Not to nitpick, but as an old OSS man I was much involved and the Stauffenberg assassination attempt was July 20, 1944, not July 1. The term "Zwanzigster Juli" has entered the German language as a euphemism for courage and sacrifice.

John Weitz

New York
