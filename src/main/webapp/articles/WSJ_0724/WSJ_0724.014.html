General Signal Posts $21.9 Million Loss In Second Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/24/90

STAMFORD, Conn. -- General Signal Corp. reported a second-quarter loss of $21.9 million, or $1.14 a share, reflecting a $60 million charge on the divestiture of three semiconductor businesses. In the year-earlier quarter, the maker of electrical controls equipment posted a profit of $18.7 million, or 98 cents per share. Sales for the current quarter were $481.6 million, down from $488.5 million.

The $60 million charge, on a pretax basis, reflected the shedding of three semiconductor businesses, which "have been unprofitable and were not forecasted to grow in accordance with targeted objectives," according to Edmund M. Carpenter, chairman and chief executive officer. Mr. Carpenter observed that "based on first-half order input and divestitures, we feel very positive about second-half results." The year-earlier quarter included a $3.1 million gain on the sale of a defense unit.

For the six months, General Signal reported a loss of $1.6 million, or nine cents per share, against profit of $38.5 million, or $2.02 per share in the year-earlier period. Sales declined to $952 million, from $966.9 million. The results also included the $60 million loss and $3.1 million gain in the respective periods.
