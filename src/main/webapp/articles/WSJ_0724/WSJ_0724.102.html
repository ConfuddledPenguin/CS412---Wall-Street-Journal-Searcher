Mergers and Acquisitions Activity Rises, Value Drops

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/24/90

SCHAUMBURG, Ill. -- The value of mergers and acquisitions proposed in the second quarter plunged 46%, despite a 12% rise in the number of transactions, as the focus of activity shifted to sell-offs of "mid-market price range" businesses, a Merrill Lynch & Co. unit says. There were 626 M&A deals valued at $30 billion that were initiated in April, May and June, compared with 561 transactions valued at $56.4 billion in the year-earlier three-month period, according to W.T. Grimm & Co.

"The megadeals of the 1980s are no longer the norm," says Beth Rosencrans, research director at W.T. Grimm.

The value of corporate combinations proposed in the second quarter also fell 13% from the $34.3 billion of transactions begun in the first quarter, as the number of transactions launched rose 6.8% from 584 in the first three months of the year, W.T. Grimm says.

The total value of the 10 biggest transactions announced in the first six months of 1990, $22 billion, is less than half the value -- $55 billion -- of transactions announced in the like 1989 period, W.T. Grimm says.
