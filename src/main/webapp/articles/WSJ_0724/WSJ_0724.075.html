China's Imports Drop

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/24/90

BEIJING -- China's imports in the first half of 1990 fell by almost one-fourth compared with the like period last year, a government official said, blaming the drop on economic sanctions imposed after China's crackdown on last year's pro-democracy movement. China's exports, meanwhile, jumped 22% from the first half of 1989, totaling $24.5 billion, Liu Xiangdong, spokesman for the Ministry of Foreign Economic Relations and Trade, said.

June's export volume of $5.32 billion was China's highest-ever in a single month.
