Marketing & Media: Blackstone, Pompadur Set Media Investing Venture

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/24/90

Blackstone Group and media investor I. Martin Pompadur said they formed a joint venture to invest $100 million of equity capital in domestic and foreign media and entertainnent properties. The investors are entering the media market at the bottom, which may be to their advantage as buyers. With uncertainty in the regulatory market and the restrictions on highly leveraged transactions that characterized the cable industry in the past, for example, cable system sales have slowed to a trickle. Radio and television stations have been hurt by a difficult advertising climate, as have newspapers and magazines.

"It's really a sluggish time" because of the various problems confronting the industry, Mr. Pompadur said, adding that a number of media properties could be helped with an infusion of equity and some operating expertise. Henry Silverman, a general partner at the Blackstone investment firm, said his group believes it is "a perfect time to get into the media business" because many operators are facing a liquidity crunch. He said the venture will specifically seek distressed situations.

Mr. Pompadur is a longtime media executive and investor who has raised several hundred million dollars since 1983 through various public limited partnerships for media investments.

Blackstone's Mr. Silverman said the $100 million will come from Blackstone's general pool of funds.
