What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/24/90

STOCKS PLUNGED in a broad sell-off that left the Dow industrials down 56.44 points at 2904.70. The industrials were off more than 105 points at one stage, triggering "circuit breakers" that helped stanch the selling pressure. Earnings disappointments sparked the slide, as investors dumped growth stocks.

Bonds closed lower, although the rout in stocks lifted prices sporadically throughout the day as investors sought the safety of Treasury issues. The dollar tumbled to a 31-month low against the mark during the session.

---

Loral won the bidding for Ford's aerospace unit, beating out a Westinghouse group and GM's Hughes Aircraft and Belgium's Alcatel.

---

Merger talks were ended between Higher Education Assistance Foundation and another student-loan guarantor, leaving open the possibility of bank losses unless the government agrees to a full rescue.

---

RJR Nabisco posted a $108 million second-quarter loss but strong gains in operating profit and cash flow.

---

Ford Motor told dealers it plans to raise prices on most 1991 models by 4% to 5%, with some models jumping as much as 9%. Some dealers and analysts predicted Ford will have trouble making the increases stick.

---

Martha Seger may resign her post as a Federal Reserve governor this year for personal reasons. Ms. Seger has been the panel's most consistent advocate of easier money.

---

Kansas City P&L launched an $857 million tender offer for Kansas Gas & Electric in what appeared to be the first major hostile bid in the heavily regulated electric industry.

---

Time Warner incurred a second-quarter loss of $189 million after interest payments and dividends, and cash flow rose only 3.3% in the quarter.

---

Exxon posted a big rise in second-quarter net income, but Amoco said its profit declined 24% while Atlantic Richfield reported a 16% drop.

---

Backer Spielvogel lost one of its most prestigious clients when Xerox pulled its $25 million-plus account and handed it to Young & Rubicam.

---

Charles Schwab was fined $375,000 by the Big Board for allegedly misusing customers' stock for its profit.

---

A joint venture owned by Nestle and General Mills agreed to buy the breakfast cereal business of Ranks Hovis McDougall for $167 million.

---

The House Judiciary Committee approved measures to investigate the causes of the S&L industry crisis and require stiff prison sentences.

---

S&L bailout costs led to a budget deficit of $11.22 billion during June, a month when corporate tax payments usually produce a surplus.

---

Canadian auto workers are in a stronger position than their U.S. counterparts as contract talks begin.

---
