House Clears Two-Part Bill On SEC and Penny Stocks

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/24/90

WASHINGTON -- The House passed legislation that would give the Securities and Exchange Commission enhanced enforcement power and would attempt to curb abuses in the penny-stock market. The bill, approved by voice vote, is a combination of two pieces of legislation. One would give the SEC its first fining power outside of insider-trading charges. In egregious situations, it would allow the agency to bar corporate officers and directors from serving in such capacities at public companies.

The other piece would force penny-stock dealers to provide additional information to investors.

The Senate already has passed a bill similar to the House's enforcement measure, but has no penny-stock legislation in any advanced stage. The bills apparently were combined to force the Senate to consider including the penny-stock measure during a House-Senate conference on the legislation.

"These two bills represent a powerful expansion of the enforcement weapons available to combat the problems of securities fraud in general and the penny-stock market in particular," said Rep. Edward Markey, the Massachusetts Democrat who heads the House subcommittee on telecommunications and finance.

Still, the penny-stock provision represents a toned-down version of the original bill, which the SEC had said could curb some legitimate capital-raising activities.
