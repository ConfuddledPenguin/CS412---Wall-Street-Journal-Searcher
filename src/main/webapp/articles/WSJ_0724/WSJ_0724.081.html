President Proposes To End Some Tariffs For Andean Nations

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/24/90

WASHINGTON -- President Bush proposed the removal of all U.S. tariffs on certain products exported by Ecuador, Bolivia, Peru and Colombia, and promised to negotiate long-term trade agreements with these countries. Mr. Bush's proposal yesterday, which requires congressional approval, is part of an administration strategy of offering trade incentives to Central and South America and, in particular, to the Andean nations, which are the world's largest producers of coca and its derivative, cocaine.

Last month, the administration proposed a comprehensive package of debt reduction and investment incentives for Latin America. Yesterday's announcement was designed to show special concern for countries attempting to fight production and distribution of illegal drugs.

It was also timed to coincide with yesterday's visit to Washington of Ecuadoran President Rodrigo Borja, whom Mr. Bush lauded for having eradicated coca production in his country in recent years.

In addition to the removal of tariffs on such products as plywood, canned crab and various agricultural goods, the Bush administration package would provide U.S. technical assistance to Latin America and the Caribbean, to aid in developing agricultural trade for the U.S. Mr. Bush also signed a package that removes tariff on 67 other products exported by the Andeans. And he signed a measure establishing a council to begin work on developing long-term trade and investment agreements between the U.S. and the Andean nations.
