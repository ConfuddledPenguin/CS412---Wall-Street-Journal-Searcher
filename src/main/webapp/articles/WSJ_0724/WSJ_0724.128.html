Trans World Music Plans Public Offering Of One Million Shares

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/24/90

ALBANY, N.Y. -- Trans World Music Corp., a music and videocassette retailer, plans a public offering of one million common shares, its first offering since July 1986, when the stock began trading over the counter. In a registration statement filed with the Securities and Exchange Commission, Trans World said that Robert J. Higgins, its president, chief executive officer and largest shareholder, will offer 750,000 shares, reducing his 67% stake to 57%, and that the company will offer 250,000 shares. The company has about nine million shares outstanding.

James R. Williamson, chief financial officer, said the offering price is expected to be "near market price." Trans World shares closed at $31.50, down $1.50, yesterday in national over-the-counter trading.

Goldman, Sachs & Co., which managed the initial public offering, will underwrite the latest offering, with an option to purchase an additional 150,000 shares.

Proceeds would be used to reduce short-term debt and finance the opening of about 120 stores in the eastern U.S. this year.

Trans World recently settled a trademark-infringement suit for $1.5 million with a rival retailer, Peaches Entertainment Corp., and last year had losses when it leased space in stores owned by Crazy Eddie Inc., a defunct electronics chain.

Separately, Mr. Williamson, the finance chief, said he is resigning, pending the appointment of a successor and an orderly transition. The company said he is relocating to the Midwest for personal reasons.
