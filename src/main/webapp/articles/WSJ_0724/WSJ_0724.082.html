Carbide Net Falls 38%, Monsanto's Increases by 2.5%

  • Author: Richard Koenig
  • Date: 07/24/90

Union Carbide Corp. reported that second-quarter profit fell 38%, while Monsanto Co. posted a 2.5% increase made possible by an assets sale. Carbide, based in Danbury, Conn., continued to be hurt by the downward pressure on prices in its mainstay commodity chemical business, though the company's earnings were toward the high end of the range of analysts' estimates.

Monsanto, based in St. Louis, would have posted a 9.6% profit decline without the one-time gain on the sale, largely because of a weather-related decline in sales of agricultural chemicals. Its operating performance was in line with analysts' expectations.

The results of these two major chemicals companies will probably be mirrored as others in the industry disclose results of the latest quarter. Other commodity makers besides Carbide, hurt partly by excess production capacity in certain lines, are expected to post sharp profit declines. More diversifed companies, like Monsanto, are expected to report more modest decreases or in some cases increases.

In the latest quarter, Carbide earned $116.0 million, or 81 cents a share, against $186 million, or $1.33 a share, a year earlier. The results included a nonrecurring gain of $20 million, or 14 cents a share, from the sale of a business to Hoechst Celanese Corp. That gain was partially offset by a charge of $8.6 million, or six cents a share, related to the expense of discontinuing certain business lines.

In chemicals and plastics, pretax operating profit fell 39% to $172 million. Profit in industrial gases increased 59% to $121 million. In carbon products, profit dropped 18% to $14 million.

Quarterly sales were $2.14 billion, down less than 1% from $2.28 billion a year ago.

Six-month profit was $210 million, or $1.47 a share, down 46% from the year-ago $387 million, or $2.76 a share.

In composite trading yesterday on the New York Stock Exchange, Carbide shares closed at $19.625, up 62.5 cents.

At Monsanto, quarterly earnings were $247 million, or $1.90 a share, up from $241 million, or $1.77 a share, a year earlier.

The company's one-time gain in the latest quarter was $31 million, or 24 cents a share, which resulted from the divestiture of certain assets of a joint venture in Japan with Mitsubishi Kasei Corp.

Pretax operating profit from crop chemicals fell 20% to $172 million from the record quarter of a year ago. In the other chemicals segment, whose products include plastic for auto windshields, profit fell 12% to $127 million, owing in large part to slower sales into the U.S. auto industry. Profit in the G.D. Searle & Co. pharmaceutical unit was $13 million, versus a $4 million loss a year ago.

Sales edged up 1% to $2.37 billion from $2.35 billion.

Six-month profit was $441 million, or $3.37 a share, down 4.8% from the year-earlier $463 million, or $3.39 a share.

Monsanto shares rose $1 to close at $50.25 in composite Big Board trading.
