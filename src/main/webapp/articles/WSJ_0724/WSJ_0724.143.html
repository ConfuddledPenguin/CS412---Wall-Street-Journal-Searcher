International Brief -- Oakbridge Ltd.: Concern Is Now the Object Of Second Takeover Effort

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/24/90

McIlwraith McEacharn Ltd., an Australian coal-mining concern, is leading a consortium with Toyo Menka Kaisha Ltd. and Nippon Oil Ltd., both of Japan, in a bid for control of Oakbridge Ltd. Oakbridge, another Australian coal mining company, is currently the target of a takeover bid by New Zealand-based Elders Resources NZFP Ltd., which holds around 85% of Oakbridge.

The McIlwraith consortium said yesterday that it will offer 88 cents a share for each common share in Oakbridge, 88 cents for each convertible note, and 0.13 cents for each listed option. That offer values all of Oakbridge at around 300 million Australian dollars ($235.5 million).

Elders Resources is bidding 90 cents a share for Oakbridge, but that offer is conditional on winning at least 90% of the shares. Otherwise the offer is 86 cents a share, McIlwraith said.

McIlwraith's bid is aimed only at acquiring Elders Resources' controlling bloc of Oakbridge shares, but it will take all shares offered to it.

It is advising other shareholders to hold on to their shares. The McIlwraith consortium currently holds 2.6% of Oakbridge's stock. It said other investors holding more than 10% of the stock won't accept the Elders Resources offer, indicating the 90% threshold won't be achieved by Elders Resources.
