Business Brief -- McDonald's Corp.: Restaurants Plan to Switch To Low-Cholesterol Frying

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/24/90

McDonald's Corp., joining the fast-food flight from cholesterol, plans to stop cooking its french fries and hash browns in saturated fat. At one time, McDonald's, based in Oak Brook, Ill., had resisted suggestions that it drop beef tallow shortening, which is heavy in saturated fat, from its cooking process. The company contended that the tallow enhanced the fries' taste.

But yesterday, after a similar move by rival Burger King, McDonald's said it will convert from a blend of tallow and vegetable shortenings to an all-vegetable oil it said is nearly free of cholesterol. The current blend is 55% unsaturated. The new cooking process will be 100% unsaturated, McDonald's said.

McDonald's has been under increasing pressure from nutrition-minded critics to drop tallow. In its announcement yesterday, the fast-food chain said the decision "was based on the very positive results from more than eight years of extensive research and customer tests."

It said the change is expected to be completed in its U.S. restaurants by year's end.
