Meritor Savings Had Loss of $136 Million In Second Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/24/90

PHILADELPHIA -- Meritor Savings Bank reported a second-quarter loss of $136 million, or $3.90 per share, as expected. The loss reflects heavy charges for recapitalization and restructuring, including a provision of $43 million for loan losses. Meritor's year-earlier losses for the second quarter were $21 million, or 61 cents a share.

Roger S. Hillas, Meritor chairman, said the results showed "progress in long-term strategic restructuring of the institution." A debt-recapitalization plan approved by federal regulators this month will increase Meritor's shareholder equity by about $98 million, which will be reflected in the third quarter.

The plan will put Meritor in compliance with all the new capital requirements under the thrift bailout law passed last year. Meritor, the nation's largest thrift in the early 1980s, has shrunk nearly 70% to about $7 billion from $13 billion.

Among the costs and charges in the quarter, Meritor had a loss of $74.5 million, or $2.12 per share, in connection with its sale of 54 branches to Mellon Bank Corp. Meritor also wrote off $172 million in goodwill connected with the branch offices sale, terminated interest rate swap and floor contracts at a cost of $55 million and established a $54 million reserve for expenses from unused office space.

Meritor also quadrupled its provision for loan losses from a year earlier to $43 million. A "significant portion" of the loan-loss provision is due to deteriorating commercial and real-estate loans and Meritor's plans to accelerate the disposal of non-performing assets, Mr. Hillas said.
