Technology: Unisys Introduces Several Mainframes, Filling Out 2 Lines

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/24/90

BLUE BELL, Pa. -- Unisys Corp. unveiled several mainframe computers that are generally 15% to 20% faster than its current models, helping the computer and defense-electronics company flesh out two important product lines. Machines in the new A16 series, toward the high end of the A series, are designed to replace the A9 and A10 and some A12 models. These are, for example, more than twice as powerful as the A10, and priced a bit less than twice as much. The five A16s are priced from $1.8 million to $5.9 million. Unisys also unveiled four A12s that are 20% faster than current counterparts, and priced at $495,000 to $2.4 million.

The company introduced machines at the high and low ends of its other major line of mainframes, the 2200. The 2200/600 ES is as much as 15% faster than current high-end machines but requires only 40% as much power and cooling. Prices range from $2.3 million to $8.8 million. At the low end, Unisys introduced the 2200/100, aimed at individual departments of big companies. The 2200/100 -- available immediately, as are all the other machines -- ranges from $115,000 to $200,000 in price.

All of the machines are likely to appeal mainly to current Unisys customers but are nonetheless important. Though Unisys's market share in mainframes has been ebbing in recent years, as more customers go for machines that are compatible with International Business Machines Corp. equipment, Unisys's mainframe lines are still its mainstay.

Unisys, which was marginally profitable in the first half after a $639 million loss last year, must keep these lines healthy until it can make the transition from reliance on big systems toward small computers that run software that is standard in the industry.
