Ford to Pay $1.2 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/24/90

WASHINGTON -- Ford Motor Co. agreed to pay a $1.2 million penalty to settle charges of failing to keep proper job safety and injury records required by the Labor Department. Separately, the department's Occupational Safety and Health Administration said the company agreed to establish a program to reduce injuries caused by repetitive motion tasks -- which often result from assembly line jobs -- at 96% of its factories.

As part of its record-keeping agreement, Ford is required to set up an audit system for workers' health records at 12 company facilities and, if the error rate is more than 5% at any one plant or nationwide, it must retrain its record-keeping staff.

The penalty results from the 1989 inspection of a Ford Electronics & Refrigeration facility in Lansdale, Pa. OSHA officials said the incident was the third time the company was cited for record-keeping violations.

To help prevent repetitive motion injuries, the company can change the design of its tools and workstations and use mechanical devices to do excessive lifting. The United Auto Workers Union, which represents Ford Workers, will partly administer the program.
