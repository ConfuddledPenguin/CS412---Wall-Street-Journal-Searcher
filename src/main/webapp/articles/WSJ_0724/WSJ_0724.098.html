New Securities Issues

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/24/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATES

NCNB Corp. -- $200 million of subordinated notes, due July 15, 2015, was priced at par to yield 10.20%. Rated single-A-3 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and single-A-minus by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets.

MORTGAGE

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $500 million of real estate mortgage investment conduit securities is being offered in 19 classes by Kidder, Peabody & Co. The offering, Series 1990-98, is backed by Fannie Mae 9 1/2% securities. Details on yields and prices weren't immediately available.

ASSET-BACKED

Sears Receivables Financing Group -- $250 million of pass-through securities backed by credit-card receivables with a coupon rate of 8.70% at 99.855 to yield 8.75%. The issue, Sears Credit Account Trust 1990C, was priced at a yield spread of 0.41 percentage point above the Treasury's five-year note. Maturity date is Aug. 25, 1995. Sears, Roebuck & Co. is servicing the underlying loans. The offering is expected to be rated triple-A by S&P and Moody's, based on the quality of the underlying loans and a subordinated class that now totals 8.5% of the senior securities. The subordinated class absorbs losses on the senior securities.

OTHER

Kingdom of Spain -- $300 million of notes, due Aug. 1, 2000, was priced at par to yield 9.125%. The noncallable notes were priced at a spread of 0.65 percentage point above the Treasury's 10-year note. Rated double-A-2 by Moody's and double-A by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Morgan Stanley & Co.

EUROBONDS

Nordic Investment Bank (agency) -- 30 billion yen ($201.5 million) of 7 1/4% bonds due Aug. 15, 1995, priced at par via Nomura International Ltd.

Landeskreditbank Baden-Wuerttemberg (West Germany) -- 300 million marks ($182.8 million) of bonds due Aug. 21, 2000, priced at 100.10, via Trinkaus & Burkhardt. Coupon for the first two years is 9 1/2%, calculated under the German method, or 30 days per month divided by 360. Coupon for the final eight years is 16% less six-month Libor, based on the Euro method, or the actual number of days per month divided by 360.

Credit Local de France (France) -- one billion French francs ($181.7 million) of 10% bonds due Aug. 20, 1995, priced at 101.72 via Societe Generale.

General Electric Capital Corp. (U.S.) -- 150 million marks of bonds due Aug. 21, 1997, priced at 101 1/4, via Merrill Lynch AG. Coupon for the first two years is 9 3/4%, calculated under the German method. Coupon for the final five years is 15 3/4% less six-month Libor, based on the Euro method.

Bayerische Landesansalt fuer Aufbaufinanzierung (West Germany) -- 100 million marks of bonds at par due Aug. 21, 1998, via Trinkaus & Burkhardt. Coupon for the first two years is 9 1/2% calculated under the German method. Coupon for the final six years is 15 7/8% less six-month Libor, based on the Euro method.

General Electric Capital Corp. (U.S.) -- 125 million Swiss francs ($89 million) of 6 3/4% public bonds due Aug. 23, 1995, priced at 101.38 via Wirtschafts & Privatbank.

Nissan Capital of America Inc. (Japanese parent) -- 10 billion yen of bonds due Nov. 16, 1993, priced at 101 1/4 via Yamaichi International Ltd. Coupon will float 1/16 point above the three-month yen Libor for the first three months and thereafter will be fixed at 7 1/2%. Guaranteed by Nissan Motor Corp. U.S.

Skopbank (Finland) -- 10 billion yen of 7 1/2% bonds due Aug. 16, 1993, priced at 101 3/8 via Nippon Credit International Ltd.
