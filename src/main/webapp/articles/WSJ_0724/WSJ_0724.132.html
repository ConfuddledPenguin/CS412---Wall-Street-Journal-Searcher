Business Brief -- Southern Co.: Atlanta Utilities Operator's Net Rose 13% in 2nd Period

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/24/90

Fueled by a strong demand for energy in June, Southern Co.'s second-quarter earnings rose 13%, to $216 million, or 68 cents a share, breaking a string of three consecutive quarterly earnings declines. In the 1989 second quarter, the Atlanta-based energy utilities operator earned $187 million, or 60 cents a share. Revenue increased to $2.01 billion in the most recent quarter, from $1.88 billion.

The company warned that it may have to take a $190 million charge against future earnings, if the Georgia Court of Appeals declines to reconsider a ruling against a Southern Co. unit. In the 5-4 decision last week, the court refused to order the Georgia Public Service Commission to reconsider a $951 million disallowance of costs related to Georgia Power Co.'s Plant Vogtle nuclear power plant.

Three years ago, Southern took a $229 million after-tax write-off, after the total budget for the plant exceeded a self-imposed cap.
