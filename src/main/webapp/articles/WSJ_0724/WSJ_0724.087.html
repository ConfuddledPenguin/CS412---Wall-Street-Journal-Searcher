Marketing & Media: CNBC President Resigns To Be a Media Consultant

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/24/90

NEW YORK -- Michael Eskridge resigned as president of National Broadcasting Co.'s consumer and business cable channel, CNBC, to start a consulting company to manage media properties. Mr. Eskridge, 45, said he wasn't asked to leave his job and that his first client will be GE Capital, a unit of NBC's parent company, General Electric Co. Mr. Eskridge, whose new office will be in the same building as CNBC in Fort Lee, N.J., said it hasn't been determined yet whether his operation will be a subsidiary of GE Capital or an independent contractor.

"I had always talked about wanting to run broadcast properties on my own someday," said Mr. Eskridge, who had management experience in both radio and television in his nearly 20 years with NBC. "It just so happens that GE Capital came to NBC asking for help on some of the broadcast properties they had financed, and they were put in touch with me." He said he has already begun helping GE Capital and that he'll be working initially with half a dozen properties, mostly television stations.

Despite a perception in the industry that NBC has been disappointed with CNBC's ratings, subscriber base and ad sales, Mr. Eskridge said the channel has been a success and its future is bright. CNBC started in April 1989 with about 13 million subscribers and has about 17 million now. The goal is 30 million subscribers.
