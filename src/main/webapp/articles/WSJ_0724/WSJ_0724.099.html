Business Brief -- Stone Container Corp.: Board Amends Rights Plan To Lower Trigger Threshold

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/24/90

Stone Container Corp., Chicago, said its board amended its shareholder rights plan to reduce certain triggering thresholds to protect the company's stockholders in the event of an unwanted takeover. The pulp and paper concern said that as a result of the change, the acquisition by a person or group of 15% or more of its common stock will entitle the holders of rights to purchase a specified amount of the company's common at half price. Under the company's existing plan, adopted in July 1988, the threshold was triggered at 20%.

Also, the company removed an exception to this feature that would have prevented its operation in the event that a person or group crossing a 30% threshold became the owner of 85% or more of the common through a cash tender offer.

Stone Container said the amendments weren't taken in response to any effort to acquire the company.
