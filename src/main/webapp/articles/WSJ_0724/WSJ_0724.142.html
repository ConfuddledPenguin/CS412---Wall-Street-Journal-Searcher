International Brief -- Siemens AG: Group Net Increased 4.7% For First Fiscal 9 Months

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/24/90

Siemens AG, a West German electronics concern, said group net profit in the first nine months of its fiscal year ending Sept. 30, 1990, rose 4.7% to 1.14 billion marks ($694.7 million) from 1.09 billion marks in the year earlier period. The West German electronics concern said that group sales in the same nine month period climbed 2.9% to 45.3 billion marks from 44.0 billion marks.

Although Siemens didn't specify its third-quarter earnings, based on available data, group net profits fell 11% to 387 million marks from 433 million marks in the 1989 third quarter.

Third-quarter group sales were down slightly as well, easing 16% to 14.9 billion marks from 17.7 billion marks. But a spokesman for Siemens said that the main reason for the comparative weakness in the third quarter this year was due largely to the receipt of some 4 billion marks for an atomic energy plant in the 1989 third quarter.

Excluding the revenue generated from the power plant, sales rose 13% in the group and 19% domestically in the third quarter, Siemens said. The company said that although the sales data can be adjusted for the power plant, no adjusted earnings data would be made available.
