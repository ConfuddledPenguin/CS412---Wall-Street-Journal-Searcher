Bank of New England Ends Talks for Sale Of Maine Bank Unit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/24/90

BOSTON -- Bank of New England Corp. said negotiations to sell its Maine National Bank unit ended because potential bidders, who weren't identified, wouldn't agree to pay an "acceptable" price for the unit. Bank of New England said it will continue to own and manage the bank as part of its regional franchise. The sale had been planned as part of a series of asset sales by the troubled bank holding company as it tries to stem losses and reduce debt.

The bank's "recovery is proceeding," said Lawrence K. Fish, chairman and chief executive officer. "We're making slow but steady progress on a number of fronts, and we feel the conditions are such that it is best to maintain the Maine National Bank franchise."

Among those believed to have been interested in acquiring the Maine bank was Fleet/Norstar Financial Group Inc., Providence, R.I.

Bank of New England said in April that it would seek to sell its banking subsidiaries in Maine and Rhode Island.

Gerard Cassidy, an analyst with Tucker, Anthony, said the planned sale of Bank of New England's Maine National Bank unit was an "important but not critical" part of the parent's restructuring program. "The major thing for Bank of New England is to reduce nonperforming assets," he said. "Maine National is not bleeding to death, so {the parent} must have decided, `Why sell it at a fire sale price?'"
