Letters to the Editor: Hispanics Just Seek A Full, Undiluted Vote

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/24/90

Your June 13 editorial "The Liberal Barrio" characterizes the Los Angeles County redistricting lawsuit as simply a liberal/conservative tug of war. You suggest that the plaintiffs in Garza v. Los Angeles County and U.S.A. v. Los Angeles County are seeking new elections in constitutional, redrawn district boundaries to undermine the board's conservative majority. You are mistaken. The issues are preserving the fundamental right to vote and ensuring that election systems do not dilute Hispanic voting strength. Filed by the Mexican American Legal Defense and Educational Fund (MALDEF), the American Civil Liberties Union and the U.S. Justice Department during the Reagan administration, the lawsuits were tried concurrently.

On June 4, a federal judge ruled the supervisors had intentionally and illegally gerrymandered district boundaries to dilute the effectiveness of the Hispanic vote. When drawing the county's five districts, the incumbents dispersed Hispanics among several districts instead of leaving their cohesive neighborhoods intact. As a result, Hispanics have seen their votes canceled out by the votes of Anglos who, because of gerrymandering, are the majority in every district.

In more than 100 years, no Hispanic has been elected to the board, even though Hispanics have always been a significant portion of the county's population. Today, about 35% of all Angelenos are Hispanic. The powerful board, which administers law enforcement, fire protection and health services, controls a $10 billion budget and governs more than eight million people.

Even after the district boundaries are redrawn, most Hispanics in Los Angeles will never have the opportunity to serve on this board. What Hispanics should have and what the U.S. Constitution guarantees them is the opportunity to vote in legally drawn districts where their votes have the same value as everyone else's.

Jose Garza

Voting Rights Director

MALDEF

San Antonio, Texas
