Marketing Brief -- GenCorp Inc.: Company Sets Pact to Sell Its Last Broadcasting Station

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/24/90

GenCorp Inc. said it agreed to sell its last broadcasting station, radio station KFRC-AM in San Francisco. The Fairlawn, Ohio, company said it agreed with competing applicants for the broadcasting license on terms for a sale of the station to Daytona Group of California. Daytona, of Ormond Beach, Calif., agreed to pay about $8 million for the station.

Proceeds would be split between GenCorp and the competing applicants, but the terms of the division won't be disclosed until the transaction is approved by the Federal Communications Commission, GenCorp said.

RKO General Inc., a GenCorp unit, at one time was a major television and radio broadcasting concern. However, its right to operate the stations was challenged by various groups because of alleged improper political payments by the parent company and an alleged lack of candor in discussing those payments in FCC proceedings.

The company has been selling its stations and sharing the proceeds with other concerns that applied for licenses to operate the RKO stations.
