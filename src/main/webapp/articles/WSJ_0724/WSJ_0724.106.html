International: Settlement Averts Closing At Irish Press Newspapers

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 07/24/90

LONDON -- A last-minute, cost-cutting agreement reached with unions at Irish Press newspapers paves the way for the Ingersoll-managed group to restore the papers to a prime position in Ireland, Ingersoll said. The group, Irish Press Newspapers Ltd., is 50% owned by U.S. publisher Ralph Ingersoll's British company, Ingersoll Publications Ltd. The remainder of the company is owned by Irish Press PLC, which belongs to Ireland's de Valera family.

The group reached the agreement with the National Union of Journalists and the Irish Print Union on Sunday, just before it was to close down the Irish Press, Evening Press and Sunday Press.

The settlement calls for 200 voluntary layoffs, an immediate start to the use of new technology, and the introduction of a basic five-shift, 35-hour workweek throughout the company. Journalists have been working a four-day, 28-hour week. The 750 workers had rejected the same proposals on Thursday.

Ingersoll Publications, which bought a half-stake in the newspaper group in December, also manages the newspapers.
