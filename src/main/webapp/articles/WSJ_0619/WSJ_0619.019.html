May Plans to Sell 14% of Common Stock In Venture Store Unit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/19/90

ST. LOUIS -- May Department Stores Co. said its Venture discount store unit filed a registration statement with the Securities and Exchange Commission for an initial public offering of about 14% of Venture's common stock. May said the offering will help set a price for the remaining Venture shares, which it intends to spin off to its own shareholders after the public offering. May previously said it was spinning off the discount store unit to concentrate on its department store operations. Before the offering, May said Venture will pay it a dividend of $305 million.

Jeffrey Feiner, an analyst at Merrill Lynch, said the announcement "furthers the likelihood" that May will use the dividend to buy back its stock. In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, May closed at $55.75, down 75 cents.

The offering of 2.3 million shares, which Venture said it intends to sell for between $16 and $18 a share, also will help strengthen Venture's capitalization at a time when the discount-store industry is in flux. Ames Department Stores Inc., a big discount chain, is closing stores and reorganizing under Chapter 11 of the federal bankruptcy code.

Venture said in the filing that it intends to step up the pace of its new-store openings. It plans four stores in the current fiscal year, five next year and eight in each of the following two years.

The lead underwriter for the offering, which is expected to close in July, is Morgan Stanley & Co.
