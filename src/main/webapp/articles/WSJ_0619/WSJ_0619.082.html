GACC Agrees to Buy Subsidiary's Notes

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/19/90

CINCINNATI -- Great American Communications Co. said it agreed to acquire a majority of the 13% senior extendible reset notes issued by its subsidiary, GACC Holding Co. The company said it will pay a net cash price of $825 for each $1,000 principal amount of notes acquired. In privately negotiated transactions, Great American said it will acquire approximately $88.6 million principal amount of the $174 million of reset notes outstanding.

Great American also said it intends to make a tender offer in the next several days for the remaining $85.4 million principal amount of reset notes.

The company said the purchase is subject to completion of financing but said it has received loan commitments from a group of banks.

Shearson Lehman Hutton Inc. is advising Great American on the offer and will serve as its dealer manager.
