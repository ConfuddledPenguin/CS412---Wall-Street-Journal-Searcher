Fourth-Period Profit Is Posted by National Semiconductor Corp.

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/19/90

SANTA CLARA, Calif. -- After a worse-than-expected fiscal third-quarter loss, National Semiconductor Corp. said it returned to profitability in its fourth quarter on the strength of record quarterly revenue. For the period ended May 27, the company posted net income of $4.7 million, or two cents a share, on revenue of $456.3 million. In the year-earlier period, the semiconductor maker had net of $77.1 million, or 73 cents a share, on revenue of $419.1 million. Year-earlier net included a gain of $174.9 million on the sale of assets. For the year, National Semiconductor posted a loss of $25 million, or 34 cents a share, on record revenue of $1.68 billion. In its previous year, the company had a loss of $23.2 million, or 32 cents a share, on revenue of $1.65 billion. The previous year's net included a gain of $220 million on sale of assets.

National Semiconductor has been hit hard by slumping prices and margins for its memory chips and customized semiconductors, conditions that have plagued the industry in general. The company posted losses for the first and third quarters of the year just ended after a string of quarterly losses in fiscal 1989.

As for the current quarter, National Semiconductor said that although its first period is a "seasonally slow" time, the company was "encouraged" by record bookings in its fourth quarter. In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, National Semiconductor closed at $7.125, down 50 cents.
