Aerospace Supplier's Manager Is Sentenced In Parts Fraud Case

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/19/90

LOS ANGELES -- A manager for a now-defunct aerospace parts supplier, McHaffie Inc., was sentenced to 20 weekends in prison and three years' probation for his role in a scheme to falsify test results on millions of nuts and bolts that are supposed to hold together aircraft engines. Two co-workers, however, had their sentencing postponed. That's so U.S. District Judge Wallace Tashima would have more time to sort out who was responsible for starting the 10-year scam, which prosecutors described yesterday as the longest-running defense procurement fraud ever uncovered. All three men and the company pleaded guilty in April.

The man sentenced, 37-year-old William Whitham, formerly the shop-floor manager at Sylmar, Calif.-based McHaffie, was "the least culpable" of the defendants, according to Stephen Mansfield, the assistant U.S. attorney handling the case.

Sentencing for the company's president, Norman McHaffie, and quality-control supervisor James Hicks was delayed until July 2; each, Mr. Mansfield said, is trying to blame the other for initiating the crime.

Mr. McHaffie faces as long as 15 years in prison and a $750,000 fine, while Mr. Hicks could be sentenced to up to 10 years in prison and fined $500,000. The company faces a maximum penalty of $1.5 million.
