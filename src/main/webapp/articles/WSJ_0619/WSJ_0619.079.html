First Executive's Chief Officer Spars With House Panel

  • Author: Kevin G. Salwen
  • Date: 06/19/90

WASHINGTON -- First Executive Corp.'s chairman and chief executive officer, Fred Carr, sparred for three hours with a House panel in what apparently was an effort by lawmakers to get him on the record for future testimony by junk-bond king Michael Milken. Mr. Carr was grilled about his relationships with Mr. Milken, the former Drexel Burnham Lambert Inc. financier who this year pleaded guilty to six felony counts. Oversight and Investigations Subcommittee Chairman John Dingell (D., Mich) also asked Mr. Carr a series of specific questions about First Executive's relationship with convicted arbitrager Ivan Boesky and others.

Messrs. Milken and Boesky are likely to appear before the subcommittee later this year in its probe of the junk-bond industry.

But Mr. Carr, adding phrases such as "I'm not certain but . . ." or "I believe," parried the assaults and offered repeated arguments for junk bonds and First Executive's investment strategy. "I believe that in the long-term . . . our investment strategy will be proven to be sound," he said. First Executive holds billions of dollars of junk bonds and has aggressively built its business using the high-yield, high-risk securities.

The responses didn't appear to sit well with Rep. Dingell, who wanted specific answers to questions about First Executive's relationship to First Stratford Corp., a Delaware-based reinsurance company that is partly owned by Mr. Milken. First Executive owns 46% of First Stratford, as does a company controlled by Mr. Milken.

Mr. Carr told the panel that he remembered no conversation later than 1981 with Mr. Milken about reinsurance, which allows insurance companies to transfer risk to a third party. "Don't people wonder who they're getting in bed with?" Mr. Dingell asked, adding that First Stratford holders must be "remarkably trusting and happy people."

Then Mr. Dingell led Mr. Carr through a series of questions about Fischbach Corp., Stone Container Corp. and Harris Graphics Corp. All three have been linked to Mr. Boesky and Mr. Milken, and First Executive has invested in the three companies. Mr. Carr patiently answered the queries, contending in part that "I have never received any assurances or guarantees from Mr. Milken" on any of the deals.

Mr. Carr insisted that Mr. Milken was never involved in any investment decisions of First Executive. Instead, Mr. Carr explained that the company invested in junk bonds because of their higher returns that more than compensated for a higher default rate.

Rep. Ron Wyden (D., Ore.) said "there seems to be two worlds at First Executive -- one for {executives} and the other for policyholders." He cited a contract that Mr. Carr recently received that includes a provision that would pay the executive $6 million if he leaves because of a change of control within three years.
