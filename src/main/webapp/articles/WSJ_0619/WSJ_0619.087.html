IMF Says Namibia Seeks Membership

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/19/90

WASHINGTON -- The International Monetary Fund said Namibia has applied for membership in the IMF. The southwest African country, which is an important producer of diamonds, also has applied for membership in the World Bank, according to officials of that international agency.

The IMF and the World Bank, each of which currently has 152 member nations, also are considering requests for membership from Switzerland, Czechoslovakia and Bulgaria. The applications may be formally approved this year.

The IMF is expected to send a mission to Namibia soon to help determine what should be the size of its contribution to the IMF as a member.
