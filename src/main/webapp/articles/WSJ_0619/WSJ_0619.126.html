American Express to Aid Dance Theater of Harlem

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/19/90

NEW YORK -- American Express Co. and the Dance Theater of Harlem announced a four-year partnership to help the world-renowned but financially struggling dance company with a $1 million grant and card-holder promotions. The dance company and American Express Travel Related Services Co., a unit of American Express, said they plan to jointly market the company's performances to American Express gold card holders.

After the unexpected cancellation of some European engagements last fall, the company was forced to cancel its winter season here and lay off its dancers for six months to cope with a $1.7 million operating deficit. With the new support, the company plans to resume operations on Oct. 1, with a possible tour to Cairo this fall.

To aid the company's expensive touring effort, American Express said it will help find supporters for the Dance Theater wherever it goes. "To obtain local sponsors is very very important," an American Express spokesman said. "We will be able to do it for them on a global basis."

Last week DTH also received a $1 million emergency grant from the Lila Wallace-Reader's Digest Fund. The grant is in two parts, with $600,000 earmarked to reduce debts and $400,000 for dancer salaries and operations. Last year DTH's total payroll was $3.3 million for a staff of 140.
