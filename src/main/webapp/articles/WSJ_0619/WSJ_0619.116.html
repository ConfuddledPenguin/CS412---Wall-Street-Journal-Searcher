Business Brief -- Foxboro Co.: Rights Plan Is Strengthened In Wake of Takeover Bids

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/19/90

Foxboro Co. toughened its anti-takeover shareholder rights plan less than a week after the company disclosed receiving several takeover offers in response to its solicitation of possible buyers. The toughening of the plan raised speculation that Foxboro, a maker of industrial process controls, doesn't like one or more of the suitors or offers it has in hand. The company, based in Foxboro, Mass., hasn't named the suitors.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Foxboro shares fell $1.25 to close at $34.875.

Under the amended plan, holders of Foxboro common would get rights to purchase preferred shares at half the market value after a party acquires 15% of Foxboro's common or after a party deemed by Foxboro to be "adverse" acquires 10% of the shares. Previously, holders could exercise the rights when someone acquired 30% of Foxboro's shares.
