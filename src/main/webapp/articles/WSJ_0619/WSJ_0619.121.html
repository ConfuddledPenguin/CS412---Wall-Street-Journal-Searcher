International Brief -- Airbus Industrie: Turkish Airlines to Upgrade Fleet with Five A-340s

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/19/90

The Airbus Industrie consortium said Turkish Airlines ordered five Airbus A-340s and took options on five additional aircraft in a bid to modernize its fleet. The first aircraft will be delivered in spring 1993. Airbus doesn't disclose the value of contracts received, but the most recently available listed price for the A-340 is about $91 million.

Turkish Airlines also ordered CFM International engines, valued at as much as $300 million, to power the Airbus Industrie A-340 planes. CFM, based in Evendale, Ohio, is a joint venture of Snecma, the Paris aircraft-engine manufacturer, and General Electric Co., of Fairfield, Conn.

Airbus is a consortium of Aerospatiale of France, Messerschmitt-Boelkow-Blohm G.m.b.H. of West Germany, British Aerospace PLC and Construcciones Aeronauticas S.A. of Spain.

Airbus said Turkish Airlines will use the new planes on its nonstop service to the U.S. and Asia. The airline already operates 13 A-340s.
