U.S. Firm Agrees To Sulphur Project With Soviet Union

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/19/90

NEW ORLEANS -- Freeport-McMoRan Inc. said it reached agreement with two U.S.S.R. agencies to create a joint venture to construct and operate Soviet sulfur-exporting facilities on the Black Sea. Terms weren't disclosed, although a spokesman for the energy concern said the transaction "will have a substantial impact on our company."

The agreement reached between Freeport-McMoRan and the U.S.S.R. State Association Agrokhim and the U.S.S.R. State Concern Gazprom calls for the company to act, with certain exceptions, as the venture's sole marketing agent for sulfur exports from the Soviet Union. Freeport-McMoRan said it will arrange project financing for the venture, which would be owned 60% by the Soviets and 40% by the company.

Facilities to be constructed as part of the project include a sulfur granulation, transportation, storage and terminal system with an initial capacity of 1.5 million metric tons a year of liquid and solid sulfur.
