South Africa's Trade Surplus

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/19/90

JOHANNESBURG, South Africa -- South Africa's trade surplus widened to 1.08 billion rand ($404.9 million) in May from 333 million rand a year earlier and from 969 million rand in April, the government's Customs and Excise Office said. The figures aren't seasonally adjusted, but export and import data are adjusted to bring them in line with the requirements for compiling the nation's balance of payments.

May exports totaled 4.69 billion rand, compared with 4.46 billion rand a year earlier, and imports amounted to 3.6 billion rand, compared with 4.13 billion rand.

In the first five months of this year, exports amounted to 23.71 billion rand, compared with 21.93 billion rand a year earlier, and imports totaled 17.62 billion rand, compared with 17.8 billion rand.

The trade figures include South Africa's exports of gold bullion, the country's principal source of foreign exchange earnings. Imports include the country's purchases of oil and other petroleum products, despite the longstanding embargo of the Organization of Petroleum Exporting Countries.
