London's Stock Exchange May Cut Staff 10% in '90

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/19/90

LONDON -- London's stock exchange may cut its work force this year by a further 10%, or about 260 jobs, as part of a continuing effort to reduce costs and increase efficiency. Releasing its annual report, the exchange said staff totals have fallen to about 2,600 from 3,000 in recent years. "We have clearly set targets to reduce that number further over the coming year," the exchange said.

Peter Rawlins, chief executive officer, declined at a news conference to give specific targets, though he conceded an additional reduction "on the order" of 10% is conceivable. He said the exchange will try to achieve the cutbacks without layoffs.

Mr. Rawlins said the exchange ended the year with a #453,000 ($774,403) pretax profit after rebates to member firms. The small surplus "very much reflects the essentially break-even position to which we are aiming the finances of the exchange," he said. After taxes, the exchange swung to a #603,000 loss, compared with an #11.3 million surplus a year earlier.

Meanwhile, the exchange's chairman, Andrew Hugh Smith, said he plans to meet with counterparts from other European Community exchanges in Brussels on July 3 to discuss recent efforts toward a common informationsharing and stock trading system for Europe. These efforts have prompted acrimony among various EC member states, but officials said they are continuing to try to achieve a compromise to move their unity campaign forward.
