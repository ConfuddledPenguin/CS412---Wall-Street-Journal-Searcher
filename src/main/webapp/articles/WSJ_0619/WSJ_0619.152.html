Oil Barge Runs Aground Off Coast Of Massachusetts

  • Author: Allanna Sullivan
  • Date: 06/19/90

A 465-foot barge carrying more than five million gallons of heating oil ran aground near a Massachusetts tourist haven, the second oil spill in that area in less than a week. Four of the barges's fuel tanks, with a total capacity of 1.2 million gallons, ruptured when the vessel foundered in Buzzards Bay, Coast Guard officials said. But by late yesterday only a minuscule amount of oil had leaked from the vessel, they added. Because the damaged tanks were only partly full, seawater, which is heavier than oil, rushed in and forced the oil away from the ruptures, the officials said.

"There is no significant discharge yet," said Petty Officer Elizabeth Brannan.

Don Tuchman, an official with Mobil Corp., owner of the oil cargo being carried by the barge, said: "Coast Guard officials inform us they are optimistic that the oil spilled can be easily cleaned up with no damage to beaches or sea life."

According to the Coast Guard, the barge's owner and operator, Bouchard Transportation Co., has agreed to pay for any cleanup. Within hours of the grounding cleanup contractor Clean Harbor Inc. had put booms into the water downwind of the barge. Other vessels were on their way to unload the oil.

The Coast Guard said it doesn't yet know the cause of the spill, but one official said poor visibility because of fog appeared to play a major role.

In the past two weeks, there have been two oil spills in New York Harbor and a fire aboard a tanker spilled oil into the Gulf of Mexico. Last week, the cruise ship Bermuda Star ran aground in Buzzards Bay, only two miles from yesterday's grounding, spilling about 7,500 gallons of residual fuel oil.
