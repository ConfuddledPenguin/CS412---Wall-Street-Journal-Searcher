Who's News: Nike Names Donahue As President, Chief Operating Officer

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/19/90

BEAVERTON, Ore. -- Richard K. Donahue, a director, was named president and chief operating officer of Nike Inc., assuming duties previously discharged by Philip H. Knight. The 52-year-old Mr. Knight remains chairman and chief executive officer of the maker of athletic wear. He said he will remain "as involved in Nike's operations" as he currently is, while counting on Mr. Donahue to help manage Nike's continuing growth.

"This is not a change in Nike's management structure but a supplement to it," Mr. Knight said. "We still have an extremely strong core of officers who have brought the company to where it is today. They will continue to do what they have been doing, and so will I."

Mr. Donahue, 62, is a trial lawyer and partner in Donahue & Donahue, a Lowell, Mass., law firm. He served as White House congressional liaison in the Kennedy administration. A Nike director since 1978, he has been credited with helping direct the company's international expansion over the past three years.

Mr. Donahue's appointment marks the second time Mr. Knight has decided to share some of his duties. In 1983, he appointed Robert Woodell, a vice president of world-wide marketing, to the posts of president and chief operating officer. But Mr. Woodell relinquished the posts to Mr. Knight a year later; the company, at the time, cited a clash of management styles.

Mr. Donahue, whatever else his talents, appears to have a sense of humor. He is a father of 11 children and said he couldn't turn down an opportunity to help run a company associated with fitness and whose athletic shoes are promoted by basketball superstar Michael Jordan.

"I have worked everywhere from the White House down," Mr. Donahue said, but "this is the only {job} that will actually impress my kids."
