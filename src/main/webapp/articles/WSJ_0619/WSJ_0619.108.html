Arthur Andersen Forms Venture With Soviets Firms

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/19/90

NEW YORK -- Arthur Andersen & Co. formed a joint venture with two Soviet companies, allowing the accounting and professional-services firm to audit balance sheets of Soviet concerns and of ventures between Western and U.S.S.R. companies. The venture will be owned 70% by Arthur Andersen, 25% by Promstroybank of the U.S.S.R., and 5% by an engineering company, NPO Dinamo Moscow. Arthur Andersen said it is the first of the major international accounting and consulting firms to be registered to do audits in the Soviet Union.

The move follows by a few days the announcement by KPMG Peat Marwick, also a major accounting and consulting firm, that it expects to have the first Chinese joint accounting venture approved by the Beijing government.

Arthur Andersen's venture will be "servicing international companies going into the Soviet Union and helping current Soviet enterprises structure and restructure to get ready for the global environment," said Paul W. Hoffman, a partner in Arthur Andersen's office here.

The venture marks Arthur Andersen's return to the Soviet Union. "We were there from the first days of detente in the early '70s, when we were expecting more U.S. investment," Mr. Hoffman said. But "things got very quiet in the late '70s," particularly after the Soviets invaded Afghanistan, and the firm left in 1983, Mr. Hoffman said.
