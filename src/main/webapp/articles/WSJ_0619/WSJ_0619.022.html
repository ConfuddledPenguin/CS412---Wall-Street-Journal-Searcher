Sun Oil Leaves Sudan

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/19/90

KHARTOUM, Sudan -- The U.S.-based oil exploration company Sun Oil Cos. has pulled out of Sudan, but other international companies want to obtain its concessions, the government said. The official Sudan News Agency quoted an official of the Energy and Mining Ministry that the Sun Co. unit ceased its activities in Sudan because of financial difficulties.

Sun Oil's exploration activities were in parts of Sudan outside the southern civil war zone, where such operations were suspended after hostilities broke out in 1983.

Abdel-Moneim Khogali, energy and mines minister, told SNA that Sun Oil began tapering off in December, paring its concession areas by 60%.

Sun Oil's pullout leaves Sudan's oil prospects in the hands of U.S.-based Chevron Corp. and France's Total-Compagnie Francaise des Petroles.
