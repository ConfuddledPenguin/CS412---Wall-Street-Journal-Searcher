British Unit Labor Costs Rise

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/19/90

LONDON -- Wages and salaries per unit of output in the manufacturing industry in April rose a seasonally adjusted 0.7% from March and increased 7.6% from a year earlier, Britain's Central Statistical Office said. The index, which is based on the year 1985 equalling 100, was 121.3 in April and 120.4 in March; year-earlier figures have been revised and aren't comparable.

For the three months through April, the index of unit labor costs rose 8.1% to 120.8 from a year earlier, compared with an 8.1% annual rise to 120 in the three months through March.
