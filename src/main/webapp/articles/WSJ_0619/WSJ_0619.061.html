REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: Settling S&L Accounts

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/19/90

Freshman Congressman Peter Smith of Vermont is outraged that there has been no real investigation of the role government officials played in creating the S&L scandal. So he introduced a resolution calling for an independent counsel to look into the matter. Congress has tried to exempt itself from the independent-counsel law, but Rep. Smith says it must be included. "No sitting Member or former Member should be able to escape scrutiny," he told us. Feeling the heat from outraged constituents, more than 150 House Members have signed on to Mr. Smith's idea. We're hardly among the independent-counsel law's supporters, but it would almost be worth the price of admission to see one of these locked-on-target prosecutors go cruising through Congress's participation in the S&L scandal.
