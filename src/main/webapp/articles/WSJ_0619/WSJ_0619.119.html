Banks Agree To Contribute To Trump Loan

  • Author: Neil Barsky
  • Date: 06/19/90

Three New Jersey banks have tentatively agreed to contribute about $15 million toward a $60 million loan guarantee to Donald Trump, people involved in the negotiations said. The agreement, reached in talks yesterday, would remove one major obstacle to the loan package agreed to by Mr. Trump's major creditors last week. The New Jersey banks are said to be divisions of First Fidelity Bancorp, National Westminster Bank and Midlantic National Bank. The banks couldn't immediately be reached for comment.

Even with the New Jersey banks aboard, the developer's loan package still faces obstacles in winning banks' approval: Neither Chase Manhattan Corp. nor Citicorp are believed to have persuaded other creditors who have purchased portions of their loans to go along with the plan.

Mr. Trump has been in intense discussions with his major creditors for about three weeks after evidence mounted that the cash flow from his properties would not be enough to cover interest on about $2 billion in bank loans in the coming year.

He missed $73 million in payments to Trump's Castle bondholders and to Manufacturers Hanover Corp. If Mr. Trump's bondholders are not paid by 12:01 a.m. EDT, June 27, they will have the right to foreclose on the casino, which could trigger defaults on many of Mr. Trump's other properties.

Under the tentative agreement reached with Mr. Trump's creditors, the banks would suspend interest payments and give Mr. Trump a little more than $60 million. In exchange, Mr. Trump would put up the bulk of his empire as collateral and agree to an orderly sale of many of his assets.

The banks are, in effect, betting that the $60 million will be enough to tide Mr. Trump over, and allow him to avoid selling his trophies at fire-sale prices. Mr. Trump owns three Atlantic City casinos, the Trump Shuttle, the Plaza Hotel in Manhattan, and various other real estate properties.

Mr. Trump's four major New York creditors are units of Bankers Trust Corp., Chase Manhattan Corp., Citicorp and Manufacturers Hanover Corp.
