GTE Spacenet Joins Team for Mexico, U.S. Telecommunications

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/19/90

MCLEAN, Va. -- GTE Spacenet and a Mexican telecommunications firm agreed to jointly provide data, voice and broadcast communications services via satellite between the U.S. and Mexico. The services are separate from those provided by International Telecommunications Satellite Organization, or Intelsat, a satellite-services provider owned by about 119 countries, excluding the Soviet Union and its allies.

GTE Spacenet said, however, that Intelsat has approved this new agreement, signed with Telecomunicaciones de Mexico. GTE Spacenet is a unit of GTE Corp., the Stamford, Conn., telecommunications, lighting and precision-materials company.

The operation with the Mexican firm will allow a company with a private communications network to extend the network into Mexico, allowing the company to send messages directly from office to office, said Raymond Marks, GTE Spacenet's vice president of marketing and business development. In contrast, Intelsat transmits a company's messages to a Mexican Intelsat station, which then transmits the message to the company's local office.

Additionally, Mr. Marks said that Intelsat hasn't been able to provide uniform levels of service for all regions of both countries.

The new service doesn't directly compete with Intelsat, which largely routes public transmissions, such as consumer phone calls and network TV.
