Cold War Missile Exhibit Is Slated for Smithsonian

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/19/90

WASHINGTON -- Museum curators have opened a new front in the Cold War. An exhibit coming to the Smithsonian's Air and Space Museum this week will feature a U.S. Pershing II and a Soviet SS-20 missile side-by-side. Deployment of the once nuclear-tipped missiles generated a great deal of tension in the 1970s and 1980s. Now they are part of a touring exhibit that will later open at the Central Armed Forces Museum in Moscow under the banner "Do Veri, No Pro Veri," which means "Trust, but Verify." (Military teams from both sides are busy verifying the destruction of 888 of the missiles under the terms of the 1987 Intermediate Nuclear Forces treaty.)

Then there is Checkpoint Charlie, the gate in the Berlin Wall that was the backdrop for much of the Cold War drama in the 1960s. It is heading for a West German museum, as plans are under way to make much of the rest of the wall a mere memory.
