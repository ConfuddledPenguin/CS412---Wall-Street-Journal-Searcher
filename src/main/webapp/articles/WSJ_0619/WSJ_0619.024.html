New Securities Issues

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/19/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: MUNICIPALS

Clark County, Nev. -- $100 million of industrial development revenue bonds, Series 1990, Nevada Power Co. Project, June 1, 2020, were tentatively priced by a Goldman, Sachs & Co. group to yield 7.64% in 2020 with a coupon of 7.60%. The bonds are insured and rated triple-A by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and Standard & Poor's Corp. Interest on the bonds will be treated as a preference item in calculating the federal alternative minimum tax that may be imposed on certain investors.

Columbus, Ohio -- $96.7 million of general obligation bond anticipation notes, due July 2, 1991. The issue, which consisted of five note issues in three categories, is rated MIG-1 by Moody's and Sp1-Plus by S&P and carries an underlying long-term rating of double-A-1 from Moody's. A PaineWebber Inc. group won $49.4 million of notes in Categories 2 and 3. The Category 2 notes consist of $33.5 million of limited tax sewerage system notes, $9.4 million of various purpose limited tax notes and $1 million of refuse-coal fired power plant notes. They are priced for reoffering with a coupon of 6.25% to yield 5.85%. The Category 3 notes consist of $5.5 million of 6.25% limited tax private activity municipal airport notes, priced for reoffering to yield 6%. The Category 1 notes were won by a Bear, Stearns & Co. group. Category 1 consists of $47.3 million of 6 1/4% various purpose unlimited tax notes priced for reoffering to yield 5.85%.

MORTGAGE

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $500 million of real estate mortgage investment conduit securities is being offered in 18 classes by Bear Stearns. The offering, Series 1990-82, is backed by Fannie Mae 9 1/2% securities with a weighted average coupon rate of 10.125% and weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29.42 years. Details on prices and yields weren't immediately available.

EUROBONDS

Halifax Building Society (United Kingdom) -- #350 million ($600.1 million) of floating-rate notes due July 1995, priced at par, via Credit Suisse First Boston Ltd. Reoffered at fixed price of 99.92. Coupon to float at 0.0625 over three-month London interbank offered rate. Callable from July 1993 at par.

Ente Ferrovie dello Stato (Italy) -- $500 million of 9 1/8% fungible bonds due July 6, 2009, via Morgan Stanley International Ltd. Reoffered at fixed price of 99.60. Guaranteed by Republic of Italy. Fungible on exchange date with existing $500 million issue. Puttable at par on July 6, 1999.

City of Kobe (Japan) -- $150 million of 9 1/4% bonds due Aug. 2, 2000, priced at 101.70, to yield 9.30% less fees, via Bank of Tokyo Capital Markets Ltd. Fees 2.

Reed Publishing USA Inc. (U.K. parent) -- $150 million of 9 5/8% bonds due July 9, 1997, priced at 101.025, via Credit Suisse First Boston. Reoffered at 99.525. Event risk par put option. Guaranteed by Reed International PLC.

Banque Indosuez (France) -- 200 million marks ($119.3 million) of subordinated floating-rate notes at 0.35 over six-month Libor, priced at par, and due July 6, 2000, via Trinkaus & Burkhardt. First Libor fixing July 18.

Intalstat International Luxembourg (Italian parent) -- 180 million marks of floating-rate notes at 0.125 over six-month Libor, priced at par, and due July 25, 2000, via Bayerische Vereinsbank. First Libor fixing July 23. Guaranteed by Autostrade Rome. Borrower's option to switch to 10% fixed-rate coupon on July 25, 1995.

J.P. Morgan & Co. (U.S.) -- 125 million Canadian dollars (US$106.4 million) of 13% bonds due July 9, 1993, priced at 101 7/8, to yield 12.79% less fees, via Credit Suisse First Boston. Fees 1 3/8.

Bancal Tri-State Corp. (U.S.) -- $65 million of 8 7/8% bonds due June 29, 1993, priced at 101 3/8, to yield 8 7/8%, via Mitsubishi Finance International Ltd.

WestLB Finance Curacao N.V. (West German parent) -- 100 million marks of 11 3/4% dual-currency bonds due June 29, 1993, via Westdeutsche Landesbank Girozentrale. Issue price undisclosed. Guaranteed by parent Westdeutsche Landesbank Girozentrale. First two interest payments in marks. Third interest payment and principal paid in marks or Canadian dollars. Exchange rate set at 1.40 marks per Canadian dollar.

Japan Development Bank (Japan) -- 200 million European currency units of 10 1/2% bonds due July 27, 1995, priced at 101.80, to yield 10.52% after fees via Credit Lyonnais Securities Ltd. Fees 1 7/8.
