Business Brief -- Great Western Financial Corp.: S&L Firm to Buy Deposits From Carteret Savings Bank

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/19/90

Great Western Financial Corp. said it agreed to acquire $700 million in Florida retail deposits from Carteret Savings Bank, Morristown, N.J. Great Western will pay $26.5 million, or about 3.7 cents on the dollar, for the deposits. The price includes the purchase of certain Carteret branch offices in Palm Beach and Broward counties, Fla.

Great Western, a Beverly Hills, Calif., savings and loan concern, said the acquisition will increase its Florida deposit base by 40% and substantially boost its presence in Palm Beach County, one of the fastest-growing areas of the nation.

Carteret, a unit of AmBase Corp., said the sale is a "key element" in its plan to streamline its retail banking and concentrate on New Jersey markets.

The proposed acquisition is considerably smaller than an earlier Great Western plan to acquire about $2.4 billion in deposits of CenTrust Bank, another New Jersey concern with substantial Florida holdings. That deal, which called for a cash payment of $150 million, or six cents for each dollar on deposit, collapsed when CenTrust was seized by federal regulators after running up huge junk-bond losses.
