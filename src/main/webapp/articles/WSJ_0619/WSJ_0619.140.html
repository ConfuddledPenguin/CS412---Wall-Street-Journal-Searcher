International Brief -- Minolta Camera Co.: Company and Nikon Corp. Post Sharp Earnings Gains

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/19/90

Japan's two leading camera makers, Minolta Camera Co. and Nikon Corp., reported strong consolidated earnings gains for the fiscal year ended March 31. Minolta said its pretax earnings soared 360% to 17.94 billion yen ($116.6 million) from 3.89 billion yen in the previous fiscal year. Net profit grew more slowly but still briskly, climbing 89% to 3.79 billion yen, or 13.58 yen a share, from 2 billion yen, or 7.36 yen a share. Sales increased 14% to 347.43 billion yen from 306.07 billion. Minolta officials traced the strong earnings performance in part to the yen's decline and the fact that the company's U.S. unit moved into the black during the year.

Nikon's pretax earnings went up 33% to 31.62 billion yen from 23.84 billion. Net profit jumped 63% to 18.76 billion yen, or 51.13 yen a share, from 11.51 billion yen, or 31.68 yen a share.

Sales amounted to 284.42 billion yen, showing a 12% rise from 254.30 billion a year ago.
