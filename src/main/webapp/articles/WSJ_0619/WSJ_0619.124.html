Gradco Systems Posts Loss of $28.7 Million For Fiscal 4th Period

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/19/90

IRVINE, Calif. -- Gradco Systems Inc. reported a $28.7 million loss for its fourth quarter, reflecting one-time write-downs related mostly to the company's closure of U.S. manufacturing facilities and the loss of Xerox Corp. as a major customer. The maker of paper-handling equipment for copiers and printing machinery said the latest period's loss amounted to $3.79 a share. A year earlier, Gradco had profit of $1.8 million, or 27 cents a share.

Revenue plunged 36% in the latest period to $18.5 million from $28.9 million.

Separately, Gradco said that, as a result of a newly completed private sale of a 27% stake in the company's Japanese subsidiary, Gradco will record a one-time gain of $18 million, or $2.35 a share, for its first quarter, ending June 30.

Proceeds from the sale will be used mostly to retire parent company debt and to provide working capital for the subsidiary. The sale was made to a group of Japanese institutional investors and is intended to position the subsidiary for a public stock sale in Japan, Gradco said.

Gradco's announcement was made after the close of trading. The stock ended the day yesterday at $11 a share, up 50 cents, in national over-the-counter trading.

The company said the revenue decline and much of the loss on operations was due to the withdrawal of Xerox last year as the company's largest customer. Officials said that last year Xerox began production of a line of sorting equipment for Xerox copiers.

Gradco said that on Friday it filed litigation in federal court in Los Angeles alleging that Xerox is infringing on Gradco patents. The suit asks damages and seeks an injunction barring production.

Company officials said Gradco's sales to Xerox fell to $6 million in the year ended March 31 from $27 million in fiscal 1989.

Gradco said its loss for fiscal 1990 totaled $28.1 million, or $4.02 a share, including $23 million in one-time charges. It had net income of $5.6 million, or 88 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue slid 22% to $89.5 million in the latest year from $115.1 million.
