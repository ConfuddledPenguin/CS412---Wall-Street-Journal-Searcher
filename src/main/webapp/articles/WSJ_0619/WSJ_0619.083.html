AMSOUTH Bancorp. -- Moody's Investors Service Inc. said it lowered its debt ratings on this banking company. The rating agency also said it downgraded its rating of AmSouth Bank -- the Birmingham, Ala., company's lead bank -- for long-term deposits. The changes affect about $123 million in long-term debt. The rating service said the changes reflect uncertainty about the performance of AmSouth's commercial real estate portfolio. Moody's said, however, that the performance, asset quality and capital support of the company's core businesses should continue to support AmSouth's dominant position in Alabama. The following ratings were lowered: AmSouth Bancorp. floating-rate notes to single-A-2 from single-A-1; subordinated capital notes to single-A-3 from single-A-2. The Prime-1 rating of the company for commercial paper was unchanged. AmSouth Bank long-term deposits to double-A-3 from double-A-2. The Prime-1 rating of the bank for short-term deposits was unchanged. ---

SALANT Corp. -- Standard & Poor's Corp. said it lowered its rating on $75 million of this apparel company's senior subordinated notes to D from triple-C-plus. The rating concern cited Salant's obligation to suspend interest payments on the public debt until the company cures a default on $10.8 million of bank debt. Last week, Salant, which emerged from bankruptcy court three years ago, said it may again make a Chapter 11 filing, among other options; under Chapter 11, a company operates with protection from creditors' lawsuits while it tries to work out a plan to pay or settle debts. Salant, which owns Manhattan Industries Inc. and Denton Mills Inc., last week said that, in addition to defaulting on the payment of interest and principal, it had failed to comply with certain covenants in its credit agreements with its bank lenders. And Salant said it wasn't able to make an interest payment of $1.9 million on a different issue of senior subordinated notes. That issue isn't rated, an S&P spokeswoman said.

SOUTHTRUST Corp. (Birmingham, Ala.) -- Moody's Investors Service Inc. said it lowered its long-term debt ratings for this bank holding company and the rating for commercial paper. Moody's also said the rating for long-term deposits of SouthTrust Bank of Alabama, a subsidiary, was downgraded. The changes affect about $105 million of long-term debt. The rating service said the moves reflect the potential for deterioration in SouthTrust's commercial real estate portfolio. Moody's noted, however, that SouthTrust remains profitable and well-capitalized. The following ratings are affected: For SouthTrust Corp. -- senior debentures, to single-A-3 from single-A-1; subordinated capital notes, to Baa-1 from single-A-2; and commercial paper, to Prime-2 from Prime-1. For SouthTrust Bank of Alabama -- long-term deposits to single-A-1 from double-A-3. The Prime-1 rating of the bank for short-term obligations is unchanged.

UNITED MERCHANTS & MANUFACTURERS Inc. (New York) -- Standard & Poor's Corp. said it downgraded its rating on this apparel and jewelry concern's subordinated debt to D from double-C. The company missed an interest payment due last Friday on its 13 3/4% senior subordinated notes, due 2000; the debt outstanding totals $116 million. The company is seeking to restructure its debt. Barring operating improvements and a meaningful debt restructuring, United Merchants' long-term viability is questionable, S&P, a rating concern, said.
