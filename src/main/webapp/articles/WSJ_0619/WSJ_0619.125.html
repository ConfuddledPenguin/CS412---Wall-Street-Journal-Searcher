Porsche Raises Prices On Its 1991 Models

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/19/90

RENO, Nev. -- Porsche Cars North America Inc. said it is raising the prices of its 1991 cars, following a recent rebound in sales. The price increase will be the company's first in two years. The price of the least expensive Porsche will rise 3.5%, to $43,350 from $41,900 a year ago. The most expensive model, the new 911 Turbo, will sell for $95,000, compared with $74,545 for this year's top-of-the-line Porsche models.

The move by the U.S. unit of Porsche Enterprises Inc. comes as its sales have rebounded after dropping 69% between 1986 and 1989. Sales of Porsche's luxury sports cars are up 19% for the first five months of this year.

Separately, the U.S. sales arm of Bayerische Motoren Werke AG said it will begin selling its entry-level 318is model later this month at a base price of $21,500. BMW said the car would be targeted at the growing near-luxury market.

BMW is trying to break a slump that saw its U.S. sales fall from a 1986 peak of 96,759 cars to 64,881 in 1989. BMW sales for the first five months of 1990 were down 10.2%.

"{The 318is} will certainly bring us buyers who weren't in our market range, but it's not in direct response to competition," said a BMW spokesman. He said the auto maker expects to sell 7,000 of the model this year.
