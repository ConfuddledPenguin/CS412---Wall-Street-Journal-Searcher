Credit Ratings: Western Union Debt Rated Lower by S&P

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/19/90

NEW YORK -- Standard & Poor's Corp. said it lowered its ratings on nearly $900 million outstanding of Western Union Corp.'s senior secured notes, senior unsecured and subordinated debt and preferred stock. The credit-rating concern downgraded Western Union's senior secured notes, senior unsecured and subordinated debt, and preferred stock to single-D from, respectively, triple-C-plus, triple-C-minus and single-C. The action affects about $642 million of debt and $254 million liquidation value of preferred.

Western Union, a telecommunications and financial services company, failed to make interest payments Friday on $500 million of 19 1/4% senior secured extendible reset notes due 1992 and senior unsecured 16% notes due 1991.

The company is seeking to restructure its debt, saying it can't meet debt payments from current cash flow. S&P said a restructuring would greatly reduce cash debt service and eliminate accrued dividend arrearages on the preferred. Western Union said it hopes to complete such a restucturing by mid-July.
