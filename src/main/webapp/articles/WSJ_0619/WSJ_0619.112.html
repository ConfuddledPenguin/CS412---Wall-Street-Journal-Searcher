Private-Placement Trading System Launched by NASD

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/19/90

NEW YORK -- The National Association of Securities Dealers launched its automated system for trading securities in the private-placement market. Securities firms and other institutions have been waiting for the NASD to hook up the system, known as Portal, ever since regulators in April passed rules making it easier to sell and trade privately placed securities.

Portal, short for Private Offerings, Resales and Trading through Automated Linkages, will be able to handle any bond or stock traded under the new regulations, the NASD said. The system automates trading in the estimated $200 billion private-placement market by allowing use of personal computers and Nasdaq terminals to trade. Nasdaq is the NASD's automated quotations sytem.

Twenty major U.S. and European securities firms, accounting for about 80% of the activity in privately placed stocks and bonds, have signed up for the new trading system, the NASD said. Four financial institutions, representing pension and investment funds world-wide, also have been approved as qualified buyers, it said. And 60 more institutions are expected to join soon, according to the NASD.

The Securities and Exchange Commission in April unanimously approved Rule 144a, allowing large market players to trade among themselves in the private-placement market without going back to the registration and disclosure process. Only institutions that own $100 million or more in securities can buy private placements under the rule.
