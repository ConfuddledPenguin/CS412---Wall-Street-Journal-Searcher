What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/19/90

THE U.S. PENSION-INSURANCE agency can force LTV Corp. to reassume liability for more than $2 billion in pension benefits that the company stopped funding in 1987, the Supreme Court ruled. The decision could save the troubled agency billions of dollars and hinder LTV's exit from Chapter 11 proceedings. ---

Stock prices plunged in thin trading amid skepticism about corporate earnings and interest rates. The Dow Jones Industrial Average dropped 53.71 points to close at 2882.18. Bond prices also fell on further fallout from some bad economic news. The dollar sagged against most other currencies.

---

The SEC received its third court setback in recent weeks as the Supreme Court let stand a ruling that the CFTC, and not the SEC, has the authority to regulate stock "baskets." The CFTC repeated its offer to expedite approval of the products.

---

Motorola is being ordered to stop selling its best-selling microchip in a patent dispute with Hitachi. The move could hurt Apple and other U.S. computer firms, as well as Motorola.

---

An exception in patent law for drugs also applies to medical devices, the Supreme Court ruled. The decision is a defeat for medical-technology developers with long-held patents and a victory for newer companies.

---

Donald Trump moved closer to an accord with lenders under which he would borrow an additional $60 million and suspend interest payments on other loans. Three New Jersey banks tentatively agreed to participate.

The Trump Shuttle plans to scrap more than 25% of its Saturday flights plus four Sunday round trips.

---

Occidental Petroleum's debt and preferred stock were downgraded by S&P, which cited the company's high fixed costs, heavy dividend demands and expected weak earnings.

---

NWA is expected to name Frederick Rentschler to be the Northwest Air parent's chief executive. Rentschler is currently chief at Beatrice, which is merging with ConAgra.

---

Export licenses will no longer be required for shipments of most high-technology goods to U.S. allies, the Commerce Department announced.

---

Chrysler is holding "intensified" talks with Fiat about a potential alliance in which the Italian firm may help Chrysler develop new models.

---

Prudential-Bache agreed to sell its Canadian retail brokerage business to Burns Fry. The U.S. securities firm intends to shift most of its other Canadian operations to New York.

---

CMS Energy's 49%-owned cogeneration venture entered a $2.3 billion sale-leaseback accord involving its Midland, Mich., power plant. CMS also formed a new partnership to possibly expand the facility.

---

Bankers Trust laid off 75 professionals in its flagship bank's corporate finance department, reflecting the slump in mergers and acquisitions.

---

Eight companies were sued by the federal government for allegedly polluting waters off Southern California.

---

First Boston replaced its junk-bond chief and reassigned other aides amid a deep slump in its bond business.

---

Corn and soybean futures prices rose in the wake of heavy rains over the weekend in the Midwest.

---

Henry Ansbacher's controlling shareholders plan to sell their 61.6% stake in the British merchant bank.

---
