Integrated Resources Agrees to Sell Unit To Sterik Affiliate

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/19/90

NEW YORK -- Integrated Resources Inc. said it agreed to sell a unit that is the general partner of American Real Estate Partners L.P. to a Sterik Co. affiliate for $40 million. The general partner, American Property Investors, owns more than 10% of American Real Estate Partners, a publicly traded master limited partnership. AREP manages a portfolio of more than 320 real estate properties. Sterik, based in Auburndale, Mass., and its affiliates own more than 180 properties with a fair market value of about $500 million. Meshulam Riklis, a financier whose Rapid American Corp. has interests in a variety of companies, is one of Sterik's general partners.

The sale by Integrated, which is operating under Chapter 11 protection from creditors, is subject to Bankruptcy Court approval. It is also subject to higher and better offers.

But the agreement to sell American Property Investors may be complicated by a lawsuit filed last month by investors in AREP seeking to prevent any sale of the partnership without their approval.

The suit by Oppenheimer & Bigelow Income Fund I and Edge Partners L.P., which hold a total of about 107,000 units in AREP, is pending in Delaware state court. It also seeks a unit-holder list from Integrated as a possible step in converting the partnership to a real estate investment trust, as well as access to the books and records of the partnership.

Curtis Trinko, an attorney for the plaintiffs, said they intend to "move forward to obtain court relief to block" the sale to Sterik. Mr. Trinko said the investors are considering asking the bankruptcy court to allow some kind of vote on the sale by the AREP limited partners.

But Stephen D. Weinroth, Integrated chairman, said he had been advised that the partnership agreements don't require investor approval for the sale of the general partner. He said he didn't think the suit would delay the sale.

Joseph Dempsey Jr., managing general partner of Sterik, said the company would go ahead with the purchase "regardless of the suit." Sterik has financing for the purchase.
