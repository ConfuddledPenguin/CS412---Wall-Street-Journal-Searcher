Business Brief -- Mitsubishi Corp.: Car Venture With Chrysler In Canada Being Weighed

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/19/90

Mitsubishi Corp. said it is considering setting up a joint-venture company with Chrysler Corp. in Ontario to produce medium-sized passenger cars, but the Japanese trading company emphasized that no decisions have been made. A Mitsubishi spokesman said the proposed venture, which has been under discussion for the past year, would be in charge of the stamping plants for production of LH cars, a series of models that Chrysler, based in Highland Park, Mich., plans to start producing in 1992. Success with the models is important for Chrysler, which is seeking to reverse its slipping competitiveness in the North American market. Though Chrysler has long-established ties in the U.S. with Mitsubishi Motors Co., the auto maker in the Mitsubishi group, the Canadian venture would be pursued separately, the spokesman said.

Nihon Keizai Shimbun, Japan's largest economic newspaper, reported that the new company will aim at distributing the cars in North America, and that total investment in it would reach $105 million.
