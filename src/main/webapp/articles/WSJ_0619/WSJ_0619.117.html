Technology: IBM Unveils an Advance For Note-Pad Computers

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/19/90

ARMONK N.Y. -- International Business Machines Corp. said it developed a method that allows computers to recognize overlapping handwritten letters, a step on the road toward computers that understand script handwriting. Such machines, dubbed note-pad computers, are being touted as the next big thing in computing, because they would allow people who are intimidated by technology to write with a penlike instrument on a computer screen, which can be an easier way to communicate than typing strings of complicated commands.

In addition, lightweight, portable note-pad computers would open up new applications -- inventory-taking, for example, or use by salespeople, secretaries and journalists.

Several note-pad computers are already being marketed, most notably by Tandy Corp.'s Grid Systems unit. But these early stabs at handwriting recognition require users to print clearly and to leave adequate spacing between letters. IBM said its advance, which it presented at an industry conference in Atlantic City, N.J., points the way toward eliminating these restrictions.

"This is another nice step in a continual progression to the point where a computer can recognize whatever you slap down on its screen," said Vern Raburn, chairman of Slate Corp., a Scottsdale, Ariz., company developing software for note-pad computers.
