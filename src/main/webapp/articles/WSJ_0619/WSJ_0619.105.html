Credit Ratings: Occidental's Debt, Preferred Ratings Lowered by S&P

  • Author: Frederick Rose
  • Date: 06/19/90

LOS ANGELES -- Standard & Poor's Corp. said it lowered its ratings on $8.2 billion of Occidental Petroleum Corp. debt and preferred stock, citing high fixed costs, heavy dividend demands and expected weak earnings. The New York ratings firm said Occidental's senior debt and preferred stock were downgraded to triple-B-minus from triple-B.

In addition to Occidental's securities, the downgraded senior debt includes similar ratings for Occidental's Oxy USA Inc., Occidental International Finance N.V., Oxy Petrochemicals Inc. (formerly Cain Chemical Inc.), and United Energy Resources Inc. units. Preferred stock downgraded included that of Oxy Petrochemicals and Natural Gas Pipelines of America.

Also downgraded was Occidental's subordinated debt, which was lowered to double-B-plus from triple-B-minus. The rating on the company's commercial paper was dropped to A-3 from A-2.

Standard & Poor's said the downgrades "reflect expectations that Occidental's weak earnings and cash flow protection measures will continue {to be} substandard for the foreseeable future."

Occidental declined to comment on the downgrades, which were announced after the close of trading. In New York Stock Exchange composite trading, the company's common stock closed at $26.50 a share, off 50 cents, paralleling declines in oil issues and the overall market.

Analysts have for some time noted that Occidental, an oil, chemicals and agricultural products concern, has paid substantially more annually in dividends than it makes in profit. Last year, for instance, cash dividend payments totaled $679 million against net income of $285 million, or $1.03 a share. Revenue last year totaled $20.07 billion.

S&P noted "high fixed charges stemming from aggressive debt levels and a very high common dividend payout," which, it said, "precludes meaningful improvement to the balance sheet."

The rating concern said asset sales probably will be required to supplement internally generated funds in meeting Occidental's capital-spending requirements. It said Occidental's financial flexibility is enhanced by a large, diversified asset base.

However, the rating concern said that "absent larger-than-expected asset sales, material debt reduction is unlikely" and that debt as a proportion of overall capital is likely to remain at about 55% or more. According to a recent calculation, Occidental's debt represented about 58% of the company's total capital, compared with an average of about 46% for the industry.
