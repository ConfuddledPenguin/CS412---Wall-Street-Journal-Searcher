FCC Ruling Paves the Way For a New AT&T Service

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/19/90

NEW YORK -- Southwestern Bell Telephone Co. received permission from the Federal Communications Commission to transmit its phone lists to other companies, paving the way for a new directory service from American Telephone & Telegraph Co. The service, which AT&T calls Find America, had been awaiting FCC clearance since January. Southwestern Bell will offer the service to its local business customers under the name Southwestern Bell DirectLine Custom.

The service is designed to allow corporate customers using special software to gain electronic access to phone company customer lists using a computer instead of an operator as an intermediary. The idea is to spur productivity and lower directory assistance charges.

An AT&T spokesman said the company has verbal agreements with the other six regional Bell companies as well as Cincinnati Bell and Southern New England Telephone Co. to provide their listings. This should enable AT&T to offer nationwide listings by the end of the year, the spokesman said.
