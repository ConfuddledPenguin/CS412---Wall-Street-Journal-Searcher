Britain's Government Deficit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/19/90

LONDON -- The public sector borrowing requirement, the most widely used measure of Britain's government deficit or surplus, showed a deficit of #1.7 billion ($2.91 billion) in May, compared with a deficit of #2.12 billion in April and a surplus of #890 million a year earlier, the British treasury said. In the first two months of the fiscal year that began April 1, the deficit totaled #3.8 billion, compared with a surplus of #700 million a year earlier. The government is projecting a #7 billion surplus for the year ending March 31.
