Who's News: NWA Inc. Will Name Rentschler Today To Chief's Post, Industry Officials Say

  • Author: Judith Valente
  • Date: 06/19/90

NWA Inc., parent of Northwest Airlines, is expected to name Frederick B. Rentschler as its new president and chief executive officer today, industry officials said. Mr. Rentschler is currently president and chief executive officer of Beatrice Co., the Chicago food company that entered into a merger agreement with ConAgra Inc. two weeks ago.

Mr. Rentschler, 50 years old, would succeed Frederic V. Malek, a member of the investor group led by Alfred A. Checchi that acquired NWA last year. Mr. Malek would stay on as NWA's vice chairman.

Mr. Rentschler's appointment means that the top three management positions at NWA will be held by executives with no previous airline experience. For seven months, the company has been run by Mr. Checchi, Mr. Malek and Gary Wilson, all former Marriott Corp. executives. Mr. Wilson's only other airline experience was as an NWA director.

Mr. Checchi has tapped other non-airline executives for key roles. Only two of the five vice presidents he has hired since become chairman last September have ever worked for an airline.

Mr. Checchi is said to have selected Mr. Rentschler because of his strong consumer-marketing background. Northwest is trying to shed a reputation for lackluster service. The Checchi group has said its goal is to make Northwest the airline of choice for business travelers, who usually pay the highest fares.

Toward that end, Northwest plans to spend $422 million over the next five years improving its food, upgrading its aircraft interiors and retraining its employees.

Mr. Rentschler is credited with turning around the Swift/Hunt-Wesson foods division of Esmark Inc. before Beatrice acquired that company. He was forced out in a management shake-up in 1984 after the Beatrice takeover. He returned to Beatrice in 1986 after its acquisition by Kohlberg Kravis Roberts & Co., the New York leveraged buy-out firm.

Mr. Rentschler's future was uncertain at Beatrice in light of the merger with ConAgra, an Omaha, Neb., food concern. However, officials familiar with the situation said Mr. Rentschler's NWA appointment had been in the works for weeks.

Mr. Rentschler couldn't be reached. NWA declined to comment. Mr. Checchi is expected to announce Mr. Rentschler's appointment today at a press conference in NWA's St. Paul, Minn., headquarters.

Whether Northwest suffers from its top management's lack of airline expertise remains to be seen. A number of its vice presidents and middle managers, including its operations and marketing chiefs, have extensive airline experience.

Although the Checchi team is getting high marks from the NWA staff, some employees expressed concern about Mr. Malek's outside activities. For the past several months, he split his time between NWA and his duties as chief organizer of a seven-nation economic summit planned for next month.

Mr. Malek, a friend and adviser to President Bush, is expected to play an increasing role in the Bush administration. That factor led to his decision to step down from day-to-day duties at Northwest, people familiar with the decision said.

Northwest, the result of a 1986 merger between Northwest Orient Airlines and Republic Airlines, is the nation's fourth largest carrier. It is the largest U.S. carrier to the North Pacific, a region that produces about a third of its revenue. It has spent the past three years expanding its European and domestic routes as well.

The Checchi group acquired NWA for $3.65 billion last August. The airline had a first-quarter operating loss of $89.8 million on revenue of $1.62 billion. It has forecast a modest profit, its first since the takeover, for the second quarter.
