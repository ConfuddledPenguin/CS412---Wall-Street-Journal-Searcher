Silk Greenhouse Posts A Loss; Staff Is Cut; Financial Aide Quits

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/19/90

TAMPA, Fla. -- Silk Greenhouse Inc. posted a loss for its first quarter ended April 30 and said it has reduced its work force by several hundred employees. The company also said its chief financial officer and two outside directors resigned.

The setbacks sent Silk stock tumbling to $2.125 a share, down $1.125, in national over-the-counter trading.

The company -- which sells artificial trees, flowers and plants -- said its loss totaled $5.7 million, or 60 cents a share. That compares with earnings of $360,000, or four cents a share, a year earlier. Sales rose 74% to $27.5 million from $15.8 million.

The company cited significant increases in all major categories of expenses.

To cut costs, the company said several hundred workers had been released, both through attrition and dismissals. Eighty employees were idled at corporate headquarters.

Chairman and President Jere L. Bradwell said salaries of several top executives, including his own, had been sharply reduced. The company declined to elaborate on its loss, the staff cuts or its outlook.

Silk also said John Stokely, its chief financial officer, who had joined the company only last month, had resigned for personal reasons. Moreover, two outside directors resigned, also for personal reasons.
