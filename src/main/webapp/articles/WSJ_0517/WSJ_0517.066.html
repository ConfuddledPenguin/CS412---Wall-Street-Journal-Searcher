Letters to the Editor: Earth Issues: Ballyhoo, Are You Listening?

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/17/90

Thank you for Dave Shiflett's excellent Leisure & Arts article ("Singing in the Acid Rain," April 18) decrying the emotional environmental brouhaha currently in vogue. The ballyhoo of some environmentalists reminds me of Mark Twain's remark: "There is something fascinating about science. One gets such wholesale returns of conjecture out of such a trifling investment of facts." To make sweeping, irresponsible statements about the environment is not to convince others of the righteousness of the cause. It is merely to sacrifice credibility. When the Earth itself is the issue, none of us benefit from such a method.

John Warner

Kenney, Ill.
