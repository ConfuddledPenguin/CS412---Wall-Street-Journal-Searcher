Wednesday's Markets: Stocks Drop As Rally Is Stalled --- Concern on How Far Rates to Fall Prevails; Bond Prices Decline

  • Author: Douglas R. Sease
  • Date: 05/17/90

The stock market rally that carried the Dow Jones Industrial Average to two consecutive records stalled amid uncertainty about how much farther interest rates can fall. The Dow Jones Industrial Average lost 2.77 points to 2819.68 in active trading. The rally swept the Dow 84 points higher since last Thursday, but gains tailed off to less than a point Tuesday. The rally was triggered by perceptions inflation was waning and interest rates were headed lower.

Long-term bond prices were modestly lower for the second day in a row. The dollar ended mixed.

Analysts said that despite the Dow Jones average's record highs, investors remain wary about how much further stock prices can climb without either substantial reductions in interest rates or big gains in corporate earnings, neither of which appears likely in the short term.

"The stock market, no matter what happens, is not going to go an awful lot further until we get through the first half of the year and start to see better numbers on the earnings front," said Harold MacKinney, head of the investment policy committee at Fleet National Bank in Providence, R.I.

Yet some traders contend that prices won't retreat very far simply because money managers who didn't quickly jump into the big rally Friday will view any price declines as buying opportunities.

The bond market's lack of any further gains after last week's big rally is a major restraint on stock investors. While the yield on the Treasury's benchmark 30-year bond plunged to less than 8.7% from more than 9% in just two weeks, the interest-rate decline stopped this week. Even good news on inflation failed to get any rise out of bond prices yesterday. The government reported that the consumer price index rose 0.2% in April. Bond traders said the lack of response to the data wasn't surprising given the substantial price gains last Friday.

In major market action:

Stock prices slipped in active trading. Volume on the New York Stock Exchange totaled 160 million shares. Declining issues on the Big Board were ahead of gainers 814 to 701.

Bond prices fell. The Treasury's benchmark 30-year bond lost about an eighth of a point, or about $1.25 for each $1,000 of face amount, for a yield of 8.63%.

The dollar was mixed. In late New York trading the currency was quoted at 1.6455 marks and 151.67 yen compared with 1.6465 marks and 150.80 yen Tuesday.
