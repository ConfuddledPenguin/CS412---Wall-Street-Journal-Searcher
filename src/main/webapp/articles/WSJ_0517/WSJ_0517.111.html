Business Brief -- Owens-Illinois Inc.: Loss Narrowed in 1st Period On 11% Revenue Increase

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/17/90

Owens-Illinois Inc., taken private in a leveraged buy-out in 1987, reported a narrower first-quarter net loss on 11% higher revenue, sending its publicly traded bonds higher. All of the company's major businesses -- glass containers, plastics and closures, specialized glass and health care -- posted "strong" performances, Owens-Illinois said in a Securities and Exchange Commission filing.

The Toledo, Ohio, company, taken private in 1987 for $3.64 billion by Kohlberg Kravis Roberts & Co., reported its deficit shrank to $12.3 million, or 33 cents a share, from $32.3 million, or 87 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue rose to $956.3 million from $855.4 million.

Owens-Illinois's earnings before depreciation, amortization, interest and tax rose 29% to $192.2 million from $149.5 million. And interest coverage -- the number of times these earnings exceed interest expense -- increased to 1.46 from 1.22.
