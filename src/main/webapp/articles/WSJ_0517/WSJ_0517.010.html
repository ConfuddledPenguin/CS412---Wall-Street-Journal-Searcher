Western Union Deficit Widened In First Quarter

  • Author: John J. Keller
  • Date: 05/17/90

NEW YORK -- Western Union Corp., which has been struggling to avoid bankruptcy, reported significantly wider losses and lower revenue for the first quarter. The company had a loss of $18.1 million compared with a $189,000 deficit in the year-earlier period. If undeclared dividends on preferred shares are included, the company's net loss was $29.5 million, or 37 cents a common share, against $13.5 million, or 32 cents a share, in last year's first quarter.

These numbers are based on an average 80.7 million shares in this year's first quarter because of the continuing conversion of Western Union's Class B convertible preferred shares into common, compared with the average 42.6 million common shares outstanding in last year's quarter.

Revenue fell 8.2% to $144.7 million from $158.2 million.

A company spokesman pegged the wider losses to "a continued steep decline in our telex revenues, higher interest expenses and higher operating expenses in our Financial Services unit . . . particularly from higher fees and commissions paid to agents of our money transfer and bill payment services."

Broken down, Business Services revenue from telex, electronic mail and Mailgram fell 21%, to $66.1 million from $83.5 million a year ago. This was primarily due to a "precipitous" drop in telex revenue of about 33% in the quarter, said the spokesman. But he added that revenue from electronic mail, which Western is counting on to pick up the slack, grew 25%. Financial services, including Money Transfer, voice messaging, telegram and bill payments rose 11% to $7.9 million in the quarter.

Meanwhile, Western Union's bid, filed last month, to recapitalize its debt and issue new common stock has yet to clear the Securities and Exchange Commission, the spokesman said. The company has said that it may be forced to seek protection under the U.S. Bankruptcy Code if the plan doesn't go through.

Western Union wants to issue new debt securities to holders of its senior secured reset notes due Dec. 15, 1992, which currently carry an interest payment of 19.25%, and to holders of its 16% notes due June 15, 1991. It is also seeking to reclassify its preferred stock and the Class B common shares held by its primary owner Brooke Partners Limited Partnership into new common shares.

In its quarterly report to the SEC, Western Union said that if it can't find sufficient funds to make a $51 million interest payment due June 15 on its reset notes it could be forced into a default.

Corrections & Amplifications

WESTERN UNION Corp.'s financial-services revenue grew 11% to $78.6 million. The figure was misstated in yesterday's edition. In addition, the company said it no longer plans to reclassify the Class B common shares held by Brooke Partners Limited Partnership into new common shares.

(WSJ May 18, 1990)
