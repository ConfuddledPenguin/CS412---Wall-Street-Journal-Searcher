Business Brief -- Del Webb Corp.: Holder Effort to Remove Takeover Defense Advances

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/17/90

Del Webb Corp. said a nonbinding shareholder resolution to remove the company's anti-takeover provisions is winning support from the Phoenix, Ariz., real estate developer's shareholders, based on a preliminary tally. The proposal by Los Angeles-based Webbcott Holdings, which owns a 9.7% stake and is controlled by director James J. Cotter, would request that the board redeem shareholder rights issued under a "poison pill" plan adopted last year. Less than half the votes have been tallied, but a company spokesman said a "significant majority" of the votes counted so far support the resolution. A final tally isn't expected for several days.

In April, Webb agreed to several of Mr. Cotter's demands to avert a proxy fight at yesterday's annual meeting. Mr. Cotter had planned to offer a rival slate of directors and wanted to eliminate "golden parachute" provisions for senior management. Webb agreed to expand the board by two members, and renominate four of 10 directors whose terms were set to expire, including Mr. Cotter.
