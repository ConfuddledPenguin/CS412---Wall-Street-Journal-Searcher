Upjohn in Talks to Sell South Africa Facility To Plant's Managers

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/17/90

KALAMAZOO, Mich. -- Upjohn Co. said it is negotiating to sell one of its two South African plants to a group of that plant's management. The announcement came during the pharmaceutical company's annual meeting in which shareholders defeated a proposal that would have required the company to halt all South African operations. The proposal was made by three of Upjohn's institutional investors -- the New York State Retirement Fund, the New York State Teachers' Retirement System and the State of Florida.

Upjohn officials said they're currently holding talks to sell the Asgrow S.A. vegetable and agronomic seed plant in South Africa. Upjohn said it hopes to strike a deal by the end of the second quarter, although required U.S. government approval may delay the process.

Upjohn officials said they are selling the plant because of its weak financial performance. However, they said they'll only sell the plant to buyers who guarantee to "remain committed to social programs." Upjohn said the plant now employs 100 workers.

Upjohn said it has no plans to sell or close its pharmaceutical plant in South Africa.
