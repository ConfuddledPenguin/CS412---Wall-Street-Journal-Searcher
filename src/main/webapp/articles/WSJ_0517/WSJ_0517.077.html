Money Fund Yields Moved Upward In the Latest Week

  • Author: Georgette Jasen
  • Date: 05/17/90

Yields on money-market mutual funds turned up in the week ended Tuesday, a delayed reaction to the uptick in short-term interest rates of a couple of weeks ago. The average seven-day compound yield rose to 8% from 7.96%, the highest in more than three months, according to IBC/Donoghue's Money Fund Report. Compound yields assume reinvestment of dividends.

Assets of the 428 taxable funds tracked by the Holliston, Mass.-based newsletter continued to fall, however, to $374.7 billion, their lowest point since mid-January. About $2.3 billion came out of money funds in the latest week, the seventh consecutive weekly drop.

Short-term interest rates have come down in the past week, after rising for a time before that. The three-month Treasury bill sold at Monday's auction, for instance, yielded 7.93%, down from 8.06% the week before. But the yield on the three-month bill was at 8.18% at the April 30 auction, up from 8.05% the previous week.

"We're wondering if {money fund} assets will work their way back up again, now that rates have started to fall," said Brenda Malizia Negus, editor of Money Fund Report. She noted that some of the cash withdrawn from money funds in recent weeks probably went to buy higher-yielding Treasury securities.

Money-fund yields typically lag behind overall interest rate trends, as fund managers vary the maturities of their investments to get the best possible returns. The average maturity remained at a fairly long 44 days for the fifth consecutive week, according to Donoghue's, indicating that there won't be any sharp swings in money-fund yields for at least the next few weeks. Fund investments include such things as commercial paper (short-term corporate IOUs), Treasury bills and bank certificates of deposit.

The average seven-day simple yield of the taxable funds rose to 7.70% from 7.66%. The average 30-day simple yield remained at 7.68%, while the 30-day compound average stayed at 7.98%.

Yields on tax-free funds fell for the second consecutive week. The average seven-day compound yield, for instance, slid to 5.67% from 5.74%. That's equal to 7.88% for an investor in the 28% tax bracket and 8.46% for someone paying taxes at a 33% rate.

Assets of the 221 tax-exempt funds fell by $262.3 million, to $75.3 billion. Average maturity shortened by a day, to 41 days.
