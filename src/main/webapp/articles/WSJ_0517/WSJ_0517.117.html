Court Allows Ames To Receive Financing Totaling $250 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/17/90

NEW YORK -- A U.S. bankruptcy court here permitted Ames Department Stores Inc. to receive $250 million in interim financing so the company can purchase goods from hesitant suppliers. Stephen L. Pistner, chief executive officer of Ames, called the court's ruling "very good news for Ames's creditors, employees, customers and suppliers." Ames has said suppliers have refused to ship goods and extend credit since the company filed for bankruptcy court protection last month.

Mr. Pistner said that "merchandise shipments should be back to normal in very short order." Chemical Bank, a unit of Chemical Banking Corp., is the lender of the $250 million.

Bankruptcy court Judge Howard Buschman said Ames needed only $150 million for current purchases, and that the additional $100 million was needed as an inducement for suppliers to extend credit on normal terms.

Creditors with other loans outstanding to Ames initially objected to the new loan, raising concerns about their repayment priority in relation to Chemical. The court approved the new loan after the 33 lenders reached agreement on repayment priorities, and on terms for splitting $91 million in an Ames cash collateral account.

The troubled discount retailer operates about 600 stores in 20 states.
