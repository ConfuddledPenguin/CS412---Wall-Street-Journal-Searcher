Dividend News: Savin Corp.

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/17/90

SAVIN Corp. shareholders approved a 1-for-5 reverse stock split for the Stamford, Conn., copier and facsimile marketer's 132 million shares outstanding. Management had proposed the split, according to the annual-meeting proxy material, to bring Savin's stock "to a more appropriate trading price for a company concerned with long-term development of its business opportunities." Savin, which is 55%-controlled by HCS Technology N.V. of the Netherlands, hasn't yet set a record or payment date for the split. Savin holders also passed a proposal under which HCS could continue to receive dividends on the Savin Series C preferred shares it holds, but HCS won't vote the shares that accrue as dividends. HCS recently agreed to form a 50-50 joint venture with the West German group Hoechst AG. Based in the Netherlands, the venture will market and service office equipment in Europe.
