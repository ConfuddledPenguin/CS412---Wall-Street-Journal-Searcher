Letters to the Editor: This Old House Is a-Gettin' Older

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/17/90

I should like to propose a solution to the fight over preserving Lockwood, the Virginia farmhouse where Gen. Robert E. Lee spent several nights before a crucial battle to defend Richmond (page one, April 18). While the illustration accompanying the article showed a very simple, pretty house, the description of its current state revealed a dilapidation and overwhelming invasion of termites that would make it absurdly expensive to restore and maintain. I therefore propose putting a historical marker on the site of Lockwood and making sure that any available photographs or other illustrations of the place as it was in Gen. Lee's day are included in the Virginia Historical Society's collection.

Lucinda Lee Kinsolving Leigh

Washington

---

I must say to J. Stewart Bryan III, whose company Media General Inc. is threatening to tear down the structure, that it is unfortunate he does not see the true importance of preservation. His family may have been in the forefront of the preservation movement in Virginia, but in demolishing this historic home, he will single-handedly wipe out his family's philanthropic beliefs in saving American heritage.

What will future generations glean from our past societies if all they have to experience is the Breakers, Biltmore and Monticello? I would hope that Mr. Bryan can put aside his mortal reasoning and present to my children's children a piece of vernacular American history when they travel down a Virginia road someday.

Paul K. Williams

Graduate Student '91

Historial Preservation Planning

Cornell University

Ithaca, N.Y.

---

Please be advised that the Commonwealth of Virginia is rich in most of God's blessings, and beautiful homes of historical significance are no exception. Should anyone attempt to damage Westover, Berkeley, Carter's Grove, Prestwould, Monticello, Mount Vernon, Sherwood Forest, Shirley Plantation, Stratford Hall, etc., then I would wager the Bryan family would lead the troops in a successful defense. However, for Lockwood?

Richard T. Short

Virginia Beach, Va.
