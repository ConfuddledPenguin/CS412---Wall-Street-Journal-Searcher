Chicago Board of Trade Develops Insurance Futures

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/17/90

CHICAGO -- The Chicago Board of Trade said it has developed futures contracts on health and auto-collision insurance for use by insurance companies. Details of the new contracts are being tightly guarded for competitive reasons, said the futures exchange, which soon will submit the proposed new contracts to the Commodity Futures Trading Commission for approval. It's likely to be several months before the federal regulator acts on the proposals.

The contracts are likely to be based on "baskets" of insurance policies maturing at the same time, futures industry officials speculated.

Futures contracts on insurance policies have been considered by a number of futures exchanges over the past several years. The Board of Trade seriously considered listing insurance futures contracts in the early 1970s, but it put the product on hold while it pursued interest rate futures.

Insurance futures found a new champion in Leslie Rosenthal, who had been exchange chairman when the idea for the product was floated in the early '70s. Earlier this year, Mr. Rosenthal was asked to head an exchange subcommittee that developed the contracts.

Richard Sandor, who advised Mr. Rosenthal on the product, conceded that there hasn't been a groundswell of demand for the futures contract, and that it may be a hard sell among traditionally conservative insurance companies.
