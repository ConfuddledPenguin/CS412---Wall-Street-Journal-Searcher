Marketing & Media: Disney to Continue Work On Jim Henson's Projects

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/17/90

Jim Henson, who died yesterday at age 53 of a massive bacterial infection, had already begun numerous projects at Walt Disney Co. since Disney agreed to acquire his company last year. And these projects will go forward as planned, executives said. "We vow to do all that we can to see that the extraordinary legacy of this great friend and colleague will live on," the company said in a statement.

Mr. Henson created the Muppets and a host of other characters known world-wide. Last August, Disney agreed to acquire New York-based Henson Associates Inc. (which excludes Mr. Henson's participation in the television characters on "Sesame Street") for about $100 million to $150 million. Mr. Henson has been active in every phase of Disney's operation since then, although the transaction has not been formally completed.

At Disney's theme parks, a Muppet show and daily parades are scheduled to begin at Walt Disney World's Disney-MGM Studios in Orlando, Fla., on June 15. Mr. Henson was finishing up a filmed attraction set to open at Disney-MGM next year and at Disneyland in California in 1993. A "Muppet Movie Ride" is scheduled to open at Disney-MGM in 1993. Disney officials say they don't expect any delays in the projects.
