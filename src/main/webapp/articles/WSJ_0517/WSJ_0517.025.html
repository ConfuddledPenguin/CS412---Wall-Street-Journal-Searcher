Steel Technologies Plans Joint Venture

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/17/90

LOUISVILLE, Ky. -- Steel Technologies Inc. said it signed a letter of intent to form a joint venture with the Mitsui Steel Development Co. unit of Mitsui & Co. of Japan and the LTV Steel Co. unit of LTV Corp., Dallas, to construct and operate two facilities capable of processing more than 500,000 tons of steel annually. Each of the partners is to own one-third of the joint venture, said Timothy M. Graven, president of Steel Technologies. He said the venture plans to spend $30 million to $35 million to build and equip the facilities.

He said the venture plans to have the facilities constructed within 18 months. The facilities will be in the Midwest, but Steel Technologies wasn't more specific.

Steel Technologies, a processor of flat rolled steel, said the facilities will use the latest technology in metal processing, including slitting and cutting the steel to length. They also will have the capacity to provide steel storage of about one million tons a year for customers requiring just-in-time delivery, Steel Technologies said.
