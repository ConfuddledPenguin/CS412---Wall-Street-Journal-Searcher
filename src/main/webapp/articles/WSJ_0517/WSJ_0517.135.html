Senate Clears Naming Of Mullins to Fed

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/17/90

WASHINGTON -- The Senate approved the nomination of David Mullins to the Federal Reserve Board. Mr. Mullins will leave his post as the assistant Treasury secretary for domestic finance to serve out the term vacated by Robert Heller, who left the Fed last year to become an executive vice president of Visa International. Mr. Mullins's term at the Fed will expire in 1996.

Mr. Mullins, a former Harvard Business School professor, was a principal author of the Bush administration's plan to bail out the savings-and-loan industry. Before coming to the Treasury, he was a top staffer on a task force set up by former President Reagan to investigate the 1987 stock market crash.
