Technology Brief -- N.V. Philips: Thomson S.A. Collaboration On HDTV Gathers Speed

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/17/90

N.V. Philips of the Netherlands said it signed an accord with Thomson S.A. of France to push jointly for a European launch of new high-definition television, or HDTV, technology in 1995. French government officials said the two companies, Europe's biggest consumer electronics concerns, plan to expand their joint laboratory research in HDTV. Philips declined to comment. But it said the companies estimate HDTV's launch may cost all of European industry three billion European currency units ($3.73 billion); the Philips-Thomson outlays haven't been decided. HDTV would offer extra-sharp color pictures, improved sound, and cinema-style screen formats. But HDTV sets in Europe initially may be priced at as much as 30,000 French francs ($5,410).

Billing the HDTV market as the battleground for global dominance in consumer electronics, big makers have been seeking government research subsidies. State-controlled Thomson is negotiating its five-year research budget; the accord and the big HDTV cost estimate may buttress Thomson's arguments for increased funding. Thomson declined to comment.
