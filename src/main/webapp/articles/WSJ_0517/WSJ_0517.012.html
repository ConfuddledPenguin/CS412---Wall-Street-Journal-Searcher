Landmark Retains Salomon to Study Firm's Possible Sale

  • Author: Neil Barsky
  • Date: 05/17/90

Landmark Land Co. said it retained Salomon Brothers Inc. to explore a possible sale of the company, one week after its proposed $967 million land sale was thwarted by federal authorities. The Carmel, Calif., company, which owns its real estate through its New Orleans-based Oak Tree Savings, said last week that the Office of Thrift Supervision had objected to its plan to finance 75% of the real estate sale to California developer Barry G. Hon.

Yesterday it said it would instead sell just one development to Mr. Hon for $275 million, and that it would explore restructuring or selling the entire company.

Landmark said it would realize a $170 million pretax profit if the sale to Mr. Hon closes. Landmark Chairman Gerald G. Barton said that while the Office of Thrift Supervision hadn't explicitly approved the transaction, he believed the sale fell within federal guidelines.

The OTS had objected to the previous agreement with Mr. Hon, because the transaction would have committed more than 20% of Landmark's assets to a single borrower.

The possible sale of Landmark is a direct result of the Financial Institutions Reform, Recovery and Enforcement Act of 1989, which tightened restrictions on savings and loan associations' ownership of real estate.

The company is best known for its ownership and management of golf resorts, including PGA West, La Quinta and Mission Hills, all located in the Palm Springs area. Salomon Bros.' managing director in charge of real estate, J. Steven Manolis, said the company's real estate and golf management could be attractive to Japanese buyers. But in the past six months, Japanese interests have generally shunned high-profile U.S. real estate deals, partly because of anti-Japanese sentiment.

Mr. Barton said it was possible a single buyer such as Mr. Hon, a Southern California home builder and land developer, would emerge to purchase the company's real estate, and a different investor would wind up owning Oak Tree Savings.

Landmark, which has only eight million shares outstanding, is 28%-owned by Mr. Barton and 24%-owned by Olympia & York Developments Ltd., the Toronto conglomerate controlled by the Reichmann brothers.

In American Stock Exchange trading yesterday, Landmark stock rose 50 cents a share to $10.875.
