Merrill Lynch-Run Fund Buys Firm From GE Unit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/17/90

NEW YORK -- The leveraged buy-out fund run by Merrill Lynch & Co. acquired Londontown Holdings Corp., maker of London Fog rainwear and other apparel for an indicated price of more than $63.2 million. The seller is General Electric Co.'s General Electric Capital Corp., which financed a $178 million leveraged buy-out of Londontown in December 1988. Interco Inc. sold Eldersburg, Md.-based Londontown at the time to reduce debt Interco took on to fend off a takeover bid.

GE provided the financing for the original buy-out with $215 million in debt, $20 million in preferred stock and a $75 million bridge loan by GE's securities unit, Kidder, Peabody & Co. That loan was refinanced last year with a sale by Kidder of high-risk, high-yield junk bonds.

Merrill is paying $1,631.23 a share for 38,750 Class B Common shares of Londontown Holdings, or $63.2 million, plus a sum it didn't disclose for part of an equity interest held by Londontown management. The common shares include 9,687.5 warrants sold in connection with the junk-bond offering and convertible into a 17.5% interest in the company. The transaction values the warrants at $15.8 million.

GE will continue to hold its Londontown debt and preferred stock, and the junk bonds will remain outstanding. But a spokesman wouldn't disclose GE's purchase price for its equity stake or its indicated profit or loss on the transaction.

Executives of Merrill's buy-out fund, Merrill Lynch Capital Partners Inc., couldn't be reached for comment.
