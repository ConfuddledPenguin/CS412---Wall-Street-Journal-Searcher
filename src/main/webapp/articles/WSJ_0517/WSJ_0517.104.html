New Securities Issues

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/17/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATES

Sears Roebuck & Co. -- $400 million of 9.35% notes, due May 15, 1993, priced at 99.90 to yield 9.387%. The noncallable notes were priced at a spread of 80 basis points above the Treasury's three-year note. The size of the issue was increased from an originally planned $300 million. Rated single-A-1 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and single-A-plus by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Goldman Sachs & Co.

Maytag Corp. -- $200 million of 9 3/4% notes, due May 15, 2002, priced at 99.549 to yield 9.814%. The noncallable notes were priced at a spread of 115 basis points above the Treasury's 10-year note. Rated single-A-2 by Moody's and single-A-minus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Goldman Sachs & Co.

MUNICIPALS

Wisconsin Housing Finance Authority -- $151 million housing revenue bonds, Series A, due Sept. 1, 2007 and 2017 and Series B, due March 1, and Sept. 1, 1991-2000, and Sept. 1, 2007 and 2021, tentatively priced by a Bear Stearns & Co. group to yield from 6.35% for Series B bonds due in 1991 to 8% for Series B bonds due in 2021. Series A consists of $5.47 million of 7.40% term bonds priced at par in 2007, and $26 million of 7 3/4% term bonds priced at par in 2017. Series B current interest serial bonds are priced to yield from 6.35% in 1991 to 7.20% in 2000. There are $20 million of 7.65% term bonds priced at par in 2020 and $19 million of 8% term bonds priced at par in 2021. Term bonds due in 2020 are not formally reoffered. Interest on Series B bonds will be treated as a preference item in calculating the federal alternative minimum tax that may be imposed on certain investors. The bonds are rated double-A by Moody's and single-A-plus by S&P.

East Bay Municipal Utility District, Calif. -- $60 million wastewater system subordinated revenue bonds, Series 1990, due June 1, 2001-2012, 2018 and 2020, apparently won by a J.P. Morgan Securities Inc. group. The bonds, rated double-A by Moody's and single-A by S&P, priced to yield from 6.80% in 2001 to 7.20% in 2020. Current interest serial bonds are priced to yield from 6.80% in 2001 to 7.13% in 2012. There are $19 million of 7 1/8% term bonds priced at 99 5/8 to yield 7.16% in 2017, and $15 million of 7.20% term bonds priced at par in 2020.

Tennessee Local Development Authority -- $55 million state loan program revenue bond anticipation notes, due May 31, 1991, apparently won by a Shearson Lehman Hutton Inc. group. The notes, rated MIG-1 by Moody's and SP-1-plus by S&P, were priced to yield 6.10%.

MORTGAGE-BACKED

Salomon Brothers Inc. -- $500.3 million of collateralized mortgage obligation trust Remic mortgage securities, offered in eight classes by Salomon Brothers Inc., a unit of Salomon Inc. The offering, Series 62, is backed by 10% Ginnie Mae I and II securities with a weighted average remaining term to maturity of 28.5 years. The issue, is expected to be rated triple-A by both Moody's and S&P, according to Salomon.

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $300 million of Remic mortgage securities is being offered in eight classes by First Boston Corp. The offering, Series 1990-67, is backed by Fannie Mae 9% mortgage-backed securities.

EUROBONDS

SE Banken (Sweden) -- $100 million of floating-rate notes due June 15, 2000, priced at par and with a step-up coupon, via Goldman Sachs International Ltd. For first five years, coupon floats 0.25 point above the six-month London Interbank Offered Rate; thereafter, 0.45 point above Libor. Callable at par from the fifth year on every coupon date. Fees 0.26.

British Telecom Finance BV (U.K. parent) -- 100 million European currency units of 10 5/8% Eurobonds due March 28, 1995, priced at 101.675, via Bank of Tokyo Capital Markets. Guaranteed by British Telecommunications PLC.

Compagnie Bancaire (France) -- 60 million European currency units of 11% Eurobonds due June 29, 1992, priced at 101.85 to yield 10.58%, via Banque Paribas Capital Markets Ltd.

Tasmania Public Finance Corp. (Australia) -- 50 million Australian dollars of 15 1/4% Eurobonds due June 14, 1993, priced at 101.40 to yield 15.29% less fees, via Deutsche Bank Capital Markets. Guaranteed by Treasurer of State of Tasmania. Fees 1 1/2.
