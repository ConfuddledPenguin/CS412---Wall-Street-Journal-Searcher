Japan Lending Grew in Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/17/90

TOKYO -- The pace of growth in lending activities by Japanese financial institutions during the January-to-March quarter amounted to 42.3% over the pace of growth during the year-earlier period, The Bank of Japan reported. The pace of growth in lending by large Japanese commercial banks for the period was 42.1% while the pace in lending by regional banks amounted to 50.7%.

A central bank official said much of the growth reflected an increase in foreign-currency-denominated -- or "impact" -- loans by Japanese financial institutions, as opposed to yen-denominated lending.
