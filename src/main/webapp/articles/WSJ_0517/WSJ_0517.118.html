Business Brief -- American First Corp.: Vote to Double Shares Authorized Falls Short

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/17/90

American First Corp., an Oklahoma City-based manufacturing company, said it didn't receive enough shareholder votes to double its authorized shares to 20 million. Approval required 51% of the shares outstanding to vote in favor of the measure, but only about 56% to 57% of the shares were voted, and the proposal won only 45% of the shares outstanding. Directors said they plan to resubmit the proposal to holders after the company forms a capitalization plan. American First has been trying to raise capital for its Tricon American Corp. unit.

American First also said that John W. Cox, formerly senior vice president and treasurer, was named president and chief executive officer, succeeding Gregory J. Edwards, who remains chairman. Mr. Edwards said last month that he planned to resign from day-to-day management activities.
