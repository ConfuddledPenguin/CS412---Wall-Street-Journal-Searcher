Who's News: Citicorp Private Bank Names Hocking Head Of Investment Unit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/17/90

NEW YORK -- Citicorp's Citibank Private Bank unit named James R. Hocking chief investment officer of the U.S. Investment division, signaling the bank's determination to raise its profile as a manager of assets for wealthier individuals. Mr. Hocking, 60 years old, joins Citibank from Templeton International, a unit of Templeton Galbraith & Hansberger Ltd., the London money manager. As senior vice president and portfolio manager there, his responsibilities included creating and marketing mutual funds and managing portfolios.

At Citibank, he will oversee more than 50 portfolio managers who invest $15 billion for individuals of high net worth. And Mr. Hocking will direct the bank's efforts for U.S. customers who want to invest in overseas securities.

In October 1988, Citicorp sold its Citicorp Investment Management Inc. subsidiary, which invested pension-fund assets for U.S. institutions. Other banks also have sold such operations, which are "highly price-competitive," said David E. Gibson, Citicorp's group executive for global private banking. The company did maintain its international institutional business.

But having sold the U.S. institutional line, the bank resolved to boost its individual private-banking work. "We have a big involvement and a big commitment" to individual private banking, but "we don't have the public image," the 50-year-old Mr. Gibson said. Through "selected important personnel," including Mr. Hocking, the bank hopes to boost that profile, he added.
