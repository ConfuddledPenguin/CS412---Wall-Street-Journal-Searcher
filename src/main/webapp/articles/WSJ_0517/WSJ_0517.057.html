International: Soviets Become Observers In Tariff-Trade Agreement

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 05/17/90

GENEVA -- Members of the General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade agreed to the Soviet Union's request for observer status with the world trade group, a spokesman said. David Woods said Soviet officials attended a meeting of GATT's top decision-making body yesterday after it unanimously approved Moscow's application.

U.S. Deputy Trade Representative Rufus Yerxa told the council meeting that a Soviet presence in GATT would boost its understanding of the world trading system and so help its own economic reform process.

Washington last year dropped its opposition to the Soviet Union gaining observer status in GATT but sought to delay that until December. Mr. Yerxa said the Bush administration agreed to an earlier Soviet entry, since it was backed by "nearly all" GATT members.
