Sweden's Foreign Trade Surplus

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/17/90

STOCKHOLM -- Sweden's foreign trade surplus in April narrowed to a preliminary 2.4 billion kronor ($398.9 million) from 3.4 billion kronor in March, but widened from a surplus of 1.6 billion kronor in April 1989, Statistics Sweden, the national statistics agency, said. The latest surplus exceeded the two billion-krona average prediction of analysts. Exports in April totaled 28.3 billion kronor, down 4% from a year earlier, and imports amounted to 25.9 billion kronor, down 7%.

Sweden's foreign trade surplus in the first four months was 9.4 billion kronor, unchanged from a year earlier. Exports rose 3% to 116.4 billion kronor, and imports also increased 3% to 107 billion kronor.
