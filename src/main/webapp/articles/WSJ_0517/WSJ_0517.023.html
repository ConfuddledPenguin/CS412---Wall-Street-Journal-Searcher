Integrated Program Seeks New Lender For Key Mortgage

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/17/90

NEW YORK -- American Insured Mortgage Investors, a real estate investment program sponsored by Integrated Resources Inc., said it is seeking to find a new lender for a mortgage that represents 62% of its portfolio after the borrower missed two interest payments. Integrated's AIM 84 partnership originated the $99.9 million mortgage, which is federally insured, on Windsor Court, a high-rise rental apartment in Manhattan. AIM decided to take the action in light of a soft real estate market that caused the borrower, Mastic Associates, to miss two interest payments totaling $1.9 million. The remainder of the portfolio, which totaled $166 million, includes 20 other mortgages.

Thomas M. Finn, chief financial officer of AIM Capital Management Corp., the managing general partner, said AIM 84 would distribute proceeds of the sale to investors in a range it estimated at $9 to $10 per unit. Investors would continue to hold an interest in the remainder of the portfolio, which Mr. Finn said now totals $18 per unit. The partnership, which is publicly traded, was quoted at $14, up 50 cents, in over-the-counter trading yesterday.

If AIM 84 fails to sell the mortgage to a lender in 45 to 60 days, it plans to assign it to the Department of Housing and Urban Development. In that case, AIM 84 would be entitled to 99% of the mortgage, plus accrued interest minus one month. Mr. Finn said AIM 84 is negotiating with a lender, but there isn't any assurance the transaction will be completed, or completed on these terms.

Regardless of who is assigned the mortgage, investors will lose the right to participate in the current cash flow or residual value of the property.

In addition, Integrated, which is operating under federal bankruptcy-law protection, has been trying to sell its general partnership interest in the AIM series of partnerships through a court-approved auction. Mr. Finn said the price Integrated could expect to receive "would be less" as a result of the mortgage sale, but he didn't quantify the impact.

Integrated, which manages more than 600 partnerships, has been trying to sell assets to satisfy creditors, who are owed $1.8 billion. Integrated has said it would accept a minimum bid of $40 million for its interest in four AIM partnerships sold in the mid-1980s that raised a total of $789 million.
