Marketing & Media: Walt Disney Home Video Plans `Peter Pan' Release

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/17/90

BURBANK, Calif. -- "Peter Pan," the story of the boy who never grows old, will be the next Walt Disney animated classic released on videotape, Walt Disney Home Video said. The movie will be listed at $24.99. But the Walt Disney Co. subsidiary is linking up with certain Nabisco crackers to offer a $5 rebate.

The company said the rebate price is the lowest retail price yet for The Disney Classics series of videotapes, which most recently featured "The Little Mermaid." Others in the series have been "Bambi," "Robin Hood," "Pinocchio," "Sleeping Beauty," "Lady and the Tramp," "Cinderella" and Touchstone Picture's "Who Framed Roger Rabbit."

Since the initial release of "Peter Pan" in 1953, the animated feature has been re-released in theaters six times, Disney said, grossing $145 million in current dollars. The company plans to herald the videotape with an extensive, national advertising campaign.
