Business Brief -- Canadian Pacific Ltd.: Unit Agrees to Buy Assets From Delaware & Hudson

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/17/90

The trustee for Delaware & Hudson Railway Co., Francis P. Dicello, said he accepted an offer of US$25 million from Canadian Pacific Ltd. for the operating assets of the small railroad, which is in bankruptcy-law proceedings. The offer by the CP Rail unit of Canadian Pacific is subject to approval by bankruptcy court in Wilmington, Del., Mr. Dicello said. The sale is also subject to the negotiation of labor agreements and to regulatory approval.

Delaware & Hudson owns about 600 miles of track and has rights to run its trains on an additional 900 miles. Acquiring the U.S. railroad is part of CP Rail's strategy of adding north-south rail routes connecting Canada and the U.S.

Canadian Pacific is a Montreal transportation, resources, telecommunications and real estate concern.
