Dividend News: Grafton Group Ltd.

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/17/90

GRAFTON GROUP Ltd. said in Toronto that it didn't declare a quarterly dividend on its common or Class A shares because of recent poor earnings and a depressed outlook for its retail businesses. Grafton paid two Canadian cents (1.7 U.S. cents) on each class of stock in March. It lowered the quarterly dividend last June from eight Canadian cents. Grafton is a retailer of apparel and footwear, which recently announced plans to sell or close chains that sell womenswear, discount merchandise and unisex casual wear and a home-furnishing subsidiary. The company reported a loss of 24.5 million Canadian dollars (US$20.8 million), or C$1.38 a share, for the year ended Jan. 31. Results included a loss of C$29.1 million, or C$1.56 a share, from the units that it plans to discontinue. In trading on the Toronto Stock Exchange, Grafton Class A shares closed yesterday at C$4.60, up 10 Canadian cents. The closely held common shares, which last closed at C$4.50, didn't trade.
