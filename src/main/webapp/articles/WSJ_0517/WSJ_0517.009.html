International: Mitsubishi, Daimler Pick Up Pace of Talks On Joint Ventures

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/17/90

TOKYO -- The Mitsubishi industrial group and Daimler-Benz AG of West Germany are picking up the pace of their discussions on global industrial cooperation. A spokesman for Mitsubishi Motors Corp. confirmed that Daimler has contacted Mitsubishi Corp., the trading company at the core of the Japanese conglomerate, about jointly building a large auto plant in the Soviet Union.

But the spokesman said that the talks were in the early stages and that Mitsubishi hadn't any intention of producing its own cars in the Soviet Union. "This is a subject to be discussed in the future," he said. The Japanese press also reported that the two companies are talking about producing trucks in East Germany, but the Mitsubishi Motors spokesman denied that any talks about such a project are under way.

Mitsubishi Motors and Daimler also have agreed to research jointly pollution-control devices for autos, the spokesman said. But he played down the project's significance, saying only that the two companies may exchange engineers.

In March, the two concerns made headlines when they agreed to discuss the prospect of cooperating in a range of industries, including autos, aerospace and semiconductors. Last month, Daimler and Mitsubishi Heavy Industries Ltd. agreed to conduct joint aerospace research.

Meanwhile, Mitsubishi Motors confirmed it is looking into the possibility of a joint venture with Chrysler Corp. in Europe to produce autos. But the company said this is only one of several possible ventures it is exploring with other companies, including Volvo AB of Sweden and Renault of France. Mitsubishi would be the last of the major Japanese auto makers to establish a production base in Europe, and many analysts expect the company to announce a venture later this year.

Daimler is said to be mapping out plans for growth ahead of the scheduled unification of markets by the end of 1992 by members of the European Community, and cooperation with the Mitsubishi group could be part of these plans.
