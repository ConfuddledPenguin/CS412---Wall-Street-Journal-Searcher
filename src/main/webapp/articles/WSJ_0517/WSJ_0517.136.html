Business Brief -- ICH Corp.: Company Expects to Post A Profit for First Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/17/90

ICH Corp. said it expects to report a profit for the first quarter compared with a net loss of $10.7 million, or 37 cents a share, a year earlier. The Louisville, Ky.-based insurance holding company said release of first-quarter results has been delayed because the company didn't receive financial statements in time from First Executive Corp., in which ICH has about a 20% stake, or from the purchaser of Philadelphia Life and the Denver subsidiaries that ICH sold at the end of the first quarter.

ICH said it can't be certain that its outlook is accurate until it has fully reviewed that financial information.

The company said health-insurance operations "have had improved levels of policy renewals at rates that have resulted in a somewhat improved ratio of losses to premiums. As a result, those operations achieved higher revenues and improved margins, and are ahead of our internal expectations for this year."

In composite trading yesterday on the American Stock Exchange, ICH closed at $3.375, unchanged.
