Tucson Electric Co. Might Have to Seek Bankruptcy-Law Aid

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/17/90

TUCSON, Ariz. -- Tucson Electric Co. raised the possibility that it might be forced to seek bankruptcy-law protection from its creditors if various financing plans aren't successful. In a regulatory filing, the company said its "immediate cash requirements would exceed available credit under existing bank lines" if some proceeds of certain securities offerings aren't received or other financing alternatives aren't completed.

The company estimated that an additional $120 million will be needed to meet its cash requirements through year end.

The bankruptcy threat wasn't mentioned at the company's annual meeting yesterday, where angry shareholders called for the resignation of company directors and grilled executives about the company's failed investments and depressed stock price.

In an interview after the meeting, Charles Bayless, chief financial officer, said the company disclosed the bankruptcy possibility upon legal advice, but doesn't think it is a serious possibility. The company is "more confident now" of obtaining adequate credit lines than earlier this year, he said. Tucson Electric also indicated that it plans to ask state authorities for a rate increase of about 10% by early summer.

In January, Tucson Electric eliminated its quarterly dividend on common stock based on its lack of earnings and inadequate cash flows. Last year, the company had a net loss of $82.1 million, or $3.55 a share, on revenue of $593.7 million.
