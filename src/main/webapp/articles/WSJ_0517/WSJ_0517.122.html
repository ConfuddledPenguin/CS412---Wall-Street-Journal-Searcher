Business Brief -- Westinghouse Electric Corp.: Nuclear Robotic Technology 3-Way Partnership Formed

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/17/90

Westinghouse Electric Corp., Pittsburgh, said it formed a partnership to develop robotic technology for use in the nuclear-service and nuclear-waste markets. The company said its partners in the venture are Carnegie Mellon University and RedZone Robotics Inc., both of Pittsburgh. RedZone, which originated at Carnegie Mellon, is a privately held robotics designer and fabricator.

Under the contract, the field robotics center at Carnegie Mellon will perform basic research and RedZone will develop concepts to the application stage. The partnership won't be capitalized, officials at Carnegie Mellon and RedZone said. But Carnegie Mellon will maintain a financial interest in the technologies developed at the university.

In addition to its interests in waste management, Westinghouse is a defense electronics, electrical equipment distribution, broadcasting and financial services concern.
