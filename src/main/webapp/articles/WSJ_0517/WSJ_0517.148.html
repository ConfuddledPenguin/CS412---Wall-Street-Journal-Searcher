International Brief -- Veba AG: West German Firm's Profit For 1st Quarter Rose 5.3%

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/17/90

Veba AG, a West German energy and chemical concern, said in an interim report that first-quarter profit climbed 5.3% to 237 million marks ($144.2 million) from 225 million marks a year earlier. Sales climbed 10% to 13.15 billion marks from 11.93 billion marks.

Pretax profit climbed 6.3% to 605 million marks from 569 million marks.

The company said higher volume and prices in its mineral oil division led to a "considerable" increase in that area. Oil sales climbed 9.2% to 2.53 billion marks from 2.32 billion marks a year earlier; chemical sales jumped 16% to 2.62 million marks from 2.27 million marks; and electricity sales increased 4.5% to 2.89 billion marks from 2.76 billion marks.

Veba said earnings in its electricity, trading, transportation and services divisions rose slightly from a year earlier, but weren't able to equal "the high year-earlier level" in its oil and chemical division. It didn't break down first-quarter earnings by division.
