Turkey's Foreign Trade Gap

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/17/90

ANKARA, Turkey -- Turkey's foreign trade deficit widened to $527 million in February from gaps of $91 million a year earlier and $504 million in January, the state Statistics Institute said. In February, exports on a value basis fell 1% to $988 million from $997 million in January, and rose 3.7% from $953 million a year earlier. Imports totaled $1.52 billion in February, up slightly from $1.5 billion a month earlier and up 45% from $1.04 billion a year earlier.

In the first two months of this year, the trade deficit widened to $1 billion from $383 million a year earlier. Exports totaled $1.99 billion, up 15% from $1.72 billion a year earlier, and imports registered $3.02 billion, up 43% from $2.12 billion.
