What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/17/90

CONSUMER PRICES ROSE 0.2% in April, indicating retail inflation has slowed from its rapid pace at the beginning of the year. But many analysts said the figures suggest little progress is being made in reducing the nation's core inflation rate. Meanwhile, housing starts tumbled 5.8% last month, another sign that the economy remains sluggish. ---

Deficit projections by the White House have grown so large that there is now concern that meeting Gramm-Rudman deficit targets may do more harm than good to the economy. Bush said paring the deficit by more than 1% of GNP would risk slowing the economy further.

---

The FAA ordered airlines to inspect pilot controls on three models of Boeing's popular 737 jetliner. The move came after a missing cotter pin caused a control wheel to come off in a co-pilot's hands during a flight.

---

The stock rally stalled amid uncertainty about how much farther interest rates will decline. The Dow Jones industrials closed off 2.77, at 2819.68, in active trading. Long-term bonds eased, and the dollar was mixed.

---

Circle K plans to revamp its product mix and pricing to lure back former customers and attract new ones. The convenience-store chain filed for Chapter 11 protection late Tuesday after failing to get a pact with creditors to restructure debt.

---

A First Executive insurance unit had such a sloppy accounting system for several years that regulators had trouble confirming the unit's billions of dollars in insurance reserves, New York regulators said in a report that resulted in a recent fine.

---

A major food-labeling bill moved closer to congressional approval as a House panel brushed aside the objections of many food-trade groups.

---

Farm policy makers in Congress are rushing to pass a farm bill before the budget summit agrees on any major cuts in crop subsidies.

---

The Big Board is expected to nominate Morgan Stanley's chairman, S. Parker Gilbert, as vice chairman of the stock exchange, sources said.

---

Kay Jewelers' lenders appear to be losing faith in the financially troubled jewelry retailing chain. Banks have already pulled $16.5 million in lines of credit to the company and would like to withdraw $32 million more.

---

Western Union posted sharply wider losses and lower revenue for the first quarter. The company has been struggling to avoid bankruptcy.

---

Landmark Land is exploring a possible sale of the company, one week after a $967 million land sale was thwarted by thrift regulators.

---

G. Heileman Brewing filed a complaint with the U.S. government against Canadian import restrictions against American beer.
