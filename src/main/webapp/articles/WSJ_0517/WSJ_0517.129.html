Business Brief -- Baltimore Bancorp.: Bank Concern Spurns Offer From Unit of Allied Irish

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/17/90

Baltimore Bancorp. rejected an unsolicited offer, valued at $217 million, from First Maryland Bancorp, a unit of Allied Irish Banks PLC. In rebuffing the $17-a-share offer, the Baltimore-based bank said its financial advisers had determined the offer was inadequate.

In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange yesterday, Baltimore shares closed at $14, down 87.5 cents.

The rejection by Baltimore Bancorp. thwarts AIB's plans to expand its U.S. presence. Officials at First Maryland declined to comment until they have received Baltimore's formal response to their offer.

Even before Baltimore's board met late yesterday afternoon to discuss First Maryland's offer, Harry Robinson, Baltimore's chairman and chief executive officer, told shareholders at the company's annual meeting that the bank was not for sale.

Baltimore also said there was no assurance that an agreement on a merger transaction could ever be reached because the proposal was conditioned on a due-diligence investigation by Allied Irish and its advisers. The process would have required Baltimore to open its books and records to First Maryland, a major competitor.
