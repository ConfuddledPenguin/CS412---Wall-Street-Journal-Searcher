Hong Kong-China Trade Links

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/17/90

HONG KONG -- More than half of this colony's imports and exports to China last year were related to outward processing, according to a government survey released Wednesday, providing the freshest evidence of the degree to which Hong Kong's economy is integrated with the mainland. The study, which comes less than three weeks before President Bush is to decide whether to renew China's most-favored-nation trade status, indicates that 53% of the colony's 1989 exports to China, or 76.87 billion Hong Kong dollars (US$9.87 billion) out of a total of HK$145.05 billion, are sent for processing and then reimported into the colony.

Business and industrial groups in Hong Kong have argued that the colony would be badly hurt if the U.S. were to rescind its most favorable import duties on Chinese merchandise because of the local economy's mounting links with southern China. President Bush has until June 3 to make the decision, which could be rejected by Congress.
