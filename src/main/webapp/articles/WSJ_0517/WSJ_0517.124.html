Horsham Stock Is Sold By Company's Partners In Clark Oil & Refining

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/17/90

TORONTO -- Horsham Corp. said its partners in Clark Oil & Refining Corp. sold two million subordinate voting shares of Horsham yesterday, or about a 2.6% stake. The shares, crossed on the Toronto Stock Exchange, were sold by Paul Anthony Novelly and Samuel R. Goldstein, president and chairman respectively of Apex Oil Co., a Horsham official said. Apex Oil holds a 40% stake in Clark Oil. Horsham acquired the other 60% of Clark Oil from Apex Oil in late 1988.

Paul Melnuk, Horsham's chief financial officer, said he didn't know who bought the shares. He said Mr. Novelly and Mr. Goldstein have sold about 3.5 million Horsham subordinate voting shares in the last few months and currently hold about 5 million of the shares.

Horsham is controlled by its chairman and chief executive, Peter Munk, who holds all of the company's multiple voting shares and about an 83% voting stake.

Mr. Melnuk said Apex Oil, of St. Louis is on the verge of coming out of U.S. Chapter 11 bankruptcy proceedings. "Presumabley {Mr. Novelly and Mr. Goldstein} just needed some liquidity" to help finance Apex Oil's reorganization, Mr. Melnuk said.

Messrs. Novelly and Goldstein couldn't be reached for comment.

In composite trading yesterday on the New York Stock Exchange, Horsham closed at $8.25, up 37.5 cents.
