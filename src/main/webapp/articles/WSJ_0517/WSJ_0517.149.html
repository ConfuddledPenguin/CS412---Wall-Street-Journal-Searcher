International Brief -- Deutsche Bank AG: Group Operating Earnings Climbed in First Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/17/90

First-quarter group operating profit at Deutsche Bank AG, West Germany's largest banking company, climbed 24% and the parent bank's operating earnings rose 20%, Chairman Hilmar Kopper said at the annual meeting in Essen. Partial operating profit, excluding earnings from trading for the concern's own account, climbed 16% in the group and 14% in the parent bank, Mr. Kopper said. He didn't provide specific figures.

"The demand for credit at our domestic and foreign branches and subsidiaries progressed at a lively rate," Mr. Kopper said. He said that with "stable interest rate margins," the parent bank's interest income climbed 8.6%, boosted by a 9.7% rise in business volume.

In contrast to several other West German banks, Deutsche Bank's conservative funding structure, in which it financed long-term lending activities with long-term funds, made the bank less vulnerable to the February rise in German interest rates on fears that monetary union with East Germany would damage the West German economy and ignite inflation.
