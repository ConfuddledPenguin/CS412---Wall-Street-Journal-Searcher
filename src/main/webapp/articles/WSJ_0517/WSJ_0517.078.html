North Canadian Oils To Seek Full Control Of Its Bankeno Unit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/17/90

CALGARY, Alberta -- North Canadian Oils Ltd. said it plans an offer to acquire the 13.5% of Bankeno Resources Ltd. that it doesn't already own in a transaction valued at about 18 million Canadian dollars (US$15.3 million). If successful with its planned C$4.25-per-share offer, North Canadian said it will merge with Bankeno. Both companies are Calgary-based oil and gas producers.

North Canadian said it expects to mail its offer next week. Grant Billing, North Canadian's senior vice president, said the company hopes to complete the purchase by June, subject to government and regulatory approvals.

Mr. Billing is also president of Bankeno, which said separately that its board plans to recommend acceptance of the offer. In Toronto Stock Exchange trading yesterday Bankeno closed at C$4.20, up C$1.35 a share, and North Canadian closed at C$18.125, unchanged.

North Canadian's announcement follows its proposal Tuesday to restructure the debt of Coseka Resources Ltd., of which it owns 47.6%, and ultimately merge with it.

Pending the outcome of talks with Coseka's lenders, who collectively hold about C$165 million of debt, North Canadian hopes also to conclude a merger with Coseka by June, said G. Barry Padley, senior vice president, finance.

Mr. Padley declined to offer details of the proposed Coseka debt restructuring, or merger. But he said it would largely involve cash. North Canadian had cash and short-term investments of about C$250 million at the end of 1989.
