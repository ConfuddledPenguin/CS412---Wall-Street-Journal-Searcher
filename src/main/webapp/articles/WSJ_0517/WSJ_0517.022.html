Varity Corp. Seeks To Join Operations Of K-H Corp. Units

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/17/90

TORONTO -- Varity Corp. said it proposed to amalgamate the operations of Kelsey-Hayes Canada and its Kelsey-Hayes unit in a transaction valued at about C$36 million. Varity said it plans to offer the minority shareholders of Kelsey-Hayes Canada either C$20 (US$17.01) a share or a yet to be determined number of Varity common shares for their holdings in the Canadian unit.

At last report, minority shareholders own about about 1.8 million shares of Kelsey-Hayes Canada, or about 27.3% of the total number of shares outstanding.

A spokesman for Varity said the proposal is "a logical extension" of Varity's acquisition last year of Detroit-based K-H Corp., the parent of Kelsey-Hayes Canada.

Varity said it has retained Gordon Capital Corp. to review the proposed amalgamation and to provide a fairness opinion on the value of the Varity shares to be offered in exchange for Kelsey-Hayes Canada shares.

The companies said they expect that the amalgamation couldn't occur before August.

At least two-thirds of the shareholders of Kelsey-Hayes Canada would need to approve the amalgamation at a special meeting. Varity said Kelsey-Hayes Co. would vote its 72.7% interest in favor of the amalgamation.

Kelsey-Hayes Canada has formed a committee of independent directors to review the proposal.
