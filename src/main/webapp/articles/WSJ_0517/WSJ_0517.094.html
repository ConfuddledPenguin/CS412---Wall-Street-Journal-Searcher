New Jersey Hands PPG $31.5 Million Cleanup Bill

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/17/90

TRENTON, N.J. -- The New Jersey Department of Environmental Protection said it ordered PPG Industries Inc. to pay more than $31.5 million to clean up 32 residential lots in Jersey City. The state agency said it believes PPG, based in Pittsburgh, is responsible for chromium contamination at the lots.

A spokesman at the maker of glass, chemical and coatings said, "We haven't seen the directive, so we can't comment."

The state agency said it plans to begin cleanup work at the sites this fall. If the company fails to reimburse the state for the cost of the cleanup, PPG will be liable for triple damages, Commissioner Judith Yaskin said in a release.

PPG, according to the agency, operated a chromium chemical plant in Jersey City for more than 50 years until it was closed in 1964. However, chromium waste from the plant was used as fill material at many locations in Jersey City, including the residential lots slated for cleanup, the state agency said.
