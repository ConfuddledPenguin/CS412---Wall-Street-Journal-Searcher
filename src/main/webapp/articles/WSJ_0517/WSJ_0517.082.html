Technology: Chip Sales Are Seen Flat In 1990 Before Growing

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/17/90

CUPERTINO, Calif. -- World-wide semiconductor sales are expected to remain flat this year compared with 1989, before growing at a double-digit rate through 1993, the Semiconductor Industry Association said. The trade group projected semiconductor sales would reach $48.4 billion in 1990, down nearly 1% from 1989 sales of $48.8 billion. In 1991, sales are projected at $54.5 billion, a 12.8% increase from this year; in 1992, sales are expected to total $65.2 billion; in 1993, sales are estimated at $74.7 billion, the trade group said.

"For the most part, all market segments should experience double-digit growth in 1991, 1992, and 1993," said Doug Andrey, the trade group's director of industry statistics. "Substantial growth in the MOS Logic and MOS Memory markets will be the key factors for these increases."

The market growth takes into account a stable U.S. dollar exchange rate through the balance of the forecast, the trade group said.
