Japan's Crude Steel Output

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/17/90

TOKYO -- Production of crude steel in Japan in April increased 0.2% to 9.1 million metric tons from a year earlier, the Japan Iron and Steel Federation said. A metric ton is equal to about 2,204.62 pounds. April production fell 0.7% from 9.16 million tons in March, when output was at record levels. Japanese steelmakers increased their output in the month to meet continuing demand from the construction sector. Demand for steels used in construction expanded in April, compared with a year earlier.

Strong capital investment by the private sector in Japan is underpinning growth in basic industries such as steel. Demand from other areas, such as the consumer goods industry, also has kept production high in the past year.

As of April, the Japanese economy had been expanding for 41 consecutive months, the third-longest period of continuous expansion in the nation's history.
