Who's News: James Doty Appointed SEC General Counsel

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/17/90

WASHINGTON -- The Securities and Exchange Commission named James Doty as general counsel, one of the agency's most important slots. Mr. Doty will succeed Daniel Goelzer, who recently announced plans to leave the SEC later this month after 16 years at the agency. Mr. Goelzer, who was general counsel for seven years, will join the Washington office of the law firm Baker & McKenzie.

Mr. Doty, 50 years old and a friend of Chairman Richard Breeden, recently joined the SEC as counselor to the chairman. Before joining the SEC, he was a partner in the Washington office of the law firm Baker & Botts, where he specialized in securities law. Mr. Doty is a graduate of Rice University and Yale Law School.

The general counsel is the SEC's chief legal officer, representing the commission in litigation at the appellate level and higher. The counsel is also the agency staffer charged with interpreting federal securities laws.
