Business Brief -- Chyron Corp.: Net Loss of $2 Million Reported for Third Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/17/90

Chyron Corp., a Melville, N.Y., manufacturer of television graphics and special-effects equipment, reported a $2 million fiscal third-quarter net loss on a 36% sales drop. Hurting results in the quarter were the company's halting of a deferred-payment plan for dealers and customers, expenses from unsuccessful merger discussions, and an unprofitable subsidiary.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Chyron shares fell 25 cents to $1.75.

Adjusted for a 2% stock dividend paid March 1, the loss for the quarter ended March 31 equaled 17 cents a share, compared with net income of $112,000, or one cent a share, in the year-earlier period. Sales sank to $7.7 million from $11.9 million.

Alfred O.P. Leubert, chairman and chief executive officer, said that effective Jan. 1, the company stopped offering extended payment terms. Dealers now will pare inventories and order products as they need them. "We see the loss of sales as temporary," he said.
