Marketing Brief -- Westwood One Inc.: Loss of $3.8 Million Posted For Fiscal Second Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/03/90

Westwood One Inc. said its net loss in the second quarter ended May 31 narrowed 42% to $3.8 million, or 26 cents a share, from $6.6 million, or 45 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue increased 27% to $37.4 million from $29.5 million. Operating profit was $1.7 million compared with an operating loss of $3.2 million a year earlier.

In a statement, William Battison, president and chief financial officer, attributed the narrowed losses to added productivity in "turnaround properties" acquired in the past three years. He said the progress occurred despite increased interest expense and good will amortization as a result of these acquisitions.

Westwood One, based in Culver City, Calif., produces and distributes radio programming that owns and operates the Mutual Broadcasting System and NBC Radio Networks and owns or has interests in several radio stations.
