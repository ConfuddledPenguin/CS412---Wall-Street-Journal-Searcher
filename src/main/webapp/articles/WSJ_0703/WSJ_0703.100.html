Marketing Brief -- Heritage Media Corp.: Legal Dispute May Affect Bid for Rest of POP Radio

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/03/90

Heritage Media Corp. of Dallas said a continuing legal dispute could affect its $39 million offer to acquire the 43% of New York-based POP Radio Corp. that it doesn't already own. The owner of television and radio stations said it has been informed that Thomas Dilk, a former POP Radio employee and a plaintiff in one of two class action suits whose settlement was a condition of the acquisition, hasn't withdrawn his assertions that his attorney wasn't authorized to enter the settlement agreement. The class action involving Mr. Dilk arose from an earlier acquisition proposal by Heritage, the company said.

Heritage said it believes the situation involves a dispute between Mr. Dilk and his attorney. The company hasn't concluded what action it will take in response to the situation. Heritage said its current acquisition offer expires Oct. 31.

POP Radio provides customized radio programs.
