Technology Brief -- Biocontrol Technology Inc.: Company Successfully Tests A Vascular Access Device

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/03/90

Biocontrol Technology Inc. said it concluded a successful clinical trial on animals of its vascular access device. The Indiana, Pa.-based company, a maker and developer of biomedical devices, said it plans to apply to the Food and Drug Administration immediately for approval to begin clinical studies in humans.

Biocontrol's vascular access device, known as the Bio-Port, is a drug delivery receptacle that is implanted beneath the skin. The device eliminates skin and blood vessel damage when repeated injections are necessary, such as in chemotherapy treatment.

The company said its market research indicates a $50 million annual market for these devices world-wide.
