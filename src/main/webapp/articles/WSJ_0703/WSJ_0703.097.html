Business Brief -- First Florida Banks Inc.: Price for Mid-State Federal Is Reduced to $48 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/03/90

First Florida Banks Inc. said it signed a new merger pact with Mid-State Federal Savings Bank that reduces the purchase price for Mid-State to $48 million from up to $59.7 million under an earlier pact. Tampa, Fla.-based First Florida said an examination of Mid-State by federal regulators showed Mid-State has inadequate loan-loss reserves -- a discovery that was adverse enough to permit First Florida to void the earlier merger pact.

Terms of the new agreement call for shareholders of Ocala, Fla.-based Mid-State to receive $22.43 a share; the earlier agreement called for its holders to get $25.07 a share and up to an additional $2.34 a share contingent on Mid-State's disposal of certain assets.

First Florida said the shortfall in Mid-State's reserves surfaced when the Office of the Comptroller of the Currency concluded Mid-State should have reserves of $8 million to $9 million instead of $1.3 million.

A special meeting of Mid-State shareholders to vote on the new pact is expected to be held in September or October.
