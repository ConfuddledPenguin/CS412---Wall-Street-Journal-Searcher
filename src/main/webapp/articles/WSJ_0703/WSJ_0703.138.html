What's News -- World-Wide

  • Date: 07/03/90

GORBACHEV DEFENDED his policies as a Soviet party congress got under way. The Soviet president faced down mounting criticism from left and right with a call for unity at the opening of the Communist Party forum in Moscow. He rejected conservative criticism of his social and economic restructuring as "nonsense." The situation "dictates the imperative need to accelerate and radicalize economic reform," Gorbachev declared. The 4,700 delegates to the congress are split over how the party can retain power and prevent public support from eroding.

Lithuania's premier said the Kremlin has lifted its economic blockade completely following the republic's decision to suspend its independence bid.

IMELDA MARCOS WAS ACQUITTED of all charges in her racketeering trial.

A federal jury in New York acquitted the former Philippine first lady of charges that she used $200 million allegedly stolen from the Filipino government to buy jewelry, artwork and real estate in the U.S. The jury also found Adnan Khashoggi innocent of the charges against him. The Saudi businessman was accused of helping to conceal ownership of Marcos property. The lack of evidence -- and doubts that a U.S. court was the proper venue -- hurt the prosecutors' case, jurors said.

Marcos backers immediately claimed the verdict as a victory not only for her, but as a vindication of her late husband, Ferdinand, who died in September.

ABOUT 1,400 MOSLEMS DIED in a stampede of pilgrims in the city of Mecca.

The people suffocated or were trampled in a pedestrian tunnel leading to the Saudi Arabian city, witnesses said. They said the stampede began when some pilgrims stopped in the air-conditioned tunnel and there was a rush outside of people pushing to escape the 112-degree heat. They also said the ventilation system appeared to fail. According to one diplomat, most of the victims were Malaysians and Indonesians.

The stampede shattered what had been a peaceful observance of the annual Hajj, or pilgrimage, and was the worst incident in recent years.

---

East German factory workers staged a series of warning strikes after the weekend economic merger with West Germany. The protests were said to be aimed at getting better salaries and working conditions, but some workers were also hoping to apply pressure to prevent their plants from having to shut down.

---

Bush met with aides in Maine to prepare for a NATO summit he said will bring changes but no "bombshell" in the alliance's military strategy. The president is expected to urge NATO to overhaul its policy for repelling a Soviet attack by making the use of nuclear arms an unlikely last resort. The London summit begins Thursday.

---

Rebels surrounded Monrovia, cutting all major land routes out of the Liberian capital and severing telephone and telex links, according to guerrilla sources. Water and electricity supplies to the city were halted last week after the insurgent forces captured a waterworks and hydroelectric dam.

---

NASA engineers are focusing on a small misalignment of fuel tanks on two space shuttles as they search for hydrogen leaks that have grounded the shuttle fleet. The mystery of the leaks is throwing the shuttle schedule into disarray and is threatening to delay at least two missions that are planned this summer, according to NASA officials.

---

Mandela addressed the Irish Parliament, alluding to Ireland's fight against "unrelenting tyranny" to gain independence from Britain in the 1920s. The black leader also asked British leaders to follow the example of the Pretoria government and not require a truce before talking with the Irish Republican Army.

---

Panama has filed 16 criminal charges against toppled dictator Noriega, including charges of murder, torture, human-rights violations and drug trafficking, authorities said. Noriega was ousted by a December U.S. invasion and has been awaiting trial in Miami on drug-trafficking charges.

---

Israel's Shamir appealed to Egyptian President Mubarak to take the initiative and work for Mideast peace. The Israeli prime minister's statement, made in Jerusalem to a delegation sponsored by U.S. lobbyists for Israel, coincided with an Israel Radio report that Bush had written to Mubarak urging an Israeli-Egyptian summit meeting.

---

Yugoslavia splintered further as the republic of Slovenia adopted a declaration on full state sovereignty and another republic, Serbia, battled an independence declaration by ethnic Albanians in Kosovo province.

---

Provincial authorities will negotiate with Communist rebels in the Philippines rather than use military force to try to obtain the release of a U.S. Peace Corps member, according to local officials. The 26-year-old volunteer was kidnapped on June 13.
