Who's News: Hollingsworth Quits Top Job at Hercules; No Successor Named

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/03/90

WILMINGTON, Del. -- Hercules Inc. said its chairman and chief executive officer, David S. Hollingsworth, will retire in 1991, leaving open the question of who will succeed him. Mr. Hollingsworth, 62 years old, will be stepping down before the mandatory retirement age at Hercules of 65. A 42-year veteran of the chemicals and aerospace company, he took the top job in March 1987.

In a memo distributed to Hercules employees yesterday, Mr. Hollingsworth said he had accepted the chief executive's post with the understanding that he preferred to stay in the job for no more than four years.

The memo made no mention of recent difficulties at the company, including technical troubles and order delays in its rocket-booster operations, which led to a charge against earnings in 1989 of $221.9 million. The charge left the company with a loss for the year of $81.3 million, or $1.76 a share, on sales of $3.09 billion.

Stuart E. Eizenstat, a Washington lawyer who heads the Hercules board's nominating committee, issued a statement saying, "While the other members of the board and I may have wished that David had been willing to remain until age 65, we respect his wishes as expressed to us at the time of his selection" as chief executive.

Hercules didn't give any indication as to whether its 58-year-old president, Fred L. Bruckner, may be favored in the selection of a successor to Mr. Hollingsworth. Mr. Eizenstat said there are several candidates within Hercules, and he suggested that the board will also be looking outside the company by saying it "does not want to foreclose any options.'

The company disclosed Mr. Hollingsworth's plans shortly before the close of composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange, where Hercules shares rose 62.5 cents each to close at $34.
