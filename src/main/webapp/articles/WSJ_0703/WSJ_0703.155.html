Freddie Mac Sets Mortgage Program With Michigan

  • Author: Mary Lu Carnevale
  • Date: 07/03/90

Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. teamed with the state of Michigan, lenders, mortgage insurers and community groups to provide up to $500 million in loans for low-income and moderate-income home buyers. Freddie Mac figures the program will help up to 10,000 people buy homes in Michigan and will serve as a prototype for other states.

The program offers 30-year, fixed-rate, single-family mortgage loans with a minimum 5% down payment through participating lenders. Freddie Mac has agreed to buy $250 million of such loans over a two-year period beginning in October, and the state can extend the program for one year and expand Freddie Mac's purchases by an additional $250 million.

Under the program, borrowers whose incomes are close to or below the state's median family income and can't qualify for a conventional loan could spend up to 33% of their monthly income on housing. Current guidelines allow for 28% with a 5% down payment.

Also, up to 1% of the down payment can come from nonprofit community organizations or governmental or community agencies, and another 1% can come from "sweat equity" work performed before a buyer goes to closing.

The program also includes counseling for potential borrowers by community organizations that have been approved by Freddie Mac and the state. And Freddie Mac said borrowers without substantial credit histories can use records of regular payments to power companies, telephone companies, landlords and others. In addition, the program requires mortgage insurance on at least 75% of the value; Freddie Mac and Michigan said they would share losses that aren't covered by insurance.

Freddie Mac, a federally sponsored, publicly traded corporation based in Reston, Va., operates a secondary market in mortgage loans, buying loans from lenders and packaging them into securities for sale to investors.
