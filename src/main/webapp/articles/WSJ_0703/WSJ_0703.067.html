SEC Submits Plan For Paying Investors In Wang-Lee Case

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/03/90

WASHINGTON -- The Securities and Exchange Commission said it filed a plan in federal court for investors to receive more than $19 million in profits allegedly obtained by Stephen Sui-Kuan Wang Jr. and Chwan Hong Lee through insider trading. In a 1988 complaint, the SEC charged Messrs. Wang and Lee with violating insider-trading laws by trading in securities of at least 25 companies. The suit alleged that Mr. Wang obtained the nonpublic information while employed as an analyst at New York-based Morgan Stanley & Co., a unit of Morgan Stanley Group Inc.

Under the terms of a settlement of insider-trading charges with the two men, the SEC said it obtained full disgorgement of their allegedly illegal trading profits of more than $19 million.

The SEC's proposed plan calls for the money to be distributed to investors who sold securities in the companies named in the insider-trading suit on the same day or days that Messrs. Wang and Lee purchased the securities.

The settlement also called for Mr. Lee to pay a fine of $1,503,977, plus $4,560,472 in taxes owed from his allegedly illegal trading activity, the SEC said. The commission said Mr. Lee is now a fugitive from an indictment relating to the illegal activities alleged in the SEC suit.

Mr. Wang earlier plead guilty to three felony charges involving securities trading related to his employment at Morgan Stanley. He was sentenced to eight months in prison and has completed his sentence, the agency said.
