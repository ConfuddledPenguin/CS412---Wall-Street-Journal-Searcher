Kollmorgen to Buy Vernitron's Stake, Settle All Litigation

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/03/90

SIMSBURY, Conn. -- Kollmorgen Corp. said it agreed to buy hostile suitor Vernitron Corp.'s stake and to settle all litigation related to a proxy fight and failed merger accord. The settlement concludes Vernitron's effort to take over Kollmorgen, which began about 18 months ago. The two companies signed a standstill agreement prohibiting each of them from buying the other's securities, participating in proxy contests involving either company, or otherwise seeking to control or influence the management of either company for 10 years.

Kollmorgen, a maker of motors and electro-optical systems, purchased Vernitron's remaining 424,600 shares in Kollmorgen for $12.125 a share, or about $5.2 million. Vernitron at one point held 593,000 shares, or about 5.5% of the Kollmorgen common. It bought the shares at an average cost of $14.04 a share; average net proceeds of the sales were $11.76 a share, a Vernitron spokesman said.

In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange yesterday, Kollmorgen shares closed at $12, down 25 cents.

In addition, Kollmorgen paid Vernitron $2 million related to litigation and other disputes. Vernitron, a Deer Park, N.Y., closely held maker of motors and controls, had total expenses of about $20 million on the takeover fight, including the loss on the stock position, the Vernitron spokesman said.

The companies said in a joint statement that the settlement, which was "in the best interests of shareholders and other constituencies" of both companies, would avoid the expense and burden of litigation.

Vernitron had sought to take control of Kollmorgen through a proxy fight this year.
