International: Norway Oil Union Accepts Oslo's Back-to-Work Order

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 07/03/90

OSLO -- A Norwegian oil workers' union said it resolved to accept a government back-to-work order yesterday but did not exclude the possibility of wildcat strikes on offshore platforms. The central committee of the Norwegian Federation of Oil Workers advised 4,000 striking workers to return to work because compulsory arbitration ordered by the government earlier yesterday is legally binding, said deputy leader Oddleiv Tonnessen.

The union had considered defying the government order, claiming it violates international trade union agreements.

A union official, speaking unofficially, said unsanctioned wildcat strikes might break out at some offshore fields.

The 6,000 member union began shutting Norway's North Sea production of 1.63 million barrels a day of oil and 63 billion cubic feet a month of natural gas at midnight Saturday. Last-minute negotiations had failed to settle a new contract.
