New Securities Issues

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/03/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: MORTGAGES

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $1.3 billion in debentures, consisting of $700 million in new callable debentures due July 11, 1994, to yield 8.65% and $600 million of premium callable debentures due July 10, 2000, yielding 9.15%. The four-year issue, callable on or after July 11, 1992, was priced at par, with a spread of 0.32 percentage point above four-year Treasurys. The 10-year issue, callable on or after July 10, 1993, was also priced at par, with a spread of 0.74 percentage point above 10-year Treasurys. The 10-year issue has a redemption, or call, price of 104.575, the percentage of the principal amount redeemed equal to 100% plus one-half the coupon rate on the debentures plus accrued interest on the amount redeemed. The redemption price will decrease on each succeeding interest payment date as set forth in Fannie Mae's Supplement to the Guide to Debt Securities dated June 26, 1990.

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $350 million of real estate mortgage investment conduit securities is being offered in 10 classes by First Boston Corp. The offering, Series 1990-92, is backed by new stripped mortgage securities formed from Fannie Mae 9% securities. First Boston took a portion of the Class 1 principal-only strips and a portion of the Class 2 interest-only strips from the new trust SMBS 000082-CL and used them as collateral for the Remic. Yield and price details weren't immediately available.

EUROBONDS

Istituto Credito Oficial (Spain) -- 25 billion yen of 7% bonds due July 31, 1995, priced at 101 3/8 to yield 7.12% less fees via IBJ International Ltd. Fees 1 7/8.

Lavoro Bank Overseas N.V. (Italian parent) -- 300 million marks of floating-rate notes at the six-month London interbank offered rate, priced at par and due May 31, 1995, via Trinkaus & Burkhardt. First coupon is for four months' interest and will be four-month Libor. First Libor fixing July 27. Guaranteed by the parent, Banca Nazionale del Lavoro.

IBJ Leasing Co. (Japan) -- 50 million marks of 9 3/8% bonds due July 24, 1995, priced at 101 3/8 to yield 9.02%, via Industriebank von Japan (Deutschland) AG. Guaranteed by Industrial Bank of Japan Ltd.

Okobank (Finland) -- $60 million of 9 3/8% bonds due July 24, 1997, priced at 102 to yield 9.35% less fees via Fuji International Ltd. Fees 1 7/8.

ALSK-CGER Ifico (Belgian parent) -- Two billion Luxembourg francs of public bonds due July 31, 1996, priced at 101 1/2 via Banque International de Luxembourg. Subordinated guarantee from ALSK-CGER, Brussels.

Genfinance S.A. (Belgian parent) -- One billion Luxembourg francs of 9 1/2% bonds due Aug. 7, 1997, priced at 101 3/4 via Banque Generale du Luxembourg. Guaranteed by Generale Bank Brussels.

Parbel International Finance N.V. (French parent) -- One billion Luxembourg francs of 9 1/2% bonds due July 31, 1995, priced at 101.95 via lead manager Banque Paribas (Luxembourg) S.A. Guaranteed by Banque Paribas Belgium.

Vorarlberger Kraftwerke (Austria) -- 55 million Swiss francs of 7% privately placed notes due July 26, 1995, priced at 101 1/4 via Credit Suisse. Fees 1 1/2.
