National Education Says Insurer Will Pay Portion of Settlement

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/03/90

IRVINE, Calif. -- National Education Corp. said the liability insurer for its directors and officers agreed in principle to pay about $5.5 million of a $11.9 million settlement of several class-action lawsuits against the company. The shareholder suits alleged that National Education made misleading statements about its financial condition last year. Federal court approval of the settlement is expected in about 30 days, said a spokesman for the vocational training concern.

A private arbitrator also reached a decision concerning severance payments and other benefits claimed by the company's former chief executive officer, David Bright. The arbitrator had ruled that the dismissal of Mr. Bright wasn't "for cause" as defined by his contract.

National Education didn't disclose terms of the decision. The "net" effect of the insurance proceeds and the arbitration settlement will be a "small positive contribution" to results in the second quarter, the company said. The spokesman declined to say whether the company would report profit or loss in the period.
