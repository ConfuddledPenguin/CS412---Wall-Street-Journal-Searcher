HealthVest Agrees To Transfer Its Lease On Tennessee Facility

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/03/90

AUSTIN, Texas -- HealthVest, an ailing real estate investment trust, said it agreed to transfer the operating lease on Eastwood Medical Center to Regent Health Group Inc., a closely held hospital-management company. The Eastwood facility, a 243-bed hospital in Memphis, Tenn., will continue to be operated by Healthcare International Inc. until the new lease is signed. Signing, said HealthVest officials, is contingent on successful formation of a physician limited partnership and is expected by Aug. 31.

HealthVest had a net loss of $116.8 million, or $10.57 a share, for 1989, much of it the result of troubles at Healthcare International, which leases many of its properties. The two companies have had a standstill agreement for several months and have extended it to Oct. 15, 1990, according to HealthVest officials. Terms of the extension call for payments of $2.4 million a month by Healthcare plus net cash proceeds from its hospital in Tustin, Calif.

HealthVest also has a standstill agreement with its lenders that is to remain in effect until Dec. 14 while it tries to restructure about $252 million in debt.
