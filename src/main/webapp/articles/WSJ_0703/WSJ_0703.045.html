Atlantic Richfield Pays $800 Million To End Tax Case

  • Author: Pauline Yoshihashi
  • Date: 07/03/90

LOS ANGELES -- Atlantic Richfield Co. paid $800 million to the Internal Revenue Service to resolve a long-running dispute over windfall profit taxes on the company's Alaskan North Slope crude oil production from 1980 to 1985. The IRS had sought roughly $1.8 billion in back taxes and interest. It filed a "notice of proposed deficiency" in 1988 seeking $577 million plus interest for alleged unpaid taxes for the years 1980 through 1983. In April, the agency filed a similar notice for $212 million plus interest for 1984 and 1985.

An Arco spokesman said the payment won't have a material effect on the oil and energy company's earnings. At the end of 1989, the company's reserves included $756 million for unspecified deferred liabilities. The spokesman said the company set aside additional reserves in the first quarter that "more than cover" the $800 million payment.

The dispute involved a tax levied on crude oil above certain price levels starting in 1980. The agency and Arco disagreed on the proper method for constructing a taxable "wellhead" price for the crude, which requires certain assumptions about transportation costs and other factors. Determining that price was complicated, as most of the oil is produced and transported before it is sold.

An internal IRS document from 1987 indicated that the agency was studying whether several oil companies overstated costs of transporting oil to the continental U.S. and thus depressed the imputed value of the crude. The methods Arco used for several years were believed to result in a lower price than those used by other Alaska oil producers.

Arco maintained its calculations were appropriate but changed its method in early 1985 to a weighted average value reported by other northern Alaska oil producers. The Arco spokesman said the windfall tax ceased to apply with the plunge in oil prices in 1985, and the tax was repealed in 1988.

An IRS spokesman in Washington declined to discuss the issue, citing taxpayer confidentiality. Arco has settled a dispute with the Alaska Department of Revenue for production taxes.
