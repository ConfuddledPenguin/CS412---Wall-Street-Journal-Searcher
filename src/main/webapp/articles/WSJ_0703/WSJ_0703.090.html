Technology: Medicare Benefits Expand To Help Cover Pap Smears

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/03/90

WASHINGTON -- The Department of Health and Human Services said that Medicare benefits have been expanded to help pay for pap smears. Congress approved the new benefits last year in an effort to reduce the death rate from cervical cancer in women over 65 years old. Effective Sunday, Medicare benefits cover one pap smear every three years for most women, and more frequently for women with a high risk of cervical cancer. Forthcoming regulations will define the high-risk group. Medicare pays 80% of approved charges.

The cure rate for cervical cancer, which is treated with surgery and radiation, is relatively good if the disease is found early. Over the last 15 years, the incidence of invasive cervical cancer has fallen about 4% a year among women 65 and older, primarily because of increased use of pap smears.

But a study by the congressional Office of Technology Assessment earlier this year found room for improvement. Only about half the women over 65 had a pap smear within the last three years, and at least 25% of all elderly women had never had a pap smear. The American Cancer Society estimates that among women 65 and older, there will be 3,700 new cases of invasive cervical cancer this year and 2,500 deaths.

The government expects about 4.1 million claims for the new benefit in 1991, the first full year of coverage, at a cost of about $30 million.
