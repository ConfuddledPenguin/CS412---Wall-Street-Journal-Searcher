Brandon Securities Unit Is Expelled From NASD

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/03/90

NEW YORK -- The National Association of Securities Dealers expelled Brandon Securities & Investments Inc. and barred a principal of the firm from membership for allegedly raising more than $1.5 million from investors and then misapplying or misappropriating the funds. The NASD, the self-regulatory agency for the over-the-counter markets, also fined the North Providence, R.I., firm $1.5 million. Brandon, a subsidiary of Brandon Securities Inc., and the principal, Ernest E. Michaud of Kingston, R.I., consented to the sanctions without admitting or denying wrongdoing.

The NASD alleged that Mr. Michaud also supplied customers with fictitious statements containing false representations concerning their investments. Mr. Michaud also didn't respond when the NASD requested information, the agency charged.

Neither the firm nor Mr. Michaud could be reached for comment.

In a May 22 letter from the NASD's Boston office to its general counsel's office in Washington, the agency said, "Because of the egregious nature of the allegations in issue," the NASD "notified the U.S. Attorney's Office for the state of Rhode Island."

The NASD staff "is assisting the {Federal Bureau of Investigation} in relation to a criminal investigation," the letter said. "We are also assisting the state of Rhode Island Securities Division in their investigation."
