U.S., Britain Reach Accord For Expanded Air Services

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/03/90

WASHINGTON -- The U.S. and Britain have reached an agreement to expand air services, Transportation Secretary Samuel Skinner said. The agreement is an amendment to the bilateral pact that governs air service between the U.S. and Britain. The amendment, finalized Thursday, will increase the number of trans-Atlantic routes.

The new agreement makes permanent American Airlines's right to serve the Chicago-Manchester route. The unit of AMR Corp. has been serving the route under temporary authority, department officials said. In addition, the U.S. government may select airlines for two additional routes between the U.S. and non-London airports.

Britain may authorize a second airline for the London-Boston route now flown by British Airways, and open two routes between non-London airports and the U.S.
