Business Brief -- Great American Bank: San Diego Thrift Converts Into Federal Savings Bank

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/03/90

Great American Bank, attempting to combat its problems by any means possible, said it converted from a state chartered bank into a federal savings bank. The new name is Great American Bank, a Federal Savings Bank. Like many thrifts, San Diego-based Great American has been hurt by real estate losses, especially in Arizona. The conversion to a federal savings bank won't alter its operations, but it will eliminate state regulatory assessments that total about $500,000. Great American, the nation's seventh-largest thrift with about $15.4 billion of assets, would welcome any help: Federal regulators have given it until the end of the year to come up with $350 million or face conservatorship.

On Friday, the thrift announced that its board of trustees named Robert Kemper, 61 years old, to serve as its new chairman, chief executive officer and president. Mr. Kemper was scheduled to take over for retired chief executive Gordon Luce and former president Paul Lindland yesterday. The move was seen by industry analysts as a reflection of continuing regulatory pressure on Great American.
