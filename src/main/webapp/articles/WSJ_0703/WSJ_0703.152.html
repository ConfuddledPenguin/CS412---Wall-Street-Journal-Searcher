International Brief -- Broken Hill Proprietary Co.: Company Predicts Increase In Earnings for Fiscal Year

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/03/90

Broken Hill Proprietary Co. of Australia expects to increase earnings this fiscal year ending May 31, 1991, executives of the steel, oil and mining company said yesterday. Brian Loton, BHP's chief executive, said the company "is in excellent financial shape" and he predicts a "satisfactory" year.

Last week BHP reported that operating profit after tax rose 6.4% to 1.1 billion Australian dollars (US$872.1 million) in the fiscal year ended May 31 from A$1.04 billion a year earlier.

Mr. Loton said one reason for stronger profits this fiscal year is the expected start-up of BHP's 57.5%-owned Escondida copper mine in Chile. Development of the mine is ahead of schedule and it could start production in December.

"This mine will be one of the lowest cost operations in the world and will produce around 4% of the world's copper supplies. It is an extraordinarily large and rich deposit," Mr. Loton said.
