International Brief -- Tate & Lyle PLC: U.K. Sugar Firm Decides To Drop Bid for Berisford

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/03/90

Tate & Lyle PLC, a British sugar company, said it will not proceed to bid for Berisford International PLC, a British food, property and jewelry concern. In a statement, Tate and Lyle said its appraisal of some of Berisford's non-sugar businesses showed a takeover would be ill-advised. Berisford had no immediate comment on Tate & Lyle's decision.

Last month, Britain's Department of Trade and Industry ordered an investigation of the competitive impact of such a merger on the country's sugar industry. Berisford and Tate & Lyle together control about 95% of the country's sugar supplies.

A Tate & Lyle spokesman said the investigation "led Tate & Lyle to conclude that it didn't want to proceed with a bid for the company."

The inquiry also found that an acquisition of Berisford would jeopardize Tate & Lyle's cane sugar refining margins. A Tate & Lyle spokesman said a merger with Berisford, a large sugar beet refiner, might have led to an end to European Community and U.K. government payments, which support Tate & Lyle's cane sugar refining activities against competition in the EC.
