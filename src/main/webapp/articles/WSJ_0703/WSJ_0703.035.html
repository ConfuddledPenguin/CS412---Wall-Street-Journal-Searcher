Japan Trade Surpus Widens

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/03/90

TOKYO -- Japan's trade surplus widened to $2.79 billion for the first 20 days of June, from $2.63 billion in the year-earlier period, the Ministry of Finance said. Exports for the 20-day period rose 4.1% to $14.42 billion, from $13.85 billion a year ago. Imports rose 3.7% to $11.63 billion from $11.22 billion.

Japan's trade surplus contracted in May to $3.13 billion from $3.76 billion in the previous year.

Forecasting that the surplus will start to expand again soon, economists stressed in May that the yen's weakening in foreign-exchange markets was encouraging exports, while slower growth in the domestic economy was dampening imports.
