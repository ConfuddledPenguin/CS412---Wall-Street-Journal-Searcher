Business Brief -- Smith Corona Corp.: Connecticut Firm Expects `Small Loss' in 4th Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/03/90

Smith Corona Corp. said it expects to post a "small loss" for the fourth quarter ended June 30 and that officials will recommend a dividend cut. G. Lee Thompson, chairman and chief executive officer, said management will recommend that the quarterly dividend be reduced to about five cents a share after a scheduled 15-cent-a-share dividend is paid Friday.

The New Canaan, Conn., maker of portable electronic typewriters and personal word processors said the expected loss reflects "charges relating to inventory write-downs and nonrecurring provisions for major overhead expense reduction programs."

Smith Corona, which went public at $21 a share a year ago, has been reeling from a series of adverse developments, including a 51% decline in fiscal third quarter net income. The stock fell 87.5 cents yesterday to $5.375 in composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange. Hanson PLC of Britain holds 47.9% of Smith Corona.

In the year's first nine months, the company had net income of $34.4 million, or $1.14 a share.
