Who's News: Staloff, Ex-President Of Comex, Becomes Head of Bloom Staloff

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/03/90

PHILADELPHIA -- Arnold Staloff, former president of New York's Commodity Exchange Inc., became president and chief executive officer of a specialist and market making firm on the Philadelphia Stock Exchange. The company, renamed Bloom Staloff Corp., from Bloom & Co., was founded in 1985 by James Bloom, 46 years old, who becomes chairman of the firm. Mr. Staloff will hold a substantial equity interest in the new company.

Mr. Staloff, 45, was president of the Comex from August 1989 until his resignation in April. Previously, he was head of marketing and new product development at the Philadelphia exchange, and president of its Philadelphia Board of Trade subsidiary. Mr. Staloff began his career at the Securities and Exchange Commission in 1969.

During his 16 years with the Philadelphia exchange, Mr. Staloff played a key role in the introduction of foreign currency options and development of a stock index-like product called Cash Index Participations (CIPs) that became a bone of jurisdictional contention between the SEC and the Commodities Futures Trading Commission.

"For me this is an opportunity to enter an entrepreneurship mode and capitalize on my knowledge of the market," said Mr. Staloff. He also added that he is delighted to be re-entering the Philadelphia Stock Exchange community. He said that he hopes to resume working with the exchange and contribute ideas about new products through committee assignments.

The new firm, Bloom Staloff Corp., will continue the work of its predecessor, Bloom & Co., which was one of the largest specialist firms on the Philadelphia exchange.
