International Brief -- BASF AG: Two North American Lines Are Put on Auction Block

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/03/90

BASF AG, a West German chemical company, put its U.S. flavors and fragrances operating unit and its North American ink business up for sale, saying the non-core lines don't fit into its long-term growth strategy. BASF hired Wasserstein Perella & Co., New York, to find a buyer for the Fritzsche Dodge & Olcott unit, which develops, makes and markets products world-wide for the nutrition and cosmetics industries. It retained Morgan Stanley & Co., New York, to arrange the ink business's sale.

Fritzsche Dodge, acquired in 1980 from the family that founded it in 1798, has 850 employees, 550 of them in the U.S. BASF declined to disclose financial details, but Fritzsche Dodge's annual sales have been estimated at about $150 million. A spokesman in the U.S. said the divestiture proposal shouldn't affect the unit's capital spending plans of about $600 million this year, up from $460 million in 1989.

The printing ink operations in the U.S. and Canada, with about 1,400 employees, make and supply printing ink and auxiliary products to the publication, packaging and commercial printing industries.
