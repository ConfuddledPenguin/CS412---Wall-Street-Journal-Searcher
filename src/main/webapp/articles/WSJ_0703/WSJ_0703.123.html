Technology: Three Firms Explore Possibility of Team For Rocket Engine

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 07/03/90

Three rocket engine makers said they are exploring the possibility of forming a team to develop a propulsion system for a new family of launch vehicles designed to lift heavy payloads into space at a fraction of the current cost. Rockwell International Corp.'s Rocketdyne division, the Pratt & Whitney division of United Technologies Corp., and Aerojet, GenCorp's aerospace segment, said the team approach was requested by the National Aeronautics and Space Administration, the government agency overseeing the engine development program.

Rocketdyne's project marketing manager, David Wright, said that the team might become effective as early as Oct. 1 if an agreement can be reached on melding the companies' nine existing contracts for the project's initial phase. The next two phases, which he valued at $600 million to $700 million over five to six years, would begin in October 1991.

The companies said they will design an engine using liquid hydrogen and liquid oxygen propellants with a 580,000-pound thrust level. The new engine would be the first significant advance in rocket engine technology since the early 1970s, a Rocketdyne spokeswoman said.
