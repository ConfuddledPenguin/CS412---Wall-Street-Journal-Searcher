Business Brief -- Mattel Inc.: Mattel Expects to Report Higher 2nd-Quarter Profit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/03/90

Mattel Inc. said it expects to report earnings of about $19 million, or 38 cents a share, in the second quarter. A year earlier, the Los Angeles toy maker reported net income of $16.6 million, or 33 cents a share. Second-quarter sales, it's estimated, similarly climbed 16% to about $315 million, vs. $272 million a year earlier, in the face of a soft retail sales market.

John W. Amerman, chairman and chief executive officer, said the results are consistent with the company's projections of double-digit revenue growth for 1990. Mattel's stock fell $2.25 to close at $22.125 a share in composite New York Stock Exchange trading yesteday, as the projections are somewhat below what many analysts had been expecting.

Thomas Kully, an analyst with William Blair & Co. in Chicago, attributed part of the second-quarter softness to the weakness among large retailers, as well as an increasing shift to just-in-time inventory. Analysts said Mattel's stock also was affected by weak performances among its competitors, such as Hasbro Inc.'s recent announcement of an anticipated 50% drop in second-quarter profit.
