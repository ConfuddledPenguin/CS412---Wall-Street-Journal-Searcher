Business Brief -- Fluor Corp.: Iron Ore Processing Unit Sold to Big River Minerals

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/03/90

Fluor Corp., Irvine, Calif., said it sold its iron ore processing unit to St. Louis-based Big River Minerals Corp. for about $12 million, resulting in a one-time after-tax gain of less than 10 cents a share for the third quarter ending July 31. Pea Ridge Iron Ore Co. had been carried on Fluor's books as a discontinued operation since a 1987 reorganization when Fluor began entertaining offers to buy it. Fluor in recent years has sold off natural resources investments in gold, zinc, oil and copper, and is entertaining inquiries about its lead investments. Last March, Fluor said it had retained an investment banking firm to explore options for its Massey coal-producing unit.

The company, which earned $68 million or 40 cents a share, in the second quarter ended April 30, is focusing on its core business of engineering, construction and technical services.

Pea Ridge operates an iron ore mine and pelletizing plant with about 250 employees near Sullivan, Mo. Big River is a closely held investment company formed in 1988 by executives of former Fluor unit St. Joe's Minerals Corp., including former Fluor President John Wright. Big River produces coal, zinc, precious metals and iron ore.
