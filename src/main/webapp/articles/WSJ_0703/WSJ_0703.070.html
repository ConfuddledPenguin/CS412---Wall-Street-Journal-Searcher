Morton International Sells Stake in Venture To Japanese Partner

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/03/90

CHICAGO -- Morton International Inc. said it sold a 40% stake in Toray Thiokol Ltd. to Toray Industries Inc., its Japanese partner in the joint venture, for about $30 million. The maker of specialty chemicals and salt said it expects to report a gain of about $13 million, or 27 cents a share, on the sale. The move reduces Morton's holding in the venture to 5%. Morton's ownership of the Toray Thiokol stake dates to 1969, when the U.S. company was known as Thiokol Corp.; the company now known as Morton International was spun off by Thiokol Corp. in 1989.

A spokeswoman said the sale followed an "economic and strategic assessment" of the operation, and added that there are "no current plans" to sell the remaining 5% holding.

Morton also said it will record provisions totaling about $7 million, reducing earnings about 15 cents a share, "for additional anticipated costs related primarily to previously divested operations and to last year's separation from Thiokol Corp."

Both the sale and establishment of the partly offsetting provisions occurred in the fourth quarter ended Saturday; Morton said its earlier earnings projection of $2.60 to $2.80 a share for the fiscal year just ended didn't include the effect of either action. Taken together, the two moves would have an indicated net effect of boosting earnings about 12 cents a share and moving the projection's range to $2.72-$2.92 a share. The spokeswoman said the original range for its operating results still holds, exclusive of the unusual items.

In the first nine months, net income was $98.5 million, or $2.05 a share, on sales of $1.21 billion.
