Consumer Credit in Britain

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/03/90

LONDON -- New consumer credit extended by finance houses, building societies, other specialist credit grantors and retailers totaled a seasonally adjusted #3.97 billion ($6.95 billion) in May, up from #3.69 billion in April and #3.58 billion in May 1989, the Central Statistical Office said. The amount of consumer credit outstanding in May totaled #28.12 billion, compared with #27.79 billion in April and #25.49 billion in May 1989.

In the three months through May, new consumer credit extended rose 3% to #11.39 billion from #11.04 billion in the previous three months.

Fixed-sum lending to consumers was #1.55 billion in May, up from #1.43 billion in April and from #1.48 billion in May 1989. The amount of fixed-sum lending outstanding in May rose #196 million to #20.22 billion, compared with April's rise of #94 million to #20.03 billion.

Lending through bank credit cards rose to #2.29 billion in May from #2.19 billion in April and #2.02 billion in May 1989. The amount of credit card lending outstanding in May rose #77 million to #7.19 billion, compared with a rise in April of #39 million to #7.11 billion.
