Probe of Alleged Collusion By Airlines Is Broadened

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/03/90

WASHINGTON -- The Justice Department said it expanded its investigation into possible antitrust violations by major U.S. airlines. James Rill, the department's antitrust chief, said that prosecutors are investigating a pricing arrangement between two airlines whose names he declined to disclose.

The Justice Department is conducting four other investigations of possible anticompetitive activity in the airline industry. These involve the actions of major carriers that dominate certain large airports, known as hubs; the relationship between airlines and the industry's computerized clearinghouse for fares; possible collusion related to certain fare changes made in September 1989; and the buying and selling of slots at Chicago's O'Hare airport by United Airlines.

Individual airlines under Justice Department scrutiny have denied any wrongdoing.
