Bloomingdale's Stamford, Conn., Dallas Stores to Close

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/06/90

NEW YORK -- Bloomingdale's said it will close its branches in Dallas and Stamford, Conn. Chairman and Chief Executive Marvin Traub said the closings followed a routine review of the company's operations. The two stores generate about 4.5% of the 17-store chain's revenue.

According to an offering prospectus prepared last year by First Boston Corp., Bloomingdale's was expected to produce $107.5 million of earnings before interest and taxes in its fiscal year ending Jan. 31, on revenue of $1.3 billion. Bloomingdale's parent, Federated Department Stores Inc., a unit of Campeau Corp., filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection in January.

Retail experts say the decision to close the Dallas and Stamford stores makes business sense because sales were declining at both units. The Stamford store generated about $35 million in revenue for the fiscal year ended January 28, 1989; while the Dallas store produced about $30 million in sales during that period.

The Dallas store opened in 1983 with much fanfare, but it never really caught on with local shoppers. Over the last three years, revenue declined more than 11%. Bloomingdale's opened its Stamford store in l954. Since l985, Bloomingdale's revenue at Stamford has declined one or two percentage points.

Gordon Cooke, executive vice president for sales promotion at Bloomingdale's, said that the chain doesn't have plans to close additional stores.
