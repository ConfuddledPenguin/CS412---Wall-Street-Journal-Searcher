Marketing & Media: New York City Council Bill Would Ban Free Cigarettes

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/06/90

NEW YORK (AP) -- The City Council voted 33-to-0 yesterday to prevent tobacco companies from distributing free cigarettes to potential customers or smokers. The main target, according to the bill's sponsor, Sal Albanese, (D., Brooklyn), was to stop people age 18 or younger from being tempted to smoke. Mr. Albanese said cigarettes are distributed free on street corners and that youngsters can get them.

"In ending this reprehensible practice," the Brooklyn legislator said, "New York City will not only become the largest jurisdiction in the nation to ban the free distribution of tobacco products to the public, but as large as the next six jurisdictions combined."

Mr. Albanese said at least 15 other cities, including Boston, Atlanta, Cincinnati and Austin, Texas, also bar the free distribution of cigarettes.

He said the legislation would bar individuals and businesses from distributing tobacco products in public places or at public events for free or at any price less than the combined cost of the required taxes.

The bill still requires the approval of Mayor David Dinkins.
