Justice Department Decides It Won't Join Suit Against Hughes

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/06/90

LOS ANGELES -- The U.S. Justice Department decided it wouldn't join a lawsuit against Hughes Aircraft Co. brought by a Hughes employee who accused the company of overcharging the government $50 million on development of radar systems for military aircraft. The suit was filed in federal court in Los Angeles last year by William J. Schumer, a former contracts manager for Hughes's radar systems group. Mr. Schumer is currently assistant contracts manager for Hughes missile systems group.

The suit alleged that between 1983 and 1988, Hughes shifted costs from contracts for the F-15 jet fighter to contracts for similar work on the B-2 stealth bomber and the F-18 jet fighter. Hughes is a unit of General Motors Corp.

Hughes has denied any wrongdoing. The suit is the third filed against Hughes under the False Claims Act that the government has declined to join since February. The government hasn't yet taken any action on a fourth such suit filed against the company last year, Hughes said.

Dean Francis Pace, a Los Angeles attorney for Mr. Schumer, said he hadn't been informed of the government action.
