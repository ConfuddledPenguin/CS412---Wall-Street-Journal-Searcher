International Brief -- Skandinaviska Enskilda Banken: Agreement Set to Acquire Rest of Scandinavian Bank

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/06/90

Skandinaviska Enskilda Banken, Stockholm, agreed to acquire the 78.3% of its London-based affiliate, Scandinavian Bank Group, that it doesn't already own, the latest step in the big Swedish bank's drive to expand in European investment banking and fund management. SE Banken said it will pay #152.7 million ($225.8 million) for the rest of Scandinavian Bank, valuing the commercial and merchant bank at #195 million. Sellers are Den norske Bank of Norway and Union Bank of Finland, each with a 31% stake; Unibank of Denmark, 12.5%; and Landsbanki of Iceland, 3.8%. The five banks took Scandinavian Bank private last December in a deal that valued it at #179 million.

Scandinavian Bank, which had shifted to investment banking and international lending, had a 1989 pretax loss of #3.2 million, its second consecutive deficit. But its attraction is its international network, bringing SE Banken offices in Sydney, Australia, and Brazil, and a potential jewel, a Swiss unit, Banque Scandinave en Suisse, in which Chicago-based Northern Trust Corp. holds a 21% minority interest.
