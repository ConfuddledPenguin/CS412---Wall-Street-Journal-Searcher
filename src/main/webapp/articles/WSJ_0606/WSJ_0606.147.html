FDIC Again Rejects Offer for Its 26% Stake In Continental Bank

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/06/90

WASHINGTON -- The Federal Deposit Insurance Corp. said it rejected an offer for its 26% stake in Continental Bank Corp. made by private investors, Continental and some of its executives. The offer, a Continental official said, was made at about $15 a share, or near market price last week, for a total of more than $200 million for the equivalent of 14.2 million shares that the FDIC owns.

The FDIC said it "remains committed to realizing full value for its holdings, and will explore all options for divesting its ownership in the company."

The FDIC became a shareholder of Chicago-based Continental as part of the agency's 1984 rescue of the former Continental Illinois Corp., the holding company for Continental Illinois National Bank & Trust Co.

Along with the market, Continental stock has rallied since last week. Yesterday, in composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange, Continental's common closed at $17, up $1, on volume of 194,400 shares.

Thomas C. Theobald, Continental's chairman and chief executive officer, said in a statement: "I'm dumbfounded that in a little more than six months the FDIC has passed up two legitimate opportunities to sell its minority interest in Continental. Not only have they failed to turn their asset into cash, but they've continued ownership in a healthy bank, which is a wholly unwarranted role for a regulator."

The FDIC turned down a proposal from Continental last fall to buy the agency's stake through a public offering.
