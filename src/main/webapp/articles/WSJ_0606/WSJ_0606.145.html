Board of Qintex Unit Approves Assets Sale For Over $65 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/06/90

BEVERLY HILLS, Calif. -- Qintex Entertainment Inc., which last October filed for bankruptcy-law protection, said its board approved the sale of operating assets for more than $65 million. The company is a unit of Qintex Ltd. According to a Qintex spokesman, the debt outstanding of the company as of Oct. 31 totaled $91.8 million. The company said funds from the asset sales will be allocated to creditors in accordance with the reorganization plan to be filed with the bankruptcy court.

Qintex, which produces and distributes filmed entertainment products, said it agreed to sell its entertainment assets for $42.4 million to RHI Entertainment Inc., a private, New York-based television production company. The sale includes the company's library, accounts receivable, development projects and $7 million cash, Qintex said. The Qintex spokesman said RHI's owners include Robert Halmi Sr., a Qintex co-chairman of the board.

Qintex said HR Broadcasting Inc., a private owner and operator of independent television stations, of which Qintex has an interest in the form of notes and convertible debentures, will sell some assets. Qintex said as a result, it would receive at least $23 million from HR Broadcasting's sale to ABRY Communications, a Boston-based private owner of independent television stations.

The sale includes television stations WTTO in Birmingham, Ala., and WCGV in Milwaukee.

Qintex Entertainment is 43%-owned by Qintex Australia, a unit of Qintex Ltd., based in Brisbane, Australia. Both sales are subject to, among other things, regulatory approvals and approval of the U.S. Bankruptcy Court.
