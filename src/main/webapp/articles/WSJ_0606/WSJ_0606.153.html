Regency Cruises Sees Loss on Operations In the Second Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/06/90

NEW YORK -- Regency Cruises Inc. said it expects to report a second-quarter operating loss, reflecting unrest in Latin America, bad Caribbean weather and a ship that ran aground last month. William Schanz, the company's chairman, disclosed the expected loss at the company's annual meeting, but didn't provide further details, including whether Regency will report a net loss.

He did say that Regency's cruise business off the coast of Alaska is "currently booked at approximately 85% of capacity." Most of that business is done in the third quarter, which a spokesman said is traditionally the company's best period.

A second-quarter loss would be Regency's third consecutive quarterly deficit. In the first quarter, Regency had a loss of $2 million, or nine cents a share; in last year's fourth period, the company had a loss of $1.2 million, or five cents a share.

The company pegged its first-quarter and second-quarter woes to a lack of tour interest in Panama, prior to and following the U.S. invasion; a State Department advisory that Americans steer clear of Colombia; and Hurricane Hugo, which devastated the Caribbean.

Last month, an electrical snafu forced one of Regency's three ships, the Regent Star, to run aground in Delaware Bay. The company subsequently canceled a five-day cruise, and scratched a two-day cruise because of a small fire on the ship three days later.

But a spokesman said things could improve by the third quarter because Alaska cruise bookings are strong. And Regency has pulled out of the problem-plagued Caribbean, opting instead for a potentially more lucrative New York-Montreal route.

Meanwhile, holders at the meeting approved adoption of a stock-option incentive plan for executives that calls for the issuance of one million new common shares. Holders also re-elected three directors to three-year terms, including Mr. Schanz, Fred A. Mayer and Roland J. Hawkins.
