Trump Is Facing Suit Over Casinos From Bondholders

  • Author: Wade Lambert and Neil Barsky
  • Date: 06/06/90

NEW YORK -- A lawsuit was filed on behalf of bondholders of two of Donald Trump's casinos, accusing the developer of a breach of fiduciary duty in connection with the opening of his Taj Mahal casino. The lawsuit, filed in federal court in Manhattan, alleges that Mr. Trump's promotion of the $1 billion Atlantic City, N.J., casino hurt the performance of his Trump Plaza and Trump's Castle properties.

The lawsuit claims Mr. Trump violated Securities and Exchange Commission requirements by not disclosing that he gave the Taj Mahal confidential information about the two casinos' clients.

"In order to pay the interest on these bonds, you need cash flow and for cash flow you need the high rollers," said Edward Labaton, an attorney for the plaintiff, Peter Stuyvesant Ltd.

Mr. Labaton said the plaintiff seeks class-action status on behalf of all affected bondholders.

The lawsuit seeks a court order requiring Mr. Trump to sell his interests in Trump's Castle and Trump Plaza if he continues to use the confidential information to benefit the Taj Mahal.

Since the Taj Mahal opened in April, revenues at the two properties have fallen by as much as 25% from the year before. For the month of May, however, Trump Plaza revenue rose about 24%, though that result is said to have been distorted by a single baccarat player's loss of $10 million.

A spokesman for Mr. Trump declined to comment.

The lawsuit said bondholders were assured that the value of their securities would not be hurt by Mr. Trump's opening of Taj Mahal.

On Monday, this newspaper reported that Mr. Trump was in intense discussions with his bank creditors to restructure his $2 billion in bank debt. Apparently as a result, the casinos' bond prices plummeted, raising speculation that Mr. Trump would not be able to make interest payments. Trump Castle Funding's 13 3/4% bonds, maturing in 1997, declined 2 1/2 points to end at 58 1/2. Trump Plaza Funding bonds stabilized and finished the session at 81 1/4, up 1/4 point.

Trump Taj Mahal's bonds did not fare much better, falling 2 7/8 points to 54 1/8.
