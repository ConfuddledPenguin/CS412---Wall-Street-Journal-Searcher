Travel Agents Are Charged In a Frequent-Flier Case

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/06/90

LOS ANGELES -- The federal government charged three Southern California travel agents with fraudulently taking advantage of American Airlines' frequent-flier program. According to U.S. Attorney Robert L. Brosio, a 22-count indictment alleged that the three agents created a list of more than 50 fictitious passengers between 1986 and 1988, then tampered with American's computer-system records by crediting the fake travelers with miles that had been flown by real people who weren't members of the airline's AAdvantage program.

An American spokeswoman claimed without elaborating that a "significant" number of miles were involved. She said the unit of Dallas-based AMR Corp. uncovered the alleged scheme during a mid-flight cross-check of passenger names against the names on checked baggage. It forwarded the case to the Federal Bureau of Investigation.

The indictment charges James Robert Winkleman and Phillip Ross Rinker of Woodland Hills, owners and operators of North Ranch Travel agency, and Brenton Neil Mullins, of Playa Del Rey, with conspiracy, wire and mail fraud and illegally using fictitious names.

Howard Cole, an attorney for Mr. Rinker, said he "emphatically denies" any wrongdoing by his client. Attorneys for Messrs. Mullins and Winkleman couldn't be reached.
