Phillips-Van Heusen Says Profit Fell 78%, Plans Preferred Swap

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/06/90

NEW YORK -- Phillips-Van Heusen Corp. reported a 78% drop in fiscal first-quarter net income and said it is negotiating to swap about half its preferred stock for less-expensive subordinated debt. The apparel company's announcement got a positive response from analysts and from investors, who sent Phillips-Van Heusen's shares up 62.5 cents, to $22.25, in New York Stock Exchange composite trading.

For the quarter ended April 29, PhillipsVan Heusen said net income sank to $643,000 from nearly $3 million a year earlier. On a per-share basis, the results were a 15-cent loss and a 10-cent profit, respectively, both reflecting more than $2 million of preferred-stock dividends. Sales rose 4.9% to $155.4 million from $148.1 million.

"We were looking for a 20-cent loss and they came in at 15 cents. That was encouraging," said Jay J. Meltzer, analyst at Goldman, Sachs & Co.

The preferred stock-for-debt swap, involving about half Phillips-Van Heusen's $72 million of preferred stock, could save the company as much as $1.2 million after tax annually, said Irwin W. Winter, the company's chief financial officer. The preferred, which is held by Prudential Insurance Co. of America, carries an 11.25% interest rate.

In addition, Mr. Meltzer noted that the swap "will benefit earnings because interest costs are deductible while preferred dividends are not."
