International Brief -- De La Rue PLC: Pretax Profit for Year Rose 41%, Printing Firm Reports

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/06/90

De La Rue PLC, a U.K. specialty printing group, said it pulled through a year of restructuring with a 41% rise in pretax profit, to #38.5 million ($64.7 million) from #27.3 million, and forecast a further rise in earnings in the year ahead. But a #57.3 million extraordinary charge, largely against the sale and closure of several electronics and printing technololgy units, led to a loss attributable to shareholders of #37.6 million for the year ended March 31, compared with a #2.14 million profit a year earlier.

Sales, hit by the disposals, fell 33% to #349 million from #523 million. Nevertheless, the company said it would recommend a final dividend of 10 pence (16.8 cents), lifting the total payout for the year even with the 13.25 pence paid a year earlier.

During the year, De La Rue disposed of its Crosfield Electronics printing equipment manufacturer. It also agreed to sell its Printrak unit and escaped a hostile takeover bid by Norton Opax PLC.
