Chicago Merc Fines And Expels Member

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/06/90

CHICAGO -- The Chicago Mercantile Exchange said it fined William A. Walsh $35,000 and expelled him from membership for making fraudulent Swiss-franc futures trades in 1988. Mr. Walsh, one of 48 individuals who was indicted last year by a federal grand jury here on fraud charges, pleaded guilty to making the illicit trades and is cooperating with the government investigation of trading fraud. He is expected to be called as a government witness in the trial of three Swiss-franc futures traders that is under way in U.S. District Court here.

As part of his guilty plea arranged with the U.S. attorney, Mr. Walsh will pay an unspecified amount of money as restitution to customers he defrauded.
