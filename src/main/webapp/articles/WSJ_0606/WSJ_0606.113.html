International Brief -- Boots Co.: British Retailing Venture Is Set With W.H. Smith

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/06/90

Boots Co. and W.H. Smith Group PLC announced a joint venture in Britain's do-it-yourself market that will create a company to own and manage both British retailers' home-improvement stores. Boots and W.H. Smith will each own 50% of the venture, which will have net assets of #104 million ($174.3 million) and trade under the name of W.H. Smith's Do It All chain. By combining the Do It All stores and Boots's Payless chain, the venture will have 230 outlets.

Payless, acquired by Boots in August as part of a #900 million hostile bid for Ward White Group PLC, a diversified stores and manufacturing group, had pretax profit of #8.7 million on sales of #273 million in the 14 months ended March 31. Do It All had pretax profit of #7.7 million on sales of #238 million in the year ended June 3, 1989.

"This is a major realignment in the {do-it-yourself} market," said Malcolm Field, group managing director of W.H. Smith and chairman of the joint-venture company. He said the companies "believe that this market has considerable scope for profitable growth."
