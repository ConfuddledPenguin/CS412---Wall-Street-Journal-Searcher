World Bank Agency Reinsures GE Project

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/06/90

WASHINGTON -- The Multilateral Investment Guaranty Agency, a World Bank affiliate, said it has approved a $30 million reinsurance contract covering investments by General Electric Co. in an electric lightbulb manufacturing venture in Hungary. The Overseas Private Investment Corp., a U.S. government agency, previously authorized $130 million in investment insurance guarantees for the project. The World Bank agency, in effect, will assume $30 million of the OPIC risk. Covered risks include possible losses arising from currency transfers and expropriation.

GE announced earlier that it was joining with the Hungarian Credit Bank in a joint venture to acquire, modernize and expand Tungsram Co., a Hungarian manufacturer of lighting products.

The World Bank agency said it also will provide $50 million in reinsurance coverage for a joint venture in Chile. Canada's Export Development Corp. previously authorized a $158 million insurance policy to support a $276 million investment by Placer Dome Inc. in the gold and silver mining venture in the Chilean province of Copiapo.
