Who's News: Lauer Gets Goodrich Operating Chief Post, Designated President

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/06/90

AKRON, Ohio -- B.F. Goodrich Co. said John N. Lauer was elected chief operating officer and will become president when Leigh Carter retires on Sept. 1. The move makes Mr. Lauer, 51 years old, a likely candidate to succeed John D. Ong as chairman and chief executive officer of the plastics, specialty chemicals and aerospace concern. But because Mr. Ong is 56, any succession appears to be some years away.

Mr. Lauer joined Goodrich as executive vice president on Jan. 1, 1989, from Hoechst Celanese, the U.S. unit of Hoechst AG of West Germany, for which he was responsible for engineered plastics operations.

Mr. Lauer was hired by Goodrich with the understanding that he would succeed Mr. Carter, who is 64, as president and chief operating officer. He was elected to the Goodrich board last September.

Mr. Carter was president and chief executive of Tremco Inc., a Cleveland-based producer of sealants and other specialty chemical products, when Tremco was acquired by Goodrich in 1979. He was elected an executive vice president of Goodrich in 1982, vice chairman in 1984, and president and operating chief in 1986.

He helped complete the lengthy transformation of Goodrich from a maker of tires and industrial rubber products into a company that gets about half of its $2.4 billion in annual revenue from polyvinyl chloride plastics and related products, slightly more than 30% from specialty chemicals, and nearly 20% from aircraft brakes and other aerospace products and services.
