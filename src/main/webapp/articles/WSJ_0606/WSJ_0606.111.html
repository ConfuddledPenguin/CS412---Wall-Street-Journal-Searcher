Kodak Sells Unit to Japan Firm

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/06/90

ROCHESTER, N.Y. -- Eastman Kodak Co., a photographic, chemical and pharmaceutical company, said it completed the sale of its Charlotte, N.C.-based Verbatim Corp. subsidiary, which makes and distributes floppy disks, to Mitsubishi Kasei Corp. of Japan. Terms weren't disclosed. Analysts had estimated that Kodak would receive between $190 million and $200 million for Verbatim. Kodak paid $175 million when it bought the business in 1985. Verbatim had been marginally profitable for Kodak.
