Vote in Senate to Limit Debate on Crime Bill Fails

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/06/90

WASHINGTON -- Senate proponents of an omnibus anti-crime bill failed to limit debate on the legislation, which could end up being buried under a mountain of proposed amendments. The vote was 54-37 to restrict further debate to 30 hours, but under Senate rules, 60 votes are needed to impose the limit. Democratic proponents of the bill are expected to try again to limit debate in a vote scheduled for tomorrow morning.

Yesterday's vote was a short-term victory for opponents of gun control, who failed last week to defeat a provision that would impose a three-year ban on nine types of semi-automatic "assault" weapons. If cloture isn't achieved, debate on more than 240 pending amendments could last for weeks, forcing the Senate's Democratic leadership to pull the omnibus bill.

Democratic staff members said the party's leadership believes that cloture "is within reach," noting that nine senators didn't vote yesterday and that several others may be persuaded to switch their votes.
