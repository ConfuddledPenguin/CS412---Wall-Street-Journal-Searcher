New Securities Issues

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/06/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATE

American Electric Power Co. -- $436.6 million of 9.82% secured lease-back obligation bonds offered in equal issues of $218.3 million by each of two AEP units: AEP Generating Co. and Indiana Michigan Power Co. The bonds, due 2022, were priced at par. The bonds, which have a semiannual sinking fund and an average life of 27 years, were priced at a spread of 137.5 basis points above the Treasury's 30-year bellwether bond. Rated Baa-2 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and triple-B by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Goldman Sachs & Co.

Home Savings of America -- $250 million of 10 1/2% subordinated notes due June 12, 1997, priced at 99.70 to yield 10.56%. The notes, which are non-callable for 5 3/4 years, were priced at 207 basis points above the Treasury's seven-year note. The size of the issue was increased from an originally planned $200 million. Rated Baa-1 by Moody's and triple-B-plus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets. Home Savings of America is a unit of H.F. Ahmanson & Co.

Morton International Inc. -- $200 million of 9 1/4% credit-sensitive debentures due June 1, 2020, priced at 99.14 to yield 9.335%. The non-callable debentures were priced at 92.5 basis points above the Treasury's 30-year bond. They are called credit-sensitive securities because the coupon will be adjusted upward in case of a rating downgrade or downward in the event of a rating upgrade. Rated single-A1 by Moody's and double-A-minus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Goldman Sachs & Co.

Boise Cascade Corp. -- $125 million of 9.85% notes due June 15, 2002, priced at par. The size of the issue was increased from $100 million. The non-callable notes were priced at 140 basis points above the Treasury's 10-year note. Rated Baa-1 by Moody's and triple-B-plus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by First Boston Corp.

Northern States Power Co. -- $100 million of 9 3/8% first mortgage bonds due June 1, 2020, priced at 98.75 to yield 9.50%. The bonds, which are non-callable for five years, were priced at 106 basis points above the Treasury's 30-year bond. Rated double-A-1 by Moody's and double-A by S&P, the issue will be sold through Goldman Sachs & Co.

MUNICIPAL

Washington Public Power Supply System -- $875 million of nuclear project refunding revenue bonds for Project Nos. 1, 2 and 3, Series 1990B, priced by a First Boston Corp. group. The issue was increased from $702.3 million at the preliminary pricing and was restructured and repriced late in the day to lower some yields. The Project No. 1 bonds were priced to yield to 7.885% for bonds due in 2017, which are callable in two years. Project No. 2 consists of bonds due in 2012 that were priced to yield 7.55%. Project No. 3 bonds yield to 7.674% in 2018. The bonds have been rated double-A by both Moody's and S&P. Fitch Investors Service Inc. has rated the issue double-A-minus. Some maturities are insured. The bonds were oversubscribed, and there will be no unsold balance remaining, the underwriter said.

Industrial Development Authority of Maricopa County, Ariz. -- $201.9 million of refunding revenue bonds priced by a PaineWebber Inc. group. The bonds, for Samaritan Health Services Hospital, were priced to yield from 6% in 1990 to 7.31% in 2013. The $155 million Series A bonds were priced to yield from 6.60% in 1996 to 7.31% in 2013. Series B bonds totaling $46.9 million were priced to yield from 6% in 1990 to 7.20% on term bonds due in 2019 and zero-coupon bonds due in 2006-09. The issue is insured and triple-A-rated.

California School Board Association of Sacramento -- $175 million of 6% certificates of participation priced by a Piper Jaffray & Hopwood Inc. group. The certificates, due July 3, 1991, were priced to yield 6.25%. They are rated SP1-Plus and have a letter of credit from Fuji Bank Ltd.

Monroe County, N.Y. -- $104 million of various anticipation notes, apparently won by a Chemical Securities Inc. group and a First Boston Corp. group. Chemical Securities won the $69 million June maturity; the 5.93% notes were priced to yield 5.85% June 14, 1991. First Boston said it won the $35 million April maturity; the 5.85% notes were priced to yield 5.75% April 30, 1991. The issue is rated double-A by Moody's.

Hawaii -- $100 million of general obligation bonds priced by a Merrill Lynch Capital Markets group. Yields range from 6.15% in 1993 to 7.10% in 2009 and 2010. The issue is rated double-A/double-A.

Mayor and City Council of Baltimore -- $90.8 million of refunding revenue bonds priced by a Kidder, Peabody & Co. group. The issue consists of $56.5 million of Series 1990A water project bonds, priced to yield from 6% in 1992 to 7.125% in 2020. There also is $34.3 million of Series 1990A wastewater project bonds, also priced to yield from 6% in 1992 to 7.125% in 2020. The issue is insured and triple-A-rated.

Allegheny County, Pa. -- $84.8 million of Series 1990A airport revenue bonds priced by a Smith Barney, Harris Upham & Co. group. The bonds, for Greater Pittsburgh International Airport, were priced to yield from 6.25% in 1992 to 7.429% in 2018. The bonds are insured and triple-A-rated.

MORTGAGES

Chemical Mortgage Securities Inc. -- $77.1 million of 9% Remic mortgage securities offered in four classes by Chemical Securities Inc. The offering, Series 1990-1, is backed by 30-year fixed-rate mortgages with an weighted average coupon rate of 10.70% and weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29.3 years. Price and yield data weren't immediately available. The issue is expected to be rated triple-A by S&P, based on the quality of the underlying loans and a letter of credit covering 12% of the deal from Credit Suisse. The loans were originated by Chemical Bank and Chemical Financial Services Corp.

EUROBONDS

Istituto Bancario San Paolo di Torino (Italy) -- $250 million of subordinated variable rate depository receipts due July 26, 2000, via Salomon Brothers International Ltd. Initial coupon will float at 0.25 point above the U.S. dollar three-month London interbank offered rate. Alternatively, for first five years, coupon will be Libor plus 0.50 point, and from year 6 to maturity, at a spread of 0.60 point.

Osterreichische Kontrollbank AG (Austria) -- $200 million of 9 1/8% Eurobonds due July 12, 2000, priced at 99.60 via Morgan Stanley International Ltd. Fees 0.325.

Ford Credit Funding PLC (U.S. parent) -- #100 million of 13 3/4% Eurobonds due July 9, 1993, priced at 101.275 via Samuel Montagu & Co. Guaranteed by Ford Motor Co. Fees 1 3/8.

International Finance Corp. (agency) -- 10 billion Spanish pesetas of 13 3/4% Eurobonds due June 22, 1995, priced at 101 1/4 via Bankers Trust International Ltd. Fees 1 5/8.

Bacob Overseas Ltd. (Cayman Islands) -- 50 million Canadian dollars of 12 3/4% Eurobonds due July 9, 1993, priced at 101.80 via ScotiaMcleod. Guaranteed by Bacob Savings Bank. Fees 1 3/8.

Republic of Ireland (sovereign) -- 100 million Swiss francs of 7 3/8% public bonds due July 12, 2002, priced at 101.63 via Credit Suisse. Fees 3.
