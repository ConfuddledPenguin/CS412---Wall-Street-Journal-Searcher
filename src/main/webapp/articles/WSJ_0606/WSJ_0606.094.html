International Brief -- Kubota Ltd.: Consolidated Pretax Profit Declined 29% in Latest Year

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/06/90

Kubota Ltd., a Japanese maker of farm equipment and ductile iron pipes, announced that despite a 14% gain in net sales, consolidated pretax profit fell 29% in fiscal 1989 to 31.85 billion yen ($209.3 million) from 45.12 billion yen. Net sales rose 14% in the year ended March 31 to 821.88 billion yen from 721.39 billion yen.

Many of the newly consolidated subsidiaries posted strong sales growth, contributing to the net sales gain.

But operating profit was knocked down 15% by higher sales, administrative and general expenses, to 36.31 billion yen from 42.55 billion yen.

Higher depreciation costs, caused by expanded production facilities, cut into sales income as well, Kubota said.

Net income tumbled 59% to 7.60 billion yen from 18.43 billion yen.

Kubota cited losses from higher interest rates, foreign-exchange losses and increased costs for starting new businesses, such as computer-related fields through its subsidiaries, for the plunge in net.
