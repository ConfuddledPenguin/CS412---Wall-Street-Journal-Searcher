Taiwan's Exports Fell in May

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/06/90

TAIPEI, Taiwan -- Exports in May fell 10.2% to US$5.64 billion -- the first double-digit decline since September 1988 -- and were off 1.05% from April, the government said. In the first five months of this year, exports totaled US$26.59 billion, down 1% from a year earlier. Trade officials cited mainly a stronger Taiwan dollar and rising labor costs, which made locally manufactured products less attractive to foreign buyers.

Imports in May totaled US$4.38 billion, down 7.2% from a year earlier. In the first five months, imports rose 5.5% to US$22.75 billion, compared with a year earlier.

May's trade surplus was US$1.26 billion, down 19.3% from a year earlier but up 60.76% from April. In the first five months, Taiwan registered a surplus of US$3.84 billion, off 27.5% from a year earlier.
