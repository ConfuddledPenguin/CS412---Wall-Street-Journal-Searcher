Who's News: CT Financial Services Chief Mervyn Lahn Plans to Resign Post

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/06/90

TORONTO -- Mervyn Lahn, chairman and chief executive officer of CT Financial Services Inc., said he plans to resign Dec. 30. CT Financial is 98.2%-owned by a unit of Montreal-based Imasco Ltd. Mr. Lahn is also chairman and chief executive officer of Canada Trustco Mortgage Co., Canada's second-largest trust concern in terms of assets. Canadian trust companies are similar to U.S. thrifts.

Mr. Lahn, 56 years old, said he is resigning because, after 17 years as chief executive officer, he wants to make way for younger executives. He said CT Financial's board is expected to name his successor on June 25. The new chief executive officer will be someone currently with the company, he said.

Mr. Lahn said he has no specific career plans, but he won't join another trust concern after he leaves CT Financial.

Imasco, a consumer products and financial services concern, is 40.4%-owned by B.A.T Industries PLC of the U.K.
