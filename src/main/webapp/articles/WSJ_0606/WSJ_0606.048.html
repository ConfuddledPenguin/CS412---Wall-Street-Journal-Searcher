Who's News: Nebraskan Nominated To Board of FDIC

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/06/90

WASHINGTON -- President Bush named Andrew C. Hove, a Nebraska banker, to the Federal Deposit Insurance Corp. board. The nomination of Mr. Hove, which was expected, requires confirmation by the Senate.

Mr. Hove would fill a position on the FDIC board created by last year's savings-and-loan bailout law. That legislation expanded the board to five members from three, adding the director of the Office of Thrift Supervision, as well as the seat Mr. Hove would fill. The other members are the FDIC chairman, the comptroller of the currency, and another director chosen by the president, C.C. Hope, a retired vice chairman of First Union National Bank of Charlotte, N.C.

Mr. Hove is chairman of Minden Exchange Bank & Trust in Minden, Neb., which had $75 million in assets as of March.
