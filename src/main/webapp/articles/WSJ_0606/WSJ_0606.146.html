Business Brief -- NL Industries Inc.: West German Unit Signs $945.6 Million Loan Pact

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/06/90

NL Industries Inc., Houston, said its Kronos International Inc. unit, based in Leverkusen, West Germany, signed a seven-year term-loan agreement for 1.6 billion marks ($945.6 million), which will be used for debt repayment and other corporate purposes. The specialty chemicals concern, which is controlled by the Dallas investor Harold C. Simmons, last year used proceeds from a loan denominated in marks in part to buy substantial stakes in Lockheed Corp. and Georgia Gulf Corp.

An executive with Mr. Simmons's holding company, Valhi Inc., said the loan, which matures in June 1997, will increase NL's cash position by about $200 million, some of which will be used for NL's new Lake Charles, La., chemical plant. The proceeds also will be used to repay about $602 million of existing West German mark financing. Kronos generates about 55% of the company's sales and a substantial amount of its cash flow.

The bank group is led by Hypobank International S.A. and Banque Paribas.
