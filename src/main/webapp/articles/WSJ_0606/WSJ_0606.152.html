Fluor Unit and Firm In East Germany Set Joint-Venture Pact

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/06/90

IRVINE, Calif. -- Fluor Corp. said that its Fluor Daniel G.m.b.H. West German unit signed a letter of intent with Ingenieurbetrieb Anlagenbau Leipzig (IAL) to jointly pursue hydrocarbon, industrial, and other projects in East Germany and other East European countries. A Fluor spokeswoman said the company was already negotiating two "small" contracts with IAL, the largest East German engineering and construction-services company. The Fluor spokeswoman said the company has had a West German office for 30 years. The current letter of intent, she said, "is a first step, a good way to gain experience in a new market."

Separately, Fluor reported that earnings in the second-quarter ended April 30 jumped 59% to $38 million, or 47 cents a share, compared with $23.9 million, or 30 cents a year ago on fewer shares outstanding. Revenues for the quarter climbed 12% to $1.83 billion, from last year's $1.63 billion.

For the six months ended April 30, the engineering, construction, and technical services company reported that net income rose 45% to $66.2 million, or 82 cents a share, compared with $45.5 million, or 57 cents a share. Revenues rose 21% to $3.71 billion, compared with $3.05 billion a year ago.

The spokeswoman said the main reason for the sharp rise in net income was a $19 million, or 23 cents a share, reversal of tax reserves no longer required.
