Marketing & Media: Journal's Peter Gumbel Wins Livingston Award

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/06/90

NEW YORK -- Peter Gumbel, Moscow bureau chief of The Wall Street Journal, was named a winner of one of the Livingston Awards for Young Journalists, for excellence in international reporting. Mr. Gumbel, 32 years old, was recognized for stories detailing how attempts to restructure the Soviet Union's centrally planned economy have, in some ways, made life more difficult in that country.

The Livingston Awards, which include a $5,000 cash prize, are made annually to journalists under age 35 for excellence in local, national and international reporting.
