International Brief -- Argyll Group PLC: Fiscal 1990 Pretax Profit Rose 27% to $381.2 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/06/90

Argyll Group PLC, a British food and drink retailer, said pretax profit for the year ended March 31 rose 27% to #227.5 million ($381.2 million) from #178.7 million a year earlier, citing big gains from its Safeway supermarket chain -- formerly part of the U.S. chain but now unrelated. Profit after taxes but before special items also rose 27% to #159.2 million from #125.1 million. Per-share earnings rose 25% to 17.2 pence from 13.8 pence. Profit attributable to shareholders rose 30% to #163.3 million from #125.1 million, boosted by a #4.1 million gain from proceeds from an asset sale; the year-earlier period didn't have any special items.

Sales increased 12% to #4.1 billion from #3.7 billion.

Operating profit rose 20% to #224.6 million from #187.8 million. Argyll said Safeway contributed 68% of total sales and 71% of operating profit. Safeway sales increased 35% to #2.8 billion and operating profit rose 50% to #158.8 million.

The company proposed a final dividend of 4.80 pence, making the total payout for the year 7.25 pence, up 17% from 6.20 pence a year earlier.
