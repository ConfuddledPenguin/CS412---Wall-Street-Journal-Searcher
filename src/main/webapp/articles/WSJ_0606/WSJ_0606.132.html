World Markets: Tokyo Share Prices Finish About Unchanged; London Stocks Gain Slightly in Volatile Trading

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 06/06/90

Tokyo stock prices bounced up from midday lows to finish trading nearly flat. Most stock prices moved in a narrow range throughout the day. Trading was moderate, traders said, reflecting investors' caution ahead of tomorrow, the last trading day for June stock index futures and options.

Meanwhile, London share prices gained slightly in volatile, heavy-volume trading.

Tokyo's Nikkei Index of 225 issues shed 3.75 points to close at 32921.62, up from the intraday low of 32855. The index gained 34.25 points Monday.

At mid-morning Wednesday in Tokyo, the Nikkei index had retreated 81.94 points to 32839.68.

Tuesday's volume on the first section was estimated at 600 million shares, in line with 634 million shares Monday. Decliners outnumbered advancers 544 to 394, while 183 issues were unchanged.

The Tokyo Stock Price Index of all issues listed in the first section, which gained 0.69 point Monday, fell 2.31 points, or 0.10%, to 2424.93.

However, the buying spree continued on the second section amid growing volume. The surge partly reflected the participation of investors who abandoned the first section, which can be swayed by trading linked to the June index futures and options, traders said.

The second section index rose 20.23 points, or 0.50%, to 4013.17, posting gains for the sixth consecutive day. The index rose 18.32 points Monday. The section's volume was estimated at 29 million shares, up from 20 million shares Monday.

The bitter memory of March's plunge on the Nikkei Index is preventing investors from active buying this week, observers said. In March, stock prices were pushed down by heavy selling triggered by waves of selling linked to arbitrage unwinding of March stock futures.

As of May 25, the balance of June contracts buying was 518.154 billion yen ($3.4 billion), according to the Tokyo Stock Exchange, which began disclosing data on futures trading in April to help ease investors' fear of the impact of arbitrage selling.

Selling related to the unwinding of June futures may pull down stock prices, said Fumihiko Ishii, manager of investment research at Nomura Securities. "But the drop would be only temporary," he said.

"In March, sentiment was extremely bearish and expectations for future declines were dominant. It caused selling by arbitragers that prompted more selling by general investors," Mr. Ishii said.

Bullish sentiment is stronger at the moment so if stock prices fall, it won't shake the market's recent stability, he added.

Also, an increasing amount of positions have been rolled over to the September contract without being unwound, he said.

Some fund managers said they will avoid buying aggressively until Friday morning, as settlement prices for June stock futures and options are determined by Friday's opening prices.

A fund manager at Dai-Ichi Mutual Life Co. said if stock prices drop sharply ahead of the expiration of contracts, many institutional investors will take it as a good buying opportunity.

Selling pressure is much lighter than in March as institutional investors, the main sellers in March, haven't bought much over the past months, he said.

In addition to concern about the impact of the unwinding of June futures contracts, profit-taking and uncertainty about the direction of domestic interest rates and exchange rates discouraged investors from continuing to buy construction and shipbuilding shares, both of which are considered interest rate-sensitive, traders said.

Among construction stocks, Shimizu fell 30 yen to 2140 yen. Ohbayashi fell 10 yen to 1730 yen, and Kajima declined 20 yen to 1980 yen.

Among shipbuilders, Hitachi Zosen dropped 15 yen to 875 yen, while Ishikawajima-Harima Heavy Industries lost 40 yen to 1190 yen.

Investors shifted their attention to electrical shares and other stocks with strong earnings predictions for the current fiscal year, traders said. High-priced stocks were especially popular as their prices usually move up sharply on a relatively small amount of buying, traders said.

Kyocera advanced 80 yen to 7950 yen, while Fanuc rose 150 yen to 7240 yen. Pioneer was up 200 yen at 6200 yen.

Fuji Photo Film gained 60 yen to 4130 yen on expectations of a gratis distribution, traders said.

In London, meanwhile, the Financial Times-Stock Exchange 100 Share Index gained 1.1 points to 2380.1.

Stocks opened strong, gaining 19.9 points to its peak just after the start of official trading. The market got a further boost from the firm opening of the stock futures sector, with the June FT-SE 100-index trading at a 35-point premium to cash.

However, those gains were quickly eroded by the disclosure of disappointing results by Great Portland, which prompted a sell-off in the entire property sector. The FT-SE 100 index shed 16.5 points at the day's low.

However, a favorable opening on Wall Street, combined with a firmer pound and the emergence of bargain-hunters in London, helped that market steady up.

Volume was 618.2 million shares, up from 430 million shares Monday.

The Financial Times 30 Share Index advanced 0.5 point to 1893.9.

In the futures markets, the June FT-SE 100-index contract ended at 2423, up from Monday's close of 2422.

Many dealers still expect the stock market to creep higher, in the absence of any negative news. Although they mostly concede that the market is set for a correction, sentiment appears to be favoring a further test of upside potential.

Among stocks to advance, Allied-Lyons gained 16 pence to 520 pence (27 cents to $8.71) on continued takeover rumors, as did Scottish & Newcastle, up 4 pence to 338 pence; Guinness advanced 9 pence to 786 pence; while Midland Bank rose 10 pence to 310 pence on stock shortage following a recent market downgrading.

Among stocks to drop, Pearson ended down 14 pence at 755 pence; and property stocks Land Securities fell 17 pence to 509 pence, and MEPC, dropped 17 pence to 501 pence.

Meanwhile share prices closed higher in Frankfurt, Stockholm, Brussels and Zurich, lower in Paris, mixed in Amsterdam and unchanged in Milan. South African gold stocks closed slightly up.

And in Asian and Pacific markets, stocks ended higher in Sydney and Wellington, mixed in Hong Kong, and lower in Seoul, Taipei, Singapore and Manila.
