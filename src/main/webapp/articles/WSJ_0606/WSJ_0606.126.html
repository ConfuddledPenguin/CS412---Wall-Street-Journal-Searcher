Washington Lawyer Pleads Guilty to Felony Charges

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/06/90

WASHINGTON -- A Washington lawyer who made headlines last summer when his private plane mysteriously crashed into the Atlantic Ocean pleaded guilty to five felony counts, including defrauding clients and submitting counterfeit documents to federal agencies, a U.S. attorney said. A grand jury had indicted the lawyer, Thomas Root, in March on various counts of counterfeiting federal documents, forgery, and defrauding his clients, among other charges.

Mr. Root's guilty plea in Washington federal court came a day after he was indicted on 455 counts of securities fraud and related charges by a separate grand jury in Nashville, N.C.

Mr. Root, 37, couldn't be reached for comment yesterday.

His guilty plea pertained to his representation of clients seeking radio licenses before the Federal Communications Commission. As a result of the plea, he faces up to 35 years in prison and $1.25 million in fines, U.S. Attorney Jay Stephens said. No date for sentencing has been set.

Mr. Root, in a July flight from Washington to North Carolina, reportedly blacked out in the cockpit of his single-engine plane. The plane eventually crashed off Bermuda. Mr. Root survived, but he was pulled from the water with a gunshot wound in his stomach.
