Security Pacific Corp. Ex-Aide Is Sentenced In Drug-Money Case

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/06/90

LOS ANGELES -- A former supervisory officer of Security Pacific National Bank's central vault, 27-year-old Jose O. Lopez, was sentenced Monday to 46 months in prison for conspiracy in a drug-money laundering operation. Also sentenced for the same offense was Geno Apicella, 27, to 37 months in prison. Both men pleaded guilty Feb. 28 to the conspiracy charges, which came after an undercover investigation by Internal Revenue Service special agents and internal investigators of Security Pacific National Bank, a unit of Los Angeles-based Security Pacific Corp.

During the four-month investigation, an IRS undercover agent posing as a money man for narcotics traffickers gave $364,000 to be laundered through Security Pacific by the group, which Mr. Lopez headed.

In addition to Messrs. Lopez and Apicella, five other defendants pleaded guilty to conspiracy and were previously sentenced.
