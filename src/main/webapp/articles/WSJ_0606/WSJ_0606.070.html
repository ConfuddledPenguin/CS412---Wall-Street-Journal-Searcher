Business Brief -- Nevada Power Co.: Contract with SCEcorp Unit Is Rejected by Regulators

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/06/90

Nevada's utility regulators rejected a power purchase contract between Nevada Power Co. and SCEcorp's Mission Energy Co. unit, citing "unreasonably high costs for the project." "The evidence showed that Nevada Power can get a better deal for ratepayers," said Tom Stephens, chairman of the Nevada Public Service Commission. "There is a clear need to adjust the price of this contract."

Under the proposed contract, Nevada Power would purchase electricity generated by a new 210-megawatt power plant that Mission would build on property adjacent to a Nevada Power station in the east Las Vegas valley. Nevada Power estimates the plant would cost about $88 million to build, and it has agreed to buy the electricity for $6.16 per kilowatt per month.

The commission has given Nevada Power 30 days to either negotiate a contract price that would be similar to what it would cost if Nevada Power built the project itself, or develop an alternative.

Nevada Power plans to renegotiate with Mission, a spokeswoman said. Officials of SCEcorp, a Rosemead, Calif.-based utility holding company, weren't available for comment.
