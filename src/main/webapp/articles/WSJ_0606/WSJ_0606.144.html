Marketing & Media: Nielsen Will Change Way It Reports Black Audiences

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/06/90

NEW YORK -- Responding to concern that black television viewership is underreported, Nielsen Media Research said it would improve the way it measures the size of minority audiences. The changes, to take effect next year, will include breaking out black demographics in monthly reports on TV show ratings. Under the new system, for instance, there will be categories such as black men 18-to-49 years old. Currently, there is no such break-out.

Nielsen also proposed introducing a national black "pocketpiece" report including household and demographic information based on TV viewing among blacks. Such reports on the general viewing audience are already produced for broadcasters.

Separately, Tribune Entertainment Co, a division of Tribune Co., distributor of such shows as "Soul Train" and "Dionne & Friends" with singer Dionne Warwick, said it would begin an awareness campaign to encourage blacks to participate in viewing surveys.

Also participating in the campaign are two advertising agencies, Burrell Advertising Inc. and Uniworld Group Inc. One possibility is to get a rap singing group to appear in public-service announcements.

Blacks return Nielsen diaries at a 42% response rate, compared with the so-called general market, where diary returns run at a 55% rate, according to David Woolfson, vice president of new business for Nielsen Media Research, a subsidiary of Dun & Bradstreet Co.
