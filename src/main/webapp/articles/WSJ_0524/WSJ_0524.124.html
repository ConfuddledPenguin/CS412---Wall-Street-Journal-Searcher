Elf Signs Accord With Soviet Union On Oil Exploration

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 05/24/90

Societe Nationale Elf Aquitaine, the French oil company, signed an oil exploration and production agreement with the Soviet Union. The agreement, announced in Paris after being signed in Moscow, provides Elf exploration rights on between 13,500 and 15,500 square miles in the Volga-Caspian region of the Soviet Union.

Company and Soviet oil officials called it the first such pact between the Soviet Union and a Western oil partner. Several U.S. oil companies, including Chevron Corp., have been negotiating with the Soviets but so far haven't concluded exploration agreements.

Few details of the Elf-Soviet agreement were disclosed, and some still must be worked out. But Loik Le Floch-Prigent, Elf's president, called it a "classic" exploration and production agreement.

Until recently, the Soviet Union restricted foreign oil company participation to technical assistance. But the government now admits its oil industry is having problems and needs outside assistance. Oil production has been declining, although the Soviet Union still is the world's largest producer with output of slightly under 12 million barrels a day.
