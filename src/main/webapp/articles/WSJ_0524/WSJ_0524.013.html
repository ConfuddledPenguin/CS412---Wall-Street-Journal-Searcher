New Securities Issues

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/24/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATES

Ford Capital B.V. -- $500 million of 9 1/2% debentures, due June 1, 2010, priced at 99.68 to yield 9.536%. The noncallable debentures were priced at a spread of 95 basis points above the Treasury's 30-year bellwether bond. Rated double-A-2 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and double-A by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets. Ford Capital B.V. is the Dutch finance unit of Ford Motor Co.

General Motors Acceptance Corp. -- $500 million issue of 8 7/8% notes, due June 1, 2010, priced at 99.958 to yield 8.885%. The size of the issue was increased after pricing from an originally planned $300 million. The notes can be put back to the company at par in the fifth, 10th and 15th years. The noncallable notes were priced at a spread of 35 basis points above the Treasury's 30-year bellwether bond. Rated double-A-3 by Moody's and double-A-minus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets.

Storage Technology Corp. -- $150 million of 8% convertible subordinated debentures, due May 31, 2015, was priced through underwriters led by Salomon Brothers Inc. The size of the issue was increased from an originally planned $125 million. The debentures are convertible into common stock at $35.25 a share, representing a 23.14% conversion premium over Wednesday's closing price. The issue is rated Ba-3 by Moody's and single-B by S&P.

MUNICIPALS

Battery Park City Authority, New York -- $225 million revenue bonds, Series 1990, due May 1, 1998-2001, 2006, 2015 and 2020, tentatively priced by a Morgan Stanley & Co. group to yield from 7.10% in 1998 to 7.80% in 2015. Current interest serial bonds are priced at par to yield from 7.10% in 1998 to 7.35% in 2001. There are $30.2 million of 7.60% term bonds priced at 99.5 to yield 7.65% in 2006. There are $92.28 million of 7.70% term bonds priced at 98.9 to yield 7.80% in 2015. There are $83.1 million of 6.50% term bonds priced at 86.03 to yield 7.70% in 2020. The bonds are rated single-A by Moody's and single-A-minus by S&P.

Long Island Lighting Co. -- $100 million of tax-exempt bonds, offered at 6.5% and will have a 30-year maturity. In a news release, the company said proceeds will be used to reimburse its treasury for expenditures on projects that qualify for tax-exempt financing in accordance with Internal Revenue Service regulations. The bonds are issued by the New York State Energy Research and Development Authority.

North Carolina Housing Finance Agency -- $75 million single-family mortgage revenue bonds, Series L, due Sept. 1, 2032 and Series M, due Sept. 1, 1992-2000, tentatively priced by a Donaldson, Lufkin Jenrette Securities Corp. group to yield from 6.30% in 1992 to 7.90% in 2029. Series L consists of a single portion of 7.60% bonds priced at par in 2032. Series M consists of current interest serial bonds priced at par to yield from 6.30% in 1992 to 7.10% in 2000. There are $7.86 million of term bonds 7 3/8% term bonds priced at par in 2010. There are $8.4 million of term bonds priced at par in 2016 and $34.7 million of 7.90% term bonds priced at par in 2029. The bonds are rated double-A by Moody's and singleA-plus by S&P. Interest on the Series M bonds will be treated as a preference item in calculating the Federal atlernative minimum tax that may be imposed on certain investors. Series L bonds aren't subject to the Federal alternative minimum tax.

San Antonio Water Authority, Texas -- $67 million of prior lien water revenue bonds, Series 1990, due May 1, 1991-2000, with capital appreciation bonds due 2001-2012 and prior lien water system revenue refunding bonds, Series 1990 A, due May 1, 1991-2007, repriced and restructured by a Smith Barney Harris Upham & Co. group. The repricing lowered some yields by five basis points. It also raised the yield on some capital appreciation bonds by five basis points. The restructuring created bonds maturing in 2006. The bonds are now priced to yield from 6.10% in 1991 to 7.65% for capital appreciation bonds due in 2011 and 2012, changed from priced to yield from 6.10% in 1991 to 7.6% for capital appreciation bonds due 2010-2012. Series 1990 current interest serial bonds are priced at par to yield from 6.10% in 1991 to 7.05% in 2000, changed from 6.10% in 1991 to 7.10% in 2000. Capital appreciation bonds are priced to yield to maturity from 7.25% in 2001 to 7.65% in 2011 and 2012, changed from 7.25% in 2001 to 7.60% in 2010-2012. Series 1990 A refunding bonds consist of current interest serial bonds priced at par to yield from 6.10% in 1991 to 7.35% in 2006 and 2007, changed from 6.10%% in 1991 to 7.35% in 2005. The bonds are rated double-A by Moody's and single-A-plus by S&P, excluding capital appreciation bonds, which are AMBAC-insured and rated triple-A by Moody's and S&P.

Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $300 million of Remic mortgage securities, offered in 12 classes by First Boston. The offering, Series 178, is backed by FHLMC 9 1/2% securities with a weighted average coupon rate of 10.25% and weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29.50 years.

EUROBONDS

Tokyo Tatemono Co. (Japan) -- 20 billion yen of 7% Eurobonds due June 27, 1997, priced at 100 5/8 to yield 7.23% less full fees via Daiwa Europe Ltd. Fees 1 7/8 points.

Bell Canada (Canada) -- 125 million Canadian dollars of 12 3/8% global bonds due June 20, 2000, at an issue price of 101.30, via UBS Philips & Drew Ltd. Reoffered at fixed price of 99.80, for a yield of 12.05%.

Queensland Treasury Corp. -- 100 million Australian dollars of 12% Eurobonds due July 15, 1993, at an issue price of 98.44, via S.G. Warburg & Co. Reoffered at a fixed-price of 98.123, which gives a yield of 14.70%. Transferable into QTC's domestic issue.

Queensland Treasury Corp. -- 100 million Australian dollars of global 12% bonds due March 15, 1992, with initial price of 95.875 to yield 14.69%, via Merrill Lynch International.
