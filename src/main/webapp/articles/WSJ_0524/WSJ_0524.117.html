International Brief -- Ranks Hovis McDougall PLC: Pretax Profit Rises 0.2%; Drop for Year Is Expected

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/24/90

Ranks Hovis McDougall PLC reported a slim 0.2% rise in pretax profit for the six months to March 3 and warned that full-year earnings are unlikely to match the record levels attained last year. Pretax profit for the food group climbed to #81.7 million ($138.3 million) from #81.5 million the previous year, below the range of market expectations.

RHM cited increased competition and a 60% surge in interest costs as factors that eroded profit. As well, the group said weather conditions in the U.K. hurt consumer spending. "The hot summer of 1989 adversely affected consumers' spending patterns on food, and the return to more normal expenditure was delayed by the mild winter," RHM said.
