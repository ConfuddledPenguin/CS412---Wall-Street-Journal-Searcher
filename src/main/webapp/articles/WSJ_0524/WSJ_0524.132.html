Business Brief -- SmithKline Beecham PLC: Judge Approves $13 Million Settlement for Shareholders

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/24/90

Philadelphia Common Pleas Court Judge I. Raymond Kremer approved a $13 million settlement on behalf of about 25,000 SmithKline Beckman Corp.'s shareholders on Tuesday. The $13 million will be paid pro-rata to shareholders of SmithKline who held shares between April 10, 1989, and June 26, 1989.

Last year, several groups of SmithKline shareholders filed class-action suits, alleging that the company's directors breached their fiduciary duties by not seeking potential acquirers willing to pay a higher price than the one offered by Beecham Group PLC and by placing devices, such as a poison pill and golden parachutes, in the way of a hostile bid. SmithKline ultimately merged with Beecham to form SmithKline Beecham PLC.

According to the settlement agreement, the defendants "vigorously deny all liability," but settled in order to "avoid the substantial expense, inconvenience, and distraction of continued litigation."
