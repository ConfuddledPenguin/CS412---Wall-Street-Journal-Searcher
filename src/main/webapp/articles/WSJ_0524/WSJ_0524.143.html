Who's News: Bank of New England Appoints Kent Price Top Financial Officer

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/24/90

BOSTON -- Bank of New England Corp. named Kent Price, a former Citicorp executive, as chief financial officer, continuing the management shake-up at the troubled bank. Mr. Price, 46 years old, will be responsible for, among other things, the continued downsizing of the bank holding company, including the sale of its Maine and Rhode Island banking operations. He will succeed David Payne, who has been chief financial officer and is taking early retirement in July.

At least a half-dozen top executives have left the bank in recent months as its financial difficulties have mounted.

Mr. Price, who is finishing up chores as chief operating officer and chief financial officer for Barr Rosenberg Investment Management in Orinda, Calif., has already started work at the bank's headquarters in Boston.

Prior to a one-year stint at Barr Rosenberg, Mr. Price spent much of the past two decades overseas. He worked for Citicorp for 12 years, heading numerous foreign operations. Four years ago he left Citicorp to take hold of the reins of Chloride, an industrial and electronics concern experiencing financial difficulty. "It was a turnaround situation and I was responsible for reducing the size of that company from about $1.2 billion to $600 million to $700 million," Mr. Price said in an interview.

Analysts viewed Mr. Price's appointment as a positive move, but noted that the bank's future in large part depends on the length and depth of the current economic slump in New England. Bank of New England, among the most aggressive banks in the region in the 1980s, posted a loss of $1.11 billion last year, largely because of problem real estate loans.
