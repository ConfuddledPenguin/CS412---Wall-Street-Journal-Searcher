Contel Sets Venture For Cellular Phone System in Hungary

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/24/90

ATLANTA -- Contel Cellular Inc. said it signed a joint-venture agreement with Hungaria Telekom Kft. to design, build and operate a national cellular telephone system in Hungary. The venture will be owned 50% by Contel Cellular and 50% by private Hungarian investors with Hungaria Telekom. Contel Cellular, however, will have principal management, technical and operational responsibility, and expects to name a managing director and other top personnel soon. Contel Cellular is 90%-owned by Contel Corp.

Contel said the venture is strategically important. Contel Corp. Chairman Charles Wohlstetter said the Hungarian system, which uses the same bandwidth as a system in neighboring Austria, "will provide Contel Cellular with a pre-eminent entry into the Central European market."

Contel said that it expects to provide service to at least 50,000 subscribers by 1993 and that it eventually could serve the all of Hungary's 10.6 million citizens. The company estimates costs will total about $35 million in the first three years.

The venture, which would be the first national cellular system in Eastern Europe, needs approval by the Coordinating Committee for Multilateral Export Controls, or Cocom, an organization of the U.S. and 16 allies that coordinates policy on exports to communist countries. The U.S. has proposed easing Cocom rules to allow the export of all but the most sophisticated telecommunications equipment, and the liberalization is likely to be approved at a high-level Cocom meeting in Paris next month.

The Hungarian government gave the Contel-Hungaria Telekom project an operating concession for 15 years, with a five-year renewal option that expires in 2010. Contel said the venture will construct the system beginning in Budapest, where it faces competition from another venture involving U S West Inc., a Denver-based regional Bell telephone company that has a 49% stake in a venture that plans to develop a cellular system in Budapest. The U S West project also is subject to Cocom approval.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Contel shares closed at $27.75, down 12.5 cents.
