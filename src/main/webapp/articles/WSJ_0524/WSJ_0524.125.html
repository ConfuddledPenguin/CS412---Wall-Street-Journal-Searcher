International: Australia Blocks Merger Of Leading Bank, Insurer

  • Author: Special to the Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 05/24/90

CANBERRA, Australia -- The Australian government said it won't approve a proposed merger of Australia & New Zealand Banking Group Ltd. (ANZ) with National Mutual Life Association of Australasia Ltd. The proposed merger, announced in April, would have created the biggest financial concern in Australia.

Paul Keating, Australia's treasurer, said the merger would be "contrary to the national interest and shouldn't proceed."

ANZ is one of Australia's four biggest banks, while National Mutual is the second-largest life-insurance company. Therefore, Mr. Keating said, a merger "carries substantial implications for the future structure and direction of the financial system in this country."

He said Australia needs effective competition in banking, life insurance and financial services in general.
