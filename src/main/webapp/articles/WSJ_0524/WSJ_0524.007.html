Dividend News: Pinnacle West Sees No Payout For Three Years

  • Author: Frederick Rose
  • Date: 05/24/90

PHOENIX, Ariz. -- Pinnacle West Capital Corp., which suspended its 40-cent-a-share common stock dividend in October, probably won't resume a payout for another three years, Richard Snell, chairman, president and chief executive officer, told the annual meeting. Pinnacle West, a utility holding company, eliminated its payout in the face of mounting losses from its MeraBank thrift unit and considered applying for bankruptcy law protection. MeraBank is now in the hands of federal regulators.

In a 2 1/2-hour meeting punctuated by calls for the board's resignation and by angry suggestions of management incompetence, Mr. Snell repeated Pinnacle West's opposition to a $1.82 billion takeover offer by PacifiCorp and a $500 million bid from the quasi-municipal Salt River Project utility for certain electric service territory. Both offers were made last week and were formally rejected by Pinnacle West on Tuesday.

Mr. Snell said that Pinnacle West can recover from its ill-timed thrift and real estate diversification that has cost the company more than $900 million in losses. In the 12 months ended March 31, Pinnacle West, which includes the profitable Arizona Public Service Co., had net losses of $559.3 million, or $6.44 a share, on $1.5 billion in operating revenue.

"Listen closely to this answer -- this company will survive," Mr. Snell told one testy shareholder.

But many in the audience were less upbeat. "The bottom line is that this board has failed; if this were a college course, they would have flunked," shareholder Richard Baron told Mr. Snell.

About half of the more than 1,000 people in the room stood when asked by a shareholder how many would accept PacifiCorp's cash offer of $21 a share. And questioner after questioner challenged Pinnacle West's rejection of the offer. Mr. Snell later said the informal poll wasn't representative.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Pinnacle West's stock closed at $16, up 12.5 cents, on volume of 553,300 shares.

Officials of PacifiCorp, a utility holding company based in Portland, Ore., didn't directly address the meeting. However, a number of its officials were in the audience.

Verl Topham, a PacifiCorp executive vice president, said following the meeting that PacifiCorp would like to bring its courtship of an unwilling Pinnacle West "to some sort of conclusion and get on with it." PacifiCorp officials have declined to rule out hostile action and have said that they may make a decision shortly.
