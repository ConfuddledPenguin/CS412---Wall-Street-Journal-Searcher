Business Brief -- Pic 'N' Save Corp.: Firm Offers Board Seats, Bid Panel to Hostile Suitor

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/24/90

Pic 'N' Save Corp., struggling to fend off a hostile investor group, offered the group minority representation on its board and on a newly formed committee to evaluate bids to buy the company. The Dominguez, Calif., retailer of closeout merchandise put itself up for sale last week in an apparent effort to discourage shareholders from joining Girard Partners L.P.'s efforts to oust Pic 'N' Save's board. Girard has said it will conduct its own auction for the company if it gains control.

Pic 'N' Save reiterated that it stands ready to negotiate all aspects of a confidentiality agreement with Girard to induce the group to join in the company's bidding process. Pic 'N' Save offered Girard two seats on its board, which would be expanded to nine from seven members.

The new committee would be expanded to five from three members if Girard accepted the proffered board seats. Three of Pic `N' Save's outside directors were named to the committee, headed by Bruce Karatz, president and chief executive officer of Kaufman & Broad Home Corp. The other two outside directors weren't included because they are scheduled to be out of the country until mid-June, a Pic 'N' Save spokesman said.

In La Jolla, Calif., David H. Batchelder, Girard's controlling partner, promptly rejected Pic 'N' Save's offer.
