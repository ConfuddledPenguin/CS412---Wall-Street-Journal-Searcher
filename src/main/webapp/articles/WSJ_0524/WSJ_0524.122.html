Business Brief -- Carnival Cruise Lines Inc.: Cruise Line Files Lawsuit Against Two Finnish Firms

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/24/90

Carnival Cruise Lines Inc., Miami, said it is seeking more than $700 million in damages from two Finnish companies that contracted to build three cruise ships for Carnival. In a suit filed in U.S. District Court in Miami, Carnival alleges that Oy Wartsila AB and Valmet Oy AB, both based in Helsinki, procured contracts to build the ships by misrepresenting the financial condition of their shipbuilding subsidiary, Wartsila Marine Industries Inc.

Wartsila Marine filed for bankruptcy in October 1989 without fulfilling the contracts, Carnival said. Masa Yards, a shipbuilding operation formed by Wartsila Marine's customers, eventually completed construction of two Carnival ships -- the Fantasy, which began service in March, and the Ecstasy. A third ship, the Sensation, is yet to be built.

Officials at Oy Wartsila and Valmet Oy couldn't be reached for comment.
