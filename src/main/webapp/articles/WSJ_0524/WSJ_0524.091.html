Novell Inc.'s Results For Fiscal-2nd Period Surpass Expectations

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/24/90

PROVO, Utah -- Novell Inc. reported higher-than-anticipated results for the fiscal-second quarter, reflecting the continuing boom in the business of tying computers together in networks. For the quarter ended April 28, Novell said profit rose 78% to $20.5 million, or 60 cents a share, from $11.5 million, or 35 cents share, a year ago. Revenue rose 9% to $120 million from $110.4 million.

Most analysts expected earnings of 53 to 54 cents a share and revenue in the range of $114 million to $118 million.

Novell principally provides software that allows personal computers to share printers, information and programs. The company said the latest quarter's results reflect the profitable effect of having a growing share of its revenue -- 75% compared with 63% a year earlier -- come from software sales. "There is a direct correlation between that and our gross margins," a spokesman said.

Novell said the results reflected $400,000 in expenses from its recently aborted merger with Lotus Development Corp., Cambridge, Mass. The $1.5 billion transaction fell through last weekend after the two companies couldn't agree on the composition of a combined board. Novell said "additional immaterial merger costs" would be recognized in the fiscal-third quarter.

For the six months, Novell said profit rose 58% to $35.6 million, or $1.05 a share, on revenue of $225.9 million, from profit of $22.5 million, or 68 cents a share, on revenue of $209.1 million a year earlier.

The results were announced after markets had closed.
