Business Brief -- Sea-Land Services Inc.: Company to Assist Soviets In Handling Rail Freight

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/24/90

Sea-Land Services Inc. said it reached agreements with cargo-shipping interests in the Soviet Union for Sea-Land to share its transportation information and management systems. Alex Mandl, chairman and chief executive officer of Sea-Land, an ocean container ship company, said Sea-Land will help the Soviets improve their handling of containerized freight on the Trans-Siberian Railroad between Asia and Europe. Mr. Mandl said Sea-Land will provide information and management resources, but not major capital dollars.

Edison, N.J.-based Sea-Land is a unit of CSX Corp., a transportation and resource company based in Richmond, Va.

"This is an effort with modest investment to expand our distribution network into that part of the world," said Mr. Mandl. He added that Sea-Land seeks to participate in fees generated by the venture from freight shipments. "It won't translate into huge dollars for us next month, but it will generate good potential for us down the road and it's another step in the U.S. and Russia being able to work together," he said.
