International Brief -- KLM Royal Dutch Airlines: Fiscal 1990 Initial Results Show 9% Drop in Earnings

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/24/90

KLM Royal Dutch Airlines reported that rising aviation fuel prices were partly responsible for a 9% drop in net income in its year ended March 31. Although the results are only preliminary, the Dutch airline said earnings fell to 340 million guilders ($181 million) from 374 million guilders in fiscal 1989. The company will issue final figures June 13.

KLM also said its earnings were hurt by higher levels of depreciation and an increase in financial expenses.

Despite the drop in net, the Dutch airline reported a revenue gain of 8.3% to 6.5 billion guilders from 6 billion guilders.

Operating profit before interest payments fell to 310 million guilders from 390 million guilders in fiscal 1989.
