Business Brief -- Chemical Banking Corp.: McDonald's Franchise Loans Are Packaged Into Securities

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/24/90

Chemical Banking Corp. is packaging into securities a bunch of loans to holders of McDonald's Corp. fast-food franchises. Two Chemical units, Texas Commerce Bank and Chemical Securities Inc., announced the formation of a separate company, Golden Managers Acceptance Corp. That company, known as Golden Mac, is making loans to McDonald's franchise owners for their restaurants or improvements. The loans then will be used as collateral for top-rated commercial paper, a very short-term corporate IOU bought by institutional investors.

Golden Mac is designed to provide existing and new owners of McDonald's franchise restaurants with an alternative source of capital at highly competitive rates, Texas Commerce and Chemical said. Chemical Securities plans to get the program going in early June with the sale of about $20 million of commercial paper. Golden Mac is authorized to issue $125 million of paper, which has been dubbed "McPaper" by some in the market.

The McDonald's program marks the first time that franchise loans have been packaged into securities.
