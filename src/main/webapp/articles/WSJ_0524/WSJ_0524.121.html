Hunt Brothers Win Ruling In $314 Million Tax Case

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/24/90

WASHINGTON -- In a decision that could trigger new wrangling over the remains of the Hunt oil fortune, a U.S. Tax Court judge here ruled that brothers Lamar Hunt, William Herbert Hunt and Nelson Bunker Hunt don't owe $314 million in additional federal income taxes for 1982. The Internal Revenue Service case against the Hunts, which was heard last summer, involved a partnership they established to refinance the huge debts resulting from their speculation in the 1979-1980 silver market. The IRS claimed the partnership was an elaborate ruse used by the brothers to avoid paying taxes on a loan they never repaid.

But in a lengthy decision, Judge Jules G. Korner III sided with the Hunts, ruling that the partnership was valid. He also criticized IRS tactics, such as last-minute subpoenaing of numerous bank officials, calling it an effort to "cast an all-encompassing net in the search for information with which to build a case."

An IRS spokesman said the government hadn't decided whether to appeal the tax court decision. Federal attorneys involved in the case couldn't be reached for comment.
