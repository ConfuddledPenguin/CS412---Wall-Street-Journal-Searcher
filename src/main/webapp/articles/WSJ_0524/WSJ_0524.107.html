International: Banks and Brazil Discuss Debt-Equity Swap Fund

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/24/90

LONDON -- Midland Bank PLC is leading commercial-bank talks with the Brazilian government to establish a $1.5 billion fund of bank claims that can be swapped into equity in state-owned companies Brazil intends to transfer to private hands. A spokesman for Midland -- its $2 billion in loans outstanding to Brazil makes it the country's biggest British banking creditor -- said the company has had "preliminary discussions" with Brazilian officials about the fund. The bank hopes to reach a preliminary accord in the next few months, prior to a September launch. Chase Manhattan Corp., New York, and Bank of Tokyo are among the banks considering joining the fund as sponsors, Midland said.

The discussions come as Brazil, pursuing its bold anti-inflation program, prepares to resume negotiations on its $115 billion foreign debt and mount its drive to sell state companies. Brazil hasn't paid interest on its $65 billion of medium-term and long-term bank debt since September.
