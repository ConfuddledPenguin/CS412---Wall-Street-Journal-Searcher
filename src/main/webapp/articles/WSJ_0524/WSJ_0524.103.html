Business Brief -- CMS Energy Corp.: Panel Limits Proceeds Use From Midland-Assets Sale

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/24/90

Michigan's utility commission restricted the ways CMS Energy Corp. can use $1.5 billion it planned to get after the sale of Midland Cogeneration Venture assets. The order comes after a state court injunction that barred CMS from transferring the funds. CMS was scheduled to receive $1.2 billion in notes and $341 million in equity related to the cogeneration venture from its Consumers Power Co. unit, in exchange for CMS debentures.

CMS stock fell a sharp $2.375 to close at $28.625 yesterday in composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange.

The commission said in its order that CMS, based in Dearborn, can use proceeds from the transaction only "in ways that will secure {Consumers Power's} financial health." The commission said that may include repaying about $750 million in Consumers Power bank debt and maintaining a balanced capital structure for the unit.

CMS called the order "unlawful," particularly the "attempted assertion over the non-utility, non-jurisdictional assets of the company." CMS said it will appeal.
