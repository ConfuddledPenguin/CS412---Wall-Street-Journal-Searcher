Business Brief -- Builders Transport Inc.: Dissident Holder Files Suit Over Annual Meeting Date

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/24/90

A dissident shareholder filed suit, demanding that Builders Transport Inc. set a date for the company's annual meeting. The holder, David C. Walentas, filed the suit in Delaware Chancery Court. Mr. Walentas and Stanford M. Dinstein, another holder, hold a total of roughly 6.8% of the Camden, S.C., trucking company's 4.9 million shares outstanding.

In February, Builders Transport signed a non-binding letter of intent to be acquired for $15 a share, or $73.6 million, by closely held Comcar Industries Inc., an Auburndale, Fla., trucking company. In December, Builders Transport rebuffed a $13-a-share offer from the two shareholders.

Mr. Walentas intends to solicit proxies at the annual meeting in an effort to replace the board, said Jonathan Wagner, attorney for Mr. Walentas.

The company hasn't scheduled the annual meeting because it is reviewing another takeover proposal it received from Trism Inc., the Joplin, Mo., parent of trucking firm Tri-State Motor Transit Inc. of Delaware, said Robert E. Lee Garner, Builders Transport's general counsel and corporate secretary. Trism has proposed to pay $14 cash plus $3 of preferred stock for each Builders Transport share.

The company has sued Mr. Walentas in federal court in Columbia, S.C., charging him with violating securities laws; Mr. Walentas has countersued to invalidate an employee stock ownership plan.
