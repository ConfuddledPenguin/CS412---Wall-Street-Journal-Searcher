CS First Boston Hires Raziano From Shearson

  • Author: William Power
  • Date: 05/24/90

NEW YORK -- CS First Boston Inc., completing a management shakeup, hired a Shearson Lehman Hutton Holdings Inc. executive who directed Shearson's cost-cutting and layoff efforts in recent years. The hiring by CS First Boston of Shearson's chief administrative officer, Robert M. Raziano, enabled CS First Boston to fill two new senior-management posts.

In the more senior level appointment, Anthony P. Grassi, a First Boston Corp. managing director, was named chief financial and administrative officer of CS First Boston. The job includes business planning, budgeting and all financial and administrative systems. First Boston is CS First Boston's U.S. investment bank

Mr. Raziano, meanwhile, will become head of finance, administration, systems and operations at First Boston. Mr. Raziano resigned from Shearson this week and his new job -- which combines the former functions of chief financial officer and the head of administration, systems and operations -- is effective June 18.

Mr. Raziano assumes the duties of former First Boston Chief Financial Officer John E. Toffolon Jr., who resigned, and Fenton R. "Pete" Talbott, the former head of administration and systems who CS First Boston said will assist Mr. Raziano "in the transition."

At Shearson, Mr. Raziano was well known as the central figure in all of that firm's cost-containment efforts, including the brokerage firm's thousands of layoffs. While chief operating officer of Shearson's London unit during 1987, for example, he sliced 150 of the unit's nearly 1,300 employees. CS First Boston wouldn't say it hired Mr. Raziano simply to help with layoffs, but that is clearly part of his job.

"The reason we have hired Mr. Raziano is because he is one of the best people on the Street in finance, administration, systems and operations," said a CS First Boston spokesman, "and clearly part of that function is making sure we operate as efficiently as possible."

A Shearson spokesman said there are no plans at this time to find a successor to Mr. Raziano. Because Shearson plans soon to split into separate brokerage and investment-banking units, Mr. Raziano's old job won't exist anymore but will probably be divided up.

CS First Boston has been in a cost-cutting mode. The firm, which is 44.5%-owned by the parent of Swiss bank Credit Suisse, reported a slim $11 million profit last year; this year it is trying to cut costs by at least 10%, and is expected to trim hundreds of employees from its world-wide staff of 6,000.
