Technology: Telecommunications Firms Attract Venture Capital

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/24/90

NEW YORK -- Telecommunications companies attracted the most computer-technology venture capital investment in 1989 while funding of computer software and services companies fell sharply, according to a survey by Technologic Partners. Of the $1.24 billion invested, more than 25%, or $320 million, went to telecommunications products and services companies. That's an increase of 55%, said Technologic Partners, a Manhattan-based research firm. It found that 1988's No. 1 target for investment, computer software and services, fell 23% to $208 million.

"In the last five years some of the most successful new companies have been telecommunications companies," said Richard A. Shaffer, principal at Technologic. Meanwhile, he said, the computer business has become a hodgepodge of technologies and players that confounds investors.

"Seed," or early stage financing, a growing segment in the past five years, was another loser in 1989, falling 10% to $62 million, or 33 seed financings, compared with $69 million, or 41 seeds, in 1988.

While later-stage, or "mezzanine," financings also declined, middle-stage investments garnered greater support from investors who held back until companies were well under way. Mr. Shaffer said this should continue as long as the market for initial public offerings remains slow.

Other top targets for investment, according to Technologic, included computer peripheral companies, third-ranked at $140 million; integrated circuit manufacturers ($133 million); and computer systems suppliers ($130 million).
