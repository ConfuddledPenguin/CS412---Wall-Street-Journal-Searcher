Prime Motor Inns To Sell 65% Stake In Franchise Systems

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/24/90

FAIRFIELD, N.J. -- Prime Motor Inns Inc. said it agreed to sell a 65% stake in its Ramada and Howard Johnson franchise systems to an investment partnership. The agreement calls for Prime to retain roughly a 35% equity stake in a joint venture consisting of 490 Ramada locations and 430 Howard Johnson locations. The joint venture will be managed by the investment partnership, Blackstone Capital Partners L.P., an affiliate of the Blackstone Group.

Prime, one of the largest U.S. hotel companies, is retaining its AmeriSuites and Wellesley Inns chains and its Rodeway Inns international franchise system.

Prime also said it had signed a confidentiality agreement related to the Ramada and Howard Johnson franchises with Manor Care Inc., the Silver Spring, Md.-based franchiser of rival Quality Inns hotels. When Prime first announced its accord with Blackstone earlier this month, Manor Care made public a letter to Prime indicating interest in buying the two franchises without making a formal offer.

Prime said its agreement with Blackstone allows Prime's board to entertain other offers made before the transaction with Blackstone closes, if Blackstone is paid certain unspecified fees. It isn't clear when the transaction, subject to certain conditions, is expected to close.

Under the agreement with Blackstone, Prime will receive about $140 million, including $133 million in cash and $7 million in receivables, plus other consideration which Prime didn't outline. The company previously valued the total transaction at about $200 million, including about $60 million in notes.

An official recently said Prime had paid down about $40 million of its $320 million bank debt, and that proceeds from the Blackstone accord would be used to pay half the remaining $280 million.

Manor Care Inc. couldn't be reached for comment on the confidentiality agreement it signed with Prime Motor Inns.
