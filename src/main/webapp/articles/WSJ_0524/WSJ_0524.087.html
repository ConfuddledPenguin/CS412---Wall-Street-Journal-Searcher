Lawrence Brothers And Chairman Deny Price-Fixing Charges

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/24/90

NEW YORK -- Lawrence Brothers Inc. and Chairman John A. Lawrence denied any wrongdoing in connection with price-fixing charges filed against them and other makers of architectural hinges. The Sterling, Ill., company and the executive were among nine defendants, including the New Britain, Conn., tool maker Stanley Works, that were indicted by a federal grand jury in St. Louis recently.

Stanley Works and the president of its Stanley Hardware division, Richard G. Martin, have said they would fight the charges. An attorney for John F. Hollfelder, a former vice president of sales for the Stanley division, declined comment.

An attorney for Hager Hinge Co. of St. Louis said the company "has been aware of the investigation and has been working with the government since its very early stages."

Scranton, Pa.-based McKinney Products Co., its president, David B. Gibson, and a former president of McKinney, Robert A. Haversat, declined to comment. Mr. Haversat now is president and chief executive officer at Esstar Inc., the New Haven, Conn., parent of McKinney.

In July 1988, Stanley Works had said it had received a subpoena from a federal grand jury investigating possible antitrust violations in the architectural-hardware industry.
