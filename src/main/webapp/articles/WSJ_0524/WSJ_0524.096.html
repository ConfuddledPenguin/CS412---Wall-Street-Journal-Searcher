AT&T and Soviets Agree To Explore Joint Venture

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/24/90

NEW YORK -- American Telephone & Telegraph Co. agreed with the Soviet Ministry of Communications to explore joint ventures in telecommunications services, equipment manufacturing, and research and development. AT&T said it will send scientists from AT&T Bell Laboratories to Moscow later this year to discuss projects ranging from digital switching technology to satellite networks aimed at upgrading the Soviet Union's rickety phone system.

News of the agreement, signed quietly earlier this month, surfaced in CommunicationsWeek International, an industry newspaper. Last December, AT&T agreed to use the U.S.S.R.'s Intersputnik satellite network for the first time to handle communications traffic between the U.S. and the Soviet Union, a proposal the State Department has asked the Federal Communications Commission to reject.

AT&T also is seeking to supply fiberoptic cable and transmission equipment to one of Moscow's biggest telecommunications projects, planned construction of an 8,680-mile, $500 million cable linking Europe and Asia.

In serving technology-starved Eastern Europe, AT&T Network Systems International recently received a contract to supply digital switching equipment to Poland to handle international phone traffic. Yesterday, AT&T Bell Labs said 10 of Poland's top telecommunications engineers will visit Bell Labs as part of a free training program sponsored by the U.S. Telecommunications Training Institute, also hosted by BellSouth Corp., Digital Equipment Corp. and MCI Communications Corp., AT&T said.
