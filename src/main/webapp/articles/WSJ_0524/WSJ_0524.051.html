Who's News: Nomura Securities Hires Salomon Program Trader

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/24/90

NEW YORK -- In another sign that Nomura Securities Co. is making a major commitment to program trading worldwide, the Japanese firm said it hired Michael Halem from Salomon Brothers Inc., a unit of Salomon Inc. Program trading is the simultaneous, rapid-fire buying or selling of 15 or more stocks. A disputed form of such trading, stock-index arbitrage, involves large, computer-assisted stock sales or purchases with offsetting tradings in stock-index futures. The new post for Mr. Halem, 31 years old, follows Nomura's recent hiring of Joseph R. Schmuckler, who resigned from a pivotal program trading post at Kidder, Peabody & Co., a General Electric Co. unit, in March to join Nomura.

While at Salomon, Mr. Halem helped develop that firm's extensive program trading efforts in Japanese stock markets.
