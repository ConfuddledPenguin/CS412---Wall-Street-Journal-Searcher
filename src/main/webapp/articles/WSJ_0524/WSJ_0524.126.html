Negotiators Are Planning To Speed Budget Talks

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/24/90

WASHINGTON -- Congressional and White House negotiators agreed to pick up the pace of budget summit talks after Congress returns from its Memorial Day recess. "It's important that we get over the seminar stage and into the negotiating stage within a brief period," White House Budget Director Richard Darman said. "If they {the talks} drag on and on, it tends to defeat the purpose."

At a meeting yesterday, Mr. Darman and the congressional coordinators of the talks, House Majority Leader Richard Gephardt (D., Mo.) and Sen. Wyche Fowler (D., Ga.), scheduled morning and afternoon sessions for June 6 and 7, aides said. Their hope is to finish reviewing deficit forecasts and previously proposed deficit-reduction plans at those sessions or, if necessary, at another session on June 12.

After that, Mr. Darman said, "I would hope we get down to talking about possible compromises." Democrats insist the next step is for the administration to offer a solution to the deficit problem they have been outlining, but it isn't clear the administration is willing to go along.

So far, the talks have been taking place only for two or three hours Tuesday and Thursday afternoons. They continue this afternoon. Congress isn't in session next week.
