Credit Ratings: WPPSS Bonds Are Given A Higher Rating by S&P

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/24/90

NEW YORK -- Standard & Poor's Corp. said it raised the rating on Washington Public Power Supply System's $6.3 billion of revenue bonds outstanding for WPPSS units 1, 2 and 3 to double-A from double-A-minus. The rating concern said it also upgraded to double-A from double-A-minus $17.1 million of WPPSS bonds issued for the Hanford generating project and $119 million of bonds issued by the Eugene, Ore., Water & Electric Board for the Trojan nuclear project.

Standard & Poor's also said it has given a double-A rating to the supply system's planned offering next month of between $600 million and $800 million of refunding revenue bonds for WPPSS units 2 and 3, which didn't default.

The higher credit rating "reflects the improved financial flexibility of the Bonneville Power Administration," which secures all these bonds through net billing agreements, S&P said.

Scott Clements, WPPSS manager of refinancing and investment operations, said the supply system was delighted by the upgrade. "We think Bonneville will continue to benefit from the refunding as we complete the rest of the program, hopefully before the end of the year," Mr. Clements said.
