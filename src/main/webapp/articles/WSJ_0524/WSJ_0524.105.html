International Brief -- Kingfisher PLC: British Government Denies Retailer's Takeover Attempt

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/24/90

The United Kingdom's Monopolies and Mergers Commission said that it wouldn't allow retailer Kingfisher PLC to proceed with its hostile #461 million bid for retailer Dixons PLC. The bid was referred to the commission in January. The commission concluded that the proposed acquisition would have combined the two largest retailers of electrical goods and would bring about a "significant reduction" in the competition of electrical retailing.

The Kingfisher bid had valued each Dixons share at 120 pence, and was conditional on its not being referred to the commission.

Kingfisher's units hold a combined market share of about 9.6%, while Dixons and its electrical-goods retail subsidiary hold a market share of about 16%. Analysts reckon the percentages for both companies are significantly higher in some areas.

Both Kingfisher and Dixons suspended their shares on the London Stock Exchange pending the announcement, then resumed trading, with the former closing up at 311 pence and the latter closing down at 124 pence.
