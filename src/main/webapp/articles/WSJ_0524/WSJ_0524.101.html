Security Pacific Affiliate To Increase Local Lending

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/24/90

LOS ANGELES -- Security Pacific Corp.'s Pacific Northwest affiliate said it would make a minimum $1 billion in community-based loans during the next 10 years, the latest big bank to boost its support for such projects. "What is new is the fact that we are making a public commitment to a minimum dollar amount for an extended period of time," the Seattle affiliate, Security Pacific Bancorp. Northwest, said.

The commitment covers Washington, Oregon and Alaska, and is aimed primarily at low-income and moderate-income housing and small-sized and minority-owned business, officials said. The Security Pacific affiliate has about $10.2 billion in assets. Last year, its community loans approximately totaled $83 million, and so the latest commitment, averaging $100 million annually, would represent roughly a 20% annual increase.

Big banks have come under some pressure in recent years from local activists and federal officials to boost such lending, which is generally mandated by the federal Community Reinvestment Act. Security Pacific Corp. and Wells Fargo & Co., based in San Francisco, announced large commitments of their own earlier this year.

"Top managements are recognizing there may be less risk in inner-city loans than with some of the other loans in their portfolio," said Robert Gnaizda, a San Francisco public-interest lawyer who has met with Security Pacific officials over community-lending policies.
