Ex-Consumers' Gas Official Plans Offer If Rival Bid Barred

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/24/90

TORONTO -- A retired executive of Consumers' Gas Co. said he plans to lead an effort to acquire the company, if he can persuade regulators to prevent its proposed acquisition by British Gas PLC for 1.1 billion Canadian dollars (US$927 million). F. Warren Hurst, the retired official, said he formed a private company, ProNational Gas Inc., which filed an objection to the acquisition plan with the Ontario Energy Board. The ProNational intervention argues that an acquisition of Canada's largest gas utility by a foreign concern wouldn't be in the public and customers' interests.

Mr. Hurst said he hasn't tried to raise financing, because there isn't any assurance the Ontario board will reject the British Gas proposal. GW Utilities Ltd., which is controlled by the Reichmann family of Toronto, already has agreed to tender its 82% stake in Consumers' Gas to the British Gas offer. GW Utilities officials weren't available to comment on Mr. Hurst's announcement.

The Ontario board is expected to hold hearings on the takeover bid next month. If it rejects the application by British Gas, Mr. Hurst said, ProNational will seek to raise short-term financing for a bid with the same C$34-a-share terms to be offered under the British company's proposal. He said ProNational would then resell most of the shares to Consumers' Gas's customers and other parties.

Mr. Hurst, 64 years old, was chief financial officer of Consumers' Gas until 1971 and a director until 1973. He described himself as semi-retired, but remains on several boards. He said he has other partners in ProNational, but declined to name them.

Mr. Hurst's announcement didn't hurt stock prices: Consumers' Gas shares rose 37.5 Canadian cents yesterday, to close at C$31.125 on the Toronto Stock Exchange. GW Utilities shares closed at $28.50, down 12.5 cents, in American Stock Exchange composite trading.
