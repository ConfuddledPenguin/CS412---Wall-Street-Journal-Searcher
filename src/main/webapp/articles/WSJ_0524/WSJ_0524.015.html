GE Says It Settled Its Lawsuit Against Daimler-Benz AG

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/24/90

FAIRFIELD, Conn. -- General Electric Co. said it settled its suit against Daimler-Benz AG that had charged the West German company with breaking its agreement to jointly develop a new generation of jet engines. Under the settlement, GE said it will get additional revenue from their existing long-term programs with Daimler-Benz on engines that generate 50,000 to 75,000 pounds of thrust. These engines are used in Boeing 747, the Airbus A310 and McDonnell Douglas MD-11 airplanes.

GE, however, agreed to end its agreement with Daimler on developing the next generation of engines with 85,000 to 95,000 pounds of thrust, the so-called GE90 engines.

GE sued Daimler-Benz last April when, it alleged, the German concern's Motorenund Turbinen-Union unit, known as MTU, broke the agreement with GE and entered into "a memorandum of understanding" with United Technologies Corp. to pursue a wide range of commercial engine programs.

As part of the settlement, MTU will destroy all proprietary information it has on the GE90 engine. MTU will be free to proceed with its agreement with United Technologies' Pratt & Whitney unit to develop the PW4000 engine, which will compete with GE90.

GE declined to say how much additional revenue it will receive as a result of the renegotiated terms on existing long-term contracts with MTU. But an official familiar with the settlement estimated the amount to be "several hundred million dollars" over 10 to 15 years.

Daimler-Benz couldn't be reached for comment.
