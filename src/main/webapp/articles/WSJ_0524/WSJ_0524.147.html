Dun & Bradstreet Acts To Sell the Majority Of Its Donnelley Unit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/24/90

NEW YORK -- Dun & Bradstreet Corp. said it put up for sale most of its Donnelley Marketing division, continuing its policy of shedding nonstrategic businesses. Donnelley Marketing is a leading direct-mail distributor of cents-off coupons and consumer merchandise, and also provides mailing lists and mailing services. D&B said the only other segment of Donnelley Marketing -- Donnelley Marketing Information Services, which markets demographic information -- isn't for sale.

Without the information-services segment, D&B said, Donnelley Marketing had 1989 revenue of about $225 million, or 5.2% of D&B's total revenue. The financial-information services giant said Donnelley Marketing is profitable, but wouldn't give any further details.

D&B also refused to disclose the price it expects to get for most of Donnelley Marketing, but one analyst said it will likely fetch in excess of $200 million. Industry insiders speculated that the price could go as high as $400 million or $500 million.

The sale decision fits into D&B's recent policy of selling businesses that don't fit into its overall strategy. In recent months, D&B has announced plans to sell five such units: Datastream International, Interactive Data, Neodata Services, Petroleum Information and Zytron. D&B has retained investment bank Goldman, Sachs & Co. to facilitate the sales.

D&B is focusing on information services for three areas: risk management, marketing, and yellow-pages directories; and on software for business applications.

Joseph Laird, an analyst who follows D&B for Veronis, Suhler & Associates Inc., said the sale made sense. "The part that they're shedding is emphatically not an information business, while the part that they're keeping is," he said. "It's a perfectly understandable decision."
