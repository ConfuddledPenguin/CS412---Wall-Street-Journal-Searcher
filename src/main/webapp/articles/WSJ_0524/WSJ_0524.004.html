West German Bankruptcies

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/24/90

BONN -- West German business bankruptcies dropped 8.6% in the first quarter of 1990 from year-earlier levels, continuing a trend of declining insolvencies that began in 1986, the Wiesbaden-based Federal Statistics Office said. The number of business bankruptcies fell to 2,321 during the quarter from 2,540 a year earlier. The total number of insolvencies, which includes businesses and individuals, dropped 7.6% to 3,529 from 3,819 a year earlier.

A Statistics Office official said the declining numbers are probably illustrative of the improving economic climate in West Germany. He added that the average size of the insolvencies has been shrinking steadily because fewer big companies are going bankrupt.
