Who's News: Carbide Names Lichtenberger To Added Posts

  • Author: Mary Lu Carnevale
  • Date: 05/24/90

Union Carbide Corp. appointed H. William Lichtenberger, who has been president of its chemicals and plastics unit for the past four years, to the additional post of president and chief operating officer of the corporation. Robert D. Kennedy, chairman and chief executive officer, gave up the title of president, and the chief operating officer slot is a new one at Carbide.

In addition, Mr. Lichtenberger, 54 years old, and Joseph E. Geoghan, 52, vice president, general counsel and secretary, were elected directors, bringing board membership to 14.

The Danbury, Conn., company also named John A. Clerico chief financial officer. Mr. Clerico, 48, has been vice president, treasurer and principal financial officer, and has filled the top financial officer slot since J. Clayton Stephenson, former vice chairman and chief financial officer, retired a year ago.

Mr. Kennedy, in an interview, said the promotions "recognize some very capable people," and take some pressure off him. Mr. Kennedy, 57, said that Mr. Lichtenberger's new responsibilities "will broaden his experience." Asked whether the moves were intended to set up a succession, he said: "We don't get into the business of nominating heirs apparent. But the organization speaks for itself."

Mr. Lichtenberger joined the big chemical company 31 years ago as a research engineer and held a number of positions in Carbide's industrial gases business before being named president of the solvents and coatings materials division in 1985. In 1986, he was named president of the chemicals and plastics operation. The unit had $5.6 billion in revenue in 1989 and contributed nearly 80% to the company's operating profit last year.

Reporting to Mr. Lichtenberger are vice presidents for strategic planning, human resources and the controller and principal accounting officer. Other top executives will continue to report to Mr. Kennedy.
