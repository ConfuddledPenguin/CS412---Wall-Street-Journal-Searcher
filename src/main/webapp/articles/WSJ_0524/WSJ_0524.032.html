Safety Agency Intensifies Probe of Hyundai Excels

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/24/90

WASHINGTON -- The National Highway Traffic Safety Administration intensified its investigation into alleged steering-wheel vibration problems in 1987-1989 model Hyundai Excels. In recent months, the agency and the manufacturer have received almost 300 complaints about the budget cars, which are made by Hyundai Motor Corp. of South Korea. Owners allege that strong vibrations in the steering wheels make the cars difficult to control at highway speeds. There have been reports of two accidents and two injuries in connection with the alleged defect.

The stepped-up inquiry, which covers 707,000 cars, is known as an engineering analysis, the second of NHTSA's three levels of investigation. The agency plans to conduct additional tests of the cars and to analyze the two reported accidents.

The agency also said that it has begun a preliminary evaluation -- its lowest level investigation -- into alleged brake problems in 1986-1989 Ford Taurus and Mercury Sable cars. Both are made by Ford Motor Co. The agency has received two complaints alleging failure of brake disk hubs. About 1.2 million vehicles are covered by the inquiry. No accidents or injuries have been reported.
