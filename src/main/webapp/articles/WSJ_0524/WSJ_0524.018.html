Hunter, Ex-Partner Of Dominelli, Pleads Guilty to 4 Charges

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/24/90

SAN DIEGO -- Nancy Hoover Hunter, former girlfriend and business partner of convicted swindler J. David Dominelli, pleaded guilty to four felony charges in exchange for immunity from further criminal prosecution. Mrs. Hunter, 51 years old, pleaded guilty in federal court here to single counts of mail fraud and lying to a Commodities Futures Trading Commission agent. She also pleaded guilty to two counts of selling unregistered securities in connection with a currency-trading scam that bilked investors out of about $80 million.

Neither Mrs. Hunter nor her two attorneys could immediately be reached for comment.

The charges stemmed from money Mrs. Hunter received from Mr. Dominelli, who is serving a 20-year sentence for admittedly masterminding the scheme. Mrs. Hunter was an executive in J. David & Co., La Jolla, Calif., from 1979 until 1984, when the firm was forced into bankruptcy.

Under the plea agreement, Mrs. Hunter won't spend any more time in prison than the 10-year sentence she received in March for tax evasion related to her former links with Mr. Dominelli. Jury selection was to have started Tuesday for her retrial on 192 counts; the first panel of jurors had deadlocked.
