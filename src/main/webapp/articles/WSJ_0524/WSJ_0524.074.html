Politics & Policy: How Poll Was Conducted

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/24/90

The Wall Street Journal/NBC News poll was based on nationwide telephone interviews of 1,007 registered voters conducted last Friday through Tuesday by the polling organizations of Peter Hart and Robert Teeter. The sample was drawn from 263 randomly selected geographic points in the continental U.S. Each region of the country was represented in proportion to its population. Households were selected by a method that gave all telephone numbers, listed and unlisted, an equal chance of being included. One registered voter, 18 years or older, was selected from each household by a procedure to provide the correct number of male and female respondents. The results of the survey were minimally weighted by education and marital status.

Chances are 19 of 20 that if all registered voters in the U.S. had been surveyed using the same questionnaire, the findings would differ from these poll results by no more than 3.2 percentage points in either direction. The margin of error for subgroups would be larger.
