Hong Kong Legal Officer Sentenced for Corruption

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/09/90

HONG KONG -- Despite pleas for leniency and promises of future cooperation with government investigators, the former chief of the commercial crime unit of Hong Kong's Legal Department was sentenced to eight years in prison for corruption. Charles Warwick Reid last month pleaded guilty to a charge of possessing assets deemed disproportionate to his official income.

At Friday's sentencing, Hong Kong's chief justice, Sir Ti Liang Yang, ordered Mr. Reid to pay the local government HK$12.4 million ($1.6 million), the value of his assets when he was formally charged in March 1989. Mr. Reid has 28 days to appeal the court's decision.

Chief Justice Yang allowed a two-year reduction of the maximum penalty of 10 years because of Mr. Reid's guilty plea and his willingness to cooperate with the government.

Mr. Reid's lawyer, Anthony Corrigan, Queen's Counsel, argued that the court's leniency would elicit assistance from the defendant in bringing other corrupt government officials and businessmen to trial.
