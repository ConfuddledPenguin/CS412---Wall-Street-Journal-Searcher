N.V. Philips to Sell Certain Operations To Royal Nedlloyd

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/09/90

EINDHOVEN, the Netherlands -- N.V. Philips said it plans to sell its Dutch transport and freight-forwarding operations to Royal Nedlloyd Group N.V. Philips and Nedlloyd didn't disclose terms of the planned sale but said the operations involved employ about 450 people. The two companies said they are still discussing the services that Philips would need under the new arrangement.

By turning over its transport and logistics departments to outside specialists, the Dutch electronics giant is seeking to concentrate on its core businesses.

For Rotterdam-based Nedlloyd, the purchase would be the latest in a series of acquisitions that have boosted its annual revenue from road transport to about two billion Dutch guilders ($1.1 billion) from 700 million guilders two years ago. The acquisitions have made Nedlloyd one of Europe's biggest trucking and logistics companies and reduced its dependence on the depressed ocean-shipping trade.
