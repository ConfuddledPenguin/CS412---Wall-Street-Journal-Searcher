Pizza Chain Faces Charge Of Religious Bias in Hiring

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/09/90

BALTIMORE (AP) -- The state Human Relations Commission has charged Domino's Pizza Inc. with religious discrimination for refusing to hire a bearded Sikh man, whose religion bars him from shaving. The nationwide chain requires all employees to be cleanshaven, but no Maryland health law prohibits food service employees from having beards.

The June 28 commission complaint said that Prabhjot S. Kohli, of Catonsville, was refused employment when he applied for a job as manager of a Domino's store in December 1987.

Herb James, personnel director of LOOC Inc., the Domino's franchise that interviewed Mr. Kohli, said the complaint was "a slap in the face to us when we are desperately trying to work things out with this individual."

Mr. James, quoted in Saturday's Baltimore Sun, said Mr. Kohli knew the dress code and policies when he interviewed for the job and that he also was unwilling to work the weekend and night hours required. He said other company jobs that did not involve contact with the public were available, but Mr. Kohli refused to consider them.

Mr. Kohli, now an engineer with the State Highway Administration, said he applied for the advertised position of senior manager because he had 18 years' experience as a salesman. He said he was not interested in the other jobs.

The complaint, filed with the state administrative hearing examiner, asks that Domino's pay Mr. Kohli for two years of lost earnings and that he be hired as a Domino's manager. The examiner's office will hold a public hearing later.
