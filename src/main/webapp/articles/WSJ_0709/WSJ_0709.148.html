REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: Captive Customers

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/09/90

The currency union in Germany worked well last week, but was marred when some stores charged East Germans exorbitant prices for food and other basics. Some reports attributed the price-gouging to capitalist greed, but the East German government discovered the real problem. The two state-run grocery chains set up by the communists had suddenly become unregulated state monopolies and could charge what they wanted. Last Friday, the East Germans began allowing local governments to sell the stores to private buyers. By selling the stores to different investors this instant privatization should drive prices down quickly.
