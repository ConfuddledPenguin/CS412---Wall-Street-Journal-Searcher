Business Brief -- Volkswagen AG: U.S. Sales Arm Increases Five '91 Models' Base Prices

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/09/90

Volkswagen AG's U.S. sales arm in Troy, Mich., said it increased base prices of five 1991 models an average of 2%. The West German auto maker increased by $200 each the base prices of its Golf GL two-door, to $8,895, and its GTI and Jetta GL two-door, both to $10,195, and boosted by $220 the price of its Passat GL Sedan to $14,990. The VW Vanagon base price rose $280 to $14,360. VW said it will announce prices of its two remaining cars, the Cabriolet and Corrado, when these 1991 models arrive in showrooms.

Volkswagen has been holding down its prices to counter slumping U.S. sales in recent years. "This minimal pricing move will have little impact, because in October of 1989, retail prices were reduced by 6.7% to 16.6% on these same models, with exception of the Passat, which was introduced in March at an extremely competitive price," a company spokesman said.

Volkswagen's move follows a rare mid-year price increases by the U.S. Big Three auto makers, which were seeking to mitigate "sticker shock" when they announce prices on this fall's 1991 models.
