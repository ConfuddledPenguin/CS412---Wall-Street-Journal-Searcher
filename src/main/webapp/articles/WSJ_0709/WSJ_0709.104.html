Brunswick Corp. Plans To Close Eight Plants, Lay Off 610 Workers

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/09/90

SKOKIE, Ill. -- Brunswick Corp. said it plans to close eight plants and lay off 610 workers in response to "ongoing softness in the demand for marine products." This maker of pleasure boats said the moves are a continuation in its consolidation process, started last fall.

Brunswick said the employee cutbacks will affect 8% of the work force in two of its largest divisions, the U.S. Marine and the Sea Ray. It expects to take action within the next two months.

The company said it plans to close four of its U.S. Marine division's boatbuilding plants on Sept. 10. It will also close a plant that produces component parts.

As a result of those five plant closings, Brunswick said 350 workers will be laid off. The plants' current production operations will be transferred to the U.S. Marine division's remaining 15 plants.

Also, Brunswick said its Sea Ray division is laying off 260 employees at three plants, but the plants' production operations will continue.

The company said that these actions will not hurt its 1990 financial results. Instead, it anticipates that "they will improve efficiencies and cut costs."
