Business Brief -- CMS Energy Corp.: Curbs Lifted on Firm's Use Of Debt, Equity in Plant

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/09/90

CMS Energy Corp. said a Michigan circuit court judge removed restrictions on the company's use of its $1.5 billion of debt and equity interests in the Midland Cogeneration Venture. But the court said that CMS's Consumer's Power Co. utility unit must maintain a $450 million corporate bond, as ordered earlier by Michigan regulators.

In its ruling, the court removed all restrictions on the use by Dearborn, Mich.-based CMS of the $1.2 billion in notes and $300 million in equity it received in return for its contribution to the cogeneration venture. The venture is a 1,380-megawatt power plant converted from CMS's failed effort to build a nuclear plant in Midland.

The court action also relieved CMS of having to file a $100 million surety bond previously required by another court.

CMS said the decision will become effective Aug. 3, and the company isn't planning an appeal.
