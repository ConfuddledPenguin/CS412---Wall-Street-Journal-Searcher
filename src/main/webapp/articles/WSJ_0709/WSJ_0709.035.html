Stockbrokers' Average Pay Increased 10.4% Last Year

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/09/90

NEW YORK -- Wall Street may be suffering from a recession, but the lowly stockbroker still commands a good paycheck. Average earnings of U.S. securities brokers who deal with the public rose 10.4% last year to $78,711 a broker, the Securities Industry Association reported in its annual survey of broker pay.

It was the first gain in broker pay since 1986, when average broker earnings rose to a record $97,100 from $79,575 in 1985, the securities association said.

Why the pay gain last year? Brokers who serve the public get paid largely through commissions on transactions, and their commissions are higher when the market rises. Last year, the Dow Jones Industrial Average rose about 27%. Also, the pay gains reflect a business pickup last year at various regional brokerage firms taking part in the survey.

The securities association's pay survey covered 52 member brokerage firms, which employ 39,000 sales people, or close to half of the industry's total sales personnel.
