Discovery Airways Loses U.S. Operating Authority

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/09/90

WASHINGTON -- The government revoked Discovery Airways' operating authority, saying the airline failed to take steps to ensure that it isn't owned or controlled by foreigners. The Transportation Department said Discovery failed to meet several conditions it imposed when it granted the airline provisional operating authority last spring. Under U.S. law, foreigners can't control or own more than 25% of the stock in American airlines.

Hawaiian Airlines, a unit of HAL Inc., had filed suits charging that Discovery was foreign owned.

Transportation Secretary Samuel Skinner said that even after being given "numerous opportunities to satisfy our concerns over its foreign control and compliance problems . . . {Discovery} has failed to do so or even to show any credible inclination to do so."

The department had required that Philip Ho, owner of 75% of Discovery's voting stock and president of Japanese-owned Nansay Hawaii, place his Discovery shares in a voting trust to prevent Nansay from "exercising control over Discovery through Mr. Ho." It also ordered the carrier to appoint an executive committee to direct corporate policy and to prohibit one of the airline's founders, an Italian citizen, from serving on it or performing any liaison functions.

The Transportation Department said Discovery still can seek a permanent license in a hearing before an administrative law judge.
