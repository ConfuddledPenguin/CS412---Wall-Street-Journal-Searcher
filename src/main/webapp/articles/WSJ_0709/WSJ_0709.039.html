Program Trading Accounts For Less Big Board Volume

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/09/90

NEW YORK -- Program trading accounted for an average of 10.9% of New York Stock Exchange volume, or 15.6 million shares daily, in the week ended June 22, the exchange said. This was down from an unusually large 22.3% of volume the previous week, which included program trading associated with the quarterly expiration on June 15 of stock-index futures and options.

The Big Board's latest weekly data on program trading showed that the biggest program traders in the week ended June 22, accounting for about 70% of all program activity, were Morgan Stanley Group Inc., the Kidder, Peabody & Co. unit of General Electric Co., Susquehanna Investment Group, the First Boston Corp. unit of CS First Boston Inc., and Merrill Lynch & Co.

Program trading is the simultaneous, rapid-fire purchase or sale of 15 or more, and often hundreds, of stocks. The most common form is stock-index arbitrage, in which stocks are bought and sold with offsetting trades of futures and options. The Big Board said that of the five most active program-trading firms, only Merrill Lynch's program trading didn't involve index arbitrage.
