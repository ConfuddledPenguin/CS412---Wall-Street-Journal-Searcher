St. Paul Bancorp Posts Loss of $20.8 Million For Second Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/09/90

CHICAGO -- St. Paul Bancorp Inc. reported a loss of $20.8 million, or $1.72 a share, for the second quarter after the federal Office of Thrift Supervision ordered its primary subsidiary to increase loan-loss reserves. The thrift said the added reserves don't relate to any new specific loan problems but instead reflect more stringent standards. St. Paul said it disagrees with the regulators' ruling and retains the right to seek arbitration of the additional $26 million general reserve ordered for its St. Paul Federal Bank for Savings unit.

In the year-earlier quarter, St. Paul posted net income of $5.4 million, or 45 cents a share.

The company also said it agreed with thrift regulators to confine future multifamily lending to Illinois or within 100 miles of its Chicago home office, and to reduce the concentration of such loans secured by property outside Illinois. Previously, St. Paul had been a significant lender in California as part of a nationwide business strategy.

As a result, St. Paul said its bank's loan-origination volumes are expected to decrease significantly. Separately, St. Paul said it is re-evaluating its policies on possible mergers and acquisitions. "We've become more cautious," a spokesman said.
