ABF Mulls an Offer To Berisford for Some Of Its Food Concerns

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 07/09/90

LONDON -- Associated British Foods PLC, or ABF, said it's considering making an offer for British Sugar and other food-manufacturing companies of Berisford International PLC, the troubled United Kingdom food, financial-services, property, commodities and industrial group. Berisford previously had said it was willing to sell any part of its business to reduce its net debt of #1.02 billion ($1.82 billion). At the same time, the company reported a sharp deterioration in net earnings attributable to shareholders as a result of provisions taken against New York property investments.

ABF, which owns 23% of Berisford, said the only solution open to Berisford is the early sale of its profitable British Sugar unit. Analysts have estimated the sale of the sugar operations could fetch as much as #1 billion.

A spokesman for Berisford said the two companies had been involved in talks for two weeks with a view to ABF making an offer for all of Berisford. "There are several people who have been discussing various things with Berisford," he said.

He added that some of those involved in talks were foreign companies.

Harry Bailey, ABF's chief executive officer, said ABF wants to "sit down and discuss" making an offer for British Sugar that could also include Berisford's flavors, fragrances, and fruits and nuts importing units as well as businesses that are associated with British Sugar, like its beat pulp unit.

ABF said an early sale of British Sugar would allow Berisford to realize optimum value for its remaining assets and reduce the need for forced sales.

Mr. Bailey said ABF had #1 billion in cash available for a purchase, although he said that figure couldn't be used as an indication of what ABF considered British Sugar to be worth.

British Sugar is the main component of Berisford's Bristar food and agribusiness division. In the six months ended March 31, British Sugar's profit before interest and taxes rose 19% to #59.1 million from #49.6 million the year before. Berisford's overall profit before interest dropped 21% to #57.2 million from #72.6 million.

In its latest results, ABF said pretax profit for the year to March 31 jumped 20% to #284 million from #237 million and earnings per share rose 18% to 14.67 pence from 12.4 pence.
