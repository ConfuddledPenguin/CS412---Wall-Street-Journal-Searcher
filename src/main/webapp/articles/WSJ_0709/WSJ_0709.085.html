Sale of Spain's Enasa Threatened As Man May Pull Out of Plan

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/09/90

MADRID -- Spanish truck maker Empresa Nacional de Autocamiones S.A., or Enasa, looks increasingly like it will have to seek an alternative to plans for its linkup with MAN AG and Daimler-Benz AG. Though Spanish officials insist that they still hope to go ahead with the plan to sell 80% of Enasa to the two West German companies, the likelihood that MAN will withdraw from the plan appears to be increasing.

The European Community Commission and the West German cartel office have opposed the accord on the grounds that it infringes competition rules.

MAN, meanwhile, appears to have decided that the transaction is less attractive than it thought, because of new business opportunities in Eastern Europe.

If MAN does pull out, Spanish Industry Minister Claudio Aranzadi said, Daimler will seek a new partner to join it in taking over the Spanish truck maker.

For its part, Mr. Aranzadi said, the Spanish government won't go ahead with plans to purchase 8% of MAN's shares.

Terms of the original agreement signed with the two West German companies in December valued Enasa at 36 billion pesetas ($356 million); MAN and Daimler were to take 60% and 20% of Enasa, respectively.

Since the agreement, however, the uncertainty surrounding Enasa's future has caused its sales to fall, while MAN's shares have risen sharply.
