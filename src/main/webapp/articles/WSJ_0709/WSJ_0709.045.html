Iberia Makes Only Offer To Buy Argentine Airline

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 07/09/90

BUENOS AIRES -- A consortium led by Spain's Iberia Air Lines made the only offer for the Argentine government-owned airline Aerolineas Argentinas when bids were submitted Friday. Terms weren't disclosed, and the government reserved the right to reject the offer. A ruling was expected this week.

An 85% share of Aerolineas is for sale for $236 million, plus $1.5 billion of the airline's debt paper.

The Iberia group's offer was made in conjunction with local businessmen who own the small Argentine airline Austral and the First Boston Corp. investment banking unit of CS First Boston Inc.

The Italian carrier Alitalia, Varig Brazilian Airlines and Fort Worth, Texas-based AMR Corp., parent of American Airlines, all had expressed interest in the sale but didn't submit bids.

The Aerolineas sale is part of the Argentine government's plan to restructure the country's economy by reducing the role of the state. Last month, the sale of 60% of the state telephone company was granted to Telefonica S.A. of Spain and Bell Atlantic Corp., Philadelphia.
