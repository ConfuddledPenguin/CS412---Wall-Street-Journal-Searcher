Business Brief -- First Federal Bancorp Inc.: Lawsuit Seeks to Rescind Acquisition of Failed Thrift

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/09/90

First Federal Bancorp Inc. said it filed suit to rescind its federally assisted acquisition of a failed thrift, naming the Office of Thrift Supervision and the Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation. The suit seeks the rescission of the acquisition of Citizens Federal Savings Bank in Jacksonville, Fla., by a First Federal Bancorp subsidiary, alleging that actions taken by the regulatory agencies have frustrated the purpose of the acquisition and that, therefore, the acquisition is void and unenforceable. First Federal Bancorp, Pontiac, Mich., took over Citizens Federal Savings Bank in September 1988 for $500,000 plus an agreement to inject $4 million in capital.

The suit further alleges that any action taken by the federal government toward the appointment of a conservator or receiver of the subsidiary, First Federal Savings Bank & Trust, is unwarranted.

The Federal Deposit Insurance Corp. had no comment on the suit.
