Business Brief -- Peoples Bancorp of Worcester: Deposits of a Savings Bank To Be Bought From FDIC

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/09/90

Peoples Bancorp of Worcester Inc., a bank holding company, said it agreed to acquire about $300 million in insured deposits of Milford, Mass.-based Milford Savings Bank from the Federal Deposit Insurance Corp. Milford Savings was declared insolvent and closed by the Massachusetts banking commissioner on Friday.

Under the agreement, Peoples Bancorp, based in Worcester, Mass., will assume Milford Savings' total deposit liability of about $300 million from the FDIC for a premium of $100,000. Peoples Bancorp also will have the option, for 90 days, to buy certain loans after reviewing Milford's loan portfolio. Peoples Bancorp said the five Milford Savings offices will reopen as Peoples Bancorp branches today.

The Milford acquisition is Peoples Bancorp's second regulatory-assisted acquisition of a failed Massachusetts bank. In April 1989, the company assumed certain assets and the deposits of Leominster, Mass.-based First Service Bank for Savings, which was closed by the state banking commissioner in March 1989.
