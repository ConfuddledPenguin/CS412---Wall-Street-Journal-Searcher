Letters to the Editor: Break Away Bases Get the Call: Safe]

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/09/90

I was very pleased to see that someone outside the medical profession has recognized Roger Hall for his accomplishments in the fields of baseball and softball (page-one article on Rogers Break Away Bases, May 25). The article characterizes Mr. Hall and his idea as a curiosity, a human-interest anomaly, with little credibility or backing. Mr. Hall's product is supported by substantial medical studies that have scientifically documented the effectiveness of his Break Away Base, and the impact this base could have on professional and recreational athletes.

The Rogers Break Away Base was a breakthrough in sports safety equipment and should have been welcomed as a needed progressive improvement. For 13 years, Mr. Hall, far from receiving encouragement for making our national pastime a safer sport for all, met with resistance from the sports and business sectors.

In 1986 we initiated a study at the University of Michigan in which we found that 71% of all recreational-softball injuries were due to sliding. We then instituted a four-year study and found that 96% of all softball-related injuries due to sliding could be prevented, and that 99% of the health-care costs could be prevented. It was then that the Centers for Disease Control became involved to perform an actuarial analysis on our data, based upon use of Mr. Hall's product, and found that the implementation of these bases across the U.S. could prevent approximately 1.7 million injuries a year, with a saving of $2 billion in acute health-care costs a year.

The medical community, in particular the orthopedic-surgery community, has embraced Mr. Hall's product with open arms.

David H. Janda, M.D.

Orthopedic Surgery Associates

St. Joseph Mercy Hospital

Ypsilanti, Mich.
