Letters to the Editor: Let's Clear the Air About the EPA's Views

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/09/90

I am writing to follow up on an environmental item that appeared in "Notable & Quotable" on April 20. The item begins: "Environmental Protection Agency analyst E.G. Vallianatos, writing in the Oct. 10 issue of the Chicago Tribune . . ." and goes on to reprint Mr. Vallianatos's fanatical diatribe against capitalism as an "earth-heating, destructive, suicidal system." I wish to make it very clear that Mr. Vallianatos's personal opinions expressed in the quoted piece represent neither the policies of the Environmental Protection Agency nor the views of this administration. Mr. Vallianatos is a midlevel analyst in the biological division of EPA's Office of Pesticide Programs and has no professional knowledge of nor acquaintance with the development or dissemination of policy regarding the problems of climate change or global warming.

In the future, may I suggest that the opinions of individual EPA employees no more represent agency policy than the opinions of one working for General Motors, the Sierra Club or the Marriott Corp. Unless officially transmitted, they are personal views.

Lewis S. Crampton

Associate Administrator

Office of Communications and Public Affairs

Environmental Protection Agency

Washington
