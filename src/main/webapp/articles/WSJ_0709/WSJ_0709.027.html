Sumitomo Cement Venture

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/09/90

TOKYO -- Sumitomo Cement Co. established a joint venture in South Korea to produce calcium carbonate, a material used to make better-quality printing paper, company officials said. The joint company, Korea Fimatic Co., is capitalized at one billion won ($1.4 million) and is 60%-owned by a local concern, Korea Electric Chemical Co., and 20% each by Sumitomo Cement and Fimatic Ltd., a Japanese chemical company.

The joint venture this month will start building a plant capable of producing 3,000 tons of calcium carbonate a month and will sell the product to paper companies in South Korea. The plant is to go into operation next February.
