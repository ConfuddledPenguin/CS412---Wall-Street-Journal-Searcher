Dividend News: N.V. Philips Won't Make '90 Payout, Analyst Thinks

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/09/90

An analyst at Amsterdam-Rottendam Bank N.V. predicted that N.V. Philips, the troubled Dutch electronics giant, wouldn't pay a dividend for 1990. A spokesman for the Dutch electronics concern declined to comment.

Last week, Philips's new chairman, Jan Timmer, announced plans to eliminate 10,000 jobs, or 3.5% of the company's work force, and forecast a loss of about two billion guilders ($1.1 billion) this year, compared with net profit of 1.37 billion guilders in 1989. At that time, he refused to say whether Philips would be able to afford a dividend.

In 1989, the dividend was two guilders a share, the same rate the company has paid since 1983.

The Amro analyst, Peter Berg, urged investors to be cautious in buying Philips shares because the company had released only a rough outline of its strategy for stanching huge losses in its computer and semiconductor divisions.

On the Amsterdam Stock Exchange Friday, Philips shares slipped to 30.20 guilders from 30.50 guilders Thursday.

Meanwhile, Mr. Timmer said in an interview with Philips's in-house newsletter that the company needs a "change of culture" and urged executives to shed their bureaucratic habits, while advising employees to renounce "the mentality of meekly accepting . . . things because you think you are fighting a losing battle."
