Datapoint Corp. Loses Appeal in Patent Case; Financial Impact Seen

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/09/90

SAN ANTONIO, Texas -- Datapoint Corp. said a federal court of appeals has reversed significant portions of a 1988 trial court decision in a patent infringement suit, raising the possibility of a "highly material" adverse financial impact against the company. The troubled computer systems maker was sued in 1982, at the height of its economic success, by Northern Telecom Inc., a unit of Montreal-based BCE Inc., which claimed Datapoint had violated a data entry computer patent. But the case wasn't tried until 1987, after Datapoint had suffered a string of financial reverses, and resulted in a ruling that Datapoint had infringed the patent but that the patent was unenforceable.

The more recent decision reverses that part of the trial court ruling that found the patent unenforceable and paves the way for possible award of damages. Datapoint said it will seek to have the appeals court decision reversed.

Although Datapoint said it could not predict the amount of any award, it acknowledged that any award to Northern Telecom "could be highly material." Northern Telecom initially claimed its damages were at least $65 million.

An award of that magnitude would be a serious blow for Datapoint. For its third quarter ended April 28, Datapoint reported a loss of $8.4 million, or $1.07 a share, compared with a loss of $21.3 million, or $2.35 a share, in the same period a year earlier.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading, Datapoint shares closed Friday at $2.375, up 25 cents.
