Massachusetts Banking Bill Is Signed by Gov. Dukakis

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/09/90

BOSTON -- Massachusetts Gov. Michael Dukakis, as expected, signed into law an interstate banking bill that allows banks outside New England to buy or open banks in the state. The law will take effect in 60 days. A bank, however, can choose to opt out of the interstate banking provisions until July 1, 1992, if two-thirds of its directors vote to do so.

Until now, only other New England-based banks have been able to enter the Massachusetts market.

Gov. Dukakis said, "By opening up Massachusetts to out-of-state banks, we will help stabilize our banking industry, as well as increase the amount of capital available which can potentially help the credit crunch in this state."

Analysts, however, don't expect out-of-state banks to descend on Massachusetts any time soon. Many of the state's banks are suffering from slumps in the local real-estate market and economy, and therefore aren't considered attractive takeover candidates.
