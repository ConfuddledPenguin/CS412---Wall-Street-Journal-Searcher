Who's News: Pittston Co. Names Farrell as President And Operating Chief

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/09/90

GREENWICH, Conn. -- Pittston Co. named Joseph C. Farrell to the new position of president and chief operating officer, making him the odds -- on choice to succeed chief executive Paul W. Douglas when he retires next year. The 63-year-old Mr. Douglas, who is scheduled to step down in September 1991, said the promotions of Mr. Farrell, 54, and David L. Marshall, 51, who was named to the new post of vice chairman, would make for an "orderly process of transition."

Messrs. Farrell and Marshall were previously executive vice presidents.

Mr. Douglas became chief executive of Pittston in 1984 with the charge of streamlining the then-troubled coal mining and transport concern. He has largely succeeded, though not without suffering a nine-month coal miners' strike that ended in January. Mr. Farrell was the company's chief negotiator in the contract talks with the United Mine Workers union.

In addition to the promotions, Pittston said it formed a new, four-man office of the chairman, consisting of Messrs. Douglas, Farrell and Marshall and Robert D. Duke, senior vice president and general counsel.
