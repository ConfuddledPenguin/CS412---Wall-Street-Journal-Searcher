BKLA Bancorp Expects Loss

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/09/90

WEST HOLLYWOOD, Calif. -- BKLA Bancorp expects a second-quarter loss of $400,000 to $425,000, reflecting loan write-offs and an increase in loan-loss reserves at its principal subsidiary. The unit, Bank of Los Angeles, wrote off $988,000 in problem loans and bolstered its loss reserve by $700,000, partly based on discussions with federal regulators who completed an examination of the bank in June, BKLA said. In the year-ago quarter, BKLA had net income of $267,000.

The bank holding company said it expects to return to profitability in the third quarter and be profitable in the year. BKLA said that its capital-to-assets ratio continues to exceed regulatory requirements and that the company's "liquidity position remains sound."
