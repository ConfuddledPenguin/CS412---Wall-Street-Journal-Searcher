What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/09/90

UAL'S UNIONS said five major banks agreed to lead the labor group's effort to get financing for a proposed $4.38 billion acquisition of United Airlines. The buy-out group, which faces an Aug. 9 deadline, said the banks insist that part of the financing come from non-banking sources, such as aircraft suppliers. ---

The jobless rate fell to 5.2% in June although only 40,000 new jobs were created during the month. Many economists believe unemployment can't go any lower without rekindling inflation. The June figures aren't likely to prompt the Fed to ease credit.

Stock prices gained despite a rise in interest rates following the employment report. The Dow industrials advanced 25.74 points to 2904.95.

---

RJR Nabisco has been deluged with $7 billion of loan commitments from banks for its debt refinancing plan, more than triple what it needs. The influx suggests that big money is once again becoming available for leveraged-company transactions.

---

The S&L fraud investigation will concentrate initially on 100 thrifts, following Democratic charges that the administration wasn't pursuing the investigation vigorously enough.

---

British Airways ordered 21 Boeing 747-400 jets valued at $3.8 billion and took options on 12 others, which could bring the deal's value to $6.4 billion.

---

Renault plans to begin a $99 million tender offer this week for the 55% of Mack Trucks it doesn't already own.

---

A tax increase passed by the Massachusetts Legislature will put a 5% levy on fees charged by lawyers, accountants, engineers and architects. Business leaders said it would further cripple the state's sluggish economy.

---

E.M. Warburg isn't considering selling any of the Ingersoll newspapers it acquired last week, although it may face drawn-out negotiations over debt payments to bondholders.

---

Lead and zinc prices are expected to jump as union workers at Cominco Ltd., Canada's biggest producer of the metals, prepared to strike today.

---

Shearson was fined a record $500,000 by the Big Board for its trading in E.F. Hutton stock when it was discussing taking over Hutton in 1986.

---

A chemical plant explosion in Texas that left 17 dead is being investigated to determine whether contract workers were a factor. The Arco plant had 430 full-time employees, 80 of them lower-paid contract workers.

---

Digital Equipment is expected to unveil today a new generation of its strong-selling MicroVAX computer line, marking a fresh challenge to IBM in the minicomputer market.

---

Lord Geller will merge into another WPP Group ad agency, marking the end of the once-hot shop that never fully recovered from the defection of six top executives in 1988.
