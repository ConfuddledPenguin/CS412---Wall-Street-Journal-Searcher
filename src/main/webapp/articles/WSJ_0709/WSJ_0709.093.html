Bolt Technology Says It Can't Repay Debt; Luskin Retires as Chief

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/09/90

NORWALK, Conn. -- Bolt Technology Corp. said it can't repay $6.7 million in debt principal and has entered into discussions with its bankers to avoid seizure of collateral. Separately, the maker of seismic equipment for mineral exploration said Chairman Bernard Luskin, 65 years old, had retired as president and chief executive officer. Succeeding him will be Raymond M. Soto, formerly treasurer and senior vice president, finance.

Mr. Soto said in an interview that a bankruptcy-court filing by Bolt is a "possibility but not a probability," adding, "We'll be having discussions with the bank over the next several days." The loan is owed to Connecticut Bank & Trust Co., a unit of Bank of New England Co.

For the first nine months of the fiscal year ended June 30, Bolt posted a loss of $2.4 million, or 51 cents a share, compared with a loss of $1.1 million, or 22 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue for the first nine months of fiscal 1990 totaled $4.2 million, compared with $6.1 million the year before.

Mr. Soto declined to specify results for the quarter just ended but said they "were in line" with the trend for the first nine months.
