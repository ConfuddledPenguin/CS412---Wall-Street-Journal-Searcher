Dividend News: Hamptons Bancshares Suspends Its Dividend

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/18/90

SOUTHAMPTON, N.Y. -- Hamptons Bancshares Inc. said it temporarily suspended quarterly cash dividends while it is boosting provisions for loan losses from real estate. The company paid 11 cents a share quarterly in the first half. The suspension will save Hamptons Bancshares $93,360 this quarter.

On July 16, the bank holding company reported a six-month net loss of $593,000, or 70 cents a share. President John F. Kidd said the company within the past month has taken several steps, including limited layoffs, and elimination of bonuses, certain benefits and payment of directors' fees. In the second quarter, the company added $1.3 million to loan-loss reserves, up from $120,000 a year earlier.

Officials wouldn't speculate on the duration of the dividend suspension, but said they would consider reinstating the payout when the company's capital position shows a strong positive trend.

Hamptons Bancshares owns the Bank of the Hamptons, Hamptons Mortgage Corp. Leasehampton Corp. and Appraisal Center Inc. The company has $153 million in assets.
