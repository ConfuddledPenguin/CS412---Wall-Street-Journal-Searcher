Technology: Chevron Unit, Daicel Sign Pact

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/18/90

TOKYO -- Daicel Chemical Industries Ltd., a major Japanese maker of acetic acid, said it signed a technology agreement with Chevron Research & Technology Co., a subsidiary of Chevron Corp. Under the agreement, Daicel Chemical officials said, Chevron Research of San Francisco, will provide their company with technological know-how for the production of general-purpose polystyrene resin, high-impact polystyrene resin and other resins.

Those resins are used in manufacturing office automation equipment, consumer electric products, television cabinets and other goods to make them more durable, or more fire or impact-resistant.

On the basis of the accord, Daicel Chemical will build by the autumn of 1992 a plant capable of producing 50,000 tons of resins a year, according to company officials.
