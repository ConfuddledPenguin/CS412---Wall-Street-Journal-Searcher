Apple Bancorp To Scrap Poison Pill If Stahl Buys Shares

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/18/90

NEW YORK -- Apple Bancorp Inc. said it will cancel its poison-pill anti-takeover measure if real estate investor Stanley Stahl buys all shares tendered to him and holds a majority of the stock before the annual meeting. The bank holding company has been fighting Mr. Stahl's $38-a-share tender offer, but yesterday said its board is now neutral on the takeover offer. The bank also postponed its annual meeting to Oct. 10 from Oct. 1 to give Mr. Stahl more time. Mr. Stahl's offer expires Oct. 1.

A spokesman for Mr. Stahl declined to comment.

Apple Bancorp said that its financial adviser, Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette Securities Corp., expressed no opinion on whether the offer is fair or unfair to shareholders. And given "adverse market conditions for the sale of healthy thrift institutions," Apple said it has been unable to line up an alternative transaction.

Mr. Stahl currently is soliciting proxies from shareholders in an effort to take control of Apple's board. The bank said that if Mr. Stahl buys all shares tendered to him before the meeting, it also will withdraw its board nominees. Earlier this month, Mr. Stahl said more than two million shares had been tendered, enough to provide him a 76% stake in Apple when combined with shares he already owned.

But Apple directors are "concerned that if Mr. Stahl succeeds in taking control of the company in the proxy contest, he will either terminate his tender offer without purchasing any shares" or cut the $38 offer price, said Jerome R. McDougal, chairman and chief executive officer.

Poison pills such as the one Apple's board adopted last November allow shareholders to buy, under certain circumstances, more shares at half price. The plan was designed to make a hostile takeover prohibitively expensive.

Apple Bancorp's shares rose 37.5 cents to $33.125 in New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday.
