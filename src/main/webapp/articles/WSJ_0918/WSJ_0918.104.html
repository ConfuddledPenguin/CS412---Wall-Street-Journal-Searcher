CSX Corp. said the Virginia Retirement System proposed an exchange of a portion of CSX's RF&P Corp. dividend obligations for the stock of an RF&P subsidiary that holds the RF&P railroad. The Virginia Retirement System also proposed that it purchase CSX's remaining RF&P dividend obligations and its RF&P voting shares at $35 a share. In yesterday's edition, terms of the transaction were misstated. (See: "Business Brief -- CSX Corp.: Purchase of Rail Operations of RF&P Corp. Is Closer" -- WSJ Sept. 17, 1990)

---

PRIME MOTOR INNS Inc. said bank lenders were considering extending the maturity date of existing loans and allowing the company to retain a part of proceeds from asset sales. Yesterday's edition incorrectly said lenders had agreed to the extension and to the sharing of proceeds.

(See: "Prime Motors Inns, Rebuffed by Lenders, Says It May Seek Chapter 11 Protection" -- WSJ Sept. 17, 1990)
