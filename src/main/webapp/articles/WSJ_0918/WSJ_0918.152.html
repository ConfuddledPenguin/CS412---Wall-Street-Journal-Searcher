House Panel Plans Hearing On U.S. Debt Sale Rules

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/18/90

WASHINGTON -- A House subcommittee is planning hearings on whether the huge U.S. government securities market needs more federal oversight of sales techniques. The 1986 Government Securities Act, which precludes regulation of government securities sales practices, will expire next year. That has some members on Capitol Hill questioning the current sales environment, where $225 billion of debt securities are traded daily, according to House figures. "The evidence of current abuses in the government securities market raises the issue of whether such an exemption from sales practice regulation is still warranted," Reps. Edward Markey (D., Mass.) and Jim Cooper (D., Tenn.) wrote to the Securities and Exchange Commission. Rep. Markey is chairman of the House Telecommunications and Finance Subcommittee of which Mr. Cooper is a member. A subcommittee staffer said the hearings likely will be held next year.

Last week, the General Accounting Office called for regulation of government securities market sales practices. If sales practice rules "make sense for other securities markets, then they also make sense for the government market as well, because there are similar opportunities for abuse in both types of markets," the GAO said.

The Treasury Department, SEC and Federal Reserve Board are due to submit a joint report to Congress shortly on the government securities market.
