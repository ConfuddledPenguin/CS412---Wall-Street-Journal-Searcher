Dividend News: Four REITs Change Payout Dates, Citing Cash-Flow Uncertainty

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/18/90

DALLAS -- Four real estate investment trusts controlled by Gene E. Phillips and William S. Friedman, former top executives of Southmark Corp., said dividends will be determined on an annual basis after the trusts' current calendar year ends, Dec. 31. Currently, dividends are determined on a quarterly basis. A spokeswoman attributed the change to continuing uncertainty over the ability of many of the trusts' borrowers to meet their obligations and variable cash flows caused by the expense of improving the trusts' real estate portfolios.

Despite the change in policy, the trusts will continue making distributions agreed to earlier this year as part of the settlement of a class-action suit over management of the trusts. The four trusts agreed to make a minimum of the following per-share distributions by May 23, 1991: Continental Mortgage and Equity Trust, 94 cents; National Income Realty Trust, $1.96; Income Opportunity Realty Trust, 33 cents; and Transcontinental Realty Investors, Inc., $1.06. Per-share amounts are based on current numbers of shares outstanding, the spokeswoman said.
