Citicorp Identifies About $200 Million Of Possible Cost Cuts

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/18/90

NEW YORK -- Citicorp has identified some $200 million of possible cost cuts in its global finance operation, with at least another $50 million likely beyond that, someone close to the business said. The potential expense cuts are part of Citicorp's plan to streamline the operation, which provides commercial and investment banking services in the U.S., Canada, Europe, Japan, Australia and New Zealand.

The bank holding company hasn't mandated any specific number of people to be cut or amounts to be saved by the 53 managers who run the businesses within Global Finance, the person close to the business said.

When the plan to cut expenses was disclosed, talk among the staff in the operation was that 1,000 jobs would be lost. The bank has no target, the source said, but a recent published report that some 3,000, or 20%, of the staffers in the operations would likely be pared is "excessive."

The $200 million of identified cuts, which will take at least 12 months to execute, might come through restructurings and divestitures of businesses, the person added.

In July, Michael A. Callen, Citicorp's head of global finance for developed countries, said that costs and revenue in the operation had become imbalanced. But he said that "there won't be any slashing and burning like we've seen elsewhere" in the company. At the time, Citicorp said it eliminated two layers of managers in global finance.

A number of major banks have announced significant staff cuts this year.
