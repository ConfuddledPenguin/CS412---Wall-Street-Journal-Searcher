Business Brief -- Hollywood Park Operating Co.: Holder Seeks Resignation Of Chief Executive Everett

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/18/90

Randall D. Hubbard, who recently acquired a 9.9% stake in Hollywood Park Operating Co., called for the retirement of Marjorie L. Everett, the Inglewood, Calif. race track's chairman and chief executive officer. In a letter to company directors dated Thursday and filed with the Securities and Exchange Commission, Mr. Hubbard proposed that he succeed the 68-year-old Mrs. Everett "who should resign before the next annual meeting." He also asked to be added to the board along with four of his designees.

"It is my intention to devote substantial personal time and effort toward the revitalization of Hollywood Park," said Mr. Hubbard, who is also president of AFG Industries Inc. in Fort Worth, Texas. "I believe that the interests of all the shareholders require that fundamental changes be made in the operation."

Mrs. Everett couldn't be reached. A company spokesman said he couldn't comment beyond noting that the company must hold its annual meeting within the next four months.

Mr. Hubbard said he wanted board representation last month after he disclosed his holdings in Hollywood Park and in Hollywood Park Realty Enterprises Inc., which owns the track. Hollywood Park Operating manages the track. The companies' stocks are paired for trading.

In the letter, Mr. Hubbard said he hoped to avoid the need for spending substantial amounts of time and money on both sides in a proxy contest. He urged the directors to consider his proposals at their next meeting.
