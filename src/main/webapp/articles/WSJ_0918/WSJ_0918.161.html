Business Brief -- Silicon Valley Group Inc.: Firm Scraps Stock Offering, Sets Bank Credit Facility

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/18/90

Silicon Valley Group Inc., citing adverse market conditions, said it scrapped a proposed public offering of its stock, and reached a new four-year, $50 million secured credit facility with a group of banks. The semiconductor processing equipment maker had planned on using part of the proceeds of the three million common share offering, registered last month with the Securities and Exchange Commission, to retire debt from a May 1990 agreement to acquire control of the Optical Lithography operations of Perkin-Elmer Corp.

In national over-the-counter trading yesterday, Silicon Valley Group stock closed at $6.375, about 39% less than its August peak of $10.375.

The San Jose, Calif., company added that it had already borrowed $20 million under the new credit agreement, arranged with a group led by San Francisco-based BankAmerica Corp.'s main banking unit, to help pay off an interim loan from the Perkin-Elmer acquisition.
