International: Bertelsmann Unit, Maxwell Plan East German Venture

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 09/18/90

FRANKFURT, West Germany -- Gruner & Jahr AG, which is 74.9%-owned by West German media group Bertelsmann AG, signed an agreement with British publisher Robert Maxwell to take over East Germany's largest newspaper and magazine publishing group. The accord to acquire Berliner Verlag still must be approved by an East German government commission responsible for sales of assets formerly owned by the East German Communist Party, a Gruner & Jahr spokesman said.

Under the transaction, Gruner & Jahr and Mr. Maxwell would each pay 50% of the takeover's cost. The spokesman declined to give an exact amount but said the price was between 250 million and 300 million marks ($159.1 million to $191 million).

In other German publishing-related developments, Munich-based Burda G.m.b.H. said it is considering launching a newspaper in East Germany, which will merge with West Germany into a single nation Oct. 3. Burda said the East Berlin-based newspaper will be part of a joint venture between Burda Holding and News International, a unit of the News Corp. media group operated by Australian publisher Rupert Murdoch.
