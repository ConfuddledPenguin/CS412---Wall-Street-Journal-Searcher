Pan Am to Postpone $53 Million Payment To Its Pension Plans

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/18/90

NEW YORK -- Pan Am Corp. said it will defer $53 million in pension-plan payments until November. The loss-plagued company, which has had to postpone pension payments several times in recent years, said it reached a deferral agreement with the Internal Revenue Service and the Pension Benefit Guaranty Corp., a government agency that insures pension plans.

The pact covers payments that Pan Am was supposed to make on Saturday to the principal pension plan at its Pan American World Airways unit.

Pan Am said that on Nov. 1 it will pay $30 million of the $53 million it owes. If it gets money before then from its previously announced plan to sell assets, the company said it agreed to make its payments earlier. Pan Am said it will pay the remainder of $23 million on Feb. 28, 1991.

The airline holding company said it will use shares of its Pan Am Shuttle Inc. unit as collateral for the pension money it owes.

Industry observers said the company negotiated the deferrals because it faces a cash crunch. Pan Am had cash-on-hand of $100.1 million at June 30, down from $203.9 million at Dec. 31.
