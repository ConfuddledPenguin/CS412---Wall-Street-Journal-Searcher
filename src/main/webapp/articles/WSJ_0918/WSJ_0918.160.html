Technology: Apple Joins Two Groups Debating Unix Software

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/18/90

CUPERTINO, Calif. -- Apple Computer Inc. said it wouldn't take sides in the computer industry rivalry over development of the Unix operating system. Apple said it decided to join both Unix International and the Open Software Foundation, groups that have been sparring for more than two years over trying to turn the Unix software, originally developed by American Telephone & Telegraph Co., into a computer-industry standard.

The computer maker said the decision "bolsters Apple's support of industry standards and ensures earliest access to emerging Unix technologies."

The Open Software Foundation, which includes International Business Machines Corp. and Digital Equipment Corp., was formed largely because of concern that AT&T and Sun Microsystems Inc., partners in Unix International, had too much control over the Unix marketplace because they controlled the development of the basic Unix software.
