Letters to the Editor: Loopholes Cause Credit-Report Abuse

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/18/90

I read with great interest your informative Aug. 14 page-one article on repairing bad credit ratings. Recently, the credit records of a person with a name similar to mine were merged into my credit files. As a result, I was harassed by a collection agency, denied credit and failed a background investigation by my new employer. It took me a year to have my credit-bureau records corrected. The credit-reporting abuses that the article described and that I experienced are the result of major loopholes in the Fair Credit Reporting Act. Reps. Schumer, Rinaldo and Lehman have introduced separate bills to strengthen the act. Until this happens, creditors, credit bureaus and collection agencies will be able to continue to traffic in false consumer-credit information with impunity.

John J. Casson

Brooklyn, N.Y.
