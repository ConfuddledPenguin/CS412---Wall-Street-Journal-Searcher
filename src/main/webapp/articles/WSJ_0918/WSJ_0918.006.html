Oracle Systems Fires 400 Workers In Cost-Cutting Bid

  • Author: Susan C. Faludi
  • Date: 09/18/90

Oracle Systems Corp. laid off 10% of its domestic work force and made major changes at the management level, in an attempt to recoup from its expected first quarterly loss ever. About 400 people were fired Monday, effective immediately, and four top executives resigned, were shorn of their titles or took a leave of absence. The cuts are likely to save the company at least $24 million a year, analysts estimated. The company declined to comment on the cost savings.

Oracle cut two levels of its five-layer sales hierarchy, folded Oracle USA's finance and administration group into its corporate finance and administration division, and consolidated its various international organizations into a single division. Michael Musson, investor relations manager, said the company does not anticipate any more cuts and will not comment on specifics until it releases its financial results Sept. 25.

Following the shakeout, the senior vice president of the intercontinental organization, Peter R. Tierney, departed. Oracle USA Chief Financial Officer Atam Lalchandani also resigned, although people within the company said he is leaving for personal reasons unrelated to the restructuring.

In addition, Tom Siebel, Oracle USA's vice president and general manager of direct marketing, took a one-year leave of absence as a result of the reorganization, the company confirmed. And, as previously announced, the president of Oracle USA, Gary Kennedy, stepped down as president of Oracle USA. He is still chairman of Oracle Complex Systems, a subsidiary dealing with systems integration.

The software company has been under pressure to make hefty cost reductions ever since it reported in early September that it would post a loss of about $27.4 million or 20 cents a share for the first quarter ended Aug. 31.

The company's stock was battered in over-the-counter trading yesterday, closing at $6.875, down 62.5 cents.
