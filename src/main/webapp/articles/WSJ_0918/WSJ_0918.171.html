Marketing & Media: Cineplex Odeon Plan To Sell 38 Theaters To Plitt Is Ended

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/18/90

TORONTO -- Cineplex Odeon Corp. said the $31.8 million sale of 38 of its U.S. movie theaters to Plitt Amusement Co. of Los Angeles won't go ahead as planned. Cineplex disclosed last month that the transaction might not be completed. Sources close to the proposed sale said it foundered over the reluctance of banks to finance an acquisition valued at more than six times cash flow. Analysts said the Plitt sale had an indicated value of eight times cash flow.

The transaction, announced in June, had been scheduled to close July 31.

Cineplex and Plitt said that "the matter was settled on amicable terms," and that the two companies "have released each other from any related obligations."

The Plitt sale was one of several announced by Cineplex as part of the movie theater chain's commitments to its bankers to raise $200 million through asset sales. Those proceeds are to be used to cut its long-term debt load, which stood at $479.4 million at June 30, 1990.

Allen Karp, president and chief executive officer of Cineplex, said previously that the company doesn't propose to dispose of assets at "fire-sale prices" to meet that target.

"We've got the confidence of our bankers and our major shareholders to the extent that we don't need to give things away," said a spokeswoman for Cineplex.

If Cineplex doesn't meet that target, its two major shareholders, MCA Inc. and interests affiliated with Montreal financier Charles Bronfman, have agreed to invest an additional $100 million in Cineplex in the form of subordinated financing.

To date, Cineplex has raised about $142 million in gross proceeds from asset sales.

The company spokeswoman said the theaters, which are not in areas that Cineplex has identified as core markets, will remain on the company's list of assets to be sold.
