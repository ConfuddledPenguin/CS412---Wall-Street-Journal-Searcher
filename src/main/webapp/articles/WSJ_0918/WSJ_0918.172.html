International: Brent Walker Seeks Rebate on Purchase Of Two Betting Shops

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 09/18/90

LONDON -- Brent Walker Group PLC, a British leisure concern, said it will ask Grand Metropolitan PLC to return part of the #685 million ($1.3 billion) purchase price for two betting shops. The claim stems from what Brent Walker says is worse-than-expected profit performance by William Hill Organization Ltd. and Mecca Bookmakers Ltd. Brent Walker purchased the two companies, which are based in London, in 1989.

Brent Walker didn't say how much of a rebate it would request from Grand Met, except to say it would be "substantial."

The purchase agreement between the two companies called for an adjustment to the purchase price if the combined pretax profit of William Hill and Mecca fell below #51 million. Brent Walker said the combined pretax profit of the shops was "substantially below" that figure, according to accounts drafted by its auditors.

A source familiar with the betting operations said that Brent Walker may ask for an adjustment of as much as #166 million. But Grand Met said a review of the accounts by its auditors, KPMG Peat Marwick McLintock, and outside auditors, Coopers & Lybrand Deloitte, shows that there is no need for an adjustment to the purchase price.

Grand Met also rejected Brent Walker's claim that Brent Walker isn't liable to make a final #50 million payment, due Sept. 25. A Grand Met spokesman said the payment is a separate issue from any adjustment of the purchase price.

Sources familiar with the two sides said discussions to resolve the dispute are being arranged. If the companies fail to resolve the dispute between them, they will turn to an independent auditor.
