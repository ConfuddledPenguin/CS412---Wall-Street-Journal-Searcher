U.S. Prosecutors Decide Against Retrying Barry

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/18/90

WASHINGTON -- Federal prosecutors said they won't retry Mayor Marion Barry on 12 counts of cocaine possession and perjury on which a jury failed to reach a verdict last month. Mr. Barry was convicted Aug. 10 in federal court here on one count of possessing cocaine. He was acquitted on another possession count. His sentencing is set for Oct. 26. The mayor faces a maximum penalty of one year in prison and a $100,000 fine.

Jay Stephens, the U.S. attorney here, said in a statement, "While the defendant mistakenly has flaunted the jury's failure to reach a unanimous decision as to his guilt on all counts, when all the dust has settled, the essential truth will remain that the chief executive of this city, charged with the responsibility of leading the fight against illegal drugs and violence, has himself been convicted of contributing to the human devastation."

Mr. Barry, whose lawyer conceded to the jury that the mayor had occasionally used drugs, said he was relieved but declined to elaborate. As a result of his being prosecuted, Mr. Barry had announced that he wouldn't run for a fourth term as mayor. But he remains an independent candidate for an at-large seat on Washington's City Council.
