Technology Brief -- Xicor Inc.: Company Cuts Work Hours Of Employees Up to 25%

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/18/90

Xicor Inc. said it is temporarily reducing work hours for most employees by 10% to 25% until continuing sluggish orders pick up. The Milpitas, Calif., semiconductor manufacturer, which employs roughly 1,250 people, said the scaled-back hours would continue until bookings improve "significantly." It added that it eliminated 36 jobs as the result of reorganizing certain operations and trimming nonessential staff.

Xicor also said that "unless bookings for quick turn deliveries increase significantly in the near future, we will not be able to attain profitability in 1990."

The company had a net loss of $4.8 million, or 26 cents a share, on revenue of $20.4 million, for the second quarter ended June 17. It said it expects to report increased revenue and a narrowing of losses for the third quarter ended Sept. 9.
