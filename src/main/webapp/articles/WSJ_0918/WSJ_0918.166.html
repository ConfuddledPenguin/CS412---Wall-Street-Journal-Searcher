Business Brief -- VeriFone Inc.: Concern Says Net Is Likely To Fall Short of Estimates

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/18/90

VeriFone Inc. said that third-quarter earnings would likely fall short of analysts' estimates, and that it has implemented cost containment measures in anticipation of slower revenue growth. "Revenue growth in the second half of 1990 hasn't met our internal expectations," Hatim Tyabji, president and chief executive officer, said in a prepared statement. He cited shortages in petroleum and North American emerging businesses operations, but said the company's financial market and international operations are continuing to exceed expectations.

VeriFone, a Redwood City, Calif., supplier of transaction automation systems, became publicly held in March. Its stock traded as high as $26 a share in July, but has been drifting lower in recent weeks. In national over-the-counter trading, VeriFone closed yesterday at $12.875 a share, off $2.

According to Zacks Investment Research Inc., analysts expected VeriFone to earn 25 cents to 27 cents a share for the third quarter. In the year-earlier quarter, VeriFone earned $2.6 million, or 13 cents a share, on revenue of $34.8 million.
