Business Brief -- REIT Co. of California: Trust to Be Self-Managed When Its President Retires

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/18/90

Real Estate Investment Trust Co. of California said it will become self-managed after the retirement of its president, William Walters Jr., at the end of the month. Mr. Walters, 65 years old, will remain a trustee. He also owns William Walters Co., a real-estate concern that has acted as the trust's management adviser.

John F. Carpenter, 43, will succeed Mr. Walters as president of the trust. He was formerly senior vice president of Grubb & Ellis Co., a San Francisco-based commercial real-estate services concern.

"No material change is anticipated in the current operating focus," said the trust, based in Los Angeles, which owns and operates California shopping centers, apartment complexes, industrial buildings and commercial projects. Most of the adviser's 12 employees will become direct employees of the trust and the others will help Mr. Walters manage his personal investments.

Investors increasingly prefer self-managed trusts because of concerns about potential self-dealing between an outside manager and its clients, said LeRoy E. Carlson, William Walters Co. vice president and chief financial officer. After Oct. 1, he will hold the same titles with the trust. "This is being done now partly because of Mr. Walter's retirement," Mr. Carlson said.
