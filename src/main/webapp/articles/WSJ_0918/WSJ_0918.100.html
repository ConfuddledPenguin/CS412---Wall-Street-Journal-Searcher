Credit Rating: Sears's Debt Is Downgraded By Rating Firm

  • Author: James P. Miller
  • Date: 09/18/90

Moody's Investors Service Inc., saying a continuing decline in consumer spending will "inhibit" Sears, Roebuck & Co.'s efforts to boost results at its merchandising group, lowered its rating on about $6.9 billion in debt issued by the Chicago concern. The rating company said it took the action because it thinks "improvement within the intensely competitive merchandising sector has not been nor will be achieved as quickly as originally anticipated." Moody's also cited the "increased use of leverage in Sears's businesses."

Moody's put Sears ratings under review last month.

Yesterday, the New York company said "the continued decline in consumer confidence in the economy and therefore, in consumer spending, will inhibit Sears's merchandising group's efforts to reverse the decline in its market share and to increase profitability."

The "slower-than-anticipated rollout of Sears's power format strategies" -- under which the retail and financial services giant has been adding brand-name products to its lineup of house brands -- and the "challenge" of boosting sales through the company's everyday-low-pricing policy are "expected to continue to hamper gains in sales productivity."

Moody's also said that Sears's "slow and incomplete implementation of cost-cutting steps" has made "meaningful" improvements in earnings and cash flow difficult to achieve, and projected Sears will have continued difficulty in narrowing its cost position in comparison to competitors.

However, Moody's said, Sears has demonstrated that its merchandising efforts can give it "merchandise selection and presentation comparable to specialty stores." Should the company complete the reformatting of a majority of its stores, trim costs and streamline distribution, Sears "should be able to capitalize on its national franchise and significantly improve earnings performance," Moody's said.

A Sears spokesman, citing company policy, declined to comment on the move.

Lowered to single-A-2 from single-A-1 were Moody's ratings on senior debt of the parent, as well as its Sears Roebuck Acceptance Corp., Sears Overseas Finance N.V. and Discover Credit Corp. units.

In addition, Moody's reduced to single-A-3 from single-A-2 ratings on long-term deposits at Sears's Greenwood Trust Co. and Sears Savings Bank units.

Also yesterday, Moody's downgraded its "insurance financial strength rating" on Sears's Allstate Insurance Co. and Allstate Life Insurance Co. to double-A-1 from triple-A. While the insurance operation "enjoys a leadership position in personal lines, strong capitalization and excellent asset quality," Moody's said, its ratings were cut because of the "increased risks to Allstate policyholders that could result from credit deterioration at Sears."

Moody's also placed under review the triple-A insurance financial strength rating at Allstate's PMI Mortgage Insurance subsidiary, because of the downgrade at PMI's Allstate parent. The review will focus on the quality and scope of additional contractual support from a third party, which PMI officials have said they are seeking, Moody's said.
