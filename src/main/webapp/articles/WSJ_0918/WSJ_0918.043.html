British Aerospace Is Sued

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/18/90

LONDON -- The U.K. Department of Trade and Industry said it sued British Aerospace PLC and Rover Group PLC to recover #44.4 million ($84 million) in subsidies made illegally to the two companies at the time of British Aerospace's takeover of the troubled motor car group in 1988. The action to recover the money was forced on the agency by the ruling of the European Commission earlier this year that said secret financial concessions made to British Aerospace amounted to illegal state aid.

British Aerospace had until Aug. 17 to repay the money. Since it hasn't yet done so, the government said it was obliged to carry out the EC's directive by trying to recover the funds through the courts.

Because of the ongoing court proceedings, British Aerospace wouldn't comment on the writs, beyond saying the company must appeal the EC decision "to protect our shareholders." It isn't known when the Court of Justice will render its decision.
