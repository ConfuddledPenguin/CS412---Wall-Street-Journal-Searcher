Canadian Minister Warns Economy May Shrink More

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/18/90

OTTAWA -- Canadian Finance Minister Michael Wilson warned that the Canadian economy could show a further contraction in the current third quarter following a 0.4% decline in the April-June period. Mr. Wilson also predicted that the government's fiscal 1991 budget deficit will be 1 billion to 1.5 billion Canadian dollars (US$864 million to US$1.30 billion) higher than the C$28.5 billion gap forecast in the government's Feb. 20 budget. Fiscal 1991 ends March 31, 1991.

The minister said in a speech that he now expects the country's gross domestic product to show inflation adjusted growth of about 1% this year, down from a budget forecast of 1.3%. Gross domestic product is the total value of a nation's output of goods and services.

Mr. Wilson said the government's anti-inflation policy was working. In recent months, a moderation of economic activity has been accompanied by a moderation in the growth of credit demand, he said.

He attributed the deterioration in the government's budget deficit outlook to rising debt costs resulting from higher-than-expected interest costs.
