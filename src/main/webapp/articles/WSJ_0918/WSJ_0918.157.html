Business Brief -- Justin Industries Inc.: Group Makes Another Offer For Firm, Lowering Price

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/18/90

Six months after its $18.50 a share, or $140.5 million, offer for Justin Industries Inc. was rejected, a dissident group holding 11.4% of Justin has made a second offer, this one for $18 a share. In a filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission, the dissident group, led by Perry H. Sutherland of Kansas City, Mo., and Barry S. Rosenstein of New York, said that it made the $18-a-share offer in a Sept. 14 letter to Justin's board, requesting a response by tomorrow. If the offer is rejected, the group said that it will consider other strategies for acquiring the Fort Worth, Texas, company.

The group attributed the 50-cents-a-share reduction in its offer to Justin's planned acquisition of Tony Lama Co. for $18 million, or $9 a share, plus the assumption of about $34 million of debt. The group contended that the acquisition of Tony Lama, which lost $1.8 million in 1989, would lower the value of Justin. Although the dissidents are seeking to block the acquisition in court, they said the $18-a-share offer assumes the acquisition will go through.

John Justin, Justin chairman and the holder of about 20% of its shares, didn't return phone calls seeking comment.
