U.S. Seeks to Ensure That Banks Fulfill Proxy-Voting Duties

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/18/90

WASHINGTON -- The Labor Department launched an effort designed to ensure that banks fulfill their proxy-voting duties as trustees for pension plans. David Ball, assistant secretary for the Labor Department's Pension and Welfare Benefits Administration, said the department's enforcement division has started a study into how banks get proxy ballots to trustees and whether the process gives the shareholders a chance to vote properly. He said he expects the department inquiry to prompt banks to review their own policies, thus acting as a self-policing mechanism.

"For corporate democracy to work well, persons entitled to vote need to be able to get relevant information in a timely fashion and to receive their ballots in time to cast them," Mr. Ball said.

The Labor Department conducted a similar review of investment managers recently to ensure that they were managing assets in the interests of plan beneficiaries.

Mr. Ball said the department's regional offices will contact major banks in their areas to determine whether the banks are delivering proxies "in a timely fashion" to pension plan fiduciaries. The department also will be asking about the banks' procedures for determining whether the people voting the shares are the appropriate plan fiduciaries, he said.

Mr. Ball also said the Labor Department is considering a measure that would help beneficiaries determine whether pension managers have a proxy-voting policy. Currently, participants can find out about the specific plan's investments but can't learn much about whether the plan has a broader policy concerning proxy voting.
