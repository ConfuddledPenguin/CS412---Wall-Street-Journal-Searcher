New Securities Issues

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/18/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report. CORPORATE

Champion International Corp. -- $150 million of 9.65% notes due Oct. 1, 1995, priced at par. The noncallable notes were priced at a spread of 114 basis points above the Treasury's five-year note. Rated Baa-1 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and triple-B by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Goldman Sachs & Co.

World Savings & Loan Association -- $100 million of 10.25% subordinated notes due Oct. 1, 1997, priced at par to yield 10.25%. The noncallable notes were priced at 150 basis points above the Treasury's seven-year note. Rated single-A2 by Moody's and single-A-minus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets. World Savings & Loan is a unit of Golden West Financial Corp.

MUNICIPAL

New York State Urban Development Corp. -- $228.1 million of correctional capital facilities revenue bonds, Series 1, due Jan. 1, 1992-2003, 2016 and 2020, tentatively priced by a Goldman Sachs & Co. group to yield from 6.50% in 1992 to 8.00% in 2016. Current interest serial bonds are priced to yield from 6.50% in 1992 to 7.50% in 2003. There are $118.2 million of 7.75% term bonds priced at 97.29 to yield 8.00% in 2016 and $66 million of 7.50% term bonds priced at 97.67 to yield 7.70% in 2020. Ratings of single-A/triple-B-plus are expected.

Rhode Island Housing and Mortgage Financial Corp. -- $125.8 million of home ownership opportunity bonds, Series 3-A due Oct. 1, 2010 and 2016, and Series 3-B due April 1 and Oct. 1, 1992-2000, 2019 and 2022, priced by a Goldman, Sachs & Co. group to yield from 6.40% in 1992 to 8.05% in 2022. Series 3-A consists of $43.8 million of bonds. There are $20.9 million of 7.80% term bonds priced at par due in 2010 and $22.8 million of 7.85% term bonds priced at par due in 2016. Series 3-B consists of $82 million of bonds. Current interest serial bonds are priced at par to yield from 6.40% in 1992 to 7.25% in 2000. There are $40 million of tender option bonds due in 2019 that are not formally reoffered and $34.8 million of 8.05% term bonds priced at par due in 2022. Interest on the Series 3-B bonds will be treated as a preference item in calculating the federal alternative minimum tax that may be imposed on certain investors. Interest on the Series 3-A bonds will not be subject to the tax. Ratings of single-A-1/ double-A-plus are expected.

MORTGAGES

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $300.2 million of Remic mortgage securities offered in 11 classes by Bear, Stearns & Co. The offering, Series 1990-127, is backed by Fannie Mae 10% securities with a weighted average coupon rate of 10.625% and weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29.58 years.

Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $300 million of Remic mortgage securities offered in 10 classes by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets. The offering, Series FHLMC Gold 1007, is backed by Freddie Mac 10 1/2% gold participation certificates with a weighted average coupon rate of 11.25% and weighted average remaining term to maturity of 28.5 years. Price and yield data weren't immediately available.

EUROBONDS

ICI (Australian) Finance Ltd (U.K. parent) -- 60 million Australian dollars of 14 1/4% Eurobonds due Oct. 22, 1993, issue price 101 1/2, via Hambros Bank Ltd. Guarantor ICI (Australia) Ltd. Fees 1 1/2.

Czechoslovakian Trade Bank (Czechoslovakia) -- 350 million marks of 10% bonds due Oct. 5, 1995, priced at 101 to yield 9.73%, via Deutby Statnibank Ceskoslovenska, the state central bank. Fees 2.
