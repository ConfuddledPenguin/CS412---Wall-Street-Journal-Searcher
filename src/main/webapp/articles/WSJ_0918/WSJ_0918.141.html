Pandick Inc. to Reorganize Under Chapter 11 Filing

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/18/90

NEW YORK (AP) -- Pandick Inc., a printer of prospectuses, proxy materials and other financial documents, filed for bankruptcy-court reorganization, becoming another casualty of a business slowdown on Wall Street. James Alexis, president, attributed the Chapter 11 filing to the "steady deterioration" of the financial markets since the October 1987 stock-market crash.

Under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code, a company is protected from creditors while it continues to operate and tries to reorganize.

Mr. Alexis said in a statement that Pandick's business has dropped more than 50% over the past two years. During that time, the mergers and acquisition business has slowed markedly and Wall Street firms have cut back sharply.

The turmoil in the financial markets since Iraq's Aug. 2 invasion of Kuwait further curtailed the company's business and worsened its problems, Mr. Alexis said.
