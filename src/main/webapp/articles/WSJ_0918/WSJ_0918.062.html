Barden in Pact To Be Acquired; Stock Price Soars

  • Author: Suein L. Hwang
  • Date: 09/18/90

Barden Corp., said it signed a definitive agreement to be acquired for $131 million by FAG Bearings Corp., the U.S. subsidiary of FAG Kugelfischer Georg Schaefer KGaA, a West German manufacturer of bearings and industrial systems. News of the transaction, based on an offer of $66.50 a share, sent Barden shares soaring. They closed at $63, up $30.50, in national over-the-counter trading.

Barden, headquartered in Danbury, Conn., is the last independent U.S. manufacturer of precision ball bearings, with about 20% of the domestic market.

Under the agreement, FAG Finance Corp., a unit of FAG Bearings, will begin a tender offer for Barden's nearly two million shares no later than Friday. FAG Finance doesn't own any Barden shares at present. Barden said it expects the offer to be completed next month, subject to U.S. regulatory review.

Barden President Stanley Noss, in a joint statement with FAG Bearings, said Barden had entertained "informal overtures" from interested parties over the years, but none had resulted in serious discussions.

"We were looking for a partner with strong resources and technical capabilities so we could address the market efficiently. Foreign vs. domestic doesn't mean anything" in terms of who would acquire the company, Mr. Noss said. Barden said it and FAG Bearings, based in Stamford, Conn., would continue to operate separately and that all of Barden's employees would be retained.

Walter Schwarz, president of FAG Bearings, cited an "excellent strategic fit" of the two companies. FAG produces heavy-duty bearings, a range of standardized ball bearings, and aircraft and instrument bearings. Mr. Schwarz said FAG plans to move its instrument-bearing operations from Stratford, Ontario, to Barden's Danbury facility, as well as introduce new lines there.

In 1989, Barden had sales of $98.2 million and earnings of $2.9 million. FAG Kugelfischer had 1989 revenue of $2.8 billion.

In the past five years, Barden's three major domestic competitors -- MPB Corp., Fafnir Bearings and New Hampshire Ball Bearings Inc. -- have all been acquired. MBP was bought by Timken Co. last May, Fafnir Bearings is now a division of Ingersoll-Rand Co. and New Hampshire Ball Bearings is a unit of Minebea Co. of Japan.
