New Ford Canada Offer

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/18/90

TORONTO -- The Canadian subsidiary of Ford Motor Co. said it plans to make a new contract offer today that it hopes will settle a three-day-old strike by almost 13,000 unionized auto workers. Donald McKenzie, chief negotiator for Ford Canada, said the two sides were "still a fair distance apart." He added, however, that "now is the time to pick up the pace of negotiations."

The strike has closed nine Ford plants in Canada and has threatened to curtail some of the company's key U.S. operations that depend on parts manufactured north of the border.

Emerging from a brief meeting with union leaders, Mr. McKenzie said the company was still drafting its new offer, which will cover a range of economic issues, including wages and cost-of-living allowances. Ford Canada officials wouldn't elaborate on the specifics of the proposal.

Robert White, president of the Canadian Auto Workers union, took a wait-and-see attitude to the company's willingness to inject new momentum into the contract talks. Mr. White warned, however, that the union was still prepared to walk away from the bargaining table if there wasn't any progress made toward reaching a contract settlement by the end of the week.

But Ford's Mr. McKenzie indicated that the company expected movement from the union as well. "If there isn't progress over the next couple of days, then obviously we've got a major problem."
