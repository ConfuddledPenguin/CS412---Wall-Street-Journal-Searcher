United, TWA, Northwest Join Rivals in Fare Boost

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 09/18/90

United Airlines, Trans World Airlines and Northwest Airlines said yesterday they are matching other airlines' attempt to raise domestic fares 4.2% as of Oct. 1. The fare rise is almost certain to go into effect. The three carriers were the last major airlines to match rivals' plans to raise fares.

United is a unit of UAL Corp., and Northwest is a subsidiary of NWA Inc.

The carriers said the fare rise will affect all of their domestic fares. Through their lobbying group, the airlines have presented a separate proposal before the U.S. Transportation Department and foreign governments to raise international fares 6% to 10% on Oct. 1 as well.

If the 4.2% domestic fare rise goes into effect, it will be the second widespread increase in about a month. Airlines raised their domestic fares 5.3% on Aug. 31.

The airlines are battling fuel costs that have risen as much as 50% since Iraq invaded Kuwait in early August. Because fuel expenses are the second-largest operating expense for the carriers, airline executives say their carriers have been particularly stung by the rise in oil prices.
