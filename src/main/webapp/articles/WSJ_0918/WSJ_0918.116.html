Exxon Is Developing Natural Gas Reserves Of Offshore Alabama

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/18/90

NEW ORLEANS -- Exxon Corp. disclosed it has started a $1 billion development of its deep natural gas reserves in Mobile Bay, offshore Alabama that should lead to production in late 1993. The company said its Exxon USA unit started construction in March of a 300-million cubic feet per day onshore gas treatment facility, and has awarded major contracts for other key parts of the project. The development will include three offshore production platforms, 36 miles of pipelines to connect the platforms and the treatment plant, and the drilling of six new wells. Six existing wells are also being completed. The wells are being drilled to an average depth of 22,000 feet.

When completed, the Exxon development will be the largest of three offshore natural gas projects currently under way in that area, according to John H. Steele, Exxon USA Eastern Production Division manager. Mobil Corp. and Shell Oil Co. also are developing reserves there, he said. Mr. Steele said Exxon began acquiring leases in the area in 1981 and has so far invested about $1 billion there. The estimated productive life of the wells is 30 to 35 years.

Production platforms in 15-to-45-foot deep Alabama waters will be connected to the gas treatment facility in south Mobile County where hydrogen sulfide and carbon dioxide will be removed to prepare the gas for sale. Exxon said H.B. Zachry Co. of San Antonio, Texas, was awarded the primary gas treatment plant contract and Stearns-Roger Inc. of Denver, Colo., was named engineering subcontractor. Value of the contracts wasn't disclosed.
