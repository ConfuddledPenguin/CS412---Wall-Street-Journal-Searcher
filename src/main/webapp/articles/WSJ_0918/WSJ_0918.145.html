Who's News: Abbott Labs Names Hodgson President, Succeeding Schuler

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/18/90

ABBOTT PARK, Ill. -- Abbott Laboratories, filling posts vacant since strife erupted in its executive suite last year, named Thomas R. Hodgson president and chief operating officer, effective Oct. 1. Mr. Hodgson is currently executive vice president of Abbott and president of Abbott International, a division.

Abbott, a medical products company, said Mr. Hodgson, 48 years old, will be responsible for Abbott's operating divisions, as well as corporate engineering, corporate hospital marketing and TAP Pharmaceuticals, Abbott's joint venture with Takeda Chemical Industries Ltd. of Japan.

Mr. Hodgson succeeds Jack W. Schuler, who resigned in August 1989 citing "differences in philosophy" with Robert A. Schoellhorn, then-chairman and chief executive officer. Mr. Schuler's resignation was considered a forced departure.

Earlier this year, the Abbott board ousted Mr. Schoellhorn from his executive positions. After a fight with the company, Mr. Schoellhorn, who had remained an employee of Abbott, retired.

Mr. Hodgson becomes Abbott's fourth chief operating officer in 10 years. James Vincent and G. Kirk Rabb resigned in 1980 and 1985, respectively, also citing differences with Mr. Schoellhorn.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Abbott closed at $40.375, up 12.5 cents.

J. Duncan McIntyre was named to succeed Mr. Hodgson as president of Abbott International. Mr. McIntyre, who is currently a vice president and responsible for European operations at Abbott International, is also being promoted to senior vice president of the parent company.

Also, Abbott promoted to senior vice president the following vice presidents: Paul N. Clark, pharmaceutical operations; John C. Kane, Ross Products; John G. Kringel, hospital products; Thomas M. McNally, chemical and agricultural products; and David A. Thompson, diagnostic operations.
