Former Amex Systems Chief Pleads Guilty To Bribery Charges

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/18/90

ALEXANDRIA, Va. -- The former chief executive officer of a Los Angeles defense contractor pleaded guilty to bribing Navy officials for their assistance in obtaining military contracts, federal prosecutors said. Manuel Caldera, former chairman, chief executive and principal stockholder of Amex Systems Inc., waived indictment and pleaded guilty in federal court here to paying thousands of dollars to two Navy engineers for their help in obtaining and maintaining contracts to build transit receivers for Navy ships, according to U.S. Attorney Henry Hudson. The guilty plea arose out of the government's continuing investigation of defense procurement fraud, known as Operation Illwind.

Mr. Caldera made the illegal payments in connection with six Navy contracts awarded to his company between 1978 and 1984, according to his plea agreement. The contracts had a value of roughly $40 million, with potential follow-on value of an additional $100 million between 1984 and 1988, Mr. Hudson said in a statement. Mr. Caldera sold Amex Systems to Allied-Signal Inc. of Morristown, N.J., in November 1984 for $45 million, the prosecutor said.

Mr. Caldera, 59 years old, couldn't be reached for comment. He faces a maximum sentence of seven years in prison.

A spokesman for Allied-Signal said the company sold Amex Systems to Science Applications International of San Diego in 1988.

A senior official at Science Applications said Amex Systems was merged into its operations shortly after the purchase. The official said Mr. Caldera "was not part of the operation when we acquired it."
