Business Brief -- Chyron Corp.: Video Graphic Firm Files For Chapter 11 Protection

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/18/90

Chyron Corp., Melville, N.Y., filed for protection from creditors under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code, as it said it would last week. The maker of computer video graphic equipment, caught in a dispute between its two major lenders, said it couldn't repay $10 million in loans called by Manufacturers Hanover Trust Co.

Chyron was hoping to restructure loans and obtain additional financing, but Manufacturers Hanover and Chyron's other major lender, European American Bank, couldn't come to terms on sharing loan collateral. Manufacturers Hanover recently sued European American, charging fraud and breach of contract in the dispute. Chyron also was named in the suit, but Manufacturers isn't seeking any relief from Chyron. European American has $20 million in loans outstanding to Chyron.

"I feel like an innocent bystander shot in a subway," said Alfred O.P. Leubert, Chyron's chairman. "It's a wrong that should be righted."

Chyron officials said the company, in its filing for Chapter 11 protection in U.S. District Court in Brooklyn, N.Y., listed assets of $57 million and liabilites of $41 million.

Chyron, which employs 355, had a net loss of $1.9 million on revenue of $44.1 million in 1989.
