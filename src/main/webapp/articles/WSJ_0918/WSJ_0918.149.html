Business Brief -- Itel Corp.: S&P Lowers Debt Rating After Henley Group Accord

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/18/90

Standard & Poor's Corp. downgraded its ratings on more than $1 billion of Itel Corp. debt, citing the increased leverage that would result from Itel's agreement to buy back up to 18.7 million common shares from Henley Group Inc. S&P downgraded to single-B-minus from single-B its rating on Itel's $925 million in senior subordinated debentures and $82.5 million of preferred stock. It also lowered ratings on $200 million of Itel's Signal Capital Holdings Corp. unit's senior notes and on debt assumed by Itel Rail of Pullman Leasing Co. equipment trust certificates and of Trailmobile Finance Corp. sinking fund debentures.

S&P said the pending share repurchase from Henley, which is being financed through asset sales, will increase debt leverage during an anticipated economic slowdown. Itel said it had expected the downgradings.
