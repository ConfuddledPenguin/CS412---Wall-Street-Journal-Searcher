First Union to Focus On Lines in 4 States, End Acquisition Binge

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/18/90

NEW YORK -- First Union Corp., after a hefty banking and savings and loan acquisition binge in the late 1980s, intends to concentrate on increasing market share rather than further expansion through acquisition. Edward E. Crutchfield Jr., chairman of the Charlotte, N.C., banking company, noted that because of the acquisitions, First Union's earnings have been sluggish the past three years. "Now we'll try to make them pay off."

Mr. Crutchfield said he doesn't disagree with analysts' consensus estimates of per-share earnings of about $2.80 in 1990. "Unless the wheels come off in the fourth quarter we will reach that."

In 1989, First Union earned $256.2 million, or $2.40 a share. In the first half of 1990, profit rose 2% to 149 million, or $1.39 a share, compared with the year-earlier $146 million, or $1.37 a share.

Since 1985, when the U.S. Supreme Court allowed regional interstate banking, the company has made 22 acquisitions, including Florida National Bank for $850 million in January. It now has about $38 billion in assets, compared with $7 billion in 1985.

Mr. Crutchfield said the company's five-year goal will be to double the earnings of 1989 through consolidation and cost cutting. Also, it would attempt to increase market share in regions where it already does business rather than through expansion into other regions.

Mr. Crutchfield said the goal would be to increase market share in the southeast region to 16%. The company says it currently has about an 8.2% share of the market in four states -- North Carolina, South Carolina, Georgia and Florida -- where it has most of its offices. "I don't see going out to other states," Crutchfield said. "I'd rather have the 16% market share in the four regions."

Much of the growth is based on company's projection that the region will experience a heavy influx in population, and indications that the area's GNP growth will be double the nation's GNP growth of about 2%.
