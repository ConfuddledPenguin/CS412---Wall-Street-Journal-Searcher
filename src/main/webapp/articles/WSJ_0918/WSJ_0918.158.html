Marketing & Media: Geffen Ends Agreement With Rap Music Producer

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/18/90

HOLLYWOOD -- Geffen Records said it ended its agreement to manufacture and distribute music produced by Def American Recordings, escalating the controversy over violent and sexually graphic lyrics in popular music. Geffen had already refused to release Def American's latest record by The Geto Boys, a Houston-based rap group whose lyrics go well beyond those of 2 Live Crew -- the group that was arrested in Florida earlier this year. In one Geto Boys song, a man murders a woman and copulates with the corpse.

Geffen had already released records by other controversial Def American acts like Andrew Dice Clay and the heavy metal groups Slayer and Danzig, often removing the Geffen logo from the packages but distributing them nonetheless.

The Geto Boys simply accelerated a growing disenchantment with Def American's lineup, the company said. "It's a personal choice," said Chairman David Geffen. "I think anyone has the right to make any record they want, but I don't have to produce it. It's one thing to tell dirty jokes -- I'm not offended by 2 Live Crew -- but murder and mutilation, I don't want to be a part of it."

Def American's contribution to Geffen's profits is less than 1%, the company said. Time Warner's record division, which already agreed to manufacture the Geto Boys album, is expected to distribute the record and the whole Def American line. Unresolved was what label within Time Warner's sprawling music business would lend its name to Def American. Geffen itself distributes its records through Time Warner, though in 1991 it will become a unit of MCA Inc., which acquired it for about $600 million last March.
