REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: Tots Freed of Balance

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/18/90

The forced-busing war isn't over in Seattle. The school board voted 4 to 3 this month to relax integration mandates so as to permit students to attend where they and their parents wished. But the local NAACP chapter threatened to sue and, more important, the U.S. Education Department said it might have to cut off funding for "magnet" schools in the district because they could be out of racial balance. Morever, the state warned construction money could be lost. So the board, with what a member called an "economic gun to its head," postponed the new plan for a year. But it did manage to end busing of kindergartners. Result: The Seattle schools, after two decades of steady enrollment decline, gained 2,500 students this fall.
