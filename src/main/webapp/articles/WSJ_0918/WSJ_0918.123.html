Business Brief -- Financial Corp. of Santa Barbara: Company Seeks Protection With a Chapter 11 Filing

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/18/90

Financial Corp. of Santa Barbara said it filed for protection under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code in an attempt to "ensure an orderly disposition of the company's remaining assets." The company's main asset, Santa Barbara Savings & Loan Association, was seized in April by federal regulators. Financial Corp. officials said they recently reached a tentative agreement to dispose of the company's check-processing operation, its one remaining operating subsidiary.

Officials said the company's "most significant" creditors are the holders of $8.4 million in principal amount of convertible subordinated debentures issued by Santa Barbara Savings. The S&L defaulted on the debentures after the federal seizure, and the debenture trustee is now seeking repayment directly from Financial Corp.
