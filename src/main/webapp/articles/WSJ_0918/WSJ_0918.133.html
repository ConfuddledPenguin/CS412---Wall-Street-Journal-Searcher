Business Brief -- General Motors Corp.: Components Unit to Spend Over $1 Billion in Europe

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/18/90

General Motors Corp.'s Automotive Components Group said it plans to spend over $1 billion over the next five years on expanding its industrial capacity and on research and development in Europe. The manufacturing investment will involve the construction of several new production facilities in Europe, ACG Europe President Mark McCabe told a news conference. The funds will also be used to modernize the division's 49 existing plants in Europe, he said.

Mr. McCabe said he expects the European market for car parts to grow to $100 billion by the year 2000 from around $60 billion at present.

He said the forecast is based on the assumption that new-car sales in Western Europe will rise to 14.2 million units by 1995 from 13.5 million at present. Demand from Eastern Europe could add another 3.5 million cars to the 1995 figure, he noted.
