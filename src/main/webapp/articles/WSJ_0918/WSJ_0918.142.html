Business Brief -- King World Productions Inc.: Firm Withdraws Its Plan For $36 Million Offering

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/18/90

Citing weakness in the stock market, King World Productions Inc. withdrew its proposed $36 million public offering for Merlin Program Capital Inc. New York-based King World, a syndicator of television shows, filed the Merlin offering with the Securities and Exchange Commission two months ago. Its plan was to set up Merlin as a fund to expand production. Each investment unit was to include one share in Merlin, priced at $16 a share, plus a warrant to purchase a common share of King World in several years at a price set now.

However, as the stock market has declined in recent weeks, King World's stock has fallen about 15%, closing yesterday at $21.50 a share. The warrants would have been priced at about a 30% premium to the current stock price, said Jeffrey Epstein, chief financial officer of King World. "We don't want to dilute current shareholders by issuing equity at these prices," he said.

The withdrawal of the offering won't constrain King World's ability to develop new television shows, said Mr. Epstein.
