Business Brief -- General Signal Corp.: Concern's Agreement to Sell Ultratech Stepper Unit Ends

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/18/90

General Signal Corp., Stamford, Conn., said that an agreement to sell its Ultratech Stepper unit to an employee group has been canceled by mutual agreement of the parties. Instead, General Signal said that Arthur W. Zafiropoulo has been named president of Ultratech. Mr. Zafiropoulo most recently headed General Signal's international semiconductor operations and was president of the company's Kayex unit.

General Signal, a maker of process controls, electrical equipment and semiconductors, had reached a agreement in July to sell 300-employee Ultratech to New Enterprise Associates, a San Francisco-based venture capital firm. A General Signal spokesman said the agreement collapsed due to inability to agree on the amount of financing from General Signal.

Ultratech Stepper, which makes photolithography equipment for semiconductors, accounted for about $40 million of General Signal's sales of $1.92 billion in 1989.
