Former Tax Preparer Is Sentenced to Prison

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 09/18/90

CHICAGO -- A federal judge here sentenced a former tax preparer to two years in prison for failing to report more than $230,000 of his income. Judge Suzanne B. Conlon also sentenced 34-year-old Kenneth H. Stenzel to five years on probation and ordered him not to work in any fiduciary capacity during that time. Mr. Stenzel had pleaded guilty to the tax charges as part of a plea agreement with federal prosecutors.

Mr. Stenzel, who operated a tax-preparation service known as Kenneth G. Stenzel & Associates in Addison, Ill., failed to file income-tax returns reporting the income from his business for 1983 through 1985.
