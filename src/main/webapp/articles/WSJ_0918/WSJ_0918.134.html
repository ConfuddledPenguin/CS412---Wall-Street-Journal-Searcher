Business Brief -- American Greetings Corp.: Fiscal 2nd-Quarter Net Rose 24% on 10% Revenue Gain

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/18/90

In its fiscal second quarter ended Aug. 31, American Greetings Corp. earned $6.2 million, or 20 cents a share, up 24% from the year-earlier $5 million, or 16 cents a share. Revenue increased 10% to $311.8 million from $282.7 million. The company said its strong performance "is in line with our expectations and reaffirms our projection of a record year in revenue and earnings for fiscal 1991." In fiscal 1990, the company earned $72.2 million, or $2.25 a share, on revenue of $1.31 billion.

The Cleveland-based greeting card maker earned 80 cents a share for the six months ended Aug. 31, the highest first-half net in its 84-year history.

The company attributed the record earnings to cost control measures and gains in core card lines, "anytime" cards and accessories including gift bags and boxes.

Net for the first half jumped 13% to $25.5 million, or 80 cents a share, from $22.5 million, or 70 cents a share. Revenue for the first half increased 9% to $623 million from $572.5 million.
