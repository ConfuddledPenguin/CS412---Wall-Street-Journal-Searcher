Education Department Calls a Halt to Funds For Georgia District

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/18/90

WASHINGTON -- The Department of Education stopped disbursement of all federal education funds to the DeKalb County School District, Georgia's largest, after finding violations of a law that bars discrimination against handicapped persons. The order will halt more than $7 million in annual federal aid to the 72,000 students in the suburban Atlanta school system. The department found that DeKalb County illegally refused to allow federal inspectors to investigate allegations of discrimination against handicapped students.

County officials contend that the department's Office of Civil Rights doesn't have the authority it claims to enforce the Education for the Handicapped Act.

Funding-termination actions are "rarer than hen's teeth," said Richard Komer, deputy assistant secretary of education for civil rights. The last such order was in 1982, and this is the first time the department has stopped funding over alleged discrimination against handicapped students, he said.

DeKalb County, which has been fighting since 1984 to challenge the department's attempt to use an anti-discrimination statute to police the Education for the Handicapped Act, has asked a federal district court to enjoin the government from terminating funding.
