Manville Dispute With EPA

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/18/90

DENVER -- Manville Corp. said it settled a dispute with the Environmental Protection Agency over its Waukegan, Ill., facility, agreeing to pay $165,000 in penalties. The EPA alleged that Manville violated a consent decree that stipulated how the company would clean up asbestos wastes at the facility by improperly grading the site, causing asbestos emissions. Manville maintained that the grading was done according to the consent decree plan, and no violations had taken place.

The company said it settled the matter with the EPA "to resolve the case quickly." In the settlement, the government stipulated that it never alleged that the violations were intentional. The $7 million cleanup was completed in May.
