Who's News: Myerson Quits Asian Office Of Wasserstein Perella

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/18/90

NEW YORK -- Toby S. Myerson resigned as head of Wasserstein Perella & Co.'s Tokyo office, and will be succeeded by a former executive of Nomura Securities Co., which owns 20% of Wasserstein Perella. The appointment of Shin Kijima, currently a managing director at Wasserstein Perella, comes after more than a year of rumors that the relationship is strained between the merger boutique and Nomura. A spokesman for Wasserstein Perella denied the rumors, but added: "Mr. Kijima, in his new position, strengthens the ties between Wasserstein Perella and Nomura."

Though Wasserstein Perella has worked on a number of medium-sized merger deals involving Japanese companies, it hasn't participated in any Japan-U.S. transactions valued at $1 billion or more, unlike several of its competitors.

Mr. Myerson, who held the post at the merger boutique for about 15 months, will return as a partner to the law firm of Paul, Weiss, Rifkind, Wharton & Garrison, where he was a partner before he joined Wasserstein Perella in June 1989.

"We are sad to see {Mr. Myerson} leave our Tokyo effort as a result of his desire to return to New York, but he will remain a valued friend and adviser," said Joseph R. Perella, chairman of Wasserstein Perella.

Mr. Kijima, 40, has worked for Wasserstein Perella in New York for the past two years, and will move to Tokyo in his new post. Previously, Mr. Kijima was president of Nomura Bank (Switzerland) Ltd.

Wasserstein Perella also named Yoshitaka Kitao, a managing director of the firm, as head of its Japanese international advisory business in London.
