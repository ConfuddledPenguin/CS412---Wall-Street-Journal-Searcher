What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/18/90

GM AND THE UAW AGREED on a three-year contract. The terms call for the automaker to spend as much as $4 billion to sweeten a range of job and income security programs. The agreement imposes restrictions on GM's ability to close plants but apparently doesn't require the company to find work for some 30,000 GM union members laid off during the past three years. ---

A United Airlines union group seeking to buy United's parent, UAL, plans to meet with banks this week about a new offer of $175 to $180 a share, or $3.82 billion to $3.92 billion, said people familiar with the deal.

---

The G-7 nations' finance ministers and central bankers will meet in Washington Saturday to discuss higher oil prices, recent interest rate rises, and the decline in the dollar.

---

Charles Keating is to be arraigned today on criminal fraud charges in a California state court. The charges are said to include allegations of fraudulent sales of debentures through the now-collapsed Lincoln S&L.

---

Crude oil prices soared to records on the New York Mercantile Exchange following events that pointed up the likelihood of a war in the Middle East and possible supply disrup tions. The October crude contract ended $1.87 a barrel higher at $33.63.

---

Budget negotiators continued to haggle over what price the Democrats would demand for a cut in the capital-gains tax, and Bush renewed his call for such a reduction.

---

Colonial Realty is the target of efforts by six banks to force the company into involuntary bankruptcy-court liquidation. Colonial's financial distress raises the possibility of losses to the many individual investors in its real-estate partnerships.

---

U.S. bankers are concerned that economic weakness will lead to increased defaults on credit-card debt.

---

Italy's Pirelli proposed to West Germany's Continental that they merge their tire businesses to challenge France's Michelin, America's Goodyear and Japan's Bridgestone.

---

U.S. bank regulators would be allowed to raise deposit insurance premiums to whatever levels they feel necessary under a bill passed by the House. Separately, bank regulators settled a dispute over capital levels.

---

NCR is expected to unveil today a redesign of its product line based on Intel's computer chips and software.

---

Sears debt was downgraded by Moody's, which lowered ratings on about $6.9 billion of the company's securities. The ratings agency said a continuing drop in consumer spend ing will hamper the company's efforts to boost merchandising results.

---

The Japanese stock market is the focus of new futures and options offered by three major U.S. exchanges.
