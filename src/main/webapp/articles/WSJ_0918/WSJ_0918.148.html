Electronic Library Favored For Tax-Exempt Market

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/18/90

WASHINGTON -- A Municipal Securities Rulemaking Board proposal to create an electronic library for municipal bond disclosure documents is winning support from those who have an interest in the municipal bond market. Out of more than 90 public-comment letters received so far by the Securities and Exchange Commission, most favor the plan to create a Municipal Securities Information Library System.

The comment period ends soon, and the SEC is expected to consider the plan before year end, perhaps next month.

"Access to information about securities and issuers has been identified as a chronic problem for the market," said Savannah, Ga., Finance Director Richard Evans, a proposal supporter.

The prospective library's costs "are more than offset by the market efficiencies it will provide," said North Carolina Treasurer Harlan Boyles.

Another supporter, the Municipal Securities Committee of the North American Securities Administrators Association, said it would ultimately like to see the SEC and MSRB mandate the filing of continuing updates by bond issuers.

MSRB Chairman Thomas Sexton, managing director of First Boston Corp., said: "The Board's primary purpose is similar to the Commission's -- investor protection comes first." He said "when investors do not have access to complete information about municipal securities, the prices they pay for these securities may not be the fair market value."
