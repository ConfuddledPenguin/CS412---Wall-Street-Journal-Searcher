Moody's Reviews Ratings of Three Japanese Banks

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 09/18/90

TOKYO -- Moody's Investors Service Inc. said it is reviewing its ratings of the long-term debt of three Japanese banks and some units for possible downgrading. The three banks are Mitsubishi Bank, rated triple-A; Tokai Bank, rated double-A-2; and Long-Term Credit Bank of Japan, or LTCB, also rated double-A-2. The respective Prime-1 ratings of the short-term debt of the banks aren't being reviewed, Moody's said.

Mitsubishi and Tokai are Japanese "city banks," institutions with nationwide networks of branches. LTCB is one of Japan's three designated long-term credit banks, which are primarily wholesale lenders.

Last month Moody's downgraded two Japanese city banks, Dai-Ichi Kangyo Bank and Fuji Bank, after placing them under review in the spring. And still earlier this year Moody's downgraded several trust banks, Mitsui Trust & Banking Corp., Mitsubishi Trust & Banking Corp. and Yasuda Trust & Banking Corp.

Now put under review, Moody's said, are:

-- Mitsubishi Bank's long-term deposits, senior debt and standby letter-of-credit obligations and the senior debt of the bank's guaranteed subsidiary, Mitsubishi Bank of Australia Ltd.

-- Tokai Bank's long-term deposits, senior debt and letter-of-credit backed obligations.

-- LTCB's long-term deposits, senior debt and long-term standby letter-of-credit obligations, Long-Term Credit Bank of Japan Finance N.V.'s senior debt and LTCB Australia Ltd.'s long-term deposits.

Moody's cited Mitsubishi Bank's "relatively weak core profitability and competitive positions among its Japanese peers" and "vulnerability to domestic asset-quality deterioration."

In reviewing Tokai Bank, Moody's will look at the lender's quality of assets, especially "its domestic real estate-related loan portfolio and its overseas credits," as well as its core earnings and competitiveness, the rating concern said.

Meanwhile, LTCB's "weakening franchise as a wholesale bank" as well as the bank's asset quality will come under scrutiny, Moody's said.
