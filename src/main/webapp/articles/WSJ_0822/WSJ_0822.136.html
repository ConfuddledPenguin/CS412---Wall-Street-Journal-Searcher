Business Brief -- Dayton Hudson Corp.: Profit Rise of 7% Posted Despite Acquisition Costs

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/22/90

Dayton Hudson Corp. said net income rose 7% in the fiscal second quarter, despite the negative impact of its recent acquisition of the Marshall Field's department store chain. The Minneapolis-based retailer earned $59.8 million, or 75 cents a share, for the period ended Aug. 4, compared with $56 million, or 72 cents a share, a year earlier. The Field's acquisition reduced earnings in the quarter by about 15 cents a share, the company said.

Revenue for the period rose 3.2% to $3.2 billion from $3.1 billion.

The company said it expects the Field's acquisition to reduce earnings in the third and fourth quarters by a total of five cents a share. The company said interest and depreciation expenses related to the Field's acquisition would offset Field's contribution to operating earnings in 1990.

For the first six months, Dayton Hudson said net income rose 28% to $123.9 million, or $1.56 a share, from $96.8 million, or $1.23 a share. The latest period includes a net gain of $6.6 million, or nine cents a share, for accounting changes.

Revenue in the six months rose nearly 6% to $6.25 billion from $5.92 billion.
