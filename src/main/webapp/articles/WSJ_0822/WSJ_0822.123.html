Business Brief -- Canadian Imperial Bank: Bank, Four Partners Form An Investment Company

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/22/90

Canadian Imperial Bank of Commerce said it and four partners have committed $100 million to form a Hong Kong-based investment company. The Toronto-based bank said newly formed CEF New Asia Partners Ltd. is seeking additional investors to increase its capital to as much as $250 million over the next six months.

It said the other founding partners in CEF New Asia are Hong Kong's Cheung Kong (Holdings) Ltd., General Electric Co.'s GE Capital Corp. subsidiary, and Mitsubishi Corp. and Nikko Securities Co. of Japan.

Canadian Imperial said the new concern will make equity investments and engage in merchant banking in the Asia-Pacific region.

It said the new investment company is sponsored by CEF Holdings Ltd., a financial services concern jointly owned by the Canadian Imperial and Cheung Kong.

Robert McBain, group managing director of CEF Holdings, was named chairman of CEF New Asia. S. Steve Chu, former head of CEF Holdings' corporate finance division, was named managing director of CEF New Asia.
