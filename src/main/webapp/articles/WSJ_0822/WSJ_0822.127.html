Ex-Vendor for Unit Of Southern Co. Gets Fine and Probation

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/22/90

ATLANTA -- A former vendor for a Southern Co. unit who made illegal political contributions on behalf of the electric utility was fined $12,000 and sentenced to two years probation. In June, Fred Suttles Jr., president of Outdoor Media Inc., pleaded guilty in federal court here to filing a false corporate tax return. He agreed to cooperate with a continuing federal investigation of the affair.

Outdoor Media, an outdoor advertising firm in Pensacola, Fla., rented billboard space to the Gulf Power Co. unit of Southern. According to court papers, Mr. Suttles directed his employees and several of his vendors to contribute $40,550 to state and local political candidates designated by Gulf Power; he then reimbursed the workers and vendors. The income-tax charge relates to his listing those reimbursements as legitimate business expenses.

The Internal Revenue Service asserted that Mr. Suttles agreed to the scheme to win Gulf Power advertising contracts. The scheme was intended to circumvent federal law, which bars utility holding companies from making political contributions.
