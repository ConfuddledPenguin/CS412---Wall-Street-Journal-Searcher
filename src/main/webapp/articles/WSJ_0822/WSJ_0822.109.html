Business Brief -- Control Data Corp.: Approval Received to Sell Mainframes to Soviet Union

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/22/90

Control Data Corp. said the Commerce Department granted an export license to ship six of the company's largest computers to the Soviet Union, where they will monitor nuclear power plant performance. The Cyber 960 mainframe machines made by the Minneapolis-based manufacturer are valued at a total of $32 million and are believed the most powerful exported to the U.S.S.R. Shipment will begin next month, Control Data said.

Ordered after the disaster at the Soviets' Chernobyl reactor, the computers will analyze safety factors at civilian power-generating plants. The export license followed assurances that the computers would be limited to safety analysis.
