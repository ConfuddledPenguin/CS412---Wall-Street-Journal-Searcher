Business Brief -- U.S. Gold Corp.: Denver Company to Form Venture With Homestake

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/22/90

U.S. Gold Corp. said it reached an agreement in principle with Homestake Mining Co. to form a joint venture at U.S. Gold's Tonkin Springs gold mining project near Eureka, Nev. Terms weren't disclosed. The agreement requires approval of Denver-based U.S. Gold's senior secured bank lender and its subordinated debt holders, and requires settlement of existing liens on the property.

Under the joint venture, the two companies will continue to develop the project's bio-oxidation technology for extracting gold from ore, and they will jointly own the technology. Homestake of San Francisco will provide funds for operating the project, which is currently cash-strapped, and for further exploration at the Tonkin Springs property.

The Tonkin Springs bio-oxidation project, which uses bacteria to release gold from sulfidic ores, is the first commercial application of the technology in the U.S. The operation has produced about 2,000 ounces of gold, but revenue has fallen far short of paying for the $31 million project, which has been plagued by cost overruns and delays.
