PepsiCo Expects $5 Million Net Gain In Third Quarter

  • Author: Michael J. McCarthy and Clay Chandler
  • Date: 08/22/90

PepsiCo Inc., following a successful offering of Kentucky Fried Chicken stock in Japan, said it expects to take several gains and charges in the third quarter that will result in a net gain of $5 million. The snack food, soft drink and restaurant company, which is based in Purchase, N.Y., expects to report a one-time gain of $50 million after tax from the sale of stock in an initial offering of its Kentucky Fried Chicken joint venture in Japan.

At the same time, Pepsi will record several charges that will total about $45 million after taxes. Among other things, the charges cover the planned closings of certain underperforming restaurants, and a provision for potentially uncollectible receivables from certain retail customers.

In the year-earlier third quarter, Pepsi had net income of $269.3 million, or $1.02 a share.

Under the offering of Kentucky Fried Chicken Japan stock, Pepsi said it would report a pretax $86 million gain from the sale of the 2.25 million shares and a $24 million equity gain from the sale of two million new shares by the joint venture. The Japanese business is a joint venture of Mitsubishi Corp. and Pepsi.

Under the offering, which took advantage of high prices provided by Japan's still-inflated stock market, each partner will retain a 30.5% interest in the company. The shares were sold at a price of 8,470 yen a share, or about $57 -- the maximum offering price of the range set by the Japanese Ministry of Finance, Pepsi said. The stock sale was the largest initial public offering in Japan this year.

Partly because of its down-home Southern American image -- personified by the goateed Colonel Sanders -- Kentucky Fried Chicken has been popular in Japan. Its first outlet opened 20 years ago, and with some 900 restaurants in the densely populated country now, Kentucky Fried Chicken has grown to an $800-million-a-year enterprise. Japan is the chain's largest international market.

Leonard Schutzman, a Pepsi senior vice president, said Kentucky Fried Chicken Japan plans to use the new capital to expand operations in the Japanese market. Despite already being Japan's biggest foreign fast-food chain, the company has ambitious goals for the future. Management thinks the Japanese market can sustain as many as 2,000 additional Kentucky Fried Chicken outlets, according to a spokesman at Kentucky Fried Chicken Japan.
