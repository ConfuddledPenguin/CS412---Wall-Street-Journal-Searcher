New Securities Issues

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/22/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report. MUNICIPAL

Delaware Transportation Authority -- $130 million of transportion system senior revenue bonds, 1990 Series, due July 1, 1991-2004 and 2010, tentatively priced by a Dillon Read & Co. group to yield from 5.90% in 1991 to 7.40% in 2010. Current interest serial bonds are priced to yield from 5.90% in 1991 to 7.25% in 2004. There is $58.2 million of 6 3/4% term bonds priced to yield 7.40% in 2010. The bonds are rated single-A-1/double-A-minus.

State of New York Mortgage Agency -- $123 million of homeowner mortgage revenue bonds, Series RR, due Oct. 1, 2010 and 2017, and Series SS, due April 1 and Oct. 1, 1992-2000, and Oct. 1, 2019 and 2022, tentatively priced by a Bear Stearns & Co. group to yield from 6.20% in 1992 to 7.95% in 2022. Series RR consists of $28.5 million of 7.65% term bonds priced at par due in 2010. There also is $36.4 million of 7 3/4% term bonds priced at par due in 2017. Series SS current interest serial bonds are priced at par to yield from 6.20% in 1992 to 7.20% in 2000. There are $15.7 million of 7 1/2% term bonds priced par due in 2019 and $28.2 million of 7.95% term bonds priced at par due in 2022. Interest on the Series SS bonds will be treated as a preference item in calculating the federal alternative minimum tax that may be imposed on certain investors. Series RR bonds are not subject to the tax. The bonds are rated double-A/double-A.

Clark County, Nev. -- $53.8 million of transportation improvement limited tax bonds, Series 1990, due March 1 and Sept. 1, 1991-2000, via a Clayton Brown & Associates Inc. group. The bonds, which are insured and triple-A-rated, were priced for reoffering to yield from 5.90% for bonds due March 1, 1991, to 6.95% for bonds due March 1 and Sept. 1, 1999. Bonds due in 2000 were not formally reoffered.

Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $501 million of floating-rate and inverse floating-rate funding note pass-through certificates issue, Series 195, via Lehman Brothers. The offering is backed by 9% Freddie Mac giant mortgage participation certificates provided by Structured Asset Securities Corp., a subsidiary of Lehman Brothers. The offering consists of four recombinable inverse floater classes and one floating-rate class, each representing ownership interests in one of five individual funding notes. The coupon rate of each recombinable inverse floater class resets quarterly based on changes in the three-month London interbank offered rate and a multiplier formula. All classes pay principal and interest quarterly.

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $500 million of Remic mortgage securities offered in 12 classes by Kidder, Peabody & Co. The offering, Series 1990-116, was priced Aug. 16. It is backed by Fannie Mae 9% securities with a weighted average coupon rate of 9% and weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29.58 years.

Shawmut National Remic Trust 1990-A -- $218 million of home equity loan pass-through certificates, via Morgan Stanley & Co. The securities have a coupon rate of 9.15% and are priced at 99 25/32 to yield 9.26%, or 100 basis points above the interpolated two-year and three-year Treasury notes yield. The seller of the underlying home equity loans is Shawmut Bank, and the servicer of the loans is Connecticut National Bank. The securities have an expected average life of 2.5 years based on an annual prepayment rate of 20%. Expected final maturity is July 15, 1997. Legal final maturity is Nov. 15, 2005. Connecticut National Bank and Shawmut Bank are units of Shawmut National Corp., Hartford, Conn.

EUROBONDS

Interfinance Credit National (France) -- Six billion yen of 10% Eurobonds due Sept. 13, 1991, priced at 101 1/8, via New Japan Securities Ltd. Redemption linked to Nikkei Stock Index. Credit National guarantor. Fees 1 1/8.

Council of Europe (agency) -- 200 million marks of bonds due Sept. 14, 2000, at par, via Trinkaus & Burkhardt. Coupon 10% for first three years, payable annually, and 16% less six-month London interbank offered rate for final seven years, payable semiannually. Fees 0.20.

West German Post -- Two billion marks of 9% bonds due Sept. 1, 2000, at 100.70, yielding 8.89%, via Deutsche Bundesbank. Fees 7/8.
