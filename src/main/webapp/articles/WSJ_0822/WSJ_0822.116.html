Caesars World Raises Caesars New Jersey Bid to $22.25 a Share

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/22/90

LOS ANGELES -- Caesars World Inc. said it agreed in principle to increase by 25 cents a share, to $22.25 a share, or $48.4 million, its tender offer for the 13.4% stake it doesn't already hold in Caesars New Jersey Inc. The sweetened offer is part of a settlement of litigation brought by certain shareholders of Caesars New Jersey. They charged that, among other things, the company's original $47.9 million offer was inadequate.

Caesars World, an owner and operator of hotels and casinos, also extended its offer to Sept. 7 from today, and said it might further extend the offer until final court approval of the settlement, which isn't expected for at least two months. Caesars New Jersey owns and operates Caesars Atlantic City, a hotel and casino.

Caesars World also agreed to amend its offer to pay interest on $22 a share if the offer is extended more than 30 days after the mailing of supplemental offer materials describing the settlement and the amendment. The mailing is expected this week.

Caesars World also agreed that if it purchases at least 549,792 Caesars New Jersey shares, giving Caesars World at least 90% of the outstanding shares, it will merge Caesars New Jersey with Caesars World or a Caesars World subsidiary. In the merger, shareholders would receive $22.25 a share, without interest. Of the 2,175,945 Caesars New Jersey shares that Caesars World doesn't already own, about 1.5 million had been tendered as of yesterday.
