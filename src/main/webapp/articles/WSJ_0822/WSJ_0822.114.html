Business Brief -- Dun & Bradstreet Corp.: Medical Publishing Division Of Unit Is Put Up for Sale

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/22/90

Dun & Bradstreet Corp., New York, said it is putting up for sale the medical publishing division of its IMS International unit, as it continues to divest itself of nonstrategic businesses. The communications division of IMS International publishes medical journals and provides marketing and consulting services for the pharmaceutical industry. The division had revenue of $125 million in 1989.

D&B said that while the division is profitable, it wants to focus D&B's investments in IMS International's core market research business. IMS International was acquired in May 1988 in a transaction valued at $1.6 billion.

The financial-information services company said it still expects 1990 earnings to be around $2.80 a share.
