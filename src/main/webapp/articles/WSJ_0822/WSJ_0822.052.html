Technology: Prime Computer-MIPS Project

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/22/90

NATICK, Mass. -- Prime Computer Inc., a computer maker, said it will adopt MIPS Computer Systems Inc.'s reduced instruction-set computing, or RISC, microprocessor technology in a new line of computers, as part of a technology partnership with the Sunnyvale, Calif., microprocessor designer and computer systems maker. Terms weren't disclosed. The RISC chips will be at the heart of Prime's EXL 7000 system, based on a version of the industry standard Unix software operating system, Prime said. Separately, Prime said it introduced transaction-processing software for its Unix-based computer systems.
