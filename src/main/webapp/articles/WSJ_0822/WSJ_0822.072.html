Dividend News: Smith Corona Reduces Payout to Five Cents, A Cut of Two-Thirds

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/22/90

NEW CANAAN, CONN. -- Smith Corona Corp., as expected, cut its quarterly dividend to five cents a share from 15 cents. The reduced dividend is payable Oct. 5 to shareholders of record Sept. 21. The maker of typewriters and personal word processors has had a series of setbacks in the year since Hanson PLC, the company's British owner, sold 50% of Smith Corona's stock to the public.

For the fiscal fourth quarter ended June 30, the company reported a loss of $1.5 million, or five cents a share, on sales of $79.8 million. In fiscal 1990, it earned $32.9 million, or $1.09 a share, on sales of $471.4 million.
