Business Brief -- Crystalate Holdings PLC: Vishay Intertechnology Says Bid for Company Is `Moot'

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/22/90

Vishay Intertechnology Inc., Malvern, Pa., said its bid to acquire all of Crystalate Holdings PLC for about $52 million was "rendered moot," but the company still hopes to buy Crystalate's U.S. operations. Vishay, a maker of resistors, sensors and strain gauges, made its original offer in April because Crystalate sought help in trying to thwart a hostile bid by TT Group PLC. But Vishay said its bid lapsed because of a lengthy delay by British regulators. In the meantime, about 56% of the target company's shares were tendered instead to TT Group, which had bid only about $47 million.

Despite that setback, Vishay said it intends to negotiate with TT Group to acquire the two U.S. units of Crystalate, a U.K.-based maker of resistors and automotive sensors. The units, with a combined 10.4% share of the U.S. market for fixed resistors, have combined annual sales of about $60 million.

Felix Zandman, Vishay's chairman and chief executive, said TT Group officials have indicated that "they are interested in selling" the U.S. operations of Crystalate. "We still want to buy, provided the price is right," he said.
