Waxman Industries Says Net Income Fell 36% in 4th Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/22/90

BEDFORD HEIGHTS, Ohio -- Waxman Industries Inc. expects to report that fiscal fourth-quarter net income fell 36% to $1.4 million, or 14 cents a share, largely as a result of higher interest expenses, said Armond Waxman, president and co-chief executive. However, operating profit for the quarter ended June 30 rose to $9 million from $7.8 million. In the year-earlier period, the wholesaler of plumbing, electrical and hardware products for the do-it-yourself home repair market had net income of $2.2 million, or 24 cents a share.

Fourth-quarter revenue rose to $111 million from $94.6 million, following the May 1989 acquisition of Ideal Group of Cos., a Canadian company that now accounts for 55% of Waxman sales. The acquisition also boosted the company's interest expense to $6.7 million from $4.6 million last year.

Fourth-quarter results were also hurt by Canada's 14 3/4 prime rate -- the highest since 1972. Moreover, about 10,000 members of the construction trade were on strike in Ontario, one of Waxman's major profit centers, for two months during the fourth quarter.

"The strike came at a very bad time for us," Waxman said in an interview. "That, coupled with the high interest rates, really slowed down renovation, remodeling and construction." He said he's optimistic interest rates will go down, following a recent half-point drop in the prime.

In the current fiscal year, Waxman said the company will concentrate on reducing debt, and consolidating operations. He said he expects to retire about 10% of the existing $220 million in debt.

One debt-reduction approach will be to sell and lease back about 12 facilities in Canada to generate cash to retire debt. In the U.S., Waxman will concentrate on tightening inventories and receivables, reducing working capital and consolidating warehouse operations.

"Our strategy is to pay down debt and be more efficient in operations by doing more rationalization of our business," Waxman said.
