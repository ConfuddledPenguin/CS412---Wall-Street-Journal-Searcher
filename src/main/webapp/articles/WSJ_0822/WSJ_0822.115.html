Business Brief -- Franklin Electronic Publishers Inc.: Seiko Agrees to Distribute Firm's Products in Japan

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/22/90

Franklin Electronic Publishers Inc. said it reached an agreement with Seiko Instruments Inc. to distribute its electronic reference products in Japan. Franklin, a leading producer of hand-held electronic dictionaries and thesauruses with voice synthesizers, said it is the first distribution agreement between the two companies, although Franklin has long been a big buyer of Seiko's liquid display crystals. It agreed to join hands with Seiko after nearly two years of discussions.

Morton E. David, chairman of Franklin, said he decided to go with a distribution agreement rather than a licensing agreement because he did not want to give away the company's advanced software capabilities.

Although Mr. David said it is too early to set sales targets for the Japanese market, he is optimistic that the electronic reference products will become popular learning tools for Japanese who are determined to learn English. The devices use speech synthesis to pronounce U.S. English words.

Mount Holly, N.J.-based Franklin currently sells its products in 33 countries.
