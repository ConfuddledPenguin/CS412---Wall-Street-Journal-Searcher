International Brief -- Skandinaviska Enskilda Banken: New Share Issue to 3 Allies In Nordic Banking Cleared

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/22/90

Directors of Stockholm-based Skandinaviska Enskilda Banken, or SE Banken, approved a 577 million Swedish kronor ($100.1 million) issue of new shares to three Nordic banking allies, a long-awaited move allowed by the recent abolition of a ban on foreign ownership in Swedish banks. The action puts the final touch on Scandinavian Banking Partners, a six year-old alliance of SE Banken, Unibank Denmark, Union Bank of Finland and Den Norske Banken, which extend customers access to one another's branch networks, operate a joint cross-border payments system and join forces in Scandinavian corporate finance activities. The partners agreed in 1984 to swap stakes in one another.

SE Banken's offering represents a 2.5% stake, with Union Bank to get a 1.1% holding and Unibank and Den Norske Banken 0.7% each. SE Banken said the newly issued shares equal the combined value of its 3.2% stake in Union Bank, 3.7% in Den Norske Banken and 1.4% in Unibank. The banks will pay 88.42 kronor for each new SE Banken A-free share, which has 1/10th of a vote. Each SE Banken A-restricted share has one vote.
