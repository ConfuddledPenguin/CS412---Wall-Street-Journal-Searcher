Cummins Engine Co. Ratings Are Increased On $861 Million Debt

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/22/90

NEW YORK -- Moody's Investors Service Inc., saying Cummins Engine Co.'s capital structure and debt protection have improved because of a recent equity investment by three friendly corporate holders, raised its ratings on the company's $861 million in debt. Last month, Ford Motor Co., Tenneco Inc. and Japan's Kubota Ltd. agreed to pay $250 million for a 27% aggregate stake in the maker of diesel engines. Moody's said it thinks the Columbus, Ind., concern's "planned use of the proceeds from the equity infusion to pay down outstanding debt and to redeem preferred stock will lead to a significant reduction in the company's leverage and fixed charges." Moody's also said it feels that the alliances with strategic investors associated with the buying "offer good opportunities for profitable new business."

The rating concern said, however, that it "expects Cummins's earnings and cash flow to remain under pressure over the intermediate term," because of "soft industry demand, persistent price discounting and the ongoing need to invest large sums in capital improvement and product development programs."

Moody's raised its ratings on Cummins senior debentures to Baa-3 from Ba-1, increased its subordinated debentures to Ba1 from Ba-3, and raised its rating on Cummins preferred stock to "Ba-1" from "Ba-3."
