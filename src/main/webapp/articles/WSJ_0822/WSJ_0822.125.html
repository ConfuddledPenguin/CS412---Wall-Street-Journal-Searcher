Business Brief -- Mitchell Energy & Development: Budget for Capital Spending In Fiscal 1991 Is Increased

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/22/90

Mitchell Energy & Development Corp., The Woodlands, Texas, said it increased its capital spending budget for fiscal 1991 ending Jan. 31 by 9.1% to $227 million from $208 million. The revised sum represents an increase of about $32 million, or 16%, from year-earlier capital spending of $195 million.

The largest part of the increase, about $12 million, is allocated for Mitchell's transmission and processing division, primarily for the construction of underground natural gas storage facilities. The division's total spending budget now stands at $29 million, about the same as a year earlier.

The remainder of the increased sum, about $7 million, is earmarked for Mitchell's real estate division and provides for, among other things, the purchase of a hotel in Galveston, Texas. The division's fiscal 1991 expenditures will total $98 million, compared with $82 million in fiscal 1990.
