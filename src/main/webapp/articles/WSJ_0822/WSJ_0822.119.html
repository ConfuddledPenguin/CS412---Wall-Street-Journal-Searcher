Business Brief -- Abrams Industries Inc.: Deficit May Total $300,000 For the Fiscal First Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/22/90

Abrams Industries Inc. expects to report a loss of $250,000 to $300,000 in the first quarter ended July 31, said Bernard W. Abrams, chairman and chief executive officer. The loss will amount to eight cents to 10 cents a share. A year ago, the company had net income of $192,320, or six cents a share. Sales are expected to range from $16.5 million to $17.5 million, up from $12.9 million.

The Atlanta construction, manufacturing and real estate concern blamed the loss squarely on lower shipments of store fixtures. Mr. Abrams said that certain shipments the company had expected to make in the quarter were delayed and will be "moved back further" in the year.

Mr. Abrams stressed, though, that the construction and real estate businesses were profitable. "We expect all segments to be profitable in the second quarter {ending Oct. 31}," he said.

Mr. Abrams conceded that the biggest business -- construction -- has considerable exposure to increasing oil prices, but said he believes the company is better poised to handle the effects than it was in the mid-1970s. Contracts have shorter terms now, typically about six months, which allow Abrams to make more timely energy-cost estimates when preparing contracts.
