Deere's Net Rose 16% In 3rd Period on Sales Of Farm Equipment

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/22/90

MOLINE, Ill. -- Deere & Co., citing continued strong North American retail sales of John Deere agricultural equipment, said earnings for its fiscal third quarter rose 16% to $114.1 million, or $1.50 a share. In the same quarter the previous year, the maker of farm and industrial equipment had net income of $98.2 million, or $1.30 a share. Sales for the quarter, ended July 31, rose 6% to $2.06 billion from $1.93 billion.

For the nine months, the company earned $336.6 million, or $4.44 a share, compared with $277 million, or $3.69 a share, the previous year. Sales rose 11% to $5.82 billion from $5.22 billion.

Deere said the quarter and year-to-date improvements are attributable to increased sales, higher production and improved operating efficiency in its North American farm equipment operations.

The company said that despite the strong sales of its agricultural equipment, declining consumer spending for durable goods is resulting in lower retail sales of its lawn and grounds care products.

"This pattern will likely continue during the remainder of this year and into early 1991," Deere said. The company added that it now expects retail sales of lawn and grounds care products to be "significantly lower" in the fourth quarter.

Looking ahead, Deere said it is now "less certain" because of the effects of a weaker economy on consumer confidence and spending and construction activity.
