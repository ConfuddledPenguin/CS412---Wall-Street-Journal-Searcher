Integra Hires Firm To Manage Properties In Effort to Cut Costs

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/22/90

DALLAS -- Integra-A Hotel & Restaurant Co., severely strapped for cash, said it hired out the management of 24 properties in an attempt to cut costs and avoid a more drastic step, such as bankruptcy. By hiring Westbrooke Services Corp. to manage 24 of the 31 hotels owned and/or operated by Integra, the company said it will realize "a major reduction in overhead costs." David Steadman, chief executive officer, said that such a reduction was needed to help the company stay afloat.

"The company does have liquidity problems, and one of the first things we have to do is achieve some reduction in costs," said Mr. Steadman. With the Westbrooke agreement, "we have done that," he said. He declined to specify the cost reductions expected to result from the Westbrooke agreement.

"As to the future, there are a variety of ways of restructuring a company, and one is through a bankruptcy filing," he said. "We would prefer not to do that, but we would if we had to." Other possible solutions to Integra's liquidity problems include mergers, divestitures and joint ventures, the company said.

In the year ended Dec. 29, Integra posted a loss of $12.7 million, or $1.48 a share, on revenue of $91.3 million. In the first six months of this year, the company posted a net loss of $3.2 million, or 38 cents a share, on revenue of about $44 million.

Under their agreement, Westbrooke will provide management, accounting and consulting services at the 24 Integra properties, which are currently experiencing an "aggregate break-even cash flow." The agreement is structured so that Westbrooke's fees will rise with cash-flow improvements.
