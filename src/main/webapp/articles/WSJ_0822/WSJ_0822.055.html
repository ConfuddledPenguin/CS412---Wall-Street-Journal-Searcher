Japanese Firms Get China Job

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/22/90

TOKYO -- Mitsubishi Heavy Industries Ltd. and the trading house Toyo Menka Kaisha Ltd. said they received an order for a coal-handling facility valued at about 12 billion yen ($81.9 million) from National Technical Import & Export Corp. of China. The plant will be installed at Qingdao Port in Shantung Province by the end of 1993, company officials said. The order is part of a package of Japanese economic assistance to China, awarded in 1984. The new plant will have loading and unloading equipment capable of handling 15 million metric tons of coal annually, of which 10 million tons will be exported; a metric ton is equal to about 2,204.62 pounds.
