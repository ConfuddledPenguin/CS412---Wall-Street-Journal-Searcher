Letters to the Editor: Consider the Source Of Bias Charge

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/22/90

Your Aug. 6 editorial "Metzenbaum and Keating" declares, without qualification or amplification, "Employees of Senator Cranston's {voter registration} groups have since revealed that they were routinely pressured to register only Democrats." The allegation is completely false. In 1987 and 1988, I helped raise funds for a number of nonpartisan voter-registration groups in California and elsewhere. One of the California groups was the Center for Participation in Democracy. The center had 535 employees and scores of volunteers. Two of those employees, who worked out of an office in Oakland, were reported in the press in August 1989 as having made the allegations you cite. I am aware of no other such incidents.

The center was scrupulous in instructing its people to register everyone eligible, regardless of party preference, and to avoid influencing the choice of party. As the law permitted, the center concentrated much of its efforts in minority areas, where voter registration is low and where registrars have to "reach out" energetically if voter interest is to be stimulated.

The center's nonpartisan performance cannot be faulted. After the 1988 elections, a check with the secretary of state and the county clerks of the most populous counties showed that 67% of the voters processed by the center in those minority areas registered Democratic; 33% registered Republican, with other parties, or they declined to state.

That's hardly evidence of bias, and it compares favorably with two other nonpartisan groups that also conducted registration drives in minority communities. One of those groups had 61% Democratic registrants; the other, 87%.

Bias can more legitimately be charged to the two disgruntled employees who made the false accusations. The press identified them as Lawrence Ross and Dino Blackburn. Both had quit the center shortly before they were due to be fired; Mr. Blackburn for, among other things, refusing to register minority voters, and Mr. Ross for filing fraudulent voter-registration affidavits.

Sen. Alan Cranston (D., Calif.)

Washington
