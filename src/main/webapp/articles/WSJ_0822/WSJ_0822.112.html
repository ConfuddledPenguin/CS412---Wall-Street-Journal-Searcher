CD Yields Are Flat On Small Units, Rise On Larger Deposits

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/22/90

NEW YORK -- Yields were unchanged on small-denomination certificates of deposit sold directly by major banks, but continued to rise on large deposits and broker-sold CDs. On a small-denomination one-year CD, for instance, the average yield remained at 7.52% in the week ended yesterday, according to Banxquote Money Markets, an information service based here. The average yield on small six-month CDs slipped to 7.37% from 7.38%, Banxquote said. Oneyear and six-month CDs are considered the most popular among individual investors.

Yields on some CDs sold by major brokerage firms, however, increased about a tenth of a percentage point, as did yields on large-denomination, "jumbo" CDs sold directly by banks. The average yield on a one-year broker-sold CD rose to 7.99% from 7.89%. On a one-year jumbo CD, generally more than $90,000, the average yield increased to 7.73% from 7.63%.

Broker-sold CDs and jumbos tend to respond quickly to interest-rate changes because of competition for institutional money. The crisis in the Middle East, along with a weaker dollar and higher interest rates abroad, are pushing up U.S. rates, said Norberto Mehl, chairman of Banxquote.

Yields on Treasury bills sold at Monday's auction rose to 7.80% from 7.66% for three-month bills, and to 7.85% from 7.70% for six-month bills.

A few of the 18 banks surveyed by Banxquote offer yields higher than 8% on small deposits for two and five-year maturities. The highest average yield in the latest Banxquote survey was 8.53% on five-year CDs sold by broker-dealers.
