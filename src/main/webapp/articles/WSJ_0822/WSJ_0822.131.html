Business Brief -- Aura Systems Inc.: Firm to Produce Actuators For Big U.S. Auto Maker

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/22/90

Aura Systems Inc. said it has entered into a contract valued at roughly $34 million with one of Detroit's Big Three to provide electromagnetic actuators for automobiles. An actuator is a device that takes an electrical input and then generates a force that can move a part. Aura Systems declined to say precisely what its actuators will be used for, but said the components will be incorporated into six-cylinder cars.

"They will have a special application at first, but then they will become standard at a later point," Harry Kurtzman, Aura's president, said in an interview. "The details should all come out in the next few months."

Mr. Kurtzman said that the auto maker asked not to be named at this time.

Pre-production development and testing is scheduled to begin immediately, Aura Systems said, with initial production of about 1.7 million units scheduled to start in summer 1991. That batch of units, priced at about $20 apiece, will take about a year to build.
