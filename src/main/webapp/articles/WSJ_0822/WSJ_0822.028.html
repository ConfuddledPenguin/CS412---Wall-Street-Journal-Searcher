Philadelphia Note Issue Backing Still Uncertain

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/22/90

NEW YORK -- No decision has been reached on whether a planned Philadelphia note issue totaling about $400 million will be backed by a letter of credit. "It's probably more likely it will be several" banks each backing a portion of the issue, said Sheila Connolly, vice president of P.G. Corbin & Co., the city's financial adviser.

Philadelphia has solicited a number of banks to provide credit enhancement for the deal, Ms. Connolly said. A decision is expected within the next day or so, she added, but declined to say which banks are involved.

An official with the lead underwriter, Merrill Lynch Capital Markets, said a decision was expected today.

At least one bank appears interested in backing at least 25% of the issue, according to a Philadelphia official who asked to remain anonymous.

The notes, which would mature in May 1991, are expected to be sold within the next few weeks. Proceeds will be used to help the city buy time to solve its fiscal problems.

Philadelphia officials declined to comment on the sale.
