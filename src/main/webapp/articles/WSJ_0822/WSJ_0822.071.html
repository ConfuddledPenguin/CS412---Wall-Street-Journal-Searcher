Businessland Charged In Suits With Violating U.S. Securities Laws

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/22/90

SAN JOSE, Calif. -- Businessland Inc. said investors have filed two class action lawsuits against the company, alleging violations of federal securities laws. The company said the suits, filed in federal court here, were filed in connection with the announcement two weeks ago of Businessland's fiscal 1990 loss of $21.5 million, or 73 cents a share. News of the loss hammered Businessland stock, which plunged 58% to $2.875 the day it was reported. In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Businessland shares closed at $2.75, down 37.5 cents.

Businessland didn't elaborate on the complaints, but it would appear that the charges are related to disclosure requirements in the period leading up to the announcement of the company's earnings. The computer retailer said the lawsuits were "completely without merit" and said it would contest them vigorously.
