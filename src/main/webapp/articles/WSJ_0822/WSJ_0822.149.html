What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/22/90

STOCK PRICES PLUNGED in a broad sell-off that began in European markets. The Dow Jones industrials fell 52.48 points to 2603.96 in hectic trading. Bonds closed slightly lower after falling sharply early in the day. The dollar rose against the yen but fell against the mark. ---

European stock prices sank on rumors of Mideast fighting. Germany's blue-chip DAX Index plunged 5.2%. London's FT-SE 100 retreated 2.3%.

---

Gasoline and oil prices swung wildly, ending mostly higher. Separately, the Energy Department doesn't expect to tap the Strategic Petroleum Reserve until next month at the earliest, barring more fighting.

Salomon is benefiting from soaring oil prices, through the Wall Street firm's Phibro Energy unit.

---

U.S. car makers shaved more than 53,000 vehicles from planned third-quarter output, apparently due to uncertainty related to the Mideast. More reductions could come this week.

---

MCI is speeding up the conversion of its network to digital technology, which will result in marketing advantages, lower operating costs and a likely write-down of old equipment that could total $550 million.

---

National Semiconductor intends to eliminate several product groups, lay off 2,000, or 6.2%, of its employees, and incur a $140 million charge.

---

Wang won a federal computer contract valued at up to $841 million over five years. The deal is a big boost for the firm, which has been struggling with huge losses and declining sales.

---

Donald Trump won approval from New Jersey casino authorities for his $65 million bank bailout.

---

Manville asbestos plaintiffs' attorneys fees were capped at 20% of claims by a federal judge.

---

USAir is firing about 1,500, or 3%, of its workers, and indicated more cutbacks are likely. Separately, the carrier lost a bitter union battle.

---

The U.S. budget gap was $25.93 billion in July, over $7 billion more than in July 1989, the government said. Higher spending was blamed.

---

Short interest for the month ended Aug. 15 rose 4.1% to a record volume on the Big Board, but fell slightly on the American Stock Exchange.

---

American International Group's falling stock price may force the insurer to sweeten its bid for International Lease Finance in order to salvage the $1.3 billion transaction.

---

Stotler settled charges with commodities regulators, but the closed futures brokerage firm still faces a hearing before securities officials.

---

IBM introduced three low-end minicomputers that could compete with personal computers and workstations.

---

PepsiCo expects to post a $50 million third-quarter gain from the sale of Kentucky Fried Chicken stock in Japan, which will more than offset $45 million in charges in the period.

---

NVR Limited Partnership posted a $53.3 million second-quarter loss, due mainly to a restructuring. The homebuilder and developer said it will omit a quarterly payout to unit holders.

---
