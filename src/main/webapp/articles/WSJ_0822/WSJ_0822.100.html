TWA Plans to Increase Domestic Fares Aug. 30

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/22/90

MT. KISCO, N.Y. -- Trans World Airlines Inc. said it plans to raise domestic fares Aug. 30 because of rising jet fuel prices, likely prompting other carriers to delay their fare increases until then. TWA said it will raise fares 5.3%, the same amount that other airlines had announced last week. Those increases are scheduled to take effect tomorrow but will now probably be postponed until Aug. 30.

Carriers, which rarely raise fares without the participation of their competitors, give advance notice of the increases to give others time to match them.

TWA said the cost of jet fuel, one of the industry's biggest expenses, has risen significantly since Iraq invaded Kuwait early this month.

The industry-wide fare increases will affect all tickets for an indefinite time. Most airlines are still offering discounts on heavily restricted fares that require at least one-week advance purchase. The discounts, which range from 15% to 40% on regular prices, also require that tickets be purchased by Aug. 29, the day before TWA plans to raise its fares.
