Business Brief -- Ford Motor Co.: New Escort Model Offered For the European Market

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/22/90

Ford Motor Co., fighting to retain its profitable share of the European car market, is introducing a new model of its strong-selling Escort today. Ford's European subsidiary, U.K.-based Ford of Europe Inc., said it spent almost $2 billion over the past five years developing the new Escort, the most it has ever invested in a new European car. Company officials project annual sales of 650,000 a year.

"Escort is our largest program ever in Europe," says a Ford spokesman. "We believe it puts us in a strong position to face increased competition."

In recent years, Ford and rival U.S. car maker General Motors Corp. have counted on record profits from the booming European car market to cushion their difficulties at home. European operations accounted for almost 40% of Ford's world-wide net income on cars last year, and for more than 60% of GM's. Despite the debut of the new models in late April, Escort's U.S. sales this year are off about 22%.

The European Escort is targeted at the "small family" segment of the market, the largest and most fiercely competitive sector of Europe's car market, accounting for almost one in three of all new cars sold, Ford says.

Although the new Escort was redesigned to add interior space, its boxy styling resembles the older model."It's evolutionary, not revolutionary," says James Longhurst, an auto analyst at James Capel & Co.
