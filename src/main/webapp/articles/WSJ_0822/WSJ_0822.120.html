Business Brief -- First State Financial Services Inc.: Investor Proposes Ending Or Easing Firm's Defenses

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/22/90

David N. Bolger, a Ridgewood, N.J., investor, submitted to First State Financial Services Inc. a proposal to ask shareholders to eliminate or ease certain of the West Caldwell, N.J., company's anti-takeover provisions. Mr. Bolger, who is part of a group holding 9.9% of the savings and loan holding company, said the provisions artificially reduce its stock price and insulate management from market forces. Noting that company insiders control almost 20% of the stock, Mr. Bolger said he wants to reduce the shareholder vote required for a takeover to a simple majority from 80%. First State officials declined to comment on Mr. Bolger's proposal.

In early August, Mr. Bolger said he wanted the board to take action against officers responsible for what he asserted was corporate mismanagement, waste of assets, misconduct and breaches of fiduciary duty.
