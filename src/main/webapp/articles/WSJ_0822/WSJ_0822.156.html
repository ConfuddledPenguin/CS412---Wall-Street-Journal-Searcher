Who's News: Stardent Shareholders Oust Ex-Chairmen Following Lawsuit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/22/90

NEWTON, Mass. -- Stardent Computer Inc. said that its shareholders removed its former co-chairmen from the board and affirmed a closer relationship with Kubota Corp., Stardent's largest investor. Allen H. Michels and Matthew H. Sanders III, who had co-founded one of Stardent's two predecessor companies, sued Kubota last month alleging that the Japanese tractor maker was conspiring to steal Stardent's technology for making graphics supercomputers. Kubota and Stardent have both denied the allegation.

Stardent's board had already stripped the two men of their co-chairmen titles in the wake of the suit. However, they remained directors under company bylaws.

"We see this as a direct consequence of actions proposed by the Japanese-dominated board," said Mr. Michels. "Mr. Sanders and I are undaunted by such blatant interference and will continue to press our case vigorously in the courts."

Meanwhile, lawyers for the pair filed yesterday an amendment to their original suit, adding Stardent Computer and Stardent President John Poduska to the list of defendants. The amendment also adds the charge that Kubota and the other defendants unlawfully discharged Mr. Michels and Mr. Sanders.

Stardent, which is owned by employees and venture capital investors, said that Kubota will invest as much as $60 million in Stardent over the next 12 months raising its stake to 28% from 22%. Kubota has already invested about $100 million in Stardent and one of its predecessors.

Meanwhile, Kubota will take over development of Stardent's current product line while Stardent will concentrate on new product development. Kubota remains Stardent's exclusive manufacturer and retains all Asian distribution rights.
