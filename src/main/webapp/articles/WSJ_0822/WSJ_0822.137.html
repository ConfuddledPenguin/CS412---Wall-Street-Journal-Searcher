Business Brief -- General Electric Co.: Engine Unit Gets 2 Orders With Value of $130 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/22/90

GE Aircraft Engines, a unit of General Electric Co., said it received two orders for its CF6-80C2 turbofan engine with a potential value of $130 million. The Evendale, Ohio, company said Olympic Airways of Greece selected the engine for four Airbus Industrie A300-600R airplanes, two of which are firm orders and two of which are options. The engine order is valued at $85 million, the company said.

In addition, LACSA, a Costa Rican airline, selected the engine for two Airbus Industrie A310-300 airplanes, the company said. The engine order is valued at $45 million.

General Electric, based in Fairfield, Conn., has businesses in broadcasting, lighting, industrial products, plastics, financial services and aircraft engines.
