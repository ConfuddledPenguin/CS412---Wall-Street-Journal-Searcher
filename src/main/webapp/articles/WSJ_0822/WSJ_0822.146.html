Who's News: Hawaiian Electric Says Pratt to Retire From Parent, Unit

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 08/22/90

C. Dudley Pratt Jr., president and chief executive of Hawaiian Electric Industries Inc. and chief executive of its Hawaiian Electric Co. subsidiary, said he will retire from those posts at the end of the year. Mr. Pratt, 62 years old, advanced his plans by one year after he was nominated to become one of four trustees of an estate that is the second-largest landowner in the Hawaiian Islands.

Mr. Pratt will remain a director of the Honolulu-based holding company, which was formed in 1981.

Robert F. Clarke, group vice president-diversified companies, has been named to succeed Mr. Pratt as president and chief executive of the holding company. Mr. Clarke, 48, joined the company in 1987 as vice president of strategic planning and helped to implement the company's successful diversification strategy.

Edward J. Blackburn, currently president and chief executive of Hawaiian Insurance & Guarantee Co., a Hawaiian Electric Industries unit, will succeed Mr. Clarke as group vice president-diversified companies.

Thomas S. Adams, senior vice president of Hawaiian Insurance & Guarantee, will succeed Mr. Blackburn as the insurance company's president and chief executive. Harwood D. Williamson, group vice president-utility companies, who is also president of Hawaiian Electric Co., assumes the additional post of chief executive of the utility company from Mr. Pratt.

Hawaiian Electric Industries provides electric service to 96% of the residents of the Hawaiian Islands and also has interests in financial services, maritime transportation and real estate development.
