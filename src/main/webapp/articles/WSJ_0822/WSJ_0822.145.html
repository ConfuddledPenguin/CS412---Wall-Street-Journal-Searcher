Dividend News: American First Corp. To Cede Tricon Unit To Preferred Holders

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/22/90

OKLAHOMA CITY -- American First Corp., unable to pay dividends owed to holders of preferred stock, said it is giving them something else instead -- ownership of the company's largest subsidiary, Tricon America Corp. The holding company wouldn't specify a value for Tricon, except to say it is "substantially less" than $10 million. It said Tricon, a concrete building and fiber glass vault maker, accounted for virtually all of the parent's revenue.

After American First missed a dividend payment totaling $25,000, holders of its $10 convertible preferred stock demanded full redemption of the $1 million issue, the company said, adding that it was unable to comply. At that point, provisions of the preferred issue called for transfer of all the common stock of Tricon America to the preferred holders. American First said most of the preferred is held by a limited partnership, ML Oklahoma Venture Partners.

In the nine months ended June 30, American First's loss widened to $2.9 million, or 56 cents a share, from $1.4 million, or 26 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue for the latest nine months was $7.2 million; the company didn't have any year-earlier revenue, because it didn't yet own Tricon.

American First President John W. Cox said, "We did not have the available resources to continue to fund Tricon's operations and were unsuccessful in our attempt to raise capital from outside sources."

The company also has minor interests in real estate and in computer leasing.
