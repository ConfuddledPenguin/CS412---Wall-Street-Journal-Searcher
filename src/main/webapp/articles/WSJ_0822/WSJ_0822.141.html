International Brief -- Pakhoed Holding N.V.: Merger Talks Being Held With Another Dutch Firm

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/22/90

Pakhoed Holding N.V., a Dutch transport and tank storage concern, said it is holding merger talks with Furness N.V., a Dutch shipping company. Dutch analysts said that if the merger proceeds, as expected, this would complete a long-term consolidation with four Dutch companies effectively running stevedoring services in Rotterdam, Europe's largest port: Pakhoed, Van Ommeren Ceteco N.V., Koninklijke Nedlloyd Groep N.V. and Internatio-Muller N.V.

Under the terms being discussed, Pakhoed would offer one share or certificate of share in Pakhoed plus 130 guilders for two shares or certificates of shares in Furness, which analysts valued at 140 million guilders ($79.4 million) to 160 million guilders.

Separately, Pakhoed posted a 64% increase in first-half profit to 69.2 million guilders, including a gain of 8.7 million guilders from an asset sale, from 42.2 million guilders a year earlier, when Pakhoed had a net loss from extraordinary items of 1.2 million guilders. Excluding extraordinary items, Pakhoed's profit from ordinary operations rose 39% to 60.5 million guilders from 43.4 million guilders.
