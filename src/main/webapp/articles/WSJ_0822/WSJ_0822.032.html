Chip Firm Plans Big Cutbacks, Reorganization --- National Semiconductor Set To Scrap Some Products, Lay Off 6.2% of Workers

  • Author: Stephen Kreider Yoder
  • Date: 08/22/90

National Semiconductor Corp., in an effort to reverse years of poor performance, said it will eliminate several product groups, lay off 6.2% of its employees and incur a $140 million charge. As part of the cutbacks, National will close two assembly lines and reorganize its product groups. National's move is an attempt to reduce its dependence on so-called "commodity" chips -- standard semiconductors that have come under competitive pressure from other companies that make them -- and to focus on developing more specialized chips that offer higher profit margins.

Commodity-chip makers such as National and Advanced Micro Devices Inc. have been battered by low-priced chips from Japanese and other makers, while companies such as Intel Corp. enjoy fat profits from more specialized chips.

"National Semi is the stereotypical commodity company" that hasn't adapted to changes in the chip market, said analyst Drew Peck of Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette. "They're now making concessions they probably should have made five years ago."

The Santa Clara, Calif., company said it will stop making high-density versions of chips called static random-access memories, or SRAMs. National will shut down a brand-new line in its Puyallup, Wash., factory that it set up just this year to make SRAM chips that can hold more than a million pieces, or one megabit, of computer information.

Over the next 18 months, National said, it also will shut down a Tucson, Ariz., plant that assembles chips for the military, which are suffering from lower demand because of Pentagon budget cutbacks. And the company will slow production of chips called CMOS gate arrays. The high-density SRAM market hasn't developed as well as expected and profit margins in the gate arrays are too low, said Charles E. Sporck, president and chief executive officer.

National also said it will reorganize its six product divisions into four. As part of the closures and reorganization, National said it will fire about 2,000 employees world-wide over the next few months and will incur a one-time charge of about $140 million in the quarter ending Aug. 26.

The cutbacks come after a year of trimming and a string of disastrous quarters. The company laid off 2,000 employees in January 1989 and later closed plants in South Korea and Singapore, taking charges of about $54 million in the year for the cutbacks.

The company had a net loss in five of its past eight quarters. Although it eked out net income of $4.7 million in its last quarter, the chip maker is expected to incur a large net loss in its current quarter because of the one-time charge. And National expects operating profit for the quarter to be below analysts' expectations. Analysts had expected the company to break even on operating profit.

National's stock, which has fallen about 38% since early June, closed at $5 a share, up 12.5 cents, in composite trading yesterday on the New York Stock Exchange.
