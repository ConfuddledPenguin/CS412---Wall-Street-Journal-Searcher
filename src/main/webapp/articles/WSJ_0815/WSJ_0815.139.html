Business Brief -- Interco Inc.: Firm Makes Debt Payment On Its Medium-Term Notes

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/15/90

Interco Inc. made an $8.6 million interest payment on its medium-term notes, indicating the company is confident its banks and bondholders will agree to restructure its $1.9 billion in debt. The payment was the first sign of progress since the troubled furniture and shoe company announced two weeks ago that it had reached a "basic agreement" on a debt restructuring plan. "They certainly wouldn't have made that payment if they didn't think they had a deal," said Wilbur Ross, a Rothschild Inc. managing director who is advising holders of junk bonds issued in Interco's disastrous 1988 recapitalization.

If St. Louis-based Interco hadn't made the payment, medium-term noteholders could have pushed the company into bankruptcy law proceedings as early as today, when the grace period was set to expire. The medium-term notes predate the 1988 recapitalization.
