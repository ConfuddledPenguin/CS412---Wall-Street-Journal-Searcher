New Jersey Moves To Block ITT Units On Insurance Sales

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/15/90

New Jersey moved to block ITT Corp. from selling any insurance in the state if an ITT unit continues efforts to withdraw from the auto insurance market. Insurance Commissioner Samuel Fortunato directed all ITT units to surrender their certificates to sell insurance if the one unit selling auto insurance, Twin City Fire Insurance Co., goes ahead with plans to stop selling its lines.

The commissioner's order is the latest development in a dispute over the state's new auto-insurance law. Proponents of the law, which was enacted this year, say it is meant to reduce rates and relieve the financial troubles of a fund that had been established to insure high-risk drivers.

But an ITT spokeswoman said Twin City has projected "severe losses" under the law. The company has challenged the law in court, so far without success.

A spokesman for the state insurance department said it was moving against ITT because Twin City was further along than other insurers in trying to pull out of auto insurance in the state. He said several others have made "overtures" about pulling out, while still other insurers have approached the department about starting new auto insurance lines in New Jersey.

Gov. Jim Florio said in a statement that the insurance commissioner was acting under a "long arm" provision that empowers the state to act against companies who balk at the law. "If they won't sell us car insurance, we won't let them sell us anything," he said.

ITT, based in New York, didn't have any immediate comment on the order. But the spokeswoman said it would affect 10 of the company's certified insurance subsidiaries doing business in New Jersey.

She said the aggregate premium volume of those subsidiaries in the state last year was $224.6 million. Of that, she said, $72.6 million came from Twin City, though that figure includes homeowners' lines as well as auto insurance.

The governor's office said Twin City insures about 23,000 cars, or 1% of the New Jersey market.

All the ITT units affected by the order are part of the company's ITT Hartford Insurance Group subsidiary. The commissioner's order directs ITT Hartford to cease operations in the state over five years at a gradual pace to protect policyholders. The order doesn't apply to life and health policies already in effect that can only be canceled at the option of the insured.
