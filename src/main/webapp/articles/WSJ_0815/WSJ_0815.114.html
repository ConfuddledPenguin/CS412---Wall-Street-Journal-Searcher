Western Union Seeks Shareholder Approval For Recapitalization

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/15/90

NEW YORK -- Western Union Corp. adjourned its annual meeting for the third time since June as it struggled to win approval of its recapitalization plan from shareholders. Western Union plans to reconvene the annual meeting today at noon at the Ramada Inn in Montvale, N.J.

The company has said it will have to seek bankruptcy-law protection if it cannot win approval of the plan to reclassify its preferred stock into common shares and exchange new debt securities and common stock for its present senior notes.

Last month, a representative for the company's bondholders said that most would be willing to go along with the exchange offer.

However, the company must first get stockholder approval of the reclassification. A spokesman said the company is close to getting the two-thirds majority vote necessary to approve the reclassification.

Meanwhile, Western Union's negotiations over a new three-year contract with the Communications Workers of America grew intense. The CWA filed a complaint with the National Labor Relations Board, charging Western Union with unfair labor practices after it locked out 2,500 unionized workers over the weekend and replaced them with nonunion workers.

The workers, including employees in the company's central telephone bureaus, computer centers and other facilities had been working without a contract since midnight Aug. 6 when the old contract expired. However, Western Union said it couldn't operate "under the ever-present threat" of a strike as it tried to work out a new contract.
