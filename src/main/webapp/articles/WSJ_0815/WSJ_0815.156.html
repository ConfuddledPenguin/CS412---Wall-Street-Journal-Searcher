Turner Broadcasting Posts Quarterly Loss On Goodwill Games

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/15/90

ATLANTA -- Turner Broadcasting System Inc. reported a second-quarter loss of $8.3 million, or 13 cents a share, due to a $31 million charge to cover losses from the Goodwill Games. Last year, Turner Broadcasting had net income in the second quarter of $5.5 million, and a loss of four cents a share after paying preferred stock dividends.

The large hit from the Goodwill Games, which brings Turner Broadcasting's total loss from that event to $44 million, casts doubt on the prospects of Turner Broadcasting sponsoring a third self-styled Olympiad in 1994. Though its chairman and chief executive officer, Ted Turner, said 11 days ago that he wants to back a third games in Leningrad, the company's board, controlled by the nation's largest cable operators, may veto the idea on financial grounds. The company had set a goal of reducing the $26 million loss that it suffered from the first Goodwill Games in Moscow in 1986.

Despite the problems from the Goodwill Games, Turner Broadcasting reported a 22% surge in revenue in the second quarter from a year earlier, to $322.8 million from $264.5 million. The company attributed the revenue growth to a 24% rise in revenue from its entertainment segment -- thanks mostly to advertising and subscription gains from its Turner Network Television network. Operating profit from entertainment fell sharply because of the Goodwill Games loss.

From news, Turner Broadcasting's revenue climbed 13%, while operating profit was flat.

For the six months, Turner Broadcasting reported a loss of $4 million, or 19 cents a share, compared with net income of $5.1 million, and a loss of 12 cents a share after paying preferred dividends, a year earlier. Revenue during the half rose 24% to $613.6 million from $496.5 million.
