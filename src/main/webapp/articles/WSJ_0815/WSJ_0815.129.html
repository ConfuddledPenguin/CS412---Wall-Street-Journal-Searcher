Business Brief -- Stage II Apparel Corp.: Firm Reports $750,000 Loss; Citing Ames' Bankruptcy

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/15/90

Stage II Apparel Corp., a distributor of low-priced casual clothing for men and boys, reported a second-quarter loss on a plunge in sales, which the company blamed on the bankruptcy court filing of Ames Department Stores Inc., a major customer. New York-based Stage II reported a $750,000 loss, compared with earnings of $379,000 in the second quarter of last year. Sales dropped 24% to $15.4 million from $20.2 million in the second quarter of 1989.

Thomas Hammill, the company's controller, declined to specify how much of the sales decline resulted from the halting of shipments to Ames in the wake of its Chapter 11 filing. He added, however, that Stage II had resumed shipping to Ames as part of the retailer's reorganization plan.

"The financial results for the rest of this year will be no worse than those of this quarter, and I am confident that the company will return to profitability in the near future," said Stage II Chairman Jack Clark in a prepared statement.
