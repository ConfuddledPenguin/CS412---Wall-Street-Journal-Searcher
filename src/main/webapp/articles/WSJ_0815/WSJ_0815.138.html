Business Brief -- Southwest Gas Corp.: Net Loss of $6.1 Million Reported for 2nd Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/15/90

Southwest Gas Corp. posted a second-quarter net loss of $6.1 million, or 33 cents a share, compared with net income of $388,000, or less that one cent a share, a year ago. Revenue fell 6.4% to $171.7 million from $183.4 million.

The Las Vegas, Nev.-based natural gas and financial services concern attributed the net loss to increased costs associated with providing gas service to the company's growing customer base; increased depreciation expense and general taxes relating to natural gas pipe replacement programs in Arizona; and costs for expanding and upgrading facilities.

Gas operations had a loss of $8.5 million for the quarter, compared with a loss of $3.1 million a year ago. Operating earnings from the company's financial services subsidiary, PriMerit Bank, fell to $3.4 million in the quarter from $4.5 million a year ago, beacuse of a drop in real estate development income and increased general and administrative expenses due to the expansion of PriMerit's branch network in Nevada and Arizona. This was offset by improved net interest margins, the company said.

For the six months, net income fell 33% to $15.3 million, or 74 cents a share, from $22.9 million, or $1.16 a share. Revenue rose less than one percent to $445.3 million from $441.7 million.
