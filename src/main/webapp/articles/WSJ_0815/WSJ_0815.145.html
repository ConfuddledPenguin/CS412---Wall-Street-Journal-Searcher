PALO ALTO, Calif. -- CF Motor Freight, a unit of Consolidated Freightways Inc., said it asked the Interstate Commerce Commission to approve a fuel surcharge to offset the sudden oil price increases touched off by the Middle East oil crisis. If the federal agency approves the surcharge, it would be effective Friday.

In a letter to its customers, CF Motor Freight said that, if approved, the surcharge would initially add 1% to the cost of shipments of less than a truckload, and 3.47% to full-truckload shipments. They would change weekly, however, based on the fluctuating cost of fuel.

The surcharge is described as a straight pass-through of increased costs, and it would be suspended when costs return to normal, Consolidated Freightways said.

Consolidated Freightways is a trucking and air freight company.
