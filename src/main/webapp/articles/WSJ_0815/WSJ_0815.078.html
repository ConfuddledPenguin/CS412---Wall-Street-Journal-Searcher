Continental Air Holders Sue to Stop Buy-Out of Lorenzo

  • Author: Bridget O'Brian
  • Date: 08/15/90

Several Continental Airlines Holdings Inc. shareholders sued to stop the proposed purchase of Frank Lorenzo's stake in the airline company by Scandinavian Airlines System. In addition to Mr. Lorenzo, who stepped down as chairman and chief executive officer of Continental Holdings, the plaintiffs also sued that company, its directors and Jet Capital Corp., the tightly controlled holding company that owns a special class of Continental Holdings stock. SAS was not named in the suits.

The suits -- three of them filed in Delaware Chancery Court and a fourth filed in U.S. District Court in New Jersey -- allege that Continental directors breached their fiduciary duty by agreeing to a buy-out in which Mr. Lorenzo and Jet Capital's other shareholders receive $14 to $18 apiece for their shares. In American Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Continental Holdings shares closed at $5.25, down 87.5 cents.

Mr. Lorenzo agreed last week to sell all his stock in Continental Holdings to SAS and to vacate his management posts at both the holding company and its Continental Airlines unit, although he will remain a director. Mr. Lorenzo would receive more than $30 million, including $10.8 million for his shares in Jet Capital, and SAS would buy most of his stock options, also at a premium. The transaction would boost SAS's equity stake to 16.8% from 9.8%.

The Delaware suits charge that Mr. Lorenzo would receive amounts vastly exceeding the value of his stock options and his employment contract. Similarly, the New Jersey suit claims SAS is paying Mr. Lorenzo four times the value of his equity. The New Jersey suits ask for unspecified damages and all the suits seek to stop the transaction.

A Continental Holdings spokesman said the company believes the transaction to be proper, and that it was scrutinized by attorneys and financial advisers for all the parties involved. "We are confident the directors will be absolved of any wrongdoing," he said.
