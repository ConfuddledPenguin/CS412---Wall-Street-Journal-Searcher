New Securities Issues

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/15/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report. CORPORATES

Willamette Industries Inc. -- $150 million of notes priced at par to yield 9.625%. The non-callable notes were priced at a spread of 97 basis points above the Treasury's 10-year note. Rated single-A-3 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and single-A-plus by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Salomon Brothers Inc.

Beverly Enterprises Inc. -- $40 million of 9% convertible subordinated debentures due Aug. 15, 2000. Each debenture is convertible into common stock at an initial conversion price of $6.45 a share. The offering was managed by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets and Stephens Inc.

MUNICIPALS

California -- $4.1 billion revenue anticipation notes, due June 28, 1991, tentatively priced by a Bank of America NT&SA group to yield 5.80%. The notes, rated MIG-1/SP1Plus, were priced as 6 1/4% securities to yield 5.80%.

New York State Dormitory Authority -- $230 million State University of New York state university education facilities revenue bonds, due May 15, 1992-2000, 2010, 2018 and 2020, tentatively priced by a Prudential-Bache Capital Funding group to yield from 6.25% in 1992 to 7.55%% in 2018. Current interest serial bonds are priced to yield from 6.25% in 1992 to 7.15% in 2000. There are $39.04 million 7 3/8% term bonds priced at 99.21 to yield 7.45% in 2010. There are $98.66 million 7% term bonds priced at 93.63 to yield 7.55% in 2018. There are $34.06 million 6 1/8% term bonds priced at 84.22 to yield 7.45% in 2020. The bonds are rated single-A by Moody's and single-A-minus by S&P.

Massachusetts Housing Finance Authority -- $150.9 million single-family mortgage revenue bonds, Series 13, due June 1, and Dec. 1, 2014 and 2023, Series 14, due 2014 and Series 15, due 1991-2005, priced by a PaineWebber Inc. group to yield from 6.10% in 1991 to 7.95% in 2023. In Series 13 there are $24 million 7.65% term bonds priced at par, due Dec. 1, 2014, and $66 million 7.95% term bonds priced at par, due June 1, 2023. In Series 14 there are $31 million 7.70% term bonds priced at par due Dec. 1, 2014. Series 15 current interest serial bonds are priced at par to yield from 6.10% in 1991 to 7.50% in 2004 and 2005. Interest on Series 13 and 15 bonds will be treated as a preference item in calculating the Federal alternative minimum tax that may be imposed on certain investors. The bonds are rated double-A by Moody's and single-A-plus by S&P.

Ohio -- $115 million higher education capital facilites general obligation bonds, Series II-1990 A, due May 1, 1991-2005, apparently via a Bear Stearns & Co. group with a bid setting the net interest cost at 6.863%. The bonds, MBIA-insured and rated triple-A by both Moody's and S&P,e priced to yield from 5.85% in 1991 to 6.90% in 2003 and 2004. Bonds due in 2005 are not formally reoffered.

Alabama Housing Finance Authority -- $100 million single-family mortgage revenue bonds, Series C-1, due Oct 1, and April 1, 2010 and 2016 and Series C-2, due Oct. 1, 1992, 2002, and 2021 and 2022, tentatively priced by a Goldman Sachs & Co. group to yield from 6.25% in 1992 to 7.80% in 2022. Current interest serial bonds are priced at par to yield from 6.25% in 1992 to 7.25% in 2002. There are $15.97 million 7.45% term bonds priced at par due Oct. 1, 2010. There are $17.76 million 7.55% term bonds priced at par due April 1, 2016. There are $24 million 7.40% term bonds priced at par due Oct. 1, 2021 and $31 million 7.80% term bonds priced at par due April 1, 2022. The bonds are rated triple-A by both Moody's and S&P. Interest on the Series C-2 bonds will be treated as a preference item in calculating the Federal alternative minimum tax that may be imposed on certain investors. Interest on the Series C-1 bonds due in 2010 and 2016 isn't subject to the alternative minimum tax.

Michigan State Housing Development Authority -- $59.5 million single-family mortgage revenue bonds, 1990 Series C, due June 1, and Dec. 1, 2003-2005, and 2015, with capital appreciation bonds due 2006-2010, and Series D, due June 1, and Dec. 1, 1991-2002, and 2019, tentatively priced by a Lehman Brothers group to yield from 6.10% in 1991 to 7.80% in 2019. Series C current interest serial bonds are priced at par to yield from 7.20% in 2003 to 7.35% in 2005. There are $14.16 million 7.55% term bonds priced at par due Dec. 1, 2015. Capital appreciation bonds are priced to yield to maturity from 7.50% in 2006 to 7.60% in 2009 and 2010. Series D current interest serial bonds are priced at par to yield from 6.10%% in 1991 to 7.30% in 2002. There are $18.61 million 7.80% term bonds priced at par in 2019. A rating of double-A from Standard & Poor's Corp. was expected, according to the lead underwriter. Interest on the Series D bonds will be treated as a preference item in calculating the Federal alternative minimum tax that may be imposed on certain investors.

Texas City Industrial Development Corp. -- $53.3 million marine terminal refunding revenue bonds, Series 1990, Arco Pipe Line Company Project, due Oct. 1, 2020, tentatively priced by a First Boston Corp. group to yield 7.38%. The bonds, rated single-A by Moody's and single-A-plus by S&P, are priced as 7 3/8% securities at par.

MORTGAGES

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $1.05 billion issue of Remic mortgage securities, offered in 17 classes by First Boston Corp. The offering, Series 1990-108, is backed by Fannie Mae 9% securities with a weighted average coupon rate of 9.75% and weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29.5 years.

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $200 million issue of 8.70% notes, due Aug. 21, 1995, priced at par to yield 8.70%. The notes, which are non-callable for one year, were priced Monday at a spread of 37.5 basis points above the Treasury's five-year note through underwriters at Goldman Sachs & Co.

Federal Farm Credit Banks -- $150 million of 8.71% notes, priced initially at par. The notes, which are non-callable for one year, were priced Monday at a spread of 37.5 basis points above the Treasury's five-year note.

EUROBONDS

Tamura Corp. (Japan) -- 93 million Swiss francs of privately placed convertible notes due Sept. 30, 1995 with a fixed 3 7/8% coupon at par, via Swiss Bank Corp. Put option March 31, 1993 at a fixed 109 3/4 to yield 7.39%. Each 50,000 Swiss franc note convertible from Oct. 1, 1990 to Sept. 20, 1995 at a 2 1/2%premium over the closing share price Aug. 20, when terms are fixed.
