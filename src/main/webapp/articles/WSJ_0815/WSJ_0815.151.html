Mortgage Funds Say They Took Control Of 19 Lots From VMS

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/15/90

CHICAGO -- Eight publicly traded funds originally sponsored by VMS Realty Partners stepped up their efforts to wrest control of real estate properties from the troubled partnership syndicator. Three of the mortgage funds said that they and the First National Bank of Chicago had taken possession of 19 Los Angeles area office buildings from VMS. The funds said that as of June 30 they were owed $154 million on the properties and that First Chicago, the senior lender, was owed $255.4 million.

The buildings, known collectively as the Buckeye Portfolio, include addresses in Beverly Hills, Calif., Hollywood and the San Fernando Valley, including several on Wilshire Boulevard.

The funds -- VMS Mortgage Investment Fund, VMS Mortgage Investors L.P. II and VMS Mortgage Investors L.P. III -- said the foreclosures took place after VMS filed bankruptcy-law petitions on three groups of the buildings last week. Under the settlement agreement, the funds allowed First Chicago to take possession of properties that owed some $43 million to the funds.

A spokeswoman for First Chicago wouldn't comment on the agreement.

Previously, in two separate transactions three VMS funds took possession of the Silver Sands Motel and a 55-acre oceanfront development in Key Biscayne, Fla. The funds also negotiated the removal of VMS as joint venture partner in a downtown Chicago condominium development and in a suburban Chicago townhouse project. VMS, which once claimed to control a $9 billion real estate empire, raised $1.1 billion from the public through the funds as a source of mortgage loans. The funds severed all management ties to VMS earlier this year and VMS said it is liquidating its operations. VMS spokesmen couldn't be reached for comment.
