BankAmerica Unit Syndicate

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/15/90

SAN FRANCISCO -- Bank of America said it led a 26-member underwriting syndicate in the sale yesterday of a $4.1 billion issue of revenue anticipation notes for the state of California. The state said it was the largest issue of its kind in state history. Bank of America, a unit of BankAmerica Corp., added it was the largest such syndicate it ever led.

The notes, which mature on June 28, 1991, were sold at a net interest of 6.05%, according to the California State Treasurer's office. Proceeds of the issue are expected to fund the state's cash-flow management program for the fiscal year ending June 30, 1991.

Bank of America said the issue received a rating of MIG-1 from Moody's Investors Service Inc., and SP-1+ from Standard & Poor's Corp. Both represent the highest possible rating on short-term municipal notes, the bank added.
