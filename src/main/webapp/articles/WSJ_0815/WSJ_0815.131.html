International Brief -- Industrial Equity (Pacific) Ltd.: Investment Firm Sells Stake In Department Store Chain

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/15/90

Industrial Equity (Pacific) Ltd., the Hong-Kong based investment company controlled by New Zealand investor Sir Ronald Brierley, confirmed that it sold a stake of about 10% in the French department store concern Societe Anonyme des Galeries Lafayette. The French press has been speculating this week that Industrial Equity had been the seller in a block transaction which was performed by the brokerage DLP James Capel. An Industrial Equity spokesman, reached by telephone in London, refused to identify the buyer of the block, which he said was sold late Friday.

According to the spokesman, the sale of the stake in Galeries Lafayette was part of a "redeployment of resources" within Industrial Equity's holdings. "We're actually quite happy with the company, especially its development in the U.S. It wasn't a change of perception of Lafayette, it was more an internal decision," he explained.

The spokesman declined to specify whether his company had warned Lafayette Chairman Georges Meyer of the sale prior to Friday's action. Mr. Meyer told French newspapers that he hadn't been aware that the transaction would take place.

However, the Industrial Equity spokesman said that "our relationship {with Lafayette} has always been very cordial, we have a tremendously high regard for them and we tried to put them in the picture as much as we could."
