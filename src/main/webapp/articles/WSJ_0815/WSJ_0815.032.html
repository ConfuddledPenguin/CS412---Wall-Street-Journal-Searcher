Lloyd's Raises Its Rates On Cargo Touching Jordan

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 08/15/90

LONDON -- The Lloyd's of London War Risks Rating Committee has recommended sharply higher rates to insure cargoes being transported to or from Jordan. A spokesman for the Institute of Underwriters said this was because Jordan had become the only conduit for goods moving into or out of Iraq, since the imposition of a United Nations embargo on trade with Iraq and Kuwait.

Before the announcement by the War Risks Rating Committee, insuring a cargo moving to Jordan by marine craft against war risks cost 0.0275% of the insured value. The same insurance will now cost almost four times more, or 0.1% of the insured value.

The cost of insuring air cargoes to or from Jordan has risen by about the same rate to 0.075% of the insured value, from 0.02% previously.

The committee also recommended changes in the schedule for cargoes moving through Saudi Arabian ports or places from Dammam north.

Whereas rates for Saudi Arabian Gulf ports or places were previously "held covered," or decided by underwriters based on individual voyages, insurance for cargoes being transported by marine craft to or from these ports against war risks will now cost 0.5% of the insured value.

Insuring cargoes being transported by ship to Saudi ports or places south of Dammam will now cost 0.05% of the insured value.

Insuring air cargoes to or from any part of Saudi Arabia will now cost 0.075% of the insured value, whereas this area was previously held covered.
