Yields on Small CDs Sold Directly by Banks Eased in Latest Week

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/15/90

NEW YORK -- Yields on small-denomination certificates of deposit sold directly by major banks continued to ease in the latest week, although large deposits and certificates of deposit sold by brokerage firms posted higher yields. The average yield on a small-denomination six-month certificate of deposit, for instance, slipped to 7.38% from 7.42% in the week ended Tuesday, according to Banxquote Money Markets, an information service based here. On a one-year certificate of deposit, the average yield dipped to 7.52% from 7.57% for small denominations.

On deposits of $90,000 and up, however, the average yield rose slightly to 7.53% from 7.51% for six months and to 7.63% from 7.60% for one year, Banxquote reported. Broker-sold certificates of deposit posted bigger increases in yields. The largest jump was on one-year deposits, with the average yield rising to 7.89% from 7.72% in one week.

Brokerage firms, which negotiate rates with banks and thrifts whose certificates of deposit they sell, offer higher average yields across the board. On five-year certificates of deposit, the average yield at broker-dealers was 8.38%, compared with 7.87% on smaller deposits at major banks and 8.14% on $90,000 and up.
