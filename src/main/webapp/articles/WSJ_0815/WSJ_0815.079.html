Tuesday's Markets: Dollar Falls As Stocks, Bonds Gain --- Currency Hits a Low Against the Mark; Share Trading Is Thin

  • Author: Christopher Winans
  • Date: 08/15/90

NEW YORK -- The dollar fell broadly against major currencies, reaching a record low against the mark. The dollar's slide came amid signs that Mideast tensions could be easing. That led traders to shift their attention back to the weakening U.S. economy and to start selling dollars.

Stock and bond prices edged higher. Bond prices, which like stocks started the day heading lower, turned around after it was reported that Jordan's King Hussein had been to Iraq and was on his way to the U.S. to meet with President Bush. That raised hopes that some sort of diplomatic settlement might be in the works.

The Dow Jones Industrial Average rose 0.99 to 2747.77, but again volume was thin, as 130.3 million shares changed hands in New York Stock Exchange trading.

With the dollar less appealing as a haven, traders concentrated on growing signs of economic weakness in the U.S., including weaker than expected growth in July retail sales. Traders think a continued economic slowdown will mean lower U.S. interest rates, which reduce the dollar's appeal to investors.

"The currency market is reasserting the direction that was in place" before the Iraqi invasion of Kuwait Aug. 2, said Jeffrey Mondschein, a managing director of Continental Bank.

James Solloway, chief economist at Argus Research, said that while stock-market investors may have been cheered by the prospect of a cooling off in the Mideast, optimism about a peaceful settlement may be unwarranted.

Bond prices drew some strength from traders selling short-term Treasuries to buy longer-term debt on the bet that short-term prices will ease as new supply comes to auction in the next few weeks.

In major market action:

Stock prices rose. Advancing issues outnumbered decliners, 852-640.

Bond prices rose a quarter of a point, or $2.50 for each $1,000 of face amount, in light trading. The yield on the Treasury's benchmark 30-year bond eased to 8.77%.
