Fed Sees New England's Slump as a Regional Woe

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/15/90

WASHINGTON -- Federal Reserve Board Chairman Alan Greenspan said the economic slump in New England isn't likely to provoke a national recession and won't "interfere with the conduct of monetary policy in a fundamental way." In a written statement responding to a letter from Senate Banking Committee Chairman Donald Riegle (D., Mich.), Mr. Greenspan said the central bank "doesn't have the tools to deal surgically with regional or sectoral problems."

Mr. Greenspan said the current slump in New England stems from "setbacks" in the defense, high-tech and financial-services industries. This brought a downturn in real estate, which in turn left banks and other lenders with heavy losses.

"I don't see the sort of direct contagion in these problems that will lead to national recession, and while they are an important feature of the economic and financial environment that must be considered as we make our decisions, I don't believe that they `interfere' with the conduct of monetary policy in a fundamental way," he said.
