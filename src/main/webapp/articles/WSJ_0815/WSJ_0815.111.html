Business Brief -- Crown Resources Corp.: Firm Expects to Post Loss Of $830,000 for 2nd Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/15/90

Crown Resources Corp. expects to report a loss of about $830,000, or seven cents a share, for the second quarter ended June 30, but company Chairman Mark Jones told Dow Jones the mining company should be profitable in the second half of the year. Crown, which has been producing gold with a joint venture partner since February, had revenue of about $1.8 million for the quarter. In the year-ago second quarter, the Denver-based company reported a loss of about $323,000, or four cents a share, on no revenue. Average shares outstanding have increased about 37% to 12.3 million shares. For the first six months, the company expects to report net income of nearly $2.2 million, or about 17 cents a share, compared with a loss of about $618,000, or about seven cents a share. Revenue for the first six months of 1990 was nearly $1.9 million compared with about $3,000 for the first six months last year. The net income in the most recent six months includes a $5 million gain recorded in the first quarter on the sale of a property option.

Crown, which is primarily an exploration company, is also exploring for gold on three other properties: one at the southern end of the Carlin Trend in Nevada, one on the Utah trend and one in northern Washington near its Crown Jewel property. The company said its finding costs so far have averaged only $5 an ounce, well below the industry average.
