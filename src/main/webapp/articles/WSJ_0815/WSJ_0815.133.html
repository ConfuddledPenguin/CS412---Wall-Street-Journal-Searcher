International Brief -- Deutsche Babcock AG: Engineering Firm to Post Pretax Loss for Fiscal Year

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/15/90

The West German heavy engineering group Deutsche Babcock AG expects to post an as yet unspecified pretax loss for its fiscal year ending Sept. 30, after suffering a 15 million mark ($9.5 million) loss in the six months ended April 30. "We have hard times ahead of us," Oberhausen-based Babcock's new chairman Heyo Schmiedknecht said. At around the start of fiscal 1990, Babcock had expected to post a pretax profit of between 50 million and 70 million marks for the full year.

"We must reckon on a clear pull-back of our financial results," the 51-year-old executive said, adding that "we are expecting a loss" before taxes for 1990.

The company reported back in May that it suffered an undisclosed pretax loss in the first five months of its fiscal year and that it didn't expect a profit for the full year.

In fiscal 1989, Deutsche Babcock's net income fell 91% to 3.8 million marks from 40 million marks a year earlier. The sharp decline prompted Babcock to omit its regular annual cash dividend for fiscal 1989.
