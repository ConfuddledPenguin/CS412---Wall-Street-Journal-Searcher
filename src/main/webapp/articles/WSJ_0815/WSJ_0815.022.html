Letters to the Editor: Making Sky the Limit Can Cloud Home Sale

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/15/90

Your July 25 page-one story regarding the self-delusion home sellers are inflicting upon themselves serves as a lesson for many in today's post-boom marketplace. An unrealistic price set for a home only points to the seller's naivete and the broker's unwillingness to do his job: counsel the client to recognize what the market will bear. The worst advice is to set a high price "and see what happens." What usually occurs -- as evidenced by the case study of the folks in Rye, N.Y. -- is that the property doesn't sell, the asking price is continually "adjusted," all parties involved get fed up and, worse, the property is stigmatized. Who wants to admit to buying something that's been knocked around and passed up by half the world?

An owner has the privilege of setting any price he wishes. But it's important to measure the benefits of a lower price that may lead to a quick sale and lessen the risk that the property will become corrupted by an unrealistic view of what the true value is. It's never been more appropriate to return to the time-honored rule that "it's only worth what someone will pay for it."

Eric H. Killorin

Middlebury, Vt.
