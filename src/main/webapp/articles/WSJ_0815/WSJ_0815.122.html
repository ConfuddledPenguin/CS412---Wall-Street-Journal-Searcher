Business Brief -- Duracell Holdings Corp.: Company Reports Earnings Of $6 Million for Fiscal '90

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/15/90

Duracell Holdings Corp., the battery producer taken private in a June 1988 leveraged buy-out, reported earnings of $6 million for the year ended June 30 compared with a year-ago loss of $106 million. Operating cash flow -- earnings from operations plus depreciation and amortization -- advanced 18% to $306 million from $260 million. Duracell, based in Bethel, Conn., reported sales climbed 6.8% to $1.33 billion from $1.25 billion.

Duracell had been a part of Kraft Inc., which now is part of Philip Morris Cos., the giant New York foods, tobacco and brewing concern.

Sales in the U.S, and Europe were particularly strong, and consumers continue to move to alkaline batteries, Duracell's specialty, from zinc-carbon batteries, a spokesman said.
