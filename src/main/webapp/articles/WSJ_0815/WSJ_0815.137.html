International Brief -- Hanson PLC: Nine-Month Pretax Profit Rose 26% at British Firm

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/15/90

Hanson PLC, Britain's largest industrial conglomerate, reported a 26% rise in pretax profit in the nine months ended June 30, helped by first-time contributions from Consolidated Gold Fields. Pretax profit in the latest nine months rose to #939 million ($1.77 billion) from #742 million a year earlier. The results were at the top end of expectations, which ranged between #910 million and #940 million.

Profit after taxes but before extraordinary items totaled #712 million, up 26% from #567 million in the year-earlier period. Earnings per share rose to 14.6 pence, up 13% from 12.9 pence in the year-earlier period.

Sales climbed 3.6% to #5.17 billion from #4.99 billion the year earlier.

Hanson's chairman, Lord Hanson, described the results as very good in view of "the difficult conditions affecting some sectors of our business" and said progress so far at the company's U.K. and U.S. operations "allows us to be confident about the balance of the financial year."

Hanson didn't break out the size of the first-time contribution from Consolidated Gold Fields, the British mining and minerals group it acquired in August 1989. The #3.5 billion takeover was Britain's largest ever.
