Letters to the Editor: Criticism of Florio Was Hardly Objective

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/15/90

Your July 30 Politics & Policy article "New Jersey's Florio Has Won Battles but Faces Tax Revolt, Loss of Assembly" quoted Stephen Salmore opining on the loss of Gov. Florio's credibility, and it identifies Mr. Salmore as a political scientist at Rutgers University. Mr. Salmore is on the Rutgers faculty, but he also is a paid adviser to Republican candidates. Recently, he conducted a seminar for local Republican nominees, advising them on strategies to counter Gov. Florio. Had your reporter known about this connection, he might not have given as much credence to Mr. Salmore's "spin." In any case, it is a fact that readers might find helpful.

Gov. Florio has proposed fair, honest answers for the serious budget problems he inherited. He has enacted a reform of our state's tax system that will ease the burden of high property taxes on the middle class. While policy disagreements and analysis are a welcome part of the process, politically motivated statements masquerading as objective commentary should be put in perspective.

John Shure

Director of Communications

Office of the Governor

State of New Jersey

Trenton, N.J.
