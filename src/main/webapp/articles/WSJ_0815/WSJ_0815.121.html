Gap Inc. Expects To Post 30% Rise In 2nd-Period Net

  • Author: Richard B. Schmitt
  • Date: 08/15/90

SAN FRANCISCO -- Gap Inc., heading off a general slump in retailing, expects to report more than a 30% increase in fiscal second-quarter net on a 24% increase in sales. Donald Fisher, chairman and chief executive officer, said in an interview that net for the period ending Aug. 4 would be more than 30% higher than net of 40 cents reported in the year-earlier second quarter. He said sales in the latest quarter were $405 million, up 24% from $326 million a year earlier.

Analysts had expected Gap to earn in the range of 45 cents to 50 cents a share. The results are also somewhat surprising because the apparel company got such a poor start to the quarter. Comparable store sales rose a disappointing 6% in May as inclement weather doused the traditional beginning of the summer buying season. After the May report, some analysts reduced estimates of second-quarter net for the company to as low as 33 cents.

Mr. Fisher said warmer weather and strong consumer acceptance of new merchandise in mid-June helped to correct things. Comparable store sales rose 24% in June and 28% in July -- and a strong 19% increase for the quarter. Margins were "very, very good" because of the popular new merchandise and the reduced need for discounting, he added. Mr. Fisher also cited "meaningful" progress at the Banana Republic division, a perennial money-loser that should turn a profit this year.

For the first half, Mr. Fisher said, Gap expects to report earnings at least 35% higher than the 83 cents reported a year earlier, on a 27% increase in sales to $807 million from $636 million a year earlier.

He said "our goals for the second half are more modest than the first half" because of the uncertainty with the economy. But he said Gap hopes to still outperform the industry. Gap expects to operate roughly 1100 stores at year-end compared with about 960 a year earlier; Mr. Fisher said the company is considering additional expansion in Europe and has been "looking around" in Japan.

In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange yesterday, Gap closed at $55.25, down 50 cents from Monday's close.
