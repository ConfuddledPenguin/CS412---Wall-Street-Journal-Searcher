OTC Focus: Rally in Telecommunications Stocks Pulls Market Higher; Utility Index Gains

  • Author: Craig S. Smith Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 08/15/90

The over-the-counter market drifted higher on a rally in telecommunications stocks in a session otherwise stalled by fear. Many investors remain afraid of taking a position in the market with the Middle East crisis unresolved.

"People are scared," says Robert McCready, institutional trader at A.G. Edwards & Sons. "Nobody wants to be long or short."

Traders were unimpressed by the buying that focused on cellular telephone stocks, calling it a "technical bounce" after days of selling in the group.

"There's no news to drive them," says Robert Antolini, head of OTC trading at Donaldson Lufkin & Jenrette. He adds that many of the stocks "haven't had an uptick in days."

Gains in stocks such as LIN Broadcasting, McCaw Communications and Vanguard Cellular Class A drove the Nasdaq Utility Index up 11.74, or 2.1%, to 568.88.

Meanwhile, the Nasdaq Composite Index added 2.03, or about 0.5%, to 410.33. On the New York Stock Exchange, the composite index rose 0.25%.

OTC advancers led decliners, 1,114 to 809.

Mr. Antolini says the lack of sellers in the OTC market may mean there is an underlying desire to buy stocks.

Volume totaled a dismal 107.5 million shares, but was well ahead of Monday's 87.6 million shares when power outages in New York City closed some of the biggest OTC trading desks.

LIN Broadcasting rose 3 to 62 3/4, McCaw Communications added 1 1/4 to 22 1/2, and Vanguard Cellular Class A gained 1 3/8 to 23. Vanguard reported yesterday that its loss for the latest second quarter narrowed to 47 cents a share from 58 cents a year ago.

MCI Communications and Telecom USA completed their merger, under which MCI acquires Telecom USA's common stock for about $1.25 billion. MCI added 3/4 to 37 yesterday.

Intel led the most active list, dropping 1 1/4 to 36 1/4 on volume of more than 3 million shares. Bear Stearns downgraded its rating on the stock to "hold" from "buy" and removed the stock from its recommended list. The actions were caused by continuing concern that Advanced Micro Devices may emerge from a copyright suit with rights to "clone" Intel's popular 80386 microprocessor. But not all of Wall Street agrees that the case could hurt Intel. Last Thursday, Merrill Lynch reiterated its positive rating on the stock. Bear Stearns didn't change its earnings estimates for the company.

Genzyme added 1 1/4 to 19 1/4 on heavy volume. Genzyme and newly formed Neozyme, filed a combined registration statement with the Securities and Exchange Commission for the public offering of 2.5 million units each consisting of 1 Neozyme share and one warrant to buy one share of Genzyme stock. Neozyme will receive all of the proceeds from the offering, but these funds will be used to engage Genzyme to perform research and development and clinical testing of a selected group of health care products.

Chiron rose 1 1/4 to 39 1/2 on nearly double its average daily volume. Chiron reported profit of 20 cents a share in the latest second quarter, up sharply from the year-ago quarter when the company reported a loss of 30 cents a share. Revenue in the latest second quarter nearly doubled to 17.8 million from 19.2 million a year earlier.

Caere continued to climb as the market responds positively to the company's hand-held scanner that enters text directly from the printed page into a computer. Caere introduced its "Typist" scanner for use with Apple Computer's Macintosh at last week's MacWorld Conference in Boston. Caere added 2 1/4 to 23 3/4, after rising 1 1/4 Monday.

Sundowner Offshore Services lost 1 5/8 to 11 3/8. The company said it expects its third quarter profit to fall below the profit reported for the first and second quarters this year. The company also reported second quarter profit today of $436,781, down from $527,826 a year ago. The company attributed the decreases to idle periods for its rigs. Sundowner went public last month at $9 a share in an offering managed by Alex. Brown & Sons.

Image Bank tumbled 3/4 to 4 1/4 after announcing that it expects to report a loss for the third quarter ended June 30. The company, which operates an extensive photograph library, said it expects the loss for the quarter to total about $584,000, including a write-off of about $700,000 from currently non-utilized foreign tax credits. Revenue for the third quarter is expected to have increased to $13.4 million from $10 million.

Westcott Communications dropped 3/8 to 7 1/4, after falling 1 1/2 points Monday. Yesterday, Alex. Brown downgraded its rating on Westcott and lowered its 1990 earnings estimates for the company. Wescott produces specialized televison programming for automobile dealers and police departments.

Crest Industries, which lost 1/4 point Monday, fell another 1 5/8 to 4 3/8 yesterday. The company reported that its first quarter profit fell to $850,247 from $1,356,452 a year earlier. Crest went public in July at $6.75 a share in an offering managed by Raymond James & Associates.

Ashton-Tate gained 1/4 to 8 3/4 after it authorized the buy-back of up to two million of its shares. The software company has about 27 million shares outstanding.
