General Homes Wins Clearance For Realignment

  • Author: Christi Harlan
  • Date: 08/15/90

General Homes Corp. obtained a court order allowing the company to voluntarily reorganize under Chapter 11 of the U.S. Bankruptcy Code, reversing its opposition to an involuntary reorganization sought last month by three bondholders. The Houston-based homebuilder set the stage for a voluntary reorganization last week when it obtained a court order allowing it to pay suppliers and contractors. The order was a critical step toward preventing unpaid suppliers and contractors from filing liens to block the sales of homes and lots.

"We are entering this phase in about the best position we can be in," said General Homes president W.A. LeSage. "There shouldn't be a disruption in our business."

As recently as two weeks ago, Mr. LeSage had said General Homes would seek to dismiss the involuntary Chapter 11 petition filed against the company July 11 by three bondholders.

Yesterday, Mr. LeSage said the involuntary reorganization created "a lot of uncertainty" for the company and that voluntary reorganization "made a lot of sense."

"This should be an opportunity to finally restructure this company," Mr. LeSage said.

The company had been struggling for three years to overcome losses on its portfolio of raw land, whose value plummeted in the Texas real estate depression.

The Texas real estate market is now showing signs of recovery, particularly in the Houston area, where most of General Homes' land holdings are located. The company also holds real estate in the Dallas-Fort Worth area; Orlando, Fla.; and Phoenix, Ariz.

"The land, at this point, is an asset that could bring value to the company," Mr. LeSage said.

General Homes currently owes about $225 million to its senior secured lenders, who hold liens on all of the company's real estate assets. The principal amount of the company's subordinated debt is about $140 million.

The voluntary Chapter 11 petition doesn't affect the company's units, including a title company and a mortgage subsidiary, Mr. LeSage said.

General Homes is represented in the reorganization by the Houston-based law firm of Sheinfeld, Maley & Kay.
