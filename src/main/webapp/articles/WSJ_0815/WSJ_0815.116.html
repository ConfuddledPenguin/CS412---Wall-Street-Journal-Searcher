Who's News: Trump Organization Appoints Bollenbach Top Financial Officer

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/15/90

NEW YORK -- Donald J. Trump named Stephen F. Bollenbach, who had been chief financial officer of Promus Companies, as the new -- and first -- chief financial officer of the Trump Organization. The appointment, made at the behest of Mr. Trump's banks, essentially gives Mr. Bollenbach the task of saving the one-time billionaire's empire.

While at Promus, Mr. Bollenbach helped engineer a spinoff of the company's Holiday Inns division, giving equity holders a $65-a-share dividend. Mr. Bollenbach also has acted as chief financial officer for Daniel Ludwig, the shipping magnate who lost $1 billion in a project in Brazil.

He will face serious challenges in his new position with Mr. Trump, who has never employed a chief financial officer before. Asked about the task before him, Mr. Bollenbach said: "This is just like the Super Bowl."

The most pressing problem will be to persuade holders of $1.3 billion in bonds secured by Mr. Trump's three Atlantic City casinos to forfeit $215 million in principal and interest payments over the next year. In return, Mr. Trump is expected to offer bondholders equity in his properties.

Other Trump assets, such as the Plaza Hotel and Trump Shuttle, also aren't making enough money to meet their interest payments. Under a $65 million bank loan agreement negotiated in June, Mr. Trump will defer interest on some of his loans.

"There are some wonderful assets," Mr. Bollenbach said. "The problem is finding the best financing given the bad economy in the Northeast."

Meanwhile, Mr. Trump suffered a setback in the Apellate Division of New Jersey Superior Court yesterday when a panel of judges refused to overturn a New Jersey Casino Control Commission ruling requiring Mr. Trump to release sensitive financial documents related to his bank loans. Mr. Trump's attorneys are expected to appeal the decision to New Jersey Supreme Court today.
