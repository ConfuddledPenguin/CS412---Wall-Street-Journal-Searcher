Whitman Removes Hussman Corp. Unit From Auction Block

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/15/90

CHICAGO -- Whitman Corp. took its Hussmann Corp. unit off the auction block after offers for the commercial refrigerator manufacturing concern were judged inadequate. The consumer-products concern also said it will take a special charge against earnings this year to reflect its reorganization into an operating-company structure from a holding company. It didn't say how large the charge will be.

Concerning Hussmann, James W. Cozad, chairman and chief executive officer, said "the economic value of the company is clearly greater than the price we were offered, so we have withdrawn it from the market." Whitman's other units include specialty food maker Pet Inc., a PepsiCola bottler and the Midas International automotive service franchiser.

Previously, Sanyo Electric Co., a Japan-based maker of consumer electronics and appliances, was said to have submitted the highest bid for St. Louis-based Hussmann. That bid, which was between $500 million and $600 million, was said to be below Whitman's expectations. However, Sanyo last month denied it had submitted a formal offer.

Whitman, adding that orders for Hussmann equipment have reached record levels after a weak first half, said it will seek "to realize Hussmann's improving potential as a Whitman company." Hussmann has about 40% to 45% of the market for refrigerators and refrigerated display cases for supermarkets and about 25% of the market for such equipment used in convenience stores. In 1989, it had a profit of $60 million on revenue of $789 million.
