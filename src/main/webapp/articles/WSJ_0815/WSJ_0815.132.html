Business Brief -- Henley Group Inc.: Spinoff Plan Is Amended; Itel Stake to Be Liquidated

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/15/90

Henley Group Inc., seeking to streamline the company, amended a previous plan to spin off two of its units and said it will liquidate its stake in Itel Corp. At least a portion of Henley's 38.5% stake in Itel will be bought back by Itel; the remaining shares will either be purchased or underwritten by Itel, Henley said.

The transaction, coming about two months after Henley announced its decision to sell its Itel shares as part of a liquidation plan, will net Henley about $375 million, all of which will be used to pay down its $415 million in bank debt, a company spokesman said.

Itel is a Chicago-based leasing company. Henley is a Hampton, N.H., industrial holding company.

Henley, which has been reorganizing and spinning off companies following its spinoff from Allied-Signal Corp. four years ago, also modified a divestiture plan disclosed two months ago. Under the latest plan, Henley said it won't spin off its Pneumo Abex unit, as previously announced. The company had said in June that it would distribute 35% of its Pneumo Abex unit to shareholders. But Henley said it will move forward with its plan to spin off its Fisher International Inc. unit, although the Fisher spinoff plan no longer includes two million shares of Itel that Henley was going to pass on to the spun-off Fisher operation.
