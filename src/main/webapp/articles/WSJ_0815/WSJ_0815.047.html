CBOT to Introduce Futures Tied to Japanese Markets

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/15/90

CHICAGO -- The Chicago Board of Trade said it will next month begin trading its first futures contracts based on Japanese stock and bond markets. The exchange said it will launch long-term Japanese Government Bond futures and futures-options trading Sept. 27, along with its so-called TOPIX futures and options, which will be based on the Tokyo Stock Price Index.

The CBOT and the Tokyo Stock Exchange agreed in 1988 to cooperate in launching TOPIX and Japanese Government Bond futures at the CBOT, and U.S. Treasury bond futures at the Tokyo Stock Exchange.

Uncertainty caused by the 1987 stock-market crash slowed development of the plan. The recent sharp decline in prices at the Tokyo Stock Exchange increased interest in financial instruments that would allow investors to hedge holdings in Japanese stocks, similar to the measures currently afforded by futures contracts based on such U.S. market indicators as the Standard & Poor's 500 index.
