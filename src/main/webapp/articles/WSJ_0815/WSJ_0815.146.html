What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/15/90

VENEZUELA BACKED off from indications that it would increase oil exports. Saudi Arabia still hasn't substantially boosted its output, but said it is committed to do so. The U.S. was counting on both nations to offset the loss of Iraqi and Kuwaiti crude. Meanwhile, British Petroleum said it could produce 200,000 more barrels a day from Alaska.

The U.S. offered to mediate between Chevron and a California county in a dispute holding up production of 100,000 barrels a day in oil.

---

The FDIC proposed boosting banks' deposit insurance fees by $1.1 billion next year to help ensure that it can cover future bank failures.

---

More airlines slashed certain fares, and several carriers delayed any fuel-related ticket-price rises.

---

Bush accused Democrats of breaking a pledge to offer a deficit-cutting plan and vowed to veto any bill that "busts the budget." The Democrats denied making such a promise.

---

The dollar fell against major currencies, reaching a record low against the mark. Stocks and bonds edged higher. Equities in Tokyo rebounded Tuesday after Monday's plunge.

---

The price of gold has risen 7% in the past three days, to $411.50 an ounce, and further tension in the Mideast could push it even higher.

---

Retail sales edged up 0.1% in July, after rising 1.1% in June and falling the three previous months. Last month's flat performance preceded the rise in oil prices, which could lead consumers to curb their spending.

---

Margin calls by brokers are increasing in frequency amid the stock market's decline this month.

---

Sales of North American-made cars and trucks dropped 18.5% in early August. Industry officials are pessimistic about the rest of 1990.

---

GM and Ford are raising car prices aggressively in what could be a boon to their Japanese rivals.

GM agreed to strong job security guarantees to end a local strike. The pact could be model for GM and UAW.

---

Wheeling-Pittsburgh reached a tentative labor accord that would give workers 11% of the steelmaker and allow it to emerge from Chapter 11.

---

Goldman Sachs named Robert E. Rubin and Stephen Friedman to be senior partners and co-chairmen. They succeed John L. Weinberg.

---

Limited Inc.'s second-quarter profit rose 24% to $79 million, as sales jumped 17%. But the quickly expanding retailer's same-store sales fell an estimated 5% in June and July.

---

AmBase agreed to sell its troubled insurance unit to European investor group Vik Brothers for $544 million.

---

Pepsi is expected to name David Wozak as its top marketer. He is now marketing chief at the Pizza Hut unit.

---

Cineplex Odeon's shares sank 15% after the Toronto cinema concern said it may not be able to complete the sale of some U.S. theater assets.

---

General Homes won court approval to reorganize under Chapter 11. The home builder had opposed an involuntary reorganization sought last month by three bondholders.

---

Corrections & Amplifications

LIMITED Inc. aims to be a $10 billion-a-year company with 10% after-tax profits by the mid-1990s. In some editions yesterday, the percentage of the profit margin was misstated. Also, Limited's same-store sales increased 5% in June and 6% in July. In yesterday's What's News column, it was incorrectly stated that same-store sales fell in those months.
