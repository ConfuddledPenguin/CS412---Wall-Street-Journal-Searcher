Eastern Air Posts $35.6 Million Loss For 2nd Quarter

  • Author: Bridget O'Brian
  • Date: 08/15/90

Eastern Airlines reported a second-quarter loss of $35.6 million, a deficit that would have been four times as deep without asset sales. In addition, the Miami-based carrier, still operating under Bankruptcy Court protection, said it expects to have losses for the rest of the year as fuel prices continue to rise and it struggles to rebuild its operations.

Eastern said it had gains of $114.8 million in the latest quarter from asset sales. A year earlier, it had a $129.3 million loss after gains of $261.8 million. Revenue for the quarter was $545.2 million, up substantially from last year's $89.6 million, when Eastern was practically shut down by a strike.

Despite the narrower loss and the revenue gain, other signposts don't bode well for Eastern. The airline filled 61.5% of its seats in the first six months, far below its break-even point. And that traffic came from highly seasonal, heavily discounted tickets.

A bankruptcy judge in April wrested control of the airline from its parent, Continental Airline Holdings Inc. (formerly Texas Air Corp.), and the airline said bookings fell for April and May as a result of the bad publicity.

For the six months, the airline had a net loss of $172 million on revenue of $1.1 billion. For the 1989 first half, the net loss was $384.5 million on revenue of $710.2 million.

Meanwhile, a federal bankruptcy judge in New York granted Eastern temporary relief from major provisions in its labor contract with pilots.

"It is clear that prompt cost reductions, including pilot labor cost reductions, are essential if an imminent liquidation of the estate is to be avoided," Judge Burton Lifland said in his ruling.

The pilots' union issued a statement saying it was disappointed but not surprised by the court action. "We'll consider whether to appeal," Richard Seltzer, an attorney for the Air Line Pilots Association, said. "We think this decision was without a basis in fact and in law."
