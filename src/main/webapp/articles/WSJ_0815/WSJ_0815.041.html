Marketing & Media: Chicago Tribune Scraps Its One-Page Fax Edition

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/15/90

CHICAGO -- The Chicago Tribune pulled the plug on its highly touted fax edition. Tribune Co.'s flagship newspaper was the most recent paper to get on the facsimile bandwagon and the first to pull out. Despite a heavy promotion campaign, the Tribune said its Tribfax service had poor earnings.

The Tribune began sending a one-page summary of headlines and business news to customers April 4. The paper had hoped to have between 1,000 and 2,000 subscribers at an annual rate of $400 by year's end. The product was available nationwide.

The Minneapolis Star-Tribune and Times Mirror Co.'s Hartford (Conn.) Courant remain in the fax business. Dow Jones & Co. -- publisher of this paper -- Knight-Ridder Co. and New York Times Co. are producing, testing or studying facsimile products in various markets.
