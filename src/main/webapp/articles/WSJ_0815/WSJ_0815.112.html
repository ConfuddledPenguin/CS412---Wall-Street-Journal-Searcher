Chrysler Discusses Linkups With 3 European Firms

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/15/90

WINDSOR, Ont. -- Chrysler Corp. chairman Lee A. Iacocca said the auto maker has discussed potential ties with three European manufacturers, but said there won't be an agreement within the next month and dismissed the possibility of a full-scale merger. Mr. Iacocca, speaking at a plant ceremony marking the start of production for the 1991 minivans, said Chrysler has talked with Regie Nationale des Usines Renault of France, Volvo AB of Sweden and Fiat S.p.A. of Italy. He also said Chrysler has talked with several other auto companies about possible arrangements.

The negotiations with Fiat have sparked speculation in Europe and the U.S. that the Italian company may agree to distribute Chrysler products in Europe, help Chrysler develop new vehicles or, possibly, buy a sizable equity stake in Chrysler.

Mr. Iacocca said an equity stake by another auto maker in Chrysler isn't imminent, but may come in the future.

The company later clarified Mr. Iacocca's comments about Renault and Volvo talks, saying those discussions primarily involve "commercial arrangements."

Chrysler shares fell 12.5 cents yesterday to $13.125 in composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange.

"Everybody's talking to everybody," Mr. Iacocca said. "I think over time everybody's going to need some kind of affiliation or partnership."
