Who's News: American Health Taps Atkins for Top Spot As Meyer Steps Down

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/07/90

NEWPORT BEACH, Calif. -- American Health Services Corp. said E. Larry Atkins was named president and chief executive officer, succeeding S. Lewis Meyer, who resigned those positions, as well as those of chairman and director. The change follows the sale earlier this year of new preferred stock and warrants representing a 30% stake in the operator of medical diagnostic imaging centers to OmniCorp Holdings Inc., U.S. arm of OmniCorp A.G., a Swiss concern that is, in turn, controlled by Swiss investor Werner Rey.

OmniCorp acquired its stake for $7 million and assumed three seats on American Health's seven-member board.

A spokesman for American Health said Mr. Meyer, 46 years old, resigned after "differences over the company's direction as a whole between not only Omni's directors, but all the directors led to a feeling that it was time for a change."

Mr. Atkins, 43, has been executive vice president and chief operating officer of American Health. The company spokesman said the position of chairman will remain vacant for the moment.

American Health stock closed yesterday unchanged, at $1.50 a share, in over-the-counter trading. Announcement of the change was made shortly before the close of trading.
