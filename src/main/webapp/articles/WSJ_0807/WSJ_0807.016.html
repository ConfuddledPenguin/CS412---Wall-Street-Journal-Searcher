International: Mazda, Fuji Heavy Join The Rush to East Germany

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 08/07/90

TOKYO -- Japan's Mazda Motor Corp. and Fuji Heavy Industries Co. Ltd. are proceeding to set up sales networks in East Germany, spokesmen for the companies said. Mazda is planning to establish an office in East Berlin and to set up around 50 sales and service shops throughout East Germany by year end. The company is scheduled to export its first 500 passenger cars to East Germany this month, the spokesman said.

Mazda, which has the largest market share in West Germany among Japanese car makers, is aiming to take the top spot in East Germany as well, Mazda's spokesman said.

Fuji has signed contracts with 20 local dealers to sell its cars in East Germany and plans to increase the number. Its West German subsidiary, Subaru Deutschland, will administer sales in East Germany, Fuji's spokesman said.

With the setting up of sales networks by Mazda and Fuji, all Japanese passenger-car makers, except mini-car makers Daihatsu Motor Co. and Suzuki Motor Co., either have started or have decided to start sales in East Germany.
