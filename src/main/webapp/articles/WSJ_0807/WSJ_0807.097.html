Marketing & Media: Disney Sets Low-Priced Video for `Pretty Woman'

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/07/90

LAS VEGAS -- In an aggressive marketing move, Walt Disney Co. will release the film "Pretty Woman" on video in October, priced for direct sale to consumers at $19.95, but probably available for much less from discounters. The announcement, at the annual Video Softwares Dealers Association convention, was somewhat surprising as Disney's other major hit this year, "Dick Tracy," was seen as a more likely candidate for sell-through release.

"Pretty Woman" is a modern-day fairy tale about a female prostitute. The movie carries an "R" rating and will provide an alternative in a marketplace glutted with the child-oriented sell-through releases for Christmas, Disney executives reasoned. The film is the biggest box-office hit of the year, grossing about $165 million so far.

The film will be only the third major R-rated release ever marketed for sell-through: "Good Morning, Vietnam" and "Lethal Weapon" were the others, and neither sold close to the seven million to eight million copies Disney thinks it can sell of "Pretty Woman."

The pricing strategy is aggressive because most sell-through titles are sold at $24.98 and above, and studios put a minimum advertised price on them to prevent excessive discounting.

"The biggest film of the year at the most affordable price," said Bill Mechanic, Disney's video chief. "I don't think we're taking risks."
