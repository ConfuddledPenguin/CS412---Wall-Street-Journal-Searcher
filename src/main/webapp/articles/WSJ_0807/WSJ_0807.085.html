Eastern Airlinees, 8 Officials Plead Not Guilty to Charges

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/07/90

NEW YORK -- Eastern Airlines and eight of its top managers pleaded not guilty to federal charges that they failed to perform mandatory maintenance on the carrier's planes and falsified related records. A federal grand jury in Brooklyn indicted Eastern and nine current and former management employees last month. One of the managers pleaded guilty July 30.

The 60-count indictment charged that some employees engaged in "pencil whipping," or signing off on work that wasn't performed, at Eastern's maintenance bases at John F. Kennedy International Airport and La Guardia Airport in New York and Hartsfield International Airport in Atlanta.

Eastern currently is being overseen by a trustee appointed by the U.S. Bankruptcy Court in Manhattan, where the company filed a petition under Chapter 11 of federal bankruptcy law in March.
