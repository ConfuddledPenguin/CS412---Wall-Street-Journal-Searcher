International Brief -- Schering AG: Earnings for the First Half Rose 12% to $114.3 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/07/90

Schering AG of West Germany, a pharmaceutical and chemical company, said first-half profit rose 12% to 182 million marks ($114.3 million) from 162 million marks a year earlier. But sales, damped by strongly negative foreign currency translation factors, edged up just 0.8% to 3.22 billion marks from 3.19 billion marks.

Buoyed by strong first-half profit growth, the company upgraded its cautious estimate of flat full-year earnings, saying it expects an improvement.

Profit for the parent company, which includes domestic performance and exports but excludes results of most foreign and some domestic units, rose 12% to 137 million marks from 122 million marks. The parent's sales increased 4.4% to 1.69 billion marks from 1.62 billion marks. Its sales abroad were up 6.1% at 1.16 billion marks, compared with the year-earlier 1.09 billion marks, and sales in West Germany rose 0.8% to 530 million marks from 526 million marks. On a world-wide group basis, including the parent's results, foreign sales were flat at 2.537 billion marks.
