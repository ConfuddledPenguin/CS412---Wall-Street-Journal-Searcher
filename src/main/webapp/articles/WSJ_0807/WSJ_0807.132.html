Investor Sues MCEG, Alleges the Firm Filed Deceptive Statements

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 08/07/90

LOS ANGELES -- An investor has sued Management Co. Entertainment Group Inc. and five of its current and former officers and directors for allegedly issuing misleading statements and failing to disclose the company's precarious financial condition. Elfriede Glancy, in a complaint that seeks class action status in federal court here, charged that the entertainment company failed to disclose that it was struggling to avoid bankruptcy and was having trouble arranging financing.

The suit claims the company's problems were not reflected in its 1989 annual report and its 10-K report to the Securities and Exchange Commission. Officials at MCEG weren't available for comment.

The suit alleges that MCEG failed to report that it would not be able to adequately fund and operate its business after a proposed acquisition of distributor Virgin Vision Ltd. for approximately $83 million.

MCEG said in July that it was negotiating a standstill agreement with G.E. Capital, a unit of General Electric Co. in connection with $72.5 million in loans that are past due. The company borrowed that amount as part of its financing for the acquisition last year of Virgin Vision, an independent movie television and video distributor.

The company said it is currently working on restructuring those loans. However, in a July filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission, the company said if it is unable to reach an agreement on restructuring the loans, it could be required to seek protection from creditors under federal bankruptcy laws.

The suit seeks unspecified compensatory and punitive damages.
