Letters to the Editor: Can Conservatives Stick Together?

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/07/90

As a right-leaning radical centrist, permit me to question the validity of Albert R. Hunt's July 11 editorial-page column, "Without the Glue, the Conservatives Are Balkanized." While some conservatives and neocons do, indeed, seem momentarily disoriented by the apparent collapse of the Empire of the Commiczars, they will soon rediscover their primal "glue," as Mr. Hunt calls their (and my own) anti-socialist instincts.

This will happen, is happening. Simply because the left's communoid dream of "True Socialism" is alive and well-pretending, of course, that it will now succeed because it has never before been tried.

In its attempt to rise from the ashes, TruSoc (as George Orwell might have called it) will slowly become recognized as the neo-enemy, perhaps not as overtly criminal but just as insidiously dangerous as communism itself. Here is the story in geopolitical rhyme and verse:

Conservatives like us, they say

Have lost our cause and primal glue;

For due to communism's fall,

We have no further good to do.

But hark] Our noble cause remains,

While leftist pseudo-libs do try

To resurrect from Lenin's grave

The "Dream" they hope will never die.

That Dream, called Socialism True,

Is now reborn, the left does claim.

For communism's heinous crimes,

The Dream, they say, was ne'er to blame.

The left would socialize us all --

Intent to fill each space and void

With stuff so very Lenin-like,

It looks and smells like "communoid."

So now, the Cold War does resume

Twixt us and reborn Socialism,

Whose cause the left does now promote

As neo-Marxist Gorbachism.

Jim Guirard Jr.

Washington

---

Mr. Hunt characterized the Rockford Institute as "eccentric." I object. A more accurate characterization might be "independent" or perhaps even "unpredictable," since our writers and editors do not march in lock step with groups based in Washington.

Eccentric is a word better used for those institutions within the Beltway. I say that because the ideas and actions of these groups are often out of step with the rest of the U.S.

Michael Warder

Executive Vice President

Rockford Institute

Rockford, Ill.
