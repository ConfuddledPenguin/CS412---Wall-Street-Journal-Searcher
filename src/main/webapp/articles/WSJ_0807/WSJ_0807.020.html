Enterprise: Court Backs Southland In State Franchise Dispute

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/07/90

NEW YORK -- A New York state trial court, backing Southland Corp. in a dispute with New York's attorney general, said franchisers in the state may negotiate deals that vary from the terms in franchise documents. "The decision, the first of its kind, is expected to have a broad impact on the way franchisers operate nationwide," said David J. Kaufmann, attorney for Southland, Dallas-based franchiser of 7-Eleven stores.

The office of Attorney General Robert Abrams is studying whether to appeal the decision of Justice Elliott Wilk.

The dispute arose last year, after Southland negotiated a deal with the Riese Organization, under which the New York real estate concern would open 7-Eleven stores in Manhattan. After the negotiations, Southland moved to amend its franchise papers. Attorneys for the state argued that amendments must come first; talks, second.

Before clearing the amendment, the state asked Southland to sign an "assurance of discontinuance" of improper franchising practices and pay $1,000 in costs. Southland did both, but sought in the court case to establish that New York franchise law permitted the original negotiations.

Justice Wilk ruled that the office of the state's attorney general had exceeded its authority "by creating a substantive requirement that a franchise must be offered on terms unilaterally decided upon by the franchiser, without input from prospective franchisees via post-registration negotiations."
