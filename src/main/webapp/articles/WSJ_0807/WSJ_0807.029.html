Firstar Will Acquire Banks of Iowa Inc. In $216 Million Pact

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/07/90

MILWAUKEE -- Firstar Corp. said it agreed to acquire Banks of Iowa Inc. in a stock swap valued at about $216 million. The acquisition, which requires approval of both banking concerns' shareholders, would give Firstar the second-biggest banking presence in Iowa, a state that suffered from the mid-1980s farm crisis but has since rebounded strongly. Firstar has assets of about $8.5 billion; Banks of Iowa, Des Moines, has assets of about $2.5 billion.

Earlier this year, Banks of Iowa ended a nearly decade-old agreement to be acquired by First Bank System Inc., Minneapolis, saying it would seek another suitor.

Under the agreement, Firstar would issue 2.7 common shares for each of the approximately 2.9 million Banks of Iowa common shares outstanding. Firstar closed yesterday at $28, unchanged in composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange; Banks of Iowa's shares closed at $68.75, up 75 cents in over-the-counter trading.

The total price is about 1.2 times Banks of Iowa's June 30, 1990, common equity of $181 million.

Firstar said it expects to trim data processing and payroll expenses at the combined banking concerns and boost Banks of Iowa's income through increased lending and other services, so that the issuance of new shares won't be dilutive after 1992.

Holder meetings are expected this October. Completion of the transaction should take place after the first of next year, when Iowa's new interstate banking law becomes effective.
