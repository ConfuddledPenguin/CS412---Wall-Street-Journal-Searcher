Marketing Brief -- Vestron Inc.: Tentative Pact on Purchase By Live Entertainment Set

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/07/90

Vestron Inc. said it tentatively agreed to be acquired by Live Entertainment Inc. and will seek the approval of holders of about $158 million in Vestron debt. The proposed acquisition marks the final winding down of Vestron, a video distributor and movie maker whose fortunes had soared with the hit movie "Dirty Dancing" only three years ago. The company ran into debt problems last year after the videotape market became glutted, as bids for movie rights skyrocketed, and after $100 million in promised financing for the company collapsed.

Terms of the proposed sale weren't disclosed. Vestron's chairman, Austin Furst, who holds about 85% of Vestron's common shares, couldn't be reached for comment.

It wasn't immediately clear whether Vestron, based in Stamford, Conn., would operate as a separate subsidiary of Live Entertainment, which is based in Van Nuys, Calif., and is 48%-owned by Carolco Pictures Inc. Vestron in the past year has sold overseas video-distribution operations and a U.S. TV-programming unit. Its remaining assets include distribution rights to 1,500 titles and the film-sequel rights to "Dirty Dancing."
