UNITED MERCHANTS & MANUFACTURERS Corp. (New York) -- Standard & Poor's Corp. said it raised its rating on this textile maker's subordinated debt, citing the company's payment of interest arrears and its debt restructuring. S&P upgraded to double-C from D the rating on 13.75% senior subordinated notes, due 2000, and 4% convertible subordinated debentures, due in November. The rating concern also said United Merchants' newly issued 3.5% senior subordinated secured debentures, due 2009, are rated double-C. The new debt was issued as part of the debt restructuring, in exchange for a part of 13.75% and 15% debentures. About $170 million of debt is affected. Despite the debt restructuring, a third bankruptcy-law filing is possible, because of continuing poor operations, S&P said. United Merchants' viability remains questionable, without significant operating improvements and asset sales, it said. ---

U.S. BANCORP. (Portland, Ore.) -- Standard & Poor's Corp. said it lowered its ratings on $435 million of debt of this bank holding company and its subsidiary banks, citing "steady weakening of capitalization." The rating concern downgraded senior debt to single-A from single-A-plus and subordinated debt to single-A-minus from single-A, and affirmed the single-A-1 rating on commercial paper.
