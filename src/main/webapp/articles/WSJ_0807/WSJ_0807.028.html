Britain's Retail Sales Volume

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/07/90

LONDON -- Retail sales volume in June fell a revised 2.6% from May and rose 1.1% from June 1989, Britain's Central Statistical Office said. Market forecasts had anticipated the final June figures to be unchanged from the provisional 2.8% month-to-month decline and 0.9% year-to-year rise. The index, in which January 1985 equals 100, was 122.4 in June, compared with 125.7 in May and 121.1 in June 1989.

In the three months through June, retail sales volume rose 0.6% from the previous three months and 1.75% from a year earlier, higher than the provisional estimate of 1.6% from a year earlier.

The value of retail sales in June increased 8% from a year earlier; this was a full percentage point higher than the provisional estimate of 7%.
