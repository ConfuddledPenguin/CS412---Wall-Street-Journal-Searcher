Senate Confirms Gramm To Second Term at CFTC

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/07/90

WASHINGTON -- The Senate by voice vote confirmed Wendy Gramm to a second term as chairwoman of the Commodity Futures Trading Commission. Mrs. Gramm, 45 years old, was approved to serve a five-year term at the commission, which she has headed since 1988. Before joining the agency, which oversees the commodity industry, she worked at the Office of Management and Budget.

Mrs. Gramm is a free-market theorist who was first appointed by President Reagan.
