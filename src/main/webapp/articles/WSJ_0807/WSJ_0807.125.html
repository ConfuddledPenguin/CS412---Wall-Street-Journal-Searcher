British Investors Plan To Buy Proxy Firm Once Run by Carter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/07/90

NEW YORK -- Transwood Consortium Fund, a British investment partnership, said it plans to buy what is left of former proxy solicitor Don Carter's business. Mr. Carter in the mid-1980s built up a profitable business helping figures such as T. Boone Pickens wage proxy fights. But Mr. Carter's own finances came under investigation by New York state last year. Mr. Carter resigned from his firm, then known as the Carter Organization, and last winter pleaded guilty to grand larceny and tax evasion charges.

Mr. Carter's former business, currently known as Quorum Communications Inc., employs 70 people, down from its peak of 140. Quorum is at "break-even," according to Peter Earl, a Transwood executive leading the acquisition proposal.

Mr. Earl said Transwood plans to pay 7.1 million pounds ($13.1 million) to buy Quorum and its British parent, VPI Group PLC.
