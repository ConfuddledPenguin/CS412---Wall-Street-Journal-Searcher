W.R. Berkley Faces Charge of $10 Million Following Arbitration

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/07/90

GREENWICH, Conn. -- W.R. Berkley Corp., an insurance holding company, said it could face a third-quarter charge of $10 million, or 84 cents a share, following arbitration of a reinsurance dispute. The potential charge stems from an unfavorable ruling by an arbitration panel involving a dispute between a Berkley subsidiary and several of its reinsurers. The reinsurers had refused to cover losses paid by Admiral Insurance Co., a property-casualty unit of Berkley, on bonds that guaranteed the performance of real-estate limited partnerships.

The arbitration panel's ruling requires the reinsurers to cover some of the losses, but far less than Admiral had requested. The lower-than-expected award would require Berkley to write down amounts previously reflected as reinsurance receivables.

"We are disappointed in the award and are presently considering our alternatives, including the possibility of appeal or other legal action," said Robert S. Gorin, senior vice president and general counsel of Berkley.

In the third quarter of 1989, Berkley -- whose subsidiaries operate in regional property and casualty insurance, specialty lines of insurance and insurance service operations -- reported earnings of $12.1 million, or $1.03 a share.

Berkeley shares closed yesterday at $36, down $2, in national over-the-counter trading.
