Big Board to Fine U.S. Unit Of Nomura Securities Co.

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/07/90

NEW YORK -- The U.S. subsidiary of Nomura Securities Co. will be fined $180,000 and censured by the New York Stock Exchange for alleged violations of exchange rules, according to people familiar with the case. A hearing is scheduled for today to examine a settlement surrounding allegations that Nomura Securities International Inc., the U.S. unit of the giant Japanese securities firm in 1987 violated net capital requirements. These involve situations where a firm allows its net capital to fall below levels prescribed by the exchange.

People familiar with the case said the violations were relatively small, involving several hundred thousand dollars.

As in most disciplinary cases, Nomura will settle without admitting or denying wrongdoing, according to individuals familiar with the case. A Nomura spokesman declined to comment; so did an exchange spokeswoman.

While not the biggest fine, the penalty would be one of the steepest leveled against a Big Board member firm. Since 1987, the exchange and other "self-regulators" of the markets have been under increased pressure to impose stiffer fines for violators of market rules.

Last month, the New York Stock Exchange censured American Express Co.'s Shearson Lehman Brothers Inc. unit and fined it a record $500,000 in a case involving Shearson's trading of E.F. Hutton & Co. stock during 1986 takeover talks.
