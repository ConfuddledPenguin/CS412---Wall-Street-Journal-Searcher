International: Lufthansa to Press Its Bid To Buy Stake in Interflug

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/07/90

BRUSSELS -- Lufthansa German Airlines said it intends to press its case for buying 26% of the East German state-owned carrier Interflug, despite the West German Federal Cartel Office's warning that the bid will be rejected. The antitrust agency has noted that Lufthansa, 51%-owned by the state, already dominates the West German domestic air market and the market from West Germany to European destinations. A Lufthansa-Interflug combination would control 53% of these markets.

But Lufthansa said Interflug is too small to affect Lufthansa's market position greatly. Interflug has drastically trimmed its flight program recently. Moreover, Lufthansa has no landing rights at airports in East or West Berlin. A value hasn't been assigned to the 26% Interflug stake Lufthansa wants to buy.

"We don't understand why the cartel office objects," Lufthansa said. "But their decision isn't final. We have until mid-August to write them and state our intentions." Under West German law, any stake exceeding 25% is considered a merger. The antitrust agency said Lufthansa could challenge a ban in court. Or Lufthansa could ask the West German Economics Ministry to overrule such a decision, in which case the airline would have to prove that the proposed acquisition is for the common economic good.

Interflug said it will back Lufthansa's effort to win approval for the deal, though Interflug itself can't respond to the West German cartel agency.
