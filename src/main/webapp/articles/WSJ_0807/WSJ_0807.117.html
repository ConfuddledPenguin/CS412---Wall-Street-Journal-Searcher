Technology Brief -- Tandy Corp.: Electronics Retailer Unveils 6-Pound Portable Computer

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/07/90

Tandy Corp. introduced a six-pound portable computer, equipped with hard-disk and floppy-disk drives and priced at $1,999, the first in what could become a deluge of "notebook" computers. The Tandy 1500HD is lighter and more powerful than the 1100FD model, introduced in September by the Fort Worth, Tex.-based electronics retailer. Since then, the market for notebook computers, slim enough to slip into a briefcase but powerful enough to run popular business software, has become crowded and competitive.

The Tandy 1500HD raises the competitive stakes, analysts believe. "It's a pretty aggressive price," said Liz Buyer, a vice president of Needham & Co.

The computer comes equipped with a 20-megabyte hard-disk drive and a 1.44 megabyte floppy drive. It uses a NEC V-20 microprocessor, a faster version of the Intel 8086 chip.

Tandy said the computer will be available in Radio Shack stores in September.
