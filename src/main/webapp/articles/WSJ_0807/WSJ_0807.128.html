Judge in Menendez Case Allows Tapes as Evidence

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/07/90

SANTA MONICA, Calif. -- A psychologist's taped sessions with the sons of slain Live Entertainment Inc. Chairman Jose Menendez will be admitted as evidence in the sons' murder trial, a state court ruled here. The decision by Judge James Albracht, which may unveil a key piece of evidence in the celebrated case, overrode conventional confidentiality of doctor-patient communications. The judge accepted prosecutors' arguments that Lyle and Erik Menendez, accused of murdering their parents, allegedly threatened psychologist Leon Jerome Oziel, thereby making the tapes a proper subject for court review.

A lawyer for Lyle Menendez said both brothers plan to appeal the ruling.

Judge Albracht said his written opinion in the matter would remain sealed until Aug. 15. However, he summarized his view that none of the remarks were privileged because the psychologist had reason to believe that the brothers were a threat -- a threat that should be disclosed to avoid the danger.

Jose Menendez and his wife, Kitty, were shot Aug. 20 in their Beverly Hills, Calif. Their sons alerted police, saying they had discovered the bodies on their return from the movies.

However, the sons were charged with the murders in March, and the tapes played a prominent role when Los Angeles County district attorney Ira Reiner said they contained "very important" evidence supporting allegations that the brothers killed their parents to gain access to a $14 million estate.
