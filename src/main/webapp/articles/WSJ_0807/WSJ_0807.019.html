Four Settle Charges Of Stock Exchange

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/07/90

NEW YORK -- The American Stock Exchange disciplined three former employees of Interstate Securities Inc. and one of Advest Group Inc. for alleged rule violations. The four settled the Amex charges without admitting or denying wrongdoing.

The former employees at Interstate, which is now Interstate/Johnson Lane, Charlotte, N.C., are Jeffrey Siegel of New York and Clifford Butcher and Brenda Scott of Tampa, Fla. The former employee of Advest Group, Hartford, Conn., is Matthew Kulkin of Boston.

Mr. Siegel was barred from association with Amex member firms for two years; Ms. Scott was fined $12,000; Mr. Butcher was suspended from certain options transactions for six months and fined $7,000; and Mr. Kulkin was fined $10,000.

Mr. Butcher declined to comment; the others couldn't be reached.
