Marketing & Media: WIC Agrees to Buy Media Properties In Western Canada

  • Author: Gary Lamphier
  • Date: 07/10/90

VANCOUVER, British Columbia -- Fast-growing WIC Western International Communications Ltd. said it agreed to acquire the media holdings of Alberta entrepreneur Dr. Charles Allard in a cash and stock transaction valued at about 157.4 million Canadian dollars (US$135.5 million). The proposed purchase would add two Alberta television stations, as well as western Canada's Superchannel pay-television service, and various production assets to WIC's existing stable of radio, television, and satellite communications operations.

"It is a major acquisition. It would make WIC one of the largest, if not the largest, private broadcasting concern in Canada," said Barry Gruman, a Montreal-based communications analyst with First Marathon Securities Ltd.

The purchase, which requires regulatory approval, would also bolster WIC's position as western Canada's dominant broadcaster, which it secured last year with its C$200 million purchase of broadcasting assets from Toronto's Maclean Hunter Ltd.

WIC agreed to acquire Edmonton, Alberta, television station CITV, and Red Deer, Alberta, television station CKRD, for C$90.8 million, as well as various related production and other assets for C$19.1 million.

WIC also agreed to acquire, for about 3.7 million new WIC Class B nonvoting common shares, all of the shares of Superchannel's parent, closely held Allarcom Pay Television Ltd., that WIC didn't previously own. Under prior agreements, WIC had acquired 21% of Allarcom's common shares.

Mr. Allard, an Edmonton financier who built his communications holdings after successes in real estate, holds with his family a controlling stake in Superchannel and the Alberta television stations.

In Toronto Stock Exchange trading yesterday, WIC's Class B shares closed at C$13, up 12.5 Canadian cents, giving the stock component of the transaction an indicated value of C$47.5 million. Mr. Allard will hold an equity stake of about 22% in WIC once the transaction closes, a WIC executive said. Mr. Allard and his son will also become WIC directors.

The transaction, which isn't expected to close until at least the end of the year, would reunite WIC's expansion-minded president and chief executive, Douglas Holtby, with his former boss, Mr. Allard. Mr. Holtby joined WIC about a year ago after serving as president of Mr. Allard's broadcast operations.

During his tenure at Allarcom, Mr. Holtby helped it score some successes on the production side, most notably with SCTV, a comedy program produced by Edmonton station CITV that was subsequently picked up in the U.S. by the NBC network.
