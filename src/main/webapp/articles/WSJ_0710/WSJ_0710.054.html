International Brief -- Peugeot S.A.: Concern Would Be Satisfied With Stable 1990 Earnings

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/10/90

Peugeot S.A. Chairman Jacques Calvet said in Paris that the car maker would be satisfied if its 1990 earnings show little change or even a slight decline from the 10.30 billion francs ($1.86 billion) earned in 1989 after payments to minority interests. Mr. Calvet's statement, made to journalists, reiterated caution he voiced at the group's annual meeting two weeks ago, when he said he wouldn't be disappointed if consolidated cash flow for all of 1990 matched the 1989 total of 18.53 billion francs. He extended that caution to net profit, saying that a stable 1990 result would be "satisfactory."

The main uncertainty facing the sector this year has been concern about a demand slowdown after several years of brisk European sales that reached a record in 1989. Mr. Calvet was calm on this front, predicting that French car demand would rise 4% to 5% this year. He also reiterated his forecast that the European market would be little changed or even slightly higher than its 1989 performance.
