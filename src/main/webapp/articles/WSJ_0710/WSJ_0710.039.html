ART

National Gallery of Art, Washington, D.C. -- "The Sculpture of Indonesia" -- More than 130 objects from the eighth through 15th centuries, the classical period of Indonesian art. Works include Buddhist and Hindu stone sculpture, bronzes, small figural objects of gold and silver, and ceremonial objects. Of special interest is a life-size Buddha, one of 504 made for the largest Buddhist temple ever built, Borobudur. Fourth Street at Constitution Avenue NW, through Nov. 4. The show then travels to Houston's Museum of Fine Arts (Dec. 9, 1990-March 17, 1991); New York's Metropolitan Museum of Art (April 21-Aug. 18, 1991); and San Francisco's Asian Art Museum (Sept. 28, 1991-Jan. 5, 1992).

Art Institute of Chicago: "The Chicago Skyscraper: Selections From the Permanent Collection" -- More than 50 drawings and models document the development of the skyscraper in the Windy City. Included are drawings of Murphy/Jahn's Northwestern Terminal Tower (1976-87); early prints for Burnham and Root's Rookery; and fantasy drawings from the Chicago Architectural Club's 1983 competition to redesign the tops of Chicago skyscrapers. Michigan Avenue at Adams Street, July 23-Dec.31.

IBM Gallery of Science and Art, New York: "Rediscovering Pompeii" -- More than 200 frescoes, sculptures, vessels, games, articles of jewelry and everyday objects from first-century Pompeii. The exhibit also features more than 20 interactive computer programs enabling visitors to take an electronic "walk" through the city's buildings, and see a time-lapse sequence of the eruption of Mount Vesuvius in 79 A.D. 590 Madison Ave., July 12-Sept. 15.

Max Protetch Gallery, New York: "Alan Sonfist: Art of the Environment" is a 25-year overview of works by a site-specific artist who does not inflict hostile piles of rusting metal on squawking neighborhoods. The 20-some art works include bronze castings of endangered oaks done for a private estate outside Florence, and a series of collages showing the process of his art, which often involves the re-creation of historic landscapes. 560 Broadway, through July 27.

MUSIC/FESTIVAL

Santa Fe Opera's 34th season runs through Aug. 25 and features performances of Puccini's "La Boheme" (July 11, 20, 30, Aug. 2, 7, 14, 21 and 25); Mozart's "Cosi Fan Tutte" (July 13, 31, Aug. 6, 11, 15 and 24); Gluck's "Orfeo ed Euridice," with Marilyn Horne as Orfeo (July 14, 18, 27, Aug. 4, 8, 17 and 23); Strauss's "Ariadne Auf Naxos" (July 21, 25, Aug. 3, 9, 16 and 22); and East German composer Siegfried Matthus's "Judith," with Stephanie Sundine in the title role (July 28, Aug. 1, 10, 18). Santa Fe Opera Theater, Santa Fe, N.M. (505) 982-3855.

Santa Fe Chamber Music Festival's 18th season runs through Aug. 20. Highlights include: premiere performances of two commissions by Pulitzer Prize-winning composers Ellen Taaffe Zwilich and Ned Rorem; festival debut performances by the Moscow-based Shostakovich String Quartet quartet; a piano recital in honor of Aaron Copland's 90th birthday; and baroque compositions played on period instruments. St. Francis Auditorium. (505) 982-1890.

Festival d'Ete International de Quebec: The Italian coloratura mezzo-soprano Cecilia Bartoli makes her North American debut with a program of Mozart and Rossini arias, among them Sesto's aria from "La Clemenza di Tito," and the show-stopping "Tanti Affetti" from "La Donna del Lago." The Quebec City Symphonic Orchestra is conducted by Simon Streatfield. Grand Theatre de Quebec, July 12. (418) 692-4540.

Mostly Mozart Festival: Ms. Bartoli then travels to Lincoln Center to sing one of the master's concert arias, a tune from "Cosi fan Tutte," and "Tanti Affetti." Jean Pierre Rampal also appears on the program with a flute concerto by Pleyel. Gerard Schwarz conducts. July 17, 18.

Mozart's comic one acter, "The Impresario," features Frances Ginsberg and Sally Wolf slugging it out as rival prima donnas and Vincent Cole as the frustrated producer. This Tuesday and Wednesday, with the Wednesday performance telecast on PBS. Alicia de Larrocha playing Mozart's Piano Concerto in C major, K. 503, rounds out the program. Avery Fisher Hall, New York. (212) 874-2424.
