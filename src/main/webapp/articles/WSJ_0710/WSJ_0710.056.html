International Brief -- BASF AG: West German Firm Studies Expanding Printing Lines

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/10/90

The West German chemicals group BASF AG said it's in negotiations with other suppliers over the possibility of expanding its synthetic printing plate business into a broader range of products for the printing industry. But a spokesman at the company's Ludwigshafen headquarters couldn't rule out the possibility that BASF might eventually decide to dispose of the printing plate lines altogether if it can't reach an accord over broadening its scale of operations. "Everything is possible," he remarked.

Monday's report of the plan to seek partners in the printing sector follows news last week that BASF wants to dispose of two of its other fringe businesses, one printing-related. BASF said it is looking for buyers for its Fritzsche Dodge Olcott flavors and fragrances business in the U.S., as well as its North American printing ink operations. The company had said it wanted to dispose of those operations because they aren't core businesses.
