Technology Brief -- Apple Computer Inc.: Low-Cost Line of Printers Is Introduced for Macintosh

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/10/90

In an effort to stem erosion of its market share in laser printers, Apple Computer Inc., Cupertino, Calif., unveiled a new low-priced line of printers for its Macintosh computer. Apple's new low-end machine, the Personal LaserWriter SC, is slower than older models but is priced at $1,999, 29% less than Apple's previous lowest-priced model. Apple's new line also includes the full-featured NT model, which lists for $3,299, compared with $4,599 for Apple's previous full-featured model.

The SC machine is designed to plug into a single Macintosh, while the NT model can be connected to several machines, including non-Apple computers, at once. Both machines print about four pages a minute, compared with eight pages per minute on the older machines.

Apple has been losing market share in the laser-printer business to Hewlett-Packard Co., which has targeted Apple's customers by undercutting Apple's printer prices. Apple figures that its lower prices will attract new users who don't need to print in high volumes.
