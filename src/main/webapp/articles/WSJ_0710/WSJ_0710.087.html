Business Brief -- Mack Trucks Inc.: Renault's Proposed Buy-Out Draws Shareholder Lawsuit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/10/90

Three shareholders of Mack Trucks Inc. filed a lawsuit alleging that the proposed $99-million buy-out of the company by Renault Vehicules Industriels is unfair. Renault, a unit of state-owned Regie Nationale des Usines Renault, announced earlier that it plans to start a $6-a-share tender offer Thursday for the 55% of Mack it doesn't own. Filed in federal court in Philadelphia, the suit alleges that an announcement of Mack's poor financial outlook on the same day was an effort "to lull {investors} into having a belief that the most that they were entitled to" was the Renault offer.

The suit, which names Mack and Renault as defendants, asks the court to order Renault to divest itself of any benefits it may obtain from the alleged scheme. It seeks class-action status. The plaintiffs are Barbara Zarowitz, Lepow Equities and Peter Stuyvesant Ltd.

A spokesman for Mack, based in Allentown, Pa., declined to comment on the suit. Renault, based in Paris, couldn't be reached for comment last evening.
