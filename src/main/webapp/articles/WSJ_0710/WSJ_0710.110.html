Cross-Border Investments Soared in '89, Study Says

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 07/10/90

Net investment in the stocks of one country's companies by investors in another country soared to a record $92.3 billion last year, nearly triple the previous high of $31.7 billion set in 1986, a Salomon Brothers Inc. study found. The firm said that gross cross-border equity investment climbed to a record $1.6 trillion last year -- or 14.5% of world stock turnover -- from $1.2 trillion a year earlier. British money managers led the way, followed by those from the U.S. and Japan. Swiss portfolio managers ranked fourth.

These numbers mean that one stock transaction out of every seven world-wide involves a foreign investor. Ten years ago, that ratio was less than one in every 16, according to Salomon.

As of last year, the value of international shares held by foreign investors stood at $830 billion, or 7.2% of the aggregate value of world stock markets, up from $640 billion in 1988.

Still, Salomon said: "We believe the globalization of equity markets has significantly further to go." The firm estimated that the average investment portfolio contains less than 10% of international assets, compared to what Salomon contends should be an optimal level of 15%-20%.
