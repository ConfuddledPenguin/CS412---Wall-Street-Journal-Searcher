Who's News: Ballistic Recovery Systems Inc.

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/10/90

ST. PAUL, Minn. -- Ballistic Recovery Systems Inc., a maker of aviation safety and defense systems applications, said its 58-year-old chief executive officer, Robert J. Keyport, resigned to accept a post with another company. The company said Mr. Keyport had been hired to establish an operating plan designed to make it profitable.

Ballistic Recovery said that it named Joseph R. Little, 52-year-old general manager of its Paranetics division, to be interim chief executive officer, and that its board formed a search committee to select a successor within 60 to 90 days. The board indicated that Mr. Little is a candidate under consideration by the committee.
