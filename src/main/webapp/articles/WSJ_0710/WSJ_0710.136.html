Hughes Aircraft Chairman Denies Budget Allegations

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/10/90

LOS ANGELES -- Hughes Aircraft Co. Chairman Malcolm R. Currie denied that he improperly handled Pentagon budget secrets in the early 1980s, maintaining that such allegations are based on "innunendoes and hearsay." In response to a Wall Street Journal story last Friday about an ongoing federal grand jury probe of illegal gathering and swapping of Pentagon budget documents, Mr. Currie issued a letter to employees of Hughes, a unit of General Motors Corp. In the five-paragraph letter, Mr. Currie said, "I did not know or approve of the unlawful activities in question." The letter also said that prosecutors "advised us that I am not a target of this criminal investigation."

Further, he said, "I offered my testimony freely and voluntarily" to federal investigators and no civil lawsuit is "contemplated against Hughes or myself" by the government.

Mr. Currie declined to be interviewed for last Friday's Journal story. The company issued a brief comment saying that Mr. Currie "did not have any knowledge" of illegal conduct by a Hughes employee convicted last month of trafficking in bootlegged Pentagon documents.

The story said Mr. Currie had been under criminal investigation, and had been interviewed by federal investigators, but wasn't a target of the grand jury inquiry. Law enforcement officials in Washington couldn't be reached yesterday, but repeatedly have indicated that they haven't ruled out some sort of civil action against Mr. Currie and others.
