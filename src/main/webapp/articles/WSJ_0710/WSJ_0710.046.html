Business Brief -- Kuhlman Corp.: Plastics Line's Sale to Unit Of Belgian Firm Completed

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/10/90

Kuhlman Corp., Troy, Mich., said it completed the sale of its plastics operations to the U.S. subsidiary of Solvay & Cie., a Brussels-based chemical corporation, in a transaction valued at about $30.6 million plus the assumption of $14.5 million in debt and other liabilities. The deal leaves Kuhlman primarily a maker of electrical transformers. The Kuhlman Plastics Group made, among other things, plastic fuel tanks for General Motors Corp.'s Chevrolet Corsica and Beretta cars. But Kuhlman officials said the continuing slump in U.S. auto sales has been reducing the profitability of the plastics operations.

The April sale agreement called for Solvay America Inc. to pay about $24.4 million immediately, with about $6.3 million deferred over five years. The price had been subject to change upon the closing of the deal, but Kuhlman didn't disclose whether the payment was altered.

A Kuhlman spokesman said the company will elaborate on its future plans and uses for the proceeds from the sale in a forthcoming interim report.
