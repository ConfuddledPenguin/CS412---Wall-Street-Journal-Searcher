General Instrument Says Another Firm Seeks Data

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/10/90

NEW YORK -- General Instrument Corp. said a potential bidder has emerged that may be interested in topping a $1.42 billion, $44.50-a-share bid for the company by Forstmann, Little & Co. The cable TV equipment maker said an unnamed "financial entity" has expressed interest in obtaining information about the company and evaluating a possible transaction. But it said there aren't any negotiations under way.

In response to the disclosure, General Instrument stock rose 62.5 cents to $46.25 a share in composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange.

Although the stock is trading well above the Forstmann Little offer, some takeover stock traders are skeptical that a higher bid will emerge. These traders and some investment bankers say the company had already been offered informally for sale during the past year without attracting any other bids.

Documents filed with Forstmann Little's offer said Forstmann's advisers, Goldman, Sachs & Co. and Lazard Freres & Co., will share a fee of $8 million if the purchase is completed. General Instrument's adviser, Merrill Lynch & Co., stands to receive a fee of roughly $8 million based on the current offer and more if shareholders receive a higher price.
