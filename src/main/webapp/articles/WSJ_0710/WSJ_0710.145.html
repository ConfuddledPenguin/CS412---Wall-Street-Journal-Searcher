Coach Leatherwear Sues three Concerns Over Handbag Sales

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/10/90

NEW YORK -- Coach Leatherwear Co. has filed a lawsuit in federal court against Ann Taylor, a New York-based women's retail chain, and two other defendants for selling handbags that it says copy three of its best-known and recognized styles. In a lawsuit filed on May 21 in the Southern District of New York, Coach charged that the defendants' sale of allegedly imitation Coach bags constituted "false designations of origin and false and misleading descriptions" with the intention of "creating confusion and deception in the marketplace . . . and for the purpose of implying an association between these products sold by Ann Taylor and Coach."

Coach seeks a jury trial and damages "in excess of $500,000."

Neither Ann Taylor nor the other defendants returned phone calls seeking comment.

In court papers, Coach charged that Laura Leather Ltd., the manufacturer of the bags, made identical copies of the Coach handbags and sold them to Ann Taylor. Coach claims that since 1973 it has sold $400 million worth of handbags.

"There are millions of versions of Coach bags and Coach has never complained, but these are exact imitations, you can't tell the difference," said Daniel Ebenstein, the attorney representing Coach. He said that "Coach has received many complaints from Coach customers who believe that we are making special private-label products for Ann Taylor, which is reselling them for 30% less than Coach stores."

He said that since the suit was filed one of the defendants, Ron's Elegance Center, a New York retail shop, has agreed to stop selling the handbags.

"Ann Taylor told us that they have no intention of reordering the bags, but they are not willing to consent to an injuntion, therefore we are proceeding with our case," Mr. Ebenstein said.
