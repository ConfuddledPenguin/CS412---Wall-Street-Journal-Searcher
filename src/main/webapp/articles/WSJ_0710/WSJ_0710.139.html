CRT Group to Set Up Options Trading Firm With Mitsubishi Trust

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/10/90

CHICAGO -- In the latest combination of Chicago futures and options acumen and foreign financial clout, Chicago Research & Trading Group Ltd. said it agreed to form a trading joint venture with Mitsubishi Trust & Banking Corp., Japan's largest trust bank. The CRT Group joint venture will give the closely held Chicago trading company increased access to Japanese financial markets and Mitsubishi's corporate customer base, said Malcolm Hanney, CRT Group executive vice president. The joint venture, which is subject to regulatory approval in the U.S. and Japan, will emphasize off-exchange trading of currency and interest-rate management products, he said.

Late last month, the futures brokerage unit of Chicago-based Rodman & Renshaw Capital Group Inc. said it was forming a futures trading joint venture with Creditanstalt-Bankverein, the largest Austrian bank, to tap Central and Eastern European markets. And O'Connor & Associates, a closely held Chicago trading firm, formed a futures and options trading joint venture with Swiss Bank Corp. last December.

Chicago's futures and options exchanges are the world's most active, but their individual member firms have traditionally been relatively undercapitalized. To tap the growing global off-exchange markets in options and other so-called derivative financial products, trading firms are increasingly turning to banks or other deep-pocketed partners to provide the necessary credit backing.

Trading firms need to significantly boost their financial backing to participate in off-exchange transactions made directly with other financial institutions-transactions that don't have the benefit of an exchange clearinghouse, where members pool funds to assume the credit risk of every transaction.

CRT Group's primary dealer in government securities, CRT Government Securities Ltd., is expected to be incorporated into the joint venture, Mr. Hanney said.
