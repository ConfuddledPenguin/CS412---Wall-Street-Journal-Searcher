Pic `N' Save Suitor Appears to Retreat From Plans for Bid

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/10/90

LOS ANGELES -- Less than a week before the July 15 deadline set by Pic `N' Save Corp. to receive acquisition bids for the company, a partnership controlled by investor David H. Batchelder appeared to back away from earlier indications that it would submit a bid. Girard Partners Limited Partnership said in a filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission that such an acquisition would require additional equity beyond what its partners have committed, as well as debt financing. Girard said there can be no assurance that it will make an offer.

Girard also said that it will renew its proxy solicitation to gain control of Pic 'N' Save's board if the Dominguez, Calif.-based discount retailer hasn't entered into an agreement to sell itself by July 15. Pic 'N' Save and Girard declared a truce last month while management conducts its sale auction.

In the filing, Girard said that if its nominees are elected to Pic 'N' Save's board, they wouldn't expect to seek an immediate sale of the company, but would attempt to increase stockholder value through the management and operation of the company as an ongoing business.

Girard said it may decide to sell its stock and drop its pursuit of Pic 'N' Save if its proxy fight fails. However, Girard didn't rule out taking other steps, either alone or with other shareholders, to obtain control of the company. Girard holds a 6.7% stake in Pic 'N' Save, and Bill M. Thomas, a Girard nominee, holds an additional 4%.

A Pic 'N' Save spokesman declined to comment on the filing. In La Jolla, Calif., Mr. Batchelder didn't return telephone calls for elaboration on the partnership's intentions.

Pic 'N' Save shares closed yesterday at $11.75, off 25 cents.
