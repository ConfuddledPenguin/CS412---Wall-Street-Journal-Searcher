Business Brief -- Wal-Mart Stores Inc.: Letter of Intent Is Signed For $28 Million Acquisition

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/10/90

Wal-Mart Stores Inc., Bentonville, Ark., said it signed a letter of intent to acquire Western Merchandisers Inc., Amarillo, Texas, in exchange for $28 million of Wal-Mart common stock. Under terms of the transaction, Western Merchandisers will spin off its retail book, music and video businesses currently handled by a host of companies, including Hastings Books & Records, into a company named Hastings Books & Records Inc. The spinoff will leave the rack jobbing, OneStop and warehouse operations as Western Merchandisers' sole operations. Wal-Mart said Western Merchandisers will eventually become a unit.

The companies said shareholders will be given the option of retaining their shares in Western Merchandisers and ultimately exchanging them for Wal-Mart common stock, for Hastings, or for a combination of the two.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading, Wal-Mart rose $1.50 to $32.75.
