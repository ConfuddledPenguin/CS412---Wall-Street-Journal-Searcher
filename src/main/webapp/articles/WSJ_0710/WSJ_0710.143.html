Monday's Markets: Bonds Sag Again As Stocks Rise, Dollar Is Mixed

  • Author: Christopher Winans
  • Date: 07/10/90

NEW YORK -- Bond prices continued to sag over Friday's unexpectedly strong employment report and concerns about how well the market will absorb this week's heavy load of government offerings. Stocks drifted higher in thin trading. The Dow Jones Industrial Average added 9.16 points to close at 2914.11. The dollar was mixed, but surprisingly stable, considering all the focus on the ebullient British pound.

Long-term U.S. Treasury bond prices fell nearly half a point, or $5 for each $1,000 of face amount. But trading was light as investors waited to see how today's Resolution Funding Corp. auction of $5 billion of 30-year bonds goes. Adding to the supply tomorrow will be the government's sale of $8 billion of seven-year notes.

"Supply is weighing heavily on people's minds," said Michael Rosen, an analyst at First Interstate Bank, Los Angeles.

In the stock market, investors are nervously awaiting the onslaught of second-quarter earnings reports and watching how the bond market responds to its new supply. Hugh Johnson, chief investment officer at First Albany Corp., said, "Nobody wants to be a hero and step up to the plate before we see those earnings reports."

In major market action:

Stock prices rose on volume of 119.4 million shares in the New York Stock Exchange trading. Advancing issues edged out decliners, 764-673.

Bond prices fell. The yield on the Treasury's benchmark 30-year bond rose to 8.54%.

The dollar ended mixed. In late New York trading, the currency was quoted at 1.6530 marks and 151.15 yen, compared with 1.6520 marks and 151.70 yen Friday.
