Business Brief -- Potomac Electric Power Co.: Utility Wins Rate Increase Of One-Fourth Its Request

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/10/90

Regulators in the District of Columbia granted Potomac Electric Power Co. a rate increase of $9.5 million, about one-fourth of the $38.5 million increase the utility requested last September. The Public Service Commission also cut the Washington utility's rate of return on equity to 12.35% from 13%.

The commission said it denied most of Potomac Electric's requested increase because the company has not been aggressive enough in encouraging customers to conserve electricity. The company recently began a five-year program to encourage conservation through $90 million in customer rebates and incentives.

A spokeswoman for the utility said the 1.6% increase is "totally inadequate to meet the company's needs." The spokeswoman noted that the commission has ordered rate decreases of about $50 million since 1984 for tax and other reasons.

The rate increase, the company's first since 1984, was intended to cover "all those costs that have gone up" because of inflation, the spokeswoman said.

The company said it will consider its options, including an appeal, while waiting for the commission's final order, expected in about two weeks.
