AT&T Network Unit Says 1,584 Employees Accept Plan to Retire

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/10/90

BASKING RIDGE, N.J. -- American Telephone & Telegraph Co. said 1,584 employees in its Network Services division agreed to retire this year, under a pension plan that was sweetened to induce retirements. These employees will count toward the division's goal of cutting 6,000 jobs this year, a division spokesman said.

AT&T is determining how many targeted job cuts have been realized as a result of attrition, transfers and promotions, the spokesman said. The division expects to announce, possibly as early as today, how many "surplus" employees still exist and thus are likely to be laid off.

A spokesman for the New York-based parent company said AT&T as a whole seeks to eliminate 13,000 jobs this year. The company initially estimated it would eliminate 10,000 to 12,000 jobs, but later revised the figure.

The spokesman wasn't able to say how many of the 13,000 targeted jobs have been eliminated so far. But he said about 800 jobs were eliminated during the first quarter in the Business Communications Systems group.

AT&T has about 282,000 employees, including about 30,000 in the Network Services division.

While cutting jobs in many staff and technical categories, AT&T has been increasing employment in computer sciences, marketing and sales. A major theme is to increase revenue-producing jobs, while lowering costs through the use of new technology to eliminate behind-the-scenes workers, the spokesman said.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, AT&T shares closed at $38.25, up 12.5 cents.
