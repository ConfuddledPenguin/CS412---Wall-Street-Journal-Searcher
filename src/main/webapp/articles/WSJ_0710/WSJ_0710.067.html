Computer Hackers Plead Guilty in Case Involving BellSouth

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/10/90

ATLANTA -- Three men, part of a closely knit group of computer hackers calling themselves the Legion of Doom, pleaded guilty to conspiring to defraud BellSouth Corp. of computer information. In February, the three men, Franklin E. Darden Jr., 24 years old, of Norcross, Ga.; Adam E. Grant, 22, and Robert J. Riggs, 22, both of Atlanta, were indicted as participants in an alleged scheme to disrupt telephone service in the Southeast.

BellSouth, through two subsidiaries, oversees phone service in Alabama, Mississippi, Georgia, Tennessee, Kentucky, Louisiana, Florida and the Carolinas.

Yesterday, Mr. Darden and Mr. Riggs each pleaded guilty to one count of conspiring to commit computer fraud. Both face a maximum penalty of five years in prison and a $250,000 fine. Mr. Grant pleaded guilty to possessing 15 or more devices that provided access to BellSouth computers with intent to commit fraud. He faces up to 10 years in prison as well as a $250,000 fine.

Neither Mr. Riggs nor Mr. Grant could be reached for comment. Mr. Darden said he had agreed to cooperate with BellSouth in helping to secure the company's computer systems. Acknowledging that he was "more or less a hacker," Mr. Darden said his actions were simply a hobby at first. "I hope anybody that's hacking out there now hears this and stops," he added. Sentencing for all three men is set for Sept. 14.

A hacker is a person who attempts to gain unauthorized access to computer files in various systems.

The U.S. attorney in Atlanta, Joe Whitley, said yesterday that the three individuals belonged to the so-called Legion of Doom, a cadre of 15 computer hackers in Georgia, Texas, Michigan, Illinois, Florida, New York and other states.

Mr. Whitley said the group disrupted the telecommunications industry, monitored private telephone lines, stole proprietary information, stole and modified credit information, fraudulently obtained property from various companies, and disseminated information that allowed other computer hackers to enter BellSouth and other computer systems.
