Business Brief -- Flight International Group Inc.: Company Settles 3 Lawsuits For Stock, Cash and Notes

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/10/90

Flight International Group Inc. said three shareholder lawsuits against the company, officers and directors, and its accounting firm were settled for cash, stock, and notes valued at $5.25 million. Flight International, based in Newport News, Va., is an aviation-services company.

Under the settlement, Flight International will issue 340,000 new shares of common stock to shareholders who purchased company stock between June 21, 1988, and Sept. 29, 1989. Flight International has about 3.7 million shares outstanding.

The class-action suits alleged that the company filed inaccurate financial statements for fiscal 1988 and fiscal 1989. In March, the company restated its fiscal 1989 earnings to a loss of $3.8 million, or $1.29 a share, from previously reported net income of $3.0 million, or $1.01 a share.

Flight International valued its portion of the settlement at about $1 million, based on an assumed stock value of about $3 a share. The company's stock was delisted by NASDAQ several months ago and does not actively trade.

The remaining defendants contributed the rest of the settlement, the company said. Those defendants include Douglas G. Matthews, the company's former chairman; John W. Orange Jr., former chief financial officer; and Deloitte & Touche, the company's independent accounting firm during the alleged misstatements.
