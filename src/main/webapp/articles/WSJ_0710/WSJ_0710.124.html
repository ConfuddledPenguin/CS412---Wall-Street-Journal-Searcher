Business Brief -- National Intergroup Inc.: Firm Raises Purchase Price For Permian Partners L.P.

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/10/90

National Intergroup Inc., Pittsburgh, said it agreed to raise to $3.85 the price it will pay for each cumulative convertible preference unit of Permian Partners L.P. The increased purchase price, up from the original $3.75 offer, is part of its settlement of litigation related to its tender offer for Permian.

National Intergroup began a tender offer in May, but the purchase price was subject to reduction on a per-unit basis to reflect any distributions on the preference units.

Under the settlement, National Intergroup said it won't reduce the price to reflect the regular quarterly distribution for the quarter ended June 30.

The settlement is subject to approval by the Delaware Court of Chancery.

National Intergroup is an oil transportation and aluminum and steel fabrication company. Permian Partners is a limited partnership involved in oil and gas gathering and marketing.
