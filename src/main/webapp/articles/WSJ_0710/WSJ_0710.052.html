ConAgra Says U.S. Asked for More Data On Beatrice Purchase

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/10/90

OMAHA, Neb. -- ConAgra Inc. said federal antitrust regulators requested additional information about the company's planned $1.34 billion cash and stock purchase of Beatrice Co. In early June, ConAgra agreed to acquire Beatrice from Kohlberg Kravis Roberts & Co., which had taken the company private in 1986. At that time, the transaction had been expected to close in about a month.

ConAgra said the request, under Hart-Scott-Rodino regulations, extends the waiting period for completion of the transaction until 20 days after the Federal Trade Commission's request has been fully met.

Both food companies received the request, and in a joint statement noted that it isn't unusual for the FTC to seek additional information in such a transaction.

Neither company would disclose what data the agency are seeking, and the FTC wouldn't comment.

John McMillin, an analyst with Prudential-Bache Securities Inc., pointed out that "normally, big deals get more attention" from regulators. The analyst added that there isn't a "tremendous amount of overlap between" the two companies.

Mr. McMillin noted, as have others, that ConAgra and Beatrice are both major forces in the prepared-meats business, and that the FTC may be scrutinizing the antitrust implications in that field.

ConAgra's chairman and chief executive officer, Charles M. Harper, has said he wasn't worried about antitrust problems, saying "we think we're below the threshold of their {regulators'} concern level."

The companies said they plan a full and prompt response to the FTC request.
