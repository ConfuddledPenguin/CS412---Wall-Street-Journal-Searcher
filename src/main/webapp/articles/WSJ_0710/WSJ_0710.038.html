Italy's Car Sales Fell in June

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/10/90

MILAN -- Car sales in Italy fell 5.7% in June, but rose 1.8% in the first half, compared with year-earlier levels, Anfia, the national association of car manufacturers, said. June car sales declined to 208,790 from 221,492, and first-half sales increased to 1,363,061 from 1,338,535.

Domestic car makers, led by Fiat S.p.A., which produces Fiat, Lancia and Alfa Romeo brands, accounted for 52.64% of the domestic auto market in June and 54.43% in the first six months. Anfia said the Fiat Uno remained the most popular on the domestic market, with 33,231 sold in June and 244,317 in the first six months. Fiat's Tipo took second place, with 17,315 sold in June and 126,996 in the first half.

Among foreign brands, Volkswagen AG of West Germany led June sales with 10,967 cars sold to the Italian market. But Ford Motor Co., Dearborn, Mich., led the foreign brands in the first half, when it sold 73,074 Fiesta models in Italy.
