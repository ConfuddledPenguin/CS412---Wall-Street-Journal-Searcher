Philadelphia Market Plans To Extend Trading Hours

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/10/90

PHILADELPHIA -- The Philadelphia Stock Exchange said it plans to extend its trading day in foreign-currency options, beginning Sept. 16. The exchange said its current 14-hour trading day will be stretched to 19.5 hours during months of daylight-saving time, to coincide with afternoon trading in the Far East and in Pacific Rim countries. In fall and winter months, the trading day will become 20.5 hours.

New hours will run from 7 p.m. to 2:30 p.m. EDT the following day. Current hours run from 7 p.m. to 11 p.m. EDT and from 4:30 a.m. to 2:30 p.m. EDT. During fall and winter, trading will begin at 6 p.m. EST.

In September 1987, the exchange introduced an evening trading session for foreign-currency options to accommodate increased demand in the Far East. In January 1989, the exchange added an early-morning session to accommodate the European market.

Total daily volume in currency-option contracts traded on the exchange has grown to represent about $2 billion in underlying value.
