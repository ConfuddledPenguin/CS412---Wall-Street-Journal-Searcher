Business Brief -- Lazare Kaplan International Inc.: Jewelry Marketer Says Net Rose 3.3% in Its 4th Period

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/10/90

Lazare Kaplan International Inc., a New York-based diamonds and jewelry marketer, said fiscal fourth-quarter earnings rose 3.3% on a 23% sales gain. A spokesman wasn't available to elaborate on results for the quarter ended May 31. But in a news release explaining results for the year, Lazare Kaplan pointed to increases of 29% in sales of polished diamonds and 47% in sales of rough diamonds. For fiscal 1990, earnings leaped 90% as sales climbed 36%.

Fourth-quarter net income rose to $2.4 million, or 74 cents a share, from $2.3 million, or 72 cents a share, a year earlier. Net for each period includes tax-loss carry-forwards of $768,000 and $719,000, respectively. Sales rose to $34.3 million from $28 million.

For the year, net increased to $5.3 million, or $1.65 a share, from $2.8 million, or 88 cents a share, in fiscal 1989. Net for each period includes tax-loss carry-forwards of $1.7 million and $860,000, respectively. Sales advanced to $109.7 million from $80.5 million.
