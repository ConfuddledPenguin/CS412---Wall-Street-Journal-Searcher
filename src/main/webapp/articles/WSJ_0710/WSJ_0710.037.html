Continental Air Traffic Slips

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/10/90

HOUSTON -- Continental Airlines said its June traffic fell 6.8% to 3.4 billion revenue passenger miles from 3.65 billion a year earlier. A revenue passenger mile is one paying passenger flown one mile.

The carrier's load factor, or percentage of available seats occupied, fell in June to 62.2% from 67%. The year-earlier period included results of a $1 promotional ticket for children traveling with a parent.

For the first six months of 1990, traffic rose minimally to 18.65 billion revenue passenger miles from 18.62 billion a year earlier. Load factor rose to 59.4% from 59%.

The carrier is a unit of Continental Airlines Holdings Inc., formerly Texas Air Corp.
