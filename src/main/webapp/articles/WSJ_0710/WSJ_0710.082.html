Hong Kong's Domestic Exports

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/10/90

HONG KONG -- Hong Kong's domestic exports fell 6% in April from a year ago, while re-exports grew 10%, the government reported. The value of imports rose 6% during the month from a year ago, with fuels posting the biggest increase of 86%. Dollar figures weren't provided with the data.

Fuels re-exports, which more than doubled on the year, were the top growth category among goods transshipped through Hong Kong.

Domestic exports of clothing and textiles, the largest local export sector, edged down 4% and 2% respectively, from the previous year. Radios, which plummeted 44%, and domestic electrical appliances, which plunged 36%, were the export sectors hardest hit by the downturn.
