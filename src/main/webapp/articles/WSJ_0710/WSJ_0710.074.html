France's Largest Retailer Signs Trump Tower Lease

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/10/90

NEW YORK -- Donald Trump signed France's largest retailer, Galeries Lafayette, to a lease in Trump Tower, his building on Fifth Avenue here. The Galeries Lafayette store, which caters to an affluent clientele, will replace Bonwit Teller, whose lease was purchased by Mr. Trump in April for $5 million. Bonwit Teller had paid more than $4 million a year.

Under the lease terms, Galeries Lafayette, which will occupy about 80,000 square feet of space, will pay about $5 million a year, or $62.50 a square foot, for the first six years.

In years seven through 10, the store will pay $8 million a year, and it will pay $9 million a year in years 11 through 16, according to a person familiar with the transaction. Also, Mr. Trump will receive a "special fee" of about $2 million a year for five years.

The deal is considered positive news for Mr. Trump, but it's not expected to alter his debt troubles. Mr. Trump is currently restructuring $2 billion in loans from his banks, and he must still service $1.3 billion in casino debt.

Last month, he received an additional $20 million from his banks, as part of an eventual $65 million package that would allow him to defer interest on $800 million of his bank debt.
