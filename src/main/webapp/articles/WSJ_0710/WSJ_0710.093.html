General Signal Agrees To Sell Stepper Unit To Employee Group

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/10/90

STAMFORD, Conn. -- General Signal Corp. said it agreed to sell its Ultratech Stepper unit to an employee group in a cash and paper transaction in which it will retain a minority interest. Precise terms weren't disclosed. General Signal, a maker of process controls, electrical equipment and semiconductors, said Ultratech Stepper accounted for about $40 million of its 1989 sales of $1.92 billion. A stepper is a photolithography tool used to put circuit designs on semiconductors.

C. Woodrow Rea, a general partner in New Enterprise Associates, a San Francisco-based venture capital firm and a former president of Ultratech, heads the employee group. Under the pact, General Signal will provide "significant financing."

General Signal had planned to combine Ultratech, based in San Francisco, with General Signal's GCA Stepper unit, which has about twice the sales of Ultratech. The agreement to sell Ultratech, with about 300 employees, "was a more attractive alternative to the risks associated" with the merger plan, a General Signal official said.

The transaction will allow General Signal "to focus its research and marketing efforts" on the world-wide market for GCA Stepper's technology.
