J.W. Gant Is Fined For Alleged Misdeeds In Penny-Stock Sales

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/10/90

NEW YORK -- The National Association of Securities Dealers fined J.W. Gant & Associates Inc., an Englewood, Colo., brokerage firm, $30,000 and ordered it and two employees to return nearly $196,000 to customers because of alleged overcharges for securities. Without admitting or denying wrongdoing, the firm; Salvatore A. Venezia, a principal and vice president at Gant; and James S. Gad, a representative and trader at the firm, consented to the sanctions.

The NASD, the self-regulatory agency for the over-the-counter market, charged that Gant charged excessive markups for the initial public penny-stock offering of what the agency identified only as a Colorado producer of a soy-based, non-dairy, frozen dessert.

The initial offering consisted of units priced at 10 cents a piece; each unit included a common share plus a warrant.

The firm "dominated and controlled the market" for the securities and used that position to sell the securities "at markups ranging from 11% to 184% over the prevailing market price," the NASD said.

Mr. Gad declined to comment; Mr. Venezia couldn't be reached. The NASD also suspended both Messrs. Gad and Venezia for 15 days.

The firm was suspended for six months from making markets in and executing transactions as a principal in securities that aren't traded on the NASD's Automated Quotation System, unless some part of the securities is traded on Nasdaq.

Gant previously was the subject of a disciplinary proceeding; the NASD fined the firm $2,500 for failing to maintain minimum net capital.
