Analyst Is Accused Of Violations In SEC Lawsuit

  • Author: Wade Lambert And William Power
  • Date: 07/10/90

NEW YORK -- The Securities and Exchange Commission filed a civil lawsuit in federal court in Manhattan alleging that Peter E. Butler, currently a securities analyst at First Boston Corp., violated securities laws in recommending the stock of Memory Metals Inc. in 1985 and 1986. Mr. Butler, a vice president at First Boston, a unit of CS First Boston Inc., was a vice president in the research department of PaineWebber Inc. until about January 1987. A First Boston spokesman said the suit pertains to Mr. Butler's actions while at PaineWebber and that First Boston isn't a party to it.

Warren Colodner, an attorney for Mr. Butler, said, "Mr. Butler cooperated fully in the SEC investigation and he believes he did nothing wrong." Mr. Colodner said the SEC began its investigation in 1986.

PaineWebber declined comment.

In the lawsuit, the SEC seeks a court order barring Mr. Butler from violating securities laws. It also asks the court to order him to turn over all the profits from the alleged wrongdoing involving the stock of Memory Metals, which later changed its name to Memry Corp.

The SEC said Memory Metals had only nominal sales and sustained losses during the period of alleged wrongdoing. The agency alleges that Mr. Butler misled investors and committed fraud to encourage the sale of Memory Metals securities. The SEC also alleged that Mr. Butler had bought Memory Metals stock for his own account and was selling his shares while recommending that others buy.

The SEC said that Mr. Butler made predictions as to specific price increases and other business developments "without a reasonable basis and while knowing, or reckless and not knowing, that such statements were materially false or misleading."

The SEC also said that Mr. Butler failed to disclose information about the risk of investing in Memory Metals and problems at the company.

The First Boston spokesman said the firm has been aware of the SEC investigation for several years. "Peter Butler is an employee in good standing," the spokesman said. "We have reviewed the allegations in depth with both Mr. Butler and his outside counsel. Mr. Butler denies the allegations and we support his decision to defend himself."
