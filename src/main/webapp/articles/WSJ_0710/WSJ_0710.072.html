Ex-Financial Chief At Merrill Lynch Plans to Quit Firm

  • Author: William Power
  • Date: 07/10/90

NEW YORK -- Courtney F. Jones, Merrill Lynch & Co.'s chief financial officer until last year, said he is leaving the big securities firm by year-end. Mr. Jones, 50 years old, is now a senior investment banker and a Merrill director. He gave up the chief financial officer's position a year ago after four years on the job.

Looking back, Mr. Jones said that when he resigned as chief financial officer, he thought Merrill Lynch was "in pretty good shape." But instead, he said, "The world really died." Mr. Jones's successor, Thomas H. Patrick, has gone on to spearhead Merrill's huge retrenchment efforts that gave it a net loss of $213.4 million last year and has included thousands of layoffs.

Merrill Lynch Chairman William Schreyer and President Daniel Tully said in a statement that they were sorry to see Mr. Jones go, but that they understood his desire to "seek new professional challenges."

Mr. Jones said he's looking for a chief operating officer position, or something similar, on or off Wall Street. He said he enjoys working at big, public firms like Merrill or his previous employer for 23 years, General Motors Corp. But Mr. Jones said he is through being a chief financial officer. "It's fundamentally an advisory role," he said, rather than an "operating officer role, where the job is more directing strategy of the firm."

As for Merrill and its recent troubles, Mr. Jones said he is optimistic about the firm's future despite the overall slowdown in investment banking and brokerage.

"Some parts of our business are very much under fire," Mr. Jones said. "But all in all, I think Merrill Lynch is very well positioned for the future; it just appears we've got a ways to go to get through this period of softness."
