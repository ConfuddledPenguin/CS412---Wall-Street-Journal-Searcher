International Brief -- Legal & General Group PLC: Victory Reinsurance Unit's Sale to Dutch Firm Is Set

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/10/90

Legal & General Group PLC of Britain, an insurer, said it agreed to sell its Victory Reinsurance Ltd. unit to Netherlands Reinsurance Group, 51%-owned by Nationale Nederlanden N.V., one of the world's largest insurance companies, in a deal valued at #140 million ($249.9 million). Legal & General will receive #122 million on completion of the sale and will keep an additional #18 million related to four Victory subsidiaries that it will retain, the company said. It said the sale is part of its strategy to concentrate on its core businesses of life insurance and pensions, British general insurance and investments.

Victory businesses, excluding the ones being retained by Legal & General, accounted for #15.9 million of Legal & General's #142.3 million in pretax profit last year. Victory's principal operations are in Britain, Australia and Canada.

Legal & General concluded that Victory needed to be "considerably larger if it were to compete effectively over the longer term with the major international reinsurers," the official said, noting that reinsurance is capital-intensive.
