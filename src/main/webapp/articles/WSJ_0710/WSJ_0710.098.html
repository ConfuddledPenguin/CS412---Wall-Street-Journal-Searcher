Interco Reports Loss For Fiscal 1st Period Totaling $41.3 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/10/90

ST. LOUIS -- Interco Inc. reported a net loss of $41.3 million for its fiscal first quarter, but the widening deficit isn't likely to affect the company's talks with bondholders about restructuring almost $900 million in debt. Interco is a furniture and shoe maker that borrowed heavily in 1988 to fend off a hostile takeover.

Rothschild Inc., financial adviser to bondholders who have been grumbling about Interco's debt restructuring plan since it was announced in May, presented a counterproposal last week in separate meetings with the company, its bankers and its medium-term note holders. Details of the bondholders' plan couldn't be learned. But holders have said they don't like the company's plan because it relies heavily on issuing new common stock, instead of interest-paying bonds. They also have suggested that the company's bank lenders should make more concessions.

Wilbur Ross, a Rothschild senior managing director, said only that the counterproposal is "more favorable" to bondholders. "The real issue is what's a fair distribution of values here and that's what we tried to address in the counterproposal," he said.

Mr. Ross added that the first-quarter loss was in line with expectations and wouldn't affect the discussions. He said the bondholders expect to receive a response in the next few days.

Interco said only that the talks with bondholders "have been constructive."

For the quarter ended May 31, Interco's net loss widened to $41.3 million, or $1.64 a share, from $31.6 million, or $1.29 a share. The company said its Converse and Florsheim shoe operations earned more than a year ago, but its furniture business was soft.

Lower interest expenses, however, helped narrow the loss from continuing operations to $34.1 million, or $1.46 a share, from $41.4 million, or $1.54 a share, a year earlier.

Sales from continuing operations declined 24% to $357.9 million from $468.7 million.

Nevertheless, Richard B. Loynd, Interco's chairman, predicted that full-year earnings for the company's four core units -- Converse and Florsheim shoes and Broyhill and Lane furniture -- will exceed last year's results.
