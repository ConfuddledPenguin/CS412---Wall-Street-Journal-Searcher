Business Brief -- Pitney Bowes Inc.: Company Says Net Income Fell Slightly in 2nd Period

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/10/90

Pitney Bowes Inc. said it expects to report a slight drop in second-quarter net income from the year-earlier $61.8 million, or 78 cents a share. The Stamford, Conn., maker of postage meters, copiers, voice-processing systems and other products said it will report revenue growth of about 12% from $693.6 million a year earlier.

"However, our net income is weaker than expected because of continuing weak performance in our operations in Australia and Germany; because of lower net income at Monarch Marking Systems due to soft retail market conditions; and because of higher-than-anticipated costs of reorganizing our copier business," the company said.

The company said it doesn't expect to meet analysts' per-share earnings estimates of $3.50 to $3.70 for all of 1990. But the company said it does expect net income to exceed the $252.8 million, or $3.19 a share, earned in 1989, when revenue was $2.88 billion.
