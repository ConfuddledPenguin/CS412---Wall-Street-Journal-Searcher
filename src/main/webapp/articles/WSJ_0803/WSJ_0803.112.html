Simmons's Baroid Corp. Considers Spinoff Plan

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/03/90

HOUSTON -- Harold Simmons' holdings, like an amoeba, keep multiplying by dividing. One of the companies controlled by the Dallas financier, Baroid Corp., has said that it is considering a plan to separate into two publicly traded companies through a tax-free spinoff. Baroid itself was created in December 1988, when it was spun off from NL Industries, also controlled by Mr. Simmons through his Valhi Inc. holding company.

Baroid said that under the plan, which would be considered during a board meeting scheduled the week of Aug. 20, one company would consist of its existing petroleum services business while the other would comprise the company's Titanium Metals Corp. of America, its titanium metals business and certain mining operations.

In March, Baroid acquired the 30% of Titanium Metals that it didn't already own from Allegheny International Inc. for $50 million.

Robert J. Leidich, Baroid's vice president and general counsel, said the company is considering the spinoff because the two businesses are different. The company hopes that, separately, the two would be more highly valued by the market.

Valhi currently holds 43% of Baroid and about 63% of NL. In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange, Valhi closed yesterday at $12, down 25 cents.
