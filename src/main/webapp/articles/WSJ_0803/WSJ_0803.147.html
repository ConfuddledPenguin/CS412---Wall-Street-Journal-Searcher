Business Brief -- Hilton Hotels Corp.: Note Offering Is Suspended; Volatile Stock Market Cited

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/03/90

Hilton Hotels Corp., prompted by the volatile stock market, said it temporarily suspended its offering of liquid yield option notes due in 2005 with a principal amount of $650 million. Barron Hilton, the hotel and casino company's chairman, said Hilton's stock doesn't reflect the company's value. Hilton's stock has been pushed down in recent days by disappointing second-quarter earnings, concerns about the casino industry and a lackluster travel market.

The offering of zero-coupon notes would result in proceeds of about $270 million to the Beverly Hills, Calif., company, which Hilton had said it planned to use for general corporate purposes. The notes would be subordinated and convertible into Hilton common. A company spokesman said Hilton will proceed with the offering if the stock recovers sufficiently.
