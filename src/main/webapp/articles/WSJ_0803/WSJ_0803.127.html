Business Brief -- Continental Airlines Holdings Inc.: First Profit Is Reported For a Quarter Since 1986

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/03/90

Continental Airlines Holdings Inc., Houston, recorded its first profitable quarter since late 1986, but it would have posted another loss without special gains totaling $123.2 million at its Continental unit. In addition, the parent, which recently changed its name from Texas Air Corp., lost control of its Eastern Airlines unit early in the second quarter and no longer has to report that troubled carrier's losses.

For the quarter, Continental Airlines Holdings had net income of $98.3 million, or $2.21 a share, on revenue of $1.51 billion. In the year-ago quarter, it reported a net loss of $109.3 million, or a loss of $2.88 a share on revenue of $1.4 billion.

The company said it was excluding the results of Eastern Airlines from April 19 on, when a bankruptcy judge appointed a trustee to take control of the unit. As a result of an accounting change, then, the Continental Holdings' second-quarter results include only 18 days of Eastern results, in which the financially ailing carrier had a loss of $12.6 million on revenue of $115.9 million.

For the six months, the parent company reported a net loss of $8.8 million, or 51 cents a share, on revenue of $3.37 billion, compared with a net loss of $364.9 million on revenue of $3.28 billion.
