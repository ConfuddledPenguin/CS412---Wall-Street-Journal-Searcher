NLRB Sets Charges Against Nordstrom Over Union Dispute

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/03/90

SEATTLE -- The National Labor Relations Board said it will charge Nordstrom Inc. with several violations of federal labor law in its handling of a dispute with the United Food & Commercial Workers union if the retailer doesn't agree to settle the case by Aug. 10. The labor board already has charged Nordstrom with violations stemming from a yearlong dispute with United Food & Commercial Workers locals 1001 and 367. Contract talks with the two locals, whose pacts covering Seattle-area salespeople expired last year, broke down after the company proposed making union membership optional.

In the latest case, Larry McCargar, assistant to the labor board's regional director in Seattle, said the agency found merit to union allegations that Nordstrom illegally assisted some employees' efforts to decertify the union, disciplined and discriminated against employees who supported the union and promised employees additional benefits if the unions are decertified, among other things.

A spokesman for Nordstrom said the company is "not agreeing with what the NLRB has said, but we are going to move to settle."
