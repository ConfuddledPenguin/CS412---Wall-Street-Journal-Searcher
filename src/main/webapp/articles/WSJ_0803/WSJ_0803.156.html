Marketing & Media: Proposal Is Offered By Group to Heritage Entertainment Inc.

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/03/90

LOS ANGELES -- Heritage Entertainment Inc. said it received a proposal "for a capital investment" and "restructuring" from an investment group including Adelson-Baumgarten Productions, Braintree Partners and East-West Capital Associates. Heritage, a maker of low-budget films, wouldn't provide any details about the proposal or the potential investors. Heritage said that a committee of its board is considering the proposal.

In American Stock Exchange composite trading, Heritage shares closed yesterday at $1.625, off 25 cents. The announcement came before the start of trading.

On Wednesday, the company said that the committee recommended rejection of a proposal from Roger Corman, president of Concorde/New Horizons Corp., a closely held film production company specializing in low-budget horror movies, and his longtime associate, Brad Krevoy.

Heritage also said it tentatively agreed with City National Bank to extend the company's revolving credit agreement to Nov. 1 from last Tuesday. The commitment amount was reduced to $12.5 million from $15 million.

Last week, Heritage said it expected to post "significant losses" for its year ended March 31 because of its re-evaluation of certain unamortized film costs. In fiscal 1989, Heritage reported losses of $5.6 million.
