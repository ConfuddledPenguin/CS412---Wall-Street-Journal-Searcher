Delta Air, Pilots Agree On New Contract Terms

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 08/03/90

Delta Air Lines reached an agreement on a new contract with its pilots that will make them the most highly paid in the airline industry, a union official said. The agreement, which still must be ratified by Delta union leaders, shortens to three years the length of time new pilots are paid at lower wages than higher-seniority pilots. That hotly contested issue had been the main sticking point in contract talks, which had dragged on for 19 months.

The proposed contract also calls for wage increases of 12.5% over its three years, with newer pilots receiving on average a 40% increase over the life of the contract.

A spokesman for the airline said Delta was pleased to reach an agreement, but declined further comment.

Officials of the pilots union said they were satisfied with the new terms. "We have the best contract in the industry," said a union spokesman. The union hailed the contract as a significant step toward eliminating the lower "B-scale." Previously, pilots had to work at the lower wage rate for five years before jumping to the higher wages in effect for pilots with more seniority.
