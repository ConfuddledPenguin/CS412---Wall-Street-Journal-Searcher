Total Assets of U.S. Money-Market Funds Rise to $395.42 Billion

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/03/90

WASHINGTON -- Assets of the nation's 521 money-market funds increased $800.1 million, to $395.42 billion, in the week ended Wednesday, according to the Investment Company Institute, a trade association. Assets of 251 general-purpose funds rose $541.5 million to $136.44 billion, and assets of 102 broker-dealer funds rose $258.7 million to $161.01 billion. Assets of 168 institutional funds were virtually the same as the previous week, at $97.97 billion.

"Several consecutive weeks of solid inflows into money-market funds brought their assets to the highest level ever," said Jacob Dreyer, vice president and chief economist at the institute. He said the increases occurred "despite a gradual easing of short-term rates and some steepening of the yield curve," adding that the funds "succeeded in maintaining their competitive yield relative to alternative short-term" investments.

Mutual-fund assets were revised through July 4 to include eight new institutional funds with assets totaling $2.8 billion. For the previous week, the revised total of assets of funds is $394.62 billion.
