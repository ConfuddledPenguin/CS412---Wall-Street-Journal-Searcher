Program Trading Volume Rose on Big Board in Week

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/03/90

NEW YORK -- Program trading accounted for an average of 9.6% of New York Stock Exchange volume, or 16 million shares a day, in the week ended July 20. That was up from 7.9% the previous week, the Big Board said in its weekly report on the computer-aided trading strategies.

The most active program traders for the week were Morgan Stanley Group Inc. (17.4 million shares), General Electric Co.'s Kidder, Peabody Group Inc. (10.8 million shares), Goldman, Sachs & Co. (7.1 million shares), Susquehanna Investment Group (6.1 million shares) and First Boston Corp., a subsidairy of CS First Boston Inc. (5.5 million shares). These five firms accounted for 58% of all program trading on the exchange.

Program trading is the purchase or sale of a group of at least 15 and often hundreds of different stocks. Stock-index arbitrage, which involves stock purchases and sales with offsetting trades in futures and options, was led by Kidder Peabody, which accounted for 32% of the total.
