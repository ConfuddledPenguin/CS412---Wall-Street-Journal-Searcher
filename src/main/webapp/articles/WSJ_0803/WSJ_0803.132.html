Business Brief -- McDonnell Douglas Corp.: Firm Adopts Plan to Guard Against Hostile Takeovers

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/03/90

McDonnell Douglas Corp. adopted a shareholder rights plan to guard against unwanted takeovers. Although the aerospace giant said the plan "isn't being adopted in response to any effort to acquire control" of the company, its balance sheet has been weakened of late by cost overruns on several military programs and continued development expenditures on the MD-11 commercial jet. The company last month announced it will eliminate up to 17,000 jobs by year-end in an effort to cut costs by as much as $700 million annually.

Under the rights plan, shareholders of record Aug. 13 will receive dividends allowing them to buy McDonnell Douglas common stock at half price once a suitor gets 20% or more of the company. If the suitor gets more than 20% but less than 50%, McDonnell Douglas has the option to exchange one share of common stock, or a fractional share of a new series of preferred, for each right other than those held by the suitor.

The rights expire in 10 years unless redeemed earlier. McDonnell Douglas may redeem the rights at one cent per right any time until 10 business days following the acquisition of a 20% stake in the firm.
