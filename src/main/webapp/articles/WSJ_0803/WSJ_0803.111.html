Business Brief -- Great Country Bank: Boost in Loan-Loss Reserves Results in 4th-Quarter Loss

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/03/90

Great Country Bank said it had a $2.3 million loss in its fourth quarter, ended May 31, after increasing its loan-loss reserves. The Ansonia, Conn., savings bank said it added $3.3 million to reserves, boosting fiscal year provisions for loan losses to $15.1 million. For the year, the bank reported a loss of $9 million.

Great Country said the added reserves reflect its expectations of problem loans related to a weakened Connecticut real estate market. Property values have been dropping, leaving many banks with difficult-to-sell real estate after foreclosing on delinquent mortgages.

In the 1989 fiscal fourth quarter, Great Country had net income of $677,000, or 31 cents a share. For all of fiscal 1989, it had net of $3.2 million, or $1.45 a share.
