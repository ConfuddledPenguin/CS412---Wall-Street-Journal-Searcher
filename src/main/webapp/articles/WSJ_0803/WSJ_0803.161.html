Northeast Utilities Extends Target Date In Planned Purchase

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/03/90

MANCHESTER, N.H. -- Northeast Utilities said it and the committees representing investors and creditors of Public Service Co. of New Hampshire agreed to extend to Sept. 30 the target date for the first step of Northeast Utilities' proposed $2.3 billion acquisition of PS New Hampshire. Northeast Utilities, based in Hartford, Conn., is currently managing PS New Hampshire, which has been operating under Chapter 11 of the Bankruptcy Code since 1988. Under Chapter 11, a company can continue operating while protected from creditors' claims.

Under the bankruptcy reorganization plan, PS New Hampshire will replace its existing financial obligations with new debt and preferred and common stock. Northeast Utilities had expected to complete the refinancing by Aug. 1.

The reorganization plan, under which Northeast Utilities will ultimately acquire PS New Hampshire has already been approved by shareholders, creditors and the federal bankruptcy court.

Northeast Utilities said the delay in the target date for the refinancing was needed primarily to assess appeals that may be filed as a result of the New Hampshire Public Utilities Commission's approval last month of a rate plan filed in connection with the reorganization.

Parties to the rate case have 20 days to ask that the commission reconsider its decision and the commission has 10 days to respond. If the commission denies requests for reconsideration, the parties have 30 days to appeal to the state supreme court.

If the rate plan approval is appealed in court, Northeast Utilities said a further delay of the reorganization could occur.

Final federal regulatory approval of the acquisition of PS New Hampshire is expected by late spring.
