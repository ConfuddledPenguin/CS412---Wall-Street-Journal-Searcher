Futures Trader Chosen By Chicago Board of Trade

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/03/90

CHICAGO -- The board of directors of the Chicago Board of Trade elected Neal E. Kottke, a self-employed futures trader on the exchange, vice chairman of the board for the remainder of the year. Mr. Kottke succeeds William O'Connor, who was named chairman of the exchange Wednesday to succeed Karsten "Cash" Mahlmann, who resigned under fire for his ties to financially troubled Stotler Group Inc. Mr. Mahlmann on Wednesday also resigned as chairman of Stotler Group and its failed futures brokerage unit, Stotler & Co.

Mr. Kottke has been a governor of the affiliated Board of Trade Clearing Corporation since 1985. He was a director of the exchange from 1981 through 1983, and in 1983 also served as vice chairman of the Board of Trade.
