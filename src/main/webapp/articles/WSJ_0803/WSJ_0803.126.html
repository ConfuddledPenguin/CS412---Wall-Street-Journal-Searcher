Stark Calls Health Chief A `Disgrace to His Race'

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/03/90

WASHINGTON -- Rep. Fortney Stark charged that Health and Human Services Secretary Louis Sullivan, who is black, is "a disgrace to his profession and his race" because of his opposition to national health care and abortion. Dr. Sullivan demanded an apology from the California Democrat, who made his remarks twice yesterday, once in front of cameras. "I wish he had the guts to make his comments to my face," Dr. Sullivan said in a statement. "It's too bad ultraliberals like Pete Stark haven't progressed to the point that they can accept the independent thinking of a black man that does not conform to their own stereotyped views."

The White House issued a statement saying that Rep. Stark's "bigoted assault on the integrity and ability of Secretary Sullivan is an affront to the Congress and the Democratic party. We trust that the Democratic party and its chairman, Ron Brown, will disassociate themselves from Congressman Stark's ill-tempered and shameful remarks." Mr. Brown is also black.

Mr. Stark, a leader on health issues, is pushing a costly national health plan that the Bush administration opposes. "The people who are going to suffer because of the policies he's blindly accepting from the administration are the minorities in this country," Mr. Stark said of Dr. Sullivan.

Mr. Stark made his remarks at a time when racial tensions in the capital already are unusually high because of the drug trial of District of Columbia Mayor Marion Barry. Some of the mayor's supporters assert that the charges against him were racially motivated.
