Business Brief -- Washington Bancorp: Washington Bank Subsidiary Placed Into Conservatorship

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/03/90

Federal regulators took control of National Bank of Washington following the bankruptcy-law filing of its parent, Washington Bancorp. The Office of the Comptroller of the Currency said it placed the bank into conservatorship and appointed William S. Ogden conservator. Mr. Ogden, formerly vice chairman of Chase Manhattan Bank, served as chief executive of Continental Illinois National Bank & Trust Co. after the government took control of it in 1984.

The regulators said a conservator was appointed to safeguard the bank's assets of about $1.6 billion and to provide for an orderly sale of the bank.

Washington Bancorp. filed Wednesday for court protection from creditors under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code. The company isn't related to Washington Bancorp Inc., based in Hoboken, N.J.
