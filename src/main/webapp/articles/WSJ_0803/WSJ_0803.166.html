Marketing & Media: Times Mirror Is Said To Have Bought 50% Stake in La Opinion

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/03/90

LOS ANGELES -- Times Mirror Co. is expected to announce that it purchased a 50% interest in La Opinion, one of the nation's largest Spanish-language newspapers. A spokeswoman for Times Mirror, which publishes The Los Angeles Times and other newspapers, said last night that she couldn't confirm the report. La Opinion is based in Los Angeles.

The Los Angeles Times already publishes a monthly Spanish-English section that circulates in Spanish-speaking neighborhoods. Newspaper executives had been rumored to be considering making the section weekly.

Besides the Los Angeles Times, Times Mirror publishes Newsday and New York Newsday, the Baltimore Sun newspapers, the Hartford (Conn.) Courant, the (Allentown, Pa.) Morning Call, the (Stamford, Conn.) Advocate and Greenwich (Conn.) Time. Times Mirror also has interests in cable television, book publishing and other businesses.

La Opinion was founded Sept. 16, 1926, Mexico's independence day, by Ignacio Lozano Sr. and has become one of the largest and one of the most financially successful Spanish-language dailies in the U.S. Ignacio Lozano Jr. serves as chairman and editor in chief, while his son and daughter, Jose Ignacio Lozano and Monica Lozano Centanino, serve as publisher and managing editor, respectively.

Jose Lozano did not immediately respond to telephone calls late Thursday.

La Opinion's circulation is about 100,000, nearly all of which is sold on newsstands. Over the years, it has broadened its coverage beyond the Mexican-American community to serve the growing Central American community in the Los Angeles area.

According to internal Times projections, Southern California's Hispanic population will swell from about 29% of the region's population currently to 40% by 2010.
