World Bank Approves $300 Million Loan To Poland for Reforms

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/03/90

WASHINGTON -- The World Bank approved a $300 million loan to Poland, primarily to help the Eastern European country pay for essential imports as it moves ahead with its economic reform program. The international agency said the loan, which will be disbursed in two installments, also would support an expansion of Poland's so-called safety-net programs, including unemployment compensation and retraining of workers displaced during the current effort to restructure various industries.

World Bank President Barber Conable promised earlier that the bank would provide loans to Poland to support the country's economic reforms. The $300 million loan is its first credit of this type to the country, according to bank officials.

In another action, the World Bank cleared a $125 million loan to Indonesia to help provide credits to small borrowers, primarily in rural areas.

The bank also approved a $75 million loan for rural development projects in Colombia's Andean and coastal areas.

The World Bank loans carry a variable interest rate, currently 7.72% annually.
