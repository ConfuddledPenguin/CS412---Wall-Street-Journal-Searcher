Credit Ratings: RJR Debt Ratings Affirmed by Moody's

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/03/90

NEW YORK -- Moody's Investors Service Inc. affirmed its current ratings on $21 billion of RJR Nabisco Inc. debt, despite RJR's plans to overhaul its balance sheet. The rating firm also assigned a below-investment-grade rating, B-3, to the $1.8 billion of convertible preferred stock RJR plans to issue as part of its $6.9 million refinancing.

Moody's, which has generally rated RJR's bonds below the ratings given them by Standard & Poor's Corp., said RJR's recapitalization should favorably impact the food and tobacco giant's capital structure over the long term. However, it said potential increases in sales and excise taxes at the federal and state levels could hamper RJR's ability to boost its free cash flow from raising cigarette prices in the U.S.

Moody's said its existing ratings on RJR's debt, most of which are below investment grade, or junk, "continue to accurately reflect" the risks RJR faces.
