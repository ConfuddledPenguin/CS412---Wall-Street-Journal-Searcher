Business Brief -- Lone Star Technologies Inc.: Unit's Reorganization Plan Gives Parent 100% Control

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/03/90

Lone Star Technologies Inc., Dallas, said its Lone Star Steel Co. unit filed a plan of reorganization in U.S. bankruptcy court in Dallas calling for the parent to retain 100% of the unit by contributing up to $36 million in new financing. Under the plan, Lone Star Steel would pay off 61% of creditors' allowed pre-bankruptcy nonpriority claims in cash, using internally generated funds and the parent company's contribution, Lone Star Technologies said. Lone Star Steel lists $211.7 million in pre-petition liabilities.

Lone Star Technologies said the plan calls for the unsecured creditors committee to dismiss a lawsuit it filed on the unit's behalf seeking to recover $109.3 million from the parent.

Lone Star Technologies said that, if creditors reject the plan, the unit will proceed with an alternative plan to provide creditors with full recovery of their claims in cash and securities of the unit. Under the alternative plan, creditors would receive $70 million in cash, $50 million face amount of subordinated notes, $30 million in liquidation preference of preferred stock and 46% of the unit's common.
