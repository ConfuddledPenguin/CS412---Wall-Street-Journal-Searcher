Chinese Firm Agrees To Presidential Order To Sell Mamco Stake

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/03/90

WASHINGTON -- The China National Aero-Technology Import and Export Corp. said it agreed to sell Mamco Manufacturing Inc. to comply with an order by President Bush, who required the action on grounds of national security.

The Chinese company said the ownership of Seattle-based Mamco, which makes aircraft parts for U.S. commercial aircraft manufacturers, will be transferred to DeCrane Aircraft Holdings Inc., a private Midwest firm. The price wasn't disclosed.

U.S. trade law allows the president to suspend or prohibit the acquisition of a U.S. company by a foreign party if he finds they are contrary to national security interests. The decision to force Catic to sell its stake in Mamco was the first time President Bush rejected an acquisition on such grounds.

Initially, Catic was ordered to dispose of its stock in Mamco, acquired in November 1989, within three months of Feb. 1. This deadline was subsequently extended for another three months.

U.S. officials said Catic, an export-import company of China's Ministry of Aerospace Industry, has business dealings with various U.S. companies, including commercial aircraft manufacturers. The Chinese government ministry engages in aerospace research and development and is responsible for the design and production of military and commercial aircraft and missiles in China.
