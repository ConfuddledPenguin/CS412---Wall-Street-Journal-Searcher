Pratt & Lambert Net Fell 7% on Sales Drop In the Second Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/03/90

BUFFALO, N.Y. -- Pratt & Lambert Inc. said soft demand for adhesives depressed second-quarter net income 7% from a year earlier, but it expects "reasonably strong" third and fourth quarters. Net fell to $2.3 million, or 40 cents a share, from $2.5 million, or 41 cents a share, a year earlier. The company's stock repurchase program reduced average shares outstanding 6% for the 1990 period. Sales fell 2% to $65.4 million from $66.7 million a year ago.

The paint, coatings and adhesives concern declined to make a specific earnings projection. In the 1989 third quarter the company earned $1.9 million, or 33 cents a share, on sales of $63 million.

Chairman R. D. Stevens, Jr. said the modest sales decline in the latest quarter reflected soft demand for adhesives used in construction, footwear and recreational vehicles. "While we don't look for an early recovery in these markets, we anticipate that strengthening sales during the second half in other lines will more than eliminate the current shortfall," Mr. Stevens said.

In the first half, net fell 2% to $3 million, or 52 cents a share, from $3.9 million, or 62 cents a share, a year earlier. Sales fell 1% to $124.8 million from $126.1 million.
