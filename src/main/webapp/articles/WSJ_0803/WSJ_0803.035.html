Prime Computer May Get $100 Million of Contract

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/03/90

NATICK, Mass. -- Prime Computer Inc.'s share of an Army computer contract is expected to amount to more than $100 million, the largest in the company's history, industry sources say. The $700 million, multiyear contract was awarded Friday to Electronic Data Systems Corp., a General Motors Corp. unit. Spokesmen for both companies refused to disclose how much of that contract would go to Prime.

The contract calls for EDS to provide up to 20,000 computers and systems for general office use by the Army, Navy and defense logistics agency by 1995. A spokeswoman for the Army's information systems procurement agency confirmed that Prime's machines will be "the heart of the system." But she wouldn't break out the company's potential share.

The subcontract appears to be a major win for Prime, which has been struggling to cut costs and reverse huge losses it suffered in the wake of a hostile takeover attempt. Prime was taken private in 1989 by a unit of D.H. Whitney & Co., a New York investment firm.
