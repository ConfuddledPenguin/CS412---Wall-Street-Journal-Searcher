Toyota, Fujitsu Agree To Form a Company To Develop Software

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 08/03/90

TOKYO -- Japan's Toyota Motor Corp. and Fujitsu Ltd. agreed to establish a company to develop and market engineering software, the two companies said. The joint venture, Toyota System Research Inc., will be set up next month in Nagoya, with Toyota taking a 65% interest and computer maker Fujitsu putting up the remaining 35% in the 300 million yen ($2 million) project, Toyota said.

The venture would aim to match Fujitsu's basic knowledge as a computer maker with Toyota's applied knowledge as a computer user to develop software capable of responding to technological advances in laboratory and factory automation, artificial intelligence and computer-aided engineering, or CAE, Toyota said.

The company will initially focus on developing and marketing software for the automobile industry, chiefly for rapidly expanding CAE applications, Toyota said.

Sales for the first year are targeted at 500 million yen, with a goal of three billion yen annually after five years.

Operations are tentatively scheduled to begin in November, with a work force of 30 people. Over the next five years, the number of employees is seen rising to 250.
