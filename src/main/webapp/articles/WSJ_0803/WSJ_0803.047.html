U.S. Shoe Shareholders Drop Class-Action Suit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/03/90

CINCINNATI -- U.S. Shoe Corp. said a class-action suit, filed against the company after it retained Merrill Lynch Capital Markets to explore sale of all or parts of the specialty retailer, was conditionally dismissed at the request of plaintiffs' counsel. The suit, brought by shareholders who bought their shares after Aug. 15, 1988, was dismissed by U.S. District Judge Carl B. Rubin in Cincinnati.

James J. Crowe, vice president and general counsel at U.S. Shoe, said plaintiffs made a number of allegations, including questioning whether management was serious about selling all or part of the company.

In April 1989, Merill Lynch pulled out of an agreement to acquire the company's footwear division in a $422.5-million leveraged buy-out. Merrill Lynch didn't offer any explanation, but U.S. Shoe attributed the action to concern about the junk-bond market and the performance of the struggling, footwear division.

The court order also includes dimissal of litigation against various U.S. Shoe directors and officers and against Merrill Lynch & Co.
