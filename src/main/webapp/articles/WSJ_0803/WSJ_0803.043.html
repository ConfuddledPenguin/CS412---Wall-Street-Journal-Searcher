New Securities Issues

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/03/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report. MUNICIPAL

Pennsylvania Housing Finance Authority -- $80 million of single-family mortgage revenue bonds, via a PaineWebber Inc. group. The bonds, which are subject to the alternative minimum tax, were priced at par to yield from 6.30% in 1994 to 7.65% in 2023. There are $6.7 million of 7.45% term bonds priced at par due in 2010 and $60.2 million of 7.65% term bonds priced at par due in 2023. The issue is expected to receive a double-A/double-A rating.

EUROBONDS

State Development Institute (Hungary) -- $200 million of Eurobonds due Aug. 31, 2000, with a 10 1/2% coupon via Daiwa Europe Ltd. Reoffered at 99.60. Principal guaranteed at maturity by World Bank. Interest and principal guaranteed by the republic of Hungary. Fees 1/2.

Chase Manhattan Bank Luxembourg (U.S. parent) -- 400 million Canadian dollar two-tranche Eurobond issue of depository receipts backed by Canadian government bonds. C$200 million of 10 1/2% bonds due June 1, 1995, at an issue price of 101 1/2, backed by Canadian government 10 1/2% securities due 1995. C$200 million of 10 1/2% bonds due in Sept. 1, 2000, at issue price of 102 5/8, backed by Canadian government 10 1/2% securities due July 2000. Fees 1 7/8 on first tranche and 2 on second.

Elkraft Power Co. (Denmark) -- 10 billion yen of 7 1/2% Eurobonds due Aug. 24, 1993, priced at 101 3/8, via Sanwa International Ltd. Fees 1 3/8.

Skopbank (Finland) -- seven billion yen of Eurobonds due Jan. 31, 1992, via Sanwa International Ltd. Coupon will float 0.0625 point above the London interbank offered rate for the first three months, and thereafter will be fixed at 7 1/2%. Fees 1 1/4.
