Kansas Gas & Electric Urges Rejection of Bid By Kansas City P&L

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/03/90

WICHITA, Kan. -- Kansas Gas & Electric Co. said its board recommended that shareholders reject the $27-a-share, or about $857 million, hostile tender offer launched last month by Kansas City Power & Light Inc. The utility's response wasn't unexpected; earlier this week, it filed suit to, among other things, block its unwanted suitor from looking at its holder list.

Kansas Gas & Electric said its financial adviser, Morgan Stanley & Co., considered the offer price inadequate. Directors cited reservations about the "many conditions" Kansas City P&L placed on its offer, and the "many months, perhaps more than a year, that could be required in attempting to satisfy those conditions, if indeed they can all be satisfied."

Kansas Gas & Electric said the offer "doesn't reflect the values inherent in the company," and "raises substantial and serious legal questions and numerous uncertainties under federal and state statutes." Kansas City P&L has said its bid is in "full compliance with all applicable laws."

The two neighboring utilities already jointly own 94% of a big nuclear plant. Kansas City P&L launched its unusual hostile bid after repeated overtures for a friendly deal failed.

Kansas City P&L said yesterday it believes the bid price is "full and fair." It added, "We continue to emphasize our strong desire to achieve a negotiated transaction."
