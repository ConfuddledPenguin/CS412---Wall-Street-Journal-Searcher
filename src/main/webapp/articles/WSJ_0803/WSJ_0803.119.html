Business Brief -- Allied-Signal Inc.: Manufacturer Plans to Build Polyester Plant in France

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/03/90

Allied-Signal Inc., Morris Township, N.J., said it will build a $160 million polyester-fiber plant in Longwy, France, to serve passenger car tire, seat belt and other markets. The plant is expected to begin operating in early 1993 and employ about 300.

Allied-Signal, an aerospace, automotive-products and engineered-materials company, currently makes high-performance polyester in North Carolina; its industrial polyester sales total about $150 million a year, including European exports.

The company said the new plant will serve European customers as well as take advantage of new markets in Eastern Europe. Although the European tire market currently uses rayon, nylon and polyester reinforcement, the company said it believes European tiremakers will "follow the lead of U.S. and Far East manufacturers who have switched to the use of all polyester for tire reinforcement."
