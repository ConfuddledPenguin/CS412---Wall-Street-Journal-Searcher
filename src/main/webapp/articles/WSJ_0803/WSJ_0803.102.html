Rockwell Receives EPA Order

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/03/90

EL SEGUNDO, Calif. -- The Environmental Protection Agency said it served Rockwell International Corp. with a federal enforcement order charging hazardous waste violations at its facility in Palmdale, Calif. The EPA said it proposed a civil penalty of $54,400. In addition to paying the penalty, Rockwell was ordered to come into compliance with Federal hazardous waste regulations within 30 days.

Rockwell, a defense and industrial concern, said a March EPA audit of its Palmdale facility determined that there were nine violations of applicable environmental regulations. Five of these violations were corrected within one week of the inspection and another one was corrected in July, Rockwell said. The company said it is working with the EPA to resolve the remaining issues to insure full compliance with federal regulations.
