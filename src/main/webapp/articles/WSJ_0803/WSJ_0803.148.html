S&L Bailout Agency Sued By Housing-Rights Group

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/03/90

WASHINGTON -- A housing-rights group sued the government's savings-and-loan bailout agency, contending that the agency isn't adhering to procedures to promote low-income housing. The suit, filed in federal court in Chicago, alleged that the Resolution Trust Corp. isn't sufficiently publicizing homes made available when it sells off the assets of sick thrifts, hasn't appointed certain groups as "clearinghouses" for the real estate information, and is restricting the flow of houses into the program.

ACORN, or the Association of Community Organizations for Reform Now, said it is seeking a preliminary injunction to award ACORN funds for homes that were sold "improperly" and to appoint ACORN as a clearinghouse for the information.

The savings and loan cleanup law called for public housing agencies and those with low incomes to have a 90-day first look at homes and apartments appraised at below-average prices.

A spokesman for the RTC said the agency hadn't yet seen the suit and couldn't comment in detail. But he said that ACORN hadn't yet officially requested to be designated a clearinghouse for the information and that the RTC had made catalogs of the property available to ACORN. "We're sorry they're unhappy," he said.
