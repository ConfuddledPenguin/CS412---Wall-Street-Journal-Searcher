Thor Industries Files Suit Against Rexhall For Violation of Pact

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/03/90

JACKSON CENTER, Ohio -- Thor Industries Inc. said it filed suit against Rexhall Industries Inc., alleging that Rexhall violated a non-compete agreement, among other things. Thor is seeking more than $10 million in damages. Thor, a maker of recreational vehicles, said the action stems from its purchase in 1985 of Establishment Industries Inc. from several individuals, including Rexhall's founder and majority shareholder. At the time, Thor said it had obtained non-compete agreements.

The suit, filed in Los Angeles federal district court, also names Herbert F. Boeckmann II and William J. Rex. Mr. Boeckmann was the principal shareholder of Establishment. Mr. Rex, a former president of Establishment, is founder and majority shareholder of Rexhall Industries, which is a Saugus, Calif.-based maker of motor homes.

Officials at Rexhall couldn't immediately be reached for comment.

Also a party in the suit is Airstream Inc., a Thor subsidiary. Airstream alleges that Rexhall has unfairly competed and infringed on a trademark. Airstream seeks to enjoin the use of the trademark. The subsidiary also seeks unspecified damages, Thor said.
