Business Brief -- Thomson McKinnon Asset Mgmt.: Concern Settles With SEC Over Client-Fee Disclosures

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/03/90

Thomson McKinnon Asset Management L.P., a New York-based mutual-fund adviser, settled with findings by the Securities and Exchange Commission that it failed to properly disclose certain fees charged customers. Without admitting or denying the charges, Thomson McKinnon agreed to comply with required internal procedures.

The SEC asserted Thomson McKinnon didn't adequately disclose multiple fees or that it benefited from the "float" -- or interest earned -- on customer funds before the funds were invested for customers.

The SEC noted that the retail business has been sold and the company no longer earns float benefits on customer transactions.
