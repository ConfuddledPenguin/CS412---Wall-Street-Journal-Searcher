The Iraqi Invasion of Kuwait: Big Italian Bank Declines To Comment on Iraq Loan

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 08/03/90

MILAN -- Banca Nazionale del Lavoro today declined to say if it was considering action with respect to the $2.7 billion it is owed by Iraq. A spokesman of Italy's largest commercial bank said only that it is "attentively following the situation" and will "in any case abide by any decision the (Italian) government might take."

The bank last year was rocked by an international financial scandal involving nearly $3 billion of irregular credits to Iraq. The credits were extended by the bank's U.S. branch in Atlanta without the authorization of its North American management.

The bank's top management was forced to resign over the scandal, which is still being investigated by U.S. and Italian authorities.
