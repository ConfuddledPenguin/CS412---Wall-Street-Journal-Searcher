U.S. Court Dismisses Class-Action Lawsuit Against Polaroid Corp.

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/03/90

CAMBRIDGE, Mass. -- Polaroid Corp. said the U.S. Court of Appeals for the First Circuit dismissed a class-action suit brought against it by Irving Backman, on behalf of investors who purchased stock and options in early 1979. In January, a majority of three judges on the court voted to overturn a $30 million judgment against the company for not promptly disclosing material information, and ordered the case retried in a lower court.

Polaroid appealed that decision, and in today's ruling a majority of the full U.S. Court voted to dismiss the suit.

As reported, Irving Backman alleged the company didn't promptly disclose news of problems with its Polavision product. The product was withdrawn from the market in the fourth quarter of 1979, after the company took a $68 million write-off.

The court ruled that Polaroid "did not, at any material time, know that Polavision was a commercial failure."

In July 1987, a federal jury awarded $9.75 a share in damages to shareholders who had bought stock in January and February of 1979, for a total of $30 million.
