Air New Zealand Ltd. To Release 600 Staff, Scrap Fokker Fleet

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 08/03/90

WELLINGTON, New Zealand -- Air New Zealand Ltd., the country's international air carrier, said it is firing 600 staff and scrapping its fleet of 15 Fokker Friendship F27 aircraft to become more competitive on domestic services. Jim Scott, chief executive of Air New Zealand, said the provincial routes will be included on main trunk routes serviced by its bigger Boeing 737 aircraft because of heavy competition on those main routes.

Air New Zealand's main competitor on the trunk routes is Ansett New Zealand Ltd., a unit of Ansett Transport Industries Ltd., an Australian domestic airline owned jointly by Rupert Murdoch's News Corp. and transportation company TNT Ltd.

"Today's reality is that Air New Zealand is contesting the main trunk routes with a totally overseas-owned competitor who has chosen to concentrate its efforts on these higher volume and, thereby, more profitable routes," Mr. Scott said.

The company said that since Air New Zealand isn't allowed to contest Ansett's home markets in Australia, it can't afford any longer to serve less-profitable provincial routes in New Zealand with the F27 fleet.

Air New Zealand is 35% owned by Brierley Investments Ltd. of New Zealand and 20% owned by Qantas Aiways Ltd., Australia's state-owned international airline. Japan Air Lines Co. and American Airlines Inc. each own 7.5%, and the remaining 30% was floated on the New Zealand stock exchange in 1989.
