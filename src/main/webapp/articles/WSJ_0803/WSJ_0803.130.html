International Brief -- Deutsche Bank AG: Partial Operating Net Rose 18% in First Half of 1990

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/03/90

West Germany's largest banking group, Deutsche Bank AG, reported yesterday that its partial operating profit in the first half of 1990 rose 18% to 2.2 billion marks ($1.39 million) from 1.869 billion marks in the first six months of last year. Deutsche Bank was the third major West German banking group to report firm first half growth in its partial operating profit, which is before taxes and excludes results from the bank's trading activities for its own account.

Deutsche Bank didn't provide specific figures over earnings that include results from its own trading activities. But the company said such full operating profit in the first half rose 17% when compared with one-half of the full-year total in all 1989.

West German banks usually don't publish what their full operating profit is. They simply characterize it or disclose percentage changes. That is because the banks claim that the results of their trading activities should be confidential.

Deutsche Bank said that its 1990 first-half world-wide group results were aided by the first-time inclusion of recent acquisitions. Most notable of these were U.K. merchant bankers Morgan Grenfell and German specialty banking group Grunelius KG, among others.
