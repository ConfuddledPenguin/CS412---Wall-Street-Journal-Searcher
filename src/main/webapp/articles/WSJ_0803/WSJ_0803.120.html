The Iraqi Invasion of Kuwait: Iraqis Bring U.S. Workers To Undisclosed Location

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/03/90

WASHINGTON -- Iraqi troops invading Kuwait picked up a small group of American oil workers and moved them to an unknown location, U.S. officials said. It wasn't clear whether the Iraqi troops intended to capture the workers, who were near the Iraq-Kuwait border, or merely were moving them out of the way of the invasion, officials said. But American diplomats in Iraq were being ordered by the State Department to ask the Iraqi foreign ministry to locate the workers, and to tell Iraq's leaders that they would be considered responsible for the workers' fate.

President Bush, asked about the workers at a news conference, said he didn't know of the incident but warned that there would be a "dramatic" effect on the U.S. attitude if Americans were "threatened or harmed."
