Hearings Planned on Roles In Deregulation of Thrifts

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/03/90

WASHINGTON -- Rep. Henry Gonzalez (D., Texas), chairman of the House Banking Committee, isn't tired of savings and loan associations yet. The maverick Texan populist said he will hold hearings in September to shed light on the role of government officials in the late 1970s and early 1980s on the deregulation of thrifts. Those called to testify include: former Treasury Secretaries Donald Regan and G. William Miller, and former top thrift regulators, including Richard Pratt, Edwin Gray and Danny Wall.

The hearings, which begin Sept. 12, occur as Congress, the Bush administration and both political parties frantically look for something or someone to blame for the mess, which will cost more than $130 billion, without including hundreds of billions of dollars more in interest and other costs. Fraud has received the most blame, and Congress is winding up the current session with legislation to punish future S&L looters more harshly.

Less has been said about the culpability of decision-makers, who deregulated thrifts while slashing budgets of examiners who were to police the newly freed institutions.

"Today there is mass confusion and mounds of misinformation about what did and did not contribute to the savings and loan problem," Rep. Gonzalez said. "We have an obligation to establish a clear record."
