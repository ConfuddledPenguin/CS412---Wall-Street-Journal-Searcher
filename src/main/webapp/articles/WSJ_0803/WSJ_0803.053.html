NASD Expels Atlanta Firm, Imposes Fine

  • Author: Robert Daniels
  • Date: 08/03/90

NEW YORK -- Habersheir Securities Inc. of Atlanta was expelled from the National Association of Securities Dealers, and a principal of the firm was barred from the securities industry for alleged failure to maintain accurate records and required net capital. The disciplinary action was among a number taken by the NASD, the self-regulatory agency for the over-the-counter markets.

Habersheir, also known as W.D. Fard Securities Inc., also was fined $15,000 while the principal, Raymond R. Khalif of Newark, N.J., was assessed $10,000. The firm and Mr. Khalif, who couldn't be reached, were fined and suspended under an NASD action announced early this year.

Hampton Securities Inc. and principal Delores Easthom, both of West Palm Beach, Fla., settled with the NASD, agreeing to a total fine of $147,700.

The firm, which went out of business around last September, had been expelled under a previous action; Ms. Easthom was barred as a principal and suspended in any capacity for two years. Without admitting or denying wrongdoing, they consented to findings that the firm charged unfair prices for securities. Ms. Easthom couldn't be reached.

Marvin W. Fulford of Asheville, N.C., was fined $205,000 and barred. The NASD charged that Mr. Fulford, who couldn't be reached for comment, misappropriated customers' securities and kept the proceeds.

Prudential-Bache Securities Inc., a unit of Prudential Insurance Co. of America, $90,000; Union Planters Investment Bankers Group Inc., and three principals, James Neal Augustine, Lindsay Hill and Robert Joseph Finley, all Memphis, Tenn., total $75,000; Heiner & Stock Inc. of Minneapolis and principal Randall R. Heiner, total $15,000. Heiner & Stock must disgorge almost $50,000, and Mr. Heiner was suspended 10 days.

Also, Cardinal Financial Equities Inc., and principal Leo Charles Loevner, Fairfax and Burke, Va., respectively, total $30,000 fine. Mr. Loevner was barred as principal and suspended in any capacity for 30 days; Heidtke & Co. and principal Lyman O. Heidtke, both Nashville, Tenn., total $20,000 fine and suspended from effecting securities transactions for five business days except for certain unsolicited customer orders; Allegiance Securities Inc., New York, and Joseph Anthony Giordano, Elmwood Park, N.J., total $20,000 fine. Mr. Giordano was suspended as principal for one year and must requalify by exam.

And Kochcapital Inc. and principal Russell Gordon Koch, Bellevue and Issaquah, Wash., respectively, total $20,000 fine and must make restitution; First Nutley Securities Inc. and principal Ronald Durando, both Nutley, N.J., $15,000 fine. Mr. Durando was suspended 60 days; Shearson Lehman Hutton Inc., New York, and principal T. Clark Octigan, Chesterfield, Mo., $10,000 fine. Shearson also was fined an additional $10,000 and Mr. Octigan must undertake compliance-related duties.

All of the above except Heiner & Stock and Mr. Heiner settled the NASD's charges without admitting or denying any wrongdoing.

Except as noted below, the individuals and firms involved in NASD disciplinary proceedings couldn't be reached for comment:

Jackson W. Moore, president of Union Planters Corp., parent of the investment subsidiary, said the NASD matter "involved some transactions that happened probably close to two years ago." The firm already has charged off the fine, he said. "Rather than going to the expense and distraction of going to litigation, we proceeded with a negotiated settlement of the matter," the executive said.

Mr. Heiner said, "We disagree with the interpretation by the NASD of the facts. The firm did not benefit by the amount the agency told us we had to give back."

Mr. Ingber said, "The arbitration hearing is just symbolic. Everybody told (me), `you're just wasting your time.' They were right."

Mr. Heidtke said the firm made a market in a local low-price stock. Because the issue was a pink-sheet stock, regulators argued that the markups the firm charged should have been based on the bid price, or on the firm's cost, rather than on the more-usual asked price.

Shearson declined to comment, as did Messrs. Castille, Crawford, Durando, Giordano, Halloran, Ostrand and Thacker.

A spokeswoman for Prudential-Bache said the NASD matters occurred "several years ago" and that the firm settled without admitting or denying wrongdoing "to put the matter behind us."

A spokesman for Kochcapital declined comment.
