Business Brief -- Southland Corp.: Company's Exchange Offers Declared Effective by SEC

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/03/90

Southland Corp., Dallas, said the Securities and Exchange Commission declared effective the company's registration statement, which reflected the terms of its revised exchange offers. The exchange offers, which are set to expire Aug. 15, are the first step of a two-part plan in which Ito-Yokado Co. of Japan and Seven-Eleven Japan Co. would pay $430 million for a controlling stake in Southland, the troubled parent of the 7-Eleven retail convenience-store chain.

Southland said the steering committee that represents its public security holders supports the exchange offers, which would give bondholders and preferred shareholders about 25% of newly issued Southland common.
