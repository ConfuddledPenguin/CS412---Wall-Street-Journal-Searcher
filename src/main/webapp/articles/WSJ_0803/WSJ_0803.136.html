International Brief -- Istituto per la Ricostruzione: Group Profit Climbed 27% In '89; Revenue Rose 13%

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/03/90

Istituto per la Ricostruzione Industriale (IRI), Italy's giant state holding company, yesterday posted group earnings of 1.606 trillion lire ($1.38 billion) in 1989, up 27% from 1.263 trillion lire a year earlier. IRI said in a statement that its industrial sector contributed 876 billion lire to group profit, while its banking division, net of minority interests, accounted for 730 billion lire.

Group revenue rose 13% to 66.747 trillion lire in 1989 from 59.017 trillion lire in 1988. Of this amount, IRI's revenue from foreign sources totaled 9.5 trillion lire last year, up 14% from 1988.

IRI didn't comment on its 1990 results.

Gross operating earnings rose 14% to 14.542 trillion lire from 12.733 trillion lire, while cash flow climbed 12% to 9.232 trillion lire from 8.233 trillion lire in 1988.

The IRI group, which combines more than 500 operating companies and makes products ranging from biscuits to steel to satellites, employed some 416,000 people as of the end of 1989.
