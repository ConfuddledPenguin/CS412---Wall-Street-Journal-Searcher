International Brief -- Midland Bank PLC: U.K. Firm's Pretax Profit Was Lower Than Expected

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/03/90

Midland Bank PLC posted pretax profit for the six months ended June 30 of #36 million ($66.8 million); profit was weaker than expected, although it reversed a year-earlier loss of #531 million. Midland, a major British commercial bank, was expected to post pretax profit somewhere between #150 million and #203 million.

Pretax profit was curbed by #300 million in loan provisions, #234 million of which was to cover exposure in the U.K. market. It had only #90 million in provisions in the year-earlier period to cover domestic loans.

Midland's profit was also cut by a #38 million charge against interest-rate swaps with U.K. municipalities that have been voided by the British courts.

Pretax profit before provisions, however, fell in the latest half to #363 million, down 19% from #449 million.

The bank said profitability was also hurt by narrower U.K. interest margins, reduced interest payments by Third World debtors and the continued effects of its misjudging the direction of U.K. interest rates last year.
