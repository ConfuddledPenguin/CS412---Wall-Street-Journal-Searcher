Clinical Technologies Founder Files Suit Against 3 Directors

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/03/90

NEW YORK -- Solomon S. Steiner, founder of Clinical Technologies Associates Inc., filed suit, charging three company directors with improperly trying to oust him from his position as president and chief executive officer. In a lawsuit filed in state Supreme Court in Manhattan, Mr. Steiner charged Chairman Arnold J. Friedhoff and directors Howard M. Pack and Luis Menocal III with "unjustified and improper acts" designed to "(entrench) themselves in control" of Clinical Technologies.

On Tuesday, the company announced that its board had voted 3-2 to dismiss Mr. Steiner and name Michael M. Goldberg, founding partner of an investment firm specializing in health-care companies, to the top operating posts.

The Elmsford, N.Y., company is developing a technology by which drugs that presently can be administered only by injection -- such as insulin for diabetics -- can be taken orally. Mr. Steiner, who has a doctorate in physiological psychology, founded Clinical Technologies in 1985.

Messrs. Friedhoff, Pack and Menocal couldn't be reached for comment. Scott Mollen, an attorney for Clinical, said the company filed a countersuit against Mr. Steiner. He said the company denies the charges, and is "confident the parties will work out their disputes."
