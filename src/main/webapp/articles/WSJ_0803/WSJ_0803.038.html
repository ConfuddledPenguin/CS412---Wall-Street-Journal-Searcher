Lafarge Coppee S.A. Set to Adopt Strategy To Block Takeovers

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 08/03/90

PARIS -- Cement maker Lafarge Coppee S.A., judged a prime takeover target because of its loosely structured capital base, plans to adopt a "poison pill" strategy to ward off potential predators. Europe's largest cement company announced that, in the event of an unwelcome takeover attempt, it will launch bids for the shares its doesn't already own in three of its foreign units -- Lafarge Corp. of the U.S., Cementia AS of Switzerland and Asland S.A. of Spain.

As a result, any buy-out attempt would have to include a bid for the entire ensemble, boosting the cost of the takeover to about 40 billion francs ($7.52 billion) from the 25 billion francs needed to take control of the parent company.

The parent currently holds 57.4% of Lafarge Corp., 60.4% of Cementia and 49.8% of Asland.

The plan is subject to approval by the Comite des Operations des Bourses, France's regulatory body for the equities market.
