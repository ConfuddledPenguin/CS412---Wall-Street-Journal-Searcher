Help-Wanted Ads Post Decline for June

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/03/90

NEW YORK -- Help-wanted advertising continued its steady decline, slipping in June to its lowest level since the mid-1980s. But the Conference Board, which compiles the advertising index, said it was optimistic that help-wanted advertising will pick up in the second half as the labor market improves.

The help-wanted index fell to 133 from May's 134 and from a year-earlier 147. The index, a seasonally adjusted indicator of hiring plans, uses 1967 as a base of 100.

The independent business research group said it projects a sustained growth of at least 2% in the gross national product in the second half, which is expected to boost the job growth rate, and in turn, advertising for jobs.

"I'm not looking for a huge bounce back, but rather a subdued one," said Ken Goldstein, an economist with the Conference Board.

Still, that's good news for job-seekers who have seen help-wanted ads steadily decline. The index in October reached a high of 150, but now is at its lowest point since 1985. The index is compiled from surveys of advertising volume in 51 major newspapers every month. However, the Conference Board noted that 37% of the newspapers reported rising want-ad volume, compared with a revised 22% in May and 24% in June 1989.

Much of the improvement in the job market is expected to come from growth in export-driven industries in the second half, Mr. Goldstein said. "And it remains a feature of this outlook that the turnaround is likely to show up earliest in the Western labor markets and then sweep eastward."

The Conference Board said ad volume rose modestly in four regions while five regions had declines. Ad volume increased in the West South Central region, 1.7%; South Atlantic, 0.8%; New England, 0.5%; and Mountain region, 0.4%.
