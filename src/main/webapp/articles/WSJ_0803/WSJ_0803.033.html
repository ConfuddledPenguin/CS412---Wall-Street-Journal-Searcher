Ex-Theater Owner Jailed For Contempt in Arkansas

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/03/90

William Anthony Rand, whose now-defunct Rand Theaters Inc. was furiously expanding last year, was jailed by a state judge in Little Rock, Ark., for contempt of court. The demise of Mr. Rand's highflying, movie-theater empire was described in a page one article in December in this newspaper.

Mr. Rand was jailed along with his wife, Mary Ann, by Judge Robin Mays after a hearing over $71,000 he had been ordered to deposit with the court. The money was the proceeds of a sale of a home in the resort community of Eden Isle in Cleburne County, about 100 miles north of Little Rock. First American Title Insurance Co. of the Midwest, Memphis, Tenn., filed suit in March seeking part of the proceeds from the sale. Its attorney, Marian M. McMullan, claimed in court that the Rands hid the money by putting it in bank accounts and by distributing it among friends and relatives.

Ms. McMullan also says Mr. Rand is the subject of a federal criminal investigation in Little Rock over the operation of the theater chain, and state tax officials say they turned over office records in response to a federal grand jury subpoena earlier this year. The U.S. Attorney's office in Little Rock wouldn't comment, but it is believed part of the investigation involves alleged fraud in the leasing of theater equipment.
