Playboy Enterprises Holders Sue Over Bid For Dual-Class Stock

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/16/90

CHICAGO -- A shareholder lawsuit alleged that Playboy Enterprises Inc.'s recently implemented dual-class structure is unfair to minority stockholders. The suit, filed in the chancery division of Cook County state court here, claims the "true purpose" of the plan is to "enable Hugh Hefner to retain voting control of Playboy while disposing of up to threequarters of his Playboy holdings."

The suit charges Playboy and certain officials with breach of fiduciary duty, among other things, and asks the court to order a reversal of Playboy's recapitalization plan.

In May, Playboy announced plans to convert its common stock into separate voting and nonvoting classes. The publishing and entertainment concern touted the plan, which was approved by holders, as a benign business tool under which it could issue nonvoting shares without diluting founder and former Chairman Hugh Hefner's 71% controlling stake. Mr. Hefner's support meant passage of the measure was a foregone conclusion.

The shareholder suit says that Mr. Hefner, as a "self-interested and majority shareholder, had the ultimate power to determine the outcome of the shareholder vote." Because he held that power, Playboy "had a heightened and unflinching duty to look after the interests of the minority shareholders when implementing the plan, which it failed to exercise."

A Playboy spokeswoman said the company hadn't yet seen the suit, but added, "We are extremely confident the court will uphold the validity" of the recent move. The plan "provides substantial benefits to all of the company's shareholders," she said, and "doesn't change Mr. Hefner's historic control of the company."
