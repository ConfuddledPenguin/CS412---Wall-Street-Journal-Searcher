Business Brief -- Union Valley Corp.: Low 2nd-Quarter Demand Cited in $2.9 Million Loss

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/16/90

Union Valley Corp., citing reduced demand for new homes in the New Jersey market, said it incurred a net loss of $2.9 million in the second quarter. That compares with year-ago net income of $536,000, or 11 cents a share. Revenue fell 53% to $12.2 million from $26 million.

In a related matter, Howell, N.J.-based Union Valley, said it may dispose of certain assets outside its main real estate business under terms negotiated with First Fidelity Bank, Newark, N.J., to pay back debt. First Fidelity asked for the repayment of $84.1 million in debt due Aug. 1.

The commercial and residential builder said losses from disposed assets may be added to its reserves for possible losses on certain real estate assets.

In addition to possible disposition of assets, the company said it is in talks with the bank to refinance the debt and to obtain long-term funding for its core home building business.

Sales backlog in the first half was $29.2 million, or 233 units, compared to $47.7 million, or 314 units, a year ago, the company said. The backlog includes non-binding reservations.
