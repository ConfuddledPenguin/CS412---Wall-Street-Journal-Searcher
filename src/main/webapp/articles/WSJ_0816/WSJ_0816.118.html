Marketing & Media: Scripps to Pay $100 Million To Improve Denver Tabloid

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/16/90

DENVER -- E.W. Scripps Co. said it will spend $100 million on a new production center for the Rocky Mountain News. The company plans to buy seven eight-unit Goss Colorliner offset presses and new distribution equipment that will be installed at the News's satellite production center. Color production at the News, a tabloid newspaper, is constrained by its current production facilities. The new presses will allow the paper to print up to 48 pages with full color each day. The company said the result will be a "more vibrant and attractive newspaper."

The News has a daily circulation of about 365,000 and a Sunday circulation of about 415,000. The competing Denver Post, owned by William Dean Singleton and Richard Scudder and operated by MediaNews Group of Houston, has a daily circulation of 247,000 and a Sunday circulation of 409,000. Mr. Singleton said the News' plans will "bring them into the 20th century where everyone else is."

Separately, the News reported that it is interested in taking a minority position in a baseball-ownership group if Denver is awarded one of the planned National League expansion teams. In an election Tuesday, Denver voters approved the levy of a one-tenth of 1% sales tax to pay for a new baseball stadium. The levy won't be imposed unless the city gets a team.
