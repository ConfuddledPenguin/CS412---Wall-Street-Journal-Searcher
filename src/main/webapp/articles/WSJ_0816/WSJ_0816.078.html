Broker, Ex-Print Aide Must Pay Back Funds In Insider-Trade Case

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 08/16/90

A judge ordered a stockbroker and a former printing-plant employee to pay $66,099 for committing securities fraud by trading on information from advance copies of Business Week. The Securities and Exchange Commission sued former Prudential-Bache Securities Inc. broker Brian Callahan of Anaheim, Calif., and former print worker William Jackson of Long Beach, Calif., in January. In May, a jury found that the men had traded on confidential information.

The SEC charged that Mr. Jackson, who worked at the Torrance, Calif., printing plant of R.R. Donnelly & Sons Co., one of the companies that prints Business Week for McGraw-Hill Inc., traded on information from the magazine before it hit the newsstands, and then passed the information on to Mr. Callahan.

U.S. District Judge Stephen V. Wilson ordered Mr. Callahan to repay the $1,148.19 he made, plus $8,000 in commissions he earned by trading on the information. He was also fined $27,444.57, three times the amount he was ordered to return and the maximum allowed under securities laws. Mr. Jackson was ordered to repay profits of $19,506.99 and was fined $10,000.
