Thomson Corp. Picks Hall as Chief Executive Of Publishing Group

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/16/90

TORONTO -- Robert Hall was named to succeed Robert Jachino as chief executive officer of Thomson Corp.'s specialized publishing group, which last year accounted for 39% of Thomson's $5.11 billion sales and 50.5% of its operating profit. Mr. Jachino, 56 years old, who also is an executive vice president of Thomson, said in a statement that he planned to give up his executive positions next Jan. 1, to spend more time with his family. He will continue as non-executive chairman of the group, which produces a wide range of specialized publications in printed and electronic forms.

Thomson said Mr. Hall, 59 years old, also will succeed Mr. Jachino Jan. 1 as a Thomson director. In the meantime, Mr. Hall will become chief operating officer of the group Sept. 1. Mr. Hall currently is chief executive officer of Thomson Financial Services, which publishes financial information.

Thomson also publishes U.S. and Canadian newspapers and operates a leisure travel business in Britain.
