Marketing Brief -- Carolco Pictures Inc.: Firm's Net Jumps, Boosted By Success of `Total Recall'

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/16/90

Carolco Pictures Inc.'s second-quarter net income soared to $8.8 million, or 28 cents a share, from $2.6 million, or nine cents a share, a year earlier, on the strength of the box office hit "Total Recall." Revenue of the Los Angeles entertainment company more than doubled to $81 million from $34.2 million, largely because of "Total Recall," which has grossed $114 million so far.

"We expect additional contributions from `Total Recall' during the remainder of 1990 from both the domestic theatrical marketplace and the home video release by Live Home Video in December," said Peter Hoffman, president and chief executive officer. The film's release in overseas markets continues through the end of the year, and he said foreign results are expected "to exceed the domestic results."

Carolco's revenue from feature films rose to $67.9 million from $27.9 million in the quarter, while television syndication revenue rose to $10 million from $5.1 million. Carolco recognized $1.1 million as its share of earnings from Live Entertainment Inc., of which it holds a 54% stake, as compared with $840,000 a year earlier.

For the six months, Carolco's net rose to $6 million, or 20 cents a share, from $812,000, or three cents a share, while revenue increased 91% to $100.2 million from $52.5 million.
