Business Brief -- Thomas Cook Group Ltd.: U.S. Agency Clears Firm's Deak International Purchase

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/16/90

Thomas Cook Group Ltd. closed the purchase of the North American retail foreign-exchange operations of Deak International Ltd., after the Federal Reserve Board cleared the U.S. portion of the transaction. The purchase is valued at about $12 million.

Thomas Cook, a travel-services specialist, is a unit of the London banking concern Midland Bank PLC. Deak, which has interests in retail foreign exchange and precious metals, is a unit of Jarden Morgan Ltd., a New Zealand financial-services concern.

Cook acquired 72 branches in the U.S. and 38 in Canada. Earlier this year, Cook acquired 20 Deak retail branches in the U.K. and one in New Zealand. Cook plans to convert the Deak branches to the Cook name; Deak's retail operations posted revenue of US$35 million for the year ended April 30.
