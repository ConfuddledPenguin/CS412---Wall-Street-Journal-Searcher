Business Brief -- Telesphere Communications Inc.: Firm to Report Net Income Soared for Second Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/16/90

Telesphere Communications Inc. expects to report tommorrow that net income for the second quarter soared to about $700,000, or about three cents a primary share, on a 40% revenue increase to about $62 million, Eugene Ruiz, Telesphere executive vice president, said in a interview. In the year-earlier second quarter, the long-distance telephone concern earned $90,000 on revenue of $44.2 million.

Mr. Ruiz said the company expects earnings for the full year to exceed the 1989 profit of $789,000, or five cents a share, which included a $624,000 gain from a tax-loss carry-forward.

Last year was the first profitable year for Telesphere since 1982.

Telesphere, Oakbrook Terrace, Ill., is the largest supplier of interactive 900 services, which for the most part have been used for so-called gab lines or to provide jokes and updates on soap operas. About half of the company's revenue is generated by 900 service, the rest comes from supplying operator assisted long distance service to hotels and small business.

Harry Thompson, Telesphere chairman, said the company is trying to interest consumer products companies and retailers to experiment with using 900 lines as marketing tool.

Sears, Roebuck & Co. and Montgomery Ward & Co. are among the companies studying whether to use the service, a spokesman said.
