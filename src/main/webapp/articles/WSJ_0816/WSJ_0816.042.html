Letters to the Editor: Beware Congress's Disarming Character

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/16/90

Your July 30 editorial "Defense in Free Fall" should be mandatory reading for every American voter. Without emotion you have focused on the key issue: Will America allow itself to be disarmed by Congress as it did after World War I, World War II, Korea and Vietnam? As you pointed out, the country can afford to have a national defense posture that will allow it to influence world events in the future. The president's proposed budget will bring military spending as a percentage of the GNP down below what it was prior to World War II and will reach that point through a logical, responsible drawdown. Within his proposed budget is the B-2 Stealth bomber, which is affordable and is a necessity for strategic deterrence.

I find it appalling that the House Armed Services Committee voted to kill the B-2. I have confidence that the Senate and, it is hoped, the full House, will support the B-2. The country should be concerned about the possible irreparable damage to our nuclear deterrent force as well as our total military posture.

John T. Chain Jr.

General, USAF

Commander in Chief

Strategic Air Command

Omaha, Neb.
