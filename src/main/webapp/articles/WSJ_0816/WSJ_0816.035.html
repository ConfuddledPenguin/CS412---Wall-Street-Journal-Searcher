Letters to the Editor: Chasing Paper Down The Road to Ruin

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/16/90

Your July 11 page-one article "Costly Advice: Wall Streeters Helped Interco Defeat Raiders -- but at a Heavy Price" reminded me that hardly a day goes by that you don't report several instances of why this country is in economic decline. Far too many people, far too much time and far too many resources are devoted to innovative accounting, tax avoidance, mergers, acquisitions and litigation. U.S. industry is headed, in the main, by "professional" managers who have no background in or dedication to the product or service lines of their companies. Profit, risk aversion and perks seem to be their foremost priorities. Meanwhile in Europe and Japan, managers lead their companies through technological and institutional innovations.

Several years ago, Robert B. Reich characterized this phenomenon in his book "The Next American Frontier." He accused today's managers of having "replaced product entrepreneurialism with paper entrepreneurialism."

James P. Loomis

Columbus, Ohio
