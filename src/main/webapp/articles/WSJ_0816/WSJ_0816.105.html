Marketing & Media: Coppola and Company Are Dismissed by Judge From Chapter 11 Status

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/16/90

SANTA ROSA, Calif. -- A federal bankruptcy court here dismissd Chapter 11 bankruptcy proceedings filed six months ago by movie director Francis Ford Coppola and his company Zoetrope Productions. Mr. Coppola, best known for directing the film epic "The Godfather," had filed for personal and corporate protection under Chapter 11 in January, seeking protection from creditor lawsuits while he worked out a plan to pay his debts.

Zoetrope Productions, a unit of San Francisco-based Zoetrope Corp., then listed debts of $28.9 million and assets of $22.2 million.

John Peters, president of Zoetrope Corp., said in a telephone interview yesterday that Mr. Coppola had emerged from both his personal and corporate Chapter 11 proceedings, and confirmed that the court had also approved a proposed settlement of related litigation. However, he declined to elaborate.

Mr. Coppola had been embroiled in a dispute over a loan from the Singer family of Canada, which financed his movie, "One From the Heart."

"I can't characterize it, or cast more light on its significance at this time," Mr. Peters said of the settlement. Mr. Coppola's attorneys couldn't be reached.
