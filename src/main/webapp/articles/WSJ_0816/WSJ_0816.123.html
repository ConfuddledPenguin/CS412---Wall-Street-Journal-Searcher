Marketing & Media: Warner-Lambert Tests Gum With No Sugar, No Stick

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/16/90

MORRIS PLAINS, N.J. -- In a bid to capture a bigger share of the post-bubble gum set, Warner-Lambert Co. said it is testing a new sugarless gum that won't stick to dentures. The health care and consumer products company said the new brand, called Cristal, will be available in southern Texas and Louisiana by mid-September. Cristal was launched in Canada last year.

With such brands as Trident sugarless gum, Bubblicious bubble gum and Chiclets sugar-coated gum, Warner-Lambert already controls about 30% of the $1.8 billion U.S. market for chewing gum. But that market is slow-growing and extremely competitive.

With Cristal, the company is taking aim at consumers 35 years old and over, who have crowns, caps or bridges. The company estimates that some 30 million Americans wear dentures, and that at least 80 million have had serious dental work.

"Historically, gum chewing declines with age," said Maria Gagnier, category director for gum at Warner-Lambert. "But as Baby Boomers age, gum chewing has become more acceptable -- just like Rock & Roll music."

Warner-Lambert is also hoping Cristal will stick it to top gum rival Wrigley Co., which lords over the U.S. market with an estimated 50% share. Wrigley virtually created the U.S. market for non-stick gums with its Freedent brand, launched in 1975. The company is now testing a sugarless version of Freedent, according to a Wrigley spokesman.
