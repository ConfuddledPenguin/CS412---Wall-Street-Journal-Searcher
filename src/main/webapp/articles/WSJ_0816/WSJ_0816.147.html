Teledyne Ex-Official Charged in Scheme To Defraud the Navy

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/16/90

NEW YORK -- In federal court in Manhattan, the former president of a Teledyne Inc. division was charged with making false statements to the Defense Department in connection with an alleged scheme to defraud the Navy on a helicopter contract. The defendant, Cirino Giampapa, 69 years old, of Massapequa, N.Y., was president of Teledyne Hydra-Power, a division of the parent company's Teledyne Industries Inc. unit. In a settlement with the Defense Department, the unit last year paid $11.9 million to cover overcharges stemming from the scheme, as well as interest and penalties, the U.S. attorney's office in Manhattan said.

Walter Bonner, an attorney for Mr. Giampapa, could not be reached for comment.

Avraham Moskowitz, the assistant U.S. attorney in charge of the case, said Teledyne Hydra-Power submitted false claims totaling $4.5 million between 1980 and 1986. The alleged scheme involved inflating the number of hours worked. The figures were provided to the Defense Department and to its general contractors to justify prices charged by the Teledyne division for helicopter parts it made.

A Teledyne spokesman said the company's auditing staff uncovered the problem in 1986, which was then flagged to the Defense and Justice departments.

The U.S. attorney's office said that Michael Rosenberg, 41, of Suffern, N.Y., a former controller at Teledyne Hydra-Power, pleaded guilty to one count of conspiring to defraud the U.S. in connection with the alleged scheme. Morton Baine, 54, of New Rochelle, N.Y., also a former controller at the division, was charged with participating in the alleged conspiracy.

Carl Bernstein, an attorney for Mr. Baine, said his client pleaded innocent.

Elkan Abramowitz, an attorney for Mr. Rosenberg, couldn't be reached for comment. The U.S. attorney's office said Mr. Rosenberg's sentencing is set for Oct. 17 before U.S. Judge Vincent Broderick.
