Credit Ratings: Moody's Cuts Ratings On Long-Term Debt Of First City Bancorp

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/16/90

NEW YORK -- Moody's Investors Service Inc. said it downgraded its long-term debt ratings of First City Bancorp of Texas, citing the bank holding company's exposure to the weak Texas real-estate market. The investment rating service also downgraded its rating for long-term deposits at the company's lead bank, First City, Texas-Houston N.A., for the same reason.

First City said management "strongly disagrees" with the move and "has a far more positive view of the improving Texas economy and its impact on First City's real-estate portfolio."

Moody's, while acknowledging "some improvement" in Texas real estate, said the large real-estate portfolio in Collecting Bank, a separate entity set up by First City Bancorp to liquidate problem loans, as well as the real-estate holdings of the lead bank, "could require substantial further provisioning and may make it difficult to establish an ongoing earnings stream."

The changes, affecting about $353 million of debt, lowered the holding company's senior obligations to Ba-2 from Ba-1; subordinated obligations to Ba-3 from Ba-2; and senior preferred stock to Ba-3 from Ba-2. Moody's confirmed the company's commercial paper rating at Not Prime.

Meanwhile, the rating of the lead bank's long-term deposits fell to Baa-3 from Baa-2, while the rating for short-term deposits was confirmed at Prime-3, Moody's said.
