Business Brief -- Charter Medical Corp.: Hospital Chain Posts Drop In Third-Quarter Earnings

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/16/90

Charter Medical Corp. reported a big drop in third-quarter earnings. For the quarter ended June 30, Charter posted a net loss of $40.6 million on revenue of $315.2 million, compared with a net loss of $5.1 million on revenue of $308 million for the year-earlier period.

The Macon, Ga.-based hospital chain also said it made a scheduled $39 million interest payment on its 14.25% and 14% subordinated debentures. "We're current with all our debt and expect to remain so," said J. Rodney Laughlin, Charter's executive vice president for human resources and development.

He said the company has been working since June to improve cash flow by cutting costs, mainly through reducing personnel. In addition, he said, Charter will receive $42 million from the sale and leaseback of 12 hospitals to Health Care Property Investors Inc. of Los Angeles.
