Auto Insurer Backs Down Under New Jersey Order

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 08/16/90

An insurance group within ITT Corp. edged back from a confrontation with the state of New Jersey by agreeing to continue writing auto insurance policies in the state if necessary to keep the rest of its business there. ITT Hartford Insurance Group, which has said its total premium volume in New Jersey last year was $224.6 million, was responding to an order by the state insurance commissioner, Samuel Fortunato, who ordered all ITT Hartford units to surrender their certificates to sell insurance if one of those units, Twin City Fire Insurance Co., withdrew from the state's auto insurance market.

Twin City, the only ITT Hartford unit selling auto insurance in New Jersey, has protested that it would suffer "severe losses" under the state's new auto-insurance law. The unit had premium volume of $72.6 million last year, though that figure includes premiums on homeowner lines as well as auto coverage.

The state says Twin City insures about 1% of New Jersey drivers.

ITT Hartford said it "will continue to operate within the letter and spirit of the law." But the company signaled its dispute with the state isn't over, saying it will seek "administrative and legal remedies" to Mr. Fortunato's order. The company has already been in litigation with the state over the law.

The insurance commissioner was acting under what state officials call a "long arm" provision in the law meant to strengthen the state's position in dealing with insurers who want to pull out of the auto insurance market.
