Who's News: New York Yankees Put Nederlander Onto Center Stage --- Theatre Owner Is Selected To Succeed Steinbrenner As Team General Partner

  • Author: Meg Cox
  • Date: 08/16/90

NEW YORK -- In a choice that seemed to come out of left field, the New York Yankees named a theater owner and producer to succeed George Steinbrenner as general partner. Robert Nederlander, president of the Nederlander Organization and a limited owner of the baseball team, was voted into the job by his fellow owners at a meeting in Cleveland. Mr. Nederlander couldn't be reached for comment.

Mr. Steinbrenner said, "I have great confidence in Robert as my selection. He comes from a family that has been associated with my family for 20 years."

A Yankees spokesman said that Mr. Nederlander was approached after Mr. Steinbrenner's son, Hank, told his father "he wasn't interested" in running the Yankees.

The appointment of Mr. Nederlander, having been approved by a majority of the 18 limited partners, must also be approved by three-quarters of the American League owners and a majority of the National League owners.

Mr. Nederlander, 57 years old, is described by New York theater producers as soft-spoken, low-key and businesslike, working behind the scenes in the family business. "Robert is the quiet member of the family; very decent and well respected," said producer Arthur Cantor. "He's a lawyer, a good businessman and he won't do anything without weighing it. He is temperamentally totally opposite from George Steinbrenner."

"Anyone who can survive in the theater can survive in baseball: the Nederlanders are very gutsy," said another Broadway producer, David Brown. "Baseball is a kind of theater: they have a lot in common."

Mr. Nederlander is one of five brothers and a sister, most of whom are closely involved with the family business, run primarily from New York and Detroit. The Nederlanders own or lease about 30 theaters and concert venues around the country and in London. Mr. Nederlander's more flamboyant brother, James Nederlander, is chairman of the Nederlander Organization in New York and directly involved with booking the theaters and producing shows. Robert Nederlander handles primarily financial and real estate aspects of the family business, said Nederlander general manager Arthur Rubin.

The Yankees limited partners were asked not to talk to the press about the appointment, but a source close to the team said that Mr. Nederlander was "a tremendously popular choice," adding, "It's going to be a whole different ballgame."
