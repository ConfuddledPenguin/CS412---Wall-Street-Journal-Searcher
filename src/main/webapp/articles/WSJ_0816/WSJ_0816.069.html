Investment Banks Hire Fewer MBAs, Survey Finds

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/16/90

NEW YORK -- Fewer students who earned a master of business administration degree this year found their way to Wall Street, according to a new survey. The nation's top 14 investment banks hired 221 graduating MBAs this year, an 8% decrease from the 240 MBAs hired during 1989, according to the study by the Whitney Group, a New York-based executive search firm that specializes in financial services. This year's figure is nearly 50% below hiring levels in 1987, before the October market crash, the survey said.

Of the investment banking firms polled, eight said they hired fewer MBAs than last year, including Dean Witter Reynolds Inc., Salomon Brothers Inc., Shearson Lehman Brothers Inc. and Smith Barney, Harris Upham & Co. Two of the companies surveyed said they were hiring the same number as last year.

Not all the firms queried said they were pulling back, though. Four companies, including Kidder, Peabody & Co. and Dillon, Read & Co., said they had hired more MBAs than last year.

"Caution remains the trend, with most investment banking firms moderately bearish on the number of deals they foresee," said Gary Goldstein, managing director of the Whitney Group.
