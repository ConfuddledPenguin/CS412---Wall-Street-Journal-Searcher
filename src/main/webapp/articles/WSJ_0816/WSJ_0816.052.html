National City Unit Gets Insurance Award Totaling $21.7 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/16/90

CLEVELAND -- National City Corp. said a subsidiary, First National Bank of Louisville, was awarded a judgment of $21.7 million, plus interest, against insurance companies that provided coverage for employee fraud. The Federal District Court in New Orleans instructed Aetna Casualty & Surety Co., a unit of Aetna Life & Casualty Co., Hartford, Conn., and the Chubb Group of Insurance Cos., Warren, N.J., to pay the sum to First National Bank of Louisville under blanket bond policies. The judgment, which is subject to appeal, is for the full amount of the insurance coverage purchased by the bank, National City said.

First National filed the claim to recover a portion of its losses on eight loans totaling $30 million that the bank made in 1985 and 1986. The bank said Kevin L. DeWitt, then a loan officer in the bank's real estate loan department, falsified credit information and other documents, leading the bank to make loans to borrowers who weren't creditworthy. The bank said it was able to prove that Mr. DeWitt received at least $40,000 for his help in arranging the loans.

Mr. DeWitt last February pleaded guilty to felony charges of falsifying bank documents, but withdrew his plea when a U.S. district judge in Louisville refused to accept a plea bargain that the judge said was too lenient. Mr. DeWitt, who is awaiting trial on those charges, couldn't be reached for comment. His attorney declined to comment.

Aetna said it hasn't decided whether to appeal the judgment, and attorneys for Chubb couldn't be reached.
