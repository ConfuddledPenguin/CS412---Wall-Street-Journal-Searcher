Who's News: Thomas Fritz Resigns From Top Three Posts At Investors Savings

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/16/90

MINNEAPOLIS, Minn. -- Investors Savings Corp. said Thomas Fritz resigned as chairman, president and chief executive officer of the thrift holding company because of "differences in management style." James M. Burkholder, chief operating officer and chief financial officer, and John G. Lohmann Jr., secretary of the company and chief lending officer, will assume portions of Mr. Fritz's responsibilities until the board finds a successor, Investors Savings said.

In an interview, Mr. Burkholder characterized Mr. Fritz's departure as "friendly" and the result of a "mutual decision" reached with the board.

Mr. Fritz, a co-founder of the $575 million asset thrift, "is fully supportive of our management team and . . . as a major shareholder, his confidence in our future remains undiminished," Investors Savings said in a letter to employees made public yesterday. Mr. Fritz couldn't be reached for comment.
