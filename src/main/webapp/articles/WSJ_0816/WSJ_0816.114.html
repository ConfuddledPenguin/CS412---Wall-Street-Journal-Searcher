International Brief -- Bond Corp. Holdings Ltd.: Shareholders of Firm's Unit Agree to Purchase Breweries

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/16/90

A settlement for the sale of Alan Bond's Australian breweries appeared to head off threats by disgruntled U.S. investors to liquidate the operations. The accord means Mr. Bond's brewing stake will be diluted by a big new shareholder-New Zealand brewer Lion Nathan Ltd. For months, Bond Corp. Holdings Ltd. has been trying to sell the breweries to its 58%-owned Bell Resources Ltd. unit.

Shareholders of Bell Resources approved that company's proposal to purchase Bond Corp.'s Australian breweries for a previously agreed price of A$1.85 billion. Bell Resources has a longstanding loan to cash-strapped Bond Corp. of A$1.2 billion; it has been turned into a deposit on the breweries. The sale is part of a corporate overhaul intended to reduce debt that totals about five billion Australian dollars (US$4.03 billion).

Just ahead of the shareholders meeting, Bell Resources announced a proposal to sell half the breweries to Lion Nathan, partly in return for a commitment from Lion Nathan to provide A$300 million to help fund the repurchase of US$510 million in debentures issued to American investors by Bond Brewing Holdings Ltd., the Bond group's main Australian beer unit.
