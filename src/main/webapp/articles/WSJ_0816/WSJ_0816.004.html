Letters to the Editor: S&L Scandal: the Nature of the Beast

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/16/90

I never cease to be amazed that the American people, and especially our political and business leaders, continue to miss the mark when it comes to making needed changes ("Tricky Issue: Thrift Scandal, Viewed as Political Dynamite, Is Slow to Detonate" page one, July 13). The problem is not that major responsibility for the thrift debacle should be placed on either Democrats or Republicans. The real problem is -- given man's nature to take unfair advantage of his fellow men -- that any type of government subsidy serves as an open invitation for would-be plunderers to swarm in.

Until we get the federal government out of subsidizing financial institutions, we will continue having one scandal after another. Of course, the same thing can be said for government subsidies or "aid" to farmers, small businesses, big corporations, the poor, the homeless, the blind, the aged.

There is only one solution to the scandals and scams, and that is to limit the role of civil government to the one and only job it is competent to do: maintain law and order so that people can be free to serve each other in an atmosphere of free competition.

Tom Rose

Professor of Economics

Grove City College

Grove City, Pa.

Your article reminds me about your very comprehensive stories several years ago about Democrats Jim Wright, former House speaker, and Tony Coehlo, former majority whip, and their involvement in the S&L scandal.

To now quote Democratic National Chairman Ron Brown and then not publish a quote from the Republican National chairman shows your unfairness. Kevin Phillips does not speak for the Republican Party.

We are all angry about the S&L mess, but any insinuation that President Bush is the person most responsible ignores history and the facts.

This problem was created by stupid tax loopholes and S&L regulations, created by a Democratic Congress to benefit real-estate wheelers and dealers.

Joyce Hugman

Gladewater, Texas
