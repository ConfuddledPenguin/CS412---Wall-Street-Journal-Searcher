What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/16/90

INDUSTRIAL OUTPUT leveled off in July, indicating that the economy continues to stumble. Factory production slipped 0.1% and utility output fell 0.4%, but mining posted a 1% gain. The nation's industrial operating rate fell to 83.4% of capacity, down from 83.6% in June, which could temper some of the inflation pressure stemming from higher oil prices. ---

Cutting U.S. dependence on foreign oil by up to 550,000 barrels a day is envisioned under a Bush strategy calling for voluntary conservation and higher domestic output. The plan would replace much of the crude the U.S. imported from Iraq and Kuwait.

---

Stock and bond prices closed slightly higher after Bush damped early optimism about a possible resolution with Iraq. The Dow Jones industrials rose half a point to close at 2748.27. The dollar ended mixed.

---

Trump could have a negative net worth of as much as $294 million, newly released documents show. And despite a bank bailout, the developer could have a $106 million negative cash flow in the year ending in April.

Atlantic City's casinos posted a collective $4.3 million loss before extraordinary items, marking the industry's worst second quarter ever. Trump's three casinos fared much worse than the other nine houses.

---

Drexel's bankruptcy lawyers were harshly criticized by a federal judge for the size of their fees. He reduced their requested payments by 14%.

---

Tokyo stock prices soared Wednesday, giving the Nikkei its third-largest single-day advance. The index jumped 1439.59 points, or 5.4%. But analysts aren't confident that the market's recent slide is over.

---

Ford's plan to keep $100 million from the pension fund of a defense unit Ford is selling to Loral is being questioned by the Pentagon, which says it may lay claim to the money.

Ford is idling two car-assembly plants and extending a shutdown of a third in the face of sagging sales.

---

Bank of New York's plan to acquire Northeast Bancorp for $195.6 million was rejected by the Fed, which said the deal would hurt Bank of New York's capital position.

---

First Executive's customers redeemed $1.2 billion of insurance policies in the second quarter. The figure, though declining, indicates that the firm is still under financial pressure.

---

Sun Microsystems and IBM signed a patent-licensing accord under which Sun will pay royalties to IBM. The pact comes in the wake of IBM's tough patent-enforcement policy.

---

Genentech and Abbott Labs settled a two-year-old patent dispute about Genentech's TPA, removing the last legal cloud over the heart drug.

---

Tektronix, Zenith Electronics and four other firms were picked by the Pentagon to work on military uses of high-definition television.

---

Salomon proposed to offer an equity security that would allow investors to bet on the price of oil.

---

Columbia S&L estimates the cost of selling its junk-bond portfolio at $50 million or more, and said its retail deposit base eroded by nearly $250 million in the past four months.

---

Imperial Oil agreed to sell refining and marketing assets in Canada's Atlantic Coast provinces, plus some other assets, in three transactions valued by analysts at about $260 million.
