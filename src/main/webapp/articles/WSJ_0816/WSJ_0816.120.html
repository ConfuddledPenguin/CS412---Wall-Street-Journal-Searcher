Washington Energy Unit Settles Dispute Over Preferred Stock

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/16/90

SEATTLE -- Washington Energy Co. said its Washington Natural Gas Co. unit reached an out-of-court settlement with former holders of its Series D and E preferred stock. The preferred holders were identified as Ford Motor Credit Co., MDFC Equipment Leasing Co., Westinghouse Credit Corp. and Nationwide Life Insurance Co. and its affiliates.

The issue arose from the company's decision in January 1987 to repurchase the preferred shares prior to their maturity, based on the utility's evaluation of the Tax Reform Act of 1986. However, a spokeswoman said the preferred owners objected to terms of the repurchase.

The settlement, pending a definitive agreement, would result in a charge against earnings of about 17 cents a share, or $2.6 million after taxes, for the fiscal year ending Sept. 30. The company has about 16 million shares outstanding.

Washington Energy had profit of $3.3 million, or 17 cents a share, in the most recent quarter ended June 30.

The utility company's stock closed at $20.125, down 25 cents, in national over-the-counter trading yesterday.
