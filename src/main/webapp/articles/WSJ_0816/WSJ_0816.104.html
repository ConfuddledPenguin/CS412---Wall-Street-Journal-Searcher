Continental Air to Match Competitors and Cut Fares

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/16/90

HOUSTON (AP) -- Continental Airlines said it will cut fares for flights after Labor Day, matching reductions already announced by several other major carriers. The carrier said it will lower its discount fares for fall vacation travel in the continental U.S. by an average of 30%.

Continental said the lowered fares will be exempt from a pending 5.3% increase that the carrier and several others plan to implement in about a week to defray the rising costs of jet fuel.

The airline's reduced fares apply to round-trip tickets purchased by Aug. 29 for travel between Sept. 5 and Dec. 16, excluding the five days around Thanksgiving.

On Tuesday, United, Northwest and American offered lower fares in response to the post-Labor Day fare cuts announced Monday by Trans World Airlines and Eastern Airlines.

The 5.3% fare increases were to have gone into effect yesterday, but Continental, United Airlines and Northwest Airlines said they were delaying the increases until Wednesday. American postponed the increases until next Thursday.

The carriers said they were taking the action for competitive reasons and that the delay meant they would absorb higher fuel costs for the time being.
