Credit Ratings: Continental Bank's Securities Ratings Downgraded by S&P

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 08/16/90

NEW YORK -- Standard & Poor's Corp. said it downgraded its ratings on Continental Bank Corp.s senior debt to Triple-B from Triple-B-plus and on its preferred stock to Double-B-plus from Triple-B-minus. Also, S&P said it affirmed the rating on the corporation's commercial paper at Single A-2.

Separately, S&P said it downgraded the ratings on Continental Illinois Overseas Finance Corp. N.V.'s subordinated debt to Triple-B-minus from Triple-B.

Also, Continental Bank N.A.'s senior notes, long-term certificates of deposit, and letter of credit backed issues were lowered to Triple-B-plus from Single-A-minus, and its short-term certificates of deposit and letter of credit backed issues are affirmed at Single A-2, the rating agency said.

S&P said about $1 billion in term debt outstanding is affected.

S&P said: "Continental is almost entirely focused on wholesale banking lines of business, whose core profitability has eroded as a result of a lower volume of transactions, particularly for leveraged buyout financings. The company has initiated a restructuring program to help rein in overhead expenses that had gotten out of line in a slower business environment.

"Even assuming full implementation of the restructuring program," S&P said it "expects core profitability will remain under pressure given the intense competition and industry overcapacity in wholesale banking."

S&P also said it is "concerned about Continental's large exposure to highly leveraged transactions and the recent deterioration in that portfolio's asset quality."

"Partly offsetting these concerns are an adequate level of capital and full reserves for the remaining exposure to lesser developed countries," S&P said.
