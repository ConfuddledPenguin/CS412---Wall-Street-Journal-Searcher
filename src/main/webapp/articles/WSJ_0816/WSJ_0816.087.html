SEC Issues Injunction To Minkow, Officers In ZZZZ Best Fraud

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/16/90

LOS ANGELES -- A year and a half after Barry Minkow was sentenced to a 25-year prison term, the Securities and Exchange Commission has slapped injunctions on him and several other officers and associates of his collapsed carpet-cleaning firm, ZZZZ Best Co. In response to an SEC suit alleging various fraudulent representations in ZZZZ Best's registration and financial statements, Mr. Minkow and eleven other defendants agreed to decrees permanently enjoining them from violations of various securities laws. Two other defendants, Maurice Rind and Mark M. Roddy, haven't signed consent decrees.

The SEC said it is seeking $700,000 from Mr. Rind, the amount paid to him by ZZZZ Best for 22 electrical generators that were listed on the company's books as worth $2 million. By inflating the value of the generators, ZZZZ Best was able to obtain a listing of its securities on the National Association of Securities Dealers Automated Quotation System, the SEC said. An attorney for Mr. Rind couldn't immediately be reached for comment. Mr. Roddy, who was sentenced to five years for his role in the ZZZZ Best affair, is in prison in Texas and couldn't be reached for comment.

Mr. Minkow, chairman and founder of ZZZZ Best, was sentenced in March, 1989, to 25 years in prison and ordered to pay $26 million in restitution for his role in the ZZZZ Best fraud. An SEC spokeswoman said the delay in filing this latest action came because several of the allegations weren't in the original federal criminal case, and the allegations took considerable time to investigate.
