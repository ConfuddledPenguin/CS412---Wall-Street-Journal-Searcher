New Securities Issues

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/16/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report. CORPORATES

Household Finance Corp. -- $150 million of 9% senior notes due Sept. 1, 1995, priced at 99.80 to yield 9.05%. The non-callable notes were priced at a spread of 72 basis points above the Treasury's five-year note. Rated single-A-2 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and single-A-plus by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Salomon Brothers Inc.

Southern California Edison Co. -- $100 million of 8 1/4% first and refunding mortgage bonds due Aug. 15, 1993, reoffered via a group led by Morgan Stanley & Co. at 99.80 to yield 8.325%. The bonds, which are non-callable, were priced at 25 basis points above the Treasury's three-year note. The issue is rated double-A-2 by Moody's and double-A by S&P.

MUNICIPALS

Tennessee Housing Development Agency -- $78.6 million of single-family mortgage revenue bonds, via a Merrill Lynch Capital Markets group. The bonds, rated single-A by Moody's, were priced at par. Serial bonds yield from 6.20% in 1992 to 7.40% in 2005. There are term bonds yielding 7 1/2% due in 2010 and 7 5/8% due in 2022.

MORTGAGES

Chase Mortgage Finance -- $111 million of 9.35% Remic mortgage securities offered in four classes by Greenwich Capital Markets Inc. The offering, Series 1990-D, is backed by whole loans with a weighted average coupon rate of 10.93% and weighted average remaining term to maturity of 27.5 years.. Prices range from 82.03125 for the accrual class with an average life of 18.11 years to 100.0625 for the class with an average life of 3.39 years. The issue is rated double-A-2 by S&P and double-A by Moody's.

EUROBONDS

State Bank of New South Wales (Australia) -- 100 million Australian dollars of 14 1/4% Eurobonds due Sept. 12, 1994, priced at 101 1/8 for a yield less fees of 14.47% via Deutsche Bank Capital Markets Ltd. Guaranteed by the government of New South Wales. Fees 1 3/4.

Aegon N.V. (Netherlands) -- 100 million guilders of bonds due Oct. 1, 1997, priced at 101.15 via Rabobank Nederland. Coupon 10% for first two years and 16 3/4% less the 12-month Amsterdam interbank offered rate for the final five years. Investor guaranteed a minimum return of 4 1/2%. Fees 1.
