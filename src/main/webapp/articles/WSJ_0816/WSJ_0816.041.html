Letters to the Editor: Art Is in the Eye Of the Art Experts

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/16/90

My thanks to Jack Flam for speaking the embarrassing truth about art as we approach the turn of this century and as I unrelentingly struggle to experience whatever it is displayed in galleries across the nation ("Mixed-Up-Media: The Same New Thing," Leisure & Arts, July 25). To my dismay, I see this trend taken to the Biennale and I continue to find myself at the mercy of those with the power to honor and display what I consider mediocrities. It seems that even the "shocking" pieces, monotonic in their effect, fail in their alleged purpose as they move ever closer to the saturated categories of violence and pornography.

Are art experts playing the part of a Hans Christian Anderson character, convincing themselves that these are works of substance? And am I to feel the guilt of an honest commoner as I am instructed to revise my understanding of what art is?

Perhaps the Biennale should return to the noncompetitive symposium it was in 1972. Only then might we be spared a "mindless repetition" of Biennale 1990 in the years to come.

Stacie A. Krafczek

Sturgis, Mich.
