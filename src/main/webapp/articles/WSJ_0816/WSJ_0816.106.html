Allegheny International Faces a Chapter 11 Delay

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/16/90

PITTSBURGH -- Allegheny International Inc.'s expected emergence from Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection after 2 1/2 years was delayed at the 11th hour for technical reasons. The postponement of the closing -- and a halt in the company's stock trading on the New York Stock Exchange -- came after the Nuclear Regulatory Commission raised some questions about the company's Chemetron unit, a shell company that apparently still retains a nuclear license. The NRC must approve any transfer of ownership or control when a nuclear license is involved.

The delay doesn't appear to pose serious problems, however. The bankruptcy court has already given final approval to Allegheny's reorganization under a new name, Sunbeam/Oster Corp. And the prospective owners, Japonica Partners, indicated yesterday that they plan to work toward a resolution giving it control of the consumer products company.

Directors expect to install new top management, led by Chairman Paul B. Kazarian, once ownership is officially transferred to Japonica.
