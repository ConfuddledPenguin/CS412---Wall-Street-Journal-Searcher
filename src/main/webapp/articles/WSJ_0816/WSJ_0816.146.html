Fruit of the Loom Stake Is Cut by Farley In $43 Million Sale

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/16/90

CHICAGO -- William Farley's investment empire continued to shrink with a sale of $43 million of Fruit of the Loom Inc.'s common. The sale to Land Free Investment Ltd., based in the Channel Islands, apparently reduces the voting stake in the underwear manufacturer controlled by Mr. Farley to little more than 50% from nearly 60% two years ago, according to people close to Mr. Farley's closely held interests.

Farley officials didn't return phone calls seeking comment yesterday.

Mr. Farley was pressured to make yesterday's sale by some of his bankers, sources said. They said profit will be used largely to pay overdue bank debt. A $37.9 million payment to certain bondholders that was due yesterday probably won't be made for now, they said.

Meanwhile, negotiations continued among Mr. Farley, the bondholders and bankers over control of West Point-Pepperell Inc., according to sources close to that situation. Mr. Farley hasn't been able to complete last year's $1.56 billion tender offer for West Point-Pepperell, a textile concern based in West Point, Ga.

Mr. Farley's financial strength, which was based on junk bonds, ebbed after the collapse of his investment banker, Drexel Burnham Lambert Inc.

Farley Inc., the selling entity in yesterday's stock transaction, said in a Securities and Exchange Commission filing that profit from the sale will "significantly reduce the likelihood" of the "adverse consequences of an unfavorable restructuring of Farley Inc."

Yesterday's sale involved 3,750,000 Class B shares of Fruit of the Loom at $11.50 a share. Farley's SEC filing said it sold 335,000 Fruit of the Loom Class A shares on the open market between June 19 and July 26 for $12 to $13.25 a share.

Fruit of the Loom closed yesterday at $12.625 a share, up $1.125, in American Stock Exchange composite trading.
