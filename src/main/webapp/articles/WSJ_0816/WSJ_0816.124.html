Bechtel-Led Group Wins High-Speed Rail Franchise

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/16/90

SAN FRANCISCO -- A consortium led by Bechtel Group Inc. was awarded a franchise for the development of a $5 billion high-speed rail system linking Southern California and Nevada. The planned 265-mile system would connect Anaheim, Calif., and Las Vegas, Nev., by an elevated magnetized railway carrying trains that can reach speeds as high as 300 miles an hour, Bechtel said.

The Super Speed Maglev Transport system is to be a team project, coordinated by Bechtel and involving technology from Transrapid International of Germany, financing by C. Itoh Co. of Japan, and operations and maintenance services of Amtrak.

While the system isn't expected to be completed before 1997, Bechtel said the project will eventually create 13,000 to 25,000 permanent jobs in California and 23,000 to 28,000 permanent jobs in Nevada, along with 10,000 temporary construction jobs.

Bechtel said it expects the system to attract some five million round-trip riders a year by the year 2000, drawn by fares projected at 30% less than commercial airline ticket prices.
