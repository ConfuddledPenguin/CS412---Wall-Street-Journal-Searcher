Marketing & Media: Pearson's Penguin Says Chief Executive Resigned Position

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/16/90

NEW YORK -- Penguin USA, a unit of London-based Pearson PLC, announced the resignation of Morton Mint, chief executive officer. Mr. Mint has only been in the post since late October, after years of running Penguin's operations in Canada. The resignation was abrupt and shocked the publishing community. Mr. Morton couldn't be reached for comment, nor was Peter Mayer, executive chairman of Penguin USA, available. The company released a statement that said the two executives had "policy differences with respect to management of the company in the future."

Penguin named Marvin Brown, chief administrative officer, to the chief executive post "for the time being." The company said Penguin USA will move toward more decentralization within the company, with each of the adult divisions having its own president as well as publisher.

Penguin has been one of a number of publishing houses experiencing turmoil in the past year after a time of rapid growth by acquisition. Last fall, Penguin closed the adult publishing division of E.P. Dutton, firing a number of editors in what many in publishing felt was an insensitive and abrupt manner.
