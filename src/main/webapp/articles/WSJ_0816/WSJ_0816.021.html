Letters to the Editor: Garbage In, Garbage Out

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/16/90

The solution to "AWFUL TV," suggests Robert Goldberg in his July 23 Leisure & Arts article, is for parents to unplug the television set. Society's problem is that TV is the parent to millions of kids -- latchkey kids, one-parent kids, two-parents-working kids. Mr. Goldberg says that the average family watches seven hours of TV a day. How much do poor families watch? How many poor kids get to bed before the 8 p.m. start of adult programming?

Parents, of course, teach mostly by example. So what does our TV parent teach? Sex, violence and stupidity. Moreover, the voltage increases every season. For the coming season, Mr. Goldberg tells us, CBS President Howard Stringer has promised to exchange "bland" and "dull" for "coarse" and "crude."

Why do we need programs so stimulating that they glue us to our TV sets? The British have dull TV, and good manners. The Japanese have silly TV, and a powerful work ethic. Neither have the violent, salacious fare so readily available on American TV.

Yes, Mr. Goldberg, I agree that the real problem is from "the neck up." But if we're inputting garbage into the tender, receptive brains of children, to use a computer analogy, what do we expect for output? And what are the consequences to our society?

Richard A. Lewis

New York
