International: ICI Sells Its Stake In Enterprise Oil To 2 Securities Firms

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/16/90

LONDON -- Taking advantage of the bull market in oil, Imperial Chemical Industries PLC sold its 24.9% stake in Enterprise Oil PLC to two British securities firms, Warburg Securities Ltd. and Cazenove & Co., for #679.6 million ($1.29 billion). The two companies immediately sold the 113.3 million shares for a total of #687.7 million to about 80 institutions in what apparently was the largest "bought deal," or crossed trade, in U.K. history. Most of the institutions were British, although there were some U.S. and Japanese buyers, said Phillip Ellick, a director of Warburg, the lead broker in the deal.

The sale is a check to Societe Nationale Elf Aquitaine, the French state-controlled energy and chemical company, which earlier this year had sought to buy ICI's Enterprise stake. Elf currently owns 25.2% of the British North Sea oil producer, whose earnings are expected to jump because of the Persian Gulf oil crisis.

ICI made an estimated #500 million on the sale, said Colin Short, an executive director. ICI acquired the stake in 1987 by selling its oil and gas interests to Enterprise.

The sale of the Enterprise stake had been expected for some time. ICI, which has moved aggressively into specialty chemicals to offset cycles in the bulk chemical business, has been shedding peripheral lines.

By fragmenting the Enterprise stake among dozens of holders, ICI and its brokers apparently averted a political problem that might have arisen if a majority interest in a British oil company was acquired by a foreign state-controlled entity.

Enterprise contributed #37.1 million to ICI's #1.53 billion 1989 pretax profit. Given the sharp rise in oil prices recently, it might have added #60 million this year and up to #75 million in 1991, said Dion Steward, an analyst at Paribas Capital Markets Group.
