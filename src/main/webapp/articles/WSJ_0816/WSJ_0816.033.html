Letters to the Editor: It Ain't Over Till It's Over, We Thought

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/16/90

Terry Eastland's July 23 editorial-page article about the coming Supreme Court appointment contained two items that seemed odd to me: 1) He writes that President Eisenhower declared his appointment of Justice William Brennan to the court was "his worst mistake." Historical scholars will usually concede that statement was made about Chief Justice Earl Warren.

2) Is there some secret message in the description of the writer at the article's end? It says he is "completing a book on the Reagan and Bush presidencies." Really? So soon? Should Dan Quayle call the moving van?

Alfred J. Lurie

New York
