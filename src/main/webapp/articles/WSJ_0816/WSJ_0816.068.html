Lands' End, Squeezed By Discount Sales, Posts $2.2 Million Loss

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/16/90

DODGEVILLE, Wis. -- Lands' End Inc., hurt by discount sales needed to clear out excess inventory, reported a surprising loss of $2.2 million, or 12 cents a share, for its second quarter ended Aug. 3. The catalog-sales concern, which in recent quarters has been troubled by sluggish sales and swelling inventories, had been expected to report a downturn in earnings from the year-earlier period's $2.2 million, or 11 cents a share. But the lowest estimates didn't foresee a loss.

The latest period's loss came despite a 12% increase in sales, to $117.1 million from the year-earlier $104.2 million.

Liquidations of excess inventory represented about 16% of overall sales during the latest quarter, compared with about 10% a year earlier. The company also said that selling, general and administrative expenses climbed 22% -- significantly higher than the sales growth.

The announcement came after the stock market closed. Analysts are likely to react today, cutting full-year earnings estimates that had averaged around $1.25 a share compared with $1.45 a share a year earlier.

But Sharon Rappaport, a Montgomery Securities analyst, said the news isn't all bad. "They've taken the first step in addressing their problem," she said. Ms. Rappaport has been saying for some time that Lands' End's inventories are too high and its merchandise is dull.
