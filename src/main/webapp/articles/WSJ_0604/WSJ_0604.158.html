CML Group Says Net In 3rd Period Fell 10% While Sales Increased

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/04/90

ACTON, Mass. -- CML Group Inc. said third-quarter net income dropped 10%, while sales rose 10% to $58 million, from $52.7 million a year ago. Separately, the company told analysts that following weak sales growth in February and March, sales rebounded sharply in April and May, rising 23% and 25%, respectively, from year-earlier levels.

The direct marketer of consumer products said earnings, including discontinued operations, for the quarter ended April 28, fell to $2.5 million, or 38 cents a share, compared with $2.3 million, or 35 cents a share, in the year-earlier quarter.

Profit from continuing operations in the third quarter fell sharply, to $1.7 million, or 26 cents a share, from a year-ago $3.8 million, or 59 cents a share. CML sold its Carol Reed unit in February and the Boston Whaler power boat company last August.

"CML has turned the corner -- and their numbers were right on the button, for a change," said Harry E. Wells, an analyst with Adams Harkness & Hill, Boston. "Things are really looking up."

CML Chairman Charles M. Leighton said that while the softness in income was anticipated, NordicTrack and the Nature Company performed ahead of expectations. But he added that the "continued sluggishness of the retail apparel environment" hurt the performance of the Britches of Georgetown group of stores.

CML also said it has gained a foothold in the Far East with the signing of an agreement with the retail subsidiary of the West Japan Railway System to begin opening CML's Nature Company stores in Japan. The flagship store will open in Osaka in May 1991, CML said.

The move will be CML's first foray into international markets. Mr. Leighton said he felt Japan will be "a perfect market" for Nature Company stores. Payment will be on a royalty basis, CML said.

For the nine months ended April 28, net income was $664,000, or 10 cents a share, including discontinued operations, compared with $11.1 million, or $1.71 a share, for the year-ago period. Sales rose to $217.9 million, from $187.8 million in the same period last year. Income from continuing operations for the nine months rose to $14.6 million, or $2.20 a share, from year-ago $13.7 million, or $2.10.
