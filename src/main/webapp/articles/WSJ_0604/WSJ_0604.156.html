Canada to Drop Form For Exports to U.S.

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/04/90

OTTAWA -- The Canadian government decided to eliminate an export declaration form for most Canadian goods bound for the U.S. The decision will benefit more than 15,000 Canadian exporters, by eliminating the need for more than 200,000 documents each month, the government said.

The move was made possible by a U.S.-Canada agreement, whereby each country will use the other's import statistics to produce its export figures.

The U.S. and Canada have more bilateral trade than any other pair of countries. Their merchandise trade in the first three months of this year totaled US$42 billion.
