Business Brief -- ABM Gold Corp.: Deficit for 1990 Expected; Charges From Mine Cited

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/04/90

ABM Gold Corp., Toronto, said it expects to report a loss for 1990, mainly because of higher depreciation and amortization charges from its Colomac gold mine, scheduled to begin commercial production Sept. 30. ABM, 52%-owned by Northgate Exploration Ltd., had 1989 net income of US$2 million, or 12 cents a share, on revenue of $7 million. At the annual meeting, where shareholders approved changing the company's name to NorthWest Gold Corp., John Kearney, chairman, president and chief executive officer, said, "ABM's income statement will substantially change when Colomac achieves commercial production . . . and revenues and expenses from the project appear on ABM's financial statements." Last year, ABM had depreciation and amortization costs of $570,000. Mr. Kearney wouldn't specify how much these charges will increase this year, but said they will be "much, much higher."

He estimated Colomac's maximum output is 200,000 ounces of gold a year, at an average operating cost of $260 an ounce. The low-grade, open-pit mine in the Northwest Territories has an eight-year life.
