Business Brief -- Nashville Network: Radio Broadcaster to Halt Round-the-Clock Operations

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/04/90

Nashville Network's radio division, which delivered around-the-clock programming via satellite emphasizing country music, said it would cease full-time operations at the end of the month. The 18-month-old service, called TNNR, will continue to produce a weekly show that is sold through syndication. TNNR is a joint venture of Opryland USA, a privately held company owned by Gaylord Broadcasting Co., and Group W Satellite, a unit of Westinghouse Electric Co.

Since the network began in December 1988, it signed only 63 affiliates. According to a spokeswoman for Group W, an unspecified number of on-air personalities will be laid off.

The venture cost the partners between $5 million and $10 million, said Robert Unmacht, managing editor of M Street Journal, a Washington, D.C., publication that follows the radio industry.

The discontinuation of TNNR will not affect TNN, a cable TV network, the Group W spokeswoman said.
