Japan's Car Sales Rise

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/04/90

TOKYO -- Sales of cars, trucks and buses in Japan increased 6.1% in May from a year earlier, to 444,040 units, a new record for the month, the Japan Automobile Dealers' Association reported. Sales of midsized passenger cars in particular have been rising, helped by introduction of a 3% consumption tax in April last year; that tax replaced a heavier commodity tax, and lowered sticker prices of midsized cars more widely than those of small-sized cars.

Among manufacturers, sales of the two largest car makers in Japan posted healthy increases. Toyota Motor Corp. posted an 8.4% rise, to 194,199 units. Nissan Motor Co. gained 5.5% to 103,420 units. Sales by the two makers accounted for 67% of total sales in May.

Mitsubishi Motor Corp. sales were up 3.3% at 27,606 units; while Mazda Motor Corp. sales rose 10.9%, to 36,172 units.
