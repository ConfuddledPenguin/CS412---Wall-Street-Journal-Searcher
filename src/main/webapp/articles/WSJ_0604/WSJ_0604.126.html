Business Brief -- Allianz AG: Investment in East Germany Has Floor of $589.2 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/04/90

Allianz AG Chairman Wolfgang Schieren said the West German insurance giant's planned investment in East Germany will total at least one billion marks ($589.2 million). Mr. Schieren said in an interview with the weekly business magazine Wirtschaftswoche that the sheer costs of Allianz's plan to buy into the East German insurance market also have made it less controversial, because "in the Bonn Economics Ministry, as well as at the Cartel Office, people realize that financially strong companies are needed for the economic reconstruction of East Germany."

Allianz said in late March that it intended to take a 40% stake in East Germany's state insurance monopoly. In the face of strong criticism, Allianz repeatedly asserted it isn't intent on monopolizing the newly opened East German insurance market. Mr. Schieren said that "we don't want to buy a state monopoly to continue it as a private monopoly, but all insurance companies from West Germany and other foreign countries can start in East Germany at the same time."
