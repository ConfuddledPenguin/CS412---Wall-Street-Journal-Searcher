Kane-Miller Corp. May Seek to Garner Control of Amrep

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/04/90

NEW YORK -- Kane-Miller Corp. said it may seek control of Amrep Corp. through a proxy solicitation, tender offer, merger or other transaction. Kane-Miller, a Tarrytown, N.Y., food processor, holds 8.1% of Amrep, a builder of communities and international distributor of magazines.

On the news Friday, Amrep rose 25 cents to $7.625 in New York Stock Exchange composite trading. At that price, Amrep's about 6.6 million shares would cost about $50 million.

Howard W. Friedman, Amrep's chairman and chief executive officer, declined to comment.

In a Securities and Exchange Commission filing, Kane-Miller said that on May 30 it asked Amrep for a shareholders list, to use if Kane-Miller should decide to solicit proxies to gain control of Amrep's board.

Kane-Miller spoke with Amrep about board representation, but the discussions were "uneventful," the filing said. Kane-Miller said it has "extensively studied" public information on Amrep and its plans might change.

Another substantial Amrep holder is Peter B. Bedford, president of the Lafayette, Calif., real estate developer Bedford Properties Inc. In January, Mr. Bedford disclosed a 5.2% stake and said he intended to raise it to as much as 10%. In late April, he said the stake had risen to 8.7%. A spokesman for Bedford Properties wouldn't comment.

For the nine months ended Jan. 31, Amrep had a net loss of $492,000, or seven cents a share, compared with net income of $1.1 million, or 16 cents a share, in the year-earlier period. Sales rose 5% to $70.3 million.
