Hiring Plans Remain Modest, Says Survey By Manpower Inc.

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/04/90

MILWAUKEE -- Hiring plans by U.S. employers continue to be modest, although activity in the Midwest and Southwest is stronger than elsewhere, according to a Manpower Inc. survey. The employment services concern said its latest quarterly survey of 15,000 companies found that 27% plan to enlarge their work forces in the third quarter, while 8% plan reductions.

The figures are the weakest for any third quarter since 1986, Manpower said. They are barely more positive than the results for the second quarter, when 26% of the employers anticipated hiring and 8% foresaw reductions.

The survey found big differences in the outlook from region to region. "The current employment outlook really depends on where you live," said Mitchell S. Fromstein, Manpower's president.

Activity appears strong in the Midwest and Southwest, he said, but there is "a very visible weakness" in the Northeast, where only 21% of companies polled expect to increase hiring and 10% plan cuts. The comparable figures were 29% and 7% in the 1989 third quarter. In the Midwest and West, hiring projections are ahead of second-quarter levels and only marginally below last year's.

Industries with the most active hiring plans are in the construction and services groups, where activity is traditionally brisk in the summer, and in the wholesale and retail trades, which have recovered some of the strength they lost in the second quarter.

Manufacturing shows further softness, meanwhile, and the finance, insurance and real estate sector continues to slow down. That sector has been showing a year-by-year deterioration in hiring plans since 1986. For the coming three months, only 19% of those companies expect to add staff, the lowest figure since early 1983, while 8% plan cutbacks.
