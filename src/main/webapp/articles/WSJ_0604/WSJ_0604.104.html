New Securities Issues

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/04/90

The following were among Friday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATES

BellSouth Savings & Employee Stock Ownership Trust -- $275 million of amortizing medium-term notes, due July 1, 2003, were priced at par to yield 9.19%. The notes were priced at a spread of 0.70 percentage point above the Treasury's 10-year note. Rated double-A-1 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and triple-A by Standard & Poor's Corp., the non-callable issue will be sold through underwriters led by Morgan Stanley & Co.

Dayton Hudson Corp. -- $150 million of 9.35% medium-term notes, due June 16, 2020, was priced at 99.945 to yield 9.36%. The non-callable notes, which may be put back to the company in the seventh year, were priced at a spread of 0.89 percentage point above the Treasury's seven-year note. Rated single-A-3 by Moody's and single-A by S&P, the issue will be sold through Goldman, Sachs & Co.

Boston Edison Co. -- $100 million of 9 7/8% debentures, due June 1, 2020, were priced at 99.09 to yield 9.97%. The debentures, which are non-callable for 10 years, were priced at a spread of 1.55 percentage points above the Treasury's 30-year bond. Rated Baa-2 by Moody's and triple-B by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Goldman Sachs.

Commercial Credit Corp. -- $100 million of notes, due June 15, 2010, were priced at par to yield 8.70%. The non-callable notes, which may be put back to the company in the third, sixth, ninth, 12th and 15th years, were priced at a spread of 0.375 percentage point above the Treasury's three-year note. Rated Baa-1 by Moody's and single-A-minus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Bear, Stearns & Co.

International Paper Co. -- $100 million of 9.40% notes, due June 1, 2002, was priced at 99.703 to yield 9.44%. The non-callable notes were priced at a spread of one percentage point above the Treasury's 10-year note. Rated single-A-3 by Moody's and single-A-minus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by First Boston Corp.

Mountain States Telephone & Telegraph Co. -- $90 million of 9 5/8% debentures, due June 1, 2030, were priced at 99.417 to yield 9.682%. The debentures, which are non-callable for five years, were priced at a spread of 1.25 percentage points above the Treasury's 30-year bond. Rated double-A-3 by Moody's and double-A-minus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Morgan Stanley. Mountain States Telephone is a unit of U S West Inc.

EUROBONDS

Union Bank of Finland (Finland) -- $200 million of variable rate notes, due July 9, 2000, priced at par via Merrill Lynch International. Initial margin is London interbank offered rate plus 0.15 percentage point, or, alternatively, Libor plus 0.5 for first five years and Libor plus 0.625 for second five years.

Swedish Export Credit Corp. (Sweden) -- 30 billion yen ($197.6 million) of 6 7/8% bonds due June 28, 1993, priced at 101 1/4 to yield 6.92% via lead manager LTCB International Ltd.

Skopbank (Finland) -- $100 million of 50-year variable rate notes priced at par, via Merrill Lynch International. Extendible into a perpetual note, on amendment of Finnish law. Initial margin is 7/16 over Libor, or, alternatively, Libor plus 0.75 for first five years, then Libor plus 0.875 for next five years and Libor plus 1.25 thereafter.

Dresdner International Financial Markets (Australia) Ltd. (German parent) -- 75 million Australian dollars (US$57.6 million) of 15 1/4% bonds due June 23, 1993, priced at 102 via Dresdner Bank.
