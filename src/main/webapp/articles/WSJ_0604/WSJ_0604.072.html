U.S. Lawsuit Is Filed In Shearson Account's Securing SBA Loans

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/04/90

NEWARK, N.J. -- The federal government filed suit in U.S. District Court here, charging that a predecessor of the New York-based securities firm Shearson Lehman Hutton Holdings Inc. improperly disbursed the proceeds of an account. The government claims that the account was put up as security for two loans, totaling $602,000, that the Small Business Administration made to Connecticut Aerosols Inc., Milford, Conn. Terms of the loans, which were made to the company in 1975 and 1978, required that at least $275,882 be left in the account until the loans were paid, the complaint states.

Connecticut Aerosols and its president, John J. Franchini, defaulted on the loans, the civil action alleges. The SBA sought the account's proceeds, the complaint charges, but Shearson or its predecessor, E.F. Hutton & Co., "liquidated the account and paid" the proceeds to Mr. Franchini "or otherwise disbursed" them.

Shearson has refused to make any payment to the government, the complaint states. The suit demands that Shearson pay the remaining principal and accrued interest on the loans as of Feb. 1 -- a total $405,006 -- plus the interest accrued since then.

A spokesman for Shearson said the firm, a subsidiary of American Express Co., New York, hadn't seen the complaint and couldn't comment. Mr. Franchini couldn't be reached for comment.
