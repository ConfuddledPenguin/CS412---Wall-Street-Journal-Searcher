Credit Ratings: Ratings on Xerox's Debt, Preferred Stock Are Under Review

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/04/90

NEW YORK -- Standard & Poor's Corp. said it will consider lowering its ratings on debt and preferred stock of Xerox Corp. and several of its subsidiaries. About $2 billion of the parent company's debt is affected.

S&P said its review is a result of the possible effect of problems at the company's Crum & Forster insurance unit. Separately, S&P said it will consider lowering its rating on Crum & Forster's claims-paying ability, citing uncertainty about the quality of the company's reinsurance arrangements and continuing weakness in the property-casualty market.

S&P said it was concerned about Crum & Forster's potential for producing charges against Xerox's earnings, and its possible need for capital infusions from the parent. It also said it will review Crum & Forster's ability to contribute to dividends to Xerox, a business equipment and financial services concern based in Stamford, Conn.

Aside from the Crum & Forster rating, which is double-A, S&P placed on its CreditWatch list: Xerox's senior debt, rated single-A-plus; its subordinated debt, rated single-A; its preferred stock, rated single-A-plus; Xerox Credit Corp.'s senior debt, rated single-A-plus; and Xerox Credit Overseas Finance NV's senior debt, rated single-A-plus.

In a statement, Xerox said: "We are obviously disappointed at this announcement and will meet with representatives of Standard & Poor's in the near future. Our balance sheet is healthy and we have sound strategies in place to improve financial performance and shareholder value."
