Business Brief -- Integrated Resources Inc.: Bidding Expires for Auction Of Interest in Partnerships

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/04/90

Integrated Resources Inc., New York, said the bidding expired last week in the auction of its general partnership interest in its American Insured Mortgage Investors partnerships. Integrated, which is operating under federal bankruptcy-law protection, said it didn't receive any unconditional, cash bids of at least $40 million, as required under the court-approved auction. But Integrated said it received bids exceeding $40 million with certain contingencies, which it didn't identify. The American Insured Mortgage Investors partnerships hold federally insured mortgages. They raised $789 million from 66,000 investors in the 1980s. Integrated recently said it was seeking to find a new lender for a mortgage in one of the partnerships, after the borrower missed two interest payments. The $99.9 million mortgage, which the American Insured 84 partnership originated, was one of 21 in a portfolio totaling $166 million.

Under the auction procedure, Integrated must decide whether to accept a bid by June 15. The bankruptcy court must approve any bid accepted by Integrated.
