Technology: Daisy Systems Says Bondholders Seek Bankruptcy Status

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/04/90

BOULDER, Colo. -- Daisy Systems Corp. said some of its bondholders filed a petition in federal court in Denver seeking to force it into involuntary bankruptcy proceedings. The struggling maker of computer-aided engineering stations has been in default on $118 million in debt for several months, starting with missed interest payments last November. Much of the debt was incurred when Daisy acquired Cadnetix Corp. for $200 million at the end of 1988. About $40 million of the debt is in the form of secured loans, with the balance comprising two series of convertible debentures.

A Daisy spokeswoman said bondholders and the secured creditor, Heller Financial Inc., have held a series of discussions to avert the bankruptcy filing. But a one-year statute of limitations on the right to file the action, created by the Cadnetix acquisition, expired May 30. The petition was filed before the deadline, according to the spokeswoman, but negotiations continue "and meanwhile we are operating our business as usual."

But disappointing sales and resulting losses have continued the company's liquidity problems. Daisy posted a loss for the first quarter ended Dec. 31 of $18.3 million, or $1.03 a share, wiping out remaining shareholder equity. Second-quarter results continued the trend, as the company's net loss widened to $21.6 million, or $1.08 a share, from $10.4 million, or 60 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue slumped to $33 million from $47.2 million.

Daisy closed Friday in over-the-counter trading at 37.5 cents a share, down 12.5 cents.
