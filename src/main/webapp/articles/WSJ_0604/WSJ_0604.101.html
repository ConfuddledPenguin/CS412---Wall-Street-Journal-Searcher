Keating and Bondholders Are Close to Accord on Suit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/04/90

SANTA ANA, Calif. (AP) -- Investors who lost $192 million in junk-bond investments by the collapse of American Continental Corp., parent of Lincoln Savings & Loan Association, are close to a settlement with a law firm for the company, lawyers said. Lawyers for Charles H. Keating Jr., former head of Lincoln and chairman of American Continental, are negotiating with lawyers for 11,500 bondholders who have sued to recover their losses.

Almost 22,000 small investors purchased American Continental bonds at Lincoln's 29 Southern California branches. The bonds became worthless after the parent company filed for protection from creditors under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code in April 1989 and federal regulators seized the thrift unit.

Ten federal lawsuits, filed on behalf of 11,500 bondholders, were consolidated into one lawsuit.

A settlement could be completed as early as tomorrow, said Ken Feinberg of Kaye, Scholer, Fierman, Hays and Handler Inc., the firm representing Mr. Keating. A lawyer for the bondholders said he agreed.

Any signed settlement is subject to final approval of federal and state judges hearing the bondholders' lawsuit.
