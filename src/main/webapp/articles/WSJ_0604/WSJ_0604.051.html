Baton Broadcasting Expects Fiscal '90 Net To Decrease Sharply

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/04/90

TORONTO -- Canada's largest publicly traded television broadcasting company, Baton Broadcasting Inc., said it expects earnings to drop more than two-thirds from last year as a result of higher production costs and lower-than-expected gains in advertising revenue. Douglas Bassett, president and chief executive of Baton, said he expects the company to report net income of between 20 Canadian cents and 25 Canadian cents (17 and 21.3 U.S. cents) in the fiscal year ending Aug. 31.

A year earlier, net was 20.4 million Canadian dollars ($17.3 million), or 74 cents a share.

"This has been an absolutely lousy year, financially," Mr. Bassett said.

He said "significant" increases in programming costs helped push the company's net down to C$4.2 million, or 15 cents a share, for the first six months, compared to C$14.3 million, or 52 cents, in the previous year's first half.

In the first eight months of the company's fiscal year, he said Baton's flagship Toronto television station increased spending on new programming by about 79.2% over the previous year.

He said a 1.6% increase in advertising revenue in the same period is less than expected, and won't be sufficient to cover heavy spending on programming. Nor will programming expenses be cut, he said.
