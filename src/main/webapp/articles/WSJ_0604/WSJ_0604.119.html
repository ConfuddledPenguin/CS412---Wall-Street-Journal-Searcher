SEC Charges Ex-Broker Misspent Athletes' Funds

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/04/90

WASHINGTON -- The Securities and Exchange Commission is trying to sack a former Robinson-Humphrey Co. broker for allegedly misappropriating more than $1 million from at least 18 professional football players. In a complaint filed in federal court in Atlanta, the SEC charged that broker Oscar Ayala of Marietta, Ga., defrauded the players, who held accounts at the Shearson Lehman Hutton Inc. unit from 1988 to the present. The SEC asks that he be enjoined from such activity and seeks unspecified relief.

The agency alleges that Mr. Ayala, instead of purchasing securities for his clients, misappropriated at least $1 million in funds for his personal use. A federal judge has issued a temporary restraining order freezing Mr. Ayala's bank and securities accounts and prohibiting the destruction of any records relating to the transactions.

The agency's Atlanta regional administrator, Richard Wessel, said Robinson-Humphrey brought the matter to the attention of the SEC and other law enforcement agencies. Mr. Ayala's lawyer, Gary Freed, said that his client also is under investigation by the Justice Department for possible criminal prosecution and that his client would be eager to huddle with the SEC to settle the case. He added, "We feel that all of the accusations are not accurate and are exaggerated."
