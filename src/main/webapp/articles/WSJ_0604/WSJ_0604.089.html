Business Brief -- General Motors Corp.: Some 1985 Models Recalled For Air-Fuel Calibration

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/04/90

General Motors Corp. has started recalling about 410,000 1985-model cars that exceed the federal exhaust standard for carbon monoxide, the Environmental Protection Agency said. The models are the Buick Electra, Century and Somerset Regal; the Oldsmobile Ninety-Eight Regency, Calais and Cutlass Ciera and Cruiser; and the Pontiac Grand Am. All have 3.0- or 3.8-liter multiport fuel-injected engines.

The repair, which should take about 30 minutes, is free. The EPA discovered the problem during routine testing. To correct it, GM will install a modified computer memory chip to alter the engine's air-fuel calibration.

Excessive amounts of carbon monoxide -- a byproduct of incomplete fuel combustion -- can weaken heart contractions, and can be particularly harmful to people with lung disease, anemia or cerebral-vascular disease.
