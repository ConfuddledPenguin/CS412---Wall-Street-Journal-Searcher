Workplace (A Special Report): Openers --- Office Memos: Some Predictions About the Workplace of the Future

  • Author: Kerry Pechter
  • Date: 06/04/90

Lots of Chiefs

In 2000, there will be 81.6 million baby boomers between the ages of 35 and 55. But there will only be 56.1 million people between 20 and 35 for them to manage. One result: In 2010, the typical large business will have half as many management levels and one-third as many managerial positions as it does today.

(Sources: WEFA Group; Forecasting International)

What Paperless Office?

Computers will definitely eliminate some paper. But there will be plenty left. By 2000, U.S. businesses will still need a space equivalent to all of the office space in Pittsburgh to file the 120 billion sheets of new paper they will generate every year.

(Source: Wohl Associates)

Talk, Talk, Talk

Despite fax machines and electronic mail, businesses will still be addicted to the telephone. One prediction: Businesses will double their use of long-distance telephone service by 2000.

(Source: International Resource Development Group)

Milk Break

To keep new mothers working in the office, more companies will be offering breast-feeding breaks as part of their package of benefits.

(Source: Research Alert)

Ballot Boss

Companies, concerned about declining morale and productivity, may allow employees to select supervisors by vote.

(Source: World Future Society)

Pencils Down

Drug testing will proliferate in the workplace -- but it will be less invasive. Within 20 years, most companies will give workers periodic performance-based tests -- gauging such things as short-term memory or object recognition -- to see whether they are impaired by drugs.

(Source: Institute for the Future)

Cleaning Up

Between now and 2000, about 556,000 janitorial and maid-service jobs will be created. That's more than the combined new jobs for computer programmers, accountants and auditors.

(Source: Bureau of Labor Statistics)

Just Relax

Employers will increasingly provide mental-health days to give workers some relief from job stress.

(Source: World Future Society)
