Plan by Recognition Equipment to Buy Its Stock Is Delayed

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/04/90

DALLAS -- Recognition Equipment Inc. said it delayed a planned tender offer for $15 million to $20 million of its stock for up to six more months while it works on improving its business and continues to try to sell assets. The maker of document-processing equipment initially agreed to purchase its stock when Prospect Group Inc., a New York leveraged buy-out concern, made a $15 million loan to the company. However, Prospect later agreed to let Recognition use the funds for short-term working capital for as long as four months after it made the loan in February.

Recognition said its board moved to further amend the agreement with Prospect after considering the capital needs associated with its plans to focus on its imaging equipment and because planned asset sales are moving more slowly than expected. It also cited pending settlement discussions with the U.S. Federal Reserve Banks over a contract dispute involving currency-processing systems.

The company said that delaying the tender offer until as late as the end of November will allow it to look at its results for the year ending Oct. 31. A spokeswoman said the decision isn't the result of a liquidity problem, saying the company had approximately $48 million in cash and equivalents at April 30.
