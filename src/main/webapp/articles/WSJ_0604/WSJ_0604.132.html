Charter Medical Corp. To Close 25 Centers, Lay Off 600 Workers

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/04/90

MACON, Ga. -- Charter Medical Corp., as part of an effort to trim costs, said it will close about 25 outpatient counseling centers and idle about 600 employees across the country. Charter, a psychiatric-care company, went private in a 1988 buy-out and recently encountered problems in its fiscal second quarter, ended March 31, when it posted a $67 million loss on revenue of $475.2 million. The year earlier, Charter had a $14.6 million loss on revenue of $397.3 million.

The most recent figures, which included a $35 million pretax charge for accounting problems, raised some concerns on Wall Street about the company's ability to service its debt. At the time, Charter said it would cut capital expenditures and other costs to ensure its ability to meet its payments.

The company's executive vice president for human resources and development, Rod Laughlin, said yesterday that the current closings and terminations reflect efforts to cut costs as well as seasonal layoffs in the hospital business. Patient loads, he said, typically lighten in the early summer.

Charter has between 250 and 300 counseling centers across the country, Mr. Laughlin said, and a total of about 20,000 employees. The layoffs, he said, some of which will come through attrition, involve primarily administrative positions as opposed to those involved directly in medical care. All the moves are expected to be completed in the current quarter, he said.

Mr. Laughlin said the company doesn't expect to make any further closings or dismissals.
