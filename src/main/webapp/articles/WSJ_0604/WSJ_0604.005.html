Marketing & Media: Pathe Says Accord Is Near on Offer For MGM/UA

  • Author: Rhonda L. Rundle
  • Date: 06/04/90

LOS ANGELES -- Pathe Communications Corp. expects to complete its $1.27 billion tender offer for MGM/UA Communications Co. this week, although analysts cautioned that unresolved financing issues could still trip up the deal. Theodore Cohen, Pathe executive vice president and assistant general counsel, said through a spokesman that he thinks the $20-a-share offer will be wrapped up on schedule. "At this stage, he is confident it will close Thursday," the spokesman said.

A spokesman for MGM/UA, based in Culver City, Calif., said late Friday that "everything we have seen indicates that the deal is still on track."

That optimistic view was bolstered last week after Pathe disclosed a few more details of its financing arrangements, which hinge on Luxembourg-based Comfinance S.A., the closely held main vehicle for Pathe principal Giancarlo Parretti, and on Time Warner Inc., the New York entertainment and publishing company.

Time Warner had vaguely agreed in April to "arrange for or provide" $650 million to Pathe "on a bridge loan basis" in exchange for a lien against the UA film library. Time Warner later indicated the loan might have a six-year term. But in a filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission last week, Pathe spelled out that Time Warner plans to make the loan directly. A technical revision of the agreement calls for Time Warner to pay the funds directly to Pathe, rather than funnel them through MGM/UA.

However, the Time Warner financing pact was still being negotiated late last week. One possible snag is that Time Warner has insisted that the bulk of Pathe's remaining financing be in the form of equity, not debt. Pathe has said that Comfinance will provide $450 million from working capital and available funds.

Raising a red flag, the filing states that "while {the Comfinance} arrangement is technically structured as a loan despite the requirement of the Time Warner agreement that it be provided as equity, {Pathe} believes that the Comfinance loan is equity for purposes of the Time Warner agreement since the Comfinance loan may be repaid" with Pathe common stock.

A Time Warner spokesman declined to speculate on how big a stumbling block this might be, saying only that "we are evaluating that funding arrangement."

Some analysts remain unconvinced that Pathe will complete the acquisition, despite having put up $150 million in earnest money. "I still have skepticism," said Jeffrey Logsdon, an analyst at Crowell, Weedon & Co. in Los Angeles. "It's difficult to obtain comfort that Comfinance has the capability" to come up with the funds.

Mr. Logsdon and other analysts have speculated that Kirk Kerkorian, who holds an 82% stake in MGM/UA, may provide some of the needed equity. The MGM/UA spokesman reiterated Friday that Mr. Kerkorian hasn't decided whether he will do that.
