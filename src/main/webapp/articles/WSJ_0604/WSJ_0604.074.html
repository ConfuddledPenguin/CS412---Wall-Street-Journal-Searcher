Orient-Express Hotels Posts 1st-Period Profit Vs. Year-Earlier Loss

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/04/90

NEW YORK -- Orient-Express Hotels Inc. reported profit of $14.4 million, or $1.48 a share, in the fiscal first quarter, vs. a loss of $3.1 million, or 32 cents a share, in the year-ago quarter. But the quarter's results included a onetime gain of $19.4 million on the sale of a hotel and golf course in England. On an operating basis, the hotel and travel company's loss widened to $4.9 million from $4.4 million. Orient-Express attributed the wider loss to the fact that the Easter vacation period fell in the second quarter this year, while it fell in the first quarter in 1989. Revenue rose 5.4%, to $9.9 million from $9.4 million.

Orient-Express also said it agreed in principle to sell a beach resort in the Bahamas to a private investor for $4.2 million. The deal is subject to approval by the Bahamian government, and isn't expected to close until Aug. 31, the company said.

Orient-Express also owns or operates cruise ships and tourist trains, including the London-to-Venice Orient Express.
