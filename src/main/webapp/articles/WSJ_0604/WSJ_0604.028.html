International: Israelis Charge Yair Klein In Military-Export Case

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 06/04/90

Yair Klein, a lieutenant colonel in the Israeli army reserve, was charged Thursday in Jerusalem magistrates court with exporting military know-how without a permit and violating the obligations of a reserve officer. Mr. Klein, 46 years old, is wanted in Colombia for allegedly training cocaine-cartel hitmen. His name has also been mentioned in connection with a shipment of arms bound for Antigua that wound up at the ranch of a major Colombian drug trafficker. An inquiry into that transaction opened in Antigua Friday but was adjourned until July 16.

Mr. Klein was the subject of a page-one Wall Street Journal article last month.

The indictment didn't mention possible links to drug dealers, which Mr. Klein has denied since the accusations first surfaced in August. The court heard only that Mr. Klein agreed with a representative of a group known as the Organization of Cattle Ranchers to train them in military combat, and that he made two trips to Colombia to train members of the Organization of Farmers. Combat training without a license constitutes an illegal military export.

Mr. Klein hasn't yet submitted a defense statement to the court. However, in an April interview with this newspaper he said he didn't seek a permit because the farmers were private individuals who weren't part of a military force. He denied knowing of any links between the farmers and drug cartels.
