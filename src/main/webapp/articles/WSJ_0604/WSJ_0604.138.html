Pacificorp Calls Halt For Now to Buy-Back Of Stock for Merger

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/04/90

PORTLAND, Ore. -- PacifiCorp said it temporarily stopped open-market purchases of its stock in order to allow a quiet period for setting an exchange ratio related to the $250 million stock and cash acquisition of North-West Telecommunications Inc. PacifiCorp, an electric and telephone utility, has been buying its stock to accumulate the about 10.5 million shares it will need at current prices to complete the North-West purchase.

In composite trading Friday on the New York Stock Exchange, PacifiCorp closed at $21.50, unchanged.

As reported, PacifiCorp's agreement to buy the La Crosse, Wis.-based telephone company calls for it to pay North-West holders $225 million of PacifiCorp common stock plus $25 million in cash.

PacifiCorp had about 247.1 million common shares issued at March 31. However, of that total, it held about 7.5 million shares in treasury for the North-West transaction. A spokesman declined to say how many shares PacifiCorp has repurchased since March 31.

PacifiCorp said it ceased repurchases on Friday and expects to value its stock for the North-West transaction based on open-market trading in 10 trading days between June 5 and June 18. After that latter date, it may resume repurchases, PacifiCorp said.

A spokesman said the suspension of repurchases isn't related to PacifiCorp's $1.82 billion all-cash bid to acquire Pinnacle West Capital Corp. That offer, made last month, has been rejected by Pinnacle West, an electric utility based in Phoenix, Ariz.; however, PacifiCorp has hinted that it may pursue Pinnacle West despite its board's opposition.
