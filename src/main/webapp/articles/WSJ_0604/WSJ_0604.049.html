Puerto Rico Banks Get Merger Approval But With Conditions

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 06/04/90

SAN JUAN, Puerto Rico -- Banco Popular de Puerto Rico and BanPonce Corp. said the commissioner of financial institutions of Puerto Rico issued conditional approval of the two institutions' proposed merger. The banks -- Puerto Rico's two largest -- said they filed a motion for reconsideration with the commissioner, because the approval contains restrictions they consider "highly burdensome."

A Banco Popular spokesman said the major conditions imposed on the merger include an 18-month period of oversight by the commissioner's office; no charges for transactions at automatic teller machines; no increases in interest rates and finance charges on loans to small companies with $500,000 or less in debt; and a requirement that two-thirds of local deposits be used to finance local loans.

Banco Popular and BanPonce have agreed to the merger in a transaction valued at $324 million. Under their agreement, which has been approved by stockholders of both companies, BanPonce holders will be able to exchange each of their shares either for shares in the new entity or for cash. In each case, the exchange is valued at $56.25 a share.
