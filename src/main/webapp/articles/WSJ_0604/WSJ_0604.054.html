Circus Circus Reports 1st-Quarter Profit Rose 1.1% to $19.2 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/04/90

LAS VEGAS, Nev. -- Circus Circus Enterprises Inc., a hotel-casino company, reported net income for its first quarter ended April 30 rose 1.1% to $19.2 million, or 70 cents a share, from $19 million, or 64 cents a share, a year earlier. Average shares outstanding in the latest quarter declined to 27.5 million from 29.9 million a year earlier.

Revenue climbed 4.6% to $133.3 million from $127.5 million.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading Friday, Circus Circus shares fell $1.625, to close at $61.125.

The company said operating results at its Circus Circus-Reno location in Nevada "rose markedly" from the year-earlier period, when performance was soft because of bad weather. The company's two properties in Laughlin, Nev., also posted increases in operating results.

But the company's three Las Vegas properties had "moderately lower" results in the quarter, reflecting a drop of "several percentage points" in casino revenue, the company said. Walk-in traffic at Circus Circus-Las Vegas was hurt by competition from Golden Nugget Inc.'s new Mirage resort on the Las Vegas Strip, Circus Circus said.
