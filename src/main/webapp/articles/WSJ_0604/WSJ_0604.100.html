Business Brief -- Prospect Park Financial Corp.: Bank Holding Firm to Post Charge for Fourth Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/04/90

Prospect Park Financial Corp. said it will report a charge against earnings for the fiscal fourth quarter ended April 30, and likely won't meet certain federal capital requirements by July 1. The amount of the charge wasn't specified. The bank holding company based in West Paterson, N.J., said the charge will reflect a further deterioration of the New Jersey real estate market, where its Prospect Park Savings Bank has made loans and where two of the bank's units have pursued development projects.

President Edward Gurak said he believes Prospect Park Savings Bank won't meet regulatory requirements for risk-based capital by July 1, when those requirements change to account differently for banks' real-estate development activities. The future regulatory compliance of the bank's tangible and core capital isn't clear, he said.

If Prospect Park fails to meet any capital requirement, it must file a plan with the federal Office of Thrift Supervision showing how it will meet that requirement by Dec. 31, 1994, Mr. Gurak said.
