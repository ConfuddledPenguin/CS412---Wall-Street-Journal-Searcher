Korean Inflation Rises, Trade Deficit Widens

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 06/04/90

SEOUL, South Korea -- South Korea reported its highest monthly inflation rate and widest monthly trade deficit in nine years. Consumer prices rose 1.9% in May from the previous month, the largest monthly gain since June 1981, according to the Economic Planning Board. In addition, the nation registered a trade deficit of $810 million in May, the widest since December 1981, when the trade balance showed a gap of $894 million, according to the Ministry of Trade and Industry.

The Economic Planning Board also reported that wholesale prices rose by 1.1% in May from the previous month.

As a result of the monthly gain, consumer prices surged 6.7% during the first five months of this year, exceeding last year's inflation rate of 5.7% and this year's yearly target of 5% to 7%.

Wholesale prices rose 3.1% during the first five months.

The Ministry of Trade and Industry said exports in May rose 5.6% from a year earlier to $5.22 billion, while imports surged 20% to $6.03 billion, leaving a deficit of $810 million, on a customs clearance basis.

As a result, the trade balance during the first five months of this year showed a gap of $3.1 billion, exceeding government projections of $2 billion for the year.
