Las Vegas Newspaper Merger

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/04/90

WASHINGTON -- Attorney General Dick Thornburgh authorized two Las Vegas newspapers to combine their business operations, the Justice Department said. The Las Vegas Sun and Las Vegas Review Journal will combine business operations but maintain separate editorial and reporting staffs. Such joint operating arrangements are allowed under the Newspaper Preservation Act, which provides for exceptions to federal antitrust restrictions in the interest of preserving struggling newspapers.

In applying for the antitrust exemption, the two newspapers asserted that the Sun is a "failing newspaper" and thus deserves special treatment. The Justice Department's antitrust division had recommended that the attorney general approve the joint operating arrangement without holding an evidentiary hearing.
