International: British & Commonwealth Is Entering `Proceedings'

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/04/90

LONDON -- After giving up on a possible rescue package to be worked out with creditors, troubled financial-services concern British & Commonwealth Holdings PLC agreed to "enter administration," which is comparable to U.S. Chapter 11 bankruptcy proceedings. On Friday, following unsuccessful negotiations with banks, Britain's Securities and Investment Board removed B&C from the list of financial concerns where other regulated financial houses can place client money. The SIB, after consulting with the Bank of England, explained in a brief statement that it took the unusual step "as a matter of urgency in consultation with the other regulators concerned, in the interests of investor protection."

British & Commonwealth began to teeter in April when it was forced to write off #550 million ($923.2 million) in losses at its Atlantic Computers PLC unit. It placed the unit in the hands of an independent administrator.

Exco International PLC, a B&C subsidiary acquired in late 1986, won't be part of the proceedings, but will be spun off sometime this summer. Exco deals in money brokerage, foreign exchange and money markets around the world. It hasn't been affected by B&C's problems and has maintained its own capital base separate from the parent company.

Richard Lacy, chairman of Exco, said a "management buy-out is the most likely outcome," but he left open the possibility the company might be merged into another financial concern." It's expected that Exco would be acquired for about #200 million ($340 million).
