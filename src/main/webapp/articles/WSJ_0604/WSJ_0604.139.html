Politics & Policy: Massachusetts Democrats Set for Governor's Primary

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/04/90

SPRINGFIELD, Mass. -- Boston University President John Silber, whose maverick gubernatorial candidacy divided the state Democratic party, narrowly won a spot on the primary ballot, while former state Attorney General Francis X. Bellotti won the party's endorsement. The long-dominant state Democratic party, facing its strongest challenge in years, also gave Michael Dukakis's lieutenant governor, Evelyn Murphy, a place on the ballot. State Rep. John Flood was eliminated.

Mr. Silber, on leave from his university post, fought against a rule that required support of at least 15% of party delegates to get on the ballot. He said it excluded candidates who had popular support but were opposed by liberal party insiders. Mr. Silber has led in recent polls of likely primary voters.

The convention here was marred by picketing Springfield police officers, who delayed its start by more than three hours as delegates refused to cross the line. But the picketers, some of whom were chanting "four more years," clearly sided with the Republican party, leading to charges that the protest -- over a contract dispute -- was deliberately staged to disrupt the convention.

A White House political aide, Ronald Kaufman, and state Republican Party Director Alexander Tennant were in Springfield during the weekend, but denied any involvement in the protest.
