Who's News: Domtar Inc. Names Campeau Chairman And Desjardins Chief

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/04/90

MONTREAL -- Domtar Inc., as expected, named Jean Campeau chairman and Pierre Desjardins president and chief executive officer, succeeding James H. Smith, who held all three posts and is retiring from the paper and building products concern. The appointment of Mr. Desjardins, 48-year-old president of Labatt Breweries of Canada, Toronto, a unit of John Labatt Ltd., is effective Sept. 1, when Mr. Smith, 58, will remain chairman until Mr. Campeau's arrival Nov. 1.

Mr. Campeau, also 58, is stepping down as chairman and general manager of Caisse de Depot et Placement, the Quebec government's pension-fund agency. Mr. Campeau (who isn't related to the Toronto businessman Robert Campeau) will leave Caisse de Depot on June 30, after 10 eventful years at the agency.

Mr. Campeau is already intimately familiar with Domtar, because Caisse de Depot and another Quebec government agency hold a controlling 44% stake in Domtar.

Mr. Campeau, who has been a hands-on manager at Caisse de Depot, is expected to take an active role at Domtar. There is considerable speculation here that Mr. Campeau will take advantage of a consolidation of the Canadian forest products industry in the next few years to add to Domtar's businesses.

Mr. Campeau said in a prepared statement that he is "honored to be chosen to preside over the development of this Quebec multinational {company}." He declined to elaborate.

At Labatt, a spokeswoman said a successor to Mr. Desjardins won't be named for at least three weeks.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading Friday, Domtar shares closed at $10.875, unchanged.
