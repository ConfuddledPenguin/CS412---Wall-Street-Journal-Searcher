Thrift Regulators Seize First Annapolis, Investors of Florida

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/04/90

WASHINGTON -- Savings-and-loan regulators took over First Annapolis Savings Bank, Annapolis, Md., and placed it in the government's program for failed institutions. First Annapolis had reported assets of $732.1 million and negative tangible capital of $65.9 million.

Regulators also took over Investors of Florida Savings Bank, Deerfield Park, Fla., and placed it in receivership. The thrift had assets of $282.6 million and negative tangible capital of $1.1 million.

Both institutions will be run under the direction of the Resolution Trust Corp., the government agency set up last year to sell off and liquidate hundreds of failed thrifts.

Separately, the RTC said it sold the deposits of Spindletop Savings Association, Beaumont, Texas, to First City, Texas Beaumont, also of Beaumont. Spindletop Savings had total assets of $243.9 million and total liabilities of $352.3 million.

First City will pay the RTC $10,000 to assume $288.1 million in 9,800 deposit accounts. First City will buy $22.4 million of Spindletop's assets and will have options to acquire most of the remaining assets.

The RTC will give First City $271.9 million to back the deposits and will retain $221.5 million of the assets. The RTC expects its ultimate cost from the transaction to be $250.1 million.
