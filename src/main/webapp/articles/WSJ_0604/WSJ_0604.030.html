First Executive's Chairman To Testify for House Panel

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/04/90

WASHINGTON -- First Executive Corp.'s chairman and chief executive, Fred Carr, has agreed to testify before the House oversight and investigations subcommittee about insurance and junk bonds. The subcommittee also is expected to subpoena attorneys and accountants whom investigators have connected with Drexel Burnham Lambert Inc. partnerships, according to individuals close to the panel. Those partnerships, made up mostly of Drexel employees, invested in a number of Drexel deals and have been the subject of subcommittee investigations.

"We want to establish how big these partnerships were, how much money was going through them," said one subcommittee official. The panel also is eager to learn what investments were made through the partnerships as well as whether there were misdeeds involving Drexel offerings and other investments.

The subcommittee already plans to offer immunity to former Drexel junk-bond chief Michael Milken in exchange for his testimony on the high-yield securities.

Mr. Carr's testimony, set for June 18, will focus on the insurance industry, in which First Executive is a sizable player, as well as practices in the junk-bond industry. First Executive was a major investor in junk bonds.

A spokesman for Mr. Carr said the executive "wants to talk about how he has managed to maintain the strong capital position in the industry nothwithstanding his junk-bond holdings." On Mr. Carr's relationship with Mr. Milken, the spokesman said, "He did business with Milken, but he did business with a lot of people."
