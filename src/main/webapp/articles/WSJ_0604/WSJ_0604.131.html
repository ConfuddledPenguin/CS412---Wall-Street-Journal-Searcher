Who's News: Laventhol & Horwath Realigns Management

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/04/90

PHILADELPHIA -- Laventhol & Horwath said a new senior management team was appointed to serve under Chief Executive Officer Robert N. Levine. Mr. Levine, who formally took office on Friday after being elected last November by the accounting firm's partners, said in a statement that he assembled the team of six managers "to strengthen the firm's commitment to professional quality and build client confidence."

Laventhol, the seventh-largest accounting firm in the U.S., has been hurt in the last few years by lawsuits accusing it of sloppy work and by defections of key employees. It has been forced to pay out more than $50 million in judgments and claims in the last three years.

Serving under Mr. Levine will be Jacob R. Brandzel as national partner for professional services, including accounting, auditing and tax work. He had served as the firm's national tax partner since 1987.

Reporting to Mr. Brandzel are James B. Leach, national accounting and auditing director, and Frank J. O'Connell, national tax partner.

Reporting to Mr. Levine are David E. Arnold, national partner for consulting services; Mark I. Murovitz, national partner for technology and innovation; and Edward A. Suarez, national partner for administration.

Laventhol also said several regional managing partners took office Friday:

Arthur R. Nemiroff heads the Los Angeles office, succeeding Philip T. Winik, who retires.

Kenneth L. Weiss heads the San Diego office, succeeding Howard Sibelman, who was promoted to accounting and auditing director for Southwest and Southern California offices.

Jerome L. Lefkowitz heads the Boston office, succeeding David Love, who retires. Mr. Lefkowitz also manages the Providence, R.I., office.

Philip Piser heads the Tampa, Fla., office, succeeding Don Garrison, who left the firm for personal reasons.

Also, Sheldon J. Epstein formally assumed responsibilities as Midwest area managing partner in Chicago. He had replaced Kenneth I. Solomon, who resigned, at the beginning of the year.
