Letters to the Editor: Let America Beware Economic Imperialism

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/04/90

As a former journalist who covered the semiconductor area of the electronics industry, I especially appreciated your May 4 page-one feature "Partnership in Peril" about John Hall's experiences with Japanese businessmen. In the late 1960s a strong consensus concluded that the Japanese semiconductor capabilities were so comparatively crude that their best hopes for catch-up (technological parity and commercial success) rested on acquisition or theft. After Mr. Hall, the Japanese suddenly possessed strong capabilities.

We underestimated Japan's determination in chips, cars, consumer appliances and more. Many of us knew the danger of Soviet political infiltration, but did not see Japan's economic infiltration.

We have no evidence that the Japanese have any sense of business integrity that extends to the rest of the world, that their "word" in deals or agreements is credible, or that they practice free enterprise as a two-way street.

I fear that the opening of Japan's markets will be even narrower than the opening of Russia's and that we will have to wake up to the serious risks from a Japan that cannot be trusted, that is supremist and economically imperialistic.

Mark B. Leeds

New York
