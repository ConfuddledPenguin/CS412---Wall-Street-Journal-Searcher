Bush Is Mulling Ban On Some Oil Drilling

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/09/90

WASHINGTON -- President Bush said he plans to prevent oil drilling in "certain highly sensitive areas," but aides say a formal decision still is weeks off. Mr. Bush, who made his comment in response to questions at a convention of the American Society of Newspaper Editors, has been debating for months whether to allow drilling in three environmentally sensitive tracts off the coasts of Florida and California. A task force Mr. Bush formed to study the question sent him a series of options early this year, but Mr. Bush hasn't announced a decision.

In response to a question about whether he would consider banning all offshore drilling, Mr. Bush said he wouldn't approve of any general ban, but then added: "We will be announcing a policy that prohibits drilling in certain highly sensitive areas." He didn't refer specifically to the controversial Florida and California tracts under consideration, but seemed to be referring to them.

Administration aides said a final decision about the tracts appears to be several weeks off. Mr. Bush has asked for additional information about the offshore areas and possible options before making his decision, but he hasn't received that information yet, officials said.

Mr. Bush's task force recommended a series of options, which aides said would have the effect of delaying a federal sale of drilling rights on the tracts for periods ranging from two to 10 years. But officials said the administration also is considering the possibility of subdividing the sensitive tracts so that drilling could be blocked in the most environmentally fragile areas while it proceeds in other areas.
