Business Brief -- Kinark Corp.: Quarterly Loss to Result From Camelot Hotels Sale

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/09/90

Kinark Corp. said it agreed to sell its Camelot Hotels for $12 million to a private company in California, resulting in a $6.7 million loss from discontinued operations for the fourth quarter. The operator of chemicals facilities and galvanizing plants said that the sale resulted in a net loss for 1989 of $4.7 million, or $1.33 a share. The Tulsa, Okla.-based company had profit from continuing operations of $2 million, or 56 cents a share for the year.

In 1988, Kinark had net income of $1.4 million, or 41 cents a share.

The company, which has been trying to sell the hotels for some time, said it expects to complete the Camelot sale within 90 days and use the proceeds to repay bank debt.
