Bechtel Group Studies Soviet Research Site For `High-Tech City'

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/09/90

SAN FRANCISCO -- Bechtel Group Inc. said it signed an agreement to conduct a preliminary study aimed at turning a Soviet Union research community into an area similar to California's Silicon Valley. Bechtel said the study is part of a plan to convert Troitsk, located about 20 miles from Moscow, into a "entrepreneurial, high-technology city." Troitsk is now the site of the Kurchatov Institute of Atomic Energy, where much of the country's scientific research is conducted.

Bechtel said there are plans to upgrade the area's airport, and install advanced telecommunications facilities at Troitsk. Also, Bechtel said it's hoped that a conference center, an international hotel, "U.S.-style housing" and recreational facilities will be developed. Terms of the agreement weren't disclosed.

The closely held engineering and construction concern said it's too early to estimate the cost of the project, but said the Soviets have indicated they plan to spend $100 million a year to build a series of such high-tech communities. With the plan, Bechtel said, the Soviets "hope to convert their purely research facilities at Troitsk into a series of entrepreneurial ventures capable of jump-starting their nation's faltering economy."
