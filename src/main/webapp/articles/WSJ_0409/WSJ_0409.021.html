Jacor Reaches Pacts To Sell Radio Stations To LBI and Capstar

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/09/90

CINCINNATI -- Jacor Communications Inc. said it agreed to sell radio stations WMJI-FM in Cleveland and WYHY-FM in Nashville, Tenn., to LBI Holdings Inc., Los Angeles, for $31.3 million. Jacor also agreed to sell stations WQIK-AM/FM in Jacksonville, Fla., to Capstar Communications Inc., Austin, Texas, for $13.5 million.

Jacor said it will have a gain of about $13.3 million on the LBI Holdings transaction and an $8 million gain on the sale to Capstar. Jacor said it will use the proceeds from the sales to further its plans to buy "more valuable stations in much larger radio markets."

LBI Holdings was recently formed by Carl E. Hirsch and Robert F.X. Sillerman, formerly partners in Legacy Broadcasting, Jacor said. Mr. Hirsch also is a former president of Malrite Communication Group, a Cleveland-based broadcasting concern.

Capstar already owns radio stations in Nashville; Greenville, S.C., and Jackson, Miss. Jacor owns 13 radio stations, a cable television system and other communications businesses.

In national over-the-counter trading Friday, Jacor shares jumped 37.5 cents to close at 5.375.
