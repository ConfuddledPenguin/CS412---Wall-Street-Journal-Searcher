Bush Renominates Gramm To CFTC Chairman Post

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/09/90

WASHINGTON -- President Bush nominated Wendy Gramm for a second term as chairman of the Commodity Futures Trading Commission. During Mrs. Gramm's two years on the panel, she and the CFTC have faced a steady barrage of criticism for not policing the Chicago commodity exchanges more closely. But defenders say Mrs. Gramm has allowed the exchanges to expand their product lines and develop into the most innovative markets in the world.

Separately, White House Chief of Staff John Sununu met with officials of the Chicago commodity exchanges and Illinois Gov. James Thompson to discuss the battle over regulation of the futures markets. The meeting was billed as a chance for futures-markets leaders to take their case to the White House.

The Treasury Department and the Securities and Exchange Commission are lobbying to move regulation of some financial futures to the SEC from the CFTC, a move the Chicago exchanges have said would produce tighter supervision that would hurt their markets.
