KeyCorp to Report Earnings Rose 17% During First Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/09/90

NEW YORK -- KeyCorp expects to report first-quarter earnings of 83 cents to 88 cents a share, generally in line with securities analysts' estimates. Earnings rose about 17% from last year's first-quarter results of 72 cents a share, or $31 million.

William H. Dougherty, chief financial officer of the Albany, N.Y.-based banking company, said in an interview, "We will be comfortably within the range of analysts' estimates." Mr. Dougherty said the improved results will come from operations and not from nonrecurring items, except for a small gain on the sale of securities.

For the full year, he said that KeyCorp earnings will be "at the lower end" of the $3.60 to $3.70-a-share range of analysts' estimates, as a result of the less robust economic environment and harsh regulatory climate. Last year, KeyCorp had earnings of $136.9 million, or $3.20 a share.

"We decided that it would not be wise to be pushing hard to increase loan demand in this environment," Mr. Dougherty said.

Nonperforming loans, he added, are expected to rise slightly to 1.55% of total loans from 1.45% at the end of 1989.

Mr. Dougherty said KeyCorp's Maine bank recently was examined by bank regulators and that "no adjustments whatsoever" were made to the bank's portfolio of problem loans.

At its Alaska bank, nonperforming assets will fall to 8% from its peak of 12%. "Alaska has bottomed out, and we will be reporting a profit in Alaska this year," Mr. Dougherty said.

In addition, Mr. Dougherty said KeyCorp has a small exposure in real estate, a problem area for many banks. Out of a loan portfolio totaling $9.9 billion at year end, condominium and construction loans total only $56 million and $438 million, respectively, he said.
