Business Brief -- Ford Motor Co.: International Plans Could Result From Fiat Discussion

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/09/90

Ford Motor Co. and the Fiat Group of Italy confirmed they are holding talks that could lead to world-wide arrangements involving their tractor and farm equipment operations, while preserving their product and market identities. The two companies also said they are exploring, in separate discussions, possible areas of cooperation in the heavy-truck business. Both companies said they have agreed that no further announcement will be made until there are definitive agreements.

Friday's announcement followed reports that Dearborn, Mich.-based Ford and Fiat are discussing the possible sale of Ford's New Holland farm-equipment subsidiary to Fiat. Such a sale would be consistent with Ford's recent strategy of shedding non-automotive operations; for example, the auto maker has put its aerospace unit up for sale.

Ford and Fiat are partners in the heavy-truck business in Europe.
