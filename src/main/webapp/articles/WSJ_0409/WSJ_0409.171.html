Pact Set for Computer & Communications, Competitor to Merge

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/09/90

SAN DIEGO -- Financially troubled Computer & Communications Technology Corp. said it agreed in principle to merge with closely held Sunward Technologies, resulting in new, 55% majority control of the company by Sunward shareholders. Computer & Communications Technology also disclosed that it has technically defaulted on $8.7 million of debt and that it expects to report a $12.4 million fourth-quarter loss, largely because of one-time charges.

Revenue for the latest quarter was about $17.6 million. The loss for 1989 totals about $17.3 milllion on revenue of $61.5 million, the company said.

The proposed merger is subject to shareholder and bank approval. Both companies make magnetic recording heads for computer disk drives and jointly represent about 40% of the world-wide non-captive market for such products, said Gary Hall, chief financial officer of Computer & Communications Technology. Both companies are based in San Diego.

The merged company will adopt the Sunward Technologies name. However, Computer & Communications, technically the continuing entity, will issue 14.5 million new shares to Sunward holders. Computer & Communications Technology currently has about 11.3 million shares outstanding.

Everett Bahre, chairman, president, and chief executive officer of Computer Communications & Technology, said the merger will "substantially reduce manufacturing and operating expenses" as well as add about $6.5 million of cash at Sunward to the combined balance sheet. Sunward had revenue last year of $42.1 million.

Sunward has manufacturing operations in the Philippines, and about 1,500 employees. Computer Communications & Technology, with manufacturing operations in South Korea and Puerto Rico, has about 2,000 employees.
