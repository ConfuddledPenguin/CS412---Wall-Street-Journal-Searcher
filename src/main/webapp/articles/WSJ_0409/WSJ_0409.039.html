Business Brief -- Valmont Industries Inc.: Official Indicates Earnings For Quarter Held Steady

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/09/90

Analyst projections that Valmont Industries Inc. will report per-share net income in the range of 33 cents to 36 cents for its first quarter, ended April 1, represent a "fair estimate," William F. Welsh II, president and chief executive officer, said. Those results would equal or fall slightly below the Omaha, Neb., company's year-ago net of $4.2 million, or 36 cents a share, on revenue of $189.4 million.

Valmont makes irrigation systems, industrial products including steel poles and tubing, and additionally holds an 82% stake in ValCom Inc., a reseller of personal computers.

The company's irrigation business will have an "excellent year" in 1990, but "not quite as strong" as it has been in the past couple of years, when drought conditions spurred sales in the Farm Belt, Mr. Welsh said in an interview. The improved health of the general farm economy this year, he said, will help offset the absence of a "drought situation." Domestic demand remains "strong," he said, adding that the company's export market -- which covers some 70 nations -- is also "quite good."
