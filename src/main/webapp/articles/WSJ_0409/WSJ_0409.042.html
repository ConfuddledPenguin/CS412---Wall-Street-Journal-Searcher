Eagle Financial, Webster End Their Pact to Merge

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/09/90

NEW YORK -- Two Connecticut thrift holding companies, Eagle Financial Corp. and Webster Financial Corp., ended their agreement to merge, citing "the deterioration of the region's economy and the uncertainties of the regulatory environment." In the merger, each share of Eagle Financial, which is based in Bristol, Conn., was to be converted into 0.95 share of the new holding company. Each share of Webster, based in Waterbury, Conn., was to become one share of the new firm.

The companies announced the pact in October and definitively agreed on it two months later. The banks emphasized that each has capital exceeding regulatory requirements, but they said the merger wouldn't be in the best interests of the companies or their shareholders right now.

With assets of $708.5 million at Dec. 31, Webster is the parent of First Federal Savings & Loan of Waterbury. Eagle, with assets of $472.3 million on that date, holds Bristol Federal Savings Bank and First Federal Savings & Loan Association of Torrington.
