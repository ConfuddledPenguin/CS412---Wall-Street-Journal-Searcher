Credit Ratings

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/09/90

BANK OF NEW ENGLAND (Boston) -- Moody's Investors Service Inc. lowered ratings on about $750 million of long-term debt, subordinated debt and preferred stock. The rating agency said Bank of New England's increase in problem loans during the first quarter has "raised greater uncertainty regarding the viability of the institution." Moody's lowered Bank of New England's senior debt to Caa from B3, its senior subordinated debt to Ca from Caa, its shelf registration for senior debt to (P)Caa from (P)B3 and its preferred stock to `ca' from `caa.' Moody's confirmed the "not prime" rating for commercial paper. Moody's also confirmed the ratings of Bank of New England's subsidiary banks for deposit obligations, based on its view that depositors would receive "substantially better" regulatory protection than holding company creditors. Bank of New England N.A. and Connecticut Bank & Trust remain rated at Ba3 for long-term deposits and "not prime" for short-term deposits. The banks' obligations backed by letters of credit remain at Caa.
