Seitel and Digicon Form Joint Venture To Buy Data Library

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/09/90

HOUSTON -- Seitel Inc. said it formed a joint venture with Digicon Geophysical Corp. to acquire Digicon's existing proprietary seismic data library. Seitel said it intended to offer one million common shares to help pay for the venture. The venture, to be known as Digitel Data, will be paid for by a $4 million nonrecourse loan from Seitel, collateralized by the venture's data bank. With the loan proceeds, the venture will buy Digicon's rights to a seismic data bank of 9,500 miles of U.S. onshore seismic data and 155,000 miles of offshore data. Seitel's loan will be repaid through revenue from the licensing of this data.

Seitel said it intended to file with the Securities and Exchange Commission a proposed public offering of one million shares of common stock. Half the issue, 500,000 shares, will be newly issued; 350,000 will be offered by its principal stockholder, Helm Resources Inc.; 150,000 shares will be offered by three individual stockholders. Seitel's proceeds from the sale will go to pay for the proposed joint venture with Digicon and for other purposes.

Seitel develops proprietary seismic data and arranges the acquisition and marketing of that data. Based in Houston, Digicon is currently operating under Chapter 11 of the Bankruptcy Code; approval of the bankruptcy court is necessary to complete the transaction, as well as consent from Digicon's principal lender.
