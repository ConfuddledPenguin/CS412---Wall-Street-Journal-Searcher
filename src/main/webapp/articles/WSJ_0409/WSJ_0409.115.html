Marketing Brief -- Gallimard: Family Member Is Selling Stake in Publishing House

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/09/90

One of the feuding siblings battling for control of the Gallimard publishing house gave up the fight Friday and announced she will sell her shares to a bank. Isabelle Gallimard, 39, will give up nearly her entire 12.5% stake in the family business to Banque Nationale de Paris (BNP) for an undisclosed price, according to a statement.

The move leaves her sister Francoise, 49, and brother Christian, 46, alone trying to wrest control from another brother, Antoine, 42, the firm's president and largest shareholder.

The sale also effectively blunts efforts by the giant Bouygues construction company from bidding for Gallimard. BNP said it would hold the shares for several years. The sale to BNP increases outside shareholdings in the family-controlled business to about 30%.

Ms. Gallimard said she based her decision on the inability of the siblings to agree on how the firm should be operated.
