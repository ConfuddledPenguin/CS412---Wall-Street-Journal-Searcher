Canada's Jobless Rate Declines

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/09/90

OTTAWA -- Canada's unemployment rate declined to a seasonally adjusted 7.2% of the work force in March, its lowest level since August 1981, Statistics Canada said. The February rate was 7.7%.

The unemployment decline was attributed to a drop of 91,000 in the labor force, the sharpest decrease for any month since July 1973. An analyst for the federal agency said Statistics Canada wasn't certain why the labor force declined so sharply in March.

Full-time employment fell 47,000 in March while part-time employment rose 16,000. Manufacturing employment declined 34,000, continuing a downward trend that started in the 1989 second quarter.
