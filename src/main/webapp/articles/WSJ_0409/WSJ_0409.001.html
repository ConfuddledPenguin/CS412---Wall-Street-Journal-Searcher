Letters to the Editor: Smog Tax: Dirty Deal For Most Consumers

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/09/90

The oasis of rationality that you find in various proposals to control air pollution in San Francisco is one that would reduce smog by taxing it directly ("Paying the Tailpiper," Review & Outlook, Feb. 28). Presumably your favored solution to a famine would be to charge a special levy on food. Those who could not afford the resulting higher prices would expire, thus neatly solving the problem. You may not have come to bury San Francisco in ridicule, but if the solution you advocate is adopted, the rest of the country will do it for you. I am not at all surprised that the scheme is being promoted by a cabal of local business interests, for it is replete with all the features that would appeal to such a group.

First, it socks it to consumers rather than business, as it seeks to reduce pollution by curbing the number of miles driven, rather than the development of cars that are less polluting.

Second, it is designed to be a bonanza for all those companies that will be selling tuneups, cleaner fuel, anti-smog devices and replacements for junked old cars, as consumers struggle to keep the smog tax within the bounds of what they can afford. If they lose the battle, they will have to walk or use public transportation.

Third, those who use employer-provided cars or have the resources to replace their vehicle every few years (in which number we should probably count the CEOs of the illustrious companies you name) will either pass the smog tax on to the consumer through their product prices, or pay it from their pocket change. Meanwhile, they will drive by the walkers and those waiting at the bus stop at 48 mph on properly priced highways.

No doubt the proposal would solve the highway and fresh-air famine, but somehow I prefer a scheme that would give everyone fair usage of the nation's infrastructural resources. Even-numbered license plates on the roads on even-numbered days sounds good to me.

Subhash Mukerji

White Plains, N.Y.
