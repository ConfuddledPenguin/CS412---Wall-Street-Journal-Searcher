Repap Enterprises Inc. Loses Some Confidence In 1990 Profit Forecast

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/09/90

MONTREAL -- Repap Enterprises Inc. "isn't as confident" as it was six weeks ago that it will at least match 1989 net income this year, said George Petty, chairman and chief executive, after a presentation to analysts here. Last year, Repap had net income of 93.1 million Canadian dollars (US$79.7 million), or C$1.79 (US$1.53) a share, on revenue of C$1.22 billion. In February, Mr. Petty said in an interview that the pulp and coated paper producer expected to at least match 1989 net income this year.

Mr. Petty said the first half looks "tougher-than-expected," but Repap is "still very optimistic" about the second half. He said the Canadian dollar and interest rates are higher than anticipated this year, both of which hurt earnings.

In over-the-counter trading Friday, Repap closed at $7.50, up 12.5 cents.
