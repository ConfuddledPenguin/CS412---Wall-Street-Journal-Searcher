Financing Business: Curragh Resources Inc.

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/09/90

CURRAGH RESOURCES Inc., a closely held mining concern, said it filed a prospectus for an international initial public offering of shares to raise gross proceeds of about $80 million (U.S.). The Toronto-based company said it will offer 6,250,000 subordinate voting shares from its treasury at about $13 each. In addition, it said Australia's Giant Resources Ltd., which owns 44% of Curragh, will sell 2,750,000 shares, reducing its stake to about 21%. Adrian White, Curragh's executive vice president, finance, said the company will assist Giant in selling privately the rest of its Curragh shares as part of Giant's previously reported plan to dispose of its Canadian mining assets. Giant is 43% owned by Pioneer International Ltd., one of Australia's biggest companies, which has announced plans to sell its stake in Giant. The public offering represents about 27% of Curragh's shares. Mr. White said Frame Mining Corp., a company controlled by Clifford Frame, Curragh's chairman, president and chief executive officer, will hold about 90% of the voting power through its holding of Curragh multiple voting shares. Mr. White said proceeds of the offering will be used to repay long-term debt, which stood at 213 million Canadian dollars (US$182.3 million) at Dec. 31. He said Curragh also will probably issue $100 million (U.S.) in debentures later this year.
