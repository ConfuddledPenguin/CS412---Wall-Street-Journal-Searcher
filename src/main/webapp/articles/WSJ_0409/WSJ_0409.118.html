Eliot Bank Posts Loss For Quarter, Warns Of Possible Receiver

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/09/90

BOSTON -- Eliot Bank reported a fourth-quarter loss of $30.5 million, or $16.25 a share, and said regulators may appoint a receiver if it fails to restore net capital to the required level. Results included a $23.8 million provision for loan losses, as troubled real estate loans contributed to a surge in nonperforming assets. In the year-earlier period, Eliot earned $165,000, or nine cents a share.

The bank warned that its net capital is "significantly below the minimum required" by regulators and that failure to raise the capital could cause the Massachusetts Commissioner of Banks and the Federal Deposit Insurance Corp. to "take such actions as they consider appropriate, including the possible appointment of a receiver for the bank."

Eliot is the second Massachusetts bank in recent days to warn of the possibility that regulators might appoint a receiver. Home National Corp. of Milford, Mass., last week used nearly identical wording in disclosing a capital shortfall.

At Dec. 31, 1989, Eliot's total assets were $489.3 million and stockholder equity was negative $13.8 million. Nonperforming assets were $133.9 million at year end, comprised of $104.3 million in nonaccruing loans and $29.6 million in foreclosed and "in substance foreclosure" property.

Eliot said it has reduced its reliance on borrowed funds and other volatile liabilities.

For 1989, Eliot reported a loss of $50.5 million, or $26.89 a share, compared with year-earlier profit of $2.8 million, or $1.47 a share.
