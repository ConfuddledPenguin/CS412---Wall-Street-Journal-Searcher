Fisher-Price Says Defects Found in Child Car Seats

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/09/90

EAST AURORA, N.Y. -- Quaker Oats Co.'s Fisher-Price division said as many as 20,000 Fisher-Price child car seats made this year may be defective. Metal tongues, which go between the legs and connect with shoulder safety straps, could break in a crash, Fisher-Price said. It blamed the problem on a defective die at the tongue's vendor, which Fisher-Price wouldn't identify.

Fisher-Price said it had received no reports of injuries or deaths because of the defect. A spokeswoman said the division has substantial reserves to cover potential liability. The financial impact on the third quarter, just ended, should be "minimal."

Opting for a part replacement plan rather than a recall, Fisher-Price is making available free modification kits that include a new tongue and tools to install it.

The suspect car seats are model 9101 sold during February and March. Seats with an inspection sticker dated April 1990 or later meet government safety standards, the company said. Those wishing modification kits should call 800-327-9980 during business hours, Fisher-Price said.
