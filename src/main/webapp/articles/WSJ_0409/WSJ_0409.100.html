Coastal Corp. to Build Oil Facility in Aruba

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/09/90

HOUSTON -- Coastal Corp. said it will build an oil storage and trans-shipment facility in San Nicolas, Aruba, where it is restarting a former Exxon Corp. refinery. Coastal, a diversified energy company, said the facility would include construction of storage tanks that could hold as much as eight million barrels of crude. That would be in addition to 3.4 million barrels of storage capacity already built or under construction at the refinery.

The company also said it plans to build a dock capable of handling ocean tankers that can carry as much as 3.5 million barrels of crude.

"The facility gives flexibility in buying crude in large volumes and shipping it out as needed," a Coastal spokesman said. The Aruba refinery, scheduled to begin processing 150,000 barrels a day of crude by this summer, will initially make diesel fuel and heating oils. It will also make low-sulfur feedstocks that can be shipped to U.S. refineries for the production of gasoline.

The Coastal spokesman said the company didn't yet know what construction costs would be until bids were received. One industry official estimated the price would exceed $40 million.
