Credit Ratings: USAir Debt Ratings Lowered by Moody's; Expansion Is Factor

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/09/90

NEW YORK -- Moody's Investors Service Inc., citing USAir Group Inc.'s planned expansion effort and "reduced cash flow," lowered its ratings on the carrier's long-term debt of approximately $875 million. Moody's said it expects the company's "debt-protection measurements will remain depressed over the intermediate term in the wake of the difficulties in integrating Piedmont into USAir." USAir acquired Piedmont Aviation Inc. in 1987.

A "cyclically weaker operating outlook for the industry," Moody's said, will limit USAir's ability to return to its "historical earnings levels." The airline's ability to reduce its currently high leverage may be limited by USAir's plans to spend more than $1 billion annually during the next two years to modernize and expand operations, Moody's said.

A USAir spokeswoman said the report "speaks for itself as a fair reflection of the company's current situation."

The rating concern lowered the parent company's commercial paper to Prime-3 from Prime 2. Ratings on equipment-trust certificates and pass-through certificates issued by the airline's USAir Inc. unit were downgraded to Baa1 from A3; USAir Inc.'s senior debentures and industrial-revenue bonds were lowered to Baa2 from Baa1.

Also lowered were the ratings on equipment-trust certificates issued by the company's Piedmont Aviation unit, to Baa1 from A3; Piedmont industrial-revenue bonds were reduced to Baa2 from Baa1.
