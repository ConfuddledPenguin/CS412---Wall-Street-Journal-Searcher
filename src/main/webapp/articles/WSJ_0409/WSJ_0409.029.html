U.S. Contends Exports Of Mexican Cement Are Unfairly Priced

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/09/90

WASHINGTON -- The Commerce Department ruled that Mexican companies are exporting hydraulic cement to the U.S. at unfairly low prices. The preliminary anti-dumping decision will require importers of Mexican cement and clinker, an intermediate material, to post bonds or make cash payments to cover penalty duties that may be assessed later.

The department's final ruling is due by June 19, after which the U.S. International Trade Commission would decide within 45 days whether the imports were injuring a domestic industry. If both findings are affirmative, penalty duties would be assessed.

The department said the imports of cement and clinker totaled about $111 million last year. The dumping margins, and thus the potential duties, range as high as 56.68% for cement produced by Cemex S.A.. The margin is 51.73% for Apasco S.A. de C.V. and 51.16% for most other companies. However, one Mexican producer, Cementos Hidalgo S.C.L., hasn't exported cement or clinker to the U.S. at unfairly low prices, the agency said.

Under the Anti-Dumping Act, the Commerce Department can find a violation if imports are reaching the U.S. market at prices below the producer's home-market price for the same products.
