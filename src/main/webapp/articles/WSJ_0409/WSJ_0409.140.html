Stanford University Head Puts Priority on Teaching

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/09/90

PALO ALTO, Calif. -- Stanford University President Donald Kennedy called for the university to make teaching, rather than research, its top priority. In an annual address last week to the university's Academic Council, Mr. Kennedy said both teaching and research are important to the university, but "the relative weight has shifted over time" toward research. He said Stanford educators "need to talk about teaching more, respect and reward those who do it well, and make it first among our labors."

Stanford needs to pay more attention to students, particularly undergraduates, he said. There is a "suspicion that we have lost focus in designing and delivering a well planned, challenging and inspiring education to our undergraduates," Mr. Kennedy said. He criticized faculty members who reduce teaching duties to concentrate on research, leaving "too many of Stanford's classes" taught by visiting or temporary staffers.

Mr. Kennedy's remarks come at a time when educators around the nation are calling for colleges and universities to commit more resources to students, and move away from the "publish or perish" mentality that often results in teachers, eager to secure tenure, emphasizing their research activities.
