Canadian Pacific Says Forest Products Slump To Pull '90 Net Down

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/09/90

MONTREAL -- Hurt by substantially lower earnings at its forest products subsidiary, Canadian Pacific Ltd.'s net income this year "is going to be down from 1989" net from continuing operations of 664.6 million Canadian dollars (US$568.7 million) or C$2.09 (US$1.79) a share, said William Stinson, chairman, president and chief executive officer. After a presentation to analysts, Mr. Stinson said that while earnings at 79.7%-owned Canadian Pacific Forest Products Ltd. will drop substantially, it's "too early to tell on balance" how Canadian Pacific's other businesses will perform.

Including income from discontinued operations, Canadian Pacific had net income of C$745.2 million, or C$2.35 a share, last year. Revenue was C$11.02 billion.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading Friday, Canadian Pacific closed unchanged at $20.875.

Mr. Stinson told analysts that first-quarter revenue was up about 5% at wholly owned CP Rail, but earnings will be "about flat with '89." He said grain traffic, which accounts for about 25% of CP Rail's business, still isn't "up to the levels anticipated."

Mr. Stinson said 87.1%-owned PanCanadian Petroleum Ltd. will report higher first-quarter earnings due to higher oil prices. He said Canadian Pacific's Fording Coal Ltd. unit, is "going to do better {in 1990} than the C$40 million after tax" it earned in 1989.

Canadian Pacific is a Montreal-based transportation, natural-resources, telecommunications and real estate concern.
