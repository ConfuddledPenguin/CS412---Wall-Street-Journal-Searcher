Carolco Net Fell 57% In the Fourth Period

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/09/90

LOS ANGELES -- Carolco Pictures Inc. reported a 57% drop in fourth-quarter earnings, as revenue from feature films declined and operating costs soared. The Los Angeles entertainment concern posted net income of $8.2 million, or 27 cents a share, down from $18.7 million, or 63 cents a share, a year earlier, when box office receipts from "Rambo III" boosted the bottom line. Revenue fell 1% to $54.5 million from $54.9 million.

Feature-film revenue fell 10% to $41.5 million from $46.2 million. That decline was partly offset by higher revenue from television syndication and other operations. Operating costs nearly trebled in the latest quarter, to $7.1 million from $2.5 million a year earlier.

Also pushing earnings lower was a 69% plunge, to $2.2 million from $6.9 million, in contributions from Carolco's 48% stake in Live Entertainment Inc., a distributor of video and music products. The drop at Live reflected higher amortization of accounting goodwill and an increased taxrate.

For the year, Carolco net fell 61% to $14 million, or 46 cents a share, from $35.5 million, or $1.15 a share, a year earlier. Revenue dropped 13% to $143.1 million from $164.6 million.
