International: U.S., Czechoslovakia Reach Bilateral Trade Agreement

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 04/09/90

WASHINGTON -- U.S. Trade Representative Carla Hills said the U.S. and Czechoslovakia reached a bilateral trade agreement that will be signed Thursday. Mrs. Hills said the agreement clears the way for each government to lower its tariff rates for imports from the other country, by offering "most favored nation" tariff treatment under the rules of the General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade, or GATT.

Mrs. Hills said Czechoslovakia will extend "national treatment" to U.S. companies that establish bank accounts and pay for goods within that country and will permit U.S. companies to repatriate hard currency earnings from trade without any delays. Czechoslovakia also agreed to strengthen its system protecting patents, copyrights and other intellectual property rights.
