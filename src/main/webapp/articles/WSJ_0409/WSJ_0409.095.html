Technology: Seagate's Notice From IRS

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/09/90

SCOTTS VALLEY, Calif. -- Seagate Technology Inc. said it received a statutory notice from the Internal Revenue Service that it may owe up to $112 million in taxes. The maker of disk drives said it was notified of a "possible deficiency" for the taxable years 1981 to 1987. Seagate said it believes it has "meritorious defenses" to the IRS adjustments. The company said it is preparing a petition to the U.S. Tax Court.

Seagate said it believes the likely outcome of the litigation won't have a material effect on its financial position.

A Seagate spokeswoman said the company wouldn't explain why the government believes it owes the money. "There isn't anything further I can say. We have no comment beyond the press release. That's all the information that's available."

In the six months ended Dec. 31, Seagate had net income of $59.2 million, or $1.03 a share, on revenue of $1.07 billion.

The dispute is believed to involve how Seagate accounts for its profits from overseas operations. Seagate has manufacturing facilities in Asia, and can increase and possibly defer overseas profits depending on the prices it charges its domestic units for overseas production.
