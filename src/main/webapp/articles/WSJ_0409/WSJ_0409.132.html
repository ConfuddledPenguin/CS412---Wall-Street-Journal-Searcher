First Interstate's Annuities

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/09/90

LOS ANGELES -- First Interstate Bancorp said its unit, First Interstate Bank of California, has secured licenses for employees at 12 branches to begin selling annuities. First Interstate is entering the insurance market, after Proposition 103, a voter initiative, allowed California banks to directly sell insurance products. The bank holding concern said it has reached an agreement with Essex Corp., a New York independent third-party vendor of annuities, under which Essex will train bank employees to sell the products, and provide marketing support.

First Interstate said it expects to be selling annuities in 65 more branches by the end of the year. First Interstate's fixed-rate annuities are interest-earning insurance contracts, marketed at customers over 50 years old, and are intended to supplement retirement income.
