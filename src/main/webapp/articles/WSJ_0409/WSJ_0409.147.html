Business Brief -- A. Copeland Enterprises Inc.: Firm Had a Loss in 1989 And Is Seeking Refinancing

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/09/90

A. Copeland Enterprises Inc. said it had a net loss of $35.9 million in 1989 and said it is trying to refinance some of its debt. The owner of Popeyes Famous Fried Chicken and Biscuits, which last year acquired Church's Fried Chicken Inc. for about $392 million, said the loss resulted primarily from about $55.6 million in interest expense related to the Church's purchase.

Copeland said in New Orleans that last month it received modifications to certain covenants in its loan agreements, a reduction in the interest rate on its bridge loan and a deferral of $7.5 million in bank-debt amortization due in 1990. It said it requested the changes after a deteriorating junk-bond market prevented it from refinancing some of its current debt with high-yield subordinated debt, as planned.
