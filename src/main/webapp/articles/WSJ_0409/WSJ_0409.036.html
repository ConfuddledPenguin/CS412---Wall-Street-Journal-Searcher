Court Ruling on BTR Suit In Norton Co. Bid Is Slated

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/09/90

BOSTON -- A U.S. District Court judge here said he expects by Wednesday to rule on a suit by BTR PLC seeking to block Norton Co. from delaying its annual meeting. BTR, a London-based international conglomerate, last month made a $75-a-share, or $1.64 billion, hostile bid for Norton, a Worcester, Mass., maker of abrasives and engineering materials. Norton rejected the bid as inadequate and delayed its annual meeting to June 26 from April 26, saying it wanted more time to consider its options.

BTR then sued Norton to have the canceled meeting reinstated for April 26, charging that Norton's management plans to use the delay to "change Norton's shareholder base" in order to win an expected proxy fight. Norton said it would vigorously defend against the suit and filed a countersuit to enjoin BTR's tender offer and to stop BTR from soliciting proxies.

In a hearing Friday, U.S. District Court Judge Joseph Tauro said he hopes to issue a ruling on the annual meeting date by Wednesday.

BTR earlier said it would start a proxy fight to unseat Norton's board "quite soon" if it prevails in its suit to reinstate Norton's original annual meeting date. BTR indicated it wouldn't proceed with its planned proxy fight for some weeks if the new meeting date were allowed to stand.
