International: German Economic Growth Revised Sharply Upward

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 04/09/90

FRANKFURT, West Germany -- West Germany's five leading research institutes revised their economic-growth forecasts upward sharply for both this year and next, the newspaper Welt am Sonntag reported. The research institutes are now projecting 1990 growth could reach 4%, equal to last year's strong expansion and above their earlier estimates of between 3% and 3.5%, the newspaper said. They revised their growth forecast for 1991 to 3.5% from 2.5%.

The institutes said the unification of the two Germanys was giving the economy an extra push. Their spring report won't be officially released until today.
