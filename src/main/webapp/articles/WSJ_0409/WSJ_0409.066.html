Business Brief -- Circon Corp.: Firm Refinances, Buys Back $9 Million Convertible Debt

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/09/90

Circon Corp., Santa Barbara, Calif., said it refinanced much of its debt, buying back a $9 million convertible debenture at a discount and revising bank borrowings. The maker of miniature television cameras for medical uses said it expects to report a $700,000 after-tax gain on the debenture repurchase. In 1989, the company had net income of $302,000, or six cents a share, on $49.6 million in sales.

Circon said it bought the debenture for $7.5 million from the holder, a unit of British-based BOC Group PLC. The debenture was convertible into one million common shares, or about 16% of Circon's stock outstanding.

BOC Group said more than a year ago that it was interested in selling the debenture.

Circon said it arranged a new $6 million, five-year bank term loan in connection with the purchase, and a $10 million two-year line of credit, supplanting an earlier $8.25 million line of credit.

The refinancing overall has reduced current company debt by $966,000. However, annual interest costs will increase by about $40,000 this year due to higher interest rates on the new bank debt, said R. Bruce Thompson, executive vice president and chief financial officer.
