Poco Petroleums Ltd., And Voyager Energy Call Off Merger Talks

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/13/90

CALGARY, Alberta -- Poco Petroleums Ltd. and Voyager Energy Inc. said they have called off talks aimed at merging the two oil and natural-gas concerns after their financial advisers weren't able to agree on an exchange ratio. Voyager and Poco proposed in March to merge, forming a company that would have been one of Canada's top 12 oil and natural gas producers. Independent committees of both companies' boards were appointed to negotiate the terms and conditions of a merger.

Further details of the breakdown in discussions weren't disclosed, and spokesmen for the two companies couldn't be reached for comment.

Poco, 49%-owned by Toronto-based Corona Corp., a gold-mining concern, has about 41.1 million shares outstanding, while Voyager has about 26 million shares outstanding. In trading on the Toronto Stock Exchange Friday, Poco shares closed at 7.50 Canadian dollars (US$6.54), up 12.5 Canadian cents, while Voyager shares closed at C$7.125, up 50 Canadian cents.

Poco had cited the merger as a chance to expand its operations and achieve a critical mass in some areas of its exploration and development activities. The two Calgary-based companies are predominantly natural-gas producers, and both have most of their assets in the province of Alberta.
