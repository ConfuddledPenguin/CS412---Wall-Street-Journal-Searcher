The Mideast Conflict: Pump Prices for Gasoline Decline but Not by Much

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/13/90

Gasoline pump prices fell Friday for the first time since Iraq invaded Kuwait, but not by much. After prices ran up 15 cents to 20 cents a gallon in many parts of the nation, a handful of major oil refiners began to cut wholesale prices and it seems some of those reductions are filtering on to the corner service station. Some big oil companies froze their prices.

Companies said they made the unsually quick cuts in response to falling crude-oil markets and to President Bush's plea for pricing restraint.

The American Automobile Association said the average price nationwide for self-serve unleaded gasoline declined 0.3 cent to $1.25 a gallon. The AAA survey found wide disparities from area to area. In Michigan prices dropped by one cent and in Indiana by five cents, while prices rose one cent in Texas.
