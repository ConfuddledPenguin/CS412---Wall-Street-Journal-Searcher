Hawaiian Airlines Fare Increases

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/13/90

HONOLULU -- Hawaiian Airlines said surcharges have resulted in fare increases to compensate for higher fuel costs, which have recently risen 17%. The cost of trans-Pacific flights increased $10 each way and South Pacific routes will cost passengers $15 more each way, the airline said. A fuel surcharge raised inter-island flights $2.50 each way this past week.

The airline said it would resist increasing air fares as much as 10%, as other airlines have done, in hopes that oil prices will stabilize. The airline has 230 daily flights to the West Coast, South Pacific islands and Australia.
