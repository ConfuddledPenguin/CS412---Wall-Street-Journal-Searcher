The Mideast Conflict: Spending Cuts Won't Apply To Accounts of Military

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/13/90

WASHINGTON -- The Bush administration exercised its option to exempt all military personnel accounts from any across-the-board spending cuts this fall. "This is considered to be in the national interest . . . to maintain the force levels required for our national security," Budget Director Richard Darman said in notifying Congress of President Bush's decision on Friday.

About $84 billion of spending cuts will be triggered in October by the Gramm-Rudman deficit-reduction law, unless Congress and the president agree on a way to shrink the deficit or alter the law before then.

Even though Mr. Bush has exempted military personnel, the law requires that other Pentagon spending accounts absorb half of any Gramm-Rudman cuts. The Office of Management and Budget estimates these accounts would be slashed by 34.9%. Non-defense accounts covered by the law would be cut by 31.9%. Social Security and many other government benefit programs are exempt.

Earlier last week, White House spokesman Marlin Fitzwater indicated that Mr. Bush was planning only to shield some portion of the military personnel accounts from the cuts.
