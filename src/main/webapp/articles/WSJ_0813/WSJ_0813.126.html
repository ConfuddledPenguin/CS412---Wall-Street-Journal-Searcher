Mexico to Get $300 Million From Development Bank

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/13/90

WASHINGTON -- The Inter-American Development Bank approved a $300 million loan to Mexico for improvements in the country's transportation and telecommunications industries. The IADB said this was its first loan to support whole sectors of a country's economy. Previous IADB lending to Latin American countries has been linked to specific economic development projects.

The IADB said the $300 million loan will be used by the Mexican government to improve the efficiency, reduce costs and expand the capacity of its trucking industry. It will also support improvements in Mexico's telecommunications services through the privatization of Telefonos de Mexico, a government-owned company.

IADB officials said the loan to Mexico is a co-financing venture with the World Bank, which has approved $380 million in other credits to support the same programs.

Meanwhile, the U.S. Export-Import bank said it will guarantee $20.5 million in loans to Mexico for the purchase of about $24.1 million in railroad equipment from General Electric Co. and General Motors Corp.

The guarantee was extended to Mexico's Nacional Financiera, which will arrange for the loans from U.S. commercial banks or other private lenders.
