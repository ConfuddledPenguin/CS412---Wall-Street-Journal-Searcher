New Securities Issues

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/13/90

The following were among Friday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report. MORTGAGES

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $300 million of Remic mortgage securities offered in eight classes by Nomura Securities International Inc. The offering, Series 1990113, is backed by Fannie Mae 9 1/2% securities with a weighted average coupon rate of 10.125% and weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29.58 years. Among classes for which details were available, planned yield spreads ranged from 80 basis points over two-year Treasury securities to 115 basis points over 10-year Treasurys.

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $300 million of Remic mortgage securities offered in 11 classes by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets. The offering, Series 1990-115, is backed by Fannie Mae 10% 30-year securities. Among classes for which details were available, yields ranged from 8.359%, or 45 basis points over two-year Treasurys, to 9.851%, or 116 basis points over 10-year Treasurys.

Residential Funding Mortgage Securities -- $197 million of 1990-9 Remic mortgage securities offered in 10 classes by Bear Stearns & Co. The offering is backed by whole loans with a gross weighted average coupon rate of 10.659% and a weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29.67 years. Price and yield data weren't immediately available.

ASSET-BACKED

Citicorp Mortgage Trust IV -- $155 million of Remic mortgage securities offered in eight classes by Nomura. The collateral consists of 9.80% whole loans with a weighted average coupon rate of 10.98% and weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29.33 years and a Sumitomo letter of credit covering 16% of the deal. Among classes for which details were available, planned yield spreads ranged from 75 basis points above one-year Treasury securities to 165 basis points over 30-year Treasurys.

EUROBONDS

HMC Mortgage Notes 6 PLC (U.K.) -- #140 million of floating-rate notes due in September 2030 via Credit Suisse First Boston Ltd. Issued at par, reoffered at a fixed price of 99.75. Coupon floats at a margin of 0.25 point above the three-month London interbank offered rate until Sept. 19, 2000. Thereafter, margin is 0.50 point above Libor. Callable on each coupon date beginning September 1993 at par. Redemption on pro-rata basis or by open market purchases. Three years of substitution, then 10 years of further advances of up to #29.4 million. Average life is expected to be 7.5 years, based on 15% prepayment. Fees 0.35.

Jujo Paper Co. (Japan) -- 200 million Swiss francs of privately placed convertible notes due Sept. 30, 1995, with a fixed 3 3/4% coupon at par via Swiss Bank Corp. Put option March 31, 1993, at a fixed 109 7/8 to yield 7.29%. Each 50,000 Swiss franc note convertible from Sept. 17, 1990, to Sept. 22, 1995, at an estimated 2 1/2% premium over the closing share price when terms are fixed Thursday. Fees 1 1/2.
