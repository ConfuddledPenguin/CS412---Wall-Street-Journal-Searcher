UAW Official Says `True Job Security' Is Key to Contract

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/13/90

TRAVERSE CITY, Mich. -- A top United Auto Workers union official said the union would moderate its pay demands and back three-shift production schemes at Big Three assembly plants in exchange for "true job security." "I think management is right that they need more flexibility to keep their plants running at capacity and to keep jobs in this country," Stan Marshall, the UAW vice president leading the union's negotiations with Chrysler Corp., said Friday at an industry conference here.

But Mr. Marshall said the union has no interest in Chrysler's proposal to pay UAW members with stock in exchange for wage and work-practice concessions. He also said the union isn't interested in Chrysler's proposal to pay employees at certain factories for achieving specific productivity goals, such as reducing absenteeism or improving quality.

Chrysler officials had no comment on Mr. Marshall's remarks. Both General Motors Corp. and Chrysler have made union approval of three-shift production a major objective of their current national contract negotiations with the UAW.

Analysts say expanding vehicle production over three shifts from two could yield as much as $200 million more a year in gross profits from a plant building a popular model. GM credits three-shift work schedules for helping to produce record profits in the past three years at its European operations.
