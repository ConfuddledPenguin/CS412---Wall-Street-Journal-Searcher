Child World's Board Supports the Purchase Of Stock by Toys Plus

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/13/90

AVON, Mass. -- Child World Inc. said its board unanimously approved the previously announced purchase by Toys Plus Inc. of the 18% of Child World's shares that are currently in public hands. The purchase price will be $14 a share, or $28 million. Toys Plus recently was formed by Trefoil Capital Investors Limited Partnership, which last month agreed to purchase CNC Holding Corp.'s 82% stake in Child World in a "non-cash transaction." Terms of that transaction haven't been announced, and Child World said negotiations concerning it are continuing.

CNC is a Cleveland-based holding company. Trefoil was formed by the executives of Shamrock Holdings Inc., a holding company controlled by Roy E. Disney, a California investor and nephew of the late Walt Disney.

Child World, the nation's second-largest toy retailer after Toys "R" Us Inc., said its merger with Toys Plus is conditioned on Trefoil's prior purchase of the CNC stake in the retailer. Child World would be the surviving corporation after the merger. Trefoil earlier said it plans to retain Child World's current management.

Child World, which operates 175 toy supermarkets in 31 states, has been squeezed for capital. Last year, the company cut overall inventories 8.1% to save cash. For the first quarter, ended May 5, Child World reported sharply higher losses of $7.4 million, or 65 cents a share, because of a shortage of sale items after the 1989 Christmas season.

Child World said its board found Trefoil's $14-a-share offer for its public stock "to be fair and in the best interests" of shareholders. Trefoil earlier said that its capital could help reinvigorate the cashstarved retailer.
