GTE Corp. Forms New Business Group With Parker as Head

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/13/90

NEW YORK -- GTE Corp. formed a Telecommunications Products & Services Group and named the chief of its mobile communications business to head it. The group comprises four businesses: GTE Government Systems, Information Services, Mobile Communications and Spacenet. It will be headed by Terry S. Parker, president of Houston-based GTE Mobile Communications.

Mr. Parker, 45 years old, has also been named to the company's policy committee and may end up supervising several business units of Contel Corp., which is merging with GTE.

A spokesman said GTE made the appointment in part to relieve Charles R. Lee, GTE's president and chief operating officer, who had supervisory responsibility for the units now in Mr. Parker's group.

"This will give Chuck more time to work on strategic planning and work more closely with {GTE chairman James L. Johnson} on corporate vision, direction and culture," the spokesman said.

Mr. Parker will run Mobile Communications until a successor is found.

Meanwhile, a federal judge in Boston set aside a jury verdict against GTE Products Corp., including damages of $15.9 million awarded to Cool Light Co., Hollywood, Calif.

Cool Light, a maker of television studio lights that generate less heat than conventional units, alleged that GTE Products breached a contract to manufacture parts of Cool Light's product and instead produced its own version of the light.

However, Judge John J. McNaught questioned the logic of the June 29 verdict and ordered a new trial. Cool Light was unavailable for comment.
