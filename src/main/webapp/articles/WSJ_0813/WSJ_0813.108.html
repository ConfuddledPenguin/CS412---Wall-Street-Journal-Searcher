SL Industries Expects To Report a Deficit For Its Fourth Period

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/13/90

MOUNT LAUREL, N.J. -- SL Industries Inc. said it expects to report a loss for the fourth quarter, ended July 31. The company also said that because of increasing softness in most phases of its business, it expects to report that earnings from operations fell significantly from the year-earlier period. In the fiscal 1989 fourth quarter, per-share earnings were 14 cents, adjusted for a 6% stock dividend distributed last November.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading Friday, SL slumped $1.75 to close at $4.875.

In a series of announcements, the company said it plans to repurchase up to one million shares, or 15% of its common stock, and will establish in the fourth quarter a pretax reserve of $3.5 million, or about 33 cents a share. The reserve is for environmental cleanup related to manufacturing plants the company operated in the 1960s and 1970s.

The company also said it has accelerated spending at its Electrostatic Technology Inc. subsidiary, which has developed a way to coat materials with polymers. Demand for the technology, the company said, "has been growing rapdily." SL Industries further said that it sold its SL Plastics unit Aug. 2. It said profit on the transaction wasn't material, and it didn't disclose the purchaser or terms of the transaction.

Daryl L. Renschler, SL president and chief executive officer, said the company "remains in excellent financial condition, with only 4% of long-term debt to total capital." He added that book value at April 30 was $5.49 a share and that developmental expenditures continue to be funded internally.

SL Industries designs, manufactures and distributes engineered products.
