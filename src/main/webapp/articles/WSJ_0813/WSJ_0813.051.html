Pic `N' Save Puts Batchelder, Associates On Its Board, Agrees to Other Measures

  • Author: Frederick Rose
  • Date: 08/13/90

DOMINGUEZ, Calif. -- Pic 'N' Save Corp. forestalled a threatened proxy battle with Girard Partners L.P. and investor David Batchelder by agreeing to put Mr. Batchelder and two associates on the board, widen a planned stock buy-back and lift two anti-takeover measures. The pact, announced by the two sides Friday, appears to give Girard and Mr. Batchelder much of what they have sought since April, when they disclosed a 6.7% stake in Pic 'N' Save, which operates a chain of stores specializing in new "closeout" merchandise.

But the pact also leaves unclear where control of Pic 'N' Save is headed and whether Girard, which earlier said it would make a bid for the company, might later pursue an offer.

"There's no limitation on Girard or on any other shareholder," said Mr. Batchelder, an investor in La Jolla, Calif., who was once Texas oil man T. Boone Pickens, principal strategist. Mr. Batchelder declined, however, to say whether the Girard group will increase, decrease or leave unchanged its holding.

As part of the pact, Pic 'N' Save agreed to redeem its so-called "poison pill" takeover defense and to urge shareholders to amend the company's bylaws to exempt the company from a Delaware restriction on financial transactions by a hostile acquirer.

Despite its relatively small current holding, the Girard group has acquired a large representation on Pic 'N' Save's board, with three members of a reconstituted eight-member board being named by Girard and a fourth member, acceptable to both sides, apparently being named by other major holders.

Among other things, it appears that Girard will have effective control over the selection of a new president and chief executive officer. As reported, the current incumbent, Lewis B. Merrifield III, has resigned effective Jan. 1. Mr. Merrifield, who remains on the board, has agreed not to vote on matters related to his own succession.

Mr. Batchelder declined to say whether a successor had been selected. However, he added that "the issue will be dealt with promptly."

Pic 'N' Save stock barely budged after the announcement, rising 25 cents a share Friday to close at $10.25 in active over-the-counter trading. The stock had climbed from a 52-week low of $8.75 a share last Monday.

In announcing the agreement, Pic 'N' Save reiterated an earlier statement that it is "aggressively pursuing" efforts to expand to $150 million a current $60 million common stock repurchase authorization. At Friday's closing price, the $150 million would buy approximately 42% of the about 34.9 million shares outstanding at July 31. The current $60 million authorization would buy about 17%. But the bigger buy-back is contingent on suitable financing and neither side would discuss the progress, if any, in that area.

A company spokesman said that under its $60 million authority, Pic 'N' Save will start repurchases today on the open market through a solicitation group led by Goldman, Sachs & Co.

Pic 'N' Save, whose retail operations have been strained of late by rapid expansion, has been seen as a test case for the high-profile Mr. Batchelder, considered a bright and seasoned strategist in his work for Mr. Pickens and for Los Angeles investor Marvin Davis in his failed attempts to buy NWA Inc., the parent of Northwest Airlines, and UAL Corp., the parent of United Airlines.

The early results with Pic 'N' Save -- Mr. Batchelder's first independent effort -- weren't auspicious. Three months after announcing that it expected to make a $447 million offer for Pic 'N' Save, Girard said it doubted that it could raise sufficient cash to make an attractive bid to holders. Analysts wondered aloud about the attractiveness of Pic 'N' Save. And talks with management stalled repeatedly. But Girard pressed on with plans for a proxy contest.

Now, it remains to be seen whether Mr. Batchelder can steer Pic 'N' Save to more profitable ground. The company had net income of $5.9 million, or 17 cents a share, on $119 million in sales in the April 29 fiscal first quarter.

Clearly under pressure from Girard, management has set sharp cost-cutting measures designed to increase cash flow. Last week, it announced that, after the closing of 14 of its 190 stores, the facilities were returned to a previous owner and the inventory was sold for an undisclosed amount to a joint venture 50%-owned by New York-based Lionel Corp.

Under the agreement, Pic 'N' Save's board was expanded to eight members from seven and, with the resignation of three incumbents, three new nominees from the Girard group were accepted as directors. The fourth new member was agreed upon by both sides.

The directors from Girard group are: Mr. Batchelder; James J. Zehentbauer, a partner of Mr. Batchelder's; and Bill M. Thomas, a former chief financial officer of Pic 'N' Save who has sided with Mr. Batchelder and Girard. Mr. Thomas owns a 4% stake in Pic 'N' Save in addition to Girard's 6.7%.

The director accepted by both sides is Peter Willmott, former chairman, president and chief executive officer of Carson Pirie Scott & Co., a food services concern.

The incumbent directors who remained are: Arthur Frankel, chairman and former chief executive officer of the company; Mr. Merrifield, the departing president and chief executive officer; Ronald Spogli, general partner of Freeman Spogli & Co.; and Bruce E. Karatz, president and chief executive officer of Kaufman & Broad Home Corp.
