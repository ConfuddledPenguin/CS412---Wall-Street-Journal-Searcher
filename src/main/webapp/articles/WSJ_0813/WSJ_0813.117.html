Marketing & Media: Disney, Cinergi Reach Distribution Pact for Movies

  • Author: Richard Turner
  • Date: 08/13/90

HOLLYWOOD -- Walt Disney Co. said it reached a five-year exclusive motion picture distribution agreement with Cinergi Productions Inc., a new company formed by former Carolco Pictures Inc. co-chairman Andy Vajna, best-known for co-producing the "Rambo" series and "Total Recall." The deal, which had been rumored in Hollywood for months, represents a departure for Disney's film division, which, unlike other studios, hasn't had outside producers regularly finance and make movies for it to distribute. Though Disney has wanted to increase the number of films flowing through its distribution pipeline, it also likes to exert tight controls over the production of these films. Last year it started its own new film unit, Hollywood Pictures, rather than resort to a distribution deal or acquisition.

Disney will still have strong behind-the-scenes input into how Cinergi's films are made, industry observers say. And Disney will be able to release a series of expensively made films without having to pay for them or sully its image as a cost-conscious producer unwilling to pay huge sums for "talent."

Disney executives see Cinergi as a source of big-budget action/adventure films, a genre they themselves generally have avoided. Cinergi's first project is "The Stand," a love story set in the Amazon rain forest. The film features the director-actor team from "The Hunt for Red October," John McTiernan and Sean Connery, with a script by Tom Schulman, who was involved in writing Disney's two biggest hits of last year, "Dead Poet's Society" and "Honey, I Shrunk the Kids."

Mr. Vajna said his company expects to produce two to three films a year: "event-oriented" pictures with "big stars and big directors."

Under the agreement, Disney acquires all North and South American distribution rights, including home video and television, while Cinergi and Mr. Vajna, who is experienced in international distribution, retains distribution rights for the rest of the world. Partly by pre-selling these foreign rights, Cinergi will finance the pictures and Disney will receive a fee for marketing and distribution.

Mr. Vajna co-founded Carolco with Mario Kassar in 1975 and departed last year, selling his 36% stake to Mr. Kassar for more than $100 million.
