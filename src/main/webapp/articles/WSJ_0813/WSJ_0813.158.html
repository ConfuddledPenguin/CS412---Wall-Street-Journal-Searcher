Program Trades Average 9.9% of Big Board Activity

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/13/90

Program trading accounted for an average of 9.9% of New York Stock Exchange volume, or 17 million shares a day, in the week ended July 27. That was up slightly from an average of 9.6%, or 16 million shares a day, in the previous week, the Big Board said in its weekly report on computer-aided trading.

The most active program traders for the week were General Electric Co.'s Kidder, Peabody Group Inc. (16.3 million shares), Morgan Stanley Group Inc. (12.7 million), Goldman, Sachs & Co. (7 million), PaineWebber (6.5 million) and Merrill Lynch & Co. (6.5 million). These five firms accounted for 58% of all program trading on the exchange.

On July 23, when the Dow Jones Industrial Average fell nearly 60 points in the first hour of trading, the Big Board said Kidder Peabody sold 351,000 shares in program-trading strategies for its own account and 383,000 shares as an agent for customers between 10 and 10:30 a.m. Salomon Inc.'s Salomon Brothers unit sold 543,000 shares for customers in program-trading strategies.

The same day, the Dow fell to a loss of 106.94 points by 10:46 a.m., but then rallied 49 points in 14 minutes, paring losses to 57.94 points by 11 a.m. During that time, Merrill Lynch bought 805,000 shares for customers. Susquehanna Investment Group sold 592,000 shares for its own account. CS First Boston Inc.'s First Boston Corp. unit bought 419,000 shares for its own account and 460,000 shares to facilitate customer orders.

Program trading is the purchase or sale of a group of at least 15 and often hundreds of different stocks. Stock-index arbitrage, which involves stock purchases and sales with offsetting trades in futures and options, was led by Kidder Peabody, which accounted for 38% of the total.
