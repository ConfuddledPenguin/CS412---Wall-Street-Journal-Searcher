Technology: Weyerhaeuser Recycling Plant

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/13/90

TACOMA, Wash. -- Weyerhaeuser Co. said it will open one of the nation's largest paper recycling facilities in Portland, Ore., by the end of the year. The plant will be devoted exclusively to the collection, sorting and redistribution of old newspapers and other paper products. The new facility will supply the giant pulp and paper company's North Pacific Paper Corp. newsprint mill in Longview, Wash. Company officials are working to expand production there by as much as 50% and add a de-inking facility by mid-1991.

The new plant is expected to handle 700,000 tons of paper a day, which represents about 150,000 daily newspapers, and employ 70 to 80 people. The company owns and operates 16 recycling facilities.

Corrections & Amplifications

WEYERHAEUSER Co.'s new paper recycling facility, when opened, will handle 700 tons of paper a day. In Monday's edition, the capacity of the recycling plant was misstated.
