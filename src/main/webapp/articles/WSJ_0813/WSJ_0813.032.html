New Stock Listings

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/13/90

NEW YORK -- The New York Stock Exchange announced two new listings, the American Stock Exchange one, and the National Market System of the over-the-counter market two. Of the new listings, two were initial public offerings.

Church & Dwight Co., a Princeton, N.J., maker of chemicals, begins trading Wednesday under the symbol CHD on the Big Board. It previously traded over the counter.

NS Group Inc., a Newport, Ky., holding company for four principal operating subsidiaries, including Newport Steel Corp. and Kentucky Electric Steel Corp., also begins trading Wednesday, under NSS. It previously traded on the American Stock Exchange.

Joining the Amex, is Bradmar Petroleum Corp. of Oklahoma City, Okla., an oil and gas company, which began trading as an initial public offering on a "when issued" basis under BPC.WI.

On the OTC market, Easel Corp., a Woburn, Mass., maker of software products for business graphics applications, began trading as an initial public offering under EASL.

Keene Corp., a New York maker of composite materials and structures for aerospace, defense and commercial use, began trading under KEEN. It recently was spun off from Bairnco Corp.

Separately, the Pacific Stock Exchange began trading options on Betz Laboratories Inc., a Trevose, Pa., maker of water treatment chemicals, and Century Telephone Enterprises Inc., a Monroe, La., telephone holding company.
