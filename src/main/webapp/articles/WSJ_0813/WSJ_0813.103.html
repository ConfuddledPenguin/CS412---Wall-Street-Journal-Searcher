Badger Meter Class B Shares

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/13/90

MILWAUKEE -- Badger Meter Inc. said it scheduled a special shareholders meeting for Sept. 18 to vote on a proposal to allow seven corporate officers to become holders of the company's Class B shares, which have so-called super-voting rights. Until now, only existing Class B holders could buy Class B shares as they became available. The maker of flow measurement products, which has about 562,785 B shares outstanding, said the proposal doesn't involve the issuance of new B shares.

The B shares have 10 votes a share vs. one vote for regular common.

The proposal, the company said, is aimed at increasing its ability to "attract and retain high quality officers." The proposal covers only the top seven officers.
