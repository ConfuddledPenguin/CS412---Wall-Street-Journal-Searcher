Who's News: Chiefs of Two Thrifts In Puerto Rico Resign

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/13/90

The chief executives at two large and troubled thrifts in Puerto Rico resigned Friday. In Ponce, Ramiro L. Colon Jr. stepped down as president and chief executive officer of Ponce Federal Bank. Mr. Colon, who has been affiliated with the thrift for over 30 years, will continue as chairman of the board during an interim management transition period, the thrift said.

Ponce Federal said that, subject to regulatory approval, Hans H. Hertell, an attorney in Puerto Rico and public-interest director of the Federal Home Loan Bank of New York, was named its president and chief operating officer to succeed Mr. Colon.

Separately, Mariano J. Mier resigned as chairman, president and chief executive officer of First Federal Savings Bank of Puerto Rico, based in San Juan, the thrift said.

First Federal said in a statement that Angel Alvarez Perez was named acting president and chief executive and German Malaret was named as the new chairman. Officials at First Federal Savings couldn't be reached to comment on the executive changes.

Two other top officers at First Federal also resigned last week: Arturo J. Somohano, former senior executive vice president and chief operating officer, and Fernando Ochoa, former senior vice president for investments.

Both thrifts have been under the close scrutiny of federal thrift regulators because of troubled loan portfolios.
