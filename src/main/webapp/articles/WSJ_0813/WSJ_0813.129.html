Business Brief -- Interlake Corp.: Aerospace Parts Tested Incorrectly; Inquiry Begins

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/13/90

Interlake Corp. said that some tests of aerospace parts produced by the company weren't performed properly. The company said it is investigating and doesn't believe any of the parts are unsafe. The testing problems occurred at a Cleveland plant Interlake sold in May to Wyman-Gordon Co., which notified Interlake about the testing deficiencies and corrected the problem.

The plant makes fittings, brackets, manifolds, housings and swirlers, which are used to mix fuel and air, and sells these products "throughout the aerospace industry," a spokesman said.

Interlake, based in Oak Brook, Ill., said that some tests weren't handled properly and that some parts were welded without customer approval. Interlake wouldn't identify the customers, except the U.S. government, but said it has informed them all. It said it also hired Arthur Andersen & Co., the accounting firm, to review the investigation into the problem.

The parts made at the plant sold for about $50 each, on average, the spokesman said, and the plant produced annual sales of between $8 million and $13 million while owned by Interlake.
