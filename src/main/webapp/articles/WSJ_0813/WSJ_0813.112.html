Bank of New England Plan To Merge Units Is Blocked

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/13/90

BOSTON -- Bank of New England Corp. said a Massachusetts state court judge temporarily blocked the previously announced merger of the company's main Boston-based affiliate with its affiliate bank in Western Massachusetts. As reported, the troubled bank holding company had planned to merge its Springfield, Mass.-based Bank of New England-West affiliate with its Bank of New England, N.A., Boston, as part of its program to streamline operations and cut costs.

A Bank of New England spokesman said the state court granted a motion to block the merger that had been made by a group of 16 investors in the bank. They were shareholders of a small Massachusetts bank that previously merged with another bank, which, in turn, was acquired in 1986 by Bank of New England Corp.

The shareholders are seeking additional payment for their holdings in the original bank. They assert that Bank of New England's currently planned merger could hurt their chances of receiving the payments, which a state judge earlier ordered Bank of New England to make to them. The bank has appealed that order.

The Bank of New England spokesman said, "We believe {the state court's action} is a temporary obstacle and expect to resolve all legal issues and proceed promptly with the merger."
