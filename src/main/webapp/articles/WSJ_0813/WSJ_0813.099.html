Business Brief -- Comstock Resources Inc.: Maple Gas to Purchase Gas, Install Processing Facilities

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/13/90

Comstock Resources Inc., Dallas, said it reached a definite 15-year agreement for the sale of all casinghead gas produced at its 54,000-acre Bivins Ranch lease in Texas. Comstock said Maple Gas Corp. agreed to purchase the gas and to install processing facilities that will have a capacity of 20 million cubic feet a day by Dec. 1. Subject to Comstock's deliverability, capacity will increase to 40 million cubic feet a day by April 1. Comstock said production would come from 102 wells, each producing about five barrels of oil and at least 180,000 cubic feet of gas a day. Comstock said the number of producing wells on the lease would increase.

Comstock said Maple, a private Texas company, would construct pipeline connections and gathering facilities at an estimated cost of $20 million. Starting Dec. 1, Maple will pay Comstock based on 90% of residue gas and 70% of all liquids extracted by the processing facilities, Comstock said.

Comstock said that proceeds from its public offering in May are being used to fund completion of its well and drilling program on the Bivins lease. It said the proceeds were also used to pay off all bank debt.
