Price Stern Sloan Inc. And Mattel Sign Pact For Barbie Doll Books

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/13/90

LOS ANGELES -- Mattel Inc. said it signed an exclusive licensing agreement with Price Stern Sloan Inc. to produce a series of books, including a cookbook, based on its Barbie doll toy. The books will add to a plethora of products Mattel is introducing around the image of Barbie, the world's best-selling plastic playmate. Already, the toy maker has unvieled several new Barbie Style products, including a line of girl-sized costumes, bed sheets and accessories.

Over time, Mattel has said it plans to take Barbie beyond the toy category and into children's clothing, beauty products and other consumer goods -- hoping that by doing so, the company will bolster sales of the doll.

Mattel said it is considering several proposals from movie producers to take Barbie to the silver screen. A Mattel spokesman said a decision on whether to make a movie hasn't been made. One consideration the company has always had is that a Barbie movie, which would show how the doll supposedly sounds and acts, would impinge on each individual girl's fantasies about the doll, the Mattel spokesman said.

Barbie accounted for $600 million of Mattel's $1.2 billion in total revenue for 1989.

Los Angeles-based Price Stern Sloan, a publisher and distributor of children's books, said it plans to introduce the first of several lines of Barbie books in the spring of 1991.

In addition, Price Stern said that in July, it entered into a licensing agreement with Walt Disney Co. to produce an electronic wand with a Mickey Mouse head and to publish six books featuring Disney characters.
