Western Union Corp. Locks Out Workers Amid Contract Talks

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/13/90

UPPER SADDLE RIVER, N.J. (AP) -- Western Union Corp. on Saturday locked out 2,000 employees in the middle of contract talks with their union. The company did not indicate whether the workers would be rehired if it signs a new contract with their union, the Communications Workers of America, which represents a total of 2,500 workers at Western Union.

Western Union said the replacements would not affect its money-transfer, telex, electronic mail and paper-based message services.

"We sincerely regret that, in the absence of a union contract, we have reluctantly had to take this defensive action," said Robert J. Amman, Western Union president and chief executive officer. "We cannot operate under the ever-present threat of an employee strike while continuing to bear the cost required to ensure that the company would survive one."

Western Union has a total of 3,400 employees. The CWA represents operators, customer relations clerks, clerical workers and field technicians.

Dan Beckstead, the chief union negotiator, deplored the lockout, saying the union had not given the company any indication it intends to strike.

"In fact, we told them we wanted to continue to negotiate in an attempt to reach an agreement," Mr. Beckstead said. CWA workers have authorized their leadership to call a strike.

Western Union said many of the replaced CWA workers were from its central telephone bureaus in Bridgeton, Miss., and Reno, Nev.; its computer centers in Middletown, Va., and Bridgeton.

It temporarily replaced them with workers from Western Union headquarters and trained temporary replacements, the company said.

The company has been negotiating with representatives of the CWA since July 23. The previous two-year contract expired Aug. 6, but management and union negotiators have continued to meet to try to agree on a new pact.
