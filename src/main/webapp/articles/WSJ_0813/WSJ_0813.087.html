The Mideast Conflict: Mosbacher Is Taking Part In Gulf Crisis Discussions

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/13/90

WASHINGTON -- Commerce Secretary Robert Mosbacher said his recusal from energy-policy matters has been waived because of the crisis in the Persian Gulf. "I have a temporary waiver from my recusal because we're talking about the Iraq-Kuwait problem. With something that pressing, the president wanted his full team," Mr. Mosbacher said in an interview.

Mr. Mosbacher has previously been recused from energy-policy matters because of possible conflicts of interest. Before taking his present post, he spent his career in the oil business in Houston, where he managed his family oil concern Mosbacher Energy Corp. The company is now run by his son Robert Mosbacher Jr.

Discussing the current crisis, the commerce secretary was critical of those retailers of oil and gas who have increased their prices drastically. "I think it's shortsighted of these guys. If I was in business, I'd be a little more circumspect. Long-term it isn't smart business," he said.

The longstanding friendship between President Bush and Mr. Mosbacher dates back to the 1960s, when both men sought their fortunes in the oil exploration business in Texas.
