Cardis Loss Shrank In Fiscal 4th Period; Sales Declined 1.9%

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/13/90

CERRITOS, Calif. -- Cardis Corp., which late last year emerged from its Chapter 11 bankruptcy-law reorganization, reported that its fourth-quarter net loss narrowed to $1.1 million, or 11 cents a share, from a net loss of $13.8 million, or $1.26 a share, a year ago. The wholesale distributor of automotive parts, supplies, and accessories, said improved operating results reflect reduced interest expense since it was not charged on debt deferred by its Chapter 11 bankruptcy.

Cardis said that the year-ago net loss included a loss from discontinued operations of $4.1 million, an extraordinary loss from debt restructuring and its reorganization of $1.7 million, and a gain from an accounting change of $3.7 million.

Cardis reported the loss on a 1.9% decline in sales to $21.2 million from $21.6 million a year earlier.

For the year ended April 30, the company reported an operating loss of $3 million, or 28 cents a share, compared with an operating loss of $22.2 million, or $2.03 a share, a year ago. In the current year, an extraordinary gain from debt restructuring and reorganization of $67.9 million made net income $64.9 million, or $5.92 a share. In the previous year, a loss from discontinued operations of $5 million, an extraordinary loss of $1.7 million, and a gain of $3.7 million from an accounting change, gave the company a net loss of $25.3 million, or $2.31 a share.

Cardis reported a 9.5% drop in sales to $84.8 million from last year's $93.7 million.
