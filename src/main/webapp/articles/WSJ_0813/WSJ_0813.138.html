Business Brief -- AmeriFirst Bank: Thrift Signs Pact to Accept Strict Regulatory Control

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/13/90

AmeriFirst Bank of Miami said it signed an agreement with the Office of Thrift Supervision that puts it under the strict control of regulators in the areas of commercial lending, foreclosures and development real estate. AmeriFirst said in a statement that the agreement permits federal regulators to appoint a conservator or receiver for the institution if it deems the step necessary.

The thrift said the pact allows "the bank to continue to serve fully its retail and deposit customers."

AmeriFirst has been hurt by a large portfolio of troubled commercial real-estate loans.

Earlier this month, after spending more than a year as chairman of the board, M. Lee Pearce, a prominent Miami businessman, resigned, saying he felt he had done all he could. He was succeeded by Laurence Brodym, a local dentist.

As reported, AmeriFirst recently said a capital plan, which included an infusion of private capital, appeared "infeasible," and, as a result, the Office of Thrift Supervision rejected it.
