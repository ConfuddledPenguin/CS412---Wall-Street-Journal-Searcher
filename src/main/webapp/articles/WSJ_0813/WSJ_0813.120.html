German Publisher Forms Electronic Media Division

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/13/90

FRANKFURT -- West German publishing group Axel Springer Verlag AG said Friday it has formed a new Electronic Media division. West Berlin-based Springer said the new operating unit will be responsible for the group's expansion into the rapidly growing global electronic communications sector. It cited specific areas such as "new (communications) technologies and mobile communications."

Springer added that it will place a priority on growth in the electronic media market. That would include shareholdings in satellite television stations, among others, officials said.

Meantime, the company that publishes West Germany's popular mass-circulation daily newspaper, Bild, said it will continue to expand aggressively in the newspaper and magazine markets despite "hard competitive pressures" in West Germany.

The company also said it plans to broaden its presence abroad. Springer has been expanding into Eastern Europe since the liberalization of the East Bloc.
