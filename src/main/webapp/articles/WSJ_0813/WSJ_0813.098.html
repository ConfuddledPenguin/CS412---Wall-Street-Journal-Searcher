Lionel, Perlmutter Venture

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/13/90

NEW YORK -- Lionel Corp. said it formed a joint venture with Isaac Perlmutter, former head of Odd Lot Trading Corp., to purchase the Job Lot/Pushcart retail chain from Pic "N" Save Corp. Through the venture, Job Lot Associates L.P., Lionel and Mr. Perlmutter will contribute $3 million each to the Job Lot/Pushcart chain, which consists of 14 closeout stores in the New York metropolitan area. Under the agreement, Lionel will contribute only inventory to the stores; Mr. Perlmutter will contribute cash and inventory.

Lionel, which operates 94 specialty toy stores in 15 states, has been under pressure from some shareholders to find a buyer. Earlier this year, the company turned down investor Robert I. Toussie's offer of $8 a share, or $94.4 million.

Mr. Perlmutter is a principal in a media-buying company that has Lionel as a client, and Lionel has purchased toy inventory from companies controlled by Mr. Perlmutter's Admerex International Inc.

Trendex Capital Management Corp., a Fort Lauderdale, Fla., concern representing a dissident group of Lionel shareholders with a 5% stake, had asked Lionel to form a panel of outsiders to examine Lionel's relationship with Mr. Perlmutter -- a request the company refused. The Trendex group also has asked Lionel not to impede prospective acquirers such as Mr. Toussie, who dropped his bid earlier this year when the stock was trading for $5.625 a share.

In American Stock Exchange composite trading Friday, Lionel stock closed at $2.625 a share, unchanged.
