U.K. Data Give New Signs Of Economic Slowdown

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 08/13/90

LONDON -- Fresh evidence of a British economic slowdown emerged from a study showing sluggish consumer spending and slow retail sales growth. The Confederation of British Industry's latest monthly survey of distributive trades found low growth in retailing activity across the United Kingdom. The survey also found that demand among wholesalers and automobile companies was lower than a year ago.

"The economic slowdown is continuing to have a pervasive impact throughout the distributive trades," said the report, to be released today. The report provides fresh evidence that the British government's tight-money policy is beginning to slow consumer activity.

Despite a modest rise in monthly retail demand, orders by retailers were no higher than a year ago, the survey found. Particularly hard hit have been machinery and office-equipment sales and the auto industry.

"We are now witnessing a fall in distributors sales as a whole for the first time since the survey began seven years ago," said Andrew Sentance, the confederation's director of economic affairs.
