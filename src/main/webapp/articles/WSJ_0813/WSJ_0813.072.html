Nippondenso of Japan And a Unit of Fiat Plan U.K. Venture

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 08/13/90

TOKYO -- Nippondenso Co. said it and Italy's Magneti Marelli S.p.A. agreed to establish a joint venture to produce car air conditioners in Britain and to exchange shares in two subsidiaries. The two companies will form the joint venture to pursue market opportunities in the quickly developing European market, the Japanese company said.

Nippondenso is the second-largest member of the Toyota group and is 23.3%-owned by Toyota Motor Corp. Milan-based Magneti Marelli is 60%-owned by Fiat S.p.A.

Nippondenso will hold 75% of the joint venture, and Magneti Marelli the remainder.

The new firm is to be located in Shropshire and capitalized at an initial investment of around #65 million ($121.4 million). The two parents aim for it to turn out 400,000 units each of automotive air conditioners and heaters by 1995, Nippondenso said.

Under the agreement, Nippondenso also will acquire 25% of Magneti Marelli's Borletti Climatizzazione S.r.l. unit, which makes air conditioners and heaters in Italy, while Magneti Marelli will take 25% in Nippondenso's ND Marston Ltd., a maker of radiators in the U.K.
