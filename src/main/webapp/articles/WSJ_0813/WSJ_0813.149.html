Homestead Posts $30.2 Million Loss In Second Quarter

  • Author: Richard B. Schmitt
  • Date: 08/13/90

MILLBRAE, Calif. -- Homestead Financial Corp. reported a surprising $30.2 million net loss for the second quarter, reflecting loss reserves mostly associated with the California real estate market. The $2.7 billion-asset parent of Homestead Savings cited an $18 million general loss reserve established in the wake of recent regulatory examinations as well as a $16.7 million provision for potential losses on the planned sale of four apartment complexes owned by its land development subsidiary. The company added that the sale of the apartment complexes, representing $129.9 million in assets, was part of a plan to improve capital.

The net loss equaled $2.66 a share, compared with a net loss of $18.7 million, or $1.65 a share, a year earlier. Homestead said the latest loss left the thrift unit out of compliance with federal minimum tangible capital rules, and that it would be amending a filing with regulators regarding plans for improving its capital position.

Homestead's situation points up how the nation's real estate woes are starting to afflict even the perennially healthy California market. A number of banks and thrifts in the state have recently reported growing loan troubles from real estate, reflecting the effect of high residential prices, a glut of commercial space and a growing credit crunch affecting developers that is a direct effect of the S&L debacle.

Charles J. Vaughan, Homestead's executive vice president and chief operating officer, said the latest losses largely reflect problems with the thrift's real estate subsidiary that is being hurt because potential developers and buyers are having trouble getting financing. "If you have to own real estate in the U.S., most people would rather own it in California. But what this says is that when you have a tightening of credit nationwide, no state is totally immune," he said.

Homestead said that, excluding loss provisions, its net interest income for the quarter increased to $8.7 million from $5.2 million a year earlier.

For the six months, Homestead reported a net loss of $25.7 million, or $2.26 a share, compared with a net loss of $40.1 million, or $3.55 a share, a year earlier.
