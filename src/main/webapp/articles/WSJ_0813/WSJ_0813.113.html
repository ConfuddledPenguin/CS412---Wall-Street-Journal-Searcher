Riggs Buys Most Assets Of Bank in Washington

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/13/90

WASHINGTON -- Riggs National Corp. acquired most of the assets and deposits of the National Bank of Washington for a $33 million premium after federal regulators declared the bank insolvent and closed it. National Bank of Washington was a unit of Washington Bancorp., Washington, D.C., which filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection Aug. 1.

Under the agreement with the Federal Deposit Insurance Corp., Riggs National Bank assumed about $1 billion in deposits and will purchase about $1.1 billion in assets, mostly loans.

FDIC will retain about $500 million of "adversely classified" assets and may be forced to keep certain other loans, Riggs said.

Separately, the Treasury Department said it fined National Bank of Washington $368,000 for money laundering just before the bank was closed. The Treasury said that the bank failed to report currency transactions as required under the Bank Secrecy Act. In 1988, former bank senior vice president Roger Schultz pleaded guilty to several violations under the secrecy act.
