Business Brief -- Webster Clothes Inc.: Edison Brothers Stores Plans To Buy Baltimore Operation

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/13/90

Edison Brothers Stores Inc., moving to expand its group of menswear stores, plans to acquire Webster Clothes Inc. for $19.2 million. St. Louis-based Edison said it agreed to pay $6 a share for Webster's common stock. Fifty cents a share will be placed in escrow, subject to certain events and adjustments. A Webster official said the company will disclose details of the contingencies on Monday in its merger filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission. Certain Webster shareholders, who own 80% of the stock, have granted Edison an option to purchase their shares at the merger price.

The merger, subject to approval by Webster shareholders as well as other conditions, is expected to close in late September, Edison said.

Edison operates about 1,200 menswear stores. Webster, based in Baltimore, operates 128 young men's clothing stores under the names Webster, Zeidler & Zeidler, and Aggio.
