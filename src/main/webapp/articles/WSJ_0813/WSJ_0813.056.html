Scholastic to Offer 4.2 Million Shares In September for Up to $88.2 Million

  • Author: Kathleen Deveny
  • Date: 08/13/90

NEW YORK -- Scholastic Corp., a publisher of children's textbooks and magazines that went private in 1987, said it plans an initial public offering of 4.2 million common shares valued at as much as $88.2 million. Scholastic said in a Securities and Exchange Commission filing that 3.3 million shares will be offered by the company and 930,000 shares will be put up for sale by the family of founder Maurice R. Robinson, which controls the voting power through a separate class of shares.

The offering, expected in late September, will be made concurrently in the U.S. and internationally -- 3.4 million shares in the U.S. and 800,000 shares internationally, the company said. The offering will be managed by Goldman, Sachs & Co. and Alex. Brown & Sons Inc. The offering plan provides an overallotment to the underwriters of 630,000 shares.

At the projected offering price of $18 to $21 a share, the company said, the offering would raise from $75.6 million to $88.2 million. Including the public offering, the company has about 9.4 million shares outstanding.

Scholastic said it intends to use proceeds from the offering to pay bank debt. The company's debt was $46.5 million at May 31, but because Scholastic typically has a negative cash flow during the summer months, that figure is likely to climb before the offering is completed.

The voting power is vested in the company's Class A stock. Richard Robinson, chairman and chief executive officer, and two trusts controlled by descendants of company founder Maurice R. Robinson, own approximately 95% of those shares.

The company said holders of common stock will be entitled to elect one-fifth of the company's board, which now has 12 members.

Scholastic hasn't paid dividends since 1986, and has no current plans to pay dividends on its common or Class A stock in the future, the company says.

Scholastic, formerly known as SI Holdings Inc., was a publicly traded company until 1987, when a group led by management acquired the company for about $84 million in cash and securities. After the offering, current shareholders will control 67.3% of the company, which they acquired at an average cost of $1.35 a share. The company's new investors will own 32.7% of the company's shares, acquired at an average estimated price of $19.50.

For the year ended May 31, Scholastic reported net income of $2.3 million, compared with a loss of $4.8 million the previous year. Revenue was $374.6 million, up from $299.6 million.

Scholastic will apply to have its common stock included in the National Market System of the over-the-counter market, under the symbol SCHL.
