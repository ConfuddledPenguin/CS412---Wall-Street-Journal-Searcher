Southmark Corp. Leaves Chapter 11, Starts Distributions

  • Author: Christi Harlan
  • Date: 08/13/90

DALLAS -- Southmark Corp. said it emerged from Chapter 11 proceedings and began distributions to creditors and shareholders under the reorganization plan approved last month by a U.S. Bankruptcy Court judge. In accordance with the plan, Southmark said it transferred $76 million in cash along with new securities to various agents on behalf of its creditors and shareholders. Holders of old Southmark securities will receive their portions of the cash, new notes, new preferred stock and new common stock upon surrender of their old certificates.

As part of its reorganization plan under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code, Southmark also transferred $100 million in assets to San Jacinto Savings Association, its Houston-based thrift unit. The company said it won't have management control of San Jacinto.

The thrift will be exempted from the planned liquidation of most of Southmark's assets. The proceeds of the liquidation will be used to redeem the new notes and new preferred stock of the reorganized real estate and financial services concern.

The company proposed deferring payment of some legal and other professional fees incurred with its reorganization until April 15, 1991, to maintain adequate working capital. The deferral, agreed to by professionals in the case, total about $8 million, the company said.

Southmark also said Glen Adams was named chairman, president and chief executive officer. Mr. Adams, as previously announced, succeeds Arthur G. Weiss, who resigned.

Mr. Adams previously managed the liquidation of Great Western Sugar Co. and its subsidiaries. He has also held senior positions at Texas Industries Inc. and Electronic Data Systems Corp.

Eight of Southmark's 10 directors also resigned and will be succeeded by directors to be nominated by the company's unsecured creditors' committee. The company said the two remaining directors, James A. Colquitt and Arthur L. Crowe, agreed to remain in office for a transition period of about one week.
