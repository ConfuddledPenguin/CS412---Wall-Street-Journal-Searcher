Coca-Cola Venture To Spend $187 Million For New Plant in U.K.

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/13/90

ATLANTA -- Coca-Cola Co., seeking to build its fast-growing business in Britain, said its British joint venture plans to invest $187 million to build a new soft-drink plant. The joint venture, Coca-Cola & Schweppes Beverages Ltd., is 49%-owned by Coca-Cola and 51%-owned by Cadbury Schweppes PLC.

The plant, to be completed in the spring of 1992, will be located in southern England, possibly in Banbury or Northampton.

The announcement of the new plant comes on the heels of the opening of another plant the joint venture recently completed in Wakefield, in northern England. The Wakefield plant is the largest soft-drink production facility in Europe. Like the Wakefield plant, the new facility will produce soft drinks in plastic bottles and cans.

Coca-Cola will need the extra plant capacity, according to a spokesman, to keep up with growing demand. Unlike the U.S., where the soft-drink market is more mature and limped along at a 2.5% growth rate last year, industry soda volumes in the U.K. jumped 13%. And sales of Coke products in that country are growing at double to triple that rate, the spokesman said.

The joint venture's job is to boost consumption levels by making soft drinks more available in restaurants, vending machines and grocery stores, and by generating advertising to increase public awareness of the products.
