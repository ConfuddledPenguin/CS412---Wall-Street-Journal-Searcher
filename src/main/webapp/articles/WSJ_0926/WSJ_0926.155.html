Ex-Lawyer Pleads Guilty in Alleged Laundering Scheme

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/26/90

NEW YORK -- Fred Stella, a former lawyer, pleaded guilty to three felony counts in connection with an alleged money-laundering scheme involving a former official of Bear, Stearns & Co. In federal court in Manhattan, Mr. Stella, 51 years old, pleaded guilty to one count of conspiring to obstruct justice and two counts of obstructing justice. He was indicted in May along his brother Joseph Stella, 46, formerly head of Bear Stearns' stock-loan department, and two others. Joseph Stella resigned from Bear Stearns in April. Bear Stearns, a unit of Bear Stearns Cos., wasn't involved in the alleged wrongdoing.

The four defendants were accused of laundering $1.7 million in checks that allegedly reflected the proceeds of embezzled securities from Bankers Trust Co. and fees from stock-loan transactions that others sought to avoid paying taxes on. Bankers Trust is a unit of Bankers Trust New York Corp.

Fred Stella, of Brooklyn, is scheduled to be sentenced Dec. 5 by federal Judge Charles Haight. The other defendents are to go on trial Oct. 5 before Judge Haight.

In addition to Joseph and Fred Stella, the indictment named Roger C. Capozzi Sr. and Joseph A. Pontarelli. The indictment accused Mr. Capozzi of cashing checks for Fred Stella through a Brooklyn clothing-embroidery business. Mr. Pontarelli was accused of cashing checks for Mr. Stella through a Manhattan liquor store and check-cashing business.
