Marketing & Media: CBS Wins Ratings Race In New TV Season 1st Week

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/26/90

NEW YORK -- CBS narrowly won the prime-time ratings race for the first week of the 1990-91 television season, its first weekly victory since January 1987. According to figures compiled by the A.C. Nielsen Co., CBS Inc.'s network, which has ranked third among the three major networks for three consecutive years, finished the week with an average rating of 13.6% of U.S. homes and a 23% share of the nightly viewing audience. A unit of General Electric Co., the NBC network, which has been No. 1 for five years, ran second in the week with a 13.4 rating and 23 share. Capital Cities/ABC Inc.'s ABC network was third with a 13.2 rating and 22 share. And News Corp.'s Fox Broadcasting Co., with five nights of programming, had a 5 rating and 8 share. (A ratings point represents 931,000 or 1% of the nation's TV households; shares are the percentage of sets in use.)

In other ratings news, the first two installments of "The Civil War," a 12-hour series broadcast on the Public Broadcasting Service, set a record as the largest audience ever for a PBS program. On Monday, it registered a 9.1 rating and 13 share, according to overnight Nielsen ratings from 24 major markets.

Despite the strong showing by CBS in prime time, the "CBS Evening News with Dan Rather" slid into third place, attracting 8.5% of U.S. homes. ABC's newscast was in first place for the 37th week with 10.3% of homes, and NBC was second with 8.8%.
