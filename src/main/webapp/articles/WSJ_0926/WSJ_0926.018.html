Technology: NEC Develops a Chip Process

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/26/90

TOKYO -- NEC Corp. said it has come up with a new fabrication process to make basic materials for large-scale integrated chips, allowing mass production of LSI circuits for use in supercomputers. Officials said the company developed the process to meet increasing demand for computers capable of processing data with high velocity. By concentrating computing power, LSI chips allow manufacturers to increase speed markedly.

Using the new process, called chemical vapor deposition, NEC is able to build layers of ultrathin silicon and silicon germanium for the chips at a rate 50 times faster than conventional fabrication processes.
