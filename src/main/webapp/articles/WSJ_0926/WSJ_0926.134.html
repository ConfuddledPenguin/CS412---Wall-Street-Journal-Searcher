Business Brief -- Stride Rite Corp.: Third-Period Net Rose 24%; Gains for Keds Line Cited

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/26/90

Stride Rite Corp., citing the continued popularity of its Keds shoe line, reported that net income for the third quarter, ended Aug. 31, rose 24% to $16.5 million, or 63 cents a share, on a 15% sales increase to $148.2 million. In the year-earlier quarter, the Cambridge, Mass., footwear retailer had net income of $13.3 million, or 49 cents a share, on sales of $128.5 million.

Stride Rite's Keds division posted a 24% sales increase in the quarter, partly because of increased shipments of women's and children's leather products, which supplemented the growth of Keds basic canvas products, the company said.

Third-quarter sales at Stride Rite's Children's Group rose 5% from last year's quarter, while Sperry Top-Sider sales posted a 7% increase.

For the nine months, Stride Rite's net income rose 21% to $46.1 million, or $1.74 a share, while sales rose 14% to $414.8 million.
