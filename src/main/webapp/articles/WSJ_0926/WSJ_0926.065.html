New Securities Issues

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/26/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report. CORPORATE

Coastal Corp. -- $400 million of debt securities in a two-part offering priced through underwriters at Lehman Brothers. The first part, $250 million of 10 3/8% notes due Oct. 1, 2000, was priced at 99 7/8 to yield 10.395%, a spread of 137.5 basis points above the Treasury's 10-year note. The second part, $150 million of 10 3/4% debentures due Oct. 1, 2010, was priced at 99.59 to yield 10.80%, a spread of 165 basis points above the Treasury's 30-year bond. The issue is rated Baa-3 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and triple-B-minus by Standard & Poor's Corp.

MUNICIPAL

Washington Public Power Supply System -- $250 million of revenue refunding bonds, Nuclear Project No. 1, Series 1990C, priced by a Goldman, Sachs & Co. group to yield from 6.60% in 1992 to 8.04% in 2017. Current interest serial bonds are priced to yield from 6.60% in 1992 to 7.80% in 2003. Uninsured 7% current interest serial bonds maturing in 1995 are priced to yield 7.10%. There are $25.2 million of 7 3/4% term bonds priced at 99 1/2 to yield 7.80% in 2008 and $65 million of 8% term bonds priced at 99 1/2 to yield 8.04% in 2017. The issue has been rated double-A by Moody's, S&P and Fitch's Investors Service, except for bonds due in 2000 and 2008, which are insured and triple-A-rated.

California Public Works Board -- $165.1 million of lease revenue bonds, 1990 Series A, priced by a Morgan Stanley & Co. group to yield from 6.00% in 1991 to 7.55% in 2010. The issue has been rated double-A by S&P and Fitch's and conditional double-A by Moody's. Term bonds have a 7% coupon and are priced to 7.55% to 2010.

Clark County School District, Nev. -- $120 million of school improvement limited tax revenue bonds, via underwriters led by First Boston Corp. Yields were tentatively set at 6.10% in 1991 to 7.70% in 2010. The First Boston group won the $47 million of Group 3 bonds, which comprises two term bond maturities, priced to yield 7.65% in 2007 and 7.70% in 2010. A Lehman Brothers group won $40 million of Group 1 bonds, which were priced to yield from 6.10% in 1991 to 7.25% in 2000. A Prudential-Bache Capital Funding group won the $33 million of Group 2 bonds, which were priced to yield from 7.20% in 2001 to 7.55% in 2005. Group 1 and 2 bonds are insured and rated triple-A by Moody's and S&P. The Group 3 term bonds have been rated single-A-1 by Moody's and single-A-plus by S&P.

MORTGAGES

Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $300 million of Remic mortgage securities offered in eight classes by Nomura Securities International. The offering, Series FHLMC Gold 1011, is backed by Freddie Mac 9 1/2% gold participation certificates. Among fixed-rate classes for which details were available, spreads were 80 basis points over the Treasury's when-issued two-year note for securities with an average life of 2.44 years, 103 basis points over five-year Treasurys on those with an average life of 5.778 years and 103 basis points over seven-year Treasurys for the class with an average life of 7 3/4 years. Class D floats at 75 basis points over the one-month London Interbank offered rate, and Class F floats at 90 basis points over one-month Libor. Both those classes have an 11.50% cap. The collateral has a weighted average coupon rate of 10.125% and weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29.17 years.

Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $300 million of Remic mortgage securities offered in 12 classes by Prudential-Bache Securities Inc. The offering, Series FHLMC Gold 1012, is backed by Freddie Mac 8% gold participation certificates with a weighted average coupon rate of 9.05% and weighted average remaining term to maturity of 25.58 years. Price and yield data weren't immediately available.

EUROBONDS

IBM International Finance N.V. (U.S. parent) -- 150 billion lira of 12 3/4% Eurobonds due Oct. 31, 1994, issue price 101 1/2 via Banca Commerciale Italiana. Fees 1 5/8.

Ashikaga Bank Ltd. (Japan) -- $100 million of floating-rate loan participation certificates due September 2000 at par through Nikko Securities Co. (Deutscheland) G.m.b.H. via Nikko Securities Co. (Europe). Coupon at Libor plus 35 basis points to 1995, three-month Libor plus 45 basis points to 2000. Callable from 1995 at par. Fees 0.30.

BHH International Finance PLC (U.K.) -- #90 million of floating-rate notes due Oct. 5, 1995, at par via Paribas Capital Markets Ltd. Coupon 37.5 basis points above three-month Libor. Notes backed by commercial mortgages; rating enhanced to triple-A by guarantee on principal and interest from Financial Security Assurance and rights to a pool of commercial property. Fees undisclosed.

Shimano Industrial Co. (Japan) -- $200 million of Eurobonds due Oct. 12, 1994, with equity-purchase warrants, indicating a 5 1/8% coupon at par, via Nikko Securities Co. Each $10,000 bond carries two warrants, exercisable from Oct. 26, 1990, through Sept. 30, 1994, to buy shares at an expected 2 1/2% premium to the closing price when terms are fixed Monday. Fees 2 1/4.
