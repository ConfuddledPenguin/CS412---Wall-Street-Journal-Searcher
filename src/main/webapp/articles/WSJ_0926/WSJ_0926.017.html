Marketing & Media: Maxwell Venture Fund Sets Sights on Eastern Europe

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/26/90

WASHINGTON -- The British publisher Robert Maxwell said he is organizing a $250 million venture-capital fund to make equity and related investments in emerging businesses in Eastern Europe. Mr. Maxwell, chairman and chief executive officer of Maxwell Communication Corp., said that he will commit $52 million to the fund, and that New York-based Merrill Lynch & Co., which is handling the private placement, will commit $5 million.

Mr. Maxwell said he is having "serious discussions" about possible investments in Eastern Europe. In Moscow, for example, he said, "We have been offered a major building that needs completing" for use by Western companies that can pay rent in foreign currency. And he said, "Newsprint mills in certain European countries need modernization, and are willing to pay in newsprint. There's clearly a demand for newsprint."

Mr. Maxwell said he will be the general partner of the fund, which will provide not only investments but "know-how" to Eastern European ventures in which the fund takes a minority stake. He said he hopes the fund will "get off the ground in the next few weeks."

He called the $250 million sum "a beginning. Obviously, if we are successful, it could go substantially bigger. They in Eastern Europe need to privatize all their firms in order to join the free-market economy."
