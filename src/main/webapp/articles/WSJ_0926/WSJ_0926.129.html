D&B Alleges 2 Firms, Ex-Aide, Violated Database Copyright

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/26/90

NEW YORK -- Dun & Bradstreet Corp. charged a former employee and two Chicago companies with copyright infringement and unfair competition in business information services. The business information company, in a lawsuit filed in U.S. District Court in Chicago, charges that International Business Lists Inc., Inter-Direct Inc. and Gary E. Walter improperly copied a database of U.S. businesses and their executives compiled by D&B and sold it to their customers.

The suit also charges that Mr. Walter breached an agreement not to compete with D&B for two years after he left the company. The suit says Mr. Walter left D&B around Sept. 1, 1988.

D&B's lawsuit seeks to stop the defendants from marketing the data, and it asks for unspecified damages. Mr. Walter, president of International Business Lists, called D&B's charges "absolutely untrue." Inter-Direct is the former name of International Business Lists, he said.
