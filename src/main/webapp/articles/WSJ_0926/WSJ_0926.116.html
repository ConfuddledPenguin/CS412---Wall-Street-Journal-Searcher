Cummins to Report Third-Quarter Loss With Sales Sluggish

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/26/90

COLUMBUS, Ind. -- Cummins Engine Co. said it expects to report a loss for the third quarter as a result of slack demand, even after a one-time gain of up to $34 million from the repurchase of debt securities. The diesel-engine maker said that, excluding the gain, its third-quarter loss will exceed the $39.7 million loss reported in last year's third quarter. Slow North American demand for heavy truck engines and related parts is expected to continue through the fourth quarter, leaving the company with a loss for that period as well, Cummins said.

Cummins said that it will report a $12 million extraordinary gain in the third quarter from the repurchase of $200 million principal amount of Liquid Yield Option Notes, or Lyons, from Merrill Lynch, Pierce, Fenner and Smith Inc. in a private transaction. The securities trade at a deep discount to their face value and pay interest at maturity.

The company said an additional one-time gain in the third quarter of up to $22 million will be realized if all of the $375 million principal amount of its remaining Lyon securities outstanding are tendered in response to an offer beginning today.

Cummins said it intends to file an offering of as much as $150 million in medium-term notes to fund the repurchase of the Lyon securities. Also helping fund the repurchase will be a private placement of $74 million in tax-advantaged notes by Cummins's employee stock ownership plan. The private placement proceeds will be used to repay a promissory note the ESOP issued to the company in July 1989, Cummins said.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Cummins shares rose $1.25 to close at $36.25.
