Business Brief -- Telephone & Data Systems Inc. Firm Acts to Swap Interests For Shares of U.S. Cellular

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/26/90

Telephone & Data Systems Inc. offered to swap its interests in certain rural cellular service areas for 12.5 million shares of U.S. Cellular Corp.'s common. If approved, the exchange would boost TDS's stake in U.S. Cellular's common stock to 87.1%, from 81.5%. U.S. Cellular said it hasn't yet reviewed the proposal.

The proposal calls for TDS, a Chicago concern, to transfer its service-area interests to U.S. Cellular in return for 3.5 million common shares and nine million Series A common shares. TDS, a communications service company, would also get paid for related investments and expenditures.

U.S. Cellular, a Chicago-based cellular telephone systems operator, said an independent committee of its board will study the offer and respond. The proposal, subject to documentation and regulatory requirements, needs the approval of the committee and U.S. Cellular shareholders.
