Credit Ratings: Moody's Downgrades Its Ratings on Debt Of Chase Manhattan

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/26/90

NEW YORK -- Chase Manhattan Corp.'s big restructuring isn't allaying the fears of one rating concern that harder times are ahead for the nation's second-largest banking concern. Moody's Investors Service Inc. yesterday downgraded its ratings on Chase's long-term debt and commercial paper. Moody's cited the continued growth in Chase's nonperforming real estate loans, the deteriorating Northeast economy and the bank's exposure to other risky loans.

But Chase shares rose 87.5 cents, to close at $12, in New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday. Volume of more than 1.7 million shares made Chase the fourth most-active issue on the Big Board.

Last Friday, Chase said it will fire 5,000 employees, provide an additional $650 million for possible loan losses, and slash its quarterly dividend to 30 cents a share from 62 cents. Moody's said that these actions "are positive steps," but that it remains concerned about Chase's exposure to commercial real estate, less-developed countries and highly leveraged transactions.

Moody's said the funding and liquidity positions of both the holding company and its Chase Manhattan Bank unit remain sound. It also said Chase's credit standing is supported by management's capital-building efforts and the company's significant undervalued assets.

Chase said it was disappointed by Moody's actions. "Even after the aggressive action Chase took on Sept. 21, our capital is in excess of $11 billion and our capital ratios exceed the government's guidelines that take effect in 1992," the bank said in a statement. The bank also noted that as part of its restructuring announcement last Friday, it indicated it expects fourth-quarter earnings to be "in excess of $140 million." Chase said earlier that as a result of the restructuring program, it expects to report a third-quarter pretax loss of about $625 million.

Moody's lowered its ratings on Chase Manhattan's senior debt to Baa-3 from Baa-2, subordinated debt to Ba-2 from Baa3; preferred stock to Ba-2 from Baa-3, and commercial paper to prime-3 from prime2.
