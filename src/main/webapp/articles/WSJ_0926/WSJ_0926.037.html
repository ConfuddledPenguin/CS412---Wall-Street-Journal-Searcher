Amoco, Occidental Petroleum Settle Antitrust Lawsuits

  • Author: Caleb Solomon
  • Date: 09/26/90

Amoco Corp. and Occidental Petroleum Co. said they tentatively settled long-simmering antitrust suits alleging they fixed natural-gas prices in the Midwest in the early 1980s. A spokesman for Amoco in Chicago said, "Details of the settlement are being held confidential pending final documentation and approval by the court." An Occidental spokesman in Los Angeles also declined to provide details on the size of the settlement but said it "will have no materially adverse impact on Occidental's consolidated financial position."

In a recent Securities and Exchange Commission filing, Amoco indicated that a series of claims arising out of the same charges totaled about $5.6 billion against all defendants. However, in the filing, Amoco called the claims "grossly inflated" and predicted that the case wouldn't have a material impact on it, either.

The settlement was reached yesterday just as the trial was set to begin in federal district court in Kansas City, Kan. A third defendant, the pipeline company Williams Cos. of Tulsa, Okla., hasn't settled and trial is scheduled to begin today, said Neil Woerman, chief of staff for the Kansas attorney general, a plaintiff in the suit.

Late yesterday Williams couldn't be reached for comment.

Mr. Woerman also declined to disclose the size of the settlements. He said the court has asked that settlement documents be filed Friday, but isn't certain lawyers will meet the deadline.

The suits were filed by attorneys general in Kansas and Missouri as well as retail utilities in the states. The suits alleged that units of Amoco, Occidental and Williams used a 1980 change in natural-gas pricing rules to amend contracts to sell and supply gas to those utilities that doubled the price.
