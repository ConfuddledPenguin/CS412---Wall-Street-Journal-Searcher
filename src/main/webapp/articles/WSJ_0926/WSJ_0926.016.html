HONDA MOTOR Co. will make driver- and passenger-side air bags standard on all cars sold in the U.S. by late 1993. In yesterday's edition, a company spokesman incorrectly stated that the stripped-down versions of some models might not have standard air bags. (See: "Autos: Honda Follows Lead of U.S. on Air Bags" -- WSJ Sept. 25, 1990)

---

AIRCO GASES said a recent surcharge on industrial gas products will affect about 150,000 customers. A figure provided by the company on the number of customers was incorrect in last Wednesday's edition.

(See: "BOC's Airco to Raise Industrial Gas Prices" -- WSJ Sept. 19, 1990)
