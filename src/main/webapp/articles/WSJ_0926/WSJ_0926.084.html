Who's News: USX Names T.C. Graham Vice Chairman

  • Author: Clare Ansberry
  • Date: 09/26/90

PITTSBURGH -- USX Corp., creating a clear pattern of succession in its top operations, named Thomas C. Graham to the newly created position of vice chairman, steel and diversified group. The 63-year-old Mr. Graham had been president of the company's USS unit, overseeing the nation's largest steel operations. Succeeding him in that post is Thomas J. Usher, 48, who had been executive vice president, heavy products.

The steel and energy concern also named Robert M. Hernandez, 46, as senior vice president, finance and treasurer. He succeeds William E. Lewellen, 65, who is retiring. The moves take effect Monday.

USX has a mandatory retirement age of 65, and steel-industry observers had been speculating about Mr. Graham's successor for more than a year. Most speculation centered on Mr. Usher; Reuben L. Perin Jr., 52, executive vice president, commercial, sheet and tin; and James T. Bradford Jr., 63, executive vice president, operations, sheet and tin. Mr. Usher has been credited by analysts with shoring up the company's difficult structural, plate and tubular businesses.

Unlike his predecessor, Mr. Usher began his career at USX's steel business. Mr. Graham was recruited in 1983 from LTV Corp.'s Jones & Laughlin Steel unit to succeed William R. Roesch, who was ill and died later that year. Mr. Graham, who oversaw major shutdowns at the then-U.S. Steel during the industry's toughest years, is largely credited with making it one of the most profitable large steel makers in the nation. Industry observers have speculated that Mr. Graham might be interested in running USX's steel operations, should they be spun off.

With Mr. Graham's retirement from USX in sight, "it's pretty clear that people have begun to focus more on the need for orderly succession," said steel analyst Charles Bradford. And the fact that Mr. Usher is a steel-operations executive indicates the company wants to continue to "zero in on operations," Mr. Bradford said.

He also said the move puts Mr. Usher in place in time for the coming negotiations with the United Steelworkers of America, noting that Mr. Graham had been involved in shaping previous labor pacts. USX's labor contract expires in January.

Mr. Usher will continue to report to Mr. Graham, who also assumes the supervision of the company's diversified business. Mr. Hernandez had been president of the U.S. diversified group and had reported directly to Chairman Charles A. Corry, 58. Mr. Hernandez, who will be responsible for all corporate financings, domestic-project financings and leasing activities, will now report to W. Bruce Thomas, 63, vice chairman, administration, and chief financial officer.

Charles C. Gedeon, 50, was named president of the U.S. Diversified Group, replacing Mr. Hernandez, and senior vice president, raw materials, of USS. Mr. Gedeon had been senior vice president, related resources, for USS and USX.
