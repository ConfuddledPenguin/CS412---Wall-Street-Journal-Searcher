BOC's Airco to Raise Industrial Gas Prices

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/19/90

MURRAY HILL, N.J. -- Airco Gases is adding a surcharge on all delivered industrial-gas products, affecting as many as 10,000 customers. Airco, a U.S. unit of BOC Group of Britain, also expects it will have to boost list prices on all industrial-gas products between 6% and 10% before year's end. A spokeswoman attributed the added charges to increases in oil prices and their impact on distribution costs.

Merchant liquid oxygen and nitrogen consumers will pay a surcharge of three-quarters of one cent per 100 cubic feet for large-tank deliveries and as much as two cents for lower-volume customers. The charge on argon and hydrogen supply will range from two to five cents per 100 cubic feet.

---

Corrections & Amplifications

AIRCO GASES said a recent surcharge on industrial gas products will affect about 150,000 customers. A figure provided by the company on the number of customers was incorrect in last Wednesday's edition.

(WSJ Sept. 26, 1990)
