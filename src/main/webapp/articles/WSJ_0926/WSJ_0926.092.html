ENVIROSOURCE Inc. (Stamford, Conn.) -- Moody's Investors Service Inc. said it lowered its ratings on long-term debt and preferred stock of this environmental services company, citing the company's weak operating performance. Moody's dropped the ratings on the company's 14% extendible reset notes due 1998, and its class G $7.25 cumulative convertible preferred, to Caa from single-B-3. About $160 million of long-term debt is affected. ---

SUPERMARKETS GENERAL HOLDINGS Corp. (Woodbridge, N.J.) -- Standard & Poor's Corp. said it lowered its ratings on about $700 million of subordinated debt of the grocery store and retailing concern to triple-C-plus from single-B-minus. S&P said the downgrade reflects "lower-than-anticipated sales and subsequent weak cash generating ability." S&P said the company, which was taken private in a management buy-out in 1987, "continues to be highly leveraged, and interest expense places a substantial call on cash flow."

UJB FINANCIAL Corp.'s rating on its preferred stock was cut to Ba-2 from Baa-3 and its commercial paper to not prime from prime-3 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. The credit-rating company's downgrading affects $250 million of securities for the Princeton, N.J., holding parent of United Jersey Bank. The rating action reflects "continuing deterioration in the credit quality of UJB's real estate loan portfolio, which will likely require substantially higher levels of reserves, and lead to more significant charge-offs," Moody's said.
