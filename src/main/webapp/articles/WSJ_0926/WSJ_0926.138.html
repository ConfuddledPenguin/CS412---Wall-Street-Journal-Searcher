Business Brief -- Aluminum Co. of America: Details of a Joint Venture With Kobe Steel Disclosed

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/26/90

Aluminum Co. of America, Pittsburgh, disclosed details of a joint venture with Kobe Steel Ltd. of Japan to produce aluminum can stock, and said the companies will explore broader cooperation, such as automotive applications. Alcoa and Kobe will make an initial equity investment of 10 billion yen ($72.8 million) in a new plant in Japan to produce and market aluminum can stock, mainly for the Japanese and Asian beverage container markets; the total investment, including debt, wasn't disclosed. The 50-50 venture, KSL Alcoa Aluminum Co., is to begin production in 1993, with annual capacity of 180,000 metric tons; each ton is equal to about 2,204.62 pounds. The companies also will study forming a venture to produce aluminum products for the automotive industry in Japan or the U.S. or both, and explore technical exchanges in areas such as recycling.

For Alcoa, the region offers growth, as only 35% of cans in Japan are made of aluminum, compared with 95% in the U.S. Kobe, which imports aluminum ingot from various suppliers, said it will get a stable supply from Alcoa.
