Business Brief -- Gentex Corp.: Accord Is Set to Resolve Patent Dispute on Mirror

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/26/90

Gentex Corp. of Zeeland, Mich., and Donnelly Corp. of Holland, Mich., said they agreed in principle to resolve a patent dispute about an automatically dimming rearview mirror. On May 10, Gentex sued Donnelly, claiming it infringed upon the patent for the mirror sold by Gentex, and Donnelly also filed suit, seeking a judgment that Donnelly's mirror system didn't infringe upon Gentex's U.S. patent. In the first half, Gentex sold 137,000 of the mirrors, which automatically dim to eliminate headlight glare, up from 93,000 a year earlier.

Under the agreement, Gentex would become a 50% shareholder of Donnelly's Irish unit and Donnelly would receive newly issued Gentex common shares to equal about a 12% stake, and Donnelly would retain operating control of the unit. The accord also would give Donnelly the right to buy additional Gentex shares in the market to lift its stake 35%. If these shares can't be acquired in the market, Donnelly would have to option to buy enough shares from Gentex, at $11.625 each, to reach a holding of about 30%.
