Jury Convicts CBN Worker For Messages Sent on Cable

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 09/26/90

A federal jury in Norfolk, Va., convicted a Christian Broadcasting Network Inc. technician on two counts for cutting into a Playboy Channel movie with a Biblical message to repent. A CBN spokeswoman said that the Federal Communications Commission failed to establish conclusively that Thomas M. Haynie had interrupted the Playboy Channel transmission and that Mr. Haynie intends to appeal. Neither Mr. Haynie nor his attorneys were available for comment. The case was handled by the FCC and the Justice Department.

Mr. Haynie was indicted in April on six counts alleging that he sent transmissions over CBN equipment that interrupted both Playboy Channel and American Exxxtasy programming on Sept. 6, 1987. The message to viewers: "Thus sayeth the Lord thy God. Remember the Sabath (sic) and keep it holy. Repent for the kingdom of heaven is at hand."

The count of intentionally interfering with a communications satellite, a felony, carries a maximum sentence of 10 years in prison and a $250,000 fine. Unauthorized use of a satellite uplink transmitter is a misdemeanor with a maximum sentence of a year in jail and a $100,000 fine.
