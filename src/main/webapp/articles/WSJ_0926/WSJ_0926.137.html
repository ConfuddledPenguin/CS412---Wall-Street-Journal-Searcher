UPS and Yamato Form Joint Venture In Air Cargo Trade

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/26/90

GREENWICH, Conn. -- United Parcel Service of America Inc. and Yamato Transport Co. of Japan, eager for a bigger share of the booming air cargo trade between the U.S. and Japan, have formed a joint venture. Closely held UPS, the world's largest package delivery company, and Yamato, Japan's largest, named the venture Unistar Air Cargo. A UPS official confirmed that the new venture would be capitalized at more than one billion yen ($7 million), but wouldn't disclose the exact figures.

UPS and Yamato have been cooperating on small package delivery services since 1986, and UPS officials said the new venture expands current ties. Unistar allows the two companies to provide door-to-door package service from anywhere in the two countries. Analysts called the combinaton logical, as UPS gears up to start direct air cargo flights to Japan sometime after October. "UPS continues to chip away, to expand a little bit at a time," says David M. Guthrie, an analyst at Morgan Keegan in Memphis. "They should be a strong competitor."

Unistar will have its U.S. headquarters in Chicago, with branches in New York, Atlanta, Los Angeles, San Francisco and Anchorage, Alaska. In Japan, its operations will be based in Tokyo. Officials said they plan to add 15 American branches in early 1991.

Analysts said since UPS's ties with Yamato are widely known in the industry, it would not substantially alter the existing competitve mix. Nonetheless, the intensely competitive international air cargo market, which generates $8 billion a year, is not expected to cool down anytime soon. And Japan is the fastest-growing area.

Currently, Federal Express Corp. is the only all-cargo carrier offering scheduled services to Japan. But UPS, which won the right to fly five times a week to Tokyo through negotiations between the U.S. and Japanese governments last November, will start competing soon with Federal Express for air freight forwarding business.

And in May, Japan Air Lines, West Germany's Lufthansa airline and the Japanese trading company, Nissho Iwai Corp, joined the fray when they bought a stake in DHL Worldwide Express.

UPS, which had revenue of more than $12 billion in 1989, delivered more than 2.8 billion packages and documents in all 50 U.S. states and in more than 180 countries. Yamato, one of Japan's oldest transportation companies, had revenue of $2.4 billion in 1989.
