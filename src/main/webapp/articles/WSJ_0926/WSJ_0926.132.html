International Brief -- Total-Cie. Francaise des Petroles: First-Half Profit Fell 33% Accounting Change Is Cited

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/26/90

The French energy group Total-Cie. Francaise des Petroles (CFP) reported that its consolidated profit for the first half of 1990 fell 33% to 1.27 billion francs ($244 million) on an attributable basis, compared with 1.91 billion francs a year earlier. Total-CFP noted, however, that the latest results were largely influenced by regular accounting adjustments in the value of its petroleum inventories, which lopped 1.4 billion francs off its earnings in the first half after having added 1.7 billion francs a year earlier. These latest adjustments, which reflect changes in the market price of oil, are expected to be offset during the second half of the year by price rises tied to the Persian Gulf crisis.

Total said its first-half consolidated revenue was 53.13 billion francs, up from 51.13 billion a year earlier.
