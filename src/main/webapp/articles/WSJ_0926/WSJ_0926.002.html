Bank-Broker CD Yields Climbed in Latest Week

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/26/90

NEW YORK -- Yields on certificates of deposit at major banks and brokerage firms rose in the week ended Tuesday. Average yields on small-denomination CDs sold directly by banks edged up slightly. The average yield on a six-month CD rose to 7.39% from 7.37%, according to an 18-bank national survey by Banxquote Money Markets, a New York information service. Meanwhile, the average yield on a five-year consumer CD remained at 7.91%.

Up significantly more were average yields on small-denomination CDs sold by leading broker dealers. The average yield on a six-month broker-sold CD rose to 7.92% from 7.75%, according to Banxquote. The average yield for a one-year broker-sold CD rose to 8.02% from 7.74%, while the average for five-year maturities rose to 8.70% from 8.35%.

Meanwhile, average yields for large-denomination CDs, which typically require deposits of $90,000 or more, were also up. The average yield on a one-year "jumbo" CD rose to 7.83% from 7.73%, while the average for a five-year maturity rose to 8.31% from 8.27%.
