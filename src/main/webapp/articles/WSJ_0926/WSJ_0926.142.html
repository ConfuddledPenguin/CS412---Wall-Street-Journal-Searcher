Business Brief -- Ford Motor Co.: Auto Company Is Recalling Vans for Seat-Latch Work

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/26/90

Ford Motor Co., Dearborn, Mich., said it is recalling approximately 14,500 of its 1989-90 Aerostar minivans equipped with "Quad" captain's chairs to replace latch assemblies in the second-row, right-hand bucket seat. The company said it is concerned that these seats, which are designed to tilt forward to permit access to third-row seats, may unlatch during severe frontal impacts. However, the company said it is unaware of any accident in which the seat has unlatched. In a second action, Ford is notifying owners of a voluntary safety recall of approximately 18,000 of its 1989-90 L-Series heavy trucks to replace an air-brake check valve that may malfunction and not close as intended. Ford said it was unaware of any accidents stemming from this valve malfunctioning.

Owners will be asked by mail to return the vehicles to local dealers to have the work performed at no charge.
