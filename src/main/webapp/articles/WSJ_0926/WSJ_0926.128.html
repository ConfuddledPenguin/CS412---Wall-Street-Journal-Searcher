International Brief -- Pirelli S.p.A.: Earnings in the First Half Fell 9.9% to $117.6 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/26/90

Pirelli S.p.A. said consolidated profit fell 9.9% to 137 billion lire ($117.6 million) in the first half from 152 billion lire in the 1989 period. The key holding company of the Italian tires and cables group predicted a similar decline in earnings for all of 1990 compared with the 320 billion lire earned in 1989.

Pirelli blamed the earnings drop in the year's opening half on the 61% decline in profit at its Netherlands tire division, Pirelli Tyre Holding N.V.

Consolidated revenue was flat at 5.272 trillion lire, while gross earnings edged down to 280 billion lire from 285 billion lire.

Pirelli said that barring a "further deterioration of the international situation" it expects 1990 revenue to be little changed from last year's 10.342 trillion lire.

The company didn't make any reference to its hostile bid last week for Continental AG of West Germany. Continental on Monday rejected Pirelli's offer to merge its tire division with Continental and take control of the West German tire maker.

Pirelli hasn't commented on Continental's decision not to accept the offer.
