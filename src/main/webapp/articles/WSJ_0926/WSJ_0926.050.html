Keating's Petition For Review of Bail Rejected by Court

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/26/90

LOS ANGELES -- A California appeals court declined to review Charles H. Keating Jr.'s $5 million bail, leaving the indicted former American Continental Corp. chairman to begin a second week in jail. Chicago attorney Stephen C. Neal, who maintains the 66-year-old Mr. Keating is deeply in debt and unable to raise such an amount, could appeal next to the state supreme court, although he declined to comment yesterday on what his next legal step.

Mr. Keating and three other officers of either Phoenix, Ariz.-based American Continental or its Lincoln Savings unit in California were charged by a state grand jury with defrauding California purchasers of about $250 million of uninsured American Continental bonds by making false claims about the safety of the securities. They surrendered early last week and are scheduled for a formal arraignment on Oct. 5.

The co-defendents returned home last weekend after successfully petitioning for reductions from the $1 million bail originally set for them. At the same time, however, the state Judge Gary Klausner refused to reconsider Mr. Keating's $5 million bail, and said he believed the Arizona businessman might have reasons to flee from the criminal prosecution, and other legal and financial problems.

Mr. Neal criticized as "wholely inappropriate and inept" remarks on Friday by Los Angeles County District Attorney Ira Reiner, who said Mr. Keating's mug shot "ought to be posted in every boardroom in the United States," and compared the Keating case to that of fugitive financier Robert Vesco.

Mr. Keating's attorneys maintained in their petition to the appeals court that Mr. Keating is "unlawfully imprisoned" because he doesn't present a flight risk and has bail "50 times higher than bail set for other defendants faced with identical charges." Mr. Neal had told Judge Klausner that Mr. Keating is interested only in defending himself against the charges, but that he "can't prepare a defense from L.A. County Jail. It is impossible."
