SCEcorp Unit Costs Disallowed by Agency

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/26/90

SAN FRANCISCO -- The California Public Utilities Commission disallowed $48.3 million of expenses incurred by Southern California Edison Co. from 1985 through 1987 under a power-purchase contract with the Kern River Cogeneration Co. Southern California Edison, a unit of SCEcorp in Rosemead, Calif., "negotiated a contract that was too favorable to Kern River Cogeneration and much less favorable" to Edison's customers, said Commissioner Stanley Hulett. Kern River Cogeneration is 50%-owned by an SCEcorp affiliate and 50% by a Texaco Inc. affiliate.

Edison said it would appeal the decision, which it called "extremely unfair and totally unjustified." Edison said it presented testimony to the commission last year showing that the contract is more beneficial to Edison's customers than purchases mandated under standard contracts authorized by the commission.

Edison said it was pleased that the commission "recognized (the company) has not engaged in self-dealing with its non-utility affiliate as claimed by the Division of Ratepayer Advocates," an independent arm of the commission that represents consumers.

The commission ordered SCEcorp to limit future transactions with its affiliates to "standard contracts," or to seek prior approval of any non-standard contracts.
