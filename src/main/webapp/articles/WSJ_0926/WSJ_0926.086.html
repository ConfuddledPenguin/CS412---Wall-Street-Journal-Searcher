International: De Benedetti Firm to Shed Third of Stake in Generale

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/26/90

PARIS -- Carlo De Benedetti's French holding company, Cerus S.A., agreed to shed about a third of its 14.4% stake in Societe Generale de Belgique S.A. The move, in which 4.8% of the company will be sold, will leave Cerus with less than 10% of Generale's shares, a reminder of its failed 1988 takeover bid for the Belgian holding company.

Cerus will exchange the shares for stock in Cie. Financiere de Suez, the French banking group that came to Generale's rescue against Mr. De Benedetti, an Italian financier, two years ago. Cerus also will acquire small stakes in some Suez allies as part of the exchange, Paris financial sources said.

Mr. De Benedetti won't take a cash loss, even though the shares are down almost 40% from what he paid. Yesterday, on the Brussels Bourse, Generale shares fell 50 Belgian francs ($1.56) to 2,125 francs ($66.40). In 1988, Cerus paid an average of 3,500 francs a share.

Detailed terms of the swap weren't available. But at current share prices, a 5% stake in Generale is valued at about 6.8 billion francs.

Today's agreement will leave Mr. De Benedetti holding Generale paper which he will still want to sell, analysts said.

Before the transaction, Cerus had slightly under 3% in Suez, while Suez had about 4% in Cerus and a further 10% in Mr. De Benedetti's Italian parent company, Cofide.

Cerus will stop consolidating Generale's earnings in its group accounts and will cause an earnings drop.

Last year, Generale accounted for almost one-third of Cerus's 1.24 billion French franc ($238 million) net profit.
