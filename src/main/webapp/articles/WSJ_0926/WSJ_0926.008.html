Technology: Surgeon General Reports On Benefits of Not Smoking

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/26/90

WASHINGTON -- Smokers of all ages experience immediate and substantial health benefits when they quit, the U.S. surgeon general reported in her latest report on smoking and health. Twenty previous reports from the Public Health Service have focused on the dangers of smoking, but Surgeon General Antonia Novello said she decided to focus on the benefits of giving up smoking in order to stress the point that "it's never too late to quit smoking."

"Persons who quit smoking before age 50 have one-half the risk of dying in the next 15 years compared with continuing smokers," Dr. Novello said. The risk of coronary heart disease, the leading cause of death in the U.S., falls by half within one year of the cessation of smoking, according to the report.

Many smokers fear that they'll gain weight if they quit. The report said, however, that weight gain averages only five pounds, and that the risk from the additional weight is far less than that of continuing to smoke.
