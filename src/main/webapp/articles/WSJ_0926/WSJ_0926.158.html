Timken to Upgrade Alloy Steel Plants At $170 Million Cost

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/26/90

CANTON, Ohio -- Timken Co., a bearings and steel producer, said it will invest $170 million to improve its Canton alloy steel production plants. The project is part of a $1 billion capital spending program that Timken launched last year. The company has detailed projects accounting for about one-third of the money, including $210 million for its alloy and specialty steels plants.

Timken said it will replace a bloom continuous caster at its Harrison Steel plant here with new casting equipment. Also, it will improve the small bar rolling mill and bar finishing operations at the plant. Investment in the Harrison facility, which makes alloy steels, including some used in Timken's bearings, will total $110 million, Timken said.

The company said it will spend $32 million at the adjacent five-year-old Faircrest Steel Plant to improve productivity and product quality. And it will spend $28 million to upgrade computer systems that serve both facilities.

Rust International Corp. was named project manager for the work, Timken said. Completion dates for the improvements range from mid-1992 through mid-1993.

Those programs, along with a previously announced $40 million investment at Timken's Latrobe specialty steel unit, make up the bulk of the planned spending for steel plant improvements, Timken said. When completed, they will make Timken competitive globally in alloy and specialty steels, both in quality and cost, the company said.

The largest part of Timken's $1 billion investment will be for bearings plants, but the major bearings projects haven't been announced. The company has announced plans for building a $23 million bearings plant in Altavista, Va., and an investment of $13 million to modernize production of large bearings.
