What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/26/90

ECONOMIC GROWTH SLOWED to a meager 0.4% in the second quarter, revised data show, well below the 1.2% the government estimated earlier. The White House continued to insist that the economy isn't in a recession, even though the revised figures don't reflect recent soaring oil prices. ---

FDIC Chairman Seidman said he favors scaling back the agency's insurance coverage for billions of dollars of pension funds held in banks.

---

MCA stock rose $19.50 to $54 a share on news that the company has been in talks that could lead to its acquisition by Matsushita, the Japanese consumer-electronics giant. People close to the talks say the takeover price could be $80 to $90 a share, or $6.7 billion to $7.5 billion.

The television networks hope Matshushita's talks with MCA will help ease regulations that currently bar them from the syndication market.

---

Bush urged world leaders to ensure the success of trade talks, saying they are threatened by an impasse over agricultural subsidies. Earlier, EC farm ministers backed a subsidy reform plan unacceptable to the U.S.

---

Milken's lawyers asked a judge to sentence the former Drexel junk-bond king to community service. Prosecutors prepared to release new allegations painting Milken as a major white-collar criminal.

---

Fuel-economy legislation was doomed for the year when lawmakers lost a Senate procedural vote.

---

The administration is split over an Energy Department proposal to sell a half million barrels of oil a day from the Strategic Petroleum Reserve.

---

Car and truck sales slipped 5.7% in mid-September, a surprisingly strong performance in light of soaring gasoline prices and a slowing economy.

---

Stocks rebounded as oil prices fell. The Dow Jones industrials advanced 32.67 points to 2485.64. Bonds rose.

---

Bush appeared to be losing support from House Republican Leader Michel for a cut in capital-gains taxes, a priority in the budget talks.

---

Citibank plans to lay off 100 workers in its leveraged-lending unit, a senior bank official said.

---

CS First Boston is quietly preparing to package $1.1 billion in troubled "bridge" loans and sell them.

---

Elders IXL Ltd. posted a $1.08 billion fiscal-year loss, biggest in Australian corporate history.

---

Neil Bush should have told more about his ties to a business partner for whom he tried to arrange a line of credit, the former chief lending officer at Silverado S&L testified.

---

Shearson Lehman must pay $800,000 in punitive damages to a South Dakota investor, a jury decided.

---

Reebok said it expects earnings to be lower for the third quarter and flat for all 1990, despite strong sales for its inflatable athletic shoe, the Pump.

---

Donald Trump's bankers are expected today to demand that he slash salaries and institute layoffs at the Trump Shuttle in return for maintaining control of the troubled airline.
