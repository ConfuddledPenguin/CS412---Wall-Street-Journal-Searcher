Business Brief -- Sears, Roebuck & Co.: Retailer Fires 213 Workers At An Unprofitable Division

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/26/90

Sears, Roebuck & Co., Chicago, fired 213 employees at its unprofitable catalog unit as part of a broad attempt to cut costs and improve retailing profits. The latest cuts represent 20% of the catalog unit's headquarters staff, which is shrinking to 821 people, from about 1,700 in January 1989. The catalog unit, with annual sales of about $4 billion, hasn't made money since 1985. Sears predicts it will be profitable next year.

Sears also may reduce the number of employees staffing catalog desks at its 850 stores and its free-standing catalog offices. The company currently is installing a new computer system that it expects will reduce the time required to handle merchandise returns by 35% to 40%. That system, now in operation at 200 catalog locations, could result in further staff reductions, a spokesman acknowledged.
