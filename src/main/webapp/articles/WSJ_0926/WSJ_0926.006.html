EC Aide Issues Warnings On Increases in Oil Prices

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/26/90

BRUSSELS -- The European Community's energy commissioner, Antonio Cardoso e Cunha, warned against further rises in oil prices and called the recent surge "totally unjustified and indefensible." Mr. Cardoso e Cunha accused traders of speculating "at the expense of the consumers." He said oil stocks remain at unusually high levels, despite the Persian Gulf crisis, a fact known by "everyone on the market."

He also disputed the perception that a Gulf war would result in oil shortages. "The speculators should remind themselves that both countries continued to produce and export oil throughout the Iran-Iraq war, despite repeated attacks on their oil facilities," he said, and predicted that markets would soon realize this, leaving companies and traders holding high-priced oil to face big losses. "They will deserve no public sympathy," he said. "But in the meantime, we are all suffering from their misguided actions."

The International Energy Agency on Friday is to discuss oil stocks, but the EC commission isn't viewed as likely to recommend releasing strategic stocks to push prices down.
