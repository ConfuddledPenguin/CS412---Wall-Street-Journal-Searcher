Marketing & Media: Paramount Names Kirkpatrick to Post At Production Unit

  • Author: Laura Landro
  • Date: 09/26/90

Paramount Pictures Corp., in a move that has been rumored for weeks, named David Kirkpatrick, 39 years old, as the president of its motion picture group's production operations. He succeeds Sidney Ganis, 50, who entered into an exclusive long-term agreement yesterday to develop and produce motion pictures for Paramount.

Mr. Kirkpatrick left Walt Disney Co. in July to become an executive vice president of the motion picture group at Paramount, where he had previously worked. Prior to his tenure of more than a year at Disney, Mr. Kirkpatrick had left Paramount to be president of the motion picture group of Weintraub Entertainment Group for two years.

Mr. Ganis and Mr. Kirkpatrick couldn't be reached for comment. Frank Mancuso, the chairman of Paramount, a unit of Paramount Communications Inc., also couldn't be reached. Mr. Ganis's independent production agreement is a typical arrangement when a top executive is replaced at a movie company.

The change is widely viewed in Hollywood as a move by Paramount to bolster its future performance at the box office and to allay concerns that it is too heavily focused on marketing and distribution and not enough on creative development, production and talent relationships. Although Paramount has the current box office hit, "Ghost," it has been disappointed by the results of other recent films such as "Days of Thunder," and "The Two Jakes."

Mr. Ganis joined Paramount in 1986 as head of marketing and was named co-president of the motion picture group in 1988, responsible for production. The move raised eyebrows at the time because Mr. Ganis had no previous experience in the creative side of movie production, and didn't bring any strong relationships with talent to the job.

Although Mr. Ganis is widely liked and considered a talented marketing strategist, he was unable to develop the combination of talent relationships and tough negotiation skills necessary to succeed at overseeing the studio's annual production slate and dealing with often-difficult negotiations over movie production issues, Hollywood executives said.

Mr. Kirkpatrick has never run a big studio's movie operations either, but he has spent most of his career on the production side of the business, and is known for cultivating good talent relationships with producers, directors, writers and actors. For example, he has a strong relationship with Eddie Murphy, who is in the midst of renegotiating his exclusive contract with Paramount, and one of the reasons he was brought back was to help repair the rifts in that relationship.

"David has great expertise in talent relationships, and he has strong creative production capabilities," said Jerry Bruckheimer, who along with partner Don Simpson has an exclusive production agreement at Paramount that has included movies such as "Beverly Hills Cop II," "Top Gun" and "Days of Thunder." Mr. Bruckheimer added, "He's been a great executive to work with and he brings many years of expertise to the job."

Still pending against Mr. Kirkpatrick is a suit filed by Disney charging that when he left Disney for Paramount, he took another executive and a film project with him, in violation of a contract. Mr. Kirkpatrick's attorney said the suit was without merit. Ironically, Paramount sued Weintraub and Mr. Kirkpatrick when he originally left the studio to take the Weintraub post. The suit was finally settled.
