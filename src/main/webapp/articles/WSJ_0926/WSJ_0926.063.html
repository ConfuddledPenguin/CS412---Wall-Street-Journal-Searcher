REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: Cranston's Political Capital

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/26/90

We see where California's Senator Alan Cranston has rushed forward to attract news coverage as the first Member of the Senate to announce his intention to vote against David Souter's Supreme Court nomination; Mr. Souter didn't sign the necessary blood oaths on behalf of abortions. Surely there is something more appropriate Senator Cranston can do with his political capital. Why not help bail out Charlie Keating, the S&L impresario, now languishing in a California jail for want of $5 million bail. In the process of seeking senatorial intervention with the thrift regulators, Mr. Keating contributed nearly $1 million to Mr. Cranston's voter-registration operation. With Mr. Keating now pleading poverty, perhaps Senator Cranston could disgorge some of the earlier distributions to help poor Charlie make bail.
