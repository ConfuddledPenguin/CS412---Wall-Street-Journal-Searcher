Carolco Shareholder Sues Over Buy-Back Of Chairman's Shares

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 09/26/90

LOS ANGELES -- A Carolco Pictures Inc. stockholder filed suit against the company's board, charging that Carolco's recently announced buy-back of 3.4 million common shares from its chairman is a "breach of fiduciary duty and an abuse of control." According to the complaint, filed by stockholder Arthur-Magna Inc. in state court here, Carolco Chairman Mario Kassar will make a profit of about $11.5 million on the sale and still own 57% of Carolco's shares.

The action, technically filed on Carolco's behalf as a derivative lawsuit, charged that the agreed upon purchase price of $13 a share, to be paid to Mr. Kassar, is $5.75 a share higher than the closing price of the shares on the New York Stock Exchange Monday, when the buy-back was announced.

The funds for the repurchase were "apparently derived" from the sale of convertible preferred shares last June to Canal Plus Productions and Pioneer LDCA for about $90 million, the complaint said.

"Thus, defendants saddled the corporation with substantial added liability through the issuance of the preferred stock, which was utilized primarily to fund the inequitable stock repurchase complained of herein," the complaint said.

A Carolco spokesman at the entertainment concern's headquarters in Los Angeles declined comment on the suit.
