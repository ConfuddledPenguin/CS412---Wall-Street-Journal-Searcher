The late English strategist Basil Liddell-Hart discussing the end of the first world war in "The Real War," (Little Brown, 1930):

{T}he much-abused Napoleonic saying that God is on the side of the big battalions received yet another historical contradiction from the Alexandrine principle of quality over quantity. Once again the blend of superior hitting power with superior mobility played havoc with an army which pinned its faith to weight of human bodies.
