Business Brief -- MCI Communications Corp.: New High-Capacity Service To Be Offered by Company

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/26/90

MCI Communications Corp. said it plans to offer high-capacity data transmission services that can provide an alternative to private-line service. MCI, the nation's second largest long-distance company, said it will begin rolling out its "virtual private data services" in mid-1991, beginning with switched services over high-speed T1 and T3 circuits. T1 circuits have the capacity of 24 telephone lines, while T3 circuits are equivalent to 672. Both are now available only as dedicated circuits or as part of special services that must be reserved in advance.

The new data services, targeted primarily for big corporate customers, were demonstrated at the Telecommunications Association's annual convention in San Diego, with Cray Research Inc. using switched T3 circuits to link a supercomputer in Minneapolis with a workstation in San Diego.

Don Heath, MCI's vice president for data marketing, said the new services mean that a "computer's location won't be an obstacle" to researchers, industrial designers, financial experts or other users.
