Business Brief -- Brush Wellman Inc.: Third-Quarter Profit to Fall On Sales Drop of About 8%

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/26/90

Brush Wellman Inc. said third-quarter earnings will be "significantly less" than the 22 cents a share recorded for the year-ago period. Sales for the quarter ending Oct. 1 will be about 8% below year-earlier levels, the company said. Henry G. Piper, president and chief executive officer, said several of Brush Wellman's markets have been hurt by weakness in the economy and by production interruptions at certain government facilities.

For the 1989 third quarter, the Cleveland maker of engineered materials reported net income of $3.6 million on sales of $77.6 million.

The company expects seasonal improvements in the fourth quarter, but "it is clear that second-half sales will fall short" of 1989 levels, Mr. Piper said.
