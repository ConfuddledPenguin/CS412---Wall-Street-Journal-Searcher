International Brief -- Banco Exterior de Espana: Banco Hispano Americano Merger Idea Under Study

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/26/90

Banco Exterior de Espana confirmed it is studying a possible merger with Banco Hispano Americano, but denied talks between the two Spanish banks are under way. "We are not in negotiations, nor do we have a merger plan," an Exterior official said, adding that combining the state-owned bank with Hispano would imply privatization and require government approval. A Hispano spokesman also emphasized that a merger with Exterior isn't in the offing.

Exterior, Spain's seventh-largest bank, is 51.4%-owned by the government. The state industrial holding company Instituto Nacional de Industria and the Bank of Spain jointly own a 13.94% stake in Exterior, and the financial services company Factorex has an 8.42% interest. The rest is listed on Spanish stock exchanges.
