Credit Ratings: Massachusetts Bond Issue Put on S&P CreditWatch

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/26/90

BOSTON -- A move by Massachusetts to build investor confidence in its approaching bond issue received an unsympathetic response yesterday from Standard & Poor's Corp. S&P rated the state's coming $1.04 billion bond issue triple-B and placed the issue on the rating concern's CreditWatch surveillance list, joining the triple-B rating on the state's $5 billion of general obligation bonds.

Last week, Massachusetts state officials said they will set up an "irrevocable trust fund" for the new Series A bonds that will receive sufficient personal income-tax receipts each month. Another rating concern, Moody's Investors Services Inc., swiftly responded by giving the new issue a Baa-1 rating, slightly better than the worst-in-the-nation rating of Baa on the state's general obligation bonds.

In elaborating on its rating, S&P said, "Continued negative economic news and lower-than-projected July and August receipts have resulted in two identified revenue shortfalls totaling $464 million, some $310 million of which was not identified until last Monday."

An S&P spokeswoman also said the rating was affected by "risks of a potential budget imbalance" from the proposed tax-rollback referendum by the Citizens for Limited Taxation. The additional security provisions provided by the dedicated trust fund, she said, "were not viewed as sufficient enhancement to raise ratings, given the history of fiscal stress and the current budget environment."
