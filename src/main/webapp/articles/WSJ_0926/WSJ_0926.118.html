Treasury Plans Offer Of Short-Term Bills To Raise $1.45 Billion

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/26/90

WASHINGTON -- The Treasury plans to raise $1.45 billion in new cash with the sale Monday of about $18.4 billion in short-term bills to redeem $16.96 billion in maturing bills. However, the Treasury will postpone the auction unless it has assurances that the debt ceiling will be raised.

The offering will be divided evenly between 13-week and 26-week bills maturing on Jan. 3, 1991, and April 4, 1991, respectively. The bills will be available in minimum denominations of $10,000.

Tenders must be received by 1 p.m. EDT Monday at the Treasury or at Federal Reserve banks or branches.
