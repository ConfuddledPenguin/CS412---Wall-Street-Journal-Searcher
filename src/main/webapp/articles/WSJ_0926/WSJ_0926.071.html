Business Brief -- Jefferson Smurfit Group: Irish Paper Concern to Build New York Recycling Plant

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/26/90

An Irish paper company said it will build a $400 million plant in upstate New York that would recycle old newspapers into newsprint. Dublin-based Jefferson Smurfit Group, one of the largest producers of recycled paper and paperboard products, said it plans to build a mill that would recycle 330,000 tons of waste paper a year into newsprint, said a company spokesman. The plant could be built and running in five years, the spokesman said. Sites in upstate New York are being considered but state and company officials refused to identify them.

Smurfit Recycling Co., a St. Louis-based unit of the Irish parent, will collect newspapers from across the Northeast for use at the mill. Smurfit already operates three paper recycling mills, one in California and two in Oregon.
