Milken Called Qualified For `Domestic Peace Corps'

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/26/90

If Michael Milken's attorneys get their way, the former $550 million-a-year man will be sentenced to work with the Los Angeles Police Department to help inner-city youth. The attorneys proposed that Mr. Milken develop educational and vocational programs to "combat the problems of drugs, crime, unemployment and illiteracy threatening to destroy the lives of inner-city youth in Los Angeles."

The proposal was based on a letter sent to U.S. District Judge Kimba Wood by Daryl F. Gates, chief of police of Los Angeles. Mr. Gates said in his letter that he met Mr. Milken only once, several years ago, but that he believes Mr. Milken would be uniquely qualified to establish a "domestic peace corps" to help underprivileged children.

In their sentencing memo, Mr. Milken's lawyers promised that he would work, at a minimum, from 8 a.m. to 6 p.m. daily, "and weekends {if the court desires.}"

The memo noted that "Michael would not find this work easy; nor would it be risk-free." Chief Gates proposed that Mr. Milken work "not behind a comfortable corporate desk, but in the communities targeted by the program."

The government is expected to propose instead that Mr. Milken serve a long prison term, and that its length be dependent in part on his willingness to cooperate in the government's continuing investigation of securities-related crime.

Mr. Milken's lawyers oppose any attempt to link the length of the sentence to his cooperation, arguing that his plea agreement made clear that he would cooperate only after his sentencing.
