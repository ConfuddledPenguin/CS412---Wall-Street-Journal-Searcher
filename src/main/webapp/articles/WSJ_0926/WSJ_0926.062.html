Who's News: Optelecom President Will Retire as Soon As Successor Is Picked

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/26/90

GAITHERSBURG, Md. -- Optelecom Inc. said its president and chief operating officer, George Konkol, will retire as soon as the company chooses a successor. Mr. Konkol, who will be 67 years old next month, joined the maker of communications equipment and laser systems in 1985. He said his departure isn't for health reasons or because of any unhappiness about his relationship with the company.

"I've been here five years now," he said, "and I think a young, vigorous company like this one should have younger leadership." He noted that William H. Culver, Optelecom's chairman and chief executive officer, is 64.

After his successor is named, the company said, Mr. Konkol will serve as a consultant "for a period of time to be mutually agreed upon."

Optelecom said its board has formed a search committee that is "actively reviewing" candidates.
