Business Brief -- Bond International Gold Inc.: Selling of 810,000 Ounces Of Gold on Futures Is Set

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/26/90

Bond International Gold Inc. said it has sold forward 810,000 ounces of gold at an average price of $425 an ounce. The gold mining company said the average length of the futures contracts is one year. The sale will allow the Denver company to cut its debt in half in two years, the company said, and will improve its profitability. A spokesman declined to say whether the lower debt will make the company profitable.

For the second quarter, heavily indebted Bond International Gold reported a net loss of $12.7 million, or 22 cents a share, on sales of $141.8 million. LAC Minerals Ltd. bought 65% of Bond International Gold for $379 million in November 1989 from Alan Bond of Australia.

The company operates five gold mines in the U.S., Canada and Chile.

Bond International stock fell 25 cents yesterday to close at $6.25 a share in New York Stock Exchange composite trading.
