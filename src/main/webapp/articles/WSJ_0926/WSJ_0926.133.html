Business Brief -- Portland General Corp.: ESOP Is Set Up, Will Buy Common for $36 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/26/90

Portland General Corp. said it established an employee stock ownership plan that will buy about $36 million of the electric utility and energy holding company's common stock on the open market over the next six months. The Portland, Ore., company's electric utility subsidiary, Portland General Electric Co., will lend the money to the ESOP to buy the shares. Funding for that loan will come through either a private placement or public offering of debt by the utility unit. Salomon Brothers Inc. is acting as financial adviser. The ESOP will hold about 2.25 million of the parent company's 46.3 million common shares outstanding, Portland General said.

"This plan enables us to offer our employees a benefit package that is competitive within our industry," Ken Harrison, chief executive officer, said. "Since an ESOP generally promotes stock ownership among employees, this gives employees of all Portland General's companies a longterm stake in the corporation's success and allows them to participate in its growth and profits."

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Portland General shares closed at $15.625, up 12.5 cents.
