Officer of Freedlander Mortgage Firm Enters Guilty Plea to Fraud

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/19/90

WASHINGTON -- A senior executive of Freedlander Inc., the Mortgage People, pleaded guilty to fraudulently obtaining more than $200 million from the Federal National Mortgage Association and other investors, the Justice Department said. Eve Freedlander, a member of the family that founded the Richmond, Va.-based second-mortgage lender and that continues to control it, filed the guilty plea yesterday in federal court in Richmond, according to the Justice Department.

Neither Ms. Freedlander nor the other members of her family who run Freedlander Inc. could be reached for comment yesterday. Freedlander Inc. is the nation's fourth-largest second-mortgage lender, the Justice Department said. It made second-mortgage loans and packaged them into securities, which it sold to institutional and private investors. The company also serviced these loans on behalf of the investors by collecting monthly payments and foreclosing on loans that weren't repaid.

In a statement, U.S. Attorney Henry Hudson said that the scheme involved the submission of false loan-performance information to the Federal National Mortgage Association, also known as Fannie Mae, and other buyers to induce them to purchase the loans originated by Freedlander. He said the company hid "the nonperforming nature of many of the loans which it sold to encourage investors to continue to purchase those loans."

Fannie Mae has filed a civil lawsuit against Freedlander Inc., seeking more than $20 million in damages. Fannie Mae, which runs a secondary market in home loans, purchased more than $200 million of these loans from Freedlander Inc. from November 1982 through August 1985. The suit is still pending, Fannie Mae said.

Ms. Freedlander faces a maximum sentence of 15 years in prison and $750,000 in fines.
