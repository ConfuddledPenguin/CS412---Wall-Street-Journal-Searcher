Who's News: Bergen Brunswig's Emil Martini Slates Retirement as Chief

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/19/90

ORANGE, Calif. -- Bergen Brunswig Corp. said its longtime chief executive officer, Emil P. Martini Jr., will retire on Aug. 31. Mr. Martini, who will be 62 years old on Aug. 9, will remain chairman. "It won't be merely a ceremonial function," a spokesman for the drug and videocassette distribution concern said. "He will continue to be available."

Mr. Martini will be succeeded as chief executive by his 58-year-old brother, Robert E. Martini, who will remain Bergen Brunswig president and relinquish the post of chief operating officer to Dwight A. Steffensen, 46, who also will remain executive vice president.

Emil Martini spent "all of his working life" at Bergen Brunswig or its predecessor, Bergen Drug Co., which was founded by the brothers' father, the spokesman said. Bergen Brunswig's sales have jumped to more than $4 billion from about $200 million since the merger of Bergen Drug and Brunswig Drug Co. in 1969. Emil Martini was named chief executive in 1970.

In American Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Bergen Brunswig Class A shares closed at $28.75, down 37.5 cents.
