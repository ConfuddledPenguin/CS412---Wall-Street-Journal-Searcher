Business Brief -- Supercomputing Solutions Inc.: Loss Widens in 2nd Quarter And Prompts Cost Cutting

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/19/90

Supercomputing Solutions Inc. said its loss widened to $1.1 million, or 17 cents a share in the second quarter, and said it plans to cut its staff by 50% and take other cost-cutting steps due to "an uncomfortable cash position." In the year-earlier period, the San Diego-based maker and seller of supercomputers and software reported a loss of $743,836, or 17 cents a share.

In the recent quarter, revenues more than doubled to $738,723 from $360,752. The gains were due to the company's completion of a subcontract for the initial phase of the Direct Solution of Turbulence Program for the Department of Defense and the completion and sale of the company's first CAPPS-9064 supercomputer to TRW Inc.

However, the company said research and development costs linked to the CAPPS-9064 resulted in significant losses, leaving the company in an "uncomfortable cash position."

The company said its cost-saving efforts will include cutbacks in travel and capital-equipment expenditures as well as the curtailment of a research grant to Princeton University.
