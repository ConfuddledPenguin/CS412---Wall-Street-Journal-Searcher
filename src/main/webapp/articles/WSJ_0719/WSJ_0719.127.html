Who's News: Comex Board Picks Halperin, of the Merc, To Be New President

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/19/90

NEW YORK -- The board of the Commodity Exchange Inc., as expected, named David A. Halperin its new president. Mr. Halperin, 47 years old, had been executive vice president of the New York Mercantile Exchange, a post he occupied since December 1989. He came to the Merc from the Comex, where he had been employed for eight years. His last post at the Comex was executive vice president responsible for economics and research, new product development, operations and computer systems. Mr. Halperin moved to the Merc last year as part of a reorganization plan.

"I feel very fortunate to have been able to work at the highest level of policy development and operations" at both Comex and the New York Mercantile Exchange, "which has given me insight into the needs of both exchanges," he said.

Martin Greenberg, Comex chairman, said, "His (Mr. Halperin's) professional experience with our organization and his industry insight make him the perfect man for the job."

One former exchange official says he thinks Mr. Halperin will do well at the Comex because "he knows where all the bodies are buried."
