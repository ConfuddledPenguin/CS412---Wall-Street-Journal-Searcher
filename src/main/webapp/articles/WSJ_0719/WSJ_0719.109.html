Business Brief -- Union Pacific Corp.: Net Was Flat in Quarter Despite 9.2% Rise in Sales

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/19/90

Union Pacific Corp. said second-quarter earnings were flat at $153 million, or $1.53 a share, compared with $152 million, or $1.41 a share, a year earlier. Revenue climbed 9.2% to 1.78 billion, from $1.63 billion.

The Bethlehem, Pa., company said net income from its railroad rose 4% to $141 million, despite a softer economy and the non-recurrence of last year's favorable retroactive tax claims. But the railroad's higher profit was partly offset by a 21% drop in earnings from Union Pacific Resources, the company's energy and mineral business, which fell to $43 million from $55 million.

Union Pacific said the sharper rise in net earnings per share reflects the effect of its stock repurchase program; average shares outstanding declined to 100.4 million from 110.6 million.

For the first half, the company's earnings fell 3% to $281 million or $2.80 a share, from $290 million or $2.63 a share. Revenue rose 10% to $3.51 billion, from $3.18 billion.
