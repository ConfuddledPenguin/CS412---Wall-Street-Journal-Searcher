Steel Production Slips 2%

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/19/90

BRUSSELS -- Production of crude steel by the 35 countries that account for 97% of output in the Western world slipped 2% to 39,436,000 tons in June, according to the International Iron and Steel Institute. The year-earlier figure was 40,258,000 tons. Japan, which accounted for 8,982,000 tons, boosted output 0.2%, while the U.S.'s production of 7,357,000 tons was down 0.8%. The European Community output fell 3.9% to 11,662,000 tons. The biggest slump was in Brazil, where production tumbled 22.4% to 1,519,000 tons.

For the first six months, the IISI said output slipped 2.5% to 239,758,000 tons from the July to December 1989 total. Again Brazil was the worst-affected producer. Output fell 17.6% to 10,124,000 tons.
