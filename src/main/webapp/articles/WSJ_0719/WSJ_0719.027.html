Dividend News: Cooper Tire Splits Stock 2-for-1, Boosts Cash Dividend by 33%

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/19/90

FINDLAY, Ohio -- Cooper Tire & Rubber Co. directors voted a 2-for-1 split of the common stock and raised the dividend by 33%. The producer of replacement auto and truck tires also reported a 9.2% rise in second-quarter net, in spite of tire price discounting. Net was $16.4 million, or 80 cents a share on current shares, up from $15.1 million, or 74 cents a share, a year earlier. Sales rose 2.3% to $234.7 million from $229.9 million.

The dividend on the new shares will be six cents quarterly, equal to 12 cents on current stock, an increase from the nine cents paid in each of the past four quarters.

Additional shares will be distributed on Aug. 20 to shares of record July 30. The initial dividend on the split stock will be paid Sept. 30 to stock of record Sept. 7.

Cooper directors think there will be a better market for the stock at the lower price resulting from the split, said Ivan W. Gorr, chairman and chief executive officer. The company last split the stock, also 2-for-1, two years ago, and has raised the dividend every year since 1979. Cooper stock close yesterday at $36.625, up 37.5 cents.

Second-quarter net was in line with analysts' expectations, said Harry W. Millis, tire and rubber analyst with McDonald & Co., Cleveland. He expects the company to earn about $3.25 a share on current shares for the full year, up from $2.84 in 1989.

Tire producers slashed prices last spring because of excess inventories arising from the first-quarter plunge in auto and truck production. Although Cooper doesn't sell tires to the auto makers, it was affected because other tire makers dumped into the replacement market tires that they had produced for the auto plants.

The worst of the discounting appears to be over, Mr. Millis said. Also, most tire producers have announced increases in list prices for replacement tires averaging about 3.5%, effective during August and September.

Profit for the first half rose 19% to $30.2 million, or $1.47 a share, from $25.4 million, or $1.24 a share. Sales increased 4.3% to $443.3 million from $425 million.
