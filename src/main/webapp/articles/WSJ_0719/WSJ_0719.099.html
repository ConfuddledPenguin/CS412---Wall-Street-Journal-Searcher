French Stockbrokerage Will File for Bankruptcy

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/19/90

PARIS -- French stockbrokerage Tuffier-Ravier-Py & Associes said it will file for bankruptcy following the failure of its last-minute attempt to find a backer. The demise of Tuffier-Ravier-Py will be the largest bankruptcy in the French stock market and provides the first test for the bourse authorities' 200-millionfranc ($36.3 million) guarantee fund set up in 1988.

The broker is one of the largest French stockbrokers and is part of Tuffier & Associes, an independent diversified financial services company controlled by Thierry Tuffier.

The Paris Bourse on Friday suspended Tuffier-Ravier-Py from all stock trading because it was unable to pay its bills. Mr. Tuffier said last week that the group's brokerage arm had run up heavy losses this year due to a squeeze on its margins since fixed commissions were abolished last year as part of France's 1988 stock-market reforms. He said then that all French brokers had been hit similarly by the lower commissions and by a fall in volume this year on the stock market.

But rival French brokers said that Mr. Tuffier's problems spring in large part from his group's over-ambitious expansion as it diversified into financial futures, corporate finance, money-market trading and other new fields.

Mr. Tuffier said this week that his firm now needed loans of up to 300 million francs to stay on its feet.
