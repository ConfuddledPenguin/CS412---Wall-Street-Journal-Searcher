Business Brief -- H.F. Ahmanson & Co.: Thrift Holding Company Says Earnings Jumped 51%

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/19/90

H.F. Ahmanson & Co., parent of Home Savings of America, said its second-quarter earnings increased 51% to $60.1 million, or 61 cents a share. The Los Angeles thrift holding company attributed the results to strong growth in new mortgage loans, which totaled a record $4.2 billion for the quarter.

The growth is impressive because it comes at a time when home sales are slowing and "generally mortgage lenders are having trouble putting loans out the door," said Allan Bortel, an analyst at Sutro & Co.

Ahmanson's said its second-quarter nonperforming assets increased by $51 million to $597 million, or 1.25% of total assets. It also said that it may sell its real-estate development operations because of new capital rules it said will take effect by 1994.

The thrift company's stock fell 12.5 cents to $21.875 yesterday.
