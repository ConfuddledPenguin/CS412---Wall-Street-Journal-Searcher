International Brief -- British Steel PLC: U.K. Company to Acquire 40% of Spanish Steel Group

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/19/90

British Steel PLC said it agreed in principle to acquire 40% of Spain's Aristrain steelmaking group, with La Corporacion Banesto, the industrial conglomerate of Spain's Banesto banking group, buying another 40%. The purchase price wasn't disclosed in a statement released by British Steel, which also said that provision had been made for Ensidesa, the Spanish national steel company, to acquire a shareholding up to a maximum 10%. The balance will be retained by current shareholders.

A British Steel spokesman said it was premature to discuss the acquisition price. The current family shareholders will retain 20% of the concern under the accord. Any Ensidesa purchase would come jointly from British Steel and LCB's holdings.

Aristrain, based in Madrid and the Basque country, is Spain's largest specialist producer of steel sections for the construction industry. Its annual output is about 1.5 million tons, which is marketed through its national distribution network.
