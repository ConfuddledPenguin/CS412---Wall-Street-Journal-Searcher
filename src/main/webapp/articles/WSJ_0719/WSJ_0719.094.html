Business Brief -- Washington Bancorp.: Holding Firm Posts Loss Of $80 Million in Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/19/90

Washington Bancorp., reporting a second-quarter loss of $80 million, or $11.28 a share, said its ability to continue operating is in doubt unless it can find new capital, restructure assets and reach an accomodation with its creditors. In the 1989 second quarter, the Washington, D.C.-based bank holding company posted net income of $3.5 million, or 51 cents a share.

Much of the loss for the quarter ended June 30 came from an increased provision for loan losses. After reviewing Washington Bancorp.'s assets with federal regulators, the company added $61 million to loan loss reserves, compared to a $2 million provision in the year-ago period. The company also realized losses of $15 million on asset sales and write-downs.

The tremendous loss seems to have torpedoed the company's plan to refinance its debt by issuing $45 million in new secured notes. The debt restructuring was announced in May, after the company determined it wouldn't be able repay $36.7 million in commercial paper. That program is "no longer feasible," the company said.

The company said it hopes to come up with another plan to improve its capital ratios and repay its debt. However, "No assurances can be given that commercial paper holders or other creditors will be repaid in full," said Chairman John J. Mason.
