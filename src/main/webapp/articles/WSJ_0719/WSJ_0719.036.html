Brazil's Trade Surplus Shrinks

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/19/90

RIO DE JANEIRO -- Stable imports and slipping exports caused Brazil's trade surplus to shrink 32% to $1.15 billion in June from a year earlier, the Federal Trade Bureau said. Exports totaled $2.5 billion f.o.b., which means the seller agrees to put an item on a transportation vehicle at no charge, but the buyer pays the actual transportation costs. The figure was down from $3.23 billion in May. Imports were $1.35 billion f.o.b., down from $1.5 billion last month.

The trade surplus accumulated over the past 12 months reached $13.26 billion f.o.b., down from $19.52 billion f.o.b. registered in the previous 12 months.
