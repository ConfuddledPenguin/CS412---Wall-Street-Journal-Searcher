Business Brief -- Clothestime Inc.: Share Net Is Seen Falling About 30% in 2nd Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/19/90

Clothestime Inc. said it sees about a 30% decline in second-quarter per-share profit to about 11 cents a share from 16 cents a share. In the year-ago quarter ended July 29, the apparel-store chain posted net income of $2.3 million, or 16 cents a share, on sales of about $52 million. The second quarter ends on July 28.

Clothestime said that comparable store sales this month are running about 9% lower than last July. Total monthly sales are expected to be about $17 million, roughly the same as last year, a company official said.

Clothestime attributed the lower-than-expected sales mainly to a lack of television advertising this month. Last summer, the company reoriented its marketing to focus on somewhat older customers and to sell more brand-name clothes. Clothestime said its "competitive pricing" on the brand-name merchandise has resulted in "slightly lower gross margins."
