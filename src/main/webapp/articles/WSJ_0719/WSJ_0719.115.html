Business Brief -- Great American Management: Partnership Agrees to Sell 18% of Firm for $50 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/19/90

The partnership that controls Great American Management & Investment Inc. agreed to sell an 18% Great American stake, for $50 million, to two investment funds affiliated with the investment-banking partnership of Hellman & Friedman. Great American, Chicago, has interests in manufacturing, real estate, financial services, agricultural chemicals and fertilizers. Investor Sam Zell serves as chairman, chief executive officer and president. Along with the estate of the recently deceased Robert Lurie, Mr. Zell controls Great American through a partnership known as Equity Holdings Ltd.

Under the agreement, the partnership controlled by Mr. Zell will sell the Hellman & Friedman funds two million common shares, at $25 each. The sale will trim the Equity Holdings stake to 74% from 92%; the publicly traded portion of Great American shares will be unchanged at about 4.5%, with the remainder in the hands of other Great American insiders.

Great American has approximately 11.1 million common shares outstanding.
