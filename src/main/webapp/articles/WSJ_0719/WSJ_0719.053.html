Credit Ratings: Moody's to Review Central Illinois PS Debt and Preferred

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/19/90

SPRINGFIELD, Ill. -- Moody's Investors Service Inc. said it placed about $566 million of Central Illinois Public Service Co.'s long-term debt and preferred stock under review for a ratings downgrade. The rating concern said it will examine "whether a recent weakening in debt protection measures, continuing regulatory delays, as well as growing business and operating risks in the electric utility, will permit CIPS to maintain Moody's highest rating."

Moody's said it will look at a revenue-refund order the company faces. It also noted that current industry and regulatory trends mean "the ability of any electric utility to maintain financial indicators supportive of the Aaa rating may be in doubt."

Under review are Central Illinois PS's first mortgage bonds and secured pollution-control bonds, rated triple-A; unsecured pollution-control bonds and preferred stock, rated double-A-1. The utility's commercial paper, rated prime-1, isn't under review.

A Central Illinois PS official said that "within the last two months, three of the five triple-A-rated utilities have been downgraded," leaving only Central Illinois PS and Citizens Utilities Co. of Stamford, Conn., with Moody's highest rating.

"We're certainly concerned" about the possible downgrading and the "uncertainty" it creates for shareholders, the official said. At the same time, he said, "We also understand Moody's interest in examining the entire electric utility industry to determine if present-day business risks endanger the triple-A rating of any company."

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Central Illinois PS shares closed at $21.125, down 25 cents.
