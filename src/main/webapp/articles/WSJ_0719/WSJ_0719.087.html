Money-Fund Assets Rose $3.2 Billion In the Latest Week

  • Author: Georgette Jasen
  • Date: 07/19/90

Investors poured another $3.2 billion into money-market mutual funds in the latest week, as yields remained nearly unchanged. Assets of 430 taxable funds totaled $382.4 billion at the close of business Tuesday, according to IBC/Donoghue's Money Fund Report. The average seven-day compound yield, which assumes reinvestment of dividends, remained at 7.95%.

Martha Wittbrodt, editor of the Holliston, Mass.-based newsletter, predicted that yields will begin to slide in the next few weeks, reflecting the Federal Reserve's recent easing of interest rates. She said money-fund yields could decline a tenth of a percentage point or so over the next three to four weeks.

Average yields on taxable funds have been at or just below 8% for most of this year.

Most of the new money added to the funds in the week ended Tuesday -- about $2.5 billion -- came from institutions, Donoghue's reported. Ms. Wittbrodt said institutions that took money out of the funds several weeks ago, when yields on competing investments were slightly higher, may be putting it back in now that those yields are falling. Yields on three-month Treasury bills sold at Monday's auction, for instance, were 7.88%, compared with 8.05% in late June.

Money-fund yields typically lag behind general interest-rate trends by a few weeks because portfolio managers can vary the maturities of their investments to take advantage of the best yields. The managers typically lengthen maturities when rates are falling and shorten them when rates are rising.

Average maturity of the taxable funds grew by one day in the week ended Tuesday, to 44 days. That's within the range of the last few months; average maturity has been as short as 29 days and as long as 53 days in recent years. Money funds invest in such things as short-term Treasury securities, commercial paper (short-term corporate IOUs) and bank certificates of deposit.

The average seven-day simple yield of the taxable funds slipped to 7.66% from 7.67%. The average 30-day simple yield also eased to 7.66% from 7.67% and the average 30-day compound yield dipped to 7.95% from 7.97%.

Yields on tax-exempt funds fell faster. The average seven-day compound yield of 224 tax-free funds fell to 5.23% from 5.30%, Donoghue's reported. The latest yield is equivalent to a taxable yield of 7.26% for an investor in the 28% tax bracket and 7.81% for someone paying 33% in taxes.

Assets of the tax-exempt funds remained flat at $78.9 billion. Average maturity lengthened by two days to 51 days.
