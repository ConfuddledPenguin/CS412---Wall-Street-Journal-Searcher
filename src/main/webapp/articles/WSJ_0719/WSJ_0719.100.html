Business Brief -- Graphic Scanning Corp.: Board Looks for `Alliance' To Generate New Capital

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/19/90

Graphic Scanning Corp. said its directors decided to seek an "alliance" with a "strategic partner or partners" to develop new businesses and generate new capital. Based in Englewood, N.J., Graphic Scanning Corp., which is involved in paging, cellular telephone and wireless cable operations, has faltered in previous efforts to improve its financial position. Discussions to sell a controlling interest in its Digital Mobile Communications Ltd. business, a U.K. telecommunications company, collapsed last year, as did talks of a possible merger with McCaw Cellular Communications Inc.

The company said its directors had determined that developing its telecommunications businesses is "beyond the scope" of its current resources. Termination of the Digital Mobile sales discussions "creates immediate capital needs," the company added.

It said it also is looking at other alternatives to raise capital, including selling securities or assets or entering into other joint ventures.

For the nine months ended March 31, Graphic Scanning had a net loss of $38.5 million, or $1.19 a share, on revenue of $71.1 million.
