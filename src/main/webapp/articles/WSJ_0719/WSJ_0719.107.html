Business Brief -- Wavetek Corp.: Earnings Are Disappointing, Firm Explores Possible Sale

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/19/90

Wavetek Corp. posted disappointing fiscal third-quarter profit and said it retained a financial adviser to explore various options to maximize shareholder value, including a possible sale of the San Diego company. John F. Kessler, chief executive officer, said "operational results weren't adequate" although net income more than doubled to $515,000, or six cents a share, in the June 30 period from $234,000, or three cents a share. Revenue rose 9% to $22.9 million from $21 million.

Pretax earnings of $522,000 were about half of what the company had hoped for, Mr. Kessler said. New orders in the quarter fell to $17.4 million from $23.7 million in the year-ago period. He said the main problem is erosion in demand for the company's electronic test-and-measurement equipment, which accounts for about 60% of sales.

Mr. Kessler said Wavetek expects to dismiss about 100 people, or about 10% of its work force, by the end of the week, reflecting the company's lowered sales and profit expectations. After the cuts, the company will have 850 to 900 employees.

Wavetek retained Peers & Co., a Chicago investment banking concern, to assist it in arranging a "strategic alliance" or sale of the company.

Wavetek closed yesterday at $4.25, up 37.5 cents, in over-the-counter trading.
