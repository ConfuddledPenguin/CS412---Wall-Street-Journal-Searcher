Business Brief -- Bamberger Polymers Inc.: Restraining Order Prevents Ex-Employees' Competition

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/19/90

Bamberger Polymers Inc. said it secured a temporary restraining order against 11 former sales and marketing employees, who, it said, are starting a competing plastics business. Bamberger, a New Hyde Park, N.Y., plastics merchant, earlier this week said loss of the employees would have a "material adverse effect" on its sales and earnings from the Northeast and West Coast operations in the third quarter.

The order restrains the former employees from operating a plastics business.

Walter Craig, legal counsel for Helm Resources Inc., a holding company that controls about half of Bamberger, said the defecting workers are "engaged in basically the same business they were involved in, the trading and sale of thermoplastic resins." Mr. Craig said that Bamberger suspects another company provided the workers financial backing.

The company also is seeking damages against the former employees in a proceeding in New York state Supreme Court in Nassau County. A request for a preliminary injunction will be considered Tuesday.

None of the defendants could be reached for comment.
