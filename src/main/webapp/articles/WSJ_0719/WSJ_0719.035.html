FTC Sues to Bar Purchase Of Assets by Arco Chemical

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/19/90

WASHINGTON -- The Federal Trade Commission sued to block Arco Chemical Co.'s $220 million acquisition of certain business lines from Union Carbide Corp. The suit, filed in federal court in Washington, seeks an injunction stopping the sale and rescinding the agreement on antitrust grounds. The agreement provided for the sale to Arco Chemical of two Carbide chemical lines.

The FTC authorized its staff to file the suit last month. But at the time Arco Chemical held out hope that antitrust issues could still be resolved through negotiation. The company, based in Newton Square, Pa., has already made full payment to Carbide, and both companies say the payment is nonrefundable under the terms of their agreement.

The FTC has declined to comment on whether the court-ordered rescission it is seeking would entail the return of Arco's payment.

In any case, the agreement provides that Arco Chemical has rights to proceeds from a subsequent sale of the Carbide assets to a third party. Carbide, based in Danbury, Conn., still operates the businesses at issue.

Arco Chemical is 83% owned by Atlantic Richfield Co., Los Angeles, although Arco Chemical's stock trades separately.

Both companies issued statements saying they believe their positions will be upheld in court. An FTC spokeswoman declined to comment.
