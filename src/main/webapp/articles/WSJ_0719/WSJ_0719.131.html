Georgia Gulf's Profit Fell 68%; Sales Slid 25% in Second Period

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/19/90

ATLANTA -- Georgia Gulf Corp., in its first earnings report since it recapitalized to thwart an unwanted takeover, said second-quarter net income plunged 68% on a 25% drop in sales. The chemical company reported net income of $15.7 million, or 50 cents a share, compared with $49.3 million, or $1.91 a share, a year earlier. The latest results reflected recapitalization costs, including such items as legal fees, of about $15.2 million. The quarter's earnings also included $16.9 million in interest expense related to the recapitalization.

Georgia Gulf adopted the recapitalization plan in April, ending a protracted and bitter takeover battle with Dallas investor Harold C. Simmons.

Sales dropped to $218.7 million from $292.3 million. Georgia Gulf attributed the decrease largely to the recent sale of its Freeman Chemical Corp. subsidiary.

In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange exchange yesterday, Georgia Gulf fell 12.5 cents to close at $10.375. The company released its earnings after the market closed.

For the first half, earnings fell 49% to $53.1 million, or $1.90 a share, from $104.7 million, or $4.04 a share, a year earlier. Sales dropped 23% to $470.4 million from $610 million. "Considering that in the first six months of last year the chemical industry experienced some of its strongest markets ever, the current level of earnings, while down, represents a good performance," a company news release said.
