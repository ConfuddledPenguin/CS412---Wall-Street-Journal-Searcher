Tokyo Exchange To List Japan's Kentucky Fried

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/19/90

NEW YORK -- Kentucky Fried Chicken Japan, a joint venture between PepsiCo Inc. and Mitsubishi Corp., is expected to be listed on the Tokyo Stock Exchange's second section Aug. 21, said Hidemi Suzuki, executive vice president of corporate finance at Nikko Securities International Inc. Nikko Securities International's parent, Nikko Securities Co. Ltd., is the lead manager for the planned, initial public offering of six million shares of KFC Japan. Suzuki confirmed that the offering price range of between 6,510 yen and 8,470 yen "is the one Nikko Tokyo is going to use." At the current exchange rate of 148 yen to the dollar, that equals a range of about $44 to $57.

Suzuki said that U.S. law bars U.S. residents or institutions from buying new Japanese issues. However, Japanese branches of U.S. securities firms and foreign brokerages are allowed to participate.

Pepsi and Mitsubishi each currently own 48.7% of KFC Japan's 15.75 million shares, and the balance is held by an employee stock-ownership plan.
