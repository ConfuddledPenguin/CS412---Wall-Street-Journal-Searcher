Who's News: Drexel Restructures Board, Gives Control To Outside Members

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/19/90

NEW YORK -- Drexel Burnham Lambert Group Inc. restructured its board so that outsiders now officially run what remains of the once-powerful securities firm. Drexel said its new chairman will be current board member Ralph S. Saul, formerly chairman of Cigna Corp., head of First Boston Corp.'s management committee and president of the American Stock Exchange. He succeeds John Shad, who remains a director.

John F. Sorte remains chief executive officer, and was elected to the additional post of president, succeeding Frederick Joseph, who was Drexel's longtime leader and who becomes vice chairman. Mr. Sorte will also be chairman of the Drexel Burnham Lambert Inc. brokerage unit, succeeding Edwin Kantor, who becomes a senior executive vice president.

Drexel filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy-law protection in February. Although most of the firm and its units have been liquidated, Drexel has said that it plans to emerge from bankruptcy proceedings as a going concern.

Three new outside directors were elected to the Drexel board: Fletcher L. Byrom, former chairman of Koppers Co.; Deborah Hicks Midanek, a managing director at Solon Asset Management Corp. and former Drexel employee, and Frederick W. Zuckerman, vice president and treasurer of Chrysler Corp. who has announced plans to leave the auto maker.

The board now includes eight members, five from outside the firm.

Mr. Sorte said the board's restructuring reflects "our determination to protect the creditors and preserve equity values."

Mr. Hills, a former SEC chairman, resigned from Drexel's board, citing a conflict of interest with his law firm.

The only three Drexel employees who remain on the Drexel board are Mr. Joseph, Mr. Sorte and Richard J. Wright, chief financial officer. Others leaving the Drexel board include Joseph Vitanza, Howard Brenner and Robert Linton.
