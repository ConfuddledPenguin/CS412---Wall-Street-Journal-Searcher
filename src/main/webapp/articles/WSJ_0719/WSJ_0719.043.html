DeSoto Agrees to Sell U.S. Paint Operation To Sherwin-Williams

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/19/90

DES PLAINES, Ill. -- DeSoto Inc. said it agreed to sell its domestic consumer paint business to Sherwin-Williams Co. for about $80 million. The Sherwin-Williams pact is the latest in a series of agreements which, taken together, represent a near-total liquidation, with proceeds to be distributed to shareholders. DeSoto, a maker of paints, chemical coatings and detergents, has since last November been fighting off a hostile $50-a-share tender offer from Sutton Holding Corp., a New York investor group.

Last week DeSoto announced that it had agreed to sell its industrial coatings business and its surfactant and emulsifier lines for about $200 million. The company disclosed then that it was "very close" to an agreement on the sale of its paint operation.

The paint unit's operations have been estimated at more than $150 million in 1989, but competitive pressures have generated operating losses in recent quarters.

As with the earlier unit-sale agreements, the planned sale of the paint operation is subject to approval by shareholders and regulators. DeSoto said it will distribute the net proceeds -- expected to be around $60 million, or about $14.97 a share -- to shareholders. Combined with the $27.84 the company has said it expects to distribute from proceeds of the agreements announced earlier, DeSoto holders stand to receive about $42.81 a share from the agreements so far.

Still unsold is certain proprietary technology, which is on the block but not expected to garner a significant price, and the company's household-detergent business, which had revenue last year of about $72 million. DeSoto has said it will "for the present" retain the detergent operation.

DeSoto undertook the sale of the company's principal lines after a Delaware court, in the course of the company's legal dispute with Sutton, ordered it to take steps to maximize shareholder value.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading, DeSoto shares closed at $46, up 12.5 cents.
