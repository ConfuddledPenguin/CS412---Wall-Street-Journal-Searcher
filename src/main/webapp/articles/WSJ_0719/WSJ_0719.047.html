Chemical Builds Asset-Backed Plan For Allied Stores

  • Author: Constance Mitchell
  • Date: 07/19/90

NEW YORK -- Chemical Banking Corp. said it structured an asset-backed commercial-paper program for Allied Stores Corp., the big retailer that is operating under protection of the bankruptcy court. Chemical said it marks the first time that a company operating under Chapter 11 would be able to sell commercial paper. Investors usually buy commercial paper, or short-term IOUs, only from the most credit-worthy corporations.

Chemical said Allied's commercial paper will be securitized by the credit-card receivables of various Allied retail stores, which along with Federated Department Stores Inc. filed for protection against creditors in January. They are both units of Canada's Campeau Corp., which is headed by Robert Campeau.

The offering must be approved by the U.S. Bankruptcy Court in Cincinnati, which will meet to discuss the proposed transaction on July 26, according to a Chemical Bank spokeswoman.

Chemical said the new arrangement will replace Allied's existing $700 million receivables loan facility with Citibank, which was scheduled to expire July 31, and will result in an annual cost savings to Allied of about $10 million.

Although Chemical is structuring the deal, it won't be issuing the commercial paper. Mason Funding Corp., an independent special-purpose corporation, authorized an initial three-year program of $650 million in commercial paper, which may be increased to $721 million.

The commercial paper will be backed by a surety bond issued by Financial Guaranty Insurance Corp., a unit of General Electric Co., and a 15% letter of credit from Chemical Bank and a consortium of other institutions. Because of these "credit enhancement" features, Allied's commercial paper is expected to be rated A-1 plus by Standard & Poor's Corp. and P-1 by Moody's Investors Service Inc.
