Business Brief -- Kerr-McGee Corp.: Division's Assets to Be Sold For Around $210 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/19/90

Kerr-McGee Corp., Oklahoma City, said it agreed to sell the assets of its soda products division to Searles Valley Acquisition Inc. for about $210 million. Searles is a company formed by D. George Harris & Associates, a management group based in New York. Kerr-McGee is a chemical and energy company. The agreement is subject to financing.

Kerr-McGee said it will report a $35 million after-tax loss on the sale, but the company didn't say when it would post the loss. A company spokesman said he couldn't immediately elaborate on the news release issued late yesterday.

The company said assets of the division include three chemical processing facilities in Trona, Calif., that produce soda ash, sodium sulfate, potash and boron products. The division has 1,100 employees and had 1989 sales of $200 million.
