Technology Brief -- Apple Computer Inc.: Net for Fiscal 3rd Quarter Surged 25%, Revenue 9.3%

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/19/90

Apple Computer Inc., continuing to post healthy financial results despite concerns that it has a weak mix of products, reported a 25% rise in profit for its third fiscal quarter ended June 19. Apple, based in Cupertino, Calif., said net income rose to $119.8 million from $96.1 million the year earlier. Per-share net rose 30% to 96 cents from 74 cents a year earlier on a greater number of shares outstanding. Revenue grew 9.3% to $1.36 billion from $1.25 billion the year before.

However, Apple warned that results for its current quarter may not equal those of a year ago because of high spending on products being developed.

In the fall, the company is expected to revamp the low end of its Macintosh computer line with at least two new models that sport lower prices and higher performance than current ones. Analysts fear that Apple's demand may soften somewhat as the introduction of these products, tentatively set for October, nears.

The third-quarter results, released after the market closed yesterday, were generally in line with expectations. In a statement, John Sculley, Apple's chief executive officer, attributed the results to strong demand for its higher-end Macintosh computers and to price cuts that have spurred demand for low-end Macintoshes.
