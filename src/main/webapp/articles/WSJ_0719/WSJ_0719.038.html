Citicorp Plans to Reduce Its North American Costs

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/19/90

NEW YORK -- Citicorp said it will soon disclose details of a plan to curb out-of-line expenses in its North American corporate banking operations. The nation's largest bank didn't provide any further information, but analysts said it is almost certain that such expense reductions will result in layoffs.

Thomas Jones, executive vice president for financial control, told analysts that "expenses are going up too much for the level of business" in North America. "We'll be bringing that in line."

Mr. Jones said the problems in Citicorp's North American business stem mostly from the reduced level of corporate mergers and acquisitions and from the cost of credit.

James McDermott, a banking analyst and executive vice president of Keefe, Bruyette & Woods Inc., said that any effort to significantly reduce expense growth would almost certainly involve layoffs. "The thing is, I have no idea what dimension they're talking about."

He said that a comprehensive expense control effort presumably would also involve "a review of business lines and decisions about where they want to operate and in what fashion."

Other money center banks already have announced plans to restructure their corporate business to reflect the lower volume of business since the leveraged buy-out and merger and acquisition binge of the late 1980s dried up.
