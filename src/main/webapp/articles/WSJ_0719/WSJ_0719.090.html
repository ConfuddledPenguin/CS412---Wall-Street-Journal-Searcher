Business Brief -- Ashton-Tate Corp.: Firm's $12.6 Million Loss Is Larger Than Expected

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/19/90

Ashton-Tate Corp. reported a larger-than-expected net loss for its second quarter, a further sign that the company's position in the database software market is slipping. Ashton-Tate posted a net loss of $12.6 million, or 47 cents a share, for the quarter. Net revenue declined 19% to $48.1 million.

The Torrance, Calif., company's fortunes have been hurt by a long delay in the release of a new database product and failed effort at diversifying into other software markets.

"It was expected to be a bad quarter, but this was worse than expected," said David Bayer, an analyst at Montgomery Securities, who had anticipated that Ashton-Tate would have a loss of only $500,000 for the quarter.

Mr. Bayer said the company's sales fell in the quarter because customers anticipate that Ashton-Tate will shortly introduce a revamped version of dBASE 4, its flagship product. The company declined to comment on the introduction date.
