`Major' Criminal Actions Due by U.S. in S&L Fraud

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/19/90

WASHINGTON -- A top investigator for the Resolution Trust Corp. said the agency expects "major" criminal actions to be brought eventually against officials of 40% of the failed savings-and-loan associations taken over by the government. James Dudine, assistant director of RTC investigations, said the agency also expects to bring civil actions charging malfeasance by directors and officers at 55% of the insolvent thrifts. The RTC, created by last year's S&L legislation to sell off and liquidate hundreds of insolvent thrifts, has taken over about 450 thrifts and has sold or liquidated about 210.

The RTC believes civil claims against accountants will be brought in 22% of the cases, and against attorneys in about 18%, Mr. Dudine said.

RTC investigators also believe about 15% of the failed thrifts colluded with other thrifts in fraudulent transactions such as joint real-estate deals and loan swaps, he added.

Timothy Ryan, director of the Office of Thrift Supervision, said that agency was concerned that judges aren't giving sufficiently stiff sentences to those convicted of S&L fraud. Though the Justice Department has pointed to some executives who have received as much as 30-year sentences, that is "the exception," Mr. Ryan said.
