Dataproducts, Chief Are Sued by Holder Over Stock Decline

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/19/90

LOS ANGELES -- An investor has sued Dataproducts Corp. and its chief executive officer, Jack C. Davis, for allegedly issuing false and misleading positive statements about the company before its stock took a tumble last September. The drop in the stock price, to $9.50 a share on Sept. 8 from as high as $13.875 in August, came after Dataproducts announced a restructuring that resulted in a one-time charge of $45 million. That move came as Dataproducts sought to resist a hostile bid from DPC Acquisition Corp., a New York investment group led by Crescott Investment Associates. This year, Dataproducts was acquired by a venture formed by two affiliates of Hitachi Ltd., the Tokyo-based electronic equipment maker, for about $160 million.

Nadeem Faruqi, a purchaser of 100 Dataproducts shares, said in his lawsuit filed in federal court here that the misleading statements began May 3, 1988, with a news release calling "solid ink technology" the future of office printing, when the company knew it was facing technical and marketing difficulties.

The Woodland Hills, Calif., maker of computer printers and other data handling equipment also covered up problems with its SI 480 printer and airline ticket printing products, the suit charges.

Mr. Davis said yesterday that he hadn't seen the suit and thus couldn't comment on it.

The suit, which seeks class-action status on behalf of those who purchased stock between May 3, 1988, and Sept. 7, 1989, seeks unspecified damages.

In the year ended March 31, Dataproducts had a net loss of $8.1 million, or 41 cents a share, on revenue of $333.7 million. It had net income of $3.8 million, or 19 cents a share, on revenue of $353.4 million the year before.
