Letters to the Editor: Jim Wright Protests Charge as Untrue

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/19/90

Your July 16 editorial "The Price of Silence" makes a very valid point that "the essential cause of the S&L debacle is in danger of being lost amid the political name-calling." Unfortunately, you revive and give currency to a grossly inaccurate bit of name-calling in which I was the unhappy target. You cite a singularly contemptible charge "that Mr. Wright threatened to expose one S&L regulator as a homosexual and browbeat others to go easy on Democratic campaign contributors."

That charge is untrue. To make a threat of that kind would be totally alien to my character. I did not do anything of that sort and I would not. Nobody who knows me well at all would believe that I had ever been so crude as that.

My judgment, like that of other mortals, is imperfect. I have made my share of mistakes, no doubt. But I do not want that particular bit of calumny left unanswered in the public record.

Jim Wright

Member of Congress, 1953-1989

Speaker of the House, 1987-1989

Fort Worth, Texas
