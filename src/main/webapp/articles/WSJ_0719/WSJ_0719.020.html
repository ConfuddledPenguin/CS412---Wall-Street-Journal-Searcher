SEC Mildly Supports Bid To Regulate Planners More

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/19/90

WASHINGTON -- Securities and Exchange Commission member Mary Schapiro offered mild support for proposed legislation that would beef up regulation of the financial-planning industry. The bill, proposed by Rep. Rick Boucher (D., Va.), would force better disclosure by people calling themselves investment advisers and toughen penalties the SEC could impose on wrongdoers.

The legislation won't be considered seriously in this legislative session, particularly because Republicans on the House subcommittee on telecommunications and finance oppose several of the bill's provisions. For example, Rep. Matthew Rinaldo (R., N.J.) expressed concern that the bill would put burdensome additional rules on already regulated insurance agents, securities professionals, accountants and lawyers.

Ms. Schapiro, representing the SEC in testimony before the subcommittee, also expressed concern about regulatory overkill, but offered the agency's backing for stepped-up oversight of financial planners. She said the agency needs to raise fees charged on planners so that the SEC can afford to do more inspections.

A recent GAO report criticized SEC oversight of the financial-planning industry, contending that registering planners may be doing "more harm than good." Ms. Schapiro argued that the SEC does what it can given the relatively small amount of money allocated, a point the GAO report also made.
