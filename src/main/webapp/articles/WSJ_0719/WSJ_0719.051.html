Economy: Fina's Profit Rose By 29% to Record In Second Quarter

  • Author: Caleb Solomon
  • Date: 07/19/90

American Petrofina Inc. registered record earnings for the second quarter, suggesting that major oil companies with large exposure to gasoline refining will also post gains but that overall industry profits will be mixed. A roughly $3-a-barrel drop in the price of crude last quarter from a year earlier depressed exploration and production results across the board. Natural-gas prices and sales were also down. But that same decline in crude prices boosted refining and marketing profits because oil is the primary feedstock for gasoline.

With many refineries shut down for maintenance last quarter, demand was strong enough to keep gasoline prices fairly steady, said Warren Shimmerlik, a securities analyst with County NatWest USA in New York. As a result, he estimated that refinery margins on gasoline were about $4.60 a barrel, more than $1 greater than the 1989 second period.

Fina, based in Dallas, said net income rose 29% to $53.8 million, or $3.48 a share, from $41.8 million, or $2.71 a share, a year earlier. Revenue declined 1.7% to $832.6 million from $847.4 million. "Lower crude-oil prices, only partially offset by lower product prices, contributed to higher refining margins," said Ron W. Haddock, chief executive officer.

Of the nation's largest oil companies, Chevron Corp. and Texaco Inc. could post record refining and marketing results and year-to-year increases in overall net income, said Bryan Jacoboski, an analyst with PaineWebber Inc. in New York. For the industry, "it's going to be a mixed bag," he said.

Fina said results for petrochemicals, which also use crude as their primary feedstock, were flat. However, analysts expect most large oil companies to report declines in petrochemical earnings. Through the first half of 1989 chemical profits and prices were at or near record highs but have since fallen because of increased supplies.

Early in the third quarter it appears that exploration and production profits will strengthen because crude prices have recovered in recent weeks. But the quarter may be weaker than the second, analysts said, because oil prices may not have recovered enough to offset the expected declines in refining and marketing.

In American Stock Exchange composite trading, American Petrofina closed at $76, down 62.5 cents.
