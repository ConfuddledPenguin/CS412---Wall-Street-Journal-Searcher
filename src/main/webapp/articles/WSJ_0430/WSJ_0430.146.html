Marketing Brief -- Filmstar Inc.: Firm Makes Bid for Assets Of AEG Acquisition Corp.

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/30/90

Filmstar Inc., Los Angeles, said it made a bid to acquire the assets of AEG Acquisition Corp., a closely held entertainment concern that is in bankruptcy-law proceedings, for $10 million in cash and securities. Filmstar, which provides services to independent movie producers, said its bid consists of $7.5 million in cash and the balance in securities.

The bid is for some of AEG's receivables, its domestic or foreign distribution rights to about 117 movies, seven unproduced movies, and interests in about 20 other development projects, as well as four offices and two residential properties that AEG owns.

Alan Saffron, AEG chairman and chief executive officer, said bids have been received from two other parties that he declined to name. One is an all-cash offer, "which the estate favors very much," he said.

AEG has been operating with protection from creditors under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code since July.
