Ferro Profit Slumped 51% in First Quarter As Sales Increased 3%

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/30/90

CLEVELAND -- Ferro Corp. reported a 51% drop in first-quarter net income to $6 million, or 26 cents a share, from $12.4 million, or 60 cents a share, a year earlier, mainly because of problems in Latin America. Sales rose 3%, to $279.7 million from $271.5 million. The company previously said it expected to report an earnings drop of more than 50% for the quarter.

Government measures to fight inflation in Brazil and Argentina severely restricted economic activity in those countries and drove up interest costs, Adolph Posnick, chairman and chief executive officer, told the annual meeting. As a result, Ferro had a first-quarter loss in Latin America, he said.

Latin America is an important market for the producer of specialty coatings, chemicals and plastics, providing 25% of the company's 1989 profit.

In addition, lower U.S. auto and appliance production in the first quarter cut into domestic earnings. And a French chemical plant was shut down from January through March for equipment modifications, he said.

Mr. Posnick said management is optimistic about the outlook for the remainder of the year. However, he said it is too early to forecast whether the company can earn enough in the next three quarters to offset the slow start.

Brazil, which is Ferro's largest Latin American market, will continue to be a drag on profit in the current quarter and probably for several additional quarters, he indicated.

However, domestic demand has improved for a number of Ferro's products, he said, and the company is benefiting from cost-reduction efforts undertaken in the first quarter. The French chemical plant is back in operation, and business prospects in Europe are strong, he said. Europe provided 42% of 1989 profit.

For all of 1989, Ferro earned $49.7 million, or $2.25 a share, on sales of $1.08 billion.
