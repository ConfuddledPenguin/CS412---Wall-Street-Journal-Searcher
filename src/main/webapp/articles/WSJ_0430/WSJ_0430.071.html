Japanese Steel Exports Fall

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/30/90

TOKYO -- Japanese steel exports in the year ended March 31 fell 18% to 19 million metric tons, marking the fifth consecutive annual decline from year-earlier levels, the Japan Iron and Steel Federation said. Exports in fiscal 1990 fell to the lowest level in nearly 20 years, dropping below 20 million tons for the first time since 1970. Steel exports for March followed the downward trend, dropping 26% from a year earlier to 1.5 million metric tons. A metric ton is equal to about 2,204.62 pounds.

Sluggish steel exports reflect consistently strong domestic demand for Japanese steel products, the federation said. As a result, an official said, Japanese steelmakers were preoccupied during the year with meeting the needs of domestic industries, such as shipbuilding, construction and auto manufacturing.

Export prices for the latest fiscal year rose to a historic high of $774 a ton, the official said.
