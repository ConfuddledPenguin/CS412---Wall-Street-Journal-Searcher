International Brief -- Fuji Heavy Industries Ltd.: Payout Cut of 50% Planned; Sales and Profit Woes Cited

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/30/90

Fuji Heavy Industries Ltd., the maker of Subaru cars, said Friday that it plans to cut its year-end dividend in half to two yen (1.3 cents) a share for the fiscal year ended March 31 because of slumping sales and plunging profits. Company officials said the proposal to halve the dividend will be submitted to a general shareholders' meeting scheduled for late June.

In the first half of the fiscal year, Fuji paid an interim dividend of four yen, unchanged from a year earlier.

Officials said the Japanese company has decided to cut the year-end dividend, as both sales and profits sagged from the prior fiscal year. Profits, especially, will show a sharp drop, they said.

During the fiscal year, officials said, car sales both in the domestic market and overseas slipped below the prior year's levels. The setback in the domestic market wasn't especially serious, as demand remained high, but sales in the U.S. showed a conspicuous decline, according to officials.
