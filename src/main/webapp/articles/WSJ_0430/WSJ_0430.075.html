Digital Equipment Staff-Cut Effort Shy of Expectations

  • Author: John R. Wilke
  • Date: 04/30/90

Digital Equipment Corp., burdened by soaring personnel costs in a period of slack sales growth, is moving more slowly than expected with its effort to reduce its huge work force. The big computer maker, based in Maynard, Mass., said it expects 2,000 employees will leave by its fiscal year end on July 1, out of a total of 5,000 who will be offered voluntary severance packages by that time. As of March 31, Digital's work force stood at 125,800.

Digital also indicated that an expected fourth-quarter charge against earnings may not be necessary. If 2,000 employees leave, "then the restructuring charge already announced will be adequate to cover separation costs," a spokesman said. Digital took a $150 million charge in the third quarter for work-force reduction costs, and had warned that further charges were likely in coming quarters.

After the charge, third-quarter net income fell 90% from the year-earlier period, while revenue grew just 4.3%. Those results reflect the weakness of much of the computer industry over the past year.

The disclosure marked the first time Digital outlined the scope of the staff-reduction program. But the numbers fall well short of earlier indications by people inside the company and the forecasts of many analysts, who had predicted cutbacks ranging from 5,000 to 8,000.

"If this is it, Wall Street is likely to be disappointed," Carol Muratore, an analyst with Morgan Stanley & Co., said. "It's no surprise they're moving slowly, but I don't think 2,000 is enough."

Digital shares dropped $1.375, to close at $82, in New York Stock Exchange composite trading Friday.

Digital didn't exactly say that it is finished in making cuts. The spokesman said that results of the voluntary severance program will be evaluated at the end of the fiscal year, and "if there is another program . . . it is likely to be different" from the current program. The current incentive program provides insurance benefits for a year and lump-sum payments of between 40 weeks' and two years' salary, depending on length of service.

The spokesman emphasized that no layoffs are planned. Through the voluntary program, Digital has eliminated jobs at plants in Phoenix, Ariz., and Colorado Springs, and facilities in Massachusetts and New Hampshire.
