American President Posts 1st-Period Loss Totaling $2.5 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/30/90

OAKLAND, Calif. -- American President Cos. said it had a first-quarter loss of $2.5 million, or 21 cents per share. It earned $5.8 million, or 14 cents per share, in the year-earlier period. The shipping and transportation concern said revenue in the quarter ended April 6 increased 3%, to $587 million, from $571 million.

The company attributed the loss to an $11.2 million pretax loss in its transportation operations, which had pretax income of $7.9 million in the 1989 quarter.

Like other transport concerns, American blamed the loss on increased fuel and equipment expenses and a decline in margins at its freight-brokerage operation. The company also cited competitive pressures in its Asia transportation business.

The Asia operations, the company said, were a drain on international revenue, but those operations still posted a 5% increase in the quarter to $405 million, from $399 million, a year earlier. Domestic revenue of $172 million was essentially unchanged from the 1989 quarter, the company said.

In composite trading Friday on the New York Stock Exchange, the company closed at $23.375, down 50 cents.

Earnings also were affected by the sale of 48 acres, resulting in pretax contribution of $6.8 million, compared with $1.4 million in the first quarter of 1989.
