Letters to the Editor: Crunching Zeros

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/30/90

In the March 19 article entitled "Brazil's Collor Stuns Nation With Harsh Economic Plan," you reported that a measure of the plan to deflate the Brazilian economy will be "the creation of the fourth new currency in as many years, the Cruzeiro, to replace the Cruzado Novo. . . ." The cruzeiro will replace the cruzado, but it is not, in fact, "new." Brazil's currency was known as the cruzeiro prior to Feb. 28, 1986. The replacement of the cruzeiro with the cruzado came at a time when severe inflationary and economic pressures were mounting in Brazil. In February 1986, inflation was at a mere 500%, compared with current inflation of more than 1,000%. At that time, the cruzeiro was trading at an official rate of $12,000 and at a black-market rate of $23,000. The Central Bank of Brazil designated the new official rate of the cruzado novo at $12. Perhaps the central bankers were tired of punching so many zeros into their calculators.

John P. Szuch

Rochester, N.Y.
