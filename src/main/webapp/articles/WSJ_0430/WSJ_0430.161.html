Business Brief -- Horten AG: Dividend for '89 Halved; Declining Earnings Cited

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/30/90

Horten AG, West Germany's fourth-largest retailer, halved its 1989 dividend to three marks ($1.78) a share, continuing its widely fluctuating payout pattern of recent years and citing falling profit. The company reported that 1989 profit fell 57% to 15 million marks ($8.9 million) from 35 million marks in 1988, citing costs associated with the reorganization of its Galeria department store operations. It didn't provide any more financial data, though earlier this year, Horten said 1989 sales fell 3.7% to 3.02 billion marks, citing the shedding and restructuring of some operations.

Horten is 51%-owned by Batig Gesellschaft fuer Beteiligungen G.m.b.H., the West German holding company of B.A.T Industries PLC of Britain, and 25%-owned by an investment partnership of Deutsche Bank AG and Commerzbank AG; the rest of the shares are publicly held.
