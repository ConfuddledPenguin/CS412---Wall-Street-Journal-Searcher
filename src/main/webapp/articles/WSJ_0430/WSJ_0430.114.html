Home Owners Is Put In Conservatorship By Thrift Regulators

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/30/90

BOSTON -- Federal thrift regulators took over the Burlington, Mass., headquarters of Home Owners Savings Bank, and a smaller Massachusetts bank disclosed that it is seeking assistance from the Federal Deposit Insurance Corp. The Office of Thrift Supervision placed Home Owners under government conservatorship and appointed Resolution Trust Corp. to manage the institution -- the first such action in Massachusetts since the passage of the thrift bailout law. Conservatorship allows the institution to conduct business. But it is often a prelude to a government-assisted sale or liquidation. With $3.6 billion in assets, Home Owners is one of the biggest thrifts in New England.

On Friday, the Office of Thrift Supervision said it placed Home Owners under conservatorship because it "was operating in an unsafe and unsound manner." The company is a former high-flying institution that built a nationwide mortgage-banking operation in the 1980s. The agency said Home Owners had tangible capital of negative $106.3 million as of Dec. 31.

Meanwhile, Home National Corp. of Milford, Mass., said it expects a $4.8 million first-quarter loss to reduce its shareholders' equity to a deficit of at least $4.1 million. A spokesman for Home National, which has $486 million in assets, said the bank's proposal calls for shareholders to "retain a percentage of the company." But the company wouldn't provide further details. An FDIC spokesman said the agency hasn't made a decision on the request.
