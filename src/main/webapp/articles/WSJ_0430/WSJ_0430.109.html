Semi-Tech, Partner Defy Deal to Buy Unit of Provigo

  • Author: Michael T. Malloy and G. Pierre Goad
  • Date: 04/30/90

International Semi-Tech Microelectronics Inc., based in Markham, Ontario, said it and a Malaysian partner called off an agreement to buy Consumers Distributing Co. for 165 million Canadian dollars (US$142.1 million), because of "serious deterioration" in the Provigo Inc. unit's balance sheet. International Semi-Tech, a fast-growing merchandising, manufacturing and electronics concern, said it and Berjaya Corp. of Malaysia had begun an action in Ontario courts to seek damages from Provigo, a Montreal-based food distributor.

Germain Lecours, chief financial officer of Provigo, said the company "completely denies all the allegations" by International Semi-Tech. He said Provigo regards the sale agreement as still binding.

Provigo agreed in February to sell Consumers Distributing to a partnership that is 19%-owned by International Semi-Tech and 81%-owned by Berjaya, a holding company with interests in manufacturing, retailing and gambling. The purchase was to have closed today.

A spokesman for International Semi-Tech said that after the agreement was reached, the partners received audited statements for the year ended Jan. 27, showing Consumers Distributing's balance sheet and operating results had deteriorated sharply from the unaudited statements previously supplied for the 11 months ended Dec. 31.

"I don't mean a little, I mean tremendous," he said of the difference between the two sets of statements. He said Consumers Distributing took "significant write-offs" in January. He declined to elaborate.

Mr. Lecours of Provigo declined to discuss Consumer Distributing's results. The wholly owned subsidiary is one of three nonfood units that Provigo put up for sale in October, after deciding to concentrate on its core grocery business.

The International Semi-Tech spokesman said the company and its partner "would still love to buy" Consumers Distributing, but for "a lot less" than agreed upon in February. He declined to say what price they had in mind. He said the partners' suit seeks the recovery of legal fees and expenses related to the transaction. He said the amounts hadn't been determined.

Consumers Distributing owns more than 200 stores, mostly catalog showrooms, across Canada. The acquisition was seen as establishing a North American outlet through which the Malaysian tycoon Tan Chee Yion, who controls Berjaya, could distribute consumer goods made by his Malaysia-based companies.

International Semi-Tech beat Berjaya last year in a bidding war for SSMC Inc., which made and distributed Singer brand sewing machines and consumer products. The two companies then made peace, with Berjaya becoming 21% owner of International Semi-Tech's Hong Kong subsidiary, which now owns the Singer name.

Consumers Distributing has been a drag on Provigo's earnings. Provigo sold the subsidiary's 90-store U.S. unit to International Semi-Tech's Hong Kong subsidiary last year for US$40 million.
