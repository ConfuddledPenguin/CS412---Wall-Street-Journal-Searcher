International Brief -- Marubeni Corp.: Oil Refining Joint Venture Planned in East Germany

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/30/90

Marubeni Corp., a major Japanese trading house, said it plans to join Finland's state-owned oil company Neste Oy and the West German steelmaker Thyssen AG in establishing a joint oil refining company in East Germany. The three concerns agreed to conduct a feasibility study with East German authorities, said Marubeni, which hopes to sign a formal accord and establish the joint company next year, and to complete construction of the refinery by 1994.

Initial plans call for creating the joint company to build the refinery near Rostock harbor on the Baltic coast of East Germany at a cost of about $1.2 billion, to process about 120,000 barrels of oil a day. This would be East Germany's first facility for refining oil supplied by the West. East Germany has depended on the Soviet Union for most of its oil needs, but it is planning to diversify its sources of supply, in view of the planned unification with West Germany, Marubeni officials said.

The three companies have obtained approval from East Germany and are seeking cooperation by West Germany in financing the project, officials said.
