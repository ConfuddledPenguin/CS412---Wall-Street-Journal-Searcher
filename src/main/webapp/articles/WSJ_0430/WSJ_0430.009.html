Technology & Medicine: Apple Computer Slashes Prices On Its Portables

  • Author: G. Pascal Zachary
  • Date: 04/30/90

Apple Computer Inc. slashed prices on its sole portable computer, in an effort to lift sluggish sales of the machine. The company cut the suggested price of the portable 15.3% to $5,499. The price of a version of the same model with fewer features was cut 17.2% to $4,799.

Analysts said that the price reduction should improve sales, but that Apple's portables remain more expensive than comparable machines from Compaq Computer Corp. and others. "The discounts aren't enough," said Bruce M. Lupatkin, an analyst at Hambrecht & Quist.

At 16 pounds, Apple's portable is considered too heavy and bulky to win wide acceptance at a time when competitors are offering slimmer, lighter models for one-third less money in some cases. Apple's dealers have reported weaker-than-expected interest in the machine, and some analysts have sharply scaled back sales projections.

"Frankly, the product simply isn't selling well," said Peter Rogers, an analyst at Robertson Stephens & Co.

Apple doesn't disclose sales figures for the portable and won't comment on its plans for this segment of the computer business, which grew an estimated 40% last year, far faster than the growth in desktop computers.

However, industry observers believe Apple is discussing plans to hire an independent firm to manufacture a new portable because it isn't sure it can build a small, light machine inexpensively enough. They say that Apple plans to design the machine and that Sony Corp., which supplies monitors to Apple, is among the possible builders.

Such a move would be a major departure for Apple, which currently manufactures all its computers. The company, however, does rely heavily on a variety of suppliers for such critical parts as monitors, storage devices and keyboards.

An Apple spokeswoman declined to confirm or deny that the company is considering contracting out the manufacture of a future portable. She would say only that discussions are routinely held on various topics with Apple's suppliers, including Sony.

Industry observers say that Apple hasn't decided how it will proceed, and that, in any case, it is working on new portable computers that can complement its current offering.
