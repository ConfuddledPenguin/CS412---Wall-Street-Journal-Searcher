Business Brief -- Midway Airlines Inc.: Net Loss of $22.9 Million Reported for First Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/30/90

Midway Airlines, stung by high fuel costs, reported a deeper-than-expected net loss of $22.9 million, or $2.39 a share, for the first quarter. A year earlier, net income was $2.9 million, or 17 cents a share. Revenue rose 34% to $157.2 million from $117.3 million.

The Chicago-based carrier said its bottom line is still being affected by costs associated with the start-up of a Philadelphia hub of operations. Midway has been expanding service out of Philadelphia since November. Until then, its only hub of operations was at Chicago's Midway Airport.

Last summer, Midway Chairman David Hinson cautioned that it might take a year before the expansion in Philadelphia contributes substantially to the company's earnings.

Although Midway didn't disclose how much its fuel costs rose in the first quarter, many Wall Street analysts are estimating that average prices surged 25% to 30% from a year earlier. For that reason, most airlines have been reporting losses for the first quarter.

Midway's first-quarter operating expenses rose 61% to $183.3 million from $113.7 million the year earlier.
