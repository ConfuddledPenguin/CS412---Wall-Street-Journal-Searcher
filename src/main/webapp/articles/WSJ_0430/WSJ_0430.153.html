International Brief -- Saab-Scania AB: Swedish Auto Maker's Sales Jumped 14% in 1st Period

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/30/90

Saab-Scania AB said sales jumped 14% and pretax profit increased at a somewhat faster rate in the first quarter. But the Saab Automobile AB joint venture with General Motors Corp. had falling sales and continued to struggle with costs at Swedish plants, Saab-Scania Chief Executive Georg Karnsund said.

In a speech at the annual meeting, Mr. Karnsund said Saab-Scania's first-quarter sales rose to 7.4 billion Swedish kronor ($1.21 billion) from 6.5 billion kronor a year earlier. Profit wasn't disclosed, pending the company's financial report for the year's first four months.

But Mr. Karnsund disclosed that operating profit fell at the Scania truck division in the first three months -- despite a 9% increase in sales.

Mr. Karnsund said retail sales of Saab cars skidded 15%, to 24,700 from 29,200, continuing the downward trend of recent years. The unit's operating profit fell as a result of lower volume, rapid cost increases and high interest rates prevailing in Sweden, he added.

Directors elected Anders Scharp, chief executive officer at Swedish appliance maker AB Electrolux, Saab-Scania's chairman, succeeding Sten Gustafssons.
