Futures Trading in London And New York Is Cut Short

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/30/90

NEW YORK -- Trading in London and New York futures markets was cut short Friday because of a communications breakdown in New York and by a bomb scare on one London exchange. Late Friday, a spokesman for the Commodity Exchange Center, which directs operations of the five futures exchanges here, said that technicians had "sidestepped an electric short in the distribution system. We have not yet solved the problem but we are operational at the moment."

He said the problem was expected to be corrected by the opening of trading today. Computers were functioning properly, but the problem was in the network that sends information out to customers' price-quotation tickers, he said.

The five exchanges affected here are the Commodity Exchange, the Coffee, Sugar & Cocoa Exchange, the New York Cotton Exchange, the New York Mercantile Exchange and the New York Futures Exchange. All ceased trading about 90 minutes earlier than usual.

On the London Futures and Options Exchange, trading had hardly begun when it was stopped by a bomb scare. The exchange reopened at 2:30 p.m. London time, to have a closing call. The exchange trades coffee, sugar, cocoa and gas (heating) oil futures.

New York analysts and dealers said it was the first time they can recall trading being disrupted in both New York and London futures markets on the same day.
