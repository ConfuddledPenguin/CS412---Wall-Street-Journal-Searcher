Global Airline Fares Rose an Average 6% In Year, ICAO Says

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/30/90

MONTREAL -- International passenger airline fares rose an average 6% in the 12 months ended Sept. 30, 1989, the International Civil Aviation Organization said. The Montreal-based international agency said its estimates are for normal economy fares priced in local currencies. Expressed in U.S. dollars, international fares rose 1%.

ICAO said that passenger fares on international routes rose 10% within the Asia/Pacific region, 6% on North Atlantic routes, 7% within Europe, and 9% within North America. ICAO said the average normal economy fare for an international route of 3,300 kilometers was $548.

ICAO said international general cargo rates for shipments under 45 kilograms rose an average 2% in the period. ICAO said general cargo rates on international routes rose 6% within the Asia/Pacific region, 1% on North Atlantic routes, and 9% within North America. ICAO said the average cargo rate on an international route of 3,900 kilometers was $4.64 per kilogram for shipments under 45 kilograms.

Separately, ICAO said it estimates that the world's scheduled airlines as a group earned operating profit of 4.7% of total operating revenues in 1989, compared with 6.1% of revenues in 1988.

ICAO said it estimates operating revenues for scheduled airlines were $182.5 billion last year, up 10% from $166.2 billion in 1988. ICAO statistics exclude domestic airline operations in the Soviet Union.
