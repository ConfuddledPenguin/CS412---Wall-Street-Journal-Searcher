Wang Labs Posts $146.6 Million Loss For Third Quarter

  • Author: William M. Bulkeley
  • Date: 04/30/90

Wang Laboratories Inc. reported a loss for its fiscal third quarter, ended March 31, of $146.6 million, or 89 cents a share, reflecting a number of one-time charges and continued weakness in its computer sales. In the year-earlier quarter, the Lowell, Mass., concern reported a loss of $63.7 million, or 39 cents a share. Wang's third-quarter revenue fell 19% to $583.1 million from $721.4 million.

Wang's B stock fell 25 cents to $4.50 Friday in American Stock Exchange composite trading.

Richard Miller, chairman and president, said that the larger-than-expected setback reflected "increasingly competitive industry conditions" and an internal reorganization that disrupted sales and marketing during January and February.

The one-time costs are related to the sale of certain subsidiaries to raise cash and reduce debt. Wang said it had completed the previously announced plan to sell its international lease financing operations to General Electric Capital Corp., a unit of General Electric Co., for $240 million. It also has agreed to sell two smaller units. It said losses on the sales of the discontinued operations amounted to $54.7 million.

Wang said that on a pretax basis, after eliminating interest costs, its operating loss for the period was $57.4 million compared with $36.5 million in the year-earlier period.

Mr. Miller said that completion of the subsidiary sales will generate $285 million in cash, allowing the company to cut bank debt to about $65 million, and eliminating $55 million in annual interest payments compared with last year. He added that the company is "progressing in its turnaround efforts," and hopes for "operating profitability in the year beginning July 1."

Analysts said the widening sales decline is worrisome. Barry Willman, an analyst with Sanford C. Bernstein & Co., said "while all the expense (cutting) steps are necessary, the revenue will need to be stabilized in order to restore sustained profitability."

For the nine months, Wang reported a loss of $219.2 million, or $1.33 a share, widened from a loss of $49.6 million, or 30 cents a share. Revenue fell 15% to $1.82 billion from $2.13 billion.
