New Charges Dismissed Against Defense Consultant

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/30/90

WASHINGTON -- A federal judge dismissed conspiracy and fraud charges against Thomas Muldoon, a defense-industry consultant already convicted on separate charges stemming from his role in the Pentagon bribery and procurement scandal. Judge Albert Bryan in Alexandria, Va., dismissed last month's indictment of Mr. Muldoon for allegedly funneling sensitive bid-related information to major defense contractors. In his ruling, Judge Bryan agreed with defense motions that prosecutors may have unfairly singled out Mr. Muldoon for tough treatment by seeking a second round of felony charges against him.

Mr. Muldoon, who has consistently refused to cooperate with prosecutors, is appealing his earlier conviction and 27-month prison sentence on charges of scheming to bribe a Navy acquisition official.

Prosecutors have described Mr. Muldoon as an important player in the procurement scandal, dubbed "Operation Ill Wind." The government contends that the second indictment was proper because it dealt with a separate conspiracy and a different set of government and industry officials than those mentioned in Mr. Muldoon's earlier indictment.
