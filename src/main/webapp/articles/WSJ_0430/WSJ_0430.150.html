International Brief -- Grand Metropolitan PLC: British Commission to Study Firm's Accord With Elders

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/30/90

Britain's secretary of state for trade and industry ordered a review of the proposed tie-up between Grand Metropolitan PLC and Elders IXL Ltd.'s brewing and retail operations, expressing concern there may be "effects on competition in the U.K. market for beer in terms of increased concentration. . . ." The Monopolies and Mergers Commission, the competition watchdog charged with making such investigations, was given until Aug. 21 to complete the review.

Under an elaborate agreement between the two companies reached in March, Australian-based Elders is to pay #366 million ($597.9 million) for Grand Met's breweries, which include local British beer brands Websters, Trumans, Ruddles, Wilson and Ushers. Elders is also due to receive Grand Met's local brewing and distribution rights to several non-British brands, including Carlsberg and Budweiser.

At the same time, Grand Met, a British group that has extensive interests in food products and restaurants as well as brewing, agreed to merge its U.K. public houses (pubs) with those controlled by Elders.
