Rain Damages Australia Cotton

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/30/90

CANBERRA -- Australia's cotton crop has been damaged by widespread rains that have flooded much of eastern Australia. A spokesman for the Australian Bureau of Agricutural and Resource Economics said the quality of cotton will have been spoiled by the heavy rains and this will lower export returns.

In December, the bureau estimated that Australia's cotton exports would total 508 million Australian dollars (US$383.2 million) for the year ending June 30.
