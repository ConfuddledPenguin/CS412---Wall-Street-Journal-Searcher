REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: Oakland Rejoins U.S.

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/30/90

Over 160 cities and towns declared themselves "nuclear-free zones" during the 1980s, but only Oakland, Calif., tried to restrict the transit of nuclear material and outlawed nuclear-weapons work within its borders. One physics consultant was told he would have to move out of town to comply. A federal judge has now struck down the law, saying it interfered with the federal government's authority over national defense. Oakland officials may appeal, but it looks as if they are back under the U.S. nuclear umbrella whether they like it or not.
