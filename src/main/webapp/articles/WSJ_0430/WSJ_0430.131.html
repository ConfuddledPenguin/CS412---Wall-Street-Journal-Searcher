High-Tech Outlays of U.S. To Rise 11% in Fiscal '91

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/30/90

WASHINGTON -- The federal government will spend $20.4 billion on computers, software and telecommunications services in fiscal 1991, up 11% from the current fiscal year, said Federal Sources Inc., a Vienna, Va., market-research firm that tracks government spending. Federal Sources said increased spending at the National Aeronautics and Space Administration, the Federal Bureau of Investigation, the Internal Revenue Services and the Defense Department accounted for much of the expected overall increase. Civilian agencies represent 53% of the anticipated spending, while the Pentagon accounts for the rest, the consulting company estimated. The federal government typically represents about 10% of the entire market for so-called information technology.

NASA is expected to increase its computer and telecommunications spending to get ready for the deluge of space-based data, including a project to study the Earth from space. The FBI is increasing spending to update its computerized criminal records. The IRS is enhancing several computer systems that track taxpayer compliance.

Federal Sources said that Unisys Corp. was the leading information-technology vendor in the federal market during fiscal 1989, with sales of $590.1 million. American Telephone & Telegraph Co. was second with $434.6 million, followed by International Business Machines Corp. with $376.8 million.
