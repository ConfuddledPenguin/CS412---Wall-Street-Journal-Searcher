International: Foreign Exchange Curbs Are Eliminated in Italy

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 04/30/90

MILAN, Italy -- Foreign Trade Minister Renato Ruggiero said the free flow of capital to and from Italy will help force the Italian government to reduce the public-sector deficit. Mr. Ruggiero signed a decree Friday to lift on May 14 all of Italy's remaining foreign exchange restrictions, ahead of the June 30 deadline of the European Community to abolish barriers to crossborder capital flows.

He likened Friday's move to Italy's decision in 1979 to join the European Monetary System (EMS). "The entrance of our currency in the EMS created the premise for winning the battle against inflation and to give competitiveness to our country that wasn't based on currency devaluations," he said in a telephone interview from Rome.

The measure will allow Italians to open foreign bank accounts, hold foreign currency in domestic accounts and borrow foreign currency from domestic banks. Mr. Ruggiero said the foreign exchange liberalization will be followed by measures to bring savings taxes in line with those of Italy's European partners.
