Mercedes-Benz U.S. Recall

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/30/90

MONTVALE, N.J. -- Mercedes-Benz of North America said it is recalling 1,584 of its 300-Class luxury cars to replace an electrical part in the engine cooling fan. The auto maker, a unit of Daimler-Benz AG, Stuttgart, West Germany, said heat from the suspect part could cause its plastic cover to melt and possibly burn when the auxiliary fan is working at low speed. Three incidents of melting have been reported, none involving accidents or injuries.

The cars being recalled are certain 1990-model 300E 2.6, 300E and 300E 4Matic sedans, 300TE and 300TE 4Matic station wagons and 300CE coupes.

Mercedes-Benz said dealers will replace the problem part with one that has a metal cover at no cost to customers.
