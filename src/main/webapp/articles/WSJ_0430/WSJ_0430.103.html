Staloff Resigns From Posts At Commodity Exchange

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/30/90

NEW YORK -- Arnold Staloff, president and chief executive officer of the Commodity Exchange, resigned on Friday after only nine months on the job. His resignation is effective May 1. The Comex is the nation's fourth-largest futures exchange and the biggest precious-metals marketplace. Mr. Staloff was brought in from the Philadelphia Stock Exchange with considerable fanfare because of his reputation as a developer of new financial products such as the first foreign-currency options and a computerized trading system.

The Comex has failed to keep up with rival exchanges in Chicago in the introduction of new futures products.
