Business Brief -- Gentner Electronics Corp.: Loss Is Posted for Quarter, Expected for Fiscal 1990

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/30/90

Gentner Electronics Corp. reported a loss of $241,034, or eight cents a share, in its fiscal third quarter and said it expects to report a loss for all of fiscal 1990. In the 1989 quarter, the maker of electronic audio equipment had net income of $179,622, or six cents a share. Revenue in the quarter ended March 31 declined 3% to $1.47 million from $1.51 million.

During the quarter, the company said spending on research and development peaked. The substantial costs in developing new products and slower-than-expected growth in existing product sales were primary factors in the quarterly loss, the company said.

Three products designed to enhance the company's existing line of sound equipment for use at FM radio stations and a new line of telephone products are in the final stages of development. The company said it expects to begin shipping those products "in the near future."

For the nine months, Salt Lake City-based Gentner had a loss of $274,000, or nine cents a share, compared with year-earlier net of $333,000, or 11 cents a share. Revenue increased 14% to $4.1 million from $3.6 million.
