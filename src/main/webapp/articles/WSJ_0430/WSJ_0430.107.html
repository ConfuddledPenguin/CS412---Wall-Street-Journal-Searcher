NESB Sets Pact on Default

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/30/90

NEW LONDON, Conn. -- NESB Corp., a banking concern, said it signed a letter of intent with its insurance company lenders on a plan to correct its default of covenants in a $50 million loan agreement. The plan, which needs approval by the NESB board and bank regulators, consists of early payments of interest and the pledging of certain bank assets to the insurance companies.

The company has said its first-quarter loss put it in default of covenants of a $50 million long-term debt agreement with a group of insurance companies. The money was used to finance NESB's $48 million acquisition of OmniBank. In February, OmniBank was merged with New England Savings Banks, NESB's only subsidiary.
