Noranda Net Fell 63% On 7.9% Revenue Gain During First Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/30/90

TORONTO -- Noranda Inc. reported that first-quarter earnings fell 63% to 59 million Canadian dollars ($50.8 million), or 26 Canadian cents (22.4 cents) a share. The results compare with profit of C$158 million, or 82 Canadian cents, a year ago. Revenue rose 7.9% to C$2.45 billion from C$2.27 billion.

The natural resource company said the high value of the Canadian dollar against the U.S. dollar, as well as high interest rates in the latest quarter, reduced earnings by about C$12 million.

The company reported reduced profits in its mining, forest and manufacturing operations, though profits in its oil and gas operations rose as a result of higher oil prices.

For the balance of the year, Noranda said earnings will likely continue in line with those reported for the first quarter. Alfred Powis, chairman and chief executive officer, said he expects 1990 earnings of about C$1.05 a share, down from 1989 net of C$2.19.

Mr. Powis said earnings will be depressed this year by lower prices for nearly all of Noranda's products.

On the Toronto Stock Exchange Friday, Noranda closed at C$18.375, down 25 Canadian cents.
