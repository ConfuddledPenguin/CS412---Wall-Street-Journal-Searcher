Business Brief -- MicroAge Inc.: Olivetti Holding N.V. Sells Its 30% Stake in Franchiser

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/30/90

MicroAge Inc., Tempe, Ariz., said Olivetti Holding N.V. sold its 30% stake, or 1,298,854 shares, in MicroAge through several private transactions. In an announcement made after the stock market closed Friday, the microcomputer store franchiser said the stake was sold at an average of $12 a share. MicroAge closed unchanged Friday at $11.50.

The company said 880,000 of the shares sold were purchased by Banstock Co. which MicroAge identified as a "privately held Pacific Rim investor" and 83,333 shares were purchased by Fred Israel, a Washington, D.C., investor.

MicroAge said it loaned its employee stock ownership plan funds to buy 208,333 of the shares, boosting the ESOP's holding to 313,551 of the company's common shares. MicroAge bought back the remaining 127,188 shares.

The transactions leave MicroAge with 4,182,190 shares outstanding. The company said Olivetti also surrendered its right to royalties on sales by European franchisees.
