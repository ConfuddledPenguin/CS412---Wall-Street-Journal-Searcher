Cirrus Expects to Report Profit Soared In Its 4th Period on Strong Chip Sales

  • Author: Stephen Kreider Yoder
  • Date: 04/30/90

Cirrus Logic Inc., a fast-growing semiconductor start-up concern, expects to report net income more than doubled in its fiscal fourth quarter, adding to a series of improved results from computer chip companies. Cirrus expects today to report net of $5 million to $5.5 million, or 33 cents to 34 cents a share, for the fourth quarter ended March 31, compared with the year-earlier $1.9 million, or 15 cents a share, said Michael Hackworth, Cirrus's president and chief executive officer.

The Milpitas, Calif., chip company was expected by analysts to report net of 30 cents to 31 cents a share for the quarter, Mr. Hackworth said.

Mr. Hackworth attributed the favorable results to growing strength in chip demand among office-automation equipment makers that are Cirrus's main customers. Cirrus expects to post fourth-quarter sales in the range of $27.5 million to $28.5 million, compared with $12.1 million in the year-earlier quarter, Mr. Hackworth said.

"The overall office-automation market was stronger than we had anticipated," he said. "Last year everyone was walking on eggs about 1990. We're feeling much more bullish about the market than we did five months ago."

For the year ended March 31, Cirrus expects to post net of $15 million to $15.5 million, or $1.02 to $1.03 a share, compared with $4 million, or 32 cents a share, last year. Cirrus expects to report revenue in the year of $84 million to $85 million, up from $27.5 million to $28.5 million a year earlier, he said. Cirrus had about $40 million in cash and equivalents at the quarter's end, he said.

Mr. Hackworth attributed the earnings to better-than-expected sales of its new chips used in laptop-computer screens and in computer disk drives. Cirrus's improved profits are a welcome boost for the five-year-old company, which went public last June.

And Cirrus's earnings boost comes after several big chip makers -- including Intel Corp., LSI Logic Corp. and Cypress Semiconductor Corp. -- announced better-than-expected quarters. The industry's leading indicator, the so-called book-to-bill ratio, is at its highest level since July 1988.

Cirrus's orders in the fourth quarter were strong and presage a strong first quarter, Mr. Hackworth said. He said he was comfortable with analysts' estimates that the company would have net of about $4 million, or 24 cents a share, in the quarter ended June 30, compared with net of $2.5 million, or 14 cents a share, a year earlier. Cirrus is comfortable with revenue estimates for the quarter of $29 million to $30 million, up from $14.8 million last year, he said.

Mr. Hackworth said the company plans to add 10 to 20 new chips to its lineup, which now includes about 40 chips.
