Jamaican Debt Is Rescheduled

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/30/90

PARIS -- The Paris Club of creditor governments said members agreed to reschedule payments of debt owed to them by Jamaica, but under its policy, didn't disclose the sum. The group said the rescheduled payments will be made over 10 years, with a five-year grace period. The Paris Club said Canada, France, West Germany, Italy, Japan, the Netherlands, Norway, Britain and the U.S. participated in the negotiations. Belgium, Finland, Spain and Sweden sent observers. The debt relief move follows a March 23 standby arrangement with the International Monetary Fund.
