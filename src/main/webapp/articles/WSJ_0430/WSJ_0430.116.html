Norton Expects Net To Triple This Year SEC Filing Shows

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/30/90

WASHINGTON -- Norton Co. expects net income to more than triple in 1990 to $346.1 million as sales increase 4.6% to $1.6 billion, according to a filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission. The filing was made by Cie. de Saint Gobain S.A. in connection with the French glassmaker's $1.9 billion, or $90 a share, tender offer for all of Norton's shares outstanding. Norton, a Worchester, Mass., maker of abrasives, ceramics and other products, agreed last week to be acquired by Saint-Gobain.

The filing said Norton's projected 1990 net income figure includes, among other things, a $249 million gain on the sale of its Eastman Christensen operation. For 1989, Norton had net of $105.1 million, or $5.02 a share, on sales of $1.53 billion.

To fund the acquisition, Saint-Gobain said it plans to obtain $900 million through borrowings under a 1987 credit facility with banks including Banque Nationale de Paris. A further $600 million will be obtained through a 1988 facility with banks including Chemical Bank, while the remainder of the $1.9 billion purchase price will be taken from its general corporate funds, the company said.

Saint-Gobain said it has agreed to pay its financial adviser, Lazard Freres & Co., $6 million in connection with the offer.
