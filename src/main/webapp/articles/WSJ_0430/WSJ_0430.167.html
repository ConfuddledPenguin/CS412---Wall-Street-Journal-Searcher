Xerox Is Asking Suppliers To Refund 3% on '89 Bills

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/30/90

STAMFORD, Conn. -- Xerox Corp., in an unusual approach to cutting costs, essentially asked its key suppliers to refund 3% of the amount they billed the company during 1989. Xerox also asked the suppliers to cut their prices 10% by 1991.

The requests, which Xerox said were unrelated to its record $254 million first-quarter loss, came as part of the company's continuing effort to boost efficiency in its business-equipment operation. Total costs and expenses in that operation rose 3% in the first quarter from the year before.

A Xerox spokesman said suppliers who don't comply won't necessarily face any consequences. "It isn't a demand," he said. "It's a request, a suggestion. Should a supplier decline to agree with that suggestion, that wouldn't be a determining factor in our relationship."

Xerox said it asked 350 suppliers world-wide -- mostly producers of parts for Xerox's business machines -- to reduce their 1990 billings by an amount equivalent to 3% of their 1989 billings. The spokesman said he had no estimate of the saving Xerox expects from the move, of which it notified suppliers about three weeks ago.
