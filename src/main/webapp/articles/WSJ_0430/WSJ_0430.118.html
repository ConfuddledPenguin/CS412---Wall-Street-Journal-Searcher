Bank of Boston Is Put On S&P CreditWatch In Wake of Earnings

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/30/90

NEW YORK -- Standard & Poor's Corp. placed certain debt and preferred stock of Bank of Boston Corp. on CreditWatch with negative implications, following the company's report Thursday of a 50% drop in first-quarter earnings. S&P said the first-quarter results "indicate that asset quality continues to deteriorate, resulting in risk-adjusted capital levels that are low relative to peers."

The ratings under review include those for the company's triple-B-plus senior debt, triple-B subordinated debt, triple-B-minus preferred stock, and single-A-2 commercial paper. Also on CreditWatch are First National Bank of Boston's single-A-minus/single-A-2 letter of credit-backed issues and certificates of deposit, and single-A-2 commercial paper programs of units Boston Australia Ltd. and Bank of Boston Canada, which are fully guaranteed by First National Bank of Boston.

About $800 million of debt is affected.

The ratings will remain on CreditWatch "until S&P meets with Bank of Boston's management to discuss asset quality and capital issues," the rating agency said. The bank's senior debt rating is expected to remain in investment grade, "even if an adverse rating action is made subsequent to the management discussions," S&P said.

A Bank of Boston spokesman said the company is "obviously disappointed that Standard & Poor's has put us on their watch list. We expect to be meeting with the agency very soon to discuss the details of our banking operations and help alleviate their concerns."
