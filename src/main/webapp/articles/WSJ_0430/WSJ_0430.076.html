East German Investment Plan

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/30/90

FRANKFURT -- East Germany is considering selling half of its nationalized industries to its own citizens through the distribution of investment certificates, a key economic official said. Peter Moreth, head of the East German trustee agency for public property, told the West German daily Die Welt that only the remaining half of the industrial holdings will be offered to foreign investors. That 50% share open to foreigners also includes all joint ventures and cooperation pacts signed to date, he said.

The trustee agency was set up earlier this year to take over so-called publicly owned companies, and privatize them at a later point.
