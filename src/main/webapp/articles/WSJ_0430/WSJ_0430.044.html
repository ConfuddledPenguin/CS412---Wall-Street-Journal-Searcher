U.S. Judge Sentences Stockbroker, Manager To Prison for Fraud

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/30/90

LOS ANGELES -- A federal court judge here sentenced a stockbroker and an investment manager to prison after their conviction by a jury last month on 20 counts each of securities, wire and bank fraud. Gina Loren, 53 years old, was sentenced to six years in prison, followed by five years of probation. Daniel Burkhart, 43, was sentenced to four years in prison, followed by five years of probation, said James R. Asperger, an assistant U.S. attorney who prosecuted the case.

The two convicts collected about $4.1 million from 44 victims who were told that the funds would be held in a trust secured by U.S. Treasury securities. Instead, the funds were deposited -- and then withdrawn -- from an account at Bateman Eichler, Hill Richards Inc. in Ventura, Calif., where Mr. Burkhart, who lives in Camarillo, Calif., was employed as a stockbroker.

Ms. Loren, who resides in Los Angeles and is also known as Grace Horner, diverted large amounts of the funds to purchase several luxury cars and a $1.6 million home in Orange, Calif. Mr. Burkhart received about $66,000 in payments over a three-month period for assisting Ms. Loren.

Officers at the brokerage firm alerted federal authorities when they learned of the swindle, the U.S. attorney said. The victims included a group of Catholic nuns in South Dakota and retired military personnel.

Another participant in the scheme, Charles J. Lusin, 57, previously pleaded guilty to one felony count of mail fraud for assisting Ms. Loren by preparing certain documents. He was sentenced March 26 to three years of probation and 300 hours of community service.

In an interview Friday, Mr. Lusin said he also voluntarily gave up his license to practice law in California. He is now unemployed and living in Temecula, Calif. An attorney for Mr. Burkhart declined to comment on behalf of his client. Ms. Loren and her attorney couldn't be reached.
