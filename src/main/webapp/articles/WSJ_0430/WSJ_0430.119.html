International: Stora Kopparbergs Obtains 86% of Feldmuehle Nobel

  • Author:
  • Date: 04/30/90

STOCKHOLM -- Sweden's Stora Kopparbergs Bergslags AB ended weeks of intensive bargaining by capturing West German pulp-and-paper giant Feldmuehle Nobel AG in the biggest international takeover to date by a Scandinavian company. Stora and its two biggest owners -- Wallenberg group holding companies AB Investor and AB Providentia -- said they acquired 86% of Feldmuehle's shares outstanding and intend to seek full control through a public tender offer.

Stora and its allies paid an average price of 567 marks ($337) a share, valuing the whole of Feldmuehle at 3.97 billion marks ($2.36 billion). If the offer succeeds, Stora expects to keep 75% of Feldmuehle with Investor and Providentia holding 25%.

The transaction is subject to review by West Germany's antitrust agency. But Stora's legal advisers said they don't expect objections.

The acquisition would cement Stora's position as Europe's biggest pulp-and-paper concern -- and vault the Swedish concern to a position of global leadership. Stora claims the expanded concern would be the world's biggest producer of printing paper and market pulp -- and No. 4 world-wide in overall pulp and paper capacity, trailing the U.S.'s Stone Container Corp., but roughly equal with two other U.S. concerns, Georgia-Pacific Corp. and International Paper Co.

"Consolidation among domestic forestry companies is nearly finished in Sweden, Finland and the U.S.," said Stora's chief executive officer, Bo Berggren. "Now we're seeing the same process get under way on a global scale."
