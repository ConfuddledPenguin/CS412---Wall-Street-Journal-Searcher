Broad Inc. Net Rose 22% in Second Period To Total $9.9 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/30/90

LOS ANGELES -- Broad Inc. said fiscal second-quarter net income rose 22% to $9.9 million, or 30 cents a share, as the first profits from Broad's acquisition of Integrated Resources Inc.'s core financial services businesses boosted profit. Eli Broad, chairman and chief executive officer, said he expects net for the year ending Sept. 30 to be in line with analysts' estimates of $1.20 a share.

Mr. Broad based his projection on sharply reduced overhead expenses as a result of the company's consolidating and computerizing its back-office operations here and a higher level of both investment and fee income on increased assets. They include about $4 billion of annuity reserves and assets under management acquired from Integrated in January.

"We're looking forward to continued revenue and profit increases as all the things we've done in the last two years come on line," said Mr. Broad. "We're just starting to get the benefits."

The back-office operations will be completely computerized by September, he said. Broad has spent about $15 million in the past 18 months to install laser-imaging equipment that "eliminates over 90% of the paper we have to handle."

The company's earnings in the period ended March 31 exceeded those of any quarter since Broad was transformed into a financial-service concern in March 1989 through a restructuring of the former Kaufman & Broad Inc. In 1989's second quarter, Broad had net of $8.1 million, or 25 cents a share.

Broad primarily sells tax-deferred, single premium annuities. Annuity buyers pay the company a lump sum at purchase in return for the company's repayment of principal plus interest, usually at a much later date.

The company's operating profit before investment losses rose 56%, to $13.1 million from $8.4 million; investment losses were about $5.1 million, compared with $459,000. The year-ago quarter included a $675,000 gain on the corporate restructuring.

In the first half, net was $13.2 million, or 40 cents a share, compared with a net loss of $11.2 million, or 37 cents a share, a year earlier. The earlier result included $27 million in charges related to the restructuring.
