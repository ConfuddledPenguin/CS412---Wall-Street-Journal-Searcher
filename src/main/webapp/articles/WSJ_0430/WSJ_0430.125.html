Business Brief -- Revco D.C. Inc.: Chain Will Sell 221 Stores To Reliable Holdings Corp.

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/30/90

Revco D.C. Inc. said in Twinsburg, Ohio, that it agreed to sell 221 of its stores to Reliable Holdings Corp., which was formed by Acadia Partners Limited Partnership and the management of Reliable Drug Stores Inc. Terms weren't disclosed. The drugstore chain announced in January that it would seek buyers for 712 of its 1,873 stores as part of a corporate reorganization. The company filed for bankruptcy court protection in July 1988.

The transaction with Reliable Holdings is subject to approval by a U.S. bankruptcy court. If the plan is approved, Revco would sell all its stores in Illinois, Iowa, Indiana, Kansas, Kentucky, Michigan, Missouri and western Tennessee.

Acadia Partners is a Fort Worth, Texas-based partnership that includes Robert M. Bass. Last year a group led by the Texas billionaire made an unsuccessful $925 million offer for all of Revco.

Reliable Drug Stores is a drugstore chain based in Indianapolis.
