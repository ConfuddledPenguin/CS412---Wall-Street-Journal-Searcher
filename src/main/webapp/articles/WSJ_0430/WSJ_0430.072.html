Letters to the Editor: Divorce Mediation: `Shining and Stinking'

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/30/90

To suggest ("Warring Couples Shun Divorce Mediators and Opt to Battle It Out in Court Instead," March 27) that divorce mediation has been shot down by lawyers because it represents "a threat to their livelihoods" is unfair. Generally, divorce lawyers in America are operating on "overload" and would welcome any process that could relieve them of some of their burdens and, at the same time, afford a vent for the parties' anger and frustration. Divorce mediation reminds one of John Randolph of Roanoke's comment, "It is like rotten mackerel by moonlight. It shines and it stinks." Divorce mediation is terribly attractive and seductive in concept, glistening out there on an otherwise angry landscape of discontent, but as you get closer it turns out to be somewhat odoriferous, or, at least, not as attractive as initially perceived.

Unfortunately, the mediators are uneven in training and often inexpert in application of their skills. Ofttimes they create more problems than they solve. While I suppose it is true that "there is a great deal of competition for the family law dollar," one hopes that the money will be spent for solutions that are more effective in operation albeit less appealing in presentation.

Raoul Lionel Felder

New York
