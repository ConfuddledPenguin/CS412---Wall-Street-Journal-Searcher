Letters to the Editor: `Social Entrepreneurs' Seek to Help Homeless

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/30/90

In an April 10 editorial-page article "What We Really Know About the Homeless," Randall K. Filer, a researcher, calls for more research. It should come as no surprise, then, that I, a program officer for a major foundation, would call for more programs. Mr. Filer does an excellent and thoroughly readable job of clarifying what we do and don't know about homelessness. I too am frustrated by the endless debates over numbers and causes, particularly as they relate to administration housing policies. What Mr. Filer fails to do is highlight any of the positive experiments that are being undertaken to help the nation solve the problem of homelessness. The Robert Wood Johnson Foundation and the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) have joined in a multimillion-dollar national demonstration program that will, we hope, show cities how they can rescue young homeless families with complex problems from permanent membership in the underclass.

As Mr. Filer points out, the members of this group, mostly single mothers with children under age five, are the most entitled in America, but they do not get the benefits for which they are eligible. We believe this is so because the entitlement system is unnavigable for most anybody, let alone a teen-age mother with poor skills and a history of mental illness and/or substance abuse. Also responsible is our failure to combine housing resources for these families with health and counseling services they need in order to cope.

There are many "social entrepreneurs" across the country trying similar experiments. This is just one of many promising programs now being undertaken to help the homeless, and, Mr. Filer will be happy to know, it is one that will be independently evaluated by researchers at Vanderbilt so that we can learn more about what works.

Stephen A. Somers

Senior Program Officer

Robert Wood Johnson Foundation

Princeton, N.J.
