ConAgra Unit Forms Barge Joint Venture With Japanese Firm

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/30/90

OMAHA, Neb. -- ConAgra Inc. said its ConAgra Grain Cos. unit formed a joint venture with Consolidated Grain & Barge Co., under which the two concerns will merge their barge and tow-boat operations. ConAgra, a food company, said it will hold an 80% stake in the newly formed company, Superior Barge Lines Inc. The new company will combine ConAgra's 570 barges, which transport grain from river terminals in the middle of the U.S. to export grain elevators, with Consolidated Grain's approximately 550 barges. Financial terms weren't disclosed.

For ConAgra, the deal lifts its river-barge fleet from a midsized concern to among the largest. For Consolidated -- which was acquired in 1988 by Japanese trading house C. Itoh & Co. and Zen-Noh, a Japanese farm co-operative -- the transaction appears designed to allay legal headaches over ownership of the barge operations. A statute known as the Jones Act sharply constrains foreign ownership of marine assets, and some Consolidated Grain competitors had raised that issue following the company's acquisition by the Japanese buyers.

Consolidated Grain also operates some 40 U.S. grain terminals; those operations aren't involved in the plan with ConAgra. Consolidated Grain officials weren't available to comment.
