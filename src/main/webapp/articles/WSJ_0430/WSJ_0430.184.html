Aetna's Profit Rose 3.6% in 1st Quarter To $179.8 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/30/90

HARTFORD, Conn. -- Aetna Life & Casualty Co.'s first-quarter earnings rose 3.6% to $179.8 million, or $1.61 a share, reflecting improved results in its group insurance and pension business. Yet results for the company's personal auto insurance business were disappointing. James T. Lynn, Aetna's chairman, blamed high loss costs and inadequate pricing. Its auto/homeowners business posted earnings of $1 million, including a net realized capital gains of $2 million, compared with a loss of $4 million in the year-ago quarter.

At the company's annual meeting Friday, Ronald E. Compton, Aetna's president, outlined various steps the insurer is taking to improve profitability of its personal auto insurance business.

Aetna has reduced agents' commissions on personal auto insurance in many states. To lower expenses, it has instituted stronger underwriting standards and improved its claims-handling practices.

Mr. Compton said the company will invest its capital in the auto insurance business "only in those states where the regulatory and political climates permit us to earn adequate returns on our investments." Already the company has cut back some 15% to 20% in states where the auto insurance business is considered unprofitable.

Earnings for Aetna's employee benefits group, which includes group insurance and pensions, rose to $77 million, from $42 million, in the 1989 first quarter.

Aetna's first-quarter results included net realized capital gains of $20 million, or 18 cents a share, compared with net gains of $21 million a year ago. Capital gains of $23 million from the sale of the company's Australian insurance operations were also included in first-quarter results. Revenue rose 4.3% to $4.8 billion.
