International: Import Contracts Signed By Big Japan Firms Soar

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 04/30/90

TOKYO -- Import contracts signed by Japan's major trading companies in March jumped a record 53.8% from a year earlier to 2.874 trillion yen ($18.05 billion), the Japan Foreign Trade Council said Friday. Export contracts climbed 39.9% to 3.406 trillion yen, posting the strongest year-on-year advance since March 1982.

A council official attributed the rise in export contracts to sharp increases in plant and machinery export contracts during March.

A rise in fuel import contracts paced the overall advance in the import category, with the council citing a weakening of the oil market as a factor prompting an increase in contracts signed.

The council's monthly reports are based on contract statistics provided by 18 major Japanese trading companies, and are widely considered to be short-term indicators of overall trade trends. The companies furnishing contract statistics account for roughly half of Japan's general exports and about two-thirds of imports.

In February, export contracts rose 1.2% from the year before, the first increase in three months. Imports edged up 0.3%, the smallest rise in four months.

For fiscal 1989, export contracts rose 10.1% to 14.568 trillion yen, while import contracts climbed 20.4% to 17.088 trillion yen.
