Who's News: PHM Corp. Selects Ex-Chrysler Executive As Chief Executive

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/30/90

BLOOMFIELD HILLS, Mich. -- PHM Corp. said it hired former top Chrysler Corp. executive Harold K. Sperlich as chairman and chief executive officer, succeeding James Grosfeld, who has retired. PHM, one of the nation's largest home builders, didn't elaborate on the reasons for Mr. Grosfeld's retirement, which was announced late Friday afternoon. The company said he will remain on the company's board and act as co-chairman with founder William J. Pulte of the board's executive committee. He also will serve as a consultant to the company. PHM said that it purchased 1,770,634 shares of PHM from Mr. Grosfeld "at approximately market price." He will continue to hold about three million shares, or about 13% of PHM's common stock, the company said.

PHM stock closed at $9, unchanged, in New York Stock Exchange composite trading Friday.

Mr. Sperlich, 60 years old, left his post as president of Chrysler's automotive unit in January 1988 amid reports that he had clashed with Chairman Lee A. Iacocca and other executives over the 1987 acquisition of American Motors Corp. and other issues.

While at Chrysler, Mr. Sperlich was credited with masterminding the development of two critical products: The front-wheel-drive "K-cars" that saved Chrysler in the early 1980s, and the hugely successful minivans that are central to the company's profitability now. Since leaving the auto maker, Mr. Sperlich has headed Sperlich & Associates, a consulting firm. He joined PHM's board in July 1989.

PHM announced the change at the top in conjunction with its first-quarter earnings, which rose 14% to $12.2 million, or 46 cents a share, from $10.8 million, or 41 cents a share. Revenue dropped nearly 12% to $238.7 million from $271 million.
