Who's News: Manufacturers Hanover's Head of Branches Resigns

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/30/90

NEW YORK -- The head of Manufacturers Hanover Corp.'s branch network, William A. Buckingham, resigned to take a similar position with Buffalo-based First Empire State Corp. Mr. Buckingham, 47 years old, is highly regarded within Manufacturers, and his departure, according to bank executives, was for "life-style reasons." Mr. Buckingham, an executive vice president, and his wife are both from the Buffalo region. Mr. Buckingham couldn't be reached.

In an internal bank memo, Vice Chairman Edward Miller said he accepted Mr. Buckingham's resignation "with deepest regret."

Since Manufacturers encountered some liquidity problems following the failure of Continental Illinois Bank in the early 1980s, Mr. Buckingham played an important role in increasing the bank's consumer-deposit base.

Last year, Manufacturers' core deposits accounted for 25% of net liabilities, up from 22% in 1988.

No successor has been named.
