Business Brief -- Valley Federal Savings & Loan: Federal Regulators Approve Ailing Thrift's Capital Plan

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/04/90

Valley Federal Savings & Loan Association said federal regulators approved its plan for meeting capital requirements, but as part of the plan the big thrift must raise $125 million to $150 million from outside investors by June 1991. Scott Braly, president and chief executive officer of Valley, Van Nuys, Calif., said investors currently have little interest in buying stakes in S&Ls because of the industry's problems and a huge backlog of insolvent thrifts that the government is trying to sell. But he said investors may be more interested by mid-1991. If Valley can't meet the terms of the capital plan, regulators can seek sanctions against the lender, including seizure of the institution.

Valley, which has assets of about $3 billion, earned nearly $10 million in the first quarter, with about half of the profit coming from the sale of branches. Mr. Braly said the other half of the net income represented core earnings, adding that he expected the first-quarter trend to continue through out the year, indicating full-year core earnings of roughly $20 million before special items. The thrift will continue to operate under a "lockdown" order from federal regulators, prohibiting new lending. Mr. Braly said the order wouldn't affect expected profitability.
