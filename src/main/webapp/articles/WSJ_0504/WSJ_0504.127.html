Marketing Brief -- Washington Post Co.: First-Quarter Net Drops 6%, Hurt by Advertising Slump

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/04/90

Washington Post Co. posted a 6% drop in first-quarter net income, hurt by the nationwide slump in newspaper advertising. The results were slightly worse than analysts had expected. Net fell to $39 million, or $3.16 a share, from $41.5 million, or $3.22 a share. Analysts had expected per-share earnings from $3.25 to $3.30.

Sales dropped slightly to $341 million from $341.9 million. At the Washington Post, the flagship paper that provides more than half of the company's total revenue, ad linage dropped 6%, although higher ad rates kept revenue flat.

At Newsweek magazine, the second-largest source of company revenue, sales declined 5% as ad pages fell 14%. Revenue at the broadcast division dropped 8%. But cable revenue rose 11%, while revenue from Kaplan Educational Center was up 17%. The company doesn't disclose earnings of the individiual divisions.
