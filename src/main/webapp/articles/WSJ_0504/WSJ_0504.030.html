Northeast Bancorp Files a Suit Against Bank of New York

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/04/90

NEW HAVEN, Conn. -- Northeast Bancorp filed suit charging that Bank of New York Co. breached its longstanding agreement to purchase Northeast Bancorp for about $500 million. In the suit filed in federal district court here, Northeast sought to compel Bank of New York to complete the merger agreement or pay damages of not less than $350 million. Under the merger agreement, Bank of New York is obligated to pay 250% of Northeast's book value. Northeast charged in the suit that Bank of New York breached the merger agreement by assigning a book value substantially lower than Northeast's actual book value.

The suit is the latest exchange in the quarrel between the two banks. Earlier this week, Bank of New York accused the Connecticut company of breaking the merger pact by failing to maintain accurate records and threatened to terminate the pact. Northeast said its books are in order and that Bank of New York is attempting to avoid its contractual obligations.

A Bank of New York spokesman said the company was "disappointed" by the Northeast suit. "Although we continue to prefer to resolve this matter by agreement, we are confident that the courts will sustain the Bank of New York's position that Northeast's books have materially overstated its net worth," the spokesman said.

In the suit, Northeast said that Bank of New York assigned it a book value of $12.31 a share, as of Dec. 31, 1989, based on a review of Northeast's loans. That figure was substantially below the Dec. 31 book value of $36.40 for Northeast shares. Northeast also charged that Bank of New York's disparaging remarks about Northeast's record keeping damaged Northeast's reputation and negatively affected the market price of Northeast's stock.

In national over-the-counter trading yesterday, Northeast closed at $26.50 a share, down 50 cents. In New York Stock Exchange composite trading, Bank of New York closed at $30.875, up 12.5 cents.
