Bank of New England Unit Signs a Consent With U.S. Comptroller

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/04/90

PROVIDENCE, R.I. -- Bank of New England-Old Colony N.A., a unit of Bank of New England Corp., said it signed a consent cease and desist order with the Office of the Comptroller of the Currency. The Bank of New England unit said the consent order deals "comprehensively with the development and implementation of plans, policies and procedures at the bank, and details improvements to be made in the operations of the bank."

The Bank of New England parent company signed a similar consent order with the Federal Reserve, and two of its larger units, Bank of New England N.A. and Connecticut Bank & Trust Co. N.A., have signed similar orders with the Comptroller.

Two other banking units, Maine National Bank Corp. and Bank of New England-West N.A., are expected to sign similar orders with the Comptroller shortly, Bank of New England said.

The Old Colony unit said many of the requirements and standards called for in the cease and desist order "are well under way or have already been met."
