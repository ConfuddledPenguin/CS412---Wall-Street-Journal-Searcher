Health Expenditures Rose 10.4% in 1988, Federal Agency Says

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/04/90

WASHINGTON -- The nation's health expenditures rose 10.4%, to $539.9 billion in 1988, the Department of Health and Human Services said. The double-digit rise in 1988, the latest year for which the department has figures, followed increases of 7.2% in 1986 and 8.5% in 1987. The acceleration primarily reflects a faster rise in the general rate of inflation, an HHS economist said.

In 1988, health care accounted for 11.1% of gross national product, the nation's total output of goods and services, up from 5.9% in 1965, when the nation enacted two new federal health programs -- Medicare for the elderly and Medicaid for the poor.

"Health care expenditures have been growing faster than the national economy for many years," HHS Secretary Louis Sullivan said in a statement. "This growth will strain the ability of the American people to pay for quality health care."
