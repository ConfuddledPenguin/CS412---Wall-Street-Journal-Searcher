Business Brief -- Lillian Vernon Corp.: Net Rose 23% in Quarter; New Catalog's Sales Cited

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/04/90

Lillian Vernon Corp. said its fiscal fourth-quarter net income climbed 23% to $3.1 million from a year earlier, reflecting improved operating efficiency and sales from a new catalog. The specialty catalog retailer's net income in the quarter ended Feb. 24 rose to $3.1 million, or 50 cents a share, from $2.5 million, or 41 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue rose 9.8% to $42.3 million from $38.5 million.

The earnings advance reflects, in part, better inventory management, said Lillian Vernon, chief executive officer of the Mount Vernon, N.Y.-based company.

Sales also benefited from the introduction of the firm's new At Home catalog, featuring upscale decorative home accessories. Introduced in July 1989, that catalog was mailed to 3.5 million homes. Lillian Vernon's core catalogs offer primarily inexpensively priced gifts and housewares.

Lillian Vernon shares closed yesterday in composite trading on the American Stock Exchange at $23, up 87.5 cents.

Fiscal 1990 net rose 33% to $10.8 million, or $1.75 a share, from $8.1 million, or $1.32 a share, a year earlier. Revenue rose 10% to $154.7 million from $140.6 million.
