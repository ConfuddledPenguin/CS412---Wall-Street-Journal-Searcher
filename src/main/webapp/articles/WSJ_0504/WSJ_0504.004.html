Philips Industries Reports Net Loss For Quarter, Year

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/04/90

DAYTON, Ohio -- Philips Industries Inc. reported a net loss of $45.3 million, or $1.60 a share, for the its fourth quarter ended March 31 as a result of restructuring costs and charges related to a failed takeover effort totaling $50.3 million after taxes. The diversified producer of construction, transportation and industrial products earned $8.5 million, or 29 cents a share, in the year-earlier quarter.

Sales declined 11% to $219.7 million from $245.6 million in the year-earlier period. The company isn't related to Philips Industries N.V. of the Netherlands.

Philips decided to sell some businesses and consolidate others following collapse of a planned $700 million buy-out of Philips by a new company established by Merrill Lynch Capital Partners Inc. The charges, $59.2 million before taxes, reflect anticipated losses on sale of businesses, restructuring costs and buy-out-related charges.

For the fiscal year, Philips reported a loss of $14.6 million, or 51 cents a share, versus a profit of $46.3 million, or $1.61 a share, for fiscal 1989. Sales were $927.5 million, down slightly from $941.7 million a year earlier.

Excluding the unusual charges, the company had pretax profit of $52.7 million, down 29% from $74 million in fiscal 1989. About $8 million of the drop was in operating profit of subsidiaries Philips intends to sell, the company said. Philips previously said it intends to sell its conveyor and aluminum wheel businesses.

The $7.5 million of after-tax, buy-out-related expenses will be taken in fiscal 1990 if the merger agreement with the Merrill Lynch unit is terminated before Philips releases final figures for the year. However, if the agreement isn't terminated until after the report is released, the loss will be recognized when the agreement is terminated, Philips said.

Philips repeated an earlier statement that the Merrill Lynch unit still is interested in a transaction with Philips if a new bargain can be negotiated that meets with the approval of all parties. The buy-out group couldn't obtain financing for the transaction negotiated last year.

Philips also repeated that it has provided certain information to another party that indicated possible interest in a transaction with Philips. But Philips said it is unable to predict whether any merger transaction will occur. A Philips spokesman said yesterday that there are no new developments and declined to identify the party to whom Philips provided information.
