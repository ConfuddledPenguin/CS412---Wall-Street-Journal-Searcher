Production Plans Of U.S. Auto Makers Fell 10.7% This Week

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/04/90

DETROIT -- The ten major U.S. auto makers planned to build 136,600 cars this week, down 10.7% from 153,030 a year ago. Domestic car makers said all of their facilities are scheduled to operate next week, with several plants working overtime.

General Motors Corp. said it planned daily and weekend overtime at its assembly plants in Wixom, Orion and Hamtramck, Mich., Janesville, Wis., and Lordstown, Ohio.

Ford Motor Co. has scheduled daily and weekend overtime at its Lorain, Ohio; Twin Cities, Minn.; St. Louis, Mo.; and Louisville, Ky., assembly plants.

But Mazda Motor Corp. said it reduced its production schedule for June and July by 1,300 cars. Mazda made the adjustment because of an identical drop in orders for Probe cars, which it builds for Ford at Mazda's assembly plant in Flat Rock, Mich.

In April, the ten major U.S. auto makers built 828,430 cars and trucks, down 16.9% from 997,926 the same month a year ago.

U.S. car output dropped 21.2% in April and truck and bus production was down 9.3% compared to the year-earlier month.
