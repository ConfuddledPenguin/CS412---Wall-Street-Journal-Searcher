Australia Posts Budget Deficit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/04/90

CANBERRA -- The Australian government reported a budget deficit of 1.45 billion Australian dollars (US$1.09 billion) for the first nine months of this fiscal year ending June 30, narrowing from a budget deficit of A$2.05 billion in the year-earlier period. For the full fiscal year, the government has predicted a record budget surplus of A$9.33 billion. Much of its revenue is collected in the final few months of the fiscal year.

Ralph Willis, finance minister, said in a statement that revenue for the first nine months rose 7.6% from a year earlier, while spending rose 6.3%.

Mr. Willis said the growth in cumulative spending and revenue is "broadly in line with the budget forecast" made last August.
