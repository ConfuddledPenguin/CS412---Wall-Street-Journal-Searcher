Campeau Takes Bloomingdale's Sale Sign Down --- Concern Over Tax Liability, Reluctance to Cut Price Took Retailer Off Block

  • Author: Robert Melnbardis
  • Date: 05/04/90

TORONTO -- Federated Stores Inc. pulled its Bloomingdale's chain from the auction block, a move that observers and analysts have been expecting since January. Federated, the Campeau Corp. unit that holds Federated Department Stores Inc. and Allied Stores Corp., said retaining Bloomingdale's is in the best interests of its creditors, customers and employees. Federated and Allied sought protection from creditors in January under Chapter 11 of the U.S. Bankruptcy Code. Bloomingdale's had been up for sale since early September, but the committee that was supposed to spearhead the sale disbanded in January after efforts to restructure Federated and Allied outside of bankruptcy court failed.

Federated said it intends to "aggressively" manage the 17-store chain to take advantage of its "future potential." But Bloomingdale's potential for expansion is weak and that was a big reason why Federated wasn't able to sell the division at a price it wanted, said Kurt Barnard, publisher of New York-based Retail Marketing Report, an industry newsletter.

"Bloomingdale's is a store that depends on a certain panache that is associated with New York," Mr. Barnard said. That makes it difficult to take the chain to other cities, especially because U.S. retail customers are becoming more conservative in their spending, he said.

"Bloomingdale's will have to find an identity that is captivating, but sets it apart from the crowd," he said.

Federated's announcement comes little more than one week after Investcorp International Inc. agreed to buy Saks Fifth Avenue from B.A.T Industries PLC for $1.5 billion. It is possible that Federated didn't want to be "deeply embarrassed" by selling Bloomingdale's at a bargain-basement price when Saks went for a price at which Campeau Corp. Chairman Robert Campeau once bragged he could sell Bloomingdale's, Mr. Barnard said.

Federated officials won't say what bids, if any, were received for Bloomingdale's, but industry observers say offers may have been as low as $800 million. Janet Mangano, analyst with Josephthal & Co., said that Federated may have decided to keep Bloomingdale's to avoid a huge tax liability. Federated may be better off keeping Bloomingdale's and its cash flow, which is the highest of Campeau's nine retail chains, as it tries to restructure under bankruptcy law.

Federated said "market circumstances" and Federated's financial status have "changed significantly" since Bloomingdale's was put up for sale. Mr. Barnard said Federated management and creditors probably would have preferred to sell Bloomingdale's if bids had topped $1 billion. Observers agreed that with Bloomingdale's back in the Federated fold, it is likely that other Federated or Allied assets will take its place on the auction block.
