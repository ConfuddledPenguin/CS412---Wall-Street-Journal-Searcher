Business Brief -- Reliance Group Holdings Inc.: First-Period Net Fell 64% On Revenue Increase of 8%

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/04/90

Reliance Group Holdings Inc. said earnings declined 64% in the first quarter primarily because of "difficult market conditions in some lines of business." Profit fell to $9.6 million, or 13 cents a share, from $27.1 million, or 36 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue rose 8% to $924.7 million from $854.9 million.

Profit from property and casualty insurance operations dropped 66% to $22.7 million from $67 million. New York-based Reliance blamed price competition and a decline in premiums written in 1990. However, income from life insurance as well as title and mortgage insurance rose.

Reliance has reported that its $630 million sale of its General Casualty Insurance Co. subsidiary will result in a $380 million pretax gain in the second quarter.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Reliance closed at $5.625 a share, down 12.5 cents.

---

Corrections & Amplifications

RELIANCE GROUP HOLDINGS Inc. said first-quarter earnings declined primarily because of a large year-earlier gain from the sale of an equity investment. Yesterday's edition incorrectly attributed the drop mainly to market conditions.

(WSJ May 8, 1990)
