Stockbroker Settles Complaint of Fraud

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/04/90

NEW YORK -- A broker who formerly worked at Merrill Lynch & Co. and Prudential-Bache Securities Inc. settled a complaint alleging that he misappropriated $503,000 from customers. Marc Stuart Weiner signed a consent decree in federal court in Manhattan, admitting that he had defrauded some customers, as alleged in a Securities and Exchange Commission complaint filed in September. Mr. Weiner also consented to an order directing him to repay $387,000, the amount of money that he has yet to return. However, Mr. Weiner said in an affidavit that he is currently financially unable to pay that amount.

Joel Rudin, Mr. Weiner's attorney, said the acts occurred during 1986 and 1987 because Mr. Weiner was a "compulsive gambler and had hundreds of thousands of dollars of debt to various bookies." Mr. Rudin said that for more than a year, Mr. Weiner has attended sessions of Gambler's Anonymous.

The SEC said Mr. Weiner is awaiting sentencing in a criminal case in federal court in Brooklyn. In December, he pleaded guilty to one count of securities fraud arising from some of the acts alleged in the SEC complaint.
