Corporate Profits in U.S. Will Decline, Survey Says

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/04/90

LONDON -- Corporate profits in the U.S., Canada and Britain will slide this year, while earnings growth will continue in the other major economies, according to a global survey of economists. The survey, compiled by Consensus Economics Inc., predicted that U.S. corporate profitability will slump by 3.3% this year, the U.K.'s by 0.6%, and Canada's by 7%. As for other major industrial economies, the group predicted that West Germany's corporate profitability would grow by 9% this year, Italy's by 8.7%, and France's by 7.2%. Japanese corporate profitability is expected to jump by 11.3%.

It's usually considered healthy when economies don't move in lockstep. When economic cycles are syncopated, companies in slowing economies can look to markets in still robust countries to tide them over.

In 1991, according to Consensus Economics, profitability will expand for all seven of the big industrial countries, and at rates fairly close to one another. For 1992, the group predicted that corporate profitability would increase most in Britain, where it forecast a jump of 10.9%. Meanwhile Japan would show an increase of only 6.4%.
