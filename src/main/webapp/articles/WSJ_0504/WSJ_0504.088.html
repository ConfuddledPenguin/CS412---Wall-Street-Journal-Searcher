Household Reports Net Slipped 5% In First Quarter

  • Author: Jeff Bailey
  • Date: 05/04/90

CHICAGO -- Household International Inc. reported first-quarter net income slipped 5%. But profit from continuing operations rose 18% despite a doubling of the finance concern's provision for loan losses. Moody's Investors Service Inc. has said it is reviewing Household's debt ratings for possible downgrade, citing rapid loan growth, among other things. But Donald C. Clark, chairman and chief executive officer of Household, told a meeting of securities analysts here that he has no plans to slow the pace of growth. Mr. Clark said he expects 1990 profit to surpass the record, reached in 1988, of $246.7 million, or $6.80 a share.

Net income for the Prospect Heights, Ill., company's first quarter fell to $52.2 million, or $1.36 a share, from $55.2 million, or $1.51 a share. The year-ago results include profit from discontinued operations of $11 million.

The provision for loan losses in the latest quarter was $93 million, more than twice the $44.3 million set aside last year. The increase was to cover higher charge-offs and to increase the reserve for future losses because of rapid loan growth.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Household stock closed unchanged at $42.50 a share. The shares have fallen sharply since last month, when Moody's said it was reviewing Household's credit ratings.

Problem commercial real estate assets, which include nonperforming loans and property foreclosed on or otherwise taken to satisfy debt, jumped about 16% during the quarter to $268.9 million. Mr. Clark said that figure may grow further during the year but that by year end it would be back down to the 1989 level of $231 million.

One area that concerns Moody's is Househhold's commercial real estate loan portfolio, which totaled $760.8 million at March 31. Another area of concern is so-called highly leveraged transaction loans, used to finance leveraged buy-outs, which stood at $1.1 billion. Mr. Clark said none of those were nonperforming.

Household's biggest and most profitable business, consumer lending, continued to grow rapidly during the quarter, with loans rising to $17.2 billion from $16.1 billion at year end. That includes first and second mortgages, unsecured personal loans and credit card loans. Profit rose only 9% in the business, however, because of losses at Household's British and Australian operations.
