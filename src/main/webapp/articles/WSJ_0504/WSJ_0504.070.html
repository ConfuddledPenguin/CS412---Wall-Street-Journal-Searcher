Marketing & Media: Antitrust Suit Planned Against Cinema Firms In Kansas City Area

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/04/90

WASHINGTON -- The Justice Department announced that it will file a civil antitrust suit against American Multi-Cinema Inc. and Mid-America Cinema Corp., two Kansas City-area film exhibitors. The two companies entered into a management agreement in December 1988 in which AMC, which is a unit of Kansas City, Mo.-based AMC Entertainment, obtained power to set admission prices and film rental fees for MAC's theaters in the Kansas City metropolitan area, according to a Justice Department statement. The statement also said that the two companies have a combined market share that exceeded 65% of all first-run admissions in 1989, and that this poses a significant risk to competition in the Kansas City area, in both Missouri and Kansas.

Spokesmen for the companies could not be reached for comment.
