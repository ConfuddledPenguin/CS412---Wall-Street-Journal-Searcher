Apple Bancorp Says It May Get Several Offers

  • Author: Robert Daniels
  • Date: 05/04/90

NEW YORK -- Apple Bancorp Inc. said it received indications that parties other than the real estate investor Stanley Stahl might be interested in taking over the bank holding company. The bank has rejected Mr. Stahl's $38-a-share takeover offer. Mr. Stahl controls 30.6% of Apple; his tender offer, which he made late in March, values the rest of the company at about $121.6 million.

The parent of Apple Bank for Savings said it will provide nonpublic data to these third parties. The bank declined to identify either the possible suitors or the terms of any possible proposal.

The bank also is asking bank regulators to again review Mr. Stahl's regulatory filings. In a Securities and Exchange Commission filing, the company disclosed two letters that its attorneys, Thacher, Proffitt & Wood, based here, sent to the Federal Reserve Bank of New York and the office of the New York state superintendent of banks.

The bank's attorneys ask the regulators to make a "current and careful reconsideration" of the November 1988 filings Mr. Stahl made when he sought clearance to boost his stake in Apple past 10%.

The letters refer to recent published reports, which note that Mr. Stahl once was convicted of bribing an Internal Revenue Service agent, but the conviction was reversed on appeal. Mr. Stahl had two retrials, the first ending in a hung jury and the second in an acquittal. He previously disclosed the matter to regulators.

The articles note that he also is part-owner of Manhattan's Ansonia Hotel; tenants there have had a longtime battle with management about repairs.

"Adverse public perceptions of the competence, experience or integrity of any acquiring person, such as Mr. Stahl or the members of the Stahl Group, would not be in the best interests" of the bank's depositors or the public, one letter says.

Through a spokesman, Mr. Stahl declined to comment on the letters.
