Icahn Said to Threaten Transfer of TWA's Domestic Operations

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/04/90

MOUNT KISKO, N.Y. -- Trans World Airlines Chairman Carl Icahn is threatening to transfer many of the carrier's domestic operations to America West Airlines unless the pilots union makes concessions, officials at the pilots union said. In a Tuesday meeting, first reported in the St. Louis Post-Dispatch, Mr. Icahn and officials of the TWA pilots and machinists unions met with America West President Mike Conway at Mr. Icahn's offices here to discuss the action, the union said.

America West is a small Phoenix-based airline that is mostly nonunion. It wasn't clear what if anything America West would pay for TWA's operations.

It's also uncertain how serious Mr. Icahn is about the move. The pilots and Mr. Icahn have been feuding over wage concessions for months. He has been threatening to trim the airline if the pilots don't accept $80 million in concessions. He has already sold several planes. After the Tuesday meeting, however, union officials continued to rebuff Mr. Icahn.

Officials at America West and TWA's machinists union weren't available for comment. Mr. Icahn didn't return calls to his home.

Pilots union officials said they were presented with a plan for America West to assume TWA's St. Louis hub of operations. Union officials said details were sketchy.

TWA relies primarily on its international operations and has struggled for several years to establish a strong domestic system that could feed overseas routes. Pilots union officials told members that Mr. Icahn said he plans to sell the airline's international routes after America West assumes control of the domestic operations.

Although Mr. Icahn has alluded to such a proposal in recent weeks, this was said to be the first time he formally presented it.
