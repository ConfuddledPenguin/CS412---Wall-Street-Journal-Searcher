East German Market Open to All Members Of EC, Says Minister

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/04/90

BONN -- Economics Minister Helmut Haussmann, trying to ease fears among other European Community members, said Thursday that all EC countries should have the same opportunities as West Germany in developing the East German market. Mr. Haussmann said Bonn is advising the East German government to provide tax incentives not only to West German companies wanting to do business there, but also to all interested companies.

He pointed out that the planned German monetary and economic union will open up great opportunities for foreign industry. "From July onwards, every company that's active in West Germany can also become active in East Germany," he said.

The freedom of competition in East Germany was a source of concern expressed by some EC leaders at their Dublin summit meeting Saturday.

Mr. Haussmann said he had reached agreement with EC officials that in the current first phase of German unification, the EC wouldn't have any powers to veto or limit aspects of continuing negotiations between the two countries.

Regarding reconstruction of the East German economy, Mr. Haussmann stressed the need to speed up plans to denationalize the country's state-owned industries, but he admitted that many details remain to be worked out. He said privatization is essential to cope with a large number of unemployed expected to result from the planned July 1 introduction of the Deutsche mark in East Germany.

One problem in a large-scale privatization is the impossibility to set an attractive price for key industrial properties, because asset value is extremely limited. "And of course, the earnings value of many big companies is small," said State Secretary Dieter von Wuerzen.
