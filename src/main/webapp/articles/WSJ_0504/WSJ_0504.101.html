Chinese Interest-Rate Outlook

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/04/90

NEW DELHI -- China's central bank governor said yesterday that a further decline in interest rates is unlikely and also ruled out the likelihood of a further devaluation of the yuan. Li Guixian expressed satisfaction at the results achieved by December's 22% cut in the value of the Chinese currency. He said China's exports have improved satisfactorily, while imports have been restrained, allowing a rise in the country's foreign exchange reserves.

"I think the effects are positive," he stated, but added that "We don't have any plans to continue this kind of practice," referring to prospects for a further devaluation.

Mr. Li was in India to lead his country's delegation to the 23rd annual meeting of the Asian Development Bank, which is being attended by more than 2,000 delegates, bankers and government officials.
