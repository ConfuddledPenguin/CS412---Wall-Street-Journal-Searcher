Eastern Airlines Traffic Rises

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/04/90

MIAMI -- Eastern Airlines said it flew 1.58 billion revenue passenger miles in April, a better than tenfold increase over its year-ago traffic. Eastern, a unit of Houston-based Texas Air, said the results of this year's numbers aren't comparable to last year because in 1989 the airline was still reeling from a March strike that nearly grounded the carrier. In the year-ago period, Eastern said it flew 153 million revenue passenger miles. A revenue passenger mile is one paying passenger flown one mile.

This year's load factor, or percentage of seats filled, was 63.9%, compared with last year's 42.25%.

For the first four months of 1990, Eastern flew 6.34 billion revenue passenger miles, compared with 4.51 billion in the year-earlier period. Load factor for the year to date was 63.4%; in 1989, it was 54.5%.
