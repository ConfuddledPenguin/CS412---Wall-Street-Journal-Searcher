New Securities Issues

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/04/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATES

Student Loan Marketing Association -- $150 million of notes, due May 24, 1994, priced at par for an initial yield 9.375%. The notes, which are noncallable for two years, will be reoffered at various prices through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets. The notes were priced at a spread of 34 basis points above the Treasury's four-year note.

MUNCIPALS

Ohio Water Development Authority -- $67.3 million of Series A and Ohio Air Quality Development Authority's Series B State of Ohio collateralized pollution control revenue refunding bonds, Toledo Edison Company Project, due May 15, 2019, were tentatively priced by a Shearson Lehman Hutton Inc. group to yield 8.25% in 2019.Series A consists of a single portion of $33.2 million bonds priced as 8% securities at 97.26 to yield 8.25% in 2019. Series B consists of a single portion of $34.1 million bonds priced as 8% securities at 97.26 to yield 8.25% in 2019. The bonds are rated Baa by Moody's and triple-B by S&P.

MORTGAGES

Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $520 million of Remic mortgage securities is being offered in 15 classes by Prudential-Bache Securities Inc. The offering, Series 172, is backed by Freddie Mac 9 1/2% securities with a weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29.4 years. The yields, where available, range from 9.59% to 10.88%.

Federal National Mortgage Association -- A $500 million issue of Remic mortgage securities is being offered in nine classes by Salomon Brothers Inc. The offering, Series 1990-64, is backed by Fannie Mae 10% securities. The "L" class is eligble for thrift liquidity purposes.

Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $416 million of Remic mortgage securities, offered in nine classes by Shearson Lehman Hutton Inc. The offering, Series 171, and backed by Freddie Mac 9% securities, with a weighted average coupon rate of 9.97%.

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $300 million of Remic mortgage securities, offered in nine classes by Morgan Stanley & Co. The offering, Series 1990-62, is backed by Fannie Mae 9% securities. The floater and inverse floater are pegged to the one-month London Interbank offered rate. A Morgan Stanley official said the TAC classes are structured the same way as the original TAC classes in the derivative market. That means they offer investors call protection if rates fall but extend in average life if rates rise and prepayments slow. There are no spreads available on the issue, but the spreads are tighter than those on straight Remic classes but wider than those on prepayment-protected PAC classes. The collateral consists of Fannie Mae 9s with an underlying weighted average coupon rate of 9.65% and a weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29.5 years.

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $300 million of Remic mortgage securities, offered in nine classes by J.P. Morgan Securities Inc. The offering, Series 1990-63, is backed by Fannie Mae 9 1/2% securities.

EUROBONDS

European Investment Bank (Agency) -- 200 million European currency units of 10% fungible Eurobonds due Feb. 14, 1997, priced at 99.61 via lead manager Banco di Roma. Fungible with 800 million ECU issue outstanding.

Compagnie Bancaire -- 600 million French francs of 10 1/4% Eurobonds due June 10, 1992, priced at 101.10 to yield 10.26%, via Credit Commercial de France.

Commonwealth Bank of Australia (Australia) -- $100 million (Australian) of 15 1/2% bonds due June 7, 1993, priced at 102 to yield 15.28%, via Hambros Bank Ltd. Guaranteed by the Commonwealth of Australia.

Merrill Lynch & Co. Inc. (U.S.) -- 100 million Deutsche marks of zero-coupon bonds due May 18, 2000, priced at 43.75 to yield 9.26% on an all-in basis excluding expenses, via Merrill Lynch AG.

General Electric Capital Corp. (U.S.) -- $200 million of 9 5/8% Eurobonds due Nov. 17, 1994, priced at 101 3/8, via Kidder Peabody International.

SBC Luxembourg (Swiss parent) -- 100 million Swiss francs of 8% privately placed notes due June 1, priced at 100 1/4, via Swiss Bank Corp.
