Shearson Lehman Hutton Considers a Name Change

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/04/90

NEW YORK -- Shearson Lehman Hutton Holdings Inc., whose name has been tarred in recent months, is thinking about getting a new one to forge a new identity. Officials of the big securities firm, a unit of American Express Co., have had what one calls "very preliminary" discussions about changing the company's name, or at least shortening it. One of many possibilities is going back to "Shearson/American Express," Shearson's name for a brief time after its takeover by American Express in 1981.

The discussions about a name change began last summer before the recent spate of negative publicity about Shearson; but the bad press is now one of the factors behind a possible change, people familiar with the discussions said.

One change that Shearson is seriously considering is a new name that more strongly links it with parent American Express -- a link that Shearson has already been trying to emphasize in a new advertising campaign as it tries to polish its image.

In recent months, Shearson has been battered by a series of problems. Its chairman and president both resigned earlier this year; American Express installed new management and injected more than $1 billion in new capital, acting to boost its 61% stake in Shearson to 100%.

A Shearson spokesman declined to comment on the name-change talks, which were first reported in Investor's Daily.
