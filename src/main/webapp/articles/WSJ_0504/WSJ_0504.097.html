SHL Systemhouse Seeks to Be Brought; Stock Price Increases

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/04/90

OTTAWA -- SHL Systemhouse Inc., a computer-systems integration concern with a troubled parent, has put itself up for sale. Systemhouse said it has net book value of 257.5 million Canadian dollars (US$220.8 million). Its shares outstanding have a current market value of $195.3 million. The shares rose 75 cents yesterday to close at $5.25 a share in national over-the-counter trading.

Systemhouse said it retained Wood Gundy Inc. of Toronto and Prudential-Bache Capital Funding of New York to seek a buyer for all of its shares outstanding. A spokesman declined to estimate how much the company might sell for.

Ownership of the company has been in doubt since March, when "severe financial difficulties" were reported by a holding company controlled by Systemhouse's chairman and chief executive officer, Roderick Bryden. Mr. Bryden couldn't be reached yesterday.

The closely held holding company, Kinburn Corp., said it was unable to make payments on about C$816 million of loans. Kinburn owns 50.1% of Systemhouse, and has pledged the shares against the loans. The loans include C$415 million due to BCE Inc. of Montreal, which owns 12.4% of Systemhouse.

Talks between BCE and Kinburn broke down last month, and an effort to find a buyer for Systemhouse was widely expected. Raymond Cyr, chairman and chief executive of BCE, said yesterday that it was too early to say whether his company would be interested in bidding for Systemhouse.

BCE's interests include a majority stake in Northern Telecom Ltd., a large telecommunications equipment maker. Systemhouse designs and installs integrated computer systems.

Systemhouse reported net income of C$15.3 million for the fiscal year ended Aug. 31, on revenue of C$630.8 million. It reported a net loss of C$34.3 million in the second quarter ended Feb. 28, including a C$25 million write-down of assets and a C$6.9 million provision against debt owned or guaranteed by Kinburn and Kinburn subsidiaries.
