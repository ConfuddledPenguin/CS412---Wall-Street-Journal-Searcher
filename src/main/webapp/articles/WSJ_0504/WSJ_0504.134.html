Business Brief -- Farah Inc.: Ex-Chairman Sues Concern, Directors and Top Officers

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/04/90

William F. Farah, who was removed as chairman of Farah Inc. last week, sued the company, directors and top officers to prevent them from cutting him off from the El Paso, Texas, clothing manufacturer he helped manage for 53 years. Mr. Farah, a company director and the company's largest stockholder with one million shares, or about a 15% stake, is asking an El Paso county court for a temporary restraining order to keep other board members and Farah's top management from restricting his role as a board member and company consultant.

Mr. Farah charges that board members have threatened to terminate his three-year consulting services contract. He resigned as president and chief executive officer of the company last year and was succeeded by Richard C. Allender.

In an interview conducted before the suit was filed, Mr. Allender declined to discuss the company's relationship with Mr. Farah. But when asked about Mr. Farah's pending lawsuit, Mr. Allender said: "I can't afford to be concerned about what Willie {Mr. Farah} may or may not do. I have a business to run."
