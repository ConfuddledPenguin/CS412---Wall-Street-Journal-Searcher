Prime Motor Inns Inc. Reports 98% Plunge In Third-Quarter Net

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/04/90

NEW YORK -- Prime Motor Inns Inc., as expected, reported that fiscal third-quarter profit plunged 98%, citing "intense competition in the hotel industry." And Chairman Peter E. Simon said he expects Prime's results to be "under pressure over the next several quarters" -- a near-term outlook shared by "virtually every other" hotel company.

For the quarter ended March 31, Prime reported net income of $209,000, or one cent a share, down from $18.2 million, or 55 cents a share, in the year-earlier period. Revenue dropped 17% to $82.4 million from $99.9 million. Last month, the company had estimated that per-share net dropped substantially for the quarter.

Mr. Simon also said that Prime completed the sale of long-term notes receivable to an affiliate of Morgan Guaranty Trust Co. and received $40 million from the sale of a hotel. Total proceeds of these transactions plus two previous debt financings are $110 million, $57 million of which went to pay bank debt and the rest for general purposes.

Prime also said it will concentrate its resources on its AmeriSuites all-suite properties and Wellesley Inn limited-service hotels, operations where market demand has been strong.

The company thus reported no fees from contracting work it does on properties, known as development income, nor any gains on sales of properties. In the year-earlier period, development income and property-sale gains were $13.6 million and $6 million, respectively.

Prime owns, operates or manages 139 hotels in the U.S.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Prime shares fell 12.5 cents to $7.875.
