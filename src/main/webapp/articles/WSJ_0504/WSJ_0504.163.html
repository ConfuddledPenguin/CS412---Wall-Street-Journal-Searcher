Merrill Lynch Unit, Goldome to Sell 21% Stake in Leslie Fay

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/04/90

NEW YORK -- Leslie Fay Co. said its largest shareholder, Goldome Savings Bank, and a Merrill Lynch & Co. unit plan to sell a 21% stake in Leslie Fay shares, with a current market value of $45.9 million. In a registration statement filed with the Securities and Exchange Commission, the women's apparel maker said the holders plan to sell 4,125,000 of their 19.1 million common shares outstanding. The prospectus said that Goldome, and its related Goldome Foundation, plan to sell a total of 3.5 million of the four million Leslie Fay shares they own.

A Leslie Fay spokesman said that it filed the statement because Goldome approached the company about selling the shares it owns. According to the spokesman, Goldome became a limited partner in a New Jersey partnership that in 1984 took Leslie Fay private in a leveraged buy-out. In 1986, when Leslie Fay went public again, the partnership interest was exchanged for Leslie Fay shares.

Goldome, Buffalo, N.Y., declined to comment. The thrift has been taking a number of actions to improve the company's capital position in order to meet minimum capital requirements as it focuses on consumer banking.

According to the filing, Merrill Lynch Interfunding Inc. plans to sell 655,435 of the 1.44 million Leslie Fay shares it owns. Merrill Lynch didn't return phone calls seeking comment.

Leslie Fay shares closed yesterday in composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange at $11.125, down 25 cents.

The selling shareholders have granted the underwriters of the offering -- Bear, Stearns & Co. and First Boston Corp. -- the right to purchase up to an additional 595,150 shares to cover over-allotments.
