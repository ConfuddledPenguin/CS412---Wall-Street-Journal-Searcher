Bush Makes Gesture to Iran With Inquiry on Abduction

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/04/90

WASHINGTON -- President Bush, attempting to make a goodwill gesture toward Iran without negotiating for hostages, said the U.S. will try to discover the fate of four Iranians kidnapped in Lebanon. Mr. Bush's offer, made at a news conference, doesn't have much practical effect. U.S. officials and analysts from other nations have thought for some time that the four Iranians -- three diplomats and a journalist -- have been dead since shortly after their abduction in 1982. They disappeared near a checkpoint in Beirut run by Christian militiamen.

But Mr. Bush clearly hopes that his offer will satisfy Iran's call for a gesture of thanks from the U.S. for its help in winning the release of two Americans held hostage in Beirut. "Here's something we can do" without violating U.S. policy against negotiating for release of hostages, the president said. Six Americans remain captive in Lebanon.

Iran has long complained that the West, while pressing for release of its own citizens held hostage in Beirut, has been silent about the fate of the four Iranians. Mr. Bush said that the U.S. would "go back and get any information" that might "relieve the anxieties of the loved ones" of the four Iranians.
