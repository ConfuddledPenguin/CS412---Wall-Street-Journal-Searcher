Money-Market Funds Decline $2.14 Billion As Taxpayers Dip In

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/04/90

WASHINGTON -- Assets of the nation's 509 money-market mutual funds fell $2.14 billion, to $385.72 billion, for the week ended Wednesday, after individual investors raided their fund accounts to pay their taxes. The drop, the sixth in a row, was the largest since the $5.04 billion at June 1, 1988. The numbers for the previous week were revised to show an $840 million decline.

The Investment Company Institute, a trade association based here, said that assets of 161 institutional funds advanced $1.71 billion, to $92.32 billion; 246 general-purpose funds fell $912.6 million, to $132.70 billion; and 102 broker-dealer funds sank $2.94 billion, to $160.7 billion.

"The tail end of the tax season continued to be reflected in last week's changes of money-market fund assets," said Jacob Dreyer, vice president and chief economist at the institute.

"Checks drawn on money-market funds and mailed (to the Internal Revenue Service) by last-minute filers were presented for payment last week and caused a sharp drop in assets held in individual accounts" -- the broker-dealer and general-purpose categories, he said.

"At the same time," Mr. Dreyer added, "institutional assets shot up, partly in response to a dip in the federal funds rate," the interest rate that banks charge each other on overnight loans.
