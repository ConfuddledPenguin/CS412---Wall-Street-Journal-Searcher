Who's News: Cyprus Minerals Picks Stone to Be President; Barr Named Chairman

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/04/90

DENVER -- Cyprus Minerals Co. named a new chairman and president and reorganized its top management structure. The mining company appointed former president Kenneth J. Barr as chairman, succeeding John C. Duncan, 69 years old, who was named vice chairman. Mr. Barr, 63, will remain as chief executive officer.

Chester B. Stone Jr., 55, was named president in Mr. Barr's place, as expected. Mr. Stone, formerly senior vice president for coal and iron ore, will also take the new title of chief operating officer. Mr. Stone, who had been chief financial officer until mid-1989, took over the coal and iron ore operations last year in preparation for his naming as president.

Appointed to new executive vice president positions were Donald P. Bellum, 57, and Thomas A. Williams, 54. Mr. Bellum was senior vice president for metals, and Mr. Williams has been senior vice president for specialty metals and industrial minerals. Messrs. Bellum and Williams will report to Mr. Stone, as will F. Steven Mooney, president of Cyprus Copper Co., and Donald P. Brown, president of Cyprus Coal Co.
