Australian Mutual's Assets Rise

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/04/90

SYDNEY -- Australian Mutual Provident Society, the country's largest insurance concern, said its assets under management rose 89% to 57.6 billion Australian dollars ($43.34 billion) in 1989, from A$30.4 billion a year earlier. The funds don't include assets of Pearl Group PLC, a British life-insurance concern that AMP acquired in the past year for #1.24 billion ($2.04 billion).

AMP said its total premium income rose 24% to A$4.6 billion from A$3.7 billion, and total income rose 22% to A$10 billion from A$8.2 billion.

Pearl's income totaled A$3.2 billion in 1989.

AMP has total reserves of A$5.4 billion, which have risen from A$4.6 billion in 1988. Reserves total A$13.1 billion including Pearl's reserves.
