International: Japan Stock-Dealer Firm Is Bankrupt, Says Agency

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/04/90

TOKYO -- The sharp plunge in Japanese share prices this year has forced stock dealer Akebono Kikaku Co. into bankruptcy, a credit research agency reported. Teikoku Data Bank Ltd. said Tokyo-based Akebono has accumulated debt exceeding 210 billion yen ($1.33 billion) and had allegedly issued bad notes twice since March 28. But it is also possible that the $1.33 billion represented debts by Akebono and several other companies controlled by two major stock market speculators, Komei Tai and Seikichi Endo. Whatever the source, it is the third-largest bankruptcy case on record in Japan, the agency said.

Despite a partial recovery in recent trading sessions, share prices on the Tokyo Stock Exchange are more than 20% below what they were at the start of the year. Teikoku Data Bank said Akebono had been unable to meet margin calls because of the steep fall in stock prices.

Akebono, founded in 1979, dealt in speculative trading in about 20 stock issues, including those of chemical, electric-power and pharmaceutical companies, according to the Kyodo News Service. The company also managed hotels in the downtown Tokyo district of Shinjuku.
