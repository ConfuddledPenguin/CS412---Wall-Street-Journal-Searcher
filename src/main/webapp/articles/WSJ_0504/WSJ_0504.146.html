Marketing & Media: A.C. Nielsen Can't Explain Data on TV Viewing Drop

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/04/90

NEW YORK -- A.C. Nielsen Co. yesterday told executives from the television networks and advertising agencies that it has no idea why its recent TV ratings show a sharp decline in television viewing. Nielsen ratings, which dictate how billions of dollars are spent on television advertising each year, indicate that TV viewing is down 8.1% for daytime programming, off 2.2% in prime time and is down a sharp 14% for nightly newscasts for the three networks in recent months.

Nielsen, which met privately with about 100 members of the advertising and broadcast community in a 90-minute session at a New York hotel yesterday, pledged to research the ratings drop further. But some executives remain dissatisfied. "I don't feel Nielsen has given us anything new," said Michael Eisenberg, a vice president at CBS Inc. "We still think there really is a problem."

Executives of Nielsen, a Dun & Bradstreet Corp. unit, declined to comment on the meeting yesterday, although one conceded privately that the "magnitude of these changes {in viewing} is the largest in recent memory and warrants serious examination."

Even ad executives, who in the past have tended to dismiss the network complaints about the Nielsen people meters, express concern at the latest numbers. "There is a fly in the ointment," said Joel M. Segal, executive vice president at the ad agency McCann-Erickson, a unit of Interpublic Group of Cos. "It's madness. We can't figure out {the demographics} we're buying."
