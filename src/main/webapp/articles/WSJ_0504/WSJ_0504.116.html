Business Brief -- Leo's Industries Inc.: Company Completes Sale Of 442,500 Common Shares

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/04/90

Leo's Industries Inc. said it completed a private placement of 442,500 shares of its common stock to several investors, primarily European banks and insurance companies. Leo's, which operates a chain of consumer-electronics stores and resells cellular phones, abandoned an initial public offering early this year that had tried to offer a 33% stake of its cellular-phone unit, Twentieth Century Cellular, and raise more than $20 million.

At the time the offering was withdrawn, the Long Beach, Calif., company said it would revert to its initial, smaller-scale strategy of selling stock privately.

The company's chief financial officer, Merrill Lyons, said the slight discount given to the buyers was only to accommodate a two-to-three week holding period until the shares are registered. He said the proceeds of the sale, which he wouldn't quantify, would be used for general coorporate purposes to "strengthen the balance sheet."

In composite trading yesterday on the American Stock Exchange, Leo's shares closed yesterday at $17.75, down 87.5 cents.
