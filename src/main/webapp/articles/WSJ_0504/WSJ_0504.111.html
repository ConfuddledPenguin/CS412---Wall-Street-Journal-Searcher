International Brief -- Deutsche Lufthansa AG: Airline to Hire 500 Workers From Bankrupt Air Carrier

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/04/90

Deutsche Lufthansa AG Chairman Heinz Ruhnau said the West German airline will hire approximately 500 employees of German Wings, a Munich-based private air carrier that filed for bankruptcy this week. Mr. Ruhnau told reporters that Frankfurt-based Lufthansa had planned to add 3,500 employees this year anyway. He said he ordered personnel directors to give priority to job applicants from German Wings.

The Lufthansa chairman said the company has little interest in taking over German Wings's six leased McDonnell Douglas Corp. MD-83 twin jets, because they don't fit into Lufthansa's own fleet.

With regards to Interflug, the East German national airline, Mr. Ruhnau said he favored increased cooperation between the two German carriers, but foresees a future where both continue to operate independently, even after the planned unification of the two Germanys.

Lufthansa is in the process of acquiring a 26% stake in Interflug; the two companies have also set up a catering joint venture.
