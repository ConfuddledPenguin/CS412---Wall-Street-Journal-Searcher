Initial State Jobless Claims Increased in Latest Week

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/04/90

WASHINGTON -- Initial claims for state unemployment insurance rose 24,000 in the week ended April 21 to a seasonally adjusted 372,000 from an unrevised 348,000 the previous week, the Labor Department said. In the week ended April 14, 2,474,000 persons were receiving state unemployment benefits, up 172,000 from the previous week's unrevised 2,302,000. The number of persons receiving unemployment benefits as a percentage of those covered by unemployment insurance rose to 2.4% in the week ended April 14 from 2.2% the week before.

The week ended April 21 followed the reporting period that included the Good Friday holiday, in which there were fewer days to file jobless claims. Initial claims for state unemployment insurance typically rise in weeks that follow major holidays.
