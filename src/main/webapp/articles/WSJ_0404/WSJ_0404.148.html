Business Brief -- Central Banking System Inc.: Loss in 1989 Won't Hurt Plan to Sell Banking Unit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/04/90

Central Banking System Inc.'s 1989 net loss won't impair its plan to liquidate and sell its banking unit. The Walnut Creek, Calif., bank holding company's loss narrowed to $29.7 million, or $6.95 a share, from $42.2 million, or $9.87 a share, in 1988.

The fourth-quarter loss narrowed to $1.8 million, or 42 cents a share, from $24.9 million, or $5.83 a share, a year earlier. The full-year and quarter losses don't include the fourth-quarter losses at the Central Bank and CB Insurance Agency Inc. units, which totaled $3.4 million; the losses are treated separately as assets for sale.

Central Banking still meets the financial criteria of its pact to sell the two units to Bank of the West, a San Francisco unit of France's state-owned Banque Nationale de Paris, for $42.8 million. The sale should take place in late spring, Central Banking said.

Central Banking has been plagued by losses; an audit in September uncovered $2.3 million in overstated gains, mostly from auto loans.
