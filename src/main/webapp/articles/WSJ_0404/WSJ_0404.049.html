West German Trade Surplus

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/04/90

FRANKFURT, West Germany -- West Germany's overall trade surplus narrowed to 10.63 billion marks ($6.25 billion) in February from 12.83 billion marks in January, the Federal Statistics Office said. The surplus in the current international payments account, which includes trade in goods and services plus some unilateral transfers, slimmed to a preliminary 8.3 billion marks from a revised 10.2 billion marks a month earlier, the office added.

The January current account had originally been reported at 10.8 billion marks.

The February trade and current-account surpluses also narrowed from their respective year-earlier levels of 11.4 billion marks and 8.5 billion marks.
