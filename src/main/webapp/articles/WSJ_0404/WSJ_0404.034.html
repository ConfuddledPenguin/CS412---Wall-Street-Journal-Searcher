R.G. Barry Plant Halts Output

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/04/90

PICKERINGTON, Ohio -- R.G. Barry Corp. said it halted manufacturing operations at a factory in Ciudad Juarez, Mexico, because of excessive employee turnover. The producer of washable slippers and other comfort footwear said it expects to incur costs of about $700,000 in the closing, which affected about 200 employees.

"We haven't been able to hire, train or retain enough people to allow our plant to operate at a reasonable cost level or near its capacity," said Gordon Zacks, chairman and chief executive officer.

The company said it can meet its requirements at its other facilities, including two plants in Mexico, one in China and three in the U.S.
