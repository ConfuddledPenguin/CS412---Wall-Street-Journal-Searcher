Prime Motor Inns Says Per-Share Net Fell `Substantially'

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/04/90

FAIRFIELD, N.J. -- Prime Motor Inns Inc. said per-share earnings for the third quarter ended March 31 are expected to be "substantially" below a year earlier. The owner, operator, and manager of hotels said the decline was due to continued softness in hotel occupancy and rates, as well as lack of property sales or development fee income in the quarter.

For the quarter ended March 31, 1989, Prime had net income of $18.8 million, or 55 cents a share, on revenue of $99.9 million.

Caroline S. Levy, Shearson Lehman Hutton restaurant-lodging analyst, said earnings for the quarter were being estimated recently at about 20 cents a share. However, she declined to put an estimate on the earnings in view of the announcement yesterday that Prime would put properties of Servico Inc. into escrow.

"We don't know how it all works out," Ms. Levy said. "But operating earnings are down as are margins on the sale of assets. The trends of the lodging industry are also working against Prime. Lodging room rates and room occupancy are under pressure."

Prime said it had taken into escrow deeds and other documents relating to properties of Servico, under the terms of its $74 million loan to FCD Hospitality Inc. In 1989, Prime loaned FCD money to acquire Servico. The announcement appeared to indicate that Prime may take control of the properties under certain conditions.

Prime owns, operates or manages 139 hotels with 20,500 rooms nationwide. It is also the franchiser for Howard Johnson, Rodeway Inns International, and operates the U.S. Ramada franchise system, which totals 1,100 properties with 150,000 rooms.
