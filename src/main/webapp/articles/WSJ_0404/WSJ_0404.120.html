International: Daimler Says Mitsubishi Might Join Engine Venture

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/04/90

FRANKFURT, West Germany -- Daimler-Benz AG might in the near future invite Japan's Mitsubishi group of companies to join its planned aircraft engines joint venture with the Pratt & Whitney subsidiary of United Technologies Corp., Daimler's chairman said in an interview to be published today. The chairman of West Germany's largest industrial company didn't indicate with which Mitsubishi concern he has discussed the venture.

Edzard Reuter told the West German financial newspaper Handelsblatt that it is "thinkable, though not completely discussed" that Mitsubishi could join the arrangement under which the civilian jet engine operations of Daimler's MTU Motoren- und Turbinen-Union Muenchen G.m.b.H. engine subsidiary will be merged with those of Pratt & Whitney. Daimler and United Technologies will then take cross shareholdings in those respective units.

Separately, the Deutsche Aerospace AG division of Daimler said it signed a memorandum of understanding to sell two of its defense related business lines to the West German shipbuilding group Bremer Vulkan AG for an amount that wasn't disclosed.

The agreement ends nearly seven months of uncertainty over the fate of these naval and missile technology operations that were ordered by the Economics Ministry to be divested on antitrust grounds following Daimler's acquisition of Messerschmitt-Boelkow-Blohm G.m.b.H. last year.
