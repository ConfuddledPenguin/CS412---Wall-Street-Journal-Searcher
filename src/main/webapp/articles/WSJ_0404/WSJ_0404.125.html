Who's News: Provigo Inc. Names Nadeau and Bussieres To Fill Top Positions

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/04/90

MONTREAL -- Provigo Inc. named two people to succeed its former chairman, president and chief executive officer, Pierre Lortie, who left five months ago after the board decided to sell the nonfood operations of the wholesale and retail grocery concern. Bertin Nadeau, who has effective control of 51% of Provigo's stock, was named chairman. He will share the chief executive's post with Yvan Bussieres, who also was named president.

Mr. Nadeau, 49 years old, controls Unigesco Inc., a Montreal distribution concern. Unigesco holds 26% of Provigo directly, and, through an agreement with another major holder, votes an additional 25% of Provigo's shares.

Mr. Bussieres, 46, previously was president and chief operating officer of a Provigo unit.

Mr. Nadeau said that within the newly created "office of the chief executive," he will be responsible for the company's overall direction and finances, while Mr. Bussieres will concentrate on operations.

Provigo also posted a loss for the year ended Jan. 27, as expected, but analysts said the results were even weaker than anticipated. The fiscal 1990 deficit of 51.4 million Canadian dollars (US$44 million), or 60 Canadian cents (51 U.S. cents) a share, compares with year-earlier earnings of C$51.5 million, or 61 Canadian cents a share. The results for both years include extraordinary charges and losses from discontinued operations, including a C$60 million charge for the disposal of Provigo's nonfood businesses in the latest year.

Sales rose 15% to C$6.14 billion from C$5.35 billion.

On the Toronto Stock Exchange yesterday, Provigo shares closed unchanged at C$9.25.
