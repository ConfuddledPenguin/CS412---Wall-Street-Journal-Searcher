Two Brokerage Firms In Canada to Merge, Receive Cash Infusion

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/04/90

TORONTO -- Two brokerage firms, Midland Doherty Financial Corp. and Walwyn Inc. said they agreed to merge and are taking on a new partner in exchange for a cash injection. Under terms of the agreement, shareholders would exchange each Midland Doherty share held for 1.75 shares in the combined company. Walwyn shares would be exchanged on a one-for-one basis.

Meantime, Mackenzie Financial Corp., a Toronto investment management concern, said it would pay 19.6 million Canadian dollars ($16.8 million) for 22% of the new company's shares, making it the largest single shareholder. Mackenzie said its purchase was for investment purposes.

The transactions are subject to shareholder and regulatory approvals. The companies said last month that they were holding discussions about a possible merger.

Midland is 31%-owned by Union Enterprises Ltd., which is controlled by Unicorp Canada Corp., a natural resources and financial services concern. Toronto financiers Peter and Edward Bronfman hold a 22% indirect stake in Unicorp through their merchant bank and holding company, Hees International Bancorp Inc.

Walwyn is 38%-owned by Toronto-based Fairfax Financial Holdings Ltd., an insurance and financial-services holding company.

After the merger, Fairfax will hold 19% of the combined company's shares, Union Enterprises will own 10%, and the rest will be publicly traded.

James Moir, president of Midland Doherty and chairman and chief executive of its operating unit, Midland Capital Corp., said the merger would result in some layoffs, particularly among supporting staff. Mr. Moir said he plans to leave the company after the merger because of a "duplication of management talent." He said he has no specific plans at the moment.

The new company, whose name hasn't been disclosed, will be headed by H.A. Arrell as chairman and chief executive officer. Mr. Arrell is currently chairman and chief executive of Walwyn.
