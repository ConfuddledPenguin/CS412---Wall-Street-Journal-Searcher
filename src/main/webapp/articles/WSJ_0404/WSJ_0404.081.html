Herman Miller to Buy Meridian for Stock Valued at $28.6 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/04/90

ZEELAND, Mich. -- Herman Miller Inc. said it agreed to acquire Meridian Inc. of Spring Lake, Mich., in a stock swap valued at about $28.6 million. Herman Miller said it will acquire the closely held maker of metal-case goods by exchanging 1,380,000 of its common shares for all of Meridian's shares outstanding. The shares to be issued will represent 5.4% of Herman Miller's common stock after the acquisition, Herman Miller said.

James H. Bloem, Herman Miller vice president and chief financial officer, said the transaction will neither enhance nor dilute the company's per-share earnings for the fiscal year ending June 2. The Michigan office-furniture maker said it expects to complete the transaction before May 23. Completion is subject to regulatory approval.
