UPI Closes Bureaus In 3 Western States In Restructuring Move

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/12/90

NEW YORK -- United Press International, as part of a long-running restructuring of editorial operations, is closing bureaus in three Western states and laying off some full-time reporters. UPI spokesman Milt Capps confirmed published reports that bureaus in New Mexico, Wyoming and Montana, many of them operated by one person, would be closed. As part of the restructuring, the news service has also given layoff notices to one-person bureaus in Michigan and North Dakota, Mr. Capps said.

Mr. Capps wouldn't confirm the number of full-time reporters or editors that have been or would be laid off. He said that UPI at the same time is increasing its network of stringers in those states and "exploring a number of joint ventures at the state and regional level" with State News Service and Financial World magazine.

"Virtually every bureau in the system world-wide is affected either by increases or decreases," Mr. Capps said. The changes in the editorial operations began six weeks ago, he said, and would "last a number of months."

The news service is owned by Infotechnology Inc., which also owns 47% of Financial News Network.
