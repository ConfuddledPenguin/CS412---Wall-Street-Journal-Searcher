Letters to the Editor: Longevity in the Cards

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/12/90

Your July 17 page-one article said the American Contract Bridge League believes that 95-year-old Ruth Thorpe is the league's "oldest active bridge player." But in April an article appeared in the ACBL Bulletin about my aunt, Isabel Robarts Whiteside, who was 96 years old on Dec. 10, 1989. She, not Ruth Thorpe, must be the oldest. She has been an ACBL member for 35 years and became a Life Master in 1967. While no longer participating in national tournaments, she drives from her home in Oroville, Calif., to ACBL-sanctioned games in Chico and Marysville twice a week.

Roxanne R. Harris

Wilmington, Del.
