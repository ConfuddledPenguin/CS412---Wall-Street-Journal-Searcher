Technology: IBM Acquires 5% Of Hogan Systems

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/12/90

ARMONK, N.Y. -- International Business Machines Corp. said it acquired a 5% interest in Hogan Systems Inc., a Dallas-based maker of software for the banking industry. Details of the deal weren't disclosed.

IBM said the acquisition fits a recent pattern in which the computer giant has taken equity positions in many small software concerns, bolstering companies it considers to provide strategic products. Hogan makes mostly accounting software for large banks and thrifts.

IBM also said it extended through June 1995 an agreement to market Hogan's software in the U.S., Canada and Puerto Rico, and agreed to develop some projects jointly with Hogan. The marketing agreement has been modified, however, so that Hogan receives 50% of revenue from IBM's sales of its products, rather than the previously fixed amount of $9.6 million a year.

In national over-the-counter trading yesterday, Hogan shares closed at $3.125, unchanged.
