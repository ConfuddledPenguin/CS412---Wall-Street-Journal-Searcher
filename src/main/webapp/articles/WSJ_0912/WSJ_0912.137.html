Letters to the Editor: That Wasn't Ginger You Saw With Fred

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/12/90

In your Aug. 20 article "At Columbia, Comeback Is the Big Production," you erroneously identify Fred Astaire's partner in a vintage movie poster from "You'll Never Get Rich" (Columbia, 1940) as Ginger Rogers. In fact, Mr. Astaire's co-star in "You'll Never Get Rich" was Rita Hayworth. Mr. Astaire's classic musicals with Ginger Rogers in 1933-1939 were made at the RKO Radio Studios. "You'll Never Get Rich," however, is notable not only for being one of only two movies Astaire and Hayworth made together (the other, again for Columbia, was "You Were Never Lovelier"), but also for the wonderful supporting performance by Robert Benchley and the rare cameo appearance of the Delta Rhythm Boys. It is also a fine example of an overlooked minigenre: the Hollywood movies taking a lighthearted look at military life that were made between the inception of the peacetime draft in 1940 and the Pearl Harbor attack, which abruptly sobered the nation's outlook.

L. Charles Landgraf

London
