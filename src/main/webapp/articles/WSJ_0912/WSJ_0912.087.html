Business Brief -- Wheeling-Pittsburgh Steel Corp.: Firm Is Closer to Emerging From Chapter 11 Protection

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/12/90

Wheeling-Pittsburgh Steel Corp. said ratification of a four-year contract with its union workers clears the way for the company to emerge from bankruptcy-law protection. The contract, ratified by the United Steel Workers of America, calls for a $1.50-an-hour average wage increase, which equals wage concessions union members took in 1982 and 1983 when Wheeling-Pittsburgh was short of cash because it was building new facilities.

The contract also calls for an average 50-cent-an-hour increase in each of the next three years; a $3,000 signing bonus; a $500 bonus in 1991; restoration of a service bonus; a new profit-sharing plan; a 401(k) savings plan; cost-of-living raises; and 50% payment of health plan premiums.

The company, based in Wheeling, W.Va., has been in Chapter 11 bankruptcy-law proceedings since April 15, 1985. Under Chapter 11, a company is protected from creditors while it works out a reorganization plan.
