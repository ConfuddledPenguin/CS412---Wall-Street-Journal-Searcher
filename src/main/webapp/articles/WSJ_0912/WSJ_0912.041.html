Bill to Boost Level Of Pentagon Buyers Approved by House

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/12/90

WASHINGTON -- The House approved a measure to improve the professional standards of the huge Pentagon work force responsible for buying and developing more than $100 billion in defense equipment every year. The provision would create a career-oriented "acquisition corps" in each of the service branches and establish higher standards in education and experience for the managers of major weapons programs. No Pentagon aide can step into that kind of job, for instance, unless he or she has first attended a Pentagon management-training course and has had eight years of experience in defense acquisition, according to the proposed rules.

The measure, which was tacked on as an amendment to the House defense authorization bill for fiscal 1991, passed by a 413-1 vote.

Rep. Nicholas Mavroules (D., Mass.), the principal sponsor of the amendment, said it would affect as many as 240,000 Pentagon civilians and uniformed personnel engaged in various aspects of defense procurement.

The Mavroules proposal would convert the Pentagon's system of revolving officers in and out of procurement jobs with one that more closely resembles Britain's "procurement executive" or France's delegate general for armaments. The amendment also directs the Pentagon to create a Defense Acquisition University to further the training of these corps.

The House approved by voice vote an amendment that would help the Pentagon and other federal agencies attract and retain qualified professionals. The amendment, proposed by Rep. Benjamin Gilman (R., N.Y.), would allow these agencies to reimburse employees in critical jobs for the cost of earning academic degrees and repay their student loans in exchange for a commitment to do government work for a specific period of time.

The House also voted 230-188 to block a move by New York Democrats to designate Staten Island's Navy base for immediate closure.
