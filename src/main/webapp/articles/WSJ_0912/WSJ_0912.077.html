Owners of Radar Detectors Win Victory in California

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/12/90

SAN FRANCISCO -- Drivers with Fuzzbusters can't be busted by their insurance companies, according to an order by California's insurance commissioner. The commissioner, Roxani M. Gillespie, yesterday ordered Government Employees Insurance Co., or Geico, to stop refusing to insure radar-detector owners in the state, saying the practice violates California's insurance law. The Washington, D.C., company had requested a hearing after a motorist lodged a complaint last year charging that Geico wouldn't renew his insurance after he confessed in a questionnaire that he packed a radar detector in one of his three cars.

Ms. Gillespie found in her decision that Geico had presented, "No credible evidence . . . that radar detector users as a class are high-speed drivers or that as a class they tend to drive in a manner which endangers the safety of themselves or others."

Geico won't appeal the decision immediately, but will collect better data and "will eventually prevail" in proving the connection between radar detectors and accidents, said Tony Nicely, the company's president. Insurance departments in Maryland and five other states require insurance companies to sell policies to radar-detector owners, Mr. Nicely said, but Geico refuses insurance to such owners in 42 other states because "we know of no other plausible use of a radar detector than to exceed speed laws."
