Dallas FHLB to Sell Offices to GTE Unit In Cost-Cutting Move

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/12/90

DALLAS -- The Federal Home Loan Bank of Dallas, in an effort to cut costs as the thrift industry constricts, is selling its headquarters to a unit of GTE Corp. and moving to less plush leased space. Neither party would disclose the sale price, but FHLB's president, George Barclay, said investment of the proceeds, combined with a reduction in other office expenses, "will have a favorable impact on the bank's profitability."

Mr. Barclay is one of the presidents of the 12 Federal Home Loan Banks who has sounded alarms about the declining profitability of the banks. The banks' major source of revenue is loans, or advances, to member thrifts. But demand for advances has declined as thrifts have cut their borrowings in efforts to shrink to meet stringent capital standards.

"Selling the headquarters and moving into leased office space is in the best interests of the bank and our member insititutions," Mr. Barclay said yesterday.

The FHLB headquarters, comprising about 200,000 square feet in two buildings, is being sold to GTE Southwest Inc., one of the four area operating companies of GTE Telephone Operations.

A spokeswoman for GTE Southwest said the company will move about 585 employees into the buildings in late 1991, when the company's current leases expire.
