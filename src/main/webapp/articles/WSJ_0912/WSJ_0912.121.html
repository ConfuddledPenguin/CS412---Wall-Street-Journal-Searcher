Iroquois Gas Pipeline Gets Favorable Ruling From FERC Official

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/12/90

WASHINGTON -- The proposed Iroquois natural-gas pipeline won a favorable ruling from a Federal Energy Regulatory Commission official, boosting the chances for the commission's approval of the project. An FERC administrative law judge said that the gas to be delivered by the project is needed and that the project's proposed rate structure is reasonable. The 369-mile, $582.6 million Iroquois Gas Transmission System pipeline is designed to bring Canadian gas to the northeastern U.S.

The commission had been expected to approve the project in July. Instead, faced with significant opposition from domestic producers, it set up a hearing process to allow for further examination of the need and rate issue. Now, each side gets time to submit further briefs in the case, with a final decision by the commission expected by Nov. 28.

The Independent Petroleum Association of America said that it was disappointed by the law judge's findings and that it intends to file objections with the commission. "We are hopeful that the commission will maintain an open mind on these matters," Paul Hilliard, the association's chairman, said in a statement. "We are not done yet."
