Heinz Expects Rise Of 11% for 1991 Net, To About $2.10 a Share

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/12/90

PITTSBURGH -- H.J. Heinz Co. said it expects fiscal 1991 net income to be about $2.10 a share, up about 11% over last year's $1.90 a share but a smaller gain than seen in recent years at the food company. Anthony J. F. O'Reilly, Heinz chairman, president and chief executive officer, said at the annual meeting that the company has felt pressure in recent months in its pet food and Weight Watchers brand low-calorie foods.

Its 9-Lives cat food brand has been hit by more competition following Alpo Petfoods Inc.'s entrance into the category. Alpo has gained 10% of that market, Mr. O'Reilly said.

Heinz said it plans to boost its Weight Watchers business with a test market of a weight-loss plan that offers personal counseling instead of traditional group sessions.

Mr. O'Reilly said international markets continue to expand, with affiliates in Africa and the Pacific Rim showing improved profits. Heinz plans to double the capacity at its baby food plant in China in the next few months, he said.

Heinz said it has seen only marginal improvement in its domestic tuna market; increased harvests of tuna have pushed retail prices down, despite earlier projections that buying from new sources could increase costs. Heinz has launched a new advertising campaign promoting the company's decision in April to stop buying tuna captured along with dolphins, Mr. O'Reilly said.

Also, company shareholders approved a stock option plan for key employees, including a provision that gives Mr. O'Reilly options to buy four million shares of Heinz stock. The stock plan was part of Mr. O'Reilly's new five-year contract with Heinz.
