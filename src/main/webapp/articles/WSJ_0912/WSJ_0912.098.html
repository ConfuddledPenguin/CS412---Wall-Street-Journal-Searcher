U.S. Exporters Have An Optimistic View Of Year, Data Show

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/12/90

NEW YORK -- Nearly two-thirds of U.S. companies that export are optimistic about gaining higher overseas sales in the next 12 months, a survey by Dun & Bradstreet Corp. shows. In June, the financial data and services concern interviewed 5,000 of the nation's largest export companies. According to the survey, 64% of the firms expect their sales to increase, 33% expect sales to remain unchanged and 3% predict a decline.

Among the firms with high expectations, 71% said they expect to increase exports to Western Europe, 52% to Japan and almost 30% predicted that they plan to boost exports to Eastern Europe or the Soviet Union.

Joseph W. Duncan, D&B corporate economist and chief statistician, said it appears from the survey that the increase will come only from companies that already are exporting. That trend "implies that if the export boom is to continue, long-term policies are needed to encourage nonexporters to participate in international trade," he said.

D&B said an earlier survey accurately predicted that 1989 exports would be weak compared with 1988.
