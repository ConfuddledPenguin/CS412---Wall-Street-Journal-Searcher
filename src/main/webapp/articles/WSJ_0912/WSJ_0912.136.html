Business Brief -- AmBase Corp.: Company Ends Its Attempt To Sell Reinsurance Unit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/12/90

AmBase Corp. said its Home Insurance Co. subsidiary has discontinued efforts to sell its US International Reinsurance Co. unit because it couldn't get an attractive offer. James J. Meenaghan, chairman of Home Insurance, said the company had negotiated with various potential buyers and it had reviewed one offer that exceeded the statutory value of USI Re, but decided against selling the company. "We decided the net proceeds (of this one offer) weren't enough because we believe the inherent value of USI Re is greater than the price we could obtain in today's market."

AmBase said the decision not to sell USI Re would have no impact on previously announced agreements to sell Home Insurance or a controlling interest in the company.

Last month, New York-based AmBase agreed to sell a 50.1% stake in the company to an investor group shortly after it had reached a separate agreement to sell Home Insurance to Vik Brothers International. AmBase said it would proceed with both offers until a definitive agreement is reached with one or the other.
