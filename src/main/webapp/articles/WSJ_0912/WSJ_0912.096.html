International Brief -- Amsterdam-Rotterdam Bank: Western Europe Consortium Seeks to Open Polish Bank

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/12/90

Amsterdam-Rotterdam Bank, or Amro, confirmed it is in a Western European banking consortium that is working on setting up a commercial bank in Warsaw. Amro, which recently merged with Algemene Bank Nederland, declined to identify its partners in the venture or provide detailed information. "There is a project that is under way, but at this stage there is nothing we can say about it," an Amro spokesman said. "We are only in the preliminary stage of applying for licenses and so on."

The spokesman confirmed that Amro and other banks are working in tandem with International Finance Corp., a World Bank affiliate, to set up a bank that will conduct syndicated activities. The IFC provides funding for development projects.

People in the Italian banking industry said Banca Commerciale Italiana, which has a representative office in Warsaw, and Credit Lyonnais are the other two Western banks in the consortium. They and other Western banks participate in the International Moscow Bank, founded last year.
