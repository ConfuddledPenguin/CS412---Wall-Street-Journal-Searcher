What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/12/90

A DEFICIT-REDUCTION PACT could be reached by today or tomorrow, say White House and congressional budget negotiators. Republicans and Democrats are converging on a compromise that would raise taxes by about $130 billion over five years, including an energy tax, a new luxury-goods levy and higher taxes on alcoholic beverages. ---

The Big Board approved a limited form of "off-hours" trading for large investors that could begin later this year, in a move that marks a step toward 24-hour stock trading.

---

Drexel could face a $50 million Big Board fine, following the exchange's request to bankruptcy court to initiate disciplinary proceedings against the collapsed securities firm.

---

Salomon agreed to pay $83.8 million to the Department of Energy to settle claims stemming from a probe of alleged violations of the govern ment's crude-oil price controls.

---

Chase Manhattan was forced by investors' uncertainties to reset the rate on $200 million of subordinated notes to yield 13.017%, up from the previous rate of 9.66% and just shy of the maximum permissible increase.

---

The fund that insures bank deposits could require Treasury assistance if a recession strikes, said the GAO. Some lawmakers urged an other boost in bank insurance rates.

---

First City Bancorp of Texas said it is considering seeking one or more money-center banks as minority partners to shore up the bank's finances and bolster its global standing.

---

Philip Morris appointed William I. Campbell as president and chief executive of its domestic tobacco subsidiary, succeeding Ehud Houminer.

---

A proposed accounting rule may be scaled back under pressure from banks and accounting firms. The proposal, involving debt issues, would make bank earnings more volatile.

---

Tenneco said its packaging unit is in talks to buy mills and timberland from Georgia-Pacific for about $740 million, extending the successful unit's pattern of acquisitions.

---

ASK Computer agreed to buy software-maker Ingres in an unusual four-way deal, in which ASK will use funds from stock it will sell to Hewlett-Packard and GM's EDS unit.

---

IBM will soon unveil several new computers that act as network hubs, which will put it in direct rivalry with Compaq, which controls the high end of that specialized market.

---

U.S. food-price inflation will be pushed up this year and next by the Persian Gulf crisis, according to Agriculture Department analysts.

---

Texas Instruments is asking Japanese semiconductor makers to pay higher royalties on a memory chip patent. The U.S. company's stock soared on news of its demand.
