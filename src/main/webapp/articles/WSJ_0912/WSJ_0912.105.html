International Brief -- Enasa: Spanish Aides Optimistic On Daimler's Buying Stake

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/12/90

In the search by Empresa Nacional de Autocamiones S.A., Spain's troubled state-owned truck maker, to find a foreign partner, Spanish hopes are growing that Daimler-Benz AG of West Germany will acquire an 80% stake in Enasa. Spanish officials said they are optimistic an accord with Daimler can be announced soon. A previous plan for Daimler to acquire Enasa jointly with MAN AG of West Germany foundered on competition objections by Bonn's cartel office. People familiar with the Daimler talks said West Germany has said it wouldn't object to Enasa's takeover by Daimler alone.

In the negotiations, points under discussion include government aid to Enasa. Daimler has asked for subsidies of more than 60 billion pesetas ($603 million) in return for taking over Enasa, which so far this year has had losses of about seven billion pesetas. Spain earlier rejected the subsidy sum as too high, as well as Daimler proposals for sharp cuts in Enasa's work force to about 4,700 from 5,700. Spain hopes for a takeover pact guaranteeing no layoffs, to win union agreement.
