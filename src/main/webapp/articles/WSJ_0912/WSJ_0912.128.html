Business Brief -- Enron Corp.: Company Reaches Accords On Power Project in Britain

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/12/90

Enron Corp. said agreements have been reached for power sales, joint-venture operations and gas supply for its proposed $1.1 billion power project in Teesside, England. The Houston-based energy concern said the 1,725-megawatt facility will be at ICI Chemicals & Polymers Ltd.'s Wilton Works plant in Teesside, in northeast England. ICI is a unit of Imperial Chemical Industries PLC.

The project is subject to a number of regulatory approvals and would add roughly 3% to the power-generating capacity of England and Wales. Sales agreements have been reached for all 1,725 megawatts of capacity.

The power sales agreements have an initial term of 15 years beginning April 1, 1993, with options to extend. Enron said four regional electricity companies are expected to assume a 50% equity ownership, while it will retain the remaining 50% stake. ICI has an option to purchase up to 10% of Enron's equity in the facility after commercial operation has begun. The company said it expects to realize income from certain project development fees this year, but didn't elaborate.
