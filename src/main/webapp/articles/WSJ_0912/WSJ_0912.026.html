Inside Track: Warner-Lambert Insiders Trim Stakes In Rare Move; Sales Are Relatively Heavy

  • Author: Alexandra Peers
  • Date: 09/12/90

NEW YORK -- Warner-Lambert Co. has been seized upon by money managers as a stock sheltered from the effects of recession. But some of the company's insiders are looking elsewhere for shelter. Insider selling has historically been rare at the Morris Plains, N.J., drug and consumer products company. This summer, however, presidents of three of the company's divisions have trimmed their holdings by $660,971 of stock, according to insider filings with the Securities and Exchange Commission.

Two of the sales occurred right around the time the stock hit its 12-month high of $70.125 on Aug. 1. Did the insiders believe the stock had peaked? They were out of the office yesterday at the company's annual strategic-planning meeting in Absecon, N.J., and couldn't be reached for comment.

But Peter Wolf, a spokesman for the company, said, "The (stock) market has got a lot of people making phone calls and looking to diversify." Since the officers "still retain significant holdings, (the selling) is not a display of lack of confidence. The company's doing swell. "Warner-Lambert stock hit its high last month on a prediction by Chairman Joseph D. Williams that per-share earnings would be up more than 16% this year. The same day, John F. Walsh, president of the company's international operations, sold 2,011 shares for $138,377. The shares represented 17% of his holdings.

On Aug. 8, Robert Serenbetz, president of the company's confectionary division, American Chicle, sold 4,466 shares for $226,469, according to SEC filings. That sale represented about one-third of his holdings.

Those recent sales, along with a 4,600-share sale late last June by Jay M. Gwynne, president of the company's Asia and Australia Capsugell division, totaled more insider selling than Warner-Lambert experienced in all of 1989 and the first five months of this year combined, according to SEC filings. Mr. Gwynne also sold about one-third of his holdings.

The three executives who sold shares got prices ranging from $64.25 to $68.81 a share. In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Warner-Lambert shares closed below that level, at $63.125, up 25 cents.

John Hoffman, associate director of research at Smith Barney, Harris Upham & Co., which is recommending purchase of the stock, said that although Warner-Lambert has declined in the month, so have the prices of other drug and consumer stocks. He said Smith Barney considers the stock "a predictable grower which would weather a recession."

A CHANGE OF CLOTHES: The stock of ClothesTime Inc., an Anaheim, Calif., apparel retailer, has nosedived from a high of $13.875 late last year to less than $3 lately.

Meanwhile, the company's chairman, Michael DeAngelo, has been on a selling spree. He has sold 600,000 shares since July 23 -- almost 60% of his holdings in the company -- for $2 to $3.75 apiece, SEC filings show.

As previously reported, late last year, Mr. DeAngelo sold 763,467 shares, according to SEC filings. Some of those shares fetched prices as high as $9.38, SEC filings show. Mr. DeAngelo declined to comment on the insider sales.

Elizabeth A. Armstrong, analyst at Prescott, Ball & Turben Inc., said that, for much of last year, the company experienced double-digit gains in sales at its established stores. But since about April, she said, there has been "relatively small increases, or negative growth in sales."

In over-the-counter trading, ClothesTime shares closed at $2.375, down 18.75 cents.

A STRIDENT BULL: Arnold Hiatt, chairman of Stride Rite Corp., made the largest insider stock purchase reported to the SEC last week.

Mr. Hiatt spent $2.4 million on 98,000 shares of the company, a purchase that increased his total holdings in the Cambridge, Mass., shoe retailer by more than one-third, SEC filings show. He declined to comment on his purchase.

The chairman paid an average price of $24.30 a share. In New York Stock Exchange trading, Stride Rite shares closed yesterday at $23.75, up 50 cents.

In the second quarter, ended June 1, Stride Rite had record earnings of 60 cents a share, up from 49 cents a share a year earlier.
