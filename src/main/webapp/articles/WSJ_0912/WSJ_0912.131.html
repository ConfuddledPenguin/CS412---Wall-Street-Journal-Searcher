Yields of Most CDs Fell in Latest Week

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/12/90

NEW YORK -- Yields on most certificates of deposit at major banks and brokerage firms declined in the week ended yesterday. Average yields on small-denomination CDs sold directly by banks (also called "consumer" CDs) were down slightly, according to Banxquote Money Markets, an information service. The average yield on a six-month consumer CD, for instance, eased to 7.38% from 7.42%, Banxquote said. On a five-year consumer CD, the average yield eased to 7.90% from 7.91%, according to the survey of 18 banks.

Average yields on CDs sold by leading broker-dealers fell more. The greatest decline was for a six-month broker-sold CD, which fell to an average of 7.75% from 7.92%, Banxquote said. The average yield for a two-year broker-sold CD fell to 8.13% from 8.22%.

Average yields for large-denomination CDs at major banks, which typically require deposits of $90,000 or more, were flat to down slightly. The average yield on a one-year jumbo CD remained at 7.72%, while the average for a five-year maturity eased to 8.25% from 8.27%.
