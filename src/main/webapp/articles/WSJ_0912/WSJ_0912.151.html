Cycare Systems Inc. Plans Stock Buy-Back

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/12/90

PHOENIX, Ariz. -- CyCare Systems Inc. said it would buy back as many as 500,000 shares, or about 9% of the shares outstanding, from time to time on the open market. The company, which had about $3.3 million in cash on hand for the second quarter ended June 30, said it was initiating the buy-back because it feels the stock is undervalued. In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, CyCare stock closed at $5.625, up 12.5 cents, near its low for the year. The stock's high for 1990 was $10.375.

CyCare said it would use the stock for employee stock option and incentive plans. The buy-back follows last year's program, when the company bought 420,000 shares, or 18% of its shares, for $6 a share from Hospital Corp. of America.

CyCare provides computerized processing, services and systems to the health-care industry.
