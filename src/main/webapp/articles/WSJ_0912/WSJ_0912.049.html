Business Brief -- Burritt Interfinancial Bancorp: Stock Plunges as Company Forecasts Drop in Earnings

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/12/90

Burritt Interfinancial Bancorp's stock plunged on its announcement that it expects to report that third-quarter non-performing loans, those not earning interest, will rise and that earnings in the third and fourth quarters will drop. The New Britain, Conn., bank holding company estimates that non-performing assets will rise to 5% to 6% of assets for the third quarter from about 1.1% in the second quarter. As a result, the company foresees a "significant decline" in profit for the third and fourth quarters.

In 1989, Burritt earned $1.5 million, or 44 cents a share, in the third quarter and $1.4 million, or 43 cents a share, in the fourth quarter.

The announcement temporarily halted trading of Burritt stock yesterday; when trading resumed, its stock sank to $3.50 a share, down $1.25, in national over-the-counter trading.
