Business Brief -- Sun Electric Corp.: Suntronic Unit Is Acquired By Managers, Knightsbridge

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/12/90

Sun Electric Corp., Crystal Lake, Ill., said it sold its Suntronic Systems division, which sells service station management systems, to a company owned by Knightsbridge Inc., a Chicago-based investment concern, and Suntronic's managers. Though Sun Electric didn't disclose the value of the transaction, it said one-half of the purchase price was paid in cash and the balance in senior preferred stock in Suntronic Technology Group Inc., the acquiring company.

Sun Electric said that, as a result of accounting adjustments associated with the deal, it will record a third-quarter pretax charge of $1.2 million. In the 1989 third quarter, Sun Electric had a loss of $3.5 million, or 44 cents a share, after a $2.6 million charge related to restructuring.

It said the Suntronic unit, which has annual sales of about $9 million, was sold to allow Sun Electric to focus on further developing and expanding its core business of making and selling diagnostic and test equipment for the motor vehicle industry.
