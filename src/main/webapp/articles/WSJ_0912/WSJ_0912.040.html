Business Brief -- Warehouse Club Inc.: Sale-Leaseback Transaction Runs Into Snag, Firm Says

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/12/90

Warehouse Club Inc. said a sale-leaseback refinancing transaction for as many as six facilities, which the discount merchandiser announced last month, won't close by early September, and may not close at all. The buyer, which Warehouse hasn't identified, "has informed the company that it is having difficulty in securing the required financing because of recent changes in the real estate financing and capital markets," Warehouse said in Chicago.

Warehouse added that it "cannot at present determine when or whether the transaction will close."

Warehouse had said earlier it expected proceeds of about $2.5 million from the sale/leaseback arrangement, after repayment of debt on the properties and certain other related expenses. The company said in August that it planned to use the proceeds for working capital.

Warehouse also said it will take a $1.8 million charge in the year ending Sept. 30 to cover the closing earlier this week of two unprofitable outlets, one in suburban Pittsburgh and one in Columbus, Ohio.
