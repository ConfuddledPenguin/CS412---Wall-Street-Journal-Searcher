Treasury Plans to Sell $18.4 Billion in Bills

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/12/90

WASHINGTON -- The Treasury plans to pay down about $18.43 billion on the federal debt with the sale Monday of about $18.4 billion in short-term bills. Maturing bills outstanding total $36.83 billion. The offering will be divided evenly between 13-week and 26-week bills, which will be dated Sept. 20, 1990, and mature on Dec. 20, 1990, and March 21, 1991, respectively.

Tenders for the bills, available in minimum $10,000 denominations, must be received by 1 p.m. EDT Monday at the Treasury or at Federal Reserve banks or branches.
