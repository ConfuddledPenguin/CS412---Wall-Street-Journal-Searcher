Ex-Aide of ZZZZBest Co. Settles SEC's Civil Charges

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/12/90

WASHINGTON (AP) -- The former chief financial officer of ZZZZBest Co., the scandal-wracked carpet-cleaning concern, agreed to be banned from doing any public auditing work, the Securities and Exchange Commission said. Bruce T. Anderson, a certified public accountant, agreed to the premanent ban in a settlement of an administrative action, without admitting or denying wrongdoing.

He is barred from auditing the books of companies registered with the SEC or seeking SEC registration to sell securities to the public.

In a civil suit by the SEC last month in California, Mr. Anderson was accused of knowingly helping ZZZZBest file allegedly fraudulent financial statements with the SEC, or recklessly disregarding evidence that the documents might be false.

Mr. Anderson, ZZZZBest's financial chief from July 1986 to July 1987, agreed to a permanent injunction barring him from engaging in violations of securities laws.

The company's founder, Barry Minkow, was convicted last year on various counts in connection with a scheme to bilk investors and banks of more than $100 million. He is serving a 25-year sentence. Mr. Anderson was not a defendant in the criminal proceedings.
