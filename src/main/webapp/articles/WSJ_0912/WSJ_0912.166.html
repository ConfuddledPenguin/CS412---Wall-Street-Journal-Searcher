Lotus Trims Goals For Growth, Cites Maturity of Market

  • Author: William M. Bulkeley
  • Date: 09/12/90

CAMBRIDGE, Mass. -- Lotus Development Corp. said it has lowered its growth and profitability goals, reflecting what it calls a "maturation" of the software market. Jim P. Manzi, chairman and president of the software maker, said, however, that growth and profit trends should become more predictable than they have been. Lotus is aiming for long-term revenue growth in the 20% to 25% annual range, eased from a previous 25% target. Last year, revenue grew 19% to $556 million.

Mr. Manzi said the company also has scaled back its goal of 25% pretax profit margins. But he noted that the new target of a 20% to 25% range would represent an improvement over the company's actual margins of 15% last year and 17% in 1988.

Lotus's stock fell sharply yesterday in the wake of reports that the targets had been cut to 20%. Lotus said the report was incorrect, and the stock rebounded from a low of $15.25 a share but still closed at $16.50, down $1.50, in national over-the-counter trading.

Mr. Manzi also said that the company is raising its target for return on equity to a range of 25% to 30% from 25%.

He said the lower growth goal reflects slower growth in sales of personal computers. However, he said that because Lotus will be getting more of its revenue from recurring software maintenance fees and product upgrades rather than from PC sales, the revenue will be more predictable than it has been.

"This isn't rocket science," he said. "There is a long-term dynamic going on in the entire industry." He declined to make predictions for specific periods. Lotus earlier disappointed investors by forecasting an earnings downturn for the third quarter.
