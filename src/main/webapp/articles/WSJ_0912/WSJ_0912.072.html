New Securities Issues

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/12/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report. CORPORATE

Texas Utilities Electric Co. -- $250 million of first mortgage and collateral trust bonds, due Sept. 1, 2020, priced at par to yield 10.625%. The bonds, which are non-callable for five years, were priced at a spread of 165 basis points above the Treasury's 30-year bond. Rated Baa-2 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and triple-B by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Salomon Brothers Inc.

MUNICIPAL

Connecticut Housing Finance Authority -- $278.2 million of single-family housing mortgage finance program bonds, 1990 Series B, Subseries B-1 through B-4, tentatively priced by a PaineWebber Inc. group to yield from 6.10% in 1991 to 7.70% in 2022. Subseries B-1 consists of $115.4 million of 7.50% bonds priced at par due in 2008. Subseries B-2 consists of $14.1 million of bonds. Current interest serial bonds are priced at par to yield from 6.10% in 1991 to 6.65% in 1995. Subseries B-3 consists of $25 million of 7.65% term bonds priced at par due in 2009 and $25 million of 7.70% term bonds priced at par due in 2022. Subseries B-4 consists of $98.8 million of bonds. Current interest serial bonds are priced at par to yield from 6.50% in 1995 to 7.00% in 2000. There are about $40 million of term bonds priced at par to yield 7.30% in 2003. The bonds are rated double-A-plus by S&P. A double-A rating is expected from Moody's. Interest on the Subseries B-2 and B-3 bonds will be treated as a preference item in calculating the federal alternative minimum tax that may be imposed on certain investors. The Subseries B-1 and B-4 bonds are not subject to the tax.

Chicago -- $242.3 million of O'Hare International Airport airport revenue bonds, Series 1990 A and B, due Jan. 1, 1995-2004, 2010, 2016 and 2018, tentatively priced by a Merrill Lynch Capital Markets group to yield from 6.80% in 1995 to 7.87% in 2016. Current interest serial bonds are priced to yield from 6.80% in 1995 to 7.60% in 2004; 7.25% term bonds due in 2010 are priced at 95 to yield 7.75%; 7.50% term bonds due in 2016 are priced at 96 to yield 7.87%; 6% term bonds due in 2018 are priced at 80 to yield 7.78%. Interest on the Series A and Series B bonds will be treated as a preference item in calculating the federal alternative minimum tax. Ratings of single-A-1/single-A-plus are expected.

Charlotte, N.C. -- $81.2 million of general obligation bonds, including $15.3 million of public improvement bonds, Series 1990, $43.7 million of public improvement bonds, Series 1990 A, and $22.2 million of water and sewer revenue bonds, Series 1990, due Oct. 1, 1991-2009, via a Merrill Lynch Capital Markets group. The bonds, rated triple-A/triple-A, were priced for reoffering to yield from 5.80% in 1991 to 6.60% in 1999. The current interest serial bonds are priced to yield from 5.80% in 1991 to 6.60% in 1999. Bonds due in 2000 through 2009 are not formally reoffered.

Wisconsin Housing and Economic Development Authority -- $79.3 million of home ownership revenue bonds, 1990 Series D and E, due March 1 and Sept. 1, 1992-2000, 2004, 2010, 2015, 2018 and 2021, tentatively priced by a Lehman Brothers group to yield from 6.40% in 1992 to 8.03% in 2021. Series D consists of about $23.4 million of bonds. There are $7.7 million of 7.70% bonds priced at par due Sept. 1, 2010, and $15.8 million of tender option bonds due Sept. 1, 2015 that are not formally reoffered. Series E consists of $55.9 million of bonds. Current interest serial bonds are priced at par to yield from 6.40% in 1992 to 7.30% in 2000. There are $4.9 million of 7.70% term bonds priced at par due Sept. 1, 2004, $14.8 million of tender option bonds due Sept. 1, 2018, that are not formally reoffered and $27.7 million of 8% term bonds priced at 99.63 to yield 8.03%, due March 1, 2021. Ratings of double-A/single-A-plus are expected. Interest on the Series E bonds will be treated as a preference item in calculating the federal alternative minimum tax. The Series D bonds are not subject to the tax.

Tennessee Housing Development Agency -- $72.4 million of home ownership program housing put bonds, Issue T, due Sept. 1, 1991, priced by a Goldman Sachs & Co. group to yield 6.60% in 1991. The issue consists of four tranches of bonds, which mature in 1996, 2002, 2011 and 2023. All of the bonds carry a mandatory put on Sept. 1, 1991, when the holder must allow the issuer to repurchase the security. The issue is then to be converted into a long-term security with the following maturities: $3.5 million of bonds that mature July 1, 1996, $6.1 million of bonds that mature July 1, 2002, $15.9 million of bonds that mature July 1, 2011, and $46.9 million of bonds that mature July 1, 2023. Ratings of VMIG-1/single-A are expected from Moody's. Ratings of single-A-1-plus/single-A-plus are expected from S&P.

J.C. Penney Master Credit Card Trust Series D -- $425 million of 8.70% asset-backed certificates offered at 99 23/32 to yield 8.97% through First Boston Corp. The securities were priced to yield 72 basis points over three-year Treasury notes. The securities are expected to be rated triple-A by Moody's and S&P, based on the quality of the underlying loans and a letter of credit covering 12% of the deal by Credit Suisse. The seller of the underlying credit-card receivables is J.C. Penney Receivables Inc. The servicer is J.C. Penney Co. The expected maturity is May 16, 1994, and the legal maturity is Sept. 15, 1995. There is a three-year average life.

EUROBONDS

National Bank of Hungary (Hungary) -- 200 million marks of 10% bonds due Oct. 10, 1995, priced at 100 1/4 to yield 9.93%, via Deutsche Bank. Fees 2.

ANZ Banking Corp. (Australia) -- $200 million of variable-rate notes due October 2002, via Merrill Lynch International Ltd. Sinking fund; average life of 10 years. Initial margin is 27 1/2 basis points above the three-month London interbank offered rate. Alternatively, Libor plus 60 basis points for the first five years and Libor plus 70 basis points thereafter. Commissions undisclosed.

Daiwa Kosho Co. (Japan) -- $180 million of Eurobonds due Sept. 27, 1994, with equity-purchase warrants, indicating a 5 1/8% coupon at par, via Daiwa Europe Ltd. Each $5,000 bond carries one warrant, exercisable from Oct. 15, 1990, through Sept. 20, 1994, to buy shares at an expected premium of 2 1/2% to the closing price when terms are fixed Tuesday. Fees 2 1/4.

Sumitomo Heavy Industries Ltd. (Japan) -- $340 million of Eurobonds due Oct. 4, 1994, with equity-purchase warrants, indicating a 5 1/8% coupon at par, via Daiwa Europe Ltd. Guaranteed by Sumitomo Bank. Each $5,000 bond carries one warrant, exercisable from Oct. 18, 1990, through Sept. 20, 1994, to buy shares at an expected premium of 2 1/2% to the closing price when terms are fixed Monday. Fees 2 1/4.

Toyota Motor Credit Corp. (Japan) -- 75 million European currency units of 10 5/8% Eurobonds due June 6, 1994, at 102.175 to yield 10.40% less fees via Paribas Capital Markets Ltd. Fungible with existing 150 million ECU issue. Fees 1 5/8.

ABB Capital B.V. (Swiss-Swedish parent) -- 100 million Swiss francs of 8% loan certificates (Schuldscheindarlehen) due Sept. 25, 1992, via Union Bank of Switzerland. Fees undisclosed.
