Oryx Energy Buys 17% of Its Shares From Some Trusts

  • Author: Patricia Ann McKanic
  • Date: 09/12/90

DALLAS -- Oryx Energy Co. said it paid $968 million to buy 17% of its common shares outstanding from several trusts controlled by the founding family of Sun Co. Oryx, an oil and gas exploration and production company, also said it would convert an additional 7.3 million common shares held by the Pew family trusts into a new issue of non-voting preferred stock that a trustee is expected to sell immediately, eliminating the Pew family's 27% stake in Oryx. Oryx was spun off from Sun in 1988 as Sun Exploration & Production Co. and has since changed its name.

The trustee for the family, Glenmede Trust Co. of Philadelphia, couldn't be reached for comment. Oryx said the transaction resulted after "it became apparent that Glenmede's long-term objectives were incompatible with Oryx Energy's strategic plans."

Oryx said Glenmede had sought a higher yield on its investment, pressing for an increase in the energy concern's 30-cent-a-share quarterly dividend. Robert P. Hauptfuhrer, chairman and chief executive officer, said that because of extensive cash needs for the company's exploration and production activities, "it is not our intention to increase our dividend."

Oryx paid Glenmede $53.75 a share for the 18 million common shares. In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange yesterday, Oryx shares closed at $53.625, down 25 cents. The company announced the buy-back after the market closed.

Oryx, which currently has 105 million common shares outstanding, said it will have about 87 million shares outstanding after the buy-back is completed and the preferred shares are converted back to common for new owners.

Oryx said it will pay for the shares with debt it said it will eventually eliminate through divestitures. The company said it has signed letters of intent to sell about $150 million in oil and gas assets by year-end and expects to divest another $600 million of assets by September 1991.

Separately, the company adopted a shareholders rights plan under which holders of Oryx common would be entitled to purchase one one-thousandth of a share of a new Series A preference stock at an exercise price of $150. The rights are exercisable, among other things, when a 20% or more stake in the company is acquired.
