Marketing & Media: Lifting Liquor Ad Ban In Canada Is Proposed

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/12/90

OTTAWA -- A Canadian regulatory agency proposed removing a ban on liquor advertising on television and radio. The only alcoholic beverages that can be advertised now on television and radio are beer, some wine, coolers and other beverages with less than 7% alcohol.

The proposed change was requested by the Association of Canadian Distillers. The industry group said the liquor advertising ban is unconstitutional. It also has initiated a legal challenge of the ban.

The Canadian Radio-television and Telecommunications Commission said it will make its final ruling on the proposed change after assessing public comments on issues such as the effect of various types of alcoholic beverages on driving offenses, domestic violence and work absenteeism.

Under the proposed amendments, the commission would continue to clear all alcoholic beverages ads before they were broadcast.
