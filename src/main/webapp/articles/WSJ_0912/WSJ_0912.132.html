Letters to the Editor: Homestead No Asylum From the Taxman

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/12/90

Your Aug. 3 article "Florida Is Gaining Reputation as Haven for Debtors Who Seek to Shelter Wealth" was informative and well-researched. However, another bit of information is crucial to anyone considering filing for bankruptcy. Although the article correctly stated that state laws protect a homestead from the claims of creditors in bankruptcy, with Texas and Florida having particularly generous homestead exemptions, a homestead is not protected from the IRS. Indeed, the Bankruptcy Code specifically provides that the homestead exemption does not provide protection from a properly filed tax lien. The IRS routinely seizes and sells the homes of the delinquent taxpayers.

Geoffey D. Fallon

Trial Attorney
