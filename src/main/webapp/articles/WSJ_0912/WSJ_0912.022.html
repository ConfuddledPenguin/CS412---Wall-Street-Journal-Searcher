Salomon to Pay $83.8 Million On Oil Claims

  • Author: Michael Siconolfi
  • Date: 09/12/90

NEW YORK -- Salomon Inc. said it agreed in principle to pay the Department of Energy $83.8 million to settle claims stemming from an investigation of alleged violations of the government's crude-oil price controls. Salomon said the settlement won't lead to a charge against earnings. The parent of Salomon Brothers Inc. disputed many of the department's claims but said it agreed to settle to avoid years of litigation.

The settlement is the latest from a string of claims made by the Energy Department. For several years, the department has been investigating the transactions of thousands of companies suspected of violating the government's crude-oil price controls, which were in effect during the sharp rise in oil prices in the wake of the 1973 Arab oil embargo.

The Reagan administration terminated the controls in 1981.

In September 1988, the Energy Department started administrative proceedings against Salomon, seeking payment of as much as $108 million, plus interest. But Salomon said the department's recent administrative proceedings "have reduced significantly the company's maximum potential exposure" under the government's allegations.

The planned settlement would be one of the few jolts for Salomon so far this year; both its securities business and its Phibro Energy oil unit have been thriving, particularly in the wake of the recent Iraqi attack against Kuwait and the resulting wild swings in oil and stock prices.

The settlement caps a four-year battle between Salomon and the Energy Department. In 1987, a federal judge ordered Salomon to turn over to the department virtually all its documents relating to the company's purchases and sales of crude oil from 1973 to 1980.

The judge's order came despite complaints by Salomon about the "voluminous" amount of data the department was seeking -- specified in 103 pages of subpoenas. The Energy Department was looking into whether Salomon resold crude oil at an appropriate price under the government's regulations when they were in effect.

In 1987, Salomon separately settled with the Energy Department concerning crude-oil sales from 1973 to 1977 by paying about $16.3 million, including interest.
