Nissan's Infiniti Expects Sales to More Than Double

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/12/90

DEARBORN, Mich. -- Nissan Motor Co.'s Infiniti luxury division, which stumbled in its first months on the U.S. market, said it's now on track to sell 40,000 cars in the 1991 model year, which begins Oct. 1, up from about 17,000 cars in the current period. A key to Infiniti's plans is the new G20 luxury sedan, which goes on sale this fall at a starting price of $17,500, division officials said at a product preview here yesterday. At that price, the G20, powered by a four-cylinder, 140-horsepower engine, is less expensive than many of its European competitors.

To enhance the image of its flagship Q45 sedan, Infiniti said it will offer an optional $4,000 "active suspension" system, one of the first of its type offered in the U.S. The system uses computer-controlled, hydraulic pumps that drive the wheels down into the road to counteract the jolts from bumps. Infiniti raised the 1991 model-year base price of the Q45 by 2.6%, or $1,000, to $39,000.

Infiniti officials conceded that the brand got off to a slower-than-expected start in the U.S., and suffered from comparisons with Toyota Motor Corp.'s more successful Lexus division. Lexus has sold 40,146 cars as of the end of August, compared with 13,303 for Infiniti.

William R. Bruce, Infiniti's vice president and general manager, said the division stuck too long with its initial arty ad campaign. Moreover, the division took longer to establish its dealer network than expected.

Still, Mr. Bruce said the division expects sales to rise to 80,000-90,000 cars a year within five years.
