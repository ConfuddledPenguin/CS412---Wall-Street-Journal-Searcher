Tenneco Unit, Georgia-Pacific Hold Sale Talks --- Packaging Corp. of America Would Pay $740 Million For Mills, Timberland

  • Author: Dianna Solis
  • Date: 09/12/90

HOUSTON -- Tenneco Inc. said its packaging unit is taking part in talks to buy mills and timberland from Georgia-Pacific Corp. for about $740 million. The proposed purchase would be the most ambitious yet by Tenneco's Packaging Corp. of America unit based in Evanston, Ill. Already this year, Packaging Corp. has acquired a corrugated products concern and a maker of specialty paperboard trays. The unit, despite recent softness in some of its profit margins, remains one of Tenneco's most successful operations. The unit had net income of $200 million on sales of $1.3 billion in 1989.

In Atlanta, a spokeswoman for Georgia-Pacific said the negotiations with Tenneco are in line with its plans to sell off certain assets to reduce some $4 billion in debt incurred in its acquisition of Great Northern Nekoosa Corp. But the asset sales are strategic and not essential to handling its debt load, a spokeswoman said.

Georgia-Pacific, a forest-product concern, said it also is continuing negotiations "with several interested parties" for the possible sale of its huge paper mill in Toledo, Ore., as well as box plants in Modesto, Calif. and Olympia, Wash.

A Tenneco spokesman said GE Capital Corp., a finance unit of General Electric Corp. of Fairfield, Conn., and other major investors will supply a "substantial amount" of investment capital for the acquisition, but wouldn't specify the amount. GE Capital would purchase the paper mills in Valdosta, Ga., and Tomahawk, Wis., and lease them to Packaging Corp.

Similarly, John Hancock Mutual Life Insurance Co. of Boston and Metropolitan Life Insurance Co., New York, have agreed in principle with Tenneco to acquire 540,000 acres of Georgia-Pacific timberland in Wisconsin, Michigan, Georgia and Florida, and lease it to the Tenneco packaging unit. Packaging Corp. currently manages more than 600,000 acres of timberland. The potential transaction also includes the purchase of 19 Georgia-Pacific box converting plants.

A Tenneco spokesman said the company was "confident" it would face no antitrust problems with the potential acquisition.

Packaging Corp. makes conventional brown boxes, as well as specialty packaging such as foil delicatessen trays and foam egg cartons. It operates more than 60 facilities world-wide.

Tenneco has pared down its disparate holdings in recent years, chiefly by selling its immense oil unit in 1988, but it still has far-flung holdings, including a large farm-equipment unit, a natural-gas distribution business, a ship-building unit and operations in auto parts and chemicals.
