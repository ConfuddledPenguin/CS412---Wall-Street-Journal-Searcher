Business Brief -- Kimberly-Clark Corp.: Newsprint-Mill Negotiations Over Sale Are Broken Off

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/12/90

Kimberly-Clark Corp. and New York Times Co. said they broke off negotiations to sell their jointly owned newsprint mill to Kruger Inc. after the Montreal-based paper company failed to secure financing for the purchase. The companies say Spruce Falls Power & Paper Co. in remote Kapuskasing, Ont., remains for sale. "We are disappointed," the companies said in a joint statement. "We felt that Kruger was in a better position to capitalize on the strengths of Spruce Falls."

Kimberly-Clark, a Dallas paper and consumer-products company, will not reverse the $44 million charge it took in the second quarter anticipating the sale of the mill. Losses at the mill have hurt both companies. A Kruger spokesman declined to comment.

Kimberly-Clark stock fell 12.5 cents yesterday to close at $75.50 a share in New York Stock Exchange composite trading, while New York Times stock also fell 12.5 cents a share to close at $19 in American Stock Exchange composite trading.
