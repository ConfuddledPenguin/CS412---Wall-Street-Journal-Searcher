Business Brief -- Campeau Corp.: Units Granted Extensions For Reorganization Plans

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/12/90

Federated Department Stores Inc. and Allied Stores Corp. were granted extensions of their exclusive right to come up with reorganization plans until Feb. 28, 1991. The Campeau Corp. units filed for bankruptcy-court protection on January 15.

U.S. Bankruptcy Court Judge J. Vincent Aug Jr. had already extended the deadline to today.

Once the so-called exclusivity period expires, Allied and Federated's creditors may file their own reorganization plans with the court.

Federated and Allied told Judge Aug that they were not yet ready to file the reorganization plan. But several creditor committees filed responses and objections to the lengthy extensions.

Jay Indyke, a lawyer for the Allied creditors, said his committee wanted the deadline extended only until Nov. 12. "In spite of the fact that we've been told that the debtors are moving toward {reorganization} plan negotiations . . . were only seeing bits and pieces of it and we don't have a complete picture of where they're going," he said.

David Heiman, the lawyer who represents Allied and Federated, could not be reached for comment.

It is not unusual for the 120-day exclusivity period to be extended several times in complex Chapter 11 proceedings.
