M.A. Hanna to Sell Two Operations, Buy Up to 10% of Its Stock

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/12/90

CLEVELAND -- M.A. Hanna Co. said it plans to sell its Day International printing products and textile products units and that its board authorized the repurchase of as many as 2.8 million common shares, or about 10% of its 27.9 million shares outstanding. The specialty chemicals concern said it wants to focus on its core businesses. Of the stock buy-back, it said its common is "greatly undervalued and represents one of the best investments we can make for our shareholders." It said the buy-back won't jeopardize its ability to fund growth plans, for which it said it has strong cash flow and solid balance sheet.

Stock repurchases may be made in the open market or in privately negotiated transactions, the company said.

Hanna said it retained the investment banking firm of Salomon Brothers Inc., New York, to help with the divestiture of its Day International Printing Products Co. and Day International Textile Products businesses. The printing products unit makes polymer printing blankets and other products used in the offset printing industry. It is based in Dayton, Ohio, and has annual sales of about $100 million. Sales of the Greenville, S.C., textile unit weren't disclosed.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Hanna closed at $17.75 a share, up 12.5 cents.
