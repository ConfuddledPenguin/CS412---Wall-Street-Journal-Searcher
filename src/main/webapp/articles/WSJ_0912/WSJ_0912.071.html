International Brief -- Fisons PLC: Pretax Profit Surges 35% To $167 Million for Half

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/12/90

Fisons PLC, a British pharmaceuticals and laboratory equipment maker, said its pretax profit soared 35% in the first six months of the year, helped by first-time contributions from VG Instruments. Pretax profit rose to #90.2 million ($167 million) from #67.0 million ($124 million) a year earlier, at the upper end of market forecasts.

Profit after tax and minority interests, or earnings attributable to shareholders in the absence of any extraordinary items, increased by 33% to #70.3 million from #52.8 million pounds a year before. Per-share earnings rose 20% to 10.4 pence from 8.7 pence.

The company announced that it would raise the interim dividend by 21% to 2.85 pence from 2.35 pence a year earlier.

Sales grew by 20% to #583 million from #485 million a year earlier. Trading profit rose 35% to #89 million from #66 million a year earlier.

Fisons said that its spread of business activities coupled with its size and skills, placed it in a positive position to compete in high growth international markets.

The company added that it will sell two U.K. equipment manufacturing businesses to Sanyo Electric Co., Ltd. of Japan for #10 million, in an effort to concentrate manufacturing resources on products of higher technology.
