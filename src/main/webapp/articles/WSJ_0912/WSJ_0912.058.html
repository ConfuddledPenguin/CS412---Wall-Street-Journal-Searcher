Business Brief -- Equifax Inc.: Meetings Set for Holders To Vote on Stock Swap

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/12/90

Equifax Inc. said it and Telecredit Inc. set special meetings of shareholders for Nov. 7 to vote on Equifax's acquisition of Telecredit in a stock swap currently valued at about $489 million. Equifax, an information-services concern in Atlanta, said shareholders of record of the two companies as of Sept. 21 will be entitled to vote at the respective meetings.

The definitive agreement calls for Telecredit shareholders to receive 2.69 shares of Equifax common for each share held. In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange, Equifax closed at $16.375 a share, down from $19.50 a share when the deal was announced June 21. The exchange will bring the price for Telecredit to $44.05 a share. However, Equifax has the option, but not the obligation, of increasing the exchange ratio to bring it to a $50-a-share value. Telecredit, a check authorizer and credit processor based in Los Angeles, conversely can terminate the transaction if the deal doesn't fetch a bottom price of $50 a share.

Both companies said that proxy materials will be sent to holders about Sept. 21.
