Swiss Bank Announces Plans to Dissolve Syndicate

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/12/90

ZURICH, Switzerland -- The largest bank syndicate in Switzerland, which dominates the country's bond market, will be dissolved, probably at the end of the year, according to a spokeswoman for Swiss Bank Corp. However, a Swiss Big Bank syndicate statement by Union Bank of Switzerland, or UBS, gave no indication that any decision had been made. It said an announcement would be made Sept. 26, but did not specify the subject. Swiss Bank Corp., UBS and Credit Swiss are the dominant members of the syndicate; a number of smaller banks also belong to the group.

The development follows -- but goes far beyond -- a government decree this week ordering the abolition of several cartel-like agreements in the banking industry. The dissolution of the syndicate would mean that smaller, rival syndicates will have better opportunities in the bond market.

A bank researcher said the change was sooner than expected and that small and foreign banks will compete better, but noted that the placing power of big banks will always be needed.

On Monday, Swiss Economics Minister Jean-Pascal Delamuraz ordered the syndicate to drop by the end of the year a so-called loyalty clause that prevents members of the syndicate from joining outside underwriting houses.

Jules Keller, a board member of Big Bank rival Warburg S.G. Banque Soditic SA, said ending the syndicate goes much further than abolishing the loyalty clause. The loyalty clause, however, was the main problem, he said.

The syndicate controls between 75% and 80% of the Swiss franc foreign bond market by banning members from joining other groups.
