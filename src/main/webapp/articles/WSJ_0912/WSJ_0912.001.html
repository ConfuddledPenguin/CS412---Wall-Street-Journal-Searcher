Kansas G&E Says It Is Negotiating To Be Acquired

  • Author: Carolyn Phillips
  • Date: 09/12/90

Kansas Gas & Electric Co., still cool to an $857 million hostile tender offer from Kansas City Power & Light Co., said it is negotiating to be acquired by a company it wouldn't name. The embattled Kansas G&E disclosed the talks in a filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission and also said it would provide certain "non-public information" about itself to Kansas City P&L, giving that company a chance to revise its $27-a-share offer. Kansas City P&L has said its offer was based on public information about Kansas G&E and could possibly be adjusted.

In a letter to Kansas City P&L President A. Drue Jennings, Kansas G&E Chairman Wilson K. Cadman offered the same "information . . . provided by KG&E to other third parties who have expressed an interest in exploring certain combinations with KG&E or a purchase of KG&E assets," subject to confidentiality agreements. Mr. Cadman also informed Mr. Jennings of the negotiations with the unidentified company and added that, "moreover, the company may also enter into negotiations with one or more other parties."

A spokesman for Kansas G&E wouldn't go beyond a company statement about the filing.

A spokesman for Kansas City Power & Light said last night, "It's an interesting development and we're anxious to talk to them ourselves. We look forward to seeing the same information that's being supplied to other parties."

Kansas G&E has spurned the Kansas City P&L offer, calling it inadequate and overly conditional and saying that it "raises substantial and serious legal questions and numerous uncertainties under state and federal statutes."

But the $27-a-share offer still tops Kansas G&E's recent stock price. In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange, Kansas G&E closed yesterday at $24.50, down 37.5 cents. Kansas City P&L rose 37.5 cents to $21.875 in Big Board trading.

Kansas City P&L's offer, made in late July, is set to expire Friday. As of Aug. 20, about 43% of Kansas G&E's common shares had been tendered.
