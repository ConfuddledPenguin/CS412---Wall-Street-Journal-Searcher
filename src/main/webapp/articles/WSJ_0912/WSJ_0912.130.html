Raiders Sign 20-Year Pact To Remain in Los Angeles

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/12/90

LOS ANGELES -- The Los Angeles Raiders football franchise has signed a 20-year agreement to remain in Los Angeles, ending years of uncertainty for managing general partner Al Davis, several California cities and exasperated fans. Under the terms of the agreement, the private management of the Los Angeles Memorial Coliseum will renovate the 67-year-old facility at a cost of about $145 million, which will include the construction of 204 lucrative "luxury boxes," a key demand of Mr. Davis and a perk he said he had been promised when the team moved from Oakland in 1982.

Since the Raiders left Oakland in 1982, the team has been involved in litigation with the National Football League, the city of Oakland and the Coliseum Commission. The Raiders considered moving to the Los Angeles suburb of Irwindale, were courted by Sacramento and earlier this year agreed to return to Oakland when the city offered a package worth $602 million, which was later reduced, and then finally squelched by community opposition. Meanwhile, Raiders attendance last year dwindled to less than 50,000 per game in a stadium that seats more than 90,000. The renovated Coliseum will seat about 72,000.
