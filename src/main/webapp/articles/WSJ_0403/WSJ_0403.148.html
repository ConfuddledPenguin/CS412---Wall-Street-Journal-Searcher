Marketing & Media: Pearson and Eslevier End Talks on Merger

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 04/03/90

Rumors of a broken engagement have been circulating for months, but now it's official: Pearson PLC of Britain and Elsevier N.V. of the Netherlands say they are no longer discussing a merger. In September 1988, the two publishing companies announced plans for cooperation and cross-shareholdings, saying they might eventually merge. Pearson acquired 22% of Elsevier, which in turn has a 9.4% stake in the British company.

Since then, the fall of the pound and a surge in Elsevier's share price has made the Dutch company too expensive for Pearson to contemplate, officials of the two companies say. The option of merging the companies into a 50-50 joint venture would have been unacceptable to British investors, an Elsevier spokesman said. Meanwhile, the takeover threats that appeared to be a major reason for the alliance have receded.

The two sides haven't managed to find much scope for cooperation. So far, the only project involves the use of Pearson expertise for a small Elsevier unit offering Dutch financial data via telephone lines.

But the two companies remain friendly, a Pearson official said. "We still regard Elsevier as our long-term European partner," he said, adding that the two are talking about combining certain ventures in medical publishing and may collaborate on a new financial weekly that would be distributed for free on the Continent.
