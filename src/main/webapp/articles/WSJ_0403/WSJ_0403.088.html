Cameco to Sell 33% Of a Uranium Mine For $136.8 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/03/90

SASKATOON, Saskatchewan -- Canadian Mining & Energy Corp., or Cameco, said it agreed to sell one-third of its wholly owned Rabbit Lake uranium mine in Saskatchewan to a unit of Uranerzbergbau G.m.b.H. of West Germany for 160 million Canadian dollars (US$136.8 million). Cameco will use proceeds from the sale, to Uranerz Exploration & Mining Ltd., to reduce debt to about C$325 million from about C$515 million at year end, said Thomas Gorman, Cameco vice president, finance.

Cameco, one of the non-communist world's largest and lowest-cost uranium producers, is cleaning up its balance sheet as its owners prepare for an initial public offering of shares. The governments of Saskatchewan and Canada, which respectively own 61.5% and 38.5% of Cameco, hoped to proceed with the issue last year but it was delayed as uranium prices fell below $10 a pound, or less than one-quarter of their 1979 peak. Mr. Gorman said a modest rebound to $8.80 a pound in March from $8.70 in February may signal the start of a long-awaited firming of uranium prices. But he wouldn't predict when Cameco's public offering will proceed.

The Uranerz transaction, subject to regulatory approvals and other conditions, is expected to close by July.

Rabbit Lake, one of three uranium mines that Cameco operates in Saskatchewan, has been closed since March 1989, as the company upgrades its mill in the face of depressed uranium prices. The mine's assets also include four adjacent deposits, with reserves, estimated at 177 million pounds at the end of 1988, expected to be sufficient to continue operations well beyond 2000. Uranerz already holds a 33.33% stake in the south zone of one of the four deposits, known as Eagle North.

Cameco and Uranerz also are partners at Cameco's Key Lake uranium mine and at the Star Lake gold project, both in Saskatchewan.

Mr. Gorman said that Cameco's recent discovery of an additional uranium deposit in Saskatchewan "will essentially replace the reserves that we are selling" to Uranerz, and that Cameco doesn't anticipate any further asset sales.
