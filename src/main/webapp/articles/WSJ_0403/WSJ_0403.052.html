Politics & Policy: Thrift Office Ends Limit on S&L Firms' Marketing Efforts

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/03/90

WASHINGTON -- The Office of Thrift Supervision withdrew a rule that hampered savings-and-loan owners that tried to market other financial products to their S&L customers. The Association of Thrift Holding Companies, a trade group, filed a suit challenging the rule in December, when it went into effect. The association told the federal court in Washington it will withdraw the suit.

The regulation was intended to insure privacy of depositors, the thrift office said. It required thrift-holding companies to obtain written permission from S&L customers before the parent company or other affiliate could mail marketing for other financial services, such as insurance, to those customers. The association argued that the rule was cumbersome and unworkable, and that bank-holding companies don't face the same restriction.

The regulation, said Patrick Forte, president of the trade group, "has damaged the attractiveness of some thrifts to potential buyers."

A spokesman for the thrift office said the regulators are likely to propose a new regulation that replaces the old in some form. "We are still committed to the principle outlined in the regulation as far as disclosing customer records," he said. "We did find there was good reason to take another look at the implementation of the regulation."
