International Brief -- N.V. Amev: Dutch Insurer Plans Merger With Groupe of Belgium

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/03/90

N.V. Amev, the third-largest Dutch insurance company, and Groupe AG, Belgium's biggest insurer, announced plans to merge, creating Europe's 12th-largest insurer in terms of premium income. A final pact is expected before year end. Amev and Groupe each would own 50% of a joint venture, with operating units initially separate, designed to strengthen both as the European Community moves toward freer cross-border competition. Groupe would pay 10.7 billion Belgian francs ($305.3 million) to Amev over 10 years to cover the difference in Amev's greater value. The two parents, which also have moved into banking, would keep separate stock market quotations.

The combined companies would have annual revenue of about $7 billion, assets of $35 billion, and shareholders' equity, including unrealized gains on assets, of $4 billion. They have a combined stock market value of about $4.6 billion.

Amev posted a 28% rise in 1989 profit to 354.8 million guilders ($186 million), or 5.78 guilders a share, from 276.2 million guilders, or 4.97 guilders a share, in 1988.
