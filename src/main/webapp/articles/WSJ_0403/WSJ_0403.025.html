Bush Asks Broadcasters To Back TV Marti for Cuba

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/03/90

ATLANTA -- President Bush asked broadcasters to support the U.S. government's new television broadcasts to Cuba, despite fears Havana may respond with widespread jamming of American domestic broadcasts. Cuba has been jamming signals sent to that country by the government's new TV Marti television system since the U.S. began the service late last month. Private broadcasters fear that Cuba soon may expand its jamming activity to disrupt frequencies used by private radio and television stations in the southeast U.S.

"I do understand the concerns some of you have about this," Mr. Bush told the National Association of Broadcasters convention here. "But I also understand that you represent the very principle TV Marti exists to serve -- the free flow of ideas."

Mr. Bush declared that he doesn't intend to back away from TV Marti despite Cuba's opposition. He told the convention that "the voice of freedom will not be stilled as long as there is an America to tell the truth." He urged the broadcasters to "stand for TV Marti, to stand for freedom."
