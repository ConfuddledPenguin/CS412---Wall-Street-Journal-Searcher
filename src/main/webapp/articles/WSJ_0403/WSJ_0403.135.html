  • Date: 04/03/90

MAGAZINES: Texas Monthly will close Domain magazine, a life style and home magazine, because of what the publisher labeled restrictive postal regulations. This is the first such move by a magazine publisher following a recent announcement by the U.S. Postal Service that rates for second and third class mail would be greatly increased in early 1991. Decreased spending by national advertisers in home furnishings was another factor.

WHO'S NEWS: Ann Godoff returned to her job as vice president and executive editor of Atlantic Monthly Press. About a month ago, she quit to become executive editor of Doubleday but said she decided she would rather work for a small, independent publishing house. Doubleday, part of Bantam Doubleday Dell Publishing Group Inc., which is owned by Bertelsmann AG, said it hasn't found a successor to Ms. Godoff. . . . Rich Szefc was named to the new post of senior vice president and chief financial officer of Random House Inc. He most recently was vice president and treasurer of OmniCorp Holdings Inc., the U.S. arm of a Swiss investment group. Random House is owned by the Newhouse family's Advance Publications Inc. Some of his financial duties were previously the responsibility of Joe Guadango, formerly vice president of finance, who was named to the new post of vice president of operations for Outlet Book Co., part of the company's Crown Publishing Group.
