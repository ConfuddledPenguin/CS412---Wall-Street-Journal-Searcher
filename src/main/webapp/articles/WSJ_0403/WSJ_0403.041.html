World Business Index Grows at Sluggish Pace

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/03/90

NEW YORK -- The world business cycle continues to grow at a sluggish pace, the Conference Board reported. It said the composite leading index for 11 of the world's major industrial countries grew at an annual rate of 2% in December, the same as the prior month and six months earlier. The leading indexes project future economic conditions.

The Pacific region continues to show the greatest strength, especially in Taiwan, where the leading index grew at an 8% rate, the Conference Board said. Korea's leading index followed closely behind at 7%. Japan's stood at 6%.

Growth was sluggish in Europe. West Germany's grew at 5%, and Italy at 1%. The leading indexes fell 1% in France and 2% in the United Kingdom.

Leading indexes both in the U.S. and Canada rose at a 1% pace.
