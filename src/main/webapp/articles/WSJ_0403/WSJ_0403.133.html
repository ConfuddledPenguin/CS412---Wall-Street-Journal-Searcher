Business Brief -- Great Western Financial Corp.: Company Drops Earlier Plan To Buy CenTrust Branches

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/03/90

Great Western Financial Corp. said its main unit's previously reported agreement to buy branches of CenTrust Savings Bank for $150 million fell through. Great Western said the agreement, reached last summer, included clauses that allowed either side to kill the deal if it hadn't been concluded by March 31. It said it allowed the agreement to lapse.

The collapse of the deal came as no surprise and had been foreordained by CenTrust's recent seizure by the government. CenTrust, hurt by huge junk-bond losses and by the free-spending ways of its controversial former chairman, David L. Paul, failed last month. Officials have estimated that its demise may ultimately cost the government $2 billion to $3 billion.

Under the proposed transaction, Great Western was to have bought the deposits, offices and related assets of 63 branches of Miami-based CenTrust, which by the time the agreement was announced already was the focus of increasing regulatory concern. Great Western has headquarters in Beverly Hills, Calif.
