Hotel Investors Firms Had a Combined Loss Of $60.4 Million in '89

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/03/90

LOS ANGELES -- Hotel Investors Trust and Hotel Investors Corp. posted a combined 1989 net loss of $60.4 million, or $4.97 a share, including a $55.8 million reserve related to the planned sales of certain properties. The reserve, which was about $20 million more than expected, reflects anticipated losses from the planned sale of two Las Vegas hotel-casinos, slightly lower-than-expected sales prices for other hotels, and higher transactions costs, said a spokesman for Hotel Investors Trust.

In 1988, the companies reported net income of $1.3 million, or 11 cents a share. Last year's net income included $156,000 from gains on sales of certain assets, compared with gains of $2.6 million in 1988. Revenue rose 23% to $115.5 million from $94 million.

Shares of Hotel Investors Trust, a real-estate investment trust, and Hotel Investors Corp., a hotel-management concern, are paired and trade together on the New York Stock Exchange. Yesterday, the shares fell 50 cents to close at $4.50.

The companies also said they reached a revised agreement to sell certain of their hotels and allowed a separate sales agreement involving three hotels to lapse after the tenant, Northview Corp., filed for bankruptcy-law protection.

The amended agreement calls for the sale of 27 hotels to an affiliate of EIE International Corp. of Japan for $152.2 million. Hotel Investors announced in February that it had a definitive agreement to sell 31 hotels to Park Plaza Inc., the EIE affiliate, for about $185.8 million.

The transaction was "renegotiated" after Park Plaza conducted a physical inspection and analysis of all the properties, the spokesman said. "The benefits {from the revised transaction} will be close to those expected" from the earlier agreement, he added.

Hotel Investors said a former trustee, John D. Morrissey, has rejoined the board to fill a vacancy created after John F. Rothman resigned as president and chief executive officer last month. Graeme W. Henderson, the trust chairman, was named a director of the corporation, also filling a slot vacated by Mr. Rothman.
