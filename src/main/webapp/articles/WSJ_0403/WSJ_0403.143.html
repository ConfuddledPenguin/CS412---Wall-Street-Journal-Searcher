Who's News: Kollmorgen Names John L. Youngblood Chief and Chairman

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/03/90

SIMSBURY, Conn. -- Kollmorgen Corp. named President John L. Youngblood to the additional posts of chairman and chief executive officer, and a member of the executive committee, at a special meeting of the board. Mr. Youngblood, 49 years old, was also chief operating officer. As chairman and chief executive, he succeeds James E. Swiggett, 58, who is retiring for "personal reasons," the company said. Mr. Swiggett will remain a director and member of the executive committee.

A spokesman for Kollmorgen said Mr. Youngblood's appointment was part of a restructuring plan announced in September. The spokesman said Mr. Swiggett "grew up in the interconnections business," the printed circuit board business that is being sold as part of the restructuring plan. Kollmorgen will be left with its motors and controls and electro-optical business.

Mr. Swiggett was chairman since 1989 and chief executive since 1984. During his tenure, Kollmorgen was the subject of a hostile tender offer from Vernitron Corp., a Deer Park, N.Y., industrial electronics company that is closely held. Although the companies later agreed to a friendly merger, Vernitron pulled out of the $320 million transaction in September, a week before it was to close. Kollmorgen sued, and Vernitron countersued. The companies are still involved in litigation.

Mr. Youngblood earlier had been president of the electro-optical division. He holds a doctorate in electrical engineering.

In addition, Kollmorgen elected Robert N. Parker, president of LTV Corp.'s missiles and electronics group, a director, increasing the board to 13 members.
