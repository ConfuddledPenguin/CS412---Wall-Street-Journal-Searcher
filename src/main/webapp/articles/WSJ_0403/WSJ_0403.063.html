Heritage Financial Capital Plan

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/03/90

RICHMOND, Va. -- Heritage Financial Corp. said it intends to boost the tangible capital of its Heritage Savings Bank unit by exchanging an $8.4 million subordinated note issued by the bank in 1987 for common stock. The parent company also suspended its preferred stock dividend indefinitely to conserve the bank's capital. The proposed swap, which requires regulatory and corporate approvals, would increase the bank's tangible capital by $8.4 million to a total of $2 million. The transaction would still leave Heritage Savings $10 million short of new federal requirements for tangible capital.

In over-the-counter trading yesterday, Heritage Financial shares were quoted at 25 cents bid, 37.5 cents asked.
