REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: Ancient History

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/03/90

Bad news for Washington, if not the Republic: There's evidence that for most American kids, Washington is history. A report just out by the National Assessment of Educational Progress says only about one-third of polled eighth graders know that Congress makes the laws or could identify the presidential veto. About half know that the U.S. is a representative democracy or understand the separation of powers. Still, there may be reasons for these results. Maybe they'd be better able to recognize their government if someone asked them what a $500 billion omnibus appropriation bill was. Or a continuing resolution. Most kids in grade school probably think local judges make the nation's laws. How is a 13-year-old supposed to know what a presidential veto is if nobody's spotted more than one or two since 1974? As to the U.S. being a representative democracy, is this question related to the 99% re-election rate in the House of Representatives? The reason some of their parents don't vote is that increasing numbers of Members have no opponent in the general election.

We do wonder about one thing, though. If 33% know that Congress makes laws, what does the other 66% think Congress does?
