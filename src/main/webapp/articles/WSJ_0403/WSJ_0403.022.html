Taiwanese New Bank Rules

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/03/90

TAIPEI -- Taiwan's cabinet approved regulations filed by the Finance Ministry concerning new bank establishments. Under the rules, the minimum paid-in capital for new banks will be $10 billion New Taiwan ($378.8 million) and each new bank will be required to raise 20% of its paid-in capital from a public offering on the stock market, with each investor limited to 10,000 shares.

The new regulations also indicated that the ministry may have the right to restrict the number of new banks.

The new rules came after the government's decision in July to lift a ban on formation of new banks, officials said.
