Who's News: Kingsborough, a Veteran Of Toy Business, Gets Post

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/03/90

PLEASANTON, Calif. -- Donald Kingsborough has a new company to play with. Mr. Kingsborough, former chairman and chief executive officer of Worlds of Wonder Inc., is now head of newly formed Intelligy Corp., which plans to sell educational games and toys for children. The company said it is developing products it plans to introduce in late summer.

In a news release, Intelligy cites Mr. Kingsborough's tenure at Worlds of Wonder, "one of the nation's fastest and most successful start-ups in history" -- without mentioning that it also became one of the industry's most spectacular failures.

Intelligy marks Mr. Kingsborough's third attempt to find lasting success in the toy business. He was a top sales executive for video game maker Atari Inc. when that company's market suddenly dried up. In 1983, Atari, then owned by Warner Communications Inc., posted a loss of $538.6 million. After that, he regrouped with other former Atari managers to found Worlds of Wonder in 1985. That company chalked up huge sales and zoomed to the top of the toy industry.

But in 1987, Worlds of Wonder filed for protection from creditors under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code. Mr. Kingsborough resigned in April 1988 at the request of creditors.
