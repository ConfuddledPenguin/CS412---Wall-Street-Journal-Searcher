Concern Set Up to Acquire Pepperell Defaults on Debt

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/03/90

CHICAGO -- William Farley's company that was set up for the acquisition of West Point-Pepperell Inc. defaulted on $796 million in bank debt and missed bondholder interest payments. Officials of the acquisition company acknowledged the default, which had been expected. They also said the acquisition unit was negotiating with bankers and had made an exchange offer to subordinated bondholders as part of a restructuring effort.

While details of that exchange offer weren't disclosed, a Wall Street person close to the negotiations said holders would be offered a significant equity position in the target company, West Point-Pepperell Inc., if they agreed to delayed interest payments. Interest on about $705 million in subordinated debt was to have been paid yesterday.

In a statement, a spokeswoman for Mr. Farley said the defaults by West Point Acquisition Corp. won't affect West Point-Pepperell. It said the debt is structured to prevent cross-defaults.

Still, some close to the transaction believe that default could presage a change of ownership in the huge textile maker, based in West Point, Ga., because the banks that lent Mr. Farley money to make the acquisition last year hold most of its stock as collateral.
