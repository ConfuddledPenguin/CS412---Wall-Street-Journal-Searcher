Resolution Trust Relaxes Rules for Accounting Firms

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/03/90

NEW YORK -- The Resolution Trust Corp. has eased its restrictions for hiring accounting firms that had been sued by the government for thrift-reorganization work. A memo issued late last month by Resolution Trust says that even though the accounting firm may have been sued by thrift or bank regulators, it can be hired for reorganization work under certain conditions.

The accounting firm must show that those employees and offices doing the reorganization work weren't involved in the previous litigation, the memo says. The firm must also centrally monitor compliance with the screening out of those employees previously involved in the litigation, the memo says.

L. William Seidman, chairman of the Federal Deposit Insurance Corp., said a previous policy to avoid using accounting firms that had been sued was being misunderstood. "While it's still true that an accounting firm that hasn't previously been sued in thrift or bank work would be preferred, we're giving more leeway to hire firms which had been sued, since they may be the low bidder with expertise for reorganization jobs," he added.
