Business Brief -- CPC International Inc.: Three Food Lines in Britain Purchased From SmithKline

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/03/90

CPC International Inc., a food products company based in Englewood Cliffs, N.J., said it completed the purchase of three food businesses in Britain from SmithKline Beecham PLC, a pharmaceutical and consumer-product concern, for #157 million ($258.7 million). The purchase includes the Ambrosia dessert line, including milk puddings and custards; Bovril beef bouillon products; and Marmite spread, an English bread spread made from yeast extract. The three food businesses had combined sales last year of #89 million.

The sale of the businesses, which Beecham owned before it merged with SmithKline Beckman last year, was expected. Early this year, SmithKline said it was seeking to sell some of its food brands to concentrate on strengthening its international health-care business.

CPC said the acquisitions complement its core business. Its products include such brands as Skippy peanut butter, Thomas' English muffins, Hellmann's mayonnaise and Knorr soups, sauces, bouillons and seasonings.
