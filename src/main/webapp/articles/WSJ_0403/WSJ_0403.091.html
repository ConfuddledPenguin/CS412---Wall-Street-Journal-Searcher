Who's News: James Walsh, Head Of CS First Boston's Japan Unit, Resigns

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/03/90

TOKYO -- James M. Walsh, chairman of CS First Boston Inc.'s Japan subsidiary, resigned, six weeks after the company appointed another investment banker to run the group's Asian operations. Neither Mr. Walsh nor CS First Boston would say if his departure was connected to the appointment in February of Allen Wheat as chief executive of CS First Boston Pacific Inc. The appointment of Mr. Wheat, former chairman of Bankers Trust Co.'s international arm, created a new layer of management between Mr. Walsh and CS First Boston's executives in New York.

Mr. Walsh, contacted in Hawaii, said he decided to leave CS First Boston after the firm asked him to move to London. "I wanted to stay in Japan, so I pulled the ripcord," he said. "The decision to leave was entirely my own choice."

He declined to discuss his resignation further.

A 14-year veteran of CS First Boston and its corporate predecessor, First Boston Inc., Mr. Walsh was largely responsible for building up the group in Japan. Since the firm opened a branch here in 1985, its staff has swelled from 12 people to more than 400, with offices in Tokyo and Osaka.

CS First Boston is one of 22 foreign members of the Tokyo Stock Exchange, and also belongs to the Tokyo International Financial Futures Exchange.
