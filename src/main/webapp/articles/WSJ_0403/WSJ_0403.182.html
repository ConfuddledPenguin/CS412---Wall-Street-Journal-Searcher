Amdura, Failing To Get More Credit, Files for Chapter 11

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/03/90

DENVER -- After failing to obtain additional credit from its lenders, Amdura Corp. filed for protection under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code. The action caps a long line of trouble that began when the automotive and hardware distributor and specialty manufacturer bought CoastAmerica Corp. in 1988.

CoastAmerica was acquired with a $260 million credit facility from a group of 14 banks led by Chicago-based Continental Bank. Last year, discontent over the handling of the resulting debt led to a battle between then-incumbent Amdura management and New York investor Frank J.A. Cilluffo, who succeeded in ousting Amdura's chief executive officer and board members in a consent solicitation.

Last October, after a new management team took over, the bank group gave Amdura a new $30 million credit line and extended a $40 million bridge loan made in connection with the CoastAmerica acquisition. But the new money wasn't enough to stem Amdura's troubles.

The company hasn't released financial results for 1989 but last month Amdura said that a restructuring charge to be recorded against fourth-quarter earnings would result in a "substantial" loss for the year.

Last month the company also said it was seeking additional working capital from its lenders. Those negotiations failed.

The company said that its hardware distribution subsidiaries, including CoastAmerica and Amdura National Distribution Co. would also file for Chapter 11 protection but that its manufacturing subsidiaries, Crosby Group Inc. and Harris Waste Management Group Inc. would be excluded from the filing.

The company has been trying to sell the Crosby and Harris units. Neither sale has been completed, although a sale of the Crosby unit is pending.
