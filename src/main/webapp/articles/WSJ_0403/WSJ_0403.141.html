PHILLIPS PETROLEUM Co. (Bartlesville, Okla.) -- Moody's Investors Service Inc. said it upgraded its ratings on this energy concern's senior long-term debt to Baa-1 from Baa-2, and subordinated debentures to Baa-2 from Baa-3. The rating concern cited Phillips's accelerated debt-reduction program and Moody's belief that the company should be able to fund internally the bulk of its capital program at a level sufficient to enhance its asset base. ---

UNOCAL Corp. (Los Angeles) -- Moody's Investors Service Inc. said it raised the long-term debt ratings on about $2.3 billion of Unocal's Union Oil Co. of California unit, citing the company's progress in reducing debt and restructuring its asset base to enhance profitability. The rating concern raised the long-term debt rating on Union Oil's debentures, notes, guaranteed Eurobonds and Euronotes and pollution-control and industrial revenue bonds to Baa-1 from Baa-2. The unit's shelf registration of senior debt securities was also raised to Baa-1 from Baa-2. The Prime-2 ratings for commercial paper issued by Union Oil Co., Union Oil Credit Corp. and Union Exploration Partners Limited Partnership have been comfirmed, Moody's said. A spokesman for Unocal, a diversified oil company, said it was "pleased Moody's recognizes the progress the company is making."
