Planned Parenthood Urges Campaign Against AT&T

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/03/90

NEW YORK -- Planned Parenthood Federation of America asked the public to write letters to American Telephone & Telegraph Co. expressing outrage at the company's withdrawal of funding after pressure from anti-abortion groups. In a statement, Planned Parenthood Foundation President Faye Wattleton called AT&T's decision "a capitulation to those groups who go to extreme lengths to repress, undermine, even abolish the free flow of information."

In addition to the letter-writing campaign, Ms. Wattleton has urged supporters to donate their AT&T securities to the foundation so that it will have more voting influence on the company's policies. She also canceled a $350,000 contract for an AT&T phone system.

"We don't see it as AT&T's role to take a position one way or another on this very personal matter and we won't be pushed into doing so by an intemperate publicity campaign," said an AT&T spokesman.

AT&T had been one of Planned Parenthood's biggest corporate sponsors, contributing about $50,000 annually. It has come under increasing pressure from anti-abortion activists to withdraw funding from groups that advocate abortion. A shareholder resolution, calling for such action, is scheduled for a vote at the company's annual meeting on April 18.

AT&T has urged holders to reject the resolution, but it recently pulled its funds, saying it didn't want to get drawn into the foundation's battle with anti-abortion groups.
