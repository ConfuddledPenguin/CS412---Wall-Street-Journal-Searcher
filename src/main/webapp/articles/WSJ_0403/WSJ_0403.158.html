Cablevision Systems Enters Credit Facility With Group of Banks

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/03/90

WOODBURY, N.Y. -- Cablevision Systems Corp. said it entered a 10-year, $1.15 billion credit facility with a group of banks jointly led by Citibank and Toronto Dominion Bank. The new facility represents a $250 million increase from the parent company's existing facility and is expected to close within 30 days, Cablevision said. A spokesman said the cash would be used for working capital. He declined to elaborate.

Analysts suggested the funds could be used to finance Cablevision's plan to acquire Gateway Cable from Gilbert Media Associates L.P. Announced last December, the $102.5 million transaction is expected to close next month and would give Cablevision an additional 40,000 subscribers. The company's affiliated systems currently have a 1.5 million-subscriber base.

The funds also might be put toward Cablevision's involvement in the Sky Cable plan, announced early this year, analysts said. Cablevision will spend $75 million over four years in a joint venture with Rupert Murdoch's News Corp., General Motors Corp.'s Hughes Communications unit, and General Electric Co.'s National Broadcasting Co. unit.
