Business Brief -- First Constitution Finance Corp.: Loss Reserves Are Increased, 1st-Period Deficit Expected

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/03/90

First Constitution Finance Corp., New Haven, Conn., said it raised its real-estate related loan-loss reserves for the first quarter by $37 million and will post a "significant" loss for the period. The savings institution also announced that Walter R. Miller Jr., 55 years old, will step down as president and chief executive officer of the parent company and its First Constitution Bank unit. Mr. Miller will be succeeded by John Crawford, 45, a director, as an interim president and chief executive of the parent and the unit.

Mr. Miller said he believed it was time "to bring in someone with a different perspective" to steer the bank.

First Constitution estimated that its nonperforming loans in the first quarter amounted to about $200 million, up from about $100 million at Dec. 31.

With the addition of the $37 million in reserves, the total loan-loss reserve will increase to about $80 million.

First Constitution, which has $2.4 billion of assets, didn't estimate its expected first-quarter loss. A year earlier, it earned $5.1 million, or 47 cents a share.
