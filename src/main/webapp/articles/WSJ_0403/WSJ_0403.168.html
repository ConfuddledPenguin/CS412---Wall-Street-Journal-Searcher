Business Brief -- M.A. Hanna Co.: Plans Are Made for Reserve Of as Much as $11.5 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/03/90

M.A. Hanna Co., Cleveland, said it expects to establish a reserve of as much as $11.5 million against first-quarter earnings. The charge comes as a result of the bankruptcy-law filing of a customer of a coal company in which Hanna owns a 50% stake. A spokeswoman at the natural resources and rubber products concern said the $11.5 million reflects about half of the amount the customer owes the coal company, Colowyo Coal Co.

In the 1989 first quarter Hanna earned $13.8 million, or 54 cents a share, on revenue of $256.4 million.

Hanna said the charge stems from last week's bankruptcy-law filing by a customer of Colowyo. Colowyo supplies coal to Colorado-Ute Electrical Association, which owed Colowyo about $23 million for coal shipped during March, Hanna said.

Colorado-Ute filed for protection from creditors under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code.

Hanna said it remains confident about Colowyo's prospects.
