British Order Books Show Drop

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/03/90

LONDON -- British manufacturers' order books fell to a three-year low in March and expectations are for little if any increase in industrial output over the next four months, according to a survey by the Confederation of British Industry. In its latest industrial trends report, the CBI said the decline in order books, to the lowest level since October 1986, came despite a rise in export orders.

On inflation, however, expectations of price rises were at their lowest since 1986, with only 24% of companies anticipating increases.
