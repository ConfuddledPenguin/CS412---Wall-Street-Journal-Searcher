Tandy Brands Says Its Accessories Unit Might Be Spun Off

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/03/90

FORT WORTH, Texas -- Tandy Brands Inc. said it is considering the possible spinoff of its Tandy Brands Accessories unit as a new publicly traded company. The specialty retailer and maker of men's accessories and neckwear said various regulatory and statutory matters must be resolved before the spinoff plan is formally adopted. But if approved, the company said, the plan would call for the distribution of common stock in the new company to Tandy Brands stockholders in a tax-free form.

In addition to Tandy Brands Accessories, a manufacturer and marketer of mens' and boys' leather accessories and neckwear, the company also operates Bombay Co., a retailer of specialty furniture and home accessories. The accessories division accounts for about 25% of the Tandy Brands' revenues with the Bombay division accounting for the rest, a company spokesman said.

Carson R. Thompson, Tandy Brands chairman and chief executive, said the spinoff would allow the management of each company to adopt strategies and pursue objectives appropriate to its specific business. Last year, Tandy Brands hired an investment banker to explore the possibility of selling the entire company, but called off the study after several months.

For the six months ended Dec. 31, Tandy Brands reported net income of $8.2 million, or $1.55 a share, on revenue of $77.3 million. That was up from net income of $5.7 million, or $1.43 a share, on revenue of $60.2 million for the same period a year earlier. The latest six-month period included $1.7 million in extraordinary tax benefits compared with $1.9 million in similar benefits recorded during the year-earlier period.
