Horn & Hardart Co. Will Sell East Coast Restaurant Holdings

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/03/90

NEW YORK -- Horn & Hardart Co. said it will sell its East Coast restaurant holdings, which include Bojangles' Fried Chicken & Biscuits, Tony Roma's and Arby's. A Horn & Hardart spokesman said the company has held discussions with potential buyers of the assets. But he declined to say how much Horn & Hardart expected to net from the chains, which should be sold by year's end. The group includes 51 company-operated restaurants; they represented about $95 million of Horn & Hardart's 1988 revenue, which totaled $500 million.

When the company's East Coast holdings are sold, Horn & Hardart's remaining restaurant operations will consist of International King's Table Inc., a West Coast-based chain of 83 restaurants that the company plans to keep.

Horn & Hardart said it is selling the restaurants to focus on Hanover Cos., its direct marketing subsidiary, which generated $316 million of revenue in 1988. "The opportunities presented by the direct marketing industry make it advisable to focus our financial and human capital in that direction," said Donald Schupak, Horn & Hardart's chairman and chief executive officer.

Horn & Hardart agreed to sell to Burger King Corp., a unit of Grand Metropolitan PLC, its franchise and territorial rights to operate Burger King restaurants in the New York area. Horn & Hardart is also proceeding with a plan to sell its New York City real-estate assets and Las Vegas casino interest.
