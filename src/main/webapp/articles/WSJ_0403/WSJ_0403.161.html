Texas International's Chapter 11 Revamp Takes Effect Monday

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/03/90

OKLAHOMA CITY -- Texas International Co. said its plan of reorganization will become effective next Monday, two years after it filed for protection from creditors under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code. Under the plan, the company will change its name to Phoenix Resource Cos., all old Texas International securities will be canceled, new securities of Phoenix will be issued and a new board will be established, the company said. Phoenix will continue as an independent oil and gas company, with primarily overseas business, and will retain ownership of all assets of Texas International.

The reorganization plan, which settles about $150 million in debt, was confirmed by a U.S. Bankruptcy Court here in January. Implementation remains subject to approval of the new trust indentures by the Securities and Exchange Commission, the company said.

Under the plan, trading of Texas International securities will be permanently suspended prior to the market opening Friday, and the registries of the company's securities will be permanently closed at the close of business the same day. Holders of Texas International securities will be entitled to receive new Phoenix securities, which will begin trading Monday on a when-issued basis on the Pacific Stock Exchange.

Texas International was delisted from the New York Stock Exchange in 1986. After several years of struggling under a heavy debt load, the company sought Chapter 11 protection in April 1988.
