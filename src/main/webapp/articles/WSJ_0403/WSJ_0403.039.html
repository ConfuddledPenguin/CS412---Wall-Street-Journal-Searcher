Ahmanson Agrees To Buy for Stock New York Thrift

  • Author: Carrie Dolan
  • Date: 04/03/90

LOS ANGELES -- H.F. Ahmanson & Co. said it reached a definitive agreement to acquire Home Savings Bank of New York in a stock swap valued at about $300 million. Under the agreement, Home of New York shareholders will receive Ahmanson common stock with a market value equal to about $25 a share for each of the 12 million Home of New York shares outstanding, subject to adjustments. The $25 price is about 1.2 times Home of New York's book value of $20.11 a share, or on the lower end of what healthy thrifts have been fetching recently. At a $25-a-share value, the transaction would involve about 17 million shares of Ahmanson, which had about 99.3 million shares outstanding at Dec. 31.

In national over-the-counter trading yesterday, Home of New York shares jumped $3.875 to $18.875 on volume of 830,800 shares. Ahmanson closed at $17.375, down 12.5 cents, in New York Stock Exchange composite trading.

Ahmanson said the merger would allow it to expand its savings and mortgage lending operations in the New York area, the company's second largest market after California. Under the agreement, Ahmanson would combine Homes of New York with its Bowery Savings Bank unit.

The proposed merger won't create major overlaps, Ahmanson said, and branch closings aren't expected. Home of New York, based in Bayside, Queens, has 13 offices, assets of $1.6 billion, and deposits of $1.15 billion. The Bowery has 26 branches and deposits of $5 billion. Ahmanson, parent of Home Savings of America, the nation's largest thrift, has total assets of about $44.6 billion.

Ahmanson said Home of New York had 1989 net income of $19.1 million, or $1.58 a share, compared with profit of $24.1 million, or $2 a share, in 1988. It said Home of New York's return on assets, a key indicator of profitability, was a robust 1.18% in 1989, and that its capital levels substantially exceeded regulatory minimums.

The transaction is subject to regulatory approval and approval of Home of New York shareholders. Ahmanson said it expects the transaction to close by year-end.

Terms also call for Home of New York directors to become directors of Bowery. Richard Kraemer, chairman, president and chief executive officer of Home of New York, will become chairman and chief executive officer of The Bowery, and an executive vice president of Ahmanson. He will also become a director of Ahmanson's Home Savings of America unit.

Ahmanson said the final share price in the transaction may vary, depending on the average closing price of its common for the 10 trading days prior to the day the last required regulatory approval for the transaction is obtained. If Ahmanson's average stock price is greater than or equal to $24.64 a share, the market value of shares issued to Home of New York shareholders will be $28 a share. If Ahmanson's average stock price is less than, or equal to $16.92 a share, Home of New York may decide it won't complete the transaction.
