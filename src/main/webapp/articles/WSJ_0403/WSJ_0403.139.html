International Brief -- Havas S.A.: Asset Exchange Is Slated With Bollore Technologies

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/03/90

Havas S.A., a French media, travel and advertising group, said it entered into an asset swap with Bollore Technologies S.A. of France that will make Havas Europe's leading travel agency concern. The deal calls for Havas's tourism subsidiary Havas Tourisme to acquire Bollore's SCAC Voyages unit for about 150 million francs ($26.3 million). At the same time, Havas Tourisme will acquire a 10% shareholding in Diners Club de France, the French arm of the U.S. credit-card concern.

In exchange, Bollore will become a shareholder of Havas Tourisme with a 10% stake, alongside Havas with a 65% interest (reduced from 75%) and the Bouygues S.A. construction group with 25%.

Havas said the asset swap will expand the number of Havas travel agencies throughout Europe to 500, making it the largest network in Europe, with annual sales of more than 10 billion francs.

Bollore said it intends to refocus its activities on its industrial, transportation and distribution businesses.
