Business Brief -- Maxtor Corp.: Offer of $40 Million Made For Assets of MiniScribe

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/03/90

Maxtor Corp. said it made a $40 million offer to buy substantially all of the assets of MiniScribe Corp., a troubled Longmont, Colo., maker of computer disk drives. Maxtor, a maker of data-storage products, said the offer consists of $20 million in cash, and $20 million in stock. In addition, Maxtor, based in San Jose, Calif., will lend MiniScribe as much as $30 million for working capital between acceptance of the offer and the closing date.

MiniScribe has been operating under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code since Jan. 1. Its assets are scheduled to be offered for sale in U.S. bankruptcy court tomorrow.

In Longmont, MiniScribe couldn't be reached for comment. It wasn't clear whether the company has received other bids.
