Westinghouse Gets $500 Million Job For British Plant

  • Author: Gregory Stricharchuk
  • Date: 04/03/90

PITTSBURGH -- Westinghouse Electric Corp. said it received a $500 million order to supply power generating equipment to Enron Power Corp., a unit of Enron Corp., Houston. The equipment, to be produced by Westinghouse and its joint venture partner, Mitsubishi Heavy Industries of Japan, will be used in Britain's $1 billion Teesside Power Project.

The contract is significant because it provides the six-month-old Westinghouse-Mitsubishi joint venture a key foothold in the European market and an opportunity to demonstrate whether the two companies can work together under tight deadlines. The plant is scheduled to begin commercial operation in early 1993.

Westinghouse said its initial order is for eight 169-megawatt combustion turbine-generators and two 216-megawatt steam turbine-generators. The company also said it was awarded an exclusive option to supply 10 additional combustion turbine-generators.

A Westinghouse spokeswoman declined to break out the amounts it will receive under each order, but some people in the industry valued the initial contract at between $200 million and $250 million. Westinghouse, which has been expanding its North American capacity to meet the revival in demand for combustion turbines, wouldn't say how much of the equipment would be produced by Mitsubishi.

Westinghouse said Britain's Brush Electrical Machines Ltd. will also provide some of the steam turbines.

Last year Westinghouse said its orders for power generation equipment jumped about 20%. Those bookings amounted to about 2,000 megawatts of generation, according to Westinghouse's recently released annual report. The Westinghouse spokeswoman said the company currently has a backlog of power generation equipment orders of about $1 billion.

Enron's order roughly equals all of Westinghouse's power generation equipment orders received last year. Teesside alone is a 1,725 megawatt natural gas-fired cogeneration power plant, said to be one of the world's largest.

Enron will design and build the project on the northeast coast of England. Electricity produced at the plant will be sold to ICI Chemicals & Polymers Ltd. and other customers in the United Kingdom, Westinghouse said. The plant will be England's first independent power producer.

Westinghouse also has significant interests in broadcasting, financial services, over-the-road refrigeration and electronic systems.
