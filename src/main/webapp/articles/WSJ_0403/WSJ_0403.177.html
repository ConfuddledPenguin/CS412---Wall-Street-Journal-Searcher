Fannie Mae Unveils Instrument to Boost Affordable Housing

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/03/90

WASHINGTON -- The Federal National Mortgage Association introduced a new mortgage-backed security to increase below-market mortgage funds designated for low-income buyers by the Federal Home Loan Banks. The financial instrument, called Fannie Mae Affordables, allows members of the 12 regional Home Loan Banks to pool special below-market loans originated under two programs mandated by last year's savings-and-loan rescue legislation.

The loans are funded with advances from the Home Loan Banks. Under the Affordable Housing Program, the regional banks provide subsidized advances to member institutions, while funds for the Community Investment Program are advanced at cost. The Affordable Housing Program is expected to total more than $78 million this year.

Mortgages originated under the low and moderate income program can be swapped for Fannie Mae Affordables, which are single-lender pools as low as $250,000. Institutions can keep Fannie Mae Affordables as an investment or sell them back to the regional banks to repay advances. Pools of less than $1 million won't be traded freely on the open market.

Larry H. Dale, Fannie Mae's senior vice president of multifamily finance and housing initiatives, said the company has been working out details of the program with the Home Loan Banks since the fall. "One of the real beauties of this approach is that it makes it possible for the Home Loan Banks and their members to work within an environment they know well," he said.

Fannie Mae, a federally chartered, publicly traded corporation, operates a secondary market for mortgage loans, buying loans from lenders, packaging them into securities and selling some to investors while holding others in its portfolio.
