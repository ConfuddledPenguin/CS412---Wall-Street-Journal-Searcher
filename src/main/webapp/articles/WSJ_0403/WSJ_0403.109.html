New Securities Issues

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/03/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: MUNICIPALS

Alaska Housing Finance Corp. -- $104 million general purpose revenue bonds, due June 1 and Dec. 1, 1990-1997, were tentatively priced by a Dean Witter Reynolds Inc. group to yield from 6.15% in 1990 to 7% in 1996 and 1997. Current interest serial bonds are priced at par to yield from 6.15% for bonds due Dec. 1, 1990 to 7% for bonds due June 1, 1996 and Dec. 1, 1996 and June 1, 1997. There are no term bonds. Bonds maturing in 1991 are priced to yield 6.30% for bonds due June 1, 1991 and 6.35% for bonds due Dec. 1, 1991. Bonds maturing in 1992-1996 are due both June 1 and Dec. 1 and carry the same yield each of those years. The bonds are secured by a letter of credit from the Industrial Bank of Japan Ltd., Los Angeles Agency, and a Triple-A rating is expected from Moody's Investors Service Inc.

Imperial Irrigation District, California -- $102.5 million certificates of participation, due Nov. 11, 1990-1999, 2007 and 2009, with capital appreciation bonds due 2000-2004, were tentatively priced by a PaineWebber Inc. Group to yield from 5.80% in 1990 to 7.23% in 2007. Current interest serial bonds are priced to yield from 5.80% in 1990 to 6.80% in 1999. There are $21.67 million term bonds priced to yield 7.23% in 2007 and $17.2 million term bonds priced to yield 7.20% in 2009. Capital appreciation bonds are priced to yield to maturity from 6.85% in 2000 to 7.05% in 2003 and 2004. The bonds are rated Double-A by Moody's and Double-APlus by Standard & Poor's Corp.

MORTGAGES

Citibank N.A. -- Issued a $210 million mortgage pass-through issue, Citibank Conduit Series 1990-A, backed by a pool of residential mortgage loans. The issue is expected to be rated Triple-A by both Moody's and S&P on the strength of a 100% unconditional and irrevocable financial guaranty insurance policy from Financial Security Assurance, according to a news release from FSA. The underlying mortgage portfolio is typical of uninsured pass-through issues, which are usually rated Double-A, FSA said. The mortgage loans are predominantly on owner-occupied, single-family detached houses. Further details were unavailable.

EUROBONDS

European Community (agency) -- 350 million European Currency Units of 10 5/8% Eurobonds due April 20, 1995, priced at 101.60 to yield 10.66 pc less fees, via Paribas Capital Markets. Fees 2.

Caisse Nationale des Telecommunications (France) -- 1 billion French francs of 10% Eurobonds due April 25, 2000, to yield 10.02% after fees, via Credit Commercial de France. Guaranteed by Republic of France. Fees 2.

Instituto de Credito Oficial (Spain) -- $250 million of floating rate notes paying interest at 2 1/2 basis points below the six-month London interbank bid rate, priced at par and reoffered at 99.88, via Credit Suisse First Boston. Statutory guarantee by Kingdom of Spain. Fees 0.17.

Credit Lyonnais Australia (French parent) -- $50 million (Australian) of 15 1/2% Eurobonds due May 4, 1992, priced at 101.95, via Hambros Bank Ltd. Guaranteed by Credit Lyonnais. Fees 1 1/4.
