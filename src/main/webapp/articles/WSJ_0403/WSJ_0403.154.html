What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/03/90

GM is to announce today that its president, Robert C. Stempel, will be named chairman and chief executive, succeeding Roger B. Smith, who is to retire Aug. 1. Stempel, 56, won't be succeeded as president. Instead, the world's largest industrial company will promote two executive vice presidents to vice chairmen. ---

Southland posted a $1.01 billion fourth-quarter loss and warned it may seek Chapter 11 protection if it can't complete a financial restructuring.

---

First Executive posted an $835.7 million fourth-quarter loss, reflecting increased charges for its huge junk-bond holdings. The firm also said insurance policy surrenders totaled $559 million in January and February. Also, the SEC began investigating possible securities violations.

---

U.S. stocks fell after Monday's 6.6% plunge in the Tokyo market. The dollar continued to post gains, while U.S. Treasury bonds eased.

Japanese stocks may fall further if the most-recent slide prompts margin calls, some analysts said. The Nikkei index finished Tuesday's morning session down 77.19 points.

---

Investor Harold Simmons let his $1 billion offer for Georgia Gulf expire and now plans to back the company's revised recapitalization plan.

---

Columbia S&L said declines of 50% or more in the value of certain junk-bond holdings contributed to its previously reported insolvency.

---

United Air's unions appeared to be moving toward a possible accord to buy parent UAL, sources said. Still, several issues remain unresolved.

---

Fleet/Norstar said troubled loans grew an estimated 50% in the first quarter, to $600 million. The New England bank had been considered one of the least vulnerable to the region's real estate problems.

---

Torchmark withdrew its $6.3 billion takeover bid for American General and instead will run its own slate for the insurer's board in a proxy fight. The move, coupled with increased legal maneuvering, indicated the fight may be long and messy, observers said.

---

Shearson expects a first-quarter loss of $897 million to $917 million, a securities industry record, to pay for its revamping and cost-cutting plan. The firm announced several new cutbacks as part of the effort.

---

The mortgage-backed securities market received another blow as Moody's said it may downgrade $26 billion of securities issued by S&Ls.

---

Xerox said its president, Paul Allaire, will succeed David Kearns as chief executive officer Aug. 1, though Kearns will remain chairman.

---

The newest SEC nominee is likely to be Erskine Henderson, a New York securities lawyer, sources said. He would be the second black commissioner in the agency's history.

---

Manufacturing remained sluggish in March for the 11th consecutive month, purchasing managers said. Still, there were some signs of firming in the sector. Meanwhile, construction spending climbed 2.6% in February as all sectors showed gains.

---

Trammell Crow completed a $456 million refinancing deal with Equitable Real Estate Management as part of an ongoing restructuring.

---
