Complaint Alleges Rykoff-Sexton Issued Misleading Statements

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/03/90

LOS ANGELES -- An Arizona investor alleged that Rykoff-Sexton Inc. and two of its top officers issued a series of misleading positive statements about the food-service concern's finances. Investor Rodney B. Shields, in a complaint filed in U. S. District Court here, charged that Rykoff concealed "significant problems" it was having controlling expenses related to its rapid growth and acquisitions during the period March 3, 1989 to Feb. 7, 1990. The company also allegedly faced undisclosed costs relating to a self-insured health care program.

On Feb. 7, Los Angeles-based Rykoff said it expected to report fiscal third-quarter earnings that were a fraction of analysts' expectations. Last month, the company reported net income of $610,000, or five cents a share, representing an 87% plunge from $4.5 million, or 38 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue climbed 8.2%, to $343.4 million from $317.2 million.

According to the complaint, the price of Rykoff shares dropped from a high of $29 during the period of alleged misleading statements to as low as $16.875 after Feb. 7. The stock closed at $17.25 a share yesterday in composite New York Stock Exchange trading, off 25 cents.

The suit seeks unspecified damages. Also named as defendants are Rykoff's chief executive officer, Roger W. Coleman, and Victor B. Chavez, chief financial officer.

A company spokesman said Rykoff "believes it has meritorious defenses to this lawsuit and will vigorously defend" itself.
