Business Brief -- LTV Corp.: Steel Unit's Hourly Workers Approve a Labor Contract

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/03/90

LTV Steel, Cleveland, said its hourly employees approved a new labor contract that will take effect April 15. The contract will extend until Aug. 1, 1993, or until a plan of reorganization is approved for LTV Steel's parent, Dallas-based LTV Corp., which is operating under Chapter 11 of the Bankruptcy Code.

The agreement -- covering 13,500 active employees and laid-off workers plus 46,000 retirees -- provides for restoration of previous wage and benefit concessions, additional raises totaling $1.50 an hour and an immediate payment of $1,000 per worker. The lump sum includes a $500 signing bonus and a $500 advance on the 1990 profit sharing.

Effective April 15, the employees will receive restoration of base wage cuts amounting to $1.61 an hour, LTV said, plus restored shift differentials and Sunday premiums. Employees will get raises of $1 an hour on Jan. 1, 1991, and 50 cents an hour on Jan. 1, 1992.
