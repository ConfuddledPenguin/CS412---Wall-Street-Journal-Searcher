Politics & Policy: Bush Urged to Take Lead In Move to Reduce Deficit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/03/90

WASHINGTON -- House Speaker Thomas Foley called on President Bush to take the initiative in this year's effort to reduce the budget deficit, and warned that difficulties lie ahead if Mr. Bush doesn't. At a breakfast meeting with reporters, the Washington state Democrat said the president must speak out forthrightly if there is any hope for a tax increase of the kind that Democrats believe is necessary for a meaningful budget package.

But so far the president has only sidled up to, and has failed to embrace, the tax-boosting deficit-reduction plan of Chairman Dan Rostenkowski (D., Ill.) of the House Ways and Means Committee. A stronger statement of support by the president is needed if a major deficit-reduction plan can move ahead, Mr. Foley said.

"The first action has to be taken by the president," the speaker said. "First with the president, then with us."

The House Budget Committee isn't likely to meet its deadline of April 15 to devise a budget resolution, Mr. Foley said. Reaching an agreement "is going to be difficult" if the president continues to stand on the sidelines, he added.
