Technology: AT&T Unit Unveils A Minicomputer And Other Products

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/03/90

NEW YORK -- AT&T Computer Systems unveiled several new computer and communications products, including a powerful entrant into the increasingly competitive market for minicomputers handling transactions. The new mini, which had been expected, comes in a series called the AT&T System 7000 and is being marketed to companies with heavy transaction processing needs such as hotels, financial-services companies and phone companies. It is also the product of a yearlong collaboration between the American Telephone & Telegraph Co. unit and Pyramid Technology Corp., Mountain View, Calif., which designed and builds the machine.

The 7000 is AT&T's first minicomputer product employing reduced-instruction-set-computing (RISC) architecture. RISC designs offer faster computing since the computer needs fewer internal instructions to perform tasks. The computer also uses the Unix software operating system designed by AT&T Bell Laboratories and is said to handle 170 transactions per second and up to 122 million instructions per second.

James E. Clark, AT&T product management and development vice president for minicomputers and high performance systems, said the new line expands the performance of AT&T's products beyond its current top-of-the-line 3B minicomputers. This, he added, should make AT&T competitive with low-end mainframe computers made by International Business Machines Corp. and Amdahl Corp. and RISC-based systems that AT&T expects its competitors such as Digital Equipment Corp. to bring out in 1991.

Pricing for the new computer ranges from $88,000 for a system, including software, that handles 128 computer users, to $225,000 for a system tying together 1,000 users.
