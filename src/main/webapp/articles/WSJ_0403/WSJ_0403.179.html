Kirschner Plans Sale Of Lighting Division; Holder Suit Disclosed

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/03/90

TIMONIUM, Md. -- Kirschner Medical Corp. said it is seeking to sell or spin off its troublesome Chick division, which makes surgical lights and tables, allowing the company to focus on its core orthopedic products. The company also announced the renegotiation of $35 million in bank debt. Under the new terms, the loans are payable on demand and subject to review in 90 days.

Separately, Kirschner said two stockholders filed suit against the company and three officers and directors, alleging that it deceived investors as to its financial condition. Kirschner said it denies "any wrongdoing" and intends to vigorously contest the suit, which was filed March 23 in U.S. District Court in Maryland.

In national over-the-counter trading yesterday, Kirschner shares fell $1.25 to close at $7.

The Chick business, which is more capital-intensive than Kirschner's other lines, has been a "considerable drain" on the company's profit, a spokesman said.

The division also has been a source of patent and business-agreement disputes with ALM Equipment Hospitaliers S.A. of France. In January, Kirschner lost a patent infringement lawsuit to ALM Equipment over Kirschner's Chick II surgical light. A jury awarded $820,000 in damages to ALM. Kirschner says it may appeal.

In a separate issue, last summer an arbitration panel found that ALM had unfairly breached an agreement to supply Kirschner with surgical lights and ordered ALM to pay as-yet-unspecified damages.

Kirschner said it retained investment banker Alex. Brown & Sons to assist it in developing strategies for Chick, which contributed $13 million to Kirschner's total revenue of about $68 million.

The company also said it expects to release fiscal 1989 results by the end of this week.
