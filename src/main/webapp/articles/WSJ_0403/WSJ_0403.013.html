Law: Ivan Boesky to Complete Prison Term, Be Released

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/03/90

NEW YORK -- Ivan F. Boesky will be a free man again tomorrow, when he is expected to be released from the Brooklyn, N.Y., halfway house where he is completing a three-year prison term. Mr. Boesky, once one of the most powerful figures on Wall Street, stunned the financial world in November 1986 with the announcement that he had agreed to plead guilty to one felony and had settled Securities and Exchange Commission charges by paying a record $100 million. He was sentenced to prison in December 1987.

Mr. Boesky's agreement to cooperate with the government led to the most sweeping investigations ever of the securities industry and insider trading.

Mr. Boesky began his term in a minimum security prison in Lompoc, Calif., in March 1987. He was released to the Brooklyn halfway house last December, but because the facility is overcrowded, he has been permitted to live at home and has had to check in regularly. With time off for good behavior, he has served little more than two years of his sentence.

Mr. Boesky is expected to make his courtroom debut as a government witness as soon as next month, when the government will try its case against John A. Mulheren Jr., an arbitrager. Mr. Mulheren was charged with taking stock positions for Mr. Boesky to conceal the true ownership of the securities, a tactic known as stock "parking." A lawyer for Mr. Boesky said the former stock speculator didn't know what he would next do for a living. But the attorney said Mr. Boesky isn't likely to return to the financial industry.
