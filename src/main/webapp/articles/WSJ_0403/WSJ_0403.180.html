Marketing & Media: Outlet Is Considering Selling the Company; Including TV Stations

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/03/90

PROVIDENCE, R.I. -- Outlet Communications Inc., which recently sold several broadcasting properties, said it is considering the sale of the company, including its VHF television stations here and in Columbus, Ohio. Outlet said it authorized its financial adviser, First Boston Corp., to "explore strategic alternatives that will maximize shareholder value," but that it isn't certain that "any such transaction will occur."

In over-the-counter trading yesterday, Outlet Class A shares closed at $21.25, up $2.

Outlet currently owns two VHF television stations which are NBC network affiliates, WJAR-TV in Providence and WCMH-TV in Columbus, Ohio. It recently sold two UHF television stations and two radio stations to Chase Communications of Hartford, Conn., for $120 million. Net proceeds were used to satisfy the stations' $10 million in obligations and reduce Outlet's $84 million bank debt, as well as increase its cash balance, Outlet said.

Outlet added that it is also exploring the possibility of buying up to $20 million of its notes, common stock or a combination of both. Outlet said it is considering the purchase because its bonds are trading "below face value."

Outlet, which reported 1989 net income of $15.9 million, or $2.43 a share, said it authorized First Boston to contact parties concerning the possible sale or merger of the company.

The company's 1989 earnings include a $4.6 million extraordinary credit. The company reported a loss of $21.8 million, or $3.32 a share, in 1988.

In February, a group that includes Gabelli & Co., a New York-based investment concern, reported to the Securities and Exchange Commission that it holds a 7.7% in Outlet.
