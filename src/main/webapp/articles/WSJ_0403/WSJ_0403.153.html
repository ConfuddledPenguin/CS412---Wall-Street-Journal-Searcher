Corona Ends Accord To Sell Some Assets To New York Firm

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/03/90

TORONTO -- Corona Corp. said it ended a previously reported agreement to sell its industrial mineral assets to 60%-owned Avalon Corp. but continues to negotiate the sale to Avalon of its 17% interest in Poco Petroleums Ltd. The Toronto-based gold-mining company said the agreement with Avalon was ended after a minority partner in one of Corona's mineral properties decided to oppose the sale. The transaction had been conditional on Avalon obtaining 100% ownership of the assets.

A Corona spokesman said he expects the sale of the Poco Petroleums stake to be made at "close to market value," giving the transaction an indicated value of about 51.6 million Canadian dollars (US$44.1 million).

Avalon, an energy exploration and production concern based in New York, already holds as its main asset a 32% stake in Poco Petroleums.

As reported, Poco Petroleums is negotiating a merger with Voyager Energy Inc. on a share-exchange basis. Avalon said separately that it has obtained an option to purchase about 32% of Voyager Energy's shares on a fully diluted basis from CanCapital Corp. for about C$67 million.

Poco and Voyager are oil and natural-gas concerns based in Calgary, Alberta. CanCapital is an investment concern based in Calgary. Avalon said it expects to excercise the option if the merger between Poco and Voyager is completed. The option expires Dec. 30.

On the Toronto Stock Exchange yesterday, Poco Petroleums shares closed unchanged at C$7.375 ($6.30). Avalon closed at $5.375, down 12.5 cents, on the New York Stock Exchange.
