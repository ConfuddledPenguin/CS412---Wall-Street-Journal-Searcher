Trammell Crow Big Refinancing Is Completed

  • Author: Christi Harlan
  • Date: 04/03/90

DALLAS -- Trammell Crow Co., as part of an ongoing restructuring of its debt and operations, completed a $456 million refinancing deal with Equitable Real Estate Management Inc. While the deal is well shy of the $600 million that Trammell Crow originally sought from Equitable Real Estate, it will enable the giant real estate developer to cut the interest it pays on the debt by almost 2.5 percentage points, while injecting badly needed cash into poorly performing projects in the Southwest.

The bulk of the financing will be used to pay off existing debt on about 90 Trammell Crow properties, which will be pledged as collateral for the new debt. About $90 million in remaining cash will be used to pay down Trammell Crow's debt on another 60 properties that have been unprofitable, according to Robert Whitman, chief financial officer for Trammell Crow.

When the company first approached Equitable Real Estate about a year ago, it had hoped to clear about $113 million in cash to pay down the debt on 100 ailing projects. Mr. Whitman said that list was pared to 60 through the sales of some properties and improvements in market conditions. Other properties were rejected by Equitable Real Estate because of environmental problems, and still others were mortgaged by lenders who were unwilling to accept an early pay down, Mr. Whitman said.

The refinancing with Equitable Real Estate will enable Trammell Crow to reduce its interest rates from 11.3% to about 8.8%, Mr. Whitman said. The deal will also put about 10% of Trammell Crow's Southwest projects in the hands of a single lender and one that has financed Crow projects since the early 1950s.

The properties involved in the Equitable deal were originally financed by some 30 lenders, many of whom are now feeling pressure from federal regulators to reduce their exposure to real estate losses. And the stock market added pressure of its own in early March, when rumors of loan defaults by Trammell Crow drove down stock prices of some large bank holding companies.

Equitable Real Estate President Douglas A. Tibbetts said the pressures didn't shake his company's commitment to the refinancing.

"Despite the rumors that came out recently, we maintained our comfort level," he said.

The financing is being provided by the general account of Equitable Real Estate's parent, Equitable Life Assurance Society of the U.S., and is the second large refinancing hat the company has done for Trammell Crow. In 1987, Equitable Real Estate refinanced the Dallas Market Center for $450 million.

The properties covered by the current refinancing are predominately in Texas, Arizona and Colorado, and 70% of them are industrial or warehouse buildings. About 20% of the properties are retail centers and the remainder is made up of office buildings.

The properties involved in the deal represent about 3% of Trammell Crow's national holdings of about $15 billion in real estate. The company ranks as the nation's largest residential builder and second largest commercial builder.
