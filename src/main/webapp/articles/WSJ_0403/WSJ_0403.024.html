Television Producer, 9 Partners to Buy San Diego Padres --- Tom Werner's Group Agrees To Pay Heiress Joan Kroc $75 Million for the Team

  • Author: Richard Turner
  • Date: 04/03/90

SAN DIEGO -- Will Bill Cosby throw out the first ball? Tom Werner, who with partner Marcy Carsey owns and produces TV shows including "The Bill Cosby Show" and "Roseanne," has agreed with nine partners to buy the San Diego Padres baseball team for about $75 million from McDonald's Corp. heiress Joan Kroc.

The 39-year-old Mr. Werner, a beneficiary of a hefty chunk of the some $500 million that "Cosby" netted in syndication, will be chairman of a general partnership that includes another Hollywood executive and eight San Diego businessmen.

Mr. Werner initially pursued the Padres with Russell Goldsmith, chairman of Republic Pictures Corp., after Mrs. Kroc put the team up for sale last year. In December they informally teamed up with another suitor, an investor group headed by former Coca-Cola Bottling of San Diego president, Arthur Rivkin, and Arthur Engel, chairman of Southwest Marine Inc., a ship repair concern.

The new group, which agreed to bid together in February, had the advantage of what one partner called the "glue factor," eight partners who were firmly rooted in the San Diego community.

The purchase price was lower than some estimates, which was that the team would fetch $100 million. Indianapolis radio magnate Jeff Smulyan paid $81 million for the Seattle Mariners last year, and the Padres have much higher attendance -- the team drew more than two million last season -- and a more successful team. But the buyers of the Padres were able to argue that the Padres should command a lower price for several reasons.

For one thing, the team has high operating expenses, including eight players who make more than $1 million a year; the Mariners have one of the lowest payrolls in the game. Player salaries throughout baseball have been escalating so rapidly that values of franchises may have come down in recent months, despite the $13 million annually each team will reap over the next four years as a result of new national television contracts.

Furthermore, unlike some franchises, San Diego's potential for expanding its local television market has its limits. Its media market ranks 24th of 26 teams: rather than serving an entire region like some teams, the Padres are limited by the Pacific Ocean to the west, the desert to the east, Mexico to the south and Orange County and Los Angeles to the north, where the Dodgers and Angels reign. Another factor, according to a partner, is that Mr. Smulyan has the capability of moving the team; the Padres have a 10-year lease at Jack Murphy Stadium and aren't likely to relocate.
