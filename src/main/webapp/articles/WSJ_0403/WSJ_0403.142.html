Politics & Policy: Short Bill Drafted To Allay Problem On Thrift Nominee

  • Author: Paulette Thomas
  • Date: 04/03/90

WASHINGTON -- The chairman of the Senate Banking Committee outlined quick, narrow legislation that would undercut one of the Bush administration's most compelling arguments for quickly confirming Timothy Ryan as top thrift regulator. Mr. Ryan, a Washington labor and pension lawyer, was nominated last month to be director of the Office of Thrift Supervision. The Senate Banking Committee approved the nomination by an 11-10 vote last week and sent it to the full Senate for consideration.

Some senators have complained that Mr. Ryan lacks financial experience, but the administration has argued that the nominee must be confirmed quickly. A federal court ruling could bring the savings-and-loan restructuring to a halt unless the Senate promptly confirms a new director for the thrift office. An appeals court stay has put the federal court ruling on hold until April 11.

Democrats on the Senate Banking Committee have drafted a one-page bill that would give the president the authority to appoint a temporary replacement. Sen. Donald Riegle (D., Mich.) outlined the proposal as one option that may be pursued. The bill would allow the president to name a temporary director under procedures that would pass muster with the court, and the administration would have more time to seek another candidate for the full five-year term.

The administration also has argued that Mr. Ryan's financial background isn't as important as his record as an able administrator and strong enforcer during his time with the Labor Department. Treasury Secretary Nicholas Brady has asked the Senate to "trust his judgment" on his selection of Mr. Ryan and continued calling on senators to push for the nominee.

The narrow basis of the court case hanging over the thrift bailout was that Danny Wall, director of the thrift office until late February, wasn't approved for his post under constitutional provisions. When he resigned, therefore, the administration didn't have standing under the Vacancies Act to appoint a temporary replacement.

A floor vote this week appears likely, the administration and Senate Democrats said. The legislation that Sen. Riegle discussed wouldn't necessarily have to be approved before the vote, a committee staff member said, as long as senators knew the bill was in the offing.

Sen. Riegle has hammered away that Mr. Ryan lacks the background necessary for the job at a time of crisis in the thrift industry. In a speech to the Independent Bankers Association of America, Sen. Riegle said he was unhappy about the choice for the "single most important financial position today," adding that he believes Mr. Ryan would be unqualified to be chief executive officer of any of the small banks the trade group represents.

Disclosures during the weekend that Mr. Ryan briefly tried marijuana and cocaine in the early 1970s won't help the nominee but aren't likely to be as important a factor in the Senate deliberations, congressional sources said.
