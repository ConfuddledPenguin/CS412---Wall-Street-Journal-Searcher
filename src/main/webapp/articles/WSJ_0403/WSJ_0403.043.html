U.K. Consumer Credit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/03/90

LONDON -- New consumer credit extended by finance houses, building societies, other specialist credit grantors and retailers totaled a seasonally adjusted #3.74 billion ($6.16 billion) in February, down from a revised #3.97 billion in January and up from #3.20 billion in February 1989, the U.K. Central Statistical Office reported. In the three months ended in February, new consumer credit extended rose 2% to #11.09 billion from #10.92 billion in the previous three months.
