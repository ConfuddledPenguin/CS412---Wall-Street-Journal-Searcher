UJB Agrees to Sell Credit Card Portfolio For Over $250 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/11/90

PRINCETON, N.J. -- UJB Financial Corp. agreed to sell its consumer credit card portfolio for more than $250 million to the Harris Trust & Savings Bank subsidiary of Bank of Montreal. The commercial banking concern said it will post a third-quarter gain of $50 million on the sale, which is the premium Harris Trust agreed to pay above the value of the portfolio. For 1989's third quarter, UJB reported earnings of $31.4 million, or 69 cents a share.

UJB said, however, that continuing softness in its service area's economy is forcing it to increase its provisions for possible bad loans. Such provisions plus expenses from the sale will wipe out much of the $50 million gain, it said.

UJB Chairman T. Joseph Semord cited fierce competition in the credit card market and the slowing of the economy as the main reasons for exiting the card-issuing business. UJB, which expects to close the transaction this month, will invest proceeds from the sale into other product lines, Mr. Semord said.

UJB has $13.3 billion in assets and provides financial services through 270 offices in New Jersey and eastern Pennsylvania. Bank of Montreal has about $70 billion in assets.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, UJB shares rose 12.5 cents to $12.25.
