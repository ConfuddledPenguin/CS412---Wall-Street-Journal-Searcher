Business Brief -- Builders Transport Inc.: Revised Offer From Trism Is Weighed by Company

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/11/90

Builders Transport Inc. said it is considering a revised offer to be acquired by Trism Inc. for $9 in cash, $4 in 12-year, 12% cumulative preferred stock, and one-fifth share of Trism common stock for each of its shares outstanding. Builders Transport, a truckload carrier based in Camden, S.C., said that under the proposal Trism would put $10 million in equity into a new unit that would acquire Builders Transport. Trism would finance the balance of the transaction with senior debt and a preferred stock investment from Heller Financial Inc. and Banque Paribas.

Trism, a privately held New York-based investment company, previously offered to pay $14 a share in cash and $3 in cumulative preferred stock for the company. Trism later said it might revise the proposal of $12 cash and $4 a share in 12-year, 12% cumulative preferred stock, but instead made the new offer. Builders Transport has about five million shares outstanding. Builders Transport said that it could not immediately provide the value of the offer.

Separately, Builders Transport said it agreed to sell its refrigerated-van operation as part of a restructuring. Terms weren't disclosed.

Builders Transport also said a Delaware Chancery Court dismissed a shareholder suit brought by David C. Walentas, who is currently waging a proxy contest to obtain control of the board.
