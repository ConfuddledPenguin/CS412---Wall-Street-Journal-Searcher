Business Brief -- MGM/UA Communications Co.: Shareholder Suits Settled; Company Sues 3 Insurers

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/11/90

A set of class-action suits filed in 1986 by former shareholders against MGM Entertainment Co., now MGM/UA Communications Co., and its directors have been settled with for up to $20 million. But at the same time, the Los Angeles-based entertainment company and the directors filed a lawsuit against three insurance carriers, claiming they acted in bad faith in agreeing to the settlement. MGM/UA attorney Terry Christensen called the insurers' action "a sneak attack."

If approved Friday by a Los Angeles Superior Court judge, all defendants, including MGM/UA as a corporation, would be dropped from the litigation except majority shareholder Kirk Kerkorian and his Tracinda Corp. The suits allege that when MGM/UA was acquired by Turner Broadcasting Systems Inc. in 1986, United Artists assets were sold back to Mr. Kerkorian at an unfairly undervalued price, $470 million.

The remaining litigation against Mr. Kerkorian, who is not insured by the three companies, is set to go to trial in Los Angeles Superior Court Sept. 24.

The insurance concerns are Forum Insurance Co. of Chicago, Associated International Insurance Co. of Los Angeles and National Union Fire Insurance Co. of Pittsburgh.
