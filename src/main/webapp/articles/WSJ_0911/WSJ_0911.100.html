Tandem Says Growth Softens, Acts to Keep Profit Margins Up

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/11/90

CUPERTINO, Calif. -- Tandem Computers Inc. said that its growth rate might not meet Wall Street expectations, and that it is implementing cost-cutting measures to preserve its profit margins. Tandem, which makes computers with automatic backup systems that are used by stock exchanges and banks, among others, didn't elaborate.

Analysts had been projecting that for its fourth quarter ending Sept. 30, Tandem would earn about $39 million to $44 million, or 35 to 40 cents a share, on revenue of about $510 million. J. Neil Weintraut, an analyst with Hambrecht & Quist Inc. in San Francisco, said he trimmed his estimate of Tandem's fiscal fourth-quarter earnings to 29 cents a share on revenue of about $480 million. In the fiscal 1989 fourth quarter, Tandem earned $39.4 million, or 38 cents a share, on revenue of $456.2 million.

In projecting the downturn, Tandem cited weakness in the banking industry, a major source of its business. The company said its revenue is being crimped by high interest rates in international markets, such as Australia and Britain, as well as general economic weakness in the U.S., particularly on the East Coast.

The announcement came after the market closed. In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Tandem shares closed at $13.625, up 12.5 cents.
