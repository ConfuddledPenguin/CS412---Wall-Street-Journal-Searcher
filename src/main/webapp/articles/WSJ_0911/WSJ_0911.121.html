Who's News: Concurrent Computer Picks Denis R. Brown For Three Top Posts

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/11/90

TINTON FALLS, N.J. -- Denis R. Brown, a specialist in corporate turnarounds, was named president, chief executive officer and vice chairman of Concurrent Computer Corp. as the company undergoes a restructuring effort. Concurrent also named a director, Dean W. Freed, chairman. Mr. Freed, 67 years old, had been chairman and chief executive of EG&G Inc., a provider of scientific products and services in Wellesley, Mass.

Mr. Brown, 51, succeeds James K. Sims, 44, who resigned his three posts in the wake of the company's report Friday of a net loss of $38 million, or $2.05 a share, for the fourth quarter ended June 30. The results include a $24.2 million restructuring provision.

Mr. Brown said he will be "taking a good hard look" at Concurrent's three segments, which supply computer systems to commercial, technical and government clients. He formerly was president and chief executive of Penn Central Industries Group, a subsidiary of Penn Central Corp., Cincinnati.

Mr. Sims, who presided at Concurrent from its inception in 1985 to its merger in 1988 with Massachusetts Computer Corp., had weathered a series of revenue losses in recent years. Industry downturns and economic uncertainties resulted in a previously announced near-20% reduction in the company's world-wide workforce and several plant shutdowns.

In national over-the-counter trading yesterday, Concurrent's common stock closed at 75 cents a share, down 12.5 cents.
