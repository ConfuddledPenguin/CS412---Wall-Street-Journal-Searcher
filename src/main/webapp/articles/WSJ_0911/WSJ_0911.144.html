Marketing & Media: RJR Nabisco Seeks To Sell to Hearst Its Stake in ESPN

  • Author: Dennis Kneale
  • Date: 09/11/90

NEW YORK -- After nearly a year of futile efforts to sell its 20% stake in the ESPN sports channel, RJR Nabisco Inc. may have found a buyer in Hearst Corp., which already is a partner in two cable channels with ESPN's majority owner, Capital Cities/ABC Inc. But although talks between Hearst and RJR are under way, no agreement has been reached, and a final sale price may fall well below the $250 million that debt-laden RJR Nabisco had originally sought. People familiar with the discussions, reported yesterday in Advertising Age magazine, said Hearst may end up paying $180 million to $200 million for the 20% interest if the sale goes through.

Hearst executives couldn't be reached, and RJR declined to comment. Herb Granath, president of Capital Cities/ABC's cable division, declined to comment on any discussions, saying it was a matter for RJR and Hearst. But he did say Hearst would make sense as a partner. "We've been together in other ventures, so this would be somewhat logical," he said.

Separately, ESPN promoted its No. 2 executive, Steven Bornstein, to succeed Roger L. Werner Jr. as president and chief executive officer of the sports channel. Mr. Werner resigned last week to head up a new regional-sports venture owned by cable investor Bill Daniels.

ESPN has been enormously successful, reaching 61% of the nation's cable homes. The cable network earned upward of $125 million in pretax profit last year, though profit this year is expected to fall to $90 million because of losses on the channel's first season of high-cost major league baseball games. RJR Nabisco has been frustrated in finding a buyer for its piece of ESPN because the stake carries no management authority or even a guarantee of an annual dividend.

Those areas are under the sole control of Capital Cities/ABC, which owns the other 80% of ESPN. A litany of prospective bidders -- including such Japanese concerns as Sumitomo Group -- have expressed interest in owning the smaller stake but have balked at those terms.

But Hearst officials are less fretful over such matters, given their current relationship with Capital Cities/ABC. Hearst owns about one-third of the Arts & Entertainment channel, in which Capital Cities/ ABC holds a 38% stake and General Electric Co.'s NBC network owns the remainder. Hearst also owns about one-third of Lifetime, in which Capital Cities/ABC holds one-third ownership and Viacom Inc. holds the remaining chunk.
