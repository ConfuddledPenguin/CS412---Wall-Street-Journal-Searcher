Financial Corp.'s Bankruptcy Trustee Sues U.S. Agencies

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/11/90

LOS ANGELES -- Financial Corp. of America's bankruptcy trustee has sued several federal government agencies for allegedly causing the company to lose more than $1 billion prior to the government's 1988 takeover of the company's American Savings & Loan Association subsidiary. Trustee David A. Gill's complaint, filed in federal court here, also names as defendants 10 former officers and directors of Financial Corp., including former Chairman and Chief Executive Officer William Popejoy, for allegedly mismanaging the company between September 1984 and September 1988.

Mr. Popejoy, reached at his home in Orange County, Calif., said he hadn't seen the complaint and couldn't comment. Mr. Popejoy is currently employed by an affiliate of Robert M. Bass Group Inc., which purchased American Savings from the Federal Home Loan Bank Board in December 1988.

Among other things, the complaint charges that the Bank Board, the Federal Deposit Insurance Corp. and the Federal Savings and Loan Insurance Corp. had conflicts of interests as American creditors when they forced Financial Corp. into an operating agreement as a condition for future financial advances in 1984.

At that time, the regulators installed Mr. Popejoy and the other directors at Financial Corp. and American Savings where they allegedly followed agency orders rather than exercise independent judgment. Since the agencies were creditors of American Savings but not of Financial Corp., it was in their interest to transfer assets to the thrift at less than fair value, the complaint said.

The allegedly improper transactions included the sale at excessive discounts of millions of dollars in loans and real estate by FCA Credit Corp. to American Savings and the transfer of 1900 high desert lots, millions of dollars in unexplained transfers from a Financial Corp. account at American Savings, and the payment by American Savings of below-market interest in the Financial Corp. account.

The suit seeks unspecified damages.
