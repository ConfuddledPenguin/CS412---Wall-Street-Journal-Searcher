Letters to the Editor: False Choice

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/11/90

Increased parental control of British schools, as discussed by David Brooks (editorial page, July 26), is anything but the unalloyed good news it seems to be. The crucial factor in education is curriculum. In the name of efficiency, a standardized curriculum is reportedly being promoted in British schools. The state, not parents, will decide this "standard" curriculum.

In the U.S., as in England, the "choice" of school buildings now being offered only disarms parents and makes them foolishly think they are in charge of their children's education. Instead of parents choosing their children's curriculum, America's future curriculum will be shaped by nongovernmental agencies and foundations. They will underwrite national "achievement" tests, most probably in concert with government-related agencies such as the National Assessment of Educational Progress. These tests will define and drive future curriculum in American schools.

If parents in American and British schools promoting "choice" demanded that laws be passed to confirm their right to choose all textbooks and all tests in such "choice" schools, they would be surprised at how quickly the illusion of "choice" would fade like fog before the sun.

Geraldine E. Rodgers

Retired Teacher

Lyndhurst, N.J.
