Business Brief -- Emerald Homes L.P.: Net Loss of $10.2 Million Posted for Second Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/11/90

Emerald Homes L.P. posted a second-quarter loss of $10.2 million, or $1.94 a unit, and said it is seeking capital to help it restructure its debt and obtain new borrowings. In the year-earlier quarter, the Phoenix-based home builder had net income of $478,000, or six cents a unit. Revenue in the latest quarter plunged 39% to $13.3 million from $21.9 million.

Emerald said its loss included a $4.5 million reserve, representing the full amount of Emerald's investment in Quail Creek Country Club L.P., and a $2.4 million loss from the sale of Homeplex Mortgage Investments Corp. common stock.

"Our housing sales are still strong in Phoenix despite the difficult real-estate market and slowing economy," said Lewis J. Wright, president and chief executive officer. But Emerald said it had an operating loss in the period due to slim margins and to interest expense on land bought before Arizona real-estate values fell.

"We're hoping to restructure that debt," said James E. Teiman, chief financial officer. Mr. Teiman said the partnership is about $1 million in arrears on loan payments to two financial institutions that have been taken over by the Office of Thrift Supervision's Resolution Trust Corp. Meanwhile, the company said it is having difficulty obtaining construction financing.
