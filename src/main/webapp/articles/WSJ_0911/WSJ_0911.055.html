International: Shanghai Eases Its Curbs On Foreign Bank Branches

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/11/90

HONG KONG -- In addition to new regulations for its Pudong industrial project, Shanghai announced rules allowing foreign banks to operate bank branches and other financial institutions in the city proper. Foreign bankers said that, while the scope of business may be too restrictive to make operating a branch profitable, the tough rules won't deter them from trying to gain a toehold in Shanghai, an industrial and commercial center and former regional financial center. Until now, Beijing has allowed foreign banks to open branches only in the country's five special economic zones.

Four foreign banks -- Hongkong & Shanghai Banking Corp., Standard Chartered Bank, Bank of East Asia and Overseas-Chinese Banking Corp. -- already have Shanghai branches, which they established before the Communist takeover in 1949. This number will grow as the municipal government is expected to approve the first batch of new applicants soon.
