Mr. President, I'm Ready to Fight

  • Author: Glenn Meakem
  • Date: 09/11/90

Dear President Bush: I am a 26 year old Army Reservist and I am ready, if called, to serve in the Persian Gulf. I can only speak for myself, but I believe that many other Reservists, and indeed members of my generation, share my commitment.

I volunteered to join the Army and eventually the Reserve not for tuition money and benefits, but so that I would be ready to help our country in just this kind of crisis. Make no mistake, I fear war and I fear death. I am married and I have a lot to lose. But I am willing to endure hardship and risk my life to help our country defeat an aggressive leader who could kill millions in the future if left unchecked. We need only to remember Saddam Hussein's threat "to scorch half of Israel" with chemical weapons to understand the danger.

I am not poor. I am not underprivileged. I earned my undergraduate degree at Harvard, and am about to start my second year at Harvard Business School. However, I am willing to leave my wife and delay both my education and career to serve our country in this crisis -- even if that means going to war.

Mr. President, I know that many Americans, especially members of the generation now in its 30s and 40s, can think of nothing but the tragedy of Vietnam when they see military uniforms or hear talk of war. But I have studied the history of other wars. As a result, I do not forget the 6 million Jews who were murdered in the Holocaust or the 250,000 American soldiers and sailors (young men like myself) who died fighting the dictatorial perpetrators of these and other crimes during World War II. There is no doubt that there are times when fighting a war is unnecessary -- even immoral. Perhaps Vietnam was one of those occasions. Perhaps not. But it is equally true that there are times when a democratic people must go to war to halt aggression and atrocity.

Of course it would be best if you and other world leaders could peacefully maneuver Saddam Hussein out of Kuwait and out of power. However, if you fail, please have confidence that I am prepared, as many others in my generation are, to make whatever sacrifice is necessary to do the job by force of arms.

Mr. President, 50 years ago you faced combat for your country in a just and necessary war. If you need me now, I will too.

---

Mr. Meakem is a first lieutenant in the U.S. Army Reserve.
