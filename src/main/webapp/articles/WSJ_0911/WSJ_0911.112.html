Business Brief -- H.J. Heinz Co.: Firm Reports a 13% Increase In Net for Its 1st Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/11/90

H.J. Heinz Co. reported that net income in the fiscal first quarter increased 13% to $143.2 million, or 54 cents a share. The Pittsburgh-based food-products company said it benefited from price increases in most of its product categories as well as the strength of foreign currencies against the dollar -- which makes U.S. products cheaper overseas.

In the year-earlier period, the company earned $126.5 million, or 48 cents a share, adjusted for a 2-for-1 stock split paid Sept. 27 of last year.

Sales for the quarter, ended Aug. 1, rose 7% to $1.56 billion from $1.45 billion a year earlier.

Operating profit rose 12% in the quarter to $232.9 million from $207.5 million.
