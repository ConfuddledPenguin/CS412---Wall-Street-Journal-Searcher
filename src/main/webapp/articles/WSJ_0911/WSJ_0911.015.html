Lockheed to Discuss Japan Air's Buying Stake in New Unit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/11/90

CALABASAS, Calif. -- Lockheed Corp. said it signed a memorandum of understanding with Japan Air Lines to discuss investment by JAL in the aerospace concern's newly formed Lockheed Commercial Aircraft Center Inc. subsidiary. A Lockheed spokesman said the company plans to invest about $20 million to modify a hangar and purchase equipment for the unit, which will maintain and modify Boeing Co. 747 aircraft. JAL may acquire a minority stake of 10% or more in the unit, the spokesman said. Aircraft modification is a growing business for Lockheed and other aerospace companies, as their defense business shrinks.

The Lockheed unit has agreed with Norton Air Force Base in San Bernardino, Calif., to lease two bays of a four-bay hangar. When the base closes, sometime before 1994, Lockheed will occupy the rest of the hangar. Each bay can accommodate one Boeing 747 jetliner.

JAL has the world's largest fleet of Boeing 747s and is highly experienced in maintaining and modifying the plane, Lockheed said. JAL anticipates that a significant number of its aging 747 aircraft will need modifications in the near future, Lockheed said.

Lockheed is preparing the hangars to meet commercial specifications. The facility is expected to begin operations in the first quarter of next year. After a year of operation, the unit is expected to have about 600 employees, increasing to nearly 1,000 by 1994. The spokesman said Lockeed has about 75,000 employees.
