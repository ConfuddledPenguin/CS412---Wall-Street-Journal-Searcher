Swiss Banks Are Ordered To End Cartel-Like Pacts

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 09/11/90

Jean-Pascal Delamuraz, ordered the abolition of cartel-like agreements in the country's banking industry. The long-awaited decision conforms to a request by the country's cartel commission. The banking industry has previously said it would appeal such a decision to the Swiss Supreme Court.

Mr. Delamuraz's move caps a long battle between the banks and the commission over four cartel-like arrangements. The decree became necessary after the banks and the commission failed to agree on a solution.

One of the four pacts deals with the syndicate of big banks that dominates the underwriting of Swiss-franc bonds. Mr. Delamuraz's order means that by the end of the year, the banks must eliminate the so-called "loyalty" clause of the convention, which prevents members from participating in transactions outside the syndicate.

Also at year end, the agreement setting minimum fees for documentary credits must be abolished.

Further, the decree orders the elimination by 1992 of the convention that fixes fees on securities brokerage and of the pact that governs commissions for custodial operations.
