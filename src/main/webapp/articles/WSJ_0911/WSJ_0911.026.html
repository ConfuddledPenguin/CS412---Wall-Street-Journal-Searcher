California Offers County Aid to Avert Bankruptcy

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/11/90

SAN FRANCISCO -- California has crafted an aid package that should prevent a distressed rural county from filing for bankruptcy. Last month, Butte County came within a day of becoming the first county in U.S. history to seek protection from its creditors under a special chapter in the federal bankruptcy law. But on Friday, the state's finance department, armed with emergency legislation, offered a bailout package valued at $7.4 million and recommended $6.6 million in budget cuts.

The county, home to about 180,00 people, is studying the state's offer, but its board of supervisors has decided to cancel plans for a bankruptcy filing in the meantime.

By law, counties in California must balance their budgets each year. Because of increasing state-mandated services and repeated voter-rejection of local tax increases, Butte County has found it impossible to balance its books even after cutting deeply into library, police and other services.
