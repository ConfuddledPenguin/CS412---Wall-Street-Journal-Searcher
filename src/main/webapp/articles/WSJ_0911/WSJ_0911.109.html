International Brief -- Peugeot S.A.: French Auto Maker Hopes To Match '89 Profit Level

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/11/90

Peugeot S.A. Chairman Jacques Calvet said the French auto maker is standing by its objective of maintaining its profit for this year at the 1989 level of 10.3 billion francs ($1.96 billion). Mr. Calvet told reporters that Peugeot's consolidated revenue is expected to rise at a slightly faster rate than the 5.8% increase registered for the first half.

He said that the European market for cars next year is likely to contract 3% from this year, and that car sales this year could ease to about 13.3 million vehicles from 13.4 million in 1989. He said the oil price increase resulting from the Iraqi invasion of Kuwait means that Peugeot will have to work harder to achieve its earnings goal.

Mr. Calvet said that it is too early to identify the final effect of the Persian Gulf crisis on the French market, but that demand this year will be boosted by tax cuts planned by France, which is expected to announce this week that it will reduce the top level of the value-added tax by three percentage points to 22%.
