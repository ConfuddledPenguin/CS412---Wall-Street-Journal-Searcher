Business Brief -- Thomas & Betts Corp.: Third-Quarter Charge, Cut In Work Force Are Planned

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/11/90

Thomas & Betts Corp., a Bridgewater, N.J., maker of electrical and electronic connectors, parts and systems, said it plans to take a third-quarter charge equal to 43 cents a share, and to cut 5%, or about 250, of its workers. The moves, stemming from a consolidation of manufacturing operations and cuts in operating costs, should save the company about $10 million a year, a spokesman said. The planned charge is about $13 million before taxes but will be partly offset by a pretax gain of $4 million from the disposal of real estate and certain other assets. In the 1989 third quarter, Thomas & Betts earned $12.1 million, or 71 cents a share, on revenue of $131.3 million.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Thomas & Betts shares closed at $43, down 50 cents.
