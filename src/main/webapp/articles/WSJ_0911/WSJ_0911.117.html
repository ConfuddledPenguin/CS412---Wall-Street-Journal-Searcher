What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/11/90

OIL PRICES SWUNG WILDLY, dropping in early trading then climbing on a rumor of Iraqi troop movements near Jordan. The October crude contract closed up $1.26 at $31.30 a barrel. The Dow Jones industrials finished 3.96 points lower at 2615.59. Most bond prices also declined, and the dollar edged higher against most major currencies. ---

Developer John Portman has missed loan and mortgage payments and is in emergency meetings with creditors to restructure the collateral on more than $2 billion of debt.

---

Pennsylvania lawmakers will consider a measure to create a specialized court system for business litigation, which will be patterned after the Delaware Court of Chancery.

---

BCE agreed to sell its 48.8% stake in TransCanada PipeLines for about $1.1 billion. The firm is leaving the energy business to focus on telecommunications and financial services.

---

Columbia Savings' plan to sell its junk-bond portfolio was rejected by federal regulators, increasing the likelihood that the government will eventually take over the thrift.

---

Cable-television rates for basic service would be re-regulated under a bill passed by the House. The measure would also aid new competitors.

---

The S&L cleanup strategy being followed by the government may have outlived its usefulness, said the head of the U.S. bailout agency.

---

Duplicating copyrighted software isn't an unrestricted right for computer users, even if it is for their own use, a U.S. district judge ruled.

---

A new luxury tax is being considered as Democrats seek to skew new taxes toward the rich and Republicans resist lifting income-tax rates.

---

McDonnell Douglas will revise its retiree benefit plan, transferring obligations from a company plan to a Metropolitan Life annuity. The move will let the aerospace concern recognize a $234 million third-quarter gain.

---

GM and the UAW are still far apart on job security, the top issue in their contract talks. However, both sides say they're optimistic about reaching an agreement before the Friday night strike deadline.

---

Many hotels will be pressured to install sprinklers in rooms under a bill passed by Congress. The measure was heavily opposed by some chains.

---

Tokyo stock prices soared Monday, pushing the Nikkei Index up 1118.83 points to close at 25080.90. But the index gave up 186.60 points during Tuesday morning's session.
