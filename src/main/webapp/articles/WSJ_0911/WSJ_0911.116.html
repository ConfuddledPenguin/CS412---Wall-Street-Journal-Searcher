Business Brief -- Granges Inc.: Company Settles Lawsuit Tied to Gold Mine Venture

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/11/90

Granges Inc. said it agreed to pay 5.5 million Canadian dollars (US$4.7 million) to two Toronto companies to settle a lawsuit filed against Granges by Abermin Corp. in 1988. Under terms of the agreement with CSA Management Ltd. and Goldcorp Investment Ltd., all related claims have been dropped, Granges said. The two companies, as Abermin's major creditors, became party to the suit when Abermin went bankrupt in March.

In its suit, Abermin sought to rescind a joint venture with Granges under which they developed the Tartan Lake gold mine in Manitoba. Abermin alleged that the mine's reserves were lower than first forecast, and that costs exceeded projections.

Granges initially forecast output of 50,000 ounces of gold annually at the mine, but later reduced its forecast to 20,000 ounces. Production, which began in 1987, was suspended last fall.

Under the accord, Granges said it will acquire Abermin's partnership interest in the mine, plus related mineral claims and assets. It said funds to cover the settlement will be drawn from C$20.5 million of funds previously set aside.

Granges, based in Vancouver, British Columbia, is 32.3%-owned by Australia's M.I.M. Holdings Ltd., a large mining concern.
