U.S. Court Dismisses Part of Thrift's Suit Over Capital Rules

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/11/90

WASHINGTON -- A federal court upheld the authority of the Office of Thrift Supervision to impose new capital requirements on a thrift, in a case that temporarily put the entire savings-and-loan bailout on hold. U.S. District Judge Royce Lamberth dismissed six counts in the suit filed by Olympic Federal Savings & Loan Association, Berwyn, Ill., ruling that he has no jurisdiction to prevent federal regulators from enforcing capital standards established by the S&L cleanup law.

Earlier this year, the thrift won a ruling that had the effect of nullifying actions taken by the previous top thrift regulator, on the grounds that he hadn't been appointed properly. The ruling was later overturned by an appellate court, and Olympic pursued its court case on other grounds.

In his decision issued late last week, Judge Lamberth said he is still considering two other counts, in which Olympic Federal argued that it didn't receive sufficient notice of the new capital requirements and that the regulators hadn't considered its request for federal assistance. Judge Lamberth earlier denied Olympic Federal's request for a temporary restraining order on those grounds.

Meanwhile, Timothy Ryan, director of the thrift office, predicted that the agency would be vindicated in its contentious takeover of Franklin Savings Association, Ottawa, Kan., with $11 billion in assets. In a complex case revolving around how federal regulators accounted for Franklin's losses, a federal court ruled that the government seizure was improper. The government is appealing the decision.

Mr. Ryan said that he reviewed the thrift office actions, which were launched prior to his taking his post. "I came to the very firm conclusion that the institution, Franklin, is appropriately in the Resolution Trust Corp.," he said. The RTC acts as the liquidator of government-seized thrifts. He contended that Franklin's losses presented an "extraordinary risk" to the deposit insurance fund.
