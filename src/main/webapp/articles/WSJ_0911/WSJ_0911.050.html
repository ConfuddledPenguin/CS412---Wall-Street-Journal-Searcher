Letters to the Editor: Wise Opinion Page

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/11/90

Your Aug. 22 editorial on South Africa is right on the mark. Lebanon's factionalism is a far better analog for understanding South Africa than is America's black civil-rights movement. Seven years of living in Southern Africa brought me to use the analogy of America's Indian tribes similarly to make the dynamics comprehensible to my American friends inexperienced in African political realities. The attempts of black American politicos to exploit Nelson Mandela for domestic political purposes have been shameful but also somewhat burlesque. The realities of South Africa are now beginning to overwhelm the artificial images constructed for factional advantage in the U.S. and in support of African National Congress hopes that America might pull the rug from under the Boer government so that the ANC could simply move into office -- a scenario perhaps modeled on the perception of America's performance in Vietnam.

The Journal's somewhat contradictory role in helping construct the artificial images should not be overlooked. Despite its very accurate appraisals on the editorial page, the Journal's front-page reporting often reflects the "civil rights" stance properly decried as misleading by the editors.

With such mixed signals emanating from the Journal, the reader must choose between the realities of the "news" and those of the "opinions." I personally opt for the opinion page, which more nearly coincides with my own experience, but it detracts from the Journal's reputation for consistent realism that it does not speak here with one voice.

Hobart Rathford

St. Paul, Minn.
