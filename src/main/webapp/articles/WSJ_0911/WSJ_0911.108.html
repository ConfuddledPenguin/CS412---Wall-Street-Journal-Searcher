Seagram to Buy Back Up to 5% of Its Stock In Next Fiscal Year

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/11/90

NEW YORK -- Seagram Co. said its board authorized the purchase of as much as 5%, or 4.7 million, of the Montreal distiller's common shares on the open market at various times for the fiscal year starting Sept. 25. At yesterday's closing price of $75 a share, up 12.5 cents, in New York Stock Exchange composite trading, the stock repurchase's indicated value is about $352.5 million.

Seagram, which has 94.1 million shares outstanding, can buy the stock on the Montreal, Toronto and New York stock exchanges.

This is the fifth consecutive year Seagram's board has authorized a share buy-back, a spokesman said. He added that Seagram's stock "remains a desirable investment" and that the repurchase "undoubtedly . . . would help support" the share price.

Under Seagram's most recent buy-back, which expires Sept. 24, the company bought a total of about 2.1 million common shares for an average $78.04 apiece, as of Aug. 31. That plan also allowed for a total purchase of 5% of the company's common outstanding.

Seagram recently reported a 3% increase in net income for its second quarter, ended July 31, to $205 million, or $2.18 a share.
