International: Britain's Retail Sales Volume

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/11/90

LONDON -- Retail sales volume in August fell a provisional 1.6% from July but rose 0.5% from August 1989, the Central Statistical Office said. In the three months through August, retail sales volume fell 0.9% from the previous three months but rose 1.4% from a year earlier.

The agency, in its standard warning about the volatility of the monthly series, said the three-month figures give a better indication of the trend.

The government also said that prices of manufactured goods in August rose 0.2% from July, and that producer input prices, or prices of raw materials and fuels purchased by the manufacturing industry, rose an unadjusted 0.8%.
