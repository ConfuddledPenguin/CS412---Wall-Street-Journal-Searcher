CBOE to Begin Trading Longer-Term Stock Options

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/11/90

CHICAGO -- The Chicago Board Options Exchange said that next month it will begin trading long-term options on individual equities, which the exchange said is an industry first. The so-called Long-Term Equity Anticipation Securities, or LEAPS, are designed to appeal to retail investors "with a longer time horizon who do not currently use options," the exchange said.

The long-term put and call options for selected individual stocks will differ from existing stock options by providing investors with the ability to hedge against market swings for as long as two years. In comparison, the options for the 14 companies -- including International Business Machines Corp.; Sears, Roebuck & Co. and General Electric Co. -- on whose common shares LEAPS will initially be offered, currently extend a maximum of eight months.

The CBOE already offers long-term hedging on overall market swings, through its S&P 500 Long-Term Index Option.
