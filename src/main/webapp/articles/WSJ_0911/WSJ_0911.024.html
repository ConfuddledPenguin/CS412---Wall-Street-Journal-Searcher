Letters to the Editor: Not an Isolationist

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/11/90

I was surprised to read Paul A. Gigot's Aug. 17 "Potomac Watch" column, which takes exception to my alleged position on the current crisis in the Persian Gulf. The thrust of this piece is that I am somehow leading the pack of post-Cold War right-wing conservatives urging a return to traditional U.S. isolationism. Most journalists -- even those who appear only on the editorial page -- usually give a public figure the benefit of a quote before going on to critique or characterize his point of view. However, Mr. Gigot has failed to extend even this minimal journalistic courtesy, so it is impossible for me to know what has led him to this incorrect conclusion.

Since the Iraqi invasion of Kuwait, I have made numerous public comments. By any objective standard, a reading of the transcripts of those interviews demonstrates that I have consistently emphasized:

-- One, that the U.S. has a vital interest in this region.

-- Two, that the U.S. had no alternative to responding to the local nations' appeal for U.S. military assistance to deter further Iraqi aggression.

-- Three, that the task of deterring Iraq from further aggression and seeking the removal of its armed forces from Kuwait is best handled by a multilateral response -- diplomatically, economically and militarily.

In this crisis, there are obviously dangers that we should seek to minimize or avoid. For example, I have stressed that a protracted U.S. large-scale ground presence in Saudi Arabia would be difficult for the U.S. to sustain and over time would cause severe problems for the Saudi government, both within its own country and within the Arab world. Consequently, I have urged that we follow a reinforcing strategy, in which we emphasize air power, and that our ground forces complement and support those deployed by indigenous Arab governments, particularly Saudi Arabia and Egypt. Should Iraq invade Saudi Arabia or use chemical weapons, I have said that the U.S. should retaliate in a massive way, with no sanctuaries for Iraq in its own homeland.

If Mr. Gigot believes these views constitute an "isolationist" position, then he is using a definition that Webster's does not recognize.

Sen. Sam Nunn (D., Ga.)

Washington
