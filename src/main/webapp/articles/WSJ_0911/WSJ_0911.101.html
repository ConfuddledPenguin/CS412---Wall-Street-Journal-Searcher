Business Brief -- Hees International Bancorp Inc.: Offer Proceeds for Purchase Of Canadian Express Shares

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/11/90

Hees International Bancorp Inc., a merchant banking concern controlled by Toronto's Bronfman family, said it will proceed with its offer to purchase up to 40 million common shares of Canadian Express Ltd. for up to 26 million Canadian dollars (U.S.$22.3 million). The offer of 65 Canadian cents (56 U.S. cents) a share was deferred in April after the Ontario Securities Commission withdrew an exemption from a valuation requirement it had granted to the offer.

Hees said discussions with the commission and the Toronto Stock Exchange have resulted in the original offer's being reinstituted. The bid, to be made through the Toronto Stock Exchange, is intended to take place by the end of this month.

The bid would raise Hees's voting stake in the Toronto-based merchant banking company to about 56% from 40%.

On the Toronto Stock Exchange yesterday, Canadian Express gained 8 Canadian cents, to close at 41 cents.
