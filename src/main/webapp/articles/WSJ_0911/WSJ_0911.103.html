Xerox to Pay Fine To New York State Environment Agency

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/11/90

ALBANY, N.Y. -- Xerox Corp. agreed to pay $90,000 in civil penalties to the New York State Department of Environmental Conservation for allegedly violating state air pollution control laws. The agency charged that Xerox exceeded emission limits at its Joseph C. Wilson Center for Technology in Webster, N.Y., for two years. No evidence suggested, however, that the emissions posed any health threat, the department said. Xerox agreed to the fine without admitting any of the agency's charges.

A spokesman for Xerox said that the Stamford, Conn., business-machines company's failure to comply stemmed from a series of misunderstandings. "There was certainly never any intention of deliberately not complying," he said, adding that the company has agreed to pay the fine by Oct. 4 and that the facility is in compliance with state environmental laws.
