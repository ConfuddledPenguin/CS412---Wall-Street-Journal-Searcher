International Brief -- Bond Corp. Holdings Ltd.: Talks Held on Swapping Certain Bonds for Stock

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/11/90

Bond Corp. Holdings Ltd., the troubled Australian company, is discussing with bondholders a swap of bonds for stock or stock-related instruments. The possible deal, a "scheme of arrangement" under Australian law, would affect holders of Swiss franc and deutsche mark bonds, all unsecured, and U.S. dollar and sterling-denominated convertible bonds, also unsecured and subordinated to all other creditors of the company.

Details of the talks weren't disclosed.

The shares, or debt instruments structured as shares, would not necessarily be common shares, according to the company. Some may be redeemable preference shares, which Bond Corp. would have to redeem before paying dividends on any other shares and within a set time period.

Shares also could be preferred convertible shares that would have a preferential right to a fixed payment if the company were liquidated and be convertible into ordinary shares if the firm recovers. Separately, holders of the US$200 million of Bond Corp.'s subordinated convertible notes postponed a meeting on the sale of the Australian brewery assets until Sept. 27.

Separately, Airship Industries Ltd., a British designer, manufacturer and retailer of airships that is 47%-owned by Bond Corp., said it asked its bankers to call in receivers because of financial difficulties.
