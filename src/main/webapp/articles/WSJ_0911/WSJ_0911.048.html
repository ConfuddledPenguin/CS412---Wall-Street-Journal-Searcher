Letters to the Editor: Safe Polyester

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/11/90

I am distressed and angered that you chose to begin your Aug. 28 article "New Breed of Consultant Aims to Boost Flier's Chances of Surviving a Disaster" with a cheap shot at polyester. The statement "Polyester can kill" is misleading and grossly inaccurate. Though the article later questions the contention of consultant Linda Pearson that wearing polyester apparel "can melt on skin and hamper a prompt escape" during a plane crash, the damage has already been done -- a proven misconception has taken on an aura of respectability.

Polyester is inherently flame-resistant. That Ms. Pearson recommends the wearing of cotton, a fiber that burns rapidly when ignited, adds insult to injury.

Bob Pruyn

Chairman

Polyester Council of America

New York
