Business Brief -- Healthco International Inc.: Countersuit Alleges Gemini Violated Proxy Regulations

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/11/90

Healthco International Inc. said it filed a lawsuit in federal court in Delaware alleging Gemini Partners L.P. violated proxy rules in connection with the limited partnership's consent solicitation to remove Healthco's board. Healthco, a Boston-based dental equipment company, also said that based on a review of the consents Gemini delivered to Healthco last week, that Gemini hasn't obtained consents representing a majority of Healthco's common outstanding. Last week Gemini, based in New York, said it had received a majority of consents executed by Healthco shareholders. If certified, the consents would trigger the removal of the incumbent Healthco board.

Healthco's lawsuit seeks to have written consents executed in favor of Gemini's nominees declared invalid and seeks an injunction against Gemini's consent solicitation.

Healthco alleges that Gemini violated laws by soliciting and obtaining written consents prior to dissemination of materials concerning Healthco's proposed merger with Hicks, Muse & Co. It also alleges that Gemini is unlawfully asserting the validity of written consents obtained before shareholders knew about the proposed merger, in which Hicks Muse will buy Healthco for $19.25 a share, or about $250 million.

Last week, Gemini filed suit against Healthco in Delaware Chancery Court seeking a judgment declaring that Healthco's current board has been lawfully removed by shareholders and replaced by Gemini's seven-member slate.
