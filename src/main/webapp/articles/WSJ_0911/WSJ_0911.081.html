Brady Assails Congress On Budget Negotiations

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/11/90

WASHINGTON -- Top Bush administration officials said budget negotiations with congressional Democrats are making little progress toward a deficit-reduction pact. "I would say that talks are going nowhere fast," Treasury Secretary Nicholas Brady said on ABC's "Good Morning America." And White House spokesman Marlin Fitzwater told reporters: "We are still hopeful but looking less and less optimistic."

Democratic negotiators weren't talking, but aides confirmed that the talks are moving slowly. With little progress to review, a planned meeting yesterday morning between President Bush and congressional budget negotiators was canceled.

Few details leaked from the fourth day of closed-door talks at Andrews Air Force Base near here, but longstanding differences about raising income-tax rates and cutting capital-gains-tax rates were reportedly aired. Much of the day was consumed by intraparty caucuses.

Some Democrats are uneasy about the possibility that Mr. Bush will criticize them in his televised address to Congress on the Middle East crisis scheduled for 9 p.m. today. But Mr. Fitzwater said, "I don't expect any strong criticism in the speech, rather it will be more geared to laying out our objectives . . . why especially in view of the Persian Gulf situation it's important that the U.S. put its domestic house in order."

Without some agreement to reduce the deficit or alter the Gramm-Rudman deficit-reduction law, about $85 billion in across-the-board spending cuts will be triggered Oct. 1.
