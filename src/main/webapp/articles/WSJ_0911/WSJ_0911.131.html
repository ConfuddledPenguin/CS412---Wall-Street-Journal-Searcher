Business Brief -- Chiquita Brands International Inc.: U.S. Court Upholds Verdicts In Favor of Morrell Unit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/11/90

John Morrell & Co., a unit of Chiquita Brands International Inc., said the federal appeals court in Minneapolis, Minn., upheld two verdicts in the company's favor, including a damage award now valued at about $27 million. The court cases stem from a United Food and Commercial Workers Union strike in 1987 at Morrell's Sioux Falls, S.D., plant. Morrell sells processed and fresh meats.

In 1988, a federal jury in Rapid City, S.D., determined that the strike was illegal, and awarded Morrell damages of $24.6 million. With accrued interest, this award against the union now has a value of about $27 million, the company said.

The court also upheld a related case that involved Morrell's refusing to give back pay to the strikers.

James Lyons, president of local 304A of the United Food and Commerical Workers in Sioux Falls, said lawyers at the international union are considering appealing the decision, but haven't yet made a decision. Mr. Lyons said the union already had raised the cash, in the form of treasury bonds, for the payment.

Officials at the international union couldn't be reached for comment.

Chiquita, based in Cincinnati, is a fruit and vegetable distributor. More than 80% of Chiquita's common stock is owned by American Financial Corp., a financial holding company in Cincinnati.
