BCE Agrees To Sell Stake In TransCanada --- Company, Which Will Get $1.1 Billion for Holding, Exits Energy Business

  • Author: G. Pierre Goad
  • Date: 09/11/90

MONTREAL -- BCE Inc., giving up for good on the energy business, said it agreed to sell its 48.8% stake in TransCanada PipeLines Ltd. for about C$1.3 billion ($1.1 billion) to a group of Canadian investment dealers for subsequent sale to the public. A BCE spokesman said the company decided to take advantage of an opportunity to sell TransCanada "because it really isn't consistent with our core" telecommunications and financial services businesses.

The spokesman added that a number of BCE subsidiaries have heavy capital spending programs under way and so does TransCanada. Disposing of its stake in the Calgary, Alberta, pipeline company will free resources for a push into overseas telecommunications markets, the spokesman said.

TransCanada said that BCE's sale of its stake won't hurt the pipeline concern's ability to raise funds for its projects. "We are quite confident of our ability to complete our pipeline projects," TransCanada said in a statement.

The sale marks the end of Montreal-based BCE's current effort to slim down. In a similar transaction, BCE disposed of most its stake in oil and gas concern Encor Inc. earlier this year. BCE also abandoned a disastrous foray into the real estate business earlier this year when it wrote off its stake in BCE Development Corp.

In a two-step transaction BCE said it will sell 37,500,000 units at C$17.00 a unit, or a total of C$637.5 million, to a group of 10 Canadian investment dealers.

Each unit will consist of one TransCanada share and a warrant to purchase one TransCanada share from BCE at C$17.50 until Dec. 15, 1992. If all the warrants are exercized BCE will receive an additional C$656.3 million. BCE said the units won't be offered in the U.S.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, TransCanada closed at $14.375, down 25 cents. BCE's announcement of the sale of its stake was made following the market's close.

The investment dealers will pay for the units in two parts. A first payment of C$328.1 million will be made on closing of the transaction in October. The remaining C$309.4 million is payable by Oct. 1, 1991.

BCE said it will realize a pretax gain of about C$147 million (C$119 million after tax) on the sale of the units and will realize a similar gain if all the warrants are exercised.

Also, BCE said the transaction will have an ongoing favorable impact on its earnings, but a spokesman declined to quantify that impact. BCE said the proceeds of the sale will be used principally to reduce short-term debt.

TransCanada and BCE said the 10 underwriters agreed to seek a wide distribution for the shares.
