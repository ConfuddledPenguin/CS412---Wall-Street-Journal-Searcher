Thrift Agency Sells Enterprise Savings

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/31/90

WASHINGTON -- Resolution Trust Corp., the U.S. thrift-cleanup agency, sold Enterprise Savings Bank, Chicago, as it continued its effort to sell shaky institutions rather than let a government takeover of them reduce their market value. Enterprise Savings, with $447.8 million in assets, was acquired by United Federal Bank, a unit of Westinghouse Federal Bank, Galesburg, Ill. The thrift agency expects the government's cost of the transaction to be $55 million in funds that will be advanced to United Federal to compensate for the bad assets held by Enterprise. Under the terms of the sale, United Federal will obtain virtually all of the failed institution's assets, as well as its deposits.

Enterprise Federal was closed briefly Thursday and its shareholders' interests were wiped out. It reopened as part of United Federal Friday.

The agency believes that it gets higher prices for institutions by selling them before they have been operated by the RTC for a long period of time. Three other thrifts have been sold using this approach.
