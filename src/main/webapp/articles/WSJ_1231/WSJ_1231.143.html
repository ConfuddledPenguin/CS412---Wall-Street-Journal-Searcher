What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Date: 12/31/90

THE B-2 BOMBER PROGRAM will be frozen at current production levels by the Air Force, which plans instead to use alloted funds to work on those aircraft already in the pipeline. The project's prime contractor, Northrop, is playing down the significance of the program change, but the move will give the plane's opponents a political edge. ---

Air-to-ground phone service has been opened by the FCC to four new potential providers. The companies would be the first rivals for GTE's Airfone unit, now the only service.

---

The index of leading indicators fell 1.2% in November, its fifth straight monthly drop and yet another sign of economic weakness. Separately, machine-tool makers say manufacturers are cautious about new orders.

---

The U.S. economy should post "continuing but slow growth" in most sectors, the Commerce Department said in its annual industrial outlook.

---

First Hawaiian's plan to buy First Interstate of Hawaii faces opposition from the Justice Department, which filed an antitrust suit to block the combination of the bank concerns.

---

Data General dropped a high-pro file development agreement with NTT of Japan to build hubs for private data communications networks. The move is part of the U.S. computer maker's efforts to reduce costs.

---

Stock mutual funds had a net in flow of $1.4 billion in November, up from $734 million in October. The trend reverses the outflow of investor cash that began with the Iraqi invasion of Kuwait in early August.

---

Short-term interest rates are experiencing unusually intense upward pressure in this year's annual scramble by banks for year-end funds to meet their balance-sheet needs.

---

Matsushita Electric Industrial of Japan completed its $6.59 billion tender offer for MCA, accomplishing the year's largest acquisition and the biggest takeover yet of an American company by a Japanese concern.

---

U.S. securities exchanges have fixed many of the weaknesses that led to the 1987 stock market crash, according to an SEC study of the less-severe plunge of October 1989.

---

Charter Medical will post a loss of about $210 million for its latest fiscal year and take $100 million in write-downs. Analysts say the results could force a restructuring or even more drastic steps at the hospital chain.

---

Japan's Nippon Sanso faces U.S. opposition to its planned acquisition of Semi-Gas, a maker of semiconductors. The Justice Department plans to file an antitrust suit to block the deal.

---

Western Union said it received enough notes to complete its cash tender offer and restructure its debt. The completion of the offer is expected to follow the sale of the concern's business services operation to AT&T.

---

Las Vegas, Nev., the nation's fastest growing city, faces an economic squeeze. Its ambitious development plans are nearing fruition just as the area's economy is beginning to slow.

---

The U.S. paper industry charged that European competitors are dumping magazine-grade paper at below-market prices and pushing it out of that segment of the business.

---

Canada's BCE Development said it would stop paying interest to holders of its unsecured debentures to convince senior U.S. creditors to accept smaller debt-service payments.
