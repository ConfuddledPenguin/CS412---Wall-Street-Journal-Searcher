Business Brief -- Southwestern Energy Co.: Arkansas and Missouri Clear Rate Increases for Gas Unit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/31/90

Southwestern Energy Co., Fayetteville, Ark., said its Arkansas Western Gas Co. unit received final regulatory approval from Arkansas and Missouri for rate increases totaling $7.4 million annually. The company had requested a total of $15.3 million. Arkansas granted increases of $6.5 million a year, Missouri $900,000 a year. Most of the increases apply to residential and small-business customers.

In granting its boosts, Arkansas's public service commission also initiated a review of the way natural gas purchase costs are passed on to customers, Southwestern said. The commission maintained that "certain regulatory and contractual impediments" exist in Arkansas Western's system that could prevent it from obtaining competitively priced gas, Southwestern said.

The commission in particular described a longstanding supply contract between Arkansas Western and another subsidiary of Southwestern as "diametrically opposed" to current, more flexible contracting provisions, Southwestern said. The commission will take initial testimony March 6 and has scheduled an April 16 hearing.
