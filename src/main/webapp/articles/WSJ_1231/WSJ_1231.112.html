Business Brief -- Pacific Gas & Electric Co.: California Regulators Clear Part of Pipeline Expansion

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/31/90

The California Public Utilities Commission approved Pacific Gas & Electric Co.'s plan to expand a 900-mile natural gas pipeline from British Columbia in Canada to California. The $1.2 billion project still requires approval by the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission, which has indicated it will make a decision shortly. The California regulatory approval covers only the 415 miles of pipeline within the state, and not the parts through Idaho, Washington and Oregon. The California commission's approval is conditioned on the San Francisco-based company's complying with environmental mitigation measures. It said it "made it clear that it was up to PG&E, not the commission, to assess the market demand for gas transportation to determine whether it should undertake the expansion project." Construction is to begin in early 1992; completion is expected by late 1993.

The expanded pipeline will transport an additional 755 million cubic feet a day of Canadian natural gas to California and 148 million cubic feet to the Pacific Northwest.
