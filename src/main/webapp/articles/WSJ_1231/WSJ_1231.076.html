Left-leaning historian Eric Foner writing in the Dec. 24 issue of The Nation:

In today's U.S.S.R., it is not simply the ubiquitous shortages but how people do manage to obtain things that feeds discontent. In a world of empty stores, shopping is commonly done through the back door -- that is, via bribes, barter and official or family connections. Even those in a position to benefit from this system find it unfair and humiliating. To a large extent, whom you know now determines your standard of living. There is something positively egalitarian about the way money in a market society can erase other social distinctions, about a world in which anybody with the cash can walk into a store whatever goods he or she pleases, without incurring personal obligations.
