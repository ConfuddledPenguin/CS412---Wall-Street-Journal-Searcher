The Philippines' GNP Rises 3.1%

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/31/90

MANILA, the Philippines -- The Philippine gross national product (GNP) managed to grow at a slower pace of 3.1% during the year, according to advanced estimates reported Friday by the National Economic Development Authority. This compares with 5.7% growth in 1989 and 6.58% in 1988.

A series of natural calamities -- severe drought, an earthquake and typhoons -- the Persian Gulf crisis and a wider budget deficit pulled down the country's economic performance, the agency said.

The GNP measures the value of a country's output of goods and services.

The agency's advanced estimates placed personal consumption at a higher growth of 6.2%, compared with 5.7% a year earlier. Government consumption slacked to 3.7%, from 6.2%.
