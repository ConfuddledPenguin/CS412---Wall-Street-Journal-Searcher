Business Brief -- AmeriFirst Bank: Thrift Has 4th Period Loss, Erasing Remaining Equity

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/31/90

AmeriFirst Bank, already struggling to avoid seizure by federal thrift regulators, reported a fourth-quarter loss of $129.9 million that wipes out its remaining equity. The loss, which includes $86.7 million in loan-loss provisions, amounts to $11.76 a share. A year ago, the thrift posted a fourth-quarter loss of $49.1 million, or $4.44 a share.

AmeriFirst didn't specify the size of its total reserves for bad loans. But it said it increased the reserves after the Office of Thrift Supervision and auditors KPMG Peat Marwick reviewed its assets.

An AmeriFirst spokeswoman said the bank, which has been operating under strict supervision by the Office of Thrift Supervision since August, has proposed a plan to meet capital standards through an infusion of private capital by a group including former Chairman M. Lee Pearce.

But the Miami bank now has a negative net worth of $81 million, and Sally McIver, an analyst with SNL Securities in Charlottesville, Va., said that makes it a strong candidate to be seized by regulators.
