What's News -- World-Wide

  • Date: 12/31/90

TWO LAWMAKERS FAULTED Bush for rejecting Iraq's Jan. 12 date for talks. Senate Minority Leader Bob Dole and Rep. Les Aspin, chairman of the House Armed Services Committee, said the president and Baghdad's Saddam Hussein must break the stalemate over diplomatic talks. Dole, asserting that Americans "are not yet committed to war," said he has received indications from Iraq that Baghdad may show flexibility on dates for the talks. Meanwhile, a U.S. official said he believes EC foreign ministers will reaffirm support for a Jan. 15 deadline for an Iraqi withdrawal from Kuwait when they meet Friday.

Iraqis and other Arabs will attack American and Western targets worldwide if the U.S. and its allies launch a military offensive against Iraq, an official Baghdad newspaper warned.

---

Soviet police with bulldozers razed Moscow's five-month-old "Tent City," a group of plastic and cardboard shacks near the Kremlin that had sprung up in a gesture of disillusionment with Gorbachev's reforms. An interior ministry spokesman said that 47 people were rounded up, and that 52 makeshift dwellings were destroyed.

Moldavia agreed to comply with a Kremlin order to reconsider its law making Moldavian the republic's official language. The republic's parliament acted in response to a decree issued by Gorbachev, who has sought to defuse ethnic tension in Moldavia.

---

About 40,000 Argentines gathered outside President Menem's office in Buenos Aires to protest his pardon on Saturday of key figures who served in the military dictatorship during the 1970s "dirty war" on leftists. Among those pardoned were former Presidents Videla and Viola and a co-founder of the Montonero terrorist group.

---

China's leaders approved a vague five-year plan that offers few changes or surprises but gives the government a good deal of leeway to manage the economy. The plan was announced after a six-day Communist Party plenum in Beijing involving 584 senior party members.

---

Israeli soldiers shot and wounded 14 Palestinians in the Gaza Strip, where four people were killed Saturday by troops, Arab sources said. The army confined most Gaza residents to their homes. Separately, Israeli planes attacked an Arab guerrilla post in Lebanon that military sources said had been used to launch assaults against Israel.

---

Poland's premier-designate attempted to form a transitional government that will rule until parliamentary elections expected next spring. Jan Krzysztof Bielecki, 39, a free-market economist, was named by President Walesa on Saturday to take over from Mazowiecki, who quit after losing to Walesa in last month's presidential vote.

---

Greek officials reversed themselves and said they won't go ahead with plans to pardon Greece's jailed military dictators. The scheduled pardons of leaders of the 1967-74 right-wing military government had provoked protests by left-wing politicians as well as members of the conservative government of Prime Minister Mitsotakis.

---

Two Algerian army deserters gave up after hijacking a domestic airliner, freeing their hostages and ending a two-day standoff, officials said. The plane's pilot said the duo didn't seem to know why they commandeered the Air Algerie jet carrying 88 people from Ghardaia to Algiers. The plane was forced to land in the city of Annaba.

---

Albanian Jews are being allowed to emigrate to Israel and several already have arrived, Israel army radio reported. The Jews aren't coming through official Israeli channels, but are sponsored by Jewish groups and unofficial agencies, the radio said.

---

Japan's Kaifu shuffled his cabinet over the weekend, but the changes most likely won't alter the Tokyo government's foreign and economic policies. The new cabinet will be the prime minister's third since he took office nearly a year and a half ago.

---

Hundreds of supporters welcomed Chun Doo Hwan, South Korea's disgraced former president, as he returned to Seoul following two years of self-imposed exile at a mountain temple northeast of the capital. Chun left Seoul in November 1988 after publicly apologizing for abuses of power during his seven-year rule, which ended in 1987.

---

About 800 people rallied in support of Bangladesh's deposed President Ershad, demanding his release from house arrest, where he was placed while the government investigates corruption charges. Ershad was forced to resign Dec. 6 following two months of nationwide demonstrations.

---

Havana and Moscow signed a trade and economic cooperation accord for the coming year, calling for Cuba to exchange sugar, nickel, citrus fruit, medicines and medical equipment for Soviet oil, cereals, chemicals, machinery and spare parts.
