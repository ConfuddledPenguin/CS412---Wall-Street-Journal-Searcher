International Brief -- United Industrial Corp.: Indonesian Magnate Gains Control of Singapore Firm

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/31/90

Indonesian tycoon Liem Sioe Liong has gained control of Singapore's diversified United Industrial Corp. with a share purchase that surprised analysts. UIC's president, Oei Hong Leong, sold 182 million shares at 1.60 Singapore dollars (91 U.S. cents) each to Mr. Liem's Singapore investment company. The S$291.2 million transaction reduces Mr. Oei's stake to 4.7% from 22.4%. The holding of Mr. Liem's KMP Pte. rises to 24.5% from 6.7%.

Mr. Oei is also stepping down as UIC's president and chief executive officer.

Mr. Liem's Salim Group, Indonesia's largest conglomerate, has more than 200 companies involved in banking, trading, manufacturing, property and other activities. Headed by the 74-year-old Mr. Liem, Salim has been steadily expanding its activities in Singapore. Now, Salim will in effect get control of a large block of Singapore's commercial real estate: UIC controls the country's largest commercial-property company, Singapore Land Ltd.

Neither Mr. Liem nor Mr. Oei could be reached for comment on the transaction.
