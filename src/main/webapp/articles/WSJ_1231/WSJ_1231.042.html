International: Japan's November Jobless Rate

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/31/90

TOKYO -- Japan's seasonally adjusted jobless rate in November was 2.1% of the labor force, down from 2.2% in October, the statistics bureau of the prime minister's office reported Friday. The bureau said there were 62.98 million jobholders in November, an increase of 1.19 million workers, or 1.9%, compared with the same month a year earlier.

November's decline in unemployment contrasts with October's 1.5% year-on-year increase in the number of jobless. October's figure had marked the first year-on-year increase in the number of unemployed since October of 1987.

November's figures indicate that Japan's labor market remains tight. Officials at the Bank of Japan frequently cite the need to combat inflationary pressures as they defend the central bank's tight monetary policy.

In November, 1.26 million people were unemployed, and the decrease by 70,000 people from a year earlier represented a 5.3% decline, the bureau said.
