Letters to the Editor: The Sergeant Died With Her Boots On

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/31/90

When Col. Antoine Lecerf of the French Foreign Legion (page one, Nov. 13) says a woman would never don the famous "kepi blanc" of a legionnaire, he is probably too late. If P.C. Wren (author of "Beau Geste") is to be believed, one did. Wren's book "The Wages of Virtue" contains an incident involving a woman who served in the French Foreign Legion. Criticized that this was absurd, Wren offered to accompany the critic to North Africa and show him the tombstone, with its official epitaph, of the female legionnaire. She was a European woman who served for many years in the spahis, as a cavalry trooper, and rose to the rank of sergeant. Only after she died in battle was it discovered she was a woman.

Elizabeth M. Weber

Schenectady, N.Y.
