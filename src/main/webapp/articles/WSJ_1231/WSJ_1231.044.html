International: Devaluation of Dinar

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/31/90

BELGRADE, Yugoslavia -- Premier Ante Markovic said the dinar would be devalued by 22.2% against the German mark under reform plans announced Friday to both chambers of the federal Parliament. The government appealed to Parliament to accept planned economic reforms, warning that 1991 is "crucial" for the survival of the fractious federation.

Mr. Markovic told deputies that, beginning Tuesday, the dinar would be pegged to the mark at a rate of nine dinars to one mark. The previous rate was seven dinars to one mark.

The premier also called for a wage freeze to help keep inflation down and for ceilings on public spending by the federation and Yugoslavia's six republics.
