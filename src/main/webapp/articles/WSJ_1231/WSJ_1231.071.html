Geneva Steel Reports Lower Net, Revenue For Its First Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/31/90

VINEYARD, Utah -- Geneva Steel, citing a general decline in steel prices, reported net income for the first quarter ended Nov. 30 fell 39% on 6% lower revenue. Net dropped to $7.1 million, or 47 cents a share, from $11.8 million, or $1.01 a share, for the year-earlier quarter. Revenue dipped to $124.3 million from $132.2 million. Tons shipped eased to 340,000 from 344,000 a year ago.

Increased operating, labor and freight costs as well as unscheduled mill downtime in September hurt profit in the quarter, Geneva Steel said.

The company's $239 million modernization program, which includes building two basic oxygen furnaces, a coilbox, a continuous caster and other rolling-mill improvements, is scheduled for completion by September 1992. The upgrades are designed to reduce operating costs and increase the quality of its finished products.

Geneva makes hot-rolled sheet, plate and other steel products for use primarily in central and western U.S. markets.

The company said the Linde division of Union Carbide Corp.'s Union Carbide Industrial Gases Inc. will build and operate a $25 million oxygen plant to supply oxygen and other needed gases to the new furnaces. It is scheduled for completion in 1991's third quarter.
