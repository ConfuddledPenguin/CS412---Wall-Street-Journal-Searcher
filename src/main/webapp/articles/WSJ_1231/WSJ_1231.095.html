World Markets: Tokyo Stocks Ease in Short, Pre-Holiday Session; London Equities Decline After Lackluster Week

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 12/31/90

Tokyo stock prices slipped late in Friday's half-day session, as investors took profits ahead of a string of New Year holidays that will keep the market closed until Thursday. In London, shares closed lower, after a lackluster week in which Christmas celebrations ruled out the prospect of any genuine business activity in the stock market.

In Tokyo, the Nikkei index of 225 issues was down 91.99 points at 23848.71 at the end of the morning session. On Thursday, the index gained 53.14 points. The Tokyo Stock Price Index, or Topix, of all first section issues, which gained 10.74 points Thursday, fell 6.72, or 0.39%, to 1733.83. First section volume was estimated at 180 million shares, up from an estimated 150 million in Thursday's morning session. Declining issues outnumbered advancing issues 599 to 290, and 204 issues were unchanged.

The second section index, which added 3.99 points Thursday, was down 10.91, or 0.4%, to close at 2711.15. Second section volume was estimated at three million shares, compared with 4.5 million shares a day earlier.

The Nikkei advanced to Friday's intraday high of 24055.29 in the first hour of the session, helped by buying by arbitragers and some brokerage houses, traders said. The market gave up the gains later, as declines in stock-futures prices discouraged buying interest, while approaching margin calls also put downward pressure on prices of high-technology and other equities.

Considering a possible worsening of the continuing Middle East crisis and the uncertain political situation in the Soviet Union, "Investors felt it risky to pass the New Year's Eve with stocks in their hands," said Atsushi Koishihara, general manager of stock trading at Taiheiyo Securities.

Some participants expected that the Nikkei index would finish Friday's session above the psychologically significant 24000 level. Though the index recovered from the intraday low of 23770.72 near the end of the session, the market lacked the strength to push stock prices up further, traders said.

Traders said the Nikkei index could have risen to 26000 in December if it hadn't been for the shocking news of Soviet Foreign Minister Eduard Shevardnadze's resignation.

"I think the market ended the year at an adequate level," said Hiroyuki Murai, general manager of stock trading at Nikko Securities. He said domestic interest rates are expected to start softening in the first quarter, which has recently lent support to interest rate-sensitive shares.

But the recent swelling of stock-futures arbitrage accounts indicates the possibility of strong pressure from arbitrage selling ahead of expirations of the March futures and options contracts in mid-March.

Among the most actively traded stocks on Friday, Nippon Steel closed unchanged at 448 yen ($3.28), Sumitomo Metal added 2 to 458; Hitachi Ltd. rose 10 to 1,130, Honshu Paper fell 50 to 1,760, Mitsubishi Heavy Industries eased 5 to 668, Tokyo Electric Power fell 50 to 3,650, Kawasaki Steel was down 6 at 410, NKK was up 1 at 402, Tokyo Gas eased 2 to 565, and Marubeni lost 5 to 660.

In London, most British fund managers chose to plan to unwind positions Monday, before the official two-day close of trading on the stock exchange. The majority of operators remained absent from the market Thursday and Friday, with business expected to stay muted until after the New Year break.

The quiet tone of the holiday period had led some observers to forecast small gains for Friday in the absence of fresh bearish news. But Wall Street's weak opening tone and thin trading conditions helped to foster London's modest losses for the day. Also behind the retreat, brokers said, were bearish data on the U.S. leading indicators for November, as they underlined recessionary conditions in the U.S.

The Financial Times-Stock Exchange 100-share index ended down 7.4 points at 2160.4. The intraday high was 2170.4, and the day's worst level was 2160.0.

Associated British Foods shares closed a penny lower at 420 pence ($7.93). The company said Friday that shareholders approved the #880 million ($1.66 billion) sale of its British Sugar unit to Berisford International. Berisford shares finished 2 higher at #18 ($33.97).

ECC, an industrial materials concern, closed a penny lower at 351. The company said Friday that it completed its previously reported acquisition of Georgia Kaolin for $340 million.

Among shares that bucked the market's downward trend, the electronics group Thorn EMI finished 4 higher at 677; Carlton Communications gained 5 to 396; and the beverage concern Grand Metropolitan rose 4 to 677. Traders noted that several stocks were higher, following share-purchase recommendations in domestic newspapers.

Water company shares had a strong session, as their defensive stature attracted investors. Severn Trent rose 3 to 247, Thames Water added 2 to 275, and Anglian finished a penny better at 274.

Electricity shares had a weaker session, in fairly active trading. The electricity package, comprising shares in all of the recently privatized regional electricity companies, closed 25 pence lower at #14.58 a unit.

Elsewhere, share prices closed higher in Stockholm and Brussels, and lower in Paris and Frankfurt. Stocks were mixed in Amsterdam, Madrid and Zurich and flat in Milan. South African gold stocks closed weaker. Friday was the last trading day of the year in Frankfurt. Zurich's market will be closed Monday through Wednesday. Brussels had a late surge in activity from so-called window dressing, in which investors dump speculative stocks in an effort to make their portfolios look better at year end. Stockholm's rise and Madrid's late surge were attributed to window dressing activity.

Equities closed lower in Hong Kong and higher in Wellington, and were little changed in Sydney and mixed in Singapore and Manila; some Manila gains were attributed to window dressing. The session marked the last trading day of 1990 for Manila, Sydney and Wellington. The stock markets in Taipei and Seoul were closed Friday.
