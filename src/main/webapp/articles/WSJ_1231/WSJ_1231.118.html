International Brief -- Petroleos de Venezuela: Oil Conglomerate Approves Ambitious Expansion Plan

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/31/90

Petroleos de Venezuela approved an ambitious expansion plan that would boost oil production to 3,250,000 barrels a day in 1993 from 2,330,000 now, Energy and Mines minister Celestino Armas said Friday. Production capacity in 1991 is expected to reach 2,970,000 barrels a day, enough for sustained production of 2.5 million barrels a day. Petroleos de Venezuela also approved increasing its refining capacity, now 1.1 million barrels a day, to 1.7 million barrels by 1997. Its 1990 profit reached a record $2.12 billion, and profit for 1991 is expected to exceed $3 billion. Investments in 1991 will total 250 billion bolivars ($5.03 billion), mostly for exploration.

Mr. Armas wouldn't say whether he supports returning to the pre-Aug. 2 quota system of the Organization of Petroleum Exporting Countries; he previously said quotas would be reapplied if needed to avoid a surplus in world oil supplies. Venezuela's OPEC quota was 1,940,000 barrels a day. As a result of the increase in oil prices and output, Venezuela revised its estimated 1990 oil revenue to about $14.3 billion from less than $10 billion.
