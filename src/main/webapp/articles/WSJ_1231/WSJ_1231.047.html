Capitol Bank & Trust Of Boston Liquidated

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/31/90

WASHINGTON -- The Federal Deposit Insurance Corp. liquidated Capitol Bank & Trust Co. and began paying off depositors after the Massachusetts banking commissioner closed the Boston bank. The FDIC said it decided to liquidate the bank, which had assets of $464.2 million, after failing to get an acceptable bid to purchase the institution.

When the agency liquidates a bank, it pays depositors in full up to the federal limit of $100,000. Customers with accounts larger than that usually receive at least part payment on the balance as the FDIC sells off the bank's assets. The FDIC said it would make immediate advance payments of 45% of the uninsured deposits. Depositors could get additional payments on uninsured amounts later.

The bank, which was closed on Friday, had 28,000 depositors with deposits of $438.1 million. These amounts include $25 million held by 300 depositors with accounts of more than $100,000.

Bank liquidations and depositor losses are relatively rare. In 1990, the FDIC closed 168 banks, and the accounts were fully protected in 148 of those banks.
