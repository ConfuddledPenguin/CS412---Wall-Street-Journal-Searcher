International Brief -- Campsa S.A.: Four Spanish Oil Refineries To Divide Gasoline Stations

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/31/90

The four Spanish oil refineries that control Spain's retail gasoline monopoly, Campsa S.A., agreed to take over its distribution network by July 31, an Industry Ministry official said. Cia. Espanola de Petroleos S.A., or Cepsa; Petroleos de Mediterraneo S.A., or Petromed; Ertoil S.A.; and Repsol S.A. agreed to divide Campsa's gas stations, with each refinery taking a part of the network proportionate to the size of its shareholding. Spain must end Campsa's retail monopoly by 1992 to fulfill its obligations as a European Community member. The distribution network covers 3,600 gas stations in Spain. Repsol will sell 4% of its holding to the other shareholders in exchange for rights to the Campsa trademark. Repsol will hold 65.9% of Campsa, Cepsa 17.8%, Petromed 9.1% and Ertoil 7%.

The ministry said the move was intended to foster competition in Spain's retail gas market and to increase refineries' vertical integration into the retail sector. The refineries eventually may set retail gas prices; prices are set now by Campsa beneath government ceilings, which are readjusted every two weeks.
