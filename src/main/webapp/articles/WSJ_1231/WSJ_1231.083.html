U.S. Sees Medicare Paying Doctors 7.3% More in 1991

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/31/90

WASHINGTON -- Medicare payments to doctors should increase 7.3% in fiscal 1991, the Department of Health and Human Services said. The department set the increase, which it calls a "performance standard," for all physicians' services.

HHS lists annual fee boosts for medical services under a 1989 law that aims to control the rate of increase in Medicare payments to doctors.

The figures aren't a limit on how much more the government will pay doctors in fiscal 1991, which began Oct. 1. But under the complicated 1989 law, the failure to keep Medicare fee increases within those rates of growth could lead to strict curbs in the years ahead. Over time, the new law will slow either the rise in the fees doctors charge or the growth in the volume of services, proponents contend.
