W. Bell & Co. Seeks Chapter 11 Protection

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/31/90

ROCKVILLE, Md. -- W. Bell & Co., citing mounting losses because of weak sales, filed for protection from creditors under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code. The catalog showroom retailer said its third-quarter loss widened to $5.8 million, or $1.53 a share, from $833,000, or 22 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue fell 22% to $15.7 million from $20.2 million.

W. Bell shares plunged in reaction to the announcement. In over-the-counter trading Friday, the stock fell 75 cents to close at 50 cents a share. It had been trading as high as $4 a share earlier this year.

The company said the Chapter 11 filing will enable the company to continue operating while it works with creditors to develop a plan to pay back debt. Martin Pfeifer, treasurer, said the company had about $4.9 million in long-term debt. It listed assets of $40.7 million and liabilities of $33.5 million.

Despite the filing, the company said normal customer services and store operations such as store hours, credit card charges, merchandise returns and gift certificate redemptions would continue as usual for its retail business.

The filing, made with the U.S. Bankruptcy Court in Rockville, Md., follows an offer by Hong Kong-based Venture Link Co. to acquire all W. Bell shares outstanding. The offer, which was valued at between $8.6 million and $10.5 million in October, is still pending, the company said.
