OTC Focus: Discount-Rate Cut Sparks Late Rally; Composite Index Climbs 1.2% to 370.17

  • Author: Howard Hoffman
  • Date: 12/19/90

NEW YORK -- A late-afternoon cut in the discount rate began a final-hour stock market rally and transformed what had been a dull day for over-the-counter stocks. The Nasdaq composite index was lingering about 1.5 points higher when the Federal Reserve announced it would reduce to 6.5% from 7% the rate the Fed charges banks for borrowing by member banks. The move is effective today.

News that the Fed was still in the battle to pull corporate America out of economic doldrums began a buying spree that more than doubled early gains.

The Nasdaq composite index closed 4.45 points, or 1.2%, higher at 370.17.

"The market wanted to go higher all day. The Fed gave it the impetus," said Steve Bliss, head of OTC trading for Mabon Nugent & Co.

Investors reacted to the news by buying OTC technology and industrial stocks ahead of financial issues. While the Nasdaq 100, an index of the 100 largest nonfinancial OTC stocks, rose 2.1% on the day, the Nasdaq Financial 100 index gained just 1.2%.

Apple Computer, which benefited also from a First Boston recommendation, jumped 2 1/8 to 42 1/4. Microsoft added 1 1/4 to 75 1/4. Sun Microsystems surged 1 5/8 to 22 5/8.

Intel climbed 1 1/2 to 37 1/4.

Before the cut in the discount rate lit the fuse, yesterday's trading was anything but fireworks.

The slow pace betrayed a malaise among investors that suggested to many traders the market had begun the Christmas holidays earlier than usual.

Perhaps not realizing just how prophetic his remarks would be, one trader late in the afternoon said the market displayed good tone but needed a push before it would take any bold steps in that reluctant atmosphere.

"The easiest thing to do is nothing. Absent any major news, this is going to be a quiet week," said John Giesea, manager of OTC trading for Advest.

He was right. A moment later, the Fed made its announcement, and stock prices took off.

The advance-decline line, which had been mostly in balance, shifted. The day ended with 1,059 stocks showing gains and 899 posting losses.

National Market System volume surged from a slow pace to a more moderate 103 million shares. More than 85 million shares were traded Monday.

On the New York Stock Exchange, the gains about matched the 1.2% OTC advance. The New York Stock Exchange composite index was up 1.97 points, or 1.1%, to 180.19. The Dow Jones Industrial Average closed at 2626.73, up 33.41 points.

Earnings news and outlooks drove many stocks yesterday. One technology stock to benefit from a positive forecast was AST Research.

AST shares gained 5 1/2 points to close at 32, a new 52-week high. The 20% climb came as the computer maker said it expects fiscal second-quarter earnings to climb to $1 a share from the 62 cents a share it earned a year ago. AST also announced a 2-for-1 stock split.

Dime Financial lost 1 to 5 when the company cut its quarterly dividend in half to 10 cents a share and predicted earnings for the year would drop to $4.2 million from $7.3 million a year ago. Dime Financial said, "all signs are that the economic decline in New England will extend well into next year."

Aspect Telecommunications shares tumbled 2 1/2 to 6 after the company told analysts Monday to expect fourth-quarter results to be disappointing. Dow Jones Professional Investor Report said Aspect told analysts to anticipate a break-even quarter.

Metcalf & Eddy fell 1 1/4 to 11 1/2. The engineering and construction firm reported fiscal 1990 net income declined to $1.03 a share from $1.11 a share a year ago. Metcalf & Eddy also said it would stop work on a rehabilitation program until the Puerto Rico Aqueduct and Sewer Authority makes substantial contract payments.

Paychex shares fell 2 to 17 1/4. Paychex reported fiscal second-quarter earnings improved marginally, to 22 cents a share from 21 cents in the quarter last year.

Fabricland rose 2 1/4 to 12 1/4 on news that House of Fabrics offered $13.50 a share in cash to acquire Fabricland. In a letter to the board, Fabricland President Gary Larkins called the offer "fair and generous."

Teradata closed 7/8 higher at 13 3/4. PIR suggested recent strength in Teradata's stock might be because of NCR's 9.4% stake in the company. The speculation is that NCR might want to buy all of Teradata as part of its defense against American Telephone & Telegraph's hostile takeover attempt. PIR said Teradata shares have risen about 35% in the past week.

Reuters Holdings ADRs were unchanged at 38 3/4. Rupert Murdoch resigned from the board of the international news and systems company.

Corrections & Amplifications

HOUSE OF FABRICS Inc.'s president and chief executive officer, Gary L. In the OTC Focus column in the Dec. 19 edition, Mr. Larkin was misidentified as a Fabricland officer and his comment was incorrectly attributed to the president of Fabricland, which said it hadn't responded to the bid.
