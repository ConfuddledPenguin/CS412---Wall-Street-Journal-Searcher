Child World Expects Flat December Sales

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/31/90

AVON, Mass. -- Child World Inc., helped by clearance sales, expects December sales to be "about flat" compared with last year, said Peter Hayes, its president and chief executive officer. Mr. Hayes, however, declined to disclose a dollar figure, noting that the company doesn't report monthly sales.

In the year-earlier fourth quarter, ended Feb. 3, 1990, Child World reported sales of $404.6 million. Most of that came from December sales, analysts said.

"The discounts really brought in the traffic," Mr. Hayes said. "Without the promotion our sales would definitely have been down." Mr. Hayes estimated that unit sales were up by at least 10% from last December.

Separately, in an effort to keep Child World from filing for bankruptcy protection, a committee of the toy retailer's creditors has urged other unsecured creditors to try to negotiate a debt repayment plan out of court.

The group, composed of six major U.S. toy makers, said it "recommends that each unsecured creditor refrain from taking any precipitous action to hinder the current ongoing restructuring efforts."

Child World, short on cash, owes approximately $212 million to its vendors. On Dec. 5 it informed toy makers that it wouldn't be able to pay them until after Jan. 15. But to keep its shelves well stocked it promised to pay in cash for any deliveries made in December and the first half of January.
