Letters to the Editor: Right and Wrong Distorted in Congress

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/31/90

I note (Politics & Policy, Dec. 4) that Sen. Daniel Inouye asserts that his "Keating Five" colleagues' actions in support of a campaign contributor (Keating) were "vigorous, but not improper." I submit that Sen. Inouye has no idea of what does or does not represent proper conduct. Proper conduct in office is not a function of adhering to rules and regulations; proper conduct is a question of ethics, honesty and doing what is "right." The Keating Five and most of Congress live by one simple rule: Do what you have to do to remain in office. That the members of Congress collectively justify their actions is a testimony to their distorted view of what is right and wrong. It is no wonder that to refer to an individual as a politician is to place in question the person's honesty, integrity and personal goals.

William H. Meyerholt

Mentor, Ohio
