John Hanson Bancorp's Unit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/31/90

BELTSVILLE, Md. -- John Hanson Bancorp said its John Hanson Savings Bank unit anticipated being unable to arrange for a capital infusion of $10 million by today -- one of the requirements of its capital plan that was approved by the federal Office of Thrift Supervision in July. A bank spokesman said, "We will probably resubmit an amended capital plan and they will review that." He declined to speculate on the actions the thrift office could take if the amended plan is denied. As of Sept. 30, John Hanson's assets totaled $874.6 million.

The bank, which has 15 branch offices, said it is continuing efforts to raise capital from "all viable sources" and is working with the thrift office in efforts to comply with regulatory capital requirements.
