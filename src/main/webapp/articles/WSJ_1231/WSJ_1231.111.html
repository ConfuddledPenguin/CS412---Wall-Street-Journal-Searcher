Business Brief -- General Binding Corp.: U.S. to Ask Court to Bar Acquisition of VeloBind Inc.

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/31/90

The Justice Department said it will ask a federal court to block General Binding Corp.'s proposed $50 million acquisition of VeloBind Inc. on antitrust grounds. General Binding, Northbrook, Ill., makes business machines and related office supplies. It is the nation's largest maker of high-volume mechanized binding machines, the federal agency said. VeloBind, Fremont, Calif., is the second-largest producer of such gear.

The proposed $9.77-a-share acquisition could "substantially lessen competition" in the field, particularly between the two companies, the Justice Department said. In national over-the-counter trading Friday, VeloBind shares fell $2.125, or 23%, to close at $7, and General Binding shares slid $1 to close at $15.

General and VeloBind announced their agreement in September, but antitrust regulators' scrutiny of the deal deferred its closing. In a joint statement Friday, the two companies expressed "puzzlement" at the agency's action and said they intend to hold further talks with it in an effort to resolve the department's concerns.
