International: Kaifu Shuffles Cabinet; No Change in Policy Seen

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/31/90

TOKYO -- Prime Minister Toshiki Kaifu shuffled his cabinet over the weekend, but the changes most likely won't alter Japan's foreign and economic policies. Mr. Kaifu, who had resisted a shuffle, finally gave in to power brokers within the ruling Liberal Democratic Party but found a way to keep the most important cabinet positions, as well as the top officers of the LDP unchanged.

The new cabinet will be Mr. Kaifu's third since he took over as prime minister 17 months ago. Mr. Kaifu's term as president of the LDP, and hence prime minister, expires next October. Most political observers doubt that Mr. Kaifu will get a second term. Some wonder whether he will serve out his term.

Mr. Kaifu gave the LDP faction led by former Foreign Minister Shintaro Abe an extra seat in the cabinet.

In other cabinet moves, Motoji Kondo was named agriculture minister and Eiichi Nakao, who served as director of the Economic Planning Agency under former Prime Minister Noboru Takeshita, was named Minister of International Trade and Industry.

Kazuo Aichi was named director general of the Environment Agency. Akiko Santo, a former actress, was named director general of the Science and Technology agency.

Seiroku Kajiyama, the justice minister who created a ruckus with a slur against blacks this year, got the boot.
