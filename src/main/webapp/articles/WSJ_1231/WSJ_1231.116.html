International Brief -- Barclays Bank PLC: Stake of 51% Is Acquired In Unit of French Concern

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/31/90

Barclays Bank PLC, the largest commercial bank in Britain, said it completed the acquisition of a 51% stake in L'Europeenne de Banque, the banking arm of Credit Commercial de France of France. The bank, which confirmed Dec. 13 that it had made a conditional offer to CCF for the unit, founded in 1817, also said it signed a letter of intent to acquire the balance of the French institution's interest by early 1991. It said the total value of the transaction, once completed, is 1.5 billion French francs ($288.5 million).

Under the pact, approved by the French government, Barclays acquired 16 private and retail banking offices in France and a controlling interest in an operation with 14.1 billion francs of total assets. The deal includes the Paris head office of L'Europeenne de Banque; Barclays said its French unit, Barclays Bank S.A., will consolidate operations there and dispose of its own Paris headquarters. Barclays said the acquisition will help it double its French retail banking market share and raise its French branch operations to 70.
