HOUSE OF FABRICS Inc.'s president and chief executive officer, Gary L. Larkins, said in a letter to Fabricland Inc. directors that the $13.50-a-share bid by House of Fabrics for Fabricland was "both fair and generous" to Fabricland shareholders. In the OTC Focus column in the Dec. 19 edition, Mr. Larkin was misidentified as a Fabricland officer and his comment was incorrectly attributed to the president of Fabricland, which said it hadn't responded to the bid. (See: "OTC Focus: Discount-Rate Cut Sparks Late Rally; Composite Index Climbs 1.2% to 370.17" -- WSJ Dec. 19, 1990)

CHUO KAGAKU Co., a Japanese maker of food containers, said it will sell a device, priced at the equivalent of $7,570, to condense used plastic-foam containers at shops and restaurants so they can be recycled as fuel for industrial boilers. The price of the device was misstated in the Dec. 10 edition.

(See: "International: World Wire" -- WSJ Dec. 10, 1990)
