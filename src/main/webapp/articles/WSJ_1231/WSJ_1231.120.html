Business Brief -- United National Bancorp: New Jersey Concern Expects To Post 4th-Quarter Loss

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/31/90

United National Bancorp said it expects to post a loss for the fourth quarter but a profit for the full year, after adding $2.2 million to its provision for possible loan losses. The Branchburg, N.J., bank holding company had net income in the year-ago fourth quarter of $767,000, or 36 cents a share. For the 1989 full year, United National earned $4.2 million, or $1.92 a share, adjusted for a stock dividend paid in November 1989. An official of the company said he couldn't yet estimate the size of the fourth-quarter loss.

The official said the $2.2 million addition to loss reserves would bring the total provision for possible loan losses to $2.7 million at year end. United National is the parent of Plainfield, N.J.-based United National Bank. The bank has 18 offices in central New Jersey.

The bank holding company said the addition would accommodate write-offs of $1.5 million in nonperforming loans and would help provide for future loan losses.
