Letters to the Editor: Our Wine Beverage Unfairly Tarnished

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/31/90

An article on Sept. 12 stated that our product, Cisco, is "known on the street as liquid crack." According to the Center for Science in the Public Interest (CSPI), the term "liquid crack" was "coined by concerned citizens in Washington" and not by people on the street. We find this extremely disturbing: To call a legal alcoholic-beverage product "liquid crack" dangerously diminishes the seriousness of an illegal-drug problem that has become one of our nation's worst social ills. The implication that illegal drugs are no worse than legal alcoholic beverages does a grave disservice to anti-drug campaigns.

We have never received any complaints from consumers, retailers or wholesalers about incidences of mistaking Cisco, a 20% alcohol wine beverage, for a 4% alcohol wine cooler. Nevertheless, as soon as we heard the National Council on Alcohol and Drug Dependence and the Center for Science in the Public Interest's claims that our product could be mistaken for a wine cooler, we took a series of steps to ensure there be no confusion between Cisco and low-alcohol coolers, including placing the statement "This product is not a wine cooler" on all Cisco bottles.

We are a responsible company selling a legal product. Like all producers of alcoholic beverages, we strongly support existing laws prohibiting the purchase of alcoholic beverages by minors. We also support the efforts of community leaders and concerned citizens to curb alcohol and drug abuse and to educate consumers about the dangers of drunk driving.

Marvin Sands

Chairman

Canandaigua Wine Co.

Canandaigua, N.Y.
