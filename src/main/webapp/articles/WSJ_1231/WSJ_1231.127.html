Japan Says Cement Makers Fixed Prices in 2 Regions

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/31/90

TOKYO -- Japan's Fair Trade Commission said it admonished 12 major cement makers for agreeing to fix prices and restrict sales in two regions of Japan. The companies have until Jan. 11 to challenge the commission's ruling. If there is no challenge, the commission would calculate a fine based on the amount of sales conducted under the alleged cartel agreements, which stretch as far back as 1985 and covered the northern island of Hokkaido and southwestern Honshu. A commission spokesman declined to estimate the amount of the sales.

Japan has been under pressure from the U.S. to increase fines and toughen enforcement of its antitrust laws. U.S. and other foreign authorities have argued that collusive behavior enables some Japanese companies to use their home market as a safe haven while competing aggressively for market share abroad.

An advisory commission to Japan's Fair Trade Commission this month recommended higher fines and stronger antitrust enforcement. The issue is expected to be discussed with U.S. officials in mid-January during a follow-up to this year's Structural Impediments Initiative bilateral trade talks.

Among the companies warned by the commission were market leaders Nihon Cement Co., Onoda Cement Co., Sumitomo Cement Co. and Mitsubishi Mining & Cement Co. Company officials couldn't be reached for comment.
