Hong Kong Seeks Tougher Securities-Violations Law

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/31/90

HONG KONG -- The government unveiled legislation Friday that would extend the statute of limitations for certain securities-law violations to three years, from the current six months. Tam Wing Pong, deputy secretary for Monetary Affairs, said the change would allow the Securities and Futures Commission to bring charges within three years of a suspected violation of rules covering such offenses as shortselling, keeping an improper securities register or failing to disclose interests.

The new law would limit the securities watchdog group, however, by also requiring that charges be brought within one year of its launching an investigation.

In an interview, Mr. Tam said the SFC, established 18 months ago, has found that the existing six-month limit provides insufficient time to develop a case against complicated offenses.

"The (change's) purpose is to help them bring about more successful investigations," Mr. Tam said.

Government officials said the bill is expected to be introduced in the Legislative Council in mid-January, and could become law later in the month.
