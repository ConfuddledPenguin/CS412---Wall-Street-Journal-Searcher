New York Developer Resolves Dispute With Japan Partner

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/31/90

NEW YORK -- A New York real estate developer and its Japanese partner resolved a dispute that had stalled construction of a $400 million Manhattan office building for a year, but it remains unclear which partner will eventually own the building. Under the agreement reached Friday, the Japanese partner, KG Land New York Corp., ceded its controlling interest in Americas Tower to its partner, New York Land Corp., which is controlled by the Bernstein family. The dispute was before an independent arbitration panel when agreement was reached.

KG, a subsidiary of Kumagai Gumi Co., a big Tokyo construction and development firm, obtained an option to buy the building outright through an affiliate for "fair market value." It must exercise the option between Jan. 4 and Jan. 31.

The accord should give financially troubled KG Land time to find financing to finish the million-square-foot project, which is three-quarters completed.

Last December, after investing $180 million on the building's construction, KG moved to force the project into bankruptcy proceedings, apparently because the reputation of its partner had hindered efforts to secure the necessary financing. That didn't succeed, so the dispute went to arbitration.

The Bernsteins had been entangled in numerous legal battles surrounding the U.S. holdings of the late Philippine dictator Ferdinand Marcos and his wife Imelda. The Bernsteins had represented the Marcoses in their Manhattan real estate investments.

Under the settlement, KG Land's interest in the partnership will drop from 60% to "substantially" less than 50%, according to New York lawyer Henry Bubel, who represented the Bernsteins.
