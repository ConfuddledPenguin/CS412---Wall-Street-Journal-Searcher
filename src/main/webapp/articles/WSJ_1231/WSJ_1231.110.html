Business Brief -- Wheeling-Pittsburgh Steel Corp.: Charge of $170 Million Set, To Reflect Cost of Benefits

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/31/90

Wheeling-Pittsburgh Steel Corp. said it would take a one-time, $170 million charge to earnings for the fourth quarter. The Wheeling, W. Va., steel company, which has been in Chapter 11 bankruptcy law proceedings for a number of years, earned $29.5 million in the year-ago fourth quarter. The one-time charge includes $74 million to establish a fund for post-employment medical insurance benefits granted in a labor contract the company signed earlier this month. The charge also includes $96 million to adjust prior estimates of the present value of post-employment medical and life insurance benefits.
