International: South Korea's Trade Deficit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/31/90

SEOUL, South Korea -- Burdened by increased oil imports and sluggish exports, South Korea's current account sustained a record one-month deficit of $1.21 billion in November, the Bank of Korea reported Friday. It compared with a deficit of $625 million in October and a surplus of $498 million in November 1989. The previous one-month record deficit was $930 million in December 1981.

The November shortfall brought the 11-month deficit since January to $2.52 billion, from a surplus of $4.26 billion in the same period last year.

Bank officials said, however, that this year's cumulative deficit is likely to be reduced to about $2 billion in December when merchandise trade is expected to score a surplus of $500 million.

This would be South Korea's first annual current account deficit after four consecutive years of surpluses that ranged from $4.6 billion to $14.1 billion.
