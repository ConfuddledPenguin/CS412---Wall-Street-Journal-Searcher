Campeau Units In U.S. Post Net In a Turnaround

  • Author: Larry M. Greenberg
  • Date: 12/31/90

TORONTO -- Campeau Corp.'s two U.S. retailing subsidiaries, Federated Department Stores Inc. and Allied Stores Corp., reported combined net income of $3.9 million for November, compared with a loss in the previous month. The monthly profit was the first by the two units since they filed for Chapter 11 protection from creditors under the federal Bankruptcy Code last January. Analysts said the turnaround increases the chances the units could show a profit for the fiscal fourth quarter ending Feb. 2. Campeau is a real estate and retailing concern.

In a filing with the federal bankruptcy court in Cincinnati, Federated reported net income of $1.7 million for the four weeks ended Dec. 1 and Allied had earnings of $2.2 million. The results, which compared with a combined loss of $23.7 million for the four weeks ended Nov. 3, don't include costs related to the bankruptcy-law case. The companies didn't disclose year-earlier figures.

Revenue at both units were up from the previous month, reflecting a pickup in sales in the pre-Christmas shopping season. Federated's revenue rose 27% to $459 million from $362 million. Allied had revenue of $275.4 million, up 49% from $184.4 million.

Still, the November revenues lagged behind year-earlier levels, mainly because of a slowdown in consumer demand, the companies said.

"It is no secret that this year's Christmas selling season was difficult for us, as it was for most retailers," said Allen Questrom, chairman and chief executive officer of Federated and Allied.

But Mr. Questrom said he was encouraged by a continued rise in gross profit margins at both units. He also said the amount of cash available at Dec. 1, including investments, unpurchased receivables and working capital facilities, exceeded the expectations of the company and its bank lenders.
