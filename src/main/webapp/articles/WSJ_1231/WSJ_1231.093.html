Canadian Cabinet Upholds TV Copyright-Fees Ruling

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/31/90

OTTAWA -- The cabinet of Canadian Prime Minister Brian Mulroney upheld a ruling ordering Canada's cable television industry to pay more than 50 million Canadian dollars (US$43.1 million) annually in copyright fees to compensate U.S. movie producers and other copyright holders for the use of their programs. The Canadian Copyright Board, a regulatory agency, ruled in October that cable companies must pay copyright fees on programs they retransmit from abroad. The board's ruling implemented a Canadian commitment in the U.S.-Canada free trade agreement. The Canadian Cable Television Association, an industry group, had appealed the board's decision to the federal cabinet.

Canada's Consumer and Corporate Affairs Minister Pierre Blais said the cabinet "was satisfied that the board followed the process established by law." He said the government will issue new criteria for setting royalty fees in the future.

The cable television association is expected to appeal the cabinet's decision to the Federal Court of Canada.

The fees are retroactive to January 1990. The Copyright Board is scheduled to begin another review of retransmission payments in July to set fees for 1992.

About 57% of the total royalty payment will go to a group representing producers of U.S. movies and syndicated programs.
