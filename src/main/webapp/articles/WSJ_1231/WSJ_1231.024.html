Ex-Sunbelt Official Agrees to Plead Guilty to Charge

  • Author: Hal Lancaster
  • Date: 12/31/90

DALLAS -- The Justice Department said that Jerry W. Peeples, the former president of the defunct Sunbelt Savings Association of Texas, agreed to plead guilty to a charge of causing a false statement to be made to the Federal Election Commission. In a one-count criminal information filed in federal court here, the government alleged that in July 1985 Mr. Peeples caused the treasurer of the East Texas First Political Action Committee to report a $1,000 contribution as being voluntarily made by an individual. In fact, the information charges, the money came from Sunbelt. Mr. Peeples faces a maximum penalty of five years imprisonment and a $250,000 fine. Neither Mr. Peeples nor his attorney could be reached for comment.

But the plea agreement filed with the court Friday stipulates that Mr. Peeples, who has been working recently as an accountant in Dallas, won't face charges relating to his actions at Sunbelt as long as he fulfills the terms of the agreement.

Just last week, Edwin T. McBirney III agreed to plead guilty to bank fraud and three other federal criminal charges stemming from his role at Sunbelt, one of the more spectacular Texas thrift collapses. Mr. McBirney's cooperation was much prized by prosecutors because of his extensive dealings with other thrifts and their executives during the four years that he was chairman and chief executive officer of Sunbelt Savings.

In that time, Sunbelt made 145 loans totaling $523.3 million to directors, officers and shareholders of other thrifts, according to thrift regulators, who tallied Sunbelt's total losses at more than $2 billion. The thrift was declared insolvent and merged in August 1988 with seven other Texas thrifts into a new entity, Sunbelt Savings FSB.

In a separate Texas thrift case, a federal court jury in Dallas on Friday acquitted Bob R. Franks, a former director of the defunct Metroplex Federal Savings Association, and his onetime lawyer, Earl Rutledge of Hurst.

The two men had been indicted in May on charges related to the misuse of loan proceeds from Metroplex and charges that they attempted to hamper the grand jury investigation of the loan.
