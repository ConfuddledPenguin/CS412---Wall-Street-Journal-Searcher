Marketing & Media: Orion Holder's Investment Fetches `Mermaids' Cut

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/31/90

LOS ANGELES (AP) -- Orion Pictures Corp. agreed to give its majority shareholder a cut of profits from the movie "Mermaids" in return for a $23 million investment. Shareholder John W. Kluge and his partner Stuart Subotnick, executive vice president of Mr. Kluge's Metromedia Co., will receive up to 64% of certain profits from the movie, according to a filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission.

In addition, the filing indicates the pair may receive certain profits from Orion's next scheduled release, "The Silence of the Lambs," should receipts from "Mermaids" prove disappointing.

The romantic comedy starring Cher and Winona Ryder grossed $8.3 million over the Christmas holiday weekend.

An Orion spokeswoman would not comment on the $23 million investment -- in the form of a joint venture called MetMermaids -- saying she could not reach top executives for an explanation.

Mr. Kluge holds about 70% of Orion shares.

Orion recently retained the New York investment banking firm Salomon Brothers to find new sources of money.
