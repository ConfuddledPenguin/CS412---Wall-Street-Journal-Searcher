MNC Financial Inc. Suspends Dividends

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/31/90

BALTIMORE -- MNC Financial Inc., the struggling parent of Maryland National Bank, suspended dividend payments on its common shares and on its three issues of preferred stock. MNC, which has been increasingly hurt by problem commercial real estate loans, had agreed in October that it wouldn't pay any dividends without approval from the Federal Reserve Board.

By suspending the dividends, MNC will retain about $26 million a quarter in capital that it otherwise would have paid out to shareholders. Most of those savings will come from suspending the 29 cent-a-share quarterly dividend on its 84 million common shares outstanding.

MNC also has a small issue of convertible preferred stock and two issues of adjustable-rate preferred stock. Total quarterly dividend payments on those shares would have been about $1.8 million.

MNC common shares closed at $3.375 each, unchanged Friday in New York Stock Exchange composite trading. The dividend suspension was announced after the stock market closed.

In a statement, MNC said its board would consider restoring dividend payments "depending upon regulatory approval and a variety of factors relating to the company's performance, such as its financial condition, results of operations, capital levels and other appropriate information."

MNC, which has assets of about $25 billion, is trying to sell assets, including the nation's fourth largest bank credit-card operation, to reduce debt and improve its capital position. If MNC can't find a buyer for its credit-card business, the company plans to attempt to spin off the unit with an initial public offering valued at as much as $1.3 billion.
