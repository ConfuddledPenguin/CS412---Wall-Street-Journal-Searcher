International Paper Terms Environment Suit Meritless

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/31/90

PURCHASE, N.Y. -- International Paper Co. said a $2 billion lawsuit that alleges that the company and Georgia-Pacific Corp. dumped dioxin and other pollutants into three state rivers is "frivolous and completely without foundation." The suit, which includes more than 1,800 plaintiffs, alleges that the two paper companies knew their plants were dumping toxins into the water, but continued to do so, posing a "health risk to human beings, wildlife, aquatic life and the environment in general."

James P. Melican, general counsel, said International Paper's mill in Moss Point, Miss., meets all environmental permits and limits imposed by state and federal agencies.

"This is a manufactured lawsuit filed by a handful of plaintiffs' attorneys who have been aggressively soliciting clients for months, including by advertising in local newspapers," Mr. Melican said. The company will "aggressively defend" the suit, he added.

The suit, filed in Jackson County, Miss., chancery court, seeks to enjoin the companies from continuing the alleged practices. It asks for a judgment of $150,000 plus $1 million in punitive damages, for each plaintiff.

In Atlanta, a spokeswoman for Georgia-Pacific Corp. said the company still hadn't seen a copy of the lawsuit. She said there "isn't any merit" to allegations outlined in a story about the suit in Friday's Wall Street Journal. She added that Georgia-Pacific "plans to defend itself vigorously."
