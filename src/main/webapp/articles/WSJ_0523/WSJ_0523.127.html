Harken Energy Clears Stock-Rights Offering To Raise New Capital

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/23/90

DALLAS -- Harken Energy Corp. said its directors approved a rights offering to raise more than $40 million to repay debt, pay for capital expenditures and provide working capital to two of the company's subsidiaries. Harken, an energy concern, apparently wants to raise the funds in part to recapitalize its Harken Marketing Co. unit, which at March 31 was in violation of certain bank covenants. However, Harken said that Harken Marketing's bank group has agreed to waive those covenants until July 31 if Harken and Harken Marketing agree to make a $15 million principal payment on its $76.3 million in bank debt.

Under an agreement reached earlier this week, undisclosed Harken stockholders have agreed to loan Harken Marketing $26 million, of which $15 million will be used to pay the bank principal and the remaining amount will be used for working capital. As part of that agreement, the stockholders will be able to use their loan to buy shares under the rights offerings.

The energy concern said that under the rights offering, it would allow its shareholders to buy stock in Harken Marketing, a gasoline wholesaler and retailer that had gross profit of $9.1 million on revenue of $247 million for the first quarter, and Tejas Power Corp., a natural gas gathering and marketing concern that had first-quarter operating profit of $2.5 million on revenue of about $27 million.

Separately, Harken Energy said it had a net loss for the first quarter of $2.2 million, or 10 cents a share, compared with a loss of $1.2 million, or six cents a share, last year. Revenue increased almost 10%, to $279.7 million from $254.4 million last year.
