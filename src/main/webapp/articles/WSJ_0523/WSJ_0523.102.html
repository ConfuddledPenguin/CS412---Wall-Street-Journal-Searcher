International: Cooperation By Mitsubishi, Daimler Grows

  • Author: Kathryn Graven
  • Date: 05/23/90

TOKYO -- Mitsubishi Industrial Group and Daimler-Benz AG are quickly moving ahead with plans for global cooperation. Yesterday, Yoshio Taniguchi, vice president of Mitsubishi Corp., the trading company at the center of Japan's largest industrial groupings, said that the Mitsubishi Group has received seven proposals for cooperation from the Daimler-Benz side. In response, Mitsubishi Corp. said it will establish a department within the trading concern to handle project development with the West German conglomerate. Mr. Taniguchi will head the new department.

"The speed {of the talks} is much faster than we expected," said Masao Takemoto, a Mitsubishi Corp. spokesman.

As a result, top leaders of the two international groups may meet again as early as September to discuss further plans. Originally, the two groups were slated to meet again next March, one year after initial talks for the agreement were announced. So far, all the proposals seem to have originated with Daimler-Benz. Mitsubishi has been reluctant to talk about its own ideas.

Mitsubishi officials confirmed that the West German business giant has asked Mitsubishi to supply construction and factory equipment for an auto production plant in the Soviet Union. Daimler-Benz is negotiating with the Soviet government to produce between 240,000 and 300,000 automobiles annually at a plant to be built in Gorky, on the outskirts of Moscow. Mr. Takemoto said Mitsubishi has agreed in principle to the project and will support it as a supplier of material and equipment.

In another request, Daimler-Benz has asked Mitsubishi Motors Corp. to cooperate on technology transfer of small truck production for a truck plant in East Germany.

Daimler-Benz also wants Mitsubishi Motors to help it make further inroads into the Japanese domestic market. Daimler-Benz has asked the Japanese auto maker to help with the production and sale in Japan of a new 2.5-ton to 3.5-ton van that the West Germans are currently developing. Again, Mitsubishi Motors said it is still considering the plan.

Daimler-Benz has also asked Mitsubishi to supply auto parts to its Asian subsidiaries. Mitsubishi officials said the other proposals called for cooperation on semiconductor factories, aerospace and aircraft leasing.
