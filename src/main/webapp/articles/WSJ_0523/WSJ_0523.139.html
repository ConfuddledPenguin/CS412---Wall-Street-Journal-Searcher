Galactic Offers to Buy Cornucopia With Stock

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/23/90

VANCOUVER, British Columbia -- Galactic Resources Ltd. proposed to acquire Cornucopia Resources Ltd. through a stock swap, with an indicated value of about 33 million Canadian dollars (US$28 million). Galactic, a gold producer with mines in South Carolina and Colorado, said it proposed to issue half of one Galactic common share for each share currently outstanding of Cornucopia, an exploration concern with large land holdings in Nevada's Carlin gold district.

Galactic currently has about 36.1 million common shares outstanding; Cornucopia has about 19.4 million shares issued. On the Toronto Stock Exchange yesterday, Galactic last traded at C$3.40 a share, before trading was halted for the announcement. Cornucopia's shares last traded at C$1.70 each.

Galactic said the final share ratio would be subject to negotiation, receipt of a fairness opinion from Galactic's financial advisers, and regulatory approval.

Galactic and Cornucopia are currently joint owners of the Ivanhoe gold project in Nevada, which is expected to produce about 60,000-70,000 ounces of gold next year, after a US$10 million development plan is completed.

Cornucopia officials weren't immediately available for comment.
