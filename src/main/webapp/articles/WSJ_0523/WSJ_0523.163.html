NIKKEI PUT WARRANTS' potential gains in the event of a 30% drop in the Nikkei index of 225 Tokyo stocks were understated by 100 percentage points in a story and an accompanying table in yesterday's edition because of a miscalculation. For example, in the case of the Denmark put, the gain from the May 18 level would be 148% (instead of 48%); in the case of the newer PaineWebber put, the gain would be 242% (not 142%). (See: "Putting Latest Nikkei-Index Play to Test" -- WSJ May 22, 1990)

GREYHOUND LINES INC., a unit of Dallas-based GLI Holding Co., offered to redeem about $191 million in face amount of debt for about $86 million in cash. The figures were incorrectly stated in yesterday's edition.

(See: "Greyhound Lines Hopes to Redeem Portion of Debt" -- WSJ May 22, 1990)

CARLTON COMMUNICATIONS shares declined 25 pence to 515 pence Monday in trading on London's stock exchange. The decline was misstated in yesterday's edition.

(See: "World Markets: Taiwan Index Drops 4th Time in a Row to Low For 15-Months; Equities Off in Frankfurt, Tokyo" -- WSJ May 22, 1990)
