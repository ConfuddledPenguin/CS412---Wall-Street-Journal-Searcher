Business Brief -- Millicom Inc.: Nationwide Private Network For Dispatchers Approved

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/23/90

Millicom Inc. received permission from the Federal Communications Commission to construct a nationwide private voice and data radio network for dispatchers. The network will use the 900 megahertz frequency now used for private or Specialized Mobile Radio (SMR) voice communications. New York-based Millicom wants to combine voice and data communications traffic on the same network.

Millicom Chairman J. Shelby Bryan said the company has no system for actually combining voice and data communications onto one SMR network, but is negotiating with three major suppliers to design and build such systems. He declined to identify the manufacturers.

FCC rules normally require SMR operators to construct their networks within 12 months. Millicom received a 24-month extension, to three years, because its network will involve numerous major cities and will be linked to the public phone network to carry traffic nationwide, said Richard J. Shiben, chief of the Land, Mobile and Microwave Division of the FCC's Private Radio Bureau.
