Ex-Editor of Journal Charges Dow Jones With Libel in Suit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/23/90

NEW YORK -- Jonathan Kandell, a former assistant foreign editor at The Wall Street Journal, sued Dow Jones & Co., the newspaper's publisher, for allegedly libeling him. Mr. Kandell, who worked at the Journal for about two years, says he was fired May 2 after a dispute over a Page One article he wrote about Eastern European entrepreneurs.

The Journal, in its "Corrections & Amplifications" column May 2, said that "Communist Entrepreneurs," a book by John W. Kiser III, served in part as the basis for Mr. Kandell's March 30 article. The correction said, "The article also reflected substantial direct interviewing of the three innovators by a Wall Street Journal reporter. The article should have mentioned the book and its author."

In his legal complaint, filed in federal court in Manhattan, Mr. Kandell alleged that Dow Jones libeled him in a letter of apology it sent to Mr. Kiser.

Mr. Kandell alleged that Dow Jones damaged his professional reputation and caused him to suffer future loss of income. The lawsuit seeks compensatory damages of at least $2.6 million and punitive damages of $10 million.

A spokesman for Dow Jones said, "We believe the suit is utterly without merit, and the company intends to defend itself vigorously."

Mr. Kandell's attorney, Martin Garbus, didn't return telephone calls.
