VMS Funds Returned to Profitability In First Quarter but Cut Book Values

  • Author: Andrew Patner
  • Date: 05/23/90

CHICAGO -- The eight publicly traded funds originally sponsored by VMS Realty Partners announced a return to profitability but significantly cut their book values. Two of the funds may resume distributions, according to a spokesman. VMS Mortgage Investment Fund, a real estate investment trust, or REIT, traded on the New York Stock Exchange, reported first-quarter net income fell 67% to $3 million, or 8 cents a share, from $9.1 million, or 23 cents a share, in the year ago period. Although the fund's year-to-year profit fell, the latest quarter's performance was a sharp improvement over losses reported for the fourth quarter and full year of 1989.

The fund's managers claimed a per-share book value as of March 31 of $4.75. All of the funds were issued with book values of $10 a share or unit.

In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange yesterday, the VMS Mortgage Investment Fund's stock closed at $1.875 a share, up 12.5 cents.

The funds had been hit with losses since the empire built by VMS Realty Partners, a syndicator of limited partnerships, began crumbling last year. The funds were set up by VMS as sources of mortgage cash and made loans exclusively to VMS and its affiliates. VMS and its officers also reaped millions of dollars in fees from the funds. Earlier this year, the funds declared their independence from VMS and dumped the firm as their advisers. They also suspended cash distributions to share and unit holders.

Leonard G. Levine, president of the funds, said they made no additional allowances for loan loss reserves. But Mr. Levine said a provision for $8.46 million to cover accrued and unpaid interest owed the Mortgage Investment Fund by VMS and its affiliates cut into the announced earnings. Mr. Levine said the fund had successfully negotiated the full repayment of a $6.6 million loan from a VMS affiliate plus about $388,000 of bonus interest. He said those funds were being held in "short-term investments."

There are two VMS REITs traded on the American Stock Exchange. VMS Hotel Investment Fund reported that net fell 53% to $968,478, or 10 cents a share, from $2.1 million, or 21 cents a share. VMS Short Term Income Trust reported net of $595,752, or 9 cents a share, down 66% from $1.7 million, or 25 cents a share. The two funds had provisions for uncollected interest of about $684,000 and $1.06 million respectively. The funds now quote a per-share book value for the hotel fund of $5.20 and $7.64 for the income trust.

The two REITs traded over-the-counter, VMS Strategic Land Trust and VMS Strategic Land Fund II, showed the best performance of any VMS funds and are considering resuming distributions, according to a spokesman for the funds. The land trust reported first-quarter net of $3.1 million, or 26 cents a share, down 7.7% from $3.4 million, or 28 cents a share a year ago. The land fund posted earnings of $3.1 million, or 16 cents a share, down 49% from $6.1 million, or 31 cents a share.

Although the land trust and the land fund had to set aside about $1 million and $3 million, respectively, to cover potentially uncollectable interest, the land trust gained full repayment of $67.3 million of VMS-related loans plus $5.1 million in bonus interest and the Land Fund recovered about $35 million in loans. These proceeds have yet to be invested, Mr. Levine said.

The land trust now claims a book value of $6.85 and the land fund quotes its value at $6.32.

The three master limited partnerships, or MLPs, originally sponsored by VMS also reported profits. VMS Mortgage Investors L.P. reported income of $733,224, or 10 cents a unit, down 68% from $2.28 million, or 30 cents a unit. VMS Mortgage Investors L.P. II earned $690,683, or 5 cents a unit, down 78% from $3.2 million, or 25 cents a unit. VMS Mortgage Investors L.P. III earned $2 million, or 18 cents a unit, down 31% from $3 million, or 26 cents a unit, in the year-ago period. All three funds said that they had made provisions of about $1 to $2 million for potentially uncollectable interest payments.

The three funds claim per-unit book values of $4.10, $5.51, and $6.36, respectively.

Mr. Levine said that negotiations with VMS were continuing and that auditors working for the fund had made a "preliminary" report on the funds' interests in VMS properties and the possibility of foreclosure and other options for the properties. The funds also have retained Dean Witter Reynolds, Cushman & Wakefield, Draper & Kramer, and Grubb & Ellis to provide real estate and financial advice.
