Who's News: Time Warner Shifts Magazine Officials And Forms a Unit

  • Author: Patrick M. Reilly
  • Date: 05/23/90

NEW YORK -- Time Warner Inc. shuffled top management of its magazine unit and created a subsidiary to oversee the company's regional and special-interest properties and overseas media development. Reginald K. Brack, chairman of Time Inc. Magazines, said the new subsidiary, Time Publishing Ventures Inc., also will be used to study magazines offered for sale to Time Warner.

"There are more magazines up for sale today, I suspect, than ever in history," Mr. Brack said. "We want to be organized so we can study these offers. With all that's for sale out there, all of it starts here."

Time Inc. Magazines is the largest U.S. magazine publisher. In the past five years, its number of magazines has grown to 36 from eight, though some of the recently acquired properties are jointly owned. Time last week said it will close Southpoint, a regional magazine based in Atlanta, but it continues to develop new magazines, such as Time For Kids, European editions of Sports Illustrated, and Spanish and German versions of Fortune magazine.

Christopher Meigher, executive vice president of Time Inc. Magazines, was named president and chief executive officer of Time Publishing Ventures. The division consists of the Birmingham, Ala.-based Southern Progress Corp., publisher of Southern Living, Cooking Light, and Southern Accent, among others; it also includes many of the special-interest magazines the company has acquired or established in the past two years, such as Parenting and In Health, and Sunset, a magazine Time Warner has said it will purchase from Lane Publishing Co. for $225 million.

Mr. Brack said other shifts in the company's management are being made to "revolve around customers' interests and perceptions." Donald E. Elliman, an executive vice president, will head a new "entertainment group," consisting of People and Entertainment Weekly. Robert Miller, an executive vice president, will be head of the "news, sports, and business and money groups." The news group consists of Time and Life magazines; the sports group, Sports Illustrated and Sports Illustrated for Kids; and the business group, Fortune and Money magazines.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Time Warner shares closed at $100.125, up 87.5 cents.
