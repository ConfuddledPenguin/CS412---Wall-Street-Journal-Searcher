International: Control of London United Transferred To 2 Administrators

  • Author: Craig Forman
  • Date: 05/23/90

LONDON -- Embattled London United Investments PLC transferred management control to two court-approved administrators after the big insurance holding company failed in a seven-week effort to find new funds to meet potential U.S. liability-policy claims. In a step akin to filing for Chapter 11 protection from creditors under the U.S. Bankruptcy Code, London United entered a safe harbor for as long as three months while it tries to form a plan to pay debt. People familiar with the company have said it may need as much as #100 million ($168.7 million) or more to increase reserves for expected claims of #750 million on U.S. policies such as officers' and directors' liability, asbestos and medical-malpractice insurance.

Though an eleventh-hour savior for the major player in London's substantial market for U.S. liability coverage can't be ruled out, the administrators indicated they will focus more on trying to assemble a rescue for some of the company's insurance units to satisfy creditors, rather than try to keep the holding company operating as a going concern. The administrators, Colin Bird and Alan Barrett, partners at the accounting firm Price Waterhouse, weren't available to comment. London United officials declined to comment.

David Rowland, chairman of the British insurance brokers Sedgwick Group PLC, said it and Marsh & McLennan Cos. of New York, among others, have studied possible contributions to a rescue effort.
