Business Brief -- ABB Group: Asea Brown Unit to Sell C-E Minerals to Imetal S.A.

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/23/90

Asea Brown Boveri Inc. said in Stamford, Conn., that it agreed to sell its C-E Minerals unit to Imetal S.A. for about $150 million. Asea Brown Boveri, the U.S. unit of ABB Group, Zurich, Switzerland, said it seeks to focus on its core businesses, including products and services to the power, automation, environmental control, transit and other markets.

C-E Minerals, King of Prussia, Pa., is a supplier of alumina silica calcines and fused silica used in the refractory, investment casting and semiconductor industries. Imetal, Paris, is a building materials, industrial minerals, metal fabricating and international trading concern.

Separately, Asea Brown Boveri won a $450 million contract to build an electric power station at Bahrain's aluminum smelter, officials of the state-controlled Aluminium Bahrain (Alba) said.

The power plant project station forms part of Alba's $1.4 billion plan to expand output at the island's aluminum smelter to about 460,000 metric tons a year from around 210,000.
