Monsanto and Exxon Plastic-Material Units To Join in Venture

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/23/90

ST. LOUIS -- The chemical units of Monsanto Co. and Exxon Corp. agreed in principle to combine their thermoplastic elastomers businesses, creating an independent company with initial sales of about $100 million a year. The joint venture would merge St. Louis-based Monsanto's strengths in manufacturing and marketing the rubber-like materials with Exxon's knowledge of olefin-based raw materials and fundamental polymer science.

"We think this effort will show that one plus one is going to equal three," said a Monsanto spokesman. "It's a chance to grow faster than either of us could grow alone."

The new company, to be based in St. Louis, would have comparable numbers of managers from New York-based Exxon and Monsanto, but respective ownership stakes and other details remain to be determined. Start-up is expected by year-end, contingent upon the conclusion of negotiations and various regulatory approvals in the U.S., Europe and Latin America.

Thermoplastic elastomers are used widely in engineered industrial rubber and soft plastics for automobiles, appliances, and construction materials as well as in food packaging and medical products. World-wide consumption of such elastomers exceeds 600,000 metric tons a year, representing sales of about $1.6 billion. Consumption has been growing recently at an annual rate of about 10%, according to industry studies.

Late this year, the new company is expected to introduce two products that Monsanto has been developing. One has high-temperature tolerance that makes it suited for "under-the-hood" automotive uses; the other, a material with low permeability, is intended for specialty medical applications.
