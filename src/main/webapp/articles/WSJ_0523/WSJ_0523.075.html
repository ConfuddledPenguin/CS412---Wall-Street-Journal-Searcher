Wyle Labs Says Net Increased Threefold In Fiscal First Period

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/23/90

EL SEGUNDO, Calif. -- Wyle Laboratories expects to report fiscal first-quarter net income jumped threefold as its five-year effort to refocus on semiconductor and computer sales in its electronic business bears results, Charles M. Clough, president and chief executive officer, said. Wyle stock rose sharply and traded heavily after a Smith Barney Harris Upham & Co. analyst upgraded her opinion of the stock to buy from hold. She cited stronger-than-expected first-quarter earnings.

In composite trading yesterday on the New York Stock Exchange, Wyle closed at $14, up $2.

Wyle markets semiconductors and computer systems and supplies research, engineering, and testing services to the aerospace, defense, and nuclear energy industries.

Mr. Clough in an interview said that first-quarter net was $2.9 million to $3 million, or 29 cents to 30 cents a share. That compares with year-earlier net of $1.1 million, or 10 cents a share.

Mr. Clough said sales rose 6% to $108 million to $109 million from $102.6 million a year earlier.

"Surely," he said, "this is the best the company has been operating in my eight years here," Mr. Clough said. He added, however, that the company has no current plans to boost its dividend payment.

Sales and profit increases came mostly from Wyle's growing electronic business, which accounts for 79% of sales, compared with 70% to 75% five years ago. The segment is expected to become 80% to 85% of Wyle's sales in another five years.
