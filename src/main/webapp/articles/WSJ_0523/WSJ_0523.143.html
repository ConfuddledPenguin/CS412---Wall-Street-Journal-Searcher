Chicago Merc Reaches Pact With Japanese on Globex

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/23/90

CHICAGO -- The Chicago Mercantile Exchange, with a strong assist from U.S. trade negotiators, agreed in principle to install automated futures-contract trading terminals in Japan by the end of June. But an exchange spokesman said plans for a June launch of the system, called Globex, have been pushed back by at least a few months to allow for further testing.

The move is a major victory for the Chicago Merc, which had been counting on access to Japan to help make Globex a world-wide trading system, complementing initial plans for automated trading terminals in Chicago, New York and London. Terminals will be added in Paris about six months after Globex starts trading, the spokesman said.

Japanese regulators earlier hinted they might delay clearance for the system. The foot-dragging by the Ministry of Finance was due in part, critics asserted, to fears that Globex might siphon activity from Japan's fledgling financial-futures industry.

The automated trading system is being developed by a partnership formed by the Chicago Merc and Reuters Holdings PLC, which is providing the communications-systems expertise. The system, in which orders will be entered into a central computer and then matched automatically with offsetting orders, will function before and after the Chicago Merc's regular trading hours.
