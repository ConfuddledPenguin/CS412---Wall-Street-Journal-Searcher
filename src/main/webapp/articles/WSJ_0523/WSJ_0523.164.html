Freddie Mac Plans to Buy Five-Year Balloon Loans

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/23/90

The Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. plans to start swapping, in negotiated transactions, five-year balloon loans with monthly payments based on a 30-year repayment reschedule. The loans are attractive to starter-home buyers, military families and others who plan to stay in a house for fewer than five years. Freddie Mac expects investors to find mortgage participation certificates backed by the loans attractive because of their shorter duration and more predictable maturity.

The interest rate on the loans is lower than rates for regular 30-year loans. After five years, a borrower can repay the loan, extend it, or refinance at a new fixed rate for 25 years. New rates under the extension or refinancing options will be set at half a percentage point over the current Freddie Mac 60-day required net yield. The rate increase is capped at five percentage points over the original loan.

Freddie Mac, based in Reston, Va., is a government-sponsored, publicly traded company that operates a secondary market for mortgages, buying loans from lenders, packaging them into securities and selling them to investors.

Separately, Freddie Mac said it bought $131 million in loans on multifamily properties from Citicorp Mortgage Inc., which exchanged the loans for the same amount in Freddie Mac multifamily mortgage participation certificates. Freddie Mac said the 630 loans are secured by apartment buildings, mostly small buildings with five to 12 units, in the Chicago area.

The company said it bought a $37 million multifamily loan -- the largest it ever purchased -- from Globe Mortgage Co. of Hackensack, N.J. The loan is for rental housing in suburban Chicago.
