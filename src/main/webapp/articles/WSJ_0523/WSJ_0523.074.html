American Airlines Reduces Cargo Rates, Turns to Zone Pricing

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/23/90

FORT WORTH, Texas -- American Airlines said it reduced its published airport-to-airport cargo rates 12% and simplified its rate structure by going to zone pricing. The rate reduction, effective June 1, involves about a third of the airline's cargo business. It doesn't apply to its door-to-door rates, which remain unchanged.

The airline, a unit of AMR Corp., said it was able to reduce its rates because it flies cargo in the holds of passenger jets. American Airlines flies 2,400 flights per day, and the American Eagle commuter division, also a unit of AMR Corp., flies another 1,400. "Our operating efficiency allows us to take advantage of a full schedule of flights daily and an extensive route system," said William R. Boesch, vice president, cargo. Typically, cargo services operate their fleets for a few hours a day for cargo only.

American said it reduced its published rate structure format to two pages, from 383 pages, by going to zone pricing. Zone pricing, which American currently uses for door-to-door shipments, allows a customer to use a simple grid to determine prices based on origin, destination, weight and container, American said.

Separately, American said it will begin daily nonstop service between Nashville and San Diego and Las Vegas this July, as it expands its Nashville hub. The expansion will add 20 new jet flights a day at the hub by the end of this year, the airline said. American currently flies 85 flights per day out of Nashville, and plans to increase jet operations there to about 150 flights a day by mid-1991.
