Kollmorgen Corp. Agreement

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/23/90

SIMSBURY, Conn. -- Kollmorgen Corp. said it signed a definitive agreement to sell certain assets of its discrete-wiring business to the U.S. unit of Hitachi Chemical Co., Tokyo, for about $12 million. The agreement includes patents, technology and certain other operating assets of the business. The proposed transaction doesn't include, however, a manufacturing plant in Hicksville, N.Y., which remains for sale.

The proposed sale is part of the company's restructuring plan, adopted last September, after the collapse of a merger agreement with Vernitron Corp., a Deer Park, N.Y., motors and controls company.

Kollmorgen, which has remaining interests in motors, controls and an electro-optical business, currently is resisting Vernitron's proposals to gain control of Kollmorgen's board and to offer $15 a share for Kollmorgen.
