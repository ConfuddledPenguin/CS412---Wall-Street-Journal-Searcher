Business Brief -- San Diego Gas & Electric Co.: Accord Tied to Rate Boost Approved by a State Panel

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/23/90

San Diego Gas & Electric Co. said the California Public Utilities Commission approved an agreement the company reached with several customer groups in January that will lead to a 4% overall rate increase. The increase, the first by San Diego Gas in five years, will give the company $50.8 million in additional annual revenue. It reflects a rise in inflation, increased capital spending and fuel costs and a projected rise in the cost of buying power from other Southwestern utilities.

Under the agreement, San Diego Gas said its residential rates will go up 2%, boosting a typical customer's monthly bill for gas and electricity to $61.14 from $59.79. Commercial and industrial rates will rise 6%, agricultural rates 8% and street lighting 4%.

San Diego Gas stock rose 25 cents yesterday to close at $43 a share in New York Stock Exchange composite trading.
