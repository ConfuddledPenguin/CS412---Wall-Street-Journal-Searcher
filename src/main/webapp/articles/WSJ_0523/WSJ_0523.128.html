International Brief -- Polly Peck International PLC: U.K. Concern to Lift Stake In Sansui to 70% From 51%

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/23/90

Polly Peck International PLC announced plans to raise its stake in Sansui Electric Co. to 70% from 51%. The British company, whose biggest interests include electronics and fruit, said it plans to make the Japanese company a more international business by merging it with Polly Peck electronics businesses in Italy, Taiwan and elsewhere. Polly Peck's 51% investment in ailing Sansui last January was a rare instance of a foreigner buying control of a Japanese electronics company, and sparked controversy in Japan.

But London analysts said that the problems at Sansui, once one of Japan's premier hi-fi makers, run deep. Rebuilding Sansui "is going to take time and a lot of money," said analyst Paul Beaufrere of London stockbrokers James Capel & Co.

In London trading yesterday in a generally rising market, Polly Peck's stock ended two pence lower at 431 pence ($7.27) a share.
