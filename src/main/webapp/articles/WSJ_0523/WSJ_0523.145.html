Who's News: John O'Donnell Quits Two Top Positions At American Republic

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/23/90

TORRANCE, Calif. -- American Republic Bancorp said its president and chief executive officer, John B. O'Donnell, who also held those titles at its Republic Bank unit, resigned. In a brief statement, American Republic said Mr. O'Donnell also resigned as a director of both American Republic and Republic Bank. The company quoted Mr. O'Donnell as saying in a letter to the two boards that he was resigning because he "may have inadvertently engaged in activities which appear inconsistent with company policy."

American Republic didn't specify the policy, but the statement said the bank didn't suffer any losses as a result of such activities. It added, however, that both boards still felt it to be in the best interests of the company and the bank to accept Mr. O'Donnell's resignation, effective immediately.

Neither Mr. O'Donnell nor his attorney could be reached for comment.

Thomas E. Malloy, chairman of the bank and the holding company, will also serve as acting president and chief executive of American Republic and Republic Bank while the boards conduct a search to fill those posts, the company said. Mr. Malloy didn't return several phone calls seeking comment.
