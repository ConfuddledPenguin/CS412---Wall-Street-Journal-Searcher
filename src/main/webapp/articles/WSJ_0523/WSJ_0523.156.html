International: Schering, Sandoz Cancel Agrochemical Lines Merger

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/23/90

FRANKFURT, West Germany -- Schering AG, the West German chemicals and pharmaceutical group, said the expected merger of its big agrochemicals business with that of Switzerland's Sandoz AG has been called off. In an unexpected announcement, Schering cited last-minute complications in talks over the joint venture. Both companies recently had indicated the agrochemical merger was imminent.

A Schering spokeswoman said the talks collapsed after Sandoz sought to expand the planned joint venture into business cooperation across the board. Officials of the West German company hinted that when agreement couldn't be reached on a full-scale linkup, Sandoz shied away from going through with a merger of just agrochemicals.

Sandoz officials declined to specify what the last-minute objections were that led to calling off the agreement. But a spokeswoman said Schering's claim that the deal was killed because Sandoz demanded a broader agreement wasn't a view shared by Sandoz.
