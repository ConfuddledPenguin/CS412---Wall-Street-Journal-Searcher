Technology Brief -- Microsoft Corp.: New Software Will Make Computers Easier to Use

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/23/90

Microsoft Corp. introduced a new version of its Windows software, which makes personal computers easier to use by giving the MS-DOS standard a major facelift. The product, which sells for a list price of $150, is expected to cement the company's position as the dominant supplier of the software for personal computers.

Microsoft will begin shipping Windows 3.0 immediately. It said that about 24 manufacturers of personal computers will include a copy of Windows with some of the machines they sell. The companies include AT&T, AST Research, Tandy, Toshiba and NCR.

Analysts expect Microsoft to sell five million copies of Windows in its fiscal year beginning July 1.
