Deere's Net Rose In Its Second Quarter On 11% Sales Increase

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/23/90

MOLINE, Ill. -- Deere & Co. said that strong North American retail sales contributed to a 10% increase in net income for the second quarter ended April 30. The maker of farm and industrial equipment said net was $144 million, or $1.90 a share, compared with the year-earlier $130.5 million, or $1.74 per share. Sales rose 11% to $2.12 billion from $1.91 billion.

For the six-month period ended April 30, Deere's earnings rose 24% to $222.5 million, or $2.94 a share, from $178.9 million, or $2.39 a share in the year-ago period. Sales during the six-month period increased 14% to $3.76 billion from $3.29 billion.

Deere attributed its second-quarter and year-to-date improvements to increased sales, higher production volumes and improved operating efficiency in the company's North American operations. The company also noted that the six-month results last year were adversely affected by the manufacturing start-up costs of new combines and large tractors.

"North American agricultural conditions continue to be favorable, and our outlook for retail sales of John Deere agricultural equipment remains positive," the company said.

The company said that improved moisture conditions should stimulate demand for lawn and grounds-care equipment. Outside the U.S. and Canada, the company said, retail sales of John Deere farm equipment are expected to be "modestly" higher this year.

Due to increased interest rates and slower construction activity, particularly in the eastern United States and Canada, the company said the outlook for North American retail sales of John Deere industrial equipment has become "cautious."

The company said its "overall outlook" for the remainder of the year looks positive, but inflation and price competition demands cost-control efforts and improved operating efficiency.
