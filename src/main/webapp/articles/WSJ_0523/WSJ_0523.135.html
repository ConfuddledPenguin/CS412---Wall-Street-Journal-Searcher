World Bank Affiliate Joins Dutch Bank in Loan Plan

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/23/90

WASHINGTON -- The International Finance Corp., a World Bank affiliate, and Algemene Bank Nederland signed an agreement creating a $50 million co-financing facility for projects in a number of Third World nations. Initially, the IFC and the Dutch commercial bank each will commit $25 million for the facility, which will help finance private business ventures in such nations as Indonesia, Pakistan, Sri Lanka, Morocco and Turkey.

The World Bank affiliate said it will act as the lender of record for the full amount of each loan, with ABN serving as a 50% participant in such credits. The Dutch bank, through its local branches and subsidiaries in developing countries, will identify prospective borrowers, assess the feasibility of projects and supervise them once loans are approved. Loans will range from $3 million to $6 million.

"This new form of joint lending will provide much-needed foreign currency financing for smaller projects in developing countries where our partner ABN has a strong local presence," said Sir William Ryrie, the IFC's executive vice president.
