Business Brief -- Xerox Corp.: Office Machines Unveiled With Copier, Fax Features

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/23/90

Xerox Corp. introduced, as expected, four new multifunction office machines that can act as both digital copiers and facsimile machines. The new devices, which will range in price from $4,295 to $8,995, have been sold since last year in Japan by Xerox's Japanese subsidiary. The most expensive machine -- a free-standing, 11-page-per-minute digital copier with fax capability -- also offers some cut-and-paste-type document editing features. A similar machine is being gradually introduced to the U.S. market by Japan's Ricoh Corp.

Executives of the Stamford, Conn., company said add-in cards, available later this year, will enable all the new machines to be hooked up to computers so they can function as scanners and laser printers.

But the executives added that these souped-up versions of the devices introduced yesterday won't comprise the mysterious "new family" of multifunction machines, also due later this year, to which Xerox President Paul Allaire alluded at last week's annual meeting.
