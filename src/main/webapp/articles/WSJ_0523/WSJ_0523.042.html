Commodity Funds' Gains

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/23/90

CHICAGO -- The average managed commodity fund gained 5.1% in April, according to Norwood Securities, Chicago, which tracks the performance of 132 publicly offered commodity funds. Wide fluctuations in the prices of Treasury bond and foreign currency futures, as well as grain futures during the month enabled fund managers to profit on short-term price swings in those markets, said Jay Klopfenstein, Norwood president.

For the first four months of the year the funds showed an average gain of 10.6%.
