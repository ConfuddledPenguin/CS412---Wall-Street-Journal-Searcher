Who's News: Oracle Creates U.S. Subsidiary, Names Chairman

  • Author: Richard B. Schmitt
  • Date: 05/23/90

REDWOOD SHORES, Calif. -- Oracle Systems Corp. said it created a separate U.S. operating subsidiary in an attempt to address management problems that figured in its poorly received fiscal-third-quarter earnings report. In addition, the developer of data base software said Lawrence J. Ellison, president and chief executive officer, would be assuming the additional post of chairman, succeeding Donald L. Lucas, who continues as the company's executive committee chairman.

Oracle also named Gary D. Kennedy, senior vice president and general manager of its U.S. operations, to the post of president of the new subsidiary, Oracle USA.

"The fact that we are reorganizing the U.S. operation into a subsidiary is borne out of our determination to move the financial-control organization closer to the distribution organization. The way to do that is to give them everything they need to operate as an independent subsidiary," said Peter Tierney, senior vice president. "That is a significant move on our part to again improve the control aspects in the U.S. operation, which was an area where we had an issue regarding third-quarter results."

Oracle hit a flat spot in its five-year growth spurt during its quarter ended Feb. 28, reporting nearly flat net income of $24.3 million, or 18 cents a share, on a 54% rise in revenue to $236.4 million. The lower-than-expected net largely reflected roughly 5% less revenue than analysts expected; the company itself conceded a glitch in completing certain orders, which were picked up in the fourth quarter.

The results nonetheless spooked investors, prompting Oracle stock to drop about 30% in a day as well as a number of shareholder suits claiming securities fraud. Yesterday's reorganization was announced after markets closed.

Mr. Tierney said naming Mr. Ellison to the chairmanship reflects "just a maturation of the organization. Now, Larry has a management team reporting to him that is in place and he is confident that he can broaden his role in the company."

Oracle also said Geoffrey W. Squire, formerly senior vice president and Oracle Europe chief executive officer, was named president of world-wide distribution operations as well as a corporate executive vice president. Oracle said John R. Luongo, formerly international-division senior vice president, is resigning. Mr. Tierney said the company's international operations have remained "very strong," and said that Mr. Luongo's departure was "really a classic case of an executive wanting to pursue another interest."

Finally, Oracle said Joseph Costello, president and chief executive officer of Cadence Design Systems Inc., was named a director, filling a vacancy.
