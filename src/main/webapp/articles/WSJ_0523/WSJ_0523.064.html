REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: Colonialized Colonialists

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/23/90

The poor Russians. They are playing the role of a "colony" to their Comecon partners by letting them buy raw materials cheaply, according to a Moscow weekly. For their generous subsidization -- estimated at an annual $140 a person in the case of Hungary -- the Soviets get less than nothing, surmises the current issue of Novoje Vremja. In Eastern Europe anti-Soviet sentiment is flourishing, and Moscow's political and military influence is plummeting. Machinery and equipment the Russians got from Comecon trade have been obsolescent and overpriced, the paper complains. The Soviets ignored their national interests by not insisting on market-oriented relations in Comecon, Novoje Vremja suggests. Where is it written that colonialists are immune to colonialization by their own dogma?
