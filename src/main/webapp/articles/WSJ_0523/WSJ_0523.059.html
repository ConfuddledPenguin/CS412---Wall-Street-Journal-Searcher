French Foreign Trade Deficit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/23/90

PARIS -- France's seasonally adjusted foreign trade account showed a deficit of 4.31 billion francs ($769.5 million) in April after a revised March deficit of 1.06 billion francs, according to preliminary data published by the National Customs Office. Prior to the revision, the adjusted March result had been reported as a deficit of 858 million francs.

The deficit was significantly more than Paris economists had been expecting as a result of inventory trends after a significant drawing down of stock levels in the first quarter.

On an unadjusted basis, the April result was a deficit of 3.03 billion francs after a revised March deficit of 1.08 billion francs. The March result had been reported earlier as a deficit of 877 million francs. France had an unadjusted trade deficit of 2.35 billion francs for April 1989.
