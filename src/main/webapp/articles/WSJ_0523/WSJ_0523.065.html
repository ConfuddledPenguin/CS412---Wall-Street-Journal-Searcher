Tool Maker Stanley, Others Are Charged With Price Fixing

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/23/90

NEW BRITAIN, Conn. -- Stanley Works and one current and one former executive of the tool maker were among a number of defendants charged with conspiring to fix prices on architectural hinges. A federal grand jury in St. Louis indicted Stanley Works; Richard G. Martin, president and general manager of the Stanley Hardware division; and John F. Hollfelder, who was vice president of sales for the division when he left the company in 1988.

Also charged were Hager Hinge Co. of St. Louis; McKinney Products Co. of Scranton, Pa.; David B. Gibson, president of McKinney; Robert A. Haversat, a former president of McKinney; Lawrence Brothers Inc. of Sterling, Ill., and its chairman, John A. Lawrence.

The indictment charges the defendants with engaging in the price-fixing conspiracy from at least April 1986 to April 1988.

For a corporation, the maximum penalty is as much as a $1 million fine; twice the gain the company derived from the conspiracy; or twice the loss suffered by victims. For an individual, the maximum penalty is three years in prison plus as much as a $250,000 fine or the other two factors.

Stanley Works said it and Mr. Martin would vigorously defend themselves against the charges.

Mr. Hollfelder was unavailable for comment.

Mr. Lawrence declined immediate comment. The other defendants couldn't be reached.
