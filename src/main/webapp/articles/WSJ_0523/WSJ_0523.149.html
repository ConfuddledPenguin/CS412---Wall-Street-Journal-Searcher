Business Brief -- Dayton Hudson Corp.: FTC Requests More Data On Marshall Field Purchase

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/23/90

Dayton Hudson Corp. said its planned purchase of Marshall Field & Co. for $1.04 billion will be delayed because of a request for more information from the Federal Trade Commission, which examines such proposed combinations to see if they violate antitrust laws. The Minneapolis-based retailer said that interest expense and depreciation tied to the planned purchase of Marshall Field would reduce earnings for the current fiscal second quarter by an unspecified amount. But it said that "strong profit improvement" from its core businesses would offset the reduction.

Dayton Hudson said it was confident the purchase of Chicago-based Marshall Field would be completed, despite the delay.

For the first quarter ended May 5, the company said that brisk sales at its three retail store operating companies boosted earnings before accounting changes to $58 million, or 70 cents a fully diluted share, from $40 million, or 51 cents a fully diluted share, a year earlier.
