Business Brief -- Westvaco Corp.: Paper Concern Reports Net For 2nd Quarter Fell 21%

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/23/90

Westvaco Corp. reported fiscal second-quarter earnings declined 21%, reflecting a slowdown in the paper industry plus modernization work that took one of its paper machines out of service. Net for the quarter ended April 30 fell to $46 million, or 71 cents a share, from $58.2 million, or 90 cents a share, in the year-earlier period. Sales rose 3.8% to a record $583 million from $561.7 million.

"Growth in the U.S. economy remains at only moderate levels," said John A. Luke, president and chief executive officer. He added that Westvaco, based in New York, took one of its largest coated-paper machines out of operation for most of April to modify it to make more-advanced products. Without the effect of the modernization project, which temporarily reduced plant capacity, second-quarter earnings would have declined 10%.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Westvaco shares fell 50 cents to close at $26.125.
