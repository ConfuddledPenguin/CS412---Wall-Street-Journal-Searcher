International Brief -- Siemens AG: Printer Operations to Merge With Those of Mannesmann

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/23/90

Siemens AG and Mannesmann AG said yesterday that they plan to merge their respective office printing-machine businesses effective October 1. The two big, diversified West German electronics groups said the printer operations will be merged into Mannesmann's current wholly owned Mannesmann Tally G.m.b.H. subsidiary, in which Munich-based Siemens will take a 49% stake. Dusseldorf-based Mannesmann will control the remaining 51% of the new entity, the companies said.

The merger, which is subject to antitrust review by West Germany's Federal Cartel Office, will strengthen Mannesmann Tally's position as Europe's largest manufacturer of office printers. The concern also operates in the U.S.

The post-merger company will start out with around 770 million Deutsche marks ($463.2 million) of annual sales. Of that total, the present Mannesmann Tally businesses will account for around 520 million marks, while the Siemens printer operations represent about 250 million marks of sales.

The companies declined to discuss financial details of the agreement for the merger. But a Mannesmann spokesman said cash payments aren't involved in the transaction.
