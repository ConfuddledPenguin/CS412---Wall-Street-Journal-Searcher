Drexel to Give Court a Plan For Continued Operation

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/23/90

NEW YORK -- It's not over, it seems, 'til it's over. Drexel Burnham Lambert Group Inc., the collapsed brokerage firm, says it plans to soon propose to the federal bankruptcy court that it remain in business, of an undisclosed nature.

One reason: To prop the near-worthless stock that Drexel's 1,000 shareholders currently hold. Another: To provide a vehicle for big tax-loss carry-forwards, which Drexel amassed before it collapsed, and which could be used to lower the tax bite on future profits. Unable to finance its deteriorating pile of junk bonds, Drexel filed for bankruptcy-law protection in February.

Currently, the firm has a staff of about 600, out of the 5,300 it employed before it filed for bankruptcy-law protection. The firm still has more than $1 billion in assets and about $300 million in equity, a spokesman said.

Since its collapse, Drexel has been liquidating its portfolio and getting out of securities businesses. It has also sold its junk-bond client data base and customer lists. And in three weeks, the firm plans to auction its office furniture, personal computers and other items, including five corporate cafeterias, kitchen fixtures and appliances.
