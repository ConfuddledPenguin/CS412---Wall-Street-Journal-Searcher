Average CD Yields Were Little Changed In the Latest Week

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/23/90

NEW YORK -- Yields on certificates of deposit at major banks and brokerage companies were largely unchanged in the week ended yesterday. Average yields on small-denomination CDs were down slightly, though they remained above 8% for longer-term maturities, according to an 18-bank survey by Banxquote Money Markets, a New York information service. The average yield on one-year consumer CDs eased to 8.02% from 8.04%, while the average five-year yield declined to 8.23% from 8.25%.

Investors who bought small-denomination CDs through leading brokerage firms did a bit better, as yields rose slightly. Average yields on six-month broker-sold CDs went to 8.32% from 8.22%, while average yields on two-year CDs went to 8.63% from 8.60%, according to figures compiled by Banxquote.

Meanwhile, average yields for large-denomination CDs, which typically require deposits of $90,000 or more, were largely unchanged. Average yields on six-month jumbo CDs fell to 7.91% from 7.93%, while one-year maturities rose to 8.11% from 8.10%.

The only bank in the survey that offered a CD with a yield of at least 9% was Continental Bank in Chicago, which offered a five-year jumbo CD at 9%.

"It was a very uneventful week," said Banxquote Chairman Norberto Mehl. "There wasn't any clear pattern in the direction of interest rates, so CD yields stayed within a narrow range."
