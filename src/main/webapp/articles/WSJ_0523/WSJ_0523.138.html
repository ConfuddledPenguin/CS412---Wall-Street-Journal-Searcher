Philips N.V. Is Sued For Allegedly Issuing Misleading Statements

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/23/90

NEW YORK -- Philips N.V. shareholders filed two lawsuits accusing the company of disclosing misleading information to artificially boost its stock price before the company reported a plunge in earnings May 3. The two class-action lawsuits were filed in federal court in Manhattan. The suits allege that Philips, the Dutch electronics giant, issued a series of false public statements that made it appear that the company's business prospects were promising. The suits claim that at the time of the favorable statements, the company knew that the projections were misleading.

One of the suits claims that the allegedly misleading statements were made as early as October 1989. The other suit claims the company began misrepresenting its business prospects in January 1990. The lawsuits seek unspecified damages for the decline in the stock's value for investors who bought Philips in recent months.

The suits claim that Philips led investors to believe that sales and earnings would increase for 1990. According to the lawsuits, the company was presenting a rosy but misleading picture of its expected earnings as late as mid-April 1990.

The lawsuits say the company made no mention of problems that might hurt its profitability until May 3, when it reported that first-quarter operating profit fell to six million guilders ($3.2 million), down from 223 million guilders a year earlier. On news of the drop in earnings, Philips common declined $2.875 to close at $17.125 in composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange on May 3.

In composite trading on the Big Board yesterday, the stock closed at $17.625, up 12.5 cents.
