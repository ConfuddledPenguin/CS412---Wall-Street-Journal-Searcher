McDonnell Douglas Debt

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/23/90

ST. LOUIS -- McDonnell Douglas Corp. said it renegotiated its principal revolving credit agreements totaling $1.4 billion with 28 U.S. and international banks led by New York-based Chase Manhattan. New terms extend the agreements from 1993 to April 1995. They also "give us additional flexibility to do other kinds of medium- and long-term borrowing restricted by the previous agreements," said a McDonnell Douglas spokesman.

He added that the company has said it needs to borrow about $1 billion before the end of this year, in addition to $2.5 billion of debt that was outstanding as of March 31, to "take care of inventory buildup of MD-11s in production and flight testing."
