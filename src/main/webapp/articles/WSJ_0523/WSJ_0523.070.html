International: EC Commission Lets Renault Keep Half Of Bailout Funds

  • Author: Martin du Bois and E.S. Browning
  • Date: 05/23/90

BRUSSELS -- Renault S.A. won a long-running battle as the European Community Commission allowed the state-owned auto maker to keep half of a 12 billion franc ($2.13 billion) government bailout. The commission said Renault would immediately have to pay back only 3.5 billion francs to the French government, while an additional 2.5 billion francs would be listed on its books as long-term debt. That's a big scale-down from the 8.4 billion franc pay-back originally sought by EC Competition Commissioner Sir Leon Brittan.

Moreover, French government officials indicated that they may compensate for Renault's loss by injecting more money into the company next year in the form of a capital increase.

Paris welcomed the compromise and said that France will feel free to return as much money as it wishes to the state-owned company. "We can do what we want in function of what we think is in Renault's interest and in the interest of its shareholders," French Industry Minister Roger Fauroux said.

As part of the accord with the commission, the French government won't inject any more money into Renault before next year, when the Swedish auto maker AB Volvo is to buy a 20% stake in the company. Volvo is expected to participate in any capital boost -- a move which Sir Leon said would offer a guarantee that the cash injection is a legitimate business investment, and not an illegal state handout.
