Southmark Reports $58.5 Million Loss For Its 3rd Period

  • Author: Karen Blumenthal
  • Date: 05/23/90

DALLAS -- Southmark Corp. said it had a narrowed net loss of $58.5 million, or $1.22 a share, for its fiscal third quarter ended March 31 as problems worsened at its San Jacinto Savings Association subsidiary. In the third quarter last year, Southmark had a net loss of $1.4 billion, or $23.30 a share, after more than $800 million in write-downs. Revenue for the quarter was $268.2 million, down 34% from $405.6 million last year.

The troubled real estate and thrift concern has proposed reorganizing around San Jacinto, but the Houston-based savings and loan's persisent troubles continue to raise questions about whether such a reorganization will be possible.

In a filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission, Southmark said that San Jacinto had a negative net worth of $50.8 million at March 31, compared with an estimated negative net worth of $12.1 million at Dec. 31 based on a regulatory examination. In addition, the thrift failed to meet new capital requirements and said it still hasn't implemented its business plan, which calls for it to sell or otherwise dispose of about $1 billion in assets.

According to Southmark's SEC filing, the thrift had losses of $38 million in the quarter, more than the $10 million a month in losses it had estimated it would have, and $140 million for the nine months.

Under an agreement between Southmark, San Jacinto and the Office of Thrift Supervision, Southmark has agreed to contribute $100 million more in assets to the troubled thrift and to convert $112 million of subordinated debt into equity. But even after that transaction, which must be completed by mid-July, Southmark said that San Jacinto won't meet minimum regulatory capital requirements, increasing the possibility that federal regulators will choose to seize the thrift.

However, Southmark said that San Jacinto has filed an application asking the Office of Thrift Supervision to grant it an exception to regulatory standards or hold off sanctions while it tries to implement its business plan.

For the nine months, Southmark had a net loss of $207 million, or $4.39 a share, compared with a net loss in the year-ago period of $1.13 billion, or $25.76 a share. Revenue was $791.5 million, down from $1.5 billion last year.
