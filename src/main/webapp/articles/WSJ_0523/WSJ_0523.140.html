International Brief -- British Airways PLC: Carrier Reports 29% Rise In Annual Pretax Earnings

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/23/90

British Airways PLC reported a 29% rise in pretax profit for the year ended March 31, though sales rose a more modest 14% and the company warned of increasing pressure from competition and slower growth in some of its major markets. The London-based carrier, which is Britain's biggest airline, produced a pretax profit of #345 million in the latest year, compared with #268 million a year earlier.

Net attributable to shareholders, or profit after taxes and minority interests, increased 41% to #246 million from #175 million. There were no extraordinary items in either period.

Despite the buoyant earnings, British Airways Chairman Lord King sounded a note of caution for the year ahead, during which the airline intends to expand its fleet by 10% and is committed to raising staff wages by 9.5%.

"We are aware that our costs require constant scrutiny, particularly in view of the increasingly competitive nature of the market we serve, and the softening of Western economies," he said.
