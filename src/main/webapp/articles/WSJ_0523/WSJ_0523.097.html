Business Brief -- TCS Enterprises Inc.: Company Expects to Post Substantially Lower Earnings

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/23/90

TCS Enterprises Inc. said it anticipates "substantially" lower earnings for the near future. In a statement, Tom C. Stickel, TCS chairman and chief executive officer, said the San Diego financial services company will report earnings for its fiscal second quarter that ended April 30 in a few weeks. Mr. Stickel couldn't immediately be reached to comment on the reasons for the expected earnings decline.

In 1989's second quarter, TCS had net income of $538,000, or 17 cents a share, on $3.8 million of revenue.

TCS also said it negotiated the purchase of 578,000 of its shares held by the Resolution Trust Corp. for $867,000, or $1.50 a share, and announced plans to buy more of its stock in the open market.

The RTC acquired the TCS shares when it took over Lincoln Savings, the Irvine, Calif., thrift unit of Phoenix, Ariz.-based American Continental Corp. TCS said it had about three million shares outstanding prior to the repurchase.
