Letters to the Editor: Low-Budget Disputes

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/23/90

Your April 13 Law column item "Arbitration Settles a Lot, Unsettles a Few" doesn't reflect accurately the cost-effectiveness of alternative dispute resolution. Arbitration does not favor the well-to-do. On the contrary, it is a far less expensive means of settling a dispute than going through the regular court system. A case that goes to court can cost consumers an average of $500 a day in legal fees, regardless of whether the case is heard that day. This can add up to thousands of dollars before the case is settled.

However, the largest dispute-resolution firms charge a nominal fee of $100 an hour -- an average of $400 a case. Consumers also receive fast results. Most cases are settled within 51 days.

Loretta L. Worters

Insurance Information Institute

New York
