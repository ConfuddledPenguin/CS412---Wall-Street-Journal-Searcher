Bangkok Adopts IMF Rules On Currency Convertibility

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/23/90

WASHINGTON -- The International Monetary Fund said Thailand accepted IMF rules relating to currency convertibility. Bangkok agreed to refrain from imposing restrictions on the use of its currency, the baht, for international payments. It also agreed to refrain from engaging in discriminatory currency arrangements or from using multiple exchange rates for its currency without IMF approval.

By accepting obligations under Article VIII of the IMF charter, "Thailand gives confidence to the international community that it will pursue sound economic policies that will obviate the need to use exchange and payments restrictions on current transactions, and thereby enhances its contribution to a multilateral payments system free of restrictions," the IMF said.

The IMF currently has 152 member countries, including 68 industrial nations and developing countries that have accepted the commitment to maintain convertibility of their currencies in current-account international payment transactions.
