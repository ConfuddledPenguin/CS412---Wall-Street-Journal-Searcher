Business Brief -- Computer Associates: Earnings Climbed by 58% In Fiscal Fourth Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/23/90

Computer Associates International Inc. said net income in the fourth quarter ended March 31 rose 58%, as the Garden City, N.Y., company began to recover from problems in merging with Cullinet Software Inc., acquired in September. Net rose to $66.9 million, or 36 cents a share, from $42.4 million, or 23 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue rose 1.6% to $363.7 million from $357.8 million. The 1989 figures have been restated to reflect the Cullinet acquisition on a pooling-of-interests basis.

Anthony W. Wang, president, said the latest results "show that most of the issues surrounding the acquisition of Cullinet are now behind us."

Computer Associates, the nation's largest independent software company, reported fiscal 1990 earnings rose 21% to $157.8 million, or 85 cents a share, from $130.3 million, or 74 cents a share, in fiscal 1989. Revenue increased 5.2% to $1.3 billion from $1.23 billion.

Computer Associates stock fell 37.5 cents yesterday to close at $15.50 a share in New York Stock Exchange composite trading.
