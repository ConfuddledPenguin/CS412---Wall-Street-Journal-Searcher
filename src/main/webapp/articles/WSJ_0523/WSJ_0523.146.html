Southmark Urged to Try To Recover $2.2 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/23/90

DALLAS -- Southmark Corp. should try to recover $2.2 million paid to two former thrift executives in 1988, apparently to settle disputes between the executives and Southmark and its top officials, a court-appointed examiner has concluded. In documents unsealed yesterday, examiner Neal Batson concluded that Southmark made the payment in August and September 1988 to entities controlled by former Sunbelt Savings Association Chairman Edwin T. McBirney III and former Independent American Savings Association Chairman Thomas M. Gaubert, ostensibly to settle an unfiled suit over a soured real-estate investment

But Mr. Batson also noted that terms of the settlement were kept confidential, even from Southmark's board, and questioned whether the payment also was meant to settle "substantial other unspoken matters" between Messrs. McBirney and Gaubert and Southmark's top executives, Gene E. Phillips and William S. Friedman.

Mr. McBirney said he wasn't familiar with the examiner's inquiry and couldn't comment. Neither Mr. Greenberg nor Mr. Gaubert could be reached for comment.

Separately, Mr. Batson concluded that Southmark should challenge I.C.H. Corp.'s claim as a senior creditor holding $50 million in 17.4% senior notes. Under previous management, Southmark gave I.C.H. the notes in March 1988 in exchange for $50 million in Southmark preferred stock, at a time when Southmark was insolvent, Mr. Batson said. He concluded that the transaction was unfair to other Southmark creditors.
