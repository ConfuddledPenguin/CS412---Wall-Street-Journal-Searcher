New Securities Issues

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/23/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATES

Great Western Bank -- $130 million of 10 1/2% subordinated notes, due May 30, 2000, priced at 99.625 to yield 10.562%. The notes are noncallable for 8 3/4 years and are then callable at par for the last 15 months. Rated Baa-1 by Moody's and single-A-minus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets.

MUNICIPALS

Connecticut -- $250 million special tax general obligation transportation infrastructure bonds, due June 1, 1992-2004, 2007 and 2010, tentatively priced by a Bear Stearns & Co. group to yield from 6.10% in 1992 to 7.23% in 2007. Current interest serial bonds are priced to yield from 6.10% in 1992 to 7.10% in 2004. There are $51.91 million of 7 7/8% term bonds priced at 99 to yield 7.23% in 2007. There are $63.81 million of 7 1/8% term bonds priced at par in 2010. The bonds are rated single-A-1 by Moody's and double-A-minus by S&P.

Louisiana Offshore Terminal Authority -- $102.2 million of deep water port refunding revenue bonds, Loop Inc. Project, due Sept. 1, 1993-2003, and 2010, tentatively priced by a First Boston Corp. group to yield from 6.60% in 1993 to 7.63% in 2010. Current interest serial bonds are priced at par to yield from 6.60% in 1993 to 7.40% in 2003. There are $49.27 million of 7 5/8% term bonds priced at par in 2010. The bonds are rated single-A-3 by Moody's and single-A by S&P.

Passaic Valley Sewerage Commissioners, New Jersey -- $86.5 million sewer system revenue bonds, Series C, due Dec. 1, 1991-2006, 2010 and 2020, apparently won by a First Boston Corp. group. The bonds, AMBAC-insured and rated triple-A by both Moody's and S&P, priced to yield from 6% 1991 to 7.26% in 2020. Current interest serial bonds are priced to yield from 6% in 1991 to 7.15% in 2005 and 2006. There are $11.9 million of 7.10% term bonds priced at 98 5/8 to yield 7.23% in 2010. There are $49.6 million of 7.10% term bonds priced at 98 to yield 7.26% in 2020.

Harris County Health Facility Authority, Texas -- $65 million special facility revenue bonds, Texas Medical Center Project, due May 15, 1996-2000, 2005 and 2020, tentatively priced by a First Boston Corp. group to yield from 6.6% in 1996 to 7.42% in 2020. Current interest serial bonds are priced to yield from 6.6% in 1996 to 7% in 2000. There are $8 million of 7 1/4% term bonds priced at 99 1/2 to yield 7.31% in 2005. There are $51 million of 7 3/8% term bonds priced at 99 1/2 to yield 7.42% in 2020. The bonds are MBIA-insured and rated triple-A by both Moody's and S&P.

Suffolk County, N.Y. -- $56.42 million public improvement revenue bonds, Series B, portion of a $80.07 million revenue bond and 12-month capital notes issue was won by a Citibank Securities Markets Inc. group. The bonds, due April 1 and May 1, 1991-2020, were priced for reoffering to yield from 6.10% in 1991 to 7.25% in 2013-2020. There are no term bonds. A Chemical Securities Inc. group won the balance of the $80.07 million which consists of $21.28 million public improvement bonds, Series A, due April 1 and May 1, 19912015, and 12-month capital notes, 1990 Issue, due May 31, 1991. Series A consists of current interest serial bonds priced to yield from 6% in 1991 to 7.15% in 2011-2015. The capital notes are priced as 6.6% securities to yield 6.4%. The issue is MBIA-insured and rated triple-A by both Moody's and S&P.

MORTGAGE-BACKED

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $300 million issue of Remic mortgage securities, offered in 13 classes by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets. The offering, Series 1990-71, and backed by Fannie Mae 8 1/2% securities, with a weighted average coupon rate of 9.25% and weighted average remaining term to maturity of 26.33 years. The yields, where available, range from 9.32% to 9.91%.

EUROBONDS

Standard Credit Card Trust 1990-5 (vehicle) -- $1.41 billion of global, registered, credit card-backed bonds due June 10, 1995, via Salomon Brothers International. Portion A consists of $1.25 billion of 9 3/8% bonds priced at 99.66 to yield 9.46% semi-annually, a yield spread of 80 basis points above the current five-year U.S. Treasury note. Portion B consists of $155 million of 9 5/8% bonds priced at 99.47 to yield 9.76% semi-annually , a yield spread of 110 basis points above the same Treasury issue. Settlement through Euroclear, Cedel, and Depository Trust Co.

Unilever N.V. (Netherlands) -- 500 million Dutch guilders of 9% bonds due July 2, 2000, priced at 100.30 to yield 8.95%, via AmRo Bank.

Caisse Centrale de Cooperation Economique (France) -- 150 million Swiss francs of 7 1/4% public bonds due June 22, 2005, priced at 101 3/4, via Union Bank of Switzerland. Guarantee by the French Republic. Callable from June 22, 2000 at 101, declining by 1/2 point per year.

European Investment Bank (agency) -- 200 million Swiss francs of 7 1/4% public bonds due June 26, 2000, priced at 102, via Union Bank of Switzerland.
