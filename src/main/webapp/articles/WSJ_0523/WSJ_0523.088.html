Tuesday's Markets: Bonds Rally To Give Stocks Lift --- Rumors of Funds To Reduce Deficit Prompt Price Runup

  • Author: Christopher Winans
  • Date: 05/23/90

NEW YORK -- A powerful rally in bonds helped lift stock prices that were sagging under profit-taking. The Dow Jones Industrial closed 7.55 points higher at a record 2852.23 on heavy volume. Long-term U.S. Treasury bond prices rose nearly three-quarters of a point, or $7.50 for each $1,000 of face amount, also in active trading. The dollar was mixed, rising against the West German mark as traders reassessed the eventual cost of German reunification.

Analysts said bond prices rose sharply early in the day on rumors that $50 billion in unspent congressional appropriations might be available to trim the deficit. The rally got another boost from buying by Mideastern and Japanese investors who invested proceeds from maturing Japanese government bonds in U.S. Treasurys.

The buoyancy in bonds sent stock prices shooting up at the opening bell, but profittaking on the runup killed most of the gain and left stocks trading in a narrow range the rest of the day. A last-minute rally pushed the blue chips to gains.

"The linkage between interest rates and stocks is powerful," said Byron Wien, U.S. investment strategist at Morgan Stanley. "When the long bond went through 9% {a few weeks ago}, the bears got confident about their bearishness. Then rates came back down. That, plus positive talk about the {reducing the} budget {deficit}, started scaring the bears."

Jon Groveman, president of investment bankers Ladenburg, Thalmann & Co., attributed the early stock surge to "a very emotional, desperate opening on the part of investors who'd been out of the market and were feeling left behind."

Besides profit-taking, which many analysts termed healthy, stocks were held in check by declines in the Dow Jones transportation and utilities average. Also, Kenneth Spence, director of Salomon Brothers' technical analysis group, said there is resistance in Standard & Poor's 500-stock index at the 360 level; it closed yesterday at 358.43, up 0.43.

"The market is on cruise control, and that's where it'll stay until a bullet comes along," James D. Wright, chief investment officer at Banc One Asset Management, Columbus, Ohio. "When the bullet arrives, it'll knock it down hard."

In major market action:

Stock prices rose in heavy trading. Volume surged to 203.4 million shares on the New York Stock Exchange, where advancing issues outnumbered decliners 816 to 689.

Bond prices jumped sharply. The yield on the Treasury's benchmark 30-year bond fell to 8.61%.

The dollar was mixed. In late New York trading, the currency was quoted at 1.6736 marks and 151.20 Japanese yen, compared with 1.6626 marks and 153.72 yen Monday.
