Business Brief -- Caesars World Inc.: Fiscal 3rd-Period Earnings Fell 40% to $10.5 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/23/90

Caesars World Inc., Los Angeles, reported that earnings for its third quarter ended April 30 declined 40% to $10.5 million, or 44 cents a share, from $17.4 million, or 70 cents a share, a year earlier. Most of the decline came from Caesars' Nevada casinos, particularly from low table-game win percentages at the Lake Tahoe property and construction disruptions elsewhere. Caesars' results, somewhat better than analysts had expected, prompted a 75 cent rise, to $21.125, in New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday. Caesars faces major new competitors in Atlantic City, N.J., and Las Vegas, Nev., which have been affecting its business in the past few quarters.

Revenue fell 1.7% to $217.9 million from $221.8 million.

For the fiscal first nine months, Caesars' net income fell 42% to $29.4 million, or $1.20 a share, from $50.5 million, or $2.05 a share, a year earlier. Revenue declined 2.9% to $646.6 million from $666.4 million.

Caesars also said it has bought back slightly more than one million shares this year under a three million-share buy-back authorization. It has about 24 million common shares outstanding.
