Who's News: General Development Names Two Directors, Four Others Resign

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/13/90

MIAMI -- General Development Corp. named two new directors, and four board members resigned. The two additions to the real estate developer's board are Todd G. Cole, 69 years old, vice chairman of Eastern Airlines, and James W. Apthorp, 52, executive vice president of Good Gulfstream Holding Corp.

The four directors who resigned are Reubin Askew, Howard L. Clark Jr., Edwin I. Hatch and Eben W. Payne. General Development's chairman and chief executive officer, Charles J. Simons, said each of the four departing directors "felt sufficient progress had been made in resolving the company's legal issues" and wanted to see "new people with fresh viewpoints" on the board.

In April, General Development filed for protection from creditors' lawsuits under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code, while it forms a plan to repay its debts. Earlier in the year, the company pleaded guilty to a federal charge of conspiracy to defraud about 10,000 home buyers, by charging inflated prices based on improper appraisals.

The changes in the board leave General Development with nine directors, down from 11.

Separately, General Development said the board voted to postpone until later this year the company's annual meeting, which had been scheduled for Sept. 28; a new date wasn't specified.
