Technology Brief -- Apple Computer Inc.: Concern Will Let Bitstream Sell Its Font Technology

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/13/90

Apple Computer Inc., Cupertino, Calif., lobbed another volley in the font wars by agreeing to allow Bitstream Inc. to sell Apple's font technology, programs that determine how computers represent text on computer screens and printers. Apple is trying to win converts to its font software from Adobe Systems Inc., the leader of the market for font software, which is essential in desktop publishing. Apple, one of Adobe's biggest customers, caused a stir in the personal-computer world last year when it and Microsoft Corp. announced they would offer a product rivaling Adobe's Postscript font software.

Apple's agreement with Bitstream will allow Bitstream to license Apple's font product, called TrueType, to hundreds manufacturers and software developers. Bitstream, a Cambridge, Mass., software concern, now licenses fonts to more than 370 companies around the world. Apple said it hopes to "extend the reach" of TrueType in the personal-computer market.
