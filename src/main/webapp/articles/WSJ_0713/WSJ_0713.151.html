Abitibi Recycling Mill Planned in Scotland With Two Publishers

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/13/90

TORONTO -- Abitibi-Price Inc., a forest products company, said it plans to build a 400 million Canadian dollar (US$345.2 million) recycled-newsprint mill in Scotland, in partnership with two British newspaper publishing concerns. Abitibi, Daily Telegraph PLC and Mirror Group Newspapers Ltd. agreed to acquire closely held Northern British Newsprint Ltd., as the first stage in construction of the mill. The acquisition price wasn't specified. Daily Telegraph is 77%-owned by Conrad Black's Hollinger Inc., and Mirror Group is controlled by Robert Maxwell. Officials at the two companies couldn't be reached for comment.

Abitibi will have a 50% stake in the company, which developed the project and owns the site of the proposed mill. The two newspaper concerns will each have a 25% stake in the project, Abitibi said.

The mill will convert about 270,000 metric tons of newspapers and magazines a year into 220,000 metric tons of 100% recycled-fiber newsprint, Abitibi said. A metric ton is equal to about 2,204.62 pounds. Abititi said construction could begin in early 1991 and be complete within two years, with 80% of the cost being raised through external financing arrangements, including debt issues.
