Cuomo Approves Bill Restricting Chemicals That Deplete Ozone

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/13/90

ALBANY, N.Y. (AP) -- Gov. Mario Cuomo signed legislation designed to restrict the use and sale of ozone-depleting chemicals that are widely used in refrigeration systems, air conditioners and fire extinguishers. The new law restricts and, in some cases, bans the sale of chlorofluorocarbons and halons. Both have been found to contribute to the depletion of the earth's ozone layer and to add to global warming. The ozone layer protects the earth from dangerous ultraviolet rays of the sun that can cause skin cancer, eye cataracts and other problems.

Chlorofluorocarbons are coolants that are commonly used in refrigeration systems and air conditioners. Under the new law, CFCs will have to be recaptured and recycled when large-scale refrigeration systems and mobile air conditioners are serviced. CFCs will also have to be recaptured when equipment that uses them is disposed of.

In addition, the new law bans the sale of residential fire extinguishers that use halons and the sale of CFCs or coolants in 14-ounce cans. The restrictions will be phased in during the next two years.

Gov. Cuomo said that, while the new law was a good measure, it does not go far enough toward eliminating the use of the chemicals -- a move that would be much better for the environment.
