Japan's Crude Steel Production

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/13/90

TOKYO -- Production of crude steel in Japan in June edged up 0.2% from a year earlier to 8.98 million metric tons but fell 3.8% from 9.33 million tons in the previous month, the Japan Iron and Steel Federation said. A metric ton is equal to about 2,204.62 pounds. "Japanese steel manufacturers are in a full operation" to meet continuing robust domestic demand, especially from the construction sector, a federation official said. Steel demand for non-residential building construction, such as office buildings and plants, has been growing, he said, and as public works usually begin to hit their stride in the July-September quarter, steel demand is expected to remain high.
