Big Board Program Trades Were 14% of June Volume

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/13/90

NEW YORK -- June was one of the busiest months for program trading since the New York Stock Exchange started releasing reports on the computer-aided trading activity two years ago. Program trading in June accounted for about 14% of Big Board volume, or 21.4 million shares daily, according to exchange data.

In contrast, program trading accounted for an average of only 9.9% of exchange volume in 1989 and 10.7% in the last half of 1988. Program trading has been controversial because of the market gyrations associated with some of its forms, particularly stock-index arbitrage.

For the week of June 25-29, program trading accounted for 13.7% of Big Board daily volume, the exchange said. That is a high figure for a week that doesn't include program trading associated with the quarterly expiration of stock-index futures and options.

The most active program traders for the week were Morgan Stanley Group Inc. (22.4 million shares), General Electric Co.'s Kidder, Peabody & Co. unit (15.3 million), Goldman, Sachs & Co. (11.9 million), Salomon Brothers Inc. (8.9 million) and Susquehanna Investment Group (8.4 million). These five firms accounted for 70% of Big Board program activity for the week.

About 77% of all stock-index arbitrage activity was done by Morgan, Kidder and Susquehanna.

Program trading is the nearly simultaneous purchase or sale of 15 or more, and often hundreds, of stocks. In index arbitrage, stocks are bought and sold with offsetting trades of futures and options to profit from price discrepancies in different markets.
