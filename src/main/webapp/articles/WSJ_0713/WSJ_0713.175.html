New Securities Issues

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/13/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATE

Ford Holdings Co. -- $552 million of 9 1/4% notes, due July 15, 1997, was priced at 99.871 to yield 9.275%. The noncallable notes were priced at a spread of 0.79 percentage point over the Treasury's seven-year note. Rated double-A-2 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and double-A-minus by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Goldman, Sachs & Co.

General Motors Acceptance Corp. -- $200 million of 8 3/4% notes due July 15, 2005, priced at 100.05 to yield 8.734%. In 1994 and 1997, the investor has the option to put the securities back to the company at par. The notes were priced at a spread of 0.33 percentage point above the Treasury's four-year note. Rated double-A-3 by Moody's and double-A-minus by S&P, the issue will be sold solely by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets.

ITT Financial Corp. -- $150 million of subordinated notes, due July 15, 2005, was priced at par to yield 8.85%. In 1993 and 1997, the bondholder has the option to put the securities back to the company at par. The noncallable notes were priced at a spread of 0.56 percentage point over the Treasury's three-year note. Rated single-A-2 by Moody's and single-A by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets.

DeKalb Energy Co. -- $75 million of 9 7/8% notes due July 15, 2000, priced at 99.84 to yield 9.90%. The noncallable notes were priced at a spread of 1.30 percentage points above the Treasury's 10-year note. Rated Baa-1 by Moody's and triple-B by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets.

Harley-Davidson Inc. -- $50 million of convertible debentures, due June 30, 2015, was priced at par to yield 7.25%. The debentures are convertible into common stock at $39.68 a share, representing a 18% conversion premium over the closing price. Rated Ba-3 by Moody's and single-B by S&P, the issue will be sold solely by Dean Witter Reynolds Inc.

MORTGAGE

Travelers Mortgage Services Inc. -- $93 million of real estate mortgage investment conduit securities is being offered in nine classes by Salomon Brothers Inc. The offering, Series 1990-10, is backed by 30-year fixed-rate whole loans with a weighted average coupon rate of about 10.8755% and weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29.92 years. The issue is rated double-A-2 by Moody's and double-a by S&P.

OTHER

Province of Nova Scotia -- $300 million of 9 3/8% notes, due July 15, 2002, was priced at 99.360 to yield 9.465%. The noncallable notes were priced at a spread of 0.95 percentage point above the Treasury's 10-year note. Rated single-A-2 by Moody's and single-A-minus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets.

EUROBONDS

Sumitomo Bank International Finance N.V. (Japanese parent) -- $700 million of 10-year floating-rate notes at par, via Sumitomo Finance International. Maturity August 2000. Coupon floats 0.25 point above three-month London interbank offered rate for years one to five, then at Libor plus 0.35 percentage point for the next five years. Callable after five years at par. Guaranteed on a subordinated basis by Sumitomo Bank Ltd.

Orient Corp. (Japan) -- $300 million of bonds due Aug. 2, 1994, with equity-purchase warrants, with 4 3/4% coupon at par, via Nomura International Ltd. Each $5,000 bond carries one warrant, exercisable from Aug. 16, 1990, through July 22, 1994, to buy shares at an expected premium of 2 1/2% to the closing price when terms are fixed Wednesday.

Swedish National Mortgage Corp. (Sweden) -- $200 million of fungible bonds due May 30, 1995, priced at 100.675 via sole manager Nomura International Ltd. Fungible from the payment date with a $300 million deal launched May 14.

Credit Foncier de France (France) -- 25 billion yen ($169.7 million) of 7 1/8% bonds due Aug. 29, 1995, priced at 101 5/8 to yield 7.19% less full fees, via Yamaichi International Ltd. Guaranteed by the republic of France. Fees 1 7/8.

Instituto de Credito Official (Spain) -- 20 billion yen of floating-rate notes due July 31, 2000, priced at par via IBJ International Ltd. Coupon will float at 0.50 point below the Japanese long-term prime rate. Coupon fixed semiannually. Callable July 31, 1995, and every six months thereafter. Statutory guarantee by the kingdom of Spain. Fees 0.30.

Skandinaviska Enskilda Banken (Sweden) -- 200 million marks ($122.4 million) of 9% bonds due Aug. 10, 2000, priced at 102 to yield 8.69%, via Deutsche Bank.

GMAC Australia Finance Ltd. (U.S. parent) -- 75 million Australian dollars (US$59.8 million) of 14 3/4% bonds due Aug. 23, 1994, priced at 101.65 to yield 14.78% less fees via Hambros Bank Ltd. Guaranteed by General Motors Acceptance Corp. Fees 1 3/4.

Australian Gas & Lighting Co. (Australia) -- 50 million Australian dollars of 15.375% bonds due Aug. 31, 1993, priced at 101.95 to yield 15.18% less fees via Westpac Banking Corp. Fees 1 1/2.

Banque Generale du Luxembourg S.A. (Luxembourg) -- 50 million Australian dollars of 20% dual-currency bonds due Aug. 14, 1991, priced at 101.75, via Bankers Trust International. Redemption is optional in Australian or Canadian dollars at a ratio of 87 Canadian cents for each Australian dollar.

Dresdner Finance B.V. Amsterdam (German parent) -- 150 million European currency units of 10 1/8% bonds due Aug. 9, 1995, priced at 101.60 via Dresdner Bank. Guaranteed by Dresdner Bank AG Frankfurt.
