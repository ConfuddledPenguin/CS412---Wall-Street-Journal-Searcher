Australian Rate Easing Is Unlikely After Report

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/13/90

SYDNEY, Australia -- The unexpected rise in Australian employment in June reduces the chance that monetary policy will be eased before late August when the federal government's budget is reported, economists say. But the latest labor force report notwithstanding, there is still opportunity for a further reduction in rates this year, they maintain.

"This {employment} number just reduces the likelihood of an easing" in the short run. But "we still think the economy is quite weak and we will get an ease" after the budget is reported Aug. 21, that will take interest rates down to around 14%, says Jeff Schubert, chief economist for HongKong Bank of Australia Ltd. The unofficial overnight bank lending rate is currently just under 15%.

The Australian Bureau of Statistics announced that the number of persons employed in June rose 3,000 on a seasonally adjusted basis. Market participants generally had been looking for a decline of about 20,000, and some had expected a greater fall. A rise in employment wasn't expected.

The bureau also reported that the nation's unemployment rate rose to an adjusted 6.6% in June, in line with expectations.
