Employment Firm's Ex-Chief Is Indicted On Fraud Charges

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/13/90

DALLAS -- The former chief executive officer of Diversified Human Resources Group Inc. was indicted on charges of bank fraud and money laundering in connection with a loan from a now-defunct Texas bank. William M. Brothers, who also served as president of the employment-services concern until he resigned March 31, is accused of submitting fraudulent corporate resolutions to the First State Bank of Rockwall, Texas, in order to obtain a personal loan of $385,000 in 1987.

The allegedly fraudulent resolutions purportedly stated that Diversified Human Resources would guarantee as much as $210,000 of the loan to Mr. Brothers. The Federal Deposit Insurance Corp., which took over the Rockwall bank, sued the company to collect on the guarantee but the suit was later dismissed, according to the company's 1989 annual report.

The seven-count indictment, returned by a federal grand jury in Dallas, specifically charges Mr. Brothers with conspiracy, two counts of laundering the borrowed funds and bank robbery. If convicted of all counts, Mr. Brothers would face a maximum possible sentence of 60 years in federal prison and a fine of $1.75 million.

Mr. Brothers couldn't be reached for comment. Gary Steeds, president and chief executive officer of Diversified Human Resources, said the company wouldn't have any comment on the indictment. "That's really a personal matter between him and the government, and it doesn't affect the company," Mr. Steeds said.
