Who's News: Medical Journal's Relman Plans to Retire Next Year

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/13/90

WALTHAM, Mass. -- Arnold S. Relman, editor in chief of the New England Journal of Medicine, announced plans to retire from the post next June. Dr. Relman, 67 years old, was named editor of the medical journal in 1977 and assumed his current post in 1988. In announcing his decision, he said he plans to devote himself "more to writing and teaching, and to participate more fully in public policy debates on American health care." Dr. Relman is also a part-time professor at Harvard Medical School.

The New England Journal of Medicine is published by the Massachusetts Medical Society. As the journal's chief, Dr. Relman is one of the nation's most prominent doctors. His views have helped shape medical opinions and policies at a time when the health-care system has undergone rapid changes.

During his tenure, the journal has emphasized issues of broad medical and scientific interest. Among other things, it published articles that helped identify and explain acquired immune deficiency syndrome, toxic shock syndrome and Legionnaires' disease.
