World Markets: Tokyo Stocks Advance in Light Volume; Nikkei Fluctuates in Index-Linked Trade

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 07/13/90

Tokyo stocks finished higher in light and non-focused trading, with the Nikkei Index fluctuating largely because of index-linked trading, observers said. In London, stocks ended moderate activity with prices just off the day's lows. Traders said sentiment was mixed with the pound's decline providing support but economic fundamentals still overshadowing the market.

Tokyo's market lacked investors' enthusiasm as interest rates in the bond market showed little sign of declining, despite the yen's continued strength against the dollar, traders said.

The Nikkei Index of 225 issues gained 281.14 points to 32575.32 Thursday, finishing just below the intraday high. In early trading in Tokyo Friday, the index advanced another 182.66 points to 32757.98.

Thursday's volume on the first section was estimated at 430 million shares, up from 386.9 million shares Wednesday.

Advancing issues outnumbered declining issues 503 to 421, while 197 issues remained unchanged. The Tokyo Stock Price Index of all issues in the first section rose 2.67 points, or 0.11%, to 2354.24.

The second section index was up 11.97 points, or 0.27%, to 4418.71. Volume was estimated at 35 million shares, down from 39.7 million shares Wednesday.

The Nikkei Index, which climbed above 32500 in the midmorning, declined as low as 32266 late in the afternoon as bond prices softened, traders said.

The index jumped about 300 points during the last 30 minutes of trading. But traders attributed the advance to buying linked to the July options trading that expired yesterday.

"The gain was solely technical. The sentiment wasn't strong," said Takashi Hayano, a futures and options trader at Dai-Ichi Securities.

Large-capital shares that firmed Wednesday on the yen's advance fell back as investors took profits, judging that the strengthening of the yen is unlikely to pull down interest rates, traders said.

Shipbuilders, electric utilities and largesize construction companies ended lower.

Investors went after the shares of companies that are considered likely to maintain strong earnings prospects regardless of changes in exchange rates and interest rates, Mr. Murai said.

High-tech shares advanced briskly because of strong earnings outlooks.

Sony was up 100 yen to 8800 yen (67 cents to $59.36). Kyocera soared 220 yen to 9000 yen. TDK gained 90 yen to 7440 yen, while Fanucrose 90 yen to 7550 yen.

In London, sterling's decline in early trading provided encouragement for the overseas earners. But not all of the stocks in the sector benefited from the pound's easing, as some came under pressure from profit-takers after Wednesday's rally on a sharply lower pound.

Wall Street's firm performance provided considerable support late in London's trading day.

The Financial Times-Stock Exchange 100 Share Index ended at 2370.5, up 10 points, after gaining as much as 25.8 points. The Financial Times 30 Share Index fell 7.3 points to 1870.4.

Volume rose modestly, with 460.6 million issues traded compared with 449.1 million shares Wednesday.

One of the main corporate highlights was Hawker Siddeley issuing a profit warning for 1990, saying its earnings "will fall short" of 1989 figures. The electrical and mechanical engineering concern had 1989 pretax profit of #202.1 million ($364 million) on sales of #2.15 billion. The stock fell 62 pence to 548 pence.

One dealer said one reason for the stock's decline was the placement of a chunk of shares by broker Cazenove.

Rank Organisation eased 48 pence to 789 pence. The communications and entertainment concern said pretax profit for the six months ended May 12 increased 24%, though the company forecast growing difficulties stemming from poor economic conditions in the U.K. and the U.S.

Great Universal Stores gained 20 pence to #10.63, as investors bought stock ahead of the company's results, widely anticipated to be disclosed next week.

Elsewhere, stocks closed higher in Paris, Zurich, Brussels, Milan, Amsterdam, Stockholm and Madrid. Frankfurt closed mixed. South African gold stocks closed lower.

And shares ended higher in Sydney, Hong Kong, Singapore and Wellington, and lower in Manila, Seoul, and Taipei.
