Stone Container Corp. Lines Up $400 Million In Standby Financing

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/13/90

CHICAGO -- Stone Container Corp. said banks are willing to lend it an additional $400 million, an amount that could help it ride out an earnings slump and repay other debt coming due in September. Investors appeared to welcome Stone's announcement of additional financing. The paper and packaging company's stock closed at $17, up 87.5 cents, in New York Stock Exchange composite trading.

Stone grew rapidly over the past five years, mostly by making acquisitions with borrowed money. After its biggest and most recent purchase, the $2.2 billion takeover of Consolidated-Bathurst Inc. in 1989, Stone emerged as the second-largest paper company in North America.

But Stone's business outlook has weakened this year amid a general downturn in the paper industry, and the company reported a 58% drop in first-quarter earnings. In recent weeks, Wall Street analysts have been cutting their earnings estimates for Stone "pretty severely," says Robert Schifini, a researcher at Institutional/Brokers/Estimate/System.

Arnold Brookstone, Stone's chief financial officer, said the new $400 million standby credit facility "isn't an expression of any inability to make our payments. We are a very conservative company. We believe in planning for contingencies before anything happens."

In recent weeks, attention has focused on Stone's scheduled repayment of its $2.6 billion in senior long-term debt, much of which was taken on to pay for the Consolidated-Bathurst acquisition. Although Stone has been able to make interest payments with ease, it also is obligated in September to repay $175 million of its bank debt, with more repayments to follow.

The new credit facility, Mr. Brookstone said, provides an additional possible source of cash to make its $175 million debt repayment this year. He said the repayments could also be made from operating cash flow or by temporarily borrowing more money under a bank revolving-credit facility.
