If This Show Isn't Another Bomb, They'll Do `Back to Grenada' Next

  • Author: Bill Richards Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 07/13/90

TACOMA, Wash. -- Every action epic has a sequel these days, so it should be no surprise that the Air Force is producing "Panama II." Military officials at McChord AFB here thought they had a swell idea to help celebrate the military airfield's 50th anniversary. "The most spectacular event scheduled," trumpeted a McChord news release last month, "will be a joint services re-enactment of the invasion of Panama. The actual aircraft, aircrews and soldiers who were there participating]"

But as with the original, there have been problems with the sequel.

Local newspapers began calling the exercise, scheduled for this Sunday, a "pseudo invasion." And there were also some troublesome details: Who would play Gen. Manuel Noriega? How would several hundred Panamanians be tastefully done away with?

To simplify matters, Air Force officials decided to leave Panamanians out of the new invasion altogether, and McChord's public-relations staff was ordered to stop mentioning the original locale.

"Our guys will parachute in, deploy on the ground and then walk into the crowd and shake a few hands," says Maj. Morris Bedard, the Air Force impresario who dreamed up the concept of a second Panamanian go-around. "They're calling me Cecil B. de Mille the second," he boasts. The idea behind the concept: "To let the taxpayers see what it is that we do around here."

With or without Gen. Noriega and his countrymen, the re-enactment will have plenty of action. Hundreds of paratroopers will drop onto the base runway in front of thousands of expected onlookers. "The real spectacular part will be the strafing runs by the fighter planes," Maj. Bedard says. The planes are scheduled to swoop in over the crowd and blast away at the nonexistent Panamanian enemy.

Reminded that when the Air Force dispatched a Stealth fighter to Panama to do just that during the real thing, the plane missed its target, Maj. Bedard says: "We won't have any Stealths." Besides, he says, the fighters will be shooting blanks.

Without ammunition, a location or half the original cast, can the Air Force's sequel live up to the original? Maj. Bedard dismisses the question with a de Mille-like shrug. "I wasn't there," he says. "I came up with the concept. It was up to the flyboys to restage the thing right."
