Canadian Housing Starts Fall

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/13/90

OTTAWA -- Canadian housing starts declined 7.3% in June to a seasonally adjusted annual rate of 190,000, which was their lowest level in more than four years and down from a May rate of 205,000, Canada Mortgage and Housing Corp., a federal agency, said. The decline in new housing construction is expected to continue in coming months, said Mark Burchinshaw, the agency's chief economist. He attributed the drop in starts to slower Canadian economic growth, higher interest rates and reduced consumer confidence.
