Bouygues Wins Thai Contract

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/13/90

PARIS -- Bouygues S.A. said it won a contract valued at one billion francs ($179.9 million) to build eight factories in Thailand for a local developer. The public works and construction group said the factories are part of the first phase in the construction of a town called Muang Thong Thani between Bangkok and its airport.

The contract was awarded by Bangkok Land, which is investing the equivalent of 1.7 billion francs in the project. Bouygues will be responsible for designing and building the eight facilities, which are to house light industries.

Bouygues said the project will be financed by Credit Lyonnais, Hongkong & Shanghai Bank and Bangkok Bank.
