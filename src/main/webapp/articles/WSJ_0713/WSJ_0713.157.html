Business Brief -- Quaker Oats Co.: Fisher-Price Unit to Close Facility in Holland, N.Y.

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/13/90

Fisher-Price, a unit Quaker Oats Co., said it plans to close its Holland, N.Y., manufacturing plant, idling 475 employees at the facility. Fisher-Price, the troubled toy business of the Chicago-based packaged foods concern, also said it has eliminated 245 jobs at its headquarters.

Fisher-Price said the plant closing, which will occur gradually between September and November, was necessary because of excess capacity throughout its manufacturing system and the continued decline in sales volume. The plant makes a variety of plastic toys.

The reduction in the headquarters staff was to "align the size of our work force with the smaller size of our business," Fisher-Price said.

Fisher-Price said it will maintain a staff of 950 employees at its headquarters in East Aurora, N.Y. Following the work force reductions, the company said its total staff will be about 6,400.

A spokesman for Quaker Oats said a charge will be taken in connection with the closing. He added that "we will disclose all write-downs associated with the {planned} Fisher-Price spinoff when we announce year-end results for fiscal 1990." As reported, Quaker Oats's board announced in April a plan to spin off the Fisher-Price division to stockholders.
