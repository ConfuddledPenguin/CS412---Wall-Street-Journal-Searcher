Emigrant Savings Files To Raise Its Holdings In Greater New York

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/13/90

NEW YORK -- Emigrant Savings Bank filed with the Federal Deposit Insurance Corp. for permission to raise its 9.9% stake in Greater New York Savings Bank to not more than 25% and for a change of control in Greater New York. The filing said Emigrant is considering ways to increase Greater New York's share price. But it added that neither Emigrant nor its owners, Seymour and Paul Milstein, have any current plans that would result in a merger, reorganization or liquidation of Greater New York, nor any plans to change the board of directors or the management of the company.

A spokesman for Greater New York said it is studying the regulatory filing and will make "an appropriate response" to regulatory authorities. Officials of Emigrant didn't return telephone calls.

In January 1989, Greater New York rejected an offer by Emigrant to purchase the 90.1% of the stock it didn't own for $16 a share, or about $182 million. At the time, Greater New York officials said the offer "represents a thinly disguised attempt by Emigrant to have its 9.9% position in our stock bought out after having put us into play. We won't be manipulated and expect that the market won't be fooled."

Since then, Greater New York has adopted a shareholder rights plan and a leveraged employee stock ownership plan, both aimed at deterring hostile takeover bids.

In national over-the-counter trading yesterday, Greater New York closed at $6.25, up $1.125.
