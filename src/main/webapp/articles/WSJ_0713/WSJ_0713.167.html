South Korea's Auto Exports

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/13/90

SEOUL, South Korea -- South Korea's car exports in June increased 120% to 27,240 from May but declined 2.2% from a year earlier, the Trade and Industry Ministry said. Officials said the increase from the previous month owed largely to a lack of labor strikes at auto plants. In May, strikes were under way at Hyundai Motor Co.'s plant in Ulsan, southeast of Seoul, and at other auto makers. Hyundai's shipment to the U.S. of a new subcompact model, Scoupe, also contributed to the June increase in exports. Hyundai is the largest of South Korea's five auto makers.

In the first six months, exports of South Korean-made vehicles fell 32% to 125,282, compared with a year earlier; most of the shipments were said to be cars.
