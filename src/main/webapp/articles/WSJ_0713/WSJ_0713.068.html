What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/13/90

THE FED IS POISED to ease interest rates modestly in response to growing evidence of a credit crunch, Greenspan said. The Fed chief also said the agency is working to boost capital rules for banks and to toughen their enforcement.

Stocks and bonds rallied on Greenspan's statement on interest rates. The Dow Jones Industrial Average jumped 37.13 points to close at 2969.80, a record. The dollar declined.

---

GTE and Contel agreed to merge in a $6.2 billion stock swap that would create the biggest local-exchange phone company in the U.S. and combine two large cellular units. Contel's stock soared, but GTE shares slipped.

---

GOP budget negotiators have discussed giving up on a capital-gains tax cut if Democrats stop seeking higher income taxes for the rich.

---

Saudi Arabia accepted a temporary reduction in its share of OPEC output after winning a pledge from other exporters to freeze production. Oil prices surged, as did oil stocks.

---

Motel 6 agreed to be acquired by Accor for nearly $1.3 billion. The deal will make the French buyer one of the world's largest lodging companies.

---

Castle & Cooke may sell Dole Food rather than spin it off. Several companies are interested in the unit.

---

Computer Associates stock plunged nearly 40% after the firm said it will post lower first-quarter revenue.

---

Double hulls would be required for new oil tankers, and phased in for most existing ones, under legislation approved by legislative conferees.

---

Big retailers reported a 5.8% average sales gain in June, as sunny weather boosted buying of apparel. Wal-Mart, K mart and Dayton Hudson's Target did particularly well, while Limited's same-store sales fell.

---

A mistrial was declared on 26 charges against John A. Mulheren Jr. after the jury deadlocked. The judge let stand his conviction Tuesday on conspiracy and securities fraud.

---

Blinder Robinson and its chairman were sued by the SEC for $20 million in profit from alleged illegal stock activity by the penny-stock firm.

---

Kubota was sued by the founders of a U.S. computer firm, who say the Japanese giant appropriated their technology through a minority stake.

---

Soybean futures are expected to rally today after the government raised its estimate of exports and trimmed its projection for stockpiles.

---

Curtiss-Wright declared a $30 special dividend and pledged to focus on manufacturing rather than investing.

---
