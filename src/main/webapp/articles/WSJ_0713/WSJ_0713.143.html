Castle & Cooke May Sell Dole Food Co. As Some Major Firms Indicate Interest

  • Author: Rhonda L. Rundle
  • Date: 07/13/90

LOS ANGELES -- Castle & Cooke Inc. said it has been contacted by several major companies interested in acquiring its Dole Food Co. unit and has retained Goldman, Sachs & Co. to help evaluate a possible sale of the subsidiary. The announcement is a tactical shift by Castle & Cooke, which had planned to spin off Dole Food into a new publicly traded company. Those plans were put on hold in May because of financing concerns and uncertainty over the Internal Revenue Service's tax treatment of the transaction.

Dole Food, known for its fresh fruits and vegetables, also markets packaged and canned foods, chilled and frozen juices, and assorted nuts. Last year, Dole Food contributed more than 90% of Castle & Cooke's $2.72 billion in revenue. One analyst estimated that Dole Food could fetch as much as $2.5 billion.

David H. Murdock, Castle & Cooke chairman and chief executive officer who holds a 23% stake in the company, said in a statement that a sale of Dole Food "could be an attractive alternative to the planned separation of our two businesses and we believe it is in the best interests of our shareholders to explore such a transaction."

Real estate development, largely conducted through Oceanic Properties Inc., is Castle & Cooke's other business.

Last year, when Castle & Cooke planned to split into two companies, it said the separation would allow for easier financing of growth for both the real estate development and food businesses and would allow management of both companies better opportunities to focus on their individual operations.

Castle & Cooke owns about 129,000 acres in Hawaii, 32,000 acres in California, and about 9,000 acres in Arizona. Mr. Murdock said through a spokeswoman that he wasn't pursuing a possible sale of Dole to raise money for Castle & Cooke's real estate development plans.

Larry Selwitz, an analyst at Cruttenden & Co. in Newport Beach, Calif., says that Dole Food is a "very valuable franchise" that might be particularly attractive to an international food conglomerate because of Dole's world-wide operations. Dole has many separate businesses that could be spun off to help finance an acquisition, he said.

Mr. Selwitz said a sticky negotiating point in selling the unit might be the ownership of Castle & Cooke's Hawaiian agricultural properties, which it had planned to lease to Dole under the spinoff plan. Also, Dole Food may have a "morale problem" because of uncertainties about the company's future, Mr. Selwitz said.

Earlier this week, Castle & Cooke announced "major reorganizations" at Dole and some of its divisions. David DeLorenzo was named president of Dole Food, filling a vacancy created after Robert W. Fisher left to become president of PPI Del Monte Tropical Fruit Co., a unit of Polly Peck International PLC. During the past six months, about 40 people have been laid off at Dole's packaged foods division in San Francisco.

Yesterday, in New York Stock Exchange composite trading, Castle & Cooke shares closed at $30.50, up 37.5 cents. The announcement about a possible sale of the unit was made after the close of trading.
