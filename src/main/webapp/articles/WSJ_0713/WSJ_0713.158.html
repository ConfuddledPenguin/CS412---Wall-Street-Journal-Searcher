Business Brief -- Kimberly-Clark Corp.: Operating Profit Increased By 23% in Second Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/13/90

Kimberly-Clark Corp. said operating earnings for the second quarter rose 23% to $124.4 million, or $1.56 a share, before a one-time charge. The charge of $44 million, or 55 cents a share, was related to the pending sale of a Canadian unit, Spruce Falls Power & Paper Co. The charge caused net income to decline 21% to $80.5 million, or $1.01 a share, from $101.3 million, or $1.26 a share, a year earlier.

The operating earnings exceeded analysts' expectations and the stock market reacted enthusiastically. The stock soared $4.625 to $82.25 in New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday.

Analysts attributed the earnings surge to strong sales of Kimberly-Clark's disposable diapers. The Dallas-based paper and consumer products company said sales for the quarter rose 15% to $1.61 billion from $1.4 billion in the 1989 period.

Operating results set records for the both the quarter and the first half. Excluding the second-quarter charge, net income for the six months improved 18% to $241.6 million, or $3.02 per share. Revenue was up 13% to $3.19 billion.
