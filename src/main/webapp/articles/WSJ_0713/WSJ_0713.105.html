Brady Bids to Ease Fears On Futures in the Future

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/13/90

WASHINGTON -- Treasury Secretary Nicholas Brady told a Senate panel that if Congress approves a proposal to shift oversight of stock-index futures to securities regulators, he wouldn't seek additional shifts of futures regulation. "I will oppose more sweeping changes to {Commodity Futures Trading Commission} authority if the administration's bill passes in its present form," he said at a Senate Banking Committee hearing.

The statement was an apparent effort by Mr. Brady to assuage fears of the futures industry and some legislators that a Bush administration proposal to switch index futures is simply a prelude to a broader effort to pillage CFTC authority.

The proposal would move oversight of stock-index futures from the CFTC to the Securities and Exchange Commission, which currently regulates stocks and options. Stock-index futures, which are frequently part of computerized program trades, are used by institutional investors to hedge risk in the stock market by allowing investors to bet on the movement of a basket of stocks without buying the actual shares.

The administration would like to see the proposal move as an amendment to the CFTC reauthorization bill, and senators in favor of the White House measure have held up the CFTC bill. But speculation surfaced yesterday that Sen. Patrick Leahy (D., Vt.), who heads the Senate Agriculture Committee, may try to attach the CFTC bill to the farm bill that will move through the Senate in the next week or so.

While aides close to the senator said he hasn't considered such a move, he has expressed dismay over the delay in the CFTC measure.
