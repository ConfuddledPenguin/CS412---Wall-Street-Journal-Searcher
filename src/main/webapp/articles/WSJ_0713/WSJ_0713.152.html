Westinghouse Profit Rose 11% on Flat Sales In Quarter, First Half

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/13/90

PITTSBURGH -- Westinghouse Electric Corp. posted an 11% increase in second-quarter and six-months profit despite roughly flat sales in both periods. The company said net income rose to $252 million in the quarter, or 86 cents a share, compared with $228 million, or 78 cents a share, in the 1989 second quarter. Year-earlier earnings per share were adjusted for a 2-for-1 stock split paid in May. Sales were flat at $3.18 billion.

Results were in line with analysts' expectations.

Westinghouse doesn't break out results of its various businesses, but said pretax operating profit increased substantially in both its Energy and Utility Systems unit and its Group W broadcasting business. For about a year Westinghouse has been booking increased power-generation equipment orders, much of it from independent producers. The company attributed the improved performance at its broadcasting segment to recently acquired radio stations.

Westinghouse also said operating profit at its Financial Services segment rose slightly from a year earlier. Within that segment, the company said lower results at its Westinghouse Credit business partly offset significantly higher operating profit at its community development business. Pretax profit at its Commercial division also improved.

Westinghouse said operating profit increased slightly at its Electronic Systems segment and was higher in its Industries business.

In the latest six months, net was $462 million, or $1.57 a share, compared with net of $417 million, or $1.43 a share, adjusted for the split. Sales declined to $6.04 billion from $6.06 billion.
