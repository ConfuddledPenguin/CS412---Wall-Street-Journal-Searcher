Nynex Unit Is Denounced On Bid for New York Rates

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/13/90

NEW YORK -- State Attorney Gen. Robert Abrams denounced a proposed rate increase of about $1.4 billion being sought by Nynex Corp.'s New York Telephone Co. unit, urging that Nynex cut waste and eliminate "its unconscionable business practices" instead of seeking "a bailout from ratepayers." At a news conference yesterday, Mr. Abrams said the average consumer's monthly bill would go up about 36% if the request were granted. He also said Nynex officials have "resisted us every inch of the way" as his office has tried to gather information about New York Telephone's allegedly improper dealings with the parent company's purchasing arm, Materiel Enterprises Co.

Those dealings and other questionable Nynex activities were the subject of an article yesterday in this newspaper.

Describing the investigation and review process in connection with the rate case, Mr. Abrams said Nynex has suppressed as "trade secrets" many facts that the company "would find embarrassing if the public knew."

In White Plains, N.Y., Nynex criticized Mr. Abrams for making what it characterized as "wrong and disingenuous" allegations.

In a statement, Nynex didn't say which specific allegations made by Mr. Abrams it considers to be inaccurate or disingenuous. But the company said the charges by Mr. Abrams that it disputes pertain to "the business practices of Nynex companies and their impact on the people of New York."
