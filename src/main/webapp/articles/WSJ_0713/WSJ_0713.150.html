Lilco Accord to Sell Shoreham Is Upheld By New York Court

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/13/90

NEW YORK -- A New York state appeals court upheld an agreement between the state and Long Island Lighting Co. under which Lilco is to sell its Shoreham nuclear power plant to the state for $1. The 5-0 decision, which had been expected, upheld two lower court rulings that found that state officials, led by Gov. Mario Cuomo, were duly authorized by the state legislature to enter into the agreement.

"This is great news for Lilco," said Daniel Scotto, utility-bond analyst for Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette Securities Corp.

Lilco continues to own Shoreham, pending federal regulatory approval of the transfer of the license to a state agency. Nevertheless, the Hicksville, N.Y., utility already is getting the first of the sizable rate increases that also are part of its agreement with the state-rate increases that have brought Lilco back to financial health after years of being bled by the completed but never operational Shoreham.

While the state originally said it planned to dismantle Shoreham, more recently it has had discussions about possibly converting the plant into a natural gas-fired plant.

The ruling by the appellate division of State Supreme Court follows a recent federal appeals court ruling that effectively eliminated Lilco's financial liability under a racketeering judgment against the company. Suffolk County, N.Y., officials have said they may bring another similar claim against the utility.

Lilco shares closed at $21.125, up 12.5 cents, in moderate trading on the New York Stock Exchange.
