Business Brief -- First Union Corp.: Quarter's Profit Rose 5.7%; Net Interest Income Cited

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/13/90

First Union Corp. said second-quarter earnings rose 5.7% to $78 million, or 72 cents a share, reflecting an increase in net interest income. In the year-earlier period, the Charlotte, N.C., banking company had net income of $74 million, or 69 cents a share.

First Union said the most recent quarter included an after-tax gain of $7 million from the divestiture of 13 branches in Florida. The gain was offset by an addition of an equivalent amount to the allowance for loan losses. The 1989 second quarter included after-tax gains of $2 million from the sale of investment securities.

Net interest income increased 20% to $352 million from $293 million in the 1989 second quarter. Meanwhile, nonperforming assets at the end of the most recent quarter totaled $616 million, an increase of $114 million from the end of the 1990 first quarter. Of the increase, $15 million resulted from a special exam by the comptroller of the currency. At the end of the 1989 second quarter, First Union had nonperforming assets of $247 million.

In the first half, First Union said earnings rose 2.3% to $149.2 million, or $1.39 a share, from $145.9 million, or $1.37 a share, a year earlier.
