Business Brief -- CPC International Inc.: Food Concern Reports Net For 2nd Quarter Rose 14%

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/13/90

CPC International Inc. reported second-quarter earnings climbed 14% on a sales increase of 16%, as the food producer posted stronger operating profit in all geographic areas. CPC, with prominent brands such as Hellmann's and Best Foods mayonnaise, Skippy peanut butter and Karo syrup, said that its Latin American operations slipped a bit at the end of the period. But the Englewood Cliffs, N.J., company expects the operations to turn up again by the end of the third quarter.

Second-quarter net income climbed to $95 million, or $1.23 a share, from $83.1 million, or $1.06 a share, in the year-earlier period. Sales advanced to $1.45 billion, from $1.26 billion. Shares outstanding slipped 3.6%, to 75.4 million, as the company completed a share buy-back in the fourth quarter.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, CPC shares rose $2.125 to close at $84.75.

Both operating profit and sales grew 5.3% at CPC's Best Foods, the North American consumer-foods division, led by the mayonnaise brands, Mazola corn oil, Knorr soup and other products.
