Marketing & Media: Pathe Countersuit Of $500 Million Filed Against Time Warner

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/13/90

LOS ANGELES -- Pathe Communications Corp. filed a $500 million cross-complaint against Time Warner Inc. and its units charging that Time Warner breached its agreement to bankroll half of Pathe's proposed $1.3 billion merger with MGM/UA Communications Co. Last month, Time Warner filed a $100 million lawsuit against Pathe charging "willful, wanton and malicious" breach of contract in the collapse of that pact.

In a statement, Time Warner said, "Pathe's cross complaint is just its latest attempt to divert attention from the facts, which are that Pathe breached its April 1990 agreement with Time Warner, its theatrical distribution agreement with Warner Brothers and a video rights distribution agreement with Warner Home Video."

Time Warner also said it intends to schedule the depositions of Giancarlo Parretti, the head of Pathe, and one of his partners immediately.

The Pathe suit, filed in state superior court here, charges, among other things, that Time Warner failed to honor an agreement to guarantee a $650 million bank loan or to loan Pathe that amount itself. As part of that transaction, Time Warner was going to obtain certain rights to the film libraries of United Artists, Pathe and its Cannon unit.

Time Warner has said the agreement collapsed after Pathe failed to come up with sufficient equity, taking on additional debt instead. However, the latest suit contends that Pathe did meet its obligations to obtain $600 million in non-debt financing. The suit says Pathe met all other conditions except those "excused by the acts or conduct of Time Warner." The suit alleges that Time Warner failed to provide the $650 million loan to Pathe "without any reasonable explanation."

The Pathe suit says Time Warner was unable to find an outside bank willing to lend Pathe $650 million, and it notes that Standard & Poor's Corp. placed Time Warner's debt on a CreditWatch list soon after the agreement. The suit says that although it was unable to obtain the loan, Time Warner was still "highly desirous" of obtaining the rights to the film libraries, and it pressured Pathe to make a deal more advantageous to Time Warner.

The suit alleges that Time Warner "knowingly put Pathe at risk of losing the $200 million non-refundable deposit made to MGM/UA" and a $75 million security interest granted to MGM/UA. Pathe also contends that Time Warner's actions forced Pathe to renegotiate its agreement with MGM/UA, increasing the cost of the acquisition by more than $200 million.
