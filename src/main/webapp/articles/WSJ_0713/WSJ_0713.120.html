Conte Once Blamed a Mix-Up in Names

  • Author: John R. Emshwiller
  • Date: 07/13/90

D. Constantine Conte has, in previous years, insisted that allegations against him are really just a case of mistaken identity. There are other men in Los Angeles named Dino Conte, he told the Los Angeles Times in 1983, and one of them must have the unsavory connections. Mr. Conte didn't make this claim with this newspaper in his correspondence through his lawyer, but the "double" theory has made the rounds in Hollywood. "There were two Dino Contes," insists Kurt Neumann, an executive at Warner Bros. and a friend of the producer. The "innuendo" about Mr. Conte is "well known" and is widely dismissed, he says.

Mr. Conte's claim isn't entirely frivolous. Law enforcement authorities have at least on one occasion confused him with a Dino Carmelo Conte, and in one report, as a preventive measure, included photos and other information about the two men to make sure investigators kept them straight.

However, law enforcement officers also have noted in police reports that the ex-con Dino Conte and the producer Dino Conte are one and the same. They also say that surveillance and other investigative work has tied the producer to suspected organized crime figures.

Moreover, at the request of this newspaper, Bruce R. Greenwood, a member of the American Board of Forensic Document Examiners and a frequent expert witness in trials involving document analysis, examined several handwriting samples of Mr. Conte's, including a letter he wrote while in prison in the Atlanta Federal Penetentiary and samples he has written while a producer in Hollywood. Mr. Greenwood said that based on several factors -- the slant of letters in words, their design, their proportional relationship to each other -- he concluded the same person wrote all the samples. "All of the features of the writing match," he says.

It is notable, too, that the prison file for the criminal Mr. Conte lists the same date of birth -- June 25, 1933 -- as the producer's current California driver's license. His 1964 arrest record on file with the Nassau County, N.Y., police, which also notes the same birth date, indicates that Mr. Conte was using the name Dino Harris at the time, and that his mother's first name was Thelma. A woman named Thelma Harris lives at the producer's address in west Los Angeles, according to 1988 records on file with the Los Angeles County registrar of voters.
