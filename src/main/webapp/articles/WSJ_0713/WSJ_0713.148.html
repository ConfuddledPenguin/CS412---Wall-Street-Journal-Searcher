Money-Fund Assets Gained $3.10 Billion In the Latest Week

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/13/90

WASHINGTON -- Assets of the nation's 512 money-market funds rose $3.10 billion, to $385.83 billion, in the week ended Wednesday, according to the Investment Company Institute, a Washington-based trade association. Assets of 250 general-purpose funds rose $763.5 million, to $133.98 billion, and assets of 102 broker-dealer funds rose $2.12 billion, to $160.04 billion. Assets of 160 institutional funds rose $215.4 million, to $91.82 billion.

"Money-market funds assets, especially those held in individual accounts, rebounded sharply, more than making up the losses of the preceding week," said Jacob Dreyer, vice president and chief economist at the institute. Money-fund assets were revised for the previous week to $382.73 billion. The reported decrease in assets of $2.24 billion was revised to a decrease of $2.20 billion.
