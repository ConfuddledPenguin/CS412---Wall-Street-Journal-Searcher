Lord Rayner Plans To Resign His Post At Marks & Spencer

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 07/13/90

LONDON -- Lord Rayner, chairman of Marks & Spencer PLC, is to resign on March 31, 1991, the end of the company's current fiscal year and the day after his 65th birthday. In a statement, the company said Richard Greenbury, chief executive officer, would succeed Lord Rayner as chairman. Lord Rayner, who joined the company in 1953 and has been chairman since July 1984, will also retire from the board.

Addressing the annual meeting yesterday, Lord Rayner said Marks & Spencer's broad-based merchandising strengths mean the company "has no need to join the list of retailers forecasting disappointing results."

Lord Rayner said that, in common with the rest of the British retailing industry, furniture sales are slow in center-city outlets but are performing well in other areas. Unseasonal weather in June depressed sales of summer-fashion goods, he said, but other departments -- including lingerie, socks, underwear, and clothing for children and babies -- are making "substantial gains."

The company is investing 350 million pounds (about $630 million) in the United Kingdom this year and will open 450,000 square feet of selling space. The property portfolio, consisting of owned and long-lease properties in prime retailing positions, represents a major strength, the official noted.

Overseas, expansion is planned in continental Europe, where about 40 new stores are scheduled to be opened in the next five years. Sales in the company's four Hong Kong stores for the fiscal year to date are more than double last year's level.

In Japan, sales are 30% above year-earlier levels, Lord Rayner said. Full-year sales in North America are expected to exceed 500,000 pounds ($895,000), he added.
