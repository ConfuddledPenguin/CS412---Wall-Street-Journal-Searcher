Marketing & Media: Would Murdoch `Tell All' In Possible Autobiography

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/13/90

NEW YORK -- Will media baron Rupert Murdoch tell all, joining the list of moguls publishing their memoirs? The odds look good. The chairman of media giant News Corp. is considering writing an autobiography, according to his public relations adviser in New York, Howard Rubenstein. "He may or may not do a book, but he's certainly considering it," Mr. Rubenstein said. He said Mr. Murdoch is working with senior editor Peter Brimelow of Forbes, but he has "no advance and no contract." Mr. Rubenstein quoted Mr. Murdoch as saying that if he does such a book, he may do it with British publisher Weidenfeld & Nicholson.

Although News Corp. owns a very large book publisher, Harper-Collins Publishers, with operations in both the U.S. and the United Kingdom, "an independent source suggested he should have it (his autobiography) published elsewhere," Mr. Rubenstein said.

The reasons for a Murdoch autobiography have "nothing to do with money or vanity," said Mr. Rubenstein. "There are many things that have been published about him that are highly inaccurate. This would be a way to straighten out some of the incorrect things that have been written."
