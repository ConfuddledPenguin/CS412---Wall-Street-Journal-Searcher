Peoples Bancorp Expects to Post 12% Profit Rise

  • Author: Ron Suskind
  • Date: 07/13/90

WORCESTER, Mass. -- Peoples Bancorp of Worcester Inc., which has avoided the real estate loan problems plaguing other New England savings banks, expects to report that net income rose 12% in the second quarter over income before investment gains in the year-ago period. Woodbury C. Titcomb, president and chief executive, said in an interview that second-quarter net income was about $1.3 million, or 48 cents a share, up from income before investment gains of $1.2 million, or 43 cents a share, a year earlier.

In the 1989 second quarter, investment income of eight cents a share brought net income to $1.5 million, or 51 cents a share. There was no investment income in the latest quarter.

For the year, Peoples expects earnings to be in line with analysts' estimates, showing an increase of 8% to about $2.05 a share from $1.90 a share in 1989, according to Michael S. Shaw, the bank's senior vice president and treasurer.

Mr. Titcomb said Peoples, the holding company for Peoples Savings Bank of Worcester, was expecting to "grow through acquisitions" over the next several years, continuing its current program of "careful purchases."

On July 9, Peoples paid the Federal Deposit Insurance Corp. a premium of $100,000 to assume $300 million of deposits of Milford Savings Bank, a Milford, Mass.based savings bank that was closed a few days earlier by federal regulators. Under an agreement with the FDIC, Peoples will review Milford's $280 million loan portfolio and has the option to purchase loans it deems worthy.

The Milford purchase was the second acquisition of a troubled institution by Peoples. In April 1989, Peoples paid $3 million for $301.7 million of assets of First Service Bank for Savings of Leominster, Mass., in an FDIC auction.

"Future acquisitions and consolidations is the way to go," said Mr. Titcomb, "providing they can be controlled, disciplined and significantly accretive."

Mr. Titcomb said Peoples is in a position to make acquisitions because of the prudent lending policies it followed during the mid-1980s. While other New England savings banks, many of which went public in 1986, were lending freely, "there was an awful lot of criticism of Peoples not growing, not lending," said Mr. Titcomb. But other lenders are finding many loans made during periods of swift growth are now nonperforming.

Peoples' nonperforming loans are among the lowest in the region. Although its non-performers are rising slightly, they will be less than 1% of total loans at the end of the second quarter compared with 0.35% at the end of 1989.

The bank will add $120,000 to loan-loss reserves, the regular quarterly provision it has been making since 1988, bringing reserves up to $4.6 million. That will cover nearly 100% of its non-performing loans, a higher percentage than most banks. Of the bank's total loans of $510 million, 54% of them are in residential mortgages, 24% in home equity loans -- the portfolio's fastest growing area -- and 13% in commercial real estate or construction. Nearly all the loans are in Worcester County.

The bank's equity-to-assets ratio was 9.4% at June 30, and is expected to settle at 8% after repayment of about $70 million in brokered deposits from Milford Savings over the next 45 days. Peoples expects to keep about $130 million of Milford's $300 million in deposits, bringing Peoples' total assets to $900 million by year end.

Return on assets is expected to be 0.69% for first six months of 1990 compared with 0.70% for all of 1989. Return on equity is expected to be 7.1% for first six months of the year compared with 8% for the like year-earlier period.

The company, which has raised its dividend for each of the 14 quarters since going public, "will try to find a way to keep it rising," said Mr. Titcomb.
