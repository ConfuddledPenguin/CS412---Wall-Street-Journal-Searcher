Japanese Firm Offers a Car As an Incentive to Recruits

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/13/90

TOKYO -- Japan's labor shortage has become so acute that one company is offering recruits a brand new car. Maruwa Orimono K.K., a small producer of synthetic-fiber fabrics in western Japan, said it took the step "to enhance our corporate image and thus to help secure workers."

While it has been common for companies to offer corporate housing to attract workers to crowded Japanese cities, this appears to be the first time a company has offered cars as an employment incentive. (Kentucky Fried Chicken recently sent some of its first-year employees on an all-expenses-paid trip to Hawaii.)

A spokesman for the company, which has 110 employees, says the labor supply situation in the area is "so bad" that for the past three years the company hasn't been able to hire a single high-school graduate. He says that with major companies hiring away most fresh graduates from the region, the ratio of job offers to applicants in the area now stands at about three to one. It is far higher than the nationwide figure of 1.41, which is the highest in the past 16 years.

After a three-month probation period, a Maruwa Orimono recruit is entitled to the car, but the recruit must be younger than 35 years.

Recruits can choose any car they like with a price ceiling of about 1.5 million yen ($10,095). They aren't required to return the car if they quit.

Since the decision was publicized in a local newspaper Monday, the spokesman said, two or three people have made inquiries with the company, but by Tuesday no one had been hired yet.
