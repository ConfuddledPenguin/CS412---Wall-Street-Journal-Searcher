Credit Ratings: S&P Affirms Bond Rating For State of Connecticut

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/13/90

NEW YORK -- Standard & Poor's Corp. affirmed its double-A rating on $2.8 billion of Connecticut's general obligation bonds outstanding and also assigned a double-A rating to the state's $325 million of general obligation bonds Series 1990B due 1991-2010. The move reflects the state's "fundamentally strong economic base, well above-average wealth levels, a rising but manageable debt burden and historically solid but weakened financial posture," the rating concern said. "Economic sluggishness and revenue shortfalls in the sales and corporate taxes occurred throughout fiscal 1990."

S&P estimates Connecticut closed the fiscal year ended June 30 with a $129 million budget deficit. The fiscal 1991 budget, reflecting the carry-over, is now $185 million in the red.

The rating concern expects Connecticut to close the gap, however. "The fiscal year's just starting, so they've got plenty of time," says Sally Rutherford, analyst at S&P. She added, "It might be politically easier" to deal with the budget gap "after the elections."

"The magnitude" of Connecticut's problems remains "manageable, and the state's other primary credit factors remain intact," S&P said.

Gov. William O'Neill's office declined to comment. Anthony V. Milano, secretary of the Office of Policy and Management, the state's budget arm, was unavailable for comment.
