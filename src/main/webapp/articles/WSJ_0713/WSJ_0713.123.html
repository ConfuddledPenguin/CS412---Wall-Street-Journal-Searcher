Business Brief -- Genlyte Group Inc.: Firm Reports Its Earnings In Second Period Fell 51%

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/13/90

Genlyte Group Inc., Secaucus, N.J., reported that second-quarter earnings dropped 51% on a 4.3% drop in sales. The manufacturer of lighting fixtures and controls cited effects of strikes in Canada, which have ended, and sluggish construction in the U.S. Genlyte said early in June that it expected the declines.

Net income sank to $1.7 million, or 13 cents a share, from $3.4 million, or 26 cents a share, a year earlier. Sales dropped to $123 million, from $128.5 million.

Strikes in Ontario halted construction from May 1 to July 9, and shorter wildcat strikes were called in Quebec; they seriously hurt Genlyte's results. The company expects to return to more normal operations this quarter.

Genlyte also reduced its debt by $2 million, to $162.5 million.

In national over-the-counter trading yesterday, Genlyte's shares fell 25 cents to close at $7.50.
