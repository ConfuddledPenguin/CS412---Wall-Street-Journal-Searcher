Euralair Upgrades Airbus Order

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/13/90

PARIS -- Airbus Industrie said the French charter carrier Euralair upgraded previously announced commitments for two of the European aircraft consortium's A-321 jets into two firm orders and an option on one additional plane. Financial details weren't disclosed, but people in the industry estimate an A-321's price at about $40 million. Airbus, based in Toulouse, said the planes will be delivered in the spring of 1995 and used on flights to Mediterranean resorts. Each plane will be equipped to seat about 200 passengers in an all-economy layout. Airbus said Euralair hadn't made an engine choice.

Airbus is a consortium among Aerospatiale of France, Messerschmitt-Boelkow Blohm G.m.b.H. of West Germany, British Aerospace PLC and Construcciones Aeronauticas S.A. of Spain.
