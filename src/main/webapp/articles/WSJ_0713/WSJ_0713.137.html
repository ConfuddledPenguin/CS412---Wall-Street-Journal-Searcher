Business Brief -- Air France: An Additional Six Options On Boeing 747 Jets Cleared

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/13/90

Air France said its board approved an additional six options on Seattle-based Boeing Co.'s 747-400, the superlong-range version of the aircraft. Financial details weren't disclosed. Air France said deliveries are to be made between January 1996 and April 1999, in either the passenger or Combi version; the latter carries both passengers and cargo on the main deck, while all versions carry cargo in the belly compartment below the main deck.

Air France said it already held 20 firm orders and 33 options for the 747-400, with the first, a passenger model, to be delivered in February 1991. Of the 20 firm orders, 11 are for the passenger model, four for the Combi and five for the freighter model.

As it reviews its fleet needs, the carrier said it will advise Boeing about models that will be represented among the 39 optioned aircraft.
