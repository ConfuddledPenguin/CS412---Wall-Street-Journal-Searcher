Credit Ratings: Bank of New York Has Ratings Lowered

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/13/90

NEW YORK -- Standard & Poor's Corp. lowered its rating on $1.1 billion of Bank of New York Co.'s long-term and short-term debt and preferred stock, citing its exposure to highly leveraged transactions and rising levels of problem loans. Bank of New York's senior debt was reduced to single-A-minus from single-A. The subordinated debt fell to triple-B-plus from single A-minus. The preferred stock slipped to triple-B from triple-B-plus, and commercial paper went to A2 from A1.

A Bank of New York spokesman said, "We are obviously disappointed by S&P's action, and we will work with them to get our rating upgraded."
