Politics & Policy: Catholics, Blacks Disagree With Leaders, Poll Shows

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/13/90

WASHINGTON -- Roman Catholics and blacks appear to be paying little heed to the calls of some of their leaders these days, according to the latest Wall Street Journal/NBC News poll. The survey found that Catholic voters overwhelmingly oppose excommunicating Catholic politicians who take positions favoring abortion rights. Seventy-one percent of those Catholics polled said such politicians shouldn't be excommunicated, while only 16% said they should.

Meanwhile, efforts by the Rev. Jesse Jackson and others to adopt the term "African-American" for blacks have yet to catch fire. Among the blacks polled, 59% said they prefer to be referred to as black, while 25% prefer African-American. And 70% said there has been little debate over the issue among people they know; 27% said the topic has been widely discussed.
