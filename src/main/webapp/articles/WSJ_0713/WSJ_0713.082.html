Italy's Electricity Consumption

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/13/90

MILAN, Italy -- Electricity consumption in Italy rose 3% in June and 3.3% in the first half, compared with year-earlier levels, the state electricity utility ENEL said. Consumption rose to 19.35 billion kilowatt-hours in June and to 118.7 billion kwh in the first six months. ENEL didn't provide year-earlier levels.

The rate of electricity consumption was down from a 3.7% increase in May, when demand totaled 17.02 billion kwh, and from a 3.4% rise in the first five months, when demand totaled 99 billion kwh.

Gross electricity generation in Italy rose 0.8% in June to 17.51 billion kwh, compared with a year earlier. Total electricity imports rose 9.2% in June to almost three billion kwh from 2.74 billion kwh a year earlier.
