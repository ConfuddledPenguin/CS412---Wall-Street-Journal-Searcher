Unocal Plans to Buy Amoco Unit's Stake In Alaskan Properties

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/13/90

LOS ANGELES -- Unocal Corp. said it agreed to buy Amoco Production Co.'s 62.5% interest in the Chakachatna Group of oil and gas properties in the Cook Inlet of Alaska. Terms weren't disclosed. The Chakachatna Group owns two platforms in the Granite Point field and two in the Middle Ground Shoal field, which now have a gross production rate of about 5,100 barrels of oil a day. Unocal estimated that the fields have more than 11 million barrels of developed oil reserves, with more available for development.

A spokesman for Amoco Production in Denver said the sale is "part of our longterm strategy of consolidating our producing operations geologically and geographically." Though the unit of Chicago-based Amoco Corp. has considerable holdings in Alaska, including a 10% stake in the Endicott field on the North Slope, "The Cook Inlet properties represent the only Amoco-operated production in Alaska," the spokesman said.

Amoco Production is the current operator for the group. Unocal is expected to assume this role, following a vote by the other group partners, Chevron Corp., San Francisco, and Texaco Inc., White Plains, N.Y.

The transaction, which is subject to certain consents, including a waiting period required by the Hart-Scott-Rodino Act on antitrust issues, is expected to be completed within the next several weeks, a Unocal spokesman said.
