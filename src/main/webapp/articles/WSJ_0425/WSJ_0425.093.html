Orbital Sciences Launches Initial Public Offer at $14

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/25/90

FAIRFAX, Va. -- After launching its experimental Pegasus rocket, Orbital Sciences Corp. finally launched its initial public offering. The company sold about 2.4 million shares at $14 each Monday -- $1 a share more than the company had planned last month when it abruptly canceled the offering. On the day of the proposed offering, a Wall Street Journal article appeared, describing the steep risks involved in the Pegasus project and apparently spooking the company and investors.

But since that time, things have gone well for Orbital Sciences. In early April, it successfully launched Pegasus, which is carried on the wing of a B-52 bomber and is dropped over the ocean before it ignites. Pegasus is designed to carry small satellites into space -- an emerging market that has largely been ignored by the big aerospace companies as too risky or too small.

Cash-strapped Orbital Sciences sold about 1,250,000 shares itself, raising about $17.5 million, before underwriting fees. Outside investors sold about 1,150,000 shares. Orbital Sciences didn't return calls seeking comment. Its underwriter, Alex. Brown & Sons, confirmed that the shares had been sold.

Yesterday, in its first day of trading, the rocket company's shares opened at $14.50 in over-the-counter trading and closed at $14. About 1.2 million shares were traded.
