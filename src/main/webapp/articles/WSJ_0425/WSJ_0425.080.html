Credit Ratings

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/25/90

BORDEN Co. (New York) -- Moody's Investors Service Inc. lowered the ratings on Borden's long-term debt, citing the likelihood that cash flow coverage of debt will be at lower levels than Moody's previously expected for the intermediate term. Ratings were lowered to single-A-1 from double-A-3 on various categories of debt. Last fall the food company announced a restructuring and established a $404 million after-tax reserve for plant realignments and a shrinking of its dairy business. Moody's said it believes that the actions will improve the outlook for earnings and cash generation ultimately, but in the meantime, "increased marketing expenditures needed to support brands will consume a substantial part of the restructuring gains." About $1.2 billion of debt is affected.
