Who's News: Chapman to Be Nominated To Spot on Nomura Board

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/25/90

NEW YORK -- Max C. Chapman Jr., co-chairman of the U.S. unit of Nomura Securities Co., will be nominated to the board of the parent company in a vote Wednesday in Tokyo, according to sources close to Nomura. Before joining the 40-member board of the world's largest investment bank, Mr. Chapman must be approved at another board meeting in mid-May and by shareholders at the annual meeting June 29.

If Mr. Chapman joins the board, as is expected, he will be the first non-Japanese ever chosen to serve in that group. The former president of Kidder, Peabody & Co. joined the U.S. unit Oct. 2, after being passed over for the chief executive's job at the broker-dealer's holding company, a unit of General Electric Co.

P.J. Johnson, a Nomura spokesman in New York, said that Mr. Chapman is one of several candidates for the board and that election shouldn't be taken for granted. Nevertheless, a ranking board member in Tokyo said that Mr. Chapman "clearly deserves membership on the board, and his hiring indicates our commitment to expanding Nomura's global presence."
