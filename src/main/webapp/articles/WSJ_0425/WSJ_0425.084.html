Technology: Intel Corp. Lawsuit Says Advanced Micro Infringed Copyright

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/25/90

SANTA CLARA, Calif. -- Intel Corp. filed a lawsuit in federal court in San Francisco against Advanced Micro Devices Inc., claiming that Advanced infringed on an Intel copyright on the company's microcode technology. Under a 1976 agreement between the two companies, Advanced Micro is allowed to use and copy the technology for its own use, but it isn't allowed to distribute it through sales of its own products. The microcode technology, which is software that tells a chip how to perform its function, is used in Intel's 80287 math co-processor.

The dispute began a month ago when Advanced Micro, a Sunnyvale, Calif., chip maker, told Intel that it planned to use the company's microcode techology in certain products for distribution.

"Obviously, we have a significantly different understanding," said a spokesman for Advanced Micro. The spokesman said it is the company's understanding, based on the 1976 agreement, that Advanced can indeed use the Intel technology in products beyond internal company use.

Intel officials, however, maintain that the agreement specifically bans the use of its technology for anything but internal applications at Advanced. In its suit, the maker of computer parts, boards and systems said it is seeking an injunction.
