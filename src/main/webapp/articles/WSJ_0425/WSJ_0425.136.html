Technology Brief -- Hewlett-Packard Co.: Pact Is Signed With Unit Of McDonnell Douglas

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/25/90

Hewlett-Packard Co., Palo Alto, Calif., said it signed a three-year, $75 million contract with McDonnell Douglas Systems Integration Co. that allows resale of Hewlett's 9000 family of workstations with McDonnell Douglas software. Under the agreement, the unit of McDonnell Douglas Corp. will use its Unigraphics software in the Hewlett 9000 workstations, which use chips based on Motorola and RISC, or reduced instruction set computing, technology.

The combination of Hewlett workstations equipped with Unigraphics software is designed to take advantage of the growing market for computer-aided design and computer-aided manufacturing for major auto, aerospace and manufacturing concerns, the two companies said.
