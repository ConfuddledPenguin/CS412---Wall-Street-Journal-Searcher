Business Brief -- Harsco Corp.: Investor Group's Proposal For Restructuring Rejected

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/25/90

Harsco Corp. rejected a proposal from an investor group to restructure the company, but left open the possibility for consideration of other proposals. Earlier this month, HC Partners of Washington, D.C., an investor group led by former Secretary of Defense Frank Carlucci and the Mellon family, proposed that Harsco sell off its defense operations and pay a special cash dividend of $15 a share.

The group offered to invest $100 million and possibly as much as $200 million in the Camp Hill, Pa., construction, defense and industrial concern as part of the proposed restructing. The additional $100 million, if provided, would have been used to accelerate Harsco's world-wide research, development and marketing efforts, the group said.

In a letter to Mr. Carlucci, M. W. Gambill, Harsco's chief executive officer and president, said the proposal, which was presented in a letter, was not in the best interest of the company.
