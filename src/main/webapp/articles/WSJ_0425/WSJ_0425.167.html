Technology: Tandon Improves, Drops Its Contract With Bear Stearns

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/25/90

MOORPARK, Calif. -- Tandon Corp., which hired Bear, Stearns & Co. six months ago to explore financial alternatives for the company, said it didn't renew its contract with Bear, Stearns because the computer maker's position has strengthened. Tandon's agreement with Bear Stearns, entered into last September, expired on March 31. The decision not to renew, the company said, was based primarily upon improved operating results and a corresponding strengthened balance sheet, following a company-wide organizational restructuring during the past year.

"While we anticipate continuing discussions with the investment banking community, our position today is significantly different than it was last September when we engaged Bear Stearns to identify alternatives," the company said.

At that time, the company had lost $44.9 million in four successive quarters and expressed concern about its liquidity position.

For the first quarter, the company had net income of $7 million, or 11 cents a share, against a loss of $9.9 million, or 16 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue increased about 13% to $105.1 million from $92.7 million.

Tandon said its working capital increased by $23.8 million from June 30, 1989, to March 31, 1990, while at the same time the company reduced its inventories to $94.4 million, from $120.5 million. During the same nine-month period, the company said its combined accounts and notes payable were reduced to $45.6 million, from $75.8 million, and that its cash position increased to $32.1 million, from $9.7 million.
