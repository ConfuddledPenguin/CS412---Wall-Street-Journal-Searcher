Dow Chemical Profit Fell 38% in Quarter, Hurt by Lower Prices

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/25/90

MIDLAND, Mich. -- Dow Chemical Co. said first-quarter earnings dropped 38% with the combined effects of higher feedstock costs and lower commodity prices. The chemical and specialty-product company said net income fell to $462 million, or $1.71 a share, from $743 million, or $2.72 a share, a year earlier.

Sales increased 9.3% to $4.9 billion from $4.49 billion.

Results were slightly below the expected range of $1.75 to $1.90 a share. But several analysts said they weren't downgrading estimates for the year, which ranged from $6.25 to $7.40, depending on economic forecasts.

The company said results were "good" and represented a return to "more normal" earnings levels, following a record high in the 1989 first quarter. Dow Chemical shares closed at $61, down 50 cents, in New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday.

The company said sales volume increased 18%, but prices fell nearly 8% and hydrocarbon and energy costs were up nearly 6%. Operating profit dropped 43% for chemicals and performance products, 42% for plastics and 89% for hydrocarbons and energy. Negative effects of commodity chemicals were partly offset by consumer, agriculture and pharmaceutical products, where sales rose 62% and operating profit doubled.

"Clearly, our diversification strategy is paying off," said Frank Popoff, president and chief executive officer.

Mr. Popoff said results for 1990 will be "strong," but below 1989 net of $2.49 billion, or $9.20 a share. The second-quarter outlook is improving with declining costs and rising prices for several high-volume products, he says. But analyst Theodore S. Semegran of Shearson Lehman Hutton Inc. warned that rising petrochemical capacity through 1992 would continue to shrink margins and hold down earnings this year and in 1991.
