International Brief -- Siemens AG: Fiscal First-Half Earnings Rose 15% to $436.6 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/25/90

Siemens AG of West Germany, an electronics company, said profit for its first half ended March 31 rose 15% to 749 million marks ($436.6 million) from 652 million marks a year earlier. Sales rose 16% to 30.4 billion marks from 26.3 billion marks.

The company also said that following approval by the West German Federal Cartel Office, it completed the purchase of 51% of the voting stock of Nixdorf Computer AG just after its second quarter ended.

Siemens said it had sales and earnings gains across the board, with the total value of new orders on its books rising 12% to 35.8 billion marks in the first half from 31.9 billion marks a year earlier. It said its overseas operations recorded a "particularly strong" 21.4 billion marks in new orders, up 21% from a year earlier; new orders to domestic operations rose 2% to 14.4 billion marks.
