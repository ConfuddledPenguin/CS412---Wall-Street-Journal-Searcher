MGM Grand Air Says It Will Replace 727s With Larger DC-8s

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/25/90

LOS ANGELES -- MGM Grand Air, the luxury airline unit of MGM Grand Inc., said it plans to add three reconfigured DC8 Super 62 aircraft to its main New York-Los Angeles route in August, replacing the 727-100s currently in use. The new aircraft will double MGM Grand's capacity on its New York-Los Angeles route, Charles L. Demoney, the airline's president and chief executive officer, said. Two of the 727-100s will be used for charter flights, and a third may be put up for sale, he said.

The luxury airline, which began service in September 1987, said the DC-8s will be reconfigured from 190 seats to 81 seats each. The company will spend $55.5 million to buy and refurbish the planes; that equals about $162,500 a seat. The new planes' cargo capacity may enable the airline to boost revenue 4% to 5% from cargo business, Mr. Demoney said.

MGM Grand began turning a profit after five quarters of operation, he said; it reported revenue of $30.2 million for 1989, a 36% increase from the prior year. The airline runs at a load factor of about 75%, but Mr. Demoney said it may show load factors in the high 50% range in the first months of the larger planes' operation.

The airline offers luxury transcontinental service at a fare of $1,017 one way, roughly equal to other airlines' first-class rate. Although the airline hopes to launch international service to London, Tokyo and Paris, no such move is likely anytime soon, the company said.

MGM Grand, which is controlled by investor Kirk Kerkorian's Tracinda Corp., also operates a Las Vegas casino and plans to build another Las Vegas entertainment project.
