U.S. Grand Jury Indicts 18 People On Insurance Roles

  • Author: Beatrice E. Garcia and Milo Geyelin
  • Date: 04/25/90

A federal grand jury in San Diego indicted 14 Southern California lawyers and four other people on charges of participating in a scheme to defraud insurers of as much as $50 million. The indictment alleges that Lynn Boyd Stites, a Glendale lawyer, now believed to be living in Gstaad, Switzerland, organized and controlled this network of lawyers, who often represented both sides of the same lawsuits. The longer and more complex these lawyers could make the litigation involving insurance company policyholders, the higher their fees ran, the indictment alleges. Insurers were paying the legal fees for the policyholders who were being sued and had coverage.

These attorneys were involved in nine major litigations, which spawned a myraid of other lawsuits and countersuits.

The indictment also alleges that "the Alliance," as the network of lawyers and associates is known among prosecutors, provided funding and administrative support to its various member lawyers. A bookkeeper was used to keep track of funds collected by Mr. Stites, who had a kickback arrangement with each member of the Alliance. Money also was paid to individual clients while they were defendants in lawsuits controlled by the Alliance, according to the indictment.

The 18 defendants were charged with 18 violations under the Racketeer Influenced and Corrupt Organizations Act (RICO) and 18 mail fraud violations. The case is the result of a continuing investigation by the U.S. Postal Inspection Service.

The grand jury indictment included a surprise defendant, Leonardo Radomile. Mr. Radomile, a San Diego attorney, had dealings with the Alliance, but he held himself out as an undercover informant working with the postal inspectors. He said he often wore a body wire and recorded numerous conversations with Alliance members for investigators.

Yet the indictment alleges that Mr. Radomile received financial assistance from the Alliance to run his law firm, was compensated by the Alliance for being a defendant in one case, and that he referred five other major litigations to the Alliance.

John Heisner, Mr. Radomile's attorney in San Diego, had no comment on the indictment.

Last month, a suit was filed under RICO by Fund American Cos.' Fireman's Fund Insurance Co. and Sears, Roebuck & Co.'s Allstate Insurance Co. The suit named 26 defendants, 18 of whom, including Mr. Stites, are lawyers. It alleges fraud, conspiracy and mail fraud and seeks $6.3 million in actual damages. Under RICO, a damage award could be tripled.

The insurers' suit names many of the same defendants charged by the grand jury yesterday, but they didn't name Mr. Radomile.
