International: Germanys Select July 2 For Economic, Social Union

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/25/90

BONN (AP) -- West German Chancellor Helmut Kohl and East German Prime Minister Lothar de Maiziere named July 2 as the date for economic and social union of the two Germanys, a West German spokesman said. Union in those areas will bring the divided nation a long way toward full unification after more than four decades of separation following Germany's defeat in World War II. The issue of political union, or holding an election to form a single government, remains to be worked out.

U.S. Secretary of State James A. Baker III and Soviet Foreign Minister Eduard Shevardnadze, joined by their colleagues from Britain, France and the two German states, are scheduled to meet in Bonn May 5. They will try to resolve difficult international aspects of unification, including Germany's future military stance.
