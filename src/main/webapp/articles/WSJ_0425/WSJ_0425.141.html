Business Brief -- Kuhlman Corp.: Solvay America Inc. Plans To Buy Plastics Operations

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/25/90

Kuhlman Corp., Birmingham, Mich., said it entered into a letter of intent to sell its plastics operations to Solvay America Inc. for about $45 million, which includes cash, certain deferred payments and assumption of related liabilities. The announcement came at Kuhlman's annual meeting. Solvay America is a unit of Solvay & Cie S.A. of Belgium.

The plastic operations being sold contributed about one-quarter of Kuhlman's 1989 sales of $166 million, the company said. These operations include plants in Michigan, Pennsylvania and in Ontario, Canada, plus a technical development complex in Michigan and interest in a Mexican joint venture.

"This development will enable Kuhlman to focus its efforts on our profitable electrical tranformer business, which has always been our principal business segment over Kuhlman's entire 96-year history," said Donald O. Dulude, chairman, president and chief executive officer.

In 1989, Kuhlman earned $5.1 million, or 92 cents a share, compared to a $8.1 million loss a year earlier.
