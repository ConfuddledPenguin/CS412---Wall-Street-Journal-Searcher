Inside Track: Novell Executives Sold Stock in Month Before Lotus Offer

  • Author: Alexandra Peers
  • Date: 04/25/90

NEW YORK -- Four executives at Novell Inc. sold more than $8 million of company stock in the month before the price plummeted on news of a proposed merger with Lotus Development Corp., filings with the Securities and Exchange Commission show. Raymond J. Noorda, chairman, sold $6.6 million of the software maker's shares on March 19 and March 20, according to the filings. The 153,000 shares, which were sold at an average price of $43.01 a share, represented 4% of his holdings in the Provo, Utah, company, the filings show.

Three other Novell insiders sold an additional 38,995 shares between March 13 and March 30, according to the filings.

Novell announced plans to merge with Lotus on April 5. The stock fell $4.125 in one day, to $38.75. It continues to hover near that level, closing yesterday at $37.75, down 50 cents, in over-the-counter trading.

Mr. Noorda was unavailable for comment. But David Bradford, the company's general counsel, said that when the executives sold the shares, the merger "wasn't even a glimmer in anyone's eye." Mr. Bradford, who sold 6,250 shares, 87% of his Novell holdings, in March, added: "We we're hoping the stock would go up," on the news.

In general, Wall Street analysts did view the merger positively, arguing that if the two companies successfully merge, it could prove a formidable challenge to the industry dominance of Microsoft Corp. But investors -- worried that the merger may prove difficult, and disappointed that Novell holders don't get a premium under its proposed terms -- showed their displeasure.

According to Peter Troop, head of investor relations at the company, the announcement triggered a drop because Wall Street is "choosing to see it (the merger) as a sale." Under a letter of intent signed by the companies, Lotus will issue 1,19131 Lotus common shares for each Novell common share. Negotiations for the merger continue, he said, although a shareholders' lawsuit has been filed to block it, a suit the company contends is "without merit."

The two other Novell executives who sold shares, one of whom has since left the company, were unavailable for comment. Kanwal Rekhi, executive vice president of product development, sold 1,745 shares, retaining 135,648 shares, according to SEC filings. Ronald Eliason, formerly executive vice president and treasurer, sold all his holdings, 31,000 shares, for about $1.4 million. Their shares fetched prices ranging from $41.75 to $44.13 a share.

DROP IN THE BUCKET: Sam Walton, chairman of Wal-Mart Stores Inc. and one of the world's richest individuals, sold $1.9 million of the company's stock on March 19, SEC filings show.

But while the 40,920 shares he sold would be a lot for most people, Mr. Walton retains over 218 million shares in the company.

The shares sold for $46.81 a share, compared with the company's closing level yesterday of $48.25, down 37.5 cents in New York Stock Exchange Composite trading.

Also selling Wal-Mart shares in March were the company's chief operating officer and a director, each selling small portions of their Wal-Mart stakes, SEC filings show. In a transaction related to an earlier exercise of options, Donald G. Soderquist, the chief operating officer, sold 25,000 shares for $1.2 million, or 5% of his holdings. Jack C. Shewmaker sold 15,000 shares for $68,000, representing 2% of his stake. Their shares fetched prices ranging from $44.50 to $47.50, according to the SEC filings.

Mr. Soderquist declined to comment, while Mr. Walton and Mr. Shewmaker weren't available. The company declined to comment or to confirm the SEC data.

BUSY SIGNAL: Two brothers of McCaw Cellular Communications Inc. Chairman Craig McCaw sold almost $20 million of the company's stock, a month after its acquisition of LIN Broadcasting, according to SEC filings. As shown on the accompanying Insider Trading Spotlight, the two sold 625,367 shares in the Kirkland, Wash., cellular-phone-service company on April 6.

Since then, the price of McCaw Cellular's stock has fallen 18% in over-the-counter trading. The insiders sold at $31.25 a share, the filings show. The stock closed yesterday at $25.75, down 75 cents.

The brothers -- John E. McCaw Jr., executive vice president of acquisitions, and Bruce McCaw, a director -- declined to comment.

But a company spokesman noted that McCaw took on a great deal of debt as part of the LIN Broadcasting acquisition, which was completed March 5. The decline in the price of the stock has been part of "the market's disenchantment with leveraged companies," he said. He added that the sales were part of a prearranged private sale of a stake in McCaw to the BT USA subsidiary of British Telecommunications PLC.

John McCaw sold 469,025 shares, retaining a stake of 4,500 Class A common shares and 890,016 Class B common shares, SEC filings show. Bruce McCaw sold 156,342 Class A shares, virtually all his Class A holdings, but retains almost 12 million Class B common shares, according to the filings.
