Monsanto Profit Fell 13% in First Quarter; Chemical Lines Cited

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/25/90

ST.LOUIS -- Monsanto Co. said first-quarter profit fell 13% from a year-ago, to $194 million, or $2.95 a share. The report had been expected since last month, when the company said it expected lower corporate profit because of profit declines in its core chemical businesses.

A year earlier, Monsanto earned $222 million, or $3.24 a share. Sales in the latest quarter were $2.29 billion, up 1%, from $2.27 billion a year ago.

In the crop chemicals unit, pretax profit fell to $151 million, from the record $184 million of last year. The company said many shipments of its Lasso herbicide were delayed until the second quarter because of changes in farmers' purchasing patterns. In addition, the company said, the sales mix of its Roundup herbicides tilted toward the lower-priced formulations during the latest quarter.

Analysts have said that, because the timing of crop-chemical purchases varies, results for the first six months are a better indicator of how the business is doing than are results for the first quarter. Richard Mahoney, Monsanto's chairman and chief executive officer, said the company still expects profit from the crop-chemicals business in 1990 to exceed last year's, given "reasonable weather."

In the other chief chemicals unit, whose products include plastic for shatterproof windshields, pretax profit dropped to $119 million, from $130 million. Monsanto attributed the decline to lower production in the auto industry.

The other profit decline within Monsanto came in its NutraSweet Co. unit, which had pretax profit of $67 million, down from a record $74 million a year ago. In the G.D. Searle & Co. unit, pretax profit was $4 million, against a loss of $8 million a year ago.
