Japan's Oil Imports Increase

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/25/90

TOKYO -- Crude-oil imports to Japan in March rose at an annual rate of 3.8% to 123.1 million barrels, the Ministry of International Trade and Industry said. The imports also rose 1.5% from February.

Oil-products sales in March fell 1% to 126.4 million barrels, compared with a year earlier. Inventories rose 6.6% to 71 million barrels, and production increased 7.9% to 101.6 million barrels.

The effect of Japan's unusually warm winter could be seen in kerosene sales, which fell 14% from March 1989 levels to 18.8 million barrels.

Vehicle fuels showed the best sales increase in the month, up 5.4% at 23.6 million barrels. Sales of naphtha and gasoil each rose 4.9%. Sales of other products, including fuel oil and jet fuel, declined.

Product exports from Japan continued to climb in March, as the government held to its refinery liberalization policy. These exports more than tripled to 3.4 million barrels.
