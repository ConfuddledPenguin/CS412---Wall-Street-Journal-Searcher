Marketing & Media: Times Mirror Posts 33% Decline in Profit For the First Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/25/90

LOS ANGELES -- Times Mirror Co. said its first-quarter net income fell 33% because of depressed advertising revenue and higher costs stemming from circulation gains at the Los Angeles Times. Net income for the quarter was $46 million, or 36 cents a share. In the year-earlier quarter, Times Mirror reported net of $68.8 million, or 53 cents a share. Revenue for the latest quarter rose 3.3%, to $875.2 million from $846.9 million a year ago.

Robert F. Erburu, chairman and chief executive officer, said that, as expected, the company's first-quarter net was hurt by "the weak performance of our advertising-supported business lines," despite earnings growth in its professional publishing and cable television operations.

The newspaper publishing group's operating profit fell 38%, to $55.8 million, from $90.1 million. Increased revenue at the Los Angeles Times was offset by higher operating costs resulting from an increase in circulation, to a record 1.2 million daily and 1.5 million Sunday.

Separately, Times Mirror said the newspaper publishing group's ad revenue fell 0.2%, to $131.9 million, for the four weeks ended April 1. For the first 13 weeks of 1990, ad revenue fell 2%, to $413.9 million. The latest results included 13 Sundays, one fewer than a year ago.
