Santa Fe Pacific Plans Two Spinoffs By End of the Year

  • Author: James P. Miller
  • Date: 04/25/90

CHICAGO -- Santa Fe Pacific Corp. said it plans to spin off its real-estate and energy operations to shareholders by year end, with a target for completion "sometime in the third quarter." Yesterday's disclosure marks the first time the railroad, natural-resources and real-estate concern has formally declared it will spin off its energy operation, although such a move has been widely expected.

The news, announced at the company's annual meeting, also represents a speedup in the timetable for divestiture of the company's giant real-estate holdings.

The planned spinoffs are contingent on the company's refinancing of $780 million in payment-in-kind debentures issued in a 1988 recapitalization; a spokesman said the company doesn't expect "any major problems" in arranging the refinancing. In addition, the transactions are subject to receipt of regulators' rulings that they can be done on a tax-free basis.

In composite New York Stock Exchange trading yesterday, Santa Fe shares rose 5/8, to close at $21, on volume of about 821,000 shares.

Santa Fe's continuing corporate makeover had until last week been largely stalled by a lingering potential legal problem: Under a court ruling in an antitrust case, the company last year was ordered to pay a pipeline joint venture $750 million. But last week it settled the case for $350 million, clearing the way for further progress in its restructuring plans.

In both its real-estate and energy holdings, Santa Fe's strategy has been to sell minority stakes for cash to pay down its recapitalization debt, while maintaining the bulk of the "upside potential" for its shareholders. The company's stock has for some time traded more on investors' perceptions of its assets' value than on its operational earnings.

In December, Santa Fe sold a 20% stake in its Santa Fe Pacific Realty unit to a limited partnership made up of the California Public Employees Retirement System and JMB Realty Corp. The parent has since stressed that the $398 million it received for that one-fifth stake represented a discounted price, because the partnership brought needed cash and was an early investor. The company says it values the realty operation at $3 billion.

Until yesterday, the deadline for completing the spinoff of its remaining realty holdings had been the end of 1991. The real-estate operation, based in San Francisco, has about two million acres of developed and undeveloped land in 13 states, and its plans include -- if market conditions permit -- construction of nearly 27 million square feet of buildings over the next five years.

In March, Santa Fe Pacific took its Houston-based Santa Fe Energy Resources Inc. unit public, selling an 18.3% stake in the exploration and production concern for $213 million. At the end of 1989, the company said, the energy operation had proved reserves of 251 million barrels of oil equivalent, which includes natural-gas figures. Santa Fe Energy, which went public at $18.25 a share, closed in composite New York Stock Exchange trading yesterday at $16, up 1/8.

After the spinoffs, Santa Fe will comprise the Santa Fe Railway, with revenue last year of $2.2 billion; a mining operation that the company has signaled may itself one day be spun off; and a 44% stake in Santa Fe Pacific Pipeline Partners, a publicly traded limited partnership. The mining and pipeline holdings together contributed revenue of $159.8 million last year.
