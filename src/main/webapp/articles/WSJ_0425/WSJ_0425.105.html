International Brief -- Roche Holding AG: Swiss Firm's Profit for 1989 Rose 33% to $570.4 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/25/90

Roche Holding AG, a large Swiss chemical and pharmaceutical concern, said its 1989 consolidated profit surged 33% to 852 million Swiss francs ($570.4 million) from 642 million francs in 1988, on a previously reported 21% jump in sales to 9.81 billion francs from 8.14 billion francs. Roche cited robust sales growth, a favorable economic environment, the relative weakness of the Swiss franc and "liquid fund investments."

The company said it will propose a dividend of 38 francs a share on 1989's results, or a total of 157 million francs, compared with 118 million francs paid out on 1988's results. The proposed payout can't be compared directly with that for 1988 because of a 1989 capital restructuring, Roche said.

Cash flow, a barometer of a Swiss company's performance, jumped 23% in 1989 to 1.45 billion francs, or 15% of sales. Research and development spending totaled 1.42 billion francs, or about 14% of sales. Pharmaceuticals accounted for about 45% of 1989 sales; vitamins and fine chemicals, 25%; diagnostics, 14%; fragrances and flavors, 11%; and plant protection, 5%.
