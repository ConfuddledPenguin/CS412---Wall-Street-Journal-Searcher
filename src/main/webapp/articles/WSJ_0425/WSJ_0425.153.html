Business Brief -- Equitable Resources Inc.: Utility-Holding Firm Says First-Quarter Net Fell 10%

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/25/90

Equitable Resources Inc. said it expects to report that earnings for the first quarter fell 10%, but it anticipates full-year earnings to grow. In a speech to New York security analysts, Donald I. Moritz, president of the Pittsburgh utility-holding company, attributed the decline to unusually warm weather. The company earned $1.14 a share a year earlier.

Earnings for 1990 are expected to exceed year-ago net of $2.24 a share. The company didn't elaborate.

Mr. Moritz said he expects improvements for the year because of increased production of natural gas, oil and liquids; continued gains in sales of gas and services by the company's interstate pipeline unit; and the strengthening economy of its retail distribution area.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Equitable shares fell 50 cents to close at $35.125.
