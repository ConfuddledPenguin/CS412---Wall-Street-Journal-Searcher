Business Brief -- Chips & Technologies Inc.: Quarterly Profit Falls 47% On a Revenue Rise of 16%

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/25/90

Chips & Technologies Inc., citing a sluggish market and a shortage of chips, said net income for the fiscal third quarter plunged 47% to $5 million, or 32 cents a share, from $9.4 million, or 63 cents a share. Revenue in the quarter ended March 31 climbed 16% to $66.4 million from $57.4 million.

The drop in earnings wasn't unexpected. The San Jose, Calif., microchip manufacturer said several weeks ago that its performance in the third quarter would be significantly below that of its second quarter ended Dec. 31, when the company reported revenue of $74.4 million and net income of $8.9 million, or 57 cents ashare.

The company's stock fell 62.5 cents yesterday in national over-the-counter trading to close at $16.75 a share.

The company said its more mature products continued to be affected by pricing pressures in the sluggish computer industry. The price declines on certain products were exacerbated by a delay in the introduction of several new-generation products, the company added.
