Indicator of Japan's Economy

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/25/90

TOKYO -- The leading indicator of Japan's economy increased to 50, the so-called boom-or-bust line, in February, the latest reporting month, and government officials predicted that the economy will continue to expand. The Economic Planning Agency, which announced the indicator, considers a reading above the line of 50 as a sign of future economic expansion, and a reading below that level as an indication of possible contraction ahead.

The February reading represented a pick-up from a revised 40.9 in January.

Earlier in the day, EPA Director-General Hideyuki Aizawa reported to the cabinet that Japan's economy continues to expand.

Mr. Aizawa's April report meant that the country's economy has continued to expand for 41 months in a row, almost matching the second-longest, post-war expansion of 42 months, which was set between June 1958 and December 1961.
