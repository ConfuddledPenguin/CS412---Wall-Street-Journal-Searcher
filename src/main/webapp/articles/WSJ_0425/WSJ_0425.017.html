South Korea's GNP Grows

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/25/90

SEOUL, South Korea -- Despite sluggish exports, South Korea's gross national product grew an estimated 7.1% in the first quarter, compared with 6.2% a year earlier, the Bank of Korea, the central bank, said. The recovery in growth was attributed mainly to brisk investment in production facilities. The central bank predicted 7% growth in the GNP, the value of the nation's output of goods and services, for all of 1990, on the strength of expected brisk investment in production facilities and active production activities in the manufacturing sector.

The bank also said the country's current account showed a deficit of US$1.1 billion for the first quarter, compared with a year-earlier surplus of US$1.3 billion. But it said the current account is expected to post a surplus of US$1.2 billion for 1990, though this would be significantly narrowed from a 1989 surplus of US$5.1 billion.
