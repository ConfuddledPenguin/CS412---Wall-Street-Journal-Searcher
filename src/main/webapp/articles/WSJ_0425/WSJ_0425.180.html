International: Axa-Midi Weighs Six U.S. Insurers In Purchase Plan

  • Author: E.S. Browning
  • Date: 04/25/90

PARIS -- A day after the failure of its effort to acquire Farmers Group Inc., France's Axa-Midi Assurances says it already is beginning to look at six other possible U.S. insurance acquisitions. Officials at Axa-Midi, one of France's largest insurance companies, say that by selling real estate and unneeded subsidiaries they could raise as much as $8 billion for future acquisitions.

They had anticipated paying $4.5 billion for Farmers, part of a broader effort by Sir James Goldsmith's Hoylake Investments Ltd. to acquire B.A.T Industries Inc., Farmers' parent. Axa-Midi officials say they could pay more than $4.5 billion for another concern, although they currently don't plan to use the entire $8 billion on a single purchase. They say they have acquisition hopes in Europe and Southeast Asia as well.

Axa-Midi Chairman Claude Bebear said he currently is looking at four property and casualty insurance companies and two life insurance companies in the U.S. They were presented to him while he was touring the U.S. trying to convince regulators to approve the Farmers acquisition. Unlike Farmers, all would be friendly purchases.

"In one case, a company chairman came to me and said his capital was broadly dispersed," Mr. Bebear said. "He said he fears a raid and prefers to choose his acquirer for himself. In another case, a company chairman told me his current parent intends to sell his company, and he asked whether I would be interested."

An acquisition the size of Farmers would make Axa-Midi the largest French insurance group, and the first one with such a strong U.S. presence.
