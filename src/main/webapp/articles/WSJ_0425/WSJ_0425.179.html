International: Two Big Japanese Banks Face Review by Moody's

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 04/25/90

TOKYO -- Moody's Investors Service Inc. said it will review for possible downgrades its triple-A ratings on long-term deposits and senior debt of Fuji Bank Ltd. and Dai-Ichi Kangyo Bank Ltd., and senior debt of their units. But the rating concern said downgrades, if any, aren't likely to be substantial for either bank; their short-term prime-1 ratings aren't affected.

"Fuji remains a formidable and well-managed bank," Moody's said, but cited "Fuji's tolerance for interest-rate risk, the bank's real estate-related exposures, which are among the largest of Japan's top city banks, and Fuji's disproportionate exposure to highly leveraged transactions in the U.S." It said the "review will focus on the factors contributing to Fuji's deteriorating operating performance, as well as examine in more detail the bank's exposure to riskier borrowers." Fuji had nonconsolidated assets of 55.7 trillion yen ($353.31 billion) last Sept. 30, Moody's said.

Regarding Dai-Ichi, about $1.4 billion in long-term debt is affected by Moody's review. Dai-Ichi's "overhead ratio is chronically the highest among its peers, making the bank more vulnerable to an earnings squeeze when rapidly rising interest rates narrow the bank's margins," Moody's said; also, "the bank's real estate-related loans have expanded faster than those of its peers."

Moody's said it will focus on Dai-Ichi's efforts to improve operating efficiency under rising interest rates and its exposure to real estate lending. Though Moody's regards the bank's risk level to have increased somewhat, it said it believes Dai-Ichi retains its solid domestic franchise and customer loyalty.
