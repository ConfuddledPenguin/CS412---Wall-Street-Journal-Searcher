Florida to Begin Spraying For Fruit Fly Protection

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/25/90

OPA LOCKA, Fla. -- State agriculture officials said they plan to begin spraying parts of south Florida to combat a possible infestation of Mediterranean fruit flies. Officials said yesterday that they had discovered a fifth Medfly in Dade County. The first fly was discovered about a week ago. The flies are a potentially serious threat to the state's extensive citrus crops.

The state plans to fight the flies in two ways: aerial spraying of malathion and the release of millions of sterile flies. The sterile flies mate with the wild Medflies to produce non-fertile eggs.

While discussing the sprayings, Doyle Conner, the state's commissioner of agriculture, said, "In spite of the recent adverse publicity in California, there is overwhelming scientific evidence that aerial application of malathion doesn't pose any significant public health concern."

Officials said they will widely publicize dates and times of the sprayings.
