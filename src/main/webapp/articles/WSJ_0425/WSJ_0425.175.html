CD Yields Rose At Banks, Brokers In Latest Week

  • Author: Ellen E. Schultz
  • Date: 04/25/90

Yields on certificates of deposit at major banks and brokerage firms rose across the board -- pushing past the 9% level for a five-year CD at one major bank. The increases in the week ended yesterday coincided with a sharp rise in market interest rates that has pushed yields on longer-term Treasury securities up to nearly 9%.

The biggest increases in CD yields were among small-denomination CDs sold by leading brokerage firms. The average yield on one-year broker-sold CDs rose to 8.50% from 8.19%, while the five-year-CD average rose to 8.85% from 8.57%, according to figures compiled by Banxquote Money Markets, a New York information service.

Average yields for most large-denomination CDs, which typically require deposits of $90,000 or more, were also up significantly. The greatest gains were in the longer-term maturities. Average yields on two-year jumbo CDs were 8.43%, while five-year maturities were 8.54%. In New York, Chase Manhattan Bank raised the yield on its five-year CD to 9.17%, one of the highest in the country.

"Nine percent is a psychological resistance level," said Banxquote Chairman Norberto Mehl. "Once one of the leading banks has crossed this level, other banks are expected to follow."

Mr. Mehl said that some of the leading brokers, such as Shearson Lehman Hutton and Dean Witter Reynolds, are also offering CDs that are approaching the 9% level.

Meanwhile, average yields on small-denomination CDs at 18 banks surveyed by Banxquote rose only slightly. The average yield on a one-year consumer CD rose to 8.02% from 7.96%, while five-year consumer CDs offered 8.20%, up from 8.16%.
