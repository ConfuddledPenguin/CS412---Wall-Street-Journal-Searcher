Business Brief -- Melville Corp.: First-Quarter Net Fell 68% Largely Due to Late Easter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/25/90

Melville Corp., Rye, N.Y., said first-quarter earnings plunged 68%, largely because the Easter selling period falls in the second quarter this year. Melville, a specialty retailer of apparel, footwear, prescription drugs and other businesses, said net income fell to $7.8 million, or four cents a share, from $24.2 million, or 22 cents a share, a year earlier. Sales rose 5.8% to $1.62 billion from $1.53 billion.

Melville noted that the Palm Sunday and Easter week selling periods occurred in the second quarter of 1990 but in the first quarter of 1989.

The company also noted that it issued preference stock last June on which the quarterly dividend is $3.9 million; the year earlier per-share figure didn't reflect a preference stock dividend.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading, Melville fell 50 cents $45.25.
