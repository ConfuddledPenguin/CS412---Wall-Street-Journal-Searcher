What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/25/90

MICHAEL MILKEN PLEADED guilty to six felony counts, as expected, capping a three-year probe of securities fraud and other crimes by Drexel's former junk bond chief. Milken, once the world's most-powerful financier, also agreed to pay $600 million in fines and restitution and to cooperate with the continuing federal inquiry. He will be sentenced Oct. 1 and faces up to 28 years in prison. ---

Sears Roebuck's profit plunged 59% in the first quarter due to "unsatisfactory" results in its core retail business and hefty claims at its Allstate insurance unit. Sears stock slid $1.125, to $35.375, a 52-week low.

---

IBM will introduce several low-cost home computers this summer, making a fresh assault on a market where it has done poorly in the past.

---

RJR Nabisco posted a $222 million loss for the first quarter, reflecting huge debt costs. But the food and tobacco giant showed strong gains in operating earnings and cash flow.

---

Western Union said it will file a recapitalization plan involving an exchange offer of new secured debt and common stock. The company is trying to avoid a Chapter 11 filing.

---

Durable goods orders surged 6.7% in March, prompting some analysts to suggest manufacturing may be rebounding. The rise, reflecting a jump in transporation-equipment orders, was the biggest in more than a year.

Sales of U.S.-built cars and trucks plunged 17.7% in mid-April, a poor performance in what is usually the heart of the spring selling season.

---

The film industry and other producers of copyrighted material were dealt a blow by the Supreme Court, which ruled the re-release of "Rear Window" violated copyright law.

---

Quaker Oats decided to spin off its troubled Fisher-Price toy business, allowing management to focus on problems in its foods business.

---

Salomon and Morgan Stanley had stronger quarterly results, mainly due to bond-trading windfalls. But the securities firms didn't escape the weakness in investment banking.

---

Ames Department Stores stocks and bonds tumbled further amid growing speculation the chain may seek Chapter 11 protection.

---

The nation's mortgage lenders were accused by seven states of violating federal law by requiring homeowners to place more money than necessary in escrow accounts.

---

Short interest fell 1.3% among major OTC stocks for the month ended April 12, while borrowed shares in smaller issues rose 28.3%.

---

Five oil companies posted mixed quarterly results that reflected one-time items and higher crude prices.

---

Labor costs rose 1.6% in the first quarter, fueled by higher Social Security taxes and other benefits. Costs rose 1.1% in the previous quarter.
