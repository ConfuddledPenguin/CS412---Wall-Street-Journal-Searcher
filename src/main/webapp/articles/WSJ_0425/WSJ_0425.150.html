Business Brief -- Hudson Foods Inc.: Fiscal 2nd-Period Net Fell 47%; Turkey Losses Cited

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/25/90

Hudson Foods Inc., Rogers, Ark., said net income for its second quarter ended March 31 fell 47% to $2.1 million, or 15 cents a share, from $4 million, or 31 cents a share, a year earlier, largely because of losses on turkey sales. Sales increased 2.7% to $146 million from $142.2 million.

But Hudson's latest earnings represent a significant improvement from its first-quarter results, largely because of improvements in broiler prices.

Other poultry producers, including Tyson Foods Inc. and Pilgrim's Pride Corp., have posted sharply higher earnings for the March 31 quarter, because profit margins on chickens improved from a year earlier. But those companies aren't in the turkey business, where retail prices haven't covered costs of growing and processing the birds. Of Hudson's 740 million pounds of poultry production, about 100 million pounds is from turkeys.

Hudson's first-half profit fell 59% to $2.3 million, or 16 cents a share, from $5.6 million, or 43 cents a share, a year earlier. Sales increased 6.2% to $295.4 million from $278.2 million.
