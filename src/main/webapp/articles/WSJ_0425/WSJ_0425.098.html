Allied-Signal Venture With GE to Produce Engines for Dassault

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/25/90

MORRIS TOWNSHIP, N.J. -- Allied-Signal Inc. said its aircraft-engine joint venture with General Electric Co. will produce engines for a new business jet built by Dassault, the French airplane maker. Deliveries of engines, spare parts and service for the plane are expected to generate $1 billion in revenue for the 50-50 joint venture over the life of the program, an Allied-Signal spokesman said.

Allied-Signal said the turbofan engine that will power the plane is under development by the partnership, CFE Co., formed by Allied-Signal's Garrett Engine division and GE's Aircraft Engines division.

A Dassault spokesman said deliveries of the new twin-engine eight-passenger plane, the Falcon 2000, will begin in 1994. Dassault expects to sell "several hundred" of the aircraft over the following 15 to 20 years.

Allied-Signal also makes automotive and other aerospace products and engineered materials. GE, Fairfield, Conn., has interests in aerospace, defense and industrial products, broadcasting and financial services.
