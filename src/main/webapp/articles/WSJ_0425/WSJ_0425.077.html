Tuesday's Markets: Rate Fears Force Stocks Down Again --- Bonds Finish Steady After Drop on Data; Dollar Closes Mixed

  • Author: Craig Torres
  • Date: 04/25/90

NEW YORK -- For the second day in a row, interest rate worries sank stock prices but left bonds about unchanged. The dollar closed mixed against the major currencies. The Dow Jones Industrial Average fell 12.17 to 2654.50, the average's fifth consecutive decline. Long-term Treasury bonds, which zig-zagged higher and lower during the day, closed nearly unchanged with the yield on the benchmark 30-year bond at 8.96%.

A bigger-than-expected increase in durable goods orders in March initially caused bonds to fall and stocks to rise. But the reaction to the data subsided as analysts came to view the orders gain as simply offsetting declines in January and February.

Bond yields will probably hit 9% before falling below 8.5%, analysts predict, and stock investors just can't get those higher yields out of mind. Nothing seems like a "safe" investment these days. For example, Compaq Computer, which analysts expect to report slightly higher earnings tomorrow saw nervous investors walk away from the stock yesterday, pushing it down more than 3 at one point; it closed down 1 7/8 at 94 5/8.

As a result of that kind of queasiness, declining issues again stepped ahead of advancing issues on the New York Stock Exchange by 833 to 652. "All trends are down; broader averages are masking the declines in the broad market," said Paul Lesutis, director of Provident Capital Management. "There is no real evidence that stocks are bottoming."

Mr. Lesutis and other investors think stocks are too high-priced to compete with the yields on Treasury securities. Indeed, small investors are finding Treasury bond yields attractive, says Douglas McAllister, vice president at Prudential-Bache Securities Inc. Last month, Prudential-Bache's Treasury order desk for small investors had the most business in its history, Mr. McAllister says, and the pace hasn't slackened since. "Many small investors are not content with consumer deposit" yields, Mr. McAllister says.

In major market activity:

Stocks finished lower. Volume on the New York Stock Exchange totaled 137,360,000 shares.

Bonds were unchanged. Prices on short-term Treasury notes were down less than $1 for each $1,000 face amount.

The dollar finished mixed. In late New York trading, the dollar was quoted at 1.6980 marks and 158.93 yen compared with 1.6995 marks and 157.60 yen late Monday.
