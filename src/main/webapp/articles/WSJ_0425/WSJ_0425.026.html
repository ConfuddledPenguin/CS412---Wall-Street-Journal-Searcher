GDP in Malaysia Estimated

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/25/90

KUALA LUMPUR, Malaysia -- Undercutting a rosier government forecast, the Malaysian Institute of Economic Research predicted the nation's gross domestic product will grow 7.8% in 1990 but the government-assisted, structurally independent institute cautioned that building inflation will force long-term interest rates higher. The assessment jibes more closely with the views of private analysts and economists than Bank Negara's annual report released in March. The institute's GDP forecast also represents a substantial deceleration from the 8.5% rate in 1989 and is more modest than the central bank's forecast of 8.3%. It estimated the inflation rate this year will hit 5%, exceeding Bank Negara's forecast of a 4% rise in the consumer price index, and topping by far the 1989 increase of 2.8%.
