Environmental Concerns Attached to Lease Offerings

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/25/90

WASHINGTON -- The Interior Department, fighting for congressional support for new offshore oil drilling, is promising to pay more attention to environmental considerations in offering leases. The department said it wants to allow drilling only in areas where doing so wouldn't harm marine life and where there is a good chance of finding oil. The policy would mark a departure from the Reagan administration's approach of making vast offshore areas available, only to have Congress deny money to carry out the leasing program in many of them.

The proposal is to be included in a new five-year plan for the Outer Continental Shelf that would take effect when the current plan expires in 1992. A draft plan is expected to be available for public comment in the next couple of months.

President Bush is expected soon to announce a long-awaited decision on whether he will allow certain lease sales to proceed off Florida and California. An Interior Department spokesman said the agency would incorporate the president's decision and other guidance from the White House into its draft plan.

Interior Secretary Manuel Lujan is also considering proposing that local governments be allowed to share in the revenue from oil lease sales. Currently, some revenue is shared with the states. A report on the Interior Department's proposals appeared in yesterday's New York Times.
