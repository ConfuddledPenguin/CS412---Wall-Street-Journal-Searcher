Business Brief -- General Motors Corp.: Joint Venture With Fiat Dissolved, Dispute Settled

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/25/90

General Motors Corp., Detroit, and Fiat S.p.A. of Italy have dissolved their six-year partnership in an automated factory tooling venture, and settled a dispute that arose out of the venture's work tooling up two of GM's U.S. car plants. As part of the transaction, GM has sold back to Fiat its 20% stake in the venture, Comau Productivity Systems Inc. of Troy, Mich. The companies wouldn't disclose the amount Fiat paid to GM for its Comau Productivity Systems shares. Comau Productivity Systems will now operate as a unit of Fiat's U.S. arm, the companies said.

In addition, GM and the Fiat unit said they settled a dispute that arose over the cost of tooling installed by Comau at GM's Linden, N.J., and Wilmington, Del., car factories. The terms of the settlement weren't disclosed.
