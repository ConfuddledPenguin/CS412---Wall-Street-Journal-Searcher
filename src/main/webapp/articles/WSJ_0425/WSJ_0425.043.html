West German CD Sales Soar

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/25/90

HAMBURG, West Germany -- West Germans spent a record 3.10 billion marks ($1.82 billion) on records, tapes and compact disks in 1989, a West German association said. The West German Association of Phonograph Economics said 56.9 million CDs were sold, up 46% from 1988. There were 58.1 million cassettes sold and 32.3 million singles sold. The association didn't say how many LPs were sold in 1989.
