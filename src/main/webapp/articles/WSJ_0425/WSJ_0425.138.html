Business Brief -- Piper Jaffray Inc.: Second-Period Net Fell 19%, While Revenue Grew 4.9%

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/25/90

Piper Jaffray Inc., citing swelling expenses and a downturn in some operations, said earnings fell 19% in its second quarter ended March 30, despite a 4.9% increase in revenue. The Minneapolis parent company of the investment firm of Piper, Jaffray & Hopwood Inc. said it earned $2.1 million, or 52 cents a share, compared to the year-earlier $2.6 million, or 65 cents a share. Revenue rose to $49.5 million, from $47.2 million.

Second-quarter earnings "were less than we had expected," said Charles N. Hayssen, chief financial officer. "We simply let certain expenses get slightly ahead of revenues." He said the company intends "to correct that in the next six months."

The investment firm said commission revenue climbed about 40% from last year, and trading profits are up about 15%. Asset management fees also are up about 40%, reflecting the growth of the company's Piper Capital Management unit. The company's investment banking business is down about 20%, "which is true industrywide," Mr. Hayssen said.
