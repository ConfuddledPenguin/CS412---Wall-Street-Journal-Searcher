Thermo Instrument To Acquire Finnigan For Up to $120 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/25/90

WALTHAM, Mass. -- Thermo Instrument Systems Inc. said it agreed to acquire Finnigan Corp. for $20 a share or $110 million to $120 million. Finnigan, which Thermo called the world's largest maker of mass spectrometers, has been unprofitable in the face of competitive pricing pressures. In over-the-counter trading yesterday, Finnigan surged $4.50 to $18.75. Thermo Instrument, an 80%-owned subsidiary of Thermo Electron Corp., rose 12.5 cents to $14.375. Thermo Instrument makes instruments for environmental testing.

In the first quarter, Finnigan, San Jose, Calif., had a net loss of $1.1 million, or 20 cents a share, on sales of $30.3 million.

Arvin Smith, president of Thermo Instrument, said that for the first quarter his company expects to report net income rose almost 30% to about $4.1 million, or 17 cents a share, from a restated $3.2 million or 16 cents a share. The number of shares outstanding increased to 24 million from a restated 19.7 million to reflect the conversion of debentures. Sales rose 19% to about $49 million from a restated $41.2 million, he said.

The Finnigan acquisition would be Thermo Instrument's second consecutive purchase of a struggling company. In January, Thermo Instrument completed the stock acquisition of Thermo Environmental Corp., another Thermo Electron affiliate.

Thermo Instrument's main business is making instruments used to test and monitor air, water and nuclear pollution sources. That business has been growing strongly as corporations increasingly worry about toughened pollution regulations. Thermo Environmental, whose sales account for about 30% of the combined company, runs laboratories that conduct such tests.

The Finnigan acquisition is subject to approval by each board, execution of a definitive acquisition agreement, and receipt of regulatory and other approvals.
