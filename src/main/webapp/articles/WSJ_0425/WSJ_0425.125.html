International Brief -- Hoechst AG: German Chemical Company Raises Dividend to Holders

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/25/90

Hoechst AG joined other major West German chemical companies by boosting its annual cash dividend for 1989 to 13 Deutsche marks ($7.64) a share from the 12 marks a share paid for the previous year. Earlier in the day, Bayer AG announced a similar dividend boost to 13 marks a share, while the last of the big three West German chemical groups, BASF AG, said last week it would boost its regular cash payout by a mark to 13 marks and distribute a special one-mark cash payout to holders to celebrate the company's 125th anniversary.

Hoechst also said group net profit in 1989 rose 5.7% to 2.13 billion marks from 2.02 billion marks in 1988. Group sales rose 12% to 45.9 billion marks from 40.96 billion marks.

Bayer's group after-tax profit last year rose 11% to 2.12 billion marks from 1.91 billion marks.
