Letters to the Editor: Double Standard

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/25/90

You say in the April 2 Washington Insight column that "the U.S. administration has declined to recognize Lithuania's declaration of independence, despite urgent requests from Vilnius and a 40-year U.S. policy of refusing to recognize the Soviet annexation of Lithuania and the other Baltic republics." You should note, however, that the U.S. still extends diplomatic recognition to the representatives of the old independent republics of Lithuania, Latvia and Estonia. While the White House may be hedging its bets on Lithuania, officially it still considers the Soviet Baltic republics to be illegally occupied territories that are entitled to full independence.

Bradford Trebach

New York
