Business Brief -- McKesson Corp.: Company's 4th-Quarter Net Drops 28% to $18.3 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/25/90

McKesson Corp., blaming a decline in profit at a unit and a $20 million pretax provision for customer accounts, said fourth-quarter net dropped 28%, to $18.3 million, or 40 cents a share, from $25.4 million, or 61 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue for the March 31 quarter rose 11% to $2.1 billion, from $1.9 billion.

The San Francisco-based drug distribution company said the decision to increase reserves for customer accounts reflected concern over the weakened financial condition of some of its retail customers.

The rise in reserves is reflected principally in the results of McKesson's drug distribution business, with lesser amounts charged against the service merchandising segment and Armor All Products. Profit at the 83%-owned Armor car-care products unit was disappointing, the company said, in part because the company curtailed special year-end promotions.

For the year, net slipped nearly 2%, to $98.7 million, or $2.23 a share, from $100.6 million, or $2.38 a share, in fiscal 1989. Revenue rose almost 11% to $7.8 billion from $7 billion.
