Business Brief -- Canadian Express Ltd.: Bid for Canadian Express Common Stock Is Deferred

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/25/90

Hees International Bancorp, a merchant banking concern controlled by Toronto's Bronfman family that has a 40% voting stake in Canadian Express Ltd., said it deferred its offer to buy as many as 40 million common shares of the Toronto-based industrial holding company for 65 Canadian cents (56 U.S. cents) each, pending further regulatory review. In Toronto Stock Exchange trading yesterday, Canadian Express common closed at 52 Canadian cents, up one cent.

The Ontario Securities Commission on Monday withdrew an exemption from certain regulatory requirements that it had granted to the Hees bid, which was made March 23. The commission believed Hees should have referred in its exemption application to a fairness opinion prepared by Wood Gundy Inc. in May 1989, Hees said. Hees has asked Wood Gundy to update that opinion for the commission.

The proposed bid would raise Hees's voting stake in Canadian Express to about 56%; the 40% includes a 7% in common stock and 33% in preferred stock.
