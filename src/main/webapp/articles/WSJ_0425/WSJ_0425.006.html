Letters to the Editor: Don't Get Caught With Britches Down

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/25/90

Conventional wisdom errs in concluding Mikhail Gorbachev would not use Red military power to "save the union" because of "fear of destroying his international reputation as a man of peace" ("Tense War of Nerves in Lithuania May Be Backfiring for Gorbachev," March 26). Mr. Gorbachev's political base is the Russian nationality. The percentage of Russians living in the 14 republics excluding the Russian Soviet Federated Socialist Republic ranges from 2% in Armenia to 41% in Kazakhstan, where Russians outnumber native Kazakhs. Latvia is one-third inhabited by Russians, and 28% of the Estonian population is Russian. On average, 17% of the 14 non-Russian republics is Russian.

The percentage of Russians living as inhabitants rather than occupiers in what once was called Eastern Europe is nearly zero. Predicting Mr. Gorbachev's behavior in Lithuania, Latvia or Moldavia on his past behavior in Eastern Europe is a false analogy. He had no nationalistic political commitment to the Poles, Czechs, etc., but he has a deep, back-to-the-czars political commitment to the Russian nationals living outside the Russian S.F.S.R. These were sent as colonizers to the other republics, first by the czars and then by the Communists. If Mr. Gorbachev betrays them, is it possible to believe he could retain the political support of his power base, the Russians living in Russian S.F.S.R.?

Conversations with Russians in Tallinn, Tbilisi, Alma-Ata, Tashkent, Leningrad and Moscow convey the same message: "We Russians made great sacrifices to industrialize and raise the standard of living of the other republics. Now they are out to ditch us. No way."

Instead of mouthing pieties, the West and especially the Bush administration should prepare for bloodshed rather than being caught with their britches at half-mast as happened at Tiananmen Square.

Barton S. Pulling

Visiting Assistant Professor

Political Science

Idaho State University

Pocatello, Idaho
