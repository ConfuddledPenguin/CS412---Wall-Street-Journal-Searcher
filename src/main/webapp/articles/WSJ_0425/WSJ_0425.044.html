Who's News: J.P. Morgan's de Las Heras To Join Banco Santander

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/25/90

NEW YORK -- J.P. Morgan & Co.'s head of Latin America, Gonzalo de Las Heras, resigned to take a senior job at Banco Santander, Spain's second-largest bank. Mr. de Las Heras, 50 years old, will work on the bank's international strategy, reporting to Chairman Emilio Botin. In a statement, Mr. de Las Heras said, "I'm excited about the scope of the projects Santander has asked me to tackle."

The Spanish-born Mr. de Las Heras joined Morgan in Madrid in 1978 and soon rose to head its operations there. After later moving to New York, Mr. de Las Heras played an instrumental role in Mexico's initial debt-for-securities swap arranged by Morgan in 1988.
