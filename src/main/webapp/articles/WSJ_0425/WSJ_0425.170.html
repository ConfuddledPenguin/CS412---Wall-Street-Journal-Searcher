Business Brief -- San Diego Gas & Electric Co.: Utility Firm Posts Increase Of 56% in 1st-Quarter Net

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/25/90

San Diego Gas & Electric Co. said first-quarter net income jumped 56%, in part because year-earlier earnings were depressed by a special charge. The utility holding company reported net before the payment of preferred dividends of $56.1 million, or 95 cents a share, compared with $35.9 million, or 59 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue rose 8% to $539.5 million from $499.4 million.

In the 1989 first quarter, California's Public Utilities Commission disallowed the company $25 million of costs to import power over the Southwest Powerlink.

Thomas A. Page, chairman, president and chief executive officer, also attributed the earnings improvement to "reduced utility operating and maintenance costs and improved earnings of our subsidiaries."
