Dividend News: Schering-Plough Sets 2-for-1 Split, Boosts Dividend

  • Author: Robert Daniels
  • Date: 04/25/90

Schering-Plough Corp. directors declared a 2-for-1 stock split and lifted the quarterly dividend 24%, while Warner Lambert Co. holders approved an increase in authorized shares to effect a previously announced 2-for-1 split. At Schering, the moves reflect "several consecutive years of outstanding earnings growth and good business performance," said Robert P. Luciano, chairman and chief executive officer of the Madison, N.J., pharmaceuticals concern.

The split is payable May 30 to stock of record May 4. The dividend increase, to 56 cents a share from 45 cents, is payable May 21 to stock of record May 4. At March 31, Schering had 113.3 million shares outstanding.

Shareholders at Schering's annual meeting also rejected two shareholder proposals: that the company stop doing business in South Africa, and that it report annually to holders on the scope of its use of animals in testing products and on its efforts to stop using animals for testing.

Warner-Lambert reiterated a projection that earnings per share will rise at least 16% in 1990, adjusted for the split. In 1989, Warner-Lambert earned $6.10 a share.

Shareholders at the annual meeting of the Morris Plains, N.J., health-care and consumer products company approved a doubling of authorized common, to 300 million shares. Warner-Lambert will pay the split on or about May 16 to stock of record May 2. Warner-Lambert has about 67 million shares outstanding.

Warner-Lambert holders also defeated shareholder proposals that the company quit operating in South Africa and implement cumulative voting for directors. Under cumulative voting, each shareholder has a number of votes equal to the number of shares he or she owns times the number of directors up for election. A shareholder may allot the votes among the director candidates in any way he or she wishes.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Schering shares rose 25 cents to $80.125, while Warner-Lambert's fell $1 to $105.75.
