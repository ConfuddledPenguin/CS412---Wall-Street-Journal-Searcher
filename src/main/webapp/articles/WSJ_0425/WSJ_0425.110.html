Five Firms Seek Software For Artificial Intelligence

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/25/90

NEW YORK -- Five companies announced a joint project to develop improved software technology for managing knowledge with expert systems, a branch of artificial intelligence. Carnegie Group Inc., a Pittsburgh-based software company, joined together with electronics giants Digital Equipment Corp. and Texas Instruments Inc., auto maker Ford Motor Co. and U S West Inc., a telephone company. The group said they are investing a total of $10 million over five years in what they called the Initiative for Managing Knowledge.

Dennis Yablonsky, president of Carnegie Group, said the project aims to devise a new technique for representing knowledge in computers that will let different computers and different software programs incorporate the knowledge. Unlike current expert systems, the knowledge wouldn't be based on a single expert's expertise, nor would it be in a self-contained system, he said.

David Fawcett, expert systems manager in Ford's electronics division, said that eventually the technology might allow computerized design systems laying out printed circuit boards in Dearborn, Mich., to check a knowledge base of manufacturing plants to see if a design would pose any problem.

The group said it had begun working two years ago and plans to finish the first phase late this year. It said group members will be free to use the technology or sell it to others.
