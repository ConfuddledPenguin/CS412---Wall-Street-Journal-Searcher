International Brief -- Midland Bank PLC: Warning on Profit Issued; Effect of High Rates Cited

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/25/90

Sir Kit McMahon, chairman of Midland Bank PLC, issued a profit warning, saying high U.K. interest rates are raising prospects for trouble with the bank's domestic loan portfolio. Sir Kit, speaking at the annual general shareholders' meeting, didn't provide an exact profit forecast. But he said that profit is "currently running well below the level achieved in the very different conditions prevalent in the first half of last year."

Midland reported first-half 1989 pretax profit of #531 million ($868.2 million), up from #313 million in the year-earlier period. It posted a pretax loss of #261 million for all of 1989, largely as the result of extensive loan-loss provisioning for advances to Third World nations.

"With the {British} government seeing the need to keep interest rates high, the deterioration in trading conditions is continuing and indeed intensifying," Sir Kit said.

He noted "lower overall asset growth and a further weakening of margins" are helping to eat into Midland's net interest income.
