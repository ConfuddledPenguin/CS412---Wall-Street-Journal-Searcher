Canam Manac Inc. Two Agencies to Buy Rest of Noverco Inc.

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/25/90

MONTREAL -- Canam Manac Inc. and two Quebec government agencies said they agreed in principle to pool their holdings in Noverco Inc. and to offer 15.25 Canadian dollars (US$13.14) a share, or about C$218 million, for the 27% of Noverco they don't already own. Canam Manac currently holds about 44% of Noverco and the two Quebec agencies together hold about 29% of the Montreal energy concern. Noverco's main operating unit is Gaz Metropolitain, Quebec's dominant natural gas distributor.

Trading was halted in Canam Manac and Noverco shares Friday after a news report that an agreement was reached. On the Toronto Stock Exchange Noverco last traded at C$12.625 and Canam Manac last traded at C$4.60. Canam Manac is a St. Georges, Quebec, steel products concern.

Canam Manac, Caisse de Depot et Placement, the Quebec government's pension-fund agency, and Soquip, the province's energy agency, said they will form a new holding company to hold their Noverco shares and make the tender offer. They said Canam Manac will own 50% of the holding company and the agencies 25% each.

The three concerns said the agreement is subject to regulatory approval. They said that as part of the agreement Caisse de Depot and Soquip hold an irrevocable option to acquire Canam Manac's Noverco shares. The option can be exercised on three month's notice between July 1, 1991, and March 31, 1993. Canam Manac holds an irrevocable option to sell its Noverco shares on the same terms.
