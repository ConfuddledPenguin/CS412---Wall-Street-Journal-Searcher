Business Brief -- Amdahl Corp.: First-Quarter Net Fell 34%; Competitive Pricing Is Cited

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/25/90

Citing competitive pricing pressures, Amdahl Corp. said first-quarter earnings slipped 34% to $29.9 million, or 27 cents a share, from $45.6 million, or 42 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue increased nearly 8% to $506.8 million from $470.3 million.

The Sunnyvale, Calif., computer vendor said earnings were lower because of competitive pricing pressures that accelerated soon after the first quarter of 1989 and because of a shift in product mix since that time. The company, among other computer manufacturers and vendors, has been hurt by pricing pressures in a sluggish market.

Amdahl said in early 1989 the company's 5890 mainframes constituted the majority of product shipments, but Amdahl is now delivering more 5990 systems, which have higher average selling prices but also higher manufacturing costs because of more complex technology.
