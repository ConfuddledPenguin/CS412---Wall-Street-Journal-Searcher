Who's News: Farah Names Wyman Chairman, Succeeding William F. Farah

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/25/90

EL PASO, Texas -- Farah Inc. said Thomas G. Wyman, a recently elected company director, was named chairman, succeeding William F. Farah, who will remain a board member. Mr. Wyman, 66 years old, formerly was chairman of L.G. Balfour Inc. of Attleboro, Mass., and owns a 5% stake in Farah, a clothing manufacturer.

It wasn't clear immediately whether Mr. Wyman was named over Mr. Farah's objections. Following Mr. Wyman's election, Mr. Farah, 70, wrote letters to the company's board members, urging them to either replace current management or resign. Board members apparently have refused to follow that request. Mr. Wyman said that Mr. Farah, who has a 17% stake in the company, has been unhappy with the direction of the company.

Mr. Wyman wouldn't elaborate, referring further questions to Mr. Farah, who couldn't be reached for comment.

Farah, which has been reporting declining sales and losses, says it has been taking steps to improve its inventories, deliveries and manufacturing process. In the first quarter ended Jan. 26, the company reported a loss of $589,000, narrower than its year-earlier first-quarter loss of $1.4 million.

Last year, the company discussed the sale of the company and then agreed to sell its Generra sportswear unit to a management-led group. In midyear, Mr. Farah resigned as president and chief executive officer and was succeeded by Richard C. Allender. Mr. Farah still has more than two years remaining on a three-year consulting agreement that pays him $365,000 a year and provides him with an office.

Mr. Wyman said he plans to support Mr. Allender's current strategy. "I think the company should follow the present goals which have been in place for too short a period of time," he said. "Mr. Allender is making every effort to improve the company."
