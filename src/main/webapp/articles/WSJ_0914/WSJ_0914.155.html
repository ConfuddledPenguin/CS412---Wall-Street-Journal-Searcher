Business Brief -- Canadian Pacific Forest Products: Paperworkers Strike Mills In Quebec, New Brunswick

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/14/90

Canadian Pacific Forest Products Ltd., Montreal, said workers went on strike at its newsprint mills in Gatineau, Quebec, and Dalhousie, New Brunswick. The Gatineau mill has annual capacity of 565,000 metric tons of newsprint and primarily serves North American customers. The Dalhousie mill has annual capacity of 340,000 metric tons and mostly serves European and Asian customers. A metric ton is equal to about 2,204.62 pounds.

The strike was called by the Canadian Paperworkers Union. Members of the same union struck Monday at seven Abitibi-Price Inc. mills in eastern Canada. Six of those mills produce newsprint.

Together, the strikes against the two companies have closed more than 12% of North American newsprint capacity.
