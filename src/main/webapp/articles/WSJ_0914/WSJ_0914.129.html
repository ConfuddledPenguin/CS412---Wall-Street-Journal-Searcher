International Brief -- RTZ Corp.: Earnings for First Half Fell; Lower Metals Prices Cited

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/14/90

RTZ Corp. of Britain, a major mining and minerals company, said first-half profit attributable to shareholders, after taxes and minority interests, fell 3.6% to #269 million ($500.3 million) from #279 million a year earlier, because of lower metals prices. Earnings per share fell 14% to 27.3 pence from 31.7 pence. The performance decline came despite a 38% reduction in RTZ's first-half tax payments, depressed this year by lower earnings contributions from several highly taxed, partly owned companies, and accentuated a year earlier by interim arrangements for the acquisition of British Petroleum Co.'s mineral assets. Pretax profit dropped 23% to #475 million from #617 million.

Sales declined 19% to #2.66 billion from #3.28 billion.

RTZ said it will increase the interim dividend 20% to six pence a share from five pence.
