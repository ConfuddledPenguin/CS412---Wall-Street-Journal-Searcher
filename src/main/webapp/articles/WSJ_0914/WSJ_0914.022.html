Former Chairman Of S&L in California Indicted in Fraud Case

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/14/90

LOS ANGELES -- A federal grand jury indicted former Family Savings & Loan Association Chairman Oliver A. Trigg, charging him with defrauding the thrift in a complex scheme to take control of Family using Family's own money. Mr. Trigg was charged with conspiracy, bank fraud, money laundering, false statements and tax fraud. If convicted, he faces a maximum of 94 years in prison and a $1 million fine. Three others in the case face lesser charges.

According to the indictment, Mr. Trigg, while he was serving as chairman of the board but held no equity in the thrift, arranged for Family to loan $2.5 million to a third party to develop a real estate property. Then $1.24 million of the money allegedly was laundered into Mr. Trigg's personal bank account and used to buy 51% of Family. He also allegedly lied about the transaction to federal regulators.

"The transactions involved in this case are prime examples of the kind of fraud and self-dealing that appears in so many of the troubled S&Ls around the country," said Assistant U.S. Attorney Maurice A. Leiter, who is prosecuting the case.

Mr. Trigg attracted local media attention in August 1987 when he acquired control of the thrift, partly because with assets of more than $140 million, Family was one of the largest black-owned savings and loans in the nation.

But two months later, the Los Angeles Times published an article which triggered an examination by regulators, who called in the Federal Bureau of Investigation in early 1988. Mr. Trigg resigned from Family a few months later.

Family is still in operation after Washington, D.C.-based OFC Inc. acquired control in January. President Wayne-Kent Bradshaw said the thrift expected to post a small operating profit this year and was in compliance with all federal regulations.

Mr. Trigg's attorney Arthur Barens said of the indictment, "We don't believe that Mr. Trigg did anything inappropriate or engaged in any conduct that resulted in any loss for Family Savings & Loan."
