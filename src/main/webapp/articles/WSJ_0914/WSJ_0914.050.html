Long-Distance Firms Set Service to Military in Gulf

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/14/90

NEW YORK -- Long-distance telephone companies are helping U.S. service personnel in the Persian Gulf keep in touch with families and friends in the U.S. American Telephone & Telegraph Co. and US Sprint Communications Co. have set up several free communications services for soldiers in Operation Desert Shield. MCI Communications Corp. said it could announce its own service today, probably involving some charge. All three carriers had been contacted by Sen. Alan Cranston, (D., Calif.,) and the Defense Department last week about coming up with a plan to improve communications, including video conferencing and electronic mail, between the U.S. and the military operation in Saudi Arabia.

"We're going to offer all these services, but video conferencing and E-Mail won't be for free," said a Sprint spokeswoman.

Sprint is offering a free news service on the Persian Gulf crisis. A person in the U.S. can dial a special "800" number to obtain free hourly crisis news updates via facsimile machine. The news is culled from several news wire services. The company says it has received 10,000 calls on the line so far.
