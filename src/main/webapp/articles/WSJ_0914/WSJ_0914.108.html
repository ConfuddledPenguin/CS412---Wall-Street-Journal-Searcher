Letters to the Editor: Opportunity vs. Risk In Eastern Europe

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/14/90

It is unfortunate that the last thing Stephen Friedman of Goldman Sachs expressed (Money & Investing, Aug. 15) was a seeming reluctance to examine speculative investment opportunities in Eastern Europe. True, there are awesome barriers, from the want of standard accounting systems to the lack of a modern stock exchange and convertible currencies. Eastern Europe, however, is an opportunity too good for even the conservative Goldman Sachs to pass up. It includes about 850 million literate, mostly educated people. It is also an area that, through stronger ties with the Soviet Union, could become self-sufficient in raw materials, independent of the Persian Gulf. Eastern Europe is a growth opportunity first, a highly speculative risk second.

Edward M. Gray

Boston, Mass.
