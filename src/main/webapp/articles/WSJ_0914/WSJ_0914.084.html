REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: Sense Prevails

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/14/90

In a time of urban turmoil, please note the good sense of the people of the District of Columbia. In their Democratic primary for mayor, they have nominated Sharon Pratt Dixon to run for the office held by Marion Barry. Ms. Dixon, far back in the pack before election day, had two distinguishing characteristics: She denounced Mayor Barry and she promised to fire many D.C. officials. In November she faces the Republican nominee, former D.C. Police Chief Maurice Turner. We think this outcome confirms our sense of what the Barry jury's decision to convict on only a minor charge actually represented. It reflected an awareness that the lessons of the Marion Barry story had been well absorbed by residents and that it was time for the District to get on with rebuilding its politics and its values. In selecting Sharon Pratt Dixon, District voters signaled that they are eager to renew their city.
