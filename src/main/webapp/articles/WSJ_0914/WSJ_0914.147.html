Business Brief -- Champion International Corp.: Firm Trims Spending Plans; Economic Outlook Is Cited

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/14/90

Champion International Corp. said it trimmed capital spending plans to $750 million a year from $900 million to $1 billion annually for projected spending in 1991 and 1992. Andrew C. Sigler, chairman and chief executive officer, said in Stamford, Conn., "we have nearly completed the group of major projects we announced several years ago, and with the likelihood of a softer economy ahead, this was a logical place to defer the other three projects."

The projects being deferred involve the planned conversion of the company's brown paper mill in Roanoke Rapids, N.C., to a white paper mill, the installation of an additional lightweight coated publications papers machine and construction of a new pulp mill in Brazil.

Champion is a paper, plywood and lumber company.
