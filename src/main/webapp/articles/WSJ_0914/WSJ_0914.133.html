Business Brief -- United Technologies Corp.: Sikorsky Unit Will Lay Off 350 White-Collar Employees

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/14/90

Sikorsky Aircraft, a unit of United Technologies Corp., said it will lay off 350 white-collar employees Saturday in order to make the company more competitive. The helicopter manufacturer said the layoffs, concentrated in the Connecticut area, include engineers and administrative personnel. "We decided to size the company to be leaner and more competitive, after looking at different segments of the business," a spokesman said. The cuts reduce Sikorsky's work force to 11,300.

Last February, the company, based in Stratford, Conn., laid off 300 white-collar workers as a reaction to cuts in defense spending. Fifty hourly workers were laid off in March, and another 50 were cut through attrition that month.

In the second quarter, United Technologies earnings increased 51% to $207.5 million, or $1.64 a share.

Sikorsky Aircraft has operations in Bridgeport, West Haven, Shelton and Trumbull, Conn., as well as Troy, Ala., and West Palm Beach, Fla. Its major military products include the Black Hawk and Super Stallion helicopters.
