Movie Concern Gets a Day To Answer Creditor Filing

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/14/90

LOS ANGELES -- A federal bankruptcy court judge here, granted Management Co. Entertainment Group Inc. a one-day extension to answer a creditor petition for involuntary liquidation of its movie unit. The extension was granted after the company switched law firms and amid reports that there is pressure on Jonathan D. Krane, the company's chairman and chief executive officer, to resign. Mr. Krane was the subject of a front-page profile yesterday in this newspaper. Mr. Krane couldn't be reached for comment.

In a court filing objecting to a longer extension, an attorney for the creditors said MCEG representatives had told him that "a dispute is raging and is unresolved concerning the management of the {company} and that present management is resisting unconditional ouster without some kinds of unspecified protection."

MCEG said it expects to make an announcement about plans for a reorganization today. The company has been negotiating a possible restructuring with a number of potential investors including Domino Management Co., a group headed by Norman Levy, a consultant. Mr. Levy didn't return phone calls.

Leslie A. Cohen, an attorney for MCEG, said the company's movie unit is considering filing for protection from creditors under federal bankruptcy law.

MCEG, a movie production, talent management and distribution company, is in default on more than $100 million of debt and its largest creditors include the GE Capital unit of General Electric Co. The petition for involuntary liquidation of the movie unit was filed by director Nicolas Roeg and two other creditors.
