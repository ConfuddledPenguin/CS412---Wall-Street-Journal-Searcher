C.R. Bard Inc. to Sell Subsidiary to a Unit Of Japanese Concern

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/14/90

MURRAY HILL, N.J. -- C.R. Bard Inc. said it agreed to sell its Shield Healthcare Centers subsidiaries to a U.S. subsidiary of Kobayashi Pharmaceutical Co., a privately held maker of household and medical products based in Osaka, Japan. Shield Healthcare, which has centers primarily in the Los Angeles area, sells medical supplies and equipment for home patient care. Bard, a health care products concern, said it will realize a one-time after-tax gain of several million dollars on the sale, with funds to be used to reduce Bard's short-term debt.

The Shield subsidiaries had combined sales of approximately $30 million in 1989. The acquisition is expected to be completed by Sept. 30.

C.R. Bard has struggled since last year when it was forced to temporarily withdraw coronary balloon angioplasty catheters from the U.S. market; the catheters are used as an alternative to bypass surgery to open blocked arteries.

For the second quarter, C.R. Bard's net income fell 54% to $10.1 million, or 19 cents a share, from $21.8 million or 39 cents a share. Sales fell 3% to $194.1 million from $201 million.
