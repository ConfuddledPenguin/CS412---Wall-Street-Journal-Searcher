Sarnoff Research Center

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/14/90

PRINCETON, N.J. -- The David Sarnoff Research Center signed a new agreement valued at more than $100 million to continue research and development work with the consumer electronics unit of Paris-based Thomson S.A. Under the multi-year agreement, the Sarnoff Center will continue pursuing new technologies for television systems, displays and TV receivers carrying the RCA name, officials said.

The Sarnoff Center, now a subsidiary of California-based SRI International, was started at Princeton in 1942 by the RCA Corp. Following RCA's acquisition by General Electric Co., the consumer electronics business was acquired by Thomson in 1987.

The Sarnoff Center is also part of a major consortium trying to develop high definition television systems, in which Thomson is also involved. In 1989, the Sarnoff Center posted revenue of $77.4 million.
