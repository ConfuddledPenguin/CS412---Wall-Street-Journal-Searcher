International: Brazil Signs IMF Letter, Will Resume Debt Talks

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 09/14/90

BRASILIA -- Brazil signed a letter of intent to the International Monetary Fund and pledged to resume debt renegotiations with creditors this month. The letter was the result of drawn-out negotiations that reportedly nearly fell through over disagreement about a token payment to creditors.

Economy Minister Zelia Cardoso de Mello said Brazil wouldn't make any symbolic payment of arrears to creditors of the country's $117 billion foreign debt, the largest in the Third World.

"We want a constructive, definitive and quick solution," the official said.

In the letter, Brazil requested a standby loan of $2.016 billion, to be released in six parcels by February 1992.

The government also outlined goals for growth, the budget deficit and reducing 4,274% inflation.

Some of the targets are: negative growth of 3% for gross national product this year; a spending surplus of 0.5% of GNP this year and 0% in 1991; 25% annual inflation in 1991; a reduction of the deficit of government-owned companies from 2.5% of GNP in 1989 to 1% this year and 0% in 1991.
