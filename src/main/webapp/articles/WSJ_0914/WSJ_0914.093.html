Portman Lenders Say They Made Headway On Revamping Debt

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/14/90

ATLANTA -- A group of more than 20 lenders to architect-developer John Portman said they made "positive progress" toward restructuring some or all of his roughly $2 billion of debt. Mr. Portman is facing a cash crunch and has stopped servicing tens of millions of dollars of unsecured loans, as well as the $332.6 million mortgage on his Peachtree Center office buildings here. That mortgage is held by Equitable Life Assurance Society of the U.S. and Nippon Life Insurance Co. of Japan.

The lender group, led by Citicorp and Swiss Bank Corp., met all day on Wednesday here, and said in a prepared statement that it plans to meet again soon.
