Who's News: Ex-Bank of Japan Aide Accepts Advisory Jobs

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/14/90

TOKYO -- Takeshi Ohta, a former deputy governor of the Bank of Japan, the central bank, accepted advisory positions at a Japanese bank and a British securities house. Daiwa Bank Ltd. hired Mr. Ohta this week as a special adviser, and Kleinwort Benson Group PLC said it will hire him as a non-executive director.

In Japan, it is common practice for retired senior government officials to join institutions or companies in fields related to their bureaucratic careers. The practice is known as "descent from heaven."

Gregory A. Shenkman, managing director and general manager of Kleinwort Benson's Tokyo office, said Mr. Ohta's hiring "will bring us some very valuable access within Japan."

Mr. Ohta said that he wasn't sure what role he will play at the two companies, but that he hopes he can put his experience as the Japanese central bank's top international official to use.
