Bill Provides Rental Aid To Keep Families Intact

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/14/90

WASHINGTON -- A little-noticed provision of the housing bill would create the first housing entitlement program, one requiring rental subsidies to families whose children are threatened with foster care because of inadequate housing. The program, authored by Rep. Charles Schumer (D., N.Y.), is aimed at keeping families from splitting because of deficient housing. Though deficit pressures and cost concerns could darken the plan's prospects, the proposal has some bipartisan support. The initiative would require about $1 billion in budget authority over five years, according to Congressional Budget Office estimates.

Mr. Schumer said an entitlement, which makes a specific amount of funding automatically available each year, was necessary because "otherwise these kids will be forgotten. There is such a shortage of housing dollars and such a fight over them."

Both the House and the Senate have passed legislation that would overhaul federal housing programs. But it is unclear whether the entitlement provision, which isn't included in the Senate version, will survive when the legislation goes into conference.
