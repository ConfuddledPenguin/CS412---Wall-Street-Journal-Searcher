International Brief -- Fiat S.p.A.: Concern Agrees to Acquire 60% Stake in Truck Maker

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/14/90

Italy's Fiat S.p.A. agreed to buy a 60% stake in Spain's troubled Empresa Nacional de Autocamiones S.A., or Enasa. Fiat will put up 12 billion pesetas ($120 million) in new capital, plus 1.2 billion pesetas in cash for the truck maker. The agreement by Enasa's owner, Instituto Nacional de Industria, comes as a surprise; as recently as this week Spanish officials had been discussing a possible accord to sell Enasa to West Germany's Daimler-Benz AG. Negotiations had foundered on the Daimler's insistence on big cuts in Enasa's work force and hefty Spanish government grants.

Fiat has agreed to keep on Enasa's entire 5,700 work force, to retain the use of its Pegaso brand for trucks and buses and to expand into new activities in Spain, INI officials said.

INI will retain a 40% stake in Enasa and provide 8 billion pesetas in new capital; both partners' contributions will total 20 billion pesetas in increased capital for Enasa by early next year.

Fiat officials said they have agreed to spend about 40 billion pesetas over the next few years on new industrial projects in Spain. Fiat also said that it has an option to buy INI's 40% stake in Enasa in six years' time.
