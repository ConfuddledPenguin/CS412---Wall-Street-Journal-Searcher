Korea Asia Fund Launch Is Delayed by Managers

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/14/90

HONG KONG -- The launch of the Korea Asia Fund, a $100 million mutual fund investing solely in South Korean equities, was postponed because of uncertain market conditions and weak investor interest. "We'll watch the market day by day," said a spokesman for Salomon Brothers International Ltd., one of the four lead managers. "The launch date is still uncertain." The other lead managers are Yamaichi International (HK) Ltd., Dongsuh Securities Co. and Coryo Securities Corp.

The fund, the third foreign-managed stock mutual fund investing solely in Korea, was scheduled to reach the market yesterday. But prospects soured in the poor market environment that followed Iraq's invasion of Kuwait. Analysts also noted that a flood of stock issues in Korea during the past six months has saturated the market.

Korea's Finance Ministry, which was eager for the new issue, is likely to insist that the launch take place fairly soon. Besides delaying a desired infusion of capital, the postponement threw a shadow over other expected issues. Two Korean companies, Kolon Industries Ltd. and Jindo Corp., were planning to float convertible bonds soon but now are expected to wait until the Korea Asia Fund gets off the ground.
