Canadian Pacific Ltd. Says It May Buy Back Up to 9.9% of Shares

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/14/90

MONTREAL -- Canadian Pacific Ltd. said it may buy back as many as 9.9% of its shares outstanding, which have fallen steeply in price this year. The holding company with interests in transportation, telecommunications, hotels, energy and natural resources filed for regulatory clearance to purchase as many as 31.5 million shares on Canadian stock exchanges in a 12-month period that will begin Tuesday.

Canadian companies often make such filings on a contingency basis and renew them each year without actually buying many shares. But a spokesman for Canadian Pacific said its filing wasn't a renewal and represented a belief that the shares are attractively priced.

Canadian Pacific closed yesterday at $15.75 a share, up 12.5 cents, on New York Stock Exchange composite volume of 1.6 million shares.

The company's shares have fallen about 35% on the Toronto Stock Exchange since last October, while the exchange's bellwether index of 300 stocks has fallen only about 11%. Its profit has followed a similar course. First-half net income was down 34% to 177.8 million Canadian dollars (US$152.9 million) from C$270.4 million a year earlier, mostly because of a drop in earnings at its 80%-owned Canadian Pacific Forest Products Ltd.

Analyst Michael Graham of Midland Walwyn Capital Inc. said a stock buy-back would be a sound move. Canadian Pacific's shares had a book value of C$24.46 each at the end of last year, and Mr. Graham estimates their real net asset value at C$40 to C$50 because of undervalued real estate assets. The forest-products subsidiary's problems result from a slump in demand affecting companies all across the industry.
