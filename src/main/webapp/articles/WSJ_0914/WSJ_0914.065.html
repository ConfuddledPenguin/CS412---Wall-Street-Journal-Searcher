JPS Textile Cancels Its Offers to Buy Its Notes, Preferred

  • Author: Peter Pae
  • Date: 09/14/90

NEW YORK -- JPS Textile Group Inc., after numerous failed attempts to persuade bondholders, said it terminated its offers to buy and exchange about $450 million of its notes and preferred. The company, which initiated the offers July 20 and had extended them to Sept. 20, said it would be "impracticable" to continue the offers because a majority of the debt holders "do not favor them."

The latest setback for the debt-ridden textile concern signals a growing reluctance of bondholders to refinance debt on terms available in today's deflated markets.

The company said yesterday that "financial alternatives to the offers are being explored," including the possibility of exchanging equity in the company for the notes and shares.

The action comes only a week after JPS extended its offer for the seventh time, sweetening the offer for some notes and shares. But few bondholders were swayed, and many instead sought exchange offers in which interest rates on the debt securities would be reduced in exchange for newly issued equity. "Discussions with representatives of the debt holders are expected to continue," JPS said.

JPS had hoped to use the proceeds to repay debt that it incurred when it acquired J.P. Stevens & Co., a maker of textiles and related products, for $579 million in 1988. JPS sold the notes and shares to help finance the acquisition.

The company was offering, for each $1,000 principal amount of its notes outstanding, $850 for the senior notes; $190.22 and 1.7 JPS common shares for discount notes; and $400 plus 1.45 JPS shares for subordinated notes. JPS also offered to exchange $1,000 principal amount of its 6% debentures and 1.21 JPS shares for each $1,000 principal amount of its subordinated debt outstanding. In addition, the company was offering $10 for each preferred Series A share.

The company said that although about $1.6 million principal amount of senior notes outstanding had been tendered as of Sept. 6, no other securities had been tendered.
