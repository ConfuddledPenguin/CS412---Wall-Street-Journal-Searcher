Business Brief -- Automatic Data Processing Inc.: Earnings Expected to Rise 10% or More in the Year

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/14/90

Automatic Data Processing Inc. said it expects "double-digit" growth in earnings for fiscal 1991, despite a "modest increase" in revenue. Josh Weston, chairman of Automatic Data, projects the rate of growth in per-share earnings in "excess of 10%," but said "it's unlikely that it would go up 15% or more."

In fiscal 1990, ended June 30, the company earned $211.7 million, or $2.88 a share, on revenue of $1.71 billion.

In a meeting with securities analysts, the company said revenue isn't expected to rise as much as earnings, growing moderately at a rate of about 3%. Nevertheless, the company, based in New York, said it is being aided by a lower tax rate because of its increased investments in tax advantaged marketable securites. That will help push earnings to "record levels," Mr. Weston said.

Mr. Weston said also that he expects double digit growth for earnings in the current first quarter. In fiscal 1990 fourth quarter, the company earned $59.7 million, or 81 cents a share, on revenue of $425.8 million.

Automatic Data Processing provides data processing for employer services such as payroll, brokerage transactions and automative dealerships.
