U.S. Vehicle Output For Week Set to Fall 3.3% From '89 Level

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/14/90

DETROIT -- The 10 major U.S. auto makers planned to build 136,828 cars and trucks this week, down 3.3% from 141,483 a year ago. Ford Motor Co. said it plans overtime work tomorrow at the Wayne, Mich., car assembly plant that builds the Escort. Ford has two car and two truck assembly plants idled this week but plans to reopen all four next week.

Chrysler Corp. said its assembly plants in Newark, Del., and Warren, Mich., are scheduled to work overtime tomorrow.

General Motors Corp. said it scheduled overtime this week at its Janesville, Wis., assembly plant and overtime tomorrow at its Bowling Green, Ky., assembly plant.

The 10 auto makers built 823,948 cars and trucks last month, down 5.5% from 871,879 in August 1989. U.S. car output fell 2.7% in August, and truck and bus production was down 10%.
