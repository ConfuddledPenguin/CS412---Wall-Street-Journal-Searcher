  • Date: 09/14/90

GREENSPAN SAID U.S. BANKS aren't facing a disaster on the scale of the S&L debacle, despite the banking industry's serious problems. The Fed chairman told a House panel that he also endorses the idea of raising the premiums that banks pay for deposit insurance, a move that is backed by the administration and by a growing number of lawmakers. ---

Stocks fell sharply under pressure from program selling that was sparked by a jump in oil prices and a rumor of Mideast war. The Dow Jones industrials dropped 43.07 points to 2582.67. The dollar was sharply lower for the second straight session.

---

An FDIC audit shows the cost to the government of its 1988 sales of failed savings-and-loans will be as much as $95 billion, rather than a previously estimated $39 billion.

---

Budget negotiations continued with few hints of breakthroughs on crucial remaining issues, such as Bush's proposal to cut capital-gains taxes. Still, most of those close to the discussions remained confident an accord eventually will be reached.

---

Philip Morris and RJR Nabisco have been asked by the Soviet Union to supply 34 billion cigarettes, the largest export order ever for either concern. In exchange, the companies will receive a combination of hard currency and barter items.

---

GM and the UAW have settled all but the crucial issues of job security and health benefits in their contract talks. A new pact could be reached by tonight's deadline, both sides said.

---

A probe of program trading by three major exchanges found no evidence of large-scale illicit activity, said people familiar with the project.

---

The Strategic Petroleum Reserve would be increased by 33% to a billion gallons under legislation approved by Congress. The measure also sets up a pilot reserve for refined products.

---

The accounting profession, in a reversal, now supports proposed legislation that would require auditors to notify regulators of possible illegal actions by company officials.

---

Sales of U.S.-made vehicles gained momentum in early September, rising 2.5% after a weak August.

Ford scaled back some of its 1991 model price increases, reducing some Taurus models by more than $600.

---

Southland extended its debt-swap offer for the third time amid signs that the company may be forced to file for bankruptcy-court protection to complete a proposed, $430 million bailout by its Japanese partners.

---

Carl Icahn won an unusual bankruptcy-court auction for an Integrated Resources real estate holding, for which he bid $44 million.
