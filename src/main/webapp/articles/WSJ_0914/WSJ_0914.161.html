Business Brief -- J.M. Peters Co.: Home Builder Posts a Loss Of $451,000 for 2nd Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/14/90

J.M. Peters Co., a Southern California home builder, posted a $451,000 loss for its fiscal second quarter, ended Aug. 31. The loss, equal to three cents a share, compares with a year-ago profit of $3.5 million, or 25 cents a share. Revenue fell 9.6% to $52.7 million from $58.3 million.

The Newport Beach, Calif., company said its results are reflective of "the continued downturn in sales" for single-family "move-up" housing in most areas of Southern California. The company builds homes for the "move-up" market, which is upper-income buyers who have previously owned a principal residence.

For the six months, the company had net income of $1.1 million, or eight cents a share, compared with profit of $8.5 million, or 61 cents a share, a year ago. Revenue increased 5.2% to $121.9 million from $115.8 million.
