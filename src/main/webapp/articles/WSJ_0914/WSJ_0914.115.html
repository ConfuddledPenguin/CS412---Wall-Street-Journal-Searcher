Letters to the Editor: Arab Jet Pilots: Fear Of Failure, Not Flying

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/14/90

In response to Geraldine Brooks's Aug. 16 article about U.S. and British Troops encountering Islam: The Arabs and Europeans/Americans have always made for strange bedfellows as pilots flying together in training and combat. As a Royal Air Force fighter pilot, I was involved in training, and trained alongside, nationals of Jordan, Oman, Saudi Arabia, Bahrain and Qatar. I also spent the best part of two years flying Omani fighters.

We had one prince attending fast jet school in North Wales who refused to rise before noon and frequently went AWOL on London jaunts. On the other hand, when some of these same Arab pilots did decide to "turn and turn," they proved to be excellent combat pilots.

There were also some tragic incidents resulting from the Arabs' fierce pride. The fear of failure and accompanying perceived family shame caused some alarming incidents. One student disappeared along with the chief flying instructor who was performing a "chop ride." The student was apparently borderline and rather than be sent home, the price of failure, he opted to take the jet and the instructor with him to meet Allah.

The Arab nations have truly been jet-propelled into the 21st century.

Philip Rushton

Morristown, N.J.
