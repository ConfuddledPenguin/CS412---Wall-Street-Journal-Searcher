International Brief -- Rolls-Royce PLC: Engine Firm's Pretax Profit Was Up 14% in First Half

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/14/90

Rolls-Royce PLC, the British airplane engine manufacturer, reported that pretax profit for the first half rose 14% to #115 million ($214 million) from #101 million, as buoyant demand offset the impact of a turn-of-the-year strike. The results fell below market expectations, which ranged between #120 million and #130 million.

Sales advanced 42% to #1.59 billion from #1.12 billion a year earlier.

Earnings per share, however, slipped 2% to 9.9 pence from 10.1 pence because of the larger number of shares in issue in the latest period.

Rolls-Royce, riding a boom in aircraft orders that has seen earnings improve throughout most of the industry, benefited during the latest period from a full six-month contribution from NEI, the engineering company it acquired early last year.

"The order book currently stands at a record level of more than #6 billion and the company continues to gain market share," Lord Tombs, Rolls-Royce chairman, said.
