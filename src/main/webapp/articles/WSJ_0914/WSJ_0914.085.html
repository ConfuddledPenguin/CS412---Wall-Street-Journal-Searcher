Bank of Montreal Cuts Prime Rate to 13.75%

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/14/90

TORONTO -- Bank of Montreal cut its prime lending rate a quarter of a percentage point to 13.75%, its lowest level since January. John Parish, the bank's assistant chief economist, said the rate reduction was prompted by a recent decline in short-term Canadian interest rates and by the country's economic slowdown. The prime rate is the base rate charged on loans to businesses and individuals.

It was the second time in two weeks that Bank of Montreal cut its prime rate by a quarter of a percentage point. Other major Canadian banks have kept their prime rates at 14.25%.

The Bank of Montreal action followed a decline in the Bank of Canada bank rate yesterday to 12.7% from 12.82%. The bank rate is the levy by the central bank on loans to financial institutions. The bank rate, which has declined for 12 consecutive weeks, is set weekly at a quarter of a percentage point above the average yield on three-month Canadian government treasury bills at the Thursday bill auction.
