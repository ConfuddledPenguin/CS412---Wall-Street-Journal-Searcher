International: Senate Votes to Liberalize Trade With East Europe

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/14/90

WASHINGTON -- The Senate voted to liberalize trade with the Soviet Union and Eastern Europe and tighten curbs on shipments of goods that could be used to manufacture missiles and nuclear and chemical weapons. The bill included trade benefits to the Soviets and Eastern Europeans approved by the Senate Banking Committee earlier this summer.

The bill would effectively give the Pentagon the right to review shipments of certain sensitive goods to certain nations. The Pentagon doesn't have the authority to screen exports sent to non-Communist nations.

The House has passed a reauthorization bill somewhat more generous to U.S. high-technology exporters than the Senate version.

The Senate bill, which would renew the Export Administration Act for two years, also would empower the U.S. to impose trade sanctions on nations and companies that violate international prohibitions on chemical warfare. The current export law expires Sept. 30.

A provision by Sen. Richard Shelby (D., Ala.) would increase penalties for violations of the trade embargo against Iraq and Iraqi-occupied Kuwait. The civil fine would be raised to $250,000 from $10,000, and the criminal fine to $1 million from $50,000.

The Senate also adopted an amendment by Sen. William Roth (R., Del.) that would deter the State Department from arbitrarily adding goods that are no longer controlled on national-security grounds to its own munitions list. Exporters have complained that the list is too restrictive.
