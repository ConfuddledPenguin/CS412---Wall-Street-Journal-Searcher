Marketing & Media: Playboy Enterprises Considers Acquiring Or Investing in Spy

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/14/90

NEW YORK -- Playboy Enterprises Inc. is considering acquiring or investing in Spy, a four-year-old humor magazine. Playboy executives characterized the talks as informal, but a spokeswoman said there was "real interest" on the part of Chicago-based Playboy Enterprises, publisher of Playboy magazine.

Spy has been seeking an investor, or acquirer, for most of this year -- without success. Last February, Spy's publisher Thomas L. Phillips Jr. said he and his partners had retained Morgan Stanley & Co. to consider offers from interested buyers and investors. Mr. Phillips said Spy worked with Morgan Stanley for about three months, but dropped the investment banking company after passing on several potential investors Morgan Stanley proposed.

However, industry executives said that Morgan Stanley had overestimated the value investors would put on Spy and that Spy's owners had, in turn, held out for too high a price. Morgan Stanley executives did not return calls seeking comment.

A Playboy company spokeswoman confirmed recent talks were held between Mr. Phillips and Michael Perlis, president and publisher of Playboy. Mr. Perlis wasn't available for comment. "Spy would be a nice complement to Playboy," said a Playboy spokeswoman.

After launching in 1986, Spy gained attention for its satires on the rich and famous. The magazine's notoriety reached far beyond its circulation, which Mr. Phillips said is about 150,000. Spy expanded into book publishing with the same brand of humor as its magazine, and this year produced its first TV show for the NBC network.

In recent months, Spy's advertising volume has fallen, compared with 1989 levels. Mr. Phillips said that while advertising is down, the magazine has raised its ad rates and thus was getting more revenue per page. "People figured we were in trouble last spring because we were for sale," Mr. Phillips said. "We weren't and we aren't. We are still a profitable magazine."
