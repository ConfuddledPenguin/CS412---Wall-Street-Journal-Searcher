Business Brief -- Applied Biosystems Inc.: Pretax Charge of $5 Million Incurred for Unit Relocation

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/14/90

Applied Biosystems Inc. said it would incur a $5 million pretax charge in the first quarter ending Sept. 28 to cover the cost of relocating its liquid chromatography components operation from New Jersey to California. A spokesman said the charge is equal to about $4 million, or 25 cents a share, after taxes. In the year-earlier quarter, Applied reported net income of $2.3 million, or 16 cents a share, on sales of $36.8 million.

The equipment maker for the biotechnology industry, based in Foster City, Calif., said the relocation would "enhance its ability to support its customer base, more effectively coordinate new product development efforts and improve its overall cost structure."

Liquid chromatography is a process to separate molecules. Applied said about 20% of the 113 workers employed in the operation would be relocated; the company employs 1,300 people world-wide.
