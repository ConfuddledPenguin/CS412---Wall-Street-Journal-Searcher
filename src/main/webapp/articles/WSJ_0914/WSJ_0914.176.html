Telxon Expects Profit In Sept. 30 Quarter After Year-Ago Loss

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/14/90

AKRON, Ohio -- Telxon Corp. said it expects earnings for its fiscal second-quarter to exceed 20 cents a share, excluding a nine-cent-a-share gain from purchasing about $8 million of its bonds. The company said it expects revenue to top $43 million in the quarter ending Sept. 30. For the year-earlier period, the maker of portable computers posted a loss of $3.9 million, or 30 cents a share, on revenue of $35.4 million.

For the year ending March 31, Telxon said it expects per-share earnings of 70 cents to 75 cents a share, excluding the nine-cent-a-share gain from the bond purchase and a 16-cent-a-share gain from a bond purchase earlier this year. Telxon estimated that revenue will be $170 million to $175 million.

In fiscal 1990, the company had a loss of $14.4 million, or $1.09 a share, on revenue of $143.4 million.

Telxon said it purchased in open market transactions a total of $8 million face amount of its 7.5% convertible subordinated debentures due 2012. The purchase represents about 23% of the outstanding issue, leaving about $28 million face amount outstanding. Earlier this year, the company purchased about $10 million of the bonds.
