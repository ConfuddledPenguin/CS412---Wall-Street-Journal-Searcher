Business Brief -- Manpower PLC: Company to Sell Five Units To Brook Opportunities

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/14/90

Manpower PLC said it agreed to sell five U.K.-based subsidiaries to Brook Opportunities Ltd. for about $200 million. The employment and financial-services concern said it also will receive 15% of the voting share capital of Brook, a company formed to acquire the units. Included in the sale would be non-Manpower brand operations: Brook Street Bureau, Blue Arrow Personnel Services, Extrastaff, DP Support Services, and IPS Group, all of which provide employment services. The sale essentially would complete the disposal of companies that constituted the Blue Arrow Group before it acquired Manpower Inc. in 1987. On June 4, Manpower, which is reincorporating in the U.S., sold a group of six non-Manpower units in the U.S. for $46 million.

Cash proceeds of the sale would be used to retire Manpower debt outstanding. The sale must be approved by Manpower shareholders at a special meeting early next month in London.
