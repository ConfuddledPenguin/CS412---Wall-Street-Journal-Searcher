Chyron Plans to File For Chapter 11 Status

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/14/90

MELVILLE, N.J. -- Chyron Corp., unable to secure new financing, said it intends to file for protection from creditors under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code. The maker of electronic graphics and videotape and film editing equipment said it is taking the action because it can't repay $10 million in loans called yesterday by Manufacturers Hanover Trust Co. The bank agreed to provide $9 million to Chyron as debtor-in-possession, however.

Chyron hadn't made any loan payments since June 30 and was seeking to restructure loans and obtain additional financing from its banks.

But Manufacturers Hanover and Chyron's other major lender, European American Bank, couldn't come to terms on sharing loan collateral. Chyron owes $20 million to European American.

The dispute stemmed from Chyron granting both banks in 1989 a security interest in its accounts receivable as a condition for the company to pay a quarterly dividend. European America said that it had first lien on the company's receivables and that Manufacturers Hanover had the second lien.

As result, Chyron said, Manufacturers Hanover filed suit last week against European American, charging the bank with fraud and breach of contract. Chyron also was named as a defendant in the suit, but no relief is being sought against Chyron, the company said.

Chyron had a net loss of $1.9 million on revenue of $44.1 million in 1989.
