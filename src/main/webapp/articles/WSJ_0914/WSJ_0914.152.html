Abreast of the Market: Industrials Fall 43.07 on Gulf News, Anticipation of Economic Reports

  • Author: David Wilson
  • Date: 09/14/90

NEW YORK -- Stock prices tumbled in a sell-off linked to nervousness about today's flurry of economic reports and frustration with the drawn-out negotiations on a federal deficit reduction package. Reports that Iraq had issued a warning to U.S. officials about possible terrorist attacks against American targets unsettled the market as well, traders said.

"You've got an event-driven market where everyone's really concerned about the next move overseas," compounding fears about the domestic situation, said Larry Greenwald, a block trader at Sanford C. Bernstein & Co.

The Dow Jones Industrial Average skidded 43.07 to 2582.67 -- its sharpest decline since Aug. 23 -- as stocks buckled under the weight of broad-based selling. Decliners topped advancers on the New York Stock Exchange by 1,009 to 430.

Broader indexes weren't hit quite as hard as the industrial average, but nonetheless dropped sharply. Standard & Poor's 500-Stock Index, for example, went down 3.89 to 318.65; its 1.21% drop compared with a 1.64% decline in the Dow Jones industrials. The New York Stock Exchange Composite Index dropped 1.93 to 175.16 and the Dow Jones Equity Market Index fell 3.41 to 295.39.

Meanwhile, the lackluster pace of trading over the past two weeks remained intact despite the market's retreat. "There's just very, very little to do, and it's obviously frustrating," said Thomas R. Walsh, head of equity trading at Nikko Securities International.

Big Board volume amounted to 123,370,000 shares, down from Wednesday's total of 129,890,000 shares.

A flurry of sell programs resulting from stock-index arbitrage hit the market during the afternoon, contributing to its downturn, traders said. The programs took effect as stock-index futures went down faster that the cash indexes, creating opportunities to profit by purchasing futures contracts and selling baskets of stocks.

Four major economic reports are scheduled for release before trading opens today, with the August producer price index drawing most of the market's interest. The PPI represents the first piece of inflation data affected by the surge in oil prices since Iraq invaded Kuwait.

The index is expected to be up 1.4% for the month, according to a consensus forecast of economists surveyed by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report. But analysts believe the market may pay closer attention to an index of prices excluding food and energy, expected to rise 0.3% for August, because a surge in the overall index is being anticipated.

Rao Chalasani, a technical analyst at Prescott, Ball & Turben in Cleveland, said the market's fall may already have discounted the possibility of a disappointment in the report. "It just has to continue," he said of the market's low-volume, sideways movement.

August reports on retail sales, industrial production and capacity utilization are also on the schedule for this morning.

As for the deficit-reduction talks, traders said the inability of negotiators to complete a budget package has begun taking a toll on the market. "Most people were enthusiastic that they would have that {agreement} settled by the end of this week. That doesn't seem to be the case any more," said Mr. Walsh of Nikko Securities.

Money-center bank issues were hit with selling as the market absorbed congressional testimony from Federal Reserve Board Chairman Alan Greenspan, who said the banking system is facing "all too many problems" as the quality of loan portfolios at many banks continues to deteriorate.

Chase Manhattan, which fell 1/2 Wednesday in the wake of a poorly received sale of $200 million in notes, lost another 3/8 to 16 3/4. Among other money-center issues, Citicorp fell 1/4 to 17 3/8, J.P. Morgan eased 1/2 to 32 1/8, BankAmerica lost 5/8 to 21 3/4 and Manufacturers Hanover tumbled 1 1/4 to 25 5/8.

Digital Equipment dropped 2 3/8 to 59 1/4 in composite trading of 1.3 million shares, extending a sell-off attributed to disappointment with presentations at two investment conferences earlier in the week and subsequent reductions in several analysts' earnings forecasts. Over the past three sessions, the stock has lost a total of 5 5/8 points, or 8.7%.

John B. Jones, an analyst at Montgomery Securities, said Digital's presentation at a conference hosted by his firm "didn't leave a lot of hope for a quick turnaround," according to Dow Jones Professional Investor Report.

Meanwhile, Dean Witter Reynolds lowered its projection of Digital's fiscal 1991 earnings by $1.05 a share, to $5.15 a share.

International Business Machines, a strong performer in recent sessions, skidded 2 7/8 to 105 on 1.1 million shares. Other blue chips that fell sharply included United Technologies, which tumbled 1 3/4 to 47 7/8; Coca-Cola, which dipped 7/8 to 41 1/4; and Eastman Kodak, which lost 1 1/8 to 40 3/8.

Hilton Hotels, which forecast that its third-quarter net income from operations would be about 50% lower than a year earlier, fell 2 1/4 to 29 1/4.

Motorola rose 1/2 to 61 3/8. The Wall Street Journal reported yesterday that the company has reached an agreement in principle with Toshiba to establish a semiconductor joint venture in Scotland, expanding a three-year relationship between the two concerns.

Canadian Pacific gained 1/8 to 15 3/4 on 1.6 million shares. The company said it plans to repurchase nearly 31.5 million of its common shares, or 9.9% of the shares outstanding, in a one-year buy-back program starting next week.

Corroon & Black, which jumped 6 3/4 in composite trading Wednesday after Aon offered $40 a share for the company, retreated 1 3/8 to 36 1/4 on 1.2 million shares. Britain's Willis Faber, which previously agreed to a stock-swap merger of the company, said it is still committed to that offer. Aon fell 1 1/8 to 31 7/8.

MGM/UA Communications, profiled in the Journal's "Heard on the Street" column, fell 5/8 to 14 5/8. Traders and analysts believe the stock could retreat below 10, and perhaps into a range of 7 to 8, if Pathe Communications doesn't complete its takeover of the company.

The American Stock Exchange Market Value Index fell 1.18 to 322.01. Volume totaled 9,720,000 shares.
