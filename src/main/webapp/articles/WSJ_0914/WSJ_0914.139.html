Globex System Debut Now Will Be Delayed To 1991 First Period

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/14/90

CHICAGO -- The debut of Globex, the automated after-hours futures trading system being jointly developed by the Chicago Mercantile Exchange and Reuters Holdings PLC, has been delayed until at least the 1991 first quarter. The system, which has been beset by a number of launch delays, was to have begun operating by year end, according to the partners' previous estimate.

The latest delay results from the need for continued testing of the complex computer system. Also slowing Globex is the complicated process of integrating Chicago Board of Trade financial futures contracts with the Merc contracts originally included in Globex.

The two futures exchanges had been developing rival systems, but in May the Board of Trade agreed, in effect, to fold its system into Globex. At that time, the launch date was the end of 1989.

Globex and an automated foreign-exchange trading system being developed separately by Reuters called Dealing 20002, whose start-up has also been delayed, are based on some of the same computer hardware and software, a Reuters spokesman said. But he said the delays in the two automated trading systems aren't related.
