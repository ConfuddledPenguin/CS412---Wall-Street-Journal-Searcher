Letters to the Editor: Into the Sunset, With a Beloved Friend

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/14/90

I read with much nostalgia your Aug. 3 page-one article "Americans' Love of IBM Typewriters Outlasts Company's." My secretarial career began in 1949, after learning the basic skills in high school in an excellent commercial-arts program. The pride and joy of our Office Practice Class was one of IBM's "ugly black machines with red keys." Through the years, I have used progressively more sophisticated IBM typewriters (not swayed by the salesman but by the product), and I would hope that you can appreciate my feeling that the computer can never, ever produce a letter that is as impressive as one typed on the machine that nobody can type with. But error-free, correctly spelled, proportionately spaced letters do not seem to matter any more in this fast-paced business world. When I retire in a year and a half, my boss says I may have this typewriter and you can bet it will be treated with respect and will receive good care in the hope that it will last as long as I do.

Marion DeBellis

Secretary to the President

Corvita Corp.

Miami
