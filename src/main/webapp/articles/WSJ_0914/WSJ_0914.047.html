Japanese Business Failures Dropped 8.6% in August

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 09/14/90

TOKYO -- The number of Japanese corporate bankruptcies, maintaining a downward trend, dropped 8.6% to 513 in August from a year earlier, a private credit agency said. Teikoku Data Bank Ltd. noted, however, that the number of business failures resulting from labor shortages came to a record 35 in the month.

"Because of continued favorable economic conditions and changes in the country's industrial structure now under way, labor shortages are becoming more serious regardless of area or industry," it said. "As a result, the number of companies finding themselves in trouble has been increasing sharply."

Japan's economy has been expanding for 46 months in a row because of robust domestic demand such as corporate equipment investment and personal spending. In the process of expansion, however, it has created serious manpower shortages in the country.

Agency officials said bankruptcies related to labor shortages have totaled 157 so far this year, already considerably more than the 128 recorded for the entire year of 1989. The August total was larger, though only by one case, than the previous monthly record of 34 set in July.
