SEC Charges Five With Bilking Pension Funds and Investors

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/14/90

WASHINGTON -- The Securities and Exchange Commission charged a leasing firm official, a pension consultant and three others with operating an investment scheme that cost investors, including at least 15 pension funds, more than $26 million. The SEC charged that from 1983 through 1987, Governmental Financial Services, operating under the direction of Charles Oglebay III of Upper Marlboro, Md., sold $57 million in unregistered securities to about 800 investors. Investors were told that their funds would be used to purchase equipment leases and that they would receive periodic returns based on income from the leases.

But by 1984, many of the lessees became delinquent in their rental payments or defaulted, leading Governmental Financial and Mr. Oglebay to operate what amounted to a Ponzi scheme, the SEC said. Mr. Oglebay began using funds from new investors to pay previous investors, oversold nearly $10 million in leases, and induced investors to waive their monthly payments by telling them they would be reinvested, the agency charged.

The SEC also alleged that Mr. Oglebay and Ralph McKittrick, president of New York-based McKittrick & Briggs Securities, misappropriated nearly $2.9 million of investor funds.

The agency also charged Glenn Dawson, who was president of Chesapeake Bay Pension Consultants and owned 25% of Governmental Financial, with recommending that his clients invest in the leasing firm "without disclosing any information about the securities." The agency said at least 15 pension clients invested in various programs connected with Governmental Financial and that Mr. Dawson didn't disclose that he received more than $150,000 in commissions from the company. The SEC didn't identify the pension funds.

By 1987, Governmental Financial had paid investors about $31.6 million, even though its equipment leases had produced revenue of only about $13.3 million, the SEC said. The remainder of the funds came from other investors, the agency added. Governmental Financial filed for protection under the federal Bankruptcy Code in December 1987.

Messrs. Oglebay, McKittrick and Dawson couldn't be reached for comment.

The SEC requested a federal court order directing the defendants to return all of the money, plus interest, obtained through the allegedly fraudulent scheme. The SEC also is seeking permanent injunctions against Messrs. Oglebay, McKittrick, and others named in the suit.
