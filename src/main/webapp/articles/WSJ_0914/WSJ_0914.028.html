Who's News: Comptek's Wagner Resigns; Cummings Is Elected Chariman

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/14/90

BUFFALO, N.Y. -- Comptek Research Inc. said its chairman and chief executive officer, Jack E. Wagner, resigned, effective immmediately. John R. Cummings, 57 years old, formerly vice president and chief scientist at Comptek, was elected chairman and appointed acting chief executive and president. The board said its executive committee will conduct a search over the next several months to find a permanent successor to Mr. Wagner, 42, who had worked at the company since 1969.

Comptek develops command, control communications and intelligence systems and electronic warfare systems, primarily for the Defense Department.

On the American Stock Exchange, Comptek stock closed at $5.125 a share, up 37.5 cents.

Mr. Wagner, who also was president, said the company is in sound financial shape and that a capable management team is in place. "This is an appropriate time for me to move on to other challenges," Mr. Wagner said.

In the fiscal year ended March 31, Comptek's profits more than doubled to $1.4 million, or 63 cents a share, as sales rose 3% to $49.6 million. At year end, the company's backlog of orders was at $49.5 million, up 53% from a year earlier.
