Stock-Index Arbitrage Volume on Big Board Is Given for Week

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/14/90

NEW YORK -- Stock-index arbitrage, the most controversial form of program trading, accounted for nearly half of all program trading volume at the New York Stock Exchange during the final week of August. The Big Board reported that program trading strategies accounted for 12.7% of average daily volume of 127.9 million shares during the week of Aug. 27 through 31. Some 81.2 million shares traded in program strategies, and 38.9 million shares, or 47.9% of total program volume, were traded in stock-index arbitrage strategies.

During the week, Kidder, Peabody & Co., a unit of General Electric Co., traded 15.7 million shares in program strategies, the most of any firm. Kidder also had the highest stock-index arbitrage volume, trading 7.9 million shares.

Among the top five program trading firms for the week were Morgan Stanley Group Inc. (14 million shares); Susquehanna Investment Group (8.6 million shares); Bear, Stearns & Co. (8.6 million shares), and CS First Boston Corp. (7.4 million shares). Susquehanna and Bear Stearns accounted for 58% of all proprietary program trading on the Big Board, the exchange said.
