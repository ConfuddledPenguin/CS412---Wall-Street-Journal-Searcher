Business Brief -- General Motors Corp.: Auto Maker Issues Recall For 30,700 Vans and Buses

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/14/90

General Motors Corp. said it is recalling about 30,700 1990 model vans and school buses for fuel hose defects that could cause engine fires. GM is "investigating one report of a fire in a van to see if it's related to this condition," a company spokesman said. The spokesman said GM isn't aware of any engine fires involving the school buses or any accidents or injuries resulting from a faulty fuel hose.

The recall includes about 29,000 Chevrolet Astro and GMC Safari vans with 4.3-liter or 2.5-liter engines, and nearly 1,700 Chevrolet and GMC school buses equipped with 366 V-8 gas engines. GM said a faulty hose could leak gasoline, and that could result in a fire under the hood.

Detroit-based GM said it is notifying owners about the recall by mail. Dealers will make repairs at no charge, the company said.
