International: German Cigarette Firms Merge

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/10/90

FRANKFURT, West Germany -- West Germany's Reemtsma Cigarettenfabrieken G.m.b.H. said it acquired East Germany's largest cigarette maker, Nortrak G.m.b.H., formerly the state-owned VEB Tabak Nordhausen. Reemtsma said Nortrak sold 10 billion cigarettes in 1989 and controlled a third of the East German cigarette market. The company has 1,000 employees.

Reemtsma is 51% family owned, with 49% held by Hamburg-based coffee maker Tchibo Frisch-Roest-Kaffee AG, which in turn is wholly owned by the Herz family of West Germany.
