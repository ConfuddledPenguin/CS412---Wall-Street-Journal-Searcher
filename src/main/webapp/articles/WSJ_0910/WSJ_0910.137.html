International: Lufthansa's Interflug Bid Draws British Air Protest

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/10/90

BRUSSELS -- British Airways filed a formal complaint with the European Community Commission charging Lufthansa with anti-competitive behavior regarding its bid for 26% of East Germany's state-owned airline, Interflug. British Airways, which itself held unsuccessful talks with Interflug this year, said, "We feel that if Lufthansa is allowed to carry on (with its plans for the airline) it will make free and fair competition in Germany very difficult and will not be in the general interests of fair competition."

The West German Federal Cartel Office has said it will object to Lufthansa's plans to take a stake in Interflug because such an all-German combination would be dominant in the national market. The cartel office's final ruling on the bid is expected by the end of October.

EC officials declined to comment on the British Airways complaint. Lufthansa officials weren't available for comment.

Separately, the West German magazine Der Spiegel is expected to report that British Airways has made a bid for a 49% stake in Interflug. A British Airways spokesman said that the company wouldn't confirm or deny the report.
