International Brief -- Tate & Lyle PLCL: British Sugar Group Offers To Buy Unit of Berisford

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/10/90

Tate & Lyle PLC said it has made an offer to Berisford International PLC to buy the troubled British conglomerate's British Sugar subsidiary. Terms weren't disclosed, but David Lang of Henderson Crosthwaite estimates the value of British Sugar at #1 billion ($1.91 billion).

Tate & Lyle is one of several companies currently in talks with Berisford about the sale of British Sugar.

In March, Tate & Lyle made a preliminary offer for all of Berisford International, which is posting losses related to its U.S. property and jewelry interests.

In July, Tate & Lyle withdrew the offer after the Monopolies and Merger Commission announced it would review the merger plan for its impact on competition.

Berisford Chairman John Sclater said in July that the company is seeking to sell assets, including British Sugar, to cut debt.
