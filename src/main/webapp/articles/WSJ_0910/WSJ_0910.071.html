New Securities Issues

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/10/90

The following were among Friday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report. CORPORATE

Student Loan Marketing Association -- $100 million issue of notes, due Sept. 20, 1993, was priced at par to float initially at a spread of 100 basis points above the Treasury's three-month bill. The noncallable notes will float weekly and pay interest quarterly. The rate on the notes will be reset each quarter at a spread of 100 basis points above the three-month Treasury bill, except if the three-month London Interbank Offered Rate exceeds 9% on any quarterly payment date.

EUROBONDS

TMC PIMBS Fifth Financing PLC, (special-purpose vehicle, U.K. parent) -- #250 million of floating-rate notes backed by mortgages, due Aug. 2028, at issue price of par, via Salomon Brothers International Ltd. Expected average life 4.46 years, assuming rate of repayment of 18%. Coupon initially two-month London interbank offered rate plus 1/4%, then three-month Libor plus 1/4% to Aug. 2000 and three-month Libor plus 1/2% thereafter. Callable after five years if principal amount outstanding falls below #25 million pounds; also, after 10-years, and on subsequent interest payment dates, at par. Backed by a pool of U.K. mortgages originated by the issuer and insurance for a maximum 13% of losses and 3% of liquidity by Sun Alliance and London Insurance.

Asfinag (Austria) -- 150 million European currency units of 10 1/2% Eurobonds due Oct. 5, 1995, priced at 101.95, via Paribas Capital Markets Ltd. Guaranteed by the Republic of Austria.

NEC Corp. (Japan) -- $350 million of Eurobonds due Sept. 27, 1994, with equity-purchase warrants, indicating a 5 1/8% coupon at par, via Daiwa Europe Ltd. Each $10,000 bond carries two warrants, exercisable from Oct. 4, 1990 through Sept. 20, 1994 to buy company shares at an expected premium of 2 1/2% to the closing share price when terms are fixed Sept. 13.

Saitama Bank Ltd. (Japan) -- $100 million floating rate loan participation certificates, due Sept. 27, 2000, via Merrill Lynch International Ltd. Issued by Merrill Lynch AG and by backed a loan to Saitama Bank. Coupon is three-month London Interbank Offered Rate plus 32 1/2 basis points for the first five years, stepping up to 42 1/2 over Libor thereafter. Issue price par. Callable Sept. 1995, and on any subsequent interest payment date, at par.

Izumi Co. (Japan) -- $100 million of Eurobonds due Oct. 4, 1994 with equity-purchase warrants, indicating a 5 1/8% coupon at par, via Daiwa (Europe) Ltd. Each $5,000 bond carries one warrant, exercisable from Oct. 18, 1990 through Sept. 20, 1994, to buy company shares at an expected premium of 2 1/2% to the closing share price when terms are fixed Sept. 13.
