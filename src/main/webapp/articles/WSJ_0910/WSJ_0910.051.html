Chevron Wins Jet Fuel Contract

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/10/90

WASHINGTON -- Chevron USA, a unit of Chevron Corp., received a $153.9 million Defense Logistics Agency contract for jet fuel. The award was the largest of more than $500 million in jet fuel contracts announced by the Pentagon.

Other jet fuel contracts went to Phoenix Petroleum Co., $76.8 million; Amoco Oil Co., a unit of Amoco Corp., $75.1 million; Barrett Refining Corp., $57.5 million; Hawaiian Independent Refinery Inc., a unit of Pacific Resources Inc., $56.7 million; El Paso Refining Co., $35.9 million; Western Petroleum Co., a unit of Western Co. of North America, $20.5 million; U.S. Oil & Refining Co. $14.8 million; Sun Refining & Marketing, a unit of Sun Co., $13.3 million; Giant Industries Arizona Inc., $11 million, and Gary-Williams Energy Corp., $10.7 million.

In addition, the Army awarded United Technologies Corp. a $77.6 million contract for helicopters. Hoechst Celanese Corp. got a $27 million Air Force contract for chemical defense suits.

Corrections & Amplifications

WESTERN PETROLEUM Co. is an independent company based in Eden Prairie, Minn. In Monday's edition, Western Petroleum was incorrectly identified as the subsidiary of another company.
