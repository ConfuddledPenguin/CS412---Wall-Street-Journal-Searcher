Technology: Former Syntex Scientist's Damage Award Is Reduced

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/10/90

SAN JOSE, Calif. -- A state court judge slashed to $3.5 million from $17.5 million an award of damages against Syntex Corp. for wrongful discharge of a scientist. Judge Thomas P. Hansen of Superior Court for Santa Clara County termed "grossly excessive" a prior jury decision to award former Syntex scientist Gabriel Garay $2.5 million in compensatory damages and $15 million in punitive damages levied against the Palo Alto, Calif., drug company.

Mr. Garay left Syntex after protesting the company's suppression of information about possible toxic side-effects of an anti-ulcer drug. James Der, attorney for the plaintiff, said he was "disappointed" by the judge's decision to shave the award.

He said his client has until Sept. 20 to decide whether he will accept the reduced damages. However, Mr. Der emphasized that the judge refused to overturn the verdict of wrongful discharge of Mr. Garay.

If the reduced award isn't accepted by either side, the judge said the case may go to a new trial on damages only. Syntex officials had no immediate comment on whether they would appeal the reduced award.
