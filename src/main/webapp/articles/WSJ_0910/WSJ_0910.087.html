Business Brief -- ComFed Savings Bank: Former Officer Enters Plea Of Guilty in Investigation

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/10/90

A former senior vice president of a ComFed Savings Bank unit pleaded guilty to several charges, the fourth employee to plead guilty in a federal investigation of the bank. Vincent J. Crupi pleaded guilty to five counts of bank fraud, five counts of making statements to a federally insured bank, and one count of conspiracy relating to ComFed. He was manager of ComFed's branch in Boston.

Wayne A. Budd, U.S. attorney in Massachusetts, said that if the case went to trial, the government would have introduced evidence that during the fall of 1987 and the spring of 1988, Mr. Crupi falsified information concerning the purchase price, income and assets of ComFed borrowers at a condominium development in Revere, Mass. The goverment alleged that Mr. Crupi entered into the scheme to defraud ComFed to obtain greater commissions on the loans he originated.

Sentencing was set for Nov. 2. Mr. Crupi faces a maximum sentence of 40 years in prison and $2,750,000 in fines.
