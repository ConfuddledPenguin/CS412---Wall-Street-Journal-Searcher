Justin's Board Voids Election Votes After Group Protests Bylaw

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/10/90

FORT WORTH, Texas -- Justin Industries Inc., stung by charges that its directors secretly made it more difficult for shareholders to remove them, said the board has now reversed itself. The surprise move appears to strengthen the hand of a dissident shareholder group trying to acquire the brick, building materials and boot maker. It also could make it more difficult for Justin to complete a planned acquisition of Tony Lama Co., an El Paso, Texas boot maker, for about $18 million plus the assumption of about $34 million in debt.

The controversy arose from a vote by the Justin board to raise the number of shareholders' votes needed to remove directors to 75% of total shares outstanding from 50% of the stock voted by a quorum of shareholders. Although the amendment passed in November 1989, the company failed to make the change public until last month, according to the dissident group, which is led by Perry H. Sutherland of Kansas City, Mo., and Barry S. Rosenstein of New York.

In a motion in federal court here last week, the group sought the court's permission to file a complaint claiming that Justin violated federal securities laws.

In the proposed complaint, the group seeks, among other things, for the court to void the election of Justin shareholders in the annual meeting last March; to enjoin the directors from proceeding with the planned acquisition of Tony Lama; and to order the company and its directors to pay actual and punitive damages.

Justin, in response, said it "vehemently disagrees" that it committed any wrongdoing and said it plans to "vigorously" contest the legal claims. But it added that its board rescinded its November vote anyway because it "wishes to avoid any appearance of unfairness to any of its shareholders."

Neither the company, nor a representative of the dissident group, which owns 12% of Justin's common, would comment further.

In national over-the-counter trading, Justin shares closed unchanged at $13.75. Tony Lama closed at $8.50, up 12.5 cents.
