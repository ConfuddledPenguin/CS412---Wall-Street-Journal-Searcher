Australia Firms Post 11% Drop in Profits For Latest Quarter

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 09/10/90

CANBERRA, Australia -- Pretax earnings of Australian companies fell 11% in the quarter ended June 30 from the quarter ended March 31, and dropped 25% from the year-earlier quarter, the Bureau of Statistics said Friday. The Bureau also revised downward company profits for the quarter ended March 31 to show no change from the quarter ended Dec. 31. It originally had reported that profits rose 9% in the March quarter.

Australian company profits have declined or been flat for the past four consecutive quarters. The Bureau also has revised downward profits for the quarter ended Sept. 30 to show an 11% drop, compared with a previously reported 7% decline.

Mining-company profits, which had been climbing for a year, dropped a seasonally adjusted 16% in the June quarter.

Manufacturing-company profits fell 17%, while wholesale and retail-company profits fell 5.9%.

Mining-company profits rose 10% from a year earlier, while all other sectors weakened.

For the full year ended June 30, Australian company profits before tax and seasonal adjustments fell 6.1%. Mining-company profits for the year jumped 41%, but manufacturing-company profits fell 9.4%, wholesale earnings fell 21% and retail-company profits rose 1.8%.
