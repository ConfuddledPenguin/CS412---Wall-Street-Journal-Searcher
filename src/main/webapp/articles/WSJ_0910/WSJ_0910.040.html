Healthco Majority Is Claimed by Gemini In Bid for New Board

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 09/10/90

Gemini Partners Limited Partnership, seeking to unseat Healthco International Inc.'s board, said it has a majority of consent cards executed by Healthco shareholders that, if certified, would mean removal of Healthco's current board. Healthco, a dental-equipment company based in Boston, responded by questioning the validity and the number of the consents collected by Gemini.

Gemini, a New York limited partnership with a 9.96% stake in Healthco, said it delivered to Healthco written consents representing holders of about 4,200,700 shares, or about 54%, of Healthco's common stock outstanding out of a total of 7,734,592 shares as of June 25, the record date for the consent solicitation.

But Healthco questions Gemini's count. "Based on our review, it appears that Gemini doesn't have, as it has claimed, consents representing more than 50% of Healthco's stock. Moreover, all of Gemini's consents would appear to have been obtained in violation of federal proxy rules and therefore, are invalid," Healthco said.

Gemini filed a lawsuit last week in Delaware Chancery Court against Healthco and its current directors seeking a judgment declaring that the current board has been lawfully removed by Healthco shareholders and that Gemini's seven-member slate has been elected in their place.

Healthco, which last agreed to be acquired for $19.25 a share, or about $250 million, by Hicks, Muse & Co., a Dallas leveraged-buy-out specialist, postponed the annual meeting scheduled for Sept. 6 to Sept. 25. The postponement was announced the night before the meeting was to be held. The record date for the meeting remains the same. The acquisition is subject to shareholder approval.

In its lawsuit, Gemini charges that the Healthco meeting was postponed "only after it was virtually certain that Healthco's stockholders were about to oust the board" in favor of Gemini's slate.

Gemini has charged that the board agreed to the Hicks Muse acquisition to avoid losing the consent solicitation. It said that the acquisition price is inadequate and that it may retain an investment banker to help seek buyers for Healthco.

Healthco said the meeting was postponed because the Securities and Exchange Commission expressed concern over whether stockholders would have time to react to the acquisition agreement and because of "misinformation" in the marketplace concerning the proposed acquisition.

Separately, Healthco's counsel said late Friday that the company has been told by Hicks Muse that it filed suit in Texas against the Healthco board backed by Gemini. The suit asserts that the Gemini group violated certain agreements in the merger accord between Healthco and Hicks Muse. The suit asks unspecified damages and charges breach of good faith by the Gemini-backed board.
