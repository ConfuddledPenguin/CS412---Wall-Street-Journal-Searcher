Technology Brief -- Seeq Technology Inc.: Firm to Stop Making Chips, Plans a $10 Million Charge

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/10/90

Seeq Technology Inc. said it will stop mass producing chips at its San Jose, Calif., plant and will take a pretax charge of up to $10 million to lay off 95 employees and convert the plant to a research-and-development facility. After the conversion, which is expected to be completed in March 1991, Seeq said it will have all its chips made for it by other companies under contract. Seeq said that the conversion, which will result in a $8 million to $10 million charge in its fourth quarter, will reduce expenses and allow it to concentrate on developing new products, new technologies and marketing.

Seeq began contracting out some of its production in 1989 when it formed a pact with a U.S. unit of the Netherlands' N.V. Philips, which makes parts to Seeq's specifications in an Albuquerque, N.M., plant. Taiwan's Hualon Microelectronics Corp., which holds 10% of Seeq's stock, also began making chips for Seeq in July.
