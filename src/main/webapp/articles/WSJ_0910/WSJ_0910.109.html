Business Brief -- Worthen Banking Corp.: Arkansas Concern Completes Independence Federal Deal

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/10/90

Worthen Banking Corp. said it completed the controversial acquisition of certain assets and liabilities of Independence Federal Bank, making the bank holding company's Worthen Bank & Trust Co. unit the first commercial bank in Arkansas to operate a statewide network of branches. Arkansas banking authorities had opposed Worthen's plans to purchase the failed thrift from the Resolution Trust Corp., arguing that the move would violate state restrictions on bank branching. But a federal appeals court in St. Louis ruled that the RTC, the federal agency handling the S&L cleanup, has the power to override such state banking laws.

Worthen, Little Rock, Ark., said it paid $1.5 million to acquire $320 million in deposits and certain assets of Independence, including 20 branch locations. The purchase more than doubles the number of communities served by Worthen.
