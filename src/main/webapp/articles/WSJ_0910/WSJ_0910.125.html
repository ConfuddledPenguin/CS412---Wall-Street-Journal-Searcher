International Brief -- Porsche AG: Sales Jumped 21% in 1990 At West German Company

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/10/90

Porsche AG, citing a strong climb in domestic sales and an improved product mix, said group sales in the year ended July 31 jumped 21% to 3.045 billion marks ($1.96 billion) from 2.526 billion marks in the previous year. The West German sports-car maker said earnings "improved further" from last year's earnings due to cost-cutting measures introduced last year and an increase in sales volume. It provided no details. Last year, net income more than doubled to 54.2 million marks from 25.3 million marks in the previous year.

Stuttgart-based Porsche said domestic sales climbed 26% to 943 million marks from 747 million marks while foreign sales climbed 18% to 2.1 billion marks from 1.78 billion marks. Foreign sales accounted for 69% of the company's sales, down from 70% a year earlier.

Sales of vehicles climbed 20% to 2.56 billion marks from 2.14 billion marks.

The company said sales of all models declined in the U.S., in particular sales of its 944 models. Unit sales in the U.S. fell 23% to 7,852 from 10,193.
