Business Brief -- Rax Restaurants Inc.: Fast-Food Chain to Slash Its Corporate Staff by 25%

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/10/90

Rax Restaurants Inc., hurt by slower sales at its fast-food outlets and higher operating costs, said it will slash 25% of its 130-person corporate staff as part of a restructuring plan. "It's important that we deal with our internal expenses at this time since they are such a major component of our costs," said Ira A. Hermann, president and chief executive officer of Rax, based in Columbus, Ohio.

For the third quarter ended April 30, Rax reported a loss of $736,000, or 10 cents a share, on revenue of $27.4 million.

The chain of 450 restaurants has spent heavily on new products. The products have shown promise, but other chains have introduced similar menu items.

The fast-food industry also has been hit by a drop in customer traffic, analysts said, and there is the added pressure to keep prices low as the bigger chains discount products to attract more business. The price-cutting strategies can work because some customers "at the margin notice if the price is 10 cents more or less" and will switch to other restaurants, says Michael Mueller, restaurant analyst at Montgomery Securities in San Francisco.
