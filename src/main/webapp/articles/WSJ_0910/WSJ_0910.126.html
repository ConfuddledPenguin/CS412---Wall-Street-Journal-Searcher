What's News -- World-Wide

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/10/90

BUSH AND GORBACHEV ISSUED a joint warning to Iraq's Saddam Hussein. The two leaders, meeting in Finland, told Baghdad that they would consider unspecified "additional" steps if the Iraqis failed to heed U.N. demands to withdraw from Kuwait. Although Washington has said a military strike at Iraq is still an option, Gorbachev said the Soviets wouldn't join the land and sea forces in the Mideast, stressing the crisis must be resolved politically.

Arab leaders have pledged billions of dollars to cover the cost of U.S. forces in Saudi Arabia. Separately, as Iraq's foreign minister met with officials in Iran over the U.N. embargo, hundreds of foreigners left Iraq. Palestinian radical Abul Abbas said the time might be ripe for renewed commando warfare in support of Baghdad.

Syria said that a series of explosions were heard in an Iraqi border town, possibly at a chemical plant. Baghdad denied the report from Damascus.

---

Liberia's Doe was shot in both legs and captured by a rebel faction in Monrovia, the British Broadcasting Corp. reported. More than 60 people, including dozens of the president's bodyguards, died in the clash with insurgents led by Prince Johnson, who declared himself president until an interim government can be formed, the BBC said.

---

Lebanese police reported at least 75 people were killed during a three-day offensive by the Palestine Liberation Organization to oust guerrillas loyal to terrorist leader Abu Nidal from Sidon and nearby refugee camps. The PLO claimed victory against the supporters of Abu Nidal, who broke with the PLO in 1973 to form his own group.

---

Federal and state officials are preparing to demand a third round of cleanup by Exxon next summer along the beaches of Alaska's Prince William Sound, site of the largest oil spill in U.S. history. More than 10 million gallons of crude oil spilled when an Exxon tanker rammed an underwater reef in March 1989.

---

A split developed among European Community nations over the speed and method for achieving monetary union, raising the likelihood of difficult negotiations among members this year. At a meeting of EC finance ministers Saturday, Britain won unexpected support for a more cautious approach to the issue.

---

South African police opened fire to disperse crowds of blacks in Soweto angered by an overnight attack on a squatter camp that left at least 10 people dead. Residents accused Zulu supporters of the conservative Inkatha movement of launching the nighttime attack with police help.

---

Lt. Gov. Evelyn Murphy took charge in Massachusetts after Dukakis left the state on a European trade mission. In the governor's absence, Murphy -- who is a candidate for governor -- issued a set of budget-cutting proposals Friday that she said could save $150 million annually. Dukakis is slated to return late this week.

---

Ontario's New Democratic Party sought to reassure a nervous business community after the socialist party was elected to rule the Canadian province. The party's surprise victory Thursday is of national consequence because Ontario accounts for over 40% of Canada's output and contains more than a third of its people.

---

Cambodia's warring groups endorsed a U.N. plan to end their 11-year civil war, but major obstacles have yet to be resolved. The three allied guerrilla groups and the Vietnamese-backed Phnom Penh government concluded their first day of peace talks yesterday in Indonesia.

---

Cardiologist Bernadine Healy was chosen by Health and Human Services Secretary Sullivan to become the director of the National Institutes of Health. HHS officials said Sullivan is about to choose David Kessler, medical director of the Albert Einstein College of Medicine in New York, as the next FDA commissioner.

---

Workers and intellectuals staged a joint rally in the central Romanian city of Brasov to protest what they said was the government's failure to solve the country's severe economic problems.

---

Pete Sampras beat fellow American Andre Agassi in straight sets to win the men's singles title at the U.S. Open tennis championships. On Saturday, Gabriela Sabatini defeated Steffi Graf in the women's final.

---

Died: A.J.P. Taylor, 84, popular British historian and journalist, Friday, in London, of Parkinson's disease.
