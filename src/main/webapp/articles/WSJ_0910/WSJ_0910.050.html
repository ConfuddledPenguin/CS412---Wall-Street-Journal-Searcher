Business Brief -- Baltimore Bancorp: Bank Asks Fed to Examine Actions of T. Rowe Price

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/10/90

Baltimore Bancorp asked federal regulators to investigate whether T. Rowe Price Associates Inc., the bank's largest shareholder, is illegally trying to influence the company's actions. In a letter to the Federal Reserve Board, Baltimore Bancorp alleged that T. Rowe Price, which owns 9.4% of the company, is attempting to exercise a "controlling influence" over the bank without required federal approval.

In a filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission last week, T. Rowe Price said it was considering its options in light of Baltimore Bancorp's recent rejection of an unsolicited $17-a-share offer from a unit of Allied Irish Banks PLC.

T. Rowe Price said in the filing that it "may communicate with management, other shareholders or any other interested persons to determine what alternative courses of action may better serve" its interests. The Baltimore investment firm also said it might sell or buy more shares.

"It's very clear {T. Rowe Price} is trying to influence what management is doing," said H. Rodgin Cohen, an attorney with Sullivan & Cromwell, which is representing the bank. "And you can't exercise control of a bank without getting the Federal Reserve's prior approval."

In a statement, T. Rowe Price said its actions are "consistent with all applicable laws and regulations."
