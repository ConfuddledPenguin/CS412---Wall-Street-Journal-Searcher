Three Steel Concerns In Japan Plan to Raise Fiscal 1st-Half Payout

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/10/90

TOKYO -- Three major Japanese steel companies separately announced plans to raise dividends for the fiscal first half ending Sept. 30. They cited expectations of higher sales during the period, reflecting brisk domestic demand for crude steel. The three steel concerns, Nippon Steel Corp., Kobe Steel Ltd., and Sumitomo Metal Industries Ltd., each plan to raise the interim dividend payment to 3 yen (US two cents) from 2.5 yen a year earlier.

Nippon Steel predicted, on an unconsolidated basis, that it will post a rise in sales for the first half to 1.25 trillion yen (US$888.4 billion) yen from 1.238 trillion yen.

Citing rising costs on fuel prices, as well as fierce sales competition, Nippon Steel's pretax profit is estimated at about 80 billion yen, down from 92.3 billion yen, according to officials. However, the largest Japanese steel maker said it expects drastic increases in both sales and pretax income in the second half. The company didn't give estimates.

Kobe Steel forecast that it would post unconsolidated sales of 640 billion yen, the highest half-year sales total for the company, up from 602 billion yen the year before. Pretax profit was flat at 28 billion yen, compared with 28.2 billion yen a year earlier.

Sumitomo sees its sales reaching about 570 billion yen in the first half from 544.2 billion yen the year before. The company forecast its pretax profit registered about 40 billion yen, compared with 47.4 billion yen a year earlier.
