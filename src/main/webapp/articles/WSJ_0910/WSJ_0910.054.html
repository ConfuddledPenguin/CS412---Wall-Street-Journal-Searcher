Who's News: Kidder Peabody Unit Loses Key Executive In Program Trading

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/10/90

NEW YORK -- Kidder, Peabody & Co.'s busy program trading unit has lost yet another key member. Last week, senior trader Robert Deutsch left Kidder to join Nomura Securities International Inc. The unit also recently lost options trader Ramesh Menon to County NatWest Ltd., which is building a program trading unit under the direction of executive director Gregory Kipnis, formerly of Morgan Stanley Group Inc.

The departures come six months after Nomura jumped into program trading with the hiring of Joseph R. Schmuckler, formerly in charge of customer program trading at Kidder. Kidder is a unit of General Electric Co.

Mr. Deutsch was an important member of Kidder's proprietary stock-index arbitrage unit, led by senior vice president Peter Klein, according to people familiar with the unit. When he starts at Nomura next month, Mr. Deutsch will be responsible for stock-index arbitrage strategies in U.S. markets.

"We've brought in a first-class team of traders," said Mr. Schmuckler, who has hired a staff of 10 market makers and analysts, including former members of Salomon Brothers equity department.

Thomas Ryan, head of equity trading at Kidder, said the he has no plans for restructuring the program trading unit. "We are quite pleased with the people we have and the results that we're getting," Mr. Ryan said.

For much of the summer, Kidder has been one of the most active program trading firms, principally due to a large amount of stock-index arbitrage for the firm's own account. Recent New York Stock Exchange data show that Kidder traded 6.9 million shares for its own account in program trading strategies during the week ended Aug. 24. Overall, Kidder traded 9.8 million shares in program trading strategies, and 9.3 million of that was stock-index arbitrage.

In stock-index arbitrage, traders dart between stocks, futures and options to profit from fleeting price differences.
