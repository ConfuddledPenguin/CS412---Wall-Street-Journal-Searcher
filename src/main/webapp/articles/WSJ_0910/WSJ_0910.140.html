Peat Marwick Board Said To Select New U.S. Chief

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/10/90

NEW YORK -- The board of KPMG Peat Marwick nominated Jon C. Madonna to succeed Larry D. Horner as chairman and chief executive officer of the accounting and consulting firm's U.S. practice, sources close to the company said. Although a spokesman for the firm declined to confirm or deny Mr. Madonna's nomination, he said the 34-member board had met over the weekend to nominate a successor to Mr. Horner and that partners likely would be notified today of the board's choice. Mr. Madonna, 47 years old, is currently head of the firm's San Francisco office.

The board's nomination doesn't guarantee that Mr. Madonna will be elected by a majority of the firm's 1,850 partners, scheduled to meet Oct. 3 and 4. According to the spokesman, the firm's bylaws allow for 5% of the partners to nominate their own candidates; they have until Sept. 20 to do so.

The source also said that James G. Brocksmith Jr., 49, who heads the firm's Chicago office, was nominated as deputy chairman. Robert W. Beecher, who would reach the mandatory retirement age of 60 if he stands for another term, is currently deputy chairman.

Mr. Horner announced in early August that he would not seek re-election. His terms at KPMG's U.S. and international practices end Oct. 3 and Dec. 31, respectively. P.J. Butler, senior partner of the U.K. practice, has been chosen to succeed Mr. Horner at the international practice.

KPMG Peat Marwick was formed in the December 1986 merger of Peat Marwick International and Klynveld Main & Goerdeler.
