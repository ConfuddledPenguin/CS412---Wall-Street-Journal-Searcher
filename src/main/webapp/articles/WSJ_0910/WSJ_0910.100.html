Business Brief -- Kansas Gas & Electric Co.: Deferral by U.S. Is Sought On Kansas City P&L's Bid

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/10/90

Kansas Gas & Electric Co., Wichita, asked the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission to defer action on Kansas City Power & Light Co.'s $27-a-share, or $857 million, bid to acquire Kansas G&E. Kansas G&E's motion on Friday asked regulators to deny the Kansas City, Mo., utility's request for expedited approval of its acquisition plans; the takeover bid was launched July 23. Kansas G&E claims the federal agency should wait until a federal lawsuit, which challenges the legality of the tender offer, is resolved.

The motion filed with the regulatory agency stated that "it would not be in the public interest for the commission or the parties to consider the application if the transaction forming its basis is illegal." A Kansas City P&L spokesman said the offer complies with all legal requirements.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading Friday, Kansas G&E shares closed at $25.375, up 50 cents, and Kansas City P&L shares closed at $29.75, up 37.5 cents.
