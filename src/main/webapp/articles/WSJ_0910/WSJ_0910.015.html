Technology Brief -- Olsten Corp.: Employment Firm Agrees To Acquire Upjohn Unit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/10/90

Olsten Corp., in a move that would triple its home health-care business, said it agreed to acquire Upjohn HealthCare Services Inc., a unit of Upjohn Co. Terms weren't disclosed, but some analysts estimated the transaction value at $60 million to $70 million. The unit, which provides home health-care and supplemental staffing to hospitals and nursing homes, accounted for $182 million, or about 6%, of Upjohn's total 1989 revenue.

With the acquisition, Olsten of Westbury, N.Y., obtains a national network of home health-care offices already in place and a new foothold in Canada. For Upjohn, the Kalamazoo, Mich., maker of ethical drugs, the sale effectively ends its arduous efforts to lift a business that's been a drain on profits.

"This particular division was a problem area for Upjohn," said Jerry Levine, analyst with Merrill Lynch. "It never made a lot of money for them and it didn't have synergy with the company's plans."

For Olsten, however, the acquisition allows it to "effectively blanket North America with offices,"said Frank N. Liguori, the company's chief executive officer. "It saves us years of internal growth and development."

Olsten, which provides temporary personnel for the office and home health-care markets, earned $18 million on revenue of $580 million in 1989.
