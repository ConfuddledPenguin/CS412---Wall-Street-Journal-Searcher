Business Brief -- Ford Motor Co.: Two Assembly Plants Slated For One-Week Shutdowns

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/10/90

Ford Motor Co., Detroit, said the Atlanta assembly plant that builds the Ford Taurus and Mercury Sable and the Lorain, Ohio, plant that builds the Econoline Wagon will shut for a week each because of slow sales. The Atlanta facility has been down a total of four weeks so far this year. The latest production cut comes at a time when Ford's dealer-inventory levels of the cars are relatively low. At July 31, there was a 63-day supply of the Taurus and a 50-day supply of the Sable. About a 60-65-day supply is considered an ideal inventory level in the industry.

But sales of the models are slumping. In the latest 10-day period, sales of the Taurus and Sable fell 32.7%.

The shutdowns will idle a total of 2,500 hourly workers.
