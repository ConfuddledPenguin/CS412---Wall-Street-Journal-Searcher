Business Brief -- Heineken N.V.: Dutch Brewer's Net Income Increased 18% in First Half

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/10/90

Heineken N.V. combined higher volumes of beer sales and higher prices to put the fizz back in its profit, which surged 18% in the first half. The rise to 165.4 million guilders ($94.3 million) in the first half from 140.3 million guilders a year earlier surprised Dutch analysts who had predicted a rate of earnings growth of between 5% and 10% for the Amsterdam-based beer concern.

On a per-share basis, profit was 5.15 guilders, up from the year-earlier 4.37 guilders. Analysts had expected around 4.6 guilders.

The brewer said it expects second-half earnings to at least match those of the year-earlier's 185.2 million guilders. Although the forecast was conservative, analysts noted Heineken has a tradition of understating its performance.

Sales increased by 7.5% to 4.15 billion guilders from 3.86 billion guilders.

Heineken, which announced its results after the close of trading Friday, closed at 114.7 guilders, up 0.9 guilder in a generally stronger market.
