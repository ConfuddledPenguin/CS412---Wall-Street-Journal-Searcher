Japan Concerns Seen Posting 2% Decline In Fiscal '91 Profits

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 09/10/90

TOKYO -- Earnings of major Japanese corporations are expected to fall 0.2% in the year ending March 31 from the previous year, putting an end to a three-year stretch of gains, a research institute attached to Sanyo Securities Co. said. The think tank said its survey of 494 companies showed that pretax earnings by all industries will drop as a result of surging oil prices and interest rates. Sales are expected to edge up 0.5%.

When oil companies and electric power suppliers are excluded, however, earnings will register a 3.2% gain, while sales will remain flat, the Sanyo institute said. Oil and electric power companies are expected to suffer most heavily from the rising oil prices.

The research institute, citing the reasons for its guarded earnings projections, expects oil prices to jump some 50% in the second half of this fiscal year due to the intensifying Mideast situation while the series of five increases in the official discount rate since May last year will make their combined impact felt. In addition, a slowdown in the U.S. economy will serve to brake Japan's exports, it said.

Sanyo sees oil prices averaging $24.5 a barrel during this fiscal year, up from an average $17.9 a barrel in fiscal 1990.

It forecast that manufacturer earnings will rise 3.8%, slowing from double-digit gains in the three previous fiscal years, while non-manufacturer profits will display a 7.7% decline.

The shipbuilding, construction and service industries will post pretax earnings gains of more than 10%, while the machinery and auto industries will also fare well, it predicted.

On the other hand, the paper-pulp and oil industries will each register declines, of 18% and 13%, respectively, the institute said.
