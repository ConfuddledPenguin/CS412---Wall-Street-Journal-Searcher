International: Claims Daimler Unit Sold Arms to Iraq Spurs Probe

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 09/10/90

FRANKFURT -- West German authorities are investigating one of the aerospace subsidiaries of Daimler-Benz AG for alleged violations of export-control laws in the delivery of military hardware to Iraq. The public prosecutor's office in Munich said it has begun the investigation of aircraft maker Messerschmitt-Boelkow-Blohm G.m.b.H., or MBB, a majority-owned subsidiary of Daimler, West Germany's largest industrial group.

Heinz Stocker, senior district attorney in Munich, said the inquiry follows allegations last week by a German human-rights group, the Society for Endangered People, that it uncovered what it called military hardware for Iraq at an MBB storage site in Hoehenkirchen.

MBB denied the allegations that it planned to ship military hardware to Iraq. It said it halted sales to Iraq in accordance with the United Nations embargo, of which West Germany is a participant. The company said it has been selling helicopters to Iraq since 1978, but that those machines haven't been outfitted for military applications. The company also supplies replacement parts for the helicopters, but MBB said Friday that it ceased such deliveries to Iraq Aug. 6.

MBB said the parcels at the depot are being held there because of the Iraqi trade embargo.
