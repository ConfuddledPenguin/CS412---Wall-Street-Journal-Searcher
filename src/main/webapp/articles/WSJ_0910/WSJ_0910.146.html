Marketing & Media: Double Helix, Odyssey Entertainment Merger Approved by Holders

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/10/90

LOS ANGELES -- Shareholders of Odyssey Entertainment Ltd. and Double Helix Films Inc. approved the merger of the film-distribution concerns with a new company, Communications & Entertainment Corp. Communications & Entertainment, based in Los Angeles, was formed in December for the purpose of the merger. J. David Marks, Odyssey's former chairman and chief executive officer, is president and chief executive of the new company.

Mr. Marks said that Communications & Entertainment will "cover the world marketplace demand for film." Both predecessor companies distributed foreign and U.S. films overseas, with Odyssey selling mainly to theatrical markets and New York-based Double Helix mainly to video, television and cable markets.

"The combination will resolve (Odyssey's) liquidity problems, which were at worst short-term," Mr. Marks said. Last month, Odyssey said it needed additional working capital to continue its operations at current levels. In its first quarter ended June 30, Odyssey posted net income of $263,916, including a $600,000 fee from Double Helix for consulting services.

In the merger, each share of Odyssey common stock will be exchanged for one share of Communications & Entertainment Class A stock. Each Class A share is convertible into common at a conversation ratio of .6375. Each share of Double Helix common will be exchanged for one share in the new company.

In national over-the-counter trading Friday, Odyssey shares closed at $1, up 9.36 cents, and Double Helix shares closed at $2, up 6.25 cents. Communications & Entertainment said its Class A and common shares will begin trading today under the symbols CECO.A and CECO, respectively.
