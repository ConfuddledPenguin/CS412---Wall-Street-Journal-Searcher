Morgan Stanley Topped Program Traders in Week

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/10/90

NEW YORK -- Morgan Stanley Group Inc. conducted 24.2% of all program trading during the week of Aug. 20 to 24, the New York Stock Exchange said. Morgan Stanley traded 23.4 million shares in program trading strategies for the period, 6.9 million of which involved stock-index arbitrage. The firm traded only 2.5 million shares for its own account with the rest involving customer orders. Some 96.7 million shares traded in program trading strategies during the period, according to the exchange.

In stock-index arbitrage, the most controversial form of program trading, traders dart between stocks, futures and options to profit from fleeting price discrepancies.

Overall, program trading accounted for 10.2% of the Big Board's average daily volume of 189.9 million shares for the period, down from 13.9% of average daily volume of 148 million the previous week.

Of the average 19.3 million shares of program volume executed daily during that week, buy programs accounted for an average 6.9 million shares daily and sell programs accounted for an average 12.4 million shares daily, the exchange said.

Behind Morgan, Susquehanna Investment Group traded 16.1 million shares in program trading strategies, and Kidder, Peabody & Co., a unit of General Electric Co., traded 9.8 million shares. Most of Kidder's activity involved stock-index arbitrage.
