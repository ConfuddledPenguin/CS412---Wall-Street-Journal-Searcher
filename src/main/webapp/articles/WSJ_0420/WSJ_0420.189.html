Marketing & Media: MCA Net Grew 15% In 1st Period Despite Cineplex Odeon Loss

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/20/90

UNIVERSAL CITY, Calif. -- MCA Inc. said that first-quarter net income rose 15% to $31.6 million, or 42 cents a share, despite a $21.5 million loss attributable to the entertainment concern's stake in Cineplex Odeon Corp. In the year-earlier quarter, MCA posted net income of $27.3 million, or 37 cents a share. Those results included $5.2 million of profit from Cineplex Odeon, the Toronto-based movie theater chain in which MCA holds a 49.9% stake.

Revenue in the latest quarter jumped 30% to $912.9 million from $703.3 million.

MCA results were bolstered by the strong performance of the company's filmed entertainment group, which benefited from the continuing success of such releases as "Back to the Future Part II" and "Born on the Fourth of July." Theatrical films revenue more than doubled in the quarter to $184.2 million from $79.9 million.

Revenue from the company's home video and pay television operations increased by 48% to $169.4 million from $114.3 million. However, the filmed entertainment group's results were hurt by lower foreign television sales.

MCA's pretax operating profit climbed 8% to $71.1 million, including $75.7 million from the filmed entertainment group. In the year-ago quarter, operating profit was $65.6 million, including $42.6 million from filmed entertainment.

MCA's music division posted a 8% increase in operating profit to $12.6 million, on a 35% increase in sales of $208.5 million. Book publishing profit jumped 72% to $9.9 million from $5.7 million. Retail and mail order business continued to show a loss, $9.4 million compared with $8.3 million last year, partly because the 1990 Easter holiday fell in the second quarter. Broadcasting and cable operations profits rose 8% to $3.8 million from $3.5 million.
