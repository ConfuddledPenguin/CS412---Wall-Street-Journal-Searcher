Marketing & Media: General Media to Buy 4 Computer Magazines From Capital Cities

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/20/90

NEW YORK -- Bob Guccione's General Media International Inc. said it will acquire four computer magazines from ABC Publishing Cos., a subsidiary of Capital Cities/ABC Inc. The magazines -- Compute], Compute's Gazette, Compute's PC, and Compute's Amiga Resource -- will become part of the Omni magazine division of closely held General Media. The purchase price wasn't disclosed.

Mr. Guccione, best known as the founder and publisher of Penthouse magazine, has expanded his magazine company in recent years, by acquiring such titles as Four Wheeler, for auto enthusiasts, and launching Longevity, a health magazine, last May.

Kathy Keeton, president of Omni magazine, said Omni frequently features stories on computers. She said with the computer magazines, Omni can "realize some synergy" in advertising sales and editorial matter.

"Compute] caters to the home market, and the home-office market is booming with more than 10 million people who work at home," said Ms. Keeton. "The magazines have a good future."

Compute], a monthly with circulation of 211,794, is in need of "lots and lots of editorial smarting up." The four magazines are currently published in Greensboro, N.C., but Ms. Keeton said she didn't know whether they would stay there.
