Marketing & Media: Journal Writer Wins Prize For Presidential Reporting

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/20/90

NEW YORK -- Gerald F. Seib, The Wall Street Journal's White House reporter, was named winner of the annual Gerald R. Ford Prize for Distinguished Reporting on the Presidency. The $5,000 award, sponsored by the Gerald R. Ford Foundation, recognizes journalists whose high standards for accuracy and substance help foster a better public understanding of the presidency.

Mr. Seib, 34 years old, who joined The Wall Street Journal's Dallas bureau in 1978, covered the Pentagon and the State Department before transferring to Cairo in 1985 to report on the Middle East. He returned to Washington in 1987.

Articles for which he was nominated included an examination of issues facing the first year of the Bush presidency, President Bush's style of personal diplomacy, and two articles on the erosion of presidential power.
