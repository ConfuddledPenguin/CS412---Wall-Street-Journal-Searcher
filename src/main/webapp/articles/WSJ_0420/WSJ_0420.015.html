Beanbags Are Recalled By Safety Commission Due to Infants' Deaths

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/20/90

WASHINGTON -- The Consumer Product Safety Commission announced the recall of more than 600,000 beanbag cushions linked to the suffocation deaths of 19 babies. J.C. Penney Co. of Dallas, as well as several of the products' manufacturers, voluntarily recalled the pillows, which are used to support resting or sleeping infants. The major manufacturers involved in the recall are Hollander Home Fashions, Newark, N.J., which made 467,000 cushions; and Gold Inc. of Denver, which made 110,000 pillows. Seven small companies also are participating in the recall.

CPSC officials said the agency may take legal action against one manufacturer who hasn't agreed to a recall, but officials didn't identify the company. J.C. Penney and the Army Air Force Exchange Service, which supplies military bases, sold the Gold Inc. product.

Commission officials said the 19 infants, three months of age or younger, died after being left to sleep face down on the cushions. Ten of the deaths occurred last year. The fabric-covered pillows, which are filled with polystyrene pellets, can conform to a baby's face. Suffocation occurs because newborn infants can't roll over. Early last month, the CPSC issued an urgent warning about the suffocation risk associated with the product. By mid-March, all manufacturers of the cushions had stopped production, the CPSC said.

Hollander's product was sold under the brand name Mothers Helper, and the Gold Inc. cushion was known as the Gold Bug Support Sack. J.C. Penney sold 4,700 of the Gold Inc. cushions under the names Disney Babies Toddletime Support Sack, Sleepy Time Bears Support Sack and Sesame Street Support Sack.
