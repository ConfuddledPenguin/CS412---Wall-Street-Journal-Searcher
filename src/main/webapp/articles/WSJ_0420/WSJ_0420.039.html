Initial State Jobless Claims Increased in April 7 Week

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/20/90

WASHINGTON -- Initial claims for regular state unemployment benefits rose to a seasonally adjusted 371,000 in the week ended April 7 from 338,000 in the previous week, the Labor Department said. The number of people receiving regular state benefits in the week ended March 31 rose to a seasonally adjusted 2,375,000, or 2.3% of those covered by unemployment insurance, from 2,313,000 in the previous week, when the insured unemployment rate was 2.2%.

Counting all state and federal benefit programs, the number of people receiving unemployment benefits in the week ended March 31 fell to 2,729,000 from 2,754,300 a week earlier. These figures aren't seasonally adjusted.
