Amax Inc. Reports Profit Declined 56% In the First Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/20/90

NEW YORK -- Amax Inc., hurt by a softening economy and sharply lower prices for aluminum ingot and some aluminum products, posted a 56% drop in first-quarter net income. The company, which has interests in domestic energy and gold production as well as aluminum and manufactured aluminum products, said net income in the first quarter fell to $50 million, or 57 cents a share, from $114.2 million, or $1.34 a share, a year earlier.

Sales fell 5.3%, to $927.9 million, from $979.6 million.

Allen Born, chairman, chief executive and president, said a softening of the economy that began in the 1989 third quarter reduced demand and prices for aluminum ingot and some aluminum products from the prices a year earlier. Mr. Born said first-quarter prices for primary aluminum were about 40% lower than a year ago.

Mr. Born said, however, there had been some recent strengthening of demand, reflected in prices for some aluminum products and aluminum ingot. The average price per pound of aluminum ingot is currently about 72 cents, slightly better than the 69 cents in the first three months of this year, although still lower than the year-earlier first quarter, when the price was about $1.

Andrew Silver, an analyst at Dillon, Read & Co., said Amax's results were stronger than he expected. "The company is on track for a respectable performance in a somewhat difficult environment," he said. The analyst is predicting a modest improvement for earnings in the second quarter based on somewhat higher aluminum prices, and estimates earnings of $2.50 a share for the year. He said the diversity of Amax's operations helped results during a soft cycle for aluminum, with about 40% of the company's earnings coming from non-aluminum activity.

Mr. Born said lower prices for primary ingot, as well as higher raw-material costs, were partly offset by higher shipments of semi-fabricated goods and stronger performance in the company's architectural and building aluminum products.

The official also noted improvements in the energy and gold businesses, with Amax Coal contributing $29 million to operating earnings, up from $20 million a year earlier. Oil and gas earnings rose 20% to $6 million.

"As the year progresses," Mr. Born said, "we expect the economy to strengthen and Amax's businesses and earnings to improve above present levels."

Amax has been a rumored takeover target in recent weeks. Mr. Silver, the analyst, said that Amax, as the third-largest aluminum producer and the third-largest coal producer in the U.S., has "very attractive" assets, which he estimates could be valued at $35 to $40 a share.

In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange yesterday, Amax shares closed at $25, down 87.5 cents.
