Business Brief -- Textron Inc.: First-Period Net Rose 4.5% On 2.7% Gain in Revenue

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/20/90

Textron Inc., Providence, R.I., said first-quarter net income rose 4.5% to $66.2 million, or 73 cents a share, on a 2.7% rise in revenue to $1.88 billion. Per-share earnings for the period were flat compared with the year-earlier quarter, because the number of shares outstanding rose 4% to 90.6 million from 87 million.

In the 1989 first quarter, Textron had net income of $63.3 million, or 73 cents a share, on revenue of $1.83 billion.

B.F. Dolan, chairman and chief executive officer of the aerospace, commercial products and financial services concern, said, "Textron's balanced diversity has enabled us to achieve a good start on the year, despite softness in several of our markets."

Mr. Dolan said continued strength in the company's finance and insurance businesses "more than offset" the current slowdown in its automotive divisions.

Total revenue in Textron's Aerospace Technology sector, its largest business segment, rose about 2% to $825.9 million; while revenue in the financial services sector rose 13% to $586.9 million. Revenue in the commercial products sector, however, dropped 5% to $470.6 million.
