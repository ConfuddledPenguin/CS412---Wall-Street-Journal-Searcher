Business Brief -- Schlumberger Ltd.: Net Income in First Quarter Rose 56% to $128.6 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/20/90

Schlumberger Ltd., New York, citing improvements in all its oil-field-service businesses, reported a 56% jump in first-quarter earnings. "We were estimating 50 cents {a share}, they earned 54 cents, but most of the difference was a lower tax rate," said James D. Crandell, analyst at Salomon Brothers Inc.

But he and the company sounded an optimistic note for the industry. The results "strengthen our conviction that we are at the start of an up cycle in the oil industry," said Schlumberger Chairman Euan Baird.

Net income totaled $128.6 million, up from $82.3 million, or 35 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue rose 3.4% to $1.22 billion.

Mr. Crandell noted stronger-than-expected revenue in three areas of Schlumberger: the Dowell Schlumberger 50-50 pumping-services joint venture with Dow Chemical Co.; the Anadrill well-drilling business; and the GECO operation, which provides computerized three-dimensional geological profiles.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, shares of Schlumberger rose 25 cents to close at $50.75.
