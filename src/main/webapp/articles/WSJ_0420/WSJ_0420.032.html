Colgate-Palmolive Net Rose 21% in 1st Period On an 8% Gain in Sales

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/20/90

NEW YORK -- Colgate-Palmolive Co.'s first-quarter profit surged 21% on an 8% increase in sales as each of its major businesses and geographical markets posted gains. The consumer-product company posted net income of $81.2 million, or $1.16 a share, up from $67.2 million, or 98 cents a share, a year earlier. Sales rose to $1.3 billion from $1.2 billion.

The results reflect the company's "growing global strength," said Reuben Mark, chairman, president and chief executive officer. In the Western Hemisphere, the company said, Mexico, Brazil and Canada turned in strong profit gains. And profit has begun to improve at Colgate-Venezuela, where results last year were affected by poor economic conditions.

Profits from U.S. operations were ahead of last year by "a significant margin," Colgate said. Sales and volume decreased, but that reflects Colgate's conscious effort to eliminate less-profitable products. At the same time, Colgate U.S. is increasing the revenue and profit per case of product sold, as a result of improved marketing and expense control.

Improved results in Europe reflected growth in the southern part of the continent, new productions and the favorable impact of currencies strengthening against the dollar, the company said. Thailand, Malaysia and the Philippines were the biggest contributors for Colgate-Far East/ Africa, the company said.

Colgate shares rose 25 cents each to close at $56.625 in New York Stock Exchange composite trading.
