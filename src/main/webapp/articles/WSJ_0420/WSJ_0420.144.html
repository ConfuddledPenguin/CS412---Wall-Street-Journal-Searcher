Telefonica of Spain May Lift Holdings In Foreign Concerns

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/20/90

MADRID -- Telefonica de Espana S.A. is considering increasing its stake in Chile's telecommunications system and taking shareholdings in telecommunications operations in Argentina, Mexico, Puerto Rico and New Zealand, a senior official of the Spanish state-controlled telephone company said. Enrique Used, director of Telefonica's international operations, said these plans are part of a strategy to counter expected competition in its home market as European telecommunications are deregulated.

In Chile, Telefonica has stakes in two companies, Cia. de Telefonos de Chile S.A., or CTC, and Empresa Nacional de Telecomunicaciones de Chile, or Entel.

Subject to clarification of legal issues, including points relating to Chile's competition laws, Mr. Used said, Telefonica is discussing plans that could lead to its increasing its 20% stake in Entel to 40%.

He also said Telefonica is considering bidding for part of Argentina's state-owned telecommunications company under its forthcoming privatization, and negotiating with potential partners for participation in a similar operation in New Zealand.

The Argentinian government has said it will split its telecommunications concern into two parts, covering the north and south of the country and parts of Buenos Aires, and sell each to private bidders. It has invited companies interested in submitting bids to present applications this month.

Mexico and Puerto Rico have indicated they will privatize their telecommunications services, though they haven't announced details. New Zealand, meanwhile, is considering privatizing its telecommunications services, Mr. Used said, though he declined to elaborate.

Earlier this month, Telefonica completed its $388 million purchase of a controlling stake in CTC from Bond Corp. International Ltd. It also recently bought an additional 10% of Entel, of which it had already had 10%, for $23 million from a vendor representing the Chilean armed forces.

CTC, which controls most of Chile's internal telecommunications, had profit last year of $95 million on revenue of $268 million, Mr. Used said. Telefonica will own 43.7% of CTC's shares, once a pending capital restructuring operation is concluded.

Entel, which operates Chile's intercity and international links, limits the maximum holding in its shares by any holder to 20%. But Mr. Used said Telefonica has approached other Entel holders to discuss the possibility of raising this limit to 40%. If this is approved, he said, Telefonica may seek to acquire the 10% Entel holding owned by Banco Santander of Spain, as well as shares from other potential vendors.
