West German Metal Workers

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/20/90

BONN -- The big IG Metall metal workers union rejected an offer by the employers federation to discuss a further shortening of the workweek to 35 hours once the competitive situation in the European Community will allow it. The union, which is calling for a cut in the workweek and a 9% pay increase, said the employers' offer, which also included some concessions regarding weekend work, represented "cosmetic changes to a hopelessly obsolete model."

The union's rejection of the employers' new opening underlined the lack of progress in the difficult 1990 wage negotiations. IG Metall, which represents more than 2.6 million workers in important sectors of the West German industry, has said it will stage warning strikes at certain companies in early May.

The employers federation said it wanted to set "objective criteria" that would determine extent and timing of a cut in working hours after 1993. Union officials said such criteria would mean indefinite postponement of the shorter workweek.
