Marketing & Media: Knight-Ridder Says Profit Climbed 26% In the First Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/20/90

MIAMI -- Knight-Ridder Inc. reported that first-quarter net income climbed 26% to $25.8 million from $20.5 million a year ago, due in part to lower newsprint and interest costs. The giant media concern said per-share profit rose 28% in the latest quarter to 50 cents from 39 cents, based on 51.8 million shares outstanding in the latest quarter compared with 52.6 million a year earlier.

Knight-Ridder said revenue rose 2.6% to $554.7 million from $540.7 million a year earlier.

The company said reduced losses in its Detroit newspaper operations and good performance in its Business Information Services Division, particularly its MoneyCenter and Dialog products, contributed to the improved results.

It said interest costs in the quarter fell to $14.1 million from $23 million a year earlier, as a result of the pay-down of debt with funds from the 1989 sale of eight television stations and its Pasadena newspaper.

In a statement, Robert F. Singleton, senior vice president and chief financial officer, said, "Looking ahead, Knight-Ridder is well positioned to continue to absorb a soft advertising environment and should produce among the best earnings-per-share gains in our industry this year." He added that "barring some unforeseen financial setback, 1990 will be Knight-Ridder's 15th consecutive year of growth in earnings per share from continuing operations."
