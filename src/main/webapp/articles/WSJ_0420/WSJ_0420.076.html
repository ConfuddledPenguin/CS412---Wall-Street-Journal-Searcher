EC Commission Is Critical Of U.S. Trade Practices

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 04/20/90

BRUSSELS -- The European Community Commission, determined to refute U.S. charges that it is creating a "Fortress Europe," accused the U.S. of maintaining numerous barriers to European exports. In its annual review of "U.S. trade barriers and unfair trade practices," the EC listed more than 50 impediments to trade. Among the sectors singled out by Brussels were public procurement and banking.

The EC report has been expanded considerably from last year and includes a wide range of trade practices running from high U.S. tariffs on footwear to U.S. agricultural export subsidies. More than a dozen items have been added since the 1989 report, although commission officials said only some of these were actually new practices introduced in the past year.

They said the longer list reflected the more thorough job done by EC experts in identifying U.S. trade barriers.

"We detect a tendency in the U.S. to think that in the trade game, other countries play unfair while only the U.S. plays fair . . . this simply isn't true," a commission trade official said.

He said the EC report was a response to the U.S. trade representative's annual report on barriers to foreign trade, which repeatedly has included EC trade practices. U.S. officials had no immediate response to the EC document.
