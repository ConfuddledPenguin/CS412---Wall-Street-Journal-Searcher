Letters to the Editor: Before Investing, Seek A Little `Due Diligence'

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/20/90

In your March 22 article "Conflict Issues Cloud Financial Planning," the role of "due diligence" is omitted. The most crucial question a potential investor should be asking any financial planner is, "Who performed the due diligence on the product that is being recommended, and what were that person's qualifications to do so?" Before committing to any investment, one should "diligently" inquire as to the background, credentials, track record, integrity, etc. of the syndicator, and not entirely rely on one's investment adviser's recommendation until the answers to these kinds of questions are answered satisfactorily.

Unfortunately for the client, fee-only planners bask in the luxury of being able to shirk this responsibility altogether.

James E. Newdick

President

Capital & Business Planning Inc.

Westlake, Ohio
