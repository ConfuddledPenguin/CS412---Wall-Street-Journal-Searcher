Marketing Brief -- Pulitzer Publishing Co.: St. Louis Concern Says Net In First Quarter Fell 70%

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/20/90

Pulitzer Publishing Co., citing lower profit at its flagship St. Louis Post-Dispatch newspaper and the effect of certain accounting actions, said first-quarter net fell 70%. Net income fell to $906,000, or nine cents a share, from $3 million, or 29 cents a share, in the year-ago quarter. Revenue rose 1.1% to $95.2 million from $94.1 million.

The St. Louis-based company said continued softness in advertising, especially at the Post-Dispatch, left publishing revenue 1.9% below the year-earlier quarter, although broadcasting receipts climbed 10%. "We anticipate a difficult year at the Post-Dispatch in 1990," said Joseph Pulitzer Jr., company chairman. First-quarter earnings were also hurt by higher depreciation and amortization costs connected with the acquisition in December of WDSU-TV in New Orleans.
