Profits Increased At Bell Atlantic, U S West, Nynex --- First-Period Earnings Fell At Pacific Telesis, SNET Due to Higher Expenses

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 04/20/90

Bell Atlantic Corp., U S West Inc. and Nynex Corp., aided by strong non-telephone operations, such as cellular phone service, reported strong earnings gains. Pacific Telesis Group and Southern New England Telecommunications Corp. had declines, primarily because of higher expenses and reductions in telephone revenue.

U S West Inc.

U S West managed to post an 8.9% increase in net income, even though revenue from its largest contributor, the telephone sector, was essentially flat. Jack A. MacAllister, U S West's chairman and chief executive officer, attributed the gain to solid growth in the non-telephone sector, which includes cellular operations, directory publishing and financial services.

The Denver company said revenue in the non-telephone group climbed almost 11% to $405.1 million. Telephone revenue basically was flat at $2.02 billion. Overall, U S West's revenue inched up 1.4% to $2.43 billion from $2.39 billion a year ago.

Telephone operations were hurt by lower interstate access charges and rate reductions. However, the sector had strong growth in new telephone lines, which increased 3% to 12.3 million. Minutes of local long-distance usage also rose to 9.3 million, a 5.4% jump from 8.8 million in the year-earlier quarter.

U S West said revenue for its cellular company, U S West NewVector Group Inc., surged 54% to $58.5 million from $37.9 million. NewVector reported a 66% increase in new cellular customers. The cellular unit had a loss of about $4 million, compared with a loss of $5.3 million a year ago.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, U S West fell 25 cents to $72.50.

Nynex

Nynex said earnings were hampered by a pretax charge of $39 million for the New York company's previously announced early retirement program. To date, about 3,100 management employees plan to retire.

Nynex's modest 2.8% profit increase largely reflected contributions from the company's non-telephone operations. Overall operating profit from the non-telephone business segments rose about $22 million from a year ago to about $40 million. Cellular telephone operations had a 62% rise in new cellular customers for a total of 229,000.

The company also was aided by stringent spending. Operating expenses rose less than 1% to $2.71 billion, compared with $2.68 billion a year ago. Despite the soft Northeast economy, new telephone lines grew about 208,000 to 15.2 million, a 1.3% rise.

Revenue rose 2% to $3.3 billion from $3.2 billion.

In Big Board composite trading yesterday, Nynex closed down $1.50 at $81.75.

Bell Atlantic

At Bell Atlantic, Philadelphia, net rose 7.4%, reflecting healthy results across the board.

Revenue rose 9% to $3.02 billion from $2.77 billion. The revenue growth represents strong volumes in the network services business, the acquisition of Control Data Corp.'s third-party maintenance business, and good growth in the non-telephone business.

Telephone revenue for local service increased 3.2% to $1.11 billion from $1.08 billion, while non-telephone operations, excluding financial and real estate division, rose 47% to $208.4 million.

Expenses increased 8.2% to $2.36 billion, generally reflecting the Control Data acquisition and expanded business activity.

Results also include a portion of a $42 million award to settle charges that its Bell of Pennsylvania unit used deceptive techniques to sell customers extra services, such as call waiting and speed dialing.

Bell Atlantic said cellular customer growth was stymied by above normal disconnections associated with a legal dispute involving a former sales agent. The company added 18,200 customers for a total of 207,200.

Bell Atlantic fell $1.125 to $95.875 in Big Board trading.

Pacific Telesis

Higher expenses and a rate reduction resulted in a 17% drop in net income for Pacific Telesis. Expenses rose 6.5%, primarily because of contract-related labor costs, depreciation and increased expenditures for telephone network and internal-operating systems improvements.

The San Francisco company also said results were depressed by a $391 million rate reduction associated with California's new regulatory program that became effective in January.

Overall, all operations performed well. Toll messages grew 11% to 829 million and minutes of access use increased 8.8% to 9.9 billion. The company's Pacific Bell and Nevada Bell units added more than 144,000 customers, an increase of 4.2% for a total of 13.8 million.

Cellular operations posted a 74% profit gain to $16.9 million. Cellular revenue rose 36% to $134.3 million, and new customers increased 44% to about 413,000.

Revenue rose 1.3% to $2.37 billion from $2.34 billion.

In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange, Pacific Telesis fell 25 cents to $44.50.

SNET

Southern New England Telecommunications said net dropped 6%, mainly the result of a slowing Connecticut economy and lower results from the company's telephone operations and SNET Systems unit.

In January, the New Haven, Conn., company reduced telephone rates about 5% to reflect the elimination of a gross earnings tax on telecommunications services.

Moreover, the company said higher expenses associated with higher wages and benefits and litigation against the company also hurt profit. Costs increased to $318.6 million from $313.7 million.

Revenue fell 1% to $401.5 million from $407.1 million.

In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange, SNET gained 50 cents to $38.50.
