Business Brief -- Philadelphia Electric Co.: Utility Gets Rate Increase Much Lower Than Sought

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/20/90

The Pennsylvania Public Utilities Commission granted Philadelphia Electric Co. a $242 million rate increase, which is less than half of the $549 million amount the utility requested to recover its costs of building and operating a second nuclear unit at its Limerick power station in Montgomery County, Pa. "Disallowing $300 million of the company's investment in Limerick Unit 2 will force significant reductions in the company's earnings, spending plans and common stock dividend," said Joseph F. Paquette Jr., chairman and chief executive officer. The company said its annual earnings will reduced by about $1 a share; in 1989, it earned $590 million, or $2.36 a share. It also will incur this year a one-time charge of about $255 million, or about $1.20 a share, to write off the part of its Limerick No. 2 investment that it won't allowed to recover through its rate base.

Philadelphia Electric's board will meet Monday to review the dividend, now $2.20 annually, and consider a package of possible spending cuts.
