Environmental Protection Agency analyst E.G. Vallianatos, writing in the Oct. 10 issue of the Chicago Tribune:

{Will} corporations like Exxon . . . and the other capitalists/industrialists of the world . . . begin dismantling the machines that are heating the Earth? These men and women control the world. They devour the rain forest; they manufacture the cars that pump millions of tons of carbon dioxide into the atmosphere; they own the coal, gas, oil and factories of the world.

To get away from all these wealth-creating "resources" would be inconceivable, for what would the capitalists/industrialists do? They have built a megamachine on the very specifications of a greenhouse Earth. They educate millions of young students in the wisdom of this earth-heating, destructive, suicidal system.

These same earth-heating technocrats have also been telling the Third World that "development" means to do what they did: Industrialize everything: force the peasants off the land and create mechanical cash monocultures; sell trees, bananas, coffee, vanilla, cacao, oil palm, beef and oil to the "developed" North and with the money import TVs, luxury goods like big cars, and machine guns to kill the peasant guerrillas who cause trouble.
