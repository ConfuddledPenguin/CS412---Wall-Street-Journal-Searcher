Marketing & Media: Film Script Fetches Record $1.75 Million at Auction

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/20/90

LOS ANGELES (AP) -- A film script about a private eye who joins a former football star to solve a murder sold for a record $1.75 million. "The Last Boy Scout" could have fetched $2 million, but 28-year-old screenwriter Shane Black said he preferred to sign with the David Geffen Co., an independent producer he worked with previously.

Recently, it's become almost routine to ask $1 million for prized screenplays. But industry executives said Tuesday's sale price far exceeded previous highs.

A bidding war has broken out during the past year for "spec scripts," screenplays written without having been commissioned by a producer or studio.

The boom, fed in part by an expansion push by Twentieth Century Fox, Walt Disney Co. and Columbia Pictures, ended the $300,000 benchmark price for top scripts a year ago.

Some studio executives say script auctioning has become too costly. "To get $1 million, it used to have to be a damned good script. Now, you practically pay that for the idea alone and wind up with a lot of rewrites," said David Hoberman, head of Disney's Touchstone unit.

Others like the change. "I don't know of anything that generates the excitement of the chase after a big script," said John Goldwyn, executive vice president of world-wide production at MGM/UA Communications Co.

Screenwriters increasingly are auctioning their work as studios turn away from the development process, in which writers are paid to write and rewrite ideas discussed in advance.
