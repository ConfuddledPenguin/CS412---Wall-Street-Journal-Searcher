Chrysler Corp. Says It Expects to Report `Modest' Net Income

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/20/90

WASHINGTON -- Chrysler Corp. said it expects to report "overall a modest profit" for the first quarter of 1990. The projection came in an amended registration statement filed with the Securities and Exchange Commission in connection with a proposed offering of $800 million principal amount of trust certificates due 2020.

Most industry analysts have been forecasting Chrysler would break even for the quarter ended March 31. Earlier this year, company officials hinted that the No. 3 U.S. auto maker might do somewhat better than that.

Chrysler closed yesterday at $16.375, down 37.5 cents, in composite New York Stock Exchange trading.

In the filing, Chrysler said it continued to be affected in the first quarter "by the adverse operating environment that existed during the second half of 1989, as the costs of sales incentive programs continued to affect operating results adversely. . . ."

Offsetting this were the earnings from its Chrysler Financial Corp. subsidiary "and, to some extent, {savings from} Chrysler's previously announced cost-reduction efforts." The company has pledged to cut its overhead by $1.5 billion by year end.

In the first quarter of 1989, Chrysler reported earnings from continuing operations of $338 million, or $1.44 a share, on revenue of $9.95 billion. Chrysler didn't provide an estimate in the SEC filing for revenue in the just-ended first quarter.

Chrysler did note that its world-wide factory car and truck sales during the first quarter were 493,612 units compared with 530,799 units in the fourth quarter of 1989 and 671,560 units in the first quarter of 1989.

Chrysler estimated that its total share of the U.S. and Canadian car and truck markets in the first quarter fell to 13.1% from 14.1% in the fourth quarter of 1989 and 14% for the first quarter of 1989.
