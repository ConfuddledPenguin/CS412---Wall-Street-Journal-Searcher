Business Brief -- Robert Bosch G.m.b.H.: Donation Is Set to Create New Management Institute

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/20/90

Robert Bosch G.m.b.H. will donate $15 million to Carnegie Mellon University, Pittsburgh, to create a new management institute for the study of international business issues. The Carnegie Bosch Institute for Applied Studies in International Management will be affiliated with Carnegie Mellon's business school. CMU President Richard M. Cyert will become president of the institute following his retirement June 30 from his current position.

Though the institute's research agenda and budget have yet to be determined, former Bosch Chairman Hans L. Merkle cited several areas that he believes merit investigation. They include the management of technology and the proper autonomy of operating divisions in large global corporations.

"There's a deficit of world-wide thinking among businesses. That's why we're engaging in this," he said. Bosch, the world's largest independent supplier of auto parts, is based in Stuttgart, West Germany.
