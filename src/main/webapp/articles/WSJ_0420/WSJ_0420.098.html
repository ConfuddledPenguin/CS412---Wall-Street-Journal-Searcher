Unisys Says Its Loss Narrowed in Quarter To Only $3.1 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/20/90

BLUE BELL, Pa. -- Unisys Corp. said its first-quarter loss narrowed sharply to $3.1 million from $78.7 million a year earlier. After allowing for preferred dividends, the loss in the latest period amounted to 19 cents a share, roughly in line with expectations. The 1989 first-quarter loss was equal to 66 cents a share.

Revenue rose 5% to $2.31 billion from $2.20 billion, slightly above expectations, but analysts remained noncommittal about the company's prospects.

Unisys attributed much of the improvement to healthier gross margins resulting from a scaling down last year that trimmed the computer maker's work force by about 8,000 jobs. Full benefits from those steps, which led to a $230-million charge in the 1989 third quarter, will kick in during the second half of this year, Unisys said. The company previously estimated the cutbacks will save it about $500 million annually.

"Overall, we believe that we are on track to reach our 1990 goal of reducing debt, restoring sound profitability and improving cash flow," James Unruh, president and chief executive, said in a statement. A company spokesman said Unisys, which has been criticized by analysts as too highly leveraged, reduced its debt by between $50 million and $100 million during the period.

Unisys said its international results were strong, but that domestic business, long a disappointing performer, "remains weak." The company has been buffeted by a price war and strong competition in the U.S. mainframe computer market, and analysts said its troubles in that area are likely to continue.

Unisys said its strongest gains were in networking products, software, services, and computers using the Unix operating system.

In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange yesterday, Unisys closed at $14.625, unchanged.
