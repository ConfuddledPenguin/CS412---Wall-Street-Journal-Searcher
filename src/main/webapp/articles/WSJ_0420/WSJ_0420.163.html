Marty Shugrue Has Background Needed To Save Eastern Air --- Former Pan Am Executive Had Tried to Turnaround Continental Airlines

  • Author: Carolyn Phillips
  • Date: 04/20/90

Maybe Marty Shugrue's business card should read "Troubled Airlines My Specialty." Mr. Shugrue started yesterday as bankruptcy court-appointed trustee of Eastern Airlines with stints at financially strapped Pan American Airways and Continental Airlines already on his resume.

His latest job, de facto chief executive officer of Eastern, undoubtedly tops the list of the toughest in the airline industry at the moment. Mr. Shugrue must rebuild the anemic Texas Air Corp. unit that has been decimated by a lengthy machinists strike and resulting Chapter 11 filing and that has lost the confidence of travelers, especially business passengers essential to profitability. U.S. Bankruptcy Judge Burton R. Lifland gave Mr. Shugrue the job after finding the management at Eastern and its parent "unfit" to operate the carrier.

"I have received strong pledges of cooperation from Eastern Airlines' management, the creditors' committee and Texas Air," Mr. Shugrue said in a statement. The veteran airline executive didn't return numerous phone calls.

A former associate of Mr. Shugrue believes the court picked the right man for the job. "Marty fits the bill real well," said Donald Garvett, a former Pan Am vice president. "He understands and respects people," but he also is "a man of action who gets things done and makes hard decisions when he has to."

Mr. Garvett credits Mr. Shugrue with shoring up travel agent support for Pan Am, cutting costs, retaining the respect of labor and keeping good managers during tough times. "Part of the reason Pan Am is in business today stems from things Marty could accomplish under difficult circumstances."

Mr. Shugrue, 49 years old, has yet to effect a clear-cut airline turnaround, however. After some 20 years at Pan Am, in jobs ranging from pilot to vice chairman, he was fired following a showdown with other board members over Pan Am's future. He left that carrier in January 1988 when it was still mired in financial difficulties.

He landed at Continental Airlines, another Texas Air unit, as that carrier's fifth president in as many years. Industry executives and analysts had heralded the much needed operating experience in marketing, personnel and adminstration he brought to Continental.

But he, too, was in for a short tenure, apparently not correcting Continental's lackluster performance quickly enough to suit Texas Air Chairman Frank Lorenzo, who brought in another executive over Mr. Shugrue, prompting him to resign in February 1989 after a year in the post.

Mr. Shugrue's appointment Wednesday isn't his first brush with Eastern. A year ago, he was affiliated with the investment group led by former baseball commissioner Peter V. Ueberroth that negotiated to buy Eastern. The deal ultimately collapsed, and Mr. Shugrue returned to his Houston-based consulting business.
