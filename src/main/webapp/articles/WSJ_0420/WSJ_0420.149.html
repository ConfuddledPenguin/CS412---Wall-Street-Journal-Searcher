Business Brief -- Reebok International Ltd.: Footwear Maker Reports 7% Increase in First-Period Net

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/20/90

Citing a 45% jump in international sales, Reebok International Ltd. reported first-quarter net income of $52.7 million, or 46 cents a share, up 7%, from $49.9 million, or 43 cents last year. Total sales were up 13%, to $554.4 million, from $488.5 million last year.

The Stoughton, Mass., footwear concern said U.S. sales of Reebok footwear grew about 2% during the current quarter.

The company said sales increases at its Rockport and Ellesse divisions and the addition of sales of its Boston Whaler division, acquired last October, were "largely" offset by a decrease in sales at its Avia unit.

A company spokesman said sales for Avia were $48.2 million in the latest quarter, down 26%, from $64.9 million in the year-ago period.

He said the Avia segment, under new management since last year, "needed focusing" and has had to be "positioned to a point from which it can grow." He declined to predict the group's annual sales, which totaled $196.2 million last year.

He said the unit is now concentrating on "pure atheletic" shoes.
