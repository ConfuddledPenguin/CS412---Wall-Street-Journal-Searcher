Business Brief -- GlenFed Inc.: Earnings Increased by 30% In Quarter; Cost Cuts Cited

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/20/90

GlenFed Inc. said improved core earnings and a sharp cut in operating costs pushed fiscal third-quarter profit up 30% to $26.3 million, or 85 cents a share, from $20.2 million, or 68 cents a share, a year earlier. Separately, the nation's fifth-largest thrift, based in Glendale, Calif., said it passed an important regulatory test during the quarter when federal examiners, in a survey of its asset-classification system, found that the company isn't required to increase its loan-loss reserves. The company also said it increased its tangible and risk-based capital to a level that continues to exceed more-stringent capital requirements imposed by the recent congressional thrift reform act.

During the quarter, GlenFed said its net interest margin -- the spread between the yield on loans and cost of funds -- improved to 2.24% from 1.72% a year earlier.

Yesterday, GlenFed stock rose 87.5 cents to close at $12.75 in New York Stock Exchange composite trading.
