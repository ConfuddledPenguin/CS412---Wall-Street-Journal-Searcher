Business Brief -- Occidental Petroleum Corp.: Natural Gas Pipeline Unit Plans Project in Oklahoma

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/20/90

Natural Gas Pipeline Co. of America said it plans to build a $51 million pipeline to move gas produced in Oklahoma's Arkoma Basin to another recently completed Natural Gas pipeline in the southeastern part of the state. Natural Gas is the interstate transmission unit of MidCon Corp., a subsidiary of Los Angeles-based Occidental Petroleum Corp.

The project involves construction of 107 miles of 24-inch pipeline running from Latimer County, Okla., westward into Pittsburg County and then south through Atoka and Bryan counties, where it will link with the recently completed line.

Compression for the proposed pipeline will be supplied by a new 4,000-horsepower compressor facility to be built in northern Atoka County. The pipeline will transport as much as 300 million cubic feet of gas a day, Natural Gas said. Construction could get under way as soon as this summer.
