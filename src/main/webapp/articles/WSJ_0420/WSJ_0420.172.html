Business Brief -- Anglo American of South Africa: Profit From Six Gold Mines Fell 15% in Latest Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/20/90

Anglo American Corp. of South Africa Ltd., Johannesburg, said first-quarter combined net income of its six gold mines fell 15% to 443.9 million rand ($167.8 million) from 523.4 million rand in the previous quarter. South African gold mines supply quarterly, rather than annual, results, because swings in revenue, tax and spending tend to obscure the longer comparison. Overall interim and year-end earnings are reported with year-to-year comparisons.

Anglo American's overall gold production in the first quarter exceeded 1.9 million troy ounces, a 3.5% decline from the quarter ended Dec. 31. Sporadic labor unrest and seismicity caused a drop in gold output by the group's second-largest producer, Vaal Reefs Exploration & Mining Co. The third-largest producer, Western Deep Levels Ltd., also lost output as a result of a fire in the previous quarter. But Free State Consolidated Gold Mines Ltd., the world's largest gold mine, improved its performance, despite some loss of production owing to industrial action and damage caused by a late-March tornado.
