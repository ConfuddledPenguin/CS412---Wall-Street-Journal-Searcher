International: Gonzalez Fraga to Regain Top Argentine Bank Post

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 04/20/90

BUENOS AIRES -- Javier Gonzalez Fraga will take over as Argentina's Central Bank president a second time during the nine-month government of President Carlos Menem. Mr. Gonzalez Fraga was Mr. Menem's first Central Bank president after the Peronist Party government was installed last July. He resigned on Nov. 27, reportedly because the Economy Ministry rejected his plans to free the exchange rate and manage foreign currency reserves differently.

In the event, Economy Minister Nestor Rapanelli was replaced last December. His successor, Antonio Gonzalez, freed the exchange rate along with controls on wages and prices.

The government recently has built up currency reserves by buying U.S. dollars in the sort of "dirty float" Mr. Gonzalez Fraga suggested last year.
