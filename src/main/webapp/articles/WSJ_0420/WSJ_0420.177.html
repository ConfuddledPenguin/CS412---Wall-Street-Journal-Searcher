Business Brief -- Newport News Savings Bank: Letter of Intent Is Signed For United Financial Merger

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/20/90

Newport News Savings Bank, Newport News, Va., said it signed a letter of intent to merge with United Financial Banking Cos., a Vienna, Va.-based bank holding company, through an exchange of shares. In the proposed transaction, the two financial institutions would exchange stock on a book value-for-book value basis, as of the last day of the month preceding the effective date of consolidation. They hope to complete the transaction by Dec. 31, subject to a definitive agreement, as well as shareholder and regulatory approvals.

The proposal also calls for two directors of Newport News Savings Bank, Robert L. Freeman Jr. and Gary A. Suttle, to become directors of United Financial. United Financial, with its primary subsidiary, Business Bank, had total assets of $78 million and equity of $13.2 million as of Dec. 31, 1989. Newport News Savings Bank would become a second wholly owned subsidiary.
