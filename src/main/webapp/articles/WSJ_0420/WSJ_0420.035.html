New Securities Issues

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/20/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: MORTGAGES

Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $300 million of five-year debentures with a coupon rate of 9.20%, priced at 99.9375 to yield 9.216%. The issue is callable at par in three years. The debentures were priced at about 0.40 percentage point above the Treasury five-year note. The issue will be sold through Freddie Mac's 18-member debenture selling group of banks and securities dealers. The issue has a maturity date of April 27, 1995. The minimum purchase amount is $5,000, with additional increments of $5,000. Interest will be paid semiannually.

EUROBONDS

Compagnie Generale d'Electricite (France) -- 5.58 billion French francs ($989 million) of 6.5% convertible bonds due Jan. 1, 2000, including a 3.58 billion franc tranche for shareholders and a two billion franc international tranche, via Societe Generale. Each bond is priced at 680 francs and is exchangeable for one share from July 1, 1991. Premium of 7.9% over Wednesday's closing price of 630 francs. Callable from July 1, 1992, to Dec. 31, 1999, at 130.

Inter-American Development Bank (agency) -- 30 billion yen ($190.8 million) of 7 1/4% bonds due May 15, 2000, priced at 101 1/8 to yield 7.34%, via Nomura International Ltd.

Swedish Export Credit Corp. (Sweden) -- 150 million Finnish markka ($37.8 million) of 13 3/4% bonds due May 17, 1993, priced at 101 3/8 to yield 13 1/4% less full fees, via Union Bank of Finland Ltd. Fees 1 3/8.

Credit Local de France (France) -- 75 million European currency units of 10 1/4% bonds due Feb. 8, 1995, priced at 100.075 and fungible with 225 million ECU issue outstanding, via Bankers Trust International Ltd.
