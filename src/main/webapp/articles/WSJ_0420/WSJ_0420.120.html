Law: Seagate Is Sued by Conner Over Film Head Shipments

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/20/90

SCOTTS VALLEY, Calif. -- In buying Imprimis Technology Inc. last October, Seagate Technology Inc. found that it also bought a lawsuit. The computer disk-drive maker said it was sued in state court in Santa Clara County by rival Conner Peripherals Inc. The suit charges that Seagate cut off Conner's supply of thin film heads from Imprimis despite a guarantee that it wouldn't do so. Conners uses the film heads to manufacture hard-disk drives.

Seagate declined further comment on the suit. Conners said it took the action because Seagate's action amounted to a "breach of contract" that has crimped production of some disk-drive products. But a spokesman said Conners has been able to find components elsewhere, and that it doesn't expect the shortage to hurt future financial results.

Conners is asking the court for unspecified damages.

Seagate's stock, meanwhile, fell $3.125 to $12.50 in over-the-counter trading yesterday. But the company said it didn't think the drop was related to the suit. On Wednesday, Seagate reported quarterly earnings of 44 cents a share, which a spokesman said were "below expectations but hardly a disaster."
