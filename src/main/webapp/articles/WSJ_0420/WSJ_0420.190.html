Marketing & Media: Disney Signs Accord For Its Films to Air On European Channel

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/20/90

STOCKHOLM -- Esselte AB signed a six-year pact with Walt Disney Co. that gives the Swedish concern's FilmNet satellite TV channel exclusive pay-TV rights to Disney feature films in Scandinavia and the Benelux countries. Hal Richardson, a Disney senior vice president, said the move "is part of a strategy to concentrate more on TV in raising the Disney image around Europe" ahead of the planned 1992 opening of the Euro-Disneyland theme park outside Paris. Terms of the agreement weren't disclosed.

The agreement follows Esselte's decision last month to sell the FilmNet unit as part of a sweeping corporate restructuring. With 430,000 subscribers to its 24-hour service in Denmark, Sweden, Norway, Finland, Belgium and Holland, FilmNet claims to be Europe's biggest satellite distributed pay-TV service.

FilmNet has struggled with mounting competition in recent years, running up hefty losses that finally triggered Esselte's retreat from the entertainment sector. Martin Lindskog, president of Esselte's entertainment division, said he was convinced the link with Disney "makes FilmNet more attractive to prospective buyers."

The planned FilmNet auction is being conducted by U.S. investment bank Wasserstein Perella & Co. Though preparations are still at an early stage, Mr. Lindskog said Esselte has been approached by about 15 prospective suitors, 10 of which he described as serious. He declined to identify any of the companies.

Mr. Lindskog expects the auction to formally commence next month and be completed during the autumn. Industry analysts estimate FilmNet could fetch as much as 2 billion Swedish kronor ($329 million).
