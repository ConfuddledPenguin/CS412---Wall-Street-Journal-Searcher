Business Brief -- Tandy Corp.: Third-Period Net Fell 20%; Sales of Some Items Lagged

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/20/90

Tandy Corp. said its net income declined about 20% for the third quarter ended March 31 as sales of consumer electronics products and low-cost personal computers continued to be sluggish. However, the Fort Worth, Texas-based electronics manufacturer and retailer said that each month of the quarter showed some improvement and that its Radio Shack stores open more than a year recorded a 4% sales gain during the period.

For the third quarter, Tandy said it had net income of $53 million, or 67 cents a share, compared to $66.7 million, or 75 cents a share, last year. Revenue rose 7.4% to $1.01 billion from $943.7 million last year. The company had about 79.6 million shares outstanding at March 31, reflecting a 10-million shares buy-back during calendar 1989.

It was the second consecutive quarter that Tandy's earnings per share had dropped below year-ago levels "and hopefully the last," said a spokesman.

The company said its introduction of more expensive computer models and the growth of its cellular telephone business were bright spots during the quarter.
