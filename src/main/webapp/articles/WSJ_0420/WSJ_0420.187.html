BROOKLYN UNION GAS Co. (New York) -- Moody's Investors Service Inc. said it lowered its rating on the first mortgage bonds and industrial revenue bonds of this natural gas company to single-A-1 from double-A-3. Approximately $600 million of debt is affected. The rating concern said its downgrade reflects expected continued deterioration in Brooklyn Union Gas's interest and cash flow coverages, partly caused by sizable capital expenditures, as well as inadequate rate relief. ---

MANUFACTURERS HANOVER Corp. (New York) -- Standard & Poor's Corp. raised its ratings on Manufacturers Hanover Corp. and its Manufacturers Hanover Overseas Capital Corp. unit's senior debt to triple-B-plus from triple-B. About $3.9 billion of debt is affected. The upgrade reflects a significant improvement in the capital structure of the firm as a result of a $750 million equity issue in late 1989 and the sale of a 60% interest in CIT Group Holdings, S&P said.

NATIONAL EDUCATION Corp. (Irvine, Calif.) -- Standard & Poor's Corp. said it lowered to triple-C-minus from single-B-minus its rating on this vocational training company's $57.5 million face amount of 6.5% convertible subordinated debentures, due 2011. The rating concern said its downgrade reflects National Education's "substantial debt-service requirements and limited liquidity caused by the unsuccessful integration of recent computer-based training company acquisitions." S&P said National Education has so far been unsuccessful in raising a minimum of $10 million in additional capital required before May 1 by a bank credit agreement. It said the company's "negative cash flow, strained liquidity, and lack of additional borrowing capacity reduce its ability to service debt."
