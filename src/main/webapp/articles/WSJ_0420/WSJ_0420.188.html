Technology: Texas Instruments Posts 85% Decrease In First-Quarter Net

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/20/90

DALLAS -- Texas Instruments Inc. narrowly avoided the first-quarter loss it had warned of in January, but only because it sold a majority interest in one of its operations. The electronics and semiconductor maker reported net income of $13 million, or five cents a share, down 85% from year-earlier net of $84.6 million, or 90 cents a share. The earnings included a pretax gain of approximately $15 million from the sale of a majority interest in the company's photomask operation. The company's operating loss for the quarter was $2 million.

Revenue declined 3.8% to $1.54 billion from $1.59 billion. Company officials blamed a weak semiconductor market.

And while Jerry R. Junkins, chairman, president and chief executive officer, said March semiconductor orders were at their highest level in more than a year, prices are still soft. "We expect the second quarter to be about as difficult as the first," he said.

Despite the gloomy short-term outlook, Mr. Junkins told shareholders at the annual meeting that semiconductor demand will grow throughout this year and approach double-digit levels by the fourth quarter. In the long term, he said, the world semiconductor market will grow from $50 billion this year to about $200 billion by 2000.

Texas Instruments also was hurt by the decline in defense spending, recording a 66% drop in defense orders for the first quarter and projecting annual revenue growth of no more than 2% for the next decade. The defense group has been Texas Instruments' second-largest revenue generator, last year contributing $2.1 billion of the company's $6.5 billion in revenue.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Texas Instruments shares closed at $35.50, down 87.5 cents.
