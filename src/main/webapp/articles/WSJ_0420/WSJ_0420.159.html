Business Brief -- McDonald's Corp.: Fast-Food Firm's Net Rose For 100th Straight Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/20/90

McDonald's Corp.'s reported a 12% increase in first-quarter earnings, the 100th consecutive quarter in which the fast-food chain's earnings and revenue have gone up. McDonald's said it earned $158.1 million, or 43 cents a share, compared with $141.7 million, or 38 cents, in the year-earlier quarter. The 1989 per-share figure reflects a 2-for-1 split last May. Revenue rose 11%, to $1.53 billion from $1.38 billion.

But the numbers seemed almost incidental to the streak. Indeed, for more than a year, McDonald's has been noting that its earnings gains were approaching the century mark.

Thus the actual feat called for something McSpecial. Accordingly, after combing through 25 years of earnings for corporate America, a McDonald's researcher happily reported that "no other company in the current Standard & Poor's 500 has publicly reported results which equal this record."

McDonald's also said its stock is held by more investment clubs in the National Association of Investors Corp. than any other security.
