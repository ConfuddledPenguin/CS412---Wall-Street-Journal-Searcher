Alcoa's Recycling of Cans Totaled 17.1 Billion in '89

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/20/90

PITTSBURGH -- Aluminum Co. of America said it collected 17.1 billion used aluminum beverage cans for recycling last year, or about 68 cans for each American. Can collectors were paid $232 million by returning the cans, which weighed 611 million pounds, to Alcoa's 2,400 recycling centers.

The collected cans are remelted, rolled into new aluminum can sheet and sold to can makers, Alcoa said.

An estimated 10 million people in the U.S. recycle aluminum cans regularly, the company said.

Overall, Alcoa said 49 billion beverage cans last year were recycled in the U.S., or 61% of the 80 billion aluminum beverage cans that were produced. This is a record increase from 1988, when 55% of 78 billion aluminum cans were recycled, the company said.
