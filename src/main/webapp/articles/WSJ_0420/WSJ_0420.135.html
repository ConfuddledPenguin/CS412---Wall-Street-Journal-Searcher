Money-Fund Assets Declined $1.25 Billion In the Latest Week

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/20/90

NEW YORK -- Assets of the nation's 508 money-market funds fell $1.25 billion, to $388.60 billion, in the week ended Wednesday, according to the Investment Company Institute, a Washington-based trade association. "With short-term interest rates continuing to hold fairly steady, the overall change in money-market fund assets held in individual accounts was small," said Jacob Dreyer, vice president and chief economist at the institute. "Massive flows of individual tax payments and refunds, typical for mid-April, also had no discernible effect on money-market fund assets. On the other hand, money-market fund assets held in institutional accounts dropped over $1 billion, most probably in response to an abrupt rise in the federal funds rate."

Assets of 246 general-purpose funds fell $467.3 million, to $133.05 billion, while assets of 102 broker-dealer funds rose $237.3 million, to $165.32 billion. Assets of 160 institutional funds fell $1.02 billion, to $90.23 billion.
