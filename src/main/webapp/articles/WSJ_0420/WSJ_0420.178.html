Business Brief -- Cie. Generale d'Electricite: Unit Gets Phone-Gear Job From a Province in China

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/20/90

Alcatel N.V., a unit of Cie. Generale d'Electricite of France, said it received a contract valued at 40 million European currency units ($48.7 million) to provide digital phone-switching equipment to the Chinese province of Sichuan. The order covers 300,000 lines of the company's System 12 digital switching, transmission equipment, telephone cables and training. Alcatel's Spanish unit, Alcatel Standard Electrica, received the order, bringing its contracts in China to five with a total value of 80 million ECUs.

The Madrid unit also got a two-phase contract totaling 54 million ECUs from Poland last week. The first phase covers 140,000 trunk lines of System 12 digital switching, and the second covers 90,000 System 12 lines and transmission equipment.

Alcatel also said its 1989 profit rose 33% to 478 million ECUs, on a 15% gain in revenue to 12.8 billion ECUs. Generale d'Electricite previously reported 1989 consolidated earnings, after payments to minority interests, of 4.94 billion francs ($878.6 million), on revenue of 143.9 billion francs.
