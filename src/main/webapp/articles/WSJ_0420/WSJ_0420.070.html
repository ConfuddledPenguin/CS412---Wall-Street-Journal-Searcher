U.K. Public Sector Borrowing

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/20/90

LONDON -- The public sector borrowing requirement, the most widely used measure of Britain's government deficit or surplus, showed a deficit of #1.63 billion ($2.67 billion) in March compared with a surplus of #1.04 billion in February and a deficit of #2.25 billion in March 1989, the U.K. Treasury said. The March deficit brought the surplus for the year ended March 31 down to #8 billion, compared with a surplus of #14.5 billion in the previous fiscal year.

The government had initially projected a #14 billion surplus for the latest year, but scaled this forecast back to #7 billion in March.
