China's Urban Jobless Rate

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/20/90

BEIJING -- Nearly four million people were laid off in China last year, pushing the urban unemployment rate to 2.6% from 2% at the end of 1988, the English-language China Daily quoted an unidentified Labor Ministry official as saying. The official said 10 million urban residents will be seeking work this year, including 3.78 million people laid off last year, as China's economy stalled, and three million young people entering the job market. Some Chinese economists have put urban unemployment at 4%. The government has said 2.9 million people were jobless last July 31.

A government austerity program aimed at cutting inflation has caused thousands of money-pinched factories to close and forced the suspension of thousands of labor-intensive construction projects.

As many as 30 million of the nation's some 140 million urban workers are underemployed, meaning they have little or nothing to do on their jobs. The government, to avoid labor unrest, has told state-run enterprises to keep workers on the payroll even when there is no work.
