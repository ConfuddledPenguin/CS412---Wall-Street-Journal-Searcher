UST, American Brands Report Profit Increases

  • Author: Kathleen Deveny
  • Date: 04/20/90

American Brands Inc. and UST Inc. both reported strong earnings for the first quarter, bolstered in part by gains from tobacco operations. American Brands' net income rose 13% to $195.4 million, or $2 a share, from $172.2 million, or $1.80 a share, a year earlier. Revenue edged down to $3.2 billion from $3.25 billion.

The consumer products and insurance company said lower exchange rates for foreign currencies, especially the British pound, cut revenue by $116 million and net income by $6 million. Earnings were also adversely affected by the absence of Southland Life, an insurance company sold in March 1989.

Operating profit from American Brands' core tobacco business rose 8% to $281 million on record revenue, driven by strong unit sales and expanding profit overseas. Unit sales in the U.S. dropped 9%, but price increases helped boost profit 3%. The company makes such cigarette brands as Carlton, Lucky Strike and Pall Mall.

The company, based in Old Greenwich, Conn., said interest expense dropped 31%, as strong cash flow allowed the company to reduce borrowing.

American Brands' U.S. tobacco volume was weaker than the industry trend, said Marc Cohen, an analyst with Sanford C. Bernstein & Co., but the company as a whole had an above-average quarter.

At UST, the U.S.'s leading marketer of moist, smokeless tobacco, earnings jumped 20% to $52.6 million, or 46 cents a share, from $44 million, or 38 cents a share, a year earlier. Sales grew 11% to $175.3 million from $157.6 million.

Domestic unit volume of the Greenwich, Conn., company's smokeless tobacco products, including the Copenhagen and Skoal brands, rose 6.6%. "That's definitely above the industry trend," said Rebecca Barfield, an analyst with First Boston Corp.

UST's earnings included a pretax gain of $3 million, or two cents a share, from the early termination of a licensing accord for the distribution of pipe tobacco.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, American Brands closed at $64.75, off $1.50, and UST closed at $26.875, down $1.125.
