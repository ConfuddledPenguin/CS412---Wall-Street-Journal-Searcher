Business Brief -- Fremont General Corp.: Board Votes to Reject Offer Of $140 Million for Unit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/20/90

Fremont General Corp. said its board voted to reject a $140 million offer for its Fremont Compensation Insurance Co. subsidiary made earlier this week by Catalina Acquisitions Inc. The subsidiary is "not for sale," said James A. McIntyre, chairman and chief executive officer of Fremont, an insurance concern based in Santa Monica, Calif. In national over-the-counter trading yesterday, Fremont shares closed off $1 at $16.50 a share.

Fremont Compensation writes workers' compensation coverages. Catalina Acquisitions is an investment vehicle formed by Hermitage Capital Corp., a Nashville insurance investment fund. The group holds a 7.8% stake in Fremont.

Mr. McIntyre said Catalina had "no response" to the board's decision.
