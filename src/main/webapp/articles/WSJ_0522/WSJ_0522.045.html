Applebee's Receives Feelers for Purchase Of All or Part of Firm

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/22/90

KANSAS CITY, Mo. -- Applebee's International Inc. said it received "unsolicited contacts" from parties interested in acquiring "all or a substantial portion" of the company. The restaurant franchiser didn't identify who had made the overtures, but said "no merger or sale negotiations are under way."

In national over-the-counter trading yesterday, Applebee's shares jumped $1.75 to close at $18.50.

The company has some 4,705,000 shares outstanding, giving it a value of about $87 million at yesterday's closing price.

Applebee's said it retained the securities firm Piper, Jaffray & Hopwood Inc. to help it "in evaluating long-term corporate strategy, including these unsolicited contacts, and in determining whether any changes in strategy would be desirable to enhance stockholder value." Piper Jaffray is a unit of Minneapolis-based Piper Jaffray Inc.

At the close of the latest quarter, Applebee's had 19 company-owned restaurants and 104 franchise restaurants. First-quarter earnings, helped by a $296,000 tax credit, were $735,000, or 16 cents a share, compared with a year-earlier loss of $363,000, or 12 cents a share, on fewer shares outstanding. Revenue, including franchise income and sales from company restaurants, rose 39% to $8.8 million.
