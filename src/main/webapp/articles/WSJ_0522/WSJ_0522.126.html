Business Brief -- American Telephone & Telegraph: Some Charges Are Reduced For Enhanced Fax Service

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/22/90

American Telephone & Telegraph Co., New York, said it cut some charges for its enhanced facsimile service originating from the U.S., effective immediately. The telecommunications company said it reduced the prices largely in response to customers' requests to receive an incentive for using the facsimile message service. The cuts applying to the multiple-copy service increase in proportion to the number of recipients. AT&T also is giving a discount on overall monthly use of the enhanced fax service, and cutting prices 20% for faxes sent to Britain and Japan.

AT&T said there are four elements to the price cuts. For the multiple service, the per-page charge drops in the U.S. by as much as 33%, depending on the number of recipients. The discounts apply if there are five or more recipients.

AT&T also is increasing by 50% the amount of information that can be sent on its standard fax page, and will give a quantity discount, based on total dollars of fax service a month, of as much as 10%, in addition to the per-page discount.
