International Brief -- Viag AG: West German Firm's Net For 1st Quarter Rose 14%

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/22/90

Viag AG, a West German energy and commodity concern, said its first-quarter group net income climbed 14% to 74 million marks ($44.9 million) from 65 million marks a year earlier, as acquisitions offset lower earnings from Viag's core aluminum and chemicals lines. Most of the increase stemmed from the first-time consolidation of Kloeckner & Co., a trading concern, and Didier-Werke AG, a maker of industrial ceramics and fire-resistant materials. Chemical and aluminum earnings fell from an unusually high year-earlier level, mostly reflecting the dollar's lower value and falling aluminum prices.

Sales jumped 68% to 4.48 billion marks from 2.67 billion marks, but excluding the acquisitions, sales would have fallen 5%.

Full-year sales will nearly double to about 20 billion marks from 10.43 billion marks in 1989, Chairman Werner Lamby said. He didn't make a profit forecast.

Viag revised its 1989 group profit to 265 million marks from an initially reported 250 million marks; this represents a 21% increase from 219 million marks in 1988.
