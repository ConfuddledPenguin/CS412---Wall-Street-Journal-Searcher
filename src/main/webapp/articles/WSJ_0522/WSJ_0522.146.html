Marketing & Media: Journal Reporter Wins A John Hancock Award

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/22/90

NEW YORK -- Johnnie L. Roberts, a reporter in The Wall Street Journal's New York bureau, won a John Hancock Award for Excellence in Business and Financial Journalism. Mr. Roberts was honored in the financial-business newspapers and magazines category for two articles examining how Dun & Bradstreet Corp. collects and disseminates information on the credit-worthiness of U.S. companies. The awards, sponsored by John Hancock Financial Services, include a $5,000 cash prize.

L. Gordon Crovitz, assistant editor of The Wall Street Journal's editorial page, was awarded a 1990 Gerald Loeb award for editorials on alleged prosecutorial abuses of the Racketeer Influenced and Corrupt Organizations law. The editorials recently also won a first place award for commentary from the New York State Bar Association.

The Loeb awards, which cite distinguished contributions to public understanding of business and financial issues, include a $1,000 cash prize. They were established in 1957 by the late Gerald Loeb, a vice president of E.F. Hutton & Co., and are administered by the John E. Anderson Graduate School of Management at the University of California at Los Angeles.
