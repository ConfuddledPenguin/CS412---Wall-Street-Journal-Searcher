Consilium to Report Earnings Surged 80% In Fiscal 2nd Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/22/90

MOUNTAIN VIEW, Calif. -- Consilium Inc. expects to report that net income for the fiscal second quarter ended April 31 surged 80%, indicating that profit for the period totaled about $1.38 million, Vice President and Chief Financial Officer Mark Finkel said. Consilium makes computer software designed to help manufacturers manage their production processes.

Mr. Finkel said the company's per-share earnings are expected to be in line with analysts' estimates of 19 to 20 cents. In the year-ago quarter, Consilium earned $766,000, or 14 cents a share. Consilium has about 6.9 million shares outstanding, compared with 5.3 million a year earlier.

Mr. Finkel projected revenue for the quarter of more than $8 million, up 38% from $5.8 million a year earlier.

Mr. Finkel said sales of the company's main software product, WorkStream, are booming, particularly overseas. International sales accounted for about 25% of the company's second-quarter revenue, he said.

He said the company is targeting new markets, particularly the defense and aerospace markets. Mr. Finkel said the company may actually benefit from the declines in defense spending that is expected in coming years as a result of the collapse of Eastern Europe's communist regimes.

"It will be an increasingly difficult business for defense companies and they'll need to control costs," he said. "Our products help do just that."

Mr. Finkel said the company has a new product in development with Digital Equipment Corp. targeted for manufacturers of such items as consumer electronics and automotive parts. The product, expected to be on the market late this year, could provide Consilium with a significant boost in sales, Mr. Finkel said.

He said the company doesn't have any debt. It had equity at the end of its fiscal first quarter of $24.7 million. For its full fiscal year, Mr. Finkel said the company expects revenue of about $32 million, about 40% above year-ago levels.
