Premier Industrial Says Net, Sales Will Rise In Fiscal 4th Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/22/90

CLEVELAND -- Premier Industrial Corp. expects to show a modest increase in sales and earnings for the fiscal fourth quarter, which will produce record results for the May 31 fiscal year, Morton L. Mandel, chairman and chief executive officer, said in an interview. The company's sales growth has been slow for the last nine months, reflecting the slowdown in the U.S. economic expansion, Mr. Mandel said. In recent weeks, orders appear to be picking up a little, he said, but there hasn't yet been a marked change.

The distributor of electronic parts and industrial-repair and maintenance products expects a fourth-quarter sales increase slightly greater than the 2.2% rise of the third period, Mr. Mandel said. It is too early to make a specific profit estimate for the quarter, he said, but there will be an increase from the $19.3 million, or 33 cents a share, of the comparable 1989 period.

Mr. Mandel said analysts' estimates that earnings for the fiscal year "will approach $1.30 a share" appear to be reasonable, but final figures won't be available for several months. Fiscal 1990 sales will be in the area of $620 million, he said.

In fiscal 1989, Premier earned $69.7 million, or $1.13 a share, on sales of $596.1 million. Share earnings for 1989 periods are adjusted for a 3-for-2 stock split effective Dec. 22, 1989.

Premier's fiscal 1990 per-share earnings will be based on an average of about 58 million shares outstanding, down from an adjusted 61.7 million shares in fiscal 1989. The company in November of 1988 retired 5,850,000 shares, after adjusting for the split, as part of a $117 million cash distribution to shareholders.

Premier expects to achieve record sales and earnings again in fiscal 1991, if the general economy doesn't deteriorate, Mr. Mandel said.
