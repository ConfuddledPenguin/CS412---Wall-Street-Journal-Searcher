New Securities Issues

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/22/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATES

Unisys Corp. -- $300 million of credit-sensitive notes, due July 1, 1997, priced at par to yield 10.30%. The notes offer bondholders protection in the form of coupon increases that are triggered when the the company's credit rating is downgraded by one full category. The noncallable notes were priced at a spread of 150 basis points above the Treasury's seven-year note. Rated Baa-3 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and triple-B by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Bear Stearns & Co.

MUNICIPALS

Alabama Housing Finance Authority -- $55 million of single-family mortgage revenue bonds, GNMA collateralized home mortgage revenue bond program, 1990 Series B, due April 1 and Oct. 1, 1992-2002, 2010, 2021 and 2022, repriced by a Goldman Sachs & Co. group to lower some yields by five to 10 basis points. The bonds are now priced to yield from 6.40% in 1992 to 7.90% in 2022, changed from a yield of 6.50% in 1992 to 7.95% in 2022. Current interest serial bonds are now priced at par to yield from 6.40% in 1992 to 7.25% in 2002, changed from priced at par to yield from 6.50% in 1992 to 7.35% in 2002. There are $7.74 million of 7.80% term bonds priced at par in 2010, changed from 7.85% at par due Oct. 1, 2010. There are $14.5 million of 7.60% term bonds priced at par due Oct. 1, 2021, which are unchanged. There are $27.41 million of 7.90% term bonds priced at par in 2022, changed from 7.95% issues at par, due Oct. 1, 2022. The bonds are rated triple-A by S&P. Interest on the bonds will be treated as a preference item in calculating the Federal alternative minimum tax that may be imposed on certain investors.

MORTGAGE-BACKED

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $300 million of Remic mortgage securities, offered in 10 classes by Shearson Lehman Hutton Inc. The offering, Series 1990-69, is backed by Fannie Mae 9 1/2% securities.

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $200 million of Remic mortgage securities, offered in five classes by B.T. Securities, the securities unit of Bankers Trust New York Corp. The offering, Series 1990-70, is backed by principal-only securities from Fannie Mae strips offerings. That collateral consists of $130 million (face amount) of principalonly strips from Fannie Mae's strips trust 11, which is backed by Fannie Mae 9% securities, and $70 million of principal only strips from Fannie Mae's trust 58 strips deal, which is backed by 9 1/2% securities.

EUROBONDS

Export-Import bank (Japan) -- $200 million of 9 1/2% Eurobonds due June 29, 2000, priced at 101 1/2 to yield 9.63%, via IBJ International Ltd.

General Electric Capital Corp. (U.S) -- 100 million Canadian dollars of 13% Eurobonds due June 18, 1992, priced at 101 1/2, via Bankers Trust International.

European Investment Bank (agency) -- 125 million European currency units of 10% fungible Eurobonds due February 14, 1997, via Nikko Securities Ltd. Fungible with an existing Ecu 1 billion issue.

World Bank (agency) -- 30 billion yen of 6 7/8% Eurobonds due June 11, 1997, priced at 101 3/8 to yield 6.92% less fees, via LTCB International. Fees 1 5/8.

Oesterreichische Kontrollbank AG (Austria) -- 150 million Swiss francs of 7 1/4% public bonds due June 29, 2005, priced at 101, via Swiss Bank Corp. Guarantee by the Republic of Austria. Call from June 29, 2000 at 101 1/2, declining by 1/2 point per year.

Swedish Export Credit (Sweden) -- 300 million Swedish kronor of 13 5/8% bonds due June 21, 1993, priced at 101.20 to yield 13.70% less fees, via Skandinaviska Enskilda Banken. Fees 1 3/8.

Commerzbank Overseas Finance NV (W. German parent) -- 150 billion of 12 5/8% Eurobonds priced at 101.4 to yield 12.70%c less fees, via Banco di Roma. Guaranteed by Commerzbank AG. Fees 1 5/8.

Interfinance Credit National (French parent) -- 500 million French francs of 10 1/4% Eurobonds due May 21, 1993, priced at 101 3/8, via Credit Commercial de France. Fungible with a 500 million franc issue outstanding. Guaranteed by Credit National.

Ford Capital BV (U.S. parent) -- 150 million pounds of 13 3/8% Eurobonds due June 12, 1997, priced at 101 5/8, via JP Morgan Securities Ltd. Offered at a fixed price of par. Guaranteed by Ford Motor Co., the parent company.
