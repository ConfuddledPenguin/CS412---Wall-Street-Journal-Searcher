Business Brief -- Valley Federal Savings & Loan: California Thrift Says U.S. Is Rescinding Certain Curbs

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/22/90

Valley Federal Savings & Loan Association, Van Nuys, Calif., said federal thrift regulators agreed to rescind certain operating restrictions as part of implementing the troubled thrift's plan to restore its capital health. In January, the federal Office of Thrift Supervision ordered Valley to halt all lending activities, among other sanctions, because it failed to meet federal minimum capital standards.

Valley said a plan approved by the OTS on May 1, and formally adopted recently by the Valley board, allows for "limited" new lending. The January OTS order also banned Valley from paying stock dividends and executive bonuses as well as transacting business with affiliates without federal clearance, restrictions which have also been lifted. The order also required Valley to quit accepting uninsured deposits, and the thrift said it continues to plan to reduce them.
