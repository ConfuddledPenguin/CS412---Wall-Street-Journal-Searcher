Marketing & Media: Belo Venture Starts Ads On Newspaper Readership

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/22/90

DALLAS -- A.H. Belo Corp. said its Universal Belo Productions joint venture has begun an advertising campaign intended to help newspapers improve readership. The joint venture, created by the newspaper and television concern and Universal Press Syndicate, will use characters from Universal features like "Cathy," "Ziggy," "The Far Side" and Dear Abby promoting newspapers through print, radio, billboard and television ads.

A Universal Belo spokesman said that newspapers will pay between $3,000 and $100,000 to use the campaign, depending on their circulation and the package of advertisements they buy.

The joint venture between Belo and Universal, which also is supposed to develop television features using the Universal characters, led Universal to pull 26 popular features, including Dear Abby, "Doonesbury" and "The Far Side," from the Dallas Times Herald. The Times Herald, Dallas's No. 2 newspaper, responded by suing Belo and its Dallas Morning News for $33 million in state court, charging contract interference and unfair competition.

A jury, however, last week sided with Belo and the Dallas Morning News, the market leader, concluding that they weren't guilty of unfair competition.
