REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: Marx's Progress

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/22/90

Yet more news on the outgoing Marxist tide. Formerly Marxist South Yemen prepared over the weekend for its union with capitalist North Yemen by knocking down statues of Lenin and Marx in public squares. Building facades also lost such embedded exhortations as, "No voice can be louder than the voice of the Party." Meanwhile, across the ocean, a "democracy flotilla" of 34 boats set sail from Key West to Cuba. As they steamed six miles from Cuba's shore, a pleasure boat elsewhere in the Florida Straits came upon two Cubans fleeing Castro's paradise on an inner-tube raft. They were turned over to U.S. immigration authorities.
