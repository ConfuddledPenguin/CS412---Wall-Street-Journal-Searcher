Sundstrand Reaches Accord With SEC On Previous Review

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/22/90

ROCKFORD, Ill. -- Sundstrand Corp. said it reached a settlement with the Securities and Exchange Commission on a previously disclosed review by the agency. Sundstrand said the SEC yesterday filed a complaint in federal district court here, charging that the company's financial reports for the 1980 through 1986 "failed to include proper disclosure of the events that formed the basis of previously resolved criminal actions against the company, and that Sundstrand's financial accounting systems were deficient during that period."

In 1988, Sundstrand pleaded guilty to four counts of fraud against the defense department and agreed to pay $115 million in fines.

While "neither admitting nor denying the SEC's charges," Sundstrand said it consented to the entry of a permanent injunction that blocks it from violating securities law in the future.

Sundstrand said it also agreed to have a special committee of independent directors "consider whether the matters in connection with which Sundstrand pled guilty to criminal charges had resulted in any violations of the federal securities laws beyond those alleged in the SEC's complaint."

The settlement, which remains subject to approval by the court, concludes the SEC's investigation of Sundstrand, the company said.

Sundstrand said it settled with the SEC "in order to avoid protracted and costly litigation over practices long since discontinued," and added that the settlement will have "no financial impact" on the firm.
