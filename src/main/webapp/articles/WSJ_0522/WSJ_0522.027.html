AMERICAN TELEPHONE & TELEGRAPH Co. (New York) -- Standard & Poor's Corp. said it affirmed its ratings on $11.1 billion of debt issued by this telecommunications concern and two of its units. The rating concern affirmed its double-A rating on AT&T's senior unsecured debt, which accounted for most of the $11.1 billion, and single-A-1-plus on commercial paper; and single-A-1-plus on commercial paper issued by AT&T Capital Corp. and AT&T Credit Corp. S&P's move came almost a month after Moody's Investors Service Inc. upgraded its rating on $9.2 billion of AT&T's senior debt to double-A-3 from single-A-1. Even after the upgrade, Moody's rating on AT&T's senior debt remains slightly lower than S&P's, which has held steady at double-A since it was downgraded because of the 1984 breakup of the Bell System; before the breakup, S&P rated AT&T triple-A. In its affirmation, which came after an annual review, S&P said financial support for AT&T's credit is expected to strengthen in the near term to mid-term, as earnings and cash flow improve. But it also noted that leverage has increased in the past two years, and that further investments in the financial sector may weaken AT&T's balance sheet in the near term. ---

GRUMMAN Corp. -- Standard & Poor's Corp. lowered its ratings on about $510 million of this Bethpage, N.Y., concern's senior debt and preferred stock. The ratings firm reduced its rating on senior debt to triple-B-minus from triple-B and on subordinated debt and preferred stock to double-B-plus from triple-B-minus. S&P said the downgrades reflect "Grumman's weakening competitive position and continued fairly aggressive debt usage within the context of deteriorating overall defense industry demand prospects." Grumman, principally a manufacturer of military aircraft, declined to comment. S&P said there is "increasing pressure on Grumman to secure new defense-electronics and aircraft-remanufacturing business to bolster future performance. This will become increasingly difficult in a period of declining military procurement."

STORAGE TECHNOLOGY Corp. -- Moody's Investors Service Inc. said it raised the rating on the Louisville, Colo., company's 13.5% senior debentures to Ba2 from Ba3, affecting about $243 million in debt. The rating concern said it believes the maker of data-storage devices will report improved results from sales of its automated cartridge library systems, which allow dozens of tape cartridges to be accessed quickly.

---

UNISYS Corp. -- Standard & Poors Corp. said it affirmed its ratings on approximately $4.9 billion of this company's debt and preferred stock, including its triple-B rating on senior debt. S&P said the Blue Bell, Pa., computer maker has experienced an erosion of its balance sheet, but that recent restructurings should return the company to profitability in the near term. Still, S&P noted, Unisys's debt-to-capital is high and its future is uncertain. For those reasons, S&P said, there is a potential for ratings downgrades.
