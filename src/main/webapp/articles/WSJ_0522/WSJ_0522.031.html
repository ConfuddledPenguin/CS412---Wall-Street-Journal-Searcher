International: Luxembourg Clears BCCI In Money-Laundering Case

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/22/90

Luxembourg banking authorities said they found no grounds for taking legal action against BCCI Holdings (Luxembourg) S.A., the parent of Bank of Credit & Commerce International, which pleaded guilty in January to money laundering in the U.S. Luxembourg officials appointed a panel of magistrates to study the matter shortly after the bank pleaded guilty in Tampa, Fla., to charges that it had obscured the origins of more than $14 million of drug money. Though the legal case didn't directly involve the Luxembourg holding company, it created embarrassing publicity for Luxembourg, which has long sought to dispel the notion that its bank-secrecy laws provide cover for money launderers and other financial criminals.

Yves Mersch, director of Luxembourg's Treasury Ministry, said in a telephone interview that the magistrates and government officials concluded that the U.S. court case reflected the actions of "individuals at a branch {of BCCI} and not a policy which would have been made in Luxembourg."

In addition, he said, only people -- not companies -- have legal liability under Luxembourg law, and BCCI officials in the grand duchy weren't charged with any offenses in the Florida case.

After BCCI's guilty plea was announced, Mr. Mersch said, Luxembourg officials told the holding company that they expected it to stop using the grand duchy as its legal headquarters for its world-wide network, which is managed by officials in London and elsewhere. BCCI's current restructuring program appears to satisfy Luxembourg's demand, Mr. Mersch said.
