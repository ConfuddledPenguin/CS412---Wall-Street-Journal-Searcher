Technology: Researchers Say Bacteria May Cause Parkinson's

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/22/90

DAVIS, Calif. -- Researchers at the University of California School of Medicine here said a bacteria commonly found in the soil may be one of the causes of Parkinson's disease. The researchers, using tests on mice and rats, said a pathogenic strain of Nocardia asteroids, a common soil organism, caused neurological signs that "appear to be identical" to those shown by people with Parkinson's.

Parkinson's disease afflicts about a million Americans, primarily those over age 55, and is characterized by limb rigidity and head shaking. The research was reported last week at the annual American Society for Microbiology meeting in Anaheim, Calif.
