Business Brief -- Birmingham Steel Corp.: Melting Work Is Halted; Charge of $6.9 Million Set

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/22/90

Birmingham Steel Corp. said it was suspending indefinitely its Norfolk steel melting operations, resulting in a one-time, fourth-quarter charge of about $6.9 million, or 57 cents a share. The Birmingham, Ala., operator of mini-mills attributed the shutdown to continued losses as well as high maintenance and electric power costs. Birmingham said it would continue to operate its Norfolk rolling mill by processing semifinished steel billets supplied from other Birmingham locations. The company said it didn't anticipate any "material change" in steel shipments or any disruptions in deliveries as a result of the suspension.

In the fourth quarter ended June 30, Birmingham earned $7.3 million, or 59 cents a share, on revenue of $119.8 million.
