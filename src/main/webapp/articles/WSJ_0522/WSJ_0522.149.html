Chrysler Is Postponing Closing St. Louis Plant To Fill Early Orders

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/22/90

HIGHLAND PARK, Mich. -- Chrysler Corp. is delaying for seven weeks the planned closing of its St. Louis I assembly plant to fill early orders for 1991 models. The St. Louis plant builds Chrysler's Dodge Daytona sports car and Chrysler LeBaron coupe and convertible. It will be the third plant Chrysler has closed in two years as the No. 3 U.S. auto maker attempts to bring its production capacity in line with declining sales.

Chrysler permanently closed its Kenosha, Wis., plant at the end of 1988. Earlier this year, Chrysler closed its aging Jefferson Avenue assembly plant in Detroit, because of slow sales of the Dodge Omni and Plymouth Horizon cars built there. Chrysler is constructing a new plant, slated to begin production in January 1992, next to the old Jefferson Avenue plant.

The company announced on Feb. 3 plans to close and mothball St. Louis I, effective Sept. 21. Yesterday's announcement postpones that closure to Nov. 9, providing almost two month's extra work for some 2,300 hourly employees. Chrysler builds minivans at a second St. Louis plant that isn't affected by the closing.

The company said extending production at the St. Louis I facility minimizes disruption of output at the plants to which it is moving Daytona and LeBaron. Retooling to add the cars at Newark, Del., and Sterling Heights, Mich., will start on Dec. 21.

Sterling Heights, which gets Daytona, will resume production the week of Jan. 14. Newark, which adds the LeBaron coupe and convertible, will resume production the week of Jan. 22.
