Business Brief -- Perry Drug Stores Inc.: Acquisition of 16 Outlets Operated by Revco Is Set

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/22/90

Perry Drug Stores Inc., Pontiac, Mich., said it agreed to acquire 16 Detroit-area stores operated by Revco Drug Stores Inc. of Twinsburg, Ohio. The price wasn't disclosed. Perry's purchase is part of a larger transaction, in which Revco is selling 221 stores through Chapter 11 bankruptcy-law proceedings to Reliable Drug Stores Inc. of Indianapolis. Sixteen of these stores in turn would be sold to Perry. The entire deal requires bankruptcy court approval, which is expected in June, Perry said.

The proposed acquisition supports Perry's strategy of filling in existing markets and concentrating on Michigan, said Jack A. Robinson, chairman and chief executive officer. After the acquisition, Perry will operate 186 conventional drugstores in Michigan and 23 in the Chicago area.

Perry plans to open 20 stores in the current year and expects to complete the divestiture of 14 A.L. Price deep-discount stores by the end of this month.
