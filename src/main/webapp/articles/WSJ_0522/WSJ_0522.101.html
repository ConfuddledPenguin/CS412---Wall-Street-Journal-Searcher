Qume Board Clears Offer of $86 Million From Buy-Out Group

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/22/90

MILPITAS, Calif. -- Qume Corp. said its board approved a sweetened $86 million, or $7.25 a share, buy-out proposal from a group including its chairman and chief executive officer, David Lee, and Singapore-based Wearnes Technology (Pte) Ltd. The offer compares with previous per-share offers from the group of $6.50 and $6.75. The Lee group already holds about 22% of Qume's roughly 11.8 million common shares outstanding.

Qume, a computer-products maker, said the agreement is subject to several conditions, including shareholder approval, which is expected to be sought no later than September. Qume said its investment banker, First Boston Corp., is continuing to solicit other offers for the company.

Separately, Qume reported net income for the fiscal-fourth quarter ended Feb. 28 of $827,000, or seven cents a share, compared with net of $2 million, or 17 cents a share, a year earlier. Sales slipped 2.8% to $54.5 million from $56.1 million. The latest quarter included a $2 million operating loss, which was more than offset by a $6.9 million gain from the sale of stock of a Taiwanese subsidiary.

For the fiscal year, Qume net rose 72% to $8.7 million, or 73 cents a share from $5 million, or 43 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue rose 21% to $222.3 million from $183.2 million.
