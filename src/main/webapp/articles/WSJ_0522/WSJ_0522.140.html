Marketing & Media: Tribune Plans to Launch Chicagoland Cable Station

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/22/90

CHICAGO -- Tribune Co., already the operator of the top prime-time independent television channel in the nation, said it will launch an 18-hour-a-day cable channel dubbed Chicagoland on Jan. 1, 1991. The company said the news, sports and information channel would draw on the resources of its locally top-billing WGN-TV, its nationally top-billing WGN radio and its Chicago-area leading newspaper, the Chicago Tribune.

Robert J. Gremillion, 35 years old, was named vice-president and general manager of Chicagoland. Mr. Gremillion, currently station manager of Tribune's New Orleans independent WGNO-TV, said Tribune had committed "several million dollars in capital and operating expenses" to the project.

Tribune has been pressing expansion into Chicago's growing suburban market to offset the softening market for metropolitan daily newspapers. The Chicago Tribune launched a separate edition for a fast-growing suburban county last year and Chicagoland will provide "extensive coverage of surrounding suburban counties," according to James C. Dowdle, president and chief executive officer of Tribune Broadcasting Co.

Chicagoland, a term coined and popularized by Tribune's late chairman and publisher Col. Robert R. McCormick, will also carry live telecasts of Tribune's baseball franchise, the Chicago Cubs. Currently, WGN is barred from broadcasting many games because of agreements with ESPN and CBS.
