Technology Brief -- International Business Machines: Price Cuts Said Imminent In Personal Computer Line

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/22/90

International Business Machines Corp. will soon cut prices on a wide range of its personal computer products, including its popular Model 55SX, industry executives said. The Armonk, N.Y.-based company also will unveil a personal computer without a disk drive to its lineup, though not until autumn, they said. IBM declined to comment on the price cuts, first reported in PC Week, an industry weekly, as was the diskless computer. But the executives said the cuts, which they didn't quantify, will apply to most of IBM's PC lineup, except for products unveiled recently: four versions of the high-end Model 80; an entry-level version of the portable Model P70; two versions of the Model 65SX; the education-oriented Model 25 286; and several new peripherals.

Regarding the diskless computer for use as part of a computer network, an IBM spokeswoman noted that James Cannavino, the executive who heads the personal systems business, said last fall that IBM will produce such a machine before the end of 1990. But she wouldn't specify a timetable for the product.
