Consolidated Edison Mulls Debt Financing

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/22/90

NEW YORK -- Consolidated Edison Co. of New York said it is contemplating financings of at least $300 million of debt in 1990. Raymond J. McCann, executive vice president and chief financial officer, said at the annual meeting that the utility expects financings of another $500 million in 1991. The financings would be used to refund maturing long-term debt and to help fund the utility's expanded construction fund, he said.

Mr. McCann said the availability of tax-exempt financing is tightening and that Con Ed anticipates the new issues will be a mix of taxable and tax-exempt debt. The plan is subject to approval by New York's Public Service Commission.
