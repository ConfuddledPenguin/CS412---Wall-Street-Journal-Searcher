Kollmorgen Meeting Delayed as Judge Asks Pact With Vernitron

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/22/90

NEW YORK -- A Connecticut state court judge postponed Kollmorgen Corp.'s annual meeting for a week, and ordered the company and a dissident shareholder to try to resolve a dispute involving control of the company. Superior Court Judge Nicholas A. Cioffi delayed until May 31, the Kollmorgen annual meeting for which Vernitron Corp. has been soliciting proxies to gain control of the board.

Vernitron, a closely held motors and controls company based in Deer Park, N.Y., has said it is prepared to offer $15 a share for Kollmorgen if its proxy contest for control of the board is successful.

Kollmorgen, a Simsbury, Conn., maker of motors, controls and electro-optical equipment, has been trying to fend off a takeover attempt by Vernitron. The two companies are suing each other over a collapsed merger agreement.

The ruling by Judge Cioffi concerns litigation related to Kollmorgen's issuance in March of convertible preferred stock to an investor group that represents about 13.8% of Kollmorgen's common shares outstanding.

The judge asked each side to name a member of a special masters committee to seek a resolution to the dispute over the preferred shares, which Vernitron asserts were issued in violation of previous court stand-still orders.

Vernitron owns almost 6% of Kollmorgen.
