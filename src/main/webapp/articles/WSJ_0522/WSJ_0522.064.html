Girard Partners L.P. Still Seeks to Oust Pic `N' Save Board

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/22/90

LA JOLLA, Calif. -- An investor group led by David H. Batchelder said it hasn't abandoned its efforts to oust the board of Pic 'N' Save Corp. and questioned the sincerity of the company's plan to put itself up for sale. The group, Girard Partners L.P., said it won't participate in Pic 'N' Save's auction because "it makes unnecessary demands on potential bidders" and "isn't designed to attract broad participation."

Pic 'N' Save fired back that its financial advisers have already begun contacting potential buyers from a list of more than 100 companies, individuals and other entities. The company also extended the bid deadline to July 15 from June 20.

Pic 'N' Save, a Dominguez, Calif., retailer of close-out merchandise, said Friday it would provide nonpublic information to buyers who sign a confidentiality agreement with several standstill provisions. Girard said the conditions were "unacceptable."

Pic 'N' Save countered that its financial advisers offered during the weekend to make "significant concessions" to Girard to induce the group to participate in the auction. Specifically, Pic 'N' Save offered to permit Girard to resume its solicitation of consents and proxies for its board nominees after July 15 if no bidder emerges. If a bidder does come forth, the date would be pushed back to Jan. 1 to permit negotiation of a sale agreement, a company spokesman said.

Girard holds a 6.7% stake in Pic 'N' Save and has been soliciting consents from shareholders to install five of its nominees on the company's seven-member board. One of the group's nominees is a former Pic 'N' Save executive who holds a 4% stake. If the group succeeds, it has said it would buy the company or auction it off to the highest bidder.

In national over-the-counter trading, Pic 'N' Save slid $1.25 a share, to $12.625, on turnover of more than one million shares.
