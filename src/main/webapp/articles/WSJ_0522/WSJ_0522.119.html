Business Brief -- Jamaica Savings Bank: Concern Will Convert Itself To Stockholder Ownership

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/22/90

Jamaica Savings Bank, a Lynbrook, N.Y.-based mutual savings bank, said it is converting to a stock savings bank through a public offering of 16 million shares of common stock. A mutual savings bank is privately held, basically owned by its depositors. The new shares are being offered to depositors, other customers and certain members of the general public at prices in the range of $7.50 to $10 a share. The mutual savings bank said the final price will be set after the completion of the offering on June 21. Jamaica Savings had deposits of $1.23 billion and retained earnings of $197.1 million as of Dec. 31. Most of its offices are in Queens County.

Jamaica Savings said it is converting to a holding company structure to expand into a wider range of financial services that are limited by its current charter.

The offering is being managed by Adams Cohen Securities Inc., Great Neck, N.Y. The conversion is subject to approval of depositors at a special meeting June 19.
