American General Defeats Board Slate Pushed by Torchmark

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/22/90

HOUSTON -- American General Corp. said it "decisively" defeated an alternative slate of directors proposed by Torchmark Corp. at its annual meeting, but beat back a nonbinding resolution seeking its sale by just a 56% to 42% margin. Although the victory was hollow -- since the big insurance and financial-services concern had already decided to put itself up for sale anyway -- an American General spokesman said the final tally "indicates our decision was totally irrespective of the vote."

Some analysts have speculated that the company decided to place itself on the auction block mostly because of a poor showing in the May 2 proxy contest. But American General has maintained that it decided to open itself to bidders to avoid being forced into a sale to Torchmark at a depressed price. As reported, Torchmark, a Birmingham, Ala., insurer, offered $50 a share, or $6.3 billion, for American General, before launching its proxy fight.

American General said preliminary results indicate shareholders voted 2-to-1 to approve the company's nominees. The slate includes three new outside directors -- Brady F. Carruth, president and chief executive of Gulf Coast Capital Corp.; Richard J.V. Johnson, chairman and chief executive of the Houston Chronicle Publishing Co.; and Robert E. Smittcamp, president and chief executive of Lyons-Magnus Co. -- and three American General officers, including Harold S. Hook, chairman and chief executive; Michael J. Poulous, president; and Henry S. Romaine, vice chairman.

In composite trading yesterday on the New York Stock Exchange, American General closed at $49.125, unchanged.
