International Brief -- BSN S.A.: French Firm Plans to Sell Its Cookie Business in U.S.

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/22/90

French food group BSN S.A. announced that it has put its U.S. cookie units up for sale as part of its strategy of focusing on high-growth markets in eastern Europe and Asia. The company said its Mother's and Salerno subsidiaries, with combined annual revenue of about $200 million, claim only 5.7% of the U.S. cookie market, and further expansion in the largely saturated sector would have been too costly.

BSN said "new perspectives are opening in the countries of Eastern Europe and in regions of strong demographic growth such as India and Asia . . . In order to take advantage of this rapid expansion, BSN has decided to sell its U.S. business."

The French food group retains U.S. units in other product categories, including yogurt maker Dannon Co. and Lea & Perrins, a producer of condiments.
