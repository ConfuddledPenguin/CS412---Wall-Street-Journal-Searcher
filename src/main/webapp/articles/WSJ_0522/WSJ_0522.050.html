Citizens Utilities Sets Agreement to Acquire Louisiana Gas Utility

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/22/90

STAMFORD, Conn. -- Citizens Utilities Co. said it signed a definitive agreement to acquire Louisiana General Services Inc., a Harvey, La., natural-gas utility, in a deal valued at approximately $125 million. Under the terms of the agreement, each of Louisiana General's approximately 5.3 million shares outstanding will be converted into 0.6719 of a share of Citizens Series B stock, which closed in national over-the-counter trading at $34 each, down $2. In New York Stock Exchange composite trading, Louisiana General shares closed at $20.25, up $3.125 each.

Citizens is a utilities concern dominated by the telecommunications properties -- mostly cellular and paging companies -- it acquired in recent years. The acquistion of Louisiana General will restore its profile in the gas business, said Ishier Jacobson, Citizens chief executive officer. Citizens owns gas operations in Colorado and Arizona.

"We were once much bigger in gas," proportionate to other Citizens businesses, said Mr. Jacobson. "We'd like to increase that share again. And of course, we're maintaining our diversification."

Mr. Jacobson said the merger wasn't sought by Louisiana General, but emerged through social discussions between a Citizen board member and a Louisiana General board member. James W. Swenson, Louisiana General's chairman, president and chief executive, said he was "absolutley happy" with the arrangement.

Louisiana General reported income of $9.7 million on revenue of $152.9 million for the year ended March 31, 1990. For 1989, Citizens posted profit of $88.7 million on revenue of $338.7 million.

Analysts said the deal makes sense, though it bucks Citizens' recent acquisition pattern. "I see Citizens as the equivalent of a mutual fund of regulated utilities," said Bert Kramer of PaineWebber Group Inc. "From that point of view, gas makes as much sense as anything else."
