International Brief -- Carven: Rothschild Group May Buy Fashion and Perfume House

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/22/90

A group led by Baron Edmond de Rothschild's Paris holding company is about to acquire a 60% stake in the Carven fashion and perfume house, people familiar with the pending transaction said. Carven is known for its Vetiver and Ma Griffe fragrances, as well as scarves, ties and air hostess uniforms. The price wasn't immediately available.

If completed, the acquisition would mark the baron's third fashion investment during the past six months. Last year, his Cie. Financiere Edmond de Rothschild acquired a 40% stake in once-famous fashion house Jacques Fath. This year, the holding company acquired 34% of Maud Frizon shoes.

Carven's ties, watches, perfumes and scarves bring in about 200 million francs ($36 million) in annual sales, half from license fees. Unlike some aging houses, Carven still is profitable, but the new owners hope to expand business sharply. Carven's sales already are strong in Japan and South Korea.
