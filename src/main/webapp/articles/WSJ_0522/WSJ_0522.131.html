Business Brief -- Members Service Corp.: Chapter 11 Status Sought; Demand by IRS Is Blamed

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/22/90

Members Service Corp. said it filed for Chapter 11 protection from its creditors after the Internal Revenue Service demanded full payment of more than $258,000 in delinquent taxes. The company, which has holdings in telecommunications, energy and financial services, said the filing was an effort to "protect shareholders and employees and save the company."

Members Service said the funds sought by the agency resulted from failure by the company to pay certain trust fund taxes and may or may not include penalties and interests. Leon Hooten, chairman and chief executive officer, said the company agreed to pay the IRS $20,000 a month, but the IRS accelerated payment. He said the company hadn't missed any payments and was current with the debt.

A spokesman for the IRS said the agency is prohibited by law from disclosing taxpayer information to third parties, without written consent of the taxpayer.

In the court filing, Members Service listed assets of almost $10 million, and combined secured and unsecured debt at about $1 million. The IRS is listed as the company's largest creditor.
