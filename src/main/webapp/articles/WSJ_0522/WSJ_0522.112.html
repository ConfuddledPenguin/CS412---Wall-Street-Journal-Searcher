Abreast of the Market: Industrials Advance 24.77 Points; Some See Average Reaching 3000

  • Author: Craig S. Smith
  • Date: 05/22/90

NEW YORK -- Stocks started weak but ended strong, with the Dow Jones Industrial Average blazing a trail into uncharted territory. The industrial average posted its third new high since last Monday. Some analysts say the average's current march won't end until the index reaches 3000.

"I don't think we are at the end of our run," said Trude Latimer, market strategist at Josephthal & Co.

She added that there doesn't appear to be any concerted follow-through on the occasional bouts of profit-taking that hit the market. Selling shaved nearly 12 points off the average on Friday, and stock prices opened lower yesterday.

But before long, stocks were rallying, led once again by secondary issues.

"Technically, we will probably stop short at 3000 {on the average}," said Ms. Latimer, at which point the market may be in for some more serious selling.

The industrial average ended the day off its highs but with a 24.77-point gain at 2844.68.

The buying started in smaller issues, lifting the broader market averages before spreading to the blue chips. The broader market ended with slightly sharper gains. Standard & Poor's 500-Stock Index closed up 3.36 to 358.00, while the New York Stock Exchange Composite Index rose 1.73 to 195.28. The Dow Jones Equity Market Index gained 3.24 to 333.10.

Peter Canelo, investment strategist at Bear Stearns, said the strength among the broader market means stocks should be able to sustain their current advance. "That breadth is telling us that the next step for the market is higher," Mr. Canelo said.

Advancers led decliners, 997 to 538. New York Stock Exchange volume totaled 166,280,000 shares, up from Friday's 162.5 million.

Nova led the most active list with a 1/2-point gain to 7 3/8 on volume of 2.5 million shares. The company announced that it is selling its rubber division to Bayer AG of West Germany for about 1.25 billion Canadian dollars (US$1.06 billion). As part of the deal, Nova will be released from about C$100 million of debt. It will use the cash proceeds to further pay down debt. Nova said the transaction may result in a modest gain.

National Semiconductor was next on the list with a gain of 1/2 point to 8 1/4 on volume of nearly 2.4 million shares.

Bank of Boston surged 1, or 7.7%, to 14. The stock gained 1/2 point on Friday after winning the bidding for $340 million in deposits of the failed MerchantsBank, a unit of Merchant Capital. The award was seen by many bank analysts as an endorsement of Bank of Boston's health by the Federal Deposit Insurance Corp.

Investors have shunned New England banks or sold the stocks short in the wake of widespread problems related to the weakening real estate market there. Yesterday's news sent short sellers into the market to buy stock with which to pay back their loans, according to Dow Jones Professional Investor Report.

Homestead Financial jumped 5/8, or more than 31%, to 2 5/8. The company declined to comment on the stock's activity.

Cable television stocks continued to advance. Analysts are now saying that the industry may escape stringent legislative regulation. Meanwhile, the industry's annual trade show began yesterday in Atlanta. Time Warner, which has extensive cable TV interests, was up 4 to 99 1/4. The stock gained 2 3/4 on Friday.

Louisiana General Services jumped 3 1/8 to 20 1/4 after signing a stock swap merger agreement with Citizens Utilities.

Sotheby's fell 1 to 17 1/2, retracing some of the gains made last week in advance of its spring series of impressionist and modern art auctions.

Geico added 2 1/8 to 152 5/8. Yesterday's "Heard on The Street" column of this newspaper said Wall Street merger specialists are speculating that investor Warren Buffett might be preparing to sell his 44% stake in the personal line insurer. The article gives Geico high marks as an investment, and says any buyer of the Buffett stake probably would pay a premium to the market price.

Baltimore Gas & Electric tumbled 1 1/4 to 29. The company's board failed to raise the quarterly dividend rate at a meeting on Friday. A Kidder Peabody analyst said the utility's board usually votes on a dividend increase at its May meeting.

Monsanto slid 1 1/4 to 100 3/4, after dropping 1/2 on Friday. The stock came under pressure last week after Goldman Sachs downgraded its rating on the stock while First Boston reiterated its "sell" rating. First Boston also cut earnings estimates for the company. A trader who handled a large sell order of Monsanto shares yesterday said the stock is still feeling the repercussions of the analysts' moves.

Technology stocks surged. International Business Machines rose 1 5/8 to 117, Digital Equipment gained 1 3/8 to 90 5/8, and Compaq Computer jumped 2 3/4 to 121 3/8. Motorola advanced 1 1/2 to 78 1/8.

Vishay Intertechnology was up 1 1/4 to 21 3/4. The company has decided to make a "white knight" offer to acquire Crystalate Holdings, Vishay's president and chief executive officer, Felix Zandman, said. Crystalate called for Vishay's aid in fending off an unsolicited offer by TT Group PLC.

Interest rate-sensitive Federal Home Loan Mortgage added 2 1/4 to 71 3/4, while Federal National Mortgage added 3/4 to 40 1/4 in Big Board trading.

On the American Stock Exchange, Cablevision System Class A, which rose 2 1/4 points Friday, added 2 1/8 to 28 7/8.

The American Stock Exchange Market Value Index rose 3.04 to 359.36. Volume totaled 12,360,000 shares.
