Business Brief -- Talman Home Federal S&L: Thrift Agrees to Be Bought By Group for $100 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/22/90

Talman Home Federal Savings & Loan Association, Chicago, said it reached a preliminary agreement to be acquired by a private investor group for $10 a share, or $100 million, in cash and notes. Under the agreement, Talman shareholders would receive $5 a share in cash, and senior subordinated notes of a newly formed holding company, Talman Bancorp, that Talman valued at $5 a share.

The investor group is led by Harrison I. Steans, who has headed a number of Chicago-area bank-holding companies during the past several years.

The proposed sale was required by thrift-bailout legislation passed by Congress last year that prohibited thrifts from including non-earning assets such as "good will" on balance sheets, said James Sherman, Talman's senior vice president. In Talman's case, goodwill amounted to $562 million; subtracting it left Talman undercapitalized in the view of federal regulators.

The new owners are expected to retain Talman's current management and to invest $300 million in Talman common stock following the purchase.
