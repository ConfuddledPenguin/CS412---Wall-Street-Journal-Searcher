Chrysler Recalls Vehicles Produced in 1987 by Unit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/22/90

WASHINGTON -- Chrysler Corp. is recalling part of American Motors' 1987 production of Jeep Cherokees, Comanchees and Wagoneers to replace overheating catalytic converters, the Environmental Protection Agency said. The vehicles have 2.5-liter, fuel-injected engines built before Oct. 16, 1986. The EPA said that 11,056 of these models were produced but that some owners already have had new converters installed under the vehicle-emission warranty.

Chrysler will notify vehicle owners of the repair, which will be free. The EPA discovered the problem during routine testing.
