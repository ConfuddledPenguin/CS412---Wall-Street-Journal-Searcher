Rosewood Proposes Adding Craib to Board Of First Executive

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/22/90

DALLAS -- Rosewood Financial Inc., whose previous efforts to take over troubled First Executive Corp. failed, said it had proposed that First Executive expand its board by one seat and appoint Donald F. Craib Jr., former chairman and chief executive officer of Allstate Insurance Group, to the position. In a Securities and Exchange Commission filing, the unit of Rosewood Corp., which is owned by the trust estate of oil heiress Carolyn Rose Hunt, said Mr. Craib says he thinks he can make a substantial contribution to the struggling Los Angeles insurer whose huge junk-bond portfolio led to a $775.6 million net loss in 1989. The company has six directors.

Previously, Mr. Craib was recruited by Rosewood as a candidate to succeed First Executive Chairman Fred Carr, whose ouster was a condition of the aborted takeover bid. Rosewood has seen a $100 million investment in First Executive common stock reduced to roughly $20 million in value as a result of the company's troubles. Mrs. Hunt's trust was hit with additional bad news last week when its troubled thrift, Southwest Savings Association, was placed under U.S. conservatorship.

A Rosewood spokesman refused to say whether the company continues to seek Mr. Carr's ouster or whether it planned to renew its previous takeover bid, which was turned down by First Executive's board.

Other First Executive holders were skeptical about whether Rosewood planned to make another offer. "I think Rosewood and the {Hunt} family have suffered so extensively that I just can't imagine the family saying, `We want to throw more good money after bad,'" said investor Travis Reed, who said he owns a 2% stake in First Executive.

First Executive officals couldn't be reached for comment.
