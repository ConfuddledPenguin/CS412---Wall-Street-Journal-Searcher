HUD's Monroe Gets Post In S&L Bailout Agency

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/22/90

WASHINGTON -- Peter Monroe, an official with the Department of Housing and Urban Development, was appointed to a top position in the savings-and-loan bailout effort. As expected, the Oversight Board of the Resolution Trust Corp. appointed Mr. Monroe, 46 years old, to be the board's president, effective June 11. The Oversight Board sets policy and oversees the actions of the RTC, which sells and liquidates insolvent S&Ls.

The post was a position of some controversy in the early months of the bailout, which began last August, when the several agencies with a role in the S&L sales disputed lines of authority. Mr. Monroe will succeed William Taylor, a Federal Reserve official, who was in the job temporarily. Mr. Taylor is expected to take over as chairman of the RTC, which is lower in the bailout hierarchy, when the current chairman, William Seidman, resigns.

Mr. Monroe has been the general deputy assistant secretary for housing for one year, and before that was president of Rutenberg Commercial Developers, Clearwater, Fla.

The Oversight Board includes Treasury Secretary Nicholas Brady, Federal Reserve Board Chairman Alan Greenspan, and Housing and Urban Development Secretary Jack Kemp. The board also includes Philip Jackson, a former Federal Reserve governor, and Robert Larson, president of Taubman Co., a real estate development concern in Bloomfield Hills, Mich.
