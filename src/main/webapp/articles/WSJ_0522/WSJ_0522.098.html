World Bank Approves $96.7 Million in Loans For Projects in India

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/22/90

WASHINGTON -- The World Bank and its affiliate, the International Development Association, approved $96.7 million in loans to India for health centers and population-control projects in rural areas. In other actions, the IDA cleared a $16.1 million credit to Bolivia to help finance and expand closely held manufacturing enterprises, and a $14 million loan to Western Samoa to rebuild roads damaged by a cyclone that hit the Pacific island nation in February.

The credits, including most of the financing for the projects in India, will be interest-free except for small administrative fees. The World Bank will provide $10 million of the loans to India at a variable interest rate, currently 7.75% a year.
