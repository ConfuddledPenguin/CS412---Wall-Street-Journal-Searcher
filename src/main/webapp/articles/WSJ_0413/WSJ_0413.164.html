Business Brief -- Torchmark Corp.: Solicitation of American General Proxies Is Allowed

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/13/90

Torchmark Corp. won the latest round in its fight to acquire rival American General Corp. when a federal appeals court stayed a preliminary injunction against Torchmark's soliciting of proxies to elect a slate of directors to American General's board. The Birmingham, Ala.-based insurer said it was pleased with the ruling and intends to proceed with the solicitation as soon as possible.

The latest ruling is an about-face from one last week in which federal Judge David Hittner sided with Houston-based American General. The company argued that Torchmark failed to notify American General of its plans 60 days in advance of the May 2 annual meeting as required by American General bylaws.

Despite Judge Hittner's ruling, Torchmark earlier this week began soliciting proxies in support of a resolution that seeks to force American General's board to sell or merge the company. Torchmark's resolution also calls for appointment of a committee of independent directors to evaluate all acquisition proposals. American General officials couldn't be reached for comment.
