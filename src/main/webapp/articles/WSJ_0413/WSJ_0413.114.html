Credit Ratings: Moody's Lowers Ratings on Debt Of Gulf Canada

  • Author: Gary Lamphier
  • Date: 04/13/90

Moody's Investors Service Inc. said it lowered its ratings on various Gulf Canada Resources Ltd. debt securities, citing the oil and gas producer's "weak earnings and fixed-charge coverage outlook" over the intermediate term. Moody's said its forecast for Calgary, Alberta-based Gulf Canada, which is controlled by Toronto's Reichmann family, reflects the company's high interest charges and preferred dividend costs, a poor outlook for its drilling operations, and falling domestic oil output.

The ratings downgrade, which Moody's said affects about 900 million Canadian dollars (US$774.4 million) of debt securities, follows an announcement last month by the agency that it had placed Gulf Canada under review for a possible ratings cut.

Moody's said its downgrade affects Gulf Canada's senior debentures, lowered to Baa-1 from single-A-2; Series 1 preferred shares, cut to Baa-2 from single-A-3; and a Gulf Canada shelf registration, reduced to (provisional) Baa-1 from (provisional) single-A-2. Moody's also lowered Gulf Canada's commercial paper rating to Prime-2 from Prime-1.

The agency said it expects Gulf Canada's ability to comfortably service debt to "remain constrained over the next several years" because of dividend requirements. As a result, it said, "capital spending is likely to outpace net cash flow generation, leading to increased financial leverage" without an equity infusion or asset sales.

A Gulf Canada spokesman said, "We're disappointed naturally, but that's their assessment. What else can I say?"

Moody's said it sees Gulf Canada's longer-term prospects as sound in light of its big reserves in frontier areas, such as the Beaufort Sea and off the east coast of Newfoundland. It said Gulf Canada has also made "significant progress" in extending its exploration prospects in Indonesia, where it gained a major foothold with its 1988 acquisition of Asamera Inc.

Moody's said this should help offset the company's shrinking output from conventional fields in western Canada.

In American Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Gulf Canada common stock closed at $13, unchanged.

Moody's said it also downgraded to Prime-2 from Prime-1 its rating on about C$160 million of short-term debt of Olympia & York (Gulf Canada Square) Ltd., a unit of Olympia & York Developments Ltd., the Reichmann family's wholly owned flagship real estate concern.

The unit was formed to issue notes secured by the corporate headquarters of Gulf Canada. A Moody's analyst said the downgrade of the unit's debt doesn't affect ratings on the debt of Olympia & York Developments or any of its other units.

Moody's said the rating cut, which followed a similar ratings downgrade last month by Canada's Dominion Bond Rating Service Ltd., was "prompted by the fact that the company's ability to meet its obligations depends largely on the receipt of lease payments" from Gulf Canada, whose debt ratings have been downgraded.
