Italy's Electricity Consumption

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/13/90

MILAN, Italy -- Electricity consumption in Italy totaled 20.58 billion kilowatt-hours in March, up 5.3% from a year earlier, ENEL, the state electricity utility, said. ENEL also said electricity demand in the first three months rose 4.3% to 61 billion kwh. A kilowatt-hour is the amount of electrical energy consumed when 1,000 watts are used for one hour.

Gross electricity generation in Italy rose 9.3% to 18.92 billion kwh in March. Net imports totaled 3.11 billion kwh, down from 3.4 billion kwh in March 1989. In the first quarter, however, net imports totaled about 8.8 billion kwh, up 2.4% from a year earlier.
