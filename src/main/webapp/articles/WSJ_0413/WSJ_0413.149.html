Business Brief -- American Realty Trust Inc.: Board Adopts Rights Plan To Deter Hostile Takeover

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/13/90

American Realty Trust Inc. said in Dallas that its board adopted a preferred share-purchase rights plan intended to deter a hostile takeover. The real estate investment trust controlled by Gene E. Phillips and William S. Friedman, former top executives of Southmark Corp., said the plan wasn't adopted in response to an effort to acquire the company. It also said it doesn't know of efforts to acquire the concern.

Terms of the anti-takeover plan call for preferred share-purchase rights to be distributed on April 23 to shares of record on that date. If a hostile suitor increases his stake in American Realty by more than 25% or if the company's board decides that a holder of 10% or more of the stock is hurting the company's business, the rights will allow shareholders other than the hostile buyer to buy American Realty stock at half-price.
