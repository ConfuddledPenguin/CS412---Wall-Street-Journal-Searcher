Business Brief -- Famous Restaurants Inc.: Chief Drops Consideration Of a Leveraged Buy-Out

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/13/90

Famous Restaurants Inc. said its improved earnings and the troubled nature of the high-yield, high-risk "junk" bond market mean that its chairman, president and chief executive officer, Dennis B. Mullen, is no longer considering a leveraged buy-out of the Scottsdale, Ariz., operator of Mexican and seafood restaurants. In February, Mr. Mullen said he and possibly other management members were exploring the feasibility of a buy-out. Mr. Mullen said he hadn't made a specific offer for the company.

The company said preliminary results for the first 12 weeks of this year reflect earnings of $312,000, up sharply from $36,000 a year earlier. Revenue rose 30% to $23.7 million from $18.2 million. Earnings were buoyed by the acquisition last May of the Casa Lupita restaurant chain, divestiture of unprofitable restaurants, and the opening of other restaurants, Mr. Mullen said.

In national over-the-counter trading yesterday, Famous Restaurants shares closed at $1.50, down 6.25 cents.
