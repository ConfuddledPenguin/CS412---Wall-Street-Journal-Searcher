Initial State Jobless Claims Fell in Last Week of March

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/13/90

WASHINGTON -- Initial claims for regular state unemployment benefits fell to a seasonally adjusted 338,000 during the week ended March 31 from 342,000 the previous week, the Labor Department said. The number of people receiving regular state benefits in the week ended March 24 was a seasonally adjusted 2,313,000, or 2.2% of those covered by unemployment insurance, down from 2,388,000 the previous week, when the insured unemployment rate was 2.3%.

Counting all state and federal benefit programs, the number of people receiving unemployment benefits in the week ended March 24 fell to 2,755,200 from 2,919,100 a week earlier. These figures aren't seasonally adjusted.
