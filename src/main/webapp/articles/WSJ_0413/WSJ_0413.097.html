Exxon Faces U.S. Suit Over Small Oil Spills In New Jersey Waters

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/13/90

NEWARK, N.J. -- Exxon Corp. was sued by the U.S. government in federal court here, seeking penalties and interest from fines the oil giant failed to pay for four small oil spills in area waterways. The civil complaint isn't related to much-larger spills that occurred in the same area in recent months, the U.S. attorney said.

According to the U.S. attorney, Exxon's Exxon Co. U.S.A. unit, Linden, N.J., refused to pay a total $1,350 of fines the Coast Guard imposed from 1985 to 1987 because the oil company claims the minor spills didn't harm the environment. The spills occurred in the Kill Van Kull and Arthur Kill waterways, which separate New Jersey from Staten Island, N.Y.

Exxon couldn't be reached for comment. The New York company and its Exxon Shipping Co. unit recently pleaded innocent to five-count criminal indictments, charging them with violating various federal laws in the nation's worst oil spill a year ago. The tanker Exxon Valdez ran aground in March 1989, spilling nearly 11 million gallons of crude oil into Alaska's Prince William Sound.
