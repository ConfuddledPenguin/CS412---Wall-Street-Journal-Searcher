Bushes Paid Over $101,000 In `89 Taxes, Return Shows

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/13/90

WASHINGTON -- President and Mrs. Bush paid $101,382 in taxes for 1989, the president's new tax return indicates. Mr. Bush reported an adjusted gross income of $456,780 for the year, returns released by the White House indicate. That income includes $189,167 from his salary as president; $6,229 in salary for the time he spent as vice president in 1989 before his Jan. 20 inauguration; and $208,274 in income from the Bushes' private trust.

In addition, a net long-term capital gain of $36,068 from the blind trust was reported.

Mr. Bush has placed nearly all his personal holdings into the blind trust, so the sources of his investment income aren't disclosed to him nor listed in detail on the tax return.

The tax return also shows that Mr. Bush received $14,282 in royalties from his autobiography, "Looking Forward." White House spokesman Marlin Fitzwater said that Mr. Bush sent half the proceeds as a contribution to the United Negro College Fund and the other half to the M.D. Anderson Cancer Center in Houston.

Separately, Vice President Dan Quayle and his wife, Marilyn, released tax returns showing they had adjusted gross income of $133,696 for 1989, and paid federal income taxes of $25,479. The Quayles' income included $106,526 from his salary as vice president and $14,572 in dividend income from Central Newspapers Inc. stock.
