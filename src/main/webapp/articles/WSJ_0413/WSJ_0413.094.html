Marketing & Media: News Stories on China And California Quake Win Pulitzer Prizes

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/13/90

NEW YORK -- Coverage of political turmoil in China won a Pulitzer Prize in international reporting for two New York Times reporters, while two California newspapers were awarded prizes for their coverage of last year's earthquake. The China coverage award was to Nicholas D. Kristof and Sheryl WuDunn. The San Jose Mercury News, which used emergency generators to print its post-quake edition, won the prize for general news reporting. The (Oakland) Tribune photo staff won the award for spot news photography.

The prizes, recognizing outstanding work in journalism and the arts, are presented annually by Columbia University.

Four reporters from the Seattle Times received the national reporting award for coverage of the Exxon Valdez oil spill.

In the arts, playwright August Wilson won his second Pulitzer Prize for drama ("The Piano Lesson") and novelist Oscar Hijuelos was cited for fiction ("The Mambo Kings Play Songs of Love").

Other arts awards included:

History: Stanley Karnow, author of "In Our Image: America's Empire in the Philippines."

General nonfiction: "And Their Children After Them," by Dale Maharidge and Michael Williamson, of the Sacramento Bee newspaper, an examination of the families originally described in the 1941 book "Let Us Now Praise Famous Men" by James Agee and Walker Evans.

Biography: "Machiavelli in Hell," by Sebastian de Grazia.

Poetry:Charles Simick's "The World Doesn't End."

Music: Mel Powell, composer of "Duplicates: A Concerto for Two Pianos and Orchestra."

Other journalism awards included:

Public service gold medals: Philadelphia Inquirer, for reporting on the blood industry, and Washington (N.C.) Daily News, reporting on carcinogens in the city water supply.

Investigative reporting: Lou Kilzer and Chris Ison, Minneapolis-St. Paul Star Tribune, for stories on alleged profiteering from fires.

Explanatory journalism: David A. Vise and Steve Coll, the Washington Post, for stories examining the Securities and Exchange Commission.

Specialized reporting: Tamar Stieber, Albuquerque (N.M.) Journal, for stories linking a rare blood disorder to the dietary supplement L-tryptophan.

Feature writing: Dave Curtin, Colorado Springs (Colo.) Gazette Telegraph.

Commentary: sports columnist Jim Murray of the Los Angeles Times.

Criticism: Allan Temko, San Francisco Chronicle, for writing on architecture.

Editorial writing: Thomas J. Hylton, the Mercury (Pottstown, Pa.); Tom Toles, Buffalo (N.Y.) News, editorial cartooning.

Feature photography: David C. Turnley, Detroit Free Press, photographs of the uprisings in China and Eastern Europe.
