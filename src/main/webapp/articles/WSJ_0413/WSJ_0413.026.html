Norwest Is Expected To Post Record Net For the First Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/13/90

MINNEAPOLIS -- Norwest Corp. expects to report that first-quarter profit exceeded the previous record of $61.1 million, or 65 cents a share, reached in the 1989 fourth quarter, Lloyd P. Johnson, chairman and chief executive officer, said. In an interview here, Mr. Johnson also said that the bank holding company's nonperforming assets rose between $12 million and $15 million for the quarter from $145 million at year end. That figure was equal to about 1.1% of total loans, which compares favorably to most other big banks.

Mr. Johnson wouldn't elaborate on the first-quarter profit expectations. In the year-ago period, Norwest reported profit of $57 million, or 60 cents a share, adjusted for a 2-for-1 stock split paid last July.

Despite the continued profit growth and relatively low level of problem loans, Mr. Johnson said he is concerned about the weakness of real estate and other parts of the economy, and that Norwest's troubled assets could increase further during the year. He wouldn't make a projection, however.

The company expects to add further to its reserves for loan losses, which at year end were 164.3% of problem loans.

Mr. Johnson said Norwest has forgone profits -- in the hope of avoiding big losses -- in recent years by not making very large loans. While the company could conceivably lend a single customer, under federal regulations, $200 million or more, it has limited its exposure to any single customer to amounts far less than that.

Richard M. Kovacevich, president and chief operating officer, said that currently Norwest's biggest single loan is $65 million.

Norwest Mortgage, which has had losses in recent years, is expected to reach the corporatewide return-on-equity level of close to 19% soon, Mr. Johnson said. To do that, the mortgage unit must originate about $5 billion of mortgage loans this year. It originated $4.4 billion last year, when it had profit of $5.8 million, against a 1988 loss of $9.7 million.
