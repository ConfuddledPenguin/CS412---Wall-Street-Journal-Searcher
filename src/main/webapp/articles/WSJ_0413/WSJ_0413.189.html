Nissan Official Backs Fuji Heavy Industries In Its Bid for Subaru

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/13/90

NEW YORK -- Fuji Heavy Industries Inc. should press forward with its efforts to acquire all the shares of Subaru of America Inc. that it doesn't already own, a top executive of Nissan Motor Co. said. "I feel it is necessary for Fuji to have a wholly owned subsidiary in the U.S. instead of an independent distributor," said Atsushi Muramatsu, Nissan's executive vice president for finance. "I feel Fuji should go forward with this purchase."

Mr. Muramatsu's remarks are significant because Nissan is rapidly taking a greater role in the affairs of financially ailing Fuji. Fuji recently announced that Isamu Kawai, a former senior executive of Nissan, will become the new president of Fuji, pending expected shareholder approval in June. Nissan is Fuji's largest shareholder with a 5.9% stake. Fuji and Nissan both are part of the industrial group led by the Industrial Bank of Japan, which itself owns another 4.9% of Fuji.

Speculation that Nissan might ask Fuji to back away from its efforts to buy Subaru hammered Subaru's stock on Wednesday, when it dropped 87.5 cents a share to close at $5.625 in national over-the counter trading. Yesterday, however, the stock recovered to close at $6.125, up 50 cents.

In January, Fuji offered to pay $147 million, or $6 a share, for the 50.4% interest in Subaru of America that it doesn't already own. Subaru said it would form a special committee of non-Fuji directors to consider the offer, but since then nothing has happened. Subaru, it's widely believed, would like to draw a higher bid from Fuji.

Subaru's sales plunged 35% in the first quarter to 24,575 cars from 37,768. The company launched its new Legacy model with high hopes last year. But the Legacy competes directly against such popular cars as the Honda Accord and Toyota Camry.

Subaru of America officials couldn't be reached for comment.
