Business Brief -- Westwood One Inc.: Fiscal First-Quarter Deficit Worsened to $7.7 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/13/90

Westwood One Inc., Los Angeles, reported that its fiscal first-quarter loss widened sharply on a rise in revenue because of increased interest costs, increased investments in programming, and good-will amortization related to its acquisition of radio stations in New York and Los Angeles. The company, which recently said it is launching its fourth major radio network, reported a net loss of $7.7 million, or 53 cents a share, compared with a year-earlier net loss of $3.1 million, or 21 cents a share. Revenue for the quarter, ended Feb. 28, increased 20% to $31.8 million from $26.5 million a year earlier.

Because of the increased seasonality of its business, Westwood One said it expected a "significant portion" of its projected loss for the year to occur in the first quarter. "As we move into our second quarter, we expect to see increasing revenues and improved operating results for the balance of fiscal 1990 from the company's radio networks and station operations," said William Battison, president and chief financial officer.
