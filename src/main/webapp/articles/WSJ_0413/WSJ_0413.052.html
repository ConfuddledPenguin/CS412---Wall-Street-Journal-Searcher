Japan's Steel Output Rises

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/13/90

TOKYO -- Japan's steel production in March grew 2% from a year earlier to 9.16 million metric tons, the Japan Iron and Steel Federation said. This brought output in the year ended March 31 to 108.1 million metric tons, the fifth-highest level in history, up 2.3% from fiscal 1989. A metric ton is equal to about 2,204.62 pounds. Steel production exceeded the 100 million metric-ton level for the third consecutive year. The steel industry was supported strongly throughout the latest fiscal year by robust domestic demand as Japan's economy continued to expand, a federation official said.

Production in March also represented an 11% increase from February, when output fell 0.9% from a year earlier, the first year-to-year decline in 12 months. The quick recovery in March indicates the underlying trend toward higher production continues, the official said.
