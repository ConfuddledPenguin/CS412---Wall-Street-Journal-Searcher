Marriott Profit Fell 39% in First Quarter On 3.5% Gain in Sales

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/13/90

WASHINGTON -- Marriott Corp. reported first-quarter earnings dropped 39%, reflecting continued overcapacity in the lodging industry. Sales for the first quarter increased 3.5% to $1.68 billion, from $1.63 billion.

Net income for the quarter ended March 23 fell to $28 million, or 27 cents a share, compared with $46 million, or 41 cents a share, a year earlier. Results for the year-earlier quarter included profit from discontinued operations of $8 million, or seven cents a share, for two restaurant businesses that are being sold. That quarter also included $5 million, or five cents a share, from airline catering operations that were divested in December, and $8 million, or seven cents a share, in lodging transaction gains.

Excluding effects of discontinued operations and one-time gains, Marriott said profit from continuing operations increased 14% during the 1990 first quarter. Per-share earnings from continuing operations increased 23% in the latest quarter, the company said.

J. W. Marriott Jr., chairman and president, said the company expects to complete the sale of its Roy Rogers fast-food restaurant during the 1990 second quarter, and has received strong interest from potential buyers of its family restaurant operations.

Mr. Marriott said lodging operating profit was up 11%, excluding transaction gains in the year-earlier quarter. Occupancy for comparable hotels increased 2% to the upper 70% range. Contract services operating profit was up 19% in the first quarter, excluding gains from divested airline catering operations.
