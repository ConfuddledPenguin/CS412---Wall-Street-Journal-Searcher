Bush Physical Uncovers `Early' Case of Glaucoma

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/13/90

WASHINGTON -- President Bush has an "early" case of glaucoma in his left eye and will be treated with eye drops, White House physicians said after the president's annual physical. Glaucoma is an eye ailment in which there is increased pressure within the eyeball and a hardening of the eyeball. It can lead to gradual deterioration of sight and can result in blindness if not properly treated.

In a written statement, the White House said that "extensive visual testing" indicated that Mr. Bush hasn't suffered any loss of sight because of the glaucoma. He will begin taking eye drops to correct the condition and will take the medication permanently, the White House said.

Otherwise, the four-hour exam indicated that Mr. Bush is in "truly excellent health," Dr. Burton Lee, the White House physician, said in the statement.
