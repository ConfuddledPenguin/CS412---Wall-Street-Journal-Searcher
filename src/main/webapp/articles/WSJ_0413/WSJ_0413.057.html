Australia's Economic Indicator

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/13/90

SYDNEY, Australia -- A leading index of the Australian economy fell 1.4% in February, compared with January, signaling a continuation of the economy's slowdown. The index is compiled by Westpac Banking Corp. and Melbourne University's Institute of Applied Economic and Social Research. Their coincident index of the current level of economic activity also has started to decline, falling 0.6% in January from December.

A Westpac official said that the gradual slowdown was expected and that the index suggests this will be prolonged. The leading index has declined in seven of the past 10 months.

A preliminary calculation of a lagging index of the economy for January shows a 5% decline, confirming slower economic growth and suggesting that imbalances in the economy, caused by strong growth in the past two years, are being overcome.
