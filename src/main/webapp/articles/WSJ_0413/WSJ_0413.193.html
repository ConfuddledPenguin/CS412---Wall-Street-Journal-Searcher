Marketing & Media: Australia's Packer Drops Hostile Bid For Bond Media Ltd.

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/13/90

PERTH -- Australian financier Kerry Packer withdrew a hostile takeover bid for Bond Media Ltd., a national television station operator that is part of Alan Bond's beleaguered corporate empire. Mr. Packer sold the network to Bond in 1987 for around 1 billion Australian dollars (US$766 million). He still is owed A$200 million from that transaction. When it looked as if the cash-strapped Bond group couldn't repay the debt, Mr. Packer began a takeover bid for Bond Media that valued the company at just A$53 million.

Mr. Packer announced in December that he would offer one share in a new company, Television Corp. of Australia, for every 10 Bond Media shares. It valued Bond Media at 10 cents a share -- the stock currently is trading at about 13 cents a share -- and attracted few acceptances.

An underwriting agreement arranged by Packer for the bid expires Sunday. Packer started wind-up, or liquidation proceedings, on April 6 in the Western Australia state Supreme Court against Bond Media. The action will be heard May 2, with an application from Bond Media to have the petition dismissed.

Bond Media, meanwhile, is trying to arrange a capital injection from foreign television companies to reduce debt, and wants to restructure its finances.

Separately, Bond Corp. International Ltd., a Hong Kong unit of Mr. Bond's corporate group, said it concluded the sale of its interest in Compania de Telefonos de Chile S.A. for US$388.5 million.

The company announced in February a preliminary agreement to sell its 53% stake in Chile's national telephone company to T.I. Telefonica Internacional de Espana S.A. of Spain.

Bond Corp. is selling assets to reduce its total debt of almost A$7 billion.
