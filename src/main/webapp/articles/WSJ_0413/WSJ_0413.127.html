Business Brief -- Peugeot S.A.: Report of a 13% Increase In Group Profit Expected

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/13/90

Analysts expect Peugeot S.A., France's sole private-sector auto maker, to announce next week that its 1989 group profit climbed by roughly 13% to about 10 billion francs (about $1.78 billion) despite a debilitating strike last September and October that shaved about three billion francs from group revenue. Group profit totaled 8.85 billion francs in 1988. Peugeot announced last February that 1989 group revenue rose 11% to 152.96 billion francs from 138.45 billion francs in 1988.

Most Peugeot-watchers are bullish about the auto group's performance this year. Group revenue in 1990 is expected to increase to more than 160 billion francs, while group earnings are seen reaching 12 to 13 billion francs.

Peugeot is scheduled to release 1989 earnings figures Wednesday.

The strike at assembly plants in Sochaux and Mulhouse cut about 60,000 vehicles -- mostly from the 205 and 405 models -- from the production of main unit Automobiles Peugeot in 1989 and tainted an otherwise positive year for the company.
