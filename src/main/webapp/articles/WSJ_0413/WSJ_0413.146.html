Business Brief -- General Development Corp.: Troubled Florida Developer Lays Off 138 More Workers

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/13/90

General Development Corp., which recently filed for bankruptcy-law protection, said it laid off another 138 employees. The troubled Florida developer said 81 employees came from its Miami headquarters, while the remaining 57 were in field offices. They were essentially the support staff for the homesite-development division, which was closed earlier this week, the spokeswoman said. That closing put 239 employees, primarily in construction and engineering, out of work.

Last week, the company filed for protection under Chapter 11 of the U.S. Bankruptcy Code when it wasn't able to pay more than $10 million in property taxes due April 1. In recent months, General Development has laid off or fired about 1,600 other workers, or more than one quarter of its work force.

General Development's troubles stem from a grand jury investigation of its selling practices. Last month, the company pleaded guilty to a federal charge of conspiracy to defraud about 10,000 homebuyers by charging inflated prices based on improper appraisals. The company's former two top executives pleaded guilty to one count of conspiracy.
