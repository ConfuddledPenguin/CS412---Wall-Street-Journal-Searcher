South Korea Plans Bond Issue

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/13/90

SEOUL, South Korea -- South Korea's Export-Import Bank plans to float 105 million European currency units ($126.9 million) of Eurobonds in May or June, bank officials said, but declined to discuss terms for the issue, designed to secure funds to help finance South Korean exports. The issue is subject to approval by the Finance Ministry, which the officials said was expected soon.

The state-run Korea Development Bank also was reported to be preparing to float foreign currency-denominated bonds for a total of as much as $500 million in several issues in the second half. Officials said the bank hasn't worked out a schedule for the proposed issues. They didn't specify the countries in which the bonds will be issued. The National Assembly authorized the bonds to obtain lending funds to finance South Korean enterprises' investment in manufacturing production facilities.

The government recently eased curbs on issuing bonds overseas.
