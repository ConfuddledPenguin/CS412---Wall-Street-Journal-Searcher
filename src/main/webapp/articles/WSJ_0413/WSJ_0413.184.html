Lone Star Unit Is Sued By a Creditor Group

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/13/90

DALLAS -- Lone Star Technologies Inc. said the unsecured creditors committee of its Lone Star Steel Co. unit is seeking to recover $109.3 million from the parent on behalf of the subsidiary. Lone Star said the committee filed an amended complaint in federal court in Dallas seeking to recover expenditures made by the unit at the parent's direction and to recover amounts alleged to have been improperly transferred to the parent.

Previously, the committee sought to recover an intercompany receivable valued at a minimum of $23 million on behalf of the unit, which is operating under protection from creditors under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code.

In the lawsuit, the creditors group alleges that the parent orchestrated a series of transactions allowing it to use the steel company's funds for its own benefit. The group also seeks to recover assets it said were diverted from the steel company to Conroe Pipe Co. and to recover the $11.5 million that the parent received from the sale of Conroe facilities in December.

Lone Star Technologies said it will defend itself against the claims.
