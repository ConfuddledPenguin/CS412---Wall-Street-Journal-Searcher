Employee Retention Plan Of Campeau Units Cleared

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/13/90

CINCINNATI -- The judge overseeing the bankruptcy-court proceedings of Federated Department Stores Inc. and Allied Stores Corp. approved an employee retention plan designed to keep key managers from defecting from the retail companies. The plan will affect about 2,300 of Federated and Allied's approximately 101,000 employees. They will be offered employment contracts, termination agreements, enhanced severance pay and bonuses. Federated and Allied, which are units of Campeau Corp., filed for protection under Chapter 11 of the U.S. Bankruptcy Code Jan. 15.

The plan approved by U.S. bankruptcy court Judge J. Vincent Aug Jr. will cost the companies about $10.6 million in the first year and $11 million in the second year, according to documents filed in federal bankruptcy court here. In addition, $5 million a year will be available for discretionary bonuses. Heads of stores, merchandise managers and corporate vice presidents will be covered by the plan.
