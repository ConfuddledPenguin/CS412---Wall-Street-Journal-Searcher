National Intergroup Files 2 Suits Against Dissident Investors

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/13/90

PITTSBURGH -- National Intergroup Inc. filed two suits against dissident investors who have challenged it in a proxy fight. The first suit, filed in U.S. District Court here, asks that the investors, Centaur Partners and Walker Street Associates, be forced to divest their holdings in National Intergroup. Centaur holds 16.5% of NII; together, the two groups own about 25% of the drug distribution and steel concern, which is based here.

The suit alleges that the two groups have made materially false and misleading statements in their solicitation of proxies. The suit also claims that the two defendants may be acting together as a group without making required disclosures.

The second suit, filed in state court here, seeks a declaratory judgment that share holdings owned by Walker Street and its affiliates as well as Centaur's investment bankers should be attributed to Centaur. If the state court grants that judgment, National Intergroup said, it will initiate a rights offering that would decrease the percentage of its stock owned by the defendants.

Walker Street Associates had no immediate comment on the suit. Centaur Partners said it's not acting as a group with Walker Street or others. Centaur termed the suit an attempt by National Intergroup to "intimidate us."
