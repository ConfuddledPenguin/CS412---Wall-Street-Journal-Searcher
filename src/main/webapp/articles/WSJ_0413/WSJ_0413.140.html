Business Brief -- Alcan Aluminium Ltd.: Quarterly Net Dropped 36%; Aluminum Price Fall Cited

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/13/90

Alcan Aluminium Ltd., hit hard by falling aluminum prices, said in Montreal that net income for the first quarter dropped 36% to $172 million, or 74 cents a share, from $269 million, or $1.16 a share, a year earlier. Revenue was $2.20 billion compared with $2.31 billion.

The latest quarter includes a one-time nonoperating gain of $63 million, or 28 cents a share. The 1989 first quarter includes a one-time nonoperating gain of $30 million, or 13 cents a share. Excluding the gains, net income dropped 54% to $109 million, or 46 cents a share, from $239 million, or $1.03 a share.

The steep drop in earnings was in line with analysts' expectations.

Alcan said aluminum ingot prices in the latest quarter were down 11% from the fourth quarter of 1989 and 30% from the year-ago quarter. While order levels are improving in the U.S. market "it's still too early to change our cautious view of prospects for 1990," Alcan said.
