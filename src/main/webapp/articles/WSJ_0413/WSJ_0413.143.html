Business Brief -- Barnett Banks Inc.: First-Quarter Net Fell 75% After Addition to Reserves

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/13/90

Barnett Banks Inc., as expected after a big addition to its loan-loss reserves, said its first-quarter earnings fell 75% to $15.5 million, or 25 cents a share, from $62.4 million, or $1 a share, a year earlier. The latest results were hurt by a $66 million charge to increase to $317 million the Jacksonville, Fla., superregional bank holding company's reserves for potential losses from problem loans. Write-offs of problem loans increased 34% to $47 million from $35 million a year earlier, and, as expected, nonperforming assets -- including about $310 million in problem loans -- increased to $455.8 million from about $340 million. Nonperforming assets include foreclosed loans and loans for which interest payments are 90 days past due.

Though first-quarter profit was at the lower end of Barnett's target range, a spokesman said the company, with assets of $29 billion, had strong loan growth of 14% and a net interest margin that improved slightly to 4.76%.
