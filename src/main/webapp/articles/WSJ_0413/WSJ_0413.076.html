Thursday's Markets: Blue Chips, IBM Report Lift Stocks --- Prices of Bonds Ease During Short Session; Dollar Rises Slightly

  • Author: Douglas R. Sease
  • Date: 04/13/90

A big buy program and an upbeat earnings outlook for International Business Machines put some sparkle into an otherwise dull stock market, while bond prices eased in a shortened pre-holiday session. The Dow Jones Industrial Average gained 22.07 points to 2751.80. But the gains came on light volume and were concentrated in blue-chip issues. Broader market averages gained only modestly. For the week, the industrial average rose 34.68 points. The stock market is closed today.

Bond trading was muted too, with little to drive the market ahead of today's release of the March producer price index, a measure of wholesale inflation. The bond market was open only part of yesterday and is closed today for the Good Friday holiday. The dollar ended narrowly higher in late afternoon New York trading.

Analysts said a buy program early in the day boosted stock prices sharply. But trading activity quickly waned after the initial flurry, leaving prices stalled at the higher levels. Speculation that IBM's first-quarter profit, to be reported Monday, will be better than expected, helped sustain the higher prices, as did demand for blue-chip oil stocks. Smith Barney, Harris Upham raised its estimate of IBM's first-quarter results by 10 cents a share to $1.70 a share and reiterated a buy recommendation.

A report yesterday that retail sales in March fell 0.6% had little noticeable impact on the stock and bond markets, although economists had predicted sales would be flat. Similarly, economists are looking for the March producer price index to be about flat.

The dollar remained locked in a narrow trading range. Rumors that the Bank of Japan might push interest rates higher during the long weekend failed to depress the U.S. currency against the yen. Traders said central-bank intervention in the past week to support the yen against the dollar hasn't had much effect. But they said a return to normal trading volume next week will be necessary to determine the underlying strength of the dollar.

In major market action:

Stock prices climbed in light trading. Volume on the New York Stock Exchange totaled 142.5 million shares. Advancing issues on the Big Board were ahead of decliners 908 to 573.

Bond prices slipped. The benchmark 30-year Treasury fell less than a quarter of a point, or less than $2.50 per $1,000 face amount. The yield rose to 8.59%.

The dollar edged higher. In late New York trading, the currency was quoted at 1.6724 marks and 158.18 yen, vs. 1.6715 marks and 158 yen Wednesday.
