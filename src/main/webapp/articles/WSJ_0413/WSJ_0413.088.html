REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: France's Hostage Release

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/13/90

The crowds that cheer wildly when hostages are released are joyful that the hostages' ordeal is over. Everyone shares in that happiness. But France's dealings with Libya to gain this release raise questions. Let us quickly note that the French are not alone in having succumbed to the temptations of over-sophisticated diplomacy. Some U.S. State Department officials are so in love with their talks with the PLO they are spinning in circles trying to turn a blind eye to recent PLO terrorist activities. But the French are playing a dangerous game by engaging in subtle minuets with the likes of the Libyans, the Iranians, and the Iraqis. After the Mirage shipments, a meeting with Yassir Arafat in Paris, a friendly visit to Tehran and an offer to Iraq to help rebuild the nuclear reactor Israel blew up in 1981, France's allies have a right to wonder what all this subtle flirting is meant to achieve. In France, the U.S. State Department, and elsewhere, process is running policy. Those who try to cuddle with beasts often end up with their hands bitten off.
