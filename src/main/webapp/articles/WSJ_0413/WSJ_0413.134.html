Justice Department Urged To Monitor Meat Packers

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 04/13/90

The attorneys general of five Midwestern states urged the U.S. Justice Department to step up antitrust enforcement in the meatpacking industry. In a letter sent yesterday to U.S. Attorney General Dick Thornburgh, the state prosecutors cited statistics that they claim indicate dangerously high concentration of market share in the meatpacking industry. For example, the state attorneys general said that the four largest competitors in the beefpacking industry control about 70% of the fed-cattle market, up from 39% in 1980.

"For the past decade, antitrust has been the forgotten area of federal law enforcement," Minnesota's attorney general, Hubert Humphrey III, said in a statement accompanying release of the letter. Mr. Humphrey signed the letter along with his counterparts from Iowa, Montana, North Dakota and South Dakota.

A Justice Department spokesman said Mr. Thornburgh hadn't seen the letter, but that federal prosecutors have "looked at the meatpacking industry because of the concerns of meat producers about concentration in that market." The department's "antitrust division is aware of the situation and will continue to monitor it," the spokesman added.
