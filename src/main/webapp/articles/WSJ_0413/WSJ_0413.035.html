Carl Karcher Posts A Loss of $8.9 Million For Fiscal 4th Period

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/13/90

ANAHEIM, Calif. -- Carl Karcher Enterprises Inc. posted a net loss of $8.9 million, or 48 cents a share, for its fourth quarter, ended Jan. 29, after taking a $14.5 million charge for the planned sale or franchising of its fast-food restaurants in Arizona. In the year-earlier fourth quarter, Karcher had net income $4.2 million, or 23 cents a share. Revenue for the quarter rose 6% to $118 million from $111.3 million.

The company said it is considering disposing or franchising some or all of its 40 Arizona restaurants, which have had losses of about $5 million a year. "It has become apparent that the Arizona market does not make economic sense for corporate operations," said Donald F. Karcher, president and chief operating officer. "The future investment required would have been prohibitive in view of the expected return." Karcher and its licensees and franchisees operate 553 restaurants in California, Arizona, Nevada, Oregon and Japan.

In addition to the one-time charge, results for the latest fiscal year were hurt by generally weak conditions in the restaurant industry, including heavy discounting and use of coupons, plus inflation of food prices, Mr. Karcher said.

For the year, net income fell 73% to $5.6 million, or 30 cents a share, from $20.8 million, or 97 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue rose 15% to $511.3 million from $445.9 million. Prior-year per-share figures were adjusted to reflect a 2-for-1 stock split paid last July.
