Pinkerton's Is Sued By Guardsmark Inc. Over Personality Test

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/13/90

VAN NUYS, Calif. -- Pinkerton's Inc. said it was sued by competitor Guardsmark Inc., a Memphis, Tenn., private security services firm, alleging that Pinkerton's engaged in unfair and deceptive trade practices. The suit involves Pinkerton's use of a personality assessment test to screen security personnel. Guardsmark claims that it has licensed the test from University of Minnesota and that Pinkerton's has no such license. Pinkerton's is a security and investigation firm.

The suit seeks to enjoin Pinkerton's from using the test or advertising its testing program. It also seeks corrective advertising by Pinkerton's to notify customers and the public that Pinkerton's hasn't been authorized to use the test.

Gerard S. Brown, Pinkerton's executive vice president and general counsel, said, "We believe this lawsuit to be entirely without merit. Pinkerton subscribes to the highest standards in its advertising and psychological testing programs. We expect that the ultimate outcome of this litigation will vindicate our practices."
