PS New Mexico Told To Reduce Its Rates

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/13/90

SANTE FE, N.M. -- The New Mexico Public Service Commission said it ordered a $2.9 million decrease in rates for Public Service Co. of New Mexico. The commission said the action closes the book on three years of deliberations involving the investment by PS New Mexico in Palo Verde Nuclear Generating Station. This decision is the last of three orders that have been issued by the commission during the past year dealing with PS New Mexico's excess capacity and investment in Palo Verde.

Originally, PS New Mexico had requested a $13.7 million rate increase, which would have resulted in a 4.4% increase for residential customers.

Meanwhile, the commission authorized a $1.9 million rate increase for El Paso Electric Co.

After the decision, PS New Mexico warned its shareholders that they "will receive little or no return on their investment over the next several years." The company said it would evaluate "possible strategic options" and has hired an investment banking firm to assist.
