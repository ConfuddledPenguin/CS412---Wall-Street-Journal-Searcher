Ad Agencies Will Post Modest Gains In Net Income for the First Quarter

  • Author: Thomas R. King
  • Date: 04/13/90

Advertising agencies, focusing on cost control, are expected to report slightly higher first-quarter earnings, according to securities analysts. Analysts who track Madison Avenue say agencies are suffering from an environment in which advertisers continue to spend only modestly, reflecting inflationary fears. Ad agencies are being forced to live with low revenue growth and are concentrating on in-house cost-cutting to improve earnings.

"Assuming we don't see any structural upheaval in the industry, I think they're going to have a generally up quarter," said Alan Gottesman, an analyst at PaineWebber Inc., New York. He said agencies are closely monitoring personnel, a major cost that they can control by holding the line on new hiring.

Mr. Gottesman and other analysts note, however, that the first quarter is a minor one in terms of absolute profits. The first quarter, along with the third quarter, is traditionally weak. Advertising agencies are most profitable in the fourth quarter, when clients spend more money in anticipation of the holidays.

Mr. Gottesman is projecting first-quarter profit of Chicago-based Foote, Cone & Belding Communications Inc. at 30 cents a share, up from 24 cents a share a year earlier.

"Whatever earnings are eked out will be eked out because of cost control, not revenue growth," agreed James D. Dougherty, an analyst at County NatWest Securities, New York. Mr. Dougherty is projecting earnings for New York-based Omnicom Group at 37 cents a share, up from 33 cents a share a year earlier. Omnicom is the parent company of the BBDO Worldwide Inc. and DDB Needham Worldwide Inc. ad agencies.

Greg Ostroff, an analyst at Goldman, Sachs & Co., New York, estimates that earnings for Interpublic Group of Cos., parent of the McCann-Erickson Worldwide and Lintas:Worldwide agencies, will come in at 26 cents a share, up slightly from 24 cents in the year-earlier quarter. "The client companies haven't been increasing budgets as fast as they have been in the last few years," Mr. Ostroff said.

Some analysts are looking for earnings to improve as the year progresses.

Mr. Gottesman of PaineWebber said there's good news in that spending among auto makers looks to be strong, despite reports to the contrary. "They're talking about retrenching, but they're not doing it," he said. "There may be some problems on the local level, but the national ad data suggests that business seems to be holding up quite well. That's a plus."

Saatchi & Saatchi Co. and WPP Group PLC, two British firms, are major players in the advertising business that don't report quarterly earnings.
