Business Brief -- State Street Boston Corp.: First-Period Net Grew 14% For Bank Holding Company

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/13/90

State Street Boston Corp., bolstered by higher fee and net interest income, reported a 14% rise in first-quarter earnings. Net income in the quarter was $27.6 million, or 73 cents a primary share, compared with $24 million, or 66 cents a share, in 1989. Fully diluted earnings were 73 cents a share compared with 64 cents a share.

The Boston bank-holding company said its gains would have been even stronger without higher provisions for loan losses. Provisions in the quarter rose to $10.4 million from $3.6 million. State Street derives most of its income from fees from financial services such as back-office work for mutual funds.

Fee revenue rose 17% in the quarter to $118.9 million from $101.6 million. State Street attributed the gain in part to growth in international and mutual fund assets under management and expanded use of international asset-custody services. Foreign exchange trading revenue rose 21% to $7.5 million from $6.2 million.

Net interest revenue rose 11% to $53.6 million from $48.2 million.
