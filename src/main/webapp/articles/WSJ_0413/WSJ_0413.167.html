Dun & Bradstreet Gets Court Support in Rift With Unit's Ex-Chief

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/13/90

CAMBRIDGE, Mass. -- A state court here rejected a request by software company founder Frank H. Dodge to block Dun & Bradstreet Corp. and its McCormack & Dodge software unit from interfering with his efforts to start a new concern. In rejecting the request for a preliminary injunction, Justice Peter M. Lauriat of the Superior Court of Massachusetts said he wasn't persuaded that Mr. Dodge was likely to prove his claim of interference by Dun & Bradstreet. He added that he was unconvinced that Mr. Dodge would be irreparably harmed without a preliminary injunction.

In addition, Judge Lauriat said, a preliminary injunction of the scope Mr. Dodge was seeking would "impose a prior restraint on {Dun & Bradstreet's} protected speech."

Mr. Dodge had filed a lawsuit contending he was fired recently without cause by Dun & Bradstreet as president and chief executive officer of the McCormack & Dodge unit. Thus, the suit argued, he wasn't bound by an agreement that bars him from competing against the software company.

Mr. Dodge and a co-founder sold McCormack & Dodge to Dun & Bradstreet in 1983, and Mr. Dodge stayed on as president. Dun & Bradstreet answered that Mr. Dodge resigned and was therefore subject to the terms of the agreement.
