Continental Air's Traffic Falls

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/13/90

HOUSTON -- Continental Airlines, a unit of Texas Air Corp., said its March traffic declined 3.8% to 3.3 billion revenue passenger miles from 3.43 billion miles a year earlier. A revenue passenger mile is one paying passenger flown one mile. The carrier's March load factor, or the percentage of available seats occupied, decreased to 61.1% from 63.1% because the year-earlier month included Easter travel results that will occur in April this year.

For the first three months of this year, Continental's traffic rose less than 1% to 9.07 billion revenue passenger miles from 9.03 billion miles a year earlier. The load factor increased to 59.5% from 57.6%.
