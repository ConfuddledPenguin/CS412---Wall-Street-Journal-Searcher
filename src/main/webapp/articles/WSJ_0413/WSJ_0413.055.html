Japan's Corporate Bankruptcies

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/13/90

TOKYO -- Corporate bankruptcies in Japan stayed at low levels both in March and in the fiscal year ended March 31, a private credit research agency said, reflecting continuing strong business performances despite the simultaneous tumble of the yen and Japanese stock and bond prices since the beginning of this year. The agency, Teikoku Data Bank Ltd., said bankruptcies fell 36% in March from a year earlier to 503 cases, marking the 63rd consecutive month of year-to-year declines. The number of bankruptcies was up 13% from February, climbing above 500 cases for the first time in four months. But this increase is unique to March, Teikoku said, as most Japanese companies settle their accounts for the year on March 31.

The value of liabilities left behind by the failed companies totaled 73.2 billion yen ($463.4 million), down 35% from the previous year, and down 3.7% from the previous month. The number of businesses that went bankrupt with debts of more than one billion yen rose to 13 from 11 a year earlier and fell from 16 in February. The average value of liabilities was 145.5 million yen, slightly more than the year-earlier 142.9 million yen.

The number of companies that went bankrupt in fiscal 1990 totaled 6,653, down 29% from a year earlier. The total amount of liabilities declined 39% to 1.146 trillion yen. This was the first time in 17 years that the total cases fell below the 7,000 mark. The amount of total liabilities was also the lowest level in 16 years.
