Coloroll's McCall Unit Agrees to Be Acquired For $45 Million Total

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/13/90

NEW YORK -- Crowthers McCall Pattern Inc. said it reached a definitive agreement to be acquired by a Philadelphia investment partnership for $45 million. Although the agreement advances reorganization efforts by McCall, which has been in Chapter 11 bankruptcy-law proceedings since December 1988, the pact leaves open the question of how claims of creditors, who are owed $64.4 million, will be satisfied. The purchase agreement, with Dimeling & Schreiber's MP Holdings Inc., is for all of the assets of McCall, a maker of home-sewing patterns. McCall intends to use the proceeds to pay for its reorganization plan.

An attorney for McCall creditors said the creditors expect to make up the difference between the purchase price and the debt through a $60 million lawsuit that is pending against Reginald F. Lewis, an investor with a 20% equity stake in McCall. Mr. Lewis led a group that purchased McCall Pattern Co. in 1984 for $24.5 million. The group sold McCall for $63 million in June 1987 to an investor group controlled by John Crowther PLC, a British textile company that was taken over by Coloroll Group PLC.

In September, McCall sued Mr. Lewis and others, including Shearson Lehman Brothers Group Inc. and a unit of Bankers Trust New York Corp. McCall charged that Mr. Lewis's highly leveraged purchase and sale of the company triggered its bankruptcy-law filing. Mr. Lewis also is the subject of a pending suit by Travelers Insurance Co., a unit of Travelers Corp., seeking $35 million in damages from a senior note on which Crowthers McCall defaulted in 1988.

As part of the agreement with MP Holdings, members of McCall's management will acquire an equity stake, the size of which officials didn't disclose. Bankruptcy court has approved a breakup fee if McCall accepts a competing bid and gives MP Holdings the right to change its offer if McCall's receives competing bids. MP Holdings posted a $2 million deposit.
