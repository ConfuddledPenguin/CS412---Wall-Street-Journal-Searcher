Business Brief -- Union Bank of Switzerland: Investment Banking Unit For Buy-Outs Set in Italy

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/13/90

Union Bank of Switzerland, the country's largest commercial bank, said it plans to set up an investment bank in Milan, Italy, that will specialize in management and leveraged buy-outs. The value of the planned investment wasn't disclosed. Union Bank said the move will mark its first direct presence in the Italian market. It said it decided to establish a unit that will focus on buy-outs because the bank "sees interesting business possibilities, especially involving medium-sized and small companies." A spokeswoman said that a decision hadn't been made on when the unit will be established but that three investment banking specialists have been selected to set up the operation.

Union Bank also said its 20% stake in Banco Internazionale Lombarda of Milan won't be effected by the new investment banking unit, because the Italian institution isn't active in equity holdings, but concentrates instead on capital markets, asset management, foreign exchange, treasuries, and hedging instruments.
