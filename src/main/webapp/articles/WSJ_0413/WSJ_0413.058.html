Who's News: Kachmar Resigns Executive Positions At Wharf Resources

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/13/90

TORONTO -- John Kachmar resigned as president and chief executive officer of Wharf Resources Ltd. after Goldcorp Investments Ltd., a major shareholder, succeeded in a proxy battle in replacing five of the eight Wharf directors with its own nominees. The moves were expected following a similar shakeup a day earlier at Goldcorp's Dickenson Mines Ltd. subsidiary, which is Wharf's largest shareholder with a 36% stake. Goldcorp directly owns 5% of Wharf.

Mr. Kachmar, 53 years old, said at the annual meeting that his resignations from executive positions at both Wharf and Dickenson were "part of an orderly and normal process." He wouldn't elaborate. Wharf and Dickenson are gold mining concerns based in Toronto.

The Wharf board later elected Robert McEwen, Wharf's chairman, to the additional post of chief executive. Mr. McEwen also is chairman and chief executive of Dickenson and president of Goldcorp.

David Libby, previously Wharf's vice president, mining, assumed the new titles of president and chief operating officer. He holds similar positions at Dickenson.

Mr. McEwen led the proxy battle against a slate of directors nominated by Wharf's management. Mr. McEwen said the rival nominations were made to place more mining engineers on the Wharf board.

Mr. Kachmar and Wharf's general counsel, Barry Fisher, withdrew their own nominations to the board. Mr. Kachmar remains a director of Dickenson.
