Shareholders' Committee Is Named in Drexel Case

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/13/90

NEW YORK -- The U.S. Trustee in Manhattan appointed a shareholders' committee for Drexel Burnham Lambert Group Inc.'s bankruptcy-law proceedings. The committee is made up of eight former Drexel employees and a holder who was an original investor in one of the firm's predecessor companies, said Deborah Midanek, the committee's chairwoman. Ms. Midanek began organizing efforts to form an equity committee in February, shortly after Drexel filed under Chapter 11 of the U.S. Bankruptcy Code. The company has been selling its assets and plans to close all its operations. Most of the company's stock was owned by employees, who largely have lost their jobs since the bankruptcy filing.

Ms. Midanek, who now works as an entrepreneur, said the committee includes seven of Drexel's 100 largest shareholders.

The U.S. Trustee's office, the Justice Department branch that administers bankruptcy-court proceedings, began contacting shareholders last month to see who was interested in serving on the equity committee.

In Chapter 11 proceedings, a shareholders' committee consults with the debtor on how the company conducts its business and liquidation. Like the creditors' committee, it also takes part in negotiating a plan for the company to pay its debt and can seek the appointment of an examiner if it opposes the debtor's actions.
