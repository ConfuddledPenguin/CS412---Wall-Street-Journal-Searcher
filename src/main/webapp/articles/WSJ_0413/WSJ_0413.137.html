Development Banks Discuss Vast Potential of East Bloc

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/13/90

TOKYO -- Officials of West European and Japanese official development banks expect that Eastern Europe's economic changes will lead to faster growth in Western economies, a Dutch banking official said. He added that funding for Eastern European economic changes wasn't expected to crowd out other economic activities.

M.J.L. Jonkhart, president of Da Nationale Investeringsbank of the Netherlands, said Eastern European political and economic changes were one of the main topics discussed in a conference this week of Western European and Japanese institutions specializing in long-term credit.

Asked if funding of East European changes would crowd out some other economic activities, he said, "I don't think so . . . . The economic consequences are such that they have a productive influence. They will in principle to a large extent be self-financing."
