Money Fund Assets Fell in Latest Week

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/13/90

NEW YORK -- Assets of the nation's 508 money market funds fell $667.6 million, to $389.86 billion, in the week ended Wednesday, according to the Investment Company Institute, a Washington-based trade association. "Weekly changes in assets of particular categories of money market funds have been very small in recent weeks as yields on funds and other short-term instruments have also fluctuated within a very narrow range," said Jacob Dreyer, vice president and chief economist at the institute. "This stability of assets is especially noteworthy during the month of April when flows of individual tax payments and refunds would tend to impart volatility to money-market fund balances."

Assets of 246 general purpose funds rose $285.1 million, to $133.52 billion, while assets of 102 broker-dealer funds fell $706.5 million, to $165.08 billion. Assets of 160 institutional funds fell $246.2 million, to $91.27 billion.
