Census Bureau Admits Returns Lagging Badly

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/13/90

WASHINGTON -- The Census Bureau admitted that returns from the 1990 census are lagging badly, in part, because Americans are treating the forms like another piece of junk mail. Nearly two weeks after the April 1 target day, the bureau said only 57% of the 103 million households that were mailed census forms have returned them. The bureau is hoping for a minimum 70% return rate by mail. It plans to count the remaining households in a massive, and costly, household canvass this spring and summer.

Census Director Barbara Bryant said each 1% of the return rate that falls below the 70% goal will cost taxpayers an extra $10 million. The money will be spent to hire and pay extra door-to-door enumerators. At the same time, census officials concede that tens of thousands of Americans haven't received their 1990 population forms due partly to erroneous mailing lists.

Census officials were prepared for a poor response rate from low-income neighborhoods and areas with high immigrant populations. But Ms. Bryant expressed surprise at the number of affluent households ignoring their forms.

"We understand that over the last 10 years, the American people have been bombarded by direct and junk mail," Ms. Bryant said. She called the census form "one piece of mail that counts."

Population totals from the decennial census are used to allocate seats in the House of Representatives and state legislatures. They are also the benchmark for handing out billions of dollars in government grants.
