Hong Kong's Payroll Costs

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/13/90

HONG KONG -- The government said payroll expenses far outstripped inflation in December, providing fresh evidence of Hong Kong's wage spiral. Monthly wages in the manufacturing sector, the colony's largest, climbed 16% in December from a year earlier, or 5.3% after accounting for inflation. The increase was even more marked in the trading, restaurant and hotels field, climbing 17%, or 6.2% in real terms. Payroll costs in the financial services sector lagged behind, rising 13% in nominal terms and 3% after inflation.
