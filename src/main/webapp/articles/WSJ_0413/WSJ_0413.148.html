Business Brief -- Costain Group PLC: Sagging U.K. Housing Sales Pulled Profit Lower in 1989

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/13/90

Costain Group PLC, a British construction, civil engineering and mining concern, reported a 38% drop in pretax profit for 1989, reflecting a depressed housing market. Costain reported pretax profit for the year of #55.1 million ($90.7 million), down from #89.2 million the year before. The market had expected the company to post pretax profit of between #70 million and #75 million.

Sales rose 22% to #1.4 billion from #1.15 billion.

Costain said it decided to write down the value of its housing land in the United Kingdom by #20 million to "reflect the current very depressed conditions in this sector of our business."

Profit after tax and minority interests but before extraordinary items declined 31% to #40.4 million from #58.9 million.

The company said its engineering and construction units posted 50% increases in operating profit. In addition, Costain's mining and commercial property businesses reported record profit. But these gains were "offset by very poor results from U.K. housing," the company said.
