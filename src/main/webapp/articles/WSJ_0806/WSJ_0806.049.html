Coats Viyella Sells Some Assets to Clear Way for Tootal Bid

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/06/90

LONDON -- Coats Viyella PLC, a U.K. textile maker, said it satisfied regulatory conditions for a bid for Tootal Group PLC, another U.K. textile group. Coats Viyella said it sold its U.K. thread business to Amann & Soehne G.m.b.H., a West German textile group, for an undisclosed sum.

The Monopolies and Mergers Commission said in October that Coats could only take over Tootal if it disposed of all of its U.K. interests in domestic sewing thread supply and its shares in Gutermann Group.

In December, the friendly #395 million ($732.6 million) bid foundered following the breakdown of talks between the two companies. Tootal said at the time that the offer failed to reflect the company's strengths and prospects.

Russell Walls, finance director of Coats Viyella, said the company was now "free from any restrictions" to bid for Tootal. He added that no negotiations were taking place between the two companies.

Coats Viyella sold its stake in the Gutermann Group to the Gutermann family in May for an undisclosed sum.
