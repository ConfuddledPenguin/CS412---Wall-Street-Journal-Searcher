International Brief -- Cerus S.A.: French Firm Agrees to Sell Stake in Societe Generale

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/06/90

Cerus S.A. said that it has agreed to sell a 1% interest in Societe Generale de Belgique to Deutsche Bank AG in the first step toward unwinding its 15% stake in the Belgian industrial holding company. A spokeswoman for Cerus, the French holding company of Italian financier Carlo de Benedetti, declined to provide the price for the sale, but 1% of Generale de Belgique can be valued at about 2.09 billion Belgian francs ($63.9 million) on the basis of 3,270 Belgian francs a share.

As reported, Cerus built up its 15% interest during a foiled takeover bid for Generale de Belgique in 1988, but has repeatedly indicated that it wants to sell the stake when prices become favorable.
