South Korea Current Account

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/06/90

SEOUL -- South Korea posted a preliminary current-account surplus of $500 million for July, its first surplus in seven months. The Finance Ministry attributed the turnaround to a surplus in the visible-trade balance in July. The ministry estimated the merchandise trade surplus at more than $400 million on a free-on-board basis; the invisible-trade balance also showed a surplus, estimated at $80 million.

South Korea's current-account showed a deficit of $1.58 billion in the first six months of this year, compared with a surplus of $2.56 billion a year earlier.
