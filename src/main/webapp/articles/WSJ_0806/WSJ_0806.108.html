Business Brief -- ADT Ltd.: Canada's Laidlaw Inc. Raises Stake to 28.8%, From 27.7%

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/06/90

Laidlaw Inc. said it increased its holdings in ADT Ltd. to 28.8%, from 27.7%. The transaction, which has an indicated value of $34.1 million, brings Laidlaw's stake in ADT to about 284.6 million common shares.

The shares were acquired from Michael Ashcroft, a director of ADT. Laidlaw granted Mr. Ashcroft an option to repurchase the stock at the price the company paid for it, $3.10 a share, for a period ending Dec. 31, 1999 -- an agreement Laidlaw can terminate on six months' notice any time after Jan. 15, 1993.

A spokesman for Laidlaw said the Burlington, Ontario-based transportation and waste-services concern would continue to evaluate market and economic conditions before deciding to purchase additional stock in ADT.

Laidlaw earlier indicated it would be reluctant to increase its stake in ADT beyond 30% because of British takeover regulations that require a company acquiring more than a 30% holding to extend an offer to the rest of the company's holders.

ADT is a Bermuda-based security services and auctions concern. Laidlaw is 47%-controlled by Canadian Pacific Ltd., a Montreal transportation, resources and industrial holding concern.
