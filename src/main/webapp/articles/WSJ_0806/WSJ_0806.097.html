Business Brief -- Mesa Limited Partnership: Concern Reports Net Loss Widened in Second Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/06/90

Mesa Limited Partnership, Dallas, citing disappointing prices for natural gas, said its net loss widened in the second quarter to $50 million, or 49 cents a unit, from $13.4 million, or one cent a unit, a year earlier. T. Boone Pickens, the company's general partner, said Mesa expects a 1990 average gas price of $1.60 per 1,000 cubic feet this year. In the latest quarter, prices fell to $1.39 per 1,000 cubic feet from $1.56 a year earlier.

Revenue fell to $62.3 million from $83.3 million.

Results from the latest quarter included losses totaling $6.9 million from securities transactions and from the sale of oil and gas properties. The year-ago figure included $23.9 million in gains from similar transactions.

For the first half, Mesa reported a net loss of $68.2 million, or 79 cents a unit, on revenue of $158.8 million, against profit of $1.2 million, or 14 cents a unit, on revenue of $188.4 million, a year earlier.
