International: Barclay's Profit For Six Months Rose Marginally

  • Author: Tim Carrington
  • Date: 08/06/90

LONDON -- Barclays PLC posted sickly earnings Friday, a day after Midland Bank PLC announced "unacceptable results" and wholesale job cuts. While both banks were expected to show poor results, both did worse than expected. The British economy -- sapped by high inflation and sluggish growth -- is the main culprit. Corporations here, particularly small and middle-sized ones, are under financial stress. Both Midland and Barclays have sharply increased their provisions for bad corporate debts.

Barclays reported a half-year pretax profit of #602 million ($1.1 billion), up 2% from #590 million the year before. But its return on average total assets slumped to 0.9% from 1.0% in 1989.

Meanwhile, the bank said it plans to sell its mercantile credit operation in the United Kingdom. Midland is also selling a subsidiary, its finance house, Forward Trust Group Ltd.

Overall, Midland's picture is bleaker. Alan Broughton, an analyst with Morgan Stanley & Co., remarked, "Midland's figures were disastrous; Barclays' figures were disappointing."

Midland, which posted an anemic half-year pretax profit of #36 million, plans to cut about 4,000 employees by the end of 1991, a reflection of the strains.

Midland is selling Forward Trust to bolster its capital base, whereas Barclays is disposing of its mercantile credit operation for strategic reasons: It duplicates services offered by Barclays' retail banking unit. In addition to #234 million of bad-loan provisions for the U.K. portfolio, Midland is saddled with an overhang of Third World debt and big trading losses from 1989.

More recently, though, it is Britain's economic woes that are hammering away at the banking industry's profits. "In every case the banks reported worse results than the analysts were looking for," said Rodney Schwartz, banking analyst with Lehman Brothers International Inc. in London. He reasons that the British economy is worse than the analysts had assumed.

Barclays' U.K. banking and treasury operations showed a first-half profit of #275 million, down from #519 million in 1989. The bank took a #290 million charge for bad and doubtful debts, an increase of #244 million from last year. Analysts expect the banking industry's problems to persist. "We're still in an environment of high interest rates and high inflation," said Morgan Stanley's Mr. Broughton. "The number of insolvencies is increasing."
