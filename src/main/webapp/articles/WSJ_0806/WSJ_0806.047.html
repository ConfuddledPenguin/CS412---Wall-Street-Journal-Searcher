Duties Are Suggested For Certain Sweaters From South Korea

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/06/90

WASHINGTON -- The Commerce Department said anti-dumping duties ranging from 1.7% to 3.17% should be assessed on imports of synthetic-fiber sweaters from South Korea. Before the duties can be applied, however, the U.S. International Trade Commission must decide whether the imports, which totaled $476.1 million last year, have injured the domestic industry. Affirmative rulings by both agencies are required in anti-dumping cases.

The duties would offset the advantage of imports priced below the cost of production or below the price charged in South Korea's home market.

The Commerce Department delayed until Aug. 20 its determination on what anti-dumping duties should be imposed on sweater imports from Taiwan. Earlier, the department found that some Hong Kong sweater manufacturers also have violated the U.S. Anti-Dumping Act.

The ITC has scheduled a hearing for Thursday on sweater imports from all three major Asian suppliers.

The Commerce Department said the dumping margins on the imports from South Korea averaged 3.17% on man-made fiber sweaters -- including jackets, vests, cardigans and pullover garments -- manufactured by Hanil Synthetic Fiber Industrial Co. and smaller amounts for sweaters produced by other companies. These included 1.2% for Chunji Industrial Co., 1.11% for Shinwon Tongsan, 0.92% for Yurim Co., 0.73% for Young Woo & Co. and 1.3% for all other South Korean producers.
