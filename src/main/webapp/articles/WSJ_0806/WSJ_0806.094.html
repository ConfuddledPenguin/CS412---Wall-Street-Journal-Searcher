Newsprint Production By Mills in Canada Declined 3.4% in June

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/06/90

MONTREAL -- Canadian production of newsprint in June dropped 3.4% from a year earlier, as the industry operated at 95% of capacity. The Canadian Pulp and Paper Association, an industry group, said Canadian mills produced 762,000 metric tons of newsprint in June, compared with 789,000 a year earlier. A metric ton is equal to about 2,204.62 pounds.

In the first six months, output fell 1.9% to 4,733,000 metric tons from 4,826,000 a year earlier.

The June operating rate of 95% was down from 97% in May and unchanged from a year earlier.

June shipments fell less than 1% to 804,000 metric tons from 809,000 a year earlier. Shipments to the U.S. fell, and shipments to Canada and other markets rose. In the first half, shipments rose less than 1% to 4,738,000 metric tons from 4,705,000 a year earlier.

The association said U.S. consumer stocks at the end of June totaled 1,308,000 metric tons, a 41-day supply, up from 1,299,000 at the end of May, a 39-day supply, and up from 1,300,000, a 42-day supply, at the end of June 1989.

Canadian mills' stocks at the end of June totaled 316,000 metric tons, compared with 358,000 at the end of May and 372,000 at the end of June 1989.
