Canada's Leading Indicator Falls

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/06/90

OTTAWA -- Canada's composite leading indicator in May fell 0.4% from April, suggesting "the onset of a recession," Statistics Canada, a federal agency, said, citing weaker household demand. May marked the fourth consecutive month that the index declined. The agency also said Canada's gross domestic product, the total value of the nation's output of goods and services, declined 0.2% in May and 0.1% in April.

The 0.4% decline in the leading economic index in May followed declines of 0.4% in April, 0.2% in March and 0.1% in February.
