UNISYS Corp. (Blue Bell, Pa.) -- Standard & Poor's Corp. said it lowered its ratings on this computer maker's senior debt to triple-B-minus from triple-B, and subordinated debt to double-B-plus from triple-B-minus. The rating concern also downgraded the Unisys Finance unit's senior debt to triple-B-minus from triple-B. S&P confirmed its double-B-plus rating on the parent's preferred stock. A total of $5 billion in debt and preferred is affected. S&P cited sluggish demand and stiff competition in the mainframe business, and the difficulty of bringing other computer products to market. It said Unisys "faces a difficult transitional period" over the next two years, and raised the prospect of further rating drops if the company has a weak second half or doesn't reduce its debt.
