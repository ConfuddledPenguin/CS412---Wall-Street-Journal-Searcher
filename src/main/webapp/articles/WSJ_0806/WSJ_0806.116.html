Economy: Domestic Auto Sales in July Increased by 1% --- General Motors Corp. Posts 2.3% Rise During Period; Still Uncertainty Looms

  • Author: Neal Templin
  • Date: 08/06/90

DETROIT -- A surge in late-July sales by General Motors Corp., Honda Motor Co. and Toyota Motor Corp. pushed up domestic car and truck sales for the month by 1% over a year ago. For the final 10 days of July, sales of North American-made cars rose 9.5% to 395,803 vehicles. That translated to a seasonally adjusted annual selling pace of 7.7 million cars, up from a 7.4 million pace a year ago and 6.9 million rate in the middle 10 days of the month.

However, the late-July gains didn't dispel uncertainty about the auto industry's near-term future in the face of rising oil prices and a weakening U.S. economy. On Friday, the government said the nation's jobless rate rose to 5.5% in July, a hefty jump of 0.3 percentage points. And gas prices shot up as much as 15 cents a gallon at service stations in the wake of Iraq's invasion of Kuwait.

"The whole business world is very concerned," said analyst Scott Merlis of Morgan Stanley & Co. "And the auto world has to be a little more concerned than other sectors."

Indeed, Ford Motor Co., fighting a slump in sales of its cars, said it will idle the Dearborn, Mich., assembly plant that builds Mustangs for one week beginning Aug. 13.

But one car dealer said he's yet to see evidence that the Persian Gulf crisis has shoppers worried. "All I know is we had a showroom full of customers {Thursday night} and we sold 28 new vehicles," said Bill Curry, general sales manager of Art Moran Pontiac GMC in Southfield, Mich. "I didn't have one customer talk about the situation in Kuwait."

GM outpaced the overall market in July, posting a 2.3% increase in overall car and truck sales. In the last 10 days of the month, GM's total car and truck sales jumped 15.1%. Sales of GM cars rose 14% in the late July period. GM's share of the car market rose to 36.2% in the month from a weak 34.8% a year ago.

Dealers said much of GM's gain was at the lower-price end of the market.

"General Motors dealers are learning how to equip cars better on the low end," said John Bergstrom, who owns five GM dealerships in Wisconsin. "We just had them too expensive for a long time. We're learning if we don't put too much equipment on them and we keep the price down we can compete against the Japanese."

Meanwhile, Japanese auto makers continued to boost their share of the car market, improving on a record performance during the first half of the year. Japanese nameplate cars captured 29% of the car market in July, up from 26% in July of 1989. Toyota's overall vehicle sales surged 21.1% while Honda posted an 11.3% gain. However, Nissan Motor Co. had a 4.9% drop in overall sales during the latest month and some of the smaller Japanese companies also had poor results.

Ford, however, saw its share of the car market fall to 20.4% for the month, down from 23% a year ago. Chrysler's car market share dropped to 8.1% in July from 9.4% a year ago. Both Honda and Toyota sold more passenger cars than Chrysler in July.

Ford suffered an 8% drop in total car and truck sales for July. In the final 10 days of the month, sales of Ford cars tumbled 14.1%, more than offsetting an 11.7% rise in truck sales. The No. 2 car maker is being dragged down by the sluggish performance of its new Escort economy car. Ford sold 20,826 Escorts in July, a 35% drop from year-ago sales.

"We used to sell 125 Escorts a month and now we're selling 25 Escorts," says John Noble, general manager of Town & Country Ford in Charlotte, N.C. The problem, he says, is that many potential Escort buyers are having trouble getting loans for the higher priced 1991 models.

Chrysler Corp., which has been struggling since February, saw its total vehicle sales drop 15.1% in July. Chrysler's car and truck sales fell 9.5% in late July.

Corrections & Amplifications

MAZDA MOTOR Corp. sold 120,030 imported cars in the Jan. 1 to July 30 period. For the period, Mazda's total car sales were 163,170 vehicles and its combined car and truck sales were 214,079 vehicles. These statistics were misstated in the car sales table in yesterday's edition because of incorrect information provided by Mazda. A corrected market share table appears {in this issue}.
