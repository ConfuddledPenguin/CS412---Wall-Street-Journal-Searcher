Elco Expects to Post 30% Drop in Earnings For Fiscal 4th Period

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/06/90

ROCKFORD, Ill. -- Elco Industries Inc., hurt by continued softness in its auto-related business and start-up losses at a joint venture, expects to report profit fell 30% in the fourth quarter ended June 30. Jack W. Packard, chairman and chief executive of the maker of fasteners, said earnings fell to about $1.4 million, or 29 to 30 cents a share, from $2 million, or 43 cents a share, a year earlier. Sales rose slightly, to about $40 million from $39.4 million a year earlier.

Elco's consumer business was strong, Mr. Packard said, but failed to offset lower margins in its auto-parts business. Mr. Packard said losses at the Rocknel Fastener joint venture with Japan's Nagoya Screw Manufacturing Co. reduced fourth-quarter earnings about $300,000, or six cents a share. The joint venture was set up to supply U.S. factories of Japanese auto makers.

Mr. Packard said he expects the venture's sales to reach an annual rate of about $8 million by June 1991 and for it to show a profit by the fall of that year.

Elco's fourth-quarter estimates indicate earnings for the year of about $4.8 million, or $1.01 a share, down from $7.8 million, or $1.65 a share, a year earlier.
