What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/06/90

CORPORATE PROFITS fell 11% in the second quarter, a Wall Street Journal survey finds, the fourth decline in a row. The U.S., meantime, said the jobless rate rose to 5.5% in July, the highest in two years. Those developments, coupled with a sharp rise in oil prices following Iraq's invasion of Kuwait, suggest the economy could be heading into a recession. Faced with a weakening economy, the Fed may move as soon as this week to lower interest rates. ---

Gasoline prices leaped as much as 15 cents a gallon and are likely to go higher despite outcries from motorists. Other petroleum prices may rise sharply, as many countries said they won't buy oil from Iraq or Kuwait.

---

Stock prices tumbled, sending the Dow Jones industrials down 54.95 points. Long-term U.S. bonds again fell sharply. In Monday morning trading, Tokyo stocks plunged 3.7%.

---

U S West was criticized by a federal judge for withholding documents from a Justice Department investigation into the firm's sale of switching services to the federal government.

---

Blackstone Group is trying to put together an offer to buy financially troubled Greyhound Lines at the behest of the Amalgamated Transit union, which struck Greyhound in March.

---

New-car sales jumped 9.5% in late July, but the surge didn't dispel uncertainty about the near-term industry outlook in the face of rising oil prices and a weakening U.S. economy.

---

Bank of New England posted a second-quarter loss of $33.3 million, despite one-time gains totaling $91 million. The loss increases the odds that the bank will have to seek new capital and may be sold outright.

---

Allegheny International's board succumbed to pressure from banks, creditors and shareholders and agreed to a plan to turn the firm over to archrival Japonica Partners.

---

The Texas economy may get a boost from higher oil prices, but it appears unlikely the upturn will shrink the cost of the savings-and-loan bailout. Nearly half of the S&L real estate seized by the government is in Texas.

---

The Senate passed a bill to expand the SEC's ability to gather data on parents of brokerage firms and to allow the agency to close the stock market in times of extreme stress.

---

Lotus Development announced a cost-cutting program that includes a hiring freeze and layoffs. The software firm's earnings have slumped.

---

The White House nominated Richard Roberts, a former aide to Alabama Sen. Richard Shelby, to the Securities and Exchange Commission.

---

National Intergroup reported a $281.7 million loss for the June quarter as wholesale drug business gains failed to offset losses in other areas.

---

Oppenheimer will name managing director Edward C. Stanton III to head its mergers department in a bid to beef up its deal-making presence.
