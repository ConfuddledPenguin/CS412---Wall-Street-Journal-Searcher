Air Quality Agency Approves the Merger Of California Utilities

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/06/90

LOS ANGELES -- The South Coast Air Quality Management District approved the proposed merger between Southern California Edison Co. and San Diego Gas & Electric Co., thereby removing a major obstacle to the combination of the two utilities. Southern California Edison is a unit of SCEcorp. Rosemead, Calif. The merger was approved by SCEcorp. and San Diego Gas & Electric shareholders in April 1989. The $2.56 billion transaction is still awaiting approvals by the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission and the California Public Utilities Commission. In June, the Justice Department said it wouldn't oppose the proposed merger.

Environmentalists have opposed the merger, charging that it would worsen air pollution in the Southern California area. The South Coast Air Quality Management District is the air pollution control regulation agency for Los Angeles, Orange, Riverside, and San Bernardino counties. The district, which has the worst air quality of any metropolitan region in the U.S., has begun a plan to clean up the air to comply with federal clean-air standards.

The Air Quality Management District's approval of the merger requires Southern California Edison to more than offset pollution increases in the South Coast Air Basin. To accomplish this, the utility would underwrite the costs for other polluters to switch from internal combustion engines to electrical motors to run their equipment.

As part of the proposed merger, Southern California Edison would shift generation of electricity from older San Diego power plants to more efficient Edison plants in the South Coast Air Basin. Environmentalists and others had charged that this shift would interfere with plans to clean up the South Coast Air Basin's polluted air.
