Sallie Mae to Price $600 Million of Notes Due February 1991

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/06/90

WASHINGTON -- The Student Loan Marketing Association, or Sallie Mae, said it will price $600 million in short-term floating-rate notes due Feb. 14, 1991. Settlement of the notes will occur Thursday. When-issued trading is expected to begin at 8:20 a.m. Tuesday.

The notes will be priced at a spread to the bond equivalent yield of the 91-day U.S. Treasury bill.

The interest rates on the notes will be reset on the day following each weekly Treasury bill auction.

The notes will be offered in the U.S. domestic market and the Eurodollar market through selling groups managed by Sallie Mae.

The notes will be issued in book-entry form only in denominations of $1,000 and in $1,000 increments.
