Perini Is Suspending Payout as Cash Flow Fell in 2nd Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/06/90

FRAMINGHAM, Mass. -- Perini Investment Properties Inc. said it suspended its dividend and announced a decline in second-quarter cash flow to three cents a share from 23 cents in the year-ago period. The real-estate investment trust blamed the decline on a drop in distributions from joint ventures, which it called "largely a timing item," increased interest payments in the quarter and a decline in cash flow from certain operating properties.

The company said it expects these conditions to affect operating cash flow for the remainder of the year. But it said that certain planned transactions "could significantly improve" results.

In a statement, the company said it elected to suspend the dividend in keeping with the board's policy of paying out 50% to 60% of current cash flow. But the statement said the board reaffirmed its belief that the net current per-share value of the company's assets is "substantially in excess" of the current share price.
