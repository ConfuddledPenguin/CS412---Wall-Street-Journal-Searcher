Jardine Subsidiary to Sell Asset to Ex-Unit of Bond

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 08/06/90

HONG KONG -- In one of the richest building sales since the local property market slump began more than a year ago, Hongkong Land Holdings Ltd. has agreed to sell a 44-story office tower to a former subsidiary of Bond Corp. Holdings Ltd. for 1.72 billion Hong Kong dollars (US$221 million). The bluechip landlord, which controls much of Hong Kong's prime office space, purchased the building's site at the colony's first land auction in 1841 through the antecedents of its parent, Jardine Matheson Holdings Ltd.

Hongkong Land's managing director, Alasdair Morrison, said the sale of the World Trade Centre in the Causeway Bay shopping district is part of the company's strategy to concentrate on properties in Hong Kong's core business district and pursue other investment opportunities. While it has indicated intentions to expand in Thailand, he said the group doesn't have any specific projects in mind.

Analysts said Hongkong Land is likely initially to use a portion of the cash to pay off a portion of its debt, estimated around HK$6 billion, and may apply part to buy back its sharply undervalued shares in the market, once such a move is permitted probably later this year by local regulators.
