The Mideast Conflict: Canada Arranges to Secure Kuwaiti Assets at Its Banks

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/06/90

OTTAWA -- Canadian External Affairs Minister Joe Clark said the Canadian government has reached an agreement with the Canadian Bankers' Association to prevent Kuwaiti money from being withdrawn from Canadian financial institutions. Mr. Clark said Canada doesn't have the same legal basis as other countries, including Britian and the U.S., to freeze foreign assets. But the move, he said, will add up to a "de facto freezing of Kuwaiti assets in Canadian institutions, whether those institutions are in Canada or elsewhere."

Members of the Canadian Bankers' Association include Canada's commercial banks and other financial institutions.

Mr. Clark said he couldn't estimate the size of Kuwaiti holdings in Canadian financial institutions. He said the measure wasn't applied to Iraqi holdings because there aren't "significant amounts of Iraqi assets" in Canada.
