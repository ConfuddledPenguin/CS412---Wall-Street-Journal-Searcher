Eastern Pilot Union Clears Strike; Move Lacks Teeth

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/06/90

MIAMI -- The Eastern Airlines pilots union said its members authorized a strike if they don't reach agreement on a new contract, but any such job action will have little effect on the carrier. Most of the union pilots still aren't flying for the airline. After being nearly grounded by a strike and subsequent bankruptcy-law filing last year, Eastern refilled its cockpits with 1,800 replacement pilots and pilots who crossed the picket lines. By the time the Air Line Pilots Association abandoned its job action last November, the airline said it had all the pilots it needed and refused to reinstate the former strikers.

Eastern has said repeatedly that any pilots strike would be a "non-event." Martin R. Shugrue Jr., the court-appointed trustee who is running Eastern, said, "It's hard to understand how pilots who are not working can go on strike."

Eastern's 3,600 pilots initially went on strike in sympathy with the airline's machinists. The current dispute centers on the contract for pilots, in which they have said they are willing to forfeit wage increases in favor of getting their old jobs back.

The pilots union said contract talks are scheduled to resume this week, supervised by the National Mediation Board, the federal agency that oversees airline labor.

Federal mediators last month declared the two sides at an impasse, and began a 30-day countdown that expires at midnight EDT Aug. 10, after which the airline could impose its own contract and the pilots would be free to strike.
