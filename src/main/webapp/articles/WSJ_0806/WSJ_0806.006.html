Business Brief -- Huntway Partners L.P.: Second-Quarter Distribution Cut 71% to 10 Cents a Unit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/06/90

Huntway Partners L.P., Valencia, Calif., slashed by 71% its second-quarter distribution to its preference unitholders to 10 cents a unit, from 34.5 cents a unit. Huntway, an owner and operator of three refineries that produce liquid asphalt for road construction and repair, said the distribution will be paid Aug. 29 to unitholders of record Aug. 14.

The partnership said it expects the reduced distribution to be paid in future quarters until cash flow improves, reflecting an expected deterioration in operating margins because of lower liquid asphalt prices and "rapidly rising crude oil costs."

Funds available for unitholder distributions also will be depressed by "significant bank principal payments and capital expenditures" in coming quarters, Huntway said. "Management believes that the reduction of Huntway's outstanding indebtedness and the upgrading of its facilities are the appropriate actions" for the partnership's long-term health.

Huntway also reported second-quarter net income of $3.4 million, or 29 cents a unit, compared with a restated net loss in the year-earlier quarter of $475,000, or four cents a unit. Revenue in the latest quarter rose 24% to $29.9 million from $24.1 million.

However, operating results fell short of forecasted levels due to lower-than-expected sales volume in Southern California, Huntway said. The partnership said that it "does not anticipate that the operating performance of the second quarter is sustainable" for the rest of the year.

Huntway said it anticipates increased demand for liquid asphalt late this year, reflecting the implementation last week of a five-cent-a-gallon increase in the California gas tax. The increased revenue is earmarked for state transportation spending, including construction and maintenance of roads and highways.
