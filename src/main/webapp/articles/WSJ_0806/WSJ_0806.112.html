Business Brief -- Grubb & Ellis Co.: Spending Cutback Is Slated For Rest of 1990 and 1991

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/06/90

Grubb & Ellis Co. said it was trimming planned expense outlays by $7 million for the rest of 1990 and $11.5 million next year, reflecting the soft national real estate market. The real estate services concern said in San Francisco that the cuts amount to about 8% of budgeted expenses for the 1990 second half and a 6.4% reduction for next year. Its plans include the closure or trimming of nine offices, elimination of 75 staff positions and "major reductions in travel and advertising."

"The real estate services industry is in the midst of a difficult economic period and we need to adapt to changing market conditions," said Harold A. Ellis Jr., chairman and chief executive officer. He said the firm was taking the steps "in response to reductions in revenue expectations due to softness in real estate markets nationwide."

For the second quarter, Grubb & Ellis reported a net loss of $1.8 million, or 11 cents a share, compared with profit of $340,000, or two cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue declined to $84.6 million from $91.7 million.
