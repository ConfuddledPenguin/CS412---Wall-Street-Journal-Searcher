Integrated Resources, Krupp Set New Pact For Partnership Sales

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/06/90

NEW YORK -- Integrated Resources Inc. reached a revised agreement to sell interests in four investment partnerships to Krupp Cos., in an apparent setback to CRI Inc., which has sought control of the partnerships by another route. Integrated is a former real-estate syndicator and financial-services firm that has been operating under bankruptcy court protection since February.

Krupp is a Boston-based investment firm that manages more than $2 billion in investments, of which more than $1 billion are mortgage funds similar to the Integrated investments.

CRI is a real-estate syndicator based in Rockville, Md.

The agreement in principle between Integrated and Krupp, for as much as $45 million in cash and notes, would give Krupp the general-partner interests in the partnerships, known as American Insured Mortgage Investors. It eliminates a previous condition, that CRI's competing bid be dropped. CRI wants to gain control of the partnerships through a proposed offer for the partnership units.

At the same time, Integrated obtained a second court order blocking CRI from proceeding with two lawsuits against it until Aug. 13. CRI's proposed offer is outside the court-approved auction of the partnership interests. Integrated has been seeking to sell assets to satisfy creditors, who are owed a total of about $1.8 billion.

"A significant concern of many of the {AIM} investors was the uncertainty of finding a qualified investment manager to replace Integrated, and that matter is now resolved," said Laurence Gerber, president of Krupp.

U.S. Bankruptcy Court Judge Howard C. Buschman III, who is overseeing Integrated's reorganization proceedings here, also agreed to consider on Aug. 13 whether to approve Integrated's revised agreement to sell to Krupp. But he denied a request by Integrated to stop CRI from proceeding with its exchange offer for the AIM units, which is pending before the Securities and Exchange Commission.

Under the revised terms, Krupp agreed to pay Integrated $28 million in cash, a $10.6 million interest-bearing promissory note at closing and $6.5 million a year after the closing. The previous Krupp offer was for $38.6 million in cash and the $6.5 million a year after closing.

CRI couldn't be reached for comment.
