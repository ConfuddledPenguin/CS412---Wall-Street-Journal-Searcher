The Mideast Conflict: Northwest Air's Prices Rise To Offset Higher Fuel Costs

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/06/90

ST. PAUL, Minn. -- Northwest Airlines said it will raise ticket prices for domestic flights $8 each way starting Thursday to offset rising jet-fuel prices, prompted by the Iraqi invasion of Kuwait. But other carriers said only that they were studying the issue. Airlines generally don't raise fares across the board, if a major competitor balks.

The move by the Northwest Airlines unit of NWA Inc. comes during a disappointing travel season that has prompted many airlines to offer unusual discounts. Northwest stressed that its fuel surcharge isn't a "permanent revision" to its pricing structure and will be reviewed periodically.

The airline said the surcharge is necessary because its fuel prices have risen eight cents a gallon since Wednesday and are expected to increase further. Fuel is an airline's second-largest expense after labor costs.
