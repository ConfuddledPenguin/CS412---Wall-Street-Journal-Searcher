International Brief -- Waterford Wedgewood PLC: Annual Profit to Be Hurt By Strike Earlier This Year

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/06/90

Waterford Wedgewood PLC Chairman Howard Kilroy said a 14-week strike at the Anglo-Irish glass and dinnerware maker's crystal division, which ended in July, will hurt 1990 earnings, but he didn't make any specific forecasts. In a letter to shareholders, he said, "The strike has been extremely damaging to us, both in financial and human terms," though new work rules should result in "significant cost savings" in the future; the crystal division's plant was shut in the April strike, when workers refused to implement a cost-cutting plan. "The heavy costs associated with the strike will clearly have a seriously negative effect on our 1990 results," he said, "but it is obviously premature for me to make any forecasts of a financial nature at this time."

Mr. Kilroy also said supply difficulties and a weak dollar "were working seriously against us." The company depends heavily on the U.S. market.
