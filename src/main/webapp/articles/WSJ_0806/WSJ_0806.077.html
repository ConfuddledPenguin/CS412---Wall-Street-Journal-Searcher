New Securities Issues

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/06/90

The following were among Friday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report. MORTGAGE

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $500 million issue of Remic mortgage securities, offered in 17 classes by Salomon Brothers Inc. The offering, Series 1990-105, is backed by Fannie Mae 9 1/2% securities with a weighted average coupon rate of 9.5% and weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29.5 years. The floating-rate bond, class F, is at an initial rate of 80 basis points above one-month Libor, with an 11% cap.

EUROBONDS

Alliance & Leicester Building Society (U.K.) -- 75 million pounds of 13 1/4% Eurobonds due Oct. 4, 1999, priced at 101.40 to yield 13.35% after full fees, via JP Morgan Securities Ltd. Fees 2.

State Bank of New South Wales (Australia) -- 50 million New Zealand dollars of 14% Eurobonds due Sept.7, 1993, priced at 102 to yield 13.78% less fees, via Fay Richwhite (U.K.) Ltd. Guaranteed by the government of New South Wales. Fees 1 1/2.

ASLK-CGER Ifico (Belgium) -- $85 million of floating-rate notes due in August 2000, priced at par, via Sumitomo Finance International Ltd. Coupon will float 42.5 basis points above the six-month London Interbank Offered Rate. Callable from the fifth year on every coupon payment date at par. Sinking fund that will begin retiring 20% of the issue each year from the sixth year. Guaranteed on a subordinated basis by ASLK-CGER Bank.

Dainichiseika Colour & Chemical Mfg. Co. (Japan) -- 100 million Swiss francs of privately placed convertible notes due Sept. 30, 1995, with a fixed 3 1/2% coupon at par, via Credit Suisse. Put option on March 31, 1993 at a fixed 107.625 to yield 6.26%. Each 50,000 Swiss franc note convertible from Sept. 11, 1990 to Sept. 22, 1995. Final terms fixed Aug. 9.

Atsugi Nylon Industrial Co. (Japan) -- 190 million Swiss francs of privately placed convertible notes due Nov. 30, 1995, with a fixed 3 1/2% coupon at par, via Swiss Bank Corp. Put option on May 31, 1993 at a fixed 108.25 to yield 6.28%. Each 50,000 Swiss franc note is convertible from Sept. 17, 1990 to Nov. 17, 1995 at a premium of approximately 2 1/2% over the closing share price Aug. 9, when terms are fixed.
