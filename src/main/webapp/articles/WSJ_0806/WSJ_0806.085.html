Who's News: Star States Corp. Says 2 Top Officials Resign

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/06/90

WILMINGTON, Del. -- Star States Corp. said J. Walton St. Clair Jr., its 60-year-old chairman and chief executive officer, and Thomas K. Kerstetter, 39, president and chief operating officer, resigned. The company gave no reason for the moves.

C.G. Cheleden, a director, was elected interim chairman and a board committee was appointed to find a new chief executive. Mr. Kerstetter agreed to remain at the company to assume the chief executive's responsibilities during the transition period, the company said.

Star States also said it has decided against constructing a new headquarters building in Wilmington, resulting in a write-off of $8.8 million. The company posted a second-quarter loss of $26.6 million, or $5.61 a share, compared with profit of $610,000, or 13 cents a share, a year earlier.

Star States is the parent company of Wilmington Savings Fund Society, FSB, Fidelity Federal Savings & Loan Association, and several other leasing and financial services companies.
