Technology: Sun Microsystems to Unveil Least Costly Color Model

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/25/90

MOUNTAINVIEW, Calif. -- Sun Microsystems Inc., turning up the heat on its competitors in the workstation business, is set to unveil today its least expensive color computer. Aimed at engineers and scientists, the workstation will cost about $8,000, industry observers said. It will run at speeds faster than Sun's new $5,000 SCL computer but slower than its $9,000 Sparcstation 1.

Sun also is expected to disclose plans to sell some of its products through personal-computer retailers for the first time. The company initially has tapped three retailers for the task, Tempe, Ariz.-based MicroAge Inc., Exton, Pa.-based Intelligent Electronics Inc., and New York-based Nynex Corp.'s retail arm.

The move represents a continuation of Sun's efforts to expand its distribution. It now sells its products directly or through specialists in the sale of high-end systems.

"Sun has a large-enough installed base that many customers really just want to expand their networks by adding more machines," said Seymour Merrin, a computer retail analyst in Palo Alto, Calif. He said personal-computer retailers should be able to handle those customers, giving Sun "a place on store shelves that will be hard for competitors to take away."
