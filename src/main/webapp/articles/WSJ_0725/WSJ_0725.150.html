Business Brief -- Northern Telecom Ltd.: Company Posts 27% Increase In Second-Quarter Earnings

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/25/90

Northern Telecom Ltd., buoyed by lower costs and strong orders, posted a 27% rise in second-quarter net income and a 12% increase in revenues. Net income at the Canadian telecommunications equipment manufacturer was $95.7 million or 37 cents per share compared with $75.3 million or 29 cents per share in last year's second period. Revenues in the quarter were $1.7 billion versus $1.52 billion last year.

In the first six months, revenues rose 13% to $3.27 billion from $2.9 billion in last year's first half. Net income rose 37% to $174.8 million or 67 cents per share from $127.9 million or 49 cents per share last year.

"I'm reasonably pleased by our results," said chairman Paul G. Stern. "The growth performance was across the board in all our markets, U.S., Canada and overseas. And we benefited from lower interest expenses and our cash is strong."

Order input for the quarter was $1.71 billion, a 7.5% increase over the $1.59 billion recorded in last year's period. Order backlog for the period was $1.85 billion, a 9% increase from last year's $1.7 billion.

Order input and backlog growth failed to match the 16% growth in last year's second quarter. But Mr. Stern said, "We have not seen a slowdown. Orders tend to fluctuate from quarter to quarter. . . We're going to have a respectable year, barring a disaster, which we don't see."
