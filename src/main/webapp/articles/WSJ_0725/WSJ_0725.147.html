Sears Roebuck 2nd Period Net Declined 39%

  • Author: Francine Schwadel
  • Date: 07/25/90

CHICAGO -- Sears, Roebuck & Co. said second-quarter profit fell 39% because of higher insurance claims and lower results at its core U.S. retailing business. The company said it earned $237.9 million, or 69 cents a share, compared with $391.2 million, or $1.11 a share, a year ago. Earnings from continuing operations in last year's second quarter were $334.9 million, or 95 cents a share.

Revenue in the period rose 4% to $13.85 billion from $13.28 billion a year ago.

The results were slightly better than the grim projection the company issued earlier this month, which prompted analysts to lower their forecasts. Consequently, the results had little impact on Sears stock. In composite trading Tuesday on the New York Stock Exchange, Sears closed at $33.75, up 12.5 cents.

Still, some analysts found cause for concern. Expenses at the Sears merchandise group jumped almost 10% because of what analysts described as higher advertising costs and increased reserves for uncollectible credit card balances, but revenue rose only 2%. "This is the second quarter in a row where expenses have been a problem," said Wayne Hood of Prudential-Bache Securities Inc. "They have to demonstrate that they can drive better revenue growth during the back-to-school season."

Sears said merchandise group earnings, which include international and Sears credit card results as well as its core U.S. retail business, fell 32% to $110 million from $161.6 million. But profit at the U.S. retail operation plunged more precipitously -- by 78% to $17.3 million from $78.4 million a year earlier.

Profit at the company's Allstate insurance unit fell 29% to $142.2 million from $199.8 million because of underwriting losses, including claims from recent fires in California.

Earnings at the Dean Witter financial services unit rose 31% to $58.2 million from $44.5 million, reflecting better Discover credit card results.

Earnings at the Coldwell Banker real estate unit fell to $7.3 million from $46.4 million a year ago. Last year's results included $56.3 million in earnings from discontinued operations.

For the first half, Sears said its net income declined 47% to $344.2 million, or $1.00 a share, from $648.9 million, or $1.81 a share, a year earlier. Revenue rose 4% to $26.46 billion from $25.44 billion.
