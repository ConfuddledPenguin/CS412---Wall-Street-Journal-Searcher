Business Brief -- Consolidated Edison Co.: Utility's 2nd-Period Profit Fell 25% to $49.1 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/25/90

Consolidated Edison Co. reported second-quarter net income sank 25%, reflecting higher operating expenses and interest charges and lower earnings from investments. The New York-based utility's profit fell to $49.1 million, or 18 cents a share, from $65.7 million, or 24 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue rose 3% to $1.26 billion from $1.22 billion.

Operating expense rose 4.4% to $1.16 billion, reflecting particularly a 15% jump in the costs of fuel Con Ed uses and of power it buys from outside sources. Investment earnings dropped 38% to $15.6 million, while net interest charges rose 8.7% to $61.6 million.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Con Ed's shares closed unchanged at $23 a share.
