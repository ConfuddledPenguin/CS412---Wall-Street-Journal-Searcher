GM, Consortium to Build 1,000 Natural Gas Trucks

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/25/90

LOS ANGELES -- General Motors Corp. said it plans to build a fleet of 1,000 low-polluting natural-gas-powered pickup trucks next year in a joint effort with a consortium of 10 California, Texas and Colorado utilities. General Motors' GMC Truck division will produce the natural gas trucks and sell them through selected GMC dealers, with particular emphasis on fleet buyers in California and Texas, a company spokesman said.

Consortium members plan to use the vehicles in their work fleets. For example, San Francisco-based Pacific Gas & Electric Co. intends to buy 30 to 50 of the vehicles at an expected cost of $15,000 each. PG&E also will sell the gas for the trucks for about 57 cents a "compressed therm" -- equivalent to about a gallon of gasoline -- at a pumping station in Concord, Calif., and 13 others set to open this year.

PAS Inc. of Troy, Mich., will develop and install the fuel system.

The consortium is providing $935,000 to help cover the costs of design and production of the vehicles.

Other members of the energy consortium include: San Diego Gas & Electric Co., San Diego; Southern California Gas Co., Los Angeles; El Paso Natural Gas Co, El Paso; Energas Co., Midland, Texas; Enron Corp. and Entex, both of Houston; Lone Star Gas Co., Dallas; Southern Union Gas Co., Austin, Texas, and Natural Fuels Corp., Denver.
