International Brief -- Sapporo Breweries Ltd.: Unconsolidated Pretax Profit For First Half Rose 2.3%

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/25/90

Sapporo Breweries Ltd., Japan's second-largest beer brewer, reported that its unconsolidated pretax earnings for the first half rose 2.3% to 5.69 billion yen ($38.4 million) from 5.56 billion yen a year earlier. Net income climbed 18% to 2.83 billion yen, or 8.48 yen a share, from 2.41 billion yen, or 7.21 yen a share, as Sapporo's extraordinary losses tumbled 31% to 640 million yen and extraordinary gains totaled 31 million yen from sales of securities. In the year-earlier period, there were no such gains; extraordinary losses stemmed from disposal of some fixed assets and the dismantling of a unit.

First-half sales rose 2.5% to 220.29 billion yen from 214.99 billion yen.

Sapporo officials said demand for beer in the domestic market expanded about 8% from a year earlier, as a result of new products. Beer sales, by far Sapporo's largest line of business, rose 4% to 202.19 billion yen. Sales of soft drinks slipped 5% to 9.71 billion yen, partly because of slumping demand for carbonated soft drinks.
