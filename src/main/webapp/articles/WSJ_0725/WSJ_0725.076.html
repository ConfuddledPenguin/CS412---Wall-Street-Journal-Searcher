ITT Net Jumps 63% In Quarter, Aided By Sale of Alcatel Stake

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/25/90

NEW YORK -- ITT Corp. reported second-quarter earnings of $397 million, or $3 a share, a 63% increase over the year-earlier figure of $244 million, or $1.70 a share. Net income for the quarter included an after-tax gain of $139 million, or $1.07 a share, on the June sale of 7% of Alcatel N.V. to Compagnie Generale d'Electricite in Paris. ITT retains a 30% stake in Alcatel N.V., a Paris-based telecommunications firm.

"The reason ITT outperformed most of its peers is its international exposure. It generates 50% of its operating profit outside of the United States," said Theresa Gusman, an analyst at Salomon Bros. in New York.

For the first six months, ITT earned $650 million, or $4.88 a share, compared with $444 million, or $3.10 a share, for the first half of 1989.

Excluding the after-tax gain from Alcatel and a first-quarter after-tax gain of $47 million from the sale of 2 million shares of Compagnie Generale d'Electricite, ITT earnings were $3.45 a share for the first six months.

The company said operating results at the ITT Hartford unit rose as a result of higher investment income and improved life insurance operations, offsetting the continued depression in North American casualty underwriting. Income at ITT's defense segment declined during the quarter because of reduced volume in some contracts and a strike at one unit.
