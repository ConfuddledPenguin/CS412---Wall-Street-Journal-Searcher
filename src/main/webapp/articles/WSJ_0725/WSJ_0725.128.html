Who's News: Arco Shifts Managers, Taps Environmentalist For Added Board Seat

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/25/90

LOS ANGELES -- Atlantic Richfield Co. made several management changes and elected a new director of the oil, gas and mineral concern. Named senior vice presidents were Harrell E. Bilhartz Jr., E. Kent Damon Jr., Anthony G. Fernandes and William C. Rusnack.

Mr. Bilhartz, previously president of Arco Coal Co., became president of Arco Alaska Inc., succeeding William E. Wade Jr., a senior vice president, who became president of Arco Oil & Gas Co. Mr. Wade succeeded James A. Middleton, who became head of West Coast petroleum operations.

Mr. Damon, previously vice president and investment officer, became senior vice president, planning and control.

Mr. Fernandes, previously vice president and controller, succeeded Mr. Bilhartz as president of Arco Coal.

Mr. Rusnack, previously vice president, corporate planning, became president of Arco Transportation Co., succeeding Harold C. Heinze, who resigned to pursue other interests, Arco said.

Also, Steven J. Shapiro, assistant treasurer, was elected vice president, corporate planning, succeeding Mr. Rusnack. Marie L. Knowles, vice president, financial, control and planning, at Arco International Oil & Gas Co., succeeded Mr. Fernandes as vice president and controller.

Frank D. Boren was elected a director, increasing board membership to 16. Mr. Boren is a lawyer, a real estate developer and former president of the Nature Conservancy. He is the first professional environmentalist named to the board, Arco said.
