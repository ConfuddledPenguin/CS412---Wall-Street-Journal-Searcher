International Brief -- Oakbridge Ltd.: Elders Lifts Bid for Firm To Match Competing Offer

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/25/90

Elders Resources NZFP Ltd. raised its bid for Oakbridge Ltd. to 88 Australian cents (69 U.S. cents) a share, matching an offer made Monday. Australian coal miner McIlwraith McEacharn Ltd. is leading a consortium with Toyo Menka Kaisha Ltd. and Nippon Oil Ltd., both of Japan, in a bid for control of Oakbridge, another Australian coal company.

Elders is a New Zealand-based natural-resources company.

The McIlwraith consortium is trying to pressure Elders to sell it the 85% of Oakbridge that Elders holds.

Elders said it raised its bid to 88 cents a share from 86 cents. That offer will be raised to 90 cents if Elders wins 90% of Oakbridge.

John Cornelius, Elders secretary, said in a statement to the Australian Stock Exchange that the McIlwraith bid is "highly presumptuous." He added: "We are buyers of Oakbridge, not sellers. Elders Resources has made no decision to sell Oakbridge or any other assets for that matter."
