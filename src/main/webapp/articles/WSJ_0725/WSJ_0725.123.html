Who's News: Kellogg's Buckland Leaves European Post To Head British Firm

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/25/90

BATTLE CREEK, Mich. -- Kellogg Co. said Ross Buckland resigned as director of its European operations to head another food concern. Mr. Buckland, 47 years old, was responsible for Kellogg's lines in Europe, its largest overseas market with nearly $1 billion in sales, since 1985. He also was a corporate vice president and chairman of Kellogg Co. of Great Britain.

The company said he was leaving to become chairman and chief executive officer of Unigate PLC of Britain, a milk, meat and other food-products company. A successor at Kellogg wasn't named.

Kellogg also said Carlos M. Gutierrez, 36, was named executive vice president, sales and marketing, at its U.S. Food Products division, succeeding William H. Weintraub, who was named vice president and assistant to the chairman. Mr. Weintraub had held the marketing post since last September.

Mr. Gutierrez, who also was named a corporate vice president, will retain responsibility for product development. He joined the company in 1975.
