Business Brief -- Ryder System Inc.: Transport-Services Company Posts Flat 2nd-Period Profit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/25/90

Ryder System Inc. posted nearly flat earnings for its second quarter, breaking a yearlong string of steep declines in profit and signaling that the company might be poised for a turnaround. Ryder reported second-quarter net income of $32.1 million, or 39 cents a share, compared with $32.4 million, or 40 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue inched up to $1.31 billion from $1.29 billion.

M. Anthony Burns, chief executive officer of the Miami-based transportation-services concern, said Ryder expects to show a solid profit in the third quarter, compared with a net loss of $27.6 million, or 38 cents a share, in the year-earlier period, including a $57 million after-tax charge.

The second-quarter results, analysts said, offered evidence that a seven-point "profit improvement plan" initiated by the company last year is starting to pay off.

In line with its plan to slash costs, Ryder has sold eight businesses in the past year and a half, Mr. Burns said. Further divestitures are possible.
