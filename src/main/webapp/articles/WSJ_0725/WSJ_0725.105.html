Business Brief -- Computer Associates: Company Reports 66% Drop In First-Quarter Earnings

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/25/90

Computer Associates International Inc. said earnings for its first quarter ended June 30 plummeted 66% on a 16% decline in revenue. The results were roughly in line with expectations, revised July 12 when the Garden City, N.Y., software concern said that revenue would fall significantly and that earnings would probably decline.

In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange yesterday, Computer Associates shares closed at $9.125, up 50 cents, but still 45% lower than their July 11 closing price of $16.50.

When Computer Associates announced its expected revenue decline, Wall Street analysts said they weren't convinced by the firm's main explanation -- that the sales force spent too much time explaining a new software strategy called CA '90s and not enough time selling software.

But Anthony Wang, president, reiterated that explanation, calling the decline "a near-term disappointment."

Net income fell to $3.6 million, or two cents a share, from $10.7 million, or six cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue fell to $255.1 million from $303.2 million.
