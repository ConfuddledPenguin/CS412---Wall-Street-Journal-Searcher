Business Brief -- Allied-Signal Inc.: Second-Quarter Profit Fell By 27% at Technology Firm

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/25/90

Allied-Signal Inc. said second-quarter earnings fell 27%, primarily because of losses in its Brazilian automotive operations and a one-time gain a year earlier. The Morristown, N.J., advanced technology company, with aerospace, automotive-product and engineered-material businesses, reported earnings fell to $121 million, or 88 cents a share, from $166 million, or $1.11 a share, a year earlier. The year-earlier results included a gain of $23 million, or 16 cents a share, from an insurance settlement for Union Texas Petroleum Holdings, of which Allied-Signal owns 40%. Sales were about flat at $3.2 billion.

The company said automotive operations were "hit hard" by a two-month production interruption in Brazil, caused by the government's anti-inflation measures. It also cited continued weakness in the U.S. car and truck market for after-market products.

In the first half, net fell 14% to $250 million, or $1.78 a share, from $292 million, or $1.96 a share. Sales rose to $6.2 billion from $6.1 billion.
