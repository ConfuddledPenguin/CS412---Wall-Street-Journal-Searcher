Business Brief -- Heritage Entertainment Inc.: Movie, TV Production Firm Projects `Significant Losses'

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/25/90

Heritage Entertainment Inc. said it expects to post "significant losses" for the fiscal year ended March 31, although it didn't quantify them. Heritage also said it has received a proposal from an entity it didn't identify to provide "substantial equity capital" primarily to expand Heritage's theater chain. Heritage didn't disclose the size of the potential investment.

The company said it is also talking with several other parties regarding possible debt or equity financing, "and is exploring other alternatives" through an independent committee of its board.

The Los Angeles movie and television production company said its losses relate primarily to the re-evaluation of certain unamortized film costs and an increase in its expense to cover bad debt.

In fiscal 1989, Heritage had a net loss of $5.3 million, or 99 cents a share, on revenue of $6 million. The company said results for fiscal 1990 won't be completely comparable because it didn't acquire the Landmark and Seven Gables theater chains until last June. For the first nine months of fiscal 1990, the company had a net loss of $1.2 million, or 17 cents a share, on revenue of $18.2 million.
