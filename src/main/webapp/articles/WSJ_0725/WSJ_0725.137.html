Business Brief -- Ivaco Inc.: Canadian Steelmaker to Sell 2 Units to BTR of Britain

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/25/90

Ivaco Inc., a Montreal-based steelmaker, said it agreed to sell two wholly owned subsidiaries to a unit of BTR PLC, a British holding company, in a transaction that would reduce Ivaco's long-term debt by about 125 million Canadian dollars (US$108.2 million). Ivaco declined to disclose terms of the transaction, but reports from London said BTR agreed to pay US$112 million. The two Ivaco subsidiaries operate plants in the U.S. and Canada that make fabrics used in the paper-making industry.

The transaction, which requires approval by U.S. and Canadian regulators, would result in a pretax gain of about C$30 million, Ivaco said. Ivaco had 1989 profit of C$12.9 million, after a C$19.8 million one-time charge and a C$4.3 million loss on discontinued operations. On Dec. 31, it had long-term debt of C$550.6 million.
