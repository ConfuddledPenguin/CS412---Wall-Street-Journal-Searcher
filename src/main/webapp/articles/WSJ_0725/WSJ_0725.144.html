Merger Cleared for Sovran And Citizens & Southern

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/25/90

ATLANTA -- Citizens & Southern Corp. and Sovran Financial Corp. said their plans to merge were approved by the Federal Reserve Board. Moreover, the two bank holding companies said they decided to abandon the name they had chosen -- Avantor Financial Corp. -- in favor of the more prosaic C&S/Sovran Corp.

The companies began using Avantor when they announced plans for the merger in September. But since then, they have heard from "our customers, employees, communities and shareholders," said Albert B. Gornto, Sovran's chairman and chief executive officer. "Their input," he said in a prepared statement, "reinforced the value of retaining and incorporating into our new name the names under which we have historically operated so successfully."

Bennett A. Brown, Citizens & Southern's chairman and chief executive, who will hold both those posts in the new company, said he expects the merger to be completed by Aug. 31, pending a 30-day review by the Justice Department. Citizens & Southern is based in Atlanta; Sovran is in Norfolk, Va.

With $50 billion in assets and more than 1,000 banking offices in seven states and Washington, D.C., C&S/ Sovran will be the 12th largest bank holding company in the nation.
