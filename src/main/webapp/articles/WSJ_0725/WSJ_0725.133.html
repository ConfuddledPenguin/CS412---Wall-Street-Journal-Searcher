Merrill Stops Selling Dollar-Denominated Japan Warrant Issues

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 07/25/90

LONDON -- Merrill Lynch International Ltd. has stopped making a market in U.S. dollar-denominated Japanese equity-warrant issues, a spokesman for the firm said. "We were unable to make a sufficient return on our capital as the market is suffering from excess capacity and increased competition," said the spokesman for the Merrill Lynch & Co. unit.

About 30 staff members have been laid off, including traders, salespeople and administrative employees. Merrill has a staff of about 2,100 across Europe, with more than 1,400 based in London.

The firm entered the business in November 1988, and was a market maker in 250 to 300 issues, representing roughly three-quarters of all liquid issues oustanding.

Merrill will continue to make a market in Swiss-franc denominated equity-warrant bond issues by Japanese companies. It is a market maker in about 150 Swiss franc issues, the official said.

The official added that Merrill will continue to be involved in customer agency business and will also continue to trade in U.S. dollar equity-warrant deals in connection with its arbitrage and derivative activity.

The cuts are part of Merrill's current business rationalization process. According to the spokesman, the firm now intends to concentrate on the equity derivatives business.
