From an article by P.J. O'Rourke in the July 12-16 Rolling Stone:

I've been writing about the American-political process for some two years now. All that time I've been looking for a straightforward issue. But everything I've investigated -- election campaigns, drug abuse, savings and loan regulation, social security, housing, foreign policy, the environment -- has turned out to be a lot more complicated than I expected. There were always angles I hadn't considered, aspects I hadn't weighed, complexities I'd never dreamed of. Until now. Until I got to the 1990 Farm Bill. Here at last is a simple problem with a simple solution. Drag the thing behind the barn and kill it with an ax.
