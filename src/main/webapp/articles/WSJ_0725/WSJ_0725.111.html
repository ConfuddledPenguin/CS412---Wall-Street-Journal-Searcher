Business Brief -- Honeywell Inc.: Defense and Marine Systems Spinoff Is Closer for Holders

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/25/90

Honeywell Inc., inching closer to a spinoff of its defense and marine systems business, said holders would receive one share of the new company for every four shares of Honeywell common owned. The Minneapolis-based electronic controls and components concern said that while directors aren't expected to formally approve the spinoff until late this quarter, the proposed company would be called Alliant Techsystems Inc. And it would seek a listing on the New York Stock Exchange.

Honeywell also announced a senior management slate for Alliant Techsystems, led by Toby G. Warson, formerly chief executive officer of Honeywell's U.K. unit. Last year, the defense and marine systems business reported sales of $1.1 billion.

Honeywell said it still is awaiting a ruling from the Internal Revenue Service that a spinoff would be tax-free to its holders.

Spinning off its weapons business, at a time of anticipated decline in government military spending, is part of a Honeywell strategy to increase shareholder value.
