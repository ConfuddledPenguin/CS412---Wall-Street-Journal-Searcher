REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial): Albania South?

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/25/90

The temperature's rising in Fidel Castro's Cuba. On Friday, a young couple with an infant burst into the Spanish Embassy compound. "They were yelling and screaming hysterically, things like `We can't go on living in this country,'" a Spanish diplomat said. Nine more Cubans entered the Spanish Embassy on Sunday, bringing the total there to 18. Other Cubans are holed up in the Swiss, Czech and Italian missions. The last time we saw this in Cuba it ended up in the mass exodus known as "Mariel." On April 5, 1980, 12 Cubans commandeered a city bus and crashed it into the Peruvian Embassy in Havana, killing a guard. Once inside they demanded asylum. By April 8, more than 10,000 Cubans had squeezed into the Peruvian Embassy. Fidel opened the floodgates, also sending thousands of jail and mental-institution inmates to Miami with the genuine dissidents.

This time Fidel is determined to stem the flow before it gets out of hand. Cuba's deputy foreign minister announced Tuesday that none of the 25 Cubans sheltering at the Havana embassies would be allowed to leave Cuba. Fidel is already hard pressed by dwindling assistance from East European economies, and he may have become upset by a new round of embassy refugees. Most recently in Albania, and before that in Erich Honecker's East Germany.
