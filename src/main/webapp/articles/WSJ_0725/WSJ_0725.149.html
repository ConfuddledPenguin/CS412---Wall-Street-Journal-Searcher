International: Alan Bond Fails to Win Extension on Stock Sale

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/25/90

LONDON -- The four major shareholders in British Satellite Broadcasting Ltd. (BSB) refused troubled Australian businessman Alan Bond any further extensions to sell his stake in the television company, the shareholders said yesterday. Granada Group PLC, Pearson PLC, Reed International PLC and Chargeurs S.A., of France, said the latest extension ran out July 19 for Mr. Bond's company, Bond Corp. Holdings Ltd.

The effect of the expiration is to lower Mr. Bond's stake in the second satellite television network for the U.K. to 7.4% from an original 37%, said BSB.

The major shareholders had given Bond a number of extensions on time limits in which to sell his stake without it being diluted after a #450 million ($821.9 million) rights issue in January. If the deadline had been met, the buyer could have taken up a position in rights issues that would have maintained the higher stake in BSB.

Now analysts suggest Mr. Bond's stake is worth about #30 million at market value, against his total original investment of #155 million.

A number of potential buyers of the Bond stake had been mentioned by analysts. Robert Maxwell, chairman and chief executive of the media conglomerate Maxwell Communication Corp., was considered the favorite, despite a potential conflict in cross-media ownership.

Mr. Maxwell wasn't immediately available for comment.
