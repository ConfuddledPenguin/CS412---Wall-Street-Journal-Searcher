Alcan Aluminum Sets Tentative Labor Pact After a Strike Begins

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/25/90

KITIMAT, British Columbia -- Alcan Aluminium Ltd. and the Canadian Association of Smelter and Allied Workers reached a tentative three-year contract yesterday, hours after about 1,600 workers struck the company's smelter and related power plant near here. Union members are scheduled to vote on the proposed pact early today and could be back to work immediately, averting a halt of output at the smelter. The plant, which at capacity produces about 260,000 metric tons of aluminum annually, is temporarily being operated by management personnel, an Alcan spokesman said. Montreal-based Alcan has significant aluminum operations in the Quebec province as well as in British Columbia.

The new pact would replace a two-year contract that expired at midnight PDT Monday. The proposed accord provides for annual wage increases of about 6% in each year of the pact, plus other increases, including immediate signing bonus payments, a union official said.

The average hourly wage at the smelter under the previous pact was $19.48 Canadian dollars (US$16.86), Alcan's spokesman said.
