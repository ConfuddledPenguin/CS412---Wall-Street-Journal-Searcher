Who's News: Howard Savings Taps First Fidelity's Tuggle As Chief Executive

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/25/90

LIVINGSTON, N.J. -- Howard Savings Bank named William P. Tuggle III chairman and chief executive officer, succeeding Donald F. McCormick, who resigned in January after the bank posted a wider-than-expected fourth-period loss of $85.3 million, or $5.94 a share. The 57-year-old Mr. Tuggle currently is president and chief executive officer of First Fidelity Bank of New Jersey and senior executive vice president and a director of its parent, First Fidelity Bancorp., Lawrenceville, N.J.

Leo J. Rogers Jr., 54, who continues as Howard's president, had acted as chief executive while the bank sought a new top officer.

Anthony Terracciano, 51-year-old chairman and chief executive at First Fidelity Bancorp., will assume Mr. Tuggle's responsibilities on an interim basis. "Bill will be missed at First Fidelity, but we wish him all good fortune with the challenge that lies before him," Mr. Terracciano said.

Howard had $4.9 billion in assets at June 30. Like many banks and thrifts, the bank's non-performing loans -- those not paying interest for 90 days -- have grown as real-estate markets have softened in the Northeast.

Monday, Howard reported a second-quarter net loss of $15.7 million, or $1.09 a share, after adding $25 million to its loan-loss reserves. Non-performing loans climbed 25% from March 31 to $503 million at June 30.

In national over-the-counter trading yesterday, Howard shares fell 25 cents to $3.875.
