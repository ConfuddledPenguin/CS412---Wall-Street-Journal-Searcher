Tuesday's Markets: Stocks Rise, Reclaiming Part of Loss --- Oil Stocks Deliver Fuel to Shares; Bonds Ease, Dollar Mixed

  • Author: Christopher Winans
  • Date: 07/25/90

NEW YORK -- Again the stock market was a killing field for companies delivering bad profit news, but buoyant oil stocks pushed many stock-price barometers higher. The Dow Jones Industrial Average gained 17.82 to close at 2922.52 in active trading, regaining less than a third of Monday's 56-point loss.

Bond prices eased in light trading, with little economic news to guide traders. For much the same reason, the dollar ended mixed.

Investors pounded shares of companies with negative earnings news, which were spread randomly across most industry groups. Dow Chemical, Morgan Stanley and Capital Cities/ABC all fell more than 5% because of profit disappointments.

While investors continue to be unforgiving on poor earnings, said Joseph DeMarco, head of equity trading at Marinvest, "people are still looking for places to go to. Today it was energy."

Oil stocks indeed fueled the market's afternoon advance, said Christopher Pedersen, director of trading at Twenty-First Securities. "Without the oils, the Dow did nothing."Chevron, Exxon and Texaco -- all in the Dow average -- rose sharply on renewed prospect of armed conflict in the Mideast.

The most interesting aspect of yesterday's stock action was the ease with which traders took Monday's volatility in stride. "The professionals are getting used to knowing how to handle these one-day blips," Mr. Pedersen said.

The bond market was affected by selling left over from Monday, as traders unwound positions developed in response to the stock market decline. Roberta D. Olson, fixed-income portfolio manager at Banc One Asset Management Corp., said, "Too many people were wounded. They are sitting back and assessing the situation. It's completely related to the stock market."

Bond investors had been focusing on inflation, what the Federal Reserve was saying, and the prospect of more bonds coming to market; then stocks suddenly took center stage. "It has forced a lot of people to reassess what fundamental issues we should be looking at now," Ms. Olson said. In major market action:

Stock prices rose on volume of 182 million shares in New York Stock Exchange trading, with declining issues edging advancers, 755 to 753.

Bond prices fell 3/8 of a point, or $3.75 for each $1,000 face amount. The yield on the Treasury's benchmark 30-year bond rose to 8.58%.

The dollar was mixed. In late New York trading, the currency was quoted at 1.6215 marks and 148.78 yen, compared with 1.6220 marks and 148.20 yen Monday.
