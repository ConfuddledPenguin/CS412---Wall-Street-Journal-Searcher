Business Brief -- Alberta Government Telephones: Telephone Utility to Sell 50% of Novatel to Bosch

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/25/90

Alberta Government Telephones, Edmonton, said it agreed to sell 50% of its Novatel Communications Ltd. unit, a maker of cellular telephones, to Robert Bosch G.m.b.H. of West Germany, an international manufacturing concern. Terms weren't disclosed, though the price was said to be about 150 million Canadian dollars (US$129.8 million). An Alberta Government Telephones spokesman wouldn't confirm this estimate.

Calgary, Alberta-based Novatel, which has about 1,200 employees in plants in Alberta, is expected to generate revenue this year of about C$400 million, the spokesman said, up from C$300 million last year.

Alberta Government Telephones is owned by the province of Alberta, which is preparing to take the big telephone utility public later this summer. The spokesman said the partnership with Bosch would expand Novatel's opportunities globally.

The proposed sale is subject to various regulatory approvals.
