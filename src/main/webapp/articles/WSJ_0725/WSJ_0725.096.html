Tonka Reports Loss Totaling $19 Million For Second Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/25/90

MINNETONKA, Minn. -- Tonka Corp. reported a second-quarter loss of $19 million, or $1.30 a share, reflecting declines in its maturing Ghostbusters line and its discontinued Sega video game line. A year earlier, the toy maker earned $100,000, or two cents a share. Revenue fell 23% to $146.3 million from $189.4 million.

The company, which earlier this year predicted a "significant" second-quarter loss, also cited disappointing early sales of "certain" promotional products, which it didn't name. It added that revenue gains at its Parker Brothers and Tonka International divisions only partly offset declines at its Kenner and Tonka Products units.

On a pretax basis, the loss was at the pessimistic end of analysts' expectations. But the company said it adopted a conservative approach for its first-half tax accrual, resulting in "only limited tax benefits" from the second-quarter pretax loss.

The announcement came after the market closed, and analysts were still reviewing their full-year forecasts late yesterday. Carol Palmer of Duff & Phelps Inc. said she expects Tonka to scale back its marketing plans in Europe somewhat in an attempt to control expenses.

In the first six months, the loss widened to $29.3 million, or $2.01 a share, from $6 million, or 76 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue fell 20% to $282.2 million from $351.6 million.

Tonka Chairman Stephen G. Shank said he was "disappointed" by first-half results but continues "to expect a profitable second half." He described as "excellent" the early retail results of the company's Batman action figures and vehicles, adding that they could become a "significant contributor" to revenue in the second half. He also said he is "very encouraged" by early retail sales of Kenner's Baby Alive doll and preliminary test-market results of Tonka Products' new line of stuffed toys called Wrestling Buddies.

However, the company said it is "uncertain whether second-half results will be sufficient to offset the first-half deficit, and there is a real possibility that Tonka will not be profitable for the year."
