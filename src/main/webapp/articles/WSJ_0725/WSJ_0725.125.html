American Airlines Scraps Plan to Levy Airport Fees

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/25/90

FORT WORTH, Texas -- American Airlines backed down from its plan to impose special surcharges on passengers flying out of five U.S. airports. The AMR Corp. unit had announced earlier this month that it would collect the special fees because of what it claimed were higher costs -- such as landing and gate fees -- associated with those airports. The surcharges were to become effective Aug. 1.

The airports and the surcharges were: New Jersey's Newark, $4; New Orleans International, $1.50; New York's La Guardia and Florida's West Palm Beach and Fort Myers, $1. American would have realized revenue of about $4 million as a result.

American said it was unable to implement the surcharges because of unspecified "competition." No other airline matched American's fees.
