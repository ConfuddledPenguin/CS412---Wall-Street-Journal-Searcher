Houston's Judge Jones Was A Top High Court Prospect

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 07/25/90

Before picking Judge David Souter of New Hampshire to fill a vacant seat on the Supreme Court, President Bush interviewed another candidate, administration officials said. The president's other top candidate was federal appeals court Judge Edith Jones of Houston, the officials said. They said Mr. Bush summoned both Judge Jones and Judge Souter to Washington and talked to each in the White House Monday.

About 4:15 p.m., two hours after completing the interviews, Mr. Bush called Judge Souter into the private presidential study and told him he had been chosen. Mr. Bush then announced the decision at a 5 p.m. press conference.

Other top contenders, the officials said, were federal appeals court Judge Laurence Silberman of Washington and Solicitor General Kenneth Starr. The disclosure of the other candidates may be significant, because it could give clues about Mr. Bush's preferences if another seat opens on the Supreme Court.

Separately, the White House said that it has enlisted Kenneth Duberstein, who was former President Reagan's chief of staff, to devise a strategy for shepherding the Souter nomination through the Senate confirmation process. Mr. Duberstein, who also is a former White House legislative liaison, has extensive contacts with Republicans and Democrats on Capitol Hill.
