Pepsi-Cola to Sell Franchise To Earvin `Magic' Johnson

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 07/25/90

Pepsi-Cola Co. is selling its Washington, D.C., bottling franchise to a partnership headed by basketball star Earvin "Magic" Johnson and Black Enterprise magazine publisher Earl Graves, individuals familiar with the transaction said. Mr. Johnson, of the Los Angeles Lakers, had told Pepsi of his interest in owning such an operation when he signed a multi-year endorsement contract with the soft drink firm last year, according to industry executives. In recent years, Pepsi has generally been acquiring bottling franchises from independent owners, rather than selling them. Pepsi-Cola is a unit of PepsiCo, Purchase, N.Y.

The Washington franchise sells about four million cases a year, according to Beverage Digest, a trade newsletter. Individuals close to Pepsi said the Johnson/Graves partnership is not acquiring a stake in the plant that services their franchise area.

"For Pepsi it isn't so much good will with minorities," said Paine Webber Inc. analyst Emanuel Goldman of Pepsi's motivation for the deal. "It's having Magic Johnson, who appeals to a wide segment of the population."

Although Pepsi officials declined to comment, they are expected to announce details of the transaction at a Washington news conference today. Mr. Johnson's business representatives could not be reached for comment.
