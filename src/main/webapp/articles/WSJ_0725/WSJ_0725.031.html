PepsiCo Net In 2nd Quarter Rose by 10%

  • Author: Michael J. McCarthy
  • Date: 07/25/90

PepsiCo Inc., paced by big earnings gains in its fledgling overseas markets, said second-quarter net income rose 10%. Stripping away several one-time credits and charges, Pepsi's earnings would have increased 19%, as the company outperformed the sluggish fast-food industry in the U.S. and peddled more chips, chicken and soft drinks to consumers abroad.

In line with analysts' estimates, Pepsi's second-quarter net income was $292.5 million, or $1.10 a share, compared with $264.9 million, or $1 a share.

The latest quarter's net included a two-cent-a-share write off of soda and snack receivables, resulting from the recent bankruptcy-law filing of a major customer, Circle K Corp. The quarter also included three cents a share in earnings dilution from Pepsi's acquisition last year of two British snack companies. The year-ago quarter's net included several gains and charges, the net effect of which was a credit of six cents a share.

Sales for Pepsi, of Purchase, N.Y., totaled $4.2 billion, up 17% from $3.59 billion.

In snack foods, Pepsi's biggest profit maker, second-quarter international sales more than doubled to $347.7 million while profits jumped 75%. The latest results include the operations of the big British snack companies. But even without them, the overseas snack-food business sold 14% more chips, as measured in tons. In the U.S., by contrast, where the snack-food market is more mature and competitive, Pepsi's Frito-Lay unit showed a 5% increase in volume. That gain translated, after higher promotional costs for new products and some charges, into a 7% profit growth in the U.S.

In soft drinks, unlike main rival Coca-Cola Co., most of Pepsi's profits come from the U.S. And with a 4% increase in second-quarter sales by the company's domestic bottlers, compared with about a 3% gain for Coke, Pepsi uncorked a 12% rise in domestic operating profit, excluding a one-time charge. Diet Pepsi and Mountain Dew led the volume increase in the U.S.

The nation's largest restaurant operator continued to shake off the fast-food doldrums in the second quarter. Same-store sales for company-owned operations, an important indicator of restaurant health that compares sales for units open at least a year, rose 10% at Pizza Hut, 15% at Taco Bell and 5% at Kentucky Fried Chicken. Putting those gains in perspective, industry-leader McDonald's Corp.'s company-owned stores, hurt by industrywide price discounting, last week reported about a 1% decline in second-quarter same-store sales.

A combination of new products, like Hot Wings at Kentucky Fried Chicken, low-priced items like tacos and burritos at Taco Bell, and expanded delivery service at Pizza Hut, all helped Pepsi generate improved operating results from its restaurants.

For the first half, Pepsi's net income increased 10% to $474.4 million, or $1.78 a share, from $430 million, or $1.62 a share. The latest period's net includes the charge for the Circle K receivables; the year-ago period profit includes the six-cents-a-share net credit.

First-half sales rose 20% to $7.88 billion from $6.55 billion.
