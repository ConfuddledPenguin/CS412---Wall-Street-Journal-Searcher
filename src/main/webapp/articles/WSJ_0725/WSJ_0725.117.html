Senator Seeks Compromise On Shift of Duties to SEC

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/25/90

WASHINGTON -- The head of the Senate Securities subcommittee said he hopes a compromise can be reached on a Bush administration-backed plan to shift some power from commodity regulators to the Securities and Exchange Commission. Sen. Christopher Dodd (D., Conn.) said that if discussions on a compromise collapse, the legislation will become "a jump ball on the {Senate} floor." The focus of the bill would move regulation of stock-index futures from the Commodity Futures Trading Commission to the SEC.

Despite support from the securities industry, the proposal appears to be headed for defeat. Backers of the bill are looking to other areas to find a compromise. The primary target, Sen. Dodd confirmed, is oversight of futures margins, which currently are set by the commodities exchanges and can't be overruled by a regulator except in emergencies. Senate aides said any compromise would create a provision for federal oversight of margins, or good-faith money that must be put up in order to trade.

At a Senate Banking Committee hearing, the heads of the futures exchanges urged Congress to keep marginsetting authority at the exchanges.
