Business Brief -- Weyerhaeuser Co.: Firm Reports 26% Decline In Second-Quarter Earnings

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/25/90

Citing a 40% decline from a year ago in its pulp and paper sales, Weyerhaeuser Co. reported a 26% drop in its second-quarter earnings to $114.3 million, or 54 cents a share, from $154.9 million in the year-earlier period. The second quarter ended July 1. Sales of the forest products, real estate and financial services concern declined 10% to $2.39 billion, from $2.64 billion for the year-ago quarter.

The sale of the company's gypsum, wood paneling and nursery and garden units have "obviously had an impact on our sales," said a company spokesman. The final stage of the company's refocusing effort will be completed by the third quarter, he added.

The Tacoma, Wash.-based company also said weaknesses in several areas contributed to the poor earnings results. Forest products results were flat year-to-year, and its Weyerhaeuser Real Estate Co.'s earnings were off substantially from year earlier. However, the company said its financial services units were strong.

For the six months, earnings dropped 19%, to $244.3 million, or $1.15 a share, from $303 million, or $1.43 a share, a year ago.

Sales declined 6%, to $4.67 billion, from $4.97 billion for the six months period.
