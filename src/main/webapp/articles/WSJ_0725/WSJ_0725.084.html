New Securities Issues

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/25/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: MUNICIPALS

New York City -- $1.1 billion of general obligation tax anticipation notes, Fiscal 1991 Series A, due June 28, 1991, was won by eight underwriting groups, city officials announced. The 6 1/4% notes, rated MIG-1 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and SP1-Plus by Standard & Poor's Corp. will be reoffered to yield 5.90%. The following groups won the deal: Goldman, Sachs & Co. and Carmona Ferrand Montes Securities Corp., $675 million; Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette Securities Corp., $100 million; Lehman Brothers, $100 million; Bear, Stearns & Co., $50 million; First Boston Corp., $50 million; Manufacturers Hanover Securities Corp., $50 million; PaineWebber Inc., $50 million; First Albany Corp., $25 million. A total of 76 bids were placed from 24 firms, city officials said. The net interest costs ranged from 5.89% to 5.994%, with the average net interest cost at 5.9688%.

Minnesota Public Facilities Authority -- $71 million of water pollution control revenue bonds, 1990 Series A, due March 1, 1993-2003, and 2012, with capital appreciation bonds due 2004-2006, were tentatively priced by a Kidder, Peabody & Co. group to yield from 6.10% in 1993 to 7.15% in 2012. Current interest serial bonds are priced at par to yield from 6.10% in 1993 to 6.95% in 2003. There are $22.8 million of 7.10% term bonds priced at 99.50 to yield 7.15% in 2012. Capital appreciation bonds are priced to yield to maturity from 7.05% in 2004 to 7.10% in 2005 and 2006. The bonds are rated double-A-plus by S&P.

Alaska Energy Authority -- $61 million of power revenue bonds, Bradley Lake Hydro Electric Project Issue, Second Series, due July 1, 1992-2015, 2013 and 2021, with capital appreciation bonds due 2006-2010, were tentatively priced by a John Nuveen & Co. group to yield from 6.10% in 1992 to 7.30% in 2021. Current interest serial bonds are priced at par to yield from 6.10% in 1992 to 7.15% in 2005. There are $7.5 million of term bonds priced at par to yield 7.20% in 2013. There are $29.8 million of 7 1/4% term bonds priced at 99 3/8 to yield 7.30% in 2021. Capital appreciation bonds are priced to yield to maturity 7.10% in 2006-2010. The bonds are insured and rated triple-A by Moody's and S&P.

Dade County, Fla. -- $55 million of aviation revenue bonds, Series T, due Oct. 1, 1992-2005, were priced by a Lazard Freres & Co. group. The bonds are priced to yield from 6.15% in 1992 to 7.22% in 2005. There are no term bonds. The bonds are insured and rated triple-A by Moody's and S&P. Interest on the bonds will be treated as a preference item in calculating the federal alternative minimum tax that may be imposed on certain investors.

Richmond, Va. -- $50 million of general obligation revenue anticipation notes, Series 1990 B, due June 28, 1991, apparently were won by a Chemical Securities Inc. group. The 6 1/4% notes, rated MIG-1 by Moody's and SP1-Plus by S&P, were priced for reoffering to yield 5.90%.

New Jersey Health Care Facilities Financing Authority -- $48.8 million of revenue bonds, Holy Name Hospital Issue, Series B, due July 1, 1994-2004, 2010 and 2020, were tentatively priced by a Dillon Read & Co. group to yield from 6.10% in 1994 to 7.07% in 2020. Current interest serial bonds are priced to yield from 6.10% in 1994 to 6.90% in 2004. There are $10.8 million of 7% term bonds priced at 99 1/2 to yield 7.05% in 2010. There are $18.1 million of 6 3/4% term bonds priced at 96 to yield 7.07% in 2020. The bonds are insured and rated triple-A by Moody's and S&P.

MORTGAGE

Rural Housing Trust 1987-1 (FHA) -- $540 million of regular subordinated mortgage pass-through certificates is being offered with a pass-through rate of 3.33% at a price of 70 5/8 to yield 10.15%. The securities, through Salomon Brothers Inc., were priced at a yield spread of 1.75 percentage points over the interpolated yield on five-year and seven-year Treasury notes. The expected ratings are single-A-3 by Moody's and single-A-minus by S&P.

ASSET-BACKED

Chrysler Auto Receivables Co. -- $1 billion of securities backed by retail installment contracts on new or used autos and light trucks with a coupon rate of 8 3/4% at 99 23/32 to yield 8.856% through Merrill Lynch Capital Markets. The offering, through U.S. Auto Receivables Trust 1990-2, was priced at a yield spread of 0.77 percentage point over the Treasury's two-year note. Expected final maturity is July 15, 1993. Legal final maturity is Aug. 15, 1995. The securities have a 1.57-year average life, assuming prepayments at 1% of the original loan balance. A triple-A rating is expected from S&P and Moody's based on the quality of the underlying loans and a subordinated class that now equals 9% of the senior securities. The underlying loans are being serviced by Chrysler Credit Corp., a unit of Chrysler Corp.

Towers Healthcare Receivables Funding Corp. -- $56.5 million of two-year 1990 bonds backed by third party health-care receivables at par to yield 10.20%. Towers Healthcare Receivables Funding is a unit of Towers Financial Corp., which underwrote the deal and is servicing the underlying loans. The offering is rated double-A by Duff & Phelps Inc. Anthony DiNicolas, senior vice president, finance, at Towers Financial said the "private placement two-year note is the first time health-care receivables have been used as exclusive collateral in a note transaction." Funds paid to health-care facilities from insurance companies after a person seeks health care are third party health-care receivables.

EUROBONDS

Ricoh Co. (Japan) -- $300 million of bonds due Aug. 9, 1994, with equity-purchase warrants, with the coupon fixed at 4 3/4% and par issue price, via Nomura International Ltd. Each $5,000 bond carries one warrant, exercisable from Aug. 23, 1990, through Aug. 26, 1994, to buy company shares at an expected premium of 2 1/2% to the closing share price when terms are fixed Tuesday.

Osterreichische Kontrollbank (Austria) -- $200 million of 9% bonds due Aug. 28, 1995, at 100 1/4 via J.P. Morgan Securities Ltd. Guaranteed by Austria.

Nachi Fujikoshi Corp. (Japan) -- $150 million of bonds due Aug. 9, 1994, with equity-purchase warrants, indicating a 4 3/4% coupon at par via Yamaichi International Ltd. Guaranteed by Tokai Bank. Each $10,000 bond carries two warrants, exercisable from Aug. 23, 1990, through Aug. 2, 1994, to buy company shares at an expected premium of 2 1/2% to the closing share price when terms are fixed Monday.

KFW International Finance Inc. Delaware (West German parent) -- 150 million Canadian dollars (US$129.8 million) of 11 3/4% bonds due Aug. 22, 1995, priced at 101.125 via Dresdner Bank AG. Guaranteed by Kreditanstalt fur Wiederaubfau.

British Gas International Finance (United Kingdom parent) -- 100 billion lire ($84.3 million) of 12 1/4% bonds via Banca Commerciale Italiana. Maturity date Aug. 24, 1994, issue price is 101.40, yield 12.32% less fees annually. Fees 1 5/8.
