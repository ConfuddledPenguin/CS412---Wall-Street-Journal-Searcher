Mitel Files Claim Against AT&T In Florida Court

  • Author: Rose Tamburri
  • Date: 07/25/90

OTTAWA -- Mitel Corp. said it filed a claim in a Florida court against American Telephone & Telegraph Co. for allegedly spreading "misleading information" about the company. The value of the claim wasn't disclosed.

John Jarvis, Mitel president, told a shareholders' meeting here that AT&T suggested in letters to Mitel's U.S. customers that British Telecommunications PLC's plan to sell its 51% interest in Mitel was "a cause for concern" about Mitel's future. Mr. Jarvis said the AT&T letters offered Mitel customers a trade-in allowance if they exchanged Mitel's telephone switching system for an AT&T product.

A spokesman for New York-based AT&T said he had just learned of the Mitel claim and couldn't comment on it until it was examined.

Mr. Jarvis said AT&T's "aspersions" about Mitel's "viability" were "without foundation." He said it was too soon to assess what negative impact the "misleading information" would have on Mitel. Mr. Jarvis said the company was concerned that its "future business prospects and reputation" could be damaged.

Mr. Jarvis told shareholders that a "number of very impressive companies" have expressed interest in buying British Telecommunications' stake in Mitel. He said British Telecommunications is talking to some of the companies.

Mitel also reported yesterday that the company had a net loss of 3.1 million Canadian dollars (US$2.7 million) on revenue of C$100 million in the first quarter ended June 29. In the year earlier, Mitel had net income of C$4.2 million on revenue of C$102.7 million.

Mitel shares closed at C$2.45 a share on the Toronto Stock Exchange, down 15 Canadian cents.

Mr. Jarvis said Mitel's first-quarter loss was in line with the company's expectations. He said Mitel's forecasts and advanced bookings had indicated a slow start in the year, but he said there are some early indications that the Kanata, Ontario, company has increased its market share in Britain in the second quarter.

"It is clear that these two facts taken together, slow sales and market share increases, indicate the difficult nature of the market and the appropriateness of our strategy to become a more broadly based telecommunications company," he said.

Mr. Jarvis said Mitel's sales outside North America were down more than 8% in the first quarter from a year earlier. Mr. Jarvis said he was confident that Mitel will have a "positive performance" in these markets as the year develops.

Mr. Jarvis said orders in markets outside North America have been "quite strong" in recent weeks. He predicted the company would have an upturn in sales in the second quarter.

Mr. Jarvis also said Mitel plans to launch a major advertising campaign in the fall "aimed at increasing awareness of the company" in the U.S. and Britain. He said it will help "counter the misleading information" distributed by AT&T.
