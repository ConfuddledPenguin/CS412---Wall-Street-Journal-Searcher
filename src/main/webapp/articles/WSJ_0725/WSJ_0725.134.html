Digital Sound Holder Sues Over Initial Offer

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/25/90

CARPINTERIA, Calif. -- Digital Sound Corp. said a shareholder filed a lawsuit in federal court in Los Angeles against the company, which designs, makes and sells products for voice messaging, interactive voice reponse and other applications. Digital Sound said the complaint, which seeks class-action status, alleges omissions and misstatements in connection with the company's initial public offering Feb. 27, and subsequent public statements. The suit doesn't specify any amount of damages sought, the company said. The plaintiff wasn't identified.

The company said that it believes the lawsuit is without merit, and that it intends to contest the action vigorously.

Digital Sound went public at $8.50 a share. The stock plunged $4.25 on July 2, after the company said it expected to report a loss for the second quarter, citing a slower-than-anticipated order rate from certain major customers. Digital Sound shares closed at $4, down 50 cents, in national over-the-counter trading yesterday.
