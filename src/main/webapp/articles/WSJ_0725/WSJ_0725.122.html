CPA Group Guides to Lack Consent of Financial Board

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/25/90

NORWALK, Conn. -- The American Institute of Certified Public Accountants plans to start issuing guidelines on financial accounting issues without Financial Accounting Standards Board agreement. In the past, the AICPA, the field's major professional organization, has advised the FASB whenever it chose to issue guidelines on financial accounting and reporting standards. Although the FASB didn't control the AICPA decisions, any FASB objections offered were taken into account.

The FASB is the accounting profession's chief rule-making body, while the AICPA guidelines tend to focus on more technical matters.

Both groups aired their views at the FASB headquarters here. Thomas P. Kelley, group vice president, professional, for the AICPA, said its board's action gives it an explicit ability to issue guidelines. "There is a feeling that we should become more active in the accounting standards area," Mr. Kelley said. He said the AICPA wanted to speed up the process, noting that the FASB sometimes has difficulty reaching agreement.

But some critics fear the policy change will prove confusing to accountants. P. Norman Roy, president of the Financial Executives Institute, said the change would be "extremely divisive."

The FASB plans to draft a response.
