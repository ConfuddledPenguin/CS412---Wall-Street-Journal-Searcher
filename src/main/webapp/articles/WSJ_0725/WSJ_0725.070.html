Yields Fell on CDs From Big Institutions In Weekly Survey

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/25/90

NEW YORK -- Yields on certificates of deposit at major banks and brokerage firms fell slightly in the week ended Tuesday. The average yield on six-month small-denomination CDs sold directly by banks slipped to 7.55% from 7.62%, according to a nationwide, 18-bank survey by Banxquote Money Markets, a New York information service. The average for two-year consumer CDs fell to 7.81% from 7.88%.

Average yields on small-denomination CDs sold by leading broker dealers were also down. The average yield on a one-year CD fell to 7.93% from 8.10%, while the average for two-year CDs fell to 8.08% from 8.13%, according to Banxquote.

Average yields for most large-denomination CDs, which typically require deposits of $90,000 or more, declined. The exception was the average yield on five-year jumbo CDs, which edged up to 8.21% from 8.19%. The average yield on one-year jumbo CDs eased to 7.79% from 7.81%, while the average for two-year maturities slipped to 7.97% from 8%.
