Money Funds Draw Over $1 Billion From Individuals

  • Author: Georgette Jasen
  • Date: 06/14/90

Individual investors poured more than $1 billion into money-market mutual funds in the latest week, despite flat yields and near-record highs for the stock market. The average seven-day compound yield eased to 7.95% from 7.96% in the week ended Tuesday, according to IBC/Donoghue's Money Fund Report. Assets of 427 taxable funds tracked by the Holliston, Mass.-based newsletter grew about $1.2 billion to $377.1 billion, the highest level since early May.

The money came from individual investors, said Martha Whittbrodt, editor of the newsletter. Assets of funds open only to institutions dropped about $434 million, she said, adding that the cash probably went into the rising stock market. The Dow Jones Industrial Average reached a record high of 2935.19 June 4.

Money-fund yields have been at or just below 8% since January. The latest slip reflects a general decline in short-term interest rates.

The average maturity of the funds' investments -- such things as commercial paper (short-term corporate IOUs), Treasury bills and certificates of deposit -- shortened by a day, to 43 days. The average maturity has been 43 or 44 days for nine weeks, indicating that a marked change in yields isn't likely.

Ms. Whittbrodt said yields probably will remain in the same range until the Federal Reserve acts to change interest rates -- something she doesn't expect soon.

The average seven-day simple yield of the taxable funds slipped to 7.65% from 7.67% in the latest week. The average 30day simple yield eased to 7.68% from 7.69%, and the average 30-day compound yield slid to 7.98% from 7.99%.

Yields on tax-free money funds fell more sharply, reflecting the rate decline in the municipal-bond market since the end of April. The average seven-day compound yield, for instance, dropped to 5.37% from 5.49%. That's equal to a 7.46% taxable yield for an investor in the 28% tax bracket and 8.01% for someone paying 33% in taxes.

Assets of the 220 tax-exempt funds tracked by Donoghue's rose about $1 billion, to $77 billion. The average maturity shortened by a day, to 38 days.
