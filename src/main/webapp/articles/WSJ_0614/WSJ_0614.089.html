Law: Eight Ex-Employees Of Grand Wilshire Charged With Fraud

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/14/90

LOS ANGELES -- Eight former employees of Grand Wilshire Group, one of California's largest minority-owned businesses that owned about 25 car dealerships before it collapsed in 1988, were charged with defrauding numerous lenders out of about $50 million. Glendora, Calif.-based Grand Wilshire was controlled by Eminiano (Jun) Reodica Jr., a Philippine immigrant who disappeared two years ago after the company came under investigation, said U.S. Attorney Ronni B. MacLaren, who is prosecuting the case.

Mr. Reodica, who wasn't named in the criminal information filed in U.S. District Court here, is still under investigation, Ms. MacLaren said.

The employees, including an accounting manager, two operations managers, and Mr. Reodica's personal secretary, face various charges related to their roles in the alleged scheme to use customer-loan contracts improperly as collateral to secure huge credit lines for the dealerships.

The employees deleted negative credit information from customers' credit reports, submitted the same contracts as collateral to as many as three different lenders, and hid customers' failure to make their car payments, the information said. Imperial Savings Association in San Diego, First Los Angeles Bank, and General Electric Capital Corp. were among the allegedly victimized lenders.

If convicted, the eight defendants face maximum penalties ranging from five to 25 years in prison and fines of $250,000 to $1,250,000, Ms. MacLaren said.
