World Bank Clears $680 Million in Loans For Venezuela's Use

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/14/90

WASHINGTON -- The World Bank approved $680 million in loans for Venezuela to support reforms of the country's financial sector and efforts to convert public-sector enterprises to private ownership. World Bank officials said $162.5 million may be used by the Venezuelan government to support its debt-reduction arrangements with commercial bank creditors. This is the second time the bank has approved a set-aside of part of its loans to Venezuela to support debt-reduction arrangements. Last June, also at the request of the Venezuelan government, it set aside about $187.5 million for debt-reduction out of $755 million in economic adjustment credits.

The World Bank said the newly authorized loans included $350 million to facilitate the transfer of many government-owned enterprises to private owners and to restructure the operations of other public-sector companies.

Another $300 million loan will support financial sector reforms, including the privatization of several commercial banks owned by the country's central bank. Venezuela's Agricultural Development Bank will be liquidated. The Industrial Bank, the country's largest public commercial bank, and its four regional banks will be "restructured under this operation," the World Bank said.

A third credit, amounting to $30 million, will help finance technical assistance projects to strengthen the management of several Venezuelan government ministries involved in government financial management and investment policy-making decisions, the World Bank said.

The loans are for 15 years and will carry a variable interest rate, currently 7.75% a year.
