Woolworth's Capital Spending

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/14/90

NEW YORK -- Woolworth Corp., a retailer, said it is raising its capital spending budget for the year ending Jan. 26, 1991, to $365 million from the $325 million it allotted earlier. The new sum, which represents a 15% increase from the $315 million in capital outlays a year earlier, will be used primarily to open 825 new stores, up from 735 announced earlier. More than 90% of the new sites will be specialty stores, such as Foot Locker, Lady Foot Locker, Kids Mart, Champs Sports, and Afterthoughts/Carimar.
