International Brief -- Stora Kopparbergs Bergslags AB: Company Pulls Out of Bid For Forest Products Group

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/14/90

Stora Kopparbergs Bergslags AB confirmed that it has withdrawn from a planned joint takeover with Finland's Kymmene Oy of French forest products group Chapelle Darblay S.A. Stora, the large Swedish pulp and paper concern, said its pending 4 billion mark ($2.4 million) acquisition of West Germany's Feldmuehle Nobel AG would have posed potential antitrust difficulties in both France and West Germany for the purchase of a 50% stake in Chapelle Darblay. Stora denied that it pulled out under pressure from the French government or at the request of West German antitrust authorities.

The French Economics Ministry approved the sale of Chapelle Darblay to Kymmene alone. Officials said Stora had informed Kymmene and French authorities as early as mid-May they wouldn't proceed with the purchase.

Officials said Kymmene exercised a clause in the accord giving them the right of first refusal to Stora's stake.

The purchase price for Chapelle Darblay was 1.32 billion French francs ($231.2 million).
