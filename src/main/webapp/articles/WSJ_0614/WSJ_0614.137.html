Law: Ex-Texas Bank Aide Gets 12-Year Sentence For Committing Fraud

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/14/90

AUSTIN, Texas -- A federal judge ordered Richard Homer Taylor, the former president of National Bank of Texas to serve 12 years in prison, the maximum sentence, for committing fraud that contributed to the Austin bank's demise. Judge James Nowlin of the U.S. district court here also ordered Mr. Taylor to pay $19,099 in restitution to the Federal Deposit Insurance Corp.; to pay $505,000 in other fines; and to make restitution of $221,000 to Franklin Federal Bancorp of Austin, the receiver for a defunct thrift that had losses from loans to Mr. Taylor.

In January, Mr. Taylor pleaded guilty to two counts of bank fraud and one count of income-tax fraud stemming from a scheme in which other individuals obtained loans at his direction and later transferred the loan proceeds to him.
