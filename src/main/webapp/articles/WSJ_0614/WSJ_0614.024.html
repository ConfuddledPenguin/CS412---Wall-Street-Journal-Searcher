Placer Dome Plans To Continue Pursuing Stikine; Shares Jump

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/14/90

VANCOUVER, British Columbia -- Placer Dome Inc., a big gold producer, said it plans to continue its previously announced 229.5 million Canadian dollar (US$195.7 million) offer to acquire Stikine Resources Ltd., half-owner of the promising Eskay Creek gold project. The announcement, which followed last week's disclosure by Placer Dome that it might withdraw its bid, sparked a sharp jump in Stikine's share price on the Toronto Stock Exchange. Stikine shares closed yesterday at C$67.375, up C$3.875, on volume of 186,009 shares.

Separately, Placer Dome disclosed late yesterday that it acquired 40,600 additional Stikine shares in open-market purchases on the Toronto exchange, raising Placer Dome's stake in the company to 5.2%, from 4.7%. A spokesman wouldn't say whether Placer Dome intends to make further open-market purchases before its bid expires Tuesday.

The share price of Stikine, a Vancouver-based gold exploration company, had slumped after Placer Dome said it might cancel its C$67.50-a-share offer, in view of a lock-up agreement under which Stikine's 42% owners agreed to tender their stock to a rival bidder, Corona Corp., a Toronto-based gold producer.

"The market has recognized that the Placer Dome offer is superior to the Corona proposal," Anthony Petrina, Placer Dome's president and chief executive officer, said. By threatening to withdraw its bid, "They {Placer Dome} were able to highlight the difference between their offer and Corona's offer," said Barry Allan, gold analyst with Prudential-Bache Securities Canada Ltd., Toronto.

Corona has offered to swap 6.5 Corona Class A shares, plus two shares of a newly created unit, Corona Gold Corp., for each Stikine share. Stikine holders also would be eligible subsequently to receive a special Corona Gold stock dividend.

Analysts have had difficulty valuing Corona's offer, which is part of a broad corporate reorganization plan by Corona. But Mr. Allan said the lock-up pact, which calls for the exchange of 8.5 Corona Class A shares for each Stikine share, under certain conditions, established a "down-side" benchmark for Corona's offer.

Based on the C$7.375 closing price of Corona Class A shares yesterday on the Toronto Stock Exchange, the "downside" value of Corona's offer is about C$62.69 a Stikine share.

Corona already owns an interest in Eskay Creek through 45%-owned Prime Resources Group Inc., a Vancouver-based gold exploration company. Prime owns 50% of Eskay Creek and operates the gold project, which is in northwestern British Columbia.
