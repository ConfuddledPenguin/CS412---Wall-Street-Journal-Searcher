Dividend News: Progressive Bank Inc. Omits Its Dividend

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/14/90

PAWLING, N.Y. -- Progressive Bank Inc., which cut its quarterly dividend to 12.5 cents a share from 27.5 cents for the first quarter, omitted the payout for the second period and added $5 million to its reserve against possible losses on bad loans. The board omitted the dividend because the real estate market continues to be weak and nonperforming loans -- those not earning interest -- have increased, Progressive said.

E. Hale Mayer, chairman and chief executive officer, said, however, that after the charge, the company's capital stands at 8.63% of total assets, exceeding regulators' requirements.

Progressive, holding company for Pawling Savings Bank, last paid out 12.5 cents a share April 30. It currently has 2.9 million shares outstanding. The total reserve for possible loan losses is $14.5 million.

The company's shares were halted in national over-the-counter trading yesterday before the market opened. They last traded at $7.75.
