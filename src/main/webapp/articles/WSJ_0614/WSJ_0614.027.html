International: Philips Joins Matsushita On Compatibility Device

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 06/14/90

EINDHOVEN, The Netherlands -- Dutch manufacturer N.V. Philips Gloeilampenfabriek and Matsushita Electric Industrial Co. of Japan have agreed to form a joint venture to develop the standard for the "domestic digital bus," or D2B, a device that would make compatible audio and visual parts manufactured under different specifications. For the D2B system to work, one of its integral parts is a computer chip that must be designed to certain standards, according to a Philips spokesman. The spokesman noted that at this time only Philips and Matsushita are actively involved in trying to set that standard.

Philips will hold a 75% stake and Matsushita a 25% stake in the venture, D2B Systems Co., which will be based in London and be initially funded with #3 million ($5.1 million). The Philips spokesman said that the proportions of the stakes in the joint venture don't necessarily reflect the amount of work accomplished by either firm in developing the D2B standard.

Both Thomson S.A. of France and Sony Corp. of Japan, neither of which are working on a D2B standard, have recognized the need for a single standard and have agreed to support the standard developed by Philips and Matsushita, the Philips spokesman said.
