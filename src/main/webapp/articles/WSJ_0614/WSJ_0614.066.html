Prime Motor Alters Accord, Will Sell Franchise Systems

  • Author: Robin Goldwyn Blumenthal
  • Date: 06/14/90

Prime Motor Inns Inc., seeking more cash to pay debt, said it agreed to sell all of its Howard Johnson and Ramada franchise systems rather than retain a 35% stake in them as previously planned. Under the new accord, Prime will receive $170 million for the two franchise systems from Blackstone Capital Partners L.P., an investment partnership that is an affiliate of Blackstone Group, a New York-based investment banking firm.

But although the new agreement calls for Prime to receive about $40 million more, it will give up the 35% interest it would have kept under the old agreement with Blackstone. With about $60 million face amount in notes Prime would have received, the initial transaction totaled about $200 million.

However, a spokeswoman for Prime, based in Fairfield, N.J., said the company values the 35% equity interest in the franchise systems and the original note at considerably less than $60 million.

Peter E. Simon, Prime chairman, said the increased cash payment will enable Prime to "significantly reduce" debt and to invest some proceeds in its AmeriSuites and Wellesley Inns limited service hotels, which it plans to expand.

Prime is one of the largest U.S. hotel companies. Including its Rodeway Inns International franchise system, Prime has a total of about 1,100 properties with about 150,000 rooms. Those include 475 U.S. Ramada properties with 75,000 rooms and 450 Howard Johnson properties with about 55,000 rooms. The company also has more than $200 million of construction projects under way.

Prime, which expanded in a period of softness in the real estate market and tight financing, has about $280 million in bank debt. It is still owed about $200 million by FCD Hospitality Inc., which last year borrowed from Prime to purchase Servico, a hotel operator, from Southmark Corp.

Officials of Prime have said that tomorrow is the cut-off date for FCD to start paying back its loan before Prime would start attaching assets. But Prime is still "evaluating" alternatives, Joseph P. Gaynor III, senior vice president of corporate finance, said. He said Prime is considering selling Rodeway as well.

Another reason Prime may have chosen to sell the franchises outright was the interest a competing company, Manor Care Inc., had expressed in them, said Henry R. Silverman, Blackstone's general partner who will be chairman and chief executive officer of the new company. Officials of Manor Care, the Silver Spring, Md., franchiser of Quality Inns, couldn't be reached for comment. Mr. Silverman is a former chairman and chief executive of Days Inns of America Inc.
