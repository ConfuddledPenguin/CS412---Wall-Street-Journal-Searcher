International Brief -- Tate & Lyle PLC: Firm Confident on Pursuing Berisford, Despite Inquiry

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/14/90

Tate & Lyle PLC said it remains confident it will be allowed eventually to bid for Berisford International PLC, despite the British government's ordering an investigation of the competitive effect of the two sugar concerns' combining. The Department of Trade and Industry yesterday ordered a Monopolies and Mergers Commission inquiry, to be completed Sept. 28. A 1987 bid by Tate & Lyle for Berisford was blocked on competitive grounds. Merging the two producers would leave Tate & Lyle in control of 95% of Britain's sugar supplies. Berisford officials weren't available to comment.

Tate & Lyle hasn't indicated what price it is willing to pay for Berisford, a financially troubled conglomerate that also has financial services and property operations. In the year ended Sept. 30, Berisford had a net loss attributable to shareholders of #24.6 million ($41.9 million), reversing year-earlier profit of #33.2 million; pretax profit rose 5.7% to #107.3 million. Tate & Lyle reiterated that terms of any offer will depend on the value and financial standing of Berisford's non-sugar interests.
