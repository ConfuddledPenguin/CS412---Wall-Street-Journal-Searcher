International: Mitsubishi Group, Daimler-Benz Form Aerospace Venture --- Companies Say Study Group To Plan Research Projects Is Only a `Starting Point'

  • Author: Yumiko Ono
  • Date: 06/14/90

TOKYO -- Mitsubishi Group and Daimler-Benz AG are taking the first steps to extend their nascent global cooperation into aerospace. Mitsubishi Heavy Industries Ltd., the aerospace arm of the giant Japanese industrial group, said it will set up a study group with Daimler-Benz to conduct research in four areas: small passenger planes, engines for those planes, space technology and ultrasonic aircraft.

The two industrial groups announced an agreement in March to pursue cooperation in a wide range of industries, including automobiles and semiconductors. This is the groups' first joint effort in aerospace.

A Mitsubishi Heavy spokesman, however, emphasized that the latest agreement is only to form a study group that would map out possible future projects. "This is only the starting point," the spokesman cautioned. Whether the research would immediately lead to broader cooperation in aerospace isn't clear yet, he said. The spokesman said the two groups will dispatch several engineers later to discuss topics for research.

There has been considerable speculation since the cooperative agreement was announced that the two groups would work to develop a small passenger plane. Mitsubishi Heavy's chairman, Yotaro Iida, indicated in a recent interview that the company's agreements with Boeing Co. governing joint development of long-range passenger jets limits the Japanese company's freedom to compete with Boeing in the market for aircraft with 100 seats or more. That limit places greater pressure on Mitsubishi, which is a relative newcomer to the international aircraft market, to carve out a niche in the area of small passenger jets.

The two industrial giants are also considering research into high-altitude, ultrasonic aircraft that could cut the flight time between Japan, the U.S. and Europe.

Daimler-Benz also has proposed cooperating with Mitsubishi Motors Corp. in the international motor-vehicle market. The West German conglomerate has asked Mitsubishi for equipment to help construct an auto assembly plant it plans to build in the Soviet Union and for technology for a small-truck plant in East Germany.

Mitsubishi Corp., the trading house that leads the Mitsubishi Group, is setting up a department to handle project development with the German conglomerate.
