Varity to Ask Holders To Approve Paring Its Preferred Shares

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/14/90

TORONTO -- Varity Corp. took the first step toward improving the trading value of its common shares, announcing that it will seek shareholder approval to reduce the number of preferred shares outstanding. The industrial products concern said it may offer holders of preferred shares cash or common shares in return for their stakes in the company, reducing the number of preferred shares outstanding and its annual dividend obligations.

In the year ended Jan. 31, 1990, Varity said it paid dividends totaling about $18.7 million to holders of the 11.8 million Class 1 and two million Class 2 preferred shares.

The company has said often that it regards the existence of the preferred shares, and the substantial dividend payments, as "a major impediment" to further improvement in the trading value of its common shares as well as its earnings.

For fiscal 1990, Varity reported net income of $92.1 million, or 35 cents a share, on revenue of $2.38 billion. In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange yesterday, Varity's common closed at $3, off 12.5 cents, on volume of 451,100 shares.

Varity said its board hasn't decided whether or when such proposals would be made, or what form they would take. Currently, the company has about 249 million common shares outstanding.

It said shareholders will vote on the amendments at the annual meeting July 30. The amendments must be approved by two-thirds of the votes cast by holders of common shares, Class 1 shares and Class 2 shares, each voting separately as a class or series.

Jack R. Nowling, a Varity spokesman, said the company is still considering "a number of measures" to increase value for common shareholders, apart from the conversion of the preferred shares.

He said those measures could include a common share buy-back, making use of the $275.5 million of cash the company had as of April 30. But he added that any announcements regarding moves to improve value for common shareholders aren't likely to be made until the annual meeting.
