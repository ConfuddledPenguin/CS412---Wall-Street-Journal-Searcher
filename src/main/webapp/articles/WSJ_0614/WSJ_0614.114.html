American Century Debenture Holders Clear Plan on Assets

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/14/90

SAN ANTONIO, Texas -- American Century Corp. said its debenture holders have approved a reorganization plan under which substantially all of its assets will be acquired by Tescorp Inc., a Houston-based manufacturer and distributor of oilfield products. Tescorp's shareholders also have approved the plan, which now goes to a June 18 confirmation hearing before a federal bankruptcy court. Under terms of the plan, the holder of a $1,000 American Century debenture will receive 67 shares of Tescorp common stock plus 86 shares of Tescorp convertible preferred stock and warrants for an additional 245 shares of common. The preferred stock initially will be convertible for $3.50 a share and will receive cash dividends of 50 cents a year. The warrants, which have a three-year term, have an initial exercise price of $3.50 a share but that will escalate in the second and third years.

American Century stockholders will receive warrants with the same terms and conditions for a total of 847,375 shares of Tescorp common. American Century owned Commerce Savings Association, a thrift that was placed in federal conservatorship last year.

Tescorp said it has applied to list its common stock, preferred stock and warrants on the NASD automated quotation system. Its unaudited results of operations for the 12 months ending March 31 showed a net loss of $955,000 on revenue of $20.7 million, compared with net income of $1 million on revenue of $21.4 million in the previous year.
