Zale in Licensing Pact With Tsurukame Corp. For Franchises in Japan

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/14/90

DALLAS -- American brand names are big business in Japan -- big enough to entice Tsurukame Corp. to license the name of Zale Corp., the giant U.S. jewelry store chain. Officials of Tsurukame, Japan's second-largest jewelry store chain, have just completed initial training in Zale's merchandising techniques at the company's Dallas headquarters, said Zale president Irving R. Gerstein, and expect to open their first Zale franchise in downtown Tokyo in September. Initial plans, he added, are for 200 stores within five years, with Zale receiving an undisclosed percentage of retail sales for use of its name.

"It's a no-lose situation for us," said Mr. Gerstein, who attributed Tsurukame's interest to a Japanese fascination with American brand names.

The arrangement includes an agreement by Zale to buy a 1% stake in Tsurukame for 160 million yen ($1 million). Zale, the world's largest jewelry store chain, is owned jointly by Peoples Jewellers Ltd. of Toronto, and by Swarovski International Holding A.G., a Swiss firm.
