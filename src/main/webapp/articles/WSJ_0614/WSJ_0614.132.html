Dividend News: Amgen Will Redeem Debentures, Declares 2-for-1 Split of Stock

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/14/90

THOUSAND OAKS, Calif. -- Amgen Inc., whose stock price has nearly doubled over the past 12 months, declared a 2-for-1 stock split and said it would redeem its convertible debentures. The biotechnology company said the split would be distributed Aug. 10 to stock of record July 31.

Amgen will redeem its $50 million face amount of 8% convertible debentures July 25 at a price of $5,386.67 for each $5,000 face amount of debentures. The conversion rate is 96.8992 shares for each $5,000 principal amount, equal to a price of $51.60 a share.

In national over-the-counter trading yesterday, Amgen shares rose $1.50 to $74 a share. The stock's large premium over the conversion price makes it more attractive for debt holders to convert their debentures into Amgen common than to tender the debentures to the company.

Assuming conversion of all of the debentures, Amgen said it will have about 37.2 million shares outstanding after the stock split. The conversion will save the company about $4 million in annual interest expense.
