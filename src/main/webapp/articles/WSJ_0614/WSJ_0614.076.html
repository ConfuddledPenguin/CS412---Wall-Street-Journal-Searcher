Who's News: Prudential-Bache Forces Tozer to Quit; Parent's Burton Named Vice Chairman

  • Author: Michael Siconolfi
  • Date: 06/14/90

NEW YORK -- Prudential-Bache Securities Inc., in an attempt to turn its fortunes around, forced out its president, W. James Tozer Jr., and named a veteran Prudential Insurance Co. of America executive, Arthur H. Burton Jr., to be vice chairman. This is Mr. Burton's second stint at the insurer's Prudential-Bache brokerage unit. The 56-year-old executive most recently was senior vice president and head of human resources at Prudential; in 1988, he briefly was assistant to Prudential-Bache's chairman and chief executive officer, George Ball.

Prudential-Bache said in a statement that Mr. Tozer, president for 16 months, resigned "to seek a management challenge as a chief executive, probably in a financial-service industry turnaround situation." But Prudential-Bache executives privately said Mr. Tozer was forced out to bolster the firm's profitability.

Mr. Tozer, who had worked in the banking industry before joining Prudential-Bache, couldn't be reached to comment. Last year, Prudential-Bache, the nation's fourth-largest securities firm with about 6,500 brokers, had a loss of $51 million.

Mr. Burton will join Mr. Ball, the chief executive, and James Gahan, president of Prudential-Bache Capital Funding, as a member of Prudential-Bache's office of the chairman. In his new post, Mr. Burton will oversee the firm's mutual funds, risk arbitrage and administration operations.

In an interview, Mr. Burton said his biggest challenge is "regaining profitability" for the firm and pushing its staff to be more efficient. He plans to do that by helping the firm create products, including mutual funds. And he said he will help promote the firm's selling more of its own mutual funds, a recent trend among big securities firms.

Last year, 65% of all funds sold by Prudential-Bache brokers were those the firm itself manages. Prudential-Bache, like most other securities firms, pays its brokers more for selling in-house funds than outside portfolios. Prudential-Bache is the nation's 11th-largest mutual-fund manager, with assets of more than $35 billion.

Mr. Burton joined Prudential in 1955. He was named regional group manager in the Prudential's Eastern Home Office in 1965. He also held several other posts at the company, including a stint as president of North Central operations, until he joined Prudential-Bache in 1988.

In a memo to employees, Mr. Ball described Mr. Tozer as "a valuable and hard working manager." Mr. Ball also said Prudential-Bache won't fill the president's post.

"Many businesses today are opting for this type of structure to avoid barrierism of various kinds," Mr. Ball said in the memo. He added, "There are no hidden agendas in this."

Separately, Prudential-Bache said its international group, headed by William Custard, now will report to Leland Paton, president of the Capital Markets group of Prudential-Bache Capital Funding, which includes the global investment banking, institutional and capital markets divisions of the firm.
