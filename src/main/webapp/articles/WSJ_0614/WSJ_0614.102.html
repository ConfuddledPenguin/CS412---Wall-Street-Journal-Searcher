Business Brief -- Houston Industries Inc.: Utility to Record Charge In Second-Quarter Results

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/14/90

Houston Industries Inc. said it will record a $23 million second-quarter charge resulting from the disallowance by regulators of a portion of its investment in a nuclear plant. In the year-ago quarter, net income was about $56.7 million, or 45 cents a share, on revenue of $950.4 million. Results for the period included a $26.3 million gain on the sale of assets.

The Houston-based utility said the charge is related to the disallowance of $375.5 million of its Houston Lighting & Power Co. unit's $2.8 billion investment in the South Texas Project. HL&P holds a 30.8% ownership stake in the $5.4 billion facility near Bay City, Texas. Other owners include the cities of Austin and San Antonio, Texas, and Central & South West Corp., a Dallas utility holding company.

The company said the Public Utility Commission of Texas this week also confirmed a $227 million base rate rise. The utility implemented that rate increase in June 1989 under a system that allows utilities to initiate interim rate increases, pending regulatory approval.
