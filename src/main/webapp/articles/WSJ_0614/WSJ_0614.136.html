Business Brief -- Kollmorgen Corp.: Investor Group Raises Stake In Motors Maker to 19.5%

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/14/90

A group including Tinicum Enterprises Inc. of Garden City, N.Y., said it raised its stake in Kollmorgen Corp. to the equivalent of 19.5%. In a Securities and Exchange Commission filing, the group said it bought 707,100 shares in the open market from May 11 to June 12 for $10.50 to $11.80 each, or a total of $7.9 million.

The group now controls 2,424,691 Kollmorgen common shares, assuming the conversion of Series D preferred stock it holds.

In March, Kollmorgen, a Simsbury, Conn., manufacturer of motors and electro-optical systems, sold preferred stock convertible into 11.9% of Kollmorgen on a fully diluted basis to Tinicum. The sale was made during Vernitron Corp.'s unsuccessful attempt to take control of Kollmorgen's board.

Vernitron, a closely held Deer Park, N.Y., manufacturer of motors and controls, recently reduced its stake in Kollmorgen to 4.8%.

Kollmorgen couldn't be reached for comment. In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Kollmorgen's shares were unchanged at $11.625.
