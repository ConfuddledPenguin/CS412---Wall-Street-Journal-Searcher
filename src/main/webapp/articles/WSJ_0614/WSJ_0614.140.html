Business Brief -- American Telephone & Telegraph: Order for Phone Equipment For China's Army Received

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/14/90

American Telephone & Telegraph Co., New York, said it received a $5 million order from a Chinese electronics supplier to provide sophisticated telephone equipment for the Chinese army. The agreement to ship digital telephone exchanges to China Electronic System Equipment Corp. is awaiting U.S. Government approval. But an AT&T spokesman said the company is confident it will get clearance, as the order is an extension of a contract signed in 1985, and the Bush administration recently renewed Beijing's status as a most-favored trading partner.

The spokesman also said AT&T received permission from the U.S. and its 16 Western allies to ship transmission equipment to a joint venture that it set up with Chinese partners in May 1989. He said AT&T had been awaiting approval for a year from the Coordinating Committee on Multilateral Export Controls. The venture, AT&T Shanghai Ltd., is owned by Dutch-based AT&T Network Systems International and by Shanghai Telecommunications Equipment Factory and Shanghai Optical Fiber Communications Engineering Corp.
