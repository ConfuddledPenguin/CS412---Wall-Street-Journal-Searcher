International Brief -- Cable & Wireless PLC: Pretax Profit for Fiscal '90 Rose 25% to $898 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/14/90

Cable & Wireless PLC of Britain, a telecommunications concern, reported pretax profit for the year ended March 31 jumped 25% to #527 million ($898 million) -- exceeding market forecasts -- from #420 million a year earlier, citing strong results in Asia and its British domestic phone service. Sales rose 51% to #2.32 billion from #1.53 billion.

Cable & Wireless also said Lord Young, former secretary of state for the Department of Trade and Industry, is to become executive chairman, succeeding Lord Sharp, who is to retire Oct. 1. Lord Young headed the agency last June, when it awarded Cable & Wireless a license to develop and operate a new generation of public mobile telephones for the 1990s.

Fiscal 1990 profit after taxes and minority interests and before special items rose 15% to #328 million from #285 million a year earlier. Earnings per share rose 12% to 31.3 pence from 27.9 pence. Gains of #487 million in the latest period and #129 million a year earlier were from sales of shares in a unit and associated companies. Profit attributable to shareholders nearly doubled to #815 million from #414 million.
