Marketing & Media: Elsevier N.V. Plans To Sell 33% Stake In Wolters Kluwer

  • Author: Bob Hagerty
  • Date: 06/14/90

Elsevier N.V. said it intends to sell its 33% stake in a rival Dutch publisher, Wolters Kluwer N.V. Based on the current market price, Elsevier's 17.8 million nonvoting certificates, representing Wolters Kluwer shares held by a trust, are valued at a total of 926 million guilders ($485 million). Elsevier's finance director, Cornelis Alberti, said the proceeds would be used for acquisitions.

Mr. Alberti said that the sale, subject to "satisfactory market conditions," is likely to take place in September and that Elsevier hopes to sell the certificates at a price near the stock market level. Elsevier named Swiss Bank Corp. as the lead manager for the offering, which is expected to be aimed at a wide international distribution of the certificates.

The Elsevier stake stems from a hostile takeover bid the company made for Kluwer BV in 1987. Kluwer responded by rushing into a defensive merger with Wolters Samsom Groep N.V. to form the current Wolters Kluwer. Elsevier's consolation prize was a 33% stake in Wolters Kluwer, for which it paid about 575 million guilders.

Since then, Elsevier has pursued close cooperation or a merger with Wolters Kluwer, but the latter company has repeatedly rebuffed the advances. Because the certificates carry no voting rights, Elsevier's stake gave it very little influence. Nor were the financial rewards high: Elsevier collected about 19 million guilders of dividend income from Wolters Kluwer last year, producing a return of about 2% on the market value of the investment.

Elsevier's Mr. Alberti said his company decided to sell because of that low return and because it had failed to bring about cooperation. But he said Elsevier remains interested in closer ties with Wolters Kluwer, and even in the possibility of an eventual merger. Once Elsevier no longer carries the "big stick" of a 33% stake, he suggested, Wolters Kluwer might be more inclined to discuss cooperation.

Wolters Kluwer's chairman, Mijndert Ververs, hasn't ruled out the possibility of joint ventures with Elsevier, but he said yesterday that he was pleased with Elsevier's decision to sell its stake.

Elsevier, which had 1989 sales of 1.96 billion guilders, specializes in scientific, professional and trade publications and owns several Dutch newspapers. Wolters Kluwer, whose 1989 sales totaled 1.88 billion guilders, is a specialist in legal and tax publications.
