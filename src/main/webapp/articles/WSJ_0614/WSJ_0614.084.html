Politics & Policy: Estimate of Bailout Cost For Thrifts Is Increased

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/14/90

WASHINGTON -- The Congressional Budget Office increased its estimate of the cost -- excluding interest -- of resolving the savings-and-loan mess to $150 billion, about $20 billion more than projected by the Treasury Department. The projection is based on the assumption that about 1,700 S&Ls will eventually be seized by the government, and that losses will accelerate. The CBO in March had put the cost at $80 billion, based only on the thrifts under government control at that time. The Treasury has estimated the government may take control of roughly 1,000 thrifts.

Treasury Secretary Nicholas Brady will appear before the House Banking Committee today; he is expected to ask the Congress for more cash for the bailout effort. "The CBO estimate will be the subject of intense questioning by the committee," said committee Chairman Henry Gonzalez (D., Texas).

Yesterday, William Seidman, chairman of the Resolution Trust Corp., which must sell the failed S&Ls and their bad investments, repeated that the agency will run out of cash required to facilitate the transactions some time in the fourth quarter. The agency projects it will sell between 50 and 75 institutions, with roughly $40 billion in assets, in the July-September quarter. "Obviously, if we don't have the money, we can't continue," he said.
