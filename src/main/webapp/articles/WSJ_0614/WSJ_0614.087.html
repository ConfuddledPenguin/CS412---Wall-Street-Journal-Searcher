KPMG Peat Marwick Aims At China Joint Venture

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/14/90

NEW YORK -- KPMG Peat Marwick, the international accounting and consulting firm, said it expects to have the first Chinese joint accounting venture approved by the Beijing government. KPMG, which supervises KPMG Peat Marwick partnerships from here and Amsterdam, said it signed an agreement with the Chinese Institute of Certified Public Accountants to establish the firm by year end. The new firm will be called KPMG Peat Marwick China.

The new firm will provide audit services to local Chinese companies and to Sino-foreign joint ventures. Currently, all companies in China, including joint ventures with foreign partners, must be audited by Chinese accounting firms, KPMG said. If a joint venture uses a non-Chinese firm, the foreign partner must pay the entire accounting fee.

KPMG established its first office in China in 1983. The joint venture will function as a Chinese firm, KPMG said, providing tax, audit, consultancy and other services offered by international firms.
