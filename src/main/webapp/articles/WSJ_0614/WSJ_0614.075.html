Credit Ratings: S&P Lowers Ratings On Debt of Household International Unit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/14/90

PROSPECT HEIGHTS, Ill. -- Standard & Poor's Corp., citing the "continued rapid growth" of Household International Inc.'s "relatively weaker subsidiaries," lowered its ratings on the approximately $9 billion in debt of the company's main finance unit, Household Finance Corp. The rating concern said Household Finance is being burdened by supporting the growth of "these weaker operations," such as Household Bank, and the British subsidiary HFC Bank PLC. Household Finance also has "experienced diminished profitability, reduced loss-reserve coverage and somewhat aggressive debt leverage," S&P said. Household International and Household Finance have an agreement with thrift regulators to maintain 6% regulatory capital at Household Bank, and "the downgrade at the bank reflects the rating adjustment at Household Finance Corp.," S&P said.

S&P reduced its ratings on Household Finance's senior debt to single-A-plus from double-A-minus; subordinated debt to single-A from single-A-plus; preferred stock to single-A from single-A-plus, and commercial paper to single-A-1 from single-A-1-plus.

Ratings on Household Bank's certificates of deposit were lowered to what it listed as "single-A/A-" from "single-A-plus/A-1," which wasn't explained and S&P officials weren't available to elaborate, and the Household Financial Corp. unit's senior unsecured debt to single-A-plus from double-A-minus.

S&P affirmed its ratings on Household International, the parent company, one of the nation's biggest consumer finance concerns.

Household International said in a prepared statement that it is disappointed with the ratings downgrades for its units. "S&P questioned the growth of Household Bank, which we view as a strategic investment for future profitability," the company said. It also disputed S&P's statements that profitability has diminished at Household Finance.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Household International shares closed at $52.625, up 25 cents.

---

Corrections & Amplifications

STANDARD & POOR'S Corp. lowered its rating on certificates of deposit of Household International Inc.'s Household Bank unit to single-A/A-1 from single-A-plus/A-1. The new rating was misstated in yesterday's edition.

(WSJ June 15, 1990)
