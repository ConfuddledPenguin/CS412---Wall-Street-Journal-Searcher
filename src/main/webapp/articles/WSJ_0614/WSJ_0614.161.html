International Brief -- Pechiney International S.A.: Weak Dollar Seen Fostering Decline in Consolidated Net

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/13/90

The weakness of the dollar against the French franc is likely to foster a yet unspecified decline in Pechiney International S.A.'s consolidated net income for 1990, a spokesman for the French-controlled packaging and aluminum company said. The official said the anticipated decline would mainly reflect the fact that most of the company's revenue is in dollars while its annual accounts are expressed in francs. This is an important element because the U.S. currency has averaged 5.70 francs so far this year, compared with the 6.38-franc exchange rate used in its accounts for 1989, he said.

Another factor weighing on the company's results so far this year has been weak aluminum prices, which could trim results for this branch in dollar terms, the spokesman said. Narrowly lower profits are also anticipated from the group's electrical parts operations, he said, but added that it was too early to speculate on the scope of the anticipated declines because they hinge on the dollar's performance over the rest of the year.
