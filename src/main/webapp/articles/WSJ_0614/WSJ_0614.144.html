New Securities Issues

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/14/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATE

Coca-Cola Enterprises Inc. -- $250 million of 8.35% notes due June 20, 1995 priced at 97.351 to yield 9.02%. The noncallable notes were priced at a spread of 67 basis points above the Treasury's five-year note. Rated single-A-2 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and double-A-minus by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Salomon Brothers Inc.

Commercial Credit Corp. -- $100 million of 9.20% notes due June 15, 1995, priced at 99.899 to yield 9.225%. The non-callable notes were priced at 87.5 basis points above the Treasury's five-year note. Rated single-A-2 by Moody's and single-A-minus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Smith Barney, Harris Upham & Co.

MUNICIPAL

New York City -- $710 million of general obligation bonds due Feb. 15 and Aug. 1, 1991-2028, tentatively priced by a Merrill Lynch Capital Markets group to yield from 6.40% in 1991 to 7.90% in 2018-2028. There are no term bonds. The bonds are rated single-A-minus by S&P. The rating is still under review by Moody's, but a single-A rating is expected, according to an official with the lead underwriter.

Port Authority of New York and New Jersey -- $100 million of consolidated revenue bonds, 69th Series, due Dec. 1, 1991-1999, and June 1, 2025, with capital appreciation bonds due Dec. 1, 2000-2011, priced by a Merrill Lynch Capital Markets group to yield from 5.90% in 1991 to 7.28% in 2025. Current interest serial bonds are priced at par to yield from 5.90% in 1991 to 6.70% in 1999. The 6.80% serial bonds due in 1999 are priced at par. There is $27.8 million of 7 1/8% term bonds priced at 98 to yield 7.28% due June 1, 2025. Capital appreciation bonds are priced to yield from 6.80% in 2000 to 7.10% in 2007-2011. The bonds are rated single-A-1/double-A-minus.

Franklin County, Ohio -- $97.5 million of convention center revenue bonds due Dec. 1, 1994-1999, 2019 and 2020, with capital appreciation bonds due 2000-2010, tentatively priced by a PaineWebber Inc. group to yield from 6.30% in 1994 to 7.23% in 2019. Current interest serial bonds are priced at par to yield from 6.30% in 1994 to 6.80% in 1999. There are $52 million of 7% term bonds priced at 97 1/4 to yield 7.23% in 2019 and $8 million of 6% term bonds priced at 86 to yield 7.13% in 2020. Capital appreciation bonds are priced to yield from 6.90% in 2000 to 7.20% in 2008-2010. The bonds are insured and triple-A-rated.

Albuquerque, N.M. -- $88 million of Joint Water and Sewer System revenue bonds, Series 1990A, due July 1, 2015, with capital appreciation bonds due 2003-2008 and Series B, due 1991-2003 and 2006, tentatively priced by a Smith Barney group to yield from 6.15% in 1991 to 7.22% in 2015. Series A consists of $41.8 million of 6% bonds priced at 86 to yield 7.22% in 2015. Capital appreciation bonds are priced to yield from 7.10% in 2003 and 2004 to 7.20% in 2007 and 2008. Series B current interest serial bonds are priced to yield from 6.15% in 1992 to 7.05% in 2003. Serial bonds due in 1991 are not formally reoffered. There are $13.4 million of 7% term bonds priced at 98.58 to yield 7.15% in 2006. The bonds are rated single-A-1/double-A, except for capital appreciation bonds, which are insured and triple-A-rated.

Connecticut Housing Finance Authority -- $83.8 million of housing bonds due Nov. 15, 1995-2004 and 2009, tentatively priced by a Shearson Lehman Hutton Inc. group to yield from 6.50% in 1995 to 7.50% in 2009. Current interest serial bonds are priced at par to yield from 6.50% in 1995 to 7.40% in 2004. There are $24.2 million of 7 1/2% term bonds priced at par due in 2009. Ratings of double-A/double-A-plus are expected, according to an official with the lead underwriter. Interest on the bonds will be treated as a preference item in calculating the federal alternative minimum tax that may be imposed on certain investors.

Maryland-National Capital Park Commission -- $59.2 million of various purpose and refunding revenue bonds due 1991-2010. A Harris Trust & Savings Bank group placed the winning bid for $40 million of various purpose and refunding revenue bonds secured by Prince Georges County, Md. The bonds, rated double-A/double-A, were priced for reoffering to yield from 5.90% in 1991 to 7.00% in 2008-2010. The remainder of the issue, $19.2 million of various purpose revenue bonds secured by Montgomery County, Md., was won by a Merrill Lynch Capital Markets group. The bonds, rated triple-A, were priced for reoffering to yield from 6.00% in 1992 to 6.95% in 2008-2010. Bonds due in 1991 are not formally reoffered.

Rosemont, Ill. -- $59 million of general obligation corporate purpose bonds, 1990 Series A, due Dec. 1, 1995-2002, with Series B capital appreciation bonds due 1992-2006 and Series C capital appreciation bonds due 1991-2007, tentatively priced by a Smith Barney group to yield from 6.20% for bonds due in 1991 to 7.25% in 2006 and 2007. Series A consists of current interest serial bonds priced at par to yield from 6.40% in 1995 to 7.05% in 2002. Series B capital appreciation bonds are priced to yield to maturity from 6.30% in 1992 to 7.25% in 2006. Series C capital appreciation bonds are priced to yield from 6.20% in 1991 to 7.25% in 2006 and 2007. The bonds are insured and triple-A-rated.

MORTGAGES

Union Bank of Switzerland -- $300.2 million of Remic mortgage securities offered in nine classes by UBS Securities. The offering, Series UBS Trust 6, is backed by Ginnie Mae securities with a weighted average coupon rate of 10% and weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29.17 years. Price and yield data weren't immediately available.

EUROBONDS

Browning-Ferris Industries Inc. (U.S.) -- $350 million twotranche convertible subordinated debentures due July 18, 2005, via Credit Suisse First Boston for international tranche of $225 million); U.S. tranche via First Boston Inc. and Goldman Sachs. Indicated coupon between 6 3/4% and 7% at par issue price. Expected conversion premium 18% to 22% above share price when terms are fixed by next Wednesday. Callable after July 1993 at 103, declining by 1% to par in 1996. Exercise period Aug. 27, 1990, to maturity. Fees 2 1/2.

General Electric Capital Corp. (U.S. parent) -- $250 million of 9% Eurobonds due July 5, 1993, priced at 101.45 to yield 8.97% less fees, via Swiss Bank Corp. Fees 1 3/8.

Best Ltd. (Cayman Islands) -- $111.3 million of floatingrate notes due July 1993, priced at 100.10, via Sumitomo Trust International Ltd. Coupon at 1/4 point above six-month London interbank offered rate. Secured by 15 billion yen of dual currency depositary receipts issued by BCI and Cariplo via Law Debenture Trust Corp. Fees 0.01.

Banco di Sicilia (Italian parent) -- $100 million of subordinated floating-rate notes through Bankers Trustee Co. due June 2000, priced at par, via IBJ International. Coupon in first five years at 1/2 point above six-month Libor and thereafter at 5/8 point above Libor. Once-only call at end of fifth year at par. Otherwise, amortized at rate of 20% each year. Fees 0.25.

Caisse Nationale des Autoroutes (France) -- 225 million European currency units of 10 1/2% Eurobonds due July 17, 1995, priced at 101 3/4 to yield 10.53% less fees via Credit Lyonnais. Guarantee by France. Fees 1 7/8.

European Investment Bank (agency) -- 20 billion yen of 6 5/8% Eurobonds due March 15, 2000, priced at 100 3/8 via Nomura International PLC. Fully fungible 91 days after payment date with existing 40 billion yen deal launched Feb. 21. Fees 1 7/8.

Japan Development Bank (Japan) -- 150 million Canadian dollars of 12 1/8% Eurobonds due July 6, 1995, priced at 101.55 to yield 12.21% less fees via IBJ International Ltd. Guarantee by Japan. Fees 1 7/8.

World Bank (agency) -- 200 million marks of 9% Eurobonds due June 29, 2000, priced at 99.50 to yield 9.07%, via Commerzbank. Guarantee by World Bank. Callable from 1992 to 1995 at par. Fees 1 3/4.

General Motors Corp. (U.S.) -- 100 million Swiss francs of 7 1/4% privately placed notes due July 6, 1994, priced at 101 3/4, via Credit Suisse. Fees 1.38.
