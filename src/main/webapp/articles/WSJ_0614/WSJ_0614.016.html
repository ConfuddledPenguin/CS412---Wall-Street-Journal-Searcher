Chrysler Plant Improvements

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/14/90

HIGHLAND PARK, Mich. -- Chrysler Corp. said it will spend $17.7 million to improve its assembly plants in Newark, Del., and Sterling Heights, Mich. The plant improvements, scheduled to begin in early July, are intended to improve car quality and customer satisfaction. One improvement, called the "hot car concept," will supply electrical power to the cars as they move through the assembly process, allowing operators to verify performance of electrical parts and circuitry at the time of installation.

The Newark plant builds the Dodge Spirit and Plymouth Acclaim; the Sterling Heights plant builds the Dodge Shadow and Plymouth Sundance.
