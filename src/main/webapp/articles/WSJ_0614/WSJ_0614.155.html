Big Board Plans Trades After Hours

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/14/90

NEW YORK -- The New York Stock Exchange said it plans to start "after-hours" trading later this year, and it may expand to overnight trading in 1991. The plans for trading after traditional hours, under discussion for two years, marked the first step in the Big Board's slow evolution to electronic trading of stocks. The exchange's specialists, or floor traders assigned to make markets in individual stocks, won't participate physically in an after-hours scheme, but they may share in the revenue.

Current trading hours are from 9:30 a.m. to 4 p.m., Eastern time.

The plans are sure to stir unrest on the Big Board floor. Top specialists in recent weeks have said they didn't anticipate that the Big Board would soon go ahead with after-hours trading.

But now the exchange seems anxious to give the impression that it is moving forward in an area that many critics have said it has been slow to pursue.

The Big Board has seen an outflow of its traditional trading business to overseas and to U.S. regional exchanges. More important, the Big Board is facing strong competition from an increasing array of electronic networks and private dealers.

A Big Board spokesman said the exchange is prepared to "quickly put a system in place" for after-hours trading, assuming the market wants one.

The American Stock Exchange, industry executives said, also has a number of electronic trading plans in the works. It is close to announcing a new after-hours trading system for options.

Kenneth Leibler, president of the American exchange, said after-hours trading is "clearly a trend of the 1990s. I think globalization has been something widely discussed in the '80s, and it will happen in the '90s. Extended trading is probably something every market, depending on its product mix, will probably participate in."
