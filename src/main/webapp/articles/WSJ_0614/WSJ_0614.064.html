Letters to the Editor: You Could Get Hoist On the `Common Good'

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/14/90

In your May 15 editorial "Community Standards Elevated" you hail Judge Frank Altimari's opinion that begging in the subway "often amounts to nothing less than assault" on passengers. I don't know if you or the appeals judges actually ride the subways, but as a daily passenger I can say with total honesty that I've never felt "assaulted" or endangered by beggars. Annoyed, often; depressed, always; but assaulted, never. I'll tell you what does scare me a little: It's the notion of "common good." You're quick to raise this banner against a class of people you dislike, but think what might happen if it fell into the wrong hands, especially the hands of certain "L"-type people. Tougher emission standards, compulsory car pooling, seizure of warehoused apartments, more affirmative action, universal health insurance, all for the "common good." Is it really worth opening such a Pandora's box just to throw a few beggars out of the subway?

I've always admired your deft, totally unembarrassed use of double standards; it's one reason I find your editorials so much fun. But be careful how you handle principles, they may backfire.

Tim Aurthur

Long Island City, N.Y.
