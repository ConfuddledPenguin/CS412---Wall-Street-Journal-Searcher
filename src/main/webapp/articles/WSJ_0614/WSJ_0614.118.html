Reichmann Family To Boost Buy-Out Bid For GW Utilities Ltd.

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/14/90

TORONTO -- Toronto's Reichmann family said it will boost its offer to buy out minority shareholders of GW Utilities Ltd. to 40 Canadian dollars (US$34.13). A spokesman for the Reichmann's closely held holding company, Olympia & York Developments Ltd., said the offer was increased by C$4 a share "to reflect the potential increase in the value of the shares as a result of transactions announced since the going-private proposal was made."

In January, Olympia & York proposed to offer shareholders C$36 a share, subject to shareholder approval at a special meeting. The company already holds an 89.3% stake in GW Utilities.

Since then, GW Utilities has announced plans to tender its 82% stake in Consumers' Gas Co. to a C$1.1 billion bid by British Gas PLC. In April, Olympia & York announced a reshuffling of its other energy assets, which would see GW Utilities's subsidiary, Home Oil Co., merge with Gulf Canada Resources Ltd., 74.3% owned by Olympia & York.

If either of those transactions don't receive necessary shareholder and regulatory approvals, the Reichmann spokesman said Olympia & York "would come back with another privatization offer {for GW Utilities} similar to the first one."

In trading on the Toronto Stock Exchange yesterday, GW Utilities closed at C$35, up 88 Canadian cents.
