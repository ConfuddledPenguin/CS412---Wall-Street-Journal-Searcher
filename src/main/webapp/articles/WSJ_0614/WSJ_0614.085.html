Technology: Hitachi Will Make And Sell Chips Based On a Hewlett Design

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/14/90

TOKYO -- Hitachi Ltd. said it will make and sell microprocessors based on an advanced design by Hewlett-Packard Co., Palo Alto, Calif. The deal gives Hitachi, a major supplier of semiconductors, a leg up in the competition to supply a new class of microprocessors called reduced instruction set computing, or Risc, chips. These microprocessors, which form the heart of computers or control devices, are generally more powerful than traditional microprocessors.

Hewlett-Packard said last year it would try to market its Risc chips, which are used only in the company's own computers. Last July, Hewlett-Packard and Hitachi agreed to develop jointly a high-speed Risc chip.

"This is a big boost for H-P," Andrew Allison, an independent computer analyst, said of Hitachi's announcement. "It puts the world on notice that H-P is serious" about broadening the use of its Risc chips. By doing so, Hewlett-Packard can encourage more easily software outsiders to develop software for its Risc chip, which in turn will benefit sales of its computers.

The arrangement should help Hitachi compete better against Intel Corp. and Motorola Inc., U.S. firms that also are seeking to supply Risc chips, Mr. Allison said.
