Credit Ratings: Moody's Downgrades Ratings on Security Pacific, Wells Fargo

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/14/90

NEW YORK -- Moody's Investors Service Inc. said it lowered its ratings on debt of two of the nation's biggest banks, Security Pacific Corp., Los Angeles, and Wells Fargo & Co., San Francisco, because of worries about real estate and leveraged buy-outs. The rating concern downgraded five Security Pacific debt issues or securities, including its rating on the company's senior debt to single-A-1 from double-A-3. In all, $7.4 billion in Security Pacific's parent-company securities and debt issues are affected. Moody's also reduced its ratings on certain debt of several Security Pacific subsidiaries.

Moody's downgraded $3.2 billion in Wells Fargo debt and securities, among them senior debt, which went to single-A-1 from double-A-3. Moody's also lowered its rating on some debt of Wells Fargo subsidiaries.

Moody's, citing softening in the California real estate market, said both Security Pacific and Wells Fargo may have to boost reserves for real estate losses. And it said both companies may be exposed to losses from lending on leveraged buy-outs; several buy-outs have fallen apart lately, because their debt loads proved too high. In a leveraged buy-out, a small group of investors acquires a company largely with debt that ultimately is paid with funds generated by the acquired company.

Officials of Security Pacific and Wells Fargo couldn't be reached to comment on the Moody's actions.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Security Pacific shares closed at $40.375, down 75 cents, and Wells Fargo shares closed at $81.625, off $2.375.

Robert Smith, president and chief executive officer of Security Pacific, said the banking concern "strongly disagrees" with Moody's lowering of its debt. "We have maintained a well-balanced loan portfolio consistent with demands of the marketplace, and with adequate and appropriate levels of reserves." Wells Fargo couldn't be reached for comment.
