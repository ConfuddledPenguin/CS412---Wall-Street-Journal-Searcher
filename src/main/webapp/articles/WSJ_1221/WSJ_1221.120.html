IBM-Sears Venture, Prodigy, to Pay Texas Costs of Ad Inquiry

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/21/90

AUSTIN, Texas -- The Prodigy electronic network has agreed to pay $12,500 to the Texas Attorney General's office and refund money to certain Texas subscribers who may have been misled by its ads. Prodigy, a White Plains, N.Y., joint venture of the Armonk, N.Y., computer company International Business Machines Corp. and the Chicago retailer Sears, Roebuck & Co., said in September that it would charge subscribers a fee of 25 cents for each personal electronic mail message in excess of 30 per month. In the same month, Prodigy mailed about 700,000 ads to Texans, saying electronic mail was free, the attorney general's office said.

A Prodigy spokesman said the increased fees were accurately portrayed to customers and necessary because some network users were sending out thousands of promotional messages. Prodigy agreed to settle because charges filed under the state's deceptive trade practices act would have been too expensive to defend against, the spokesman said. Prodigy expects to refund only a few thousand dollars to users. The payment to the Attorney General's office is intended to reimburse the agency for investigative costs.

Last month, Prodigy banned at least seven people who had protested an increase in its electronic-mail charges. Several of the banned individuals organized a dissident group that collected 19,000 supporters.
