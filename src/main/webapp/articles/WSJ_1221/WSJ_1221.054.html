Wheeling-Pittsburgh Payment Will Bring Cleveland-Cliffs Gain

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/21/90

CLEVELAND -- Cleveland-Cliffs Inc. said it expects to post a fourth-quarter gain of $47.1 million, or $4.03 a share, as part of the reorganization of Wheeling-Pittsburgh Steel Co. The gain reflects cash and stock payments to both Cleveland-Cliffs and a Canadian iron ore joint venture managed by the iron ore concern. In the 1989 fourth quarter, Cleveland-Cliffs earned $13.3 million, or $1.12 a share.

Wheeling-Pittsburgh's reorganization plan was confirmed this week in a federal bankruptcy court. Distributions to creditors such as Cleveland-Cliffs will follow a 10-day appeal period.

Cleveland-Cliffs said pretax receipts are expected to consist of $56 million in cash and 968,889 new Wheeling-Pittsburgh common shares valued at $10.9 million. Wabush Mines, a Canadian iron ore joint venture managed by Cleveland-Cliffs, is expected to receive $39.7 million, consisting of $33.3 million in cash and 568,889 shares. Cleveland-Cliffs' indirect share will be $2.9 million after tax, which will be retained in Wabush to fund capital expenditures including a major emission-control project.

Cleveland-Cliffs said its claims reflect damages incurred when Wheeling-Pittsburgh rejected certain ore purchase contracts and partnership obligations at the Tilden mine, a Michigan iron ore facility managed by Cleveland-Cliffs, in 1985.
