SKF Doesn't Expect Any 4th-Quarter Profit

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 12/21/90

STOCKHOLM -- SKF AB, the Swedish rolling bearing group, said it doesn't anticipate any profit for the last quarter of 1990, following a decline in industrial production and weakened sales. A statement from the group said the "fourth quarter is not expected to contribute to the group's income for 1990." The company said adverse market conditions had prompted the company to substantially reduce production in order to adjust to the lower level of demand.

At the nine-month interim period, the company reported earnings after financial items of 1.78 billion kronor ($318.7 million), which, following the group's announcement, is probably what the group expects full-year results to be. The figure represents a 31% drop from 1989, when the group's profit after financial items was 2.59 billion kronor.
