General Dynamics, McDonnel Douglas Issue Layoff Warning

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/21/90

ST. LOUIS -- McDonnell Douglas Corp. and General Dynamics Corp. said they are notifying a total of 8,000 employees -- as early as Jan. 2 if the troubled A-12 Navy attack plane program is canceled. The announcements came as the two aerospace concerns negotiate with the Navy and aides to Defense Secretary Dick Cheney to try to salvage the program, which has been plagued by engineering snafus and cost overruns. The Pentagon threatened to cancel the program, and earlier this week gave the contractors until Jan. 2 to come up with a plan to solve the problems.

Yet despite the public threats, all parties appear to be working toward a compromise that would probably slow development of the A-12 and impose some kind of financial penalty on the two companies for past mistakes, but still keep the plane alive.

McDonnell Douglas's McDonnell Aircraft Co. unit has about 6,000 employees in the program, mostly in St. Louis, and General Dynamics has 4,000, mostly in Fort Worth, Texas. Both companies said the employees being notified of the potential layoffs won't be limited to those working on the A-12.
