Letters to the Editor: Cyrano Author Failed To Nose Out the Bard

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/21/90

In the review of the Gerard Depardieu film of "Cyrano de Bergerac" (Leisure & Arts, Nov. 29), Julie Salamon claims that Edmond Rostand wrote the play in 1870. That would make the author two years old at the time, as he was born in 1868. The right date should have been given as 1897. Andre Gide, in an entry in his journal about that time, wrote that some people in France were comparing Rostand with Shakespeare, adding the quip, "Every generation has the Shakespeare it deserves." Perhaps Gide, who later recommended rejecting Proust's great novel when he was a reader for a publishing house, was too harsh in judging Rostand, who after all wrote one of the few 19th-century plays still performed frequently in France. Incidentally, in connection with the current French film several newspaper articles have referred to the Jose Ferrer English-language version without mentioning the earlier French film with Claude Dauphin (which I saw in Montreal in 1949 and probably was not shown commercially in the U.S.).

Ralph De Gorog

Professor Emeritus

Romance Philology

University of Georgia

Athens, Ga.
