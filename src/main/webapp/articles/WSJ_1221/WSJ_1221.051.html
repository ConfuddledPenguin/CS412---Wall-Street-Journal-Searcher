New Securities Issues

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/21/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: MUNICIPAL

California Health Facilities Financing Authority -- $145 million of revenue bonds, 1990 Series A (Kaiser Permanente issue), due Dec. 1, 1993-2002, 2010 and 2020, priced by a PaineWebber Inc. group to yield from 5.75% in 1993 to 7.10% in 2020. Current interest serial bonds are priced to yield from 5.75% in 1993 to 6.75% in 2002. There are $42.2 million of 7% term bonds priced at 99 to yield 7.09% in 2010 and $90 million of 6 1/2% term bonds priced at 92 5/8 to yield 7.10% in 2020. The bonds are rated double-A-2 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and double-A by Standard & Poor's Corp.

Milwaukee Metropolitan Sewerage District -- $100 million of 5 3/4% general obligation capital purpose notes due Jan. 7, 1992, tentatively priced by a Chemical Securities Inc. group to yield 5.30%.

Babylon Industrial Development Agency, N.Y. -- $55.5 million of variable rate demand notes due July 1, 2010, tentatively priced by a Donaldson Lufkin Jenrette Securites Corp. group to yield 3.90% in 2010. The notes are backed by a letter of credit from Credit Local de France and carry a longterm rating of triple-A and a short-term rating of VMig-1 from Moody's. The notes are not rated by S&P.

Illinois Development Finance Authority -- $51.7 million of Waste Management Inc. 7 1/8% project revenue bonds due Jan. 1, 2001, tentatively priced at par by a Merrill Lynch Capital Markets group. Interest on the bonds will be treated as a preference item in calculating the federal alternative minimum tax that may be imposed on certain investors.

ASSET-BACKED

Chase Manhattan Credit Card Trust -- $500 million of 8.55% asset-backed certificates that Moody's said are backed by MasterCard and Visa receivables originated by Chase Manhattan Bank (USA), primarily through prescreened direct mail solicitations. Moody's said it assigned a rating of triple-A to the certificates based on the quality of the receivables, an 11% letter of credit furnished by Union Bank of Switzerland, an assessment of the transaction's structure and the "experience and expertise" of Chase Manhattan Bank (USA) as seller and servicer. Chase Manhattan Bank (USA) is a subsidiary of Chase Manhattan Corp. The transaction is underwritten by Chase Securities Inc., Moody's said.

Hibernia National Bank 1990-A Grantor Trust -- $248.4 million of 8.30% securities backed by auto and light truck retail installment sales contracts sold and serviced by Hibernia National Bank, New Orleans, offered through First Boston Corp. at 99 21/32 to yield 8.459%. The yield is 125 basis points above the Treasury's two-year note. Underwriters expect the offering to be rated triple-A by Moody's based on the quality of the receivables and a letter of credit from Union Bank of Switzerland covering 13% of the deal. An average life of 1.62 years is expected, based on annual prepayments of 1.3% of the original loan balance. Legal final maturity is Dec. 15, 1995. Hibernia National is a unit of Hibernia Corp., New Orleans.

EUROBONDS

DynaWorld Bank & Trust Co. (Mexican parent) -- $50 million of 10 1/2% Eurobonds due Jan. 17, 1996, priced at 93.75 via Yamaichi International Ltd. Guaranteed by Banca Serfin. Investors have an option to put the bonds after the third year at 96.35. Registered and in bearer form. Fees 1 3/8.

Tokyo Tatemono Co. (Japan) -- 20 billion yen of 7.4% Eurobonds due April 23, 1996, priced at 101.15 via Yamaichi International Ltd. Short first coupon; payment date is Jan. 23, 1991. Fees 1 7/8.

Tokyo Tatemono Co. (Japan) -- 10 billion yen of 7 1/4% Eurobonds due April 23, 2001, priced at 100 3/4 via Daiwa Europe Ltd. Payment date is Jan. 23, 1991. Fees 2.
