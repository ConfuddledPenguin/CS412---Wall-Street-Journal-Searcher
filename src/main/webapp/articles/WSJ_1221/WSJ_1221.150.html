Business Brief -- Sprague Technologies Inc.: Sanken Electric Completes Purchase of Chip Business

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/21/90

Sprague Technologies Inc. said it completed the sale of its semiconductor business and as a result will report a loss from discontinued operations of about $49 million in the fourth quarter. In the year-ago quarter, Sprague had a net loss of $96.8 million. The company makes capacitors and other electronic parts.

"The good news is that they finally found someone to buy this business for $58 million in cash, which is a lot of money for Sprague and offsets their existing debt," said Charles Neuhauser, an analyst at Legg Mason Wood Walker Inc. in Baltimore.

Sprague, based in Stamford, Conn., said it sold its semiconductor business to Sanken Electric Co. of Japan as part of its plan to divest certain unprofitable businesses. The company said the tax loss from the sale will give it a net operating loss carry-forward of about $145 million at year end.

Sprague said it intends to use a portion of the proceeds to repay bank borrowings. In addition, the company said about $30 million to $40 million of cash and debt capacity will be available for further investment in new products and process improvements for the core businesses.
