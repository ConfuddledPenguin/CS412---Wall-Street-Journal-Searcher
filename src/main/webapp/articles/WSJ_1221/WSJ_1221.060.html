Marketing & Media: Matsushita Says Backlash May Kill MCA Purchase

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/21/90

TOKYO -- Matsushita Electric Industrial Co. said it was so concerned about U.S. reaction to its purchase of MCA Inc. that it was prepared to end negotiations if a major political backlash occurred. Masahiko Hirata, the Matsushita executive vice president who led the company's talks with MCA, said at a news conference that Matsushita told MCA that if anti-Japanese sentiment "was going to become a very big issue, we weren't going to proceed" with the negotiations.

Mr. Hirata said Matsushita's specific plans for the U.S. entertainment company, which it agreed last month to acquire for about $66 a share, or about $6.59 billion, won't be completed until the takeover is officially finalized, probably early next month. He reiterated that a main reason for the MCA purchase was to strengthen development of Matsushita's high-definition television hardware.

Matsushita has consistently maintained that it will take a hands-off approach in managing MCA. While both companies will form an executive committee to devise strategies, Mr. Hirata said, only a few Matsushita employees will be transferred to MCA's California headquarters. Akio Tanii, Matsushita's 62-year-old president, recently said the company won't interfere with the creative independence of MCA's film producers.

Corrections & Amplifications

MATSUSHITA ELECTRIC INDUSTRIAL Co. had been concerned, before completing negotiations last month, that a political backlash in the U.S. could kill its pending acquisition of MCA Inc. This was misstated in a headline on an article in Friday's edition.

(WSJ Dec. 24, 1990)
