Credit Ratings: S&P Downgrades Debt Of C&S/Sovran Units

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/21/90

ATLANTA -- Standard & Poor's Corp. said it downgraded its rating on the senior debt of Citizens & Southern Corp. and Sovran Financial Corp. to single-A from single-A-plus. Both banking companies are units of C&S/Sovran Corp.

The rating concern also said it lowered its ratings on the companies' subordinated debt to single-A-minus from single-A and on Citizens & Southern's preferred stock to triple-B-plus from single-A-minus.

S&P said its action affects about $625 million of long-term debt and results from, among other things, problems in the banks' commercial real estate portfolios.

S&P also downgraded to single-A-plus from double-A-minus/single-A-1-plus the certificates of deposit and letter of credit-backed issues of Citizens & Southern National Bank, Atlanta; Citizens & Southern National Bank, South Carolina; Sovran Bank, Richomond, Va.; and Sovran Bank/Maryland.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, C&S/Sovran shares closed at $15.875, down 12.5 cents.
