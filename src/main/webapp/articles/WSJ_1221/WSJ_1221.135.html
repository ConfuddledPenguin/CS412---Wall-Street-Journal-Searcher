U.S. Reserve Assets In November Rose To $83.06 Billion

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/21/90

WASHINGTON -- U.S. reserve assets rose $207 million in November to $83.06 billion. The latest figure compares with the $72.57 billion level of reserve assets in November 1989, the Treasury said. Last month's rise followed a $2.83 billion rise in October.

U.S. reserve assets consist of foreign currencies, gold, special drawing rights at the International Monetary Fund and the U.S. reserve position at the IMF.

The Treasury report showed U.S. holdings of foreign currencies rose $220 million in November to $52.07 billion. That's up $9.37 billion from November 1989.

The U.S. gold stock was down $1 million in November to $11.06 billion from the prior month. A year earlier, it was at about the same level.

The country's ability to draw foreign currency from the International Monetary Fund decreased $195 million in November to $8.87 billion. That is down from $9.06 billion a year earlier.

U.S. holdings of IMF special drawing rights were up $183 million last month to $11.06 billion. That compares with $9.75 billion a year earlier.
