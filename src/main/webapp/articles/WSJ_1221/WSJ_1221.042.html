Sweden Eases Restrictions On Foreign Bank Branches

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 12/21/90

STOCKHOLM -- The Riksbank, Sweden's central bank, decided to allow foreign banks with branches in Sweden to participate in the Riksbank clearing and to make overnight borrowings in the Riksbank according to the same rules that apply to domestic banks. The decision means foreign bank branches can operate in Sweden under the same conditions as Swedish banks.

Participation in the Riksbank clearing and permission to borrow from the Riksbank had been allowed only to Swedish banking companies.

The decision further opens the Swedish banking system to foreign banks. In August, foreign bank branches gained permission to operate in Sweden. Before that, only subsidiaries of foreign banks were permitted.
