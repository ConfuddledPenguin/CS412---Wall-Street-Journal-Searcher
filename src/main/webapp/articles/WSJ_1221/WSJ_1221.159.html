Business Brief -- ABB Group: Unit Revises Agreement To Sell Kaolin Business

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/21/90

Asea Brown Boveri Inc. said a unit revised its agreement to sell its Georgia Kaolin business to ECC Group PLC, after discussions with the U.S. Justice Department. The Justice Department had been reviewing the agreement in light of antitrust regulations.

The revised agreement calls for the ABB unit to sell the major part of the Georgia Kaolin business to ECC for about $340 million, with ABB retaining one facility known as Dry Branch. The Dry Branch facility will have a capacity to produce 800,000 tons of kaolin a year. Its 1991 revenue is estimated at about $80 million; ABB said profitability is expected to increase when the plant is operated as an independent unit. Kaolin is used as a coating and filler in producing high quality paper.

As reported, the initial purchase price last May was about $520 million for the entire Georgia Kaolin business. ABB acquired the Georgia Kaolin business when it acquired Combustion Engineering Inc. about one year ago. Georgia Kaolin had 1989 revenue of $170 million.

Asea Brown Boveri, Stamford, Conn., is part of ABB Group, Zurich, the world's largest electrical engineering concern with annual sales of about $25 billion. Asea Brown Boveri's products and services include those for the power, process, industrial automation and environmental control markets. It has about $6 billion in sales in the U.S.

ECC Group, Cornwall, England, is a producer of white pigments with annual sales of about $1.6 billion.
