Business Brief -- Newmont Mining Corp.: Gold Unit Will Produce 1.5 Million Ounces in 1991

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/21/90

Newmont Mining Corp. told analysts in New York that its gold unit expects to produce 1.5 million ounces in 1991, down about 200,000 ounces from this year. The company had estimated it would produce 1.54 million ounces of gold in 1990, but that projection was increased to 1.7 million ounces in July. Production in 1990 rose because an old leach pad at the Gold Quarry mine produced more than the company expected in 1990. Gordon R. Parker, chairman and chief executive officer, told analysts that the leach pad can't be expected to do the same in 1991.

Mr. Parker said 1991 cash costs for gold production will be under $240 an ounce, compared with $215 in 1990. Energy, labor and material costs will increase, he said.
