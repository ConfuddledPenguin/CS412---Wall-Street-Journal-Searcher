Business Brief -- CML Group Inc.: Irwin Jacobs Sells His Stake Of 14.6%, or 930,000 Shares

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/21/90

Minneapolis investor Irwin Jacobs has sold his entire position, approximately 930,000 shares or 14.6% of the common stock, in CML Group Inc., a direct marketer of consumer products. Mr. Jacobs, who abandoned his bid to take over the company in April, said he sold his stake before year end for tax purposes, according to a statement issued by CML. The purchaser, Shearson Lehman Brothers Inc., then resold the stock to retail and institutional customers.

"For CML, it takes care of two concerns," said Mark Perlgut, a spokesman for CML. "Many investors have stayed away from the stock because they were unsure about what Irwin Jacobs would do with his shares and some were concerned about what it would do to the Acton, Mass., company's liquidity if Mr. Jacobs had to unload the stock in a large block at a low price."

Sources involved in the transaction said Mr. Jacobs sold the stock for approximately the average price of his purchases over the past year, about $18 dollars a share. Mr. Jacobs representatives would not comment on the sale.

In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange yesterday, CML shares closed at $18.375, down $62.5 cents.
