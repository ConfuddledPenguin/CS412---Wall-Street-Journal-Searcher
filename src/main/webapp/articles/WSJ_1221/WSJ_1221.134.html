Chief Executive Quits As Nordbanken Faces Growing Loan Losses

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/21/90

STOCKHOLM -- The chief executive officer of Sweden's second-largest bank resigned suddenly, intensifying questions about its recent lackluster financial performance. Nordbanken's Rune Barneus, 52, was respected as an unorthodox but successful banker. But his reputation had been tarnished in recent months by an acrimonious public battle with the Swedish Post Office and ballooning loan losses that forced Nordbanken to slash its forecast for fullyear pretax profit by 1.2 billion Swedish kronor ($214.9 million), or 37%, late last month.

In a statement, Nordbanken's chairman Tony Hagstroem called those credit losses "disturbing" and added that they made Mr. Barneus's position "difficult." But the bank, which is 71%-controlled by the Swedish government, offered no other explanation for the resignation.

Nordbanken's board was scheduled to meet this afternoon to discuss a possible successor.

A year ago, Mr. Barneus reached the pinnacle of his career to date. Nordbanken, under its previous name PK Banken, paid 5.6 billion kronor to acquire Nordbanken, a medium-sized regional bank of which Mr. Barneus was president. PK Banken's previous president, Christer Zetterberg, has been lured away to head automaker AB Volvo. And gaining the services of Mr. Barneus -- a former senior executive at PK Banken -- was a key to the Nordbanken purchase.
