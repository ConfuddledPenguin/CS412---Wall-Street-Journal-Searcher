Abreast of the Market: Stocks Close Mixed After Dropping On News of Shevardnadze's Move

  • Author: Craig S. Smith
  • Date: 12/21/90

NEW YORK -- The stock market ended little changed, having recovered from an opening drop on news of Soviet Foreign Minister Eduard Shevardnadze's sudden resignation. Mr. Shevardnadze quit amid growing domestic turmoil in the Soviet Union, warning that the country was slipping toward dictatorship.

European markets tumbled on the news, and the stock market here opened on a sharp downdraft. The Dow Jones Industrial Average plummeted 26 points before stabilizing and then climbing back to positive territory by noon.

The average ended with a 2.73-point gain at 2629.46.

"They keep driving the market down but it snaps right back," said Richard Eakle, president of Eakle Associates.

While the Shevardnadze news darkened the political backdrop, traders say the market recovered as implacable portfolio managers continued their year-end buying.

"A lot of people are doing a little dressing up today," said Edward J. Shopkorn, director of equity trading at Mabon Nugent. Mr. Shopkorn said the money managers were putting excess cash to work by buying highly liquid large-capitalized stocks such as International Business Machines, which in turn helped send the industrial average higher. IBM rose 1 to 113 3/4.

The broader market remained fractionally lower, however. Standard & Poor's 500-Stock Index eased 0.08 to 330.12 and the New York Stock Exchange Composite Index slipped 0.03 to 180.32.

Decliners led advancers, 813 to 726, with 507 stocks ending unchanged. Volume totaled 174,700,000 shares, compared with 180.4 million Wednesday.

Dudley A. Eppel, senior vice president of equity trading at Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette, said trading volume should jump today as monthly futures contracts and options expire. Otherwise, said Mr. Eppel, the market should continue to drift as the end of the year approaches.

Aside from mounting war worries over the Middle East, Mr. Eakle of Eakle Associates said the stock market has some things working in its favor that mitigate the downside risk in share prices.

"Oil is still headed south, as are interest rates, and there is a perception that inflation is receding," he said.

Crude oil for February delivery fell 54 cents yesterday to $26.39 a barrel.

Mr. Eakle also said that the internal dynamics of the stock market are showing signs of strength. Trading volume is increasing on days that the market rises, he said, while the number of stocks that hit new lows each day is decreasing.

Some banking shares rallied again. The group has been strong since the Federal Reserve Board cut the discount rate to 6.5% from 7% on Tuesday.

Manufacturers Hanover jumped 1 5/8 to 24 1/8 on heavy volume that included some dividend-related trading. Manufacturers said on Tuesday that it would pay its regular 82-cent dividend to stock of record Jan. 2.

Citicorp gained 1/2 to 13 3/4, BankAmerica rose 1 3/8 to 28, J.P. Morgan added 7/8 to 46, and Bankers Trust rose 5/8 to 45 1/4.

First Chicago, whose First National Bank of Chicago unit cut its prime lending rate by 50 basis points to 9.5%, closed unchanged at 18 1/2.

Chase Manhattan was left behind, however. The stock fell 3/8 to 10 3/4.

Travelers finished up 1/8 to 17 1/8 on volume of almost two million shares. Property/casualty insurer Continental Corp. has acquired at least 3% to 3.5% of Travelers' stock, according to USA Today. A 634,500-share block, representing about 0.6% of Travelers' outstanding shares, changed hands shortly after noon.

Sprague Technologies added 3/8 to 3 7/8. The company said it completed the sale of its semiconductor group to Sanken Electric for $58 million in cash. Sprague said the sale will result in a charge of about $49 million in the fourth quarter. Proceeds will be used to repay bank borrowings and free up capital for further investment.

Symbol Technologies added 7/8 to 12 1/4. NCR decided to withdraw from the market a hand-held laser scanner that Symbol claimed infringed on its patents. Symbol said it dismissed its pending patent-infringement suit against NCR.

Convex Computer rose 7/8 to 11 5/8. The company said it installed one of its supercomputers at the University of Houston.

Urcarco fell 3/4 to 3 7/8. Merrill Lynch downgraded its rating on the stock Wednesday and cut earnings estimates for the company.

Rollins Environmental Services added 5/8 to 9 3/8. First Boston is recommending that its clients buy the stock. Rollins says maintenance at its Deer Park, Texas, incinerator has been completed and the facility became operational Wednesday.

Rupert Murdoch's News Corp. dropped 7/8 to 7 1/2. An article in yesterday's New York Times highlighted the company's efforts to reschedule a mountain of short-term debt. A number of small banks are refusing to go along, raising fears a settlement could be weeks, even months, away, the Times said.

L.A. Gear closed unchanged at 15. The company said late Wednesday that it completed a $360 million credit facility arranged by Bank of America as agent for itself and a syndicate of domestic and international banks.

Hunt Manufacturing added 1 5/8 to 12 1/4. The company boosted its quarterly dividend to eight cents a share from 7.75 cents.

Coca-Cola Enterprises added 1/4 to 15 3/8. The company said its board approved a program to buy back as many as 15 million common shares. The company said, however, that it doesn't expect to buy back any shares under the program until the second or third quarter of 1991.

The American Stock Exchange Index fell 0.16 to 306.49.
