International: Angola Will Rejoin De Beers's Diamond Sales Organization

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/21/90

LONDON (AP) -- Angola, a major diamond producing nation, is re-entering the De Beers Centenary AG diamond powerhouse, ending a five-year absence, De Beers said. Endiama, the Angolan state diamond company, and De Beers agreed to re-establish their relationship, De Beers said. Angola has been marketing its diamonds independently since 1985, after civil war prompted De Beers to pull out, a De Beers spokesman said.

Under the agreement, De Beer's London-based Central Selling Organization will market Angola's diamonds from its Cuango region, or about three-quarters of the country's annual production of one million carats, the De Beers spokesman said.

De Beers also will provide Endiama with a $50 million loan to increase production in the Cuango area, and an additional $50 million over the next five years to develop other diamond sources in Angola.

About 80% of the world's annual diamond production is sorted and sold by De Beers's Central Selling Organization, which had diamond sales of $4.1 billion in 1989.
