Business Brief -- Canadian Pacific Ltd.: Strikes End at Two Mills, Workers Ratify Contracts

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/21/90

A Canadian Pacific Forest Products Ltd. official said workers at its Gatineau, Quebec and Thunder Bay, Ontario, newsprint mills ratified new contracts, ending lengthy strikes. The CP Forest official said a tentative contract agreement was reached at its Dalhousie, Nova Scotia, mill and workers there will vote on the pact within the next few days. The three CP Forest mills have a combined annual capacity of about 1.3 million metric tons, or about 8% of North American newsprint capacity. A metric ton equals about 2,204.6 pounds.

The CP Forest said the Gatineau and Thunder Bay mills, both shut by strikes in September and October respectively, will return to full operation by Jan. 2. CP Forest, Montreal, a pulp and paper concern, is 80%-owned by Canadian Pacific Ltd., a natural resources, transportation, communications and real estate concern. Both companies are based here.
