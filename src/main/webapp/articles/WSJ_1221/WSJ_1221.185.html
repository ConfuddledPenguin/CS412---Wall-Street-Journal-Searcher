What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/21/90

PRUDENTIAL-BACHE will post a $250 million loss for 1990, one of the worst performances ever by a U.S. brokerage firm. Its parent, Prudential Insurance, is adding more than $200 million in new capital to bolster the sagging firm. The big loss also raised new questions about how long George Ball will remain as chairman of Prudential-Bache. ---

Merck disclosed that it developed a novel type of drug to fight the AIDS virus and that it began pilot tests of the drug in humans last month.

---

Rostenkowski said he no longer plans to reintroduce early in January a proposed surtax on millionaires. President Bush, meantime, said he won't include his twice-failed proposal to cut capital-gains taxes as part of his fiscal 1992 budget request.

---

The Big Board's new chairman, William Donaldson, wants to greatly expand the number of foreign stocks traded on the exchange. Mr. Donaldson will take office Jan. 1.

---

The dollar surged against other major currencies amid a growing threat of political instability in the Soviet Union. U.S. markets mostly shrugged off the developments.

---

Sunbelt Savings' ex-owner, Edwin T. McBirney III, is expected to plead guilty today to federal criminal charges, less than 24 hours after a jury convicted Don R. Dixon, ex-owner of Vernon S&L in Dallas.

---

Bank of New England bondholders tentatively agreed to a debt-for-equity swap that would give them 92% of the ailing bank's common stock.

Bank of Boston said it will have a "substantial loss" in the fourth quarter, ensuring a loss for the full year.

---

BankAmerica plans to bid to acquire failed thrifts in Texas, New Mexico and California as part of its effort to dominate retail banking in the West, company executives said.

---

California's Supreme Court issued two rulings that could limit employees' ability to seek damages for emotional distress stemming from wrongful discharge. But another ruling aids employees in some cases.

---

Consumer spending inched up 0.1% in November as incomes increased only slightly, suggesting consumers won't contribute to economic strength in the next few months.

---

Short interest showed little change on both the Big Board and Amex in the month ended Dec. 14, after rising strongly for the past year.

---

GE was selected to design the engine for Japan's controversial FSX fighter plane over United Technologies' Pratt & Whitney division.

---

Midwest manufacturers are feeling the pinch of the weak economy, largely due to auto industry cutbacks.

---

Capital spending plans are being scaled back. In a government survey, businesses said they plan to raise spending a cautious 2.4% in 1991.
