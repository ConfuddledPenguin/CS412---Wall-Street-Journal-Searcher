Who's News: PaineWebber Group's Logan Quits as Head Of Institutional Line

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/21/90

NEW YORK -- Kent A. Logan, PaineWebber Group Inc.'s managing director in charge of institutional customers, resigned from the firm. Mr. Logan, who couldn't immediately be reached for comment, in recent years made a name for himself among institutional investors. PaineWebber has been one of the main Wall Street brokerage firms to overhaul the way it charges big institutional investors for stock trading and research -- for example, by taking away free amenities from less-active investors. Mr. Logan had emerged as one of Wall Street's spokesmen on such changes in the institutional equity business.

PaineWebber this year overhauled its capital markets operations, consolidating all its sales, trading and research operations for stocks and bonds under one umbrella headed by Charles Milligan and Margo Alexander. As a result, the authority of some employees was cut. In August, PaineWebber's head of takeover-stock trading, William Kaye, resigned partly for this reason.

A PaineWebber spokeswoman confirmed Mr. Logan has left the firm this week, but declined further comment. A successor to Mr. Logan hasn't been named.
