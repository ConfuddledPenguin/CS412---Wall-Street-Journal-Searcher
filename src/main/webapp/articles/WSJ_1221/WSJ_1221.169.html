Business Brief -- Shaw Brothers (Hong Kong) Ltd.: Firm's Net Income Fell 27% For Half From Write-Down

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/21/90

Shaw Brothers (Hong Kong) Ltd. said net income for the six months ended Sept. 30 tumbled 27% to 54 million Hong Kong dollars (US$6.9 million) from HK$74 million a year earlier. For the period, the film and property group controlled by Sir Run Run Shaw had a write-down of HK$34 million on its investment in Hong Kong Cable Communications Ltd., the local cable-TV franchise that was abandoned last month by its five partners.

Excluding the write-down, the company said profit climbed 19% to HK$88 million. Earnings per share dropped to 13.62 Hong Kong cents a share from 18.46 Hong Kong cents a share, while revenue grew 8.7% to HK$100 million from HK$92 million.

Group directors proposed an interim dividend of seven cents a share, unchanged from a year earlier.

Shaw Brothers attributed the growth in operating profit to an increased contribution from its Singapore property arm, following the renovation of a commercial building it owns there.
