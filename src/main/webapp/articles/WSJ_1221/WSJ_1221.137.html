Justice Department Plans Review of TWA-AMR Deal

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/21/90

WASHINGTON (AP) -- The Justice Department said it would review a proposed $625 million deal between TWA and American Airlines to determine whether the sale of international routes and airport facilities would violate antitrust laws. The department made its announcement after receiving a request for an antitrust inquiry from Sen. John Danforth (R., Mo.) who opposes the sale.

Sen. Danforth, in a letter to Attorney General Dick Thornburgh, said the proposed deal "inevitably will lead to a dramatic reduction in domestic airline competition."

Danforth is the senior Republican on the Senate Commerce Committee, which handles airline issues, and has been a leading critic of consolidation in the airline industry. Trans World Airlines Inc. has a major hub of operation at the St. Louis airport.

AMR Corp.'s American Airlines unit earlier this week proposed buying all of TWA's routes from the U.S. to London and TWA's takeoff and landing rights at Chicago's O'Hare International Airport. It also would acquire TWA's gates at the Chicago airport.

The route sales must be approved by the Transportation Department, but Sen. Danforth said the Justice Department has the power under antitrust laws to "investigate and remedy market practices and structures that deny low-priced competitive air service to the traveling public."
