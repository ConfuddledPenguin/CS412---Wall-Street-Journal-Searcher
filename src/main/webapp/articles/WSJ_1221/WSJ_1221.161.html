Business Brief -- Nalco Chemical Co.: Firm to Buy ICI's Share Of Three Joint Ventures

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/21/90

Nalco Chemical Co. said it agreed to pay Imperial Chemical Industries PLC $168 million for ICI's interests in joint ventures between the companies in England, Canada and Australia. Nalco, a Naperville, Ill., specialty chemical maker, now owns 49% of each venture: Nalfloc in England, Alchem in Canada and Catoleum in Australia. The ventures, which have total annual sales of about $185 million, make specialty chemical products for the mining, waste treatment and other industries.

A Nalco spokesman said the agreement fits its strategy of international growth. The spokesman said one-third of Nalco's 1989 sales of $1.07 billion came from abroad, and that the company wants to increase that to 50% some time in the middle of the decade.

The transaction, subject to regulatory approval, is expected to close in the first quarter of 1991.
