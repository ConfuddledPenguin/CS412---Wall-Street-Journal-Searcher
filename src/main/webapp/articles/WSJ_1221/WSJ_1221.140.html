Business Brief -- General Motors Corp.: Acquisition of Navco Unit Of Xerox Corp. Completed

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/21/90

General Motors Corp. completed the acquisition of Xerox Corp.'s Navco auto-insurance unit for $250 million. The sale follows the signing of a definitive agreement announced last October. Navco, based in St. Louis, provides personal automobile insurance primarily to members of associations through its wholly owned subsidiaries, National General Insurance Co. and National General Assurance Co.

GM acquired Navco through the Motors Insurance Corp. unit of its General Motors Acceptance Corp. subsidiary. Motors Insurance sells a broad range of insurance coverages.
