Business Brief -- Natural Alternatives International: Company Expects to Post Moderate Loss for Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/21/90

Natural Alternatives International Inc. said it expects a decline in revenue and a "moderate loss" in its fiscal second quarter ending Dec. 31. President Mark A. Ledoux said he expects the second-quarter loss to be $50,000 to $100,000 on revenue of $1.9 million to $2 million.

For the year-ago quarter, the San Marcos, Calif., maker of vitamins and minerals reported net income of $236,158, or six cents a share, on revenue of $2.7 million. The company has about four million shares outstanding.

Natural Alternatives said results in the recent quarter were affected by inventory adjustments by certain major customers. The company said it expects that previous order patterns will be restored in the third quarter.

The company said it is negotiating with several large organizations for new private label distributing arrangements. Mr. Ledoux said he expects one of the arrangements to be completed in time to "contribute substantially" to revenue and earnings in the second half of fiscal 1991.
