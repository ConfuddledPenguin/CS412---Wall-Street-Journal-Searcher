Marketing Brief -- New York Times Co.: Company to Increase Prices For Its Flagship Newspaper

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/21/90

New York Times Co. said it will raise the daily price of its flagship newspaper's national edition to 75 cents in the Midwest and the Southwest. A company spokeswoman said the price increases are the result of higher costs for production and distribution. The increases go into effect Jan. 14. The Times had been charging 50 cents for daily copies of its national edition in the Midwest and 60 cents in the Southwest.

The price for the national edition in the Southeast was raised to 75 cents last September, but the national edition on the West Coast will remain at 50 cents. The daily paper costs 50 cents in the Northeast, except in the tri-state area near New York, where it is 40 cents.

Major newspapers, including The Wall Street Journal, have for the first time in years raised their daily price. Last week, Times executives said they would study "pricing strategies" for the New York Times, but no price increase was planned in New York.
