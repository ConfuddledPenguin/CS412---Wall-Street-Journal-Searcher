Business Brief -- Yerkes & Michels: Accounting Firm Is Banned From Serving Any Thrifts

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/21/90

The Office of Thrift Supervision permanently banned Yerkes & Michels, an accounting firm based in Independence, Kan., from serving any thrift, thrift-holding company or subsidiary that is federally regulated. Under a consent order in connection with the firm's audit of First Federal Savings & Loan Association of Independence, the thrift office charged that the 1988 and 1989 audits weren't conducted by qualified public accountants. The thrift office also said that Yerkes & Michels reported the true financial condition of First Federal, which meets all capital requirements, but that it relied on government examiners' paperwork, and didn't independently review the thrift.

Yerkes & Michels couldn't be reached for comment. Under the consent order, the firm neither admits nor denies the grounds of the order.

The thrift office also issued a cease-and-desist order requiring Yerkes & Michels to correct its auditing practices, and repay audit fees of $51,000 that the firm charged First Federal. It must also establish minimum credentials for any partners and staff assigned to bank or credit union audits.
