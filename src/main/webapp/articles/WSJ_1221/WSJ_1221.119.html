ConAgra Earnings, Sales Increased 38% In Second Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/21/90

OMAHA, Neb. -- ConAgra Inc., its results swollen by its recent $1.34 billion acquisition of Beatrice Co., reported a 38% jump in both earnings and sales for the second quarter ended Nov. 25. The food concern said net income rose to $88.3 million, or 60 cents a share, from the year-earlier $63.9 million, or 52 cents a share. Sales climbed to $5.33 billion from $3.86 billion.

Per-share earnings reflect an increase in the number of common and equivalent shares to 137 million from the year-ago 122.7 million. ConAgra, as part of the Beatrice acquisition, issued seller Kohlberg Kravis Roberts & Co. more than 11 million common shares, along with 142,000 shares of convertible preferred.

In addition, per-share figures in the latest period also reflect the payment of $6.1 million in preferred dividends: such payments are deducted before per-share earnings are calculated. In the year-ago quarter, ConAgra had only a nominal $147,000 in preferred payments.

The company said its latest earnings were "highlighted by good earnings balance, aggressive activity in new consumer products and excellent progress bringing the Beatrice businesses on board."

Beatrice earnings, only two weeks of which were included in the fiscal first quarter results, "to date have been ahead of plan," ConAgra said.
