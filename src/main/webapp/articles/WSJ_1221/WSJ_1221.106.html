Business Brief -- Sun Electric Corp.: Concern to Take a Charge, Post Loss for 4th Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/21/90

Sun Electric Corp. said it plans to report a fourth-quarter loss and will post a $2.2 million charge in the quarter to restructure its U.S. automotive diagnostic and test equipment business. In the year-ago fourth quarter, Sun Electric had a loss of $2.4 million, or 31 cents a share, on sales of $46.7 million.

The Crystal Lake, Ill., maker of auto test and diagnostic equipment said the restructuring will involve the reorganization of the company's manufacturing and distribution operations and a restructuring of its field sales, service and training organization.

Sun Electric said it expects that the productivity improvements and cost reduction measures should help to offset the effects of competitive pricing pressure in the domestic marketplace. However, it said, as a result of current competitive pressures on margins, expenses associated with establishing a new service industry training program and certain start-up activities, the company expects a loss in the fourth quarter prior to the inclusion of the charge and a $700,000 tax expense.
