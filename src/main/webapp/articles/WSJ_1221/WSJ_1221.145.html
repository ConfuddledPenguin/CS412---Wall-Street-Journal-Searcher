Business Brief -- CSC Industries Inc.: Ownership Structure Altered By Equity Issued to Firms

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/21/90

CSC Industries Inc. said it has more than doubled the number of shares outstanding, resulting in $17.5 million of new equity and a change in the ownership structure of the company. The steelmaker, based in Warren, Ohio, said it sold 11.6 million newly issues common shares for $14.5 million. Of that total, Daido Steel Co., a principal shareholder of CSC, purchased 5,840,000 shares, and C. Itoh & Co., Marubeni Corp., and Okaya & Co., each purchased 1,920,000 shares. CSC generated an additional $3 million of equity by converting $3 million of debt with Imetal of Paris into 2.4 million new common shares.

The transactions boosted the number of CSC's shares outstanding to 22,744,915 from 8,744,915. It also boosts Daido's ownership to 38.2% from 32.5% and ownership by each of the three Japanese trading companies to 8.4%. Imetal's ownership was reduced to 23% from 32.5%. The stake held by remaining shareholders was reduced to 13.5% from 35%.

The company said the additional equity would be used to finance improvements at its 35-inch rolling mill complex and its 12-inch bar mill.
