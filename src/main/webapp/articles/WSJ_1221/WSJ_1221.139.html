Who's News: Quirk to Resign as Head Of Fund in Massachusetts

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/21/90

BOSTON -- Paul Quirk, executive director of Massachusetts' public-employee pension fund for six years, announced plans to resign amid a ruckus over his role in a possible proxy fight for USX Corp. Mr. Quirk, 49 years old, said he would resign effective Jan. 15, but would continue working on a contract basis until March 15 to aid the transition to his successor, who hasn't been named.

Mr. Quirk earlier this week withdrew from a five-member group backed by New York investor Carl Icahn that may launch a proxy fight against USX next spring. Mr. Quirk also returned the $15,000 plus 300 shares of USX stock Mr. Icahn agreed to pay each group member.

While Mr. Quirk's role in the Icahn group had been cleared by the Massachusetts fund's lawyers, it drew fire as inappropriate from some of the fund's nine-member governing board.

Mr. Quirk said the latest controversy accelerated departure plans he disclosed to the fund's governing board last summer. With a new governor taking office in January, Mr. Quirk said he had, in any event, intended to leave his post by the July start of the fund's new fiscal year. He said he had no job plans yet.

Mr. Quirk, as executive director of the fund, known formally as Massachusetts Pension Reserves Investment Management Board, oversaw the retirement system's growth to nearly $3 billion in assets from about $400 million in 1984.
