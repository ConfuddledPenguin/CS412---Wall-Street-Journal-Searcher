Business Brief -- Fiat S.p.A.: Italian Car Maker and Ford Sign Pact for Joint Venture

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/21/90

Fiat S.p.A. and Ford Motor Co. said they signed definitive agreements to form a company that will merge Fiat's Fiat-Geotech unit and Ford New Holland, Ford's subsidiary that makes tractors and farm and industrial equipment. Last July, the two companies announced plans to form the joint venture, in which Fiat would get an 80% stake and Ford 20% plus an undisclosed cash payment. Industry analysts have estimated that Ford would probably receive more than the $330 million it paid for the New Holland operations three years ago. A Ford spokesman said the company won't disclose the amount of cash it will get until the venture receives final government approvals in the U.S. and Europe.

Ford and Fiat said the new company intends to preserve the individual market and product identities of both Fiat-Geotech and Ford New Holland.

Corporate headquarters of the new company will be in London.
