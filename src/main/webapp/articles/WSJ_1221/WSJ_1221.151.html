Business Brief -- Sotheby's Holdings Inc.: Firm's Sales Decreased 50% In Fall Art-Auction Season

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/21/90

Sales of art and antiques at Sotheby's Holdings Inc. fell 50% in the fall art-auction season from the year-earlier period. The New York auction house's sales during the season, which runs from September through mid-December, totaled $751 million, down from $1.51 billion in 1989, the company said.

Sotheby's sales for all of 1990 totaled $2.434 billion, compared with 1989 auction sales of $2.946 billion, a decline of 17%.

Michael L. Ainslie, president and chief executive, said two important collecting categories, Impressionist and Contemporary Art, have experienced extraordinary price increases in recent years, "but now, affected by a worldwide climate of political and economic uncertainty," those markets "understandably could not maintain these levels." He added that sales in 1990 could be seen as "the beginning of the return to normalcy."

London-based Christie's International PLC, Sotheby's chief rival in the art-auction business, yesterday reported a 39% drop in dollar terms in its fall-season sales. Both firms have previously announced cost-cutting measures and layoffs intended to trim 1991 costs by about 10%.
