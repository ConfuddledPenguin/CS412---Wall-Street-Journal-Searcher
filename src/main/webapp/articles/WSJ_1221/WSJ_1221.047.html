Real Estate Developer, Aide Charged With Loan Fraud

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/21/90

BOSTON -- A condominium developer and real estate salesman were arrested yesterday and charged by federal prosecutors with bank fraud involving more than $11 million of condominium loans. The 31-count indictment charged that William W. Lilly, 45 years old, a Lynnfield, Mass., developer, and one of his sales managers, Donald Glusgol, 62, of Peabody, Mass., offered falsified condominium mortgages as collateral for obtaining $7 million in loans from First Mutual Bank for Savings, a troubled Boston savings bank.

First Mutual eventually sold the loans for $9 million to Royal Palm Savings Association, a West Palm Beach, Fla., savings and loan that became insolvent and was placed in conservatorship in April 1989.

Messrs. Lilly and Glusgol couldn't be reached for comment.
