CMS Charge Of $450 Million Set for Quarter

  • Author: Gregory A. Patterson
  • Date: 12/21/90

DEARBORN, Mich. -- CMS Energy Corp. said it will take a charge of about $450 million against fourth-quarter earnings and post a loss for the year because of a regulatory ruling yesterday affecting a failed nuclear power plant. Analysts said the charge had been expected, in light of actions by the Michigan Public Service Commission. In the first nine months of 1990, CMS earned $145 million, or $1.78 a share. In 1989, net income was $312 million, or $3.80 a share.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, CMS jumped $1.75 to closed at $27 a share.

CMS announced the charge after an administrative law judge for Michigan's Public Service Commission proposed yesterday that CMS Energy be allowed to recoup $1.34 billion of the $2.14 billion it hasn't recovered from its abandoned nuclear plant in Midland, Mich. CMS had asked for rate increases that would allow it to recover the full $2.14 billion.

The judge's recommendation won't become final until the full commission issues a final order, which is expected in the latter half of next year. But CMS opted to take a charge now, based on the judge's recommendation.

CMS's Consumer's Power Co. utility abandoned its effort to build the Midland nuclear plant in 1984 after spending $4.1 billion. The ruling relates to the amount CMS hasn't recovered after converting part of the plant to a gas-fired cogeneration facility.

The recommended recovery would occur over a 10-year period and would provide the company an annual electric rate increase of $111.5 million during that time.

CMS is still in litigation over the amount it may recover through electric rates for the costs of the cogeneration facility that began operation in March.
