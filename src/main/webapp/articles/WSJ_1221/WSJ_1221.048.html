International: Continental AG Measure To Deter Takeover Voided

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 12/21/90

BONN -- A German court ruled Thursday that an anti-takeover clause Continental AG adopted in 1989 is invalid. Continental, a German tire maker, is the target of a takeover attempt by the Italian tire maker Pirelli S.p.A.

The district court in Hanover didn't explain its decision. It ruled on the matter after a Continental stockholder filed a complaint against the Hanover-based company. Continental has rejected the financial terms of Pirelli's offer, but it insists it still is willing to talk to the Italian company about a merger. Under the 1989 decision reached at the company's annual meeting, the limit on voting rights can be changed only with the support of holders of 75% of company shares. It had required only a simple majority.

Many German companies have clauses that limit stockholders' voting stakes to 5% even if their holdings are larger. The measure makes it difficult for potential buyers to take over a company, an obstacle that is heightened by the need to get 75% of the votes to change the rules.
