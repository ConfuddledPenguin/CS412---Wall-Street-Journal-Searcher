Business Brief -- Denison Mines Ltd.: Concern Has Buy-Out Talks With 2 Interested Parties

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/21/90

Denison Mines Ltd., facing a possible cash shortage next month, said it was in discussions with two separate parties interested in acquiring the uranium mining concern. William James, who was appointed president and chief executive officer of Denison last week, said the talks were at a "very preliminary" stage. Mr. James wouldn't identify the potential buyers, but said the talks were continuing.

"We'll talk to anybody {about acquiring Denison}. We need the money. We're tight," he said.

Mr. James said Denison, which has been plagued by low uranium prices for several years, could still sell some of its assets to raise cash. The company has been unable to sell its overseas oil and gas assets since putting them up for sale last year. Denison, based in Toronto, also has interests in potash and coal mining.

Denison must repay 150 million Canadian dollars (US$129.4 million) in bank loans next month. Mr. James said the company is seeking an extension. Denison also faces a deadline next March to retract C$175 million in preferred shares.

On the Toronto Stock Exchange, Denison Class B, nonvoting shares closed yesterday at 70 Canadian cents, up six Canadian cents. The Class A shares gained five Canadian cents to 75 Canadian cents.
