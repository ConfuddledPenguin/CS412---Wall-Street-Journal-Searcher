Business Brief -- Metropolitan Circuits Inc.: Parent, as Unit Did Earlier, Files for Bankruptcy Shield

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/21/90

Metropolitan Circuits Inc., a Santa Ana, Calif., maker of printed circuit boards, said it filed for protection from its creditors under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code. Last month, the company's sole operating unit, Metropolitan Circuits of California, sought bankruptcy-law protection. Both petitions were filed in bankruptcy court in Santa Ana. In recent months, the companies have tried to raise new capital to pay their debts, among other things. The unit's filing was made to block a court order obtained by its largest creditor, General Electric Co., that would have let GE seize some of the unit's assets.

In its filing, the parent company said it has assets of $1.5 million and liabilities of $3.8 million. Last month, the unit received preliminary approval from the court to use its cash for operations; a final hearing on that issue is scheduled for today.

The parent company filing "is one more step in our ongoing efforts to realign our business operations for the continued benefit of our customers, suppliers, employees and creditors," said Richard J. Sandberg, chief executive officer.
