Office Depot Inc., Office Club to Merge In $137 Million Deal

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/21/90

BOCA RATON, Fla. -- Office Depot Inc. and Office Club Inc. said they agreed to merge in a transaction valued at about $137 million. The two providers of office supplies said Office Club, based in San Francisco, would become a wholly owned subsidiary of Office Depot and would continue to operate under its own name with its current format.

Analysts generally applauded the agreement, saying that, while there would be some short-term dilution in Office Depot's earnings, the match will give Office Depot both a large presence on the West Coast and a stronger management team.

The combined companies will have sales of about $1.3 billion in 1991, analysts said, further strengthening Office Depot's position as the No. 1 player in the so-called super-store segment of office-supply sales. That segment may account for as much as 5% of the total U.S. market for office supplies.

The two companies said Office Club holders will receive 1.194 shares of Office Depot common stock for each share of Office Club stock. There are approximately 7.2 million shares outstanding of Office Club common. In addition, Office Depot will assume Office Club's outstanding options, currently representing approximately 675,000 shares of Office Club stock.

The transaction, which will be accounted for on a pooling-of-interests basis, is subject to shareholder and regulatory approval. The two companies said they anticipated closing the transaction next spring.

David I. Fuente will continue to serve as chairman and chief executive officer of Office Depot, which will remain based in Boca Raton. Mark D. Begelman, Office Club's chairman and chief executive, will become president and chief operating officer of Office Depot.
