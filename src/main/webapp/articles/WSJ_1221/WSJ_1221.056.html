Republic Health Says First-Quarter Profit Totaled $1.1 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/21/90

DALLAS -- Republic Health Corp. expects to report net income of $1.1 million, or 19 cents a share, for the fiscal first quarter ended Nov. 30, said Bryan P. Marsal, the company's chief executive officer. In the year-earlier period, then privately held Republic Health posted a loss of $38 million.

The company expects to report that revenue increased 22% to $118 million from $97 million, Mr. Marsal said.

The company had expected its first-quarter earnings to be "a little higher," Mr. Marsal said in an interview. But he said the recent 2% reduction in Medicare reimbursements for outpatient procedures resulted in narrower margins. Republic Health owns or leases, and operates 13 acute-care hospitals in six states, plus one surgery center.

Looking ahead, Mr. Marsal said he expected the company to stay profitable and to continue posting gains over last year's results. But he said smaller-than-expected Medicare reimbursements would continue putting a strain on earnings. As a result, he said the company is attempting to cut costs, as well as increase business from non-Medicare patients. Medicare patients now account for 50% of the company's business, Mr. Marsal said.

The company is planning to open two more surgery centers in Southern California, where its existing one operates, Mr. Marsal said.

The chief executive said he expected his company would prove to be "recession insensitive." But he did say that in hard times, patients may choose to do without elective surgery.

A year ago this month, Republic Health sought Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection to restructure total debt of about $645 million. When it emerged from bankruptcy court in May, its total debt was reduced to $379 million, and bondholders owned nearly 90% of its common; the rest was held by holders of the old preferred and common stock.

Now, the company has debt of about $350 million and equity of about $42 million, Mr. Marsal said. He said the company has no plans to pay dividends or issue additional stock.
