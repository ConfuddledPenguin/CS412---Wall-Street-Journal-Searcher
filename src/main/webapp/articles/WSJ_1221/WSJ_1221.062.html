GE's Internal Query Probes Alleged Israeli Kickbacks

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/21/90

FAIRFIELD, Conn. -- General Electric Co. said it is conducting an internal company investigation in connection with an alleged kickback scheme involving Israeli purchases of U.S. weapons. As reported in the Washington Post yesterday, police in Israel are investigating the Israeli air force procurement of military equipment from U.S. contractors. The allegations are that millions of dollars in kickbacks were siphoned off to senior Israeli military officials who helped cover up procurement fraud.

General Electric, which supplied jet engines for Israeli fighter planes, was one U.S. company cited in Israeli press accounts of the allegations, according to the Washington Post. The reports identified Herbert B. Steindler, a GE executive, as having been the manager in charge of company sales to the Israeli air force.

"GE has become aware of the investigation by the Israeli government," said a GE spokesman. "The company has initiated its own internal investigation concerning the purchase orders said to be involved in the Israeli investigation." He said GE has informed the U.S. government of its actions, adding that "GE is cooperating fully with the U.S. government investigation."

The spokesman declined to comment on when GE learned of the allegations or when it began its own investigation.
