Business Brief -- Halliburton Co.: Concern Underpaid Taxes For Six Years, IRS Alleges

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/21/90

Halliburton Co. said the Internal Revenue Service notified the company that it may have underpaid its federal corporate income taxes for the years 1980 through 1986 by as much as $247 million, excluding interest. The Dallas oil field services concern is disputing the allegation and has filed claims with the agency seeking the refund at about $115 million, excluding interest, for the six-year period in question.

A spokesman for Halliburton said "a settlement may be worked out" before the matter is heard in the U.S. Tax Court. He added that a court date hasn't been set and that the company believes that the ultimate resolution of the matter won't have any material impact on operating results.
