Credit Ratings: S&P Lowers Ratings On Travelers Units' Claims-Paying Ability

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/21/90

HARTFORD, Conn. -- Standard & Poors Corp. said it lowered its rating on the claims-paying ability of Travelers Indemnity Co. Intercompany Pool, the property-casualty company of Travelers Corp., to double-A from double-A-plus. The rating concern said the action on the insurer reflects S&P's view that "property-casualty conditions will continue to deteriorate over the near term."

S&P also downgraded to double-A from double-A-plus its rating on the subsidiaries of the property-casualty company, including Charter Oak Fire Insurance Co., Phoenix Insurance Co., Travelers Indemnity Co. of America, Travelers Indemnity Co. of Illinois and Travelers Indemnity Co. of Rhode Island.

This marked the third S&P downgrading of Travelers Corp.'s businesses since the company announced a $650 million addition to loss reserves for mortgage loans and real estate investments and a dividend cut on Oct. 5. S&P has since reduced its ratings on the senior debt and commercial paper of the holding company, and the claims-paying ability of Travelers Insurance Co., the life insurance company, to single-A-plus from double-A-minus.

In the property-casualty company's downgrading, S&P cited its expectation that Travelers Corp.'s problems with its real estate and mortgage portfolios will leave the property-casualty business less flexible financially.

A Travelers spokesman said that the rating action was expected and that a double-A rating is "considered very strong."

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Travelers shares closed at $17.125, up 12.5 cents, on volume of more than 1.9 million shares.
