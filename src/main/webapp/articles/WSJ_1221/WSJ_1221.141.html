Business Brief -- BSD Bancorp: Loan-Loss Reserve Boost Is Set by San Diego Firm

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/21/90

BSD Bancorp said it plans to increase its loan-loss reserves by about $1 million, resulting in "minimal" fourth-quarter profit. For the full year, the San Diego bank holding company expects profit to be about equal to nine-month profit of $3.4 million. Fourth-quarter profit last year was $1.4 million, or 40 cents a share; full-year profit was $4 million, or $1.13 a share.

The company said its reserve increases include $721,000 in commercial loan write-offs at Bank of San Diego and other "special reserves" to provide "additional cushion for the anticipated recessionary environment" next year.

BSD said its three major banks, including Bank of San Diego, recently underwent examinations by representatives of the Federal Deposit Insurance Corp. and that "all charge-offs directed by the regulators have been taken."
