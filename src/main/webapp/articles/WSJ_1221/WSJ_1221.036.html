News Corp. Shares Plunge In Sydney Due to Rumors

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/21/90

SYDNEY -- Nervous investors sent News Corp. shares plunging 20% as rumors swirled that the flagship of media mogul Rupert Murdoch is heading for trouble. The shares closed at 4.35 Australian dollars, or US$3.36, apiece on the Australian Stock Exchange, down A$1.09 from Wednesday's close, prompting a query from the exchange.

Mr. Murdoch, who controls about 45% of the company and is chief executive, told officials that News Corp. "knows of no reason for the recent variation" in the price, and he tried to scotch fears about the progress of the proposed rescheduling of most of the company's A$10.53 billion (US$8.13 billion) bank debt.

In a statement, Mr. Murdoch said that News Corp. is "very pleased" with its bank negotiations and that "a large majority of banks" in the group "have already indicated agreement to the terms." News Corp. originally was aiming to pin the bank rescheduling down by mid-December, but the company has since indicated that the debt negotiations are likely to drag into January.

The rumors -- some of them probably fanned by short sellers, investors who bet that News Corp.'s stock price will drop -- included talk that the company would be hit with a tax bill of A$1 billion by the Australian government.
