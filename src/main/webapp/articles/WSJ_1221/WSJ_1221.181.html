Business Brief -- New Brunswick Electric Power: Long-Term Contract Signed For Fuel From Venezuela

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/21/90

New Brunswick Electric Power Commission said it signed the first long-term North American contract for Venezuelan liquid bitumen, a low-cost fuel produced from the vast deposits of tar sands in Venezuela's Orinoco Belt. New Brunswick Power, owned by New Brunswick province, signed a multimillion-dollar contract with Bitor America Corp., a unit of state-owned Petroleos de Venezuela, to purchase as much as 16,000 barrels a day of liquid bitumen for 20 years. The utility will use the fuel, which has the trade name Orimulsion, as power-plant fuel.

New Brunswick Power said it will spend 338 million Canadian dollars (US$291.6 million) over the next four years to convert a power plant to burn Orimulsion. It will install a new smokestack scrubber to reduce emissions produced from burning the bitumen, which has a high sulfur content. Orimulsion is a mixture of water and extra-heavy bitumen, a tarlike crude. Like conventional crude, Orimulsion can be shipped by tanker or pipeline, but costs less than half the price.
