Business Brief -- Black & Decker Corp.: Emerson Electric to Buy Mallory Unit for $90 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/21/90

Black & Decker Corp. said it agreed to sell its North American Mallory Controls business for $90 million but expects to report fourth-quarter earnings lower than previously estimated. The Towson, Md., power-tool maker said it sold Mallory Controls, which it acquired as part of last year's acquisition of Emhart Corp., to Emerson Electric Co. It said the Brazilian portion of the business is being offered for sale separately.

Emerson Electric, a St. Louis-based maker of electronic products, couldn't be reached for comment.

Black & Decker has been selling assets in order to meet payments on the $2.8 billion loan it used to buy Emhart. Last month it sold its U.S. and Canadian assets in True Temper Hardware as well as capital stock in True Temper Ltd. in Ireland to Huffy Corp. for $55 million.

The company also said it expects lower earnings due to lower sales in the U.S. and deteriorating business conditions in Brazil. "While we expect to offset most of the sluggishness in the U.S. through aggressive cost-containment programs, the difficulties in Brazil will result in lower-than-expected earnings in the fourth quarter," said Chairman and Chief Executive Officer Nolan D. Archibald. However, he also noted that the company expects a profitable fourth quarter compared with the net loss it reported last year.

Mr. Archibald said the company is actively pursuing the sale of the Brazilian Mallory Controls business and will implement major cost reductions in all Brazil operations.

In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange, Black & Decker shares closed down 25 cents to $10.75.
