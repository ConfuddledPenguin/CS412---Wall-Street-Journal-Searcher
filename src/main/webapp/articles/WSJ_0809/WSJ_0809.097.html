REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: Banned on Broadway

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/09/90

Actors' Equity has read new meaning into the term the Great White Way. This week it barred acclaimed English actor Jonathan Pryce from re-creating on Broadway his role in the London hit "Miss Saigon." Mr. Pryce is white and his character is Eurasian. It follows, according to Actors' Equity, that his appearance would be "an affront to the Asian community." The producer has canceled the show, which by the way has parts for 34 minorities, mostly Asians. We hadn't realized that the people in theater, not to mention Manhattan's economy, were so flush that they could kill plays for such novel reasons. Black actor Denzel Washington opens off-Broadway next week in Joseph Papp's production of "Richard III," the humpbacked English king. Somehow, we doubt that Actors' Equity will launch a protest against this casting or berate Mr. Papp for not hiring the handicapped.
