What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/09/90

PHILLIPS PETROLEUM CUT its wholesale gasoline price by 4.5 cents a gallon, and Arco froze prices for a week. The firms acted amid an outcry over alleged gouging by oil firms and after Bush asked the industry to "show restraint." Crude prices, meanwhile, sank as much as $3 a barrel on news that Saudi Arabia and others are prepared to boost output. But such production increases are unlikely to fully offset the loss of supplies from Iraq and Kuwait. ---

Frank Lorenzo will sell most of his stake in Continental Airlines to SAS and will step down as chairman and chief executive, according to people familiar with his plans. Delta Air President Hollis Harris is expected to succeed Lorenzo as chief executive.

---

The stock market rallied on falling oil prices. The Dow Jones industrials rose 24.26 points. The Mideast turmoil disrupted the Treasury's quarterly securities auction, but kept the dollar firm against most currencies. Tokyo stocks rebounded sharply Wednesday.

---

American Airlines, Midway and America West each announced a 10% fare rise to offset higher fuel costs.

---

Sears's chairman reassumed the reins of the company's giant merchandising operation in what amounts to an admission that the firm's retailing approach isn't working.

---

The economy was slowing markedly even before oil prices shot up, the Fed reported. The East and parts of the Midwest were especially weak.

The survey adds to evidence that a recession is near, if not yet here.

---

The FDIC is expected next week to consider raising the premiums paid by banks to insure their deposits.

---

Money-market fund assets soared $6.8 billion in the past week, much of it moved from stock and bond funds, as investors sought refuge from the fallout of the Mideast crisis.

---

A UAL union group won a 60-day extension of the financing deadline for a proposed buy-out of the company.

---

A strike at a GM parts plant could force shutdowns at eight car and truck assembly sites within two days. The immediate impact demonstrates the Big Three's vulnerability as a Sept. 14 national strike deadline nears.

---

Eastern Europe could suffer a devastating blow from higher energy prices caused by the Mideast crisis. In the West, the EC also now expects slower growth and higher inflation.

---

KPMG Peat Marwick's Larry Horner won't seek re-election as chairman and chief executive of the consulting and accounting firm.

---

Volkswagen is offering a money-back guarantee, for three months or 3,000 miles, on one of its models.

---

Pacific Enterprises posted a $229 million second-quarter loss after taking $275 million in write-downs on its oil and retailing operations. The firm disclosed that its retailing chief quit and said it may close 10% of its stores.

---

A Bear Stearns broker was suspended for at least two years for his alleged role in an insider-trading case involving LTV Corp.'s former chairman.

---

Chemical Banking created a six-member office of the chairman to run the parent and its main bank units.
