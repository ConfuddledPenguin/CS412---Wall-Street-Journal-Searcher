Bull HN to Purchase Honeywell Subsidiary

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/09/90

BILLERICA, Mass. -- Bull HN Information Systems Inc. said it reached a preliminary agreement to purchase Honeywell Federal Systems Inc. from Honeywell Inc. Terms of the agreement weren't disclosed, though Bull HN and Honeywell Inc. have begun seeking necessary government approvals for the transaction. Honeywell Federal Systems, a 1,600-employee company based in McLean, Va., provides systems integration, computer products and technical services to the U.S. government. It had sales of $274 million in 1989. Honeywell Inc. retained the federal systems division after placing most of its computer operations into a joint venture with Groupe Bull of France in 1987. Last year, Groupe Bull purchased majority ownership of the joint venture, originally called Honeywell Bull, and renamed it Bull HN. NEC Corp. of Japan is a minority investor. Honeywell Federal Systems has been the exclusive distributor of Bull HN products to the federal government.

By agreeing to sell the federal systems division, Honeywell is shedding the last remains of its computer manufacturing, sales and service operation, which generated $1.9 billion in revenue in 1985, or about a quarter of Honeywell's overall business. It leaves Honeywell, a Minneapolis-based company with 1989 revenue of $6.1 billion, as a controls systems company, providing products like thermostats and industrial-process control systems to homes, buildings, manufacturers, and the space and aviation industries.
