State of California Demands Rate Cuts From Two Insurers

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/09/90

SAN FRANCISCO -- The California Department of Insurance ordered Safeco Insurance Co. to roll back rates by $41 million and California State Automobile Association by $92 million in a test of the agency's authority under the Proposition 103 insurance-reform initiative. Proposition 103 required the state's property and casualty insurers to cut rates by 20%. But the state supreme court subsequently held that any rollbacks couldn't deprive insurers of a "fair" rate of return. The state insurance commissioner, Roxani Gillespie, has set that rate at 11.2% in figuring rollbacks, although the insurance industry is continuing to challenge that rate in court.

The department set hearings for Sept. 17 on the proposed rollbacks for Seattle-based Safeco, and CSAA, which has offices in San Francisco and Los Angeles, and both entities said they would contest the orders. CSAA is nonprofit motorists' association.

Proposition 103, in theory, subjects roughly 600 insurers in the state to rollbacks, but the cuts are expected to be relatively small because of the state high-court ruling.
