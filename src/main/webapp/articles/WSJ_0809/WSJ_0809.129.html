International Brief -- Suntory Ltd.: First-Half Pretax Earnings Fell 10% to $33.6 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/09/90

Suntory Ltd. of Japan, a privately held beverage and food concern, said first-half pretax profit fell 10% to 5.05 billion yen ($33.6 million) from 5.63 billion yen a year earlier, citing higher interest and investment costs. Net income declined 15% to 2.07 billion yen from 2.42 billion yen.

Sales rose 2.2% to 361.8 billion yen from 354.08 billion yen, partly because of changes in Japan's tax structure. Sales of alcoholic beverages, excluding beer, fell 5% to 204.429 billion yen from 215.19 billion yen. But Suntory noted that the basis for the year-earlier comparison was distorted by a consumption tax that was introduced in April 1989 and replaced higher taxes on hard liquor and wine. Sales of beer increased 2.6% to 96.16 billion yen from 93.76 billion yen. Sales of foodstuffs rose 36% to 61.204 billion yen from 45.127 billion yen.

During the half, Suntory bought Cerebos Pacific Ltd., a Singapore holding company with interests in the manufacture and distribution of foodstuffs.
