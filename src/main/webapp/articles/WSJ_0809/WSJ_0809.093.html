Bush to Notify Congress On U.S.-Mexican Trade

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/09/90

WASHINGTON -- The U.S. hopes to complete a bilateral free-trade agreement with Mexico by December 1992, U.S. Trade Representative Carla Hills said. At the White House, President Bush said he would notify Congress in September of the two governments' intention to negotiate such an agreement. Under U.S. law, Congress first must authorize these negotiations, which are likely to get under way in June 1991.

Both President Bush and Mexican President Carlos Salinas de Gortari indicated earlier this year that they intended to negotiate such an agreement to forge tighter economic links between their two countries.

Ambassador Hills told reporters that the proposed agreement would cover trade in goods and services, investments, and protection of intellectual property rights of companies doing business in both countries. The agreement won't cover immigration issues.

Mrs. Hills and the Mexican commerce minister, Jaime Serra Puche, submitted a joint report to their presidents, recommending that the two countries undertake formal negotiation of a bilateral free-trade accord.
