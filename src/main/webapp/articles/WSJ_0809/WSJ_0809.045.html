Who's News: Bodman Is Appointed To Senior AT&T Post

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/09/90

BASKING RIDGE, N.J. -- American Telephone & Telegraph Co. named Richard S. Bodman senior vice president, strategic planning and corporate development, effective Aug. 23. Mr. Bodman, 52 years old, succeeds John Segal, who left last year to become vice chairman of Contel Corp., the Atlanta-based telecommunications service company. Mr. Bodman is currently president of Washington National Investment Corp., a Washington, D.C.-based investment firm that acquires large minority positions in companies with sales ranging from $50 million to $500 million. His background reinforces AT&T's recent strategy of making acquisitions and joint ventures with smaller companies as it tries to enter new markets. In July, AT&T announced it would acquire parts of Western Union Corp.'s business services group, including the EasyLink electronic mail service and Telex wire service. Last year, it acquired Istel Ltd., a London-based information services company.

In addition, Mr. Bodman will be a member of AT&T's executive committee; the company rarely admits those recruited from outside to join that committee. An AT&T spokesman said Chairman Robert E. Allen was "looking for new perspectives."
