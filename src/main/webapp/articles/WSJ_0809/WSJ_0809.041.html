International: European Firms Study Joint Steel Venture

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 08/09/90

BRUSSELS -- Arbed S.A. of Luxembourg and Cockerill Sambre S.A. of Belgium said they are discussing a 50-50 joint venture to combine their production and sales of flatsteel products. The share prices of both companies surged about 10% after the talks were disclosed.

"We are seeking a European dimension comparable to that of British Steel, Usinor-Sacilor and Thyssen," a spokesman for Cockerill Sambre said. The two companies said bigger scale is necessary to "assure the future of these activities in terms of applied research, new technologies and investments."

The joint venture would have annual sales of around 130 billion Belgian francs ($3.96 billion), about half from each company. Both companies derive around a third of their sales from flat-steel products. The two said they would retain their separate identities and pursue their other activities independently.

Within three months, the two companies aim to reach a preliminary conclusion on whether to pursue the proposed joint venure.

Cockerill Sambre is about 85%-owned by Belgium's Walloon regional government. The Luxembourg government owns about 31% of the voting rights in Arbed, and Societe Generale de Belgique S.A. owns 25.5%.
