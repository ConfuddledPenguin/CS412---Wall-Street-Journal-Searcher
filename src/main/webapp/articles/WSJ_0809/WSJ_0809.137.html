Business Brief -- Temple-Inland Inc.: Container Unit to Acquire Two Box-Making Companies

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/09/90

Temple-Inland Inc., of Diboll, Texas, said its Inland Container Corp. unit agreed in principle to acquire two box-making companies and made plans to upgrade and expand an existing mill. The combined costs of the acquisitions and expansion will be about $100 million. Inland Container wouldn't say how much it intends to pay for either Crockett Container Corp. of Santa Fe Springs, Calif., or Pakway Container Corp. of Indianapolis. But Inland Container said the two privately owned companies had annual sales in their last fiscal years totaling about $115 million. Inland Container said its 1989 sales exceeded $1.1 billion.

Inland Container said the acquisitions would increase its corrugated-box production by about 10%. The company said it intends to keep existing management at Crockett and Pakway.

The two acquisitions are subject to the execution of definitive agreements, Temple-Inland said.

Temple-Inland is a wood-products and financial-services company that earned $207.4 million last year, or $7.50 a share, on revenue of $2.12 billion.
