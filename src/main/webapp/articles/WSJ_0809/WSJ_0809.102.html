Business Brief -- Keystone Camera Products Corp.: Borrowing Ability Boosted, Allowing Increase in Staff

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/09/90

Keystone Camera Products Corp. said it boosted its borrowing ability, helping the Clifton, N.J., company to hire 270 additional employees to handle a $3.5 million backlog of Christmas orders. The Congress Financial Corp. unit of CoreStates Financial Corp., a Philadelphia-based bank holding company, agreed to allow Keystone to borrow as much as an additional $600,000 on a short-term basis. The funds, with additional credit and the proceeds of Keystone's sale of its interest in a lease, will permit the company to boost its work force to about 800.

The company is moving into production two new motorized "point and shoot" 35mm cameras, the Easy Shot 500 and 700 models, priced between $30 and $50. The cameras have features including automatic film wind and rewind, and a lens that doubles the magnification of pictures.
