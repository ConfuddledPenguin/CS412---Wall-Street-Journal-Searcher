Business Brief -- Computer Factory Inc.: Firm Had Loss for Quarter, Plans Retail Consolidation

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/09/90

Computer Factory Inc. reported a net loss of $7.3 million, or 85 cents a share, for its third quarter ended June 30 and said it plans to consolidate some retail operations. The Elmsford, N.Y., computer dealer has suffered more than most from the withering price war on personal computers over the past year and a half. Computer Factory's stock has lost more than 85% of its value over the past two years, closing yesterday at $2.25, unchanged, in New York Stock Exchange composite trading.

Computer Factory said part of its loss could be attributed to a $2.6 million charge related to the consolidation, but it also had a loss of $4.7 million from operations. That compares with net income of $82,000, or one cent a share, in the year-earlier period. Sales climbed 2.3% to $83.9 million from $82 million.

The company said it will try to return to profitability by closing some of its smaller, marginal branches and consolidating them into so-called superstores, which will offer a broader array of products. Computer Factory said it plans to open four of the large stores over the next nine months.

In the nine-month period, the company had a net loss of $10.4 million, or $1.21 a share, compared with year-earlier profit of $6.5 million, or 73 cents a share. Sales rose 6.7% to $297.7 million from $279.1 million.
