International Brief -- Standard Chartered PLC: Pretax Profit for First Half Reverses Year-Earlier Loss

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/09/90

Standard Chartered PLC of Britain, a banking concern, posted first-half pretax profit of #110 million ($206.1 million), or 14.4 pence a share, which was in line with market estimates and marks a modest turnaround from a loss of #47.6 million, or 30 pence a share, a year earlier, when results were hurt by extensive provisions for loans for less-developed countries. Pretax profit was aided by a net reduction in total developing-country provisions to #775 million from #876.1 million a year earlier. Pretax earnings before these provisions fell 8% to #192 million from #208.2 million. But pretax profit growth was held in check by tighter interest margins, Third World loan sales, and relatively flat operating results from African and Middle East operations, as well as provisions against British corporate loans and some troubles with advances in Australia and Hong Kong. The bank had issued a series of profit warnings this year, largely because of its exposure to some troubled British corporate borrowers.
