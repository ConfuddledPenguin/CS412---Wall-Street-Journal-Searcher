International Brief -- Cie. de Saint-Gobain: French Company's Revenue Increased 2.6% in First Half

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/09/90

French glass and construction-material concern Cie. de Saint-Gobain said consolidated revenue grew 2.6% in the first half to 34 billion francs ($6.4 billion) from 33.2 billion francs in the 1989 first half. The company said sales were virtually flat on a comparable structural basis. Saint-Gobain attributed the loss to weakness in the U.S. dollar and the British pound against the French franc.

The decline in the dollar hurt the company most, a spokesman said, because the U.S. unit's loss of about 10% against its French counterpart severely devalued the company's U.S. revenue in franc terms, even though the volume of Saint-Gobain's business in the U.S. market held close to first-half 1989 levels.

The spokesman said North American operations accounted for about 22% of the company's revenue.
