U.S. Park Service to Move Some Yosemite Operations

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/09/90

SAN FRANCISCO -- The National Park Service, in a concession to longstanding environmental concerns, said it would move the operations of 20 agency buildings from Yosemite National Park to El Portal, a town outside the park's southwest border. Although the agency didn't indicate when the move would occur, the decision was praised by the Wilderness Society as an important step in relieving the national park of increasing congestion and pollution. A year ago, the park service said the move, which was conceived in a 1980 general management plan, may be unrealistic.

Park officials said plans call for razing most of the smaller buildings inside the park and incorporating them into a central maintenance facility in El Portal. Officials had previously made plans to move housing for agency employees outside the park.

Wilderness Society officials said the move was "an important step in the right direction," but noted that several facilities, including the park's main administrative offices, would still remain in the park.
