MERCK & Co. has proposed a plan for drugs sold to Medicaid with price discounts of 10% to 15% in the first two years and 10% to 25% in the fifth year, and the discounts would be granted by all drug companies, not just Merck. The details of the Merck plan were misstated in yesterday's edition. (See: "Medicine: Drug Concerns Seek to Block Medicaid Curbs" -- WSJ Aug. 8, 1990)

---

A SYNTRO CORP. EXECUTIVE, clarifying statements he made in yesterday's edition, said the company expects to be operating at a break-even level Sept. 30, the end of its fiscal year, but still expects to report a net loss for the year. Further, he said the company has a 40% share of the U.S. market for its swine vaccine. Also, the company had a $1.6 million loss for the nine months ended June 30. The amount of the loss was misstated in yesterday's edition.

(See: "Business Brief -- Syntro Corp.: Firm, Upjohn Settle Suits on Swine Vaccine Patents" -- WSJ Aug. 8, 1990)
