SANTA FE PACIFIC Corp. (Chicago) -- Moody's Investors Service Inc. said it may upgrade its ratings on debt of this railroad, natural resources and real estate concern, if Santa Fe's financial flexibility improves. Ratings under review include the company's senior subordinated payment-in-kind, or PIK, debentures, rated single-B-2. The rating concern also is reviewing several issues of Santa Fe's subsidiary, the Atchison, Topeka & Santa Fe Railway: equipment trust certificates, rated single-A-3; mortgage bonds, Baa-3; and adjustment mortgage bonds, Ba-1. Moody's said it views favorably Santa Fe's refinancing of its PIK debentures, its proposed spinoff of its real estate and energy units, and its plan to speed up the restructuring of its railroad operations. The refinancing, which includes an offer to exchange equity for $200 million of the debentures, could improve the company's leverage and debt coverage if bondholders tender enough of their holdings, Moody's said, and the railroad restructuring could enhance the company's cash flow.
