Letters to the Editor: The Only Drug With No Good Uses

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/09/90

With reference to your June 25 article "Smoking Curbs Get a New Lift From EPA Plan," I can only ask why the government did not begin smoking restrictions back in 1964, when the first surgeon general's warning on smoking was issued. Tobacco is the only drug that has absolutely no good uses, especially when used as intended. Yet, the tobacco industry (death merchants) and many smokers continue to fabricate excuses to allow this travesty to continue, rather than to work to find ways to solve the multitude of problems created by tobacco consumption.

When I hear of the tobacco states creating legislation to actually promote smoking, rather than to attempt to curb it, I can only say that the responsible politicians are not exercising the prime objective of the government: to protect the people.

A lot of people in this country need to wake up. While we claim to be fighting a "war on drugs," the tobacco industry continues to commit homicide at the rate of 400,000 Americans each year. We must do everything we can to stop them.

Douglas F. Hindle Jr.

Arlington, Texas
