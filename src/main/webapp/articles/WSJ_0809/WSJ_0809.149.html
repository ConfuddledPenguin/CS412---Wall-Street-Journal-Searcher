What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/03/90

PHILIPS PLANS to cut 10,000 jobs, about 3.5% of its work force, and expects a loss this year of about $1 billion. But newly installed Chairman Jan Timmer said the Dutch electronics giant won't give up on its troubled computer and semiconductor operations. Philips's share price tumbled on the announcement. Analysts criticized the job cuts as too small. ---

Ralph Ingersoll II is relinquishing his debt-burdened U.S. publishing empire to partner Warburg Pincus. In exchange, Ingersoll will get Warburg's 50% interest in a publisher of English and Irish newspapers.

---

General Instrument received a $1.42 billion buy-out bid from Forstmann Little, which has dominated the buy-out field this year. General Instrument's stock surged on the news, and competing bids for the maker of cable-TV gear could materialize.

---

Saks's trading profit totaled $26.4 million in 1990's first half, 20% less than new owner Investcorp forecast when it bid for the retailer. The firm plans to slash Saks's capital spending.

---

Lotus sued Borland, saying Borland's Quattro software violates the copyright of Lotus's 1-2-3 spreadsheet program. The suit follows Lotus's victory last week in a similar case.

---

Coca-Cola named its top person in Europe, M. Douglas Ivester, to head the Coca-Cola USA unit, fueling speculation that he is the front-runner to succeed the parent firm's president.

---

Manufacturers showed more signs of emerging from their long slump, according to purchasing managers, whose index of indicators rose in June. Separately, construction spending slipped 0.4% in May, its second drop in a row, the government said.

---

Ratners Group of Britain offered to acquire Kay Jewelers in a stock swap. The U.S. company's board unanimously approved the proposed deal, but the transaction could be held up by holders of Kay's junk bonds.

---

The dollar declined, continuing a pattern that some traders think may persist. Stocks gained and bonds held even in thin trading. The Dow Jones industrials rose 18.57 points.

---

Foreign holdings in the U.S. exceeded U.S. holdings abroad last year by $663.75 billion, nearly 25% more than the year before, but the government said the number was inaccurate.

---

Soybean prices rallied wildly amid forecasts of intense heat in the Midwest and the Mississippi Delta.

---

Southland disclosed details of its new debt exchange offer. Bondholders reacted with cautious optimism. The plan includes a reverse stock split.

---

Thrift regulators eased temporarily new restrictions on the size of S&L loans to a single borrower.

---

US Sprint formed a joint venture with the Soviet government to provide high-speed data communications services in the U.S.S.R., beginning as early as this year's fourth quarter.
