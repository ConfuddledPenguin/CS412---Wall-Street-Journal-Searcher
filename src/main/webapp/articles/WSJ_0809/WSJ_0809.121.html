Marketing Brief -- PolyGram N.V.: Analysts Estimate Earnings Fell 15% to 25% in 1st Half

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/09/90

PolyGram N.V., the recorded music company 80%-owned by N.V. Philips of the Netherlands, is expected to report Tuesday that its first-half net income fell 15% to 25%, London investment analysts said. Several analysts predicted, however, that PolyGram would still show a rise in net for the full year.

The first half was disappointing partly because of teething problems with two record labels PolyGram has acquired in the past 13 months, Island International Ltd. and A&M Records Inc. Analysts said efforts to blend those labels into PolyGram's distribution system held up some record releases and produced exceptional costs.

In addition, the acquisitions increased PolyGram's interest costs. Analysts also noted that the year-earlier half was exceptionally strong.

James Golob, an analyst at S.G. Warburg Securities, estimated that PolyGram's first half net tumbled 25% to 111 million Dutch guilders ($62 million) from 148 million guilders ($83 million) a year earlier. But he predicted that full-year net would rise 11% to 370 million guilders.

Analysts at James Capel & Co. forecast a drop of about 15% for the half and a rise of 13% for the year.

Despite the slow start, Warburg's Mr. Golob said that the "strategic logic of the two acquisitions is still very much intact." Long a powerhouse in Europe, PolyGram has said it aims to use Island and A&M to bolster its presence in the U.S.

Philips sold 20% of PolyGram late last year in a public offering totaling about one billion guilders.
