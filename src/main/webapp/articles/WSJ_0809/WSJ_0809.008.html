Who's News: Chemical Banking Forms Six-Member Chairman's Office

  • Author: Douglas R. Sease
  • Date: 08/09/90

NEW YORK -- Chemical Banking Corp. created a six-member "office of the chairman" to run the bank-holding company and its principal bank subsidiaries. Walter V. Shipley, the 54-year-old chairman and chief executive officer of Chemical Banking, said that he, Vice Chairman Robert J. Callander and four other executives will constitute the new office, which replaces a 15-man management committee.

Mr. Callander, 59, was named president of the holding company. The four other executives are Edward A. O'Neal Jr., 46; William H. Turner, 50; William B. Harrison, 47; and Marc J. Shapiro, 43. Messrs. O'Neal, Turner and Harrison were named vice chairmen of the Chemical Bank subsidiary. Mr. Shapiro continues as president and chief executive officer of Texas Commerce Bancshares. The four positions are equal in stature, Mr. Shipley said.

Mr. Shipley said Chemical has been telling analysts and investors for the past year that they should view the company as four basic businesses -- an institutional bank, a technology-based transaction business and two regional banks, one in New York and the other in Texas. "We have now positioned those four businesses at the top level of the organization," he said.

In the new structure Mr. Harrison will be responsible for world-wide institutional banking activities, Mr. O'Neal will run the transactions business, Mr. Turner will be responsible for regional banking in New York and Mr. Shapiro will continue to run the Texas bank.

The naming of three vice chairmen is somewhat reminiscent of an earlier structure in which Chemical had three presidents. In 1983 the company named Mr. Callander, Robert Lipp and Thomas Johnson, presidents. But Mr. Lipp left Chemical in 1986 and the organization returned to a more traditional structure, with Mr. Callander serving as one of two vice chairmen and Mr. Johnson as the sole president. Mr. Johnson left Chemical last December. The other vice chairman, Richard S. Simmons, 62, plans to retire Oct. 15.

Mr. Shipley said the reorganization of Chemical's top management isn't a prelude to further cost-cutting measures, noting that Chemical's expenses declined in each of the last three years. "This corporation is operating at lower expense base today than it was in 1987," he said.

Chemical laid off about 2,000 employees in 1987 and 1,000 more earlier this year.
