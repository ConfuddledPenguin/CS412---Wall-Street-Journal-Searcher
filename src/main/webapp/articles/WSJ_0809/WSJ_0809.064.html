Two More Suits Allege Ill Effects Of Lilly's Prozac

  • Author: Sue Shellenbarger
  • Date: 08/09/90

CHICAGO -- Eli Lilly & Co. was hit by two more lawsuits charging that its new antidepressant drug Prozac causes thoughts of suicide and has other destructive side effects. The suits are the third and fourth of their kind in recent weeks. Each seeks unspecified punitive damages against the drug maker on behalf of patients using Prozac, which has quickly become the most widely prescribed antidepressant in the country. A Lilly spokeswoman declined yesterday to comment on the litigation. The company reiterated, however, that its data on Prozac, which has been used on more than two million patients, don't show "a cause-and-effect relationship between our product and suicidal thoughts or acts."

One suit, filed late Tuesday in state court in Marion County, Ind., seeks unspecified damages on behalf of Janet C. Sims, a 23-year-old woman from Noblesville, Ind. She claims Prozac caused her "to suffer grievous, serious and severe permanent physical and personal injuries, mental and emotional distress, pain and suffering, adversely affecting her earning capacity and ability to enjoy life."

Another claim, filed in federal court for the Northern District of Illinois in Chicago, asserts that a suburban Chicago man, Ralph Langer, attempted suicide after taking Prozac in 1988 by intentionally driving his car into a truck. Mr. Langer, who is seeking $1 million in compensatory damages and unspecified punitive damages, fractured two vertebrae in the accident and still suffers from the injuries, according to the complaint.

In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange, Lilly common closed at $78.375 a share yesterday, up $2.625.

Last month, Lilly was sued in state court in Suffolk County, New York, for $150 million in damages by a New York woman, who charged that Prozac caused her to make attempts at suicide. Separately, the widows of three victims in a September 1989 mass shooting by a Prozac user in Louisville, Ky., also filed suit in state court in Jefferson County, Ky., seeking $150 million in punitive damages.

In a recent internal informational document on Prozac, the Food and Drug Administration described the reports of adverse effects as "not unexpected . . . Many {of the reports} involve events that are already described in Prozac's labeling" and "are of the type that would be expected . . . in depressed patients," the agency said. Prozac's labeling warns of the general risk of suicide in depressed patients, the agency said.

However, the FDA said Lilly recently revised its Prozac labeling to include specific mention of cases where patients exhibited "obsessive preoccupation with thoughts of suicide" after taking Prozac.
