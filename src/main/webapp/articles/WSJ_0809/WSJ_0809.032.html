British Aerospace Gets Order

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/09/90

WASHINGTON -- British Aerospace PLC's U.S. unit said that AMR Corp.'s AMR Eagle Inc. regional airline subsidiary has placed a follow-on order for 35 BAE Jetstream Super 31s, with options on a further 15 aircraft. The total value of the order plus options is about $200 million, British Aerospace Inc. said.

The order exercises options for 25 aircraft AMR Eagle had reserved as part of the Jetstream transaction announced in June 1989, plus an additional 10 aircraft.

The airline also has existing options for 50 of the new Jetstream 41 aircraft, to be operated in a 29-seat configuration.

Delivery of the first Jetstream Super 31 from this second order is scheduled for November with remaining aircraft delivered by the end of 1991.

AMR Corp. also is the parent of American Airlines.
