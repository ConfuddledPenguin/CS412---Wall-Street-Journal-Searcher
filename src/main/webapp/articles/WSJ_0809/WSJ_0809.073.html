Marketing & Media: Spelling Entertainment Ends Plan for Possible Sale

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/09/90

LOS ANGELES -- Spelling Entertainment Inc. said it "is no longer exploring a possible sale of the company." Spelling, an entertainment production and distribution company, put itself on the block 10 months ago, saying it had retained Merrill Lynch & Co.'s Merrill Lynch Capital Markets unit and Drexel Burnham Lambert Inc. to look into "various strategic alternatives for enhancing shareholder value, including the possible sale of the company."

At the time, it said several companies had expressed an interest in Spelling. The company was put on the block just six months after control of the concern switched to Cincinnati investor Carl Lindner from producer and founder Aaron Spelling. At the time of the announcement, some analysts said they didn't think the company was that attractive because of little continuing programming.

Spelling executives couldn't be reached for comment about how much interest had been generated. The company said in a press release that the interest it had received didn't reflect management's valuation of the company.

In the third quarter ended April 30, net income fell nearly 64% to $954,000, or three cents a share, from $2.6 million, or nine cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue declined 40% to $37.4 million from $62.1 million.
