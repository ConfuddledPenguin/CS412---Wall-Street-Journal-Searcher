New Securities Issues

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/09/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report. MUNICIPAL

Massachusetts -- $600 million of 7 1/8% general obligation bond anticipation notes due Oct. 17, priced by a Goldman Sachs & Co. group to yield 5.85%. The bonds are rated MIG-1/SP-1-Plus.

New York City Municipal Water Authority -- $285 million of water and sewer system revenue bonds, priced by a Smith Barney, Harris Upham & Co. group. The 1991 Series A bonds were priced to yield from 6.20% in 1991 to 7.70% in 2019. Serial bond yields range from 6.20% in 1991 to 7.50% in 2005. Zero-coupon capital appreciation bonds due in 2006-10 yield 7.25%. The issue has been rated single-A/single-A-minus. There are $76.8 million of 7.25% term bonds priced to yield 7.40% in 2015, $88.9 million of 7 1/2% term bonds priced to yield 7.70% in 2019 and $69.1 million of 6% term bonds priced to yield 7.55% in 2020.

Port Authority of New York and New Jersey -- $100 million of consolidated bonds, 70th Series, via a Prudential-Bache Capital Funding group. The bonds were priced to yield from 7.10% in 2005 to 7.40% in 2025. The issue is rated single-A-1/double-A-minus. Term bonds have a 7 1/4% coupon and are priced to yield 7.40% in 2025.

MORTGAGES

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $500.2 million of Remic mortgage securities offered in 19 classes by Kidder, Peabody & Co. The offering, Series 1990-109, is backed by Fannie Mae 9 1/2 securities. Price and yield data weren't immediately available.

Collateralized Mortgage Obligation Trust 63 -- $500 million of Remic mortgage securities offered in nine classes by Salomon Brothers Inc. The offering is backed by Government National Mortgage Association securities with a weighted average coupon rate of 9% and weighted average remaining term to maturity of 26.33 years. Among classes for which details were available, planned yield spreads on the 9% coupon issue ranged from 60 basis points above three-year Treasury notes to 130 basis points above 10-year Treasurys. There is an optional redemption at par when 5% of the bonds remain outstanding.

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $300 million of Remic mortgage securities offered in 14 classes by Citicorp. The offering, Series 1990-110, is backed by Fannie Mae 9% securities. Price and yield data weren't immediately available.

ASSET-BACKED

Discover Card Trust -- $500 million of 1990C Class A credit card pass-through certificates offered with a coupon rate of 8.75% at 99.765 to yield 8.87% through Dean Witter Reynolds. The securities yield 70 basis points over the Treasury's three-year note. The offering has an average life of 36.5 months and an expected final maturity of 42 months. The legal maturity is 5 1/2 years. The underlying credit-card receivables were sold by Discover Receivables Financing Corp., a unit of Sears, Roebuck & Co. and serviced by Delaware-based Greenwood Trust Co. The securities are expected to be rated triple-A by Moody's Investors Service and Standard & Poor's Corp. based on the quality of the underlying loans and a subordinated class now totaling about 10% of the senior securities.

Southeast Bank Credit Card Trust -- $160 million 1990-B issue of securities backed by credit card receivables, offered with a coupon rate of 8.60% at 99 21/32 to yield 8.762% through Goldman Sachs & Co. The securities yield 75 basis points more than two-year Treasury notes. A triple-A rating is expected from Moody's and S&P based on the quality of the underlying credit card loans and a letter of credit from Credit Suisse covering 13% of the deal. The seller and servicer of the underlying loans is Southeast Bank. Expected final maturity is Jan. 15, 1993, and stated final maturity is Nov. 15, 1994.

AGENCY

Federal Farm Credit Banks -- $275 million of bonds due Sept. 5, 1995, priced at par for an initial yield of 8.74%. The bonds, which are noncallable for two years, will be reoffered at various prices. The bonds were priced at a spread of 31 basis points above the Treasury's five-year note through underwriters Morgan Stanley & Co.

EUROBONDS

There were no major European debt issues priced.
