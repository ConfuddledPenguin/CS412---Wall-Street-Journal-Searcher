Trucks Cleared to Pass On Fuel-Cost Rises Quickly

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/09/90

WASHINGTON -- The Interstate Commerce Commission gave truckers permission to pass on fuel-cost increases to shippers more quickly. The commission, citing the "sudden and often severe escalation" in fuel costs because of the Persian Gulf conflict, gave truckers permission temporarily to raise their rates with only a three-day notice instead of the usual seven-day notice.

"It is obvious," the ICC said, "that motor carriers must be afforded an improved opportunity to adjust rates, fares and charges to offset unanticipated fuel expenditures."

The action was in response to a request by the American Trucking Associations and other trucking groups, which had asked the ICC to let truckers raise rates with only a one-day notice. Kenneth Simonson, an American Trucking Association economist, said that the group was "happy" with the ICC action.

The three-day notice requirement will be in effect until Sept. 23, the ICC said. The commission took similar action last January in the face of rapid fuel-cost increases. For a 45-day period, it allowed truckers to raise rates after only a three-day notice.
