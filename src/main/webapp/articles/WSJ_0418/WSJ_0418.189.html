Unicorp American Reports Wider Losses For Quarter and Year

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/18/90

NEW YORK -- Unicorp American Corp., reporting sharply wider losses of $53 million and $96 million for the 1989 fourth quarter and full year, respectively, said it was trying to restructure a loan agreement that is already in default. Even if those talks succeed, the troubled real-estate and financial-services concern said, it can't continue operations for the next 12 months without an infusion of $15 million in new funds.

The mounting problems will force Price Waterhouse to express on Unicorp American's year-end financial statements "substantial doubt about the company's ability to continue as a going concern," Unicorp said.

The per-share loss in the fourth quarter was $3.06. A year earlier, the company had a net loss of $1.7 million, or 10 cents a share. Revenue rose 3% to $64.9 million from $63 million. The 1988 figures were restated, although it was unclear why. The number on the company's news release has been disconnected, according to a phone company recording.

For all of 1989, the loss per share totaled $5.61. A year earlier, Unicorp had a net loss of $8.7 million, or 57 cents a share. Revenue increased 9.4% to $263.1 million from $240.5 million.

In the negotiations with its bank lenders, which Unicorp didn't name, the company is seeking a waiver of certain covenants in the loan agreement, it said. It also is trying to get an extension of the loan's expiration date beyond August.

Assuming that the banks extend the loan agreement, Unicorp said, "the company believes that it will need at least $15 million in additional funds to continue its operations . . . for the next 12 months." It said it is talking with the lenders and its Canadian parent about the additional financial needs.
