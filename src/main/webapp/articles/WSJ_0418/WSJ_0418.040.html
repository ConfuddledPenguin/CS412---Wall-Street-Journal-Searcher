Letters to the Editor: Epilepsy Drug

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/18/90

Your March 1 article "FDA Asks Firms to Change Epilepsy Drug's Packaging" cited Tegretol, the brand name of Ciba-Geigy, and carbamazepine, sold as the generic, as losing one-third of their effectiveness if stored in humid conditions. We at the Epilepsy Institute in New York received hundreds of phone calls after the article appeared. In order to advise people with epilepsy correctly on this issue, we contacted the FDA, only to learn that the degree of humidity and length of time were not available. Thank you for making this information available to your readership.

Reina Berner

Executive Director

Epilepsy Institute

New York
