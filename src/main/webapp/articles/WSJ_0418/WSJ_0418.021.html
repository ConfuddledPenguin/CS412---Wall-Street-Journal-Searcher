OECD Inflation Rate Slows

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/18/90

PARIS -- A drop in energy prices and slower growth in food costs contributed to a decline in the monthly pace of consumer price growth in February for the 24 member nations of the Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development. The organization said consumer prices rose 0.6% in February, compared with a rise of 0.8% in January. But for the 12 months through February, OECD consumer prices rose 6.4%, higher than the 6.2% annual rate recorded in January.

The OECD said a 0.2% decline in energy prices contributed to the slower February rise, after energy prices grew 1.9% on average in January. Food prices in February rose 0.9% from January, about half the rise reported in the previous month. Excluding food and energy prices, the underlying rate of inflation in the OECD rose 0.5% in February from January, compared with 0.3% in January from December.
