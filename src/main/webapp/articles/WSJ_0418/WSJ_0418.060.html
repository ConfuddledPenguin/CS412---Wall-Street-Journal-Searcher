International: Kyotaru of Japan Buys U.S. Restaurants

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 04/18/90

TOKYO -- Kyotaru Co., a major Japanese restaurant chain operator, made its second big thrust into the U.S. market this year. It said it had acquired two restaurant chains and a portion of a third for $94.4 million as part of its strategy to internationalize operations.

Kyotaru said its U.S. subsidiary, Kyotaru International Inc., has purchased all shares of Paragon Steakhouse Restaurant from its holding company, Paragon Restaurant Group, for $86 million.

The chain currently has 54 restaurants, with about half in California and Arizona, and overall sales of $97.6 million a year. The acquired chain has overall assets of $53 million and net assets of $34.7 million.

Kyotaru International also acquired all the stock of Love's Enterprise Co., a family restaurant chain with 22 outlets, primarily in California, from BSB Food Enterprise Co.

The price of the acquisition was $2.6 million. Love's Enterprise has overall assets of $1.4 million and net assets of $1.2 million, with yearly sales of $3.3 million.

Kyotaru's third acquisition was made through its subsidiary, KFF Management Inc., which purchased 16 Arby's roast beef sandwich fast-food restaurants from DGH Food Service Inc. for $5.8 million.

The 16 Arby's, located in California's Los Angeles County, have yearly sales of $9.7 million.

Earlier this year, Restaurant Associates Industries, which operates about 120 restaurants, said in New York that Kyotaru acquired an 85% interest in the company.

Restaurant Associates, which went private in 1988, operates Mamma Leone's restaurant in New York, as well as the Brasserie. It also operates the Acapulco, Charlie Brown's and The Office restaurant chains.

Kyotaru has annual sales of about $500 million, with operations in department stores, railroad stations and shopping centers.
