Duncan Cook Sues One of Its Founders, Who Denies Charges

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/18/90

HOUSTON -- The small but well-respected Texas investment banking firm of Duncan, Cook & Co. sued one of its founders, alleging that he diverted millions of dollars for personal use. The company also said it is changing its name to Gulf Partners Inc., effective immediately.

In a lawsuit seeking at least $5 million in damages, the firm charges former President and Treasurer Stephen C. Cook with channeling company money into his own bank accounts to satisfy large personal cash needs. It alleges that he used the funds to underwrite costs related to a $1.4 million house in an exclusive neighborhood here, a 2,800-acre recreational estate in New York, more than a million dollars of investments and "a high standard of living."

Mr. Cook, an original shareholder in the firm, denied the allegations. "Although I will accept responsibility as the former chief executive officer for certain administrative problems, the lawsuit seriously distorts the reality and clouds a number of economic and compensation issues involved," said Mr. Cook. He declined to elaborate.

The suit says Mr. Cook, who was responsible for Duncan Cook's daily operations and maintaining its books and records, resigned from all his positions at the firm when his "wrongful acts" were discovered.

Mr. Cook, however, said he left to start his own investment banking firm. "I intend to proceed with that," he said. "I have enough friends in the investment community who know me well enough that I'm confident they will keep an open mind until this litigation is finalized."
