Maxus Energy Faces $51.5 Million Waste Cleanup

  • Author: Frederick Rose
  • Date: 04/18/90

Maxus Energy Corp. will pay a $2.5 million fine and about $51.5 million to clean up 26 chromium waste sites in the New Jersey cities of Kearny and Secaucus, a spokeswoman said. The chemical waste resulted from operations by Diamond Shamrock Chemical Co. many years ago.

The fine -- the largest ever paid to the state of New Jersey for environmental violations -- is far less than the $25 million the state once said it would seek.

However, the $51.5 million cleanup commitment by Maxus is substantially larger than previous disclosures by the company, although the outlays won't affect the company's public projections of environmental cleanup costs for this year and next, because most of the money will be provided in 1992 and 1993.

Diamond Shamrock Chemical was sold to Occidental Petroleum Corp. in 1986 for about $910 million. Maxus, now an independent Dallas petroleum producer, indemnified Occidental at the time of the sale against the costs of environmental cleanup.

According to a statement by New Jersey Gov. Jim Florio, the fine and remedial work were agreed to in a pact between Occidental's chemicals unit and the state's Department of Environmental Protection. However, the negotiations were undertaken by Maxus, which also will fund and oversee the cleanup. Chromium operations in New Jersey were shuttered by Diamond Shamrock in 1972 -- long before the sale to Occidental.

The cleanup work will be financed by Maxus through a $20 million letter of credit and a $31.5 million working fund that is to be filled over a four-year period, the company's spokeswoman said. Payment to the fund will total $4 million this year, $7.5 million next year, and $10 million in each of 1992 and 1993.

The outlays for this year and next are already accounted for in projections by Maxus that it will spend $18.7 million this year on environmental compliance and $23.1 million next year.

However, the total cost of the plan is substantially higher than Maxus's disclosure in its year-end financial report that interim remedial measures at sites in Kearny and Secaucus would cost about $6 million and that certain further cleanup in Kearny would cost about $8 million.
