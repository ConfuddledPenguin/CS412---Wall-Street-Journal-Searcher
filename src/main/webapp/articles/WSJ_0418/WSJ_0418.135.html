International Brief -- Societe Generale de Belgique S.A.: Earnings Posted for 1989, After Revised Loss for 1988

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/18/90

Boosted by a sharp profit rise from nonferrous metals and mining, Societe Generale de Belgique S.A. posted consolidated 1989 earnings of 20.1 billion Belgian francs ($578.9 million), compared with a revised year-earlier loss of 872 million francs. Operating profit rose to 16.8 billion francs from a revised 9.1 billion francs. Data for both consolidated and operating profit exclude earnings from companies in which Generale holds a noncontrolling minority interest.

Most of the gain in operating earnings came from the nonferrous metals and mining unit, ACEC-Union Miniere S.A., which benefited from high prices for zinc, nickel, and other nonferrous metals on world markets in 1989. ACEC's contribution to profit more than tripled to 9.6 billion francs.

The healthy performance also reflected Generale's emergence from a yearlong restructuring program, which involved heavy one-time losses. Extraordinary items contributed 3.3 billion francs to 1989 results, compared with a revised one-time extraordinary loss of 10 billion francs in 1988, mostly related to restructuring.
