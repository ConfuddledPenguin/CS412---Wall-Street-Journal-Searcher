Marketing & Media: American Express Unit Buys Another Regional Magazine

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/18/90

NEW YORK -- American Express Publishing Corp., continuing to expand its magazine properties, agreed to acquire Southwest Media Corp., publisher of D Magazine, the city magazine of Dallas and Fort Worth, Texas. American Express Publishing purchased the 15-year-old magazine, an upscale monthly, from Southwest Media, a closely held company whose majority owner is Ray L. Hunt. Terms weren't disclosed.

The acquisition adds another title to what has become the country's largest owner of city-regional magazines. American Express Publishing, a unit of American Express Co., last year acquired Atlanta magazine, and in recent years added L.A. Style and New York Woman magazines. The company also publishes Travel & Leisure, Food & Wine, and Departures magazines. D Magazine has a paid circulation of more than 100,000.

In the last decade, a faltering economy has led to the closing of at least eight upscale magazines across Texas. Thomas O. Ryder, president of American Express Publishing, says, however, that the economy of the state has turned a corner and is improving.

"The Dallas-Fort Worth area has been seriously depressed for some years, but there are clear signs the market is on its way back solidly," said Mr. Ryder. "We want to rebuild with it." He pointed to a rebound in new-home sales as one positive economic sign.

Mr. Hunt said he considered keeping D Magazine "as a profitable investment." But added, "in order for it to grow, I felt it needed to become part of a larger publishing operation."
