Polaroid Profit Rose 23% in First Quarter, Trailing Estimates

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/18/90

CAMBRIDGE, Mass. -- Polaroid Corp. reported a disappointing 23% rise in first-quarter earnings on a sales decline of 1.4% and again scaled back its own projections for earnings growth in 1990. Citing a "slower than expected" world-wide business environment, the company said it now expects only "moderate growth" in 1990, instead of the "substantially higher" earnings it had projected in November.

A Polaroid spokesman declined to be more specific about the company's outlook for 1990.

For the first quarter, Polaroid posted earnings of $31.8 million, or 43 cents a share, compared with $25.9 million, or 29 cents a share, in the like 1989 quarter. The year-earlier figure was after $38.5 million pretax charge representing the cost of fighting off a hostile takeover bid by Shamrock Holdings Inc. Sales slipped to $436.2 million from $442.3 million.

The company's results, which were released shortly before the stock market closed, were below Wall Street estimates of about 48 cents a share, according to Prudential-Bache analyst Alex Henderson. Mr. Henderson had been expecting 52 cents a share. Polaroid closed at $45.50, down 25 cents, in New York Stock Exchange composite trading.

Mr. Henderson expressed concern about the timing of Polaroid's introduction of a new line of film and said he plans to lower his earnings estimates for 1990 to about $2.50 to $2.55 a share from $2.75 and for 1991 to $3.10 to $3.15 a share from $3.25.

"My basic concern is, in order for them to make their second-half numbers, they need to introduce the new Hybrid IV instant film early enough to start ramping it up in the third quarter to be able to fill the shelves at Christmas," Mr. Henderson said. Instead, he said, the company is suggesting that the product won't be available in the third quarter and will only be available in limited volume in the fourth quarter.

I. MacAllister Booth, Polaroid's chairman and chief executive, said the company decided to lower its own expectations after reviewing general world-wide economic conditions. "In light of this changing climate, we have lowered our expectations for the year." He added, however, that the company expects moderate growth in both sales and operating profit in 1990.
