Dow Chemical, Huntsman Offer Recycling Program

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/18/90

WASHINGTON -- Two plastics manufacturers will sponsor the first comprehensive recycling program in four national parks this year in hopes of preventing bans on plastic by showing that it is recyclable. Dow Chemical Co. has agreed to pay for collecting and recycling various plastics, glass and aluminum in Acadia National Park in Maine, Great Smoky Mountain in Tennessee and North Carolina, Grand Canyon's south rim in Arizona, and Yosemite in California, said Joseph Gorrell, a National Park Service official.

A Dow spokesman said Salt Lake City-based Huntsman Holdings Corp., the nation's largest producer of polystyrene plastic, has agreed to share the program's cost. The five-year program is to be announced tomorrow in Washington.

Dow, a major plastics producer based in Midland, Mich., proposed the program because many states and local governments have adopted or are considering laws that would ban or restrict plastic packaging and products in an effort to reduce solid waste.

"We know we have to get the plastic recycling rate at least as high as aluminum and glass if we are going to compete with those materials," said the Dow spokesman. Only about 1% of plastic is currently recycled, compared with about 55% of aluminum and about 11% of glass.
