Sweden's Tax Overhaul Plan

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/18/90

STOCKHOLM -- The Swedish government said domestic households' disposable income would increase 2% next year, under the final version of the tax overhaul proposal it unveiled Tuesday. The Social Democratic minority government also proposed in the document to allow companies to deduct 65% of the value of their stock in foreign units. The revisions, which aim to cut and simplify taxes while broadening the tax base over a two-year period, reduce the top marginal tax to 50% from 80.2%.

State tax will be canceled on wages of up to 180,000 kronor ($29,502), and those wage earners will pay only city or local council tax of about 30%. About 85% of all taxpayers will pay only the local tax.

The 76 billion-krona ($12.46 billion) overhaul is guaranteed passage by the Riksdag, Sweden's parliament, despite the goverment's lack of majority after its deal with the opposition Liberal Party last week.
