Marketing Brief -- Gannett Co.: Profit for 1st Quarter Flat; Reduced Ad Spending Cited

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/18/90

Gannett Co., citing sharply reduced spending for newspaper advertisements, posted flat first-quarter net income. The Arlington, Va., media company's profit was $75 million, or 47 cents a share, compared with $74.9 million, also 47 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue eased less than 1% to $813.4 million from $817.6 million.

The year-ago results included a pre-tax gain of $9 million, or three cents a share, on the sale of newspaper operations.

Gannett blamed its flat performance on a deteriorating consumer economy and restructuring in the retail industry, saying this produced "the poorest advertising environment in almost 20 years." Gannett, the publisher of 83 daily newspapers including USA Today, said ad revenue declined 3%, reflecting a 6% decline in ad volume. Retail ad linage was off at most of its newspapers and was "particularly soft" at its eastern newspapers. USA Today's ad pages for the quarter dropped to 851 from 896 a year earlier. But because of rate increases, ad revenue rose 8%.
