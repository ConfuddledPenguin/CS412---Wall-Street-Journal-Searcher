Business Brief -- TRW Inc.: First-Period Net Fell 17% Higher Interest Costs Cited

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/18/90

TRW Inc. reported a 17% decline in first-quarter profit mainly because of higher interest expense. The automotive parts, defense and space contracting and information services concern earned $57 million, or 92 cents a share, down from $69 million, or $1.12 a share, a year earlier. The Cleveland-based company reports share earnings on a fully diluted basis.

Sales rose 12% to $1.99 billion from $1.79 billion.

Operating profit from the three major business groups rose to $159 million, from $150 million a year earlier, TRW said, but interest expense climbed to $52 million, from $29 million in the 1989 quarter. Borrowing for two major acquisitions in 1989, combined with soaring interest rates in Brazil to raise interest costs.

Auto and truck-parts operations showed a 7% rise in operating profit on a 15% sales gain, TRW said. Higher sales of occupant-restraint systems, in addition to a strong European market, more than offset the soft North American market and problems in Brazil.

Operating profit from space and defense contracting rose 12% on a 4% sales rise. And the information systems and services business had a 40% operating profit rise.
