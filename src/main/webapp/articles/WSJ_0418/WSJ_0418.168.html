Business Brief -- Baroid Corp.: Jury in New Orleans Finds Firm Innocent of Conspiracy

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/18/90

Baroid Corp. said a New Orleans jury found it, a retired vice president and other defendants innocent of trying to fix prices for oil-well completion fluids in 1986 and 1987. The Houston oil-field services concern -- controlled by Dallas investor Harold C. Simmons -- and retired executive Kenneth D. Tanner were indicted along with two other companies and an executive of one of those concerns in August.

The indictment charged that Baroid, then a unit of NL Industries Inc., and the two other companies conspired to increase their prices for completion fluids by decreasing the amount they paid customers for fluids that weren't used.

The company said it was "gratified" that the jury agreed with its position that it wasn't guilty of any offense.
