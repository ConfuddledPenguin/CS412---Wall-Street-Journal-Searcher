Raiders' Deal to Return To Oakland Is in Jeopardy

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/18/90

OAKLAND, Calif. -- A deal to bring the Los Angeles Raiders football team back to Oakland is in serious trouble due to mounting public opposition. Oakland Mayor Lionel Wilson said he planned to ask the Oakland city council late yesterday to rescind its approval of the proposal.

The mayor's action followed the delivery late last week of anti-Raiders petitions bearing 33,000 signatures, about 13,000 more than necessary to place the issue before Oakland's voters. Opponents had argued that the offer, which guaranteed the football franchise $428 million in payments over 15 years, would place an unwarranted burden on Oakland's treasury.

Only five weeks ago, Mayor Wilson had euphorically proclaimed the return of the team. But at a press conference this week, the mayor -- facing re-election this year -- said that if the Raiders deal "isn't dead, it's dying." Raiders officials themselves had said that they would kill the deal rather than face a ballot initiative.

However, Mayor Wilson didn't rule out renewed talks with the Raiders, and some Raiders boosters said there was a slim chance that a new accord could be struck to eliminate a major sticking point, that of guarantees by taxpayers. Opponents have charged that taxpayers would be forced to make up a $200 million deficit even if Raiders games sold out over the next 15 years.
