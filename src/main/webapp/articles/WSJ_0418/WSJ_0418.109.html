Activist's Plea Terminates Attempted-Murder Trial

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/18/90

STAMFORD, Conn. (AP) -- A New York woman's surprise decision not to contest attempted murder charges abruptly ended a trial that promised to shed light on the increasingly bitter and sometimes violent battle between animal-rights activists and corporations that experiment on animals. Fran Trutt, of Queens, N.Y., pleaded no contest in Superior Court in Stamford Monday to charges of attempted murder, possession of explosives and bomb manufacturing in exchange for a one-year prison sentence.

Ms. Trutt had been accused of trying to kill Leon C. Hirsch, chairman and chief executive officer of U.S. Surgical Corp., by planting a sophisticated pipe bomb near his company parking space in November 1988. Her arrest threw a spotlight on the animal-rights movement and the extent to which some activists would go to protect animals from the corporate lab. U.S. Surgical, Norwalk, Conn., uses live dogs to demonstrate its staplers to surgeons.
