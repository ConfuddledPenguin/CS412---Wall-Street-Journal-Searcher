Dividend News: Canadian Pacific Forest Payout Cut Due to Poor Results

  • Author: Robert Melnbardis
  • Date: 04/18/90

TORONTO -- Canadian Pacific Forest Products Ltd. cut its quarterly dividend 69% to 20 Canadian cents (17.2 U.S. cents) from 65 Canadian cents and reported substantially lower first-quarter earnings. Canadian Pacific reported a first-quarter profit of $4.8 million Canadian dollars (US$4.1 million) or 11 Canadian cents a share, down from net income of C$65.1 million, or C$1.48 a share, a year earlier. Sales declined 9.7% to C$642.1 million from C$711 million.

While the dividend reduction was expected, analysts who had been forecasting a 1990 net income of as much as C$3 a share are revising their earlier estimates.

"Everyone is a little shell-shocked that we could be off the mark so much," said one analyst, who added that he expects that the company will earn more than C$1 a share this year.

Canadian Pacific attributed the big earnings drop to lower net selling prices for newsprint and lower shipment volumes for market kraft woodpulp. Cecil Flenniken, chairman and chief executive officer, said after the company's annual meeting that it cut the dividend because of the poor first-quarter results.

"We see how {the dividend} compares with other forest products companies, and we seem to be completely out of line with {them}. But because of the earnings drop, we believe we should be a little bit more conservative with the dividend," Mr. Flenniken said.

Canadian Pacific Forest is 79.7%-owned by Canadian Pacific Ltd., a transportation, telecommunications and real estate conglomerate based in Montreal.

In trading on the Toronto Stock Exchange yesterday, Canadian Pacific closed at C$29, down C$3.50. On the New York Stock Exchange, Canadian Pacific Ltd. closed at US$20, down 12.5 US cents from Monday.

Despite the company's weak earnings in the first quarter, Mr. Flenniken, who declined to make a specific profit forecast, said he expects that the newsprint and market pulp markets aren't going to get worse. He said the current list price for northern bleached softwood kraft pulp is likely to remain at US$830 a metric ton in North America and US$840 a metric ton in Europe despite the lower volume of shipments in the industry. About 90% of Canadian Pacific Forest's market pulp production consists of that premium fiber, which will continue to be in high demand, he said. Market pulp is woodpulp sold on the open market to producers of paper, sanitary and other products. A metric ton equals 2,204.62 pounds.

On the newsprint side, Mr. Flenniken said producers don't appear to be backing down from their previously announced intention to raise the list price for newsprint on June 1 to US$685 a metric ton from US$650 a metric ton. Analysts aren't as optimistic. They don't think the newsprint price increase will stick, and they're forecasting a drop in market pulp prices this year.
