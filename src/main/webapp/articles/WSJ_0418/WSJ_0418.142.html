Marketing Brief -- Rax Restaurants Inc.: Fast-Food Chain to Switch To P&G's Crisco for Frying

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/18/90

Rax Restaurants Inc., a Columbus, Ohio-based chain of 500 restaurants, said it will switch to an all-vegetable oil for its fried foods next month. Rax will will replace its blend of animal and vegetable fats for Procter & Gamble Co.'s Crisco oil and promote its use of cholesterol-free Crisco in its menus and ads. This marks the first time Cincinnati-based P&G has sold Crisco oil to a restaurant chain; it was sold only in supermarkets.

Rax's switch comes amid growing concerns about the types of fats used in fast-food restaurants. McDonald's Corp. said this month it will test an all-vegetable oil to replace its beef tallow and vegetable blend used for french fries. Pillsbury Co.'s Burger King said it plans within two months to test french fries made in an all-vegetable oil. Hardees Food Systems Inc., a unit of Imasco Ltd., has used all-vegetable oil in its restaurants since 1987.

Rax said its switch will cost the chain between 30% and 40% more for its oil, but this can be reused more often than the animal-fat blend. P&G said it will give merchandising allowances and marketing help to Rax to introduce Crisco oil use.
