Business Brief -- Safeway Stores Inc.: First-Quarter Net Rose 71%; Improved Margins Are Cited

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/18/90

Safeway Stores Inc. said first-quarter net income surged 71% as a result of improved gross margins and a decline in interest expense. The Oakland, Calif., supermarket chain posted profit of $15.5 million, or 17 cents a share, up from $8.8 million, or 12 cents a share, a year earlier. Safeway had 88.2 million shares outstanding in the 1990 quarter ended March 24, compared with 79.7 million a year earlier.

Safeway said revenue for the quarter grew about 3% to $3.34 billion from $3.23 billion. The company said the increase came despite the fact that the important Easter shopping period occurred this year after the close of the quarter.

Safeway attributed its improved margins to the addition of profitable speciality departments in numerous stores, an aggressive merchandising campaign and a streamlining of its companywide buying practices. Safeway also said its interest expense fell 6% in the quarter to $83.8 million from $89.2 million a year earlier.

Safeway has been closely held since 1986, when it was taken private in a $4.3 billion leveraged buy-out by Kohlberg Kravis Roberts & Co. The company recently announced plans to go public again by offering 10 million, or 10%, of its shares later this month.
