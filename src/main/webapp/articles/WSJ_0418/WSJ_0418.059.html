Letters to the Editor: We Scrutinize Cars With Scientific Care

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/18/90

Without malicious intent, you erred in describing the annual survey of Consumer Reports subscribers, upon which our automobile Frequency-of-Repair records are based, by saying "it isn't scientific" ("Big Three Boost Car Quality but Still Lag," March 27). To the contrary, our survey techniques and data analyses are highly sophisticated and thoroughly scrutinized by experts inside and outside Consumers Union. Our data and the published findings are as accurate and objective as is scientifically possible in representing the experiences of our 4.5 million subscribers. This is CU's hallmark, and one we take great pride in. You also asserted that CU's data showed that six out of seven GM owners responding to our survey are unwilling to buy the same model again. Nothing in our magazine comes even close to such a conclusion.

R. David Pittle

Technical Director

Consumers Union Mount Vernon, N.Y.
