What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/18/90

CONSUMER PRICES ROSE 0.5% in March, pushing the first-quarter inflation rate to 8.5%, the highest in eight years. Though economists don't expect that pace to continue, the figures suggest inflation remains a problem. There also were signs of continued weakness in the economy.

Treasury bond prices slid following the inflation report, pushing the yield on the benchmark 30-year issue to 8.72%, the highest in almost a year. Stocks and the dollar were mixed.

---

Great American Bank said it needs to raise $350 million in new capital by year-end because its auditor forced it to boost loan-loss reserves. The disclosure raises questions about the future of the nation's eighth-biggest S&L.

---

Employers were dealt a blow by the Supreme Court, which upheld a federal labor policy that companies can't oust a striking union by hiring new workers and asserting the employees don't support the union.

---

Citicorp and NCNB posted lower-than-expected quarterly earnings as problem commercial loans ballooned.

Three big California thrifts reported robust quarterly profits, driven by a healthy housing market and widening net interest margins.

---

Ex-arbitrager Robert Freeman was given a four-month jail term and ordered to pay $1 million in fines for his role in an insider-trading case. He agreed in August to a guilty plea and to resign from Goldman Sachs.

---

Xoma sued rival Centocor for alleged patent infringement, sparking the latest such fight in biotechnology.

---

The NASD has proposed a long-awaited rule to tighten the limits on fees charged by mutual funds. But the rule wouldn't drastically change the way most funds charge investors.

---

Amcor Capital faces more woes. The IRS proposed disallowing some 1986 deductions by the agricultural tax-shelter firm. Also, two men with ties to Amcor were indicted.

---

Deutsche Bank plans to form a venture with East Germany's central bank, paving the way for the West German bank to take a lead role in East Germany's finance industry.

---

NCR's stock plunged $5.375, to $65.375, after the computer maker posted a 15% drop in quarterly profit.

---

Microsoft's profit soared 83% in its latest quarter as the software maker continued to outperform competitors.

---

Donaldson Lufkin was charged by the SEC with illegally using customers' stock that was left in the care of the firm's back-office operations. Donaldson settled the suit for $5.6 million.

---

General Electric posted a 12% increase in first-quarter profit, citing strong gains at most major businesses, except for the NBC unit.

---

Norton's takeover defense against BTR of Britain is expected to get some help from a new Massachusetts law. But takeover speculators say the law may only delay a BTR victory.
