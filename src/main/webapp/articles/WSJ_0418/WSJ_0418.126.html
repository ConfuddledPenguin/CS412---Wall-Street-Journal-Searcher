Southwestern Bell, Ameritech Say Profits Edged Up in Quarter

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 04/18/90

Two regional Bell operating companies reported modest first-quarter earnings growth. Southwestern Bell Corp. said net income increased 3.8%, reflecting strong growth in access lines and cellular telephone customers. Ameritech Corp. said its earnings rose less than 1% because of one-time expenses related to work force reductions. St. Louis-based Southwestern Bell posted net income of $247.8 million, or 82 cents a share, up from $238.8 million, or 79 cents a share, in the year-ago quarter. Revenue increased 3.5% to $2.13 billion from $2.06 billion.

Edward E. Whitacre Jr., Southwestern Bell chairman, said cellular customers increased by 59,000, or 15.4%, and access lines increased by 114,600, or 1%, during the first quarter. Operating expenses rose 4.1% to $1.67 billion. Positive rate adjustments also contributed to higher earnings, Mr. Whitacre said.

Ameritech's net income grew 0.9% to $303.5 million, or $1.13 a share, from $300.8 million, or $1.12 a share, in the year-ago first quarter. Revenue rose 4.7% to $2.62 billion from $2.50 billion. Expenses increased 5.6% to $2.18 billion from $2.06 billion.

William L. Weiss, Ameritech chairman, said results were affected by one-time expenses of $54 million related to work force reductions totalling 1,265 at Illinois Bell and Michigan Bell. Excluding one-time charges, expenses rose 3.2%. Customer access lines increased by 142,000, or 0.9%, and cellular customers increased by 15,000, or 6.2% during the first quarter.
