International: Deutsche Bank Plans Venture In East Germany

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 04/18/90

FRANKFURT -- Deutsche Bank AG said it has signed a letter of intent to form a banking joint venture in East Germany with Deutsche Kreditbank AG, the commercial banking arm of Staatsbank, East Germany's central bank. The accord paves the way for West Germany's largest commercial bank to take the leading role in the East German financial industry when the country's monetary union with West Germany is completed this summer. No financial details were disclosed.

Deutsche Bank said the East German partner will contribute facilities for the creation of a branch network while Deutsche Bank "contributes its know-how, its training capacity and its organization."

The joint venture will be formed once the necessary legal framework is in place, Deutsche Bank said. Starting April 1, East Germany introduced a Western-style commercial banking system, but so far hasn't granted any banking licenses to any West German or other foreign banks. All major West German commercial banks are planning to set up a branch network in the country once they are allowed to operate there.

Under the old Communist regime, the Staatsbank had a monopoly on all major financial transactions in East Germany, combining both commercial and central bank activities. But last month, the Staatsbank spun off its commercial banking activities into Deutsche Kreditbank, which is now a joint stock company temporarily owned by the Staatsbank.

Beside Kreditbank, there are only savings banks and cooperative banks operating in East Germany at the moment.

Deutsche Bank disclosed in late March that it is in talks with Kreditbank about various forms of cooperation and even considering acquiring an interest in the East German bank. Last month, Chairman Hilmar Kopper denied that it is weighing taking a direct stake in Kreditbank, but expressed interest in some of Kreditbank's facilities.

At the time, Mr. Kopper said he would like to see about 250 Deutsche Bank branches with 4,000 to 6,000 employees in the long term, but said the realization of that "vision" will take several years.

Kreditbank operates 150 branches all over East Germany and has 9,000 employees.
