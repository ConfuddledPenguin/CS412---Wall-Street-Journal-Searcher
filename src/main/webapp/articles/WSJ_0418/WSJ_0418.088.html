Who's News: Horsham Corp. Names Munk Chief Executive

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/18/90

TORONTO -- Peter Munk, chairman of Horsham Corp. was named chief executive officer, succeeding Ian Delaney who will resign as president and chief executive, the investment and oil and gas concern said. Jeremy Garbutt, 40-year-old chief financial officer of American Barrick Resources Corp., was named president and chief operating officer of Horsham. American Barrick, a big gold mining firm, is 22.4% owned by Horsham.

Mr. Delaney, 46 years old, said he resigned to set up his own corporate finance business. He said he will retain his 4.5% stake in Horsham's stock and remain a director of the company. "I'm not going too far away," he said. Mr. Delaney said he left his job as a merchant banker to create Horsham three years ago. "We've been successful beyond our wildest expectations . . . but {Horsham} doesn't need to be run by a merchant banker."

Mr. Munk, 62, is founder of both Horsham and American Barrick and controlling shareholder of Horsham. Horsham said he will take "an active role in the management" of the company.

American Barrick said it named Gregory Wilkins chief financial officer, succeeding Mr. Garbutt.
