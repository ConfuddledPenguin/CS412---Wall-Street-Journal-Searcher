Yamaichi Faces Loss In Client Bankruptcy

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/18/90

TOKYO (AP) -- Yamaichi Securities Co. of Japan said it faces a $24 million loss in connection with a bankruptcy filing last month by one customer, an institutional investor. Yamaichi, on orders in February from the customer, a company that operates coffee shops, bought three million, or one-seventh, of the shares outstanding of Nippon Seisen, an Osaka wire maker, for about $44 million for delivery in four days. Yamaichi was to get a $70,520 commission, but the customer "failed to settle the transaction and went bankrupt on March 30," Yamaichi said. Meanwhile, Nippon Seisen shares dived to $6.54 Tuesday from $14.65 in February, amid the Tokyo market's plunge.

If negotiations for remedy fail, Yamaichi said, it may go to court, citing possible manipulation, the illegal depressing or raising of prices artificially so they don't represent true value, accomplished by creating an appearance of active buying or selling, either alone or with others.
