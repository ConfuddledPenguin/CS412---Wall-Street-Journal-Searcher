Marketing & Media: McGraw-Hill Net Fell 47% in 1st Period; Year's Rise Is Forecast

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/18/90

NEW YORK -- McGraw-Hill Inc. said first-quarter net income fell 47%, although the publishing and business information company forecast improvement "throughout the year." Net income fell to $16.5 million, or 34 cents a share, from $31.2 million, or 64 cents a share; the year-earlier figure includes a gain of $8 million, or 16 cents a share, from a change in accounting for income taxes. Without the gain, net would have fallen 29%. Revenue rose 7.6% to $420 million from $390.3 million.

Joseph L. Dionne, chairman and chief executive officer, said the results were in line with expectations of a soft first quarter. He noted that the joint-venture school publishing company with Macmillan Inc. has increased the seasonality of the business; McGraw-Hill's share of the venture's first-quarter loss was $12.8 million, "actually less than anticipated. We expect fine results from the joint-venture company for the full year."

Borrowing for acquisitions increased interest expense to $12.5 million from $4.7 million; the company's tax rate, because of divestitures, was 43% in the latest period, up from 30% a year ago.

Mr. Dionne said the company expects to achieve its target for 1990 earnings of $3.50 to $4 a share (which, based on about 48.8 million average shares outstanding, would indicate net income in a range of $170 million to $195 million).

Operating profit for the largest unit, information and publication services, fell 29% to $19 million from $26.7 million, reflecting continuing investments in the construction information business, a shortage in certain operations of the computer and communications business unit, and decreased advertising at Business Week magazine.

Broadcasting operating profit fell because of increased programming costs and softness in some markets. Operating profit from financial services rose 22% to $32.1 million from $26.4 million.
