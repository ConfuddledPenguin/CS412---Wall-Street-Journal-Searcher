Finnish Inflation Eases

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/18/90

HELSINKI -- Finland's inflation rate fell to 6.5% in March from 7.4% in February and was down from 6.6% a year earlier, the Central Statistical Office said. The principal reason for the easing in the consumer price index was a slower rise in home prices, the statistical office said.

House prices went up 10.7% in March, against more than 13% earlier this year and in 1989, the office said.
