Technology: Sony Selects Site To Make TV Tubes In U.S. Expansion

  • Author: Alecia Swasy
  • Date: 04/18/90

PITTSBURGH -- Sony Corp. said its decision to produce television picture tubes in New Stanton, Pa., is the first step in plans to invest between $500 million and $1 billion in the U.S. in the next few years. The Japanese electronics concern plans to spend $300 million retooling an idled Volkswagen AG plant to produce one million television tubes annually for its large-screen Trinitron sets sold in the U.S. and in export markets. The plant will have 1,000 employees within three years after production begins in 1992.

Sony said part of the additional investment will be targeted for its television manufacturing plant in San Diego. That plant will use more computerized manufacturing, moving more of the labor-intensive work to facilities in Mexico, Sony officials said.

Though Sony also was expected to announce plans to assemble television sets in New Stanton, company officials said yesterday that a decision hasn't been made on that issue. They said the company is still considering several locations, including the former Volkswagen plant. Sony officials met this week with Kentucky officials to assure them that that state was still being considered for the television assembly plant.

Eventually, the site chosen for television assembly could be home to Sony's first U.S. production of high-definition television sets. Neil Vander Dussen, president of Sony Corp. of America, said Sony is awaiting federal government guidelines on the new technology, expected to be released in 1991, before deciding where to locate U.S. production.

In winning over Sony for its television picture-tube plant, Pennsylvania competed with several other states, including Kentucky and Ohio. The Pennsylvania incentive package promises about $23.5 million in low-interest loans for renovations and job training grants, among other things. Kentucky apparently offered a hefty package valued at between $30 million and $40 million, including free land and funds for infrastructure improvements and training workers.

But Sony officials said they favored the New Stanton site over others because of its proximity to interstate highways and the availability of skilled workers.

Moreover, Pennsylvania agreed to waive a $40 million mortgage owed by Volkswagen in exchange for ownership of the West German auto maker's plant and 1,200-acre site. Sony will pay about $21 million in lease payments for about 800 acres of the property.
