Business Brief -- Citizens & Southern Corp.: Bank Exam Could Require Charge Against Earnings

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/18/90

Citizens & Southern Corp. indicated that results of a federal bank examination may cause it to take a charge against first-quarter earnings to offset possible loan losses. The Atlanta-based banking concern wouldn't specify the potential increase in its loan-loss reserve or the actual loan losses for the period. But it said that "because of weakened conditions within commercial real estate markets since year-end and comparable actions taken following the {Office of the Comptroller of the Currency} exam at other banks, increases in nonperforming loans are expected."

John J. Mason, an analyst at Interstate/Johnson Lane Inc., estimated recently that Citizens' problem loans may rise by about $70 million. The report didn't rule out, however, a $100 million increase. This would prompt an rise in the company's loan-loss reserve and subsequently reduce first-quarter earnings to about 44 cents a share, Mr. Mason said. In the 1989 first quarter, Citizens had a profit of $57.8 million, or 90 cents a share.

Citizens said it will delay release of its first-quarter earnings results until the special examination of its commercial real estate portfolio is completed.
