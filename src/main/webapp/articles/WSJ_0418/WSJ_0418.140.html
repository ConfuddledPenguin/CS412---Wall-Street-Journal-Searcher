Business Brief -- AmeriFirst Bank: Judge Approves Settlement Of 4 Shareholder Lawsuits

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/18/90

AmeriFirst Bank, Miami, said a federal judge approved its $4.5 million settlement of four shareholder lawsuits against it. The thrift said members of the class action have until June 1 to submit a claim to share in the settlement, which results from suits alleging it misled shareholders about its financial condition in public filings.

As previously reported, AmeriFirst agreed to pay its shareholders $4.5 million, of which $2 million was placed in escrow in February. The balance is due within 30 months.

The company said the settlement was an essential first step toward its objective of raising new capital to meet regulatory requirements.

Meanwhile, AmeriFirst said it is continuing to press its own suit against certain former top managers and outside auditors, claiming that lending and auditing practices led to the company's current financial woes. Under the settlement, the thrift will share part of any funds recovered from the suit with the shareholder class members who purchased the bank's stock from November 1986 through December 1989.
