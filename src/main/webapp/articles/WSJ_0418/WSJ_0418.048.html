California Tops FDIC List Of Risky Real Estate Areas

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/18/90

DALLAS -- California leads a list of potentially risky real estate markets identified by the Federal Deposit Insurance Corp. as part of its effort to help lenders and regulators sidestep future trouble spots. The ranking of 40 real estate markets represents the FDIC's first attempt to identify areas where office vacancy rates and construction rates are rising, while office employment is declining.

Six California cities fell within the top 25 markets with higher-than-average vacancy and construction rates and lower-than-average employment rates. Anaheim was ranked third overall, and Sacramento was 24th on the list. In between were San Jose, Los Angeles, Riverside and San Diego.

At the top of the FDIC's list were Phoenix, Ariz., and Nashville, Tenn. Houston is ranked at the bottom of the list, indicating few office construction starts, a declining vacancy rate and growth in office employment.

FDIC Chairman William Seidman, who released the study here yesterday, cautioned that a market's ranking doesn't signify it is headed for a real estate crash.
