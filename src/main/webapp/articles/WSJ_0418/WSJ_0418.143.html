Business Brief -- Campeau Corp.: Two Jordan Marsh Stores To Close in New England

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/18/90

Allied Stores Corp. will close its Jordan Marsh stores in Lowell, Mass., and Danbury, Conn. The store closings are the first since Allied and Federated Department Stores Inc. filed for bankruptcy court protection on Jan. 15. Federated and Allied spokeswoman Carol Sanger said the store closings aren't related to the bankruptcy-court filings of the two Campeau Corp. units. Robert Gottlieb, Jordan Marsh senior vice president for marketing, said both closings had been under consideration before the Chapter 11 proceedings began. But, he said, the filings may have "sped up the process a little bit. Certainly cash flow becomes a major factor."

Both stores were poor performers, according to Mr. Gottlieb. The Lowell store will close June 20, the Danbury store, around July 15.

Mr. Gottlieb said Jordan Marsh would try to find jobs at other stores for the 82 employees of the Lowell store and the 129 employees of the Danbury store.

A lawyer representing Allied creditors complained that the company hadn't sought approval from the bankruptcy court judge. Such approval isn't needed for decisions made in the ordinary course of business.
