Smith Corona Corp. Reports 51% Decline In Its 3rd-Period Net

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/18/90

NEW CANAAN, Conn. -- Smith Corona Corp. reported that fiscal third-quarter net income fell 51%, as sales of its pricier typewriters and word processors eased. Strong demand for lower-priced portable electronic typewriters continues, but it's not enough to offset slow high-end sales, said G. Lee Thompson, chairman and chief executive officer. He cited a sluggish U.S. economy plus price competition from Asian manufacturers.

For the third period ended March 31, net fell to $6.1 million, or 20 cents a share, from pro forma net of $12.4 million, or 41 cents a share, in the year-earlier period. Smith Corona went public in August 1989. The year-ago figures also reflect a charge of five cents a share, stemming from the company's termination of a performance incentive plan.

Sales dropped 9.1% to $110.6 million from $121.8 million.

Smith Corona's "U.S. market position continues strong, and we will remain as price competitive as necessary to retain our leadership position, even at the expense of short-term earnings," Mr. Thompson said.

He said that Smith-Corona's international business, especially in Europe, "is very promising." He added that the company has been able to pare costs and inventories.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Smith Corona fell 50 cents to close at $9.125.
