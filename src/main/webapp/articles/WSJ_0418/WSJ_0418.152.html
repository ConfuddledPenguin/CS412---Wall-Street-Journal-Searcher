Business Brief -- BCE Inc.: Control of 13.5% of SHL Seized From Kinburn Unit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/18/90

BCE Inc., a Montreal-based telecommunications, manufacturing, financial services and energy concern, said it seized control of about 13.5% of the common shares of SHL Systemhouse Inc., an Ottawa comuter systems-integration concern, from Kinburn Technology Corp. Kinburn still owns the 5.1 million SHL shares, a BCE official said, but BCE now holds the right to vote them. BCE owns an additional 12.4% of SHL common. BCE took control of the shares after Kinburn's parent, Kinburn Corp., an Ottawa-based holding company, failed to pay back loans of about 400 million Canadian dollars (US$342.4 million) made by BCE to Kinburn. The shares were pledged as collateral against the loans, which fell due April 9. BCE said it took control to position itself for maximum value from the loans.

BCE said shares held by Kinburn in a number of companies, including an additional 30% of SHL, also are pledged against the loans. BCE said its rights over this additional collateral are junior to those of a banking syndicate led by Royal Bank of Canada, which has about C$350 million in past-due loans to Kinburn.
