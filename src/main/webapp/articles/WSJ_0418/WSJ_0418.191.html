Young, Sorg Each Agree to Sell Parts Of Their Operations

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/18/90

NEW YORK -- Charles P. Young Co. and Sorg Inc., both financial printers that are operating under bankruptcy-court protection, separately agreed to sell parts of their operations. Young will sell certain operations in New York, Atlanta, Boston and Washington, D.C., to Devon Group Inc., a graphic arts company based in Stamford, Conn., for $4.6 million.

Young will continue to operate its Fidelity Printing Co. unit in Houston, and will liquidate its printing equipment, an attorney for the company said. Young was created in January 1989, when the printing company was bought by CPY Acquisition Co. from Service Resources Corp.

Devon Group plans to combine its Aztech Financial Printers subsidiary with the Young operations, and to call the new subsidiary Aztech-Chas. P. Young Group. An official of Devon, which had 1989 revenue of more than $170 million, said adding the Young operations would make Aztech the fifth-largest financial printer in the U.S. in terms of revenue, from the 10th largest. Aztech's chairman and chief executive officer, Andrew I. Cramer, will assume those duties at the merged operation; Michael F. Weinberg, president of Chas. P. Young New York, will be president.

Separately, Sorg, another financial printer operating with protection from creditors under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code, accepted in principle an offer from New York-based Bowne & Co., one of the largest financial printers, to purchase the Sorg name and other assets for $6 million, plus amounts to be determined depending on the assets sold.

Sorg, which listed assets of $31 million and liabilities of $25 million when it filed under Chapter 11 last August, said the agreement doesn't include its real estate, Chicago office and various other assets.
