Business Brief -- AM International Inc.: Firm Sees Loss for Period; Reorganization Charge Cited

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/18/90

AM International Inc. said a "significant charge" in its fiscal third quarter ending April 30 -- related to a reorganization of the company -- will result in a loss for the quarter. The company wouldn't specify the amount of the charge or projected loss. In the year-earlier third quarter, AM earned $8.1 million, or 12 cents a share, on revenue of $223 million.

The Chicago-based graphics equipment concern said the charge is the result of its strategic plan to focus on its core business -- which prints business forms -- by disposing of non-core business units.

AM said it has agreed to sell its Digi Electronic Products unit to Barco N.V., a Belgian-based company, for $8 million. Last month, AM agreed to sell two other non-core business units, AM Technologies and its machine tool business of AM Wohlenberg.

The company said the revamping also includes the previously announced consolidation of its Multigraphics and International divisions; the segmenting of the company's AM Graphics business into separate units; and further consolidations of its European operations. AM will also write down the assets of its computer graphics business.

The graphics equipment concern also said that while it expects to post a profit in the fourth quarter, the "magnitude of the restructuring charge will eliminate that profit," resulting in a loss for the year. For the year ended July 31, 1989, AM earned $31 million, or 46 cents a share, on revenue of $850.6 million.
