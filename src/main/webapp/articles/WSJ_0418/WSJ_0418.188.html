United Telecom to Acquire GTE Stake Of 19.9% in US Sprint for $500 Million

  • Author: Johnnie L. Roberts
  • Date: 04/18/90

United Telecommunications Inc. said it plans to acquire GTE Corp.'s remaining 19.9% stake in US Sprint Communications Co. for $500 million. William T. Esrey, United Telecom's chairman, president and chief executive officer, told shareholders that the Kansas City, Mo., company will pay book value for the interest, in a transaction expected to be completed by early July.

The purchase was expected, under terms of a 1988 agreement in which United Telecom and GTE ended their 50-50 partnership in the long-distance telephone service venture, and United Telecom boosted its holding to 79.1%. US Sprint is the third-largest long-distance telephone service, with $1.2 billion of revenue in the first quarter, up 24%.

Separately, United and GTE, based in Stamford, Conn., posted first-quarter profit gains of 41% and 8%, respectively. United's revenue grew by 24% and GTE's 9%. Both gains were in line with the expectations of financial analysts.

Yesterday, in composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange, United Telecom closed at $39.875 a share, down 25 cents a share. GTE closed at $66, off 37.5 cents a share.

At United's annual meeting, as expected, shareholders approved a measure to rename the company Sprint Corp. after purchase of the 19.9% stake from GTE. And Mr. Esrey, 50 years old, assumed the title of chairman, succeeding Paul H. Henson, 64, who retired but who will continue as a director.

In an interview, Mr. Esrey said United plans to finance the transaction with loans and that the acquisition won't dilute earnings. "We always said we would exercise the {purchase} option when financial results of Sprint justified the cost of the buyout," he said.

Analysts concurred in his optimism about the long-term outlook. Sprint's "momentum is very strong," said Jack Grubman of Paine Webber.

For the first quarter, United had net income of $106.9 million, or 51 cents a share, up from $75.6 million, or 36 cents a share, the year earlier. Revenue jumped to $2.02 billion from $1.77 billion.

At Sprint, operating profit climbed sharply -- to $85 million from $31 million as revenue jumped 24% to $1.2 billion. Even so, the revenue growth didn't meet internal expectations largely because, analysts said, one or two contracts weren't booked in the period by Sprint's Telenet communications network operation.

United's local telephone company operations posted operating earnings of $169 million, up $12 million, or 7.6%, from a year ago. Revenue rose more than 3% to $662 million from $641 million the year before.

At GTE, net totaled $355.2 million, or $1.05 a share, up from $329.2 million, or 97 cents a share, the year before. Revenue increased to $4.5 billion from $4.12 billion.

Despite a $101 million rate reduction, GTE's local telephone operations posted a 4% rise in operating profit to $720 million on a 5% revenue increase to $3.2 billion. Among other things, the results reflect higher foreign revenue, a 4% increase in lines in service and an 11% jump in minute-of-use of local long-distance calling.

Telecommunications products and services showed a sharp increase to $37 million from the year-ago $10 million, which included a provision for a one-time loss on a government contract. Revenue swelled 37% to $798 million from $580 million.

Operating profit in electrical-products operations fell 8.2% to $67 million, reflecting stiff competition, lower foreign results and the impact of the softening automobile industry. Sales increased 3% to $567 million from $548 million.

The mobile communication business, which provides cellular service, attracted 56,000 new customers for a total of 319,000, the company said.
