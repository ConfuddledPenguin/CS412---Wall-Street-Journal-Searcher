Marketing & Media: New Magazine Will Offer Tips From Soviet Managers

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/18/90

FRAMINGHAM, Mass. -- Conventional wisdom may deem Soviet management technique an oxymoron. Nevertheless, a U.S. concern plans a Soviet magazine celebrating Soviet managers' methods. International Data Group, a closely held company that publishes Computerworld and 135 other magazines and newspapers around the world, plans to publish 50,000 copies of the new magazine, Manager, starting next month.

The magazine will be produced by Information Computer Enterprise, a joint venture between International Data and its Soviet partners that started publishing PC World USSR in 1988. It won't be distributed outside the Soviet Union. There, circulation will be controlled but those selected will still have to pay for it.

Patrick J. McGovern, chairman of International Data, said "this is the first business magazine published in the U.S.S.R. to offer case studies and practical advice on operating a profitable venture in the new competitive environment." Articles will be written by Soviet managers.
