Business Brief -- Boeing Co.: Carrier Reaches Tentative Settlement in Age-Bias Suit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/18/90

Boeing Co. said it reached a tentative settlement of an age-discrimination lawsuit brought on behalf of 29 pilots who were removed from flight-test status when they turned 60. Under the tentative agreement with the Equal Opportunity Employment Commission on a five-year consent decree, Boeing agreed to raise the maximum flying age for its pilots to 63 from 60. The Seattle company also will establish a $4.4 million fund to be paid to the 29 pilots, representing "what the former pilots would have received under the current Boeing Pilot Early Leave Program."

The commercial-airplane maker and defense contractor added that in connection with raising its maximum flying age to 63, it will establish an "enhanced medical protocol to determine the pilot's fitness to fly after age 60." Boeing's "age 60" policy, similar to the Federal Aviation Administration requirement for commercial airline pilots, was instituted in 1980.
