Steel Output in 35 Nations

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/18/90

BRUSSELS -- Output of crude steel by companies in 35 nations, including the U.S., Japan and West European countries, fell 1.5% in March to 41.8 million metric tons from 42.4 million tons a year earlier, the International Iron and Steel Institute said. A metric ton is equal to about 2,204.62 pounds. The U.S. led the decline in production, with output falling 4% to 7.8 million metric tons. This decline and a 5.9% drop in steel production in 21 other countries offset a rise in European Community nations and Japan; EC output rose 2.4% to 12.6 million metric tons, and Japanese production increased 2% to 9.2 million tons.

For the first quarter, steel output of the 35 nations eased 2.5% to 119.7 million metric tons from 122.9 million tons a year earlier. The countries included in the steel institute's monthly survey accounted for 61.6% of world steel production in 1989 and for 97.2% of world output, excluding the Soviet Union and other East European countries, China and North Korea.
