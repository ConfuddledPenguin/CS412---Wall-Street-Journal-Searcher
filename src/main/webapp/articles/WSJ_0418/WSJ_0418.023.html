South Korean Investment

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/18/90

SEOUL -- South Korean corporate investment overseas surged in the first quarter to $383 million on an approval basis, the Bank of Korea said. The central bank said the tally involved 125 equity projects.

Officials said South Korean companies have been increasingly moving operations to low-wage countries in a bid to boost export competitiveness weakened by increased wage and other domestic production costs, while seeking better market access to industrialized countries.

By region, North America, mostly the U.S., attracted $164 million for 30 projects, almost 10 times the year-ago level.
