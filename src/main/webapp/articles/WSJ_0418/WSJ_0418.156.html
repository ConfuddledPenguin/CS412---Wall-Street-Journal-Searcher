Business Brief -- Honeywell Inc.: Board Asks Concern to Plan Spinoff of Defense Business

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/18/90

Honeywell Inc.'s board asked the company to develop plans to spin off its billion-dollar Defense and Marine Systems business, saying the move seems to "offer the best value for shareholders." Separately, Honeywell reported a 43% increase in first-quarter earnings to $90.1 million, or $2.29 a share, up from $62.9 million, or $1.46 a share, a year earlier. Sales gained 6.6% to $1.53 billion from $1.43 billion. The Minneapolis-based controls maker cited continuing strong market conditions in its industrial systems and commercial flight systems business for the strong results.

A spinoff of the Defense and Marine line could be approved by the third quarter, the company said. In July, Honeywell said it was reducing its dependence on weapons sales and that it planned to shed its Defense and Marine Systems line.
