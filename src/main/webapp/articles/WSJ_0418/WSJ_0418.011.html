Letters to the Editor: Single-Vendor Policy Works Best in Yosemite

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/18/90

Your March 27 page-one story on Yosemite Park & Curry Co. and its parent, MCA, harped on an unfair and false theme that Curry somehow acts improperly by making a profit in Yosemite. It does not. Curry is the only major company in Yosemite because Congress created and has endorsed repeatedly the concession system in the national parks, which provides for a major concessionaire in each national park. Congress adopted this plan because it found that both the public and the protection of the park are better served by one carefully regulated concessionaire than by a hodgepodge of competing vendors. Each price charged, all products and services, the hours of operation and the level of service are all specifically authorized and regulated by the National Park Service. Even minor changes in construction or operations must be approved.

The financial arrangements between the government and Curry are governed by the system created by Congress. This law also provides that service to the visitor and protection of the resource are more important than the return of fees to the government. The company has agreed to increase its building-use fees and currently is negotiating an increased franchise fee.

In short, Curry is in the park as a contractor to the Park Service to provide those services the Park Service determines are in the public interest, in an environmentally sound manner, and at totally regulated prices.

Thus, the underlying theme of your story that there is something untoward about making a profit under that detailed system set up by Congress and administered by the Park Service is extremely unfair.

Curry did not, as you state, urge its visitors to vote for expansion of facilities; it suggested they participate in the planning process as others were doing. The company's position has always been that the overnight-visitor level should remain unchanged. We did not ask for more hotel rooms.

The movie rental and barber shop you describe in pejorative terms are authorized by the NPS and are primarily for the benefit of employees, who otherwise might have to drive 100-mile round trips on twisting, mountain roads for these services.

Yosemite Park & Curry Co. shares Superintendent Mike Finley's concern for increased funding to protect the park and reduce the crowding on the few days it does occur. We have carried out a wide range of environmental and interpretive programs not required by our contract to assist and supplement the park's services in these efforts. We look forward to continuing to serve the public and protect the park as we have in the past.

Edward C. Hardy

President

Yosemite Park & Curry Co.

Yosemite National Park, Calif.
