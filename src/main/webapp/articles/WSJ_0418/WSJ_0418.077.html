Wasserstein Perella Forms Fund With Banque Paribas

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/18/90

NEW YORK -- Banque Paribas and Wasserstein Perella Group Inc. formed a $366 million fund to provide "mezzanine financing" for friendly merchant-banking transactions and European corporate restructurings. The fund, called European Mezzanine Fund, will be jointly managed by Paribas and Wasserstein Perella; it is in the form of European Currency Units, or ECUs, totaling 300 million. Other participants are Commerzbank of West Germany and Amsterdam-Rotterdam Bank.

Paribas and Wasserstein Perella said they anticipate a second closing soon to accommodate additional investors. Stephen A. Oxman, deputy chairman of Wasserstein Perella, an investment bank based in New York, said it was possible the additional investment could add between 50 million ECUs and 100 million ECUs to the fund.

Banque Paribas is part of Paris-based Paribas Group, an investment bank and financial services company with assets of more than $140 billion.

Mezzanine financing tends to occupy the same place in a company's capital structure as junk bonds. It generally consists of subordinated debt and associated equity securities, such as warrants. Mr. Oxman said there were several other such funds in Europe, all smaller than the European Mezzanine Fund.

He said the fund wouldn't focus on a particular industry or country.
