International: British Concern Plans Asset Sales Due to Unit's Woes

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 04/18/90

LONDON -- British & Commonwealth PLC, a financial services concern, said it will begin a #750 million ($1.22 billion) asset sale following the discovery of severe troubles at Atlantic Computers PLC, its computer leasing subsidiary. Peter Thompson, non-executive chairman of B&C, said the sales would leave a core business that is "cash-producing as opposed to cash-demanding," with money brokering its central theme.

Mr. Thompson made his comments at a news conference following an announcement that B&C would write off its #550 million investment in Atlantic and put the subsidiary in the hands of an administrator.

The executive said B&C will seek buyers for many of its main subsidiaries, including its merchant banking group. But he said a decision on which other divisions are to be sold hasn't been made.

Mr. Thompson said proceeds from the sales will be used to reduce the company's debt to about #250 million from #1 billion.

B&C currently consists of five main divisions, apart from its computer leasing business: money brokering, merchant banking, fund management, professional services, and property and investments.

Mr. Thompson said Atlantic's problems only became clear "in the last few days" as a result of three parallel studies of B&C's businesses. One of the reviews indicated that Atlantic's assets and profits were "materially overstated," B&C said.

John Gunn, B&C group chief executive, said prior to the studies Atlantic had been expected to show a 1989 pretax profit of #20 million to #40 million. It now expects Atlantic to show a "substantial loss."

Mr. Thompson said Atlantic was operating in an environment where its margins were being cut by increasingly severe competition and technological breakthroughs which rendered its leased equipment obsolete more rapidly than the company had anticipated.

B&C appointed Price Waterhouse, the accounting firm, administrator to Atlantic. B&C officials said the decision to appoint an administrator provides the company extra flexibility in handling Atlantic's removal from B&C.
