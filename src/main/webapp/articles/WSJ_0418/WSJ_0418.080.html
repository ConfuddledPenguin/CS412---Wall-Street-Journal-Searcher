International: Eurotunnel May Require An Added $815 Million

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 04/18/90

LONDON -- Eurotunnel PLC, the Anglo-French cross-channel tunnel consortium, told its main creditor banks that it may need about #500 million ($815 million) on top of the #7.2 billion it had previously targeted as its total project costs, banking sources said. Eurotunnel asked the bankers last week if they would commit to added funding, the sources said. They said the consortium said the funds would serve as a financial cushion against rising interest rates and other contingencies, including cost overruns still technically in dispute with contractors.

A number of project estimates were still under discussion and negotiations over interim and permanent financing for the project weren't yet complete, the sources said. They said that a firm commitment on permanent financing may take months to hammer out, although an agreement over providing Eurotunnel with further short-term operating funds was increasingly likely.

Eurotunnel said in January when it reached an agreement with its banks over a short-term credit of #400 million that it was preparing fresh project estimates. It said those estimates would be disclosed when it released its 1989 final results on April 23.
