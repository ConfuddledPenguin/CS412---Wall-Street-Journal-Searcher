New Securities Issues

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/18/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATES

San Diego Gas & Electric Co. -- $100 million of first mortgage bonds, due April 15, 2020, was won in competitive bidding by a Merrill Lynch Capital Markets group and was priced with a coupon of 9 5/8% at 98.45 to yield 9.785%. The bonds, which are non-callable for 10 years, were priced at a spread of 1.05 percentage points above the Treasury's 30-year bellwether bond. The winning bid resulted in a net interest cost to the company of 9.833%. The issue is rated double-A-3 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and single-A-plus by Standard & Poor's Corp.

MUNICIPALS

Santa Clara County Traffic Authority, Calif. -- $274.9 million of sales tax revenue bonds, (limited tax bonds), 1990 Series, due April 1, 1992-1995, were tentatively priced by a Morgan Stanley & Co. group to yield from 6.20% in 1992 to 6.50% in 1995. There are no term bonds. The bonds are insured and rated triple-A by Moody's and S&P.

Louisiana -- $263 million of gasoline and fuels tax revenue bonds, 1990 Series A, due Nov. 15, 1993-2000, and 2004, with capital appreciation bonds due Nov. 15, 2001 and 2002, were priced by a Goldman, Sachs & Co. group. The bonds are priced to yield from 6.65% in 1993 to 7.45% in 2004. Current interest serial bonds are priced to yield from 6.65% in 1993 to 7.30% in 2000. There are $63.5 million of 7 1/4% term bonds priced at 98.24 to yield 7.45% in 2004. Capital appreciation bonds are priced to yield to maturity to 7.25% in 2001 and 2002. The bonds are rated single-A by Moody's and single-A-minus by S&P, excluding bonds due in 1996-1998, 2001 and 2002, which are insured and rated triple-A by both firms.

Tennessee Housing Development Agency -- $75 million of home-ownership program revenue bonds, Issue P due July , 1998-1999 and 2017, with capital appreciation bonds due Jan. 1 and July 1, 2000-2010, and Series Q, due July 1, 2022, were tentatively priced by a Smith Barney, Harris Upham & Co. group to yield from 6.90% for Series P bonds due in 1998 to 7.88% for Series Q bonds due in 2022. Current interest serial bonds are priced at par to yield from 6.90% in 1998 to 7.00% in 1999. There are $23 million of term bonds priced at par to yield 7 5/8% in 2017. Capital appreciation bonds are priced to yield to maturity from 7.10% in 2000 to 7.50% in 2008-2010. Series Q consists of $35 million of term bonds priced at par to yield 7 7/8% in 2022. Interest on Series Q bonds will be treated as a preference item in calculating the federal alternative minimum tax that may be imposed on certain investors. A single-A rating is expected from Moody's, and a single-A-plus rating is expected from S&P, according to an official with the lead underwriter.

MORTGAGES

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $300 million of real estate mortgage investment conduit securities is being offered in 15 classes by PaineWebber Inc. The issue, Fannie Mae's Series 1990-53, is backed by Fannie Mae 9% securities with a weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29.4 years. Details on yields weren't immediately available.

ASSET-BACKED

Chrysler Financial Corp. -- $609.3 million of securities backed by automobile loans with a coupon rate of 9 1/4% at 99 35/64 to yield 9.501% through First Boston Corp. The issue is being made through Asset-Backed Securities Corp. 7, a company formed by First Boston for issuing asset securities. The issue was priced at a yield spread of 0.80 percentage point above the Treasury two-year note. The securities have an estimated average life of 1.5 years. The scheduled maturity date is April 15, 1995. The securities are expected to carry triple-A ratings from Moody's and S&P based on the quality of the underlying loans and a letter of credit covering 9% of the issue from Credit Suisse. Principal and interest payments will be made monthly.

EUROBONDS

Deutsche Girozentrale (West Germany) -- 125 million Swiss francs ($83.9 million) of 7 1/2% public bonds due May 21, 1995, priced at 101 1/2 via Swiss Bank Corp.

Toyota Motor Credit Corp. (U.S.) -- 100 million Swiss francs of 8% privately placed notes due May 8, 1993, priced at 101.13 via Swiss Bank.

Ascii Corp. (Japan) -- 50 million marks ($29.8 million) of 9 1/8% private placement bonds due April 20, 1994, priced at 101 1/2 to yield 8.67%, via Industriebank von Japan (Deutschland) AG. Guarenteed by Industrial Bank of Japan.
