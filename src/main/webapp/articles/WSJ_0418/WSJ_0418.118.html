Business Brief -- EAC Industries Inc.: Hardware Maker Will Post Loss of About $12.5 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/18/90

EAC Industries Inc. said it will report a loss of about $12.5 million for the fiscal year ended Jan. 31 due to "significant problems" at the company and its Ideal Security Hardware subsidiary. A year earlier, the Chicago-based maker of hardware and printing supplies had a loss of $1.7 million on sales of $31.5 million.

The company said the loss resulted mainly from the write-off of intangible assets, such as $5.5 million in relocation costs for the recent move of Ideal Security to Johnson City, Tenn., from St. Paul, Minn., as well as the write-off of $1.2 million of good will.

EAC also cited fourth-quarter accounting and audit adjustments at Ideal Security of $2.3 million.

It further attributed the loss to disruptions at Ideal Security resulting from the recent move, legal and consulting fees related to attempts to sell EAC or its subsidiaries, and provisions for losses from discontinued operations.

In spite of the loss, EAC said its other subsidiary, Goodren Products Corp., continues to generate profits in excess of expectations.
