UPS Could be Fined As Much as $625,000 On OSHA Charges

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/01/90

WASHINGTON -- United Parcel Service faces possible federal fines of $625,000 on charges of worker-safety violations after a call from Nassau County, N.Y., police warned OSHA that a UPS driver was injured by a hazardous chemical carried by his delivery truck. The Labor Department's Occupational Safety and Health Administration charged UPS with 312 willful record-keeping violations at its Uniondale, N.Y., facility, including 310 instances of improper recording of employees on restricted work activity. OSHA said it has issued numerous citations for record-keeping violations by UPS over the past 16 years.

United Parcel Service of America Inc., the closely held package-delivery company based in Greenwich, Conn., said it was studying the complaint and hadn't decided on a response. Most of the citation, however, dealt with "alleged violations of written procedures and guidelines," which are "complex and subject to conflicting interpretations," UPS said.

The company also was cited for failure to develop an emergency response plan, or to train all workers in how to respond to an emergency involving hazardous materials, and for not supplying a proper respirator for employees handling hazardous-materials spills.

OSHA said its inspection of the Uniondale facility resulted from a call from the Nassau County police hazardous-materials team, after methylene chloride, a suspected carcinogen, spilled from a box bound for delivery in the truck. The driver touched the chemical, then put his hand to his face, sustaining hand and facial injuries, OSHA said.

UPS has 15 working days to contest the citations before the independent Occupational Safety and Health Review Commission.
