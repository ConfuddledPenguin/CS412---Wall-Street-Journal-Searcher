Who's News: Air Products Names Harold A. Wagner To a New Position

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/01/90

ALLENTOWN, Pa. -- Air Products & Chemicals Inc. appointed Harold A. Wagner executive vice president in charge of world-wide gases and equipment, the company's largest business, with about $1.5 billion in annual sales. Mr. Wagner, 54 years old, president of European operations for the industrial gases and chemicals concern since July 1988, assumes the new position June 1. Analysts say the promotion suggests that Mr. Wagner is being groomed to succeed Dexter F. Baker, chairman and chief executive officer, following the recent abrupt departure of Frank J. Ryan, president and chief operating officer. Mr. Ryan, 58, who had been considered Mr. Baker's likely successor, was forced out in a management clash with Mr. Baker, who assumed Mr. Ryan's positions. Mr. Baker is 62 and must retire at 65, but the company said discussion of a successor is "premature."

Joseph J. Kaminski, vice president for corporate planning, will succeed Mr. Wagner as head of European businesses. Wayne A. Hinman, director of corporate marketing development and planning, succeeds Mr. Kaminski.

The company also announced these management changes: James H. Agger, vice president and general counsel, was appointed to the additional position of corporate secretary, succeeding Richard A. Gray Jr., who is retiring. Donald J. Orr, vice president of business divisions in the chemicals group, was promoted to senior vice president; Robert E. Gadomski, vice president and general manager of process systems, was appointed group vice president; and Stanley R. Roman, vice president and general manager of industrial gases, was named group vice president.
