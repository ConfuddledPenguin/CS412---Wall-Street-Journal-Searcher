Advanced Micro, Unit Of Samsung Settle Dispute Over Patents

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/01/90

SUNNYVALE, Calif. -- Advanced Micro Devices Inc. and Samsung Semiconductor Inc. said they settled two-year-old litigation over Advanced patents covering semiconductor circuits. In its suit, filed in August 1988 in federal court in San Jose, Calif., Advanced said that Samsung infringed on patents covering its AM22V10 programmable array logic chip, one of Advanced Micro's leading products.

As part of the settlement, Samsung Semiconductor, the San Jose, Calif., unit of South Korea-based Samsung, acknowledged that the Advanced patents are legally valid and said that in two years it would stop selling certain products, including chips protected by the patents.

Advanced said it would receive royalties from the resulting sales, which cover existing Samsung commitments to customers, although an Advanced spokesman declined to elaborate on the monetary amount.

The Advanced Micro complaint was a countersuit to an earlier lawsuit filed by Samsung against Advanced Micro, in which Samsung was seeking to have the basic patent covering Advanced's chip invalidated. Advanced Micro sued three other concerns on the same patent-infringement claims.

Those claims remain outstanding against Atmel Corp. and Cypress Semiconductor Inc., both of San Jose; and Gazelle Microcircuits Inc., Santa Clara, Calif.

With the settlement of the litigation, the two companies said they agreed to discuss possible future business relationships in areas of mutual technological interest.

Advanced Micro stock rose 12.5 cents yesterday to close at $8.75 in New York Stock Exchange composite trading.
