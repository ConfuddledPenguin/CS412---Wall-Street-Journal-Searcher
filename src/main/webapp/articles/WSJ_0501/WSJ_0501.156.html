ITC Rules Imports Of Gear Used for Print May Be Unfairly Traded

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/01/90

WASHINGTON -- The U.S. International Trade Commission ruled that imports of photo-typesetting and image-setting machines from West Germany may be injuring domestic manufacturers of similar equipment. The preliminary ruling stems from a complaint filed under the Anti-Dumping Act by Varityper Inc., a subsidiary of AM International Inc., Chicago. The complaint contends that Linotype AG and other West German manufacturers are exporting this equipment to the U.S. at "unfairly" low prices. The ruling means the Commerce Department and the ITC will continue to investigate the complaint, which involves imports from West Germany of about $50 million a year. The proceeding could lead to duties on the imports.

In its trade complaint, Varityper alleged that the West German machines are reaching the U.S. market at prices ranging from 6.3% to 17% below the home-market price for the same products or below the foreign cost of production.
