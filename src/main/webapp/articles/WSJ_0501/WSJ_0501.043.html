Who's News: Consolidated Edison Names McGrath Chief To Succeed Hauspurg

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/01/90

NEW YORK -- Eugene R. McGrath was named chief executive officer of Consolidated Edison Co., effective Sept. 1, succeeding Arthur Hauspurg. Mr. Hauspurg, who will reach the big utility company's mandatory retirement age of 65 in August, said he will recommend to the board at its May 22 meeting that Mr. McGrath also succeed him as chairman.

Mr. McGrath, 48, was named president and chief operating officer of Con Ed last year, signaling to analysts that he was in line to take over when Mr. Hauspurg retired. Previously, Mr. McGrath, a 27-year Con Ed veteran, was executive vice president, operations.

For the first quarter, Con Ed had net income of $128.8 million, or 56 cents a share, down 10% from $143.2 million, or 63 cents a share. For 1989, net totaled $606.1 million, or $2.49 a share, on revenue of $5.55 billion.

Analysts said the executive change comes at a critical time for Con Ed. Mr. McGrath's "biggest challenge is to forecast the future of New York City's economy given the current uncertainties," said Gregory Enholm, an analyst at Salomon Brothers Inc. "Will the 1990s be a repeat of the 1970s, which caused significant financial distress at Con Ed, including a dividend omission? Or will the 1990s be a continuation of the 1980s, when New York City's recovery significantly improved Con Ed's financial health?"

Mr. Enholm said Con Ed may have to step up programs for controlling demand or build new power plants if economic growth continues. However, if the New York economy falters, Con Ed will need to take action to buoy profits. "He needs to make some big decisions that will affect Con Ed for the 1990s," said Mr. Enholm.

In his recent positions at Con Ed, Mr. McGrath has been a strong advocate of incentives for major commercial customers to use less energy in peak periods. Such demand-management programs reduce the need for building expensive new power plants.
