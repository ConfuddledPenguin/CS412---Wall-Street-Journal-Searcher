Dividend News: American Indemnity Financial Corp.

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/01/90

AMERICAN INDEMNITY FINANCIAL Corp. said directors voted to slash its quarterly dividend 86% to two cents a share from 14 cents, to allow the Galveston, Texas, property and casualty insurance concern to reinvest a greater part of its earnings. The dividend is payable May 15 to stock of record May 11. The quarterly payout was cut from 28 cents in July 1987. A spokesman said the latest cut reflects disappointing results for 1989 and the first quarter. American Indemnity posted a first-quarter net loss of $1.3 million, or 65 cents a share, compared with year-earlier profit of $1.1 million, or 57 cents a share. It cited unusually large fire and auto-related claims in January and February, and said its premiums written fell about 19% to about $17 million in the quarter, as part of an effort to tighten underwriting standards and improve its premium-to-surplus ratio.
