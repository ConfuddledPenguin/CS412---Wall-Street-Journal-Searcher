Navy Gives Grumman $570.6 Million Pact For Aircraft Parts

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/01/90

WASHINGTON -- Grumman Corp. received a $570.6 million Navy contract for F-14D and E-2C aircraft parts.

McDonnell Douglas Corp. was given a $565.3 million Navy contract for F/A-18 aircraft and parts and for T-45 aircraft trainers.

General Electric Co. got a $200.9 million Navy contract for F-14D aircraft engines.

Hughes Aircraft Co., a unit of General Motors Corp., got a combined $129 million contract for Air Force air defense systems and Navy radar systems.

Raytheon Co. was awarded an $83.9 million Navy contract for ship targeting systems.

Honeywell Inc. received a $34.1 million Navy contract for mine warfare systems.

Talley Defense Systems Inc. won a $29.1 million Army contract for artillery projectile parts.

Martin Marietta Corp. was issued a $20.6 million contract for operating an Army ammunition plant and for Air Force aircraft electronics equipment.

Sierracin-Sylmar Corp. was given a $17.4 million Air Force contract for aircraft canopies.

Rockwell International Corp. received a $17.3 million Air Force contract for missile parts.
