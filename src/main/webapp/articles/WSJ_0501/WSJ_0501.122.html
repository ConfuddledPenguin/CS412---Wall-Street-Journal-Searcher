Ex-Concert Promoter Sentenced to Prison For Leasing Scheme

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/01/90

LOS ANGELES -- U.S. District Judge Manuel Real sentenced a former concert promoter to 15 years in prison for his role in a leasing scheme that defrauded 14 banks, thrifts and other financial institutions of $23 million. Kimball Dean Richards of Whittier, Calif., will be placed on five years' probation after completing the sentence and will be barred from engaging in any entertainment-related businesses during that time. The issue of restitution hasn't been made final, said Assistant U.S. attorney Richard E. Robinson. A lawyer for Mr. Richards wasn't immediately available to comment.

On March 8, a federal jury convicted Mr. Richards, 34 years old, for his role in a scheme to defraud the institutions by the sale of leases on non-existent audio recording and concert sound and lighting equipment. Last year, two other men involved in the scheme were sentenced to prison and each ordered to make restitution to the victims of $21.9 million.

The defrauded institutions include Union Bank; a unit of Bank of Tokyo; a unit of Great Western Financial Corp.; and units of General Electric Credit Co. and First Interstate Bancorp.
