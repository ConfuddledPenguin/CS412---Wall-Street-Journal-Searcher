Mitsubishi to Recall Vehicles That Exceed Emission Standards

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/01/90

WASHINGTON -- Mitsubishi Motors of America, the marketing unit of Mitsubishi Motors Corp., will recall 102,000 of its 1986 passenger cars that exceed the federal emission standard for carbon monoxide, the Environmental Protection Agency said. The cars, equipped with 1.5-liter engines, are the Mitsubishi Mirage, the Dodge Colt and Plymouth Colt. All were built by Mitsubishi, but most were sold by Chrysler Corp. Chrysler will fix the Dodge and Plymouth cars, the EPA said.

The repair, which will be free to car owners, consists of modifying the carburetor, recalibrating the automatic choke and, if needed, replacing the secondary air filter.

Carbon monoxide, which results from incomplete combustion of fuel, disrupts the delivery of oxygen to the body's organs and tissues and impairs heart function. It can be especially harmful to people wih lung disease, anemia and peripheral vascular disease.

Car owners will be notified of the recall by mail.
