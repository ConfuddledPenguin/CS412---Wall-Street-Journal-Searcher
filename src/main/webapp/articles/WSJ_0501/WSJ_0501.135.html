Business Brief -- Tosco Corp.: Effort to Sell the Company Or Refining Business Ended

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/01/90

Tosco Corp. said it abandoned efforts begun late last year to sell the company or its refining business. But the Santa Monica, Calif., oil refiner said discussions would continue on the possible sale of its Seminole Fertilizer Corp. unit, which it purchased last June for about $135 million including expenses.

Tosco's chief executive officer, Thomas D. O'Malley, said in a statement that offers received for the company didn't "reflect the strategic position and earning power of the Avon refinery. Therefore, we will continue to operate and improve our refining assets and explore alternative methods to maximize values for our shareholders."

Tosco also said it will resume its stock buy-back program, which had been suspended after Tosco had acquired about $20 million of a possible $50 million of its own shares.

Tosco also reported a first-quarter loss of $6.9 million, or 66 cents a share, compared with a loss of $8 million, or $1.48 a share, a year earlier. Revenue rose to $477.2 million from $274.7 million. The 1990 results include a $9.4 million loss for the Seminole Fertilizer unit.
