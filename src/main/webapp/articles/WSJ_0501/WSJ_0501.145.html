Business Brief -- Allegheny International Inc.: Partnership Secures Funding For Plan of Reorganization

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/01/90

Japonica Partners said it has lined up $425 million of bank financing for its plan to reorganize Allegheny International Inc., which is operating under Chapter 11 of bankruptcy-law procedures. An attorney for Japonica, a New York investor group trying to take over Pittsburgh-based Allegheny, said it has obtained loan commitments of $175 million from Chase Manhattan Bank, $175 million from Security Pacific and $75 million from Midland Montague, an affiliate of Midland Bank PLC., a British bank.

Japonica said previously the partnership would supply more than $120 million of equity capital.

Allegheny, which produces Sunbeam and Oster appliances and other consumer products, filed for bankruptcy court protection in February 1988. Allegheny management has its own plan for reorganizing the company, which involves paying creditors mainly with stock.

With the bank commitments, cash that Allegheny has on hand and the Japonica equity, Japonica said, it has the funds needed to gain control of Allegheny and provide it with working capital.
