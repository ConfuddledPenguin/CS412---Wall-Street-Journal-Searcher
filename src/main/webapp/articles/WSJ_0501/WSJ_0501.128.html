International Brief -- Imperial Chemical Industries PLC: First-Quarter Pretax Profit Fell 6%, Bettering Estimates

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/01/90

Imperial Chemical Industries PLC, a British chemical and pharmaceutical concern, said first-quarter pretax profit fell 6% to #414 million ($677.1 million) from $442 million a year earlier. But the results still were far better than market expectations, which ranged from #340 million to #380 million.

Profit after taxes and minority interests fell 1.1% to #265 million from #268 million a year earlier. Per-share earnings eased 2.8% to 38.1 pence from 39.2 pence.

Sales rose 7.5% to #3.45 billion from #3.21 billion.

Chairman Denys Henderson said earnings were "relatively encouraging in a period of considerable economic uncertainty." Pretax profit was hit by declining profit margins in general chemicals, petrochemicals and plastic products, amid sagging demand world-wide, ICI said, but strong sales in pharmaceuticals and agrichemicals helped offset this decline.
