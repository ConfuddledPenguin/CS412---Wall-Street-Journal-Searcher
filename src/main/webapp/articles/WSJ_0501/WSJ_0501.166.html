Chrysler Considers Options After Failing To Sell Defense Units

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/01/90

HIGHLAND PARK, Mich. -- Chrysler Corp. is "considering its options" after unsuccessful attempts to sell two defense-related subsidiaries, the company said in a Securities and Exchange Commission filing. The company said it hasn't received satisfactory offers for the two units, Electrospace Systems Inc. and Airborne Systems Inc. The statements came in an amended registration statement involving its $1 billion bond offering last week.

A Chrysler spokesman said the company wouldn't elaborate on possible options. But financial analysts said Chrysler may decide to wait out current conditions depressing the defense market in hopes of attracting better offers, or to enter a joint venture with another company.

Chrysler's stock price has been sliding lately, but analysts say the main reason is poor car sales rather than fruitless efforts to sell the two defense-related companies. In New York Stock Exchange composite trading, Chrysler closed Monday at $14.50, down 25 cents.

The defense and aerospace market turned sour a few months before December, when Chrysler put up for sale its Chrysler Technologies unit. That unit includes Electrospace, Airborne and Gulfstream Aerospace. In March, Chrysler sold Gulfstream, a manufacturer of commercial aircraft, for $825 million. Selling the other two units would help raise additional cash that Chrysler needs in its core automobile business.

Industry observers say uncertainty about future funding for defense-related contracts has brought more sellers than buyers into a depressed market. Ford Motor Co. also is trying to sell its aerospace operations, but hasn't succeeded so far.

"The climate now is like trying to roll a lead ball up the hill," said Howard Rubel, an aerospace industry analyst at C. J. Lawrence, Morgan Grenfell Inc.
