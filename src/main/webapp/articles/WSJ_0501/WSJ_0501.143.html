Business Brief -- Bolt Beranek & Newman Inc.: Fiscal Third-Quarter Loss Widened to $32.4 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/01/90

Bolt Beranek & Newman Inc. reported a net loss for the third quarter ended March 31 of $32.4 million, or $1.77 a share, widened from a net loss of a $5.2 million, or 29 cents a share, in the year-earlier period. The loss in the latest quarter included $20.4 million, or $1.12 a share, in pretax one-time charges related to laying off 300 workers, and scaling down its communications and advanced computers units. The Cambridge, Mass., concern said its software and technology divisions remain profitable.

The widened loss came on a 13% revenue decline, to $64.9 million from $74.5 million. A spokesman said the decline reflected the end of funding from a limited partnership and the end of a Delta Airlines contract held by a now-defunct subsidiary. He said those factors "didn't affect" the bottom-line losses.

Stephen R. Levy, chairman and chief executive officer, said that Bolt Beranek's "poor operating performance over the past 18 months" reflects slower defense spending and increased competition in the computer industry.
