International Brief -- Veba AG: Firm Earned $208.4 Million On Its Feldmuehle Holding

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/01/90

Veba AG Chairman Klaus Piltz said the West German chemicals and energy group earned 350 million marks ($208.4 million) on the sale of its 51% holding in Feldmuehle Nobel AG to Sweden's Stora Kopparbergs Bergslags AB. Mr. Piltz said the total consists of 300 million marks in profit reflecting the difference between purchase and sales price of the holding, and an additional 50 million marks in dividends.

The Veba chairman said the company is selling the holding it bought 11 months ago mainly because there isn't enough synergy between Feldmuehle's main paper business and Veba's activities. He also cited Feldmuehle 5% limit on individual shareholders' voting rights as a factor that made taking full control of the company too expensive.

Bo Berggren, chief executive of Stora Kopparbergs Bergslags, said Stora will offer 540 Deutsche marks a share to holders of the remaining 15% of Feldmuehle shares it doesn't hold. It paid an average of 567 marks a share for Veba's holdings.
