Business Brief -- Kay Jewelers Inc.: Virginia Chain Reports Loss Of $5.6 Million in Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/01/90

Kay Jewelers Inc., which had warned investors and analysts that first-quarter results would be weak, reported a loss of $5.6 million, or 45 cents a share. The loss exceeded the loss of $3.8 million, or 31 cents a share, the Alexandria, Va.-based jewelry chain posted a year earlier. Sales fell to $76.5 million from $79.4 million.

Company officials said higher interest costs from last year's debt offering and higher borrowing to finance the opening of new stores hurt results.

As part of a cost-containment program, openings of new stores have been cut to limit expenses. Kay is slashing annual operating expenses $14 million.

Sales remained sluggish in January, after a weak fourth quarter. But the company said sales picked up a bit towards the end of the quarter.
