Belo Says Complaint Filed by Dallas Rival Is Dismissed by FCC

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/01/90

DALLAS -- A.H. Belo Corp. said the Federal Communications Commission dismissed a complaint filed by the Dallas Times Herald calling for Belo's Dallas television license to be revoked. In a complaint filed last year, the Dallas Times Herald charged that a recent agreement between Belo, owner of the Dallas Morning News and WFAA-TV in Dallas, and Universal Press Syndicate was intended to reduce media competition in Dallas.

As part of the Belo-Universal Press agreement, Universal Press moved 26 features, including "Doonesbury," "The Far Side" and "Dear Abby," to the Morning News from the Times Herald. The agreement called for the two companies to study developing television programs based on Universal's features and to develop an advertising campaign for newspapers using Universal's characters.

Belo said the FCC found that the Times Herald failed to establish a case against it. The Times Herald, owned by DTH Media Inc., couldn't be reached for comment.

A trial is under way in state court in Houston in which the Times Herald has charged Belo and the Morning News with violating antitrust laws and interfering with its contract. Belo said a decision in that case is expected within a few days.
