New Securities Issues

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/01/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATE

Chrysler Corp. -- The size of its Auburn Hills Trust guaranteed exchangeable certificates, due May 1, 2020, was increased to $1.1 billion from $1 billion because of strong demand. Earlier, the offering had been increased to $1 billion from $800 million. Chrysler said the $1.1 billion offering is the largest 30-year debt deal offered by an investment grade-rated industrial company. Details of the offering, priced at par to yield 12 3/8%, were announced Thursday.

Mountain States Telephone & Telegraph Co. -- $100 million of notes, due May 1, 2000, was priced with a 9 1/2% coupon at 99 to yield 9.658%. The non-callable notes were priced at a spread of 60 basis points above the Treasury's 10-year note. Rated double-A-3 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and double-A-minus by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Morgan Stanley & Co. Delivery is scheduled for May 8 with interest accruing May 1. Mountain States Telephone is a unit of U S West Inc.

Northwest Bell Telephone Co. -- $75 million of notes, due May 1, 2000, was priced at 99 with a 9 1/2% coupon to yield 9.658%. The non-callable notes were priced at a spread of 60 basis points above the Treasury's 10-year note. Rated double-A-3 by Moody's and double-A-minus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Morgan Stanley & Co. Delivery is set for May 8 with interest accruing May 1. Northwest Bell Telephone is a unit of U S West Inc.

MORTGAGES

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $300 million of real estate mortagage investment conduit securities was offered in 10 classes by Shearson Lehman Hutton Inc. The offering, Series 1990-59, was structured with coupons ranging from 7% to 10%. Pricing information was not immediately available. The collateral consists of Fannie Mae's 10% securities with an underlying weighted average coupon rate of 10.7% and weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29.9 years. The issue settles June 29.

AGENCY

Federal Farm Credit Banks -- $250 million of notes, due May 17, 1994, was priced at par for an initial yield of 9.39%. The notes, non-callable for two years, were priced at a spread of 35 basis points above the Treasury's four-year note. The notes will be reoffered at various prices through Merrill Lynch Capital Markets. Delivery is set for May 17 (flat).

EUROBONDS

Deutsche Bank Australia (West German parent) -- $50 million (Australian) of fungible 14 1/2% Eurobonds due May 2, 1995, priced at 100 7/8 via Deutsche Bank Capital Markets. Outstanding A$100 million issue launched March 14. Fees 2.

Relocation Funding No. 1 PLC (vehicle) -- #60 million of limited recourse asset-backed secured floating rate notes due April 1994, priced at 100 and paying interest at 0.40 point above the one-month sterling London interbank offered rate, via Commerzbank, London Branch. Amortization after three years. Fees undisclosed.
