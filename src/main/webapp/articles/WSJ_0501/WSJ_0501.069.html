Letters to the Editor: Why General Mills Withdrew New Cereal

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/01/90

Your April 3 story on psyllium grain's ability to reduce cholesterol said: "But psyllium isn't without controversy. Last year, the Food and Drug Administration looked into the safety of its use in large quantities in cereal. In response, General Mills Inc. took a psyllium-based cereal off the market."

General Mills actually removed Benefit from test markets because of disappointing consumer sales.

General Mills submitted a 1,313-page petition to the FDA arguing that psyllium as consumed in Benefit cereal is completely safe and should be declared "generally recognized as safe" by the agency.

R.C. Shulstad

Director of Public Relations

General Mills Inc.

Minneapolis
