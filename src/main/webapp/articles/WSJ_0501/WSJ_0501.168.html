Letters to the Editor: Art Mall Story Contrary To Picture You Painted

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/01/90

Your March 28 article concerning the New York City art and antiques center known as Place des Antiquaires ("Art Mall Tries to Be More Than a Still Life") makes the gratuitous statement that Place des Antiquaires is one of New York City's "most glaring retailing mistakes," yet never supports this claim with objective criteria. Some of the shops are bringing in an impressive $2,000 per rentable square foot in sales. You also omitted the fact that 16 of our 45 dealers have expanded beyond their original premises and rented more space.

You apparently talked to a handful of dealers who are not doing as well as others or who have complaints about a variety of noneconomic factors. Obviously, such situations exist, but that is perfectly normal for a group of 45 competing merchants, and no cause for the alarming conclusions drawn.

John C. Lesher

Director of Leasing

Place des Antiquaires

New York

---

As a retailer within the premises of Place des Antiquaires, I was among the first of the pioneers who shared a vision of success that this innovative and elegant atmosphere would afford the "smaller" antique dealer. In our gallery the art-buying public has overwhelmingly given Place des Antiquaires "thumbs-up" with its checkbooks and charge cards. Indeed, we expanded our premises this past January.

Obviously, we find the "traffic" more than adequate, and are considering a further expansion in the future here.

You would have the reader believe that half of Place des Antiquaires is vacant and unrented. This is simply not true. The resident dealers are renting 90% of the available gallery space, and that doesn't leave much room for many new faces, which must first pass muster with management.

Eugene Tosk

President

Orientations Gallery

New York
