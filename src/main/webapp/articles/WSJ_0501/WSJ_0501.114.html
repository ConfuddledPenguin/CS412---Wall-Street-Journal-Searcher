Aetna Files Lawsuit Against Massachusetts Over Car Insurance

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/01/90

BOSTON (AP) -- Aetna Casualty & Surety Co., a unit of Aetna Life & Casualty Co. of Hartford, Conn., filed suit against the state, claiming that Massachusetts laws controlling withdrawal from the state's car insurance market are unconstitutional. "The insurance commissioner sets the prices so price competition is nonexistent," said John J. Martin, president of Aetna's personal financing security division.

"Worse still, an insurer that decides to leave the private passenger auto insurance market in light of such conditions confronts a massive hurdle to any orderly exit from that market because it must pay what amounts to extortion or lose all its licenses to any other insurance business," Mr. Martin said in a statement.

Dorothy Krenzer, a spokeswoman for the state Insurance Division, said the agency wouldn't have any comment until Commissioner Timothy Gailey has reviewed documents on the suit.

John Hawkins, an Aetna spokesman, said the firm seeks to exit from the Massachusetts car insurance market.

Mr. Hawkins said: "Over a period of years, we have either lost money or made so little that it's not an acceptable business environment for us."

Under state law, insurers must pay an assessment for eight years in order to withdraw from the Massachusetts car insurance market without losing their license to sell other kinds of insurance.

The insurance commissioner also can revoke all licenses granted an insurer if it pulls out of the car insurance market because of the state's insurance laws.
