EUA Extends Offers For Unitil, Fitchburg Until Early September

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/01/90

BOSTON -- Eastern Utilities Associates said it extended its tender offers for Fitchburg Gas & Electric Light Co. and Unitil Corp. from yesterday until midnight Sept. 7, 1990, in order to give the Securities and Exchange Commission additional time to review the proposed acquisitions. EUA has offered $36 a share for the common stock of Fitchburg and $40 a share for the common stock of Unitil.

Fitchburg and Unitil, which have agreed to merge with each other, have repeatedly stated their opposition to the EUA offers.

In announcing the extension of the two offers, EUA's chief executive officer, Donald G. Pardus, stated: "EUA remains totally committed to its goal of acquiring Fitchburg and Unitil because it is in the best interests of their respective stockholders, customers and employees.

"We reiterate our willingness to sit down with their managements to negotiate a transaction that assures their stockholders will receive premium value for their common stock while avoiding the further unnecessary expense of a contested transaction." He said Eastern would also consider a stock transaction if shareholders preferred that.
