Business Brief -- First Bank System Inc.: Lawsuit Over Management Of Bond Holdings Settled

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/01/90

First Bank System Inc., Minneapolis, said it reached an out-of-court settlement of a lawsuit filed in 1988 against the company in connection with the investment strategies of its bond portfolio, a major portion of which was sold in 1988. The bank-holding company said the agreement won't result in a loss to First Bank or a cash benefit to shareholders other than the six plaintiffs, who will receive "nominal payments."

First Bank said its insurance carrier will pay $2.5 million to the company, which will be used primarily to pay costs associated with the lawsuit.

As part of the agreement, First Bank said it agreed to add an outside director who has senior investment-services experience; revise and formalize guidelines and policies relating to its remaining bond portfolio; and retain an independent expert to advise it on the management of the bond portfolio and balance sheet.
