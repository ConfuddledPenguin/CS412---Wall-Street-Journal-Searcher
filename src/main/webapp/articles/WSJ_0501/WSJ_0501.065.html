Marketing & Media: Pathe Is Given Extra Week to Make Payment on MGM

  • Author: Richard Turner
  • Date: 05/01/90

LOS ANGELES -- Pathe Communications Inc. said it had been granted an extra week to make the next $50 million down payment toward its $1.27-billion acquisition of MGM/UA Communications Co. In a Securities and Exchange Commission filing, Pathe said it agreed to make the payment by May 17, rather than May 10. It is the third of four $50 million payments to be deposited into an escrow account that MGM/UA could sue to draw from.

Pathe also said it had extended its tender offer for MGM/UA until May 10. The $20-a-share offer had been set to expire yesterday. A Pathe spokesman said the extension was necessary so that SEC filings could be made.

Yesterday's filing also said that Luxembourg-based Comfinance S.A., the closely held main vehicle for Pathe principal Giancarlo Parretti, had agreed to provide $450 million for Pathe to buy MGM/UA shares, and would receive about a one-third stake of MGM/UA in return. The filing still didn't say where Comfinance would get the money, referring only to "other affiliates" and "European interests."

As of last Friday, 1.8 million shares of about 53 million MGM/UA common shares outstanding had been tendered. But Kirk Kerkorian's Tracinda Corp., which along with a Kerkorian charity own about 82% of MGM/UA common stock, has yet to tender its shares. The tender offer may again be extended until June 23.

Last month, Time Warner Inc. acknowledged that it was providing a $650-million bridge loan toward the acquisition, plus another $200 million in film production financing it said it would help Pathe raise. In return, Time Warner gets a lien on the UA library and warrants for 20% of the new company.

In recent SEC filings, Pathe has indicated it will raise some of the $450 million by selling certain MGM/UA assets, like its one-third interest in United International Pictures, an international film distribution partnership with MCA Inc. and Paramount Communications Inc. Another possibility mentioned by Pathe is to sell the foreign home video rights it holds to the old MGM library, now owned by Ted Turner's Turner Broadcasting System Inc.

But reports in the trade paper Hollywood Reporter that MGM/UA's TV division is being pursued by Sony Corp.'s Columbia Pictures unit were denied. "There's no truth to this," said MGM/UA TV chief David Gerber.

An individual close to the situation said Columbia had been in discussions about acquiring the division several months ago, before the Pathe bid, but had backed off, and that Pathe had indicated no willingness to spin off the division. Mr. Parretti previously had indicated an interest in television when he was part of a 1988 bid for MGM/UA's TV unit by Pathe Entertainment President Alan Ladd Jr. and Mr. Gerber.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading, MGM/UA closed at $18.625, up $1.125.
