Thermo Instrument Begins Tender Offer For Finnigan Corp.

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/01/90

WALTHAM, Mass. -- Thermo Instrument Systems Inc. said it began its $20-a-share tender offer for all shares outstanding of Finnigan Corp. Thermo expects the acquisition to cost between $110 million and $120 million. Finnigan, based in San Jose, Calif., makes mass spectrometers. Thermo makes pollution-monitoring instruments and performs laboratory services.

The offer expires May 25, unless extended. It is conditional on Thermo's obtaining a majority of Finnigan's shares outstanding.

Thermo said to help fund the transaction it sold a $12 million principal amount 7% convertible subordinated note due 2000 to Thermo Electron Corp., which owns 80% of Thermo Instrument. The note is convertible into Thermo Instrument common at $15.625 a share.

In American Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Thermo Instrument rose 12.5 cents, to $14.125. In over-the-counter trading, Finnigan rose 62.5 cents, to $19.625. Thermo Electron fell 12.5 cents to $32.125 in composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange.
