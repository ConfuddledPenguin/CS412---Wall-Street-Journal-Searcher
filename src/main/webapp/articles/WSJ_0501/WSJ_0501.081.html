NCNB Unit Will Auction $260 Million of Small Loans

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/01/90

DALLAS -- The Texas banking crisis has been an auction aficianado's dream as thrifts, banks and the federal government have used the auctioneer's gavel to close sales of land, houses, cars and antiques. Now brave bidders can go for actual loans. NCNB Texas Corp., a unit of NCNB Corp. of Charlotte, N.C., said yesterday that it will auction $260 million of small commercial and consumer loans that the bank has already charged off.

The loans, inherited by NCNB Texas from the banking units of First RepublicBank Corp., will be auctioned May 30 in about 40 portfolios packaged by market, such as Dallas, Houston, San Antonio and Austin.

The auction, a first for NCNB Texas, is patterned after Bank of America's sale last July of $35 million in credit-card debt. Since then, the auctioneers who handled the sale, Ross-Dove Co. of Foster City, Calif., have wielded the gavel in another credit-card debt sale for a private collection agency and in two auctions of mixed loans for the Federal Deposit Insurance Co.

Unlike most loan auctions, which are conducted by sealed bid with minimum bid amounts set by the sellers, these are "open-cry" auctions with no minimums, said Ken Fisher, regional vice president of Ross-Dove.

Mr. Fisher said the sellers have collected as little as one-tenth of 1% on the principal amount of the debt, or loans to as much as 80% of the principal.
