Cargill Ordered to Renew Chicken Delivery Contract

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/01/90

JACKSONVILLE, Fla. -- Cargill Inc. has been ordered by a U.S. District Court judge to renew its contract with a northern Florida chicken grower. Under such contracts, Cargill delivers baby chicks to growers, who raise them and are paid per pound by Cargill for the weight the chickens gain.

Cargill quit delivering baby chicks to Arthur Gaskins, president of the Northeast Florida Broiler Growers' Association, in August after that group filed a lawsuit against the big, Minneapolis-based agribusiness concern. That continuing lawsuit alleges, among other things, that Cargill underpaid the chicken growers.

The case was discussed in a page one story in The Wall Street Journal in January detailing the plight of chicken growers around the country.

Mr. Gaskins had petitioned the court for a preliminary injunction requiring Cargill to continue to do business with the chicken farmer.

In his order granting a motion for preliminary injunction, Judge John H. Moore ruled that Mr. Gaskins and the U.S. Justice Department, which has joined the ongoing growers' case, have a substantial likelihood of winning their case on several claims, including the claim that Cargill terminated Gaskins in order to discourage growers from supporting the growers' organization.

A spokesman for Cargill said the company received the order late yesterday and "will be studying the next appropriate action on our part."
