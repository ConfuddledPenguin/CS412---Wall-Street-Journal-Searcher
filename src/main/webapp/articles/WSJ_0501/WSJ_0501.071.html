Farm Prices Last Month Rose 0.7% From March

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/01/90

WASHINGTON -- Farm prices in April rose 0.7% from March, led by increases in cattle and hog prices, the Agriculture Department said. Average prices received by farmers were 2% higher than in April 1989, the department said.

Beef-cattle prices averaged $75 a hundred pounds, up 80 cents from March, while calf prices averaged $102, up $2.90. In the hog markets, barrow and gilt prices averaged $53.20 a hundred pounds in April, up $1.50, while sows averaged $48.70, up $3.

Milk prices averaged $13.10 a hundred pounds, down 60 cents from March, the department said. Egg prices averaged 64.2 cents a dozen in April, down 8.9 cents. Broiler prices fell 3.2 cents to 33.2 cents a pound in April, while turkey prices slipped 0.2 cents to 37 cents a pound.

Fruit prices rose 11% in April, led by oranges and grapefruit, the department said. Vegetable prices fell 13%, with tomato prices taking a dive.

Corn prices rose 15 cents to $2.52 a bushel in April, while sorghum grain rose 22 cents to $3.92 a hundred pounds. Barley slipped one cent to $2.17 a bushel, while oats fell two cents to $1.37 a bushel, the department said.
