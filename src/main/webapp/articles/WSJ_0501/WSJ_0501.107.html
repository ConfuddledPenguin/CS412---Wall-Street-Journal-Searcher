Business Brief -- Forest Oil Corp.: Gas Write-Down to Result In Loss for First Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/01/90

Forest Oil Corp. said it will write down $84 million in oil and gas reserves, reducing first-quarter earnings by $55 million and resulting in a loss for the quarter. Forest Oil said the write-down is primarily attributable to a significant decrease in the spot market price of natural gas between January and March. The Denver-based company said it sells 80% to 90% of its natural gas on the spot market. The company also attributed the write-down to a revision in reserves and production-rate estimates on Gulf of Mexico properties.

In the first quarter last year, Forest Oil had net income of $1.8 million, or 5 cents a share, on revenue of $63.9 million. Those earnings included a $12 million write-down of oil and gas properties.
