Business Brief -- Texas Air Corp.: Continental Airlines Earned $21.3 Million in 1st Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/01/90

Continental Airlines, a unit of Texas Air Corp., reported first-quarter net income of $21.3 million, largely because of a one-time gain from a lawsuit settlement. The carrier had a net loss in the year-ago quarter of $16.5 million. Continental, based in Houston, was pushed into the black by special items totaling $69.7 million, mostly from a hefty settlement with UAL Inc.'s United Airlines on a computer reservations system lawsuit. In the year-ago quarter Continental had $30.6 million in special items, mostly from sale-leaseback of aircraft.

Quarterly revenue was $1.27 billion, compared with last year's $1.24 billion. Operating profit was $8.8 million, compared with $19.8 million in 1989, although the loss before income tax and special items was mostly unchanged. In 1990, Continental's loss before special items was $48.4 million; last year it was $47 million.

A spokesman for Texas Air said the parent would report its earnings shortly.
