CORPORATE MERGERS can be dismantled by federal courts through antitrust suits by states or private citizens, the Supreme Court ruled. The decision, clearing the way for California to challenge the merger of supermarket chains Lucky Stores and American Stores, raises the risks for companies involved in mergers. ---

Personal spending rose 0.4% in March, mainly because consumers paid higher prices to keep up with inflation. Personal income jumped 0.8%, but much of that stemmed from a boost in farmer subsidies.

Inflation worries are rising among economists, in part because the Fed is under pressure to avoid moves that might hurt the economic expansion.

---

Bank and thrift failures in New England could surge in the next few years, surpassing the more than dozen insolvencies of the past two decades, analysts and regulators say.

---

A major program trader, Susquehanna Investment, was fined $170,000 by the CBOE for alleged abusive trading in stocks and stock options. The fine is part of a crackdown on alleged intermarket abuses.

---

Ashton-Tate's chairman and chief executive, Edward Esber, resigned from the troubled PC-software maker. Executives said the firm may be for sale, but an official denied it.

---

Union Texas Petroleum is seeking a buyer, and analysts say the company could fetch up to $2 billion. Union Texas is controlled by Kohlberg Kravis and Allied-Signal.

---

Donald Trump's Taj Mahal casino had a strong first month of operation, but his two other Atlantic City casinos appear to have suffered as a result, unaudited early tallies indicate.

---

IBM expects a "substantially improved" performance this year and sees a "very bright" future, Chairman John Akers told the annual meeting. The more positive tone sent IBM's stock up $1.625, to $109.

---

Torchmark was ordered by Tennessee regulators to halt its proxy battle with American General. But Torchmark plans a legal challenge.

---

Boeing's profit plunged 34% in the first quarter, though operating earnings surged 87%. As expected, the aerospace giant boosted its dividend and split its stock three-for-two.

---

Armstrong World Industries said it won at least three of the four board seats contested by Canada's Belzberg family, which has been mulling a bid for the company. The final tally isn't expected until at least May 11.

---

GM was dealt a blow in the Supreme Court, which let stand a ruling allowing ex-workers to sue GM for refusing to consider rehiring them.

---

Ames Department Stores hired retailing consultant Stephen Pistner to rescue the discount chain from the financial woes of its Zayre stores.

---

PepsiCo's profit rose 10% in the first quarter despite continued expenses from previous acquisitions. Operating earnings jumped 22%.
