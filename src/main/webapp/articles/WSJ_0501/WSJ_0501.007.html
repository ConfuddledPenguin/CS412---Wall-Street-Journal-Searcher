Credit Ratings: Moody's Cuts Ratings On Interco Inc.'s Debt Of About $2.2 Billion

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/01/90

ST. LOUIS -- Moody's Investors Service Inc. lowered its ratings on Interco Inc.'s approximately $2.2 billion in debt, saying "debt protection measures will remain weak, even if the company is successful in restructuring its maturing bank debt." The rating concern noted that Interco, a maker of shoes and furniture, "has yet to reach agreement with its bank syndicate to extend the maturity of a $244.5 million bridge loan beyond the due date of May 7." Assuming an agreement is reached to restructure the bank debt, Moody's said it believes Interco's four core operations "will still be unable to support the present overall debt burden, even after reductions achieved by currently planned asset sales."

Moody's said that it believes "further capital restructuring will therefore be necessary," and that "there is a significant risk that the terms of such a capital restructuring will not be favorable to bondholders."

Interco borrowed heavily in late 1988 to pay special dividends to shareholders and fend off a takeover threat. Asset sales have gone more slowly, and garnered lower prices, than the company's management had anticipated. In March, it reached a tentative pact with its bank lenders, pushing back the May 7 deadline for the bridge loan into 1992. A definitive agreement still hasn't been announced.

Interco said in a prepared statement that the Moody's action "doesn't reflect any change in the operating results of Interco's four core companies, which continue to perform well. Since the company has not yet made an announcement on the extension of the May 7 due date, or on the results of studies examining alternatives to improve Interco's financial position, Moody's action is not unexpected at this time."

Moody's downgraded Interco's senior notes to single-B-3 from single-B-2, and industrial development bonds, senior subordinated debentures, senior subordinated discount debentures, junior subordinated pay-in-kind debentures, and preferred stock to Caa from single-B-3.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Interco shares closed at 62.5 cents, up 6.25 cents.
