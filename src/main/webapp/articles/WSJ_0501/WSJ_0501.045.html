Who's News: Reuters Holdings Appoints Sanderson To Head Instinet Unit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/01/90

NEW YORK -- Reuters Holdings PLC, London, appointed Michael Sanderson president and chief executive officer of Instinet Corp., its equities trading unit. Mr. Sanderson, 47 years old, who recently resigned as head of Merrill Lynch & Co.'s Canadian operations, will succeed Robert H. Reid, 61, who has managed Instinet since 1988.

Mr. Sanderson's appointment suggests Instinet "has been doing well," said James Mendelson, an industry analyst at Morgan Stanley & Co. "Instinet is showing healthy growth and is finally profitable," he added.

Instinet, acquired by Reuters in 1987, traded 2.6 billion shares of mostly over-the-counter securities in 1989, up from the 922 million shares traded in 1986.

Analysts said that Mr. Sanderson will bring more of a marketing orientation to Instinet. Mr. Reid, a former New York Stock Exchange executive, had been responsible mostly for expanding Instinet's roster of stocks traded. A Reuters spokesman said Mr. Reid will work at Instinet "at least through the end of the year" and will continue the expansion of U.K. and other European securities on the Instinet system.

Reuters is an international news and financial information provider.
