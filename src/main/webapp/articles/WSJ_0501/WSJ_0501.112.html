U.S. Trade Official Cites `Helpful' Japanese Actions

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/01/90

WASHINGTON -- U.S. Trade Representative Carla Hills said Japan still has barriers to U.S. construction companies, but she declined to designate it as a country that denies "fair and equal market opportunity" to U.S. companies seeking public-works contracts. Mrs. Hills was required to decide by yesterday whether to invoke provisions of U.S. law that might be used later to bar Japanese companies from bidding for contracts to help build or supply equipment for U.S. airports.

She noted that Japan has taken some "helpful" steps to open up its market, and that some new contracts for public-works projects in that country have been awarded to U.S. construction companies. She added that the U.S. early next month will initiate a new round of talks with Japan on "the whole range of problems related to access to the Japanese market for construction services."

The U.S., she concluded, will continue to "monitor" Japan's market-opening measures. If the next round of bilateral negotiations prove to be "unsatisfactory," she said, the Bush administration would consider "appropriate action."
