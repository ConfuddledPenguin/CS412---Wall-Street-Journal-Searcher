Superior Auto Is Sued By U.S. for Failing To Install Seat Belts

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/01/90

WASHINGTON -- The Justice Department filed a $543,000 suit against Superior Auto Sales Inc., an auto importer, charging that the Buffalo, N.Y., company failed to install seat belts or air bags as required by law. The company is believed to be the nation's largest "gray market" importer of cars to the U.S., according to a statement issued by the department.

The lawsuit, filed in federal court in Buffalo, was brought by the Justice Department on behalf of the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration. It is intended to enforce a law requiring foreign-car importers to install passive restraints. The law was passed in 1984 and took effect on a phased-in basis. By last September, all imported cars were required to comply with the safety regulations.

A Justice Department spokesman said the number of cars involved wasn't known at this time. A company spokesman couldn't be reached for comment.
