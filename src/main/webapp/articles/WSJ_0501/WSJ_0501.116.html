Business Brief -- Nestle S.A.: Capital-Boost Plan Dropped; `Deteriorated' Market Cited

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/01/90

Switzerland-based Nestle S.A., the world's biggest food concern, said as expected that it will withdraw a proposed capital increase, citing "the deteriorated situation on the stock market." The 1-to-20 rights issue of registered shares, through which Nestle had hoped to increase its capital to about 364.9 million Swiss francs ($250.8 million) from 346.5 million francs, wouldn't have improved the company's financial structure, under current market conditions, Nestle said.

Nestle in late March said it would complete the capital increase only "if the stock exchange conditions are favorable." Nestle's withdrawal move follows decisions by several other large Swiss companies, including Ciba-Geigy AG and Alusuisse Lonza Holding AG, to cancel or postpone their capital increases.

Analysts and stockbrokers have been highly critical of the number of planned capital increases announced by Swiss companies in recent months, saying they were depressing an already sluggish market.
