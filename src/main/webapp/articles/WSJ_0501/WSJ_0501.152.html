International Brief -- Dresdner Bank AG: Talks on a Joint Venture In East Germany Reported

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/01/90

Dresdner Bank AG said it is holding talks with Deutsche Kreditbank AG over a banking joint venture in East Germany. Dresdner confirmed a statement by Kreditbank President Edgar Most that the two banks are holding talks.

But West Germany's second-largest banking group declined to disclose details of the negotiations, which Mr. Most said involved the establishment of a joint banking network of around 50 branches.

Frankfurt-based Dresdner has a stated strategy of expanding broadly and swiftly into East Germany as soon as regulations and the contemplated economic and monetary union between the two Germanys allow.

Mr. Most, who is chief executive officer of East Germany's first full-service, or universal, banking institution, didn't disclose further details of the contemplated venture, other than to say that Kreditbank expects to sign a letter of intent soon to set up the joint venture. Mr. Most was quoted as saying that one-month-old Kreditbank is aiming to broaden its business ties with West German banks.
