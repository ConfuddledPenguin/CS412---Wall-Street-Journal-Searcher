Construction Contracting Seen Slipping This Year

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/01/90

NEW YORK -- Construction contracting in 1990 will slide a bit, reflecting the continuing decline of commercial building, according to Dodge/Sweet's Construction Outlook. The construction market forecasting arm of McGraw-Hill Inc. valued construction activity this year at $263 billion, down 1% from 1989.

George A. Christie, vice president and chief economist of McGraw-Hill's Construction Information Group, said the national housing performance reflected regional variations. "Slowdown may be the proper term for what has happened to the national economy, but for the Northeast real estate market it is spelled recession," he said.

Mr. Christie said that although one-family housing starts are likely to increase 10% in central and western regions, a decline in multifamily construction principally in the Northeast will keep total housing starts from advancing much beyond 1.4 million units, a 2% gain. He added that the contracting value of nonresidential building will drop 7% this year, continuing a slide that began in 1986. "Although a temporary rebound is possible as the national economy improves, the overriding weakness of commercial construction will again become dominant before year end," Mr. Christie said.
