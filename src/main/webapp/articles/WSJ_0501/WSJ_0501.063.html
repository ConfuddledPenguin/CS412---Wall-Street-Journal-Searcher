Dividend News: Employers Casualty Co.

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/01/90

EMPLOYERS CASUALTY Co., Dallas, said its directors voted to omit a quarterly cash dividend for June because of poor operating results in the first quarter. The company last paid a 10-cent dividend in March. The insurance concern reported a loss of $9.1 million, or 91 cents a share, for the quarter. A year earlier, the company reported net income of $5.5 million, or 55 cents a share. Separately, the company said Ernest L. Proud Jr., formerly executive vice president, was elected president and chief executive officer. Mr. Proud succeeds Dewey Williams, who retired as president, and assumes the duties of former Chief Executive Officer John F. Stephens, who remains chairman.
