Business Brief -- Reliance Group Holdings Inc.: Insurance Subsidiary Is Sold For Total of $630 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/01/90

Reliance Group Holdings Inc. said it completed the previously announced sale of its General Casualty Co. subsidiary to Winterthur Swiss Insurance Co. for $630 million. As a result of selling the Sun Prairie, Wis., insurance company, Reliance said it will post a pretax gain of about $380 million in the current quarter.

New York-based Reliance sold General Casualty at nearly three times its book value. Standard & Poor's Corp., the rating service, has said that the sale to the Swiss company "will strengthen the statutory surplus of Reliance's insurance operating subsidiaries" and improve its consolidated financial leverage.

In the 1989 second quarter, Reliance posted a $6 million net loss, including an after-tax charge of $8.6 million to settle lawsuits.

Reliance shares closed yesterday in New York Stock Exchange composite trading at $5.625, up 25 cents.
