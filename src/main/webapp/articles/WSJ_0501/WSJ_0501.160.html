Nuclear Power Plant At Seabrook Is Shut For Up to six Weeks

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/01/90

SEABROOK, N.H. -- The Seabrook nuclear power plant, which recently received a commercial operating license, will be shut down for four to six weeks to modify a turbine. The plant has been in a phase-in period and was operating at less than 10% capacity when it was shut down on Saturday because readings indicated a shaft in the turbine could vibrate beyond specifications.

The shutdown is the third for the plant since the Nuclear Regulatory Commission issued the license in mid-March. Critics of Seabrook called for an "independent construction inpsection" of the plant, contending that the shutdowns are a sign of poor workmanship and an indication that it isn't safe to operate.

New Hampshire Yankee, which manages the plant for 11 New England utilities that own a stake in Seabrook, has rejected such criticism, noting that previous studies have determined that Seabrook has been built to NRC guidelines. Regarding the problems found while the plant is working up in stages to full-power production, a spokesman for New Hampshire Yankee said a purpose of the phase-in period is to find "areas where improvements can be made."
