OTC Focus: Shares Rise Late in Day on Buying Swell Set Off by Program Trades on Big Board

  • Author: Craig S. Smith
  • Date: 05/01/90

NEW YORK -- The over-the-counter stock market rose late in the day on a swell of buying set off by computer-driven programs on the New York Stock Exchange. Traders say that the buy programs hit the Big Board around 2 p.m. EDT, drawing buyers into the OTC market to catch the wave.

The Nasdaq Composite Index, which treaded water for most of the day, rose 2.09, or 0.5%, to 420.07 in the last hours of trading. The Big Board Composite Index added 0.41%. OTC advancers led decliners, 907 to 897. Total Nasdaq volume fell to a slim 105.8 million shares from Friday's 116 million shares.

Ed Mazzella, senior vice president of OTC trading at Shearson Lehman Hutton, says he was surprised that stock prices moved as much as they did. He didn't expect much activity until after the Treasury announces details of its quarterly refunding tomorrow. The yield on the Treasury long bond eased to 8.99% from the 9% level it reached last week. The drop in the yield came as bond prices rose gradually, helping stock prices.

Laidlaw Transportation led the most active list with volume of more than 2.5 million shares. The stock closed at 19 5/8, up 1/8.

Lattice Semiconductor gained 1 7/8 to 12 1/2. The company reported fourth-quarter net income of 26 cents a share, up from nine cents a share a year earlier.

Northeast Bancorp dropped 4 7/8 to 28 1/4 as its ailing merger pact with Bank of New York takes another turn for the worse.

Bank of New York is threatening to end the agreement, claiming that Northeast has breached the merger pact by failing to maintain accurate books. Northeast said it is in compliance with the agreement. The merger ran into trouble in late April when the two parties disagreed over Northeast's book value.

Technology stocks fared mildly better. On the most active list, Sun Microsystems gained 7/8 to 24 5/8, Apple Computer has added 1/4 to 39 3/8, Seagate Technology was 3/8 point ahead to 11 1/2 and Cirrus Logic gained 5/8 to 12 7/8.

Cirrus reported its fourth-quarter net income more than doubled to 34 cents a share from the 15 cents a share recorded the year earlier.

IDB Communications Group rose 1 to 9 1/4. Swissair agreed to buy a 5% stake in the company for $13 a share.

U.S. Healthcare jumped 1 to 13 1/4 after reporting earnings of 25 cents a share in the first quarter, up from 11 cents a share a year earlier.

Neworld Bancorp added 1/2 to 7 1/2. A Massachusetts investor received regulatory clearance to increase his stake to nearly 20% from slightly less than 9%.

American Indemnity Financial lost 1 1/2 to 4 3/8 after reporting a 65-cent-a-share loss in the first quarter, compared with profit of 57 cents a share a year earlier.

Costar gained 2 to 24 1/4 after reporting profit of 31 cents a share in the first quarter, compared with 13 cents a share a year earlier.
