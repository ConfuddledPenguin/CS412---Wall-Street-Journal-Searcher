Business Brief -- Bank Building & Equipment Corp.: Company Seeks Protection In U.S. Bankruptcy Court

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/01/90

Bank Building & Equipment Corp. of America, St. Louis, said it filed for protection from creditors under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code. Last autumn, the company disclosed that it had uncovered financial irregularities at one of its divisions, and that it expected the problems to reduce earnings by $3.3 million. It subsequently launched a corporate "downsizing" and financial restructuring.

For the fiscal year ended Oct. 31, the company had a loss of $10.4 million, which it said was attributable "primarily to mismanagement and irregularities in the Loughman Cabinet division."

Bank Building filed the petition for reorganization in federal Bankruptcy Court in St. Louis. It said it plans to sell off all its real estate, plant machinery and equipment that won't be required for operations in the reorganized concern, and that it expects all existing contracts to be "completed as scheduled." Company officials couldn't be reached for comment.

Bank Building plans, designs and constructs facilities for financial and medical clients.
