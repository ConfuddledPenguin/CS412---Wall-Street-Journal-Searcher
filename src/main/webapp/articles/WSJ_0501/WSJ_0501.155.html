Business Brief -- Allied-Signal Inc.: Hennessy Foresees Decade Of International Expansion

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/01/90

Allied-Signal Inc., Morris Township, N.J., will expand its international business in the 1990s, according to Chairman and Chief Executive Officer Edward L. Hennessy Jr. Speaking at the company's annual meeting, Mr. Hennessy noted that 36% of the company's revenue is generated outside the U.S. and about 30% of the employees work in other countries. He said, "I intend for these percentages to grow in the 1990s as we improve our positions in markets throughout the world."

Mr. Hennessy said capital expenditures will rise this year from $541 million in 1989.

Company-funded research, development and engineering will fall this year from 1989 levels to "slightly more than $600 million," because of "some divestitures and a tighter focus on our key development programs," Mr. Hennessy said. However, a rise in customer-funded research, development and engineering will keep total research spending at $1.2 billion this year, the same as 1989, Mr. Hennessy said.
