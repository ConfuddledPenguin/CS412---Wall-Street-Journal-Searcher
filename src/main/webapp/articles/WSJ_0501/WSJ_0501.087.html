Nomura Units, Barr Rosenberg Join in Research

  • Author: Michael R. Sesit
  • Date: 05/01/90

Two U.S. units of Japan's Nomura Securities Co. are linking up with quantitative investment specialist Barr Rosenberg to study ways in which computers can be applied to managing securities portfolios. Shareholders of the new joint venture, Nomura Rosenberg Investment Technology Institute, will be Mr. Rosenberg, Nomura Holding America Inc. and Nomura's U.S. research arm. Mr. Rosenberg, who designs mathematical models to manage money, will be chairman. The institute will be staffed by about 10 professionals from Nomura along with Mr. Rosenberg; it will be located in San Francisco.

Nomura said that the new institute will do research in international investment theory, particularly focusing on ways to use quantitative methods in managing diversified portfolios, perhaps involving securities denominated in different currencies and invested in various markets.

Nomura already has a relationship with Mr. Rosenberg. In 1987, the two teamed up to form Rosenberg Asset Management in Japan. The concern primarily uses quantitative methods to manage funds for Japanese investors in the Tokyo market. Nomura also owns 49% of Rosenberg Institutional Equity Management Inc. of Orinda, Calif., which manages more than $2 billion of Japanese money in the U.S.

While small, the new institute is another example of the growing links between Japanese investors and U.S. portfolio managers. It also reflects the increasing Japanese interest in "quantitative" investing, in which funds are invested according to computer-based models.

Besides Nomura, Nikko Securities Co., Sumitomo Bank Ltd., Orix Corp., Industrial Bank of Japan Ltd. and Long-Term Credit Bank of Japan Ltd. all have links with American money managers. In addition, both Yamaichi Securities Co. and Daiwa Securities Co. have hired U.S. academics to design computer-driven investment models for various markets. In the biggest "quantitative" connection to date, Nikko last June agreed to pay $125 million for 50% of the money management unit of Wells Fargo & Co. The deal was completed earlier this year.
