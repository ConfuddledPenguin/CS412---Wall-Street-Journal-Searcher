Business Brief -- Whitehall Corp.: Cambridge Capital's Effort To Unseat Board Defeated

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/01/90

Whitehall Corp., a Dallas-based defense concern, said its management's slate of directors was re-elected, defeating a slate nominated by Cambridge Capital Fund Limited Partnership, a New York investment fund. Neither side could give the actual voting results, but Cambridge said one of its candidates received as much as 46% of the votes cast. Whitehall wouldn't comment on that claim.

Cambridge also said that while it questions the proxy tabulations, it acknowledged that it didn't receive the votes needed to unseat Whitehall's board. It also said it is withdrawing its offer to acquire the 90.4% of Whitehall that it doesn't already own for $20 a share, or about $62 million. Still, a Cambridge spokesman said the group is "contemplating our alternatives." In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Whitehall shares closed at $14.375, down 62.5 cents.
