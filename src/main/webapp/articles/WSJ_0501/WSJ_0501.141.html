Business Brief -- Nashua Corp.: Dutch Auction Buy-Back Of Common Shares Begins

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/01/90

Nashua Corp. launched a Dutch auction tender offer for as many as 2.75 million of its common shares. The Nashua, N.H., maker of coated paper and computer products and photofinishing concern will offer tendering shareholders a price in the range of $39 to $44 a share. The company has 9.2 million shares outstanding. In such a Dutch auction, a company states the number of shares it will buy during a stipulated period with a price range within which shareholders can tender their shares. The company will select a single purchase price within the range to enable it to buy all the shares it wants.

Nashua said the offer isn't conditioned on any minimum number of shares tendered. Funding for the offer is coming from a portion of the proceeds of the recently completed sale of the company's international office systems business to Gestetner Holdings PLC of London. Salomon Brothers Inc. will manage the offer.
