International: Allianz, Navigation Mixte To Merge Insurance Lines

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 05/01/90

MUNICH, West Germany -- Allianz AG said that it has agreed to merge its French insurance businesses with those of Cie de Navigation Mixte to form a new joint-venture company that will be one of France's largest private insurers, of which the West German company will own 65%. Allianz, which also is Europe's largest insurance group, said the planned merger of the respective French businesses in effect expands an agreement last November, when Allianz acquired 50% of Navigation Mixte's French insurance operations.

"From the very beginning, the intention was to merge the (company's French insurance operations) one way or another under one holding company," an Allianz spokeswoman said. She noted that an unsuccessful hostile takeover attempt of Navigation by Cie. Financiere de Paribas last year had forced her company and its French partner to delay talks on the merger of the insurance lines in France until now.

Under West German accounting rules, the new joint venture's sales and earnings will be consoldiated into the annual accounts of Allianz.

Under the joint venture agreement, Navigation Mixte will hold 34% of the new French company, which will have around 10 billion French francs ($1.78 billion) worth of premium income annually. Allianz said third parties, which it didn't identify, would hold the remaining 1% stake in the new joint venture.
