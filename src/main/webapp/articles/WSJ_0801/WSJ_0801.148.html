House Passes Bill to Lift Federal Borrowing Limit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/01/90

WASHINGTON -- The House passed and sent to the Senate a bill that would raise the federal borrowing limit to $3.444 trillion from $3.122 trillion, sufficient to allow government borrowing through September 1991. Congressional leaders expect the bill to bog down in the Senate and a shorter-term extension to take its place. A short-term alternative already has been moving in the House even as the lawmakers voted yesterday. The House Ways and Means Committee has approved a measure that would authorize the Treasury to keep borrowing through Oct. 15. This version may reach the House floor later this week. The Treasury says it will default unless Congress lifts the debt ceiling by Aug. 15.

The congressional recess is scheduled to begin Friday, although Senate leaders say they may delay their departure in order to continue working on a deficit-reduction package.

Yesterday's debt bill, which was approved 221-205, included a provision backed by Rep. Byron Dorgan (D., N.D.) that would remove the Social Security Trust Fund from the Gramm-Rudman calculation of the federal budget deficit.
