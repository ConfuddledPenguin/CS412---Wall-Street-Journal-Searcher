Japan's Economic Indicators

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/01/90

TOKYO -- The Japanese economy's leading indicators for May rose to 63.6, well above the boom-or-bust line of 50.0, suggesting continued steady growth for the economy, Japan's Economic Planning Agency reported. The leading indicators were up from a revised 54.5 in April. It was the third consecutive month that the index stayed above 50.0.

The agency interprets any figure above 50.0 as a sign that the economy should continue to expand over the next six months, an official said. Any number below 50.0 is seen as a sign that the economy could begin to contract.

Although four of the 11 components of the index -- new job offers, real machinery orders, construction orders and consumer durable shipments -- fell from the previous month, agency officials called the decline a reaction to high previous levels rather than a sign of slowdown.
