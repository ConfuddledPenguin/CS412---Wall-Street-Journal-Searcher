Oppenheimer Management To Be Bought, Report Says

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/01/90

NEW YORK -- Oppenheimer Management Corp.'s top executives have apparently teamed up with Massachusetts Mutual Life Insurance Co. to purchase Oppenheimer Management, a $14 billion fund company, from British & Commonwealth Holdings PLC. Oppenheimer Management's president, Jon Fossel, declined to comment on the report, which first appeared in Investment Management Weekly, a Boston newsletter. Mr. Fossel did, however, say he expected to make an announcement on the sale this morning.

British & Commonwealth, a U.K. financial-services group, purchased Oppenheimer from another British firm, Mercantile Holdings, shortly before the 1987 stock-market crash. But in early June, British & Commonwealth entered "administration," the U.K. equivalent of the U.S.'s Chapter 11 bankruptcy-law proceedings, and it is now looking to sell Oppenheimer and other assets to reduce its $2 billion of debt. Oppenheimer Management isn't related to Oppenheimer & Co., the U.S. brokerage firm.

Springfield, Mass.-based MassMutual, the U.S.'s 11th largest life insurance company, manages and distributes MassMutual Integrity Funds, a group of mutual funds that were launched in 1988 and have more than $350 million in assets. MassMutual's agents sell both the firm's own mutual funds and those of other fund companies, including Oppenheimer's.
