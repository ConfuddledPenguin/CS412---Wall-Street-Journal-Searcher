Matsushita Electric Enters Joint Venture With Whirlpool Unit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/01/90

BENTON HARBOR, Mich. -- Whirlpool Corp. and Japan's Matsushita Electric Industrial Co. said they formally established a joint venture that will make vacuum cleaners in the U.S. for domestic and export markets. To form the venture, the Osaka, Japan, concern purchased a majority position in what was formerly Whirlpool's Danville, Ky., division. Terms of the transaction, including Matsushita's specific stake, weren't disclosed.

Whirlpool announced in February that it was seeking a buyer for the division, which makes vacuum cleaners primarily for resale by Sears, Roebuck & Co. under the Kenmore brand name.

But in June, Whirlpool reached a tentative agreement with Matsushita to form the venture, which is expected to permit the Japanese concern to expand its manufacturing and marketing base in North America.
