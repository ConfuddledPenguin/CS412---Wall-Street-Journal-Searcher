Ruling Against Volkswagen AG

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/01/90

FRANKFURT -- West Germany's Federal Cartel Office yesterday said it has made a preliminary ruling against Volkswagen AG's practice of requiring its dealers to offer customers only leasing contracts from a VW financial-services subsidiary. The West Berlin-based antitrust watchdog said in the informal decision that Volkswagen's dealers are being unfairly hindered from seeking working relationships with other leasing companies, which at the same time are being deprived of potential customers.

The ruling, which could be made legally binding at a later date, is important since Volkswagen is West Germany's largest producer of cars, via its Volkswagen, Audi and Seat brands. The company also claims to be Europe's largest car maker.

The Cartel Office noted that the Wolfsburg-based company's V.A.G. Leasing G.m.b.H. subsidiary controls about 20% of the West German vehicle leasing market.
