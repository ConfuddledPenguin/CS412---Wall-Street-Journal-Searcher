GPU Nuclear Unit to Pay U.S. Nuclear Agency Fine

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/01/90

NEW YORK -- GPU Nuclear Corp. said it will pay a $50,000 fine imposed by the federal Nuclear Regulatory Commission for a 1987 incident in which a control room shift supervisor at a Three Mile Island nuclear reactor unit was found sleeping on duty. The utility, a subsidiary of General Public Utilities Corp., Parsippany, N.J., said the NRC confirmed the findings of a 1987 company investigation that led to the dismissal of the shift supervisor.

Although the commission said its investigation found that prompt and thorough action was taken by the company to prevent a recurrence, it levied the fine because the incident wasn't dealt with adequately by site managers.

The company said a site manager, who was slow to act and allowed the shift supervisor's behavior to go uncorrected for months, was dismissed.

The company said an internal investigation began after people at the unit sent anonymous notes to top managers accusing the shift supervisor of sleeping on the job.

The utility said the incident occurred while damaged fuel was being removed from the Three Mile Island Unit 2 reactor vessel. That work was completed last January, the company said.

In 1979, a reactor cooling system failed, allowing radioactive water to flood the Three Mile Island nuclear power plant near Harrisburg, Pa. It was one of the nation's worst nuclear power plant accidents.
