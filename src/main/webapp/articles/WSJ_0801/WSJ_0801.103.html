Business Brief -- Brooklyn Union Gas Co.: Judge Recommends Increase Of $40.4 Million in Rates

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/01/90

Brooklyn Union Gas Co., New York, said an administrative law judge recommended that it be allowed a $40.4 million rate increase, which would be the utility's first rate gain in five years. The increase, to take effect in October, would reflect a 12% return on equity. Brooklyn Union Gas had sought a $49 million increase, reflecting a 13.2% return on equity, to cover increased maintenance and other expenses. The utility has until Aug. 14 to challenge Judge Edward D. Cohen's recommendation before the New York State Public Service Commission, which is expected to make a final decision by October.

The commission's staff several months ago proposed a $28 million increase, representing an 11.2% return.
