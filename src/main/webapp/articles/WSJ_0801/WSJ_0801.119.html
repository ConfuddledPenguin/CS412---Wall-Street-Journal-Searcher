International Brief -- National Westminster Bank PLC: Concern Posts 22% Increase In 1st-Half Pretax Earnings

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/01/90

National Westminster Bank PLC, Britain's second-largest commercial bank, said yesterday its pretax profit for the six months ended June 30 rose 22% from the year-earlier period to #431 million ($796.3 million). Pretax profit in the first half of 1989, which was hurt by provisions against loans to lesser developed countries, was #352 million.

As in the year-earlier period, the bank's pretax profit in the latest period was curbed by #425 million in net loan charges.

But even though the latest period's loan provisions were down 27% from #585 million in the first half of last year, the key difference this time around is that the provisions were to cover exposure in the U.K. and U.S. markets rather than in developing countries.

Lord Alexander, chairman of NatWest, issued a cautious statement, saying, "These profits are considerably lower than we would have wished." Lord Alexander stressed, however, that "problem-country debt is largely behind us" and that NatWest's "underlying core business is sound."
