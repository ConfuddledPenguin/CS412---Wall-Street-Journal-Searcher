International: Saab-GM Sales Venture

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/01/90

STOCKHOLM -- Saab Automobile AB, the fledgling joint venture between General Motors Corp. and Saab-Scania AB, agreed to begin selling GM's U.S.-built cars, light trucks, pickups and vans through its Swedish dealer network. The move offers GM a national sales base in Sweden in time to tap an expected surge in pickup and van sales in coming years. In turn, the addition of GM models could help beleaguered Swedish dealers plug a gap caused by plunging sales of new Saabs this year.

The GM marketing alliance also underlines Saab Automobile's growing autonomy by thrusting the unit into head-on competition with its Swedish parent. GM's biggest rival in Sweden's burgeoning pickup and van market is the Swedish sales unit of Volkswagen AG -- in which Saab-Scania owns a controlling 67% stake.

Saab officials expect to sell about 5,000 GM vehicles -- representing revenue of about one billion Swedish kronor ($170.8 million) -- during the pact's first year. Last year, GM sold about the same number of vehicles here, directly imported from the U.S. by less than a dozen independent dealers.

In all, Swedes bought about 10,000 new U.S. autos last year -- a sharp increase in volume compared to the early 1980s when the buoyant dollar effectively priced U.S. gas guzzlers out of the Swedish market.
