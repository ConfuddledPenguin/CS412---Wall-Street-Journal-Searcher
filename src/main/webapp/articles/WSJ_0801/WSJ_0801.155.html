What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/01/90

UAL'S STOCK PLUNGED $16.875 a share to $140 after two more banks, Citicorp and Chase Manhattan, indicated they had withdrawn for now from efforts to finance a $4.38 billion buy-out of the United Air parent. The moves, following Chemical Bank's withdrawal, leave the bid in doubt nine days before a financing deadline. ---

Chrysler's profit skidded 47% in the second quarter. Aggressive costcutting and surging results from financial services prevented a loss.

---

The dollar fell more than 1% against the yen and the mark, stalling a bond rally and contributing to a mild retreat in blue-chip stocks.

---

Ford agreed to sell its farm-equipment unit to a new joint venture 80%-owned by Fiat. The deal virtually completes Ford's exit from all businesses except autos and finance.

---

The FDIC's fund to cover bank failures may suffer a bigger-than-expected loss this year, perhaps totaling $2 billion, the agency's head said.

---

Tougher sentences for S&L crime and more funding to prosecute such violations were passed by the House.

An ex-U.S. official said he regretted the sale of an ailing thrift to a once-indicted insurance executive.

---

USX's second-quarter profit fell 15% as oil gains failed to offset lower steel earnings and natural-gas losses.

---

Interco's lenders agreed in general to restructure $1.9 billion in debt, averting for now a bankruptcy-law filing by the furniture and shoe concern.

---

Xerox posted a 27% decline in second-quarter profit despite encouraging results in its major business areas.

---

Walt Disney Co. unveiled plans for a $2.3 billion waterfront theme park and resort in Long Beach, Calif.

---

Schering-Plough should be allowed to market interferon to treat a form of hepatitis, an FDA panel urged.

---

A commodities firm linked to Chicago Board of Trade Chairman Karsten Mahlmann was charged by the U.S. with misusing investor funds.

---

Dutch retail giant KBB bought toy-store chain F.A.O. Schwarz from investment banker Peter Morse for a sum thought to be about $40 million.

---

Curbs on sales pitches delivered by fax machines and automatic phone dialers passed the House, but Bush advisers will recommend a veto.

---

Pic 'N' Save unveiled plans to cut costs and resume a stock buy-back and disclosed the resignation of chief executive Lewis Merrifield. The retailer's moves to boost its share price may satisfy a potential hostile bidder.

---

Bush and senior Democrats are giving up any hope of reaching a budget accord before Labor Day.

---
