Business Brief -- Eagle Financial Corp.: Company Plans Buy-Back To Enhance Stock Value

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/01/90

Eagle Financial Corp., hoping to enhance its stock value, said it plans to buy back up to 10% of its shares outstanding. The Bristol, Conn., bank holding company said it would repurchase the shares periodically over the next 12 months in the open market and in unsolicited negotiated transactions. The company said it has about 2.7 million shares outstanding. The shares closed yesterday at $8.75, up 25 cents, in American Stock Exchange trading.

Eagle also said Ernest J. Torrizo, a director of the First Federal Savings Bank unit, and Steven E. Lasewics Jr., a director of the Bristol Federal Savings unit, were named directors of the parent company, increasing the board membership to 10.

The boards for the subsidiaries have been replaced by the Eagle board, which will serve concurrently as the board for the subsidiaries, the company said. The reorganization reduces the total number of directors to 10 from 19.
