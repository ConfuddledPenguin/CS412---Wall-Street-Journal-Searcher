Kemper Corp. Posts $111.2 Million Loss For Second Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/01/90

LONG GROVE, Ill. -- Kemper Corp., racked by $161.1 million in charges to cover problems at its securities-brokerage division, reported a second-quarter loss of $111.2 million, or $2.28 a share. In the year-earlier period, the insurance and investment-services concern had net income of $121.7 million, or $2.46 a share. Revenue rose 12% in the latest period to $757.9 million, from $677.8 million.

The one-time charges that hurt Kemper's latest results include $141.1 million -- some $15 million more than it had earlier projected -- to cover an overhaul effort at its brokerage operations. The company earlier said it would take a $126.5 million charge to write down good will and other intangible assets at the brokerage concerns, and to cover costs associated with a staff-reduction program in that segment.

Additionally, the results include a previously disclosed $19.5 million net charge to cover an arbitration panel's May finding that the company should pay a former employee $38.2 million.

Kemper said the revamping of its brokerage operations "is proceeding on track," and that it continues to expect to have the restructuring "in place by year end." At quarter's end, the company said, it has cut the brokerage segment's work force by about 200 employees, "well on our way to our 600 target."

Through the latest first half, Kemper's life-insurance operation showed narrower margins, and earnings at its property-casualty and reinsurance segment were off as well, in part because of higher-than-normal losses from severe weather in Europe and the U.S.

Kemper didn't separately break out the segments' performance for the second quarter.
