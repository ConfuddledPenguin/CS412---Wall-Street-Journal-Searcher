Sallie Mae Promises To Lend More Funds To Loan Guarantor

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/01/90

WASHINGTON -- The Student Loan Marketing Association said that it has agreed to lend another $200 million to the Higher Education Assistance Foundation, the financially troubled student-loan guarantee agency. Sallie Mae said the Education Department asked it to provide the short-term financing while the government continues its efforts to find an entity to take over the assets and operations of HEAF. Sallie Mae, a government-chartered, stockholder-owned corporation that has created a secondary market for student loans, and several other guarantee agencies are candidates to take over HEAF's guaranteed student-loan portfolio, which totaled $8.8 billion as of July 17, according to congressional auditors.

The government has engaged Kidder, Peabody & Co., the Wall Street unit of General Electric Co., to help it sort out its options.

The additional $200 million short-term financing is collateralized by student loans, and the Education Department has assured Sallie Mae that it will reinsure 100% of these loans. Prior to the latest loan, Sallie Mae had loaned HEAF $200 million and loaned affiliates of HEAF about $600 million. In addition, Sallie Mae owns about $2.4 billion of loans guaranteed by HEAF. Sallie Mae asserts that it faces no risk on these assets because they are fully reinsured by the Education Department.
