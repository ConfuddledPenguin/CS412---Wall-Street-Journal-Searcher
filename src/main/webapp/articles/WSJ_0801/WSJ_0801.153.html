New-Home Sales Rose 8% In June After May Increase

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/01/90

WASHINGTON -- The government reported that sales of newly built single-family houses rose 8% in June, following a 2.3% increase in May. Sales in June ran at an annual rate of 581,000 units, according to the report by the departments of Commerce and Housing and Urban Development. The figures are adjusted for seasonal changes.

Sales fell 5.7% in April, the report said. Analysts suggested that the decline in mortgage rates over the past few months may have led to a rebound.

Many economists regard the home sales figures as unreliable, however, because they are often heavily revised in subsequent months. The government itself said in the report that the margin of error is so great -- plus or minus 12 percentage points -- that the 8% increase reported for June may in fact turn out to have been a decline.

The departments put the average price of newly built homes at $152,900 in June, up from $149,700 in May. They said the median price was $129,900, up from $127,900 in May.
