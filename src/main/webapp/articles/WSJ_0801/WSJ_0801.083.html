U.S. Urges Approval Of Phone Companies' Service to New Zealand

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/01/90

WASHINGTON -- The Justice Department recommended allowing Ameritech and Bell Atlantic Corp. to provide long-distance service between the U.S. and New Zealand and to own satellites and undersea cables for that service. The New Zealand government, as part of its effort to privatize state-owned companies, selected Ameritech and Bell Atlantic in June to buy the country's phone company, Telecom Corp. of New Zealand, for about $2.46 billion.

The Justice Department made its recommendation to Judge Harold H. Greene, who oversees the consent decree that broke up the Bell System in 1984. Among other things, the consent decree bars the seven regional Baby Bells from providing long-distance service.

The department concluded that "there is no substantial possibility" that the two companies will impede competition in providing telecommunications between the U.S. and New Zealand. The department asked Judge Greene to decide the matter promptly because the transaction goes to closing on Sept. 12.

A spokesman for Ameritech said the New Zealand partnership was pleased and encouraged that the department "has recommended the court act expeditiously to grant the waiver."
