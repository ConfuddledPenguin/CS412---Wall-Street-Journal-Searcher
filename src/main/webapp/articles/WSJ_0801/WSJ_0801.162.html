Urcarco Is Accused In Suits of Breaches Of Securities Laws

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/01/90

FORT WORTH, Texas -- Urcarco Inc. said two lawsuits had been filed in federal courts in Dallas and Fort Worth accusing the high-flying operator of used car lots of violating securities laws. Also named as defendants were certain Urcarco executives, including Chairman Clifton Morris, and, in one case, the company's independent auditors.

Urcarco said one suit accuses the company of concealing a "serious liquidity crisis" from the public. The company said that the other suit asserts that its financial statements weren't prepared in conformity with generally accepted accounting principles. The suits also challenge the company's revenue and income recognition policies and the adequacy of its allowance for loan losses. Each suit seeks class action status, Urcarco said.

Describing the allegations of a liquidity crisis as "groundless, frivolous and in bad faith," the company said it currently has about $65 million in cash on hand and an available bank line of credit of $45 million. The company also said it has repeatedly reviewed its financial statements and accounting policies with the Securities and Exchange Commission and believes they are consistent with generally accepted accounting principles.

"The company will vigorously defend these suits," the company said.

Operating 17 car lots in Texas, Urcarco sells used cars to people with credit problems, charging high interest rates on the resulting loans. Since going public last November, the company has frequently reported dramatic increases in earnings and seen its stock triple in value, climbing to a high of $25.50 last April. But the stock has tumbled since then.

In national over-the-counter trading yesterday, Urcarco closed down 50 cents at $10.875.
