New Securities Issues

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/01/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report. CORPORATES

Sears, Roebuck & Co. -- $300 million of 9 1/4% notes due Aug. 1, 1997, priced at 99.885 to yield 9.272%. The size of the issue was increased from $250 million. The non-callable notes were priced at a spread of 0.98 percentage point above the Treasury's seven-year note. Rated single-A-1 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and single-A-plus by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Goldman, Sachs & Co.

American Express Credit Corp. -- $200 million of 8 5/8% notes due Aug. 1, 1994, priced at 99.65 to yield 8.73%. The non-callable notes were priced at 0.625 percentage point above the Treasury's four-year note. Rated double-A-2 by Moody's and double-A by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Lehman Brothers.

Kimberly-Clark Corp. -- $100 million of 9% notes, due Aug. 1, 2000, was priced at 99.64 to yield 9.055%. The non-callable notes were priced at a spread of 0.70 percentage point above the Treasury's 10-year note. Rated double-A-2 by Moody's and double-A by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Goldman Sachs.

MUNICIPALS

Huntsville, Ala., Solid Waste Disposal Authority -- $87 million of solid waste disposal and resource recovery revenue bonds, Series 1990, have been priced by a Merrill Lynch Capital Markets group. The bonds were priced to yield from 6.40% in 1994 to 7.40% in 2014. They are rated triple-A by Moody's and S&P and are insured.

Oklahoma Industries Authority -- $76.4 million of hospital revenue bonds, Series 1990 A, have been priced by a Goldman Sachs group. The total amount of the issue has been raised slightly from $76 million. The bonds, for the Baptist Medical Center of Oklahoma, yield from 6.20% in 1993 to 7.20% in 2014. Bonds due in 2000 and later are callable Aug. 15, 2000, at 102, declining to par on Aug. 15, 2002.

MORTGAGE

Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $400 million of subordinated callable debentures has been priced at par to yield 9.15%. The securities are due Aug. 8, 2000, and are callable on or after Aug. 8, 1995. The securities must be purchased in minimum amounts of $5,000 with additional increments of $5,000. The securities were priced at a spread of about 0.80 percentage point over 10-year Treasury notes.

Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $300 million of real estate mortgage investment conduit securities is being offered in 15 classes by UBS Securities, a unit of Union Bank of Switzerland. The offering, Series FHLMC 190, is backed by Freddie Mac 9 1/2% securities with a weighted average coupon rate of 10.12% and weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29.23 years. Pricing was at market levels.

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $300 million of Remic securities is being offered in 14 classes by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets. The offering, priced Friday through Series 1990-101, is backed by Fannie Mae 9% securities.

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $300 million of Remic securities is being offered in 16 classes by J.P. Morgan Securities. The offering, Series 1990-102, is backed by Fannie Mae 9% securities with a weighted average coupon rate of 9.70% and weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29.67 years. Full details on yields and prices weren't immediatly available.

EUROBONDS

West German government (sovereign) -- three billion marks ($1.89 billion) of 8 1/2% bonds due Aug. 21, 2000, at 100 1/2 to yield 8.42%, via Deutsche Bundesbank. Entire amount placed via the fixed federal bond consortium.

Unilever NV -- One billion French francs ($188 million) of 9 7/8% bonds due Sept. 4, 1997, priced at 101.40 to yield 9.97% less fees via BNP Capital Markets Ltd. Fees 1 7/8.

Deutsche Genossenschaftsbank (West Germany) -- 250 million marks of bonds with equity-purchase warrants attached for Berliner Handels-und Frankfurter Bank stock with a 3 1/2% coupon due Dec. 21, 1995, and priced at par, via Deutsche Genossenschaftsbank. Each 1,000 mark bond carries two warrants, exercisable from Oct. 2, 1990, to Dec. 1, 1995, to buy two shares of BHF stock for 510 marks apiece. Each 10,000 mark bond carries 20 warrants to buy 20 shares of stock under the same terms.

Abbey National Treasury Services PLC (United Kingdom parent) -- 150 million European currency units of 10 1/8% bonds due Aug. 21, 1995, priced at 101 1/8 to yield 10 1/8% less fees via Swiss Bank Corp. Guaranteed by Abbey National PLC. Fees 1 7/8.

Credit Foncier de France (France) -- 150 million European currency units of 9 3/8% bonds due Dec. 27, 1999, priced at 98.97 via Societe Generale. Fungible with the original 150 million ECU issue launched in November 1989. Guaranteed by the Republic of France.

European Telecommunications Satellite Organization (agency) -- 100 million European currency units of 10% bonds due Aug. 28, 1992, priced at 101 1/8 via Dresdner Bank.
