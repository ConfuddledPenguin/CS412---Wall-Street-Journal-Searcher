Grossinger's Hotel Files For Chapter 7 Bankruptcy

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/01/90

NEW YORK (AP) -- Grossinger's Hotel and Country Club, the Catskill Mountains retreat where Rocky Marciano trained and Eddie Fisher launched his career, has filed for bankruptcy, the hotel's trustee said. A voluntary Chapter 11 reorganization petition filed in December by Grossinger's in U.S. Bankruptcy Court in White Plains, N.Y., was converted to a Chapter 7 petition yesterday, said the hotel's court-appointed trustee, Barbara Balliber-Strauss.

Ms. Balliber-Strauss declined to discuss details of the action, but said the hotel and its contents will likely be liquidated under the Chapter 7 petition.

Grossinger's registered its first guests in 1914 and was run by the Grossinger family for 71 years. Losing money, the sprawling resort was sold in 1985 by the family to a group of New York City investors for $9 million.

The tudor-style resort in Sullivan County, about 120 miles northwest of New York City, has been empty since 1986 when it was closed for a multimillion dollar renovation and expansion.

The halls are lined with black-and-white photographs of famous guests, fighters who trained there and entertainers who performed there, including Jack Dempsey and Lucille Ball.
