International Brief -- Petrofina S.A.. Petrochemical Concern's Net Fell 12% for 1990 First Half

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/01/90

Petrofina S.A. said lower petrochemical prices and the weak dollar knocked its net profit down 12% in the first half. The Belgian oil and petrochemical company said net profit totaled 10.13 billion Belgian francs ($306.8 million), down from 11.49 billion Belgian francs a year earlier. The company didn't provide figures for total sales.

On the Brussels Bourse, Petrofina shares slipped 225 Belgian francs to close at 11,800 Belgian francs. Patrick de Bellefroid, chief analyst at Puissant Baeyens Poswick & Cie., called the results "a bit disappointing" and said his firm might have to reduce its forecast for the full year, which had called for earnings about even with 1989's 21.82 billion Belgian francs.

Though Petrofina's petrochemical results were down substantially in the half, the company said profit margins began to improve in the second quarter for two of its main products, high-density polyethylene and polypropylene.

The weak dollar hurt Petrofina's results partly because the company derives substantial income from its American Petrofina Inc. unit.

In Europe, Petrofina said its sales of gasoline and other motor fuels rose 8%.
