CD Yields Eased a Bit In Most Recent Week

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/01/90

NEW YORK -- Yields on certificates of deposit at major banks and brokerage firms declined slightly in the week ended Tuesday. The average yield on six-month small-denomination CDs sold directly by banks slipped to 7.53% from 7.55%, according to an 18-bank survey by Banxquote Money Markets, a New York information service. The average for two-year consumer CDs fell to 7.79% from 7.80%. However, the average yield for a five-year consumer CD remained at 8.02%.

Average yields on small-denomination CDs sold by leading broker dealers were also down. The average yield on a one-year CD fell the most, to 7.80% from 7.93%, while the average for five-year CDs fell to 8.27% from 8.34%, according to Banxquote.

Average yields for large-denomination CDs, which typically require deposits of $90,000 or more, declined. The average yield on a one-year jumbo CDs eased to 7.68% from 7.79%, while the average for two-year maturities slipped to 7.90% from 7.97%.

"Many big banks cut their jumbo rates, which may be a sign that they expect rates to fall further," says Norberto Mehl, chairman of Banxquote.
