International: Stalinism-Steeped Albania Allows Foreign Investment

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 08/01/90

VIENNA -- Communist Albania announced it will permit foreign investment and joint ventures. It also decreed limited freedom to stage public demonstrations. The new economic measures decreed by the Tirana leadership appeared designed to alleviate economic backwardness, provide technical know-how, improve living conditions and circumvent a ban on foreign credits.

The decrees were approved by the presidium of the People's Assembly and reported by the state news agency ATA only weeks after some 5,000 Albanians, holed up in foreign embassies, were allowed to emigrate from Albania, the poorest country in Europe and a lone stronghold of Stalinism.

"Foreign embassies, firms or individuals or Albanians residing abroad can make investments or carry out related economic activities," said a decree issued on "the protection of foreign investments in the People's Socialist Republic of Albania."

While the official announcement didn't specifically mention joint ventures, it said that in Albania "enterprises with participation of foreign capital can carry out their economic activity in various sectors of the economy."
