Kansas City Royals Scout For a Potential Buyer

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 08/01/90

The Kansas City Royals baseball team, wallowing in the cellar of the American League West's standings, is on the block. The team said that it has hired a financial adviser, Morgan Guaranty Trust Co. of New York, to pursue bids for the team. Currently, the Royals are jointly owned by Ewing Kauffman, the founder of Marion Laboratories Inc., a unit of Dow Chemical Corp., and Avron B. Fogelman, a Memphis-based developer.

Mr. Fogelman was the subject of a page one story in The Wall Street Journal last summer, when troubles paying his bills led to layoffs at his Fogelman Properties Inc. and speculation that his problems might force him to sell his interest in the team -- or prompt creditors to seize it.

Mr. Fogelman and Mr. Kauffman couldn't be reached for comment.

Kansas City is one of baseball's smallest markets, but, with a payroll of about $24 million, according to Sports Illustrated, the Royals rank as this year's highest-paid baseball team. During the seven years that Mr. Fogelman and Mr. Kauffman have owned the team, the Royals nabbed two American League championships and a World Series.

As part of the purchase of the team, a bidder must be approved by Major League Baseball and assume a recently signed 25-year stadium lease. Moreover, bidders must negotiate a legal thicket involving, among other things, a $34 million loan made to Mr. Fogelman by the Royals.

Mr. Kauffman has the right to match a bid for the team, the Royals said.
