Strikes Expected Today At 2 Canadian Steel Firms

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/01/90

TORONTO -- About 16,000 workers were expected to begin striking this morning at Canada's two largest steelmakers. The walkouts would follow the breakdown of contract talks between the United Steelworkers of America and officials of Stelco Inc. and Algoma Steel Corp., a subsidiary of Dofasco Inc.

The expected shutdowns come with Canada's steel industry under pressure from a slowing domestic economy and increased penetration of the Canadian market by U.S. steel producers.

Leo Gerard, director of District 6 of the United Steelworkers, said, "There's no way we can prevent a strike." He said the union, representing about 10,000 workers at Toronto-based Stelco, has rejected what the company has called its final contract offer, which proposed wage increases of about 2.5% in each year of a three-year pact. The union is seeking a bigger wage increase and better protection against inflation for workers and retirees.

At Algoma, where the union represents about 6,000, monetary issues haven't been broached, Mr. Gerard said. Talks with the Sault Ste. Marie, Ontario, company broke down over language concerning the contracting-out of work.

Both companies said an orderly shutdown of operations has been under way for several days in anticipation of a strike. Fred Telmer, president of Stelco's steel division, also said inventory stockpiles have been transferred to customers.
