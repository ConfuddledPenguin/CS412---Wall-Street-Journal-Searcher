ITT Buy-Back Plan For 10 Million Shares Is Approved by Board

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/01/90

NEW YORK -- ITT Corp. said its board has approved a plan to purchase additional shares of its common and preferred, up to an equivalent of 10 million common shares. The shares will be purchased on the open market. The new authorization is an extension of a program announced in April 1987, allowing the company to purchase up to an equivalent of 10 million common shares. In March 1988, the board authorized the purchase of an additional five million common shares.

In June, ITT said it intended to use the majority of the proceeds from the sale of 7% of Alcatel N.V. to buy back stock. Compagnie Generale d'Electricite of France paid ITT $640 million for the Alcatel shares. Alcatel is a telecommunications manufacturing joint venture between ITT and CGE.

"We continue to believe that ITT shares are an excellent buy in the stock market today," said Rand V. Araskog, chairman and chief executive officer.

To date, the company has bought back about 14.5 million common shares. ITT also purchased 11.7 million common shares in connection with the formation of an employee stock ownership plan established in 1989. Also, the company has redeemed 1.2 million common equivalent shares of convertible preferred stock since 1987. This brings the total number of shares repurchased or redeemed to over 27 million shares.
