Business Brief -- Lincoln Bancorp: Bank Adopts New Names To Avoid Identity Problem

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/01/90

Lincoln Bancorp and its main subsidiary, Lincoln National Bank, said they changed their names to avoid further confusion with Lincoln Savings & Loan Association, an Irvine, Calif.-based thrift that was seized by the government last year. The new names, effective immediately, are California United Bank, N.A. for the unit, and CU Bancorp for the Encino, Calif.-based holding company. The changes were approved by shareholders in June, but were delayed after another financial institution, United California Savings Bank, tried unsuccessfully to prevent the adoption of the new names.

CU Bancorp said that the mistaken-identity problem caused by its old name was worsened by the fact that its president and chief executive officer, John J. Keating, has the same last name as Charles H. Keating Jr., chairman of Phoenix-based American Continental Corp., former parent of Lincoln Savings & Loan.
