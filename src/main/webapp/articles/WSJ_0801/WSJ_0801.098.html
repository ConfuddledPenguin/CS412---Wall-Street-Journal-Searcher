Business Brief -- Boeing Co.: Unit Plans a Tender Offer Of $20 Million for UTL

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/01/90

Boeing Co., Seattle, said a subsidiary plans to make a $20 million cash tender offer to acquire UTL Corp., a Dallas-based designer and maker of electronic warfare systems. Boeing said its $4.75-a-share offer, which will begin by Monday, has been approved by the boards of UTL and Boeing's ARGO Systems unit in Sunnyvale, Calif. The offer is subject to certain conditions, including the tender of at least a majority of UTL's 4.2 million shares outstanding, Boeing said.

In national over-the-counter trading yesterday, UTL shares rose $1.25, to close at $4.625. Boeing shares fell 87.5 cents, to close at $57.75 in New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday.

A UTL spokesman said that Boeing, which currently accounts for about 40% of UTL's backlog, is one of the company's four major customers. UTL supplies systems used for reconnaissance and surveillance by Boeing's Airborne Warning and Control System aircraft, better known as AWACS.

"By including UTL in the Boeing operating structure, we will significantly improve our ability to meet schedule and performance commitments to our national and international military customers," said Dan Pinick, president of Boeing's Boeing Defense & Space Group.
