Who's News: Employers Casualty Adds to Reserves, Posts 2nd-Period Loss

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/01/90

DALLAS -- Employers Casualty Co., citing skyrocketing claims in its general liability and workers' compensation insurance lines, said it added about $90 million to reserves in the second quarter, leading to a net loss of $85.7 million, or $8.49 a share. The insurer also said John F. Stephens, 74 years old, retired as chairman and as a board member. He was succeeded as chairman by Ernest L. Proud Jr., the company's 57-year-old president and chief executive.

In the second quarter last year, the company reported a net loss of $21.6 million, or $2.15 a share. In the latest six months, the net loss widened to $94.8 million, or $9.40 a share, including $100.6 million in reserves, from a net loss of $16.1 million, or $1.60 a share in the year-ago period. Adjusted book value at June 30, 1990, fell to $89.4 million, or $9.18 a share, from $242.6 million, or $24.93 a share, a year earlier, the company said.

The company said the major portion of the reserve increase is for liability losses from policies written many years ago. "Ultimate loss payouts are being heavily impacted by settlements and jury awards that are being made in the highly litigious environment that currently exists in Texas," said Mr. Proud.

Employers said it plans to reduce premiums written to a net annualized volume of $150 million, and is looking for ways to cut expenses. In recent years, net premiums have averaged above $400 million annually.
