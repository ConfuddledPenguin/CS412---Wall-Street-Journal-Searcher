International Brief -- Bridgestone Corp.: Tire Maker's Pretax Profit Rose 13% in 1990 First Half

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/01/90

Bridgestone Corp. reported that unconsolidated pretax profit for the six months ended June 30 rose 13%, to 46.11 billion yen ($311.6 million) from 40.82 billion yen in the year-earlier period. Net income increased 27% to 24.41 billion yen, or 31.71 yen a share, from 19.21 billion yen, or 26.19 yen a share.

Sales at the Tokyo-based tire maker rose 5%, though receipts from North American markets were flat, the company said. Total sales were 352.21 billion yen, compared with 334.56 billion yen.

Sales of tires rose 2% to 267.78 billion yen from 261.44 billion yen, and made up 76% of total sales. Sales in other areas, including industrial rubber, chemicals and sports products, increased 15% to 84.43 billion yen from 73.12 billion yen.

Domestic sales accounted for 68% of the total and rose 6% from a year earlier. Overseas sales rose 5%.

Bridgestone forecast pretax profit for the full year of 97 billion yen, net income of 51 billion yen and sales of 730 billion yen.
