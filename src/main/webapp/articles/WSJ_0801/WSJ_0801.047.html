Who's News: Clinical Technologies Associates to Dismiss Steiner as President

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/01/90

ELMSFORD, N.Y. -- Clinical Technologies Associates Inc. said its board in a 3-2 vote decided to dismiss Solomon S. Steiner as president and chief executive officer. The board named Michael M. Goldberg to the top operating posts and offered Mr. Steiner, 52 years old, who founded the company in 1985, the option of retaining his board seat and running the clinical division. Mr. Steiner rejected the offer, and indicated that he might take legal action.

Clinical Technologies is developing a method by which drugs that can be administered only by injection -- such as insulin for diabetics -- can be taken orally.

"The company has come to the point where it must expand.. .and improve its financing," said Arnold J. Friedhoff, the 66-year-old chairman. "We have substantial new research facilities and ventures. And we felt that (Mr.) Steiner could help by running the clinical division and (Dr.) Goldberg could best serve the company by becoming president and chief executive."

Dr. Goldberg, 31, is founding partner of Montaur Capital Corp., a New York investment banking firm specializing in healthcare companies.

In over-the-counter trading yesterday, Clinical Technologies shares were quoted at $12.625 bid, up 12.5 cents.
