Technology: Ashton-Tate Releases A Revamped Version Of Database Package

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/01/90

TORRANCE, Calif. -- Ashton-Tate Corp. began shipping a new version of its flagship dBASE IV software package, which industry observers said should enable the software maker to reverse a yearlong decline in its revenue and regain lost share in the database market. "This shores things up," said Jeffrey Tartar, editor of Softletter, an industry newsletter.

However, the release of the new dBASE IV, the most widely used database application for personal computers, isn't likely to restore Ashton-Tate to the prominence it enjoyed during the mid-1980s. "For real excitement, Ashton-Tate must get out quality products consistently," said Adam Green, a database consultant in Lexington, Mass. Rejuvenation "isn't a matter of selling a single product."

Shortcomings with the initial version of dBASE IV, introduced nearly two years ago, forced Ashton-Tate to promise delivery of a revamped version. But the revisions proved so difficult to make that last year the company stopped predicting when it would finish the job. Even now the company will only say the product has been "thoroughly tested." Users who have tested it say it is reliable.

"Unlike {the first} dBASE IV, this one is going to work as advertised," said Michael Masterson, president of the Silicon Valley dBASE Users Group. The program is a programmable application that stores and retrieves data according to the user's requirements.

Ashton-Tate plans to give a free copy of the new version to every registered owner of dBASE IV, which lists for $795. The company yesterday also released a version of dBASE to run on minicomputers made by Digital Equipment Corp., which plans to offer the product with its machines.
