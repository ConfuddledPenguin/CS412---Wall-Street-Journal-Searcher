Redland's Stake in Joint Venture

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/01/90

LONDON -- Redland PLC said it will take an initial 20% stake in a European plasterboard joint venture headed by Lafarge Coppee S.A. of France. Lafarge, a French building products manufacturer, and Redland will combine their plasterboard operations and buy out CSR Ltd.'s minority shareholding in Redland Plasterboard PLC.

Lafarge, whose other gypsum-product businesses and majority stake in National Gypsum Co. are excluded from the venture, will pay #39 million ($72 million) and Redland #16 million toward the acquisition of CSR's 49% stake in Redland Plasterboard.

Redland, which under the agreement is entitled to expand its stake in the new venture to 33.4% after five years, said the combination will form Europe's second-largest plasterboard group. BPB Industries PLC, a British gypsum products manufacturer, is currently the continent's largest plasterboard supplier.
