International: Daimler Alters Its Strategy In Seeking Enasa Takeover

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 08/01/90

FRANKFURT -- Daimler-Benz AG said it is now prepared to negotiate the takeover of Spain's state-owned truck maker, Empresa Nacional de Autocamiones S.A., either alone or in partnership with the government. Daimler, which is West Germany's largest industrial group and one of the world's largest producers of cars and trucks, said it has decided to seek an Enasa takeover on its own rather than in partnership with other European truck makers because it hasn't been able to find a suitable partner.

Originally, Daimler had sought to take a 20% stake in Enasa, with the West German engineering and truck group MAN AG acquiring 60%. The plan called for the two companies to manage Enasa jointly. But the arrangement was rejected two weeks ago by the West German Federal Cartel Office on antitrust grounds. MAN then dropped out of the running for Enasa.
