Procter & Gamble Co. Weighs Expanding Plant Near Cincinnati

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/01/90

CINCINNATI -- Procter & Gamble Co. is considering a $200 million expansion of its century-old Ivorydale plant near this city. P&G officials say the soap and detergent plant is one of several midwestern U.S. plants that could be expanded as part of its restructuring.

Local officials expect the expansion following the Cincinnati consumer products company's request for a property tax abatement on $200 million in plant improvements.

P&G yesterday won a 75% property tax abatement for new buildings and machinery at the site. The abatement could save P&G up to $15 million in taxes over 10 years, said David Main, executive director of Hamilton County Development Co., the county's economic development agency.

A spokeswoman for P&G said the abatement makes the Ivorydale site competitive with other locations, but expansion plans aren't official because "there are several projects under consideration."

Other P&G sites that could be expanded include laundry and cleaning products plants in Alexandria, La., and Augusta, Ga., or a peanut-butter plant in Lexington, Ky. P&G could choose to build a plant at another site, according to a company spokeswoman.

It's unclear whether the Ivorydale expansion will include new products or simply shift production from other plants that P&G plans to close.

For instance, P&G has announced plans to close its Port Ivory plant in Staten Island, N.Y. That plant employs 425 people and makes soaps, detergents and cleaners.
