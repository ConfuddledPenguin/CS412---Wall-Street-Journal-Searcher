MCA Will Reverse Some Aspects of Sale Of LJN Toy Unit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/01/90

UNIVERSAL CITY, Calif. -- MCA Inc., whose foibles in the toy business seemed behind it earlier this year with the sale of its toy operation to Acclaim Entertainment Inc., has once again found that the business isn't all child's play. The big entertainment concern said yesterday that it agreed with Acclaim on several measures that, combined, appear to reverse a substantial portion of the earlier sale of MCA's LJN Toys Ltd. unit for an estimated price of about $30 million.

The settlement calls for MCA to take back some assets of LJN, including both receivables and inventory. As well, MCA agreed to pay Acclaim $1 million in cash and up to $2 million of its Acclaim stock, representing a portion of a stake MCA received in April with the sale of LJN.

In addition, MCA agreed to share with Acclaim part of the proceeds from the future sale of MCA's remaining stake in Acclaim.

For its part, Oyster Bay, N.Y.-based Acclaim, one of the largest licensees for Nintendo computer games, agreed to lift restrictions on the 1.2 million shares of Acclaim stock held by MCA, freeing MCA to sell those shares.

Officials of both companies say MCA is expected to return about 180,000 shares of stock to Acclaim, leaving MCA with a little over a million shares.

An MCA official said MCA will sell "substantially all" of its remaining shares in a secondary offering tied to a currently planned offering of two million new shares of Acclaim. Acclaim currently has about 18.7 million shares outstanding, including the shares issued to MCA.

According to Gregory E. Fischbach, chairman and chief executive officer of Acclaim, differences between the two companies arose when two sets of accountants poured over the assets with very different views of their values.

Neither Mr. Fischbach nor Richard Baker, vice president and controller of MCA, would discuss the value or details of the inventory and receivables returned to MCA.
