Business Brief -- Whitehall Corp.: Ex-Suitor Says Acquisition Wouldn't Be `Justifiable'

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/01/90

Cambridge Capital Fund Limited Partnership, a New York investment company, said it doesn't believe it can structure a purchase of Whitehall Corp., a Dallas-based defense contractor, that would be "economically justifiable" and still be acceptable to Whitehall's shareholders. Cambridge earlier this year attempted to acquire Whitehall, proposing to acquire the 90.4% that it didn't own at the time for about $20 a share. But Cambridge lost a proxy battle to place five directors of its choosing on the Whitehall board in April. The investment firm then withdrew its offer to buy the company, but said it would continue to explore alternatives.

In a filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission, Cambridge said it reached its conclusion following the recent loss of business at Whitehall's Aero subsidiary in Florida and the resignation of Whitehall's chief financial officer.

Cambridge said it believes "a substantial redirection" of Whitehall's business strategy is needed to "halt the erosion of value of the stockholders' investment." Officials at Whitehall didn't return phone calls seeking comment.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Whitehall shares closed at $12, down 50 cents.
