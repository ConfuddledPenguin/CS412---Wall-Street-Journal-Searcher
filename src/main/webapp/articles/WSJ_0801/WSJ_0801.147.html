International: France's Framatome Says Holders' Dispute Hinders the Group

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/01/90

PARIS -- Executives at French nuclear power plant builder Framatome said the long-running wrangle between shareholders is seriously hindering the group. But, they said, the dispute should be resolved by early September. Company officials also threw a new element into the debate by calling for Framatome's one-time rival Schneider S.A. to take a 20% stake in the company to counter the unwelcome presence of Compagnie Generale d'Electricite, or CGE.

Framatome has been the focus of a dispute between the state and the private sector since CGE agreed last March to buy construction group Dumez Group's 12% stake in the company, thereby raising its own stake to 52%. State-owned firms and Framatome's employees own the remaining 48% of the group's capital.

France depends heavily on nuclear power, and so CGE's move drew opposition from President Francois Mitterrand and the governing Socialist Party, neither of whom wanted to see such a strategic industry pass out of the public sector. They asked CGE to cut its stake to less than 50%.

Marcel Chabrillac, Framatome's director for nuclear engineering, sharply attacked CGE yesterday. "CGE has never brought us anything. It has just taken its dividends. . . . For some of its shareholders, Framatome is nothing more than a cash cow," he said.

Mr. Chabrillac also said that the uncertainty over the group's future had held up its plans to develop new business, especially in the U.S. and in China.

A CGE official said later that the company was willing to discuss cutting its stake, but he declined to give further details.

A Schneider official said an alliance between the engineering group and Framatome was sound industrial logic because the two companies already worked together on many nuclear plant projects.

But officials at Schneider and Framatome said several tricky details remained to be resolved: Both Schneider and CGE are reluctant to share a role in Framatome with the other; a price has to be set for any Framatome shares that CGE sells. And the government, a key player in the negotiations, is unlikely to smile on Schneider's entry into Framatome.
