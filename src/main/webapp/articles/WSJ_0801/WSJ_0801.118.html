Farm Prices in July Held at June Levels, Rose 2.7% From 1989

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/01/90

WASHINGTON -- Average prices received by U.S. farmers in July were unchanged from June, as higher prices for milk and vegetables offset lower beef and grain prices. Farm prices last month were still 2.7% higher than in July 1989, the Agriculture Department said. Milk prices averaged $14.10 per hundredweight, up 30 cents from June. Commercial vegetable prices rose 20% in July, while fruit prices rose 7.9%.

Beef cattle prices averaged $72.40 per hundredweight, down $2 from June, the department said. Prices for steers and heifers fell $2.10, to $75.40 per hundredweight, while cow prices slipped 80 cents to $53.10. Hog prices averaged $60.10 per hundredweight, unchanged from June, but were $14.20 higher than a year ago. Barrows and gilts rose 30 cents to $61.60 per hundredweight, while sows dropped $4 to $47.

Wheat prices averaged $2.81 a bushel, down 27 cents from June, according to the department. Corn prices slipped two cents to $2.61 in July, while oats fell 12 cents to $1.22 a bushel.
