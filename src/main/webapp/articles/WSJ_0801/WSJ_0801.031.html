Dividend News: Triton Energy to Sell Assets, End Dividend In Plan to Boost Stock

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/01/90

DALLAS -- Triton Energy Corp., hoping to bolster its sagging share price, said it will sell assets, reduce debt, cut overhead and eliminate its current 10-cent quarterly dividend. The company's restructuring is the latest in a series of moves, which include earlier asset sales and a poison pill plan adopted in June, to keep raiders at bay. New Zealand investor Sir Ronald Brierly has accumulated 10.9% of the oil and gas exploration company and has said he might seek control.

Triton said it hopes to raise $150 million by selling aviation operations at nine airports, some of its U.S. oil and gas interests and its securities portfolio. Triton also expects to spin off its seismic equipment subsidiary, Input/Output Inc., in a public stock offering. "The primary focus of the company will be redirected toward . . . oil and gas exploration projects," said Chairman William I. Lee.

The company said it will use the proceeds to reduce long-term debt. One-time charges related to the sales will deepen the losses that Triton expects for its fourth quarter and fiscal year ending May 31.

The company's losses have battered Triton stock over the past year. In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Triton shares closed up 25 cents at $11.875.
