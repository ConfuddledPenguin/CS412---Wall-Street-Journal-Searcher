Business Brief -- Commonwealth Mortgage: Some Operations to be Sold To Affiliate of Bass Group

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/01/90

Commonwealth Mortgage of America L.P. said it made a preliminary agreement to sell its mortgage-servicing operation and $7.2 billion mortgage-servicing portfolio to an investor group affiliated with Fort Worth, Texas, billionaire Robert M. Bass. It wouldn't disclose terms. Included in the sale is a data-processing center owned by Commonwealth Federal Savings Association, an insolvent thrift that owns all the Series I preferred units of limited partner interests in Commonwealth Mortgage. The thrift is controlled by the Resolution Trust Corp., a government agency.

Buyers include Castine Partners Ltd., an affiliate of Robert M. Bass Group, and Barron Financial Group, a Tustin, Calif. mortgage bank.

The proposed sale, part of a planned liquidation of Commonwealth Mortgage, would strip the company of "substantially all remaining assets," a spokesman said. Proceeds will be used to reduce debt, the company said, adding that holders of the Class A units are unlikely to receive any liquidating distribution.

Commonwealth Mortgage said its mortgage-servicing operation, which services loans nationwide, and its staff of about 450 will continue to be based in Houston.
