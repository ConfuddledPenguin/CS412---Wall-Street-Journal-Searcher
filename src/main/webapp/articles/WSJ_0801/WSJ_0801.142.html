Southern Co. Unit To Sell Power Plant For $810 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/01/90

ATLANTA -- Georgia Power Co. said it agreed in principle to sell one of its coal-fired power plants in a transaction valued at about $810 million over four years. The company, a subsidiary of Southern Co., said it plans to sell unit No. 4 of its Scherer electric generating plant in Forysth, Ga., to Florida Power & Light Co. and Jacksonville Electric Authority. The sale will be made in stages during the years 1991 through 1995. Florida Power & Light, a unit of FPL Group Inc. in North Palm Beach, Fla., would eventually own 76% of the 846-megawatt unit; Jacksonville Electric Authority would own the remainder.

Georgia Power said the first part of the sale, totaling about $300 million, is expected to be completed in the first six months of 1991. The entire transaction is subject to negotiation of final agreements and regulatory approvals.

James L. Broadhead, chairman and chief executive officer of Florida Power & Light, said the move will fulfill a significant portion of the utility's need for additional generating capacity.

Georgia Power said it hasn't determined how it will use proceeds from the proposed sales, but added that one alternative is a special redemption at par of outstanding first mortgage bonds.

Unit No. 4 of the Scherer facility was completed in 1989. Units Nos. 1, 2 and 3 are owned jointly by Georgia Power, Oglethorpe Power Corp., the Municipal Electric Authority of Georgia, Gulf Power Co. and the city of Dalton, Ga.
