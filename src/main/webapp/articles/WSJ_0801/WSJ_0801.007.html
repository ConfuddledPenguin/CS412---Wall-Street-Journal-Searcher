Business Brief -- UnionFed Financial Corp.: Quarterly Loss is Posted; Loan-Loss Provision Cited

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/01/90

UnionFed Financial Corp. said a sharply higher, $9.4 million provision for loan losses resulted in a June 30 fiscal fourth-quarter loss of $1.7 million for the Los Angeles-based thrift holding company. In the year-earlier quarter, UnionFed had an $800,000 provision for loan losses and a $5.6 million profit for the period. The latest period's loss equaled 23 cents a share, compared with net income of $1.10 a share a year earlier.

UnionFed said its sharply higher addition to reserves resulted from deterioration in commerical real estate markets and sounded a back-to-basics theme. "The bank and its subsidiaries intend to concentrate new lending and development activities primarily on residential properties located in California," Roger L. Kringen, chairman and chief executive officer, said.
