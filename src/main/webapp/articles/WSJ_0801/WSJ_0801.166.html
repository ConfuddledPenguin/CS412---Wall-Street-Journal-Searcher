Freeport-McMoran To Acquire All Shares Of Its Oil-Gas Unit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/01/90

NEW ORLEANS -- Freeport-McMoRan Inc. said it will acquire all the shares of Freeport-McMoRan Oil & Gas Co. that it doesn't already own, citing lackluster investor interest in the unit. In March, the diversified energy concern converted an oil and gas limited partnership into Freeport-McMoRan Oil & Gas, a corporation, in hopes of broadening the investment appeal of the operation and avoiding what it called "onerous" tax reporting requirements for partnerships.

Yesterday, Freeport-McMoRan said its "expectation that Freeport-McMoRan Oil & Gas would be a more attractive investment vehicle" than its predecessor "has not been realized in the marketplace." It said it will issue between 0.270 and 0.329 share of its common stock for each share of the oil and gas company not already held, according to a formula it has devised. The parent owns 81.5% of the unit.

A Freeport-McMoRan spokesman said the exact number of additional shares to be issued hasn't been determined, although it is expected to be between 6.2 million and seven million. Freeport-McMoRan had about 54 million shares outstanding at June 30, and Freeport-McMoRan Oil & Gas about 23 million.

Freeport-McMoRan stock closed yesterday in New York Stock Exchange composite trading at 35.125 a share, down 25 cents. Freeport-McMoRan Oil & Gas stock, also traded on the New York exchange, soared two dollars a share to ten dollars a share.

Separately, Freeport-McMoRan said it would extend payment of a special 25-cents-per-share cash dividend for the next four quarters, in addition to its regular quarterly dividend. The company has been paying the special dividend since January 1989. Its regular dividend is 37.5 cents a share.
