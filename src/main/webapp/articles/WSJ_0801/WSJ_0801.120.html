Business Brief -- Usinor-Sacilor: J&L Credit Rating Change To Affect Long-Term Debt

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/01/90

Moody's Investors Service Inc., New York, said it upgraded the ratings on J&L Specialty Products Corp.'s senior subordinated notes to double B-3 from single B-2. The credit rating change affects about $200 million of long-term debt. The ratings agency said it based its upgrade on the financial strength of J&L's new parent, French-based Usinor-Sacilor, which Moody's views as investment grade.

Moody's said it believes that J&L, a stainless-steel maker based in Pittsburgh, should benefit from Usinor-Sacilor's commitment to augment investments in its American operations, the parent's better access to financial markets, and its expertise in stainless-steel production.

Usinor-Sacilor, headquartered in Paris, is one of the world's largest steel makers.
