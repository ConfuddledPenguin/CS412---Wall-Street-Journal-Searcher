Slower Growth Is Forecast For Industrialized Nations

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/01/90

NEW YORK -- Slower growth looms for the world's major industrialized countries, judging from the countries' leading indexes, the Conference Board said. The board's latest International Economic Scorecard shows the composite index of leading indicators for the 11 major countries as flat, indicating slower growth for the rest of the year. The board said that, although none of the world's three major regions looks encouraging economically, some individual countries show strength.

In Europe, West Germany's index rose 5%; in the Pacific region, South Korea's index rose 6%; in North America, the U.S. index is growing at a 1% rate.

The growth rate for the 10 major industrialized countries outside the U.S. was 5% a year ago but has dropped steadily to 2% the past four months.

"An important implication of the general pattern is that the outlook for continued growth in U.S. exports has become less sanguine," said Edgar R. Fiedler, economic counselor for the Conference Board.

Among other indexes, Japan's showed a 2% growth rate, as did France's. The indexes for Britain and Canada each showed a 1% decline.

The Conference Board is a nonprofit business-research organization.
