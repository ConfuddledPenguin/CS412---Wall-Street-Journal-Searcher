Times Mirror Files $7.1 Million Lawsuit Against an Insurer

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/01/90

LOS ANGELES -- Times Mirror Co. has sued Federal Insurance Co. for allegedly failing to reimburse the media concern for a $7.1 million settlement of a class-action suit over a company pension plan. According to a complaint filed in federal court here, Warren, N.J.-based Federal issued a "fiduciary liability" policy to Times Mirror in September 1987, but has asserted that the policy does not cover a dispute over the "excess assets" of a Dallas Times Herald pension plan.

Federal Insurance officials in Warren and in New York City couldn't be reached for comment late yesterday.

The class-action lawsuit against Times Mirror was filed in Texas in December 1988. It charged Times Mirror with breaching its fiduciary duty to employees by attempting to terminate a pension plan and retaining about $14 million of excess assets.

A settlement increasing benefits to the lawsuit class members by about $7.1 million was approved in June last year.

According to the Times Mirror complaint, Federal argued that Times Mirror was not acting in a fiduciary capacity in regard to the pension plan, the class suit did not allege a "wrongful act" as defined in the insurance policy, and the policy does not cover benefits becoming due in the future such as pension benefits.

Federal has, however, paid more than $750,000 in legal and actuarial costs arising out of the class action. The Times Mirror suit seeks a declaration of rights plus $7.1 million.
