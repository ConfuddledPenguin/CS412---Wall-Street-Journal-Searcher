Business Brief -- Nordstrom Inc.: Company to Settle Charges Of Violating Labor Laws

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/20/90

Nordstrom Inc. said it agreed to settle charges of violating several federal labor laws in its handling of a dispute with the United Food & Commercial Workers Union. In the tentative agreement with the National Labor Relations Board, Nordstrom doesn't admit guilt, nor is it required to pay a fine. But the retailer promised it wouldn't allow certain alleged practices that resulted in charges the labor board found to have potential merit. Those include discrimination against employees taking part in union activities and promising workers additional benefits if the unions are decertified.

The violations stemmed from a yearlong dispute with the United Food & Commercial Workers locals 1001 and 367. Contract talks with the two locals, whose pacts covering Seattle-area salespeople expired last year, broke down after the company proposed making union membership optional.

A spokesman for the unions declined to comment on the proposed settlement.
