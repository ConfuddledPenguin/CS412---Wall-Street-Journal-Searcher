Business Brief -- HAL Inc.: Hawaiian Airlines' Parent Shows $26.9 Million Loss

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/20/90

HAL Inc., the parent of Hawaiian Airlines, reported that its second-quarter net loss grew more than fivefold to $26.9 million, or $12.42 a share, because of heavy investments and soft market conditions. In last year's second quarter, the Honolulu-based company posted a net loss of $4.3 million, or $2.20 a share. The year-ago loss reflects an $8.4 million gain on the sale of property and equipment. Revenue slid to $79.6 million from $84.2 million a year ago.

HAL said heavy investments in aircraft improved its on-time performance, and were expected to "slow our turnaround." But unexpectedly soft market conditions, coupled with the suspension of its military charters "made this quarter especially rough," the company added.

For the six months, HAL's loss more than tripled to $43 million, or $19.89 a share, from $13 million, or $6.68 a share, in last year's period. The year-ago loss reflects a gain on sale of property and equipment of $8.4 million. Revenue declined to $160.7 million from nearly $168 million a year ago.
