Justice Agency Gives Nod To Offshore Pipelines Plan

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/20/90

WASHINGTON -- The Justice Department filed a consent decree that would allow Offshore Pipelines Inc. to complete its proposed acquisition of marine-construction vessels and other assets from Brown & Root Inc., a unit of Halliburton Co., for about $80 million. The decree would settle an antitrust suit also filed Friday that challenged the acquisition. The agreement requires Offshore Pipelines, based in Houston, to divest itself of two Brown & Root barges used for laying or burying oil and gas pipes offshore.

James Rill, head of the Justice Department's antitrust division, said that without the divestiture, the proposed acquisition would be anti-competitive in the U.S. portion of the Gulf of Mexico.

Offshore Pipelines' sale of the barges, Mr. Rill said, "will ensure that the acquisition of Brown & Root's vessels by OPI will not raise the cost of offshore oil and gas exploration and production" in the gulf, and will create more competition in that market.

Dallas-based Halliburton said it will realize a pretax gain of about $33 million for the sale.

The assets include 23 derrick, pipelay and other marine construction vessels, as well as interest in certain marine construction support facilities in the Far East and the U.S. The sale of a marine construction facility in the Middle East to Offshore Pipelines is expected to be completed later this year.
