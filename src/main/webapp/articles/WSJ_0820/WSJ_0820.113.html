Business Brief -- Dart Drug Stores Inc.: Maryland Discount Retailer To Liquidate the Company

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/20/90

Dart Drug Stores Inc. said it will liquidate the company because it has been unable to develop a plan of reorganization acceptable to creditors. The Landover, Md., discount retailer, which owes about $130 million, said it plans to close all 65 Fantle's Drugstores in the Washington area by early November.

Dart Drug filed for protection from creditors under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code about a year ago. The company said it plans to sell its inventory and store leases as soon as it has authorization from the bankruptcy court, but declined to estimate proceeds.

The company said it owes about $24 million to its major secured creditor Mellon Bank Corp. in Pittsburgh. Trustees for the largest groups of unsecured bondholders are Continental Stock Transfer & Trust Co., with debt of about $101 million, and Signet Trust Co., with about $29 million.

In April 1989, Dart Drug changed the name of its stores to Fantle's and tried a new store format in an effort to boost sales and profits. Sheldon W. Fantle, chairman and chief executive officer, said strong competition and a sluggish economy prevented the company from fully implementing its marketing strategy.
