Business Brief -- FJW Industries Inc.: Two Ex-Officials of Firm Indicted on Fraud Charges

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/20/90

A federal grand jury indicted two former officials of closely held FJW Industries Inc. on charges that they tried to defraud the government on a contract to supply equipment for U.S. Army tanks. FJW, based in Elgin, Ill, won a total $1.2 million in contracts to provide the Army with night-vision periscopes for its M-60 battle tank, the indictment says. According to the indictment, the company bought used periscopes from an Israeli company, then sent them to Singapore, where an FJW subsidiary disassembled the periscopes; the parts were then shipped to FJW in Illinois, and assembled into periscopes FJW sought to pass off as new equipment.

The indictment says the defendants -- Barry Warzak, vice president, operations, and George Bravo, vice president, finance -- repeatedly provided false documents certifying that the contract was being properly fulfilled.

FJW wasn't named in the indictment. Messrs. Warzak and Bravo couldn't be reached for comment.
