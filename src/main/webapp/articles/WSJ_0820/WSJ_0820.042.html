Mortgage Rates Dropped Last Week, Survey Says

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/20/90

BUTLER, N.J. -- Mortgage rates dipped last week, according to a national survey of 2,000 lending institutions. Thirty-year, fixed-rate mortgages declined to an average 10.14% in the week ended Friday, from 10.17% the previous week. That compares with an average of 10.16% one month ago, and 10.2% last year, said Paul Havemann, vice president of HSH Associates.

Adjustable-rate mortgages "have fared pretty well too," declining to an average 8.19% last week from 8.21% the previous week, Mr. Havemann said. That is well below the average 8.37% the previous month and 8.63% a year earlier.
