Integrated Resources, Krupp Fail to Reach Pact on Partnerships

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/20/90

NEW YORK -- Integrated Resources Inc.'s agreement to sell its interests in four investment partnerships to Krupp Cos. for as much as $45 million in cash and notes collapsed. The failure to reach a final agreement appears to remove a big hurdle to CRI Inc.'s proposed hostile tender offer for the partnerships, known as American Insured Mortgage Investors. CRI is a real estate investment firm based in Rockville, Md.

Integrated is seeking to sell its general partnership interest, which was valued at about $25 million in the Krupp agreement, as well as its right to manage the partnerships. Integrated estimates it receives management fees of about $8 million a year, before costs. If CRI's bid succeeds, Integrated would lose the right to the management fees.

The sale of Integrated's interests in the AIM partnerships, which was being conducted through a court-approved auction, is part of Integrated's effort to raise cash to satisfy claims of creditors, who are owed a total of about $1.8 billion.

Although substantive issues between Integrated and Krupp had been resolved, the deal dissolved, Krupp said, when Integrated rejected a provision permitting Krupp's lender to reassess its commitment if further litigation arose before closing.

Stephen D. Weinroth, Integrated chairman, said he wasn't sure how Integrated would proceed. "We have several avenues to pursue," he said, including negotiating with others who had expressed interest, and offering investors possible changes in the management contract that would make it more difficult to remove the manager. Mr. Weinroth wouldn't rule out negotiating with CRI.

Laurence Gerber, president of Krupp, a Boston real estate investment manager, said, "The failure to consummate this deal will result in investors being the biggest losers." He said it was unlikely another agreement with Integrated would be reached.

CRI is proposing to gain control of the partnerships by approaching investors directly and offering to exchange units in two ventures it manages for the AIM units. A CRI spokesman said the firm filed lawsuits in state courts in Delaware and California last week to obtain a list of AIM investors.

The CRI spokesman said CRI would proceed with its proposed offer, which it filed with the Securities and Exchange Commission in June, as soon as it receives the lists and SEC clearance. CRI also has filed lawsuits to force Integrated to reverse changes in its management contracts on the partnerships.
