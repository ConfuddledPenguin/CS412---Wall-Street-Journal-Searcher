Education Aid Foundation Says Chief Will Step Down

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 08/20/90

The chairman of the Higher Education Assistance Foundation has agreed to resign as part of the federal government's resolution of the troubled guarantee agency's financial woes, a HEAF spokesman said. The spokesman for the private nonprofit corporation based in Overland Park, Kan., said that Richard Hawk has accepted a request from the U.S. Department of Education that he step down.

The Education Department has been working on a rescue plan for HEAF, guarantor of $9.3 billion in student loans, since announcing last month that HEAF was in serious financial trouble.

Although the government is reviewing several proposed plans for other companies to acquire HEAF, it hasn't yet decided which plan to accept.

David Westbrook, the HEAF spokesman, said that HEAF has been reassured by the government that any resolution will not lead to significant layoffs of HEAF's 800 employees. "Mr. Hawk's position is that if the (Education Department) needs someone to be accountable for this problem he's willing to be that person," Mr. Westbrook said.

Student Loan Marketing Association, a publicly owned company, and United Student Aid Funds, a nonprofit loan guarantor, have each submitted merger rescue plans to the government as proposed solutions to HEAF's problem.
