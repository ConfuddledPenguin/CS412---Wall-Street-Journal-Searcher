Japan Said to Face Jump In Price of Oil From Iran

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/20/90

TOKYO -- Iran has secured an unusually large price increase from Japanese customers on long-term contracts for crude oil to be loaded between September and December, industry sources said. The increases, which by previous agreement will apply to other Asian buyers of Iranian crude, change the value of Iranian oil so much that other Persian Gulf producers are expected to raise their prices as well.

"The Saudis will have to follow," said one trader, noting that oil sold by Saudi Arabia and other producers, which like Iranian oil is priced against Omani or Dubai crudes, will otherwise seem inexpensive.

Beginning in September, the price of Iranian Heavy, which makes up the bulk of Japan's crude supplies from Iran, will cost a monthly average price equivalent to the price of Dubai Fateh crude plus 25 cents a barrel. That is an increase of $1.40 a barrel from the price Iran obtained from Japan in July and August.

Iranian Light crude will cost a monthly average price equivalent to the price of Omani crude plus 35 cents a barrel, an increase of 60 cents a barrel over the current price.

The actual cost of the crude is even greater than the increases suggest because of jumps in the prices of Omani and Dubai crudes since the latest Middle East crisis began Aug. 2.

Dubai crude has gained $7.40 a barrel in the past month. If that price holds in September -- and many say it will go even higher -- Japan will pay $8.80 a barrel more for Iranian crude than it did in August.
