Chrysler Offers Buy-Out To Blue-Collar Workers

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/20/90

HIGHLAND PARK, Mich. -- Chrysler Corp. offered a lump-sum buy-out of as much as $65,000 to its blue-collar workers willing to leave the company. The No. 3 U.S. auto maker is in the midst of a $1.5 billion cost-cutting program, which includes early retirement for some white-collar workers. Chrysler also is entering contract talks with the United Auto Workers union, in which job security will be an issue.

The offer to about 60,000 of Chrysler UAW workers is similar to programs used at Ford Motor Co. and General Motors Corp. UAW contracts with the auto makers specifically provide for such "early termination" payments.

Chrysler officials said they expect about 600 workers to take the offer before the Aug. 24 deadline. The company said about 450 already have signed up. Ford said only five of its workers have taken the payments this year. The payments range from $12,000 for workers with at least one year of service to $65,000 for more experienced workers.
