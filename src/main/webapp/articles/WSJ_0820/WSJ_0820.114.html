Business Brief -- Geo. A. Hormel & Co.: Net Income Declined 12% In the Fiscal Third Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/20/90

Geo. A. Hormel & Co. said its fiscal third quarter net income dropped 12% because it had to pay high prices for hogs. The Austin, Minn.-based meat processor said profit was $12.6 million, or 16 cents a share, for the quarter ended July 28, compared with $14.3 million, or 18 cents a share, a year earlier. The year-earlier per-share figure is adjusted to reflect a 2-for-1 stock split paid Jan. 31.

Sales rose 16% to $654.7 million from $564.0 million.

Profit margins were hurt by the jump in the price of hogs, which the company buys to make meat products. Although the price of pork products jumped by some measures to a record level in supermarkets earlier this summer, Hormel said it still wasn't able to immediately pass along all of its increased costs to customers.

The price of fattened hogs was high earlier this year because of unusually small herds. Hormel said that the supply of hogs is gradually increasing and that its raw material costs are moderating.
