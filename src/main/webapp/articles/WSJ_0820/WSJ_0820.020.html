Canadian Consumer Prices

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/20/90

OTTAWA -- Canada's consumer price index rose a seasonally adjusted 0.4% in July from June, Statistics Canada, a federal agency, said. The increase was the same rate as that recorded in June. The July boost was mainly attributed to increases in transportation and food prices.

Separately, Statistics Canada reported that Canada's merchandise trade surplus widened to 1.73 billion Canadian dollars (US$1.51 billion) in June, partly because of a sharp drop in imports. In May the surplus totaled C$630 million.
