Moody's Is Considering Downgrading Sears's Debt

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/20/90

NEW YORK -- Moody's Investors Service Inc. said it is considering downgrading its ratings on $6.9 billion in Sears, Roebuck & Co. debt because of the company's "ongoing difficulty" improving its retailing returns. Moody's also cited its concern about the company's future performance. Sears has seen its debt ratings decline in the past year and a half as both Moody's and Standard & Poor's Corp. have raised questions about whether it can turn around its $31 billion-a-year retail business. In Chicago, a spokesman for the retailing and financial services concern declined to comment.

Any downgrade wouldn't add much, if any, to Sears's borrowing costs because its debt ratings remain high and Sears has vast borrowing power. But the agency's action adds to the skeptical chorus in the investment community about Sears's chances for a turnaround.

Underscoring the point, Richard Mercier, a Moody's assistant vice president, said "We're concerned that the merchandise group has not turned around as quickly as anticipated in early 1989." That's when Moody's last downgraded Sears securities.

Moody's said its review will focus on Sears's "ability and likelihood of reversing the decline in {its} market share," the company's options to reduce costs and streamline its distribution system and "possible restructuring actions."
