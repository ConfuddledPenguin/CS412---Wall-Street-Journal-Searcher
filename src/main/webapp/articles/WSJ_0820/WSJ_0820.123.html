First Executive Wins Round in Bid to Bar Release of Study

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/20/90

First Executive Corp. won a victory for the moment in its effort to keep secret the details of consulting work that the Los Angeles insurance holding company has said affirms its financial strength. The ruling by the New York State Supreme Court, Appellate Division, bars the New York State Department of Insurance from releasing certain actuarial reports until a full appeal can be heard -- likely sometime in November -- said a lawyer for Joseph Belth, the publisher of a newsletter and a frequent critic of First Executive. Mr. Belth is seeking the reports under New York state freedom of information law.

First Executive has said that it will release the reports, together with certain updated information, by the end of this month. However, the company has at the same time opposed Mr. Belth's efforts to gain access to copies of the reports that are on file with New York insurance regulators. Friday's court ruling makes it unclear whether First Executive will hold to its timetable, announced in court earlier this month, or whether it will continue to oppose public dissemination of the reports.

The company at first opposed all disclosure of the reports. It later said it needed time to prepare what it has called the updated information to accompany the documents, which were prepared earlier this year by Milliman & Robertson, consulting actuaries, at the request of California regulators but paid for by First Executive. The studies, summaries of which have been made public, subject the company's troubled insurance units to various hypothetical adverse financial scenarios.

First Executive has told a variety of audiences, ranging from clients to a congressional committee that the reports support its assertions of sufficient financial strength. A company spokesman Friday failed to respond to a request for comment on the New York court decision.
