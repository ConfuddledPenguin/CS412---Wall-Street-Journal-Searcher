OIL PRICES MAY LEVEL OFF in the coming week following Saudi Arabia's pledge to boost output as much as two million barrels a day, making up about half the amount lost from the Iraq-Kuwait embargo. The Saudis plan to take the action with or without OPEC's approval. Some other oil exporters, including Venezuela and the United Arab Emirates, also are expected to increase output. Last week, benchmark U.S. crude topped $28 a barrel, up about $8 since the Iraqi invasion of Kuwait Aug. 2. ---

Interest rates are expected to continue climbing around the world, which could spell more trouble for the U.S. bond market. Bonds were mixed Friday, while stocks tumbled on growing Mideast tensions. The dollar hit a new low against the mark.

Junk bond investors are running for cover again amid fears of a recession. But the sell-off this time is focused on issues that have the most to lose in an economic downturn.

---

The U.S. trade deficit shrank to a seven-year low of $5.07 billion in June, helped by lower oil prices and record exports. But the improvement isn't expected to continue because of the recent surge in petroleum costs.

---

Far West Financial said thrift regulators rejected its plan to boost capital, and they imposed operating restrictions on the S&L concern. Far West increased loss reserves by $16.9 million for the second quarter.

---

Federated and Allied Stores asked a bankruptcy court for a five-month extension to file a reorganization plan. The request by the Campeau retail units will be heard next month.

---

PaineWebber's chief of takeover stock trading, William Kaye, quit after the firm moved to cut his authority and change his compensation.

---

A decision on Trump's $65 million bank bailout was delayed until tomorrow by New Jersey casino regulators. The authorities approved Merv Griffin's plan to have Resorts International emerge from Chapter 11.

---

Times Publishing rid itself of a troublesome shareholder. The parent of the St. Petersburg Times agreed to buy back the 40% owned by a group including billionaire Robert Bass.

---

Magazines are expected to ask advertisers for hefty 1991 rate increases of 4% to 8% despite a continued slide in circulation.

---

U.S. beer shipments rose 1.8% in the first half after nearly a decade of stagnating sales, a trade group says.

---

AMR's regional airline has nearly completed negotiations on an aircraft order that could total as much as $800 million, people in the industry say.
