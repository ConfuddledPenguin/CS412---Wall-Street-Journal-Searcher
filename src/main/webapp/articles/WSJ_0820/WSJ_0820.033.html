Agency Says Taiwan Sweater Sales Unfair

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/20/90

WASHINGTON -- The Commerce Department ruled that manufacturers in Taiwan have exported sweaters to the U.S. at prices about 21.4% below "fair value." If the U.S. International Trade Commission concludes later that the imports have injured domestic manufacturers of man-made fiber sweaters, the government will order extra duties under the Anti-Dumping Act to offset the price margins.

The ITC is expected to issue its final decision in the next few weeks on whether anti-dumping duties are required on sweater imports from South Korea and Hong Kong, as well as from Taiwan.

According to the ITC, imports of man-made fiber sweaters from all three Asian suppliers totaled nearly $1 billion last year.
