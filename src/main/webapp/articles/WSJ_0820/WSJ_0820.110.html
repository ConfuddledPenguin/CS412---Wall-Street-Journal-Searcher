Business Brief -- Puget Sound Power & Light Co.: Transmission Line Project Set With Federal Agency

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/20/90

Bonneville Power Administration, a federal agency that sells power wholesale in the Northwest, and Puget Sound Power & Light Co., a Bellevue, Wash., utility, said they agreed to construct various transmission lines north of Seattle at a joint cost of $21 million. A spokeswoman for Bonneville Power, based in Portland, Ore., said the transmission lines will be in the fast-growing areas of Whatcom and Skagit counties, and will provide about 850 more megawatts of power and 21 miles of transmission lines to the two counties.

Under the agreement, Bonneville will rebuild one of its transmission lines and update equipment at some of its power substations. Puget Sound Power & Light will build two lines branching off of the rebuilt Bonneville power line.

The Bonneville spokeswoman said each company will receive half of the power from the lines, or 425 megawatts each. The lines are expected to increase both companies' ability to draw on Canadian power supplies. The agreement requires an environmental impact report and U.S. Energy Department approval.
