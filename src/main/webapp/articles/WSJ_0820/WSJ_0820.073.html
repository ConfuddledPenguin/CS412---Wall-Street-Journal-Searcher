Arco, With Demand High, May Raise Gasoline Prices

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/20/90

LOS ANGELES -- Atlantic Richfield Co. said it is having trouble meeting demand at the gasoline pump, and the oil company appears to be preparing its customers for a potential increase in prices. Arco, which froze its gas prices Aug. 8 in the wake of President Bush's request that oil companies act responsibly in pricing, said more than 270 of its stations have run out of regular unleaded gasoline at some pumps during the past week and a half. On average, Arco has been able to replenish supplies in about 2 1/2 hours.

"We want to hold on as long as we can to comply with the president's request" on pricing, an Arco spokesman said. Arco said last week that its freeze will remain in effect, but that it will review pricing daily.

Arco's regular unleaded gasoline is priced between four and 12 cents below the competition's, and averages about $1.16 a gallon in Los Angeles. As a result, sales volume at the company's 1,700 stations, situated in five Western states, has jumped about 9% since the price freeze. "As we see volumes increase, we see more runouts," the spokesman said. "And our inventories are tight."

Arco gets most of its crude oil from its fields in Alaska and refines it in Washington state and Southern California. But the company has purchased gasoline on the spot market -- 100,000 barrels so far -- because of the increased demand and a fire last week at Arco's Carson, Calif., refinery that temporarily cut gasoline production by about 15,000 barrels a day. Production at that refinery was expected to be back to normal today.
