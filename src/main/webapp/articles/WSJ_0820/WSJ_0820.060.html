U.S. Sues Keating to Gain Accounting of His Finances

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/20/90

WASHINGTON -- The Justice Department sued Charles Keating, the chairman of the former parent of the defunct Lincoln Savings & Loan Association, to force him to provide an accounting of his finances. The suit, filed in U.S. District Court in Los Angeles on behalf of the Office of Thrift Supervision, is designed to enforce an order issued by the office against Mr. Keating and officers and directors of the failed S&L. The suit seeks to require Mr. Keating to refrain from transferring any assets out of the country and to give the office 48 hours notice before making any transaction involving assets greater than $5,000.

The Justice Department said it filed the suit after discussions between Mr. Keating and the government failed to produce an agreement on Mr. Keating's compliance with the order.
