EXXON Corp. (New York) -- Standard & Poor's Corp. said it reaffirmed Exxon's long-term debt rating despite a recent announcement that the company had challenged in tax court an Internal Revenue Service tax assessment of $6.5 billion. The long-term debt rating of Exxon and its related entities was affirmed at triple-A and commercial paper at single-A-1-plus, S&P said. S&P said given the oil company's "superior financial strength," the assessment doesn't affect Exxon's credit quality.
