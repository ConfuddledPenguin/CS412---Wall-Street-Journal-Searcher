Japan Machine Orders Slowed to 4.8% Rise In June, Agency Says

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 08/20/90

TOKYO -- Annual growth in orders from Japan's machinery industry slowed to 4.8% in June, the Economic Planning Agency said. While still healthy, growth was down from double-digit rates seen in four of the previous five months. Machinery orders increased 13% in May.

Despite weakness in the private sector, an official said the agency expects growth to accelerate. Private-sector orders should rebound as business investment is strong, she said.

The agency said orders to machinery makers totaled 1.933 trillion yen ($13.12 billion) in June. On a seasonally adjusted basis, orders totaled 1.859 trillion yen, down 1.4% from May.

Private capital investment in such big-ticket items as machinery is one of two primary factors in Japan's current economic boom. Together with high personal consumption, capital investment has kept the nation's economy growing for 44 consecutive months, the second-longest expansion since the World War II.

Orders from the private sector fell to 1.09 trillion yen, down 13% from year-earlier levels. On a seasonally adjusted basis, orders totaled 967.88 billion yen, down 14%. June was the second consecutive month in which private-sector orders fell; in May, private-sector orders dropped 5.5%.

The official pointed to strong growth in private-sector orders in April and preceding months as evidence orders will rebound.

Within the private sector, orders from shipbuilders increased 29% in June, while orders from the commercial-fishing industry rose 11%.

Orders from the paper industry, meanwhile, plunged 55% and orders from steel makers fell 28% in June.

Public-sector machinery orders increased 4.2% in June to 153.97 billion yen. On a seasonally adjusted basis, public-sector orders fell 1.6% to 217.15 billion yen.

Overseas orders rose 71% to 545.98 billion yen. On a seasonally adjusted basis, they rose 18% to 536.25 billion yen.
