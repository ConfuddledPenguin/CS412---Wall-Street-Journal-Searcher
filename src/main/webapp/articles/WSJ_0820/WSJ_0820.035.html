U.S. Imports of Steel Were Stable in June

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/20/90

WASHINGTON -- U.S. imports of steel-mill products in June were virtually the same as in May, but fell 5.4% from the year-earlier month, according to an American Iron and Institute compilation of Commerce Department figures. Imports in June totaled 1,466,413 tons, compared with 1,455,803 tons in May and 1,549,428 tons in June 1989, the trade group said. For the first six months of 1990, imports fell 9.2% to 7,896,842 tons.

Steel exports fell to 317,750 tons in June from 351,964 tons in May and 403,278 tons in the year-earlier month.

Steel imports for the first nine months of the federal government's Voluntary Restraint Agreement accounted for 17% of total steel consumption, below the 19.1% target, according to the American Institute for International Steel Inc., a trade group.
