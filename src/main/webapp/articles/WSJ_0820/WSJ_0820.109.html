Business Brief -- FundAmerica Inc.: Discount Buying Club Files For Chapter 11 Protection

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/20/90

FundAmerica Inc., a discount buying club under investigation by Florida authorities, filed for protection from creditors under Chapter 11 of the federal bankruptcy laws. The company, which sells $100 annual memberships entitling members to rebates on vacations, credit-card purchases, long-distance telephone calls and purchases of consumer goods, listed assets of $17,457,000 and liabilities of $14,446,000.

FundAmerica Inc. is owned by Fund America Holdings Ltd., a closely held British Columbia corporation.

Irvine, Calif.-based FundAmerica Inc. filed for bankruptcy-law protection in U.S. Bankruptcy Court in Santa Ana, Calif. Florida authorities had filed a cease-and-desist order against the company, charging that it is an elaborate pyramid scheme. FundAmerica's founder had resigned in the wake of his arrest last month on charges of organized fraud and conducting an illegal lottery.
