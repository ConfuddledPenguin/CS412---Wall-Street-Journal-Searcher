International: West Germany Arrests 7 Over Iraq Poison-Gas Plant

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 08/20/90

WEST BERLIN -- Seven people were arrested in connection with the alleged delivery of a poison-gas plant to Iraq, a West German prosecutor said. Prosecutor Friedrich Hoffmann in Darmstadt said Friday that among those arrested was a man he identified only as "al-Kadhi," an Iraqi specialist employed by the West German foreign intelligence service.

Mr. Hoffmann said the arrests, which came after several years of investigation, took so long because evidence was needed to prove that the plant allegedly delivered to Iraq was constructed so that it could produce poison gas.

He said among those arrested were employees of Pilot Plant in Dreieich, a Darmstadt suburb south of Frankfurt; workers in the export section of Preussag AG in Hanover, and from Walter Engineering Trading in Hamburg. Company officials couldn't immediately be reached for comment.

Mr. Hoffmann emphasized that the accusations of illegal shipments to Iraq were directed at individuals and not at the companies he named.

Separately, the news weekly Der Spiegel reported over the weekend that the East German National People's Army helped Iraq gear up for chemical warfare -- by directing construction of a training ground in Iraq where the weapons could be tested.
