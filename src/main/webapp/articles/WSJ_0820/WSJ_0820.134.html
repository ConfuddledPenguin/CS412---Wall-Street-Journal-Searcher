Air Zimbabwe Finds Cracks in Boeing 707s

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 08/20/90

HARARE, Zimbabwe -- The state airline withdrew three Boeing Co. 707 airliners from service after cracks were found in the wing spars, officials said. The planes are expected to be grounded for several weeks for repairs and tests, a spokesman for Air Zimbabwe said.

A fourth Boeing 707 was given a clean bill of health and is flying normal routes, he said.

The spokesman said the four planes were checked after Boeing, Seattle, warned operators of possible wing cracks, particularly in ageing 707s.

The state airline acquired its 707s second-hand a decade ago.

Air Zimbabwe also operates two Boeing 737s and one recently acquired extendedrange 767.
