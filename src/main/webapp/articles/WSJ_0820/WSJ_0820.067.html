Campeau Units Seek More Time To File Plan

  • Author: Larry M. Greenberg
  • Date: 08/20/90

CINCINNATI -- Federated Department Stores Inc. and Allied Stores Corp. said they have asked the U.S. bankruptcy court to extend for five months the period during which the companies have the exclusive right to file a reorganization plan. The request didn't come as a surprise, as the companies previously indicated they would probably seek such an extension.

Federated and Allied, both subsidiaries of Toronto-based Campeau Corp., said they needed the additional time "because of the scope and complexity" of their reorganization effort. The companies also said the extension would allow management to concentrate on the stores' operations during the key Christmas retail season.

In bankruptcy cases, the debtor has a right to file its own reorganization plan before creditors have a chance to suggest an alternative. But the debtor must file within a designated period or lose that right.

Federated and Allied are seeking an extension to Feb. 28 from Sept. 12, when the period of exclusivity currently is due to expire. The companies said a hearing on the request is scheduled for Sept. 11. They said the action isn't expected to affect the timing of a presentation to creditors of a future business plan, which is scheduled for October.

Kurt Barnard, publisher of the Retail Marketing Report, said the current difficult retailing environment complicates efforts by the companies to come up with a reorganization plan that is acceptable to creditors. He said an extension of the deadline beyond Christmas might give Federated and Allied a chance to reverse their recent losses and "put an encouraging face on their prospects."

In their latest reporting period, Federated and Allied posted a combined loss of $27.2 million for May on revenue of $129.5 million. Federated Department Stores consists of Bloomingdale's, Abraham & Straus, Rich's/Goldsmith's and Burdines. Allied Stores consists of Jordan Marsh, Maas/Jordan Marsh (Florida), Stern's and The Bon Marche.

Federated and Allied filed for protection under Chapter 11 of the U.S. Bankruptcy Code on Jan. 15 and were given until May to file a plan of reorganization on an exclusive basis. This period was then extended. According to Campeau filings, the two units have a combined $7.3 billion in debt to be settled under Chapter 11 proceedings.
