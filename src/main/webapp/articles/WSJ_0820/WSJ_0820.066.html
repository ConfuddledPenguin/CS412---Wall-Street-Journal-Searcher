International: Polly Peck Chief Backs Off Earlier Hint of a Buy-Out

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/20/90

LONDON -- Everybody is entitled to a mistake, but it looks like the chairman of Polly Peck International PLC may have used up his quota with London's institutional investors. In a change of heart that torpedoed his company's stock price Friday, Asil Nadir, the mercurial chairman and chief executive of the British food and electronics concern, said he won't be taking his company private after all. His decision came five days after he announced that he might make a bid -- valued by analysts at about #1.3 billion ($2.49 billion) -- for the 74% of the company he doesn't already own.

The company's shares tumbled 78 pence to close at 324 pence.

"Anybody outside {the company} must be a wee bit surprised about what's going on," said David Rough, a portfolio manager for Legal & General Assurance PLC.

The official reason for the change of mind was that the bid idea wasn't popular with many investors, who apparently "indicated that they wouldn't wish to see Polly Peck become a private company." Mr. Nadir didn't return telephone calls seeking elaboration.

But David Fawcus, group financial director, said Mr. Nadir decided his possible bid wouldn't attract enough shares to take the company private. Mr. Fawcus denied speculation by London analysts that Mr. Nadir had simply failed to raise the financing needed for a bid. "He satisfied the board and its financial advisers that the money was in place" to bid, Mr. Fawcus said.
