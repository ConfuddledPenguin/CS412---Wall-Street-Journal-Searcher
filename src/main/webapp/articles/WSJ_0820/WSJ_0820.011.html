New Securities Issues

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/20/90

The following were among Friday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report. MORTGAGE

Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $300 million of notes, due Aug. 28, 1995, priced at par to yield 8.80%. The notes, which are noncallable for one year, were priced at a spread of 35 basis points above the Treasury's five-year note. The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets.

Federal Home Loan Bank -- $265 million of notes, due Aug. 21, 1995, priced at par to yield 8.75%. The notes, which are noncallable for one year, were priced at a spread of 32 basis points above the Treasury's five-year note. The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Goldman Sachs & Co.

EUROBONDS

JPM GMBH (U.S. parent) -- $500 million of loan participation certificates due September 2000, via JP Morgan Securities Ltd. and joint lead DKB International, with coupon at three-month London Interbank Offered Rate plus 25 basis points at a price of par for first five years and three-month Libor plus 35 points thereafter. Offered is structured to fund and maintain subordinated loan to Dai-Ichi Kangyo Bank, London Branch. Call at par on any interest payment date from fifth year.

Staatsbank Berlin (East Germany) -- Five billion German marks of floating-rate notes due September 1995, via Commerzbank AG. Coupon is at six-month Frankfurt Interbank Offered Rate plus five basis points, at a price of par. First coupon fixing is Sept. 3. Backed by East German government, with West German government expected to assume such liabilities upon unification.

Narasaki Sangyo Co. (Japan) -- 50 million Swiss francs of privately placed notes due Sept. 12, 1995 with equity-purchase warrants and fixed 5 3/16% coupon at par, via Citicorp Investment Bank (Switzerland). Guarantee by Takugin Bank. Warrants exercisable Sept. 25, 1990 to Sept. 1, 1995. Warrant terms and conditions to be fixed Aug. 22.
