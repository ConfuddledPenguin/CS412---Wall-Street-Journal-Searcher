Business Brief -- Kansas Gas & Electric Co.: Alternatives Sought on Bid By Suitor Kansas City P&L

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/20/90

Kansas Gas & Electric Co., Wichita, Kan., said it instructed its investment bankers to seek alternatives to the proposed $27-a-share hostile tender offer from neighboring utility, Kansas City Power & Light Inc. KG&E's board earlier recommended that shareholders reject Kansas City P&L's $857 million offer on the grounds that it was inadequate and too conditional. A spokesman for KG&E said alternatives include the possible sale of the company, but he declined to disclose what companies, if any, are involved in talks. Any discussion of that information "might jeopardize the continuation of discussions," KG&E said. In Kansas City, Mo., a spokeswoman for Kansas City Power & Light Co., said, "We look forward to negotiating all aspects of our offer with Kansas Gas & Electric.

"Our fully financed, all-cash tender offer for the company is a win-win" situation for the Wichita concern's shareholders, employees and customers, the spokeswoman asserted.
