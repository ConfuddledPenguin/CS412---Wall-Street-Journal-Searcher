New Stock Listings

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/20/90

NEW YORK -- The New York Stock Exchange announced two new listings, the American Stock Exchange added one, and the National Market System of the over-the-counter market added four.

Cash America Investments Inc., Fort Worth, Texas, an owner and operator of pawnshops previously traded on the American Stock Exchange, begins trading Friday on the Big Board with the symbol PWN. Mexico Equity & Income Fund Inc., New York, a closed-end management investment company, started trading on Wednesday as an initial public offering under the symbol MXE.

Joining the Amex, CII Financial Inc., a Burbank, Calif., holding company for California Indemnity Insurance Co., previously traded over-the-counter, began trading Thursday, as CII.

On the OTC market, Gerrity Oil & Gas, a Denver drilling company, began trading Wednesday, as GOIL.

Lunar Corp., a Madison, Wis., maker of products for diagnosing and treating metabolic bone diseases, began trading Tuesday, under LUNR. Marcam Corp., a Newton, Mass., supplier of applications software for process manufacturers, began trading Thursday, under MCAM.

Southmark Corp., a Dallas real estate and financial services firm, recently reorganized after bankruptcy proceedings, and previously traded on the Big Board, began trading Monday, on a when-issued basis under SMRKV for common stock and SMRPV for preferred shares.

Separately, the Pacific Stock Exchange began trading options on Cracker Barrel Old Country Store Inc., owner and operator of country store-theme restaurants, under CBRL.
