International: Soviets May Loosen Law On Foreign Ownership Role

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/20/90

MOSCOW -- The Soviet government has submitted a bill to parliament that would allow foreign companies to become sole owners of new enterprises in the Soviet Union, Prime Minister Nikolai Ryzhkov said. It would be the first time in at least 60 years that foreigners have been allowed to wholly own businesses here. Under current law, foreign companies must work with Soviet firms in joint ventures.

"It is impossible to move toward a market economy while the country is isolated from the world economy," Mr. Ryzhkov said today, in remarks carried by the official Tass news agency. "One can no longer rely on joint ventures alone."

Tass quoted a senior official of the State Foreign Economic Commission as saying the bill would encourage foreign investment by granting substantial benefits for joint ventures in which the share of foreign capital exceeds 30%.
