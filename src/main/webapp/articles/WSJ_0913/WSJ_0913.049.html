Market Conditions Delay Construction Of GE Plant in Spain

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/13/90

PITTSFIELD, Mass. -- General Electric Co. confirmed reports that it is delaying construction of a $2.5 billion plastics facility in Spain because of weakening world-wide market conditions. The plant was to be near Cartagena on the Mediterranean coast with completion scheduled for 1992.

A spokeswoman for the GE Plastics unit said a new construction schedule is being discussed with the Spanish government but declined to disclose details. The Spanish government has donated the site and is to bear 30% of the cost through subsidies and grants.

Robert Reitzes, an analyst who follows chemicals and plastics at Mabon Nugent & Co., said rising oil prices since the Mideast crisis are forcing plastic makers, already skittish in a weakening economy, to rethink expansion plans. He cited Union Carbide Co. and Du Pont Co., among several, considering "indefinite delays."

GE Plastics first announced its plans in October 1988, saying the investment would total $1.7 billion. In 1989, it increased the planned investment to $2.5 billion for a larger manufacturing facility. Since then, the world-wide economy has softened, especially demand in the automotive market, the spokeswoman said.

GE expects to export 90% of the plant's output, silicon sealants for the construction industry and polycarbonate plastics for autos, and computer consoles. The facility was to begin production in two to three years from the date of the initial announcement, with a work force of about 500 and eventually expanding to 1,500 workers.

The spokeswoman said the company's commitment to build the facility hasn't changed.

---

Corrections & Amplifications

DU PONT Co. and Union Carbide Co. say neither is delaying nor considering a delay of any plastics plant construction. In Thursday's edition, an article incorrectly included them among companies considering such delays.

(WSJ Sept. 17, 1990)
