Valley Capital Unit To Continue Surcharge After Ruling by Court

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/13/90

LAS VEGAS, Nev. -- Valley Capital Corp.'s Valley Bank unit said it would continue charging a $1 surcharge on 100 of its automated teller machines around the state after a federal appellate court ruled againt a challenge by Plus System. The surcharge, levied to ATM customers mostly at machines in casinos, is unique to Nevada, which passed a law last year making it unlawful for bank card networks to dictate whether banks could use the surcharge. Denver-based Plus System Inc. challenged the constitutionality of that law, which a federal appeals court upheld.

Valley Bank had sued Plus System on antitrust grounds because Plus System refused to cooperate on the surcharge for its customers. Valley Bank's gross revenue on the surcharge is about $200,000 a month, according to Jon Joseph, executive vice president and general counsel. A Valley spokesman said he expected more Nevada banks to follow suit.

Plus System attorneys couldn't be reached, and it wasn't known if they would appeal. The bank-card network is owned by a group of banks and Visa International Inc.
