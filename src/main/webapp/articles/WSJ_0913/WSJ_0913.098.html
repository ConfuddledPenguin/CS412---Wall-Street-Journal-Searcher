Law: Two Judges Call Off Trial Of Former Asbestos Makers

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/13/90

BROOKLYN, N.Y. -- A federal judge and a New York state judge called off plans for an unusual, joint federal-state trial against former asbestos makers because settlement talks have successfully resolved most of the cases. Federal Judge Jack B. Weinstein of Brooklyn and state Justice Helen Freedman of Manhattan had scheduled the trial to begin Tuesday if efforts to settle the approximately 500 cases failed. In an order, the judges said that almost all of the cases have been settled or are in the process of being settled and that a joint trial won't be necessary.

The personal-injury cases involve former Brooklyn Navy Yard workers who were exposed to asbestos on the job. Asbestos, once widely used in insulation, causes cancer and respiratory ailments years after exposure. More than 40 former asbestos makers and distributors were named as defendants.

The two judges teamed up earlier this year to consolidate the cases and push for a settlement in a federal-state effort. The hearings rotated from state to federal court, with both judges presiding at most of the proceedings.

In their order, the judges praised Kenneth Feinberg, the court-appointed special master who oversaw the settlement efforts.

The settlement amounts haven't been disclosed. The cases that aren't settled are scheduled to go to trial in separate state and federal proceedings beginning next week.
