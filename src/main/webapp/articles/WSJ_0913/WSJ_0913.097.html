Letters to the Editor: Not So Enthusiastic About Mr. Gorbachev

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/13/90

We very much appreciate the review ("The Sick Soviet Bear," Leisure & Arts, Aug. 28) by Charles Wolf Jr. of our book, "Meltdown: Inside the Soviet Economy," but we would like to correct one point of great importance. Mr. Wolf reports that we "repeatedly urge reliance on and support for Mikhail Gorbachev." To the contrary, in our book we note that the dynamics of economic reform are no longer in his hands, and we have publicly criticized the Bush administration for putting all its eggs in Mr. Gorbachev's basket. Mr. Gorbachev may have missed the opportunity to use privatization to strengthen the Soviet Union against political disintegration. The movement toward autonomous republics might require the assignment of property rights, in whole or part, on a republic basis. Paul Craig Roberts

Karen LaFollette

Washington
