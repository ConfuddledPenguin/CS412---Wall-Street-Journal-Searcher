Nymex Investigates Rumor That Caused Price Surge

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 09/13/90

NEW YORK -- The New York Mercantile Exchange said it started an investigation of false rumor-spreading that may have resulted in the sharp run-up of crude oil futures prices Monday. In heavy trading in the final 20 minutes of the Nymex energy futures session, prices of crude oil futures and petroleum product futures for October surged over $1.60 a barrel.

An exchange spokeswoman said it had no suspects at this time, but the offender could be fined as much as $250,000 and suspended from trading. She added that the exchange would notify the Commodity Futures Trading Commission and the Justice Department.

The spokeswoman added that based on previous Nymex investigations there wasn't much confidence anyone would be charged.

Traders and analysts that were aware of the investigation doubt that the Nymex would be able to locate and discipline a culprit.

One trader noted after learning of the investigation that troop movements inside of Iraq and near Turkish and Syrian borders were confirmed by the U.S. State Department early Tuesday. Those movements, and early, limited knowledge of such activity, he said, could have been the base for the talk in oil markets Monday.
