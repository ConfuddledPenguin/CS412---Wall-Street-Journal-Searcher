Letters to the Editor: Israel Waged War Only to Defend Itself

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/13/90

Alexander Cockburn's Aug. 16 column proves that no matter how outrageous and one-sided his columns, he can top himself. First he takes the musings of "a former Jordanian minister" about Israeli expansion into Jordan and establishes that as a policy that might be followed by Israel -- without a shred of evidence.

Then he cites Amnesty International's reports on atrocities in Iraq, but unless my memory fails me, in all his articles seeking out Middle Eastern infractions of human rights he has managed to continually cite Israel and until now ignore violations in Arab countries.

And then he totally ignores contemporary history by comparing Israel's defensive wars of self-preservation to Iraq's unprovoked attack on Kuwait. While one can argue about Lebanon and might quibble about 1967 (when virtually all observers considered Egypt's closing of vital waterways as tantamount to an attack), only a fool could consider Israel's response to an attack on its holiest day in 1973 wrong. Mr. Cockburn is of course too opinionated to cite 1948, when all surrounding nations attacked Israel or the fact that Jordan attacked Israel in 1967, leading to Israel's capture of former Jordanian territories, or that Saddam Hussein, in this very paper less than two months ago, said that the Arab world's biggest mistake was ignoring Israel's offer to negotiate after the 1967 war and return captured territories.

Israelis do not have "immense joy" because of the PLO support of Iraq. They know that Saddam is a tyrant and that if Israel had not acted to stop his nuclear capability in 1981, American soldiers would need far more than gas masks today.

Stephen J. Kohn

New York
