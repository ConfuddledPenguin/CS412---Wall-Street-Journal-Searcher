Business Brief -- Whitehall Corp.: Cambridge Capital Discloses It May Seek Board Seats

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/13/90

Cambridge Capital Fund L.P., which holds 14.1% of Whitehall Corp., said it may seek seats on Whitehall's board even though it lost a proxy contest in May. In a Securities and Exchange Commission filing, Cambridge said it is concerned about the continuing deterioration in Whitehall's operations. Last month, Whitehall reported a second-quarter loss of $359,000, or 11 cents a share, compared with year-earlier profit of $380,000, or 11 cents a share. Revenue fell 53% to $4.1 million.

Cambridge said it is considering the solicitation of written consents from holders, or the calling of a special meeting, to remove some or all of Whitehall's directors and replace them with its own nominees.

Whitehall officials couldn't be reached. A Cambridge attorney wouldn't comment.

In the filing, Cambridge also said it raised its stake to 14.1%, or 478,300 shares, including 23,600 shares bought from Aug. 24 to Tuesday at $11.50 to $12.125 each.
