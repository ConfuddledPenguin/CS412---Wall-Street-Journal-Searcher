Venezuelan Non-Oil Exports

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/13/90

CARACAS -- Venezuelan non-oil exports have jumped 55% since last year, the government said. The country exported more than $2.3 billion in non-oil products from January through August 1990, up from $1.5 billion in the year-earlier period, the Central Office of Statistics and Information said.

Venezuela exported $260 million in non-oil goods last month, the highest level since March.

The government has been trying to encourage the non-traditional exports by allowing the local currency, the bolivar, to devalue. The currency has gone from an average of about 38 bolivares to the dollar in 1989 to a current 49 bolivares.

Nearly 90% of Venezuela's export income comes from oil sales.
