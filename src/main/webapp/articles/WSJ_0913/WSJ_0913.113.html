Ford Foundation Offers Grants to Fight Racism On College Campuses

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 09/13/90

The Ford Foundation launched a program to help combat racism on college campuses. The foundation announced $1.6 million in grants to help 19 colleges and universities add courses that deal more directly with such issues as cultural diversity, ethnic heritage and racial oppression. The initiative comes at a time when many campuses -- and the nation as a whole -- are dealing with outbreaks of racial prejudice and tension.

"To reach the roots of intolerance and improve campus life, we must make the teaching of non-Western cultures a basic element of undergraduate education, said Franklin Thomas, president of the New York foundation. "The minority population under the age of 18 in the U.S. has risen from 15% in the 1950s to 36% today, and the number is still growing. As the face of America changes, education must help American students learn to live and work together," he said.

Foundation officials said that the grants are just the first step in what will be a multiyear program involving other institutions.
