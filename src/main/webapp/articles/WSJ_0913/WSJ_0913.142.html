Ex-Southmark Chairman's $960,000 Bonus Is Blocked

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/13/90

DALLAS -- A federal bankruptcy judge here refused to allow Southmark Corp. to pay a $960,000 bonus to Arthur G. Weiss, who marshalled the real estate and financial services company through its recent reorganization. The bonus would have supplemented the $480,000 annual salary and $480,000 in severance benefits to be received by Mr. Weiss, who stepped down as Southmark's chairman in mid-August as part of the court-approved reorganization plan for Southmark.

U.S. Bankruptcy Judge Stephen Felsenthal said yesterday that he couldn't "in good conscience" approve the bonus payment to Mr. Weiss. "The court is very skeptical about compensation packages from companies that are able to pay only a fraction of their debts," the judge said. Southmark's reorganization plan provides for creditors to receive just $300 million in cash and securities to satisfy claims totaling $2 billion.

Southmark directors approved Mr. Weiss's bonus in January, about a year after he had taken the helm following the directors' ouster of Gene E. Phillips as chairman and William S. Friedman as vice chairman. Southmark filed in July 1989 for reorganization under Chapter 11 of the U.S. Bankruptcy Code.

Mr. Weiss, reached at his home in Atlanta, said he was disappointed by the judge's decision but could understand the reasoning behind it. "I've been compensated fairly in this," he said, adding that any appeal would be carefully considered. "I think I did go out with honor, and to have a fight now wouldn't do anybody any good," he said.

Separately, Judge Felsenthal ruled that Southmark has fulfilled the requirements of its reorganization plan relating to stock and cash payments to creditors and the transfer of $100 million in assets to the company's thrift unit, San Jacinto Savings Association of Houston. The judge's order effectively seals the plan from any further changes, though parts of it have been appealed by Messrs. Phillips and Friedman.
