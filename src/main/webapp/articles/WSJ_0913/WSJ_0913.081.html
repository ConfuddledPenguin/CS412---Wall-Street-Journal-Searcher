Corna and Its Owner Agree to Sanctions Imposed by NASD

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/13/90

NEW YORK -- Corna & Co., a Columbus, Ohio, securities firm, agreed to be expelled from the National Association of Securities Dealers, and its owner was barred from the industry as regulators charged them with 20 violations of securities laws and rules, including stock parking. Without admitting or denying wrongdoing, the firm and David A. Corna consented to the sanctions imposed by the securities industry's self-regulatory agency. The NASD also fined the firm $100,000 and Mr. Corna, of Dublin, Ohio, $150,000. Corna & Co. was the subject of eight prior disciplinary actions, the NASD said.

Mr. Corna was unavailable for comment.

The main charge in the NASD's disciplinary complaint is that Corna & Co. executed a stock-parking scheme to conceal a lack of sufficient capital to conduct a securities business. Parking securities involves placing them with another entity to conceal their true ownership and reclaiming them later.

In Corna & Co.'s case, the NASD said that in February and March 1988, the firm sold short the stock of First World Cheese Inc., a Maplewood, N.J., cheese marketer.

Short sales are effectively bets that a stock's price will decline. However, First World's stock jumped in price. The NASD charged that Corna suffered more than $200,000 of losses on the short sales, and as a result was at least $100,000 below net capital requirements for seven months.

The NASD charged that instead of ceasing business, as federal law requires, Corna & Co. placed its short positions with 19 different customer accounts, hiding its net-capital deficiency.

The agency also charged Mr. Corna with creating two fictitious customer accounts, which allegedly defrauded the firm through which Corna & Co. cleared its business. And the agency charged Mr. Corna with forging the signatures of several customers and former employees on various documents.

First World Cheese wasn't charged with any wrongdoing.
