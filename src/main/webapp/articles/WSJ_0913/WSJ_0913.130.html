Business Brief -- Occidental Petroleum Corp.: Cleanup of Waste Landfill To Cost Firm $45 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/13/90

Occidental Petroleum Corp. said it agreed to pay about $45 million as part of a plan to clean up a hazardous waste landfill in Niagara Falls, N.Y. The plan, proposed in federal court in Buffalo, N.Y., calls for Occidental to build and operate for about $25 million a containment and waste-extraction system at the S-Area landfill, which was operated by Hooker Chemical Co., acquired by Occidental in 1968. The site is where 63,000 tons of hazardous waste were disposed, according to the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency. The system is expected to be completed in 1996.

In addition, Los Angeles based Occidental has agreed to spend $64.9 million over a 20-year period to build with the city a new $67.8 million drinking water treatment plant on a site adjacent to the landfill, according to Thomas L. Jennings, vice president for environmental affairs at Occidental's chemical operations. These funds were diverted from a plan to clean up the existing water plant on the same site.

Aside from Occidental Chemical Corp. and the EPA, other parties to the agreement are the U.S. Department of Justice and New York state departments of environmental conservation, law, health and the city of Niagara Falls.
