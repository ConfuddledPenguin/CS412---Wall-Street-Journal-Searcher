National City Units to Get Food-Stamp Program Role

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/13/90

COLUMBUS, Ohio -- National City Corp. said two of its units were selected by the U.S. Agriculture Department for a pilot program offering electronic delivery of food-stamp benefits. The bank holding company said the project is expected to give it an edge in what it called a potentially large long-term revenue source. A National City spokeswoman declined to make any projections of earnings from the project.

National City said the units involved are National Processing Co. and First National Bank of Dayton.

Under the program, recipients of food stamps -- which the federal government provides to low-income individuals who meet its guidelines -- will receive a plastic card similar to a bank automated teller machine card. A microprocessor chip embedded in the card will provide access to the person's records and enable the amount of food stamps withdrawn to be deducted from the amount the recipient is entitled to, without requiring a call to a central computer.
