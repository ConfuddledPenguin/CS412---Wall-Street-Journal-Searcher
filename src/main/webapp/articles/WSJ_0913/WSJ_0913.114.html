ServiceMaster Has Accord With Waste Management Inc.

  • Author: Brett Pulley
  • Date: 09/13/90

DOWNERS GROVE, Ill. -- ServiceMaster L.P. and Waste Management Inc. agreed in principle to form a new pest control, cleaning service and lawn-care joint venture by combining operations with total revenue of about $920 million. The two suburban Chicago companies said the venture, to be known as ServiceMaster Consumer Services Limited Partnership, will operate as a unit of ServiceMaster.

The planned partnership will consist of Waste Management's pest-control business and TruGreen lawn-care business. ServiceMaster will contribute its consumer services business, including its residential and commercial cleaning services, the Terminix termite and pest control businesses, the Merry Maids maid business and the American Home Shield home warranty business.

The agreement calls for Waste Management to receive common equity in the partnership representing 20% of the total equity and a preferred interest convertible into another 2.1% of the common equity of the partnership, according to the companies. ServiceMaster will initially hold 80% of the common equity.

The two businesses that Waste Management is contributing had 1989 revenue of $170 million, and ServiceMaster's contribution of a mix of franchise and company run businesses had over $750 million in consumer level revenue for the same period.

Waste Management said it valued the two businesses it contributed, and the value of the partnership interest and convertible preferred it will receive, at about $180 million, giving the venture an indicated total value of about $818 million.

ServiceMaster, a health care and cleaning concern which had $1.5 billion in revenue last year, said the partnership was "a great addition" that would make Terminix the leading pest control business in the country.

For Waste Management, the nation's largest collector of municipal garbage with over $4.4 billion in revenue, the agreement will rid the company of the responsibility of running its consumer services business, while maintaining an equity position.

Under the agreement, ServiceMaster and Waste Management will develop and participate in joint marketing efforts in both the consumer services and management services areas. Waste Management will be represented on the venture's board, but won't participate in day-to-day management.

"We're going to continue to focus on environmental services and ServiceMaster is going to focus on consumer services, and our shareholders will participate," in earnings from the operations it is contributing, said a Waste Management spokesman. "That's why we didn't sell" the operations, he added.

The transaction is subject to approval of both company's boards. The companies said they expect to reach a definitive agreement by the end of the year.

In composite trading yesterday on the New York Stock Exchange, ServiceMaster closed at $22, up 75 cents, and Waste Management closed at $35.50, down 75 cents.
