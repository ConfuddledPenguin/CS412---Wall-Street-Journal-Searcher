Business Brief -- TW Holdings Inc.: Concern Makes Prepayments Of $70 Million on Its Debt

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/13/90

TW Holdings Inc. said it is making two prepayments totaling $70 million on senior debt, reflecting "better than anticipated" operations. TW, which in December 1989 acquired TW Services Inc., the giant food-services concern with 1989 revenue of $3.5 billion, said a $40 million prepayment made yesterday was applied to four quarterly principal payments of term A senior debt, scheduled to begin Dec. 1.

TW, a Spartanburg, S.C., provider of food, vending and medical services said the other $30 million, which it plans to pay today, will reduce the bridge portion of its senior bank loan to $267.5 million. The company said it expects to pay off the balance of the bridge loan by Dec. 1 with proceeds from planned asset sales and real estate financings.

Karl Sedlarz, vice president and treasurer, said cash flow has been better than expected, largely because of controls on capital expenditures and strong revenue, which rose 7.7% in the six months ended June 30 to $1.8 billion from $1.67 billion a year earlier.

TW owns Canteen, the big contract food and vending service, Denny's restaurants, and the Quincy's Family Steakhouse chain, and is the largest franchisee of Hardee's restaurants.

Coniston Partners, the New York-based investment concern, and associated parties control TW Holdings.
