Contract Proposal Rejected By Canada Auto Workers

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/13/90

TORONTO -- The Canadian Auto Workers union rejected a contract proposal by the Canadian subsidiary of Ford Motor Co., and both sides predicted it will be difficult to avert a strike scheduled to begin tomorrow at midnight. Terms of the contract proposal, which covered economic issues such as wages, vacations and cost-of-living allowances, weren't disclosed. Both sides referred to the offer, the first tabled by the company since negotiations began last week, as a "basis for further discussion."

CAW President Robert White said Ford was moving in the right direction with its contract proposal. But he said the amount of money the company was offering was "not even close" to the union's demands. He said he doubted there was enough time to reach a settlement before the strike deadline. The current contract, covering 12,800 Ford workers, expires tomorrow.

Ford Canada spokesman James Hartford said a tentative contract agreement before the strike deadline was "achievable," but only with "considerable effort."

"One of the serious players here is the clock," Mr. Hartford said.

The CAW began contract talks last Wednesday after selecting Ford as its strike target. Any contract reached will serve as a model for later talks with the Canadian subsidiaries of General Motors Corp. and Chrysler Corp.
