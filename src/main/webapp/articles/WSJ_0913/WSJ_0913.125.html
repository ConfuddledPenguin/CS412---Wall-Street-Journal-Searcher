Business Brief -- Equimark Corp.: Shareholder's Suit Charges Concealment of Bad Loans

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/13/90

Troubled Equimark Corp., Pittsburgh, was hit with a shareholder lawsuit, alleging that the bank-holding company concealed its bad loans. The suit, filed by one shareholder as a class-action complaint, also alleges Equimark misled the public into believing it would achieve consistent growth and earnings. And the suit claims that Equimark overstated its profits for the past several years by failing to set adequate reserves for loan losses.

An Equimark spokeswoman said she hadn't seen the suit and couldn't comment on it.

The suit comes one week after Alan S. Fellheimer announced his resignation as chairman of Equimark. He left amid criticism for his overly ambitious growth strategy, brusque management style and mounting losses.

The suit named Mr. Fellheimer and several current and former executives as defendants, including Mr. Fellheimer's wife, Judith. Mrs. Fellheimer was a high-ranking executive who left Equimark earlier this year.
