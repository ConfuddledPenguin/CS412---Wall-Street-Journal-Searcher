Ames Stores Net Loss For Quarter Widened To $152.5 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/13/90

ROCKY HILL, Conn. -- Ames Department Stores Inc. reported a second-quarter net loss of $152.5 million, or $4.15 a share, but said that operating losses weren't as bad as it had expected. The discount retailer, which is operating under Chapter 11 protection of the federal Bankruptcy Code, said the loss widened from a restated loss of $11.1 million, or 38 cents a share, in the year-ago quarter.

Stephen L. Pistner, who took over as Ames chief executive officer in May after the bankruptcy-law filing, said the company's losses before nonrecurring charges were "significantly less than we expected because of better-than-expected summer clearance sales."

Ames said that before the nonrecurring charges, its operating loss was $79.4 million. Nonrecurring charges include a $40 million adjustment of accounts payable during the bankruptcy claims filing process, a $20 million reserve for inventory markdowns and a $9.1 million write-off of certain management information systems.

Ames sales declined 54% to $563.3 million from $1.22 billion.

Ames started racking up big losses last year after acquiring the Zayre discount store chain in an ambitious expansion attempt. Many of the Zayre stores proved to be money-losers, and Ames closed 221 of them in June.

Mr. Pistner said that during the quarter Ames "essentially restarted the business," getting suppliers to start shipping merchandise again and resuming advertising.

For the six months, Ames net loss widened to $583.4 million, or $15.71 a share, from $27.5 million, or 90 cents a share, a year earlier. Sales fell 33% to $1.55 billion from $2.33 billion.
