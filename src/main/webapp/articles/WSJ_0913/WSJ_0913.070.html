Kansas P&L Said To Be Negotiating In Acquisition Bid

  • Author: Randall Smith
  • Date: 09/13/90

Kansas Power & Light Co. is the previously unidentified third party negotiating to rescue Kansas Gas & Electric Co. from an $857 million hostile bid from another local utility, according to people familiar with the situation. However, the same people said the talks between the two utilities haven't progressed very far.

They and some takeover stock traders theorized that disclosure of the talks late Tuesday by the target, Kansas Gas & Electric, may have been aimed at getting the first bidder, Kansas City Power & Light Co., to increase its $27-a-share offer.

In composite trading yesterday on the New York Stock Exchange, Kansas Gas & Electric's stock rose $1.25 to $25.75 in response to its disclosure after the market closed Tuesday that it has begun acquisition talks with an unidentified third party.

Yesterday, Kansas City Power & Light, based in Kansas City, Mo., asked to be given "equal opportunity with other parties" to negotiate an acquisition of Wichita-based Kansas Gas. Kansas Power & Light is based in Topeka.

One reason some traders were skeptical about Kansas Power's chances of acquiring Kansas Gas is that Kansas Power carries a somewhat smaller stock-market value than Kansas Gas. "You've got an $800 million company trying to buy a $1 billion one," said one.

Officials at Kansas G&E and Kansas P&L couldn't be reached to comment late yesterday.
