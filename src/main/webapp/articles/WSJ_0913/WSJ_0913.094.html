Business Brief -- General Motors Corp.: GM Opens Engine Facility Under '87 Labor Contract

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/13/90

General Motors Corp. said its engine division will provide jobs to about 135 laid-off hourly workers at a new engine remanufacturing business to be located in Lansing, Mich. The venture, while small, has symbolic importance as contract talks between GM and the United Auto Workers union head toward a strike deadline tomorrow night. The UAW is demanding that GM step up efforts to re-employ idled workers by launching ventures such as this.

The engine remanufacturing business is the first venture to be funded by a $100 million fund established under the 1987 UAW-GM national contract. The fund is designed to provide new jobs that aren't included in the scope of GM's operations, the auto maker said. GM officials wouldn't disclose the cost of the business.

The remanufactured engines will be sold through the GM service parts operations, a GM spokesman said.
