Arco Agrees To Pay Alaska $287 Million --- Settlement Ends Dispute Of Royalties Due State From North Slope Oil

  • Author: Rhonda L. Rundle
  • Date: 09/13/90

LOS ANGELES -- Atlantic Richfield Co. said it agreed to pay Alaska $287 million to settle a 13-year dispute over royalties due the state from Arco's Alaskan North Slope oil production. The settlement will result in a third-quarter charge of about $25 million. A large reserve that covers most of the settlement amount along with other potential liabilities was set up piecemeal over many years, Arco said.

Analysts said the charge will have a negligible impact on third-quarter profit and may be offset by one-time gains, including a previously announced $300 million legal settlement with Southern Natural Gas Co. In the year-earlier quarter, Arco earned $379 million, or $2.19 a share, on revenue of $3.71 billion.

The settlement agreement includes an approach for calculating future oil royalties on most of Arco's current North Slope leases. "This methodology is intended to reduce the potential for future conflict," said William E. Wade Jr., vice chairman of Arco Alaska Inc., an Arco unit.

Alaska Governor Steve Cowper said the settlement "marks the beginning of a new era of cooperation between the state and Arco which will mean a more amicable payment system and which also will lead to increased development on the North Slope."

Arco said the settlement resolves all major claims involving its North Slope oil production that were raised by Alaska State in a 1977 lawsuit, including charges of negligent misrepresentation, breach of implied covenants of good faith and fair dealing, and breach of fiduciary duty.

"The settlement is fair and isn't an admission of wrongdoing on our part," an Arco spokesman said.

The company said that 11 other North Slope oil producers were originally named in the suit, including Chevron U.S.A., a unit of Chevron Corp.; BP Exploration (Alaska) Inc. and Standard Alaska Production Co., units of British Petroleum Inc.; Exxon Corp.; Louisiana Land & Exploration Co.; Mobil Corp.; FMP Operating Co., a unit of Freeport-McMoran Inc.; Marathon Oil Co., a unit of USX Corp.; Getty Oil Co., a unit of Texaco Inc., and Phillips Petroleum Co.

Amerada Hess Corp. settled in December with a $319,000 payment, the state said.

Alaska has been seeking a total of about $902 million from the oil producers, including $320 million from Arco in royalties and interest for the period June 1977 through November 1986. The $287 million settlement encompasses production through February of this year, Arco said.

"The settlement is a positive in that it eliminates uncertainty," said L. Eugene Nowack, an analyst at Dean Witter Reynolds Inc. He expects Arco to record one-time gains of roughly $190 million in the third quarter and a slight increase in operating profit.

In a strong market for oil stocks, Arco shares rose $2.375 to $138.625 in New York Stock Exchange composite trading.
