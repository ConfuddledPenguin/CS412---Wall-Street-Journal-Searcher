Who's News: Columbia Pictures Retains Lieberthal As Chairman of Unit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/13/90

CULVER CITY, Calif. -- Gary Lieberthal, chairman of the television division of Columbia Pictures Entertainment Inc., signed a new long-term employment agreement and will be elected to the board of directors of Columbia, a Sony Corp. unit. Mr. Lieberthal is the only head of a major Columbia division to remain in his post after Sony's purchase of Columbia late last year. Peter Guber and Jon Peters took over as co-chairmen of Columbia soon after the purchase, succeeding Victor Kaufman, who resigned. Messrs. Guber and Peters subsequently recruited Frank Price to head the Columbia Pictures unit, succeeding Dawn Steel, who resigned. Mike Medavoy was chosen to run the Tri-Star Pictures unit after Jeff Sagansky left to run CBS Inc.'s Entertainment Division.

While Columbia's movie operations had been plagued by management turnover and box office flops prior to the Sony purchase, the television operation has been stable and successful since Mr. Lieberthal took the helm in 1987. Profits from the television operation have carried the movie business through numerous losing seasons, and Columbia Pictures Television is considered one of the company's most valuable assets, with a library of nearly 25,000 television episodes.

Columbia's current television series include "Who's the Boss?," "Married . . . With Children" and "Designing Women." Mr. Lieberthal joined the television division in 1986, when Columbia acquired Embassy Communications Inc., where he was chairman and chief executive.
