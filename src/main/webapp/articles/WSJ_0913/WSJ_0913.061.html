Business Brief -- Swissair: Planned Sale of Hotel Unit To Aoki/Westin Collapses

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/13/90

Swissair was dealt a blow in efforts to control costs when the planned sale of its hotel unit, Swissotel AG, to the Aoki/Westin Group of Japan fell through. The national airline, 23%-owned by Swiss federal and cantonal authorities, signed a letter of intent in March to sell its interests in 16 luxury hotels to the Japanese construction and hotel group that owns Westin Hotels & Resorts of the U.S. Swissair cited "uncertainty in world financial markets" and the complexities of the deal for the sale's collapse.

Analysts had valued Swissotel at as much as 750 million Swiss francs ($565 million).

An airline spokeswoman said the strengthening Swiss franc against the yen and the dollar made Swissotel more expensive for Aoki. Further, there were difficulties in financing the four U.S. hotels in the chain, she said.

Swissair in March said it wanted to sell the unit because substantial investment would be required to develop Swissotel into a viable international chain.
