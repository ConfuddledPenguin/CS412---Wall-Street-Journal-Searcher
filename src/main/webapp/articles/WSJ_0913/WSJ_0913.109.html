Abreast of the Market: Industrials Advance 13.12 Points, Led by Energy and Metals Issues

  • Author: David Wilson
  • Date: 09/13/90

NEW YORK -- The stock market, led by energy and metals stocks, moved higher in continued light trading. The Dow Jones Industrial Average rose 13.12 to 2625.74 as gains in three blue-chip oil issues -- Exxon, Chevron and Texaco -- and in Aluminum Co. of America boosted the 30-stock measure by a total of about 10.40 points.

Traders attributed a late surge to speculation that Iraqi leader Saddam Hussein might be overthrown, spurred by a report from Egypt's state-run news agency that his control of the country is being undermined by a growing dissident movement and a coup was likely.

The rally took the market back up to the vicinity of session highs reached just after trading opened. President Bush's address to Congress, in which he declared that the Iraqi leader "will fail" in his efforts to annex Kuwait, served as an inspiration for the early gains, traders said.

In between, concerns about a statement from Iran expressing opposition to the U.S. military presence in the Persian Gulf and periodic bouts of futures-related program selling kept stocks under pressure, traders said.

"The market's still in the same quandary," said Richard Meyer, head of institutional trading at Ladenburg, Thalmann & Co. "Without news, the market has no direction in which to go."

On the New York Stock Exchange, 129,890,000 shares were traded, compared with 113,220,000 shares Tuesday. Advancers led decliners by 778 to 638.

Among broader market indexes, Standard & Poor's 500-Stock Index climbed 1.50 to 322.54, the New York Stock Exchange Composite Index went up 0.70 to 177.09 and the Dow Jones Equity Market Index rose 1.07 to 298.80.

Several analysts said the heavy selling which had preceded the market's recent lull -- and the increasing pessimism developing among investors -- indicate that the market is about due to break out of its recent trading range with a rally.

Since Aug. 23, the Dow Jones industrials has stayed within a range of about 40 points on a closing basis -- between its Aug. 29 reading of 2632.43 and its Aug. 30 close of 2593.32.

"We've had plenty of excuses for {stocks} to go down again," said Michael Metz, a market strategist at Oppenheimer & Co., and the show of stability provides evidence that investors "who just have to get out {of the market} and have to raise cash have already done it."

Meanwhile, the latest survey of investment advisors by Investors Intelligence -- a popular indicator of market sentiment -- showed a sharp increase in the level of market bearishness.

Of the market letter writers surveyed a week ago, 56% held a pessimistic outlook on stocks while only 30% were bullish and 14% were expecting a short-term correction. The readings were in line with levels reached at the market's lows in late January, when more than 57% of the advisers were bearish and less than 30% were bullish.

The data "would indicate that a lot of people have already moved to the sidelines," said Michael Burke, editor of the survey for Chartcraft Inc., New Rochelle, N.Y. That should insulate the market from any sharp drops over the short run, he added.

Exxon went up 1/2 to 51 3/8 in composite trading of 1.3 million shares, while Chevron advanced 1 to 77 1/8 and Texaco climbed 1 3/4 to 64 3/8. Among other oil stocks, Mobil gained 3/4 to 66, Amoco added 7/8 to 58, Unocal added 5/8 to 32 7/8 and Atlantic Richfield went up 2 3/8 to 138 5/8. In the oilfield-services sector, Schlumberger rose 1 1/4 to 66 7/8 and Halliburton rose 3/4 to 54 7/8.

Alcoa advanced 2 to 68 1/4, while Alcan Aluminium gained 1/2 to 22 1/8 and topped the exchange's list of most active issues with 3.5 million shares. Reynolds Metals, which said it expects third-quarter earnings to be close to the second quarter's $1.75 a share, jumped 2 3/8 to 68 3/8; its forecast was higher than many analysts had projected.

Elsewhere in the metals sector, Amax rose 1 1/2 to 26 and Phelps Dodge climbed 1 7/8 to 63 3/4 on the Big Board. Maxxam, which owns Kaiser Aluminum & Chemical, jumped 3 3/8 to 62 on the American Stock Exchange.

Corroon & Black rallied 6 3/4 to 37 5/8 on three million shares. Aon expressed interest in buying the insurance company for $40 a share, or about $840 million. Corroon & Black has agreed to a stock-swap merger offer from Willis Faber, a British insurer. Aon dropped 1 1/2 to 33.

Browning-Ferris Industries dipped 1 1/2 to 33 on 1.9 million shares after several analysts, in response to a more cautious outlook from the company, lowered their earnings estimate for the fiscal year ending this month and the following year.

Prudential-Bache Securities analyst Vishnu Swarup, who lowered his fiscal 1991 forecast for Browning-Ferris by five cents a share to $2.30, also downgraded the stock's rating to "hold" from "buy." Mr. Swarup told Dow Jones Professional Investor Report his actions reflected economic weakness in the Northeast, which produces 12% to 13% of the company's revenue. He maintained his fiscal 1990 estimate at $2 a share.

Waste Management, another pollution-control issue, also was a victim of heavy selling. The stock dipped 3/4 to 35 1/2 in composite trading of about 1.3 million shares. But ServiceMaster, which agreed to combine certain operations with two Waste Management units in a partnership, rose 3/4 to 22.

Santa Fe Pacific fell 1/2 to 14 7/8 on 1.9 million shares. The company said it would swap 14.5 million common shares for $215.6 million in debentures under an exchange offer which expires Tuesday.

Gulf States Utilities skidded 1 3/8 to 10 1/8. The Texas Supreme Court, overturning an earlier appeals-court ruling, decided that state utility regulators didn't have the authority to reconsider $1.4 billion in costs related to the company's River Bend nuclear plant in setting rates.

MNC Financial rose 3/8 to 7 1/8 on 1.3 million shares. Its board approved plans to sell $180 million in preferred stock to Alfred Lerner, a director, and maintained the company's common-stock dividend at 29 cents a share. MNC shares dropped in heavy trading last week amid rumors that the sale was in jeopardy and the dividend would be reduced.

The American Stock Exchange Market Value Index rose 0.18 to 323.19. Volume totaled 9,260,000 shares.
