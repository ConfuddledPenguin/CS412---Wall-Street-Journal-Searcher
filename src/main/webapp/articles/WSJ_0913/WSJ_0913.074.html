International Brief -- BTR PLC: British Firm's Pretax Profit Increased 6.6% in First Half

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/13/90

BTR PLC, one of Britain's largest industrial conglomerates, reported a 6.6% rise in pretax profit for the first half of the year and said it would issue one free bonus warrant for every 15 shares held. The profit before tax of #530 million ($984.7 million), from #497 million a year earlier, was at the lower end of market forecasts, which ranged from #525 million to #550 million.

BTR set the exercise price of the warrants at 370 pence. If all the warrants are exercised in the allowed periods in 1994 and 1995, they would add 116 million shares to the company's current 1.75 billion ordinary shares in circulation and raise #429 million in capital.

Profit after tax, less minorities, rose 6.7% to #302 million from #283 million a year earlier. Per-share earnings improved 5.5% to 17.4 pence from 16.5 pence.

Net profit attributable to shareholders, however, fell 7.9% to #302 million from #328 million. BTR didn't report any extraordinary items in the half-year, although in the previous period the company had a #45 million extraordinary gain from the proceeds of its sale of National Tyre, a tire and car replacement parts distributor, for #140 million.

Sales were up 2.5% to #3.47 billion from #3.39 billion.
