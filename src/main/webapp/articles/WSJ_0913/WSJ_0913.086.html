International Brief -- Bouygues S.A.: Ivory Coast Awards Unit Electricity System Contract

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/13/90

Bouygues S.A. said a unit has received a 20-year contract valued at 2 billion French francs ($376.1 million) annually to manage the Ivory Coast's electricity distribution system. The French construction and public works group said La Saur S.A., which specializes in electricity distribution and is one of the largest water management companies in France, also agreed to invest about 100 million francs to update the West African country's electricity network and pay debt accrued by Cie. Ivoirienne d'Electricite.

The group said La Saur will request technical assistance from Electricite de France International, a branch of the French state-run electricity company.

A Bouygues official said the contract grew out of the Ivory Coast's need to supply the necessary expansion and maintenance investments required in the electrical sector. The country's population is projected to increase to 20 million by 2000 from about 14 million currently.

The group said the contract will boost La Saur's annual revenue to about 6 billion francs this year.
