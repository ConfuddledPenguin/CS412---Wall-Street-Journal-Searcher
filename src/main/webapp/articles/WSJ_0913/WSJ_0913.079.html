American Air Sues FAA to Block Plans By United at O'Hare

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/13/90

FORT WORTH -- American Airlines said it sued the Federal Aviation Administration to block an agency decision yesterday that would allow rival United Airlines to change certain flight schedules at Chicago's O'Hare International Airport. The suit, filed in the U.S. Court of Appeals in Washington, is the latest development in a running spat between the two carriers over schedules at O'Hare, one of the nation's busiest airports. The two carriers and affiliated commuter lines account for 80% of the traffic there.

Last month, American was granted a temporary restraining order barring United from publishing new schedules for that airport. A hearing is set for Sept. 17. In its latest suit, American said, the appeals court ordered filings by both sides by Sept. 19. American is a unit of AMR Corp.; United is a unit of UAL Corp.

The latest round was kicked off yesterday when the FAA announced it was authorizing United to move certain evening flights away from time periods into which American had recently moved flights. The FAA said United's changes would reduce anticipated delays and air traffic controller workloads in those periods. The authorization would take effect Oct. 1 and expire Jan. 15, 1991.

The seeds of the scrap were planted last March, when American and United at the behest of the FAA agreed to accept certain scheduling restrictions to reduce congestion at O'Hare. In June, American told the FAA that it was withdrawing from the arrangement because it was suffering competitively and was rescheduling some evening flights within the flexibility allowed under its official "slots." A "slot" is an assigned block of time in which an aircraft is supposed to arrive or depart.

United then proposed moving some of its flights, but to times not included in its formal "slots," which triggered American's opposition. American said the new times would give United an unfair competitive edge.
