K mart Looks to New Logo To Signify Broad Changes

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/13/90

TROY, Mich. -- K mart Corp. plans to ditch its familiar red and turquoise signs in favor of a new logo. Chairman Joseph Antonini acknowledged that changing a symbol as well known as K mart's is risky because it could alienate shoppers. But, he said, "We wanted to signify the changes taking place inside the store. This is what we have to do."

The new logo, which will appear in ads as well as signs at all 2,231 K mart stores by 1995, features a big red "K." The word "mart" is written in smaller white script inside the K. It was designed by Anspach, Grossman, Portugal Inc., New York.

Retailing and image consultants have contended for some time that the old logo conveyed the impression that K mart hadn't changed much since its early discounting days in the 1960s, when it attracted shoppers with low-priced light bulbs, hangers and other odds and ends. Nowadays, K mart sells more stylish apparel and home goods. And it just embarked on a massive $2.3 billion store renovation program to enlarge its outlets, widen aisles and improve its displays.

Mr. Antonini defended the logo change. "Our research showed you could do anything you wanted with the mart," he said, "but don't lose that big red K."
