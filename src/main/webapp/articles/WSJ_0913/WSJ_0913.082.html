Letters to the Editor: A False Report Of False Reports?

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/13/90

In his Aug. 15 Rule of Law column relating to problems for Roger and Juliana Pilon, Gordon Crovitz, referring to Michael Shaheen, the Justice Department ethics chief, suggests Mr. Shaheen may have issued an erroneous report on the Pilons. He states, "If Mr. Shaheen issued the annual report despite knowledge that Mr. Pilon had been cleared, this might be a case of J. Edgar Shaheen. Mr. Shaheen has been the only director of the post-Watergate ethics office, which was formed in 1976. Longevity may breed a dangerous insularity." Perhaps Mr. Crovitz's "J. Edgar Shaheen" reference was to the fact that J. Edgar Hoover was a longtime director of the FBI. However, the unfortunate sentence structure implies that Mr. Hoover, too, issued false reports on individuals. I am not aware of any such charges made about Mr. Hoover.

Paul V. Hagan

Former Chairman

Philadelphia Chapter

Society of FBI Agents

Merion, Pa.
