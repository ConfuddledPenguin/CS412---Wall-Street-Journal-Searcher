Business Brief -- Landmark Land Co.: Regulators Set Some Curbs On Oak Tree Savings Unit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/13/90

Landmark Land Co., Carmel, Calif., said federal regulators subjected its Oak Tree Savings Bank unit to operating restrictions because of insufficient capital. Landmark said the "supervisory agreement" includes limits on new lending and certain other actions, including the payment of dividends and the hiring of directors and employees, without clearance from the federal Office of Thrift Supervision. Landmark said the restrictions are customary for thrifts that fail to meet higher capital standards established under last year's thrift-bailout law. It added that Oak Tree, based in New Orleans, had already been curtailing its lending since the beginning of the year.

Landmark also said that it received "several proposals" in reply to a plan to sell much of Oak Tree's real-estate holdings but that it couldn't predict whether a sale would be completed. An earlier agreement to sell nearly $1 billion in real estate to developer Barry Hon collapsed because of regulatory objections.
