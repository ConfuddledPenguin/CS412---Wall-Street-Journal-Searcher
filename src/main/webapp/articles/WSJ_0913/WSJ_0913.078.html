International Brief -- British Aerospace PLC: Flat Earnings Performance Recorded for the First Half

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/13/90

British Aerospace PLC said pretax profit for the first six months eased 0.7% to #146 million ($270.8 million) from #147 million a year earlier. The results, which matched market expectations, were damped by a #28 million charge related to a prolonged industrial dispute at the aerospace division of British Aerospace, which is one of the country's largest industrial concerns with interests in auto making and property development.

Profit after taxes and minority interests, or net profit attributable to shareholders, was unchanged at #102 million. Earnings per share eased 0.3% to 35.6 pence from 35.7 pence. Despite the flat earnings performance, the company said it will boost its interim dividend to 8.9 pence a share, up 9.9% from the 8.1 pence paid in the year-earlier first half.

Sales climbed 20% to #4.79 billion from #4 billion a year earlier.

The company said cost-reduction programs helped profit margins in several divisions, including both commercial aircraft and defense equipment.
