Technology: GTE's Airfone Unit, Comsat Sign Accord To Offer Global Service

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/13/90

NEW YORK -- GTE Airfone Inc. signed an agreement with Communications Satellite Corp. to carry Airfone's aircraft-based phone traffic internationally by mid-1991. The move by the GTE Corp. unit comes as rival international phone services such as British Airways Skyphone and Aeronautical Radio Inc. have been formed to challenge Airfone's position in the fledgling aircraft phone service business.

"Our customers . . . require the availability of global communications while inflight from anywhere in the world," said Mark S. Schneider, Airfone's marketing vice president.

Airfone will use the satellite ground stations in Southbury, Conn. and Santa Paula, Calif., that are operated by Comsat Aeronautical Services, a unit of Comstat, which is based in Washington. The network also will have data-carrying capability.

In addition, Comsat will arrange for additional global links through the satellite system operated by the International Maritime Satellite Organization (Inmarsat).

Airfone, which began operations in 1984, has installed air-to-ground phones in more than 1,300 aircraft or about a third of the U.S. airlines' domestic fleet. Airfone also operates Railfone, a public phone service on Amtrak trains. In April, the Federal Communications Commission formally established aircraft phones as a regular communications service and opened the field to potential competitors. GTE has been trying to block from the business one of those rivals, In-Flight Phone, which was founded by Airfone's creator John D. Goeken. Their squabble is now in the courts.
