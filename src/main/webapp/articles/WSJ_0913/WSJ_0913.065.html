Marketing & Media: Sears to Offer Voucher To Users of Charge Cards

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/13/90

CHICAGO -- Sears, Roebuck & Co. hopes to boost sales to charge-card customers by offering cash certificates that can be applied to future purchases. The retailing and financial services concern, struggling to reverse years of sagging earnings, is introducing the "frequent shopper" program in time for the Christmas season. With a recession looming, executives also hope the program will increase their store's appeal to cautious consumers.

"It won't be tremendously significant, but it will be a plus," said Laurence Cudmore, Sears retail president.

But others aren't so sure. "I don't see it as a real strong incentive" for shoppers, said Kurt Peters, editor of a trade publication called Credit Card News.

The reason: Sears is offering customers who accumulate at least $200 in purchases on their cards certificates valued at 1% of those purchases. The average Sears charge-card customer spends $300 to $400 a year, meaning a bonus of $3 or $4.

Mr. Cudmore acknowledged that the bonus doesn't seem substantial. But he said when the program was tested in Baltimore and Pittsburgh, Sears found "a very definite difference in performance (at stores in the pilot program) versus the control stores."

He declined to estimate how much the program might cost Sears. Sears has 40 million charge-card accounts, but it considers only 26 million of them active. A spokeswoman said the bonus club, which starts Saturday, could help Sears attract customers who haven't shopped its stores in a while.
