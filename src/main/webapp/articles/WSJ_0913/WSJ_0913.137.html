International Brief -- Montedison S.p.A.: Enimont Chemical Venture With Italy to Be Dissolved

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/13/90

Founding shareholders of the troubled Italian chemical venture Enimont S.p.A. agreed to dissolve the year-old public-private partnership, Italy's state industry ministry said. Enimont's founders also laid out steps to transfer ownership of the company, possibly within 30 days, to one of the two partners, the state energy agency Ente Nazionale Idrocarburi and Montedison S.p.A., a chemical company.

"The parties agree that ENI will communicate its offer to acquire or sell {its Enimont stake} within 15 days . . . and Montedison will convey its answer within 15 days of the offer," the ministry statement said. Under guidelines set out by the government last week, if Montedison refuses to buy the agency's stake, the government will acquire Montedison's holding. Each partner owns 40% of Enimont, and minority shareholders own the remaining 20%.

Profound differences between the two main shareholders have marred Enimont from its inception, keeping the company from becoming the model of state-private cooperation it set out to be.
