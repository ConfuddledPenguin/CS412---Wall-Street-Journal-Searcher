Business Brief -- Westinghouse Electric Corp.: Sale of Beverage Business To Managers Is Completed

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/13/90

Westinghouse Electric Corp., Pittsburgh, said it completed the sale of its soft-drink beverage business to a group led by management of the unit. Westinghouse also purchased a 15% interest in the new venture. Terms weren't disclosed. Westinghouse, a diversified company with interests in broadcasting, financial services, community development and electrical parts distribution, signaled last year that the beverage group was on the block, as it had been hurt by pricing competition in the soft-drink industry.

The beverage group, based in Vernon, Calif., has about 2,000 employees and is the largest bottler of 7-Up. Its portfolio of brands includes RC Cola, A&W Root Beer, Sunkist Orange and Hawaiian Punch. It also distributes Seagram's Wine Coolers, and Perrier and Evian bottled water. Its western division includes facilities and bottling franchises in southern and central California; Las Vegas, Nev.; Phoenix and Tucson, Ariz.; and Albuquerque, N.M. The Caribbean division includes Puerto Rico and the U.S. Virgin Islands.
