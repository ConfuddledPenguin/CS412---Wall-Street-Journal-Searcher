Business Brief -- Ecolab Inc.: European Business to Merge With Henkel Cleaning Unit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/13/90

Ecolab Inc., in a continuation of a business relationship with West Germany's Henkel KGaA that began last December, said the two companies have agreed to combine their European cleaning and sanitizing businesses. Ecolab, a cleaning and maintenance concern based in St. Paul, Minn., will also acquire Henkel's cleaning and sanitizing businesses in 18 non-European countries and plans to acquire Henkel's interest in cleaning and sanitizing businesses in several other countries.

Under the agreement, Ecolab will pay $74 million and Henkel will receive 5.6 million shares of Ecolab's common stock. Each company will hold a 50% stake in the joint venture company, Henkel-Ecolab, which will have revenue of about $750 million.

Henkel, a chemical products and packaging concern, will have representation on Ecolab's board proportionate to its stock ownership. Ecolab currently has 23.5 million common shares outstanding.

Henkel will also get back the $110 million it paid for its initial investment in Ecolab. In December Ecolab sold Henkel 1.1 million shares of 7% Series A convertible preferred stock. Ecolab said the shares will be retired.

The transaction is subject to certain regulatory approvals and is expected to be completed early next year.
