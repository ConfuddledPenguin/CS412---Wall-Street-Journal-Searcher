International Brief -- Butte Mining PLC: Offer Is Made to Acquire Two Australian Companies

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/13/90

Butte Mining PLC of Britain has made a takeover offer for two Australian gold mining concerns, Vam Ltd. and Perseverance Corp., that values both companies at a total of about 46.8 million Australian dollars (US$38.2 million). Butte is offering two new Butte common 10-pence shares for every three shares of Vam and four new Butte common 10-pence shares for each Perseverance share outstanding. At exchange rates last Thursday, the offer values each Vam common share at 23.1 cents, which values the whole company at A$26.5 million. It values Perseverance shares at A$1.39 each and the company at A$20.3 million.

Vam owns about 49% of Perseverance. An associate of Butte has agreed to accept Butte's offer for its 16.5% of Vam, entitling Butte to that stake. Butte doesn't have any entitlement to Perseverance shares.

Vam and Perseverance had combined gold production of about 35,000 ounces in the fiscal year ended June 30. They are forecast to reach an aggregate annualized level of 65,000 ounces by mid-1991.
