Business Brief -- Honda Motor Co.: Base Prices Are Increased For Several 1991 Models

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/13/90

Honda Motor Co., Gardena, Calif., said it is raising base prices on several 1991 models as much as 7.2%, although it is freezing prices on some versions. The biggest price increase is on the Prelude 2.0 Si. The 1991 model will have a base price of $14,945, up 7.2% from a year earlier. Honda said spokesmen weren't immediately available to comment on the reason for the move.

The Japanese auto maker is boosting the price on its most expensive Prelude, the Si four-speed equipped with automatic transmission, 4.9% to $19,450. Honda said it will also raise the base price on its 1991 Civic three-door hatchback to $6,895, up 3.9% from a year earlier.

The auto maker isn't changing prices on some other 1991 models, including the CRX HF and the CRX Si.

Honda said that, on average, its 1991-model base reflect an increase of 1%, or $140 a car. That average includes a modest increase on the Accord, the top-selling car model in the U.S. Its base price, announced earlier, will increase $200, or 1.6%, from the 1990 model.
