Court Rules Costs In Gulf States Case Can't Be Reviewed

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/13/90

AUSTIN, Texas -- The Texas Supreme Court ruled that the state's public utility commission doesn't have authority to reconsider some $600 million in nuclear plant construction cost overruns in setting the electric power rates of Gulf States Utilities Co. The court confirmed an earlier lower court ruling that once the commission set aside those costs in a 1988 rate case, it couldn't take them up again in a later one. The costs were presented again in a different format in a 1989 Gulf States rate case; the utility said the commission had instructed it to resubmit the costs in that case with better justification.

The ruling didn't disallow the construction costs or rule on the merits of the original commission decision to set them aside. It only confirmed a lower court ruling that said by giving the utility "a second opportunity to prove the same facts," the public utility commission incorrectly rejected a hearing examiner's conclusion.

The costs at issue are part of the New Orleans-based utility's $3.1 billion investment in the River Bend nuclear plant in Louisiana. About $1.4 billion of that has been challenged as not prudent expenditures. The portion of the overruns considered by Texas regulators amounted to about $600 million. The remainder applied to the utility's rates in Louisiana; regulators there have disallowed that portion and the company is appealing the decision.

Gulf States said it will seek a rehearing from the Texas Supreme Court. If that fails, it said it would consider appealing to the U.S. Supreme Court. It also said it would press pending litigation that it brought against the Texas commission in 1988, challenging on its merits the agency's original decision to set aside the costs.

The utility reiterated previous statements that if all efforts to have the $1.4 billion considered in its cost structure fail, it would be forced to take after-tax write-offs of up to $600 million.
