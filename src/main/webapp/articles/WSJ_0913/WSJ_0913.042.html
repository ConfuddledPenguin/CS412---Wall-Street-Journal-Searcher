Dun & Bradstreet to Sell Soviet Business Directories

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/13/90

NEW YORK -- Dun & Bradstreet Corp. said it will market directories on Soviet business data to companies in the West. Dun & Bradstreet signed an agreement with the Information and Publishing Center of Goskomstat, the U.S.S.R.'s state committee on statistics. The company plans to offer the Soviet data in four directories, beginning in the fourth quarter of 1990.

One directory will include the names and addresses of all operational joint ventures. Another directory will provide data on 500 of the largest Soviet manufacturing businesses. The third will list names and addresses of Soviet export enterprises. The fourth will be an annual directory containing historical Soviet import and export trade statistics for 1988, 1989, and 1990, and quarterly data for 1991 forward.

"The information made available by this agreement will help Western firms to evaluate emerging business opportunities in the Soviet Union," said Charles W. Moritz, Dun & Bradstreet's chairman and chief executive officer.

Each directory should sell for less than $100 although a price has yet to be set, according to the marketer of business information and related services.
