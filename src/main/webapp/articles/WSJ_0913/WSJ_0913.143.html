What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/13/90

AON MADE A BID of $40-a-share, or $840 million, for insurance broker Corroon & Black. The offer challenges a $703 million stock-swap pact under which Corroon agreed to merge with Willis Faber, a London-based insurance broker. Aon's move is one of the first major public bidding battles in a year marked by a dearth of high-profile takeover fights. ---

William Zeckendorf Jr. is restructuring loans on two of his real estate projects and is said to be negotiating to sell a major piece of his company to Japan's Tobishima construction firm in exchange for a cash infusion.

---

Bank regulators are likely to get congressional authority to boost industry insurance premiums following a second bleak government review of the health of the fund.

---

The dollar hit a 13-month low against the yen, partly because of expectations of lower U.S. interest rates. Many traders think the U.S. currency will fall even further.

---

Budget negotiators labored to overcome differences on taxes and on how to enforce a deficit-reduction accord. Participants see little chance of an agreement before tomorrow.

---

Cincinnati Milacron, the last big U.S. maker of heavy robots, is quitting the business. Milacron agreed to sell its robot operation to a unit of Switzerland's Asea Brown Boveri.

---

Darling-Delaware lacks cash to make interest payments on its junk bonds, just 20 months after paying a $179 million dividend to a few prominent investors who own the company.

---

Atlantic Richfield agreed to pay Alaska $287 million to settle a dispute over royalties due the state from the concern's North Slope oil production. The settlement will result in a third-quarter charge of about $25 million.

---

Canada's Hibernia oil field will be developed by a group led by Mobil Oil Canada, under an agreement expected to be signed tomorrow.

---

General Dynamics named James Mellor to succeed Herbert Rogers as president and chief operating officer. The move emphasizes the defense firm's focus on international sales.

---

Philadelphia is postponing again the sale of $375 million of short-term bonds after Swiss Bank withdrew its guarantee for a portion of the offering by the cash-strapped city.

---

Nissan unveiled its 1991 Sentra, with which the Japanese auto maker hopes to regain U.S. market share.

---

Brazil will ease its limitations on imports of computers and electronics, as part of Collor's campaign to liberalize the country's economy.

---
