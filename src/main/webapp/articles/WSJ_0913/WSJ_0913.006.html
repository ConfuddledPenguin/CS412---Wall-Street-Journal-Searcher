International: European International Traffic

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/13/90

BRUSSELS -- The number of passengers carried on European airlines' international flights rose 11% in July, the third consecutive month of increases above 10%, the Association of European Airlines said. But the association said July probably marked the end of that growth, citing the effects of higher oil prices and fuel costs that quickly followed Iraq's Aug. 2 invasion of Kuwait.

Routes in Europe, across the Atlantic and to Asia and Australia all showed above-average growth, the association said, while South Atlantic, sub-Saharan Africa and Middle Eastern routes had smaller increases. The sharp decline in traffic to North Africa continued, the association said. Air freight rose 7.4% in July, the second highest rise this year.
