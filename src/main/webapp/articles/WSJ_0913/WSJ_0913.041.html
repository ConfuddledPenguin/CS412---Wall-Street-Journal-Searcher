Alberta's Offering Of Phone Utility Draws Big Interest

  • Author: Gary Lamphier
  • Date: 09/13/90

EDMONTON, Alberta -- The Alberta government said it expects to raise 951 million Canadian dollars (US$819.1 million) from an initial public offering of shares of Telus Corp., the new holding company that owns Alberta's big telephone utility. Alberta telecommunications minister Fred Stewart said the C$12-a-share issue is the largest equity offering ever in Canada. He added the offering has drawn "overwhelming" interest in Alberta, where investors can buy Telus shares for C$6 each, and pay the balance next September.

Since investor demand in Alberta exceeded the issue's size by about C$450 million, Mr. Stewart said the government has had to limit the number of shares that can be purchased on the two-part installment plan to a maximum of 900 per investor.

Demand for the offering, which is expected to be completed by Sept. 28, has proven a windfall for the issue's 11-member underwriting group, led by RBC Dominion Securities Inc. of Toronto. With trading volumes down sharply this year in Canada and many securities dealers posting operating losses, the estimated 3.7% average underwriting and selling commission earned by dealers on the issue has offered some welcome relief.

"It has been a very good issue for us," said Tony Melvyn, a broker in the Edmonton office of Levesque Beaubien Geoffrion Inc., a securities dealer based in Toronto.

The offering of more than 79.3 million Telus shares, representing a 60% stake in the company, has also drawn heavy interest from the 11,500 eligible employees of Alberta Government Telephones, Telus's main subsidiary. About 84% of the big utility's staff subscribed to the issue, Mr. Stewart said.

As previously reported, the Alberta government first announced in May its plans to privatize Alberta Government Telephones, which is Canada's third largest telecommunications concern with assets of about C$2.8 billion and 1989 revenue of C$1.2 billion.

The government intends to retain the remaining 40% stake in Telus for at least a year.
