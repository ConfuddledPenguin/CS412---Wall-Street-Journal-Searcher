Money Funds Grew Total of $2.4 Billion In the Latest Week

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/13/90

NEW YORK -- Investors poured $2.4 billion into money market mutual funds in the latest week. Assets of the 435 taxable funds tracked by IBC/Donoghue's Money Fund Report increased $1.1 billion, to $399.9 billion, the Holliston, Mass., newsletter said. The average seven-day compounded yield, which assumes reinvestment of dividends, remained at 7.75% in the week ended Tuesday.

Money fund portfolio managers lengthened the average maturity of taxable funds' investments by a day, to 47 days, indicating that the managers expect short-term interest rates to decline. "They want to lock in the higher yields," said Martha Wittbrodt, editor of Donoghue's. The funds invest in such things as commercial paper (short-term corporate IOUs), short-term bank certificates of deposit and Treasury bills. Average maturity is now the longest it's been since August 1987.

The average seven-day simple yield of the taxable funds remained at 7.47%. The average 30-day simple yield slipped to 7.48% from 7.49%, and the average 30-day compounded yield eased to 7.76% from 7.77%.

Assets of 231 tax-free funds tracked by Donoghue's rose $1.3 billion to $81.2 billion, a record, the newsletter said. The average seven-day compounded yield rose to 5.54% from 5.43%. The latest yield is equal to 7.69% for an investor in the 28% tax bracket and 8.27% for someone paying 33% in taxes.

The average maturity of the tax-exempt funds remained at 59 days.
