Sales Charges Removed On 3 Lexington Funds

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/13/90

SADDLE BROOK, N.J. -- Lexington Management Corp., a unit of Piedmont Management Co., New York, said it will eliminate sales charges on purchases of shares of three mutual funds, effective Sept. 12. The three funds are: Lexington Corporate Leaders Trust Fund, with assets of $87.6 million; Lexington Global Fund, with assets of $52 million; and Lexington Technical Strategy Fund, with assets of about $9 million. Previously, the fund management company imposed maximum sales charges of between 4% and 5% on the funds.

Lexington Management, which manages about $2.5 billion of assets, also offers eight mutual funds that never carried purchase and redemption fees. Company officials said the popularity of these funds prompted them to drop the fees on the other three funds.
