Business Brief -- CRI Insured Mortgage Association: Plan to Buy Partnerships Led by Integrated Amended

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/28/90

CRI Insured Mortgage Association Inc., a real estate investment trust based in Rockville, Md., amended its plan to acquire four mortgage investment partnerships managed by New York-based Integrated Resources Inc. In a Securities and Exchange Commission filing, CRI said it is proposing to swap cash plus shares of either CRI or a new company it formed for all the units outstanding of the four partnerships.

Under the previous offer, the partnerships' unit holders would have received only shares. Now CRI proposes to pay 10% of the units' exchange value in cash and 90% in stock. CRI said the plan must be cleared by the SEC and by holders of a majority of CRI shares.

Integrated, which is operating with protection from creditor lawsuits under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code, manages investment partnerships. It has been fighting the CRI offer because it would lose certain management fees if the bid succeeds. This month, a plan under which Integrated would sell its interests in the partnerships for as much as $45 million to Krupp Cos. collapsed. Krupp is a Boston-based real estate investment manager.
