New Securities Issues

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/28/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATE

Marriott Corp. -- $150 million of senior notes, due Sept. 1, 1993, were priced at par to yield 9 1/8%. The non-callable notes were priced at a spread of 77 basis points above the Treasury's three-year note. Rated Single-A-3 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and Single-A-minus by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets.

MORTGAGES

Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $1 billion of Gold Remic, or real estate mortgage investment conduit, securities offered in nine classes by Prudential-Bache Securities Inc. The offering, Series FHLMC Gold Remic 1001, is backed by Freddie Mac Gold 9 1/2% issue and was structured to yield between 9% and 9 5/8%.

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $1 billion of Remic mortgage securities offered by Goldman Sachs & Co. The offering, Series 1990-119, is backed by Fannie Mae's 9 1/2% issue.

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $563 million of Remic mortgage securities offered in 10 classes by Kidder Peabody & Co. The offering, Series 1990-117, is backed by Fannie Mae's 10% securities. Coupons on classes for which details were available were set at 8.95%.

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $500 million of Remic mortgage securities offered in 12 classes by PaineWebber Inc. The offering, Series 1990-120, is backed by Fannie Mae's 9% securities. Coupons on classes for which details were available were set at 9%.

EUROBONDS

Clarion Co. (Japan) -- 150 million Swiss francs of privately placed convertible notes due Sept. 30, 1995, with a fixed 4 1/2% coupon at par via Swiss Bank Corp. Put option on March 31, 1993, at a fixed 110 to yield 8.16%. Each 50,000 Swiss franc note is convertible from Oct. 1, 1990 to Sept. 20, 1995. Final conversion terms fixed Aug. 30. Fees 1.50 points.
