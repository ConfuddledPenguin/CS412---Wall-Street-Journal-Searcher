Financing Business: Consolidated Freightways Inc.

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/28/90

CONSOLIDATED FREIGHTWAYS Inc., Menlo Park, Calif., trying to recover from continuing losses at its Emery Worldwide air-freight unit, said it signed agreements with Bank of America for financing worth $350 million. A spokesman for the trucking and air-freight company said in June that it had negotiated an $850 million credit agreement with a subsidiary of Chemical Banking Corp. But the spokesman said that changes in the company's management, announced last month, and improvements in its business resulted in a new credit deal for a lower amount. The deal with Bank of America calls for Consolidated to receive a fixed-rate term loan of $100 million and "standby" credit of $250 million. Consolidated said the financing is subject to "customary conditions," but declined to provide details. Consolidated, which bought Emery in 1989 for $470 million in cash and debt, has struggled financially this year, posting a net loss of $37.5 million for the first half of the year, mainly because of Emery's woes. But the company said Emery is on the mend, citing "stabilized levels of revenue and reduced expenses" at the air-freight unit.
