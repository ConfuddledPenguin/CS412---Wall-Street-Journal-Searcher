Merrill Holders Settle Five Suits Involving Losses

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/28/90

NEW YORK -- Merrill Lynch & Co. shareholders settled five lawsuits brought by the holders that involved losses in trading transactions and certain real estate investments, according to an attorney for Howard A. Rubin, a former senior trader of mortgage-backed securities for Merrill Lynch. The attorney, George B. Yankwitt, said in a statement that the holders agreed to have the suits dismissed and that Merrill Lynch will receive $17,525,000 directly from its directors' and officers' liability insurers.

Mr. Yankwitt said three of the suits arose out of an estimated $250 million loss sustained by Merrill Lynch in April 1987. In those lawsuits certain Merrill Lynch holders said a portion of the loss was due to purchases of mortgage-backed securities by Mr. Rubin and further claimed that Mr. Rubin's supervisors -- Brian Barefoot, Robert Andres and Greg Herschell -- had failed to supervise his trading activities.

The shareholders sought more than $250 million in damages on behalf of Merrill Lynch. Mr. Rubin has denied engaging in any unauthorized trades or other wrongful conduct, Mr. Yankwitt said.

Two of the lawsuits said that directors of Merrill Lynch violated their fiduciary duties in connection with losses sustained by Merrill Lynch in other transactions, which included a lease agreement in the World Financial Center and a resort development in St. Thomas, U.S. Virgin Islands.

A spokesman for Merrill Lynch said that the company believes all the allegations "except those relating to the conduct of Howard Rubin" to be "completely without merit."

The attorneys said that the settlement does not require Mr. Rubin or any of the other individual defendants to pay any money to Merrill Lynch. The settlement agreement recognizes that the defendants have denied any liability or wrongdoing.

Mr. Yankwitt said Merrill Lynch will provide Rubin with a general release from all potential claims.
