Business Brief -- Playboy Enterprises Inc.: Company Buys Back 200,000 Of Its Nonvoting B Shares

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/28/90

Playboy Enterprises Inc. said it bought back 200,000 of its nonvoting Class B shares from Industrial Equity (Pacific) Ltd. for $825,000, or an indicated $4.125 each. The Chicago-based publishing and entertainment company had earlier announced it would repurchase as many as 200,000 of the nonvoting shares "through open-market purchases and negotiated purchases." Yesterday, Playboy said its board authorized the purchase of as many as an additional 200,000 Class B shares, which it said it considers to be "undervalued."

Playboy created the Class B common as part of a recapitalization effort designed, in part, to let it issue new stock without diluting the 71% holding of former Chairman Hugh Hefner.
