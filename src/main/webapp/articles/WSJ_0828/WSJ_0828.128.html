Stotler Group Inc. Files for Bankruptcy In Chapter 7 Petition

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/28/90

CHICAGO -- Stotler Group Inc., the troubled futures concern, admitted it couldn't solve its financial problems and filed a Chapter 7 bankruptcy petition at the federal Bankruptcy Court here. The move, which was expected, came three weeks after commodity funds managed by Stotler forced the company into involuntary Chapter 11 bankruptcy proceedings, claiming Stotler owed them more than $5 million. It was the latest wrinkle in the collapse of the futures brokerage firm linked to former Chicago Board of Trade Chairman Karsten Mahlmann.

In a Chapter 7 bankruptcy, the bankrupt firm is liquidated after the courts have determined that reorganization isn't worthwhile; creditors often receive a fraction of their claims, and stockholders typically get nothing. In a Chapter 11 proceeding, the company is reorganized and continues to operate; security holders often get a reduced ownership position.

Stotler's troubles came to light last month with the disclosure that it failed to meet Chicago Board of Trade capital requirements. Mr. Mahlmann, who was Stotler's chairman, has said he wasn't involved in the firm's daily operations. But he became the first CBOT chairman in 130 years to resign from the nation's largest futures exchange.

Stotler President Thomas M. Egan said in a statement Friday that "it is now in the best interests of all creditors that the further administration of these companies' affairs be handled in an orderly manner by the bankruptcy court, in accordance with bankruptcy laws." He added that "a return to the shareholders of Stotler Group appears unlikely."

Stotler also said it offered to settle Securities and Exchange Commission charges related to its failure to maintain sufficient capital, without admitting or denying the agency's findings.
