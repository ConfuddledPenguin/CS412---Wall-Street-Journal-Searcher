Triton Energy to Mull Shareholder Request To Sell Some Interests

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/28/90

DALLAS -- Triton Energy Corp. said it will consider the request of a major shareholder that Triton sell its three biggest partly owned units. The request came from Industrial Equity Ltd., which holds a 10.4% Triton stake and has said it might seek control. In a letter filed Friday with the Securities and Exchange Commission, Industrial Equity said it had encouraged Triton to dispose of its interests in Triton Europe, Crusader Ltd. and Triton Canada -- assets that Industrial Equity called mature and no longer strategic. Industrial Equity is controlled by New Zealand investor Robert Brierly.

Michael P. McInerney, Triton vice president, said the company's first priority was the completion of its previously announced restructuring program. "But after that, the sale of those subsidiaries is something we would consider," he said.

He declined to estimate the value or size of the three units. But previously the company estimated that its stake in Triton Europe -- the largest of the three -- was worth $180 million.

In the SEC letter, Industrial Equity said it generally approved of Triton's restructuring plan, which calls for the sale of aviation operations, a securities portfolio and a seismic-equipment subsidiary. The plan is designed to redirect the company's focus toward oil and gas exploration.

Industrial Equity also said in the letter that it had sold 110,000 Triton shares, lowering its stake to 10.4% from 10.9%.

As previously reported, Triton took a $31.1 million fiscal fourth-quarter charge for the write-down of certain assets in connection with the restructuring plan. For the year ended May 31, the company recorded a net loss of $54.2 million, or $2.93 a share, on revenue of $260.1 million, after a $593,000, or three cent a share, extraordinary gain from a tax-loss carry-forward.
