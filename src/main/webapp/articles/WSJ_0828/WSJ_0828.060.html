Whittaker Considers Spinoff to Shareholders Of Biotechnology Unit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/28/90

LOS ANGELES -- Whittaker Corp. said it may spin off to shareholders its biotechnology unit, which will contribute about 17% of Whittaker's expected $190 million sales for the current fiscal year. A Whittaker spokesman said a decision hadn't yet been reached but said "chances are we'll do it," probably early next year. The company's directors and secured lenders would have to approve the transaction, however. It would also have to be structured on a tax-free basis, Whittaker said.

The unit makes and sells diagnostic tests used by doctors and hospitals and laboratory cell-culture media.

Whittaker also posted results for its third quarter, ended July 31. In the period, the concern had net income of $4 million, or 47 cents a share, compared with $5 million, or 62 cents a share. Year-earlier net included a loss from continuing operations of $3.2 million and a gain of $5.5 million on the disposal of discontinued operations. Sales rose 7.1% to $48 million from $44.8 million.

Whittaker has sold several businesses in the past year as part of a recapitalization plan. Whittaker's continuing operations, besides the biotechnology unit, are in aerospace.

For its first nine months, an $85.2 million gain on the disposal of discontinued operations more than offset a $363,000 loss from continuing operations. Net was $80.7 million, or $9.61 a share, compared with $22.4 million, or $3.03 a share.

Marcus Schloss & Co., a New York investment firm, also disclosed in a filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission that it boosted its stake in Whittaker to 7.6% for investment purposes.
