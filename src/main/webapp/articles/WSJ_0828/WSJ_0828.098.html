Business Brief -- Coca-Cola Co.: Food Unit Plans Price Cut For Frozen Orange Juice

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/28/90

Coca-Cola Foods, which produces Minute Maid orange juice, said it plans to cut the price of its frozen concentrated juice to reflect cost decreases. The Coca-Cola Co. division, the nation's largest orange juice maker, said it would reduce the price of a 12-ounce can of frozen juice by 20 cents to 25 cents. A typical can currently costs about $1.60 to $1.90, the company said.

In recent weeks, Brazilian processors have announced price decreases on orange concentrate, reflecting strong supplies. In taking advantage of the lower costs, Coca-Cola Foods hopes to stimulate growth in frozen juice volume, which it previously predicted would decline at a double-digit rate this year.

The price cuts may stem some of that decline, but they aren't deep enough to completely erase price hikes taken earlier this year to offset a freeze in Florida. In December, for example, a typical can of Minute Maid frozen orange juice sold for $1.20 to $1.50.

Still, orange juice is a price-sensitive beverage, and a price decrease is certain to stimulate demand for frozen juice, a lethargic category that has suffered from consumers' preference for the convenience of ready-to-drink, chilled beverages.
