Ex-President of North Atlantic Fisheries Inc. Receives $10,000 Fine

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/28/90

ROCHESTER, N.Y. -- Peter Kolokouris, former president of North Atlantic Fisheries Inc. here, was sentenced to a $10,000 fine and five years of probation in U.S. District Court here. Mr. Kolokouris had pleaded guilty to filing a false tax return and filing a false statement with regulators about the company's initial public offering.

Mr. Kolokouris, appearing before federal Judge Michael Telesca, faced a sentence of as much as 13 years in prison and $110,000 of fines, said Charles M. Pilato, assistant U.S. attorney in charge of the case.

In June, without admitting or denying wrongdoing, Mr. Kolokouris settled a lawsuit filed against him by the Securities and Exchange Commission by giving up a total $2.1 million of stock in North Atlantic and three other companies. That suit charged that the executive had a secret ownership interest in the company's underwriter and that he received kickbacks from the underwriter.

John Parinello, attorney for Mr. Kolokouris, said his client had helped to initiate the civil settlement and that this cooperation was reflected in the outcome of the criminal proceeding.

The criminal and civil charges both were filed in June 1989. The civil settlement also bars Mr. Kolokouris for five years from being an officer, or owning 5% or more, of any public company.

North Atlantic Fisheries, a fish processor, is being overseen by a court-appointed receiver.
