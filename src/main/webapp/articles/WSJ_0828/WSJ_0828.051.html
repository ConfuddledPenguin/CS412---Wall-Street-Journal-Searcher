REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: No One Sillier Than U

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/28/90

It looks as if Irish rock singer Sinead O'Connor won't be playing New Jersey's Garden State Arts Center again. She announced backstage Friday that she wouldn't perform if the U.S. national anthem were played first, as is policy at the state-run center. Officials felt it was too late to cancel her performance. She wasn't acting out of solidarity with Roseanne Barr, last seen spewing spit onto the turf after singing "The Star Spangled Banner." No, Sinead O'Connor objects to how the U.S. government grants arts subsidies. "I feel very strongly about censorship," she said, "and I don't want to go onstage after the anthem of a country that's arresting people and harassing people for expressing themselves onstage." We haven't heard about any arrests in the United States yet of chocolate-covered women, but censoring the national anthem does give new meaning to Ms. O'Connor's hit, "Nothing Compares 2 U."
