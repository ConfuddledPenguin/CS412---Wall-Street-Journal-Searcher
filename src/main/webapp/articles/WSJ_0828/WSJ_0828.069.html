International Brief -- Hoechst AG: Roussel-Uclaf Unit Reports 24% Rise in First-Half Net

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/28/90

Roussel-Uclaf S.A., the French company that makes the abortion pill RU 486, reported a 24% increase in consolidated profit for the first half of 1990 to 319 million francs ($60.9 million) from 258 million francs the previous year. The pharmaceutical group, which is 54.5% controlled by Hoechst AG of West Germany, said its strong profit growth was based on a 13% gain in consolidated revenue to 6.58 billion francs from 5.81 billion francs a year before. On the basis of a comparable group structure, the increase was only 7.1%.

Roussel-Uclaf said earlier this year that it expects to report a 10% rise in its profit for all of 1990, excluding exceptional items.
