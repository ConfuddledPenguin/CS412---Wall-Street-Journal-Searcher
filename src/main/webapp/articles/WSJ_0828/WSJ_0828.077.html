Jet Makes Maiden Flight In Air Force Job Rivalry

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/28/90

EDWARDS AIR FORCE BASE, Calif. -- A version of the Air Force's next-generation fighter jet, built by Northrop Corp. and McDonnell Douglas Corp., took to the skies for the first time yesterday, setting the stage for a showdown with another version of the aircraft built by a rival team of aerospace companies. The twin-engine YF-23, with a Northrop test pilot, Paul Metz, at the controls, climbed to 25,000 feet, and functional checks of basic subsystems were performed during the early-morning, 50-minute flight. United Technologies Corp.'s Pratt & Whitney division made the engines on the YF-23.

"The airplane flies very clean, much cleaner than we expected," Mr. Metz said. "During the climb-out, I was using considerably less power than I expected, and the chase airplanes were on afterburners just to stay with me -- with my landing gear down."

The other team, composed of Lockheed Corp., Boeing Co. and General Dynamics Corp., is scheduled to roll out its Advanced Tactical Fighter, the YF-22, tomorrow at a Lockheed site in Palmdale, Calif. The first flight of that plane is expected to be next month.

The Air Force, which wants 750 of the jets to replace the aging F-15 fighter, is scheduled to choose the winning team in April and award the full-scale development contract three months later. A second YF-23, which was delivered to a test facility here in June, will fly with General Electric Co. engines.
