Marketing & Media: New American Talks With Rival Publisher Of Magazine for Men

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/28/90

NEW YORK -- New American Magazine Co. is negotiating for the possible merger of its men's magazine, Smart, with the as-yet-unpublished Men magazine. Owen Lipstein, chairman of New American, confirmed that he was discussing a possible acquisition of Men, which will begin circulation next year. It is owned by Norris Publishing, the Fairfield, Conn.-based company newly formed by Christopher Kimball, former publisher of Cook's magazine, and Peter Kaplan, the former editorial director of the now-defunct Manhattan inc.

Mr. Lipstein said he hopes to complete an agreement some time next week.

At Norris Publishing, Mr. Kimball wouldn't comment on whether a merger would result in an entirely different publication. Smart, a general-interest magazine for men, has a literary bent. Men magazine has been described by Mr. Kimball as a "field guide for men in their 20s and 30s."

The talks come as publishers are rushing to create new general-interest and life style magazines, with a handful of editors jumping to new jobs at rival titles. Should the negotiations break down, Mr. Kimball said he would follow through with the launch of Men in early 1991. "After 25 years of the woman's movement, men are back. The question is how you design a magazine for that need," he said.

Smart's Mr. Lipstein is looking to shore up the fortunes of his two-year-old magazine. Last week, in a surprise move, Smart's founding editor Terry McDonell was named the new editor of Hearst Corp.'s Esquire magazine. Earlier this month, Mr. Lipstein sold 50% of his New American Magazine Co. to Tokyo-based Japanese Independent Communication Co., bringing in cash to pay debt accumulated in the 1980s.
