Exxon Unit Settles Lawsuit Over Pollution of Waterway

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/28/90

TRENTON, N.J. (AP) -- Exxon Co. USA will spend $2.8 million to improve environmental safeguards at a refinery to settle a lawsuit over the fouling of a New Jersey waterway, officials said. Exxon Co. USA, based in Houston, is a division of New York-based Exxon Corp.

The settlement resolves allegations that Exxon violated terms of its state waste-water discharge permit at its refinery in Bayonne, which discharges into the Kill Van Kull waterway.

The New Jersey Public Interest Research Group and Friends of the Earth filed the lawsuit in May 1989 in federal court in Newark under the Clean Water Act, which provides for such citizen action.

Exxon, without any finding or admission of liability, agreed to invest $2.8 million for "environmentally beneficial mitigation projects" at the Bayonne facility, the public interest group said.

An Exxon spokesman said the agreement "demonstrates Exxon's commitment not only to comply fully with all permit requirements but also to take further responsible steps designed to reduce or eliminate waste materials at their source."
