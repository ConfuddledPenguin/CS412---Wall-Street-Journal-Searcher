West German Wholesale Sales

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/28/90

FRANKFURT, West Germany -- Wholesale sales in West Germany in July jumped 10% from a year earlier in real, or price-adjusted, terms, as the economic and monetary union with East Germany got under way, the Federal Statistics Office said. The price-adjusted data are based on 1985 levels. In nominal, or unadjusted, terms, West German wholesale sales increased 10% to 74 billion marks ($47.45 billion).

The statistics agency said sales of finished products, as well as of raw materials and intermediate goods, rose strongly in what was a "very clearly marked development" last month. The July rise was especially strong, compared with the real 4% annual drop in wholesalers' sales in June, when sales also were down 5% in nominal terms.
