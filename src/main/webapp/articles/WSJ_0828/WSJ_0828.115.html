International Brief -- Komatsu Ltd.: Construction Machine Maker Raises Earnings Estimates

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/28/90

Komatsu Ltd., Japan's top maker of contruction machines, said it revised its sales and earnings projections upward for the fiscal year ending March 31 because of stronger-than-expected domestic demand. Komatsu officials said unconsolidated sales will total about 690 billion yen ($4.7 billion), up from 660 billion yen estimated last May, while pretax earnings will reach 50 billion yen, up from an initial 45 billion yen estimate.

Profit is now seen at around 21.5 billion yen, only slightly higher than 21 billion yen in the original projection.

Officials said their company is more bullish because of unexpectedly strong domestic demand for construction and industrial machinery that they said apparently reflects sustained economic growth.

Komatsu expects sales to amount to some 980 billion yen, up from 950 billion yen estimated in May, while it sees consolidated pretax and earnings at about 77 billion yen and 35 billion yen, respectively. Its previous projections called for pretax earnings of 73 billion yen and profit of 34 billion yen.
