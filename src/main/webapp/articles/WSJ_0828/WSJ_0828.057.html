Investment in Philippines Falls

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/28/90

MANILA, the Philippines -- Foreign investments in the Philippines dropped 73% to 269.5 million pesos ($11.4 million) in June from one billion pesos a year earlier, the Securities and Exchange Commission reported. The June figures on paid-up foreign equity investments in domestic and foreign stock companies showed a 64% drop from 739.1 million pesos in May.

The commission noted that investor confidence significantly waned with the discouraging impact of controversies surrounding the Luzon Petrochemical Corp. and the pull-out of Marubeni Corp. from a planned industrial estate southeast of the capital.

Luzon Petrochemical and its Taiwanese principal, USI Far East Corp., have been accused of trying to obtain behest loans from Philippine financial institutions.
