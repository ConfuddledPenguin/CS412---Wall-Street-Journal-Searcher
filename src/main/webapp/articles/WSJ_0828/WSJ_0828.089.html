Dividend News: Payout Set by Great American Management Of 83.6 Cents a Share

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/28/90

CHICAGO -- Great American Management & Investment Inc. declared a cash dividend of 83.6 cents a share for its minority holders as a way to get out of the savings and loan business. In an unusual step, Great American concurrently declared "a dividend paid in property" of all the shares in a Florida thrift it owns, to a controlling partnership headed by Great American's chairman.

Both dividends are payable tomorrow to holders of record last Friday.

The thrift, Firstate Financial A Savings Bank in Orlando, Fla., now will be owned by Equity Holdings Ltd., which is the majority shareholder in Great American. Equity Holdings is owned by Great American Chairman Samuel Zell and the estate of his late business partner, Robert Lurie.

"Great American feels that being in the S&L business today is looked at in a negative light," said company vice president Susan Obuchowski. "It could hurt Great American in any kind of refinancing or equity offering. The company thought it was in its best interests to no longer be in the savings and loan business."

Ms. Obuchowski said the Orlando thrift, with assets of $210 million, isn't encountering financial difficulty. The move, she said, was designed to relieve Great American of any unpleasant associations with the national savings and loan bailout.

The dividend to minority shareholders in Great American was calculated to be equal on a value-per-share basis, to the Orlando thrift the partnership received.
