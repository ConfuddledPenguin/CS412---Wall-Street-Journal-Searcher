Business Brief -- Loews Corp.: Concern Holds 7.92% Stake In Champion International

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/28/90

Loews Corp. said it holds a 7.92% stake in Champion International Corp.'s common shares outstanding. Loews is a New York-based holding company whose interests include hotels, movie theaters, insurance and tobacco. Champion is a Stamford, Conn., producer of paper and wood. Billionaire investor Lawrence Tisch is the chairman of Loews.

Berkshire Hathaway Inc., controlled by stock-picker Warren Buffett, has a substantial holding in Champion's convertible preferred stock.

In a filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission, Loews said it holds 7,326,100 Champion shares, including shares bought from June 25 to Aug. 23 at prices ranging from $26 to $30.375 per share.

The shares were bought for investment reasons, Loews said.

Late last year, Loews got what it described as "routine" advance clearance from the Federal Trade Commission to purchase the Champion shares as an investment. FTC clearance was needed under the Hart-Scott-Rodino Act for the purchase of more than $15 million of stock.

In composite trading yesterday on the New York Stock Exchange, Loews closed at $92.875, up $1.75, and Champion closed at $27.375, up 37.5 cents.
