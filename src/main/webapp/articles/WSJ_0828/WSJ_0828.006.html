Who's News: United Technologies Shuffles Management At Two Subsidiaries

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/28/90

HARTFORD, Conn. -- United Technologies Corp. reshuffled top management at two subsidiaries, Carrier Corp. and Otis Elevators. When the changes take place Saturday, Karl J. Krapek, president and chief operating officer at Otis, will become chairman, president and chief executive officer at Carrier. He succeeds William A. Wilson, who will become a senior vice president at United Technologies until he retires at the end of March. Jean-Pierre van Rooy, president of Otis's North American operations, will succeed Mr. Krapek.

The reorganization comes as Carrier, a maker of air conditioning and heating units, is coming under intense competition from domestic and foreign manufacturers. United Technologies' second-quarter results reflected an $18 million after-tax charge for a Carrier compressor-plant closing. Also, Carrier's profit dropped sharply because of a weak U.S. market for residential air conditioners.

In 1989, Carrier contributed about $4 billion of United Technologies' $19 billion in revenue. The unit is United Technologies' second largest subsidiary in terms of sales, after aircraft-engine producer Pratt & Whitney.

A spokesman for United Technologies said Mr. Wilson "made his own decision to take early retirement." The spokesman wouldn't speculate as to why Mr. Wilson, who is 56 years old, wants to step down and he said he had no idea what Mr. Wilson will do afterward. Mr. Wilson's decision to retire early prompted the changes, the spokesman said.

Struggling to keep ahead of the competition in heating and cooling systems, Mr. Wilson has been pushing Carrier to improve its product line. In the past three years, Carrier has introduced 93 products, which now account for 70% of the company's North American sales, the spokesman said.

United Technologies makes high-technology products and provides services for the aerospace, automotive and building systems industries. Otis makes elevators and escalators.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, United Technologies rose 62.5 cents to $49.375.
