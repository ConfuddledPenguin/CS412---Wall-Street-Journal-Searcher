Subaru Holders Clear Fuji Deal

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/28/90

CHERRY HILL, N.J. -- Subaru of America Inc. said its shareholders approved an agreement under which Fuji Heavy Industries Ltd. of Japan will buy the 50.4% of the auto distribution company that it doesn't already own in a transaction valued at about $208 million. Under the accord, holders of Subaru, the U.S. distributor for cars made by Fuji, will receive at least $8.50 a share -- $6 from Fuji and an additional amount equal to at least $2.50 that is expected to come from proceeds of the sale of Subaru's portfolio of vehicle leases.

Fuji acted to acquire all of Subaru earlier this year. Most Japanese and European auto makers have moved in recent years to take complete control of their U.S. distribution operations.
