Who's News: Broker Fired by Shearson For Remarks Finds a Job

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/28/90

NEW YORK -- Mal Lowenthal seems to have landed on his feet. The high-producing stockbroker, fired by Shearson Lehman Brothers Inc. for calling the brokerage business "dirty" in an article published in USA Today, was hired by Wertheim Schroder & Co.'s New York office.

Mr. Lowenthal, in the Aug. 3 newspaper article, commented on why there are so many complaints about brokers: "Because this is a dirty business. Our product is money. And money attracts scum." He also said a broker is a "dummy" if he focuses only on his own firm's research ideas. Shearson, an American Express Co. unit, said in response that Mr. Lowenthal's views didn't "conform with the firm's philosophy of quality service."

Wertheim Schroder, by contrast, yesterday called Mr. Lowenthal a "consummate professional" with a good record and "an excellent group of clients." The firm said, "He shares our philosophy of putting our clients' interests first."
