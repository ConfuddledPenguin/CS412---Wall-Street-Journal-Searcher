Technology: Hewlett-Packard Co. Unveils Printer Model To Rival Dot-Matrix

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/28/90

PALO ALTO, Calif. -- Hewlett-Packard Co., continuing its aggressive marketing, introduced what it called an improved DeskJet computer printer model at its lowest price ever. The company said its DeskJet 500 will carry a list price of $729 and will offer better performance than the two DeskJet printers it replaces. The newer of those two printers was introduced in May 1989 and lists for $995.

The DeskJet uses a non-impact technique that results in sharper print and quicker printing than inexpensive dot-matrix printers, which are often criticized for the quality of their printing. Hewlett-Packard makes its DeskJet printers in the U.S., while Japanese and other overseas manufacturers dominate the dot-matrix business.

Analysts said the new printer, which is expected to retail for less than $500, should steal business from dot-matrix printers. "H-P is meeting an important price point," said John Seybold, editor of Seybold's Report on Desktop Publishing. "This is deliberately priced to take a very significant share from dot-matrix printers."

Hewlett-Packard will take in an estimated $1.4 billion in revenue from computer printer sales this year. Besides nonimpact printers, the company sells the more expensive laser printers, which are credited with sparking a boom in the publication of computer-produced documents and newsletters.
