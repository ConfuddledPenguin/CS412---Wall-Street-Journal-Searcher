Business Brief -- Excel Industries: Firm to Purchase Division Of Johnson Controls Inc.

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/28/90

Excel Industries Inc., Elkhart, Ind., said it agreed to purchase the door-systems division of Johnson Controls Inc. of Milwaukee for an undisclosed amount of cash. The maker of windows for cars, trucks and other vehicles said the division is expected to have annual sales this year of $80 million. Excel said the division will boost its 1990 revenue to about $300 million. In 1989, Excel had sales of $259.9 million.

Johnson Controls said the sale reflects its desire to focus on its automotive seating and related interior-systems operations. "We don't believe we need to be in the door-systems technology business," a spokeswoman said.

Excel said the acquisition of door systems, which supplies window-regulator systems for the automotive market, will "strongly position the company in the rollup glass area." The company said it has worked closely with the Johnson Controls division in the past two years in developing a modular door cartridge, which combines all internal functions of side-window, rollup glass in a module that can be assembled into the sheet metal door.

The transaction is scheduled to close on Oct. 1.
