BellSouth Corp. Agrees To Join Partnership For Paging System

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/28/90

ATLANTA -- BellSouth Corp.'s paging unit and CellTelCo Nationwide Paging Partnership agreed in principle to form a joint venture to operate a nationwide paging service. CellTelCo, a Memphis-based partnership that owns one of the three licenses granted to build and operate nationwide paging systems, will contribute its license in exchange for 50% of the new company. BellSouth's MobileComm paging unit will provide network facilities and capital and will market the paging service. MobileComm expects to start offering service after reaching a definitive agreement.

Under the agreement in principle, MobileComm will offer to buy an equity interest in CellTelCo. In addition to a definitive agreement, the joint venture is subject to approval of CellTelCo partners, the Federal Communications Commission and other regulators.

Also, MobileComm and Robert Blow, general partner of CellTelCo, announced that they executed an option agreement under which MobileComm can acquire R.B. Management Services' 20% partnership interest in CellTelCo.
