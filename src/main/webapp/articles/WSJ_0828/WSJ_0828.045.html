MEI to Acquire 3 Beauty Salons

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/28/90

MINNEAPOLIS -- MEI Diversified Inc. said it agreed definitively to acquire three companies that operate professional beauty salons in department stores for $50 million, plus assumption of certain liabilities. MEI said it will own 80% of a new company formed to acquire Glemby Co. of New York; Essanelle Salon Co.; and Maxim's, both of Minneapolis. The remaining 20% of the new company will be owned by Minneapolis-based Regis Corp., the current owner of both Essanelle and Maxim's.

MEI said the combined companies have annual sales of about $400 million and will operate more than 2,000 salons in the U.S. and Canada.

The acquisition is scheduled to be completed by Friday.
