Consumer Confidence Sank In August to a 7-Year Low

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/28/90

NEW YORK -- Consumer confidence sank to a seven-year low as the index compiled by the Conference Board plunged almost 18 points to 83.8 in August from July's 101.7. The index is now 31 points below the year-ago period and the decline steeper than that registered immediately after the October 1987 stock market crash.

Fabian Linden, executive director of the board's consumer research center, said that a recession is more likely now than it has been in many years, given the economic uncertainties in the Middle East.

While consumer confidence has been edging down since the year began, in this latest survey consumers expressed increasing concern about current economic conditions and a marked pessimism in their expectations for the next six months. The monthly survey, conducted for the Conference Board by National Family Opinion Inc. of Toledo, Ohio, uses 1985 as a base year of 100 for the index and covers 5,000 households throughout the U.S.

The proportion of consumers who feel conditions are "bad" rose three points to almost 24%; those who feel conditions are "good," fell three points to 20%. Almost 19% of those surveyed fear conditions will worsen in the next six months, up from 12% in July.

Opinions about employment were especially gloomy. Still, buying plans retreated only modestly, the board said, citing respondents' plans for buying homes and cars.
