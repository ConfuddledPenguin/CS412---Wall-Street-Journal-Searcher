AMR's American Air Wins a Court Order Against United Air

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 08/28/90

A federal judge in Fort Worth, Texas, granted a request by American Airlines to prohibit United Airlines from making any immediate changes to its flight schedules at Chicago's O'Hare International Airport. American alleged that the planned changes violated a federal agreement and would give United an unfair advantage in Chicago. Judge John McBride issued a temporary restraining order barring United, a unit of UAL Corp., Chicago, from changing its schedules before the court can hold a full hearing on the matter next Tuesday.

Because of the high volume of traffic at O'Hare, the federal government restricts the number of takeoffs and landings per hour. As a result, the government in the past has given United and American permission to coordinate their flight schedules to ease delays and prevent overburdening the air traffic control system.

American, a unit of AMR Corp., Fort Worth, alleged that United planned to move up the departure times of many of its flights as of Oct. 1. American said that United had already filed a notice stating its plans and that the changes would put American at a competitive disadvantage.

United called American's complaint "without basis in fact." The carrier said it was only responding to changes American had said it was planning to make in its Chicago flight schedules as of Sept. 15. United also said the proper forum for solving the dispute is the Federal Aviation Administration, which regulates air traffic at O'Hare.

The FAA could step in if it believed any of the changes contemplated would result in worse delays or additional problems for air traffic controllers. United and American are the dominant airlines at O'Hare. United operates 729 flights daily out of Chicago; American operates 581. Most of the changes contemplated affected flights departing between 7 p.m. and 8 p.m.
