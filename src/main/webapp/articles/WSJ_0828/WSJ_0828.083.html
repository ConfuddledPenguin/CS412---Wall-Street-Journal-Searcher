E-Systems Inc. Pleads Guilty to Fraud; Firm To Pay $4.6 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/28/90

DALLAS -- E-Systems Inc. pleaded guilty to charges that it falsified test results and defrauded the U.S. government on contracts for Army field radios. It agreed to pay $4.6 million to settle civil and criminal charges. An E-Systems spokesman said the settlement will allow the electronic systems and products company to continue doing business with the U.S. government. "All of our problems have been cleared up," he said.

E-Systems said it will pay a $2 million fine and $1.8 million in restitution to settle criminal charges filed in a Tampa, Fla., federal court as well as $850,000 to settle civil charges brought by the Department of Justice. As part of the plea agreement, E-Systems said it will drop its pending $38 million lawsuit against the federal government.

E-Systems took an $8.6 million write-off against its second-quarter earnings in anticipation of the guilty plea, the spokesman said.

The tactical field radios were supplied to the Army by E-Systems through its Memcor division, which has since been discontinued. The indictment charged that E-Systems and others falsified performance records of the radios and concealed repairs and defects.

This month, three former Memcor managers and a former supervisor were charged by a federal grand jury with falsifying records and test results to conceal failures and defects in radios supplied to the U.S. Army.

A production supervisor, Sadonna S. Hobbs, was convicted Jan. 31 of perjury relating to denials of her activity in connection with suits filed by E-Systems before the Armed Services Board of Contract Appeals. She is serving a 16-month federal prison term.
