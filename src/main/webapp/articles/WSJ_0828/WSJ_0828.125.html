Oracle Says Review Lets Stand Results For Last Full Year

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/28/90

REDWOOD CITY, Calif. -- Oracle Systems Corp. said its review of certain revenue transactions has not resulted in any restatement of either revenue or net income for its year ended May 31. However, the company said the review has resulted in restatements of the quarterly results for the fiscal year. It said the changes result from "the company's determination that a portion of revenue and related sales expenses reported in some fiscal quarters should have been recognized as revenue and expenses in different periods."

The announcement by Oracle, which makes computer software, came after the close of trading yesterday. A spokeswoman declined to elaborate on the terse prepared statement.

Oracle posted record revenue of $970.8 million and net of $117.4 million, or 86 cents a share, for fiscal 1990. But in recent months, the company's marketplace success -- it sells popular database software for minicomputers and mainframes -- has been partly obscured by concern over its accounting methods.

In March, the company cited an accounting glitch as the reason for lower-than-expected revenue in its third quarter. The day after the announcement, the company's stock fell 31% on record-breaking volume for a single over-the-counter issue.

As restated, Oracle's revenue was unchanged for its first quarter. Revenue totaled $209 million for the second quarter, compared with $224.6 million originally reported. For the third quarter, revenue totaled $245.6 million, compared with $236.4 million reported. For the fourth quarter, revenue totaled $340.8 million, compared with $334.4 million.

As restated, Oracle's net was unchanged for the first quarter. For the second quarter, net was $19 million, compared with $28.5 million reported. For the third quarter, net was $29.8 million, compared with $24.3 million. For the fourth quarter, net was $56.8 million, compared with $52.9 million.
