Business Brief -- Cyprus Minerals Co.: Mining Company to Spend $92.5 Million on Expansion

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/28/90

Cyprus Minerals Co. said it will spend $92.5 million to modernize and expand its Miami, Ariz., copper smelter. Cyprus said the expansion would command a sizable portion of the Denver mining company's 1991 capital expenditure budget, which has been about $200 million a year for the past couple of years. The expansion, which is designed to make Cyprus self-sufficient in copper smelting capacity, will be completed in mid-1992, the company estimated. The move will increase Cyprus's concentrate smelting capacity to 650,000 tons from 450,000 tons annually.

The completed facility will reduce the consumption of electric power, the biggest cost in producing copper, a spokesman said. The upgrading of the facility has been part of the company's long-term plans since it bought the smelter from Inspiration Resources Corp. in 1988.

Cyprus said that regardless of the plans for the expanded smelter, the company will "meet its obligations" in a copper-concentrate smelting contract it has with Magma Copper Co., which runs through mid-1996. Magma processes about 150,000 tons of copper concentrates annually for Cyprus at a San Manuel, Ariz., smelter.
