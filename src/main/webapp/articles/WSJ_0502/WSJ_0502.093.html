Savings-Bond Interest Rate Is Raised for Next 6 Months

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/02/90

WASHINGTON (AP) -- U.S. savings bonds will earn interest at a 7.01% rate from May 1 through Oct. 31, up slightly from 6.98% during the previous six months, the Treasury Department said. Treasurer Catalina Villalpando said the new rate "will keep savings bonds in the competitive range with other market-sensitive instruments during the coming months."

Savings-bond interest rates are changed each May 1 and Nov. 1 to reflect open-market rates. They are based on 85% of the yield from five-year Treasury securities, which have averaged 8.25%.

Bonds must be held at least five years to earn market-based rates. However, holders of savings bonds are guaranteed a minimum rate, which currently is 6%.
