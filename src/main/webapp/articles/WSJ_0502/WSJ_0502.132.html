Business Brief -- Temco National Corp.: Foster Medical Supply Inc. Of Massachusetts Buys Unit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/02/90

Temco National Corp., Columbus, Ohio, said it sold its Wendt-Bristol Medical Supply division to Foster Medical Supply Inc., Dedham, Mass., for $10 million. Temco said it will have a nonrecurring profit on the transaction of about $1 million, or 19 cents a share, in the second quarter. In addition, repayment of bank loans with the sales proceeds will cut interest costs enough to increase profit by 12 cents a share annually, the firm said.

Temco said it intends to concentrate on manufacture of health care products. The division sold by Temco was a wholesaler of medical products with annual sales of $25 million to $30 million.

Separately, Temco said it had a 1989 loss from continuing operations of $375,775, or eight cents a share, on revenue of $23.4 million. In 1988, Temco earned $45,867, or three cents a share, from continuing operations on revenue of $17.3 million. The firm had a loss in 1989 of $961,253, or 20 cents a share, compared with a loss of $168,992, or 13 cents a share.
