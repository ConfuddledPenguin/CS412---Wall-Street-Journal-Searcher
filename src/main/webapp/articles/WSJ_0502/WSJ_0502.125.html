International Brief -- German Wings: Small West German Airline Halts All Flight Operations

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/02/90

German Wings Luftfahrtunternehmen G.m.b.H., a small, struggling West German airline, said that it halted all flights just before midnight Monday and that it will hold a news conference this morning at its headquarters near Munich. Yesterday was a holiday in West Germany. The one-year-old airline, which has been searching for a European partner for the past four months, said another small West German airline, Aero Lloyd, is accepting passengers holding tickets for German Wings flights.

"All flights have been stopped," a German Wings spokesman said. "We don't know anything more." Sources said the airline has begun bankruptcy-law proceedings, but this couldn't be confirmed.

News reports have said that German Wings had a loss of 50 million marks ($29.8 million) in its first year of operations. Just one week ago, the airline said it planned to announce the name of a new partner and to report its first-year financial data at a news conference scheduled for last Friday, but on Thursday it canceled the session.
