Letters to the Editor: Athletes Mistreated

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/02/90

I am glad to see Frederick C. Klein take the schools to task for the gross mistreatment of their athletes (Leisure & Arts, March 23). The schools, pro teams and networks all make big bucks and the players are signed to pristine non-compensatory contracts, we are led to believe. The young basketball player who died on the court recently had a $1 million disability insurance policy (not life) for which he had paid $6,600 out of pocket. I don't know many collegians who can afford that kind of money for anything, much less insurance. And now Notre Dame announces a $6 million adder in TV revenues over the next few years by virtue of a new contract with NBC. That comes to more than $10,000 per year per man on the roster, whether fourth-stringer or scholarship hero. It doesn't seem right.

Charles M. Hart

Cranford, N.J.
