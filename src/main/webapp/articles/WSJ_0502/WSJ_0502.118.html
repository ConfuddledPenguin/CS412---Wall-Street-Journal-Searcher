International: Bank of Japan Announces Major Restructuring Plan

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 05/02/90

TOKYO -- The Bank of Japan announced a major restructuring aimed at strengthening its monetary operations and promoting the monitoring of the activities of financial institutions at home and abroad. The central bank's reorganization, which goes into effect May 28, will reduce the number of its offices, departments and institutes to 16 from 18.

The bank said a Market Operations division will be established to monitor both money and capital markets and to execute daily monetary operations in a comprehensive manner. A Bank Relations division and Capital Markets division will oversee activities of financial institutions at home and abroad.

A Financial and Payment System Department will be established to cope with the increased risks to the financial system posed by the trends toward financial deregulation, globalization and computerization, the bank said. And an International Department will combine the functions of the current Foreign Department with the overseas research function of the current Research and Statistics Department.

To expand the central bank's international network, the representative office in Hong Kong will be made the Bank of Japan's third chief representative office, for Asia and Oceania, joining New York, for North and South America, and London, for Europe, Africa and the Middle East. Another representative office will be added to Washington, D.C., bringing the total number of offices to six.
