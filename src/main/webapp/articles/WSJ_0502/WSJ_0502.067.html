Society Corp. to Pay $5.6 Million in Suit On Trustcorp Merger

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/02/90

CLEVELAND -- Society Corp. said it settled a shareholder suit pending against Trustcorp Inc., Toledo, before Trustcorp was merged into Society last Jan. 5. The settlement, subject to approval by the federal court in Toledo, calls for Society to pay $5.6 million, the company said.

Society, a bank holding company with assets of $15.4 billion, said the settlement isn't expected to have a significant impact on 1990 earnings. Society said it is seeking reimbursement under insurance in force at the time the suit was filed.

Early in 1989, six shareholders or former shareholders of Trustcorp filed suit against Trustcorp and certain of its officers and directors. The suit alleged that misleading statements about Trustcorp's loan portfolio and general condition caused the plaintiffs to lose money by purchasing Trustcorp shares that were artificially inflated in price because company statements failed to reveal problems in the loan portfolio. The stock subsequently fell when the bank holding company made large additions to loan-loss reserves.

In national over-the-counter trading, Society closed at $28.75, down 75 cents.
