Business Brief -- Occidental Petroleum Corp.: Two Units Ordered to Pay $28 Million to Coastal Unit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/02/90

A federal court in Cheyenne, Wyo., ordered two units of Occidental Petroleum Corp., Los Angeles, to pay $28 million to Colorado Interstate Gas Co. for wrongful interference in a contractual relationship between Colorado Interstate and a gas supplier. The judgment was recently disclosed by Occidental in a regulatory filing. Occidental also said it has appealed the judgment to the 10th Circuit Court in Denver.

The litigation began in 1984 when Colorado Interstate, a unit of Houston-based Coastal Corp., sued Natural Gas Pipeline Co. of America and NGPL-Trailblazer Inc. for alleged antitrust violations, breach of contract and wrongful interference.

Last September, the 10th Circuit Court overturned a lower court ruling that ordered the Occidental units to pay $412 million to Colorado Interstate on the antitrust and breach-of-contract charges. But the circuit court upheld Colorado Interstate's wrongful interference claim. The two sides disputed the amount of the claim.

The Wyoming ruling affirmed Colorado Interstate's claim that the judgment was for $28 million, including interest. Occidental maintained that the amount should be only $8 million. Separately, a Coastal spokesman said Colorado Interstate petitioned the U.S. Supreme Court last week to review the circuit court's ruling on the antitrust and breach of contract allegations.
