Energy Department Plans To Resume Tritium Output

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/02/90

WASHINGTON -- After a halt of more than two years to resolve safety and management problems, the Energy Department tentatively plans to resume producing a key component of nuclear bombs by late December or early January. When the government in 1988 closed down its tritium production reactors at the department's Savannah River facility in South Carolina, fears were raised that the nation's nuclear weapons arsenal would be weakened. Tritium, a perishable radioactive gas, is used to enhance the power of nuclear bombs.

One reactor at Savannah River is scheduled to begin low-power testing in December, with production to resume soon thereafter. All three reactors at the facility are planned to go back in production by the end of next year. The restart plans, which have been delayed, are contingent on an assessment of environmental implications.

"Until I am assured that the environment, safety and health considerations have been satisfactorily addressed, I will not approve restart of the reactors at Savannah River," Energy Secretary James Watkins said.

Improvements already completed or planned at Savannah River, which is run by Westinghouse Electric Corp., include increased training for employees and changes designed to make operations at Savannah River more like those at commercial nuclear facilities.
