New Securities Issues

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/02/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATE

Philip Morris Cos. -- $150 million of senior notes, due Aug. 15, 1991, were priced at par to yield 9%. The non-callable notes were priced at a spread of 53 basis points above the bond equivalent yield of the Treasury's one-year bill. Rated single-A-3 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and Single-A by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets.

MUNICIPALS

Minneapolis, Minn., Housing Redevelopment Authority $159.4 million of health care system revenue bonds were tentatively priced by a Goldman Sachs & Co. group. The issue, for the city of St. Paul, consists of Series 1990 A, Series 1990 B, and Series 1990 C. The $100 million Series A bonds comprise serial bonds that were priced to yield from 6.30% in 1991 to 7.375% in 2002. Term bonds due in 2005 were priced with a 7.40% coupon at 99.085 to yield 7.50%; term bonds due in 2014 were priced with a 7.40% coupon at 98 to yield 7.58%. The Series A bonds, MBIA-insured, have a triple-A rating from both Moody's and S&P. The $38 million Series B bonds comprise serial bonds that yield from 6.60% in 1991 to 7.60% in 2001. Term bonds due in 2014 were priced with an 8% coupon to yield 8.15%. The Series B bonds are rated single-A by Moody's and single-A-minus by S&P. As for the $21.4 million Series C bonds, they comprise serial bonds that were priced to yield from 7.00% in 1995 to 7.60% in 2001. Term bonds due in 2019 were priced with a 8% coupon to yield 8.15%. The Series C bonds are rated single-A by Moody's and single-A-Minus by S&P.

Central Arizona Water Conservation District -- $152.5 million of revenue bonds were tentatively priced by a John Nuveen & Co. group. The Series 1990 A issue, for the Central Arizona Project contract, was priced to yield from 6.60% in 1994 to 7.624% on term bonds due in 2010. Zero-coupon capital appreciation bonds were priced to yield 7.25% at maturity in 2008. The issue has been rated single-A-1 by Moody's and double-A-minus by S&P.

Chicago, Ill. -- $75 million of gas supply revenue bonds were tentatively priced by a Shearson Lehman Hutton Inc. group. The issue, for Peoples Gas Light & Coke Co., 1990 Series A, is subject to the alternative minimum tax. The bonds, due May 1, 2020, carry 8.10% coupons and were priced at par. A rating of double-A-3 is expected from Moody's and double-A-minus from S&P.

Maryland Health and Higher Education Facilities Authority -- $68.6 million of revenue bonds were tentatively priced by a Goldman Sachs & Co. group. The bonds, for Sinai Hospital of Baltimore, were priced to yield from 6.10% in 1991 to 7.53% in 2019. The AMBAC-insured issue has triple-A ratings from both Moody's and S&P.

MORTGAGES

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $750.1 million of real estate mortgage investment conduit securities were offered in 23 classes by Bear Stearns & Co. The offering, series 1990-60, is backed by Fannie Mae's 9 1/2% securities. There were no immediate details from Bear Stearns.

First Boston Corp. -- $500 million of real estate mortgage investment conduit securities were offered through its Collateralized Mortgage Securities Corp. unit. The issue, CMSC 1990-3, is backed by Government National Mortgage Assocation's 9 1/2% securities. No other information on the issue was immediately available from First Boston.

Nomura Mortgage Capital Corp. -- $400 million of real estate mortgage investment conduit securities were offered in 14 classes by Nomura Securities International Inc. The offering, Series 1990-1, and backed by Ginnie Mae's 10% securities, has been structured to yield from 9 1/2% to 10%.

Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $300 million of real estate mortgage investment conduit securities were offered in 14 classes through Citibank. The offering, Freddie Mac's series 170, and backed by Freddie Mac's 10% securities, was structured to yield from 8% to 10%.

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $300 million of real estate mortgage investment conduit securities were offered in 15 classes by First Boston Corp. The offering, Series 1990-61, is backed by Fannie Mae's 10% securities. No details were available.
