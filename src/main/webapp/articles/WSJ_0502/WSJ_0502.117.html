Business Brief -- Braniff Inc.: Accord on Sale of Rights To Acquire Jets Is Reached

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/02/90

Braniff Inc., operating under bankruptcy-court protection, said it agreed to sell rights to buy as many as 74 Airbus Industrie A320 jets for $50 million to Airbus. Airbus, a European aircraft consortium, is essentially buying back the rights to its own planes.

Also, Airbus is waiving $32 million in bills that Braniff owes it for spare parts. As part of the agreement, Braniff will be returning the spare parts to Airbus.

Braniff, which has been grounded since last fall, said the purchase is subject to higher and better offers, as well as approval by the federal bankruptcy court in Orlando, Fla., where Braniff is based.

The company said it has until July 26 to market the short-range A320 contract rights and the spare parts. It said that, among other things, competing offers must be at least 10% higher than the Airbus offer.
