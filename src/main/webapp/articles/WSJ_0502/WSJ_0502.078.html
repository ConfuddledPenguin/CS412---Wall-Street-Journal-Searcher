OTC Focus: Stocks Shrug Off Falling Bond Market And Post Slight Gains in Quiet Session

  • Author: Anne Newman
  • Date: 05/02/90

NEW YORK -- Over-the-counter stocks shrugged off the declining bond market and hung on to slim gains in quiet trading. The Nasdaq Composite Index closed up 1.78 at 421.85, a 0.42% rise, slipping from an earlier gain of 2.08. Volume rose to 124.1 million shares from 105.8 million shares Monday.

"When the bond market weakened, we hung in there," said Lance Zipper, head of OTC trading at Kidder Peabody. "The breadth is a little bit better," he added, noting that advancing stocks outpaced declining shares, 1,048 to 852.

However, Mr. Zipper and William Sulya, director of OTC trading at A.G. Edwards & Sons in St. Louis, noted that most of the day's trading was among professional investors hunting selective bargains.

Oracle Systems dominated action late in the day after word spread that the software maker's chief executive officer and president, Lawrence Ellison, will meet this afternoon with securities analysts in New York. The day's most active stock, Oracle dropped 1 1/8 to 14 3/4 on 2.7 million shares, 59% more turnover than average.

Analysts, who are divided in guessing what the company will say, suggested the stock fell amid nervousness about the meeting. Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette software analyst Scott Smith said in an interview that he expects the Belmont, Calif., company to report a positive long-term outlook. Merrill Lynch analyst Stephen T. McClellan said he expects "some handholding" amid caution the company might not achieve its short-term objectives.

MCI Communications led the Nasdaq utility index, which outpaced other Nasdaq industry groups. The utility index gained 0.8%, closing up 4.84 at 584.42. Peter Coolidge, senior OTC trader at Kidder Peabody, said he saw buying of selective utility shares in the wake of a sell-off in that group amid fears of higher interest rates.

MCI Communications, the day's second most active stock, rose 3/8 to 35 3/8 after announcing it received a $375 million, five-year contract to provide long-distance services to the Navy Resale and Services Support Office. The contract is expected to produce $75 million in annual revenue.

Aspect Telecommunications traded actively after an initial public offering Monday of two million shares priced at $14.50 each. Shares of the San Jose, Calif., equipment concern closed unchanged at 15 1/4 on nearly 1.4 million shares.

Apple Computer added 1/4 to close at 39 5/8 after unveiling a set of products that links certain Digital Equipment systems with Apple's Macintosh computers and AppleTalk networks.

Merrill Corp. rose 1 1/4 to 8 3/8 after the St. Paul, Minn., provider of typesetting, distribution and printing services said it expects to report record revenue and net income for the fiscal first quarter ended Monday.

Syncor International advanced 3/8 to 6 7/8 after the Los Angeles pharmacy service company announced it will buy back 500,000 shares, or 5% of its common stock outstanding.

Amgen jumped 1 1/2 to 65 1/2 after this newspaper reported yesterday that the Thousand Oaks, Calif., biotechnology company won a round in a patent infringement suit involving Genetics Institute and Japan's Chugai Pharmaceutical Co. Shares of Genetics Institute of Cambridge, Mass., closed unchanged at 36 1/4.

International Game Technology gained 1 5/8 to 34 3/8 on 199,000 shares, more than four times average volume. The Reno, Nev., maker of casino games reported net income for the second quarter ended March 31 was 58 cents a share, up from 24 cents a share a year earlier.

Syntellect added 1 7/8 to close at 19 3/8 after the company's executive vice president and chief financial officer, C. Philip Chapman, spoke bullishly about the outlook for electronic voice processing before a Hambrecht & Quist technology conference in San Francisco, according to Dow Jones Professional Investor Report. The company went public in late March at $15 a share.

FNB Rochester gained 3/4 to close at 11 after announcing it received an unsolicited acquisition offer. The Rochester, N.Y., bank holding company declined to identify the suitor and said no formal agreement had been reached.

Among the day's losers was System Software, which tumbled 3 1/4 to 20 3/4 after Mr. Smith of Donaldson Lufkin pulled the Chicago software company from the firm's list of recommended stocks to buy, according to Professional Investor Report. Noting the stock has risen 33% since December, Mr. Smith said he expects the company's business to slow in the second half of the year.

Employers Casualty fell 2 3/4 to 7 1/4 after the Dallas insurance concern said it's omitting a quarterly cash dividend for June because of poor operating results in the first quarter. The company last paid a 10-cent dividend in March. The company reported a first-quarter loss of 91 cents a share, compared with profit of 55 cents a share a year earlier.

Helix Technology lost 1/2 to close at 9 after the Waltham, Mass., maker of cryogenic refrigeration equipment reported net income for the first quarter of three cents a share, down from 37 cents a share the year before. The company also authorized the purchase of as many as 350,000 of its 2.5 million shares outstanding.
