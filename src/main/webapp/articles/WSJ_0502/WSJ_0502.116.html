Business Brief -- Freeport-McMoRan Inc.: Chevron U.S.A. to Sell Oil, Gas Reserves to Partnership

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/02/90

Freeport-McMoRan Resource Partners, a New Orleans-based limited partnership affiliate of Freeport-McMoRan Inc., said it agreed to acquire oil and gas reserves from Chevron U.S.A. Inc. for $137 million in cash plus $13 million in development costs already incurred. Chevron U.S.A. is a subsidiary of Chevron Corp. The partnership estimates it will spend about $100 million for the development of the reserves. Currently estimated at 35 million to 50 million barrels of oil and eight billion cubic feet of natural gas, the reserves are located in the same caprock reservoir on Main Pass Block 299 as the partnership's sulphur reserves. They were discovered when the partnership drilled for sulphur exploration wells.

Chevron, which developed the discovery, has retained the right, under certain conditions, to participate in the proceeds of future production if the price or volumes exceed certain levels.

Main Pass Block 299 is located offshore Louisiana in the Gulf of Mexico, about 90 miles southeast of New Orleans.
