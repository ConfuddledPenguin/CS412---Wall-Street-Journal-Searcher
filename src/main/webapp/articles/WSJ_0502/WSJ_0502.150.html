Trusthouse Forte Agrees To Buy Watergate Hotel

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/02/90

WASHINGTON -- The North American subsidiary of Trusthouse Forte PLC has agreed to buy the Watergate Hotel in Washington, D.C. from the British Coal Board, a pension fund that owns the 237-room luxury hotel. Terms of the purchase aren't final, a Trusthouse Forte spokesman said. Trusthouse Forte, a British hotel and catering company, said the hotel recently underwent a $13 million renovation, and an additional $5 million improvement plan is scheduled. Financing for the purchase will be arranged in Japan by Nikko Securities Co. International.

The purchase would include the hotel and health spa on about an acre of land, and not the apartments and other portions of the Watergate complex, the spokesman said. Average room rates at the hotel range between $200 and $300 a night.

The Watergate lent its name to the 1972 break-in and electronic bugging incident at the nearby headquarters of the Democratic National Committee, which eventually culminated in the resignation of President Richard M. Nixon.

The British Coal Board has sold off most of its U.S. property portfolio, which it amassed over the past decade when the real estate market was generally stronger.
