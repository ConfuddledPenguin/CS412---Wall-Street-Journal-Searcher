International Brief -- National Employers' Mutual Ltd.: Insurer, Pressed by Creditors, Plans to Sell Its Operations

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/02/90

National Employers' Mutual General Insurance Association Ltd. of Britain said it plans to dispose of its operations world-wide in an effort to satisfy creditors. The move follows the company's statement over the weekend to regulators in Victoria, Australia, that it isn't able to continue paying claims on policies in Australia. The British insurer, which as a mutual group isn't listed on any stock exchange, is proposing to establish an arrangement under a U.K. law that would allow it to sell its assets without the pressure of a legal liquidation. Australian insurance officials have begun legal action to liquidate the company.

Stanley Hallowell, the company's managing director, said that the move was triggered by the Australian claims. He declined to say what the company hopes to earn from the asset sales.

The Australian business was closed in 1985. Australian regulators say the company has liabilities there of more than 200 million Australian dollars (US$150 million).
