Interest Rates Rise Again At Citicorp Weekly Auction

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/02/90

NEW YORK -- Interest rates rose again at Citicorp's weekly auction of commercial paper, in part reflecting concern by debt-ratings agencies about the banking company's credit quality and capital adequacy. The average rate rose to 8.451% at Citicorp's $50 million weekly auction of 91-day commercial paper, or corporate IOUs, from 8.3725% at last week's sale. Last week's rate had risen nearly 15 basis points in the wake of an announcement from Moody's Investors Service Inc. that it was considering lowering its debt ratings of Citicorp.

Late last week, Standard & Poor's Corp. lowered its senior debt ratings for Citicorp, although commercial paper was affirmed at single-A-1 plus.

At the latest auction, bids totaling $405 million were submitted for the 91-day paper. Accepted bids ranged from 8.448% to 8.454%.

Citicorp also said that the average rate rose to 8.49% at its $50 million auction of 182-day commercial paper from 8.395% at last week's sale.

Bids totaling $390 million were submitted. Accepted bids ranged from 8.485% to 8.495%.

The bank holding company will auction another $50 million in each maturity next Tuesday.
