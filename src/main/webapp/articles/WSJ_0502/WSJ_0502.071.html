Quantum and Maxtor, Makers of Disk Drives, Post Strong Results

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 05/02/90

Two makers of high-performance hard-disk drives reported strong earnings gains in the fiscal fourth quarter ended March 31, reflecting strong demand from makers of personal computers, workstations and computer networks. Quantum Corp., based in Milpitas, Calif., said net income rose 86% to $12.8 million, or 46 cents a share, from $6.9 million, or 27 cents a share, a year earlier. Sales rose 67% to $129.8 million from $77.7 million. A competitor, Maxtor Corp., based in San Jose, Calif., said net jumped more than fourfold to $6.5 million, or 30 cents a share, from $1.4 million, or seven cents a share, a year earlier. Sales climbed 45% to $141.6 million from $97.7 million.

Both companies reported strong gains in sales of new 3 1/2-inch disk products, earnings from certain discontinued products and continued strong demand for older products from computer makers. Quantum also announced that its board approved a plan to buy back up to three million shares of its common stock in the open market.

Quantum reported fiscal 1990 earnings of $47.2 million, or $1.71 a share, up nearly fourfold from $12.9 million, or 45 cents a share, in fiscal 1989. Sales more than doubled to $446.3 million from $208 million. For the fiscal year, Maxtor reported net of $18.9 million, or 90 cents a share, more than three times the $5.2 million, or 26 cents a share, earned in fiscal 1989. Sales rose 40% to $491.1 million from $351 million.
