Who's News: Ondaatje Resigns From the Boards Of Pagurian, Hees

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/02/90

TORONTO -- Christopher Ondaatje, chairman of Pagurian Corp. and vice chairman of Hees International Bancorp, resigned from the boards of both companies. A Hees executive said the merchant banking concern, which is indirectly controlled by Toronto financiers Peter and Edward Bronfman, is in the early stages of finding replacements for Mr. Ondaatje on the boards.

Mr. Ondaatje created Pagurian as a book publishing concern in 1967 and expanded it into an investment holding company with interests in Canada and overseas. In December 1988, Mr. Ondaatje sold control of Pagurian to Hees in exchange for Hees common shares. He was named Hees vice-chairman at that time.

Mr. Ondaatje, 57 years old, said he is stepping down from the Pagurian and Hees boards because "it is time that I did something else." He plans to become more actively involved in publishing in Canada and less so in the financial services sector.

He said his principal task since selling control of Pagurian has been to gradually hand over management of Pagurian to executives of Hees, Brascan Ltd. and Edper Enterprises Ltd. Brascan is a resource concern controlled by the Bronfmans through Edper.

Mr. Ondaatje said he currently holds five million Pagurian shares and 1.5 million Hees shares. At last report, Pagurian had shares outstanding of about 84.9 million, and Hees had roughly 79.3 million.

Mr. Ondaatje didn't elaborate on his plans in publishing, but he said he was "very disappointed" by the sale last December of the Financial Times of Canada by Southam Inc. to Thomson Corp. Mr. Ondaatje had expressed an interest in buying the weekly financial newspaper.
