Ex-Day-Care Operator Pleads Guilty to Fraud In Communications

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 05/02/90

CHICAGO -- A former day-care center operator pleaded guilty in U.S. District Court here to defrauding telecommunications companies out of nearly $600,000 as the head of a nationwide computer-hacking ring. Leslie Lynne Doucette, 36 years old, working with 152 alleged co-schemers, fraudulently obtained 481 telephone credit-card codes, according to the plea agreement, and then published them in voice-mail systems that were accessed by other hackers. The scheme was discovered when a suburban Chicago real-estate brokerage complained that its voice-mail system had been invaded by the hackers, who had changed the system's passwords.

Federal prosecutors charged Ms. Doucette last year in the scheme, which according to the indictment resulted in a loss of $1.6 million of telephone and computer services from the communications concerns, including American Telephone & Telegraph Co., ITT Corp., and Western Union Corp.

The one count to which Ms. Doucette eventually pleaded guilty carries a maximum sentence of ten years in prison, and a $1 million fine.
