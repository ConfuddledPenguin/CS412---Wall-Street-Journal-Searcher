Digital Negotiates Business, Technology Accord With Ingres

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/02/90

MAYNARD,Mass. -- Digital Equipment Corp. is negotiating an equity investment and technology agreement with fast-growing software maker Ingres Corp. Ingres, the Alameda, Calif.-based software developer, which changed its name last year from Relational Technology Inc., had sales of $130.7 million in the fiscal year ended June 30. It develops relational data base software for Digital computers and other computer designs.

A Digital spokesman said the two companies are discussing "both a technology and a business partnership" that would result in a minority investment by Digital.

Digital rarely makes investments in other companies, though it has broken that tradition recently for access to new technologies. Last year, it bought a 5.7% stake in chip maker MIPS Computer Systems Co., Sunnyvale, Calif., and it holds warrants to purchase up to 18%.

Digital is especially interested in Ingres's software for Unix, an increasingly popular computer operating system developed by American Telephone & Telegraph Co. and viewed as "open" because it is widely licensed by AT&T to others. Digital has become increasingly aggressive in the Unix market in the past year, as sales of its proprietary VAX line have slowed.

Separately, Digital unveiled a suite of software products designed to allow its computers to work easily with computers made by Apple Computer Inc., Cupertino, Calif.

The products tying the two companies' computers together are the first to emerge from a widely heralded technology alliance disclosed two years ago. They were announced in New York by Kenneth H. Olsen, Digital's president, and Apple Chief Executive Officer John Sculley.

Digital will market the products, which among other capabilities allow Digital computers to be used as file servers tying together networks of Apple Macintosh computers. The software packages make it possible to share data, electronic mail, printers and other devices.
