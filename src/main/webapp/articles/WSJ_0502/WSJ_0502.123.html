Business Brief -- Kinark Corp.: Steel Partners L.P. Submits $24.8 Million Takeover Bid

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/02/90

Kinark Corp. said its board received a $7-a-share, or about $24.8 million, acquisition proposal from Steel Partners Limited Partnership, a New York investment partnership. Kinark shares closed at $5.625, down 25 cents, in American Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday. Kinark said that it told Steel Partners the Tulsa, Okla., company isn't for sale, but that its board will consider the bid. The proposal is the latest attack on the embattled company, which owns galvanizing plants and chemical-storage facilities. Altair Corp., Lincolnshire, Ill., has said it is nominating three directors to Kinark's eight-member board at the May 23 annual meeting in opposition to Kinark's three nominees. Altair owns 6.7% of Kinark.

Kinark said Steel Partners has indicated it owns about 4.4% of Kinark's 3.5 million shares outstanding. Steel Partners, which includes Larry Butler and Warren Lichtenstein, said its bid is contingent on the group's arranging financing and Kinark's completing its planned sale of its hotel properties for $12 million. Kinark had first-quarter profit of $626,000, or 18 cents a share, on sales of $8.3 million.
