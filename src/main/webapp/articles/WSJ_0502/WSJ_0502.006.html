Marketing & Media: Cahners Publishing Hires Beni to Pursue More Acquisitions

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/02/90

NEWTON, Mass. -- Cahners Publishing Co., which has been on a buying binge of U.S. magazines, hired veteran magazine publisher John J. Beni to pursue more acquisitions and start-ups of consumer publications. Mr. Beni, 58 years old, was named senior vice president and general manager of Cahners's Consumer Magazine division. He had been president and publisher of New York Times Co. Magazine Group's McCall's magazine.

Cahners Publishing is the U.S. publishing subsidiary of London-based Reed International PLC. Cahners publishes 65 trade magazines and newspapers, as well as 10 consumer magazines, such as Modern Bride and American Baby. In 1989, Cahners's trade and consumer publications had revenue of $500 million.

Backed by Reed's deep pockets, Cahners has paid premium prices for new U.S. properties in recent years. In 1987, Cahners paid Diamandis Communications Inc. $64 million for Modern Bride. In 1989, it paid $825 million for News Corp's travel and hotel industry publications and electronic publishing properties.

Mr. Beni, who was once president of Gruner + Jahr U.S.A. Publishing, said Cahners is in the midst of negotiating acquisitions of existing magazines as well as spinning off new magazines from existing titles. Cahners spun off Health Kids magazine from American Baby and, most recently, Your Prom magazine from Modern Bride. Most Cahners magazines are distributed largely on newsstands or under controlled circulation, such as to doctors' waiting rooms.

"We are looking for line extensions based on our existing publications," said Mr. Beni. He added that possible new magazines could come from Cahners's Modern Bride and its boating group of trade titles. As for acquisitions, Mr. Beni said, "If there is anything out there that is a good fit, the money will be there to buy it, be it one magazine, or a collection of magazines."
