Blinder Robinson To Appeal Decision Of an SEC Judge

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/02/90

ENGLEWOOD, Colo. -- Blinder, Robinson & Co. said it plans to appeal sanctions against it that were handed down last week by a Securities and Exchange Commission hearing officer. The penny-stock brokerage firm, once the nation's largest, said it plans to appeal the decision of Brenda P. Murray to the commission and then, if needed, to take "all necessary and appropriate legal steps" to appeal an adverse SEC decision in the courts.

An appeal was expected. Blinder, a unit of Intercontinental Enterprises Inc., has bitterly contested regulatory and legal actions against it, and the current case has been under way for several years. Another hearing officer ruled in 1985 that Blinder Robinson and founder Meyer Blinder violated securities laws in a 1980 stock underwriting, and the commission concurred in 1986. But Blinder appealed in 1987; in early 1988, an appeals court panel sent the case back to the SEC.

In her decision, Ms. Murray suspended Blinder Robinson for 45 days and barred the firm from underwriting securities offerings for two years. She also barred Mr. Blinder from associating with any brokerage firm for at least two years.

Responding to the judge's ruling, Blinder argued that Ms. Murray had ignored the appeals court ruling and said the confrontation between it and the commission will continue "as long as the SEC continues to attempt to justify its systematic destruction of one commercial enterprise and the professional career of its founder under the guise of protecting the public interest."
