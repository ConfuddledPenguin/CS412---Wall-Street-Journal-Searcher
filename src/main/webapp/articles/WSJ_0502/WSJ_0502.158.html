Credit Ratings: Domtar Inc. Halves Quarterly, Posts Loss On Continuing Lines

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/02/90

MONTREAL -- Domtar Inc. halved its quarterly dividend to 6.25 Canadian cents (5.37 cents U.S.) and reported a first-quarter loss from continuing operations. The reduced dividend is payable June 15 to stock of record May 15.

"I would be less than honest if I said I exude confidence" about Domtar's short-term prospects, James Smith, chairman, president and chief executive officer, said after the annual meeting.

"We don't really see any signs of a near-term turnaround" in the company's paper and building-products markets, Mr. Smith said, adding that Domtar wouldn't "be unhappy" with a break-even second quarter.

Montreal-based Domtar reported a first-quarter loss from continuing operations of C$10 million, or 15 Canadian cents a share, compared with profit from continuing operations of C$20 million, or 19 Canadian cents a share, a year earlier. Sales fell to C$611 million from C$623 million a year earlier.

Net income in the latest period was C$20 million, or 19 Canadian cents a share, including a C$23 million after-tax gain from the sale of most of Domtar's salt business. A year ago, net was C$26 million, or 26 Canadian cents a share.

Investors "shouldn't have been surprised" by the dividend cut, said Raymond Kilroy, an analyst with Nesbitt Thomson Deacon Inc. Mr. Kilroy said Domtar "will have to struggle" to post a profit for the full year.

Domtar is the third major Canadian forest-products concern to cut its dividend in recent months. Last December, Abitibi-Price Inc. halved its quarterly dividend to 12.5 Canadian cents. Two weeks ago, Canadian Pacific Forest Products Ltd. slashed its quarterly payout 69% to 20 Canadian cents.

Yesterday, in New York Stock Exchange composite trading, Domtar closed at $10.25, off 25 cents.
