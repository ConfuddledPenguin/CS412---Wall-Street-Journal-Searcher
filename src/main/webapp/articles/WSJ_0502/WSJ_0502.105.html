Treasury's Mullins, Waiting to Join Fed, Sounds Like Old Hand

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/02/90

WASHINGTON -- David Mullins hasn't joined the Federal Reserve yet, but he already sounds like an old-timer. Mr. Mullins is the assistant Treasury secretary who is awaiting Senate approval to a seat on the Fed's board of governors. In written answers submitted to the Senate Banking Committee recently, Mr. Mullins said he would oppose immediate release of the minutes of Fed meetings. Immediate release of the minutes has long been urged by outsiders, but is staunchly opposed by Fed officials, who value their secrecy. Mr. Mullins said release of the minutes "may add to volatility" in the financial markets.

Mr. Mullins also said he would oppose legislation requiring regular audits of the Fed by the Congress's General Accounting Office, saying that would compromise "the independence of the Fed."

But Mr. Mullins expressed some disagreement with Fed Chairman Alan Greenspan on which federal regulator should set margin, or credit, requirements, for stock-index futures. Mr. Mullins said the Fed may be the appropriate agency; Mr. Greenspan has argued against giving the Fed that power.

The Treasury official indicated little interest in his own department's efforts to intervene in foreign-exchange markets, saying: "In my view, intervention in the foreign-exchange markets has never been an especially important tool of United States monetary policy."
