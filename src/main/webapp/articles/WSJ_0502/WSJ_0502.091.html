Airlines to Pay $1 Million For Anti-Terrorist Leads

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/02/90

WASHINGTON -- The Air Transport Association, a U.S. airline-industry group, announced it will pay as much as $1 million for information that helps authorities thwart terrorism directed at airlines. The $1 million bounty will be used to supplement the State Department's offer of as much as $2 million for such information in cases of airline terrorism overseas. Unlike the State Department's program, the ATA's offer will apply to information related to domestic cases as well.

"It was the unanimous judgment that the combined reward of $3 million . . . is a meaningful amount in terms of providing a deterrent, a disincentive or an encouragement to someone to come forward with the information," said Robert Aaronson, ATA's president. Twenty-three airlines will contribute money to the reward pool.

William Baker, assistant director of the criminal-investigation division of the Federal Bureau of Investigation, said nearly 800 people have been murdered by terrorist bombs on airlines.
