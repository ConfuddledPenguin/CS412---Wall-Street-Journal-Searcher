Hill & Knowlton Stirs Ire Over Its Work for Turkey

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/02/90

WASHINGTON -- Hill & Knowlton, whose Washington office last month disclosed plans to wage an anti-abortion campaign on behalf of the nation's Roman Catholic bishops, is generating new controversy over another client: the government of Turkey. In a letter to Hill & Knowlton Chairman Robert Gray, Armenian Assembly of America accused the New York-based public relations firm of seeking to "censor" U.S. schoolbooks regarding the 1915 mass deaths of Christian Armenians in Turkey. Armenian Assembly labels the 1915 events as genocide. Estimates of those killed range from several hundred thousand to more than one million.

Under an agreement signed last June by representatives of Hill & Knowlton and the Turkish embassy, the firm agreed to help "rectify substantial factual errors about contemporary Turkey and Turkish history contained in secondary-school social science textbooks."

Tacan Ilam, press consulate for the Turkish embassy, said Hill & Knowlton wasn't asked to promote any particular interpretation of the 1915 events in U.S. schoolbooks. He said the Turkish embassy was responding to a number of factual errors about Turkey in U.S. textbooks, none of them involving the Armenian deaths. Hill & Knowlton hasn't yet recommended specific changes in textbook language, according to its senior vice president, Chuck Pucie, but he added that "is not to say we would not do so."
