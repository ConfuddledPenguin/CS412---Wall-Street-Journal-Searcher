Prudential-Bache Cutting British Equities Operations

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/02/90

LONDON -- Prudential-Bache Securities Inc. joined the list of international financial firms that are trimming their equities operations here. The U.S. securities house, a unit of Prudential Insurance Co. of America, Newark, N.J., said it is "substantially downsizing" its equities operation in Britain because of lackluster returns. The firm, which started making markets in British stocks about a year ago, is cutting about 20 of 30 jobs in the line and eliminating most of its equities-research department.

A spokeswoman said Prudential-Bache will continue to make markets in 10 big British stocks, and provide brokerage services. "Market conditions in the U.K. have quite simply forced us to reassess (our) commitment to the U.K. equities market," the firm said.

Citicorp, Chase Manhattan Corp. and Morgan Grenfell are among the firms that have sharply reduced or eliminated their British equities operations amid overcapacity and lackluster profits in the competitive London markets. Prudential-Bache employs about 500 people in London, in a variety of foreign exchange, trading and corporate finance activities.
