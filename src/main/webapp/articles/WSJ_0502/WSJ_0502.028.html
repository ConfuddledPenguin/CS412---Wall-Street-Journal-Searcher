Georgia-Pacific Considering Sale Of Certain Assets

  • Author: Rick Christie
  • Date: 05/02/90

ATLANTA -- Georgia-Pacific Corp. said it is considering the sale of some assets to pay down debt from its recent $3.74 billion acquisition of Great Northern Nekoosa Corp. While the forest-products concern declined to estimate the value of the assets under study, one industry analyst said three mills alone that might be sold could bring $1.1 billion to $1.2 billion.

Georgia-Pacific said the decision to sell some assets resulted from a study of the huge portfolio of holdings resulting from the merger with Nekoosa. The study, not surprisingly, turned up some operational overlap, particularly among containerboard assets, said T. Marshall Hahn, Georgia-Pacific's chairman and chief executive officer.

"This is a strategic decision, not one of necessity," Mr. Hahn said. "In addition, we wanted to squelch any rumors before they got started of certain mills and plants being sold."

Indeed, Georgia-Pacific had sought to play down the need to sell assets throughout its four-month battle to acquire Nekoosa. "The view always was that they would sell two or three mills to work down the initial debt," said Gary Palmero, an Oppenheimer & Co. analyst who made the $1.1 billion to $1.2 billion estimate. "The company is in a position where it's not necessary, but it's certainly desirable."

Georgia-Pacific said assets targeted for possible sale include mills in Georgia, Oregon and Wisconsin that make linerboard, corrugating medium and kraft paper -- used to make containerboard for boxes; several thousand acres of accompanying timberland, and 17 box plants around the country.

Mr. Hahn, while declining to estimate how much the assets might fetch, said any proceeds would be used to reduce Georgia-Pacific's debt load.

Oppenheimer's Mr. Palmero said the three mills "are all well-run mills that will be in demand. The fact that Georgia-Pacific isn't desperate only adds to the value."

Mr. Hahn said Georgia-Pacific "will move without delay" now that it has completed its study. "We've had a good many inquiries about these and other assets," he said. "That obviously puts us in a position to move ahead."

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading, Georgia-Pacific closed at $41.625, up 62.5 cents.

Separately, Ronald P. Hogan and James C. Van Meter were elected Georgia-Pacific directors, bringing total membership to 16. Mr. Hogan, 49 years old, is president and chief operating officer. Mr. Van Meter, 52 years old, is executive vice president, finance, and chief financial officer.
