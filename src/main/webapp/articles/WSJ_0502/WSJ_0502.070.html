Hallwood Energy Offer Brings in $22.5 Million Of Partnerships' Assets

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/02/90

DALLAS -- Hallwood Energy Partners L.P. said its offer to exchange its units for interests in 18 oil and gas partnerships brought in tenders representing an estimated $22.5 million in limited partnership interests, or roughly one-third of the total assets it was soliciting. The company said a majority of the limited partners of Hutton/Apache 1982 Energy Income Fund Ltd. and Hutton/Apache Energy Income Fund II agreed to swap their $13.9 million in limited partner interests for Hallwood units, receiving one Hallwood unit for each $15 of exchange value. In addition, limited partner interests representing $8.6 million in exchange value in the other partnerships also tendered their interests.

Hallwood had estimated the value of the reserves in the 18 partnerships at about $66 million.

Hallwood said it expects to complete its merger with Energy Development Partners Ltd. and the limited partners next week, giving it reserves of about 9.8 million barrels and 142 billion cubic feet of natural gas.

Earlier this month, unit holders of Energy Development agreed to merge with Hallwood and to receive 0.34 Hallwood unit for each Energy Development unit.
