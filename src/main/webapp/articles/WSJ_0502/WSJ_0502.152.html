Texas Commissioner Puts Large Insurer In Conservatorship

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/02/90

DALLAS -- Texas Employers Insurance Association, once the largest writer of workers' compensation insurance policies in Texas, was placed in conservatorship by the state insurance commissioner. The appointment of a conservator "will enable {the insurer} to wind down its affairs in an orderly manner," Commissioner A.W. Pogue said.

Texas Employers reported assets of $307.3 million and liabilities of $297.9 million as of March 31. The liabilities don't include about $57.6 million in deferred assessments due the state's assigned-risk pool, the fund created to provide coverage for employers in hazardous businesses who couldn't obtain insurance elsewhere. The fund is financed by insurers based on their shares of the Texas workers' compensation market.

Texas Employers cited its liabilities for the assigned-risk pool when it stopped issuing new workers' compensation policies Oct. 1. But the company was unable to reverse its losses and was placed under supervision by the state insurance commissioner on March 6, a less stringent measure than conservatorship.

Under conservatorship, payment of Texas Employers' policyholder claims will take precedence, and a partial payment to the assigned-risk pool is possible in two-to-four years, the commissioner said.
