Business Brief -- MCI Communications Corp.: Pact Valued at $375 Million Over Five Years Is Signed

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/02/90

MCI Communications Corp., Washington, said it signed a telephone-service contract valued at about $375 million over five years, the largest contract in the company's history. MCI said it reached the agreement to provide long-distance services with the Navy Resale and Service Support Office. That office provides merchandise, food and personal services for U.S. Navy families. The agreement covers calls from pay phones at Navy, Marine Corps and Coast Guard sites throughout the contiguous U.S., Hawaii and Puerto Rico.

The contract is the latest MCI has won since a 1989 federal court order to open the pay-phone market to carriers besides American Telephone & Telegraph Co. MCI, which recently won similar contracts with the city of Dallas and Philadelphia International Airport, among others, estimates total annual sales in the U.S. long-distance pay-phone market at $2 billion.

Subcontractors to MCI will be Bell Atlantic Corp. and a unit of Pacific Telesis Group.

In national over-the-counter trading, MCI closed at $35.375, up 37.5 cents.
