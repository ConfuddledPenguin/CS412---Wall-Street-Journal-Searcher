Business Brief -- Rogers Communications: Chief Raises Voting Stake By Converting His Preferred

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/02/90

Rogers Communications Inc. said the company's president and chief executive, Edward Rogers, raised his voting stake in the company from 81%, to 89%, by converting some preferred share holdings to Class A voting shares. Mr. Rogers held the 4,150,000 Series VII convertible preferred shares through his wholly owned Rogers Real Estate Development Ltd.

A spokesman for the Toronto-based telecommunications concern said the company had planned to exercise its right to buy back the series at 10 Canadian dollars (US$8.59) a share, if Mr. Rogers hadn't decided to convert the preferred shares.

The conversion will save the company C$4 million in dividend payments in 1990, the spokesman said. Because the dividend rate is pegged to Canada's prime rate, which has risen steadily in recent months, he said the dividend payments have become "burdensome."

In trading on the Toronto Stock Exchange, the Class A shares of Rogers closed at C$11, unchanged. The widely held Class B non-voting shares closed at C$7.13, down 38 Canadian cents.
