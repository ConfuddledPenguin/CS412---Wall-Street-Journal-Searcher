South Korea's Trade Deficit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/02/90

SEOUL, South Korea -- South Korea had a trade deficit of US$390 million in April, the fourth consecutive monthly gap but the smallest since January, though it widened from a $19 million deficit a year earlier, the Trade and Industry Ministry said. Deficits for January, February and March all exceeded $600 million.

For January through April, the trade balance showed a deficit of $2.33 billion, compared with a surplus of $39 million a year earlier. The four-month aggregate surpassed the government's year-end projection of $2 billion in deficits.

In April, exports totaled $4.96 billion, up 3.8% from a year earlier, and imports jumped 12% to $5.36 billion. In the four months, exports rose only 0.1% to $18.87 billion, and imports soared 13% to $21.2 billion.
