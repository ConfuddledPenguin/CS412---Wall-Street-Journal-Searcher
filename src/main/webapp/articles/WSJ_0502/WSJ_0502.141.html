International: BCCI Discloses Net Loss Of $498 Million for 1989

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/02/90

LONDON -- Bank of Credit & Commerce International said it had a net loss of $498 million in 1989, largely as a result of $600 million in loan charges. The closely held bank, which had a net loss of $48 million in 1988 after loan provisions totaling $145 million, also confirmed that the Abu Dhabi government injected $400 million to help it meet international loan standards. The injection, which included the issue of new shares in the bank, boosted Abu Dhabi's stake to 77%.

Earlier this week, it was reported that Abu Dhabi had become the majority holder of BCCI by acquiring a 20% stake held by Saudi Arabia's Bin-Mahfouz family. Even before the acquisition and cash injection, the emirate and its ruling family members represented the largest block of shareholders in the bank, which is based in Luxembourg.

BCCI said it had operating profit before loan provisions of $224 million, up from $192 million in 1988. It added that total capital after the cash injection was $1.48 billion, up from $1.42 billion at the end of 1988. Total assets were $23.5 billion, up from $20.6 billion.

BCCI didn't disclose full 1989 operating results. Its brief statement followed reports that it would announce sharp losses for the year and possibly sweeping organizational changes.
