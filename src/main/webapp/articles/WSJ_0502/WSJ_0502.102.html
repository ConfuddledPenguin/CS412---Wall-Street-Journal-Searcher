Yields on CDs Rose Across the Board For Latest Week

  • Author: Ellen E. Schultz
  • Date: 05/02/90

NEW YORK -- Yields on certificates of deposit at major banks and brokerage firms rose across the board for the week ended yesterday, but continued to trail those available to investors on Treasury securities. The biggest gains were among small-denomination CDs sold by leading brokerage firms. Average yields on six-month CDs rose to 8.37% from 8.29%, while average yields on five-year CDs climbed to 8.93% from 8.85%, according to figures compiled by Banxquote Money Markets, a New York information service.

CDs sold directly by banks offered somewhat lower yields, according to an 18-bank survey by Banxquote. The average yield on small-denomination, one-year CDs was 8.08%, up from 8.02% the week before, while five-year consumer CDs offered 8.26%, up from 8.20%

For large-denomination CDs, which typically require deposits of $90,000 or more, the average six-month yield was 8.13%, up from 8.04%, while one-year yields were 8.33%, up from 8.25%

Despite the increases, investors could do even better with Treasurys, which have offered sharply higher yields in the past couple of weeks because of renewed inflationary fears. Six-month Treasury bills, for instance, were yielding 8.5% yesterday, while investors willing to lock up their money for three or more years could get yields in excess of 9%.

"Banks will be under a lot of pressure to increase CD yields even further to make them competitive with equivalent Treasury securities," said Norberto Mehl, chairman of Banxquote.

Typically, bank yields lag Treasury yields when interest rates are rising. That's because banks adjust their yields periodically to reflect the need for deposits and local competitive factors; yields on Treasury securities are tied directly to developments in the money markets.

Currently, CD yields show considerable variation from region to region, with banks in Texas and Florida offering some of the least attractive levels. For example, the five-year CD average in Texas is 7.50%, compared with 8.47% in New York and 8.53% in Pennsylvania. Average yields on three-month CDs were 7.13% at Florida banks and 7.21% at Texas banks, compared with 7.88% at both Pennsylvania and New York banks.

Chase Manhattan Bank in New York is offering a 9.17% yield on five-year jumbo CDs, one of the highest in the nation, according to Banxquote. Continental Bank in Chicago is offering a 9% yield on five-year jumbos.
