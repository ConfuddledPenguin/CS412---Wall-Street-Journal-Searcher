Federal Jury Convicts Florida Federal Thrift Of Falsifying Data

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/02/90

ST. PETERSBURG, Fla. -- Florida Federal Savings Bank was convicted by a federal jury of falsifying statements in connection with government-backed student loans. In early 1988, Florida Federal came under scrutiny for allegedly filing as many as 17,000 fraudulent insurance claims on $35 million dollars in student loans. The investigation helped trigger a management shake-up that included the resignation of Florida Federal's chairman.

The federal jury in Tampa found the company guilty of 43 counts of falsifying statements between November 1986 and July 1987. Sentencing is set for June 27. Florida Federal faces a possible fine of $21.5 million and restitution of $18 million.

Two former officers of the company -- Robert O. Harmas, 42 years old, of Noblesville, Ind.; and James J. LaMantia, 59, of Largo, Fla. -- were also convicted of falsifying statements. Neither could be reached for comment.

The thrift's chief executive officer, John W. Sapanski, said in a prepared statement: "We are disappointed in the verdict. However, resolution of the student loan problem has always been the new management's primary objective, so that we can proceed with implementing a plan to meet {federal} capital requirements. The conclusion of the trial and the sentencing will represent significant steps toward resolution of the problem."
