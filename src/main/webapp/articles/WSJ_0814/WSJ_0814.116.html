Business Brief -- International American Homes: Real Estate Concern Takes Write-Down of $6 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/14/90

International American Homes Inc. said that it took a $6 million pretax write-down on its real estate inventory in its fiscal first quarter and that it would suspend operations in Southeast Florida and curtail them in California. For the quarter ended June 30, the Clark, N.J.-based company reported a loss of $4.1 million, or 59 cents a share, after the write-down. In the year-earlier period, it had a profit of $1.6 million or 23 cents a share. Revenue in the recent quarter was $13.4 million, down from $41 million a year ago.

The company, which has been operating under bankruptcy court protection since April 16, said the write-down was necessary because of the continuing slump in the home building industry.

In addition, the company saw its home sales revenue and profit margins fall in all market areas.

In the wake of its filing for Chapter 11 protection from creditors under the U.S. Bankruptcy Code, construction lenders have been unwilling to finance any new projects, making it difficult to restore normal operations, the company said.

The company continues to market homes in the Washington, D.C., Baltimore, and Tampa, Fla., areas. It said is attempting to dispose of all excess inventory.
