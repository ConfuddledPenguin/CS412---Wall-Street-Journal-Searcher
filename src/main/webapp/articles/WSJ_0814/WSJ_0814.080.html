Marketing & Media: Archer Communications, PolyGram Sign an Accord

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/14/90

CALGARY, Alberta -- Archer Communications Inc. and PolyGram NV signed a licensing pact under which PolyGram plans to use a computerized sound mixing process developed by Archer, the companies said. Under terms of the nonexclusive five-year accord, the Dutch music company will pay Archer a royalty of 1% of total net retail sales of all PolyGram recordings that use Archer's sound technology, known as QSound.

Neither company would forecast the potential value of the agreement, though PolyGram said it plans to release at least 20 recordings featuring QSound during the next 18 months.

A PolyGram spokeswoman wouldn't say which of the company's stable of rock bands and other popular musicians would be the first to use QSound, but she said the first of the recordings could be out by Christmas. PolyGram's best-known artists include Sting, Bon Jovi, U2, and Def Leppard.

Earlier this year, Archer reached a pact with Japan's Nintendo Co. to use QSound in home video games. It is also negotiating licensing agreements with other music and video recording companies, an Archer executive said.

Archer's accord with PolyGram includes an option under which PolyGram can acquire as much as 15% of Archer's common shares, at $17.50 each, or a total of about $37 million, during the next two years.
