Politics & Policy: Enserch Unit Gets Nuclear Reactor Design Contract

  • Author: Barbara Rosewicz
  • Date: 08/14/90

WASHINGTON -- The Energy Department awarded two contracts, each potentially valued at $1.5 billion over 10 years, for advanced design work on two types of new nuclear reactors. The department selected Ebasco Services Inc., a subsidiary of Enserch Corp. of Dallas, to provide advanced design work for a heavy-water reactor to produce tritium, a perishable radioactive gas used in making nuclear weapons. Ebasco was selected over Westinghouse Electric Corp., a department spokesman said.

For the other contract, to design a modular, high-temperature, gas-cooled reactor, the agency selected a consortium consisting of General Atomics, Combustion Engineering Inc., Stone & Webster Engineering Co. and Burns & Roe Co. This type of reactor, based on a relatively new technology, would use a helium coolant. The consortium had no competition for the long-term contract, the government spokesman said.

The department wants to build two new reactors to replace its aging and crippled tritium reactors at Savannah River, S.C. One would be built at the Savannah River site, the other at the Idaho National Engineering Laboratory near Idaho Falls, Idaho.

However, Congress has questioned whether two reactors are necessary, especially given the thaw in relations with the Soviet Union. Construction money to complete either of the reactors hasn't been appropriated.

The new reactors, which if built would come on line in the year 2000, are to incorporate "passively safe" systems that offer safety features reducing the dependence on operators and mechanical parts in accidents.
