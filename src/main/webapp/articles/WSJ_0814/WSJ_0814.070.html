Walden Pond Development Project Has Been Scrapped

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/14/90

CONCORD, Mass. -- Plans to build a controversial condominium project near Walden Pond, made famous by author Henry David Thoreau, have been scrapped. The Trust for Public Land, a conservation group, agreed to purchase for $3.5 million about 25 acres on which the 139-unit project was to have been built. The project was to have included 42 low-income units.

"This is a major victory for us," said Thomas Blanding, a Thoreau scholar and president of the Thoreau Country Conservation Alliance, a development opponent. "Walden is the symbol of a balanced relationship between man and nature." The dispute over the project was the subject of a page one article in the Journal.

The trust also promised state officials to find a suitable alternative site for low-income housing within a year, said Kathi Anderson, director of Walden Woods Project, a conservation effort. Failing that, the group will have to build low-income housing on part of the 25 acres it purchased yesterday.

The development project drew attention after a group of about 50 musicians and actors, including Don Henley, Bonnie Raitt and Carrie Fisher, backed the anti-development effort. The group banded together to raise about $425,000, but critics charged that the protest was a ruse by wealthy Concordians to keep out the poor.
