Monday's Markets: Stocks Rise On Bargain Hunting --- Industrials Gain 30.20; Bond Prices, Dollar End With Declines

  • Author: Christopher Winans
  • Date: 08/14/90

NEW YORK -- Stock prices rebounded sharply in thin trading as bargain-hunters picked through the market after a weekend that passed without a much-feared meltdown in the Middle East. The Dow Jones Industrial Average rose 30.20 points to 2746.78 as 122.8 million shares traded on the New York Stock Exchange. Bond prices fell amid continued worries that inflation is picking up. The dollar weakened against most other major currencies, with traders betting that the Federal Reserve will soon move to lower U.S. interest rates.

Some of the last-minute surge in the Dow industrials was powered by futures-related program buying, but traders said they detected a broadly focused wave of bargain-hunting.

"It was as if investors started coming in with a whole bunch of confidence," said Paul Hennessey, head of equities trading at Boston Co. He noted, however, that volume was restrained by a power failure in lower Manhattan, which hampered some securities firms.

Joseph J. McAlinden, director of equity research at Dillon Read, said investors are beginning to recognize that companies other than those in the oil industry, such as basic and precious metals producers, will be able to make money despite higher inflation.

"I'd rather own stocks that can act as a hedge against inflation than bonds that get hurt by it," he said.

But while stock investors may be able to find ways to prosper amid higher inflation, fixed-income investors have more to worry about. And yesterday, a sharp jump in gold prices reinforced inflation fears.

Currency traders pushed the dollar lower on the growing expectation of lower U.S. interest rates, which would make dollar-denominated investments less attractive compared with foreign instruments. Traders are betting that the Fed will have to ease short-term rates to stave off a recession, which has grown more likely as a result of higher oil prices, said Lorenzo Troni, foreign-exchange trader at PaineWebber International Ltd.

In major market action:

Stock prices fell on thin Big Board volume. Declining issues edged out advancers, 766 to 726.

Bond prices fell a quarter of a point, or $2.50 for each $1,000 of face amount in light trading. The yield on the Treasury's benchmark 30-year bond rose to 8.80%.

The dollar ended lower. In late New York trading, the currency was quoted at 1.5765 marks and 150.40 yen, compared with 1.5905 marks and 150.45 yen Friday.
