Technology & Science: AT&T, Japan Firm Sign an Agreement On Chinese Project

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/14/90

NEW YORK -- American Telephone & Telegraph Co. has signed an agreement with Japan's international communications carrier and the telecommunications authority of China to upgrade China's international communications system. The agreement, with Japan's Kokusai Denshin Denwa and the Directorate General of Telecommunications of China's Ministry of Posts and Telecommunications, calls for the partners to build a new system -- including a satellite earth station network and the first undersea fiber-optic cable to China -- in two phases.

In the first phase, to be completed by next year, a network of satellite earth stations will be built providing digital voice and data communications services to major office buildings in Beijing and Shanghai. Satellites of the International Telecommunications Satellite Organization, or Intelsat, will tie the network together.

The second phase, which is scheduled to be built and operating in 1993, is a 800-mile, digital fiber-optic submarine cable that will link Japan's Kyushu Island and the Chinese coast near Shanghai. The cable is expected to carry about 35,000 phone calls simultaneously, "a significant expansion over the existing system," according to an AT&T spokesman, who declined to provide further details.

William B. Carter, AT&T's executive director of submarine systems, said the China system will provide one of the last major links in a digital communications system encircling the globe. He said equipment suppliers for the systems haven't been chosen, but said the project should cost between $20 million and $45 million.
