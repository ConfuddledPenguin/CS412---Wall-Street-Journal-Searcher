REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: Jurymandering

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/14/90

Congressional arrogance finds new meaning in the pending legislative appropriations bill for Congress. Roll Call, the Capitol Hill newspaper, has discovered a last-minute committee addition that would exempt Members from serving on juries in their home districts. Members are now routinely excused from jury duty on account of their business in Washington, but that's not enough for Democratic Rep. Vic Fazio, the chief defender of Congressional perks. He says several Members asked him to draft the law, because, "it's a hassle in the sense that you have to go through that work" of getting excused from service. GOP Rep. Jerry Lewis promises a fight on the House floor next month, and even Democratic Rep. Jack Brooks, chairman of the Judiciary Committee, says the law "hardly seems necessary." Rep. Fazio and his friends apparently feel so invulnerable to defeat that they are willing to try to enact laws disconnecting them from the dreary responsibilities of their own districts.
