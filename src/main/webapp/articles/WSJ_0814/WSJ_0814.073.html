Technology: Semiconductor Orders Rose 3.5% in July, Group Says

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/14/90

SAN JOSE, Calif. -- Orders for semiconductors in the U.S. market ran 3.5% higher in July than a year ago, the Semiconductor Industry Association said. However the microchip industry's sensitive book-to-bill ratio -- an index of sales health -- has contracted from the levels of the preceding two months, in keeping with the industry's seasonal summer doldrums.

Average monthly order bookings for July based on a three-month moving average were $1.189 billion, up from $1.148 billion in the year-earlier period.

However, the trade group said July orders were down 6.4% from the $1.270 billion figure posted in June 1990, and they decreased 12.8% from the April level of $1.363 billion.

Semiconductor shipments, or billings, in July were $1.079 billion, down 6.4% from the $1.153 billion level of July 1989, and down 16.7% from the $1.296 billion level in June 1990.

Comparison of the three-month averages of both new order bookings and order shipments resulted in a book-to-bill ratio of 1.00, up from the year-earlier ratio of 0.92. A ratio of one indicates new orders and shipments are in balance. A ratio above one indicates sales are expanding, while a ratio below one indicates new sales are contracting below the level of shipments.

The July book-to-bill ratio of 1.0 is the lowest since last November, when the ratio was 0.95.

"It's not outstanding news," an SIA spokesman said. "But it is consistent with the industry's usual summer dip and with earlier predictions for flat to slightly negative growth" for the balance of 1990.
