Bank of Montreal Cuts Prime

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/14/90

TORONTO -- Bank of Montreal, one of Canada's major commercial banks, cut its prime lending rate to 14.25% from 14.75% effective today. It was the first cut in the bank's prime rate since October 1987.

The prime rate cut followed a drop in the Bank of Canada's bank rate last week to 13.34% from 13.42%. The central bank's key bank rate has declined a total of 56 basis points in the past seven weeks mainly because of an upswing in the Canadian dollar. The bank rate is the levy which the central bank charges on its loans to financial institutions.

Canadian commercial banks last adjusted their prime rates in April when the rate rose half a percentage point to 14.75%.
