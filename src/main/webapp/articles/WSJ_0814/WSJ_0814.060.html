Business Brief -- Greyhound Lines Inc.: Bus Line Raises Fares 2% To Meet Higher Fuel Costs

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/14/90

Greyhound Lines Inc. implemented a 2% across-the-board fare increase to compensate for higher fuel costs. Greyhound spokesman George Gravley said the cost of fuel to the Dallas-based passenger line rose almost 35% over the first nine days of August, from 59 cents a gallon (pre-tax) to 79 cents. Since then, the price has gone down a percentage point or two, he said.

Fuel accounts for 4.6% of costs at Greyhound. "That's a lot less than at American Airlines," Mr. Gravley said. The airline increased its fares 10% last week.
