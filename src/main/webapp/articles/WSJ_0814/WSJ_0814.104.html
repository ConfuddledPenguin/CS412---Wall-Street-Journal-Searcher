International Brief -- Precision Mecanique Labinal S.A.: French Concern's Revenue In 1st Half Increased by 9%

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/14/90

PARIS -- Precision Mecanique Labinal S.A. reported yesterday that its consolidated revenue for the first half of 1990 rose to 3.832 billion francs ($718 million) for a 9% gain from the year-earlier total of 3.512 billion francs. The engineering group said in a communique that the sales gain was led by the firm tone of civil aviation and automobile cable markets. These elements were partly offset, however, by weakness in the computer and military aviation sectors.

Labinal underlined that part of its first-half revenue gain reflected the first-time consolidation of recently acquired units such as Technofan and Labauto Iberica. It said the advance would have been limited to 5% after adjustment for these structural changes.

The group hasn't yet published comparable first-half earnings figures. For all of 1989, Labinal's consolidated net income after payments to minority interests was 272 million francs on revenue of 6.800 billion francs.
