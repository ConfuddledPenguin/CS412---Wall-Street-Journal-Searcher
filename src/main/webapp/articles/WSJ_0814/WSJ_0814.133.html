New Securities Issues

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/14/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report. MORTGAGES

Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $500 million of real estate mortgage investment conduit securities is being offered in 10 classes by Prudential-Bache Securities. The offering, FHLMC 194, is backed by Freddie Mac 8 1/2% securities with a weighted average coupon rate of 9.40% and weighted average remaining term to maturity of 25.67 years. Details on prices and yields weren't immediately available.

EUROBONDS

Fujitsu Ltd. (Japan) -- $330 million of bonds due Aug. 30, 1994, with equity-purchase warrants, indicating a 5% coupon at par, via Nikko Securities Co. Each $10,000 bond carries two warrants, exercisable from Sept. 19, 1990, through Aug. 9, 1994, to buy company shares at an expected premium of 2 1/2% to the closing share price when terms are fixed Friday.

Chujitsuya Co. (Japan) -- 120 million Swiss francs ($91.3 million) of 7 3/4% public bonds in two tranches. First tranche of 70 million Swiss francs issued at 100 1/2, maturing Sept. 5, 1997, guaranteed by Yokohama Bank. Second tranche of 50 million Swiss francs is issued at 100 3/8, maturing Sept. 5, 1995, guaranteed by Fuji Bank.

Deutsche Bank Finance N.V., (West German parent) -- 100 million Australian dollars (US$79.8 million) of 14% bonds due Sept. 7, 1993, priced at 100.95 to yield 14.24% less fees via Deutsche Bank Capital Markets Ltd. Guaranteed by Deutsche Bank AG. Fees 1 1/2.

ANZ Banking Group (Australia) -- 75 million Australian dollars of 14 3/4% bonds due Sept. 20, 1993, priced at 101.95 to yield 14.55% less fees via Samuel Montagu & Co. Fees 1 1/2.
