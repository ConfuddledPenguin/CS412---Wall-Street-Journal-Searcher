Who's News: McDonnell Douglas Names David Little To C-17 Program

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/14/90

LONG BEACH, Calif. -- McDonnell Douglas Corp.'s Douglas Aircraft Co. division named David A. Little vice president-deputy general manager of its C-17 military cargo plane program, in further executive shuffling designed to shore up performance on the Air Force contract. Mr. Little, 47 years old, previously general manager of the program, succeeds Jack Hayden, 43, who assumed the newly created post of vice president-deputy general manager for fiscal management and support.

Mr. Little will concentrate on manufacturing operations, and will work with Leonard F. Impellizzeri, staff vice president for engineering and advanced systems for the St. Louis-based parent who in May was named vice president-deputy general manager of the C-17, with responsibility for technical aspects of the program.
