Who's News: Tyson Foods' Chief Plans To Head European Office

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/14/90

SPRINGDALE, Ark. -- Like many U.S. companies, Tyson Foods Inc. is establishing a beachhead in Europe. Unlike most others, though, Tyson's commander-in-chief is leading the invasion. Don Tyson, the chairman and chief executive officer of the chicken processing company, told his staff last week that he plans to establish a home and office in England sometime after the first of the year. He said he plans to spend the first two weeks of each month in Arkansas and the last two in the U.K., tending to the expansion of his chicken empire there. He expects he'll maintain his part-time residence in England for at least six months.

Mr. Tyson, known as a hands-on executive who is reluctant to delegate, said he isn't sure whether the company's chicken products will fly in Europe. "I want to take the credit if it goes well," he said. "If it doesn't, I'll face that too."

Currently, Tyson holds 50% of a company that sells hens and roosters in the U.K. and several European countries. Mr. Tyson plans to grow and process chickens at the company, as well as market the company's chicken products.
