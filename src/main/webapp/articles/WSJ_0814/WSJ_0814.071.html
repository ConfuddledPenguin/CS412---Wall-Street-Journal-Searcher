Letters to the Editor: Deifying Japanese, Dumping on Detroit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/14/90

Each time I think I have seen the limits to which you will go to denigrate the American auto industry (while deifying the Japanese producers), you surprise me by going even further. Your July 9 page-one story on General Motors' new Saturn Division is a case in point. The fact that an American company is investing $3 billion and doing virtually everything the press preaches American industry must do to compete against the Japanese doesn't necessarily mean that it deserves the media's unmitigated praise. At the same time, I don't think it deserves to be second-guessed before the first car is ever built as you did in your treatise on how Honda got more for less. Another Journal example of how those infallible Japanese manufacturers have outwitted the dumb Americans.

We wait somewhat impatiently for you to balance the scales a little. Here's one thought for starters. Has anybody noticed that Honda Prelude sales in 1986 were 80,000 units, but fell like a rock to only 43,000 units in 1989?

We would like your readers to know that despite what you write, the Japanese do not walk on water and American companies can -- and are -- turning out some top-quality, high-value cars and trucks in this country. It's unfortunate that the American media either don't agree or don't care or simply refuse to tell the story.

A.C. Liebler

Vice President

Communications

Chrysler Corp.

Highland Park, Mich.
