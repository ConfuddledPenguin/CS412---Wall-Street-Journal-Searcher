Mobil Will Buy Australian Assets From Exxon Corp.

  • Author: Allanna Sullivan
  • Date: 08/14/90

NEW YORK -- Mobil Corp. said it agreed to purchase the stake it doesn't already own in two refineries and a network of gasoline stations in Australia from Exxon Corp. Neither Exxon nor Mobil would disclose the transaction's value, but industry officials put it at about $400 million.

The purchase includes about 300 gasoline stations and 90 fuel truck depots. Mobil already owns 700 gasoline stations there.

In addition, the acquisition includes Exxon's 35% share of a joint-venture company currently operated by both companies. The company, Petroleum Refineries Proprietary Ltd., runs two refineries. One, located in Altona, processes 120,000 barrels a day of crude oil. The other refinery, in Adelaide, handles 65,000 barrels of oil daily.

Mobil has been attempting to increase its presence in Australia and Asia, where the use of oil has increased sharply in recent years. "The acquisition of these assets will advance our strategy of increasing strength in the world's fastest-growing market for petroleum products," said Allen Murray, Mobil chairman.

The acquisition, expected to be completed by year end, will boost Mobil to a tie with British Petroleum Co. as the second-largest marketer of gasoline in Australia. It will be providing service to 20% of Australia's motorists. Mobil is currently ranked fourth, serving 14% of the populace.
