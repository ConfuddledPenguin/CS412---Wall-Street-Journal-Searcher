Politics & Policy: Barry Switches Affiliation; Run for Council Possible

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/14/90

WASHINGTON -- Mayor Marion Barry switched his party affiliation to independent from Democrat as a prelude to a possible run for an at-large seat on the Washington City Council. Mr. Barry took the preliminary step toward reviving his political career only three days after he was convicted of a misdemeanor cocaine-possession charge. A federal court jury acquitted the three-term mayor on another cocaine-possession charge and deadlocked on 12 other counts, including drug possession, conspiracy and perjury. A sentencing date hasn't been set.

Mr. Barry has said he won't run again for mayor. At a news conference yesterday, he refused to commit himself to any particular race, including one for a seat on the 13-member council. He had to switch political affiliation to remain eligible for the November ballot. He must make a final decision whether to run within the next two weeks.

Smiling enigmatically, the mayor said he changed his registration "to keep my options." He acknowledged, however, that some close associates have urged him to leave politics.
