Technology & Science: Wistar Institute Plans Outdoor Test Of Rabies Vaccine

  • Author: Richard Koenig
  • Date: 08/14/90

PHILADELPHIA -- Wistar Institute said its researchers plan to start a field test of a genetically engineered rabies vaccine Aug. 20. The test will be the first outdoor use in the U.S. of a recombinant animal virus. Wistar, a biomedical research center, plans to distribute vaccine-laden baits over part of Parramore Island, off the eastern shore of Virginia. The goal is to protect raccoons against rabies. If the trial succeeds, the bait might be used elsewhere, since raccoons are common carriers of the rabies virus. Wistar is especially interested in using the vaccine to control current rabies outbreaks in the mid-Atlantic states.

Wistar received federal approval for the trial more than a year ago. But the trial was delayed as the institute sought clearances from state authorities who had questions about safety and liability. A proposal to do a trial on an island off South Carolina was blocked by state health authorities, at least temporarily. Virginia regulators granted approval just last month.

Parramore Island, which is owned by the Nature Conservancy, has a large raccoon population but is uninhabited by people and lies some four miles from the mainland. The choice of a remote island for the trial reflects Wistar's efforts to allay public concerns, though the institute maintains the vaccine poses no hazard. Similar tests in the wild have already been carried out in Europe.

Wistar made the vaccine by inserting a small piece of genetic material from the rabies virus into vaccinia virus, which serves as a carrier for vaccine delivery. The institute says the genetic material from the rabies virus isn't infectious but can produce a protective immune response. It adds that it has tested the vaccine in some 35 species in captivity without ill effects.
