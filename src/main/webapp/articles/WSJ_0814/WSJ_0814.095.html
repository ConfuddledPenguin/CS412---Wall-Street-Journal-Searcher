Trefoil Extends Deadline In Child World Acquisition

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/14/90

BURBANK, Calif. -- The first public investment by Roy E. Disney's new "white squire" fund appears to have hit a snag, as the group postponed reaching a definitive agreement to acquire a controlling stake in Child World Inc., the nation's second largest toy retailer. The fund, Trefoil Capital Investors L.P., said it extended until Aug. 24 the deadline for the agreement to acquire an 82% stake in Child World held by Cleveland-based CNC Holding Corp. An agreement was supposed to have been concluded by last Friday.

Trefoil said only that the companies "have agreed to mutually explore . . . the possible resolution of outstanding issues relating to this proposed acquisition," and that "there is no assurance that the parties will reach final agreement."

Exact terms of the proposed transaction haven't been disclosed, although Trefoil has said it would follow up the purchase with a $14-a-share, or $28 million, cash offer for the 18% of Avon, Mass.-based Child World now in public hands.

Trefoil is a $450 million investment fund formed by Mr. Disney and principals of his Shamrock Holdings Inc. investment concern. The fund is designed to invest in companies burdened by heavy debt.
