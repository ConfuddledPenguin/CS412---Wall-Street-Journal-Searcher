Equitec Posts Loss In Quarter, Discloses Default on All Debt

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/14/90

OAKLAND, Calif. -- Equitec Financial Group Inc., posting a net loss for the second quarter of $3.4 million, or 68 cents a share, disclosed it now is in default on all of its debt obligations outstanding. For the year-ago period, the financial services company had a loss of $3.3 million, or 68 cents a share. That figure includes a loss of $2 million from discontinued operations.

Revenue for the recent period declined 30%, to $8.3 million from $11.8 million.

For the six months ended June 30, Equitec posted a net loss of $8.8 million, or $1.78 a share, which includes losses from continuing operations of $7.7 million and from discontinued operations of $1.1 million.

For the year-earlier period, Equitec had a net loss of $4.6 million, or 93 cents a share, which included losses from continuing operations of $2.6 million, and from discontinued operations of $2 million.

Revenue for the recent six months declined 25%, to $16.8 million from $22.3 million.

During the quarter just ended, Equitec said it failed to make the interest payment on its 12% senior subordinated debentures, thus placing it in default on all its debt outstanding.

Richard L. Saalfield, Equitec chairman and chief executive officer, said the company continues to work with its advisers to "investigate alternative methods of restructuring its debt and other obligations."
