Con Ed Personnel Faced With Bribery, Extortion Charges

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/14/90

WASHINGTON -- The Labor Department's inspector general charged 17 former and current Consolidated Edison Co. employees with bribery and extortion involving cash from Con Ed customers, electrical contractors and others. The inspector general's office of labor racketeering filed criminal complaints in federal and state courts in New York. The complaints alleged that the Con Ed employees extorted cash in return for, among other things, overlooking safety hazards such as natural gas leaks, allowing illegal gas and electrical hookups to go unreported, and expediting the providing of gas or electrical service.

The investigation is part of a probe by the labor-racketeering office into corruption in the New York construction industry, said Stephen Cossu, the office's New York agent-in-charge.

Complaints were filed in federal district court in Brooklyn charging extortion and fraud against seven persons of Con Ed's Bedford/Bushwick branch, including two managers.

Ten people including a supervisor were charged in state court with extortion and bribery, including allowing illegal hookups for Manhattan customers, at a restaurant, auto repair shop, laundromat and an apartment building, and permitting delays in repairs of gas leaks at an apartment building and a restaurant.
