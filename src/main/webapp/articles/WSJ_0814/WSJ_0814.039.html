U.K. Producer Output Prices

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/14/90

LONDON -- U.K. producer output prices, or prices of manufactured goods, rose 0.3% in July from June, while producer input prices, or prices of raw materials and fuels purchased by manufacturing industry, fell an unadjusted 0.4% in July from June, the U.K. Central Statistical Office said. An average of market forecasts, showed that input prices were expected to have fallen 0.6% in July while output prices were expected to have risen by 0.4%.

After seasonal adjustment, input prices rose 0.6% in July from June and were down 1.1% from July 1989. This compares with June's 1.4% monthly fall and 2.4% yearly fall. The statistical office said it provided a seasonally adjusted index for input prices as they are more subject to seasonal fluctuations than output prices.

After seasonal adjustment, the index of input prices was at 102.9 in July, up from 102.3 in June and down from 104.0 in July 1989.
