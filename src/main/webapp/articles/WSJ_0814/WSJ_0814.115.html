Business Brief -- Cablevision Systems Corp.: Firm Posts Loss in Quarter, Cites Acquisition Expenses

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/14/90

Cablevision Systems Corp. reported a second-quarter loss of $67.7 million, or $3.09 a share, reflecting interest costs and depreciation expense tied to acquisitions of cable-TV systems the Woodbury, N.Y., company made last year. Operating cash flow-earnings from operations before depreciation and amortization-advanced 15%, however, to $61.6 million.

In February 1989, Cablevision acquired cable-TV systems from National Amusements Inc.'s 84%-owned Viacom Inc. subsidiary. And last December, the company acquired certain systems from a subsidiary of Times Mirror Co.

The loss compares with net income of $24.1 million, or 99 cents a share, in the year-earlier quarter. Revenue climbed 13% to $141.2 million from $125.4 million.
