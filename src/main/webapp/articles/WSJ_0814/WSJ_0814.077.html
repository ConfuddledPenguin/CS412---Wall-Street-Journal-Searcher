Letters to the Editor: Faithful Diarists Of the American Dream

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/14/90

I am absolutely delighted with my brand-new subscription to The Wall Street Journal, and I thought you'd like to know. I get it every day at 5:30 p.m., and I genuinely look forward to its arrival. I may be unusual among your subscribers, in that I spend about 24% of my monthly income for my subscription to the Journal: I'm in prison, and have less than $50 a month to spend.

You also may be interested to know that each issue gets circulated among a group of readers, in return for other publications. The Journal has real barter value in this society, but the barter group is small: There are only a few inmates who care what Alan Greenspan said yesterday (or who he is in the first place).

Thanks for doing such a good job. You can be certain I'll continue to be an enthusiastic subscriber even after this period in my life is over. Although I'm signing my name, please don't use it; I've had more publicity than I want by now.

Inmate E-23884

Vacaville, Calif.
