Letters to the Editor: Reining In Journalists Who Ruin Reputations

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/14/90

Michael Gartner ("We Wuz Robbed] (That's Only My Opinion, Of Course"), Viewpoint, July 19) is exactly right. It is only his opinion. And I disagree with him.

What the Supreme Court seems to be saying in its latest ruling on the First Amendment is not that free speech should be shackled but rather that published opinions rendered on another ought to be based at least on some semblance of fact, regardless of who is doing the rendering -- journalist, lawyer, securities analyst, banker, whoever. In layman's language this means there's now some litigable limit on how loosely one's good name and reputation may be trashed in public. Somehow that strikes me as a fair attempt to rein in First Amendment abuses.

In other words, I would contend that most of us "wuz helped]" not robbed by the court's decision.

Fraser P. Sietel

Senior Vice President

Director, Public Affairs

Chase Manhattan Bank

New York
