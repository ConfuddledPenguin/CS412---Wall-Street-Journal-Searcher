STATE OF CONNECTICUT -- Moody's Investors Service Inc. said it confirmed its double-A rating on general obligation bonds of the state, which plans to sell $325 million of the bonds Tuesday. In April, the rating concern downgraded the bonds' rating from double-A-1. Moody's said that Connecticut has strong backing for its bonds, but that it has exhausted reserves and its finances are "vulnerable to economic slowdown." ---

LIONEL Corp. (New York) -- Moody's Investors Service Inc. said it downgraded to Caa from single-B-2 its rating on this specialty toy maker's 8% convertible subordinated debentures, due 2007, and 12.375% subordinated notes, due 1996. The rating concern's move affects about $64 million of Lionel debt still outstanding from the two issues, which initially totaled $70 million. Moody's didn't elaborate on the rating change.

---

Corrections & Amplifications

LIONEL CORP., whose debt was recently downgraded, is a New York toy retailer. The company's business was misstated in Monday's edition. Lionel Corp. is unrelated to closely held Lionel Trains Inc., Mount Clemens, Mich., which manufactures toy trains under the Lionel name.

(WSJ July 18, 1990)
