Dissident Group Seeks To Unseat the Board Of Sherritt Gordon

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/16/90

TORONTO -- A group of dissident shareholders of Sherritt Gordon Ltd. said it plans to launch a proxy fight to unseat the company's current board and management. The group, which has formed Canada SherGor Enterprises Inc., said it requested the company to call a shareholders meeting, where it plans to present for a vote an alternative slate of board nominees. Canada SherGor said it expected a new board would appoint the group's principals, Ian Delaney and Bruce Walter, to executive positions at Sherritt.

A Sherritt Gordon official said the company had no immediate response to the announcement. Sherritt, based in Toronto, has interests in nickel refining and fertilizer production.

Messrs. Delaney and Walter resigned in April as chief executive officer and vice president, respectively, of Horsham Corp., a Toronto investment concern. Since then, Mr. Delaney said the two have built up a stake of between 5% and 10% of Sherritt through market purchases of stock. The group also has the backing of Eric Sprott, who heads Toronto brokerage firm Sprott Securities Ltd. In a statement, Mr. Sprott said he won't be paid for his support.

Mr. Delaney criticized Sherritt's current management for not doing "much to mitigate the cyclicality of the commodities" it is involved with. He also was critical of the current board's low level of investment in Sherritt stock. As of April, Sherritt directors owned a combined total of about 0.04% of the company's stock.

Mr. Delaney said he doesn't intend to sell any of Sherritt's assets "for short-term benefit." Instead, "we intend to build the company and to make {it} our principal business interest," he said.

Under Ontario company law, the Sherritt board has 21 days to call a shareholders meeting.
