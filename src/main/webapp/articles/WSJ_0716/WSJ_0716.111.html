Business Brief -- Ampal-American Israel Corp.: Three Holders File Lawsuit To Stop Planned Loan Sale

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/16/90

Ampal-American Israel Corp., New York, said it was being sued in New York state court by three plaintiffs who claim to be holders of Ampal's Class A stock. Ampal has industrial, agricultural, real estate, hotel and other interests in Israel.

The suit, filed as a class action, seeks to enjoin the proposed sale of the industrial loan portfolios of Ampal's three banking subsidiaries in Israel to Bank Hapoalim B.M. Under an agreement reached earlier this month, Bank Hapoalim would acquire the loan portfolios for $875 million, their book value at Dec. 31.

Ampal, which said it intends to "vigorously defend the action," said the plaintiffs contend the purchase price is unfair. Ampal's president and three other directors also were named as defendants.
