New Securities Issues

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/16/90

The following were among Friday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATE

Caterpillar Inc. -- $150 million of 9 3/8% senior notes due July 15, 2000, priced at 99.30 to yield 9.484%. The noncallable notes were priced at a spread of 102 basis points above the Treasury's 10-year note. Rated single-A-2 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and single-A-minus by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets.

Household Financial Corp. -- $150 million of 9 5/8% senior subordinated notes due July 15, 2000, offered at 99.774 to yield 9.66% through an underwriting group led by Lehman Brothers. Rated single-A-3 by Moody's and single-A by S&P, the noncallable notes were priced at 122 basis points above the Treasury's 10-year note.

MORTGAGES

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $400 million of Remic mortgage securities offered in 13 classes by Morgan Stanley & Co. The offering, Series 1990-94, is backed by Fannie Mae 9 1/2% securities. Price and yield data weren't immediately available.

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $200 million of Remic mortgage securities offered in eight classes by Nomura Securities International. The offering, Series 1990-96, is backed by Fannie Mae 10 1/2% securities with a weighted average coupon rate of 11.125% and weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29.10 years. Among classes for which details were available, yields ranged from 9.25%, or 84 basis points above two-year Treasury securities, to 9.67%, or 130 basis points over 10-year Treasurys.

EUROBONDS

American Express Credit Corp. (U.S.) -- $150 million of 9% Eurobonds due Aug. 9, 1993, issue price of 101.025, reoffered at 99.85 via Lehman Brothers.

Czechoslovakian Trade Bank (Ceskoslovenska Obchodni Banka) -- 250 million marks of 10% bonds due Aug. 2, 2000, at 100 3/4 to yield 9.8%, via Commerzbank. Guaranteed by Czechoslovakian State Bank. Noncallable. Fees 2.

Japanese Telecom (Japan) -- 60 million marks of 9 1/4% private placement bonds due Aug. 3, 1995, priced at 101 3/4 to yield 8.9%, via IBJ Deutschland. Guaranteed by IBJ Tokyo. Fees 1 7/8.
