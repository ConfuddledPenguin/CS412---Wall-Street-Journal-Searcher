International Brief -- Ciba-Geigy AG: Big Swiss Company's Sales For First Half Eased 2.3%

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/16/90

Ciba-Geigy AG, Switzerland's largest chemical and pharmaceutical concern, said 1990 first-half sales fell 2.3% to 10.89 billion Swiss francs ($7.83 billion) from 11.15 billion francs a year earlier. But in local-currency terms, sales rose 4%. The difference in the percentage changes owes to the Swiss franc's appreciation the dollar, yen and pound.

Typical of most Swiss companies, Ciba-Geigy didn't disclose first-half profit or provide an outlook for second-half sales. But it said it will disclose first-half earnings in an interim report to shareholders in mid-August.

Despite the overall first-half sales decline, sales in the pharmaceuticals division, which accounts for the largest share of the total, rose 3% to 3.13 billion francs. The agricultural division's sales slipped 3% to 2.79 billion francs. In local-currency terms, pharmaceuticals sales were up 11% and agricultural sales were up 3%.

Ciba-Geigy in 1989 earned 1.56 billion francs, up 18% from 1.33 billion francs in 1988.
