New Stock Listings

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/16/90

NEW YORK -- The New York Stock Exchange an- nounced two new listings, and the American Stock Exchange six. Two of the new listings are initial public offerings.

Live Entertainment Inc., a Van Nuys, Calif., maker and retailer of entertainment software products, joins the big board Wednesday, under the symbol LVE. It previously traded over the counter.

Nuevo Energy Co., an independent oil and gas company based in Houston, began trading last week as an initial public offering under NEV.

Joining the Amex, Essex Financial Partners L.P., a Virginia Beach, Va., multistate savings and loan holding company, begins trading limited partnership units on Tuesday, under ESX.

Foundation Health Corp., a Rancho Cordova, Calif., managed health care services company, began trading last week as an initial public offering under FH.

Helionetics Inc., a maker of electronic power conditioning products in Irvine, Calif., began trading under ZAP. It also trades on the Pacific Stock Exchange, and previously traded over-the-counter.

Rhone-Poulenc S.A., a French pharmaceutical and chemical company, began trading contingent value rights on a "when-issued" basis, under RCV.WI.

Tecogen Inc., a Waltham, Mass., maker of packaged co-generation systems and gas-fueled air conditioners, which previously traded over-the-counter, begins trading Thursday, under TGN.

Universal Zoltronics Corp, a Mt. Kisco, N.Y., maker of high-voltage power conversion systems, which also previously traded over-the-counter, begins trading Thursday, under UVL.

The contingent value rights are being issued in connection with Rhone-Poulenc's purchase of a majority stake in Rorer Group Inc., on the basis of one right for each share not owned by Rhone-Poulenc. The Amex expects them to be distributed by the end of the month, when Rorer will be renamed Rhone Poulenc Rorer Inc. In the event the shares of this company are trading below $98.26 on July 31, 1993, the right requires Rhone Poulenc to pay holders the difference, to a maximum $46.26 a right. The rights would cease trading on that date, unless Rhone Poulenc extends them one year with a maximum value of $44.14.

Separately, the Chicago Board Options Exchange said Cadence Design Systems Inc., a San Jose, Calif., maker of software for integrated circuit design automation, and UJB Financial Corp., a Princeton, N.J., banking company began trading under CDQ and UJB, respectively.

Corrections & Amplifications

CONTINGENT VALUE RIGHTS being issued on the American Stock Exchange in connection with Rhone-Poulenc S.A.'s purchase of a majority stake in Rorer Group Inc. are scheduled for August distribution. Their maximum value at July 31, 1994, if Rorer extends them one year, would be $54.12 each. Yesterday's edition incorrectly stated the distribution date and maximum value.

(WSJ July 17, 1990)

UNIVERSAL VOLTRONICS Corp., a Mount Kisco, N.Y., maker of high-voltage power conversion systems, begins trading on the American Stock Exchange tomorrow under the symbol UVL. It is 54%-owned by Thermedics Inc., Woburn, Mass. Universal's name was incorrect in Monday's edition.
