Study Says Gains In Pay to Exceed Inflation in '91

  • Author: Robin Goldwyn Blumenthal
  • Date: 07/16/90

NEW YORK -- Pay raises in the U.S. will outpace inflation next year more than any time in the past three years, a survey by the Conference Board found. The Conference Board, an independent business research group, said pay increases for U.S. salaried workers will average about 5% this year and are expected to rise to an average of nearly 5.5% in 1991. The findings were made in the board's annual private-sector salary survey, which covers 537 companies in various industries.

The board's current projection for the rise in inflation for 1991 is 4.5%, while pay increases for exempt employees are expected to rise to 5.5%. Exempt employees are workers such as salaried professionals and supervisors who are excluded from overtime provisions of the federal wage and salary laws.

The report noted salary increases would be greatest in the diversified services and insurance industries, with gains averaging about 5.5% this year and 5.6% in 1991. Wholesale and retail trade companies reported the lowest salary increases, averaging 5% both this year and next. Average increases for nonexecutive salaried workers in manufacturing will be 5% in 1990 and slightly higher next year.

Executives will receive slightly higher pay increases than other salaried workers both this year and next, with increases averaging 5.5%.

Of the companies surveyed, 82% are using at least one variable pay program, the board said. Eligibility for variable compensation that once was mostly limited to executive and sales personnel is now being extended to other employee groups, the board said.

The variable pay programs "provide an alternative to fixed salary costs while enabling employers to foster, enhance and reward various forms of achievement," said Elizabeth Arreglado, Conference Board compensation specialist.

The most popular form of variable pay is special cash awards for outstanding individual contributions, which was used by 38% of those polled. About 30% offer incentive pay for employees who reach specific performance goals; 29% use recognition programs; 27% have current pay profit-sharing plans; 23% give lump-sum merit awards; 18% offer team incentives; 11% have gain-sharing and skill-based pay programs; and 6% have two-tier pay plans.
