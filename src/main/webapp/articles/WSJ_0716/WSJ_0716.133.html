Iberia and American Reach Pact on Access To Booking System

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/16/90

MADRID -- American Airlines and Iberia Air Lines, Spain's state-owned airline, reached an agreement in a bitter dispute over Iberia's legal access to computer booking systems in the U.S. Iberia agreed to rejoin American's world-wide Sabre computer reservation system in exchange for a modification of the system by American.

In a statement Friday, the Spanish airline said the AMR Corp. unit pledged to change the way international flights are listed in its computer displays. Iberia had complained that it was discriminated against on the company's displays.

American also said it would sell its services to travel agencies at cost, no longer underselling Iberia's booking services. The company added that the new formula was more in line with European Community standards.

The rift between the two airlines began in March when Iberia said it would withdraw from American's Sabre reservation system, claiming the system prejudiced customers against buying Iberia tickets.

American said Iberia's withdrawal was aimed at harming the U.S. carrier's marketing efforts with Spanish travel agents.

The fight intensified when American Airlines filed an unfair-competition complaint against Iberia with the U.S. Department of Transportation.

When the department upheld the complaint, Iberia retaliated by bringing the dispute to EC officials.

Since then, EC and U.S. officials have met and urged the two airline companies to resolve their differences.
