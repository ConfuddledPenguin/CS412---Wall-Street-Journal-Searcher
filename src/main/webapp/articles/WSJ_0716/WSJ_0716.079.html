Gallimard Family Members To Give Up Control of Firm

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 07/16/90

PARIS -- Two of the siblings who had fought for control of the famed Gallimard publishing house have agreed to sell their shares to a bank, setting the stage for the family to lose majority control, an investment firm said Friday. Terms weren't disclosed. If the sale is completed, it would place 54.3% of the shares in France's largest independent publishing house in outside hands, marking the end of 79 years of family control.

Francoise Gallimard and her brother, Christian, have agreed to join their younger sister, Isabelle, in selling their shares to the state-owned Banque Nationale de Paris, according the international investment bank Henry Ansbacher.

Ansbacher was retained by Francoise in January to determine the value of the publishing house.

Francoise, 49, and Christian, 46, had been trying to wrest control of the company away from another brother, Antoine, 42, the firm's president and its largest single shareholder.

According to Ansbacher, Francoise and Christian decided to follow their younger sister's example by selling their shares, giving Banque Nationale de Paris a total of 36.8% of the Gallimard shares. Other outside shareholders control 17.5%.
