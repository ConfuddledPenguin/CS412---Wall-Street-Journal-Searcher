Sears Will Post 40% Decline In Quarterly Net

  • Author: Francine Schwadel
  • Date: 07/16/90

CHICAGO -- Sears, Roebuck & Co. said it expects to report second-quarter profit plunged about 40% because of higher-than-anticipated insurance claims and its deteriorating retail business. The company projected earnings of about 65 cents a share, compared with $391.2 million, or $1.11 a share, a year ago. Earnings from continuing operations in last year's second quarter were $334.9 million, or 95 cents a share.

Edward A. Brennan, chairman and chief executive officer of the retailing and financial services concern, said he continues "to expect a solid performance for the second half of the year, based on our outlook for continued moderate growth in the economy."

But the announcement prompted analysts, many of whom have been trimming their forecasts in recent weeks, to cut their estimates further. Edward Weller of Montgomery Securities said he is now forecasting full-year earnings of $1.35 billion, or $3.94 a share, down from $1.51 billion, or $4.30 a share, in 1989. "It looks like the eighth year of earning approximately $4 a share," Mr. Weller said.

In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange Friday, Sears fell 37.5 cents to close at $34.25. Sears is scheduled to report second-quarter results next week.

The company blamed higher claims at its Allstate insurance unit partly on recent fires in California and said profit at its merchandise group would be lower than a year ago. Analysts noted that the Sears retail business is still struggling to meet its sales plan, and that earnings were under pressure because of markdowns.

Analysts nevertheless saw some cause for optimism. "They're doing more and more of the right things," Mr. Weller said. He cited a pickup in catalog sales after a decade of stagnation and the company's new Brand Central appliance and electronic departments, which are performing well.
