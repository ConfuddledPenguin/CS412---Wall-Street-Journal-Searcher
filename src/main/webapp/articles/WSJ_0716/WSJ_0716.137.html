Business Brief -- United Technologies Corp.: Carrier Corp. to Close Compressor Plant in 1991

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/16/90

HARTFORD, Conn. -- United Technologies Corp. said its Carrier Corp. subsidiary will close a compressor plant next year, resulting in an after-tax charge of $18 million for its second quarter. After the charge, which totals $30 million before taxes, United Technologies expects second-quarter earnings to be about the same as the $197.3 million, or $1.50 a share, net income of the year-before period. A spokesman for United Technologies, which makes aerospace, building and automotive products, said second-quarter results would be reported within the week.

United Technologies said the compressor manufacturing plant in Syracuse, N.Y., was being closed because Carrier can purchase compressors for its residential air conditioners for "significantly less" than it can make them at the facility. The closing, which is set for early next year, will affect 309 employees. United Technologies anticipates many of the employees will be transferred to other parts of Carrier, the spokesman said.

Carrier, a heating ventilation and air-conditioning equipment company with $4 billion in sales last year, already was purchasing about half its compressors from other suppliers, said Howard A. Rubel, an aerospace analyst at C.J. Lawrence, Morgan Grenfell Inc.
