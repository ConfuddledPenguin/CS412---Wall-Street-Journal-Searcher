Credit Ratings: Moody's Cuts Ratings On Debt of National Bank of Hungary

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/16/90

NEW YORK -- Moody's Investors Service Inc. said it downgraded its rating on National Bank of Hungary debt to Ba-1 from Baa-2. The rating concern said the action affects about $2.9 billion in long-term debt of the National Bank of Hungary, which is the conduit that channels foreign-currency borrowings into Hungary's treasury.

Moody's cited several reasons for its move.

As communist regimes collapse in Eastern Europe, Moody's said, "Hungary will have to compete with the sudden -- and much larger -- level of demands for credit from all countries in the area."

Economic and political crises have intensified payments difficulties throughout the Eastern European region, Moody's said, and private lenders are reluctant to increase, or even maintain, their credit exposure there.

And Moody's said that as the East Bloc unravels, Hungary's growth in output will be hurt and pressures on employment will be created. "As Hungary is forced to compete in the world markets, its hard-currency cash flow is likely to suffer as well," the rating concern said.

Officials of the National Bank of Hungary office in New York couldn't be reached to comment.
