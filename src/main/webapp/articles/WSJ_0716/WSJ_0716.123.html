Semicon May File Under Chapter 11; Declining Sales Cited

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/16/90

BURLINGTON, Mass. -- Semicon Inc., citing declining sales, said it won't be able to pay interest on its debt and may file for protection from creditors under Chapter 11 of the U.S. Bankruptcy Code, unless agreements can be worked out with them. The semiconductor devices maker said it is holding discussions with its banks and debenture trustee. A company spokesman characterized talks as optimistic.

For the year ended June 30, 1989, Semicon had net income of $1.6 million, or 50 cents a share, on sales of $15.5 million. However, Semicon said, because of the continuing recession in the semiconductor industry, its annual revenue has shrunk to about $10 million.

With sales at that level, the company said it has enough money only to run the business in fiscal 1991, and not to meet its debt service obligations.

Semicon said it missed a $268,125 semiannual interest payment due July 15 on its 13% convertible subordinated debentures. The company said it is seeking to restructure its debt, adding that unless it can reduce debt expense significantly, it won't be able to continue as a viable company.

Semicon said it plans to release financial results late next month.
