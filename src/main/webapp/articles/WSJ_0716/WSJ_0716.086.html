International: Tapie Expected to Disclose Financing for Adidas Deal

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/16/90

PARIS -- Bernard Tapie and his lead banker are promising today to confound the skeptics who said he would have trouble completing his takeover of West German sporting goods giant Adidas AG. After a week's negotiations, his lead banker said it has arranged the financing package that Mr. Tapie had said he would disclose today. Credit Lyonnais's Societe de Banque Occidentale unit announced Friday that it has more offers of funds than Mr. Tapie needs. (One of Mr. Tapie's other main banks, Credit Agricole, said Friday that it didn't have enough information to make a decision.)

Although Mr. Tapie has refused so far to disclose the price of the Adidas purchase, it seems likely to be well above the 889 million French franc ($160.5 million) market valuation of Bernard Tapie Finance S.A., his main holding company. So far, the flamboyant French financier hasn't explained how he will fund the takeover or how it will affect his other companies.

Because of that, French securities regulators last week ordered trading suspended in Bernard Tapie Finance and its two listed subsidiaries, industrial scale-makers Terraillon S.A. and Testut S.A. It was after the suspension that Mr. Tapie promised to disclose financial details today.
