Credit Ratings: S&P Cuts Ratings On Xerox Corp. Debt Valued at $2 Billion

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/16/90

NEW YORK -- Standard & Poor's Corp. lowered its ratings on about $2 billion of debt of Xerox Corp. and certain subsidiaries, asserting that the company's profitability "will remain under pressure" and that debt leverage will remain near current levels over the intermediate term. S&P lowered senior debt ratings of Xerox, Xerox Credit Corp. and Xerox Credit Overseas Finance N.V. to single-A from single-A-plus. Subordinated debt was lowered to single-A-minus from single-A and preferred stock to single-A-minus from single-A-plus. The single-A-1 commercial paper rating was affirmed.

A spokesman for Stamford, Conn.-based Xerox, a manufacturer of copiers and duplicators, had no comment on the S&P announcement.

S&P said document processing business profitability has improved, but "the deteriorating profitability of the financial services side . . . continues to drag down overall returns."

It noted that Xerox's Crum & Forster Insurance Group is facing a "continuing downturn in the property casualty insurance industry."

S&P also said it lowered its claims-paying ability rating for Crum & Forster Insurance Group to single-A-plus from double-A. It had been placed on S&P's Creditwatch surveillance list June 1 because of concern about the quality of reinsurance recoverables and potential impact on capitalization.

S&P said that since the mid-1980s, Crum & Forster has "instituted needed controls" to strengthen its reinsurance program but suffers from "reinsurance exposures from business written prior to this time."
