Prudential-Bache Plans New Marketing Strategy

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/16/90

NEW YORK -- In the battle to pull in new mutual-fund customers, Prudential-Bache Securities is planning a new strategy: Let 'em in free. Prudential-Bache, which runs the country's 13th largest fund group with $17 billion in assets, has asked the Securities and Exchange Commission for permission to sell its mutual funds to customers of other broker-sold mutual funds -- without charging a sales commission.

A Prudential-Bache spokesman declined to comment on the filing, noting that the SEC still hasn't declared it effective. But others note that, while Prudential-Bache, a unit of Prudential Insurance Co. of America, may be the largest fund group ever to make such an offer, its offer isn't unique.

"We've done it for three years, and we're not the first," said Edward Mahoney, president of AMEV Advisers Inc., which allows owners of other broker-sold funds to buy AMEV funds, provided they show proof that they sold the other fund within the previous 60 days.

The idea is to allow a broker to switch a customer between funds without the client's being charged two sets of commissions.

What has Prudential-Bache got to attract other fund customers? Kanon Bloch Carre & Co., a Boston mutual-fund research firm, gives high marks to Prudential-Bache's utility fund, its intermediate-term government-bond fund and its convertible-bond fund. But overall, Kanon Bloch ranks Prudential's fund family as 40th out of 94.
