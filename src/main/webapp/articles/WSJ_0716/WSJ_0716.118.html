Marketing & Media: Former Ingersoll Unit To Sell Chanry String To Its Original Owner

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/16/90

Community Newspapers Inc., the newspaper chain that until recently belonged to Ingersoll Publications Co., agreed to sell its string of weekly newspapers on Long Island, N.Y., to its original owner. Stanley Henry, chairman of Distribution Consultants Inc., said he will acquire the assets to Chanry Communications Ltd., which publishes This Week newspapers, a chain of 78 weeklies on Long Island. Mr. Henry was president and publisher of Chanry before selling it in 1987 to Community Newspapers, one of several newspaper holding companies once controlled by Ingersoll.

Community Newspapers has been seeking a buyer for the chain for months. The newspapers have been battered by a recession on Long Island that has turned the once-profitable chain into a money loser. Robert Jelenic, chief executive officer of Community Newspapers, said no other asset sales were planned. Community Newspapers and Ingersoll's other U.S. newspaper companies were acquired by investment banker E.M. Warburg Pincus & Co. in early July after Warburg ended its partnership with Ralph Ingersoll, chairman of Ingersoll Publications.

A spokesman for Community Newspapers said that while Chanry's liabilities will be assumed by Mr. Henry's Distribution Consultants, "the cash involved in the transaction will be plus or minus $1 million." In 1987, Community Newspapers paid an estimated $30 million for Chanry, including the cost of upgrading the papers from shoppers to weekly newspapers heavy with local news.

Mr. Henry, who built the Chanry chain, said it was "not a great time" to be acquiring newspapers. "But I am a Long Island native," Mr. Henry said. "There is a recession now, but I believe it will come back." Mr. Henry is also chairman of Alternate Postal Delivery Inc., a national private deliverer of magazines, catalogs and direct mail.
