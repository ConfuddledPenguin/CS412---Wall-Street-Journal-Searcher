Italy Urges the EC To Discuss Energy With U.S. and Japan

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/16/90

BRUSSELS -- Italy asked the European Community Friday to launch a broad debate with the U.S. and Japan on global energy issues, including a discussion on imposing taxes on energy consumption. In a report, the Italian government said the "rapid growth" in global energy use demands a concerted international effort to cut consumption, save energy and promote renewable sources of it.

"All these actions . . . need to be international with the more industrialized countries taking the lead so as to prevent serious consequences for the world economy," the report said.

The report assesses global energy output and consumption trends. It will be discussed at a meeting in Rome today by ministers responsible for energy matters of the 12 EC nations.

The energy document said the EC's energy goals until 1995, drafted in 1985, are outdated because oil prices have dropped sharply since then, and the 1986 accident at Chernobyl shattered confidence in nuclear power.

"However, the most important reason for rethinking all energy matters is the concern over the environment that has arisen in recent years and specifically the fear of a global warming of our planet due to the action of greenhouse gases," the report said.
