French Broker's Suspension Worrying Market Insiders

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/16/90

PARIS -- Tuffier-Ravier-Py & Associes' brokerage firm was suspended from trading Friday, following an inability to settle its bills. Stock market insiders now fear the action will frighten some foreigners away from the French market. Tuffier, one of the five largest French brokerages, is the largest to run into financial trouble following France's 1988 stock market deregulation. It is the main unit of the brokerage group Tuffier & Associes.

Tuffier said its brokerage arm posted a 62 million-franc loss ($11.3 million) in the first five months of the year, after a 16 million-franc net profit for 1989, down from a 52 million-franc net profit for 1988.

In practical terms, the suspension means that Tuffier's stock brokerage activities are frozen where they were at the end of the day Friday, when the suspension was announced. The suspension may not directly affect the group's other subsidiaries, which weren't mentioned in the exchange authorities' statement.

If the brokerage doesn't return to business, a rescue fund will reimburse cash deposits up to 500,000 francs, and share transactions up to 2.5 million francs.

Foreign experts say they hope the incident won't seriously affect investment in the French market. "When a business in the marketplace does have trouble, it does make people nervous generally," said Geoffrey Kelly, a director of Barclays de Zoete Wedd Ltd., Barclays Bank's London investment banking unit.
