U.K. Retail Price Index Rises

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/16/90

LONDON -- Britain's retail price index rose 0.4% in June from May and was up 9.8% from a year ago, the Central Statistical Office said. The underlying level of inflation, which excludes mortgage payments and community tax charges, was up 6.9% in June, compared with 7.0% in May.

The statistical office said the 9.8% annual rise was the highest since March 1982, when the advance was 10.4%. The office attributed the rise to higher housing costs, as well as further increases in gas and electricity prices. The office noted that sharp falls in some food prices were offset by increases in other foods.
