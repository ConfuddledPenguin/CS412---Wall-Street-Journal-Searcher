Business Brief -- Suffield Financial Corp.: Loss of $2.2 Million Posted For Quarter After Charge

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/16/90

Suffield Financial Corp., citing a continued sluggishness in New England's real-estate market, charged off about $2.1 million in loan-loss provisions and posted a second-quarter loss of $2.2 million, or 36 cents a share. This compares with a $472,000 profit, or eight cents a share, the Suffield, Conn., bank-holding company reported the year earlier.

Suffield said it wrote off about $1.7 million of nonperforming loans in the quarter and took the $2.1 million charge to replenish its loan-loss reserves.

"There has been no significant increase in nonperforming assets during the second quarter," a company release said, adding: "Nevertheless, we believe it is in the best interests of the company to increase the allowance for loan losses during these uncertain economic times in New England."

For the six months, the company reported a loss of $5.4 million, or 90 cents a share, compared with earnings of $988,000, or 16 cents a share, the year earlier.
