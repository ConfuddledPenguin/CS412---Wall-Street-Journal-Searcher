Business Brief -- Cambrex Corp.: Venture Seeks Part of Line Of American Cyanamid Co.

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/16/90

Cambrex Corp., a specialty chemical maker based in East Rutherford, N.J., said it intends to become the majority partner in a joint venture that would acquire part of the textile-chemical business of American Cyanamid Co., a Wayne, N.J., maker of chemicals and pharmaceuticals. The parties declined to disclose terms of the proposed transaction. But Cambrex said the operations to be acquired include Cyanamid's textile-chemical plant in Charlotte, N.C., and have annual sales exceeding $20 million. The deal is subject to a definitive pact, board approvals and other conditions. Cyanamid confirmed that the transaction outlined by Cambrex is under negotiation, but it declined to say why it would sell the textile-chemical operations.

Cambrex's minority partner in the venture would be an investor group led by L. David Moore, an executive most recently with Interchem Inc., then an Atlanta unit of RTZ Corp., London. Reached in Atlanta, Mr. Moore said he left Interchem as president last October, shortly after it was acquired by Rhone-Poulenc S.A. of France.
