Business Brief -- Chock Full O'Nuts Corp.: Coffee Producer May Take Charge of 41 Cents a Share

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/16/90

Chock Full O'Nuts Corp. said it might have to take a charge of as much as 41 cents a share because it terminated certain leases it had with Riese Organization, the big closely held New York-based restaurant operator. Riese subleases space in some properties that the coffee producer and food-service company leases from another landlord. Riese hasn't paid rent to Chock Full since February because it contends that the properties had deficiencies including broken air conditioners and elevators. Chock Full asserts that the cost for any such repairs falls to Riese.

If Chock Full needs to take the charge, a total of as much as almost $3.4 million after tax, it would cover past-due rent, accrued future rents, deferred costs, and estimated future expense incurred in regaining the properties and carrying them until it re-rents them.

For the nine months ended April 30, Chock Full earned $6.8 million, or 87 cents a share, on $181 million of sales. The earnings reflect a $1 million gain from a debt redemption.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading Friday, Chock Full's common fell 62.5 cents to $7.75.
