Business Brief -- Comfed Bancorp.: Thrift's Revamp Plan Fails To Get Federal Approval

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/16/90

Comfed Bancorp., struggling to keep from being declared insolvent by federal regulators, said a plan to restructure the thrift failed to receive approval by the federal Office of Thrift Supervision. Comfed, Lowell, Mass., said that the action was "not unexpected," but that it continues to seek regulatory approval.

Last month, the holding company for Comfed Savings Bank said it expected to receive a report from its auditors, expressing serious doubt about its ability to continue as a going concern.

The company also reported a net loss of $38.2 million, or $4.57 a share, for its fourth quarter ended March 31. A year earlier, Comfed had a loss of $3.5 million, or 42 cents a share. For the fiscal year, the company's net loss was $47.9 million, or $5.73 a share, compared with a year-earlier loss of $3.5 million, or 42 cents a share.

Comfed has assets of $1.4 billion. The thrift's decline, as with many others in New England, came as a result of a soured real estate market in the region.
