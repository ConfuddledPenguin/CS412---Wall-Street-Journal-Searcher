Business Brief -- Bundaberg Sugar Co.: Company's Operating Profit Rose 24% for Fiscal 1990

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/16/90

Bundaberg Sugar Co. of Australia reported that after-tax operating profit for the year ended April 30 rose 24% to 28.2 million Australian dollars (US$22.5 million) from A$22.8 million a year earlier. An estimated 10.7% increase in the price of sugar to A$361 a metric ton-equal to about 2,204.62 pounds -- boosted profit, the company said, and all major sectors of the company performed strongly. Bundaberg forecast in February that its profit would rise at least 15%, largely because of higher sugar prices.

Pretax operating profit rose 25% to A$46.8 million from A$37.5 million. The company had extraordinary profit of A$32.7 million from selling its interest in a coal joint venture; the year-earlier period didn't have any special item. Proceeds were used partly to eliminate long-term debt and purchase assets to strengthen core businesses, Bundaberg said.

Revenue increased 6.8% to A$299.9 million from A$280.8 million.

The dividend was raised 33% to 20 cents a share from 15 cents.
