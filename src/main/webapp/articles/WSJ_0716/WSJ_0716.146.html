Business Brief -- Micron Products Inc.: Creditors Force Numedco Into Bankruptcy Proceedings

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/16/90

Micron Products Inc., New York, said unsecured creditors of its troubled Numedco Inc. subsidiary have moved to force Numedco into bankruptcy-law proceedings. Micron said the creditors filed a complaint in U.S. Bankruptcy Court in Philadelphia. Rather than undergo involuntary proceedings, Numedco will ask for protection under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code, Micron said. Under Chapter 11, a company continues to operate, with protection from creditor lawsuits, while working out a plan to settle debts.

Kuno Laren, chairman of Micron, said Numedco, which has been unprofitable for seven quarters, has been attempting to sell assets to satisfy secured debt. Mr. Laren estimated that the unsecured creditors, unpaid since June 6, are owed $3 million.

Micron is a maker of medical products; Numedco, which is curtailing operations, has been a distributor of medical and surgical supplies. According to Mr. Laren, the parent company hasn't guaranteed any of Numedco's unsecured debts.

Micron said its manufacturing business remains profitable "and will continue."
