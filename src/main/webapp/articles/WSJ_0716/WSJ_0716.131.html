Corporate Data Investor Group May Sell Its Stake

  • Author: Kathleen A. Hughes
  • Date: 07/16/90

LOS ANGELES -- Corporate Data Sciences Inc. said an investor group that includes its president, Keith Brackpool, is negotiating the possible sale of a 37% stake in the company to an unidentified investor group and two British companies. In December 1988, the investor group that includes Mr. Brackpool, Nelson Asset Management, purchased its stake for $20.6 million from the same two British companies, Polly Peck PLC and Telfos Holdings PLC.

Corporate Data currently has 13.5 million common shares outstanding.

According to the company's financial statements, one of the British companies, Telfos Holdings, is also a principal in Nelson Asset Management, which is based in Bermuda. The other principals in Nelson Asset Management are Kazan Corp., a private European investment group, and Alfred Shoenberg, a former director of Corporate Data and the current president of Aril Group Inc., which is 50%-owned by Corporate Data.

In a related move, the company said James Frew, 63 years old, resigned as chairman and a director of Chicago-based Consolidated Packaging Corp., which is 92%-owned by Corporate Data Sciences. The company said Mr. Frew's resignation from the packaging company is a "prelude to the anticipated involvement of a new investor group."

Mr. Frew couldn't be reached for comment. A Corporate Data spokesman said his resignation from the post "was mutual." He will continue as a consultant.

Corporate Data Sciences said the two British companies have also provided credit facilities to Nelson in connection with the purchase of the 37% stake. The company said Poly Peck has extended the due date of a loan to Nelson until Aug. 30 to allow a "satisfactory conclusion" to the talks.

Corporate Data is involved in a restructuring that will divide it into two separately traded companies, Consolidated Packaging, a paper concern, and Aril Group Inc., a holding company that will have kitchen-cabinet operations. Mr. Brackpool said he plans to resign as president of Corporate Data when the restructuring is completed.

Corporate Data said it expects to complete the previously announced exchange of its Medallion Kitchens subsidiaries for 16.9 million shares of Aril Group Inc. common stock before the end of this month. The exchange will increase Corporate Data's stake in Aril to 81% from about 50%. Aril, formerly called Marcom Telecommunications Inc., sold its telecommunications business last December, leaving it without any operating business.

When that exchange is completed, Corporate Data said it will distribute 22.1 million shares of Aril to Corporate Data stock of record Jan. 22 at a rate of 1.63 shares for each share of Corporate Data held.
