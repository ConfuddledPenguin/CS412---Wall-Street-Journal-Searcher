Business Brief -- Southeast Banking Corp.: Net Loss of $20.7 Million Is Posted for 2nd Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/16/90

Southeast Banking Corp., Miami, reported a second-quarter net loss of $20.7 million, or 66 cents a share, reflecting a previously announced surge in nonperforming assets. Southeast had net income of $32.3 million, or $1 a share, a year earlier.

In mid-June, Southeast said it expected to report a second-quarter loss because of the worsening condition of the Florida real-estate market. In line with that expectation, the banking company said nonperforming assets rose to $619 million as of June 30, from $474 million at March 31. The reserve for loan losses was boosted to $279 million, or 2.73% of loans, from $258 million at the end of March.

Nonperforming assets are loans that have been foreclosed or are more than 90 days past due. Loan-loss reserves are a fund that banks set aside to offset expected losses from bad loans.

For the first half, Southeast reported a loss of $77.5 million, or $2.41 a share, compared with earnings of $65.4 million, or $2.04 a share, a year earlier.
