Business Brief -- Iomega Corp.: Quarterly Profit Grew 80%; Output Performance Cited

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/16/90

Iomega Corp., citing better manufacturing performance, said its second-quarter earnings were up 80% on a slight increase in sales. Iomega, which makes data-storage equipment for computers, said its net income was $3 million, or 18 cents a share, up from $1.7 million, or 10 cents a share, in the year-ago period. Sales grew 4.6% to $27.2 million from $26 million.

The Roy, Utah, company attributed the net income rise to increased gross margins stemming from better manufacturing performance and product-cost reductions. Iomega said its gross margins were 48% in the quarter, compared with 41% in the year-ago period. The company said its improved margins allowed it to invest more in sales and marketing and in research and development in the period.
