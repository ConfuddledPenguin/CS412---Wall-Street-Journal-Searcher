Business Brief -- Permanent Savings Bank: Regulators Close N.Y. S&L, Sell Deposits to Key Bank

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/16/90

Federal bank regulators closed Permanent Savings Bank, Niagara Falls, N.Y., and sold its deposits to Key Bank of Western New York National Association. Under the terms of the transaction, Key Bank, of Buffalo, N.Y., will assume $319 million of Permanent's deposits, and will purchase $321.7 million in assets at a discount of $499,000.

The Federal Deposit Insurance Corp. said the transaction reduces the possibility of loss to the bank-insurance fund.
