`People Mover' to Be Built At Newark Airport by '94

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/16/90

NEW YORK -- The Port Authority of New York and New Jersey authorized a $378 million people mover system at Newark International Airport. The system, scheduled for completion by 1994, will transport passengers between the airport's terminals and parking lots and replace most of the current on-airport bus routes.

Von Roll Transport Systems Inc., Thun, Switzerland, will design, construct, operate and maintain the system, which will consist of automated vehicles on fixed, elevated guideways. The company has built similar systems in Australia and England.

The system is part of a major Port Authority redevelopment program; officials project that Newark will be handling 40 million travelers a year by the late 1990s, double last year's volume.

The Port Authority also approved a $40 million project to convert Newark's North Terminal to a Continental Airlines air cargo facility and build a 160,000-square-foot hangar adjacent to it.

Continental, the principal unit of Continental Airlines Holdings Inc., received a commitment from the New Jersey Economic Development Authority to finance the project.
