Bird Corp. Says Profit From Ongoing Lines Gained in 2nd Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/16/90

NEW YORK -- Bird Inc., meeting with financial analysts here, said it expects earnings from continuing operations to be 45 cents to 50 cents a share for the second quarter, compared with one cent a share in the corresponding quarter last year. Despite depressed conditions in the housing market, the building materials distributor said its performance improved with the help of concentration in its repair and remodeling sector, and with its plant modernization programs.

In the year-ago second quarter, ended June 30, 1989, a $5.3-million gain on the sale of one of Bird's units, plus a tax-loss carryforward of $3.3 million, brought final net income to about $9 million, or $2 a share. Sales were $32.7 million.

Final results for the 1990 second quarter are to be released later this month.

Earnings from continued operations for the first six months of 1990 are expected to be approximately 60 cents a share, compared with 11 cents in the year-ago period.

The Westwood, Mass., concern said that it plans to sell a 40% interest in its Housing Group in a private placement of securities. The offering is managed by C.J. Lawrence, Morgan Grenfell Inc.
