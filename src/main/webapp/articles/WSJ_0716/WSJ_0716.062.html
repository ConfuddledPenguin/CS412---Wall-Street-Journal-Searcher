Credit Ratings: Corporate Credit Quality Fell in Quarter, S&P Says

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/16/90

NEW YORK -- Corporate credit quality fell sharply in the second quarter, Standard & Poor's Corp. said. The rating concern, part of McGraw-Hill Inc., said it lowered ratings on about $190 billion of debt in the quarter and raised ratings on $34 billion. The number of downgrades, 207, greatly exceeded the 50 upgrades.

Two large debt issuers, Ford Motor Credit Co. and Chrysler Financial Corp., contributed heavily to the volume of downgraded debt in the latest quarter. "In addition, non-U.S. corporate rating changes were all negative," S&P said. It also noted bank holding companies had 23 downgrades but only one upgrade.

Separately, S&P said municipal credit quality remained weak in the second quarter, largely because of downgrades among housing issues and ratings of National Rural Utilities Cooperative Finance Corp.

S&P estimated that it lowered ratings on $14.5 billion of municipal debt and raised ratings on $8.8 billion. Downgrades totaled 139 in the quarter, and upgrades 45.
