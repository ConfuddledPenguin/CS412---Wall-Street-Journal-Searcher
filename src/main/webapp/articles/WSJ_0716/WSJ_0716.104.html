Business Brief -- First New York Business Bank: Second-Period Net Plunged As Loan-Loss Reserves Rose

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/16/90

First New York Business Bank Corp., New York, said second-quarter earnings plummeted after additions to its loan-loss reserves in a soft New York metropolitan area real estate market. The holding company for First New York Bank for Business said net income was $61,000, or two cents a share, against $1 million, or 27 cents a share, in the year-earlier quarter. It said it added $3.9 million to reserves. The quarter's results also reflect a one-time gain of $2 million on the sale of a leasehold.

For the six months, First New York reported net income of $794,000, or 21 cents a share, against $1.9 million, or 51 cents a share, the year before.

Martin A. Simon, chairman, said that "in light of current economic conditions, especially in the real estate area, the company's priorities for the first six months were to strengthen our capital ratio while maintaining high loan loss reserves." The loss reserve now stands at 1.36% of loans, and total capital is 8.34% of assets, the company said. Total assets at June 30 stood at $712.2 million.

First New York stock closed Friday at $5, up 37.5 cents, in national over-the-counter trading.
