NESB's Bank Unit Plans to Shed 13% Of Staff and 3 Offices

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/16/90

NEW LONDON, Conn. -- NESB Corp.'s New England Savings Bank unit plans to eliminate 56, or 13%, of its jobs, close two offices and sell a third in a major cost-cutting move. The bank didn't specify the charge it will take for the job cuts, to be made through dismissals and attrition.

But it "is not going to be that large. What we will save over the balance of the year will more than offset the severance costs," Seymour Smith, chief financial officer at NESB, said. The bank expects to save $1.4 million a year with the job cuts, plus $900,000 from other operating-cost cuts.

NESB plans to close offices in Branford and Clinton Plaza and will sell the Milford office. The job reductions, coupled with those taken earlier this year, will leave the bank with 358 employees, down 21% from 455 a year ago.

Mr. Smith declined to estimate second-quarter results, saying the bank holding company, which has $1.3 billion in assets, will report them within about two weeks. In the year-earlier second quarter, NESB had a net loss of $6.8 million, after a $16 million provision for possible loan losses.
