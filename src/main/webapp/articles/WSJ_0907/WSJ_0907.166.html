Business Brief -- CMS Enhancements Inc.: Firm Posts Quarterly Loss; Inventory Write-Down Cited

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/07/90

CMS Enhancements Inc. said it had a net loss for the fourth quarter, ended June 30, of $1.7 million, or 55 cents a share, compared with year-earlier net income of $1.2 million, or 48 cents a share, restated to reflect a 1-for-4 reverse stock split Dec. 5. In the latest quarter the Tustin, Calif., company had 3.2 million shares outstanding, up 24% from the year-earlier restated 2.6 million shares. Revenue in the quarter dropped 22%, to $33.6 million from $42.8 million.

CMS makes and markets mass-data storage devices and accessories for most major brands of personal and laptop computers. Jim Farooquee, president, said the fourth-quarter loss is mainly the result of a $5 million write-down of the company's tape backup subsystem inventory. Revenue dropped because of increased competition, he added. The executive said the company looks at fiscal 1991 with "cautious optimism."
