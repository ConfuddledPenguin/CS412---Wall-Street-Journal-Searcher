International Brief -- MAN AG: West German Firm's Profit For Fiscal 1990 Rose 25%

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/07/90

MAN AG of West Germany, an engineering concern and vehicle maker, said in a preliminary report that its group profit for the year ended June 30 rose 25% to 320 million marks ($205.2 million) from 254 million marks a year earlier. Sales increased 11% to 18.95 billion marks from 17.05 billion marks. Of the total, sales in West Germany rose 13% to 7.38 billion marks, and foreign sales advanced 10% to 11.57 billion marks.

The board on Oct. 19 will decide on the annual cash dividend, which was eight marks a share for the previous year.

MAN said new orders in fiscal 1990 were strong, rising 8.7% to 20.74 billion marks, sparked by new orders abroad, which rose 12% to 12.6 billion marks. Domestic orders increased 4%.

MAN also said it plans to acquire "in the coming years" the 20% of Steyr Nutzfahrzeuge AG that it doesn't already own, but wasn't specific about timing. MAN last year acquired 80% of the unit from the Austrian industrial concern Steyr-Daimler-Puch AG, which owns the remaining 20%.
