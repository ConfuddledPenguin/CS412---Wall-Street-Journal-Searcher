Marketing & Media: Oscar Mayer Foods to Settle Its Suit Against Sara Lee

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/07/90

MADISON, Wis. -- The food fight is over. Oscar Mayer Foods Corp. said it agreed to settle the patent infringement lawsuit it filed in January that accused Sara Lee Corp. of copying its design for a ready-to-eat product called "Lunchables."

Under terms of the agreement, Sara Lee will, among other things, redesign its own "Lunch 'n Munch" package by Dec. 31, said Oscar Mayer, a processed meat business of Philip Morris Cos., New York.

Oscar Mayer said a design change that Sara Lee has already made wasn't satisfactory.

Oscar Mayer wouldn't disclose the sum that it said Sara Lee will pay it as a retroactive royalty on the packages that Sara Lee has already produced.

Oscar Mayer's ready-to-eat "Lunchables" line and the "Lunch 'n Munch" line sold by Sara Lee contain trays of cheese, crackers and processed meat.

Oscar Mayer complained that the Sara Lee package was too similar to its own -- a square see-through box containing a three-compartment tray.

A Sara Lee spokeswoman said the company wouldn't comment.
