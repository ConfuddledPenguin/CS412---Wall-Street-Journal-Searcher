Fitchburg to Seek Alternatives to Bid By Eastern Utilities

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/07/90

WASHINGTON -- Fitchburg Gas & Electric Light Co. said its board authorized the company's management to explore several alternatives to a proposed buy-out by Eastern Utilities Associates, including a merger or business combination with a third party. In a filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission, the Fitchburg, Mass.-based company said it may engage in discussions with third parties. These discussions could lead to negotiations with respect to a merger, leveraged buy-out, recapitalization, restructuring, the sale of debt or equity, or a major acquisition, the company said.

The board's decision came at a Sept. 5 meeting, after Fitchburg's independent committee reaffirmed its earlier decision to reject Eastern Utilities's offer. Eastern Utilities earlier offered $36 a share for Fitchburg, and $40 a share for Unitil Corp., which agreed to merge with Fitchburg last year.

The committee concluded that greater value would be realized by remaining independent or pursuing a merger, the company said.

A spokesman for Fitchburg said the latest filing will allow the company more "flexibility" and "options" not previously available. Fitchburg Gas & Electric Light said it has had Merrill Lynch Capital Markets as its financial advisor since announcing its merger agreement with Unitil Corp. last year.
