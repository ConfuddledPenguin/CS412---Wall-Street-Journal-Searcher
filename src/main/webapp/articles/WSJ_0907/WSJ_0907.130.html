Fleet/Norstar Units Agree to Guidelines Of U.S. Regulators

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/07/90

PROVIDENCE, R.I. -- Fleet/Norstar Financial Group Inc. said its New Hampshire and Connecticut bank units have entered into "memoranda of understanding" with federal banking regulators concerning lending and management practices. In a filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission, Fleet said the memoranda -- a mild form of regulatory punishment that serves as an acknowlegement of past mistakes being corrected or since corrected -- include capital adequacy, loan administration, reserve and charge-off requirements, and underwriting practices.

A number of New England banks have faced much harsher regulatory disciplinary actions. Bank of New England, for example, was forced to sign a cease-and-desist order in March, one of the agency's strictest regulatory actions in its arsenal, while Bank of Boston entered an agreement in October to review a number of management functions.

Fleet/Norstar's New Hampshire and Connecticut units comprise about 11% of Fleet's $35 billion in assets.

In the filing, Fleet said that at the request of its regulators, it will "continue to refine and strengthen oversight and reporting systems and procedures to permit it to respond to the current negative economic environment."
