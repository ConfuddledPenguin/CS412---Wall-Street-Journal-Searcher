Technology Brief -- Apple Computer Inc.: U.S. Division Cuts Prices On Popular Macintosh IIci

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/07/90

Apple Computer Inc.'s USA division said it slashed prices as much as 20% effective yesterday on its popular Macintosh IIci personal computer. The price reduction is tied to the Cupertino, Calif., company's Oct. 15 introduction of three new lower-priced personal computer models, said analyst Bruce M. Lupatkin of Hambrecht and Quist in San Francisco.

"It's basically product positioning," Lupatkin said.

Apple officials denied this was the case, saying they were simply following up on a successful marketing program two months ago under which dealers were given flexibility in offering discounts on the product.

"Since then we've seen sales of the IIci take off," said Bob Puette, president of Apple USA.

The prices were slashed $1,500 on Apple's four system configurations for the Macintosh IIci. The highest-priced model with a 4MB RAM/SuperDrive floppy, for instance, was cut to $5,969 from $7,469. The Macintosh IIci was introduced in September 1989 and is considered in the middle of the company's Macintosh line.

Apple Computer shares closed at $35.75, down 25 cents, in national over-the-counter trading yesterday.
