New Securities Issues

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/07/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report. MUNICIPAL

California Housing Finance Agency -- $185 million of home mortgage revenue bonds, Series D, due Aug. 1, 2010 and 2031, with capital appreciation bonds due Feb. 1 and Aug. 1, 2000-2004 and Aug. 1, 2020, tentatively priced by a Merrill Lynch Capital Markets group to yield from 7.40% in 2000 to 8.10% in 2020. There are $31.2 million of 7 3/4% term bonds priced at par due in 2010 and $129.9 million of 7 7/8% term bonds priced at par due in 2031. Capital appreciation bonds are priced to yield from 7.40% in 2000 to 7.75% in 2004. There is $104.4 million of capital appreciation bonds priced to yield 8.10% to maturity in 2020. The bonds are rated double-A/single-A-plus.

Virginia Housing Development Authority -- $132.9 million of commonwealth mortgage bonds, 1990 Series A, Subseries A-STEM, due June 26, 1991, and 1990 Series B, Subseries BSTEM-II, due April 24, 1991, priced by a First Boston Corp. group. There are $64.2 million of 6.45% bonds priced at par due June 26, 1991, and $68.7 million of 6% bonds priced at par due April 24, 1991. Interest on the Series A bonds will treated as a preference item in calculating the federal alternative minimum tax that may be imposed on certain investors. The Series B bonds are not subject to the alternative minimum tax. The bonds are rated V-MIG-1/single-A-1plus.

New York State Medical Care Facilities Finance Agency -- $114 million of FHA-insured hospital mortgage revenue bonds for Presbyterian Hospital, priced Wednesday by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets group. The issue, 1990 Series A, due Feb. 15, 2000, 2009 and 2025, is priced to yield from 7.10% in 2000 to 7.79% in 2025. There are $19.8 million of 7.10% term bonds priced at par due in 2000, $38.3 million of 7.70% term bonds priced at 99 3/4 to yield 7.73% in 2009 and $56.1 million of 7.70% term bonds priced at 98 7/8 to yield 7.79% in 2025. Ratings of double-A/double-A are expected on the issue.

New York City Industrial Development Agency -- $84 million of special facility revenue bonds, 1990 American Airlines Inc. Project, due July 1, 2019 and 2020, tentatively priced by a Bear Stearns & Co. group to yield 8.06% in 2019 and 8.07% in 2020. There are about $13 million of 7 3/4% term bonds priced at 96 1/2 to yield 8.06% in 2019 and $71 million of 8% term bonds priced at 99 1/4 to yield 8.07% in 2020. Interest on the bonds will be treated as a preference item in calculating the federal alternative minimum tax. The bonds are rated single-A-3/single-A.

University Community Hospital Inc. (A Florida Corp. Not for Profit) -- $81.4 million of revenue and refunding revenue bonds priced Wednesday by a Merrill Lynch Capital Markets group. The issue, Series 1990, due Sept. 1, 1992-2002, 2007 and 2011, is priced to yield from 6.10% in 1992 to 7.55% in 2011. Current interest serial bonds are priced at par to yield from 6.10% in 1992. There are $29.2 million of 7 3/8% term bonds priced at 99 to yield 7.48% in 2007. There are $32.2 million of 7 1/2% term bonds priced at 99 1/2 to yield 7.55% in 2011. The bonds are insured and triple-A-rated.

MORTGAGES

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $300 million of Remic mortgage securities offered in seven classes by Nomura Securities International Inc. The offering, Series 1990122, is backed by Fannie Mae 9 1/2% securities with a weighted average coupon rate of 10.125% and weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29.58 years. Yields are to range from 8.84%, or 82 basis points over two-year Treasury securities, for a class with a weighted average life of 2.46 years to 9.92%, or 108 basis points over 10-year Treasurys, for securities with an average life of 11.37 years. A floating-rate class was priced to yield 85 basis points above the one-month London interbank offered rate, with an 11.5% cap.

EUROBONDS

Mitsui Taiyo Kobe (Asia) Ltd. (Japanese parent) -- $1 billion of subordinated floating-rate notes due September 2000 via Mitsui Taiyo Kobe International. Interest at three-month London interbank offered rate plus 0.30 point for the first five years and then Libor plus 0.40 point until maturity. Issue price is par. Callable after five years. Subordinated guarantee by Mitsui Taiyo Kobe Bank. Fees 0.20.

Kawasaki Steel Corp. (Japan) -- $350 million of Eurobonds due Sept. 20, 1994, with equity-purchase warrants, indicating a 5 1/8% coupon at par, via Nomura International plc. Each $5,000 bond carries one warrant, exercisable from Oct. 11, 1990, through Sept. 6, 1994, to buy shares at an expected premium of 2 1/2% to the closing price when terms are fixed Tuesday. Fees 2 1/4.

Nippon Kinzoku Co. (Japan) -- $70 million of Eurobonds due Oct. 4, 1994, with equity-purchase warrants, indicating a 5 1/2% coupon at par, via Nikko Securities Co. Guaranteed by Fuji Bank Ltd. Each $5,000 bond carries one warrant, exercisable from Oct. 18, 1990, through Sept. 20, 1994, to buy shares at an expected premium of 2 1/2% to closing price when terms are fixed Wednesday. Fees 2 1/4.

Lasmo PLC (U.K.) -- #90 million issue of 7 3/4% convertible bonds due Oct. 4, 2005, at par, via Swiss Bank Corp. Conversion price #5.64, a premium of 15.1% over first day's close. Fees 2 1/2%.

Council of Europe (agency) -- 100 million Canadian dollars of 11 3/4% Eurobonds due Sept. 20, 1992, priced at 101.625 to yield 11.75% less fees via Bankers Trust International Ltd. Fees 1 5/8.

GMAC Australia Finance Ltd. (U.S. parent) -- 75 million Australian dollars of 14 1/4% Eurobonds due Oct. 11, 1995, priced at 101.15 to yield 14.50% less fees via Hambros Bank Ltd. Guaranteed by General Motors Acceptance Corp. Fees 2.

Flora 5 Ltd. (Japanese parent, special-purpose vehicle) -- 10 billion yen of 7 1/4% Eurobonds due Nov. 17, 1992, priced at 100.175 via LTCB International Ltd. Redemption linked to Japanese government bond futures contract. Short first coupon. Fees 1 3/8.

Staatsbank Berlin (East Germany) -- Six billion marks of floating-rate notes at the six-month Frankfurt interbank offered rate plus 0.05 point, priced at par and due Sept. 26, 1994, via Westdeutsche Landesbank Girozentrale. First Fibor fixing Sept. 1. Guaranteed by the West German government. Commissions total 10 basis points.

Province of Newfoundland (Canada) -- 125 million Swiss francs of 7 1/2% public bonds due Oct. 5, 1999, priced at 101 via Union Bank of Switzerland. Call from Oct. 5, 1998, at 101. Fees 2.75.

ABB Asea Brown Boveri Capital B.V. (Swiss parent) -- 75 million Swiss francs of 8% loan participation certificates (Schuldscheindarlehen) due Sept. 25, 1992, via Credit Suisse. Fees undisclosed.
