Japanese Bank Picks Shearson's Herrmann For New Merger Unit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/07/90

Industrial Bank of Japan Ltd. said it is forming a company to provide mergers and acquisitions advice to U.S. and Japanese corporations. The new company, named Bridgeford Group Inc., will be headed by John A. Herrmann Jr., a senior managing director in the mergers and acquisitions department of Shearson Lehman Brothers Inc.

Bridgeford will be capitalized at $50 million. Hideo Ishihara, deputy president of Industrial Bank of Japan, said: "capital isn't important. IBJ itself is squarely behind this."

Over the past 10 years, Mr. Herrmann -- who has spent 25 of the last 29 years at Shearson Lehman or one of its predecessor firms -- was responsible for the management of both Shearson Lehman's international merger and acquisitions business and its corporate sales and divestiture group.

Besides serving Japanese companies seeking to invest in the U.S., Mr. Herrmann said that Bridgeford would also advise American companies seeking to penetrate the Japanese market and would send its expertise to Industrial Bank of Japan's merger efforts in Europe.
