Kawasaki Heavy Industries To Make Part of Airbus Jet

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 09/07/90

TOKYO -- Japan's Kawasaki Heavy Industries Co. will produce part of the A-321 passenger jet being developed by European airplane manufacturing consortium Airbus Industrie, a Kawasaki official said. An official contract will be signed in mid-October between Kawasaki and British Aerospace PLC, one of Airbus Industrie's parent companies which makes bodies for Airbus airplanes.

The A-321 jet, a long-bodied passenger jet still under development, is scheduled for test flight in the spring of 1993 and marketing in the spring of 1994. There are more than 100 orders for the new jet so far, according to the official.

The new plane, with a capacity of 180 passengers, will be made by extending the body of the company's A-320 jet on both ends by a total of 7 meters.

Kawasaki will be the sole producer of skin panels to be attached to the A-320's tail end.

Details of the agreement are under negotiation, the official said.

The latest agreement is part of Kawasaki's business strategy aimed at increasing sales in its private sector division to reduce the company's dependency on the Self-Defense Agency of Japan.

Demand by the agency is expected to decline in coming years due to a world-wide trend toward disarmament. Orders for Kawasaki's P3C anti-submarine patrol planes, for example, are likely to fall more than 30% in the next fiscal year, the official said.
