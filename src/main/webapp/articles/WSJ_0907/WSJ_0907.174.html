International Brief -- Stora Kopparbergs Bergslags AB: Plan to Shed Certain Lines Of Feldmuehle Is Shelved

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/07/90

Stora Kopparbergs Bergslags AB of Sweden, a pulp and paper concern, said it shelved the expected sale of non-forestry operations of Feldmuehle Nobel AG of West Germany until at least next year. Instead, Stora unveiled plans to sell three Swedish units, probably before year end, to help finance its four billion mark ($2.56 billion) takeover of Feldmuehle, initially announced in April.

Stora's share of the purchase price swelled unexpectedly yesterday, when its board agreed to acquire the 24.9% Feldmuehle Nobel stake held by AB Investor and AB Providentia, affiliated holding companies in Sweden's Wallenberg group. The holding companies, Stora's biggest shareholders, had joined forces with the forestry giant in its pursuit of Feldmuehle and had been expected to continue as minority owners and play key roles in disposing of the West German concern's non-forestry operations.

Analysts said the withdrawal probably reflects increasing financial strains within the Wallenberg group, caused by a flurry of big acquisitions in recent months.
