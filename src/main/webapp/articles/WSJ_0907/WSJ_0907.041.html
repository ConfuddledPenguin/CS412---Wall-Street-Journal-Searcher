Letters to the Editor: Monkey Business

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/07/90

If Karen Elliott House is correct in describing Jordan's King Hussein as "a little organ grinder leading around a giant gorilla on a leash" (editorial page, Aug. 14), then how are we to describe the world leaders who played footsie with Saddam Hussein during and after the Iran-Iraq war? I'm amazed that Ms. House failed to mention the $50 billion (and perhaps considerably more) in weapons and electronics equipment shipped to Iraq by the U.S., West Germany, France, the Soviet Union and Japan. If Americans get killed in Saudi Arabia, it won't be with camels made in Jordan, but with sophisticated military hardware. We were absolute fools to believe that we could safely build up Saddam Hussein as a counterweight to Iran without taking major risks ourselves. We've done the same before with the likes of Manuel Noriega, and sooner or later the piper must be paid.

If King Hussein was the organ grinder, about the only role that leaves for the West is that of the little monkeys begging for favors from the monster it created.

Dennis M. Howard

Brookside, N.J.
