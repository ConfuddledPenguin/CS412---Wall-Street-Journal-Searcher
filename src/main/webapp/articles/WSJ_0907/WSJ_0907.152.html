Business Brief -- Prudential Insurance Co.: Officials Complete Buy-Out Of California Realty Unit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/07/90

A group of senior executives have completed a buy-out of Prudential California Realty from its parent, Prudential Insurance Co. of America. Terms of the sale weren't disclosed. Prudential California Realty was originally part of Merrill Lynch's real estate business, which Prudential Insurance acquired a year ago. Since the acquisition, the Newark, N.J.-based insurance company has been selling blocks of the residential realty offices to local management because it believes that the residential real estate business is best managed at the local level.

Prudential California will be the largest independently owned real estate firm in the state, with 78 offices extending from the San Francisco Bay area to San Diego and a sales force of more than 4,200.

George Rathman, who was chairman of the Prudential Insurance unit, will serve as chairman and chief executive officer of the newly independent company. Rick Merrill, formerly a regional president, was named president and chief operating officer, and Robert LeFever, another regional president, will serve as executive vice president. Four regional directors will serve as limited partners: Joseph Balla, Rolinda Smith, Roger Ewing and Robert Foster. Steve Games will continue as president of the company's San Diego office.
