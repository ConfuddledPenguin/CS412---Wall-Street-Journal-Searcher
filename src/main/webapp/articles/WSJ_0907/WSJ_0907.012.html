Thomson-CSF S.A. Gets 3 Airport Orders

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 09/07/90

PARIS -- Thomson-CSF S.A. said it received three civil aviation contracts, all for undisclosed amounts. France's state-controlled defense and electronics group said it received an order for a voice communications control system for Britain's Civil Aviation Authority. The new equipment will be used to handle communications at Manchester Airport.

Thomson-CSF also announced a contract for a terminal control radar system for Denmark's Aarhus-Tirstrup airport, to be operational by mid-1991.

Also, the company said it won an order from Belgium's national air control system for an autonomous monopulse secondary radar to be used at Brussels National Airport. The order also includes an upgrading of a station in Saint Hubert. The new equipment is scheduled to come into service at the end of 1991.
