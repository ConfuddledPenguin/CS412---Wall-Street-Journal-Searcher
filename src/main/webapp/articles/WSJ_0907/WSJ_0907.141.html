Business Brief -- Tucson Electric Power Co.: Utility to Seek Surcharge In Addition to Rate Boost

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/07/90

Financially-troubled Tucson Electric Power Co. said it plans to ask the Arizona Corporation Commission later this month for an 18-month surcharge of 2% in addition to a 10% to 12% increase in base electricity rates. The Tucson-based utility said it also will ask the regulatory commission for permission to phase into its rate base recovery of certain costs related to Unit 2 of the Springerville Generating Station, which began operations in June.

The commission previously has indicated that the plant shouldn't be included in the utility's rate base because the plant's power capacity isn't needed, a Tucson Electric spokesman said, adding: "We think it is needed and we are using some power from it."

Tucson Electric said its most recent rate increase, 11.85%, went into effect last October. The utility had requested about a 20% increase. The previous increase was in October 1983. Tucson Electric serves about 750,000 people in the Tucson area.
