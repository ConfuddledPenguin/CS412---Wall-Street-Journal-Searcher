Business Brief -- Standard Brands Paint Co.: Fiscal 2nd-Period Net Loss Posted on 8.7% Sales Drop

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/07/90

Standard Brands Paint Co. reported a net loss for the fiscal second quarter ended July 29, and announced the closing of 19 Texas and California stores in order to improve its financial position. The paint and home-supplies retailer posted a quarterly net loss of $3.7 million, or 75 cents a share, versus net income of $1.9 million, or 27 cents a share, a year earlier. Sales fell 8.7% to $79.9 million from $87.5 million in the prior-year quarter.

The results include a one-time restructuring charge of $4.7 million, in addition to a $1 million allowance for losses from discontinued operations. The company, based in Torrance, Calif., closed 16 stores in Dallas, Houston, and San Antonio, Texas, and one distribution facility, as well as three California stores in Merced, Chico and Redding.

For the six months, Standard Brands reported a net loss of $4.9 million, or $1 a share, versus net income of $3.5 million or 47 cents a share, a year earlier. Sales fell 6.4% to $152.6 million from $163 million a year ago.

Stuart D. Buchalter, chief executive officer, said the closed Texas properties have an estimated value of $20 million, and proceeds from their sale will be used to pare down Standard Brand's long term debt of $150 million. The company still plans to open eight stores in California, Nevada and Arizona over the next year. It also plans to open three branches of its Art Stores in California.
