Technology: IBM and Sears's Prodigy Improves Videotext Service

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/07/90

NEW YORK -- Prodigy announced several improvements to its videotext service, including the availability of an electronic encyclopedia and very fast updating of sports scores. The joint venture between International Business Machines Corp. and Sears, Roebuck & Co. also has speeded up the service, which people use to shop, gather information and communicate through personal computers connected to the Prodigy service over phone lines. One of the main criticisms of Prodigy over the past two years has been that it took too long for the system to respond.

Prodigy, which now has 460,000 users in 270,000 households, said it expects that number to continue to grow rapidly, to more than one million users by the end of next year. Among other reasons, Prodigy said it has completed its rollout of the service, so that it's now available nationwide.

Still, market researchers have said Prodigy needs millions of users before it can start to turn a profit. Estimates of IBM's and Sears's spending on Prodigy thus far range into many hundreds of millions of dollars.

Prodigy executives, who acknowledged that users have bought fewer items through the service than had been expected, remain optimistic that consumers will eventually get used to shopping electronically.
