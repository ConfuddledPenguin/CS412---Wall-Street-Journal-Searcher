Technology: Mosquitoes Carrying Virus Prompt Warning by CDC

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/07/90

ATLANTA -- The Centers for Disease Control said several state health agencies have detected "unusually early and high levels" of mosquitoes carrying viruses that cause encephalitis, indicating "a potential risk for epidemic transmission" to people. The CDC said surveillance conducted in Texas, Florida, Massachusetts, New Jersey and New York of birds and mosquitoes that are involved in the cyclical transmission of the viruses signals a potential for a higher incidence of encephalitis in humans.

"We don't want to alarm people unnecessarily," said Dr. Ted Tsai, assistant director for science at the CDC division of vector-borne infectious diseases in Atlanta. "To be conservative, what we'd like to say is there is more of the virus being transmitted in nature than is usual, so there's a potential, or possibility, that it could lead to a higher occurrence in people than usual."

The CDC said St. Louis Encephalitis, the more common type transmitted by the mosquito vectors, is the leading cause of epidemic encephalitis in the U.S. The last major outbreak, in 1975, resulted in nearly 3,000 cases of the disease, which has a 7% fatality rate.

The CDC said various states, including Massachusetts, New York and Florida, have already stepped up mosquito-control efforts to battle the spread of the viruses.
