United Gaming Posts Sharp Declines in Net For 4th Quarter, Year

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/07/90

LAS VEGAS, Nev. -- United Gaming Inc. reported substantial profit declines for the fiscal fourth quarter and year, and said it named two new senior executives as part of the expansion of its casino and gaming-machine route businesses. For the fiscal fourth quarter ended June 30, the company reported net income of $72,000, or one cent a share, compared with $1,449,000, or 17 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue was $25,894,000, vs. $18,010,000 a year ago.

The quarter included $1,665,000 in charges that stemmed from several unexpected events, including a customer's involuntary bankruptcy filing that resulted in a charge of $667,000 and the withdrawal from another casino project that resulted in a charge of $630,000. The company also scrapped a planned public financing because of poor market conditions.

For fiscal 1990, the company posted net of $3,895,000, or 45 cents a share, vs. $5,779,000 or 70 cents a share for the prior fiscal year. Revenues was $90,205,000 compared with $65,389,000 a year earlier. The company said it expects 1991 fiscal firstquarter results to be affected by lower revenue at one casino that is being remodeled, employee severance costs, and the elimination of some stops on its gamingmachine route.

Also, Carole A. Carter, vice president, was named president and chief operating officer. She succeeds David A. Scheinman as president, who remains a director and consultant. John W. Alderfer, former chief financial officer of the Bicycle Club in Los Angeles, was named vice president of finance and chief financial officer. He succeeds Robert E. Huggins, who continues to head operations at the Plantation and Mizpah sites.
