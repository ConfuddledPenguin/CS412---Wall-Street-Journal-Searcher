Czech Imports May Receive `Most Favored' Treatment

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/07/90

WASHINGTON -- President Bush cleared the way for the approval of "most favored nation" treatment for imports from Czechoslovakia, and asked Congress to approve a bilateral trade agreement with the country. The White House announced that the president has decided to approve a waiver of the so-called Jackson-Vanik restrictions in the 1974 trade act to permit extension of U.S. tariff concessions to Czechoslovakian imports.

Deputy U.S. Trade Representative Julius Katz said the Bush administration hopes Congress will approve the waiver and the trade accord before adjourning later this year.

Both the trade agreement and waiver must be approved by Congress before the U.S. can reduce its import duties on products from Czechoslovakia. The approval would make that country eligible for the same type of most favored nation treatment given to imports from many other nations.
