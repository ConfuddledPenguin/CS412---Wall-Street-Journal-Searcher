Business Brief -- Imperial Oil Ltd.: Affiliate to Build Pipeline For Natural Gas in Canada

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/07/90

An affiliate of Imperial Oil Ltd., Toronto, said it plans to build a 22 million Canadian dollar (US$19.1 million) pipeline to transport natural gas liquids from the Caroline field, 62%-owned by Shell Canada Ltd., a Calgary, Alberta-based unit of the Anglo-Dutch Royal Dutch/Shell Group. The Federated Pipe Lines Ltd. affiliate is jointly owned by units of Imperial, which is 70%-owned by New York-based Exxon Corp., and Calgary-based Interhome Energy Inc., which is controlled by Toronto's Reichmann family. Federated said it received approval from Alberta energy regulators to build the 32,000-barrel-a-day pipeline. Construction is to begin in late 1991.

The 95-mile pipeline will transport gas liquids from the Caroline field, about 75 miles northwest of Calgary, to Federated's pipeline system, and on to storage and processing facilities near Edmonton, or enhanced oil recovery projects northwest of the city. Shell Canada last week received regulatory approval to begin developing its Caroline field at a cost of about C$825 million. Output is expected by early 1993.
