Ramsay Health Care To Report Decline In 4th-Quarter Profit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/07/90

NEW ORLEANS -- Ramsay Health Care Inc. expects to report net income of two cents or three cents a share for its fiscal fourth quarter after a 12-cent-a-share refinancing charge. The company, an operator of psychiatric hospitals, is already seeing some "real savings in interest" because of the debt restructuring completed during its fourth quarter ended June 30, Bradley J. Velie, Ramsay's chief financial officer, said in an interview.

In addition to the refinancing charge, the fourth-quarter earnings of $300,000 to $400,000 also includes an operating loss carry-forward roughly equal to the net income. Revenue for the quarter rose to about $40 million. In the year-earlier quarter, Ramsay earned $1.6 million, or 11 cents a share, on revenue of $38.8 million.

For the year, Ramsay expects to report net income of between $4.9 million and $5.1 million, or about 33 cents to 34 cents a share, on revenue of about $153 million. In fiscal 1989, the company earned $3.7 million, or 26 cents a share, on revenue of $147.7 million.

Under the refinancing, Ramsay converted most of its floating debt to fixed and capped the rest. Mr. Velie said the new debt package also allows the company to defer certain principal payments for up to three years and carries much less restrictive covenants.

Mr. Velie said Ramsay's pretax profit is a better indication of the company's financial recovery. For fiscal 1990, pretax profit nearly doubled to $10 million from $5.1 million for the year before, he said. "Cost control measures have put us in a very good strategic place for future years," he said, adding that he expects pretax income to increase by 15% to 20% for the current year.

Ralph Watts, Ramsay's president and chief executive officer, said in the interview that the industry is going through a period of consolidation as large chains cut back and some not-for-profit hospitals fail.

"There will be a lot of things for sale in the next couple of years," he said. "We believe there will be some bargains out there." Continuing its debt reduction efforts is a top priority for Ramsay, Mr. Watts said, but the right acquisition opportunity wouldn't be ignored.

The company currently has 16 facilities and a total of 1,433 beds. The occupancy rate for its hospitals has decreased in the past year to about 54.6% from 57.3%, Mr. Watts said, primarily because of shorter stays by patients and increased competition in some markets.

The company is also pursuing opportunities in Canada and in Europe and expects soon to announce its first project outside the U.S., Mr. Watts said.
