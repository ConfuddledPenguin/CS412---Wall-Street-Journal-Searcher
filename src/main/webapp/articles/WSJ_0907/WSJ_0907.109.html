Greenspan Says Credit Has Tightened Further

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/07/90

WASHINGTON -- Federal Reserve Board Chairman Alan Greenspan said his reading of the Fed's most recent survey of bank officers is that "a substantial minority" further tightened lending standards this summer. "The tightening was noticeable for medium-sized businesses but extended as well to large and smaller firms," Mr. Greenspan said in written replies to questions from the Senate Banking Committee.

The Fed released the results of its regular survey of senior loan officers at the end of August, but Mr. Greenspan hadn't commented on them publicly before. The Fed chairman described the survey as "a reliable indicator of current thinking at large commercial banks."

When the Fed nudged down short-term interest rates in July, Mr. Greenspan told the banking committee that the Fed was trying to offset the effects of the credit crunch. He said the Fed might ease them again if it saw evidence that the credit crunch was worsening.

Fed officials said late last month that they lean toward further easing, but are reluctant to move until financial markets, roiled by the Persian Gulf crisis, calm down.

As previously reported, 43% of the domestic banks surveyed in August said they had raised the hurdles for lending to medium-size companies since May. More than a third reported more stringent standards for large and small borrowers.
