L.A. Gear Says Net Fell in Quarter Despite Sales Rise

  • Author: Rhonda L. Rundle
  • Date: 09/07/90

LOS ANGELES -- Athletic-shoe maker L.A. Gear Inc., breaking its recent silence on financial matters, said that net income in its fiscal third quarter ended Aug. 31 was lower than a year ago, despite a 40% sales rise to about $300 million. In the year-earlier quarter, L.A. Gear posted record profit of $22 million, or $1.21 a share, on sales of $215 million.

While the announcement apparently didn't contain any surprises, analysts said they were relieved to get some hard information. "There hasn't been much guidance on sales trends during the quarter and estimates have varied dramatically," said Alice M. Ruth at Montgomery Securities Inc. in San Francisco.

Analysts have slashed their profit and sales forecasts since L.A. Gear surprised Wall Street with a 36% drop in second-quarter profit. On June 1, the highflying shares plunged $8.50, or 22%, on volume of 6.6 million shares after L.A. Gear indicated that it would have its first quarterly drop in net income in three years.

Kevin J. Ventrudo, chief financial officer, said the company wanted to put out its third-quarter sales figures as soon as they were available so that "everybody would be working off the same page of the playbook." L.A. Gear has been inundated recently with inquiries about sales, he said.

Gross incoming orders in the quarter reached $240 million, a 15% increase over the year-ago period, L.A. Gear said. The gross profit margin is "anticipated to be higher than in the second quarter" when it was 34.8% "It's too early to tell" by how much, Mr. Ventrudo said. There won't be any extraordinary items in the quarter, he added.

Mr. Ventrudo said he doubted that the announcement would significantly affect Wall Street forecasts. He said that analysts' third-quarter sales estimates range from about $290 million to $330 million. For the full fiscal year, per-share estimates range from about $2.30 to $3.50.

Gary M. Jacobson, an analyst at Kidder, Peabody & Co., predicted heavy volume in L.A. Gear's shares today but little price movement. "Expectations are quite low right now and this doesn't disappoint those low expectations," he said.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, L.A. Gear shares closed down 37.5 cents at $16.50. The sales figures were released after the close of trading.

Mr. Jacobson said that earlier this year L.A. Gear "got caught with too much product" as it began to lose its appeal with customers. L.A. Gear has had to cut prices on these older footwear styles, thereby eroding its margins.

L.A. Gear's newer basketball sneakers and other "performance" shoes that compete directly with offerings from Nike Inc. and Reebok International Ltd. haven't caught on quickly, some analysts said. A new line advertised by singer Michael Jackson "has been receiving mixed reviews," Mr. Jacobson said. "The jury is still out," countered Mr. Ventrudo.
