What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/07/90

WORLD OIL PRICES SURGED on pessimism over the Mideast crisis and the outlook for winter supplies. Speculative buying helped push some crude prices beyond $31 a barrel. The jump came even as some small producers planned to follow OPEC in raising output. Stocks fell, with the Dow Jones industrials drop ping 31.93 points to 2596.29. Tokyo and London shares declined as well. ---

Equimark's Alan Fellheimer resigned as chairman of the troubled bank holding concern. The move follows the Tisch family's disclosure that it holds a 6.8% stake in the firm.

---

Franklin Savings' return to its previous management from control of federal regulators was blocked by a U.S. court's temporary stay.

---

The major U.S. retailers posted modest sales gains. But analysts say weakness in home furnishings and early fall merchandise indicate diffi culties through the winter season.

---

U.S. businesses are paring capital-spending plans because of growing concern about the economy, the latest Commerce Department survey in dicates. Outlays for 1990 are now ex pected to increase 5.4%, down from the previous estimate of 6.8%.

---

G-7 officials will meet in Paris next week to discuss the dollar's recent decline and consider if actions are needed to bolster the currency.

---

U.S. bank earnings fell 24% in the second quarter to $5.3 billion, largely on a $1.9 billion jump in Northeastern institutions' loan-loss reserves. Bad real estate loans remain the major problem for the industry.

---

Both parties' leaders vowed to present separate $50 billion deficit-reduction packages as their opening bids in renewed budget talks today.

---

Motorola plans to start shipping its much-delayed 68040 microproces sor in October. The delays have in turn set back production at computer makers that rely on the chip.

---

Lotus Development is issuing the latest upgrade to its 1-2-3 spread sheet, a move that could help reverse the company's earnings difficulties.

IBM announced a joint venture with Metaphor Computer to produce an underlying layer of software aimed at speeding development of personal-computer software uses.

---

L.A. Gear expects to post fiscal third-quarter profit below that of a year ago, when the sneaker maker reported a record net of $22 million.

---

Goodyear expects to post a third-quarter loss of about $65 million mostly related to 1,180 planned layoffs in its European operations. The tire maker also expressed concern over adverse effects on raw material costs due to the Mideast crisis.
