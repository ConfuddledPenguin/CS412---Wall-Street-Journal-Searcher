GM Plans to Close Buick Reatta Plant For Two More Weeks

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/07/90

DETROIT -- General Motors Corp. said it will shut the assembly plant that builds the Buick Reatta for an additional two weeks because of slow sales. The plant was to have reopened this past Tuesday after a seven week shutdown. About 600 workers will be laid off temporarily. As of July 31, GM had a 97-day supply of Reatta cars, according to Ward's Automotive Reports, a trade journal. This level is considerably higher than the 65-to-70 day range regarded as optimal.

Overall, the 10 major domestic auto makers planned to build 111,612 cars this week, up 2.8% from 108,575 made a year ago.

Ford Motor Co. said it scheduled Saturday overtime at its truck plants in Kansas City, Mo., St. Louis, Mo., and Louisville, Ky.

Chrysler Corp. said its assembly plant in Newark, Del., is scheduled to work Saturday overtime.
