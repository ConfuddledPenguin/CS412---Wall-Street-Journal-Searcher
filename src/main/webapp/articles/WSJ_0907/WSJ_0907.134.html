Business Brief -- Reckitt & Colman PLC: Company Says Pretax Profit Rose 15% for the 1st Half

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/07/90

Reckitt & Colman PLC said pretax profit rose 15% for the first half of the year, with strong Continental European and Latin American earnings offsetting a decline in the U.K. The British food and household goods maker said pretax profit rose to #113 million ($215.3 million) from #98.6 million in the year-earlier period, comfortably within market expectations which ranged between #110 million to #115 million.

Profit after tax and minority interests increased by 15% to #71.9 million pounds from #62.4 million. Per-share earnings rose to 47.8 pence from 42.0 pence.

Net profit attributable to shareholders rose 7% to #65.8 million from #61.5 million. Reckitt & Colman reported an extraordinary loss of #5.33 million in the latest figure; in last year's period, the company had an extraordinary loss of #780,000.

The company said it would raise its half-year dividend by 15% to 12.25 pence from 10.65 pence.

Reckitt & Colman said sales rose 14% to #843 million from #737 million.
