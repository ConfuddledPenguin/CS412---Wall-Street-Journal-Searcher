Market Pulp Output By Canadian Mills Declined 10% in July

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/07/90

MONTREAL -- Canadian production of market pulp in July dropped 10% from a year earlier, as the industry operated at 87% of capacity. The Canadian Pulp and Paper Association, an industry group, said Canadian mills produced 566,000 metric tons of market pulp in July, compared with 629,000 metric tons a year earlier. A metric ton equals about 2,204.62 pounds.

The July operating rate was up from 80% in June but down from 99% a year earlier.

For this year through July, Canadian mills produced 3,818,000 metric tons of market pulp, down 9.2% from 4,207,000 metric tons a year earlier. For the first seven months, the industry operated at 86% of capacity, down from 97% a year earlier.

Market pulp is wood pulp sold on the open market to producers of paper, sanitary and other products. The statistics exclude pulp consumed at the producing mill or shipped to another mill that is affiliated with the producing mill. Canada is the world's largest producer of market pulp.

Total market shipments in July dropped 14% to 441,000 metric tons from 513,000 tons a year earlier. Shipments to all markets were down.

Market shipments for the first seven months also fell 14% to 3,479,000 metric tons from 4,062,000 tons a year earlier.

Canadian producer inventories at the end of July totaled 698,000 metric tons, a 44-day supply, which was up from 573,000 metric tons, a 35-day supply, at the end of June, and from 384,000 tons, or a 20-day supply, at the end of July 1989.
