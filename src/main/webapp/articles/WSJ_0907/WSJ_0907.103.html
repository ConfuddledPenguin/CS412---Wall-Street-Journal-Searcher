Belzberg Family Sells 5.16% Stake in Asda, Taking a Paper Loss

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/07/90

VANCOUVER, British Columbia -- Canada's Belzberg family sold at a loss its 5.16% stake in Britain's Asda Group PLC on the London Stock Exchange. Terms of the sale weren't disclosed, and officials with Belzberg flagship First City Financial Corp., the Vancouver-based holding and investment concern through which the family acquired the Asda stake, weren't available for comment.

But the paper loss -- excluding carrying and related charges, and fluctuations in currency exchange rates -- is believed to be around US$45 million, based on an analyst's estimate previously confirmed by First City.

The loss marks the latest in a string of investments that turned sour for First City, including its 11.7% stake in Armstrong World Industries Inc., which the company sold May 31 at a net estimated loss of $13.6 million.

Last week, First City reported a first-half loss of 27.7 million Canadian dollars (US$24 million), or 53 Canadian cents a share, on revenue of C$436 million, compared with prior-year net before extraordinary items of C$58.9 million, or C$1.07 a share, on revenue of C$456 million. A special gain made net last year C$182.6 million, or C$3.32 a share.

First City said that the stake in Asda, a big retailer, wasn't an "appropriate long-term investment" and it would like to emphasize its financial services, manufacturing and real estate operating companies.

First City sold the bulk of its holdings to a group of institutions, according to brokers in London. At last report, First City held about 65 million Asda shares.

In London Stock Exchange trading yesterday, Asda's shares closed at 109 pence (US$2.07) each, down 3.5 pence. First City acquired its Asda shares at an average price of about #1.50, according to a report by Toronto's First Marathon Securities Ltd. that was later confirmed by a First City executive.

Tony McNeary, an analyst with London's County NatWest Securities, predicted that with the Belzbergs gone, Asda's share price "will remain there (at 109 pence) for a while and probably won't motor back up."
