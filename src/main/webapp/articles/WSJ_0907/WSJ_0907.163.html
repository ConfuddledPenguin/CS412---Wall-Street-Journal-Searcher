Judge Extends Freeze On Payment of Claims By the Manville Trust

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/07/90

NEW YORK -- A federal judge extended a freeze on the payment of claims by the Manville asbestos personal-injury trust while it continues to negotiate its refinancing. Judge Jack B. Weinstein of federal court in Brooklyn had ordered the trust to come up with new financing by yesterday. But in his latest order, the judge said that negotiators had requested additional time to work out details of the proposal. The judge extended the freeze until Oct. 6, but the new financing is expected to be announced before then.

The trust was set up as part of Manville Corp.'s bankruptcy court reorganization plan in 1988 to pay the thousands of asbestos personal-injury claims against the company. Asbestos, once widely used in construction and shipyards, causes cancer and respiratory ailments years after exposure.

Under the reorganization plan, Manville can't be sued by asbestos victims; instead, all asbestos personal-injury claims must be filed against the trust, which is funded by the company.

Although the trust has assets of $2.6 billion, it has run out of cash to pay claims and has said it won't begin paying claims filed after 1989 for at least 25 years. Judge Weinstein has ordered the trust to restructure its financing and payment plans to overcome that delay.

The company and the trust declined to comment on their refinancing negotiations. Currently, new funding from the company isn't scheduled to begin until next fall. The freeze on claim payments allows exceptions for hardship cases.
