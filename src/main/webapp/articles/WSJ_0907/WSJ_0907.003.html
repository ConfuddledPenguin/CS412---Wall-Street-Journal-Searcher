Politics & Policy: Group of Seven Meets Next Week On Dollar's Drop

  • Author: Peter Truell
  • Date: 09/07/90

SEOUL, South Korea -- Senior officials from the Group of Seven industrialized countries will meet in Paris Monday and Tuesday to discuss the dollar's recent decline and to review developments in the world economy, a U.S. Treasury official said. The meeting comes at a time when the economic slowdown in the U.S. has pushed the value of the dollar down sharply against foreign currencies. The dollar-yen exchange rate, for instance, is at its lowest level this year. The officials will have to decide whether any action is needed to stem the dollar's decline.

Such a gathering of the G-7 deputies is usually held prior to the annual meetings of the International Monetary Fund and World Bank, which this year are scheduled to take place in Washington in late September.

The G-7 officials also will attend so-called "working party three" meetings of the Paris-based Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development, a group of 24 rich nations, the Treasury official said. The OECD working group meets regularly to discuss the international economy.

During the two sets of Paris meetings, officials are expected to review the impact of Iraq's Aug. 2 invasion of Kuwait on oil prices and other recent economic developments.

Treasury Secretary Nicholas Brady is here leading a U.S. delegation on a whistlestop to raise contributions in support of the American-led military effort in the Persian Gulf and for Mideast countries -- particularly Egypt, Jordan and Turkey -- whose economies have been badly harmed by the Persian Gulf crisis. Western officials say the United Nations trade embargo against Iraq will be more effective if those three countries get $3.5 billion of economic assistance.

The G-7 countries are the U.S., Japan, West Germany, Britain, France, Italy and Canada.
