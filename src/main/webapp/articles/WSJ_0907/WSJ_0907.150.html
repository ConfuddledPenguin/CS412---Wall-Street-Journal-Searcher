Marketing & Media: Norman Lear's Series For Late-Night Slot On CBS Is Scrapped

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/07/90

NEW YORK -- A planned late-night series created by producer Norman Lear for CBS Inc.'s television network has been scrapped one month before it was scheduled to premiere. The half-hour program, "Jody Gordon and the News," was to have been two 15-minute shows woven together and was to have marked the return to television of Mr. Lear, who created such CBS hits as "All in the Family," "One Day At a Time" and "Maude."

A CBS spokeswoman said Mr. Lear asked to be let out of his contract so he could concentrate on "Sunday Dinner," a prime-time series he is developing. The late-night show was to air at 12:40 a.m., following reruns of "Wiseguy" and "21 Jump Street."

CBS hasn't succeeded in gaining a foothold in the late-night time period, most recently failing with the "Pat Sajak Show." As previously reported, CBS is considering a new half-hour news show for the 11:30 p.m. slot, to be anchored by CBS News correspondents Charles Kuralt and Lesley Stahl.
