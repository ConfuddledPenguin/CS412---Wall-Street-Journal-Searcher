Industrial Nations' Indexes Still Signal Slow Growth

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/07/90

NEW YORK -- The world's industrial economies continue to show signs of slow growth. The Conference Board said its International Economic Scoreboard shows the composite indexes of leading indicators for 11 of the world's major industrial economies growing at an annual rate of 2%, the same figure reported three months ago. Since this figure predates the crisis in the Middle East, the report suggests even slower growth ahead.

"The recent curtailment of oil supplies and rise in the cost of oil will likely add a depressing influence to the slow growth prospects already shown by the leading indexes for most countries," the report said.

The report looks at leading indicators because these measures are generally thought to signal future economic trends.

Europe is showing the greatest signs of strength, especially West Germany, where the leading index is growing at an annual rate of 4%. France is not far behind, with the leading index rising at 3%. Italy's rate is 1%. The U.K. index has fallen 1%.

Japan continues to lead the Pacific region, with its index growing at 3%, followed by New Zealand at 1%. Indexes in Korea and Taiwan both dropped by 1%, while Australia's fell 3%.

In North America, Canada's leading index is now falling at a rate of 3%, while in the U.S. the leading index is growing at 1%.

The indexes are compiled monthly by the Center for International Business Cycle Research at the Columbia University Business School. The Conference Board is an independent business research group.
