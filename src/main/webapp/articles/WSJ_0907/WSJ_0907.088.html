International: Australia May Sell Part Of Qantas, Woo Airlines

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 09/07/90

SYDNEY, Australia -- Qantas Airways might court major foreign carriers as shareholders, Chairman Bill Dix said. The Australian government, which owns Qantas, is considering the sale of at least part of the international airline. "In the context of consolidation in world aviation, I wouldn't be at all surprised to see Qantas taking additional equity stakes in smaller carriers, like those we already hold in Air New Zealand and Air Pacific," Mr. Dix said at a tourism seminar. "Furthermore, I wouldn't rule out the acquisition of a stake in Qantas by some of the major carriers with which we do and will be doing business. Japan Air Lines, {AMR Corp.'s} American, British Airways and Lufthansa {German Airlines} come to mind as carriers we'll be working with closely."

The Australian government's cabinet yesterday decided to sell stakes in Qantas and Australian Airlines, one of two major domestic airlines. The cabinet also decided in principle to expose the government-owned monopoly telephone company to competition.

Prime Minister Bob Hawke didn't say how much of the airlines' share capital the government might sell. Analysts speculated that stakes of at least 49% might be put on the market. The Labor Party still must approve the sales.

Mr. Dix said that "without access to the necessary capital funds, both debt and equity, Qantas will be unable to maintain the growth needed to service Australia's expanding tourism industry."
