Arco Unveils Gasoline For California Market That Burns Cleaner

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/07/90

LOS ANGELES -- Atlantic Richfield Co. introduced a cleaner-burning high-octane gasoline that will replace its current premium unleaded gasoline in Southern California. On a hot, smoggy day at a local gasoline station, Arco Chairman Lodwrick M. Cook ceremoniously unveiled what the company calls "EC-Premium," which the station was selling for $1.359 a gallon.

EC-Premium follows the introduction a year ago of EC-1, a form of emission-control regular gasoline intended for pre-1975 cars and pre-1980 trucks. As for unleaded regular, which represents 60% of Arco's gasoline sales, Mr. Cook said that it would take four to five years to bring out a reformulated version, would cost the company about $2 billion and "depends what the government mandates." Tough new California regulations and federal legislation are pending.

Mr. Cook said the new gasoline was costing the company four cents a gallon more to produce, but he said wholesale prices would not be increased "in the immediate future" except to reflect increases in crude prices. He said Arco hoped to make up the difference by increasing market share. He said the cost of developing EC-Premium and bringing it to market was "a few million" because it didn't require major changes in the refining process.

EC-Premium reduces benzene emissions by about 55%, compared with its old unleaded premium, the company said, and is also lower in aromatics, olefins and evaporative pressure. Like EC-1, the new gasoline will be sold at stations from Santa Barbara to the Mexican border, and east to the Arizona state line. Sales of leaded regular gasoline have increased 3% since EC-1 was introduced, even though the customer base of cars and trucks without catalytic converters has been shrinking through attrition.
