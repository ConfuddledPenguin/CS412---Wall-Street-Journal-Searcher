French Firms' Unit Sells Truffaut Chain To Cora-Revillon S.A.

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 09/07/90

PARIS -- Rhone-Poulenc S.A. and Lafarge Coppee S.A. said their joint subsidiary, Clause S.A., sold its wholly owned Truffaut S.A. unit to the French trading and distribution concern Cora-Revillon S.A. Terms weren't disclosed, but people close to the two companies said the purchase price was roughly equivalent to one year's revenue for Truffaut, or about 700 million francs ($133.9 million).

Truffaut has a chain of garden centers throughout France and is the leader in its sector.

A spokesman for Lafarge, Europe's biggest cement producer, said the sale was in line with the stated intention of Rhone-Poulenc and Lafarge to spin off Clause's assets that weren't directly related to its core activity of seed production.

Separately, state-owned Rhone-Poulenc said it intends to sell its polyvinyl chloride operations in Spain to Atochem S.A., a unit of the French state-controlled oil company Societe Nationale Elf Aquitaine.

On Wednesday, Rhone-Poulenc said its first-half group profit fell 14% because of the strength of the French franc and economic austerity in Brazil.
