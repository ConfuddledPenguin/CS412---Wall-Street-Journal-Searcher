Business Brief -- Tektronix Inc.: Firm Posts Quarterly Profit After a Series of Setbacks

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/07/90

Struggling Tektronix Inc. showed an earnings turnaround during its fiscal first quarter, after reporting losses or declining earnings since 1985. The Beaverton, Ore., electronics testing and measurement concern said it earned $9.4 million, or 32 cents a share, for the quarter ended Aug. 18, compared with a loss of $1.9 million, or six cents a share, in the year-earlier period.

The upswing comes after the concern took several cost-cutting steps this year, including closing its facilities in Clark County, Wash.; shutting down a plant in Guernsey, one of the U.K.'s Channel islands, and laying off 10% of its 13,000-member work force.

Sales climbed 3% to $302.5 million from $294.1 million.

The company's shares closed yesterday at $15, up 62.5 cents, in New York Stock Exchange composite trading.
