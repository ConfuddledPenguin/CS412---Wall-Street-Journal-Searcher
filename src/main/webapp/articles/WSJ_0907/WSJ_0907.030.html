Letters to the Editor: Too Many Banks

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/07/90

"Financial Loss: Big Banks Are Plagued by a Gradual Erosion of Key Profit Centers" (page one, Aug. 1) considers a sad story about the dismal profit prospects in the banking industry. It omits one critical fact that we all take for granted, and one issue that can serve to alter future performance. The fact is that we simply have too many banks with enormous redundancies. With more than 13,000 banks performing functions that could be accomplished by fewer than 100 across the country, expense rationalization is a necessity for having this industry earn reasonable rates of return. The regulatory process simply must permit combinations that serve both the customer and the bank shareholder.

Joel M. Stern

Managing Partner

Stern Stewart & Co.

New York
