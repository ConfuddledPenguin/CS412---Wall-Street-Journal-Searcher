Pinnacle West Holder Sues, Calling PacifiCorp Agreement `Greenmail'

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/07/90

PHOENIX, Ariz. -- A Pinnacle West Capital Corp. shareholder charged the utility holding company and its directors with entering into a "greenmail" pact with PacifiCorp "to neutralize PacifiCorp's takeover threat." PacifiCorp, a utility company based in Portland, Ore., agreed Friday to drop its 10-month pursuit of Pinnacle West in exchange for seasonal power swaps with Pinnacle West's Arizona utility unit in an agreement valued at $260 million.

In a suit filed Wednesday in state court here, the shareholder alleges that the transaction is a breach of the defendants' fiduciary duty and was reached by the directors primarily to entrench themselves and company management.

A Pinnacle West spokesman said he couldn't comment on the suit because the company hadn't seen it. PacifiCorp, which wasn't named as a defendant, said it doesn't comment on litigation.

The suit seeks to block consummation of the power-swap agreement and unspecified damages if it is completed. The suit was brought by Moise Katz of New York, who owns 100 Pinnacle West shares, "for the benefit" of the company.

Greenmail is a term usually applied to the practice in which outsiders buy a company's stock and then entice the company to buy the shares back at a premium to avoid a takeover. In the suit, greenmail refers to the power-sharing agreement being used to avoid a takeover.

Since that agreement was reached, the price of Pinnacle West shares, which are traded on the New York Stock Exchange, has dropped $3.125 to $13.
