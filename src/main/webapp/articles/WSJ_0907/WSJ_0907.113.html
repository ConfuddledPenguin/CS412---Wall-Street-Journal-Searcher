Business Brief -- Prince Holdings Inc.: Sports Equipment Concern To Be Acquired by Edizione

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/07/90

Prince Holdings Inc., the maker of tennis rackets and other sports-related products, said it agreed to be acquired by Edizione Holding S.p.A. of Italy, which controls apparel maker Benetton and ski-equipment maker Nordica. Terms weren't disclosed. Prince said its senior management would be retained by Edizione, and that Prince managers would maintain a small stake in the company.

The Princeton, N.J., company said it expected to report sales of about $185 million for fiscal 1990, ending Sept. 30. In 1989, the company reported operating profit of $18 million on revenue of $158 million.

Prince said the transaction will provide much needed capital for the company's plans to enter into other sporting goods businesses such as golfing equipment and sports apparel. In addition, the company said it decided to sell so it could expand its world-wide sales efforts. "This sale will enable Prince to continue its growth and development," said John M. Sullivan, chairman of Prince Holdings.

Prince was acquired in 1987 in a leveraged buy-out led by senior management and Brentwood Associates, a Los Angeles-based investment firm, from ChesebroughPonds Inc., a unit of Unilever Group. In October 1988, it acquired Ektelon, maker of racquetball equipment, from Browning, a unit of Fabrique Nationale Herstal S.A. of Belgium.
