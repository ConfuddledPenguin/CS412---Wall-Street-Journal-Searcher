International Brief -- Thames Television PLC: Pretax Profit for Half Fell 34%; Ad Slowdown Cited

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/07/90

Thames Television PLC of Britain, a broadcasting concern, reported that pretax profit for the six months ended June 30 fell 34% to #10.3 million ($19.6 million) from #15.7 million a year earlier, damped largely by sluggish advertising revenue. The year-earlier half ended Sept. 30, 1989; the company has changed to a calendar-year accounting period. Net profit attributable to shareholders, or profit after taxes and extraordinary items, totaled #6.59 million, a decline of #71.1 million. Per-share earnings fell 34% to 14.5 pence from 22.1 pence.

Sales rose 10% to #183 million from #166 million.

The dividend is unchanged at 5.15 pence a share.

Thames in June warned of poor interim earnings, saying that depressed advertising revenue, new tax arrangements, and costs relating to its $89 million acquisition of Reeves Communications Corp. of the U.S. would weigh heavily on the results.

Thames said BET PLC and Thorn EMI PLC, which each own 28% of the company, are still in the process of disposing of their stakes. It didn't disclose further details.
