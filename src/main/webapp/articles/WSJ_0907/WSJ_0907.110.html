Group Seeks Inquiry Into Proposed Deal By Builders Transport

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/07/90

WASHINGTON -- A dissident group led by David C. Walentas said it has asked the Securities and Exchange Commission to investigate information it says casts doubt on the legitimacy of a proposal for a business combination between Builders Transport Inc. and Comcar Industries Inc. In a letter to the SEC, Mr. Walentas said a trucking industry executive told him that he learned from a Comcar executive that the February letter of intent between the two companies was signed by Comcar as a favor to Charles C. Gay Sr., chairman of Builders Transport at the time.

Mr. Walentas said in an interview that Builders Transport, a truckload carrier based in Camden, S.C., was "trying to repel" his group's December proposal to acquire the company at $13 a share, or $54.6 million, which he said the company "rejected out of hand without speaking to us."

Following the announcement of the Comcar proposal, Builders Transport's common stock rose from $9.75 a share to $13.25 a share. Builders Transport said in June that Comcar wasn't willing to proceed with the transaction.

Robert Garner, general counsel of Builders Transport, denied Mr. Walentas's allegations. "We never would have entered into an agreement with Comcar if we had any reason to doubt the legitimacy . . . of (Comcar's) offer," Mr. Garner said.

Mr. Garner said Builders Transport is continuing discussions with another suitor, Trism Inc., which it said is pursuing financing for an offer.
