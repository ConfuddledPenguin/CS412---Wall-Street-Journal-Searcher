Charges Are Amended Against Former Officer Of Columbia Savings

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/07/90

WASHINGTON -- Thrift regulators said they amended civil charges against Thomas Spiegel, seeking an unspecified amount of additional restitution from the former executive of Columbia Savings & Loan Association. The new charges by the Office of Thrift Supervision accuse Mr. Spiegel of causing the thrift to incur an additional loss of $29.2 million by building lavish new corporate headquarters in Beverly Hills, Calif. The building, according to the regulators, was built without regard to cost, and included a multilevel gymnasium, "survival chamber" bathrooms with bullet-proof glass, and an independent air and food supply.

In July, the OTS filed civil charges against Mr. Spiegel, accusing him of using thrift funds for such acquisitions as a huge gun collection, aircraft and $7,000 in Michael Jackson concert tickets. The agency sought $24 million in restitution and penalties at that time. Mr. Spiegel denied all charges.

Contrary to past practice, the hearing for Mr. Spiegel will be public, the regulators said, as will an administrative hearing of civil charges against Charles Keating, former chairman of the parent of Lincoln Savings & Loan Association, Irvine, Calif. Neither hearing date has been set.
