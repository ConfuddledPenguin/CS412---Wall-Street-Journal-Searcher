Business Brief -- Getty Petroleum Corp.: Gasoline Marketer Had Loss Of $1.9 Million in Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/07/90

Getty Petroleum Corp., a major independent gasoline marketer, reported a fiscal second-quarter loss of $1.9 million, or 14 cents a share, after an inventory-related charge of $3.3 million. At the same time, Getty said directors authorized the purchase of as many as an additional 500,000 of its shares. Under a 500,000-share authorization made in June 1989, Getty has bought back 413,073 shares.

The net loss in the quarter ended July 31 compares with net income of $1.9 million, or 14 cents a share, in the year-earlier period. Revenue rose 7.9% to $320.6 million from $297.1 million, largely because of higher merchandise sales at Getty's convenience stores and higher sales of gasoline to wholesalers.

But earnings were hurt, Getty said, because of volatility in its product costs and because "the significant increase in petroleum prices during the second quarter could not be passed along" to customers "because of competitive marketing conditions."
