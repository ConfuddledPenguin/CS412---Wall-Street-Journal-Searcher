Business Brief -- Niagara Exchange Corp.: Riedman Ends Proxy Fight, Will Buy Preferred Shares

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/07/90

Niagara Exchange Corp. said Riedman Corp., ending its proxy fight with the insurance company, agreed to buy 978,000 shares of Niagara preferred for $8.25 a share, or about $8 million. Niagara, of Buffalo, N.Y., has also agreed to expand its board to nine members from seven to include two Riedman representatives.

Riedman, which owns about 14% of Niagara's 1.8 million common shares outstanding, had launched a proxy fight to win the board seats. This accord ends the proxy fight and certain litigation arising from Niagara's previous plans to sell preferred stock to another investor.

Riedman is a Rochester, N.Y., insurance agency.

Niagara said it will use most of the proceeds from the sale of the Series C cumulative convertible preferred stock to replenish funds in its Exchange Insurance Co. subsidiary, which was hit by claims following Hurricane Hugo.
