Airbus Signs Accord To Sell 2 Types of Jet To Taiwanese Carrier

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 09/07/90

PARIS -- Airbus Industrie said it signed a firm contract to sell two A320 aircraft and four A300-600R aircraft to Foshing Airlines of Taiwan, which also took options to buy four other A300-600Rs. Airbus didn't give financial details of the order, but industry sources estimate that one A320 costs about $40 million and that an A300-600R costs a little more than $77 million. Prices vary depending on the spare parts and training selected by the customer.

Airbus said deliveries to Foshing of the A320s, to be powered by International Aero Engines V2500 engines, will begin in 1992. The A300-600Rs, equipped with Pratt & Whitney PW-4000s, will arrive in 1994.

Firm orders for the Airbus's A300 and A310 aircraft now total 657 from 78 customers, while orders for A320s and A321s stand at 707 from 33 customers.

Airbus is a consortium of Aerospatiale of France, Messerschmitt-Boelkow-Blohm G.m.b.H. of West Germany, British Aerospace PLC and Construcciones Aeronauticas S.A. of Spain.
