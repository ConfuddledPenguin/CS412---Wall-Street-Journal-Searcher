Brazil's VASP Airline Sold to Consortium For $43.7 Million Total

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 09/07/90

SAO PAULO, Brazil -- The state-owned airline, Viacao Aerea Sao Paulo, was sold for $43.7 million to a private consortium of VASP employees and a wealthy businessman. The sale took place in an auction at the Sao Paulo Stock Exchange. The VOE/Canhedo consortium, which was the only group bidding, purchased 60% of the company's shares. The privatization of VASP had been in the works for more than two years.

The Sao Paulo state government retained 40% of the shares but is expected to reduce this stake to 30% within three months. Airline employees will be able to purchase 10% of the shares owned by the government.

VOE was recently formed by VASP employees to participate in the bidding. It owns 20% of the consortium. The consortium's majority partner, Wagner Canhedo, owns several transportation, mining, agricultural and tourism companies. The consortium will have to renegotiate VASP debt of $750 million.

Mr. Canhedo said he hopes to increase VASP's national and international flights.

VASP has a fleet of 21 Boeing Co. 737-200 and eight Boeing 737-300 planes, and three aircraft made by Airbus Industrie, a European consortium. The airline has routes to most major Brazilian cities and to Aruba. It also operates charter flights to Cuba and Argentina.
