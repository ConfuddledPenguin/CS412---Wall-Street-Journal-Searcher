Spanish Firm, Dillon Read Plan Investment Firm

  • Author: Nicholas Bray
  • Date: 09/07/90

MADRID -- Banco Hispano Americano said it plans to launch an industrial investment firm in Spain with Dillon, Read Ltd. of London and the Rockefeller family investment arm, Rockefeller & Co. The firm will have an initial capital of six billion pesetas ($61.2 million) and will invest in quoted and non-quoted Spanish companies, with a view to realizing capital gains over a three-to-five-year period.

Holdings will be evenly divided between Spanish partners, led by Hispano, and non-Spanish partners, led by Rockefeller.

Rockefeller will take a 30% stake alongside an undisclosed non-Spanish partner, while Dillon Read, a unit of Dillon, Read & Co., will split 20% with another non-Spanish partner.

On the Spanish side, Hispano will share a 50% holding with construction firm Ferrovial S.A., family-controlled investment firm Abengoa S.A. and a possible fourth partner.

Ferrovial chairman Rafael del Pino and Abengoa chairman Javier Benjumea are Hispano's two largest individual holders, with a combined holding of about 6%.

According to a Dillon Read official in London, the new company will debut in "a few weeks" and list on the Madrid stock exchange "in due course." According to this official, the recent plunge in prices on the Madrid stock exchange hasn't upset the group's investment plans.

"It's a very opportune moment to start making investments," he said. "We find lower prices a plus . . . we're not dealing with an overheated market any more."

The new firm's management team has been culled from investment firm Bestinver, controlled by one of Ferrovial's principal competitors, Jose-Maria Entrecanales, the owner of construction firm Entrecanales & Tavora S.A.

Earlier this year, Entrecanales and Ferrovial were at loggerheads over Ferrovial's bid to take over a third construction firm, Cubiertas & Mzov S.A., in which Entrecanales has a stake. The dispute was finally settled amicably, with Ferrovial selling the holding which it had built up in Cubiertas.

Jose-Ignacio Benjumea, who as Bestinver's managing director was one of the participants in the Cubiertas struggle, will run the new company. He will be assisted by Carlos Prado, formerly Bestinver's industrial specialist, and Santos MartinezConde, formerly head of Bestinver's research department. Mr. Benjumea is a nephew of Javier Benjumea, the Abengoa chairman.
