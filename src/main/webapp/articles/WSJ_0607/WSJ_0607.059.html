New Securities Issues

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/07/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATE

Du Pont Co. -- $1.85 billion redemption amount of zero-coupon convertible subordinated notes due June 14, 2010, priced at $218.55 for each $1,000 note for a yield to maturity of 7.75%. The size of the issue was increased from an originally planned $1.1 billion. The notes can be put back to the company in the fifth, 10th and 15th years for cash, common or subordinated repayment notes, at Du Pont's option. The notes are convertible into common at $47.375 a share, representing a 19.56% premium over yesterday's closing price. Rated double-A-3 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and double-A-minus by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through First Boston Corp.

Georgia-Pacific Corp. -- $600 million of credit-sensitive notes in a two-part offering priced through underwriters led by Bear Stearns & Co. The first part, $300 million of 10% notes due June 15, 1997, was priced at par. The non-callable notes were priced at a spread of 150 basis points above the Treasury's seven-year note. The second part, $300 million of 10.10% notes due June 15, 2002, was priced at 99.937 to yield 10.109%. The non-callable notes were priced at 165 basis points above the Treasury's 10-year note. The notes are called credit-sensitive because the coupon on the securities will be adjusted upward in case of a credit rating downgrade and downward in the event of a rating upgrade. The issue is rated Baa-3 by Moody's and double-B-plus by S&P.

Southern California Edison Co. -- $200 million of 9 1/4% first and refunding mortgage bonds due June 15, 2021, via a group led by Salomon Brothers Inc., to be reoffered at 95.919 to yield 9.67%. The bonds, which are non-callable for five years, were priced at 123 basis points above the Treasury's 30-year bond. The issue is rated double-A-2 by Moody's and double-A by S&P.

Staples Inc. -- $70 million of 7% convertible subordinated debentures due June 1, 2015. The debentures are priced at par and are convertible into common at $30 a share. Goldman, Sachs & Co. is lead manager.

MUNICIPAL

University of Texas Board of Regents -- $80 million of 5 1/4% permanent university fund 25-year variable-rate notes, apparently won by a Goldman Sachs & Co. group. The notes, which are rated double-A/VMIG-1 by Moody's and single-A-1-plus by S&P, are priced initially to yield 4.49%. The notes have a mandatory put date of June 11, 1990.

MORTGAGES

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $300.1 million of Remic mortgage securities offered in 13 classes by Shearson Lehman Hutton. The offering, Series 1990-76, is backed by Fannie Mae 9 1/2% securities with a weighted average coupon rate of 10.20% and weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29.50 years. Price and yield data weren't immediately available.

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $300 million of Remic mortgage securities offered in 11 classes by Goldman Sachs & Co. The offering, Series 1990-77, is backed by new stripped mortgage securities formed from Fannie Mae 10 1/2% securities. Goldman Sachs took all the principal-only strips from the new strips trust, 79-CL, and most of the interest-only strips from the trust and used them as collateral. The use of a portion of the interest-only strips effectively lowered the coupon rate on the collateral. The remaining interest-only strips not pledged to the Remic were sold separately. Price and yield details weren't provided. There is a weighted average coupon rate of 11.11% and a weighted average term to maturity of 28.5 years.

AGENCY

Farm Credit System Financial Assistance Corp. -- $325 million of 15-year fixed-rate, non-callable bonds to be dated and delivered June 12. The interest rate on the bonds will be announced today. The offering will be made through a limited selling group of securities dealers and dealer banks. Members of the Federal Farm Credit Banks Consolidated Systemwide Bonds Selling Group also are eligible to participate through the reallowance. The issue will be available in book-entry form only, through the Federal Reserve System.

EUROBONDS

Lavoro Bank Overseas N.V. (Italian parent) -- 10 billion yen of 6 7/8% Eurobonds due Sept. 29, 1995, priced at 101 3/8 via Daiwa Europe Ltd. Guaranteed by Banca Nazionale del Lavoro. Fees 1 7/8.

Swedish Export Credit Corp. (Sweden) -- 50 million European currency units of 11% Eurobonds due June 27, 1990, priced at 101.625 via Sanwa International Ltd. Fees 1.

Kemira International Finance (Finland) -- 150 million Finnish markka of 13 1/2% Eurobonds due July 27, 1993, priced at 101.375 via Postipankki. Guaranteed by Kemira Oy. Fees 1 3/8.

Aegon N.V. (Netherlands) -- 100 million Swiss francs of 7% privately placed notes due June 26, 1995, priced at 101 5/8 via Swiss Bank Corp. Fees 1.50.
