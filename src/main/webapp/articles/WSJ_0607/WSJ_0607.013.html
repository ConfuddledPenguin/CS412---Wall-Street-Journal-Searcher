Swiss Apply to Join IMF, Mull World Bank Entry

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/07/90

WASHINGTON -- Switzerland formally applied for membership in the International Monetary Fund, and is expected to apply to join the World Bank. Switzerland has long had close informal ties with the both institutions. It is a member of the "Group of 10" industrial nations, which stands ready to lend to the IMF, when necessary, to help it provide credit to countries having international payments difficulties.

In addition, its three major commercial banks frequently have been underwriters of World Bank debt, and Switzerland has contributed to various special World Bank accounts. The country also is a financial backer of the International Development Association, a World Bank affiliate which provides interest-free credits to poorer Third World nations.

Assuming IMF member countries approve the Swiss application and the country takes the necessary legal steps to enable it to sign the IMF's articles of agreement, it could become a member by year end, according to a spokesman for the 152-nation agency. World Bank officials said a Swiss request for membership there will be processed as soon as it is received. Countries seeking to join the World Bank first must qualify for membership in the IMF.

The Swiss Federal Council decided "in principle" in 1982 to join the two international agencies, but it temporarily shelved the idea in 1986 after Swiss voters rejected the Swiss government's proposal to join the United Nations.
