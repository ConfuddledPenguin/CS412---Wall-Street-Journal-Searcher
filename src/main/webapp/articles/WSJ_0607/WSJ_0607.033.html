Alcoa Closes Its Bauxite Mines

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/07/90

PITTSBURGH -- Aluminum Co. of America said it closed its last bauxite mines in the U.S., resulting in the layoffs or retirements of 200 employees. The mines, in Saline County, Ark., are adjacent to Alcoa's bauxite refinery and alumina chemical facilities. The company said the mining and refining operations were shut down because of insufficient reserves of bauxite.

Alcoa also said it will close permanently about 275,000 metric tons annually of alumina production capacity. Alumina is the raw material from which aluminum, and alumina chemicals, are made. A metric ton is equal to about 2,204.62 pounds.
