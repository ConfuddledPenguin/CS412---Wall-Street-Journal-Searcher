Niagara Mohawk, 4 Other Utilities Settle New York Litigation

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/07/90

NEW YORK -- Niagara Mohawk Power Corp. and four other utilities agreed to absorb a total of $263 million of cost overruns on their Nine Mile Point 2 nuclear power plant as part of a final settlement of litigation with New York state. In Albany, Paul L. Gioia, analyst at First Albany Corp., said the settlement should have only a "minor impact" on Niagara Mohawk, the largest plant owner.

Nine Mile Point 2 went into commercial operation in April 1988, some eight years behind schedule and billions of dollars over its estimated cost. Since going into service, it has been hit with equipment and other operating problems that have led to numerous lawsuits involving defendants that include the plant's contractors.

Yesterday, the New York state Attorney General's office said that customers of the five utilities will receive $28 million in refunds. That sum represents money previously collected from customers to cover the cost of buying replacement power from other utilities when Nine Mile Point 2 was forced out of service in October 1988.

Niagara Mohawk said its customers will receive the largest refund, about $18 million, reflecting the utility's 41% stake in the generating facility. Other plant owners are Long Island Lighting Co., 18%; New York State Electric & Gas Corp., 18%; Rochester Gas & Electric Corp., 14%; and Central Hudson Gas & Electric Corp., 9%.

The five utilities also will absorb $203 million in construction costs that they had wanted to pass on to their customers. Niagara Mohawk said its share of this disallowance will total $66 million, but it added that its books already reflect the writedown.

In addition, the utilities gave up their fight to increase the cap on how much of Nine Mile Point 2's total $6.59 billion cost they could collect in rate increases from customers. The now-permanent $4.16 billion cap was set in 1986.

A separate part of the agreement calls for General Electric Co. to provide $32 million of maintenance and equipment on the plant to settle litigation with the utilities arising from the plant's problems.
