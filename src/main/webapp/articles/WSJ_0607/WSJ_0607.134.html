U.S. Jury Convicts Former Consultant Of Pentagon Fraud

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/07/90

ALEXANDRIA, Va. (AP) -- A former defense consultant was convicted of conspiring to funnel sensitive military-procurement data to a corporation bidding on a $100 million project. A federal district court jury found Mark Saunders guilty of conspiracy, conversion of government property and six counts of wire fraud.

He was the fourth person convicted by trial in the Operation Ill Wind investigation into Pentagon fraud, although 34 individuals and corporations have pleaded guilty as a result of the inquiry, which began nearly two years ago.

The jury found that Mr. Saunders was a middleman in a scheme to pass confidential government information from George Stone, a Navy procurement director, to Thomas Muldoon, a defense consultant for Norden Systems Inc., a division of United Technologies Corp.

Witnesses said Mr. Muldoon then sold the information to Norden, one of 10 companies competing for a contract to design sophisticated radar and communications systems for the Marine Corps. Norden dropped out of the competition when the investigation began.

Mr. Stone has pleaded guilty to conspiracy and other charges in the case, as have three former Norden executives. Mr. Muldoon has been convicted of participating in an unrelated scheme to bribe Pentagon procurement officials.
