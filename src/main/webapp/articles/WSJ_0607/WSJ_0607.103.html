Business Brief -- Toro Co.: Firm Puts Fiscal '90 Profit Below Analysts' Projections

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/07/90

Toro Co. said it anticipates that net income for its fiscal year ending July 31 will be about $1.60 a share, or 20 to 30 cents less than some analysts' forecasts. Last year, the Bloomington, Minn., maker of outdoor maintenance and irrigation equipment earned $22.1 million, or $2.10 a share, on sales of $643.6 million.

In a statement released after the close of New York Stock Exchange trading yesterday, Toro blamed its decline on continuing wet and cool weather in many of its major markets. It also cited costs from consolidating operations of Lawn-Boy Inc., the lawn-mower manufacturer it acquired last year from Outboard Marine Corp.

Toro said its problems will cause it to post a loss of about 15 cents a share for the fiscal fourth quarter. In the year-earlier quarter, Toro earned $2.3 million, or 21 cents a share, on sales of $137.5 million.

The company added that its woes probably will delay product shipments and hurt earnings in the first fiscal quarter of 1991.

Toro closed yesterday at $28.25, up 37.5 cents, in Big Board composite trading.
