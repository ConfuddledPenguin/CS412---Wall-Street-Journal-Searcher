S&P Forms Apparel Group

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/07/90

NEW YORK -- Standard & Poor's Corp. said it formed a new industry group from the Retail Specialty group to consist only of apparel retailers. The concern also transferred Nacco Industries from the coal group to the manufacturing group. The new group in the company's 500-stock index, Retail Specialty (Apparel), has four companies: Charming Shoppes, Gap Inc., Limited Inc. and TJX Cos. The remaining companies in Retail Specialty are Circuit City, Home Depot, Lowe's Cos., Melville Corp., Price Co., Tandy Corp. and Toys "R" Us.

Standard & Poor's said the shift of Nacco reflects its change from primarily coal production to manufacturing. The company also makes forklift trucks and household appliances.
