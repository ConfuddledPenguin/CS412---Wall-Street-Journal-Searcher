International: Former Communist Chiefs Detained by Czech Police

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/07/90

PRAGUE, Czechoslovakia (AP) -- The government rounded up at least seven former Communist Party leaders yesterday in a move likely to hurt the chances of Communist candidates two days before the nation's first free election since 1946. The party is running a distant third in opinion polls.

Detained was Vasil Bilak, the former party official thought to have invited in the Soviet troops who crushed the "Prague Spring" reform movement of Alexander Dubcek in 1968. At least six other former senior Communists were questioned.

In another political development, Josef Bartoncik, a major Christian Democratic politician accused of having been a secret police informer, was denounced by the minister charged with investigating past secret police operations and urged to quit politics.

Yesterday's developments seemed likely to benefit the Civic Forum movement of President Vaclav Havel, already favored to win tomorrow's elections. The Christian Democrats are running second in the surveys.

The state news agency CTK said Mr. Bilak was held "on suspicion of committing serious criminal activity."

Prominent Communists questioned yesterday included former president and party chief Gustav Husak; Milos Jakes, the party chief ousted in last winter's pro-democracy revolution; and former Premier Jozef Lenart, state TV news said.
