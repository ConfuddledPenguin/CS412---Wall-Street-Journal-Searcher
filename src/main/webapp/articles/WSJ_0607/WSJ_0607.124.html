ITC Plans to Reject Kodak Plea to Drop Import Investigation

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/07/90

WASHINGTON -- Eastman Kodak Co. apparently has failed in its efforts to persuade the government to drop an investigation that could lead to higher tariffs or import quotas on fixed-focus 35mm and 110-type cameras from Asia, Latin America and other parts of the world. The investigation was launched in response to a complaint by Keystone Camera Products Corp., Clifton, N.J., calling for import restrictions "on behalf of the U.S. industry." Despite objections from Kodak, the U.S. International Trade Commission was readying a letter notifying the Rochester, N.Y.-based company that it would continue the inquiry, U.S. officials said.

The officials said the ITC will decide in late July whether to recommend to President Bush that he order restrictions on the imports.

The Keystone petition was filed under a provision of the 1974 Trade Act which allows the government temporarily to block imports that are harming a domestic industry, even if the harm doesn't result from any trade violation.

Keystone argued that without such protection, it will be forced to shift its production to other countries. It charged that Kodak "has, for all intents and purposes, abandoned U.S. production {of the fixed-focus cameras} in favor of importing from facilities in low-cost countries."

Kodak attorneys replied that the company is producing and assembling 35mm cameras in U.S. factories, although they said it has been assembling some U.S.-manufactured parts for 35mm and 110-type cameras in factories in Mexico and Brazil for eventual sale in the U.S. market.
