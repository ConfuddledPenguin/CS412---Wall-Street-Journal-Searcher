Business Brief -- BASF AG: U.S. Unit Plans to Close New Jersey Plant This Year

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/07/90

BASF Corp. will close the Kearny, N.J., plant at which it produces phthalic anhydride, a raw material used in making plastic resins, and dismiss most of the 140 employees there. The U.S. unit of West Germany's BASF AG will close the facility by year end, it said in Parsippany, N.J.

Phthalic anhydride is used to make plasticizers, in turn used to make plastic products soft and flexible. A spokesman said that plastics containing plasticizers are used in such diverse items as vinyl raincoats and phone-cable insulation.

The spokesman declined to provide revenue for the operations being closed but said the plant hasn't been profitable for several years. Costs of closing won't affect earnings, he said.

BASF will manufacture the products only in West Germany and Spain. BASF will continue to market the phthalic anhydride and plasticizers produced by Sterling Chemicals Inc. of Houston at Sterling's Texas City, Texas, facilities.

BASF Corp. makes chemicals, pharmaceuticals, plastics, coatings, printing inks and other products.
