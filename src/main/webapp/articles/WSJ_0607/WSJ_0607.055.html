Technology: Hitachi Introduces a Line Of Mainframe Computers

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/07/90

NEW YORK -- Hitachi Ltd. of Japan introduced a line of mainframe computers, the first of the new generation of IBM-compatible machines that will come out in the next few quarters. The machines, as previously reported, easily outdistance those on the market from International Business Machines Corp., Fujitsu Ltd. and Amdahl Corp., which is 46%-owned by Fujitsu and sells machines that Fujitsu designs. Consultants said that the Hitachi machine with three central processors can operate at 124 million instructions per second, and that the four-processor machine runs at 150 mips. By contrast, IBM's most powerful machine runs at 112 mips, and the fastest Fujitsu/Amdahl mainframe runs at 108 mips.

The machines coming from IBM and Fujitsu/Amdahl are expected to be roughly comparable to the Hitachi hardware. Still, Hitachi, which has been the laggard at the high end of the mainframe market, is finally expected to become a player there.

Hitachi said shipments will begin in the third quarter. Its 80%-owned U.S. arm, Hitachi Data Systems, said it will begin shipments in the 1991 second quarter. That, again, puts it roughly on a par with IBM and Fujitsu/Amdahl, which are expected to begin U.S. shipments between the 1991 second quarter and the end of the year.

Hitachi Data said a typical three-processor system will be priced at $11.8 million, and a typical four-processor system at $14.4 million. Consultants said these prices are about 10% to 15% less than IBM charges for each unit of computing power, the normal discount needed to compete with IBM.
