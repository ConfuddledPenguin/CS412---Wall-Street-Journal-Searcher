Economy: Greenspan Says Economy Hasn't Deteriorated Lately

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/07/90

WASHINGTON -- Federal Reserve Board Chairman Alan Greenspan said that despite recent weak employment numbers, he doesn't see any underlying deterioration in the U.S. economy. The Fed chairman's comments, made at an international monetary conference in San Francisco, followed a report in The Wall Street Journal Tuesday that Fed officials have no plans to ease short-term interest rates in the near future.

Mr. Greenspan also said that it is unclear whether the unification of Germany will lead to higher interest rates around the globe, "I think it's much too soon, at this stage, to make any judgments about what the effect on interest rates will be," he said. Mr. Greenspan said there was little good information about how the monetary union of East and West Germany would affect the economy, or overall savings and investment.

"Without having a particularly good fix on those critical points, I don't see how it is realistic to make judgments about what" interest rates will be, he said.
