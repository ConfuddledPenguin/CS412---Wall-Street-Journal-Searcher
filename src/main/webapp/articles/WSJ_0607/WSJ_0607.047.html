New Car Sales in Britain in May

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/07/90

LONDON -- May new-car sales in Britain, measured by registrations, totaled 173,896, a 13% decline from a year earlier but a 4.2% rise from April, the Society of Motor Manufacturers and Traders Ltd. said. Imports took a 55% share of the market in May, down from 58% in April and 56% a year earlier. Total imports, including cars produced overseas by British manufacturers, fell 1% to 95,207 in May, compared with the previous month, and were off 14% from a year earlier.

Ford Motor Co., Dearborn, Mich., sold 47,996 cars in May, up 11% from April but down 12% from a year earlier. Ford's market share was 28%, up from 26% in April and unchanged from a year earlier. Detroit-based General Motors Corp.'s May sales advanced 9.7% to 27,685, compared with April, and slipped 8.9% from a year earlier. GM's market share edged up to 16% from 15% both in April and a year earlier.
