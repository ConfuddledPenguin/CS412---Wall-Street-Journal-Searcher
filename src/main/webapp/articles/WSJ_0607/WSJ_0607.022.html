Letters to the Editor: Your Attack on a Myth Repeats Another Myth

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/07/90

Your otherwise-excellent May 11 editorial "Real Soldiers," which attacks the myth of Soviet "liberation," repeats the myth of 20 million Soviet civilian deaths during World War II (your figure of 27 million total deaths less seven million military deaths, of which almost half were sustained in the wars of aggression against Poland and Finland, when the Soviets were allies of the Nazis). In his carefully documented book "Stalin's Secret War," Nikolai Tolstoy puts the figure of Soviet civilian casualties attributable to the Germans at about seven million. The balance was directly or indirectly attributable to Soviet policies during the Finnish campaign -- compelling the evacuation of people suspected of pro-German sympathies; NKVD purges; unprecedented mortality in the greatly swollen camps of the Gulag, and 2.5 million Soviet refugees handed back to the Soviets by the Allies in 1945 (Operation Keelhaul).

Anthony T. Bouscaren

Professor of Political Science Emeritus

Le Moyne College

Fayetteville, N.Y.
