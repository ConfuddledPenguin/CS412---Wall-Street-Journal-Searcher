Business Brief -- Hooker Corp.: L.J. Hooker to Liquidate 5 of Its 6 Sakowitz Stores

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/07/90

L.J. Hooker Corp. said it will liquidate five of the six stores that comprise Sakowitz Inc., a Houston-based specialty chain. A sixth store, in a Hooker-owned mall outside Cincinnati will remain open indefinitely to preserve the value of the mall, according to a Hooker spokesman.

Sakowitz filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection this May. Atlanta-based L.J. Hooker, which is a unit of Australia-based Hooker Corp., filed for Chapter 11 in August 1989. L.J. Hooker has since liquidated its B. Altman & Co. chain, as well as most of Bonwit Teller.

Robert Sakowitz, former president and chief executive officer of the retail chain, said in a statement that he "deeply regrets" the end of a Houston institution and noted that he had spent many months trying to raise capital to buy the chain.
