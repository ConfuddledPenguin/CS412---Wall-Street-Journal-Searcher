International Brief -- Thames Water PLC: Utility, Recently Privatized, Says Profit Topped Forecast

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/07/90

Thames Water PLC, one of Britain's recently privatized utilities, reported a pretax profit of #179 million ($106.1 million) for the year ending March 31, topping the forecast it made last November. The pretax profit compares with the #170 million forecast in the privatization prospectus published a month ahead of the December share sale.

The results aren't directly comparable with a year earlier, when pretax profit totaled #207 million, due to changes in the firm's capital structure and the exclusion of the government-owned National Rivers Authority from the latest year.

Sales rose 9.5% to #611 million from the #588 million Thames gathered under government ownership a year earlier.

Profit after taxes totaled #166.3 million, while per-share earnings were 43.6 pence.

Extraordinary charges of #18.7 million, compared with #3.2 million a year earlier, were comprised of #11.3 million in costs related to the share sale and #7.4 million relating to unfunded pension liabilities.

Net profit attributable to shareholders after the extraordinary items stood at #147.6 million in the latest year.
