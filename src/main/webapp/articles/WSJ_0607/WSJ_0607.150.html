Who's News: Kinder-Care Names Samper as President And Chief Executive

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/07/90

MONTGOMERY, Ala. -- Kinder-Care Learning Centers Inc. said it named J. Phillip Samper, the former vice chairman of Eastman Kodak Co., as president and chief executive officer of the big child-care company. Mr. Samper, 55 years old, is a 28-year Kodak veteran. He had overseen Kodak's photographic products business, but decided to retire at the end of last year following Kodak's appointment of Kay R. Whitmore as chairman and chief executive officer. Mr. Samper, who had spent much of his career building Kodak's international business, was Mr. Whitmore's chief rival for the top jobs at Kodak.

Mr. Samper succeeds Tull Gearreald, who has served as interim president and chief executive officer of Kinder-Care Learning Centers since February. Mr. Gearreald will remain a director of the company and a partner in Lodestar Group, a New York investment banking company that holds about 63% of Kinder-Care Learning Centers.

In addition to Mr. Samper, the company added six other directors to its board, as Lodestar continues to strengthen its control over the company. The new directors are: Herbert Baum, president of Campbell North America; Jack Greenberg, senior executive vice president and chief financial officer, McDonald's Corp.; Malcom Hopkins, former vice chairman and chief financial officer, St. Regis Corp.; Geraldine Laybourne, president of Nickelodeon; Dr. Sandra Scarr, a University of Virginia psychology professor; and Wayne Thornbrough, chief investment officer of Lodestar.

The appointments, six of which were to fill vacancies, expand the board to 12 members.

Late last year, Lodestar sought to acquire the 37% of Kinder-Care that it didn't already own, but the company rejected the Lodestar proposal.
