Anheuser-Busch Files Complaint With FTC Against Adolph Coors

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/07/90

Anheuser-Busch Cos. said it filed a complaint with the Federal Trade Commission charging rival brewer Adolph Coors Co. with "false and deceptive" advertising practices for touting its Rocky Mountain roots. The flap began after Coors started testing an advertising campaign that brags about the "Clean, fresh, pure Rocky Mountain Spring water" used to brew its beer. The 30- and 60-second television advertisements cut back and forth between quiet scenes of Colorado mountains and pictures of the crowded highways of big cities.

Anheuser took its gripe public after Coors announced on Tuesday its plans to buy a Memphis, Tenn., brewery from Stroh Brewery Co. "We wonder how that company can maintain its `Rocky Mountain identity' while brewing in Memphis," said Michael J. Roarty, executive vice president of corporate marketing at Anheuser-Busch.

Mr. Roarty said that the Coors campaign implies that its beers are brewed using only Colorado water. But Coors actually makes some beer in a packaging facility in Virginia by mixing local water with beer concentrate, according to Mr. Roarty. The water is fine, but "the advertising is deceptive, and we think they should stop it," he said.

In Golden, a spokeswoman for Coors said all Coors beer is currently brewed in Golden with Rocky Mountain spring water, and "we've made no decision to brew anywhere else." The spokeswoman also said that all beer starts as "high-gravity beer," including beers made by Anheuser-Busch, which are later blended with water and carbon dioxide to meet government alcohol-content requirements. Coors does ship about 15% of its high-gravity beer to Virginia, where it is blended with water and packaged, the spokeswoman said.
