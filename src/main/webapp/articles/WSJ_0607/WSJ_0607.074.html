Merrill Lynch Launches a Fund

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/07/90

LONDON -- Merrill Lynch & Co., a New York-based securities firm, said it launched Austro-Hungary Fund Ltd., a $50 million closed-end fund to invest in listed stocks in the Austrian and Hungarian markets. A closed-end fund issues a fixed number of shares that are traded like stocks. The dollar-denominated fund is expected to be the first foreign company listed on the Hungarian Stock Exchange when that market reopens on June 21, said Charles Lillis, a director of Merrill Lynch's international unit here.

The fund's portfolio will be equally divided between Austrian and Hungarian issues, with the Austrian investments emphasizing companies with business links to Hungary, said Peter Kysel, a managing director of Lloyds Investment Management International, the new fund's adviser.
