Business Brief -- Atlantic Richfield Co.: Company Sells Main Interest In California Oil, Gas Field

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/07/90

Atlantic Richfield Co. said it sold its 88% interest in the South Cuyama oil and gas field to Santa Barbara Partners of Oklahoma City. Terms weren't disclosed. Santa Barbara Partners includes Cuyama Oil and Gas Co., controlled by Stream Energy Inc, Oklahoma City; Trio Petroleum Inc., Bakersfield, Calif.; and Hallador Production Co., Denver. Stream Energy said it will be the field operator.

The South Cuyama field produces about 1,000 barrels a day of crude oil and 2.5 million cubic feet a day of natural gas from 93 wells. The sale also included a gas treatment plant in New Cuyama, Calif.

Discovered in 1949 by Richfield Oil Co., a predecessor company of Atlantic Richfield of Los Angeles, the field reached peak production in 1951 and continued to be significant through the early 1960s. Atlantic Richfield said its California oil production is currently about 50,000 barrels a day.
