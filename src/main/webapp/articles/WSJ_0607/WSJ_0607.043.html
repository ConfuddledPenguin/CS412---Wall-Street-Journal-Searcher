Borrowing by Governments And Private Sectors Rises

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/07/90

WASHINGTON -- Borrowing by households, nonfinancial businesses and federal, state and local governments grew at a seasonally adjusted annual rate of 7.9% in the first quarter, up from 7.7% the prior quarter, the Federal Reserve Board said. Borrowing by the federal government grew at a 13% annual rate in the first quarter, while private borrowing grew at a 6.3% rate, the Fed said. First-quarter borrowing by the nonfinancial sector totaled $771.2 billion, consisting of $295.8 billion by the federal government and $475.4 billion by the private sector.

Total credit-market debt owed by the nonfinancial sector at the end of the first quarter was $9.99 trillion, compared with $9.81 trillion at the end of the fourth quarter and $9.24 trillion a year earlier.

Within private-sector borrowing, nonfinancial business borrowing increased at a 6.3% rate, compared with 6.6% the previous quarter. Household borrowing rose at a 6.5% rate, compared with 9.6% the previous quarter. State and local government borrowing grew at a 5.5% rate, compared with 2% the previous quarter. State and local borrowing is included in the total private borrowing figures.

For all of 1989, total nonfinancial debt grew 7.8%, compared with 9.1% in each of 1988 and 1987.
