Salomon Beats Out Rivals to Capitalize On Eastern Europe --- Securities Firm Will Advise U.S. Investment Agency On Unusual New Fund

  • Author: Michael Siconolfi
  • Date: 06/07/90

NEW YORK -- A big Wall Street firm found a way to profit from the upheaval in Eastern Europe. Salomon Brothers Inc. was picked by the U.S. Overseas Private Investment Corp. to be financial adviser to OPIC's fledgling Eastern European Growth Fund.

The securities firm, a Salomon Inc. unit, beat out nine other investment banks to get the job.

The fund expects to raise $200 million from investors in a private placement. Its launch represents an unusual step by OPIC, a U.S. government agency that insures companies investing overseas against political risk.

The fund can invest only in three countries where OPIC has a mandate: Hungary, Poland and Yugoslavia. The agency is holding negotiations with Czechoslovakia.

The fund is seeking to attract large investors, such as pension funds and insurance companies, in the next few months; $50 million of its assets will be backed by OPIC guarantees.

Under the Bush administration, OPIC has become more of a U.S. foreign policy tool. The agency provides investment data, financing and risk assurances -- usually for big institutional investors -- in 116 countries in Latin America, Europe, Asia and Africa.

Because the new fund will be structured as a limited partnership, it won't be actively traded. The investment will have a 15-year life, and investors can't get their money out until at least four years have passed, Salomon said.

The fund is likely to invest in serviceoriented companies, such as communications and environmental concerns, said Michael Holland, chairman of the fund and head of Salomon Asset Management Inc. Mr. Holland said he expects the fund to be able to invest in many more countries during its life.

Salomon isn't saying what its fee will be for advising the project, except to say that its financial reward will be based on how well the fund performs.

The Eastern European Growth Fund isn't the first fund venture for OPIC. The agency has launched an African Growth Fund for development in Sub-Sahara West Africa, run by Equator Bank, Hartford, Conn. The African fund, which seeks to raise $20 million, hasn't raised all the money, OPIC said.
