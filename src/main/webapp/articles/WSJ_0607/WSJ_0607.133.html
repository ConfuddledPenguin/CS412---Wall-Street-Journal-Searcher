Business Brief -- Seagram Co.: Distiller Reports 4.6% Drop In Net for Fiscal 1st Period

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/07/90

Seagram Co.'s net income fell 4.6% in the first quarter ended April 30, reflecting lower unremitted equity earnings at Du Pont Co., the Wilmington, Del., chemical and energy concern in which the Montreal-based distiller holds a 24.3% interest. Unremitted equity earnings are reported in Seagram's results, although the company doesn't get any payments.

Net dropped to $187 million, or $1.97 a share, from $196 million, or $2 a share, a year earlier when more shares were outstanding. The unremitted earnings, representing Seagram's share of Du Pont's profit, fell 27% to $79 million from $108 million. Dividend income from Du Pont rose 14% to $66 million from $58 million.

Operating earnings rose 26% to $157 million from $125 million, while sales advanced 5.4% to $1.29 billion from $1.23 billion. The rises reflect strength in international wine and spirits operations, particularly at the Brazilian affiliate, which was strong, prior to economic controls imposed by the country's new government.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Seagram's shares fell 62.5 cents to $86.25.
