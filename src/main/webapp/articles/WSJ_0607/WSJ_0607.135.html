Armstrong Sets Pact With Belzberg Firms To Resolve Disputes

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/07/90

LANCASTER, Pa. -- Armstrong World Industries Inc. said it agreed to resolve all "outstanding issues" between the company and affiliates of Canada's Belzberg family by paying the Belzbergs a net sum of about $4.4 million. On May 30, the Belzbergs' various holding companies sold their 11.7% stake in the flooring and building-products concern at an estimated $18 million loss, ending a yearlong effort to effect a takeover of the company.

Under the agreement, the Belzbergs dropped all litigation against the company, including a challenge to Pennsylvania's recently enacted takeover law and a lawsuit seeking a binding vote on five corporate-governance amendments. Armstrong withdrew all countersuits.

The Belzbergs also agreed to limit their holdings of Armstrong stock to no more than 50,000 shares for 10 years. Any stock owned can be held only for investment purposes, and the Belzberg companies are specifically prohibited from engaging in a business combination with Armstrong.

As part of the formal settlement, Armstrong will pay the Belzbergs $5 million, and the Belzbergs will pay Armstrong $605,000.

Armstrong said it agreed to settle the issues to end the disruption and expense caused by the suits. "The settlement is a natural consequence of the sale of the Belzberg shares," a spokesman said.
