United Sweetens Mileage In California Air Rivalry

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/07/90

CHICAGO -- UAL Corp.'s United Airlines unit is raising the industry ante in the lucrative California market. United said it is awarding members of its frequent-flier program triple mileage for flights between San Francisco and Los Angeles. It said the offer is retroactive to April 7 and will remain in effect until year end.

It also said United frequent fliers who make 10 one-way trips in the California corridor between April 7 and Dec. 31 will earn double mileage for non-corridor flights that include one of several California airports.

The action comes in the wake of stiff competition in the California market. United is in the midst of a heated battle with several of the country's biggest carriers for dominance in the West Coast corridor, the busiest market of any pair of metropolitan areas in the country, which also includes Oakland and San Jose to the north and Burbank, Ontario, Long Beach and Orange County to the south. In the past several months, carriers have been beefing up flight schedules, introducing low promotional fares and hyping service changes in bids to gain a competitive edge.

United called the latest move its "strongest commitment in its history to California and the West Coast."

A spokeswoman for American Airlines, a unit of AMR Corp., said the carrier hasn't decided whether it will match United's action. "We don't think it's a smart move for American Airlines at this time," she said. USAir Group Inc. said it is studying the move. Officials at Delta Air Lines weren't available to comment.
