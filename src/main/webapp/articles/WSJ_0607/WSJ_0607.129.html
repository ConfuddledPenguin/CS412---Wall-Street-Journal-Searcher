Business Brief -- Hewlett-Packard Co.: Pact to Supply Equipment To Mentor Graphics Is Set

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/07/90

Hewlett-Packard Co. said it agreed to supply Mentor Graphics Corp. with computer equipment valued at up to $400 million. The three-year contract, under which Mentor will buy workstations, software and service, replaces an 18-month, $100 million contract that expired this month. Under the new contract, Mentor will also buy new workstations that Hewlett-Packard is developing, including machines that use as their central controller Motorola Inc.'s new 68040 microprocessor. Hewlett-Packard, based in Palo Alto, Calif., plans to unveil its 68040 machines on June 18.

Mentor designs and sells computer systems that are used to design electonic components and software as well as in computer-aided publishing. Mentor, based in Beaverton, Ore., buys machines from Hewlett-Packard, loads them with Mentor software, and resells them under the Mentor nameplate.
