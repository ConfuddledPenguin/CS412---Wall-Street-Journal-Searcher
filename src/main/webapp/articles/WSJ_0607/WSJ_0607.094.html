GM, Ford Operations Top List of Holders Of Certain U.S. Loans

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/07/90

DETROIT -- General Motors Corp. and Ford Motor Co. financial service operations topped all commercial banks in 1989 as the nation's largest holders of U.S. commercial and industrial loans, according to a study by SMR Research Corp. The study showed that GM ended the year with $23.2 billion in commercial and industrial loans, a drop of 0.4%. That decline compared with a 60% rise in loans by Ford to $16.8 billion. Most of GM's commercial and industrial loans financed car dealer inventories and other property. Ford's big increase came from the acquisition last year of the Associates Corp. finance company.

Citicorp, the nation's largest commercial bank holding company, came in third, according to the study with $16.3 billion of the loans. That figure represents a 4.4% decline in the U.S., although Citicorp dominated world-wide business lending because of the company's sizable portfolio of loans to foreign corporations. At year end, Citicorp had $37.6 billion in commercial and industrial loans outstanding.

Stuart A. Feldstein, SMR president, said many banks have been large originators of business loans, but resell the loans to other lenders.

The SMR study found that the total domestic commercial and industrial loan market reached $815.2 billion last year -- up 5.5% from a year earlier. Growth of the loans at banks, which still hold about three-quarters of the market, was 4.3%.
