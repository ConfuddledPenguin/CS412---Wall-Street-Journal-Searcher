Pic `N' Save Wins Round in Board Battle With Girard Partners

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/07/90

LOS ANGELES -- A federal judge here barred Girard Partners L.P. from soliciting shareholder support for its campaign to oust Pic 'N' Save Corp.'s board until Girard makes certain additional disclosures about itself. U.S. District Court Judge Robert Bonner ruled that Girard can't resume solicitation of shareholder consents and proxies until 10 days after the information is filed. He also ordered Pic 'N' Save to delay the annual meeting until July 2 from June 20 and extended the deadline for Girard's delivery of consents to Pic 'N' Save to June 22 from June 10.

Pic 'N' Save, a Dominguez, Calif., discount retailer, said that in granting the preliminary injunction, the court found that Girard's solicitations violated U.S. securities laws. The court also dissolved that portion of an order issued last week that restrained Pic 'N' Save from soliciting revocations of consents and proxies for the annual meeting.

In La Jolla, Calif., David H. Batchelder, Girard's controlling shareholder, didn't return telephone calls for comment.

Pic 'N' Save recently put itself up for sale in an apparent attempt to convince investors that the incumbent board is more dedicated than Girard to promoting shareholder interests. Girard holds a 6.7% stake in the company and is trying to gain a majority of board seats.
