Marketing & Media: NBC Expects Ad Revenue Shortfall of $200 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/07/90

NEW YORK -- National Broadcasting Co. said it expects a shortfall of $200 million in advertising revenue this year because of a decline in the broadcast network audience and NBC's own faltering prime time lineup. NBC President Robert C. Wright disclosed the expected shortfall at a meeting with the network's 200-plus affiliates at NBC's annual convention in Washington, D.C.

NBC, a unit of General Electric Co., has experienced a slide in viewership from key demographic groups. The result was the network had to give advertisers free time, or make good, when a show failed to deliver the audience size guaranteed to a sponsor.

In the past TV season, NBC was off 12% from last year in women viewers 18 to 49 years old and 14% in men 18 to 49, according to A.C. Nielsen Co. figures.

All three major broadcasting networks have been hurt by a sharp drop in ratings that network officials said is the result of problems with Nielsen's methods of compiling data. Nielsen officials insisted there is nothing wrong with its system, although it has agreed to examine ways of improving it.

Although NBC finished the 1989-90 season No. 1 for the fifth year, its ratings were down 9% from last year. And last month, CBS Inc.'s TV network finished only one-tenth of a ratings point behind NBC. May is one of four months each year that local stations use ratings to determine advertising rates.
