Whittaker Posts Rise Of 49% in Net Income For Its Second Period

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/07/90

LOS ANGELES -- Whittaker Corp. posted a 49% increase in fiscal second-quarter net income to $75.1 million, or $9.06 cents a share, swollen by a $80.5 million nonrecurring gain that more than offset a loss from continuing operations. The one-time gain, equal to $9.71 a share, was related to discontinued operations. In the year-ago quarter, Whittaker had restated net income of $12.6 million, or $1.75 a share, including a gain from discontinued operations of $5.1 million, or 72 cents a share.

The loss from continuing operations was $3.3 million, or 40 cents a share, compared with year-ago restated income from continuing operations of $3.2 million, or 43 cents a share.

Sales climbed 12%, to $51.2 million, from $45.5 million. Whittaker said that its year-ago figures were restated to reflect the sale in March of the company's special chemicals segment, and the reinstatement to continuing operations of two businesses in its aerospace segment. Whittaker's continuing operations are now in aerospace and biotechnology.

The company blamed the sharp declines in income mainly on increased expense related to its recapitalization last June. The quarter also was negatively impacted by a $4.3 million net charge, equal to 52 cents a share, for the write-off of certain unamortized loan costs related to financing of the recapitalization plan.

Whittaker borrowed $360 million from a $432.5 million credit facility for the recapitalization after rejecting a takeover bid from a closely held investment concern in December 1988. Earlier this week, Whittaker replaced the facility with a new $140 million facility from a group led by Security Pacific National Bank. Of the $140 million, Whittaker said it has borrowed $47 million.
