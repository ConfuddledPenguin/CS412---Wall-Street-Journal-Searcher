Ethics Package Is Adopted By Voters in Los Angeles

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/07/90

LOS ANGELES -- Voters here adopted an ethics package that requires increased financial disclosures by city officials, bans outside employment by officials, and requires partial public financing of campaigns. But it also gives substantial pay raises to several top officials. The ballot measure, affirmed by 57% of the voters, emerged as a result of controversy last year over Mayor Tom Bradley's outside financial dealings. At the time, Mr. Bradley appointed a commission to draft a new city code of ethics.

The ethics package adopted by voters was a combination of that commission's proposal and separate proposals made by City Council members. The proposals were the subject of heated debate, and council members tacked on numerous amendments, including the pay boosts. The measure increases pay of the 15 council members 40% to $86,157, and the mayor's pay 9.2% to $112,004.

Geoffrey Cowan, the public-interest attorney who headed the outside commission to draft the new code, called the ballot measure "the most comprehensive package of ethics and finance reform in the country."
