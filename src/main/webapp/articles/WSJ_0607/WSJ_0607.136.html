Business Brief -- Eagle-Picher Industries Inc.: Units to Be Sold to Fund Asbestos Liability Reserve

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/07/90

Eagle-Picher Industries Inc. said it intends to sell three businesses as part of a previously announced plan to fund asbestos litigation liability. The businesses to be divested -- its Trim, Orcomatic and Injection Molding divisions -- make a variety of molded rubber and trim parts for the automotive industry. They accounted for less than 5% of Eagle-Picher's $729.9 million in sales for the year ended Nov. 30, 1989, a company spokesman said.

Eagle-Picher, Cincinnati, is a defendant in about 60,000 separate lawsuits stemming from its manufacture, from 1934 until 1971, of an asbestos-containing insulating cement used principally in shipbuilding and refinery construction. The company said it has already paid nearly $500 million in asbestos claims, and expects to pay at least that amount to resolve current and future litigation.

At the end of its fiscal first quarter, ended Feb. 28, Eagle-Picher had an underfunded reserve of $462 million for such litigation. Proceeds from the sale of the three designated divisions will be used to fund that reserve.
