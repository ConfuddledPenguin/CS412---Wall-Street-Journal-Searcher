Credit Ratings: Federal Express Has Credit Rating Lowered By Moody's Investors

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 06/07/90

Federal Express Corp., the air-express carrier, was stung yesterday with lower ratings on about $1 billion of long-term debt. Moody's Investors Service Inc., which made the downgrades, cited the more difficult than expected merger with Tiger International Corp., and Moody's belief that cash flow measurements, among other things, will remained constrained "over the intermediate term."

Analysts said they weren't surprised by the downgrades, which largely reflect the company's well-publicized problems. Late last month, Federal reported a sharp and unexpected drop in April shipment growth, an announcement that sparked a 10% tumble in the company's stock price.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Federal's common closed at $48.25, down 25 cents.

The rating cut isn't "market moving" news, said Kevin Murphy, an airline analyst with Morgan Stanley & Co. "Federal is in the black and paying the bills, but there's no visibility to earnings momentum," he said. He called the rating analysis "rational and well done."

Moody's said it cut the ratings on Federal's senior notes and industrial revenue bonds to Baa-2 from Baa-1. It also lowered the rating on Tiger's convertible subordinated debentures to Baa-3 from Baa-2. The ratings for Flying Tiger Line Inc.'s industrial revenue bonds ratings were downgraded to Baa-1 from A3.

In Memphis, Tenn., Federal declined to comment.
