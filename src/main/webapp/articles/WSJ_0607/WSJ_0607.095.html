Valley National Corp. Hires First Boston To Sell 2 Bank Unit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/07/90

PHOENIX, Ariz. -- Valley National Corp. said it has hired CS First Boston Corp. to solicit offers for its Utah and California bank subsidiaries. Valley National Corp.'s main bank is Valley National Bank of Arizona with assets of $9.2 billion. Units to be sold are Valley Bank & Trust Co. of Utah, with assets of $944 million, and California Valley Bank, with assets of $111 million.

The company said that it decided to concentrate its assets in Arizona and that the sale of the units will strengthen its financial structure. The units will be sold at a price in excess of book value.

Valley National Corp., the holding company for Arizona's largest bank, has been undergoing difficult times because of the depressed Arizona real-estate market and nonperforming real-estate loans. In the first quarter, profit was $607,000, or three cents a share, compared with $18.9 million, or 96 cents a share, a year earlier. In 1989, Valley National had a loss of $149.2 million, or $7.55 a share, compared with profit of $67.3 million, or $3.41 a share, in 1988.

A large number of investors are evidently betting that the price of the company's shares will decline in the future. A spokesman said that 25% of the company's shares are held as shorts. In a short sale, an investor sells stock he doesn't yet own by borrowing it from a broker to deliver it to the purchaser. The investor hopes to make a profit by buying an equal number of shares later at a lower price to replace the borrowed stock.
