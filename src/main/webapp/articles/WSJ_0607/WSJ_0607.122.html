Business Brief -- TransTechnology Corp.: Company Expects to Report 4th-Quarter, Year-End Loss

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/07/90

TransTechnology Corp.'s chairman, Arch Scurlock, said that fourth-quarter and year-end results for this maker of aerospace-defense and industrial products would be worse than expected, and that the company would report a net loss in both periods. TransTechnology also said it would report a loss from continuing and discontinued operations.

The Los Angeles-based company didn't specify the size of the anticipated operating loss, which won't be reported for two weeks. Lee M. Parker, vice president, investor relations, characterized it as "significant" for both periods. He said that larger-than-anticipated inventory write-downs had produced the losses.

In addition, Mr. Parker said the company expects revenue for the year to drop 15.74%, to $198 million. He noted, however, that in 1989, the company sold two units which had contributed about $21 million to revenue. The remaining drop in revenue was due to the closing of a factory, among other things, he said.

For the year ended March 31, 1989, the company reported net income of $9.2 million, or $1.80 a share. The net income included a loss from discontinued operations of $1.2 million. Revenue for the year was $235.2 million.
