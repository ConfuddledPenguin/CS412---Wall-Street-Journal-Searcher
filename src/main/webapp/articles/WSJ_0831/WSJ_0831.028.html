Who's News: Comprehensive Care Names Former Official To Two Top Positions

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/31/90

ST. LOUIS -- Comprehensive Care Corp. said its newly elected board named James P. Carmany, 51 years old, as president and chief executive officer. Mr. Carmany, a former executive vice president who left the company last year in a dispute with management, also was named a director.

Comprehensive Care, a provider of chemical dependency programs, said its former president, W. James Nicol, who was removed as chief executive officer, will continue to serve as a board member. Norman L. Perry, president of Tree Products Enterprises, was named chairman of the company, succeeding Stanley Nelson, who was also removed from the post.

The new board was elected Aug. 20 after a prolonged fight between the former management and a dissident group of stockholders. All of the directors, except Mr. Nicol, are new to the board. There are now eight board members.

Comprehensive Care said the board also named Donald G. Simpson, a former officer of the company, to the new post of executive vice president and chief operating officer.
