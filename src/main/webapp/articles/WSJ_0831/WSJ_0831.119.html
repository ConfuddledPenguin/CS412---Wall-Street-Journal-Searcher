Business Brief -- Centel Corp.: Concern Is Planning to Sell Electric Operations by 1991

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/31/90

Centel Corp., a cellular-communications concern, said it intends to sell its electric operations by January to focus more attention on its local-exchange telephone and cellular businesses. Chicago-based Centel provides electric service to more than 136,000 customers in Colorado and Kansas. Centel Electric had revenue of $165 million in 1989, while reporting operating profit of $29 million.

But the company's telephone and cellular operations are its bread and butter. "This proposed sale continues our restructuring to focus on those operations that provide greater opportunity for growth and return for shareowners," said John P. Frazee Jr., Centel's chairman and chief executive officer.

The company recently agreed to sell its Centel Communications Systems unit, a voice and data systems operation, to a group headed by investor Edward H. Lavin Jr., and now is seeking to sell its electric unit as well.

In the past three years, Centel has acquired more than $1 billion in cellular properties while selling its cable television operations and Centel Federal systems, which sells data systems to federal agencies. It also has divested itself of its interest in two long-distance carriers.

Centel indicated it had received overtures from entities interested in buying Centel Electric, but wouldn't say who the potential buyers might be.
