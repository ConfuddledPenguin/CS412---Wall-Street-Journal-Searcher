Hedged Securities To File Chapter 11 Petition, Lawyer Says

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/31/90

ENGLEWOOD, Colo. -- Hedged Securities Associates Inc. and its founder, James D. Donahue, plan to file today for protection under the federal Bankruptcy Code, according to Robert Dill, an attorney who represents Mr. Donahue and the firm. Mr. Dill said Hedged Securities, a limited partnership, plans to file under Chapter 11, which gives businesses protection from creditors while formulating a reorganization plan. Mr. Donahue plans to file under Chapter 13, which gives similar protection to individuals, Mr. Dill said.

The planned filings mark the latest chapters in an investment scandal that began unfolding last week when Mr. Donahue told a meeting of 500 investors that he had lost more than $100 million of their money through stock-option trading. Mr. Donahue's message was made via videotape.

Weyerhaeuser Co., based in Tacoma, Wash., filed a lawsuit yesterday in U.S. District Court in Denver accusing Mr. Donahue of fraud, breach of contract and violation of certain federal retirement securities laws. Weyerhaeuser said its pension fund lost an estimated $35 million by investing in Hedged Securities.

In a lawsuit filed last week in a state district court here, Paradigm Partners Inc. of Chicago said a pension fund it controls lost more than $15 million through its Hedged Securities investments. In a similar suit, Houston-based Union Texas Petroleum Holding Inc. said its pension fund lost about $8 million.

On Wednesday, attorneys for Paradigm and Union Texas asked a state judge to appoint a receiver to take over the troubled firm. Hedged Securities staved off that action by agreeing not to oppose the appointment of a receiver if it fails to file for bankruptcy by early this afternoon.

Mr. Dill said the "sheer volume of names and contracts" prevented Hedged Securities from making a bankruptcy filing earlier in the week.
