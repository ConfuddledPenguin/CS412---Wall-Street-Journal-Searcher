Galactic Resources, Cornucopia Merger By Stock Swap Is Set

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/31/90

VANCOUVER, British Columbia -- Galactic Resources Ltd. and Cornucopia Resources Ltd. said they agreed to a previously proposed merger, through a stock swap with an indicated value of about US$29.3 million. Under the planned merger, which is expected to close by December, pending various conditions including shareholder approvals, Galactic will issue 0.55 of a new Galactic common share for each of Cornucopia's shares outstanding, the companies said. Galactic owns 16.3% of Cornucopia's 19.4 million shares.

In national over-the-counter trading yesterday, Cornucopia shares closed at US$1.4375, up 9.375 cents, and in American Stock Exchange trading, Galactic shares closed at US$2.75, down 12.5 cents.

Robert Friedland, Galactic's founder and vice chairman, said the combined company, which will be run by Galactic's current management, is expected to produce more than 200,000 ounces of gold next year from its mines in South Carolina, Colorado and Nevada, ranking it among North America's larger producers. Galactic expects to produce about 140,000 to 150,000 ounces of gold this year, he said.

Galactic initially proposed to merge with Cornucopia in May, by swapping half of a Galactic share for each share of Cornucopia. Galactic has about 45.7 million shares outstanding.

Separately, Galactic said a unit and its partner agreed to refinance Galactic's 48%-owned Ridgeway mine in South Carolina. Under the refinancing, Galactic said, the partners will repay a US$31.4 million gold loan, among other things, netting Galactic a profit of about US$3.3 million.
