Marketing & Media: News Corp., Whittle Pick Different Salon Strategies

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/31/90

NEW YORK -- Two media barons who had targeted the nation's beauty salons as avenues for introducing new media properties are headed in different directions. Rupert Murdoch's News Corp. said it has scrapped its heavily publicized plan to create a commercially sponsored video network for beauty salons. The plan, the Salon Network, was announced in February, but it was shelved because of weak response from key advertisers.

Whittle Communications L.P., however, said it will unveil next week a revamped magazine for the nation's beauty salons. Called American Style, the slick magazine takes the place of Southern Style, an oversized magazine that had been distributed free of charge in salons in southern states. Southern Style was discontinued last year after its sole sponsor, Procter & Gamble Co., pulled out. Procter & Gamble, however, is one of several advertisers in the new American Style.

Both projects are part of a wave of new media properties that attempt to go beyond TV, newspapers and magazines in targeting audiences in supermarkets, doctors' offices and at sporting events.

Copies of American Style will be sent next week to 140,000 beauty salons. Craige Johnson, American Style publisher, said 25% of women visiting the salons are expected to read a copy of the magazine, which will be published every two months.
