Letters to the Editor: Keep Congress Out of Court

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/31/90

The effort by some members of Congress to avoid jury duty ("Jurymandering" editorial, Aug. 14) points out the blindness of Congress as to what the American people think of them. What rational American would accept a member of Congress on his/her jury anyway? If the member were accepted, the defendant's day in court would be prolonged endlessly by jury subcommittees and the need for an environmental-impact statement. And if by chance two members of Congress of opposite parties were seated, the jury would be hung and the judge would have to declare a mistrial. No matter how you look at it, given the proven character and integrity of our 535 national legislators, justice would be delayed if not denied, and the taxpayers would pick up the tab -- again. Robert B. Meredith

Mill Valley, Calif.
