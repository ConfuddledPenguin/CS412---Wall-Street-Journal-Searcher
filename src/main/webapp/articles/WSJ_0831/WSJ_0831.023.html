Malaysian Exchange Ends Debacle of Detoured Shares

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/31/90

KUALA LUMPUR, Malaysia -- The case of the missing Malaysian shares has closed. Nik Mohamed Din Yusoff, executive chairman of the Kuala Lumpur Stock Exchange, said all investors with valid share-purchase agreements have been given their securities, concluding an embarrassing episode that dragged on for more than six months and tarnished the credibility of the Malaysian exchange soon after it severed its last links with the more developed Stock Exchange of Singapore.

At the height of the affair in mid-February, about 150 million shares, valued by securities analysts at more than 250 million Malaysian dollars (US$93 million), had been mislaid.

The delivery of share certificates to the wrong brokerage and incorrectly recorded trades left the 53 member brokerage firms and exchange officials unable to cope with soaring trading volume.

Last week, the final claims outstanding were filled by the exchange's clearinghouse, the Securities Clearing Automated Network System, known as Scans. The only party that stands to lose from the incident is Scans itself, Nik Din said.

The clearinghouse covered the claims by distributing shares it bought on the open market and by recovering from brokerage firms shares that had been delivered to them by mistake.

Nik Din declined to specify the amounts of shares bought by the clearinghouse or retrieved from brokers.
