Business Brief -- Henry Birks & Sons Ltd.: Talks on Selling 40 Stores In U.S. Held With Gordons

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/31/90

Henry Birks & Sons Ltd., Montreal, said it is holding talks on selling 40 jewelry stores in the U.S. to James C. and Daniel P. Gordon, veterans of the business. William Meder, chief operating officer, said closely held Birks set a Sept. 30 deadline to conclude a transaction with the Gordon brothers, who approached Birks. The brothers are part of the family group that controlled Houston-based Gordon Jewelry Corp., which Zale Corp. acquired last year for about $312 million. James Gordon said he and his brother "want to get back in the jewelry business.

Mr. Meder said the discussions are still at an early stage. He said the 40 stores up for sale operate under a variety of names in cities such as Chicago, Detroit and San Diego. Birks's 14 remaining U.S. stores, in San Francisco and Boston, aren't on the block. Birks owns 220 stores in Canada and the U.S. Mr. Meder said Birks had 1989 sales of 387 million Canadian dollars (US$339 million).
