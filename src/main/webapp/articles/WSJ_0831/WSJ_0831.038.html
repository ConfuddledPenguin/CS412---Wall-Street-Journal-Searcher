PACs' Contributions Rose During the Past 18 Months

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/31/90

WASHINGTON -- Contributions from political action committees to candidates running for federal office continued to rise during the past 18 months, with incumbents receiving far more than their challengers, according to a Federal Election Commission report. The report said that from Jan. 1, 1989, to June 30, 1990, PACs contributed almost $94 million to candidates running for federal office, up 6.1% from the 1988 period and up 32% from 1986. Overall, PACs contributed more than $101 million to federal officeholders and their challengers, but about $7 million of that was given to candidates raising money for future campaigns or retiring past debts.

As in past reports, the election commission said that incumbents fared better than their challengers, receiving $79 million compared with only $6.5 million.

Corporate PACs were the most generous, contributing $35 million to federal officeholders and their challengers. Trade group and health-related PACs were next, giving $26 million, followed by labor PACs, with $20 million.

Democrats enjoyed a greater share of PAC largess than did their Republican counterparts, receiving $59 million compared with $34 million.

The Realtors Political Action Committee led all PAC contributions, giving more than $1.5 million. American Telephone & Telegraph Co. led corporate PAC contributions, giving slightly more than $1.1 million, while the Teamsters committee led labor PACs, with contributions of more than $1.3 million.
