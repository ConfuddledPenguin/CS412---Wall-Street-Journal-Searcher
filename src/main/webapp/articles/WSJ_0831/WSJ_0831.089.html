Best Buy Sues Unit Of Onkyo, Charging Price-Fixing Scheme

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/31/90

MINNEAPOLIS -- Electronics and appliance retailer Best Buy Co. sued Onkyo U.S.A. Corp., alleging that the maker of audio parts refused to supply its products to Best Buy because the retailer wouldn't go along with what it said were Onkyo's "price-fixing schemes." In the complaint, Best Buy alleges that Onkyo set prices on its products at artificially high levels and penalized Best Buy for refusing to go along with the prices by taking away products that provide millions of dollars in Best Buy sales. The suit charges Onkyo with antitrust violations and breach of contract, among other things.

In Ramsey, N.J., an official of Onkyo U.S.A., a division of Japan's Onkyo Corp., said: "There is no merit to those charges, and we'll defend ourselves vigorously. Onkyo dealers are free to set their own prices."
