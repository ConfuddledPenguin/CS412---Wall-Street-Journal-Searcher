REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: Snow Job

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/31/90

It cannot be doubted that the political culture "inside the Beltway" has contributed mightily to this country's outlandishly large and "uncontrollable" budget. But Washington wouldn't be Washington without folks outside the Beltway panting after it for handouts. Example: The Ketchum Area Rapid Transit district carts passengers around the Sun Valley resort area in Idaho for free. Even then, the buses are rarely crowded. Vacationers use them, but the local service workers "don't seem to want to," one board member noted. Nevertheless, KART may expand to provide yearround "rush hour" commuter service for nearby towns in the Wood River Valley, which is growing rapidly with expensive homes. Break-even cost: $2.91 per rider if the buses were full. How to pay for this unwanted luxury? Of course] Board member Guy Coles wants to seek federal matching funds. We'll be watching how Idaho's conservatives in Congress handle this one.
