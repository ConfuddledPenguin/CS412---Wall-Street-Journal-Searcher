Some Sutton Holders Seek to Buy DeSoto's Detergents Business

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/31/90

NEW YORK -- Sutton Holding Corp. said certain of its shareholders are interested in acquiring DeSoto Inc.'s detergents business by acquiring all of the remaining equity in the company. In a filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission, Sutton said the proposed acquisition would occur following consummation of DeSoto's pending sale transactions.

DeSoto is in the midst of completing a number of agreements that will represent a liquidation of nearly all the company's assets with the exception of its detergents business. New York-based Sutton Holdings earlier sought to acquire all of DeSoto, but terminated its $50-per-share bid last month.

In the SEC filing, Sutton said the proposed acquisition would be subject to various conditions, including financing and completion of a satisfactory legal and business review of the company.

A spokesman for DeSoto, a Des Plaines, Ill., maker of paints, chemicals and detergents, hadn't any comment on the filing.

Separately, DeSoto said the Federal Trade Commission has requested additional information on the planned sale of its surfactants and emulsifier business to Witco Corp., New York. In addition to that planned sale, DeSoto also agreed to sell its industrial coatings business to Courtlands PLC of London.

The company said it plans to comply with the FTC request as "promptly as possible."
