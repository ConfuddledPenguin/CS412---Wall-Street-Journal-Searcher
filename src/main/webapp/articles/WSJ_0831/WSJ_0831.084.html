Pearson Posts Rise In Pretax Earnings Of 15% for 1st Half

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 08/31/90

LONDON -- Pearson PLC, the widely diversified U.K. publishing group, said its pretax profit for the first half of the year rose 15%, with oil services and investment banking activities contributing the strongest earnings growth. Pretax profit rose to #97.9 million ($190.4 million) from #85.4 million. The result matched the upper end of market expectations.

Profit after taxes and minorities totaled #65.9 million, up 20% from #54.9 million. Per-share earnings rose 16% to 24.8 pence from 21.4 pence.

Net income attributable to shareholders fell by more than half to #64.9 million after an extraordinary loss of #1 million. Net income attributable totaled #159 million a year earlier, helped by a #104 million extraordinary gain on asset disposals.

Pearson said it would pay an interim dividend of 10.75 pence a share, up 19% from nine pence for the year-earlier period.

Pearson Chairman Lord Blakenham cautioned against using the first-half earnings boost as a guide for the company's full year.

Together with the traditional seasonal bias toward heavier earnings in the second half, the chairman noted that "there are obvious uncertainties facing the major world economies during the remainder of 1990."

Overall sales totaled #711 million, up 11% from #641 million a year earlier.

Pearson's Information and Entertainment division, which includes the Financial Times newspaper and its 23% interest in Dutch publisher Elsevier, showed a 13% rise in trading profit to #57.7 million from #51.1 million. Sales rose a similar 12% to #478 million from #427 million.
