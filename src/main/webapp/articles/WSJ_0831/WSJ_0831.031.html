United Parcel Service Is Selected to Serve Cargo Route in Japan

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/31/90

WASHINGTON -- The Transportation Department selected United Parcel Service Co. to serve a new all-cargo air route to Japan. UPS plans to operate six flights a week using Boeing 747 aircraft between Louisville, Ky.; Anchorage, Alaska; and Tokyo, according to the department.

Emery Worldwide Airlines, a unit of Consolidated Freightways Inc., was the only other carrier that applied for the route authority. It was awarded backup authority. The department found that UPS's proposed service would deliver more capacity than Emery's and faster service to shippers in the market.

The cargo route was authorized in the November 1989 agreement between Japan and the U.S. that called for a substantial expansion of air service between the countries.
