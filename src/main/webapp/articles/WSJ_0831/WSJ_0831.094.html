TCBY Shareholder Files Suit Charging Firm Hid Problems

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/31/90

LITTLE ROCK, Ark. -- A shareholder of TCBY Enterprises Inc. filed a class-action lawsuit late Wednesday in federal court here charging that the company defrauded investors by concealing problems and misrepresenting its financial condition. The plaintiff, Kenneth Irvine, says in the lawsuit that he bought 500 shares of TCBY stock on Jan. 8 for about $20 a share. Subsequently, Mr. Irvine contends, the company repeatedly hid problems and misrepresented its outlook, until admitting in a news release this week that earnings had fallen.

As previously reported, TCBY said Monday that its earnings for the third quarter ending Aug. 31 would drop to between 25 cents and 30 cents a share, from 43 cents a share in the year-earlier period. Since the announcement, the stock -- which has been dropping for months -- has fallen to below $8 a share.

Mr. Irvine seeks unspecified damages on behalf of everyone who bought TCBY stock between Oct. 16 of last year and Aug. 27, 1990.

Mimi Hurst, a TCBY spokeswoman said, "Our attorneys have examined the litigation, and they and we have concluded that the allegations are without merit. We intend to vigorously defend ourselves." TCBY, based here, is a large franchiser of retail shops that sell frozen yogurt.
