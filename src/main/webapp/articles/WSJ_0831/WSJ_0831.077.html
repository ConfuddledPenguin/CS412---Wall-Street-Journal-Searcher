BMW Sees Output Of Autos This Year Rising to a Record

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/31/90

FRANKFURT, West Germany -- Bayerische Motoren Werke AG said it expects record auto output for this year. The Munich-based maker of luxury, high-performance autos and motorcycles said its production of 328,000 units in the first seven months represented a 3% increase from a year earlier.

For all of 1990, BMW said it expects to produce about 520,000 cars, up from last year's record total of 511,476 units, and up from its early-August projection of more than 500,000 units.

"Our {productive} capacity is fully taxed," Robert Buechelhofer, a member of BMW's management board, said, adding that "BMW is again facing a production record."

Mr. Buechelhofer said customer demand for BMW's top-line models has been buoyant this year, especially for the new BMW Coupe and the prestigious 7-series. To meet the firm demand for these models, BMW operated extra shifts at its production lines during the usual summer-holiday factory break, the executive disclosed. He said the company was able to assemble an additional 1,160 7-series autos and 500 Coupes by keeping production lines operating during the vacation break. Moreover, BMW was able to shorten some of the continuing delivery lag times for these models, Mr. Buechelhofer said.

Neither the company nor the executive disclosed any new forecasts for BMW's profit and sales for 1990. In early August, when BMW reported first-half profit rose 8.3% to 352 million marks ($225.7 million), on a sales rise of 2.2% to 13.84 billion marks, the company said only that it expected "satisfactory" sales and earnings for the full year. In 1989, BMW earned 558.1 million marks on sales of 26.52 billion marks.

Meanwhile, Mr. Buechelhofer noted that BMW also has been sustaining "pleasing" sales growth in its motorcycle division. Output and deliveries in the first seven months rose 25% to 19,700 units, compared with year-earlier levels, he disclosed.

By the end of the year, the company expects to build more than 31,000 motorcycles, up 20% from 25,761 units last year, he said.

With the domestic economy booming, BMW and other West German car makers have been able to defy industry analysts' expectations this year and post strong production and order gains, compared with those a year earlier. A soft car market previously was forecast for Europe as a whole. Other West Germany auto makers also have instituted extra shifts this year to help reduce growing order backlogs.
