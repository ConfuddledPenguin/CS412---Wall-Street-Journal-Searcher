Marketing & Media: Disney Alleges Ex-Film Executive Breached Contract

  • Author: Richard Turner
  • Date: 08/31/90

LOS ANGELES -- Walt Disney Co. filed suit against a former top movie executive, charging that when he left Disney for Paramount Pictures last month, he took another executive and a film project with him, in violation of a contract. The executive, David Kirkpatrick, was president of production at Walt Disney Pictures and Touchstone Pictures for 16 months before quitting to become executive vice president of Paramount's motion picture group, a unit of Paramount Communications Inc.

Mr. Kirkpatrick declined to comment, but his attorney, Howard Weitzman, called the lawsuit "a complete overreaction," and said of Disney, "Mouses that live in glass houses shouldn't call other persons louses." This is a reference to the fact that Disney Chairman Michael Eisner, along with movie chiefs Jeffrey Katzenberg and Richard Frank and several other key executives, all used to work at Paramount. Disney wouldn't comment.

Mr. Kirkpatrick himself had served at Paramount with members of the Disney team for eight years. After a two-year stint at closely held Weintraub Entertainment Group, he joined Disney, where he was the No. 2 movie executive at Touchstone and Disney, working with David Hoberman and reporting to Messrs. Katzenberg and Frank.

The suit alleges that when Mr. Kirkpatrick asked to be released from his five-year employment contract, he signed an agreement prohibiting him from hiring away any Disney employees for two years or disclosing any confidential information about the company. However, the suit says, he offered a job to Disney film executive Donald Granger, a former Weintraub employee, and Mr. Granger recently joined Paramount as a vice president of production. The suit also says Mr. Kirkpatrick interfered with a negotiation over a prospective film by producers Ed Kaplan and Steve Tisch, "The Dead Letter Department."

Although Mr. Hoberman described Mr. Kirkpatrick's departure from Disney as "amicable" at the time, the lawsuit says that Mr. Katzenberg "remonstrated" with Mr. Kirkpatrick after he left about alleged contract violations but that Mr. Kirkpatrick was "unsympathetic, suggesting that he could exploit loopholes in Disney's contractual schemes of protection."

Mr. Weitzman said, "I don't know what Disney's agenda is; the only thing I can think of is that there's a lot of hostility between them and David."

The complaint, filed in Los Angeles Superior Court, alleges fraud and breach of contract. It seeks an injunction against further alleged contract violations and unspecified damages.
