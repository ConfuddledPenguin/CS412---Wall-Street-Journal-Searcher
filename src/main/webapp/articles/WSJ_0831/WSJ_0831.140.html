Canon Earnings Rose 55% in Half On Brisk Demand

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 08/31/90

TOKYO -- Canon Inc., a major Japanese maker of cameras and business machines, said that its consolidated pretax earnings in the first half of the year surged 55% to 64.17 billion yen ($446.8 million) from 41.29 billion yen a year earlier, because of brisk demand. Net income in the six months ended June 30 jumped 55% to 25.61 billion yen, or 32.55 yen a share, from 16.54 billion yen, or 22.17 yen a share.

Sales amounted to 832.82 billion yen, showing a 33% gain from 627.7 billion yen.

Encouraged by the brisk results, Canon said it has revised its 1990 earnings projections upward. It now sees pretax earnings at about 140 billon yen, sharply higher than an initial estimate of 105 billion yen made in February. Net is estimated at 55 billion yen, up from an original 43 billion yen projection. Canon expects 1990 sales to total some 1.7 trillion yen, rising from 1.5 trillion yen.

Canon officials said the company has decided to revise the projections in light of stronger-than-expected demand.

In the first half, they said, demand was brisk for such products as copiers, laser beam printers and other computer peripherals, as well as facsimile machines.

Sales in the business machines division climbed 31% to 665.58 billion yen, accounting for as much as 80% of the company's entire business.

Camera sales came to 114.43 billion yen, up 52% from a year earlier. Single-lens reflex cameras, as well as 8mm video equipment, helped sustain sales, Canon officials said.

Sales of optical machines and other related products rose 18% to 52.81 billion yen. Equipment for the production of semiconductors was among the products that officials said contributed to the healthy gain.

During the six months, Canon did 71% of its business overseas, with sales totaling 591.38 billion yen. They represented a 38% year-to-year surge.

Domestic sales climbed 21% to 241.45 billion yen.
