Business Brief -- Fibreboard Corp.: Texas Court Rules Concern Can Appeal Asbestos Claims

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/31/90

Fibreboard Corp. said that a Texas court has issued a ruling that will allow the company, a maker of various building materials, to pursue an appeal of claims against it in 175 asbestos-related lawsuits. Under the ruling, Concord, Calif.-based Fibreboard estimates it will have to post a bond of $3 million to mount its appeal to the U.S. Fifth Circuit Court of Appeals. Prior to the ruling, the company feared the lower court would ask for "an excessive amount" of money to be posted as bond, making it impossible for Fibreboard to pursue an appeal, a spokesman said.

The ruling was issued by Judge Robert Parker of the U.S. District Court, East District of Texas in Beaumont. Fibreboard said it thinks it will be several months before it appeals the judgments in the asbestos-related lawsuits, since many of the cases are still pending.
