Technology: IBM to Raise Prices By 4% on Wide Range Of Its Product Line

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/31/90

ARMONK, N.Y. -- International Business Machines Corp. said it will raise prices 4% on a wide range of computer hardware and software products, effective Jan. 1. IBM said the price increases are the result of a regular review, and won't apply to some of the company's big revenue producers, such as the 3090 mainframe computer family and the system unit of the PS/2 personal computer. But the new RS/6000 work station is affected, as are the hot-selling 3390 mainframe disk drives and most models of the AS/400 minicomputer.

Analysts expect IBM to announce a successor to the 3090 on Sept. 5, and to offer big discounts on mainframes until the new machine can be delivered in late 1991. The PS/2 has been aggressively priced for some time, as IBM fights to protect its market share.

Also excluded from the price increases are some maintenance, service and network-use charges; customer education; operating system software for personal computers; memory for the RS/6000 and 3990 computers; the LaserPrinter and Proprinter; and any product or new price announced since June 1.
