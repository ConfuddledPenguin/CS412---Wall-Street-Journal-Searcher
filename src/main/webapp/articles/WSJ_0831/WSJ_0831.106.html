Business Brief -- Atalanta/Sosnoff Capital Corp.: Board Authorizes Buy-Back Of About 200,000 Shares

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/31/90

Atalanta/Sosnoff Capital Corp. said its board has authorized a buy-back of as many as 200,000 shares of its common stock in open-market transactions. Atalanta, New York, said the timing of the purchases will depend on market conditions. The asset management firm, once a highflier on Wall Street, currently has about 10 million shares outstanding. A spokesman said the board saw this as a good time to get the stock at a price below book value.

The company's earnings and assets have all fallen during the first six months of 1990. Net income dropped 38% to $1.8 million, or 21 cents a share, for the first half from against $2.9 million, or 35 cents a share, a year earlier. Assets under Atalanta/Sosnoff's management have also dipped to $1.2 billion at June 30, compared with $1.7 billion last year.

In New York Stock Exchange trading yesterday, Atalanta/Sosnoff closed at $2.875, up 37.5 cents a share.
