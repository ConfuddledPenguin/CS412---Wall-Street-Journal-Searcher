International Brief -- British Coal Corp.: Fiscal-Year Operating Profit Fell 73% to $258.7 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/31/90

British Coal Corp., the state-owned national coal mining concern, reported that operating profit for the year ended March 31 plunged 73%. It cited geological problems, tough competition and a third consecutive mild British winter. Operating profit, or profit before interest charges and exceptional items, fell to #133 million ($258.7 million) from #498 million a year earlier. The overall loss, after special items, plummeted to #5.08 billion from #203 million, as the British government moved to write off British Coal's past losses as part of changes in the company's financial structure.

British Coal had #4.64 billion of special items, including #4.16 billion for the asset write-offs and provisions against pensioners' concessionary coal charges. Year-earlier results included a #269 million charge related to the cost of restructuring. Interest charges jumped 33% to #574 million from #432 million.
