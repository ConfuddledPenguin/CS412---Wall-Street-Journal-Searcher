Bank of New England Isn't on Brink, U.S. Insists

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 08/31/90

William Seidman, chairman of the Federal Deposit Insurance Corp., said there wasn't any truth to rumors that Bank of New England Corp.'s major bank was facing seizure by federal regulators. "That's just not true; it's not about to be closed," he said.

A torrent of hearsay has circulated recently that the bank would be seized before Labor Day weekend. The talk reached a fever pitch yesterday when Ted DiMauro, a candidate for Congress in Massachusetts' second district, issued a news release saying -- erroneously -- that the FDIC had liquidated the bank.

Representatives of the campaign did not return telephone calls.

After reporting a $1.1 billion loss last year, the bank is struggling to return to profitability. The bank continues to be hurt by the slumping New England economy and a large portfolio of nonperforming loans, especially in real estate.

The FDIC's Mr. Seidman said he is scheduled to meet Tuesday with Lawrence Fish, Bank of New England's chairman, "to talk about their plans for restructuring the bank."

The comptroller of the currency, as the bank's primary regulator, normally would be the main government contact with Bank of New England. But Mr. Seidman said he is meeting with Mr. Fish because the bank has requested that the FDIC waive some of its rules to facilitate the restructuring.

"This is ridiculous," said Lee Cross of the comptroller's office. "First off, the FDIC doesn't even recommend the closing of a bank. The Office of the Comptroller does. And we haven't. This is a categorical denial. We have not recommended the bank be closed."
