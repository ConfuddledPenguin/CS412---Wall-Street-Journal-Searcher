Business Brief -- Alco Standard Corp.: Company Says It Will Sell Food Systems Group Units

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/31/90

Alco Standard Corp., Valley Forge, Pa., said it decided to sell the companies in its Alco Food Systems group because they appear to lack "the inherent capacity" to achieve long-term growth goals. The distribution and services concern said that no sales transactions are pending and that it doesn't know how much the units might bring.

The company didn't indicate how much the units contribute to annual sales and profit, but it described them as "collectively profitable."

Separately, Alco Standard said it expects to establish a reserve of about $8 million, or 13 cents a share, in its fourth quarter, related to a judgment entered recently in litigation. It didn't elaborate. The company expressed confidence in having "excellent grounds to prevail on appeal," but said it believes the reserve is an appropriate precaution.

For the fourth quarter ended Sept. 30, 1989, the company reported net income of $33.8 million, or 81 cents a share, on revenue of $1.08 billion.
