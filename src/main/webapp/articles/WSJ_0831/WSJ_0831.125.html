Brazil Says It Won't Pay Overdue Interest This Year

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 08/31/90

BRASILIA (AP) -- Brazil's central bank president said the country won't make any payments this year on overdue interest owed on its $114 billion foreign debt, the largest in the developing world. "The only gesture we are disposed to make is to negotiate realistically with our creditors," Ibrahim Eris said in an interview with foreign reporters.

Brazil, which some analysts say has had an undeclared moratorium on debt payments for more than a year, is late on payments of $6 billion to private banks and $2 billion to the so-called Paris Club, the 10 industrialized countries with the greatest influence in the International Monetary Fund.

It wasn't clear whether Brazil's move might be matched by other countries in Latin America, which has a combined foreign debt of $434.6 billion, according to a study by Latin American Economic System, Caracas, Venezuela.

Latin American economic ministers are to meet in Caracas next week, and the worsening debt problem will be among the issues discussed. They have complained that debt-relief programs such as the one created by U.S. Treasury Secretary Nicholas Brady are insufficient.

Other countries overdue on interest payments include Argentina, Ecuador, Peru, Panama, Costa Rica, the Dominican Republic, Bolivia and Paraguay.
