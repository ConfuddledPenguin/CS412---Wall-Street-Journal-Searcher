Business Brief -- Texas Industries Inc.: Stock and $50 Million Paid To Complete 1985 Purchase

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/31/90

Texas Industries Inc., Dallas, completing payment of an acquisition made in 1985, said it paid $50 million and 1.2 million of its common shares, or 11% of its stock outstanding, to Co-Steel Inc. of Toronto. In New York Stock Exchange composite trading, Texas Industries closed unchanged at $19, giving the stock a value of $22.8 million.

The cash and stock represent the second half of a two-part payment agreement between Texas Industries and Co-Steel. In 1985, Co-Steel sold its 50% stake in Chaparral Steel to Texas Industries for $42 million plus a payment to be based on Chaparral's performance in fiscal 1986 through 1990.

The agreement called for the stock part of the payment to be placed in a five-year trust starting in 1990. During the five years, Texas Industries will retain voting rights, as well as first-refusal rights to purchase the shares if Co-Steel decides to sell them. The shares increase Co-Steel's Texas Industries stake to more than 20%.
