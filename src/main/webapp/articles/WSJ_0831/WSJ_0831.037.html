California's Butte County Is on Verge of Bankruptcy

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 08/31/90

A rural county in northern California is threatening to seek protection from creditors under federal bankruptcy law. Should it seek bankruptcy protection, Butte County would be the first county in U.S. history to do so, says the National Association of Counties in Washington.

Butte County is among roughly two dozen rural counties in California, and scores more across the nation, that face fiscal crises. In at least one state, distressed counties have forced a measure onto the ballot calling for special aid for counties and ending a practice whereby state governments mandate services that counties must pay for.

Butte suffers from this "mandated" problem as well, but it has been pushed to the brink of bankruptcy by a recent decision by California's governor to veto a $20 million bailout of Butte and other distressed counties.

Will Randolph, Butte's chief administrator, said the county's board of supervisors will make a final decision about filing bankruptcy today. Absent a promise of relief from the state, Mr. Randolph said, "the most likely scenario is that we'll instruct our attorneys to file {for bankruptcy} after Labor Day."

Butte County has a population of roughly 150,000. Though it includes the thriving city of Chico, home to a state university, the unincorporated areas of the county are generally poor. Mr. Randolph said that years of budget-cutting in the 1980s have taken their toll.
