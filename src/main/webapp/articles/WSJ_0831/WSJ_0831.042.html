Volkswagen Reports Group Profit, Sales Rose 6.2% in 1st Half

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 08/31/90

WOLFSBURG, West Germany -- Volkswagen AG said group profit rose 6.2% in the first half of 1990 to 428 million marks ($274.4 million) from 403 million marks a year earlier. Sales at the West German auto maker also rose 6.2% to 35.24 billion marks from 33.18 billion marks.

Volkswagen said profitability was hurt by increasingly strong competition, economic difficulties in Brazil and Argentina, and disadvantageous foreign exchange developments. The company cited good domestic and foreign car demand as a factor in unit sales, which rose 2.8% to 1,544,791 from 1,502,485 units a year earlier. But sales slipped 2.1% within West Germany to 442,585 units from 452,027 units, while foreign sales rose 4.9% to 1,102,206 units from 1,050,458 units a year earlier.

The company said it expects the good economic conditions that have characterized the West German market so far to continue in the second half. It also cited expectations of higher demand from the economic union formed between the two Germanys July 1.

Sales of Volkswagen cars in Western Europe rose 1.7% to 1,179,011 units. The company said high demand for its Passat, Golf and Jetta models couldn't be fully satisfied because of capacity restraints. VW plans special weekend shifts in September to help satisfy demands for its cars.

The company cited hardening competition, overcapacity and price wars as negative factors in the U.S. market, but said it succeeded in boosting deliveries 0.9% to 101,458 cars. The introduction of the Passat model had a positive impact, Volkswagen said.

Volkswagen boosted car output to 1,576,083 units in the first half from 1,519,689 the year before. The company said Wednesday it is almost certain it will have record output of more than three million units for all of 1990.
