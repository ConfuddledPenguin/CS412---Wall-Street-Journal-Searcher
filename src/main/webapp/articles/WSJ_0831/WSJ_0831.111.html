International Brief -- Esselte AB: Swedish Firm's Pretax Profit Plunged 70% in First Half

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/31/90

After reporting a 70% plunge in first-half pretax profit, Sweden's Esselte AB slashed its full-year profit forecast by 50% and said it will intensify a world-wide cost-reduction drive including hefty job cuts at all major divisions. Esselte, the world's biggest office-supplies group, blamed the need for retrenchment on a surprisingly rapid downturn in key markets that accelerated during the second quarter.

The company's chief executive officer, Hans Larsson, declined to estimate how many jobs would be lost. He said details would be disclosed within a month.

Esselte said first-half profit before tax plunged to 97 million Swedish kronor ($16.9 million) from 327 million kronor a year earlier. Operating profit skidded 26% to 445 million kronor from 603 million kronor the previous year, with divisions for office products and information systems reporting the steepest declines.

Esselte's financial costs during the period ballooned 49% to 295 million kronor -- mainly the result of heavy borrowing to finance several acquisitions last year. First-half sales increased 7.7% to 8.56 billion kronor from 7.95 billion kronor a year earlier, the company said.
