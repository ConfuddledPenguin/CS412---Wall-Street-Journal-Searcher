Business Brief -- Circus Circus Enterprises Inc.: Second-Period Net Fell 25% On $11.2 Million Write-Off

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/31/90

Circus Circus Enterprises Inc., Las Vegas, Nev., reported net income of $16.3 million, or 59 cents a share, or a 25.2% drop, for its second quarter, ended July 31, compared with net of $21.8 million, or 76 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue rose 26% to $176.4 million from $139.7 million. The results included an $11.2 million write-off of pre-opening expenses for the company's Excalibur casino resort in Las Vegas, which opened June 19. Excluding the write-off, earnings would have amounted to 86 cents a share, which was within analysts' expectations.

In its first 43 days of operation, the 4,032-room Excalibur resort generated about $11 million in operating income and had an operating profit margin of 25%, the company said, and it accounted for nearly all the company's growth in revenue and operating income. Circus Circus Las Vegas, the company's other major Las Vegas Strip property, posted a decline of more than 15% in operating income. The company cited a drop in walk-in traffic caused by the Excalibur opening and the presence of Golden Nugget Inc.'s new Mirage resort. July also was a soft month for casinos, as the July 4 holiday came in midweek.

For the six months, Circus posted net of $35.6 million, or $1.29 a share, down 13% from $40.8 million, or $1.40 a share, a year earlier. Revenue rose 16% to $309.7 million from $267.2 million.
