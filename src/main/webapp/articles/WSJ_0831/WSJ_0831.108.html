Business Brief -- Intermark Inc.: Merger With Triton Group Receives Holder Approval

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/31/90

Shareholders of Intermark Inc. and its 51.6%-owned Triton Group Ltd. approved their merger by a wide margin at separate annual meetings yesterday. Intermark, based in La Jolla, Calif., said 91% of Intermark shares represented in the voting were cast in favor of the merger valued at about $76.2 million. The merger also received 91% of votes cast by Triton's non-Intermark holders.

As a result of the combination, effective today, Triton holders are to receive 1.4 shares of Intermark common stock for each share of Triton common. Intermark, a San Diego-based holding company, is the surviving entity. It will have interests in 12 operating companies in retailing, restaurants, real estate and other industries.

Intermark's stock closed at $5 a share, down 50 cents a share, in American Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday. Triton's stock, in its last day of trading on the New York Stock Exchange, closed at $6.625, down 37.5 cents a share.

About 225 shareholders attended one or both annual meetings, which were relatively quiet. A small group of union employees that had previously sought to halt the merger didn't disrupt the sessions.

The group of United Food & Commercials Workers withdrew its motion for a preliminary injunction in Delaware Chancery Court earlier this week without giving a reason.
