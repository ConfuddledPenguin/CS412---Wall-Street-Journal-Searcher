Marketing Brief -- John Wiley & Sons Inc.: Profit Rose 17% in Quarter; Technical-Sector Sales Cited

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/31/90

Publisher John Wiley & Sons Inc. reported net income for the first quarter ended July 31 grew 17%, helped particularly by the scientific, technical and medical publishing sector. Fiscal first-quarter net climbed to $2.1 million, or 48 cents a share, from $1.8 million, or 41 cents a share, in the year-earlier period. Revenue rose 2.7% to $75.4 million from $73.4 million.

Revenue and profit at Wiley's professional and trade publishing businesses also improved, the company said, but results declined in educational publishing, which a spokesman called a cyclical and very competitive business.

"We're putting some money and focus" into the educational business, the spokesman said. Wiley doesn't see immediate gains, but it expects the unit to turn up, perhaps over the next couple of years, he added.

During the second quarter of fiscal 1990, Wiley, based in New York, sold its occupational and nursing publishing lines. The company narrowed the loss at its training division.

In national over-the-counter trading yesterday, Wiley's shares closed unchanged at $35.
