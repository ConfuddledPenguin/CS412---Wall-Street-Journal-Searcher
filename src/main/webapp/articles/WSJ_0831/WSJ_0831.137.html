Comshare Is Looking Beyond North America For More Acquisitions

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/31/90

ANN ARBOR, Mich. -- Comshare Inc. President Richard L. Crandall said the computer software company "expects to make more acquisitions" in its current fiscal year and is "looking beyond North America" to continue expanding its product lines. "In addition to internal growth, we intend to do some acquisitions, although they are likely to focus along product lines that relate to products we already have," Mr. Crandall said. "However, it's likely that acquisitions we would make outside the U.S. will be in territories where we presently have a direct sales force."

Comshare has operations in the United Kingdom, Germany, France and Australia, he said. Mr. Crandall said the company also will look at developing more "strategic alliances" with other companies.

Comshare declined to forecast earnings for its first quarter, ending Sept. 30. Jackson Spears, an analyst at Chicago Corp., estimates first-quarter earnings at 22 cents a share and earnings for the year at $1.65 a share.

In the year-earlier first quarter, Comshare reported net income of $1.12 million, or 20 cents a share, on revenue of $22.1 million.

For the year ended June 30, Comshare reported net of $6.9 million, or $1.25 a share, on revenue of $104 million. The fiscal 1990 results were adjusted to reflect a 2-for-1 stock split in May.

About 50% of Comshare's revenue is generated from markets outside North America, the company said. Its primary business is developing and marketing executive information systems and financial consolidation systems for business managers.

T. Wallace Wrathall, Comshare's chief financial officer, said the company has increased its sales force by 20% in the past five months in preparation for a product introduction next month and as part of a sales expansion in Europe and Australia.
