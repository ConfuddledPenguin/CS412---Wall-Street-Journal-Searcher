Business Brief -- Goodyear Tire & Rubber Co.: Ohio Firm to Lay Off 800 As Part of Output Cutback

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/31/90

Goodyear Tire & Rubber Co. said it will lay off 800 workers as part of a production cutback at three tire plants. Goodyear, based in Akron, Ohio, blamed "continued sluggishness" in vehicle production and a corporate effort to reduce inventories for the cut in output.

Goodyear said it will lay off 270 employees at the Union City, Tenn., tire plant and 530 at the Valleyfield, Quebec, facility. The company said the duration of the layoffs will depend on tire demand.

In addition, the Danville, Va., radial truck tire plant will reduce production for one week, Goodyear said.

Goodyear previously announced the layoff of 360 employees at the Valleyfield plant.

The company had a second-quarter loss of $9.4 million, partly because of lower auto and truck output in North America that led to a buildup of tire inventories and weak replacement tire prices. Also, the company launched a restructuring of North American tire sales and marketing that will cut employment by 1,100 and led to a $21.4 million restructuring charge.
