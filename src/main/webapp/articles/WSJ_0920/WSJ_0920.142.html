Technology Brief -- Apple Computer Inc.: Upgraded Operating System For Macintosh Is Delayed

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/20/90

Apple Computer Inc., struggling to complete a new operating system for its flagship Macintosh family, said it won't complete the job until the first half of next year. Apple, whose stock fell 87.5 cents to $32.50 in over-the-counter trading yesterday, has billed the operating system, a software program that controls the basic functions of the Macintosh, as the biggest advance in software for the computer since its introduction in 1984. Apple had hoped to ship the operating system to customers this summer, but in May pushed the date back to October.

This delay shouldn't harm Apple since the operating system is mainly aimed at Apple's high-end computers, said Bruce Lupatkin, an analyst at Hambrecht & Quist. In mid-October, the company is expected to introduce two low-end computers, which can't make much use of the new operating system anyway, he said.

Delays in the release of the operating system are worrisome, however, since Apple is under pressure from Microsoft Corp., which supplies operating systems to Apple's competitors in the personal computer business, to make further improvements to Macintosh software. Apple said it expects to release test copies of the operating system to software developers in October.
