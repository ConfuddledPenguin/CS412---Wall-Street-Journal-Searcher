CIBC Plans to Sell A British Subsidiary, Retains S.G. Warburg

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/20/90

TORONTO -- The Canadian Imperial Bank of Commerce said it plans to sell its British mortgage financing unit, CIBC Mortgages. The Toronto-based bank said it has retained investment firm S.G. Warburg to advise it on the sale. It also said it has received expressions of interest from several financial institutions in its unit's $3.83 billion mortgage loan portfolio.

A spokesman for the bank declined to identify those interested parties or to say what price the bank hopes to get for the mortgage lending concern.

But he said CIBC will sell the profitable unit, one of the six largest mortgage lenders in Britain, only for "the right price."

The bank decided to sell the unit "for strategic reasons," the spokesman said. CIBC has begun to move out of retail banking operations abroad, focusing instead on wholesale operations such as its corporate finance and project financing services.

"We've built this up over the last four to five years, made it profitable and decided this would be a good time to sell," the spokesman said.

Analysts said CIBC is following in the footsteps of other Canadian banks in backing away from its overseas ventures.

"There's been a retrenchment in recent months as the banks realized things weren't working out in those markets the way they'd expected them to," said Kersi Doodha, an analyst with Maison Placements Canada Inc.

Mr. Doodha said CIBC is wise to sell the mortgage lending unit while it is still profitable, pointing to rising interest and inflation rates in Britain.

He said the sale of the unit would also help the bank meet its target of reducing expenses by $130 million over the next three years.
