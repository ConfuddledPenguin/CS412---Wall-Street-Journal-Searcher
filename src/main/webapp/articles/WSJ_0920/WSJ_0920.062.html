Tax Man's Demands May Cause Demise Of Mustang Ranch --- IRS Claims That the Brothel Owes Up to $13 Million; Trustee Has Tough Time

  • Author: Richard B. Schmitt
  • Date: 09/20/90

RENO, Nev. -- The Mustang Ranch, perhaps the nation's best-known legal brothel, may be on its last legs. On Tuesday, the brothel, which has been trying since 1983 to reorganize itself under federal bankruptcy laws, was ordered liquidated by a federal bankruptcy judge here. The judge acted upon a petition by Mustang Ranch's largest creditor, the Internal Revenue Service, which claims to be owed up to $13 million in back taxes from the 105-room brothel, located a few miles east of here. The tax dispute has been simmering for years.

The order came after the brothel's latest attempt to sell itself and raise capital collapsed. At least twice in recent years, the owners of Mustang Ranch have attempted, unsuccessfully, to raise money in public offerings. The latest IRS move followed an unsuccessful attempt to sell the brothel outright to a closely held company, although the brothel's owners claim it was the IRS's own objections that caused that deal to fall apart.

The liquidation order has disrupted normal operations at Mustang Ranch, according to Peter Perry, a Reno attorney for the brothel. The court appointed a bankruptcy trustee to oversee operations, but the trustee is having a hard time of it. "Nobody wants to work for the trustee, so they all left," Mr. Perry says. "She went out there, and tried to take over. But who wants to work for her? People have got to really worry about her expertise" in running a brothel.

Mr. Perry said the brothel plans to fight the liquidation order; the trustee and the IRS couldn't immediately be reached.
