Business Brief -- Seeq Technology Inc.: Firm Will Take a Charge To Cover Plant Conversion

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/20/90

Seeq Technology Inc. said it will take a one-time charge of $8 million to $10 million in the fourth quarter, ending Sept. 30, to cover costs it incurred by converting a wafer-processing production plant to a development facility. The company also said it expects revenue of about $12 million for the quarter, up from $10.9 million a year earlier.

Seeq, which makes data-communications products, also said its board reaffirmed the San Jose, Calif., company's authority to buy back as many as 850,000 shares of its common stock from time to time in the open market and in block transactions. The board originally approved the buy-back in September 1988, for Seeq's use under the company's restated 1982 stock-option plan. Since then, Seeq hasn't bought back any of the stock.
