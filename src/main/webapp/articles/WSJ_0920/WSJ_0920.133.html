International Brief -- BCE Inc.: Company Expects to Form Group to Bid for TelMex

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/20/90

BCE Inc., parent of Bell Canada, expects to form a group that will bid for control of Telefonos de Mexico S.A., a BCE official said. As previously reported, the Mexican government intends to sell a 20% control block in TelMex to a consortium that includes one or more foreign concerns. The 20% control block represents just over half of the full-voting shares in the Mexican telephone monopoly. The government owns just over 50% of TelMex. It would sell its remaining shares, new limited-voting shares created under a recapitalization this summer, at a later date.

The BCE official said putting together a group to bid for control of Telmex is "a priority" for the Montreal-based holding company. He said a Mexican concern would own about half of the consortium while BCE and a U.S. telephone company would each own about 25%. The BCE official said the company is in talks with a number of U.S. telephone companies, which he declined to identify, regarding the bid. He said the government has set a deadline of Nov. 15 for bids.

BCE entered the Mexican telecommunications market this spring as a member of two of the eight consortiums that were awarded licenses to build and operate cellular telephone systems in the country. Also this spring, BCE joined forces with MCI Communications Corp. and others to form a group in New Zealand that will compete with that country's main telephone company.
