Technology: Businessland to Fold Unit Into Operations; More Layoffs Planned

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/20/90

SAN FRANCISCO -- Businessland Inc., continuing to cut costs, said it plans to lay off 10% of its work force and merge its ComputerCraft unit into main computer-dealer operations. The moves will cost about 370 people their jobs and result in Businessland, the nation's largest computer dealer, taking a $1 million charge against earnings in its first quarter ending Sept. 30.

This year, Businessland has repeatedly cut costs and reorganized in order to stem operating losses tied to price competition, rapid overseas expansion and poor performance of its ComputerCraft unit. In January, the company trimmed its work force by about 5% and since has posted losses for three consecutive quarters.

The decision to fold ComputerCraft into its main operations follows months of informal efforts by Businessland to sell the unit, which it purchased two years ago for about $25 million in stock. Businessland mainly sells computers directly to large corporations through its own sales force, while ComputerCraft -- soon to be renamed Businessland -- has 16 stores, mainly in the West, and relies heavily on walk-in customers.

David Norman, Businessland's chief executive officer, said the new arrangement will save the company money, but some analysts said it was tantamount to discontinuing ComputerCraft.

"It's a death knell," said Seymour Merrin, a computer-dealer analyst in Palo Alto, Calif. Direct sales and retail "are so different, there is no way to run them under the same roof."

Mr. Norman said Businessland also plans to cut expenses by eliminating its regional sales managers and reducing inventory by roughly 20% in the current quarter.
