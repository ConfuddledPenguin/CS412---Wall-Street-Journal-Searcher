International: EC Panel Seeks More Cuts In Farm-Subsidy Proposal

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/20/90

BRUSSELS -- The European Community's top agricultural official was forced to withdraw a crucial proposal on farm-subsidy cuts when a majority of fellow EC commissioners demanded more substantial rollbacks. Aides to Agriculture Commissioner Ray MacSharry said a majority of EC commissioners wanted to signal the U.S. that the Community was serious about a successful outcome to the current round of world trade talks taking place under the General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade. GATT negotiators gave all countries until Oct. 15 to put their final offers on the table. Because of the bitter dispute on farm subsidies between the EC and the U.S., many experts are worried that the talks could fail.

Mr. MacSharry had proposed cutting overall farm support by only 30% over 10 years, using 1986 spending as a basis. U.S. Trade Representative Carla Hills, in Brussels yesterday, said that would effectively be a much smaller reduction, perhaps as little as a 10% off today's levels, as 1986 was a high benchmark. The U.S. this week softened its stand, saying it would accept a 70% cut in subsidies and deeper cuts in export refunds.
