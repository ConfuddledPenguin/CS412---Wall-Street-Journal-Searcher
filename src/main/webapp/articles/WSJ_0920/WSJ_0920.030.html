Niagara Mohawk Says It Will Eliminate 1,100 Jobs by 1992

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/20/90

SYRACUSE, N.Y. -- Niagara Mohawk Power Corp. said it plans to phase out more than 1,100 jobs by 1992 in an effort to reduce costs by as much as $64 million in its nuclear and fossil-fuel power divisions. The majority of cuts will occur in the nuclear division, which would bear $55 million of the spending cuts. The planned reductions include 450 company positions and 650 positions now filled by contractors. The investor-owned electric and gas utility has more than 11,000 employees, excluding contract workers.

The company said that while it expects to achieve most of the job reductions through attrition and streamlining of current job responsibilities, 17 nuclear management employees will be displaced this year.

The company is also seeking cost reductions of $9 million in fossil-fuel generation and plans to eliminate 17 positions in that division.

The cost-reduction measures are part of a continuing program to improve operating efficiency, Niagara Mohawk said. Similar efforts are under way in regional operations and corporate departments, with implementation expected early next year.

Niagara Mohawk experienced financial difficulties in recent years, although reported earnings for fiscal 1990 are more favorable. In fiscal 1989 ended June 30, the company's earnings sank 36% to $105.6 million, or 78 cents a share, from $159.7 million, or $1.21 a share, in 1988. Due to a recent regulatory agreement that allowed it to recoup costs on one of its reactors, earnings for 1990 rose back to $129.1 million, or 95 cents a share.

Niagara Mohawk serves two million electric and gas customers throughout most of upstate New York.
