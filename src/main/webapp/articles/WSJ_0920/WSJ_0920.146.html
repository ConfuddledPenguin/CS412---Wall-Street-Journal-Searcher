Cargill to Set Up Offices In Soviet Union, Poland

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/20/90

MINNEAPOLIS -- Cargill Inc. said it plans to open offices in Moscow and Warsaw to seek potential investments in the Soviet Union and Poland. Cargill, one of the largest exporters of farm goods to the Soviet Union, will be among the first of the large U.S. grain exporters to establish a physical presence there.

Grain trading officials said yesterday that they expect other U.S. exporters to follow suit in the wake of Soviet President Mikhail Gorbachev's visit to Minnesota in June.

Cargill said Juels Carlson, president of Cargill's world-wide molasses operations, will head the Moscow office. It is slated to open early next year.

Edgar T. Savidge, account manager of Cargill's soybean processing plant in Liverpool, England, will manage the Warsaw office, due to open Oct. 1.

Cargill said the executives will study possible investments in commodity processing and merchandising, transportation and agricultural products, among other areas.

Over the past 15 years, Cargill's annual exports to the Soviet Union of grain and oilseed-related products have ranged between four million metric tons and 8.5 million metric tons.
