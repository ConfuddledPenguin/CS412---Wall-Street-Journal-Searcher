Economy: August Housing Starts Fell For Seventh Month in a Row

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/20/90

WASHINGTON -- Housing starts fell for the seventh consecutive month in August for their longest decline in eight years. Builders started work on new homes at an annual rate of 1,127,000 last month, the Commerce Department said, down 1.7% from July's 1,147,000. August starts were running 15% below a year earlier. They haven't fallen for seven months in a row since the last recession in 1981-82.

These are the first housing-start figures to appear since the Iraqi invasion of Kuwait and might reflect shaky confidence in the wake of the crisis. "Frankly, I think the housing industry probably would have begun to rebound by August if not for the Persian Gulf crisis," said Mark Obrinsky, a senior economist at the Federal National Mortgage Association.

Starts on single-family homes fell 3.2% last month to an annual rate of 840,000 after declining 2.4% in July to 868,000. Starts on apartments and other multifamily dwellings rose 2.9% in August to an annual rate of 287,000 units after plunging 7% in July to 279,000.

Despite these figures, however, David Seider, chief economist for the National Association of Home Builders, said the market for multifamily dwellings is in a "bona fide recession condition," with the falloff in single-family housing starts less dire.

All the numbers were adjusted for seasonal fluctuations.
