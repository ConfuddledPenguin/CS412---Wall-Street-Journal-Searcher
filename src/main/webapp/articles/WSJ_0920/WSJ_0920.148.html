U.S., U.K., Japan Develop Securities Trading Pact

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 09/20/90

WASHINGTON -- Securities regulators from the U.S., Britain and Japan agreed to pursue initiatives that will encourage cross-border securities trading among the three largest securities markets. The communique also indicates that Securities and Exchange Commission Chairman Richard Breeden has enlisted regulators from Britain and Japan to help promote his continuing effort to raise margins on stock-index futures contracts.

The communique states that regulators from the three countries agree that low margins "may affect the stability of the securities markets, including the frequency of extreme price movements."

"It is very important that this communique set forth the views of the authorities from the U.K. and Japan," on the issue of stock-index futures margins, Mr. Breeden said at a news briefing held near Washington. Mr. Breeden took the opportunity to repeat his contention that Congress should act to raise stock-index futures margins.
