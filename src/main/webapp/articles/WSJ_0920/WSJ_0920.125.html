Business Brief -- Pennzoil Co.: U.S. Court Backs Purchase Of 8.8% Stake in Chevron

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/20/90

A federal district court threw out Chevron Corp.'s challenge to the Pennzoil Co. purchase of a stake in the San Francisco-based oil giant. U.S. District Judge Samuel Conti of San Francisco found that Pennzoil didn't violate antitrust law when it bought 8.8% of Chevron's common stock outstanding for $2.1 billion in open-market purchases last fall. Chevron had sought to block the purchase on grounds that Pennzoil misrepresented its intentions.

Pennzoil said it was "delighted" with the ruling. The Houston-based company has spent $2.1 billion of the $2.6 billion it won in its highly publicized legal battle with Texaco Inc. to buy into Chevron. In July, Pennzoil said it was considering additional purchases of Chevron stock with the remaining Texaco settlement proceeds.

Chevron said it would consider an appeal, but must review the opinion first. "We're disappointed with the outcome," said Kenneth T. Derr, Chevron's chairman and chief executive officer.

Pennzoil has maintained that it bought the stake for investment purposes.
