Abreast of the Market: Industrials Drop 13.86 as Greenspan Paints Gloomy Economic Picture

  • Author: David Wilson
  • Date: 09/20/90

NEW YORK -- Pessimism about the outlook for interest rates, the economy and corporate earnings took its toll on the stock market, as prices fell in moderate trading. The Dow Jones Industrial Average dropped 13.86 to 2557.43, as selling produced sharp setbacks in Merck, General Electric and Primerica.

Among broader indexes, Standard & Poor's 500-Stock Index dropped 2.00 to 316.60, the New York Stock Exchange Composite Index dropped 0.97 to 173.91 and the Dow Jones Equity Market Index fell 1.78 to 293.30.

Declining issues topped advancers by 883 to 599 on the Big Board, where the pace of trading improved modestly. About 147,530,000 shares changed hands in New York Stock Exchange trading, compared with 141,130,000 shares Tuesday.

Trading is expected to swell tomorrow as the result of activity stemming from the quarterly expiration of stock-index futures and options -- also known as the "triple witching hour."

Stocks held firm as the latest session began, but fell after Federal Reserve Board Chairman Alan Greenspan's testimony before a congressional panel damped speculation that the central bank would soon ease its monetary policy in order to revive the economy.

"People were looking to get some inspiration" from Mr. Greenspan, said John A. Conlon Jr., a managing director at Rothschild Inc. But the Fed chairman said any shifts in central-bank policy would depend on factors "which are shifting day by day."

Also, Mr. Greenspan noted that inflation hasn't eased despite sluggishness in the economy and that weakness in the dollar could produce further inflationary pressures.

Merck fell 3 1/8 to 76 7/8 in composite trading of 2.9 million shares after Vector Securities, a Deerfield, Ill., investment concern specializing in health-care stocks, downgraded the issue and lowered its long-term projection for profit growth at the company to 15% from 18%.

Analyst Patricia Padgett Lea said that conversations with doctors involved in the clinical trials of Proscar, Merck's prostate drug, had led her to conclude it will be only a "marginal drug," rather than the blockbuster many had expected.

Merck, which said it expects the results from the trials to be released early next year, wouldn't comment on its financial expectations for the drug. But Ms. Lea said annual sales could eventually reach $200 million, rather than the $1 billion or more projected.

MBIA fell 3 3/8 to 28 1/2 on 1.2 million shares, reflecting concerns about the possible impact of the city of Philadelphia's financial troubles on the municipal bond-insurance firm. The company, whose shares skidded 3 1/2 Tuesday, said it has about $9 million of exposure to the city next year. MBIA also disclosed that it is reinstituting a program to repurchase up to one million common shares.

GE, the parent company of municipal bond insurer FGIC Corp., went down 2 1/2 to 56 1/8 and led the list of most active issues as three million shares changed hands. Primerica dropped 1 7/8 to 21 1/4 on 1.4 million shares; Federal National Mortgage Association, another financial-services issue, dipped 1 to 26 1/2 on 2.5 million shares.

American General plummeted 6 3/8 to 33 5/8 on two million shares after the company's board cleared management to consider a restructuring plan -- involving the sale of its home-service insurance operations -- as an alternative to selling the company.

Stocks depressed by lowered earnings expectations included Claire's Stores, which skidded 3 3/8 to 12 in active trading; Lowe's, which dropped 1 1/2 to 21 1/2; Armstrong World Industries, which went down 2 to 27; and Pitney Bowes, which fell 4 1/8 to 30 1/8.

Claire's fell after Alex. Brown & Sons lowered the company's earnings estimate for the current fiscal year to a range of $1.20 to $1.25 a share, from $1.30 a share, citing a slowdown over the past few weeks in sales growth at stores open at least a year. The firm also downgraded the stock to "buy" from "strong buy."

Prudential-Bache Securities lowered its fiscal 1991 and 1992 earnings projections on Lowe's, citing a softening economy and other concerns, and cut its investment rating to "sell" from "hold."

Kidder Peabody reduced its 1991 forecast on Armstrong World by 90 cents a share, to $3, and downgraded the issue to "underperformer" from "hold." Analyst Barbara K. Allen said the company faces "a period of turmoil" in the flooring industry, stemming from new products introduced earlier this week by a competitor, Mannington Mills.

Pitney Bowes, in disclosing plans to stop remanufacturing used copier equipment, said it expects this year's net income to fall short of the year-earlier level. The company said its decision to scrap its remanufacturing business would result in a third-quarter pretax charge of about $86 million.

Occidental Petroleum retreated 1 3/8 to 21 3/8 on 2.6 million shares and traded as low as 20 1/4, a 52-week low, amid rumors that the company would lower its dividend. Occidental denied the speculation.

Waste Management, whose board approved the repurchase of up to 25 million shares over the next 24 months, advanced 1/2 to 34 5/8 on 1.4 million shares. The total represents about 5% of its shares outstanding.

Other active issues to advance amid the broad market's retreat included International Business Machines, which added 1 1/8 to 108 3/8 on 2.2 million shares; Philip Morris, which went up 7/8 to 46 on three million shares; and Eastman Kodak, which rose 1/2 to 42 1/8 on 1.6 million shares.

Quaker Oats jumped 2 3/8 to 45 5/8 amid speculation that the company would receive a takeover offer from Coca-Cola, which dropped 1 1/2 to 39 3/4. A Coca-Cola spokesman dismissed the rumor, calling it "totally ridiculous."

Corroon & Black skidded 3 1/2 to 32. Aon reiterated that it won't pursue a hostile offer for the company, which rejected its $40-a-share offer in favor of proceeding with a stock-swap merger with Willis Faber, a British insurance broker.

The American Stock Exchange Market Value Index fell 0.72 to 318.97. Volume totaled 10,270,000 shares.
