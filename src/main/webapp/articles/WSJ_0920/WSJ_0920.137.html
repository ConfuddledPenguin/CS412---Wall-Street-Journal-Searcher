What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/20/90

THE ECONOMY is moving closer to a recession as a result of the jump in oil prices, Fed Chairman Greenspan told Congress. He said the central bank's ability to resist a recession by lowering interest rates has been frustrated by inflationary pres sures. However, he didn't rule out a long-anticipated move to lower rates.

A Fed survey shows that the Mideast crisis already is raising manufacturers' costs, damping consumer buying and causing widespread uncertainty in the business community.

---

Northwest Airlines received a $500 million cash infusion from Airbus Industrie and General Electric. The European consortium arranged the low-interest loan after Northwest agreed to buy 75 more Airbus planes and take options on 30 others. A GE team will supply the engines for the aircraft.

---

Pan Am plans to slash 2,500 jobs, eliminate some trans-Atlantic service and shift to smaller planes to combat higher fuel costs and light traffic.

---

Northrop's chairman retired just as an International Chamber of Commerce ruling surfaced saying that $6.2 million in payments by the firm to South Korea were meant to induce Seoul to buy Northrop fighter planes.

---

The Reichmann family is trying to sell a nearly 20% partnership interest in its U.S. real estate portfolio. Industry officials said the stake could fetch as much as $800 million.

---

The FCC approved broad changes in the way it regulates fees long-distance carriers pay for access to local phone networks. It projected billions of dollars in savings for consumers, but some user groups were skeptical.

---

American General said it may settle for selling only its home-service life insurance business, claiming it's having trouble finding a buyer for the entire financial-services concern.

---

The S&L bailout agency told a House panel that it will need about $122 billion in funding for the fiscal year starting Oct. 1, more than double what was anticipated a year ago.

---

Donald Trump wants to restructure $245 million in debt payments still due on loans made for the Trump Shuttle. Mr. Trump cited soaring fuel costs and a soft Northeast economy.

---

Small investors seeking punitive damages in arbitration disputes with brokers suffered a setback when a judge ruled punitive damages can't always be awarded in such cases.

---

First Executive's claims that unreleased reports prove its long-term financial viability appear to fall far short of that assertion.

---

The budget standoff isn't giving a political advantage to the Republicans or the Democrats, a Wall Street Journal/NBC News poll found.

---

The life insurance industry is likely to see its federal tax bill jump by $2 billion a year, partly because stock and mutual insurance companies haven't resolved a tax squabble.

---

Time Warner will consolidate its publishing operations into a new unit that will include its magazine business and book publishing operation.

---

The GAO is concerned bank regulators are making some of the same mistakes that led to the S&L crisis.
