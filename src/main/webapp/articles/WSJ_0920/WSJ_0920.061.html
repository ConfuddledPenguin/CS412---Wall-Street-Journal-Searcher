Who's News: Maybelline Chief Quits Three Months After Acquisition --- Mulloy Says Plan Was to Stay Only Through Transition Of Wasserstein Purchase

  • Author: Kathleen Deveny
  • Date: 09/20/90

Just three months after Maybelline Inc. was acquired by Wasserstein, Perella & Co., the cosmetics maker's president and chief executive officer, Gary Mulloy, has abruptly resigned. Company insiders said Mr. Mulloy, who had worked at Maybelline for 15 years and served as chief executive since January, quit because of mounting interference from his superiors at Wasserstein.

Robert B. McKeon, chairman of Wasserstein, Perella Management Partners, an arm of the parent, called charges of management interference inaccurate, saying he has visited Maybelline's Memphis headquarters only four times since the company was acquired.

"When we acquired the company back in June, our plan was to keep Gary through our transition," Mr. McKeon said. "He's just moving on to other things." Mr. McKeon said he personally will fill Maybelline's top job until Mr. Mulloy's successor is chosen. Company insiders said a candidate wasn't apparent.

Reached at home yesterday, Mr. Mulloy also said he had planned to remain at Maybelline "only through the transition period." He added that he was interested in leaving the cosmetics industry and would prefer the management challenge of a turnaround situation. "It's time for me to do something else," he said.

Yet at the time of the acquisition, Mr. Mulloy told reporters he planned to stay at the company indefinitely. And company insiders said Mr. Mulloy was upset recently over what he believed was interference in the company's advertising strategy. There had been rumors on Madison Avenue that the company was considering changing advertising agencies.

Earlier this month, Maybelline consolidated its advertising business at DDB Needham Worldwide in Chicago, moving a small account for its Shine Free cosmetics line from the Bloom Agency in Dallas. A spokeswoman for DDB Needham said she didn't believe the Maybelline account was up for review. "We just got a new piece of business from them," said the spokeswoman. "We've hired several new creative people to work on the account."

Mr. Mulloy's departure marks the latest twist in Maybelline's labyrinthine corporate history. Schering-Plough put the cosmetics unit on the block in December. In April, MBP Acquisition Corp., an investment group that included Playtex Family Products Corp., had agreed to acquire Maybelline for $300 million. When that deal collapsed suddenly a few months later, Wasserstein was apparently poised to step in. The sale price was unchanged at $300 million.

With 1989 sales of about $300 million, Maybelline is the No. 2 brand of cosmetics sold through mass merchandise outlets. Although its share of the mass market for cosmetics has held at about 18%, it continues to trail rival Cover Girl Cosmetics, which was acquired by Procter & Gamble Co. last year.
