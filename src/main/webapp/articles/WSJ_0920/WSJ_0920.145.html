Marketing & Media: News Corp. Asks Exchange To Permit Nonvoting Stock

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/20/90

SYDNEY, Australia -- Australian Stock Exchange officials said News Corp. has approached them to allow the company to issue nonvoting stock, a type of security currently prohibited by the exchange. The move apparently is an attempt by media mogul Rupert Murdoch to raise equity without diluting the 45% stake in News Corp. that his family controls.

A senior News Corp. official in New York declined to comment. But for Mr. Murdoch, News Corp.'s chief executive officer, the ability to issue such restricted-voting securities would provide an alternative to his failed effort last year to create a separate media vehicle to further his international expansion. That company, named Media Partners International Ltd. and dubbed "Son of News Corp." by Mr. Murdoch, was to be managed and 20%-owned by News Corp.

But investors shunned the new venture and it was abandoned. Meanwhile, News Corp.'s debts have spiraled. Mr. Murdoch has been selling assets to reduce debt, and he has taken News Corp. off the acquisition trail.

Issuing nonvoting stock, however, would give Mr. Murdoch "the potential to play the same game on {News Corp.'s} existing balance sheet," says Viktor Shvets, who tracks News Corp. for Baring Securities (Australia) Ltd.
