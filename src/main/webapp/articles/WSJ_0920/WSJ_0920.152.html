Big Western Investment In Eastern Europe Planned

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/20/90

NEW YORK -- Large Western companies are gearing up to invest billions of dollars in Eastern Europe over the next five years, a new survey indicates. More than 30% of the corporations polled plan to spend a total of $3.2 billion in Eastern Europe in the next five years, according to accounting firm DRT International. Another 33% plan investments but didn't say how much. DRT surveyed 128 companies world-wide with assets or sales of more than $1 billion.

Most companies planning to invest in Eastern Europe intend to do so through joint ventures with existing Eastern European enterprises. Export-import opportunities, which are particularly popular among Japanese companies, came in second.

In the survey, 23 European companies said they expect to invest $1.7 billion; 13 U.S. companies, $332 million; and 12 Japanese companies, $1.1 billion.

Nearly all of the Western Europe respondents do business in Eastern Europe or plan to do so in the next five years; more than half of the North American and 63% of Japanese companies said they expect to have a stake in Eastern Europe in the future.
