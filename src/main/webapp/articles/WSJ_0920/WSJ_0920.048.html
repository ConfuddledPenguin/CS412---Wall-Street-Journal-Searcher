Letters to the Editor: As Always, Wait Till the Fat Lady Sings

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/20/90

To characterize President Carter's actions in 1979 with respect to the price and supply of oil as "dithering" (as Peter Truell does in the Aug. 20 Outlook column) is to ignore the facts. The revolution in Iran began pushing up world oil prices early in that year. U.S. oil prices were still controlled, however, under the 1975 Energy Policy and Conservation Act. As of June 1, 1979, the law gave the president discretionary power to revise or eliminate oil-price controls. In fact, President Carter did initiate gradual removal of controls from specific categories of oil, with domestic crude prices scheduled to reach the world price by September 1981, when controls would expire. By the end of 1979, Congress had passed a windfall-profits tax to collect about half of the oil companies' gains from price decontrol.

Reliance on the market to moderate the effects of price and quantity changes may not be immediately satisfying as a military response. Before singing paeans to President Bush's "decisive action," however, it is prudent to await the fat lady's aria.

Marilu McCarty

Associate Professor of Economics

Georgia Institute of Technology

Atlanta
