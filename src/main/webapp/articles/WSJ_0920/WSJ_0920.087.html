National Steel Corp. Prices

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/20/90

PITTSBURGH -- National Steel Corp. said it will raise prices on steel sheet products, effective with shipments Dec. 30. Base prices will rise in the range of 5% to 8% depending on the product line. The base price will increase $35 a ton on hot-rolled sheet, $47 a ton on cold-rolled sheet, and $49 a ton on coated products. The company said it will also increase prices on a variety of extra charges. National Steel is 70%-owned by NKK Corp. of Japan and 30% by National Intergroup Inc.

The increases follow similar announcements by other major steel makers, including USX Corp., and Bethlehem Steel Corp., made within the past two weeks.
