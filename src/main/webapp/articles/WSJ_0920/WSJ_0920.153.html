Firms to Pay on Charge Of Misusing Union Funds

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/20/90

WASHINGTON -- Shearson Lehman Brothers Inc., Stein Roe & Farnham Inc. and two other firms agreed to pay nearly $3 million to settle federal charges of misusing assets of three Teamsters Union funds. The Labor Department's Pension and Welfare Benefits Administration said that under consent orders in federal court in Syracuse, N.Y., Shearson, the brokerage unit of American Express Co., agreed to pay $2 million to three Teamsters pension and health funds in Utica, N.Y., as restitution for alleged improper transactions made on behalf of those funds. Stein Roe, a Chicago investment manager, agreed to pay $625,000, and Renaissance Investment Management Inc., Cincinnati, and First Albany Inc., Albany, N.Y., a brokerage firm, agreed to pay a combined $180,000, the agency said.

The agency charged Shearson and Stein Roe with violations including transferring stocks with falling prices to the Teamsters fund accounts while switching valuable stock to friends, relatives and customers of Shearson brokers. Renaissance and other investment firms were charged with letting the funds' cash balances under their management be treated as Shearson credit balances and with allowing payment of excessive commissions to Shearson and First Albany.

A spokesman for Shearson said the employees handling the accounts were fired in 1985 after the firm's own internal investigation. He said the firm cooperated fully with investigators "and agreed to settle without admitting or denying the allegations to avoid protracted litigation."

Stein Roe officials couldn't be reached for comment.
