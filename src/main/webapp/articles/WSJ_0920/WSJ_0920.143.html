Neil Bush to Defend Role In Failed S&L Next Week

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/20/90

WASHINGTON -- The federal government's case against Neil Bush, the president's son, involving his role as a director of failed Silverado Banking, Savings & Loan Association, isn't likely to wind up until next spring, a spokesman for S&L regulators said. Next week, Mr. Bush, will defend his actions before an administrative law judge in Denver. The Office of Thrift Supervision is seeking a cease-and-desist order to prohibit Mr. Bush from engaging in certain activities if he holds a similar post at a financial institution in the future. Mr. Bush served on the board of the Denver thrift from 1985 to 1988.

Mr. Bush, who as an outside board member had no day-to-day role at the thrift, has denied wrongdoing and has fought the order. Thrift regulators said he failed to disclose to other Silverado board members his separate business relationship with two large Silverado borrowers. Several Silverado executives have been barred from the S&L and banking industries under government consent agreements.

The administrative law judge is to make his recommendation for action by Dec. 6, and following another month for written rebuttals, OTS Director Timothy Ryan will have 90 days to either agree or disagree with the recommendation. Mr. Ryan's ruling may be appealed in court.
