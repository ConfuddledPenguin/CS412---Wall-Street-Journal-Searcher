Dividend News: Furr's/Bishop's Omits 3rd-Quarter Payout In Conversion Plan

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/20/90

LUBBOCK, Texas - Furr's/Bishop's Cafeterias L.P. said it won't pay the regularly scheduled cash dividend of 32.5 cents a preferred and subordinated unit for the third quarter. The decision was a previously announced but unreported part of a proposal made to unitholders last week by the general partner, Cavalcade & Co., to convert the master limited partnership to corporate ownership.

Under the proposal, for each partnership unit owned, Furr's/Bishop's 20,000 public limited partners would receive 75 cents in cash, one-fourth of a common share, and six-tenths of a share of $9 convertible preferred stock. The convertible preferred would have a stated cumulative annual dividend of $1.12 a share, payable quarterly, increasing to $1.80 a share on the 10th anniversary of the merger.

The proposed conversion has been deemed fair by an independent committee of the Furr's/Bishop's board and its financial adviser, Bear, Stearns & Co., but must be approved by a majority of the preferred and subordinated units.

If the proposed corporate conversion doesn't take place, the partnership said it will reconsider paying the 32.5 cents-a-unit dividend for the third quarter to preferred units only.
