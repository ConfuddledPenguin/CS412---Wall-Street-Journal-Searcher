Business Brief -- Valhi Inc.: Chemical Firm Sets Accord With NL on Merger Plan

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/20/90

Valhi Inc. and NL Industries Inc., two companies controlled by Dallas financier Harold Simmons, said they had entered into a definitive agreement on a previously announced plan to merge. Under the plan, Valhi, a chemical and industrial concern that currently holds about 66% of NL, would acquire the remaining equity in the petroleum services concern in a transaction valued at $411 million. The companies said the merger will not be completed if the average price of Valhi common shares stays below $9.50 a share for the 20 trading days ending two trading days before a shareholder vote on the proposal. Valhi can terminate the merger if its average price for the same period is above $13 a share.

Consummation of the merger is subject to, among other things, approval by Valhi's shareholders and approval by a majority of NL shares present and voting, other than those held by Valhi. Both the NL and Valhi boards have already approved the merger agreement, the companies said in a joint statement.

The companies said they expect to mail a joint proxy statement to shareholders in late October and that their respective meetings of shareholders, called to consider the transaction, would probably be held in December.
