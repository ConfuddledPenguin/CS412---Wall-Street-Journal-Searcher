CBOT and CME to Study Unifying Some Functions

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/20/90

CHICAGO -- The Chicago Board of Trade and the Chicago Mercantile Exchange said they have formed a joint committee to explore ways to cut costs and increase efficiency by unifying certain functions, procedures and operations. The committee will focus specifically on the areas of international marketing, systems development, operations and clearing, the CBOT and CME said.

The joint effort is part of "a new era of cooperation," said one committee member. The CME and CBOT agreed in principle this year to become partners in Globex, an automated after-hours trading system being developed in conjunction with Reuters Holdings PLC. The two are also sharing the costs of developing AUDIT, a hand-held computer terminal for recording trades.
