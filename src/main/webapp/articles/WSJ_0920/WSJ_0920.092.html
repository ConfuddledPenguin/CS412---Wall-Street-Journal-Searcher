Who's News: FarWest's Kayne Quits As President, Director And Chief Executive

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/20/90

NEWPORT BEACH, Calif. -- FarWest Financial Corp., buffeted by recent losses and tough new regulatory capital standards, said Fred Kayne resigned as president, chief executive officer and a director. Mr. Kayne, a former Bear, Stearns & Co. managing director who has been president and chief executive of FarWest since February 1988, said the decision wasn't related to FarWest's problems. Rather, he cited the thrift-bailout law last year, which greatly limited diversification opportunities for thrifts and meant that "I could not be involved in expanding the company."

Mr. Kayne, who will continue as a part-time consultant, said he plans to spend more time managing his own investments.

The S&L holding company is controlled by the Belzberg family of Canada, and William Belzberg will continue as chairman. Mr. Kayne said he didn't know of any plans to name a new president and chief executive to succeed him.

FarWest reported a net loss of $3.7 million, or 47 cents a share, for the second quarter. Last month, federal thrift regulators subjected its FarWest Savings & Loan Association subsidiary to a number of operating restrictions after rejecting a plan by the thrift to bring its capital levels into compliance with regulatory standards.

Among other factors, FarWest, a major junk-bond investor, has been hurt in recent months by the declining junk-bond market.
