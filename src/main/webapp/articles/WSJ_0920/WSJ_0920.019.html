Astrex Inc. Reviews Accounting Policy Following Incident

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/20/90

PLAINVIEW, N.Y. -- Astrex Inc. began reassessing its accounting procedures after reporting that Ronald S. Kass, its president and chief executive officer, charged at least $150,000 in personal expenses to the company. Mr. Kass, who resigned as chief financial officer of the electronics parts distribution company, "has acknowledged causing such payments to be made for his personal benefit," the company said, and has agreed to make restitution.

The company said the payments had been made over 2 1/2 years, in payments Mr. Kass caused to be made to third parties; many of the payments were classified for accounting purposes as capital assets. The total amount of the payments hasn't been determined. Mr. Kass is continuing to serve as chief executive and president at his base salary, although his employment contract has been terminated, now making him an "employee at will."

Astrex directors said they believe "it is in the best interests of the company for Mr. Kass to remain employed."

Astrex said the $150,000 represents less than 3% of the company's assets, but noted that "there is currently no assurance" that Mr. Kass's acts "will not adversely affect the company."

An audit committee of the board and an independent accounting firm have been investigating the matter since August. Mark Schindler, a director, said no criminal investigation is under way. "It's not likely that this will go that far," he said. The committee will report next week on the total amount involved and whether any further action will be taken against Mr. Kass, who has been with the company for more than 20 years.

Mr. Kass and his attorneys at Jacobson & Mermelstein in New York declined to comment.

Mr. Schindler said that the board decided to retain Mr. Kass as an employee because he "is a key man in the electronic connector business" and is "familiar with all the matters of the company."

Separately, Astrex reported that on Sept. 1, it failed to pay interest of approximately $425,000 due on $8.8 million of 9.5% debentures due Sept. 2000.

Astrex distributes over 25,000 electronic parts and components to about 4,000 customers, chiefly in the Northeast region. Astrex had a net loss of $355,000 or 33 cents a share on revenue of $4.3 million for the fiscal first quarter ended June 30.
