Business Brief -- Public Service Co. of Colorado: Borrowing Is Seen to Pay For Converting Idled Plant

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/20/90

Public Service Co. of Colorado, Denver, said it expects to borrow, either through a private placement or public offering, about $200 million to help pay costs associated with converting its idled nuclear electric generating plant to natural gas power. The utility said it expects to use about $125 million of the borrowing to pay for removing the remains of radioactive fuel from the St. Vrain plant north of Denver. PS Colorado stopped generating electricity at the plant, the nation's only gas-cooled, high-temperature reactor, in August 1989 after 10 years because of operational problems and excessive costs. The company said it may have to spend $47 million of the borrowed funds to build a temporary storage facility for spent fuel from the plant.

PS Colorado said state regulators cleared a private placement, though plans are still preliminary. It hasn't filed documents with the Securities and Exchange Commission on a possible public bond offering. It said it doesn't expect now to use any of the funds to finance the plant's $85 million dismantling and conversion.
