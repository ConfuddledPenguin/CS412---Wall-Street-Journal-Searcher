International: EC Leaders Set Timetable For Integration Treaties

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/26/90

DUBLIN -- European Community leaders agreed on a timetable for writing separate treaties on political union and economic and monetary integration, but discussions here made it clear the drafting process could be a tug-of-war. At the first day of a two-day summit, the 12 EC leaders set the opening of the political union conference for Dec. 14 in Rome, followed the next day by the opening of the economic and monetary union conference. The two conferences will decide on amendments to the EC's founding Treaty of Rome, giving EC institutions vast new powers.

But the leaders made little progress reconciling conflicting visions over exactly where the treaty amendments should lead. French President Francois Mitterrand said he would ultimately like to see the EC states bound together in some kind of federation. But other leaders want a much looser arrangement.

The leaders adopted a broad text that leaves almost all options open, and detailed discussions will come up only once the conference gets under way.

On the complex question of integrating EC monetary policies and creating a single currency, there is even more disagreement. The leaders made little progress deciding how long it should take before they cede national control of economic and monetary policy.
