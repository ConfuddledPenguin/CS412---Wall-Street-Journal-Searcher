Sonat Agrees to Pay Arco $300.5 Million Settlement

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/26/90

LOS ANGELES -- Southern Natural Gas Co. agreed to pay Atlantic Richfield Co. $300.5 million to settle a lawsuit for damages resulting from an accident last year on an Arco offshore drilling platform in the Gulf of Mexico. Seven workers were killed and 23 injured in the March 19, 1989, fire and explosion on Arco's South Pass "B" platform.

An Arco spokesman said he didn't know when or how the $300.5 million would be paid. The settlement came during a recess of the trial of a suit brought by Arco in April against Sonat, two of its affiliates and Pipe Lines Unlimited Services, Houma, La. The trial began June 11 in state court in Texas.

In the suit, Arco charged Birmingham, Ala.-based Sonat and Pipe Lines Unlimited, an oil transportation concern, with negligence that caused the fire. Sonat's pipeline was connected to Arco's platform.

The pact resolves all of Arco's claims against Sonat, the Arco spokesman said, including damages to the platform and lost production. Prior to the accident, the platform produced about 18,000 barrels a day, the spokesman said. It has remained shut since then.

The settlement assigns Arco's claims against Pipe Lines Unlimited to Sonat, the Arco spokesman said. Separate settlements were reached earlier, he said, between Sonat and families of the accident victims.
