What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/26/90

AMERICAN INTERNATIONAL is buying International Lease Finance, an aircraft-leasing concern, for cash and stock valued at about $1.3 billion. With a cash-rich insurer and financial-services firm as its parent company, International Lease may enjoy more flexibility in how it peddles its portfolio of jetliners. ---

The U.S. is pursuing a plan to create a global radio network to be run by government and industry. It would need a $1 billion satellite system and a new generation of digital radios. Opposition from broadcasters is likely.

---

RJR Nabisco has budgeted $250 million in fees for its debt refinancing, suggesting that patching up leveraged firms may be the financial community's next big moneymaker.

---

Siebe won an auction to buy Foxboro of Massachusetts for $656.4 million. Shares of the British maker of control devices tumbled amid concern that the purchase price is too high.

---

Bond prices dropped as investors worried about too many issues coming to market. Stocks also fell, in part on some bad earnings news. The Dow Jones industrials lost 12.13 points.

Canada's financial markets recovered after an initial sell-off yesterday following the demise of a constitutional accord on Quebec's status.

---

Gold prices rose after South Africa's central bank said it actively supported the market in recent weeks.

---

Thrift regulators issued rules on how much thrifts can pay in dividends, in an effort to make S&Ls more attractive to investors.

---

U.S. auto sales fell 3.6% in midJune from a year ago, as the Big Three's slippage more than offset gains by Japanese makers. But analysts said the figures suggest that the current sales slump may be over.

---

Chase Manhattan plans to slash expenses by at least $300 million, or 8%, over the next six to 12 months, and possibly lay off 3,000 people.

---

Trump's bankers raced to try to complete a $65 million bailout of the developer by this morning and are expected to provide interim financing.

---

Caterpillar disclosed it expects "substantially lower" earnings and flat sales this year. The company's stock tumbled on the announcement.

---

Michelin warned of possible losses and announced the elimination of 2,260 jobs. The French tire maker's stock plunged 8.3% on the news.

---

Motorola and Hitachi agreed to hold talks in an attempt to settle their computer-chip patent dispute.

---

Armco expects a second-quarter loss and may omit its dividend due to problems in its reinsurance business.

---

Southern Co. units will receive $300 million before taxes in a settlement with Gulf States Utilities of a dispute over power sales contracts.

---

Melville agreed to buy Imasco's Peoples unit for $330 million to create the fifth-largest U.S. drugstore chain.
