Business Brief -- Occidental Petroleum Corp.: Estimate of Oil Reserves In North Sea Field Lifted

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/26/90

Occidental Petroleum (Caledonia) Ltd., a London unit of Los Angeles-based Occidental Petroleum Corp., said a step-out, or appraisal, well in the Saltire field in the North Sea indicates the field's estimated recoverable oil reserves are 30% greater than formerly believed, or about 130 million barrels. Previously, the field's recoverable reserves of high-quality oil were estimated at 100 million barrels.

Occidental said the appraisal well had flows at stabilized daily rates of 9,635 barrels of oil and 13 million cubic feet of gas.

The Occidental unit said the test-well results also indicate that the Saltire field, about 120 miles northeast of Aberdeen, Scotland, is larger than previously believed. As a result, the company will seek Britain's approval to develop the field, starting next fall, at an estimated cost of $600 million. Production is expected to begin in the second half of 1992. Occidental is the well operator and holds a 36.5% interest in it.
