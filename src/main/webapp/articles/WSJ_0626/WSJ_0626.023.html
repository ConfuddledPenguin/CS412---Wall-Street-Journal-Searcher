GE Capital Plans to Offer Financing to Ailing Firms

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/26/90

STAMFORD, Conn. -- General Electric Capital Corp. said it plans to join the parade of securities firms, banks and law firms in the burgeoning field of offering financing to overly indebted companies. The financing from GE Capital, a unit of General Electric Co., would allow distressed companies to reduce their debt before or after seeking bankruptcy-law protection from creditors.

Jeffrey H. Coats, head of GE Capital's new restructuring group, said the company can move faster than banks in such situations because it can offer equity financing, as well as debt financing, and doesn't necessarily need to redistribute the debt. He said GE Capital plans to expand the number of people devoted to restructuring from 12 to 20 by the end of the year.

Recently, he said, GE Capital provided $50 million in credit to L.J. Hooker Corp. for use prior to the liquidation of its Bonwit-Teller and B. Altman retail units. GE also financed a $75 million management buy-out of Hooker's Merksamer Jewelers unit.
