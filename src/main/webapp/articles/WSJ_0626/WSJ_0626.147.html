Congress Clears Bill On Amtrak Subsidies For Next Two Years

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/26/90

WASHINGTON (AP) -- Congress passed an Amtrak authorization bill after removing the railroad anti-takeover provision that drew a veto from President Bush. The bill, approved by voice votes in the House and Senate and sent to the White House, caps federal subsidies for the government-owned passenger railroad at $684 million in the fiscal year beginning Oct. 1 and at $712 million the following year.

In vetoing an earlier version, Mr. Bush said he accepted the spending figures but objected to a requirement that the Interstate Commerce Commission review proposed acquisitions of the 16 largest freight railroads by non-railroad companies. The House voted to override the veto, but the Senate narrowly failed to get the needed two-thirds majority to override.

"Because the president's concerns have been addressed . . . the current version of the bill is acceptable to the administration," the White House said.

Rep. Thomas Luken (D., Ohio), chairman of the House subcommittee on transportation, said the bill authorizes enough money for Amtrak to replace and upgrade its passenger cars. The actual appropriations, however, still must be determined in a separate spending bill.

In addition to Amtrak service, the bill would clear legal impediments to starting commuter rail service between northern Virginia and the District of Columbia.
