NFL Players Sue League In Dispute Over Licensing

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/26/90

NEW YORK -- The National Football League Players Association filed a lawsuit accusing the NFL of illegally interfering with the union's licensing business. In a suit filed in federal court in Manhattan, the union accused the NFL of intimidating potential licensees to stop doing business with the players' association. It also accused the league of enticing individual players into breaking their licensing agreements with the association.

The suit claims that the NFL is trying to cripple the union financially by hurting its licensing business, the union's main source of revenue. Last year, licensing brought in $6.1 million in revenue for the players' association, the suit said.

The suit seeks at least $40 million in damages for the alleged antitrust violations.

The suit accused the NFL of threatening the union's licensees by telling them they wouldn't be allowed to use the league's own trademarks unless they broke their licensing agreements with the players' association. It said the NFL offered to protect the licensees from any legal actions the union might take as a result of abandoned agreements.

The suit also charged that the league enticed several individual players into breaking their agreements with the association by paying the players $8,500 to $100,000 each to assign licensing rights to NFL's own licensing arm.

NFL officials couldn't be reached for comment.
