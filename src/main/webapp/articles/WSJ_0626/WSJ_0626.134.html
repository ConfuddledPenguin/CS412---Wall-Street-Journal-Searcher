Business Brief -- Hamilton Robinson & Co.: New York Investment Firm Acquires Deutz-Allis Corp.

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/26/90

Hamilton Robinson & Co., New York, said it has purchased Deutz-Allis Corp. from Klockner-Humboldt-Deutz of Cologne, West Germany, in a transaction valued at about $100 million. The transaction was financed through equity provided by Hamilton Robinson, a private investment firm, and the sale of Deutz-Allis's receivables to Whirlpool Financial Corp. Deutz-Allis managers will have an equity interest in the new company, which will be private. Hamilton Robinson didn't break down the interests or disclose the receivables.

Deutz-Allis of Atlanta, which has annual sales of $300 million, makes combines and distributes farm implements and tractors. Before 1985, it was part of Allis Chalmers Corp. Klockner-Humboldt-Deutz is an industrial machinery company.

Hamilton Robinson manages a $40 million fund for management buy-outs and other investments.
