Dividend News: Community National Bancorp Sets Payout, Its First in 14 Years

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/26/90

NEW YORK -- Community National Bancorp Inc. directors declared a 5% stock dividend, the first payout in 14 years by the Staten Island, N.Y., holding company for Community National Bank & Trust Co. of New York. The dividend is payable July 25 to stock of record July 3.

At the annual meeting, Community National shareholders also approved a proposal to reduce authorized shares to 15 million from 50 million. The move will reduce the franchise tax the bank pays in Delaware, where it is incorporated; the tax is partly based on the number of shares a company is authorized to issue. The cut in authorized shares doesn't affect shares outstanding. Community National has around three million shares outstanding.

The bank reiterated that second-quarter earnings will drop from the year-ago period but that it is hoping for an upturn in the second half.

In the first quarter, net income fell 73% to $211,000, or seven cents a share, from $793,000, or 30 cents a share, a year earlier. The bank's provision for possible losses on bad loans grew to $1.3 million from $200,000. Assets climbed 32% to $408 million.

In American Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Community National shares rose 25 cents to $6.75.
