Letters to the Editor: Business Is Business, Not a Big Nursemaid

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/26/90

If the quote from Rep. Patricia Schroeder in your May 16 Notable & Quotable is accurate, then Rep. Schroeder is woefully ignorant of the historical role of business in a capitalist society if she believes the role of corporate America is to act as a public service rather than to buy and sell its businesses. In fact, the role of corporate America is to do business profitably and legally by acting in its own best interest, which may or may not involve buying and selling its businesses. This proper corporate behavior has, for the most part, proved mutually beneficial to the public and the corporations, and has provided the greatest good for the greatest number of people. Rep. Schroeder must be talking about the role of business in the Soviet Union, where, philosophically, business acts as a public service and has helped produce the standard of living the Soviet people enjoy today.

Davide Cutler

Student

School of Continuing Education

New York University

New York
