Ex-Postal Aide's Suit Against Recognition Equipment Is Settled

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/26/90

DALLAS -- Recognition Equipment Inc. said it settled a lawsuit filed by James V. Jellison, a former senior assistant postmaster general, charging that the company, two former executives and others tried to remove him from his position with the Postal Service. Under terms of the out-of-court settlement, the manufacturer of document processing equipment said it paid an undisclosed amount in return for the release of all claims against the company and the former executives, William G. Moore Jr., a former chief executive officer, and Robert W. Reedy, a former vice president.

The suit, filed in federal court in Washington, was based on an indictment against Recognition and the two officers, alleging that they tried to manipulate the award of $400 million in Postal Service contracts. A federal judge subsequently acquitted all the defendants in the case.
