New Securities Issues

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/26/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATES

World Savings -- $100 million of 9.90% notes due July 1, 2000, priced at 99.438 to yield 9.99%. The notes, which are non-callable for 8 3/4 years, were priced at a spread of 145 basis points above the Treasury's 10-year note. Rated singleA-2 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and single-A-minus by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets.

EUROBONDS

Metropolis of Tokyo (Japan) -- $175 million of 9 1/8% Eurobonds due July 18, 1995, priced at 101.80 to yield 9.14% less fees via IBJ International Ltd. Fees 1 7/8.

Austrian Industries (Austria) -- three billion Austrian schillings (one billion schilling international tranche) of 6% bonds due July 3, 1995, priced at par via lead managers Creditanstalt-Bankverein. If the company issues equity, bondholders can exchange each bond for one warrant that will allow purchase of a share with nominal value of 1,000 schillings. Exercise period will be 13 months beginning from the date of listing on the Vienna Stock Exchange. Alternately, bondholders can opt to redeem the bonds at 108.5. If no equity is issued, redemption price will be 118.5. Fees 1.675.
