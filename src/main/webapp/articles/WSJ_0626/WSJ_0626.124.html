Technology Brief -- Microsoft Corp.: New Software Sales Exceed Forecasts, Company Says

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/26/90

Microsoft Corp. says sales of its new software product Windows 3.0 have so far exceeded its most agressive predictions. The Redmond, Wash,-based software company didn't disclose its internal forecasts or say how much sales outstripped expectations. The new software tool was introduced with fanfare just five weeks ago.

However, David Readerman, an analyst with Shearson Lehman Bros. said he expects Microsoft to sell 500,000 units of Windows 3.0 between its May 22 introduction date and June 30.

In response to the crush of demand, Microsoft said it is establishing a 24-hour hotline providing user support for the new product. The company said it also plans to add 60 additional people to its support staff to handle consumer needs.
