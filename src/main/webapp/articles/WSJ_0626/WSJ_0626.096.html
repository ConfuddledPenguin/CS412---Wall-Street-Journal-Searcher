Who's News: Chevron Corp.

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/26/90

CHEVRON Corp. (San Francisco) -- The company's Chevron U.S.A. Inc. subsidiary named seven vice president/general managers to head new business units for exploration, land and production. The positions were created as part of a restructuring of the domestic oil and natural gas subsidiary, based in San Francisco. John Cameron, vice president of the western region, was named vice president, western production business unit. Gene Thomas, manager of the Denver division of western region production, becomes vice president, Rocky Mountain production business unit. Jim Hooks, general manager, exploration, for the eastern region, becomes vice president, western exploration business unit. Will Lewis, general manager of production in the central region, becomes vice president, central production business unit. Burt Branstetter, regional counsel in Chevron U.S.A.'s law department, becomes vice president, land business unit. Bob Herrin Jr., vice president of manufacturing for Warren Petroleum Co., a Chevron subsidiary, becomes vice president, Permian Basin production business unit. Dave Lott Jr., general manager of exploration and land at the Chevron U.S.A. headquarters staff, becomes vice president, eastern exploration business unit. The appointments are effective Aug. 1.
