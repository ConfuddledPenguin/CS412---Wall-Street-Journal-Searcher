Hawaiian Airlines Attempts to Block Rival Through Suits

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/26/90

HONOLULU -- Hawaiian Airlines said it filed suits aimed at blocking a competitor, Discovery Airways. The HAL Inc. unit said it is seeking to recover $9.9 million in alleged damages and asking for a federal investigation of the air carrier, which it claims is un-American because of its reliance on Japanese loans.

In a two-pronged litigation, Hawaiian Airlines filed one suit in federal court charging that Discovery violated the Federal Aviation Act, and another suit in the First Circuit Court of Hawaii charging Discovery with unfair competition.

Hawaiian's attempt to stop its rival's operations is based on an assertion that Discovery is "foreign owned, and therefore not an American corporation."

Discovery officials couldn't immediately be reached for comment. Discovery was formed last summer by ex-airline executives who announced they wanted to enter the inter-island air carrier business.

Hawaiian claims it has lost $3.3 million in revenue since Discovery began operations and asks the state court to award it that amount in actual damages, plus an additional $6.6 million in general and punitive damages.

In addition, Hawaiian says it will ask the Justice Department to investigate Discovery's actions before the Department of Transportation.

HAL has weathered a turbulent year so far. Former baseball commissioner Peter Ueberroth completed his $37 million takeover of HAL in January. In April, the company disclosed plunging passenger traffic on Hawaiian Airlines in the wake of extended aircraft downtime and reduced capacity on inter-island routes.

As a result, HAL posted a loss of $46.9 million, or $24 a share, for 1989, more than five times the year-earlier loss of $8.8 million, or $4.48 a share.
