Dutch Retail Sales Rise 6%

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/26/90

AMSTERDAM -- Dutch retail sales in April increased a provisional 6% from the year before, the Dutch Central Bureau of Statistics reported. The 6% gain doesn't take into account increases caused by inflation, which pushed prices 2.2% higher in April, compared with April 1989.

The strong rise in retail sales occurred despite the fact that the long Easter weekend fell in April this year and in March last year, the government said.

Department store sales, however, fell 4.1%.

The strongest growth came in the food sector, where sales rose 7.7% from a year earlier. Sales in the non-food sector advanced just 5.5%, but sales of household articles jumped 18.5%. Sales of flowers and plants rose 12.7% and drugstore sales increased 10.7%.

In the food sector, sales of meat were up 5.1%, sales of groceries added 5.8% and sales of milk and dairy products climbed 5.3%, the statistics bureau reported.
