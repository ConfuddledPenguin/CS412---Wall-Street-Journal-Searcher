Marketing & Media: Carolco Pays $3 Million For Original Screenplay

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/26/90

HOLLYWOOD -- The dizzying escalation in movie script prices hit its highest water mark yet, as Carolco Pictures Inc. paid $3 million for an original screenplay. The price paid for the script, "Basic Instinct" by former journalist Joe Eszterhas, surpasses the $1.75 million paid by Geffen Co. for Shane Black's "The Last Boy Scout" in April.

According to a report in the trade paper Daily Variety confirmed by individuals close to the negotiations, the 24-hour auction began last Thursday when MGM/UA Communications Co., which had contractual first crack at the script, had to "pass" on it. MGM/UA's film activities have been slowed by a proposed acquisition by Pathe Communications Corp. Several major studios joined the bidding but Carolco, which recently produced "Total Recall," prevailed.

Mr. Eszterhas, author of "Flashdance," "Music Box" and "Betrayed," gained notoriety last year when he departed powerful Creative Artists Agency to rejoin his former agent Guy McElwaine at International Creative Management. Mr. Eszterhas wrote a heated letter, later publicized, accusing CAA chief Michael Ovitz of threatening him. Mr. Ovitz denied it.
