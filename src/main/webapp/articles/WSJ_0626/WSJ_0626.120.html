MGM Grand Air Plans An Upgraded Coach Class

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/26/90

BEVERLY HILLS, Calif. -- MGM Grand Air wants to ease one of the business traveler's greatest burdens: coach-class travel. The small transcontinental luxury airline said it will begin offering Aug. 16 a new, upgraded service for standard coach-class fare. For the standard unrestricted Los Angeles-New York coach fare of $623 one way, MGM Grand Air will offer such frills as roomy leather seats, telephones, video monitors and gourmet meals.

The airline, a unit of MGM Grand Inc., has offered a first-class service, at a current unrestricted fare of $1,017 one way, since September 1987.

The airline's newly reconfigured DC8 Super 62 aircraft will seat 79, with 39 passengers in first class and 40 in coach. Those planes will replace MGM Grand's 727-100s and will nearly double its capacity. The bigger planes prompted MGM's decision to offer the new service, rather than trying to fill all seats with first-class passengers.

The cheaper fare raises questions as to whether MGM Grand's current clientele will try to save a few bucks by moving to the back of the plane. Charles Demoney, the airline's president and chief executive officer, said the company believes those customers will still want first-class service.

Last year, the airline reported a load factor of 73.9%, and revenue of $30.2 million.
