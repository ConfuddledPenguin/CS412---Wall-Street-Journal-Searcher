Who's News: Citizens Utilities Picks Tow for Top 2 Posts

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/26/90

STAMFORD, CONN. -- Citizens Utilities Co. named Leonard Tow, a cable television and cellular telephone executive, chairman and chief executive of the utility and telecommunications company. As chairman, Mr. Tow fills a position vacant since last year. As chief executive, he succeeds Ishier Jacobson, who retires June 30.

Mr. Tow, 60 years old, will continue as chairman and chief executive of Century Communications Corp., New Canaan, Conn., which owns about 2.2% of the Series A common shares of Citizens Utilities.

Mr. Tow, who has been a director of Citizens for almost 15 months, said the two companies are "not linked from a standpoint of operations, but there is an interest in the field of cellular telephones." Asked how Mr. Tow will be able to run both companies, a spokeswoman said: "Management teams at both companies are very strong; they will run day-to-day operations."

As previously announced, Daryl A. Ferguson, 51, senior vice president of operations and engineering, succeeds Mr. Jacobson, 68, as president of Citizens. Mr. Ferguson was also named chief operating officer, effective July 1.
