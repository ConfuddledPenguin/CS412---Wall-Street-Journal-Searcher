Letters to the Editor: An Un-Trendy Sears? Totally Awesome

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/26/90

Your May 9 page-one article "Fashion Statement" provided this old Sears veteran a rare insight into the latest scheme to achieve a trendier image for the giant retailer. But I think you may have been misled into thinking something new is happening across the aisle from the paint cans and manure spreaders. I remember very well before my retirement three years ago that the same blandishments wafted reassuringly from the lips of a parade of successive senior managers and fashion consultants. Can it indeed be possible to reinvent the wheel?

If Sears, Roebuck & Co. ever decides to stick with any strategy long enough to see it work, the power of that venerable merchandising organization will be awesome. But in fact, Sears has tinkered with so many, often-conflicting, strategies since the early 1970s (and earlier) that it should come as no surprise that it is struggling in today's hostile competitive environment.

Lloyd S. VanSchoyck

Palm Harbor, Fla.
