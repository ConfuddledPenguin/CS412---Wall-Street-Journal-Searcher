Business Brief -- Ciba-Geigy AG: Present Currency Rates Could Push Down Profits

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/26/90

Ciba-Geigy AG, a Swiss chemical group, said present currency rates are making it harder to reach management's 1990 goal of at least matching last year's profit. Hans-Peter Schaer, chief financial officer, declined to make more specific profit forecasts, saying it was still too early.

Profits will hinge on developments in currency rates and on the company's efforts to control costs, Schaer said, adding that to match last year's group net profit of 1.557 billion Swiss francs ($1.10 billion) is the company's goal.

Schaer said Ciba-Geigy was not yet planning investment in factories and fixed assets in Eastern Europe. However, the company is boosting its marketing and is trading with companies there.

Speaking on Eastern Europe to a Zurich business group, Schaer later predicted that financial risks of investment and trade will increase for a period, then gradually diminish, as Eastern European countries reform their economies.

For large companies with a long-term view, he said, "the cost of lost opportunities is in all likelihood larger than the risks to be taken."
