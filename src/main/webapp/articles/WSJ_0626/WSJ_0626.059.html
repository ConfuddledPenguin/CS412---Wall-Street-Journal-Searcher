Marketing & Media: Conde Nast Buys Cook's Magazine And Will Fold It

  • Author: Patrick M. Reilly
  • Date: 06/26/90

Conde Nast Publications Inc. said it acquired Cook's magazine with the intent of folding the publication and adding its subscriber base to its own upscale food monthly, Gourmet. Cook's joins the growing list of magazines that are either closing down or being folded into other magazines in an increasingly difficult advertising climate. Cook's is published by Pennington Publishing Inc., a unit of the Stockholm-based Bonnier Group, one of the largest media companies in Scandinavia.

Financial terms weren't disclosed.

Cook's guaranteed to advertisers a circulation of about 225,000, but in the past year had failed in several issues to meet that guarantee. At Cook's, close to 30 editorial and sales staffers are expected to lose their jobs.

A glossy, sleekly designed magazine started in 1979, Cook's targeted well-heeled readers who liked to cook their own gourmet meals and entertain stylishly. However, Cook's has been unable to compete effectively in a crowded field that includes such upscale epicurean magazines as Gourmet, Knapp Communication Corp.'s Bon Appetit, American Express Publishing Corp.'s Food & Wine, and Telemedia Inc.'s Eating Well.

The factors leading to Cook's demise, however, aren't exclusive to the food magazine category. Cook's Publisher Bob Young said that like other consumer magazines, Cook's profits were squeezed by aggressive rate negotiating on the part of advertising agencies and their clients during a general advertising slump.

"Unlike past recessions in the magazine industry, magazines are cutting whatever deal they can with advertisers. Because of it, the revenues per page are plummeting," said Mr. Young. "Even those magazines that are on the crest are being pressured to offer deals."

Small to midsized national magazines have been hardest hit by rate negotiation coupled with slashed advertisers' ad budgets. Last week, after losing as much as $10 million, Metrocorp closed down Manhattan, inc. and combined its staff with Fairchild Publications' M magazine. Other recently folded magazines include Stern Publications' 7 Days, Time Warner Inc.'s Southpoint, and CMP Publications' Long Island Monthly. Several publications, such as Family Media Inc.'s Taxi and Model, and New American Magazine Co.'s Psychology Today, have suspended publication for now with promises to readers and advertisers of being relaunched.

"Our closing just reflects the tenor of the times," said Cook's Mr. Young.

Corrections & Amplifications

CONDE NAST PUBLICATIONS Inc. acquired the subscription list of Cook's magazine, and not the magazine as reported in yesterday's edition. Cook's publisher, Pennington Publishing Inc., had previously decided to close the magazine after efforts to find a buyer failed.

(WSJ June 27, 1990)
