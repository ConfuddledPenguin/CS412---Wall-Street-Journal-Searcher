AIG to Buy ILF For $1.3 Billion In Cash, Stock --- Chairman Says Acquisition Part of Plan to Expand Financial Services Group

  • Author: Beatrice E. Garcia and Rick Wartzman
  • Date: 06/26/90

American International Group Inc. said it agreed to acquire aircraft lessor International Lease Finance Corp. in a cash-and-stock transaction valued at about $1.3 billion. International Lease Finance, based in Beverly Hills, Calif., is one of the fastest-growing companies in the jetliner remarketing business. But, by nature, it has a heavy debt structure and is eager to tap into the giant pool of cash that American International, one of the world's largest insurance and financial-services firms, is able to provide.

"With the tremendous appetite for capital that we have, this is a very appropriate merger," said Steven Udvar-Hazy, International Lease Finance's president. In fact, as part of the transaction, American International plans to provide its new subsidiary up to $125 million in financing.

Maurice Greenberg, chairman of New York-based American International, said the acquisition is part of the insurer's strategy to expand its financial-services group, which was formed in 1987. American International is already in the interest-rate and currency swap business, as well as in specialty financing and office equipment leasing. It also recently formed a commodity trading operation, known as AIG Trading, with three former Drexel Burnham Lambert Inc. officials.

Mr. Greenberg said International Lease Finance "provides a diversified, rapidly growing source of earnings to AIG," adding that he believes the International Lease Finance purchase and the formation of AIG Trading will help the financial-services group double its operating profit next year.

Dan Hersh, who tracks International Lease Finance for the Los Angeles brokerage Bateman Eichler, Hill Richards Inc., said he thinks that having American International as a parent will give International Lease Finance more flexibility in the way it peddles its portfolio of aircraft, which now consists of 98 airplanes with an asset value exceeding $3 billion.

"They can take bigger risks with credit now that they're out of the public market," he explained, adding that being a "quasi-private" subsidiary will make them more akin to their chief rival, GPA Group Ltd., of Shannon, Ireland.

Mr. Udvar-Hazy said that International Lease Finance had been looking for a "strategic combination" since late last year and was first introduced to American International in March. Becoming a part of such a cash-rich concern, he asserted, should "lower the company's funding costs and, in turn, allow us to lower our leasing rates to our clients."

International Lease Finance has been highly profitable, riding a world-wide wave in air travel and airplane sales. Just yesterday, the company reported that second-quarter earnings soared 57% to $24.7 million, or 64 cents a share, from a year-earlier $15.7 million, or 41 cents a share. Revenue for the period ended May 31 jumped 28% to a record $111.7 million from $87.1 million.

In 1989, International Lease Finance earned $61.2 million. American International's financial-services group posted operating net of $149.8 million last year.

American International said it will use internal sources to fund the cash portion of the acquisition, and Mr. Greenberg added that the buy-out would cause "virtually no dilution" of American International's earnings this year.

Terms of the agreement call for International Lease Finance holders to receive either $32.50 in cash or 50 cents in cash plus 0.3 American International common share for each International Lease Finance share outstanding, subject to a maximum number of shares and certain other adjustments.

According to the deal between the two companies, if the market price of American International's stock exceeds $104 a share during a specified period prior to the merger, the exchange rate will be adjusted so that the value of the American International common stock portion will equal $31.20 per International Lease Finance share. Similarly, if American International's stock price is below $86 at the time of the closing, the value of the American International stock portion will be adjusted to equal $25.80 per International Lease Finance share.

The acquisition is still subject to International Lease Finance shareholder approval, the delivery of various opinions on the tax consequences of the move and other conditions. Certain terms also might have to be adjusted to reflect American International's previously announced 5-for-4 stock split, to be paid as a 25% stock dividend on July 23. The merger is nonetheless expected to close in the fourth quarter.

The financial markets closed yesterday before the acquisition was announced and before International Lease Finance disclosed its second-quarter results. American International shares closed at $96.625 down $2.375 in New York Stock Exchange composite trading. International Lease Finance shares fell 50 cents to $27.125 in national over-the-counter trading. Some short-sellers have been playing the company's stock of late, expecting the aircraft buying boom to slow and International Lease Finance's bubble to burst.

The aircraft-leasing company will operate as a wholly owned unit of American International, though it will remain an indpendent debt issuer. International Lease Finance currently has on the market about $1.5 billion in commercial paper and medium-term notes.

Mr. Udvar-Hazy will remain president of the unit, which will maintain its headquarters in Beverly Hills. Louis Gonda will remain executive vice president. And Leslie Gonda will keep his post as International Lease Finance chairman, while also becoming an American International board member. All three men, who together founded International Lease Finance in 1973 and collectively control more than 50% of the company's 36 million shares outstanding, said they have opted to take stakes in American International.
