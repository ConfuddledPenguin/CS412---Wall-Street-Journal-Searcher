Metropolitan Life Wins Round in Fight Over RJR's Bonds

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/26/90

NEW YORK -- Metropolitan Life Insurance Co. won a skirmish in its court battle concerning bonds of RJR Nabisco Inc. that were issued before the food and tobacco company's late-1988 leveraged buy-out. Met Life, which owns about $240 million of RJR's old bonds, filed suit in 1988 arguing that the buy-out hurt the value of the old bonds and broke pledges made to bondholders. Among the remedies sought by Met Life was early repayment of the bonds, some of which don't come due until 2016.

A federal appeals court overturned a lower-court ruling to which Met Life had objected. Met Life wants to start a 60-day clock running in which RJR must cure the alleged default on its bonds or face a series of steps that bondholders can take. RJR had asked a lower court to, in effect, freeze the clock until its claim can be heard that there hasn't been any default.

The lower court had granted RJR an open-ended injunction. But yesterday's appeals court ruling disallowed that injunction and directed the lower court to resume proceedings.

A decisive verdict in the Met Life suit still is a long way off. Both sides are expected to file briefs with the federal district court this summer. The district judge, Michael B. Mukasey, isn't expected to hear the case until October and isn't expected to rule until early 1991. Whatever his verdict, it's likely to be appealed, and that could take another 18 months or longer.
