Seven of 8 VMS Funds to Continue Distribution Ban

  • Author: Andrew Patner
  • Date: 06/26/90

CHICAGO -- All but one of the eight publicly traded funds originally sponsored by VMS Realty Partners said they would continue to suspend distributions to shareholders despite recouping about $108 million from the ailing syndicator this year. At the first annual meetings since the unravelling of the syndication giant began earlier this year, Leonard G. Levine, president and chief executive officer of the funds, told shareholders of three of the funds that no distributions would be made because of pending class-action lawsuits against the funds.

One fund, VMS Strategic Land Trust, will pay a special dividend of 21 cents a share and a regular quarterly dividend of 10 cents a share, amounting to about $3.7 million in distributions. Mr. Levine said the trust could be liquidated "in the not-too-distant future."

In addition to the litigation, Mr. Levine told shareholders that the funds needed to conserve their cash because VMS continued to withhold mortgage payments to the funds and because existing loans may have foreclosure costs or require payments to senior lenders. Mr. Levine characterized the situation as "terribly unfortunate."

In an interview, Mr. Levine, a former officer of VMS who helped structure the funds, said that negotiations with VMS's chief operating officer, Peter T. Cyrus, take place about "friendly foreclosures" on a "daily basis." The funds haven't yet taken possession of any VMS properties, Mr. Levine said, although VMS is making payments on "less than five" of some 100 properties.

Mr. Levine told shareholders that the discussions were often "very heated. VMS is trying not to pay anything."

The eight funds, including five real estate investment trusts, or REITs, and three master limited partnerships, or MLPs, raised $1.1 billion from about 100,000 investors in the late 1980s to make loans exclusively to VMS and its affiliates.

VMS, which boasted as recently as last February that it was one of the world's largest and most successful real estate management and development firms, disclosed major cash problems that month and the independent directors took control of the eight funds.

Xerox Corp., which owns a 25% stake in VMS, took a $400 million first-quarter charge to reflect its investment loss in the partnership.

Mr. Levine said the funds had seized control of secured cash accounts and "significant amounts of interest reserves" at VMS to protect against "the unfortunate event of any bankruptcy by VMS." Mr. Levine, who hasn't any current affiliation with VMS, said the partnership promoter was "in a state of winding down."

The funds also are in "daily contact" with Joel A. Stone, VMS's president, over unsecured VMS assets, including Rockresorts, the Boca Raton Hotel and Club in Boca Raton, Fla., and the Omni Park Central Hotel in New York City, Mr. Levine said. In addition, the funds are attempting to gain control over guarantee funds established by VMS to make distribution payments to shareholders. Mr. Stone couldn't be reached for comment.

Mr. Levine said the funds would continue their recent profitability "but not at the same level as the first quarter" when VMS was still making interest payments for two months. He said the funds would move from being "exclusively mortgage REITs" to a "hybrid" of "mostly equity and some mortgages" as they resolve their demands on VMS.

In other business, shareholders overwhelmingly re-elected the independent directors of the Land Trust, VMS Mortgage Investment Fund and VMS Strategic Land Fund II. The two other REITs meet Tuesday. The three MLPs don't hold annual meetings.
