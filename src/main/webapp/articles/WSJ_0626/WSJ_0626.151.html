LTV Unit Officials Receive Suspensions Amid Federal Review

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/26/90

DALLAS -- The president and four other top employees of a division of LTV Corp.'s Missiles and Electronics Group were suspended indefinitely pending the completion of a review by the federal government. The five men work for the Buffalo, N.Y., Sierra Research division of the LTV unit. An LTV spokesman said the division develops radar and other defense electronics, and its primary government contracts are with the Navy and Air Force.

The spokesman wouldn't comment on the nature of the government review of Sierra Research programs. He said the suspensions, which were effective Friday, are intended as "a prudent measure . . . to make sure everything is clean and on the up and up." The spokesman said none of the five men has been charged "in any way" with wrongdoing.

In a letter to employees of the LTV Missiles and Electronics Group, group President Robert N. Parker said, "these suspensions are with pay and are not intended to prejudge any involvement of these individuals in the matters being reviewed by the government."

Mr. Parker said he will assume the duties of suspended President Tom Cobb. Also suspended were Stu Penny, vice president of business development; Roger Witt, vice president of operations; Gene Streips, a director of business development, and project engineer Jack Borkowski. Mr. Witt declined to comment, and the others couldn't be reached for comment.
