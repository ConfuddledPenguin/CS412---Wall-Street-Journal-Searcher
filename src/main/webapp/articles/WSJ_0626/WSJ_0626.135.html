Law: Trader Gets Prison Term For Rigging Transactions

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/26/90

NEW YORK -- A securities trader accused of helping set up $4 billion in rigged U.S. securities transactions was sentenced to one year and a day in prison for conspiring to defraud the U.S. The trader, Paul Foont, 51 years old, of Manhattan, pleaded guilty in April. Jeffrey Feldman, 42, of Skillman, N.J., also pleaded guilty in the scheme and is to be sentenced July 5 in federal court in Manhattan.

Mr. Foont was sentenced by U.S. District Judge Charles Haight. He could be eligible for parole after serving four months.

Mr. Foont was head trader for a series of limited partnerships called the Cralin partnerships. Mr. Feldman was a principal and promoter of the partnerships. The U.S. attorney's office in Manhattan charged that the partnerships were used in the pre-arranged transactions to create phony losses for tax deductions of about $140 million. The partnerships were marketed to investors for their tax benefits, and investors didn't know that the tax losses weren't legitimate, prosecutors said.

Because the crime occurred in the early 1980s, new federal sentencing guidelines don't apply in this case. Assistant U.S. Attorney Robert Cleary said that under the new guidelines, Mr. Foont would have been sentenced to 63 to 78 months in prison.
