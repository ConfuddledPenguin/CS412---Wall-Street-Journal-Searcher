Letters to the Editor: Pulitzer Keeps Track Of What's on the Boards

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/26/90

Edwin Wilson is, of course, correct in noting that the production each year of 200 new plays at theaters all across the U.S. makes the task of Pulitzer Prize drama jurors a difficult one ("Good Regional Play Points Up Bad Pulitzer Problem," Leisure & Arts, June 14). Like Mr. Wilson, the Pulitzer Prize Board is keenly aware of this situation and continually seeks ways to ensure that regional theater is covered as thoroughly as possible. For some years now it has encouraged its drama jurors to travel at board expense to regional theaters that appear to have particularly promising offerings. And this year, in keeping with a decision taken last November, it has increased the number of drama jurors from three to five in order to make possible a broader geographic representation. Between these measures and the fact that as professional critics our drama jurors can reasonably be presumed to be aware of what is going on at theaters in their various regions of the country, I would submit that the Pulitzer selection system is a good deal more equitable and comprehensive than Mr. Wilson recognizes.

Robert C. Christopher

Administrator

The Pulitzer Prizes

Columbia University

New York
