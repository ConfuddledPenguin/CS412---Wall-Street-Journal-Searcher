Business Brief -- Jackson Products Inc.: Concern Arranges Financing Including Debt and Equity

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/26/90

Jackson Products Inc., Dallas, said it has arranged permanent financing of $80.4 million in the form of senior term, subordinated term and revolving credit facilities as well as common equity capital. The credit facilities were provided by Westinghouse Credit Corp. of Pittsburgh, which will also be an equity participant with Haas & Partners, Thermadyne Industries Inc. of St. Louis, and James Mills, the chairman and chief executive officer of Thermadyne Holding Corp., parent of Thermadyne Industries. Westinghouse Credit is a unit of Westinghouse Electric Corp. Thermadyne Industries, principally owned by Haas and Mills, is a leading domestic manufacturer of products and accessories used in cutting and welding metal. Haas & Partners is a Dallas-based private investment firm.

Jackson Products designs, manufactures and distributes safety equipment for the welding, construction and general industrial markets. The company was acquired by Haas and Mills last October for $67 million. Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette Securities Corp. provided the bridge loan for the acquisition which is being replaced by the permanent financing.
