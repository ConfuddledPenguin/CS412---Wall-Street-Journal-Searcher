Japan's Crude-Steel Output

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/26/90

TOKYO -- Reflecting robust domestic demand, Japan's crude steel production in the July-September quarter will grow 4.1% from a year earlier to 27.8 million metric tons, the highest level for the quarter in 10 years, the Ministry of International Trade and Industry predicted. A metric ton is equal to about 2,204.62 pounds. Ministry officials estimated total domestic steel consumption in the July-September quarter will be 19.4 million metric tons, up 3.3% from a year earlier and the second-highest level on record.
