Credit Ratings: New Jersey Issues Removed From S&P's CreditWatch

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/26/90

NEW YORK -- Standard & Poor's Corp. removed New Jersey's triple-A general obligation and related agency bonds from a list of state issues being watched for possible downgrade, after the state Legislature passed a series of revenue-raising measures. On March 19, S&P had placed New Jersey on its CreditWatch list with negative implications, after Gov. James Florio's budget indicated a "substantial gap-closing" measure would be needed to balance the budget. Over the weekend, the Legislature approved a package that included a $1.5 billion increase in revenue from a boost in the state's sales taxes and a $1.3 billion increase in revenue from a rise in the state's income tax.

"We had fiscal concerns that they took quick action to correct," an S&P spokesman said.

S&P's latest move means that New Jersey will remain one of only eight states whose debt is rated triple-A by both S&P and Moody's Investors Service Inc.
