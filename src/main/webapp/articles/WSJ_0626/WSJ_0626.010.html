Marketing & Media: Journal's Letters Editor Wins Stamp of Approval

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/26/90

NEW YORK -- Ned Crabb, editor of The Wall Street Journal's letters column, won a special recognition for headline writing. Mr. Crabb received a Wuxtry award from the International Society for General Semantics, San Francisco, and its quarterly, Et cetera. The Wuxtry award is named for the cry of newsboys at the turn of the century.

Some of Mr. Crabb's clever headlines included "Apocalypse Soon" over a letter expressing concern about the effect of crack and other drugs on America, in response to a Wall Street Journal front-page story; and "Woodstock: Monday Morning Coming Down" over a letter about one person's disappointment at the music festival two decades ago.

Mr. Crabb is the author of a novel, "Ralph," and a contributor of humorous essays to the editorial page.
