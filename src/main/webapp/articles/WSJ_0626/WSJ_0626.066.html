Letters to the Editor: Not a `Lost' Language

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/26/90

Your May 16 page-one article on the return of the Cornish language says, "Only one other lost tongue has been successfully revived to everyday use -- Hebrew -- and it had the full weight of the state of Israel behind it." Hebrew was never a lost language in the sense that Cornish was. While Hebrew fell from conversational use for more than a thousand years, throughout that time it continued to be the language of religion, study and intellectual dialogue, much as Latin had been in Western civilization up to the 16th century. All educated Jews could read and speak at least enough Hebrew to get by in synagogue. It did not require "the full weight of the state of Israel" to bring it back to colloquial use, but, like the Cornish, only the will of the people who lived there.

Elliott Milstein

West Bloomfield, Mich.
