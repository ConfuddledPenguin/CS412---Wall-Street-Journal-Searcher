OECD's Unemployment Rate

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/26/90

PARIS -- The average unemployment rate for 16 of the world's most industrialized countries rose to 6% of the work force in April from 5.9% in March, the Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development said. The latest result represents only the second time the OECD's standard unemployment rate has risen since a general downward trend emerged in 1986 to bring the rate down 1.8 percentage points over three years. But the organization said the rate has been steady at 6% since last December, except for the March decline to 5.9%.

The sharpest unemployment gains in April were posted by the U.S. and Finland, with marginal rises recorded by Canada, Japan, Australia and Italy. The unemployment situation improved somewhat in France and Sweden, however.
