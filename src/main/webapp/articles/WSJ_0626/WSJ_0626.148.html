International: Koito Claims Pickens Is Greenmailing Firm For Kitaro Watanabe

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/26/90

TOKYO -- Koito Manufacturing Co. claimed it has evidence that its largest shareholder, T. Boone Pickens Jr., is trying to greenmail it. Mr. Pickens dismissed the accusation and said he plans to buy more Koito stock. Through Texas-based Boone Co., Mr. Pickens has a 26% stake in Koito. But the company has repeatedly rejected his demand for representation on its board.

Yesterday, it released a copy of what it said was Boone Co.'s 1989 Franchise Tax Report to the state of Delaware, which showed total gross assets of about $1.9 million as of Dec. 31. Koito said that as of that same date, Boone Co. was registered on Koito's books as the owner of 39.4 million of the company's shares, with a market value at the time of about $950 million.

Koito asserts that the difference between the two figures reflects its contention that Boone Co. isn't the beneficial owner of the shares and is acting as an agent for Kitaro Watanabe, which Koito claims has tried to greenmail the company before. A greenmailer tries to force a company to buy back its shares at a price higher than the market price.

But Boone Co. officials released a copy of what they said was an amended filing, showing assets of more than $916.6 million. They said that they made a mistake in their initial filing, dated Feb. 21, by failing to include overseas holdings in Koito, and that the amended filing reflects the holdings.

"If they think they found a smoking gun, it just blew up in their face," Mr. Pickens said in a statement. He didn't say how many additional Koito shares he plans to buy, but he insists he is a long-term investor, not a greenmailer.
