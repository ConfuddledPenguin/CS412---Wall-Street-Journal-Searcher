Technology & Health: Hitachi, Motorola Agree to Pursue End to Patent Suit

  • Author: Marilyn Chase
  • Date: 06/26/90

Hitachi Ltd. and Motorola Inc., under pressure from a court deadline this week in their patent squabbles, have decided to try to work them out on their own. The two companies said they have agreed in principle to discussions aimed at ending a dispute involving the Hitachi H8/532 microprocessor and the Motorola MC 68030 microprocessor.

"This is not a final agreement," a Hitachi spokesman said, and it isn't expected to bring about a swift resolution of the 18-month-old litigation. "Hitachi would like to see an agreement in two to three months," he said.

Nor would it resolve any of the other intellectual property and technology disputes in which the two electronic giants have long been embroiled, the spokesman added.

A spokeswoman for Motorola, Schaumburg, Ill., said she couldn't discuss any details of the negotiations.

"As to what's on the table right now, we can't talk about any of it," she said.

The microprocessors at stake in the suit are lucrative sources of revenue for the combatants.

Hitachi doesn't sell the H8/532 yet in the U.S. However, it represents about 1% of Hitachi's world-wide semiconductor revenues, or $30 million. And Hitachi had counted on extending its marketing of the H8/532 to the U.S. in the near future, the spokesman said.

Motorola's market-leading MC 68030 microprocessor is used in powerful machines made by Apple Computer Inc. and Hewlett-Packard Co.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading, Hitachi closed at $95 a share, down $1.75 from Friday's close. Motorola closed at $84.125 a share, down $1.875.

On March 29, a federal court judge ruled that neither company could manufacture or distribute the disputed products in the U.S. because each side is infringing, the Hitachi spokesman said.

Motorola, for its part, obtained a temporary stay of the injunction against product sales. Last week, however, a judge vacated that stay in a setback to Motorola, which promptly appealed to the U.S. Court of Appeals in Washington.

That stay of judgment is due to expire Friday, increasing the pressure on both companies to reach a settlement out of court.
