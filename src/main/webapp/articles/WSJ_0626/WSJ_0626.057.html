Consumer Prices in Italy

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/26/90

MILAN, Italy -- Italy's consumer prices in June are likely to have risen about 5.6% from a year earlier, down slightly from the 5.7% year-to-year increase in May, according to provisional cost-of-living statistics from six major cities, which usually reflect a nationwide trend. Local inflation indexes in June rose between 0.2% and 0.9% from May and were up between 5.3% and 6.4% from a year earlier. These figures followed monthly increases between 0.3% and 0.5% in May, when the year-to-year increases varied from 5.2% to 6.5%. If the data are confirmed by the state, inflation would be at its lowest level since December 1988, when the consumer price index rose 5.5% from a year earlier.

Italy's overall consumer price index in May rose 0.3% from April and 5.7% from a year earlier.
