International Brief -- Unilever N.V.: Company Extends Its Offer For Margarinbolaget Stake

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/26/90

Anglo-Dutch food manufacturer Unilever N.V. said it has extended an offer to acquire the remaining 50% of Swedish food manufacturer Margarinbolaget AB, that Unilever doesn't already own. The offer is conditional on the approval of the transaction by the Swedish government and would take place sometime in January or February of 1991.

Under the terms of the offer, Unilever said it would increase the equity of Aritmos group of Sweden, which currently holds the other 50% stake in Margarinbolaget, by 650 million kronor ($107 million). The transfer of the 50% stake in Margarinbolaget to Unilever would allow Unilever to expand its food manufacturing capabilities in Northern Europe while giving Aritmos the equity to expand its sports and leisure products business.

Margarinbolaget, which has annual sales of around $247 million, sells a range of brand name margarines, frying products and dairy products.
