May Consumption Of Newsprint Rose An Estimated 3.2%

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/26/90

WASHINGTON -- U.S. newsprint consumption last month rose 3.2% to 1.070 million metric tons from 1.037 million metric tons in May 1989, according to preliminary estimates by the American Newspaper Publishers Association. The industry group said U.S. daily newspapers used 793,154 metric tons of newsprint in May, up 1.7% from 780,228 metric tons a year earlier. A metric ton equals 2,204.62 pounds.

Estimated newsprint consumption by all U.S. users in the first five months of 1990 was up 0.8% to 5.060 million metric tons from 5.020 million in the same period a year earlier. Consumption by daily newspapers for the first five months of 1990 was up 0.7% to 3.790 million metric tons from 3.764 million metric tons.

The publishers association said that newsprint inventories of newspapers in its statistical sample averaged a 40-day supply in May, compared with a 41-day supply in May 1989. It said stocks at North American mills decreased about 19.4% from a year earlier.

The association said U.S. newsprint mills operated at 95% of capacity in May and 97% during the first five months. Canadian mills operated at 97% of capacity in May and at 94% during the first five months of the year.
