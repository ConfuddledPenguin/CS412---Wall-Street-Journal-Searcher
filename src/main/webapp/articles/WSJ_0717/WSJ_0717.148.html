Business Brief -- Anchor Savings Bank: Deposits at 18 Banks Sold To Meet New Capital Rules

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/17/90

Anchor Savings Bank definitively agreed to sell $400 million of deposits at 18 of its Georgia branches to the Trust Co. of Georgia unit of SunTrust Banks Inc. Terms weren't disclosed, but the sale price will be released in public filings later, said John V. Brull, Anchor senior vice president and chief financial officer.

The savings bank has said that shedding its Georgia interests is part of its plan, approved by regulators in mid-April, to comply with new thrift-capital standards. Anchor wants to concentrate on the New York metropolitan area.

Trust Co. takes over the checking, savings and certificate of deposit accounts of Anchor's branches in Fulton, DeKalb, Douglas, Cobb, Gwinnett and Chatham counties. Trust Co. won't take over the real estate and leases, and Anchor will seek other buyers for them, Mr. Brull said. Anchor also will retain the unsecured loans from those branches in its portfolio.

Anchor, which entered the Georgia market in 1982, has six other branches in the state. Anchor has agreed to sell one of them to Home Federal Savings Bank of Atlanta and is close to a sale agreement on another, Mr. Brull said. The four others are for sale.
