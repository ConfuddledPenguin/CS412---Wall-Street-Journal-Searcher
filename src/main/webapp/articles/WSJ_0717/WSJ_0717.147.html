Florida Federal to Pay $17.9 Million to U.S. As Part of Settlement

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/17/90

TAMPA, Fla. -- Florida Federal Savings Bank, embroiled in a two-year investigation of its student loans, agreed to pay the U.S. $17.9 million as part of a civil settlement. The settlement, disclosed by a U.S. attorney in Florida, covers "false claims" filed with the Department of Education by the thrift under the Guaranteed Student Loan Program. That program makes low-interest loans available to students for college expenses.

In 1988, Florida Federal, St. Petersburg, Fla., came under scrutiny for allegedly filing as many as 17,000 fraudulent insurance claims on $35 million in government-backed student loans. The investigation helped spark a management shake-up that resulted in the resignation of the thrift's chairman.

Earlier this year, Florida Federal was fined $2,150,000 after its conviction on charges, including conspiracy and filing false claims, related to the student-loan case. Two former officers were also convicted of falsifying statements, and a third has been indicted on several charges and awaits trial.

Florida Federal has also agreed to correct the records of students whose credit standing was hurt by the fraud committed by Florida Federal, the U.S. attorney's office said.

In St. Petersburg, Florida Federal officials couldn't be reached.
