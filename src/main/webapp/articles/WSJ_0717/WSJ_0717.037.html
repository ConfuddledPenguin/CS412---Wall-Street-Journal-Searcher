Alcoa Posts 43% Drop In 2nd-Quarter Profit On Lower Ingot Prices

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/17/90

PITTSBURGH -- Aluminum Co. of America, hurt by sharply lower aluminum ingot prices, reported that second-quarter earnings fell 43% to $161.9 million, or $1.86 a share. A year earlier, Alcoa earned $285.2 million, or $3.21 a share. Sales for the quarter fell slightly to $2.69 billion from $2.76 billion in the 1989 second quarter.

The results, which were in line with analysts' expectations, reflect a 24% drop in aluminum ingot prices, Alcoa said.

In addition, the results reflected revenue of only $6 million from the company's Brazilian affiliate, down from $61 million for the first half of 1989. The drop is linked to actions taken by Brazil's government to improve that country's economy. Lower fabricated activity also depressed earnings.

For the six months, Alcoa reported earnings of $310.5 million, or $3.55 a share, down from $561.2 million, or $6.33 a share, a year ago. Sales were $5.33 billion, down from $5.38 billion.

Earnings for the 1990 second quarter include favorable translation and exchange adjustments of $9.9 million, or 11 cents a share, compared with $19.7 million, or 22 cents a share, in the quarter the previous year. For the six months, the favorable adjustments were $5.8 million, or seven cents a share, compared with $20.9 million, or 24 cents a share, in 1989.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading, Alcoa closed at $69.25, up 50 cents.
