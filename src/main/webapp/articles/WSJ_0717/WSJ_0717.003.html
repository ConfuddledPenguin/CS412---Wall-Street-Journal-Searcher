Investor Group Hires PaineWebber in Bid To Acquire Justin

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/17/90

WASHINGTON -- A group of investors holding 11.6% of the stock of Justin Industries Inc. of Fort Worth, Texas, said it hired PaineWebber Inc. as a financial adviser in its effort to acquire the brick, building materials and boot company. The group, led by Perry H. Sutherland and Barry S. Rosenstein, also laid out its plans for Justin in a filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission. The group said it would sell or close the company's unprofitable businesses, which it lists as Featherlite Building Products, Tradewinds Technologies and Northland Publishing. Mr. Sutherland said the sales would leave Justin in its core businesses of boot, brick and some industrial equipment manufacturing.

In addition, the group said it would sell excess real estate assets and other investments and cut costs by reducing overhead and possibly by consolidating facilities.

The group also filed a counterclaim in federal court in Texas, alleging that Justin officials and its board violated proxy regulations by failing to disclose adequately the compensation of John Justin, company chairman. The counterclaim also alleges that the company's proxy materials didn't disclose severance packages guaranteed its officers.

The countersuit seeks invalidation of the election of directors at the most recent annual meeting, invalidation of the severance agreements, the holding of a new meeting and dissemination of more information. Mr. Justin declined to comment on the suit or the plan filed with the SEC.

The countersuit was filed in answer to a suit filed by Justin against the group in June. In that suit, Justin alleged that the group didn't disclose in its initial filing with the SEC in November that it intended to try to acquire Justin. In March, Justin rejected an $18.50 a share, or $140.5 million offer from the group.
