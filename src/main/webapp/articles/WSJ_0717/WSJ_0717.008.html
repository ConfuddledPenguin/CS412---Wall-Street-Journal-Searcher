Temple-Inland Profit Rose 24% on Increase In Sales in 2nd Period

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/17/90

DIBOLL, Texas -- Temple-Inland Inc. reported net income rose 24% in the second quarter because of a decrease in tax rates, despite lower operating earnings in most of its businesses. Earnings rose to $65.7 million, or $1.18 a share, from $52.9 million or 96 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue rose to $587 million from $489 million.

In the first six months, net rose 19% to $127.9 million, or $2.30 a share, from $107.4 million, or $1.94 a share, a year earlier. Revenue rose to $1.2 billion from $963 million.

The company's container and containerboard group, its largest segment, said operating earnings declined about 26% in the quarter. Temple-Inland said that though box shipments "remained strong," average selling prices of corrugated cardboard declined and manufacturing costs increased. Bleached paperboard earnings also declined.

The company's financial services group, however, reported operating earnings of $15.1 million compared with $1.1 million a year earlier. The company said Guaranty Federal Savings Bank contributed $7 million to operating earnings. The tax savings that boosted earnings for the quarter came as a result of tax benefits associated with the purchase of Guaranty.
