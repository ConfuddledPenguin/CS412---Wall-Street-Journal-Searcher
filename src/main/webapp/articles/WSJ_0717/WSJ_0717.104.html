International: In Japan, Shearson Sells American Express Cards

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/17/90

TOKYO -- Clients of Shearson Lehman Hutton Asia Inc. are used to being sold stocks, bonds and financial services. But the latest product the firm is pushing comes straight from above: American Express cards. Employees of the stockbroking and investment-banking subsidiary of American Express Co. are soliciting members for the parent company's credit-card operation in Japan. Instead of the commissions that brokers are accustomed to earning, successful card sellers earn prizes like cameras, golf clubs and bicycles.

Not surprisingly, officials at Shearson and American Express don't see any conflict between selling stocks and promoting credit cards. "A lot of these guys are taking this seriously," says Marc A. Bouchard, chief operating officer at Shearson Lehman Hutton Asia. Still, he adds, "we're not doing it professionally. And, actually, most people who are being asked are suppliers rather than clients. It's less embarrassing."

American Express says it routinely asks company employees to promote its cards in Japan. "Shearson Lehman people are part of the American Express family," says Yoji Takesaki, a spokesman for American Express in Japan. "And all American Express people are responsible for finding as many new clients as possible." American Express employs 1,239 people in Japan, including 320 at Shearson Lehman Hutton.
