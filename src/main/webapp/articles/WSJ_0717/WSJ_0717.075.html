New Securities Issues

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/17/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATE

Wal-Mart Stores Inc. -- $500 million of notes, due July 15, 2000, was priced at par to yield 9.10%. The non-callable notes were priced at a spread of 0.65 percentage point above the Treasury's 10-year note. Rated double-A-3 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and double-A by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Goldman, Sachs & Co.

J.C. Penney Co. -- $250 million of 9.45% notes, due July 15, 2002, was priced at 99.85 to yield 9.47%. The non-callable notes were priced at a spread of 1.02 percentage points above the Treasury's 10-year note. Rated single-A-1 by Moody's and single-A-plus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by First Boston Corp.

Cardinal Distribution Inc. -- $70 million of subordinated debentures, due July 1, 2015, was priced at par to yield 7.25%. The debentures are convertible into the company's common stock at $34.20 a share. Rated Ba-2 by Moody's and double-B-minus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Smith Barney, Harris Upham & Co.

EUROBONDS

Chrysler Auto Receivables Co. (special purpose vehicle, U.S. parent) -- $525 million of car loan receivables-backed 9 1/4% bonds due Aug. 16, 1996, at an issue price of 101.295 and fixed reoffered price of 99.72 via Swiss Bank Corp. Callable in the fifth year. If not called, coupon converts to a floating-rate monthly coupon of 1/2 point above the London interbank offered rate.

Societe Nationale des Chemins de Fer Francais (France) -- 750 million French francs ($135.4 million) of bonds fungible with two billion francs of bonds outstanding, due April 12, 1997, and priced at 98.55 to yield 9.95% less full fees, via Credit Commercial de France. Guaranteed by the Republic of France. Fees 1 7/8.

Banque Paribas (France) -- 130 billion lire ($107.5 million) of 12 3/8% bonds due Aug. 24, 1994, priced at 101 3/8 via Banca Nazionale de Lavoro.

Japan Telecom Co. (Japan) -- 50 million Swiss francs ($35.7 million) of 7 1/2% privately placed notes due Aug. 3, 1995, priced at 100 1/4 to yield 7.44%, via Fuji Bank (Schweiz) AG. Guaranteed by Fuji Bank in Tokyo.
