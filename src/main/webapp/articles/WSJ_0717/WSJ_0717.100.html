Who's News: AT&T Computer's Bridge Is Reassigned

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/17/90

NEW YORK -- American Telephone & Telegraph Co.'s computer unit is down to one president. Gordon J. Bridge, the former International Business Machines Corp. executive who shared the presidency of AT&T Computer Systems with Richard A. McGinn, has been reassigned to head a new unit within AT&T's Data Systems Group.

The new unit is tentatively named Global Messaging, according to a spokesman for AT&T Computer Systems. He declined any further comment on the change, but said Mr. Bridge's new unit will combine AT&T's electronic messaging business with those of Western Union Corp.

Earlier this month AT&T agreed to purchase the ailing Western Union's Business Services unit, which includes telex and electronic mail businesses, for $180 million in cash. While the pact awaits Federal Communications Commission approval, the two companies expect to complete the transaction by Sept. 30.
