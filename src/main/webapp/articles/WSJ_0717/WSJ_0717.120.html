Credit Ratings: S&P Downgrades Ratings On Debt of United Airlines

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/17/90

CHICAGO -- Standard & Poor's Corp. downgraded about $700 million in debt issued by UAL Corp.'s United Airlines unit, citing unsettled labor disputes and burdensome leases. S&P said the United ratings remain on its CreditWatch list "with negative implications," pending the airline company's possible acquisition by its employees. UAL declined to comment.

The rating concern cut its ratings on $200 million of industrial revenue bonds to triple-B-minus from triple-B, and on $500 million of subordinated debt to double-B-plus from triple-B-minus. It cited "erosion that has already occurred" in the airline's credit, and said the ratings could be lowered further after a buy-out or financial restructuring.

United's contracts with its three major unions have expired and are in negotiation or mediation; progress isn't likely until the airline's future ownership is resolved, S&P said. Failure to complete the buy-out would leave unhappy employees reluctant to agree to productivity and work-rule concessions sought by management, it said.

Completion of the buy-out would improve the labor situation but add substantial debt to the balance sheet, S&P said. United's total debt was about $1.7 billion at March 31, lower than prior levels, S&P said, but the "debt equivalent of United's off-balance-sheet leases has mounted rapidly to approximately $5 billion, and will rise further as new aircraft needed for fleet expansion and modernization are delivered." It said the new equipment will lower operating costs and facilitate growth, but raise fixed financial obligations significantly.
