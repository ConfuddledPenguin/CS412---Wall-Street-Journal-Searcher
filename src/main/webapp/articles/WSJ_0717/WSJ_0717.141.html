Lee Enterprises Buys Rest of Joint Venture From Nippon Paint

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/17/90

DAVENPORT, Iowa -- Lee Enterprises Inc. said it paid $100 million to acquire the remaining 50% stake in its Napp Systems Inc. joint venture from partner Nippon Paint Co. of Japan. The two companies have operated jointly the San Marcos, Calif.-based maker of printing plates for 17 years.

Lee, which operates a chain of small-to-midsize newspapers and has broadcast holdings, said it and Nippon Paint "have agreed that single company ownership is better than a joint venture" to meet what it called "increasing competitive pressures and technological innovations." Lee said it expects the unit will be able, with a single management team, to respond more swiftly to such conditions.

The venture, which had 1989 sales of about $75 million, is profitable, according to Lee. The company declined to disclose the venture's earnings. Lee has historically, under accounting rules, reported its half of the venture's net income both as earnings and in its revenue figures. Once the transaction is completed, Lee's revenue will show the full amount of Napp's sales.

Chief Financial Officer Michael J. Riley said Napp has about 50% of the market in its specialized printing-plate field, "and that figure is growing."

A "lot of analysts are expecting (Lee's earnings for 1990) to be down, after analyzing our basic businesses," Mr. Riley said. But as a result of the transaction to take full ownership of Napp, "Our bottom line should be up for the year," he said.
