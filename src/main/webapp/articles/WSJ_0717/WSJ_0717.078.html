IRS Simplifies Tax Form For Up to 6 Million People

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/17/90

WASHINGTON -- As many as six million taxpayers will be able to switch to a simpler form to file 1990 tax returns, the Internal Revenue Service said. The IRS has added several new lines to its 1040A form so that taxpayers may use it to report pension or annuity income, payments from an individual retirement account, taxable Social Security benefits, estimated tax payments or credit for elderly or disabled citizens. In the past, taxpayers reporting these items were required to use the longer 1040 forms.

The tax agency released galley proofs of its 1990 tax forms, tables and rate schedules.
