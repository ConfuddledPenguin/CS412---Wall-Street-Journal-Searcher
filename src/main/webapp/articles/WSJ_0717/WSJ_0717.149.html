What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/17/90

STOCKS SOARED AGAIN with the Dow Jones industrials rising 19.55 points to a record 2999.75, just shy of the 3000 level. Much of the impetus for the advance came from strong second-quarter profits for some companies. Bonds were little changed and the dollar ended mixed. ---

IBM earnings climbed 5.2% in the second quarter, the first time in years that the computer giant has put two respectable quarters back to back. Revenue rose a strong 8.4%, although declining in the mainframe sector.

---

McDonnell Douglas will slash 14,000 to 17,000 jobs by the end of the year to trim costs. The cutbacks by the nation's largest defense contractor are the largest announced so far this year by any aerospace company.

---

Columbia S&L's sale of junk bonds was closer to getting off the ground, with its portfolio attracting bids within 10% of its $2.9 billion book value, people close to the sale said.

---

An Ohio judge threw out the bribery case against Rite Aid Corp. and its president, saying the evidence and testimony presented during a trial was insufficient to give to a jury.

---

All asbestos claims must be consolidated in a single court, a judge in Cleveland ordered. The move further complicates the Manville case and may set off a judicial turf war.

---

Revco D.S. creditors may have grounds to sue former shareholders and others responsible for Revco's failed $1.25 billion leveraged buy-out, according to a preliminary report prepared by a special examiner.

---

Chase Manhattan's earnings fell 62% in the latest quarter, as problem real estate loans worsened. First Chicago reported a 35% drop in profit while Bank of New York posted a 39% decline. Midlantic had a loss.

---

Spending cuts totaling $100 billion will be triggered this fall unless a deficit-reduction pact is reached, the administration warned. It said the fiscal 1991 deficit, excluding the S&L bailout, will soar to $168.8 billion.

---

Ford will boost spending to market its highest-volume cars and put incentives on the 1991 Ford Escort and Mercury Tracer. Ford also will invest $80 million in a Hungarian parts plant.

---

Southland reached an agreement in principle with senior debtholders and a Japanese suitor, averting the possibility of bankruptcy proceedings.

---

The Fed plans to relax limits on securities activities by U.S. banks abroad and broaden the businesses those units can enter without specific approval from the central bank.

---

The CBOT directed Stotler & Co., the futures brokerage subsidiary of Stotler Group, to boost its capital by the close of business tomorrow or begin liquidating customer accounts.

---

Whitman Corp. is negotiating to sell its Hussman refrigerator manufacturing unit to Sanyo Electric for $500 million to $600 million, according to people familiar with the talks.

---

NCR posted a 4.5% rise in second-quarter earnings, citing strong foreign sales that offset continuing weakness in the U.S. computer market.

---

RJR's junk bonds soared after the company said it would retire many of the issues as part of a refinancing.

---
