Business Brief -- General Development Corp.: Developer May Be Forced To Pay Out Larger Claims

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/17/90

General Development Corp. said it will probably have to pay much larger claims than it originally thought for a revised plea-bargain agreement to settle charges that it defrauded thousands of home buyers. The developer, which is operating under federal bankruptcy law protection, was dealt a blow last week when a federal judge rejected its plea-bargain agreement with the U.S. attorney in Miami. Under the restitution part of that agreement, General Development would have had to pay home buyers $80 million to $100 million over 25 years.

General Development said the court gave the parties until Aug. 7 to submit a revised plea agreement and restitution plan. Failing that, the court will set a date to begin the company's trial. The company, which said it has been negotiating with the U.S. attorney, said it believes it will be able to develop a plan by Aug. 7 that will satisfy the court.

However, General Development said, "It is likely that the revised terms of the restitution plan would result in substantially larger claims for restitution than originally anticipated."
