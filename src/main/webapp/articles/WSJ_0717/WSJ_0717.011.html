International: Nissan, Toyota Post Record Sales Rises For First Six Months

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 07/17/90

TOKYO -- Toyota Motor Corp. and Nissan Motor Co. announced separately that they posted double-digit increases in domestic sales in the first six months, marking records for a half-year period. Toyota's domestic sales rose 14% to 1.3 million units. Nissan's Japanese sales totaled 732,453 units, up 12%.

Domestic production by the top two Japanese auto makers was also higher, but exports were notably lower, reflecting the trend toward overseas production.

Exports from January to June by top market-share holder Toyota were 851,886 units, down 6.6% from a year earlier. Nissan's exports plummeted 17% to 461,124 units.

In June alone, domestic sales by Toyota rose 5.2% to 224,875 units, the 15th consecutive month of gains and a new high for the month. Nissan's domestic sales in June rose 5.3% to 125,670 units.

Exports in June by Toyota were up 11%, the first year-on-year gain in three months, but Nissan's exports slumped 9.1% in the month.
