Business Brief -- Borland International Inc.: Software Concern Reports Net Surged in Its 1st Period

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/17/90

Borland International Inc. reported unexpectedly strong increases in fiscal first-quarter profit and revenue. The Scotts Valley, Calif., computer software company said profit nearly tripled to $4.6 million, or 32 cents a share, in the quarter ended June 30, compared with $1.6 million, or 13 cents, a year earlier. Analysts had expected Borland to earn between 23 cents and 28 cents a share. The company cited increased sales of its Paradox database, Quattro Pro spreadsheet and Turbo C++ programming language.

Borland said revenue increased 78% to $41.1 million from $23 million a year earlier. The results were released after markets closed. In national over-the-counter trading, Borland closed up 75 cents at $18.875.
