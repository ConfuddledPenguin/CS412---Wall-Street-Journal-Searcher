Daiwa Plans Hungary Venture

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/17/90

TOKYO -- Daiwa Securities Co., one of the top four brokerage houses in Japan, is planning to set up a securities company in Budapest in a joint venture with Hungary within a month, a Daiwa official said. The new company will be capitalized at about $10 million, of which Daiwa will take up more than 50%. The rest will come from the National Bank of Hungary and the Hungarian Foreign Trade Bank.

Hungary, which is undergoing privatization of government-owned companies and which reopened the Budapest Stock Exchange on June 21, needs know-how on every aspect of the securities business, the Daiwa official said.

Responsibilities of the new company will include underwriting of stocks issued by privatized Hungarian companies, research and gathering information.
