Marketing & Media: Matheson, Grodnik Resigns as Co-CEOs Of National Lampoon

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/17/90

LOS ANGELES -- Tim Matheson and Dan Grodnik resigned as co-chairman and co-chief executive officers of National Lampoon, citing the previously announced sale of the humor magazine to J2 Communications for more than $4 million. The resignations came as a surprise, because J2 had said Messrs. Matheson and Grodnik would head the National Lampoon division after the acquisition was completed. James P. Jimirro, president and chief executive officer of J2, a video producer and distributor, will oversee the operation of the magazine.

Messrs. Matheson and Grodnik weren't available for comment. However, a spokesman for the men said, "They have guided the company through a transition, and I don't think they fully planned to continue." He also said the magazine has been losing money, and the sale to J2 "gave them an opportunity to plug the leaks and bolster the value of their stock."

After the merger, Messrs. Matheson and Grodnik will hold a total of about a 30% stake in J2. Yesterday, J2 said it signed a definitive agreement under which National Lampoon shareholders will receive two-thirds of a share of J2 common for each National Lampoon share. They will also receive a warrant to buy one share of J2 at $3.50 or $3.75 each, depending on the trading level of the stock.

In over-the-counter trading yesterday, J2 shares closed at $2.625.

Mr. Matheson and Mr. Grodnik wrested control of the magazine from Matty Simmons, the magazine's former editor, chairman and largest shareholder.

The duo helped to increase advertising pages to 40 from 10 and brought back traditional advertisers such as Chrysler Corp.

Mr. Jimirro, president and chief executive officer of J2, is a former head of Disney Channel and Disney Home Video.
