Marketing & Media: MGM/UA Receives $53 Million as Part Of Pathe Buy-Out Bid

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/17/90

CULVER CITY, Calif. -- MGM/UA Communications Co. said it received a $53 million payment from Pathe Communications Corp. as part of Pathe's revised $1.3 billion acquisition of the once-mighty studio. Last month, Pathe and MGM/UA revised and extended the $20-a-share proposed transaction, with Pathe agreeing to pay MGM/UA shareholders an extra $1.50 a share. In the agreement, $4 of the $21.50 is to be paid out by July 20 to holders of record July 10.

Under the original tender offer, Pathe already had deposited $200 million into an escrow account as a down payment on the acquisition. The $53 million represents the remainder needed to make the interim $4 payment to shareholders, who will keep the money even if the deal collapses. If it goes through, holders would get the remaining $17.50 in October.

The prior agreement between the two companies had been backstopped by a proposed $650 million loan by Time Warner Inc. to Pathe. That agreement fell apart, Pathe and Time Warner have sued each other, and Pathe is looking for new sources of financing.

MGM/UA also said that according to a ruling by the New York Stock Exchange, MGM/UA shares had been trading on the basis of one share of stock plus a "due bill" of $4 since July 3. It is expected that the stock will begin trading "ex-payment" one business day following the payment date, the company said.

The latest payment brings to $253 million the amount Pathe has advanced toward the transaction since last March. Pathe has also given MGM/UA a $75 million security interest in three upcoming Pathe films. According to the revised agreement, Pathe must deposit $50 million payments on Aug. 21 and Sept. 20.
