Holder of First City Bancorp. of Texas Sues, Saying He Was Misled

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/17/90

HOUSTON -- A First City Bancorp. of Texas shareholder filed suit in federal court here charging the bank holding company and others with misleading investors about the health of its loan portfolio. The lawsuit, filed by Jerry Krim of Arlington, Va., alleges that First City, several officers and directors, and Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette Securities Corp., the company's investment banker, made excessively optimistic statements about First City's prospects in proxy materials issued in connection with a federally assisted bailout of the company in 1988, as well as in subsequent annual reports. Donaldson Lufkin is a unit of Equitable Life Assurance Society of the U.S.

The complaint accuses the new management team of A. Robert Abboud, chairman and chief executive officer, of failing to disclose "the true extent to which (First City's) loan portfolio was composed of high-risk loans to debt-laden companies who were having difficulties meeting cashflow projections."

In recent months several such loans have been placed on nonperforming status, contributing to what First City says will be a decline in second-quarter earnings.

The plaintiff, who is seeking to have the lawsuit granted class-action status, requests unspecified compensatory and punitive damages.

Neither First City nor DLJ would comment on the complaint. In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange yesterday, First City shares closed at $21.25, down $1.75.
