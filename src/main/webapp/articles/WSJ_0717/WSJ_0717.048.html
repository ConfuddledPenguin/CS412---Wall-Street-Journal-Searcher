Southmark Plan Is Approved

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/17/90

DALLAS -- Southmark Corp. said its plan to reorganize around its San Jacinto Savings Association unit was confirmed by a bankruptcy court judge. Under terms of the plan, creditors will receive $76 million in cash and a package of securities in the reorganized Southmark, once a $9 billion real-estate and financial-services conglomerate. Southmark shareholders also will receive shares in the reorganized company.

Southmark will contribute $100 million in new capital to troubled San Jacinto and will liquidate its remaining assets. The plan will be put into effect in mid-August.
