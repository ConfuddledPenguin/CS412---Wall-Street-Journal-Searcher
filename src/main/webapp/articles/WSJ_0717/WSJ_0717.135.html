International Brief -- Alcoa of Australia Ltd.: First-Half Operating Profit Rose 5.2% to $308.5 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/17/90

Alcoa of Australia Ltd., an alumina and aluminum company, reported that first-half operating profit after taxes rose 5.2% to 395.9 million Australian dollars (US$308.5 million) from A$376.2 million a year earlier.

Revenue rose 7.6% to A$1.5 billion from A$1.4 billion.

The company said profit improved mainly because of higher alumina shipments, favorable gold mining operations, a weaker Australian currency and higher interest income from short-term investments. These improvements were partly offset by lower aluminum prices and higher raw materials costs.

Alcoa is one of Australia's largest exporters; it said it accounts for more than 5% of the country's merchandise exports.

The outlook for the rest of this year looks sound, despite some slowing in the U.S. economy, the company said. But Alcoa warned that if the Australian dollar remains at its current level, second-half earnings will be lower.
