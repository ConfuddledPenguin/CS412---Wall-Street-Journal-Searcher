Triangle Corp. Adopts Poison Pill in Wake Of Rothenberger Bid

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/17/90

STAMFORD, Conn. -- Triangle Corp. directors adopted a so-called poison-pill measure designed to make hostile takeover bids prohibitively expensive. The tool manufacturer's board said that Triangle will distribute rights to buy preferred stock to common-share holders of record July 26. Under certain circumstances, the rights would entitle the holders to buy Triangle's common shares at half price.

Triangle's board adopted the poison pill in light of the purchase of "an ownership position" in the company by Helmut Rothenberger, chief executive officer of the Frankfurt hand-tool manufacturer Rothenberger Werkzeuge AG.

"Mr. Rothenberger has not filed a Schedule 13-D with the Securities and Exchange Commission, and Triangle does not know how much stock he (or any group of which he is a member) intends to acquire," the company said.

Triangle also said it doesn't know what Mr. Rothenberger's "intentions may be generally," adding that he says he is willing "to acquire more shares at reasonable prices."
