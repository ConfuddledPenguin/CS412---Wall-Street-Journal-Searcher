Tropicana Receives $510,000 Settlement In Distribution Suit

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 07/17/90

Purity Products Inc., Baltimore, and a sister company, Purity Products of Virginia Inc., Petersburg, Va., have agreed to settle an 18-month-old suit brought by Tropicana Products Inc., Bradenton, Fla., a unit of Seagram Co. In the federal suit, Tropicana alleged that the two distributors secretly sold the Tropicana products to retailers and wholesalers outside the Baltimore area in breach of their distribution contracts. Tropicana's general counsel, Steven B. Gold, says the scheme allowed the distributors to pocket promotional discounts paid by Tropicana and to profit further by undercutting other distributors in those areas that also sold Tropicana products.

Mr. Gold says the distributors agreed to stop diverting Tropicana products and to pay Tropicana $510,000 rather than face trial on racketeering charges and punitive damages exceeding $1 million.

Ivan Goldstein, president of both distribution companies, declined to comment.

Last year, a federal judge in Tampa, Fla., denied the distributors' motion to dismiss the suit. Tropicana, the judge ruled, had listed hundreds of violations by the distributors that showed a pattern of racketeering occurring in a specified period of time.
