Business Brief -- GAF Corp.: Four Banks Agree to Pledge $800 Million for Refinancing

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/17/90

GAF Corp. said it received a commitment letter from four banks for $800 million of long-term senior financing to refinance debt incurred in its 1989 management buy-out. The refinancing, by Chase Manhattan Bank, Bank of New York, Bank of Nova Scotia and Banque Paribas, is expected to close next month and will enable GAF to redeem $50 million outstanding of senior subordinated increasing-rate notes due March 15, 1994, the company said. The notes carry an interest rate of 16.5%. Terms of the new bank financing are under negotiation, a GAF spokeswoman said.

The Wayne, N.J., maker of specialty chemicals and building materials retired $235 million of the increasing-rate notes this year as well as $200 million of senior debt after the sale of its surfactant business to a limited partnership headed by a unit of Rhone-Poulenc S.A. of France.

The agreement with the banks permits the company, after the closing of the refinancing, to begin an exchange offer for all or a portion of the company's subordinated merger debentures, which public holders of GAF received in the 1989 buy-out and whose aggregate principal amount, together with accrued interest, was $239 million as of June 30, GAF said.
