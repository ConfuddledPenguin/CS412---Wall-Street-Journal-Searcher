Business Brief -- Maxicare Health Plans Inc.: Firm's Reorganization Plan Wins Approval of Judge

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/17/90

Maxicare Health Plans Inc., which filed for bankruptcy law protection in March 1989, said that a bankruptcy court judge in Santa Ana, Calif., approved its reorganization plan. Maxicare said in Los Angeles it needs to raise additional cash before it can emerge from bankruptcy-law proceedings, which a spokesman said the company hopes to do by Oct. 1.

The reorganization plan calls for creditors and shareholders to receive $129.3 million in cash, $67 million in notes and newly issued common shares and warrants. Maxicare said it expects the new securities to be traded in the over-the-counter market.

Maxicare was once the nation's largest publicly traded health maintenance organization with more than two million members in 26 states. After its huge debt payments exceeded its revenue, Maxicare was forced to close or sell many of its health plans and lost many of its members. Maxicare now has about 300,000 members in seven states.
