Whitman Gets Bid From Sanyo For Hussmann --- Price of Up to $600 Million For Refrigeration Firm Said to Be on the Table

  • Author: David B. Hilder
  • Date: 07/17/90

NEW YORK -- Whitman Corp. is negotiating to sell its Hussmann Corp. commercial refrigerator manufacturing unit to Sanyo Electric Co. for $500 million to $600 million, according to people familiar with the talks. Sanyo, a maker of consumer electronics and appliances based in Osaka, Japan, last week submitted the highest bid for the St. Louis-based Hussmann unit, according to people familiar with the bidding. However, even Sanyo's bid was somewhat below Whitman's expectations, and Whitman must now decide whether to proceed with the sale, the people said. In addition, Whitman is trying to see whether any other bidders will offer a price or terms for Hussmann that are more attractive than Sanyo's bid. The exact terms of Sanyo's bid couldn't be determined.

A spokesman for Whitman said the company received bids for Hussmann last week, but declined to identify the bidders or comment on whether Sanyo was among them. "When a determination is made as to the adequacy of the bids, we will announce it to the public," the spokesman said.

In Osaka, a Sanyo spokesman said he had no information on any talks with Whitman. The spokesman said the company's policy is not to comment on mergers or acquisitions until they have been arranged.

Whitman said in March it would explore the possible sale of Hussmann to concentrate on its packaged goods and soft-drink business. Whitman makes candies and owns the Pet Inc. food manufacturer, a Pepsi Cola bottling operation and the Midas International auto service shop franchiser.

Morgan Stanley & Co., a New York investment banking firm, has handled the Hussmann auction for Whitman. A Morgan Stanley spokesman declined to comment.

The price for Hussmann could disappoint Wall Street analysts, some of whom who had estimated that Hussmann could command a price of more than $700 million. Lately, estimates of Hussmann's price have been dropping, encouraged by Whitman's management.

Joel Tiss, a conglomerate analyst at Shearson Lehman Hutton Inc., said Whitman officials told him that after-tax proceeds from the Hussmann sale "will range between $400 million and $500 million." He added: "Our own estimates say it should be toward the lower end of that."

Mr. Tiss said that his original estimate of Hussmann's pretax sale price was between $600 million and $800 million but that the actual sale price "might be closer to $600 million to $750 million."

A Whitman official confirmed that the company's chairman had told analysts recently that after-tax proceeds from the sale would be between $400 million and $500 million.

David S. Moore, a conglomerate analyst at Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette Securities Corp., said he is expecting Whitman to realize after-tax proceeds of about $525 million, which he said implied a pretax sale price of "close to $600 million."

Mr. Moore said he is concerned that the sale of Hussmann, which had a small loss in the first quarter, "might be bad timing. We think strategically, it's a sound move, but we're not convinced that the timing is absolutely perfect."

Sanyo is a major producer of refrigerators in Asian and Pacific markets. Sanyo has a plant in San Diego that makes small refrigerators and in 1986 bought a plant in Indiana that makes large refrigerators for the consumer market.

Hussmann has about 40% to 45% of the market for refrigerators and refrigerated display cases for supermarkets and about 25% of the market for such equipment used in convenience stores. In 1989, Hussmann had a profit of $60 million on revenue of $789 million.
