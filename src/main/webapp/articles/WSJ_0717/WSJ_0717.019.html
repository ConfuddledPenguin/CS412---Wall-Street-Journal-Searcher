Economy: Inventories in May Rose 0.4% as Sales Advanced 0.9%

  • Author: Rodney Ferguson
  • Date: 07/17/90

WASHINGTON -- Business inventories rose slightly in May, the Commerce Department reported, but they were outpaced by an increase in sales. Inventories of retailers, wholesalers and manufacturers rose to $799.40 billion in May, up 0.4% from $796.05 billion in April. Sales reached $538.33 billion, up 0.9% from the April figure of $533.60 billion.

Inventories equaled 1.48 months' sales in May, meaning it would take companies just under 1 1/2 months to sell off their inventories at the month's buying pace. The ratio was down slightly from April's 1.49 but was higher than March's 1.47.

Total sales rose on the strength of the manufacturing and wholesale sectors, with retail sales down 0.6%. Analysts saw little encouragement in the sales figures. "I don't think the sales rise signals a rebound in this economy," said Gary Ciminero, chief economist for Fleet/Norstar Financial Group Inc.

Automobile inventories were down slightly in May, prompting some apprehension about the rise in non-automotive inventories. "There is some concern about inventories going up outside of automobiles," said Daniel Friel, economist for NCNB Corp. of Charlotte, N.C. "Those inventories have gone up enough to be of some concern."

All the numbers were adjusted for seasonal variations.
