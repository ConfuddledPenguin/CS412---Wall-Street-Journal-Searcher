Who's News: DeMarco Is Forced To Resign as Head Of Pierre Cardin USA

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/17/90

NEW YORK -- Nicholas J. DeMarco, head of Pierre Cardin USA, said he was forced to resign his post after 12 years and filed a suit against the Paris-based fashion design company. Mr. DeMarco's civil action, filed in New York State Supreme Court, has been transferred to federal court in Manhattan. The suit charges Mr. Cardin and his company, S.A.R.L. de Gestion Pierre Cardin, with wrongful termination, breach of contract, wrongful conversion of U.S. licensing royalties and fraud. The plaintiff seeks compensatory damages of "not less than $5 million" and punitive damages of "not less than $5 million."

In an interview, Mr. DeMarco said that he has maintained an "excellent relationship with Cardin" and speculated that he was being forced to leave his post to "facilitate the eventual sale" of Mr. Cardin's world-wide licensing business. He said he has reason to believe the businesses were up for sale but declined to elaborate. He said Mr. Cardin hasn't returned numerous phone calls and messages.

Maxwell Stolzberg, a Manhattan attorney for S.A.R.L. de Gestion Pierre Cardin, said: "Mr. Cardin has his own business reasons for (Mr. Marco's) termination and it had nothing to do with any sale of his business or licensees. . . . As far as I am aware, the licenses are not up for sale. . . . The contract that he had with the Cardin organization provided for a termination on notice, and that notice was given."

Pierre Cardin's U.S. licensees encompass 32 companies that manufacture women's, men's and boy's apparel, generating about $201 million annually at wholesale prices, according to Steve Weiner, chairman of the Pierre Cardin licensees in the U.S.
