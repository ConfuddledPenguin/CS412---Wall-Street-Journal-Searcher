Technology: NCR Surprises, Posts Results That Top 2nd-Period Sales, Revenue Expectations

  • Author: John R. Wilke
  • Date: 07/17/90

NCR Corp. surprised investors with solid profit and revenue gains for the second quarter, pushing the big computer maker's shares higher in active trading. Dayton, Ohio-based NCR reported net income of $115 million, or $1.66 a share, up 4.5% from $110 million, or $1.42 a share, in the 1989 second quarter, when the company had a larger base of shares outstanding.

Revenue climbed 5% to $1.61 billion from $1.53 billion.

NCR said strong foreign sales offset continuing weakness in the U.S. market. Despite the slow domestic sales, the company said it still expects to achieve profit and revenue gains for the year.

"The quarter looked good almost across the board: Sales were up, gross margins were up, and discretionary spending was down," Don Young, an analyst with Sanford C. Bernstein & Co., said. The one negative factor was continuing weakness in domestic orders, he said.

Mr. Young also said he expects NCR to report strong earnings for the current quarter, but he called the fourth quarter "a question mark," as the company is to begin a risky product-line transition in the fall.

Investors welcomed the company's earnings report, bidding NCR shares up $3.875 each to close at $68 in New York Stock Exchange composite trading, with 407,100 shares changing hands. Average daily turnover in the stock is about 250,000 shares.

Favorable currency-exchange rates in the second quarter also boosted NCR's results, analysts said, an effect that should extend into the third quarter. And analysts lauded the company's tight rein on expenses. "NCR has some of the best cost controls in the industry, and that was a significant factor in the second quarter," Jay P. Stevens of Dean Witter Reynolds Inc. said. "They manage factory inventories closely, and they don't have excess people."

NCR's chairman and chief executive officer, Charles E. Exley, said orders abroad set a record in the second quarter, showing a "substantial gain" from year-earlier activity. The improvements offset a decline in U.S. orders, he said.

Bank automation systems and check-processing equipment were particularly strong, while the NCR Tower series of computers posted a third consecutive record quarter in sales, the company said.

Mr. Exley said second-half results will be helped if foreign-exchange rates continue at current levels. "Despite the softness of U.S. incoming orders in the second quarter, we still see the opportunity to achieve single-digit growth in revenue and net income for the full year," he said.

During the second quarter, NCR purchased 2.2 million of its shares under a previously announced buy-back program. NCR said the buy-backs continued to boost per-share earnings while hurting net income. The company had an average of 69.3 million shares outstanding in the latest quarter, down from an average of 77.7 million a year earlier. Higher research spending also cut into earnings, the company said.

First-half net dropped 2.3% to $167 million, or $2.38 a share, from $171 million, or $2.17 a share. The increase in earnings per share reflected the stock buy-backs. Revenue grew 3.3% to $2.87 billion from $2.78 billion.
