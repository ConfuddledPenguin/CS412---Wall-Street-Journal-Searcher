International Brief -- Algemene Bank Nederland N.V.: Two Dutch Banks to Merge And Join World's Top 20

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/17/90

Algemene Bank Nederland N.V. and Amsterdam-Rotterdam Bank N.V. said they reached a definitive agreement to merge and predicted flat combined earnings this year. The two banks, which in March said they were discussing a merger, plan to create a new institution, ABN Amro Holding N.V. Based on current share prices, the new banking company would have a stock market value of about 9.3 billion guilders ($5 billion). With assets of 402 billion guilders, it would be among the top 20 banks world-wide.

ABN shareholders have the right to swap their shares for those of the new holding company on a 1-for-1 basis; Amro holders are being offered two shares in the new company for each Amro share held. The offer expires Aug. 17, and the two banks said the merger could be completed by the end of August.

The merger plan still requires approval from shareholders of both companies and various regulatory agencies.
