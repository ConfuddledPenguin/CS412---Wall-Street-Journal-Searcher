Hong Kong Trade Deficit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/27/90

HONG KONG -- Hong Kong's May trade deficit narrowed to 2.77 billion Hong Kong dollars ($355.8 million) from HK$2.91 billion a year earlier, the result of a sharp rise in exports, the government reported. May's trade deficit also shrank markedly from the April shortfall of HK$3.37 billion.

Domestic exports grew 6% to HK$18.83 billion from HK$17.70 billion a year ago and shot up 15% from April's HK$16.41 billion.

Hong Kong's re-exports surged 25% to HK$34.89 billion from HK$28.01 billion the previous year and were up 13% from HK$30.86 billion in April.

Total exports jumped 18% to HK$53.72 billion from HK$45.71 billion in May 1989 and up 14% from HK$47.27 billion the previous month.

May's imports were up 16% to HK$56.49 billion from HK$48.62 billion a year earlier and 12% higher than HK$50.64 billion in April.
