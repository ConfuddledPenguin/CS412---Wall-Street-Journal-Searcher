Business Brief -- BellSouth Corp.: New Zealand Grants License For Cellular Phone Service

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/27/90

BellSouth Corp., Atlanta, said its international unit was awarded a license to provide cellular telephone service in New Zealand. The regional telecommunications company said the New Zealand Ministry of Commerce also awarded cellular licenses to two other companies, but these weren't identified.

BellSouth said it will conduct further market research before determining the magnitude of its initial investment. The New Zealand cellular telephone market has 3.5 million potential customers, according to a BellSouth estimate.

The licenses granted to BellSouth and others follow a decision two weeks ago by the New Zealand government to sell its state-owned telephone company to Ameritech of Chicago, Bell Atlantic Corp. of Philadelphia, and two New Zealand companies for about $2.46 billion.
