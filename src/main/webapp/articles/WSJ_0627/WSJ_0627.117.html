Business Brief -- MCI Communications Corp.: Company Sets Loan Pact For Telecom USA Merger

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/27/90

MCI Communications Corp. said it signed an agreement with 22 banks to borrow as much as $1 billion for its merger with Telecom USA Inc. MCI, the nation's second-largest long-distance carrier, said it will use the new credit as well as an existing credit line and its own funds to finance the $1.25 billion merger, which it expects to complete in late summer or early fall. The new loan agreement is a reducing revolving credit with a three-year final maturity, where the amount of money that can be borrowed declines over time. A competitive bid option allows the 22 banks, led by Bank of America, to bid against each other to provide funds.

MCI agreed in April to acquire Atlanta-based Telecom USA, the nation's fourth-largest long-distance carrier, for $42 a share. After the merger, MCI will still be No. 2 in the industry, after American Telephone & Telegraph Co.
