Big Board Is Asked to Plan Soviet Seminar on Markets

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/27/90

NEW YORK -- The Soviet Union has invited the New York Stock Exchange to organize a seminar on the development of securities exchanges. The seminar, to be held in Moscow in October, will be jointly sponsored with the U.S.S.R. Ministry of Finance and Gosbank, the state bank.

Soviet Deputy Prime Minister and State Economic Reform Commission Chairman Leonid Abalkin invited the Big Board chairman and chief executive officer, John J. Phelan Jr., to lead a delegation of U.S. financial experts. Mr. Abalkin called the initiative a "concrete step" toward the development of a securities market in the Soviet Union.

The invitation follows a suggestion first made by President Bush to Soviet President Mikhail Gorbachev at their Malta meeting in December 1989. In the intervening months, there have been discussions between Soviet government officials and the New York Stock Exchange concerning the scope, timing and administrative details of such a seminar.

The Soviet government recently authorized creation of public companies with the right to sell shares and issue bonds.
