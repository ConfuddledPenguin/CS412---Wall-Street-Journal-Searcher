Credit Ratings: Baltimore Gas Ratings Lowered by Moody's

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/31/90

NEW YORK -- Moody's Investors Service Inc. said it lowered the credit ratings of Baltimore Gas & Electric Co. because of continued outages at the company's Calvert Cliffs nuclear units, accelerating replacement energy costs and uncertain prospects for rate increases. The credit rating change affects about $2.5 billion of the company's securities. Ratings lowered include those on first refunding mortgage and secured pollution control revenue bonds, to single-A-1 from double-A-3; shelf registration of first refunding mortgage bonds, to (P)single-A-1 from (P)double-A-3; unsecured pollution control revenue bonds and other unsecured debt, to single-A-2 from single-A-1; unsecured pollution control revenue bonds, to single-A-2/VMIG1 from single-A-1/VMIG1; preferred stock, to single-A-1 from double-A-3; preference stock, to single-A-2 from single-A-1; and shelf registration of preference stock, to (P)single-A-2 from (P)single-A-1.

Responding to the downgrading, the utility said in a statement that it believes it "should be able to recover these expenditures" related to replacement power and maintenance and has filed for base and fuel rate increases with the Maryland Public Service Commission.
