Credit Ratings: Some Massachusetts Debt Stays on S&P CreditWatch

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/31/90

NEW YORK -- Standard & Poor's Corp. kept Massachusetts' triple-B rated general obligation bonds on its CreditWatch list with negative implications. The ratings agency said the watch reflects the state's continued failure to pass a new budget and the continuing uncertainty over whether a sales tax on services that was passed will be reconsidered by the legislature.

It also said there are concerns about a tax-cutting initiative that voters will consider in November.

Standard & Poor's said that "legislative inaction over the last two years" has left $1.8 billion in operating deficits over the last two years and promise "a legacy of debt for perhaps the next two administrations." It said "while the economy is in a recession, long-term prospects remain positive."
