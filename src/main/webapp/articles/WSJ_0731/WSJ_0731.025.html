Financing Business: Verit Industries

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/31/90

VERIT INDUSTRIES, a real estate concern, said it completed the private sale of 61,500 shares of a new Series B convertible preferred stock to a Liechtenstein investment company for $15.4 million. A spokesman for Verit, based in Phoenix, Ariz., declined to identify the purchaser. Verit said it used $14.7 million from the sale proceeds to buy a 67% interest in Touristic Club International AG Vaduz, a leisure company, which has as its principal asset the Hotel Viking, a resort hotel in southern Portugal. Each Series B preferred share is convertible into 100 Verit common shares. The purchaser agreed to vote its shares in the same manner as Verit's management for five years. The agreement also places a limit on the number of common shares that the purchaser may sell in any 12-month period for five years. Verit is involved in real estate development in California and Texas.
