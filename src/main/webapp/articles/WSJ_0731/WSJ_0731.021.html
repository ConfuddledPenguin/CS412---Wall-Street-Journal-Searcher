Beverly Enterprises To Offer Debentures Totaling $40 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/31/90

PASADENA, Calif. -- Beverly Enterprises Inc. said it plans a public offering of $40 million of convertible debentures, which would be the second major sale of securities this year by the health-care concern. Beverly, which is moving its headquarters to Fort Smith, Ark., effective tomorrow, said proceeds from the new issue will be used in its continuing financial restructuring. The offering will be managed by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Beverly shares closed at $6.375, down 25 cents.

A Beverly spokesman said the debentures will be convertible at the greater of either $8 face amount of debenture for each new common share, or a 20% premium over market value at the time the sale is completed. At the $8-a-share rate, the issue is convertible into a total of five million new common shares. The company has about 65 million common shares outstanding.

Beverly, which operates nursing facilities, retirement-living centers and home-health centers, sold 11.5 million new common shares last March through a public offering that netted the company about $45.7 million. Proceeds from that issue, as with the plan for the latest offering, were used to refinance debt.

Schuyler Hollingsworth, Beverly vice president and treasurer, said the company has about $470 million in debt to be restructured. Following completion of the planned debenture sale, Beverly plans three further transactions later this year, including bank borrowings and private placements, to complete the debt refinancing, Mr. Hollingsworth said.
