Business Brief -- LTV Corp.: Concern Reports Earnings Rose Sharply in 2nd Period

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/31/90

LTV Corp., Dallas, said second-quarter net income more than doubled because of a special gain this year and a special charge a year earlier. Net rose to $76.6 million, or 59 cents a share, from $30.3 million, or 21 cents a share, a year earlier. The 1990 quarter reflected a gain of $44.3 million on the sale of a stainless steel unit. The 1989 quarter was reduced by $80 million in write-downs and charges on aircraft production assets.

Operating profit fell 64% to $36.6 million from $102.6 million before charges a year earlier. Revenue fell 4.5% to $1.59 billion from $1.66 billion.

Steel operations contributed almost all of the decline in operating profit. LTV, which is operating under protection of bankruptcy law, attributed the decline to lower prices, lower sales and higher labor costs.

In the first half, profit rose to $120.8 million, or 91 cents a share, from $106.9 million, or 82 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue fell 6% to $3.08 billion from $3.28 billion.
