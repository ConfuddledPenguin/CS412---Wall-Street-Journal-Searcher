Technology Brief -- Hitachi Ltd.: Company Has Developed New Disk Memory System

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/31/90

Hitachi Ltd. said it has come up with a magnetic disk storage subsystem, for use in large-scale, general-purpose processors, which allows for what it claims is the world's largest computer memory storage. The Japanese computer and electronics company said the new product, H-6587 series, can store up to 35 gigabytes of data -- the equivalent of more than 70 years of a typical Japanese language newspaper -- or 2.3 times more than Hitachi's preceding model.

Hitachi enhanced head-disk assembly technology, in an attempt to ensure large storage in drastically less space. As a result, on a per-gigabyte basis, a configuration using the new systems requires only one-third the space of existing systems, officials said.

Hitachi officials call the latest development the completion of Hitachi's computer devices aimed to cope with market demand for highly sophisticated information systems.

The company will start marketing the new product Aug. 1.
