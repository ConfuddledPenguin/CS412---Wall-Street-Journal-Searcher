Who's News: Lumex Says Hillery Resigned Two Posts; Firm Will Cut Staff

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/31/90

BAY SHORE, N.Y. -- Lumex Inc. said that David Hillery, 53-year-old vice president of the health-care products company and president of its Cybex division, resigned to pursue other interests. Separately, Lumex said it would cut staff through attrition and dismissals and would pare expenses as it reported a second-quarter net loss of $849,000, or 20 cents a share, on 2.2% lower sales. In the 1989 second quarter, Lumex earned $469,000, or 11 cents a share. Sales eased to $20.8 million from $21.3 million.

One factor in the weaker results was that U.S. sales of Cybex's rehabilitation equipment fell from the year-earlier period; international sales continued to be strong, Lumex said.

Lumex division sales of bathroom seating and safety aids for the sick and the elderly rose from the year-ago period but fell short of expectations, the parent said. The Lumex division also makes canes, crutches, wheelchairs and other ambulatory aids.

Lumex employs about 1,030; the company declined to estimate the number of staff cuts it anticipates.

Lumex expects to return to profitability for the rest of the year, said Lawrence Cohen, 58, chairman and chief executive officer. Mr. Cohen will assume Mr. Hillery's duties in the interim.

Mr. Hillery, who was also a director of Lumex Inc., was unavailable for comment.

In American Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Lumex shares fell 50 cents, to close at $6.25.
