International Brief -- Banco Espanol de Credito: Spanish Bank's Pretax Profit For First Half Surged 17%

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/31/90

Banco Espanol de Credito of Spain reported that first-half pretax profit surged 17% to 35.29 billion pesetas ($357.2 million). The bank, known as Banesto, said financial revenue grew 33% to 212.29 billion pesetas, boosted by a 41% jump in loans and financial instruments. It didn't provide comparative data.

Banesto's efforts to lure depositors, by offering high-yield "super accounts" with interest rates of about 13%, helped increase consumer deposits and bolster financial revenue, but the accounts also sharply raised financial costs, which soared 50% to 142.87 billion pesetas, the bank said.

Analysts said the results were better than expected, and noted that Spain's big banks have been able to offset the burden of high-yield accounts by increasing loan rates and service charges.
