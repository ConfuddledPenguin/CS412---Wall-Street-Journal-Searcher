Who's News: Consolidated Freightways Accepts Chief's Resignation

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/31/90

MENLO PARK, Calif. -- Consolidated Freightways Inc., a transportation concern whose earnings have been going downhill fast since an acquisition last year, said its board "accepted the resignation" of Lary R. Scott, president and chief executive officer. Consolidated said Chairman Raymond F. O'Brien, 68 years old, would assume the additional post of chief executive and J. Frank Leach would succeed Mr. Scott as president. Mr. Leach, 69, has been a Consolidated director for 16 years.

Consolidated has been hurt as a result of its April 1989 acquisition of Emery Air Freight Corp. for $230 million and the assumption of $240 million in debt. Emery previously had losses for three consecutive years because of stiffening international air-freight competition. Mr. Scott considered the purchase as central for Consolidated to compete in the global freight-hauling business.

But since the acquisition, Consolidated has posted a string of quarterly net losses, and last month, it suspended its quarterly dividend. Consolidated officials and Mr. Scott couldn't be reached for comment.
