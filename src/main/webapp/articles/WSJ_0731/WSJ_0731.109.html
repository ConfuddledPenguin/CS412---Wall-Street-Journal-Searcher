Ex-Eastern Air Foreman Pleads Guilty in Fraud Case

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/31/90

NEW YORK (AP) -- A former Eastern Airlines foreman pleaded guilty to charges that he ignored vital maintenance and safety repairs and then falsified records to make it appear the work had been done. Elia Dragone, who worked at Kennedy International Airport, was the first employee of the troubled airline to plead guilty in a broad scheme that allegedly allowed unsafe airliners to fly.

Eastern and nine of its managers were charged last week in a 60-count criminal indictment that alleges conspiracy to defraud the government, wire fraud, falsification of documents and obstruction of justice.

Mr. Dragone, named in 20 counts of the indictment, pleaded guilty to conspiracy and obstructing a Federal Aviation Administration investigation into the airline. He faces five years in prison and a $250,000 fine on each count at sentencing Oct. 17.

According to a plea agreement, Mr. Dragone will testify against Eastern and its employees. He also will cooperate in investigations of the airline by the FAA and other government agencies.

The indictment named Eastern and nine high-level management employees, who will be arraigned Aug. 6.
