Otis, Soviet Agency Form Joint Venture For Elevator Market

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/31/90

FARMINGTON, Conn. -- Otis Elevator Co. said it is forming a joint venture in the Soviet Union to pursue the $1 billion-a-year market for new elevators. Otis, a wholly owned subsidiary of United Technologies Corp., said it will own 55% of a new company that will manufacture, install and service elevators in the Soviet Union using Otis designs, manufacturing technology, and installation and maintenance methods. The rest of the partnership will be formed by Shcherbinka Lift Plant, a unit of Liftmash, the Soviet agency responsible for making elevators.

Otis said it also will supply manufacturing equipment, management personnel and training for Soviet workers at a new facility under construction at the existing Shcherbinka Lift Plant, 15 miles from Moscow. The plant will make elevator equipment for offices and small hotels, Otis said.

The Soviet Union is the world's largest national market for new elevator equipment, with demand for 60,000 new units a year, Otis said.

Otis is the world's largest elevator company, with 1989 revenue of $3.3 billion, 70% of which came from outside of the U.S.

United Technologies, based in Hartford, Conn., also has interests in aerospace, air conditioning and automotive products.
