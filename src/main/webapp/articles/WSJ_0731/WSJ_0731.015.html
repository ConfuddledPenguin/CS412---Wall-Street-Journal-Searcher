June Construction Contracts Eased 2%, Says F.W. Dodge

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/31/90

NEW YORK -- New construction contracts eased 2% in June from May, reflecting stability in the second quarter, following a volatile first quarter. F.W. Dodge, the forecasting unit of McGraw-Hill Inc., said nonresidential building contracts rose 5% in June, to $86.02 billion, from a seasonally adjusted $82.06 billion in May. Non-building construction, including public works and utilities, dropped 14%, to $44.8 billion, from $52.3 billion. The non-building construction drop was essentially a retreat to its former level after a spate of projects temporarily elevated May's total. The value of newly started one-family homes slipped 1%, to $109.94 billion, from $111.34 billion in May.

George A. Christie, chief economist at F.W. Dodge, said the steepest part of the decline in construction activity this year, amounting almost to a "free fall," was confined to the first quarter. A smaller second-quarter decline and more recent firming of monthly contracting data suggest a more stable building market, Mr. Christie said.

In the first six months, new construction totaled $121.3 billion, which was 8% below the year-ago level and the first interruption of continuous expansion in construction activity in seven years.

At midyear, the Northeast region posted the deepest decline in building, 24%, followed by the South, down 11%. The only gain was in the North Central region, still ahead of 1989 by 5%. The West was virtually even with its year-ago level of contracting.
