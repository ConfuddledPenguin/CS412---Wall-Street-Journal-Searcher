Letters to the Editor: Possible Solution To S&L Quagmire

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/31/90

I have a possible solution to the S&L mess (page one, July 3). Have two types of deposits: uninsured and insured. The uninsured deposits will pay market interest rates to the depositor, the insured ones two points below. The two points will go to the Federal Deposit Insurance Corp., to cover the insurance. Thus, the cost to the financial institution will be the same. The depositor will watch the performance of his bank and decide how safe its operation is. We eliminate giant federal supervision. The financial institution should publish figures monthly so everybody can figure out the depositors' confidence in the financial institution and its performance.

Some private evaluators will appear to advise depositors, the funds generally will stay close to home, the amount of large, brokered deposits will decrease, and the two points going to the FDIC may be used to pay for the current bailout.

Michael Hoch

Professor Emeritus of Materials Science

University of Cincinnati

Cincinnati
