Campeau Ends Bid to Get Holder Vote on Asset Sales

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/31/90

TORONTO -- Campeau Corp. withdrew a shareholder resolution to authorize the sale of substantially all of its Canadian real-estate assets, but said it still plans to sell roughly two-thirds of the assets. The resolution was part of a package to restructure Campeau Corp.'s ailing finances. Robert Campeau, chairman and chief executive officer of Campeau Corp., said the resolution was "premature" without first obtaining standstill agreements from the company's two principal creditors, Olympia & York Developments Ltd. and Edward J. DeBartolo Corp.

As a result, Mr. Campeau said he opposed the resolution at this time, leaving the company's management short of the two-thirds majority necessary to pass the measure.

The withdrawal of the resolution doesn't appear to substantially change Campeau Corp.'s plans. Mr. Campeau said shareholder approval would be sought at a later date if management decides to sell more of the real-estate assets, which carry a total book value of about $1 billion.

Since Jan. 1, Campeau Corp. has raised $94 million from the sale of Canadian real estate.

The company had a net loss of $1.74 billion in the year ended Jan. 31, mainly because of heavy financing costs and write-downs related to its U.S. retailing operations, which have sought protection from creditors under Chapter 11 of the U.S. Bankruptcy Code.
