New Securities Issues

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/31/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATES

General Motors Acceptance Corp. -- $300 million of 8 3/4% notes due Aug. 1, 1995, priced at 99.64 to yield 8.84%. The non-callable notes were priced at a spread of 66 basis points above the Treasury's five-year note. Rated double-A-3 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and double-A-minus by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by First Boston Corp.

Westinghouse Credit Corp. -- $150 million of 8 7/8% notes due Aug. 1, 1995, priced at 99.974 to yield 8.88%. The noncallable notes were priced at 70 basis points above the Treasury's five-year note. Rated single-A-2 by Moody's and single-A by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Morgan Stanley & Co.

Salomon Inc. -- $100 million of 9% notes due Sept. 15, 1994, priced at par. The price was set at 91 basis points above the Treasury's four-year note. Rated single-A-2 by Moody's and single-A-plus by S&P, the issue will be sold through Salomon Brothers Inc.

Norwest Financial Inc. -- $100 million of 8 5/8% senior notes due Aug. 1, 1993, priced at 99.858 to yield 8.679%. The noncallable notes were priced at 63.5 basis points above the Treasury's three-year note. Rated double-A-3 by Moody's and double-A-minus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets.

Avco Financial Services Inc. -- $100 million of 8 7/8% notes due Jan. 15, 1995, priced at 99.75 to yield 8.95%. The non-callable notes were priced at 85 basis points above the Treasury's four-year note. Rated single-A-2 by Moody's and single-A by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Salomon Brothers Inc. Avco Financial is a unit of Textron Inc.

EUROBONDS

CMS No. 5 (special-purpose vehicle) -- #250 million of Eurobonds with final maturity of Aug. 24, 2027, at par issue price and fixed reoffering price of 99.85 via S.G. Warburg. Initial coupon of 0.25 point above three-month London interbank offered rate. After year 10, coupon steps up to a margin of 0.50 point. Expected to have an average life of 4.5 years. Fees 0.25.

National Australia Bank (Australia) -- #150 million of variable-rate notes due Sept. 6, 2000, via Salomon Brothers International Ltd. Initial coupon will float 35 basis points above three-month Libor for sterling. Alternative margin 65 basis points above Libor for first five years and 75 basis points above Libor thereafter. Fees undisclosed.

Credit Lyonnais (France) -- one billion French francs of 10% fungible Eurobonds due June 6, 1995, priced at 101.60 via Credit Lyonnais. Fungible from payment date with existing one billion franc issue launched May 9. Fees 1 7/8.

Staatsbank Berlin (East Germany) -- five billion West German marks of floating-rate notes at the six-month Frankfurt interbank offered rate plus 0.05 point, priced at 100.09 and due Aug. 3, 1993, via Deutsche GirozentraleDeutsche Kommunalbank. First rate fixing next Tuesday. Fees 0.10.

Baden-Wuerttemberg (West Germany) -- 300 million marks of bonds priced at par and due Sept. 4, 1996, via Trinkaus & Burkhardt. Coupon for first two years 9 1/2%. Coupon for final four years is 15 3/4% less the six-month Frankfurt interbank offered rate. Fees 0.30.
