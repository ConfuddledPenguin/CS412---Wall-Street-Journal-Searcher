Santa Fe Pacific Plans To Refinance Debt

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/31/90

CHICAGO -- Santa Fe Pacific Corp. announced plans to refinance its $780 million of high-yielding senior subordinated debentures through a debt-for-equity swap and redemptions. The 16% pay-in-kind debentures, due in 2003, were issued as part of a 1988 restructuring undertaken in the face of an unsuccessful acquisition overture. The railroad, real estate and natural-resources concern has said that refinancing the so-called PIK debentures, which became callable in March, was a prerequisite to its plans to spin off its real estate and energy operations.

Under the plan announced yesterday, debenture holders will be offered a chance to swap $1,000 face value of the debentures for Santa Fe Pacific common stock valued at $1,060. The exchange offer is limited to $200 million face value of the debentures.

If the offer is oversubscribed, the company said, the offer will be on a pro-rated basis. The value of the Santa Fe common to be exchanged will be based on the average of the stock's closing price on the 10 trading days prior to the Aug. 27 closing of the offer.

In addition, the company said, a subsidiary has filed a registration statement covering the planned offering of $210 million of variable rate exchange debentures due 2010. Under "certain circumstances," the company said, those debentures would be exchangeable for common units outstanding of Santa Fe Pacific Pipeline Partners, a limited partnership in which Santa Fe Pacific Corp. holds a 44% interest. Santa Fe said it intends to use the net proceeds from the offering of the variable-rate debentures to pay down an additional portion of the PIK issue.

The remaining about $400 million in outstanding PIK debentures will be redeemed at $1,030 in cash for each $1,000 face value, on Oct. 31. The company will issue long-term debt to finance the redemption, adding that it expects the interest rate on the long-term obligations to be "significantly below" the rate it is paying on the PIK issue.
