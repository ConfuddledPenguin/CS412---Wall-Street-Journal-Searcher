Business Brief -- Equitable Real Estate Shopping: Two Malls That Are Bulk Of Assets Could Be Sold

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/31/90

Equitable Real Estate Shopping Centers L.P., New York, said it may sell the two shopping malls that make up the bulk of its assets. Shearson Lehman Hutton Inc., general partner for the publicly traded partnership, said that a decision hadn't been reached to sell the properties and that no transactions are imminent.

The partnership acquired the shopping centers, Brookdale Mall near Minneapolis and Northland Mall near Detroit, for a total of $130.5 million from Equitable Life Assurance Society of the U.S. in 1987.

Shares in the partnership closed at $6.875, up 62.5 cents, in the New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday.
