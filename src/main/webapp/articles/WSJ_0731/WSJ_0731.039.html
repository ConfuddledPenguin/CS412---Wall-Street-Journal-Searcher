National Heritage And Life Care Centers Call Off Their Merger

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/31/90

DALLAS -- National Heritage Inc. and Life Care Centers of America said they decided to call off their planned merger because of recent reversals concerning National Heritage assets. Under the terms of a deal struck last April, National Heritage and its 22% owner Southmark Corp. were to contribute general partnership interests, management contracts, mortgages and other assets to a new publicly traded entity in exchange for a 40% equity stake in the concern.

Life Care Centers, a closely held company based in Cleveland, Tenn., was to contribute its assets in return for a 60% stake in the new entity.

Since then, Southmark has ceded control of its private limited partnerships to Lpimc Inc. of Southbury, Conn., after losing a proxy contest. Southmark is disputing an apparent loss in a second proxy vote. The takeovers not only reduce the Southmark assets to be contributed to the new company, but also jeopardize National Heritage management contracts at the nursing homes owned by the syndicates.

National Heritage said it is actively seeking management and lease contracts with unaffiliated nursing home companies. It is also streamlining its operations by divesting subsidiaries unrelated to nursing home care. In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, National Heritage stock closed at 68.75 cents a share, down 18.75 cents.
