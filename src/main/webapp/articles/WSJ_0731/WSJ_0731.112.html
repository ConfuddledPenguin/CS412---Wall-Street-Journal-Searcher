Business Brief -- Cyclops Industries Inc.: Steelmaker Posts Net Loss Of $11.3 Million for Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/31/90

Cyclops Industries Inc., citing lower prices on flat-rolled steel products and charges related to a previously announced restructuring, reported a second-quarter net loss of $11.3 million, or $1.62 a share. In the same period a year earlier, the Pittsburgh-based steelmaker had a profit of $13 million, or $1.81 a share. Sales fell 12% to $306.2 million from $348.8 million.

Earlier, the company said it expected to report losses for the quarter and the year as a result of restructuring its specialty steel division and realigning certain carbon strip operations.

The restructuring involved separating its Cytemp Specialty Steel division into two business units and phasing out certain products. That action will result in the closing of a Pittsburgh plant in the second half of this year, the company said. Even without the restructuring charge, Cyclops said the Cytemp division had a $5.3 million pretax loss for the first six months of the year compared with a pretax operating income of $5.3 million in the first six months of 1989.
