International Brief -- Dresdner Bank AG: Partial Operating Profit Rose 8.9% in First Half

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/31/90

Dresdner Bank AG said its group partial operating profit rose 8.9% in the first half to 956.1 million marks ($593.1 million) from 878.1 million marks a year earlier. West Germany's second-largest banking group said its parent bank alone posted a 24% rise in partial operating profit in the first half to 603.8 million marks from 487.7 million marks a year earlier.

Dresdner, like most West German companies, reports its financial results on a world-wide, or group, basis as well as on a domestic, or parent company, level that excludes foreign and some domestic subsidiaries.

Partial operating data are before taxes and exclude gains or losses from the company's trading operations for its own account.

Frankfurt-based Dresdner didn't report group full operating profit, which includes the trading results, for the first half. But the company characterized the full operating profit for the group in the first half as "developing clearly more favorably" than the partial figures.

For the parent bank, full operating profit attained "double-digit" growth, Dresdner said, without providing data.
