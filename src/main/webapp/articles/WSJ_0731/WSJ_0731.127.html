Business Brief -- Consolidated Enfield Corp.: Toronto Company Increases Its Stake in Packaging Unit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/31/90

Consolidated Enfield Corp. said it exercised rights granted by Consumers Packaging Inc. to acquire an additional 4,529,781 common shares of the Toronto-based packaging concern. The 39.6-million Canadian-dollar (US$34.2 million) purchase brings Enfield's stake in Consumers Packaging to 57.6% from about 50%.

Consumers Packaging said it intends to use the C$45.4 million proceeds of the rights issue to reduce corporate debt.

Enfield, a Toronto-based holding company indirectly controlled by financiers Peter and Edward Bronfman, said the acquisition was for investment purposes.

Earlier this month, Enfield paid C$17.5 million to acquire a block of 1,224,489 Consumers Packaging shares from a former Enfield subsidiary, Federal Pioneer Ltd.

In trading on the Toronto Stock Exchange yesterday, Consumers Packaging closed at C$9, up 25 Canadian cents. Shares of Consolidated Enfield closed at C$10, down C$1.50. Before the rights offering, the company had about 20.8 million common shares outstanding.
