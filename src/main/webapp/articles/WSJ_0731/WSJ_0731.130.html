NEC-China Venture Pact Near

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/31/90

HONG KONG -- NEC Corp., one of Japan's top computer makers, and Capital Iron & Steel Complex, China's leading steel producer, are expected next month to sign an agreement to set up a $150 million venture to produce integrated circuits, the English-language China Daily reported. The planned Beijing factory is to be capable of producing 50 million integrated circuits a year, the paper said, but didn't provide further details.

When NEC outlined the project last January, it said Japan would first have to lift economic sanctions it imposed on China following the Tiananmen Square massacre in June 1989. Japanese Prime Minister Toshiki Kaifu, at the economic summit meeting in Houston this month, obtained the understanding of other major industrial countries to resume economic relations with China, and this apparently cleared the way for the NEC project to proceed.
