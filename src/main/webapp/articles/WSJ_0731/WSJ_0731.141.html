International: Japan Reports June Drop In Foreign Trade Pacts

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 07/31/90

TOKYO -- Import and export contracts signed by Japan's major trading houses in June edged lower from year-earlier levels, an industry group said. Import contracts dropped 4.1% to 1.517 trillion yen ($10.18 billion) in June, after plunging 32.4% in May. The May decline was the first year-on-year fall in import contracts since October 1989.

Export contracts for the month were almost unchanged from year-earlier levels, edging down 0.4% to 1.079 trillion yen.

An official of the Japan Foreign Trade Council said the declines represent a reaction from high year-earlier levels. He called the factors behind the declines temporary, saying they don't suggest a reversal of the overall upward trend. For the January-June half-year period, export contracts climbed 18.8% from year-earlier levels, while import contracts rose 12.8%.

The June results reported by the council are based on contract statistics provided by 18 major Japanese trading companies and are widely considered to be short-term indicators of overall trade trends. The trading companies furnishing contract statistics account for roughly half of Japan's general exports and about two-thirds of imports.

The council said June was the first time in 26 months that import contracts have fallen below year-earlier levels for two consecutive months.

Despite a 66.5% rise in fuel import contracts to 688.5 billion yen, almost all other product categories declined from year-earlier levels, led by the machinery sector.

The council said the 72.8% plunge in machinery-import contracts to 72.5 billion yen was a reaction to the large number of airline-import contracts signed the year before.
