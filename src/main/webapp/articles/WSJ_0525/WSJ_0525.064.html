Short Interest Falls for OTC's Smaller Stocks --- Largest Issues Ease by 1.74% In Month Ended in May; Computer Shares Fell

  • Author: Anne Newman
  • Date: 05/25/90

NEW YORK -- Short interest among smaller over-the-counter stocks dropped 25.9% in the month ended mid-May, giving back nearly all of the increase a month earlier. Meanwhile, short interest among the OTC market's largest stocks continued to ease, slipping 1.74%.

The short interest in several computer stocks dropped significantly, including Intel. The short position in the Santa Clara, Calif., semiconductor maker plummeted 73.6% after last month's 64.8% rise. Short positions also fell more than 50% in Softsel Computer Products, Inglewood, Calif., and Conner Peripherals, San Jose, Calif. On the other hand, the short interest in Oracle Systems, Belmont, Calif., rose 57.2%.

Nordstrom led the list of companies with the largest increase in the number of shares sold short. Short interest in the Seattle retailer surged 104.52% to 2.66 million of Nordstrom 81.6 million outstanding shares.

First Executive, the Los Angeles life insurance company that once held a large portfolio of junk bonds, retained its lead with a short position of 10.4 million shares. In distant second place was Valley National Corp., a Phoenix, Ariz., commercial bank, with a short position of 4.2 million shares.

Several other financial issues surfaced on lists indicating large volume or percentage increases in short interest, among them: BayBanks, Boston; Bank South, Atlanta; Washington Bancorp., Washington, D.C.; SouthTrust Corp., Birmingham, Ala.; and Great Bay Bankshares, Dover, N.H.

To sell short, traders borrow shares and sell them in hopes the stock's price will decline so they can buy back the shares later more cheaply. Short interest is the number of shares that haven't yet been purchased for return to lenders.

Although a substantial short position reflects heavy speculation that a stock's price will decline, some investors consider an increase in short interest bullish because the borrowed shares eventually must be bought back. Some short interest doesn't reflect any opinion on the stock at all but shows up as part of another strategy, such as arbitrage or dividend capture.

The National Association of Securities Dealers, the self-regulatory agency for the OTC markets, reported that short interest in 2,338 National Market System issues, the largest OTC stocks, slid to 248,818,151 shares at mid-May from 253,218,788 in mid-April among 2,346 shares.

Short interest in 1,301 smaller securities traded on the National Association of Securities Dealers Automated Quotations system fell to 31,671,000 shares for May, down from 42,790,704 for April among 1,252 issues.

The National Market System's short-interest ratio at mid-May fell to 2.59 from 2.69 a month earlier, while the short-interest ratio for other OTC issues dropped to 0.9 from 1.24 a month earlier.

The ratio is the number of trading days at the market's average daily trading volume that would be required to convert the total short interest position. It is considered by many a measure of negative sentiment. Some analysts suggest, however, that the ratio has weakened in value as an indicator because options and other investment products can be used to hedge short positions.
