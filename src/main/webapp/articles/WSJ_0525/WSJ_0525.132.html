Marketing & Media: Last `Twin Peaks' Episode Hooked 22% of Audience

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/25/90

NEW YORK -- The final episode of "Twin Peaks," a television drama, left a passel of unsolved mysteries but lured 22% of the audience -- the highest share since its debut six weeks ago. "Twin Peaks" was the top-rated program in the 10-to-11 p.m. time period. It was seen by 12.6% of all U.S. homes and by 22% of all TV sets in use at that hour, according to A.C. Nielsen Co. ratings.

The final episode fared well among viewers under 50 years old, whom advertisers want to reach. It drew 34% of women ages 18 to 34, who were watching television Wednesday night; 26% of women ages 35 to 49; and 28% and 22% of men in those respective age groups. The series has ranked 49th in terms of how many homes tune in -- but it ranks No. 28 among women ages 18 to 49.

"Twin Peaks" joins the ABC lineup as a full series next fall. Fans will have to wait until then to resolve myriad mysteries the series raised.

"Twin Peaks" earned the highest ratings of any ABC show in its time slot in five years, said Alan Wurtzel, senior vice president at the Capital Cities/ABC Inc. network.
