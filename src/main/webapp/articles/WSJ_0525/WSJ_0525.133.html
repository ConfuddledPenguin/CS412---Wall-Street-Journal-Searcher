Some Boeing 737s Lack Pin for Steering Controls

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/25/90

SEATTLE -- Boeing Co. said that airline inspections of its 737-model jetliners have turned up seven planes missing the cotter pin that secures a bolt in the planes' steering controls. The pins are being installed by Boeing field-service personnel. "It's a quick adjustment that just takes a second or two," a spokesman for the aircraft maker said.

The inspections were conducted after a missing pin caused a control wheel to come off in the hands of the co-pilot of a West German airliner in flight last week. Boeing later discovered a missing pin on a second 737 at its Renton, Wash., production plant.

Apart from the plane being readied for delivery, the defective planes are all owned by overseas airlines. Boeing said the West German report is the only one it has received of an in-flight incident. The pilot landed the plane safely using the second set of controls.

The Federal Aviation Administration ordered U.S. airlines to inspect pilot controls on three models of Boeing 737 jetliners after Boeing reported the cotter-pin problem last week. Yesterday, an agency spokesman said that so far it doesn't appear that any domestic carriers are affected. The agency said it expected a final report "in a matter of weeks."
