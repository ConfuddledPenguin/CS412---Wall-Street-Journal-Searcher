Service America Says It Is in Default on Loan By Subsidiary of GE

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/25/90

STAMFORD, Conn. -- Service America Corp. said it is in default on a $195 million loan from General Electric Capital Corp. and expects its losses to widen for the fiscal year ended March 31. The food-service company blamed higher operating costs, increased competition, and weakness in factory employment in automobile plants and textile mills where it has significant operations. The company also cited special charges for severance and relocation expenses related to senior management changes made in the last six months.

Service America said its senior creditor, GE Capital, advised the company that under the loan accord, Service America won't be permitted to make any payments on its subordinated debt. The company said a $10.8 million interest payment on its $127 million subordinated debt is due June 15.

Service America said it and GE Capital, a unit of General Electric Co., Fairfield, Conn., are discussing a restructuring of its senior and subordinated debt. The company plans to file a registration statement within 45 days to offer a proposal to restructure its subordinated debt. Meanwhile, it expects to be able to conduct normal operations and to continue to draw against its $70 million revolving-credit agreement with GE Capital to make payments to suppliers, employees and other third parties except for subordinated creditors.

Service America said it expects to report that net losses in the fiscal-fourth quarter and full year widened but that revenue increased in both periods. In the fourth quarter of last year, the company reported a net loss of $6.3 million on revenue of $256.1 million. For the full 1989 fiscal year, Service America had a net loss of $14.4 million on revenue of $1.04 billion.
