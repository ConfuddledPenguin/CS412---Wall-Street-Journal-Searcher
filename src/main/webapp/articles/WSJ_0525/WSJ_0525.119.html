Sikes, Premark Agree To Settle Suits Filed After Buy-Out Offer

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/25/90

LAKELAND, Fla. -- Sikes Corp. and Premark International Inc. said they agreed to settle two lawsuits that had been filed against Sikes after Premark had offered to acquire the company. In April, Sikes agreed to be acquired by Premark for $16.40 for each Class A share, or about $201 million. Moreover, Premark will purchase 861,136 Class B Sikes common shares for $26.78 apiece.

The two complaints alleged that Sikes' directors had breached their fiduciary duties by failing to obtain the highest price for the company and by adopting an allegedly unfair purchase-price allocation between the Class A and Class B shares.

Under the settlement, Sikes will establish a special fund for Class A shares and will reduce to $3 million from $5 million a fee payable to Premark.

Premark, based in Deerfield, Ill., markets consumer and restaurant products. Sikes makes ceramic tile.
