Initial State Jobless Claims Were Flat in May 12 Week

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/25/90

WASHINGTON -- Initial claims for regular state unemployment benefits totaled a seasonally adjusted 353,000 in the week ended May 12, unchanged from the previous week, the Labor Department said. The number of people receiving regular state benefits in the week ended May 5 fell to a seasonally adjusted 2,379,000, or 2.3% of those covered by unemployment insurance, from 2,440,000 in the previous week, when the rate also was 2.3%.

Counting all state and federal benefit programs, the number of people receiving unemployment benefits in the week ended May 5 fell to 2,329,600 from 2,460,900 a week earlier. These figures aren't seasonally adjusted.
