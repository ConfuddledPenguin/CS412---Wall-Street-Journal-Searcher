OTC Focus: Market Gains in Contrast to Big Board; Technologies Continue to Spur Rise

  • Author: Anne Newman
  • Date: 05/25/90

Over-the-counter stocks rose buoyantly for the 11th consecutive session. But traders warned the market's rally could stumble today because of a pessimistic earnings outlook released last night by software maker Adobe Systems. The Nasdaq Composite Index rose 1.42 to 458.29, up 0.31%, in contrast to the 0.71 slip in the Dow Jones Industrial Average. Advancing shares led decliners, 1,043 to 892, as volume fell to 174.3 million shares from 188.7 million Wednesday.

Adobe, based in Mountain View, Calif., announced after the market closed that it expects earnings for the second quarter ending June 1 to be lower than its expectations and analysts' estimates. Adobe shares plunged about 15 in "third market" after-hours trading to a range of 32 1/2 to 35 1/2, said Jerry Newman, institutional trader for Cowen & Co. in San Francisco. Adobe closed in Nasdaq trading at 50 1/2, up 1 1/2.

Mr. Newman cautioned that the after-hours drop in Adobe may spill over to some other technology shares, particularly software stocks. However, he said that he expects to see profit-taking, rather than "panic selling."

Even before the Adobe news, traders were expecting some profit-taking in the OTC market today because of the prolonged run-up and the three-day weekend.

Jerry Mullins, head of OTC trading at Dean Witter Reynolds, echoing Mr. Meisler, said he expects some investors will take profits this morning before leaving early for the three-day Memorial Day weekend. "I'm still sitting here with a lot of `buy' orders, but they've become a little pricey at this point," he said, echoing Mr. Meisler. Both men said they're seeing buy orders for smaller stocks from large institutions.

While larger stocks are leading the OTC market's recent gains, acquisition interest in smaller OTC companies is finally attracting some investors to those shares, said Binkley Shorts, portfolio manager of the Over-The-Counter Securities Fund, Fort Washington, Pa. The average capitalization of companies in the $300 million fund is $60 million.

"There's a breath of life in small-cap" stocks fueled by bargain hunters looking for companies to buy, he said in an interview. Companies such as Stockholder Systems, COBE Laboratories and Neeco recently agreed to be acquired, he noted. Others, such as Child World, have hired investment bankers to explore "strategic alternatives." And some companies, such as Horizon Industries, are trying to fight off potential takeover threats, he added.

Technology shares continued to rise yesterday amid analysts' recommendations.

Dell Computer moved into the most actives list after the Austin, Texas, personal computer maker reported net income for the first quarter ended May 4 rose to 28 cents a share from 11 cents a year earlier. Dell gained 1 7/8 to 12 on 2.2 million shares.

Novell jumped 4 3/4 to 49 on 2.9 million shares after the Provo, Utah, maker of personal computer networks reported late Thursday that net income for the second quarter ended April 28 jumped to 60 cents a share from 35 cents a year earlier. The strong performance prompted Shearson analyst Maria F. Lewis to upgrade her short-term investment opinion on the stock to "attractive" from "neutral." The analyst also said that Novell's "increasing status as a benchmark technology issue" makes it likely the stock will continue to perform well as technology stocks come back into vogue.

Lotus added 2 3/4 to 36 3/4 on 1.5 million shares. The stock received favorable comments from Cowen & Co. analyst Mary Meeker, who repeated her "buy" opinion on the stock. Also, the New York Times favorably profiled a new software program developed by the Cambridge, Mass., company.

Among other technology issues, Quantum gained 1 3/4 to 22 5/8. Shearson analyst Doug van Dorsten said the Milpitas, Calif., disk drive maker's sales and earnings could grow 35% to 50% over the next 12 to 18 months. Mr. van Dorsten upgraded his opinion on Quantum to a near-term "buy" from "attractive."

Players International rose 1 1/16 to 5 1/8 after the Calabasas, Calif., gaming-services company said it expects to report pretax operating income for the fiscal year ended March 31 of $2 million, up from $655,000 in the previous year.

Shares of National Media tumbled 1 3/8 to 10 1/2. William Campbell, vice president and secretary of the provider of marketing services, told Dow Jones Professional Investor Report he heard that an investment newsletter issued a "sell" recommendation on the stock late Wednesday. Mr. Campbell also cited speculation that earnings for fiscal 1990 ended in March will be disappointing. However, he said there's no truth to that speculation and added that he remains comfortable with estimates that earnings will be reported at $1 a share for the fiscal year, up from 28 cents in fiscal 1989.
