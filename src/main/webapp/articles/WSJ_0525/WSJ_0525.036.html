Who's News: Gerber Scientific Picks Webster for President Of Its GSP Subsidiary

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/25/90

SOUTH WINDSOR, Conn. -- Gerber Scientific Inc. said it named a new president to its Gerber Scientific Products Inc. subsidiary, and formed a new wholly owned subsidiary to focus on the eyewear industry. The factory-automation concern also reported an 8.3% profit drop for its fourth quarter ended April 30, and a 1.3% profit drop for the year.

Gerber said Ronald Webster, a 30-year company veteran, will replace David Logan as president of GSP, which makes automation systems for the sign-making, graphic arts and related industries.

Mr. Logan, who joined Gerber in 1957 and founded GSP in 1980, has decided to retire, the company said. He will continue to serve as a director and consultant to GSP and Gerber Optical Inc., the new Gerber subsidiary.

The company named Kenneth Wood, former head of Gerber's optical division, as president of Gerber Optical. The subsidiary, which aims to automate eyewear makers, was formed from Mr. Wood's old division.

In the fourth quarter, Gerber said it had a 1.3% gain in sales, to $80.9 million from $79.9 million in the year-ago period. But profit slipped to $8.3 million, or 35 cents a share, from $9.1 million, or 38 cents a share on a greater number of shares outstanding.

For the fiscal year, Gerber posted a 2.6% sales gain, to $307.2 million from $299.3 million in 1989. Profit fell slightly, to $32.4 million from $32.9 million, but pershare earnings rose one cent to $1.36 because of a 1.9% drop in average shares outstanding.
