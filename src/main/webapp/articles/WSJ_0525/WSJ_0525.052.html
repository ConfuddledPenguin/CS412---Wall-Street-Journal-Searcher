Marketing & Media: Dow Jones's Telerate Lays Off 41 Workers In Cost-Cutting Move

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/25/90

NEW YORK -- Telerate Inc. said it laid off 41 employees in its Americas and corporate groups as part of cost-cutting moves. Telerate is the financial information-service subsidiary of Dow Jones & Co., publisher of The Wall Street Journal.

In a letter to employees, Carl M. Valenti, Telerate's president, said sales staffs were "only minimally affected" by the cutback. Telerate has about 2,500 employees. Mr. Valenti said he doesn't anticipate any further layoffs in the Americas group, which includes U.S., Canadian and Latin American operations, or elsewhere in the immediate future.

Mr. Valenti said Telerate's Europe-Gulf region and Asia-Pacific region "continue to show strong progress" and are reporting double-digit sales growth. However, the Americas group "is having a much tougher time this year" due to contraction in Wall Street firms and continued problems in the U.S. thrift industry.

He said the Americas group revenue has declined and Telerate earnings "are down significantly so far this year, due mostly to major increases in expenses."
