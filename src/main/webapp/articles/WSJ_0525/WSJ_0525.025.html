Japan Prime Rate May Drop

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/25/90

TOKYO -- Japan's long-term prime rate is likely to drop 0.3 percentage point to 7.6%, according to an official at one of the long-term credit banks. The decision by the three long-term credit banks, which reset their benchmark lending rate at the end of each month, is expected Friday afternoon, the official said. The rate is set 90 basis points above the coupon rate of the new monthly issues of five-year debentures of the banks. The three institutions are Industrial Bank of Japan, Long-Term Credit Bank of Japan and Nippon Credit Bank.

The official cites the improvement in the Japanese long-term credit markets.
