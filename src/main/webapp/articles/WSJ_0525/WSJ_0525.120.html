Imperial Corp. Unit Loses Attempt to Bar A Receivership Move

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/25/90

WASHINGTON -- The Office of Thrift Supervision said a federal judge denied a request by Imperial Savings Association, San Diego, which sought an injunction barring regulators from placing the thrift in receivership. Imperial is already under government conservatorship. Imperial, a unit of Imperial Corp. of America, asked for the injunction in the wake of a March ruling involving another thrift. In the earlier ruling, a district court said that the top savings-and-loan regulator hadn't been properly approved by Congress and thus couldn't order that thrifts be taken over by the government. Congress then moved quickly to approve a new head of the Office of Thrift Supervision, Timothy Ryan, in April and made that argument moot.

The Office of Thrift Supervision said U.S. District Judge Judith Keep in San Diego ruled that Imperial "hadn't shown its arguments were likely to succeed, and that the potential harm to Imperial was outweighed by the harm to the public that could result."

Under conservatorship, the government takes management control of the thrift. Under receivership, the government wipes out the owners' interests in thrift and can sell the assets. The OTS didn't say whether it plans to place Imperial in receivership.
