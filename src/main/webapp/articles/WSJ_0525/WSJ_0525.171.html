New Securities Issues

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/25/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATES

National Rural Utilities Co-op -- $150 million of 9 1/2% collateralized trust bonds, due May 15, 1997, priced at 99.931 to yield 9.512%. The noncallable bonds were priced at a spread of 85 basis points above the Treasury's seven-year note. Rated double-A-3 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and single-A-plus by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Shearson Lehman Hutton Inc.

Arizona Public Service Co. -- $125 million of 10 1/4% first mortgage bonds, due May 15, 2020, priced at 99 to yield 10.358%. The bonds were priced at a spread of 180 basis points above the Treasury's 30-year bond. Rated Baa-3 by Moody's and triple-B-minus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Salomon Brothers Inc. Arizona Public Service is a unit of Pinnacle West Capital Corp.

Southern New England Telephone Co. -- $120 million of 9 5/8% medium-term notes, due Feb. 15, 2030, priced at 98.05 to yield 9.824%. Rated double-A-1 by Moody's and double-Aplus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Salomon Brothers Inc.

TRW Inc. -- $100 million issue of 9.35% notes, due June 4, 2020, was won in competitive bidding by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets and was priced at 99.650 to yield 9.40%. The noncallable notes, which may be put back at par to the company in 10 years, was priced at a spread of 83 basis points above the Treasury's 10-year note. The issue is rated single-A-2 by Moody's and single-A-minus by S&P.

ITT Financial Corp. -- $100 million of senior notes, due June 1, 2010, was priced at par to yield 8 7/8%. The notes, which may be put back to the company in the fifth, 10th and 15th year, was priced at a spread of 37 basis points above the Treasury's when-issued five-year note. Rated single-A-1 by Moody's and single-A-plus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets. ITT Financial is a unit of ITT Corp.

MORTGAGE-BACKED

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $500 million of Remic mortgage securities is being offered in 14 classes by Bear Stearns & Co. Inc. The offering, Series 1990-72, is backed by 9 1/2% Fannie Mae securities.

OTHER

Midlantic National Bank -- $254 million of securities backed by automobile loans with a coupon rate of 9.20%, priced at 99 45/64 to yield 9.33%, via First Boston Corp. The issue, through Midlantic 1990-A Auto Grantor Trust, was priced at a yield spread of 84 basis points over the Treasury's 8 1/2% when-issued two-year notes of May 1992. The final maturity is May 15, 1995. The issue has an estimated average life of about 1.4 years assuming monthly prepayments at 1.3 pc of the original loan balance. The issue is rated triple-A by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and Standard & Poor's Corp., based on the quality of the underlying loans and a surety bond covering 12% of the deal from Capital Markets Assurance Corp., an insurance company specializing in financial guarantee owned by Citicorp. There is no guarantee from Midlantic Corp., the parent company.

EUROBONDS

Istituto Bancario San Paolo di Torino (Italy) -- 10 billion yen of 7.3% Eurobonds due June 21, 1993, via a special purpose vehicle, the Law Debenture Trust Corp. Priced at 101 3/8 to yield 7.3% less fees. Lead managed by Fuji International Ltd. Fees 1 3/8.

Rautaruukki (Finland) -- 200 million Finnish markka of 13 1/2% Eurobonds due July 5, 1995, priced at 101 7/8 to yield 13 1/2% less fees via Postipankki. Fees 1 7/8.
