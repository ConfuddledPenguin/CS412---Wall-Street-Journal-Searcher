Who's News: Ogden Corp. Names Richard Ablon to Post Of Chief Executive

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/25/90

NEW YORK -- Ogden Corp.'s board named Richard Ablon, who heads the company's largest business unit, as its new president and chief executive officer. As chief executive, Mr. Ablon, 40 years old, succeeds his father Ralph Ablon, 73 years old, who remains chairman of the facilities-management company. The post of president had been vacant.

Richard Ablon will continue as president of Ogden Allied Services Corp., which generates more than $1 billion a year in revenue from abatement and decontamination services, management of buildings and industrial facilities, airline-industry services, and promotion of sports and entertainment events.

The younger Mr. Ablon said company operations will continue as in the past. "In terms of day-to-day reality, it isn't much different from before." However, he said Ogden is unlikely to follow through on a possible spinoff of Ogden Allied, which his father had mentioned to analysts in a November meeting.

The younger Mr. Ablon also predicted Ogden will post record revenue and earnings in 1990. The previous highs for both measures were set in 1989, when revenue totaled $1.37 billion and earnings hit $67 million, or $1.68 a share.

Ogden's other major business, Ogden Projects Inc., builds and manages solid-waste-to-energy plants and conducts hazardous-waste disposal operations. It had $329 million in revenue last year. Ogden Corp. holds about an 85% interest in Ogden Projects, which is a public company.

Richard Ablon joined the company after graduation from college in 1971. He previously managed Ogden's entertainment and aviation businesses.
