Who's News: Great American's Luce Plans to Retire At 65, Later This Year

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/25/90

SAN DIEGO, Calif. -- Gordon Luce, chairman and chief executive officer of Great American Bank, said he would retire this year upon reaching age 65. "It is a time for change, and perhaps a new perspective will assist the bank in moving forward in these changing times," Mr. Luce told the annual meeting. The thrift doesn't have a mandatory retirement program for executives.

A longtime friend and advisor to Ronald Reagan, Mr. Luce has been chief executive of the thrift since 1969 and chairman since 1979. He will remain on the Great American board.

During the meeting, Mr. Luce also confirmed reports that company officials had attempted to lure Federal Home Loan Bank of San Francisco president James Cirona to succeed him, but that the parties "could not work out the appropriate package for his giving up the pretty outstanding position he already has."

Great American is the nation's eighth-largest thrift but has had huge loan problems in Arizona and must raise $350 million in new capital by year end or face a possible federal takeover.
