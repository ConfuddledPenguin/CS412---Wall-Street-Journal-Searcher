Kollmorgen to Close Division If a Buyer Cannot Be Found

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/25/90

SIMSBURY, Conn. -- Kollmorgen Corp. plans to close its multiwire division in Hicksville, N.Y., in 60 days, if the company fails to sell it. The closing of the division would complete Kollmorgen's planned disposition of its electronic interconnections business. James A. Eder, Kollmorgen secretary, said the closing, together with other sales in the business segment, wouldn't significantly affect earnings.

The multiwire plant, which has 142 employees, manufactures proprietary interconnection products. Kollmorgen will retain its operations in motors and controls and electro-optical instruments.

John Youngblood, Kollmorgen chairman, president and chief executive, said the company would continue to try to find a buyer for the division, but he said the company believed that is unlikely.

Mr. Eder said the entire electronic interconnections business was classified as a discontinued operation last September, when the company announced a restructuring that called for disposing of the entire segment. That segment once acccounted for one-third of Kollmorgen's operations. Mr. Eder said provisions against earnings for a possible closing of the multiwire division had previously been made.

Kollmorgen has been trying to fend off a takeover attempt by Vernitron Corp., a closely held motors and controls company based in Deer Park, N.Y. The two companies are suing each other over a collapsed merger agreement, and Vernitron is mounting a proxy contest for control of Kollmorgen's board. Vernitron has said it would offer $15 a share for 50% of Kollmorgen's shares, if it is successful in its proxy solicitation.
