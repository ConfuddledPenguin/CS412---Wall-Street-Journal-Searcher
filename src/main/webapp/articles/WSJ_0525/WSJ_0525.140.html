Business Brief -- Par Pharmaceutical Inc.: President Granted Proxies Equal to 2 Million Shares

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/25/90

Par Pharmaceutical Inc. said its president was granted proxies from four shareholders to vote about two million of its shares in matters relating to board membership. The proxies, when combined with the shares owned by the president, Kenneth I. Sawyer, give him beneficial ownership of 17.8% of the Spring Valley, N.Y., generic drug company. In a Securities and Exchange Commission filing, Mr. Sawyer said he was granted the proxies to "help assure" certain governmental agencies involved in investigating and reviewing the generic drug industry and the company that the shareholders support new management installed in September.

The proxies give Mr. Sawyer the right to vote the about two million shares in matters relating to election or removal of directors and filling board vacancies.

Par has been the target of federal investigations for the past two years. Earlier this year Par and its majority-owned Quad Pharmaceuticals Inc. unit were fined a total of $400,000 for illegal gratuity payments made to an official of the Food and Drug Administration. Par and Quad both have recalled products amid concerns about adherence to regulatory procedures.
