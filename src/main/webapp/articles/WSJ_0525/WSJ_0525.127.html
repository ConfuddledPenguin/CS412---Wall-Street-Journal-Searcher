Hyundai Motor, Union Sign Revised New Pact

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 05/25/90

SEOUL -- After overnight talks, Hyundai Motor Co.'s union signed a revised new labor contract with the company. Union President Lee Sang-bum, who signed the document, said the union's leadership decided to tell some 27,000 union members to return to work at Hyundai's Ulsan plant by ending the strike that began May 15.

The plant, 160 miles southeast of Seoul, is South Korea's largest auto plant. Hyundai Motor is a unit of Hyundai Corp.

The union president told reporters that the union leadership decided to effect the new contract without putting it to a vote of members.

The union and the management had to renegotiate after a proposed earlier contract agreed upon through negotiations was voted down by a majority of union workers in a general meeting Tuesday. They said the union's demands weren't fully met.

The contract contained an average monthly pay increase of 7.6% or a raise of $54.93. In addition, it calls for an average increase of $20.91 in production and other monthly allowances, up $5.35 dollars from the amount in the rejected contract.

The union had demanded a 19% increase in their monthly average pay of $621 while the company had offered a 6% raise.
