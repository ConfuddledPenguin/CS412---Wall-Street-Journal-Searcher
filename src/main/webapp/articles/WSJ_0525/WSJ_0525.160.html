Japan's Crude-Oil Imports Rise

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/25/90

TOKYO -- Crude-oil imports to Japan rose 26.7% on a yearly basis in April to 119.1 million barrels, the Ministry of International Trade and Industry said. The increase reflects increased domestic refining and Japanese refiners' apparent effort to buy above-average quantities of oil early in the year. The refiners had expected oil prices to rise during this year, but prices have fallen. Refiners have found themselves largely unable to take advantage of the lower prices, because of a lack of storage space.

The kinds of oil Japan imported in April show the country's move into a lighter -- and more expensive -- crude refining slate. Mideast nations supplied 71.6% of Japan's needs, or 85.2 million barrels.

Domestic petroleum products sales rose 3.3% from April 1989. Sales of fuel oil, jet fuel, and naphtha all fell from year-earlier levels. Sales of kerosene, vehicle fuels, and gasoil were all higher. Products exports were sharply higher, as a result of liberalized government refining policies.
