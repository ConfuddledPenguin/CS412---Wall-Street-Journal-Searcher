Business Brief -- Kinark Corp.: Steel Partners L.P. Offer Of $24.8 Million Is Rejected

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/25/90

Kinark Corp. said its directors rejected an unsolicited $7-a-share, or $24.8 million, offer from Steel Partners L.P., but said it would consider another proposal later. Kinark, a Tulsa, Okla., owner of galvanizing plants and chemical-storage facilities, said the board concluded that the company is best served by continuing to pursue its present business plan. It also said the offer was subject to several conditions, which made the possibility of completing a transaction uncertain.

However, Kinark said it will consider a new proposal from Steel Partners, a New York investment partnership, after Kinark completes the planned sale of its Camelot Hotels for $12 million, if the offer has fewer uncertainties that the most recent proposal. Steel Partners has indicated than it owns about 4.4% of Kinark.

Kinark also said it expects to disclose election results from its Wednesday annual meeting around June 1. Altair Corp., Lincolnshire, Ill., which owns 6.7% of Kinark, nominated three directors to Kinark's eight-member board. Those candidates were nominated in opposition to Kinark's three director nominees.
