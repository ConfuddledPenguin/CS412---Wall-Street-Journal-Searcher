Money-Fund Assets Rose $94.7 Million During Recent Week

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/25/90

NEW YORK -- Assets of the nation's 503 money-market funds rose $94.7 million, to $381.41 billion, in the week ended Wednesday, according to the Investment Company Institute, a Washington trade association. Assets of 242 general-purpose funds fell $750.1 million, to $129.48 billion; assets of 102 broker-dealer funds rose $703.4 million, to $159.13 billion. Assets of 159 institutional funds rose $141.4 million, to $92.8 billion.

Mutual-fund assets were revised for the previous week ended May 16 to $381.32 billion. The reported decrease of $1.78 billion was revised to a drop of $2.18 billion.

"After a string of weekly declines caused by a variety of transitory factors, assets of money-market funds rose slightly in the last reporting week," said Dr. Jacob Dreyer, vice president and chief economist at the institute. "The modest rebound in their assets occurred despite rising stock and bond prices, which are usually associated with money being switched out of short-term and into long-term funds."
