Ex-Thortec Officials Settle SEC Charges Of Inflating Results

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/25/90

WASHINGTON -- The Securities and Exchange Commission charged four former officials of Thortec International Inc. with overstating the company's revenue and earnings in 1986 and 1987. Without admitting or denying the charges, each of the officials consented to a permanent injunction barring them from future violations of securities laws.

Thortec, which has since changed its name to URS Corp., is a San Francisco-based architectural and engineering firm. The four individuals are Richard Towle, former chief financial officer; Arthur Whipple, former chief accounting officer; Arthur Stromberg, former chairman and chief executive officer; and Franklin Agardy, former chief operating officer.

The SEC alleged that the individuals manipulated the company's accounting records to record an extra $8.4 million of revenue and earnings for the fiscal year ended Oct. 31, 1986, when the company reported revenue of $115.7 million and net income of $8.7 million.

The commission also alleged that results for the first two quarters of fiscal 1987 were inflated by $5 million.

"The individuals settled to put the matter behind them and avoid protracted litigation," said Theodore Levine, a Washington, D.C., attorney for Messrs. Stromberg, Agardy and Whipple. "These were viewed as technical accounting issues, and they wanted to resolve the matter. . . ."

In Palo Alto, Calif., an attorney for Mr. Towle declined comment.

In San Francisco, a spokesman for URS Corp. noted that the company isn't named in the complaint, and that the four individuals who were are no longer with the company. He said class-action suits against the company arising out of charges, including some that concerned the SEC, were settled last year "and are now behind us."

He said "the company, over a year ago, voluntarily restated certain of its financial statements, relating to all of the matters covered by the SEC complaint." He added, "We have a new management team, new auditors and very strong financial controls in place now to ensure that a repetition does not occur."
