World Bank Clears Loans to South Korea For Research Projects

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/25/90

WASHINGTON -- The World Bank cleared $76.6 million in loans to South Korea for technological research projects. The bank said $45 million will go to universities and $31.6 million to several technology institutes, including the Genetic Engineering Center, which supports South Korea's biotechnology industry. Bank officials said the loans will help strengthen South Korea's capacity for technological innovation, while reducing its dependence on imported technology.

Other loans approved by the World Bank and its affiliate, the International Development Association, included these recipients:

-- Rwanda, $40 million from the IDA for roads and transport-sector projects.

-- Tanzania, $38.3 million from the IDA for primary and secondary schools.

-- Caribbean Development Bank, based in Barbados, $32 million from the World Bank and IDA, to help finance business ventures and other projects in a group of Caribbean island countries.

-- Colombia, $24 million from the World Bank to expand community child-care and nutrition projects for preschool children.

-- Central African Republic, $19 million from the IDA to help preserve forests and wildlife areas.

The World Bank loans carry a variable interest rate, currently 7.75% a year; the IDA credits will be interest-free, except for administrative fees.
