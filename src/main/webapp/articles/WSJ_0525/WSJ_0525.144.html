Business Brief -- Standard Chartered PLC: British Bank Makes Changes In Its Management Positions

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/25/90

Standard Chartered PLC, a major British bank based in London, announced changes in senior management positions, which will make Deputy Group Chief Executive Bill Brown a group deputy chairman. The changes, to go into effect June 1, were made as part of the bank's efforts to strengthen its banking and fee-earning businesses. Geoffrey Williams, who retires as deputy chairman of J. Henry Schroder Wagg in July, is to become Standard's group executive director responsible for merchant banking joint ventures with Westdeutsche Landesbank Girozentrate.

John MacKenzie, group executive director, merchant banking, will have responsibility for the strategic enhancement of fee income, expanding less asset-intensive businesses and identifying new opportunities in financial services, the company said.

Chairman and Group Chief Executive of Standard Chartered, Rodney Galpin, said the company wants to increase management's focus on "banking operations and to intensify efforts to generate fee-earning products and develop fee earning businesses."
