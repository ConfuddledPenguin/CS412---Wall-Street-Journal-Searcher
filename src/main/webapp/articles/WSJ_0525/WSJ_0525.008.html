Japan Rules Out Rate Demand

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/25/90

TOKYO -- Japan won't answer U.S. demands that Japan completely liberalize interest rates on all bank deposits within one year, a high Finance Ministry official said, according to Kyodo News Service. U.S. Treasury Undersecretary David Mulford called for the complete liberalization of Japanese rates Tuesday following two days of U.S.-Japan financial market talks that ended with both sides far apart on the question of how quickly interest-rate deregulation in Japan should proceed.

At a joint news conference following the U.S.-Japan talks Tuesday, Japan's Vice Finance Minister for International Affairs Makoto Utsumi denied that Japanese banks are gaining a substantial advantage from the continuation of some interest-rate regulations. Mr. Utsumi said both countries agree that further liberalization is desirable but disagree on how quickly liberalization should proceed.
