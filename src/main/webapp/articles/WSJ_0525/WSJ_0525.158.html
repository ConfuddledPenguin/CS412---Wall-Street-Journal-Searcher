Business Brief -- American Express Co.: Accord Is Signed to Acquire Epsilon Data in Stock Swap

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/25/90

American Express Co. said it signed an agreement to acquire Epsilon Data Management Inc., a software and services company, in a stock swap valued at about $64 million. American Express said Epsilon, of Burlington, Mass., will be operated as part of its Direct Marketing and Travelers Cheque Group, a unit of American Express Travel Related Services Co.

Epsilon shareholders, who must approve the agreement, will receive 0.655 share of newly issued American Express common stock for each Epsilon share they own, subject to adjustment if American Express stock falls below $28 or rises above $31 before the shareholders meet to ratify the deal.

In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange, American Express closed at $30 a share, up 25 cents.

For the fiscal year ended May 31, 1989, Epsilon posted a loss of $1.1 million, or 37 cents a share, on revenue of $51 million. For the quarter ended Feb. 28, the company earned $552,000, or 19 cents a share, on revenue of $13 million.
