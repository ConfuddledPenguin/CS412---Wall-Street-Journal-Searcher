Marketing & Media: Imagine Films' Harris Leaves to Be Producer

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/25/90

LOS ANGELES -- After months of negotiations, Imagine Films Entertainment Inc. said Robert A. Harris, who had been president of motion pictures and television since 1988, left the company to become an independent producer. Mr. Harris said he decided to leave the entertainment company because he wasn't spending as much time as he'd like on Imagine's motion picture activities and instead was concentrating on TV. Mr. Harris formerly was president of MCA Inc.'s TV group, but he wants to make movies.

No less than two of Imagine's annual shareholder meetings were postponed as a result of the debate over Mr. Harris's complex termination agreement with the company. Mr. Harris, who was less than two years into a five-year contract, owns a stake in Imagine, and his compensation was tied to the company's profits under various complicated formulas. He was also a director (he has relinquished his board seat), which the company said accounted for the meeting postponements. In April, the meeting was called off at the last minute, sending Imagine shares soaring on apparent speculation that the company was on the brink of being acquired.

Mr. Harris will produce his first two motion pictures for Imagine, whose co-chief executive officers are Brian Grazer and actor/director Ron Howard. Imagine recently placed a TV series, "Parenthood," on NBC's fall lineup. Imagine produced the film of the same name for MCA's Universal Pictures. MCA owns a 25% stake in Imagine, assuming conversion of warrants and bonds, and has an agreement to distribute most of Imagine's movies. But Carolco Pictures Inc. will distribute the coming movie, "The Doors," to be directed by Oliver Stone.
