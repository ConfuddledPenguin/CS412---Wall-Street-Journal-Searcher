Former Pru-Bache Broker Indicted In Money Scheme

  • Author: William Power and Michael Siconolfi
  • Date: 05/25/90

NEW YORK -- Gary Zahn, a former top-ranking broker at Prudential-Bache Securities Inc., was indicted by a federal grand jury in Brooklyn on 13 counts related to laundering more than $400,000 for clients. Mr. Zahn had been known as a "million-dollar" broker at Prudential-Bache, though it was unclear whether he had generated more than $1 million of commissions over any given year. He was a senior vice president of investments and a member of the chairman's council, reflecting his status as one of the firm's 90 top-grossing representatives.

A spokeswoman for Prudential-Bache, a unit of Prudential Insurance Co. of America, said, "We terminated" Mr. Zahn "as a result of this matter" in March. "We have cooperated with and continue to cooperate with the U.S. attorney's office on this matter."

The indictment charges that from February 1982 until about March 1988, Mr. Zahn and others, who aren't named, helped clients evade the federal law requiring that cash transactions exceeding $10,000 be reported to the Internal Revenue Service. Each count in the indictment, which was issued in U.S. District Court, carries a maximum penalty of five years in prison and a $250,000 fine.

Franklyn H. Snitow, attorney for Mr. Zahn at Snitow & Pauley in Manhattan, declined to comment on whether his client would fight the charges. The attorney did say, however, that Mr. Zahn "faithfully and properly served both Pru-Bache and his clients."

The law, which is designed to help the government in criminal, tax, and regulatory inquiries, requires disclosure of the identity of the person who conducted the transaction or the organization or person for whom the transaction was completed.

Mr. Zahn is charged with having received cash from clients and then processing the funds in bank checks of less than $10,000. Prudential-Bache then didn't file the required reports on the transactions, the indictment alleges.

In October 1987, PaineWebber Group Inc.'s top stockbroker, Gary Eder, was sentenced to six months in prison and given a $510,000 fine in a money-laundering case. Mr. Eder admitted helping various customers of the firm hide at least $700,000 from the IRS by avoiding the filing of required currency-transaction reports.
