Big Board Bars Teletypist Over Missing Attache Case

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/25/90

NEW YORK -- The New York Stock Exchange barred from membership a former Teletype operator for Smith Barney, Harris Upham & Co., who the Big Board said misappropriated an attache case, containing $12,700, that an exchange member left on the trading floor. The exchange also found that the teletypist, Cosimo Louis Pellegrino, a resident of Brooklyn, made material misstatements to the Big Board in connection with the incident.

Mr. Pellegrino couldn't be reached for comment. Smith Barney declined to comment.

In January 1988, the exchange said, a floor broker, whom the exchange declined to identify, left the attache case on the floor of his booth and then left the area. When the broker returned, the case was missing.

The floor broker asked Mr. Pellegrino about it, and the teletypist told him that an individual who worked on the floor took the briefcase. Mr. Pellegrino also said he would try to get it back. A couple of days later, Mr. Pellegrino returned the attache case to the broker, but $900 of the $12,700 was missing.

It is uncommon, but not unheard of, for floor brokers to have cash on the exchange floor; some independent brokers use their booths on the floor as offices and may have some cash around for operations or personal reasons.

Mr. Pellegrino gave the Big Board the alleged perpetrator's name and address and said he worked on the floor. The exchange checked its employment records, interviewed floor employees, sent certified letters to the address Mr. Pellegrino provided, contacted the owner of the building there, and tried to get a phone number for the individual, all to no avail.

An exchange hearing panel found that Mr. Pellegrino took the case and lied about how he got it.
