Resorts' Debtholder Committees Approve Revised Revamp Plan

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/25/90

NEW YORK -- Resorts International Inc.'s debtholder committees approved a revised reorganization plan with terms slightly sweeter than outlined in a plan the company filed in April. The hotel-casino company, which entered bankruptcy-law proceedings last December, plans to file the revisions to the plan with the bankruptcy court next Thursday. Resorts has said it expects to emerge from Chapter 11 proceedings by summer's end. Under Chapter 11, a company is protected from creditors while it seeks to work out a plan for paying its debts.

The revisions essentially kept intact the April plan, which calls for the swap of a total of about $930 million of debt. The latest plan continues to give debtholders a 78% equity stake in the company. Merv Griffin, Resorts' chairman, who paid $365 million for the company in a leveraged buy-out, will keep a 22% equity stake and would name a majority of the board.

The revised plan essentially speeds up interest payments on about $187.5 million of senior secured notes and increases the principal amount on new 10-year debentures by about $5 million. In addition, Mr. Griffin would increase the cash infusion he would make to $28.5 million from $26 million in exchange for cancellation of a $50 million note his Griffin Co. owed to Resorts.

The reorganization plan would leave the company with total debt of $430.3 million. Resorts will be responsible for payment of about $325 million of that amount; the other $100 million will come from rental payments on the lease held in Showboat Hotel & Casino in Atlantic City.

A spokesman for Resorts said the company believes it has the necessary votes to win approval for the plan. Holders of two-thirds of the value of the bonds and 50% of the bondholders are required for approval.

Balfour Investors Inc., a major bondholder that isn't represented by the committees, is said to favor the plan. The Resorts spokesman said the company won't try to sell its Paradise Island property in the Bahamas until the plan is approved by debtholders.
