Toyota, C.Itoh Study Feasibility of Venture With Czech Car Maker

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/25/90

TOKYO -- Toyota Motor Corp. and C.Itoh & Co. are studying the possibility of setting up an auto assembly joint venture in Czechoslovakia with a local car manufacturer, a spokesman for C.Itoh said. The spokesman said a joint inspection team from the two Japanese companies is currently in Czechoslovakia. The team has expressed its intention, in meetings with government and auto industry officials, to go ahead with the project.

The Czech auto company is Bratislava Automobile Works, or BAZ, located in the city of Bratislava.

A Toyota spokesman said the Japanese inspection team left for Czechoslovia on May 12, by request of the Czech government.

The Toyota spokesman described the meetings as a preliminary step to explore "a variety of possibilities," adding that no concrete plans have been made.

Although the C.Itoh spokesman said no details of the inspection team's visit will be forthcoming until after the team returns, he said the companies are considering assembling autos with a high percentage of parts made in Toyota's U.S. facilities.
