Who's News: Barucci Is Selected As Managing Director Of Italian State Bank

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/25/90

MILAN, Italy -- Piero Barucci, the president of the Italian Banking Association (ABI), was nominated yesterday to replace the long-serving Lucio Rondelli as new managing director of state bank Credito Italiano, ending weeks of political maneuvering over the appointment. Mr. Barucci's nomination, one of several affecting the top management of both Credito Italiano and its sister state bank Banca Commerciale Italiana (BCI), was a late choice after protracted discussions between the bank, the state holding company Istituto per la Ricostruzione Industriale (IRI) and political parties in Rome.

Mr. Barucci, 56 years old, has been president of the Tuscan bank Monte dei Paschi di Siena since 1983, a job he will now have to give up, as well as a professor of political economy at the Universities of Florence and Siena, and ABI president since 1987.

The nomination came at a board meeting of IRI in Rome yesterday, and was announced as the Credito Italiano annual shareholders' meeting was already in progress in Genoa.

While IRI nominates top officials in the banks under its control, they still have to be formally approved by the banks themselves. IRI owns 65% of Credito Italiano, which is quoted on the Milan Bourse, and the bank's board is expected to meet in the next few days to discuss the nomination.

Mr. Rondelli, who had been managing director for 21 years, was leaving due to having reached the mandatory retirement age of 65. He reportedly declined an offer of a vice presidency of the bank with some executive functions.
