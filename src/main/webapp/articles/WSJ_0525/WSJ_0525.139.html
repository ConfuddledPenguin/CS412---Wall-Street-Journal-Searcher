Japan's Nonlife Insurers Had Lower Net for Year

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 05/25/90

TOKYO -- Large growth in redemptions and claims as well as financial instability drove down profits at Japan's nonlife insurers in fiscal 1989. Interest-rate deregulation in Japan has led policy holders in recent years to redeem annuities in favor of higher-yielding bank deposits, resulting in lower investment income and diminished assets as well as large payments. In the fiscal year ended March 31, growth in investment income was also depressed by instability in the Japanese financial markets.

To counter redemptions of annuities, the companies have relied on growth in policies such as auto insurance for business. An unusually high number of road accidents in the year left all the companies exposed to large claims from shareholders. Profits on insurance operations at the four largest Japanese nonlife insurers fell sharply in the year.

Those insurers are Tokio Marine & Fire Insurance Co., Yasuda Fire & Marine Insurance Co., Taisho Marine & Fire Insurance Co. and Sumitomo Marine & Fire Insurance Co.
