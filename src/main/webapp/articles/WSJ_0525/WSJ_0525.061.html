Taunton Technologies And VISX Inc. Sign Agreement to Merge

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/25/90

MONROE, Conn. -- Taunton Technologies Inc. and VISX Inc. said they signed a definitive agreement to merge. Under the planned merger, which has been approved by the boards of both companies, one share of Taunton stock will be issued for each 1.3597 shares of VISX outstanding, resulting in VISX shareholders' owning about 32% of the combined company. The planned merger is subject to shareholder approval.

The companies said Charles R. Munnerlyn, chairman and chief executive officer of VISX, which is based in Sunnyvale, Calif., will become chairman and chief executive of the combined companies. Alan R. McMillen, current president of VISX, will become president. John W. Warner, current chief executive of Taunton, will consult on the evaluation of products, patents and technology for the combined companies.

Officials of both companies said that the merger would result in the termination of patent litigation between the companies. Taunton and VISX had been engaged in interference proceedings over rights to patents for the use of lasers to correct nearsightedness and other eye disorders.

Taunton said it expects VISX shareholders to receive about 2.5 million shares of the combined company valued at about $27 million. Shares of Taunton closed yesterday on the national over-the-counter market at $11 bid.
