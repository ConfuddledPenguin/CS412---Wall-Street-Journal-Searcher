Technology: Dell Computer Net More Than Doubled In Fiscal 1st Period

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/25/90

AUSTIN, Texas -- Boosted by unusually strong overseas sales growth, Dell Computer Corp. said it had record revenue and net income in its May 4 first quarter. Net income more than doubled to $5.3 million, or 28 cents a share, from the year-earlier level of $2.1 million, or 11 cents a share. Revenue increased 38%, to $120.3 million from $87.5 million a year earlier, reflecting the highest quarterly sales in the computer maker's history.

Dell's earnings "exceeded our expectations," said Michael Dell, chairman and chief executive. They also marked a sharp turnaround from the past several quarters, when the company was working off a surplus of high-cost inventory that severely pinched its margins and reduced earnings to as low as one cent a share.

While domestic revenue was up 15% from a year ago, most of Dell's gains were made overseas, where sales more than doubled from last year's first quarter. International sales now represent 36% of Dell's world-wide revenue, said Mr. Dell, compared with 23% a year ago. Dell has international subsidiaries in the United Kingdom, Canada, West Germany, France and Sweden.

But Mr. Dell cautioned that the current quarter will not show the same growth rate, partly because much of the first-quarter international sales gain was caused by year-end government orders.

And while Dell's domestic business is "holding its own," Mr. Dell added, margin pressures are increasing as competitors try to increase market share through more-aggressive pricing.
