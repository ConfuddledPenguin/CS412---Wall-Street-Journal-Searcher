Letters to the Editor: Surprise -- Metal CDs Are Actually Plastic

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/25/90

Your April 18 editorial "Are CDs Recyclable?" commits a major gaffe in calling music CDs "metal" and "weeds in (the) garden" (]) of our green, biodegradable earth. The shiny CDs are sleek new laser plastics not likely to be recycled because the music -- whether Beethoven or Bon Jovi -- is not likely to be discarded. You should also know that these small, optical prerecorded discs are also being widely introduced as CD ROMs, with 1,000 times the capacity of standard prerecorded data-storage discs for personal computers. A larger laser disc (videodisc) format with interactive video is capable of 5,000 times today's capacity.

CD ROMs can be far cheaper than today's computer discs or their drives of equal capacity. Although introductory prices for CD ROM players have been higher, within a few months they will likely decline to substantially less (about $500). These new devices are easily connected to desktop computers already in use. Are we ready to use 1,000 to 5,000 times the info available last week? Our educational missions will be challenged sharply by increased demands for machines, materials and the production of specially formatted interactive video.

There is one desktop computer for every three adults aged 18 to 64 in the U.S., a ratio that likely will double over the next five years. You can bet that in 10 years, or soon thereafter, there will be nearly as many personal computers as adults. New educational initiatives are necessary to help our students best cope with the wrenching changes resulting in the school, home and office. Alas, the CD "weeds" will even be in every editorial office.

Glenn Ralston

Indianapolis
