Australia Current-Account Gap

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/25/90

SYDNEY -- Australia's current-account deficit is expected to have narrowed in April from March, but economists say that is due to fewer business days in April rather than to a contraction in average daily imports. The current-account deficit for April is scheduled to be announced Monday.

An AP-Dow Jones survey of 20 economists shows they expect, on average, a current-account deficit of 1.2 billion Australian dollars (US$919.6 million) before seasonal adjustment, narrowing from deficits of A$1.8 billion in March and A$1.3 billion in April 1989. Forecasts range from A$800 million to A$1.4 billion.
