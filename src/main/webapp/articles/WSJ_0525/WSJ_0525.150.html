Senate Panel Clears Plan To Open Japanese Banking

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/25/90

WASHINGTON -- The Senate Banking Committee passed a measure aimed at giving U.S. regulators leverage to open Japan's financial markets to greater participation by U.S. banks and securities firms. The bill, passed by a voice vote, would give U.S. financial regulators the power to impose sanctions against foreign financial companies whose home countries don't open their markets to U.S. companies. Regulators would be able to deny applications from foreign financial concerns to start or expand their U.S. operations.

The committee passed the bill after accepting an amendment by Sen. Christopher Dodd (D., Conn.) that would effectively prevent the measure from affecting financial firms from the European Community. The amendment was a signal the committee is mostly concerned about the growing power of Japanese banks in the U.S., while U.S. banks are frustrated with the restrictions they face in Japan.

The bill, co-sponsored by committee Chairman Donald Riegle (D., Mich.) and its ranking Republican, Utah Sen. Jake Garn, is opposed by the Bush administration, which argues it can gain more access for U.S. companies through the current trade negotiations.
