Compaq Computer Directors Approve 2-for-1 Stock Split

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/25/90

HOUSTON -- Compaq Computer Corp., whose stock price has sailed well above $100 a share, said its directors approved a 2-for-1 split of its common stock. The fast-growing computer concern said it believes the move will make its stock more affordable for smaller shareholders. Institutions hold an estimated 70% to 75% of Compaq's shares.

In addition, companies often split their shares in the belief that a lower price will help the stock price keep rising. Compaq closed at $122, down 87.5 cents, in New York Stock Exchange composite trading.

Compaq said the stock split will be paid July 6 to holders of record June 29.

Daniel C. Benton, an analyst with Goldman, Sachs & Co., said the move shows that Compaq, the nation's third-largest maker of personal computers, is confident about its prospects. But, he said, "I don't know that there is a whole lot of empirical evidence that when you split a stock, the stock does better.

"I think it's mainly psychological for the companies," he said.

Since February, Compaq shares have soared to more than $120 from around $80. Other technology stocks also have climbed, fed by European demand for computers and stronger-than-expected U.S. sales. Analysts attributed Compaq's surge to renewed shareholder confidence after Compaq's first-quarter earnings, reported last month, surpassed most expectations. The company said net income rose 11% to $92.2 million, or $2.13 a share. Sales increased 28% to $872.2 million.

Compaq said in February that its share of the market for high-end personal computers was dropping and warned of stiffer competition in the personal-computer business. But it said an unexpected 56% rise in overseas sales helped fuel its first-quarter earnings increases.
