Business Brief -- Apple Computer Inc.: Accord Set to Settle 2 Suits By Holders for $5.7 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/25/90

Apple Computer Inc., Cupertino, Calif., said it agreed to pay $5.7 million to settle two shareholder lawsuits arising out of a plunge in its stock last year. The class-action suits were filed on behalf of purchasers of Apple stock between Jan. 17 and Jan. 27, 1989. During that time, Apple sharply reduced its earnings outlook for the fiscal-second quarter, ended March 31; the revision sent the stock reeling. The suits alleged violations of state and federal securities disclosure laws.

Apple said a portion of the settlement, which is still subject to approval of the federal court for the Northern District of California where the suits were filed, will be paid by an insurance company. The company said it continues to deny any wrongdoing, and agreed to the settlement to avoid the expense of further litigation.
