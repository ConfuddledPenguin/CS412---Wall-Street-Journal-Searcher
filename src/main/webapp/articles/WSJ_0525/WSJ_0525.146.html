Business Brief -- Micron Technology Inc.: Amstrad Plans to Sell Stake As Chip Demand Is Weak

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/25/90

Micron Technology Inc., Boise, Idaho, beset by lackluster demand for computer chips in recent quarters, apparently has lost the interest of a major shareholder. The chip maker said it plans to register next month 3.6 million, or nearly 10%, of its common shares, for sale by Amstrad PLC. Amstrad, a major British electronics concern, acquired the stake in October 1988 for $75 million, or $21 a share.

The original transaction had been considered part of a strategy by Micron to ensure long-term demand for its chips as well as an attempt by Amstrad to secure stable supplies of Micron's then-scarce dynamic random access memory chips.

Lately, Micron has been beset by a chip glut. For the fiscal-second quarter ended March 1, it reported net income of $14,000, or less than a penny a share, on revenue of $77.5 million, compared with profit of $29.2 million, or 77 cents a share, on revenue of $113.8 million a year earlier.

In national over-the-counter trading, Micron closed at $14, down 62.5 cents.
