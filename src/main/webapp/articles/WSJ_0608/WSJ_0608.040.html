Urcarco Seeks Credit Increase

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/08/90

FORT WORTH, Texas -- Urcarco Inc., an operator of Texas used-car lots, said that it is negotiating with its lead bank, NCNB Texas National Bank, to increase its credit line to about $100 million from $45 million, and that it is discussing the possibility of selling notes based on its auto-note portfolio and of beginning to sell commercial paper. The company has a healthy appetite for cash, because it purchases used cars for cash and then provides financing for the cars when they are resold.

Urcarco said that it raised $108.4 million last week from an offering of 5.8 million common shares, and that it has about $125 million in cash and available bank credit lines. A spokesman said the company hopes to have "a full array of other types of debt underlying the equity," which now is more than $200 million.
