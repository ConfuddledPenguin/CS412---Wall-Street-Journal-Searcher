Business Brief -- Medtronic Inc.: Company Sweetens Proposal For Medical Pump Maker

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/08/90

Medtronic Inc. sweetened its stock swap proposal for Bio-Medicus Inc., a maker of blood pumps used in surgery. Under the new plan, the Minneapolis-based maker of biomedical devices is offering to exchange one Medtronic share for 3.15 Bio-Medicus shares. Previously, it had been proposing to swap one Medtronic share for 3.3 Bio-Medicus shares.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Medtronic shares closed at $79.625, up 87.5 cents. Under Medtronic's revised offer, that price would value each Bio-Medicus share at $25.278.

In national over-the-counter trading yesterday, Bio-Medicus shares jumped $1.375 to close at $23.125.

Medtronic has characterized its bid as friendly, but Bio-Medicus's board met shortly after receiving the original bid in mid-May and took no action on the proposal. A spokesman said that Bio-Medicus's directors will meet to consider the new proposal. He declined further comment.

Under a 1987 pact, Medtronic can't boost its current 5.5% stake in the company above 10% without Bio-Medicus board approval.
