Traffic Rises for Two Airlines

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/08/90

FORT WORTH, Texas -- Two Texas-based air carriers reported increases in May traffic. American Airlines, a unit of AMR Corp., said traffic rose 3.5%, to 6.40 billion revenue passenger miles from 6.18 billion in the year-earlier period.

A revenue passenger mile is one paying passenger flown one mile.

American's load factor, or percentage of seats filled, fell to 61.9% from 62.8%, the Fort Worth company said.

Southwest Airlines, Dallas, said it flew 854.3 million revenue passenger miles in May, up 7.3% from 796.5 million in the year-earlier period. Load factor for the month was 61.1%, compared with 61.6%.
