Barry Blau & Partners To Restate Its Results For Certain Past Years

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/08/90

FAIRFIELD, Conn. -- Barry Blau & Partners Inc., a direct response advertising agency, said it will restate its financial results for certain prior years as a result of "an understanding" with the Securities and Exchange Commission. The adjustments, of an unspecified amount, involve deferred compensation aspects of a 1984 employment agreement with Barry Blau, the company's chief executive officer. In addition, the company will accelerate in prior years the write-off of certain deferred costs.

The company said the restatement won't "materially affect" stockholders' equity as of March 31. The company said the vested portion of the deferred compensation agreement has been waived by Mr. Blau and has been contributed back into the company as capital; the other deferred costs had been treated as expense as of March 31.

Fred Kaseff, chief financial officer, said the restatement would include the financial results for the nine months ended March 31, when the company reported net income of $69,839 or 4 cents a share, on revenue of $45.4 million.

The company also said that, apart from the accounting issues, it anticipates that over the near term, earnings will suffer "due to budget reductions and program cutbacks at certain clients." Mr. Kaseff declined to elaborate.

The company has been reviewing a number of accounting issues raised by its former accountants Coopers & Lybrand in the 10-Q report for the three months ended Dec. 31. At that time, the accounting firm said it believed certain prior-year results should be restated, and that its report on the fiscal year ended last June 30 shouldn't be relied upon.

Subsequently, the company's board hired accounting firm BDO Seidman to analyze the accounting issues. The company said it decided to restate its figures on the basis of consultations between the company, BDO Seidman and the SEC staff.
