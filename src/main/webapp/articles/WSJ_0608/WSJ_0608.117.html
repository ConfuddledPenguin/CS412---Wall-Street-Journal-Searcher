Business Brief -- ALC Communications Corp.: Long-Distance Service Firm Vastly Restructures Debt

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/08/90

ALC Communications Corp., a long-distance telephone service company based in Birmingham, Mich., said it significantly restructured its debt. The company obtained $7.5 million in additional financing from a group of three institutional investors; in exchange, the investors will receive two-year notes paying 15% interest, and warrants granting them the right to purchase 17% of ALC's common stock. ALC said a "significant majority" of holders of its senior subordinated debentures accepted a deferment of interest payments. ALC's revolving credit line, provided by Foothill Capital Corp., was renewed for a two-year term, under substantially unchanged terms. ALC's majority shareholder, Communications Transmission Inc., converted its $20 million bridge loan, due September 1990, to a term loan, due June 4, 1992, at a reduced interest rate.

"As a result of these and other concessions we have obtained, the company's cash operating costs have been reduced by over $1.2 million per month," Marvin Moses, ALC's chief financial officer, said.
