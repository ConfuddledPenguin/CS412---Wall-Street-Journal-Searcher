U.S. Auto Makers Set 2.3% Rise in Output For Current Week

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/08/90

DETROIT -- The 10 major U.S. auto makers planned to build 143,755 cars this week, a 2.3% increase from 140,505 a year ago. But for the month of May, U.S. car and truck output fell 16.9% to 874,443 vehicles from 1,051,850 a year ago. U.S. car output dropped 7.6% in May, while truck and bus production was down 33.7% compared with the year-earlier month.

Separately, auto makers said all car assembly plants in the U.S. will operate next week, with several working overtime.

General Motors Corp. said it has scheduled Saturday overtime at its Lordstown, Ohio, assembly plant.

Ford Motor Co. has scheduled Saturday overtime at its Kansas City, Mo., and Louisville, Ky., truck plants, and daily overtime at its car plants in Wixom and Wayne, Mich.

Chrysler Corp. said it has planned overtime at its St. Louis II, Sterling Heights and Warren, Mich., factories.
