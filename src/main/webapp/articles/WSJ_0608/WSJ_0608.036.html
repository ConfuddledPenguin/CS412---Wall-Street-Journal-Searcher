International: Argentina Pays Creditors Interest on Foreign Debt

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 06/08/90

BUENOS AIRES -- Argentina made a $40 million interest payment to foreign creditors, the first since foreign debt payments were suspended more than two years ago, economy ministry officials said. Carlos Carballo, undersecretary of economy, confirmed that payments were renewed following "arduous" discussions in New York between Central Bank Vice President Javier Gonzalez Fraga and individual creditor banks.

The interest payment, ministry individuals said, is considered "a gesture of good will" aimed at aiding Mr. Gonzalez Fraga in talks that began this week with the steering committee of a consortium of private creditors.

Argentina, the developing world's third-largest debtor, owes a total of $60 billion plus interest arrearages that could reach $8.5 billion by year end.

The government, ministry individuals said, hopes to negotiate an agreement to pay $300 million in overdue interest by year end. Creditors reportedly have sought payment of $500 million. If negotiators reach an agreement, Argentina would make another interest payment of between $70 million and $80 million.
