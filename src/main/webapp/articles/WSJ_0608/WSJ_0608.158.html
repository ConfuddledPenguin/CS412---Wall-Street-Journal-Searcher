Budget Talks Find Will To Cut Spending Elusive

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/08/90

WASHINGTON -- White House and congressional budget negotiators appear to have discovered that, for all their vows to reduce the deficit, there's little appetite for cutting spending outside the Pentagon. A couple of Republicans emerged discouraged from yesterday's review of government spending, but most participants said they are resigned to another week of what Sen. Pete Domenici (R., N.M.) called "a learning experience, a get-people-involved experience."

Sen. Domenici said he doubts the parties will begin any significant bargaining for another 10 days. White House Budget Director Richard Darman called that assessment "realistic," although he has been pressing for a faster pace.

When Mr. Darman expressed impatience at yesterday's session, participants said, Sen. Wyche Fowler (D., Ga.) responded by reading excerpts from a 1967 speech by then-Rep. George Bush asserting that President Lyndon Johnson wasn't exercising presidential leadership.

House Majority Leader Richard Gephardt told negotiators he still hopes for an agreement in principle this month and vowed longer and more frequent meetings toward that end, participants said.

The budget summit agreed to form a congressional-administration staff group to prepare a list of ways to cut spending on federal entitlement programs, Mr. Darman said.
