General Cinema Says Second-Quarter Net Fell to $29.8 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/08/90

CHESTNUT HILL, Mass. -- General Cinema Corp. reported a 97% drop in fiscal second-quarter net income, chiefly because of a gain in the year-earlier period from discontinued operations. The operator of cinemas and specialty retailer said net for the quarter ended April 30 fell to $29.8 million, or 40 cents a share, from $893.9 million, or $12.10 a share, in the year-ago period.

The latest period included a pretax gain of $587,000 on the sale of securities. A year ago, the company had a pretax gain of $27.1 million on the sale of securities, and had profit of $859 million from discontinued operations, which included $855.1 million from the disposal of operations. Profit from continuing operations was $34.9 million, or 47 cents a share, a year ago.

Revenue rose 20% to $642.8 million from $536.9 million.

In the first six months, net fell 95% to $49.9 million, or 68 cents a share, from $938.3 million, or $12.71 a share, in the year-ago period.

The latest period included a pretax loss of $586,000 from the sale of securities. A year ago, the company had a pretax gain of $82.9 million from the sale of securities, and profit from continuing operations of $72.4 million, or 98 cents a share.

Revenue was $1.16 billion, up 19% from $976.6 million.

The company did not predict results for the third quarter, but said it is unlikely its theater division's earnings will exceed the $12.2 million earned in the past year's summer quarter.
