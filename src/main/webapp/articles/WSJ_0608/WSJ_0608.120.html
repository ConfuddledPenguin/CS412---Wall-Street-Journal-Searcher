French Oil Group Set To Sell Headquarters, Boost Capital Base

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 06/08/90

PARIS -- Total-Cie. Francaise des Petroles intends to put its Paris headquarters up for sale in the near future, but the oil concern gave no indication of the size of the capital gain the sale would bring. Company Chairman Serge Tchuruk told shareholders that the oil group also intends to boost its capital base in the near future, but he declined to reveal the magnitude of the planned capital increase.

Mr. Tchuruk said the group's financial performance since the beginning of this year has been satisfactory in the exploration and production sectors, while refining and distribution businesses have been steady but still below average industry levels of profitability in Europe.

Mr. Tchuruk said it is "imperative" for Total to improve its profitability. He pointed out that last year Total's profitability, defined by earnings as a percentage of revenue, was 6.4%, well below the industry average of 9%. He said it was Total's firm intention to improve the group's profitability to more than 10%, if possible, within the next 2 1/2 years.

Answering shareholders' criticism that the group's dividend has remained unchanged since 1985, Mr. Tchuruk said Total shareholders fare much better than those of most other big French groups in terms of dividend as a percentage of earnings per share.

In Total's case, this came out at 38% for 1989 compared with 30% for a sample group of 14 big French industrial groups, he said. He also noted that Total's shares were yielding 4.2% on the basis of a share price of around 700 francs ($122.81), compared with 2.6% for the sample group.

Mr. Tchuruk, who is also Total's chief executive officer, said the group's 400 million tons of crude-oil reserves in the Middle East will become an increasingly strategic asset, as the region will become the key area for the international oil market.

He reaffirmed his belief that there is no future for the petrochemical industry in countries that are oil importers. But he said Total is studying possible partnerships in the petrochemical field with concerns from oil producing countries.
