Bipartisan Panel Suggests Shortening Conventions

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/08/90

WASHINGTON -- A bipartisan commission is recommending that the political parties shave a day off their four-day quadrennial conventions in an effort to sharpen their appeal to television voters. The commission, which includes five current or past national party chairmen, also recommends that each of the major television networks allocate three hours of air time for use by the parties and presidential candidates in the months leading to the presidential election.

The commission suggests that during the parties' air time, only the presidential and vice-presidential candidates be permitted to address the public. The use of pre-produced materials such as taped candidate biographies would be barred.

Democratic Party Chairman Ronald Brown already has indicated his willingness to tinker with the national political convention. He is toying with proposals either to compress the proceedings or to conduct some of them over a weekend, rather than during the week.

Commission members said taking a day off the conventions would make the proceedings seem less tedious. "We've got to make them more enlightening and better to watch," said former GOP Chairman Frank Fahrenkopf.
