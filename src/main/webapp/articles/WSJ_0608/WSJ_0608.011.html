Initial State Jobless Claims Increased in Latest Week

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/08/90

WASHINGTON -- Initial claims for regular state unemployment benefits rose to a seasonally adjusted 356,000 in the week ended May 26 from 342,000 the previous week, the Labor Department said. The number of people receiving regular state benefits in the week ended May 19 was a seasonally adjusted 2,397,000, or 2.3% of those covered by unemployment insurance, from 2,429,000 the previous week, when the insured unemployment rate was also 2.3%.

Counting all state and federal benefit programs, the number of people receiving unemployment benefits in the week ended May 19 fell to 2,247,900 from 2,330,400 a week earlier. These figures aren't seasonally adjusted.
