Technology Brief -- Calgene Inc.: Quarterly Charge Planned From Layoffs, Consolidation

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/08/90

Calgene Inc., an agricultural biotechnology concern based in Davis, Calif., said it will incur a "significant" one-time charge in its third quarter ending June 30, citing layoffs and severance payments, and a greenhouse closure in consolidating sites. A spokesman said an estimate of the charge wasn't available. In the year-earlier quarter, Calgene had a net loss of $1.7 million, on revenue of $8.1 million.

Calgene also said that this year, it will change its Sept. 30 fiscal year end to June 30, a time of seasonally low activity in agriculture, when accounting relating to inventory and other factors is easier and less expensive than in September.

Calgene said it has stopped development programs in genetic engineering of certain plant hybrids and of alfalfa, and laid off 35 employees, or about 11% of the total work force, from those programs last week. These moves and the closure of one of Calgene's three greenhouse operations are aimed at focusing the company on its core projects of oils modification, development of cotton that isn't damaged by herbicide, and creation of improved-quality tomatos. Calgene said the actions will reduce expenses by at least $3 million annually.
