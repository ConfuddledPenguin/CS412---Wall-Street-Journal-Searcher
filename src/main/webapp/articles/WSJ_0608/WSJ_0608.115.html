Business Brief -- Norton Co.: Panel to Begin Investigation Into Firm's Merger Accord

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/08/90

Compagnie de Saint-Gobain, a French glassmaker, said the Committee on Foreign Investment in the U.S. will begin an investigation within 45 days into the merger agreement between its unit, SG Acquisition Corp., and Norton Co., of Worcester, Mass. Saint-Gobain said that because of the investigation, it has extended the expiration date of its $90-a-share tender offer for all outstanding shares of Norton common to June 27. The offer was scheduled to expire today. As of Wednesday, 8.7 million Norton shares, or about 41% of the shares outstanding, had been tendered to Saint-Gobain.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Norton's stock fell $1.50, to $87.875.

The government committee, which regulates foreign investment in the U.S., will take its findings to President Bush within 45 days, who will then announce any decision to take action within 15 days.

The committee completed an initial 70-day review period on Wednesday. A spokesman for the committee had no comment on the investigation. A spokesman for Saint-Gobain said the company doesn't "see any problem with the acquisition."
