Industrial Nations Grow At Steady, but Slower Pace

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/08/90

NEW YORK -- The world's industrial economies are growing at a steady but slowing pace, latest figures indicate. The Conference Board, a business information service, said its International Economic Scoreboard shows that the composite leading index for 11 major industrial countries is growing at a 1% annual rate, down slightly from the 2% growth rate three months ago and 3% rate six months ago. The figure suggests slower growth ahead, the report said.

The report looks at leading indicators, or measures thought to signal economic turns because they generally rise or fall in advance of the general economy. The report for the most part covers statistics as current as February and March.

The Pacific region continues to show the greatest strength, especially in Taiwan, where the leading index is growing at an 8% annual rate; Korea's rate is 5%. Growth has slowed in New Zealand, 1%, and Japan, 0%.

"The weakest economy among these 11 countries is still Australia, where the long drop in the leading index -- declining at a 6% rate -- looks more and more like a recession," the report said.

In Europe, growth rates are "essentially flat," except for West Germany's 5% annual growth rate. Others include Italy, 2%, France, -1% and the United Kingdom, -3%.

In Canada, the leading index is growing at a 1% rate while in the U.S. the leading index is flat.

The indexes are compiled monthly by the Center for International Business Cycle Research at the Columbia University Business School.
