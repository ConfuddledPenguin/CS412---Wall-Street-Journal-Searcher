Business Brief -- Easco Hand Tools Inc.: Shareholders Clear Proposal To Be Bought by Danaher

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/08/90

Easco Hand Tools Inc. holders approved a proposal for Danaher Corp. to acquire Easco in a transaction valued at about $170 million. The companies said the merger took effect yesterday. Terms of the agreement, which was initiated in February, called for Danaher to issue new stock to be exchanged at a ratio of 0.4175 Danaher share for each Easco share outstanding. George Sherman, who recently left a senior executive position at Black & Decker Corp. to become Danaher's president, said the merged company will have annual sales of about $1 billion.

Danaher said the transaction includes the assumption of about $50 million of Easco debt.

Easco, a Hunt Valley, Md., producer of mechanic's hand tools and specialized automotive-service tools, had sales of $205.6 million in 1989. Easco had 13.9 million weighted average shares outstanding in 1989.

Danaher, a Washington maker of tools and transportation products, has 1989 revenue of about $756 million. Danaher said it had about 23.8 million fully diluted common shares outstanding in 1989.
