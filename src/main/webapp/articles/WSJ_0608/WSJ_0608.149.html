Technology: Hitachi Says It Developed A 64-Megabit DRAM Chip

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 06/08/90

TOKYO -- Hitachi Ltd. said it has developed a prototype for a "next-generation" 64-megabit dynamic random access memory chip with extremely low power requirements. The prototype, which Hitachi says is the world's first, has 16 times the memory capacity than the four megabit DRAMs that are the largest capacity chips in mass production today. The next largest chips, with capacity of 16 megabits, are still in the experimental stage.

Hitachi officials said the prototype consumes approximately one-tenth the electricity required by the 16-megabit DRAMs the company unveiled recently, suggesting a new step toward miniaturization of products such as laptop computers.

The prototype is capable of operating on a 1.5-volt battery cell, because of the use of dense packing technology.

An industry analyst said the timing of the announcement "isn't a mystery," considering the speed with which Japanese semiconductor makers are developing advanced chips.

The Japanese industry also is working to develop advanced mass-production techniques, he noted, adding he expects Hitachi to begin producing the chips by 1996. Hitachi said it didn't know when mass production would begin.
