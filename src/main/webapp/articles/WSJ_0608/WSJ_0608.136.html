Greyhound Measures For Summer Travel Approved by Judge

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/08/90

BROWNSVILLE, TEXAS -- A federal bankruptcy judge approved a series of measures that allow Greyhound Lines Inc. to keep its buses on the road for the start of the summer travel season. Judge Richard S. Schmidt said he would permit Greyhound to pay salaries and operating expenses until June 28, when he scheduled another hearing to discuss the matter. He also permitted the company to release $2 million in pre-filing COD payments from package-express customers and to honor charter bus deposits made before Monday, when Greyhound and five other units of GLI Holding Co. filed for protection under Chapter 11 of the federal bankruptcy code.

"This enables us to get on with business as usual," Greyhound spokesman George Gravley said of the decisions.

But the proceedings also set the stage for continued wrangling between Greyhound and the Amalgamated Transit Union, which has waged a three-month-long strike against the company. Among the $29.5 million in pay, benefits and workers' compensation claims to be honored were $2.5 million in paychecks issued, but not cleared before the Chapter 11 filing. In a move affecting top Greyhound executives, ATU attorneys successfully negotiated a $2,000-per-employee cap on honoring those checks.

Before the hearing, Greyhound withdrew a tender offer it made two weeks ago to redeem about $191 million in face amount of debt for about $86 million in cash. J. Michael Doyle, senior vice president and chief financial officer, said the action was taken "so that we can focus on mapping out a sound debt restructuring plan for the court."
