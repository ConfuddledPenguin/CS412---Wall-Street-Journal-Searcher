Marketing & Media: News Corp. to Sell 49% Of Hong Kong Newspapers

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 06/08/90

Rupert Murdoch's News Corp. said it will make a public offering of 49% of its Hong Kong newspaper business, which includes the South China Morning Post, Hong Kong's largest English-language daily. Sydney, Australia-based News Corp., which said last month it would sell off an undisclosed portion of South China Morning Post Holdings Ltd., didn't specify how much it intends to raise through the offer. The company said it plans to provide further details Monday at a news conference.

Outside estimates of the newspaper's value range from four billion to five billion Hong Kong dollars (US$514.1 million to US$642.7 million). The sale comes as News Corp. attempts to sell some of its assets to cut the almost $7 billion in debt created by an aggressive acquisition spree in recent years.

News Corp. said it has applied to the Hong Kong Stock Exchange to list the South China Morning Post's entire issued capital of 1.5 billion ordinary shares. But the company said it will keep 51% of the shares, retaining the majority holding.

The public will be offered 17.5% of the Post's shares, while an additional 17.5% will be placed with institutional and professional investors. United Overseas Bank Ltd. has agreed to acquire a further 9% of the shares, while Singapore Press Holdings Ltd. will acquire 5%.
