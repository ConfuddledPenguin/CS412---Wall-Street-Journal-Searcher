Uruguayan Consumer Prices

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/08/90

MONTEVIDEO, Uruguay -- Prices of consumer goods rose 5.6% in May, boosting Uruguay's 12-month inflation rate to three digits for the first time in nearly 16 years, the government said. The May increase brought inflation in the past 12 months to 103.5%. Prices rose 38% in the first five months of 1990. The inflation rate for all 1989 was 89%.

The government of President Luis Lacalle, who was inaugurated March 1, has said it will take steps to reduce the budget deficit, considered to be the principal cause of the rising inflation rate.
