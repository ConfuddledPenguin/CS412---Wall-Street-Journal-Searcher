Business Brief -- Nevada Power Co.: About 900 Workers Strike, Affecting Entire Operation

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/08/90

Nevada Power Co., Las Vegas, Nev., said about 900 clerical and plant workers went out on strike, affecting the company's entire operation. The company said it believed it could provide service for its 328,000 existing customers by using supervisory and salaried personnel. But new construction for residential and business connections will be delayed or suspended, the company said.

"That's where the crunch is going to come," said David Barneby, vice president for power supply. "We're in the midst of a huge building boom here."

The union local's business manager, Jim Brimer, disagreed. "There will be tremendous delays in restoring service if there are any power outtages," he said. Las Vegas has experienced 100-degree heat all week. On Wednesday, members of the International Brotherhood of Electrical Workers overwhelmingly rejected a tentative four-year contract. The company rejected a new union proposal yesterday, prompting the walkout. The major issues are wages, medical benefits and overtime.
