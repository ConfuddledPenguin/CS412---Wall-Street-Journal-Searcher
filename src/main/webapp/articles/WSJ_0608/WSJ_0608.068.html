Firm Says Teledyne Ruined Its Business; Seeks Treble Damages

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/08/90

LOS ANGELES -- A manufacturer of parts for microwave systems has sued Teledyne Inc. for allegedly ruining its business by buying exclusive rights to certain technology and then not using it. H-U Development Corp., in a complaint filed in Los Angeles Superior Court, claimed that under a 1989 agreement Los Angeles-based Teledyne was to pay royalties for use of H-U's "magnetic switching" technology in new microwave systems that Teledyne produced. H-U is based in Santa Monica, Calif.

A Teledyne spokesman said he hadn't seen the complaint and declined comment.

Teledyne, a manufacturing and insurance concern, also acquired most of H-U's equipment and moved it to San Jose, Calif., where its microwave division is located, the complaint said.

After that, the complaint said, Teledyne didn't allocate production facilities to the new products, quoted "ridiculously high" prices and long delivery periods to potential customers, and continued producing its old systems without the H-U technology.

The complaint seeks at least $10 million in compensatory damages and treble punitive damages.
