Business Brief -- IRT Corp.: X-Ray Equipment Company Completes New Financing

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/08/90

IRT Corp. said it has completed new lender bank agreements and a private-equity financing of about $4 million, led by Euro-American-I L.P. of Menlo Park, Calif. IRT, which makes automated X-ray equipment used to monitor manufacturing processes, said the equity investment consists of about $4 million of convertible preferred stock purchased by investors and members of the IRT board. Four of the five IRT board members are buying some $650,000 of the preferred, each share of which is priced at $16 and is convertible into 10 common shares. The stock initially carries a cumulative annual dividend of 7%.

IRT of San Diego said proceeds from the investment will be used in part to pay existing credit with Sanwa Bank. IRT also said Sanwa has renewed its line of credit for $3 million through April 1, 1991, under new agreements.

In addition, IRT said that Citibank, a unit of Citicorp, restructured a $5 million senior subordinated note to an amended senior subordinated seven-year note with amortization beginning in fiscal year 1993. The note initially has reduced interest rates. Citibank also forgave a note for $885,000, originally due April 1, 1990, and forgave accrued but unpaid interest of about $200,000.
