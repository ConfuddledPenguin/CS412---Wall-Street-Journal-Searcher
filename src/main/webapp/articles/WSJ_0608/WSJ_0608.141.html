Former Dallas Lawyer Agrees to Plead Guilty In Vernon S&L Case

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/08/90

DALLAS -- Laurence Vineyard Jr., a former Dallas lawyer and onetime owner of a Colorado thrift, agreed to plead guilty to a charge that he misapplied the proceeds of loan from the defunct Vernon Savings & Loan Association of Dallas, the Justice Deparment said. The charge, filed yesterday in federal court in Dallas, involves a $12.7 million loan that Mr. Vineyard obtained from Vernon Savings on the same day he was indicted for fraud in connection with his ownership of Key Savings & Loan Association of Englewood, Colo.

Mr. Vineyard had financed his purchase of Key Savings with a loan from Vernon and was to pay down a portion of that debt with the new loan. Instead, according to the new criminal charge, Mr. Vineyard drew $2.6 million on the loan and used the money to purchase a $1.18 million mansion and to establish trust funds for his children.

Mr. Vineyard, 41 years old, currently is serving a five-year prison term for his conviction on fraud charges related to Key Savings. The new fraud charge carries a maximum penalty of five years in federal prison and a fine of up to twice the loss he caused to Vernon Savings.
