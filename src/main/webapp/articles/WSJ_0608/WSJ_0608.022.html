Letters to the Editor: Please Do Justice To Gathers's Memory

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/08/90

I was most distressed by Charles M. Hart's May 2 letter to the editor in which he implies that "the young basketball player who died on the court recently . . ." (meaning Hank Gathers of Loyola Marymount University) was somehow illicitly provided $6,600 with which to pay the premium on a disability-insurance policy. The facts are that the decedent arranged financing with a local bank in a transaction that, I am informed, is not unique among college basketball players of Mr. Gathers's quality. If he does not suffer a career-threatening injury the loan is to be paid off out of the anticipated signing bonus; but, if he is not signed because of injury, the loan is paid out of the proceeds of the policy.

The late and much lamented Hank Gathers, fondly remembered as "Hank the Bank," was an extraordinary athlete. He was also an extraordinary human being. He was a senior at LMU and, like every other senior teammate of his, would have graduated this academic year. The degree he worked so hard to achieve (and would receive if alive) will be granted to him posthumously.

Hank Gathers was a class human being and he had the good fortune to have attended a class institution. Neither of them deserves Mr. Hart's innuendo.

Robert S. Rose

Los Angeles
