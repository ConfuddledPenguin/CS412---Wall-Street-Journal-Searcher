Italy's May Car Sales Slip

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/08/90

MILAN -- Italian car sales were down 1.1% in May from a year earlier but were still 3.3% higher in the first five months of 1990 than in the same period in 1989, according to Anfia, the national association of car manufacturers. Anfia said 220,990 cars were sold in May, compared with 223,474 in May 1989. A total of 1,154,290 cars were sold in the first five months, compared with 1,117,043 a year earlier. Italian car manufacturers, led by Fiat S.p.A., which makes Fiat, Lancia and Alfa Romeo autos, accounted for 53% of the market in May and 54 3/4% in the first five months.

Among foreign makers, Ford Motor Co. led the market in May with 17,933 units sold and a market share of 8.1%. Volkswagen AG was second with a 7.17% market share and 15,843 cars sold.
