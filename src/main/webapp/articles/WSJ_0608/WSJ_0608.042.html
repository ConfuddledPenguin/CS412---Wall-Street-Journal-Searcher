Technology: IBM Helps Data Firms Reverse Growth Slowdown

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/08/90

STAMFORD, Conn. -- Strong first-quarter growth at International Business Machines Corp. helped the information industry buck a two-year slowdown, according to a report by a market-research firm here. Gartner Group Inc. said IBM's 9.2% revenue growth during the period beat the industry rate for the first time in five years. That performance led to a reversal of the industry's two-year trend toward slower growth: Gartner said revenue posted a 7.7% gain from the year-earlier period, compared with 5.5% year-to-year growth in the fourth quarter of 1989.

But Gartner noted that industrywide profits declined slightly, to 6.6% from 6.7%. It attributed the drop to a continuing mainframe-computer price war, a move away from proprietary systems, and a shift to smaller, less profitable computers.

Gartner added that the shift to smaller computers has affected the software market markedly, with companies that cater to the desktop market taking share away from mainframe and minicomputer software vendors.

Gartner also noted explosive growth in cellular-telephone subscriptions, and "hearty" growth by several microchip concerns. The company compiled its data from 221 publicly held corporations in data processing, telecommunications and electronic components.
