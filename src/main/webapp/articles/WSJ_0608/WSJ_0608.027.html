Canada Decides to Delay Long-Flight Smoking Ban

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/08/90

OTTAWA -- The Canadian government decided to delay for three years a planned smoking ban on international flights of more than six hours by Canadian carriers. The Transport Department said the ban, which was to have taken effect July 1, will now be implemented July 1, 1993.

In the meantime, the department said carriers will be required to reduce the proportion of seats for smokers on long-range flights by 25% July 1 and by similar amounts annually until July 1, 1993, when the ban takes effect.

The department, in explaining its decision to delay the ban, said Canadian airlines and some labor groups have expressed concern that the Canadian airline industry would suffer "serious declines in passenger traffic in markets which have higher percentages of smokers, such as Eastern Europe and the Far East."

A smoking ban currently applies to all domestic Canadian flights, to Canada-U.S. flights and to international flights of less than six hours.
