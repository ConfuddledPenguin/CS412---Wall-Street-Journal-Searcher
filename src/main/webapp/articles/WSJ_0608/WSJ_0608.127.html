International Brief -- Coloroll Group PLC: U.K. Firm Says It Entered Receivership as Talks Fail

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/08/90

Coloroll Group PLC said it went into receivership after the company was unable to arrange a refinancing strategy with Candover Investments PLC, a venture capital group. Coloroll, a U.K. home fashion and home products concern, has been involved in talks with Candover since May. Coloroll issued a strong profit warning in January, as returns from various operations waned in the wake of a downturn in the U.K. housing market and consumer spending.

Accounting firm Ernst & Young, the appointed receiver, and Coloroll weren't available to comment.

Financial problems have dogged Coloroll for several months, as the group's ratio of debt to equity rose following the purchase of John Crowther Group PLC, a British cloth and carpets maker, in a stock swap for #215 million ($363 million).

Coloroll planned to sell off several Crowther units, but they became less attractive as U.K. interest rates rose before the sales could be put into effect.

For the six months to Sept. 30, Coloroll reported a 50% drop in pretax profit to #10.1 million.
