Business Brief -- Lonrho PLC: Firm's Interim Pretax Profit Fell 8.3% From Last Year

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/08/90

Lonrho PLC, a British trading conglomerate, said interim pretax profit eased 8.3% from year-earlier levels, when taking into account last year's disposal of bulk whisky stocks. Sales of platinum group metals and gold contributed "significantly" to earnings in the current period, the company said. Excluding last year's #38 million ($64.2 million) disposal of bulk whisky stocks, pretax profits rose 34%.

Profit attributable to shareholders before extraordinary items fell to #56 million from #70 million last year.

The company said in addition to the contribution from mineral extraction and refining, its Hondo Oil & Gas division saw improved profits in the first half. The group's hotels and newspaper interests in the U.K. produced higher profits, although the manufacturing and motor companies were hit by the downturn in the British economy. The company didn't provide a detailed breakdown by division and officials weren't immediately available for comment.
