Business Brief -- Compression Labs Inc.: Supplier of Video Circuitry Agrees to Sony Unit Pact

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/08/90

Compression Labs Inc. said it agreed to supply Sony Corp.'s U.S. unit with video-conference equipment. Compression said that no dollar amount was specified but that the agreement could provide tens of millions of dollars in revenue over the next several years. Under the agreement, Compression will supply Sony with "video codecs," circuitry used to squeeze video signals onto phone lines or airwaves that otherwise are too small to carry the signals. Codecs are used in video conferencing to transmit without distortion the moving images of people at conference tables.

"Sony's a big company and this is going to be a very large market in a couple of years," the company said.

Compression Labs is a San Jose, Calif.-based supplier of codecs. The company also is working on circuitry to compress signals for high-definition television, which requires many times the amount of information that conventional TV signals contain.
