Business Brief -- PPG Industries Inc.: Pittsburgh Company Agrees To Venture With Essilor

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/08/90

PPG Industries Inc. said it agreed in principle to form a joint venture with France's Essilor International to make plastic lenses for consumer eyeglasses. PPG said it would own 51% of the new company, which would be based in St. Petersburg, Fla. The agreement, which has been approved by Essilor's board, must be approved by PPG directors. Terms of the venture weren't disclosed.

The new firm would make the plastic lenses, which darken when exposed to bright light. The product would be sold exclusively to lens casters for ultimate distribution to consumers. PPG said such lenses have been available only in glass.

PPG said the venture is part of the company's strategy to generate 40% of total chemicals revenue from specialty products by 1994. The Pittsburgh maker of glass, chemicals and coatings said it has invested more than $10 million to research and develop the new plastic lens, which has been test marketed under the Transitions trademark since July. Mass U.S. distribution is planned for 1991, with international sales expected to begin in 1992.
