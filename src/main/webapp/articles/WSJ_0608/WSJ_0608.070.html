Business Brief -- Ipsco Inc.: Charge of $16.9 Million Is Set for Second Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/08/90

Ipsco Inc., a Regina, Saskatchewan, steelmaker, said it will report a charge of 19.8 million Canadian dollars (US$16.9 million), or C$1.39 a share, against its second-quarter net income, as a result of asset write-downs and severance payments. A year earlier, Ipsco earned C$6.8 million, or 48 Canadian cents a share, on revenue of C$127.9 million.

The company said it wrote down the value of its Canadian assets producing tubular steel products used in the oil and gas industry, as a result of an expected decline in demand. The assets had a net book value before the write-down of C$77.2 million, Ipsco said. Even without the write-down, the company said it would have recorded a second-quarter loss, owing to falling sales of flat-rolled steel and a delay in shipping a large order of large diameter pipe, for environmental reasons.

Ipsco said its third-quarter outlook is positive, from the expected shipment of two large steel pipe orders. But if the Canadian dollar maintains its high value against the U.S. currency, Ipsco said its sales to traditional U.S. markets could be further affected.
