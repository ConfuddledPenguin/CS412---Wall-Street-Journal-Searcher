Technology: IBM Computer Centers Planned in Eastern Europe

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 06/08/90

International Business Machines Corp. said it plans to set up computer centers in Hungary and Czechoslovakia, in conjunction with academic institutions, to advance technology and initiate research projects. IBM Europe will provide the centers with advanced hardware, a library of systems and application software, and training and education for the staffs. It plans to lend the computer equipment to the countries, including an IBM 3090-model mainframe priced at about $1 million, a spokesman for the Armonk, N.Y.-based company said.

This represents the first part of a broader initiative IBM plans in Central and Eastern Europe to foster information-technology advances and give scientists and others access to tools to communicate in their fields or participate in international projects. The centers will be linked by a Vienna computer gateway to Western academic networks.

Setting up the centers is subject to export licenses being granted by the U.S. and the Coordinating Committee on Multilateral Export Controls. IBM said the Hungarian and Czechoslovak partners will sign a document that states the end use of the computers will be for peaceful purposes.

Once established, the IBM centers will be staffed and operated by the local IBM partners, for access by academics, students and people in industry. IBM's partner in Hungary will be the University of Economics in Budapest; in Czechoslovakia, Charles University and the Czech Technical University.
