International Brief -- Nixdorf Computer AG: Net Loss of $623.5 Million Is Reported for Last Year

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/08/90

Nixdorf Computer AG of West Germany posted a 1989 net loss of about 1.07 billion marks ($623.5 million), compared with year-earlier profit of 26.4 million marks after extraordinary gains. The financially troubled computer concern, which is being taken over by Siemens AG of West Germany, said its pretax operating loss last year widened to 849 million marks from 59.8 million marks in 1988.

The 1989 net loss figure reflects unspecified charges to account for the cost of Nixdorf's continuing corporate overhaul, the company said. In 1988, the modest profit included 152.8 million marks of extraordinary gains.

Analysts had been anticipating a Nixdorf loss of as much as one billion marks for 1989, which the company has characterized as the worst year in its history.
