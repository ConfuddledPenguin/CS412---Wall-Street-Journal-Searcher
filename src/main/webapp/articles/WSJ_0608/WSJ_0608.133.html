What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/08/90

CONAGRA AGREED to acquire Beatrice from KKR for $1.34 billion in cash and stock. The price, half what Beatrice insiders once hoped for, still gives KKR a 50% annual return on its investment. If approved by regulators, the deal will give ConAgra such brands as Hunt's and Wesson, boosting the food giant's clout in the dry grocery business. ---

The group seeking to buy UAL promised to pay $9 million to Gerald Greenwald, the former Chrysler Vice Chairman spearheading the buy-out, even if the takeover fails.

---

GM won a nearly $1 billion pact to supply pollution-control and engine-control parts to the Soviets' biggest auto maker. The five-year accord comes as GM is waging an aggressive expansion into Eastern Europe.

The U.S. and its Western allies agreed to ease their embargo on high-technology sales to the East bloc.

---

Fed Vice Chairman Johnson will return to academia when his term expires Aug. 3, despite the president's apparent willingness to reappoint him.

---

Chrysler's Iacocca agreed to stay on for an unspecified period after his contract expires in December 1991. The departure of his onetime heir apparent prompted the announcement.

---

Stock prices fell for a third consecutive day, pushing the Dow Jones Industrial Average down 14.32 points to 2897.33. Bonds inched higher on evidence of slowing economic growth.

---

Retail sales rose a disappointing 3.5% in May from a year earlier as cold, wet weather held down results, especially for summer clothes, outdoor products and air conditioners.

---

Capital spending is expected to rise 6.7% in 1990, less than the 7.8% increase projected earlier in the year. The latest survey of businesses reflects uncertainty about the economy.

---

Columbia S&L's plan to sell its $2.9 billion junk-bond portfolio includes novel financing terms that are boosting the sale's chances of success.

---

Schering-Plough's accord to sell Maybelline for $300 million to a group including management collapsed, but Wasserstein Perella stepped in to buy the cosmetics unit at the same price.

---

A bill to reregulate cable TV was passed by a Senate panel after a compromise with the industry unraveled. The legislation faces many obstacles.

---

Drexel's creditors rejected the parent concern's reorganization plan, and said they will seek to oust management and recover executive bonuses.

---

The Chicago Merc agreed to pay a $100,000 penalty for allegedly failing to report violations by member firms. It is the second-largest such settlement by a commodity exchange.

---

Grain futures prices jumped as wet weather in the southern Midwest continued to stall planting.

---

SmithKline named Squibb ex-president Jan Leschly to run the London-based firm's world-wide drug business, succeeding John Chappell, who quit over management philosophy.

---

TRW's $736 million project to build a "space tug" to boost satellites into higher orbits was canceled by NASA.
