Business Brief -- NEC Corp.: Company Has New Method For Making Memory Chips

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/08/90

NEC Corp. has developed a new siliconwafer etching technology, taking a crucial step toward mass production of 64 megabit dynamic random access memory chips, the company announced. The latest semiconductors to be mass produced are 4 megabit chips while 16-megabit chips are still in the experimental stage.

A NEC spokesman said the new technology, called Super-ECR Plasma Etching, makes it possible to quickly etch the tightly packed, complicated structures necessary for the next-generation chip.

The spokesman said the new technology will first be applied to the production of 16-megabit chips.

NEC's newly developed technology, using plasma over the silicon wafer, makes it possible to etch electric circuits at high speed.

Compared with previously developed plasma etching methods, the new technology is faster, finer and causes less damage to adjoining layers of wafer membrane, the spokesman said.
