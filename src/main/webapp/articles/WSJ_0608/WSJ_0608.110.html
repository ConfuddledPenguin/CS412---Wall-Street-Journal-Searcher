Business Brief -- Transcisco Industries Inc.: Firm to Make Investment In Florida's Piper Aircraft

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/08/90

Transcisco Industries Inc., San Francisco, said it agreed to make a "substantial" investment in Piper Aircraft Corp. and to take over Piper's parts-distribution business. Transcisco said a letter of intent signed with Piper calls for Transcisco to form a separate company to take over Piper's world-wide parts business. Transcisco said the letter also calls for it to receive warrants to acquire an unspecified amount of stock in Piper, a troubled Vero Beach, Fla., maker of small airplanes.

The acquisition of the Piper business, if completed, would fit into Transcisco's effort to expand transportation businesses beyond the company's basic rail-equipment maintenance-and-leasing operations, Transcisco said. More than 90,000 piper airplanes are flying around the world, it said, representing a large market for replacement parts.

The transaction provides the privately held Piper with the working capital it needs to deliver its order backlog of aircraft, which Piper estimates at over $200 million, and to develop new models, Transcisco said. Terms of the proposed acquisition weren't revealed.
