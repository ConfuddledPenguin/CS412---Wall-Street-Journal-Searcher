Marketing & Media: Time Warner to Amend Loan to Pathe, Which Is Seeking to Acquire MGM/UA

  • Author: Richard Turner
  • Date: 06/08/90

LOS ANGELES -- Pathe Communications Corp. said Time Warner Inc. agreed to amend its accord to provide a $650 million loan to Pathe, which is seeking to acquire MGM/UA Communications Co. for $1.27 billion. Pathe also gave itself another week to complete its $20-a-share tender offer for the studio. Observers have been skeptical about the bid by Pathe and its chairman, Italian financier Giancarlo Parretti, but Time Warner apparently is anxious to do whatever it takes to get the deal done. The change in the agreement would allow Pathe to retain more revenue from the new films it would produce after acquiring the studio, a move that also would make it easier for Pathe to borrow money.

If the transaction is completed, Time Warner would get distribution rights to MGM/UA's valuable library and Pathe's new films in return for the loan. Time Warner has a lien on the library as security for the loan, and also warrants that could give it 20% of the new company.

Pathe also said its offer, which was set to expire yesterday, has again been extended, this time to June 15. It can't be extended beyond June 23, according to the initial purchase pact. Pathe has already deposited $150 million in cash toward the purchase price, and a fourth $50 million down payment is due tomorrow.

The original agreement with Time Warner provided that Warner Bros. would collect a distribution fee on the library and Pathe's new films, and that Pathe would be required to devote 65% of its remaining revenue to repaying the loan. The amended agreement allows Pathe to retain 100% of the new film revenues, after distribution fees, though it still must repay the loan; the agreement on the library revenue remains the same.

"This gives us flexibility," said the Pathe spokesman.

Pathe said it had also obtained a commitment for $125 million in production loans from a bank and was discussing with Time Warner whether that would fulfill an agreement to obtain $200 million.

Pathe also provided details about the additional $450 million to be advanced to Pathe by Luxembourg-based Comfinance S.A., the closely held vehicle for Mr. Parretti. First of all, Pathe said it only needed $366 million, not $450 million, which will be provided in return for stock in the new company. The funds primarily will come from various lines of credit from other affiliated companies of Mr. Parretti.

The details seem to indicate that Pathe has arranged the financing without MGM/UA majority shareholder Kirk Kerkorian having to retain a stake, but an MGM/UA spokesman said Mr. Kerkorian hadn't made a final decision.

The Time Warner agreement stipulates that Pathe's additional funding must be in debt, not equity, and negotiations with Time Warner are still going on over this issue, said the Pathe spokesman.

Although some cynical observers have maintained that Time Warner's aim is to see Pathe default so it can grab the library, Warner Bros. Chairman Robert Daly dismisses the idea.

"This is not a deal that we make and hope he goes bankrupt," said Mr. Daly in an interview recently. "If Pathe does well and they make eight or ten movies a year, we have a distribution services agreement with them. So the upside of this deal could be great and that's what we are really betting on."

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, MGM/UA shares closed unchanged at $18.25, and Pathe shares fell 37.5 cents to close at $4.

Corrections & Amplifications

TIME WARNER Inc.'s loan agreement with Pathe Communications Corp. stipulates that additional financing procured by Pathe be in equity, not debt. The terms were misstated in Friday's edition.
