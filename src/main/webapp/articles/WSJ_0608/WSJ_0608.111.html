Business Brief -- Playboy Enterprises Inc.: Recapitalization Completed After Approval of Measure

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/08/90

Playboy Enterprises Inc., Chicago, said it completed a recapitalization plan, under which it will convert its common stock into voting and nonvoting classes. The proposal was criticized by some holders, but passage was never in question, as it had the support of Hugh M. Hefner, founder and former chairman, who holds a 71% stake in the publishing and entertainment concern.

After the measure was approved, Playboy said, it carried out a 1-for-2 reverse split of its common stock, and reclassified that reduced number of shares as voting Class A stock. It then paid a stock dividend of three shares of new nonvoting Class B common stock for each share of Class A common. The two classes will trade separately on the New York Stock Exchange, beginning today, Playboy said.

The dual-class arrangement will allow Mr. Hefner, should he wish to do so, to retain his majority voting stake while shedding up to three-quarters of his shares. That was what irked some shareholders, including Industrial Equity (Pacific) Ltd., which has a 7.6% Playboy stake.
