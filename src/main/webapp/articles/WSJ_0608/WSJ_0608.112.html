Business Brief -- Browning-Ferris Industries Inc.: Pact With Weyerhaeuser On Paper Recycling Is Set

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/08/90

Browning-Ferris Industries Inc., a Houston-based waste-management concern, said it agreed with Weyerhaeuser Paper Co. to recycle as much as 10,000 tons of paper a month in the Western U.S. Terms weren't disclosed. Under the contract, the unit of Weyerhaeuser Co., a Tacoma, Wash., forest products company, will buy newspaper, cardboard and office paper collected by Browning-Ferris in its participating recycling programs in Arizona, California, Nevada, Oregon and Washington.

Browning-Ferris said the paper recycling program also will extend to British Columbia, Canada. Newspapers from the collected material will be shipped to Weyerhaeuser's Norpac mill in Longview, Wash., which can process 500 tons of newspaper a day.

The company said the pact guarantees the purchase of 120,000 tons of newspaper, cardboard and high-grade office paper a year.
