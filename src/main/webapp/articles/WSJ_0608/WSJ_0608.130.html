International Brief -- Pilkington PLC: Higher Interest Costs Help Trim Pretax Profit by 3.4%

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/08/90

Pilkington PLC said its pretax profit for the year to March 31 fell 3.4%, as higher interest costs and reduced investment income offset a slight gain in its big glassmaking business. Pretax profit was #314 million ($186.1 million), down from #325 million a year earlier. Profit after taxes dropped 7.6% to #186 million from #201 million, while earnings per share eased 8.4% to 25 pence from 27.3 pence.

Net profit attributable to shareholders managed a slim 1.2% rise to #179 million from #176 million. The group took an extraordinary charge of #6.9 million, chiefly the result of a provision against the effect of hyperinflation on its Argentinian operations. The charge was down from the #24.4 million extraordinary loss a year earlier.

Chairman Antony Pilkington said the "economic climate in a number of the markets in which the group operates is more uncertain today than has been the case for some time." However, the group said it would still recommend a final dividend of 7.57 pence a share that would increase the total payout for the year to 10.5 pence from 9.5 pence.
