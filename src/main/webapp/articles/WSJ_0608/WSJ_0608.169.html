Royal Dutch/Shell Considers Renovations At Its Pernis Refinery

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 06/08/90

ROTTERDAM, the Netherlands -- Royal Dutch/Shell Group completed a study on restructuring operations at its Pernis refinery and will make a decision on whether to go ahead with the two billion guilder ($1.06 billion) renovation in the first half of 1991, a company spokesman said. Shell spent approximately 2.5 billion guilders upgrading Pernis with a hydrogen-conversion unit and related facilities, which produce higher than normal yields of lighter petroleum products, such as gasolines, from heavier crudes. Work on the hydrogen-conversion unit, which started in 1984, ended last year.

The spokesman declined to specify what new processes or operations the study called for. But he said the restructuring at the Group's largest European refinery would be specifically aimed at reducing emissions of sulfur dioxide and nitrous oxide and producing lighter products from heavier crude oils.

The spokesman declined to confirm whether the switch to facilities capable of processing heavier oils was a defensive move aimed at repositioning the refinery ahead of an expected drop in output of light, low sulfur oils.

But he did say: "When we look to the far future, we see that we will have to get heavier oils out of the ground."

If Shell proceeds with the restructuring, new construction at the plant would be completed by 1995, roughly five years before Dutch government-mandated reductions in emissions go into effect.

The study envisions reducing emissions of sulfur dioxide and nitrous oxide from the plant by 70% from 1980 levels. Current levels are 55% less than 1980 levels.

Output at the 350,000-barrel-a-day capacity plant won't be interrupted by the restructuring efforts, the spokesman said.
