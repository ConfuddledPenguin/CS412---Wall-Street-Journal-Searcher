VW Outlines Plans To Expand Venture With Yugoslavians

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/08/90

WOLFSBURG, West Germany -- Volkswagen AG outlined plans for an expanded joint venture to produce and market autos and car parts in Yugoslavia. The company said previously that it intended to expand its relationship with the Yugoslavian industrial group UNIS. Under a new agreement, Volkswagen will increase its stake in the auto maker Tvornica Automobila Sarajevo to 50% from 49%. UNIS will hold the remaining 50% of in Tvornica, which will be transformed under new government rules into a West German-style private limited-liability company, Volkswagen said.

Volkswagen, West Germany's largest auto maker, said it will boost its stake in the Yugoslavian company by subscribing to a planned, though unspecified, increase in Tvornica's share capital.

Volkswagen said Tvornica has begun a 200 million-mark ($118.3 million), five-year capital investment program, ending in 1995. The investments will be directed toward modernizing the Yugoslavian venture's national production network in an effort to boosts its competitive strength.

Volkswagen said the most important of the new projects at Tvornica, already a major supplier of diverse auto parts for Volkswagen cars, will be to make rear axles to be supplied to the West German company's Seat auto subsidiary in Spain. Those deliveries are expected to begin by the end of this year. The company hopes to gear up to assemble car engines by the end of 1991.

The capital investment program also is aimed at boosting Tvornica's output of complete Volkswagen autos to about 45,000 units in 1992 from an aggregate 35,000 VW Golfs, Jettas and Caddy models last year.

The joint-venture company hopes to boost annual sales in 1992 to the equivalent of 700 million marks from 484 million marks in 1989, Volkswagen said. The Yugoslavian venture projects export sales in that period to rise to about 220 million marks from 150 million marks.

Volkswagen has gotten off to a quick start in broadening its business and operating presence in Eastern Europe, following the opening of the Berlin Wall late last year. The company has a major car development, production and marketing venture planned for East Germany, and is negotiating a similar arrangement with Czechoslavakia's auto maker, Skoda.
