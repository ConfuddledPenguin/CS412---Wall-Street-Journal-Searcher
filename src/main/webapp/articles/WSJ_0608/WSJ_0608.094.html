Credit Ratings

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/08/90

FIRST FIDELITY BANCORP. (Lawrenceville, N.J.) -- Standard & Poor's Corp. lowered its ratings on about $750 million of debt, citing the regional banking concern's recent announcement of an expected increase of as much as 40% in nonperforming assets. S&P lowered its subordinated debt rating on the company, which operates in New Jersey and Pennsylvania, to double-B-plus from triple-B, and its rating on preferred stock to double-B from triple-B-minus. Other ratings were also lowered, S&P said. The rating concern put the company on its CreditWatch list for a possible lower rating in May after First Fidelity said second-quarter loan losses could be as much as $300 million and announced a dividend cut. S&P said the increase in problem loans could marginally exceed the company's estimates. "While the downside risk is limited, given the company's relatively moderate exposure to commercial real estate and highly leveraged transactions," S&P said, "First Fidelity's poor financial performance over the past several months has weakened its ability to handle new problems." It said that although the company has bolstered credit policies and sought to deal with its costs, "a weak operating environment will delay the financial benefits from these changes."
