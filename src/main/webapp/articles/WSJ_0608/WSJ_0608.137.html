Trump's 3 Casinos In New Jersey Aren't For Sale, Official Says

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/08/90

ATLANTIC CITY, N.J. -- Edward Tracy, the president of Donald Trump's Atlantic City holdings, said none of the beleaguered casino owner's three casinos are for sale. Mr. Tracy tried to allay concerns that Mr. Trump's casino properties were not doing well, and predicted they would produce "an avalanche of cash" this year.

Since the Taj Mahal opened in April, revenue at Trump Plaza and Trump's Castle has fallen, compared with a year earlier. Revenue at the Taj Mahal has also fallen short of expectations. But Mr. Tracy said reports of the Taj's revenue results have been unfair because the reports failed to account for about $16 million of non-casino income.

Mr. Tracy said he expects as many as 1,100 additional layoffs at the Taj. He said such layoffs were routine for a new casino.

Meanwhile, Mr. Trump named Gary Selesner president and chief operating officer of Trump Plaza Hotel and Casino. Mr. Selesner was previously acting president and chief operating officer.
