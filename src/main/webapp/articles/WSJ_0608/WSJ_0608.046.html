USX Says 57.1% of Shares Rejected Spinoff Proposal

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/08/90

PITTSBURGH -- USX Corp. said 57.1% of its shareholders rejected a proposal by New York investor Carl Icahn to spin off at least 80% of the company's steel operations. The preliminary count of the May 7 balloting showed that 75,021,397 shares, or 42.9% of shares cast, voted in favor of the plan, including Mr. Icahn's 33.9 million shares. About 28.4 million shares abstained. Mr. Icahn proposed shedding steel operations, with shareholders receiving a dividend for that business.

"In rejecting Mr. Icahn's call to spin off the steel business as a dividend, the shareholders have decisively supported our policy of well considered and prudent corporate change," said USX chairman Charles A. Corry.

Mr. Icahn said his proposal failed because a handful of institutions, while agreeing with his goal, voted with the company because of "strong indications" that management would undertake a restructuring. They were afraid, he said, "a positive vote might have the `backlash' effect of restricting their methods of restructuring."

The company won all but two of the 11 proposals before shareholders. In those two instances, shareholders favored the right to vote on the company's shareholder rights plan and the right to keep votes confidential. Both proposals were recommendations and, as such, aren't binding. But they likely will be reviewed by the board.

Mr. Icahn said he was "particularly interested" in the company's response to what is "now a shareholder mandate to remove or submit to a vote the company's `poison pill.'"
