New Securities Issues

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/08/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATE

Bankers Trust New York Corp. -- $200 million of 9 1/2% subordinated notes due June 14, 2000, priced at 99.397 to yield 9.595%. The non-callable notes were priced at a spread of 115 basis points above the Treasury's 10-year note. Rated single-A-2 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and double-A-minus by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Shearson Lehman Hutton Inc.

Whirlpool Corp. -- $200 million of 9 1/2% notes due June 15, 2000, priced at 99 7/8 to yield 9.519%. The non-callable notes were priced at 105 basis points above the Treasury's 10-year note. Rated single-A-2 by Moody's and single-A-plus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Goldman Sachs & Co.

MUNICIPAL

New York State -- $4.1 billion of 6.60% 1990 tax and revenue anticipation notes, tentatively priced by a Shearson Lehman Hutton Inc. group to yield 6.10%. The notes, rated MIG2/SP-1, are non-callable and are due March 28, 1991.

Triborough Bridge and Tunnel Authority, N.Y. -- $435.8 million of Convention Center Project bonds, Series E, due Jan. 1 and July 1, 1992-2000, 2010 and 2011, with capital appreciation bonds due Jan. 1, 2001-2003, and 2012, tentatively priced by a First Boston Corp. group to yield from 6.10% in 1992 to 7.62% in 2010. Current interest serial bonds are priced to yield from 6.10% in 1992 to 7.37% in 2000. There are $210.9 million of 7 1/4% term bonds priced at 96 1/4 to yield 7.62% in 2010 and $39.5 million of 6% term bonds priced 84.40 to yield 7.50% in 2011. Capital appreciation bonds are priced to yield from 7.35% in 2001 to 7.45% in 2003. There is also a capital appreciation bond priced to yield to 7.60% in 2012. The bonds are rated single-A by Moody's. A triple-B rating is expected from S&P, according to an official with the lead underwriter.

Cook County, Ill. -- $130 million of 6.25% general obligation 10-month tax anticipation notes, Series June 1990, due April 1, 1991, via a Shearson Lehman Hutton Inc. group. The notes, rated MIG-1 by Moody's, were priced for reoffering to yield 5.75%.

Illinois -- $90 million of general obligation bonds, Series of June 1990, due June 1, 1991-2011, via a Prudential-Bache Capital Funding group. The bonds, rated triple-A/double-A-plus, were priced for reoffering to yield from 5.80% in 1991 to 6.95% in 2008-2011.

MORTGAGES

Merrill Lynch Mortgage Investors -- $121.5 million of 9.70% Class A Remic pass-through certificates backed by manufactured housing loans, priced at 99.7813 to yield 9.85%, via Merrill Lynch Capital Markets. The issue, MLMI 1990-C, was priced at an undisclosed spread above the Treasury's 8 1/2% notes due August 1995. The underlying loans are being sold by Security Pacific Housing Services. The expected final maturity is Jan. 15, 2001. The issue, which has an estimated average life of 4.8 years, is rated double-A by S&P based on the quality of the underlying loans and a subordinated class of $28.5 million that absorbs any losses on the senior securities. The subordinated securities have been sold privately.

CMO Mortgage Trust III -- $100 million of Series 1 Remic mortgage securities offered in nine classes by Smith Barney, Harris Upham & Co. The offering, priced May 31 at market levels, is backed by Ginnie Mae 10% securities issued under the Ginnie Mae I and II programs. The weighted average remaining term to maturity is 28.5 years.

EUROBONDS

Mitsubishi Estate Co. (Japan) -- $200 million of 9 1/4% Eurobonds due June 20, 1997, priced at 101.325 via Nikko Securities Ltd. Fees 1 7/8.

Nordic Investment Bank (agency) -- $200 million of 9% Eurobonds due July 2, 1993, reoffered at a fixed price of 99.975 via Morgan Stanley International Ltd. Fees 0.20.

Collateralised Mortgage Securities No.4 PLC (special purpose vehicle) -- #200 million of floating-rate mortgaged-backed notes due June 29, 2030, reoffered at a fixed price of 99.80. Fees 0.31.

CB 1 (special purpose vehicle) -- 900 million French francs of floating-rate notes due Jan. 26, 1994, at a fixed reoffered price of par via Societe Generale. Coupon at 1/16 point above the three-month Paris interbank offered rate. Expected average life is 1.74 years; expected annual amortization rate, 11%. Securitized consumer loans made by a subsidiary of Compagnie Bancaire, Cetelem. Fees 1/4.

Landeskreditbank Baden-Wuerttemberg (West Germany) -- 200 million marks of 17% less six-month London interbank offered rate floating-rate notes, priced at par and due July 6, 2000, via Trinkaus & Burkhardt. Fees 0.30.

Enso Finance BV (Finland) -- 300 million Finnish markka of 13 1/2% bonds due July 20, 1993, priced at 101 via Kansallis-Osake-Pankki. Guaranteed by Enso Gutzeit OY. Fees 1 3/8.
