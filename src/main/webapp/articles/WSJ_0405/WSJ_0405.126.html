TEXAS INSTRUMENTS Inc. (Dallas) -- Moody's Investors Service Inc., citing intense competition in the semiconductor business and reduced growth in the defense arena, said it downgraded the debt ratings of this big electronics concern, affecting about $1.14 billion in debt. The rating concern said that it cut the rating because it believes the company's cash flows and profit margins will be lower "over the intermediate term." Further, it said that joint ventures and customer alliances, along with higher royalty payments, won't be enough to offset the huge capital requirements of the company's semiconductor business. Moody's said it cut Texas Instruments' senior debt rating to single-A-2 from single-A-1, its subordinated debt rating to single-A-3 from single-A-2 and its preferred stock rating to single-A-3 from single-A-2. A Texas Instruments spokesman said the company, which had more than $600 million in cash and short-term investments at year end, has a strong balance sheet and believes its overall financial situation will remain strong.
