California Regulators Let Mission Insurance Move Assets to Trusts

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/05/90

LOS ANGELES -- California insurance regulators said a state court approved a rehabilitation agreement for Mission Insurance Co's that, among other things, calls for the transfer of $29 million in assets to government trusts from the Los Angeles-based insurer, which has been in bankruptcy proceedings since 1987. Completion of the transfer is subject to approval by a federal court here overseeing the Chapter 11 bankruptcy proceedings of Mission Insurance Group, the parent of Mission Insurance Co's. A hearing on the plan is scheduled for May 9.

Under the rehabilitation plan, which was announced in January, the assets and liabilities of Mission Insurance Co's will be transferred to trusts overseen by the state insurance commissioner, after which the shells of the companies will be released from insurance liquidation proceedings and over to Mission Insurance Group.

The shells of the Mission Insurance Co's contain tax-loss carry-forwards that make them attractive, said Dana Brooks, a lawyer acting for the California Department of Insurance.

The transfer to the government trusts from Mission Insurance Co's will include $14 million in cash and 5.9 million common shares of Mission Insurance Group, representing about 29% of the stock outstanding. Other assets accumulated by state regulators to cover claims against Mission Insurance Co's include about $400 million recouped to date from settlements with reinsurers, Ms. Brooks said.

California's Department of Insurance has sued to recover about $2 billion in reinsurance -- an amount now reduced by the settlements. Reinsurers, who assume certain risks from insurance companies in return for a portion of the premiums, have resisted paying Mission claims, alleging that Mission had provided misleading financial information.

Mission Insurance Co's insolvency was one of the largest insurance company failures in history. Under Chapter 11, a company is protected from creditor lawsuits while working out a plan to pay its debts.
