Chevron Buys Rest Of French Concern From Rhone-Poulenc

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/05/90

SAN FRANCISCO -- Chevron Corp. said its Chevron Chemical Co. unit acquired the 50% it didn't own in Orogil S.A., a French-based maker of fuel and lubricating additives, from Rhone-Poulenc S.A. for an undisclosed amount. Chevron acquired its original stake in Orogil and an option for the remaining 50% in 1987. It took over management at that time. Orogil operates a plant at Gonfreville, France. Since the 1987 investment, marketing of Orogil's output has been integrated with Chevron's world-wide additives marketing.

Chevron, based in San Francisco, also said it will close its lubricating oil additives plant in east Los Angeles early next year, shifting that production to other facilities.

The company attributed the closure to the plant's age and to increasingly stringent air quality requirements in the Los Angeles area. The plant has about 60 employees. A spokesman for Chevron said the company's newest investment in Orogil and its intended closure of the Los Angeles facility aren't related.

Separately, Chevron's Pittsburg & Midway Coal Mining Co. unit said it signed a letter of intent to form an equal joint venture with Black Beauty Resources Inc. for the operation of five coal mines in Indiana and Illinois. The amount of Chevron's payment for its 50% stake in the partnership wasn't disclosed. The venture will be known as Black Beauty Coal Co.

The reserves acquired contain largely low-sulfur coal, which Chevron has said it expects to become increasingly sought after with new clean-air standards.

Production from the five Black Beauty mines totaled 4.2 million tons last year. Pittsburg & Midway, which operates nine mines in six states, produced 15.1 million tons in 1989. Closely held Black Beauty Resources is based in Evansville, Ind.
