Greenspan Urges Lowering Of Capital Costs for Banks

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/05/90

WASHINGTON -- The most important thing the government can do to increase the international competitiveness of U.S. banks is to reduce their cost of capital, Federal Reserve Board Chairman Alan Greenspan said. Testifying before a House Banking Committee task force on international competitiveness, Mr. Greenspan also urged once again that Congress lift restrictions on the ability of banks to deal in securities and engage in other nonbanking activities.

The Fed chairman also repeated a call to repeal legislation restricting the ability of banks to operate across state lines.

"There is reason to believe that the opportunity for a bank to diversify the products or services it offers or to diversify geographically may in some cases raise its rate of return and lower its risk," he said.

Most other countries, but not Japan, allow banks to engage in a broad range of financial activities. Mr. Greenspan has been arguing for some time that the U.S. should do the same in order to compete internationally.
