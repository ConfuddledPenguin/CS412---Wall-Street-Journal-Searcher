Survey Underscores Crisis Involving Texas Real Estate

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/05/90

DALLAS -- In a survey that underscores the real estate crisis in Texas, the Finance Commission of Texas found that banks, thrifts and the federal government control $43.55 billion of Texas real estate that has been repossessed or will be shortly. "It's a frightening number," said commission Chairman R. Dary Stone, president of Delphinance Development Corp. of Dallas.

About 45% of the property covered by the survey is raw land, a circumstance that doesn't bode well for Resolution Trust Corp. or Federal Deposit Insurance Corp. as they attempt to market the repossessed assets of thrifts and banks.

"There's just not a market for land," Mr. Stone said. "That's why the FDIC's and RTC's job is so difficult. They're going to be here for a long time."

And the size of the problem could be as much as 50% larger, Mr. Stone suggested, because the survey -- released yesterday -- didn't include nonperforming loans or real estate controlled by out-of-state thrifts and banks.

Land accounted for about $19.43 billion of the real estate covered by the survey. Retail, office and industrial properties, whose market is recovering in some parts of Texas, accounted for about $11.47 billion of the portfolio. Multifamily developments, including apartment complexes, which are selling well in most parts of Texas, made up about $7.47 billion of the total.

The survey covers real estate and nonperforming loans held by thrifts, banks and the government as of June 30, 1989. Mr. Stone said he doesn't expect that the $43.55 billion value will have changed much in intervening months.
