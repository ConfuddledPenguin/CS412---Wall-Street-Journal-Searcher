Business Brief -- Western Union Corp.: Service With IBM Is Set For Electronic Mail Systems

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/05/90

Western Union Corp., Upper Saddle River, N.J., and International Business Machines Corp. said they agreed to provide jointly a service that would enable customers to communicate between each other's electronic mail systems. Both systems will integrate a communication standard known as X.400, developed by the Consultative Committee for the International Telephone and Telegraph, the international standards committee.

The adoption of the standard will allow communication between the IBM Information Network's Screenmail service and Western Union 400 service, which is fully integrated with Western Union's EasyLink electronic mail service.

Western Union said the interconnection with IBM Screenmail is "a further step to achieve a full and open interconnection" with major public electronic messaging systems. Western Union provides a broad range of messaging, financial and other services. IBM is the Armonk, N.Y.-based computer giant.
