Georgia-Pacific Land Gift Is Studied by Grand Jury

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/05/90

ATLANTA -- A federal grand jury is investigating whether Georgia-Pacific Corp. inflated for tax purposes the value of Florida swampland that it donated to a state water management agency in 1984, a grand jury witness said. A spokeswoman for Georgia-Pacific, an Atlanta-based forest-products concern, declined to comment.

John Flanagan, deputy assistant executive director of the Suwannee River Water Management District, in Live Oak, Fla., said he and another agency member appeared under subpoena before a grand jury in Atlanta March 13.

Mr. Flanagan said Martin Weinstein, an assistant U.S. attorney, inquired about the circumstances under which Georgia-Pacific donated 5,400 acres of land to the water management district, a conservation and management agency, in 1984. He said the grand jury also subpoenaed the agency's records related to Georgia-Pacific's donation of the Santa Fe Swamp tract, which is north of Little Santa Fe Lake in Bradford County.

Mr. Flanagan said the U.S. attorney is focusing on a $24 million tax deduction that Georgia-Pacific took as a result of the donation. Georgia-Pacific reportedly purchased the land in 1981 for $1.9 million. Prosecutors couldn't be reached for comment.
