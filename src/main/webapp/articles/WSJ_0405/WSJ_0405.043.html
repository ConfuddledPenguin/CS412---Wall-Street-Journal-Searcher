New Securities Issues

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/05/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATE

Cyprus Minerals Co. -- $150 million of notes, due April 1, 2002, was priced at par to yield 10.125%. The non-callable notes were priced at a spread of 151 basis points above the Treasury's 10-year note. Rated Baa-2 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and triple-B by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by First Boston Corp.

Lousiana Power & Light Co. -- $100 million of first mortgage bonds, due April 1, 2020, was won in competitive bidding by a group led by Salomon Brothers Inc. The bonds will be reoffered at 98.125 to yield 10.33%, or a spread of 174 basis points above the Treasury's bellwether bond. The issue, which is non-refundable for five years, is rated Baa-3 by Moody's and triple-B by S&P.

MUNICIPALS

New Jersey -- $350 million various full faith and credit general obligation bonds, due April 1, 1992-2011, apparently were won by a Goldman Sachs & Co. group. The bonds, rated triple-A by both Moody's and S&P, were priced for reoffering to yield from 6.10% in 1992 to 7.10% in 2010 and 2011. Current interest serial bonds are priced to yield from 6.10% in 1992 to 7.10% in 2010 and 2011. There are no term bonds.

Massachusetts Bay Transportation Authority -- $205 million general obligation bonds, due March 1, 1991-2000, 2003, 2005, 2010 and 2015, were priced by a Merrill Lynch Capital Markets group to yield from 6.50% in 1991 to 7.85% in 2015. Current interest serial bonds were priced to yield from 6.50% in 1991 to 7.20% in 2000. There are $20 million term bonds priced at 99.16 to yield 7.35% in 2003. There are $16.5 million term bonds priced at 97.97 to yield 7.35% in 2005. There are $51.4 million term bonds priced at 92.44 to yield 7.75% in 2010. There are $73 million term bonds priced at 97.54 to yield 7.85% in 2015. The bonds are rated Baa by Moody's and triple-B by S&P, excluding bonds due in 19952000 and 2005, which are MBIA-insured and rated triple-A by both agencies. Term bonds due in 2003 are FGIC-insured and also rated triple-A by both agencies.

MORTGAGES

Advanta Corp. -- $53.7 million of Class A certificates were sold privately by two wholly-owned subsidiaries, Colonial National Bank USA and Advanta Mortgage Corp. USA, representing ownership interests in Advanta Home Equity Loan Trust 1990-1. The issue was underwritten by Morgan Stanley & Co. The trust holds certain fixed-rate, closed-end home equity loans and will elect real estate mortgage investment conduit status. The certificates are insured by Financial Guaranty Insurance Corp. and rated triple-A by both Moody's and S&P.

EUROBONDS

ADT Ltd. (Bermuda) -- #100 million of cumulative preference shares, due July 31, 2005, exchangeable into ordinary shares of BAA PLC, the British airports ownership and management group, via Credit Suisse First Boston. Fixed price is #1,000 per preference share. Shares pay 8% semi-annual dividend. Conversion premium is expected to be 15%19% above share price when terms are fixed on or before April 10. Cash option, so that when preference shares are exchanged, issuer may elect to pay the cash equivalent. Callable from July 1993 at par, subject to BBA shares exceeding exchange price by percentage equal to put price. Put option on July 31, 1995 indicated at a price to yield 50-75 basis points above the 12% U.K. gilt due 1995. Fees 2 1/2.

World Bank (agency) -- 1 billion French francs of 10% eurobonds due May 9, 1997 priced at 101 1/2 to yield 10.05% less fees, via Credit Commercial de France. Fees 1 3/4.

Nestle Holding Inc. (Swiss parent) -- 100 billion lire of 13 1/4% eurobonds due May 4, 1993, priced at 101 1/2, via Istituto Bancario San Paolo di Torino. Fees 1 3/8.

Republic of Turkey (sovereign) -- 250 million deutsche marks of 10% bonds due April 24, 1997, priced at 102 to yield 9.59%, via Commerzbank AG. Fees 2 1/4.

Eurofima (agency) -- $75 million (Australian) of 14 5/8% eurobonds due May 9, 1994, priced at 101.60, via Deutsche Bank Capital Markets. Fees 1 3/4.

Girozentrale Vienna (Austria) -- 1.5 billion Austrian schillings of floating rate notes due April 1998 priced at par and paying interest at 1/8 point below the three-month Vienna interbank offered rate, via Girozentrale Vienna. Call option from 1993 at par and on every payment date thereafter.
