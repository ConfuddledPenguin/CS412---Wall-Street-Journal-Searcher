Greyhound Boosts Service, To Offer Some Free Tickets

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/05/90

DALLAS -- Hoping to bolster business prior to the pivotal Easter travel weekend, strikebound Greyhound Lines Inc. said it was increasing service in its Eastern division and offering passengers in some cities free-round trip tickets if buses don't arrive within 15 minutes of their scheduled time. Greyhound Lines, a unit of GLI Holding Co., said it would begin offering hourly departures from New York to Boston, Philadelphia and Washington, D.C., on Fridays and Sundays and that service between New York and eight Northeastern cities, plus Miami, had been restored to pre-strike levels.

The company said the free round-trip tickets would be available to passengers traveling between any of nine cities, including Boston, Philadelphia and Washington.

A company spokesman said the additions would put the company's miles of scheduled service at about 55% of prestrike levels and that, as of Tuesday, Greyhound was carrying about 47% the number of passengers as the same day a year ago. Officials at the striking Amalgamated Transit Union claim both figures are inflated.

The promotional campaign comes as a walkout by 9,300 ATU members enters its second month. Amid continued vandalism and sniper attacks on Greyhound buses, no talks have been scheduled.
