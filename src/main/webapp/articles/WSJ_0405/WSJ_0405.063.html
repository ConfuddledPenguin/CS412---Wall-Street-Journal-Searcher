Trades in Issue Suspended

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/05/90

WASHINGTON -- The Securities and Exchange Commission suspended trading in Southland Communications Inc. for 10 days, beginning yesterday, because of questions about control of the company and recent market activity. The agency said it stopped trading in the Santa Ana, Calif., radio-paging company because of "a possible undisclosed change in control." Moreover, the SEC said it had questions about the identity of the owners of Southland's common and preferred shares.

In Santa Ana, Calif., company officials weren't available to comment.
