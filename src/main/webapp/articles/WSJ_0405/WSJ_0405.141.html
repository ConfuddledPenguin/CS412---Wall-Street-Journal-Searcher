Business Brief -- Whitehall Corp.: Board Will Oppose Effort By Firm for Proxy Fight

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/05/90

Whitehall Corp. said its board will "vigorously oppose" an effort by Cambridge Capital Fund L.P. to lead a proxy fight against the company. Cambridge, a New York investment company, yesterday proposed acquiring the 90.4% of Whitehall that it doesn't already own for $20 a share and said it plans to nominate five directors to Whitehall's board at the company's annual shareholders meeting April 23.

Whitehall said that all its shareholders should be allowed to "realize the full value of its strategies," which have left the company debt-free and with more than $10 a share in cash and equivalents.

Whitehall's chairman, Lee D. Webster, said in a news release that the company's board will consider Cambridge's takeover proposal "in due course."

Whitehall, based in Dallas, modifies and maintains aircraft, sells underwater acoustic technology to the U.S. Navy and makes marine sensor systems.
