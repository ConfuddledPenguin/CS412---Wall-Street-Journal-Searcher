Hate-Crime Statistics Bill Is Sent to Bush for Signing

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/05/90

WASHINGTON -- The House gave final congressional approval to a bill that would require the Justice Department for the first time to collect and publish statistics on so-called hate crimes. The measure, passed 402-18, now goes to President Bush, who is expected to sign it.

Hate crimes are those motivated by prejudice based on a victim's race, religion, national origin or sexual orientation. Despite increased federal investigation and prosecution of such crimes, they have persisted in recent years and may have even increased, according to surveys conducted by various private organizations.

"Many would say the problem has escalated," said Rep. Barbara Kennelly (D., Conn.), a leading sponsor of the legislation. The bill would require the Justice Department to collect hate crime statistics and publish them annually so that federal, state and local law-enforcement officials can measure trends and deploy resources more effectively.
