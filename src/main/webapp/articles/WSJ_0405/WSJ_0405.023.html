Bairnco Says Unit's Insurance May Be in Doubt

  • Author: Robert Daniels
  • Date: 04/05/90

NEW YORK -- Bairnco Corp. reaffirmed its plan to spin off its Keene Corp. unit to shareholders, but it said the insurance coverage that until now has protected Keene from protracted asbestos-related legal settlements may be in doubt. Bairnco's shares have fallen more than 50% in the past week and have lost nearly two-thirds of their value since the start of the year. On Tuesday, Shearson Lehman Hutton Inc. removed the stock from its recommended list, citing uncertainty about the asbestos litigation Keene faces and the adequacy of its insurance coverage.

The litigation stems from Baldwin-Ehret-Hill, a business that Keene acquired in 1968. The BEH unit was shed in 1974 although it stopped making products containing asbestos in 1972.

Bairnco's shares rebounded a bit yesterday, closing at $6.25, up 12.5 cents, in New York Stock Exchange composite trading.

Bairnco said in its annual report, filed with the Securities and Exchange Commission last Monday, that at the end of 1989 about 70,300 bodily-injury cases were pending, and in the first quarter, "new bodily-injury cases have continued to be filed at a high rate."

From the mid-1970s through the first quarter of 1990, Keene's settlement and defense costs of settling nearly 60,000 bodily-injury cases totaled $260 million, of which insurers paid $251 million, Bairnco's filing said.

Keene estimates that at March 31, it had about $143 million of applicable insurance coverage remaining for asbestos claims, "but substantially all" of it "is either subject to litigation or dispute or will not be available until after such insurance litigation or disputes or other issues are resolved," the annual report says.

Further, although "a large number" of bodily-injury cases have been resolved for Keene, the company said it can't predict the number of future claims or the rate of dispositions.

Keene also has $30 million of insurance coverage that it calls "uncertain" because it is "owed to Keene by insolvent" insurers. Keene is trying to recover part of the amount from liquidators and state guaranty funds.

The company said it is confident that it will win the insurance litigation.

Keene makes heat-shielding devices. After the spinoff, which was announced in January, Bairnco plans to concentrate on specialty laminates and adhesives, shielding systems, and cutting products.

"The whole worry is about this asbestos problem," says Arthur Buffman, analyst at Jesup & Lamont Inc. The analyst notes that recently, institutions held about half Bairnco's shares, creating a "herd instinct. Until that dries up, it'll be a problem temporarily. They've got terribly good people running the show, and in time they'll resolve it."

Mr. Buffman said that "even though they might not be justified, people are putting in" asbestos claims. Bairnco said in its filing that "recent claims data suggest that increasing proportions of bodily-injury cases are being filed" by people who had only minimal exposure to asbestos and are less likely to have suffered injury.

Keene also is a defendant in some 83 property-damage cases pending at Dec. 31. In January, three more were filed, the filing said, adding that 29 states are seeking to hold Keene and other companies liable for asbestos abatement in public buildings in those states.
