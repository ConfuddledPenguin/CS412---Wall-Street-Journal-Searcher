Ambrose Evans-Pritchard, a contributor to the Spectator, writing in the April 1 issue of National Review:

The Nicaraguan people have extinguished the romantic dream in the most subversive way possible, by ballot. They voted against all this romance, and poetry, and patriotism, and revolutionary machismo. They want to be dull, like Costa Ricans. Dull and fat in the American back yard.
