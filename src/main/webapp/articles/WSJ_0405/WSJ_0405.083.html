Senate Clears Kelley For New Fed Term

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/05/90

WASHINGTON -- The Senate confirmed the nomination of Edward Kelley to a new term on the Federal Reserve Board. The nomination of Mr. Kelley to a full 14-year term that began Feb. 1 was approved by a voice vote. A former Houston businessman, Mr. Kelley is a longtime friend of both President Bush and Secretary of State James Baker. He rarely speaks publicly about the economy or Fed policy and isn't thought to have a strong influence on monetary policy.

Before joining the Fed board in 1987, Mr. Kelley, 57 years old, was chairman of Investment Advisors Inc., a Houston money manager. He is a graduate of Rice University and received a master's degree in business administration from Harvard University.
