Maxtor Corp. Offer To Buy MiniScribe Accepted by Court

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/05/90

SAN JOSE, Calif. -- Maxtor Corp. said its bid to buy substantially all the assets of troubled disk-drive maker MiniScribe Corp. was accepted by the U.S. Bankruptcy Court in Denver. Maxtor's bid totaled $46 million, consisting of $21.5 million in cash, $20 million in stock, and $4.5 million in cash and accounts receivable to be released by MiniScribe's Hong Kong and Singapore facilities. A Maxtor spokeswoman said she couldn't elaborate on the breakdown.

MiniScribe, based in Longmont, Colo., has been operating under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code since the beginning of the year. In January, the company said it would sell its assets to the highest bidder so that new investors could start with a clean slate and creditors could divide the proceeds.

Wearnes Technology of Singapore also bid for MiniScribe's assets.

MiniScribe, which last year said its previous management had improperly manipulated its financial results, sought protection from creditors under the federal Bankruptcy Code after it was unable to settle a number of shareholder and bondholder lawsuits against it.

The Maxtor spokeswoman said the company won't be acquiring any of MiniScribe's debts, or any liability for lawsuits. Maxtor is a maker of data-storage products.
