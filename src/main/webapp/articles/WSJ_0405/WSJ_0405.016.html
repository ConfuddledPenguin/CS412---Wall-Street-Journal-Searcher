Ashland Oil to Sell Its Boiler Business To Deutsche Babcock

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/05/90

ASHLAND, Ky. -- Ashland Oil Inc. agreed to sell its industrial boiler business to Deutsche Babcock Aktiengesellschaft of West Germany. The company declined to disclose the proposed sale price. Initially, Deutsche Babcock will buy a 20% interest in the Ashland unit, Riley Consolidated, which is based in Worcester, Mass. Deutsche Babcock is obligated to buy the remaining 80% by Sept. 25, 1991, if certain operating results are achieved. If the goals aren't met, Ashland could be required to repurchase the 20% interest for a nominal price, Ashland said.

Riley designs and produces steam generating boilers, mainly for electric utilities. Annual sales are about $100 million, said H. Kerner Smith, Riley president and chief executive officer. He said the unit has been incurring losses, but "is turning the corner, and ongoing operations are profitable." Sales have been as high as $250 million during years of high spending by electric utilities, he said.

The purchase will give Deutsche Babcock an entry into the U.S. boiler market, Mr. Smith said. Deutsche Babcock is a major participant in the world market, he said, with more than $1.5 billion of annual sales of boilers and related products.

Ashland said the agreement will have no effect on fiscal 1990 earnings. Transaction results will be deferred until September 1991, Ashland said. Ashland is an oil refiner and marketer that has diversified into chemicals, engineering and other activities.
