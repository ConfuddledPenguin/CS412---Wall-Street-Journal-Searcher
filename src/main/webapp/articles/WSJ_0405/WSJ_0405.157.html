Business Brief -- American Savings Bank: Report of 1st-Quarter Loss Expected; Charge Is Cited

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/05/90

American Savings Bank said it expects to report a first-quarter loss following a $4 million charge resulting from nonperforming real estate loans. American Savings, White Plains, N.Y., didn't estimate the extent of the loss. In the 1989 first quarter, it earned $6.5 million, or 88 cents a share.

The thrift said it continues to evaluate "the upward trend" in the bank's level of nonperforming assets and the adverse conditions in the real estate market, and will determine an appropriate provision for loan losses accordingly.

American Savings also announced the resignation of its executive vice president in charge of real estate lending, H. L. Van Varick, 50 years old. The thrift said a successor hadn't been appointed.

American Savings common stock tumbled 50 cents yesterday to close at $4.125 a share, in New York Stock Exchange composite trading.
