Business Brief -- Consolidated Rail Corp.: Track-Service Union Barred By Court From Any Strike

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/05/90

Consolidated Rail Corp. said it received a permanent injunction, barring the Brotherhood of Maintenance of Way Employees from striking the freight railroad. Philadelphia-based Conrail said a federal court in Pennsylvania ruled that current talks on a new labor contract conformed with the Railway Labor Act and that the threatened strike violated a provision of the law.

The union represents 6,000 employees who maintain Conrail's tracks. Jeff Zeh, international president of the Detroit-based union, said that "obviously we're disappointed."

Mr. Zeh objected to what he called "the slow pace" of the negotiations, and he said that Conrail's plan to restructure -- including a stock buy-back, an employee stock ownership plan, and work-force cuts -- would hurt the union.
