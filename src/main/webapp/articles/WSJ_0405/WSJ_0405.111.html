Business Brief -- Casual Male Corp.: Creditor Protection Sought Under the Bankruptcy Code

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/05/90

Casual Male Corp., citing a troubled menswear retail market, said it filed for protection from its creditors under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code. Earlier, Casual Male reported that efforts to sell the Shrewsbury, Mass., company hadn't been successful and that it will probably report a loss of more than $6 million for its fiscal year ended Jan. 27.

The clothing retailer said that "significant" competition from discount retailers and costs associated with an aggressive store-expansion program were the principal factors contributing to its problems. Casual Male opened more than 100 stores last year and currently operates more than 335 units.

In January, Casual Male said it arranged a $14.5 million credit facility with Shawmut National Bank that is secured by inventory and equipment. Last month, the company also arranged for an unsecured loan of $2.3 million from certain major stockholders.

Casual Male will continue to operate under court protection from creditors while it restructures and reorganizes.
