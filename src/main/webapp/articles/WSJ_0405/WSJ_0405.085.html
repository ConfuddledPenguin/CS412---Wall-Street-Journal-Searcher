Campeau's U.S. Units Project 3% Decline In 1st-Period Sales

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/05/90

CINCINNATI -- Campeau Corp.'s troubled U.S. retail units projected that first-quarter sales will be down 3% and second-quarter sales will be roughly equal to those of the same quarter last year. Federated Department Stores Inc. and Allied Stores Corp. announced cash flow and sales projections for the first and second quarters of 1990 at a meeting of creditors of the two Campeau units. Campeau's first quarter runs from February through April. Comparisons between 1990 and 1989 figures didn't include sales from new stores.

Cash flow in the first quarter was projected to be $29 million for Federated and $14 million for Allied.

Company officials said that the flat sales projections for the second quarter provide evidence that Federated and Allied are recovering from their Chapter 11 federal-bankrputcy court filing on Jan. 15. The companies predicted cash flow in the second quarter will be $24 million for Federated and $6 million for Allied.

At the meeting yesterday, James Zimmerman, president of Federated and Allied, told creditors that since January the companies had instituted cost-cutting measures at the corporate and divisional levels that would save $50 million annually. The savings were in payroll and nonpayroll cuts, he said.

About 250 creditors and lawyers attended the informational meeting, which is mandated by the U.S. bankruptcy code. Allen I. Questrom, chairman and chief executive officer of Federated and Allied, told creditors that the company had succeeded in retaining and motivating personnel and stocking its stores with merchandise.

"We have substantially stabilized our organizations and our work force, and in almost all areas of our business we have managed to restore normalcy to our ongoing operations," Mr. Questrom said.

Creditors were invited to ask executives questions but few did so. One lawyer who attended said that creditors didn't yet have enough financial information to ask questions. The companies aren't required to file detailed schedules of assets and liabilities until June 1.
