Eastern Airlines Seeks to End Pact With Union Pilots

  • Author: Bridget O'Brian
  • Date: 04/05/90

Eastern Airlines said it asked for bankruptcy court permission to break its pilots union contract in an attempt to reduce its costs. The financially ailing Texas Air Corp. unit, suffering heavy losses, estimated that abrogating the contract would save it more than $50 million a year. If Eastern can break the contract, it can unilaterally cut pilot pay and change work rules.

The carrier has lately been struggling with its unsecured creditors over a repayment proposal the creditors consider inadequate, and has said it planned new steps to slash costs, including seeking termination of its pilot union agreement, as well as reducing operations and selling more planes.

This is the second time Eastern has filed for permission to break the contract. It first did so last June, but withdrew the request within a month. If this attempt is successful, it would mirror a move made seven years ago by Texas Air chairman Frank Lorenzo, who put Texas Air's Continental Airlines unit into Chapter 11 to abrogate its contracts. Continental emerged from bankruptcy court a low-cost, low-fare airline.

Eastern's pact with the Air Line Pilots Association expired in July 1988, and it has been negotiating with the union for more than two years for a new contract. Under airline labor law, terms of the old contract stay in effect until a new one is signed or federal mediators declare the two sides at an impasse.

A spokesman for the pilots union said Eastern filed its request after the National Mediation Board, which oversees airline labor contracts, recessed indefinitely talks between the pilots and the airline.
