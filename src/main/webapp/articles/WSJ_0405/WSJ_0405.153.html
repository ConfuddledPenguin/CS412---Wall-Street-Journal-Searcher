Business Brief -- PSI Holdings Inc.: Rate Reduction Is Ordered For Public Service Indiana

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/05/90

Indiana utility regulators rejected a proposed three-year rate freeze for Public Service Indiana and instead ordered a 4.25% rate reduction. The Plainfield, Ind., company said that as a result, it faces higher potential costs associated with anticipated clean-air legislation. Nonetheless, the unit of PSI Hold ings Inc. said it doesn't plan to appeal yesterday's Indiana Utility Regulatory Commission ruling, which allows it a 15.76% return on equity, which as of last Dec. 31 totaled $560.5 million.

The rate reduction will cost the utility about $37.3 million in revenue a year, the company said. A spokesman said Public Service Indiana hadn't yet determined when the rate decrease would go into effect.

PSI Holdings shares rose 75 cents to close at $17.25 a share yesterday, in New York Stock Exchange composite trading.
