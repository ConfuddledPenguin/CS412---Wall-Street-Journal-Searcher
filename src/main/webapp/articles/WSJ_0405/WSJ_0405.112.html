Business Brief -- Banca Nazionale del Lavoro: An Annual Group Net Loss Of $192.1 Million Is Posted

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/05/90

Banca Nazionale del Lavoro, Italy's largest, state-owned commercial bank, posted a consolidated net loss of 240 billion lire ($192.1 million) in 1989. Milan-based BNL, which emerged last autumn at the center of a major financial scandal involving its Atlanta branch and some $3 billion of irregular loans to Iraq, said the losses reflected the plunge in profit announced recently by its parent bank.

The parent bank reported a loss of 498 billion lire in 1989, compared with profit of 105 billion lire a year earlier. BNL attributed the losses to a major cleanup of its balance sheet following the scandal.

The 1989 consolidated loss "significantly absorbs the 498 billion-lire loss posted by the parent bank," BNL said in a statement.

BNL also reported that consolidated gross profit, net of extraordinary items, rose 31% to 1.058 trillion lire last year from 807 billion lire in 1988. BNL's directors also proposed a total of 778 billion lire be allocated for risk funds, against 390 billion lire in 1988.
