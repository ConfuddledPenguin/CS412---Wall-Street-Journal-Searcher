Business Brief -- BFS Bankorp Inc.: Plan Backed for Investors To Raise Stake From 9.9%

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/05/90

BFS Bankorp Inc. backed a plan under which Fredric H. Gould, Gould Investors L.P. and BRT Realty Trust would seek clearance to boost their stake in the thrift to as much as 24.9% from 9.9%. BFS also agreed to expand its board to six members from five, to add Mr. Gould, who is chairman of BRT and a general partner of Gould Investors.

Also, James A. Randall, president and chief executive officer of BFS, said the holding company for Bankers Federal Savings expects to settle its litigation with First Capital Group Inc. shortly.

First Capital had proposed to pay $16 a share, or $23 million, for BFS, which rejected the bid as inadequate. The Redondo Beach, Calif., investment firm waged a proxy fight, and one of its executives was named to BFS's board. BFS filed suit to block First Capital from making any offer and to void any proxies that First Capital solicited.

A spokesman for First Capital was unavailable for comment.
