Technology: AT&T Is Entering Service-Based Sector Of Voice Mail Market

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/05/90

NEW YORK -- American Telephone & Telegraph Co. is entering the service-based voice mail market, using a system it developed with VoiceCom Systems Inc. The network-based phone messaging service, called AT&T Voice Mail, is a national service that will operate continuously using AT&T 800 service and touchtone telephones. Subscribers to the service don't purchase equipment, but rather pay a monthly service charge and an additional per-message fee. An installment fee of $10 per mailbox also will be charged.

The service allows the user to create, send and receive voice messages. The telecommunications company is targeting businesses whose employees travel a lot or whose offices are spread out.

Neither of AT&T's two largest long-distance competitors, MCI Communications Corp. and US Sprint Communications Co., a unit of United Telecommunications Inc., plans to offer such a service. Telecom USA, Atlanta, the fourth-largest carrier, has been offering voice mail service since 1988.

AT&T already operates in the private voice-mail market, where customers purchase their own equipment. The company estimates the overall market for subscription-based voice mail ranged from $175 million to $200 million in 1989.

The private product-based market, where companies buy in-house systems, is estimated at about $500 million a year by Kenneth G. Bosomworth, president of International Resource Development, a telecommunications consulting firm.

VoiceCom, based in San Francisco, is a provider of voice messaging and processing services.
