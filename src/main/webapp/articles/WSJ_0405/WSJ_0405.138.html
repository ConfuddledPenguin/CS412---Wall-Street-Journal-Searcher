National Heritage Inc. Sets a Merger Accord With Life Care Centers

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/05/90

DALLAS -- National Heritage Inc., a troubled nursing-home management concern, said it agreed to merge with Life Care Centers of America in a stock transaction. Terms call for the two companies to become a new concern under the Life Care name. Current National Heritage stockholders would receive one share of stock in the new company for each common share now owned, giving them almost 40% of the new concern.

Owners of Life Care Centers, a closely held company based in Cleveland, Tenn., and its affiliates would receive about 26 million new common shares, or just over 60% of the new company. The companies said the stock is expected to be publicly traded.

The merger is contingent on the companies reaching a separate agreement with Southmark Corp., a troubled thrift and real-estate concern that directly owns about 22% of National Heritage. The transaction calls for Southmark to contribute three leased health-care properties and mortgages, advances to its health-care limited partnerships and its general partnership interests in its health-care limited partnerships to the new company in exchange for preferred stock.

The merger, which is subject to several conditions, is expected to be completed in the fall, and the companies said the valuation of the assets contributed to the new concern could change before then. Pro forma financial statements from Life Care aren't expected until the end of this month.

National Heritage, which operates nursing homes and retirement centers, many of which are owned by Southmark partnerships, also said it has reserved the right to negotiate with other parties interest in acquiring it.
