E-Systems' Auditor Notes Federal Inquiry

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/05/90

DALLAS -- E-Systems Inc. said its auditor noted in its opinion of the company's financial statements that it couldn't determine the potential financial impact of a U.S. government investigation into whether E-Systems violated procurement regulations. The explanatory paragraph included in Ernst & Young's letter to shareholders and directors marked the first time that the auditor has raised the issue in its opinion, even though the dispute with the U.S. Army dates back to 1985.

In its annual report, E-Systems said it hasn't taken any provisions for liability or loss related to the litigation and can't determine the outcome of the matter. However, it said that it considers potential charges as "extremely serious" and that an indictment could lead the government to suspend or debar the company and its subsidiaries from receiving new federal contracts.

According to the company's financial statements, the Army terminated two contracts, valued at $19 million, on Dec. 31, 1985, saying the concern defaulted on its agreement to build military tactical radios. E-Systems appealed the termination and sought $13 million in claims in a case that was to be tried in March 1988.

However, in February 1988, the government told E-Systems that it was beginning a criminal investigation into whether the company violated procurement regulations at its Memcor division. The division has since been discontinued.

The government hasn't filed any formal allegations or indictments against the company. But E-Systems said that in January of this year, a former first-line supervisor of the company who had given a deposition at the Army's request in the civil case was convicted of three counts of perjury and one count of obstruction of justice. E-Systems said the former employee wasn't a witness for the company, but her conviction "may have adverse implications for the successful prosecution" of its civil case.

An E-Systems spokesman wouldn't elaborate, saying that the matters are in litigation.
