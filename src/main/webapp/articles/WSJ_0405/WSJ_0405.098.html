U.S. Postpones the Launch Of Pegasus Winged Rocket

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/05/90

WASHINGTON -- The Pentagon postponed until today at 3:07 p.m. EDT, the maiden launch of the Pegasus winged rocket. The launch, which had been scheduled for yesterday, was scrapped because of cloudy weather. Pegasus managers were concerned that flying the rocket through clouds could inadvertently set off a spark that would ignite the rocket. Pegasus, built by privately held Orbital Sciences Corp. of Fairfax, Va., is the first U.S. rocket launcher developed since the space shuttle. The crewless rocket is dropped from the wing of a B-52 over the Pacific Ocean and is supposed to vault into space carrying two small, government-owned satellites.
