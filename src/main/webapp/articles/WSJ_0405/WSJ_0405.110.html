Two Big Securities Firms Raised Top Salaries in '89

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/05/90

NEW YORK -- There is one place you won't find signs of Wall Street's recession: in the executive suites of some big securities firms. Three top executives at Morgan Stanley Group Inc. received 24% pay increases with salaries exceeding $5 million last year, the firm's proxy material shows. Chairman S. Parker Gilbert, President Richard B. Fisher and Vice Chairman Robert F. Greenhill all received cash compensation of $5.5 million, up from $4.4 million in 1988.

Managing directors Lewis W. Bernard and John J. Mack also received 16% pay raises in 1989, to $3.7 million, the proxy material shows.

At PaineWebber Group Inc., Chairman Donald B. Marron received a 64% pay raise, to $2.1 million in 1989. Donald E. Nickelson -- PaineWebber president until his retirement in January -- and Paul B. Guenther, presidents of an operating division, both received $1.1 million last year, raises of 39% and 72%, respectively.

Morgan and PaineWebber declined comment.

These two concerns thrived last year as other investment banks on Wall Street wilted. Morgan, with a 12% rise in earnings, posted net income of $443 million last year -- the second highest on Wall Street after Salomon Inc. PaineWebber's 1989 net income surged 23%, to $52 million.
