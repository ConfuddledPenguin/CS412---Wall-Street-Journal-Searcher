International: Philips, Olivetti Confirm Discussions on Cooperation

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/05/90

Spokesmen for NV Philips and Ing. C. Olivetti & Co. confirmed rumors that the two electronics companies are discussing possibile cooperation, but they gave few details. A spokesman for Philips in Eindhoven, Netherlands, called the discussions preliminary and noted that his company has contacts with many other potential partners as well. He refused to say which operations were under discussion.

A spokeswoman for Olivetti, based in Ivrea, Italy, said the talks involved "printers and electronic components." She said the discussions were part of "a stage of diffused negotiations with a number of European companies for other possibilities of collaboration." Olivetti added that no exchanges of shares between the companies were foreseen.

Some analysts said Philips and Olivetti might be discussing cooperation in small computer systems. Philips has long said it was looking at possible partners for its computer operations, and Chairman Cornelis van der Klugt hinted last month that the company might have an announcement on the matter soon.

Philips reported in March that the computer operations had heavy losses in 1989, but it didn't quantify them.
