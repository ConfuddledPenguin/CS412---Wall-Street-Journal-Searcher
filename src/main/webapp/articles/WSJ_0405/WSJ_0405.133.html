Business Brief -- Cie Generale d'Electricite: French Holding Company's Earnings Surged in 1989

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/05/90

Cie. Generale d'Electricite (CGE) said profit after payments to minority interests surged to 4.94 billion francs ($865 million) in 1989 from 2.16 billion francs ($378 million) in the previous year, as the French holding company pursued a variety of acquisitions and alliances to expand its business internationally. Pierre Suard, CGE chief executive officer, said at a news conference that 1989 was a "very prosperous and rich year" that saw the electricity group's consolidated revenue rise to 143.9 billion francs from 128 billion in 1988.

Mr. Suard said the CGE executive board has proposed to boost the per-share dividend to 11 francs from the nine francs a share paid out in 1988.

Mr. Suard also announced that the company plans to issue a five billion-franc convertible bond issue to help finance recent acquisitions and to prepare CGE for other "opportunities" that appear in the future.
