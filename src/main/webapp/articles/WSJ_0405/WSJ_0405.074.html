Italy Appears Ready To End Its Restraints On Capital Movement

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/05/90

ROME -- Italy is ready to lift its last remaining restrictions on capital flows by the end of this month, monetary officials here said. The action would allow Italian residents to open savings and checking accounts abroad and hold foreign-currency accounts in Italy.

Under the European Community deadline for the first stage of monetary union, member states must lift all capital-flow restrictions by July 1, and Italy is the last of the major countries to do so. Monetary officials here say the government now is prepared to act in the next few weeks -- in advance of May 6 local elections -- rather than wait for the EC deadline.

"The decision to remove remaining restrictions on capital movements is imminent," Carlo Ciampi, the Bank of Italy governor, said in a speech to businessmen. The action, he said, would conclude "the `long march' of our economy toward the objective of full freedom of capital without sacrificing exchange-rate stability."

Citing a strong, stable lira and high domestic interest rates, officials expressed confidence that the end of controls wouldn't lead to a big outflow of savings, as once was feared.

Nonetheless, monetary and other officials generally agree that a reduction in the 30% withholding tax on bank interest earnings will be necessary to minimize the transfer of savings to foreign-currency accounts. Italy's central bank wants to cut that tax rate to 20%; others in government want a more modest initial cut -- to 25%.
