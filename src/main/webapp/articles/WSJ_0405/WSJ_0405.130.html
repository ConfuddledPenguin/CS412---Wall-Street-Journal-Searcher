Delta Air Lines Says Profit Fell in Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/05/90

ATLANTA -- Delta Air Lines said it estimates its net income for the March quarter was far lower than last year's results, although it still will report a profit. The airline said its profit margin was being squeezed by a combination of softer demand by leisure travelers and higher fuel prices. In addition, last year's quarter showed particularly high profits because Delta's main competitor in its Atlanta hub, Eastern Airlines, had been hit by a strike and practically grounded.

Thomas J. Roeck, Delta's senior vice president of finance and chief financial officer, said in a statement that while fuel prices have declined somewhat since last year, they will still drive up fuel expenses for the first three months by $85 million to $90 million.

Separately, Delta said its March traffic fell 4.7% to 5.05 billion revenue passenger miles from 5.3 billion miles last year. A revenue passenger mile is one paying passenger flown one mile. Load factor, or the percentage of seats filled, also dropped sharply for the month to 61.5% from 68.7% a year ago.

For the year to date, Delta said it flew 13.61 billion revenue passenger miles, up slightly from last year's 13.57 billion miles. Load factor for the first three months was 57.5%, down from 61% in 1989.
