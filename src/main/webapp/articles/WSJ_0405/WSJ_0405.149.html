First Federal Savings To Buy Five Branches Of CrossLand Savings

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/05/90

NEW YORK -- CrossLand Savings agreed to sell its five Utah retail branches, accounting for a total $75 million of deposits, to First Federal Savings Bank, Salt Lake City, for cash. Terms weren't disclosed.

CrossLand's retail banking operation has 115 offices in eight states with a total $8.7 billion of deposits. The Brooklyn thrift said the sale of the Utah deposits was a move to focus on areas where it has a larger market share. It will maintain its CrossLand Mortgage Corp. subsidiary in Salt Lake City.

Conditions of the branch sale include the signing of a definitive agreement and receipt of clearance by regulators, and regulators may scrutinize CrossLand's proposed divestiture. As is the case with many thrifts, CrossLand is out of compliance with certain capital standards, and regulators often are wary when such thrifts sell assets.

A spokesman for CrossLand said he believed the branches are profitable, but "the growth potential for us hasn't been tremendous in that market." The thrift anticipates no regulatory objections to the sale, he added.
