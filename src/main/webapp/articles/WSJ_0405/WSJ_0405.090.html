Penny-Stock Fraud Is the Target of Bill Introduced in House

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/05/90

WASHINGTON -- The chairman of the House finance subcommittee introduced a bill to crack down on penny-stock fraud and better regulate small stocks. The legislation, offered by Rep. Edward Markey (D., Mass.), would broaden the power of the Securities and Exchange Commission to bar "crooked promoters and consultants from the securities business."

The bill also would force all penny stocks to be traded through an automated quotation system in an effort to bolster surveillance and information flow. The system would also help investors get more accurate pricing of the stocks.

The bill received immediate support from Rep. John Dingell (D., Mich.), chairman of the Energy and Commerce Committee, who joined as a co-sponsor and called penny-stock fraud "a national embarrassment."

Penny-stock fraud has emerged as a critical enforcement problem for the SEC, which has set up a task force to deal with the issue. By one estimate, such fraud bilks investors of $2 billion annually, though regulators haven't been able to certify that figure.
