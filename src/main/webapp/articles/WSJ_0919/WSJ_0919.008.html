Canadian Trade Surplus Narrows

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/19/90

OTTAWA -- Canada's merchandise trade surplus narrowed to 1.2 billion Canadian dollars (US$1 billion) in July, partly because of an increase in imports of autos and auto parts, Statistics Canada, a government agency, said. In June the surplus totaled C$1.81 billion.

The U.S. took 77% of the total July exports and supplied 70% of Canada's imports.
