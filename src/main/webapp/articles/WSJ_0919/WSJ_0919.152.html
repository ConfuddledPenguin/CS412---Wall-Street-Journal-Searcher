Business Brief -- Communications Satellite Corp.: Rival Expected to Appeal Dismissal of Antitrust Suit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/19/90

Pan American Satellite is expected to appeal a federal court decision to dismiss the company's $1.5 billion antitrust suit against Communications Satellite Corp. The decision in federal court in Manhattan was on the ground that Comsat has immunity from such lawsuits. PanAmSat, a satellite operator based in Greenwich, Conn., filed the suit in July 1989, alleging attempts by Comsat to limit competition in the market for satellite transmissions. The court said that agreements between the federal government and International Telecommunications Satellite Organization (Intelsat) as well as the International Organizations Immunities Act gave Comsat, as the U.S. signatory to Intelsat, immunity from antitrust suits.

"My sense is that we will appeal," said Frederick Landman, president of PanAmSat. He said that company lawyers were examining the Friday decision. "We think the court gave an incorrect reading" of the antitrust immunity issue, Mr. Landman said.

Comsat, based in Washington, D.C., welcomed the decision, and reiterated its view that the lawsuit was groundless and ironic because Comsat had urged other Intelsat members to approve PanAmSat's operations.
