Blackstone Group, Financiere Indosuez To Form Merger Unit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/19/90

NEW YORK -- Blackstone Group, the investment banking boutique, joined forces with a French investment bank, Financiere Indosuez, to advise on mergers between companies in the U.S., France and Belgium. Blackstone and the French concern said each would make an equity investment of undisclosed size in the other. The new venture, Blackstone Indosuez, is aimed at the burgeoning area of cross-border mergers in Europe.

Financiere Indosuez has already invested $25 million in Blackstone's merchant banking fund, and yesterday Blackstone said it also plans, along with some of its existing investors, to invest up to $50 million in a merchant banking fund set up by Compagnie de Suez, the French parent of Financiere Indosuez.

The Suez group, an industrial and financial holding company, controls Societe Generale de Belgique, based in Brussels, Belgium.

Roger Altman, vice chairman of Blackstone, said the two firms have been working together informally for two years. "One of the reasons we're confident that this joint venture will be successful is that we lived together for a couple of years before getting married," Mr. Altman said. Blackstone already has alliances with other firms in Japan, Britain, and Canada.

As part of the alliance, four Financiere Indosuez employees will move from their existing offices here to Blackstone's office. In turn, three Blackstone employees here will transfer to Financiere Indosuez in Paris.
