Business Brief -- H.H. Robertson Co.: Investor Group Buys 18.8%, Will Fight Against Merger

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/19/90

A New York investor group led by Douglas A. Kass boosted its stake in H.H. Robertson Co. to 18.8% and said it will launch a proxy battle to fight management's efforts to merge with another construction firm. In a filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission the group said that it will solicit proxies for an upcoming special shareholder's meeting. At the meeting, holders will vote on the company's proposed $150 million cash and stock buy-out of Ceco Industries, Inc., of Oakbrook Terrace, Ill.

Mr. Kass couldn't be reached for comment.

After the buy-out, Robertson's shares outstanding would double between 13.5 million and 14 million. It would give 25% of the stock to Ceco and another 25% to Swiss investment company Frontera S.A. which agreed to invest $40 million to help fund the cash portion of the buy-out in exchange for 25%, or between 3.3 million and 4 million shares of Roberston.

Analysts now believe the Kass group is trying to stave off the merger to generate buyer interest in Robertson.

First City Financial Corp., a concern controlled by the Belzberg family with a 25% stake in Robertson, agreed to support the buy-out.

In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange, Robertson closed unchanged at $8.
