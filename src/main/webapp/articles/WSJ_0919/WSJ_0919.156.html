Gasoline Price Declines By 0.2 Cent, Survey Finds

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/19/90

HOUSTON -- The average price of regular unleaded gasoline at self-service pumps is $1.292 a gallon, down two-tenths of a cent from a week earlier, the American Automobile Association said yesterday after a nationwide spot check of stations. The AAA's Fuel Gauge Report said the average price is 21.7 cents a gallon higher than it was Aug. 1, the day before Iraq invaded Kuwait, leading to a worldwide embargo against oil from Iraq and from occupied Kuwait. The AAA, however, said supplies of gasoline remain adequate through the U.S.

Prices retreated slightly in the Midwest, Southeast and West in the latest report, while small increases occurred in New England and in the Middle Atlantic and Great Lakes states. Prices were unchanged in the Southwest.
