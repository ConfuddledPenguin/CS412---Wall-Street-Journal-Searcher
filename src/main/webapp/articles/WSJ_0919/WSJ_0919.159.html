Business Brief -- Electronic Data Technologies: Firm Expects to Post Loss In Fiscal Fourth Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/19/90

Electronic Data Technologies said it will post a fiscal fourth-quarter loss and announced executive changes and work force reductions in the wake of a sharp drop in sales. The company, which makes computerized systems for the gambling industry, said the loss reflects increases in reserves for accounts receivable and inventory, severance costs related to planned layoffs, and write-downs of certain products.

The loss will be "fairly large but it's too early to say how big" said Diane Van Son, director of finance. The quarter and year end Sept. 30. In the year-earlier quarter, the company had net income of $564,000, or 15 cents a share.

Ms. Van Son said the company began to experience a sharp decline in orders from its casino customers about four months ago.

Electronic Data Technologies said its chief financial officer, David Brogan, resigned two weeks ago "to pursue other interests." Most of his duties have been assumed by William Vargas, corporate controller.

Ms. Van Son said the company plans to pare its work force by about 20%. The company now has about 300 employees in Las Vegas and Reno, Nev.
