International Brief -- Societe Generale de Belgique: Group Expects Weak Profit As Units Report Earnings

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/19/90

Societe Generale de Belgique, Belgium's largest holding company, is facing a difficult period as a string of units are expected to report disappointing first-half earnings. The Belgium holding company's recently restructured minings and metals unit, Acec-Union Miniere, reported first-half net income of 3.7 billion Belgian francs ($116.1 million). Analysts estimate that a year-earlier figure would have been about 6.3 billion Belgian francs.

Acec-UM said revenue remained at about the same level as a year ago.

In 1989 Acec-UM accounted for 57% of SGB operating profit. SGB's other main units are also expected to report lower first-half profits, pulling down the group's total earnings, which will be released Oct. 3.

Some analysts, however, are optimistic about SGB's future. "Their long-term future isn't too bad," said Guy Lerminiaux, of Deeway Servais & Cie, a Belgian brokerage firm. "They've got some interesting projects, and I think their management is doing a good job."
