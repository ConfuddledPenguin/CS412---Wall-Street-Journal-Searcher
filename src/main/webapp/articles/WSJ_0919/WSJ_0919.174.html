Chairman Sikes Says FCC Will Mitigate Syndication Rules

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/19/90

NEW YORK -- Federal Communications Commission Chairman Alfred C. Sikes delivered his most optimistic assessment yet that the FCC is likely to ease federal rules barring the three television networks from the rerun-sales business. Mr. Sikes told a broadcast industry luncheon here that by early next year, the FCC will likely rule on how to soften the so-called Financial Interest and Syndication Rules. The regulations block the networks from selling series reruns to local stations, forbid them from sharing in the profits from reruns sold by other companies and prohibit the networks from taking partial ownership stakes in programming.

"There is an emerging consensus, I believe, in Washington that these rules do need some adjusting," the FCC chairman said to the International Radio and Television Society.

Mr. Sikes said the Federal Communications Commission will hold a major hearing with all parties in the rerun-rules dispute some time before the end of this year, with a final ruling expected in the first quarter of 1991.

If the rerun rules ultimately are lifted, the entire U.S. entertainment business could undergo a realignment as television networks would be freed to acquire, or merge, with the studios that supply most of their prime time series.

For years the ABC, NBC and CBS networks have fought to lift the rules, which were first imposed in 1970 when the networks controlled over 95% of the viewing audience. Their share now is 65%, with cable and independent stations making steady gains.

The major opposition in the rerun battle has come from Hollywood's major studios, which largely control the $3 billion-a-year market for reruns and other syndicated programming.
