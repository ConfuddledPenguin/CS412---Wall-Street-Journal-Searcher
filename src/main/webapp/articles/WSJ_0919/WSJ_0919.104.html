Two Air Canada Pilots Cited Under Smoking Law

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/19/90

OTTAWA -- Two Air Canada pilots were charged with illegally smoking on separate Canadian domestic flights. The charges were the first to be filed under a year-old law that prohibits smoking in federally regulated workplaces, including government buildings, banks and airlines. The charges were filed following complaints by an Air Canada employee.

If convicted, the pilots, Jack Haycock and Eppie Mulder, could be fined up to 50 Canadian dollars (US$43). An Air Canada spokesman said the airline will decide whether to discipline the pilots after the court's ruling.

The Canadian law bans smoking on all domestic flights, most North American flights, and international flights under six hours. The ban is to be extended to all overseas flights in 1993.

Air Canada, one of Canada's largest airlines, voluntarily extended the ban to cover its longer overseas flights starting Oct. 1. The airline estimated that 99.8% of its flights will be smoke-free after the smoking ban is extended next month. The ban won't apply to its London-Bombay-Singapore route, where smoking remains popular.
