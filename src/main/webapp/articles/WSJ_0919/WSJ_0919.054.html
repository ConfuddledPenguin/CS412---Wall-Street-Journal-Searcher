Certificates of Deposit Showed Mixed Yields In the Latest Week

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/19/90

NEW YORK -- Yields on certificates of deposit at major banks and brokerage firms were mixed in the week ended yesterday. Average yields on small-denomination, or "consumer," CDs sold directly by banks were virtually unchanged. The average yield on a six-month CD eased to 7.37% from 7.38%, according to an 18-bank national survey by Banxquote Money Markets, a New York information service. The average yield on a five-year consumer CD rose to 7.91% from 7.90%.

Also mixed were average yields on small-denomination CDs sold by leading broker dealers. The average yield on a three-month broker-sold CD fell to 7.74% from 7.87%, according to Banxquote. The average yield for a two-year broker-sold CD rose to 8.24% from 8.13%.

Meanwhile, average yields for large-denomination CDs, which typically require deposits of $90,000 or more, were flat to up slightly. The average yield on a two-year jumbo CD remained at 7.92%, while the average for a five-year maturity rose to 8.27% from 8.25%.
