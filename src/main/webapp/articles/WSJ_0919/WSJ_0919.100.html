REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: Whites Get Preference

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/19/90

We have come across a nice little case study in the difference between the way the public sector and private sector might approach a similar problem. The postmaster in Kansas City last month recommended that local patrons forgo red and green envelopes with their Christmas cards this year. He says the automatic-sorting machines that "read" addresses can't handle color. But the Postal Service offered to print address labels for free, because it turns out the machines can't read poor handwriting, either. You can't blame the U.S. Mail for trying to speed things up but you have to think that a private operator would find ways to exploit the festive spirit rather than dampen it. The card makers are happy enough to oblige the jolly buyer of their wares.
