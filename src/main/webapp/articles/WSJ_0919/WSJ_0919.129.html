Stotler Settles SEC Charges On Government Securities

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/19/90

WASHINGTON -- The Securities and Exchange Commission said that Stotler & Co., a now-closed Chicago futures firm, settled SEC charges of securities law violations in connection with its government-securities business. Without admitting or denying any wrongdoing, Stotler agreed to have its registration as a government-securities brokerage firm revoked. The charges stem from Aug. 21, when the SEC and Commodity Futures Trading Commission brought action against the firm. Stotler settled the CFTC charges but hadn't resolved the SEC matter.

Stotler is the futures-brokerage arm of Stotler Group Inc., which was partly owned by former Chicago Board of Trade Chairman Karsten Mahlmann. Mr. Mahlmann resigned his CBOT post after another Stotler unit was charged by the CFTC with making loans with customer funds from commodity pools.

The SEC alleged that Stotler & Co. failed to maintain sufficient capital, failed to file documents and kept outdated and improper records of capital levels and of positions in the government-securities markets. The complaint also charged that Stotler didn't notify the SEC that its net capital was below the required minimum.

Stotler Group filed a Chapter 7 bankruptcy petition in federal bankruptcy court late last month.

In Chicago, attorneys for Stotler & Co. weren't available for comment.
