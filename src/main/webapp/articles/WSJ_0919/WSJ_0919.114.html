New Jersey Power Outage Affects Telerate Service

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/19/90

NEW YORK -- Telerate Inc.'s financial information service was stopped for more than two hours yesterday due to a power outage at its main computer operation in Jersey City, N.J. Telerate service was knocked out at around 7:15 a.m. EDT because of an explosion at a Public Service Electric & Gas Co. facility that serves the Telerate building in Jersey City, according to a spokesman for Dow Jones & Co., which owns Telerate.

The outage affected Telerate's news and market commentary subscribers, who couldn't access the information services. Telerate's foreign exchange trading services weren't affected.

The Dow Jones spokesman, who said Telerate's services have returned to normal, said he didn't expect any further complications.

Telerate provides live quotes of U.S. government securities and other debt instruments, futures prices and additional financial data, as well as other news and information.
