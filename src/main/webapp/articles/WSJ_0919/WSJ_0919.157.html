Business Brief -- DWG Corp.: Proposed Legal Settlement With Granada Is Advanced

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/19/90

A federal judge in Cleveland preliminarily approved a proposed legal settlement between DWG Corp., Miami, and Granada Investments Inc., New York. U.S. District Court Judge Thomas D. Lambros said he will consider any shareholder objections to the settlement at a fairness hearing on Nov. 14. As part of the settlement, three independent, court-appointed directors will serve on DWG's board for five years, heading the audit and compensation committees.

DWG and Granada agreed on Sept. 4 to end their five-month battle. Granada sued DWG's board in April 1989, charging that DWG directors had shirked their fiduciary responsibility by not seriously considering Granada's $22-a-share, or $572 million, offer for DWG in March 1989. The suit also attacked the high salary and other perquisites of DWG Chairman Victor Posner, the Miami financier. DWG owns enterprises such as Arby's Inc. and Royal Crown Cos., and is the crown jewel of Mr. Posner's empire. DWG countersued, claiming Granada's bid wasn't a serious offer, rather, a scheme intended to manipulate the market for DWG shares and allow Granada to benefit from the stock's price increase.
