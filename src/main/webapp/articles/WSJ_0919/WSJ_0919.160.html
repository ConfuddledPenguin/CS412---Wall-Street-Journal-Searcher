Who's News: Pickens to Resign as Head Of Shareholders Group

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/19/90

WASHINGTON -- T. Boone Pickens is stepping down as chairman of United Shareholders Association, the shareholder-rights advocacy group he founded four years ago. Mr. Pickens, chairman of Mesa Petroleum L.P., said that the organization needs "new blood to get to the next plateau" and emphasized that he will remain committed to USA. Mr. Pickens will be succeeded as USA chairman Oct. 1 by Charles Cox, a former member of the Securities and Exchange Commission, who is senior vice president of Lexecon Inc., a Chicago economic consulting firm. Mr. Cox left the SEC last year, after six years as a commissioner and a prior stint as chief economist.

USA, which claims 64,000 members in 38 chapters, has been active in soliciting SEC proxy action to enhance shareholder rights. The group has fought against management bonuses like "golden parachutes," and against what it called "excessive management entrenchment techniques."
