Technology Brief -- National Semiconductor Corp.: Company to Supply Conner With Disk-Drive Circuits

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/19/90

National Semiconductor Corp. said it signed a long-term agreement to supply Conner Peripherals in San Jose, Calif., with integrated circuits for its disk-drive products. The company declined to disclose the value. The agreement covers a range of devices from mixed analog and digital disk-drive circuits to digital-to-analog converters, Applied System Integrated Circuits and memory devices. It formalizes a relationship between the two companies dating back to Conner Peripherals' original product development efforts, a spokeswoman for National Semiconductor, based in Santa Clara, Calif., said.

"There will now be at least one or more National Semiconductor products in all Conner disk drives," the spokeswoman said.

National Semiconductor makes and markets semiconductor and board-level connnectivity products. Conner Peripherals makes and markets disk drives for workstations and portable and desktop computers.
