Steel Imports Grew By 8.5% During July

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/19/90

WASHINGTON -- Steel imports rose 8.5% in July, the highest tonnage level since October 1989. July imports were 1,591,000 tons, up from 1,466,000 tons in June, according to the American Iron and Steel Institute's compilation of Commerce Department figures.

Imports for the first seven months of 1990 were 9,488,000 tons, down almost 6.6% from 10,157,000 tons in the year-earlier period.

The July import level is 19.5% of consumption, up from 17% in June and 19.3% in July 1989.

However, the American Institute for International Steel Inc. noted that steel imports for October 1989 through July averaged 17.2%-which is below the 19.1% voluntary limit set by the government in October.

At the same time imports rose, exports declined 5% in July, to 302,000 net tons from 318,000 net tons in June. Exports in July 1989 were 303,000 net tons.
