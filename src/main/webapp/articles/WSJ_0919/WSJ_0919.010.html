Brazil's Trade Surplus Shrinks

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/19/90

RIO DE JANEIRO -- Rising oil imports caused Brazil's trade surplus to shrink to $1.03 billion in August, down 29% from a year ago, the government's Foreign Trade Department said. Exports in August were practically unchanged from July but down 9% from a year earlier. Imports rose 23% from the previous month and were 5% higher than in August 1989.
