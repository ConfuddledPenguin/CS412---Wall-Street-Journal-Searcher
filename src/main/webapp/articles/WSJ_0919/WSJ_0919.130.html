Ford Canada Raises Offer

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/19/90

TORONTO -- The Canadian subsidiary of Ford Motor Co. presented a new contract proposal offering more money in a bid to end a four-day-old strike by about 13,000 unionized employees. While calling the company's latest package a "serious" offer, Robert White, president of the Canadian Auto Workers union, said the proposal still fell short of many of the the union's minimum demands in important areas such as wages, additional holiday time, job security and fringe benefits.

The union leader appeared to back away from his earlier threats to abandon the bargaining table, and he said negotiations would continue in an effort to reach a contract settlement.

Ford has "obviously rejected a number of our bottom-line proposals. We'll have to see if we can do anything about that in the next couple of days," Mr. White said.

Donald McKenzie, Ford Canada's chief negotiator, said the company's latest offer was a "significant improvement" over its first proposal made last week, which was swiftly rejected by the union. The CAW has made clear it didn't dispute the "direction" of the offer, but rather the amount of money put on the table.

"My sense is {the new offer} will move things forward rapidly," Mr. McKenzie said. He wouldn't provide details of the latest package.

The strike by Ford Canada employees has shut nine of Ford's plants in Canada and threatened to hobble operations at some key Ford plants in the U.S.
