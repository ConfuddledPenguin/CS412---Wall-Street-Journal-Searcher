Marketing Brief -- Bertelsmann AG: Company Sells 49% Stake Of Its Italian Book Club

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/19/90

Bertelsmann AG said it sold 49% of its book club in Italy to publishers Rizzoli-Corriere della Sera Editori. Terms of the transaction weren't disclosed. The West German publishing company said it could expand the market share for Euroclubs Italia through Rizzoli's influence in Italy.

Bertelsmann, one of the world's largest operators of book clubs, said its Euroclubs Italia book club had 700,000 members in 1989 with sales of 75 million German marks ($48.5 million). Walter Gerstgrasser, Bertelsmann managing board member, said the company will retain 51% of the book club as well as its current management.

Last year, Rizzoli had the equivalent of 3 billion marks in sales and controls about one-fourth of the Italian book market, according to Bertelsmann.

A spokesman for Bertelsmann said the company has had "good experience" with partnerships established with other book clubs in France and Britain.
