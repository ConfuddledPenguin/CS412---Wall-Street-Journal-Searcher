Business Brief -- Ralston Purina Co.: Charge in Fourth Quarter Of $10 Million Is Expected

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/19/90

Ralston Purina Co. said it expects to report a fourth-quarter pretax charge of about $10 million, or about 10 cents a share, from the closing of a pet food plant later this year. Ralston, based in St. Louis, said yesterday it expects to close its Mechanicsburg, Pa., plant in "late November or early December," depending on the company's production schedule.

The company said the closure affects about 160 employees, 100 of whom are paid on an hourly basis and 60 of whom are salaried. The company said it will provide those employees with severance and outplacement benefits.

Ralston called the closing an "economic necessity." The company said that it is facing "more production capacity than needed," citing increased plant efficiency.

Ralston said production from the Mechanicsburg plant, which makes dry cat and dog food, will shift to other pet food plants. The company said it has seven other pet food plants in the U.S.
