Business Brief -- Apple Bancorp Inc.: Stahl Assails Establishment Of Two Expense Reserves

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/19/90

Stanley Stahl agreed to Apple Bancorp Inc.'s plan under which his tender offer would go forward, but the real estate investor demanded that the New York bank holding company rescind two expense reserves it set up last week. The parent of Apple Bank for Savings said Monday it would drop its opposition to Mr. Stahl's $38-a-share tender offer if the investor buys all shares tendered to him and holds a majority of Apple's stock before its annual meeting Oct. 10.

But Mr. Stahl noted that on Sept. 14, the bank set up a $2 million trust to pay expenses that its officers and directors might incur in connection with litigation between the bank and the investor. He said the establishment of that and another $1.5 million trust violates a condition of his tender offer.

Apple said Mr. Stahl's "unwillingness to unambiguously commit to complete his tender offer . . . demonstrates the risk shareholders face if {his} nominees for the board should take control" of Apple through the proxy contest he is waging.

Mr. Stahl's offer values Apple's nearly 4.6 million shares outstanding at a total of $175 million. He has said he controls about 76% of Apple, including his own stake plus shares tendered under his offer.
