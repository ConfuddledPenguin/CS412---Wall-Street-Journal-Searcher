MCI to Buy OverSeas Telecommunications In Global Expansion

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/19/90

WASHINGTON -- MCI Communications Corp. said it agreed to purchase OverSeas Telecommunications Inc., an international satellite and fiber-optic concern. Terms weren't disclosed. The move is part of MCI's continuing effort to boost its international voice, data and video communications business. Since 1982, MCI has sought to expand its global reach primarily through acquisitions.

The long-distance telecommunications concern bought Western Union International Inc. in 1982; RCA Global Communications Inc., a high-speed data and telex concern, in 1987; and a 25% stake in Infonet Services Corp., an international dataswitching network and computing-services concern, last January.

Privately held OTI, based in Alexandria, Va., provides digital communications services and operates 24 earth stations world-wide, and thus will give MCI a larger presence in 27 countries. OTI specializes in small customer premises satellite services between the U.S. and Latin America. It also offers satellite earth-station project management, overseas private-line channels and international video services.

Annual revenue for OTI wasn't available.

Robert B. Morris III, a telecommunications analyst with Goldman, Sachs & Co., said the acquisition is important to MCI because the company holds such a small share of the global marketplace and is "virtually unknown" outside the U.S. "By purchasing other entities, MCI will raise its global presence and expand its market," he said.

MCI's current share of the international telephone market ranges between 12% and 13%, while American Telephone & Telegraph Co. dominates with an estimated 80% to 85% share.

OTI will give MCI a stronger foothold in such markets as West Germany, France, Spain, Singapore, Japan, Australia, Mexico, Argentina and Brazil.
