Corroon Rejects Aon's $840 Million Bid In Favor of a Merger With Willis Faber

  • Author: Beatrice E. Garcia and Richard Gibson
  • Date: 09/19/90

Corroon & Black Corp. spurned a $40-a-share, or $840 million, acquisition proposal from Aon Corp. and said it will proceed with plans to merge with Willis Faber PLC of London in a stock swap currently valued at $30.01 a share, or $615.2 million. But Aon, saying it found Corroon's rejection "incredible," reiterated its offer and said its all-cash bid "remains on the table and is not being withdrawn." Initially, the Chicago-based insurer had said it wouldn't proceed if Corroon, an insurance-brokerage concern, opposed the bid.

New York-based Corroon, which late yesterday unanimously rejected Aon's bid, said it favored the international presence it would gain by merging with the British insurance broker. Richard Miller, Corroon's chairman and president, said the planned merger with Willis Faber "has become the centerpiece of our longstanding plans to internationalize our operations. . . ."

In a statement issued last night, Aon Chairman Patrick G. Ryan said, "We find it incredible that they came to this conclusion without even talking to us or to our investment bankers about our proposal. They have never heard our domestic and international strategy for a combined Corroon/Aon which, like our price, is clearly superior to a combined Willis/Corroon."

Mr. Ryan said, "Our proposal was superior when made and has become even more superior due to a fall in the Willis Faber stock price." Indeed, since it launched its bid in June, Willis shares have declined, while Corroon's jumped last week on news of Aon's bid.

Yesterday, Willis Faber fell two pence to 201 pence in London trading. When Willis Faber made its merger proposal to Corroon June 4, the offer was valued at $34.72 a share, or $712.3 million, based on Willis Faber's closing price of 265 pence.

Corroon shares closed at $35.50, down 37.5 cents, in New York Stock Exchange composite trading. Aon rose 25 cents to $32.25.

Takeover-stock traders said Corroon's stock is likely to fall $2 a share or more today because of the bid rejection, which was announced after the market closed yesterday.

With Aon pressing on with its bid for Corroon, the fight could hinge on legal precedents set in last year's battle for Time Inc. In the Time case, the Delaware Supreme Court ruled that Time could reject an all-cash hostile bid from Paramount Communications and proceed with plans to merge with Warner Communications Inc. in a stock swap. Under the terms of the stock swap, former Time shareholders would own about 40% of the merged company's shares.

The Delaware courts ruled that such a transaction didn't constitute a change in control of Time and therefore Time's board wasn't required to consider other bids, such as Paramount's.

Corroon, like Time, is incorporated in Delaware. Corroon shareholders will own 40% of the merged companies and the rest will be owned by Willis Faber shareholders. Some takeover experts believe the Delaware courts are likely to allow Corroon to reject any Aon offer and complete the proposed merger with Willis Faber.
