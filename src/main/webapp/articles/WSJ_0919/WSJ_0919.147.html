Former CIA Agent Clines Convicted of 4 Tax Charges

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/19/90

BALTIMORE -- A former CIA agent who procured arms as part of the secret Iran-Contra network supporting the Nicaraguan rebels was convicted of four tax charges. Thomas Clines was found guilty yesterday by a jury in federal court in Baltimore of making false statements on tax returns to hide his arms-dealing profits in the Iran-Contra affair. The jury deliberated for about three hours before convicting Mr. Clines on all four counts in the indictment.

Mr. Clines, whom prosecutors had portrayed as a central figure in the Contra arms deals, is scheduled to be sentenced Oct. 31. He faces a maximum prison term of 16 years and as much as $1 million in fines.

His lawyer attempted unsuccessfully during the 10-day trial to portray her client's tax return misstatements and omissions as minor and at worst, negligent, not criminal.
