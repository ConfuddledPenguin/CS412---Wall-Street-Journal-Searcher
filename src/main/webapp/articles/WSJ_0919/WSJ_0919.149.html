Business Brief -- NewMil Bancorp: Loan-Loss Reserves Boosted, Quarterly Payout Suspended

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/19/90

Anticipating increases in nonperforming loans and securities losses, NewMil Bancorp of New Milford, Conn., parent of New Milford Savings Bank, said its board voted an addition of about $700,000 to the loan-loss reserve, equivalent to its expected earnings for its first quarter ending Sept. 30. The board also suspended the regular quarterly dividend of 15 cents a share for "the foreseeable future."

The move will increase NewMil Bancorp's total loan-loss reserve to $2 million. New Milford Savings Bank operates 12 banking offices in northwestern Connecticut. NewMil Bancorp had fiscal 1990 earnings of $3.8 million.

The company said its decision was in response to a regulatory opinion that its reserve was inadequate against nonperforming loans, which include foreclosed loans and loans for which interest payments are 90 days past due. In addition, substantial losses are expected as the value of the company's stock portfolio depreciates.
