General Development Reaches New Accord With Government

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/19/90

MIAMI -- General Development Corp. said it reached a new plea-bargain agreement with the government involving an alleged conspiracy to defraud thousands of home buyers. The new agreement between the beleaguered real estate developer and U.S. Attorney Dexter Lehtinen calls for General Development to make restitution to home buyers of as much as $160 million over 25 years.

The company's original plea bargain, which was rejected by U.S. District Judge Lenore Nesbitt in July, had called for General Development to make restitution estimated at $80 million to $100 million. Judge Nesbitt said the first agreement "didn't give her enough flexibility to determine an appropriate sentence in light of the scope and severity of the charges."

The new agreement, according to a company spokeswoman, calls for General Development to plead guilty to one count of the original 16-count indictment against the company. That count alleges that the company conspired to violate a federal mail-fraud statute in connection with the sale of homes.

In March, the U.S. attorney unsealed a 16-count indictment against General Development and its financing unit, GDV Financial Corp. The indictment, which culminated a two-year grand jury investigation, alleged that the company had schemed to sell its Florida homes, primarily to out-of-state buyers unfamiliar with market conditions, at grossly inflated prices based on improper appraisals.

Prosecutors said 9,600 to 10,000 home buyers were affected. The company's appraisals were based on what prosecutors say were other overpriced General Development homes -- not properties in the community at large -- and thus exceeded fair-market value, prosecutors said.

General Development, which filed for bankruptcy-law protection in April, said the new agreement will be filed today with the U.S. District Court and the U.S. Bankruptcy Court in Miami.
