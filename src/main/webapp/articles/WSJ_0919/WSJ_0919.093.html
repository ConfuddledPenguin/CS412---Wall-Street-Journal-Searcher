So, You Had Expected Diamonds; Just Read It and Try Not to Weep

  • Author: Virginia Daut
  • Date: 09/19/90

"Paradise Dream" is the torrid tale of Ivan and Anne Marie, who fall in love while searching for an archaeologist lost in a Hawaiian jungle. Their passion blossoms as they overcome obstacles large and small, including a lost corkscrew on page 102 that threatens to ruin their beach picnic until Ivan, charmingly, pushes the cork into the wine bottle with his car keys. At least that's in the "Paradise Dream" written for Ivan and Anne Marie Avetissian of Moorpark, Calif. Mr. Avetissian ordered the $150 deluxe edition of the novel to celebrate the couple's fifth wedding anniversary. The corkscrew scene came from the personal-romance questionnaire he faxed to the author, Evelyn Brown.

Ms. Brown has founded a publishing enterprise on the observation that all happy couples are alike -- almost. Using a computer program to fill in the blanks, Ms. Brown can crank out a 100-page hard-cover novel in an hour. At last count, she had rewritten "Paradise Dream" 1,500 times.

Ms. Brown will cast a couple's names in the leading roles for $45. For another $15 she'll throw in their hobbies, best friends, favorite flowers, first kiss, pets, special songs, and other details. In one version, a guest sits on a glass table during the wedding reception: "The bride, groom and their guests spent the rest of the day pulling glass shards from Jo's derriere."

Ms. Brown created Swan Publishing in 1987 out of a pair of hobbies: computers and steamy novels. She enrolled in a "Writing Romances" course and six months later completed "Our Love," a tale about a newspaper reporter and an allegedly-but-not-really-corrupt developer. Realizing that the romance-fiction market was impossibly crowded, she decided to cut out the middlemen -- agent, publisher and supermarket -- and sell customized books directly by mail.

From her garage in Placentia, Calif., Ms. Brown publishes "Our Love," "Paradise Dream" and her latest, "Lotto Love," in which a couple wins the lottery but then loses the winning ticket. Customers can choose the sensuality level of their text -- from the Candlelit Edition ("like the network version") to the Burning Embers Edition ("the cable version").

Pleased customers have come back for second and third orders, with a different paramour each time. "I had a guy tell me all about his girlfriend, and (how) he loved her and so on," Ms. Brown says. He later requested another novel -- for his wife.
