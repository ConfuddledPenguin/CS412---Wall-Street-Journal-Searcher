Stokely USA Expects Quarter's Net to Fall By Half; Pricing Cited

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/19/90

OCONOMOWOC, Wis. -- Stokely USA Inc. said it expects net income for its second quarter ending Sept. 30 will fall by half, because of lower prices for its canned and frozen vegetables. The company, which processes and markets vegetables, fruits and juices under its own brand and for private-label venders, also said it expects sales to drop about 10%, in part because it opted for a "nonaggressive" sales policy earlier this year.

In the year-ago second quarter, Stokely had earnings of $3.2 million, or 38 cents a share, on sales of $60 million.

The company said prices for its products declined earlier this year, based on "buyer expectations of larger-than-normal supplies being produced." Stokely's own assessment was that poor planting and growing conditions this spring made those projections premature, and so the company didn't cut prices to get contracts from some canning accounts. "We have a number of customers we have to service on a twelve-month basis," said a spokesman. "If we think we're going to have less product available," he said, the company is disinclined to engage in price cutting to get additional contracts.

"The outlook for supply and pricing still remains uncertain," said Chairman Joseph B. Weix. A spokesman said the industry is "not looking at an oversupply situation," citing among other things the "possibility of significant demand increases related to European crop failures and to our military buildup in the Mideast," factors which might boost currently depressed margins. The "disappointing" projected results, Mr. Weix said, thus "are not necessarily indicative of results for the balance of the fiscal year."

In national over-the-counter trading yesterday, Stokely shares fell $3.125, to close at $13.
