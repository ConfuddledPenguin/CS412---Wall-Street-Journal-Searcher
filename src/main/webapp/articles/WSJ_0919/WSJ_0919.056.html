Technology: Xerox to Unveil Workstation And Software

  • Author: Laurence Hooper
  • Date: 09/19/90

Xerox Corp. is to begin its latest bid to become a player in the office computing market today, introducing a computer workstation and a range of software products, industry executives said. The workstation is a departure for Xerox because it will be built by Sun Microsystems Inc. around Sun's widely available Sparc microprocessor, the executives said. Xerox, an office-products company based in Stamford, Conn., has sold only proprietary workstations to run its well-regarded Viewpoint software, used mainly for document-processing. Xerox will also introduce a new version of Viewpoint written for Sun's version of the Unix operating system, an industry standard.

Industry executives said Xerox also will introduce a skein of software packages intended to tie together the various computers and programs used in most offices, in an attempt to give users of computer networks a consistent, easy-to-understand view of their work. Aside from Unix, it will work with DOS and OS/2, the operating systems used by most personal computers.

Xerox's new computing "environment," while still tilted toward document-processing applications, will be presented as a systemwide approach similar to highly publicized products such as International Business Machine Corp.'s OfficeVision or Hewlett-Packard Co.'s New Wave, the executives said.

The company is likely to promote its reputation as the originator of many of the innovations in these packages, and thus the most natural provider of software that exploits them. A Xerox spokeswoman declined to comment on today's announcement, which will be made in San Francisco.

On Oct. 2, Xerox is expected to announce a large multifunction copier-printer that will hook up with the company's new products as well as a host of other hardware and software products. That machine also will have scanning and facsimile capabilities, and is expected to play a large part in Xerox's push into office computing systems.
