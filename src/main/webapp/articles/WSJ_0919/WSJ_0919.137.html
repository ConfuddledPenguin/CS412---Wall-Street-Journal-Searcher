International Brief -- Groupe Strafor S.A.: French Concern to Acquire Tool Maker in Stock Swap

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/19/90

France's Groupe Strafor S.A. plans to acquire Facom S.A. through a stock swap valued at 2.06 billion francs ($397 million). The companies said they will ask their boards next week to approve the exchange of 13 Strafor shares for every 10 Facom shares. Strafor shares closed yesterday at 1,015 francs, down 25 francs from when trading was suspended Monday for the announcement. Facom shares declined 80 francs to 1,290 francs.

The 2.06 billion franc valuation is based on the new value of Strafor shares and the fact that about 1.56 million Facom shares are outstanding.

The move will establish Strafor as the holding company for the group, with control of almost all of Facom's shares. Facom will be set up as a separate operating unit.

The groups said they will have about 15,000 employees when the merger is completed.

Facom, which claims to be the European leader in the hand tools market and third world-wide, had consolidated net profit of 141 million francs in 1989 after payments to minority interests. Revenue totaled 1.89 billion francs.

Strafor, which is active mainly in the areas of office furniture and metals, generated consolidated net profit of 165 million francs after payments to minority interests last year on revenue of 5.4 billion francs.
