Who's News: KPMG Board Elects Jim Butler Chairman Of International Offices

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/19/90

NEW YORK -- KPMG said its board, as expected, elected Jim Butler as chairman of its international practice, succeeding Larry Horner. KPMG, the international accounting and consulting firm formed in the 1987 merger of Peat Marwick International and Klynveld Main Goerdeler, also named Johann Steenmeijer to the new post of chief executive and deputy chairman, effective Nov. 1. Both Mr. Butler, 61 years old and Mr. Steenmeijer, 57, are currently senior partners in the British and Dutch practices, respectively of the firm, which is based in Amsterdam.

Mr. Horner, 56, said in early August that he wouldn't seek re-election as chairman of the international practice or of the U.S. practice, KPMG Peat Marwick. Last week, Jon C. Madonna, 47, was nominated chairman and chief executive of the U.S. practice. He needs majority approval of the U.S. practice's 1,850 partners, who are scheduled to vote Oct. 3 and 4, when Mr. Horner's term in that practice expires. KPMG has a total of 6,100 partners worldwide.

Mr. Horner took over the top positions at Peat Marwick in 1984 and at the international organization in 1988. He was said to be the driving force behind the merger of the firms into the largest of the Big Six accounting and consulting firms. He said in declining to seek another term that the firm needed an executive who could stay longer in the positions, which carry six-year terms. The mandatory retirement age for the positions is 60.
