Dental-Goods Makers Are Asked to Back Up Anti-Plaque Claims

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/19/90

WASHINGTON -- The Food and Drug Administration asked manufacturers of toothpastes and mouthwashes to submit scientific data to justify the anti-plaque claims on their product labels. The FDA didn't accuse the manufacturers of misleading consumers. But it said much research has been done in the area of oral health care since anti-plaque toothpastes and mouthwashes were first reviewed by the agency as non-prescription drugs. In recent years, the products have been heavily promoted as preventing, among other things, the buildup of dental plaque and gum disease.

If the data don't support the various claims, the FDA said it would require the manufacturers to drop them. Anti-plaque toothpastes are touted as containing abrasives that provide mechanical action to remove plaque. Mouthwash manufacturers claim their products work differently, through anti-microbial or chemical activity.

Under the federal Food, Drug and Cosmetic Act, the safety and effectiveness of such product ingredients must be demonstrated, an FDA spokeswoman said.
