Marketing Brief -- Topps Co.: Quarter's Profit Rose 42%; Summer Movie Hits Cited

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/19/90

Riding the popular wave of "Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles," and other entertainment blockbusters, Topps Co. reported jumps in sales and net income for the fiscal second quarter. Topps, a Brooklyn, N.Y., marketer of collectible picture cards featuring popular sports and entertainment figures, said net income jumped 42% to $14.2 million, or 30 cents a share, from $10 million, or 21 cents a share, in the year-earlier quarter. Sales rose 27% to $71.2 million.

Net for fiscal six months rose 52% to $28.2 million, or 60 cents a share, on a sales increase of 33% to $154.4 million.

Chairman Arthur T. Shorin said the results reflected a significant increase in sales of picture cards featuring "Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles," "New Kids on the Block" and "Dick Tracy." He added that second-quarter sales include about $6 million in sports card sets that were shipped earlier this year because of retailer requests.
