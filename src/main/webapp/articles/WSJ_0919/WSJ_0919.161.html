Two Directors Sue New York Bancorp Over Role on Board

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/19/90

NEW YORK -- Two New York Bancorp Inc. directors who won their seats after a proxy fight are charging the rest of the board with refusing to allow them to help supervise the holding company's bank. The charges are in a suit filed in Delaware Chancery Court by the company's largest shareholder, Patrick E. Malloy III, who has a more than a 9% stake, and an attorney, Michael A. McManus, who has less than 1%. Mr. Malloy also is president of Malloy Enterprises Inc., a Sag Harbor, N.Y., real estate and investment firm.

In January, Messrs. Malloy and McManus won a proxy fight for two board seats. They took the seats after settling New York Bancorp's lawsuit challenging the shareholder vote in the fight.

New York Bancorp, the Queens, N.Y., parent of Home Federal Savings Bank, declined to comment on the suit.

The suit charges that the plaintiffs have been unable to help oversee Home Federal because the bank's affairs are dealt with only by directors of the bank, not by directors of the holding company. The lawsuit charges that the holding company's board departed from its routine by not naming Messrs. Malloy and McManus to Home Federal's board.

In national over-the-counter trading yesterday, New York Bancorp shares fell 37.5 cents to $11.375.
