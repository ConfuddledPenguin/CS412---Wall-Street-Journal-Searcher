Credit Ratings: Ratings Are Lowered On $1.1 Billion of Debt Of Oryx Energy Co.

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/19/90

NEW YORK -- Moody's Investors Service Inc. said it downgraded the ratings on about $1.1 billion of debt of Oryx Energy Co., Dallas. The rating agency cited diminished financial flexibility resulting from a previously reported announcement by the company that it would buy back, using debt, about $1 billion of common stock. Moody's noted that the oil and gas exploration concern's financial leverage is already high as a result of a past purchase of $1.1 billion of reserves and properties.

Moody's downgraded Oryx's notes and debentures to Ba-2 from Baa-2; a shelf registration to a preliminary Ba-2 rating from a preliminary rating of Baa-2; convertible subordinated debentures to single-B-1 from Baa-3 and the rating for commercial paper to Not Prime from Prime-2.

In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange, Oryx fell $1.125 to $51.875.
