Credit Ratings: Ford Motor's Debt Reviewed by Moody's; Downgrade Possible

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/19/90

DETROIT -- Moody's Investors Service Inc. said it is reviewing $40 billion of Ford Motor Co.'s debt for possible downgrade. Moody's review was prompted by the softness in the U.S. auto market, said Carol Verscell, the rating agency's assistant vice president. She said Ford remains secure in the high-grade area, and will be reviewed again in the next several weeks.

Ford has lowered its estimate for overall car and truck sales in 1990. Ford had forecast the U.S. market at 14.3 million cars and trucks in 1990, which is down from its original estimate of 14.5 million vehicles. Ford predicted the sluggish sales would continue into the first half of 1991.

Moody's said it also will assess the potential capital requirements of Ford's wholly owned subsidiary, First Nationwide Bank. About $1.7 billion of First Nationwide's debt is being reviewed, Moody's said.

Ford yesterday said Moody's action "isn't warranted," and questioned the rating agency's decision to review its debt in light of "what may prove to be a short term situation." Ford said it will discuss the action with Moody's officials.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading, Ford closed at $34.25, up 50 cents. The announcement came less than 30 minutes before the stock market closed.

Among the ratings now under review by Moody's are the double-A-2 rating for Ford's senior debt and double-A-3 on the company's subordinated debt.

Moody's also is reviewing ratings for First Nationwide Bank, including the single-A-1 rating on its senior debt and the single-A-2 rating on its subordinated debt.
