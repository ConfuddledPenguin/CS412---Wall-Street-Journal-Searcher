International: Mitsubishi Group, Daimler Expected To Set Ventures

  • Author: Marcus W. Brauchli and Jacob M. Schlesinger
  • Date: 09/19/90

TOKYO -- Six months after pledging to form a global business alliance, Japan's Mitsubishi Group and West Germany's Daimler-Benz AG are expected to approve the first joint projects here today. The agenda for the first top-level meeting since March between the huge industrial groups could include small-scale automobile- and part-making ventures in the East bloc, aerospace development, and an aircraft-leasing partnership.

Officially, a Daimler spokesman said, Chairman Edzard Reuter and other officials are in Japan primarily to mark the listing of the company's stock on the Tokyo exchange. "We don't expect any concrete agreements," the spokesman said. And a spokesman for Mitsubishi Corp., the trading company at the heart of the industrial group, said there was "no specified agenda."

The most concrete plans appear to center on cooperation between Mitsubishi Motors Corp. and Daimler-Benz. The two companies already cooperate in some businesses. Mitsubishi officials have said that Daimler-Benz has asked the Japanese to supply equipment and construction for an auto plant in the Soviet Union, and to provide technology for a small-truck plant to be built in East Germany.

One of the most closely watched areas of the emerging alliance is aerospace, where Mitsubishi Heavy Industries Ltd. is trying to build expertise. Both groups have floated ideas for cooperation. Daimler-Benz hinted it might invite Mitsubishi to participate in a planned aircraft-engine manufacturing venture with the Pratt & Whitney subsidiary of United Technologies Corp. And Mitsubishi Heavy Industries said it would seek to cooperate in developing a 75-seat passenger plane.

Those moves worry some American industry and government officials, who fear that the Japanese are beginning to loosen their exclusive relationship with U.S. aerospace companies.

Japanese newspapers also have reported that Mitsubishi and Daimler are considering a tie-up with Ireland's GPA Ltd., the world's largest aircraft-leasing firm. Mitsubishi has a seat on the GPA board, reflecting its 15% equity ownership. But the Mitsubishi spokesman said he "cannot confirm such speculative stories." In Shannon, GPA declined to comment.
