Exchanges to Share Data On Weak Member Firms

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/19/90

The nation's major stock and futures exchanges said they agreed to share regulatory information on troubled member firms. The agreement among the 23 exchanges calls for sharing financial and operational data on firms that are considered at risk of falling below so-called "early-warning" capital requirements set by the exchanges or the federal government.

The pact was drafted by the Intermarket Financial Surveillance Group, which was formed after the October 1987 stock market crash in an effort to boost cooperation.

The group includes exchanges and has as observers representatives of the Securities and Exchange Commission and Commodity Futures Trading Commission.
