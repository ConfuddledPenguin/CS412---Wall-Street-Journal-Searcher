Technology Brief -- International Business Machines: Software to Enhance Talk With Rivals' Units Offered

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/19/90

International Business Machines Corp. unveiled communications software that it said will make it easier for some of its computers to talk to those made by other companies. IBM, based in Armonk, N.Y., said one of the products will let its personal computers and AS/400 minicomputers communicate with other companies' equipment via the Open Systems Interconnection software standard, which is widely used by non-IBM computers.

Another product will let IBM mainframes talk to personal computers in networks that use the Ethernet standard, which also is used extensively to link equipment made by IBM's competitors.

IBM also announced mainframe software that it said will let its electronic-mail systems tie into public networks such as MCI Mail, by conforming to an emerging standard known as X.400.
