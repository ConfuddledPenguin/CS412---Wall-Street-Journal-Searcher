Business Brief -- Itel Corp.: Company Buys Back 25% Of Shares Held by Henley

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/19/90

Itel Corp. repurchased 4,675,000 of its shares from Henley Group Inc. last week at $18 a share, or about $84 million. In a filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission, Henley said it now holds 14 million Itel shares, or 33% of those outstanding.

In August, Itel, a leasing company, agreed to buy 25%, or 4,675,000 shares, of the 18.7 million shares that Henley owned at $18 a share.

Itel also agreed to buy another 4,675,000 shares held by Henley before the end of the year for about $91 million, or $19.50 a share.

Itel has an option to buy the remaining 9,350,000 shares that Henley owns at $21 a share if it makes the purchase by March 31, 1991, or at $22 a share if it buys the stake by July 31, 1991. If Itel doesn't exercise the option, it must underwrite the sale of the 9,350,000 shares at $22 a share, or $206 million, according to the pact.

On Monday, Standard & Poor's Corp. downgraded its rating on more than $1 billion of Itel debt, citing the increased leverage prompted by Itel's agreement to buy back shares from Henley. S&P said the buy-back, which is being financed through asset sales, will increase debt leverage during an expected economic downturn.
