Who's News: Charles J. Many Is Expected to Get Nynex Belgian Job

  • Author: Mary Lu Carnevale
  • Date: 09/19/90

Nynex Corp., signaling a more aggressive posture overseas, is expected to name Charles J. Many to head a new international office in Brussels, Belgium. Mr. Many, president of Nynex Mobile Communications, the company's cellular unit, has a reputation within the cellular industry as a tenacious executive with a keen interest in marketing. He has also been a driving force in Nynex Mobile's push into emerging cellular technologies. Earlier this summer, for instance, Nynex announced plans to build a personal communications network in Manhattan based on a technology called code division multiple access, or CDMA, which allows many more simultaneous conversations than other technologies.

Like other Baby Bells, Nynex is playing catch-up with San Francisco-based Pacific Telesis Group, which teamed with European partners to win an important contract to build and operate a cellular system in Germany. Although Nynex and its U.K. partners aggressively pursued cable television franchises in the U.K., the company's European business largely consists of computer software sales and consulting.

A Nynex spokesman declined to comment on reports that Mr. Many would soon be transferred to Europe, and Mr. Many wasn't available. The spokesman acknowledged that the company is positioning itself to take advantage of twin forces at work in the global telecommunications market: privatization and deregulation. "We're very serious about international markets," the spokesman said. Important parts of that strategy, he said, are cellular and data communications. In April, Nynex set up a Worldwide Information & Cellular Services Group, headed by Ivan G. Seidenberg, a corporate executive vice president.

The company is expected to make other executive changes, including naming Mr. Many's successor at Nynex Mobile and reorganizing Materiel Enterprises Co., the purchasing arm for New York Telephone and New England Telephone, people familiar with the situation said.

Overseas operations last year accounted for only a small fraction of Nynex's $13.2 billion in revenue. But the company has said that it is counting on foreign ventures to bring in as much as 20% of its revenue by the end of the decade.

"The fact that Nynex has chosen a cellular guy is good," said Jack Grubman, an analyst with PaineWebber Inc. "The only thing that makes sense in Europe is cellular as opposed to becoming some minority partner in a land-line network."

Mr. Many, a native of New York City, joined Bell Laboratories in 1960 and held several management positions before moving in 1975 to New York Telephone, where he held jobs in management information systems, networking and marketing. From there, he moved to Nynex Business Information Systems Co., holding jobs as vice president of sales, vice president of new ventures and vice president of marketing. He became president of Nynex Mobile in 1986, and has briefly been chairman of the Cellular Telecommunications Industry Association, the main trade group for the cellular industry.
