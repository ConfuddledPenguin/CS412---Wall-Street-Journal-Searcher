Proposal for Joint Ventures Made on Antitrust Liability

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/08/90

WASHINGTON -- The Justice and Commerce departments proposed legislation offering companies involved in joint production ventures reduced risk of antitrust action by the government. The bill would require the courts to consider such economic benefits as reduced production costs and increased competitiveness with foreign industries before concluding that joint ventures violate antitrust laws. It also would reduce antitrust liability to single damages from treble damages for companies involved in such cooperative ventures, provided the companies clear the joint ventures in advance with the government.

Commerce Secretary Robert Mosbacher said the legislation would be especially helpful to high-technology industries, although he declined to cite any examples. "It's going to work across the broad spectrum," he said. Mr. Mosbacher added that the bill would help U.S. industries battle foreign competition, especially from Japan and Europe.

In 1984, Congress reduced antitrust liability for companies involved in joint research and development projects. Sen. Howard Metzenbaum (D., Ohio), a co-author of that legislation, said that extending the protection to manufacturing carried the approach too far. He added that the new proposal "would do little to improve America's competitiveness and would do much to undermine the laws that promote competition."
