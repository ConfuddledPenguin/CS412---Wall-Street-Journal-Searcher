ITT Increases Stake In Western Union, Becomes Big Holder

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/08/90

NEW YORK -- ITT Corp. became Western Union Corp.'s second largest owner in late March. Western issued an additional 7.8 million shares, or 9.02%, of its common stock to ITT under terms of its December 1987 agreement to purchase ITT World Communications Inc.

That agreement said that if Western Union's stock fell below $2.05625 a share at Dec. 31, 1989, ITT would receive the difference between $2.05625 and the market value of the stock multiplied by 6,565,350.

In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange, Western Union closed at 56.25 cents, up 6.25 cents.

The new shares raise ITT's stake in Western Union to 16.58% of its 86.8 million shares outstanding and 7.8% of voting rights in the beleaguered telecommunications company. That is second to Western's largest shareholder, New York financier Bennett S. LeBow, who owns 43.4% of Western's common, or 46.4% of its voting rights.

"This is purely pursuant to our 1987 agreement," said a Western spokesman. "This is not ITT buying additional shares."

Actually, ITT's new status could be short-lived if a recent plan by Western to restructure its debt wins approval from shareholders. Western wants to swap its existing debt securities for new notes and to increase its common outstanding to 368 million shares. This would shrink ITT's stake to about 3.9%.
