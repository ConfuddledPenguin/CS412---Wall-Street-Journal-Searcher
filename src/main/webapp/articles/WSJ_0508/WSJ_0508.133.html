Christie's First Art Auction Of Season Is Disappointing

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/08/90

NEW YORK -- Christie's International PLC reported somewhat disappointing results from its evening sale of contemporary art here last night. For the first night of the two-week spring auction season, roughly one-third of the works of art didn't sell. The total for the sale came in at $40.2 million, well below the firm's lowest estimate of $50 million.

However, strong bidding by Japanese collectors and investors allayed concerns of some dealers that the Tokyo stock market's recent decline would trim demand from that usually lucrative area. The auction house said Asian investors were responsible for 39% of the dollar total of the art works sold, compared with 7% at Christie's previous major sale of contemporary art in November. A Japanese bidder paid $1.1 million for Andy Warhol's "Myths", a giant silk-screen of pop-culture icons. The price fell within Christie's estimated range.

The top-selling piece at the auction was Roy Lichtenstein's "Kiss II," a large oil on canvas in comic-book style, which sold for $6.1 million, also within the estimated range.

Sotheby's Holdings Inc. is to hold its auction of contemporary art this evening.
