International Brief -- Enimont S.p.A.: Earnings Fell 21% in 1989, Failing to Meet Estimates

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/08/90

Enimont S.p.A. reported 1989 net profit fell 21% to 750 billion lire ($614 million) from pro-forma net earnings of 945 billion lire in 1988. The profit figure was down significantly from a forecast by former Chairman Lorenzo Necci in January that the chemical venture, based in Milan, Italy, would post net profit of 900 billion lire last year. Enimont also predicted net profit in 1990 would be around 450 billion lire, down sharply from Mr. Necci's 1988 prediction that the company would post earnings of 1.2 trillion lire in 1990.

Enimont was formed at the beginning of 1989 by merging the chemical interests of state energy agency Ente Nazionale Idrocarburi and private chemical group Montedison S.p.A.

Hailed as a model of public-private cooperation, Enimont's profitability has been badly damaged by fighting between the two founders over strategy and investments, analysts agree. A cyclical downturn in the chemical industry has also kept pressure on earnings, experts said.
