Business Brief -- American Telephone & Telegraph: Pan Am Awards Computer, Network Systems Contract

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/08/90

American Telephone & Telegraph Co. received a five-year contract valued at $40 million from Pan American World Airways Inc. for computer and network systems. New York-based AT&T will install personal computers and local area network systems for managing the airline's reservations and baggage-handling operations in about 100 locations world-wide.

The contract, the second-largest commercial computer contract ever received by AT&T, also calls for the company to maintain computer equipment used by the airline from other suppliers.

The idea, said Robert O. Wagner, Pan Am's vice president of information services, is to create the "airport of the future" where ticket issuing, baggage handling and boarding are automated.
