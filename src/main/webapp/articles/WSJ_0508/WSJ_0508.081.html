Who's News: Two Leave Citicorp As Pinch Continues In Government Bonds

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/08/90

NEW YORK -- In a sign of the continuing profit pressures on government bond dealers, the head of Citicorp's government bond operation and the chief trader resigned. In an internal memo, Citicorp last week said that David A. Jones, who headed the bond operation, and Lawrence Isabel, the chief trader, had resigned to pursue other interests.

The resignations came as Citicorp divided the unit and reassigned responsibilities. The U.S. government securities portion of the bond unit was combined with foreign exchange and hedging instruments under David Young, division executive for exposure management. Henry Mlynarski was named head of trading and sales of U.S. government securities. Municipal securities and certain other instruments were placed under Robert Laughlin, formerly division executive for equipment finance and leasing.

Michael A. Callen, Citicorp vice chairman, said in an interview that government bonds "continue to be a real tough business." He spoke highly of Messrs. Jones and Isabel, calling them "first-rate."

Neither Messrs. Jones nor Isabel could be reached for comment.

In recent months, Citicorp, like other primary dealers, has scaled back its bond trading in the face of slim margins and continued high costs.
