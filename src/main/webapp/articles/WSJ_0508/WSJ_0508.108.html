Occidental Unit Says Soviet Project Stalled Following Complaints

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/08/90

DALLAS -- Occidental Chemical Co. said a proposed $200 million project in the Soviet Ukraine was stalled after local residents complained about toxic pollutants from an existing petrochemical complex there. "We agreed to a one-year delay at the request of our joint-venture partner, which owns the complex," said an Occidental Chemical spokesman. The partner is Chlorvinyl, a state-owned company under the direction of the Soviet Chemical Industry Ministry.

On April 10, opponents of Occidental's project sponsored a "town meeting" in Kalush near the complex, said a spokeswoman for the Natural Resources Defense Council, a U.S. environmental advocacy group. Residents want the existing plant to "be brought up to standard" before any expansion takes place, she said.

Toxic emissions from the Chlorvinyl complex exceed maximum permissible concentrations set by the Soviet government, according to the National Resources Defense Council. Since November, Soviet law has required an environmental assessment for new industrial projects prior to their approval.

Occidental, a unit of Los Angeles-based Occidental Petroleum Corp., signed an agreement with Chlorvinyl two years ago to construct and operate two plants at the complex that would make polyvinyl chloride, a widely used plastic. Occidental said it would have a 25% stake in the project. The existing plant makes vinylchloride, among other chemicals.
