Landmark Land Unit Financing for Hon Faces U.S. Objections

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/08/90

CARMEL, Calif. -- Landmark Land Co. said the Office of Thrift Supervision is objecting to its plans for financing the sale of a major part of its real estate and resort holdings for nearly $1 billion. The move casts doubt upon nearly $500 million in expected pretax profit from the proposed transaction.

The planned sale of the company's premier golf, tennis and development properties to California developer Barry G. Hon for $967 million called for Landmark's Oak Tree Savings Bank unit to finance the sale.

However, in a supervisory objection letter the agency expressed "concern we'd have too high a percentage of our assets, about 20%, tied up in one borrower," said Gerland G. Barton, Landmark's president. "I'm surprised, since we're getting about 25% down and we'd have a first mortgage on the property," Mr. Barton added.

Landmark and Mr. Hon are attempting to restructure the proposed transaction to address the agency's concerns.

Inability to recast the deal to the agency's satisfaction would kill the sale, Landmark said, and cancel out an expected $470 million in pretax profit that would have resulted from closing the transaction.

Moreover, Landmark said it had hoped the sale would help it satisfy all capital requirements of the Financial Institutions Reform, Recovery and Enforcement Act.

Landmark's Mr. Barton said Mr. Hon, the owner of Hon Development Co., of Laguna Hills, Calif., was seeking alternative financing for the planned purchase. He "is coming back with a proposal he anticipates will be acceptable," he said.
