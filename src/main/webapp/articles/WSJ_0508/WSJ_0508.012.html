Help-Wanted Ad Volume In U.S. Declined in March

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/08/90

NEW YORK -- The Conference Board reports that total help-wanted ad volume fell one point in March, but a rebound is expected. The Help-Wanted Advertising Index, a seasonally adjusted indicator of hiring plans, fell from 140 in February, to 139, the lowest point in three months. That figure is 11 points lower than the year-ago level of 150.

First-quarter economic activity was strong enough to stop a further decline. Ken Goldstein, a Conference Board economist, said growth in the second and third quarters is expected to be slightly faster.

Ad volume, measured in 51 major U.S. newspapers, declined in six of the nine major regions of the country. The West North Central region recorded the steepest drop -- 11%. And the East South Central and East North Central regions showed increases of 4.5% and 2.5% respectively. Mr. Goldstein said, "Hardest hit areas continued to show improvements, although at an anxiety-inducing slow pace."
