Business Brief -- Chrysler Corp.: Recall Set for 383,700 Cars With High Exhaust Levels

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/08/90

Chrysler Corp. is recalling about 383,700 of its 1985-model cars because they fail to meet federal auto-exhaust standards for hydrocarbons and carbon monoxide, the Environmental Protection Agency announced in Washington. The company agreed to the recall after the EPA discovered the problem during routine testing. The affected models are the Plymouth Horizon, Turismo, Reliant and Reliant wagon; and the Dodge Omni, Charger, Aries and Aries wagon. All have 2.2-liter engines.

Chrysler will replace part of the carburetor choke system. The service will be free for car owners.

Hydrocarbons contribute to the formation of smog when combined with sunlight and other pollutants. Excessive carbon monoxide impairs heart function and is harmful to people with lung disease and anemia.
