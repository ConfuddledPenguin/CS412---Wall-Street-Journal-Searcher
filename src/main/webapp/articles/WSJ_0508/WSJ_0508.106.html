Baxter Reiterates Propriety Of Sale of Plant in Israel

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/08/90

CHICAGO -- Vernon R. Loucks, Baxter International Inc.'s chairman and chief executive officer, reiterated that the company sold its Israeli plant for business reasons, and not to win removal from the Arab League blacklist of companies doing business in Israel. Mr. Loucks told shareholders at the company's annual meeting that while the issue "is a source of some embarrassment . . . because it has been brought up {in a way that} presumes guilt" on Baxter's part, the company had since 1974 been studying problems with its Israeli business that were unrelated to its Arab League blacklisting.

As reported in a page one story in The Wall Street Journal earlier this month, Baxter was removed from the Arab blacklist last year after more than a decade of being shut out of most Middle Eastern markets. Its de-blacklisting followed by about a year the sale of the Ashdod, Israel, plant that prompted Baxter's blacklisting in the first place.

A U.S. anti-boycott law forbids the sale of Israeli facilities to win removal from the Arab League blacklist.

The Securities and Exchange Commission and the Commerce Department are reviewing the matter, and the audit committee of the Baxter board has commissioned an outside lawyer to conduct an independent investigation.
