Technology Brief -- NCR Corp.: Computer Line Is Unveiled Based on Intel Corp. Chip

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/08/90

NCR Corp. said it unveiled a family of high-end personal computers that are among the first based on Intel Corp.'s new i486 chip. The family includes workstations that operate at 27 million instructions per second and are 32% faster than most personal computers based on a slower version of the i486 chip, NCR said. The new computers are targeted for such "computer-intensive" applications as economic and financial modeling, computer-aided design, graphics and animation, the Dayton, Ohio-based computer maker added.

The new computers can use the UNIX, MS OS/2 and DOS operating systems, NCR said. Prices start at $14,195 for a desktop personal computer called the PC486/MC33 system. Prices for a network and database "server" for multiple users, called the S486/MC33, start at $16,195.

The new computers will be available in July, NCR said.
