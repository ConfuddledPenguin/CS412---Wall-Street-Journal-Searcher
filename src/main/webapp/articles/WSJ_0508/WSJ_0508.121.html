Business Brief -- RWE AG: Group Profit Rise Is Shown For First 9 Months of '90

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/08/90

RWE AG said its group profit rose in the first nine months of fiscal 1990 on a 6.2% rise in sales. The West German energy concern said group sales in the period ended March 31 rose to around 31 billion marks ($18.61 billion) from 29 billion marks a year ago.

Speaking at the company's annual shareholding meeting, Chairman Friedhelm Gieske didn't give any profit figures. But citing the group's earnings-oriented dividend policy, he indicated a possible increase in the fiscal 1990 dividend from nine marks a share. RWE will also increase its special reserve fund for the first time since 1984, Mr. Gieske said.

Full-year sales are expected to climb 13% to 43.8 billion marks from 38.9 billion marks in fiscal 1989, partly reflecting the partial consolidation of construction group Hochtief AG, in which RWE boosted its stake to 56% from 46% this year by buying a 10% interest from Commerzbank AG.
