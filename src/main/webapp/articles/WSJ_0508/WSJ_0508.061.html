Pennsylvania Seeks To Bar Safeco Unit's Bid to End Coverage

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/08/90

HARRISBURG, Pa. -- Insurance regulators have asked a state court to block Safeco Insurance Co. of America's refusal to renew its 8,000 auto-insurance policies in Pennsylvania. Safeco's refusal may be illegal under a 1968 state law that governs the circumstances that allow an insurer to stop renewing policies.

Safeco, a unit of Seattle-based Safeco Corp., has already ignored a cease and desist order, issued by the Pennsylvania Insurance Department last week when it learned that the insurer was sending out the nonrenewal notices. The insurance department now wants the court to issue a mandatory injunction to keep Safeco from canceling its policies.

Today the insurance department will ask the same court to preliminarily block Harleysville Mutual Insurance Co. from canceling its 1,200 policies as they come due.

Safeco and Harleysville are two of more than 100 insurance companies that have failed to comply with an auto-insurance law passed by the Pennsylvania state legislature in February. Some have asked for permission to raise their auto rates when they should be lowering them and two companies have tried to pull out of Pennsylvania entirely.

Insurers had to file by May 1 a new schedule of auto insurance premium rates, incorporating the rate rollbacks mandated by the new law. The law calls for rollbacks of 10% for all drivers and up to 22% for those who gave up their right to sue in court except in cases of severe injuries. The rollbacks are due to begin July 1.

Robert Casey, Pennsylvania's governor, has asked the insurance department to explore all legal options to prevent insurance companies from thwarting the implementation of the law.

"We don't intend to sit by while the industry maneuvers through countless lawsuits, failure to file in a timely manner and other dilatory tactics to deny policyholders the rate relief they are entitled to under the law," said Constance B. Foster, Pennsylvania's insurance commissioner.

A Safeco official said the company hadn't yet seen any court papers filed by the insurance department and couldn't comment.
