April Housing Starts Fell 11.9% in Canada

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/08/90

OTTAWA -- Canadian housing starts fell 11.9% in April from March as rising mortgage rates damped housing demand. April housing starts declined to a seasonally adjusted annual rate of 199,000 from a March rate of 226,000, Canada Mortgage and Housing Corp., a government agency, said.

April was the first month since February 1988 that the housing-start rate has fallen below 200,000.

Annual interest charges on a five-year mortgage have risen to 14 1/4%-14 1/2% from 12% in January. Each percentage point rise in mortgage rates has pushed thousands of would-be home buyers out of the market, said Bill Hopkins, vice president of the Canadian Real Estate Association. Sales of existing homes in March fell 22.2% from a year earlier.

Ontario showed the biggest drop in new housing activity in April, with urban starts falling 32.5% to an annual rate of 52,000, the lowest level for the province in more than four years. The number of new single, detached houses in Toronto that were unsold rose to more than 500 from about 150 in mid-1989. Actual starts in the city in January-April were down 44% from a year earlier.
