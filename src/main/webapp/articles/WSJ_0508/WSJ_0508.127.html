Marketing Brief -- New Line Cinema Corp.: Independent Producer Posts Profit; 2 Movies Are Cited

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/08/90

New Line Cinema Corp. reported it swung to a profit in the first quarter, helped by the release of two motion pictures, "House Party" and "Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles." New Line, a New York-based independent producer and distributor of movies, earned $739,000, or 11 cents a share, compared with a net loss of $287,000, or four cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue more than doubled to $29 million from $11.1 million.

New Line credited the results to the first 23 days of "House Party," which opened March 9; and the first two days of "Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles," which opened on March 30. To date, "House Party" has grossed more than $22 million, and "Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles" has grossed more than $100 million.

New Line expects the bulk of theatrical distribution revenue for the Turtles movie to be recorded in the second quarter. The company expects both movies to provide additional revenue as they are released in ancillary markets.

In trading on the American Stock Exchange, New Line closed Monday at $10.375, up 25 cents.
