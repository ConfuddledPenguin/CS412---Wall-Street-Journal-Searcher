Amoco Unit Seeks Sale of a 5% Interest In Canadian Oil Plant

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/08/90

CALGARY, Alberta -- Amoco Corp.'s Canadian unit said it retained First Boston Corp. to sell a 5% interest in the Syncrude Canada Ltd. oil sands plant. Amoco Canada Petroleum Co. owns 3.8% of Syncrude, whose Fort McMurray, Alberta, plant last year produced about 148,000 barrels a day of synthetic crude, or roughly 10% of Canada's oil output. The Amoco unit manages an additional 1.3% of Syncrude for Encor Inc., a Calgary-based oil and gas producer.

Amoco Canada didn't offer a value for its Syncrude stake. The government of Alberta, which last year offered its 16.7% stake in Syncrude for sale, said its interest has a book value of more than 500 million Canadian dollars ($430.1 million).

Amoco Canada said First Boston has already contacted several prospective buyers. It didn't elaborate.

Syncrude is owned by an eight-member consortium led by a unit of Imperial Oil Ltd., with a 25% stake. Toronto-based Imperial is 70%-owned by Exxon Corp., New York.
