International: Australia's Rouse Is Jailed For Political Bribe Offer

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 05/08/90

HOBART, Australia -- Australian businessman Edmund Alexander Rouse, former chairman of the ENT Ltd. media group, which owns radio stations in California, was jailed for three years for offering a bribe to a politician. Mr. Rouse was also fined 4,000 Australian dollars (US$3,022) for the bribe offer, which was made to a Tasmanian state politician.

Mr. Rouse had pleaded guilty to one charge of offering a bribe to a politician in June last year, and another count of improperly using his position as an officer of ENT. He resigned as chairman of ENT last June, after he was charged with the bribery offense.

In sentencing Mr. Rouse, the court said he had tried to nullify the democratic process by offering A$110,000 to an Australian Labor Party politician to cross the floor of Parliament and vote with the Liberal Party on a key issue.

The move was aimed at saving the Liberal State government, which was then ruling Tasmania with a minority government, and to protect Mr. Rouse's business interests.

In addition to the seven California radio stations, ENT also owns three Australian television stations and the Launceston Examiner daily newspaper, and has interests in motels and cinemas in Australia.
