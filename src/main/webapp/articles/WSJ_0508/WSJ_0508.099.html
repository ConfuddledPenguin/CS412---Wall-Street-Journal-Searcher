OTC Focus: Technology Stocks Lead Market Rally, But There Are Signs of Possible Fading

  • Author: Anne Newman
  • Date: 05/08/90

The over-the-counter market again rode the coattails of technology stocks, but there were signs by the end of the session that the rally might be fading. The Nasdaq Composite Index closed up 2.62 at 431.23, a 0.6% gain, with advancing shares leading declining stocks, 984 to 825. The Nasdaq 100 index of larger issues, including some leading technology shares, surged 1.09% to 430.06, its highest level since mid-April. Volume slipped to 122.6 million shares from 136.8 million.

Institutional interest continued to outpace buying among small investors, said Ralph Costanza, manager of the OTC department at Smith Barney, Harris & Upham. Mr. Costanza suggested investors had expected disappointments, but not positive news, among OTC and technology stocks and are now catching up with the rally.

By midday, interest appeared to peak as such stocks as Microsoft backed off their highs for the day, said Nicholas Karos, senior OTC trader at Piper Jaffray & Hopwood, Minneapolis. After hitting 66 in intraday trading, Microsoft finished 2 1/4 higher at 64, a 52-week closing high. Mr. Karos suggested profit-taking might continue today.

Technology companies continued to dominate the news, amid both actual and anticipated announcements of improved computer products.

Intel led the day's most-active list after NCR Corp. unveiled a family of high-end personal computers based on Intel's 486 chip. Merrill Lynch analyst Thomas Kurlak said in an interview that the Santa Clara, Calif., semiconductor maker's shares also remain in favor after company officials told analysts last week that April orders are strong. Intel rose 7/8 to 43 1/8 on 2.3 million shares.

Shearson Lehman Hutton software analyst David Readerman attributed the continuing rally in the technology sector to new software products expected from such companies as Microsoft, and new hardware products expected in the second half of the year. In an interview, Mr. Readerman also noted that first-quarter earnings for technology companies outpaced other industry groups, largely because of strong international demand.

However, Mr. Readerman said that while the long-term outlook for technology companies is looking better, he thinks that some stocks, including as Microsoft, are fully valued in the short term.

Reports that Microsoft will unveil its long-awaited Windows 3.0 software later this month helped drive the Redmond, Wash., computer company's shares higher. Mr. Readerman called the stock fairly priced, noting that it's trading at 22 times his estimated fiscal 1991 earnings for Microsoft of $2.90 a share. In the long-term, however, he says the stock is attractive.

Lotus gained 1/8 to 33 1/2 after the Cambridge, Mass., software maker said it will ship a new version of its 1-2-3 software program with improved graphics capability during the third quarter. Noting that the stock traded as high as 33 1/2, Mr. Readerman attributed some of the strength to an additional announcement by Lotus that at some point it will ship a version that will be compatible with Microsoft's upgraded Windows 3.0 program.

MCI Communications gained 3/8 to 38 1/4 after the long distance carrier declared a new semi-annual dividend of five cents a share. The company said it expects to maintain that level for two to three years.

Centocor jumped 2 to 35 1/4. The Malvern, Pa., biotechnology company Saturday reported the results of clinical trials of Centoxin, a drug used to treat septic shock, before a medical meeting in Washington. Some analysts told Dow Jones Professional Investor Report the results were bullish for the stock, with Robertson Stephens analyst Mark Simon saying he is maintaining a "buy" rating on Centocor. Shares of Xoma, a Berkeley, Calif., company developing a competing drug, rose 1/4 to 19 1/4.

Nordstrom rose 1 3/8 to 29 1/8 after a CBS "60 Minutes" segment on the Seattle, Wash., clothing retailer wasn't as negative as some investors had expected, traders said.

Washington Bancorp tumbled 5 1/2 to 3 1/2. The holding company of National Bank of Washington, D.C., said it isn't able to repay about $36.7 million of unsecured commercial paper obligations because some unidentified but unrelated banks cut off some lines of credit. The company said it will discontinue its commercial paper program.

Mediagenic fell 1 3/8 to 3 after the Menlo Park, Calif., company said it will report a $19 million loss on revenue of $65 million for the fiscal year ended March 31. The publisher of entertainment software said it hired an investment banker and may put itself up for sale.

Newbridge Networks lost 1 3/8 to close at 8 1/4 after the Kanata, Ontario, maker of digital networking products said it will not meet revenue and earnings expectations for the fourth quarter ended April 30.

Shares of B&H Bulk Carriers dropped 5/8 to 4 3/8 after the New York company reported a loss for the first quarter ended March 31 of 16 cents a share, compared with net income of 67 cents a share a year earlier.
