Fluor Corp. to Pay $125 Million to Buy Rest of Doe Run Co.

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/08/90

IRVINE, Calif. -- Fluor Corp., which holds a 57.5% stake in Doe Run Co., said it agreed to pay $125 million to acquire the rest of the company from Homestake Mining Co. Homestake said the sale of its 42.5% interest in the lead production company would result in after-tax proceeds of about $95 million and a net gain of about $40 million. The sale, contingent only upon government approval, is expected to close this summer.

Under the joint ownership arrangement, most decisions required approval of both partners. "The sale will resolve differences of opinion about the direction of the business," said Harry M. Conger, Homestake chairman and chief executive officer.

Doe Run, based in St. Louis, is the largest integrated lead producer in North America, with about a 60% share of the U.S. market, Fluor said. Last year, Doe Run posted $68 million in operating profit on $260 million of revenue.

Full ownership "increases the strategic options available," said Fluor's chief executive officer, Les G. McCraw. For example, Doe Run is expected to expand its activities in the recycled lead market, a spokesman said. The largest market for both new and recycled lead is automobile batteries.

Fluor is an engineering and construction concern with investments in lead and coal. Homestake, based in San Francisco, is a gold-mining company with interests in the U.S., Australia, Chile and Canada.
