IMF Policy Unit Agrees To Boost Resources 50%

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/08/90

WASHINGTON -- The International Monetary Fund's policy-making committee agreed to a 50% increase in the multilateral institution's resources, officials said. The decision, which is likely to take up to two years to implement, was reached at meetings of the IMF's powerful Interim Committee late yesterday, according to U.S. and Argentine officials who attended the sessions. It will raise the resources of the IMF to about $180 billion from a current level of about $120 billion.

Members of the committee also agreed to a U.S.-backed formula for sorting out close to $4 billion of payments arrears to the IMF. Under the formula, 11 countries that are deeply in arrears will be lent further funds by the IMF to cover their overdue payments but might forfeit gold they previously pledged to the fund if they fail to honor their obligations as they work their way out of their difficulties.

A U.S.-backed proposal to amend the IMF's articles to allow the organization to suspend member countries that are severely delinquent on their debts also was accepted by the committee.

The committee also agreed that it will next consider the issue of replenishing the IMF's resources in 1993, a move requested by several developing nations. The U.S. and other industrial nations had proposed 1995 for such consideration. The IMF gathers its resources by requiring its 152 member nations to subscribe to quotas. In an exchange of assets, member countries pledge some of their reserves to fulfill their quotas.
