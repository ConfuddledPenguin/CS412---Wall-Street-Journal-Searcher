Atlantic Richfield Reports 22% Decline In First-Quarter Net

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/08/90

LOS ANGELES -- Atlantic Richfield Co. said that first-quarter net income fell 22% but that operating profit, exclusive of one-time items, rose 10%. The Los Angeles-based oil company -- periodically an aggressive buyer of its own stock -- also said it repurchased 2.1 million common shares in the first quarter for $232.3 million.

The repurchases reduced Arco's total stock and stock equivalents outstanding to 166.2 million shares at March 31, down 1.2% from 168.3 million at Dec. 31, 1989, and off 5.9% from 176.6 million shares at Dec. 31, 1988.

Net fell to $547 million, or $3.28 a share, from $704 million, or $4 a share, a year earlier.

The latest period included a $323 million, or $1.94-a-share, gain from a change in accounting for income taxes, offset in part by charges of $233 million. The charges included provisions for taxes, anticipated environmental cleanup costs, and early debt redemption.

The year-earlier period included a $634 million, or $3.60-a-share, gain from the sale of stock in a subsidiary, partly offset by $345 million in charges for future environmental and other expenses.

Revenue climbed 6.4% to $4.22 billion from $3.96 billion. The number of average shares, on which the per-share profit is calculated, fell to 166.9 million from 176.1 million a year earlier.

Lodwrick M. Cook, chairman and chief executive officer, said operating profit rose to $457 million from $415 million a year earlier.

Mr. Cook attributed improved operating results to higher crude oil prices, higher refined product prices, and to increased natural gas sales.
