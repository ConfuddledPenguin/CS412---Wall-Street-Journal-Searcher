Who's News: Spanish State Official Expected to Be Picked As Chairman of Iberia

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/08/90

MADRID -- Spanish state-industry official Miguel Aguilo is expected to be named soon as chairman of Iberia, Spain's national air carrier. Company officials confirmed that Iberia's current chairman, Narcis Andreu, will leave shortly to become chairman of the Belgian subsidiary of state-controlled Banco Exterior de Espana.

Sources at Instituto Nacional de Industria, or INI, the state holding company that owns Iberia, said Mr. Aguilo is expected to be appointed to the Iberia post later this month. The changeover is expected to take place after an Iberia shareholders' meeting in June.

Mr. Aguilo, 45 years old, has run INI's shipbuilding division for the past three years, boosting orders substantially and helping to cut the division's losses to 23 billion pesetas ($220 million) last year, a 25% narrowing from 1988.

Known as a competent manager with a gift for teamwork, Mr. Aguilo is close to Spain's ruling Socialist Party. Before joining INI, he held various posts in enterprises run by the Madrid regional government, including the chairmanship of the Madrid water company, Canal de Isabel II.

At Iberia, he will have to tackle the challenges posed by increased competition in Europe's aviation market at a time of weakening demand for tourist flights to Spain.
