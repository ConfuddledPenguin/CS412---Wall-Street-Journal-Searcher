International: Coopers & Lybrand Settles Claim by Hong Kong Bank

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/08/90

HONG KONG -- Overseas Trust Bank, which was declared insolvent and taken over by the government in 1985, settled a negligence claim against accounting firm Coopers & Lybrand, the bank's auditor until the takeover. Overseas Trust alleged that Coopers & Lybrand failed to audit the bank's accounts properly, and therefore should take at least some responsibility for the losses that caused the insolvency and subsequent takeover.

The settlement, announced Monday, involves a payment by Coopers & Lybrand to the bank, which remains under government management. The amount of the payment wasn't disclosed. Coopers & Lybrand, one of the world's largest accounting firms, isn't required to admit liability.

Overseas Trust Managing Director David Turner said the bank considers the payment a satisfactory settlement of the lawsuit, which he described as "basically a negligence action."

Roderick Chalmers, a partner with Coopers & Lybrand, said the firm continues to maintain its "denial of the allegations" in the lawsuit as well as its "denial of liability." In New York, Coopers & Lybrand's in-house lawyers weren't available for comment.

After the government takeover, Overseas Trust announced a consolidated loss totaling HK$3.05 billion (US$391.4 million), for the fiscal year ended June 30, 1985. Subsequent police investigations led to fraud convictions and prison terms for several senior bank executives and their business associates.

Coopers & Lybrand wasn't implicated in the criminal investigation.
