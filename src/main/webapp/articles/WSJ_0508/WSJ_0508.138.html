Business Brief -- MCA Inc.: Firm Leaves Joint Venture With Spectacor Management

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/08/90

MCA Inc., Universal City, Calif., said it withdrew from its joint venture with Spectacor Management Group to operate the Los Angeles Coliseum and Sports Arena. Philadelphia-based Spectacor, whose majority partners are the Pritzker and Snider families, said it won't seek to replace MCA in the venture and will continue to operate the two sports facilities, home of the Los Angeles Clippers basketball team and the Los Angeles Raiders football team as well as teams from the University of Southern California. MCA/Spectacor has managed the sites since 1988.

Spectacor is trying to put together a $145 million package to keep the Raiders in Los Angeles that would involve privatizing and redeveloping the publicly owned Coliseum.

Al Teller, chairman of MCA's Music division, said, "Stadium development, as opposed to management, is not within our current corporate and strategic goals." The two companies will continue their association in marketing concert merchandise.
