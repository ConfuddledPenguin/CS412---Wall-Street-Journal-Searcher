Business Brief -- Apple Bancorp Inc.: Stahl Can Get Nonpublic Data for Standstill Accord

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/08/90

Apple Bancorp Inc. said it will give nonpublic information to Stanley Stahl, if the New York real-estate investor agrees to a two-year confidentiality and standstill accord. Through a spokesman, Mr. Stahl declined to comment on the announcement, made in a Securities and Exchange Commission filing by the New York parent of Apple Bank for Savings. The agreement would require that for two years, Mr. Stahl refrain from buying more Apple shares or making an offer for the company without approval from the bank's directors.

Mr. Stahl, who controls 30.6% of Apple, in late March offered $38 a share, or about $121.6 million, for the rest. The bank has rejected the bid. Apple also said last week that it would provide nonpublic data to investors, other than Mr. Stahl, who have indicated interest in taking over the holding company.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Apple shares fell 50 cents, to close at $43.75.
