Business Brief -- Washington Bancorp: Firm Can't Repay Certain Unsecured Paper Obligations

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/08/90

Washington Bancorp said it is unable to repay about $36.7 million of unsecured commercial-paper obligations because some lines of credit were cut off by other banks. The Washington, D.C.-based holding company for the National Bank of Washington said about $25.8 million of its $36.7 million of commercial paper outstanding matured yesterday and can't be repaid unless new debt financing is arranged "promptly."

In addition, the company said its National Bank of Washington unit agreed to abide by tougher operating requirements imposed by the Comptroller of the Currency because of regulators' concerns about the bank's financial condition.

The agreement calls for prompt action to "increase the availability of liquid assets" at the bank, the company said. The bank also agreed to conduct a "special review" of its commercial real-estate loan portfolio and "certain other loans" that weren't identified.
