Ahmanson Reduces Its Recourse Liability On Mortgage Loans

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/08/90

LOS ANGELES -- H.F. Ahmanson & Co., parent company of Home Savings of America, said it has reduced its recourse liability on $6.2 billion of mortgage loans it previously sold to the Federal National Mortgage Association. The recourse provision required Ahmanson to compensate the mortgage agency if certain losses occurred from foreclosures. The affected mortgages had to be backed by more capital under a risk-based weighting used by regulators. Ahmanson removed the recourse provision by buying financial guarantees to replace its obligation to Fannie Mae. The move comes as part of the thrift holding company's program to increase its capital ratios as required by federal regulations governing the operations of the nation's thrifts.

"Home Savings of America exceeds by an ample margin all current capital requirements imposed by the Financial Institutions Reform, Recovery and Enforcement Act of 1989 (FIRREA)," said Richard A. Diehl, chairman and chief executive officer.

He added that along with the company's acquisition of Home Savings Bank of New York, the action will help the company meet the new regulation's fully phased-in capital requirement as soon as possible.

Effective with the reduction of recourse liability, Home Savings' risk-based capital ratio will increase to 7.57% from 7.11%, effective with the move. Its tangible capital ratio remains unchanged at 3.51%, and its core capital ratio remains at 4.37%.
