Business Brief -- Leggett & Platt Inc.: Tender Offer for Dresher Is Extended Until May 9

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/08/90

Leggett & Platt Inc. extended its $2.50-a-share tender offer for Dresher Inc. to May 9 from May 4, because antitrust regulators haven't yet cleared the proposed $11.4 million acquisition. Leggett, a Carthage, Mo., maker of furniture and bed parts, began the offer for the maker of brass beds early last month. But on April 19, it disclosed that the Justice Department's Antitrust Division had requested additional information. Leggett warned at the time that the offer might have to be extended.

As of the original May 4 deadline, Dresher said, about 2,544,000 shares, or 56% of Dresher's outstanding common, had been tendered or guaranteed for delivery.

Leggett & Platt and Dresher said they both "continue to believe" that the proposed acquisition of Dresher "will not violate the antitrust laws."

Leggett's extended offer expires at 11:59 p.m. EDT May 9.
