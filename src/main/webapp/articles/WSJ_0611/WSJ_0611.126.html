Kemper Corp. Says U.S. Is Investigating Trades by Ex-Aide

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/11/90

CHICAGO -- A lawyer for Kemper Corp. confirmed published reports that the U.S. attorney's office is investigating stock-index futures trades by a former Kemper mutual-fund manager. The inquiry arose from a class-action lawsuit filed in February 1989 against the company and former Kemper money manager Thomas A. Richards, who has since left the firm to start his own business, said Joan Hall, a lawyer for Kemper.

"They are looking at Tom Richards's trading in stock-index futures," Ms. Hall said. "I don't think it's appropriate for me to describe exactly what the company has provided, but it's an accurate statement to say that we're cooperating fully."

Neither Mr. Richards nor his attorney, James Streicker, could be reached yesterday for comment.

The civil suit, filed by two shareholders in public funds formerly managed by Mr. Richards, alleges that Mr. Richards diverted unprofitable stock-index futures trades to the funds, while assigning profitable trades to a Kemper employee profit-sharing account.

Ms. Hall said she didn't know when federal authorities began their inquiry of the trades. "We do not think the allegations have any merit," she said. "We do not think there was any wrongdoing on behalf of Mr. Richards or anyone else at the company."
