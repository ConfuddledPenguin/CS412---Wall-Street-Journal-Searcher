Bush Aide Urges Rate Cuts If Major Deficit Pact Is Set

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/11/90

WASHINGTON -- The chairman of the Council of Economic Advisers said it would be "irresponsible" for the Federal Reserve not to reduce interest rates if Congress and the Bush administration settle on a budget that substantially cuts the federal deficit. "The Federal Reserve could offset any such fiscal contraction" resulting from a deficit accord, said Michael Boskin, the top economic adviser to President Bush. "It should, and it would be irresponsible if it didn't."

Mr. Boskin said, however, there was "no formal deal" between the Bush administration and the Fed to lower rates. "If there's a serious, credible, multiyear budget deal accomplished, that should take a lot of pressure off of long-term interest rates, and it should give {the Fed} the elbowroom to lower interest rates," Mr. Boskin said yesterday on the NBC News program "Meet the Press."

Fed officials have indicated in recent days that they are happy with the current "moderately restrictive" monetary policy, as Fed Gov. Edward Kelley described it. Though some indicators, such as slow employment growth, have pointed to a stalling economy, Fed officials are concerned about a continuing threat of inflation and see no signs of weakness in some other statistics such as the level of inventories.
