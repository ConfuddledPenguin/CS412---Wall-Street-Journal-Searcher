Canadian Pacific's Offer For Railroad Is Approved

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/11/90

The trustee for the Delaware & Hudson Railway said the U.S. Bankruptcy Court in Wilmington, Del., approved Canadian Pacific Ltd.'s offer of $25 million for assets of the railroad, which is in bankruptcy-law proceedings. Francis Dicello, the trustee, said the agreement is subject to negotiation of labor agreements and to regulatory approval. He said the Bankruptcy Court asked the Interstate Commerce Commission to render a decision on the transaction within 90 days of submission.

Delaware & Hudson owns about 600 miles of track in New York and Pennsylvania and has rights to run its trains on an additional 900 miles of track.

Mr. Dicello said Canadian Pacific's plans to rehabilitate the line and increase freight traffic over it will increase rail competition in the Northeast. Delaware & Hudson provides some of the last remaining rail competition in the Northeast to Consolidated Rail Corp., the Philadelphia-based railroad.

The agreement includes commitments for capital improvements to the Delaware & Hudson of $3 million from New York and $5.5 million from Pennsylvania, Mr. Dicello said.

Canadian Pacific is a Montreal transportation, resources, telecommunications and real estate concern.
