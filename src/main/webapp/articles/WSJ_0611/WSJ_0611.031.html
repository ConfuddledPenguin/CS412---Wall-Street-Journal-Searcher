Help-Wanted Advertising Stayed Sluggish in April

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/11/90

NEW YORK -- Help-wanted advertising continued its sluggish pace in April, and remained sharply lower than the year-earlier level. The Conference Board said its help-wanted advertising index fell to 138 in April, down from 139 in March and 21 points below April 1989.

The index, a seasonally adjusted indicator of hiring plans, has remained about the same since February. The index uses 1967 as a base of 100.

Ken Goldstein, a Conference Board economist, said, "If business activity is indeed picking up, as we think is the case, an increase in want-ad volume should start showing up shortly."

Regionally, want-ad volume rose in the East South Central and East North Central areas 5.8% and 4.4%, respectively, but declined elsewhere.

Hardest-hit was New England, where help-wanted advertising has declined 34% in the past year. The Pacific region, down 3.5%, suffered the mildest decline.

The Conference Board, a business-research organization, surveys help-wanted advertising volume in 51 major newspapers every month.
