Pepsi's Taco Bell Recalls Bottles Sold in Promotion

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/11/90

IRVINE, Calif. -- Taco Bell Corp. said it recalled 300,000 plastic sports water bottles sold last week as part of a nationwide promotion, because it discovered that a mouthpiece part could be swallowed by children. Taco Bell, a Mexican-style fast-food chain, said no injuries were related to the water bottle. But the company, a unit of PepsiCo Inc., Purchase, N.Y., said it decided to stop selling the bottles when a customer reported that his 21-month-old child had dismantled one of the bottles and tried to swallow the mouthpiece. Last Friday, Taco Bell said it had learned that two other children had done the same thing.

Taco Bell last Monday began selling the water bottles for 99 cents each with a food purchase. Initial plans called for the sale of about 2.5 million of the bottles, which were part of a $12 million "Tacos to Go" advertising campaign.

The recall is voluntary and is being coordinated through the U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission, Taco Bell said. Commission officials couldn't be reached to comment. The bottle's manufacturer, Specialized Bicycle Components of Morgan Hill, Calif., said it halted production of the bottles, which it has made for 10 years, while it discusses the matter with the safety commission.

Taco Bell said people can return the bottles for refunds. A spokesman said the company hadn't determined the cost of the recall.
