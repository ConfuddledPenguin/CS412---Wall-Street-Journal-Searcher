Dofasco Steel Unit Hires Goldman Sachs To Sell Some Assets

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/11/90

SAULT STE. MARIE, Ontario -- Algoma Steel Corp. said it hired Goldman, Sachs & Co. to find a buyer for its U.S. coal and iron ore interests. Algoma Steel, a unit of Dofasco Inc. of Hamilton, Ontario, Canada's largest steelmaker, said its coal interests consist of four operations in West Virginia. Last year these operations produced about three million net tons of steam coal and 1.6 million net tons of metallurgical coal.

Algoma Steel said its U.S. iron ore operations are held through its partnership interests in the Tilden Mine near Ishpeming, Mich. The company said its share of production at the mine is 2.6 million tons a year.

Other owners of the Tilden mine are Stelco Inc. of Hamilton and Cleveland-based Cleveland-Cliffs Inc., which also manages the mine. Cleveland-Cliffs said total production at Tilden this year is expected to be 6.5 million tons.

Algoma Steel, which didn't disclose an asking price, said the planned sale is consistent with the company's strategy of focusing on its core steel manufacturing business. It said proceeds will be used for general corporate purposes and to reduce debt.
