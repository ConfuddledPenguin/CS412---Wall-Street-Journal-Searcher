Philips Venture With Soviets

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/11/90

EINDHOVEN, The Netherlands -- N.V. Philips Gloeilampenfabriek said that its Hamburg medical systems subsidiary has formed a joint venture with two Soviet partners to install and service Philips medical equipment in the Soviet Union. Philips will work with the Moscow-based Republican Children's Clinical Hospital and the Research and Technical Services Cooperative Tomotek to repair and service equipment on the spot.

Prior to the formation of the joint venture, parts and service technicians were flown to Moscow to repair the medical machinery. Philips noted that the former approach resulted in unacceptable delays in repairing equipment.

Of the three partners, Philips will hold the lion's share of the venture with a 55% stake; the children's hospital will hold a 30% share, with the remaining interest in the hands of Tomotek.
