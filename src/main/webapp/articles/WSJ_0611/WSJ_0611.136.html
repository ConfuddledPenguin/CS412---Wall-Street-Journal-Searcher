Marketing & Media: Street News Founder Resigns and Is Focus Of State Investigation

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/11/90

NEW YORK (AP) -- An inquiry by State Attorney General Robert Abrams into the finances of a newspaper sold by the homeless is now focusing on the paper's founder Hutchinson Persons, who quit last week, a spokesman said Saturday. New York Newsday, quoting sources, reported that employees of Street News have told the attorney general's office that as much as $250,000 is unaccounted for.

"We received reports that he may have left and may have taken money with him. We're investigating those charges," said a spokesman for Mr. Abrams.

A message left on Mr. Persons's answering machine wasn't immediately returned, but he and others have said repeatedly that those who started the paper six months ago worked for little or no money until he accepted a salary of less than $50,000 early this year.

Wendy Koltun, the paper's co-founder who left Street News in January in a disagreement over Mr. Persons's management style, said she was confident the allegations that Mr. Persons took money were false.

She said she believes the rumors about him taking money began with a disgruntled employee who wanted to start her own newspaper.

Street News began publishing in November. It has been sold on streets and subways around Manhattan by homeless people who keep 50 cents of the 75 cents for each sale.
