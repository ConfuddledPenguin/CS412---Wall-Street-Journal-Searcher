AIG to Buy Fischbach In Exchange of Stock Valued at $44 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/11/90

NEW YORK -- American International Group Inc. said it agreed to acquire Fischbach Corp. through a subsidiary in an exchange of stock valued at $11 a Fischbach share, or about $44 million. AIG, a New York-based insurer, disclosed last month that it was considering such a purchase. The company is responsible for a large portion of the performance bonds for Fischbach, an electrical and mechanical contracting concern.

About $600 million of performance bonds have been issued to the New York-based contractor. The acquisition is widely viewed as an effort by AIG to protect itself from potentially large performance-bond claims.

In connection with the transaction, AIG said it exercised an option to acquire, at $11 a share, the 54% stake in Fischbach held by APL Corp., which is controlled by Miami financier Victor Posner.

AIG said APL held a total of 2,068,370 shares of Fischbach common, as well as about $6.5 million principal amount of Fischbach convertible subordinated debt.

In a statement filed with the Securities and Exchange Commission, AIG disclosed that it also formed a business relationship with Peter Kiewit & Sons Inc., a privately held construction company based in Omaha, Neb., that is expected to provide management consulting services to Fischbach.
