Californian Convicted Of Fraud in Mortgage Investment Scheme

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/11/90

LOS ANGELES -- A federal jury convicted David Arnold Feldman, 50 years old, of Palos Verdes Estates, Calif., of defrauding financial institutions, including BankAmerica Corp., of about $144 million in a mortgage investment scheme. Mr. Feldman was convicted on three counts of mail fraud, but was acquitted of two other counts. He faces as long as 15 years in prison. Sentencing by Judge Dickran Tevrizian is scheduled for Aug. 20.

Two other principal figures in the mortgage fraud pleaded guilty earlier this year to reduced charges of mail fraud in the case. They are Ken Borden Rogers, 50, of Huntington Beach, and George Charles Ash Jr., 45, of Alta Loma. The three were indicted last year for scheming to sell what proved to be practically worthless mortgage loans to 20 banks and savings and loan associations outside California.

The men had promised high interest rates and had assured investors that their investments were protected by independent underwriting policies, among other safeguards.

Most of the loans later went into default, and Bank of America, as the trustee for most of the mortgage securities pools, repurchased them and took a total of $95 million in charges against the 1984 earnings of its parent, BankAmerica Corp.

When the three men were indicted by a federal grand jury last year, prosecutors called the case one of the largest single frauds ever perpetrated in the savings and loan business.

Mr. Feldman had been free on bond but was ordered returned to custody to await sentencing.
