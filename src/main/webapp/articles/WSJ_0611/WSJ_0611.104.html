PS of New Hampshire Posts $24 Million Loss In Pension Investment

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/11/90

MANCHESTER, N.H. -- Public Service of New Hampshire said it had a loss of $24 million in one of its three pension plan investment portfolios during the last two years. The utility company said it will have to pay more into the fund in future years to keep up the asset level. The company also said that if no recoveries are made, the annual pension expense will increase by about $1 million annually, from $8.5 million. This increase would not affect customers' bills, the company added.

The losses have been offset by gains in Public Service's other pension portfolios; the overall pension plan has assets of about $100 million, the company said.

Public Service said its pension plan's assets are sufficient to fund all of its obligations to its employees and retirees.

Public Service has initiated a review of its investments that resulted in the losses, and the company will determine whether any additional remedies are available to recover them.
