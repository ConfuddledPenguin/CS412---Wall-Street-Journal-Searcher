Mercapital Agrees To Take 40% Stake In Brokerage Firm

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/11/90

MADRID -- Mercapital S.A., a Spanish financial company 25% owned by Cie. Financiere de Suez of France, said it will take a 40% stake in Spanish brokerage firm Usera & Morenes. The accord, reached after several weeks of talks, is designed to strengthen Mercapital's position in the financial-services market, a spokesman said.

It also reflects the growing difficulties for independent brokerage firms in Spain's increasingly competitive stock market.

Usera & Morenes, set up last year as a partnership between three stockbrokers at the time of changes on Spain's stock market, was for much of last year one of Spain's market leaders. But it ran into problems in relation to an inquiry by the Spanish National Stock Market Commission into irregularities allegedly committed at the time of a stock offering by oil company Repsol S.A. last year.

Though no sanctions have been announced against Usera & Morenes, the negative publicity caused by the allegations led to a fall in its business in the first quarter of this year. At the same time, staffing problems forced it to slash staff by around 50%, contributing to a first-quarter loss of 212 million pesetas ($2 million).

Following its acquisition, the brokerage firm will become a direct subsidiary of Mercapital's banking unit, Bancapital. The price of the purchase will depend on an audit of the firm currently in process, Mercapital said. Bancapital is 35% owned by Mercapital and has four offices, in Madrid, Barcelona, Bilbao and Valencia.
