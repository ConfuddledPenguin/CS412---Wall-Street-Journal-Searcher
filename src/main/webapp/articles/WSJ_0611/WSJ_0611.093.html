New Securities Issues

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/11/90

The following were among Friday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATE

Great Western Bank -- $150 million of subordinated notes, due June 15, 2000, was priced with a 10 1/4% coupon at 99.96 to yield 10.256%. The notes, which are non-callable for 8 3/4 years, were priced at a spread of 180 basis points above the Treasury's 10-year note. Rated Baa-1 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and Single-A-minus by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Shearson Lehman Hutton Inc.

Cleveland Electric Illuminating Co. -- $100 million of first mortgage bonds, due June 1, 2020, was priced with a 10% coupon at 98.825 to yield 10.125%. Rated Baa-2 by Moody's and Triple-B-minus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Morgan Stanley & Co. The bonds, which are non-callable for five years, were priced at a spread of 170 basis points above the Treasury's 30-year bond.

MORTGAGES

Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $300 million of Remic mortgage securities was offered in five classes by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets. The offering, Series 181, is backed by Freddie Mac's 8 1/2% securities.

EUROBONDS

Outokumpu Finance B.V. (Finland) -- 200 million Finnish markka of 13 1/2% Eurobonds due Aug. 6, 1993, priced at 101.375 to yield 13.5% via Postipankki. Guaranteed by Outokumpu Oy. Fees 1 3/8.
