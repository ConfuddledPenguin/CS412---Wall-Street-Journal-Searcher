Jury Awards $5,270,000 To Mother in Toyota Case

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/11/90

TORRANCE, Calif. (AP) -- A faulty Toyota pickup design was cited as a jury awarded $5,270,000 to a woman for injuries suffered in a 1984 accident. Officials with Toyota Motor Sales U.S.A. Inc., a subsidiary of Toyota Motor Sales Ltd., said the award was one of the largest ever imposed against the company. Attorneys for the company said an appeal would be filed.

The Superior Court jury also awarded $100,000 to the two daughters of Cynthia Gosper, 35 years old, of Garden Grove, Calif., who was left a quadriplegic from the accident. The daughters suffered minor injuries in the same accident.

Jurors found that a design flaw in the 1981 4-wheel-drive SR5 pickup Mrs. Gosper was driving allowed it to roll off a highway after it was struck from behind. The driver's side door popped open, throwing Mrs. Gosper from the truck.

Toyota attorneys insisted that the truck's design is safe and that the force of the collision caused it to roll over.

Sam Cole, an analyst with the Center for Auto Safety, a Washington-based consumer group, praised the outcome. "I think the jury was correct in their decision that the vehicle was designed in an unstable manner," he said.
