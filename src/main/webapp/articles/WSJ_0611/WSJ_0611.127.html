Lafarge Coppee S.A. Reaches Agreement With East Germans

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/11/90

PARIS -- Lafarge Coppee S.A., the world's second-largest cement company, said it reached an investment and cooperation agreement with East Germany's largest cement concern, Karsdorf. Under the agreement, France's Lafarge said it will help modernize Karsdorf's factory and, if approved by the East German government, take a "significant" stake in the company. Lafarge didn't say how large the stake would be.

The announcement marks one of the first significant French investments in East Germany.

East German officials have announced a series of joint ventures involving West German interests, and have expressed disappointment that French concerns weren't more active in East Germany.

Lafarge said Karsdorf is located near Leipzig and produced roughly one-third of East Germany's 1989 cement output of 12 million metric tons.

Lafarge said its own annual cement production capacity is 50 million metric tons, located primarily in France, Spain and North America. A metric ton is 2,204.62 pounds.
