Credit Ratings: Walt Disney Notes Are Rated Single-A By Standard & Poor's

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 06/11/90

Standard & Poor's Corp. said it gave a Single-A rating to Walt Disney Co.'s $1.5 billion face amount of subordinated liquid-yield option notes, due 2005. The rating agency also affirmed Disney's A-1 commercial paper ratings. Standard & Poor's said that the company has substantially increased capital expenditures in recent years. But it said the proposed financing and expansion projects aren't expected to significantly alter Disney's capital structure. "Moreover, the company is likely to be able to maintain returns on assets and permanent capital at well above average levels, even if some future investments offer modestly lower returns than now prevail."

Walt Disney issued the notes to help pay for the multibillion-dollar expansion of its U.S. theme parks and resorts over the next decade.

A spokesman for Walt Disney, Burbank, Calif., said: "We're pleased with the rating. It's what we expected."
