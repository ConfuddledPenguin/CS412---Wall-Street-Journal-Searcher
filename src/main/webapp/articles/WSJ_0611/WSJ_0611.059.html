CHASE MANHATTAN BANK isn't one of the institutions that count money in bank-advised mutual funds toward the minimum balance necessary for free checking, as was reported in Friday's edition. (See: "Your Money Matters: Bank-Lobby Shopping for Mutual Funds May Be Convenient, but at What Price?" -- WSJ June 8, 1990)

---

URCARCO Inc. sold five million shares at $19.875 each on May 31, but went public last November at $11 a share. Friday's OTC Focus column incorrectly identified the May stock sale as an initial public offering.

(See: "OTC Focus: Market Ends Lower on Profit-Taking; Newport News Savings Bank Falls 1 to 3" -- WSJ June 8, 1990)

---

U.S. CAR AND TRUCK OUTPUT for May fell 4.3% to 1,007,112 vehicles from a year ago. Truck output for the month rose 2% to 379,660 vehicles. These figures were misstated in Friday's edition.

(See: "U.S. Auto Makers Set 2.3% Rise in Output For Current Week" -- WSJ June 8, 1990)
