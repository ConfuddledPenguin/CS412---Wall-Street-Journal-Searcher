GND Holdings Loss Widened in Quarter And the Fiscal Year

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/11/90

WAYNE, N.J. -- GND Holdings Corp., parent of Grand Union Co., said its net loss in the fiscal fourth quarter widened as depreciation and amortization of assets increased following the purchase of the supermarket chain. For the 12 weeks ended March 31, the loss widened to $14.4 million from $12.4 million a year earlier. Sales grew 2.5% to $653.1 million from $637 million.

For the year, the net loss widened to $53.8 million from $14.9 million. Sales rose 7.3% to $2.92 billion from $2.72 billion. For the year, earnings before depreciation and amortization, last-in, first-out accounting provisions, unusual items, interest and taxes rose 20% to $170 million from $141.5 million. Total interest expense increased to $136.7 million from $95.3 million.

GND, whose principals include the New York investment firms of Miller Tabak Hirsch & Co. and Salomon Inc., acquired Grand Union in July in a leveraged buy-out. GND said that for comparative purposes, results of GND for the 36 weeks following the purchase were combined with results of GU Acquisition Corp., the predecessor company, for the 16 weeks before the acquisition. The combined results are compared with those of GU Acquisition for the year ended April 1, 1989.

Earnings for the year before LIFO provision, depreciation and amortization, unusual items, interest and income taxes, increased 14% to $39.5 million from $34.8 million. GND said results below the line of earnings before LIFO provision, depreciation and amortization, unusual items, interest and income taxes, aren't comparable on a year-to-year basis, because of the acquisition of Grand Union last July.

Interest expense in the quarter rose to $35.6 million from $24.5 million.

The purchase agreement last year called for the Miller Tabak-Salomon group to pay $60 million for an 80% stake, and for management to pay $15 million for the remainder. The buyers also assumed about $300 million in existing debt, and said they would refinance $545 million in bank debt and issue about $325 million in new, high-yield junk bonds.
