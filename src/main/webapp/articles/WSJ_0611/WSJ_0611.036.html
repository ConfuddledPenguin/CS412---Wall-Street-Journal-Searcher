International: Portugal Unveils Program To Cut Deficit, Inflation

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 06/11/90

LISBON -- The government unveiled plans to slash the public deficit and lower inflation, in a bid to prevent Portugal from falling behind as the European Community drives toward economic and monetary union. Finance Minister Miguel Beleza said the economic adjustment plan would cut public-sector spending and widen the indirect tax net. Some indirect tax rates may be raised, he said.

Economists have warned that Portugal's large public-sector deficit has overheated the economy, boosting inflation and damaging Portugal's chances of meeting EC deadlines for monetary integration. The government forecasts the 1990 deficit will reach about 610 billion escudos ($4.09 billion). Inflation in April was running at an annual rate of 12.9%, about double the EC average.

Mr. Beleza said the adjustment program will aim to halve the public-sector deficit in five years to 3.5% of gross domestic product. Inflation will fall to well below 5% by 1995, he said.

Portugal must fully participate in EC plans for economic and monetary union, to influence the shape of the 12-nation trade block, he said.

He restated Portugal's commitment to place the escudo in the exchange-rate mechanism of the EC's European Monetary System soon, but insisted the adhesion would have to be handled cautiously. The exchange-rate mechanism fixes nine EC currencies at a set rate against the European currency unit, and permits them to fluctuate within fixed limits on either side of that rate. Portugal, Greece and Britain are the only EC nations outside the mechanism.
