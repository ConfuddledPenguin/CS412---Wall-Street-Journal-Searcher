What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/11/90

TRUMP'S MAJOR bankers agreed tentatively to lend him $50 million more and to let him suspend interest payments on about $2 billion of debt. The developer would pledge as security some of his remaining uncollateralized assets and would possibly forfeit equity in other holdings. The accord, which needs the approval of smaller creditors, would give Trump time to improve his operations and wait out a real estate slump. ---

The Fed is looking into possible irregularities in the handling and trading of U.S. bank loans to Latin America. Improprieties may include the use of privileged information.

---

Real estate seized from failed thrifts could be sold by the government in scores of $500 million chunks under a plan S&L bailout officials are developing to speed the sale process. Critics of the idea say the government would be forced to offer huge price discounts to attract purchasers.

---

Ames Department Stores plans to close nearly a third of its stores and cut its staff by 18,000, or 36%. The closings in the Midwest and the South will leave Ames, now in Chapter 11, concentrated in the Northeast.

---

General Dynamics and McDonnell Douglas face probable second-quarter write-offs because of delays in developing the Navy's A-12 attack airplane. The Navy is reviewing the project.

---

Boeing is expected to receive today a nearly $4 billion order from Korean Airlines for 23 jumbo jets. The deal would be Boeing's third giant order from Asia in the past few weeks.

---

American Air won a U.S. recommendation that it fly a coveted route from Chicago to Tokyo. The route could generate $300 million in annual revenue. The tentative selection is a blow to Chicago-based United Air.

---

Stock prices declined Friday for the fourth day in a row. The profit-taking focused on entertainment, retailing and financial issues. The Dow Jones Industrial Average tumbled 34.95 points to close at 2862.38.

---

U.S. brokerage firms paid a record $82,714 in compensation per employee in 1989, despite a 26% drop in profits.

---

Delays in planting grain because of the Midwest's wettest spring in 50 years are leading some economists to predict worsening food inflation.

---

The Resolution Trust Corp. faces a test in federal court this week over its ability to override state laws in the sale of insolvent S&Ls to banks.

Lewis Ranieri bought the failed Murray Federal S&L, expanding his Texas thrift empire into Dallas.

---

CBS is negotiating to produce programming for cable's Discovery Channel. CBS is the only major broadcast network without a cable venture.

---

Pinnacle West's Arizona Public Service utility unit said it will slash its work force further and revamp operations to hold down its rates.
