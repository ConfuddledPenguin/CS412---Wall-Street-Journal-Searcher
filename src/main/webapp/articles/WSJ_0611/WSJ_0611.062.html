New Stock Listings

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/11/90

NEW YORK -- The American Stock Exchange announced one new listing and the National Market System of the over-the-counter market announced five initial public offerings.

On the Amex, Gamma Biologicals Inc., a Houston maker of diagnastic instruments, previously traded over-the-counter, began trading under the symbol GBL.

On the OTC market, Fleer Corp., a Philadelphia marketer of sports picture cards and confectionary products, began trading under FLER. Gensia Pharmaceuticals, a San Diego maker of products for cardiovascular disease treatments, began trading under GNSA. K-Swiss Inc., a Pacoima, Calif., maker of athletic footwear, began trading under KSWS. Pioneer Fed BanCorp, a Honolulu holding company for Pioneer Federal Savings Bank, began trading under PFBC. Workingmens Capital, a Bloomington, Ind., holding company for Workingmens Federal Savings & Loan Association, began trading under WCHI.

The Pacific Stock Exchange began trading options on Franklin Resources Inc., a San Mateo, Calif., investment management firm, under BEN; on PacifiCorp, a Portland, Ore., energy, mining and telephone services company, under PPW; and on Software Publishing Corp., a Mountain View, Calif., computer software maker. Software Publishing options also began trading at the Philadelphia Stock Exchange.
