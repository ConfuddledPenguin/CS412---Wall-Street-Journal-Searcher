International Brief -- Yaskawa Electric Manufacturing: Fiscal-Year Pretax Earnings Rose 39% to $61.1 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/11/90

Yaskawa Electric Manufacturing Co., a leading Japanese maker of motors, said its consolidated pretax profit for the year ended March 20 increased 39% to 9.33 billion yen ($61.1 million) from 6.73 billion yen a year earlier. Net income rose 24% to 3.71 billion yen, or 16.18 yen a share, from 2.99 billion yen, or 13.20 yen a share.

Sales increased 16% to 201.26 billion yen from 173.54 billion yen.

Sales of robots and related equipment grew 20% to 100.516 billion yen, helped by brisk demand for industrial robots. Manufacturing systems sales rose 10% to 32.95 billion yen, because of increased investment in facilities by the steel and the paper and pulp industries. Sales of heavy electric machinery increased 7.9% to 62.89 billion yen.

Yaskawa predicted consolidated pretax profit for fiscal 1991 will increase to 10.2 billion yen, an indicated rise of 9.3%. Net income is expected to rise to 4.2 billion yen, or 13%, and sales to 221 billion yen, or 9.8%.
