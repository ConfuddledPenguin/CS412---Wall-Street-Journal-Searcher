International: Saab Auto Venture Plans To Shut Three Parts Plants

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/11/90

STOCKHOLM -- Stung by ballooning losses in this year's first four months, Saab Automobile AB's new management moved to cut costs by closing three Scandinavian parts plants. The move launches an overhaul of Saab's manufacturing operations that was expected following General Motors Corp.'s purchase late last year of a 50% stake in the Swedish auto maker. The remaining stake is owned by Saab-Scania AB. In a separate phase of the overhaul, Saab directors approved a hefty investment program at the Nystad, Finland, plant where contract assembly of GM's Opel Calibra model will begin in March.

Saab said it plans to halt the manufacture of wiring harnesses at plants in Kramfors, Sweden, and Halden, Norway -- as well as seat production at two other Swedish facilities. Buying wiring harnesses and seats from outside suppliers could cost the jobs of as many as 1,400 workers, nearly 9% of the total work force, Saab said.

The moves would save an estimated 200 million Swedish kronor a year ($32.7 million). That's only about one-third of the 600 million kronor a year Saab says it plans to save on parts purchasing.

Such cost reductions took on added urgency as Saab disclosed its pretax loss in the four months grew to 995 million kronor from 450 million kronor a year ago. The company said it expects the "loss rate" to decrease during the rest of 1990.
