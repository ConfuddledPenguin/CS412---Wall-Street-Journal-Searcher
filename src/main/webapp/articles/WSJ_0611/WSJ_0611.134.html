International Brief -- Daihatsu Motor Co.: Consolidated Pretax Profit For Fiscal '90 Tumbled 45%

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/11/90

Daihatsu Motor Co., a Japanese auto maker, said its consolidated pretax earnings for the year ended March 31 tumbled 45% to 8.68 billion yen ($56.9 million) from 15.7 billion yen a year earlier. Net income plunged 78% to 1.47 billion yen from 6.67 billion yen.

Sales rose 1.7% to 766.07 billion yen from 753.1 billion yen.

Pretax earnings were hurt by growing marketing and administrative costs, which rose 5.4% to 120.7 billion yen, and by nonoperating costs, which surged 39% to 12.39 billion yen. Extraordinary gains shrank sharply to 336 million yen in fiscal 1990 from 3.92 billion yen a year earlier, when Daihatsu sold fixed assets.

Motor vehicle sales, which accounted for 78% of total sales, fell 1.2% to 599.22 billion yen, but its parts sales climbed 12% to 92.17 billion yen. Sales of the company's other products went up 15% to 74.69 billion yen.
