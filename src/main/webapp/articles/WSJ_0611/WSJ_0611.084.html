Michigan Court Grants Stay of Earlier Order In CMS Energy Case

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/11/90

DEARBORN, Mich. -- The Michigan Court of Appeals granted a stay Friday of an earlier state regulatory order blocking CMS Energy Corp. from completing the sale and leaseback of Midland Cogeneration Venture assets. The ruling was the final hurdle for the transaction, which is scheduled to close June 15. State utility regulators had been trying to stop the sale and leaseback, saying that unregulated CMS Energy was trying to move assets from its regulated Consumers Power Co. utility unit.

But while the court allowed the deal to take place, it also required Consumers Power to post a $450 million bond to cover possible customer refunds.

The Michigan Public Service Commission ordered the posting of the bond because it questioned whether CMS corporate restructuring and asset transfers violated a special 1985 order that allowed the utility higher rates to stave off financial collapse. CMS officials previously said they wouldn't post the bond, but a company spokesman said that position has been changed to permit the sale and leaseback to go through.

The appeals court also decided not to immediately take up the question about the state commission's jurisdiction in restricting the transfer or use of CMS interests in the cogeneration venture. CMS said the court said it will handle that issue "under normal appellate procedures."
