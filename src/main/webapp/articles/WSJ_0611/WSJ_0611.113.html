Business Brief -- Bio-Medicus Inc.: Medical-Gear Maker Accepts Medtronic's Sweetened Bid

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/11/90

Bio-Medicus Inc. said it agreed to be acquired by Medtronic Inc. under a sweetened bid involving an exchange of stock. Bio-Medicus will swap 3.15 shares of its common stock for each share of Medtronic. Initially, Medtronic proposed an exchange rate of 3.3 shares of Bio-Medicus for each of its shares.

Bio-Medicus is an Eden Prairie, Minn., maker of blood pumps used in surgery. Medtronic is a Minneapolis maker of biomedical devices.

The companies said the new exchange ratio depends on the price of Medtronic stock staying between $77.50 and $92. In New York Stock Exchange composite trading Friday, however, Medtronic shares closed at $76.25, down $3.375. In national over-the-counter trading, Bio-Medicus shares closed at $23.75, up 62.5 cents.

The final exchange ratio will be based on the closing price 10 days before the effective date, which is projected to be 75 to 120 days from now.

---

Corrections & Amplifications

MEDTRONIC Inc.'s bid for Bio-Medicus Inc. calls for Bio-Medicus holders to exchange 3.1 shares for one Medtronic share, subject to certain stock-price provisions. In yesterday's edition, the ratio was misstated.

(WSJ June 12, 1990)
