Business Brief -- Nintendo Co.: Request Is Denied for Order To Restrict Galoob's Sales

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/11/90

A federal judge in San Francisco denied a request by Nintendo of America Inc. for a temporary restraining order that would have kept Lewis Galoob Toys Inc. from selling a video-game device. Nintendo sought to stop Galoob from distributing its "Game Genie," a gadget that makes Nintendo games easier to play. Nintendo of America, a unit of Japan's Nintendo Co., filed a lawsuit against the South San Francisco company June 1, alleging that the Game Genie lets consumers infringe on Nintendo copyrights by altering Nintendo games.

In addition to filing for the temporary restraining order, Nintendo had sought an injunction. While denying the request for the restraining order, the judge scheduled a formal hearing for June 29 on Nintendo's request for a preliminary injunction.

"We believe very strongly that we don't infringe on anyone's rights," said Steven Klein, Galoob executive vice president.

Lynn Hvalsoe, Nintendo general counsel said the company achieved its objectives. "We went to court to get a hearing date . . . and we got it."
