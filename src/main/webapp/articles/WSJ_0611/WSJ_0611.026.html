Western Union Issues 9.6 Million Shares To Its Bank Lenders

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/11/90

NEW YORK -- Western Union Corp. issued 9.6 million of its common shares to its bank lenders to cover terms of its December 1987 financial restructuring program. Under that plan, which retired $227 million in bank debt, Western Union agreed to issue additional common to its 31 banks if its stock price fell below $2.05625 at Dec. 31, 1989.

In composite trading Friday on the New York Stock Exchange, Western Union shares closed unchanged at 50 cents.

The arrangement with the banks is similar to one that Western Union had with ITT Corp. Last month, Western Union issued an additional 7.8 million, or 9.02%, of its common shares to ITT under terms of its December 1987 agreement to buy ITT World Communications Inc. The agreement with ITT also carried the same $2.05625 trigger.

The new issue gave ITT 16.58% of Western Union's common, making it the second-largest owner next to New York financier Bennett S. LeBow, who owns 43.4%. By contrast, the block held by the banks is widely dispersed and difficult to count, according to a Western Union spokesman, who added that he wasn't sure how much of the company's stock banks currently hold.

Western Union plans to distribute the additional stock from a pool of 17.39 million new common shares that it registered with the Securities and Exchange Commission Friday.

The company spokesman said ITT will receive 7.8 million of the registered shares since it had originally taken its new stock in the form of so-called legend shares, which guarantee ownership but cannot be traded. Those 7.8 million legend shares now will convert to registered shares, he said.

Meanwhile, Western Union, which has been struggling to avoid a filing under bankruptcy laws, faces a big hurdle this Friday when it must pay $51 million of interest on its senior notes. Western Union has said it may be forced to default on the payment if it can't find sufficient funds to cover it. Annual interest payments at the company total $116 million.

To ease its debt burden, Western Union has proposed a plan, now awaiting SEC clearance, that would give new debt securities to its bondholders and reclassify its preferred stock into new common shares in exchange for more favorable terms on the $530 million debt from its notes.

The spokesman said so far there has been no progress on the exchange offer either from the SEC or bondholders.
