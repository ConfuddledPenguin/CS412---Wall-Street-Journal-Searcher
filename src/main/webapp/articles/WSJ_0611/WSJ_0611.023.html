Marketing & Media: E.W. Scripps Plans to End Operating Pact With Persis

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/11/90

CINCINNATI -- E.W. Scripps Co. said it reached an agreement in principle to terminate a joint operating agreement with Persis Corp. Scripps said the move would result in a second-quarter charge of $23 million, or 30 cents a share. In the year-ago second quarter, the media company earned $25.1 million, or 32 cents a share.

Scripps owns the Knoxville News-Sentinel; Persis, based in Honolulu, owns the Knoxville Journal. Under the joint operating agreement, the News-Sentinel provides sales, production and distribution for the Journal. After the agreement ends, the Journal intends to publish separately.

As part of the termination agreement, the News-Sentinel will pay Persis $40 million in lieu of payments the Journal would have received under the joint operating agreement from 1991 to 2005.
