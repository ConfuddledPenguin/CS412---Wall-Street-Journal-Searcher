Pinnacle West's Arizona Utility Plans More Cuts

  • Author: Earl C. Gottschalk Jr. and Sonia L. Nazario
  • Date: 06/11/90

Arizona Public Service Co., the main unit of Pinnacle West Capital Corp., said it will slash its work force further and will revamp operations to hold down its rates, which it conceded are among the highest in the utility industry. The proposed belt-tightening moves come in the wake of Pinnacle West's rejection of both PacifiCorp's $1.82 billion takeover bid and Salt River Project's $500 million proposal to buy some of the assets of Phoenix-based Arizona Public Service.

Pinnacle West suspended its 40-cent-a-share common stock dividend in October, and announced at its May 23 annual meeting that it probably won't resume payouts for another three years.

Arizona Public Service, which has already slashed its work force to 7,700 employees from 9,100, said that the current announced cuts would go beyond the 150 previously planned for 1990. A spokesman said the company hasn't determined the exact number yet. It also is attempting to reduce a 21%, three-year rate increase it had requested from the Arizona Corporation Commission beginning in 1991.

"We must reduce our costs to be around in the 1990s," the spokesman said.

Spokesmen at PacifiCorp and Salt River Project said the downsizing plans won't affect their bids. A spokesman for PacifiCorp, a Portland, Ore., utility, said "it's unfortunate that the company has to take such Draconian moves against their own employees."

Arizona Public Service said it has hired the Lexington, Mass., consulting firm of Temple, Barker & Sloan to assist in the cutbacks.

The Phoenix utility said it has been trying to cut costs since 1988 through the dismissals, cuts in capital expenditures and by encouraging out-of-state businesses to move into its utility territory.

The Arizona Public Service spokesman said the utility has been "under a multitude of pressures to cut costs."

PacifiCorp, which has made several recent hostile acquisition bids for Pinnacle West, said that if it took over the company it would increase utility rates 2% per year for four years. Arizona Public Service, the state's largest utility, had proposed annual rate increases of 6.5% between 1991 and 1994.

Salt River Project, Arizona's second largest electric utility, said in its offer for certain distribution and transmission facilities in the Phoenix area that it wouldn't raise rates in the acquired areas for five to seven years until those rates are equal to the lower Salt River rates. In the past two weeks, a Phoenix councilman has proposed that the city study the possibility of seizing some Arizona Public Service utility territory and giving the business to someone else, possibly Salt River Project, which serves areas surrounding Phoenix.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading, Pinnacle shares closed Friday at $15.875, off 25 cents.
