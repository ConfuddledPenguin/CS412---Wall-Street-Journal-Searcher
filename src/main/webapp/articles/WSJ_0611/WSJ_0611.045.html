German Monetary Reserves

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/11/90

FRANKFURT, West Germany -- West Germany's gross monetary reserves eased by 200 million marks to 102.6 billion marks ($60.6 billion) in the week ended May 31, the Bundesbank, the central bank, said. Foreign liabilities rose, however, by 100 million marks to 53.2 billion marks. The bank doesn't publish a figure for net monetary reserves. But after extrapolation from the available data, West German net reserves are indicated to have eased by 100 million marks to 49.5 billion marks in the latest week.

The reserve totals stabilized in the latest reporting week, after a sharp 1.4 billion-mark increase in the week ended May 23, when the central bank's coffers were swollen by interest income received from foreign investments, primarily its holdings of U.S. Treasury notes.
