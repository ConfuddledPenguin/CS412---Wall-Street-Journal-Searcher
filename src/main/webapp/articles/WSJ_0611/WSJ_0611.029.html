Law: Skadden Arps, Paris Firm Reach Pact for Association

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/11/90

PARIS -- New York law firm Skadden, Arps, Slate, Meagher & Flom announced plans for an association with Bredin, Prat, Saint-Esteben, Grandjean & Morabia, a corporate-law specialist here. The two law firms said they don't plan to merge. But because they don't have offices in each other's home cities, they will refer business to each other and cooperate closely.

Bredin Prat of Paris also said that as soon as statutory changes here permit -- probably in September of 1991 -- it will merge with a new U.S.-led group of lawyers in Paris, creating one of this city's first Franco-American firms.

The U.S. lawyers, formerly with the Paris office of the U.S. firm Coudert Brothers, will begin using Bredin Prat's offices and working closely with the French firm as of July 1. Until the merger, they will remain a legally separate entity, to be called Schepard, Baxter & Associes.

Two months ago, two other Paris-based firms, one U.S.-led and the other French, announced merger plans, creating what would be one of Paris's largest firms. S.G. Archibald, one of the country's oldest non-French firms, and Courtois Bouloy Lebel & Associes, a large French firm, said they will merge once the law permits. They too will work closely together while they wait.
