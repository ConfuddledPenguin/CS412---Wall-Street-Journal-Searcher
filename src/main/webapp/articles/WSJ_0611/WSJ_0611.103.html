Business Brief -- Texas Instruments Inc.: Jobs for Defense to Be Cut By 1,000 Over 12 Months

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/11/90

Texas Instruments Inc. said it will reduce its defense systems work force by about 1,000 employees over the next year in response to reduced defense spending. The Dallas-based semiconductor and defense concern said that all its Defense Systems and Electronics Group sites, which employ about 22,000 people, will be affected by the decision.

Texas Instruments said it expects the overall financial impact from the reductions to be minimal.

The company also announced it agreed with a unit of the Netherlands' N.V. Philips to swap designs for a group of computer chips called bus-interface logics.

Under the agreement, Texas Instruments and Philips Components-Signetics will make and sell each other's bus-interface chips, which act as go-betweens in computers, allowing chips to communicate with each other and to outside equipment such as telephone lines.

Texas Instruments estimates the world market for bus-interface chips at about $850 million.
