GM's Electronics, Aircraft Units Expect Revenue Increases

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/11/90

KOKOMO, Ind. -- The two businesses that make up General Motors Corp.'s GM Hughes Electronics Corp. division said they expect revenue will reach a combined $11.8 billion, up from $11.4 billion a year ago. The Hughes Aircraft Co. unit said it expects 1990 revenue to rise to about $7.5 billion from $7.4 billion a year earlier, while GM's Delco Electronics unit projected its revenues will reach $4.3 billion, up from $4 billion in 1989.

Hughes Aircraft President D. Kenneth Richardson said that Hughes was projecting sales of $10 billion "in a few years."

"In order to do so, we plan to maintain our position in the military market, but grow rapidly in other areas," Mr. Richardson said. "We expect the ratio of military to non-military will change from the present 75% to 25%, to become 60% to 40%."

Donald J. Almquist, chairman, president and chief executive officer of Delco Electronics, said the unit's long-range business plan calls for sales of more than $6 billion in 1995.

Although GM car sales have been dropping in recent years, Mr. Almquist said Delco Electronics sales have been rising "because of the rapidly increasing electronic content in motor vehicles and because of increasing sales to non-GM customers."

Today the average electronic content per GM North American vehicle is about $650. Mr. Almquist said that average could reach $1,500 by 1995.

Officials from the two GM Hughes operations made their projections during a media tour of Delco Electronics' facilities. Delco also announced it formed a research and development program to work on vehicle communications systems with a unit of Hughes Aircraft. Delco said it expects the first products from the new program to be cellular-based equipment being developed with Motorola Inc.
