International Brief -- Occidental Petroleum Corp.: Firm, 3 Others Are Said To Agree to Soviet Project

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/11/90

Four companies from Japan, Italy and the U.S. agreed to build a $7 billion petrochemical project in the Soviet Union in the largest-ever Soviet-Western joint venture, Japanese news reports said. The companies are Marubeni Corp., a major Japanese trading house; Occidental Petroleum Corp. of the U.S. and Italian chemical companies Montedison S.p.A. and Enichem.

They agreed with the Soviet Petroleum Industry Ministry to produce petrochemical products using crude oil from Tengiz, an oil field just north of the Caspian Sea, the reports said.

An official at Marubeni declined to comment, saying he has no knowledge of the project.

The four companies will invest 49% and the Soviet side will finance the remainder of the project, the reports said.
