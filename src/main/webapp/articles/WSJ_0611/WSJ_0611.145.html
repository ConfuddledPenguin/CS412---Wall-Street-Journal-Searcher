REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: Market Civilization

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/11/90

Future historians may pinpoint last Friday as the date communism finally conceded the rhetorical war. With a visiting Margaret Thatcher sitting beside him and nodding in approval, Mikhail Gorbachev told a live, televised news conference in Moscow that the creation of a market economy in the Soviet Union was "essential." When reminded of criticism that he was abandoning communism, Mr. Gorbachev went further still. "The market is not an invention of capitalism," the Soviet leader declared. "It has existed for centuries. It is an invention of civilization."
