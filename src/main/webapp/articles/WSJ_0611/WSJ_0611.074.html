Technology: Cypress Semiconductor Sets Accord With Soviet Group

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/11/90

SAN JOSE, Calif. -- Cypress Semiconductor Corp. said a Soviet technology group agreed to grant the company a license to make chips designed by the group and to use the groups' manufacturing technology. Soviet chip-making technology is thought to be as much as 10 years behind that of the U.S. and Japan. But Cypress President T.J. Rodgers said the Soviets have some excellent chip designs and manufacturing processes, such as waste disposal, that look promising on paper. Technology constraints have forced bright Soviet engineers to build more efficient circuits than those of Western chip makers, he said.

"Their tech is 1978 but they've got a lot of frustrated, very talented people" who have ideas and designs that could translate into competitive chips if made in the U.S., Mr. Rodgers said.

Representatives from the Soviet group visited Cypress's headquarters last week and showed the company a list of Soviet-designed chips, including chips that process voice signals and perform functions needed in high-definition television, Mr. Rodgers said. Under the agreement, Cypress will put $100,000 in an escrow account from which the Soviet group, the International Center for Informatics and Electronics, will withdraw royalties.
