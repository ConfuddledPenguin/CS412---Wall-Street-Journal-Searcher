Henley Modifies Plan To Spin Off 2 Units

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/11/90

HAMPTON, N.H. -- Henley Group Inc. said it modified its plan to reorganize and spin off its two largest operating units as separate companies. Henley said it decided to distribute 35% of the shares of Pneumo Abex Corp., an aerospace parts company, instead of 100% as originally proposed. It said the planned distribution of all of the shares of Fisher International Inc., formerly Fisher Scientific Group Inc., a laboratory equipment maker, won't be affected.

The revisions are designed to benefit Henley shareholders by simplifying the financing arrangements in the reorganization plan, the company said.

Henley, spun off from Allied-Signal Corp. four years ago with 35 different businesses that didn't fit in Allied's plans, has been reorganizing and spinning off companies in an effort to increase shareholder value.

After the distributions, Henley would continue to hold a company that makes brakes, a methanol plant in Chile, 14.7 million shares of Itel Corp., and certain other investments. The company said it expects to liquidate or dispose of those assets during 1991.

Corrections & Amplifications

HENLEY GROUP Inc.'s Pneumo Abex Corp. subsidiary will continue to own its brakes division after Henley distributes 35% of Pneumo Abex to shareholders. Henley, which dropped an earlier plan to spin off 100% of Pneumo Abex to shareholders, would have purchased the brakes division from Pneumo Abex under the earlier plan, Henley said, clarifying a story in yesterday's edition.

(WSJ June 12, 1990)
