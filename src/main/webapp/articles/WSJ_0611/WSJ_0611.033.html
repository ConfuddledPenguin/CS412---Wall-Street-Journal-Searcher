International: Elf Aquitane Lifts Notice Of Force Majeure in Gabon

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/11/90

LONDON -- France's Societe Nationale Elf Aquitaine, one of the major crude oil producers in the West African state of Gabon, lifted the force majeure notice it issued May 28 on loadings of crude from the country's main oil port of Port Gentil, industry sources said. People close to the companies which had received the force majeure notice said the loading ban had been lifted about the middle of last week, and that new loading dates for cargos affected by the move had been received.

Elf imposed the force majeure notice, which informed buyers of crude from the company that original loading plans would have to be canceled, following rioting in Port Gentil to protest the government of Omar Bongo.

During the disturbances, French troops were sent to Gabon. Many oil industry personnel and dependents were evacuated and oil production in the country was considerably reduced.

Oil output has now apparently recovered, industry officials said.

The other main oil-field operator in Gabon, Royal Dutch/Shell Group, didn't issue force majeure notices during the disturbances, and cargos loading from Shell's Gamba loading terminal were only slightly delayed, the sources said.

Gabon produces fairly light, sweet crude oil which is popular with U.S. and European refiners. Its total oil production is estimated at about 260,000 to 280,000 barrels a day.
