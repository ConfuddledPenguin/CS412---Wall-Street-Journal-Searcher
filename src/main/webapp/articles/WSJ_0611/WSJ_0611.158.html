Macy Indicates A Profit Decline For Fiscal Year

  • Author: Jeffrey A. Trachtenberg
  • Date: 06/11/90

NEW YORK -- R.H. Macy & Co. earnings before interest, taxes and depreciation will be down 20% for the fiscal year ending July 31, according to company projections. The information, contained in a report that Macy disclosed to various members of the financial community, shows that earnings before taxes, interest and depreciation will drop to $743.2 million from $925.9 million a year earlier. Sales are expected to increase 6.1% to nearly $7.40 billion from about $6.97 billion a year earlier.

The profit decline reflects the retailer's disastrous Christmas quarter, during which earnings before interest, taxes and depreciation fell to $210 million from $391.5 million, a decrease of 46%.

For the fourth quarter, ending July 31, Macy sales are expected to climb about 7% to $1.70 billion from $1.59 billion a year earlier. Earnings before interest, taxes and depreciation are expected to gain 6.7% to $166 million against $155.6 million.

At the same time, Macy projected an upbeat look for the 12 months ending Feb. 2, 1991. During that period the company believes earnings before interest, taxes and depreciation will climb 35% to $980.1 million from $726.6 million, while sales will increase 4.1% to $7.52 billion from $7.22 billion.

In recent weeks, several credit-related companies tightened their policies on merchandise shipped to Macy.

In a speech last week, Edward Finkelstein, chairman, said there have been reports that Macy may consider the sale of non-operating assets or an exchange offer, or seek a new investor. "We are actively examining various opportunities to reduce and/or refinance our debt," said Mr. Finkelstein. He emphasized that Macy has ample liquidity to meet its obligations.
