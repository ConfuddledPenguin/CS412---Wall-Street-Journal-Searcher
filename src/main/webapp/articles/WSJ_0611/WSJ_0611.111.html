International Brief -- American Telephone & Telegraph: Company Sells Partial Stake In Italian Holding Company

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/11/90

American Telephone & Telegraph Co. has sold part of its shareholding in Cie. Industriali Riunite S.p.A. to Sprind Gestione S.p.A., another company controlled by the Italian financier Carlo De Benedetti. Angelo Abbondio, chairman of Sprind Gestione, said in an interview with the financial weekly Mondo Economico that Sprind had agreed to buy AT&T's 91 million non-convertible savings shares for about 250 billion lire ($200.9 million).

AT&T acquired the stake in Mr. De Benedetti's key industrial holding company last July in a swap against its 22% stake in Ing. C. Olivetti & Co. S.p.A., the computer manufacturer controlled by CIR.

The U.S. telecommunications giant will continue to hold 91 million ordinary shares in CIR, representing 18.6% of its ordinary capital and worth about 520 billion lire.

Sprind Gestione is the management company for the mutual funds of Finanza & Futuro S.p.A., an investment house controlled by Mr. De Benedetti.
