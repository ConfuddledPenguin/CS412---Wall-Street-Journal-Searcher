Transamerica Agrees To Buy Unit of First Interstate Bancorp

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/11/90

SAN FRANCISCO -- Transamerica Corp. said it agreed to acquire First Interstate Bancorp's consumer-lending operation for $108 million, in what is part of First Interstate's effort to shore up battered earnings. Under the agreement, expected to close in July, Transamerica will acquire First Interstate's Nova Financial Services operation, which had about $430 million in receivables on March 31. First Interstate said it expects a pretax gain of more than $90 million from the sale.

The gain on the sale was higher than some analysts expected. "The level of profit is good," said analyst Donald Crowley at Keefe, Bruyette & Woods, who expected the unit to sell for $70 million to $80 million. Selling the Nova unit was the biggest part of First Interstate's divestment strategy in which, he said, "They're trying to get rid of what they consider nonstrategic assets." First Interstate put Nova up for sale last March and is also trying to sell its Los Angeles headquarters building.

The accord expands Transamerica's finance group, which provided about half of the company's 1989 net earnings of $332 million on revenue of $6.8 billion. Transamerica, with $30.3 billion in assets, had $3.1 billion in finance receivables outstanding on March 31. Transamerica said the Nova acquisition will increase its total assets by about 5% to $9.5 billion.

In trading on the New York Stock Exchange Friday, Transamerica's stock closed at $39.375, down 62.5 cents; First Interstate's shares closed at $42.375, down 50 cents.
