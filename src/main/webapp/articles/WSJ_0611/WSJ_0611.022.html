Singapore Telecom Outlays

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/11/90

SINGAPORE -- Singapore Telecom will spend $1.45 billion in the next five years on infrastructure, Koh Boon Hwee, chairman of the state-owned company's statutory board said Friday. A total of $342.1 million will be spent on switching equipment, $402.1 million on transmission plant and equipment, and $297.2 million on underwater cable systems, Mr. Koh said.

In 1994, the national telephone switching network will be fully digitalized, leading to better transmission and quality telephone service, he added. "By next year, the capacity of our international telephone exchanges will be expanded from 4,500 to 10,000 circuits. Plans have also been made for a third satellite earth station at Seletar. It will be ready by 1995," Mr. Koh said.

In the past five years Telecom has spent $972 million to build up telecommunication infrastructure.
