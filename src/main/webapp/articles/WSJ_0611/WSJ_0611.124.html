Quick & Reilly Boosts Projection of Charges Tied to Acquisitions

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/11/90

NEW YORK -- Discount broker Quick & Reilly Group Inc. boosted, to 44 cents a share from 34 cents, its projection of the acquisition-related charges it will take for the fiscal year ending Feb. 28. The company had made the 34-cent estimate in late March, but it said last week that that number reflects only the costs of the acquisitions by its JJC Specialist Corp. The revised charge reflects costs of all of Quick & Reilly's acquisitions for fiscal 1991.

The estimated charges are net of any tax benefits the company might receive from the acquisitions.

Quick & Reilly also expects to report that per-share earnings for the first quarter, ended May 25, were flat at 31 cents. Earnings for the year-earlier period were $2.9 million on revenue of $24.4 million.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading Friday, Quick & Reilly fell 25 cents to $15.25.
