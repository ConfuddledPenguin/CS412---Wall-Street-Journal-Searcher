Business Brief -- SunTrust Banks Inc.: Second-Quarter Net Rose 3% After Reserve Increase

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/11/90

SunTrust Banks Inc., Atlanta, reported a 3% increase in second-quarter net income, to $88.5 million, or 69 cents a share, reflecting an additional provision in its loan-loss reserve. In 1989's second quarter, SunTrust had net of $85.9 million, or 66 cents a share.

As reported, a special examination by the Office of the Comptroller of the Currency prompted the banking company to provide loan-loss reserves of $64.6 million for the quarter. That figure is $24.8 million more than the 1989 second quarter and $26.6 million more than the 1990 first quarter.

SunTrust said the negative effect of the additional provision on earnings was partially offset by two unrelated purchase accounting adjustments on earlier acquisitions totaling about $19 million.

The company, which has $31.3 billion in assets, said nonperforming assets totaled $503 million as of June 30, compared with $296.3 million a year earlier. The most recent figure includes $322.1 million in problem loans.

For the first six months of the year, net rose 3.8% to $176.4 million, or $1.38 a share, from $169.9 million, or $1.31 a share, the year earlier.
