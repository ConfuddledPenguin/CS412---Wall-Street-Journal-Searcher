Who's News: I-Flow Elects Earhart To Added Post of Chief

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/11/90

IRVINE, Calif. -- Donald M. Earhart, I-Flow Corp.'s recently named president, was elected to the additional post of chief executive officer and as a director. Mr. Earhart, 46 years old, is the third chief executive at I-Flow since the company went public Feb. 1. He succeeds Michael A. Pentopoulos, 44, who was named interim chief executive officer after cofounder Eric Brown stepped down in late February. Mr. Brown resigned because of "strategic differences with the board," an I-Flow executive said yesterday.

Mr. Earhart joined the company as president and chief operating officer in June. "I came on board with the promise that I would become CEO in a short period of time," Mr. Earhart said.

His appointment to the board expands it to five members. Mr. Pentopoulos resigned to pursue his business interests in a number of other medical ventures.

Prior to joining I-Flow, Mr. Earhart was vice president of Allergan Inc. and president of its optical division. He also spent seven years in various executive posts at Bausch & Lomb Inc.

I-Flow is developing a multi-drug infusion system for the home health-care market. Mr. Earhart said the company expects to have its first product available for sale by year end.
