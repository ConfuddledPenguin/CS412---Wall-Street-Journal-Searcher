Norwest Holds Talks On Possible Purchase Of Denver Bank Firm

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/11/90

MINNEAPOLIS -- Norwest Corp. is holding discussions with United Banks of Colorado Inc. over a possible acquisition of the Denver bank-holding concern. In a joint release, the companies said "no definitive agreement has been reached," and cautioned there is "no assurance" a deal will be struck.

Rumors the company might be the recipient of a bid sent United Banks of Colorado shares upward on unusually heavy volume yesterday, before trading was halted late in the session. The announcement came after the close of trading yesterday.

National over-the-counter trading in United Banks was halted late in the session. At that point, the stock was trading at $20, up 1 1/8 from Monday's close.

While the stock seems likely to climb today, yesterday's closing price values United Banks at about $306 million.

The state of Colorado will open to nationwide interstate banking in January. United Banks, the state's largest holding company with assets of $5.9 billion, disclosed in April that it has held talks with out-of-state banking companies interested in a possible acquisition aimed at gaining entry to the state. Norwest, with assets of about $26.7 billion, was among those identified by United Banks as discussing an acquisition.

Norwest currently has banking operations in Minnesota, Wisconsin, Indiana, Illinois, Iowa, Nebraska, North Dakota, South Dakota, Montana, and Arizona, and it recently reached a definitive agreement to acquire Wyoming National Bancorp., its first bank in that state. Norwest, which in May paid $215 million to acquire First Interstate Corp. of Wisconsin has made it clear that it intends to pursue further growth through acquisition.

United Banks of Colorado has a book value, or shareholders' equity, of $26.47 a share. Acquisitions of financially healthy financial institutions typically involve a price in excess of book value, which suggests that any Norwest proposal for United Banks would cost it upwards of $400 million.
