Oki Electric to Buy Technitron

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/11/90

TOKYO -- Oki Electric Industry Co., a maker of communications equipment, agreed to buy Technitron PLC of Britain, a computer-printer marketing company, for #25 million ($45.2 million), company officials said, and a final agreement is likely to be signed in August. Approval by Technitron shareholders is needed. The proposed acquisition is part of Oki's plan to expand its business in the European Community prior to the region's market unification by the end of 1992. Technitron, which is capitalized at #15 million and has about 500 employees, had 1989 sales of #77 million.

Since 1977, Technitron has marketed Oki's printers in Britain, Italy and Scandinavia and these account for more than 70% of Technitron's printer sales. The Technitron purchase would enable Oki Electric to capture about 80% of printer sales in Europe through its own marketing networks. Oki already maintains three marketing units, one in Britain and two in West Germany.
