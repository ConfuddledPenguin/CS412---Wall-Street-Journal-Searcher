A&P Ends Capital To U.K. Firm, Citing Poor Management

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/11/90

NEW YORK -- Great Atlantic & Pacific Tea Co. said it won't commit any more capital to Isosceles PLC of the U.K., but would be willing to manage the supermarket company, with is struggling with heavy debt. "We could do a better job of managing the company" than those who are currently making decisions, A&P Chairman James Wood said at the annual meeting. All the same, he added, "we haven't been invited to take management control despite the obvious need." A&P has a 20% interest in Isosceles.

A&P, which is 52%-owned by Tengelmann Group of West Germany, reported a 12% rise in net income for the fiscal first quarter ended June 16. The increase was achieved despite a 1% decline in sales -- and despite net interest expense equivalent to seven cents a share in connection with the company's investment in Isosceles.

Mr. Wood said first-quarter net income was $51 million, or $1.33 a share, up from $45.4 million, or $1.19 a share, a year earlier.

Sales were $3.40 billion, down from $3.44 billion. Mr. Wood said 13 fewer stores were in operation in the latest period. He also cited "a sluggish sales climate in several regions that should improve as the year goes along."

The company also announced a quarterly dividend increase, to 20 cents a share from 17.5 cents a share. The dividend is payable Aug. 6 to shares of record July 20.

Last fall, as the result of a takeover battle over Gateway Corp. of Britain -- which Isosceles won -- A&P in turn wound up with a 20% interest in Isosceles. A&P is "carefully evaluating the current situation" as Isosceles develops plans to restructure heavy debt incurred in the Gateway takeover, Mr. Wood said.

A company spokesman said A&P's investment in Isosceles is valued at about $150 million at current exchange rates.
