International Brief -- All Nippon Airways: Purchase of About 30 Jets For $3.31 Billion Planned

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/11/90

All Nippon Airways of Japan will spend three months examining four passenger airplanes and intends to purchase about 30 medium-sized jets for a total of 500 billion yen ($3.31 billion), an official said. The planes include the 767-X, a high-technology jet that can seat 407 people, being developed jointly by Japan and the U.S.; St. Louis-based McDonnell Douglas Corp.'s MD-11 with a seating capacity of 380; and the European consortium Airbus Industrie's A-330, with a capacity of 385, and its A-340, seating 262 to 295.

All Nippon, which operates 105 aircraft and is scheduled to receive a previous order for 20 more jets at year end, is one of several airlines shopping for a new generation of wide-body aircraft to replace older McDonnell Douglas DC-10s, Lockheed Corp. L-1011s and Boeing 747s.

All Nippon will introduce the new planes over four to five years starting in 1995, the official said. This is aimed at meeting increasing passenger demand from three big airport construction projects in Japan planned for completion by the mid-1990s.
