REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: RICO Goes Pfffft

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/11/90

RICO's balloon continues to deflate. Prosecutors have had a high time of it in recent years, using the perverse glamour of a "racketeering" indictment against all manner of non-Mob defendants -- from Wall Street to the Marcoses to the Chicago trading pits. A jury in Chicago has just told the prosecutors to get serious again. It returned eight relatively minor guilty verdicts out of more than 100 counts filed against three futures traders on the Chicago Mercantile Exchange. A RICO indictment stood at the center of the case. If prosecutors discover white-collar crime, they should pursue it. But let them find a basis for prosecution that the public can recognize and respect. The law called RICO isn't it.
