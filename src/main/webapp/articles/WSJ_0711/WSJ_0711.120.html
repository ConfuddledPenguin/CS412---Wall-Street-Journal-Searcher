Technology Brief -- Martin Marietta Corp.: Firm Wins NASA Contracts For a Total of $24.7 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/11/90

Martin Marietta Corp. said it was awarded contracts valued at $24.7 million to build instruments for two National Aeronautics and Space Administration space exploration missions. The aerospace and defense company, of Bethesda, Md., said it will receive $11.6 million to build the so-called Cometary Ice and Dust Experiment, to be used to study the elemental and chemical composition of dust and ice particles from the comet Kopff.

The company said the second contract, for $13.1 million, is for a spectrometer that will fly aboard the astrophysics facility scheduled for launch in 1997. The device will enable scientists to measure the energy level of rays recieved from extremely distant celestial bodies.
