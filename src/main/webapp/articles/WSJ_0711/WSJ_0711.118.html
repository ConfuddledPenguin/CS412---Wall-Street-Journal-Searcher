Business Brief -- Public Service Co. of Colorado: Westinghouse Team Chosen For Conversion of Plant

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/11/90

Public Service Co. of Colorado selected a team of Westinghouse Electric Corp., Morrison Knudsen Corp. and Black & Veatch to negotiate a contract for the decommissioning and conversion of the Fort St. Vrain nuclear power plant in Colorado. Westinghouse said the contract is expected to be worth about $200 million.

The project, which will include converting the nuclear plant into a 330-megawatt gas-fired facility, is expected to be completed in five years. The Fort St. Vrain plant, 40 miles from Denver, closed in 1989.

Westinghouse is a Pittsburgh-based broadcasting, financial services and power generation concern. Morrison Knudsen, based in Boise, Idaho, is a construction planning firm. Black & Veatch, based in Kansas City, Mo., is an engineering and architectural design firm.
