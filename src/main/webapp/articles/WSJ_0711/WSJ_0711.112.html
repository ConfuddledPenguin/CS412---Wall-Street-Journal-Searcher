International Paper Reports a 19% Drop In 2nd-Quarter Profit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/11/90

PURCHASE, N.Y. -- International Paper Co. reported that second-quarter earnings dropped 19% on lower prices for uncoated printing and writing paper and for containerboard, used in making corrugated cartons. The results substantially exceeded analysts' expectations, however, and investors buoyed the company's stock price.

The paper, packaging and wood-products giant said its European paper and packaging operations performed strongly, as did specialty products, which include chemicals used in paints and adhesives; fabrics; and commercial photographic film and paper.

Second-quarter earnings sank to $185 million, or $1.70 a share, from $229 million, or $2.04 a share, in the same period a year earlier. Sales rose 6.7% to $3.2 billion from $3 billion.

The results "were significantly better than I was expecting," said Mark S. Rogers, analyst at Prudential-Bache Capital Funding, who had pegged International Paper earnings at $1.55 a share.

In linerboard, used to line corrugated packaging, and market pulp, the basic material for paper, "the down trend wasn't as strong as I thought," Mr. Rogers said, adding that those product areas are likely to continue to drop industrywide in the second half.

But while uncoated paper hurt the year-to-year comparison, prices have been recovering quarter to quarter this year, and "that's sustainable," the analyst said. "That product area looks well-positioned and is on a cyclical upturn after bottoming (early) this year."

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, International Paper's shares rose 87.5 cents to $52.50.
