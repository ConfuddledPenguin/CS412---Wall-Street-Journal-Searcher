Schering-Plough Announces Program For Early Retirement

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/11/90

MADISON, N.J. -- Schering-Plough Corp. announced a temporary program that may induce early retirements, at a pretax cost projected at "less than $15 million." The drug maker said the program won't affect earnings materially. The company said it continues to project that per share earnings will rise 18% to 20% this year. For 1989, the company had net income of $471.3 million, or $4.18 a share, on sales of $3.16 billion.

Schering offered enhanced pensions to nearly 900 U.S. employees who are 55 years or older with at least 10 years of service. An undisclosed number of foreign employees received comparable offers.

The program, available until Aug. 10, gives retiring employees an extra five years of age and length of service for purposes of calculating pension benefits. That means bigger pensions.

The program applies retroactively to employees who retired on or after last Dec. 31, the company said.

Though it's costly in the short term to induce retirements, many companies do so in order to lower long-term head counts or payroll costs. Retiring workers often can be replaced with younger, lower-paid workers, and in some cases companies manage to get along without any replacements.

Because of divestitures and cost-cutting moves, Schering's head count has declined in recent years. A company spokesman said there were 21,300 employees at the end of 1989, down from 25,000 five years earlier. Schering said its retirement incentives "may serve to further streamline the organization."

Schering closed at $48.125, down $1.25, in New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday.
