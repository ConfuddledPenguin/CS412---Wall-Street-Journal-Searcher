Letters to the Editor: Cap on Matriculation

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/11/90

As I read Tim W. Ferguson's profile on Sam Steiger, Arizona's colorful candidate for governor ("A Libertarian Governor?" editorial page, June 18), I noticed one inaccuracy about the University of Arizona (as well as Arizona State University). Enrollment at the UA is not 40,000 as reported; the total count as of fall 1989 is 35,569. Enrollment at ASU is 43,546 as of last fall, not 65,000. Enrollment management is one of UA President Henry Koffler's top priorities, and he has adopted a plan to limit enrollment to 38,000 between now and 1995.

However, the challenge of educating the state's young people is a major concern for universities and community colleges alike. High-school graduates in Arizona will increase from 31,000 in 1990 to 43,000 in 2000, based on the number of students currently enrolled in our elementary schools.

Sharon Kha

Office of Public Information

University of Arizona

Tucson, Ariz.
