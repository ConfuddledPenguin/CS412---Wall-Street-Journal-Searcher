Yields Mixed on CDs During Latest Week

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/11/90

NEW YORK -- Yields on certificates of deposit at major banks and brokerage firms were mixed in the week ended Tuesday. Average yields on small-denomination CDs sold directly by banks were flat to slightly lower. The average yield on a six-month consumer CD remained at 7.64%, while one-year CDs slipped to 7.82% from 7.83%, according to an 18-bank survey by Banxquote Money Markets, a New York information service.

Average yields on small-denomination CDs sold by leading broker-dealers were up slightly. Average yields on one-year CDs rose to 8.19% from 8.15%, while average yields on two-year CDs rose to 8.32% from 8.22%, according to Banxquote.

Meanwhile, average yields for large-denomination CDs, which typically require deposits of $90,000 or more, were also up slightly. Average yields on one-year jumbo CDs rose to 7.93% from 7.88%, while five-year maturities climbed to 8.23% from 8.20%.

The increase in yields on broker-sold and large-denomination CDs was in response to a slight rise in Treasury yields last week, said Norberto Mehl, chairman of Banxquote. Big brokerage firms usually negotiate rates with banks and thrifts whose CDs they sell; banks tend to adjust yields more quickly on jumbo CDs in response to changes in Treasury bill yields because they compete for the same insitutional money.
