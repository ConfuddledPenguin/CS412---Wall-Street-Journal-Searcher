Transportation Drug Tests Show 3.6% Positive Rate

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 07/11/90

About 3.6% of American transportation job holders and applicants had illegal or abusable drugs in their systems when tested by SmithKline Beecham Clinical Laboratories, King of Prussia, Pa. The results were the first released under the federally mandated drug testing program that began last December for transportation workers and applicants, including airplane pilots and mechanics, railroad employees, truck drivers and bus drivers.

SmithKline, a unit of London-based SmithKline Beecham PLC, said it analyzed urine samples from 118,000 transportation workers and applicants from Dec. 21, 1989, to June 30, 1990.

The company said that 3.1% of 65,000 transportation job holders tested positive for one or more of five substances: marijuana, cocaine, amphetamines, opiates and the hallucinogenic tranquilizer phencyclidine. SmithKline said 4.2% of 53,000 applicants for transportation jobs also tested positive. SmithKline didn't break down its results by mode of transport.

SmithKline said that in the general work force 10.4% of nearly one million job applicants and workers tested positive for one or more of the five substances. Harry Groome, president of SmithKline Beecham Clinical Laboratories, said "the good news is that drug use in the transportation industry is less prevalent than in other parts of society. The bad news is that drug use in safety-sensitive transportation jobs can put people's lives at risk."
