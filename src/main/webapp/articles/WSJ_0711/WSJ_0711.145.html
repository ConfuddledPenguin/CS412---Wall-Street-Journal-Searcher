American Airlines Slates Surcharges At Five Airports

  • Author: Bridget O'Brian
  • Date: 07/11/90

American Airlines said it will impose surcharges on passengers originating travel from five U.S. airports beginning Sunday. The airline, a unit of AMR Corp., of Fort Worth, Texas, said it was setting a special "passenger facilities charge" because of what it says are higher costs associated with the airports.

The airports and the surcharges are: New Jersey's Newark, $4; New Orleans International, $1.50; New York's La Guardia and Florida's West Palm Beach and Fort Myers, $1.

The surcharge will be imposed only on passengers originating travel in those cities. Passengers making round trips from these cities will be charged only once.

American said it was passing along to passengers special costs associated with landing at those airports. A spokesman for American said: "Our corporate real estate people identified charges we feel are excessive or unreasonable to our cost of doing business at these airports."

At Newark, for example, the airline said it was paying gate fees it considers too high. American has recently taken over several gates from NWA Inc.'s Northwest Airlines and it said the airport was charging it $1.5 million more than Northwest had paid annually.

At the two Florida airports, American was quibbling with what it considered unreasonably high airport operating budgets. Typically, airports pass along increases in their operating budgets to the airlines that land there. In New Orleans, the dispute centers on landing fees.

At La Guardia, American is disputing certain construction costs, specifically the type of flooring used in its new terminal.

Several years ago, American imposed similar charges on passengers flying out of Las Vegas when it was disputing construction costs at that airport. The surcharge lasted for 18 months, an American spokesman said, before it was dropped.

The spokesman said American planned to keep the current surcharges in effect for one year, after which it will have recouped the extra costs.
