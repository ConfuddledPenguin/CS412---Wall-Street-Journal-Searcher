Molson Cos. Plans Debt, Equity Issue To Finance Growth

  • Author: Suzanne McGee
  • Date: 07/11/90

TORONTO -- Molson Cos. said it plans to raise 300 million Canadian dollars (US$259.3 million) through an equity and debt issue in order to finance expansion of its current businesses. Molson said it will issue four million Class A nonvoting shares at C$37.625 each to the Canadian public, as well as C$150 million of one-year floating-rate redeemable debentures.

Two Toronto-based securities dealers, RBC Dominion Securities Inc. and Gordon Capital Corp., agreed to lead the underwriting group for the equity issue and to buy the debentures, Molson said.

Analysts described the issue as essentially a two-stage equity offering, because Molson can require that debenture holders redeeming the securities apply the proceeds to purchase Class A shares, at a price based on trading prices at the time of redemption. Molson said it can redeem the debentures any time after Nov. 1, 1990.

Philip Koven, an analyst with Richardson Greenshields Inc. in Toronto, said it's clear from the size and nature of the offering that Molson is interested in expanding its brewing interests.

Officials at Molson, which has interests in brewing, specialty chemicals, retailing, and sports and entertainment, declined to say which of these areas the company is seeking to expand.

Marshall Cohen, Molson's president and chief executive officer, said at the annual meeting last month that the company will seek to reap further benefits from its links with the Australian brewing concern Elders IXL. In August 1989, Molson and Elders merged their North American brewing operations into a joint venture, Molson Breweries of Canada.

Mr. Cohen, who couldn't be reached for comment following the announcement yesterday, said at the annual meeting that the Elders' alliance "has provided us with an opportunity to participate in the Foster's Brewing Group."

As reported, Elders proposed last March to restructure its assets, turning the company known as Elders IXL into a publicly traded brewing concern to be known as Foster's Brewing Group Ltd.

Molson's move to raise financing has triggered speculation that the company may use the funds to invest in Foster's Brewing, possibly by buying shares held by closely held Harlin Holdings Pty., which is closely associated with John Elliott, Elders' chairman.

In trading on the Toronto Stock Exchange yesterday, Molson's Class A shares closed at C$37 a share, down C$1.625, as investors reacted to the fear of diluted earnings. Molson has about 22.7 million Class A shares outstanding and 10.2 million Class B shares.
