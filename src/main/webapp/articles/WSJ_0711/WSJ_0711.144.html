Hilton Hotels Profit Fell 9% in 2nd Period On 3% Revenue Gain

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/11/90

BEVERLY HILLS, Calif. -- Hilton Hotels Corp., feeling the effects of new competitors in the Las Vegas, Nev., casino market, reported a 9% drop in second-quarter earnings to $38.2 million, or 79 cents a share, from $42 million or 87 cents a share a year earlier. Revenue for the hotel and casino company rose 3% to $273.8 million from $265 million a year earlier. Operating income dropped 8% to $73.8 million.

Operating income from the casinos dropped 21% to $29 million for the quarter, while the hotel segment posted a 3% increase in operating income to $44.8 million. Much of the decline came from the Las Vegas Hilton, which reported an unexpectedly sharp 56% decline in operating results.

Maurice Scanlon, Hilton's senior vice president-finance, attributed part of the year-to-year decline at the Las Vegas Hilton to a strong quarter a year earlier, and to a decrease in wagers at table games. That stemmed largely from new competitors, including Golden Nugget Inc.'s Mirage casino resort.

For the six months, net income rose 15% to $63.3 million, or $1.31 a share, from $55.1 million, or $1.14 a share, a year earlier. Revenue rose 10% to $593.1 million from $490.1 million.
