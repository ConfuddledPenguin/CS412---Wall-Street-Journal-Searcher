Harvard, Ecuador Sign `Debt-for-Scholarship' Pact

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/11/90

CAMBRIDGE, Mass. -- Harvard University agreed to retire about $5 million of Ecuador's national debt in return for the creation of a scholarship fund. Harvard said the "debt for scholarship swap" marked the first time distressed Third World debt would be paid off in a way that promoted education.

In essence, the agreement allows Ecuador to retire its debt at a discount and obtain scholarships to Harvard for Ecuadorean students. Harvard said the agreement would allow the university to provide more scholarships than it otherwise could.

Harvard said it will buy Ecuadorean debt at a price equal to its current value in the secondary market -- about 15% of face value. Robert Scott, Harvard's vice president for finance, said the university anticipated spending up to $700,000 for about $5 million in face value of Ecuadorean obligations.

Harvard will donate the debt to an Ecuadorean foundation, which will exchange it for government bonds valued at about half of the face-value amount Harvard purchases -- or about $2 million to $2.5 million. The new Ecuadorean obligations, Mr. Scott said, would be sold by the foundation and the proceeds used to buy dollar-denominated securities that will be the scholarship fund's capital.

Most of the income from the fund will support about 70 scholarships for Ecuadoreans over the next 10 years, roughly doubling the number of scholarship students from Ecuador at Harvard. About 20% of the fund's income will pay for research grants and internships for Harvard students and faculty in Ecuador. Mr. Scott said the scholarship fund would generate between $100,000 and $200,000 in annual income.
