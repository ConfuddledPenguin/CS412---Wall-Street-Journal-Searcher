Khashoggi Counts Dropped By U.S. Attorney's Office

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/11/90

NEW YORK -- The U.S. attorney's office in Manhattan dropped the remaining criminal charges against Adnan Khashoggi, who was acquitted with Imelda Marcos in a federal racketeering trial last week. The motion to dismiss the two counts of conspiracy and racketeering must be approved by U.S. Judge John Keenan, who presided over the Marcos trial. In a court filing, Assistant U.S. Attorney Charles LaBella said the two charges were based on the same allegations on which Mr. Khashoggi was acquitted.

The Saudi businessman was extradited from Switzerland in July 1989 to stand trial on charges of obstruction of justice and mail fraud in the Marcos case. Under Mr. Khashoggi's extradition arrangement, he couldn't be tried on the other two charges, although they remained pending, because Switzerland doesn't have a Racketeer Influenced and Corrupt Organizations law.

Mrs. Marcos, the former first lady of the Philippines who has lived in Hawaii since 1986, was found innocent of all charges by a Manhattan federal jury.
