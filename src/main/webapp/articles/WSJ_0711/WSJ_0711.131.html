Oracle Systems Corp. Reports a 58% Rise For Fiscal 4th Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/11/90

BELMONT, Calif. -- Oracle Systems Corp. posted a 58% increase in fourth-quarter earnings after a disappointing third quarter. Oracle said net income for the quarter ended May 31 was a record $53 million, or 39 cents a share, compared with $33.5 million, or 25 cents a share, in the year-ago quarter. Revenue was up 55% to a record $334.4 million from $215.9 million.

The results are in contrast to the software company's flat income growth in the third quarter, when Oracle broke a five-year streak of rapid growth. The poor results caused its stock to plunge 31% to $17.5 in one day. The stock began climbing again in May, closing at $22.375, up 87.5 cents, in national over-the-counter trading yesterday. The fourth-quarter results were announced after the close of trading.

Oracle attributed the improved earnings, in part, to improved cash flow and stronger-than-expected cash collections for the quarter. Last March, the company blamed its third-quarter debacle on accounting glitches and said it would resume high growth in the fourth quarter. The flat third quarter shook Wall Street out of a long love affair with Oracle, which had quarterly net growth rates in 1989 ranging from 65% to 84%.

For the fiscal year, Oracle said, net income was up 44% to $117.4 million, or 86 cents a share, from $81.8 million, or 61 cents a share the previous fiscal year. Revenue increased 66% to $971 million from $584 million.
