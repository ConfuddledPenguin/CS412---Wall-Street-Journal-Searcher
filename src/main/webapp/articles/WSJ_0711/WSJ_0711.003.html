Business Brief -- Carolina Power & Light Co.: Second-Quarter Charge Set, Rate Case Decision Cited

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/11/90

Carolina Power & Light Co., Raleigh, N.C., said it will take a charge of about $81.6 million, or 97 cents a share, against second-quarter earnings, citing a decision in a rate case, but declined to estimate the net effect on its results. The utility had year-earlier net income of $72.6 million, or 81 cents a share, on operating revenue of $558 million.

Carolina Power said it agreed to a North Carolina Utilities Commission decision on its $3.9 billion Harris nuclear plant near Raleigh. The commission ruled that $389.4 million of that cost, while "prudently incurred," couldn't be included in the company's rate base, because three of four planned generating units at the plant had been canceled.

The company said the order will keep its revenue at current levels, with no immediate decrease in consumer rates, but in the long run, rates will decline because of the removal of the $389.4 million from the rate base. It said this sum will be treated as canceled operations and amortized over about six years without any return.
