American Pacific Sets Pacts for Utah Plant With German Firm

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/11/90

LAS VEGAS, Nev. -- American Pacific Corp., a specialty chemical maker and industrial real estate developer, announced definitive cooperation agreements with Dynamit Nobel AG of West Germany for the design, construction and operation of a sodium azide plant in Iron County, Utah. Sodium azide is the main chemical used to inflate automotive air bags. There is no U.S. producer of the material, American Pacific said.

The estimated capital cost of the plant, which will be owned and operated by American Pacific's American Azide Corp. unit, is about $20 million. The plant is to have annual capacity of 1,000 metric tons. A metric ton is equal to about 2,204.62 pounds. Dynamit Nobel, which will provide technical assistance to help get the factory completed and running, has retained the option to purchase 30% of the common stock of American Azide.

Dynamit Nobel's obligation to proceed with the project beyond the first phase, however, is subject to American Pacific's getting the required financing. "Although there can be no assurance that financing will be obtained, the company has had preliminary discussions with a number of financing sources and is optimistic that satisfactory financing arrangements will be made," said Fred D. Gibson Jr., American Pacific's chairman.

The plant will be built by United Engineers & Constructors Inc., a division of Raytheon Co., Lexington, Mass. Construction is planned for the spring of 1991 and the plant's start-up for the 1991 fourth quarter. The plant will be designed for "timely and economic expansion" to 2,000 metric tons of capacity annually, American Pacific said.
