FDIC Said to Mull Filing Civil Charges Against Neil Bush

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/11/90

WASHINGTON -- The Federal Deposit Insurance Corp. is considering filing civil charges against President Bush's son Neil and other former officials of a Colorado thrift, but a final decision hasn't been made yet, an agency spokesman said. Neil Bush, a Denver oil businessman, was an outside director of Silverado Banking Savings & Loan Association, which failed in late 1988 at an expected cost to the government of $1 billion. The Office of Thrift Supervision has charged him in an administrative action with violating conflict-of-interest rules as a Silverado director. Mr. Bush has denied any wrongdoing.

The FDIC routinely files civil charges against directors and officers of failed institutions, as well as outside attorneys and accountants, in cases where the government bank and thrift insurance funds suffer a loss. The agency typically brings charges of negligence and breach of fiduciary duty to the institution, seeking to cover its losses. Banks and thrifts usually carry insurance for their officers and directors to protect them from such suits.

ABC News reported last night that the FDIC is about to file a $200 million civil suit against Mr. Bush and other former Silverado officials, but the FDIC spokesman, said "no final decision" has been made on whether to bring a suit. High-level officials at the FDIC confirmed that a final decision hasn't been made, but one person said a decision "isn't too far off."
