Technology Brief -- DNA Plant Technology Corp.: Biotech Firm Wins Patent For Crop Protection Process

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/11/90

DNA Plant Technology Corp. said it received a U.S. patent for a gene-based method of protecting crops from certain fungi, including those that cause rotting in fruits and vegetables after they have been harvested. The Cinnaminson, N.J., biotechnology firm said the patent covers its process of transferring into various plants the gene for chitinase, an enzyme that breaks down chitin, a crucial component of many fungi.

Many plants naturally produce chitinase, but not in sufficient quantities or at the right time to fight off harmful fungi, the company said. Currently, chemical fungicides are used to protect plants.

Products incorporating the new process would not reach supermarkets before the mid-1990s, the company said.
