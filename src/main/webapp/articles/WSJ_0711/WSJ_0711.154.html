Technology: Encore Computer To Post Sizable Loss For Second Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/11/90

FORT LAUDERDALE, Fla. -- Encore Computer Corp. said it expects to report a significant second-quarter loss. The company also said it plans to lay off about 130 employees from its staff of 1,825 workers. Encore, after years of struggling, appeared to be poised for a turnaround in April 1989, when it acquired Gould Inc.'s computer business from Nippon Mining Co. of Japan. At a stroke, Encore increased its sales sixfold, acquired a big presence in Europe, and quintupled its sales force to 165 people.

Yesterday, however, Encore's chairman and chief executive officer, Kenneth G. Fisher, said that a revenue shortfall and restructuring costs linked to new product introductions would result in a sizable loss for the second quarter.

The company declined to estimate the loss, saying it would report final results in about two weeks. For the year-earlier period, Encore had a net loss of $14.9 million, or 48 cents a share, on revenue of $41.5 million.

The new products are Encore's Seahawk and Gemini computer systems, used primarily in flight simulators to train military and commercial pilots. A company spokesman said the revenue shortfall stemmed from a gap in sales as the company moved to bring the new products to market. The Seahawk computers, the spokesman said, are now being shipped to customers, and the Gemini systems will start to be shipped in the fourth quarter.

The restructuring costs, Encore said, would result in the layoff of about 130 people. The company spokesman said the cutbacks will be made "across the board," in such departments as manufacturing, finance and customer service.

Despite the setback in the second quarter, Mr. Fisher, citing the company's focus on its new products, said: "Revenue could return to profitable levels in the third quarter."
