Casino Analyst Files Claims Against Firm, Trump Over Firing

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/11/90

NEW YORK -- Marvin Roffman, the casino analyst who lost his job after making critical remarks about the financial prospects for Donald Trump's Taj Mahal casino, has taken legal action against both his former firm and Mr. Trump. Mr. Roffman filed an arbitration claim for wrongful discharge against his former employer, Janney Montgomery Scott, a securities firm owned by Penn Mutual Life Insurance Co. The claim is being brought before a New York Stock Exchange arbitration unit.

Mr. Roffman also is suing Mr. Trump in a Pennsylvania federal court alleging that Mr. Trump interfered with Mr. Roffman's employment by threatening to sue Janney Montgomery unless Mr. Roffman publicly apologized for his remarks about the Taj Mahal.

For each action, Mr. Roffman is seeking compensatory damages in excess of $1 million, plus unspecified punitive damages. He also is seeking reinstatement by Janney Montgomery Scott.

On March 20, the day Mr. Roffman's negative comments about the casino appeared in this newspaper, Mr. Trump wrote a letter to Norman Wilde Jr., president of Janney Montgomery, stating: "I am now planning to institute a major lawsuit against your firm unless Mr. Roffman makes a major public apology or is dismissed."

Mr. Roffman first sent an apologetic letter to Mr. Trump but withdrew it the next day, saying: "I retract the letter." That day, Mr. Roffman was fired.

Mr. Trump's attorney denied the casino owner and developer had anything to do with Mr. Roffman's termination.

An attorney for Janney Montgomery said Mr. Roffman was fired for repudiating a letter to Mr. Trump apologizing for his remarks about the Taj Mahal. The attorney said the letter was written by Mr. Roffman after his supervisor warned him, in writing, not to send it.
