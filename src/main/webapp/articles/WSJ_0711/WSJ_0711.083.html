Technology: UPS Trucks Will Use Gas In Los Angeles Experiment

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/11/90

LOS ANGELES -- In a bid for cleaner air, United Parcel Service Inc. said it will convert 20 of its trucks here to run on natural gas within the next year. United Parcel Service is running the test program in conjunction with Southern California Gas Co. If the program goes smoothly, UPS said it will consider converting the rest of its Los Angeles fleet of more than 2,000 trucks to natural gas.

Southern California Gas said vehicles powered by natural gas emit 65% less ozone-forming pollutants than those using gasoline and other fuels.

UPS has had a similar test program with Brooklyn Union Gas Co. involving ten trucks in the New York area for more than a year. "That's going extremely well and that's why we thought we could expand into Los Angeles," said Siro DeGasperis, a spokesman for Greenwich, Conn.-based UPS. "The emissions have been significantly reduced and the things we feared, such as loss of power, didn't materialize."

As part of the test program, Southern California Gas will provide the natural gas and both companies will share the cost of the conversion equipment for the trucks. A spokesman for the utility said there are currently about 30,000 vehicles in the U.S. which run on natural gas. Most of those are owned by gas utilities.
