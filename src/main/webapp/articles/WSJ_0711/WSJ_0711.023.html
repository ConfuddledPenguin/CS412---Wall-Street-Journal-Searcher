International: Tapie Firm Shares Suspended Pending Data on Adidas Bid

  • Author: E.S. Browning
  • Date: 07/11/90

PARIS -- Flexing its recently expanded muscles, France's securities market watchdog ordered Bernard Tapie Finance S.A. shares suspended from trading because the company hasn't adequately informed investors about its plan to acquire Adidas AG. Mr. Tapie's company, which is nearly 60% controlled by the French financier personally, responded by saying it would release details of its takeover plans, and its partners, next Monday. The company said it was making the announcement to quiet talk about "the alleged trouble we are having putting together a group of backers to help us finance Adidas."

Mr. Tapie still refuses to say how much he is paying for the 80% stake in the sporting goods giant that he has agreed to buy from West Germany's Dassler family. But his announcement said that when the takeover price is disclosed, it would be clear that worries over his ability to finance the purchase are "ridiculous."

The financier didn't say which banks are helping provide funds, but said that contrary to press reports no bank has yet refused to back him. He told a radio interviewer that he had signed a secrecy agreement with the Dasslers, but his statement later said that Adidas had "authorized" him to release information Monday.

A spokeswoman for the French markets regulator, the Commission des Operations de Bourse, said the agency has rarely ordered a share suspension for lack of proper financial information, a power it gained as part of expanded authority granted by the government earlier this year.

"Market regulations clearly require every quoted company to give prompt information about any event that will change its financial situation," the spokeswoman said.

She said it isn't clear how Mr. Tapie is making the acquisition or which Tapie-controlled company would end up holding his Adidas stake. Until Mr. Tapie publishes such information, the spokeswoman said, his company's shares, and the shares of its two quoted subsidiaries, industrial scalemakers Terraillon S.A. and Testut S.A., will be suspended from trading.

Before promising to release information, Mr. Tapie had made his own request for a trading suspension -- until Aug. 11. His company explained that West Germany's Cartel Office, which must approve major takeovers, has until then to rule on the Adidas acquisition.

The commission spokeswoman said that it was a highly unusual request, but that she didn't have an official reaction.

The controversy is typical of the 47-year-old Mr. Tapie, a flamboyant former television personality and singer who has made a specialty of rescuing troubled companies. He long has enjoyed making surprise announcements and unexpectedly changing his position. Since acquiring control of the Marseilles soccer team in the mid-1980s and then launching a career as a left-wing member of the French Parliament in 1988, he has been increasingly in the public eye.
