International Brief -- Ahold N.V.: Asko Drops Takeover Effort For Dutch Retail Concern

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/11/90

Asko Deutsche Kaufhaus AG's decision to throw in the towel in its battle with Dutch retailer Ahold N.V. ends nearly a year of bitter dispute between the two companies and also demonstrates once again the virtual invulnerability of Dutch defenses against a hostile takeover. West German Asko is a wholesaler of food and other consumer goods, and a retailer of food and drink. Ahold is a food retailer.

Asko said that it would sell its entire 13.1% stake in Ahold to a consortium of Dutch and West German banks led by Amsterdam-Rotterdam N.V. and Deutsche Bank. The banks in turn will privately place the shares in as wide a distribution as possible with private investors, according to a statement issued by Ahold.

An Ahold spokesman indicated that no further contacts between the companies is likely given the fact that Ahold and its partners in the Associated Marketing Services (AMS), a European retailing alliance, have successfully recruited another West German company, Allkauf AG, to replace Asko as the West German representative in the AMS.

Analysts agree that this marks the end of Asko's bid for a strong presence in Western Europe outside of West Germany.
