Japan's Machinery Orders Up

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/11/90

TOKYO -- Reflecting a steady uptrend, orders for machinery received by Japanese manufacturers in May climbed 13.4% from a year earlier to 1.658 trillion yen ($250.7 million), the Economic Planning Agency announced. Orders dipped 0.2% last December, interrupting the increase that started in October 1988, but they have been rising again each month since the beginning of 1990.

The sustained increase is evidence of Japanese corporations' desires to modernize, in anticipation of better business in the future, officials said.

The latest gain was also attributed to the fact that the government, having failed to pass its fiscal 1989 budget through parliament in time, held down orders in May 1989.
