Reporter Who Was Jailed Over Source Is Released

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/11/90

SAN ANTONIO, Texas -- Two weeks into a six-month sentence, a television reporter here was freed after a source whose identity he was protecting identified herself to attorneys. "She came forward," Brian Karem, the reporter, said. "I proved my point and I would keep my promise again." Mr. Karem surrendered his notes and confirmed the name of the source who helped arrange a phone interview last year with jailed murder suspect Henry David Hernandez. Mr. Karem works at KMOL-TV in San Antonio.

Two other sources had already identified themselves, but Mr. Karem refused to give up his notes or testify without the permission of the third, Mr. Hernandez's cousin Deborah Ledesma. He was jailed for contempt of court June 27 and his case stirred national debate on reporter privilege under the First Amendment.

Mr. Hernandez, on trial with his brother Julian for the March 1989 murder of San Antonio policeman Gary Williams, said in the phone interview that he shot the officer in self-defense. Attorneys had insisted that the name of the third source was important to admission of the interview as evidence.

Mr. Karem's request for an emergency stay of the jail sentence pending an appeal was rejected by the Supreme Court earlier this week.
