Letters to the Editor: Government Contracts Just Industry Welfare?

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/11/90

Concerning your May 22 article "Raytheon Wins Army Contract of $414 Million," I find it very interesting how this wonderful country of ours and its leaders have developed a unique system of social/economic benefits. For those in need, welfare programs have always been, if not grudgingly promulgated, done so in a way to make recipients of benefits feel somewhat less than full, productive citizens. When it comes to industry, as very clearly noted, there seems to be an air of what a nice thing has been accomplished. Avoiding layoffs, as by this "Army contract" (God forbid it should be called a welfare program), is laudable insofar as the workers are concerned, but one wonders whether a cheese-manufacturer client of mine in Brooklyn, whose business has fallen off, might be helped by a Pentagon contract for a few tons of cheese so as to keep his work force employed.

Harold B. Somer

Westbury, N.Y.
