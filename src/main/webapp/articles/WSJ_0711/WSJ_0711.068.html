MUNICIPAL

New York State Dormitory Authority -- $869.7 million of bonds, including $530.3 million of revenue bonds and $339.4 million of special obligation bonds, priced by a PaineWebber Inc. group to yield from 5.80% for special obligation bonds due in 1991 to 7.68% for revenue bonds due in 2014. The revenue bonds are due May 15, 1992-2004, 2010, 2014, 2016 and 2017. In this category, current interest serial bonds are priced to yield from 6.50% in 1992 to 7.55% in 2004. There are $63.9 million of 7.50% term bonds priced at 99.75 to yield 7.52% in 2010, $172.1 million of 7.38% term bonds priced at 96.63 to yield 7.68% in 2014, $133.3 million of 7% term bonds priced at 92.75 to yield 7.65% in 2016 and $42.3 million of 6% term bonds priced at 82.25 to yield 7.55% in 2017. Term bonds due in 2010 are non-callable. Ratings of single-A by Moody's and single-A-minus by S&P are expected. The special obligation bonds are due Nov. 1 and May 1, 1990-2000, with capital appreciation bonds due May 1 and Nov. 1, 2001-2010. These current interest serial bonds are priced to yield from 5.80% in 1991 to 6.70% in 2000. Serial bonds due in 1990 weren't formally reoffered. Capital appreciation bonds are priced to yield from 6.75% in 2001 to 7% in 2006-2010. Triple-A ratings are expected from Moody's and S&P.

California Housing Finance Agency -- $243.7 million of home mortgage revenue bonds due Aug. 1, 2010, 2014 and 2030, with capital appreciation bonds due Feb. 1 and Aug. 1, 2001-2005, tentatively priced by a Merrill Lynch Capital Markets group to yield from 7% in 2001 and 2014 to 7.63% in 2030. There are $31.3 million of 7 1/4% term bonds priced at par due in 2010, $21.8 million of 7% term bonds priced at par due in 2014 and $161.8 million of 7 5/8% term bonds priced at par due in 2030. Capital appreciation bonds are priced to yield from 7% in 2001 to 7.20% in 2004 and 2005. The bonds are rated double-A by Moody's and single-A-plus by S&P. Interest on the bonds due in 2030 will be subject to the federal alternative minimum tax that may be imposed on certain investors.

Wisconsin -- $200 million of 10 months operating notes, due June 17, 1991, mostly via a BT Securities Corp. group. The firm purchased $175 million of the notes. All of the notes carry a coupon of 6 1/4%, with $100 million of notes priced to yield 6.10% and $75 million of notes priced to yield 6.12%. A PaineWebber Inc. group purchased the remaining $25 million of the notes and priced them to yield 6.09%. The notes are rated MIG-1 by Moody's and SP1-plus by S&P.

Massachusetts Port Authority -- $91.1 million of revenue bonds, Series 1990, due July 1, 1992-2004, 2010 and 2020, tentatively priced by a Merrill Lynch Capital Markets group to yield from 6.50% in 1992 to 7.60% in 2020. Current interest serial bonds are priced to yield from 6.50% in 1992 to 7.35% in 2004. There are $17.7 million of 7 3/8% term bonds priced at 98.71 to yield 7.50% in 2010 and $53.1 million of 7 1/2% term bonds priced at 98.82 to yield 7.60% in 2020. The bonds are rated double-A by Moody's and Fitch Investors Service Inc. and double-A-minus by S&P, except for bonds due in 1997-2020, which are insured and rated triple-A by Moody's and S&P. Interest on the bonds will be treated as a preference item in calculating the federal alternative minimum tax.

Missouri Housing Development Corp. -- $85 million of single-family housing revenue bonds, due June 1, and Dec. 1, 1992-2005, 2013, 2016 and 2022, were tentatively priced by a George K. Baum & Co. group to yield from 6.10% in 1992 to 7.75% in 2022. Current interest serial bonds are priced at par to yield from 6.10% in 1992 to 7.15% in 2005. There are $22 million of term bonds priced at par to yield 7 1/4% in 2013. There are $12.8 million of term bonds priced at par to yield 7 5/8% in 2016 and $34 million of term bonds priced at par to yield 7 3/4% in 2022. The bonds are rated triple-A by S&P. Interest on the bonds will be treated as a preference item in calculating the federal alternative minimum tax that may be imposed on certain investors.

Broward County School Board, Fla. -- $67.7 million of certificates of participation, due July 1, 1991-2002, and 2010, were tentatively priced by a Bear, Stearns & Co. group to yield from 6.00% in 1991 to 7.29% in 2010. Current interest serial bonds are priced to yield from 6.00% in 1991 to 7.05% in 2002. There are $38 million of 7 1/8% term bonds priced at 98 1/4 to yield 7.29% in 2010. The bonds are insured and rated triple-A by Moody's and S&P.

East Baton Rouge, La. -- $56 million of single-family mortgage revenue bonds, Series A due Feb. 1 and Aug. 1, 1993-2003, 2008, 2015, 2020 and 2023, and Series B due Aug. 1, 2012, were tentatively priced by a Howard Weil Labouisse Friedrichs Inc. group to yield from 6.40% in 1993 to 7.82% in 2023. Series A current interest serial bonds are priced at par to yield from 6.40% in 1993 to 7.25% in 2003. There are $5.2 million of term bonds priced at par to yield 7 5/8% due Aug. 1, 2008. There are $4.5 million of term bonds priced at par to yield 7.40% due Feb. 1, 2015. There are $16.8 million of term bonds priced at par to yield 7.60% due Feb. 1, 2020, and $16.9 million of 7.80% term bonds priced at 99 3/4 to yield 7.82% due Aug. 1, 2023. Series B consists of a single $6 million tranche of bonds priced at par to yield 7.40% due Aug. 1, 2012. The bonds are backed by the Government National Mortgage Association and rated triple-A by Moody's. Interest on the Series A bonds will be treated as a preference item in calculating the federal alternative minimum tax that may be imposed on certain investors.
