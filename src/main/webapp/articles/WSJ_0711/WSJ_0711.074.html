Technology & Health: Red Cross Fails to Note Possible AIDS-Tied Blood

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/11/90

WASHINGTON -- American Red Cross blood banks failed to notify the organization's Washington headquarters of 224 possible instances of AIDS contamination, according to the Food and Drug Administration. In all instances, the blood banks had been alerted by physicians that AIDS patients believed they had been infected through blood transfusions. All of the blood banks are in the Washington, D.C., region, which has one of the highest urban rates of AIDS.

Under an agreement with the FDA, the Red Cross has been required since 1988 to monitor its blood banks more closely than it had in the past. But most of the cases of possible contamination occurred before 1985, according to an FDA spokeswoman.

The FDA's findings appear in an inspection report awaiting final review. Its contents were first reported yesterday in the Philadelphia Inquirer.

The Red Cross said that since AIDS testing and screening had begun, "only six cases of transfusion-associated AIDS have been reported."
