International Brief -- Bell Group Ltd.: Maxwell Told to Put Stake In Concern Back on Market

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/11/90

British publisher Robert Maxwell must put back on the market a 14.9% stake in Bell Group Ltd. of Australia that he acquired in April. But his lawyers said he may be able to retain the stake. Bell Group, an affiliate of Bond Corp. Holdings Ltd., publishes the main morning newspaper in Perth, the West Australian, and Mr. Maxwell has been seeking a foothold in Australian newspaper publishing.

Mr. Maxwell purchased the stake in Bell Group for 485,900 Australian dollars (US$390,400), or one Australian cent a share, from David Aspinall, managing director of Bell Group, and regulators alleged that the sale breached Australia's takeover rules. Mr. Aspinall, who earlier acquired a 16.6% stake in Bell Group, was deemed to be an associate of the controlling holder, Bond Corp., so his purchase should have triggered a takeover offer for the whole company, regulators said.

In the settlement, Mr. Aspinall must offer for sale his 5.4 million Bell Group shares and Mr. Maxwell must offer his shares. The shares will carry a reserve price that hasn't yet been disclosed. Bell Group shares currently are trading at seven Australian cents. Mr. Maxwell can bid for the shares he already owns, and if no other buyer offers the reserve price, Mr. Maxwell can retain the shares.
