Marketing & Media: Lawsuit Seeks to Ban Sales Of Sony's Audio Recorders

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/11/90

NEW YORK -- A class-action lawsuit was filed asking a federal judge to ban Sony Corp. from selling its new digital audio tape recorders. The lawsuit, filed in U.S. district court in Manhattan, alleges that the recording devices, which make nearly flawless recordings from compact disks and other sources, are designed solely to enable consumers to violate music copyrights by making unauthorized versions. The action was filed on behalf of songwriters and other owners of music copyrights in the U.S.

The lawsuit seeks a court order prohibiting Sony from distributing and marketing the DAT recorders, which it began shipping to retailers last month, as well as blank DAT cassettes. The lawsuit also seeks a declaration by the court that unauthorized home recordings made on the DAT machines violate music copyrights, and that Sony will be liable for those alleged infringements.

Michael Oberman, an attorney for the plaintiffs who brought the suit, said a recent survey by the Roper Organization found that nearly everyone interested in buying a DAT machine intends to tape prerecorded music.

Sony, in a statement, said the suit is "totally without merit," adding that "DAT is the latest development of a product that has been widely used in the U.S. for more than 30 years -- the home tape recorder."
