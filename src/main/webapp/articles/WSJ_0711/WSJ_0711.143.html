Credit Ratings: Time Warner Ratings On Debt, Preferred Are Affirmed by S&P

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/11/90

NEW YORK -- Standard & Poor's Corp. said it affirmed its ratings on debt and preferred stock of Time Warner Inc. but remains concerned about the company's sizable debt repayments coming due in March 1992. The ratings concern noted that Time Warner's agreement to guarantee $650 million of debt of Pathe Communications Corp. "appears unlikely to be consummated." That debt was to help finance Pathe's proposed $1.3 billion takeover of MGM/UA Communications Co.

The agreement prompted a review of the company's debt rating because, together with other recent acquisitions, it would have increased Time Warner's "already substantial financial risk," S&P said.

Pathe has said it is seeking other financing, prompting a lawsuit by Time Warner.

S&P affirmed $1.5 billion of rated long-term debt at double-B, and $5.6 billion of preferred stock at single-B-plus. Commercial-paper ratings weren't under review.

Referring to the 1992 debt repayments, extendable to March 1993, "which are expected to require refinancing," S&P said its outlook for Time Warner's debt rating is negative.

Repeating previous statements, S&P said it continues to consider Time Warner's business positions, cash-generating capacity, and asset values favorably, "despite the company's aggressive financial profile."
