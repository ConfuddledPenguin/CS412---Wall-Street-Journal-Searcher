Dow Jones Reports 2nd-Quarter Net Fell 28% on Flat Revenue

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/11/90

NEW YORK -- Dow Jones & Co. reported a 28% drop in second-quarter net income, reflecting an earnings slump in the publishing company's information services activities. Earnings fell to $34.9 million, or 35 cents a share, from $48.4 million, or 48 cents a share, in the year-earlier period. Revenue edged up to $441.6 million from $437.7 million.

Operating income for Dow Jones's business publications rose 7.5% to $37.6 million, despite flat revenue and a decline in advertising linage. Business publications include The Wall Street Journal, its overseas editions and Barron's magazine.

At Dow Jones's information services group, however, operating income slumped 53% to $27.6 million. Information services consists of Telerate, which provides financial market data via computer, as well as various database packages and wire services.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Dow Jones common stock rose 37.5 cents to close at $23.50 a share. The stock had sunk to a seven-year low of $21.50 earlier this month as securities analysts lowered their earnings estimates in expectation that the information services results would be weak.

Some analysts are concerned about Telerate's efforts to establish a foreign-exchange trading service. That venture ran up a pretax start-up loss of $51 million last year, when it was 50%-owned by Telerate and 50%-owned by American Telephone & Telegraph Co. Telerate and the currency-trading service now are owned solely by Dow Jones. By setting up a currency trading service, Telerate is competing with Reuters Holdings PLC, which dominates the foreign-currency data market.

A Dow Jones spokesman said the trading service's losses are narrowing, but the service "isn't expected to be profitable this year or next." The service's software is being upgraded, he said, and developing a revenue base will take time. "This is a long-term commitment by Dow Jones," he added.

Dow Jones said Telerate's other North American business was hurt by "retrenchments taking place at major Wall Street investment firms and throughout the savings and loan industry."

Dow Jones said its second-quarter earnings were reduced 12 cents a share by increased interest expense and good will amortization charges relating to its January 1990 acquisition of the 33% of Telerate that it didn't already own.

Dow Jones also said that "a significant reduction in the rate of expense growth is expected in the second half in the information services segment, notably at Telerate." It also said Wall Street's retrenchment and its impact on revenue is expected to continue.

In the second quarter, Ottaway Newspapers Inc., Dow Jones's community newspapers unit, reported a 16% drop in operating income to $10.6 million.

In the quarter, Wall Street Journal advertising linage declined 8.7% while Barron's linage was off 7.4%.
