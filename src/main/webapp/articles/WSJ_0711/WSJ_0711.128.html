International Brief -- Societe Lyonnaise des Eaux: Water-Treatment Company Plans Merger With Builder

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/11/90

Societe Lyonnaise des Eaux, a diversified French water-treatment group, and building company Dumez S.A. said they plan to merge but declined to provide further details until after their boards meet to approve the plan. The two companies, which obtained a suspension of their shares on the Paris stock exchange Monday, said their boards will meet today.

The move came as a surprise to some analysts, who didn't expect Lyonnaise des Eaux to merge with a company whose profit margins are generally only half its own and whose business is vulnerable to swings in demand for construction. Some analysts suggested, however, that the merger would allow the two companies to catch up with their main rivals in efforts over the last two or three years to build multiservice conglomerates.

Lyonnaise des Eaux shares were suspended Monday at 702 francs ($126.60) each, while Dumez shares were frozen at 641 francs each. At those levels, the common stock of Lyonnaise des Eaux and Dumez would be worth just over 20 billion francs and seven billion francs, respectively. Lyonnaise des Eaux also has several convertible bonds outstanding.
