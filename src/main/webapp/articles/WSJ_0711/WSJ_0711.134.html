Charter Federal, FBI Investigating a Theft At Branch in Virginia

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/11/90

BRISTOL, Va. -- Charter Federal Savings Bank said the Federal Bureau of Investigation and the thrift are jointly investigating the embezzlement of at least $1.2 million from one of the firm's branches. Charter said the scheme involved the diversion of funds deposited by a small number of customers at the Jefferson Street branch in Roanoke, Va., principally through certificates of deposit.

L. Aubrey Goodson, Charter's president, said the prime suspect is a former bank employee who held a "responsible" position at the branch and quit shortly before the loss was uncovered. The former employee's whereabouts are unknown, Mr. Goodson said.

Mr. Goodson added that the thrift is not sure if any other employees might have been involved.

Charter said its previous and current bonding companies refused to cover the loss, and the thrift said it plans to file suit against them if no agreement can be reached. Charter's depositors are also insured by the Federal Deposit Insurance Corp.

Charter said the embezzlement won't affect its ability to repay its depositors.
