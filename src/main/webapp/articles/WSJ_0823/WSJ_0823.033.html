Rep. Gingrich Urges Cuts In Taxes to Reduce Deficit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/23/90

WASHINGTON -- Rep. Newt Gingrich, the second-ranking House Republican, further complicated deficit-reduction talks by calling for tax cuts rather than tax increases. The Georgian, who is a budget summit negotiator, said yesterday that he is putting together a package of tax cuts that will include a reduction in the rate on capital gains and, perhaps, a cut in the Social Security payroll tax. The aim is to stimulate the economy at a time when it appears on the verge of recession, he said.

Previously, Rep. Gingrich had expressed more willingness to increase taxes to help reduce the budget deficit. A spokesman said, "He is still in favor of the summit process and reaching the targets."

Still, his latest statement is yet another reminder that Mr. Gingrich and the House Republicans he represents are stalwarts against a tax increase. That position is yet another obstacle to reaching a fiscal 1991 budget accord, which would include tax increases and spending restraints.
