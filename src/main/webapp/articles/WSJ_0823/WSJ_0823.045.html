Have You Heard the New Slogan? Join the Party and See the World

  • Author: Dana Milbank
  • Date: 08/23/90

PITTSBURGH -- The local Republican Party still may not win elections, but at least now it offers a winning ticket. The woebegone county GOP, outnumbered by Democrats 2 1/2 to 1, has a new scheme to register Republican voters. How will it combat the electoral imbalance? Fiery rhetoric? Grass-roots appeal? A heavy dose of idealism?

"An exciting contest," proposes county GOP Chairman Henry Sneath, in a letter to fellow Republicans. He wants local youths to compete in registering Republican voters in Pennsylvania's 18th Congressional District. The winner will be selected in a raffle.

The grand prize: "An all-expense-paid weekend for two to Washington, D.C." But for those drooling for a trip to the Potomac, there's a catch: The tightfisted GOP will only pay $50 each for the winner and guest, including transportation and hotel.

The lucky couple will be chaperoned the entire weekend by doting party elders who will save airfare by driving them on the five-hour trek. The winner's discount hotel room will be paid for with leftover frequent flier mileage. And if you're going with your sweetheart, you may have to sleep in separate rooms, says Sallie Duhon, a 55-year-old ward coordinator who will chaperone the trip.

"It's not the fanciest trip," admits Rick Santorum, the Republican candidate for the 18th District. There may be no free lunch in Washington, but county Republicans hope party fat cats in the capital will pick up part of the tab.

When the 18th District, in suburban Pittsburgh, was redrawn in 1981, it gave the GOP a chance to gain a congressional seat. But the Republicans still haven't won it, nor do they hold any of the other four seats in Southwestern Pennsylvania.

Some 25 eager young Republicans have signed on, but the target of the registration drive, Democratic incumbent Doug Walgren, hasn't let the extravaganza disrupt his New England vacation. Says Jonathan Delano, Mr. Walgren's chief of staff: "No one is quaking in their boots."

One Republican volunteer, David Rice, 21, says he wasn't lured by the prize. "I don't see how you could be," he says.

Maybe that's because he hasn't heard about the "attractive runner-up prizes." These include tickets for the Republican "victory cruise" on the Allegheny River. The party has high hopes for the election, but apparently not too high: The cruise sails three weeks before Election Day.
