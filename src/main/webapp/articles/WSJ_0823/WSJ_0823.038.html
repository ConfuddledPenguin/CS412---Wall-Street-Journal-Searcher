OTC Focus: Unlisted Shares Plunge in Final Hour En Route to 4th Straight Losing Session

  • Author: Howard Hoffman Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 08/23/90

Despite an initial surge at the opening bell, over-the-counter stocks eased by midmorning and then fell drastically in the last hour of trading on their way to another closing loss. Traders say uncertainty about a resolution to the crisis in the Middle East is preoccupying investor minds. They say until there is positive news from the Persian Gulf the market can expect more erosion of stock prices.

"The market can take bad news and it can take good news. It can't take uncertainty," says Robert McCready, a senior trader for A.G. Edwards & Sons in St. Louis.

The Nasdaq Composite Index ended the day at 374.84, a 4.84 point loss. The Nasdaq's broad index's 1.3% loss was slightly better than the New York Stock Exchange Composite Index's 1.5% decline. The Dow Jones Industrial Average fell 1.7%, or 43.81.

OTC stocks surged at the opening bell only to show losses again before the first hour of trading was complete. Traders found little consolation in the opening burst and even less in a steep decline during the last hour.

The Nasdaq Composite Index had been down about two points at midafternoon.

Declining issues led advancers 1,267 to 722. Volume was 146.1 million shares compared with 166.5 million shares Tuesday.

This was the OTC's fourth consecutive losing session.

Robert O'Toole, head of OTC trading for Shearson Lehman Brothers, suggested that investors sold off telecommunications, cable and cellular stocks in an effort to take whatever profit remains in their portfolios.

The Nasdaq Utility Index outpaced the broader market's slump, closing 2.9% lower.

MCI Communications, the most active OTC issue, fell 5/8 to 33 on two million shares. Tele-Communications Class A shares fell 7/8 to 10 1/4. American Television & Communications was down 1/2 to 29 1/4.

McCaw Cellular was off 1 to 17 3/4. LIN Broadcasting tumbled 2 7/8 to 54 1/8. Contel Cellular dropped 1/2 to 13 1/2.

Nike has been hit by major declines recently and yesterday was no exception.

The athletic-shoe maker's shares fell 5 3/8 to 62 despite apparently good news that local black community groups in Portland, Ore., Nike's headquarters, would not join a boycott of Nike and its products. "Operation Push" has urged a boycott, claiming Nike does not provide adequate employment or business opportunities for blacks.

Traders speculated that investors are skeptical of Nike shares because of general market woes and concerns that a prolonged recession would hurt sales of expensive athletic shoes.

Elmwood Federal Savings Bank shares lost 50.9% of its value diving 6 3/4 to 6 1/2. Susquehanna Bancshares cited changes in market conditions late Tuesday when it terminated its agreement to purchase Elmwood Federal for about $24 million. Susquehanna shares added 3/4 to close at 17 3/4.

Justin Industries signed a definitive agreement to acquire Tony Lama Co. for $9 a share or about $52 million. Tony Lama jumped 3 1/2 to 8. Justin Industries fell 3/4 to 14 1/4.

Mission-Valley Bancorp and Northern California Bancorp announced they would merge. Their merger agreement calls for Northern California shareholders to receive 1.23 new shares for each existing share. Mission-Valley shares added 2 to close at 12 1/4. Northern California declined 1 1/2 to 16.

Xoma fell 1 3/4 to 18 1/4 on concerns about the Food and Drug Administration's requests for more information about Xoma drugs intended for the treatment of septic shock and complications associated with bone marrow transplants.

NovaCare announced it plans to offer another four million common shares in a public offering. NovaCare shares dropped 1 5/8 to 18 7/8.

Autodesk was one point higher at 46 3/4. The company reported late Tuesday that net income for the second quarter rose to 59 cents a share from 46 cents a year ago.

Stuart Hall fell 1 5/8 to 9. The company disclosed Tuesday that another investor group has expressed interest in acquiring a 7% stake in the company. Stuart Hall said it is not negotiating with that group but has negotiated with another group also interested in a 7% stake.
