Business Brief -- Anheuser-Busch Cos.: Plan to Build Theme Park, Resort in Spain Is Shelved

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/23/90

Anheuser-Busch Cos., a St. Louis-based brewer, confirmed that in April, it put "on hold" its plan to build a $300 million theme park and resort in Spain, citing difficulties with municipal officials in the region. Anheuser last year said it intended to build the park in the Costa Brava region, on the Mediterranean Sea near Barcelona. The 1,580-acre first phase was to include a golf course, hotel, retail facilities, homes and condominiums, and the theme park. In a possible second phase for expansion, initially expected to be completed in 2000, Anheuser tentatively planned to spend an additional $275 million.

But the municipality with jurisdiction over the park property separated in late 1989 into two cities, which then voided previous pacts on taxes and services, according to Anheuser. The company said that the situation hasn't been resolved, and that it is investigating "other suitable sites," should terms not be reached. A spokesman declined to elaborate on potential sites.
