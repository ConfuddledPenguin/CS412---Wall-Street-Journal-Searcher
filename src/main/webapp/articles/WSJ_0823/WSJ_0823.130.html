Old Kent Financial Plans to Continue Acquisition Strategy

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/23/90

DETROIT -- Old Kent Financial Corp. will "continue to aggressively seek acquisitions," particularly to increase its presence in the Chicago market, said chairman John C. Canepa. Canepa, speaking after addressing a group of Detroit analysts, said the bank holding company has been considering the acquisition of savings and loan associations because "today's pressure on bank stocks and expectations of sellers . . . has slowed acquisitions" of commercial banks. Savings and loans -- either solvent or bought from federal regulators -- offer "an avenue for low-cost expansion."

B.P. Sherwood III, the Grand Rapids, Mich., company's vice chairman and treasurer, said Old Kent will not seek to buy thrifts to enter new markets. He said the company will instead use its acquisitions to "enhance existing market presence."

Sherwood said Old Kent expects its loan activity to moderate in the final two quarters of the year. He said the company has seen its commercial and consumer lending taper off recently, while residential lending has remained steady.

The Old Kent officials said the company recently tightened its real-estate lending policies and re-emphasized Old Kent's policy of only lending in markets in which it has "a physical presence."

Canepa said Old Kent will seek to lower its expenses by taking such steps as consolidating some of its subsidiary banks and centralizing mortgage operations throughout the company.

Old Kent holds 21 banks with 195 offices in Michigan and Illinois. In the second quarter ended June 30, the company reported net income of $22.3 million, or 83 cents a primary share, compared with $21.2 million, or 81 cents a primary share, a year earlier. Old Kent had $8.1 billion in assets on June 30 compared with $8.2 billion a year earlier.
