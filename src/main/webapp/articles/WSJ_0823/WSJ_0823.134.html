Caesars World Posts 55% Earnings Decline For Fourth Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/23/90

LOS ANGELES -- Caesars World Inc. reported that earnings for its fourth quarter ended July 31 fell 55% to $7.4 million, or 31 cents a share, from $16.4 million, or 66 cents a share, a year earlier. Most of the decline came from Caesar's Nevada hotel/casino operations, particularly from low table-game win percentages at Caesars Tahoe. In Las Vegas, a decrease in wagering activity at Caesars Palace was partially offset by a higher win percentage by the company over the same period a year ago.

Revenue fell 5.1% to $223.7 million from $235.7 million.

Although Caesars' results were about what analysts expected, the company's stock fell $1.875 a share to $11.875 in New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday.

The drop may reflect "growing nervousness over what the problems in the Middle East and higher oil prices could do to the travel business," said Lee Isgur, an analyst at PaineWebber Inc. in New York.

But Caesars has had other problems. Competition from new casinos in both Las Vegas and Atlantic City, N.J., has hurt its business for the past few quarters. Caesars has also blamed higher provisions for bad debts, as well as increased casino payroll and operating costs related to completing a two-year renovation and expansion program.

For the full year, net declined 45% to $36.8 million, or $1.52 a share, compared with $66.9 million, or $2.72 a share, a year earlier. Revenue fell 3.5% to $870.2 million from $902 million.

Caesars said it bought back about 1.2 million of its common shares during fiscal 1990, reducing the number of shares currently outstanding to about 24 million. During the year, Caesars said it also bought back $19.8 million of its 13.5% notes due in 1997.
