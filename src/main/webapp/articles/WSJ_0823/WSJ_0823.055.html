REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: Conviction Without RICO

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/23/90

Federal Judge Robert Aguilar was convicted yesterday of illegally alerting a criminal target about a wiretap and then lying about it to the FBI. Aside from this egregious misconduct and crime by a judge, the lesson here for prosecutors is: RICO does not pay. In March, an earlier jury acquitted Judge Aguilar of one count and deadlocked on the other counts, including one that would have declared that the judge's actions somehow amounted to "racketeering." Prosecutors dropped the RICO charge for this second trial, with predictably better results.
