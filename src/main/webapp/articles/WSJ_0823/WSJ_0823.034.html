Coffee Exchange Broker Is Fined $11,500, Expelled

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/23/90

NEW YORK -- The Coffee, Sugar & Cocoa Exchange said floor broker Stephen Amato was fined $11,500 and expelled from exchange membership for violation of trading rules. The exchange, inaugurating a policy of publicly announcing expulsions and suspensions, accused Mr. Amato of violating rules relating to order execution, "cross trading" and the proper recording of trades.

The fine imposed by an exchange adjudication committee is one of the largest the coffee exchange has ever levied. The coffee exchange is one of five New York-based commodity futures exchanges, which along with the major Chicago commodity markets have been under increasing government pressure to discipline their members more severely.

Besides his explusion as a member, Mr. Amato was permanently barred from any employment on the coffee-exchange floor.

Mr. Amato couldn't be reached for comment. The coffee exchange notified the Commodity Futures Trading Commission that Mr. Amato didn't respond to the charges when they were brought several months ago, nor did he request a hearing to rebut them, a CFTC spokesman said.
