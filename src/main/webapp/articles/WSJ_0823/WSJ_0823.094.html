What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/23/90

CRUDE OIL PRICES passed the $30-a-barrel mark, their highest level since the early 1980s. The Mideast turmoil also pushed up prices of refined petroleum in frantic trading. Gasoline futures rose nearly three cents a gallon. Oil industry officials reported tightening supplies of some products in certain countries. ---

Stocks and bonds fell sharply on the surge in oil prices. The Treasury's 30-year bond fell more than a point, pushing its yield above 9%. The Dow Jones industrials fell 43.81 points. Broad stock indexes hit their lowest points in more than a year. The dollar closed mostly lower.

---

Gold prices climbed further on the Mideast crisis. The December futures contract gained $3.30 an ounce.

---

Tokyo stock prices plunged amid anxiety about the Mideast. The Nikkei marked a post-1987 low before closing down 1086.93 points. The index fell 1,003.96 points Thursday morning.

Long-term Japanese market sentiment has turned bearish due to a liquidity squeeze, say analysts.

---

Electrolux plans to lay off 10% of its 150,000 workers world-wide in a cost-cutting move. The Swedish appliance concern said the biggest cuts would come in the U.S. The firm's second-period pretax profit fell 56%.

---

Banks are concerned about an obscure federal law that lets members of the U.S. armed forces reduce the rates they pay on consumer loans.

---

Donald Trump took his shuttle airline off the market hours after he signed the final papers on his $65 million bank bailout. The developer said the airline is now profitable.

---

The NASD's effort to expand its trading system into London has hit a regulatory snag over reporting requirements. The project could be delayed or even scuttled.

---

Money market fund assets rose $6.5 billion in the latest week to a record $400.4 billion as both individuals and institutions sought safety.

---

National Intergroup agreed to put up for sale its only operating business, wholesale drug distributor FoxMeyer, caving in to the demands of dissident holder Centaur Partners.

---

A CBS News shake-up appears imminent, and may result in the ouster of the unit's president, David Burke.

---

CrossLand Savings' plan to meet new capital requirements was rejected by federal regulators.

---

Washington Post Co. agreed to finance an experimental wireless-communications system that would use lightweight portable phones. The venture could compete with existing cellular and traditional phone services.

---

RJR's $6.9 billion refinancing is being challenged in court on grounds that it is unfair to bondholders.
