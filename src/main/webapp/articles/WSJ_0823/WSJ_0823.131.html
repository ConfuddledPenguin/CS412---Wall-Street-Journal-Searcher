ATI Medical Expects To Post $250,000 Loss For Fiscal 4th Period

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/23/90

LAS VEGAS, Nev. -- ATI Medical Inc. said it expects to report a fourth-quarter net loss of about $250,000, primarily due to nonrecurring costs associated with the start-up of its Americare subsidiary. For the fiscal year ended July 31, the company said it will post net income of about $2.4 million, or 45 cents a share, on record revenue of about $50 million.

The renter of medical equipment said the earnings, which will be recorded officially in about six weeks, will be lower than originally anticipated by the company. The expenses connected to the Americare unit reduced earnings by about $500,000, or six cents a share, it said.

In the year-earlier fourth quarter, ATI posted earnings of $663,673, or 13 cents a share, on revenue of $10.2 million. For all of fiscal 1989, the company reported earnings of $2.2 million, or 41 cents a share, on revenue of $39.1 million.

ATI said that while a lower hospital census in the last three months of the fiscal year also affected operating results, the company's total volume in July was at record levels and ATI resumed profitability during that month. The company said its cash position remains strong.

"Given the way our stock has moved, we wanted to reassure people that it was only one {troubled} quarter . . . that it isn't a disaster," Paul E. Stevenson, ATI's president and chief executive officer, said in an interview. In American Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, ATI closed at $5.875 a share, up 50 cents.

Despite the temporary disruption in earnings growth, Mr. Stevenson added, ATI should continue to show profit improvement in fiscal 1991, when revenue should reach at least $60 million, a 20% jump from 1990.
