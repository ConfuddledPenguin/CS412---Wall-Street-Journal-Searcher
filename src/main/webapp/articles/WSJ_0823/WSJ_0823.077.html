International Brief -- Pioneer Electronic Corp.: Firm Postpones Its Selling DAT Recorders in Japan

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/23/90

Pioneer Electronic Corp. of Japan will postpone the domestic sale of its digital audiotape, or DAT, recorders, initially scheduled to start in Japan this autumn, a spokesman said, citing the company's concerns about copyright protection problems involved with DAT recording. He declined to specify the company's plans or to say when the technology will be made available on the domestic market, but said Pioneer will continue to study possible marketing of the DAT recorder. Unlike analog tape recordings, digital recordings deteriorate very little; thus, record makers fear that DAT machines could result in a virtually endless stream of high-quality copies that could cut into their sales. The spokesman also said introduction of DAT decks domestically could threaten favorable sales of Pioneer's compact disk players.

Japanese companies started selling DAT recorders after the government adopted a new standard last May that limits a user's ability to make copies of CD and other digital recordings.
