Technology: David Sarnoff Center, Two Electronics Firms Win Defense Contract

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/23/90

PRINCETON, N.J. -- The David Sarnoff Research Center said it and two major electronics companies were awarded a $7.7 million contract from the Defense Department to develop a high-resolution video workstation. The new workstation, which would combine capabilities for handling text, video and graphics in a high-powered computer, will be developed by the Sarnoff Center along with Sun Microsystems Inc., Mountain View, Calif., and Texas Instruments Inc., Dallas. The Sarnoff Center is a unit of SRI International, a contract-based research and consulting firm based in Menlo Park, Calif.

The military would likely use such a computer as a building block for "combat information systems," a Sarnoff spokesman said. He said the basic technology would also have numerous commercial uses, including video teleconferencing and medical imaging.

Separately, the Sarnoff Center said it was awarded a $3 million contract for a supercomputer dedicated to processing video information.

Both awards, administered through the Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency, were originally announced last October, but no dollar amounts were specified at that time.
