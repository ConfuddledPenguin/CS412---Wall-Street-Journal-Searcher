KPMG to Open Soviet Office

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/23/90

NEW YORK -- KPMG Peat Marwick, a major accounting and consulting firm, said it will open an office in the Soviet Union in the fall, as part of its expansion into Central and Eastern Europe. Larry D. Horner, KPMG's chairman, said the Soviet office will focus initially on counseling U.S. businesses about opportunities in the U.S.S.R. Eventually, the company said, it hopes to form a joint venture to provide tax and auditing services.

The KPMG move follows similar ventures by other U.S. accounting firms in the Soviet Union. Last June, Arthur Andersen & Co. and Ernst & Young formed ventures with Soviet firms that allowed the companies to perform official audits of U.S.-Soviet businesses.
