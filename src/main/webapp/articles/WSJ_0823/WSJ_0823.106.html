Business Brief -- CMS Energy Corp.: Transmission Line Will Link Michigan, Indiana Utilities

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/23/90

CMS Energy Corp. said it agreed to jointly build a $105 million transmission line connecting its Consumers Power Co. subsidiary with a utility subsidiary of PSI Resources Inc. CMS said it plans to use the transmission line to sell electric power from its 49%-owned Midland Cogeneration Venture, a 1,370 megawatt, gas-fired plant. CMS of Jackson, Mich., said it will spend about $55 million for the project. Plainfield, Ind.-based PSI Resources will contribute about $50 million.

The companies will link their systems with a 345-kilovolt transmission line between Battle Creek, Mich., and Akron, Ind.

The transmission facilities are expected to be in operation by December 1992. The project is contingent upon regulatory approval.

The Midland Cogeneration Venture was designed as a nuclear power plant but abandoned before completion in 1984. The plant reopened on March 16 as a gas-fired plant. CMS said its Consumers Power subsidiary purchases more than 800 megawatts from Midland Cogeneration Venture.
