International: Norsk Data Breaks Even

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/23/90

STOCKHOLM -- Norsk Data AS said it broke even in this year's first half, a surprisingly early recovery from huge losses the previous two years. The Oslo-based computer concern reported pretax profit of one million Norwegian kroner ($165,000) for this year's first six months, compared with a loss of 194 million kroner a year earlier.

Sales climbed 10% to 1.22 billion kroner from 1.1 billion kroner.

Orders were flat in the first six months, but an 8% decline in operating costs helped produce a swing in operating profit to 10 million kroner, compared with a year-earlier operating loss of 214 million kroner.

Norsk Data didn't give a forecast for the full year. But Chief Executive Officer Erik Engebretsen said sales gains for various new products in several key markets showed the company was gradually regaining confidence of customers. "It shows that the painful cost-cutting measures taken last year are working," Mr. Engebretsen said.

By laying off 700 employees, or 21% of its work force during the past 12 months, Norsk Data lowered its break-even level to a level corresponding to annual sales of 2.5 billion kroner this year.
