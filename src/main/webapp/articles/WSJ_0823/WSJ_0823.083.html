Business Brief -- Forstmann, Little & Co.: General Instrument Buy-Out Completed for $1.5 Billion

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/23/90

Forstmann, Little & Co. said it completed its acquisition of General Instrument Corp., a cable-TV equipment company, for $1.5 billion. Some 95.5% of General Instrument, or 29.9 million common shares, was tendered under the New York buy-out firm's $44.50-a-share offer, which expired Tuesday at 5 p.m., a Forstmann spokeswoman said. The remaining shares will be redeemed at the same price.

Including options that were converted as part of the purchase, General Instrument had about 34.6 million shares outstanding, she added.

In connection with the deal, General Instrument said it will redeem all its remaining subordinated convertible debentures on Sept. 12 for a total price of $1,068.27, including interest, for each $1,000 face amount. Alternatively, before the redemption, holders may convert the debentures, which pay 7.25% and are due in 2012, into $1,097.01 in cash for each $1,000 outstanding.

The Forstmann spokeswoman said virtually all debentures had been converted prior to the buy-out, and she expected any remaining holders to take the cash offer.

In its fiscal year ended Feb. 28, General Instrument earned $98.7 million, or $3.27 a share, on revenue of $1.38 billion.
