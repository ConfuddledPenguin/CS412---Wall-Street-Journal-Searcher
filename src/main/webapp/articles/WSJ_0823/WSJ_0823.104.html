International Brief -- ACEC-Union Miniere S.A.: Firm, in Joint Venture, Bids For Mexican Copper Mine

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/23/90

ACEC-Union Miniere S.A. said it and Mexicana de Cobre S.A. had offered to buy the Cananea copper mine from the Mexican government for $475 million. The Belgian non-ferrous metals and mining concern said it had formed a joint venture with Mexicana de Cobre to bid for the mine, which is being auctioned as part of the Mexican government's privatization program.

ACEC-UM said it would hold a 21% stake in the joint venture, to be called Mexicana de Cananea S.A. de CV.

The company said it wouldn't comment on the details of its offer until the Mexican government had issued its response, which is required within 10 days. The Belgian company said its offer, made Monday, was the highest bid.

Spokesman Philippe de Vynck said ACEC-UM is seeking to diversify its sources of copper supply by investing in the Mexican mine.

ACEC-UM's Metallurgie Hoboken-Overpelt unit is the largest refiner of copper in Europe. ACEC-UM is 82% owned by holding company Societe Generale de Belgique S.A.
