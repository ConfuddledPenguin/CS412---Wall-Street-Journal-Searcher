Who's News: Merrill Lynch Names Sowanick to Replace Kochan as Strategist

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/23/90

NEW YORK -- Merrill Lynch & Co. said it named Thomas Sowanick as chief bond strategist, replacing James Kochan who departed after 10 years at the securities firm. Mr. Kochan left Merrill to oversee the fixed income money-management area of Baird Asset Management, a division of regional investment banking firm Robert W. Baird & Co., in Milwaukee.

At Merrill, Mr. Kochan had played an important part in mapping the firm's bond advice.

In the spring of last year, Merrill ran a huge newspaper advertising campaign, strongly recommending "high quality" bonds. That turned out to be a good call, as interest rates fell and bond prices rose. But earlier this year, Mr. Kochan said: "We are constructive" on bonds, "which is less than bullish but not bearish." Bond prices fell sharply for several months after that.

A native of the Manitowoc, Wis., area, Mr. Kochan said he had wanted to return to his home state, which the Baird position allowed him to do. Mr. Kochan also said he had "always planned on some day getting involved in the asset management function."

Mr. Sowanick had been a bond strategist under Mr. Kochan, and often filled in for him.

A Merrill spokesman said, "We are obviously disappointed at Jim's decision, and at the same time we wish him well in his new challenge. Jim has been an outstanding research professional at Merrill Lynch. His service to our sales force and clients has been exemplary. We are very fortunate to have Thomas Sowanick to orchestrate the strategy area. His role, comparable to Jim Kochan's, gives us an experienced person to continue to run the fixed income strategy effort."
