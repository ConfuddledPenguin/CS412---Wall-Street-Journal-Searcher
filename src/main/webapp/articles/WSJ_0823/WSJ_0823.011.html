International: Yen's Rise Helps Economy In Japan, MITI Reports

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 08/23/90

TOKYO -- The benefits for Japan of increased imports since the 1985 Plaza Accord have outweighed the drawbacks, the Ministry of International Trade and Industry said. Since the Group of Seven industrialized nations agreed to an appreciation of the yen against the dollar in September 1985, imports as a percentage of total Japanese goods have risen 1.95%, to 8.58% in 1989, MITI said.

MITI said rising imports have helped free up 310,000 workers for other jobs, alleviating Japan's labor shortage.

With the latest job data showing 147 job offers for every 100 job seekers, the labor-saving impact of imports has helped head off a sharp rise in wages which could have threatened Japan's economic expansion, a MITI official said.

The official said cheaper import prices also raised Japanese consumers' purchasing power and allowed more capital spending by Japanese corporations.

Among the drawbacks, MITI said the rise in imports lowered domestic production by 15 trillion yen ($102.2 billion) and lowered gross national product by 6.4 trillion yen.

MITI said although internationalization of the economy was an overall benefit to Japan, factors such as rising oil prices and a weak U.S. economy could slow the pace in coming years.
