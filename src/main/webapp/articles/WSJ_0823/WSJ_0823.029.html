The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report. MUNICIPAL

New York City Transit Authority -- $205.8 million of transit facilities revenue bonds, Series 1990, Livingston Plaza Project, due Jan 1, 1993-2004, 2020 and 2021, with capital appreciation bonds due 2005-2010, priced by a Smith Barney, Harris Upham & Co. group to yield from 6.40% in 1993 to 7.58% in 2020. Current interest serial bonds are priced at par to yield from 6.40% in 1993 to 7.35% in 2004. There are $113.5 million of 7.50% term bonds priced at 99 to yield 7.58% in 2020 and $15.3 million of 6% term bonds priced at 81.83 to yield 7.53% in 2021. Capital appreciation bonds are priced to yield from 7.40% in 2005 and 2006 to 7.50% in 2009 and 2010. The bonds are insured and triple-A-rated.

Massachusetts Bay Transportation Authority -- $85.8 million of certificates of participation, 1990 Series A, due Aug. 1, 1991-2000, 2005 and 2015, tentatively priced by a Lehman Brothers group to yield from 6.25% in 1992 to 7.50% in 2005. Current interest serial bonds are priced at par to yield from 6.25% in 1992 to 7.10% in 2000. Bonds due in 1991 are not formally reoffered. There is $14.9 million of 7.50% term bonds priced at par. Term bonds due in 2015 are not formally reoffered. The bonds are insured and triple-A-rated.

New York State Dormitory Authority -- $50.4 million of New York State Department of Health refunding revenue bonds, 1990 Issue, due July 1, 1992-2001, priced by a Morgan Stanley & Co. group to yield from 6.40% in 1992 to 7.50% in 2001. There are no term bonds. The bonds are rated single-A/triple-B-plus.

ASSET-BACKED

Colonial Credit Card Trust 1990A -- $215 million of floating-rate securities backed by Visa and MasterCard receivables offered at 42 basis points over the one-month London interbank offered rate via First Boston Corp. The securities have an expected final maturity of April 16, 1995, a legal final maturity of Oct. 15, 1996, and a 4.3-year average life at a 15.72% payment rate on the underlying loans. Interest will be reset monthly, with an 11.5% rate cap. The securities have a subordinated class that now totals 14% of the senior securities. The first principal payment will be Oct. 17, 1994. There is a principal revolving period that ends Aug. 31, 1994. Monthly interest payments begin Oct. 15. Colonial National Bank USA, the seller, servicer and originator of the underlying credit card loans, is a unit of Advanta Corp.
