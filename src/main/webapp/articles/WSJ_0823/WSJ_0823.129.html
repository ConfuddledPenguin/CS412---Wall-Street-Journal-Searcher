Consolidated Natural Gas Co.

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/23/90

PITTSBURGH -- Consolidated Natural Gas Co. said its CNG Transmission Corp. unit reached a consent agreement with the state to locate and clean up contamination from its Pennsylvania gas pipeline. A company spokesman estimated CNG Transmission would pay $900,000 for the initial search for contamination.

The utility also agreed to pay the Pennsylvania Department of Environmental Resources as much as $500,000 annually in monitoring costs.

"There's some suspicions there might be problems," he said. "We've agreed to conduct a study of our facilities, and if we find any problems, we'll clean it up."
