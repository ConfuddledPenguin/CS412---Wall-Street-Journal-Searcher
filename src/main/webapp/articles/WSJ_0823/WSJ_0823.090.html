Who's News Brief -- Comprehensive Care Corp.: Dissident Holders Succeed In Bid to Oust Directors

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/23/90

Comprehensive Care Corp., St. Louis, said dissident shareholders succeeded in their effort to oust the directors of the provider of chemical dependency programs. A spokesman for the dissident group said that the new directors intend to meet on Monday and remove the company's chief executive officer, W. James Nicol, and that the new chief executive will be James P. Carmany, a 51-year-old former Comprehensive Care executive vice president who left the company last year in a dispute with management.

The company said six nominees of the shareholders committee were elected directors: Norman L. Perry, Harvey G. Felsen, Howard S. Groth, Robert I. Miller, Charles Moore and Michael K. O'Toole. Mr. Nicol, the ousted board's nominee, also was elected a director.

The dissident group, which holds about 9.1% of Comprehensive Care's stock, launched its proxy fight after management's attempt to sell the company failed last year and the stock price collapsed. The group, called the Shareholders Committee to Rejuvenate CompCare, has said it might seek a restructuring of the company's assets and liabilities.
