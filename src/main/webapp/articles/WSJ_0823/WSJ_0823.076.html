International Brief -- DAF N.V.: Truck Maker Reports Loss Of $18 Million for 1st Half

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/23/90

DAF N.V., hurt by its heavy dependence on the weak British truck market, reported a loss of 32.1 million guilders ($18 million) for the first half. The loss, which was expected, compares with net income of 74.5 million guilders a year earlier.

For the full year, the Eindhoven-based truck maker said it expects to record a "small" net income "provided that negative general economic developments do not prevail." Nonetheless, some London stockbrokers said they were sticking with forecasts that DAF will have a loss for the year. John Longhurst of James Capel & Co. forecast a loss of 60 million guilders, while Stephen Reitman of UBS Phillips & Drew projected a 50 million guilder deficit.

The company's troubles have prompted some analysts to question whether it will be able to remain independent. DAF said it is continuing to study possible joint ventures or other cooperation projects with various possible partners in Europe and the U.S., including Navistar International Corp. A spokesman noted that DAF already cooperates with other manufacturers in some areas; for instance, the company is developing delivery vans jointly with Renault.
