International Brief -- Arabian Oil Co.: Tokyo Firm's Pretax Profit Slipped 1.4% in First Half

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/23/90

Arabian Oil Co., Japan's biggest oil producer, reported that its unconsolidated pretax profit in the first half of fiscal 1990 slipped 1.4% to 27.87 billion yen ($190.1 million) from 28.25 billion yen the year earlier. Although sales rose 1% in the half ended June 30, operating profit at the Tokyo company was down 1.5% to 28.07 billion yen from 28.5 billion yen. As a result, net income fell to 1.12 billion, down 2.6% from 1.15 billion yen the year earlier.

Per-share earnings inched down to 21.59 yen from 22.72 yen. Sales volume fell 12% to 6.7 million kiloliters. But the value of sales rose to 94.78 billion yen from 93.87 billion yen.
