Masco Industries Inc. Could Post a Charge In the Second Half

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/23/90

TAYLOR, Mich. -- Masco Industries Inc. said it could take a pretax charge of as much as $20 million, or 15 cents a share after taxes, against its second-half earnings, as a result of General Motors Corp.'s scrapping a joint program to develop the Chevrolet Beretta convertible. Masco Industries' Cars & Concepts subsidiary had been jointly working with GM's Chevrolet division to develop the convertible Beretta. A GM spokesman said the planned 1990 model originally was to be introduced in May, when it was used as the pace car in the Indianapolis 500. But because of delays, the car still hasn't reached the market.

"Based on our analysis of the convertible market and the size and price range, we concluded that a sufficient opportunity to market the Beretta convertible profitably no longer existed," the GM spokesman said. "For that reason we have discontinued the project."

Masco Industries said it "will pursue recovery of its investment" in the project, but added that "recovery is uncertain."

Masco Industries, a manufacturer of custom engineering components and specialty products, generates about 50% of its annual revenue from auto-related contracts.

Despite the possible write-off, the company "still expects to be profitable in the second half," said Richard Manoogian, Masco Industries' chairman and president.

Masco's first-half net income was down 64% from the year-earlier period, to $17.8 million, or 23 cents a share. The decline was largely the result of a decline in U.S. car and truck production, Mr. Manoogian said.
