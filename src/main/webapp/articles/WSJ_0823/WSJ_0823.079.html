Business Brief -- American President Companies: Concern Plans Cost Cutting, Reorganization With Layoffs

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/23/90

American President Companies Ltd., Oakland, Calif., said it plans a cost-cutting and reorganization effort that will lead to layoffs and write-downs before the end of 1990. The transportation and shipping concern said it didn't yet know how many layoffs there would be or how the write-downs might affect its financial results, which already are down this year because of broad problems in the shipping business. American President had 4,994 employees at year end.

The company said the process will take place in two phases, with the first focusing on eliminating "non-essential positions" and streamlining corporate organization. The second phase of the program, which will last through 1991, will focus on increasing efficiency through investments in new equipment, better training programs and other methods.

A spokesman said the moves were in response to the company's "poor" earnings performance in 1989 and so far this year. The company has been hurt by a slowdown in the general economy and by higher fuel prices, a problem that will only worsen with the current turmoil in the Middle East.

For the six months, American President had a loss of $1 million, or 22 cents a share, on revenue of $1.1 billion.
