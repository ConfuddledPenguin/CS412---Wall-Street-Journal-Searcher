AMR's Eagle Unit Places Order for ATR Airplanes

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/23/90

FORT WORTH, Texas -- AMR Eagle, the regional carrier division of AMR Corp., said it agreed to buy 25 ATR turboprop aircraft and take options for an additional 75, in a deal that could be valued at as much as $800 million. AMR, parent of American Airlines, said the order also included the leasing of an additional 16 planes made by ATR, a joint venture of Aeritalia S.p.A. of Italy and Aerospatiale S.A. of France.

AMR Eagle consists of six carriers that fly to smaller cities in the U.S. and feed passenger traffic into the larger system of American Airlines. AMR Eagle has 211 planes, and with the ATR order, expects its fleet to increase by 70 planes next year.

The ATR transaction, which had been expected, consists of orders for 16 ATR-42s and options on an additional 25, and orders for nine Super ATRs and options on 50 more of the planes. The ATR-42 is a 46-passenger aircraft, and the Super ATR is a stretch version that seats 64. The first of the planes on order are to be delivered in March, in a schedule that will stretch into 1996.

In Rome, Aeritalia valued the AMR order at $1 billion, and said this is the biggest order it has received for its planes. An AMR Eagle spokesman declined to comment on the value of the contract. But industry analysts said the $1 billion figure is on the high side. As the largest U.S. carrier, AMR typically negotiates hefty discounts for itself, particularly on large aircraft orders such as this one.

This marks AMR Eagle's second large order for planes this month. Earlier, it ordered 35 Jetstream Super 31s made by British Aerospace PLC and optioned 15 more. It also took 50 options on Jetstream 41s.
