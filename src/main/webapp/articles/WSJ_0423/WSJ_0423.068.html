PacifiCare Makes Acquisition Offer To Ailing Maxicare

  • Author: Rhonda L. Rundle
  • Date: 04/23/90

Maxicare Health Plans Inc., a Los Angeles-based health maintenance organization that has been operating under bankruptcy-law protection for 13 months, said it received an unsolicited acquisition offer from rival PacifiCare Health Systems Inc. "The offer is complex; it's about 15 pages and includes a number of components, including cash," said a Maxicare spokesman. Members of Maxicare and PacifiCare management and their advisers will meet next week to discuss it, he said. However, the offer formally expires at the close of business today.

The offer had been expected after PacifiCare said Thursday that it was preparing an acquisition proposal. Maxicare, which issued a statement Thursday saying that the company wasn't for sale, moderated its response after the offer arrived the next day.

"We will seek to determine if this offer is in the best interests of our enrollees, our health-care providers, the creditors and shareholders, and our employees and management," said Peter J. Ratican, chairman and chief executive officer.

Maxicare said it plans to proceed with a scheduled public hearing set for today in bankruptcy court in Santa Ana, Calif., to discuss the company's previously filed reorganization plan. Under terms of the plan, shareholders would receive $102 million in cash, $67 million in notes and newly issued common shares and warrants.

Wayne Lowell, PacifiCare chief financial officer, said on Thursday that the Cypress, Calif.-based company would submit a "serious" offer and that the company believes Maxicare would make an "excellent strategic addition." Roughly 40% of Maxicare's 320,000 members are in California, where PacifiCare has a strong presence. Maxicare also has operations in six other states, including Indiana, Illinois and Wisconsin.

At its peak a few years ago, Maxicare was the nation's largest publicly traded HMO with more than two million members. The company fell on hard times after it went deeply into debt to acquire two other HMOs. In the nine months ended Sept. 30, 1989, Maxicare had a $22.3 million loss on revenue of $412.3 million. Maxicare shares are traded in the pink sheets.

PacifiCare said that 70% of its 636,000 members are in California. The HMO also owns and operates HMOs in Oregon, Oklahoma, Texas and Washington and an insurance subsidiary licensed in 33 states. In the year ended Sept. 30, PacifiCare posted net income of $10.9 million on revenue of $650.2 million.

In national over-the-counter trading Friday, PacifiCare shares closed unchanged at $19.75. The acquisition proposal was announced after the end of trading.
