Ford Will Idle Plant For One Week, Cuts Orders From Mazda

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/23/90

DETROIT -- Ford Motor Co. said it will idle its Lorain, Ohio, assembly plant for a week starting next Monday, citing a shortage of orders for the Thunderbird and Mercury Cougar cars built there. The auto maker said it already had factored the temporary closing into its previously announced, second-quarter production schedules. A Ford spokesman said the market for midsized, sporty two-door coupes, such as those built in the Lorain plant, has been weaker than the car market in general this year.

The Lorain plant has about 2,700 hourly employees and produces 5,600 cars a week, using two shifts.

Separately, Mazda Motor Corp. of Japan confirmed that Ford reduced by 6,000 the number of cars it will order from Mazda's U.S. assembly plant, in Flat Rock, Mich., in May. Mazda builds the Probe model there for Ford, as well as Mazda MX-6s and 626s. The plant produced 19,344 cars last month, down 8% from a year earlier.

Mazda said the reduction won't result in layoffs for any of the 2,900 hourly employees at the plant. But the company said it is uncertain whether it will continue to employ temporary workers there. Mazda won't specify how many temporary employees it has, but union officials have put the number at about 100.

Mazda said it plans to meet the production cut by operating the line fewer hours. While the assembly line is idled, the Mazda workers will participate in additional training programs.
