Kemper's Reserve Of $19.7 Million Is Weather Protection

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/23/90

LONG GROVE, Ill. -- Kemper Corp. said it established $19.7 million in first-quarter reserves to cover weather-related losses in its reinsurance and property-casualty insurance segments. The reserves will result in an after-tax charge to net income of $13 million, or 27 cents a share. In the year-ago period, the insurance and investment services concern had net income of $80.8 million, or $1.37 a share, including $10.6 million in profit from discontinued operations.

Recent violent wind and rainstorms in Europe will affect the company's reinsurance segment. And winter-related storms in the Midwest had an impact on Kemper's property-casualty operation, the company said.

Kemper also said that a restructuring under way at its brokerage firms will contribute to an anticipated loss from that operation, which is also plagued by trading losses and lower volume. Business "improved in March," however, and the company said its securities brokers "will have a substantially smaller loss in the first quarter of 1990" than they suffered in the fourth quarter of 1988.

Corrections & Amplifications

KEMPER CORP. expects to report that its securities operations had a "substantially smaller" loss in the first quarter of 1990 than the operations incurred in the fourth quarter of 1989. In yesterday's edition, the comparison was misstated.
