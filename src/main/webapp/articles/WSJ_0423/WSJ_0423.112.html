NESB Reports Loss For Quarter, Resulting In Technical Default

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/23/90

NEW LONDON, Conn. -- NESB Corp. reported a first-quarter loss vs. year-ago profit and, as a result, is in technical default of certain loan covenants related to its 1988 acquisition of OmniBank of Connecticut Inc. The loss was $13.9 million, or $1.80 a share, compared with net income of $2.5 million, or 32 cents a share, a year earlier. The results include a $14 million provision for potential loan losses and a $2 million provision for possible losses related to real estate.

The bank said the loss put it in default of covenants of a $50 million long-term debt agreement with a group of insurance companies. The money was used to finance the NESB's $48 million acquisition of OmniBank. In February, OmniBank was merged with New England Savings Banks, NESB's only subsidiary.

NESB said it has been negotiating with the insurance companies about arranging waivers of the covenants, and discussions are continuing.

Robin Honiss, president and chief executive officer, said the provisions reflected "escalating weakness" in the southeastern Connecticut real estate market throughout the first quarter. He said the bank expects further deterioration in the real estate market in 1990 at "a more serious pace than previously anticipated."

The company's total assets declined to $1.35 billion as of March 31, from $1.5 billion a year earlier.
