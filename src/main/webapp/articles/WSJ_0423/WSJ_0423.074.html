Marketing & Media: Time Warner Buys 19.5% of Six Flags For $19.5 Million

  • Author: Kevin Goldman
  • Date: 04/23/90

NEW YORK -- Time Warner Inc. purchased a 19.5% stake in Six Flags Corp., which owns and operates seven amusement parks in the U.S. The purchase, valued at $19.5 million, makes Time Warner a player in a part of the entertainment business in which two of its competitors, Walt Disney Co. and MCA Inc.'s Universal unit, already enjoy success.

With its established stable of Warner Bros. cartoon characters, such as Bugs Bunny and Daffy Duck, Time Warner could reposition Six Flags' theme parks to emphasize the animated cadre. The Six Flags purchase is the first made by Time Warner's recently formed venture unit Time Warner Enterprises, headed by former MTV Networks president Robert W. Pittman.

The investment by Time Warner was a natural fit because Six Flags parks have been licensed by Warner Bros., a unit of Time Warner, for the exclusive use of the Looney Tunes characters for the past six years. "We think there is a lot of growth to be had in the merchandising of the Looney Tunes characters," said a spokeswoman for Time Warner.

In the future, said James Prager, executive vice president at Six Flags, the "intent" would be to have even more interaction with Time Warner properties, such as characters from the Warner Bros. movie "Batman."

The agreement also gives Time Warner options to increase its stake to as much as 50% in Six Flags. And analysts expect Time Warner to do just that. "Time Warner is tiptoeing into the theme park business, but I wouldn't be surprised to see a whole transformation of the Six Flags chain," said Richard MacDonald, a media analyst with the firm MacDonald Grippo Riely Inc.

Six Flags, based in Arlington, Texas, and owned by an investor group associated with Wesray Capital Corp. and Six Flags management, is in need of an influx of cash. Six of its seven locations need upgrading. New roller coasters will be added, for example, at a total cost of $27 million, said Mr. Prager.

Industry officials, who declined to be identified, said MCA and Paramount Communications Inc. both explored the possibility of purchasing a stake in Six Flags.

The Time Warner spokeswoman said that Time Magazine Co., a subsidiary of Time Warner, and Warner Bros.' worldwide merchandising unit will have an active role with the parks.

Last year, Six Flags had revenue of $394 million, Mr. Prager said. Total annual attendance for the seven parks is about 17 million. By comparison, Disney's Magic Kingdom, EPCOT Center, and the new Disney-MGM Studios, all in the Orlando, Fla., area, had attendance of more than 23 million in 1989.

The potential for Time Warner if the theme parks are hits is enormous. In 1989, Disney's three parks world-wide registered $2.6 billion in revenue. Analysts say that it wouldn't be unlikely for Time Warner to expand Six Flags' reach beyond the U.S.
