National Education Posts $8.4 Million Loss For the First Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/23/90

IRVINE, Calif. -- National Education Corp. posted a first-quarter net loss of $8.4 million, or 28 cents a share, reflecting continued problems at its Applied Learning subsidiary, which has had troubles with customer credits. The company indicated that the loss for the quarter could be even greater once it finalizes a wrongful-termination settlement with its former chief executive officer, David Bright. That settlement would be charged against latest first-quarter earnings, which will be restated. A recent arbitration decision found that the termination of Mr. Bright wasn't "for cause" as defined by his contract. National Education declined to estimate the amount of the settlement, but said it could be "material." The exact amount will be determined at an arbitration hearing next month.

In the year-earlier first quarter, the vocational training company had net income of $7 million, or 24 cents share.

Revenue fell 14% to $85.4 million from $99.5 million.

Separately, National Education said it elected J.W. Cwiertnia, its 48-year-old president and chief operating officer, to the additional post of chief executive officer, succeeding David C. Jones, 68, who remains chairman. Mr. Cwiertnia resigned as president of National Education in March 1989 to become chief operating officer of another company, but returned in July after Mr. Bright left.

The company also said its lenders agreed in principle to grant it a 90-day extension on a requirement that it raise $10 million in additional capital by April 30. National Education said the lenders agreed to the extension because it had positive cash flow in the latest first quarter. Mr. Cwiertnia said the company is exploring "several options" to raise the capital.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading Friday, National Education shares closed at $4.625, off 12.5 cents.
