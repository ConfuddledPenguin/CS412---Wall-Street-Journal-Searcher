Greyhound Lays Off Maintenance Workers In Garages Nationwide

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/23/90

DALLAS -- Strikebound Greyhound Lines Inc., a unit of GLI Holding Co., said Friday that it had laid off 275 maintenance workers in various garages around the country. Company spokesman George Gravley said layoffs of office personnel "could happen," but no layoffs of drivers are anticipated.

In addition to mechanics and their helpers, those laid off included parts clerks, truck drivers and other service workers, the company said. "Obviously, we have a reduced work load because of the strike," Mr. Gravley said.

The action came as an often violent strike by 9,400 Greyhound employees represented by the Amalgamated Transit Union entered its eighth week. No resumption of negotiations on a new contract have been scheduled.

Mr. Gravley said hundreds of union maintenance workers crossed the picket lines when the strike began March 2. In addition to 690 maintenance workers represented by the International Association of Machinists and Aerospace Workers, which is not on strike against the company, 695 of the 1,475 ATU-represented maintenance workers also returned to work.

The layoffs included 155 ATU members and 120 members of the machinists' union, said Mr. Gravley, who added that Greyhound hoped to eventually call them back.

Since the strike began March 2, Greyhound has struggled to operate with replacement drivers and union crossovers. The company is running about 72% of the scheduled routes it operated before the strike. Ridership has been hovering between 50% and 60% of pre-strike levels, according to the company, which says it is approaching the "break even" point in terms of revenue.

Union officials dispute that claim. "Every indicator points to financial disaster for Greyhound Lines Inc.," ATU President James La Sala said Friday. "Greyhound management is now at a crossroads. It can either negotiate a settlement to this contract dispute or allow this country's only national bus system to slide into financial ruin."
