A U.S. HOSTAGE WAS RELEASED by pro-Iranian kidnappers in Lebanon. After nearly 39 months in captivity, educator Robert Polhill was freed in Beirut by Islamic Jihad for the Liberation of Palestine, a group that holds at least two other Americans. Polhill, who was taken to Syria before heading to West Germany, was the first American captive to be released in almost three-and-a-half years. The 55-year-old New Yorker said he was "happy and grateful" about his release, but expressed concern about those still held. Bush thanked Syria and Iran for their roles in winning Polhill's freedom, and called for the release of all hostages.

The terms of Polhill's release, which followed a series of communiques from the extremists, weren't known. At least 17 foreigners are missing in Lebanon.

MILLIONS OF PEOPLE MARKED Earth Day with rallies around the globe.

An estimated 200 million people in 140 nations on all seven continents joined events dedicated to preserving the environment. More than 3,600 U.S. communities staged programs to commemorate the 20th anniversary of the Earth Day anti-pollution movement, cleaning beaches and planting trees. About 100,000 people gathered in Washington to hear environmental activists, and thousands watched a balloon decorated as the Earth rise in New York City.

Bush expressed support for a tanker-free zone off Florida's coast and indicated that a decision on offshore oil drilling would be made "very soon."

A Communist official in Moscow warned that Lithuania's independence drive is leading the Baltic republic to catastrophe, but denied that the Kremlin had imposed a total economic blockade. In Vilnius, officials reaffirmed their secessionist stand and sent the Soviet premier a list of items that Lithuania may cease supplying to Soviet industry.

The White House is moving to curtail planned investment and trade assistance to the Soviet Union in the wake of Moscow's efforts to crush Lithuania's independence bid, Bush administration officials said.

---

Voters in Croatia cast ballots in the Yugoslav republic's first free multiparty elections since before World War II. The Communists, who have ruled for 45 years, were facing a challenge from a center-right coalition and a nationalist party with separatist leanings. Neighboring Slovenians also voted in elections to choose a regional president.

---

Pope John Paul II announced plans to convene an unprecedented meeting of Europe's bishops to discuss the Roman Catholic Church's role on the Continent after the collapse of Communist governments. The pontiff, who also intends to visit Cuba in December, made his remarks at the conclusion of a two-day visit to Czechoslovakia.

---

Nigeria's military government said it crushed a coup attempt after heavy fighting against rebel troops in Lagos. The rebellion was launched by junior officers seeking the breakup of the African country's federation of 19 states in an effort to end Moslem domination. Casualty figures weren't disclosed.

---

Mandela called for an intensified armed struggle against South Africa's white minority government to spur talks to end apartheid. Speaking in Transkei, the black nationalist also said he would appeal for tougher anti-apartheid curbs when he meets with Britain's Thatcher, probably July 6.

---

Israel acknowledged that it had secretly co-financed a Jewish settlement in the Christian quarter of Jerusalem's walled Old City, leasing a building owned by the Greek Orthodox Church through a Panamanian company. Israel said it paid $1.8 million toward the settlement of 150 Jews, which sparked strikes and violent protests.

---

Solidarity urged Poland to accelerate the pace of political and economic restructuring. In a resolution, approved at a national congress in Gdansk, the trade union also demanded new elections by early 1991 to remove Communist holdovers from office.

---

China's Premier Li is scheduled to travel today to the Soviet Union for talks aimed at solidifying recently restored ties between the two Communist nations. Moscow and Beijing are expected to reach agreement on troop withdrawals along the Sino-Soviet border during the trip by Li, the most senior Chinese official to visit Moscow since 1964.

---

India's Prime Minister Singh warned Pakistan against fomenting trouble on the border. He made his statements following a clash Saturday between Indian and Pakistani troops in the disputed region of Kashmir, where Moslem separatists have been waging a pro-independence campaign.

---

Died: Erte, 97, Russian-born artist known for his art deco painting, sculpture and theatrical design, Saturday, in Paris.
