Bairnco Stockholders File Lawsuits Related To Asbestos Liability

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/23/90

NEW YORK -- Bairnco Corp. shareholders have filed a number of lawsuits, charging the company with violating securities laws in connection with its announcement that its insurance coverage against asbestos-related litigation may be in doubt. In January, New York-based Bairnco said it planned to spin off its Keene Corp., New York, subsidiary to stockholders. Earlier this month, Bairnco affirmed that plan, but it disclosed in its annual report that almost all of the $143 million of insurance coverage available to Keene at March 31 "is subject to litigation or dispute."

The lawsuits, seeking status as class actions, have been filed in the past couple of weeks in U.S. District Court in Manhattan. They name as defendants Bairnco and various officers and directors and request damages. Bairnco declined to comment on the litigation.

The company's asbestos litigation stems from Baldwin-Ehret-Hill, a company that Keene, a manufacturer of heat-shielding devices, owned from 1968 to 1974. From 1968 to 1972, some of BEH's thermal insulation and acoustic products contained asbestos.

In one suit, shareholder William Steiner charges that in its 1987 and 1988 financial statements, the company painted a rosy picture of its outlook. The 1988 annual report "reinforced the portrait of Bairnco as a successful, growing company" with its "problems behind it," the suit says.

The suit then charges that Bairnco failed to properly disclose the financial impact of an October 1988 accident at a production plant; the 1988 slowing of sales within its shielding segment; and "the most devastating" problem: the extent of its potential asbestos liability.

Bairnco's actions "artificially" inflated and maintained the company's stock price "to create the impression that the company was healthy and continuing its growth. . . ." the suit charges.

Bairnco, which makes laminates and adhesives, shielding systems, and cutting products, has said it expects to win the fight for its asbestos-insurance coverage, but its stock price has taken a hit. In New York Stock Exchange composite trading Friday, Bairnco shares closed unchanged at $6.75.
