International Brief -- Mitsui & Co.: Firm Revises Its Forecast For Revenue for Fiscal '90

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/23/90

Mitsui & Co. Ltd. revised its revenue forecast for the fiscal year ended March 31 to 20.1 trillion yen ($128.2 billion), from 18.2 trillion yen forecast earlier. In fiscal 1989, the company had revenue of 14.828 trillion yen.

A company spokesman cited dollar-denominated export and import contracts for boosting revenue, as the Japanese yen slid considerably against the U.S. currency.

Favorable contracts on chemical plants, as well as on electric machineries, also pushed revenue higher, he said.

The spokesman also ascribed the upward revision partly to gold trading reflecting the popularity of gold investment in Japan.

The company left its forecast for pretax profit and net income unchanged, standing at 62 billion yen and 21 billion yen, respectively, in part because of the slim profit margins from gold transactions.

In fiscal 1989, pretax profit was 54.89 billion yen, while net income came to 16.61 billion yen.
