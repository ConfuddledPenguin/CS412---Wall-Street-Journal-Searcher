Marketing & Media: Publishers Asked to Boost Use of Recycled Newsprint

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/23/90

LOS ANGELES -- Newspaper publishers are being pushed to increase their use of recycled newsprint. Here for the annual meeting of the American Newspaper Publishers Association, publishers are being asked to increase their use of recycled material to as much as 28% of their total newsprint consumption by 1992. Currently, use of recycled newsprint varies by newspaper, but it is estimated that most daily newspapers use less than 15% of their total in recycled newsprint.

The move toward recycled newsprint already is taking hold. Today, the Boston Globe's edition will be published entirely on recycled newsprint. The Globe had planned to publish its Sunday edition -- on the 20th anniversary of Earth Day -- but couldn't get enough recycled paper for the larger Sunday edition. The Globe, published by Affiliated Publications Inc., regularly produces about 8% of its papers with recycled newsprint. On Friday, Gannett Co.'s USA Today published a 24-page `Earth Day 1990' section entirely on recycled newsprint.

Meeting Saturday, the ANPA board of directors set the 28% consumption level and asked publishers to demand as much recycled newsprint from North American newsprint mills as they are capable of producing.

Supplies of recycled newsprint remain a problem. ANPA estimates fewer than 10 of the existing 60 U.S. and Canadian paper mills have the ability to produce recycled newsprint.
