Hitachi Venture Says It Would Weigh Sale Of Dataproducts Unit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/23/90

A joint venture owned by Hitachi Koki Co. and Nissei Sangyo Co. of Japan said it plans to explore the sale of Dataproducts New England Inc. if the venture's $10-a-share tender offer for Dataproducts Corp. succeeds. The two companies, which are affiliates of Hitachi Ltd., the Tokyo electronic-equipment maker, launched a $160 million offer for the parent company on Friday.

In a filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission, the venture, HND Corp., said it may also seek the sale of Dataproducts' properties in Milford, N.H., and Austin, Texas. Those properties are currently listed for sale.

In Woodland Hills, Calif., a Dataproducts spokesman said the filing was expected. "Hitachi is interested in our printer and supply business; the New England operations don't fit with that strategy." Dataproducts New England makes certain security-sensitive printers and data communications equipment for the U.S. military; the products might cause complications for a foreign buyer, the spokesman said.

Should it acquire control of Dataproducts, HND also said it plans to continue the company's restructuring and to review carefully the company's assets, corporate structure and capitalization to determine what additional changes, if any, would be desirable.
