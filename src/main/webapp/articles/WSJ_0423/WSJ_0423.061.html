South Africa's Trade Surplus

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/23/90

JOHANNESBURG, South Africa -- South Africa's trade surplus narrowed to 1.17 billion rand ($442.8 million) in March from surpluses of 1.79 billion rand a year earlier and 1.77 billion rand in February, the Customs and Excise office said. The figures aren't seasonally adjusted, but export and import data are adjusted to bring them in line with requirements for compiling the nation's balance of payments. Exports in March fell to 4.49 billion rand from 5.22 billion rand a year earlier, and imports declined to 3.33 billion rand from 3.44 billion rand.

In the first quarter, exports grew to 14.42 billion rand from 12.92 billion rand a year earlier, and imports increased to 10.38 billion rand from 9.99 billion rand.

The trade figures include South Africa's exports of gold bullion, its principal source of foreign exchange earnings. Imports include the country's purchases of oil and other petroleum products, despite the longstanding embargo of the Organization of Petroleum Exporting Countries.
