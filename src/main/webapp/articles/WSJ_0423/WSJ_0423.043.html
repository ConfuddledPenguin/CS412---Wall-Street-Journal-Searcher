Harold Simmons, NL Agree to Sell Stakes In Georgia Gulf Corp.

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/23/90

DALLAS -- Investor Harold Simmons and NL Industries Inc. agreed to sell the stub stock and debt they will receive from Georgia Gulf Corp.'s recapitalization for the equivalent of $14 a share, Michael A. Snetzer, a top Simmons aide, said. Through NL, Mr. Simmons owns 2,300,200 shares of Georgia Gulf, or a 9.6% stake, purchased at an average price of about $37 a share. Under Georgia Gulf's planned recapitalization, approved last week by its shareholders, holders will receive $30 cash, a share of stub stock and some debt for each share outstanding.

Mr. Simmons's transaction would give him $44 cash for each of his shares, or a profit of $16.1 million on his Georgia Gulf investment. The sale of securities is on a when-issued basis, meaning it is contingent on Georgia Gulf completing its recapitalization.

Georgia Gulf has said that it hopes to complete the recapitalization soon. Georgia Gulf, an Atlanta chemicals concern, proposed the transaction after Mr. Simmons offered to acquire the company or help it recapitalize last year.

Mr. Simmons made several offers for Georgia Gulf, including a $45 a share, or $1 billion, tender offer, before agreeing to support Georgia Gulf's proposal. Mr. Simmons said he withdrew his tender offer because he estimated that the recapitalization had a value of at least $45 a share.

Mr. Snetzer, who is president of Valhi Inc., Mr. Simmons' holding company, said he doesn't know the buyer or buyers of the stub stock and debt, but said the transaction will be handled by Jefferies & Co. NL is a Houston-based specialty chemicals company.
