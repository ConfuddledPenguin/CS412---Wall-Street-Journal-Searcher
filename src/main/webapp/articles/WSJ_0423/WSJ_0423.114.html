Marketing & Media: Tribune Co. Net Fell 9% to $33.5 Million In the First Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/23/90

CHICAGO -- Tribune Co., citing widening losses at its New York Daily News and "continued weakness" at its newsprint operations, said first-quarter earnings fell 9%, despite a 6% rise in revenue. The media concern posted net of $33.5 million, or 43 cents a share, down from $36.8 million, or 49 cents a share, a year earlier. Per-share figures reflect a decrease in average common shares outstanding to 68.1 million from 74.4 million.

Revenue rose to $583.6 million from $552.9 million.

Tribune said operating profit at its newspaper publishing group fell 10% to $55.7 million. Increased losses at the Daily News reflect "weak market conditions" as well as costs associated with continuing contract talks with unionized workers. The rest of the newspaper group showed increased operating profit, on the strength of stronger performance at the Chicago Tribune and lower prices for newsprint.

But those lower prices gave the company's newsprint-manufacturing group a 63% decline in operating profit, despite a small increase in revenue.

Tribune's broadcasting and entertainment group, helped by improved earnings at the company's television stations, reported operating profit of $6.2 million, compared with a year-earlier operating loss of $1.4 million.
