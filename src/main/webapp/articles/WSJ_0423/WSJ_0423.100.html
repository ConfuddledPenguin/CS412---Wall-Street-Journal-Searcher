Omnicare Inc. Reports $2.2 Million Net Loss, Plans Sale of Assets

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/23/90

CINCINNATI -- Omnicare Inc. said it has decided to sell its disappointing medical and industrial oxygen business, resulting in a first-quarter charge of $2.5 million, or 28 cents a share. The health care company said it's negotiating to sell a major portion of the assets of its Bunn/Xorbox Group, which had an operating loss in the first quarter of $341,000, or four cents a share. Those operations were classified as discontinued in the latest quarter.

The company reported a net loss of $2.2 million, or 25 cents a share, including the charge and loss from discontinued operations. Profit from continuing operations totaled $624,000 in the latest quarter.

In the year-ago first quarter, the company earned $973,000, or 10 cents a share. Last year's results include profit from discontinued operations of $410,000, from the company's hospital pharmacy management business, which was sold in August 1989.
