International: Korean Investors Protest Roh's Failure to Aid Market

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 04/23/90

SEOUL -- Disgruntled stock investors, shouting "Down with {President} Roh Tae-woo," demonstrated Friday to protest the lack of government measures to boost the sagging South Korean market, witnesses said. Police said they arrested eight protesters.

At least two people were injured when about 200 investors began hurling stacks of papers, booklets and chairs at several stock trading offices in Seoul, the witnesses said.

The composite index, the main measure of the Korea Stock Exchange, has been falling sharply in the past several days, dropping to 767.45 Friday, the lowest level since November 1988.

Analysts blamed the market's decline on the sluggish economy. Exports, on which the Korean economy depends heavily, fell nearly 3% in the quarter ended March 31 from a year earlier.

Despite the gloomy economic outlook, the government ruled out any short-term measures to boost the market.
