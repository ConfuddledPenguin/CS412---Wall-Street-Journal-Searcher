Dow Jones to Launch Business Data Service

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/23/90

NEW YORK -- Dow Jones & Co. said it will launch a new information service, DowVision, that will deliver business news and information through corporate computer systems directly to desktops. The information package will include news from the Dow Jones News Service (the "Broadtape"), Professional Investor Report, Capital Markets Report, Dow Jones International News Services and Federal Filings. Additional information will come from PR Newswire and Business Wire, which carry corporate news releases.

DowVision is a product of Information Services, an operating group of Dow Jones, which is publisher of The Wall Street Journal.

Software companies, so-called alliance developers, have developed software at the user end that will receive the material and manage it to customers' specifications. The software will select the data the customers have specified they want.

The companies that will make up the alliance developers include: American RealTime Services Inc., New York; Desktop Data Inc., Waltham, Mass.; GESCAN International Inc., Research Triangle Park, N.C.; and Verity Inc., Mountain View, Calif.

In the future, DowVision will provide customers with access to News/Retrieval's archive of business and financial data bases -- but without the need to dial into the system using a modem. DowVision's flat-fee pricing will eliminate per-minute charges on all Dow Jones-proprietary information, reducing a significant barrier to usage.

Dow Jones also publishes Barron's magazine and other magazines, community newspapers, and provides financial market information through its Telerate Inc. subsidiary.
