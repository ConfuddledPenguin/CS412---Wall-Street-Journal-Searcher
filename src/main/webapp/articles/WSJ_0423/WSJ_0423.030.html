International: German Auto Output

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/23/90

FRANKFURT -- West German car production surged 11% in March from a year earlier, the German Automobile Industry Association said. Output rose to 452,500 cars in March, which had 22 work days, from 409,455 in March 1989, which had only 21 days, the association said. On a calendar-adjusted basis, production was still up 6% from a year earlier.

In the first quarter, car production climbed 6% to 1,272,000 from 1,195,000 a year earlier.

Output of all vehicles, including trucks and buses, climbed 11% to 480,800 in March from 434,597 a year earlier, and was up 7% to 1,355,000 in the first quarter from 1,269,000 a year earlier.
