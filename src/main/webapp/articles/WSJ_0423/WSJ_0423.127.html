Business Brief -- Recognition Equipment Inc.: Two Former Insurers Sued For Liability Cases' Costs

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/23/90

Recognition Equipment Inc., Dallas, said it sued two former insurance carriers in state district court, seeking to recover more than $9 million under its directors' and officers' liability insurance policies. The maker of data management equipment filed the action in the Dallas court against Harbor Insurance Co., which was its primary carrier, and St. Paul Surplus Lines Insurance Co. in the bid to collect on costs and expenses related to defending Recognition and two former executives in criminal and civil lawsuits. Harbor, Los Angeles, is a unit of Continental Corp., a New York-based insurance holding company. St. Paul Surplus Lines is a unit of St. Paul Cos., a St. Paul, Minn., insurer. Both insurance policies expired last year, Recognition said.

The company and its two former executives were acquitted last November of charges that they tried to manipulate the award of U.S. Postal Service contracts for mail-sorting equipment. Recognition said that civil suits against the company relating to the criminal charges are pending, and that its total claims against the insurance companies for those lawsuits and others could exceed $10 million.
