Manville Corp.'s Net Fell in 1st Quarter; Major Charge Is Cited

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/23/90

DENVER -- Manville Corp. reported lower first-quarter earnings, largely because of a charge to reflect tax changes in Brazil, on a 2% increase in sales. The building products concern reported net income of $24.9 million, or 18 cents a share, compared with $34.3 million, or 26 cents a share, the year earlier. The company said flat year-to-year pretax earnings were offset by an $8.5 million, or seven-cents-a-share charge, to adjust for tax legislation recently enacted in Brazil, where the company has forest-products operations. Sales increased to $517.9 million, from $508.3 million.

Expenses from plant start-ups and expansions, in addition to a fall-off in automotive sales, cut income in the company's fiber glass business. But strength in the company's international forest products operation offset weaknesses in domestic construction sales, the company said.

In an unrelated matter, Manville said it is considering the sale of its 50% interest in the Stillwater platinum and palladium mining operations in southwest Montana. Manville and Chevron Resources Co. are partners in the mine, which is the only platinum mine in the U.S. and one of the few in the world.

Salomon Brothers has been retained to solicit bids for the property about which, Manville said, it has recently received several unsolicited inquiries. The company has stated it is seeking to focus its efforts on its core businesses of filtration, forest and glass-fiber products.
