Business Brief -- Corona Corp.: Stock Deal Set for Stake In Stikine-Prime Resources

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/23/90

Corona Corp., Toronto, said it will acquire a 44.6% stake in a new company to be formed through the proposed merger of two mining companies, Stikine Resources Ltd. and Prime Resources Group Inc., both based in Vancouver, British Columbia. Corona said it will acquire the stake in exchange for 16.6 million new Class A shares, giving the transaction an indicated value of about 128.7 million Canadian dollars (US$110.7 million).

Corona said the proposed transaction "represents a potential 20% increase in gold and equivalent reserves, and approximately a 10% dilution in outstanding common equity." Corona has about 141.9 million Class A shares outstanding. On the Toronto Stock Exchange Friday, the Class A shares closed at C$7.75, down 50 Canadian cents, in heavy trading.

Stikine and Prime each hold 50% stakes in the Eskay Creek property in Northern British Columbia. The property has created much speculation in the mining industry as a possible major new precious-metal and base-metal find.
