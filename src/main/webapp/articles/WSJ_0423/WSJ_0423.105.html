Armco Sets Venture With Spain's Acerinox To Make Steel Goods

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/23/90

PARSIPPANY, N.J. -- Armco Inc. said it reached a final agreement with Spanish steelmaker, Acerinox S.A. of Madrid, to form two joint ventures to produce and market stainless steel products. The U.S. joint venture, called North American Stainless, will consist of a $222 million stainless steel finishing plant to produce 48-inch and 60-inch-wide chrome nickel stainless sheet and strip for the chemical, pulp and paper, construction and food and beverage industries. The plant will have initial capacity of more than 170,000 tons.

The European venture, named Acerinox-Armco Europa S.A., will process and market chrome stainless grades for use in European automotive exhaust systems. Steel for this venture will be produced at Acerinox's plant in Algeciras, Spain, and processed in its service centers at various European locations. The products will be marketed by the joint venture company.

Existing operations of Armco and Acerinox will not be part of the joint ventures.

Armco and Acerinox had announced an agreement in principle to form the joint ventures in November 1989.
