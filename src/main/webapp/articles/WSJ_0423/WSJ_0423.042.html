Who's News: Tektronix's President, Chief Officer Resigns

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/23/90

BEAVERTON, Ore. -- Ailing Tektronix Inc. said its president and chief executive officer, David P. Friedley, resigned. Robert Lundeen, chairman, will serve as interim chief executive officer, and William Walker, a director, will serve as interim president, until a permanent successor to Mr. Friedley can be named, the company said.

Tektronix didn't elaborate on Mr. Friedley's departure. The maker of electronic measurement instruments has seen earnings flag during the past four years owing to slow growth and poor acceptance of new products.

For its third fiscal quarter ended March 3, Tektronix reported a net loss of $25.5 million, or 88 cents a share, on revenue of $421.9 million. It had a profit of $6.4 million, or 22 cents a share, on revenue of $434.1 million the year earlier.

The company has eliminated as many as 1,400 positions since the beginning of the current fiscal year. Mr. Friedley, who became president and chief executive in 1987, recently said that he had hoped product introductions would provide the company with renewed earnings growth but conceded that results have continued to be disappointing. He said the company's woes stemmed in part from a downturn in the defense industry, a major Tektronix customer.

On Friday, Tektronix shares closed at $13.375, up 50 cents a share, in New York Stock Exchange composite trading.
