Borden Says Net Rose 6.9% in First Quarter On 4.1% Sales Gain

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/23/90

NEW YORK -- Borden Inc.'s first-quarter profit rose 6.9% on 4.1% sales growth, with strong results in grocery and specialty products more than offsetting lower profit in snacks and international products. Also, Borden directors declared a 10.4% dividend increase to 26.5 cents a share, payable June 1 to stock of record May 11.

For the quarter, Borden reported net income of $63.1 million, or 43 cents a share, up from the year-earlier $59 million, or 40 cents a share. Sales climbed to $1.89 billion from $1.82 billion.

In the grocery and specialty products division, operating profit improved 38%, led by the North American pasta operations and such niche grocery products as Borden, Lite-Line and Fisher cheeses, Cremora non-dairy creamer and food-service products.

Operating profit for snacks and international consumer products fell 18% on an 11% sales increase to $454 million. The company said profit was better in the European sweet, baked snacks group, and in the Wise Foods and Humpty Dumpty North American salty snack units.

The dairy division posted a 12% drop in profit on a 7.2% decline in sales to $490 million. The sales decline reflected the absence of results from the company's former butter business, which was shed in December. Borden said it expects improved profit in the dairy division, citing declining costs for milk.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading Friday, Borden closed at $31.875 a share, unchanged.
