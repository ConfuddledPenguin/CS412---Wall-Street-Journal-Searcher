Marketing & Media: Simon & Schuster to Publish `Locked' Garbo Biography

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/23/90

NEW YORK -- A biography of Greta Garbo that's been locked in a vault for more than a decade will be published in June by Simon & Schuster, a unit of Paramount Communications Inc. Written by Antoni Gronowicz, a Polish-born poet and novelist, "Garbo" is said to contain many details about the actress's childhood and personal life, including accounts of love affairs with both men and women. A spokesman for Simon & Schuster said that the book is based on conversations over many years between Ms. Garbo and Mr. Gronowicz, a decades-long friend of the actress.

The author sold the book in 1976, said his agent Morton Janklow, getting an advance of "several hundred thousand dollars." The price might have been higher, said Mr. Janklow, but the author had demanded, out of respect for his privacy-obsessed friend, that the book remain unpublished until her death. Mr. Gronowicz died in 1985, but his wife and son, who are living in New England, will receive the royalties from what seems likely to be a best seller.
