International Brief -- Jacobs Suchard AG: Swiss Firm Weighs Cutting Global Work Force by 19%

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/23/90

Jacobs Suchard AG, a Swiss chocolate, coffee and candy company that owns the E.J. Brach candy concern in the U.S., is considering eliminating 3,000, or 19%, of its 16,000 full-time jobs world-wide, a spokesman confirmed, but declined to be specific. Chairman Klaus J. Jacobs last week was said to have told analysts of plans for such cuts in the next 12 months. The spokesman said the work-force reductions are a "logical" outcome of the "modernization and concentration" of factories, a program Suchard calls "Vision 2,000." Suchard plans to reduce the number of its factories to six major ones and three smaller ones in Europe from more than 20; some of those were obtained through acquisitions. Job cuts could occur in Europe and the U.S., but U.S. factories won't be closed, the spokesman said.

The E.J. Brach unit has presented product mix and marketing problems since it was acquired by Suchard for $730 million in 1987. In 1986, Brach had pretax profit of about $75 million, on sales of about $462 million; in 1989, it had a $50 million operating loss. Suchard has annual profit of about 275 million francs ($48.7 million).
