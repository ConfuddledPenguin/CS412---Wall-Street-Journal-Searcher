Caterpillar Net Fell 30% in First Period

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/23/90

PEORIA, Ill. -- Caterpillar Inc. said first-quarter net income fell 30% to $99 million, or 97 cents a share, from $141 million, or $1.39 a share, a year earlier. Sales rose 9% to $2.87 billion from $2.63 billion.

The maker of heavy equipment said the decline in profit reflected higher costs because of wage and benefit increases for payrolls, including higher costs for health care, product development, factory modernization and other company programs. The company also cited higher material costs and depreciation expenses. The effects of the weaker U.S dollar also had an unfavorable effect on costs in Europe, the company said.

Caterpillar said its first-quarter sales were "somewhat stronger than anticipated." The company attributed the increase in sales to higher prices but only slightly higher volume.

Sales outside of the U.S. rose 12%, reflecting higher machines sales in developing countries, particularly the Pacific Rim region. The company also said demand rose in a number of Latin American countries. In the U.S., Caterpillar said machine demand was strong in petroleum and coal-mining applications, but declined in the construction and forest products, metals and nonmetals mining industries.

The company said U.S. sales of engines were "well-below" year-earlier because of continued weakness in demand from truck makers.
