Apache Corp. Says First-Quarter Profit Jumped to $11 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/23/90

DENVER -- Apache Corp. expects to report that its net income doubled for the first quarter to about $11 million, or 25 cents a share, from last year's $5.4 million, or 16 cents a share, Raymond Plank, chairman and chief executive officer, said. Mr. Plank said in an interview that those expectations primarily were the result of unusually strong natural gas prices in January; he said earnings may exceed estimates by 10% to 20%. The company had about 44.6 million shares outstanding at March 31, up from about 33 million a year ago.

Revenue for the quarter is expected to be around $68 million, up from $60.7 million last year; cash flow for the period is expected to exceed $40 million.

However, Mr. Plank warned that "the first quarter is not going to be indicative of the results for the year ahead," primarily because natural gas prices have fallen sharply since January. But, he said, "with higher volumes and higher earnings from marketing-related operations, we should see a continuing reasonably favorable comparison to last year."

Earlier this month, Apache sold half its interest in its Nagasco Inc. gas-gathering system and its marketing operations to Natural Gas Clearinghouse, a venture in which Apache owns 31.5%. Mr. Plank said that about 20% of the company's profit, or roughly $2.2 million, came from its natural-gas marketing business during the first quarter. That business contributed profit of $4.3 million in all of 1989.

The quarterly results also were aided by increased natural-gas sales from six new platforms in the Gulf of Mexico. Oil production for the quarter, however, was down 4% because of a weather-related shutdown at one platform.

Mr. Plank said that Apache currently plans to drill about 200 wells this year, although it may re-evaluate its plans if gas prices remain depressed. The present plan calls for the company to spend about $80 million to $90 million on drilling and production this year, up from $73 million last year.

The company plans a total exploration and production budget of $140 million to $150 million, up from $126.6 million last year. Mr. Plank said Apache is actively investing in prospects overseas. Currently, all of Apache's production is domestic.
