International Recovery to Report Earnings Doubled

  • Author: Rick Christie
  • Date: 04/23/90

MIAMI -- International Recovery Corp. expects to report that net income more than doubled in the fourth quarter ended March 31. Ralph R. Weiser, chairman, said the company estimates net climbed to $1.4 million, or 30 cents a share, from $615,000, or 17 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue more than doubled to about $50 million from $19.9 millin.

For the fiscal year, the environmental and aviation-services company expects to post net of about $4 million, or 87 cents a share, up 82% from $2.2 million, or 62 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue rose to $140 million from $64.5 million.

Mr. Weiser said the increases in per-share earnings came on top of the company's issue of about 900,000 additional common shares last spring.

Two strategic acquisitions that the company made last year have fueled the growth. To expand its aviation fueling operations at foreign airports, the company in October acquired certain assets of JCo Energy Partners Ltd. of Houston for about $5.5 million. The acquisition places International Recovery's Advance Petroleum subsidiary, which services commercial aircraft, at 300 airports outside the U.S.

The company also acquired Cherokee Group Inc. of Hollywood, Fla., to bolster its underground storage-tank business. "It was a natural fit with our other environmental services businesses," Mr. Weiser said. The company is also involved in soil clean-up, and storage and disposal of used oil and hazardous waste.

Together, the acquisitions added about $55 million to revenue for fiscal 1990.

Mr. Weiser said International Recovery now plans to expand its aviation business internally. The company diversified into aviation fueling services, which now contribute about 50% of profits, to insulate its oil-recovery business from the uncertainties of the oil market.

The company is still in the market for further acquisitions to integrate into its slower-growing environmental business, Mr. Weiser said. "We're fortunate to be involved in two growth businesses," he said, adding that every major purchase of new aircraft by an airline puts more used aircraft into the markets International Recovery serves -- such as the fast-growing air-cargo industry.

He said, however, that the company still has no plans to declare a dividend, as long as it is in a "growth mode."
