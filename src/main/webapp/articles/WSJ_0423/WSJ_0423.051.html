Columbia S&L Sues Home Insurance Unit For Ending Coverage

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/23/90

LOS ANGELES -- Columbia Savings & Loan Association sued Home Insurance Co. of Illinois for rescinding liability coverage for its officers and directors on April 5, four days after the thrift announced it was insolvent. According to a complaint filed in state court here, Columbia alleges that the unit of New York-based Home Insurance Co. acted in "bad faith" by refusing to provide a defense or coverage for five lawsuits against Columbia and its officers relating to Columbia's financial problems.

George T. Scharffenberger, president and chief executive officer of Ambase Corp., parent of Home Insurance, said he "didn't know of the suit" and wasn't aware of any details.

Columbia alleges that Home began "a series of improper efforts" to avoid its obligations "almost immediately" after Columbia announced substantial losses last October. The $10 million policy was to cover the period from June 30, 1989, through April 21, 1990, the complaint says; and Columbia paid premiums of $121,000.

Among the alleged reasons given by Home for rejecting coverage were that a lawsuit had not been disclosed to it in time, and that another complaint was based on "pending litigation" in another court, Columbia said. At one point, Columbia allegedly was told that one of Home's underwriters made an "error of judgment" in binding the policy. That, the complaint charges, was not a proper basis for denying coverage either.

Columbia seeks unspecified damages for "significant financial and emotional damage" resulting from the coverage denials. It also wants a court declaration that cancellation of the policy was improper and an order barring Home from denying coverage.
