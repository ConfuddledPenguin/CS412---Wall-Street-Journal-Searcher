Business Brief -- Ciba-Geigy AG: Rights Issue Is Withdrawn; Sluggish Swiss Market Cited

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/23/90

Ciba-Geigy AG, the big Basel, Switzerland-based, chemicals group, withdrew a proposal for a capital increase with rights that would have brought the company 436 million Swiss francs. It cited as a reason the "marked deterioration" of the Swiss stock market since the proposal was made on Feb. 21. A company executive said the proposal for the rights issue of 363,270 registered shares was meant to compensate shareholders for their approval of a capital increase involving the issue of new shares for employee stock purchase plans. "The company doesn't need the money," he said, adding that there are no plans to come back at a later date with a new rights issue proposal.

The shares for the employee-stock proposal will now be obtained on the open market, the announcement said. The company had planned to seek shareholder approval to issue 360,000 new registered shares for the employee transaction. Management also had intended the rights issue to act as a "sweetener" to encourage shareholders to approve a plan allowing owners of participation certificates to exchange them for registered shares on a voluntary basis. The certificates-for-share exchange is still planned, the company said, including the creation of registered shares required for it.

The capital market proposals were part of a package for the May 9 shareholder meeting to consider, including a plan to allow foreign nationals to buy registered shares.

Ciba-Geigy's shares were heavily traded on the Zurich Stock Exchange after the announcement Friday. The bearer share rose 40 francs to 2,930 francs and its registered share added 140 francs to 2,660 francs.
