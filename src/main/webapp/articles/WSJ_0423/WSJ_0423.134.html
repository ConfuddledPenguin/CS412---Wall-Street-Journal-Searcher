Bush Acts to Ban Shipping To Protect Florida Reefs

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/23/90

ISLAMORADA, Fla. -- President Bush proposed to ban shipping from a wide swath of the southern Florida coast to protect sensitive coral reefs from pollution and ship groundings. Mr. Bush made the announcement as part of his observance of Earth Day during a Florida fishing vacation. His proposal would establish an "area to be avoided" approximately 10 miles off the Florida coast and five miles off the reef. The measure would ban vessels larger than 50 meters in length, or those carrying oil or hazardous materials, from entering the area. The area extends from Key Biscayne to the Dry Tortugas.

The administration's proposal must be forwarded to the International Maritime Organization for approval. While the measure doesn't carry the rule of law, adoption by the body indicates that 128 member nations intend for ships registered to those countries to abide by the guidelines. International accord is necessary, according to officials, because the reef line lies five to seven miles off the shoreline, beyond U.S. domestic shipping jurisdiction.

The action follows three ship groundings off the reef line last fall. No hazardous materials were spilled, but the ships caused extensive damage to the reefs, which Mr. Bush called "one of the most diverse ecosystems in the world and a unique national treasure."
