Transatlantic Holdings To Make Initial Offer Of Shares to Public

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/23/90

NEW YORK -- Transatlantic Holdings Inc., a property and casualty reinsurance company whose major shareholder is American International Group Inc., said it plans an initial public offering. Transatlantic said it plans to sell up to 6.9 million common shares, including overallotments to underwriters, to raise a maximum $186.3 million at the proposed offering price of $27 a share.

AIG, which owns approximately 41% of the company, isn't selling any shares. Of the offering, 4.8 million shares will be offered in the U.S., managed by Goldman, Sachs & Co., and 1.2 million shares outside the U.S., managed by Goldman Sachs International Ltd.

According to the filing, Transatlantic had net income in 1989 of $55.7 million, or $2.44 a share; it had total assets of $1.73 billion at year end.

The shares are being sold by a group of insurance companies, most of whom are reducing their holdings by half; Transatlantic Holdings will receive none of the funds from the offering.
