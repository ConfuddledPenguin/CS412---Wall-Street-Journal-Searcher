Chrysler Considers Spending $148 Million To Build New Engine

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/23/90

HIGHLAND PARK, Mich. -- Chrysler Corp. said it is considering a $148 million investment to build a new, more powerful engine. The auto maker confirmed reports that it is weighing an expansion of its Trenton, Mich., engine plant to build 150,000 units a year of a new 24-valve, 3.5-liter engine. Chrysler officials wouldn't say what vehicle would get the new engine.

Such a project would be consistent with Chrysler's recent efforts to improve its engines. During the 1980s, Chrysler was hurt when consumers demanded cars with bigger, more powerful engines, and Chrysler didn't build either six-cylinder or multivalve car engines.

Last year, Chrysler added a six-cylinder engine to its line, but it still doesn't produce any multivalve engines; it is to begin building its first -- a four-cylinder, 16-valve engine -- later this year, but the site hasn't been designated.

The new six-cylinder engine Chrysler is considering building in Trenton would have dual overhead cams and four valves per cylinder, two more than conventional engines have. Multivalve engines deliver increased power more efficiently than conventional engines do.

Chrysler stopped short of saying it has made a final determination to build the engine in Trenton, and it declined to say the decision hinges on getting tax breaks it is seeking from Trenton city officials. But a Chrysler spokesman did say the Trenton plant is the preferred site. The company wouldn't say whether it is considering other locations.

Chrysler said the expansion would add about 550 jobs in Trenton. About 2,700 people work at the plant now. Last year, Chrysler employees in Trenton built 338,340 four-cylinder and six-cylinder engines.
