Blackstone and Hambro To Cooperate on Mergers

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/23/90

NEW YORK -- Two closely held investment banking boutiques, Blackstone Group of New York and J.O. Hambro Magan & Co. of London, agreed to work together to arrange mergers and acquisitions involving companies in the U.S. and United Kingdom. The pact provides that each firm will assist the other in developing and completing mergers between U.S. and U.K. firms, but it doesn't involve any cross-ownership between the two concerns.

Blackstone already has affiliate relationships with Nikko Securities Co., Tokyo, which owns 20% of Blackstone, and Banque Indosuez, Paris.

Rupert Hambro, chairman of Hambro Magan, noted that mergers between U.S. and U.K. companies last year had a total value of $29.8 billion, up from only $5.4 billion in 1985.

Hambro advised Jaguar PLC in its acquisition by Ford Motor Co. and Deutsche Bank in its acquisition of Morgan Grenfell.

Peter G. Peterson, chairman of Blackstone, said so-called cross-border merger activity "certainly is a rapidly exploding field." He said Hambro Magan has "very good access at the highest levels" of U.K. companies.

Blackstone has advised Sony Corp. on its acquisitions of CBS Records and Columbia Pictures and Bridgestone Corp.'s acquisition of Firestone.
