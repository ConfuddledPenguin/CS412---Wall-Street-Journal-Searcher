SEC Examines Disclosures On A-12 Aircraft Finances

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 12/27/90

The Securities and Exchange Commission is investigating whether McDonnell Douglas Corp. and General Dynamics Corp. have made proper financial disclosures regarding the troubled A-12 aircraft program, both companies confirmed. Word of the SEC inquiry, which was first reported by Defense Week, a trade publication, comes on the heels of a separate criminal investigation into the A-12 project. That probe is being led by the U.S. attorney in St. Louis, where both companies are based.

Defense Secretary Dick Cheney has given the contractors until Wednesday to devise a plan to save the A-12, which although designed to replace the Navy's aging fleet of A-6 attack planes, has been plagued by cost overruns, technical snags and schedule slips.

"The SEC has informed us that they are conducting an informal inquiry related to our disclosures about and accounting for the A-12 program," a McDonnell Douglas spokesman said.

A General Dynamics spokesman said the company is "aware there's an investigation going on," but wouldn't comment further. In Washington, an SEC spokesman declined comment.

In the second quarter, General Dynamics registered a $450 million charge against earnings on the A-12, while McDonnell Douglas used a different accounting method and didn't take a writeoff. "We believe it's the difference in reporting that led to the inquiry," the McDonnell Douglas spokesman said.
