Who's News: Greater Washington Unveils Resignations Of Directors, Officers

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/27/90

CHEVY CHASE, Md. -- Citing "fundamental differences" between the company and shareholders, Greater Washington Investors Inc. announced the resignation of several top executives. Stephen W. Ritterbush, president and chief executive officer, and Richard P. Whitney, senior vice president, have resigned as officers and directors of the company. A group of directors also resigned, comprising John E. Gray, David T. Krausman, Hilliard W. Paige, Henry M. Ross, Charles Henry Smith Jr. and Edmund W.E. Stein.

GWI, the nation's second-oldest venture capital firm, said that David L. Chandler, chairman of the board, will serve as chief executive officer on an interim basis until a new president is found. Mr. Chandler said the resignations were the result of fundamental differences between the resigning directors and the three remaining directors: himself, Rainer H. Bosselmann and D. Irving Obrow, all of whom are associated with Johnston Industries Inc.

Johnston Industries, an operator of textile mills, is GWI's largest shareholder; Mr. Chandler also is Johnston's chairman and chief executive. In a Dec. 5 filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission, Johnston Industries said it boosted its stake in GWI to 36.9% of the common shares outstanding. At the time, Johnston didn't give a reason for boosting its holdings.

A spokeswoman for GWI declined to comment further on the resignations or the differences between the outgoing management and the shareholders, saying: "We've been instructed not to comment."
