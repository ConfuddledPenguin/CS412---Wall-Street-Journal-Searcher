Marketing & Media: `Godfather III' Achieves Biggest Christmas Gross

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/27/90

NEW YORK -- "The Godfather Part III" brought in total box office receipts of $6,387,271 on Tuesday, the film's opening day, to achieve the biggest Christmas day gross in history. Paramount Pictures, a unit of Paramount Communications Corp., said the movie, directed by Francis Ford Coppola, grossed a per-screen average of $3,509 on 1,820 screens in the U.S. and Canada.

Paramount noted the biggest individual theater receipts were in several cinema houses in the Los Angeles area, one in New York and others in Washington, Chicago and Honolulu. But the biggest single gross was in San Jose, Calif., with more than $41,000 for a day's showings.

The first two movies in the trilogy, "The Godfather" (1972) and "The Godfather Part II" (1974), were among the biggest money-makers in movie history. Each film won the Academy Award for best picture.
