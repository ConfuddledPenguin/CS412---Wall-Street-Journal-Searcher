Business Brief -- Nacco Industries Inc.: Firm Will Take a Charge Of $10.3 Million in Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/27/90

Nacco Industries Inc. said it expects to take a one-time charge of $10.3 million, or $1.16 a share, in the fourth quarter, due primarily to restructuring and plant closings involving recently acquired businesses. Nacco, a fork-lift producer and consumer products and mining concern, said in Cleveland that it expects to report a modest profit for the quarter. In the year-ago fourth quarter, net was $18.9 million, or $2.13 a share.

Nacco said about $7.6 million of the charges reflect restructuring at the company's 97%-owned Hyster-Yale Materials Handling unit. The subsidiary is shutting a plant in Brunswick, Ga., and relocating certain operations to a North Carolina facility.

The remaining charges stem from Nacco's 80%-owned Hamilton Beach/Proctor-Silex unit, including charges for restructuring operations.
