Manhattan Relocation Set By Commodities Exchanges

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/27/90

NEW YORK -- Memberships of four major New York commodities exchanges approved, as expected, a relocation of their trading floors to a new site just north of the World Trade Center in lower Manhattan. Voting by the members -- which followed approval by the four exchanges' boards on Nov. 29 -- was completed late last week. Final tallies were made yesterday, a spokeswoman for the exchanges said.

The four exchanges are the New York Mercantile Exchange, the Commodity Exchange, the Coffee, Sugar & Cocoa Exchange and the New York Cotton Exchange. The New York Futures Exchange, a unit of the New York Stock Exchange, which rents space from the Cotton Exchange, is also expected to move to the new location.

The relocation issue has been closely watched because of a battle that ensued between New York and New Jersey over which state would get the exchanges, which currently operate side by side on one floor of the World Trade Center but need more space. The new trading facility is scheduled to be available for occupancy by the exchanges in 1994.
