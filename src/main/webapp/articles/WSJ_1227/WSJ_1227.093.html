Business Brief -- Grossman's Inc.: Two Investors May Propose Restructuring Alternatives

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/27/90

Jay H. Shidler and Robert W. Holman Jr., two Honolulu real estate investors, said they may propose various restructuring alternatives for Grossman's Inc. In a Securities and Exchange Commission filing, the investors also said they might launch a proxy fight for the board at the 1991 annual meeting of the Braintree, Mass., home-improvement retailer.

Grossman's officials couldn't be reached for comment. Earlier this year, the Shidler-Holman group launched an unsuccessful bid to unseat Grossman's board.

The group also said it has been in discussions with Grossman's senior management about gaining representation on Grossman's board. The filing said the board indicated that it would agree to one representative of the group being appointed if the group would enter into a long-term standstill agreement. The group, feels, however, that the board's position is not in stockholders' best interest.
