U.S. Plans Final Probe Of Low-Priced Sales Of Chemical by China

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/27/90

WASHINGTON -- The U.S. International Trade Commission said it will conduct a final anti-dumping investigation into China's export of sodium thiosulfate. The Commerce Department already has issued a preliminary finding that dumping of the chemical is occurring. The question of how China practices trade has taken on increasing importance lately, as China's trade surplus with the U.S. threatens to leap beyond that of Germany and Taiwan next year, ranking it only behind Japan. U.S. concerns are that China is achieving much of its advantage through unfair trade practices such as dumping, defined as the selling of goods in a foreign country at less than fair value.

A U.S. final investigation will consider whether an industry in the U.S. is materially injured, or is threatened with material injury, or whether the establishment of an industry in the U.S. is materially retarded by foreign-made goods being sold in the U.S. at less than fair value.

Similar investigations are being conducted concerning possible dumping of the chemical by Germany and the United Kingdom.
