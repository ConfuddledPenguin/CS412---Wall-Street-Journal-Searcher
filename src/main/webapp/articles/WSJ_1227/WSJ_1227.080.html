International Brief -- Club Mediterranee S.A.: Group Revenue Rose 7.1% In the Latest Fiscal Year

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/27/90

French tourism group Club Mediterranee S.A. announced a 7.1% increase in group revenue for the year ended Oct. 31, 1990. The company's revenue rose to 8.15 billion francs ($1.55 billion) from 7.61 billion francs a year earlier.

The company didn't release profit figures, but earlier this month, it estimated earnings after payments to minority interests would be up about 8% in the year ended Oct. 31 from the 365 million francs posted in the year-earlier period.

The company noted, however, that it hasn't yet taken account of capital gains recorded in the latest period.

If the 1989-90 figures come in as expected, they would represent an improvement from the 3% profit decline in the first half, although they pale in comparison to a 21% advance the year before.

Several negative trends affect the company, however. Club Med is experiencing weak sales in the Mediterranean region as a result of the Persian Gulf crisis, and the economic slowdown in the U.S. has cut into North American sales for its Club Med Inc. unit.
