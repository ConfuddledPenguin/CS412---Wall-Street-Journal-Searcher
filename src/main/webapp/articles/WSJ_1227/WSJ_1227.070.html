What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/27/90

STOCKS AND BONDS rose on reports that Iraq and the U.S. are preparing for talks. The 30-year Treasury bond gained almost a point, pushing its yield down to 8.28%. The Dow Jones Industrial Average ad vanced 15.84 points to 2637.13.

Further interest-rate cuts are unlikely to result in a broad, extended stock-market rally unless investors are convinced that corporate profits will grow again, analysts say.

---

The retail slump shows no sign of abating after a Christmas season that is believed to be the worst in many years. Merchants expect tough times through the first half of next year.

---

Bank of New England bondholders said they won't approve a proposed debt-for-equity swap unless they get assurances from the government that the institution isn't about to be seized.

---

Pan Am told TWA owner Icahn, in effect, to put up or shut up in his effort to acquire Pan Am after he balked at putting the bid into writing.

---

Spending on research and product development is beginning to taper off after more than doubling in the past decade. Industry's share of such spending is projected to increase 2.2%, to $75.9 billion, next year.

---

Citrus growers in central California are seeking government disaster relief after a week of cold weather that ravaged navel orange crops.

---

Pennzoil raised its stake in Chevron to 9.4% from 9% by acquiring 1.3 million shares for $89 million. Pennzoil also said the IRS issued a favorable ruling regarding any further such purchases made before May 31.

---

Nissan's U.S. sales arm raised the base prices on its 1991 cars and trucks for the second time in four months. The action reflects the weak dollar's pressure on the auto maker's results.

---

Donald Trump will run out of money to keep his operations going unless he gets a cash infusion by mid-spring, his advisers and bankers say.

---

Short interest fell 2.3% among big OTC stocks in the latest monthly period and 5.4% among smaller issues.

---

McGraw-Hill's S&P unit and the Chicago Board Options Exchange settled a dispute over a licensing arrangement for S&P stock indexes.

---

Grain futures rallied as indications of stability in the Soviet Union eased traders' worries that the Bush administration might not grant Moscow credit to purchase U.S. crops.

---

Tiffany's stock price tumbled $4.375 to $37.25 after the jewelry retailer said earnings estimates for the current fiscal quarter are too high.

---

Tokyo stocks ended mixed in thin trading Wednesday as concerns about the Mideast and the Soviet Union discouraged participation by investors.

---
