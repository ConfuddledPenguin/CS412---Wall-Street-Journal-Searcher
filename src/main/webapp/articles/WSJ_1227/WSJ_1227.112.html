Pacific Telesis Sets 4th-Period Reserve To Shed Properties

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/27/90

SAN FRANCISCO -- Pacific Telesis Group will reserve $60 million against fourth-quarter earnings to offset losses it expects as it sheds its real estate holdings. The reserve equals 15 cents a share. For 1989's fourth quarter, the telecommunications company reported net income of $322 million, or 79 cents a share, on $2.48 billion of revenue.

In March, Pacific Telesis's PacTel Corp. subsidiary said it no longer would invest capital in PacTel Properties, its real estate unit. And recently, Pacific Telesis announced a reorganization under which it said it would slash staff overhead in its domestic cellular operations and position itself to respond more readily to wireless communication and home entertainment opportunities world-wide.

Pacific Telesis said it will shed PacTel Properties' portfolio, consisting of properties in California and Florida, over the next few years.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Pacific Telesis shares rose 12.5 cents to $45.75.
