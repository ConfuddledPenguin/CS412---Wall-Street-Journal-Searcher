Money-Fund Yields Are Little Changed Despite Falling Rates

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/27/90

HOLLISTON, Mass. -- Money-fund yields were little changed in the latest week, despite the recent decline in short-term interest rates. "Moves in recent weeks to lower interest rates have been counteracted by year-end pressures that drive rates up," said Martha Wittbrodt, editor of IBC/Donoghue's Money Fund Report. "But I expect yields to drop after the first of the year."

The average seven-day compound yield, which assumes reinvestment of dividends, remained at 7.42% in the week ended Tuesday, according to Donoghue's. The average 30-day compound yield eased to 7.47% from 7.49%.

Assets of the 453 taxable funds tracked by the newsletter, based here, decreased $1 billion, to $419.2 billion.

The average maturity of the investments in fund portfolios decreased one day, to 51 days. The funds invest in such things as commercial paper -- or short-term corporate IOUs -- short-term bank certificates of deposit and Treasury bills.

The average seven-day simple yield of the taxable funds eased to 7.16% from 7.17%. The average 30-day simple yield eased to 7.21% from 7.23%.

Assets of the 245 tax-free funds fell $50 million, to $85.5 billion, Donoghue's reported. The average seven-day compound yield increased to 5.87% from 5.55%. The latest yield is equal to 8.15% for an investor in the 28% tax bracket and 8.76% for someone paying 33% in taxes.

The average maturity of the tax-exempt funds increased one day, to 55 days.
