International Brief -- Itoman & Co.: Stake in Housing Concern To Be Sold for $66 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/27/90

Itoman & Co., a Japanese trading company troubled with huge investments in real estate, said it agreed to sell its 88.22% stake in Itoman Total Housing Corp. to two real estate companies affiliated with Sumitomo Bank Ltd. A spokesman for Itoman said Sogo Real Estate Co. and Ohtemachi Tatemono K.K. will pay a total of nine billion yen ($66 million) for the stake.

The move is part of Itoman's previously announced program to cut its property-investment debts, estimated in the fall at 876 billion yen.

In 1988, Itoman bought the stake in Itoman Total Housing, then called Sugiyama & Co., at the request of Sumitomo Bank. Sumitomo and Itoman have had close ties since 1975 when Sumitomo sent its former managing director to run the troubled trading company.

The latest agreement also calls for Itoman and seven other affiliated firms to sell some of their properties to Itoman Total Housing. The accord will help Itoman reduce its debts on a consolidated basis by 192.2 billion yen, according to the Itoman spokesman.

But the prospects for Itoman's turnaround are believed to be clouded, due to the deteriorating Japanese real estate market. In November, Sumitomo Bank said it was sending a rescue team to try to help Itoman through its problems.

Corrections & Amplifications

ITOMAN & Co.'s agreement to sell its 88.22% stake in Itoman Total Housing Corp. will reduce Itoman's debt on a consolidated basis by 196.2 billion yen ($1.4 billion). The size of the debt reduction was misstated in yesterday's edition.

(WSJ Dec. 28, 1990)
