Business Brief -- Southeast Banking Corp.: Subsidiary Negotiates Pact With IBM for Computing

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/27/90

Southeast Banking Corp., Miami, said its primary banking subsidiary is negotiating a 10-year agreement with International Business Machines Corp. under which IBM will manage the bank's data processing operations. The pact, part of a banking trend of handing over computing tasks to computer companies, will save the subsidiary, Southeast Bank, $6 million in the first year, bank officials said. The agreement will also give, within one year, the bank's 230 branches a sophisticated new system for completing transactions.

Kenneth T. Sheridan, executive vice president for systems and operations, said it would have taken Southeast three years to assemble the system on its own, and would have cost the bank more to operate it. He declined to put a value on the transaction, which Southeast Bank and IBM expect to complete next month.

Southeast Bank is attempting to cut costs as it struggles with losses from commercial real estate loans. Southeast Banking Corp. recorded a net loss of $164.4 million for the first nine months of 1990.
