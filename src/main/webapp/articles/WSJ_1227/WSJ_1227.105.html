Who's News: County Bank Names Chief Executive Green To Post of Chairman

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/27/90

SANTA BARBARA, Calif. -- County Bank named Roy G. Green, its president and chief executive officer, to the additional post of chairman, as part of a management and financial reorganization of the savings bank. Mr. Green succeeds William J. Levy, the company's largest shareholder with a more than 25% stake. Mr. Levy along with business partner Roy Millender, who holds about a 9% stake, resigned from the board but will retain their ownership interests in the bank, and intend to buy shares in a coming preferred stock issue, the company said.

"It has become increasingly clear to me that, in this demanding environment, it makes sense that County be led by an experienced banker with Roy Green's background and skills," Mr. Levy said. Mr. Green joined County Bank in 1987 after serving as president of the Federal Home Loan Bank of Dallas.

County Bank also said it completed the conversion of $22.15 million in long-term subordinated debt to preferred stock, "a move which significantly improved the bank's current core capital position to 2.3%," the company said. County said it submitted a new capital plan to the Office of Thrift Supervision last month that includes a "significantly higher" level of reserves, more conservative real estate valuations and a higher core capital goal of 5%.
