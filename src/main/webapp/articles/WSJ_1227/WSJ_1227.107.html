Business Brief -- Wet Seal Inc.: Concern Says It May Fail To Hit Analysts' Estimates

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/27/90

Analysts' estimates that Wet Seal Inc. will earn 80 cents a share for the fiscal year ending Feb. 2 "may be overly optimistic by as much as 10 to 12 cents," president Ken Chilvers said. Mr. Chilvers said lackluster holiday sales, particularly in southern California, required the Irvine, Calif., retailer of women's apparel to mark down merchandise earlier than planned.

In the year-earlier period, before the company's initial public offering, Wet Seal earned $5.9 million on sales of $78.7 million. For the 39 weeks ended Nov. 3, the company had net income of $6 million, or 56 cents a share, on sales of $79 million.

Despite the disappointing holiday results, Wet Seal plans to build 20 new stores during 1991, mostly in Florida, Mr. Chilvers added. He noted that the company's five existing stores in Florida, which opened this year, "have continued to exceed sales projections."
