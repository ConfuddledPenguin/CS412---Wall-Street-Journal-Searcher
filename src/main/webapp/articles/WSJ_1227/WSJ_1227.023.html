Tiffany & Co. Says Profit Won't Meet Previous Estimates

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/27/90

NEW YORK -- Tiffany & Co. said earnings estimates for its fiscal fourth quarter are too high based on holiday sales results, which rose but were below expectations. William R. Chaney, chairman of the jewelry retailer, said U.S. sales were sufficiently below targeted increases to justify a downward revision in estimates for the quarter ending Jan. 31, 1991. Analysts' estimates had ranged from per-share net income of $1.10 to $1.28. Mr. Chaney indicated that profit could still reach approximately the level of the year-earlier quarter, when Tiffany earned $15.9 million, or $1.02 a share.

After yesterday's announcement, Tiffany fell $4.375 to close at $37.25 in New York Stock Exchange composite trading.

Tiffany said world-wide net sales rose 8% for the eight-week period from Nov. 1 through Dec. 25, compared with the year-earlier period. Mr. Chaney said sales in the U.S. increased modestly but were below expectations, reflecting consumer concern about the economy. However, international retail sales growth continued to be strong. The company said international retail sales rose 28%; U.S. retail sales rose 2%; and direct marketing sales rose 1%, based on preliminary unaudited figures.

Although some U.S. jewelry companies had been urging customers to buy before the end of the year to avoid the scheduled federal tax increase on luxury goods, a spokesman said, "We didn't sense any impact on our holiday sales."

Mr. Chaney said the company "will enjoy a record level of sales and earnings in 1990. Although we are disappointed with the results of the holiday period in the U.S., we recognize that widespread economic concerns have affected consumer spending at every level. We are encouraged, however, that the number of customer purchases in Tiffany's U.S. stores increased over the 1989 level, although the average customer purchase declined somewhat. It demonstrated continued strong customer loyalty."

He said the company's strong financial position will enable the company to aggressively pursue its expansion plans in 1991. "While the current U.S. consumer spending slowdown is, no doubt, affecting us, we believe our longer-term opportunities are as bright as ever."

The company expects to report results in early March.
