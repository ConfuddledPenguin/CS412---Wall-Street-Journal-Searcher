Hotel Investors Corp. And Trust Receive Acquisition Proposal

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/27/90

BEVERLY HILLS, Calif. -- Investor Leonard M. Ross said he has proposed to buy all the shares of Hotel Investors Trust and Hotel Investors Corp. for $13.7 million. Mr. Ross currently owns 9.8%, or a 1,190,400 share stake in the companies, which are paired and trade together on the New York Stock Exchange.

In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange yesterday, Hotel Investors Trust and Hotel Investors Corp. shares closed at $1.625, up 50 cents.

In a Dec. 21 letter attached to a Securities and Exchange Commission filing, Mr. Ross said that the proposal would consist of a merger between Rossco Holdings Inc. or a newly organized corporation to be formed by him, called Newco, and Hotel Investors.

Under the merger proposal, holders of the paired shares would receive $1.25 a share in cash and the stock of a newly formed subsidiary, the Nevada Corp., into which all of the assets associated with hotel and gambling properties would be placed free and clear of mortgage debt. According to the filing, the stock to be received would be registered.

Earlier this month, Hotel Investors Trust, a real estate investment trust based in Los Angeles, suffered a series of setbacks in its long effort to sell most of its hotels to Park Plaza Inc., a unit of EIE International Corp. of Japan. Park Plaza told Hotel Investors that it was unlikely to complete the sale because of financing problems.
