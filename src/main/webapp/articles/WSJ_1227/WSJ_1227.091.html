Business Brief -- Bidermann Industries Corp.: Unit Sells Colours License, Business to a Joint Venture

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/27/90

Bidermann Industries Corp. said its Cluett, Peabody & Co. unit sold the "Colours by Alexander Julian" license and existing business to Collection Clothing Corp. Terms weren't disclosed, but Bidermann, based in New York, said Collection Clothing would get the U.S. rights to production and distribution of the licensed sportswear, outerwear, dress shirts and neckwear designed by Alexander Julian. Guido Petruzzi, Bidermann's president, said the sale was part of a plan to focus on Cluett's shirt business.

Bidermann said Collection Clothing is a joint venture of Alexander Julian and Aeneas Venture Corp., an affiliate of Harvard Management Group. Bidermann, major maker and distributor of apparel under various brand and designer names, is a unit of Bidermann S.A. of France. It acquired Cluett for $350 million last March from distressed investor William Farley, who needed to sell assets of West Point-Pepperell Inc. after he acquired the textile company in a leveraged buy-out.
