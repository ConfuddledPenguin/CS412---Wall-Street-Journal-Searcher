International: Japanese Retail Sales Up

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/27/90

TOKYO -- Sales at Japan's department stores and other major retail stores grew 6.6% in November from a year earlier to 1.712 trillion yen ($12.58 billion), the Ministry of International Trade and Industry said. The pace of year-on-year growth picked up from October's 4.7%. November volume continued to suffer from sluggish sales of winter clothes and heaters because of unusually warm weather, however, a ministry official said. The 6.6% year-on-year growth is still slower than levels earlier this year, when retail sales posted double-digit growth.

Meanwhile, November sales were pushed up partly by high-paced growth of food sales at both department stores and other retailers. The official said the growth reflects sharp price increases in fresh vegetables and fruit caused by unfavorable weather conditions.
