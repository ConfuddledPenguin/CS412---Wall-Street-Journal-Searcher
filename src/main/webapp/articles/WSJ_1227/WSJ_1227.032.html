Unocal Finds Natural Gas

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/27/90

LOS ANGELES -- Unocal Corp. said it discovered natural gas in a wildcat well on its Hanson Prospect in Kalkaska County, Mich. In a test, the well produced at a rate of about nine million cubic feet of gas a day through an 18/64-inch choke, with a flowing tubing pressure of 4,145 pounds a square inch, at depths of between 10,890 and 10,950 feet. Unocal calculated the absolute open flow rate at 91 million cubic feet a day.

Unocal operates and holds a 39.3% working interest in the well. Shell Western Exploration & Production Inc., a U.S. unit of Royal Dutch/Shell Group, holds the remaining 60.7%.

Separately, Unocal said it successfully tested gas in two geological areas on Block 12/27 in the Gulf of Thailand. The Moragot No. 1 well was drilled to a depth of 11,120 feet, and gas flowed to the surface. The Pailin No. 2 well was drilled to 11,572 feet and tested a combined rate of 14.8 million cubic feet of gas and 189 barrels of condensate a day through a half-inch choke. Unocal's Unocal Thailand unit holds a 35% interest in the block. It expects to drill four more exploration wells in 1991.
