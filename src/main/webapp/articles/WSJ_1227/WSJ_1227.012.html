Treasury Plans Sale Of About $20 Billion In Short-Term Bills

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/27/90

WASHINGTON -- The Treasury plans to raise $1.53 billion in new cash with the sale Monday of about $20 billion in short-term bills to redeem $18.47 billion in maturing bills. The offering will be divided evenly between 13-week and 26-week bills maturing on April 4 and July 5, 1991, respectively.

Noncompetitive tenders for the bills, available in minimum $10,000 denominations, must be received by 11 a.m. EST Monday at the Treasury or at Federal Reserve banks or branches.

Competitive tenders for the bills, available in minimum $10,000 denominations, must be received by noon EST Monday at the Treasury or at Federal Reserve banks or branches.
