Marketing & Media: Multimedia Purchase Of Controlling Stake In WKYC Is Cleared

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/27/90

WASHINGTON -- The Federal Communications Commission approved the transfer of control of WKYC-TV in Cleveland to Multimedia Inc. from National Broadcasting Co. The transaction, proposed last spring, had been challenged by the American Federation of Television and Radio Artists and the National Association of Broadcast Employees and Technicians. The agency dismissed the two unions' claims. The unions argued that the transaction would be anticompetitive and against the public interest, and that NBC would continue to influence and control the station.

Multimedia agreed to pay $65 million and assume a $36.7 million note in exchange for 100% of the voting stock, representing 51% of the equity, of a new corporation, WKYC-TV Holdings Inc. The new company will control the license of WKYC, Channel 3. NBC, a unit of General Electric Co., will receive nonvoting, convertible stock representing 49% of the equity. NBC has the option of converting its shares into voting stock a year after the transaction.
