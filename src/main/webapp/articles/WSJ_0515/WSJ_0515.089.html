NASD Authorities Cancel Membership Of Wellshire Securities

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/15/90

WASHINGTON -- The National Association of Securities Dealers said it canceled the membership of Wellshire Securities Inc., a penny-stock firm based in New York. The cancellation followed a preliminary injunction granted by a federal court in Manhattan in April at the request of the Securities and Exchange Commission. In March, the SEC filed a civil suit alleging that Wellshire and its executives made false and misleading statements about the stocks they were promoting.

The SEC complaint said that from June 1988 to the present, Wellshire, under the direction of Robert Cohen and Carol Martino, has operated a penny-stock "boiler room," selling speculative over-the-counter securities to the public through the use of abusive sales practices and inflated price predictions.

"Wellshire and Cohen and Martino haven't been given the benefit of a full trial," said Mitchell Lampert, an attorney for the executives. "But the damage has already been done." Wellshire and the two executives previously have denied any wrongdoing.
