LTX Corp. Says Loss Shrank in 3rd Period From Prior Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/15/90

WESTWOOD, Mass. -- LTX Corp. said it expects results for its third quarter ended April 30 to show a narrower loss than the $5.1 million, or 52 cents a share, recorded in the second quarter. Graham Miller, chairman and president, said the maker of semiconductor testing equipment also expects to post "better revenues," compared with $33.9 million in the second quarter. If the current trend continues, he said, the company "could be {at} break-even in two quarters" from now.

In the quarter ended April 30, 1989, LTX had a loss of $19 million, or $1.97 a share, on sales of $25.1 million.

Mr. Miller's remarks came at a news conference to announce a formal agreement under which Sumitomo Metal Industries Ltd., Osaka, Japan will pay $12 million for a 49.5% stake in LTX's Japanese subsidiary. Sumitomo will pay another $12 million for a stake of about 13% in LTX, including 1.5 million common shares and debentures convertible into an additional 1.5 million shares, at $4.25 a share.

Mr. Miller said LTX began looking for an established Japanese partner three years ago, after LTX decided to pursue the market for testing of digital semiconductors in Japan, a larger market than that for analog devices, which had been the company's usual strength. The company didn't have a need for capital at that time, Mr. Miller said. But he said that it was "fortuitous" that Sumitomo was able to supply capital at a time when LTX is trying to recoup from a year of red ink.

Shigehiko Hashimoto, general manager of Sumitomo Metal's new business division, said the investment in LTX represents part of Sumitomo's effort to diversify into electronics and other businesses, from its steelmaking base. Sumitomo Metal had sales of about $8 billion in the year ended March 31, 1989.
