MacMillan Bloedel To Close its Largest Pulp Mill for 2 Weeks

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/15/90

VANCOUVER, British Columbia -- MacMillan Bloedel Ltd. said it will close its biggest pulp mill for two weeks beginning June 25, because of soft market conditions. The temporary closure of the Harmac pulp mill, near Nanaimo, British Columbia, will reduce MacMillan Bloedel's inventory of bleached pulp by about 16,000 metric tons, it said. The move follows an extended maintenance closedown at Harmac in March, which curtailed the mill's output by about 15,000 metric tons.

Last year, Harmac produced 370,000 metric tons of market pulp, accounting for about 75% of MacMillan Bloedel's total market pulp output. Market pulp is wood pulp that is sold on the open market, to producers of paper and other products.

A metric ton equals about 2,204.62 pounds.

MacMillan Bloedel, 49.9%-owned by Toronto-based Noranda Forest Inc., said the closure will affect 800 workers at the mill and about 100 workers at the company's Thunderbird sawmill, which is scheduled to close for maintenance during the same period.

Canadian shipments of market pulp fell 13% in March, compared with March, 1989. The big drop in shipments left producers with 532,000 metric tons of inventory, a 30day supply. That compared with year-earlier inventory of just 290,000 metric tons, or a 14-day supply.
