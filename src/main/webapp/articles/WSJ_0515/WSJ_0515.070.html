Letters to the Editor: The True Conservative

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/15/90

I always read George Melloan's articles. Simply put, he brings me much information and causes me to think more deeply than I would otherwise. In regard to his April 16 Global View column, "Political Rhetoric Turned Upside Down," the reason there is so much misunderstanding concerning "liberal" and "conservative," I submit, is that the defining of these terms stops with saying that "preservation of the status quo" is thought to be the end result sought by the conservative. A true conservative is one who must have a valid reason, as distinguished from emotion or seeking to curry favor with the public, to depart from tried-and-true practices or approaches to daily life and its problems. The true conservative has learned from history or at least has sought avidly to do so.

Paul L. Gomory

Bethesda, Md.
