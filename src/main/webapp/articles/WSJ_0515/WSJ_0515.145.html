International Brief -- Nintendo Co.: Tokyo Firm's Profit Surges In Period Ended March 31

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/15/90

Leisure product manufacturer Nintendo Co. reported strong export sales and a surge in consolidated pretax profit to 67.21 billion yen ($439.7 million) in the seven-month fiscal period ended March 31. A direct comparison to past performance wasn't possible because of a change in the Tokyo-based company's fiscal year. Consolidated pretax profit was 69.458 billion yen in fiscal 1988, which ended last Aug. 31.

Net income in the interim period was 32.929 billion yen, or 313.81 yen a share, compared with 34.271 billion yen, or 490.07 yen a share, in fiscal 1988. Stock outstanding in the parent company increased by 34,980 shares in the year.

Consolidated sales were 240.233 billion yen in the seven months, compared with 291.200 billion yen in fiscal 1988. Sales of leisure products, including the company's popular video software, were 238.031 billion yen, compared with 287.984 billion yen.

Nintendo also reported pretax profit at the parent company reached 55,246 billion yen in the seven-month period on the basis of strong sales growth, particularly in exports. Pretax profit in the interim period compared with 61.413 billion yen in fiscal 1988.

Net income was 27.526 billion yen or 262.31 yen a share compared with 29.615 billion yen, or 423.32 yen a share.

Unconsolidated sales totaled 210.042 billion yen, compared with 250.177 billion yen. Leisure-product sales were 208.450 billion yen, compared with 247.857 billion yen. Exports were 152.879 billion yen, or 72.8% of total sales, compared with 250.177 billion yen, or 73.9% of total sales in fiscal 1988.

Nintendo forecast consolidated sales for fiscal 1990 would reach 440 billion yen. Pretax profit is seen at 125 billion yen and net income at 62 billion yen.

---

Corrections & Amplifications

NINTENDO Co., based in Kyoto, Japan, reported pretax profit at the parent company of 55.246 billion yen in the seven-month fiscal period ended March 31. In yesterday's edition, the profit figure and the city where Nintendo is based were misstated.

(WSJ May 16, 1990)
