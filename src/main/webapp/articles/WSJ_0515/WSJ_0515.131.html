AmBase Posts Loss Totaling $42 Million For Three Months

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/15/90

NEW YORK, N.Y. -- AmBase Corp. reported a first-quarter loss of $42 million, or $1.24 a share, after realizing losses on its junk-bond portfolio and beefing up reserves for its thrift unit. The insurance and financial-services company also suspended its quarterly five-cent common stock dividend. The company is already facing hordes of dissatisfied institutional investors who own about 40% of AmBase's shares outstanding. They've seen the value of their holdings plummet from a high of about 31 in 1986 to the current $6.375, up 12.5 cents, where the stock closed yesterday in composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange.

AmBase said it still plans to pay a special dividend to shareholders when it completes the sale of its Home Insurance Co. unit, which has been on the block since February.

During the first quarter, AmBase realized $42 million in investment losses at its insurance units. The company said the losses reflect the deterioration in the high-yield bond market and a decision to reduce the level on noninvestment grade holdings in the portfolio. The company added that the investment portfolios of these units are being repositioned to reduce the credit risks associated with investments in high-yield bonds.

The company earned $26 million or 75 cents a share in the 1989 first quarter. Revenues in the latest quarter fell 8.3% to $777 million.

The company said it expects to sign a new bank credit agreement this month. The new financing will be used to repay all existing bank debt and provide working capital until the sale of Home Insurance Co. The new facility will be fully repaid when the sale is closed, AmBase said.
