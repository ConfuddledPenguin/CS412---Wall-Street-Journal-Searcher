International Brief -- National Westminster Bank PLC: Firm Acquires Another 40% In Netherlands Retail Bank

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/15/90

National Westminster Bank PLC, Britain's second-largest commercial bank, said it acquired a further 40% stake in Van Lanschot's Beleggings Compagnie, raising its holdings in the midsized Dutch retail bank to 80%. The purchase, the price of which wasn't disclosed, has been expected since late January when NatWest said it was in talks with Rabobank Nederland over the possible acquisition of Rabobank's stake in Beleggings.

Beleggings is the holding company of F. van Lanschot Bankiers, a bank founded in 1737 and comprising 20 branches in the Netherlands and Luxembourg.

The move "represents a significant building block in developing the key customer segments that are vital to our success in Europe," said Ian Farnsworth, NatWest general manager for European business.

A NatWest official said the bank has no plans to acquire the remaining 20%, most of which is held by the Lanschot family. Britain's Commercial Union Assurance Co. PLC owns about 5% through a Dutch subsidiary, NatWest said.
