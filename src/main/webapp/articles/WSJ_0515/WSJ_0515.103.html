Credit Rating: S&P Lowers Ratings On Wells Fargo Debt

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/15/90

SAN FRANCISCO -- Standard & Poor's Corp. said it cut its rating on Wells Fargo & Co.'s senior debt to single-A-plus from double-A-minus. The rating concern also downgraded the banking company's commercial paper to single-A-minus from single-A-plus, preferred stock to single-A-minus from single-A, and letter-of-credit-backed issues to single-A-plus from double-A-minus. In all, about $3.7 billion in debt is affected.

S&P said the cuts reflect its concerns about the potential effects of Wells Fargo's high exposure to commercial real estate, as well as a major commitment to lending for highly leveraged transactions.

In a statement, Carl E. Reichardt, Wells Fargo's chairman, described the S&P action as "inappropriate."

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Wells Fargo shares closed at $73.50, down $1.375.
