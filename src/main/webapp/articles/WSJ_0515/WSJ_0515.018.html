Net Loss of American Confectionery Corp. Narrowed in Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/15/90

SACRAMENTO, Calif. -- After recent purchases of nut, candy and yogurt companies, American Confectionery Corp. expects to report a reduced net loss for the fiscal third quarter ended March 31, Steve Kircher, president, said. For the latest quarter, American Confectionery expects to report a net loss in the range of $900,000, or about 18 cents a share, compared with a year-earlier loss of $1 million, or about 21 cents, including respectively scaled-down net losses from continuing operations of $284,000, or five cents a share, and $726,000, or 15 cents a share. Besides the effect of acquisitions, Mr. Kircher said results have been skewed by a continuing strategy of converting company-owned stores to franchised operations, which is reducing revenue in the near-term.

"It is real clear that the core business is getting better," Mr. Kircher said, noting that the company's retail nut business, which had been operating at an annual $17 million loss when acquired in November 1988, was currently breaking even.

In the latest quarter, Mr. Kircher said revenue would approximate $6 million, compared with about $8 million a year earlier, with revenue from continuing operations increasing to about $4.6 million, from $4.3 million.

Mr. Kircher said results for the nine months would be "break-even to a small loss" compared with year-earlier profit of $1.1 million, or about 21 cents. Nine months revenue would be flat, or would slightly exceed the $25 million reported a year ago, and would increase to $17.4 million, from about $11 million on a continuing basis. Unlike a year earlier, the latest quarter and nine months didn't include the Easter holiday, an important time for candy sales.

American Confectionery became publicly held in March, with the acquisition of J. Higby's Inc., an unprofitable franchiser of frozen yogurt and specialty foods stores.
