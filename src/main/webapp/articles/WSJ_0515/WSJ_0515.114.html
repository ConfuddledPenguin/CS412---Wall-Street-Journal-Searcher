International Brief -- Marks & Spencer PLC: Retailer Reports 14% Rise In Pretax Profit for Year

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/15/90

Marks & Spencer PLC, a major British clothing and food retailer, reported a 14% rise in pretax profit for the year ended March 31, with a strong recovery in Europe offsetting difficulties in the U.S. Marks & Spencer said that pretax profit jumped to #604.2 million ($1.01 billion) in its latest fiscal year from #529 million a year earlier, reaching the upper limit of market expectations.

Profit after tax less minorities, or net profit attributable to shareholders, increased 13% to #389 million from #342.9 million the year before.

The company reported no extraordinary items for either the latest period or a year earlier.

Marks & Spencer's sales rose 9.5% to #5.61 billion from #5.12 billion.

The company said it was a "particularly good year" for ladies' clothing sales in the United Kingdom and Ireland, with continental Europe also showing a strong recovery in profit and sales compared with the previous reporting year.

Difficult trading conditions and severe price-cutting hit Brooks Brothers, Marks & Spencer's flagship store in the U.S. But the company's mail-order division "made good increases" there, the company said.
