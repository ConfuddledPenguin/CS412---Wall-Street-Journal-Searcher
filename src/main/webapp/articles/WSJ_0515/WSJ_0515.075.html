Letters to the Editor: Librarians Undaunted By Myriad Data Bases

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/15/90

In your April 3 article "Research Firm Taps Oceans of Data to Bail Out Clients," you say that with the number of data bases soaring, "greater expertise is required to master all the possibilities for harvesting data." Members of our association are trained to master the possibilities. That is our raison d'etre. Our members hold degrees not only in library science but also in their field of specialization. When it comes to harvesting data, we are the cream of the crop. And we're pretty good weeders too -- weeding out "irrelevant information."

A recent survey conducted in cooperation with the management-consulting firm Temple, Barker & Sloane revealed that nearly 90% of the senior-level managers in the 164 companies surveyed felt their librarians were delivering effective service, and nearly 80% agreed that librarians were responding to changing needs of companies. None of the respondents saw a decrease in the impact of the library on business. Hardly supportive of the statement that "in-house libraries might be slimmed or eliminated."

Perhaps the reason the ad executive mentioned in the article had such a long wait for information and subsequently turned to the information broker was that the firm's New York library had closed years prior to the start of his search -- a fact uncovered by one of our members minutes after she read the article.

Muriel Regan

President

Special Libraries Association

Washington
