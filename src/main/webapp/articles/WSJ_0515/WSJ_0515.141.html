International: Frankfurt Predicts GNP Growth of 3.5% to 4%

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/15/90

FRANKFURT -- The West German economy grew 2.5% in the first quarter of 1990 from the fourth quarter of 1989 after price and seasonal adjustments, the West German Economics Ministry said. The Economics Ministry said in a monthly report released yesterday that it now sees gross national product (GNP) growth for the full year of between 3.5% and 4%. That follows suggestions from Deutsche Bundesbank Vice President Helmut Schlesinger, who in a speech last Thursday said GNP would grow by "about 4%" in 1990.

In January, the Economics Ministry forecast that GNP would grow 3% in 1990. But by March, officials were indicating that that forecast, made before the proposal to introduce the Deutsche mark into East Germany, was in need of upward revision.

In February, the Economics Ministry said that fourth quarter GNP grew 1% in the fourth quarter from the third quarter.
