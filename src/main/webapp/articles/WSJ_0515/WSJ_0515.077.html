Letters to the Editor -- Safeway Debt and Net: Take a Closer Look

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/15/90

Your April 2 Heard on the Street column about the proposed Safeway Stores public offering was disturbing. Your main points were: that some prospective investors find Safeway's debt load and "lack of earnings" disturbing; and that others dislike the involvement of Kohlberg Kravis Roberts.

The first of these points was presented so devoid of context as to make it laughable. When compared with the cash flow that will have to support it, Safeway's debt load is significantly smaller than that of Kroger, another large (sales of $19 billion) public supermarket company. And when it comes to earnings, Safeway is at least making a net profit. You imply that Safeway's prospective P-E multiple of 36 is astronomical. To put this in context, neither Kroger nor Vons (also a public supermarket company, sales of $5 billion) boasts any P-E multiple at all, because both are still reporting net losses. Compared with these two perfectly respectable supermarket companies, Safeway looks a steal at 36 times.

I make no claims about KKR's treatment of bondholders in any one of its deals. But if reporters want to run anti-KKR stories, they should do so in the open, not by sly hints and suggestions.

Peter Morris

New York
