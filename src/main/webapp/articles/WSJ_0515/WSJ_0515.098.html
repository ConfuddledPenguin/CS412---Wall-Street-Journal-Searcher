TLC Beatrice Profit Totaled $133,000 In the First Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/15/90

NEW YORK -- TLC Beatrice International Holdings Inc. reported first-quarter net income of $133,000 compared with a year-ago $580,000 net loss. Operating profit and sales improved in both of TLC's segments -- food distribution and grocery products -- and favorable currency translations also helped results, the company said.

On a per-share basis, TLC posted a first-quarter loss of 23 cents, reflecting $2.3 million of preferred-stock dividends. The year-earlier loss equaled 25 cents a share. TLC's shares are closely held.

Sales climbed 25% to $310.4 million from $248.3 million.

"While we expect excellent results for the remainder of the year," said Reginald F. Lewis, chief executive officer, "our second- and third-quarter figures will determine our full-year performance because our ice-cream and dessert products are sold during the warm-weather period." TLC sells a number of European food products including Tato snack foods in Ireland and Samson ice cream in Italy.

In December, TLC canceled a proposed initial public offering as large investors shunned the plan. The company, formed two years prior as an investor group led by Mr. Lewis purchased what had been the foreign-food lines of Beatrice Cos. from investment bank Kohlberg Kravis Roberts & Co.
