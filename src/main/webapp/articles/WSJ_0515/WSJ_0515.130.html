Business Brief -- Patrick Petroleum Co.: Company Sells $20 Million Of 10.75% Eight-Year Notes

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/15/90

Patrick Petroleum Co. said it has sold $20 million of 10.75% eight-year subordinated collateralized notes to a group of institutional investors. Non-callable warrants, which expire in 1997, to purchase 800,000 shares of Patrick common stock were also issued to the participants. The Jackson, Mich.-based oil and gas exploration company said it will use proceeds to repay existing credit lines and provide "additional financial flexibility" to support its continuing oil and gas acquisition programs.

The notes, which are due in 1998, are secured by Patrick Petroleum's equity ownership in Penske Corp. and its Penske Transportation Inc. unit.

The institutional investors, headed by Westinghouse Credit Corp. of Pittsburgh, a unit of Westinghouse Electric Corp., also include Connecticut Mutual Life Insurance Co. of Hartford, Conn., and General American Life Insurance Co. of St. Louis. Boettcher & Co., of Denver, served as the private placement agent and financial adviser to the company.

"This is the best way to continue the rapid expansion of our oil and gas operations while maintaining the benefits of our Penske securities," said Patrick's president, U.E. Patrick, in a prepared statement. "We remain committed to our Penske investments and will continue to receive the benefits of future dividends and capital appreciation. Also, we will profit from this fixed-rate component of our capitalization and our relationship with these substantial financial institutions."
