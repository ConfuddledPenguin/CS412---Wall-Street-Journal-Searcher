Business Brief -- Hometown Bancorp.: First-Quarter Loss Posted, Buy-Back Plan Announced

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/15/90

Hometown Bancorp., parent company of the Bank of Darien, Darien, Conn., posted a first-quarter loss and announced a plan to buy back as much as 23.7% of its stock. The stock soared 75 cents in national over-the-counter trading yesterday to close at $3.75 a share.

The company also said Kevin E. Gage would succeed James E. Banks as president and chief executive officer of Hometown Bancorp. because, company Chairman Douglas D. Milne III said, "a more aggressive approach to management was required to steer the company through these troubled times."

Mr. Banks will become vice chairman of the company.

The loss was $2.1 million, or $1.13 a share, vs. year-earlier profit of $147,000, or eight cents a share. The company cited $2.1 million set aside for possible loan losses and other reserves, reflecting a troubled real estate market.
