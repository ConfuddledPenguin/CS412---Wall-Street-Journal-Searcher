Business Brief -- Mission Insurance Group Inc.: Unit's Rehabilitation Plan Is Approved by U.S. Court

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/15/90

Mission Insurance Group Inc. said a federal court approved the company's rehabilitation agreement for unit Mission Insurance Cos. that, among other things, calls for the transfer of $29 million in assets to government trusts. The Los Angeles-based insurer has been in bankruptcy-law proceedings since 1987. Its insolvency was one of the largest insurance-company failures in history.

The California Insurance Commission must resolve certain tax issues in connection with the plan.

Under the plan, the assets and liabilities of Mission Insurance Cos. will be transferred to trusts overseen by the California insurance commissioner. The transfer is to include $14 million in cash and 5.9 million Mission Insurance Group common shares, representing about 29% of the stock outstanding.
