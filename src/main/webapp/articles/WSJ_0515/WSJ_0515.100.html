Middleby Files Suit Against Whitman Unit For Breach of Contract

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/15/90

MORTON GROVE, Ill. -- Middleby Corp. filed suit against a Whitman Corp. unit, alleging fraud and breach of contract in the 1989 sale of the unit's food-service equipment operations. In a lawsuit filed in U.S. District Court in Chicago, the maker of food-service equipment claims Whitman's Hussmann Corp. unit misrepresented the profitability of its food-service group, which it sold to Middleby in July 1989 for $62.5 million. The operations Middleby bought include the Toastmaster, Victory Refrigeration, Southbend, Seco and Escan lines.

Middleby is seeking about $30 million it says it overpaid for the operations as a result of Hussmann's "misrepresentations and concealments." It is also asking for punitive damages of "at least $40 million." The suit names Hussmann as a defendant, but doesn't name Whitman, the parent.

Whitman announced in March that it plans to sell the Hussmann unit; Middleby said it had sought to meet with Whitman after the announcement that Hussmann would be sold, but had been "rebuffed."

A spokesman for Whitman called the suit a "crock of banana oil," and denied the allegations. In addition, the spokesman said, "Any time {Middleby} wanted to meet with us, we have met with them."
