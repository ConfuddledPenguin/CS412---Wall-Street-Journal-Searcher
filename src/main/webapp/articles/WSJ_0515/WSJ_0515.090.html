Business Brief -- Security Pacific Corp.: Mitsui Taiyo Kobe Acquires 5% Interest in Firm's Unit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/15/90

Security Pacific Corp. said in Los Angeles that Mitsui Taiyo Kobe Bank Ltd. completed its purchase of a 5% interest in Security's financial services subsidiary. The Japanese concern paid $85 million for the interest and will maintain the option of lifting its stake in Security Pacific Financial Services System Inc. to 10%.

Separately, Security Pacific's main banking unit said it acquired certain assets and assumed certain liabilities of Washington Savings & Loan Association, Stockton, Calif. Security Pacific National Bank said it concluded the plan with the Resolution Trust Corp., the agency in charge of disposing of the assets of failed thrifts. Washington Savings has four branches and more than 7,000 customers, the company said.
