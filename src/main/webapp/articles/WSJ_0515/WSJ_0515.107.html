Business Brief -- Metrobank Financial Group Inc.: Fourth-Quarter Loss Posted; Real Estate Market Cited

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/15/90

Metrobank Financial Group Inc., citing a soft real estate market, posted a fourth-quarter loss of $18 million, or $9.75 a share, and said it expects further losses unless the market improves. The loss included a $16.5 million addition to reserves to cover possible loan losses. Nonperforming assets totaled $42.4 million as of the end of 1989. A year earlier, Palisades Park, N.J.-based Metrobank earned $752,000, or 41 cents a share.

Metrobank said that it may not meet the new regulatory capital requirements imposed on all savings associations by the Financial Institutions Reform, Recovery and Enforcement Act of 1989. As a result, the company said, Metrobank may be subject to supervisory action by federal officials.

Metrobank also announced that as a result of restrictions on commercial lending, Malcolm Marsa has left as chairman and and chief executive officer. His brother Paul resigned as a director.

Michael P. Scaduto, president and chief operating officer, was named to the additional post of chief exectutive; and attorney Berek P. Don of Englewood Cliffs, N.J., already a director, was named chairman.
