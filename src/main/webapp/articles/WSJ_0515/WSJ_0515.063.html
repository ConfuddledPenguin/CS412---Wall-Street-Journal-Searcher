International: Brazil Will Begin Lifting Computer Import Limits

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 05/15/90

BRASILIA -- Brazil this week will begin lifting restrictions on computer imports and open the market to foreign multinationals to modernize its outdated industrial sector, a top official said. "Protectionism has created complacent producers and consumers," said Science and Technology Secretary Jose Goldemberg.

Brazil will publish a list Thursday of 57 products that would no longer receive subsidies and market protection, ranging from eight-bit microcomputers used for entertainment to low resolution monitors and non-laser printers.

This will open the market to identical products manufactured abroad. But these products will be charged import duties and excise taxes, he said.

The U.S. has been pressing Brazil to phase out subsidies for Brazilian makers of high-tech products and to open up the market to foreign competition.
