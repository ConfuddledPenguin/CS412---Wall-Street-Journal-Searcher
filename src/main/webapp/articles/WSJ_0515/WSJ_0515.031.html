LEISURE & ARTS: Time Off: Diversions and Excursions May 15-28

ART

The Brooklyn Museum, Brooklyn, N.Y.: "The Intimate Interiors of Edouard Vuillard" -- The first major exhibition in nearly 20 years devoted to Vuillard, whose jewel-like paintings depict scenes from 19th-century bourgeois life. The more than 100 paintings and works on paper include self-portraits, family scenes, paintings of seamstresses and portraits of the artist's intimate, the salon-keeper Misia Natanson. 200 Eastern Parkway, May 18-July 30.

The Metropolitan Museum of Art, New York: "Masterworks of Louis Comfort Tiffany" -- Windows, lamps, mosaics, ceramics, bronzes, paintings and jewelry by this master glassmaker. The show includes some of Tiffany's earliest experiments with Favrile glass and leaded glass shades, as well as such opulent later creations as the "Helen Gould Landscape Window." More than 5,000 pieces of glass were used to create this 11-foot panel, depicting a fawn drinking at a stream. 82nd Street and Fifth Avenue, through Sept. 9.

Place des Antiquaires, New York: "Chinnery & The China Coast-Paintings from the Collection of The Hongkong and Shanghai Bank" -- A first-ever exhibition of 70 paintings by 19th-century artists working in South China. Unknown Chinese artists are represented here along with Europeans such as George Chinnery, an English portraitist and draughtsman. Works include portraits of English traders and their lavish estates, Hong Kong merchants, and scenes from Asian life and trade. 125 E. 57th St., through June 2. (Monday through Saturday, 11-6). The show then leaves the country for Luxembourg and London.

Yale Center for British Art, New Haven, Conn.: "Powder Celestial: Snuff Boxes, 1700-1880" -- Men and women in all walks of life indulged in snuff, as a survey of the 300-odd European boxes collected here shows. The flasks, mulls and boxes are fashioned out of everything from wood, papier-mache and coquilla nut to tortoise shell, ivory, agate and straw. 1080 Chapel St., through June 24.

PHOTOGRAPHY

International Center of Photography Midtown, New York: "Lee Miller: Photographer" -- This fashion model, war correspondent, world traveler and chef also knew what to do with a camera. The 96 photographs on view here include powerful images from Ms. Miller's stint with the U.S. Army during World War II along with early surrealist photos and revealing portraits of Pablo Picasso, Man Ray and other friends. 1133 Avenue of the Americas, through June 10. The show then moves to the Art Institute of Chicago, June 23-Aug. 26; Santa Monica Museum of Art, Sept. 8-Nov. 4; The Rochester Institute of Technology, Rochester, N.Y., Nov. 12-Dec. 2.

DANCE

Parsons Dance Company: As a Paul Taylor dancer, David Parsons always stood out from the pack with his witty athleticism, his nonchalant virtuosity. Now his own fledgling troupe is starting to do the same. The company shows why in its first major New York season, tonight through May 20. Among the three premieres scheduled are "Nascimento," a lush ensemble work to an original score by Brazilian composer Milton Nascimento; and "Elysian Fields," an exploration of violence that features Parsons as a deceptively angelic-looking demon. Joyce Theater, 175 Eighth Ave. (at 19th Street). 212-242-0800.

MUSIC

Minnesota Opera, Minnesota: The world premiere of Libby Larsen's "Frankenstein, The Modern Prometheus" on May 25 retells Mary Shelley's sad story of the manmade monster who searches for love and understanding in a cruel world. Dale Johnson conducts a cast including Steven Tharp as Dr. Victor Frankenstein and Tom Schumacher as the monster. World Theater, 10 East Exchange St., St. Paul, Minn. 612-298-1300.

Charleston, S.C.: The Spoleto Festival U.S.A. starts off on May 25 with a gala performance of Beethoven's Seventh conducted by Leonard Bernstein. The 114-event festival includes a new production of Wagner's "Parsifal," directed by Gian Carlo Menotti, and the world premiere of a multimedia piece by Philip Glass, Allen Ginsberg and Jerome Sirlin, "The Hydrogen Jukebox." Dance, chamber music and theater are also featured this season, which closes on June 10. To order tickets by phone call 800-868-7228 or 803-577-4500.

Greenwich House, New York: Friends & Enemies of New Music celebrates its first season with a concert offering new works by, among others, Nancy Gunn and Tom Cipullo. 27 Barrow St. 212-874-4814.
