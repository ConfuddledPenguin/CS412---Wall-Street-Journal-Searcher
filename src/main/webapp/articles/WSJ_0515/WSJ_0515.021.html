Tyler Completes Sale Of Atlas Powder Co. To Imperial Chemical

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/15/90

DALLAS -- Tyler Corp. said it completed the sale of its Atlas Powder Co. unit in a stock transaction valued at about $127.8 million with Imperial Chemical Industries PLC, London. Tyler, once a Fortune 500 holding company with four diverse operating units, now has one remaining subsidiary. Under the transaction, Tyler shareholders will receive one share of a new Tyler Corp. representing Tyler Pipe Industries and the parent company's assets and liabilities, for each old Tyler share owned.

Shareholders will swap their old Tyler shares, representing Atlas Powder and $64.5 million in old Tyler bank debt, for stock in Imperial Chemical. Tyler holders can opt to receive either one Imperial Chemical American depositary receipt for each 12.7078 Tyler shares owned, or one Imperial common share for each 3.1769 Tyler shares owned. Each Imperial ADR represents four Imperial common shares.

Tyler said that based on the current Imperial ADR value, Tyler shareholders should receive roughly $6 a share for each old Tyler share.

Tyler, which will have 21.3 million shares outstanding after the transaction, said that an additional 558,413 Imperial shares, valued at $9.9 million, will be held in reserve pending certain contingencies. The company said they may be distributed to old Tyler shareholders at a later date.
