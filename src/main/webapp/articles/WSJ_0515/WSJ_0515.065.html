Who's News: Sallie Mae Chief Executive Named Dean at Dartmouth

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/15/90

WASHINGTON -- Edward A. Fox, president and chief executive officer of the Student Loan Marketing Association, was named dean of Dartmouth University's Amos Tuck School of business. Mr. Fox, 53 years old, succeeds Colin C. Blaydon, 49, who will return to teaching after taking a sabbatical to work on a book.

As Sallie Mae's first and only chief executive officer, Mr. Fox oversaw the student-loan servicing concern's evolution from a company with "a net worth of negative $50,000 on my first day" 17 years ago to one with assets of $37 billion.

Mr. Fox has taught at Georgetown University and the business schools of New York University, the University of Pennsylvania, and Harvard University.

The new dean said he intends to recruit more women and minority students and faculty to meet the changing demographic nature of the work force. "Women and minorities are replacing retiring white males," Mr. Fox said. "We should have more managers that come from those environments."

Sallie Mae said its board will meet next week to begin its search for a successor to Mr. Fox, who will step down in late summer. Separately, the company said Ronald F. Hunt, executive vice president and general counsel, resigned, effective July. Mr. Hunt, 46, will pursue private practice in his native North Carolina.
