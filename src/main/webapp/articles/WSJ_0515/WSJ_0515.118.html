Business Brief -- Mycogen Corp.: Pesticide Accord Is Reached With S.C. Johnson & Son

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/15/90

Mycogen Corp. said it entered a research and development pact with S.C. Johnson & Son Inc. to develop biological pesticides for common household pests. Under the agreement, S.C. Johnson, a private maker of consumer products for home, personal care and insect control based in Racine, Wis., will provide San Diego-based Mycogen with an undisclosed amount of research funding. Both firms will manufacture and market commercial products resulting from the research.

Andrew C. Barnes, executive vice president of Mycogen, wouldn't disclose which pests the company had targeted, but conceded that the cockroach, ant, flea, silverfish and termite would be obvious choices. Until now, Mycogen has marketed biological pesticides for commercial agricultural use only. Mycogen's products are based on natural agents such as micro-organisms, fatty acid compounds, and highly refined paraffinic oils.
