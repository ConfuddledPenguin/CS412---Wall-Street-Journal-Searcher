Business Brief -- Greyhound Lines Inc.: Carrier Sets Charter Fleets In Focus Markets for 1990

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/15/90

Greyhound Lines Inc. said it will operate charter fleets in 19 focus markets for the remainder of 1990. The unit of Dallas-based GLI Holding Inc. had charter sales offices in 34 cities prior to the continuing strike by 9,400 employees represented by the Amalgamated Transit Union. Frank Schmieder, president and chief operating officer, said the company selected the 19 metropolitan markets to launch its revamped charter operation "because of their strong year-round charter business."

Each location will have a dedicated fleet of buses reserved for charter customers. In the past, charter buses were drawn from the intercity passenger fleet. The new system will result in improved maintenance of charter coaches, the company said.

The restructuring of charter operations marks another attempt by the debt-burdened bus company to rebuild revenues that have been battered by the ATU strike that began March 2. The company announced last week that it was holding talks with lenders, creditors and suppliers in hopes of restructuring its lending agreements and debt payments.

No new negotiations have been scheduled with the striking union.
