Tucson Electric Wins Power-Sales Dispute Against San Diego Gas

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/15/90

TUCSON, Ariz. -- Tucson Electric Power Co. said that an arbitration panel ruled that the company didn't breach certain terms of a power-sales contract with San Diego Gas & Electric Co. and won't be required to refund $57 million in disputed payments to the California utility. The arbitration proceeding was begun in 1988 and the finding cannot be appealed. The dispute arose over sales of power by Tucson Electric to San Diego Gas between June 1987 and May 1989.

The dispute centered on the scheduling of available power by Tucson Electric to San Diego Gas. San Diego Gas asserted that Tucson had imposed scheduling restrictions that breached the purchase contract.

San Diego Gas also contended that Tucson Electric didn't meet its contractual obligations in terms of power capacity provided to San Diego Gas.

James Walsh, an attorney for San Diego G&E, said the two companies had filed lawsuits over the disputed power sales but had dropped the litigation and instead agreed to abide by the decision of the arbitration panel.
