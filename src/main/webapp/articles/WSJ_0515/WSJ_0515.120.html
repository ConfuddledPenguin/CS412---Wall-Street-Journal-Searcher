New Securities Issues

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/15/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATES

General Electric Capital -- $250 million issue of 8 3/4% bonds, due May 21, 2007, via Kidder Peabody & Co., priced at 99.55 to yield 8.863%. The noncallable bonds, which may be put back to the company May 21, 1995, were priced at a spread of 22 basis points above the Treasury's five-year note. The issue is rated Triple-A by both Moody's Investors Service Inc. and by Standard & Poor's Corp. General Electric Capital is a unit of General Electric Co.

Cincinnati Gas & Electric Co. -- $100 million of 10 1/8% first mortgage bonds, due May 1, 2020, priced at 99.567 to yield 10.17%. The bonds, which are nonrefundable for five years, were priced at a spread of 155 basis points above the Treasury's 8 1/2% bond maturing in 2020. Rated Baa-1 by Moody's and triple-B-plus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Morgan Stanley & Co.

MUNICIPALS

Rhode Island Housing Mortgage Finance Corp. -- $70 million homeownership opportunity revenue bonds, Series 2, due April 1 and Oct. 1, 1992-2001, due Oct. 1, 2010 and 2021 and April 1, 2022, tentatively priced by a Goldman Sachs & Co. group to yield from 6.35% in 1992 to 7.85% in 2022. Current interest serial bonds are priced at par to yield from 6.35% in 1992 to 7.20% in 2001. There are $10.39 million term bonds priced at par to yield 7.80% in 2010 and $20 million term bonds priced at par to yield 7.55% in 2021. There are $30.8 million term bonds priced at par to yield 7.85% in 2022. Ratings of single-A-1 by Moody's and single-A-plus by S&P, are expected.

MORTGAGE-BACKED

Merrill Lynch & Co. -- $300 million of GNMA-Backed Trust Remic mortgage securities, offered in 15 classes by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets. The offering, Series 3, is by a trust established by Merrill Lynch. For classes on which details are available, yields range from 9.44% to 10.50%. The issue is backed by Ginnie Mae 9 1/2% securities, with a weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29.4 years.

Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $300 million of Remic mortgage securities, offered in 19 classes by Kidder Peabody & Co. The offering, Series 176, is backed by Freddie Mac 10% securities with a weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29.4 years. The floater and inverse floater have estimated average lives of 7.14 years.

Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $300 million of Remic mortgage securities, offered in 12 classes by BT Securities Inc. The offering, Series 175, is backed by Freddie Mac 9 1/2% securities with a weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29.7 years.

EUROBONDS

International Finance Corp. (agency) -- $300 million of 9 1/4% Eurobonds due June 7, 1995, at a fixed reoffered price of 99.65, via Deutsche Bank Capital Markets Ltd.

Swedish National Mortgage Corp. (SBAB)(Sweden) -- $300 million of 9 1/2% Eurobonds due May 30, 1995, priced at 101 1/2 to yield 9.60% less fees, via Nomura International. Fees 1 7/8.

Ford Capital BV (U.S. parent) -- $250 million of 9 3/4 Eurobonds due June 5, 1997, at an issue price of 101.635, via Credit Suisse First Boston. Reoffered at a fixed price of 100.06. Guaranteed by Ford Motor Co.

Altus Finance SA (France) -- $200 million of floating-rate notes due June 6, 2000, at a fixed reoffered price of 99.95 via Credit Lyonnais. Coupon at 1/16 point above the six-month London Interbank Offered Rate. Guaranteed by Credit Lyonnais. Callable on any coupon date at par after the fifth year.

Japan Development Bank (Japan) -- $150 million of 9 1/2% Eurobonds due June 11, 1997, priced at 101.70 to yield 9.54%, via LTCB International Ltd. Guaranteed by Japan.

Procter & Gamble Co. (U.S.) -- 125 billion lire of 13% Eurobonds due Dec. 29, 1993, priced at 101.80 via Istituto Bancario San Paolo di Torino.

National Westminster Australia Bank (Australia) -- 25 million Australian dollars of 15 1/8% Eurobonds due May 10, 1993, priced at 101.75 via NatWest Capital Markets. Guaranteed by National Westminster Bank. Fungible with an existing 50-million Australian dollar issue launched in March, from the payment date.

Caisse Nationale des Telecommunications (France) -- 200 million Swiss francs of 7 1/4% public bonds due June 19, 2000, priced at 101 1/4, via Credit Suisse. Guarantee by French Republic. Call from June 19, 1998 at 102.

New Zealand -- 300 million Deutsche marks of floating-rate notes due June 8, 1997, priced at 1/16 point under the three-month London Interbank Offered Rate at par, via CSFB-Effectenbank. First LIBOR rate will be fixed June 6. Callable beginning June 8, 1995 at par.

OKOBANK (Finland) -- 200 million Deutsche marks of floating-rate notes at the six-month London Interbank Offered Rate plus 0.045 point, priced at 100.30 due June 15, 2000 via DG Bank. First LIBOR fixing June 11. Put and call beginning June 15 1995.
