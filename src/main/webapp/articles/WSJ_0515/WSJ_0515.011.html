Who's News: Curtice-Burns Foods Says Petty Will Head Consolidated Lines

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/15/90

ROCHESTER, N.Y. -- Curtice-Burns Foods Inc. said several of its divisions will be consolidated under William Petty, who is executive vice president of this food concern. Effective June 1, Mr. Petty, 58 years old, also will become chief executive of the company's Comstock Michigan Fruit division, succeeding Robert Silkett, 60, who is retiring. Several other divisions will report to Mr. Petty, including the company's Nalley's division, Brooks Food division, Farman Brothers Pickle Co. and Lucca Packing Co.

Curtice said the reorganization of its top management structure is aimed at improving its performance and will allow the company to respond more quickly to market demands.

As reported, Curtice-Burns reported a 25% decline in net income for the fiscal nine months and said it expects lower earnings for the year ending June 30 because of plant and administrative consolidations and unfavorable crop conditions.

Curtice said Tommy Murray, 50, who was named chief executive of its newly formed snack foods group last November, will be reporting to David J. McDonald, president and chief executive of Curtice-Burns. The snack group is made up of the Snyder's and Hiland divisions, Tim's Cascade Chips and the Nalley's U.S. Snack Food unit.

Other divisions reporting to Mr. McDonald are Southern Frozen Foods, National Oats Co. and the meat snack group, the company said.
