New England Mutual Life Hires Riley From Prudential to Head Loomis Sayles

  • Author: Gary Putka
  • Date: 05/15/90

BOSTON -- New England Mutual Life Insurance Co. hired Robert Riley, the No. 2 investment officer at Prudential Insurance Co. of America, as president and chief executive officer of its Loomis, Sayles & Co. unit. Industry officials said Mr. Riley's departure represents a potential loss for Prudential, where he oversaw a strategic shift that is credited with helping build assets during a six-year tenure. He will leave behind responsibility for $98.7 billion, representing all of Prudential's external, or nonpremium generated, money-management portfolios, for $30 billion in institutional and individual portfolios at Loomis Sayles.

Mr. Riley will take over his new positions on June 1 from Robert Kemp, who is helping to run a spinoff of Loomis Sayles created last October.

Under Mr. Riley, Prudential helped push what has become a growing trend in money management toward a looser, more decentralized way of handling assets. Several specialty management firms, including concerns focusing on foreign securities, portfolios that mimic market indexes, or heavily computer-based investing were bought or formed. Under the system, principals in the firms were given wider autonomy than previous in-house portfolio managers, and were rewarded according to performance.

"It's been one of the clearest displays of good money-management marketing," said David O'Donovan, director of money-manager research at SEI Corp., a Chicago pension fund consultant. Prudential has "made knowing who to deal with much clearer, so it's easier to pick and choose."

It was unclear whether Mr. Riley would be pursuing a similar strategy at Loomis Sayles, which represents the bulk of New England Mutual's $57 billion New England Investment Cos. unit. Mr. O'Donovan speculated that Mr. Riley "might open them up to different styles and different products."

But Ralph Verni, chief executive of New England Investment, said, "We want to continue in the future to foster a high degree of independence" among money-management operations, but said that Mr. Riley "won't be taking us into a lot of different" investment areas. Mr. Riley couldn't be reached for comment.

Under Mr. Riley, who recently served as executive vice president of Prudential Investment Corp. as chairman of Prudential Realty Group, Prudential's non-premium money management assets tripled to their current $98.7 billion level. Acquisitions included Jennison Associated, purchased in 1985 for $80 million. Assets under management at Jennison have grown to $21.7 billion from $15 billion at the time of the purchase.
