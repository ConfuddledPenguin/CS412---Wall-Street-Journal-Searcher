Business Brief -- Residential Resources Mortgage: Bankruptcy Court Accepts Firm's Reorganization Plan

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/15/90

Residential Resources Mortgage Investments Corp., a Scottsdale, Ariz.-based mortgage-investment company that filed for bankruptcy-law protection in January 1989, said that the U.S. Bankruptcy Court in Phoenix accepted the company's submitted plan for reorganization. The plan provides, among other things, that shareholders retain their common-stock rights in the real estate investment trust. It also calls for certain secured creditors to receive warrants to acquire as much as 30% of the company's common stock. Also, certain unsecured creditors will receive cash payments within 30 days of about 65 cents on the dollar for their claims, the company said.

After making the payments, the company will have "very nominal net worth and assets" of less than half a million dollars, said Thomas T. Hammond, Residential's chairman. He said he intends "to explore every alternative to attract new capital to the company but no such commitments exist at this time."
