Moody's Downgrades Debt of McDonnell

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/15/90

ST. LOUIS -- Moody's Investors Service Inc., citing the deteriorating outlook for defense contractors, lowered its ratings on about $3.4 billion of McDonnell Douglas Corp.'s senior and subordinated debt. Moody's downgraded McDonnell Douglas's senior debt and shelf registration to Baa-1 from single-A-3, and subordinated debt to Baa-2 from Baa-1.

The rating concern said its action also was based on McDonnell Douglas's "continuing operating difficulties at its commercial aircraft operations."

A McDonnell Douglas spokesman said the company was notified by Moody's several weeks ago that it might downgrade the debt. "Everybody knows we're experiencing financial difficulties, particularly in our transport-aircraft business, where we had an operating loss of $84 million in the first quarter," the spokesman said. "But that's also an area where we think there's tremendous potential."

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, McDonnell Douglas shares closed at $47.875, down $1.
