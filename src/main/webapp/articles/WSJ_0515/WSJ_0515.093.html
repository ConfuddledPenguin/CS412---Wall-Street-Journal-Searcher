Armstrong Wins 3 Of 4 Contested Seats For Company's Board

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/15/90

LANCASTER, Pa. -- Armstrong World Industries Inc. won three out of four board seats contested at the company's annual meeting, according to a preliminary tally of proxy votes cast. The Belzberg family, which owns about 11.5% of Armstrong, collected enough votes to elect one director. Although the vote was not binding, five governance amendments proposed by the Belzbergs were also defeated, pending a federal court decision on the proposals' validity. According to the preliminary figures, votes against each proposal ranged from 18.7 million to 19.3 million, votes in favor ranged from 15.2 million to 15.6 million, and abstentions ranged from 4.6 million to 4.9 million.

Although Armstrong said the proposals were "soundly rejected," the building products concern said it would consider the shareholders' views on them. If enacted, the amendments would make it easier for holders to force the board to take certain actions, such as approve a takeover.

The board's nominees received about two-thirds of the votes cast at the April 30 meeting, with the Belzberg nominees receiving one-third, the company said. Armstrong has a cumulative voting system for directors, in which shareholders received four votes per share to allocate any way they chose. Approximately 83% of Armstrong's 47.2 million shares voted in the election.

Armstrong said it was gratified by the results. "Despite the Belzbergs' strenuous efforts and expensive campaign, a substantial majority of our shareholders didn't buy what the Belzbergs attempted to sell," said Chairman and Chief Executive William W. Adams in a statement.

The Belzbergs, who have repeatedly indicated they might make a bid for the company, said they considered the vote on the amendments a victory, after discounting effects of the employee stock ownership plan and certain discretionary proxies that were voted by management.

Final results will not be available for several days, and all proxies are subject to review and challenge.
