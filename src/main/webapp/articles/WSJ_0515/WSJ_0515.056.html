NASA Awards Lockheed Unit $971 Million Job

  • Author: Rhonda L. Rundle
  • Date: 05/15/90

SUNNYVALE, Calif. -- Lockheed Corp.'s Lockheed Missiles & Space Co. subsidiary said it received a $971 million contract from the National Aeronautics and Space Administration to oversee design and development of a new generation of rocket motors for the space shuttle. Under terms of the five-year contract, Lockheed Missiles is the prime contractor and systems engineer for development of 20 advanced, solid rocket motors for use in the space agency's shuttle launches beginning in 1995.

The motors will be built at a plant to be constructed on 1,100 acres of federal land near Iuka, Miss., close to the Tennessee and Alabama borders. GenCorp Inc.'s Aerojet General Corp. subsidiary, Folsom, Calif., will produce the motors. Wheelabrator Technologies Inc.'s RUST International Corp. subsidiary, based in Birmingham, Ala., will manage the plant construction.

Of the $971 million award, Lockheed will receive "a little more than a quarter and two-thirds go to Aerojet," said a Lockheed spokesman. The balance will be paid to various subcontractors.

If NASA exercised an option for production of up to 88 additional motors in the mid-1990s, the contract's value could rise by as much as $1.39 billion, Lockheed said.

Aerojet's portion will be split with subcontractors, including Babcock & Wilcox, a New Orleans unit of McDermott International Inc. and Thiokol Corp., Brigham City, Utah. Aerojet will employ up to 1,000 people and Lockheed will employ about 70 at the site, Lockheed said.

The proposed rocket motors will have a lift capability of 65,000 pounds of payload, about 12,000 pounds more than the existing motors, which were refurbished after the Challenger explosion four years ago. Besides being more powerful, the new motors will be safer, mainly because there are fewer joints.

Separately, about 900 machinists union members at Lockheed's aircraft service unit in Ontario, Calif., voted to accept the company's latest employment contract offer. The settlement ends a nine-week strike by machinists at that location.

Under the contract terms, machinists will receive a 5% bonus plus $2,000 in the first year; in the second and third years, top-grade hourly employees will receive a 3% wage increase and 2% bonus, with all others receiving a 5% bonus. The agreement also contains cost-of-living allowances over the three years.
