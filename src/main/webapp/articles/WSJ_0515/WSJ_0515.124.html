Spain's CPI Rose 0.2% in April

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/15/90

MADRID -- Spain's consumer price index rose 0.2% in April from March despite a sharp drop in food prices, the country's National Statistics Institute said. The monthly increase brings Spain's year-to-year inflation rate to 7%, unchanged from March and down from 7.3% in February.

Prices excluding food prices climbed 0.7% in April from March. Food prices fell 0.6% in April from March with the year-onyear increase in food prices at 7.4% at the end of April.

The inflation figures fell within analysts' expectations, which called for a rise in the CPI of between 0.2% and 0.4%.

At the end of April, the consumer price index stood at 161.8 from 161.4 in March. The index is based on 1983 equals 100.
