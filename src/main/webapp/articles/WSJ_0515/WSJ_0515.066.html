Abreast of the Market: Industrial Stocks Rally to Record Of 2821.53 as Volume Stays High

  • Author: David Wilson
  • Date: 05/15/90

NEW YORK -- The stock market rallied to a record high on the Dow Jones Industrial Average as encouraging prospects for interest rates and inflation continued to push investors off the sidelines. Prices closed well below the day's best levels, however, as profit-taking surfaced in the afternoon amid speculation that the market had overdone its advance.

"There's a lot of people that are still saying, `We've got to correct. We've gone too far too fast,'" said Dale Tills, manager of institutional equities trading at Charles Schwab & Co., San Francisco.

The industrial average gave back most of its gains but managed to finish 19.95 higher at 2821.53 -- above the previous record of 2810.15, established Jan. 2.

After surging 63.07 points Friday, the Dow industrial average tacked on an additional 52 points through midday as a result of continued buying interest from institutional investors seeking to offset short positions, and some occasional bursts of futures-related program buying.

In the process, the average surpassed its best-ever intraday reading with ease. On Jan. 3, the 30-stock measure had peaked at 2834.04; in the latest session, it exceeded that mark by nearly 20 points.

Broader market indexes, hampered by their relatively weak performance in comparison to the Dow industrial average over the past few months, didn't come close to setting record highs of their own.

Standard & Poor's 500-Stock Index closed 1.4% below its all-time high of 359.80, set last Oct. 9. Also, the New York Stock Exchange Composite Index and the Dow Jones Equity Market Index finished 2.9% and 2.4%, respectively, below the all-time high set on the same day.

In the latest session, the S&P 500 gained 2.75 to 354.75, while the composite index added 1.41 to 193.65, and the Dow equity index went up 2.68 to 329.74. Advancing issues led decliners by 1,062 to 555 on the Big Board.

Market activity remained brisk with 225,410,000 shares changing hands. The volume was the second highest this year on the Big Board, outranked only by Friday's total of 234,040,000.

Among technical analysts, who focus on market statistics in assessing the outlook for stock prices, the broad market's relatively weak performance in comparison to the Dow industrial average has been a source of concern for some time.

But some technicians said the situation appears likely to change for the better in the wake of Friday's surge, in which the number of shares traded above their previous close on the Big Board topped the volume of decliners by a margin of more than 19-to-1.

The ratio of advancing volume to declining volume has exceeded 9-to-1 only two dozen times in the past 30 years, according to a study by Richard Eakle, president of Eakle Associates, a Fair Haven, N.J.-based research firm.

The study found that stock prices were higher three months later in 19 of those 24 instances. Six months later, the market was higher all but once -- in the aftermath of the October 1987 crash -- and the Dow industrial average showed an average gain of 17.3%.

In addition, Mr. Eakle says, measures of the market's breadth, or the relationship between advancing and declining stocks, appear to be "inextricably linked to trends in interest rates." With rates having fallen recently, further strength in the broad market can be anticipated, he says.

Ned Davis, a technical analyst based in Venice, Fla., says the market could be headed for "an upside blow-off," citing the new high in the Dow industrial average, high levels of cash and short interest among investors, and indications that many market players remain pessimistic in spite of the market's strength.

If such a blow-off takes place, Mr. Davis says, two possible targets would emerge for the industrial average -- "just below 3000 if people turn bullish rapidly and everyone is focused on 3000 as a target," or 3180, representing 30 times his estimate of $106 in 1990 dividend payments for the 30 stocks in the average.

Du Pont rallied 1 3/4 to 40 3/8 in Big Board composite trading of 2.6 million shares. Mabon Nugent upgraded its investment rating on the stock to "buy" from "neutral" and raised its earnings estimates for 1990 and 1991.

Federal National Mortgage Association, the most active Big Board issue, dropped 7/8 to 37 7/8 on 4.1 million shares as selected financial stocks became a target for profit-taking after setting the pace for Friday's surge. The stock reached 40 5/8 before pulling back in the afternoon.

Progressive climbed 1 7/8 to 40. The Wall Street Journal reported in the "Heard on the Street" column that analysts believe the company will benefit from other insurers' pullbacks from the auto insurance market.

International Business Machines rose 1/4 to 114 1/4 on 2.3 million shares, but other major technology issues fared better with investors. Digital Equipment went up 1 7/8 to 90 1/2, Compaq Computer gained 2 to 112 3/8, and Hewlett-Packard rose 1 1/4 to 48 3/8 on 1.7 million shares.

Prime Motor Inns advanced 1 3/4 to 12 1/8 on 1.7 million shares in the wake of Manor Care's expression of interest in making a bid for the company's Howard Johnson and Ramada franchise systems. As previously reported, Prime Motor agreed in principle to sell a 65% stake in the systems to Blackstone Capital Partners for about $200 million in cash and notes.

USX rose 1 to 34 1/4 on 1.9 million shares following a favorable review of the company in Barron's magazine from Frederick Leuffer, an analyst at C.J. Lawrence, Morgan Grenfell. He said the stock's intrinsic value, based on a variety of measures, is around $50 a share.

Atlantic Richfield jumped 2 7/8 to 118 3/4. In a 7-2 ruling, the Supreme Court threw out an antitrust lawsuit against the company by independent gas stations in California and Washington state.

W.R. Grace advanced 3/4 to 28 1/2 after news that California officials are testing a new kind of catalytic converter, manufactured by the company's Camet unit.

Mylan Laboratories went down 1 1/4 to 22 5/8. The company acknowledged to Dow Jones Professional Investor Report that April sales of Eldepryl, a drug produced by a joint venture of Mylan and Bolar Pharmaceutical, fell more than 7% from the March level. Bolar dropped 5/8 to 7 1/2 in American Stock Exchange trading.

The Amex Market Value Index rose 1.84 to 354.34, its 11th straight advance, but remained well below the all-time high of 397.03 set last Oct. 10. Volume totaled 16,170,000 shares compared with 16,600,000 Friday.
