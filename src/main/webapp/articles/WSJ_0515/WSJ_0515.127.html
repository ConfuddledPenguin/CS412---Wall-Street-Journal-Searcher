Business Brief -- Apple Bancorp Inc.: Holders Requested to Reject Proxy Fight by Bidder Stahl

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/15/90

Apple Bancorp Inc. urged shareholders to reject a proxy fight organized by Stanley Stahl and affirmed its rejection of the New York real-estate investor's $38-a-share tender offer. The New York-based parent of Apple Bank for Savings told shareholders in a letter, "We believe that it is coercive for Mr. Stahl to condition his offer on having you first vote to give him control" of Apple before the board "has had a full opportunity to explore and pursue financially more-attractive alternatives."

Mr. Stahl, who controls 30.6% of Apple and has offered $121.6 million for the rest, declined to comment on the letter, which Apple disclosed as part of a Securities and Exchange Commission filing. The company and Mr. Stahl have lawsuits pending against each other.

Apple's directors haven't set a date for the annual meeting; Mr. Stahl's lawsuit demands, among other things, that the board set such a date. The company has said that the board hasn't set the date in order to allow time to explore alternatives to Mr. Stahl's bid.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Apple shares closed at $44, unchanged.
