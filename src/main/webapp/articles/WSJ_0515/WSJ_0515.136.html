Technology: Searle to Offer Free Trials Of Blood Pressure Drug

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/15/90

SKOKIE, Ill. -- G.D. Searle & Co. said it will introduce a blood pressure medicine in the U.S. by giving it away for six months. Under what the drug company calls its Patient Plus program, Searle will provide doctors with vouchers to be given to patients with their prescriptions for the drug Kerlone. The vouchers can be redeemed at pharmacies as prescriptions for the drug are filled.

Searle, a unit of Monsanto Co., a St. Louis-based diversified chemicals company, is introducing Kerlone into a crowded market of drugs called beta blockers. Two of the biggest competitors will be Tenormin, sold by Imperial Chemical Industries PLC; and Lopressor, sold by Ciba-Geigy Ltd.

The Searle vouchers cover both the pharmacist's dispensing fee and the wholesale cost of the drug -- which for six months would run from about $100 to $150, depending on the dosage prescribed. Patients can be enrolled in the program from May 15 until Aug. 31. A spokeswoman for Searle said the company hasn't estimated the total value of the drug to be given away.

Searle said it hopes Kerlone will win favor with doctors because of its sustained action in the body.
