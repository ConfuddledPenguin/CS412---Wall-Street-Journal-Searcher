Southland Reports $69.4 Million Loss For First Quarter

  • Author: Karen Blumenthal
  • Date: 05/15/90

DALLAS -- Southland Corp. said it had a net loss of $69.4 million in the first quarter after a $52 million tax benefit related to the sale of a 50% stake in Citgo Petroleum Corp. Included in its $121.5 million loss before the tax benefit was $133.3 million in interest expense.

A year earlier, the convenience store retailer had a net loss of $169.4 million, including a $56 million charge related to a debt-exchange offer and $17.3 million in profit from discontinued operations. The year-ago loss from continuing operations of $130.7 million included $144.7 million in interest expense.

Revenue rose about 2% to $1.94 billion from $1.9 billion.

The company said its average merchandise sales increased about 3.6% in the quarter, while its merchandise gross profits rose about 2.5%. Retail gasoline profits declined 2.4%, largely because Southland has fewer outlets this year than last.

Southland, which went private in a $4.9 billion leveraged buy-out in 1987, has proposed swapping its public debt and selling 75% of its stock to its Japanese partners for $400 million to avert a liquidity crisis and possible bankruptcy filing. However, its debt-swap proposal already has encountered heated resistance from bondholders, many of whom don't want to accept the proposed zero-coupon bonds with longer maturities for the interest-paying debt they now own.

Southland said it currently is holding discussions with bondholders.

The company also said that its has reduced its senior bank term loan to $900.4 million from about $2.55 billion when it went private, and that it has repaid about $399.5 million this year. It still will owe $12.9 million on June 30 and $18.8 million on both Sept. 30 and Dec. 31 on that loan.
