Head of Southland Communications Inc. Is Target of SEC Suit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/15/90

NEW YORK -- The Securities and Exchange Commission filed a civil suit in federal court in Manhattan accusing Ahmad Bayaa, the president of Southland Communications Inc., and five others of a scheme to manipulate the price of Southland Communications stock. The SEC suspended trading in Southland Communications, a Santa Ana, Calif., company that leases pagers, in early April because of questions about control of the company's stock. In the civil suit, the agency accused the defendants of arranging transactions to boost the price of the securities by "creating the false illusion of widespread market interest." The suit also accuses them of violating securities law by not disclosing that they controlled more than 5% of the stock.

Ahmad Bayaa and other Southland Communications officials couldn't be reached for comment.

The SEC alleges in the suit that the price of Southland Communications securities almost doubled between March 13 and April 3, although there were no positive company developments to account for the price rise. The lawsuit charged that the stock price was manipulated in the face of heavy short selling, which usually indicates that investors expect the price of a security to decline.
