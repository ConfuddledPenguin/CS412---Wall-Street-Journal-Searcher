Del Webb Reaches Accord With Director To Avert Proxy Fight

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/10/90

PHOENIX, Ariz. -- Del Webb Corp. agreed to some demands made by a director and major shareholder in a move to avert a proxy battle by the director at the company's annual meeting May 16. The company said it agreed to renominate four of 10 directors whose terms were scheduled to expire, including James J. Cotter, who planned the proxy fight. Mr. Cotter owns 9.8% of Del Webb's stock.

In addition, Del Webb said it would expand the board by two members. One of the new directors, whose identity hasn't yet been disclosed, will be jointly nominated by Mr. Cotter and the company.

The second, nominated by the company, is Hugh Culverhouse Jr., 42 years old, a senior partner in the Miami law firm of Culverhouse & Ross, who owns 72,000 of the real-estate development concern's 9.4 million common shares outstanding.

Mr. Cotter agreed to drop plans to offer a rival slate of directors. In addition, he won't push to eliminate "golden parachute" contracts held by senior management, Del Webb said.

A company spokesman said: "We didn't think it was worthwhile to engage in an expensive proxy fight."

Mr. Cotter had intended to propose a rival slate of three directors, including himself. His term on the 10-member board is set to expire at the annual meeting, and Del Webb had said last month it wouldn't renominate him to the board.

The agreement doesn't preclude Mr. Cotter, who couldn't be reached to comment, from waging a proxy fight in the future. Despite the agreement, Mr. Cotter, chairman of Craig Corp., a Los Angeles supermarket chain and photographic-accessories concern, intends to actively solicit proxies for his proposed shareholder-rights plan at the annual meeting. Del Webb's board adopted so-called poison-pill provisions last year.
