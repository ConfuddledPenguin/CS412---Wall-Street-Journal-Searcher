Technology: Advanced Micro Says Revenue Was Flat In the First Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/10/90

SUNNYVALE, Calif. -- Advanced Micro Devices Inc. said it expected to post flat first-quarter revenue, lower than the company had anticipated. However, Advanced Micro said an extraordinary gain from a previously announced technology transfer with Sony Corp. pushed first-quarter net income above the nine cents a share it earned in the year-earlier period.

Advanced Micro said revenue was about the same as the $269.6 million the semiconductor concern generated in 1989's first quarter. It didn't give an estimate for per-share earnings. The company blamed the revenue results on lower-than-anticipated prices on chip products, notably Eproms, a type of chip that is one of Advanced Micro's main products.

Advanced Micro has been struggling for many quarters to overcome problems stemming from a broad slump in the chip industry and some basic strategic errors. Its results for the latest quarter mirror the problems encountered by several other chip makers.

W.J. Sanders, chairman and chief executive officer, said orders picked up late in the period, especially in international markets. He said that increase, coupled with already-implemented cost cutting measures, should result in better operating margins in the current quarter.

In over-the-counter trading yesterday, Advanced Micro closed at $8.50, down 37.5 cents.
