Technology: WordPerfect, Novell, Lotus to Combine Customer Services

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/10/90

SAN FRANCISCO -- Lotus Development Corp. and Novell Inc., which previously announced plans to merge, said they intend to combine customer-service operations with that of WordPerfect Inc., a closely held firm based in Orem, Utah, that dominates the market for word-processing software. The move is further evidence that Lotus and Novell intend to compete aggressively against Microsoft Inc., generally viewed as the most influential software supplier in the computer industry.

Jim Manzi, Lotus chief executive officer, said the joint service operation makes sense because each of the three firms share many customers. "Wouldn't it be wonderful if a customer gets to call one place and gets an answer to their question," Mr. Manzi said.

Lotus already exchanges technology with WordPerfect, but Mr. Manzi said WordPerfect isn't interested in merging with Lotus and Novell.

Nevertheless, Lotus and Novell say cooperating with WordPerfect will benefit them. The trio of companies "are now in a position where they can help guide the {personal computer} industry," said Raymond Noorda, Novell chief executive.

Mr. Manzi predicted that the deal between Lotus and Novell, which would give the combined company greater revenue than that at Microsoft, should be completed in 90 days. He said he expects shareholders of both companies to vote on the proposed deal in early July.

Corrections & Amplifications

LOTUS DEVELOPMENT Corp., Novell Inc. and WordPerfect Inc. will assist one another in answering questions from customers, but the service operations of each software company will remain independent. In last Tuesday's edition such efforts were described incorrectly.

(WSJ April 16, 1990)
