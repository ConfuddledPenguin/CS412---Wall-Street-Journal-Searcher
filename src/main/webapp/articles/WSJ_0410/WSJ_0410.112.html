Credit Ratings

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/10/90

STATE OF CONNECTICUT -- Moody's Investors Service lowered its ratings on the state's general-obligation bonds to double-A from double-A-1. It pointed out that "a third-successive operating deficit will fully eliminate the state's remaining budget reserve and leave the state with a sizable operating imbalance for the fiscal year ending June 30." Last week, Standard & Poor's Corp. lowered its ratings on $28 billion of Connecticut's general obligation bonds.
