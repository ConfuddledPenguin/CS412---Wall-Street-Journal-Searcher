Business Brief -- Amre Inc.: Management Group Won't Make Bid for Dallas Firm

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/10/90

Amre Inc. said that a management group that for several months has been considering making an offer to take the company private won't make a bid after all. The Dallas-based direct marketer of home-improvement products said that its president, Steven D. Bedowitz; its executive vice president, Robert Levin; and its senior vice president, Ronald L. Wagner decided over the weekend that an offer for the company wasn't in the best interests of Amre's minority shareholders, the company itself or the management group. The group said its decision wasn't influenced by questions about whether it could get financing for an offer.

Just last week, the group said it planned to make an offer to acquire the 44% of Amre it didn't already own for $8.75 a share, or roughly $54.3 million. The group met with a special committee of the company's directors to discuss its expected bid.
