Technology: Semiconductor Indicator Climbed Again in March

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 04/10/90

The semiconductor industry's leading indicator rose for the third consecutive month in March, signaling that the industry's modest rebound from depressed sales in the last half of 1989 is continuing. Doug Andrey, director of statistics for the Semiconductor Industry Association, which compiles the widely watched sales indicator, called April's improvement "mild" and said chip sales for the year are still expected to be about 3.5% below last year's levels. "We won't see strong growth until 1991," he said.

The association said the so-called "book-to-bill" ratio for April climbed to 1.09 from 1.05 in February. Chip billings for March totaled $1.3 billion, 15.2% higher than February's $1.1 billion and 5.3% lower than a year earlier.

Average monthly orders for the three months ended in March totaled $1.27 billion, 5.4% above February's figure and 0.2% higher than a year earlier.
