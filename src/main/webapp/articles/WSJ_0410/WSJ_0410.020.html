OTC Focus: Market Ends Lower as MCI Announces $1.25 Billion Buy-Out of Telecom USA

  • Author: Anne Newman and Craig S. Smith
  • Date: 04/10/90

Over-the-counter stocks barely budged in light trading as some investors headed home early to begin celebrating Passover and others prepared for a day off on Good Friday. The Nasdaq Composite Index of OTC stocks edged down just 0.72, or 0.17%, to close at 430.18. Heavy trading in MCI Communications, which fell 1 1/8 to 35 3/4 after announcing it would pay $42 a share, or $1.25 billion, for long-distance carrier Telecom USA, helped flatten the index.

Trading is expected to be light all week because of the two religious holidays. "I expect a quiet week and don't see any big swings," said Peter DaPuzzo, head of OTC trading for Shearson Lehman Hutton. "As traders, we'll provide our normal liquidity, but I don't expect people to be aggressive or go out on a limb on a week like this."

OTC indexes are market weighted, magnifying the effect of MCI's drop on the overall market. The New York Stock Exchange composite index closed about 0.3% higher.

OTC decliners led advancers 850 to 1,014. National Market System volume fell to 84,575,100 shares, compared with Friday's 102,138,400 shares. Trading in MCI accounted for more than 3.4 million shares.

MCI's announcement sparked excitement in another regional telecommunications concern, Advanced Telecommunications. Shares of the Atlanta-based company jumped 24% in sympathy with Telecom to close up 3 1/4 at 16 3/4, on about 586,000 shares, or 10 times normal volume.

Advanced Telecommunications is the nation's second largest regional long-distance carrier, behind Telecom, in an industry facing a "hot-bed" of consolidation, according to analyst Mark Dunkel of Robinson-Humphrey Co.

Mr. Dunkel says that Advanced Telecommunications looks particularly attractive in light of the nearly 100% premium MCI will pay for Telecom, or a multiple of 26 times his estimate of Telecom's 1990 earnings.

Before yesterday's gain, Advanced Telecommunications was trading at just under 10 times Mr. Dunkel's estimated earnings for the company of $1.40 a share for fiscal 1991, ending in March. The company has a "very valuable" customer base in Florida, Georgia and Texas and will be one to keep an eye on amid continuing consolidation of regional long distance carriers, Mr. Dunkel says.

Several stocks that recently took a beating rebounded in robust trading. Among them were Novell, up 1 1/4 at 40, which tumbled 4 1/8 Friday after Lotus Development agreed to acquire the company for stock. Also recovering in brisk trading was Oracle Systems, which rose 3/4 to 16. Both stocks were "oversold" last week, but regained favor yesterday, said Jerry Newman, a San Francisco-based institutional salesman with Cowen & Co.

Also on the active list, 3Com, added 1/8 at 13 3/8 after announcing that it will buy back up to one million common shares, or about 3.5% of its outstanding common stock.

Michael Foods fell 1 1/4 to 13 7/8. Dain Bosworth lowered its 1990 and 1991 earnings estimates for the company because of its recently completed acquisition of the remaining 50% of Milton G. Waldbaum Co. that it didn't previously own, according to Dow Jones Professional Investor Report. Michael Foods said it doesn't expect the acquisition to dilute earnings.
