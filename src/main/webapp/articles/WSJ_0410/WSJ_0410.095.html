Business Brief -- Transcon Inc.: Truck-a-Way Inc. Group Has 5.2% Stake in Common

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/10/90

A group including Truck-a-Way Inc., a Vacaville, Calif., trucking concern, said it holds a 5.2% stake in Transcon Inc.'s common shares outstanding. In a filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission, the group said it holds 329,000 Transcon common shares, including 266,900 bought from March 22 to March 30 for 93.75 cents to $1.375 a share.

The group said it acquired its Transcon stock for investment purposes, but that its intentions are "subject to continual review and re-evaluation."

Other group members are James D. Burke, president of Truck-a-Way, and Andrew J. Collins Jr., an officer and director of Metropolitan Trust Co., a Sherwood, Ark., diversified real estate concern.

In Beverly Hills, Calif., Transcon officials couldn't be reached to comment on the Truck-a-Way stake. Transcon, a trucking company, earlier this month said it sold its Transcon Lines carrier to closely held, Miami-based Growth Finance Corp. The price wasn't disclosed. It said the unit in 1989 had revenue of $226.5 million and operating losses of $31.6 million.
