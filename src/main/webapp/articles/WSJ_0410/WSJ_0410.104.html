International: Ford Switches Investment To Germany From Britain

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 04/10/90

LONDON -- Ford Motor Co.'s British unit said it decided to reduce its proposed #725 million ($1.19 billion) investment in an engine plant in South Wales to #500 million. Ford said the outstanding #225 million will be diverted to the company's production plant in Cologne, West Germany. The company cited the "unreliability" of supplies from the United Kingdom engine plants and other factors as the reason for its decision to switch the investment.

As a result of the switch, the South Wales plant, which was initially expected to produce 850,000 of the company's new multivalve lean-burn engines, will now produce only 300,000 engines a year.

Ford's South Wales plant employs 14,000 hourly workers and produces 50,000 Escort, Orion and Fiesta car engines a year. The spokesman said jobs at the plant won't be put at risk due to the decision to switch the investment.

Commenting on Ford's proposal to create more than 3,000 new jobs from the investment plan, a company spokeswoman said only that "the position of those jobs is as yet unclear."

She repeated company claims, however, that the British plants affected may still be granted fresh production contracts if supply and productivity problems that have afflicted the unit can be ironed out.
