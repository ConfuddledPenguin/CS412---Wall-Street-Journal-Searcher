Allstate's Suit in New Jersey

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/10/90

PRINCETON, N.J. -- Allstate Insurance Co. filed a federal lawsuit challenging New Jersey's recently enacted auto insurance reform law. The suit says the law adopted in March deprives Allstate of a reasonable return by forcing insurers to cover part of $3 billion in losses incurred by the state's high-risk insurance pool. The reform law lowers premiums by 20% and abolishes the high-risk pool, known as the Joint Underwriters Association. Three other companies, Continental Corp. of New York, Liberty Mutual Insurance Group and Aetna Life & Casualty, have also sued to block the reform plan.
