Equimark Expects To Post Sizable Loss For the First Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/10/90

PITTSBURGH -- Equimark Corp. said it expects to report a first-quarter net loss of between $13.5 million and $15.5 million, or $1.21 a share and $1.38 a share. In the year-earlier quarter, Equimark earned $5.7 million, or 50 cents a share.

Separately, Equimark said it agreed to sell most of its credit-card receivables to Banc One Corp. in Columbus, Ohio. Terms weren't disclosed. The sale includes about $350 million in Equimark accounts. Equimark will keep about $113 million in noncredit-card revolving credit receivables.

Equimark indicated in January that it would sell the credit-card business because consolidation in that industry makes it increasingly difficult for smaller credit-card issuers to compete.

The diversified financial services company has been selling certain assets to help finance its federally assisted acquisition of ailing National Bancshares of Texas, based in San Antonio.

For the latest quarter, Equimark's projected loss is due to a "significant increase" in the company's provision for possible loan losses and to losses related to the sale or write-down of certain nonperforming real estate.

Equimark said its reserve for possible loan losses at March 31 is projected to be about $6 million higher than the year-end 1989 balance of about $42 million. The loan loss reserve is also projected to cover about 75% of the total nonearning assets at the end of the quarter, as compared with 63% at the end of 1989.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Equimark closed at $9.375, unchanged.
