Savin Expecting To Post a Profit For 1st Quarter

  • Author: Robert Daniels
  • Date: 04/10/90

STAMFORD, Conn. -- Savin Corp. expects to report a profit for the first quarter, compared with a year-earlier loss, as it began to benefit from placements of its Prism I color copier, said E.P. van den Boogaard, the copier marketer's new chairman. Savin also announced a long-term supply accord with Colorocs Corp., which designs the Prism I. And the company also is still accepting and counting votes from holders of common shares on four proposals. Savin had asked its shareholders to, among other things, give the company more flexibility when paying dividends and to let the company award certain options.

For the first quarter, Savin estimates pretax earnings of $400,000, compared with a year-ago loss of $1.1 million. Savin declined to disclose the after-tax number, but Mr. van den Boogaard said it would be printed in black ink. A year-earlier, Savin had a loss of $930,000, or three cents a share.

Savin estimated that revenue advanced 3.3% to $92 million from $89 million. The first quarter is usually Savin's weakest, said Mr. van den Boogaard, who took the top post late last month.

Mr. van den Boogaard, 46 years old, also is president and chief executive officer of HCS Technology N.V., an Amsterdam distributor of facsimile machines, copiers, and computer hardware and software. HCS recently boosted its voting stake in Savin to 55% by buying the Savin holding of Colorocs Corp. Mr. van den Boogaard took the chairman's post from Charles Muench, president and chief executive of Colorocs.

At a special meeting last month, Savin presented eight proposals to shareholders. Holders agreed to cut the par value on the common and four classes of preferred stock; to let Savin pay dividends on Series-A preferred in common stock; to increase authorized Series-C shares; and to make some changes in a stock-option plan.

The special meeting was postponed to March 29 and then to April 20 to allow common-share holders to continue to vote on proposals to let Savin pay Series-C dividends with more Series-C; to grant certain stock options; to grant an option on three million Series-C shares to HCS; and if this third proposal fails, to grant to HCS as much as $10 million of common or Series-C stock.

If HCS receives and exercises the option on the three million Series-C shares, it would invest as much as $21 million in Savin, increasing its voting stake to 63%.

The votes that are coming in still largely favor the proposals, Savin said, declining to provide specific numbers. The company has said that the initial failure of the four ballot measures, which require approval from two-thirds of the common shares, stemmed from the difficulty of getting its many holders to vote.
