Business Brief -- Mitsui Taiyo Kobe Bank: Bank, Jay Dickson Brown Set Financial Joint Venture

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/10/90

Mitsui Taiyo Kobe Bank said it agreed with Jay Dickson Brown & Co. to form a joint venture for making markets in swaps, options and other financial transactions. The venture will have initial capital of $2.3 million, two-thirds of which will be provided by the Japanese bank and the rest by Jay Dickson Brown, a closely held financial-services concern. Its total net worth, however, will be $20 million, a Mitsui spokesman said. The venture, to be based in New York, plans to open offices in Tokyo and London.

Swaps are financial and currency transactions in which, for example, fixed-rate claims and liabilities are exchanged for floating-rate claims and liabilities. Option trading is trading in the right to conduct a financial transaction. Options and swaps are both designed to avert risks related to fluctuations in interest or foreign-exchange rates.
