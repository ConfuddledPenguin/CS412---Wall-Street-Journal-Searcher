AT&T Set to Announce Memorex Computer Accord

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/10/90

NEW YORK -- American Telephone & Telegraph Co. is expected to announce today an agreement to transfer its computer terminal business to Memorex International N.V. in return for a minority stake in the computer company, according to individuals close to the negotiations. Under the agreement, AT&T will transfer its IBM compatible dumb terminal business to Memorex-Telex, Tulsa, Okla. In return, AT&T could receive 5% to 10% in the unit's parent, Memorex. These individuals also say AT&T will get a seat on Memorex's board.

Use of dumb terminals, which need to be hooked to powerful central computers to operate, has been slowing as corporate customers have begun to switch to personal computers linked to networks.

But the real attraction for AT&T may be Memorex's international presence. AT&T has been eager to boost its share of the international computer market where AT&T is almost invisible. And Memorex, which posted revenue of $2.24 billion for 1989, has extensive overseas distributions, and has 10,000 employees world-wide.

AT&T's computer business mostly involves minicomputers, personal computers, networking products and software. Terminals account for a small percentage of the company's business.
