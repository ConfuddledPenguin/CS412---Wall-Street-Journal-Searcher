New Securities Issues

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/10/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATES

Rohm & Haas Co.'s Rohm & Haas Holdings Ltd. -- $150 million issue of amortizing debentures, due April 15, 2020, priced at par to yield 9.80%. The noncallable debentures, priced at a spread of 1.25 percentage points above the Treasury's 30-year bond, have an average life of 20.5 years and are guaranteed by Rohm & Haas. Rated single-A-1 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and single-A by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through Goldman Sachs & Co.

MORTGAGES

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $300 million of real estate mortgage investment conduit securities in 23 classes through Nomura Securities International Inc. The issue, series 1990-52, is backed by Fannie Mae 10 1/2% securities.

EUROBONDS

World Bank (agency) -- 45 billion yen ($285.3 million) of 7 1/4% Eurobonds due April 27, 1995, priced at 101 3/8 to yield 7.31% less fees via IBJ International. Fees 1 5/8.

European Investment Bank (agency) -- 150 million Swiss francs ($100 million) of 7 1/2% privately placed notes due May 4, 2000, priced at 101 3/8 to yield 7.30% via J.P. Morgan Securities (Switzerland). Fees 2 1/8.

Council of Europe (agency) -- 10 billion yen ($63.4 million) of floating-rate notes due April 26, 2000, paying interest at 0.40 point below the long-term prime rate, semiannually, priced at par via Daiwa Europe Ltd. Call option in April 1991 at 100.20 and thereafter annually at par. Once-only put option after seven years at par. Fees 0.25.

Dai-Ichi Kangyo Australia Ltd. (Japanese parent) -- $50 million of Eurobonds due May 12, 2000, priced at 102 via DKB International. Bonds pay interest at 0.33 point above six-month London interbank offered rate for the first four years; thereafter, fixed interest rate of 10%. Once-only call option at par in 1994. Guaranteed by Dai-Ichi Kangyo Bank. Fees 2.

OTHER

Student Loan Marketing Association -- $500 million of floating-rate notes due Oct. 11, 1990, priced at par to yield a variable rate equal to .20 percentage point above the bond-equivalent yield of the three-month Treasury bill auction rate.
