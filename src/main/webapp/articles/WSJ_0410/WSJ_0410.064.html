OPEC Output Rose in March

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/10/90

MANAMA, Bahrain -- Crude oil production by the 13-member Organization of Petroleum Exporting Countries totaled 24,090,000 barrels a day in March, up from 23,620,000 barrels daily in February, according to estimates published by the Cyprus-based oil industry newsletter Middle East Economic Survey. The newsletter said the March output is marginally higher than last year's monthly peak of 24 million barrels a day, recorded in December, and represents the highest monthly OPEC production since 1981.

OPEC production averaged 23,660,000 barrels a day during the first quarter, roughly level with the 23.7 million barrels a day recorded during the final quarter of 1989, the newsletter said.
