Business Brief -- Outboard Marine Corp.: Firm Expects to Post Loss, Cites Softening in Demand

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/10/90

Outboard Marine Corp., citing softening demand "industrywide," said it expects to post a loss of as much as $5 million for its second quarter, ended March 31, on sharply lower sales. The Waukegan, Ill., maker of marine engines and recreational power boats said it anticipates a loss of from $1 million to $5 million, or five cents to 25 cents a share, on sales "as much as 25% below" the year-ago period's $403 million in sales from continuing operations. Net from continuing operations in the year-earlier period was $9.2 million, or 47 cents a share, Outboard Marine said.

In the year-ago second quarter, the company had net income of $11.9 million, or 62 cents a share, on sales of $474.9 million. Those figures included results from Outboard's Lawn-Boy and Cushman operations, both of which it sold in the latter part of 1989.
