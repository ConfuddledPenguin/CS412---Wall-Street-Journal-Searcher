McHaffie Inc., Owner And 2 Workers Plead Guilty in Fraud Case

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/10/90

LOS ANGELES -- McHaffie Inc., its owner, and two employees pleaded guilty to criminal charges involving a scheme to falsify test data on bolts the Sylmar, Calif., defense contractor made for use in various military jet-aircraft engines. The company and its 56-year-old owner, Norman McHaffie, pleaded guilty to conspiracy and two false-statement counts, the U.S. attorney's office said. James Hicks, 45, pleaded guilty to conspiracy and one false-statement count. William Whitham, 37, pleaded guilty to two false-statement counts.

The defendants admitted that they failed to test the bolts and submitted fraudulent certifications to the government, said an assistant U.S. attorney who prosecuted the case. The certifications indicated that the bolts had been tested and met all manufacturing standards and other specifications. The scheme continued during an eight-year period beginning in 1979.

The company faces a maximum fine of $1.5 million. Mr. McHaffie faces a maximum sentence of 15 years in prison and a $750,000 fine. Messrs. Hicks and Whitham, who cooperated with the government in its inquiry, each face a maximum sentence of 10 years in prison and a $500,000 fine.
