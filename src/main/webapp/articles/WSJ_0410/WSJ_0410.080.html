Who's News: Cox to Resume Work In Investment Banking With CS First Boston

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/10/90

NEW YORK -- CS First Boston Inc. said Archibald Cox Jr. would join the firm as a member of senior management after a two-year hiatus from investment banking. Mr. Cox resigned as a managing director and head of Morgan Stanley & Co.'s London unit in early 1988. He built the London operation to 1,000 people in 1987 from only 25 people when he began in 1977.

For the past two years, Mr. Cox, 49-year-old son of former Watergate prosecutor Archibald Cox, has been "dealing with some family business," and has had two operations on his right knee. His love of sports, particularly bicycling, also led him to acquire most of the stock of Serotta Sports Inc., Middle Grove, N.Y., a closely held maker of racing bicycles.

But now Mr. Cox says he is "anxious to get back into the swing of things." His new job has him coordinating CS First Boston's European operations with its Pacific unit and its First Boston Corp. unit in the U.S. CS First Boston is 44.5%-owned by the parent company of Credit Suisse, the giant Swiss bank.

CS First Boston President John M. Hennessy has been reshaping the firm by bringing in outside talent with established reputations. In January, he shook up the firm's bond-trading operations by hiring William J. Voute and four other former top officials of Salomon Brothers Inc. The Salomon officials, like Mr. Cox, had left their former firms two years ago.

Meanwhile, Mr. Hennessy's deputy William E. Mayer in January was demoted from First Boston chief executive to head a newly created merchant banking unit.
