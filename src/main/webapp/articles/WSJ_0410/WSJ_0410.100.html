Business Brief -- Aileen Inc.: Plans to Look for Buyers Of Business Are Abandoned

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/10/90

Aileen Inc., New York, said it abandoned plans to look for buyers of all or part of its business, because of improved prospects for growth and profitability. The women's apparel manufacturer and retailer said it may continue to dispose of plants, machinery and equipment that aren't necessary for its operations.

At its annual meeting, Abe Oberlin, chairman, said Aileen adopted a program to reduce overhead, simplify manufacturing operations and manage inventories more efficiently. He said Aileen had hired a merchandising manager, whom he didn't identify.

Mr. Oberlin said he anticipates a return to profitability in the fiscal second quarter, based on a 28% increase in retail sales for the first nine weeks of the quarter. For the fiscal first quarter ended Jan. 27, Aileen had a net loss of $1.2 million, or 22 cents a share, on sales of $10.6 million.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Aileen closed at $2 a share, up 12.5 cents.
