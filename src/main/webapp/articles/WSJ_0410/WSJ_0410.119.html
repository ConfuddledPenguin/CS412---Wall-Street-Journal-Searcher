Taiwan Oil Company Set to Buy Huffington

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/10/90

HOUSTON -- A longtime Texas oil company, Huffington Corp., agreed to be acquired by Taiwan's national oil company. Terms weren't disclosed, but an industry official familiar with Huffington's assets valued them at $700 million to $900 million. Huffington's primary asset is a 20% interest in an Indonesian joint venture, which supplies natural gas to one of the world's largest liquefied-natural-gas facilities.

Chinese Petroleum Corp., the Taiwan oil company, said it recently began receiving liquified-natural gas, or LNG, from the facility. The company said that under a sales accord with Indonesia's state oil company, Pertamina, it imports 1.5 million tons a year of LNG from that country.

Roy M. Huffington, founder of Huffington about 30 years ago, said in a statement that "this acquisition represents a very promising strategic opportunity" for Chinese Petroleum.

It is believed that a group from Japan, another a big importer of LNG, was interested in acquiring Huffington. The company put itself up for sale in November.

In addition to the energy assets, Huffington owns about 11 blocks in downtown Houston and 419 acres of undeveloped Houston real estate. Huffington sold its domestic oil and gas reserves in August 1988 to France's Elf Aquitaine.
