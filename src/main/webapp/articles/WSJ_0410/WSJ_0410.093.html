Business Brief -- Hong Kong Stock Exchange: Market to Drop a Proposal For Late Settlement Fines

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/10/90

The Hong Kong Stock Exchange said it will drop a proposal to fine brokerages that fail to settle securities trades within two days of their execution, even after a proposed automatic clearing and settlement system is put into place. Sir Q.W. Lee, the exchange's chairman, portrayed the decision as a compromise intended to still more than a year of debate over details of a settlement plan.

"We've talked at length about this and we're very pleased that it's been satisfactorily resolved," Sir Q.W. said.

While the exchange's governing council embraced a two-day settlement period in October, the plan drew criticism by foreign brokerages that argued that three days were needed to settle trades with overseas customers and would be in line with international standards.

In February, the exchange relented somewhat, saying that it was considering postponing a system of penalties until after the automatic system is in place, which is expected to be in the second half of 1991. But, with major brokerages still dissatisfied, the debate persisted.

The two-day settlement system is to replace a 24-hour settlement period that is routinely flouted without penalty.
