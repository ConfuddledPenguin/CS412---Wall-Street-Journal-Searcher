Boulle Group Files $195 Million Lawsuit Against Sunshine

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/10/90

DALLAS -- A French diamond-exploration company has filed a $195 million lawsuit against Sunshine Mining Corp., alleging breach of contract and failure to perform duties associated with a joint venture in a diamond mining project in Sierra Leone. The Boulle Group, a partnership based in Troyes, France, alleges that it began a relationship with the Sierra Leone government in 1971 and developed a diamond-mining prospect there. In 1989, the brothers Jean-Raymond and Franco Boulle signed a joint venture agreement with Sunshine Mining, a Dallas-based precious metals mining company, to develop the project.

In the lawsuit filed in federal court in Dallas, the Boulles allege that Sunshine officials withheld information, executed exclusive agreements and misappropriated funds in an attempt to take the project for themselves, excluding the Boulles. Sunshine also evicted the Boulles from the joint venture offices in Dallas, the brothers allege.

The brothers attempted to work out the problems with Sunshine to save the venture, said their attorney, Robert C. Odle Jr. of Weil, Gotshal & Manges in Washington, D.C. But when that attempt failed, they filed suit, asking the federal court to either dissolve the disputed joint venture or enjoin Sunshine from interfering in the project. The Boulles are also asking for $65 million in actual damages and $130 million in exemplary damages.

The two parties are still partners in the Arkansas Diamond Development Co., which has proposed a diamond exploration project in southwestern Arkansas. That project has been controversial because it would convert a publicly owned state park to private use.

Several officials from Sunshine Mining weren't available for comment, and others didn't return phone calls.
