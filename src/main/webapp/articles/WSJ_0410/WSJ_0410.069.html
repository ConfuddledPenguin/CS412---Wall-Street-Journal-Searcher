Hong Kong Exports Fall

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/10/90

HONG KONG -- Hong Kong's total exports contracted 2% in volume terms in January from a year earlier, pulled down by a contraction in domestic exports, according to government data released Monday. While re-exports grew 1% in January, exports of items produced in the colony fell 5%. Meanwhile, imports decreased 7%.

In value terms, domestic exports rose 2% in January from a year earlier, while re-exports climbed 5%. The price of imported goods rose 3%. Actual cash values weren't provided.
