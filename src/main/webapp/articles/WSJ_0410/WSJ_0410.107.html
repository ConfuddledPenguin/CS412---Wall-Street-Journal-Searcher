Thyssen's Budd Co. Unit Agrees to Pay $1.5 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/10/90

WASHINGTON -- Budd Co. agreed to pay $1.5 million to settle Labor Department charges of workplace noise and other safety and health violations at the company's Philadelphia automotive-panel plant. Budd also agreed to set up corporatewide worker-hearing conservation and record-keeping programs, the department's Occupational Safety and Health Administration said. Budd, based in Troy, Mich., and a unit of Thyssen AG in West Germany, agreed to the settlement without admitting the alleged violations.

OSHA last December proposed penalties totaling $3.3 million against Budd for the alleged Philadelphia violations. But as with many accords, the regulatory agency agreed to the smaller fine in return for a commitment for corporatewide abatement of the alleged unsafe practices.

At the time, the $3.3 million was the fourth-largest proposed penalty for a company in OSHA history. The agency had charged Budd with noise-rule violations, including failure to tell workers they had suffered hearing loss and to have doctors review problem cases; with violations of engineering-control regulations; and with willful violations of record-keeping rules, such as the required notation of medical treatment for injuries.

The agreement calls for Budd to reduce excessive noise at the Philadelphia plant by April 30, 1991, and to begin a hearing-conservation program at all its facilities by Sept. 1, 1990.

Budd also will audit injury and illness record-keeping since Jan. 1, 1987, to see that the records comply with federal requirements, and to keep future records in compliance, OSHA said.
