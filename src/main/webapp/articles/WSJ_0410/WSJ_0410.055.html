REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: Sub-Caucasus Caucus?

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/10/90

The future face of affirmative-action policy may be coming into focus in Pasadena, California. Southern California is home to tens of thousands of people of Armenian heritage, and a special Pasadena survey has determined that at least 6,850 of them reside in that town. The $98,000 poll was done because some in the local Armenian community, which includes a few highly placed city officials, suspected discrimination in the broader city work force. So Armenians, at 5.8% of the population, will be added to the traditional categories -- black, Hispanic, Asian -- in setting quota targets for hiring. It wouldn't appear, however, that this is a particularly disadvantaged class -- the same survey placed the average Armenian household income at $39,000. Also, one of the Armenian minority, George Deukmejian, is Governor of the nation's largest state.
