Business Brief -- General Development Corp.: Homesite-Development Unit Is Closed; 239 Are Laid Off

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/10/90

General Development Corp., which filed for bankruptcy-law protection Friday, said it closed its homesite-development division, laying off its 239 workers. The cash-short Miami developer, which wasn't able to pay more than $10 million in property taxes due April 1, said "until we get our reorganization plan approved, we can't spend any money on home-site construction." In recent months, the company has laid off or fired approximately 1,600 other workers, or more than one quarter of its work force.

General Development's troubles stem from a grand-jury investigation of its selling practices. Last month, the company pleaded guilty to a federal charge of conspiracy to defraud about 10,000 home buyers by charging inflated prices based on improper appraisals. The company's former top two executives also pleaded guilty to one count of conspiracy.

Separately, citing the Chapter 11 bankruptcy filing, Moody's Investors Service lowered its ratings on about $275 million of the company's senior subordinated and subordinated debt to Ca from Caa.
