France's Foreign Trade Data

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/21/90

PARIS -- France's seasonally adjusted foreign trade account for May showed a deficit of 5.68 billion francs ($1.01 billion) on a preliminary basis, after a revised April deficit of 4.38 billion francs, which was initially estimated at 4.31 billion francs, the National Customs Office said. The latest result was significantly worse than earlier forecasts by Paris market economists, who generally expected a deficit of 2.5 billion to three billion francs; the most pessimistic estimate had been for a gap of as much as 4.5 billion francs.

Unadjusted results for May showed a deficit of 8.32 billion francs, after a revised April deficit of 3.09 billion francs and a May 1989 deficit of 9.17 billion francs. The unadjusted April 1990 deficit previously was estimated at 3.03 billion francs.

The latest results brought France's adjusted trade deficit for the first five months of this year to 12.49 billion francs, a slight narrowing from the year-earlier deficit of 14.38 billion francs. This cumulative deficit was 19.68 billion francs on an unadjusted basis, compared with 22.49 billion francs a year earlier.
