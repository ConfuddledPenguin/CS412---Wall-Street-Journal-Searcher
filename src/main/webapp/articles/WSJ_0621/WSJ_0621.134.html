Business Brief -- Triton Energy Corp.: Unit Signs Conditional Pact For Field Sale to Conoco

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/21/90

Triton Energy Corp, Dallas, said its 59%-owned subsidiary, Triton Europe PLC, has signed a conditional agreement to sell its interests in the Alba oil field to Conoco U.K. Ltd. for $61 million. The sale is subject to the consent of the U.K.'s secretary of state for energy. Proceeds of the sale are to be used to retire $45 million in long-term debt held by Triton Europe, the company said. Assets to be sold consist of non-producing interests in 12 blocks in the North Sea, including the Alba oilfield.

The sale comes against a backdrop of renewed interest in the company by New Zealand financier Sir Ronald Brierly. Last week Industrial Equity (Pacific) Ltd., which is controlled by Sir Ronald, bought 357,249 Triton common shares and raised its stake to 11% from 9.3%.

Industrial Equity has informed the Securities and Exchange Commission that it may seek control of Triton, and has antitrust clearance to acquire up to 14.9% of the company.

Triton said it has not had any contact with Industrial Equity, but noted that Sir Ronald has been a long-term investor in Triton.
