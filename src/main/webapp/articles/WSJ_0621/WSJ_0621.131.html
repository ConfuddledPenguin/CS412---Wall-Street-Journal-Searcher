Clorox to Purchase Household Items From Cyanamid

  • Author: Ken Wells
  • Date: 06/21/90

OAKLAND, Calif. -- Clorox Co. is hoping to mop up on the mix of bleach, disinfectant and insecticides. The Oakland-based concern said it will acquire the household-products business of American Cyanamid Co. for $465 million. The purchase includes Cyanamid's well-known Pine Sol cleaner and Combat insecticide labels.

Clorox called the move a natural one that will allow it to boost its already formidable presence in the $900 million all-purpose cleanser market while providing it with a quick foothold in the household insecticide business.

The stock market seemed to agree. In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange yesterday, Clorox common closed at $43.875, up 37.5 cents.

American Cyanamid of Wayne, N.J., makes medical, agricultural and chemical products.

Clorox has been considering entering the insecticide market for a number of years but had been stymied by the slow, costly and risky effort required to bring a new insecticide to the sales counter. Combat, according to Clorox, is currently the No. 2 brand in the $450 million a year household insecticide market.

Clorox said it expects the Cyanamid brands to boost revenue about $235 million annually while adding significantly to its international sales. It plans to pay for the acquisition with about $400 million in short-term borrowings, with the balance in cash.

The company said debt and other costs associated with the purchase is expected to dilute earnings by 30 cents to 40 cents a share during the first year, and ten cents a share during the second year. By the second year, however, the deal, after payment of financing costs, is "expected to generate positive cash flow," the company said. For the fiscal nine months, ended March 31, Clorox had profit of $111.3 million, or $2.02 a share.

The company's existing household products brands include Clorox liquid bleach, Formula 409 spray cleaner and Liquid-plumr drain opener. Clorox said it expects to make significant cost savings by combining manufacturing and marketing of its current labels with its newly acquired Cyanamid brands. The purchase includes a Pine Sol and Combat manufacturing plant in Mississippi that has 200 employees.
