Texan Is Indicted In State Investigation Of Insurance Industry

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/21/90

AUSTIN, Texas -- A Texas grand jury has returned the first indictments in an investigation of the state's insurance industry, triggered by the $93.5 million collapse of two companies controlled by high-rolling Texan Robert H. Walker. Mr. Walker was charged with a combined 14 counts of theft, misapplication of funds and filing of false statements in his management of National County Mutual Fire Insurance Co. and of Walker General Agency, a primary insurance wholesaler. Both companies were placed in receivership in late 1988 after National County was declared insolvent.

Also indicted, on unrelated charges of commercial bribery and misapplication of funds, were John Gordon, Frances Gordon and W.H. "Pip" Lasiter, owners of Best Lloyds Insurance Co., which was declared insolvent and placed in receivership in April of 1988. An indictment charging misappropriation of more than $1 million in premiums also was returned against R.W. Brayton, an official with the State Board of Insurance from 1976 through 1979.

State insurance regulators allegedly ignored warnings about Mr. Walker's flamboyant lifestyle and use of company premiums for unrelated expenses for several years before National County collapsed.

According to former associates, Mr. Walker used income from his auto insurance companies to create a maze of companies in various industries. He then allegedly siphoned off cash from this empire to pay the $6,000-a-month mortgage on his house, buy a nine-seat helicopter with gold-plated door handles and a yacht in Monte Carlo, among other things.

An outraged state legislature responded to National County's demise by demanding a statewide investigation of the industry, and all three members of the State Board of Insurance were replaced. Steve McCleery, first assistant district attorney for Travis County, says the grand jury is reviewing at least 60 additional cases for possible fraud.

Mr. Walker's whereabouts, meanwhile, are unknown, although he is believed to be living in Europe. Mr. McCleery said a fugitive warrant will be issued and added that his office has sought extradition in the past.
