Business Brief -- Amax Inc.: Metal Concern Plans to Cut Corporate Work Force 40%

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/21/90

Amax Inc., continuing the decentralization and consolidation of its corporate structure, plans to cut about 40%, or 80 jobs, from its total corporate work force of 200 by year end. The company, which has interests in domestic energy and gold production as well as aluminum and manufactured aluminum products, plans to close its Greenwich, Conn., office by year end. Amax, based in New York, moved its executive offices there from Greenwich in 1988 and moved other jobs there earlier this year.

An Amax spokesman said some of the Greenwich employees would be transferred to New York. He said some functions would be assigned to operating units and some jobs would be eliminated.

The spokesman said the company expects some cost savings from the layoffs, but he didn't estimate them. He said he believed the appropriate write-offs would be taken this year.

Amax, which had 20,000 employees at the end of 1989, had a 56% drop in first quarter net income, to $50 million, or 57 cents a share, a result of the softening economy and sharply lower aluminum prices. Sales fell 5.3% to $927.9 million.
