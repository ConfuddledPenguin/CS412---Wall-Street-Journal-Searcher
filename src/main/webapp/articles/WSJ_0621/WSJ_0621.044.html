CBOT Options Proposals Win Approval From CFTC

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/21/90

WASHINGTON -- The Commodity Futures Trading Commission voted to approve Chicago Board of Trade proposals to trade options on the exchange's Tokyo stock price index-futures contract and its long-term Japanese government bond-futures contract. The option on the Tokyo stock price index, known as Topix, will give the holder the right to buy or sell Topix futures contracts at a specified strike price by a certain expiration date. Option premiums will be quoted in Japanese yen.

The CFTC also directed its staff to draft an interpretation clarifying regulatory treatment of the Brent oil 15-day contract. The interpretation is expected to clarify that the Brent oil contract is a forward contract and thus exempt from CFTC jurisdiction.

A forward contract is generally an agreement that is privately negotiated between two commercial parties and isn't required to be traded on a registered futures exchange.
