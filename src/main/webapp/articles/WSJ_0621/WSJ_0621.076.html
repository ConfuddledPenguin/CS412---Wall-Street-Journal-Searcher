Disney Boosts Offering Of Zero-Coupon Bonds

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/21/90

BURBANK, Calif. -- Walt Disney Co. boosted to $2.25 billion from $1.5 billion its unusual offering of zero-coupon bonds convertible into cash tied to the market price of Euro Disneyland S.C.A., a publicly held French company. The offering size was increased at the request of Merrill Lynch & Co., the issue underwriter, because of investor demand, Disney said. Even before the offering was increased, it was believed to be the largest convertible bond issue ever.

Gross proceeds are expected to be about $927 million, Disney said. In addition, the entertainment concern has granted an option to Merrill Lynch to acquire additional notes to cover overallotments, which, if fully exercised, would increase the offering to $2.59 billion, with gross proceeds of about $1.07 billion.

Disney has said the funds would be used for capital spending and to retire more expensive commercial paper outstanding. In January, Walt Disney announced a multibillion-dollar expansion of its theme parks and resorts in Florida and California over the next decade.

The bonds, called Liquid Yield Option Notes, pay a yield of 6% to maturity. They have been priced at $411.99, and will be exchangeable for cash equal to the U.S. dollar equivalent of the market value of 19.65 shares of Euro Disneyland common stock.

Euro Disneyland, a theme park and resort complex near Paris, is scheduled to open in 1992. The company is listed on stock exchanges in Paris, London and Brussels, and is 49% owned by Walt Disney Co. It closed at the equivalent of $18.23 on the Paris exchange.
