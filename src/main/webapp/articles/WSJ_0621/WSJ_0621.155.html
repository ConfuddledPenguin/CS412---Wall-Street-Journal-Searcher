Getty Petroleum Unit And Offical Indicted In Tax-Evasion Case

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/21/90

NEW YORK -- A federal grand jury indicted a gasoline-distribution unit of Getty Petroleum Corp. and one of the unit's executives for tax evasion and conspiracy. Getty Petroleum, a Jericho, N.Y., independent gasoline marketer, said Tuesday that the office of U.S. Attorney Andrew Maloney in Brooklyn had notified the company that it was seeking the indictment.

The four-count indictment names as defendants Getty Terminals Corp. and William Rea, manager of the unit's Supply and Trading department, primarily responsible for gasoline sales.

Also charged were John Pabone, president and owner of J&J Petroleum Inc., a Staten Island, N.Y., gasoline distributor; and John Quock, president and owner of Tun Yung Oil Corp. and Westwind Petroleum Inc., both New York gasoline distributors.

A separate indictment issued yesterday charges two brothers, Richard and Robert Kennon, with filing false excise-tax returns and conspiracy. The Kennons are owners and, respectively, president and vice president of three gasoline companies: Jenny Oil Corp., Pelham, N.Y., and Oil City Enterprises Inc. and Mount Vernon Energy Terminals Inc., both in Mount Vernon, N.Y.

The indictments are part of what the Justice Department called a continuing "multi-agency investigation into an estimated $1 billion annual nationwide loss" of federal gasoline and diesel excise taxes because of fraud in gasoline wholesaling.

Getty Terminals said it is "outraged" about the indictment, which charges it with evading $946,000 of taxes in 1985. The charges are "completely baseless," the company said, and it plans a vigorous defense for itself and Mr. Rea, who couldn't be reached for comment.

Messrs. Quock, Pabone and Kennon couldn't be reached.
