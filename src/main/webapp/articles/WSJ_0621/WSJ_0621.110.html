Marketing Brief -- Media General Inc.: Newsprint Recycling Mill Is Put on Auction Block

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/21/90

Media General Inc., Richmond, Va., said it put up for sale its Garden State Paper Co.'s newsprint recycling mill in Garfield, N.J. Media General said the planned sale is part of its strategy to concentrate on its communications properties, which include daily newspapers, network-affiliated broadcast television stations, and cable TV systems.

Media General hired Goldman, Sachs & Co. to handle the sale of the recycling mill; an asking price wasn't disclosed. The mill has 220,000-ton capacity and will be sold with related recycling centers and other waste-paper operations. A Garden State Paper official said that an oversupply of newsprint in the market has led to depressed prices for newsprint, but that recycled newsprint, which is in greater demand, is scarce and selling at premium prices.

Media General will retain its one-third ownership in a recycled-newsprint operation in Dublin, Ga., which it owns with Knight-Ridder Inc. and Cox Enterprises Inc.
