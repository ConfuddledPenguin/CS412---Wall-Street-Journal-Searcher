Letters to the Editor: Exercise It Is, Futility It's Not

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/21/90

I was dismayed by an article in your May 11 Medicine & Health special report, with the headline "Exercise in Futility," showing a person inside a wheel of fortune apparently getting a stress test. A test that is two-thirds accurate is far from perfect, but it is hardly an exercise in futility. In addition, you overlooked newer developments in testing for coronary artery disease. Let me give you examples of noninvasive, safe, painless tests that are as accurate as catheterization (about 91%) and cost less than half the amount charged for a catheterization. One is called a thallium-201 imaging, which actually measures blood flow through the muscle of the heart. Another test is a combination of echocardiogram or electrocardiogram with Dipyridamole, which is especially suitable for people with impaired mobility or low cardiac reserve.

John M. Tyler, M.D.

Department of Medicine
