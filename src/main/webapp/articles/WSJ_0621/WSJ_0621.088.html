Goldcorp Investments Reaches Agreement To Avoid Proxy Fights

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/21/90

TORONTO -- Goldcorp Investments Ltd. said it reached an agreement with two former directors of subsidiary companies that heads off a proxy battle over board representation. Robert McEwen, Goldcorp's president and chief executive officer, said the company agreed to review its relationship with CSA Management Ltd. and subsidiaries Dickenson Gold Mines Ltd. and Wharf Resources Ltd. "with a view to restructuring" Goldcorp.

CSA Management, a company controlled by Mr. McEwen's family, controls practically all the votes in Goldcorp through its holding of common shares. Goldcorp's Class A shareholders, with limited voting powers, are entitled to elect two of the eight Goldcorp directors.

James McCartney, a Toronto lawyer, and Robert Simon, senior vice president and treasurer of Brascan Ltd., have launched a proxy battle against Goldcorp management to gain seats on the board. Among other things, the two questioned the investment management fees paid by Goldcorp to CSA. Goldcorp's annual meeting is scheduled for Monday.

Mr. McEwen said Goldcorp also will consider enlarging its board to 10 members, to include an additional two directors to be elected by holders of the Class A, subordinate-voting shares. He said Messrs. McCartney and Simon would be nominated to these additional board seats.

Mr. McEwen said he expects a restructuring plan to be presented for shareholder approval later this year.
