Daisy Systems Reports Breakdown in Talks With Secured Creditor

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/21/90

BOULDER, Colo. -- Daisy Systems Corp. edged closer to bankruptcy court proceedings, acknowledging a breakdown in negotiations with bondholders and a secured creditor over its default on $118 million in debt. The company also said that Heller Financial, its secured lender, has notified Daisy that it will not extend a "standstill agreement" that ended June 15 and will not guarantee its purchase of workstations from Sun Microsystems Inc.

The announcement apparently clears the way for a June 26 court hearing on a petition for involuntary bankruptcy, filed by Daisy's bondholders May 30. The maker of computer-aided engineering stations has missed interest payments since November.

According to a company spokesman, Daisy's cash shortage is severe enough to prevent its customary purchase of Sun Microsystem workstations. As a result, domestic shipments of its products in the third quarter, ending June 30, "will be significantly less" than in the quarter ended March 31. The second quarter saw a slump in revenue to $33 million, from $47.2 million a year earlier.

Daisy's troubles began with its optimistic $200 million acquisition of Cadnetix Corp. at the end of 1988, but disappointing sales have left it unable to service its debt. Although the company spokesman said Daisy is seeking third-party funds, he acknowledged that company officials are not optimistic and that failure to find help soon means Daisy "will be unable to continue normal operations."
