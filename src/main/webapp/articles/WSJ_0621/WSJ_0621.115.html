Bell Atlantic Corp., U S West Set Venture With Czechoslovakia

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/21/90

PHILADELPHIA -- U S West Inc. and Bell Atlantic Corp. said they agreed to form a joint venture with the Czechoslovakian government to build a public data network in that country. The three parties together would invest about $45 million over 10 years to develop the country's first public switched packet data network, a U S West spokesman said.

The agreement would give the regional Bells a 49% stake in the joint venture and the Czech Ministry of Posts and Telecommunications a 51% ownership.

Construction is expected to begin in the next few months after the agreement is signed, and data network service initially connecting Prague, Bratislava and Brno is due to start in 1992. Czechoslovakia has a population of about 15.5 million people and about 2.2 million telephone lines.

Construction costs would be paid in hard currency, while wages would be covered with the local currency, U S West said.

The agreement is the second in a week for the two regional Bells, which were selected by the Czech government Friday to build and operate a cellular telephone network in that country.

A switched packet data network allows large amounts of information to be sent "more efficiently" than a steady data stream, U S West said.

"Data transmission over a high-quality network is important to the economy and development of a country," U S West said.
