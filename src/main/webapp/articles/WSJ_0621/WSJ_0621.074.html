Former Officer of Unit Of Enron Draws Term In Prison for Scheme

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 06/21/90

A federal judge this week sentenced the former president of a defunct unit of Enron Corp. to one year and one day in jail for his part in an oil trading scam that cost the company more than $140 million. Louis Borget pleaded guilty to charges of fraud and income tax violations stemming from accusations that he and other officials of the Enron Oil Corp. unit created sham trades and falsified documents for personal financial gain. The sentence was imposed by Judge Gerard Goettel in the federal court in White Plains, N.Y.

Enron declined comment on the matter, citing pending civil litigation against Mr. Borget. Enron Oil, an international oil trading unit, was discontinued in 1987.

In addition to the jail sentence, Mr. Borget received five years of probation and was ordered to pay $5.9 million restitution. He has 30 days to surrender.
