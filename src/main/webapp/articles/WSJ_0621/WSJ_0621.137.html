Business Brief -- Revco D.S. Inc.: Accords to Sell 46 Stores Set in Big Divestiture Plan

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/21/90

Revco D.S. Inc., Twinsburg, Ohio, said it agreed to sell 46 stores, most of them in Alabama, as part of a plan to divest itself of 712 stores in 17 states. Terms weren't disclosed. The sales agreements require approval by the bankruptcy court in Akron, Ohio. Revco, which is operating under bankruptcy-law protection from creditors, said it now has agreements to sell 485 stores. The company said the divestitures are being made so that it can concentrate on its 1,100 drugstores in 10 eastern states.

Revco said it agreed to sell six drugstores in Alabama, and one each in Columbus, Ga., and Starkville, Miss., to Big B Inc. of Bessemer, Ala., which also will buy pharmacy files and inventory of 20 other Revco stores in Alabama and one each in Madison, Fla., Columbus, Ga., and Waveland, Miss.

Revco said it will sell two stores in Alabama to Harco Drug Inc. of Northport, Ala., which also will purchase pharmacy files, inventory or fixtures of an additional 10 Revco stores in Alabama and one in Columbus, Miss.
