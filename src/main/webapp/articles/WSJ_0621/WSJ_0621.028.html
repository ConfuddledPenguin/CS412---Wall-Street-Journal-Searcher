Letters to the Editor: Hong Kong Resembles The Warsaw Ghetto

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/21/90

In regard to Lee Yee's June 4 editorial-page story ". . . But Meantime, Hong Kong Braces for a Fall": Mr. Lee realized there is an analogy to today's situation in Hong Kong, but it is a far darker analogy than a ship heading toward a volcanic isle. With adult children abandoning their elderly parents, lovers deserting each other in search of a partner with travel papers, a community rampant with desperate greed in order to buy oneself a way out, what the current frenzy in Hong Kong most closely resembles is the terror of the Jews in the Warsaw Ghetto. During World War II the Jews of the ghetto faced with resettlement in the East found themselves hopelessly trapped by an uncaring world. For the Chinese of Hong Kong, it looks as if "never again" has come again too soon.

David K. Moty

San Diego
