Corrections & Amplifications

  • Date: 06/21/90

A STORY on page B1 of yesterday's edition incorrectly reported that Distribution Warehouse & Service Corp. and REI Distributors were named in criminal complaints brought by an individual alleging the misuse of milk crates. Neither company was named in the complaints, and neither has been accused of any illegality. The complaints name an individual at each company, but not the company itself. (See: "Nobody's Crying Yet, but There Must Be Spilled Milk Somewhere" -- WSJ June 20, 1990)

---

TRINITY COMPUTING SYSTEMS Inc., a Houston-based maker of computer software for hospitals, was misidentified in Tuesday's edition in a story about capital-gains lobbying.

(See: "Politics & Policy: Backers of Capital-Gains Tax Cut Beat Bushes to Locate Recruits for Their Lobbying Army" -- WSJ June 19, 1990)

FLEXTRONICS Inc., an electronics manufacturer, said a fiscal fourth-quarter loss that it expects to report stemmed from costs associated with the sale or closure of certain U.S. operations. In Tuesday's edition, the cause of the loss was misstated.

(See: "Business Brief -- Flextronics Inc.: Disk-Drive Maker Expects to Post 4th-Quarter Loss" -- WSJ June 19, 1990)
