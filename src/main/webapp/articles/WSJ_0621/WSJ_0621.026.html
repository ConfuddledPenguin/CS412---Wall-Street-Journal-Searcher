Letters to the Editor: I've Seen the Future, And It Is Plastic

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/21/90

I couldn't help but laugh as I read about the difficulty of persuading the public to use debit cards and merchants to install point-of-sale terminals so they can be used (Law, May 9: "Cashless Society May Be Inching Toward Reality"). You see, the future is now in Iowa. Every ATM in the state is connected through a single central switching system, so it makes no difference whose card you've got when you want to use a point-of-sale terminal or just get some cash from an ATM.

Thanks to this universality, ATMs are everywhere and point-of-sale terminals are to be found in both large and small grocery stores. Need some cash? Just hit the nearest "money machine" or get that extra $10 or even $50 with your grocery purchase with no thought about which bank has the ATM or which is "your" bank.

It's easier than writing a check. No photo driver's license or other form of identification is required and the store's limit on cash back may be higher than for a check.

There is one slight catch, but it affects only "foreigners." The Iowa network is connected to Cirrus, so pack your Plus System card away as you cross the state line and bring plenty of cash with you unless you've also got a Cirrus Card.

Bruce L. Wilson

Des Moines, Iowa
