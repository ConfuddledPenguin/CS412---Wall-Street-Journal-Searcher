Manitoba, Newfoundland Introduce Meech Lake Bills

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/21/90

OTTAWA -- The Manitoba and Newfoundland governments introduced in their respective legislatures bills to ratify the proposed Meech Lake constitutional accord, but prospects for passage of the bills appeared doubtful. The Meech Lake accord, negotiated three years ago, is a package of constitutional amendments designed to meet the constitutional concerns of Quebec and to defuse the independence forces in the largely French-speaking province. The accord would recognize Quebec as a "distinct society" within Canada.

Manitoba and Newfoundland are the only provinces that haven't yet ratified the accord. If the agreement doesn't get their approval by Saturday, it will lapse.

The Manitoba government's efforts to get the ratification bill before its legislature were delayed for a week by a filibuster by the legislature's only aboriginal member, Elijah Harper. Most Canadian aboriginal groups oppose the amendments because they say they don't address their concerns over native rights.

Prime Minister Brian Mulroney said he will address the Newfoundland legislature today in an effort to win support for the Meech Lake accord. Many Newfoundland legislators have expressed opposition to the agreement.
