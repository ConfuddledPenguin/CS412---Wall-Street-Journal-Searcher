Fox Film Gets Settlement Over Rights to `Eve' Book

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/21/90

NEW YORK -- The woman whose life was the basis for the book and movie "The Three Faces of Eve," reached a settlement with Twentieth Century Fox Film Corp. over the movie rights to her life story. Under terms of the agreement, Fox acknowleged Christine Costner Sizemore's right to grant motion picture rights to her current book, "A Mind of My Own," her previous books and any future books she may write.

Mrs. Sizemore acknowledged that Fox holds and retains all rights to "The Three Faces of Eve." The 1957 movie was a major hit and won an Oscar for actress Joanne Woodward, who played "Eve" in the story.

Mrs. Sizemore, who had been treated for multiple-personality disorder, signed the rights away to Fox for $7,000 in 1956. She previously said she wasn't represented by a lawyer and "the personalities would've signed anything" her psychiatrist recommended. Mrs. Sizemore said she was cured in 1974 and has since become an advocate for the rights of the mentally ill.

Mrs. Sizemore, who lives in Florida, had sued in federal court in Manhattan last year, challenging Fox's claim that it held all the motion picture rights to her life story.

Mrs. Sizemore said in a statement she was pleased that the differences were resolved with Fox. Officials of Fox, which is owned by News Corp., couldn't be reached for comment.
