Business Brief -- Allegheny Ludlum Corp.: Steelmaker Accelerates Plans To Advance Casting Process

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/21/90

Allegheny Ludlum Corp., Pittsburgh, said it has accelerated its previously announced plans to build and operate a commercial prototype thin-strip direct-casting machine to produce stainless and carbon steel flat products. The machine will be designed and installed by the specialty steel producer's joint venture partner, Voest-Alpine Industrieanlagenbau Ges.m.b.H., of Linz, Austria. Voest-Alpine is a supplier of casting machines. Terms were not disclosed.

Allegheny Ludlum did not say where the plant housing the machine will be located but said construction will begin in the second quarter of 1991 and is expected to be completed in the second half of 1991.

The company said it expects the new process to advance state-of-the-art steelmaking significantly because it eliminates the need for conventional slag or ingot casting or hot strip rolling that currently is used by metal concerns. The new process, Allegheny Ludlum said, is expected to be commercially available in the mid-1990s.
