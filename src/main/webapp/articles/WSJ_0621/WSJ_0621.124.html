House Rules Panel Set Flag Bill Vote for Today

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/21/90

WASHINGTON -- The Democratic majority on the House Rules Committee sent the flag-desecration amendment to the House floor for a vote today despite particularly savage demands by Republicans to wait until next week. The GOP wanted to use the weekend to round up votes for the proposed constitutional amendment, but Speaker Thomas Foley (D., Wash.) pushed for bringing it to the floor quickly. The vote is expected to be close.

During debate on the floor, Rep. Gerald Solomon (R., N.Y.) accused Mr. Foley of "kowtowing to the ilk like the Communist Youth Brigade to allow them to trample and desecrate our American flag." His remarks were challenged as improper by Rep. Barbara Boxer (D., Calif.), and he was forced to withdraw them after a ruling by Speaker Pro Tempore Jolene Unsoeld (D., Wash.) that they violated House rules concerning proper debate.

The House Judiciary Committee voted 19-17 Tuesday to send the amendment to the floor without recommendation. The Rules Committee, 9-4, set the vote for today, waiving the normal three-day waiting period.

Speaker Foley said there was no reason to delay the measure, which he said had been "debated and discussed as much as any other legislative proposal of this Congress."
