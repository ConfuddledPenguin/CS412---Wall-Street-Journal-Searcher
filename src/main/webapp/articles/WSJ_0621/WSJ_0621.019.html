Technology: Ozone Session Seems Ready To Pass Global Protections

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/21/90

LONDON -- Delegates to a world environment conference appear poised to endorse a global ban by 2000 on chloroflourocarbons and many other ozone-destroying chemicals. As a ten-day meeting on protecting the world's ozone layer opened here yesterday, delegates from the U.S., Britain and other countries expressed confidence that they will reach agreement on tougher protective measures. Besides the global CFC elimination, the measures are expected to include creation of an international fund of at least $160 million to help developing countries convert their industries to non-CFC technologies.

The measures will take the form of amendments next week to an international accord negotiated in Montreal in 1987. The Montreal Protocol committed the world's developed countries and many developing nations to a 50% drop by mid-1998 in production and consumption of CFCs and several related chemicals. CFCs are used widely as refrigerants, industrial solvents, aerosols and foam-blowing agents. Scientific research has found the man-made gases are eating away the atmosphere's natural layer of ozone gas, which helps protect life on the planet from the sun's harmful ultraviolet rays.
