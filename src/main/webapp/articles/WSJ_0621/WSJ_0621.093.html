Wednesday's Markets: Report Kills Stock, Bond Rally Bids --- `Beige Book' Hints Rates Won't Drop; Dollar Up Slightly

  • Author: Christopher Winans
  • Date: 06/21/90

NEW YORK -- Stock and bond prices rose in the morning but fell in the afternoon on yet another shred of news that suggests the Federal Reserve isn't about to ease its grip on interest rates. While stocks managed to eke out a small gain because of a spurt of program buying that erased a 10-point deficit within minutes of the final bell, bonds finished lower. The same news that undercut those markets was a factor in pushing the dollar slightly higher against other major currencies.

The Dow Jones Industrial Average finished 1.74 points higher at 2895.30 in a third consecutive day of lethargic trading. Long-term U.S. Treasury bond prices fell nearly a half point, or $5 for each $1,000 of face amount, also in quiet trading.

Bonds headed lower after the noon release of the "Beige Book" -- a bimonthly compilation of regional Federal Reserve Bank economic reports that members of the Federal Open Market Committee will study before their next meeting, July 2 and 3.

The book contained no major surprises, but it is viewed as an indicator of the Fed governors' thinking. Investors focused on two elements in it that might reinforce the Fed's current reluctance to ease interest rates.

First, the report noted strong growth in capital-goods industries. Second, the opening summary suggests the rate of inflation isn't declining, though the regional reports that follow didn't offer much evidence to back up that conclusion, said Raymond W. Stone, managing director at Stone & McCarthy Research Associates, Princeton, N.J.

Also hurting bond prices was a large supply of new corporate and municipal bond issues. Dealers taking these issues into their inventories as they begin searching for buyers tend to sell government bonds to offset the carrying cost.

Stock investors followed bond prices until the late spate of buying. "Institutional participation remains light," said Thomas R. Walsh, head of equity trading at Nikko Securities International.

In major market action:

Stock prices edged higher on volume of 137.4 million shares on the New York Stock Exchange, where declining issues outnumbered advancers, 777 to 668.

Bond prices fell. The yield on the Treasury's benchmark 30-year bond rose to 8.51%.

The dollar rose. In late New York trading, the currency was quoted at 1.6785 marks and 153.93 yen, compared with 1.6738 marks and 153.55 yen Tuesday.
