Credit Rating: General Dynamics' Debt Rating Is Cut

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 06/21/90

Moody's Investors Service Inc. said it downgraded about $670 million of General Dynamics Corp. debt, citing a slowdown in military spending and potentially major cost overruns on a Navy aircraft contract. The downgrades are part of a continuing appraisal of aerospace and defense contractors by Moody's. "The prospects for cuts in the defense budget have triggered our concern for the industry as a whole and this company in particular," said Tassos Philipakkos, Moody vice president and aerospace analyst.

Moody's said it downgraded General Dynamics' senior debt to Baa-1 from A-2. The company's subordinated debt was lowered to Baa-2 from A-3, and its commercial paper was cut to Prime-2 from Prime-1. Ratings on the company's General Dynamics Credit Corp. unit also were lowered by like amounts.

Moody's announcement was made shortly before the end of trading. General Dynamics stock closed at $34.50, off 62.5 cents, in New York Stock Exchange composite trading.

The reductions for senior and subordinated debt are by two notches, a sharper cut than the one-notch reductions that Moody's made recently on McDonnell Douglas Corp. and Grumman Corp. securities. General Dynamics and McDonnell Douglas are based in St. Louis. Grumman is based in Bethpage, N.Y. Mr. Philipakkos said that both of General Dynamics' competitors had been more recently downgraded, while General Dynamics had remained at its peak rating.

Moody's said General Dynamics is heavily dependent on military contracts, citing particularly expected production cuts for the Army's M-1 tank and the Air Force's F-16 fighter.
