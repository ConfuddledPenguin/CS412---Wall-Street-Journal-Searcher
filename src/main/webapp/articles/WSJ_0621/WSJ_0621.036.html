Financing Business: Sturm, Ruger & Co.

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/21/90

STURM, RUGER & Co.'s secondary offering of 1,830,000 shares was priced at $25 a share and sold out. The New York-based firearms maker registered the offering with the Securities and Exchange Commission two weeks ago. On the day the offering was announced, the shares closed at $23.50; they have since rebounded, and closed in New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday at $25.625, up 25 cents, on volume of 543,400 shares. As expected, the stock was offered by four shareholders, who will receive all the proceeds; these holders include Chairman William B. Ruger Sr., who sold one million shares. The underwriters, led by Kidder, Peabody & Co., had an option on 274,500 additional shares to cover overallotments. In January, with its stock price around $28, Sturm Ruger registered 1,850,000 shares, with the offering to be underwritten by Drexel Burnham Lambert Inc. On March 8, with the shares at about $24.50, the company withdrew the offering, saying the stock price was too low.
