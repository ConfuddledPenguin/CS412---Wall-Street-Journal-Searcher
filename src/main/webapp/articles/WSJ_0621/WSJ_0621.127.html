RTC Now Accepting Bids For 14 Insolvent Thrifts

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/21/90

WASHINGTON -- The Resolution Trust Corp. said it's accepting bids for 14 of its biggest insolvent savings and loan associations, with total assets of $39.8 billion. The thrifts range from City Savings Bank of Bedminster, N.J., with assets of $8.5 billion, to Yorkridge-Calvert Federal Savings Bank, Baltimore, with $540 million in assets. Both S&Ls were hurt by declining values of real estate in the Northeast, the S&L cleanup agency said.

Selling the large thrifts is complicated and expensive for the government. Most sales so far have left the thrifts' bad investments in the hands of the RTC, so the buyers get huge cash infusions to compensate for those assets.

Other institutions up for bid in the latest round are: Empire of America, Buffalo, N.Y., with $8.17 billion in assets; Merabank, Phoenix, Ariz., $6.38 billion; Benjamin Franklin Federal Savings & Loan Association, Portland, Ore., $4.97 billion; Homeowners Savings Bank, Boston, $3.62 billion; Mercury Savings & Loan Association, Huntington Beach, Calif., $2.16 billion; Sooner Federal Savings Association, Tulsa, Okla., $1.43 billion; Great American Federal Savings & Loan Association, Oak Park, Ill., $1.01 billion.

Also, City Federal Savings & Loan Association, Birmingham, Ala., $493 million; Arlington Heights Savings Association of Illinois, $510 million; North Carolina Federal Savings & Loan Association, Charlotte, N.C., $628 million; Midwest Federal Savings Bank of Minot, N.D., $960 million; Capitol City Federal Savings Association, Austin, Texas, $409 million.
