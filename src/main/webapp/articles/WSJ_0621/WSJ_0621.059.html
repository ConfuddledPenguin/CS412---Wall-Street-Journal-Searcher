Ex-Vendor for Unit Of Southern Co. Files Guilty Plea in Court

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/21/90

ATLANTA -- A former vendor for a Southern Co. unit who made illegal political contributions on behalf of the electric utility pleaded guilty to filing a false corporate tax return. Fred Suttles Jr., president of Outdoor Media Inc., entered the guilty plea in federal court here and agreed to cooperate with a continuing federal investigation of the affair.

Outdoor Media, a Pensacola, Fla.-based outdoor advertising firm, rented billboard space to the Gulf Power Co. unit of Southern. According to court papers, Mr. Suttles directed his employees and several of his vendors to contribute $40,550 to more than a dozen state and local political candidates designated by Gulf; he then reimbursed the workers and vendors. The income-tax charge relates to his listing those reimbursements as legitimate business expenses.

Court papers filed by the Internal Revenue Service said Mr. Suttles agreed to the scheme to get Gulf advertising contracts. The papers said Jacob F. "Jake" Horton, a former senior vice president of Gulf, asked for the contributions. Mr. Horton was killed in April 1989 in a corporate plane crash, the cause of which has yet to be determined.

The scheme was intended to circumvent federal law, which prohibits utility holding companies, such as Southern, from making political contributions.

Mr. Suttles, who couldn't be reached for comment, faces up to three years in prison and a $250,000 fine. His plea isn't apt to affect Gulf Power, which itself pleaded guilty last fall to two felonies -- conspiracy to make illegal political contributions and conspiracy to defraud the U.S. of income taxes. Gulf Power paid a $500,000 fine.

However, an IRS probe of individuals involved in the scheme continues, and further charges are anticipated, those familiar with the investigation said.
