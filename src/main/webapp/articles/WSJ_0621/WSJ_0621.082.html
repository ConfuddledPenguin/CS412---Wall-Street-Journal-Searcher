Midlantic Expects To Post `Modest Loss' For Second Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/21/90

EDISON, N.J. -- Midlantic Corp., citing "significant" new reserves for possible loan losses, said it expects to report "a modest loss" for the second quarter. The bank holding company is one of the major New Jersey lenders to developer Donald Trump, who has been in a cash squeeze and is holding negotiations with his lenders. But Donald W. Ebbert Jr., senior vice president at Midlantic, said the expected second-quarter loss isn't connected to the Trump loans.

Despite the expected loss, Midlantic declared its quarterly dividend of 47 cents a share on common and said it expects "to maintain adequate coverage for non-performing assets."

A year earlier, net income was $61.6 million, or $1.57 a share on a fully diluted basis. For all of 1989, net was $206.3 million, or $5.27 a share.

Net plunged in this year's first quarter to $1.4 million, or one cent a share, from $59.5 million, or $1.53 a share, fully diluted, a year earlier.

In the first quarter, the company increased its loss reserves with a provision of $108 million. At the time, it cited "continuing concerns about current business conditions, a further decline in the real estate market and an increase in Midlantic's nonperforming loans."

Midlantic said the office of the Comptroller of the Currency is scheduled to examine the company next month "and the outcome of that review will be communicated to shareholders at the appropriate time."

In April, Moody's Investors Service Inc. downgraded the debt ratings of Midlantic and its units.

In national over-the-counter trading, Midlantic closed at $15.75, up 50 cents.
