West German Shipments Rise

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/29/90

FRANKFURT -- West Germany shipped 69% more goods to East Germany in April than a year earlier, according to the West German Federal Statistics Office. Goods with a record total value of 1.17 billion Deutsche marks ($695 million) were sent to East Germany in April, up 479 million marks from April 1989. The previous record for one month was December 1989, the month after the wall separating the two German countries was opened, when a total of 1.01 billion marks was sent to East Germany. In March, 848 million marks of goods were sent.

But data indicated that East Germany's economy continues to languish ahead of the monetary and economic union with West Germany, as East German shipments to West Germany dropped 2% to 564 million marks.
