Letters to the Editor: And Their Mothers Still Love Them

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/29/90

Lee Berton's April 17 article on bad names for auditors has caused quite a stir among the 35,000 members of the Institute of Internal Auditors. We appreciate the fact that Mr. Berton was intrigued enough by one of our publications to write an article on it. In fairness to your readers and my profession, I feel I must tell the rest of the story.

It is true that two pages of the 137-page report "Roles and Relationships in Internal Auditing" by Donna Wood and James A. Wilson discuss negative names for auditors in the context of the interpersonal dynamics of internal auditing. Just prior to those pages, however, is a section on positive names for auditors, such as good guys, helpers, team players, guardians and so forth. The negative ones are a lot easier to poke fun at, but the positive ones are far more accurate.

As with any profession, there are many jokes about auditors. Sometimes, less-than-kind names are used, but seldom other than in jest. Fortunately, today, internal auditors are respected members of the organizational team.

We appreciate your interest in our profession. We look forward to seeing more on internal auditors -- the good guys.

O. Jack McGill

President

IIA Research Foundation

Institute of Internal Auditors

Altamonte Springs, Fla.
