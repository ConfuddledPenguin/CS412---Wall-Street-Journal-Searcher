International: Two Tokyo Oil Firms Report Pretax Profit Fell for the Fiscal Year

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 05/29/90

TOKYO -- Mitsubishi Oil Co., an oil refiner and distributor associated with the Mitsubishi Group, and Nippon Oil Co., Japan's largest oil company, each reported lower profits in the fiscal year ended March 31. Unconsolidated pretax profit at Nippon Oil fell to 21.216 billion yen ($140.5 million) from 28.533 billion yen in a year earlier. At Mitsubishi Oil, pretax profit went to 11.522 billion yen from 17.320 billion yen.

Higher acquisition costs, along with increased administration and marketing costs, were behind both companies' lower earnings.

Net income at Nippon Oil was 13.777 billion yen, down 21% from 17.382 billion yen. Per-share net was 11.28 yen compared with 14.47 yen.

Sales rose 11% to 1.853 trillion yen from 1.667 trillion yen in the 1988 fiscal year. Cost of sales however, which includes oil acquisition costs, rose to 1.690 trillion yen from 1.498 trillion yen. Administrative and marketing costs were higher by 4.445 billion yen, at 162.252 billion yen from 157.807 billion yen.
