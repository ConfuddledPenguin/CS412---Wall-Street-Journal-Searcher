Marketing & Media: Anheuser-Busch Pulls Plug On LA, a Low-Alcohol Beer

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/29/90

ST. LOUIS -- LA, an Anheuser-Busch Cos. beer whose name was an abbreviation for "low alcohol," now also stands for "little acceptance." The brewer said it was turning off the brand's spigot after a disappointing six-year marketing effort. The first of several beers with about half the alcohol content of regular beers, LA was an attempt to slake the thirst of those who liked beer but wanted to reduce their alcohol intake.

But that consumer niche apparently is miniscule. Industry estimates put Anheuser's LA production last year at only about 20,000 barrels -- so small that the company didn't even mention the brand in its latest annual report.

Analysts said the beer market has evolved into an all-or-none situation, where drinkers either want regular beer or beer with no alcohol at all. To meet the latter niche, Anheuser recently introduced O'Doul's, a premium-priced non-alcoholic brew.
