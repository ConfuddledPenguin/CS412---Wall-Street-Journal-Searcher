Business Brief -- Texas Air Corp.: Court Rules Worker Group Entitled to Some Back Pay

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/29/90

Continental Airlines said a federal appeals court ruled a group of employees was entitled to back pay, but awarded them less than $1 million, not the $40 million the workers had sought. The Fifth U.S. Circuit Court of Appeals in New Orleans ruled that the employees could get back pay only for the six days between the time Continental filed for bankruptcy in 1983 and when the workers struck the carrier, the carrier said. Continental, a unit of Texas Air Corp., filed for bankruptcy protection Sept. 23, 1983, unilaterally abrogating labor contracts and offering lower wages. The unions struck the carrier Oct. 1. Continental emerged from Chapter 11 protection in 1986.

A group of 474 pilots and flight attendants claimed they were owed several years of back pay, the amount they contended they would have received if the bankruptcy court hadn't approved the abrogation of their union contract. The court of appeals agreed the employees were entitled to back pay, but denied damages for the time they were on strike.

Continental said it was pleased with the ruling. Representatives of the employees couldn't be reached.
