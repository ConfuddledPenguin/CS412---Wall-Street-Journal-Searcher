Letters to the Editor: UAL's Performance Anything but Stagnant

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/29/90

Your April 9 page-one story on the unions' bid for UAL claims that "United has stagnated." This ignores what has been happening at our company over the past two years. Last year, UAL produced the second-best earnings in its history, and it placed two aircraft orders worth $19 billion. One order was the single-largest aircraft order in commercial-aviation history.

Early this year, United announced that, based on Department of Transportation figures, it has surpassed both Japan Airlines and Northwest as the No. 1 trans-Pacific carrier.

This hardly sounds like "stagnation." And there is more: United is entering the trans-Atlantic market for the first time, and beginning service to Mexico City from three U.S. gateways.

In short, the facts and record of our performance during the past two years speak for themselves.

Lawrence M. Nagin

Senior Vice President

Corporate and External Affairs

United Airlines

Chicago
