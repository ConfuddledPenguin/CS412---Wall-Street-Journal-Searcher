From an editorial on drugs in First Things, a monthly published by the Institute on Religion and Public Life:

Among the chic it is now much less chic to do drugs. Those at the bottom of society, however, the structure of whose lives were most fragile to begin with, made the mistake of believing their social betters. For them, "alternative lifestyle" turned out to mean the alternative to sustainable life. It is nice today to hear celebrities on talk shows affirm the importance of sobriety, family, and even marital fidelity. One hopes they remember the line they were chattering a few years ago. If one wants today to see the continuing social consequences of that liberationist line, one has only to visit Bedford-Stuyvesant, South Chicago, or the crack babies in the preemie wards of our city hospitals.

From an interview on March 20 with Richard Solomon, U.S. assistant secretary of state for East Asian and Pacific Affairs:

In Northeast Asia there has been no diminution of the Soviet threat. While the numbers may have been reduced somewhat, the deployment of more advanced equipment, aircraft, ships in particular, has kept the firepower, the mobility, the effectiveness of the Soviet challenge in Northeast Asia pretty much where it is. In Southeast Asia there has been a reduction in the Soviet military presence in Vietnam.

But the stability of these changes in the Soviet Union that have made these dramatic developments possible is not at all assured. And, more to the point in terms of our security relationship with the Philippines, those facilities have never been exclusively oriented toward the Soviet Union.
