Letters to the Editor: Listen Up, Guys]

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/29/90

"Is It Fair," asks Jolie Solomon in her April 20 article, "to Tailor Perks Just for Women?" The answer "Yes]" should be obvious, but the phrasing of the question tends to muddy the issue by suggesting that men and women are just two more special-interest groups with conflicting goals. Men who think this way could become much wiser by literally pondering their navels.

Male executives might approach the issue differently if it were put like this: "Should corporate benefits be structured in such a fashion that your wife, sister or daughter has career opportunities that approximate your brother's, your son's or your own?"

Now, can we talk about parental leave for men?

Mark E. Dixon

Wayne, Pa.
