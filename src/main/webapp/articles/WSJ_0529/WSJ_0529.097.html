Grossman Inc. Says Shareholder Group Seeks to Oust Board

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/29/90

BRAINTREE, Mass. -- Grossman's Inc. said a shareholder group has indicated its intent to solicit shareholder consent to unseat the company's directors. The action comes one month after Grossman's nine directors were re-elected to the board at the company's annual meeting.

In a filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission announcing the proxy contest, the dissident group indicated disastifaction with the board's decision to reject a recent financial restructuring plan it had proposed.

The proposal, made last month, included an $85 million capital infusion through the sale and leaseback by the building materials retailer of 81 properties. Grossman would have the option of buying back all the properties in five years.

In a May 21 letter Grossman's rejected the proposal saying it wasn't in the best interest of shareholders, Stanley G. Berman, executive vice president of administration at Grossman's said.

The shareholder group, led by Hawaii-based real estate investor Jay H. Shidler and Robert W. Holman Jr., a San Francisco investor, holds approximately 21.6 million shares of Grossman's stock outstanding, or 4.7% of the company.

The SEC filing also disclosed that the Holdman-Shidler group offered to acquire Grossman's last year for $10.25 a share but withdrew the proposal after Grossman's agreed to sell its Moore division.

The division, consisting of 59 stores, was sold last September for approximately $85 million to a U.S. unit of Harrisons & Crossfield PLC of London.

A month later the partnership again offered to acquire 83% of Grossman's for $9 a share in cash and the remainder for preferred stock. Grossman's didn't respond, according to federal filings.

The partnership continued to express interest in the company through last March. Instead the Holman-Shidler group said it has decided to solicit shareholder consent to remove the board. Because technically this is a consent solicitation and not a proxy contest an election can be held before the board's next annual meeting. The group said in its SEC filings that Mr. Holman and Mr. Shidler would serve as nominees for election as directors but added that it hasn't determined who the other candidates will be.
