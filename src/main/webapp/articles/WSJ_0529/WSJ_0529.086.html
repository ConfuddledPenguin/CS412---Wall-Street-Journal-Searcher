International Brief -- Mannesmann AG: First-Quarter Profit Helped By Increase in Sales of 5%

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/29/90

Mannesmann AG said its group profit in the first quarter "was at the good level of the year earlier," as sales climbed 5%. But the diversified West German steel and engineering company didn't provide specific first-quarter earnings figures.

Mannesmann recently said last year's group profit had soared 73% to 505 million marks ($300 million) on sales of 22.3 billion marks ($13.3 billion), a 9% increase.

The company said that the "favorable conditions" continued in the first quarter for most divisions. It said that gains in its machinery-making and plant-equipment divisions, along with vehicle technology, more than compensated for declines in other groups.

Mannesmann is benefiting from the inflow of business from European manufacturers who are retooling their plants in advance of the border-free Europe after 1992. Also, business is being boosted by companies that are trying to expand capacity to take advantage of the opening of Eastern Europe.
