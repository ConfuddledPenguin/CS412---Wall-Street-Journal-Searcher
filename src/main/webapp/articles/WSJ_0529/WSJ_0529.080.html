Who's News: Bairnco's Fichthorn Is Elected Chairman

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/29/90

NEW YORK -- Bairnco Corp.'s board elected Luke Fichthorn as chairman, as expected. Mr. Fichthorn, 48 years old, succeeds Glenn Bailey, the Bairnco founder who announced in January his intention to retire. Mr. Bailey, 65, was also president of the holding company. But a Bairnco spokesman said Friday he didn't know, and couldn't find out, whether Mr. Bailey still holds that position, which is Bairnco's top executive post.

Bairnco said in January that Richard Shantz, 54, will succeed Mr. Bailey as president at the completion of the company's proposed spinoff to shareholders of its Keene Corp. unit. The spokesman said that completion is imminent, and will occur before the end of the second quarter.

Messrs. Bailey, Fichthorn and Shantz couldn't be reached for a clarification of their positions.

Mr. Fichthorn is a financial adviser, and has served on Bairnco's board since the holding company was formed in 1982. Mr. Shantz is retired chairman and president of Kaydon Corp., a bearings maker that in 1984 was Bairnco's first spinoff.

Keene, which Mr. Bailey started in 1962, initially was Bairnco's predecessor; it became a subsidiary when Bairnco was established. Keene makes heat-shielding devices for aerospace, defense and commercial markets, and manages a $130 million investment portfolio.

At the annual meeting, held in Orlando, Fla., the site of its new headquarters, shareholders elected a new director, Charles Foley, 51, to fill Mr. Bailey's vacated seat on the board. Mr. Foley is president of Estabrook Capital Management Co., a New York investment advisory firm that is beneficial owner of 6.7% of Bairnco's stock.

But the company adjourned the meeting until June 7 to permit further solicitation of proxies on three matters: the reincorporation of Bairnco in Delaware; a new stock incentive plan; and a shareholder proposal on shareholder ratification of auditors.

The Bairnco spokesman said many shareholders whose stock is held in street name didn't receive their proxies in time to vote at the meeting.
