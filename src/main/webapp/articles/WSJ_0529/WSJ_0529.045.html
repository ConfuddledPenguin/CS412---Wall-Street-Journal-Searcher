Economy: Personal Income And Spending Rose In Latest Month

  • Author: Katherine Walsh
  • Date: 05/29/90

WASHINGTON -- Consumers continued to reflect optimism last month despite an increasingly cloudy economic picture. Slowed by a decline in subsidy payments to farmers, personal income grew a moderate 0.3% in April, the Commerce Department reported, the smallest increase since a 0.2% rise in September 1989. But personal spending rose 0.6% last month, after rising 0.5% in March.

Excluding farm subsidies, personal income increased 0.5% in both April and March.

Adjusted for inflation, spending rose 0.3% in April, following a revised decline of 0.1% in March.

Many analysts predicted consumer spending will continue to grow, particularly in the service sector, while the durable-goods sector will be constrained by declining sales of automobiles and household appliances.

"There will continue to be growth in the consumption sector, which is going to be crucial to holding this economy together in 1990," said Kenneth Mayland, chief economist at Society National Bank in Cleveland.

Many economists agreed the department's report presents an optimistic picture compared with the statistics released during the first three weeks of the month, which suggested the economy was quickly weakening. "It's the first report in two weeks on any economic data that was not considerably weaker than expected," said Joel Prakken, vice president of Laurence H. Meyer & Associates Ltd. in St. Louis.

However, other analysts believe the economy isn't growing stronger. The government's economic indicators suggest slow economic growth, but the government keeps revising them downward, suggesting the country is headed for a mild recession, said David Bostian Jr., chief economist at Jesup & Lamont Inc. in New York.

"The economy has had a false dawn of recovery and is in the process of slowing down again," said John Mueller, chief economist at Bell Mueller Cannon Inc., an economic forecasting firm in Arlington, Va. "We think the weakness in the economy is real and will continue for the rest of this year."

Consumers saved 5.5% of their after-tax income in April, down from 5.8% in March. The savings rate in February was 5.5%.

The figures are adjusted for seasonal variations.
