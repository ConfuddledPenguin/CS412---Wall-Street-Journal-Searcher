Triton Energy Stake Totaling 9.3% Is Held By Brierley Concern

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/29/90

DALLAS -- A company controlled by New Zealand financier Sir Ronald Brierley said it now owns a 9.3% stake in Triton Energy Corp. and may seek federal clearance to acquire 50% or more of the energy concern. In a Securities and Exchange Commission filing, Industrial Equity (Pacific) Ltd. said it has clearance under federal antitrust laws to acquire as much as 14.9% of Triton's common shares outstanding.

To get the most from its investment, Industrial Equity also said it may seek to exercise a controlling influence over Triton's affairs, pursue a seat on its board or make a tender offer for some or all of Triton's shares.

A spokesman for Triton, which has extensive overseas oil and gas holdings, said it is unsure of Industrial Equity's intentions. The Hong Kong-based investment firm is a vehicle for U.S. and British investments by Mr. Brierley.

"All we know is what has been on the financial wires," the spokesman said. "They've previously held the stock for long-term value. There have been rumors over the years of takeover potential," but he said Mr. Brierley and the company have developed a "close relationship."

In its filing, Industrial Equity said it believes current trading prices for Triton shares fail to reflect the value of the company's underlying business and assets. Triton's stock price, which traded about $15 a share in March, had fallen to below $11 recently. It jumped $1.125 Friday in New York Stock Exchange composite trading to close at $11.625.

In recent months, Industrial Equity has tangled with Cummins Engine Co. over shareholder rights issues and sought clearance under antitrust laws to raise its stake in Petroleum Helicopters Inc. to 25%.

Industrial Equity said it holds almost two million of Triton's 21.2 million shares. That includes 1.3 million shares bought between April 30 and May 22 at between $10.13 and $10.76 a share.

Triton has operations in France, Britain and the North Sea. For the nine months ended Feb. 28, it had a net loss of $19.6 million, after a $5.6 million write-down of assets, on revenue of $192.5 million.
