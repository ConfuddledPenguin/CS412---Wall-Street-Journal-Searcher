Iran to Direct $5 Billion In Currency Credits Into Its Oil Industry

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 05/29/90

NICOSIA, Cyprus -- Iran will direct $5 billion in currency credits to its oil industry this year, an amount unprecedented in the country's history, Oil Minister Gholamreza Aqazadeh said Friday, according to Tehran Radio monitored here. The credits comprise about 6% of the country's $80 billion budget for the current Iranian year, which began March 21.

The massive investments underline Iran's commitment to rebuild and update its oil industry, which was badly battered in the eight-year war with Iraq.

Oil and gas-related industries, which earn more than 90% of Iran's foreign exchange, were deprived of funds for maintenance and development as funds were channeled to the war effort.

But Tehran began rebuilding immediately after the United Nations-sponsored cease-fire halted the conflict on Aug. 20, 1988.

Inaugurating a methanol plant in the southern Iranian province of Shiraz Friday, Mr. Aqazadeh said Iran's current sustainable production capacity is 3.3 million barrels a day. Mr. Aqazadeh said it will be boosted to 3.7 million barrels a day in four months time and four million barrels a day next year.
