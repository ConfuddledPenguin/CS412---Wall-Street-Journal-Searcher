Business Brief -- First Executive Corp.: Firm's Board of Directors Rejects Rosewood Proposal

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/29/90

First Executive Corp. said its board of directors rejected Rosewood Financial Inc.'s proposal that a Rosewood nominee be elected to the troubled insurance concern's board. Los Angeles-based First Executive said that, in light of Rosewood's actions as a stockholder in First Executive, the proposal wouldn't be "in the best interests of the company, its policyholders and stockholders."

Officials of Rosewood Financial, whose previous efforts to take over troubled First Executive failed, couldn't be reached for comment over the holiday weekend.

The unit of Dallas-based Rosewood Corp., which is owned by the trust estate of oil heiress Carolyn Rose Hunt, had proposed earlier this month that First Executive expand its board by one seat and appoint Donald F. Craib Jr., former chairman and chief executive of All State Insurance Group, to the position.
