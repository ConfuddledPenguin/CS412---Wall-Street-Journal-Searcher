Marketing & Media: Reed's Cahners Unit To Buy Sail Magazine From Meredith Corp.

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/29/90

NEW YORK -- Cahners Publishing Co. agreed to acquire Sail, the leading sailing magazine in the U.S., from Meredith Corp., continuing its push into consumer magazine publishing. The price wasn't disclosed. Cahners, the U.S. subsidiary of London-based Reed International PLC, said Sail will join its Marine Publishing Group, which includes Power & Motoryacht, MotorBoat, and Motoryacht International.

Sail was an odd fit for Des Moines, Iowa-based Meredith, which publishes service-oriented magazines such as Better Homes and Gardens, Ladies' Home Journal, Country Home, and Metropolitan Home. Sail had a paid circulation of 163,000 in December 1989, according to the Audit Bureau of Circulations.

But the sailing magazine may mesh well with Cahners's other publications. "We have had pure power (boat) books, and now we are adding a pure sail book," Neal Vitale, a Cahners vice president, said, adding that the company will look for other acquisitions in the boating field.

Sail competes in a growing field of power boat and sailing magazines owned by Diamandis Communications Inc., New York Times Co. Magazine Group, and Times Mirror Co. Recently, Times Mirror Magazines acquired two marine magazines, The Yacht and Tournament Digest, which covers the salt-water fishing tournament circuit.

Cahners's mainstay still is business magazines, with 65 titles. But with acquisitions such as American Baby and Modern Bride, Cahners now has 11 consumer titles.
