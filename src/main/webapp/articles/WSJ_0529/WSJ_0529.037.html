U.S. Trade Deficit Shrank During the First Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/29/90

WASHINGTON -- The U.S. merchandise trade deficit shrank to $26.37 billion in the first quarter, on a balance-of-payments basis, the smallest gap since the fourth quarter of 1983, the Commerce Department reported. Both exports and imports set records. The department said exports grew 4.7%, to $96.04 billion, while imports, boosted by the highest volume of oil in more than 12 years, rose 1.6% to $122.42 billion.

In the fourth quarter, the deficit was $28.75 billion, a slight revision from the department's initial calculation of $28.81 billion for the period.

The quarterly balance-of-payments report differs from the government's monthly merchandise trade numbers by excluding military trade and making certain adjustments for timing, insurance coverage and valuation. The monthly trade reports are followed more closely than the quarterly numbers, but generally both show the same trends.
