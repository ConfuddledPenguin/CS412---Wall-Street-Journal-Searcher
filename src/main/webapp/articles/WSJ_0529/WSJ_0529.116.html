Business Brief -- Petro-Canada Inc.: Alberta Oil Refinery Is Shut In Wake of an Explosion

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/29/90

Petro-Canada Inc. said its 120,000-barrel-a-day oil refinery near Edmonton, Alberta, will be closed indefinitely after an explosion late Thursday killed one worker and injured two others. A Petro-Canada spokeswoman said in Calgary that the cause of the blast wasn't immediately known and that an investigation is under way.

The Edmonton plant accounts for about one-third of Petro-Canada's refined crude output. The state-owned company, which the Canadian government is preparing to take public, is Canada's second-largest oil refiner, after Toronto-based Imperial Oil Ltd., 70%-owned by Exxon Corp.

The Edmonton refinery had been closed for about five weeks for routine maintenance prior to the explosion, and was scheduled to resume production shortly.
