U.S. Newsprint Use Fell 2.7% Last Month

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/29/90

WASHINGTON -- U.S. newsprint consumption decreased by 2.7% to 1,022,172 metric tons last month from 1,050,918 metric tons in April 1989, according to preliminary estimates by the American Newspaper Publishers Association. The industry group said U.S. daily newspapers used 769,987 metric tons of newsprint in April, down 2.7% from 791,234 metric tons in April 1989. In the first four months of 1990, daily newspapers' consumption rose 0.3% from a year earlier. A metric ton equals 2,204.62 pounds.

Total estimated U.S. newsprint consumption by all users was 3,978,517 metric tons in the first four months of 1990, a marginal decrease from 3,983,339 metric tons in the year earlier.

Newsprint inventories for newspapers in the association's statistical sample averaged a 38-day supply, compared with a 41-day supply in April 1989. Stocks of North American mills decreased about 18.1% from April 1989.

Canadian newsprint mills operated at 93% of rated capacity in April and 94% for the year to date. U.S. mills operated at 97.4% and 97.2%, respectively.
