Vishay Tops Offer Made by TT Group For Crystalate

  • Author: Frank Allen
  • Date: 05/29/90

MALVERN, Pa. -- Vishay Intertechnology Inc. made a friendly tender offer valued at about $52 million to acquire Crystalate Holdings PLC, topping a hostile offer of about $47 million by TT Group PLC. Crystalate, a U.K.-based maker of resistors and automotive sensors, issued a brief statement advising its shareholders to take no action for the time being.

In the statement, Crystalate said its board believes there would be "considerable logic" in combining its operations with Vishay's. But the company also indicated that it wants a higher offer.

Vishay's chairman and chief executive officer, Felix Zandman, said he held talks last week with Crystalate officials, who had originally asked Vishay in April to make a competing bid for the company. He didn't indicate whether Vishay would be willing to increase its offer.

Mr. Zandman said Salomon Brothers International Ltd. is making the tender offer on behalf of Vishay, a maker of resistors, sensors and strain gages. The bid isn't being made to any U.S. citizen or to anyone residing in the U.S., he said.

Terms call for Crystalate holders to receive 80 pence in cash for each of the 32 million ordinary shares outstanding and 55 pence for each 5.6% cumulative preference share.

Instead of cash, holders may elect to receive one Vishay common share for every 14 Crystalate ordinary shares. However, the option of exchanging stock is limited to 1.5 million newly issued Vishay common shares. The stock swap would cover about 60% of the proposed transaction, Mr. Zandman said. Vishay has about 12 million shares outstanding.

The proposed merger, if successful, would strengthen Vishay's operating base in Europe and would boost significantly the company's market share in the U.S.

Vishay supplies Crystalate with resistor chips and other products for distribution and resale. In addition, two Crystalate units have a combined 10.4% share of the U.S. market for fixed resistors; Vishay already has a 33% share of that market. For the year ended Sept. 30, 1989, Crystalate had sales of about $180 million.

TT Group officials were unavailable to comment on the Vishay bid.
