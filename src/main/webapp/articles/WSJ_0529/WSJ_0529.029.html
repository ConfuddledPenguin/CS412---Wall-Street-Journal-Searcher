Japanese Retail Stores' Sales

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/29/90

TOKYO -- Japan's major retail stores reported sales of 1.651 trillion yen ($10.94 billion) in April, a 16% rise from a year earlier when the introduction of a 3% consumption tax curbed sales, the Ministry of International Trade and Industry said. Japanese consumer demand has been supported by growing income as the nation's economy continues to expand. In March, however, retail sales fell 2%, the first annual decline in more than five years. The ministry attributed the drop to high year-earlier sales as consumers rushed to buy before retail stores began raising prices to include the 3% tax.
