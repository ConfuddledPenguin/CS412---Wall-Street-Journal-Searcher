Business Brief -- American Telephone & Telegraph: Watertown, Mass., Facility Is Targeted to Be Closed

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/29/90

American Telephone & Telegraph Co. said it plans to close its Watertown, Mass., service center by late summer and consolidate the work in other service centers in Kansas and North Carolina. AT&T said the service center has experienced a steady decline in operations over the past six years, leading to a smaller work force. About 235 people are employed at the facility, compared with 750 when the Bell System was broken up in 1984.

A spokesman said the center specialized in repair of older, analog transmission equipment that is being replaced by digital equipment. The company said employees affected by the closing will have the option to accept early retirement, retraining, reassignment and transfer to other AT&T locations.
