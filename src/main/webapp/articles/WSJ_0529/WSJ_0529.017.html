Letters to the Editor: I Never Denigrated Laventhol's Standards

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/29/90

In your article "Laventhol & Horwath, Beset by Litigation, Runs Into Hard Times" (page one, May 17), you report that I was upset with Laventhol & Horwath's professional standards, and quote me as saying, "The urge to take such risks there was too ferocious. The dog-eat-dog atmosphere and knowledge that if you weren't willing to cut a corner, someone else would be found there who will, was too much for me." My words were a description of the pressures facing the entire public-accounting profession, and not just Laventhol alone. What I said was, "The urge to take such risks was ferocious. The dog-eat-dog atmosphere and knowledge that if you weren't willing to cut a corner, someone else would be found there who will." You changed the meaning and applied it to something other than what was intended. I never told you that I was upset at Laventhol's standards, nor that the pressure to take on questionable clients "was too much for me." I made it very clear that I did not consider Laventhol's standards any lower than any other growing accounting firm during the time I worked there and that, in fact, during my years with the firm there was a concerted effort by Laventhol to upgrade the quality of the work done by personnel who came to the firm through mergers. I would also add that Laventhol's professional standards had nothing to do with my decision to leave the firm.

Lawrence M. Reisman

New York
