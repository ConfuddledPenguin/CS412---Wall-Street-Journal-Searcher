Statewide Bancorp. Boosts Loan Reserves, Omits Cash Dividend

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/29/90

TOMS RIVER, N.J. -- Statewide Bancorp. said it increased its allowance for loan losses by $30 million, omitted its cash dividend and agreed to a close review of its main subsidiary bank. The loan-loss provision rose to $50 million from $20 million at March 31, as some real estate loans continued to deteriorate and the New Jersey real estate market remained uncertain, the bank said. One of its loan participations had been criticized by the Comptroller of the Currency, the principal regulator of both of Statewide's subsidiaries.

Statewide, which has about $2 billion in assets, said it believes the new provision is adequate, but it didn't rule out further increases. "We can't hold out to people that it definitely won't get worse," said Charles Sanders, the bank's secretary.

Statewide said the suspension of its quarterly cash dividend, which was 20 cents a share, will take effect in the second quarter and continue indefinitely. Douglas Johnson, president and chief executive officer, said the bank's previous dividend payments have amounted to about $8 million a year.

Mr. Johnson said the suspension is intended to preserve Statewide's "strong capital position," which he said is well above regulatory requirements.

The review of Statewide's lead subsidiary, First National Bank of Toms River, is the result of an agreement with the Comptroller of the Currency. It covers the adequacy of loan-loss provisions and requires the development of plans to strengthen assets, ensure adequate capitalization, and improve supervision of bad loans.
