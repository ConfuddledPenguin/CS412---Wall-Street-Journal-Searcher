RTC Sells Horizon Financial Branches As Bailout of S&L Industry Accelerates

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/29/90

WASHINGTON -- The Resolution Trust Corp. sold the 37 branches of Horizon Financial, Southampton, Pa., to First Valley Bank, Bethlehem, Pa., and Union National Bank of Pittsburgh. The transaction was part of another block of thrift sales and takeovers, as the agency accelerates the S&L industry bailout.

Horizon Financial had $1.76 billion in assets and liabilities of $2.1 billion. The RTC will receive premiums of $26.5 million from the two acquiring institutions. The RTC will advance about $1.25 billion to the acquirers and will retain about $1.75 billion of the Horizon's assets. The agency expects to recover some of the advances by selling the assets, and it estimated the ultimate cost of the transaction at $357.8 million.

Separately, the RTC placed Pima Savings & Loan Association, Tucson, Ariz., and Mercury Savings & Loan Association, Huntington Beach, Calif., into receivership. The assets of Pima Savings totaled $2.7 billion, and the assets of Mercury Savings totaled $2.2 billion.

The RTC also placed Caguas-Central Savings Bank of Puerto Rico, with $1.7 billion in assets, and American Pioneer Savings Bank, Orlando, Fla., with $1.8 billion in assets, in conservatorship. Caguas-Central Savings Bank is the first thrift in Puerto Rico to be taken over by the RTC.

Under conservatorships, the institutions are managed by the government but the owners maintain their interests in the thrifts. Under receivership, ownership interests are wiped out and the RTC prepares to sell off the thrift's assets.

In other actions by the RTC last week:

-- Mountainwest Savings & Loan Association, Ogden, Utah, was sold to Mountainwest Financial, Sandy, Utah, a newly chartered subsidiary of Sears Consumer Financial Corp. Mountainwest Savings had assets of $155.3 million and liabilities of $211.4 million. The RTC will advance about $180 million to the acquirer and retain $128.4 million in assets. The agency expects the transaction ultimately to cost $65.1 million.

-- Deseret Savings & Loan Association, Salt Lake City, was sold to Zions First National Bank, Salt Lake City, a unit of Zions Bancorporation. Deseret had assets of $131.8 million and liabilities of $221.8 million. The RTC advanced $125.3 million and retained $104.3 million in assets, and expects this transaction to cost $99.2 million.

-- The insured deposits of First Federal Savings & Loan Association of Brenham, Texas, were transferred to Kilgore Federal Savings & Loan Association, Kilgore, Texas. First Federal had $116.4 million in assets, and liabilities of $125.4 million. The RTC advanced $76.8 million, and expects the transaction to cost $36.8 million.

-- Remington Savings Association, Elgin, Texas, was placed in receivership. The thrift had $174.2 million in assets.
