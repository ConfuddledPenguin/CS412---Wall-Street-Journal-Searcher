Politics & Policy: Political Action Committees Gave Record Sum in Period

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/29/90

WASHINGTON -- Although they may be an endangered species, political action committees contributed a record $63 million to House and Senate candidates during the first 15 months of the 1989-90 election cycle. The heavy giving -- up 13% from the comparable period in 1987-88, and 51% above the 1985-86 period -- came as Senate Democrats and Republicans were both crafting separate proposals to ban PAC contributions to federal candidates as part of campaign finance overhaul legislation.

According to a study by the Federal Election Commission, House candidates received 18% more PAC money than in 1988, with Senate candidates seeing a more modest 3% increase. PAC contributions to House candidates totaled $43.6 million, while Senate candidates received $20 million. House Democratic incumbents, who traditionally receive most of PAC funds, are much less enthusiastic about restricting PAC money than their Senate counterparts.

Twenty-three PACs disbursed more than $1 million. The largest PAC, the Democratic Republican Independent Voter Education Committee (DRIVE), affiliated with the Teamsters Union, raised $6.7 million during the 15 months covered in the FEC study and spent $4.6 million. The next largest PAC, operated by the American Medical Association, raised $4.1 million and spent $2.2 million.
