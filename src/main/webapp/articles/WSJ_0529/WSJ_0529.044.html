OTC, Southwestern Bell Form Consortium for Bid

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 05/29/90

WELLINGTON -- OTC Ltd. of Australia said it has formed a consortium with Southwestern Bell Corp. of the U.S. to bid for Telecom Corp. The government is selling New Zealand's state-owned telephone company as part of a program to repay debt.

But the Commerce Commission, New Zealand's antitrust regulator, said that it has declined to give initial clearance to OTC to make a bid. A fuller investigation of OTC's application will be made immediately, the regulator said.

OTC, owned by the Australian government, has a monopoly on Australia's international telecommunications.

Peter Shaw, general manager of OTC's international division, said OTC and Southwestern Bell have had talks with a number of New Zealand companies about expanding the consortium.

"Nothing has been resolved there yet," said Mr. Shaw, who wouldn't identify the local companies. OTC already has business connections with Telecom through work on communications links between Australia and New Zealand, he said.

Mr. Shaw declined to give details of the consortium's financial arrangements, but said Southwestern Bell is the major partner in the venture.
