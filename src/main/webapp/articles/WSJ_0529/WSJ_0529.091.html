International Brief -- Fuji Heavy Industries Ltd.: Unconsolidated Pretax Profit For Fiscal '90 Tumbled 52%

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/29/90

Fuji Heavy Industries Ltd. of Japan, which makes Subaru autos, aircraft and other machinery, said its unconsolidated pretax profit for the year ended March 31 tumbled 52% to 6.65 billion yen ($44.1 million) from 13.86 billion yen a year earlier, citing surging material costs and higher administrative and general expenses. Operating losses totaled 29.63 billion yen, compared with fiscal 1989 operating profit of 2.68 billion yen. Income from dividend and interest payments helped offset pretax losses, but special losses erased extraordinary gains from sales of capital assets. Thus, net income fell 47% to 5.74 billion yen, or 9.85 yen a share, from 10.89 billion yen, or 19.81 yen a share.

Sales eased 0.8% to 658.02 billion yen from 663.44 billion yen. Exports contributed 268.65 billion yen to sales. The auto category, Fuji's biggest business, was the only area of decline, substantial enough to obliterate gains in all other sectors. Sales of autos, including exports and domestic sales, fell 2.7% to 524.4 billion yen.

Fuji intends to cut the annual dividend to six yen a share, including a 0.5 yen special payout already paid, from eight yen.
