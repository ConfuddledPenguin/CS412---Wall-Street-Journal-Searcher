Tony Bettenhausen Places 26th in Indianapolis 500

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/29/90

INDIANAPOLIS -- Race car driver Tony Bettenhausen, the subject of a Journal page one story on the finances of auto racing, finished 26th at the Indianapolis 500. Mr. Bettenhausen's race car developed engine problems early in the race Sunday, forcing him to retire after completing 76 laps of the 200-lap race. Mr. Bettenhausen expected to make a profit on the race only if he finished seventh or better, but said after the race that he planned to return to the Indy 500 next year.

His brother, Gary, finished 31st of 33 entries because of problems with a wheel bearing. Only 15 cars were still running at the end of the race, which was won by Arie Luyendyk, a native of Sommelsdyk, the Netherlands, now living in Scottsdale, Ariz.
