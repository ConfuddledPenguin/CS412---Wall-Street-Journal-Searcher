New Stock Listings

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/29/90

NEW YORK -- The New York Stock Exchange announced three new stock listings, all initial public offerings, while the American Stock Exchange announced three new stocks, two of them initial public offerings.

The Pacific Stock Exchange announced one and the National Market System of the over-the-counter market announced four new stock listings.

On the Big Board, Alliance Global Environment Fund Inc., a New York management investment firm, began trading Wednesday under the symbol AEF.

Thai Capital Fund Inc., a New York management investment firm, began trading Wednesday under TC.

Curragh Resources Inc., a Toronto producer of zinc and lead concentrates, began trading Thursday under CZP.

Joining the Amex, Celutel Inc., a Philadelphia phone service company previously traded over the counter, began trading Friday on a "when issued" basis under CLU.WI.

Chiles Offshore Corporation, a Houston drilling rig owner and operator, began trading Thursday under CHC.

Templeton Global Utilities Inc., a St. Petersburg, Fla., management investment firm, began trading Thursday under TGU.

On the Pacific Exchange, Geneva Steel, a Vineyard, Utah, steel mill owner and operator, began trading on Wednesday under GNV.

On the OTC market, Health Advancement Services Inc., a Tempe, Arizona, provider of worksite health evaluations, began trading under HASI.

Healthinfusion Inc., a Miami owner and operator of home care and infusion therapy facilities, began trading under HINF.

Mid-American Waste, a Canal Winchester, Ohio, solid waste management business, began trading under MAWS.

Pinnacle Environmental, a Salt Lake City industrial asbestos abatement company, began trading under PENV.

The American Stock Exchange began trading options on the over-the-counter stock of AST Research Inc., Irvine, Calif., a desktop computer systems maker, under ASQ and on McCaw Cellular Communications Inc., Kirkland, Washington-based, owner and operator of phone systems, under MWQ. It also began trading options on Molecular Biosystems Inc., San Diego, a maker of diagnostic and research products, under MBQ.

The Pacific Stock Exchange began trading options on Equifax Inc., an Atlanta risk management and financial control services company, under EFX, and Ohio Edison Co., an Akron utility, under OEC.

The Chicago Board Options Exchange began trading options on SciMed Life Systems Inc., a Maplegrove, Minn., maker of disposable medical products, under SMLS, and options on Legent Corp., a Vienna, Va., supplier of productivity enhancement system software, under LGNT.
