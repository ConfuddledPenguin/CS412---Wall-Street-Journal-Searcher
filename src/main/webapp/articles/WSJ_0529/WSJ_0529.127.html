Chrysler Says Zuckerman Plans to Leave Company

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/29/90

HIGHLAND PARK, Mich. -- Chrysler Corp. confirmed that Vice President and Treasurer Frederick W. Zuckerman is planning to leave the auto maker. In a statement issued on Friday, Chrysler said Mr. Zuckerman approached Chrysler management "several months ago" to say "he was considering leaving Chrysler . . . because of his strong desire and interest to involve himself in corporate restructurings or other senior financial officer activities." Chrysler said Mr. Zuckerman also "has grown tired of the travel grind" after 11 years of commuting between Detroit and his home in New York.

Chrysler said its management "reluctantly agreed to let Fred pursue the many other opportunities open to someone of his experience and expertise." Chrysler added that Mr. Zuckerman, 56 years old, "has put together an outstanding team in the treasurer's office, which will see to it that Chrysler's financial stability and strength continue to grow."

A spokesman said the company hasn't any more details about Mr. Zuckerman's departure, including where he might go or when.
