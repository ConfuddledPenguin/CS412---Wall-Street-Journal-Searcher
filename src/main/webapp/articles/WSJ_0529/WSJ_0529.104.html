Business Brief -- National Semiconductor Corp.: NEC of Japan to Sell Chips Under U.S. Concern's Label

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/29/90

National Semiconductor Corp., Santa Clara, Calif., said it entered a joint venture with NEC Corp. of Japan under which National will sell certain NEC memory chips under the National label. The pact shows how U.S. chip concerns are increasingly taking on foreign partners to round out their product lines. National's agreement covers NEC's static random access memory chips, known as SRAMs, which are considered high-volume standard parts. In recent years, National has been specializing in making high-end parts for supercomputers and other specialized products.

National said the companies also agreed to explore future cooperative efforts in SRAMs, including marketing, manufacturing and technology. National said it would start offering the NEC chips in July.
