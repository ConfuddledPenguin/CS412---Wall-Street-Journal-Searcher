Boeing Gets Order From Iberia Airlines Valued at $1 Billion

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/29/90

SEATTLE -- Boeing Co. confirmed that it received firm orders valued at $1 billion for 16 757 jetliners from Spain's Iberia Airlines. The order also includes options Iberia secured on 12 more of the jetliners plus spare parts and training. Engine selection will be made at a later date, Boeing said.

The order, disclosed by Iberia in Madrid last Friday, calls for the airplanes to be outfitted for 188 passengers, 86 in business class and 102 in tourist. Iberia said the jetliners will be delivered starting in 1993 through the third quarter of 1994.

Iberia, which is controlled by Spain's National Industrial Institute, has said it is in the midst of renovating much of its fleet, which consists of 84 aircraft ranging from McDonnell Douglas DC-9s to widebody Boeing 747s.

The airline's new Boeing airplanes will replace some of its 35 Boeing 727 aircraft, Iberia said. They will be used primarily on its route network to European, African and Middle Eastern destinations.

Last year, Iberia ordered eight airbus A-340 airplanes and took options on four others, as well as 17 McDonnell Douglas MD-87 aircraft, at a combined cost of $1.1 billion. Iberia has said it expects to replace its entire fleet by 2000.

Seattle-based Boeing said that Iberia's order for 16 of its 757s brings to 648 the number of 757s ordered by 33 customers including Iberia. The aerospace concern said that so far this year, it has had orders for 154 of the aircraft valued at about $12.4 billion.
