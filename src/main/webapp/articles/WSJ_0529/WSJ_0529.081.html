International Brief -- Mondadori S.p.A.: Leading Italian Publisher's Net for 1989 Plunged 48%

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/29/90

Mondadori S.p.A., Italy's largest publishing concern, said 1989 net income plunged 48% to 54 billion lire ($4.4 million) from 103.5 billion lire a year earlier. Directors proposed reducing dividends to 180 lire an ordinary share from 380 lire, and to 200 lire each for preferred and savings shares from 400 lire.

Mondadori blamed the earnings drop on costs stemming from last year's acquisition of L'Editoriale Espresso S.p.A., a Rome-based publisher. Revenue jumped to 2.367 trillion lire in 1989 from 1.495 trillion lire in 1988. Excluding special items, profit fell 31% to 138.3 billion lire. Debt totaled 372.5 billion lire at the end of 1989, compared with net liquidity of 220 billion lire a year earlier. The parent's profit fell to 73.9 billion lire from 78.4 lire and sales rose to 1.106 trillion lire from 1.091 trillion lire.

Mondadori is at the center of a conflict between the media magnate Silvio Berlusconi, who was named chairman in January, and the financier Carlo De Benedetti, who lost control of Mondadori in December; the two sides are in talks on a compromise that may break up the company.
