Business Brief -- Subaru of America Inc.: About 285,000 Cars Recalled To Fix Suspension Problem

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/29/90

Subaru of America Inc., Cherry Hill, N.J., said it is recalling about 285,000 cars because of a rear-suspension problem that has caused at least four minor accidents but no injuries. The distributor of vehicles made by Fuji Heavy Industries Ltd. of Japan said the models being recalled are its four-wheel-drive 1980 through 1984 sedan hatchback and hardtop cars, and 1985 and 1986 hatchbacks.

Subaru said continuous exposure to road salt may corrode the rear suspension of the cars, making them difficult to steer. A Subaru spokesman said about half of the 285,000 cars are in geographical areas where large amounts of road salt are used to aid snow and ice removal in winter. The company said it will rustproof or replace the rear suspensions free of charge.
