International Brief -- Degussa AG: Chemical Firm Posts Drop Of 13% in First-Half Net

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/29/90

Degussa AG said its group, or world-wide, pretax profit in the first half of the year ending Sept. 30 slid 13%. In the six months, ended in March, pretax profit fell to 159 million marks ($94.5 million) from 183 million marks a year earlier.

The Frankfurt-based chemical company's earnings fell despite a modest 3.1% gain in world-wide sales to 7.09 billion marks from 6.88 billion marks.

Overall earnings were dragged down by the chemical sector, where despite a 6% rise in sales to 2.35 billion marks, earnings fell by an unspecified margin. Degussa explained that profit margins were tightened by foreign exchange factors.

In its other sectors, Degussa registered both sales and earnings gains. The pharmaceutical division "posted good results," the company said, citing a slight, though unspecified, profit gain.

Degussa said it is still negotiating the takeover of U.S.-based Engelhard Corp.'s world-wide gold and silver operations. A letter of intent was signed in March.
