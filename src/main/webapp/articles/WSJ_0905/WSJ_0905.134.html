Chiles, Martinez to Vie In Florida Governor Race

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/05/90

MIAMI -- Former Sen. Lawton Chiles won an easy victory over Rep. Bill Nelson in a primary yesterday to become the Democratic nominee for governor of Florida. He will face Gov. Bob Martinez, who overwhelmed four challengers in the Republican primary to win a chance to vie for his second term.

The race is important because Florida is one of the big states in the Sun Belt that will gain congressional seats as a result of the 1990 census. The governor will play a significant role in the redistricting process.

The Chiles victory represents a return-from-retirement of Mr. Chiles, who left the Senate in 1988 after being diagnosed with having clinical depression. Mr. Chiles will campaign under a self-imposed obstacle -- a $100 limit on individual campaign contributions. The limitation is an open challenge to the convention of big-money politics.

With more than 50% of precincts reporting, Mr. Chiles was ahead of Rep. Nelson 70%-30%. Gov. Martinez won a solid two-thirds of the Republican vote.
