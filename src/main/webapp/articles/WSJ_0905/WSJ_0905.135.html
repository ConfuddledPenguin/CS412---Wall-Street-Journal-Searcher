Technology Brief -- Hewlett-Packard Co.: Firm Reduces U.S. Prices For HP Vectra Computers

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/05/90

Hewlett-Packard Co., of Palo Alto, Calif., said it reduced U.S. list prices on its top-of-the-line HP Vectra personal computers by as much as 24%, a move analysts said may be designed to combat slow sales. The reductions, which became effective Saturday, range from $300 to $4,600. The price of the HP Vectra 486 Model 670, for example, was lowered to $14,899 from $19,499. Also reduced up to 36% were Hewlett-Packard's 330-megabyte and 670-megabyte, enhanced small-device interface, or ESDI, disk drives.

Pradeep Jotwani, the company's North American PC marketing manager, said the price declines were driven by lower manufacturing costs for ESDI hard-disk drives and by lower costs for components.

Timothy R. McCollum, an analyst for Dean Witter Reynolds Inc. in New York, said the main factor behind the price decreases was that Hewlett-Packard had trouble selling the HP Vectra line since the top models were introduced in January 1989.
