Former PaineWebber Broker Is Barred From Industry, Fined

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/05/90

NEW YORK -- A former PaineWebber Inc. broker agreed to be barred from the securities industry for what the National Association of Securities Dealers alleged was excessive and unsuitable trading in two customer accounts. The New York broker, Nilda Rubiera Zim, didn't admit or deny any wrongdoing in the matter. She also consented to a $125,000 fine.

The NASD, the self-regulatory agency for the over-the-counter markets, also fined the securities firm $30,000 jointly with Joseph Patrick Tota, who managed the branch at which Ms. Zim worked.

Mr. Tota, currently national sales manager at the PaineWebber Inc. unit of PaineWebber Group Inc., also was suspended for five days as a principal. The firm and Mr. Tota neither admitted nor denied the NASD's charges.

The firm, Ms. Zim and Mr. Tota couldn't immediately be reached for comment.

The NASD charged that in March 1985, a customer opened a PaineWebber account with $190,000 of cash and stock. In the year that followed, the agency said, Ms. Zim's trades for that customer generated gross commissions of $203,165 and a loss of $11,016. Further, the agency charged that Ms. Zim executed transactions at a rate amounting to the sale of every security in the account about every 2 1/2 weeks.

In another case, the NASD said, Ms. Zim handled an account, opened in June 1985, with $100,000 of U.S. Treasury securities and $32,000 of certain units. Over the next 26 months, the NASD alleged, the account generated commissions of $98,218 and a loss of $113,806. (In October 1986, Ms. Zim left PaineWebber and joined Prudential-Bache Securities Inc., and this account was transferred to that firm. Prudential-Bache, a unit of Prudential Insurance Co. of America, wasn't charged in the matter.)

In both cases, the NASD said, PaineWebber and Mr. Tota failed to properly supervise Ms. Zim.
