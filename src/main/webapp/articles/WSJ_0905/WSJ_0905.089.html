Marketing & Media: France Approves Carat, Eurocom Ad-Buying Union

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/05/90

PARIS -- Despite worries about creating a giant, France approved the request of the Carat Holding S.A. and Eurocom S.A. advertising groups to merge their ad-buying operations. But because it fears that the new group could have excessive influence on French media, especially radio and television, France's Finance Ministry ruled that the two can cooperate only on campaigns including ads placed outside France. Theoretically, for clients placing ads solely in France, Carat and Eurocom will continue competing with each other.

The decision is a compromise, aimed at helping the French companies develop abroad. But some competitors complain that the new group still will excessively dominate the French ad-buying market, in addition to becoming an increasingly powerful force across Europe.

By Eurocom's calculation, the new group will become the third largest in Europe, behind a consortium called Media Partnership and behind another linkup including the Interpublic group and Publicis-FCB Communication. Within France, Carat, a subsidiary of Britain's Aegis Group PLC, already is the largest single media-buyer. Eurocom is second.

Media buyers act as middle men, buying large volumes of ad time and space in publications and on broadcast shows, and selling it to advertisers. In a system pioneered by Carat, the big-volume media buyers get deep discounts, which makes it hard for smaller-scale buyers of ad space to compete.
