Yen Futures Trader Pleads No Contest to Fraud Counts

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/05/90

CHICAGO -- Martin J. Riley, one of 15 yen futures traders awaiting trial in federal court here on fraud charges, pleaded no contest to one count of mail and wire fraud and two counts of violating the Commodity Exchange Act. Separately, opening arguments in the trial of the 14 other defendants were scheduled for next Tuesday.

Mr. Riley's plea before U.S. District Court Judge William Hart means that while he isn't contesting the government charges against him in the yen case, his plea can't be used as an admission of guilt in subsequent civil or administrative proceedings against him.

Neither Mr. Riley nor his attorney, William Murphy, could be reached for comment on the plea.

Many futures traders who have pleaded guilty to fraud charges stemming from the government's undercover investigation of the futures pits have been fined by the futures exchanges for admitting to violations of exchange trading rules.
