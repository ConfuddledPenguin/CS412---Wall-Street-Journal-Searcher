Placer Dome to Buy Gold-Copper Tract In British Columbia

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/05/90

VANCOUVER, British Columbia -- Placer Dome Inc. said it plans a two-step transaction under which it would acquire a big gold-copper property in north-central British Columbia, for about 260.8 million Canadian dollars (US$225.5 million). The proposed purchase of the Mount Milligan gold-copper property would add about 6.2 million ounces of gold and 900,000 metric tons of copper to Placer Dome's existing reserves. Vancouver-based Continental Gold Corp., which currently owns 69.8% of Mount Milligan, expected to bring the property into production by early 1993.

Under terms of the transaction, Placer Dome said it agreed to acquire BP Canada Inc.'s 30.2% stake in Mount Milligan for C$78.8 million, subject to regulatory approval. Once completed, Placer Dome said it then plans an offer to acquire all of Continental's 9.1 million fully diluted common shares outstanding.

Placer Dome said it would offer C$20 or one Placer Dome common share for each Continental share outstanding, for a total indicated value of C$182 million. A group of Continental insiders who hold a combined 30% stake in the company have already agreed to tender their stock to Placer Dome's bid, it said, provided the offer is made by Oct. 22.

In Toronto Stock Exchange trading yesterday, Placer Dome's shares closed at C$20.375 each, down 12.5 Canadian cents, and Continental closed at C$19.625, up C$2.625.

If successful, Placer Dome's purchase of Mount Milligan would mark the second step by the big gold producer this year to bolster its North American gold reserves. Earlier this summer, Placer Dome acquired a minority stake in British Columbia's rich Eskay Creek gold project.

Placer Dome has been seeking to expand its holdings in North America, where gold reserves tend to be more highly valued than in regions that are viewed by investors as less politically stable. A sizable chunk of Placer Dome's existing reserves are in Papua, New Guinea.
