International Brief -- Societe Nationale Elf Aquitaine: Net Income for First Half Rose 14% to $926 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/05/90

France's state-controlled Societe Nationale Elf Aquitaine said steady gains in the chemical sector helped boost its first-half net income 14% to 4.9 billion francs ($926 million) from 4.3 billion francs a year earlier. Sales weren't disclosed.

The profit pace followed flat growth in 1989, but fell short of Elf Chairman Loik Le Floch-Prigent's estimate earlier this year that earnings for 1990 may grow 20% or more.

Chemical sector operating profit rose slightly to 3.7 billion francs from 3.4 billion francs, though Elf noted that the 1989 first-half figure reflected prices for base chemicals that were "extremely high" in relative terms. The chemical results also included a one-time capital gain of 650 million francs from the sale of paint maker La Seigneurie S.A.
