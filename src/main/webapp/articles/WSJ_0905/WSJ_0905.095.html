Foreigners Were Net Equity Sellers In April and May

  • Author: Robin Goldwyn Blumenthal
  • Date: 09/05/90

NEW YORK -- Foreign investors continued as net sellers of U.S. equities in April and May, selling a combined net $3.4 billion of stocks, early data from the Securities Industry Association show. At the same time, foreign investors were only modest net buyers of U.S. corporate bonds in those months, with corporate bond purchases totaling a combined net $2.3 billion, the data indicate. In addition, the SIA called foreign investors' interest in U.S. Treasury securities "marginal" during April and May, with net purchases of Treasury securities totaling $576 million.

The figures reflect unease about the U.S. economy, as well as positive developments in the politics and economies of European countries, said David G. Strongin, director of international finance for the SIA, in a report.

"It's a sea change in how foreign investors are looking at U.S. securities," Mr. Strongin said in an interview. Ordinarily, the securities industry trade association releases quarterly figures, but Mr. Strongin said that because the "pattern of a continued divestment" continued during the two months from the previous quarter, the SIA wanted to get the figures out quickly.

In April and May 1989, foreign investors were net buyers of U.S. stocks, with net purchases totaling $960 million. They also were net buyers of U.S. Treasury securities, with net purchases totaling $7.03 billion, and net buyers of U.S. corporate bonds, with net purchases of $2.77 billion of them.

Mr. Strongin pointed out that for the first five months, all foreign activity in U.S. securities resulted in net sales of $4.3 billion, compared with net purchases in the year-earlier period of $42.3 billion.

He said the consequences of the lack of support from foreign investors, in addition to hurting stock and bond prices, likely would be higher interest rates, all things being equal. He said the SIA expects the trend to be similar in the third quarter.

In the first quarter, net sales of equities by foreign investors totaled $3.3 billion. In the quarter, net purchases of corporate bonds by foreign investors totaled $3.4 billion, a spokesman said. Net sales of Treasury securities totaled $6.5 billion, he added.

The net sales by foreign investors in U.S. securities in the first quarter represented the first net outflow since the third quarter of 1983, the SIA said.
