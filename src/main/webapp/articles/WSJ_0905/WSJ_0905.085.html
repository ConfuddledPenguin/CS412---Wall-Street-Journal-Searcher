Arco Expected to Unveil Cleaner Type of Gasoline

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/05/90

LOS ANGELES -- Atlantic Richfield Co., which caters to cost-conscious motorists in the West, will unveil a new gasoline in Southern California that is expected to be a cleaner version of its premium unleaded fuel. Analysts speculated that Arco will roll out an emission-control version of its unleaded fuel as a complement to its reformulated gasoline for pre-1975 cars that has proven very successful. Last year, Arco was the first gasoline company to scrap its regular leaded fuel in favor of the high-octane, emission-control unleaded gasoline, which is aimed at the 20% of motorists in its selling area who drive older cars.

M. Craig Schwerdt, an oil analyst with Drake Capital Securities Inc., said Arco has in the past proposed the notion of "designer fuels" for specific markets. Such fuels could address differences in altitude, smog-control standards and other variables.

An Arco spokesman declined to discuss the new product, which is scheduled to be introduced at a press conference Thursday. Arco's stock closed at $135.625, up 75 cents in composite New York Stock Exchange trading.
