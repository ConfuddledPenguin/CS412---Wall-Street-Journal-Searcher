New Securities Issues

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/05/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report. CORPORATE

Security Pacific Corp. -- $100 million of notes due Sept. 15, 1993, priced at par to float quarterly at 25 basis points above the London interbank offered rate. The initial coupon rate will be set Friday. The noncallable notes will pay interest quarterly, with the first interest payment being made Dec. 19. Rated single-A-1 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and double-A-minus by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by First Boston Corp.

MORTGAGES

Capstead Securities -- $100 million of collateralized mortgage obligations offered in six classes by Morgan Stanley & Co. The securities, which carry coupon rates of 9.60% to 9.90%, were priced Thursday. The offering, through Capstead 1990-2, is backed by 30-year whole loans with a weighted average coupon rate of 10.782% and weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29.83 years. Yield spreads and other details weren't available.

EUROBONDS

Nationwide Anglia Building Society (U.K.) -- #75 million of fungible 13 5/8% Eurobonds due March 8, 1994, priced at 102.10 via Samuel Montagu & Co. Yield, less full fees, is 106 basis points over comparable gilt, or British government bond. Fees 1 5/8.

Uniden Corp. (Japan) -- $120 million of Eurobonds due Sept. 20, 1994, with equity-purchase warrants, indicating a 5 1/8% coupon at par, via Nomura International Ltd. Guaranteed by Sanwa Bank Ltd. Each $5,000 bond carries one warrant, exercisable from Oct. 4, 1990, through Sept. 13, 1994, to buy shares at an expected 2 1/2% premium over the closing price when terms are fixed Tuesday. Fees 2 1/4.

Starts Corp. (Japan) -- 63 million Swiss francs of privately placed convertible notes due Dec. 31, 1994, with a fixed 4 3/4% coupon at par via Nomura Bank Switzerland. Put option Dec. 31, 1992, at 108.50 to yield 8.25%. Each 50,000 Swiss franc note convertible from Oct. 18, 1990, to Dec. 16, 1994. Final conversion terms to be fixed Monday. Fees 1.38.
