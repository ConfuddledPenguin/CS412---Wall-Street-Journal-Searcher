From "First Strike," a book about the 1981 Israeli attack on Iraq's nuclear reactor by Shlomo Nakdimon (Summit, 1987):

On January 26, 1976, France was the sole Western country to support an anti-Israel resolution presented to the UN Security Council . . . Forty-eight hours later, Premier Chirac arrived in Iraq . . . for a close inspection of the site picked for the French nuclear complex. . . . {He} issued specific directives to remove any administrative obstacle hindering the swift and flawless implementation of the contract. . . .

On March 20, 1980, a London newspaper reported: "Next year, Iraq will be capable of manufacturing a nuclear bomb -- with the assistance of France and Italy. . . ." {Two days before} an official {Iraqi} spokesman bitterly attacked the American "campaign against Iraqi-French and Iraqi-Italian technological cooperation in the field of nuclear energy." . . . In another statement, the Iraqi Foreign Ministry affirmed: "Removal of Zionism from Palestine is a precondition for the constitution of a nonracist international community." . . . "We want the whole of Palestine," declared Deputy Prime Minister Tariq Aziz on March 24. "We recognize neither the 1967 borders nor the 1948 partition borders."
