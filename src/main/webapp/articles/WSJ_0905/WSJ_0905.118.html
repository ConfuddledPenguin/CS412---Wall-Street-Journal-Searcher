Business Brief -- General Electric Co.: GE Engine Picked by Italy For New Navy Helicopters

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/05/90

GE Aircraft Engines, a Lynn, Mass., unit of General Electric Co., said the Italian Ministry of Defense selected an engine the division co-developed to power new helicopters for the Italian navy. GE Aircraft Engines and its Italian partners, units of Fiat S.p.A., beat out French and British engine makers for the bid, the company said. GE Aircraft Engines said it won't know the contract's value until the Italian government decides how many helicopters it will order. Once it places a firm order, 30% of the engines will be produced by GE in Lynn, and the rest in Alfa Romeo and Fiat facilities in Italy, the GE unit said.

GE Aircraft, which has annual commercial and military aircraft sales of roughly $3.6 billion, is a subsidiary of General Electric, the Fairfield, Conn., company that also has interests in broadcasting and financial services.
