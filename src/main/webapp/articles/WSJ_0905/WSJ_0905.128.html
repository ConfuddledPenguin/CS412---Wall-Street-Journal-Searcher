Credit Ratings: Ratings on PacifiCorp Debt and Preferred Are Boosted by S&P

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/05/90

PORTLAND, Ore. -- Standard & Poor's Corp. raised its ratings on about $4.7 billion of PacifiCorp debt and preferred stock, in the wake of the utility holding company's decision to end its 10-month pursuit of Arizona Public Service Co. and its parent, Pinnacle West Capital Corp., both based in Phoenix, Ariz. PacifiCorp dropped its takeover efforts in exchange for seasonal power swaps with Arizona Public Service having a combined potential value of about $260 million.

The rating concern upgraded PacifiCorp's senior secured debt to single-A from single-A-minus, and senior unsecured debt and preferred stock to single-A-minus from triple-B-plus.

S&P also revised to stable from negative its long-term outlook for the debt and preferred stock of Arizona Public Service. "Much-needed up-front cash" from the PacifiCorp deal "would allow Pinnacle to pay down its huge debt burden more rapidly and aid in restoring its common dividend over time," the rating concern said.

Pinnacle West shares tumbled $3.75 to close at $12.375 in New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday. It was the most active issue on the Big Board, on turnover of more than 1.9 million shares. PacifiCorp shares closed at $19.875, up 50 cents, in Big Board trading.
