Letters to the Editor: Ukrainian History

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/05/90

Sonia A. Boyar's Aug. 21 letter to the editor dismisses the national honor of the Ukraine on the basis of the welcome given to the invading German army of World War II. Such dismissal ignores the most critical component of Ukrainian nationalism, namely the view that the Ukraine was a nation occupied by Russian imperialism. It is worth remembering that, prior to Operation Barbarossa, the Russians had subjected the Ukrainians to severe repression during the agrarian uprisings, at an estimated cost of more than three million lives. With this history, even a Nazi convoy could be mistaken for a troop of white knights, under the presumption that the Ukrainians were being liberated from their Soviet captors. When this proved to be a false hope, the Ukrainian partisans put up a heroic fight to expel the Germans.

As evidence of Ukrainian fickleness, Ms. Boyar relies on a single reference to how some Ukrainians acted under the pressures of concentration-camp life. As one who has never been subjected to those horrors, I would not judge the morality practiced by those victims -- and neither should Ms. Boyar. After all, even the Jewish camps had their kapos.

Kenneth R. Stone

Cincinnati
