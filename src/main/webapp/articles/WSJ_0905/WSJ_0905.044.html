OTC Focus: Nasdaq Composite Gains Marginally; Adobe Systems Increases 3 1/8 to 28 1/8

  • Author: Anne Newman
  • Date: 09/05/90

Over-the-counter stocks held their ground in quiet trading as doubts about the Middle East and the economy kept many investors idle. The Nasdaq Composite Index edged up 0.46 to 381.67, a 0.1% gain. Volume remained unusually light as only 78.1 million shares traded, down from 79.9 million Friday. Declining shares overtook advancers, 1,009 to 789; the number of stocks falling to new lows swamped those reaching new highs, 104 to seven.

OTC trading is likely to remain light during the four-day workweek as investors await Friday's unemployment data for August, traders said. "I don't expect anything dramatic in a short week," said Len Hefter, manager of OTC trading at Jefferies & Co. in Dallas. Investors also are waiting to see if interest rates ease in the wake of the unemployment report, Mr. Hefter added.

The crisis in the Middle East continues to keep many investors wary of OTC stocks, said Robert King, director of OTC trading at Robinson Humphrey Co., Atlanta. "I think people are going to have to come to a decision as to what direction the Middle East {crisis} is headed towardwhether it will be a quick solution or a long, protracted stalemate," said Mr. King. "That's the key everyone is watching."

Fears of a recession also may lead to "tough sledding in the next couple of months," Mr. King said. But the stock market could recover by spring if evidence comes through that the country already has slid into a recession, he added.

Institutions dominated business at Robinson Humphrey as large investors tested stocks across the board, according to Mr. King. The market's largest stocks curried favor; the Nasdaq 100 Index of large stocks added 2.26 to 389.49 and posted the strongest gain among the Nasdaq indexes.

Adobe Systems surged 3 1/8 to 28 1/8 on 1.2 million shares. Investors embraced the Mountain View, Calif., software maker's announcement that it is expanding a licensing agreement with its biggest customer, Apple Computer. Countering reports that the two companies have drifted apart, Adobe and Apple said they will work together to provide new products based on Adobe's PostScript software and printer technology developed by Apple. Apple was unchanged at 37.

Elsewhere in the technology sector, Conner Peripherals added 1 to 21 5/8; Sun Microsystems gained 1/4 to 29; Microsoft rose 3/4 to 62 1/4; and Novell advanced 3/4 to 27 1/4. However, Oracle Systems eased 1/2 to 11 1/8.

Intel, the day's most active stock, added 1/8 to 33 1/2 after trading as low as 32 1/2. This newspaper reported that exchange-listed Advanced Micro Devices is poised to unveil a clone of Intel's lucrative 80386 microprocessor. Intel's stock, trading near its 52-week low, had already taken a beating in mid-August after a Bear Stearns & Co. analyst expressed concern about possible clones of the chip.

Elsewhere in the OTC market, Nike rebounded 3 1/8 to 68 5/8; MCI Communications was unchanged at 34; and St. Jude Medical added 3/4 to 30.

Tokos Medical jumped 1 5/8 to 11 5/8, nearly reaching its $12-a-share initial offering price of last March. The Santa Ana, Calif., provider of home-health-care services completed an acquisition of Atlantabased Physiologic Diagnostic Service for 2.7 million shares of Tokos stock. The news prompted Donaldson Lufkin & Jenrette analyst John Hindelong to repeat his "buy" opinion on Tokos, according to Dow Jones Professional Investor Report.

Mr. Hindelong told DJPIR that if Physiologic Diagnostic receives Food and Drug Administration approval of a uterine monitor for home use in high-risk pregnancies, sales of the device could contribute substantially to Tokos' sales and earnings beginning in 1991. An FDA advisory panel recommended approval of the device earlier this year.

Continental Gold surged 2 3/8 to 16 3/4 bid after Placer Dome Inc. said it plans to proceed with an offer to purchase the Canadian company. The previously proposed offer consists of $20 (Canadian) in cash or one Placer Dome share for each of Continental Gold's shares.

Amgen advanced 1/4 to 45 1/2 after trading as low as 44 earlier in the day. The Thousand Oaks, Calif., biotechnology concern said it bought $15 million of preferred stock of Regeneron Pharmaceuticals Inc., a closely held biotechnology company in Tarrytown, N.Y. The two companies also signed an agreement worth at least $11 million to Amgen to develop agents used in treating nerve disorders.

Healthco International rebounded in the afternoon to close at 17 7/8, down 3/8 after falling as low as 17 1/2. The Boston provider of home-health-care services said it agreed to an acquisition by an affiliate of Hicks, Muse & Co., a Dallas investment firm, for $19.25 a share in cash. The news came just two days before Healthco's annual shareholder meeting and as Gemini Partners, which owns a 9.6% stake in the company, is trying to replace the Healthco board in a proxy fight.

Healthco traded as high as 21 3/4 earlier this year amid buy-out speculation fueled by the Gemini Partners stake and a 7.2% position owned by a group including Texas brothers Thomas, Perry and Lee Bass. As recently as Friday, arbitragers were speculating that the company could fetch a price in the lows 20s, according to DJPIR.

Yesterday, Gemini Partners principal Emanuel Pearlman told DJPIR the Hicks Muse offer is "ridiculously low." Mr. Pearlman vowed to continue the proxy fight.

FNB Rochester fell 3/4 to 8 after the Rochester, N.Y., bank holding company halted buy-out talks with investors Cukurova Group of Istanbul, Turkey.

M. Kamenstein tumbled 5/8 to 2 7/8, although Monday the White Plains, N.Y., maker of household products reported second-quarter net income rose to 10 cents a share from restated net income of one cent a share a year earlier.
