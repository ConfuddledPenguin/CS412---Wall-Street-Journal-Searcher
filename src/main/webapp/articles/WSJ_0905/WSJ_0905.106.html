Failed Brokerage Firm In France to Be Acquired

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/05/90

PARIS -- Tuffier-Ravier-Py & Associes, a French brokerage firm that filed for bankruptcy in July, will be taken over by Francis Lagarde, a businessman who is backed by state-owned bank Credit Lyonnais. A Paris commercial tribunal cleared Mr. Lagarde's bid for the brokerage firm under a complex deal that includes an offer for all outstanding shares of the parent company, Tuffier & Associes, on the Paris bourse, a stock exchange spokesman said.

Under the terms of the operation as cleared by the tribunal, Mr. Lagarde will pay 156 million francs ($29 million) for the brokerage firm, in addition to setting aside 135 million francs to help reimburse any clients whose losses are not covered by the existing stock market guarantee fund. He will also inject about 100 million francs to recapitalize the brokerage firm and guarantee to maintain a staff of 129 people.

Mr. Lagarde, who owns a credit lease operation in Nantes, is backed by Altus Finance, a financial investment group formerly owned by Thomson-CSF and now controlled by Credit Lyonnais.

Tuffier officials were unavailable for comment. Tuffier, the largest independent brokerage firm in Paris, filed for bankruptcy after running up heavy losses this year. Thierry Tuffier, the group's chairman, blamed the group's troubles on a sharp fall in commissions and a decline in trading volume on the Paris stock market.

Tuffier's demise raised fears that other independent brokerage firms, which did not sell out to domestic and foreign financial institutions after the 1988 Paris bourse reforms, could suffer a similar fate.
