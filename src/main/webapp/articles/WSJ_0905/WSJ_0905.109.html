International Brief -- Coal & Allied Industries Ltd.: After-Tax Operating Profit For the Fiscal Year Soared

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/05/90

Coal & Allied Industries Ltd. of Australia, a coal producer, reported that operating profit after taxes for the year ended June 30 soared to 60.3 million Australian dollars (US$49.1 million) from A$2.4 million a year earlier, citing an increase in coal sales, higher selling prices in U.S.-dollar terms and a lower Australian dollar. Per-share earnings more than doubled to A$1.136 from 47 Australian cents. Total operating revenue rose 28% to A$603.9 million from A$470.5 million. Saying that increased efficiency and lower financing costs also boosted returns, Coal & Allied raised the dividend to 60 cents a share from 16 cents.

Overall profit after accounting for extraordinary items jumped 76% to A$52.2 million from A$29.7 million. In the latest period, the company had an extraordinary loss of A$8 million, related mainly to the closure of an engineering division; year-earlier results included an A$27.4 million extraordinary gain.
