Greyhound Union to Stop Payment of Strike Benefits

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/05/90

DALLAS -- The Amalgamated Transit Union will stop paying striking Greyhound Lines Inc. employees $50 a week in strike benefits as of Sept. 13, union President James La Sala said in a letter to officers of the Washington, D.C., union. About 6,000 members of the Amalgamated Council of Greyhound Local Unions, an ATU bargaining unit, have been on strike against the GLI Holding Co. unit since March 2.

Mr. La Sala said the Greyhound strike benefits, totaling about $300,000 a week, had depleted the union's strike fund to about $2 million. Further payments would make it difficult for the union to pay legal fees in connection with recent efforts to negotiate a bid to buy the bus company, he said.

Mr. La Sala said the termination of benefits wasn't a sign that the union is abandoning the strike. "We intend to continue this fight for as long as it may take until it is won or the company is destroyed," he said in the letter.

Battered by strike-related violence, Greyhound Lines filed for protection from creditors under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code in June. The union is currently in negotiations with Blackstone Group, a New York investment banking firm, on a possible employee-led bid for the company.
