What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/05/90

PETROLEUM PRICES SURGED again, sparked by a jump in gasoline. Unleaded gas for October delivery climbed 8.34 cents a gallon to 94.52 cents, while October crude gained $1.80 a barrel to $29.12. In reaction, stocks were mixed and bond prices fell. Petroleum demand appears to be rising on fears of a Mideast war, spurring consumers and companies to purchase supplies now, ahead of potential shortages.

The Strategic Petroleum Reserve may not be as easily tapped as U.S. policy makers hope, according to oil industry analysts and officials.

---

Manufacturing activity weakened in August in the second straight monthly drop, according to the purchasing managers' index.

---

First Boston named Archibald Cox Jr. president and chief executive, the latest reshuffling of top management at the big U.S. brokerage firm, which recently has been stumbling.

---

Honda and Nissan announced relatively small price boosts on some 1991 models, which could help the Japanese auto makers in their quest for greater U.S. market share.

---

An energy-consumption tax is still being considered by Democratic negotiators for this week's talks on reducing the budget deficit, despite the recent increase in petroleum prices.

---

GM's EDS unit and a group led by investor Jay Pritzker have agreed to buy the parent of Ticor Title Insurance Cos., the large real estate title insurance company. The move gives EDS a major entrance into a potentially lucrative new market.

---

Fujitsu unveiled a new generation of mainframe computers that could prove more powerful than a line IBM is expected to announce today.

Adobe Systems won a partial victory in its battle with Apple Computer and Microsoft to retain its hold on the market for desktop publishing.

---

The "payola" case against record promoter Joseph Isgro and two others was dismissed by a federal judge, who harshly criticized prosecutors for withholding evidence.

---

CBS Records' Walter Yetnikoff is stepping down immediately as chief executive of Sony's unit, accelerating his previous plans to leave.

---

Subaru of America's Harvey Lamm resigned as chairman and chief executive. Two executives of Fuji Heavy Industries, Subaru's new parent, succeed Lamm.

---

Europe's semiconductor program is falling victim to an industry shake out. N.V. Philips is withdrawing from a key project in the effort.

---

Private placements of securities plunged by one-third to $55.3 billion in the first half of this year.
