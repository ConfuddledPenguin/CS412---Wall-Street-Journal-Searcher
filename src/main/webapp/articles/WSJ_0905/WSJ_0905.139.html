Nonfinancial Firms' Productivity Rises At 1.7% Annual Rate

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/05/90

WASHINGTON -- The productivity of nonfinancial corporations rose in the second quarter at a 1.7% annual rate after falling at a 2.2% rate in the first period, the Labor Department reported. The increase in productivity -- output per hour of work -- stemmed from a rise in production at a 1.6% annual rate while working hours declined at a 0.1% pace.

The report covers all corporations doing business in the U.S. except banks, stock and commodity brokers, and finance and insurance agencies.

In addition to the new figures for nonfinancial corporations, the government also issued revised productivity statistics for other categories. Productivity of all U.S. businesses increased during the second quarter at an annual rate of 1.7%, compared with an original estimate of 1.9% and a first-quarter decline at a 1.5% annual rate.

The increase in productivity among nonfarm businesses was revised to a 1.5% annual rate from 1.6%. Nonfarm productivity also rose at a 1.6% annual rate in the first quarter. Manufacturing productivity rose at a revised rate of 4%, up from the earlier estimate of 3.7% but down from the first quarter's 5.1% pace.

The figures are adjusted for seasonal variations.
