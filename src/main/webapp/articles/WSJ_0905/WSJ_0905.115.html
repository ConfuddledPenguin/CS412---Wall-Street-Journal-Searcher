Business Brief -- Ford Motor Co.: Warranty Plans Are Altered For Lincoln, Ford, Mercury

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/05/90

Ford Motor Co. said it is offering four-year "bumber to bumper" warranty coverage with no deductible on its 1991 Lincoln models, a move that parallels a similar step by General Motors Corp.'s Cadillac division. Ford, based in Dearborn, Mich., also said it is shortening the "power-train" coverage on its Ford and Mercury badge cars, but also reducing the deductible amount that customers must pay.

The new policy for the luxury Lincoln line guarantees customers coverage on virtually all repairs -- except normal wear such as windshield wiper blades and brake pads -- for four years or 50,000 miles.

Previously, Lincoln cars had a 12 month, 12,000 mile basic warranty on all parts, and coverage of six years or 60,000 miles on the engine, transmission and related parts. However, after the basic warranty expired, Lincoln owners would pay a $100-per-visit deductible on the extended power-train coverage.

Cadillac recently told its dealers that it is eliminating the $100-per-visit deductible on its 1991 models.

Ford also said it has lowered the deductible to $50 from $100 on all Ford and Mercury cars and Ford light trucks because shortened engine and transmission coverage to four years or 50,000 miles from six years or 60,000 miles.
