Who's News: American Express Names IDS's Golub As Vice Chairman

  • Author: Kevin Goldman
  • Date: 09/05/90

NEW YORK -- American Express Co. named Harvey Golub, the president and chief executive officer of its IDS Financial Services Inc. unit, as vice chairman of the parent company. In addition, Michael P. Monaco, senior vice president and acting chief financial officer of American Express, the travel and financial services giant, was named executive vice president and chief financial officer.

The 51-year-old Mr. Golub was recruited to IDS from the consulting firm McKinsey & Co. IDS, a Minneapolis-based mutual fund, insurance and financial-planning concern, was purchased by American Express in 1983 for $727 million.

Mr. Golub also becomes chairman of IDS, a post that has been vacant, and he will continue as chief executive. The vice chairman's post at American Express also has been vacant.

Jeffrey E. Stiefler, 43, currently IDS's executive vice president of sales and marketing, was named president of the unit.

A spokesman for American Express said yesterday's moves didn't foreshadow a line of succession to James D. Robinson III, chairman and chief executive of the parent. Mr. Robinson, 54, who has been in his position for 13 years, "is in no hurry to go anywhere," the spokesman said.

Yesterday's appointments come 16 months after Louis V. Gerstner resigned as president of American Express to join RJR Nabisco Inc. Since then, the spokesman said, speculation about who would succeed Mr. Robinson has increased. The appointments of Mr. Golub and Mr. Monaco, 42, are part of a plan "to give the board of directors the maximum number of choices," the spokesman said.

"The appointments . . . continue our program to broaden and develop senior management," Mr. Robinson said in a statement.
