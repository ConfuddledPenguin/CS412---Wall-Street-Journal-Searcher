Business Brief -- Campeau Corp.: Retail Units' Losses in July Were Less Than Expected

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/05/90

Campeau Corp.'s retail units, Federated Department Stores Inc. and Allied Stores Corp., reported net losses of $50 million in July, "substantially less than expected," the companies said. In documents filed with U.S. Bankruptcy Court in Cincinnati, Federated said it had a net loss of $31.7 million for the four weeks ended Aug. 4. For the previous period, the five weeks ended July 7, Federated reported net losses of $30.6 million. Meanwhile, Allied posted a net loss of $18.3 million in July compared with $18.6 in the previous period.

The company noted that the net losses included non-cash interest and interest on the company's pre-petition debt, which account for about $35.1 million of the units' $42.6 million interest expense. The results also include a $2.5 million one-time charge due to an accounting change.

Analysts noted that seasonal factors and the absence of year-earlier comparisons make it difficult to assess Federated and Allied's progress since seeking Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection on Jan. 15.

Janet Mangano, an analyst at Jesup, Josephthal & Co. in New York, noted that the latest results were "rather encouraging."

Federated's sales for the four weeks were $263.5 million, while Allied's sales were $150.1 million.

In the combined June-July period, Federated had $3.5 million in cash flow while Allied had $1.94 million.
