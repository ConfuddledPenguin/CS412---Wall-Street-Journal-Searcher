CD Yields Are Mixed In the Latest Week

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/05/90

NEW YORK -- Yields on certificates of deposit at major banks and brokerage firms were mixed in the week ended yesterday. Average yields on small-denomination, "consumer," CDs sold directly by banks rose slightly. The average yield on a six-month CD edged up to 7.42% from 7.40%, according to an 18-bank national survey by Banxquote Money Markets, a New York information service. The average yield on a five-year consumer CD rose to 7.92% from 7.90%.

Meanwhile, average yields on small-denomination CDs sold by leading broker dealers fell. The greatest decline was for a five-year broker-sold CD, which slipped to 8.47% from 8.62%, according to Banxquote. The average yield for a one-year broker-sold CD fell to 7.87% from 8%.

Average yields for large-denomination CDs, which typically require deposits of $90,000 or more, were flat to down slightly. The average yield on a one-year jumbo CD eased to 7.72% from 7.77%, while the average for a five-year maturity remained at 8.27%.
