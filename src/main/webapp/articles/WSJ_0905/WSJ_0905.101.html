Ex-Official of Stock Firm Is Guilty of Racketeering

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/05/90

NEWARK, N.J. -- Leonard Tucker, former part-owner of a now-defunct penny stock firm, pleaded guilty yesterday to a federal racketeering charge, the U.S. attorney's office here said. Mr. Tucker was among eight high-level managers of F.D. Roberts Securities Inc. indicted on racketeering and fraud charges in March. The other seven, including two brothers of Mr. Tucker, currently await trial, according to U.S. Attorney Michael Chertoff. The defendants allegedly netted $67 million by engineering a stock-manipulation scheme from 1986 to 1988, Mr. Chertoff said in a statement.

Mr. Tucker faces a maximum of 20 years in prison and fines in the millions of dollars when he is sentenced by a U.S. District Court judge.

To date, 25 officers and high-level employees of F.D. Roberts, which was based in Paramus, N.J., and closed in February 1989, have pleaded guilty to felony charges.
