Bank of New England To Sell Rhode Island Unit for $75 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/05/90

BOSTON -- Bank of New England Corp. agreed to sell its Rhode Island subsidiary to Citizens Financial Group, a unit of the Royal Bank of Scotland Group PLC, for $75 million, finishing a program of asset sales that has shrunk the troubled regional bank by 30% in the past eight months. The sale will give the bank holding company $25 million in profit. The company, which now has $23 billion in assets, is still working for ways to raise much needed capital, including a major swap of its debt for equity.

Kent Price, chief financial officer, said the $75 million is slated for the bank's Massachusetts subsidiary, Bank of New England N.A., where the combination of low equity and problem loans, mostly in real estate, has been most serious.

At the end of the second quarter, the bank's overall capital ratio, a comparison of equity to assets that regulators use to measure a bank's health, was 1.64%, well below the 3% level regulators ordered the bank to meet by May 31, 1990. Healthy banks have levels of 5% or more. The capital level of the Massachusetts subsidiary, which has been hovering near 1%, will be raised to about 2% by the infusion of cash from the Rhode Island deal, according to Mr. Price.

Bondholders have $706 million in debentures that cost the bank more than $60 million a year in interest payments. To induce bondholders to exchange their debt for equity -- a move that would ease the bank's capital problem -- the bank needs to offer some cash. With the $75 million from sale of the Rhode Island subsidiary now spoken for, it is unclear where that cash could come from. Meanwhile, the bank has an interest payment of $12.3 million due on Sept. 15.

The bank, which reported a $1.1 billion loss last year, is struggling under a huge portfolio of nonperforming assets that currently total $2.7 billion. Those assets bring no return while costing the bank about $250 million in interest payments to customers who deposited that money.

In the deal announced yesterday , Citizens Financial Group acquired $900 million in deposits and $600 million in selected loans from the Rhode Island subsidiary's portfolio. The Bank of New England will retain about $220 million of the subsidiary's loans. Mr. Price said some of them were troubled loans, but he would not specify how many.
