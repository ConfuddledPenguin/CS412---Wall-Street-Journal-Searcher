Business Brief -- Allied-Signal Inc.: Garrett Fluid Unit Awarded Canadair Systems Contracts

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/05/90

Allied-Signal Inc. said Garrett Fluid Systems, a unit of its Allied-Signal Aerospace Co. subsidiary, has been awarded contracts with a potential value of about $60 million to provide systems for a new 50-passenger Canadair jet. A spokesman for Allied-Signal Aerospace said the projected contract value reflects the company's expectation that Canadair will build several hundred of this new type of aircraft.

Garrett Fluid Systems will develop and produce the main engine-starter system, among other components, for the new twinengine jetliner, which is scheduled to enter service in 1992.

Allied-Signal, which is based in Morris Township, N.J., has businesses in aerospace, automotive products and engineered materials. Garrett Fluid Systems, Tempe, Ariz., is a major supplier of aircraft starters.
