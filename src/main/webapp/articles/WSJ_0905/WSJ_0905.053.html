Healthco Agrees To Be Acquired By Hicks Muse

  • Author: Suzanne Alexander
  • Date: 09/05/90

BOSTON -- Healthco International Inc., in the midst of a proxy fight, said it agreed to be acquired by Dallas buy-out specialists Hicks, Muse & Co. for $19.25 a share in cash, or about $250 million. But dissident shareholders -- who are seeking control of Healthco's board -- immediately criticized the price as "ludicrously" low.

"I am livid at the treatment of the shareholders and the sale of this company at such a low price," said Emanuel Pearlman, executive general partner at New York-based Gemini Partners L.P. Gemini, an investment concern that owns 9.96% of Healthco's shares, is seeking to elect a slate of seven directors at Healthco's annual meeting tomorrow.

A person close to Healthco, noting that the company has been seeking offers for several months, said the Hicks Muse offer was the best that was made and that Healthco considers it fair. In national over-the-counter trading yesterday, Healthco's shares closed at $17.875, down 37.5 cents.

In a filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission, Gemini has said that Healthco, a dental equipment concern, should be sold or financially restructured, although it doesn't intend to make a bid for the company. Gemini holds 770,100 Healthco common shares, bought at an average price of $13.73 a share.

Mr. Pearlman added that if Gemini's candidates are elected to the board, they will "work to get a better price." Mr. Pearlman wouldn't say what price Gemini would consider acceptable or whether Gemini plans to vote against the proposed transaction, which is subject to shareholder approval. Shareholders won't vote on the acquisition at the annual meeting tomorrow, but instead at a meeting sometime this fall, Healthco said.

Healthco said the acquisition will "significantly benefit Healthco's future growth and development" and maximize "the value of our stockholders' investment." The company added that no management participation was included in the buy-out.

In the first half, Healthco had net income of $3 million, or 39 cents a share, on revenue of $115.8 million. A year earlier, net was $2.8 million, or 36 cents a share, on revenue of $108.8 million.

Thomas O. Hicks, chairman and chief executive officer of Hicks Muse, wouldn't comment on the acquisition. But the Healthco deal is in keeping with Mr. Hicks' stated goal of buying $3 billion of companies over the next two or three years. Mr. Hicks and his partners have already assembled a war chest of $170 million from institutions, Dallas companies and wealthy individuals, and are shooting to boost that buy-out fund to $300 million.

Before Mr. Hicks split with longtime partner Robert Haas last year, Hicks, Haas & Co. negotiated a dozen deals in three years and transformed $90 million of investments into cash and equity valued at $1.7 billion.
