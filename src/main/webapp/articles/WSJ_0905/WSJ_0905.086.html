McCarthy Securities To Sell 75% Stake To Sanwa of Japan

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/05/90

TORONTO -- Sanwa Bank said it agreed to purchase a 75% stake in McCarthy Securities Ltd., making it the first Japanese bank to acquire a majority interest in a Canadian securities firm. The offer comes at a time when Canadian investment firms are struggling. Prudential-Bache Securities Inc. sold its Canadian unit in June, citing continued losses, and Merrill Lynch Canada Inc. sold its retail operations in January. Of the Toronto Stock Exchange's 75 member firms, only 28 managed to eke out a profit in the first quarter of this year.

Still, Nobua Noujima, senior vice president at Sanwa Bank's Canadian operations, said the interest of Japanese investors in the Canadian market is growing and his company wanted direct access to Canadian securities.

"Canada is a very interesting market," Mr. Noujima said. "Canadian bonds in particular are more and more interesting to Japanese investors."

Terms of the acquisition weren't disclosed. The shares will be acquired from McCarthy employees, who will retain the remaining 25% stake. Leighton W. McCarthy, founder, chairman and chief executive officer of McCarthy, is the major holder of that 25%, Sanwa Bank said.

Mr. McCarthy will remain at the head of the company, which is to be called Sanwa McCarthy Securities Ltd. The purchase is subject to the approval of governmental authorities in both Canada and Japan. A Sanwa representative will be appointed to the seven-member McCarthy board.

McCarthy is one of the few remaining independent brokerage houses in Canada following recent acquisitions of securities firms by Canadian banks and mergers among smaller firms. Mr. McCarthy said Sanwa Bank approached McCarthy about the merger. He said that while the company wasn't looking for a partner, it was "feeling the pressure" of being a small, independent firm and realized the merger was necessary for growth. The company employs 62 people and has 4 million Canadian dollars (US$3.5 million) in capital.

Sanwa Bank, with assets of US$387 billion, said it will make "significant additional capital" available to Sanwa McCarthy.
