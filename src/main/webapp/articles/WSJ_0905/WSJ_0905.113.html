Dividend News: Equimark Suspends 3rd-Quarter Dividend For $470,000 Savings

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/05/90

PITTSBURGH -- Ailing Equimark Corp. suspended its third-quarter cash dividend on the bank holding company's common stock. Equimark had paid four cents a share each quarter since the second quarter of 1988. The move will save Equimark $470,000. The company's chief financial officer, Michael Jehle, said the board voted against the cash dividend because of Equimark's earnings decline. The bank had a $45.2 million first-half loss before one-time gains.

The announcement follows Friday's disclosure that the Tisch family has acquired a 6.8% stake in Equimark. A spokesman for the Tisch family company, FLF Associates, declined to comment on the dividend cutback.

In heavy composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange, Equimark shares fell 67.5 cents to $5.

Equimark said it will continue to pay the same dividends on three series of the company's preferred stock.
