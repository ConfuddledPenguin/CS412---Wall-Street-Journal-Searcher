McDonnell Douglas Wins Pact to Build 40 Jets for Kuwait

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/05/90

WASHINGTON -- McDonnell Douglas Corp. won a $140 million contract to build 40 F/A-18 Hornet jet aircraft for the government of Kuwait, the Defense Department said. The contract covers equipment purchases that were worked out several years ago under the foreign military sales program, the Pentagon said. But the agreement was just recently signed with Kuwait's exiled government, which fled the country after the Aug. 2 invasion by Iraq.

The sale is proceeding because "the government of Kuwait still exists," and the U.S. hopes the exiled government will regain its territory from Iraq, a Pentagon spokesman said.

The F/A-18 performs both fighter and attack roles. The contract, which is to be completed in September 1993, involves two versions of the aircraft, 32 F/A-18Cs and eight F/A-18Ds.

A separate contract, also being awarded to McDonnell Douglas, covers a $5.4 million order for technical publications for the Kuwaiti F/A-18s.

In other Pentagon awards, Vitro Services Corp. got a $34.5 million Army contract for operation of the development test center range.

Charles Stark Draper Laboratories Inc. won a $29.5 million Air Force contract for missile technical support.

Boeing Co. received an $18.3 million Air Force contract for radio sets.

Ocean Line of Azores Inc. was issued a $13.1 million Navy contract for cargo transport.
