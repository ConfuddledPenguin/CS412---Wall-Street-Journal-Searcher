Who's News: Suffield Financial Is Facing Lawsuit by Officers of Unit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/05/90

SUFFIELD, Conn. -- Suffield Financial Corp. said the president and chief executive officer of its Coastal Savings Bank unit filed suit against Suffield Financial in Maine Superior Court for attempting to dismiss them. Suffield Financial, a bank holding company, voted in late July to negotiate, by mutual consent, the termination of Richard Paige, the chief executive, and James McKay, the president, of Coastal Savings.

Unsuccessful in its negotiations, Suffield then voted to remove the directors of Coastal Bancorp, a subsidiary of Suffield Financial and parent of Coastal Savings, and replace them with new directors. The new Coastal Bancorp board has now called a shareholders meeting in order to consider the removal of the directors of Coastal Savings.

Suffield said it took such action because "a change in Coastal's management would enhance the future profitability of Coastal Savings." While Coastal Savings is "financially sound," the bank hasn't been as profitable as it could be, said Suffield Financial.

Messrs. Paige and McKay, who are joined in their suit by certain other directors of Coastal Savings, said in their suit that the actions of Suffield Financial are "illegal and invalid."

Suffield has removed the lawsuit to the U.S. District Court in Maine.
