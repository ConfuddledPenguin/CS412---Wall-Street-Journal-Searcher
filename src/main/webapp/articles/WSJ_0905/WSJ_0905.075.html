Mitsubishi to Recall About 167,000 Vehicles For EPA Violation

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/05/90

WASHINGTON -- Mitsubishi Motors Corp. is recalling about 167,000 cars and light trucks to fix emission problems that violate federal air-pollution standards, the Environmental Protection Agency said. The automaker is recalling 92,000 1987 model Mitsubishi Mirages, Dodge Colts and Plymouth Colts -- all with 1.5-liter engines -- for emitting too much carbon monoxide in the exhaust because of faulty vacuum-control valves.

The government said Mitsubishi also began recalling 75,000 light trucks -- with two-liter engines -- for emitting excessive carbon monoxide and hydrocarbons. Models affected by the recall are the 1986 two-wheel-drive Mitsubishi truck and Dodge Ram 50, and the 1986 and 1987 fourwheel drive Dodge Colt Vista and Plymouth Colt Vista.

The EPA detected the problems during routine auto-emissions tests. Mitsubishi will make repairs free of charge. All the vehicles were built by Mitsubishi, but the majority were sold by Chrysler Corp. under its Dodge or Plymouth nameplates. Owners, who are to be notified by mail, are advised by the EPA to schedule repairs with Mitsubishi or Chrysler dealers.
