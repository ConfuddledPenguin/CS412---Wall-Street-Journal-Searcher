Britain's Official Reserves

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/05/90

LONDON -- Britain's official reserves rose an underlying 366 million dollars (#692 million) August from July, the U.K. Treasury said Tuesday. This follows an underlying rise of $173 million in July and a fall of $405 million in August 1989.

Market economists had predicted a rise of $100 million to $200 million during August.

The underlying figure excludes official repayments and new borrowing. Including these transactions, the reserves showed a rise of $455 million in August, compared with a fall of $172 million in July and a fall of $423 million in August 1989.

At the end of August, Britain's reserves of gold, foreign currency, special drawing rights (SDRs) and European Currency Units (ECUs) stood at $39.297 billion, up from $38.842 billion in July and down from $43.208 billion in August 1989, the Treasury said.
