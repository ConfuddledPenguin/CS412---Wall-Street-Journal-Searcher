Boeing Gets $180 Million Order

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/05/90

SEATTLE -- Boeing Co. said it received an order valued at $180 million for three aircraft from Lauda Air of Austria, a private charter airline with some scheduled flights that was founded by Nicki Lauda, a former champion race car driver. The aircraft maker and aerospace concern said Lauda ordered one 767-300 extended-range jet and two 737-400s. In addition, Lauda confirmed an order placed this year but never announced for a 737-400 valued at about $37 million, Boeing said.
