International: South Korea to Contribute $220 Million to Gulf Effort

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/25/90

SEOUL, South Korea -- The South Korean government, responding to U.S. pressure, said it would donate about $220 million in cash, food, transportation and military equipment to the multinational effort against Iraq in the Persian Gulf. Treasury Secretary Nicholas Brady and Undersecretary of State Richard Solomon visited Seoul in recent weeks to argue for at least $450 million in aid. But a Seoul official said Korea's economic problems, exacerbated by recent floods, limited the size of the contribution.

Korea said it would provide $120 million in cash, air and sea transportation, and supplies such as military uniforms, tents and gas masks. Jordan, Turkey and Egypt would receive 30,000 metric tons of rice valued at $10 million and could apply for $40 million of low-interest loans. Korea also would supply $49.5 million of "necessity of life" goods to the three nations.
