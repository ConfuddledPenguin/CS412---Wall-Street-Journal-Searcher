Letters to the Editor: Worm's-Eye View

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/25/90

Your Aug. 24 article "Good News for Worms: Study Finds Genetic Clue to Aging in Nematode" notes that the recent successful mutating of a single gene in the roundworm, "Caenorhabditis elegans," creates "what might be called the Methuselahs of the annelid world." May I point out that annelids, which are segmented worms (earthworms, etc.) belong to an entirely different phylum than the roundworms, which are nematodes. The nematodes are a giant step below on the evolutionary ladder from the annelids. John A. Stone

Venice, Fla.
