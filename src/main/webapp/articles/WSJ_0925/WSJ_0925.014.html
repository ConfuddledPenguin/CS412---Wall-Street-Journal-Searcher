Letters to the Editor: Critic Paid to Dull Boston Globe's Luster

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/25/90

In some editions of your Sept. 10 issue, in a story about Affiliated Publications Inc., a Boston Phoenix media critic was quoted. It should be noted that he is paid to bash the Boston Globe and does his job well. We would simply like to present our side to the critic's assessment about the Globe lacking luster by pointing to the record:

One day before the article appeared, the Globe added an investment subsection and expanded its overall Business section; expanded its Real Estate section, and added baseball writer Peter Gammons to its roster.

One week before, the Globe opened a bureau in Berlin, only a few months after opening a bureau in Moscow. A week before that, we introduced a special Movie section. This month, we'll start our fifth zoned section in 30 months, and next month we'll begin a special Music section.

Meanwhile, our latest circulation is up 11,000 daily and 12,500 on Sunday over last year.

If that's lacking in luster -- in a New England recession, no less -- then we're guilty as charged.

John S. Driscoll

Editor

The Boston Globe

Boston
