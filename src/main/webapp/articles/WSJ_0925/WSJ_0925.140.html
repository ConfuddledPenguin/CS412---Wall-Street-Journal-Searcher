Business Brief -- Emerson Electric Co.: Board Votes to Spin Off Firm's Defense Operations

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/25/90

Emerson Electric Co., St. Louis, said its board approved a plan to spin off its defense operations as a company to be known as Esco Electronics Corp. Under the planned distribution, Emerson holders of record as of Oct. 5 will receive one Esco share for each 20 shares of Emerson. The company said the Securities and Exchange Commission cleared the spinoff. The Esco shares will be treated as a taxable distribution, it said.

Sales for the latest fiscal year of the units that will comprise Esco totaled about $600 million, or 8% of Emerson's overall sales. Pretax earnings were about $15 million, or 1.5% of the parent's overall pre-tax profit, Emerson said.

Emerson also said it won't be suspended from competing for U.S. government contracts. In May, Emerson pleaded guilty in federal court in St. Louis to inflating cost proposals on certain military contracts, agreeing to pay $14 million. Since the settlement, the company's status as a potential bidder had been under review.
