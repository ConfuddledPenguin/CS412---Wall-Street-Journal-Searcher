International Brief -- Scottish & Newcastle Breweries: Holding in Dutch Concern, Center Parcs, Lifted to 80%

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/25/90

Scottish & Newcastle Breweries PLC of Edinburgh, Scotland, a brewing and leisure concern, said it raised its stake in the Dutch leisure concern Center Parcs N.V. to 80% of the ordinary shares outstanding. The two companies said last week that they were in talks that could lead to Scottish & Newcastle's buying all the Center Parcs shares that it didn't already own.

The purchases last Friday involved 747,146, or 5.2%, of Center Parcs's ordinary shares, and 39.1% of its 5.75% sterling convertible subordinated bonds and 4.2% of its 3.75% guilder convertible subordinated bonds. Scottish & Newcastle said its holding of all classes of Center Parcs securities now stands at 70%, up from 60.3%. The price paid for the additional stake wasn't disclosed. Last Thursday, the companies said Scottish & Newcastle would pay about #180 million ($332.3 million) to acquire the balance of Center Parcs.

Though the initial terms of Scottish & Newcastle's friendly stake last year included a three-year standstill pact, Center Parcs said last week that it would benefit from being a Scottish & Newcastle unit.
