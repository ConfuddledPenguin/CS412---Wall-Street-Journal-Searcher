Maryland, Lottery Agency Discriminated, U.S. Alleges

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/25/90

WASHINGTON -- The Justice Department filed a civil rights lawsuit against Maryland and its lottery agency, charging that a state minority-hiring program discriminated against white men. The suit, filed in federal court in Baltimore, alleged that the lottery agency violated the Civil Rights Act of 1964 by refusing to consider white male applicants for four jobs. Maryland officials couldn't be reached to comment.

The Justice Department didn't specify in its filing what type of jobs were covered by the minority-hiring program. The complaint accused Maryland of "refusing to hire on the basis of their race and sex qualified white males."

The department said in its suit that the federal Equal Employment Opportunity Commission "found reasonable cause to believe the charge was true," attempted unsuccessfully to reach a settlement, and finally referred the matter to the Justice Department's Civil Rights Division.
