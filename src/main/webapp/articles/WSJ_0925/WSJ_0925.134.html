Business Brief -- Helene Curtis Industries Inc.: Net on Ongoing Operations Fell 47% in Second Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/25/90

Helene Curtis Industries Inc., citing costs of product introductions, particularly the slower-than-expected launching of its Finesse line in West Germany, said earnings from continuing operations dropped 47% to $3.5 million, or 38 cents a share, in the second quarter ended Aug. 31. Net income fell to $2.1 million, or 23 cents a share, from $6.6 million, or 71 cents a share, a year earlier.

In the quarter, the personal-care products company took a charge of 15 cents a share arising from the sale of its beauty-supply distribution business. It said that business accounted for $45 million in sales and a loss of $600,000, or seven cents a share, in the lastest fiscal year. The company's sales and earnings were adjusted to reflect the sale.

Net sales from continuing operations jumped 22% to $228.8 million. The company, based in Chicago, said its consumer business remains strong, led by such product launches as the introduction of Degree anti-perspirant in the U.S. and the Salon Selectives brand in Japan.
