Marketing & Media: Westwood One, Infinity Sign Pact on Radio Stations

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/25/90

LOS ANGELES, Calif. -- Westwood One Inc. said it signed an agreement with New York-based Infinity Broadcasting Corp. to bring on 14 of Infinity's 17 radio stations in the top U.S. radio markets as Westwood One affiliates. Terms were't disclosed. William Battison, Westwood One's president and chief financial officer, said the affiliation will give the company a wider audience for its programming and allow it to sell its commercials at a greater premium. "This will clearly add significant audience in the youth demographic, and it will help our programming business," he said. Norman Pattiz, Westwood One's chairman and chief executive officer, noted that the agreement "assures that we will continue to have strong and predictable ratings growth."

The Infinity stations -- eight of which are located in the top 10 radio markets in the country -- are WXRK-FM and WZRC-AM in New York; KROQ-FM in Los Angeles; WBCN-FM in Boston; WJJD-AM and WJMK-FM in Chicago; WYSP-FM in Philadelphia; KOME-FM in San Francisco/San Jose; WOMC-FM in Detroit; WJFK-FM in Washington, D.C.; WQYK-AM and FM in Tampa, Fla.; and WLIF-AM and FM in Baltimore.

The Infinity stations will become affiliates of either the Westwood Mutual Broadcasting System, the Westwood NBC Radio Network, the Westwood One News & Entertainment Radio Network, or the Source, Westwood One's young-adult network. Westwood one is the nation's largest distributor of radio programming and the second-largest radio network.
