Business Brief -- News Corp.: Company Unveils Details Of Capital Restructuring

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/25/90

News Corp. disclosed details of a planned restructuring of its share capital, aimed at fueling its expansion while keeping the company firmly under the control of its chief executive officer, Rupert Murdoch. Directors are asking shareholders to approve a proposal to alter the Sydney, Australia, company's authorized share capital to create a three-tiered structure. That structure would be composed of common stock, preferred limited-voting ordinary shares, and nonvoting ordinary shares. Currently, the company has only full-voting ordinary shares on issue and preference shares, issued through subsidiaries, that are redeemable, nonvoting and basically debt instruments.

News Corp.'s directors said they don't want to issue more ordinary shares because it "might have the effect of diluting" the Murdoch family's 45% controlling interest.

If shareholders approve the plan at the company's annual meeting next month -- a prospect made more likely because Mr. Murdoch's family can vote its stake -- News Corp. directors said they will make a free scrip issue to existing shareholders.
