New Securities Issues

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/25/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report. CORPORATE

ITT Corp. -- $150 million of 8 3/4% notes due Oct. 1, 1992, priced at 99.919 to yield 8.795%. The non-callable notes were priced at a spread of 63 basis points above the Treasury's two-year when-issued note. Rated single-A-2 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and single-A by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through Goldman, Sachs & Co.

Burlington Northern Railroad Co. -- $118 million two-part issue of pass-through certificates. The first part, about $68 million of 9.81% certificates maturing March 26, 2004, was priced at par. The issue has an average life of about 9.7 years. The second part, about $50 million of 10.13% certificates due March 26, 2010, also was priced at par. The issue has an average life of about 17.3 years. Rated double-A-3 by Moody's and single-A-plus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Morgan Stanley & Co.

Commercial Credit Corp. -- $100 million of 9 1/8% notes due Oct. 1, 1993, priced at par. The non-callable notes were priced at 78 basis points above the Treasury's three-year note. Rated single-A-2 by Moody's and single-A by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Smith Barney, Harris Upham & Co.

MORTGAGES

Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $500.2 million of Remic mortgage securities offered in eight classes by Bear, Stearns & Co. The offering, Series FHLMC Gold 1009, is backed by Freddie Mac 9 1/2% gold participation certificates. The offering consists of three fixed-rate classes, one floating-rate class, two inverse-floating-rate classes, one interestonly class and a residual. The collateral has a weighted average coupon rate of 10.125% and weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29.75 years. Further details weren't immediately available.

Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $500 million of Remic mortgage securities offered in 21 classes by First Boston Corp. The offering, Series FHLMC Gold 1010, is backed by Freddie Mac 9% gold participation certificates. The offering consists of seven PAC (planned amortization class) classes, five VADM (very accurately determined maturities) classes, five fixed-rate classes, one principal-only class and a small interest-only class, among others. The collateral has a weighted average coupon rate of 9.75% and weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29.50 years. Other details weren't immediately available.

Collateralized Mortgage Obligation Trust 64 -- $500 million of Remic mortgage securities offered in 11 classes by Salomon Brothers Inc. The offering is backed by Government National Mortgage Association 9% securities with a weighted average coupon rate of 9% and weighted average remaining term to maturity of 26.33 years. Among classes for which details were available, spreads ranged from 60 basis points above three-year Treasury notes for securities with an average life of 3.6 years to 140 basis points above 30-year Treasury bonds for securities with an average life of 19.5 years. There is an optional redemption at par when 5% of the bonds remain outstanding.

ASSET-BACKED

Norwest Master Trust Series B -- $400 million of 9.05% credit-card certificates priced at 99.9375 to yield 9.11% through Morgan Stanley & Co. and Norwest Investment Services Inc. The yield is 75 basis points over the yield on three-year Treasury notes. The securities are collateralized with Visa and MasterCard receivables sold by Norwest Bank Iowa, a unit of Norwest Corp. A triple-A rating from Moody's and S&P is expected, based on the quality of the underlying loans and a letter of credit from Union Bank of Switzerland covering 12% of the deal. Expected maturity is March 15, 1994. Legal final maturity is Sept. 15, 1995. The securities have a three-year average life.

EUROBONDS

Bradford & Bingley Building Society (U.K.) -- #150 million of floating-rate notes due October 1997 via Credit Suisse First Boston Ltd. Interest at three-month London interbank offered rate plus eight basis points, fixed reoffer price 99.80 (issue price par). Fees 24 basis points.

Peninsular & Orient Steam Navigation Co. (U.K.) -- 100 million Australian dollars of 14.8% Eurobonds due Oct. 11, 1995, at issue price 102 via Samuel Montagu & Co. Fees 2.

National Australia Bank Ltd. (Australia) -- A$60 million of 14% Eurobonds due Oct. 18, 1994, at issue price 101 3/4 via Dresdner Bank AG. Fees 1 3/4.

Credit Local de France (France) -- $100 million of floating-rate notes due Oct. 12, 1997, priced at par, paying 6.25 basis points below six-month Libor via Credit Lyonnais Euro-Securities Ltd. Fees 0.10.
