U.S. Budget Deficit Hits $241.72 Billion For First 11 Months

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/25/90

WASHINGTON -- With just one more month left to be recorded in the federal fiscal year, the deficit amounted to $241.72 billion, the Treasury said. The deficit target for fiscal 1990, which ends Sunday, set by the Gramm-Rudman deficit-reduction law was $100 billion. In the first 11 months of last year, the deficit totaled $145.81 billion.

The deficit for the month of August was $52.75 billion, more than double the $22.15 billion reported in August last year. Spending in August was increased by about $25 billion because wage and benefit payments normally made in September were made early to avoid Labor Day weekend delays, the Treasury said. As a result, the government may run a modest surplus in September.

Revenues in August were $78.49 billion, up 3.1% from $76.14 billion last August. Outlays were $131.24 billion, up 33.5% from $98.29 billion.

So far this year, federal receipts totaled $928.59 billion, up 4.2% from last year. Individual income taxes are 4.8% ahead of last year, and Social Security payroll taxes are 7.2% ahead of last year. But corporate tax receipts are running 9.2% behind last year's totals, primarily because profits are down.

Outlays totaled $1.17 billion in the first 11 months, up 12.8% from last year. The Treasury said the Resolution Trust Corp., the primary arm of the thrift industry clean-up operation, has spent $37.78 billion so far this year.
