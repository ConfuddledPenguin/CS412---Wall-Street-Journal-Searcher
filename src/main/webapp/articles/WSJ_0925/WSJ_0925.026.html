Marketing & Media: NBC News Mulls About 25 Layoffs; Pauley Signs Pact

  • Author: Kevin Goldman
  • Date: 09/25/90

NEW YORK -- NBC News may lay off about 25 employees, or 2.5% of its work force, according to a memo from NBC News President Michael Gartner distributed to the division. No timetable was set. Separately, anchor Jane Pauley signed a new five-year contract with National Broadcasting Co., a unit of General Electric Co., and Mr. Gartner said in the memo that her summer series, "Real Life with Jane Pauley" would return as a regularly scheduled weekly series. The program, which will serve as a mid-season replacement for a failing NBC entertainment series, is likely to return as a half-hour program, as opposed to the one-hour length in its summer run.

Meanwhile, CBS News said it would begin a nightly half-hour program at 11:30 p.m., beginning Monday, to be anchored by correspondents Lesley Stahl and Charles Kuralt that would compete with ABC News's "Nightline." Unlike "Nightline," however, the CBS News broadcast, originating in Washington and New York, will not be a single-topic show.

CBS faces stiff opposition from its 200-plus affiliates, however. Only 80% of its affiliates broadcast the current late-night offerings of such action adventure shows as "Wiseguy." In comparison, NBC has a 99% clearance rate from its affiliates for the "Tonight Show Starring Johnny Carson."

Although details of Ms. Pauley's contract couldn't be determined, individuals within the NBC network said her total contract could be worth about $7 million.

In the memo, Mr. Gartner said some employees would be reassigned to Ms. Pauley's show or an expanded investigative journalism unit. "Nevertheless," Mr. Gartner wrote, "still others whose skills don't match those needed for the new shows or any other shows, perhaps as many as 25, may be laid off." An NBC News spokeswoman declined to comment.

NBC News, with an annual operating budget of about $250 million, has about 1,000 employees, the smallest number of the major broadcast network news divisions. In comparison, CBS News, a unit of CBS Inc., has about 1,370 employees and ABC News, a division of Capital Cities/ABC Inc., has about 1,200 employees.
