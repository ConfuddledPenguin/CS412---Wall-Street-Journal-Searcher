International Brief -- Foersaekringsbolaget SPP: Accord Is Set to Increase Gota Voting Stake to 48%

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/25/90

Foersaekringsbolaget SPP of Sweden, a big mutual insurer, said it agreed to raise its voting stake in Gota AB, a financial services concern, to 48% from 4% in a transaction valued at 4.6 billion Swedish kronor ($794.4 million). The move continues the recently intensified consolidation of bank ownership in Scandinavia. SPP would be the first Swedish insurer to wrest a major stake in a bank, as Gota has securities trading and financial units but its crown jewel is Gotabanken, Sweden's fourth-biggest bank. The sellers are Proventus AB, a Swedish investment company that owns 29.8% of Gota, and Kansallis Osake Pankki, Finland's biggest bank, which holds a 14.4% voting stake.

SPP officials said the proposed link with Gota would provide the distribution clout needed to increase sales of SPP insurance and investment products. Insurance coverage for individuals, especially so-called unit-linked policies recently introduced in Sweden, is expected to become the fastest growing and most competitive corner of financial services in coming years.
