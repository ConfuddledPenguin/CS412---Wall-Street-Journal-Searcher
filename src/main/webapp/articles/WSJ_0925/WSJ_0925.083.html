Letters to the Editor: GM Kills the Goose After Egging It On

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/25/90

Regarding your Aug. 29 page-one article "Credibility Gap: UAW and Big Three Face Mutual Mistrust as Auto Talks Heat Up" on General Motors Corp.'s actions at the Flint, Mich., truck-body parts stamping plant: Can management at that plant be deemed anything but stupid? Management obviously had no conception of the optimum production rate of its manpower or equipment. It gave its employees an incentive to maximize production. When the employees doubled the production rate, according to the article, and held the labor cost constant, management rewarded the employees by paying them half as much for doubled production. Is this not known as killing the goose that laid the golden egg?

Could this stupidity be why it takes six more man-hours for GM to produce a car than it does at Japanese plants?

Ervin E. Frauenhoff

Former Stamping Press Operator

Los Angeles
