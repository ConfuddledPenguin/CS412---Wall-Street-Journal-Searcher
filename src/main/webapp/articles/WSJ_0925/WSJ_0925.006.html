Letters to the Editor: Baptist `Moderates' Are the Real Radicals

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/25/90

Your page-one Aug. 24 "What's News" item about Southern Baptists would have your readers believe that the "fundamentalists," those rascals who took control of the unsuspecting Southern Baptist Convention, represent the radical departure from the historical creedal viewpoint. A literal interpretation of Scripture long has been a cardinal tenet of Southern Baptists, along with a belief in its verbal, plenary inspiration. The characterization of the opponents of this orthodoxy as "moderates" is a further attempt to position the fundamentalists as holding the aberrant view. It is unfortunate that you cloud the truth: that a liberal group is attempting to pull the Southern Baptists from their traditional roots. The so-called moderates are staging the coup, not the fundamentalists.

Anthony Gilliam

Marlton, N.J.
