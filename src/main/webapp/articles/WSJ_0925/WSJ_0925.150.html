Who's News: Nelson, Head of Norton, Replaced by Saint Gobain

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/25/90

WORCESTER, Mass. -- John M. Nelson, the longtime president, chief executive officer and chairman of Norton Co. who said the acquisition by the Cie de Saint Gobain S.A. would not result in layoffs, was replaced yesterday by the French industrial giant. Mr. Nelson, who helped steer the company away from the hostile bidder BTR PLC in favor of Saint Gobain, will become a consultant to Norton, a maker of abrasives, plastics, ceramics and chemical-process products. The company had 1989 revenue of $1.53 billion.

His successor will be Michel L. Besson, who has been vice chairman, president and chief executive officer of CertainTeed Corp. in Valley Forge, Pa. That company, acquired by Saint Gobain in 1987, is a maker of building products.

Mr. Besson will now run both CertainTeed and Norton for Saint Gobain, according to the spokesman.

Mr. Nelson could not be reached for comment.
