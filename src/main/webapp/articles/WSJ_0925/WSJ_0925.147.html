International Brief -- Pinault S.A.: Bid Made to Acquire Rest Of CFAO in Stock Swap

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/25/90

Pinault S.A., a wood and paper concern, unveiled a friendly bid for the 67% of Cie. Francaise d'Afrique Occidentale it doesn't already own in a share swap that values all of the distribution and trading company's common at about 6.04 billion French francs ($1.14 billion). Francois Pinault, chairman of both French concerns, said the move is aimed at building an international industrial and services group focused on distribution, services such as equipment leasing, Pinault's core wood holdings and CFAO's international trading arm. He said the combined concern would have 1990 revenue of about 32 billion francs.

Under the merger bid, Pinault will offer six of its shares for every five CFAO shares outstanding. That parity represents a 37% premium over the last price quoted for CFAO, before both issues were suspended yesterday, pending the bid.

After payments to minority interests in 1989, Pinault earned 584 million francs on revenue of 10.19 billion francs, and CFAO earned 302 million francs on revenue of 31.185 billion francs.
