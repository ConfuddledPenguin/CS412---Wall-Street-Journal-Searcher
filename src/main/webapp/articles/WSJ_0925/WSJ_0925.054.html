International: Bank of Commerce Takeover

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/25/90

KUALA LUMPUR, Malaysia -- Bank of Commerce Bhd., controlled by Malaysia's dominant political party, announced a proposed takeover and subsequent partial merger with United Asian Bank Bhd. Bank of Commerce said Finance Minister Daim Zainuddin approved the complex arrangement with UAB, which was taken over in 1987 by Bank Negara, Malaysia's central bank.

The transaction would create the fifth-largest locally incorporated domestic bank in terms of shareholder funds, amounting to 210.6 million Malaysian dollars (US$78 million), and the sixth-largest in terms of assets (M$6.8 billion) and deposits (M$3.9 billion).

The arrangement effectively would make Bank of Commerce an investment holding company, among whose primary assets would be the sharply enlarged domestic banking operations under UAB.
