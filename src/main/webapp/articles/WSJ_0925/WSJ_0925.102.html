Business Brief -- Philadelphia Electric Co.: Company Expects to Take A Charge Against Earnings

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/25/90

Philadelphia Electric Co. said it expects to take a one-time charge of $249 million, or 70 cents a share, against third-quarter earnings to reflect the cost of an early retirement program. The utility said that 1,909 of its 11,273 employees, or 73% of those eligible, decided to take early retirement under the plan, announced this summer.

In the 1989 third quarter, Philadelphia Electric reported net income of about $206 million, or 87 cents a share.

The company said it expects the retirements to save $75 million a year in salaries and benefits by 1993.
