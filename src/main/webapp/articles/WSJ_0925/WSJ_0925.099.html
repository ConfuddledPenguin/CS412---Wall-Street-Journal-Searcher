Credit Ratings: Northern States Power Rating Is Decreased On $1.8 Billion Debt

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/25/90

Moody's Investors Service Inc. said it lowered the credit rating on about $1.8 billion of senior debt issued by Northern States Power Co., citing restrictive state regulation of the Minneapolis utility. The rating service said that the Minnesota Public Utilities Commission's decision last month to deny a $120.8 million rate increase to Northern States will "constrain the company's future financial performance." The company was also ordered to refund a related interim rate increase that took effect in January.

Chief financial officer James Doudiet said the downgrading was expected and "consistent with the evaluations of other agencies." The utility has petitioned the state regulator to reconsider its decision. In its request for a rehearing, the utility asked that it be granted a transitional rate increase to cover identifiable cost increases.

In denying the rate increase, the Minnesota commission said Northern States's "overall budgeting process is seriously deficient in accuracy and credibility."
