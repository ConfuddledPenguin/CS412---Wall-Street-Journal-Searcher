Chile, Banks Reach Pact on Rescheduling $1.8 Billion in Debt

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/25/90

WASHINGTON -- After just a few days of negotiations, Chile and its commercial bankers tentatively agreed to a proposed $1.8 billion debt rescheduling, and the nation said it soon will issue $320 million of bonds with the help of some of its major bank creditors. Chile, which in recent years has had the strongest economic growth in Latin America, hasn't borrowed in international credit markets in recent years but is trying to return to those markets now after several years of reform and strong export-led growth.

Because of its good economic performance, the nation has been lauded by many foreign bankers and economists. "We've been listening to the financial markets saying that Chile belongs to a different category" than other heavily indebted developing nations, said the nation's finance minister, Alejandro Foxley.

The proposed rescheduling agreement was reached between Chile and a representative committee of banks. The agreement, which still has to be ratified by the nation's bank creditors around the world, would extend $1.8 billion of debt principal payments for four years and continue an arrangement under which Chile pays interest once a year instead of twice a year. The $320 million Chile expects to raise would help the nation meet its financing needs over the next three years, Mr. Foxley said.
