Marketing Brief -- Weintraub Entertainment Group: Founder and Chief Resigns, Gets Accord With Warner

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/25/90

Jerry Weintraub joined the exodus of employees from Weintraub Entertainment Group, which earlier this month sought bankruptcy-law protection from creditors. Mr. Weintraub, the 52-year-old founder and majority shareholder of the ailing entertainment concern, resigned as chairman and chief executive officer. It wasn't clear what his future involvement with the private Los Angeles company would be.

Concurrent with Mr. Weintraub's resignation, Warner Brothers said he will produce movies for the Burbank, Calif., film studio under a "major long-term agreement." A spokesman for the Time Warner Inc. unit said details of the agreement weren't available.

Mr. Weintraub's assistant at Weintraub Entertainment directed questions about his successor to Kenneth Kleinberg, president and chief operating officer. Mr. Kleinberg didn't return telephone calls.
