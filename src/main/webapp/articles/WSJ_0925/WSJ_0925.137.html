Business Brief -- Chrysler Corp.: Firm to Recall Certain Cars From 1987, the EPA Says

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/25/90

Chrysler Corp. is recalling 106,000 of its 1987 cars to reduce excessive carbon-monoxide exhaust, the Environmental Protection Agency said. The affected models are the Plymouth Caravelle and Reliant; Dodge Aries, Daytona, 600 and Lancer; Chrysler New Yorker, LeBaron, LeBaron GTS and Town and Country Wagon. All have 2.5-liter, fuel-injected engines.

Cars emit excessive amounts of carbon monoxide in their exhaust as a result of incomplete fuel combustion. Too much carbon monoxide in the air reduces the amount of available oxygen, a situation particularly harmful to people with lung disease or anemia.

Chrysler agreed to the recall after the EPA discovered the problem during testing. The company is notifying only car owners who haven't already had their fuel injectors replaced under warranty.
