Civil Rights Groups Urge Rejection of Judge Souter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/25/90

WASHINGTON -- A major coalition of civil rights organizations urged the Senate to reject the nomination of Judge David Souter to the Supreme Court. The 180-member Leadership Conference on Civil Rights said in a two-page statement that Judge Souter hadn't shown "that he has a commitment to constitutional guarantees of individual rights and liberties."

A number of women's rights groups and abortion rights groups, all members of the Leadership Conference, previously announced their opposition to Judge Souter.

The Leadership Conference is the most influential civil rights lobby here, but its opposition isn't likely to signal real trouble for the nomination. The civil rights coalition isn't expected to mount the full-scale campaign that it did in 1987 against the nomination of Robert Bork. The decision to oppose Judge Souter wasn't made in time for the confirmation hearings, and the statement in opposition went virtually unnoticed when released Saturday.

Moreover, the opposition may be too late to have much impact. Approval by the 14-member Senate Judiciary Committee, scheduled to vote Thursday, is nearly ensured. Several committee members, whose votes against Mr. Bork were crucial, already have endorsed Judge Souter. These include Democrats Dennis DeConcini of Arizona and Howell Heflin of Alabama and Republican Arlen Specter of Pennsylvania.

The only senator who has announced opposition so far is Alan Cranston (D., Calif.), who isn't a member of the committee. The full Senate is expected to vote on the nomination next week.
