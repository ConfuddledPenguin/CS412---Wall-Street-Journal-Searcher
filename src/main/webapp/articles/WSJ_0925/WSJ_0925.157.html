Technology: EMC Corp. to Post Third-Quarter Profit, Sees Strong 4th Period

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/25/90

BOSTON -- EMC Corp. will report a third-quarter profit compared with a year-earlier loss, and expects "strong growth" in sales and earnings in the fourth quarter, Chairman Richard J. Egan said. Mr. Egan also said that a new computer disk storage technology EMC will introduce today "could add as much as $100 million" to revenue next year. The new data storage product is designed for mainframe computers.

If his forecast pans out, EMC would witness a reversal of two years of annual losses. In the most recent quarter, ended June 30, the company had net income of $2 million, or eight cents a share, on revenue of $42.7 million. In the year-ago second quarter, net income was $124,000, or one cent a share, on revenue of $34.6 million.

Mr. Egan says he expects third-quarter net income to be flat or "slightly ahead of" second-quarter results. In the 1989 third quarter, the Hopkinton, Mass., company reported a net loss of $3.6 million, or 16 cents a share, on revenue of $92 million.

Mr. Egan said gross margins have improved so far this year and that cost controls remain in place. He added that analysts' revenue estimate of $170 million to $180 million this year are "reasonable." In 1989, EMC reported revenue of $132.3 million.

The disk technology, known as RAID, or redundant arrays of inexpensive disks, combines a solid-state memory cache with dozens of small, high-capacity hard-disk drives. The arrays are smaller, use less power, and are less expensive than mainframe disk drives in use today. International Business Machines Corp., which dominates the mainframe market, also is working on the technology.

Mr. Egan says EMC will beat IBM to market when EMC begins volume shipment of RAID drives early next year.
