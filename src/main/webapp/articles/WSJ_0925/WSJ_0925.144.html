Business Brief -- Diceon Electronics Inc.: Losses for Fourth Quarter And Fiscal 1990 Expected

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/25/90

Diceon Electronics Inc. said it will have losses in its fourth quarter and year ending Saturday, including a $5 million charge related to consolidation of facilities and work force reductions. In fiscal 1989, the Irvine, Calif., maker of electronic circuit boards had a loss of $2.3 million, or 39 cents a share, on sales of $118.8 million.

Diceon said it plans to eliminate about 225 jobs, or 15% of its 1,500 employees, by year end. It also plans to halve its Chatsworth, Calif., production space to about 35,000 square feet.

Diceon said it has been consolidating its facilities in San Jose, Calif., since its acquisition of privately held NTI last year. Diceon expects to keep about 95,000 square feet of production space in that area after closing several buildings.

A Diceon spokesman said the company has been hurt by excess capacity and is "downsizing to become more efficient."
