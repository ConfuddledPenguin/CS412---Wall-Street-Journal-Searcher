Marketing & Media: `Dick Tracy' Video Is Slated for Rentals Prior to Christmas

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/25/90

BURBANK, Calif. -- Walt Disney Co.'s home video unit will release "Dick Tracy" about one week before Christmas, priced for the rental market at $94.95, according to individuals close to the situation. But in a twist, Disney will then provide extensive marketing support to retailers who want to slash prices and sell the used tapes to consumers about a month later.

The "rent and sell" program reflects Disney's ambivalence about whether "Dick Tracy" is more suited to the rental or sale markets. Last summer, video distributors said the film seemed a shoo-in to be priced at $20 to $25 for sale directly to consumers. It appeared to have all the attributes for "sell-through" -- big box office success, appeal to children, music, a distinctive visual style attractive to collectors -- and Disney executives were reported to have decided to release it in November as a sell-through title.

But "Dick Tracy" ran out of gas in theaters after the $100 million mark, while an earlier Disney release, "Pretty Woman," continued to win audiences all summer long. Disney market research showed that "Pretty Woman" was the strongest potential film for sell-through, especially to younger women.

"Pretty Woman" will join Disney's animated films "Peter Pan" and "The Little Mermaid" in stores during the Christmas buying season. Disney officials are said to feel the market will be too glutted for another major sell-through title, especially when other companies are releasing "Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles," "Total Recall" and other films.

Furthermore, Disney preferred not to wait until next year because its marketing campaign for "Dick Tracy" is losing voltage.
