Who's News: Horn & Hardart Co. Picks Catalog Chief As Its New President

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/25/90

NEW YORK -- Horn & Hardart Co., having largely refocused to become primarily a direct marketer through catalogs, will name Jack E. Rosenfeld president and chief executive officer. The 52-year-old Mr. Rosenfeld, who heads the company's Hanover Cos. catalog operations, succeeds Donald Schupak, 47, who took the three top posts at Horn & Hardart two years ago. Prior to that, Mr. Schupak had been Horn & Hardart's vice chairman, responsible for directing Hanover. He will remain as chairman for an interim period and will be a company consultant for three years thereafter.

Under Mr. Schupak, Horn & Hardart has been selling its restaurant and real estate holdings to focus on Hanover, which markets a range of goods through 22 catalogs. Recently, Horn & Hardart completed the sale of its Bojangles fast-food chain to a group including Sienna Holdings, a Los Angeles investment firm.

For 1990, Mr. Schupak forecasts Hanover will show more than $20 million of operating profit and $500 million of sales, indicating a doubling of sales and tripling of earnings from two years ago. Horn & Hardart as a whole reported a loss for the first half of 1990 of $5.7 million, or 41 cents a share, on revenue of $314.3 million.

Still to come in the restructuring, Mr. Schupak says, is a refinancing of $17 million of 11% debt and repayment or refinancing of $60 million of increasing-rate debt, currently at 10.5%. Both issues are due in October 1991. And the company must sell the last of its land and restaurant holdings, including some New York real estate and restaurants and the 80-unit West Coast restaurant chain International King's Table.

Mr. Rosenfeld says Hanover probably will reduce the number of its catalogs slightly, although he wasn't specific.
