Portman Says Liens Result From Delays Caused by Contractor

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/25/90

ATLANTA -- John Portman said the contractor that built his San Francisco Fashion Center has delayed payment to several subcontractors on the job, prompting a slew of liens filed against Mr. Portman. At least 14 liens have been filed against Mr. Portman on the 800,000-square-foot building by tradesmen and suppliers who say they haven't been fully paid for their work. Mr. Portman, who has suspended some debt repayments because of a cash shortage, is in talks with lenders on restructuring the collateral on his $2 billion of debt.

Mr. Portman said in a statement that Portman Cos. has paid all invoices issued to it by the Fashion Center's contractor, Denver-based Stolte Corp., "promptly and in full." He said "typical end-of-the-job negotiations for final close-out of the project," amounting to about $500,000, are expected to be resolved in the next few weeks.

In the meantime, Mr. Portman said, because Stolte has postponed paying some subcontractors, Portman Cos. will issue all remaining payments jointly to Stolte and the subcontractor involved in a particular portion of work.

Jim Jones, Stolte's vice president of finance and administration, said that Portman Cos. has paid all its bills to Stolte and that Stolte is in "normal" negotiations with subcontractors to pay for the large number of change orders made on the San Francisco project. He said Stolte has yet to give Portman the building's final bill, which he estimated at $3 million.

Stolte's parent company, John Holland Holdings Ltd. of Australia, is up for sale, and Mr. Jones said that possible transaction may be having some effect on resolving the bills of subcontractors. He wouldn't elaborate.

But Clem Grant, president of Dual Lock Partition Systems of Beaverton, Ore., one of the lienholders, said, "It's not normal for us to have to put aside $87,000 and negotiate after-the-fact for payment on base contract work."
