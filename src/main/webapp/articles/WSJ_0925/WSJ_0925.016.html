Letters to the Editor: Souter Will Sail An Uncharted Course

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/25/90

In their consideration of Judge David Souter's fitness to sit on the Supreme Court, senators should recall the observation of Charles Evans Hughes, just two years before the confirmation struggle over his appointment as chief justice in 1930: "If conscientious, able, and independent men are put on the bench, you cannot predict their course as judges by reference either to partisan motives or to personal or party loyalties. If you could get further down to the bedrock of convictions as to what are conceived to be fundamental principles of government and social relations, you might be able to get closer to accurate prophecy. But you cannot expect to have judges worthy of the office who are without convictions . . ." ("Hughes, The Supreme Court of the United States," page 49; 1928). If Hughes was correct, senators may learn more about Judge Souter by probing "fundamental principles" than by enticing him to speak out on current constitutional issues. D. Grier Stephenson Jr.

Charles A. Dana Professor

Franklin and Marshall College

Lancaster, Pa.
