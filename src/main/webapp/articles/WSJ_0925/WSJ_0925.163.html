Investors Sue Over Unocal's Conversion Of Union Exploration

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/25/90

LOS ANGELES -- Three investors filed suit against Unocal Corp. over the recent conversion of its Union Exploration Partners Ltd. to a corporation, claiming that investors are losing income and substantial tax benefits as a result. Unocal has said the newly created Union Exploration Corp. will pay a yearly corporate dividend, fully taxable, of only 40 cents a unit. In 1989, unit holders got $1.8188 per unit, the lawsuit says.

Unocal spokesmen couldn't be reached yesterday evening to comment on the suit, which seeks class action status and unspecified damages. Unocal said in June that it was converting the partnership to a corporation, effective Aug. 1, because of recent tax law revisions and "the improving climate for investments in domestic oil and gas exploration and development."

Unocal created the partnership in 1985 to hold its gas-rich properties in the Gulf of Mexico as part of its takeover defense against raider T. Boone Pickens. Unocal and its Union Oil Co. of California subsidiary held a controlling 95% stake and sold the rest for $22 a unit to the public, primarily small investors who wanted a steady stream of tax-deferred cash payments. In the weeks after Unocal announced the conversion, the market value of the partnership units fell to less than $12 a unit from $18.25.

In the lawsuit, filed in federal court here, investors Thomas Farber, Louis Cohen and Harold Browner allege that Unocal failed to disclose in documents issued in connection with the conversion that the original partnership agreement required consent of holders of 98% of the units for any such a conversion. The company allegedly has said it didn't need approval from the public unitholders for the change, the suit claims.
