Dividend News: Corroon & Black Holders to Receive Special Dividend

  • Author: Beatrice E. Garcia
  • Date: 09/25/90

Corroon & Black Corp. declared a special cash dividend of $2.50 a share as an enticement to shareholders, who will soon be voting on the company's merger with London-based Willis Faber PLC. Because Corroon will be filing supplemental proxy materials with the Securities and Exchange Commission, the New York-based insurance broker postponed the special shareholders meeting for voting on the Willis Faber merger from Friday until Oct. 4. The new proxy material, due to be mailed to holders tomorrow, will provide details on the special dividend and other recent events, including Aon Corp.'s unsolicited $40-a-share, or $840 million, bid for Corroon.

The insurance broker rejected Aon's offer last Tuesday. Chicago-based Aon withdrew its bid last Friday.

Robert Cuthbert, Corroon's chief financial officer, said the special dividend was needed "to facilitate the transaction." The special dividend would "focus everyone's attention on the deal" as originally structured, he added.

When the Willis Faber merger was announced in early June, it was valued at $34.72 a share, or $712.3 million. Since then, Willis Faber's share price has dropped, lowering the deal's total value.

Corroon's stock jumped several points when the Aon offer was announced, but it fell back when it appeared that the Corroon board still favored the Willis Faber merger and Aon wouldn't pursue a hostile offer. Corroon shares rose 25 cents to a $29.50 closing in New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday.

The Corroon board declared the special dividend at a board meeting held Sunday. The special dividend is payable to Corroon's holders of record on the effective date of the proposed merger with Willis Faber. The special dividend won't be paid unless the merger is completed. The special dividend was also approved by the Willis Faber board.

Willis Faber shareholders will be voting Friday on the merger with Corroon.
