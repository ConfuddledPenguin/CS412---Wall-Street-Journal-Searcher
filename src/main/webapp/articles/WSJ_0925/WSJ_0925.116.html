Marketing & Media: Time, Cable Concern To Operate Systems For Hungarian TV

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/25/90

NEW YORK -- Time Warner Inc. and United International Holdings Inc., eager to break into the Eastern European cable television market, said they formed a joint venture to operate cable television systems in Hungary. Company officials would not disclose the level of investment. This is the first tie-up between Denver-based United International, which operates several cable television systems in Europe, and Time Warner, one of the world's biggest media and entertainment companies. United International is also the largest franchisee of Blockbuster Video stores in the U.S.

A spokeswoman for Time Warner said the joint venture, which seeks to develop and operate cable systems as well as provide programming services, has been approved by the Hungarian government. Time Warner will focus on developing programming, while United International will supervise the technical side of improving Hungary's cable systems. Planning for the project is expected to be completed by early December, with new programs scheduled to be launched in early 1991.

Time Warner officials said it chose Hungary because the television market is relatively well-developed, as are the financial and legal institutions. Moreover, with some 400,000 to 500,000 Hungarians already tuning into cable television, officials of both companies are optimistic about the market for new cable programs. United International said it has been exploring the Hungarian cable market for more than a year and already has some agreements with local cable systems.
