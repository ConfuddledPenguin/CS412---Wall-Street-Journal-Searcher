International Brief -- Jose Maria Aristrain S.A.: Steelmaker Won't Sell Stake To Banesto and British Steel

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/25/90

Jose Maria Aristrain S.A., a family-owned Spanish steelmaker, canceled an agreement to sell 80% of its shares to Banco Espanol de Credito's industrial holding company and to British Steel PLC for about 40 billion pesetas ($407 million). Acquisition of the Aristrain stake would have converted the holding company of Banco Espanol, which is known as Banesto, into one of Spain's leading nongovernment-owned steel companies. It also would have given British Steel an important foothold in the Spanish market.

A Banesto official said the company will seek ways of collaborating with British Steel.

Aristrain officials weren't immediately available for comment, and British Steel declined to make a statement.

Under the accord, reached in July after months of conflicting reports about the sale, Industrial & Financiera de Banesto S.A. -- 76%-owned by Banesto -- and British Steel were each to have acquired 40% of Aristrain. The Aristrain family was to have retained a 20% stake, half of which was to be sold eventually to Spanish state-owned steelmaker Empresa Nacional de Siderurgica S.A., or Ensidesa.
