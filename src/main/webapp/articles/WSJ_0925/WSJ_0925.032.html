USAir, Air France Plan Links Between Businesses

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/25/90

ARLINGTON, Va. -- USAir and Air France, both laggards in the trend toward service hookups between domestic and international carriers, intend to create links between the U.S. carrier's domestic business and the French carrier's overseas business. As part of a proposed marketing agreement, USAir, a unit of USAir Group Inc., and Air France said they will offer each other blocked space for resale on certain international flights. Starting in the spring, USAir will allocate seats to Air France on its Philadelphia-Paris flights. In the spring of 1992, the French state-owned carrier will reciprocate by providing USAir space on its flights between Paris and another of USAir's major domestic hubs. That hub has yet to be selected.

Other cooperative efforts between the two companies may involve joint advertising and sales promotions; sharing of flight codes; cargo development; enhanced automation of seat selection and advance boarding; and shared terminal facilities at Paris's Charles de Gaulle Airport when Air France starts the Philadelphia-Paris service.

Air France, already a partner in USAir's frequent traveler program, will now offer the domestic carrier access to about 100 European points through Charles de Gaulle. In the U.S., airports served by both companies include New York's John F. Kennedy, Newark, Boston, Washington Dulles International, Miami, Los Angeles, San Francisco, Chicago and Houston.
