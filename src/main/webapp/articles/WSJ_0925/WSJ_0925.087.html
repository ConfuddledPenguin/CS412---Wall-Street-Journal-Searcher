Alcoa Is Expected To Form Venture With Japanese Firm

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/25/90

PITTSBURGH -- Aluminum Co. of America is expected to announce today a joint venture with a Japanese partner that will substantially improve the company's presence in the lucrative but unpenetrated Asian market. Specific details were not available, and Alcoa declined comment. However, a spokesman for Kobe Steel Ltd.'s New York office confirmed that Kobe had been negotiating with Alcoa. Kobe is a leading Japanese maker of can sheet and other fabricated aluminum.

Experts say a venture will most likely be for a large rolling mill, perhaps for automotive applications. A rolling operation could also give Alcoa a presence in the growing Japanese aerospace and can industries.

Aluminum analyst Peter Merner said he expects Alcoa to make a "significant investment in processing," which would be the first such investment for Alcoa on a large scale outside Europe and North America.

The potential benefit of such a venture for Alcoa, which has been exporting heavily to Japan, is clear. "The Japanese container industry is very inefficient, and Japanese rolling mills are nothing to write home about," said Robin G. Adams, president of Resource Strategies Inc., a consulting firm.

For Alcoa, this would be the first in a series of planned ventures in Asia, Europe and South America. Alcoa Chairman Paul O'Neill has said he is willing to go where the business is -- and with soft domestic demand for aluminum in the automotive and construction industries -- that increasingly means Europe and Asia. In Japan, auto makers have ambitious plans to increase aluminum usage in automobiles.

Alcoa already has joint ventures with Japanese companies Kasei Ltd. and Moralco Ltd. to make alumina chemicals, and with Shibazaki Seisakusho Ltd. to make closure mechanisms. The company has also been competing with Pechiney Corp. of France to develop products for the Japanese aerospace industry.
