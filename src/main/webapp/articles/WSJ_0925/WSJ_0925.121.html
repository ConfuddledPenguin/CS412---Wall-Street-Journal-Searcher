Family S&L Ex-Aide Enters Innocent Plea

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/25/90

LOS ANGELES -- Oliver A. Trigg, former chairman of Family Savings & Loan Association, pleaded innocent in federal court here to charges that he defrauded the thrift in a complex scheme to take control of Family using Family's own money. Mr. Trigg was indicted by a federal grand jury this month and charged with 15 counts of conspiracy, bank fraud, money laundering, false statements and tax fraud. If convicted, he faces a maximum of 94 years in prison and a $1 million fine.

According to the indictment, Mr. Trigg, while serving as chairman but holding no equity in the thrift, arranged for Family to lend $2.5 million to a third party to develop a real-estate property. Then $1,240,000 of the money allegedly was laundered into Mr. Trigg's personal bank account and used to buy 51% of Family.

Mr. Trigg attracted local media attention in August 1987 when he acquired control of the Los Angeles-based thrift, partly because with assets of more than $140 million, Family was one of the nation's largest black-owned S&Ls. Family is still in operation after OFC Inc., based in Washington, D.C., acquired control in January.
