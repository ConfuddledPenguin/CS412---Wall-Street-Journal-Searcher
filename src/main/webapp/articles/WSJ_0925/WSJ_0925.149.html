Business Brief -- Qume Corp.: Stock Falls 60% Amid Fear Acquisition Might Collapse

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/25/90

Qume Corp.'s stock fell 60% yesterday after the company said it was advised that an investor group may terminate its planned acquisition of Qume if conditions aren't met by Sunday. Qume closed at $2.75, down $4.125, in national over-the-counter trading.

The Milpitas, Calif., computer-equipment maker in June agreed to be acquired for $86 million by a group led by Qume Chairman David S. Lee and Wearne Brothers Ltd. of Singapore. One condition for the merger, Qume said, was that the Taiwan Stock Exchange Index not decline 50% or more between the time of the merger agreement and the deal's closing. Such a decline has occurred, Qume said.

The company said it will hold a stockholders meeting Thursday and, if the merger is approved, will prepare to close the deal so that the acquisition can occur if the Taiwan stock market recovers sufficiently.

Qume also said it expects to report a loss of about $1.5 million for the quarter ended Aug. 31 on revenue of about $53 million.
