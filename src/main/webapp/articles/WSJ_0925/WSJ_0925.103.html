OTC Focus: Market Plunges on Surging Oil Prices; Index at Lowest Level Since February '88

  • Author: Anne Newman
  • Date: 09/25/90

Over-the-counter stocks swooned to their lowest level since February 1988 as a surge in oil prices intensified investors' fears of a recession. The Nasdaq Composite Index plunged 10.09 to 352.16, a 2.8% drop, as volume eased to 114.8 million shares from 124.2 million Friday. Declining stocks buried advancers, 1,702 to 446, while 444 stocks fell to new lows and only 14 reached new highs.

"The pessimism is pretty strong," said Richard Holden, manager of the OTC department at Alex. Brown & Sons, Baltimore. Investors sold stocks as prices for crude oil futures reached a new high, traders said. Adding to investors' gloom is the lack of progress in resolving the nation's budget deficit or the conflict in the Mideast. "It all adds up to a pretty ugly picture," Mr. Holden said, adding that he doesn't see any indications the market will stage a major rally.

It may be the fourth quarter before OTC stocks fall to their lows for the year, said Merrill Lynch technical analyst George Constantine. "There's been a tremendous amount of damage done" to OTC stocks, he said in an interview. A large number of smaller stocks are lower now than they were in the fourth quarter of 1987 after the market collapse, he noted. By the end of August this year, 55% of the stocks traded on Nasdaq had fallen 30% or more below their highs of last year, Mr. Constantine said. On the New York Stock Exchange, 45% of the shares traded there fell a similar amount.

Because of uncertainties about the economy and about Wall Street, investors aren't paying the premiums they once did for fast-growing smaller stocks, Mr. Constantine explained. However, if the market reaches a low in the fourth quarter, the stage could be set for a rally in small stocks, he said.

Corporate stock buy-backs are providing some business for over-the-counter trading departments, said Robert King, head of OTC trading for Robinson-Humphery Co. in Atlanta.

DF Southeastern rose 1/2 to 9 3/8 after the Decatur, Ga., thrift announced it will repurchase up to $2.5 million worth of common stock. The American depositary receipts of Anangel-American Shipholdings, a Greek shipping company, added 1/8 to 9 3/4 after announcing a buy-back of up to one million shares.

Even the OTC market's jewels were tarnished, as investors took profits in some of this years's better-performing stocks.

Microsoft dropped 2 to 58 1/2; Sun Microsystems fell 1 1/4 to 27 1/4; Reuters Holdings slipped 1 1/2 to 40 1/8; Amgen tumbled 3/4 to 46 7/8; and New England Critical Care lapsed 7/8 to 21 1/8.

Apparel stocks swooned after this newspaper reported that analysts expect the industry to report another quarter of depressed earnings. G-III Apparel slipped 1 1/2 to 4 1/4 and Liz Claiborne eased 1 to 24, even though Liz Claiborne is expected to post decent third-quarter gains. Footwear maker Nike skidded 1 1/2 to 65 1/2.

Investors didn't spare the shares of companies that reported earnings disappointments.

Qume plunged 4 1/8 to 2 3/4 after the Milpitas, Calif., maker of IBM-compatible products said a proposed buy-out of the company could be in jeopardy. A group led by David S. Lee said that it will halt its plan to acquire Qume if all of its conditions can't be met by Sept. 30. Qume also said it expects to report a loss of about $1.5 million for the second quarter ended Aug. 31, compared with year-earlier net income of $3.1 million, which included a one-time gain of about $1.4 million.

Autoclave Engineers plummeted 2 1/4 to 6 1/2 after the Erie, Pa., equipment maker reported a loss for the first quarter ended Aug. 31 of 15 cents a share, compared with year-earlier net income of 23 cents.

AST Research slipped 1 7/8 to 16 7/8 after the Irvine, Calif., maker of personal computer systems unveiled a new model of one of its products and cut the price of an older model.

Collagen tumbled 1 1/4 to 18 5/8. After the market closed, the Palo Alto, Calif., biomedical concern said it plans to delay until early next year the spinoff of its Celtrix unit.

First Interstate of Iowa fell 1 to 4. Late Friday, the Des Moines, Iowa, commercial bank said it will charge off $2.1 million of commercial real estate and other commerical loans in the third quarter.

Two Ohio insurers fell sharply. Cincinnati Financial dropped 3 to 80 1/2 and Ohio Casualty fell 2 1/2 to 33 3/8. Analysts attributed the drop in Cincinnati Financial to worries about the insurer's investment portfolio and to profit-taking in the strong-performing stock, Dow Jones Professional Investor Report said.

NovaCare tumbled 2 to 16 3/8 in heavy trading after Barron's magazine raised questions about an offering of 4.5 million shares planned by the Valley Forge, Pa., provider of speech therapy services.

Among the day's few gainers, Immunex rose 1 1/2 to 28 1/4. The Seattle biotechnology concern said the Food and Drug Administration approved the use of its granulocyte macrophage-colony stimulating factor -- an agent which stimulates the production of white blood cells -- under certain conditions, for patients with life-threatening diseases who have no alternative drugs available to them.

A favorable mention in Barron's boosted the shares of KLA Instruments 1/8 to 7 3/8. The magazine said earnings should rebound for the San Jose, Calif., company.
