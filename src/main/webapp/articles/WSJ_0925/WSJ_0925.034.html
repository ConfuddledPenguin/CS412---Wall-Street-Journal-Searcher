Who's News: L.M. Ericsson Selects Jansson as President Of Its Biggest Division

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/25/90

STOCKHOLM -- Telefon AB L.M. Ericsson picked a new president for its public telecommunications division, part of a reorganization intended to broaden management control over the company's rapid international expansion. The Swedish concern said Hakan Jansson, currently a senior executive at the Radio Systems division and formerly head of research and development for Public Telecommunications, will return to his old division as its new president on Jan. 1. He succeeds Jan Stenberg, a lawyer who headed the company's biggest division since 1985.

Mr. Stenberg already was a member of Ericsson's executive committee. Under the planned reorganization, he'll now assume broader responsibilities stretching across previous divisional boundaries.

Ericsson's new chief executive officer, Lars Ramqvist, wants Mr. Stenberg and the company's chief financial officer Carl-Wilhelm Ros to take a more direct role in management of international operations. As a three-member executive committee, Messrs. Ramqvist, Ros and Stenberg will inherit reporting responsibilities for local units in Ericsson's 10 biggest markets.
