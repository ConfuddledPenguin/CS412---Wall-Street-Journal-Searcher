International: EC Imposes Largest Fine Tied to Competition Law

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/20/90

BRUSSELS -- The European Community Commission showed its mettle by imposing its biggest fine to date on a single company for alleged breach of EC competition law. The Belgian chemical company Solvay & Cie. was fined 30 million European currency units ($41.4 million), Imperial Chemical Industries PLC of Britain was fined 17 million ECUs and a unit of BASF AG of Germany was fined 1 million ECUs. The case involved alleged market-sharing and illegal pricing arrangements for soda ash, a chemical used mainly in the glass-making industry.

Solvay and ICI said the commission's decision is groundless and the two companies plan to appeal to the European Court of Justice. Solvay said the fines are "totally unjustified," while ICI said it was "shocked" by the decision. The BASF unit, Chemische Fabrik Kalk G.m.b.H., couldn't be reached for comment.

The commission said its action would help open up the EC's soda-ash market to "genuine competition," particularly by U.S. producers.
