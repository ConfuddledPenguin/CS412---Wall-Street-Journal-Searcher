S&Ls Held by Government Post Third-Quarter Losses

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/20/90

WASHINGTON -- Savings and loans under government management posted losses of $2.3 billion for the third quarter, said the Resolution Trust Corp. That was a slight expansion from the previous quarter. Losses for the 206 thrifts awaiting sale or liquidation by the government included $1.5 billion in write-downs on assets and losses on the sale of assets. Losses on assets are expected to represent the biggest slice of the $200 billion cost of the thrift-industry cleanup.

The 206 reporting thrifts held $90.7 billion in assets, which have shrunk 27% since the thrifts were seized by the government, the agency said.

Meanwhile, the longer the thrifts remain in government hands, the higher their operating costs, incurred by taxpayers, pile up. Third-quarter results include $863 million in operating losses, which stop accruing when the government disposes of those thrifts.

The losses of 247 government-controlled thrifts in the second quarter were restated yesterday to $2 billion, according to the agency. S&Ls that aren't under government management recently reported losses of $631.4 million for the third quarter.

Losses from government-controlled thrifts aren't likely to abate soon. About 218 S&Ls are considered to be immediate candidates for government takeover. Several hundred more S&Ls could fail, if the economic slowdown is protracted, regulators say.
