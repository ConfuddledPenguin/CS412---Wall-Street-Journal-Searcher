Technology: Sun Microsystems Alters Its Anti-Takeover Measure

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/20/90

MOUNTAIN VIEW, Calif. -- Sun Microsystems Inc., citing concern over hostile takeover activity in the electronics industry, strengthened its anti-takeover provisions. The computer maker said it reduced to 10% the amount of Sun stock that a buyer must purchase before triggering a "poison pill" that would dilute the buyer's holdings. Sun's anti-takeover agreement previously set the trigger point at a 20% acquisition.

William Raduchel, Sun's chief financial officer, said the move wasn't a response to any specific takeover attempt directed at Sun, but that American Telephone & Telegraph Corp.'s hostile takeover bid for NCR Corp. "certainly focussed peoples' attention on these issues."

Sun's anti-takeover provisions now give the holder of each Sun share the right to buy another share at half the market value if a person or group acquires 10% or more of Sun's stock without the company's agreement. Sun said the amendment won't affect AT&T's 19% holding in Sun as long as the phone company honors the terms of its stock-purchase agreement which forbids AT&T from buying more than 20% of Sun until 1998.
