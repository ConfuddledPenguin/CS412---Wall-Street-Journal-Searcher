Enterprise: SBA's Patrick Is Dismissed As Minority Program Head

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/20/90

WASHINGTON -- The head of the Small Business Administration's minority small-business program was removed from her job effective immediately. Erlene Patrick, associate administrator for minority small-business programs, was named to the post in August 1989. According to individuals close to the agency, she was fired because the program, which has long been plagued by controversy, hasn't improved under her leadership. The office is responsible for helping minority-owned companies compete for government contracts.

SBA officials denied that Ms. Patrick had been fired. A spokesman said that she would be reassigned as acting deputy regional administrator in Boston effective Jan. 2. Ms. Patrick couldn't be reached for comment, and SBA officials wouldn't elaborate on a terse statement announcing the new assignment.

The minority program has been under fire for alleged poor service to participating companies and for operating under unfair rules.

Before joining the SBA, Ms. Patrick was a member of the staff of the Senate Small Business Committee. She also was the principal author of the Minority Business Opportunity Reform Act of 1988, which set tough new rules for the government's program for minority-owned contractors.

In its statement, the SBA said Ms. Patrick was reassigned to the office of finance effective immediately. Her deputy, John Whitmore, will head the minority office until Jan. 2, when Ike Washington, who now heads the minority programs in the Atlanta region, will become the office's acting administrator, an agency spokeswoman said.
