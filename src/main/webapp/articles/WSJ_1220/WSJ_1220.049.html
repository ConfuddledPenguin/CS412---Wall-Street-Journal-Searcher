International: Possible Philips Break-Up Is Termed `Last Resort'

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 12/20/90

EINDHOVEN, the Netherlands -- In an apparent attempt to calm any fears over the continued existence of N.V. Philips, the president of the Dutch electronics giant said a break-up of the company would be "the very last resort." In an interview published in a company newsletter, the executive, Jan D. Timmer, said: "We are assuming that if everybody does their bit, improvements can be made throughout Philips that will allow us to maintain the company in its current form. Splitting the company up is the very last resort, a step that neither I nor Philips are ready to take."

It was the first time Mr. Timmer had publicly mentioned the possibility of a breakup of Philips if his restructuring plan fails.

Tom Gietman, an analyst with Van Meer-James Capel, speculated that the statement was intended as a warning to Philips's Dutch unions not to present obstacles to the restructuring drive.

Last summer, Mr. Timmer decreed an extraordinary cost-cutting effort aimed at trimming corporate fat and reversing losses. Part of the cutback involves eliminating 55,000 jobs in Philips's world-wide operations. The company makes a wide range of consumer electronic items from light bulbs to televisions.
