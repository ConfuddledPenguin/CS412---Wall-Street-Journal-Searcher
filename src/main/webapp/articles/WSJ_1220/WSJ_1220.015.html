Acqua Pia Antica Sells Stake

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/20/90

MILAN, Italy -- Acqua Pia Antica Marcia S.p.A., a holding company headed by financier Vincenzo Romagnoli, has sold a controlling stake in its diversified investment company Bastogi S.p.A. to a consortium led by Milan real-estate investor Giuseppe Cabassi, Italian newspapers reported. The Cabassi group reportedly will also acquire control of Rome's Galleria Colonna, a centrally located arcade offering commercial and office space.

The reports follow Mr. Romagnoli's resignation as president of Bastogi and the appointment of Alphonso Amico, a Cabassi group director, as Bastogi's managing director. Two other Cabassi directors were also named to the Bastogi board.

The total price of the acquisition was put at 500 billion lire ($445.8 million). Of that, an estimated 150 billion lire would come from Mr. Cabassi's purchase of around 40% in Bastogi, and the remainder from the Galleria Colonna sale.
