Wednesday's Markets: Bond Prices Fall; Stocks Hold Steady --- Profit-Taking Wipes Out Tuesday's Gains On Treasury Issues

  • Author: Douglas R. Sease
  • Date: 12/20/90

Bond traders cashed in all the big gains they made in Tuesday's rally, but stock market investors held on to their winnings. An accelerating round of profit-taking wiped out the gains in Treasury bond prices that occurred Tuesday in the wake of the Federal Reserve's decision to cut the discount rate. Yesterday's profit-taking came despite yet another Fed move to lower interest rates.

The central bank again cut the federal funds rate, which banks charge one another for overnight loans, this time to 7% from 7 1/4%. Still, the Treasury's benchmark 30-year bond lost more than 1 1/8 points, or more than $11.25 for each $1,000 of face amount.

But the Dow Jones Industrial Average ended unchanged at 2626.73 after wandering aimlessly all day. The dollar rebounded from the drubbing it took Tuesday, while gold prices posted an unexpected increase. Traders said the $9.50-per-ounce gain was mostly a response to stepped-up gold buying in the Middle East.

Analysts said bond traders had already bid prices higher on speculation that the Fed would continue to aggressively push down interest rates as the economy deteriorates. When the Fed announced the discount-rate cut Tuesday, some traders began to sell into the big bond rally. And when it became evident yesterday that the Fed had once again cut the federal funds rate, that was enough to persuade more bondholders to sell before the expected lethargic trading of the holiday season takes over.

The discount rate is the interest rate the Fed charges member financial institutions for loans. A change in the rate is a powerful symbol that the Fed is moving to stimulate the economy with lower rates.

"Speculators saw a reward for their correct anticipation that the Fed would ease, and they chose to take their profits rather than carry them over into the new year," said Brian Fabbri, chief economist for Midland Montagu in New York.

In major market action:

Stock prices were unchanged. Volume on the New York Stock Exchange totaled 180.4 million shares. Advancing issues on the Big Board were ahead of losers 854 to 712.

Bond prices slumped. The yield on the Treasury's benchmark 30-year issue climbed to 8.17%.

The dollar gained. In late New York trading the currency was quoted at 1.4785 marks and 134.05 yen, compared with 1.4762 marks and 132.62 yen Tuesday.
