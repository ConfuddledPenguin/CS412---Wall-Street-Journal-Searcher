Dutch Retail Sales Increase

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/20/90

AMSTERDAM -- Led by strong growth in the non-food sector, Dutch retail sales in October climbed 9.6% from a year earlier, the Dutch Central Bureau of Statistics said. Though non-food retail sales rose 12% in October, sales at department stores were up just 2%, continuing to lag behind those of other retailers, as they have all year.

In the non-food sector, these sales increases were posted: clothing, 12%; white goods, 9.5%; hardware articles, 9.1%; household articles, 22%; books and magazines, 20%; photographic articles, 20%; and optical goods, 13%. With an average price increase of 16% for motor fuels, sales at gasoline stations increased 18%. In the food sector, butcher sales rose 11% and bakery sales 8.6%.
