Dividend News: Stone & Webster Cuts Dividend to 15 Cents

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/20/90

NEW YORK -- Stone & Webster Inc. said it is cutting its quarterly dividend in half to 15 cents a share because it is retaining workers in anticipation of increasing demand. The dividend is payable Feb. 15, 1991, to stockholders of record Jan. 2, 1991.

The provider of engineering, construction and consulting services said while it expects a growing demand from electrical power utilities in the 1990s, it will not realize the benefits until after this year. It also said that it has begun a number of new projects that require large cash expenditures for payroll and start-up costs.

Separately, the company said its engineering unit was awarded a contract from oil company Saudi Aramco and its U.S. affiliate to support its pipeline management program. Terms of the agreement weren't disclosed.

Stone & Webster said it will provide engineering, construction and project management services as well as materials procurement support. The services will be provided over a number of years in both the U.S. and in Saudi Arabia.

In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange, Stone & Webster shares closed unchanged at $31.125.
