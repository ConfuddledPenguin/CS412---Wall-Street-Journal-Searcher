Merck to Expand Kelco Line

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/20/90

RAHWAY, N.J. -- Citing strong demand, Merck & Co., a pharmaceutical company, said it plans to spend $29 million to expand its Kelco division's biogum production facilities in San Diego and in Okmulgee, Okla. Biogums, produced through fermentation, are used as stabilizing, thickening and gelling agents in food and industrial products. Kelco, based in San Diego, produces algin and biogums. Merck said recent U.S. Food and Drug Administration approval of certain biogums has spurred enough industry interest in the products that additional capacity is needed.
