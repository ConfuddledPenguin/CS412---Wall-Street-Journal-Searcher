Chicago Merc Plan Seeks A Stronger Circuit Breaker

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/06/90

CHICAGO -- The Chicago Mercantile Exchange proposed tightening a so-called circuit-breaker rule limiting the one-day moves in its Standard & Poor's 500-stock index futures contract. Under the proposal, trading would be halted after the contract moved up or down 20 points, instead of the current 30-point limit. The futures exchange said other circuit-breaker rules for the contract would remain as they are now. The rules are intended to reduce market volatility.

The proposal now goes to the Commodity Futures Trading Commission.
