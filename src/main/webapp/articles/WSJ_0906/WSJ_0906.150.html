Montreal Expos Board Extends Sale Deadline To Lure Local Buyers

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/06/90

MONTREAL -- Charles Bronfman, majority owner of the Montreal Expos, said directors extended the deadline for the sale of the baseball team, giving a group of potential local buyers more time to put together a formal offer. After a meeting of the team's board Tuesday evening Mr. Bronfman wouldn't specify the new deadline but told reporters "we're pretty darned encouraged" the Expos will be sold to Quebec buyers.

Mr. Bronfman, co-chairman of Seagram Co., and his two minority partners originally set a deadline of Sept. 1 for a local bid. They set a price of 100 million Canadian dollars (US$86.2 million).

Expos president Claude Brochu and a local investment dealer have been scrambling to put together a group of local buyers. "The pressure isn't off, believe me," Mr. Brochu said after Mr. Bronfman's announcement.

So far only one potential investor has stepped forward. A unit of Desjardins Group, a financial services concern, said it is ready to put up C$5 million as part of a buying group. Desjardins also said the Quebec and Montreal governments will have to help the local group acquire the Expos.
