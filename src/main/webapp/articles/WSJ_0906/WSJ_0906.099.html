International: Italy to Sell Enimont Stake If Its Joint Venture Fails

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 09/06/90

MILAN -- The Italian government gave the green light to sell its interest in the Enimont S.p.A. chemical venture to Montedison S.p.A. if their difficult partnership fails to survive. Minister of State Industry Franco Piga first urged state energy agency Ente Nazionale Idrocarburi, which owns 40% of Enimont, to reaffirm with Montedison its willingness to pursue the joint venture under their original agreement. He also suggested a management structure distributing responsibility between the private and public partner.

However, Mr. Piga said, "If this hypothesis isn't feasible, ENI could propose to sell its entire stake to Montedison" at a predetermined price.

It was the first time the government had indicated its willingness to divest itself of the chemical venture before the time period stipulated in the founding pact.

Mr. Piga added that if Montedison didn't agree to buy out ENI at the predetermined price, ENI would buy out Montedison's stake at that same price.

Montedison has repeatedly stated its desire to buy ENI's stake; it has also refused to sell its own shareholding in the joint venture.

Despite the government's acknowledgement that Enimont should revert to single ownership if the current relationship can't be sustained, Mr. Piga didn't specify when or how the failure of the partnership would be determined.
