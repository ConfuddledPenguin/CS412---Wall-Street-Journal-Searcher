Bush Names Fund-Raiser To U.S. Post at Seville Fair

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/06/90

WASHINGTON -- Frederick Bush, who was President Bush's chief fund-raiser in the 1988 election, was appointed commissioner general of the U.S. pavilion at the 1992 Universal World's Fair in Seville, Spain. The appointment, announced by the United States Information Agency, doesn't require Senate confirmation.

Mr. Bush, who isn't related to the president, was involved in the influence-peddling scandal that unfolded last year at the Department of Housing and Urban Development. His company received a $268,000 technical-assistance grant over the objections of reviewing officials at HUD. Apparently because of that incident, which didn't result in any criminal charges, the Senate returned to the president Frederick Bush's nomination to be ambassador to Luxembourg.

In his new post, Mr. Bush will attempt to raise about $21 million for the pavilion from U.S. corporations, states and municipalities, according to a USIA official. The U.S. government will put up an additional $14 million. The USIA official said that Mr. Bush won't be paid by the government; his salary will come from nonprofit entities such as foundations.
