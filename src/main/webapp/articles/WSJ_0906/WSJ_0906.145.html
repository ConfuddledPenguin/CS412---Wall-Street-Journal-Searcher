International Brief -- B.A.T Industries PLC: First-Half Pretax Profit Fell 11% on Big Insurance Losses

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/06/90

B.A.T Industries PLC said first-half pretax profit sank 11% to #592 million ($1.1 billion) from #668 million a year earlier. Per-share earnings dropped 20% to 20.5 pence from 25.5 pence.

Sales for the British tobacco and financial services conglomerate rose 13% to #9.38 billion from #8.27 billion a year earlier. The results reflect damage arising from a protracted battle B.A.T mounted to deflect a takeover bid by Sir James Goldsmith. The company wrote off #26 million in costs for regulatory hearings and defense costs in the fight.

The battle hastened a restructuring at B.A.T that included the disposal of the company's U.S. retailing operations. These sales created #414 million in profits, which translated into a further 10.7 pence-a-share interim dividend, upping the total paid so far this year to 20.7 pence from 19.6 pence a year earlier.

The biggest setback occurred in the Eagle Star insurance business, which was hit by heavy payments from storms. Eagle, which also extends property loan guarantees, had to raise its reserves #40 million.
