Soybean-Futures Broker Pleads Guilty to Fraud

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/06/90

CHICAGO -- Bruce W. Mittelstadt, a 50-year-old soybean-futures broker, pleaded guilty to one count of mail fraud and one of commodity fraud, and agreed to testify against other indicted traders, in return for the government asking that he receive a lenient sentence. Mr. Mittelstadt was charged more than a year ago with racketeering conspiracy and more than 50 counts of wire fraud and violating the Commodity Exchange Act. Neither he nor his attorney, James Montana, could be reached for comment.

So far, eight of the 19 soybean-futures traders indicted last year for fraud have pleaded guilty, as have six of the 21 indicted yen-futures traders.

In addition, Martin J. Riley, a yen-futures trader, pleaded no contest to charges of mail and wire fraud and violating the Commodity Exchange Act. The plea has the effect of a guilty plea, except that it can't be used against him as an admission of guilt in a civil proceeding.

Over the past 10 days, two other traders, one from the yen-futures pit at the Chicago Mercantile Exchange and one soybean-futures trader from the Chicago Board of Trade, also pleaded guilty to reduced charges.

Opening arguments in the trials of the remaining 11 soybean-futures traders and 14 yen-futures traders are scheduled to begin next week; the trials could last more than four months.
