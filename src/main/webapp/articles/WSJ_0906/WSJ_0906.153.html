Judge Denies Request To Bar Certain Shows Of Time Warner Unit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/06/90

NEW YORK -- A federal judge denied a request for an injunction prohibiting Home Box Office Inc. from using any programs containing music by composers represented by Broadcast Music Inc. The injunction request is the latest episode in an entrenched and growing legal battle between the cable industry and societies such as BMI that monitor musical performances and collect fees for composers and publishers. As previously reported, HBO, a unit of Time Warner Inc., had a license with BMI that expired at the end of 1989; HBO hasn't paid performance fees to BMI for several months.

Judge John Sprizzo said, in denying the injunction, that BMI hadn't proved "irreparable damage," and added that it is clear that HBO is willing to take a new license. The judge hasn't yet ruled on the underlying issue of the case -- whether BMI can legally charge local cable operators as well as cable programmers such as HBO.

Judge Sprizzo directed HBO and BMI to try to negotiate interim fees for HBO to pay until the matter is settled. If the two parties are unable to agree on interim fees, the judge said he would set them following a hearing Oct. 5.
