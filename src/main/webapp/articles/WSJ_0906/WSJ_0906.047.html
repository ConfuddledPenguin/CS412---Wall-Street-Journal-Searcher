OTC Focus: Market Increases on Higher Volume; Survival Technology Gains 1 1/4 to 10 3/4

  • Author: Anne Newman
  • Date: 09/06/90

NEW YORK -- Over-the-counter stocks crept higher in quiet trading as investors remained cautious about the economy and the likelihood of peace in the Persian Gulf. The Nasdaq Composite Index advanced 0.77 to 382.44, a 0.2% gain. Volume rose slightly to 89 million shares from 78.1 million Tuesday. Advancing stocks overtook decliners, 1,019 to 784. But stocks falling to new lows continued to swamp those reaching new highs, 110 to 12.

At least one OTC stock got a boost from the Middle East conflict. Survival Technology rebounded 1 1/4 to 10 3/4 after the Bethesda, Md., maker of nerve gas antidote kits said it received a contract worth about $4.8 million from the Department of Defense. The shares last month rose to a 52-week high of 15 1/4 as the company began to see a pickup in orders for the kits following the U.S. troop deployment in the Persian Gulf.

Overall, traders said much of the day's trading was lackluster as investors continued to wait for news on the economic front or from the Middle East. "I haven't been made a believer in a rally," said E.E. "Buzz" Geduld, president of Herzog, Heine & Geduld in New York. Mr. Geduld said he isn't seeing enough conviction among investors in OTC stocks to convince him that the shares will rally.

Technology issues ended the day mixed. Mr. Geduld said interest in the issues wasn't very keen. Oracle Systems, the day's most active stock, fell 1/2 to 10 5/8 as some analysts reported expectations of slowing growth in the semiconductor industry.

Among other technology issues, Intel fell 1 to 32 1/2; Conner Peripherals added 5/8 to 22 1/4; Sun Microsystems gained 1/8 to 29 1/8; Adobe Systems advanced 3/8 to 28 1/2; Seagate Technology fell 1 to 9 and Microsoft tumbled 3 1/4 to 59.

Financial and insurance issues were among the day's gainers. First Liberty Financial rose 3/4 to 6 1/4; Cincinnati Financial jumped 1 3/4 to 85 1/2; insurer Old Republican International added 1/8 to 25; and NAC Re, a reinsurance holding company, advanced 1 3/4 to 30 3/4. The Nasdaq Insurance Index gained 1.88 to 447.56. Two of Nasdaq's three other financial indexes ended the day higher.

Strong earnings fed gains in two restaurant stocks: Cracker Barrel Old Country Store rose 1 1/2 to 23 1/2 and Vicorp Restaurants climbed 1 7/8 to 24 1/2. Cracker Barrel, of Lebanon, Tenn., reported that net income for the fourth quarter ended Aug. 3 rose to 47 cents a share from 36 cents a year earlier. Vicorp, headquartered in Denver, said net income for the third quarter ended Aug. 5 jumped to 41 cents a share from 25 cents a year earlier.

Peerless Manufacturing gained 1 to 16 3/4 after the Dallas maker of equipment for the natural gas industry posted operating profit for fiscal 1990 ended in June of $1.52 a share up sharply from five cents in 1989.

Earnings growth also boosted Duty Free International 1 3/4 to 20 1/4. The Ridgefield, Conn., operator of duty-free retail stores reported that net income for the second quarter ended July 31 rose to 41 cents a share from 35 cents a year earlier.

G-III Apparel Group slid 1 1/4 to 6 3/4 after the New York apparel maker said it expects to report that earnings for fiscal 1990, ended July 31, ranged between $1.70 and $1.80 a share, up from $1.28 in 1989 on a pro forma basis.

That projection is well below the range indicated last week by G-III chief financial officer Alan Feller. Mr. Feller said last Monday the company expected year-end earnings in the range of $1.95 a share, an estimate given by Oppenheimer & Co. analyst Dennis S. Rosenberg. Oppenheimer underwrote an initial offering of G-III last December at $13 a share.

Advanced Logic Research fell 1/2 to 9 after the Irvine, Calif., maker of microcomputer systems announced a 14% price cut in one of its computer products.

FNB Rochester continued its slide after calling off merger talks Tuesday with a Turkish investor group. Shares of the Rochester, N.Y., bank holding company tumbled 1 1/4 to 6 3/4 after Tuesday's 3/4-point fall.

Analysts comments sparked gains in Medical Care International and Borland International and losses in K-Swiss and Chips & Technologies, according to Dow Jones Professional Investor Report.

Donaldson Lufkin & Jenrette initiated analyst coverage of Borland with a "buy" recommendation and put the Scotts Valley, Calif., maker of computer software products on its list of shares recommended for purchase. Analyst Scott Smith told DJPIR he thinks the company's "broad product line and new product momentum" will allow Borland not only to emerge as a software leader but also weather a recession. Borland rose 1/2 to 17 1/8.

Medical Care International gained 1 1/4 to 38 after Donaldson Lufkin & Jenrette analyst John F. Hindelong repeated a "buy" opinion on the Dallas operator of outpatient surgical centers Tuesday after the market closed. Mr. Hindelong told DJPIR he expects Medical Care to earn $1.45 a share in 1990 compared with 96 cents a share in 1989. A group including FMR Corp., a Boston holding company, has an 11.8% stake in the company.

Class A shares of K-Swiss tumbled 1 1/4 to 18 1/2 after Merrill Lynch analyst Brenda Gall reduced her 1990 and 1991 earnings estimates for the newly public maker of athletic footwear. DJPIR reported that Ms. Gall maintains her near-term "attractive" and long-term "buy" opinions on the stock.

K-Swiss Chief Financial Officer George Powlick said that Goldman Sachs & Co. analyst Jay J. Meltzer also recently lowered his earnings estimates. However, Mr. Powlick declined comment on the analysts' actions and on specific estimates. K-Swiss went public June 5 at $17.50 a share in an underwriting led by Goldman Sachs.

Chips & Technologies fell 3/4 to 11 1/2 after Goldman Sachs analyst Rajiv Chaudhri downgraded his investment rating on the stock to "hold" from "attractive" and lowered his earnings estimates for the San Jose, Calif., maker of computer chips.

Mr. Chaudhri said continued pressure on prices, erosion of market share and increasing operating expenses are likely to curtail the company's earnings for the next several quarters, DJPIR reported. He cut his earnings estimate for the company's fiscal first quarter ending Sept. 30 to 15 cents a share from 35 cents, compared with the year-earlier earnings of 58 cents a share.
