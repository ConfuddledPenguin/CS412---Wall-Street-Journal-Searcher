Business Brief -- Pathe Communications Co.: MGM/UA Discussions Focus On Using Assets of Studio

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/06/90

Pathe Communications Co. could decide to partially finance its $1.3 billion acquisition of MGM/UA Communications Co. by leveraging the movie studio's assets, according to proxy materials mailed to shareholders by MGM/UA. Pathe, based in Beverly Hills, and MGM/UA, of Culver City, Calif., are in discussions about agreements in which MGM/UA would "purchase" Pathe assets such as films and theaters, and Pathe would use this cash to buy MGM/UA. In this way, by pre-selling rights to MGM/UA's film libraries and borrowing against various MGM/UA assets and receivables, Pathe could conceivably have access to all the cash MGM/UA would be capable of raising, up to the value of Pathe's assets. This could be from $500 million to $700 million, say individuals close to the negotiations. The transactions would occur simultaneously with closing the merger.

Pathe's bid was revised and extended after a potential $650 million loan from Time Warner Inc. was withdrawn, leaving a trail of litigation and more skepticism about the ability of Pathe and its principal, Italian financier Giancarlo Parretti, to finance the deal.

Shareholders will attend a special meeting Oct. 2 to vote on the tender offer, which is to be completed by Oct. 23.
