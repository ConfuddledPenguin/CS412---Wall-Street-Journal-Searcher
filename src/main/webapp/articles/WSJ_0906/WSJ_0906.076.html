New Securities Issues

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/06/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report. CORPORATE

Xerox Corp. -- $200 million of 9 5/8% notes due Sept. 1, 1997, priced at 99.50 to yield 9.724%. The notes were priced at a spread of 96 basis points above the Treasury's seven-year note. Rated single-A-2 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and single-A by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Salomon Brothers Inc.

MUNICIPAL

Maine State Housing Authority -- $110 million of single-family mortgage revenue bonds, Series A-1 and A-2, due Nov. 15, 1992-2000, 2009, 2010, 2016, 2022 and 2032, priced by a Goldman Sachs & Co. group to yield from 6.40% in 1992 to 8.13% in 2032. Series A-1 consists of $27 million of bonds, comprising $12.3 million of bonds due in 2010 that are not formally reoffered and $14.7 million of 7.80% term bonds priced at par due in 2016. Series A-2 consists of $83 million of bonds. The current interest serial bonds are priced at par to yield from 6.40% in 1992 to 7.25% in 2000. There are $10.5 million of 8% term bonds priced at par due in 2010, $51.8 million of 8.10% term bonds priced at par due in 2022 and $10 million of 8.13% term bonds priced at par due in 2032. Interest on the Series A-2 bonds will be treated as a preference item in calculating the federal alternative minimum tax that may be imposed on certain investors. The Series A-1 bonds are not subject to the alternative minimum tax. Ratings of single-A-1/double-A are expected.

Southern California Housing Finance Authority -- $80.2 million of single-family mortgage revenue bonds, 1990 Issue B, due March 1 and Sept. 1, 1994-2001, 2012 and 2024, tentatively priced by a Bear Stearns & Co. group to yield from 6.50% in 1994 to 7.75% in 2024. Current interest serial bonds are priced at par to yield from 6.50% in 1994 to 7.20% in 2001. There are $20.7 million of 7% bonds priced at par due Sept. 1, 2012, and $52.5 million of 7 3/4% bonds priced at par due March 1, 2024. Interest on the bonds will be treated as a preference item in calculating the federal alternative minimum tax. The bonds are backed by the Government National Mortgage Association and are rated triple-A by S&P.

Nevada Housing Division -- $55 million of single-family program senior revenue bonds, 1990 Issue C, Series C-1, due April 1 and Oct. 1, 1997-2000, 2010 and 2015, and Series C-2, due 1992-1997, 2021 and 2022, priced by a Goldman Sachs & Co. group to yield from 6.55% in 1992 to 7.85% in 2015. Series C-1 current interest serial bonds are priced at par to yield from 6.90% in 1997 to 7.20% in 2000. There is $5.4 million of 7.85% term bonds priced at par due Oct. 1, 2015. Series C-2 current interest serial bonds are priced to yield from 6.55% in 1992 to 7.10% in 1997. There is $5 million of term bonds due Oct. 1, 2022, that are not formally reoffered. Interest on the Series C-2 bonds will be treated as a preference item for federal alternative minimum tax purposes. The bonds are rated double-A by S&P.

Hawaii Housing Finance and Development Corp. -- $50 million of single-family mortgage revenue bonds due July 1, 1995-2005, 2010, 2019 and 2023, tentatively priced by a Dean Witter Reynolds Inc. group to yield from 6.60% in 1995 to 8.05% in 2023. Current interest serial bonds are priced at par to yield from 6.60% in 1995 to 7.60% in 2005. There are $6.6 million of 7.95% term bonds priced at par due in 2010, $20 million of term bonds due in 2019 that are not formally reoffered and $15.1 million of 8.05% term bonds priced at par due in 2023. Interest on the bonds will be treated as a preference item for federal alternative minimum tax purposes. The bonds are rated single-A-1/single-A.

ASSET-BACKED

Valley National Bank of Arizona -- $300 million of 8 7/8% credit card participation certificates priced through underwriters led by J.P. Morgan Securities Inc. at 99.83673 to yield 8.97%, or 77 basis points above the Treasury's threeyear note. The issue, Valley National Credit Card Trust 1990A, is backed by Mastercard and Visa receivables and has an average life of three years. It is rated triple-A by Moody's and S&P.

EUROBONDS

Fuji Bank Ltd. London branch (Japanese parent) -- $700 million of subordinated loan participation certificates in the name of Merrill Lynch AG, via Fuji International Ltd. as underwriter. Proceeds used to fund a subordinated loan to Fuji Bank London branch. Coupon at a margin of 0.25 point above the three-month London interbank offered rate for the first five years. Thereafter, margin of 0.35 point. Issue price is par, maturity date September 2000. Callable after fifth year. Fees 0.30.

Bank of Tokyo, Curacao, (Japanese parent) -- $700 million of subordinated floating-rate notes due September 2000, priced at par, via Bank of Tokyo Capital Markets Ltd. Subordinated guarantee by Bank of Tokyo. Coupon at margin of 0.25 point above three-month Libor for the first five years, 0.35 point afterward. Callable on every coupon date after 1995. Fees 0.25.

Nippon Credit Bank Curacao Finance (Japanese parent) -- $400 million of subordinated floating-rate notes due Sept. 27, 2000, via Nippon Credit International. Coupon at margin of 0.30 point above three-month Libor to year five, Libor plus 0.40 point thereafter. Issue price 100.25. Call after five years. Guarantor Nippon Credit Bank. Fees 0.30.

Kansai International Airport (Japan) -- $170 million of 9 1/2% Eurobonds maturing Sept. 26, 1997, at 101.85 via lead underwriter Bank of Tokyo Capital Markets. Government of Japan guaranteed. Fees 1 7/8.

British Gas PLC (U.K.) -- #175 million of 13% Eurobonds due Sept. 27, 1993, at an issue price of 101.525 via Credit Suisse First Boston Ltd. Reoffered at a fixed price of 100.275. Fees 1 3/8.

Mortgage Securities No. 2 Ltd. (U.K.) -- #50 million of floating-rate Eurobonds due September 2028 at par via J.P. Morgan Securities Ltd. Interest three-month Libor plus 18 basis points to September 1999, Libor plus 50 basis points thereafter, fixed reoffer price 99.5. Fungible with existing #150 million issue, six years average life expected. Fees 60 points.

Deutsche Bank Finance N.V. (West German parent) -- 50 million Australian dollars of 14% Eurobonds due Sept. 7, 1993, at 101.65 plus 31 days' accrued interest via Deutsche Bank Capital Markets. The bonds will be fungible with with an existing A$100 million issue from the primary settlement date of Oct. 8, 1990. Fees 1 1/2.

Electricite de France (France) -- 150 million Swiss francs of 7 1/4% public bonds due Oct. 12, 2000, priced at 102 via Swiss Bank Corp. Guarantee by the French republic. Call from Oct. 12, 1998, at 101 3/4. Fees total 2.88.

IBM International Finance N.V. (U.S. parent) -- 100 million Swiss francs of 8% loan participation certificates (Schuldscheindarlehen) due Sept. 25, 1992, priced at 101 via Credit Suisse. Certificates carry a support agreement from IBM World Trade Center, U.S. Fees undisclosed.
