Texas Judge Awards Plaintiffs $115 Million In `Communion' Case

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/06/90

LOS ANGELES -- A state court judge in San Antonio, Texas, awarded the producers of the movie "Communion" $115 million in a default judgment against units of beleaguered Management Co. Entertainment Group and Virgin Holdings Ltd., a closely held British company controlled by Richard Branson. Pheasantry Films Inc. and Bancannia Film Distribution, an Australian company, had alleged that the defendants failed to pay them $5 million owed for the production of the movie. The suit also charged that the defendants misappropriated the revenue from the distribution of the movie and failed to promote the movie properly. The award includes $100 million in punitive damages.

Management Co. Entertainment has struggled under a large debt load following its $83 million acquisition in July 1989 of Virgin Vision Ltd., a U.K. distribution company, from Virgin Group Ltd., another company controlled by Mr. Branson. Director Nicolas Roeg and two other creditors have filed a petition in federal bankruptcy court here seeking to force the company's movie unit into involuntary liquidation.

Yesterday, Stephen Chrystie, a lawyer for Mr. Roeg and the other creditors, said his clients had granted a one week extention after receiving a letter from bankruptcy counsel for Management Co. Entertainment. Mr. Chrystie said the letter said that if Management Co. Entertainment can't pay its creditors by Sept. 13, it plans to file for protection from creditors under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code. Management Co. Entertainment officials, now in London, didn't return repeated telephone calls.

Management Co. Entertainment, a movie production, talent management and distribution company, is currently in default on more than $100 million in debt. Its major creditors include G.E. Capital, a unit of General Electric Co.
