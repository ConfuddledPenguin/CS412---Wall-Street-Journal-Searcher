Who's News: Merrill Lynch's Hess Resigns From Position In Consumer Finance

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/06/90

NEW YORK -- Merrill Lynch said Dennis J. Hess, one of the architects of its successful cash management account program for individual investors, has resigned after 20 years with the company. Mr. Hess, 50 years old, was senior vice president of diversified financial services in the consumer-markets sector. A spokesman for Merrill Lynch said Mr. Hess, who will serve as a consultant to the company, resigned voluntarily. A successor wasn't immediately named.

Mr. Hess helped devise Merrill's pioneering Cash Management Account, which acts similarly to a bank and brokerage account. It combines a securities account with check-writing and credit-card privileges. Some Merrill investors put the proceeds of securities sales into the moneyfund part of the account.

Mr. Hess also had been responsible for management of the company's insurance, mortgage, lending, credit, domestic banking, trust service and real estate syndication business.

Mr. Hess was traveling yesterday and couldn't be reached for comment. In a statement, he called his more than 20 years at Merrill Lynch "highly rewarding," but said he considered now "the appropriate time in my career to seek new challenges."
