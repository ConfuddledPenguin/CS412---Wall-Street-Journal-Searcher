U.S. Picks Two Carriers For Routes to Soviet Union

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/06/90

WASHINGTON -- The Transportation Department tentatively granted authority to Alaska Airlines and Federal Express Corp. to begin air service to the Soviet Union. Alaska Airlines, a unit of Seattle-based Alaska Air Group Inc., would be permitted to carry passengers and cargo between Anchorage, Alaska, and Magadan and Khabarovsk in the Soviet Union. Federal Express, based in Memphis, Tenn., will provide all-cargo service on a North Atlantic route between New York and Moscow and Leningrad.

The actions stem from an aviation agreement in June by the U.S. and the Soviet Union to expand air service between the two nations. Pan American World Airways, a unit of New York-based Pan Am Corp., now is the only U.S. carrier that provides service.

If the department makes the authority final, which is expected, the routes will take effect April 1 and remain in effect for two years.

Alaska Airlines said: "We have been working toward this for a couple of years. We hope to begin service in mid-June."

A spokesman for Federal Express said the express delivery concern didn't have any immediate comment on the Transportation Department's action.

The department will designate three more airlines to carry passengers and cargo to the Soviet Union and an additional carrier for all-cargo service.
