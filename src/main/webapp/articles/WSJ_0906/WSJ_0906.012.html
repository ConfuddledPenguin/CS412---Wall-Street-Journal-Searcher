Campbell Soup Had $238.4 Million 4th-Period Loss

  • Author: Frank Allen
  • Date: 09/06/90

CAMDEN, N.J. -- Campbell Soup Co. reported a fiscal fourth-quarter loss of $238.4 million, or $1.84 a share, reflecting hefty charges for its expanded program of divestitures and restructuring. In May, the maker of canned soups and other foods put up for sale some of its least profitable foreign operations, conceding that those businesses couldn't deliver adequate returns. At the time, the company said it expected to record a one-time charge of about $55 million, or 43 cents a share, against earnings for its fourth quarter, which ended July 29.

In early July, Campbell expanded the restructuring effort, putting several North American businesses on the block and targeting three plants for closing. The additional moves resulted in a charge of $247 million, or about $1.90 a share.

Yesterday, Campbell said the aggregate after-tax charges were $301.6 million, or $2.33 a share, matching the amounts outlined in the earlier announcements.

In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange, Campbell shares closed at $49.25, down 50 cents.

In the year-earlier fourth quarter, Campbell had a net loss of $194.7 million, or $1.51 a share, including one-time restructuring charges of $261 million, or $2.02 a share. Fourth-quarter sales increased 11% to $1.44 billion from $1.3 billion.

David W. Johnson, who arrived in January from Gerber Products Co. to become president and chief executive officer, praised Campbell's domestic divisions for strong performance. He said there were "solid volume increases" in frozen seafood, ready-to-serve soups, frozen breakfast products and spaghetti sauces.

But he said overseas operations "continued to be disappointing." His remarks omitted the fact that Campbell's international operating earnings in the third quarter actually rose 45% to $16.7 million. Moreover, even excluding the one-time charges, the company's fourth-quarter earnings were down about 4%.

John M. McMillin, a food-industry analyst at Prudential-Bache Securities Inc., said he suspects Mr. Johnson is "finding the turnaround more difficult at Campbell than it was at Gerber." Mr. McMillin said Mr. Johnson has "sorted out the mess" at Campbell but hasn't "gotten rid of the mess yet," alluding in particular to overseas businesses that haven't been sold or revitalized.

For the year, Campbell said net income was $4.4 million, or three cents a share. In fiscal 1989, Campbell earned $13.1 million, or 10 cents a share, after restructuring charges. Excluding the one-time charges for both years, Campbell said, per-share profit rose 11% in the latest full year. Sales gained 9.4% to $6.2 billion from $5.67 billion.
