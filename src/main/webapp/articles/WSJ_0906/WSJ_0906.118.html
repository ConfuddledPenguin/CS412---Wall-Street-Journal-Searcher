Metropolitan Life, First Boston Form London-Based Unit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/06/90

NEW YORK -- Metropolitan Life Insurance Co. and CS First Boston Inc. said they have formed a London-based money-management unit that will begin offering international investment services to U.S. pension funds. The joint venture, called CS First Boston Global Fund Managers Ltd., is the latest example of how U.S. money managers are catering to a surge in international investments by U.S. pension funds.

"We have wanted to have an international investment product capability for the institutional marketplace for some time," said Kevin Putt, a Metropolitan Life vice president. "The question was whether to affiliate or to purchase" an international investment operation, he said.

The big insurer began its money-management venture with CS First Boston this year by contributing assets and money from Metropolitan Life's own pension fund. The venture currently oversees about $150 million in Metropolitan Life money, with that total expected to rise to $215 million by year end, Mr. Putt said.

The first two products offered to U.S. pension funds will be pooled funds for international stocks and bonds. MetLife owns 45% of the joint venture. CS First Boston, in which MetLife holds about a 10% stake, and CS First Boston's parent company own the rest of the venture. CS First Boston is the parent of the U.S. brokerage firm First Boston Corp.
