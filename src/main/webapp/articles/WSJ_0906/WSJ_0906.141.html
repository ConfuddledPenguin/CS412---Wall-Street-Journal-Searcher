Marketing & Media: New Line Appoints Shaye as Chairman

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/06/90

NEW YORK -- New Line Cinema Corp. named Robert K. Shaye to the newly created position of chairman. The 51-year-old Mr. Shaye remains chief executive officer. Yesterday, Mr. Shaye was succeeded as president by Michael Lynne, 49, who also took the new post of chief operating officer.

New Line's film releases include "Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles" and the "Nightmare on Elm Street" series. Its most recent release, "Pump Up the Volume," cost $4.5 million to produce, and in its first two weeks in 800 theaters, the film grossed about $5.7 million.

Mr. Lynne, who joined New Line in 1984 as general partner, was involved in New Line's purchase of a significant equity interest in RHI Entertainment Inc., and in securing North American distribution rights to the sequel of "Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles."
