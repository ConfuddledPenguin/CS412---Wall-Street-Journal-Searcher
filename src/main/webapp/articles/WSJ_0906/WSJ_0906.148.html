Abreast of the Market: Industrials Rise 14.85 on Strength Of Energy Issues; Volume Is Light

  • Author: David Wilson
  • Date: 09/06/90

NEW YORK -- Stock prices moved higher in another slow session, as the market managed to shrug off more tough talk from Iraqi President Saddam Hussein. Energy stocks were at the forefront of the advance as crude-oil prices edged toward $30 a barrel in New York trading. Some favorable analyst comments provided a boost to the gas pipeline sector.

The Dow Jones Industrial Average, which skidded to a loss of nearly 19 points in late morning trading as statements from the Middle East sparked a brief wave of selling, closed with a gain of 14.85 at 2628.22.

Among broader indexes, Standard & Poor's 500-Stock Index gained 1.30 to 324.39, the New York Stock Exchange Composite Index gained 0.80 to 177.93 and the Dow Jones Equity Market Index rose 1.10 to 300.35.

New York Stock Exchange volume, which had fallen short of 100 million shares in the two previous sessions and set successive lows for the year, remained relatively light with 120,610,000 shares changing hands. There were 953 advancing issues on the Big Board against 571 decliners.

"People are still on tenterhooks with respect to the Mideast crisis, and there's a large body of people who fear a long stalemate and don't want to make any {market} moves," said Stefan D. Abrams, chief investment strategist at Kidder, Peabody & Co.

But analysts said the market's ability to recover from its late-morning sell-off -- which took place as Saddam Hussein reiterated his call for a "jihad," or holy war, in the Mideast -- encouraged investors to do some buying.

"The market appears very much sold out," said Jack Solomon, a technical analyst at Bear, Stearns & Co., noting that the industrial average has stabilized around the 2600 level over the past week and a half and the "urgent selling" of recent weeks "seems to be completed."

Meanwhile, only 29.1% of the investment advisers surveyed last week by the Investors Intelligence service were bullish on stocks. That percentage was the lowest since December 1988, said Michael Burke, the survey's editor.

By contrast, Investors Intelligence found that 52% of the market newsletter writers had a bearish opinion of the market, while 18.9% anticipated a correction in share prices over the next few weeks.

"People are scared," Mr. Burke said, because of the potential economic damage from the recent jump in the price of oil and the potential for even higher oil prices.

Oil stocks rallied as October crude-oil futures closed at $29.77 a barrel, up 65 cents, in New York trading. Exxon gained 3/4 to 50 7/8 in composite trading of 1.3 million shares, while Chevron rose 1 3/4 to 77 5/8, Texaco rose 1 3/4 to 63 3/4, Mobil climbed 1 3/8 to 65 3/4, Amoco advanced 1 1/2 to 57 3/4 and Atlantic Richfield added 3 1/8 to 138 3/4. Within the oil-services sector, Halliburton gained 1 3/8 to 54 1/2 and Schlumberger rose 1 1/8 to 65 1/8.

Coastal climbed 1 1/4 to 36 3/8 and Enron gained 1/2 to 52, boosted by Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette's positive comments on the two gas pipeline stocks. Analyst Curt Launer told Dow Jones Professional Investor Report he expects Coastal to benefit from a seasonal rise in natural-gas prices this winter and a new refinery it will open in Aruba later this year, while Enron is "well positioned" to benefit from growth in gas demand from California. Enron Oil & Gas, 84%-owned by Enron, jumped 1 3/4 to 25 3/8.

Columbia Gas System, which Mr. Launer singled out along with Coastal as the best-positioned pipeline companies in the current environment, added 3/4 to 47. Among other pipeline stocks, Panhandle Eastern rose 3/4 to 15 1/2, Transco Energy rose 1 to 44 and Enserch gained 5/8 to 25 5/8.

International Business Machines, which unveiled a new generation of mainframe computers as expected, eased 1/8 to 102 3/8 on 1.5 million shares. The line, called System 390, represents IBM's first new mainframe series in five years.

Other key technology issues were widely mixed. Digital Equipment climbed 1 to 66 1/2 and Cray Research rose 1/2 to 35, while Compaq Computer fell 2 1/2 to 45 3/8, Hewlett-Packard lost 1 1/8 to 34 3/8 and Motorola slipped 2 to 69 1/8.

United Technologies, whose Otis Elevator unit agreed to acquire a majority stake in an East German elevator company, gained 1 5/8 to 50. Terms weren't disclosed. Separately, the company's Sikorsky Aircraft unit and Korean Air agreed to work together to produce helicopters for South Korea.

Pinnacle West Capital rebounded 1/2 to 12 7/8 on 1.7 million shares after tumbling 3 3/4 Tuesday in response to an agreement that ended PacifiCorp's fight to acquire the company. At a meeting with analysts, Pinnacle West said it expects to reduce its consolidated debt by $1 billion by the end of 1993 and restore its common-stock dividend.

Castle & Cooke rose 2 5/8 to 35 5/8. The company scheduled a news conference to discuss "employee-related matters" amid speculation that it will phase out pineapple operations on the Hawaiian island of Lanai and rumors about plans for its two major businesses, food and real estate.

Mylan Laboratories added 1 1/2 to 18 3/8. Sandoz Pharmaceutical agreed to promote Eldepryl, a treatment for Parkinson's disease which is marketed by a joint venture of Mylan and Bolar Pharmaceutical. Bolar gained 1/2 to 4 3/4 in American Stock Exchange trading.

U.S. Surgical rallied 3 7/8 to 51 3/4 and traded as high as 52 1/4, a 52-week high, after favorable comments from analysts at Dean Witter Reynolds and PaineWebber. Both analysts cited the company's prospects in laparoscopic surgery, a new technique that reduces the amount of time required for doctors to perform operations and for patients to recover.

Pier 1 Imports, which estimated that it would post net income of seven or eight cents a share for the second quarter ended Sept. 1, fell 1/8 to 7 5/8. In the year-earlier period, its net equaled 20 cents a share. Separately, the company doubled the size of a stock-repurchase program to four million shares.

The American Stock Exchange Market Value Index rose 2.67 to 325.41. Volume totaled 10,590,000 shares.
