Business Brief -- Boise Cascade Corp.: Company to Sell Paper Mill To Unit of Wiggins Teape

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/06/90

Boise Cascade Corp. agreed to sell its paper mill in Vancouver, Wash., and certain inventories for $72 million to Appleton Papers Inc., a unit of the Wiggins Teape Group Ltd. in London. Under terms of the sale, Appleton will pay $60 million for the mill and $12 million to purchase finished goods inventories.

The transaction, expected to be completed by the end of the year, comes as part of the Boise, Idaho, company's plan to boost profits by reducing its pulp and paper presence in the Northwest.

Weak paper prices and unanticipated complications with maintenance and repair at several of Boise Cascade's pulp and paper mills contributed to a shortfall in earnings for both the first and second quarters.

With the purchase of the Vancouver facility, Appleton, based in Appleton, Wis., will add approximately 100,000 tons of paper-making capacity. The 450 people currently working at the mill are to be retained under the Appleton ownership.

Boise Cascade stock closed at $30.125, up $1, in trading yesterday on the New York Stock Exchange.
