International Brief -- Instituto Nacional de Industria: Spain Aggressively Seeking Buyer for Enasa Truck Unit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/06/90

The Spanish government is pulling out all the stops in its efforts to find a partner for the troubled truck maker Empresa Nacional de Autocamiones S.A., or Enasa, owned by the Spanish state holding company, Instituto Nacional de Industria. Spain has asked the West German government to overrule its cartel office and approve a takeover bid for Enasa by MAN AG and Daimler-Benz AG, and has reopened contacts with Fiat S.P.A. of Italy, officials said. Enasa has chalked up losses of about seven billion pesetas ($70.8 million) so far this year, owing to a sales slump caused by uncertainty about the company's future.

Earlier this year, Bonn's cartel office challenged on anti-competition grounds plans for MAN to acquire 60% and Daimler 20% of Enasa. Attempts to find other buyers, including Fiat, met with failure. If a German solution fails, Spanish officials said, Enasa's parent will turn again to Fiat in hopes of negotiating an alternative solution. A Fiat spokesman said he couldn't comment on such a prospect.
