Ponce Federal Names San Juan Attorney New Bank Chairman

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/06/90

PONCE, Puerto Rico -- Ponce Federal Bank, under fire from federal regulators, named Miguel D. Lausell chairman. Mr. Lausell, 46 years old, an attorney in San Juan, has been vice chairman since May 1989. He succeeds Ramiro L. Colon Jr., who stepped down as president and chief executive officer in mid-August. Mr. Colon was continuing to serve as chairman while the thrift made several changes in management.

Last week, Ponce Federal, one of the largest thrifts in Puerto Rico, was hit with strong enforcement measures by the Office of Thrift Supervision. The measures, designed to "increase the bank's safety and soundness," according to the OTS, included a temporary cease-and-desist order issued against Mr. Colon, who is still a stockholder, and his brother, Wendell W. Colon, former counsel at the thrift and also a stockholder.

According to the OTS, the Colons have been ordered to stop causing Ponce Federal to violate federal regulations against loans to affiliated parties and insider transactions, and to post a $50,000 bond to make funds available for restitution to the thrift.

The Colons weren't available for comment yesterday.

Mr. Lausell, in a prepared statement, said: "This realignment of the board of directors should result in improved oversight of the bank."

Ponce Federal also said that Federico Montilla, an architect in San Juan, was named vice chairman.
