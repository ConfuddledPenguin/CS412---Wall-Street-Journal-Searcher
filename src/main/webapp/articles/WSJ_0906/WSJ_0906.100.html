Marketing & Media: Ex-Inquirer Editor Roberts Accepts University Tenure

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/06/90

COLLEGE PARK, Md. -- Eugene L. Roberts Jr., who retired recently as executive editor of the Philadelphia Inquirer, accepted an appointment as a tenured professor in the University of Maryland's College of Journalism. Mr. Roberts, 58 years old, headed the Inquirer's news operations for 18 years, a period during which the paper won 17 Pulitzer Prizes and achieved dominance in its market. Before joining the Inquirer, he had been a Vietnam war correspondent and national news editor for the New York Times.

He will join the journalism faculty at College Park in September 1991, teaching courses in such subjects as newsroom management, reporting, editing and journalism ethics. While at the university, he is expected to remain a consultant to Miami-based Knight-Ridder Inc., which owns the Inquirer.

Reese Cleghorn, dean of the college, said Mr. Roberts also will become a senior editor of Washington Journalism Review, a national monthly with a circulation of 30,000 that is owned by the college, and he will be involved in developing various "international initiatives" for the school.

Journalism and communications schools at other universities, including Stanford, Columbia and North Carolina, also tried to recruit Mr. Roberts.
