International: U.S. Appears Near Accord On Giving Arms to Israel

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/06/90

WASHINGTON -- The Bush administration appears close to agreeing to provide Israel with a sophisticated air-defense system and $400 million in loan guarantees to build housing for Soviet Jewish emigres. Following a meeting at the State Department between Secretary of State James Baker and Israeli Foreign Minister David Levy, an Israeli source said that the U.S. promised to supply Israel with Patriot air-defense missiles from Defense Department inventories. Israel has asked for the equipment because of threats by Iraq and because the U.S. recently agreed to supply Saudi Arabia with over $2 billion in military equipment.

In addition, Mr. Baker said "we made good progress" on working out details for providing the loan guarantees. The Israeli source said that an agreement in principle was reached and that details will be worked out in the next few days.

Earlier this year, the administration held up Israel's request for the loan guarantees, insisting that Jerusalem give assurances that they wouldn't be used to build houses in the occupied territories.

While seeking the support of Arab governments for its actions against Iraq, the U.S. has asked Israel to maintain a low profile in the Persian Gulf crisis. Mr. Levy is expected to meet with President Bush today.
