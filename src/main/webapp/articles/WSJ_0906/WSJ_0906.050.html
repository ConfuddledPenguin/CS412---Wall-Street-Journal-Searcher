Help-Wanted Ads Stayed at Low Level In Survey for July

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/06/90

NEW YORK -- Help-wanted advertising continued its sluggish pace in July, unchanged from June's five-year low. The Conference Board said its index of help-wanted advertising remained at 132 in July, sharply down from a year-earlier 147. June's figure was revised from 133. The index, a seasonally adjusted indicator of hiring plans, uses 1967 as a base of 100.

Although July's lackluster results had been expected, the Conference Board said a previously optimistic outlook for the second half has been harmed by the risk of war in the Middle East.

"As of Aug. 1, the economy in general, and the labor market in particular, was poised for a modest uptick in the second half of this year," said Conference Board economist Ken Goldstein. "However, events in the Middle East changed all that." Iraq invaded Kuwait Aug. 2, leading to the current armed standoff in the region between Iraq and U.S.-led forces and a world-wide trade embargo against Iraq.

Mr. Goldstein said exporters, business investors and consumers are more likely to put some plans on hold because of that crisis. As a result, no big staff expansions are expected in the next few months, which could hurt chances of quickly turning around help-wanted volume growth.

Nevertheless, the independent business research group said it doesn't expect the economy to head into a recession, although growth will be sluggish.

The worsening economic prospects could be harmful to the New England and Middle Atlantic states, which seemed on the verge of coming out of a steep decline. The New England region posted a 2.5% increase in want-ad volume in July.

Job advertising in the Mountain, East South Central and Pacific regions started to decline at a faster pace in July, the Conference Board said. The West South Central region remained stable, however.

The index is compiled from monthly surveys of advertising volume in 51 major newspapers.
