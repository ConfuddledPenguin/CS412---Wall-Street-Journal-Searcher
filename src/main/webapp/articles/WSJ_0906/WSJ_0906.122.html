Business Brief -- Seagram Co.: Company Says Net Rose 3% On Strong Spirits Business

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/06/90

Seagram Co. reported that net income for the second quarter ended July 31 rose 3% on a strong performance in the Montreal distiller's international wines and spirits business. Net was $205 million, or $2.18 a share, compared with $199 million, or $2.07 a share, in the year-earlier period. Revenue rose 8.7% to $1.42 billion from $1.30 billion.

The earnings reflect a charge, which Seagram declined to disclose, for 177 job cuts announced in July in the marketing and sales force of the U.S. distilled-spirits business.

Seagram's Brazilian operations outperformed its expectations for the period. However, a spokesman said the company remains "cautious" about the affiliate's operations for the full year, citing Brazil's volatile economy and the stringent economic controls the government put in place a few months ago.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Seagram shares fell 37.5 cents to $75.375.
