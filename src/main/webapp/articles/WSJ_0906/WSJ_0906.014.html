Justin Industries Inc. Challenged by Holders Over Change in Bylaws

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/06/90

FORT WORTH, Texas -- A group of dissident shareholders of Justin Industries Inc. charged that the company's board violated federal securities laws by taking too long to disclose that it had amended Justin's bylaws to make it more difficult to remove directors. The dissident group, which is headed by Perry H. Sutherland of Kansas City, Mo., and Barry S. Rosenstein of New York, filed a motion in federal court here seeking permission to file new allegations in its legal battle with Justin, a maker of boots, building materials and industrial equipment.

The group's charges center on Justin's disclosure that its board voted to raise the number of shareholders' votes needed to remove directors to 75% of total shares outstanding from 50%. Although the amendment passed in November 1989, the company failed to make the change public until last month, the dissidents charged.

The group, which owns 12% of Justin's common, earlier offered to pay $18.50 a share, or $140.5 million, for the shares it didn't own. Justin rebuffed the offer and announced a plan to acquire Tony Lama Co., an El Paso, Texas, boot maker.

According to the proposed charges, the changes would make it impossible for shareholders to oust directors because management controls at least 25% of the shares. "Shareholder democracy is a dead letter at Justin," the filing states.

The group is seeking to file the allegations as part of its response to a lawsuit filed against it by Justin charging it with failing to disclose adequately its intentions to acquire the company.

The group is seeking, among other things, for the court to declare that the proxies obtained by Justin in the March annual meeting were "ineffective" and that the election of directors was void; to enjoin the directors from proceeding with its planned acquisition of Tony Lama; and to order the company and its directors to pay actual and punitive damages.

A Justin official didn't return phone calls seeking comment.
