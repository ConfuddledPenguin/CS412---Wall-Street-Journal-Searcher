International: Group Says China to Abide By Ban on Trading in Ivory

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 09/06/90

LAUSANNE, Switzerland -- China will abide by an international ban on trade in ivory, according to an organization that monitors trade in endangered wildlife species. Beijing's decision was prompted by the closure of traditional export markets in Japan, the U.S. and Western Europe where governments have outlawed all ivory trade, according to Jacques Bernez of the Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species.

Beijing told the group it will become party to the ban next January, Mr. Bernez said.

The 103-nation group agreed on the ivory trade ban in an attempt to protect dwindling herds of African elephants.
