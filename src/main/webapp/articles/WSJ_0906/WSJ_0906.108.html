Salomon Is Planning To Almost Dismantle Philipp Brothers Unit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/06/90

NEW YORK -- Salomon Inc. outlined plans that virtually would dismantle its struggling Philipp Brothers unit. Salomon said it will fold parts of Philipp Brothers into other subsidiaries as part of a "major downsizing and repositioning" of the commodity unit, which has been unprofitable for six consecutive quarters. Other Philipp departments will be sold.

The announcement marks a full circle for Philipp Brothers: Initially, it was part of Phibro Corp., which acquired Salomon Brothers in 1981. Philipp Brothers has had a pretax loss of $83 million so far this year, tarnishing what otherwise have been impressive earnings at Salomon's securities and energy segments.

In a news release, Salomon said Philipp's precious-metal trading group will be absorbed by its Salomon Brothers Inc. securities unit. Fertilizers and countertrade departments will be folded into Phibro Energy, Salomon's big oil trading and refining subsidiary.

Meantime, Salomon is negotiating to sell Philipp's nonferrous-metal department, a group that includes trading in copper, aluminum, nickel, tin and zinc. Salomon blamed this year's first-quarter pretax loss of $62 million for Philipp on unspecified trades in nonferrous metals.

Cocoa trading will continue to be conducted within Philipp Brothers, Salomon said, although cocoa operations will be at Phibro Energy. Phillip Brothers has cut back sharply from the approximately 150 commodities it traded at the time of the Phibro-Salomon merger to a handful.

Salomon has said it will take a pretax charge of as much as $200 million, expected in this quarter, to shrink the ailing unit.
