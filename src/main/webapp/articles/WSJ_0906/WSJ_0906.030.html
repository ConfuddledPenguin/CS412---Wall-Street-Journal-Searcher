Letters to the Editor: Immigration Bill Is Bad for Business

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/06/90

Your Labor Letter regarding the immigration legislation before Congress (page one, Aug. 14) neglected to mention that under the Senate proposal, S. 358, many classes of workers no longer would be able to immigrate legally to the U.S. That bill eliminates all employer-sponsored immigration of low-skilled and unskilled workers. This will hurt many U.S. businesses and workers. Restaurants, hotels, poultry-processing plants and other industries will be hurt. Most severely affected will be working women, who will find it even harder to find care for children, elderly and handicapped family members. Professional women with demanding schedules will be hardest hit.

Current law is much too restrictive, since employers must wait more than four years to obtain visas for nonprofessional employees to fill jobs for which there are no available American workers. This is hurting American business. The Senate proposals make the situation worse.

S. 358 and H.R. 4300 must be amended to ensure that American businesses can obtain the workers they need to carry on their work.

Carolyn Ann Killea

Immigration Attorney

Washington
