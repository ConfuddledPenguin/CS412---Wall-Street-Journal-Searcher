Business Brief -- Valhi Inc.: Chemical Concerns to Merge In $411 Million Transaction

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/06/90

DALLAS -- Valhi Inc. and NL Industries, Inc., two companies controlled by Dallas financier Harold Simmons, said they agreed in principle to merge in a transaction valued at $411 million. Under terms of the agreement, Valhi, a chemical and industrial concern, would issue 1.6 shares of its common stock for each of the 22.6 million shares of NL common shares it doesn't already own. Valhi currently holds about 66% of NL, a petroleum services concern. The companies said the agreement won't be completed if the average price of Valhi common shares stays below $9.50 a share for the 20 trading days ending two trading days before a shareholder vote on the merger. Also, Valhi can terminate the merger if its average price for the same period is above $13 a share. In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange, Valhi closed yesterday at $11.375, up 25 cents a share.

The companies said the merger was subject to, among other things, approval of Valhi's board and shareholders and approval by a majority of NL shares present and voting, other than those held by Valhi. The companies said that NL's board, including its outside directors, approved the merger and received an opinion from Salomon Brothers that the proposed terms were fair to its shareholders, other than Valhi.

Steven L. Watson, vice president and secretary of Valhi, said that both managements think the merger will be beneficial to the separate shareholder groups. He wouldn't comment further.
