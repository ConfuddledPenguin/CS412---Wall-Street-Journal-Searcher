J.C. Penney Removes Line With Anti-Hunting Labels

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/06/90

DALLAS -- J.C. Penney Co., under pressure from sportsmen's groups, pulled from its shelves a line of boys' clothing carrying an anti-hunting message. Attached to the "Environmental Protection Department" line of clothing are cards that say "Absolutely no hunting of wild and free animals while wearing this garment," and "Wild life protected by man surround the world with pure thoughts."

But both Penney and the maker of the clothing, H. Cotler Co. of New York, insist they weren't trying to sell a message. "We were just trying to sell the merchandise," said Howard Cotler, head of Cotler Co. Added Duncan Muir, public relations manager for Penney: "It is our policy not to take a position one way or the other on these sorts of issues." In this case, he said, after receiving numerous complaints company officials decided that "frankly, we've made a mistake."

Muir said he didn't know how many pieces of clothing were being pulled from shelves, and he wouldn't disclose Penney's investment in the line.
