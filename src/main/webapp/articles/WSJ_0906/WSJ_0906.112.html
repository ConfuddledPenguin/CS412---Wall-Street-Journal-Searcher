Newsprint Producer's Unionized Workers Set Strike Deadline

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/06/90

TORONTO -- Abitibi-Price Inc. said its unionized workers in Eastern Canada set a strike deadline of 8 a.m., Sept. 10. Abitibi, the second-largest newsprint producer in the world, said members of the Canadian Paperworkers Union voted 77% in favor of a strike if agreement isn't reached before the deadline.

The company said negotiations with the union will resume here Sept. 7. Talks were suspended Aug. 20 so that the union could take a strike vote.

A strike would close most of Abitibi's newsprint mills, which are concentrated in Eastern Canada. Abitibi has total annual newsprint capacity of about 1.95 million metric tons, or about 8% of North American capacity. A metric ton equals about 2,204.62 pounds.

A strike wouldn't have an immediate impact on newspaper publishers and other newsprint users. Publishers of U.S. daily newspapers had an average 41-day supply of newsprint on hand at the end of July. As well, other newsprint makers can boost their output in the event of a strike. But supplies are already fairly tight and a strike at Abitibi would put upward pressure on prices, newsprint buyers said.

An Abitibi spokeswoman said that when talks were suspended only minor issues had been settled. "We're very early in the negotiations process," the official said.

The union declined to comment until it notifies its members, but a union official confirmed the vote results and strike deadline.
