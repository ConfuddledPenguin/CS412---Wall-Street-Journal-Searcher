Ex-President of Unit Of Teledyne Pleads Guilty in Fraud Case

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/06/90

WHITE PLAINS, N.Y. -- The former president of a Teledyne Inc. division pleaded guilty to one count of making false statements to the Defense Department in connection with an alleged scheme to defraud the Navy on a helicopter contract. The ex-president of Teledyne Hydra-Power, 69-year-old Cirino Giampapa of Massapequa, N.Y., entered the plea in U.S. District Court here. Also, Morton Baine, 54, of New Rochelle, N.Y., a former Teledyne Hydra-Power controller, pleaded guilty to conspiring with others at the division to defraud the U.S. and to submit false claims to the Defense Department.

The U.S. Attorney's office in Manhattan said the alleged scheme involved the submission of fraudulent labor-hour figures to inflate the prices Teledyne Hydra-Power charged for parts. The government said the false claims totaled more than $4.5 million.

Teledyne Hydra-Power is a division of Los Angeles-based Teledyne Inc., which has diverse manufacturing interests. In November, Teledyne Inc. agreed to pay the U.S. $11.9 million to cover the overcharges and penalties.

Mr. Giampapa, who will be sentenced Nov. 14, faces a possible fine of as much as $10,000 and a maximum prison sentence of five years. Mr. Baine, whose sentencing is scheduled for Nov. 8, faces a fine of as much as $250,000 and five years in prison.

Carl Bernstein, attorney for Mr. Baine, declined comment. Walter Bonner, Mr. Giampapa's attorney, couldn't be reached.
