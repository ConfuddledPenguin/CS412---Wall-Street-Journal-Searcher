Marketing & Media: Bertelsmann Picks Neuthaler to Head Bantam Doubleday

  • Author: Meg Cox
  • Date: 09/06/90

NEW YORK -- After months of searching, Bertelsmann AG appointed Paul D. Neuthaler chairman and chief executive officer of its Bantam Doubleday Dell Publishing Group unit. Mr. Neuthaler, who will assume his post Monday, fills a void left by Alberto Vitale, who quit last November to run rival publisher Random House Inc. The 47-year-old Mr. Neuthaler, with a 25-year career in publishing, has been immersed for the last decade in scientific and professional publishing, as opposed to the general-interest or trade publishing for which Bantam Doubleday is known.

The choice surprised many in the publishing industry because Mr. Neuthaler hadn't been among the rumored candidates for the job and hasn't much experience as a manager in trade publishing. While Bantam Doubleday Dell on the whole is profitable, losses continue at Doubleday, where the publisher was replaced in January. A spokesman said losses have been narrowed considerably at Doubleday, helped partly by the success of the reissued, expanded Stephen King novel "The Stand."

However, Bertelsmann executives have made clear that financial acumen was at least as important as publishing experience in a chief executive. Also, Bertelsmann has said in the past that it was interested in acquiring some professional and scientific publishing properties, which is Mr. Neuthaler's area of expertise. For the past four years, Mr. Neuthaler has been a partner in closely held JPT Publishing Group Inc., the owner of scientific and professional publishing properties with annual revenue of about $100 million.

Such publishing fields tend to be more stable and steady in their earnings, and Bertelsmann has prosperous interests overseas in technical and professional publishing. Bertelsmann hired Mr. Nuethaler in the mid-80s to search for such properties for the company to buy in the U.S. However, having spent about $800 million for Doubleday and RCA Records in 1986, Bertelsmann wasn't inclined to make additional purchases and Mr. Neuthaler left the company.

Mr. Neuthaler, who has a doctoral degree in literature and wrote his dissertation on an obscure 17th-century Welsh poet, said that while his career has lately focused on technical publishing, he got his start editing general-interest books at such publishing houses as Simon & Schuster.

"I'm a publishing executive and formats and subjects not withstanding, I think there are a lot of transferable skills," he said. "I also have a lot to learn."

Bernhard von Minckwitz, who has been interim chief executive officer, was given the new title of executive chairman of Bantam Doubleday. He won't oversee the day-to-day operations. He continues to be president of Bertelsmann Publishing Group International.
