Rash of FAA Retirements Spurs Concern by Airlines

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/06/90

WASHINGTON (AP) -- The airline industry said that it is concerned about a rash of retirements among key Federal Aviation Administration personnel, including air traffic controllers. The Air Transport Association, which represents major carriers, said the agency's loss of 341 people last month jeopardizes the FAA's critical air traffic control and airways operations.

There are approximately 50,000 FAA employees.

FAA spokesman Fred Farrar confirmed the figure and said it was twice the normal number of retirees for a month. The increase, he said, was primarily because of concern over possible changes in federal retirement rules.

Aviation Daily, which reports on the airline industry, stated on Tuesday that the retirees included 117 controllers, 84 electronic technicians, 17 engineers, 11 safety inspectors, two FAA pilots and 110 in other categories.

The Bush administration, in congressional budget negotiations, has proposed eliminating a provision that allows employees to get back their individual retirement contributions in two annual lump sums.

If the rules change, only those who retired 30 days before the program expires Sept. 30 would be eligible to get the lump-sum payments.

Mr. Farrar said controllers are eligible to retire at age 50 if they have worked for 20 years and at any age after 25 years of active air traffic control work.

FAA employees contribute 7% of their pay to the retirement program and can receive it all back in two payments at the end of service, while continuing to receive a reduced monthly payment based on government contributions to the program.
