Who's News: Parent of Emery Selects Arthur Bass To Head the Unit

  • Author: Jim Carlton
  • Date: 09/06/90

MENLO PARK, Calif. -- Consolidated Freightways Inc. said it is bringing in a co-founder of Federal Express Inc. to help rescue Consolidated's money-losing Emery Worldwide acquisition. Named by Consolidated as chairman and chief executive officer of Emery was Arthur C. Bass, 58 years old, a veteran top executive in both the airline and air freight industries who helped start Federal Express and is credited with turning around a number of troubled companies. Emery is a direct competitor of Federal Express in the air freight and express delivery market.

Mr. Bass's challenge now is to turn around Emery, which has had losses of $145 million since being acquired by Consolidated in April 1989 for $470 million in debt assumption and cash. Emery at that time was known as Emery Air Freight Corp.

Though Emery's losses narrowed to $36.6 million in the second quarter from $70 million in the first period, analysts say its future remains highly uncertain.

"The air freight market is treacherous and competition is fierce," analyst G.A. Lidgerwood of First Boston Corp. observed in a recent investment report. "Therein lies the investment dilemma."

James R. Allen, vice president of Consolidated, attributed Emery's losses to increased competition, a slowdown in the international market and a softening of the domestic air freight business. Mr. Allen said Emery also alienated some customers by imposing a 7% fuel surcharge in June 1989.

But this year, Mr. Allen said, Emery has been able to cut losses significantly by trimming the number of terminal facilities, revamping flight schedules and making other adjustments to increase operating efficiency. Mr. Allen added that Consolidated is leaving it up to Mr. Bass to determine other ways to increase revenue.

Reached by telephone yesterday, Mr. Bass said that he hadn't yet had time to devise any specific money-making strategies, pending a further look at the company he will be heading. Mr. Bass most recently was president and chief executive officer of Cooper Cos., New York.

Cooper said it named Richard W. Turner, president of CooperVision, its optometric and ophthalmic business, as its interim president and chief operating officer.

Analyst George V. Robertson of Alex. Brown in Baltimore said that Mr. Bass probably will concentrate on increasing Emery's business volume and revenue rather than on imposing any more cutbacks.

Mr. Bass's position at Emery is a newly created one. Donald G. Berger, who had headed the subsidiary as Emery president, will continue in that title but will report to Mr. Bass, according to Mr. Allen.

Consolidated's stock closed at $12.375, up 25 cents, yesterday in New York Stock Exchange composite trading.
