What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/06/90

VEHICLE SALES PLUMMETED in the U.S. last month. North American-made car and truck sales fell 21% in late August. Overall sales of domestic and imported vehicles for the month fell 17% from the 1989 period. The drop reflects consumers' uncertainty about the softening U.S. economy amid the Mideast crisis. ---

World oil supplies are shrinking due to the Mideast crisis. A U.S. official said shortages could appear by the middle of the fourth quarter.

---

Franklin Savings' takeover by the government was rejected by a federal judge, who ordered that control of the institution be returned today to its previous management.

The House banking chief proposed drastic cuts in deposit insurance.

---

Prosecutors in the Milken case will attempt to present to a U.S. judge their full case against the former Drexel executive, even though the government accepted a plea bargain to six charges tied to unlawful trading. Ivan Boesky and others may be called to testify to buttress the government's bid for a stiff sentence.

---

Both parties are divided internally over deficit-reduction plans, and the gulf between the Republicans and Democrats appears as great as ever.

---

The dollar fell sharply as investors bet that tomorrow's jobs data will push the Fed to cut interest rates. Stocks and bonds closed higher.

---

Tokyo stock prices fell again on fears that futures settlements next week could spark a massive sell-off. The Nikkei Index plunged 829.30 points, or 3.3%, to 24078.34.

---

Alex. Brown & Sons plans to nearly double its New York City presence. The Baltimore-based brokerage firm has been recruiting brokers from Wall Street rivals.

---

The Kuwaiti asset-management agency in London opened a multibillion-dollar lending facility to ease Kuwaiti banks' liquidity problems.

---

College football and ABC Sports are the targets of a complaint the FTC plans to file today, alleging antitrust violations in a TV contract.

---

The FDA demanded that Ralston-Purina and five other firms justify their health claims about oat bran.

---

The Fed effectively crippled a Delaware law that was expected to allow big banks to sell insurance nationwide. The move was aimed at a Citicorp unit's business, but banking officials say it will also bar other banks from taking advantage of the law.

---

IBM unveiled its long-awaited next generation of mainframes, which will face competition from new models from Hitachi and Fujitsu.
