REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: Students Unite]

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/06/90

Say this for the new thinking in the Soviet Union: They are at least able to recognize what needs fixing. Their schools, for instance. It now appears that the Soviets are exploring the choice movement in education. Choice is among the reforms being touted by Edward Dneprov, the new Soviet minister of education. He told the London Times that he has long wanted to dismantle the centralized and authoritarian Soviet education system. He foresees instead a system of independent public and free-market private schools that will accept school vouchers equivalent to the cost of educating a child for one year. Pilot programs are already in the works in Moscow and Rostov. Given the vociferous opposition to choice from the educational establishment in the U.S., Soviet students may have the freedom to attend the school of their choice before Americans do.
