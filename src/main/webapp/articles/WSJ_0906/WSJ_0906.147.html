Who's News: Dallas Fed Appoints McTeer as President

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/06/90

WASHINGTON -- Robert D. McTeer Jr., a 22-year veteran of the Federal Reserve System, was named president of the Federal Reserve Bank of Dallas. Mr. McTeer, 47 years old, is currently senior vice president of the Federal Reserve Bank of Richmond, in charge of its Baltimore branch. He will succeed Robert H. Boykin, who retires Feb. 1 after 37 years at the Dallas Fed, the last 10 as president.

Bush administration officials said that Mr. McTeer's chief rival for the Dallas post was Michael Darby, currently undersecretary of commerce for economic affairs and formerly assistant Treasury secretary for economic policy in the Reagan administration.

Mr. McTeer, who earned a doctorate in international and monetary economics at the University of Georgia, declined to describe his economic philosophy. For the past decade, his responsibilities at the Fed have been primarily "managerial" rather than policy-oriented, he said.

Presidents of the 12 regional Fed banks oversee banks in their jurisdictions and join the seven Fed governors in Washington in making monetary policy. Five of the Fed presidents sit as voting members of the policy-making Open Market Committee at any one time; Mr. McTeer won't have a vote until 1993.

A president of a regional Fed bank is chosen by the directors of that bank, subject to approval by the Fed's board of governors in Washington.
