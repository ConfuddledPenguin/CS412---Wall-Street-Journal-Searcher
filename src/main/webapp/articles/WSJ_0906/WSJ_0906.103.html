Business Brief -- Magna International Inc.: Plan to Reduce, Restructure Debt Is Given to Lenders

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/06/90

Magna International Inc. said it presented lenders with a plan to reduce and restructure its debt. The Markham, Ontario, auto parts maker said its long-term and short-term debt totaled 1.1 billion Canadian dollars (US$949 million) March 31.

It didn't elaborate on other elements of the plan, but said it included debt reduction of about C$400 million through July 1991, mostly through asset sales and turning some operations into joint ventures.

Magna said sales and joint ventures already arranged will generate C$184 million. It said other divestitures are expected to generate about C$150 million more.

Magna said in March it was in technical default on some loans. It had a C$203 million net loss on $1.41 billion of revenue in the nine months ended April 30 after taking a second-quarter charge of C$153 million to write down unprofitable operations.

The company said its asset sales would leave it with about 100 factories and "capacity" for more than C$2.5 billion in annual sales. Magna said results have improved recently, leading to a "small" operating profit in May and June.
