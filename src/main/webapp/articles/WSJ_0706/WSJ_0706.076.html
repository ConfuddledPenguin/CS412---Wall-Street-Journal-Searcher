Marketing & Media: `Die Harder' Vies to Grab Record on Summer Opening

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/06/90

HOLLYWOOD -- Judging by early box office returns from the July Fourth holiday, News Corp.'s Twentieth Century Fox film "Die Hard 2: Die Harder" looks like it has a chance this coming weekend to produce the top-grossing opening week of any summer movie. On 2,378 screens Wednesday -- a day when fireworks displays and holiday barbecues competed for audiences -- the film grossed $5.9 million. In Tuesday night special screenings on 1,800 screens across the country -- with some theaters featuring one show, others two -- the movie grossed $3,692,833; these totals, however, won't be included in the five-day "weekend gross" figures released Monday.

These figures could make "Die Harder" competitive with the top opener of the summer so far, Carolco Pictures Inc.'s "Total Recall," which grossed more than $35 million in its first week.

"Die Harder," co-produced by action-movie mogul Joel Silver, is estimated to have cost a whopping $62 million just to make, plus an additional $30 million to market and place into theaters. The original "Die Hard," also starring Bruce Willis, grossed $85 million in 1988.

Putting this sequel into production was one of the first official acts by new Fox film Chairman Joe Roth when he assumed his current post less than a year ago. The studio has lagged behind most other major studios in the past few movie seasons.
