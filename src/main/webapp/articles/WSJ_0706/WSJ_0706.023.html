Best Western Foods Inc.

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/06/90

TOKYO -- Kyotaru Co., a major Japanese restaurant chain operator, announced that it acquired all of the shares of Best Western Foods Inc. of the U.S. as part of a strategy to internationalize operations. The purchase price totaled the equivalent of 6.27 billion yen ($41.5 million), according to Kyotaru.

The U.S. food company processes roast beef and markets it to the U.S. sandwich chain Arby's. A Kyotaru spokesman said his company aims to expand the existing sales network that Best Western Foods holds, covering roughly half of 2,500 Arby's shops in the U.S.

The U.S. concern is located in Los Angeles, with annual sales totaling 10.05 billion yen in the latest fiscal year.
