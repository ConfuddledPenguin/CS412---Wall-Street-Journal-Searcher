Taiwan's Trade Surplus

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/06/90

TAIPEI -- Taiwan's trade surplus during the first half of 1990 totaled $4.94 billion, a 20% decline from the same period last year, the government announced Thursday. But the monthly surplus for June was $1.09 billion, up 23% from a year ago, said the directorate general of Budget, Accounting and Statistics.

Exports in the first six months this year totaled $31.87 billion, down 0.8% from the first half of 1989. Imports totaled $26.93 billion during the first half of 1990, an increase of 3.9%, the agency said.

Officials attributed the reduced trade surplus to lower exports. Higher wages and production costs have weakened Taiwan's competitiveness in foreign markets.

Taiwan's surplus with the U.S., its largest trading partner, totaled $4.04 billion in the first half of 1990, down 27% from the same period a year earlier.

Taiwan registered a surplus of $800 million with the U.S. in June, down 17.1% from June 1989.

Taiwan's exports to the U.S. totaled $1.72 billion in June, while imports totaled $920 million.

Taiwan's trade with Japan, its second largest trading partner, registered a deficit of $3.71 billion in the first half this year, rising 15.5% from period a year ago.

The June deficit with Japan was $710 million in June, up 6.1% from the June 1989.

The government has projected a 1990 trade surplus of $11 billion, compared with the $13.93 billion surplus last year.
