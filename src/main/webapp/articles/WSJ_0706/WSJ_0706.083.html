Money Funds' Assets Fell in Latest Week

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/06/90

WASHINGTON -- Assets of the nation's 513 money market funds fell $2.24 billion to $382.7 billion in the week ended July 4, according to the Investment Company Institute, a trade association here. Assets of 250 general purpose funds fell $723.9 million to $133.21 billion, and assets of 102 broker-dealer funds fell $1.29 billion to $157.88 billion. Assets of 161 institutional funds fell $222.8 million to $91.61 billion.

Individual accounts were most affected by the decline in money market fund assets. Dr. Jacob Dreyer, vice president and chief economist at the institute, said, "Strong advances in prices of stocks and bonds in the week preceding the Fourth of July drew some money out of short-term funds, especially those in the broker-dealer category, and into long-term funds."
