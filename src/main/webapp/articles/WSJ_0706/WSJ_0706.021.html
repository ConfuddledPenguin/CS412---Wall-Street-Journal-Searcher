Chrysler Plans to Idle Four Assembly Plants For Model Changes

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/06/90

DETROIT -- Chrysler Corp. said it is temporarily halting production at four of its assembly plants to prepare for model changeovers. The nation's No. 3 auto maker said it is idling for two weeks its production facilities in Belvidere, Ill., Newark, N.J., Sterling Heights, Mich., and St. Louis. The plants build the New Yorker, Imperial, LeBaron, Plymouth Acclaim, Plymouth Sundance, Dodge Spirit, Dodge Shadow and Dodge Daytona. About 10,050 hourly workers will be temporarily idled by the moves, which take effect Monday.

Chrysler said its assembly plant in Belvidere will be closed an additional week because of slow sales. That plant will resume production July 30.

General Motors Corp. said it will close its assembly plants in Van Nuys, Calif., for two weeks beginning Monday, because of slow sales of the Chevrolet Camaros and Pontiac Firebirds built there. The auto maker also said it will halt assembly for the two weeks at its Willow Run, Mich., plant to prepare for production of fleet and special-use vehicles, such as taxicabs and police cars. The plant builds the Chevrolet Corsica.

As previously reported, Ford Motor Co.'s assembly plants will be idle next week, for the auto maker's traditional summer break.

Overall, the 10 major auto makers in the U.S. planned to build 70,022 units this week, a 71% increase over 40,925 built a year ago.
