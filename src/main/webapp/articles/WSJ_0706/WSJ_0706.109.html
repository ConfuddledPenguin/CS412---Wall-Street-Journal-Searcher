Arco Coal Operation In Australia Reaches Tentative Order Pact

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/06/90

SYDNEY -- Arco Coal Australia Inc. said it reached tentative agreement with a number of Japanese and European steelmakers for the purchase of coal from a proposed new mine near Emerald in the state of Queensland. Production capacity at the proposed Gordonstone underground mine is about four million metric tons a year with reserves of about 900 million tons, said Arco Coal Australia, a unit of Los Angeles-based Atlantic Richfield Co.

Development of the mine would cost about 500 million Australian dollars (US$399 million) and take more than two years, Arco Coal Australia estimated. The company has a 65.5% interest in Gordonstone. Suncorp, controlled by the Queensland government, has a 29.5% stake and Lend Lease Corp. of Australia has the remaining 5% interest.

Ian Knight, managing director of Gordonstone Coal Management Pty. Ltd., the venture that would develop the mine, said a memorandum of understanding has been reached with Japanese steelmakers for a five-year coal shipment beginning in 1992 and rising to an annual rate of 1.2 million tons in the third year. The company also has purchase agreements with some Western European government-owned enterprises, he said.

The venture still needs various Australian government approvals and approval from Atlantic Richfield's board of directors. "It's on the agenda for the last week of this month," said a spokesman for Arco's Coal division in Denver. Arco Coal Australia is making agreements with customers before a final decision is reached to proceed with developing the mine.
