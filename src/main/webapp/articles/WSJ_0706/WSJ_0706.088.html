SALLY B. FRANK, the plaintiff in a case in which two Princeton University eating clubs were ordered to admit women, is an associate law professor at New York Law School. An article in the Legal Beat column yesterday incorrectly identified her as an associate professor at New York University Law School. (See: "Law -- Legal Beat: Two Princeton Clubs Must Admit Women" -- WSJ July 5, 1990)

---

CLEVELAND-CLIFFS Inc. said a transaction to restructure ownership of the Savage River iron ore mines in Australia would not have a "material effect" on the company's financial position or operating results. An earlier edition misstated the company's position.

(See: "Business Brief -- Cleveland-Cliffs Inc.: Restructuring Accord Set by Pickands Mather & Co." -- WSJ July 5, 1990)

---

HIBERNIA SAVINGS BANK (LA.) was incorrectly included in the "Index to Businesses" listing in yesterday's editions in reference to an article about a second-quarter loss and a decision to omit the quarterly dividend. The story referenced involved Hibernia Savings Bank of Quincy, Mass., and not Hibernia Corp., the New Orleans-based bank holding company.
