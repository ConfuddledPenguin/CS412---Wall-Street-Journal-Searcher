Business Brief -- Sony Corp.: Purchases of Semiconductors Made in Europe to Increase

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/06/90

Sony Corp. of Japan plans to increase purchases of European-made semiconductors, in line with its target to boost the ratio of foreign-made chips in its products to 20% by July 1991, a spokesman said. Japanese consumer electronics makers have been increasing their foreign chip purchases, in efforts to meet the target set in a five-year agreement between the U.S. and Japan to raise the overall share of foreign chips sold in Japan to 20% of the market.

The exact nature of the agreement, which ends in July 1991, is a matter of dispute, with the U.S. claiming that Japan pledged to meet the 20% goal and Japan saying it only promised to make efforts to reach the target.

The proportion of foreign-made chips purchased in June by Sony was between 15% and 16% of its total chip needs, most of which were U.S.-made, the spokesman said.
