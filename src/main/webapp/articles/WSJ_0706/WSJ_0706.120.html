Massachusetts Moves To Increase Its Taxes

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/06/90

BOSTON (AP) -- A conference committee of the Massachusetts Senate and House approved an increased income tax and a sales tax on some services to help reduce the state budget deficit estimated at about $1 billion. The proposed tax increases still must be approved by both the House and Senate.

The 5% sales tax on services -- opposed strongly by various business groups -- was modified by the conference committee. It would apply to fees charged by lawyers, architects, engineers and accountants, and to commercial electric and gas use, telecommunications, credit-reporting services and graphic design, among others.

The sales tax wouldn't apply until a threshold of $20,000 a year in fees per customer was reached.

Under the plan endorsed by the conference committee, the income tax, now 5.75%, would rise retroactively to 5.95% initially then would rise to 6.25% for the fiscal year that began July 1, and then would drop back to 5.95% in 1992.

Even with the tax increases, legislators estimated that the state still will have to cut spending to balance its $13.6 billion budget. Massachusetts has the lowest general obligation bond rating in the nation because of its financial problems.
