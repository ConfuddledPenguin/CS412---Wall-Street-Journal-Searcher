International Brief -- Berisford International PLC: Pretax Profit for 6 Months Skidded 41% to $38 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/06/90

Berisford International PLC reported that pretax profit for the six months ended March 31 skidded 41% to #21.4 million ($38 million) from #36.4 million a year earlier, as strong earnings in the British conglomerate's main sugar operations failed to offset losses in its property, commodity and financial services units. Profit before taxes, less minority interests and before extraordinary items dropped 22% to #16.1 million from #20.8 million. The latest results included a #160.6 million extraordinary loss, mostly from provisions against investments in the declining New York property market; year-earlier extraordinary losses totaled #38.5 million. Earnings per share fell 23% to 3.3 pence from 4.3 pence. The net loss attributable to shareholders, after taxes and special items, widened to #144.5 million from #26.3 million.

Sales rose 10% to #998 million from #907 million.

Berisford said that, given its weak financial position, the board decided to forgo an interim dividend. A year earlier, the company paid an interim dividend of 1.8 pence a share.
