Citicorp Pushes Plan to Sell Insurance in Delaware

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/06/90

NEW YORK -- Citicorp, responding to insurance industry efforts to prevent it from using a Delaware subsidiary to sell insurance, told the Federal Reserve that there aren't any legal grounds to prohibit it from taking advantage of a recently enacted Delaware law. Since the passage of the legislation giving state-chartered banks insurance powers, Citicorp has been preparing to underwrite and sell insurance through its state-chartered unit, Citibank Delaware.

In a memo to the Fed, the nation's largest banking company said federal banking regulations allow state-chartered subsidiaries of banks to engage in any activities in which state-chartered banks themselves can engage.

The insurance industry has argued that the Delaware law permits banks to circumvent federal regulations that prohibit banks from selling and underwriting insurance.
