Pfizer to Sell Citric Acid Unit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/06/90

NEW YORK -- Pfizer Inc. said it agreed to sell its citric acid business before the end of the year to Archer-Daniels-Midland Co., a Decatur, Ill., processor of soybean, wheat and corn products, including ethanol fuel. A spokesman for Pfizer, a health care and pharmaceuticals concern, said it was too soon to discuss terms of the transaction. Pfizer said the sale is expected to generate a gain.

Pfizer's citric acid business reported sales of more than $180 million in 1989 and employs 700 people. Citric acid products are used in foods and beverages and as a replacement for phosphates in laundry detergents.
