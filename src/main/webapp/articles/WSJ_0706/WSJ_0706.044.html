REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: Modern Baseball

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/06/90

We keep thinking of how Yankee pitcher Andy Hawkins lost a no-hitter because a rookie left-fielder dropped a fly ball. "That's baseball," shrugged the rookie. Sad but true. Why, we ask, should baseball continue as if it were still possible to find a league's worth of young players who can hit, field and chew gum at the same time? Perhaps baseball ought to consider revising the pathetic spectacle of shortstops who bat .200 and slugging outfielders who can't catch. Maybe baseball should do what everyone else has done: Specialize. Instead of one designated hitter, how about nine designated hitters and nine designated fielders? Traditionalists would scream, but, Pop, times have changed since you were a boy. Kids don't play pickup baseball all day anymore; they play only in organized leagues, which means some rookies arrive in the Majors with the fielding skills of a Little Leaguer. Let kids see what real fielding and batting used to look like, before they're extinct.
