Conference Board Reports Help-Wanted Ads Decline

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/06/90

NEW YORK -- The Conference Board reported that its index of help-wanted advertising continued its steady decline in May, slipping two points to its lowest level in four years. The independent business research group said the index fell to 137 in May, down from April's revised 139. The index lags 15 points behind the year-earlier level of 152. The index is based on a survey of help-wanted advertising volume in 51 major newspapers.

Conference Board economist Ken Goldstein said, "The word `cautious' best describes the labor market right now. There is a definite softness in demand." The index, a seasonally adjusted indicator of hiring plans, uses 1967 as a base of 100.

Despite increases of 7.5% in the South Atlantic region, 2.6% in the West South Central region and 0.6% in the East South Central region, the remaining six regions of the country showed declines in May from the April level.

The most severe declines continue to be in New England and the Middle Atlantic regions. Both were down about 9% in their regional indices in the past two months, and both recorded steep declines in help-wanted ad volume, by about 33% and 20%, respectively, from year-earlier levels.

Still, Mr. Goldstein expects a "turnaround," first in the Western labor markets and then eastward, spurred by export activity in the second half of this year.
