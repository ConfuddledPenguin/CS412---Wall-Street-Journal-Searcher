U.S. Judge Dismisses Insider Trading Suit Against Loomis Sayles

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/06/90

NEW YORK -- A federal judge in Manhattan dismissed an insider trading suit against Loomis, Sayles & Co., an investment affiliate of New England Mutual Life Insurance Co. The suit alleged that the investment company sold its 12.6% stake in Texas Air Corp., or about 5.3 million shares, on Jan. 24 based on insider knowledge. The next day, Eastern Air Lines, one of Texas Air's two carriers, met with unsecured creditors to outline a reorganization plan under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code, the lawsuit contended. Details of the plan were disclosed that same day, causing the stock price to drop.

The lawsuit, filed at the end of January by a Texas Air shareholder, was immediately called "frivolous" and "absurd" by New England Mutual executives. Charles L. Finlayson, general counsel at Loomis, said that G. Kenneth Heebner, then Loomis's investment manager, had decided to sell the Texas Air stock because increased competition led him to reduce his earnings estimates for the airline. Mr. Finlayson said Mr. Heebner began selling the stock at least six weeks before the final block of about 3.4 million shares was sold on Jan. 24.

Last Monday, attorneys for the shareholder, Frank Debora, filed a stipulation of dismissal with Judge Kevin Thomas Duffy of U.S. District Court in Manhattan. Judge Duffy approved the dismissal yesterday.

"I'm just satisfied that it has been put behind us," said Loomis's Mr. Finlayson. "The suit should never have been filed in the first place."
