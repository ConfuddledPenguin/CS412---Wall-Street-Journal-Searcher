Who's News: Five Dissident Holders Elected to Two Boards Of Race Track Firms

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/06/90

SAN MATEO, Calif. -- Five members of a dissident shareholder group have been elected to two separate boards of companies that own and operate the Bay Meadows race track. The California Jockey Club elected Marilyn K. Gunderson and Richard E. Perrazo as board members. Bay Meadows Operating Co. named Grant G. Gunderson, Doris L. Johnson, and Louis J. Poletti to its board. Both boards have six members.

The dissidents replace a slate of management-appointed directors, but didn't win enough seats in a May election to control either of the boards. The shareholder group, which holds a stake in California Jockey Club, has complained that the companies placed too much emphasis on real estate operations at the expense of running the race track.

A spokesman for California Jockey said that the new directors hadn't yet made any demands of management. The California Jockey Club is one of three publicly traded real estate investment trusts whose primary asset is a horse track here; Bay Meadows Operating is a publicly traded concern that oversees track operations.
