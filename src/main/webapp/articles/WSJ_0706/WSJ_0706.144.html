Technology: Altos Computer Set To Be Bought Out By Acer of Taiwan

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/06/90

SAN JOSE, Calif. -- Altos Computer Systems said it agreed in principle to be acquired by Acer Group, a Taiwan computer maker, for $94 million. The $8.35-a-share offer is considerably higher than the range of $4.87 a share to $7.37 a share that Altos stock has traded in since January. However, news of the offer sharply pushed up Altos's share price in national over-the-counter trading yesterday. The stock closed at $8.25, up 87.5 cents from Tuesday's close of $7.375.

Altos, which had been shopping for a suitor, first disclosed its discussions with Acer last week. The U.S. company, a maker of multiuser computer networks, has stumbled in recent quarters, the result of bloated inventories and higher-than-anticipated costs associated with new products.

Under the terms of the buy-out, Altos would be operated as a separate unit of Acer, maintaining its own corporate name and products. The Acer offer gives Altos "the critical mass and name recognition" it needs to grow, said Ron Conway, president of Altos.

Acer, a maker of IBM-compatible computers, said the buy-out would help Acer improve the systems capabilities of its laptop and notebook-size computers. Acer is Taiwan's largest computer maker, with more than $700 million in revenue; the company has a network of 10,000 dealers in more than 70 countries.

Altos designs, makes and markets multiuser computer networks for distribution in about 60 countries. For its third fiscal quarter ended March 31, the company posted a net loss of $1.7 million, or 16 cents a share, on sales of $32.6 million. A year earlier, it had a loss of $3.7 million, or 31 cents a share, on revenue of $32.4 million.
