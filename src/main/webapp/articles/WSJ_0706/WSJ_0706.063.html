Credit Ratings: Three Finnish Banks Get Ratings Cut by S&P

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/06/90

NEW YORK -- Standard & Poor's Corp. cut ratings on the debt of three Finnish banks, reflecting an increasingly deregulated and competitive operating environment. The three banks "have some common problems," each with its own particular difficulties, "but it all comes out of the deregulated and competitive market," said Virginia L. Manzer, an analyst at S&P.

The credit-rating company cut the senior unsecured debt of Kansallis-Osake-Pankki to double-A-minus from double-A. The bank's subordinated debt falls to single-A-plus from double-A-minus. A total of $3 billion of securities are affected.

S&P cut Union Bank of Finland Ltd.'s $1.6 billion of senior unsecured debt to double-A-minus from double-A. And Postipankki Ltd.'s $415 million of senior unsecured obligations dated Jan. 1, 1988, and later fell to double-A-plus from triple-A.
