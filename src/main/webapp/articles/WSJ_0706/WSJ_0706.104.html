Trump Pays Property Tax To New York City on Time

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/06/90

NEW YORK -- Despite his troubled finances, real estate and casino developer Donald Trump made a multimillion-dollar property tax payment yesterday as requested by the city, a spokeswoman for the Trump Organization said. "It was paid in full," the spokeswoman said.

New York City officials had asked the owners of more than 400 of the biggest real estate parcels in New York to make a total of $1.1 billion in payments by midnight yesterday.

The semiannual payments are normally due July 1, but because of delays in passing the city budget, the deadline was pushed back to July 5 for utilities and other properties valued at more than $15 million.

Among other assets, Trump owed $2.2 million on a 76-acre former railroad yard on Manhattan's West Side, $1 million on the commercial section of Trump Tower and $900,000 on the Trump Parc condominiums, according to the New York City Department of Finance.

Trump last month escaped default on $351 million of bonds issued for his Atlantic City, N.J., casinos by winning a bank loan agreement and $20 million in fresh funds. The loan allowed him to make a crucial overdue interest payment.

Trump had negotiated for weeks with his bankers in an effort to obtain a $65 million loan but ran into opposition from some lenders who did not want to participate in the new package. In the end, seven banks agreed in principle to lend the larger sum and released $20 million immediately.
