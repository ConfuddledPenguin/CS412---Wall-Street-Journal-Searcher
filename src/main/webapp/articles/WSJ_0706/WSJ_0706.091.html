OTC Focus: Market Is Lower on Earnings Outlook; Transportation Index Is Poor Performer

  • Author: Craig S. Smith Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 07/06/90

Unlisted stock prices fell further in a market shaken by more weak second-quarter earnings outlooks. Weakening expectations rattled the OTC market Monday and Tuesday, and more pessimistic projections disturbed investors yesterday.

"They're definitely putting the jitters into the market," says David Memmott, OTC trader at Dillon, Read & Co. Mr. Memmott says OTC investor enthusiasm could be damped further by a few disappointments early in the second-quarter reporting period that will begin later this month.

Many players stayed home yesterday, leaving some OTC trading desks thinly staffed in anticipation of light activity for the remainder of the week. The absences were evident in volume, which totaled a mere 97.8 million shares, compared to Tuesday's 131 million shares.

The Nasdaq Composite Index dropped 2.57, or 0.56%, to 459.19, but suffered less than the New York Stock Exchange Composite Index, which fell about 1.1%.

Robert O'Toole Sr., head of institutional sales trading at Shearson Lehman Hutton, says "buyers were on strike" yesterday.

OTC advancers trailed decliners, 676 to 1,094.

Late Tuesday, Microcom slashed its previously reported fiscal fourth-quarter and year-end results and said it also expects to report a bigger loss for the first quarter than had originally been anticipated.

The company's stock lost nearly half of its value in June after the company announced that it would lose money in the first quarter ended June 30. The stock dropped nearly 30% yesterday, down 2 5/8 to 6 1/8.

On Tuesday, Mentor Graphics startled owners of its shares by announcing that its second quarter profit would amount to a mere 10 cents a share or less, far below expectations and down sharply from the 29 cents a share reported in the quarter a year ago. The company's stock suffered a 4 3/8 point loss Tuesday and fell another 7/8 to 16 3/4 yesterday.

Specialty retailer Sharper Image slipped 3/8 to 4 7/8 after announcing that it expects to report a second-quarter loss of 15 cents to 20 cents a share, compared with last year's second-quarter profit of one cent a share. The company blamed "the current retail environment" for the loss.

Roadway Services fell 1/2 to 34 1/2. The trucking company reported this morning that profit in the second-quarter fell to 58 cents a share from 67 cents in the same quarter a year earlier. The latest quarter's earnings were below analysts' estimates.

Other OTC transportation stocks slipped, including trucker Yellow Freight Systems, off 1/4 to 26 7/8, and school-bus operator Laidlaw, whose Class B shares eased 3/8 to 23 1/8. The Nasdaq Transportation Index was the worst performing sector of the OTC market, dropping nearly 0.9%.

Hibernia Savings Bank lost 1 1/4 to 5 3/4 after omitting its quarterly dividend. On Tuesday, the bank said it will report losses for its second quarter and six months due to real estate loan-loss provisions.

Workstation makers Tandon and Sun Microsystems both edged higher. Tandon gained 1/16 to 3 13/16 on heavy volume, while Sun Microsystems gained 1/8 to 33 1/4.

Apple Computer, however, fell 1/2 to 43 1/2, while chip-maker Intel lost 1/2 to 47.

Foremost fell another 3 3/8 to 22 1/4. On Tuesday, the stock dropped 4 1/2 after the insurer said it had ended efforts to find a buyer for the company.

Hamilton Oil added 4 1/2 to 39 3/4 after it announced the discovery of natural gas in the Irish Sea off the West coast of England.

Altos Computer gained 7/8 to 8 1/4 after signing an agreement to be acquired by Acer Group for $8.35 a share.

NEC's American Depositary Receipts rose 1 1/2 to 66. The Japanese company has unveiled a mainframe computer that it calls the world's fastest. However, analysts are skeptical that the new machine will boost the company's tiny share of the world mainframe market, according to an article that appeared in this newspaper yesterday.

CCAIR, which does business as USAir Express, said it filed a petition for reorganization under Chapter 11 of the U.S. Bankruptcy Code. The stock lost 7/8 to 1 1/4.

Insurance brokers firm Poe & Associates added 3/4 to 12, while fellow brokers Hilb, Rogal & Hamilton rose 1/4 to 14. Yesterday's "Heard on the Street" column in this newspaper reported that some analysts are saying now is the time to buy insurance brokerage stocks. Those analysts say the pricing cycle for property and casualty insurance has bottomed and may be ready for an upswing. Poe and Hilb, Rogal were two firms cited as good buys by an analyst quoted in the story.

Specialty soap maker Neutrogena added 1 3/8 to 23 1/8. At least one analyst attributed the move to "short covering," in which investors who have sold borrowed shares of the stock buy new shares to repay their stock loans, according to Dow Jones Professional Investor Report. Socalled short sellers hope to gain from a drop in the price of a stock, and cover their loans when they believe a stock's price has hit bottom.
