Foreign Vehicle Sales Set Japanese Record With June Rise of 25%

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 07/06/90

TOKYO -- Sales of imported cars, trucks and buses in Japan climbed 25% to set a monthly record for June of 22,253 units from 17,840 units a year earlier, the Japan Automobile Importers' Association said. Statistics show sales of imported, foreign motor vehicles topped year-earlier levels for the 78th month in a row.

Sales for the first six months of this year set a record of 113,622 units, representing a 40% gain from 81,318 units in the first half of 1989.

The association said booming personal consumption was behind the strong gain, as Japan's economy continues to expand for the 43rd month in a row.

The strong increase from year-earlier levels was traced in part to a Japanese consumption tax imposed in April, 1989, which replaced a higher commodity tax. The price gap between Japanese cars and imported cars also has narrowed as Japanese auto makers have unveiled more expensive models.

By country, in all imported vehicle categories, West Germany was the source of 13,531 units, up 16% from 11,708 units. The trend of rising sales of West German cars since the introduction of a consumption tax is not likely to change, despite an average price rise of 3% for West German models in the domestic market.

Sales of all U.S.-made vehicles grew 28% in June to 2,898 units from 2,258 units. Sales of all British vehicles registered a 67% upsurge to 2,230 units in June from 1,338 units.

French car sales showed a 43% jump, totaling 1,520 units, while Swedish sales registered a 29% increase to 1,305 units.
