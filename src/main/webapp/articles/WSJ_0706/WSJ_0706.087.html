New Securities Issues

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/06/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: MORTGAGES

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $500 million of real estate mortgage investment conduit securities is being offered in 18 classes by Bear, Stearns & Co. The offering, Series 1990-93, is backed by Fannie Mae 9 1/2% securities. Details on prices and yields weren't immediately available.

Federal National Mortgage Association -- reopened three debenture issues outstanding for a total of $92 million in June: $17 million was added to the issue maturing July 12, 1993, with a coupon of 8.45%; $25 million was added to the issue due Sept. 20, 1996, with a coupon of 8.75%; and $50 million was added to another issue due Sept. 20, 1996, with a coupon of 8.75%. These securities were sold through the facilities of a debenture selling group member for the accounts of customers.

EUROBONDS

Banca Commerciale Italiano, London branch, (Italian parent) -- Issuing via Law Debenture Trust Corp. $200 million of variable-rate depository receipts due in July 2000, with Merrill Lynch International Ltd. as sole manager. Margin is 1/4 point above the three-month London interbank offered rate. Alternatively, coupon to float at 1/2 point above Libor for the first five years and thereafter at 5/8 point above. Fees undisclosed.

Eurofima (agency) -- 200 billion Italian lire of 12 1/8% bonds due Aug. 9, 1995, priced at 101.35 to yield 12.24% less fees, via Banco di Roma. Fees 1 3/4.

Eurofima (agency) -- 75 million Australian dollars of 15% bonds due Sept. 24, 1992, priced at 102 via Bankers Trust International Ltd. Fees 1 1/4.

Fuji International Finance Hong Kong Ltd. (Japanese parent) -- #80 million of floating-rate notes due July 24, 1994, priced at 100.20 via Fuji International Finance Ltd. Coupon will float at 0.35 point below the three-month Libor. Puttable on any interest payment date. Guaranteed by Fuji Bank. Fees 0.20.

Topdanmark AS (Denmark) -- 200 million marks of 9% bonds due Aug. 7, 1995, priced at 101 to yield 8.74%, via Deutsche Bank. Fees 2.
