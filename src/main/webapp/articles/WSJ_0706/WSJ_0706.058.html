Sundstrand to Wrap 5-Month Brazil Data Into Its 2nd Period

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/06/90

ROCKFORD, Ill. -- Sundstrand Corp. said it will "accelerate" the financial reporting of its Brazilian operation, wrapping five months' results from the financially pressed unit into the second quarter. Like those of many other companies, Sundstrand's operation in Brazil has been hit hard by effects of a stringent government-imposed anti-inflation plan.

The company's accounting move will consolidate the bulk of the worst fiscal news from Brazil into one quarter, and may also lessen misgivings investors have shown toward Sundstrand stock because of the company's Brazilian exposure.

In April, the maker of aerospace and industrial equipment said second-quarter earnings would be reduced by $2 million to $3 million as a result of its problems in Brazil. But yesterday, the company announced that it expects losses at the Brazilian unit will lower overall net by about $7 million.

In the year-ago second period, Sundstrand's net-boosted by a $10.3 million gain from sale of a division -- was $37.9 million, or 94 cents a share, on sales of $394.3 million.

Sundstrand has historically consolidated the results from Sundstrand do Brazil on a three-month lag basis, with the earnings from the quarter ended in December appearing, for example, in the parent's firstquarter earnings.

"Given the current economic situation in Brazil," Sundstrand said it would be "more informative to its shareholders if (the company) reduces this financial reporting lag to one month."

Brazil's wrenching economic program is pinching a host of U.S. companies who operate there: Last month, the stock of Caterpillar Inc. went into a tailspin from which it hasn't yet recovered, after the heavy-equipment maker disclosed bigger-than-anticipated problems in the region. Other companies feeling earnings pressure from Brazil include Allied-Signal Inc., Armco Inc. and Whirlpool Corp.
