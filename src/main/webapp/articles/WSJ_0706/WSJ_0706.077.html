Coal Mining Firm, Dairy Face Fines On Safety Charges

  • Author: Albert R. Karr
  • Date: 07/06/90

WASHINGTON -- The Labor Department proposed fines of $508,000 against Pyro Mining Co., a coal-mining concern, and $1 million against Johanna Farms, a dairy and citrus-product packaging firm, on charges of safety violations. The department's Mine Safety and Health Administration cited Pyro Mining's William Station mine in Union County, Ky., for 121 violations, including alleged electrical-safety violations and failure to make required safety examinations.

The mine-safety agency said the proposed fine stemmed from a methane explosion at the mine last September that killed 10 people. The agency said the explosion occurred after explosive methane gas accumulated without being detected.

The penalties would be a record amount for the mine-safety agency, four times the largest fine assessed for any previous single mine accident. The violations were "flagrant" and showed Pyro Mining's "negligence," William Tattersall, assistant secretary of labor for the agency, said.

Separately, the department's Occupational Safety and Health Administration cited Johanna Farms, Flemington, N.J., for violating safety and record-keeping standards. The company, which packages dairy products, citrus juices and yogurt, is a unit of John LaBatt Ltd., a holding company based in North London, Ontario.

Johanna Farms was cited for 197 willful instances of failure to report injuries and illnesses and for 23 instances of allowing workers to be exposed to unguarded machinery. OSHA said 34 employees also were exposed to unguarded sprockets and chains, among other safety violations. Gerard Scannell, assistant secretary of labor for OSHA, said the company suffered "a serious breakdown in its internal safety and health program."

Pyro Mining has 30 days to contest its citations and proposed fine before the independent Federal Mine Safety and Health Review Commission. Johanna Farms has 15 days to contest its citations and fine before the Occupational Safety and Health Review Commission.
