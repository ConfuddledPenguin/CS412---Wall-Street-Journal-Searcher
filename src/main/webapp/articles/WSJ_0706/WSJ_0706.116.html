First Executive Chairman Took Big Pay Cut in 1989

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/06/90

LOS ANGELES -- First Executive Corp.'s chairman and chief executive officer, Fred Carr, saw his 1989 compensation drop nearly as drastically as the junk-bond market did last year, taking a toll on the life insurance holding company. The company's newly issued proxy statement for its annual meeting July 30 shows that Mr. Carr received cash compensation of $604,734 in 1989, or roughly a third of the $1.8 million he received in 1988. The 1988 sum included $1.25 million in deferred incentive compensation in addition to a base salary of $600,000, according to last year's proxy.

A new employment contract signed in December provides, among other things, that Mr. Carr would receive $6 million in cash or three times his base salary and average incentive pay for the two preceeding years, if he resigns under certain conditions following a change in control at the company.

The filing further states that First Executive, once a key client of Drexel Burnham Lambert Inc., bought about $2.35 billion and sold about $1.1 billion in securities through Drexel last year before the firm's collapse. It also holds about $19 million of commercial paper issued by Drexel that is in default.

Executive Life, First Executive's operating unit, sued Drexel to recover part of the unpaid first-quarter rent on office space in Beverly Hills, Calif., that it sublet to the brokerage firm. In May, Drexel paid Executive Life $400,000 to settle the suit, the filing shows.
