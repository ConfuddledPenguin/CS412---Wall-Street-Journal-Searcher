Business Brief -- Societe Nationale Elf Aquitaine: French Oil Concern to Buy Lines of Amoco in Britain

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/06/90

Societe Nationale Elf Aquitaine, the French state-controlled oil company, said it agreed with Amoco Corp. to purchase most of the Chicago-based energy company's so-called downstream, or refining and marketing, operations in Britain by Aug. 1. The business included in the transaction would double Elf's share of the British petroleum market, the company said. About 200 service stations will be handed over to Elf, along with a 70% effective interest in the 100,000 barrel-a-day Milford Haven refinery, and various terminal and pipeline interests.

Elf said the arrangement won't include Amoco's synthetic fabrics operations in Britain. British affiliates of other Amoco units, such as Amoco U.K. Exploration Co. and Amoco Chemical, also won't be affected by the sale.

The purchase is part of Elf's strategy of reinforcing its distribution network, after closing about 40% of its European service stations in the past six years.
