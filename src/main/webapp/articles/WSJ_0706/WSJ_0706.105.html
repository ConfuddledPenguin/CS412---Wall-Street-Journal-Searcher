Placer Dome's Offer For Stikine Shares Yields Tiny Results

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/06/90

VANCOUVER, British Columbia -- Placer Dome Inc. said it raised its stake in Stikine Resources Ltd. to just 45.3% from 45%, under an extended 67.50 Canadian dollar (US$58.09) a share tender offer that expired late Wednesday. The bid result leaves control of Stikine, half-owner of British Columbia's rich Eskay Creek gold project, undecided. Placer Dome's spokesman said a 6.1% block of Stikine shares that a Toronto arbitrage firm disclosed it held last month wasn't tendered to the offer.

"We're examining our options," in light of the bid results, the spokesman said. "We need to knock on some doors to try to pry loose" the remaining Stikine shares, he said.

Placer Dome's rival, Toronto-based Corona Corp., owns 3% of Stikine and has agreed to acquire a further 42% of the company from its principal shareholders. Pending regulatory approval, Corona further plans an offer to acquire all Stikine shares that aren't held by Placer Dome.

Corona currently owns a 34% voting stake in Prime Resources Group Inc., Stikine's Eskay Creek partner.

Placer Dome and Corona, both major gold producers, have been battling for control of Eskay Creek, though Corona said earlier this week it will pursue talks with Placer Dome that could lead to joint ownership of the gold property. Placer Dome's spokesman said it would "look favorably" on talks with Corona.

In Toronto Stock Exchange trading yesterday, Stikine closed at C$71 a share, up C$1. Placer Dome's shares closed at C$18.25 each, down 37.5 Canadian cents, and Corona's Class A shares closed at C$7.375 each, down 25 cents.
