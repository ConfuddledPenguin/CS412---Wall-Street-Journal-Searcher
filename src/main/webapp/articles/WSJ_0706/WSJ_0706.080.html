Credit Ratings: MNC Financial Gets Lower S&P Ratings

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/06/90

NEW YORK -- Standard & Poor's Corp. said it lowered its ratings on MNC Financial Inc. securities of about $2.1 billion, including a preferred stock issue that S&P gave a rating below investment grade. The rating agency lowered its rating on the preferred stock to double-B-plus from triple-B-plus. Ratings on senior debt were lowered to triple-B from single-A-minus and on subordinated debt to triple-B-minus from triple-B-plus.

Also lowered to triple-B-plus/A-2 from single-A/A-1 were ratings on certificates of deposit and letter of credit-backed issues by units Maryland National Bank, American Security Bank and MBNA America.

Ratings for commercial paper of MNC Financial and unit American Security Corp. were affirmed at A-2.

S&P said the downgrades reflect "continued weakness in MNC's real estate markets and its significant concentration in construction and commercial real estate loans."

A spokesman at MNC, a financial services company based in Baltimore, declined to comment.
