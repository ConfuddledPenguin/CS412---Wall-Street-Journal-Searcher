Business Brief -- National Intergroup Inc.: Court Rules Amendment To Shareholder Plan Invalid

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/06/90

Centaur Partners, New York, has gained an edge in its proxy fight to win control of National Intergroup Inc. of Pittsburgh. Centaur said Delaware Chancery Court ruled that a National Intergroup amendment to its shareholder rights plan was not valid because it was adopted without shareholder approval. National Intergroup, an oil transportation and aluminum and steel fabrication concern, said it plans to appeal the chancery court ruling.

Under the Jan. 26, 1990, amendment, National Intergroup's "poison pill" would take effect if any single shareholder acquired more than 10% of its common stock. A poison pill is a tactic designed to make hostile takeovers prohibitively expensive.

The poison pill, which granted purchase rights to other shareholders, previously took effect only after a shareholder acquired 20% or more of the company.

Centaur currently holds 16.5% of National Intergroup's 21.8 million shares outstanding.
