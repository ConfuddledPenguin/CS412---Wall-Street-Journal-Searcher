Business Brief -- Northern Telecom Ltd.: New Brunswick Facility Set For Closing by November

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/06/90

Northern Telecom Ltd., in a continuing effort to streamline operations, said it will close a small manufacturing facility in St. John, New Brunswick, by November. The facility, which employs 122 people and has been operating since 1945, manufactures inductors and transformers used in older technology telecommunications equipment. The shutdown will bring to four the number of facilities Toronto-based Northern has closed since 1988.

"The business for those parts {made in St. John} has gone away as the technology changed," a spokesman said. "Some production will be absorbed into locations where we have overcapacity."

He said some workers can try out for sales positions in a new customer center Northern plans to open in St. John by year end. For employees who don't stay, Northern has set aside $5 million, most of which will be used for severance pay and to aid relocation and retraining.
