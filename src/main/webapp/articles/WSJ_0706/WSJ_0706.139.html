Visa and MasterCard Sue Group of Telemarketers

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/06/90

LOS ANGELES -- Visa International Inc. and MasterCard International Inc. said they filed a lawsuit charging 10 individuals and 11 companies with carrying out a telemarketing scheme designed to saddle consumers with millions of dollars in fraudulent charges. The parties lured credit-card holders to call telemarketers by promising free cars and cash prizes, sometimes passing themselves as sanctioned by MasterCard and Visa, the companies charged. Telemarketers in "boiler-rooms" then used high pressure tactics to sell water purifiers, security systems, travel packages and vitamins to card holders, they said. Visa and MasterCard said customers didn't receive merchandise and were billed as many as three times.

Visa and MasterCard said losses from the alleged scheme total $4 million, and they expect that total to grow. The companies are seeking damages and $10 million in punitive damages.
