Sumitomo Bank to Set Up Holding Company in U.S.

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/26/90

TOKYO -- Sumitomo Bank Ltd. said it plans to set up a bank holding company in the U.S. that will house its American non-commercial banking activities, specifically the bank's trust-banking and capital-markets operations. The Japanese bank also said it planned to invest about $320 million in establishing new units involved in leasing, investment management and securities.

In addition, Sumitomo said that each of the three new units will have a chief operating officer from the U.S. and that "the bank will minimize the number of Japanese personnel who will be engaged in the companies' management."

One of the advantages of a holding company's structure is that it allows foreign financial institutions to create separate legal entities, which can offer Americans such positions as chairman and president.

Sumitomo said it has applied to Japan's Ministry of Finance and to the Federal Reserve for approval of its plans.
