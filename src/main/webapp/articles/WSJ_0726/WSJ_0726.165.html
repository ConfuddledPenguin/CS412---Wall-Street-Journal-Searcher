Money Market Funds Grow by $2.4 Billion Despite Falling Yields

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/26/90

Despite declining yields, investors put another $2.4 billion into money market mutual funds in the latest week. Assets of 432 taxable funds totaled $384.8 billion at the close of business Tuesday, according to IBC/Donoghue's Money Fund Report. The average seven-day compound yield, which assumes reinvestment of dividends, dipped to 7.88% from 7.95% -- the lowest since November 1988.

Martha Wittbrodt, editor of the Holliston, Mass., newsletter, predicted that yields will continue to slip in the next few weeks, reflecting the Federal Reserve Board's recent easing of interest rates. She said the decline in money fund yields could be a 10th of a percentage point or so over the next three to four weeks.

Average yields on taxable funds have been at or just below 8% for most of this year.

Average maturity of the taxable funds remained the same in the week ended Tuesday, at 44 days. That is within the range of the last few months; average maturity has been as short as 29 days and as long as 53 days in recent years. Money funds invest in such things as short-term Treasury securities, commercial paper (short-term corporate IOUs) and bank certificates of deposit.

The average seven-day simple yield of the taxable funds slipped to 7.59% from 7.66%. The average 30-day simple yield also eased, to 7.65% from 7.66%, and the average 30-day compound yield dipped to 7.94% from 7.95%.

Yields on tax-exempt funds also declined. The average seven-day compound yield of 227 tax-free funds eased to 5.21% from 5.23%, Donoghue's reported. The latest yield is equivalent to 7.24% for an investor in the 28% tax bracket and 7.78% for someone paying 33% in taxes.

Assets of the tax-exempt funds decreased to $78.8 billion from $78.9 billion. Average maturity remained at 51 days.
