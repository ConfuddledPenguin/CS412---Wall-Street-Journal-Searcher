Business Brief -- Interco Inc.: Company May Not Make Next Payment Due Banks

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/26/90

Interco Inc., St. Louis, is telling its bankers that it doesn't expect to make an interest payment due next week on its bank debt, according to people familiar with the company's finances. Interco, a footwear and furniture company, said in May that it might file for bankruptcy protection from creditors as part of an effort to restructure its $1.9 billion in debts.

Interco said it is holding constructive negotiations with its banks and bondholders. "We believe it is inappropriate at this time to comment on any aspect of those talks," the company added.

Wilbur Ross, a Rothschild Inc. managing director, who is advising bondholders, said he doesn't expect that an Interco default on its $700 million of bank debt will greatly change the company's position with creditors.

Mr. Ross added that Interco already has defaulted on enough of its obligations that creditors could file an involuntary bankruptcy petition if they wanted to. But "as long as people feel that debt restructuring talks are making progress, there's no need for anything precipitous."
