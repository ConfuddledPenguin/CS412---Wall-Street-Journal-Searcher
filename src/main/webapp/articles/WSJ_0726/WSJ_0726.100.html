Business Brief -- London United Investments PLC: Firm Agrees With Creditors On Debt-Payment Proposal

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/26/90

London United Investments PLC agreed in principle with creditors on a debt-payment plan under which the British insurance holding company probably will pay less than full value on as much as #1 billion ($1.82 billion) in potential insurance liabilities. The company has been managed by two court-appointed administrators since May, when it failed in a seven-week effort to find new funds to meet potential U.S. liability claims. The insurance holding company's collapse ranks among the biggest insurance industry debacles in the London market in recent years.

Yesterday, the company's administrators said they intend to sell several insurance units and broking assets to raise funds to meet some future claims. The company said the most significant unit on the auction block is Hartford, Conn.-based First Reinsurance Co. A company official said First Reinsurance's net asset value is about $25 million. All told, the disposals are likely to raise less than $100 million, he said.
