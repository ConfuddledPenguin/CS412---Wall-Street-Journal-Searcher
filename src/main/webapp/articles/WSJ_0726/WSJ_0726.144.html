Panel Clears Bill to Donate Properties to Aid Homeless

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/26/90

WASHINGTON -- The Senate Governmental Affairs Committee approved legislation allowing the donation of surplus federal property to non-profit groups working with homeless people. Currently, the government grants limited-term leases to such qualified groups, an arrangement that public-interest organizations have argued prohibits them from making long-range plans.

Assistant Housing Secretary Anna Kondratas said the administration welcomed the measure and would use its regulatory authority to start deeding properties even before final passage of the measure, which the committee approved by voice vote.

Deeding the property to groups dealing with the homeless will make "a significant difference" in the program because the lease arrangement currently thwarts those groups from securing the financing needed to improve the facilities, said Wendy Adler, of the National Governors' Association.

The McKinney Homeless Assistance Act of 1987 is the primary federal vehicle for aiding the homeless. But critics have charged that one of its major programs, the surplus-property program, has been less effective than it could be because of government inefficiency and insufficient information on available properties.

"There is still no comprehensive federal guidance on how to obtain federal vacant properties," John Ols, the director of housing and community development issues of the General Accounting Office, said last week in testmony before the committee.
