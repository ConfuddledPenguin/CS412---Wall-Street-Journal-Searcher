Business Brief -- British Petroleum Co.: Canadian Unit to Lay Off 120 Employees in Revamp

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/26/90

BP Canada Inc. said it will lay off about 120 of its 1,760 employees under a reorganization plan that will result in separate units for its mining and oil and gas businesses. BP Canada, based in Calgary, Alberta, wouldn't disclose the costs associated with the layoffs, which will begin with the severance of 80 employees in the current quarter, and the balance later this year or early in 1991. The moves reflect an effort to cut costs, the company said.

"We should see a substantially improved performance in future," said Vince Vianello, vice-president, human resources. He didn't offer a specific forecast. For the quarter ended March 31, BP Canada had net earnings of 6 million Canadian dollars (US$5.2 million) or 12 Canadian cents a share on sales of C$104.7 million, down from a net of C$7.2 million or 14 cents a share on sales of C$94 million the previous year.

BP Canada is 57%-owned by British Petroleum Co.
