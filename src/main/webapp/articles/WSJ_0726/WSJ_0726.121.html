Business Brief -- Fluor Corp.: Subsidiary Wins Contract To Build Plant in Malaysia

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/26/90

Fluor Corp., Irvine, Calif., said its Fluor Daniel subsidiary was awarded a contract by Henkel Rika Sdn Bhd of Malaysia to construct a palm-based fatty alcohol production plant. Terms weren't disclosed.

The 2.6-acre plant, scheduled for startup in the second half of 1991, will be built as an addition to an existing oleochemicals complex in Malaysia. Fatty alcohols are used in the detergent industry, replacing products of petroleum origin. Henkel Rika Sdn Bhd is a joint venture between Henkel Oleochemicals Sdn Bhd of Malaysia, New Japan Chemical Co. of Japan and Lucky Ltd. of Korea.

Separately, Fluor Daniel was awarded a contract, valued at about $12 million, by Richmond Metropolitan Authority of Virginia to build a 345,000-square-foot parking structure. The nine-story structure, scheduled for completion in late 1991, will span a downtown expressway and provide parking for 1,000 cars.

Fluor Daniel is an international engineering, construction, maintenance and technical services company.
