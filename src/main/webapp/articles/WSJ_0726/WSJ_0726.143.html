Who's News: Sotheby's Appoints Ex-Film Executive To American Division

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/26/90

NEW YORK -- Sotheby's Holdings Inc. announced that Roger C. Faxon, formerly of Columbia Pictures, has been appointed chief operating officer and executive vice president of its Sotheby's North America division. The move to a management position at the New York-based auction house was an unusual one for Mr. Faxon, who has spent most of his career in the entertainment industry. He previously was the No. 2 executive to former Columbia Pictures president Dawn Steel, serving as her chief administrative and operating officer. Ms. Steel left the studio last year when it was purchased by Sony Corp. Sony has since installed new management, and a number of executives who were there during Ms. Steel's tenure have left or been replaced.

In his new position, Mr. Faxon will report to Diana D. Brooks, president of Sotheby's North America, and will oversee "management issues," among other responsibilities, according to the company. Earlier in his career, Mr. Faxon also served as an executive at Tri-Star Pictures and Lucasfilm Ltd., where he oversaw the production of "Indiana Jones and the Temple of Doom," among other films.
