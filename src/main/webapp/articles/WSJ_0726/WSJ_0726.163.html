Anheuser-Busch Net Rose 10% in 2nd Period As Sales Increased 12%

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/26/90

ST. LOUIS -- Anheuser-Busch Cos., with a 12% sales growth reflecting a further increase in the company's dominant market position, said second-quarter net income grew 10%. The brewer had earnings of $257.5 million, or 90 cents a share, up from $233.7 million, or 82 cents a share. Net sales, that is, sales after excise taxes, climbed to $2.78 billion from $2.48 billion.

In composite New York Stock Exchange trading yesterday, Anheuser-Busch rose $1.125 to $44.125.

The company's flagship Anheuser-Busch Inc. beer operation shipped 22.3 million barrels to wholesalers in the latest period, up 4.3% from the year-ago period and a 2.9% increase from the first quarter.

August A. Busch III, chairman and president, said the volume growth is "primarily attributable" to the company's introduction of its Bud Dry and Busch Light products. Both brands were introduced nationally in the first quarter.

The growth in shipments is a "very strong performance," said Kidder, Peabody & Co. analyst Roy D. Burry, "particularly in light of the strong performances" by competitors Adolph Coors Co. and the Miller Brewing unit of Philip Morris Cos.

He said Anheuser's earnings increase was despite a three to four cents a share dilution related to the $1.1 billion acquisition last year of the Sea World theme park group.

Anheuser said trade-industry figures show its U.S. beer market share in the year's first six months increased to 42.9% from 41.9%.

The company said its can-making unit had "greatly improved earnings," rebounding from "depressed 1989 levels." In addition, profit at the Campbell Taggart food operation gained, reflecting higher volume and improved profit margins in some lines, the company said.
