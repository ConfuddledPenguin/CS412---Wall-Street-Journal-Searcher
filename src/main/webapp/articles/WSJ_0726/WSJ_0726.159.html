Business Brief -- Norfolk Southern Corp.: Net Falls 10% in Quarter At Transportation Company

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/26/90

Norfolk Southern Corp. reported second-quarter net income declined 10%, as year-earlier results were helped by a gain on the sale of trucking assets. The rail and road transportation company reported net for the quarter of $155.9 million, or 95 cents a share, compared with $173.7 million, or 99 cents a share, in the 1989 quarter. The Norfolk, Va., company said the year-earlier net was a record for a second quarter. Revenue edged up to $1.18 billion, a second-quarter record, from $1.17 billion. Most of the revenue is railway-derived; the company noted a 7.2% increase in coal-shipment revenue, but a 3.2% drop in merchandise shipping revenue.

For the six months, net was down 3.7% to $296.1 million, or $1.79 a share, from $307.5 million, or $1.74 a share. Revenue rose 1.4% to $2.32 billion from $2.29 billion.

The quarter and half results included a $13.6 million gain from the sale of Norfolk's holding in Trailer Train Co. Per-share results were better than the dollar amounts indicated. The company said it is buying back about a million shares a month under a 20 million-share program authorized last fall. Shares outstanding total about 162.8 million, an official said.
