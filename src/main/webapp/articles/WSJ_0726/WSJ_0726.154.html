Suchard Holders Advised To Reject Philip Morris Bid

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 07/26/90

ZURICH, Switzerland -- Bank Vontobel & Co. has strongly advised Jacobs Suchard AG shareholders not to accept the terms of Philip Morris Cos.' $3.8 billion takeover bid for the Swiss chocolate and coffee concern. Vontobel claims that U.S. tax law provides a weapon to push the offer price higher. In a study of the proposed acquisition released yesterday, Vontobel wrote that unless New York-based Philip Morris acquires 80% of Suchard's capital it will lose tax benefits in the U.S. "We estimate that this could mean hundreds of millions of Swiss francs for Philip Morris," said one of the report's authors, Rene Weber.

This advice from Vontobel could introduce a new wrinkle in the takeover. Many other analysts and Swiss banks had reluctantly recommended that shareholders accept the offer on the assumption they had no other choice.

A Philip Morris spokesman in Zurich quoted Luc E. Vandevelde, vice president of the company's Kraft subsidiary, as saying "there will be absolutely no changes" in the prices offered to Suchard shareholders. Mr. Vandevelde has represented Philip Morris throughout the transaction.

A Suchard spokesman said it was not his company's job to comment.
