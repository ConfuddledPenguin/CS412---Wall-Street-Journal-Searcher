Textile Quotas Bill Is Sent To House Floor for a Vote

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/26/90

WASHINGTON -- The House Ways and Means Committee sent to the House floor, without recommendation, legislation requiring import quotas on textiles, apparel and footwear. The action, by voice vote, signals both reservations by key members of the panel and the futility of opposition at this stage. The House is expected to pass the measure easily, and the Senate passed a similar bill last week, 68-32. The real test will come after an expected veto by President Bush.

The bill would set up a new system of quotas to restrict imports of textiles, apparel and footwear from all foreign suppliers except Canada and Israel.
