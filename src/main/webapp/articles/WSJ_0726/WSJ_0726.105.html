RJR Refinancing Would Have Cut 10% From Interest Costs

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/26/90

NEW YORK -- RJR Nabisco Inc. said its pending $7 billion refinancing package would have trimmed its interest costs about 10% for this year's first six months to $1.46 billion. The tobacco and food company is retiring substantial amounts of its high-cost debt, including more than $4.4 billion of high-yield, below-investment-grade "junk" bonds. RJR is financing the transaction with bank loans, a new issue of convertible preferred stock, and a $1.7 billion equity infusion from its principal shareholder, Kohlberg Kravis Roberts & Co.

In formal exchange-offer documents filed yesterday, RJR said its refinancing plan is intended to "improve the creditworthinesss" of the highly leveraged company. Had the refinancing plan been in effect, RJR said that because of lower interest costs, its $330 million loss for the first half would have shrunk to $216 million before preferred dividends.

RJR also said that its debt-to-equity ratio, which stood at 24 to 1 June 30, would have dropped to 4 to 1 under the recapitalization, if preferred stock were counted as equity, or 8 to 1 if preferred were counted as debt.

RJR also disclosed more details about negotiations earlier this month to reset the interest rates on its bonds maturing in 2007 and 2009: RJR said its investment banker, Merrill Lynch & Co., had recommended new rates of 16 1/2% and 17%, respectively. But two other investment banks acting on behalf of bondholders, Dillon, Read & Co. and Lazard Freres & Co., had called for rates of 17 1/2% and 18%, respectively.

When the three investment banks couldn't reach agreement, the issue was settled July 15 by the investment bank Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette Securities Corp. It picked rates of 17% for the bonds of 2007 and 17 3/8% for the bonds of 2009. The rates are intended to make sure that the bonds trade at their full face value.
