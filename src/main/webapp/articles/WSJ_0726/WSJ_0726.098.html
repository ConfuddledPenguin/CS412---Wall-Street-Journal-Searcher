Business Brief -- James River Corp.: European Affiliate Purchases Two Greek Tissue Concerns

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/26/90

James River Corp., Richmond, Va., said its European affiliate JA/MONT N.V. bought Cartellas S.A. and Achaia S.A., two tissue companies in Athens, Greece, formerly owned by the Panopoulos family. Terms were not disclosed. The two companies, which market under the Delica brand name in Greece, posted combined 1989 sales of $40.7 million. Sales for Cartellas were $33.5 million; sales for Achaia were $7.2 million.

The two companies combined represent the second largest tissue producer in the Greek market, with a 15 to 20% share, the company said.

The acquisition is the first by JA/MONT V.A., a joint venture formed this year that combined James River's European tissue interests with those of Gruppo Ferruzzi of Italy and Nokia Corp. of Finland. The joint venture expects sales of $1.6 billion in 1990.
