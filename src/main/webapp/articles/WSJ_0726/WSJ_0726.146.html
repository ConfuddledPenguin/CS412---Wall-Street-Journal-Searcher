International Brief -- British Airways: Pact With Unit of Sabena Is Cleared by U.K. Agency

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/26/90

The U.K. Department of Trade and Industry gave British Airways the green light to participate in a planned linkup with Sabena World Airlines. The decision followed a U.K. Monopolies and Mergers Commission investigation that found that a British Airways 20% stake in the new unit of Sabena S.A., which is 53%-owned by the Belgian government, wouldn't be against the public interest.

But the Sabena World venture, which also involves KLM Royal Dutch Airlines taking a 20% stake, continues to face objections from the European Community. Last month, the EC Commission said that some aspects of the three-airline accord could violate EC competition rules and potentially could harm consumers.

Sabena World has yet to respond to the commission's objections, according to Johan Dekker, the unit's chief executive officer. The company probably will do that next month, he said, but it doesn't expect to offer to amend the agreement with the other two carriers.
