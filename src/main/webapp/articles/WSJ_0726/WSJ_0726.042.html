Otis Pike, a former congressman (D., N.Y.), writing in a July 25 syndicated column on how Supreme Court nominee David Souter should answer questions on his views:

There are issues in every land that simply must be addressed. Abortion was one of them, civil rights was another, capital punishment a third, the right to die a fourth. And when Congress ran away from those issues or wrote laws so full of passion and devoid of reason or detail as to be incomprehensible, the Supreme Court moved in. . . . A proper response by Souter to the most zealous questioners might be: "You write clear and concise laws for the nation establishing a right of privacy, defining the times and terms on which people may create a life or end one, declaring what are and are not permissible hiring practices, stating what is and what is not to be admissible evidence, and I will have no trouble limiting myself to the traditional role of determining the constitutionality of your actions. Until I see what you have produced, however, I cannot tell you whether I would find it constitutional or not."
