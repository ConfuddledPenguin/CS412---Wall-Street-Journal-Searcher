Newsprint Use in U.S. Increased 3.1% in June

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/26/90

WASHINGTON -- The American Newspaper Publishers Association estimated newsprint consumption by all U.S. users at 1,013,331 metric tons in June, up 3.1% from a year earlier. For the first half, the industry group estimated that consumption rose 1.3% to 6,078,222 metric tons. A metric ton is equal to about 2,204.62 pounds.

The association said U.S. daily newspapers used 741,120 metric tons of newsprint in June, little changed from 740,484 tons a year earlier. Daily newspapers' consumption in the first half rose less than 1% to 4,531,039 metric tons from 4,504,677 tons.

Newsprint inventories of newspapers in the association's statistical sample averaged a 43-day supply in June, unchanged from a year earlier. The association said stocks of North American mills were down 24%.

The association said Canadian newsprint mills operated at 95% of rated capacity in June and in the first half, and U.S. mills operated at 96.2% in June and 96.6% in the half.
