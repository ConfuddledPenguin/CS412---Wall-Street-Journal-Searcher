Sales at Sotheby's, Christie's Climb 40% Over Year Ago

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/26/90

NEW YORK -- Sales at the two major auction houses exceeded $5.5 billion in the 1989-1990 art-auction season, a gain of about 40% for both houses over the year-earlier period. The reporting period covers the traditional art auction season from September 1989 to August 1990. Because historically no major auctions are held in August, results include estimates for minor auctions for that month.

Despite the robust figures, there was some sales weakness in a few major auctions held in April, May and June. Michael A. Ainslie, president of Sotheby's Holdings, said there has been some "stabilization in prices." Art works "fresh to the market and properly estimated sold well, while those that did not meet these standards faced greater resistance than in recent seasons."

Sotheby's Holdings Inc. of New York said that art auction sales for the 1989-1990 season totaled $3.2 billion, up from $2.3 billion in the 1988-1989 season.

Sales at London-based Christie's International PLC were $2.4 billion, up 40% from the year-earlier sales season.

Separately, Sotheby's announced plans to open an office in Berlin in October.
