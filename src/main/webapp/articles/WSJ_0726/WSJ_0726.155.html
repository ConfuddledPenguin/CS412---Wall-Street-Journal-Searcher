International Brief -- Commerzbank AG: Bank Reports 18% Increase In First Six Months' Profit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/26/90

Commerzbank AG said partial group operating profit jumped 18% in the first half of 1990 to 611 million marks ($377 million) from 520 million marks a year earlier, reflecting wider interest margins and a comparatively weak first half last year. Full operating profit advanced at only a slighter lower rate. West Germany's third largest bank didn't provide any specific data on full operating profit, which includes income generated from trading for its own account. But Walter Seipp, Commerzbank's chairman, indicated in response to a question at a news conference in Frankfurt that full operating profit grew about 15%.

Mr. Seipp said he is "confident that the heretofore delightful business developments will continue" in the second half of the year. "In our projections for the current year," he said, "we are assuming a clearly double-digit rate of increase in our partial operating profit."
