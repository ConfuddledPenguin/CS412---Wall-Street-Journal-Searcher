Wednesday's Markets: Investors Nudge Up Dow by 8.42 --- But Trading Suggests Appetite for Growth Stocks Is Weakening

  • Author: Christopher Winans
  • Date: 07/26/90

NEW YORK -- Investors nudged stock prices higher, and did so in a way that suggests their appetite may be waning for some of the consumer-products growth stocks that led the market's recent rally. The Dow Jones Industrial Average rose 8.42 points in active trading to 2930.94. Bond prices gained and the dollar strengthened in response to data indicating the U.S. economy isn't picking up steam.

The stock market derived much of its support from a rebound in technology stocks and strength in industrial materials and precious metals issues. For example, Alcoa, Phelps Dodge and Union Carbide all posted gains, while consumer stocks such as Coca-cola, Pepsi-Co and Philip Morris fell.

Joseph McAlinden, director of equity research at Dillon Read, predicted that for the rest of the summer technology and natural-resources stocks -- along with energy stocks, which fell yesterday on profit-taking -- will push the market higher. He said he expects these groups to take turns bolstering broad market measures.

The sharp drop in the stock market Monday "was a decisive crack in the one-decision stocks," Mr. McAlinden said, referring to the relatively few reliable growth stocks about which the only decision is to buy. The turning point was McDonald's earnings report Friday; profits were one cent a share shy of some analysts' estimates. "So now we're getting some pickup in stocks that do well in an inflationary environment," he said, which is less favorable for growth companies.

The rise in bond prices -- up three-eighths of a point, or $3.75 for each $1,000 face amount -- was sparked by a weaker-than-expected durable-goods orders in June. Helping sustain the market's advance was a comment later in the day from one Federal Reserve governor, which traders interpreted to mean the Fed will continue to favor fighting inflation over preventing recession.

In major market action:

Stock prices rose on volume of 163.5 million shares in New York Stock Exchange trading. Advancing issues outnumbered decliners, 895 to 583.

Bond prices rose. The yield on the Treasury's benchmark 30-year bond eased to 8.54%.

The dollar gained. In late New York trading, the currency was quoted at 1.6250 marks and 149.89 yen, compared with 1.6215 marks and 148.78 yen Tuesday.
