Letters to the Editor: Warring Hospitals Forget Their Mission

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/26/90

Your June 6 page-one article on the costly rivalry between Borgess Medical Center and Bronson Methodist Hospital in Kalamazoo, Mich., demonstrates a sad decline in the responsiveness of some of our most cherished institutions to the communities they purport to serve. Kalamazoo's situation is not unique. But let's not forget the role we all have in subsidizing the internecine behavior. If the Kalamazoo businesses truly are fed up with their hospitals' costly habits, they can encourage their employees to drive 45 miles north to Grand Rapids in order to enjoy tremendous savings and top-quality care. This would reward more-efficient providers with the best prize of all-patient volume. If the Kalamazoo businesses encouraged effective health-care shopping, the hospitals quickly would get the message. Grand Rapids has an expectation that community interests should prevail over institutional interests. Shouldn't other communities expect the same? The health-care system exists to serve the public, not the other way around.

Philip E. Van Heest

President

Alliance for Health

Grand Rapids, Mich.
