TransAlta Utilities To Cease Operations At Its Energy Unit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/26/90

CALGARY, Alberta -- TransAlta Utilities Corp. said it will discontinue most operations of its TransAlta Energy Systems Corp. unit, resulting in a write-off of about 17 million Canadian dollars (US$14.7 million), or 12.5 Canadian cents a share. TransAlta said it decided that building automation isn't strategic to the future of its TransAlta Resources Corp. unit, a holding company through which TransAlta owns TransAlta Energy.

TransAlta, an electric utility with oil and gas and other interests, said the writeoff includes TransAlta Energy's losses so far this year, which total about C$4 million. The unit, which installs building automation equipment, such as heating control systems, lost C$6 million last year.

For the first quarter ended March 31, TransAlta had net earnings of C$54.1 million, or 30 Canadian cents a share, on revenue of C$273.3 million.

TransAlta said Andover, Mass.-based Andover Controls, a unit of London-based BICC PLC, an engineering, construction, and power company, agreed to complete all of TransAlta Energy's current projects, and to honor its other obligations, including warranties. Andover Controls makes most of the building automation equipment that TransAlta Energy has installed.
