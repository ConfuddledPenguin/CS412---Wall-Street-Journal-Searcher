Letters to the Editor: Hungry, Sick Children: A Daily Holocaust

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/26/90

As I write this (June 24), the death toll from the earthquake in Iran has exceeded 40,000. And while we all must give our full support to the relief efforts, we should note that 40,000 is also the number of children in the world who die every day from chronic malnutrition and preventable disease. The earthquake in Iran is apparently the first since 1976 (the eighth in this century) to equal or exceed the 24-hour death toll of children in our world from malnutrition and disease. The generous outpouring of media coverage and monetary gifts directed to the victims in Iran is testimony to a heartening new level of public compassion and concern. Yet our concern is awakened only for sudden emergencies in limited geographic areas. We have not yet fully awakened to the unparalleled tragedy of malnutrition and disease, nor have we recognized the low-cost opportunities to cut in half the resulting death toll of 40,000 a day.

On Sept. 29 and 30, the World Summit for Children will allow our leaders to address these opportunities fully. The potential of the summit is to save the lives of 50 million children during this decade. On Sept. 23, thousands of citizens will voice their support for the summit in 180 candlelight vigils in 16 countries. All who wish to participate should call 1-800-WE AGREE.

Jody Gatwood

Silver Spring, Md.
