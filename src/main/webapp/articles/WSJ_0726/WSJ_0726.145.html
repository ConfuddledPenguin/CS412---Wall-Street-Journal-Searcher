Business Brief -- Ducommun Inc.: Firm Reports Sharp Increase In 2nd-Quarter Net Income

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/26/90

Ducommun Inc., Los Angeles, posted a sharp increase in second-quarter net income to $620,000, or 19 cents a share, from $77,000, or two cents a share, a year ago. The company said it expects full-year 1990 results to exceed those of last year. Latest second-quarter net income included a tax credit of $258,000, or 8 cents a share. Sales of the maker of aerospace components and assemblies climbed 11%, to $19.7 million, from $17.8 million.

"We continue to expect sales and net income for the full year to exceed 1989 results," said Norman A. Barkeley, chairman, president and chief executive officer. "Our commercial aerospace business is strong, and additional reductions in overhead are being put into effect." In 1989, Ducommun had net income of $602,000, or 18 cents a share, on sales of $68.6 million.

For the first six months of 1990, the company's net income more than doubled to $896,000, or 27 cents a share, from $437,000, or 13 cents a share, a year earlier. Net income for the year-earlier period included a $456,000 loss from discontinued operations. Revenue rose 10%, to $37.9 million, from $34.3 million.
