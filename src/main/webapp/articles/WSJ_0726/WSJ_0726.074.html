Du Pont Net Slides 2.8%

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/26/90

WILMINGTON, Del. -- Du Pont Co. said second-quarter profit slipped 2.8% from a year earlier, with all the company's business segments showing declines except its energy operations. The chemical and energy concern posted profit of $694 million, or $1.02 a share, which was in line with analysts' expectations. In the year-earlier quarter, the company earned $714 million, or $1 a share. The higher per-share profit in the latest quarter is due to fewer shares outstanding.

Sales in the latest quarter rose 4% to $9.65 billion from $9.28 billion.

Du Pont attributed the drop in profit to weaknesses in the U.S. economy, where unit volume sales decreased 5%. Outside the U.S., unit sales rose 6%, almost entirely from gains in Europe.

"This lends support to a key goal of expansion in Europe to double business by the mid-1990s," Du Pont Chairman Edgar S. Woolard Jr. said in a statement.

In the latest quarter, the sharpest drop came in industrial products, where after-tax operating profit fell 15% to $135 million.
