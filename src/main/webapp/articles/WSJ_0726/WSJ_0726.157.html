Business Brief -- Farley Inc.: Assets of a Unit Are Sold To Group for $135.5 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/26/90

Farley Inc., the cash-strapped holding company controlled by investor William Farley, said it sold the assets of its aluminum die-casting operation to an investor group for $135.5 million in cash, notes and junk securities. Farley Inc. said in Chicago that the investor group, led by closely held ICM Industries Inc., paid $88 million in cash for the Doehler-Jarvis unit. It also issued Farley a $25 million subordinated note, which pays interest in like securities for the first two years, and $15 million of other so-called payment-in-kind debt.

An additional $7.5 million cash payment "was deferred," Farley said in a news release. Officials weren't immediately available for elaboration.

Under the sale, Farley Inc. received an equity interest -- whose size wasn't disclosed -- in the partnership that bought the unit. Farley retains on its books "certain liabilities" of Doehler-Jarvis totaling $100 million.
