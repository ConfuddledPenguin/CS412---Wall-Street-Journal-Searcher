Business Brief -- Engelhard Corp.: Company Reports 20% Rise In Second-Quarter Earnings

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/26/90

Engelhard Corp. said second-quarter net income rose 20% largely because of the success of its precious-metals management business. Earnings for the Edison, N.J.-based maker of specialty-chemical products and engineered materials rose to $18.2 million, or 41 cents per share, from $15.1 million, or 34 cents per share, in the year-earlier period. Sales rose 25% to $751.3 million from $603.1 million.

Orin R. Smith, president and chief executive officer, attributed the increase primarily to the company's success in building its industrial business base for precious-metal materials management services and to favorable conditions in those metals markets. That business trades in the precious metals markets for its own account and for customers'accounts. Together with the engineered-materials segment, which includes the fabrication of industrial products using precious metals as raw materials, this accounts for about 30% of total operating earnings, the company said.

Earnings for the first half of 1990 rose 12% to $32.9 million, or 74 cents per share, from $29.5 million, or 66 cents per share. Sales rose 18% to $1.41 billion from $1.19 billion.
