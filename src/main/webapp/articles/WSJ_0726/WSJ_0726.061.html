GM's Technology, Finance Units' Net Is Basically Flat

  • Author: Joseph B. White
  • Date: 07/26/90

DETROIT -- Combined profits at General Motors Corp.'s finance and technology subsidiaries were virtually flat in the second quarter, as a 25% decline in earnings at the GM Hughes Electronics unit offset gains in data processing and finance operations. GM's Electronic Data Systems Corp., General Motors Acceptance Corp. and GM Hughes units had combined earnings of $579.6 million in the quarter, up 0.5% from $577 million a year before. GM is scheduled to release earnings for the remainder of its operations today.

GM Hughes, which includes the defense technology operations of Hughes Aircraft Co. and the automotive electronics businesses of Delco Electronics Corp., saw net income in the quarter fall to $152.3 million from $203.1 million a year earlier. The decline came despite record second quarter revenue of $2.99 billion, up from $2.88 billion a year ago. GM Hughes blamed the decline on increased spending for new products at Hughes, the impact of GM vehicle production cuts on Delco, and higher taxes.

GM's Class H stock, which represents a dividend interest in Hughes earnings, closed yesterday at $18.875, down 12.5 cents in New York Stock Exchange composite trading.

At EDS, second quarter net income rose 19% to $125.3 million from $104.9 million. Revenue rose 5.7% to $1.46 billion. GM business accounted for 55% of EDS's total revenue in the latest quarter. "We anticipate having a very good year," EDS chairman Les Alberthal said in a statement. GM Class E stock, which represents a dividend interest in EDS earnings, closed yesterday at $33.75, down 50 cents in New York Stock Exchange composite trading.

GMAC, the auto maker's finance arm, said lower borrowing costs helped boost second quarter net income by 12% to $302 million from $269 million a year ago. However, GMAC said the number of new vehicles it financed in the U.S. during the latest quarter fell 24% to 541,000 cars and trucks. Outside the U.S., GMAC financed 9.9% fewer new vehicles than a year ago.

For the first half of the year, net income at GM Hughes fell 21% to $297.9 million from $376 million a year ago. Revenue eased to $5.85 billion from $5.89 billion.

EDS's first-half profit rose 14.7% to $235.1 million from $204.9 million. Revenue was up 7.8% to $2.87 billion from $2.66 billion.

GMAC's first-half earnings rose 2% to $638 million from $625 million.
