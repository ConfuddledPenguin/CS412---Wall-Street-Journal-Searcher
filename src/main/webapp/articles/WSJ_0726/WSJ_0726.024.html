Armco Steel to Spend At Least $1.5 Billion To Improve Facilities

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/26/90

MIDDLETOWN, Ohio -- Armco Steel Co. is likely to spend at least $1.5 billion to improve its steelmaking facilities over the next five years, according to its owners. Armco Steel is a joint venture between Armco Inc., Parsippany, N.J., and Kawasaki Steel Corp., Tokyo. It operates steel mills here and in Ashland, Ky.

When the joint venture was established in 1989, the companies said they planned to invest $1.1 billion over the 1989-94 period. About $200 million was invested in 1989.

A new capital spending program will be considered by Armco Steel directors next month, which will add some improvement projects to the original plan, probably extending it into 1995. Armco Inc. and Kawasaki declined to provide specifics on the additional projects.

Kawasaki purchased a 40% interest in Armco's Eastern Steel division last year to establish the joint venture. During the 1990 second quarter, Kawasaki contributed $70 million to Armco Steel, raising its ownership to 45%. Kawasaki will contribute another $70 million in 1991 and $35 million in 1992, at which time Armco Steel will be a 50-50 joint venture between the parents.

Kawasaki's payments will provide $175 million toward the capital program. The remainder will come from cash flow and borrowings, Armco said.

The spending will include an overhaul of the rolling mills to improve steel quality. A Kawasaki spokesman said the mills currently can't meet the exacting specifications of Japanese auto makers. One goal of the investment is to increase sales to the U.S. auto plants owned by Japanese auto makers, he indicated.
