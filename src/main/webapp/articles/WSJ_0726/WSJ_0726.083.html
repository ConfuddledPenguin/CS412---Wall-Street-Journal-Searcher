Credit Ratings

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/26/90

DIME SAVINGS BANK OF NEW YORK (Garden City, N.Y.) -- Standard & Poor's Corp. lowered ratings on various securities of Dime, noting "continued declining asset quality indicators" and expected increases in nonperforming assets. S&P lowered medium-term notes to triple B-minus from triple B, and commercial paper to single A-3 from A-2, and lowered ratings on certificates of deposit. The ratings concern said the detrimental effect of carrying approximately $750 million in nonperforming assets on overall profitability will be significant over the next two years.
