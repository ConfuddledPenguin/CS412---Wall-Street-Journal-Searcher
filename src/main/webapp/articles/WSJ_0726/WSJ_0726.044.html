New Securities Issues

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/26/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: Report: CORPORATE

Southern New England Telephone -- $100 million of 9.60% notes, due Feb. 15, 2030, was priced at 98.50 to yield 9.751%. The notes were priced at a spread of 1.19 percentage points above the Treasury's 30-year bond. Rated double-A-1 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and double-A-plus by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Salomon Brothers Inc.

MUNICIPALS

Washington Suburban Sanitary District, Md. -- $90 million of various purpose revenue bonds, due June 1, 1992-2014, apparently were won by a Clayton Brown & Associates Inc. group with a bid setting the true interest cost at 6.90%. The issue, commonly called "Wash Tubs," contains four types of bonds: $39 million of general construction bonds, $31 million of water supply bonds, $10 million of sewage disposal bonds and $10 million of administration building construction bonds. The bonds, rated double-A-1 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and double-A by Standard & Poor's Corp., were priced for reoffering to yield from 6.00% in 1992 to 7.00% in 2009-2012. Bonds maturing in 2013 and 2014 weren't formally reoffered. There are no term bonds.

Essex County, N.J. -- $65 million of improvement authority county general obligation bonds and lease revenue bonds, Series 1990, due Dec. 1, 1993-2004, 2010 and 2020, were tentatively priced by an A.G. Edwards & Sons Inc. group to yield from 6.00% in 1993 to 7.09% in 2020. Current interest serial bonds are priced at par to yield from 6.00% in 1993 to 6.95% in 2004. There are $12.9 million of term bonds priced at par to yield 7% in 2010. There are $37.5 million of 7.05% term bonds priced at 99 1/2 to yield 7.09% in 2020. The bonds are insured and rated triple-A by Moody's and S&P.

MORTGAGE

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $500 million of real estate mortgage investment conduit securities is being offered in 21 classes by Salomon Brothers Inc. The offering, Series 1990-99, is backed by Fannie Mae 9 1/2% securities. Details on prices and yields weren't immediately available.

EUROBONDS

Furukawa Co. (Japan) -- 150 million Swiss francs ($109 million) of privately placed notes due Aug. 15, 1995, with equity-purchase warrants. Fixed 4% coupon at par via Credit Suisse. Guarantee by Dai-Ichi Kangyo Bank. Exercise period from Aug. 27, 1990, to Aug. 4, 1995. Terms fixed Aug. 27. Exercise price of the warrants will be refixed at a not-yet-specified date if the share price at the time is below the exercise price. Call at issuer's option from Aug. 15, 1993, at 101, declining by 1/2 point annually.

Ford Credit Australia Ltd. (U.S. parent) -- 75 million Australia dollars (US$58.9 million) of 14 1/2% bonds due Aug. 29, 1995, priced at 101.75 to yield 14.57% less fees, via Hambros Bank Ltd. Guaranteed by Ford Motor Credit. Fees 2.

Postipankki (Finland) -- 75 million Swiss francs of 7 1/8% public bonds due Sept. 7, 2005, priced at 101 3/4 via Bank J. Henry Schroder AG. Call from Sept. 7, 2000, at par.
