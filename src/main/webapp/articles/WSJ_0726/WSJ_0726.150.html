Business Brief -- Fieldcrest Cannon Inc.: Textile Concern Posts Loss As Bed and Bath Sales Fall

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/26/90

Fieldcrest Cannon Inc., citing a 12% decline in sales in its bed and bath division, posted a second-quarter loss of $6.1 million, or 58 cents a share, compared with earnings of $7.7 million, or 74 cents a share, a year earlier. The Eden, N.C.-based textile concern said sales in the most recent quarter fell 8.7%, to $316.3 million from $346.4 million. The company said its results had been further undermined by the need to curtail mill schedules to reduce inventory. Additionally, Fieldcrest took a pretax charge of $1.1 million, or 10 cents a share, following the closing of its Laurel Hill, N.C., mill.

Analysts said the results were significantly worse than expected.

"It's confirmation of the business losing market share," said Deborah Bronston, an analyst with Prudential Bache Research. Fieldcrest, saddled with high operating costs, faces stiff competition from Springs Industries Inc. and West Point-Pepperell Inc., she said.

For the first half, the company had a net loss of $5.7 million, or 55 cents a share, compared with net income of $10.7 million, or $1.04 a share, a year earlier. Sales dropped 8.1% to $590.7 million from $642.7 million.
