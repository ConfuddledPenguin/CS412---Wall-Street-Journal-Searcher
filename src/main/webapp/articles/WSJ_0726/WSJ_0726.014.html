Marketing & Media: Dow Jones's New Service Delivers Reports Via Fax

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/26/90

PRINCETON, N.J. -- Dow Jones Information Services, an operating group of Dow Jones & Co., introduced "Facts Delivered," an information service that quickly delivers detailed reports on thousands of public companies via facsimile machine or electronic mail. The report will include the latest news about a company, its current stock price, a detailed profile, financial data from the company's income statement and balance sheet, earnings estimates, its performance as compared with its industry group, a summary of its recent insider transactions and a list of research reports from investment analysts.

"`Facts Delivered' is designed to provide business people a comprehensive overview of a company with a single phone call," said Richard J. Levine, vice president and editorial director of the Information Services Group.

Through an agreement with MCI Mail, customers receive the report within hours of their request. The reports may be used for competitive analyses as well as marketing and sales and investment purposes.

The reports are available for $70. The service hours are Monday through Friday 9 a.m. to 6 p.m. Eastern time.

Dow Jones publishes The Wall Street Journal, Barron's, Dow Jones News/Retrieval, five domestic and international newswires -- including Dow Jones News Service -- and the Ottaway group of community newspapers. The company also owns Telerate Inc., a supplier of real-time financial information.
