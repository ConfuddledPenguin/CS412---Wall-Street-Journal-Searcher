Business Brief -- General Instrument Corp.: Company Says Two Parties Don't Plan Buy-Out Bids

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/26/90

General Instrument Corp. of New York said two potential bidders have indicated they don't plan to try to top a $1.47 billion, or $44.50-a-share, buy-out offer by Forstmann, Little & Co. The two parties, who weren't ever publicly identified, were said by Wall Street professionals to be Kelso & Co., another leveraged buy-out firm, and Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette Securities Corp., a unit of Equitable Life Assurance Society of the U.S. Neither company would comment.

The announcement by General Instrument apparently leaves the way clear for Forstmann, Little to acquire the cable TV equipment company when its offer expires Aug. 13. Mr. Forstmann had earlier estimated the chances he would be outbid at only "one in 50."

General Instrument stock declined 87.5 cents to $44.75 in composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange after the company said one of the two parties didn't plan to bid. Trading was halted before a later announcement that the second party didn't plan to bid either, and trading didn't resume before the market closed.
