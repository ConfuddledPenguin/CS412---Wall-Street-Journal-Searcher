Business Brief -- Phillips Petroleum Co.: Energy Concern to Make New, Natural-Gas Vessels

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/26/90

Phillips Petroleum Co. said it signed a contract with Ishikawajima-Harima Heavy Industries Co. Ltd., Tokyo, for the construction of two liquefied natural-gas vessels. Terms weren't disclosed. The Bartlesville, Okla., energy concern said the 87,500-cubic-meter capacity vessels will replace the older, smaller-capacity, Arctic-Tokyo and Polar-Alaska vessels that currently transport liquefied natural gas from Kenai, Alaska, to Japan.

Phillips holds a 70% stake in the Kenai operations, with Marathon Oil Co. holding the remaining 30% interest. Marathon is a unit of USX Corp., Pittsburgh. The vessels are scheduled to be delivered in mid and late 1993.
