Who's News: PHM's Sperlich Quits As Chairman, Chief; Pulte Succeeds Him

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/26/90

BLOOMFIELD HILLS, Mich. -- Harold K. Sperlich resigned as chairman and chief executive officer of PHM Corp., and William J. Pulte, founder of the home building company, succeeded him at the company's helm. Mr. Sperlich, 61 years old, had been in command of PHM only since April. Before that he was a private consultant. Mr. Sperlich is better known as the intense auto executive who was the driving force behind the development of Chrysler Corp's. "K Cars" and its popular minivans. He resigned from Chrysler in 1988.

Mr. Pulte, 58, will resume control of the company. In 1984, he was part of a three-person office of the chief executive. Mr. Pulte also had previously been chairman of the company's executive committee. At the beginning of the year, he owned 19% of PHM's common stock.

Neither Mr. Sperlich nor representatives of PHM were available for comment on Mr. Sperlich's resignation, so the circumstances surrounding his departure aren't clear.

Nearly three years ago Mr. Sperlich resigned as president of Chrysler Motors, after recurring clashes with Chairman Lee A. Iacocca and with Gerald Greenwald, then Chrysler's second-ranking executive.

A statement released with the resignation announcement said Mr. Sperlich will continue as a consultant to PHM. The statement quoted Mr. Sperlich as saying, "I am delighted to see Bill Pulte back in the saddle again, where he belongs."

For the first nine months of this year, PHM had earnings of $5.2 million, or 22 cents a share, compared with earnings of $9.5 million, or 36 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue declined to $822.3 million from $841.7 million.

PHM shares closed at $9.875, down 37.5 cents in composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange.
