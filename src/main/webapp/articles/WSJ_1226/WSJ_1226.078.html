International Brief -- Isuzu Motors Inc.: Auto Maker's Fiscal Year Earnings Declined by 38%

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/26/90

Isuzu Motors Ltd. said pretax earnings in the fiscal year ended Oct. 31 tumbled 38% to 15.370 billion yen ($113.5 million) from 24.805 billion yen despite a 9.5% gain in sales. Net income fell 49% to 7.640 billion yen, or 7.41 yen a share, from 14.977 billion yen, or 16.30 yen a share.

Sales amounted to 1.196 trillion yen, in contrast with 1.092 trillion yen in the previous fiscal year.

Isuzu, Japan's medium-sized maker of cars and trucks, plans to keep its dividends for the year unchanged at five yen.

Company officials said sales were strong during the year owing to Japan's sustained economic growth as well as robust demand overseas.

However, they added, growing material, personnel and research and development costs cut deeply into earnings. Those costs came to some 26.3 billion yen, officials said, more than the gains amounting to about 22 billion yen resulting from cost cutbacks and better production efficiency.

In addition, they noted, extraordinary gains, which included those from the sales of fixed assets and securities, plunged to 3.536 billion yen from 12.481 billion yen in the prior fiscal year, though extraordinary losses also dwindled sharply, to 6.046 billion yen from 12.329 billion yen. Among those losses were depreciation on company investments and retirement pension payments.
