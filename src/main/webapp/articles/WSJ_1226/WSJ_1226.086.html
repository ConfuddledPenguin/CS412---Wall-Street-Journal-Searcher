American Airlines Says Pilots Are Staging Sickout

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/26/90

DALLAS (AP) -- AMR Corp.'s American Airlines charges that its pilots are staging a sickout over stalled contract talks and have contributed to flight cancellations, but the pilots' union denies there's any organized protest. American management and the Allied Pilots Association have been negotiating a revised contract for 14 months, but have made little progress.

The increase in the number of pilots calling in sick, coupled with bad weather, the holiday traffic crush and the airline's loss of 230 pilots to Operation Desert Shield led to an unusually high number of flight cancellations over the weekend, said American spokesman Marty Heires.

At its hub at Dallas-Fort Worth International Airport on Sunday, the carrier canceled 37 arriving and 37 departing flights, amounting to about 9% of its flights.

The Fort Worth-based carrier said the number of pilots calling in sick over the weekend soared to record numbers, almost 70% higher than normal, airline officials said.

Robert W. Baker, American executive vice president for operations, told the Dallas Morning News that 529 of American's 9,000 pilots were on sick leave Monday and about 500 called in sick over the weekend.

The typical pilot absentee rate is 250 during normal periods and 300 during Christmas holidays, said Mr. Heires.
