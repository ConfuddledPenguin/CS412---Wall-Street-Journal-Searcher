Business Brief -- Southwestern Bell Corp.: Manager Cuts to Reduce Quarter Net by $36 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/26/90

Southwestern Bell Corp., St. Louis, said its fourth-quarter net income will be cut by about $36 million, or 12 cents a share, as a result of one-time payments to about 1,100 managers who participated in a plan to reduce the company's management ranks. In the year-earlier quarter, the company earned $297.2 million, or 99 cents a share, on revenue of $2.32 billion.

The company said the number of participants in the plan was in line with its expectations. In October, the company offered managers on its corporate staff, as well as managers at Southwestern Bell Telephone and Southwestern Bell Yellow Pages, as much as one year's salary if they left the company by year end.

Southwestern Bell said it will report results for the fourth quarter and the year in late January.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading Monday, Southwestern Bell shares closed at $56.375, down 25 cents.
