Transtector Extends Deadline

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/26/90

HAYDEN LAKE, Idaho -- Transtector Systems Inc. said it extended to April 30, 1991, from Nov. 30, 1990, the deadline for either the company or a group of managers to terminate a previously announced plan to take the company private. The electronics company said the agreement was extended because of generally poor market conditions and its weak financial performance. The company also said it will reduce its work force by 10% and trim overhead costs because of a change in its marketing programs and the weakening of the economy.

Transtector entered into the pact with managers in May. The original date for completion, Sept. 1, was extended to Nov. 30. The company's stock was quoted at 12.5 cents a share bid in over-the-counter trading Monday.
