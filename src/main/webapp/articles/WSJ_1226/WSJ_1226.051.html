REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: Judicial Discretion

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/26/90

A federal judge in San Jose has come up with a brilliant solution to the annual holiday rush of lawsuits by the American Civil Liberties Union. One of the ACLU lawsuits this year was against an allegedly unconstitutional creche in front of City Hall in Monterey, California. Supreme Court precedents obliged the judge to hear the case, but he decided to schedule arguments for today. Other judges might want to follow this precedent for postponing hearings until the day after Christmas, or in the case of menorahs after Hanukkah. This is one case where mootness sings the loudest tidings of good cheer.
