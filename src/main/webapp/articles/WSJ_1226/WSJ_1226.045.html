All My Children

It frightens, it paralyzes,

It leaves me aghast --

This manner in which the present

Surpasses the past:

The girl who spent two hours

Powdering her nose

Earns fifty thousand a year

Designing offbeat clothes,

And the lad who flunked arithmetic

(Too busy with his scooter)

Becomes another Einstein

On an electronic computer.

-- Al Bernstein.

Empty-Nest Celebration

Our last-born child was wed today,

And, while we feel the lack,

There is a bright side, I must say --

We got our car keys back]

-- Dick Emmons.
