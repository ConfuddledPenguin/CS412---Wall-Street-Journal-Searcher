Business Brief -- U.S. Home Corp.: Some Debt Retired, Accord Is Set on Paying Off More

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/26/90

U.S. Home Corp. said that during the fourth quarter, it retired $10 million principal amount of subordinated debt and agreed to retire an additional $11.5 million of subordinated debt and $7.5 million of senior debt. The Houston-based home builder said the agreement involves two institutional holders and provides for exchanging the debt for certain real property and mortgage notes not being used in the current business operations. Completion of the transactions will result in annual interest savings of more than $3.9 million, the company said.

The company also said that, in connection with continuing discussions with its banks on restructuring its $156 million credit agreement, the banks have agreed to reschedule to Feb. 1 a $5 million amortization payment that was due Dec. 17.
