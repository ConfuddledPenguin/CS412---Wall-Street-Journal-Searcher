Canada Opens `Dumping' Case

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/26/90

OTTAWA -- The Department of National Revenue for Customs and Excise said a "dumping" investigation has been initiated regarding certain stainless-steel welded pipe originating in or exported from Taiwan. Dumping is the selling of a product in a foreign market at a price lower than the cost of production or the domestic price. The investigation follows a complaint filed by Associated Tube Industries, a division of Samuel Manu-Tech Inc. of Toronto, alleging the injurious dumping of these goods. A decision to end the investigation or to make a preliminary determination of dumping is expected within 90 days.

The department also has asked the Canadian International Trade Tribunal to judge whether there was sufficient evidence of injury to initiate this investigation. If the tribunal, in making its decision within 30 days, indicates evidence wasn't sufficient, the case will be terminated.
