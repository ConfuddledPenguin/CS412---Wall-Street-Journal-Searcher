CD Yields Continue To Slide, Particularly At Major Brokerages

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/26/90

NEW YORK -- Yields on certificates of deposit continued to slide in the latest week, with the biggest declines coming on CDs sold by major brokerage firms. The average yield on small-denomination, three-month CDs sold directly by banks slipped under 7% in the latest week, according to an 18-bank national survey by Banxquote Money Markets, a New York information service.

The three-month average eased to 6.93% Monday, from 6.99% last Thursday and 7.01% the previous Tuesday, Banxquote reported. The average yield on five-year consumer CDs declined to 7.62% Monday from 7.68% the previous Tuesday.

Average yields for large-denomination CDs, which typically require deposits of $90,000 or more, also eased. The average yield on six-month jumbo CDs declined to 7.12% from 7.16% the previous Tuesday, while the average yield for five-year jumbos declined to 7.73% from 7.81%.

The decline in yields was most noticeable at major brokerage firms, which negotiate CD rates with banks and then sell the CDs to brokerage customers. The average yields on small-denomination six-month CDs sold by leading broker-dealers declined to 7.52% from 7.67% the previous Tuesday, while the average yield for five-year broker-sold CDs declined to 7.73% from 8.15%, according to Banxquote.

Banxquote's usual Tuesday survey was conducted on Monday because of the Christmas holiday.
