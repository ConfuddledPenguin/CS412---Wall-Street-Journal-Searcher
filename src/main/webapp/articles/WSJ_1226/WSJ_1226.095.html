FCC Clears MCA To Spin Off TV Unit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/26/90

UNIVERSAL CITY, Calif. -- MCA Inc. said the Federal Communications Commission approved the spinoff of WWOR-TV, clearing another hurdle on the way to completion of the company's $6.59 billion acquisition by Matsushita Electric Industrial Co. The Secaucus, N.J., television station is being spun off to MCA shareholders because TV stations are prohibited from being more than 20%-owned by foreign interests. MCA holders are to receive an "equity stub" in the station in addition to the $66 a share they receive when the tender offer is completed. MCA has estimated the stub to be worth about $5 a share; holders will receive one share of the new company for every five MCA shares.

Analysts believe the station will be sold within a short period of time. MCA bought it for $386 million in 1987.

The acquisition of MCA by Matsushita is expected to be completed anytime within the next three weeks. The Securities and Exchange Commission still must approve the transaction, and a hearing in U.S. District Court in Los Angeles set for Friday will rule on one shareholder suit which hasn't joined five others in a tentative settlement. The tender offer is scheduled to expire Friday.

An antitrust suit filed by Go-Video Inc. seeking to block the merger is on appeal after a federal court in Phoenix denied a temporary restraining order. Another shareholder suit which had claimed that foreign ownership of a U.S. media company violated the U.S. Constitution was dismissed by a federal judge in New York last Friday. A call by Interior Secretary Manuel Lujan for a review of MCA's proposed placement of its Yosemite Park and Curry Co. into escrow pending its sale isn't thought to be an obstacle to completing the merger.
