AT&T Gulf Program Mistakenly Brought Billings to Families

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/26/90

BASKING RIDGE, N.J. (AP) -- American Telephone & Telegraph Co. said 25,000 families and friends of people serving in the Persian Gulf were mistakenly billed for telephone calls that were supposed to be free, and the charges will be canceled. AT&T's offer to let men and women serving in the Gulf make free calls during Thanksgiving week resulted in 140,000 calls. But some local phone companies did not follow through on instructions not to bill the troops' families for the calls, AT&T spokesman Richard Wallerstein said. The calls were placed to families all over the U.S.

Mr. Wallerstein said no one will have to pay for the bills, no matter how high they were. The offer was for one three-minute call for each soldier, but Mr. Wallerstein said AT&T had no way to enforce the limit. He said he had heard some of the bills had reached $500, but had no specific information.

AT&T first offered free calls home for a week in mid-October, then renewed the offer for Thanksgiving. A total of about 275,000 calls were made during the two weeks. Each offered week of free calls cost AT&T more than $1 million, Mr. Wallerstein said.

Meanwhile, AT&T announced a free call-home campaign for U.S. Navy sailors and Marines stationed on ships in the Middle East. The offer is good through New Year's Eve, and Mr. Wallerstein said he didn't expect any problems. "We'll just do the bills right this time," he said.
