Business Brief -- Calton Inc.: Pact Reached With Lenders Revising $85 Million Credit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/26/90

Calton Inc., Freehold, N.J., said it reached an accord with lenders that would revise its $85 million secured credit agreement by restructuring it and extending the term. Under the proposed restructuring, the interest rate on its borrowings will rise to half a percentage point above the prime rate from the current one-quarter of a point over the prime, and the method of determining how much may be borrowed will be changed. The revised agreement also prohibits Calton, a housing developer, from paying dividends, incurring additional debt or acquiring additional land without the lending group's consent, except under certain circumstances.

The accord is conditioned on, among other things, Calton entering into a definitive agreement to exchange certain properties and joint-venture interests for about $18 million face amount of its 16 5/8% senior subordinated notes by Jan. 10. Calton has said it has a nonbinding letter of intent to acquire the notes and expects to have a definitive agreement with the holder of the notes by Monday.

The accord with lenders would extend the credit agreement until Dec. 1, 1991, from April 30, 1991.
