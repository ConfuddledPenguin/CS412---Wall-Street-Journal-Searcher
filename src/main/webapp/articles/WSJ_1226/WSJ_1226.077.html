Marketing & Media: Nelson Holdings' Entertainment Sets Purchase by Swid

  • Author: David J. Jefferson
  • Date: 12/26/90

LOS ANGELES -- The New York financier Stephen C. Swid signed a letter of intent to acquire Nelson Entertainment Group Inc. for $30 million in cash and notes and the assumption of $186 million in debt. As part of the transaction, Mr. Swid also would infuse $30 million of capital into the ailing entertainment company, which is the principal operating subsidiary of Toronto-based NHI Nelson Holdings International Ltd. Like many other Hollywood independents, Nelson Entertainment has fallen on hard times and been searching for an investor to bail it out. For the nine months ended Sept. 30, Nelson Holdings had a net loss of $12.1 million, or $3.33 a share, on revenue of $54 million.

Mr. Swid is chairman and chief executive officer of SCS Communications Inc., which owns Cinecom Entertainment Group, Spin magazine and the textbook publisher Westview Press. For the past two years, he has been hunting for an ailing movie company to buy at bargain-basement prices, but was unsuccessful in attempts to acquire Qintex Entertainment Inc. and the now-bankrupt Weintraub Entertainment Group.

Mr. Swid began investing in entertainment companies after building his fortune in carpeting and furniture makers. In 1986, he and two partners paid CBS Inc. $125 million for CBS Songs, with a 250,000-title library that includes classics such as "Over the Rainbow" and "Singin' in the Rain," and contemporary hits by James Taylor and Marvin Gaye. Three years later, the group turned around and sold the music publishing business to Thorn EMI for $337 million.

An acquisition of Nelson Entertainment would give Mr. Swid home-video distribution rights in North America to an 800-title library, including feature films and children's and music programming, and distribution in North American home-video markets and all international markets of films co-produced with Castle Rock Entertainment, including the hit "When Harry Met Sally" and the current thriller "Misery."

Under terms of the letter of intent, a newly created company controlled by Mr. Swid would pay $5 million in cash; a $5 million note due within one year and secured by a lien on all of Nelson Entertainment's assets; and a $20 million, seven-year note, which would be non-interest-bearing and subject to prepayment under certain conditions after March 31, 1992.

In addition, Mr. Swid's new company would assume Nelson Entertainment's debt outstanding of about $166 million -- including $108 million it owes under a credit facility with its principal lender, Credit Lyonnais Bank Nederland N.V. -- plus $20 million of Nelson Holdings' debt under a secured convertible debenture issue, due March 31, 1992.

The agreement also would give Mr. Swid's new company a five-year warrant to acquire 5% of Nelson Holdings' 69%-owned Nelson Vending Technology Ltd. subsidiary, an owner and operator of videotape vending machines in the Toronto area. Nelson Holdings would get a five-year warrant to acquire a 5% stake in Mr. Swid's new company.

A Feb. 4 deadline has been set for a definitive agreement. Nelson is free to pursue other offers before that time, though it must pay separation fees if a deal with Mr. Swid isn't completed. Nelson's adviser is Lazard Freres & Co., and Mr. Swid's adviser is Ocean Capital Corp.
