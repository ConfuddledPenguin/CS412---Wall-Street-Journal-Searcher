A&P to Back Offer That Will Decrease Its Stake in Isosceles

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/26/90

MONTVALE, N.J. -- Great Atlantic & Pacific Tea Co. said it will support a new share offering by Isosceles PLC that would reduce A&P's stake in the debt-laden British supermarket retailer to 6.6% from the 19.9%. A&P, a supermarket chain that is 52%-owned by the Tengelmann Group of Germany, and Isosceles fought last fall for Gateway Corp., another British food retailer. Isosceles won the takeover battle, and A&P in turn wound up with the 19.9% stake in Isosceles.

A&P said Isosceles is proceeding with the new share offering, which is part of a previously announced restructuring to provide it with about #222 million ($418.8 million) in new equity and converted debt.

A&P said it doesn't intend to purchase any shares in the offering, but intends to vote in favor of the shareholder resolutions authorizing the transaction. A&P also said it will retain its directorship on the Isosceles board.

A&P's investment in Isosceles has a current value of about $140 million, an A&P spokesman said. "We decided not to put any more of our funds into Isosceles at this time, because we feel we have enough invested as it is," he said. "But we recognize the need for Isosceles to refinance, and this equity will help them."
