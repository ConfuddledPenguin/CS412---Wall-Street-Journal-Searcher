  • Date: 12/26/90

Monday, December 24, 1990

The key U.S. and foreign annual interest rates below are a guide to general levels but don't always represent actual transactions.

PRIME RATE: 9 1/2%-10%. The base rate on corporate loans at large U.S. money center commercial banks.

FEDERAL FUNDS: 7 1/4% high, 2% low, 1% near closing bid, 3% offered. Reserves traded among commercial banks for overnight use in amounts of $1 million or more. Source: Babcock Fulton Prebon (U.S.A.) Inc.

DISCOUNT RATE: 6.50%. The charge on loans to depository institutions by the New York Federal Reserve Bank.

CALL MONEY: 9% to 9 1/2%. The charge on loans to brokers on stock exchange collateral.

COMMERCIAL PAPER: High-grade unsecured notes sold through dealers by major corporations in multiples of $1,000: 8.50% 30 days; 8.10% 60 days; 7.85% 90 days.

CERTIFICATES OF DEPOSIT: 7.48% one month; 7.53% two months; 7.51% three months; 7.49% six months; 7.60% one year. Average of top rates paid by major New York banks on primary new issues of negotiable C.D.s, usually on amounts of $1 million and more. The minimum unit is $100,000. Typical rates in the secondary market: 8.55% one month; 8% three months; 7.70% six months.

BANKERS ACCEPTANCES: 8.85% 30 days; 8% 60 days; 7.80% 90 days; 7.65% 120 days; 7.45% 150 days; 7.35% 180 days. Negotiable, bank-backed business credit instruments typically financing an import order.

LONDON EURODOLLARS: 8 7/8% - 8 3/4% one month; 8 3/8% - 8 1/4% two months; 8 3/16% - 8 1/16% three months; 8 1/16% - 7 15/16% four months; 7 15/16% - 7 13/16% five months; 7 7/8% - 7 3/4% six months.

LONDON INTERBANK OFFERED RATES (LIBOR): 8 13/16% one month; 8 3/16% three months; 7 7/8% six months; 7 3/4% one year. The average of interbank offered rates for dollar deposits in the London market based on quotations at five major banks. Effective rate for contracts entered into two days from date appearing at top of this column.

FOREIGN PRIME RATES: Canada 12.75%; Germany 10.50%; Japan 8%; Switzerland 10.50%; Britain 14%. These rate indications aren't directly comparable; lending practices vary widely by location.

TREASURY BILLS: Results of the Monday, December 17, 1990, auction of short-term U.S. government bills, sold at a discount from face value in units of $10,000 to $1 million: 6.78% 13 weeks; 6.77% 26 weeks.

FEDERAL NATIONAL MORTGAGE ASSOCIATION (Fannie Mae): Posted yields on 30 year mortgage commitments for delivery within 30 days (priced at par). 9.74%, standard conventional fixed-rate mortgages; 7.85%, 6/2 rate capped one-year adjustable rate mortgages. Source: Telerate Systems Inc.

MERRILL LYNCH READY ASSETS TRUST: 7.37%. Annualized average rate of return after expenses for the past 30 days; not a forecast of future returns.
