International Brief -- Lucky Ltd.: Venture Will Be Formed With 2 Japanese Companies

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/26/90

Japan's Sumitomo Chemical Co. and Nippon Shokubai Kagaku Kogyo Co. Ltd. agreed to form a joint venture company with Korea's Lucky Ltd. to produce a raw material for an industrial resin. The three companies said they would build a plant in Korea to produce an ingredient for methacrylate resin, used to enhance transparency or weather resistance in items such as housing materials.

The plant, scheduled for completion in the spring of 1993, is capitalized at 24 billion won ($33.6 million). Fifty percent of financing will be provided by Lucky Ltd.; the rest will come from the Japanese firms in equal shares.

The plant will be built in the Lucky Goldstar Group's petrochemical complex in Yeochun, Korea, the companies said. The new venture will be called Lucky MMA Corporation.

The plant will make 40,000 tons annually of the MMA monomer used in the industrial resin, and 35,000 tons yearly of isobutylene, MMA monomer's raw material.

Currently, the materials that the plant is expected to make are all imported into Korea, as there are no local producers, the companies said.

The two Japanese companies, which have been cooperating in the development of their own MMA monomer operations, run a joint venture in Japan called the Nihon Methacrylate Monomer Co.
