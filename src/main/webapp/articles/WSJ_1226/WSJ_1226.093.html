Carnival Cruise Lines Expects Slight Rise In Per-Share Earnings

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/26/90

MIAMI -- Carnival Cruise Lines Inc. expects to report per-share earnings for the fiscal year ended Nov. 30 of about $1.50 to $1.55, up from $1.44 in the previous year, said Micky Arison, chairman and chief executive officer. In the year ended Nov. 30, 1989, Carnival had net income of $193.6 million on revenue of $1.15 billion.

Through the nine months ended this August, Carnival had net of $1.39 a share, indicating that this year's fourth-quarter earnings will be about 11 cents to 16 cents a share. Net in the year-earlier fourth quarter was 16 cents a share.

Mr. Arison said in an interview that the flat to lower fourth-quarter results don't reflect trouble in the company's overall cruise business, which he characterized as "good." He said he is "very satisfied" with advance bookings, which cover travel over the next six months or so.

Despite greater capacity -- this year the company has added one new ship and a greater number of "berths," or beds on another -- Carnival's bookings are currently at about the same percentage of occupancy as this time last year, he said.

The trouble spot was the company's resort and casino business in Nassau, the Bahamas. That business was "clearly impacted by the economy," particularly by the economic slowdown in the Northeast -- a key base of customers for Carnival's Crystal Palace Resort & Casino in the Bahamas, said Mr. Arison.

Discounting in the cruise industry has been more widespread than normal in recent months, the chairman conceded.

"We have been doing quite a bit of discounting on the Mardi Gras and Carnival," said Mr. Arison, referring to two of the company's older ships. "We would anticipate, to some degree, {the discounting} will continue in that segment as the economy stays soft."
