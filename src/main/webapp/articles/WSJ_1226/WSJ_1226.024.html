Who's News: Hercules to Realign Officers; Retirement Set for Engebretsen

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/26/90

WILMINGTON, Del. -- Hercules Inc. said Arden B. Engebretsen, vice chairman and chief financial officer, will retire next Tuesday, as the chemical and aerospace company realigns top management to refocus on its aerospace business and reassess its strategic direction. A successor wasn't named.

Hercules also announced a series of changes in its reporting relationships, effective Tuesday. Richard Schwartz, executive vice president and the Hercules aerospace unit's president, will report directly to Thomas L. Gossage, who is to become chairman and chief executive officer, succeeding David Hollingsworth on his retirement next Monday. All other operating units, including the fragrance and food materials group and the advanced materials unit, will report to Fred L. Buckner, president and chief operating officer.

The company also will group several of its business activities -- technology, international, planning and environmental -- to develop a new long-term direction.

Hercules was set back by difficulties recently, including technical troubles and order delays in its rocket-booster operations, which led to a $221.9 million charge against results for 1989, when the company had a net loss of $81.3 million, or $1.76 a share, on sales of $3.09 billion.
