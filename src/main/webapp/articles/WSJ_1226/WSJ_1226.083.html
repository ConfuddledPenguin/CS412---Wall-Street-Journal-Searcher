CITICORP IS RAISING the fees it charges for a wide range of services, part of an accelerating trend among banks to generate additional fee income from consumers and small-business clients. For the hard-pressed company, the fee increases are the first since 1988 and in some cases amount to more than 25%. ---

TWA owner Carl Icahn said Pan Am pulled out of a meeting about his proposed purchase of the carrier. But Pan Am officials said they hadn't committed to any meeting, and lambasted the financier for asking them to consider "a series of ramblings."

---

Major insurance concerns plan to sharply cut back or halt their commercial mortgage lending activity next year, making it more difficult for banks and developers to find long-term backing for construction loans.

---

Retailers' final results for the holiday season are expected to be the worst in many years, despite efforts by stores to lure shoppers with increased advertising and discounting.

Toy makers' stock prices have taken a beating as the industry has been hit by the budding recession and tough competition over prices.

---

Insider selling at companies is picking up for the first time since Iraq invaded Kuwait, as executives, directors and other major holders are moving to take advantage of the recent rebound in the stock market.

---

Waste Management plans to have its fast-growing international business and two other majority-owned companies swap stakes, and the concern could also take the overseas operations public within about one year.

---

A failed Salomon deal to underwrite corporate bonds in Japan has sparked a government inquiry, which is examining if rival Japanese securities firms pressured the issuers to drop the U.S. firm as lead manager.

---

Stock prices declined in an extremely light, pre-holiday session on Monday. The Dow Jones industrials dropped 12.37 to 2621.29. Bond prices fell sharply, with the Treasury's 30-year issue losing nearly point.

---

Tokyo stocks fell in thin trading Tuesday. The Nikkei Index shed 351.72 points, or 1.5%, to 23767.88.

---

Financier Stephen Swid agreed to buy Nelson Entertainment Group for $30 million in cash and notes and the assumption of $186 million in debt. Swid would also put $30 million of capital into the ailing company.

---

Mobil Oil and Vietnamese and Soviet officials are quietly exploring a possible three-way oil-production venture off Vietnam that could lead to the first U.S.-Soviet cooperation in the energy sector in a third country.
