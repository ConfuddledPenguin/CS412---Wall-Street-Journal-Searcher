International: Rebels in Pacific Declare Independence -- Via Fax

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 05/18/90

Call it the first Fax of Independence. Last June, secessionist rebels on the bejungled South Pacific island of Bougainville closed down one of the world's largest copper mines, the Panguna mine principally owned by CRA Ltd., Australia's largest mining company.

On Thursday, Reuters news agency reported, the rebels declared their independence from the nation of Papua New Guinea by sending a facsimile message to government offices, foreign missions and to the news agency.

It began: "I, Francis Ona, interim president of the Republic of Bougainville, do hereby proclaim" the island to be "a sovereign democratic and independent nation."

Government officials say they'll ignore it.

Mr. Ona, a former mine surveyor, is a 35-year-old Bougainvillean and rebel leader. Fighting between the rebels and government troops over the last 18 months has taken nearly 100 lives.
