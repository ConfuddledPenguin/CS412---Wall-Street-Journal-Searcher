Scientific-Atlanta Inc. Gets Communications Satellite Unit Job

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/18/90

ATLANTA -- Scientific-Atlanta Inc., in a move to build the audience for high-definition television, said it will supply a HDTV system to Comsat Video Enterprises. Comsat, a unit of Washington-based Communications Satellite Corp., will use the system to broadcast live events to selected locations, particularly its current stronghold, pay-per-view TVs in hotels.

Scientific-Atlanta didn't disclose the value of the contract, but the HDTV system is the third that it has agreed to provide during the past year, said William E. Johnson, the company's chief executive officer. The previous two were with Canadian companies that planned to use the technology for live broadcasts to movie theaters, he said.

In HDTV systems, Scientific-Atlanta transmits signals between TV studios or sites where events originate and the point at which they are distributed to movie theaters or closed-circuit TV sets. The company provides the equipment to beam the signal to a satellite and back to receiving stations, converting it for local broadcast.

Analysts said the Comsat order gives a boost to U.S. efforts in one area of the international competition to develop HDTV technology. "It shows we're not conceding the field to the Japanese," said Eric C. Buck, an analyst with Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette.

Comsat, which provides pay-per-view television to about 1,700 hotels, will first use the new system to broadcast singer Dionne Warwick's AIDS benefit concert, scheduled for June 30, to hotels in 20 cities across the country, said Robert J. Wussler, Comsat's president and chief executive officer. He said the hotels will be supplied with monitors capable of receiving the high-definition signal.
