  • Date: 05/18/90

FIRST EXECUTIVE Corp. intended to seek approval of an increase in its authorized capital stock at its annual meeting later this year. The plan met with objections from a major shareholder. Wednesday's edition incorrectly said the company had been considering a sale of common stock. (See: "First Executive Cash Outflow Is $1.33 Billion" -- WSJ May 16, 1990)

---

WESTERN UNION Corp.'s financial-services revenue grew 11% to $78.6 million. The figure was misstated in yesterday's edition. In addition, the company said it no longer plans to reclassify the Class B common shares held by Brooke Partners Limited Partnership into new common shares.

(See: "Western Union Deficit Widened in First Quarter" -- WSJ May 17, 1990)

---

PENSION FUNDS are permitted by federal law to trade futures on margin. The law was misstated in yesterday's edition.

(See: "Futures Shock: Tight Rein if SEC Reigns --- More Skepticism, Higher Margins" -- WSJ May 17, 1990)

---

THE DOW JONES Equity Market Index closed Wednesday at 329.15; the Technology group closed at 276.91; and the Communications Technology group including AT&T closed at 406.52. An error in a composite-trading figure led to errors in some of the data in the Dow Jones Industry Groups table, which appeared in yesterday's edition.
