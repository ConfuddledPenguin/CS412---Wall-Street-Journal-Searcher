Business Brief -- Yamaha Corp.: Unconsolidated Pretax Net Increased 17% in Fiscal Year

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/18/90

Yamaha Corp., the Tokyo-based musical-instrument maker, said unconsolidated pretax profit rose 17% to 12.11 billion yen ($79.8 million) in the fiscal year ended March 31 from 10.36 billion yen the year before. Net earnings grew 16% to 4.25 billion yen from 3.68 billion yen, despite a slight decline in sales. The company attributed the gain to successful development and promotion of high-value-added products.

Overall sales fell 3.2% to 384.7 billion yen from 397.6 billion yen, as both domestic and export sales declined.

Domestic sales edged down 0.7% to 270.6 billion yen, as the company's main product lines posted varied performances.

While sales of pianos and organs, the company's main lines, were sluggish, those of nonmusic-related products rose at a healthy pace. Household goods, such as furniture, rose 6.7% to 43.4 billion yen from 40.7 billion yen. The company's sales of yachts and motor boats posted a 15% rise to 18.6 billion yen from 16.2 billion yen. Sports equipment was up 8.8% to 26.48 billion yen from 24.33 billion yen.
