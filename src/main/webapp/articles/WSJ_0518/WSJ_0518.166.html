Marketing Brief: Noted....

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/18/90

TV: Maury Povich, the anchor of Fox Broadcasting Co.'s "A Current Affair" since it began five years ago, will leave the nightly tabloid broadcast when his contract expires next May. He will host an afternoon, daily talk show to be produced by his company, ACA Productions Inc. and distributed by Paramount Communications Inc., beginning in September 1991.

WHO'S NEWS: Raymond H. Godfrey Jr. was named president and chief operating officer of Management Co. Entertainment Group Inc., a talent-management and motion-picture concern. He succeeds Charles J. Weber, who resigned last fall. . . . Time Warner Enterprises, a unit of Time Warner Inc., made four appointments: Mark Mitzner, who was executive vice president and chief financial officer of Warner's Quantum Media Inc. unit, was named an executive vice president; Mayo S. Stuntz Jr., formerly president of Quantum Media Television and an executive vice president of Quantum Media Inc., was named an executive vice president; and Brian Bedol, who was executive vice president of QMI Television and vice president of Quantum Media Inc., and Stanley B. Thomas Jr., who was senior vice president of affiliate operations for Time Warner's Home Box Office Inc. unit, both were named senior vice presidents.
