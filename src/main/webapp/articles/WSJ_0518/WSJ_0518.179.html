Business Brief -- Havas SA: Net Increased 30% in 1989 On 20% Rise in Revenue

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/18/90

French media group Havas SA announced that its consolidated net profit after payments to minority interests rose by 30% in 1989 to 975 million francs ($175.8 million) from 751 million francs a year earlier. Consolidated revenue was up 20% for the year at 18.9 billion francs compared with 15.8 billion francs in 1988. Both the revenue and the profit figures confirm preliminary estimates that Havas released in February.

Havas said it plans to raise its net annual dividend to 5.75 francs a share, a 39% increase from the 4.13 francs paid out last year. It will be paid beginning July 17.

Speaking to analysts, Havas Director of Development Michel Boutinard-Rouelle said the company has targeted Europe as its 1990 primary growth market.

"Europe is on the rise," he said, noting that the region is expected to represent 28% of Havas's total investments by 1992, up from 24% in 1988. The North American share, meanwhile, is seen falling to 51% from 55%, while investment in the rest of the world remains fairly steady.
