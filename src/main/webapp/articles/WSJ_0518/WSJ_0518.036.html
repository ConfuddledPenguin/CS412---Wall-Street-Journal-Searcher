State Jobless Claims Rose To 353,000 During Week

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/18/90

WASHINGTON -- Initial claims for regular state unemployment benefits rose to a seasonally adjusted 353,000 during the week ended May 5 from 349,000 the previous week, the Labor Department said. The number of people receiving regular state benefits in the week ended April 28 rose to a seasonally adjusted 2,440,000, or 2.3% of those covered by unemployment insurance, from 2,398,000 the previous week, when the insured unemployment rate was also 2.3%.

Counting all state and federal benefit programs, the number of people receiving unemployment benefits during the week ended April 28 fell to 2,463,000 from 2,492,800 a week earlier. These figures aren't seasonally adjusted.
