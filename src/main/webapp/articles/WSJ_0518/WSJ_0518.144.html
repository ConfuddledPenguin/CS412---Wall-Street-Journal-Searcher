OTC Focus: Market Edges Higher as Small Investors Spur Volume Jump; Quantum Gains 1 1/8

  • Author: Anne Newman
  • Date: 05/18/90

Over-the-counter stocks continued to crawl higher as traders gave mixed reviews of the degree of enthusiasm among small investors. The Nasdaq Composite Index climbed 2.81 to 445.74, a 0.63% gain. Advancing shares broadened their lead over declining stocks, 1,111 to 836, as volume accelerated to 171.4 million shares from 152.5 million.

Small investors' orders for OTC stocks picked up at PaineWebber, according to Richard Bruno, head of OTC trading there. "I think people on the sidelines have come in," said Mr. Bruno, who estimated that the brokerage firm's orders from "retail" customers are up 25% to 30% this month.

However, Jack Bayer, head of OTC trading at Oppenheimer, said the market for smaller OTC stocks "remains stagnant." Similarly, Lawrence Rice, national manager of OTC trading at Wedbush Morgan Securities in Los Angeles, said, "It seems like a lot of people are still sitting on their wallets." Mr. Rice's firm handles trading between securities industry professionals.

Larger issues attracted most of the attention yesterday at Robinson Humphrey's New York office, according to Jim O'Neill, co-manager of OTC trading. Among the more active issues was Sensormatic Electronics, which gained 1 to close at a new 52-week high of 14 1/8 on 1.4 million shares. Mr. O'Neill said he wasn't aware of any particular news or comments fueling interest in the Deerfield Beach, Fla., maker of electronics equipment.

Quantum also was among the day's most-active shares, advancing 1 1/8 to 19 on 2.1 million shares. The Milpitas, Calif., maker of disk drives said it reached a new supply agreement with Leading Edge Products of Westborough, Mass., valued at $20 to $25 million over the next year.

MCI Communications, the day's most active stock, rose 7/8 to 41 3/4 on 2.7 million shares, and helped fuel a 0.86% gain in the Nasdaq utility index.

DST Systems jumped 1 7/8 to 14 5/8 after Kansas City Southern Industries said it plans to begin a $14-a-share tender offer for the 12.9% of the Kansas City, Mo., software concern that it doesn't already own.

L.M. Ericsson rallied 6 1/8 to 182 7/8 after Japan's Nippon Telegraph & Telephone announced that it chose the Swedish telecommunications company, along with Motorola, AT&T and seven Japanese companies, to jointly develop NTT's digital mobile telephone system.

Reuters's American Depositary Receipts joined a broad rally in other, exchange-listed British issues, gaining 1 7/8 to 59 7/8.

Nature's Bounty rose 1 1/4 to 7 after the Bohemia, N.Y., maker of vitamins, nutritional supplements and personal-care items reported late Wednesday that operating profit for the second quarter ended March 31 was 33 cents a share, up from 19 cents a share a year earlier.

Samna reached a new 52-week high, rising 1 5/8 to 9 1/2. John Throckmorton, chief financial officer of the Atlanta software maker, told Dow Jones Professional Investor Report that on May 15 the company licensed its Ami executive word processing product to Software Publishing. On Wednesday, he added, the firm began shipping another product, the Ami Professional 1.2, which is compatible with Microsoft's new Windows 3.0 operating system. The product is expected to be unveiled Tuesday. Samna's stock has nearly doubled since the beginning of the month, as investors awaited both the launch of both its own and Microsoft's products.

Software Toolworks gained 1 7/8 to 17 7/8. The Chatsworth, Calif., developer and distributor of software said Wednesday that it plans to file next month for a July offering of as many as four million shares, with about half to be sold by the company and half by certain shareholders. The company's proceeds will be used for working capital and general corporate purposes.

One Swedish issue didn't fare so well. AB Electrolux lost 1/2 to 39 3/8 after the home appliance group said that its pretax profit in the first three months of 1990 fell 41.3%, more steeply than market expectations.

Secor Bank lost 5/8 to 3 7/8 after the Birmingham, Ala., bank suspended its 10-cents a share quarterly dividend on common stock and its dividends on series A and B preferred shares. The bank said that its dividends paid have exceeded net income before dividends for each of the last three quarters.

Optical Coating Laboratory fell 3/4 to 4 1/8 after the Santa Rosa, Calif., maker of optical thin film coatings reported net income for the second quarter ended April 30 of six cents a share, down from 17 cents a year earlier.

Corrections tumbled for the second consecutive day after articles in two Texas newspapers said there's a chance state prison officials might cancel operating contracts for two prison facilities built by the Nashville, Tenn., company, according to DJPIR. A company spokeswoman called the speculation premature. Corrections, which fell 1/2 Wednesday, dropped 7/8 yesterday to 9.

Asiamerica Equities lost 2 to 5 1/4. The Vancouver, British Columbia, investment and real estate trust attributed recent volatility in its shares to "investor margin pressures," but didn't specify which investors might be having trouble meeting their margin calls. Asiamerica said there haven't been any recent material changes at the company.
