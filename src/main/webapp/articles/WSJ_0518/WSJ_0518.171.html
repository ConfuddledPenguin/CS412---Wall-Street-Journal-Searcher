Business Brief -- Armstrong World Industries Inc.: Management Wins 3 of 4 Contested Seats on Board

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/18/90

Management of Armstrong World Industries Inc., a Lancaster, Pa., building products concern, won three of the four board seats contested at the April 30 annual meeting, according to the final tally of proxy votes cast. Of its dissident slate, the Belzberg family of Canada, which owns about 11.5% of Armstrong, won one seat. Of the 155 million votes cast, board nominees received about 105 million votes and Belzberg nominees about 50 million. Under the cumulative voting system, holders got four votes a share to allocate any way they chose, including leaving them to the proxy solicitor's discretion. Management chose to cumulate its proxy votes to re-elect three directors: Barbara Hackman Franklin, president of Franklin Associates, a management consulting firm; Joseph L. Jones, retired Armstrong chairman and president; and Robert F. Patton, retired Integra Financial Corp. vice chairman. The Belzbergs cumulated their votes to elect Michael C. Jensen, a professor at Harvard Business School.

Five corporate governance amendments proposed by the Belzbergs were defeated by significant margins.
