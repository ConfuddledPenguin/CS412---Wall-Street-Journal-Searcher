Campbell Soup Posts 25% Increase in Profit For Its Third Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/18/90

CAMDEN, N.J. -- Campbell Soup Co. reported a 25% increase in third-quarter earnings as improved cost controls in North American operations and higher profits in Argentina offset continuing problems in the U.K. and Europe. In the third quarter, ended April 29, the food company earned $54.6 million, or 42 cents per share, on sales of $1.52 billion. In the 1989 quarter, net income was $43.5 million, or 34 cents a share, on sales of $1.45 billion.

Campbell International's operating earnings were up 45%, to $16.7 million, compared with last year's third-quarter profit of $11.5 million. Sales for the division were $460 million, compared with $418 million a year ago. Profitability improved in Campbell's Argentine beef operations, but the company said its British frozen-food unit and Italian biscuit operations continued to have "earnings difficulties."

The company's largest division, Campbell U.S.A., reported a 10% increase, to $91.3 million, in third-quarter operating earnings, up from $83 million last year. Sales for the division, which makes such products as V8 vegetable juice, soup and Prego spaghetti sauce, were $881 million, up only 4%, from $847 million last year. Campbell attributed the slow sales growth to general weakness in retail sales and an unusually warm winter soup season.

Nine-month earnings were $242.8 million, or $1.87 per share, on sales of $4.77 billion. In the 1989 period, Campbell earned $207.8 million, or $1.61 per share, on sales of $4.37 billion.
