Letters to the Editor: Hall of Shame Spot Isn't Wasted on Rogers

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/18/90

In regard to your hallucinatory editorial-page article of April 19 about Kenny Rogers's well-earned place on Rolling Stone's "Environmental Hall of Shame" list: Mr. Rogers called Greenpeace USA Chairman David Chatfield (whom your researchers might have discovered was the "guy in San Francisco" had research been a priority for you) in February 1989 and identified himself as an "investor" in Summit Cement Co.'s toxic-waste-incinerator project. Mr. Chatfield never gave the "off-the-record" approval attributed to him, and your reporter committed journalistic malpractice by making no effort to verify that statement (or even verify the identity of the person who said it).

Summit Cement, whose waste is both "toxic" and "hazardous," chose to build a plant in the Bahamas to escape the community opposition that has become commonplace in the U.S. due to the poor track record of waste handlers. Summit Cement's stated goal, according to papers filed with both the EPA and the Bahamian government, was to import up to 88,000 tons of waste from the U.S.

The project was not killed by "environmentalist roadblocks," but by the Bahamian government's denial of permission to import the waste. For his flacking for such an ill-advised project, Kenny Rogers deserves his spot in the Environmental Hall of Shame. Your editorial page rates an even higher ranking.

Jim Vallette

International Waste Trade Coordinator

Greenpeace

Washington
