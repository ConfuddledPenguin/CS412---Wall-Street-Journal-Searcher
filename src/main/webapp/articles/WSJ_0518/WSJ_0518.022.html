Who's News: Kroger Says Pichler To Get Added Title Of Chief Executive

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/18/90

CINCINNATI -- Kroger Co. said Joseph A. Pichler, president and chief operating officer of this food store operator, was elected chief executive officer and will assume the post June 17. Pichler, 50, succeeds Lyle Everingham, who has held the position since 1978. Mr. Everingham, 64, will continue as chairman until his planned retirement in early 1991.

At the company's annual meeting Mr. Everingham told shareholders that the changeover is "part of an orderly process designed to ensure continuity and direction. Joe and I share the same goals and objectives for Kroger."

In 1989 Kroger posted a loss of $72.2 million, or 92 cents a share, after a charge of $56.5 million related to early retirement of debt. Sales last year were $19.1 billion.

Kroger took on debt of more than $4 billion two years ago in a recapitalization that fended off two unsolicited bids.

Mr. Pichler has been president and chief operating officer of Kroger since October 1986. He was named to Kroger's board in January 1983, following the company's merger with Dillon Cos.
