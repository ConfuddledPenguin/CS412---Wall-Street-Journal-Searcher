Toyota to Produce Vehicles in Colombia With Renault, Mitsui

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/18/90

TOKYO -- Toyota Motor Corp. announced it will manufacture commercial vehicles in Colombia in a joint project with a subsidiary of France's Renault and Mitsui & Co., a major Japanese trading house. Toyota officials said their company, Japan's biggest car maker, has signed agreements with Sociedad de Fabrication de Automotores Renault S.A. (SOFASA-Renault) and Mitsui. They said Toyota has also obtained necessary government approval.

Under the agreement, they said, Bogota-based SOFASA-Renault will add Toyota's Land Cruiser and Hilux models to the line of Renault cars it has been producing.

The partners plan to make 4,000 Land Cruisers a year, beginning in October 1991. Production of the Hilux model is set for April 1992, at a rate of 3,000 a year, according to Toyota officials.

The two Toyota models will be marketed through a new Toyota dealership network that SOFASA-Renault will set up in the future.

Under the accord, Toyota and Mitsui will also acquire stakes in SOFASA-Renault. Toyota will pay $10.5 million, and Mitsui $4.5 million. As a result, Toyota officials said, SOFASA-Renault will be 74.7% owned by Renault, 17.5% by Toyota, 7.5% by Mitsui, and the rest by others.

Investment in plant and equipment totaling some $7.3 million will be made for production of the Toyota models, according to officials.

SOFASA-Renault, employing about 1,600 people, produced approximately 11,000 cars last year.
