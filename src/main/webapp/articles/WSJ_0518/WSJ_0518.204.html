AT&T to Trim Rates 2.5% for Some Calls; Reduced Costs Cited

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/18/90

NEW YORK -- American Telephone & Telegraph Co. said it will cut long-distance, evening-hour charges 2.5% -- or $100 million a year -- starting July 1. In a filing with the Federal Communications Commission, the company said the reduction relates to prices of direct-dialed interstate calls between 5 p.m. and 11 p.m., Sunday through Friday.

Under FCC price-cap regulations, AT&T files price adjustments annually. This reduction was made possible in part by anticipated savings from further cuts in the access fees charged by local telephone companies. Another factor is a change AT&T is making in directory assistance: AT&T said it will eliminate the free call to interstate directory assistance now offered each month, saying only one in four residential customers use directory assistance during a three-month period. The price of a directory assistance call is 60 cents.

When the July 1 decrease takes effect, a 10-minute direct-dialed call anywhere in the continental U.S. will cost $1.53 or less, compared with the current $1.58 or less. The decrease is AT&T's 11th since 1984, when the Bell system was broken up.

A spokesman for US Sprint Communications Co., a subsidiary of United Telecommunications Inc. of Kansas City, Mo., said: "We haven't seen the filing yet and can't comment specifically. But we are competitive and will take whatever steps are necessary to remain competitive."

An MCI Communications Corp. spokeswoman said the company is studying AT&T's moves, "and, as is our tradition, MCI will remain competitive."

AT&T added that it expects in the future to be able to cut prices an additional $60 million in other long-distance and operator services.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, AT&T closed at $42.875 a share, up 87.5 cents.
