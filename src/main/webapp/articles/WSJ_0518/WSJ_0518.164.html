Business Brief -- Gap Inc.: Retailer Posts 40% Increase In 1st-Quarter Net Income

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/18/90

Gap Inc., San Francisco, said net income increased 40% on a 30% increase in sales in its first quarter ended May 5. Net rose to $21.2 million, or 60 cents a share, from $15.1 million, or 43 cents a share, a year earlier. Sales rose 30% to $402 million. Both figures were records.

Despite the strong quarter, investors battered the company's stock, as analysts cut earnings estimates for the second quarter more than 10% in some cases.

Edward Weller, an analyst at Montgomery Securities, said under the Gap's accounting methods, some of the cost of April's markdowns will be felt in the month of May, so he reduced his estimate for second quarter per-share net seven cents, to 51 cents.

The Gap operates stores under its own name as well as Banana Republic. Mr. Weller said that he believes the latter chain, which has had difficulties lately, earned a "slight" profit in the quarter, compared to a loss a year earlier.
