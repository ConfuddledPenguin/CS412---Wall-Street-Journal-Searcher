Business Brief -- Outboard Marine Corp.: Charge of $3 Million Set For Period in Shutting Site

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/18/90

Outboard Marine Corp., a Waukegan, Ill., maker of marine engines and recreational power boats, said it will take a $3 million charge in its third quarter ending June 30 from the closing of one of its outboard-motor assembly plants this fall. Outboard also said it will idle its eight other U.S. marine power-products plants for several weeks during the remainder of its year ending Sept. 30.

In the year-earlier quarter, Outboard earned $6.8 million, or 35 cents a share, on sales of $465.9 million.

The company said it will close its Rutherfordton, N.C., plant in November to reduce costs and increase operating efficiencies, and idle the other plants to avoid building excessive inventories of marine engines during the continuing industrywide sales slump. The Rutherfordton plant, which has 290 employees, assembles Johnson and Evinrude outboard motors; assembly operations there will be consolidated at Outboard's other outboard assembly facility in Calhoun, Ga., creating as many as 200 jobs. The company expects that the consolidation could save as much as $3 million annually.
