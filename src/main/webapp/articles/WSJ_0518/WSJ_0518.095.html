Letters to the Editor: Is There a Conscience Beneath the Image?

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/18/90

With regard to John F. Budd Jr.'s April 16 Manager's Journal: Plaudits to Mr. Budd for an excellent article. It should be required reading for every corporate executive. Clearly, when even senior managers do not trust their CEOs, we have a serious problem. This is, however, not difficult to understand. When we have been exposed to the arrogance of Exxon following the Valdez incident and the incompetence of the Drexel Burnham Lambert crowd, how can we feel kindly toward large corporations? When we have seen some of our major trademarks sold at auction by the likes of Henry Kravis, T. Boone Pickens and Frank Lorenzo, is it not difficult to believe in the social conscience of businessmen? When GM lost more than 10% of the world auto market in the past decade, how do we continue to accept that "What is good for General Motors is good for the USA"?

The reputation of our politicians was damaged seriously by the Watergate incident. Will the believability of our executives suffer the same fate as a result of incidents such as those mentioned above? Yes, corporate America needs to improve its image. It also needs to improve its act or its attempts at image enhancement will be only meaningless PR gestures.

Richard A. Gwennap

Pittsburgh
