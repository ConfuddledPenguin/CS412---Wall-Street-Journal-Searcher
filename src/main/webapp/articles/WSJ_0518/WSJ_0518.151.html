Business Brief -- Navistar International Corp.: Second-Period Net Dropped 77% on 10% Sales Decline

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/18/90

Navistar International Corp. said second-quarter earnings fell 77% on a 10% sales decline, reflecting the continued industrywide slump in retail-truck sales. The Chicago maker of trucks and diesel engines said it earned $8 million, or one cent a share, on sales of $977 million for the fiscal-second quarter ended April 30. In the year-earlier quarter, the company earned $35 million, or 11 cents a share, on sales of $1.09 billion.

For the six months, Navistar had a loss of $10 million, or 10 cents a share, compared with earnings of $72 million, or 23 cents a share. Sales fell to $1.81 billion from $2.04 billion.

Industry sales of medium and heavy trucks in North America, according to Navistar, totaled 71,000 units in the quarter, down 12% from last year. Navistar said it delivered 22,200 units during the quarter, down 10%.

Navistar said it expects industry sales of heavy trucks to fall 18% to 142,000 units, and medium trucks to fall 4% to 153,000 units, in its fiscal year ending Oct. 31.
