Abreast of the Market: Industrials Resume Rally, Closing At New High of 2831.71 Points

  • Author: Craig S. Smith
  • Date: 05/18/90

NEW YORK -- Stocks resumed their latest rally, sending the Dow Jones Industrial Average to another all-time high. Pinnacle West climbed 28%, or 3 5/8, to 16 3/8 after acknowledging that tireless PacifiCorp had presented it with yet another acquisition proposal. Pinnacle has rejected three previous PacifiCorp merger proposals in the past seven months.

Eric T. Miller, chief investment officer at Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette, says the Pinnacle story is an illustration that corporate buying is still the most important source of demand for stock, whether it be through mergers, share repurchases or the occasional leveraged buy-out.

Pinnacle's news came against a backdrop of rising stock prices as the market shrugged off a wider-than-expected trade deficit for March, reported before the opening.

Computer-driven buy programs spurred the industrial average up 25 points before it settled back down to a gain of 12.03 points for the day, ending at 2831.71, a new closing high for the index.

Standard & Poor's 500-Stock Index added 0.47 to 354.47, while the New York Stock Exchange Composite Index rose 0.35 to 193.49.

Big Board volume totaled 164,770,000 shares, up from Wednesday's 159,810,000 shares. Advancers led decliners, 832 to 649.

Donaldson Lufkin's Mr. Miller said the momentum may carry over today. Others said, however, that May stock options and future contracts expire today, leaving the market vulnerable to unexpected swings.

The next big psychological hurdle for equity investors won't come until early June, when the Labor Department releases data on the nation's May employment.

The numbers are likely to clarify the economic picture that was clouded by seasonal factors earlier in the year.

Alcan Aluminium led the most active list, down 1/8 at 21 1/8 on volume of 5.3 million shares in apparent dividend-related trading. The stock, which went ex-dividend on Monday, led the most active list Wednesday on volume of more than nine million shares.

Traders said Texas Utilities was also high on the active list because of trading tied to its dividend. The stock, which carries an 8% dividend yield, goes ex-dividend May 29. It rose 1/4 to 36 7/8 on volume of more than 2.2 million shares. More than two million shares of the stock changed hands Wednesday.

Sotheby's Holdings was also high on the list with a 1/4 point gain to 18 7/8. The company held a huge auction of impressionist and modern paintings last night.

Safecard Services soared 1 to 8 3/4, a new 52-week high on heavy volume after Wertheim Schroder recommended the stock.

Philip Morris was active, easing 1/8 to 44 3/8. The company's German subsidiary has agreed to acquire a cigarette plant in East Germany, according to a report by the East German news service ADN. Terms weren't disclosed.

Trading in American Stores closed at 69, up 3 1/2 after a delayed opening. The company has agreed to sell most of its Alpha Beta stores, ending a 1 1/2 year antitrust battle with the state of California over American Stores's $2.5 billion acquisition of Lucky Stores. American Stores will continue to operate Lucky but will sell 152 of Alpha Beta's 175 stores in Southern California within the next five years.

National Westminster Bank added 1 1/8 to 33 5/8. The U.K. clearing bank is in "exclusive discussions" to buy Europeenne de Banque from Credit Commercial de France, according to the Financial Times. The acquisition, if completed, would more than double NatWest's network in France, where its presence has lagged behind Barclays and Midland.

Federal National Mortgage Association added 5/8 to 38 3/4. An important measure of the association's profitability widened in April, suggesting that the company's profits got off to a good start in the second quarter.

SmithKline Beecham's American depositary receipts gained 1 1/2 to 41 3/4. Prudential-Bache Securities upgraded its rating on the stock to a "buy" from a "hold," according to Dow Jones Professional Investor Report.

Shares of Digital Equipment Corp. slid 1, or 1.8%, to 87 3/8. Traders said the computer company's shares fell because a trade magazine reported that shipments of Digital's new VAX-9000 mainframe computer could be delayed a month or more. A Digital Equipment spokesman acknowledged some delay in the shipping schedule for the company's VAX-9000 but said it would have "no revenue impact." Dallas Kirk, director of public relations, said "the schedule has shifted somewhat, but it's nothing significant." He also said orders for the 9000 "have been great."

Hewlett-Packard continued to slide, losing 3/4 to 45 3/8 after dropping 1 7/8 Wednesday. The company's executives were said to have taken a "cautious" tone in a conference call with analysts after the release of fiscal second-quarter results Wednesday, said the investor report. It said several analysts lowered their 1990 earnings estimates over concern about Hewlett's shift toward selling more lower-margin products.

New Jersey-based First Fidelity Bancorp. jumped 1 7/8 to 19 1/2. The company announced it will pay a reduced quarterly dividend of 30 cents a share due to an expected loan-loss provision in the second quarter of as much as $300 million. The company said its internal loan portfolio review is near completion.

Shareholders of HomeFed had a different reaction to news of a rise in nonperforming assets at HomeFed Bank. HomeFed fell 2 to 30 after reporting that nonperforming assets rose 10% in April.

Westpac Banking slid 1 5/8 to 18 5/8. The company reported that its six-month operating profit fell 8% from a year earlier.

Tucson Electric Power fell 1/8 to 10 7/8. The company raised the possibility that it might be forced to seek bankruptcy-law protection from its creditors if various financing plans aren't successful, The Wall Street Journal reported.

The American Stock Exchange Market Value Index rose 1.26 to 354.94. Volume totaled 13,760,000 shares compared with 12,190,000 Wednesday.
