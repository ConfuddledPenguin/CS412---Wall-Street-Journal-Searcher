Cost of Living in Chinese Cities

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/18/90

BEIJING -- The cost of living in Chinese cities rose 4.6% in April, compared with April 1989, China's State Statistical Bureau reported. It was the biggest monthly inflation climb so far this year. The bureau said the increase was caused mainly by a 14.1% rise in vegetable prices and a 15% rise in service charges. The cost-of-living increase for the first quarter was 4.4% over the 1989 level.

A brief report on the bureau's statistics carried by the official Xinhua News Agency didn't link the stepped-up inflation to a recent government loosening of its tight credit policy. The government pullback was credited for a slight pickup in industrial production in April after a stagnant first quarter.
