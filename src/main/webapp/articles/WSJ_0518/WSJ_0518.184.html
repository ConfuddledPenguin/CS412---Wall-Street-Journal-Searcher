Business Brief -- AB Electrolux: Appliance Firm's Pretax Net Fell 41% in First Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/18/90

Swedish appliance giant AB Electrolux said pretax profit plummeted 41% in the first quarter and warned that weak demand in several key markets is likely to persist for the rest of the year. Electrolux's pretax profit skidded to 511 million kronor ($85 million) from 871 million kronor a year earlier.

Electrolux Chief Executive Anders Scharp told shareholders at the Stockholm-based company's annual meeting that plunging aluminum prices -- which hurt the company's Granges metals unit -- and fierce price competition in U.S. appliance markets accounted for most of the profit decline. And he remained skeptical about a rapid recovery, citing in particular hefty cost increases that are eating into profit margins in countries where Electrolux has concentrated its production.

As a result, Mr. Scharp said Electrolux would make fewer acquisitions and reduce the heady level of capital expenditures it has maintained in recent years. But in an immediate exception to that cautious policy, the company separately announced its first major step into Eastern Europe.

Electrolux said it agreed with the Hungarian government to acquire a majority stake in the country's biggest appliance maker, Lehel.
