Business Brief -- Ashland Oil Inc.: Natural Gas Sites' Purchase From Occidental Unit Set

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/18/90

Ashland Oil Inc. said its exploration unit agreed to buy natural gas properties from an Occidental Petroleum Corp. unit for $107 million. The Ashland, Ky., company said it will acquire about 550,000 acres of producing and undeveloped properties in West Virginia and Kentucky from Los Angeles-based Occidental's OXY USA Inc. subsidiary. Production averages about 16 million cubic feet a day.

Ashland said the acquisition, which is subject to certain government approvals, will double its acreage of gas properties. The company already produces 39 million cubic feet a day from about 500,000 acres in Appalachia. Many of the new sites are contiguous to Ashland's properties.

Analysts said the Appalachian properties are valuable because of their proximity to Eastern U.S. markets, among other things. "Producers in the region will be in a good position" as reserves in other areas are depleted, said Bernard Picchi, an analyst with Salomon Brothers Inc. in New York.
