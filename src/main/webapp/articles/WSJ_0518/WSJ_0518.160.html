International Brief -- Royal Dutch/Shell Group: Net Income for 1st Quarter Fell 36% to $1.37 Billion

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/18/90

Royal Dutch/Shell Group said first-quarter net income dropped 36% to #816 million ($1.37 billion) from #1.28 billion a year earlier, within analysts' expectations on a historic-cost basis, accounting for changes in values of inventories. On a current-cost basis, valuing the company's oil inventories at current market prices, profit fell 26% to #826 million from #1.11 billion. Operating profit edged up 2.8% to #1.48 billion from #1.44 billion.

Earnings per share for Royal Dutch Petroleum Co., the Dutch arm, fell 45% to 3.03 guilders ($1.64), and for Shell Transport & Trading Co., the British segment, dropped 36% to 9.1 pence a share.

Sales rose 22% to #17.5 billion from #14.3 billion a year earlier. Excluding taxes and excise duties, sales rose 24% to #14.2 billion from #11.4 billion.

Shell's exploration and production operations surged 73% to #508 million. Refining and marketing lines fell 15% to #290 million, hurt by a #14 million loss in the U.S. from higher raw material costs. In the 1989 first quarter, Shell had a #57 million gain in the U.S. Chemical profit plummeted 56% to #159 million; U.S. chemical sales fell. Coal and metals earnings were flat.
