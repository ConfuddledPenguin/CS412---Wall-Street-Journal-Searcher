International: France's Bull Denies Press Report of Spying Against U.S. Firms

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/18/90

PARIS -- French computer maker Cie. des Machines Bull denied a French press report alleging that France's intelligence service carried out industrial espionage against International Business Machines Corp., Texas Instruments Inc. and other U.S. technology companies. The report, in the news magazine L'Express, said the French foreign espionage service, the Direction Generale de la Securite Exterieure, recruited senior officials at the U.S. companies' French subsidiaries. The article claimed that senior French government officials were worried that, without the espionage, Bull would have trouble catching up with the competition.

It said the theft of research and marketing secrets began around 1987 and ended late in 1989 when the U.S. Federal Bureau of Investigation and Central Intelligence Agency confronted the spies. The executives in question were fired, the magazine said, and the news covered up to avoid scandal.

Spokesmen for IBM in Armonk, N.Y., and for Texas Instruments in Dallas declined to comment, as did the French government. The head of security at Texas Instruments' Nice headquarters said he wasn't aware of any espionage. He said that there is a regular turnover in management officials at Texas Instruments' French subsidiary but that he isn't aware of anyone being fired for stealing secrets.

A Bull official said the company posted a loss of 267 million francs ($48.1 million) last year, compared with profit of 303 million francs in 1988. But the official said Bull is on the mend, is advancing through an aggressive acquisition strategy and operates entirely independently of the French government.

"We formally deny this report," said the Bull official. "We haven't any relationship with the DGSE."
