Marketing Brief -- Lear's Publishing Co.: President Balson Dismissed; Magazine Partners Sought

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/18/90

John Balson, president and chief executive officer of Lear's Publishing Co., was dismissed by Frances Lear, the company's founder and editor in chief of Lear's magazine. A spokesman for Ms. Lear gave no explanation for the change, except that Ms. Lear and Mr. Balson had "mutually agreed to end their association." Mr. Balson, 48 years old, had been Lear's top executive for a year. He couldn't be reached for comment.

In a related development, Ms. Lear, through the spokesman, said she is looking for partners for the magazine company. Lear Publishing has explored "the possibility of enlarging the scope of its magazine by seeking partners within the industry and becoming part of a larger group of magazines," the spokesman said.

Ms. Lear said no replacement will be named for Mr. Balson and she will reassume the duties of chief executive.

Lear's, a monthly magazine for women over 40 years old, has grown quickly since being launched in 1988. Paid circulation has grown to more than 450,000.
