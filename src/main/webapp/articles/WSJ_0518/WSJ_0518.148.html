Business Brief -- Nikon Corp.: Unconsolidated Pretax Net Increased 8.7% in Fiscal 1989

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/18/90

Nikon Corp., the big Tokyo-based manufacturer of cameras and other optical instruments, said unconsolidated pretax earnings in fiscal 1989, ended March 31, grew 8.7% to 20.28 billion yen from 18.66 billion yen in fiscal 1988. Net profit jumped 59% to 10.87 billion yen, or 29.63 yen a share, from 6.86 billion yen, or 18.86 yen a share.

Sales rose 10% to 241.12 billion yen from 218.95 billion yen.

Nikon officials said healthy personal consumption helped push up sales of cameras, binoculars and certain other products.

Net surged as the company posted 1.18 billion yen in extraordinary gains from the sales of corporate assets, compared with 992 million yen in such profits in the previous fiscal year. Extraordinary losses, including those from sales of assets and foreign-exchange losses involving long-term foreign currency loans, shrank to 497 million yen from 1 billion yen and also helped improve the financial balance.
