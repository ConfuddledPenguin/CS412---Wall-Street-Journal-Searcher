International: Amro Is Planning to Lift Its Stake in EAB to 66.3%

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/18/90

AMSTERDAM -- Amsterdam-Rotterdam Bank NV, in an announcement that was expected, said it is set to expand its stake in European American Bancorp of the U.S. to 66.3%. The Dutch group confirmed it is in advanced negotiations to acquire the 23.15% stake in EAB now held by Generale de Banque of Belgium.

Amro holds 20% of EAB and is in the process of acquiring a 23.15% stake from Deutsche Bank of West Germany.

EAB owns a general bank with 88 offices in the New York City-Long Island region and had a loss of about $80 million in the first quarter. Factors blamed for the first-quarter loss were the substantial provisions EAB had to make for risks related to developments in the real estate market. In 1989, EAB had profit of $61 million.

Amro said the acquisition of a majority interest would enable it to enhance decision-making processes at EAB.

Amro is seeking approval from the U.S. for its planned share acquisitions. If effected, EAB will be 66.3% held by Amro with 23.45% owned by Societe Generale of France and 10.25% held by Creditanstalt Bankverein of Austria.
