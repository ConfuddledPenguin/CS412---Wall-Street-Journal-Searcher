Business Brief -- Henkel KGaA: Four-Month Results Rose; Gains for Year Are Forecast

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/18/90

Henkel KGaA, reporting a 10% rise in four-month sales and even higher earnings growth, said sales and profit will also rise for all of 1990. The West German chemicals company, whose 1989 net jumped 15% to 404 million marks ($246 million) from 352 million marks, didn't give any more details on results of the first four months. "All in all, the new year started positively for Henkel," Chairman Helmut Sihler said at a news conference in Duesseldorf.

For all of 1990, Henkel said sales should rise to 12 billion marks from 11.64 billion in 1989, when sales jumped 14%.
