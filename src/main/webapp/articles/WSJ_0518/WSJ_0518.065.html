Marketing & Media: Prime-Time Game Shows To Make Comeback on ABC

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/18/90

NEW YORK -- Prime time network television is rediscovering the format that dotted its landscape in its infancy: the game show. Capital Cities/ABC Inc.'s TV network is scheduling two half-hour game shows, "Super Jeopardy]" and "Monopoly," to run Saturdays at 8 p.m. and 8:30 p.m. beginning June 16.

The shows are produced by Merv Griffin Enterprises, a division of Columbia Pictures Entertainment, in association with King World Productions Inc. King World, the most successful distributor of syndicated shows -- including "Wheel of Fortune," "Jeopardy]" and "The Oprah Winfrey Show" -- has been trying to launch "Monopoly" in syndication for three years, but so far hasn't been successful in syndication distribution.

King World said it has a 13-week commitment from ABC for both shows; it is possible that the shows could be renewed for the 1990-91 TV season.

Stephen Palley, chief operating officer at King World, said, "We have been beating the drums for a long time that game shows are great programming." ABC, said one King World official, first approached the distributor about filling a Saturday programming void. ABC declined to comment.

This is the first time that game shows have appeared in prime time since 1970, when ABC aired two Saturday series, "Let's Make a Deal" and "The Newlywed Game."
