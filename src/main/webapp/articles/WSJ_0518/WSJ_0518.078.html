Money-Fund Assets Declined $1.78 Billion For the Latest Week

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/18/90

NEW YORK -- Assets of the nation's 507 money-market funds fell $1.78 billion to $381.71 billion in the week ended Wednesday, according to the Investment Company Institute, a Washington trade association. Assets of 245 general-purpose funds fell $640.3 million to $131.53 billion; assets of 102 broker-dealer funds fell $1.41 billion to $158.43 billion; and assets of 160 institutional funds rose $270.1 million to $91.75 billion.

"Yields on money-market funds inched up, and the yield differential favoring money-market funds over alternative investment vehicles available to individuals widened by a few" hundredths of a percentage point, said Jacob Dreyer, vice president and chief economist for the institute. "And yet assets in individual accounts declined again as individuals have drawn on their money-market-fund balances to pay for stepped-up purchases of equities and equity-fund shares as well as for Treasury bills and notes bought at last week's auction."

The latest drop was the eighth-consecutive week of decline for money-market-fund assets. The total drop for the eight weeks was about $10.5 billion, and "I think there are a couple of pretty good reasons," Mr. Dreyer said. "Until recently, you had a slight creep-up of {certificate-of-deposit} rates, which means individuals were shifting money," he said.

During the eight-week period, "institutional balances barely changed," he said. "The whole bloody thing happened in broker-dealer and general-purpose funds," which are geared to individuals.

The second reason Mr. Dreyer cited was tax season, when "people like me who owed money to the {Internal Revenue Service} paid at the last moment." And third, "in the past couple of weeks, we've had an excellent stock market, and people are dabbling" in stocks and stock funds.

Mr. Dreyer said that in the first three weeks of January, assets rose a total $15.5 billion, "so this is not the end of the world."
