Business Brief -- Hawker Siddeley Group PLC: Engineering Concern Buys Eaton Technologies of U.S.

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/18/90

Hawker Siddeley Group PLC, a British engineering concern, bought Eaton Technologies of the U.S. for 38 million pounds ($63.8 million), the company said yesterday. The purchase of the Michigan-based electric-motors manufacturer is the first major acquisition made by London-based Hawker Siddeley on behalf of its International Electric Motors Division, a unit resulting from a restructuring of its divisions, the company said.

The company earlier this year paid 7 million pounds for the electric-motors division of Electrohome Ltd., a North American motors manufacturer, and less than 10 million pounds for Bull Electric Ltd, a U.K. elevator and lift motors maker. The exact price paid wasn't disclosed.

A company spokesman said the acquisition of Eaton "adds to Hawker Siddeley's leading presence in the U.S." in electric motors.
