GM Says It Will Idle Facility in Georgia

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/18/90

DETROIT -- General Motors Corp. said it will indefinitely idle its Lakewood, Ga., assembly plant beginning Aug. 6 because of slow sales of the 1990-model Chevrolet Caprice full-sized car and the Buick Estate Wagon station wagon. About 2,200 workers will be affected. The No. 1 auto maker had announced its plans to idle the Lakewood plant in September, but didn't specify then when the closing would take place.

GM said it has scheduled daily and weekend overtime at its Janesville, Wis.; Lordstown, Ohio; Orion, Mich., and Lansing, Mich., assembly facilities.

Ford Motor Co. has planned overtime at its Wixom, Mich.; Kansas City and St. Louis, Mo.; Twin Cities, Minn., and Louisville, Ky., assembly plants.

Overall, the 10 major U.S. car makers planned to build 138,967 this week, down 11.2% from 156,524 a year ago.
