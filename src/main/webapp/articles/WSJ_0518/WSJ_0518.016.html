U.K. Public Sector Borrowing

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/18/90

LONDON -- The public sector borrowing requirement, the most widely used measure of Britain's government deficit or surplus, showed a deficit of #2.12 billion ($3.56 billion) in April compared with a deficit of #1.73 billion in March and a surplus of #890 million in April 1989, the British Treasury said. Market expectations had been for a deficit of about #1.25 billion. The government is projecting a surplus for the year ending March 31, 1991, of #7 billion.
