Malaysia's Coal Reserves

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/18/90

KUALA LUMPUR, Malaysia -- Malaysia's coal reserves of about 770 million tons, mostly located in Sarawak in Borneo island, should be tapped to meet energy demands, an official at the Ministry of Energy said. The Secretary-General of the Ministry of Energy, Telecommunications and Posts, Helmi Mohamad Noor, said that until now, Malaysia was not a significant consumer of coal, and effort should be made to tap and use this resource.

He said, "The cement industry began converting to coal in the early 1980s, and by 1987, imported coal and coke contributed 3.3 % to the final energy mix. However, it was reduced to 1.8% in 1988 due to cement plants reverting to cheaper fuel oil."

Mr. Noor said the development of domestic coal brought benefits in the form of support for regional development and the creation of employment.
