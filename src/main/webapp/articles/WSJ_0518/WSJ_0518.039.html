Group's Tender Offer For Debt of Allegheny International Expires

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/18/90

PROVIDENCE, R.I. -- Japonica Partners, a New York investor group trying to take over Allegheny International Inc., said it's cash tender offer for debentures of the troubled consumer products concern expired yesterday. Japonica said it accepted for payment all of Allegheny's 9% subordinated sinking-fund debentures due 1989 at a price of 39.8% of their principal amount; the 10 3/4% subordinated sinking-fund debentures due 1999 at 41% of their principal amount; the 10.4% subordinated sinking-fund debentures due 2002 at 27.7% of their principal amount, and the 9% sinking-fund debentures due 1994 of Allegheny's Chemetron unit at 83.8% of their principal amount that were tendered.

Based on a preliminary count, Japonica said the following were tendered; $6.7 million principal amount of the 9% subordinated sinking-fund debentures due 1989, representing 56% of such debentures outstanding; $47.7 million principal amount of the 10 3/4% subordinated sinking-fund debentures due 1999, representing 72% of such debentures outstanding; $15.1 million principal amount of the 10.4% subordinated sinking-fund debentures due 2002, representing 39% of such debentures outstanding; and $6 million principal amount of Chemetron's 9% sinking fund debentures due 1994, representing 36% of such debentures outstanding.

Allegheny and several subsidiaries, including Chemetron, have been operating under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code.
