Business Brief -- CoreStates Financial Corp.: Closing of 29 Branch Offices Planned by End of the Year

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/18/90

CoreStates Financial Corp. said it will close 29 branch offices by year's end as part of its consolidation effort after acquiring First Pennsylvania Bank. The Philadelphia company said most of the consolidations would occur in the late fall. The plan envisions a network of approximately 116 branches, operating under the First Pennyslvania name, serving retail and commercial customers.

Through consolidation of the two banks' operations, CoreStates said about 1,200 jobs will be eliminated this year.

Reiterating its promise that there would be no layoffs as a result of the consolidation, the bank said most employees have been placed in other jobs inside the company and 139 employees have been placed in an internal transitional program awaiting new assignments. Of these 139 employees, 34 have accepted severance packages, 23 have already been placed in new jobs at the company and three have taken a leave of absence.
