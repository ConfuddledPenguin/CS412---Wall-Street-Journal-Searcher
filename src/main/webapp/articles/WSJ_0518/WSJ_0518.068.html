Retiring Isn't Driving Issue For Chrysler Chief Iacocca

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/18/90

LOS ANGELES -- Chrysler Corp. Chairman Lee A. Iacocca said he hasn't "made up my mind yet" about exactly when he will retire from the No. 3 U.S. auto maker. But he told shareholders at the annual meeting here yesterday that his contract with the Highland Park, Mich., company expires at the end of 1991, and that sticking to the contract is "the plan I have at the moment."

Mr. Iacocca turned 65 years old last year, but agreed in 1987, when he signed his current employment contract, to stay on as chairman and chief executive officer beyond that age.

In answer to a shareholder's question, Mr. Iacocca said Chrysler doesn't anticipate sweetening pension payments for its executives. General Motors Corp. recently proposed changes in its plan that would nearly double pensions for 3,350 executives, including Chairman Roger B. Smith, who is to retire this summer. "We do not intend to follow GM," Mr. Iacocca said.

Replying to another question, Mr. Iacocca said he is "not interested" in a leveraged buy-out and "I don't think our board's interested in it." Rumors of such a move have circulated periodically in the past few years.

Mr. Iacocca also said Chrysler doesn't plan to cut its quarterly dividend, despite a widespread cost-cutting program at the company. Chrysler's current annual payout rate is $1.20 a share, which Mr. Iacocca said translates into about a 7.1% return. Chrysler shares closed yesterday at $16.50, down 37.5 cents, in New York Stock Exchange composite trading. "Barring a disaster," he said, Chrysler doesn't "intend to touch the dividend."
