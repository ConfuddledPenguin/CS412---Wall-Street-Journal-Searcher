Belgian Banks Ask Reuters To Limit Bond-Data Access

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/18/90

BRUSSELS -- In a battle over how the market for Belgian government bonds should be structured, major banks have ordered Reuters Ltd. to cut off access of Belgian stockbrokers to electronic pages quoting their local bond prices. Reuters -- a unit of Reuters Holdings PLC -- and several banks confirmed yesterday that the measure would be effective today, the day Belgium's stock market is scheduled to begin a new, automated bond trading system available only to stockbrokers.

The system, known in shorthand as Obli-CATS, would enable stockbrokers to trade bonds on the bourse's computerized trading network.

Banks are protesting the new system. A handful of commercial banks conduct the bulk of trading in Belgian government bonds in off-bourse transactions. Several act effectively as market-makers for the bonds.

Under current Belgian law, however, the banks aren't allowed to participate directly in the Belgian stock market.

A spokesman for Reuters, meanwhile, said the company "regrets this situation, which has arisen from a dispute among customers."

The spokesman said the Reuters was implementing the banks' requests by putting Belgian stockbrokers on "selective access" to Reuters monitors.
