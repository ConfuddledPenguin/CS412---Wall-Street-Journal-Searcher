Letters to the Editor: Vengeance Is Rightfully Ours

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/18/90

In your April 25 article on Michael Milken ("Reactions to Milken: Villain or Victim?"), lawyer Joe Jamail explained that he would be troubled by a stiff prison sentence that punished "for vengeance's sake." As a law student, I do not wish to presume more knowledge than a practicing lawyer. However, my understanding of modern criminal law is that we do punish for punishment's sake. The notion of rehabilitation, while noble, has long since been abandoned as the primary goal of the U.S. punishment system. In this light, Mr. Milken clearly deserves more than a financial slap on the wrist. Barry M. Freiman

Certified Public Accountant

Law Student

Washington College of Law

American University

Chevy Chase, Md.

---

I predict that someday -- when laws that make it a crime to succeed at business are gone -- Michael Milken will be a hero in the history books as the most spectacularly innocent victim of a witch hunt.

Cynthia Gillis

Brooklyn, N.Y.

---

A few reactions to the "Reactions:"

For Charles H. Keating Jr., executive: Of course "you don't put Michelangelo or da Vinci in jail." But Michael was no Michelangelo, and you're no da Vinci. Maybe you and Michael can be cellmates.

For Rabbi Arnold Wolf, who commented, "He should be hung by his thumbs for years and years and years. He's a very terrible person." Come on, Rabbi -- don't hold back, tell us what you really think.

For Stephen A. Wynn, CEO, Golden Nugget, who says Michael doesn't belong in prison: You roll the dice and take your chances.

For Rep. John Dingell, who questions the settlement: The Milken crimes pale in comparison with the fraud committed by the U.S. Congress on the American public in cooking the government's books each year. Our children and grandchildren should have us all put away.

For The Wall Street Journal: I missed your reaction, or perhaps you were too busy wiping the egg off your faces with your old editorials.

Gary A. Cutson

Marietta, Ga.

---

As always, history as written will be what the historian says it is. It is to be hoped that the reason of gentlemen like H. Ross Perot will be the loudest -- "Don't govern your life by what's legal or illegal, govern it by what's right or wrong." Simple but clear words that can be used as a guide to live by.

With thoughtfulness and honesty, history will, I hope, expose the rationalization of gentlemen such as Mr. Keating as what it truly is -- pure and unadulterated bunk. To suggest that the monkeys be put in charge of the peanuts is to deny the reality of the situation. Mr. Milken did not cop a plea because he is faultless. For his crimes he must be held accountable. After he pays his debt to society he very well may be able to contribute to worthwhile causes. To cast him as a victim, suggest he be placed in a position of public trust and place him in the category of Michelangelo and da Vinci is at best weak and self-serving.

Scott F. Guertin

Weymouth, Mass.
