Business Brief -- Lomas & Nettleton Mortgage: Accords Set With Holders Of $370 Million in Notes

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/18/90

Lomas & Nettleton Mortgage Investors said it reached new agreements with holders of $370 million in notes that call for it to begin payments on the debt on July 31 while extending the maturities to early 1993 from 1991. The Dallas company also said it plans to skip its August dividend payment, but expects to resume regular quarterly dividends in November. The company, which pays a dividend based on its earnings, already has declared a 21 cents-a-share quarterly dividend to be paid May 25 to stock of record last Monday.

The mortgage real estate investment trust said that the new debt agreements allow it to synchronize debt-amortization payments with its expected cash flows from its investment portfolio.

It said that $220 million of notes owed to institutional investors were to come due in full on Feb. 1, 1991, and the remaining $150 million of revolving notes matured on Oct. 25, 1991. Under the new agreements, both issues of notes will mature on Jan. 31, 1993.
