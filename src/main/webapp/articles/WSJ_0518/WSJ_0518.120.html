Former Pittsburgh Lawyer Is Indicted on 26 Counts

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/18/90

PITTSBURGH -- Charles M. Thorp III, a former lawyer who had losses of upward of $20 million on the options market, including funds he allegedly misappropriated from clients, was indicted on 26 counts by a federal grand jury here. The U.S. attorney's office here said Mr. Thorp, 49 years old, was indicted on charges of transporting stolen funds in interstate commerce and bank fraud. Among other things, Mr. Thorp was charged with transporting about $2.6 million of stolen money between Pittsburgh and Boston or New York between April 1987 and May 1989.

Mr. Thorp also is accused of fraudulently obtaining a $400,000 loan from Pittsburgh National Bank by using the name and credit of a client in April 1987. Pittsburgh National is a unit of PNC Financial Corp.

Mr. Thorp previously surrendered his license to practice law.

Neither Mr. Thorp nor his attorney could be reached for comment. A spokeswoman at the U.S. attorney's office here said a summons has been issued for his arrest.

Mr. Thorp, grandson of the founder of one of this city's law firms, was the subject of a front-page story last month in this paper.

He faces a maximum sentence of as much as 255 years in prison and fines totaling $6.5 million, or both.
