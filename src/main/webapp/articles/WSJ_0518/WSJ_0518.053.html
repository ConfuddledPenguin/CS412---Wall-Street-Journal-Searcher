Corporate Bankruptcies In Japan Fell 22% in April

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 05/18/90

TOKYO -- The number of corporate bankruptcies in Japan fell to 525 in April, down 22% from 677 the year before, a private credit research agency reported Thursday. But because of a substantial retrenchment in Tokyo stock prices since the beginning of the year, the failed companies posted heavy losses in equity holdings, bringing the total value of their liabilities in April to 211.23 billion yen ($1.39 billion), more than double the 84.95 billion yen of last year, Teikoku Data Bank Ltd. reported.

According to the agency, April's decline in corporate bankruptcies marks the 64th consecutive month of year-on-year declines. Moreover, it said the sharp rise in the value of liabilities marks the highest level this year and the first time in nine months that the total has risen above 200 billion yen.

The number of bankruptcies was up 4.4% from 503 in March, with the value of liabilities close to triple the 73.2 billion yen of March.

The number of bankruptcies due to losses on capital assets, including stock and land holdings, jumped to 12 from eight in March and only one in both January and February. Meanwhile, the liabilities involved totaled 118.09 billion yen, compared with 6.4 billion yen in March.
