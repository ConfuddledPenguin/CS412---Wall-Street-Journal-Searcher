Siemens Signs Agreement With East German Concern

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 05/18/90

FRANKFURT, West Germany -- Siemens AG said it has signed a letter of intent to cooperate with Starkstrom-Anlagenbau-Rostock, an East German power-technology company, in the field of electricity distribution. The West German electronics group said SAR has a strong presence in the northern parts of East Germany. Last week, Siemens signed virtually the same accord with Starkstrom-Anlagenbau Leipzig-Halle, a power technology group in southern East Germany.

"If these cooperations are realized, Siemens will receive a full market presence for energy transfer and distribution," Siemens said in a news release.

SAR has around 1,200 employees.

Since the beginning of the year, Siemens has signed a series of preliminary cooperation agreements with East German partners covering telecommunications, power engineering and other segments of electrical engineering.
