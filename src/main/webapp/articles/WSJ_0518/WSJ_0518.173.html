Brady Picks Powell To Be Treasury Aide For Domestic Finance

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/18/90

WASHINGTON -- Treasury Secretary Nicholas Brady has chosen Jay Powell, an executive at Mr. Brady's former firm of Dillon, Read & Co., to be assistant Treasury secretary for domestic finance. The choice has not yet been approved by the White House and is subject to Senate confirmation. But if Mr. Powell passes those hurdles, he will succeed David Mullins, who was recently confirmed by the Senate as a member of the Federal Reserve's Board of Governors.

Mr. Powell was trained as a lawyer and specializes in securities law. In recent years, he has worked in corporate finance at Dillon Read, where he became acquainted with Mr. Brady.

In the new post, Mr. Powell will oversee several critical projects under way at the Treasury. He will be in charge of a Treasury review of financial institutions and the deposit insurance system. He also will supervise a Treasury project on corporate governance, which is looking at ways to encourage more long-term thinking on the part of corporations and investors. And he will be responsible for the Treasury's debt operations.
