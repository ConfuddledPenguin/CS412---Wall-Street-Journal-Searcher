Asean Trade With Australia

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/18/90

SINGAPORE -- The Association of Southeast Asian Nations (Asean) wants more trade and investment from Australia, Acting Foreign Minister Peter Sung said yesterday. Trade between Australia and Asean has risen to 6.7 billion Australian dollars (US$5.1 billion) in 1988-89 against A$2.9 billion in 1980-81.

However, Australia's trade with the U.S., Japan, Europe, South Korea, Taiwan, and Hong Kong has grown much more than with Asean, he said but did not give figures.

Mr. Sung said, "Asean's exports to Australia have continuously faced stringent import regulations such as import licensing; labeling, packing, health, and safety standards; quarantine restrictions; and antidumping and countervailing action."

Singapore, Brunei, Malaysia, Indonesia, the Philippines, and Thailand are members of Asean.
