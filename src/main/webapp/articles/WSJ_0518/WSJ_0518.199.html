New Securities Issues

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/18/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATES

Dow Capital -- $150 million of debentures, due May 15, 2010, priced as 9% securities at 99.87 to yield 9.032%. The noncallable debentures, which may be put back to the company on May 15, 1995, were priced at a spread of .42 percentage point above the Treasury's when-issued five-year note. Rated single-A-1 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and single-A by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Goldman Sachs & Co.

Asian Development Bank -- $300 million issue of notes, due June 1, 2000, priced as 9 1/8% securities at 99.195 to yield 9.25%. The noncallable notes were priced at a spread of .56 percentage point above the Treasury's 10-year note. The issue, rated triple-A by both Moody's and S&P, will be sold through underwriters led by Shearson Lehman Hutton Inc.

MUNICIPALS

Nassau County, N.Y. -- $85.1 million of various purpose revenue bonds, due July 1, 1991-2009, apparently were won by a Prudential-Bache Capital Funding group with a bid setting the net interest cost at 6.99%. The bonds, insured and rated triple-A by Moody's and S&P, were priced for reoffering to yield from 6.00% in 1991 to 7.10% in 2008 and 2009. The issue consists of $71.5 million of general improvement bonds and $13.6 million of sewer district bonds. There are no term bonds.

MORTGAGE-BACKED

Goldman, Sachs & Co. -- two separate $300 million issues of real estate mortgage investment conduit securities backed by Government National Mortgage Association 9 1/2% securities. The offerings are being made through GS Trust 8A and GS Trust 8B. Details on yields weren't immediately available.

Veterans Administration -- $348.7 million of mortgage securities backed by its Vendee mortgages through an underwriting group headed by First Boston Corp. The offering is being made through American Housing Trust VI. Vendee loans are made by the VA to buyers of homes taken over by the VA after defaults on VA-guaranteed mortgages. This issue is backed by the VA, whose guarantee is supported by the U.S. government. For classes with details available, the securities yield as much as 10.113%, or 1.45 percentage point over the 30-year Treasury, for a security with an average life of 20.5 years. The collateral consists of Vendee loans with a weighted average coupon rate of 9.53% and weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29 years. The issue is rated triple-A by Moody's and S&P.

EUROBONDS

Finnish Export Credit (Finland) -- #200 million ($338.6 million) of 16% bonds due Dec. 2, 1991, priced at 101 7/8, via Merrill Lynch International.

KFW International Finance Inc. (West Germany) -- $300 million issue of 9 1/4% bonds due June 12, 1995, at a fixed reoffer price of 99 5/8 to yield 9.35% reoffered, via Deutsche Bank Capital Markets. Guaranteed by Kreditanstalt fuer Wiederaufbau.

World Bank (agency) -- 125 million European currency units of 10 3/8% bonds due June 27, 1995, priced at 101.55, to yield 10.43% less fees, via Credit Commercial de France. Fees 1 3/4.

Nationwide Anglia Building Society (United Kingdom) -- #100 million of floating-rate notes due June 1995, priced at 100 and paying interest at 0.10 point above the three-month London interbank offered rate, via Baring Brothers.

Council of Europe (agency) -- 150 million Swiss francs ($106.8 million) of 7 1/4% public bonds due June 18, 2000, priced at 101, via Banca del Gottardo. Callable from June 18, 1998, at 101.
