Japan Equipment Investment

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/18/90

TOKYO -- Despite recent worsening investment conditions, including higher interest rates and falling stock prices, many Japanese companies plan to spend more money in fiscal 1990 in upgrading production and other equipment, a government advisory panel said. In a report submitted to Minister of International Trade and Industry Kabun Muto, a subcommittee of the Industrial Structure Council said its survey of 1,466 companies conducted between February and March showed equipment investment will climb 13.1% in fiscal 1990 that began in April from the previous fiscal year to 17.715 trillion yen ($116.8 billion).

Financial institutions as well as insurance and communications companies were excluded from the survey.
