West Coast Video Sues MCA for Alleged Breach of Agreement

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/18/90

LOS ANGELES -- West Coast Video Enterprises Inc. has sued MCA Inc. and its subsidiary Universal City Studios for allegedly breaching an exclusive promotion agreement, after MCA's "secret" talks with West Coast Video competitor Blockbuster Entertainment Corp. The complaint, filed in state court, says the exclusive nature of the promotion, set to coincide with the opening of MCA's Universal Studios Florida in Orlando next month, was particularly important to Philadelphia-based West Coast Video because rival Blockbuster is based in Ft. Lauderdale, Florida. The promotion agreement would have given West Coast Video a marketing presence in Florida.

The promotion was to have featured game cards given to video store patrons -- with prizes, including trips to Orlando and free video rentals -- West Coast Video said.

After allegedly disavowing its agreement with West Coast Video last December, MCA this spring announced a "Blockbuster Video $10 Million Game" with Blockbuster.

West Coast is seeking at least $1 million in damages and an injunction barring any promotional campaign coinciding with the opening of the theme park. Blockbuster is not named as a defendant.

In Universal City, Calif., MCA general counsel Robert Hadl said the company hadn't yet been served with the suit, and therefore, he couldn't comment.
