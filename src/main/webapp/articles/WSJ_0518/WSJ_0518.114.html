REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: Supplemental Supper

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/18/90

While the White House and congressional representatives dance around something called the "budget summit," a real summit over real money is taking place on Capitol Hill. We know this one will cut a deal because Members of Congress are the only ones doing the dealing. It's a conference committee, which is working out the details on supplemental appropriations for the current fiscal year; the Senate's version of it reached the gassy level of $3.3 billion. In other words, Congress and various executive-branch collaborators have discovered that last year's budget wasn't enough; they've got to spend billions more. As we noted in these columns recently, a big item on the agenda is the fact that House Members have already run through the millions they appropriated last year to spend on their mailings to constituents. Now a few more details have emerged. One reason the franking account will go about $44 million into the red is that many Members plan to send out their propaganda near the fall elections using first-class postage. They are "forced" to do this because many of them have already used up their quota of 10-cent, third-class mailings.

A House Member at the conference (which is also deciding on a cornucopia of pork-barrel requests) told the Senators that the House is going to undertake "reforms" of the franked-mail system.

We suppose it would be considered utopian to suggest that one reform might be to build a kind of rehabilitation clinic somewhere in Washington for compulsive spenders. Absent that, voters can at least consider what is behind the fact that come fall, their mailboxes will be filling up with first-class junk mail.
