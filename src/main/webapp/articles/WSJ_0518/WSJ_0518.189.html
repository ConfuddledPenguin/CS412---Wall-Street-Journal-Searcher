Kay Jewelers Says Some Officers to Buy More of Its Common

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/18/90

ALEXANDRIA, Va. -- Kay Jewelers Inc. said certain officers and directors, who together own 40% of Kay's common stock, intend to buy up to one million more shares on the open market or through negotiated transactions. The move, a strong show of support at a time when the jewelry retailers' stock has dropped significantly, will help to fend off a pack of short sellers. Kay has about 12.3 million shares outstanding.

At the company's annual meeting, Chairman Anthonie van Ekris blasted the short sellers "a bad bunch of people," who have had a "devastating impact" on Kay stock. Short sellers count on a company's stock dropping by selling borrowed stock and replacing it with stock purchased at a lower price. Kay's stock has tumbled from a 52-week high of $24.375 a share, to close yesterday at $7.75 in New York Stock Exchange composite trading.

Mr. van Ekris told shareholders to "keep the faith," and reiterated his expectation that Kay "will return to levels of profitability this year." He didn't offer specific projections on 1990 earnings. For 1989, the company posted net income of $196,000, or two cents a share, on revenue of $427 million.

Earlier this week, Kay disclosed that three banks that provide $32 million of its $86 million in lines of credit want to withdraw from participation. Mr. van Ekris told shareholders he is "fully confident a revolving credit agreement will be in place in a matter of weeks."

Martin Oppenheimer, who follows Kay's bonds for Koonce Securities of Rockville, Md., suggested that management consider an outside review "to make sure their inventory control systems are good as they think they are."
