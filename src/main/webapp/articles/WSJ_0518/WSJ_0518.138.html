California Timber Groups Propose Ballot Measure

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/18/90

SACRAMENTO, Calif. -- California's timber industry, facing two ballot initiatives to restrict clear-cutting, turned in enough signatures to qualify its own initiative for the November ballot. The proposed measure, backed by the Timber Association of California and numerous industry groups, is dubbed the "Global Warming, Clear Cut Reduction, Wildlife Protection and Reforestation Act of 1990." It would limit clear-cutting of virgin redwood forests, reduce clear-cutting in other forests and authorize $300 million in bonds to finance reforestation projects in urban areas.

Enviromentalists quickly ridiculed the proposition as "Big Stump" and charged that it is an industry smokescreen to deflect voter support away from more sweeping reforms proposed by the state's conservation community. Timber industry officials say the most sweeping of the conservationists' initiatives will cost the state and local governments about $420 million annually.

A timber association spokesman said petitions submitted to the California secretary of state office contained about 700,000 signatures, double the number necessary to get on the ballot.
