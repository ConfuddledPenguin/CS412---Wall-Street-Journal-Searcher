Capital Cities/ABC's Burke Takes Chief Executive Post

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/18/90

BRISTOL, Conn. -- Thomas S. Murphy, presiding over his last annual shareholders meeting as chairman and chief executive officer of Capital Cities/ABC Inc., handed over the chief executive duties to his longtime No. 2, Daniel B. Burke, as expected. Capital Cities/ABC's board, meeting privately after an uneventful annual meeting at the company's ESPN sports channel, approved Mr. Murphy's recommendation to give the chief executive title to Mr. Burke, who remains president and chief operating officer. The duo has run the company together for 18 years, and Mr. Murphy will remain chairman and will stay active in the affairs of the ABC Television Network, in particular.

Mr. Burke told shareholders that ABC this year is expected to log the highest sales and profits of any year since the merger four years ago of the former Capital Cities Communications Inc. and the old American Broadcasting Cos. ABC earned about $165 million in pretax profit for 1989.

But ABC, which earlier this year agreed to pay $900 million for four more seasons of "NFL Monday Night Football," may run a loss on the football contract. "We aren't certain we can recover all the costs. It is just too early to say what the financial ramifications will be," Mr. Burke said.
