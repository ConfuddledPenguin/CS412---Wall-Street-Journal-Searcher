Credit Rating: Dayton Hudson Corp. Debt Rating Lowered By S&P and Moody's

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/18/90

NEW YORK -- Two major rating concerns lowered their ratings on about $2 billion of the senior debt of Dayton Hudson Corp. They said the moves reflected the increased financial risk associated with the planned acquisition of the Marshall Field department store chain for $1.04 billion.

Standard & Poor's Corp. downgraded Dayton Hudson's senior unsecured debt to single-A from single-A-plus and removed it from CreditWatch where it was placed April 23.

S&P said the Minneapolis-based retailer's debt to capitalization ratio has edged progressively higher in recent years, a result of share repurchases and debt-financed acquisitions. It said the purchase of Marshall Field, expected to be financed with long-term borrowings, will increase the company's leverage even more.

Moody's Investors Service Inc. lowered the company's senior debt ratings to single-A-3 from single-A-2 and its commercial paper to Prime-2 from Prime-1. Moody's said the downgrading recognizes the "increased debt levels, reduced cash flow coverage, and the challenges of integrating the Marshall Field acquisition into the company's existing department store operations."

A spokesman for Dayton Hudson couldn't be reached for comment.
