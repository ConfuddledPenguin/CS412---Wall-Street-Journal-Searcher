Law: SEC's Sturc to Leave Post; Led Insider-Trading Cases

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/11/90

WASHINGTON -- John Sturc, a Securities and Exchange Commission associate enforcement director who handled the agency's insider-trading cases against Drexel Burnham Lambert Inc., Michael Milken and Ivan Boesky, said he will resign at the end of April. He will join the Washington office of Gibson, Dunn & Crutcher next month.

The eight-year SEC veteran was seen as an important figure in putting together the cases that rocked Wall Street and helped make the SEC an aggressive and feared investigator of insider dealings. Mr. Sturc, a 1975 graduate of the Harvard Law School, also led the SEC's cases against Martin Siegel and Kidder, Peabody & Co.

Mr. Sturc and William McLucas, director of the division of enforcement, said the departure won't hurt the SEC's case against Mr. Milken, the only major case of his that remains open.

"His departure is a tremendous loss to the agency and the division," Mr. McLucas said, "but I don't see it as changing the focus, direction or continuity in that case in particular. The case will continue."

"Bill McLucas is a fantastic lawyer and there are other people working on these cases and seeing them to a conclusion," Mr. Sturc said. "I'm not worried about the Milken case."

Mr. Sturc's resignation comes four months after SEC Chairman Richard Breeden elevated Mr. McLucas, who was also an associate enforcement director, to head the division.
