Xerox Holder Sues Directors for Decisions Involving VMS Stake

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/11/90

STAMFORD, Conn. -- Xerox Corp. said a shareholder filed a civil lawsuit against members of its board. The suit, filed in Connecticut state court, asks that all current and several past Xerox directors pay damages of more than $400 million to the company for decisions they made regarding Xerox's investments in VMS Realty Partners, a Chicago real estate concern.

The suit also asks that the directors indemnify and hold Xerox harmless from any loss or judgment that might result from other lawsuits related to the VMS investments. According to Xerox's 1989 annual report, 12 such class-action suits have been filed against the company in federal court.

Xerox wouldn't comment on the suit.

Xerox said last week that it will take a first-quarter pretax write-off of about $400 million, essentially walking away from its investment in VMS. In February, VMS said it had ceased all but essential payments to creditors because of cash-flow problems.

The shareholder who filed the suit, William Steiner of Great Neck, N.Y., said he had no comment about the action "apart from the fact that it is justified. I really don't want to try it in the papers."
