Who's News: Bristol-Myers Names Koslow President Of a Products Group

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/11/90

NEW YORK -- Bristol-Myers Squibb Co. said it named Marvin H. Koslow president of its North American consumer products group, succeeding Ronald A. Ahrens, who is resigning. The North American consumer products group includes Bristol-Myers Products, Clairol, and Drackett Co. Mr. Koslow had been senior vice president of Bristol-Myers Squibb. A Bristol-Myers Squibb spokesman said the change had "nothing to do" with the merger between Bristol-Myers and Squibb, which occurred in October.

Mr. Koslow, 60 years old, who has worked at Bristol-Myers for 24 years, developed the corporate advertising, market research and media buying functions at the company after supervising the company's broadcast activities at Young & Rubicam, the advertising concern. Mr. Koslow also was involved in corporate communications and has managed other "key functions" in the company, Bristol-Myers said. Mr. Koslow also was a president of the Association of National Advertisers and of the World Federation of Advertisers.

Mr. Ahrens, 50, joined Bristol-Myers as president of its Bristol-Myers Products division in June 1985. He assumed his current responsibilities in June 1988.

Bristol-Myers Squibb is a pharmaceutical, nutritional, consumer products and medical devices concern with 1989 sales totaling $9.19 billion.
