Insolvent Olympic Federal Loses Two Fights in Court

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/11/90

WASHINGTON -- Olympic Federal Savings & Loan, the insolvent Berwyn, Ill., thrift whose earlier court case briefly halted the savings-and-loan industry bailout, lost two court fights to prevent regulators from seizing it. The insolvent thrift, with $1.1 billion in assets, recently won a court ruling that blocked the Office of Thrift Supervision from seizing it. The S&L had argued that the action of the thrift office was illegal, because its former director didn't receive Senate confirmation as required by the Constitution. The federal appeals court here ruled late Monday that the case, on appeal by the government, was moot because Timothy Ryan was sworn in Monday as the new director, following his confirmation by the Senate last week.

Yesterday, the U.S. district court here refused to grant the thrift a new temporary restraining order, based on another argument: that the government hadn't properly considered Olympic's request for financial assistance. But the district court will hear arguments on the merits of Olympic's case at a trial, to be held at an undetermined date.

The thrift office hasn't announced plans for taking over Olympic Federal, but because of deficiencies in its capital, a government seizure appears likely.
