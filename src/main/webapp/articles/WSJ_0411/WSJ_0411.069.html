Cubic Corp. Engineer Acquitted on Charges In False-Records Case

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/11/90

SAN DIEGO -- A Cubic Corp. engineer was acquitted on charges that he took part in falsifying records on a U.S. Army mine-detector contract. Dennis B. Fink, 41 years old, was acquitted in a retrial in federal court here, after a jury last year failed to vote unanimously to acquit or convict him on 21 counts, including conspiracy to defraud the government and submitting false records. He was acquitted on two counts at that time.

William B. Bauder, program manager for the mine-detector program, was acquitted on all counts last August. The two men were indicted by a federal grand jury in March 1988, after an investigation that began in 1984.

In 1988, Cubic, a high-technology concern, paid the federal government about $7.3 million to settle claims that it fraudulently tested the hand-held mine detectors it refurbished for the Navy.
