Business Brief -- Next PLC: British Retailer Had Loss Of $76.4 Million in Year

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/11/90

Next PLC said it had a pretax loss of #46.7 million ($76.4 million) in the year ended Jan. 31, reflecting a downturn in consumer spending and the slower growth of sales compared with retail costs. The pretax loss at the British retailer compares with a year-earlier pretax profit of #62.3 million. Market expectations had called for pretax profit of about #28 million to #32 million. In trading on London's Stock Exchange yesterday, Next dropped 8 pence to 71 pence.

The company said per-share earnings fell to to 8.4 pence from 10.9 pence. The per-share dividend was sliced 36% to 4.7 pence from last year's 7.4-pence payout.

Sales dropped 16% to #949.2 million from #1.14 billion.

Next reported exceptional costs of #73.1 million relating to a reorganization program that involved layoffs in both the Next Retail and Home Shopping operations.

Extraordinary profit was #53.7 million from the sale of various non-core businesses, the company said.

Profit attributable to holders dropped to #27.8 million from #177.5 million, as operating profit slumped to #36 million from #92.8 million.
