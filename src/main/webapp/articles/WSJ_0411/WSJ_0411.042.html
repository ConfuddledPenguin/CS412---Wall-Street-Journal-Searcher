MCI Chairman McGowan Undergoes Chest Surgery

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/11/90

WASHINGTON -- MCI Communications Corp. said its chairman and chief executive officer, William McGowan, successfully underwent exploratory chest surgery that was "completely unrelated" to his 1987 heart transplant operation. The company said the surgery was performed at Presbyterian-University Hospital of Pittsburgh, the same hospital where Mr. McGowan had his heart transplant nearly three years ago. Yesterday's surgery was performed by Dr. Bartley Griffith, a cardiothoracic surgeon, who suggested the operation following a periodic examination of Mr. McGowan. Neither the hospital nor MCI disclosed the purpose of the surgery.

Mr. McGowan, who is expected to remain hospitalized for five to seven days, was in surgery for two hours yesterday morning, an MCI spokeswoman said. A hospital spokesman said Mr. McGowan was in fair condition yesterday afternoon, and had returned to his own room about an hour after surgery. The spokesman said family members indicated the MCI chairman will make a statement today.
