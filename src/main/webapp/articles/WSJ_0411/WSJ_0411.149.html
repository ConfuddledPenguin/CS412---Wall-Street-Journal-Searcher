Business Brief -- SunTrust Banks Inc.: First-Quarter Earnings Rose Despite Widening Problems

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/11/90

SunTrust Banks Inc.'s first-quarter earnings rose to 69 cents a share, or $87.9 million, despite continued growth in its problem assets. The regional banking concern's first-quarter profit, which was in line with most analysts' expectations, compares with the year-earlier $84 million, or 65 cents a share.

Atlanta-based SunTrust, beset by further loan problems in Tennessee and mounting real-estate concern in Florida and Georgia, charged off $29.3 million in bad loans, a 51% jump from the $19.4 million charged off in the 1989 first quarter.

In addition to the charge-offs, the $31 billion-asset company increased its reserve for possible loan losses to $351 million in the period from $342.3 million at year-end 1989. The increase relects a 19% rise in nonperforming assets, which includes $323.8 million in problem loans, to $415.4 million during the latest quarter from $350.4 million at the end of 1989.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, SunTrust closed at $21.75, up 50 cents.
