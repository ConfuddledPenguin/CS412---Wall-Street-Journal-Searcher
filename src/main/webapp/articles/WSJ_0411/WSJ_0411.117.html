TELEDYNE Inc. (Los Angeles) -- Moody's Investors Service Inc. lowered its ratings on about $34 million of debt of Teledyne, reflecting the impact of the planned spinoff of the company's insurance and finance subsidiaries and federal defense budget cutbacks. Moody's lowered the rating on Teledyne's senior debt to Baa-3 from Baa-2, and on subordinated debt to Ba-1 from Baa-3. The rating concern cited Teledyne's "reduced financial measurements" that will result from the planned spinoff of the insurance and finance subsidiaries into a new company called Unitrin Inc. The new ratings also consider "some unpredictability of cash flow from the defense sector because of Department of Defense budgetary constraints," Moody's said. Moody's noted, however, "the broad diversity of Teledyne's manufacturing businesses that should be able to generate sufficient earnings and cash flow to service the higher leverage of Teledyne that remains after the spinoff." In addition to insurance and finance, Teledyne has interests in aviation, electronics, specialty metals and industrial businesses. A Teledyne spokesman said the rating action "really is no surprise." In New York Stock Exchange trading yesterday, Teledyne rose $1.125 a share to close at $71.375.
