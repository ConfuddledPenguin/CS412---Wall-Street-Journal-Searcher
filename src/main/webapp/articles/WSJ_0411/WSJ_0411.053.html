CD Yields at Banks, Brokerage Houses Show Little Change

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/11/90

NEW YORK -- Yields on certificates of deposit at major banks and brokerage firms were largely unchanged in the week ended Monday. Average yields on small-denomination CDs, which typically require minimum deposits of $10,000 or less, rose just slightly, according to Banxquote Money Markets, an information service based here. The average yield on a one-year consumer CD was 7.96%, up from 7.93%, while five-year consumer CDs offered an average 8.17%, compared with 8.15% last week.

The only significant move in the latest week was a jump in the average yield on six-month CDs at California banks to 8.19%, from 7.89%. The change reflects increases in yields for six-month CDs offered by Security Pacific National Bank and Bank of America, which rose to 8.33% and 8.35%, from 7.87% and 7.90%, respectively. This compares with a national average of 7.79%.

These increases are "probably tied to local competitive factors, such as April rollovers and the tax-filing season," said Norberto Mehl, chairman of Banxquote. "It has nothing to due with interest rates in general or other economic trends."

Banxquote usually surveys 18 big banks each Tuesday; the survey was done Monday this week because of the Passover holiday.

Average yields on CDs sold by leading broker-dealers fell slightly, with the exception of one-year CDs, which rose one hundredth of a percentage point, to 8.20%.

Also down slightly were average yields on most large-denomination CDs, which typically require minimum deposits of $90,000 or more.
