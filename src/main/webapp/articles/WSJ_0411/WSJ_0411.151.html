Business Brief -- Newell Co.: Holder of Lancaster Colony May Seek Control of Firm

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/11/90

Newell Co. said it owns a 5.6% stake in Lancaster Colony Corp. and may try to take control of the Columbus, Ohio-based specialty food, glassware and auto accessories company. Newell, a home-products concern in Freeport, Ill., said it purchased the 675,000 shares for $20.47 a share, or $13.8 million.

In a filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission, Newell said it might seek representation on Lancaster Colony's board, propose a merger, or seek control of the company.

Lancaster Colony, makers of products ranging from pasta and salad dressing to car-floor mats and gun racks, said it would fight any takeover attempt.

John B. Gerlach, president of Lancaster Colony, said Newell officials called him Monday to request a meeting. But "we're not the least bit interested in talking," he said.

Son of the company founder, Mr. Gerlach said he's the largest shareholder, owning about 30% of Lancaster Colony's 11.8 million shares outstanding.

Newell and its Anchor Hocking Corp. subsidiary have done business with Lancaster. In 1986, Newell acquired Lancaster's aluminum cookware division.
