Westinghouse Posts 11% Profit Gain For First Quarter

  • Author: Gregory Stricharchuk
  • Date: 04/11/90

PITTSBURGH -- Westinghouse Electric Corp. reported first-quarter net income rose 11% as revenue dropped slightly. The company, with interests in broadcasting, financial services, community development and power generation equipment, said net income was $210 million, or $1.43 a share, compared with $189 million, or $1.30 a share, in the year-ago quarter.

Revenue edged down to $2.86 billion from $2.89 billion.

Analysts said results were in line with expectations.

Westinghouse didn't make a specific earnings projection for the year, but said that substantial increases in orders for electronic systems and power generation equipment "are positive signs."

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Westinghouse closed at $74.75, down 12.5 cents.

In the quarter, the company had a pretax operating profit margin of 10.3%, compared with a pretax operating profit margin of 9.7% a year ago.

The company didn't break out results for its various segments, but said revenue and pretax operating profit at the financial services unit improved substantially because of significantly higher levels of loans outstanding and marketable securities.

Operating profit at the Group W broadcasting unit decreased because of soft advertising sales in the Northeast market. Revenue was about even with the year-earlier quarter, the company said.

Westinghouse said operating profit at its energy and utility systems segment rose because of higher revenue in the power generation business unit.

The company said revenue and operating profit at its industries segment gained slightly from the year-earlier quarter.

Westinghouse said revenue and operating profit improved at its commercial segment because of recent acquisitions in the furniture systems business unit.

Revenue and operating profit also improved at the company's electronic systems segment, the company said.
