Torchmark Proceeds With Seeking Proxies For American General

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/11/90

BIRMINGHAM, Ala. -- Torchmark Corp., despite last week's setback in court, began soliciting proxies in support of its resolution for American General Corp.'s annual meeting seeking the sale of the company. Torchmark said the resolution, for consideration at the May 2 meeting, calls on American General's board promptly to seek a sale or merger of the Houston insurer by hiring an investment banking firm to solicit offers to acquire the company.

Torchmark's resolution also calls for the appointment of a committee of independent directors to evaluate all acquisition proposals.

In Houston, an American General spokesman said the company will make a recommendation to holders about the resolution "in the near future."

In a letter mailed to American General holders, Torchmark's chairman and chief executive officer, R.K. Richey, said that pending approval of the resolution, the insurer plans to submit another bid for American General.

Torchmark officials wouldn't comment further.

The company earlier proposed acquiring the bigger American General, its chief rival, for $50 a share, or $6.4 billion, in cash and stock. That offer, which demanded a response within 48 hours, was subsequently withdrawn upon being rebuffed by American General.

Undeterred, Torchmark then said it planned to solicit proxies for a proposed slate of nominees to American General's board. But American General, arguing that the nominations missed a deadline, last week won a court-ordered injunction to keep Torchmark at bay.
