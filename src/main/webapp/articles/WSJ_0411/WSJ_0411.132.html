Who's News: Fuji Heavy Industries Says Nissan Executive Is Elected President

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/11/90

TOKYO -- Fuji Heavy Industries Ltd. said it will replace its president with an executive from Nissan Motor Co., fueling speculation that the larger company will exert more control over financially strapped Fuji. Fuji, which makes cars under the name Subaru, said Toshihiro Tajima, the 70-year-old president, "would release himself from the duty of president" following the shareholder annual meeting June 28. He will remain as chairman.

The company said the new president will be Isamu Kawai, 67, currently president of Nissan Diesel Motor Co. Nissan holds 4.4% of Fuji, making Nissan Fuji's largest shareholder.

Last month, Fuji said it would post a loss of 23 billion yen ($145.6 million) in the year ended March 31, sharply higher than the early projected loss of three billion yen. It's the company's first loss since its shares were listed on the Tokyo Stock Exchange in 1960.

After yesterday's announcement, Nissan President Yutaka Kume said that "as far as Nissan is concerned, if we were asked to give Fuji Heavy Industries support and assistance, we are willing to proceed in that direction on the basis of discussions once the new organization is in place."
