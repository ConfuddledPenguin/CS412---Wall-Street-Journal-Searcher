Armstrong World To Post `Significant' Rise in 1st Period Net

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/11/90

LANCASTER, Pa. -- Armstrong World Industries Inc. said first-quarter net income will be "significantly ahead" of the 1989 first quarter, when it posted earnings of $37.4 million, or 81 cents a share. The building-products concern said first-quarter earnings from continuing operations will be "comfortably higher" than a year earlier, including a gain of $2.7 million, or six cents a share, from settlement of asbestos-related litigation. In the 1989 quarter, Armstrong's results included a $2.7 million gain from such settlements.

In addition, Armstrong expects to report an unusual gain of $16.5 million, or 40 cents a share, for the latest first quarter from the sale of Georgia woodlands to an undisclosed buyer. The company said that additional proceeds from the sale, expected to exceed the amount already received, will be received in early June, subject to certain conditions.

Armstrong also adopted an accounting change, effective Jan. 1, 1990, that will increase first-quarter earnings by $8 million, or 19 cents a share.

It said sales for the just-ended quarter are expected to be more than 3% higher than the $710 million posted in the 1989 first quarter.

Armstrong said it was commenting on its results because recent projections by some analysts have been too low. Armstrong is the object of a proxy fight and possible hostile takeover bid by the Belzberg family of Canada, which owns about 11.3% of the company's stock.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading, Armstrong shares closed at $34.50, up $1.50.

Corrections & Amplifications

ARMSTRONG WORLD INDUSTRIES Inc. realized a $2.7 million gain in the 1989 first quarter as a result of certain litigation settlements. Sales in the 1989 first quarter were $619 million as recently restated by the company. In yesterday's edition, the gain was incorrectly attributed to the 1990 first quarter and the sales figure was misstated.

(WSJ Apri 12, 1990)
