Business Brief -- CAE Industries Ltd.: Link Flight Unit Awarded U.S. Air Force, Army Jobs

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/11/90

CAE Industries Ltd. said its Link Flight Simulation division, of Binghamton, N.Y., was awarded two contracts that total 51 million Canadian dollars (US$43.9 million) to modify flight simulators for the U.S. Air Force and Army. CAE, an electronic and aviation equipment maker, said a C$32.7 million contract involves modifying and upgrading three F16 flight simulators, which it built for the U.S. Air Force. That project, which includes equipment designed and built at its Houston plant, is scheduled for completion in 1993. A C$18.3 million contract is for the updating of nine Apache helicopter combat-mission simulators made by the company for the U.S. Army. Those modification kits will be developed in Binghamton. Work is scheduled to be completed in February 1992.

CAE purchased Singer Co.'s Link Domestic Simulation & Training System division in 1988 for US$560 million.
