Bank of New England Sale

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/11/90

BOSTON -- Bank of New England Corp. said it signed a definitive agreement to sell its asset-based lending subsidiary, New England Commercial Finance Corp., to National Bank of Canada. Included in the sale are about $665 million in assets, which were sold at a premium, Bank of New England said. The amount of the premium -- the difference between the sales price and the assets' book value -- wasn't disclosed.

National Bank said the assets represent loans to about 125 borrowers. It said that less than 5% of the assets included are loans for leveraged transactions and that none are real-estate loans.

The sale, expected to close by the end of April, is part of Bank of New England's previously announced asset-sales program. Proceeds of the sales are being used to reduce the company's liquidity problems and to improve its capital ratios.

National Bank of Canada, based in Montreal, has assets of 35.9 billion Canadian dollars (US$30.9 billion). Bank of New England has assets of about $25 billion.
