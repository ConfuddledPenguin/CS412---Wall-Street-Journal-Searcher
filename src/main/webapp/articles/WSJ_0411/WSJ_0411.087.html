International: Shipyard Where Solidarity Was Born Is to Go Public

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/11/90

WARSAW (AP) -- The Gdansk shipyard where the Solidarity trade union was born will be sold to workers and the public under Poland's new rules for creating private companies from stateowned enterprises, workers were told. Shipyard manager Hans Szyt read a telegram from Industry Minister Tadeusz Syryjczyk announcing the decision during a rally by 2,000 workers protesting the slow pace of government decisions on the facility's fate.

"Poland should finally understand that we were fighting for everybody, but we {at the shipyard} had nothing out of it," Solidarity leader Lech Walesa said at the rally. "The time of struggle is over. . . . Now we are fighting for ourselves."

Each share will be sold to the public for 10,000 zlotys (about $1.05). Shipyard workers can purchase 20% of the shares at half price, Mr. Syryjczyk said.

Previous discussions on saving the shipyard had focused on a much-publicised offer from Polish-born American heiress Barbara Piasecka-Johnson, but discussions apparently snagged on the workers' refusal to accept the pay and no-strike agreements she required.
