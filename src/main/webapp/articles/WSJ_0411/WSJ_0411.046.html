Apple Bancorp Board Rejects Tender Offer, Arranges Defenses

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/11/90

NEW YORK -- Apple Bancorp, mounting a defense against an unsolicited tender offer, said its directors unanimously rejected the $121.6 million proposal by its largest shareholder and directed managers to seek other possible bids. The thrift holding company said directors found the $38-a-share offer from real-estate investor Stanley Stahl to be against the company's and shareholders' interests. It said directors have been advised by Apple's financial adviser, Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette Securities Corp., that the offer was financially unfair. The company recommended that shareholders reject the offer and not tender their shares to Mr. Stahl, who already owns a 30.6% Apple stake. Apple Bancorp is the parent of Apple Bank for Savings.

The company's shares rose sharply on news of Mr. Stahl's $38-a-share offer late last month, and closed yesterday at $41.875 each, up 12.5 cents, in New York Stock Exchange composite trading. Before the offer, Apple Bancorp shares had been trading around $32 each.

Mr. Stahl couldn't be reached for comment on the rejection.

In further defensive moves, the company said it is filing a lawsuit in federal court in Delaware and bolstering Apple's anti-takeover shareholder-rights plan. Apple said the suit alleges that Mr. Stahl's bid violates federal disclosure requirements and is "materially misleading."

The shareholder-rights plan, which allows holders to acquire additional shares at half price under certain circumstances, was amended to be triggered even if a suitor owned 80% of the shares as a result of a tender offer. Previously, the plan wouldn't have been applicable under such circumstances. Another change allows directors to redeem the rights for a period after a stockholder becomes an "acquiring person" under the plan.

In the wake of the developments, Apple said, it also postponed the annual meeting, which was planned for late May. Apple didn't set a new date.

Apple had assets of $3.8 billion at the end of 1989. It has 35 branches in New York City, Long Island and Westchester County.
