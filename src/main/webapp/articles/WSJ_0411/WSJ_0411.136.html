Quantum Chemical Must Pay Damages, Federal Judge Says

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/11/90

NEW YORK -- A federal judge ordered Quantum Chemical Corp. to pay damages totaling $24.3 million to Delta Holdings Inc. for breach of contract and fraud. Delta sued in 1985 in connection with its purchase of Elkhorn Insurance Co. from Quantum in September 1983. Delta, a New Jersey holding company, paid Quantum $18 million for the Elkhorn subsidiary, which was later renamed Delta America Re Insurance Co.

In federal court in Manhattan, Judge John Keenan said that Elkhorn clearly was insolvent at the time of the purchase by Delta, which is closely held. He said that Elkhorn's books and records understated Elkhorn's liability for loss reserves by a substantial amount and that the understatement of the reserves wasn't disclosed to Delta.

The judge said that Delta had understandably relied on false representations by Quantum about the subsidiary's financial condition.

Judge Keenan ordered that the sale be rescinded. In addition to returning the $18 million purchase price, Quantum also must pay $6.3 million to cover the amount that Delta ended up contributing to the insolvent subsidiary. Quantum, based in New York, also was ordered to pay interest on the damages.

Joseph Dailey, an attorney for Quantum, formerly known as National Distillers & Chemical Corp., said it is likely that his client will appeal the decision.
