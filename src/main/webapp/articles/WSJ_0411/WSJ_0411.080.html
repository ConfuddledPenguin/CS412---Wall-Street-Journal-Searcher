International Paper Says Profit Fell 19% During First Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/11/90

PURCHASE, N.Y. -- International Paper Co. posted a 19% decline in net income for the first quarter despite a 23% rise in sales, blaming the drop on high capital spending and low prices for some products. The maker of paper and pulp products, which declined to forecast 1990 results, may face further pressure this year as it proceeds with a planned $1.1 billion in capital spending to improve plants and equipment this year.

International Paper said first-quarter net fell to $180 million, or $1.65 a share, from $223 million, or $1.96 a share, a year earlier. Sales rose to $3.2 billion from $2.6 billion. The company attributed the decline to lower prices for paper pulps and printing paper that it sells. International Paper also cited a slightly higher tax rate overall because of rates in foreign markets and in some states, and manufacturing delays caused by bad weather and plant construction.

John A. Georges, chairman and chief executive officer, said the most significant first-quarter price declines were for uncoated printing and writing papers. He said that "recent increases in industry capacity led to price reductions even though sales volume remained at healthy levels." Price increases effective April 1 indicate an improved supply-demand balance, the company said.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, International Paper rose 62.5 cents to $52.125.
