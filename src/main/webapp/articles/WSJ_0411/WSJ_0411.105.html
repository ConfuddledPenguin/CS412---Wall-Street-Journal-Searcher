OTC Focus: Market Rises as Regional Banks Rebound; Midlantic Adds 2, U.S. Bancorp Gains 1

  • Author: Anne Newman
  • Date: 04/11/90

The over-the-counter stock market overcame morning early jitters to edge up in light but broad-based trading that heartened veteran investors. The Nasdaq composite index closed up 1.72 at 431.90, or 0.4%. The market opened lower in the first hour, but firmed steadily during the day with support that wasn't confined to one market sector. Technology, biotechnology, environmental and some of the battered regional bank shares all did well.

Advancing shares just outpaced decliners, 959 to 947. Nasdaq volume picked up, with 129,503,900 shares changing hands compared with Monday's 112,366,600.

While some players stayed on the sidelines because of this week's religious holidays, traders said they saw selective buying among bargain hunters.

"The market looks great," said Richard A. Bruno, managing director of OTC trading at PaineWebber. "The tone is good, retail activity is fairly brisk for a week with two holidays and the institutional interest is pretty good."

At Kidder Peabody, senior OTC trader Peter Coolidge said the market strength was "broadly based and not dominated by any one area." Although many investors were "sitting on the fence" because of this week's holidays, he said, "activity was encouraging. Buyers stepped in to take advantage of weakness." Mr. Coolidge and PaineWebber's Mr. Bruno said there was interest in environmental issues. Shearson Lehman Hutton's OTC bank trader Eric Siefert said he saw a rebound in some regional bank shares.

Bargain hunting benefited these regional bank shares, according to Shearson Lehman Hutton's Mr. Siefert. Among the rebounders that climbed on heavy volume were Midlantic, up 2 to 15 7/8, U.S. Bancorp, up 1 to 26 3/8, Dominion Bankshares, up 1/2 to 15 7/8, BayBanks, up 3/4 to 16 and Boatmen's Bancshares, up 5/8 to 30 1/8. Crestar Financial rose 1/8 to 26 3/4.

Environmental shares got a boost from a report in this newspaper about the popularity of funds that invest in companies in volved in cleaning up the environment. Among the gainers for the day were Clean Harbors Inc., up 1/2 to 8 1/2, Sevenson Environmental Services, up 1 1/4 to 13 3/4, and Groundwater Technology, up 1 to 22 3/4.

In the biotechnology arena, the ongoing competition between Xoma and Centocor to get to the market first with a product that treats septic shock sparked activity in both shares. Centocor was due to release an abstact indicating that its product would treat more patients than Xoma's. However, analysts told Dow Jones Professional Investor Report the two studies aren't comparable. Centocor closed down 1 1/4 at 30 1/4, while Xoma closed up 1 3/4 at 18 3/4 on more than one million shares.

Technology stocks remained in favor, with MIPS Computer Systems surging 3 1/8 to close at 23 1/4 and Adobe Systems closing up 1 3/4 at 38 1/2 on strong volume.

Adobe got a fast start off the block, surging more than a point in early trading after Shearson Lehman Hutton analyst David S. Nelson boosted his short-term investment opinion to the firm's top "1" rating.

Mr. Nelson says he thinks investors haven't adequately valued the franchise Adobe has secured with its PostScript technology, a software system that integrates graphics, text and images with printers and electronic terminals. Adobe and other computer companies are expected later this month to unveil a series of products using PostScript, which may include highspeed printing and the use of color, according to Mr. Nelson.

Calling Adobe's technology an industry standard, the Shearson analyst says he thinks the company's shares should trade at a 50% premium to the S&P 500 and an earnings multiple of about 20 times earnings. Based on his estimated 1991 earnings for Adobe of $2.95 a share, Mr. Nelson says he thinks the stock could trade at about $60 a share in the next 12 to 18 months.

On the merger front, Automated Systems nearly doubled in price after Cadence Design Systems announced it will acquire Automated in exchange for stock. Cadence, based in San Jose, Calif., said it will issue one million common shares in exchange for all of the Brookfield, Wis.-based company's shares, at an exchange ratio of 0.3 Cadence shares for each Automated share. Both software companies showed gains, with Automated surging 3 to close at 6 1/4. Cadence closed up 1 1/8 at 24 7/8.

Lancaster Colony leaped more than three points before Nasdaq temporarily halted trading in the stock after Newell Co. said it now has a 5.6% position in the maker of household, automotive and food products and may seek control of it. Lancaster, based in Columbus, Ohio, shot back that it plans to oppose Newell, a Freeport, Ill.-maker of home furnishings. After reopening for trading, Newell closed up 24 7/8, up 1/4.
