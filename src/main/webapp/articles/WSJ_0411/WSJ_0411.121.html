Business Brief -- Alleco Inc.: Appeal of Bond Decision Is Sent to a Lower Court

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/11/90

Alleco Inc., Cheverly, Md., said a federal appeals court sent back to a lower court Alleco's appeal of an August decision finding it liable for breaching covenants regarding $105 million in bonds. The U.S. Court of Appeals for the Eighth Circuit remanded the case to the District Court in Minneapolis to determine whether the federal courts even have jurisdiction over the lawsuit. The District Court has 90 days to resolve the question.

Last August, the District Court ruled that the trustee of Alleco's $105 million in 9 1/2% convertible senior subordinate debentures could declare the bonds in default because the company, formerly known as Allegheny Beverage Corp., dissolved stock-conversion provisions when it went private in 1988.

Alleco appealed that decision, arguing that it wasn't responsible for the debentures because Servam Corp. agreed to assume the payment obligations when it bought Alleco's food-service operations. Servam has steadily paid both interest and principal since the 1987 purchase.
