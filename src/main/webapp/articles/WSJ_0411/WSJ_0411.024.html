Technology & Health: Frequent Douching Raises Infection Risk, Study Says

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/11/90

Frequent vaginal douching appears to increase the risk of infections of the female reproductive organs, University of Washington researchers said. In a study involving about 1,000 women, the researchers calculated that women who douched three to four times a month had a 3.6 times higher risk of developing pelvic inflammatory disease than women who used a douche less than once a month. Women who douche only once or twice a month have 2.5 times the risk of developing the disorder, they said.

"The present study does not prove that douching can cause pelvic inflammatory disease . . . but it shows an association between douching and pelvic inflammatory disease," Pal Woiner-Hanssen and colleagues reported in this week's Journal of the American Medical Association.

Pelvic inflammatory disease, a common cause of pelvic pain in women, results from infections, such as gonorrhea or chlamydia. It also can occur after miscarriages or abortions. It can result in damage to the fallopian tubes and infertility.
