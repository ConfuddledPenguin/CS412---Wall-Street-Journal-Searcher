Marketing & Media: Survey for Disney Finds Moviegoers Don't Like Ads

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/11/90

LOS ANGELES -- Walt Disney Co., which has been under attack by advertisers for its controversial ban on ads in movie theaters, said it hired a research firm to find out how moviegoers feel about ads. Not surprisingly, the survey found that moviegoers don't like ads. But the company called a news conference to announce the results.

Disney said 90% of 18,772 moviegoers surveyed by National Research Group Inc. said they don't want commercials to be shown in theaters. However, the poll found that 95% of those surveyed said they favor movie previews. The poll was conducted on March 31 in Chicago, Boston, New York and seven other communities by the research unit of Saatchi & Saatchi Co.

Jeff Katzenberg, chairman of Walt Disney Studios, said Disney originally thought taking a survey on this subject would make as much sense as "asking dental patients if they like root canal." However, he noted that some advertisers have argued that Disney doesn't have any empirical data proving that moviegoers don't like advertising.

The ban on ads, announced in February, went into effect with the recent opening of Disney's box-office hit "Pretty Woman." Richard Cook, president of Disney's Buena Vista Pictures Distribution, which distributes Disney pictures, said there haven't been any theaters that have refused to comply with the policy.
