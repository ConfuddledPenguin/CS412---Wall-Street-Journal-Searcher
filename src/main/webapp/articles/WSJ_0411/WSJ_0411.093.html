Bush Toughens His Stance On Uprising in Lithuania

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/11/90

TORONTO -- President Bush, slightly toughening his rhetoric on the uprising in Lithuania, contended that "should things deteriorate in Lithuania, it would be extraordinarily difficult" for U.S.-Soviet relations to improve quickly. Mr. Bush's comments, made to reporters after he finished a three-hour meeting with Canadian Prime Minister Brian Mulroney, came a day after Soviet leaders said they would step up pressure in Lithuania in an effort to quell the problems. The president's statement came just a week after the White House and Kremlin announced plans for a late May summit meeting focusing on arms accords.

But Mr. Bush appeared to rule out any chance that the summit might be canceled because of the Lithuania uprising, contending that "when you have complications, that's a good time to talk."

In response to a question, the president also said there is "no trilateral agreement being discussed" among the U.S., Canada and Mexico. Mr. Bush said he was pleased that Prime Minister Mulroney had met recently with Mexican President Carlos Salinas, and that he plans to meet with Mexican officials soon as well.

Mr. Mulroney said he expects the U.S. and Canada to get together this summer to work out an acid-rain accord. He said the two sides are waiting for Congress to complete the Clear Air Act, which includes measures on acid rain.
