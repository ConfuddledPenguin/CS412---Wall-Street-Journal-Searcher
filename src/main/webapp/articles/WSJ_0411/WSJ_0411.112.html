Business Brief -- Detroit Edison Co.: Fermi 2 Facility Is Closed Following Radioactive Leak

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/11/90

Detroit Edison Co. said 20,000 gallons of radioactive water leaked inside the company's Fermi 2 nuclear plant after an unplanned shutdown. No one was contaminated by the water or hurt in the shutdown, Detroit Edison said, but the plant remains closed pending investigation.

A spokesman for the utility company said the plant automatically shut down after a partial power loss. The company said it doesn't know what caused the outage.

A few hours after the shutdown, the radioactive water spilled onto the floor of the turbine room and then drained into the radioactive waste water recovery system, which processes such waste water for reuse.

In July 1988, the Nuclear Regulatory Commission warned Detroit Edison the $4.58 billion plant risked being closed unless its performance improved. The commission later dropped the facility from its list of troubled plants. Fermi 2 had been on the list for three years.
