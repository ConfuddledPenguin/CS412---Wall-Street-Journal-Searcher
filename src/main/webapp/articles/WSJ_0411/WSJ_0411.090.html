New Securities Issues

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/11/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: MUNICIPALS

California -- $500 million of various general obligation bonds, due April 1, 1991-2010 were won by a Bank of America NT&SA group with a bid setting the true interest cost at 6.845%. The bonds, rated triple-A by both Moody's and S&P, were priced for reoffering to yield from 5.80% in 1991 to 6.95% in 2006-2008.

Cleveland -- $66 million of airport-system revenue bonds, Series A, due Jan. 1, 1993-2003, 2010 and 2020, and Series B, due Jan. 1, 1991-2001, and 2020, with capital appreciation bonds due 2002-2006, were priced by a Smith Barney, Harris Upham & Co. Inc. group to yield from 6.00% for Series B bonds due in 1991 to 7.55% for Series A bonds due in 2020. The bonds were rated triple-A by both Moody's and S&P.

Ohio State University -- $54 million of general receipt revenue bonds, due Dec. 1, 1990-2003 and 2009, were priced by a Prudential-Bache Capital Funding group to yield from 5.90% in 1990 to 7.22% in 2009. The bonds were rated single-A-1 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and double-A-minus by Standard & Poor's Corp.

Brighton Area Schools District (Michigan) -- $50 million of general obligation unlimited tax bonds, Series I, due May 1, 1991, 1993-1997, with capital appreciation bonds due 1997-2010, 2015 and 2020, were tentatively priced by an A.G. Edwards & Sons Inc. group to yield from 6.00% in 1991 to 7.55% in 2020.The bonds were rated single-A-1 by Moody's and double-A by S&P.

EUROBONDS

TMC Pimbs Fourth Financing PLC (vehicle) -- #200 million ($327 million) of mortgaged-backed floating-rate notes due April 2029 at par and fixed-reoffered price of 99.76 via Salomon Brothers International Ltd. Interest is .275 percentage point above the three-month London interbank offered rate for the first 10 years, and thereafter, at a spread of .50 percentage point above Libor. Fees 0.34.

Council of Europe (agency) -- 10 billion pesetas ($93.1 million) of 14% Eurobonds due April 27, 1995, priced at 101 via lead manager Banco de Bilbao Vizcaya. Fees 1 5/8.

Nordic Investment Bank (agency) -- 300 million Finnish markkaa ($75.1 million) of 13 1/4% bonds due May 15, 1993, priced at 101 1/4 via Postipankki. Fees 1 3/8.

Oesterreichische Kontrollbank AG (Austria) -- 100 million Swiss francs ($66.7 million) of 7 1/4% public bonds due May 21, 2002, priced at 102 1/2 via Wirtschaftsund Privatbank. Guaranteed by Republic of Austria. Fees 2 3/8.

International Business Machines Corp.'s IBM Australia Credit Ltd. -- 75 million Australian dollars ($57.3 million) of 15 3/8% Eurobonds due May 8, 1992, priced at 101.85 via lead manager Westpac Banking Corp. Fees 1 1/4.

OTHER

Student Loan Marketing Association -- $250 million issue due April 25, 1994, was priced at par for an initial yield of 8.91%. The notes, noncallable for two years, will be reoffered at various prices. They were priced at a spread of .33 percentage point above the Treasury's four-year note through underwriters led by Morgan Stanley & Co.
