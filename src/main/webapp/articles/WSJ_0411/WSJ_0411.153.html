Business Brief -- Springs Industries Inc.: First-Period Net Will Drop By About Half, Firm Says

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/11/90

Springs Industries Inc. said its first-quarter earnings will be about half the 86 cents a share, or $15.3 million, reported in the same period last year. The Fort Mill, S.C., textile manufacturer attributed the expected profit decrease to "continued declines in both volume and margins in finished fabrics, and somewhat lower margins in home furnishings and industrial segments."

Last year's first quarter was one of the strongest in the company's history. That 46% increase from the 1988 period reflected improved performance in all segments, including a return to profitability of its finished-fabrics operations.

The company said first-quarter earnings "should follow more closely the pattern of 1986, 1987 and 1988, when they were 53 cents, 51 cents and 59 cents a share, respectively."

Analysts' 1990 earnings estimates for Springs ranged from $3.70 to $4.10 a share. In 1989, Springs reported earnings of $3.64 a share, or $64.9 million, on sales of $1.9 billion.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Springs shares closed at $33, down 87.5 cents.
