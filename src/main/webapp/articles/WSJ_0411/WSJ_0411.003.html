Twin Labs's Claims Against Rival Weider Dismissed by Court

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/11/90

NEW YORK -- A federal appeals court panel affirmed a lower court's decision to dismiss antitrust and tort claims filed by Twin Laboratories Inc. against its rival in selling nutritional supplements for bodybuilders, Weider Health & Fitness. In February 1989, Twin Labs, Ronkonkoma, N.Y., sued Weider, of Woodland Hills, Calif., in U.S. District Court in Manhattan. The suit charged Weider with trying to exclude competition in selling the supplements. Weider did this, the suit said, by denying ad space to Twin Labs in two bodybuilding magazines it publishes.

U.S. District Judge Michael Mukasey dismissed the suit, ruling that Twin Labs hadn't proved that Weider had monopolized the supplements market or that ad space in Weider's magazines was essential for Twin Labs to compete. The judge also said Weider made a "legitimate and responsible business decision" when it denied the ad space to Twin Labs.

Twin Labs appealed the question of whether the ad space was essential to competition. The appeals court found that Twin Labs "has remained an effective competitor with Weider despite" the refusal to accept the ads.

The appeals court also denied Twin Labs's appeal of Judge Mukasey's dismissal of its tort claim made under New York state law: that the denial of ad space was designed not as a business decision but only as a way to harm Twin Labs.

Twin Labs "is not a 90-pound weakling in whose face Weider has kicked sand," the court said. "Rather, (Twin Labs) is a muscular competitor who is complaining about the competitive process."

Alan Malasky, Weider's attorney at Arent, Fox, Kintner, Plotkin & Kahn in Washington, said that "the court of appeals' ruling confirms that Weider has acted properly in all respects."

Grant L. Clark, attorney for Twin Labs at Washington's Rivkin, Radler, Bayh, Hart & Kremer, said the company is deciding whether to further appeal, but he said that "Twin Labs is doing entirely too well for people to feel it's a weakling.
