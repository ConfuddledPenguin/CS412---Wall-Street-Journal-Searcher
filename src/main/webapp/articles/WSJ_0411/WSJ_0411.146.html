Business Brief -- Washington Bancorp.: Group Seeks Lower Price For Company After Loss

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/11/90

Washington Bancorp. reported a first-quarter loss of $5.7 million, or 80 cents a share. The loss prompted an investor group that has agreed to buy the company to demand a lower price. A year ago, the bank holding company had net of $3.6 million, or 50 cents a share.

WBC Acquistion Corp., which agreed in March to purchase the company for $19.20 a share, said it remains interested, but that the agreed-upon price is "no longer realistic." Both companies said discussions are continuing.

Washington Bancorp. said the $5.7 million loss included special charges of about $5.5 million.

In addition, the company added $3 million to loan-loss reserves, compared with a $1.8 million addition a year ago. Reserves totaled $40.4 million, compared with $45.7 million a year ago.

At the end of the quarter, the company's nonperforming loans totaled $101.3 million, from about $60 million at year end.
