Marketing & Media: Richard Swank Gets Senior Post at Edgell

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/11/90

CLEVELAND -- Edgell Communications Inc. named Richard B. Swank, 58 years old, its new chief executive officer. The appointment comes a week after founder Robert L. Edgell announced his retirement at the age of 68 amid speculation the highly leveraged magazine publisher and trade exposition company is having trouble meeting interest payments on its debt.

Edgell Communications was formed in 1987 when Mr. Edgell led a $334.1 million leveraged buy-out of the business publications and school supplies unit of Harcourt Brace Jovanovich Inc. The buy-out was financed by Kidder, Peabody & Co., a unit of General Electric Co., which remains the majority owner of Edgell Communications. To finance the deal, Kidder Peabody provided a bridge loan that was later refinanced through the sale of high-yield securities. People familiar with the company said Edgell has had trouble meeting the interest payments on those junk bonds due to rising publishing costs and generally soft advertising markets for business and professional publications.

Mr. Edgell said in an interview that the company will meet all of its interest payments this year. "Since forming the company, it's true our cash flow has been tied up in the debt structure," he said. "We are looking at restructuring so that we can go back on the acquisitions trail and build the company."

Mr. Swank most recently was head of Richard Swank & Associates, a Redding, Conn., consulting firm. In 1988, he left Dun & Bradstreet Corp., where he was an executive vice president largely responsible for trade magazine publishing and information services.
