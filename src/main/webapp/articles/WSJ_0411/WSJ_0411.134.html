Conoco Orders 2 Tankers For Oil With Double Hulls

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/11/90

HOUSTON -- A major oil company said it ordered the construction of two crude tankers designed to help reduce the chances of an oil spill. Conoco Inc., a unit of Du Pont Co., said it wanted the tankers to be equipped with double hulls, even as new regulations are being debated concerning the need for such safety devices. Constantine S. Nicandros, Conoco's chief executive officer, said the company was going ahead with its plans regardless of the regulatory outcome.

There is much debate over whether having two hulls would have prevented the Exxon Valdez oil spill in Alaska last year, but requiring double-hull vessels nonetheless has been the focus of tanker safety. Mr. Nicandros wouldn't disclose the cost of the two tankers, but he said having two hulls costs 15% more than a single-hull vessel.

Most oil tankers have a single hull, or skin, to hold oil. With two hulls, there is an extra layer of protection. Depending on a tanker's size, it can easily cost $50 million or more.

Conoco, which operates several refineries, also said it would attempt to reduce its toxic air emissions and hazardous wastes by one-third over the next few years as part of a broader environmental initiative. Initially, Mr. Nicandros said the initiative would cost Conoco more than $50 million annually.
