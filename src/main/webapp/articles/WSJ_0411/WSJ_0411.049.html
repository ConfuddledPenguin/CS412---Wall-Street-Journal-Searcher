PNC Financial Sued After Announcement Of Loan-Loss Action

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/11/90

PITTSBURGH -- Shareholders of PNC Financial Corp. filed two class-action lawsuits against the bank-holding company, alleging that PNC concealed or misrepresented the likelihood of huge increases in problem loans. The suits follow PNC's announcement Friday of an increase in loan-loss provisions by between $70 million and $80 million for the first quarter. PNC said it made the move because of federal regulators' tougher scrutiny of loan portfolios and the continued softness in the commercial real-estate market.

PNC said the higher provisions will lower first-quarter earnings as much as 40% from the year-earlier profit of $123.6 million, or $1.32 a share.

In composite trading Friday on the New York Stock Exchange, PNC fell $3.875 to $34, and for the week the stock fell $5.625 amid mounting concerns over asset quality. PNC closed yesterday at $35.25, up 50 cents.

PNC said yesterday that it would have no comment about the suits until it had a chance to study them. The bank said such suits aren't unexpected after a reported earnings decline.

The suits -- one filed in U.S. District Court in Pittsburgh, the other in U.S. District Court in Philadelphia -- both charge that PNC's chairman and chief executive officer, Thomas H. O'Brien, overstated year-ago earnings by failing to set appropriate loan-loss reserve levels.
