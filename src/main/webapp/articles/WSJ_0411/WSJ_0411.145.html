Business Brief -- Morgan Stanley Group Inc.: Nikkei Put Warrant Offer Is Postponed by the Firm

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/11/90

Morgan Stanley & Co., New York, said an offering of seven million put warrants on the Nikkei index of 225 stocks on the Tokyo Stock Exchange has been postponed. A Morgan Stanley spokesman said the offering, which was to be made by Morgan Stanley Group Inc., was reportedly postponed because of the recent volatility in the Japanese stock market. In general, increased volatility raises the price of warrants, making the option-like instruments more difficult to sell. Yesterday, in Tokyo, the Nikkei index -- which has plummeted 23.9% this year -- rose 2.5% after falling 3.8% Monday.

Meanwhile, syndicate officials at PaineWebber Inc. said they plan to price next week a similar offering by PaineWebber Group Inc. The firm is slated to offer five million Nikkei stock average put warrants and 3.5 million Nikkei stock average call warrants.

A put warrant is an option to sell a security at a specified price, usually within a limited period. A call is an option to buy a security at a specified price, also usually within a certain period.
