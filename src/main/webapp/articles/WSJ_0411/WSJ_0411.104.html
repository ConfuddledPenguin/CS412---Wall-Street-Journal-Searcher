Taiwan to Open Reserves

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/11/90

TAIPEI, Taiwan -- The Central Bank announced that it will appropriate a part of Taiwan's foreign exchange reserves to refinance Taiwanese businesses that plan to invest or seek mergers and acquisitions overseas. The move is aimed at using Taiwan's approximately $73 billion in local foreign exchange reserves to expand Taiwan's economic base and stimulate further development, Yu Chen, the Central Bank's deputy governor, said at a news conference. He said the move might trigger short-term capital outflow, but in the longer term, earnings from overseas investment are likely to extend Taiwan's economic strength.

Those eligible for the loans include companies involved in natural resource development, high technology, anti-pollution technology, and management training, he said. The Bank of Communications, Export-Import Bank of the Republic of China, International Commercial Bank of China and the China Development Corp. are the four banks designated to issue the loans.
