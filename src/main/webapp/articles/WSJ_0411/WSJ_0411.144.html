What's News: Business and Finance

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/11/90

THE THRIFT BAILOUT AGENCY flopped in its second attempt to sell 40-year bonds, a sign that investors aren't ready for bonds that have extra-long maturities. The $3.5 billion auction of so-called "bailout bonds" went poorly despite a higher-than-expected average yield of 8.89%.

Treasury-bond prices tumbled in reaction to the S&L bond auction. Stocks edged higher, while the dollar fell against the mark and yen.

---

William Farley plans to surrender majority ownership of West Point-Pepperell because he is unable to pay for the $1.56 billion acquisition. The move follows last year's bitter takeover battle with the textile giant.

---

Lockheed's board easily won its proxy battle against Dallas investor Harold Simmons. But a slate of Simmons-backed initiatives also was approved, signaling holders want more say in the defense firm's revival.

---

The Big Three auto makers are expected to post a 70% drop in first-quarter earnings, reflecting efforts to clear bloated inventories. The strategy included a 26.5% reduction in quarterly production and sales incentives averaging $1,000 a vehicle.

---

Merrill Lynch has tightened its grip on how it credits investors' securities proceeds, which could save the firm millions of dollars that had gone to clients. Merrill brokers are complaining about the move, though it is practiced by other big brokerages.

---

Bell Atlantic said its Bell of Pennsylvania phone unit agreed to pay $42 million to settle charges it used "deceptive techniques" to sell customers more services than they wanted.

---

Eastern Airlines' unsecured creditors asked a bankruptcy court to remove the carrier's management and appoint a trustee to run the troubled Texas Air subsidiary.

---

Credit Suisse's parent is discussing an alliance with Bank Leu, paving the way for a long-awaited consolidation of the Swiss banking market.

---

Two Mitsubishi Group firms plan to reunite despite expected criticism that they are trying to re-establish their prewar monopoly in Japan.

---

OPEC is growing nervous about the continued slide in oil prices. But the group is unlikely to take any action before late May, when it holds its regular midyear conference.

---

Time Warner's long-term public debt was placed on S&P's CreditWatch with negative implications due to its financing pact with Pathe.

---

Massachusetts announced steps to help firms obtain loans amid growing concern that a credit crunch may be worsening the state's economic woes.

---

Motorola posted a modest 3.3% in first-quarter earnings, but signs that its computer-chip business is rebounding sent its stock higher.

---

Westinghouse Electric reported that first-quarter earnings rose 11%, though revenue dropped slightly.
