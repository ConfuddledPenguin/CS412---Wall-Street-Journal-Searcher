What's News -- World-Wide

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/11/90

GORBACHEV RAISED the possibility of imposing direct rule over Lithuania. As the president of the breakaway Baltic republic blamed "ultra-rightist" forces for Moscow's threat to use unspecified political and economic measures against Lithuania, the Soviet leader warned that he might impose direct presidential rule in the event of "civilian conflict or confrontation." Such a step could include martial law and dissolving the republic's legislature. Gorbachev, who noted that Lithuania's independence bid could be costly to international peace, also said that the Kremlin was still striving to find a political solution to the situation.

With opposition expected to a planned economic package, Gorbachev said his advisory council would meet Saturday to discuss speeding market changes.

---

Palestinian extremists in Beirut freed a French woman, a Belgian man and their young daughter after holding them hostage for more than two years. The three were taken to Paris following their release by a Libyan-backed group headed by Abu Nidal. The move came a month after France returned three jet fighters to Gadhafi.

---

Walesa confirmed that he would run for Poland's presidency in the country's next elections, according to the state-owned PAP news agency. A top aide to the Solidarity founder said Walesa was prepared to force President Jaruzelski to resign because of the slow pace of political and economic change, Reuters news agency reported.

---

NASA scrubbed the launch of the space shuttle Discovery and said it could be as long as two weeks before a new attempt to carry the $1.5 billion Hubble Space Telescope into orbit. Space agency officials at Cape Canaveral in Florida said the countdown was halted by a faulty power unit in the spacecraft's steering system.

---

Bush said there is "an awful lot to be done" to resolve U.S.-Soviet arms-control differences before next month's superpower summit. The president, in Canada for a meeting with Prime Minister Mulroney, also said that "should things deteriorate in Lithuania, it would be extraordinarily difficult" for U.S.-Soviet ties to improve quickly.

---

Federal safety officials concluded that lapses by Boeing Co., United Airlines and the FAA contributed to the February 1989 failure of a cargo door on a jetliner over the Pacific Ocean. The resulting decompression caused several feet of cabin floor to collapse, sending nine passengers to their deaths.

---

East German legislators haggled over unfilled cabinet posts, delaying an agreement on East Europe's first freely elected coalition. The last-minute wrangling reportedly centered on the post of economic minister, who would play a crucial role in the nation's change to a free-market system and in its reunification with West Germany.

---

Greece's Mitsotakis was designated premier and asked by the president to form a new government. The conservative leader, whose New Democracy party won 150 seats in the 300-member legislature in Sunday's nationwide elections, secured the backing of a centrist deputy for a majority.

---

Health researchers have discovered new evidence linking stress on the job to high blood pressure. The study found that men who hold jobs with demands over which they have little control are more likely to have high blood pressure and to sustain physical changes to the heart than men who don't face such strains.

---

India's prime minister said the country should be "psychologically prepared" for an armed conflict with Pakistan over the disputed Kashmir valley. Singh made his remarks in New Delhi's Parliament after Moslem secessionists in the valley killed a Hindu industrialist held since Friday.

---

Western travelers in Beijing said two envoys sent last week to calm Islamic unrest in China's remote northwest were killed. Security forces reportedly killed about 50 people in subsequent riots. Regional officials have declined to comment on the reports.

---

The Census Bureau reported that the number of men in the U.S. grew faster than the population of women in the 1980s, something that hasn't happened since the first decade of the century. Death rates for men declined more rapidly than for women, but the report showed that women continue to outnumber men in the country.

---

Mongolia's Communist Party opened a meeting in Ulan Bator with an appeal for political and economic restructuring in an attempt to maintain its 70-year-old grip on power amid growing pro-democracy demands. The three-day meeting comes ahead of planned multiparty elections.

---

Mandela will lead an 11-member African National Congress delegation in talks next month with the government on black-white power sharing. Following the May 2-4 discussions, the black nationalist leader will embark on a series of foreign trips, including stops in the U.S. and the Soviet Union.
