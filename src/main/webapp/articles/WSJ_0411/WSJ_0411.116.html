Business Brief -- Bechtel Group Inc.: Contract for Airport Project Is Signed With Hong Kong

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/11/90

Bechtel Group Inc. said it entered a contract with the Hong Kong government to manage a $16 billion airport construction project, one of the largest such projects ever undertaken. The San Francisco closely held engineering and construction company said the project will take seven years to complete. It entails building a new airport, a suspension bridge that would be the longest in the world, a rapid transit system and a highway to China, among other things.

Bechtel didn't disclose exactly how much it stands to earn from the project. It said the Hong Kong government hopes to fund half the project through private funds.

Bechtel's last project of similar size was the construction of the city of Jubail in Saudi Arabia, a $20 billion project. The city is still under construction.

The Hong Kong project is the second major transaction Bechtel has announced in a week. Last week, Bechtel signed an agreement with the Soviet Union to do a preliminary study on the feasibility of converting a research center near Moscow into a technology center similar to California's Silicon Valley.
