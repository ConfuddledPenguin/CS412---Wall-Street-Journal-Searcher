Citicorp Unit Conducts Due-Diligence Review Of Thrift in Virginia

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/11/90

TYSONS CORNER, Va. -- United Savings Bank said Citicorp's Citibank unit is conducting a due-diligence examination of the savings and loan as prelude to a possible acquisition. United Savings said it expects to learn by April 30 whether Citibank intends to negotiate a definitive acquisition agreement. In a statement, United Savings said the two companies have "expressed their mutual interest in an acquisition."

United Savings said several companies have expressed interest in acquiring the thrift since it retained an investment banker to consider a sale.

A Citibank spokeswoman confirmed the statement released by United Savings, but wouldn't go beyond it. She said that since 1982, Citibank has purchased four troubled thrifts. The thrifts were in Washington, D.C., California, Illinois and Florida.
