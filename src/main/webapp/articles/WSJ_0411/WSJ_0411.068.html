Westcoast Energy Sets A Cogeneration Plant In British Columbia

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/11/90

VANCOUVER, British Columbia -- Westcoast Energy Inc. said it plans to build with a partner a 106 million Canadian dollar (US$91.3 million) cogeneration plant that will supply electricity to B.C. Hydro, a provincially owned utility. The proposed McMahon Cogeneration Plant, which Westcoast plans to build at Taylor in partnership with the CU Power division of Canadian Utilities Ltd., would supply 105 megawatts of electricity to B.C. Hydro, beginning in October 1993.

Cogeneration is the simultaneous production of electricity and steam, for use in industrial processes.

B.C. Hydro said the contract with Westcoast and its partner is valued at more than C$45 million annually, and would run 20 years from the plant's start-up date. The electric utility, Canada's third-biggest, said the new plant is part of its push to encourage independent power production in British Columbia, where consumption of electric power is growing rapidly, along with the province's economy.

Westcoast, a big natural-gas pipeline, utility, and oil and gas production concern, said the new plant would be located at the site of its existing McMahon gas processing plant, in northeastern British Columbia.
