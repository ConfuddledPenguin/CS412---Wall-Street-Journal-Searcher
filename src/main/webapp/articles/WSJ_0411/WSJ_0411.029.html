House of Commons In Canada Approves 7% Consumption Tax

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/11/90

OTTAWA -- Canada's House of Commons approved legislation to introduce a 7% consumption tax effective Jan. 1. The bill also needs to be approved by the Senate, which is expected to hold lengthy hearings on the proposal.

The value-added tax would replace a more narrowly based 13.5% manufacturing sales tax. It is to be applied to most goods and services except basic groceries, prescription drugs and medical services. Credits are to be paid to low-income Canadians to offset the tax's impact.

Canadian businesses are to levy the tax on domestic sales. They are to be allowed to claim a credit for any tax they pay on purchases of goods and services. The burden of the tax is to fall on consumers.
