Credit Ratings

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/11/90

MESA LIMITED PARTNERSHIP (Amarillo, Texas) -- Moody's Investors Service Inc. said it cut the debt ratings of Mesa Limited Partnership to B-2 from Ba-3 because the company withdrew a plan to raise as much as $400 million by selling interests in one of its prime properties. The rating concern said the move affects about $600 million in debt from Mesa, an oil and gas concern controlled by investor T. Boone Pickens Jr., and its Mesa Capital Corp. unit. Mesa had planned to use proceeds from the sale to reduce its $1.52 billion in debt. Without the offering of units of a new Hugoton Royalty Trust, Moody's said that it believes Mesa will be under pressure to generate enough cash flow to meet its required payments, including its preferred stock distributions. The rating concern said that Mesa needs either to sell assets to reduce its debt or to enjoy a "sustained rise" in natural gas prices to improve its financial position. In February, Standard & Poor's Corp. also cut Mesa's debt rating, to single-B-plus from double-B-plus. Mesa said last week that it withdrew the proposed offering because it didn't look as if it would attract the price it had hoped for. In addition, the company said that its offering document was delayed by the Securities and Exchange Commission, postponing the proposed offering from the strongest part of the natural gas season to the weakest. A spokesman, however, said that the bankruptcy of Drexel Burnham Lambert Inc., which Mesa initially chose as its underwriter, didn't delay the offering and that Shearson Lehman Hutton Inc. was ready to step in and handle the transaction. The Mesa spokesman said the rating services' moves were expected, but said the company "made the right moves from an economic standpoint."
