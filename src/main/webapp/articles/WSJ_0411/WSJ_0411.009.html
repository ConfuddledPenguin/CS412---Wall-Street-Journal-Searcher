Who's News: Sanchez Is Named Gradco's President; 4th-Period Loss Seen

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/11/90

IRVINE, Calif. -- Gradco Systems Inc. named a new president, divided its business into separate printer and copier units, and said it will take a charge against earnings resulting in a net loss for the fiscal fourth quarter ended March 31. Joe C. Sanchez, 56 years old, a 29-year veteran of Xerox Corp., was named president and chief operating officer. As president, Mr. Sanchez succeeds Keith B. Stewart, 50, who remains chairman and chief executive officer. The post of chief operating officer is new.

Mr. Sanchez also will serve as president and chief executive officer of newly created Gradco Printer Systems Inc., the domestic operating unit under the new structure that will be responsible for all Gradco's printer products business world-wide, including engineering, manufacturing and sales.

Gradco Japan Ltd., 96%-owned by Gradco and 4%-owned by Japanese investors, will be responsible for all of Gradco's copier business world-wide. Mark M. Takeuchi, 51, president of Gradco Japan, was named a corporate director, along with Mr. Sanchez. As directors, they succeed R. John Thielen, president of Gradco's Dyna V Corp. unit, and Albert B. Boese, formerly Gradco's vice president, operations.

Gradco said the charge to fourth quarter earnings stems primarily from the discontinuance of its domestic manufacturing operations. The company is in the midst of transferring all its manufacturing operations to South Korea and Japan. Gradco said the actual amount of the charge won't be determined until its year-end audit is completed, and officials declined to estimate the amount of the fourth-quarter loss.

In national over-the-counter trading yesterday, Gradco stock closed at $8 a share, down 25 cents.

Gradco also said it finalized a new $35 million credit facility with Bank of California N.A. and Mitsubishi Bank Ltd., to replace a $20 million facility with Union Bank. The new credit facility will be used for working capital, Gradco said.
