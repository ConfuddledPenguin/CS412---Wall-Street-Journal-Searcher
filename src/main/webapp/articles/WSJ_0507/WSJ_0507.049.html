Perpetual Financial Corp.

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/07/90

VIENNA, Va. -- Perpetual Financial Corp. said it expects to report a pretax charge of as much as $50 million for its fiscal second quarter to reflect an additional provision to its loan-loss reserve and a write-down of foreclosed real estate. In the year-earlier quarter, the thrift holding concern earned $5 million, or 30 cents a share. The company said it expects to report results for the latest second quarter, which ended last Monday, on May 17.
