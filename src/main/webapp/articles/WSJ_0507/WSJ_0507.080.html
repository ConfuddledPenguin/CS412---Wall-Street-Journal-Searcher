Taiwan's Trade Surplus Drops

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/07/90

TAIPEI, Taiwan -- Taiwan's trade surplus in April totaled $780 million U.S. dollars, a decline of 21% from the same period last year, the government said Friday. The Directorate General of Budget, Accounting and Statistics said April's exports reached $5.7 billion dollars, up 1.2% from a year earlier, while imports rose 6%, to $4.92 billion dollars.

Taiwan registered a trade surplus of $2.59 billion dollars in the first four months of the year, down 31% from the year-earlier period, the agency said.

Government officials attributed the lower surplus mainly to a stronger Taiwan dollar, which made locally manufactured products less attractive to foreign buyers.

In April, Taiwan had a trade surplus with the U.S. totaling $670 million dollars, down 32.1% from a year earlier. April exports to the U.S. reached $1.85 billion dollars, while imports totaled $1.18 billion dollars.
