International: Government of Norway Unveils Tax-Overhaul Plan

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/07/90

STOCKHOLM -- Norway's minority government outlined a sweeping overhaul of personal income and corporate taxation to bring the country's tax codes in line more with those of other industrial nations. Finance Ministry officials said the proposals, submitted to the Storting, or parliament, as a so-called white paper, are modeled on tax reform adopted by the U.S. and U.K. during the mid-1980s. Nominal tax rates would be slashed for both individuals and companies, but the revenue base broadened through elimination of many current deductions and other loopholes.

The government was intentionally vague about future tax rates, pending upcoming hearings by Parliament. But the top rate for personal taxation likely would decline to between 45% and 50% from 59% currently, government officials suggested.

Norway's corporate tax could be halved from the current 50.8%, but the actual change in companies' tax bills would be far less drastic. Generous deductions under the present system ensure that virtually no Norwegian company pays the official tax rate.
