Aetna Unit Dissolves Joint Venture Insuring Multinational Firms

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/07/90

HARTFORD, Conn. -- Aetna Casualty & Surety Co. has dissolved a joint venture that provided property/casualty insurance for multinational corporations because it wasn't successful in a highly competitive segment of the insurance market. Aetna, a unit of Aetna Life & Casualty Co., and Assicurazoni Generali S.p.A. of Trieste, Italy, each owned 45% of Aegen International Inc., which was formed in 1981. Taisho Marine & Fire Co. of Japan owned the remaining 10%.

Aegen wrote just $10 million in net written premiums. It faced huge competition from such larger, well established insurers as American International Group Inc., Cigna Corp. and Chubb Co.

All three of the venture partners decided that each of them individually will develop insurance coverages for U.S.-based companies doing business abroad and for foreign-based companies with U.S. ventures.

The venture maintained 30 employees at New York's World Trade Center, and they were told Friday that the company was being disbanded.

The Aetna/Generali International Benefits network, which provides employee benefits for foreign employees of multinational companies, is a separate entity and wasn't affected by this change. Aegen was separate from Aetna's international operations, which weren't affected by Friday's actions.
