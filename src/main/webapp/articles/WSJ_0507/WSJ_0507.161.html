Dow Chemical Names Buzzelli to New Post For Ecology Issues

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/07/90

MIDLAND, Mich. -- Dow Chemical Co. created a new environmental corporate vice-president position in an effort to make the company's world-wide business decisions more responsive to ecology concerns. The company says David T. Buzzelli, president and chief executive officer of Dow Chemical Canada, has been appointed vice-president and corporate director of environment, health and safety. Mr. Buzzelli, a member of Dow Chemical's management committee, will assume the post on Aug. 1 and will report directly to Frank Popoff, president and chief executive officer.

Mr. Buzzelli's job will be to "fully integrate environmental concerns into all of our business and operations decisions," Mr. Popoff says. "How we address the environment will have an enormous impact on our future success."

Mr. Buzzelli says Dow Chemical faces increasing pressure to meet higher environmental standards from regulatory agencies, consumers and communities where plants are located. In his new role, Mr. Buzzelli will coordinate all of Dow Chemical's health and safety activities, including environmental affairs, product testing and occupational health, which are currently decentralized.

After joining Dow Chemical in 1965 as a chemical engineer, Mr. Buzzelli held various positions in agricultural chemicals production, served as director of government and public affairs, and was appointed head of Dow Canada in 1986.

Denis Wilcock, executive vice-president of Dow Europe, will succeed Mr. Buzzelli and will join Dow Chemical's management committee. Yves Bobillier, president of Dow Latin America, has been named executive vice-president of Dow Europe with responsibility for the plastics group.
