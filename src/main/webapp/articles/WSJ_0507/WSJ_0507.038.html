Ex-Navy Aide Pleads Guilty In Pentagon Bribery Case

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/07/90

WASHINGTON -- Former Navy acquisition official George Stone pleaded guilty to conspiracy and another charge stemming from the nationwide investigation of Pentagon bribery and influence-peddling. Mr. Stone, who supervised Marine Corps contracts as a mid-level civilian official through the late 1980s, was indicted on broader charges two months ago for helping subsidiaries of Litton Industries Inc. and United Technologies Corp. obtain bid-related information. The two companies haven't been accused of any wrongdoing, but two defense-industry consultants and three former executives of United Technologies' Norden Systems subsidiary have been convicted of conspiring to attempt to illegally secure some $150 million of Marine Corps business.

Mr. Stone initially fought the charges against him. But Friday in Alexandria, Va., federal district court, he agreed to cooperate with the continuing grand jury inquiry and pleaded guilty to two felony counts. He could face a total of 15 years in jail and a $500,000 fine.

In documents filed with the guilty plea, prosecutors for the first time provided details about how Mr. Stone personally intervened to keep Norden in the competition for a Marine Corps radar-and-communications contract in 1988.
