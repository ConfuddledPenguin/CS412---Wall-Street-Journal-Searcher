General Dynamics to Pay $527,000 in Job-Bias Case

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/07/90

WASHINGTON -- General Dynamics Corp. agreed to pay $527,000 in back pay and salary increases to minority and women workers as part of a settlement of government job discrimination charges. The Labor Department's contract-compliance office said the defense contractor's Space Systems division in San Diego didn't fully carry out its affirmative-action program. The office identified several practices it alleged to be discriminatory.

General Dynamics agreed to the settlement without admitting the charges.

The Space Systems division agreed to extend job opportunities to black engineering applicants allegedly screened out of positions, and to give $268,771 in back pay to 21 black individuals, the compliance office said. The contractor also reviewed the salaries that it pays to minorities and women, and raised the pay by $258,590 for 42 minority men and women and 61 non-minority women.
