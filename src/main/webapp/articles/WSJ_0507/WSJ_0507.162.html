Huntaway Partners Plans Distribution To Its Unit Holders

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/07/90

VALENCIA, Calif. -- Huntway Partners L.P. said it expects to make a first-quarter distribution to its preference unit holders after reaching agreements for a capital infusion and restructuring of the partnership's bank loan. Huntway, a refiner of asphalt and petroleum products, declared a distribution of 34.5 cents a unit, payable May 30 to preference units of record May 15. But Huntway said that the payment is contingent upon completion before May 30 of the capital infusion and the revised bank loan.

Huntway's operating committee agreed to a proposal by Bankers Trust Co. to restructure the partnership's debt into a $17 million term loan and a $31 million letter of credit and working capital facility. Huntway said it must make quarterly payments on the proposed term loan, which must be paid off by the end of 1993.

Huntway said it expects to receive $5 million from the sale of 625,000 units, at $8 each, to an affiliate of its general partner. The sale would increase by 12% the number of Huntway's preference units outstanding to about 6.2 million.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading Friday, Huntway's preference units rose 62.5 cents, to close at $7.50 after trading was delayed pending dissimination of news about the agreements. Members of management and a private investor hold common units that aren't publicly traded.

Huntway said that future distributions to preference unit holders will be permitted under the revised bank agreement if the partnership's operating performance meets "certain targeted levels." A spokesman said the partnership hopes to meet those targets, but cautioned that he couldn't make any guarantees. Distributions on the common units won't be made until the term loan has been reduced to $5 million, Huntway said.
