Business Brief -- Framatome S.A.: Profit Fell by 30% in '89, But a Rebound Is Expected

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/07/90

The French nuclear energy group Framatome S.A. said profit fell by 30% in 1989 but is expected to show a significant rebound this year. Framatome said the drop in its group profit to 742 million francs ($131.4 million) from 1.06 billion francs in 1988 reflected the fact that it received payment for only one new nuclear reactor during the year, down from three in 1988.

Group revenue rose to 19.9 billion francs from 10.8 billion over the same period, but Framatome noted that this gain reflected the billing of a Superphenix generator that hasn't yet had any impact on its profits.

Looking ahead, Framatome said that "results for the year 1990 should show a significant improvement with the billing of three new nuclear reactors and the favorable evolution of profits from connecting rods activities." It didn't elaborate.

Framatome is controlled by the French government and doesn't have any publicly traded voting securities.
