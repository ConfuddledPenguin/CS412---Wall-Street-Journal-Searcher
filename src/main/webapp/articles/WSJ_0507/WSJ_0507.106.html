Mediagenic's Loss Totaling $19 Million Is Estimated for Year

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/07/90

MENLO PARK, Calif. -- Mediagenic Inc. expects to post a year-end loss of $19 million, on revenue of $65 million, and is considering the possible sale of the company. The loss is because of discontinuing certain operations and damages the company must pay in a patent infringement case, the company said.

In March, Mediagenic was ordered to pay as much as $6.4 million in damages, interest and lawyers fee in connection with a 1986 patent suit by Magnavox Co. Mediagenic is a publisher of entertainment software formerly known as Activision.

Mediagenic said it will also discontinue or shrink unprofitable product lines in the face of a stagnant domestic market for computer-entertainment software, and strong competition. The company has retained the investment-banking firm of PaineWebber Inc. to advise it on ways to find additional financing and the possible sale of all or part of the company.

A year earlier, Mediagenic had net income of $1.1 million, or 28 cents a share, on revenue of $60.6 million.
