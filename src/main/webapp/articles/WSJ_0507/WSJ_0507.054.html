World Bank Approves Loans

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/07/90

WASHINGTON -- The World Bank and its affiliate, the International Development Association, approved $455 million in loans to India, Bangladesh and Sri Lanka. India will get two loans, $235 million from the IDA and $25 million from the World Bank, to improve the training of engineers, technicians and other workers.

In other actions, the IDA approved a $105 million credit to Bangladesh for electric-power projects, primarily in rural areas. Sri Lanka will get a $90 million IDA loan to help stabilize its economy and support broad structural reforms.

The IDA credits will be interest-free except for administrative fees; the World Bank loan to India will carry a variable interest rate, currently 7.75% a year.
