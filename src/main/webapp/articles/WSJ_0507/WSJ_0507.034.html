Marketing & Media: Whittle Hires Turner Aide As Chairman of New Unit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/07/90

NEW YORK -- Whittle Communications L.P. hired the top sales executive of Turner Broadcasting System Inc. to serve as chairman of a new unit that includes the classroom news service Channel One. Knoxville, Tenn.-based Whittle said Farrell Reynolds will serve as chairman and chief executive officer of Whittle Educational Network. Mr. Reynolds, a 10-year veteran of Turner Broadcasting, had overseen all ad sales for Turner cable networks.

The new unit includes all of Whittle's properties, such as magazines, advertiser-supported wall posters and television programs, aimed at U.S. schools.

The unit's largest property is Channel One, the daily TV news show for high school students; the show, which includes commercials, began airing in March.

Educators have called Channel One blatant commercialization of public education. So far, 3,600 schools in 38 states have signed contracts to air the 12-minute daily program. Turner competes against Channel One with "CNN Newsroom," a free 15-minute daily news program without ads.

Christopher Whittle, chairman of Whittle Communications, 50%-owned by Time Warner Inc., said that the new unit plans to create more TV channels in the next three years and that some services may be expanded overseas.
