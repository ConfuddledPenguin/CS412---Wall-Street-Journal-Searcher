Who's News: Manville Reorganizes Some of Its Businesses

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/07/90

DENVER -- Manville Corp. reorganized some of its operations and named two new group presidents as part of a continuing effort to define its core businesses and better focus its marketing efforts. The maker of wood, paper and fiberglass products named Richard A. Kashnow president of the new Engineered Products Group, which will make and market the company's fiberglass mats, reinforcement fibers, and auto and aerospace filters and insulation parts. Manville's Glaswerk Schuller unit in West Germany will also be part of the group. Mr. Kashnow was vice president and general manager of the former mats, fiber and reinforcements division at Manville.

Ronald W. Mathewson was named president of the new Building Products Group, which will combine the company's fiberglass-roofing and building-insulation businesses. He was vice president and general manager of the company's former building insulation division.

Messrs. Kashnow and Mathewson were also named senior vice presidents of the corporation.

John D.C. Roach, an executive vice president, was named president of the new Mining and Minerals Group, including filtration and minerals production worldwide.

Robert Anderson was named senior vice president for science and technology. He will be in charge of health, safety, environment and research departments at the company. He succeeds William A. Sells, who has announced his plans to retire.

Manville Forest Products Corp. will continue to operate as a unit, headed by President Thomas Johnson, who assumed that title late last year.
