New Securities Issues

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/07/90

The following were among Friday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: MORTGAGE-BACKED

Morgan Stanley Mortgage Trust -- $400 million of Remic mortgage securities, offered in seven classes by Morgan Stanley & Co. The offering, Series 34, through a trust established by Morgan Stanley, is backed by Ginnie Mae 9% securities, with a weighed average to remaining to term of maturity of 26.7 years. The classes are "plain vanilla," sequential-pay securities.

UBS Securities Inc. -- $300.25 million of UBS Trust Remic mortgage securities, offered in 13 classes by UBS Securities Inc. The offering, Series 4, through a trust established by UBS Securities, has been structured to yield, where details are available, from 9.5% to 10.36%. The collateral consists of Ginnie Mae 9 1/2% securities with a weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29.75 years.

Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $250.1 million issue of Remic mortgage securities, offered in 18 classes by Donaldson Lufkin & Jenrette Securities Corp. The offering, Series 173, is backed by Freddie Mac 9% securities, with a weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29.3 years.

EUROBONDS

Woolwich Building Society (U.K.) -- 50 million pounds of 15 1/4% Eurobonds, due May 18, 1992, priced at 101 1/8, via Deutsche Bank Capital Markets.

Skopbank (Finland) -- 205 Finnish Markka of 14% bonds due May 21, 1992, priced at 100.90, via Bankers Trust International.
