Who's News: Petrofina S.A.

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/07/90

PETROFINA S.A. (Brussels) -- As expected, Petrofina announced Friday a new slate of directors, giving more power to the two Brussels-based holding companies that control it, Societe Generale de Belgique S.A. and Groupe Bruxelles Lambert S.A. The oil company named Albert Frere as chairman and Viscount Etienne Davignon as vice chairman. Mr. Frere is chairman of Groupe Bruxelles Lambert, and Mr. Davignon holds the same post at Societe Generale. Groupe Bruxelles owns about 20% of Petrofina's shares, and another company controlled by Mr. Frere holds some 5%. Societe Generale indirectly controls about 12% of Petrofina. In addition, Petrofina named 40-year-old Francois Cornelis, currently an executive director, as chief executive officer. Jean-Pierre Amory is retiring as chairman and chief executive but will remain a director. Pierre de Tillesse, who is stepping down as vice chairman, also will continue as a director. Among other appointments, Petrofina named as directors Guy Dejouany, who is chairman of Cie. Generale des Eaux of France; Gilles Samyn, a director of Groupe Bruxelles; and Henrique Bandeira Vieira, Petrofina's senior vice president, exploration and production. The board is being expanded to a maximum of 20 members from 18. Ten of the directors are to represent the big shareholders, six are to come from Petrofina's management, and the rest are to be "independent" directors.
