Horsham Corp. Plans To Boost Its Interest In Clark Oil & Refining

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/07/90

TORONTO -- Jeremy Garbutt, president and chief operating officer of Horsham Corp., said the company plans to exercise an option later this year to raise its interest in Clark Oil & Refining Corp. to 75% from 60% currently. "Our ultimate objective is to have 100% of Clark," Mr. Garbutt said after a meeting with analysts.

Horsham officials wouldn't comment on a possible price for the additional Clark stake. However, a Horsham spokesman said a 1989 independent appraisal put a value of $550 million on Clark's physical assets alone.

Mr. Garbutt said Horsham, a Toronto investment concern, can exercise its option to acquire an additional 25% of Clark anytime after Oct. 26. At the same time, he said, Clark's minority holders can elect to purchase 10% of Horsham for $10 million. He said the net result would give Horsham a 75% interest in Clark, a St. Louis oil refiner and marketer.

Mr. Garbutt said that, under the option agreement, the price of the 25% Clark stake will be 10% below a "fair valuation" as determined by independent valuators. Further increases in Horsham's stake beyond 75% are subject to negotiation with the minority holders, he said.

Clark's minority holders are Anthony Novelly and Samuel Goldstein, principals of Clark's former parent, Apex Oil Co.
