Dillard to Buy B.A.T's Ivey For $110 Million

  • Author: Karen Blumenthal
  • Date: 05/07/90

LITTLE ROCK, Ark. -- Dillard Department Stores Inc. said it agreed to acquire J.B. Ivey & Co. from B.A.T Industries PLC for $110 million, completing B.A.T's planned sales of its U.S. retailing units. The agreement follows B.A.T's agreements last month to sell its Saks Fifth Avenue unit to Arab investors for about $1.5 billion and its Marshall Field's unit to Dayton Hudson Corp. for about $1.1 billion. The London-based conglomerate, which put the retailing properties up for sale last September to fend off a takeover bid by Sir James Goldsmith, will receive about $2.8 billion from selling the U.S. stores, when proceeds from the Ivey sale and the John Breuner Co. retail furniture and rental businesses are included.

Although some in the industry had estimated that B.A.T's U.S. retail properties could fetch as much as $3 billion, the company's total take is considered quite high in light of other retailers' current financial troubles.

For Dillard, the acquisition marks its first expansion into the Carolinas, where Ivey operates 13 stores, and gives the company an additional 10 stores in Florida, where it currently operates just one Dillard outlet. Although the department-store chain had looked at Saks and bid for Marshall Field's, analysts say the Ivey stores are the best fit for Dillard, which operates about 162 department stores, primarily in the Midwest and Southwest.

The stock market also apparently liked the purchase, as Dillard's stock climbed $2.50 Friday to close at $75.75 in New York Stock Exchange composite trading.

James Freeman, Dillard's chief financial officer, said that the Ivey stores have annual revenue of more than $300 million, largely from the sale of moderate to upscale apparel. He said that Dillard hasn't yet decided if it will convert Ivey stores into Dillard department stores.

Dillard, which grew by buying regional chains through the 1980s, will finance the transaction through internally generated funds and hopes to close the purchase as soon as possible after regulatory clearance is granted.

Meanwhile, the company is expected to continue looking for possible acquisitions. With Bloomingdale's off the auction block, Campeau Corp.'s Federated Stores Inc. is expected to try to sell other regional chains. And Dillard might want to add stores in Florida, or move into Georgia, where it doesn't currently have outlets.

"We look at everything that comes along," Mr. Freeman said.
