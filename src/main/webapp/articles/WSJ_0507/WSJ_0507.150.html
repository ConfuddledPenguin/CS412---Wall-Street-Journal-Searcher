Marketing & Media: ABC's `Twin Peaks' Receives Strong Rating

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/07/90

NEW YORK -- The fourth weekly episode of the ABC series "Twin Peaks" drew 19% of the TV audience, improving its chances of joining ABC's fall schedule. The seven-week trial series, co-created by film director David Lynch, has become a barometer of ABC's turnaround efforts and of the networks' willingness to gamble on "different" shows. "Twin Peaks" started strong and then faltered, raising doubts about whether ABC, owned by Capital Cities/ABC Inc., would pick up the series.

ABC Entertainment President Robert Iger said the latest rating "was the most significant number we've had. It's very positive, and it bodes well in terms of the future of the program." "Twin Peaks" in four weeks has fared well among two key targets for advertisers, drawing 24% of women ages 18 to 34 and 28% of women ages 35 to 49 who were watching television when it aired.

The series, about sordid secrets in a small town, has fared better than any of 14 shows to inhabit its time slot since 1985, said Lawrence Hyams, ABC's director of audience analysis. "Twin Peaks" increased its audience share from the previous week despite airing in a tough time slot, opposite NBC's hit comedy, "Cheers," which won 33% of viewers last week.
