GM to Engineer, Direct `Smart Car' Project

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/07/90

ORLANDO, Fla. -- General Motors Corp. has agreed to manage and engineer an $8 million Intelligent Vehicle/Highway System project. The Orlando Smart Car project is jointly funded by GM, the Federal Highway Administration, the Florida Department of Transportation and the American Automobile Association. The project will provide navigation assistance, real-time traffic information, route selection and other information for tourists and business travelers in the Orlando area. Operations are planned for early 1992.

The project should disclose the feasibility of such a system, user acceptance and possible reduction in travel times. Other areas to be studied include increased safety, fuel savings and improvements in air quality.

This project is similar to the $1.6 million Project Path Finder that involves GM, the California Department of Transportation and the Federal Highway Adminstration. It will begin in a few months.
