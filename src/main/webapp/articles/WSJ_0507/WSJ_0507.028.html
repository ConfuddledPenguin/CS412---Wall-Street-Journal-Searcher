International: IG Metall Union Gains Raise, Bonus for Workers

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/07/90

FRANKFURT -- West Germany's status as Europe's high-cost production site was garnished Friday when management was backed into a pact giving more money to 3.7 million metalworkers -- for less work. Negotiators for the IG Metall metalworkers union and employers in the bellwether district of Stuttgart settled on a reduction of the official workweek in stages by two hours to 35 hours by 1995. Metalworkers also will receive a 6% wage increase effective from April 1, plus a one-time bonus of 240 marks ($142.64) for each of the 700,000 workers in the region's metalworking, automotive and computer industries.

Representatives from the national organizations of the union and management later agreed to the basic terms of the Stuttgart agreement, assuring its adoption nationwide after other regional negotiators had been awaiting the outcome.

IG Metall, after staging a series of warning strikes last week, scheduled more work stoppages in the other districts for today to force quick replica agreements.
