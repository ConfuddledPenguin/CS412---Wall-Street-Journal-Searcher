Senate Panel to Study Firm's Possible Conflict In U.S.-Japan Accord

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/07/90

WASHINGTON -- The Senate Governmental Affairs Committee will investigate a potential conflict-of-interest involving a Washington consulting firm that simultaneously served Japanese utilities and advised the Energy Department on a U.S.-Japan energy agreement. "We are now learning that foreign and domestic commercial interests may have had a hand not only in negotiating the agreement, but in selling it on Capitol Hill," said Sen. John Glenn (D., Ohio), chairman of the panel. He cited a recent article in The Wall Street Journal.

The agreement lessened U.S. controls over future shipments of plutonium to Japan, material derived from U.S. nuclear fuel. The company, International Energy Associates Ltd., has admitted that while advising the U.S. on the accord, it provided newsletters to the Japanese utilities describing the internal administration fight over the agreement. The newsletters also discussed "anti-plutonium hard-liners," including Sen. Glenn, who were opposed to the pact.

The firm, which has since changed its name to Environmental & Energy Services Co., insists that it fully disclosed the newsletter and its contract with the Japanese utilities to the Energy Department, as it was required to do by federal law. Energy Department officials say they were surprised to learn about the newsletters.
