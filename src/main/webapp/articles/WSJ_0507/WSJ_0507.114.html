NCNB Unit To Be Lender For IBM Project

  • Author: Christi Harlan
  • Date: 05/07/90

DALLAS -- In a major coup for NCNB Texas National Bank, International Business Machines Corp. chose the NCNB Corp. unit to finance construction of a $150 million office complex in Austin, Texas. NCNB Texas beat out a large banking concern and two Japanese banks to make the loan, according to Ralph Carestio, an NCNB corporate executive vice president.

The bank, which has been dogged by its image as a tight-fisted outsider since it entered the Texas market in August 1988, touted the IBM deal as its largest real-estate loan to date and "an excellent example of Texans helping Texans."

But the loan appears nearly risk-free. IBM will be the sole tenant of the 1.1 million square feet of office space to be built in a joint venture with Prentiss Properties, a Dallas-based real-estate developer. The seven-building complex, which will be owned by the joint-venture partners, will house about 3,400 of IBM's Austin-based employees beginning in spring 1991.

"The lease term is long enough that we've got ample debt-service coverage for a long, long time," Mr. Carestio said. "It's as good as a real-estate loan gets."

Neither Mr. Carestio nor an IBM spokesman would specify the length of IBM's lease or other details of the financing.

But Mr. Carestio said the loan "is not uncharacteristic of what's going to be happening" in real-estate lending in Texas. "You're going to see more of these kinds of things," with tenants financing their own buildings in so-called "build-to-suit" developments, he said.

Similar office projects are planned in the Dallas area by J.C. Penney Co. and GTE Corp.

Even with known tenants, however, developers aren't finding eager lenders, said Mike Prentiss, president of Prentiss Properties. (The development company scored a coup of its own with the IBM deal, beating out Trammell Crow Co. to become IBM's partner.)

"Notwithstanding who the tenant may be, {lenders} are very reluctant to finance office buildings in Texas," Mr. Prentiss said.
