Letters to the Editor: Let's Face Facts About Apartheid

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/07/90

I appreciated the article "In Africa, Independence Is Far Cry From Freedom" by George B.N. Ayittey (editorial page, March 28). However, the emotional "bleeding heart" hype in "Troubled Township: South Africa Blacks Speak Freely Now, and Pressure Pretoria" (page one, April 4) perpetuates the myth that the Afrikaners and apartheid are the cause of all social, economic and political discrepancies in Southern Africa. It was nauseating. The world community, and the U.S., has to come to grips with the phenomenon and effects of international "apartheid" so that we can, one day, deal more effectively and sensibly with the separate and very unequal development of nations and peoples.

Casper H. Badenhorst, M.D.

Chicago
