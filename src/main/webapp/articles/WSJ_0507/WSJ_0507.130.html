Suzuki, GM Venture In Canada to Double Certain Car Production

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/07/90

TORONTO -- General Motors of Canada Ltd. said its joint venture with Suzuki Motor Co. of Japan will double production of its subcompact passenger cars with the start of a second work shift in September. GM Canada is a unit of General Motors Corp. The joint venture, CAMI Automotive Inc., with one plant in Ingersoll, Ontario, currently makes 60,000 subcompacts and 40,000 sport utility vehicles a year. A CAMI spokesman said subcompact production will rise to 120,000; and the number of CAMI employees will increase about 300, to 1,700 by November, when the added shift is expected to reach full production.

The subcompacts are currently marketed by GM as the Geo Metro in the U.S., and as the Chevrolet Sprint and Pontiac Firefly in Canada. The Suzuki version is marketed as the Swift in the U.S. and Canada.

The CAMI spokesman said the increased production is needed to meet demand for the subcompacts, particularly in Canada.
