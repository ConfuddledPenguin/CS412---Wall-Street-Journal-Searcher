Kemper Sets Charge Totaling $19.5 Million For Second Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/07/90

LONG GROVE, Ill. -- Kemper Corp. said it's establishing an after-tax charge of $19.5 million, or 35 cents a share, in its second quarter for its arbitration loss to a former executive of its Prescott, Ball & Turben unit. In last year's second quarter, the insurance and financial services concern earned $121.7 million, or $2.46 a share, including profit from discontinued operations of $67.2 million.

A panel of the National Association of Securities Dealers has awarded the former executive $38.2 million. The award stems from a disagreement over his employment contract, and is one of the largest such awards in the securities business.

Kemper said Prescott, a brokerage concern, will contest and try to vacate the award.
