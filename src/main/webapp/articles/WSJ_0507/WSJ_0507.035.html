North and South Yemen Reach Currency Accord

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/07/90

SANAA, North Yemen -- North and South Yemen announced Friday that their separate currencies will be accepted in each country, starting immediately. The decision, adopted at a meeting of prime ministers Abdel-Aziz Abdel-Ghani of the North and Yassine Saeed No'man of the South, is part of the speedy steps the two countries are taking as they move toward merger.

The merger, initially planned by the end of November, is expected to be advanced by six months and proclaimed on May 26, when the parliamentary bodies of the two countries hold a joint meeting in the South Yemeni capital of Aden.

South Yemen's currency is the dinar, equivalent to 0.343 of a U.S. dollar, and North Yemen's is the rial, with 12 rials to the dollar.
