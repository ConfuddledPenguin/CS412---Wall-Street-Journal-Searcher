Business Brief -- Valhi Inc.: Firm Says 1st-Quarter Net Fell 42%, Cites Proxy Fight

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/07/90

Valhi Inc. said first-quarter net income declined 42%, in part because of the expenses its NL Industries Inc. subsidiary incurred in fighting a proxy battle with Lockheed Corp. Valhi, the holding company controlled by Dallas investor Harold C. Simmons, posted net of $14.1 million, or 12 cents a share, down from $24.3 million, or 21 cents a share, a year earlier. The 1989 results included a $6.2 million pretax gain from the sale of assets.

The company, which owns 66% of NL, a Houston specialty chemical company, said results were hurt by $11 million in expenses related to its unsuccessful effort to unseat Lockheed's board, as well as by increased interest expenses from NL's 1989 Deutsche mark financing. Part of that financing was used to purchase shares in Lockheed.
