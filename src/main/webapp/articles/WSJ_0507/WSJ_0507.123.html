Toyota Is Raising Price of LS 400 Model By 2.9%, to $36,000

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/07/90

TORRANCE, Calif. -- Toyota Motor Corp.'s Lexus division said it is raising the base price of its popular LS 400 by $1,000, to $36,000. The 2.9% increase comes less than 10 months after Toyota introduced the luxury car to the U.S. market. The price of the LS 400's smaller and less successful sister car, the ES 250, will remain unchanged at a base price of $21,050.

While Toyota officials said the price increase was instituted partly to recover higher costs of doing business, the company is clearly seizing an opportunity to boost profits on a popular model. Toyota said it has less than a 15 days' supply of LS 400s in the U.S. Demand for the 250-horsepower sedan is accelerating, despite a sluggish overall market. Since February, the daily selling rate for the model has risen 21%, to 140 cars per day.

"We knew when we announced our prices last May, we'd have to evaluate our situation in about a year," said Dave Illingworth, vice president and general manager of the Lexus division.

Toyota officials stressed that the LS 400's base price is still thousands of dollars lower than competitive European models, such as the $45,950 Mercedes 300 E, as well as Nissan's $38,000 Infiniti Q45 luxury sedan.
