Woolworth April Sales Increased 21%, Boosted By Easter Shopping

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/07/90

NEW YORK -- Woolworth Corp.'s April sales soared 21%, because the period included Easter buying while the year-earlier period didn't. Domestic sales rose to $421 million in the four weeks ended April 28, compared with $348 million a year earlier. Equally impressive, domestic sales growth for stores open at least one year increased 18%.

"Our April sales were unusually positive because we had all of the Easter business, as opposed to last year, when Easter occurred in March," said Harold Sells, chairman and chief executive of the retailer. If March and April were taken as a single reporting period, he said, domestic sales were up 8.3% and domestic comparable store sales rose 5.4%.

Mr. Sells said that Woolworth was particularly strong in Easter candies and gifts, as well as merchandise related to the Simpsons, which are popular TV cartoon characters. Ninja turtles and Nintendo game cartridges were also in demand, said Mr. Sells. The apparel business was mixed. Men's apparel was strong, but children's apparel sales softened.

"About half of our Kids Mart children's stores are in Caifornia, and last year they had an unusual heat wave that benefited us tremendously," said Mr. Sells. "This year the weather was back to normal. We showed gains, but not as much as in men's wear."

For the 13 weeks ended April 28, domestic sales increased 9.7% to $1.23 billion against $1.12 billion in 1989.
