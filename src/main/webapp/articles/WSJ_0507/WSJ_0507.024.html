Neil Bush Plans to Testify On Failure of Denver S&L

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/07/90

WASHINGTON (AP) -- Neil Bush, the president's son, said he agreed to testify before the House Banking Committee about the failure of a Denver thrift on whose board he served. The panel, headed by Rep. Henry Gonzalez (D., Texas), is looking into the December 1988 collapse of Silverado Banking, Savings & Loan Association.

Thrift regulators are expected to testify May 22, while Mr. Bush and other Silverado board members are to appear May 23. "I have agreed to participate and will continue to cooperate with Congress, the Office of Thrift Supervision and any other agencies involved," Mr. Bush said in a statement.

The thrift office has alleged that loans by Silverado to Mr. Bush's business partners, developers Kenneth Good and Bill Walters, were a conflict of interest for Mr. Bush, who was on the S&L's board from October 1985 until August 1988.

In January, regulators began disciplinary procedures against Mr. Bush that could result in a lifetime ban from the financial-institutions industry. Mr. Bush has denied any wrongdoing.
