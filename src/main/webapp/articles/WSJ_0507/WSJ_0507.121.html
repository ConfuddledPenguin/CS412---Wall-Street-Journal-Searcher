Bernard Chaus Says Net Income Fell 74% In Fiscal 3rd Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/07/90

NEW YORK -- Bernard Chaus Inc. said net income in the third quarter ended March 31 fell 74%, reflecting higher-than-normal liquidation of merchandise not shipped to Campeau Corp. stores last January. The women's apparel maker's net fell to $182,000, or one cent a share, from $691,000, or four cents a share, a year earlier. Sales fell 10% to $71.4 million from $79.5 million.

On Friday, Chaus shares closed at $4.875, up 12.5 cents, in composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange.

The recent Chapter 11 bankruptcy-law filing of Campeau, parent of Federated Department Stores and Allied Stores, has hurt many apparel makers that do business with them. Campeau, based in Toronto, is a major client of Chaus.

First-nine-month net was $295,000, or two cents a share, compared with a year-earlier loss of $4.5 million, or 24 cents a share. Sales rose 3% to $221 million from $212.7 million.
