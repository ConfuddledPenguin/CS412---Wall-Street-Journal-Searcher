Jefferies Director Monks Doesn't Seek Re-Election

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/07/90

LOS ANGELES -- Robert A.G. Monks, a consultant to institutional shareholders, said he didn't stand for re-election to the board of Jefferies Group Inc. to avoid any appearance of a conflict-of-interest. "Since I'm trying to advise people how to behave themselves, I should set a good example myself," said Mr. Monks, president of Washington-based Institutional Shareholder Services Inc., which counsels clients on corporate governance issues.

Mr. Monks said he was criticized for his role in the recent proxy fight between Lockheed Corp. and Dallas investor Harold Simmons. Mr. Simmons retained Jefferies to solicit proxies for his unsuccessful bid to nominate a rival slate of directors, which Institutional Shareholders and Mr. Monks supported.

"Even though I wasn't profiting, {from the Simmons-Jefferies relationship} there was the thought that I was a director of a company that was profiting," Mr. Monks said. There was also a question of whether Institutional Shareholders' counsel to its clients was biased by the relationship.

Mr. Monks, 56 years old, was named to the Jefferies board last year. His departure reduces the board's size to seven members. Mr. Monks has agreed to head the brokerage firm's strategic advisory committee, which will meet several times a year. The position will be unpaid, he and Jefferies said.
