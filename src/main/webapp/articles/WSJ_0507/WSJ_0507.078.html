International: Soviets' Poultry Imports

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/07/90

WASHINGTON -- The Soviet Union has emerged as a major new market for broilers and may edge out Japan this year as the traditional No. 1 foreign market for U.S. poultry, according to U.S. Agriculture Department analysts. More than 110 million pounds of broiler leg quarters were sold to the Soviet Union recently by U.S. companies, a sale valued at an estimated $50 million.

The Soviets began buying U.S. broilers last fall for the first time in years. According to the department's Economic Research Service, the 1989 sales involved about 26 million pounds.
