Business Brief -- Armstrong World Industries Inc.: Second Phase of Land Sale Results in $40 Million Gain

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/15/90

Armstrong World Industries Inc. said it completed the second phase of the sale of Georgia woodlands to an undisclosed buyer, resulting in a pretax gain of $40 million for the second quarter. In the 1989 second quarter, the Lancaster, Pa., flooring and building products maker had profit from continuing operations of $44.6 million, or 95 cents a share. Net income was $47.9 million, or $1.02 a share.

The first part of the sale, completed March 30, resulted in a first-quarter gain of $16.5 million, or 40 cents a share. The second-quarter gain is expected to exceed that amount.

Armstrong said it sold a total of 89,000 acres of woodlands, which were a source of raw materials and fuel for the company's ceiling products. The company said it will fill its ceiling needs through other sources.

Corrections & Amplifications

ARMSTRONG WORLD INDUSTRIES Inc. completed the secon phase of its sale of woodlands in Georgia for about $4 million. In Friday's edition, the sale price was misidentified as a pretax gain.
