Applied Biosystems Expects Pretax Loss For 4th Fiscal Period

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/15/90

FOSTER CITY, Calif. -- Applied Biosystems Inc. said it expects to report a "modest" pretax loss for its fourth quarter ending June 30. It didn't specify the amount of the expected loss. The scientific-instruments maker said orders and revenue both have been below expectations. It said it is mulling "additional measures" to cut costs, but didn't say what those might be, either.

Applied Biosystems projected in January that it would have "negative earnings" in its fiscal year, compared with last fiscal year, when it earned $19.3 million, or $1.38 a share, on sales of $158.1 million. Still, the news reflects further tumult at the company.

The announcement came after the stock market had closed.

In national over-the-counter trading yesterday, Applied Biosystems' stock fell 25 cents to close at $14.

That was modest compared with the 38% dive Applied Biosystems' stock took in January, after the company reported second-quarter net income swooned 56%. In March, the company's president and chief executive officer, Irvin D. Smith, was apparently forced to resign.

Applied Bioysytems has been hurt by various economic factors, including the strength of the U.S. dollar, which has hurt overseas sales. But the company's credibility has also been hurt by management's earnings surprises for Wall Street; the second-quarter profit plunge, for example, was reported just two weeks after management had been telling analysts that the company was performing well.

Last year in the fourth quarter, Applied Biosystems had net income of $4.2 million, or 30 cents a share, on sales of $36.8 million. For the nine months ended March 30, the company had net income of $6.7 million, or 48 cents a share, on sales of $120.2 million.
