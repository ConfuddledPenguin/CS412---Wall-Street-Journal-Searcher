Exxon to Comply With Law On Air Pollution in N.J.

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/15/90

NEW YORK -- Exxon Corp., New York, said it agreed with the New Jersey Department of Environmental Protection to comply with state air pollution laws by October, or spend upwards of $30 million on new equipment. Under the pact, Exxon would reduce emissions from its Bayway refinery in Linden, N.J., to 30 pounds or less an hour, from current levels of about 45-to-70-pounds of mineral dusts, sulfuric acid and sulfates. About 4.2 million gallons of gasoline and 200,000 gallons of fuel oil are produced at the facility daily.

New Jersey state officials said that "Exxon's record of non-compliance with anti-pollution regulations dates back 10 years." State officials also said the oil concern has paid fines for non-compliance in the past two years.

A spokesman for the company confirmed that it has paid fines in the past for failure to comply, but said that the non-compliance was unavoidable and "no attempt was made at anytime to try to do anything surreptitiously." Exxon is currently performing maintenance work at the Bayway facility in an effort to meet "stringent New Jersey standards" by the October deadline.

The spokesman added that the company is prepared to design, engineer and construct within 2 1/2 years -- at a cost of over $30 million -- a new system that will meet New Jersey standards, if the company cannot renovate existing equipment to meet those standards.
