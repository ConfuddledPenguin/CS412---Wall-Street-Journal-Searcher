Gulf Canada Acquires Stake in Interhome From Imperial Oil

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/15/90

CALGARY, Alberta -- Gulf Canada Resources Ltd. said it completed the purchase of Imperial Oil Ltd.'s 22.8% stake in Interhome Energy Inc. for 492.4 million Canadian dollars (US$420.1 million), or C$54.50 an Interhome share. The purchase is the first step of a complex reshuffling of assets announced in April by Toronto's Reichmann family, which owns 74.3% of Gulf Canada through the family's real estate flagship, Olympia & York Developments Ltd.

Gulf Canada, a Calgary-based oil and gas producer, plans to resell the 22.8% Interhome stake to Toronto-based GW Utilities Ltd., thereby raising GW's interest in Interhome to 63.5% from 40.7%. Olympia & York owns 89.3% of GW Utilities, and plans to take it private under a previous tender offer for its remaining shares.

The Reichmanns' plans also call for Gulf Canada to merge with Calgary-based Interhome's Home Oil Co. unit, and for Interhome's remaining oil pipeline subsidiary, Interprovincial Pipe Line Ltd., to be spun off later this year to Interhome's shareholders. The proposals are subject to holder and other approvals.

Toronto-based Imperial Oil, a large integrated oil concern that is 70%-owned by Exxon Corp., New York, said previously it expects the sale of its Interhome stake to generate a net gain of about C$195 million, or C$1.03 a share.
