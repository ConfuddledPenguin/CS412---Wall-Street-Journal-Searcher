Business Brief -- Federal Express Corp.: French Company Acquired, Air Service Abroad Boosted

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/15/90

Federal Express Corp., in moves to bolster its overseas operations, said it acquired a French trucking company and initiated air service that connects Asia, the Middle East, Europe and the U.S. Federal Express, the giant Memphis, Tenn., air-express carrier, said it is buying Transports Transvendeens Chronoservice, which has annual revenue of about 178 million francs ($31.2 million). Terms of the purchase weren't disclosed.

Federal said the company employs more than 400 people in 14 facilities in France. It has 250 trucks and vans and operates a next-day trucking service as well as an international and domestic express business.

The expanded air service, Federal said, will originate three times a week in Taipei, Taiwan. The company has previously provided direct service between Asia, the U.S. and Europe, but had never provided scheduled flights connecting Asia, the Middle East and Europe.
