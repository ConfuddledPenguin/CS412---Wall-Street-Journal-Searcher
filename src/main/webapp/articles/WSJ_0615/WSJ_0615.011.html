Spelling Reports Net Fell 64% to $954,000 In Fiscal 3rd Period

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/15/90

LOS ANGELES -- Spelling Entertainment Inc. said fiscal third-quarter net income fell nearly 64%, to $954,000, or three cents a share, from $2.6 million, or nine cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue declined 40% to $37.4 million from $62.1 million. Operating profit, according to the entertainment production and distribution company, slipped slightly to $5.3 million from $5.9 million.

Jules Haimovitz, Spelling's president and chief operating officer, attributed the lower net to increased interest and purchase-price amortization resulting from the company's acquisition of Worldvision in March 1989.

Aaron Spelling, chairman and chief executive officer, noted that the highly successful debut of the ABC-TV show "Twin Peaks" in April prompted the network to renew the program for the fall season. In addition, 13 episodes of a new half-hour series, "American Chronicles," produced by David Lynch and Mark Frost, was ordered by Fox Broadcasting Co. for airing in the fall. Spelling's Worldvision subsidiary has acquired the world-wide distribution rights for both of these properties.

In the first nine months, ended April 30, net was $5.4 million, or 13 cents a share, vs. $9 million, or 41 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue increased 19% to $119.2 million from $99.9 million.
