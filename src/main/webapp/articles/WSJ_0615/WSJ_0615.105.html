Bush Officials Plan to Send Japanese Letter on Talks

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/15/90

WASHINGTON -- Senior members of the Bush administration plan to send their Japanese counterparts a letter aimed at boosting the stalled talks on the bilateral economic relationship, administration officials said. The letter, which apparently is to be signed by Secretary of State James Baker, Treasury Secretary Nicholas Brady, Commerce Secretary Robert Mosbacher, U.S. Trade Representative Carla Hills, Council of Economic Advisers Chairman Michael Boskin, and a senior Justice Department official, is expected to be mailed early next week, the same officials said.

The talks, being carried out under the Structural Impediments Initiative, are stalled on several issues, most importantly on Japanese spending on infrastructure. The U.S. seeks a boost in Japanese infrastructure investment to 9% of gross national product from 6.3% of GNP currently. The Japanese maintain this would actually be an increase to 10% from 7.3%, as the U.S. figures don't include investment in telecommunications and some other investments.

There also are important differences between U.S. and Japanese negotiators over penalties for violations of Japan's antitrust rules, and over how to monitor enactment of the SII recommendations.

The present plan is to mail the letter to six Japanese ministers, the four chief Japanese negotiators in the SII and the Japanese Fair Trade Commission.
