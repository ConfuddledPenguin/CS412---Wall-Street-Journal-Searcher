Business Brief -- Western Union Corp.: SEC Approves Firm's Plan To Make Exchange Offer

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/15/90

Western Union Corp. got a green light from the Securities and Exchange Commission to proceed with its exchange offer aimed at averting bankruptcy court. However, SEC approval came as the beleaguered communications and financial services company said it will technically default on a $51 million semiannual interest payment due today. A spokesman said in New York that under the debt agreement, it still has 30 days to make the payment.

The company has said that current cash flow can't cover its payments on $643 million in debt and that it will be forced to seek protection under the U.S. Bankruptcy Code if it can't work out a new payment schedule with its bondholders.

Western Union wants to issue new debt securities to holders of its 19.25% senior secured reset notes due Dec. 15, 1992 and 16% notes due June 15, 1991. Debt from these notes totals $530 million. Western Union is also seeking to reclassify its preferred stock into new common shares.
