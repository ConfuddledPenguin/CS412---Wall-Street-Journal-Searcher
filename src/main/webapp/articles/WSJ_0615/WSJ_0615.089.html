REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: Tax Revolt Note

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/15/90

Meanwhile, a small piece of evidence has emerged to challenge Prop. 111's supposed lesson that voters will approve a tax if they know it will go for a specific program. This week North Dakotans voted 59% against a temporary, one-cent sales-tax increase to fund schools. Earmarking the money for education didn't help -- last December North Dakotans turned down a non-earmarked sales-tax hike by a narrower 53% margin. The real lesson may be that voters won't support tax increases for programs that they are convinced fail to deliver value for money. {See related story: "REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: After 111, the Deluge?" -- WSJ June 15, 1990}
