REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: After 111, the Deluge?

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/15/90

It has taken less than a week after Californians voted narrowly to raise the gasoline tax with Prop. 111 for the state's top Democrats to go whole-hog on taxes. As the cornerstone of a $2.3 billion tax package (the Gann spending limit won't allow any more than that), the Democratic chairmen of the Legislature's fiscal committees want to raise the upper income-tax bracket to 11% from 9.3%. Apparently, revenue growth of 7% annually hasn't kept pace with spending, so there's a big deficit and the liberal reflex is to try to soak many of the state's most productive residents further. Even after Prop. 111's win, however, the Big Democratic Tax Sponge may find California a pretty dry state. {See related story: "REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: Tax Revolt Note" -- WSJ June 15, 1990}
