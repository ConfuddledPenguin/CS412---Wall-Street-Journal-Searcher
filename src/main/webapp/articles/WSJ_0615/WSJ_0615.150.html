Business Brief -- Cetus Corp.: Biotech Firm Says Du Pont Is Infringing on Patent

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/15/90

Cetus Corp., Emeryville, Calif., said it filed suit against Du Pont Co., alleging patent infringement. The suit was filed in federal court in San Francisco. Like another suit Cetus has pending against Du Pont, the action alleges that Du Pont is selling products that infringe on Cetus's patented polymerase chain-reaction technology.

Considered a significant scientific advance, the technology allows the rapid multiplication of gene sequences and is used in molecular biology research and diagnostics, among other areas.

The suit seeks to prohibit Du Pont from infringing on Cetus' patents and asks for unspecified damages.

Cetus is a biotechnology company that develops health care products. Du Pont also manufactures biomedical products.

A spokesman for Du Pont, based in Wilmington, Del., said it doesn't believe Cetus's patents are valid, and said Du Pont intends to continue selling the products targeted by Cetus's new suit.
