SCEcorp Unit Ends Contract for Work On Three Power Plants

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/15/90

IRVINE, Calif. -- Mission Power Engineering Co., a unit of SCEcorp., said it terminated its contract for the engineering and construction of three geothermal power generating plants in the Coso and China Lake areas of California, which are owned and operated by California Energy Co., San Francisco. "We've just walked off the job," said Jack Adrian, Mission Power's president and chief executive officer.

The termination of the contract follows Mission Power's filing of a lawsuit on June 5 against California Energy and its partners in the $300 million-plus project. The complaint, filed in state court in Inyo County, accuses California Energy of making changes in the scope of the work, triggering serious delays, and refusing to pay for additional costs.

Mission Power is seeking more than $79 million in the action, plus interest and damages for alleged fraud and misrepresentation by California Energy.

Michael Heys, California Energy's president, maintained yesterday that his company has appropriately compensated Mission Power. "There is nothing due on contractural work," he said. "In fact, there has been an overpayment of $170,000."

Mr. Heys added that the power plants at the center of the dispute are "substantially complete" and, therefore, Mission Power's walkout will not cause "an interruption of operations."
