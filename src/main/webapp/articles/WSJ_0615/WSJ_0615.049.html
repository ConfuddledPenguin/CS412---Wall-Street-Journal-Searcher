Who's News: Westinghouse Chief Paul Lego Announces Management Changes

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/15/90

PITTSBURGH -- Westinghouse Electric Corp.'s new chief executive, Paul E. Lego, announced a series of top management changes, but he didn't signal who will emerge as his heir apparent. Mr. Lego, currently president, assumes the additional posts of chairman and chief executive officer on July 1.

He selected as his deputy Theodore Stern, currently executive vice president, Energy and Utility Systems, who becomes senior executive vice president. Mr. Stern, who was recently named a Westinghouse director, is 60 years old, the same age as Mr. Lego, making it unlikely that his promotion would put him in position to succeed to the top job.

John B. Yasinsky, already an executive vice president, picked up most of Mr. Stern's former responsibilities -- running the turbine and generator, nuclear power systems and waste-to-energy plant operations. Mr. Yasinsky, 51, moves to executive vice president, Power Systems. He formerly was responsible for the World Resources and Technology group.

Maurice C. Sardi, 55, executive vice president, Commercial, will become executive vice president, Commercial and Corporate Resources. He already had line responsibility for a number of diversified businesses ranging from office furniture to beverage bottling. Under the new arrangement, he assumes responsibility for real estate development, government affairs and the research center.

Gary M. Clark, 54, executive vice president, Industries, will add the Westinghouse Electric Supply operation and Offshore Manufacturing to his group. He already oversees a hazardous waste cleanup business and refrigeration equipment for trucks, among other businesses.

Burton B. Staniar, 48, chairman of Westinghouse Broadcasting Co., the company's radio and television arm, will pick up the Longines-Wittnauer Watch Co. unit and the Westinghouse Communications business.

Warren H. Hollinshead, 54, vice president and treasurer, will become vice president and deputy, finance, making him the likely successor to Harry F. Murray, executive vice president, finance, who plans to retire at year end.
