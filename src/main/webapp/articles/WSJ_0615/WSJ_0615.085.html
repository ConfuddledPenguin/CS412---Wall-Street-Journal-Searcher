Who's News: Bramalea Is Expected To Appoint Texan To Two Top Posts

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/15/90

TORONTO -- The board of Bramalea Ltd. is expected to announce after its meeting this morning that Benjamin Swirksy will resign as president and chief executive, according to a source close the company. It is expected that Mr. Swirsky's replacement will be Marvin Marshall, president and chief executive of Cullen Center Inc., of Houston, Texas, an office complex owned by Trizec Corp., of Calgary, Alberta, the source said.

Bramalea declined to comment, and Mr. Marshall couldn't be reached for comment, although a source indicated that he is in Toronto today.

At the board meeting, Mr. Swirsky is expected to be named vice chairman of Bramalea. He will remain a director and a member of its executive committee, but he won't have an active role in the company's day-to-day operations. It is expected that Mr. Swirsky will be appointed to the board of Carena Developments Ltd., and have a senior advisory role with that concern.

Bramalea is a real estate concern based in Toronto. It is 66%-owned by Trizec, which is controlled by Toronto financiers Peter and Edward Bronfman through Carena. Trizec also is 36%-owned by the Reichmann family's Olympia & York Developments Ltd., of Toronto.

Analysts view Mr. Marshall's appointment to the head of Bramalea as a sign that Trizec will exert more influence on Bramalea's business decisions. From his experience in Houston, Mr. Marshall knows how to operate in a tough real estate market, and Bramalea likely will need that kind of management, analysts said.
