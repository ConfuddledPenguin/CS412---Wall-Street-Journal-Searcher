`Peace Talkers' Jam Lines In Calls to the White House

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/15/90

WASHINGTON -- Thousands of callers accepted Secretary of State James Baker's invitation to call the White House when they get "serious about peace" in the Middle East, jamming the phone lines. Mr. Baker precipitated the onslaught at a House Foreign Relations Committee hearing, when he told the new Israeli government, led by Prime Minister Yitzhak Shamir, that Israelis must compromise if peace efforts are to succeed. Mr. Baker said, "Everybody over there should know that the telephone number is 1-202-456-1414. When you're serious about peace, call us."

About 6,000 to 8,000 individuals "on all sides of the issue," according to White House spokesman Marlin Fitzwater, took Mr. Baker's invitation to heart and began calling the White House switchboard Wednesday night and into yesterday morning. At one point, the calls caused operators to turn to an automatic response tape.

Mr. Fitzwater said Mr. Baker's remarks shouldn't be construed as blaming the Israelis solely for the slow pace of peace talks. "In the final analysis, the parties of the region have to want peace for it to happen," he said. "And we can't want it for them."

The spokesman also said, "The White House operators can expect a big Christmas present this year from the State Department." But Mr. Baker apparently felt December would be too late, and sent roses yesterday.
