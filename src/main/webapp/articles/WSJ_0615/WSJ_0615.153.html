Business Brief -- Westinghouse Electric Corp.: Commercial Paper Program To Finance U.K. Activities

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/15/90

Westinghouse Credit Corp. of Pittsburgh said it established a 250 million pound sterling ($425 million) commercial paper program to fund U.K. business finance activities of its affiliate, Westinghouse International Capital Ltd. Westinghouse Credit, the finance arm of Westinghouse Electric Corp., said this is the first time it has directly tapped the U.K. short-term debt market. The capital raised will provide corporate and real estate financing to companies in the U.K.

Barclays de Zoete Webb Ltd. arranged the commercial paper program, Westinghouse said. Barclays and NatWest Capital Markets Ltd. will be dealers for the program.

The commercial paper is rated single-A-1 by Standard & Poor's Corp. and P-1 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. The notes will be denominated in pounds sterling or, in some cases, in Eurodollars, and will carry maturities of seven days to 365 days, Westinghouse said.

The securities haven't been registered with the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission and aren't available for sale to U.S. investors.
