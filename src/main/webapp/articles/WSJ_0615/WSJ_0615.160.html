Business Brief -- Wheeling-Pittsburgh Steel Corp.: Concern Reaches Settlement On Salaried Pension Plans

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/15/90

Wheeling-Pittsburgh Steel Corp. said it reached a settlement with the Pension Guaranty Corp., the Salaried Committee and all creditors' committees that will resolve claims arising from the 1985 termination of the company's salaried pension plans. Under the agreement, Wheeling-Pittsburgh, which is operating under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code, will buy annuities to cover a portion of the benefits previously provided under the terminated plans for salaried employees who retired before April 17, 1989, or who were eligible for pension as of Nov. 8, 1985 even if they retire after April 17, 1989.

In addition, the Wheeling, W. Va., company said it will establish a plan to provide disability retirement benefits and spousal death benefits for all current salaried employees and all those receiving pension benefits.

The company said the agreement is a "major breakthrough" in the process of reorganization, but didn't estimate the cost of the programs.
