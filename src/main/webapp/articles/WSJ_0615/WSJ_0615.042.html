Marketing & Media: Wall Street Journal Wins Award for Drug Coverage

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/15/90

PHILADELPHIA -- The National Association of Addiction Treatment Providers presented The Wall Street Journal with a special award for coverage of drug issues. The association of 650 private chemical dependency treatment programs cited the Journal "for its outstanding series, `Addiction -- America's Drug Crisis,'" published last summer, and "for its continued, enlightened editorial focus on alcoholism and drug dependency." The three-part series consisted of: "Stolen Future," by Ronald G. Shafer of the Journal's Washington bureau about the drug-related death of his son, Ryan; "Baffling Plague," by Joseph Pereira of the Boston bureau about addiction; and "Lifelong Struggle," by Kenneth H. Bacon in Washington about a recovering drug addict and alcoholic.

The award was accepted for the Journal by Mr. Shafer at the association's national convention in Philadelphia. The group is based in Irvine, Calif.
