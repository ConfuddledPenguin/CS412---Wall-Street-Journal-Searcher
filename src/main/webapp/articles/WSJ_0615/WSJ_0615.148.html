Business Brief -- Lone Star Industries Inc.: Sale Set of Preferred Stock Held in Tarmac's U.S. Unit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/15/90

Lone Star Industries Inc. said it agreed to sell back to Tarmac PLC the entire issue of preferred stock it holds in Tarmac's U.S. unit for $147 million. Lone Star, a Stamford, Conn., cement and construction materials producer, said the sale to Tarmac America Inc. is part of a continuing restructuring. James E. Stewart, chairman and chief executive officer, said in a statement that the sale of the Tarmac America cumulative preferred issue and other recent restructuring actions will result in "the realization of over $325 million, the great majority of which will be in cash." A spokesman said the preferred resale was done because Lone Star needs to raise cash.

The company said the buy-back by Tarmac America is at 78% of the preferred's par value. A spokesman said Lone Star had been paid the preferred by Tarmac in exchange for some of its North American operations.

The transaction is subject to creation of a new class of preference stock that Tarmac shareholders will be asked to approve at a special meeting scheduled July 20, Lone Star said. Tarmac is a British homebuilder and construction-materials supplier.
