Business Brief -- Greater New York Savings Bank: Board Adopts Rights Plan To Block Hostile Takeovers

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/15/90

Greater New York Savings Bank, New York, said its board adopted a shareholder rights plan to prevent a hostile takeover. The rights plan goes into effect if a holder acquires 20% or more of Greater New York's shares, and would entitle other holders to purchase common with a value of twice the right's exercise price.

In a letter to shareholders, Charles J. Ohlig, chairman of the thrift, said the plan isn't intended to prevent an acquisition on terms that are favorable to all shareholders.

Greater New York last year adopted a leveraged employee stock ownership plan to increase the cost of an unwanted takeover proposal by Emigrant Savings Bank. Greater New York, with assets of $3 billion and deposits of $2.2 billion, has offices in the metropolitan area.

In national over-the-counter trading yesterday, Greater New York shares closed at $6.375, down 37.5 cents.
