Marketing & Media: GAO Says Cable-TV Rates Increased 10% in 1989

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/15/90

WASHINGTON -- The General Accounting Office found that monthly cable-television rates rose 10% in 1989, roughly double the pace of inflation -- a finding that is certain to strengthen lawmakers' resolve to pass legislation re-regulating cable. The study is a companion to a survey a year ago that showed a 29% increase in the average cable subscriber's monthly fees for the lowest-priced service between December 1986 and October 1988.

The GAO, Congress's auditing arm, said that monthly fees for both the lowest-priced service and the most popular basic service increased an average of 10%, while cable operators added only one or two channels. The consumer price index for the year rose about 5%. The average cost of the lowest-priced cable service rose to $15.95 for 31 channels from $14.50 for 30 channels, and the most popular basic service rose to $16.33 for 34 channels from $14.91 for 32 channels, the GAO said. The study also showed a 5% increase in revenue per subscriber in 1989.

The study was prepared for the House Telecommunications and Finance Subcommittee, which plans to vote on cable re-regulation legislation this month.

The National Cable Television Association said the GAO report "reconfirms that the average cable subscriber's monthly bill increased at about the rate of inflation in 1989, and is still further evidence that cable rate increases have leveled off."
