Hong Kong's Jobless Rate

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/15/90

HONG KONG -- The jobless rate in the three months ended April 30 rose to a seasonally adjusted 1.8%, its highest level since mid-1988, from 1.5% a year earlier and 1.3% in the three months ended Jan. 31, according to government data. The figures bolster a trend that government officials have predicted would stem from the colony's economic slowdown that began in last year's second half. Last month's statistics showed first-quarter unemployment at 1.7%. The provisional unemployment rate for the three months ended May 31 indicated a decline in unemployment to 1.6%.

Economists are generally hoping for a rising jobless rate, because it could indicate a slowing in local inflationary pressure, most of which stems from spiraling wages.
