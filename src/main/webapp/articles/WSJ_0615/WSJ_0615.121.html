Who's News: Chock Full O' Nuts Appoints Marvin Haas As a Vice Chairman

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/15/90

NEW YORK -- Chock Full O' Nuts Corp. named 47-year-old Marvin Haas a vice chairman of the coffee and food-service company. The company's other vice chairman is George Rudy, who is 77. Leon Pordy, Chock Full's 70-year-old chairman and chief executive officer, said that he has no plans to retire and that Mr. Haas is "moving up the ladder." Mr. Haas has been overseeing the Greenwich Mills food-service division in Secaucus, N.J., and "and he's done very well," Dr. Pordy said.

"We've really turned {the company} around," Dr. Pordy said. "We've got earnings coming out this year that are probably the highest in 10 years."

For many years, Chock Full O' Nuts concentrated on retail coffee sales, and Dr. Pordy said it now is No. 4 in that industry behind Philip Morris Cos.' Kraft General Foods, which produces Maxwell House; Procter & Gamble Co., with its Folger's brand; and Nestle S.A. But Chock Full now is also one of the largest institutional food-service firms east of the Mississippi River, he said.

In November 1988, the company acquired Jimbo's Jumbos Inc., a processor and packager of peanuts, and "that's been a very successful enterprise," Dr. Pordy said.

The balance sheet "looks good," he added. The company has just $45 million of 8% bonds and $55 million of 7% bonds outstanding, and $40 million in cash.
