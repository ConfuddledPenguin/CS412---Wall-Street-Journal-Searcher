World Bank Affiliate Clears Loan to China To Assist Farmers

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/15/90

WASHINGTON -- The International Development Association, a World Bank affiliate, authorized a $150 million loan to China to help farmers increase production of food crops, fruit, livestock and shrimp and fish on farm-type ponds. The World Bank also is considering other loans to China, but has been forced to delay action on several, including a proposed $150 million loan to help finance transportation projects. In the wake of China's crackdown in June 1989 on pro-democracy demonstrators in Beijing and other Chinese cities, the U.S. and other member countries of the World Bank have blocked approval of some loans to China. However, the U.S. has said it wouldn't oppose approval of loans that serve "humanitarian" purposes.

A senior World Bank official said the approval of the IDA credit to China brings the total newly authorized World Bank credits to that country to $590 million during the bank's current fiscal year, which ends June 30.

Separately, the World Bank approved a $385 million loan to Eletrobras, Brazil's national power company, to improve electric power transmission networks of nine regional utilities serving many parts of the country.

Meanwhile, the World Bank said it will guarantee the principal on a $100 million fixed-rate, 15-year bond issue of India's Housing Development Finance Corp., to be placed in the U.S. market by Salomon Brothers, a New York investment banking company.

The bank said this was its first such guarantee under its expanded co-financing program, which is intended to help eligible borrowing countries obtain financing in a broad range of financial markets.
