Business Brief -- Canadian Pacific Ltd.: Michael DeGroote, Director, Sells Most of His Holdings

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/15/90

Michael DeGroote, chairman and chief executive of Laidlaw Inc. sold all but 2,000 shares of his 1.9% stake in Canadian Pacific Ltd. Canadian Pacific is a Montreal-based industrial holding concern, which owns one of Canada's two railroads and also has interests in the energy and telecommunications industries. Laidlaw is a transportation and waste-management concern based in Burlington, Ontario.

RBC Dominion Securities Inc. and Gordon Capital Corp. purchased a total of 6,104,467 shares from Mr. DeGroote for distribution to buyers on the Toronto and Montreal stock exchanges at 22 Canadian dollars (US$18.77) a share.

In trading on the Toronto Stock Exchange, Canadian Pacific closed at C$21.75, down 75 Canadian cents.

Mr. DeGroote acquired a total of 12.1 million shares in Canadian Pacific when he sold it his controlling stake in Laidlaw.

Neither Mr. DeGroote nor other Laidlaw officials could be reached for comment.
