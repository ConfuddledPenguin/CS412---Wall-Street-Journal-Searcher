French Steel Firm Makes Bid

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/15/90

PARIS -- The state-owned French steel producer Usinor Sacilor S.A. said it is launching a bid for 20% of the shares outstanding of the steel trading company ASD PLC, a 77%-owned unit of Stemcor PLC, in cooperation with the British concern. The French company plans a joint venture with ASD that will set up a steel-plate trading center outside London. Usinor will own 49% of the venture, and ASD 51%. The project is subject to approval by European Community authorities in Brussels.

Usinor said the joint concern will boost ASD's steel trading revenue, which was #205 million ($350.9 million) in 1989. ASD markets about 500,000 metric tons of steel a year from 20 offices throughout Britain, making it the second-largest British steel trading concern. A metric ton is equal to about 2,204.62 pounds.
