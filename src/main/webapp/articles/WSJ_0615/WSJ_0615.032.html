Initial State Jobless Claims Fell in Week Ended June 2

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/15/90

WASHINGTON -- Initial claims for regular state unemployment benefits fell to a seasonally adjusted 351,000 in the week ended June 2 from 356,000 in the previous week, the Labor Department said. The number of people receiving regular state benefits in the week ended May 26 was a seasonally adjusted 2,400,000, or 2.3% of those covered by unemployment insurance, up from 2,397,000 in the previous week, when the insured unemployment rate also was 2.3%.

Counting all state and federal benefit programs, the number of people receiving unemployment benefits in the week ended May 26 fell to 2,194,800 from 2,246,900 a week earlier. These figures aren't seasonally adjusted.
