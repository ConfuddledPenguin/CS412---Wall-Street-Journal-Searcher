Additional Taxes Are Sought by IRS From Japanese Firm

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/15/90

TOKYO -- At least one major Japanese electronics company has received a request from the U.S. Internal Revenue Service to pay additional taxes, and others are expecting similar notice soon. The U.S. government has long alleged that Japanese companies are avoiding U.S. taxes by charging their subsidiaries too much for certain goods, such as semiconductors and videocassette recorders -- an action that depresses the stated profits of the American units.

Matsushita Electric Industrial Co. has received a request, and is "cooperating" with the U.S. government, according to an official company statement. The company added, however, that no bilateral standard exists for "transfer prices" and that the matter was still under discussion between the U.S. and Japanese governments. A spokesman refused to disclose the amount of the tax request, or when it was made.

The Nihon Keizai Shimbun, in Friday's editions, reported that Matsushita was asked to pay an added 10 billion yen ($64.6 million) in taxes; Toshiba Corp. was asked to pay 30 billion yen more, and Hitachi Ltd. 10 billion yen more.

The Matsushita spokesman said the figure reported for that company was "too high." Toshiba officials said they hadn't yet received any official request, but that they have been under investigation by the IRS for transfer pricing questions since 1985. A Hitachi spokesman said the company has been under investigation since 1986, but that it hadn't received any requests for additional taxes.

Sony Corp. said it had received requests for additional information to the IRS about a month ago, and is still working to supply the requested material.
