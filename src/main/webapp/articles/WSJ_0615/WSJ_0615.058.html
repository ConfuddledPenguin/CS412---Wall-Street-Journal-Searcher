Sweden's Consumer Prices Rise

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/15/90

STOCKHOLM -- Sweden's consumer prices in May rose 0.6% from April and 10.2% from a year earlier, Statistics Sweden, the national statistics agency, said, citing mainly alcohol, tobacco and food price increases. Consumer prices shot up 7% in the first five months of this year, the agency said, and 2.9 percentage points of the increase resulted from the major tax overhaul being gradually introduced.

Sweden's annualized rate of inflation rose to 10.2% in May from 10.1% in April, and was up from the 6.6% rate registered in May last year.

The national Price and Competition Office predicted consumer prices will increase at a rate of 10% this year.

Sweden's consumer price index, based on 1980 prices equalling 100, was measured at 206.4 in May, up from 205.2 in April.
