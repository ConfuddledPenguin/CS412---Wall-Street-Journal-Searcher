Politics & Policy: Greenspan Says Rate Drop Would Follow Deficit Pact

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/15/90

WASHINGTON -- Federal Reserve Board Chairman Alan Greenspan told Congress that he would expect a "significant" drop in interest rates if the administration and Congress agree on large deficit-reduction package. Mr. Greenspan's comments came just four days after the chairman of the president's Council of Economic Advisers, Michael Boskin, said it would be "irresponsible" for the Fed not to cut rates in the wake of a budget deal. Administration officials have worried recently that Mr. Greenspan might be unwilling, even after a budget deal is reached, to cut interest rates far enough or fast enough to prevent economic problems.

In testimony to the House Banking Committee, the Fed chairman carefully avoided promising any action by the central bank. Rather, he argued a "credible" budget deal would have a direct effect on the financial markets. "There would be a significant effect on long-term interest rates," he said, "and through arbitrage this will affect the short end of the markets as well."

He stressed that the budget deal must be "credible," saying: "If it's merely bookkeeping measures, I think the market will yawn -- appropriately."
