Who's News: Vitalink Chairman Resigns Top Positions; Stock Shows 25% Loss

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/15/90

FREMONT, Calif. -- Vitalink Communications Corp. said George Archuleta, chairman, chief executive officer and president, resigned. In national over-the-counter trading, Vitalink plunged $3.25 to close at $9.625, a 25% loss, making it the biggest percentage loser on the OTC market for the day.

Mr. Archuleta also resigned as a director. The company said he will be succeeded as chief executive by Donald J. Herman, a director, who will serve until a permanent replacement for Mr. Archuleta can be found.

The company gave no reason for Mr. Archuleta's departure. However, Mr. Herman said the company sees "a reasonable possibility" that its earnings won't meet analysts' estimates of between 21 cents and 28 cents a share for its fiscal third quarter, ending June 30.

The news of Mr. Archuleta's resignation and Mr. Herman's forecast combined to batter the stock of the data communications products firm.

Several large blocks of Vitalink stock changed hands on the day. The trading volume was 3.5 million shares.

Vitalink's stock has been sliding steadily from a 52-week high of $20.375 in October. Analysts have said that the company is being hurt by flagging sales under an agreement with Digital Equipment Corp., which resells Vitalink's remote bridge products, used to connect local area communications networks. Analysts say a different product, known as routers, is gaining favor as a network connector, at the expense of remote bridges.

In the six months ended March 31, Vitalink earned $8.8 million, or 62 cents a share, on sales of $36.9 million.
