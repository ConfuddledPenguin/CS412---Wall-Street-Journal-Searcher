What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/15/90

S&P LOWERED its ratings on debt issued by Chrysler and Ford, and affirmed ratings for GM. The agency, which cited growing competition and soft auto demand, downgraded Ford's senior debt to double-A-minus and Chrysler's to triple-B-minus, one notch above speculative grade. ---

A serious credit crunch extends well beyond the real estate industry and appears to be worsening, Commerce Secretary Mosbacher said.

---

Reebok said its biggest shareholder, Pentland Group of London, plans to sell its 31.5% stake. The sneaker company promptly adopted a "poison pill" takeover defense.

---

Gold prices tumbled on Saudi sales of the metal and the prospect of sales by the Soviets to raise cash. Central banks swooped into the market with big buy orders to halt the decline.

Stock prices eased amid growing concern about corporate earnings, while bonds gained. The Dow Jones industrials fell 1.48 to 2928.22.

---

The Soviet Union's economic uncertainty is hindering the country's efforts to secure new Western loans as wary British, Swiss and German bankers demand steeply higher rates.

---

CBS and ABC adopted NBC's plan to reduce the audience size that the network guarantees advertisers.

---

The Big Board's acceleration of plans for off-hours stock trading was triggered by fears about competing efforts by over-the-counter dealers and by the Big Board's own members.

---

Producer prices rose 0.3% in May, reversing three months of decline. The report suggests that inflation is likely to continue at a moderate pace.

---

Orix won the rights to buy 74 Airbus planes that Braniff had ordered, and plans to becomes Japan's first aircraft-leasing concern.

---

Southland sweetened its exchange offer to bondholders by about $200 million. The plan would slash the Thompson family's equity stake.

---

The thrift bailout will require an additional $30 billion to $50 billion in funding next year, Brady projected, generating criticism from Democrats.

The Resolution Trust Corp. can override state laws to permit the sale of failed S&Ls to banks, a judge ruled.

---

The Senate approved a resolution to cap fiscal 1991 discretionary spending at this year's level.

---

Philips warned that second-quarter profit will fall "appreciably" and that full-year results will be "very low." The Dutch electronics giant is in the midst of a management shake-up.

---

U.S. auto makers intend to boost output in the third quarter, despite disappointing sales. The plans reflect lean inventories and labor concerns.

---

Honeywell petitioned the government to halt imports of Minolta automatic-focusing cameras, saying they violate the U.S. company's patents.

---

A $55 billion farm bill cleared a House panel. It includes a new policy on soil and water conservation.

---
