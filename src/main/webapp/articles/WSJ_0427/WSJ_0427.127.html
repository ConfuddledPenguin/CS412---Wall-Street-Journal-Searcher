Hewlett-Packard Co.

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/27/90

PALO ALTO, Calif. -- Hewlett-Packard Co. said that 782 employees, or just under 1% of its work force, accepted an early retirement package as part of the company's previously announced plan to trim expense growth. The company, which makes computers and test and measurement equipment, said the plan was available on a voluntary basis to every employee at least 55 years old with 15 or more years of service with the company. Up to 2,400 workers were eligible for the early retirement package.

Employees who participated in the program received an amount equal to one-half month's salary for each year of service, up to a maximum of 12 months' salary. In addition, retirees received the funds that had accumulated in their accounts under the company's regular retirement plans.

The company has 94,000 employees world-wide.
