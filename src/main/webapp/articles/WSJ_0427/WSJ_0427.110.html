Thursday's Markets: Stocks Post Small Gain; Bonds Slip --- Broader Stock Indexes Display Weakness; Dollar Strengthens

  • Author: Douglas R. Sease
  • Date: 04/27/90

Stock prices rose modestly even as the ailing bond market took another turn for the worse that pushed the yield on the benchmark 30-year Treasury bond above 9%. The Dow Jones Industrial Average rose 10.14 points to 2676.58 in moderate trading. But broader market averages didn't perform as well. Analysts also noted that more issues on the New York Stock Exchange fell than rose, a signal that investors remain unenthusiastic about stocks. The dollar strengthened against most foreign currencies.

Stock prices tracked bond prices in early trading, rising a bit as the long-bond price rose, then falling in tandem with declining bonds. But as bond prices continued to sink through much of the day, stocks reversed course and rose, helped in part by some better-than-expected first-quarter earnings reports.

But analysts cautioned that with the yield on the Treasury's benchmark issue now above the psychologically important 9% level, stock prices almost certainly can't hold at current levels. They said first-quarter earnings reports, although in some instances surprisingly good, aren't nearly strong enough overall to insulate stock prices from the effects of rapidly rising interest rates.

"This break above 9% is very serious," said Ken Spence, director of the technical analysis group at Salomon Brothers. "The stock market's response may lag a little bit, but some event will set investors off."

Mr. Spence said yesterday's price gains came largely from computer-assisted program trading related to stock index arbitrage. But he pointed out that for much of April, more issues have fallen in daily trading than have risen. "That says the broad market is much weaker than the Dow," he said.

But even the bigger, well-known and more liquid blue-chip stocks in the Dow average offer only temporary safety if rates continue rising, and eventually will come under selling pressure, he predicted.

In any case, investors are looking to today's release of the government's estimated first-quarter gross national product as the potential event that could set off big moves in both stock and bond prices. While economists are predicting that first-quarter economic growth will come in at about 2.7%, more than twice the fourth-quarter growth rate of 1.1%, some bond traders are worried that it will be even higher. If that's the case, bond prices could tumble still further, taking stock prices with them.

In major market action:

Stock prices climbed in modest trading. Volume on the New York Stock Exchange totaled 141.3 million shares. But declining issues on the Big Board were ahead of gainers 764 to 677.

The dollar rose. In late New York trading the U.S. currency was quoted at 1.6803 marks and 159.23 yen, compared to 1.6785 marks and 158.75 yen Wednesday.
