Black & Decker Posts First-Quarter Profit After 3 Losses in a Row

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/27/90

TOWSON, Md. -- Black & Decker Corp. said it returned to profitability after posting losses for three consecutive quarters. The maker of power tools and household appliances said net income for the first quarter ended April 1 was $10 million, or 17 cents a share, compared with $25.9 million, or 44 cents a share, a year earlier. Sales jumped 76%, to $1 billion, from $570.7 million, reflecting the acquisition last year of Emhart Corp.

The company cited higher operating margins, brisk sales in Europe and generally stronger demand for locks and hardware in the U.S. among reasons for the improved performance. But Black & Decker's interest expenses have remained steep since acquiring Emhart, a move that pushed debt to about 80% of total capitalization.

For the latest quarter, interest expenses were higher than expected because of the sluggish pace of the company's asset-sale efforts and steeper borrowing costs in Brazil. The company said it plans to implement "additional strategies" later this year to lower its interest costs.

Those steps will include a program for selling certain receivables and an effort to restructure the company's sterling-denominated debt. The company didn't disclose how much of such debt it has. As of April 1, long-term debt was $2.85 billion and short-term obligations exceeded $800 million.

This week, Black & Decker sold Emhart's headquarters building for $17 million. Nolan Archibald, chairman and chief executive, said the company also has recently applied about $500 million in Emhart asset-sale proceeds to the reduction of debt.

However, he noted a "continuing weak retail environment" in the U.K., a recent slowdown in Australia and hyper-inflation in Brazil as factors that pose difficult challenges for the rest of this year.
