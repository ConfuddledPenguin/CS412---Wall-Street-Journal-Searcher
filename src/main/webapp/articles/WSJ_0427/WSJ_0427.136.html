Initial State Jobless Claims Declined in April 14 Week

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/27/90

WASHINGTON -- Initial claims for regular state unemployment benefits fell to a seasonally adjusted 348,000 in the week ended April 14 from 371,000 in the previous week, the Labor Department said. The number of people receiving regular state benefits in the week ended April 7 was a seasonally adjusted 2,302,000, or 2.2% of those covered by unemployment insurance, down from 2,375,000 in the previous week, when the insured unemployment rate was 2.3%.

Counting all state and federal benefit programs, the number of people receiving unemployment benefits in the week ended April 7 fell to 2,560,300 from 2,728,000 a week earlier. These figures aren't seasonally adjusted.
