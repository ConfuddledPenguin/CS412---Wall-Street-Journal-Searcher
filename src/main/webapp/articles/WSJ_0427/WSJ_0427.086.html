International: Arnault Wins the Battle With Racamier for Vuitton

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/27/90

PARIS -- Bernard Arnault won France's luxury war. His hands now are free to consolidate and develop LVMH Moet Hennessy Louis Vuitton, the conglomerate of world-famous champagne, cognac, perfume, luggage and fashion products that he runs.

Mr. Arnault's victory came yesterday, when an appeals court here refused his rivals' effort to have a large block of his shares nullified. Mr. Arnault's main rival, 77-year-old Vuitton family leader Henry Racamier, who fought him for nearly two years, immediately resigned from the group. Mr. Racamier had been chairman of the Louis Vuitton unit.

"Now we can finally build a team that will cooperate to develop our brands," Mr. Arnault said after learning of his antagonist's departure. "And now we are free to proceed with acquisitions, several of which we have under study."

He said he is considering acquiring brands both in his current product lines and in new lines, although he insisted that he won't pay inflated prices.

Mr. Arnault's troubles probably aren't over. Mr. Racamier warned after the court decision that the Vuitton family, which controls about 20% of the group's shares and 25% of voting rights, "will continue to defend vigilantly the integrity of the companies and their brands." In the past, Mr. Racamier has threatened to seek allies among other shareholders and try to block some of Mr. Arnault's decisions.
