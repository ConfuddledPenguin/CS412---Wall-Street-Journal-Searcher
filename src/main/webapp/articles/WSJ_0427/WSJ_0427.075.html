Court Clears the Sale Of Integrated Unit's Assets to Bombardier

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 04/27/90

U.S. Bankruptcy Court approved the sale of the assets of Learjet Corp., a unit of Integrated Resources Inc., to Bombardier Inc., the companies said. Bombardier agreed earlier this month to acquire the Learjet assets for $75 million. Integrated Resources, a New York investment concern, is operating under Chapter 11 protection from its creditors.

Bombardier and Integrated said the sale is expected to close on or about May 31. Under the sale agreement Bombardier also will assume Learjet's short-term debt of about $38 million.

Learjet will be Bombardier's third major aerospace acquisition since 1986 and will more than double the Montreal concern's share of the world market for business jets.

Bombardier is the dominant manufacturer of transit and passenger rail equipment in North America and a major player in Europe. Yesterday, on the Montreal Exchange, Bombardier's Class A stock closed at C$15.375 ($13.25), up 62.5 Canadian cents; its Class B shares closed at $15.25, up 12.5 Canadian cents.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Integrated common closed at 25 cents, off 3.25 cents.

Stephen D. Weinroth, chairman of Integrated, said the company expects to present to the bankruptcy court in New York a proposal for the sale of its interest in American Real Estate Partners L.P. within two to four weeks.
