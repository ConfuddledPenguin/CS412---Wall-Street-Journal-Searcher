Business Brief -- MEI Diversified Inc.: Candy, Snack Group Assets To Be Sold for $58 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/27/90

MEI Diversified Inc., a Minneapolis-based snack and health-food distributor, said it agreed to sell substantially all of the assets of its candy and snack group to Ezaki Glico Co. of Osaka, Japan, for $58 million. The sale of the unprofitable operation, which had 1989 sales of $107 million, marks MEI's planned departure from the candy and snack business, the company said. MEI is "moving into new directions," a spokesman said. MEI has been seeking to expand into other fields, and last October agreed to buy LecTec Corp., a Minneapolis-based medical products maker, for about $17.6 million; that transaction is pending.

The sale of the candy and snack group, which is expected to be completed by May 31, won't result in a gain, because the purchase price approximates the book value of the assets being sold, the spokesman said. MEI's major shareholders are the investors Carl Pohlad and Irwin Jacobs.
