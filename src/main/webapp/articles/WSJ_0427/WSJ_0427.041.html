Technology: Merck's Geriatrics Manual Is Expected to Fill `Gap'

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/27/90

WASHINGTON -- Merck & Co. announced the publication of a manual of geriatrics that is likely to join the broader Merck Manual as a reference book widely used by doctors and patients. The 1,267-page Merck Manual of Geriatrics is designed to help fill an "information gap" that often leads to "suboptimal care for the elderly sick," said William Abrams, the book's co-editor. The book covers a range of topics, including common diseases and injuries that afflict the elderly, legal and ethical issues surrounding the treatment of chronically ill patients, descriptions of Medicare, Medicaid and private insurance as well as a variety of social and psychological issues.

Merck will sell the new handbook at cost for $20 a copy. Company officials said they expect first-edition sales of about 200,000 copies.
