Japan's Motor Vehicle Exports

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/27/90

TOKYO -- Japanese exports of cars, trucks and buses in the year ended March 31 fell 6% to 5.8 million units from 6.2 million a year earlier, marking the fourth consecutive fiscal-year decline, the Japan Automobile Manufacturers' Association said. But in March, exports rose for the first time since August, increasing 0.8% to 551,997 units from 547,798 a year earlier, when the year-to-year rise was 18.5%. Except for August, vehicle exports had been declining every month from year-earlier levels since March 1989. The association said the general trend of fewer exports remains the same.

Japanese auto makers are continuing to meet overseas demand by producing at local facilities, particularly in the U.S. and Europe, which has caused Japanese exports to dwindle.
