S&P Plans Covenant Rank On Privately Placed Debt

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/27/90

NEW YORK -- Standard & Poor's Corp. said it will begin rating privately placed debt according to the quality of covenant protection for investors. The private placement market is used by U.S. and foreign companies that want to raise money without going through the paperwork and government review required in a public offering. At the end of 1989, nearly $200 billion of private placements were outstanding. The Securities and Exchange Commission recently passed a rule that will make it significantly easier for issuers to sell privately placed debt to large, sophisticated investors.

S&P, a unit of McGraw-Hill Inc., is known mostly for its credit ratings, which assess a company's likelihood of default. The new covenant ratings will assess other issues, such as how strictly a company's repayment capacity is protected, or how well a lender is protected against a takeover that might hurt credit quality.

S&P is launching its service in conjunction with Prudential Insurance Co. of America, the nation's largest private placement investor.

Frank Rizzo, an S&P managing director, said S&P at first won't announce its covenant ratings, in hopes of making issuers more willing to have their covenants rated. "Ultimately, we want to disseminate the ratings," he said.
