East Germany Gets Competition Accord For Its Drug Firms

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 04/27/90

FRANKFURT, West Germany -- The East German pharmaceutical industry will receive protection from Western competition until the end of 1992, the West German Federation of the Pharmaceuticals Industry said. Under an agreement with the East German pharmaceutical association, German companies will sell drugs only in the country for which no comparable East German products are available in that period. The supply of basic pharmaceutical products will continue to be provided by East German companies, the two groups agreed.

That will give the East German industry a grace period to adjust itself to Western productivity standards and raise the skill level of its employees ahead of the scheduled completion of a single internal market in the European Community at the end of 1992.

Over the next few years, West German drug companies will help their East German counterparts to improve their competitiveness and fulfill their obligations toward other East European countries, the West German federation said.
