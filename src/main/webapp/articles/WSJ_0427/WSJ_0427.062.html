Lower Cocoa Output Forecast

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/27/90

LONDON -- World cocoa production will fall this season for the first time in six years, but should still be well in excess of consumption, the International Cocoa Organization predicted. The London-based organization of cocoa producers and consumers forecast 1989-90 (October-September) world production of 2.37 million metric tons, compared with last season's record 2.42 million.

Cocoa consumption, which has been stimulated by low prices in the past 12 months, will grow 5.7% to a record 2.2 million tons, it said. This would still be 154,000 tons below production.

In 1988-89, cocoa production exceeded consumption by a record 321,000 tons, lifting end-of-season stocks to a record high of 1.24 million, the organization said.

Five seasons of overproduction, and a projected sixth in 1989-90, knocked cocoa prices to a 14-year low earlier this year.
