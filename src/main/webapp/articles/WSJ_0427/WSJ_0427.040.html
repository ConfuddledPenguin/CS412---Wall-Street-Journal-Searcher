Allied-Signal Net Increased by 2.4% In First Quarter

  • Author: Charles W. Stevens
  • Date: 04/27/90

Allied-Signal Inc. said profit edged up 2.4% in the first quarter, restrained by results of its automotive business, but pershare earnings climbed 5.8% because of company stock repurchases over the past year. Net income rose to $129 million, or 90 cents a share, from $126 million, or 85 cents a share, a year earlier, the company said, as results from the aerospace and engineered materials businesses offset lower earnings in the automotive segment.

Sales for the Morris Township, N.J.-based company increased 6.8% to $3.1 billion from $2.9 billion quarter.

The company said earnings from the aerospace business rose 10% to $54 million in the first three months of the year, reflecting a 4% sales increase and lower interest, sales and administrative costs. The Energy Management and Services Group and the Engine Group had higher sales and earnings, the company said. These were partly offset by slimmer margins for the Avionics Group and higher engineering and development costs for new engines and auxiliary power units, the company said.

Automotive net income fell 14% to $32 million in the first quarter, although automotive sales increased 8%, the company said. The sales increase was due in part to favorable foreign exchange and new product sales for anti-lock braking systems and air bags, the company said. Increased selling and interest costs, lower sales in the North American brake and safety-restraint businesses and lower margins for turbochargers more than offset higher after-market profits, the company said.

"We remain hopeful that the domestic automotive business will rebound in the second half of the year," said Edward L. Hennessy, Jr., chairman and chief executive officer.

The company said earnings from the engineered materials business rose 2% to $63 million in the quarter, despite a 2% drop in sales. The segment had increased earnings in the quarter from high-density polyethylene and improved performance by the company's UOP process technology joint venture, which offset lower sales and profits for fibers and uranium hexafluoride, a gas used by the nuclear fuel processors, and higher costs in the Norplex Oak laminates business that makes boards for printed circuits.

Largely due to a corporate stock-repurchase program, Allied-Signal's average shares outstanding fell to 142.8 million in the latest quarter from 148.3 million shares in the year-earlier period.
