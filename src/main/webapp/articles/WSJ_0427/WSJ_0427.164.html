Business Brief -- Norsk Hydro AS: Norsk Hydro Pretax Profit Fell 27% in First Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/27/90

Norwegian industrial and energy concern Norsk Hydro AS said pretax profit in the first-quarter tumbled 27% to 1.62 billion kroner ($249 million) from 2.23 billion kroner in the first quarter a year earlier. Norway's largest company said its operating profit fell 26% to 1.89 billion kroner from 2.57 billion kroner in the first three months of 1989, despite a 31% gain in oil and gas operating profit to 797 million kroner from 608 million kroner.

Per-share earnings were 4.00 kroner vs. 6.10 kroner in the year-earlier period.

Norsk said results were hurt by the agriculture, light metals and petrochemicals divisions, which suffered from prices lower than in 1989.

The light metals division reported a 55% decline in operating profit to 471 million kroner from 1.05 billion kroner.

Norsk announced plans to expand its Norwegian aluminum production by 44% to 915,000 metric tons a year at a cost of around 10 billion kroner.

Hydro Aluminium AS produces 635,000 tons a year of the light metal. Expansion plans concern smelters at Aardal Verk and Sunndal Verk. Per-Christian Endsjoe, executive vice president of the unit, said the first stage of the expansion could be completed as early as 1993 or 1994.
