Marketing & Media: Defunct 7 Days, in a First, Wins Prestigious Award

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/27/90

NEW YORK -- For the first time, a defunct magazine has won a National Magazine Award, the most prestigious award in the magazine industry. 7 Days, the New York City weekly that was folded by owner Leonard Stern two weeks ago, won the award for "general excellence" for publications with circulation less than 100,000. Mr. Stern had closed the magazine because of lack of advertising and being unable to convert enough free readers into paid subscribers.

Other winners in the general excellence category included: Texas Monthly, for magazines with circulation between 100,000 and 400,000; Meredith Corp.'s Metropolitan Home for magazines with circulation between 400,000 and one million; and Time Warner Inc.'s Sports Illustrated, for magazines with circulation of more than one million.

The New Yorker, owned by Advance Publications Inc., won two awards, for reporting and fiction. Conde Nast Publications Inc.'s Vanity Fair won for essays and criticism.

The awards are sponsored by the American Society of Magazine Editors and supported by Magazine Publishers of America.
