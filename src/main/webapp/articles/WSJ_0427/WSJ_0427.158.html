Business Brief -- Volkswagen AG: Marketing Venture Formed With East German Partner

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/27/90

Volkswagen AG, a West German auto maker, said it formed a 50-50 marketing joint venture with its East German partner, VEB IFA-Kombinat Personenkraftwagen. The venture, to be based in Karl-MarxStadt in East Germany, will market the East German makes Trabants and Barkas, and Volkswagen, Audi and SEAT vehicles. Individual marketing operations will be set up for the different brands.

Volkswagen has said it expects its total auto manufacturing joint-venture project with VEB IFA-Kombinat to cost about five billion marks ($2.98 billion). In March, the company said that it will invest an initial 350 million marks and that it plans to assemble about 50 Volkswagen Polos daily in a plant near Zwickau.

Volkswagen said that 400 dealers in East Germany have already been nominated for marketing of Volkswagen and Audi, and 140 for marketing of SEAT. Through the dealer network, Volkswagen said, its entire auto program, including the sale of used cars, is to be made available.
