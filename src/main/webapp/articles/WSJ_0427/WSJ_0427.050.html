Perrier Sales in New York Allowed; Dispute Resolved

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/27/90

ALBANY, N.Y. -- New York state health officials resolved a dispute with Perrier Group of America Inc. and said they would allow the Greenwich, Conn., company to begin selling Perrier water yesterday, as scheduled. Officials had delayed approval for Perrier to re-enter the state, after a sample of the bottled water showed too much sodium for the company to use the term "sodium free" on its labels, under Food and Drug Administration regulations.

"Perrier proved to the commission satisfactorily that the sample we saw was a fluke," a New York State Department of Health spokesman said. "They don't know what happened, but obviously it was a contaminated sample."

Perrier recalled its water world-wide in February, after small amounts of benzene were found in some samples. The new Perrier is marked with labels that read "nouvelle production."

Perrier Group, a unit of Source Perrier S.A. of France, said Perrier meets all FDA and New York state standards. It also said it agreed to work with the New York State Department of Health and the New York Legislature "to develop additional bottled water and mineral water standards, as appropriate."
