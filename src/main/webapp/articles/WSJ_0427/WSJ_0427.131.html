Court Throws Out Main Charge in Suit Filed Against CBOT

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/27/90

CHICAGO -- A federal court threw out the primary count of a farmer group's suit alleging that the Chicago Board of Trade acted illegally last summer when it ordered Ferruzzi Finanziaria S.p.A to sell much of its soybean holdings. The July order, made in an effort to block the Italian conglomerate from what board officials have argued was an attempt to corner the soybean market, sent soybean prices plunging.

The farmer group, known as American Agriculture Movement, said in a November suit that CBOT "manipulation" of the price caused damages of more than $100 million.

The court said in its ruling that the board's action didn't violate the Commodities Exchange Act. It also said it found "persuasive" the CBOT's arguments to have the remaining three counts dismissed, but the court said a different legal move -- a request for a summary judgment as opposed to the motion for dismissal -- would be required. The remaining counts allege CBOT violated the Sherman Antitrust Act and other common-law strictures. American Agriculture Movement couldn't be reached for comment.

The CBOT's July action has already been reviewed and cleared by the federal Commodity Futures Trading Commission.
