Who's News: Two Marshall Field's Executives to Resign Following Takeover

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/27/90

CHICAGO -- Marshall Field's top two executives said they will leave the department store chain after it is acquired by Dayton Hudson Corp. "The responsibility is so changed that I don't feel I could be effective," Philip B. Miller, Field's chairman and chief executive officer, said at a news conference. Mr. Miller led an unsuccessful management bid to buy Field's.

Field's said the chain's 56-year-old president and chief operating officer, Burnett W. Donoho, will quit too. Mr. Donoho wasn't at the news conference.

Dayton Hudson, which agreed to buy Field's from B.A.T Industries PLC for $1.04 billion last week, has said it plans to merge most of Field's central office operations into its department-store unit in Minneapolis. Mr. Miller said about 1,450 of the 1,700 Field's central office jobs will be eliminated, including the entire merchandise buying, store planning and financial operations. He said Dayton Hudson plans to keep credit operations, special events and public relations staffers in Chicago.

Sporting the white carnation and green badge that all Field's managers wear, the 51-year-old Mr. Miller said he has no plans at the moment, but will probably stay in retailing.
