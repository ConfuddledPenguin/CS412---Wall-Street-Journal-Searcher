Technology: Payment for CFCs Tax Is Due Monday, Says IRS

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/27/90

WASHINGTON -- Makers and importers of products using chlorofluorocarbons must deposit the first payment of a new excise tax on the ozone-depleting chemicals by next Monday, or pay interest on the tax, the Internal Revenue Service said. But because the IRS has yet to issue regulations enacting the new tax, the agency said it won't slap any additional penalty on businesses that fail to pay the tax by the due date. The excise tax, which was passed by Congress last year and took effect Jan. 1, amounts to $1.37 per pound of CFCs. Companies will estimate their tax based on the law.

A wide range of industries have been awaiting word from the IRS on how and when they are supposed to comply with the new law. Most excise taxes are reported quarterly, with the first-quarter returns and payments usually due by April 30. With that date fast approaching, the IRS yesterday issued a notice telling taxpayers what to do until the regulations come out.

The IRS notice also said that returns for the excise tax won't be due until 30 days after the agency issues regulations.

CFCs are used as coolants; they are also used to make plastic foam and to clean electronic parts. They are often found in air conditioners, cars, televisions and an array of other electronic products.
