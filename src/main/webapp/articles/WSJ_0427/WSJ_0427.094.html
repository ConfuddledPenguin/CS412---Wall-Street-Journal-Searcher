Money-Fund Assets Fell in Latest Week

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/27/90

NEW YORK -- Assets of the nation's 509 money-market funds fell $816.6 million, to $387.89 billion, in the week ended Wednesday, the Investment Company Institute, a Washington-based trade association, reported. Assets of 246 general-purpose funds rose $563.3 million to $133.62 billion; assets of 102 broker-dealer funds fell $1.68 billion to $163.64 billion. Assets of 161 institutional funds rose $299.8 million to $90.64 billion.

Jacob Dreyer, vice president and chief economist at the institute, said that yields on funds have remained fairly stable for the past three months, and that falling stock and bond prices have had "no apparent effect on the movements of assets into and out of money market funds." He added that with individual income-tax payment and refund checks still clearing last week, "no clear pattern of changes in assets of money-market funds was evident."
