Business Brief -- Alcan Aluminium Ltd.: Rights Program to Deter Any Unwanted Bids Clears

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/27/90

Alcan Aluminium Ltd. shareholders at the annual meeting approved a holder rights plan, with the results indicating many big holders voted against the plan. More than 14,000 holders voted 82.7 million shares, or about 54.1% of those tallied, in favor of the plan, while the 68.4 million shares, or 44.7%, that were voted against it had just 734 holders. Of the total cast, 1.2% didn't indicate "yes" or "no."

"There's a violent reaction amongst institutional investors" to holder rights plans, said David Morton, Alcan chairman and chief executive officer. After the meeting, he said the vote shows "the small shareholder is happy" with Alcan's plan. He said the votes against the plan represent institutional investors' "philosophical concerns" with such plans, designed to make unwanted takeover bids prohibitively expensive.

Under Alcan's plan, rights attached to common shares allow holders to acquire authorized but unissued stock at half the market price, under certain conditions, including a person or group's acquiring at least 20% of the shares outstanding.
