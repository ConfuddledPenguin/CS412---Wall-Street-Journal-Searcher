Business Brief -- Transamerica Corp.: Quarterly Net Fell Slightly Despite Finance-Area Gain

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/27/90

Transamerica Corp., whose 10% gain in its finance sector was offset by flat results or losses in other operations, said first-quarter net income slipped slightly to $70.3 million, or 88 cents a share, from $70.9 million, or 89 cents a share. The San Francisco-based financial services and insurance concern said that sales in the quarter ended March 31 increased nearly 2% to $1.63 billion from $1.60 billion.

Transamerica said earnings improved in its finance operations, which includes consumer and commercial lending, leasing and real estate services, but there was some weakness in other business lines. The company had $5.3 million in after-tax, weather-related catastrophe insurance losses, primarily in the personal and commercial divisions, and there was softness in the marine and recreational-vehicle rental markets.

In composite trading yesterday on the New York Stock Exchange, Transamerica's shares closed at $34.875, down 37.5 cents.
