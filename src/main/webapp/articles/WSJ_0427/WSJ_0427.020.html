Japan Mine, Factory Output

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/27/90

TOKYO -- Production by Japan's mines and factories in March rose 0.7% from a year earlier and 2.6% from the previous month, the Ministry of International Trade and Industry said, noting production of a wide range of goods was strong because of sustained economic growth. Japan's economy, drawing strength from domestic demand such as corporate investment in plant and equipment, has been expanding steadily for 41 months in a row. The industrial and mining production index for March stood at 131.9, while it was 123.7 after adjustment for seasonal factors; the year 1985 equals 100.
