Business Brief -- Whitehall Corp.: Victory Claimed in Dispute With Investment Company

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/27/90

Whitehall Corp., Dallas, claimed victory in its proxy fight with Cambridge Capital Fund Limited Partnership, a New York-based investment company. Whitehall said it is confident, based on a preliminary report from the independent inspectors of election, that its slate of five directors had been re-elected. Roger Miller, a general partner with Cambridge, said the fund wouldn't comment until the final tally is released Monday.

Cambridge had put up its own slate of directors as part of its strategy to gain control of Whitehall, which modifies and maintains aircraft systems, sells underwater acoustic technology to the Navy and makes marine sensor systems. The fund had also offered to buy the 90.4% of Whitehall stock it didn't already own for $20 a share, or about $62 million, which was rejected by Whitehall's board. Whitehall refused to meet with Cambridge to negotiate terms and said it would "vigorously oppose" Cambridge's effort to unseat its board.
