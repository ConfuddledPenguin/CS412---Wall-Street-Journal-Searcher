South Korea's Foreign Debt

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/27/90

SEOUL -- South Korea's net foreign debt increased 45% to $4.37 billion at the end of February from $3.01 billion at the end of 1989, according to the Economic Planning Board. The government agency said that during this year's first two months South Korea's total foreign debt decreased to $28.9 billion from $29.4 billion. The nation's overseas assets also fell, to $24.4 billion from $26.3 billion. Net foreign debt equals total foreign debt minus overseas assets.

The decrease in overseas assets largely reflects the country's growing trade deficit in recent months that lowered foreign-exchange holdings by the central and commercial banks here, according to an official at the agency.
