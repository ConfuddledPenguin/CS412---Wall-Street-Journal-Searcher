Compaq Computer Reports 11% Surge In 1st-Quarter Net

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/27/90

HOUSTON -- Compaq Computer Corp.'s first-quarter earnings exceeded most analysts' forecasts due to increased domestic market share and unexpectedly strong European sales. The Houston-based computer maker said it had net income of $92.2 million, or $2.13 a share, up 11% from $83.2 million, or $1.95 a share. Sales surged 28% to $872.2 million from $682.9 million.

Compaq's stock reacted favorably to the earnings announcement, closing yesterday in composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange at $98.25 a share, up $4.25.

Compaq had warned analysts in late February that its share of the market for high-end personal computers was eroding, leading to projections of little if any earnings increase in the first quarter. Most estimates ranged between $1.75 and $2.11 a share.

But a 56% increase in overseas sales exceeded company expectations, said Michael Swavely, Compaq president of North American operations. And North American sales increased 4% despite a stagnant market, indicating increased market share.

Mr. Swavely also said Compaq had preserved its 40% gross margin, reflecting strong demand for products introduced within the past six months, including a fileserver that can link several dozen personal computers.

Despite a flat first quarter for the industry, Compaq is holding to its prediction that the U.S. market will grow by 5% to 10% in 1990. Fueling such growth, said Mr. Swavely, will be new software applications that may be introduced as soon as May. "That, I believe, will renew general excitement among all customers," he said, and will in turn lead to "a very aggressive new product year for Compaq."
