Business Brief -- UAL Corp.: Wolf Tells Holders Meeting Unions' Offer Is `Doable'

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/27/90

The chairman of Chicago-based UAL Corp., Stephen M. Wolf, told shareholders he believes a buy-out proposal from United Airlines' three unions is a "doable transaction," after he was pummeled with questions from holders skeptical of the offer. Mr. Wolf was generally optimistic about the offer's chances of success. At the same time, some of the statements he made at UAL's annual meeting yesterday could hamper the unions' efforts to obtain bank financing for the $4.36 billion bid.

Mr. Wolf, who the unions want to replace, criticized the terms the bid offers to United's 26,000 non-unionized employees. He also said he plans to announce a major aircraft order shortly, a move that would place additional debt on the airline at the same time the buy-out group is trying to obtain financing.

Mr. Wolf didn't say why he believes the unions' buy-out proposal is unfair to non-unionized employees. The concessions required under the unions' proposal are far less than the concessions Mr. Wolf sought from the non-unionized workers as part of an earlier labor-management bid. That bid collapsed last fall when the buy-out group failed to obtain financing.
