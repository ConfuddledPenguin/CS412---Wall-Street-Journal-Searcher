Chrysler Seems To Mull Closing Another Plant

  • Author: Bradley A. Stertz and Krystal Miller
  • Date: 04/27/90

DETROIT -- Chrysler Corp., which will have closed two assembly plants by year-end, appears to be mulling moves that would allow it to close another one a few years from now. Chrysler currently is thinking about ways to consolidate production of certain vehicles into fewer plants, company executives recently said in trade publications. Included in the review, a company spokesman said yesterday, is a possible shift of Jeep assembly to an Ohio plant from another plant in Canada.

In a statement issued yesterday, Chrysler said "plans that could affect the Jeep assembly plant in Toledo, Ohio are under review." The statement added that "final decisions are contingent upon successful negotiations with the (United Auto Workers) and the support of state and local governmental units."

But in a Toledo television interview Wednesday night, U.S. Rep. Marcy Kaptur, D., Ohio, said Chrysler officials told her that the company's Toledo plant will receive new products after 1992. The Toledo plant, which now builds the Jeep Cherokee, Wagoneer and Grand Wagoneer, operates under a labor agreement that expires in 1992.

According to Ms. Kaptur, Chrysler Chairman Lee A. Iacocca said Chrysler will move production of the Jeep Wrangler to Toledo from its plant in Brampton, Ontario by mid-1992. She also said Mr. Iacocca told her that the auto maker will build a new sport-utility vehicle in Toledo by mid-1993.

Chrysler called Ms. Kaptur's comments "premature." A UAW spokesman declined to comment on the union's discussions regarding the Toledo and Brampton plants. The Brampton plant is represented by the Canadian Auto Workers union, a separate organization. The Wrangler is the only product built there.

Industry analysts say they think Toledo will be getting production of the small sport-utility vehicle that Chrysler is developing with Renault S.A. of France. Under such an arrangement, the Jeep Cherokee, now built in Toledo, would be shifted to a new plant that Chrysler is building in Detroit. This scenario, analysts suggest, depends on whether the state of Ohio and the city of Toledo will give Chrysler tax relief or grants to improve the Toledo plant.

Separately, General Motors Corp. said it will temporarily idle its Arlington, Texas, assembly plant starting April 30 to prepare for changes for production of the 1991 Chevrolet Caprice and Cadillac Brougham.

The No. 1 auto maker said the plant, which currently builds the 1990 version of those models and the Custom Cruiser Wagon, is scheduled to resume production in June. About 3,100 workers will be affected by the downtime.

Ford Motor Co. said its Lorain, Ohio, assembly plant that builds Ford Thunderbird and Mercury Cougar cars will be idled for one week because of slow sales. About 2,700 workers will be affected.

Overall, the 10 major U.S. auto makers plan to build 137,916 cars this week, down 15.2% from 162,705 a year ago.
