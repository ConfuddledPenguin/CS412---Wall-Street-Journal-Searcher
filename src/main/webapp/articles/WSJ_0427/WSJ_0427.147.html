Business Brief -- Whirlpool Corp.: First-Quarter Net Fell 28%; Losses in Brazil Are Blamed

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/27/90

Whirlpool Corp., hurt by deep losses in its Brazilian interests, said first-quarter net income fell 28%, despite a 3.8% revenue increase. The Benton Harbor, Mich., maker of home appliances reported net of $29.5 million, or 42 cents a share, down from $40.7 million, or 59 cents a share, the year earlier. Revenue rose to $1.64 billion from $1.58 billion.

Whirlpool said its "very strong earnings performance" in the latest period was chilled by a 26-cents-a-share loss from its equity interest in Brazilian operations; the year-ago period had a loss of two cents a share from such operations.

Brazil's economy has been brought to a "temporary standstill" by recently introduced austerity measures there, Whirlpool said, adding that there are modest signs of gradual improvement.

The company said North American revenue, operating profit and unit volume rose, and added that it recorded a "solid" first quarter in its European operations, which continue to face tight margins.

In composite New York Stock Exchange trading yesterday, Whirlpool common closed at $21.375 a share, up 37.5 cents.
