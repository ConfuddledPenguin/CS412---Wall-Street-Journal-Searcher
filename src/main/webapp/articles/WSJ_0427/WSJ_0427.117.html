FPL GROUP Inc. (North Palm Beach, Fla.) -- Moody's Investors Service Inc. said it lowered its ratings on this utility holding company's Florida Power & Light Co. and FPL Group Capital Inc. subsidiaries. The rating concern said about $4.8 billion of securities are affected. Moody's said the changes stem from Florida Power's larger construction program estimated for the 1990-1994 period, increased financing and rate-relief requirements, and expected lower internal cash generation. Ratings downgraded include Florida Power's first mortgage and secured pollution-control revenue bonds to single-A-2 from double-A-3; shelf registration of first mortgage bonds to preliminary single-A-2 from preliminary double-A-3; preferred stock to single-A-2 from double-A-3; shelf registration of preferred stock to preliminary single-A-2 from preliminary double-A-3; and unsecured pollution control revenue bonds to single-A-3 from single-A-1. Florida Power's prime-1 rating for commercial paper remains unchanged. FPL Group Capital ratings lowered include medium-term notes to Baa-1 from single-A-2; shelf registration of medium-term notes to preliminary Baa-1 from preliminary single-A-2; and commercial paper to prime-2 from prime-1.
