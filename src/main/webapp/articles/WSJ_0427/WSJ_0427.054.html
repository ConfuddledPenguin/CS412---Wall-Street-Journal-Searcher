Japanese Firms See Economy Contracting Over Next 12 Months

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 04/27/90

TOKYO -- Weakness in Japanese financial markets has changed formerly sanguine corporate views of business prospects and led many companies to expect the economy to contract over the next 12 months, according to the Federation of Economic Organizations, known as Keidanren. A survey of executives at 245 major companies, conducted in late March and early April, showed 60% believe the economy will end its protracted expansion in the second half of the fiscal year that began April 1. In a survey conducted by Keidanren in December, only 35% said they expected the economy to turn down in the second half of the year.

Despite problems in the Japanese economy, the average of companies' replies shows they expect inflation-adjusted growth of 4% in the current fiscal year.

The Japanese economy has been expanding for 41 consecutive months, fueled by high corporate investment and personal consumption.

The tightening of Japanese financial markets since January was the most-often cited cause of a contraction, according to Keidanren. Of the companies surveyed, 38% blamed financial markets for the chance of a downturn, while 29% pointed to inflation stemming from rising labor and commodity prices.
