Capital Outlays of Firms Rose 9.3% in 4th Period

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/27/90

NEW YORK -- Capital appropriations by the nation's largest manufacturers rose 9.3% in 1989's fourth quarter from the third quarter, the Conference Board reported. The increase followed declines in the second and third quarters.

The Conference Board, which surveys the 1,000 largest manufacturers quarterly, also said that capital spending rose 7.9%, after "sluggish increases" in the prior two quarters.

Looking ahead, the board said, survey results suggest an 11.2% rise in capital appropriations in 1990, and a 2.6% increase in capital spending.

"Capital spending in 1990 will barely keep pace with economic growth after running significantly ahead of the country's overall growth for the past several years," said Conference Board economist Ken Goldstein. "But in 1991, capital spending is likely to climb faster than overall economic growth and may continue rising for a year or two beyond that."
