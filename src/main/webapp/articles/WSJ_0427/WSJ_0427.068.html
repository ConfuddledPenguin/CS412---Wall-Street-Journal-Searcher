Image Bank Expects Second-Quarter Net Of $35,000 to $185,000

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/27/90

NEW YORK -- Image Bank Inc. expects to report fiscal second-quarter earnings per share of one to five cents, reflecting nonrecurring expense from both an industrywide, sales-tax audit, and settlement of arbitration on the sale of a business in 1984. For the quarter ended March 31, Image Bank, which licenses stock photos, illustrations and film, expects to report net income of between $35,000 and $185,000, Paul Eichler, executive vice president and chief financial officer, said in an interview. The nonrecurring expense totaled $250,000; Image Bank is fighting California over some additional sales tax the state collected, Mr. Eichler said.

Year-earlier pro forma net income was $48,946, or two cents a share; the net reflects a loss of three cents a share from the discontinuation of the Art Kane Photographic Workshop unit. Shares outstanding were 3.7 million in the latest period and 2.2 million a year ago. Image Bank went public last August.

Revenue grew around 50%, to $12.5 million, from $8.3 million, Mr. Eichler estimated.

For the six months, Image Bank expects to post net income of between eight and 12 cents, or $300,000 to $450,000, from the year-earlier pro forma $254,842, or 11 cents a share.

These numbers reflect the company's recent restatement of its first-quarter net downward to seven cents a share, from 12 cents, a move that knocked a third off the company's share price. The company added $365,069 to cost of sales at the Book People Ltd. subsidiary, acquired Oct. 1.

Image Bank sees a "very strong second half," however, and for all of fiscal 1990, it expects to exceed fiscal 1988's earnings of $1.3 million, Mr. Eichler said. In fiscal 1989, Image Bank earned $344,000.

Representing some 450 photographers, 150 illustrators and 38 cinematographers, Image Bank operates a library of stock photos, pictures and film. The company also has access to a major part of the film library of Turner Broadcasting System Inc. Image Bank has 51 offices world-wide, run mostly by licensees.

The artists give Image Bank the exclusive right to place their works in its library. In turn, Image Bank licensees sell rights to the works for use in magazine ads or "anything that requires an image," Mr. Eichler said.

Image Bank's licensees remit 60% of the proceeds of their sales to the company. Image Bank then splits that amount 50-50 with the photographer, illustrator or cinematographer, Mr. Eichler said.

Though Image Bank's stock cinematography operation is still unprofitable after two years, its growth prospects are particularly strong, he said.

Image Bank also publishes and markets photograph-oriented "coffee-table" books, through operations including Book People. Fiscal 1989 book operations, which didn't include this unit, produced $4.7 million of revenue, a figure that will "substantially more than double" in fiscal 1990, Mr. Eichler said.
