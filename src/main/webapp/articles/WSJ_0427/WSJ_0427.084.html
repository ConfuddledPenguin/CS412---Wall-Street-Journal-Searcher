Motorola Chip Plant in Japan

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/27/90

SCHAUMBURG, Ill. -- Motorola Inc. said it will begin constructing a $50 million assembly plant in Japan to produce semiconductors for its customers in Asia. Responding to increasing demand from customers in Japan and elsewhere in the Far East, the new plant will focus on the assembly of both high-capacity memory devices, and mid-range and high-end microcontroller chips that the company said meets expanding market needs for surface-mount packages.

The plant will be owned and operated by Nippon Motorola Ltd., a Tokyo subsidiary of Motorola, a maker of electronic equipment and semiconductor chips.
