Shawmut National Reports 37% Drop In First-Period Net

  • Author: Christopher J. Chipello
  • Date: 04/27/90

HARTFORD, Conn. -- Shawmut National Corp. reported a 37% drop in first-quarter profit to $40.6 million, or 55 cents a share, reflecting a sharply higher provision for possible loan losses. In the year-ago quarter, the bank holding company earned $64.7 million, or 85 cents a share.

The loan-loss provision was $69 million, up from $16.5 million. Higher nonperforming assets and $13 million in expenses associated with a recent regulatory examination contributed to the loss, Shawmut said.

Partly offsetting those factors were a pretax gain of $46.7 million from the settlement of certain pension obligations and a tax benefit of $8.5 million from an operating-loss carry-forward.

"Our results reflect the continuing uncertainty of the New England region and the particular problems in real estate," said Joel Alvord, chairman.

Net interest income dropped 17% to $212.6 million from $255.4 million. The net interest margin fell to 3.6% from 4.3%, dragged down by the company's higher level of nonperforming assets and a lower level of deposits that don't bear interest.

Noninterest income rose 65% to $146.5 million from $88.5 million, largely because of the gain from the pension settlement.

According to preliminary figures, nonperforming loans and foreclosed real estate totaled $1.16 billion, or 6.8% of total loans and foreclosed properties as of March 31, the company said. That compared with $1.02 billion, or 5.3%, as of Dec. 31, and $273 million, or 1.5%, as of March 31, 1989.

Total assets dropped to $24.9 billion from $27.9 billion as of Dec. 31 as the company sold investments and securitized assets to improve its liquidity and support its capital ratios. Shareholders' equity equaled 5.7% of total assets as of March 31, compared with 6.5%.

The reserve for possible loan losses stood at $772.3 million as of March 31-up from $285.1 million.

After-tax charge-offs in the first quarter were $35.6 million, including $20 million relating to Mexican debt restructuring, compared with $13.1 million a year earlier. Domestic after-tax write-offs rose to $15.9 million from $14.2 million.
