Business Brief -- General Motors Corp.: Normal Schedules Resume At Ontario Assembly Sites

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/27/90

Work resumed on normal schedules at General Motors Corp.'s three vehicle assembly plants in Oshawa, Ontario, after some workers walked off the job on Tuesday and Wednesday. The Canadian Auto Workers union employees left work to protest the suspension of a local union leader by GM. But CAW President Bob White called the walkout a violation of union policies and urged the workers to return to their jobs.

GM said it lost about 5 1/2 shifts of production, or about 30,000 cars and trucks, because of the work stoppage. The auto maker assembles Buick Regal and Chevrolet Lumina midsized cars as well as pickup trucks at the three Oshawa plants.

The auto maker called the strike illegal and met with the Ontario labor relations board yesterday to discuss the problem.
