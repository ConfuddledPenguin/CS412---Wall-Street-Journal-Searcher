Business Brief -- First Constitution Financial Corp.: Firm Posts 1st-Period Loss And Suspends Its Dividend

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/27/90

First Constitution Financial Corp. reported a first-quarter loss and suspended the dividend. The New Haven, Conn.-based company also said merger talks with an investor group unraveled. The savings institution's loss was $39.4 million, or $3.60 a share, compared with year-earlier net income of $5.1 million, or 47 cents a share. First Constitution said the principal reason for the loss was a $36.4 million addition to reserves for loan losses and real-estate investments.

The company said the value of its thrift equity portfolio was written down by $3.5 million, but John J. Crawford, president and chief executive officer, said, "Capital remains well above all regulatory requirements." First Constitution said that nonperforming assets at March 31 were $201.7 million, while reserves totaled $80.6 million. Total assets were $2.4 billion.

In October, First Constitution said it had agreed to be acquired for $25 a share, or $273.5 million, by two related investor groups created for the purpose. In February, it said the investors wanted to renegotiate the deal.

In national over-the-counter trading yesterday, First Constitution closed at $3.875, off 62.5 cents.
