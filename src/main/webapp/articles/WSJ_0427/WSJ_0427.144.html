Business Brief -- Harcourt Brace Jovanovich Inc.: Publishing Firm Had Loss Of $72 Million in 1st Period

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/27/90

Harcourt Brace Jovanovich Inc. said its first-quarter loss narrowed to $72 million from $116.1 million a year earlier. The Orlando, Fla., publishing and insurance company indicated that the smaller loss was the result of three factors. First, the publishing division's operating loss narrowed to $28.7 million from $46.1 million. Second, interest expense fell to $58.1 million from $87.4 million. And third, the year-earlier period had a loss from discontinued operations of $18.5 million.

The latest loss was $1.07 a share compared with $1.80 a share. Revenue rose 15% to $276.9 million from $241.6 million.

A Harcourt director said Wednesday that the company is considering the sale of some of its assets to Thomson Corp., a Toronto publishing and travel concern. Officials at Harcourt confirmed that the company is talking with third parties about possible asset sales and is considering the sale of some or all of Harcourt's existing public debt. But Harcourt wouldn't identify the third parties or indicate what assets might be sold.
