Business Brief -- Raychem Corp.: Loss of $108 Million Posted For Fiscal Third Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/27/90

Raychem Corp., citing a $90 million restructuring charge, said it had a loss of $108 million, or $3.01 a share, in the fiscal third quarter compared with net income of $2.4 million, or seven cents a share, a year earlier. Sales in the quarter ended March 31 increased 8% to $292 million from $270 million.

The Menlo Park, Calif., maker of aerospace, automotive, electronics and telecommunications products attributed the decline to the electronics sector. Those operations experienced price erosion, product shifts from defense to commercial markets, high unit costs in U.S. factories and costs of making newer commercial products.

As a result of the company's changing business and product mix, it said an $8 million provision for inventory obsolescence was included in the cost of products sold in the quarter. It said the obsolescence provision was $4 million a year ago.

In the first nine months, the company had a loss of $97.8 million, or $2.75 a share, compared with net of $23.4 million, or 67 cents, a year earlier. Sales climbed nearly 5% to $819.7 million.
