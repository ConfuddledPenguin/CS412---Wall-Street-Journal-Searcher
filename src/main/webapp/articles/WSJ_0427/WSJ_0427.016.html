Swedes' Buying Abroad

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/27/90

STOCKHOLM -- Swedish investors set records buying foreign shares in March, while foreigners remained net sellers of Swedish shares. The Riksbank, Sweden's central bank, said Swedes bought 10.58 billion kronor of shares ($1.72 billion) in March, up from 3.89 billion kronor in February and from two billion kronor in March the previous year.

Purchases of foreign shares have soared since the Riksbank in January 1989 removed restrictions on foreign buys as part of the deregulation of foreign controls. The March figure was the highest registered so far in a single month.
