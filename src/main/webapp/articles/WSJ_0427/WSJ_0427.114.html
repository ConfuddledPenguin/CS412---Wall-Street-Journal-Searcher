REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: Junk TV

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/27/90

Whenever Congress debates campaign reform you can be sure the real issue -- incumbent protections that limit political competition -- won't be addressed. This week Congress debated a proposal to require that all candidates be charged the lowest possible rate by TV stations for their commercials. At the same time, the House Administration Committee is about to approve an extra $203,000 to hire new technicians for the House TV studio. That's what Members use to send taxpayer-paid satellite feeds and taped programs back to their districts. Last year the existing staff had to put in more than 4,000 hours of overtime to satisfy the demand. With more than half the country able to get the privately financed C-SPAN network, the only reason a propaganda organ like the House TV studio exists is to ensure another election in which 98% of incumbents win.
