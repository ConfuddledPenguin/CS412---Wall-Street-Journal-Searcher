Rambo to Invade TV Sets in France And West Germany --- Carolco Signs Two Accords For Broadcast Rights To Movies for $50 Million

  • Author: Kathleen A. Hughes
  • Date: 04/27/90

LOS ANGELES -- Television viewers in France and West Germany will soon be able to tune in to "Rambo: First Blood, Part II" and "Rambo III." Carolco Pictures Inc. said it signed two television agreements with companies in the two European countries for the rights to broadcast past, current and future Carolco movies, including the Rambo series, for a total of more than $50 million.

The company also said it plans to announce soon a similar agreement for television broadcast rights in Spain. Carolco, which has interests in feature films, television broadcasting, and video and audio retailing, had 1989 profit of $14 million, or 46 cents a share, on revenue of $143 million.

A spokesman for the company said the bulk of the $50 million from the two agreements will be paid before year end.

As the international market for entertainment products has expanded, many studios have moved aggressively to sell the television rights to their movies overseas, particularly in Europe and Japan. Carolco previously sold the television rights only to a few films individually overseas.

"Carolco has been the only independent or major {entertainment company} that has held back their library from foreign television sales," said Jeff Logsdon, an analyst at Crowell, Weedon & Co. "Most companies are doing foreign sales, and it's healthy for these guys."

Carolco's agreement with Hermes Synchron of West Germany includes 34 feature films covering broadcast rights in West Germany, East Germany, Austria and the German-speaking territories of Switzerland and Luxembourg. The package includes the Rambo series, as well as "Angel Heart" and "Red Heat," among others, and future pictures.

The accord with Aries S.A. of France covers 34 titles, including "Field of Dreams" and "Music Box." Carolco said Canal Plus, a pay-television service in France, will broadcast the films under a separate agreement with Aries.

Carolco has a total of 80 films in its domestic library, including 40 titles acquired from Hemdale Corp., another entertainment concern. Of the total, Carolco holds the foreign distribution rights, including television rights, to about 30 films.

Carolco aquired Orbis, a domestic television syndication company, in August 1987.

Carolco shares closed at $11.25, up 12.5 cents, in New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday.
