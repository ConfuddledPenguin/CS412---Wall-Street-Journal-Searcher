New Securities Issues

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/27/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATES

Chrysler Corp. -- $1 billion of Auburn Hills Trust guaranteed exchangeable certificates, due May 1, 2020, was priced at par to yield 12 3/8%. The noncallable certificates are unconditionally guaranteed by Chrysler. The size of the issue was increased from $800 million. Rated Baa-2 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and triple-B by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Salomon Brothers Inc.

MUNICIPALS

East Bay Municipal Utility District, Calif. -- $120 million of water system subordinated revenue bonds, Series 1990, due June 1, 2001-2006, 2018 and 2020, apparently were won by a Bear, Stearns & Co. group with a bid setting the net interest cost at 7.636%. The bonds, rated single-A-1 by Moody's and single-A-plus by S&P, were priced for reoffering to yield from 7.20% in 2001 to 7.60% in 2020. Current interest serial bonds were priced to yield from 7.20% in 2001 to 7.50% in 2006. There are $78.6 million of term bonds priced at par to yield 7 1/2% in 2018 and $20.9 million of term bonds priced at par to yield 7.60% in 2020. Bonds due in 2018 are insured and rated triple-A by Moody's and S&P.

MORTGAGES

Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $300 million of real estate mortgage invesment conduit securities is being offered in eight classes by Kidder, Peabody & Co. The offering, Series 169, is backed by Freddie Mac 9% securities with a weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29.2 years. For classes with details available, the securities yield as much as 10.27%, which represents a spread of 1.27 percentage points above the Treasury 10-year note for a security with an average life of 10.97 years.

EUROBONDS

Banco de Santander (Spain) -- $200 million private placement of perpetual floating-rate notes priced at par, via Shearson Lehman Hutton International. Interest is paid at 0.575 percentage point above the six-month London interbank offered rate for the first five years, and 0.60 percentage point above Libor in years 6-10; thereafter undisclosed.

Halifax Building Society (United Kingdom) -- 300 million marks ($178.5 million) of bonds at the six-month Libor plus 1/32 due May 1995 and priced at 100 1/4, via Westdeutsche Landesbank Girozentrale.

World Bank (agency) -- 200 million Swiss francs ($137.2 million) of 7 1/2% seven-year public bonds priced at 101 1/2 via Swiss Bank Corp.

Cheltenham & Gloucestor Building Society (U.K.) -- #75 million ($122.6 million) of 15 5/8% bonds due Oct. 8, 1992, priced at 101.15 via lead manager JP Morgan Securities Ltd. Long first coupon.

General Electric Co. (U.S.) -- 130 billion lire ($105.5 million) of 13% bonds due June 4, 1993, priced at 101 1/2 to yield 12.95% less fees, via Banca Nazionale del Lavoro. Fees 1 3/8.

Toronto Dominion Bank, London branch (Canadian parent) -- 100 million Canadian dollars (US$86.2 million) of 14 1/8% bonds due June 5, 1992, priced at 101.85 to yield 13.69% less full fees via lead manager Scotia McLeod. Fees 1 1/8.

Deutsche Bank Finance N.V. Curacao (West German parent) -- 100 million Swiss francs of 7 1/2% public bonds due May 16, 1995, priced at 101 1/4 via Deutsche Bank Suisse. Guaranteed by Deutsche Bank AG.

Export-Import Bank of Korea (Korea) -- 105 million European currency units of 11% bonds due May 11, 1995, priced at 101 5/8 via lead manager Merrill Lynch International Ltd. The bonds are callable and puttable in May 1993 at par.
