Sun Microsystems Posts 18% Increase In 3rd-Quarter Net

  • Author: William Celis III
  • Date: 04/27/90

MOUNTAIN VIEW, Calif. -- Sun Microsystems Inc., continuing to contain costs while improving margins, posted an 18% rise in third-quarter earnings, generally better than analysts had expected. The maker of high-performance desktop computers said net income in the quarter ended March 30 rose to $36.7 million, or 40 cents a share, from $31 million, or 38 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue increased 27% to $632.2 million from $497.4 million.

Sun attributed the increased earnings to a host of factors, including improved inventory management and tighter financial controls instituted in recent quarters. The company, whose margins in the past deteriorated because of heavy spending, said it had a gross profit margin of 46%, its highest in 18 months.

"In most respects, it was a superlative performance," said Peter Rogers, an analyst with Robertson Stephens & Co., San Francisco. "The company outdid itself."

With this quarter, analysts said the company has largely put behind it the problems of last summer when Sun stumbled on a major product transition, had difficulty in obtaining enough parts to build and ship new products and difficulties with a new management-information system.

Still bothering some industry observers, however, is the unevenness of domestic markets. Sluggish capital spending has resulted in low or declining U.S. sales for many computer concerns. Sun, for instance, said that more than 50% of its business in the quarter was generated by European and Asian orders. A Sun spokeswoman said the continuing sluggishness would result in a modest fourth quarter in U.S. markets, but that the firm doesn't expect that to hurt overall fourth-quarter results.

Buoyed by the earnings report, which exceeded Wall Street estimates of 30 cents to 35 cents a share, several analysts boosted their expectations for the year. Mr. Rogers, for instance, said he raised his estimate to $1.20 a share from $1.10 a share.

For the nine months, the company said net declined 23% to $62.1 million, or 70 cents a share, from $81.1 million, or $1 a share. Revenue rose 32% to $1.77 billion compared with $1.33 billion.
