Italy May Clear Easing Of Foreign Exchange

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 04/27/90

MILAN, Italy -- The Italian government plans to clear today a decree that will abolish the country's remaining foreign exchange restrictions, a senior government official said. Nino Cristofori, undersecretary of the Council of Ministers, said the cabinet will discuss completion of the foreign exchange liberalization at its weekly meeting.

The move comes two months before the June 30 deadline set by the European Community Commission to lift all barriers to international capital flows.

The decree, which senior monetary sources said is likely to take effect in two to three weeks, will allow Italians to open foreign bank accounts and hold foreign currency in domestic accounts, and it completes a two-year process of gradual easing of foreign currency controls.
