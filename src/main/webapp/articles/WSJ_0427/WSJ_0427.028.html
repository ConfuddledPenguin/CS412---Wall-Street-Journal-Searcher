Dividend News: Johnson & Johnson Raises Dividend 17%; Net Income Falls 23%

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/27/90

NEW BRUNSWICK, N.J. -- Johnson & Johnson boosted its dividend 17% and reported that first-quarter net income fell 23% because of a charge related to economic turmoil in Latin America. Net income at the health products concern fell to $244 million, or 73 cents a share, from $317 million, or 95 cents a share, a year earlier. The $125 million, or 38-cent-a-share, charge included provisions for writing down and restructuring units in Argentina and for paying additional interest on now-liquidated debt in that country. Sales rose 16% to $2.84 billion from $2.45 billion.

The company increased the quarterly dividend to 34 cents a share, raising the per-share dividend on an annual basis to $1.36, compared with the previous rate of $1.16. The next dividend is payable June 5 to stock of record May 22.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Johnson & Johnson closed at $57.125, up 62.5 cents.

In the first quarter, Johnson & Johnson's consumer products business world-wide posted a 15% sales increase. International sales rose 31%, principally because of gains in Brazil, which continued to have high inflation.

Sales of pharmaceuticals rose 21% world-wide. International sales grew 26%, with domestic operations posting a 15% increase.
