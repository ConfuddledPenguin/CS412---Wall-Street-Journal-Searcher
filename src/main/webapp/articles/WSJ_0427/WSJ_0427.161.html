Business Brief -- Citizens & Southern Corp.: Earnings in First Quarter Fell 28% to $41.6 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/27/90

Citizens & Southern Corp. reported that first-quarter earnings fell 28% to $41.6 million, or 64 cents a share, reflecting a $54.8 million charge for problem loans. The quarter's results, which compare with $57.8 million, or 90 cents a share, posted the year before, were slightly better than many analysts had expected.

Last week, some analysts slashed profit estimates as much as 50%, from a range of 93 cents to 97 cents a share, following the Atlanta superregional's announcement that results of a special examination by the U.S. Comptroller of the Currency could force Citizens to take a first-quarter charge to offset possible loan losses.

The targeted exam is part of a current sweep by federal regulators of commercial real-estate portfolios at big banks in the Southeast. The exams, in most cases, have been followed by charges to the companies' first-quarter earnings.

Citizens' first-quarter addition brings its problem-loan reserve to $248.9 million, a 15% increase from the $216.2 million set aside at year-end 1989. Loan write-offs decreased 31%, however, to $22.1 million from $31.9 million at year-end.
