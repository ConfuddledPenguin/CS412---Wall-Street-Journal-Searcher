Manischewitz Seeks To Plead No Contest In Price-Fixing Suit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/27/90

NEWARK, N.J. -- B. Manischewitz Co., Jersey City, applied to plead no contest to a federal indictment that it conspired to fix the price of matzo served during Passover. The company, which earlier denied the charges and claimed that its prices are fair, faces a penalty of $1 million, and possibly more. The maximum penalty depends on the size of the company's unlawful gains or the losses of consumers, said a U.S. Justice Department spokesman.

The U.S. Attorney here objected to the company's plea request. U.S. District Judge Harold Ackerman gave the government until May 11 to file papers explaining its objection. Manischewitz will have until May 16 to respond to the government's filing. On May 23, Judge Ackerman will decide whether to accept the request.

Justin P. Walder, Manischewitz's lawyer, says the request to plea no contest "seemed like the practical and reasonable alternative when you take into account the narrow scope of indictment, the age of the allegations, the cost of defense and prosecution and everyone's time."

The conspiracy took place from "sometime in 1981" through at least April 1986, the complaint says. Manischewitz, according to the complaint, took in about $25 million of revenue from sales of Passover matzo products in the U.S from 1981 to 1986.
