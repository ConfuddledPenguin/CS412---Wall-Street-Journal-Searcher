Citadel Holding Sued by Ex-Director Over Control Fight

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/27/90

LOS ANGELES -- A former director of Citadel Holding Corp. sued the company and another former director for allegedly fraudulently preventing him from acquiring a controlling stake in Citadel. Alfred Roven, in a complaint that was filed in U. S. District Court here, charged that James J. Cotter ousted Mr. Roven from Citadel's board in 1987 so that he could gain control of Citadel, a Glendale, Calif., savings and loan holding company that owns Fidelity Federal Bank.

Mr. Roven, who is seeking $15 million in compensatory damages and $15 million in punitive damages, claimed that proxy materials distributed prior to a May 1987 annual meeting were false and misleading because they did not disclose Mr. Cotter's plan to oust Mr. Roven and install three Cotter nominees on the board.

Mr. Cotter not only won re-election to the Citadel board at that meeting, but he got approval for a change in the company's certificate of incorporation that allowed him to increase his Citadel holdings, the suit said.

Mr. Cotter then allegedly caused Citadel to oppose Mr. Roven's application to the Federal Home Loan Bank Board to acquire more than 10% of the company.

Mr. Cotter allegedly caused Citadel to spend close to $10 million pursuing the acquisition of Valley Federal Savings & Loan. That allegedly allowed Mr. Cotter and affiliated companies to sell Valley Federal stock at an inflated price before the thrift's stock price plummeted after substantial losses were made public.

During that time, Mr. Cotter concealed and then thwarted a $74-a-share acquisition offer by Home Federal Savings & Loan for Citadel so that Citadel could continue pursuing Valley Federal, the suit said.
