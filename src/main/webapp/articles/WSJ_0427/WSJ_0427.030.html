Who's News: Maxtor Picks Kamdar To Run MiniScribe Assets

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/27/90

SAN JOSE, Calif. -- Maxtor Corp. said it named one of its executives, Taroon Kamdar, president of a unit formed to manage the assets of troubled disk-maker MiniScribe Corp. Mr. Kamdar, 44 years old, has been running the Longmont, Colo.-based MiniScribe since the U.S. Bankruptcy Court in Denver approved Maxtor's $46 million bid for MiniScribe's assets earlier this month. Maxtor, which makes data-storage products, said Mr. Kamdar retains his position as Maxtor's vice president for corporate development.

MiniScribe, which last year said its previous management had improperly manipulated its financial results, sought protection in January from creditors under the federal Bankruptcy Code after it was unable to settle a number of lawsuits and bondholder lawsuits against it.
