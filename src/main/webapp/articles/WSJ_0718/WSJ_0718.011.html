Canada's Trade Surplus

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/18/90

OTTAWA -- Canada's merchandise trade surplus widened to 958 million Canadian dollars (US$826.9 million) in May, partly because of an increase in motor-vehicle exports to the U.S., Statistics Canada, a government agency, said. In April, the surplus totaled C$371 million.

Exports of trucks and other motor vehicles to the U.S. rose by C$367 million in May. The U.S. took 74% of the total May exports and supplied 70.5% of Canada's imports.
