  • Date: 07/18/90

W.R. GRACE & Co.'s second-quarter revenue increased 7.8% from a year earlier. The highlights box in yesterday's Digest of Earnings incorrectly stated that the revenue decreased. ---

UNIVERSAL VOLTRONICS Corp., a Mount Kisco, N.Y., maker of high-voltage power conversion systems, begins trading on the American Stock Exchange tomorrow under the symbol UVL. It is 54%-owned by Thermedics Inc., Woburn, Mass. Universal's name was incorrect in Monday's edition.

(See: "New Stock Listings" -- WSJ July 16, 1990)

---

LIONEL CORP., whose debt was recently downgraded, is a New York toy retailer. The company's business was misstated in Monday's edition. Lionel Corp. is unrelated to closely held Lionel Trains Inc., Mount Clemens, Mich., which manufactures toy trains under the Lionel name.

(See: "Credit Ratings" -- WSJ July 16, 1990)
