Marketing & Media: McGraw-Hill Posts 27% Decline in Net For Second Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/18/90

NEW YORK -- McGraw-Hill Inc. reported a 27% drop in second-quarter net income, but the publishing and business information company said it expects a big jump in earnings for the second half of the year. Quarter net fell to $37.2 million, or 76 cents a share, from $50.8 million, or $1.04 a share. Acquisitions made in 1989 boosted the company's interest expense about $6.3 million in the 1990 second quarter. Also, non-recurring gains and related tax benefits of the year-earlier were only partially offset by savings from a restructuring of the company's businesses. Revenue for the latest quarter rose 6.8%, to $457.8 million, from $428.4 million.

Joseph L. Dionne, chairman and chief executive officer of McGraw-Hill, said all four of the company's operating segments had revenue increases in the quarter, but only the Financial Services segment improved operating profit. Financial Services includes Standard & Poor's Rating Group, which was helped by "growing international activity and a strong performance in municipals," said Mr. Dionne.

Softness in advertising hurt McGraw-Hill's Business Week and Aviation Week magazines, pulling operating profit for the Information and Publication Services segment down about 20% from the year-earlier period, the company said.

The 27% drop in quarterly earnings follows an even bigger drop in the first quarter, but Mr. Dionne said he is still predicting that earnings for the year will fall between $3.50 a share and $4 a share. That would put operating earnings above the level of 1988. In 1989, after special charges, McGraw-Hill's net fell to $47.8 million, or 98 cents a share.
