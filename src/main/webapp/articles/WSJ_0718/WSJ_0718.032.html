Bill On Money Laundering Clears Banking Committee

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/18/90

WASHINGTON -- The Senate Banking Committee passed a bill that would give bank and savings-and-loan regulators the power to close a financial institution that had been convicted of money laundering. The regulators would be given that power in cases where both the institution and high officials of the bank or thrift had been convicted of money laundering. The regulators would also be allowed to take over an institution convicted of money laundering and replace its management.

The legislation, approved on a voice vote, is aimed at combatting what law-enforcement authorities say is vastly increased money laundering by the illegal drug trade.

Another provision, aimed at stopping money laundering in unregulated check-cashing and foreign-exchange outlets, would allow the Treasury Department to force banks and thrifts to identify such outlets if they are depositors in the institution.

The committee's bill is similar to legislation that passed the House in April.
