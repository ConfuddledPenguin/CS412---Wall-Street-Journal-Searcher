Congress Report Warns About Market Innovations

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/18/90

WASHINGTON -- A congressional report warned that technological innovation in the world's securities markets "could outpace the capability of market participants to comprehend and control its effects." The report by the congressional Office of Technology Assessment, entitled "Trading Around the Clock," also said, however, that technological innovation "could contribute to sustained liquidity and expansion." While the study acknowledges that government involvement in U.S. markets is controversial, it recommends that "at a minimum, encouragement, facilitation and leadership from government will be needed," particularly in the area of setting standards for information technology.

The report, by Congress's technological arm, attempts to outline questions facing the securities markets as they grow increasingly international.

The study contends that Congress may have to "articulate a clear statement of the national interest for the guidance of regulators." It cautions that market players often use the market with the most lenient structure for trading and contends that "one need is to prevent the erosion of the framework of prudential regulation that Congress has erected since 1934."

It adds that U.S. regulators may need to "actively encourage market officials to take a long-term view of market development, and U.S. regulators themselves may have to be encouraged to do so by the U.S. Congress."
