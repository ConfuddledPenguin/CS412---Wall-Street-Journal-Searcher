Who's News: GE's Kidder Unit Names New Head Of `Junk' Research

  • Author: Michael Siconolfi
  • Date: 07/18/90

NEW YORK -- Kidder, Peabody & Co. named a new head of its junk-bond research group a day after announcing that it will advise regulators on the sale of a sick thrift's $3 billion junk portfolio. The General Electric Co. unit named Joseph V. Amato to succeed Stephen J. Albert as head of junk, or high-yield, research. Mr. Albert will be director of corporate credit at Financial Guaranty Insurance Co., another GE subsidiary.

In an interview, Mr. Amato, a senior analyst in Kidder's junk group for five years, says his biggest challenge is "stepping through the mine fields" of the battered junk market and avoiding "other deals that are going to fail."

Although the junk market has rebounded recently, triggered by gains in some RJR Nabisco Inc. issues, Mr. Amato says he isn't "convinced the market is all back to normal."

Monday, Kidder announced that it will advise savings and loan regulators on the sale of the huge $3 billion junk-bond portfolio of Columbia Savings & Loan Association, Beverly Hills, Calif.

Kidder, for its part, isn't any stranger to regulatory pressure. In April, the firm sold its junk bonds, bridge loans and other merchant bank holdings to GE Capital Corp., GE's big corporate finance unit, for $750 million, under pressure from credit rating companies and the New York Stock Exchange to shed some of its risky investments.

Just weeks before the sale, Kidder hired two junk-bond traders from Salomon Inc., including Thomas Bernard. Mr. Amato will report to Mr. Bernard, a managing director and Kidder's head of high-yield sales.
