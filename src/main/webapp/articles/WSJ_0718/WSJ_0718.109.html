S&L Bailout Could Cost $500 Billion, Says GAO

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/18/90

WASHINGTON -- A recession and higher interest rates could push the costs of the savings-and-loan cleanup to $500 billion over 40 years, including interest, the General Accounting Office said. The GAO, the investigative arm of Congress, previously estimated that $325 billion would cover the costs of the cleanup. But in a new report it referred to $325 billion as the "least-cost estimate." The remarks came in its final audit of the now-defunct Federal Savings and Loan Insurance Corp., whose activities have been absorbed by the Resolution Trust Corp.

The $325 billion estimate, the GAO said, is "based on the Office of Management and Budget's optimistic interest-rate assumptions." But the costs could approach $500 billion if more than 560 thrifts fail and if the economy has a downturn.

The GAO said the cost of the FSLIC's transactions -- liabilities that were created before the thrift-cleanup law was passed last year -- is expected to rise significantly from the $38 billion originally estimated.
