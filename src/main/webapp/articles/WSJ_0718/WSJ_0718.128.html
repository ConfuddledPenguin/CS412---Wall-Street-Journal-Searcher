Tuesday's Markets: Dow Still At 2999.75, Unchanged --- Dollar Falls as Bonds Are Little Changed After Light Trading

  • Author: Christopher Winans
  • Date: 07/18/90

NEW YORK -- Not yet. For the third consecutive trading session, the Dow Jones Industrial Average poked above 3000 but failed to hold its ground at the close. Yesterday, the industrials spent most of the morning above 3000, then fell below as investors collected profits in stocks that had led the now-ended four-day run-up.

In the last half-hour of trading, program buying sent the industrials on a frenzied 20-point dash back to just above the 3000 level, but they slipped at the final bell. The average finished unchanged at 2999.75, echoing the record set Monday. Broader market measures closed lower.

Bonds were little changed, and the dollar ended lower, both in light activity, with investors finding little motivation in yesterday's economic news to trade. Most of the action in currency markets was centered on non-dollar trades.

Several stocks with big gains during the four-day rally, which in retrospect ended Monday, were attracting a lot of sell orders yesterday. Among them: Nike, Wal-Mart Stores and Compaq Computer.

A.C. Moore, research director at Argus Research, cited profit-taking and a directionless bond market for the failure of stock prices to continue pushing higher. Program trading was a wash, pushing prices lower in the afternoon and then abruptly higher at the close. Much of the late program buying was related to Chicago futures trading in the Major Market Index, which mimics the Dow industrials.

"It's been a very heady couple of days," said Andrew Riley, head of U.S. equity research at Yamaichi International, "so it shouldn't be any surprise that the market" didn't continue advancing to new records. In the four sessions ended Monday, the Dow industrials climbed 108 points, or 3.8%.

In effect, "We've made the 3000 mark," Mr. Riley added. "It doesn't matter any more. It will waffle back and forth, stand back, then go higher. The interesting thing is that the volume came back as this move up started" late yesterday. Volume totaled 176.8 million shares, up from 149.4 million Monday.

Peter Canelo, chief strategist at Bear Stearns, said one key to a higher market is the dollar. "It has shown a bit of weakness, and that keeps the international investor from coming in," he said.

In major market action:

Stocks were mostly lower. Decliners outnumbered advancers, 877 to 643.

Bond prices were flat. The yield on the Treasury's 30-year bond held at 8.45%.

The dollar fell. In late New York trading, it was quoted at 1.6440 marks and 147.80 yen, compared with 1.6547 marks and 148.15 yen Monday.
