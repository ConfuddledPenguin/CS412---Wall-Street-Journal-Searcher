Premark Will Take 2nd-Period Charge; Restructuring Cited

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/18/90

DEERFIELD, Ill. -- Premark International Inc. said it will take an after-tax charge of $15.9 million, or 50 cents a share, in the second quarter for certain restructuring actions at its Tupperware unit. Premark said that while its earnings for the quarter will "decline substantially" compared to the year-earlier as a result of the charge, the company doesn't expect to report a loss for the quarter.

In the second quarter of 1989, Premark, a consumer and restaurant concern, earned $23.7 million, or 68 cents a share, on sales of $657.9 million.

Earlier, Premark said it expected lower earnings for the quarter, as "declining dealer activity remains a challenge for Tupperware's U.S. business, and difficult industry conditions hamper its food equipment group's domestic operations" as well as certain other businesses.

The company said on a pretax basis, it expects to save about $25.8 million through 1992, and $12.5 million annually after that, as a result of actions associated with the charge.

These actions include the elimination of more than 300 jobs at Tupperware's U.S. manufacturing plants and the closing of a plant in Rhode Island, the company said. In addition, it said Tupperware's Orlando, Fla., headquarters will trim its staff by about 15%, or 100 workers.

Premark said the moves will help improve results in the near term, as well as "position the company to generate increased growth and broaden its customer base over the long term."
