Marketing & Media: Justice Department Seeks to Block Sale By News Corp. Unit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/18/90

WASHINGTON -- The Justice Department said it will challenge as a violation of federal antitrust law the proposed sale by Twentieth Century Fox Film Corp. of a Hollywood film laboratory to the British leisure and entertainment firm Rank Organisation PLC. Twentieth Century is a unit of Fox Inc., which is owned by Rupert Murdoch's News Corp. of Sydney, Australia.

Rank's acquisition of Deluxe Laboratories "would tend substantially to lessen competition" among specialized film labs that serve the Hollywood movie studios, the department said in a statement. The statement didn't say when or where the department intended to file a lawsuit to try to block the transaction, and officials weren't available to comment further.

Rank owns Film House Group Inc., a major Canadian film lab. By acquiring Deluxe, Rank would control roughly half of the $140 million U.S. market for producing prints of major feature films, the Justice Department said.

Fox Inc. issued a statement saying, "We intend to vigorously resist the Justice Department challenge." Rank agreed to acquire Deluxe in March for about $150 million in cash.

Rank, the largest processor of film in Europe, completed its acquisition of Film House last April from Cineplex Odeon Corp., which is 49%-owned by MCA Inc., paying about $113 million in a two-step transaction over two years. In 1988, Rank paid about $120 million for a video-duplicating venture from Bell & Howell Co., Columbia Pictures Entertainment Co. and Paramount Pictures Corp.

Rank also owns a 50% stake of MCA Inc.'s new theme park, Universal Studios Florida, which it also bought from Cineplex Odeon, for $150 million.

Rank's interests include Pinewood Studios in England, movie production, distribution and exhibition, a giant film library, hotels, restaurants and recreation facilities. Its film and television businesses reported income of $61.8 million last year, about one-fourth of the companywide total, on sales of $350.7 million.
