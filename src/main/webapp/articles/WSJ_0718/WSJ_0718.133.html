Steel Imports Climbed By 10.9% During May From a Month Before

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 07/18/90

U.S. imports of steel-mill products in May rose 10.9% from April, according to the American Iron and Steel Institute compiliation of Commerce Department figures. Steel imports for the first five months fell 10.1% from the year before, the trade group said. Imports in May totaled 1,455,803 tons, compared with 1,313,040 tons in April and 1,472,225 tons in May 1989, according to the trade group. Steel imports for the first eight months of the federal government's Voluntary Restraint Program were 17% of consumption, below the 19.1% target, according to the American Institute for International Steel Inc., a trade group.

Steel exports in May were 351,964 tons, up 5% from April but down 10% from May 1989, the trade group said. For the first five months, exports of 1,739,493 tons were down 2.9% from a year earlier.
