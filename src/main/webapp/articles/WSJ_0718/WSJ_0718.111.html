Business Brief -- Metropolitan Circuits Inc.: Stake Is Set for Consultants If They Raise New Capital

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/18/90

Metropolitan Circuits Inc. said it would sell options to buy as much as a 37.5% interest in the circuit-board maker to two consultants if they are successful in raising new capital for the cash-starved concern. Kenneth C. Cleveland and Paul G. McNamara also would become the Santa Ana, Calif., company's top executives if they raise "in the range of $2 million to $3.5 million" within 30 days, said Richard J. Sandberg, chairman, president and chief executive officer.

Mr. Sandberg said the funds would be used for working capital and to pay about $1.5 million in overdue debts to suppliers. The company has no bank debt, he said.

Mr. Cleveland, a crisis-management consultant, would become chairman and chief executive officer, and Mr. McNamara, former president of Aeroscientific, an Anaheim, Calif., circuit-board maker, would be named president and chief operating officer, if they obtain the financing. Mr. Sandberg would relinquish those titles and become vice chairman.
