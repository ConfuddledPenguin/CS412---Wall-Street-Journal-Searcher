Cordatum Inc. Files For Reorganization Under Chapter 11

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/18/90

McLEAN, Va. -- Cordatum Inc. said it has filed for financial reorganization under Chapter 11 of the Federal Bankruptcy Code, citing pending tax liens and inability to arrange refinancing. At the same time, Cordatum's two principal stockholders sued the company in U.S. District Court in Eastern Virginia, claiming fraud and securities and exchange violations.

Cordatum, a high-technology and professional services company, said it is unable to negotiate repayment of a $150,000 loan from Signet Bank in Baltimore, Md., and that it owes the Internal Revenue Service approximately $45,000 in unpaid taxes, penalties and interest for fiscal years 1985 through 1989.

The company said that three directors resigned this morning, along with the acting chief financial officer, Bill A. Short. The directors were Richard S. Hickok, James S. Gracey and George G. King. Messrs. Hickok and King were named to the board last February.

The two principal stockholders, Commonwealth Acquisitions Inc. and Trautman & Co., claimed in a federal lawsuit that the company and its former chief executive officer, Merle C. Garvis, concealed "material information concerning taxes and other obligations" in connection with the company's initial securities offering in August 1989. Commonwealth and Trautman control 88% of Cordatum.

The suit seeks $10 million in compensatory and punitive damages. Mr. Garvis could not be reached for comment.
