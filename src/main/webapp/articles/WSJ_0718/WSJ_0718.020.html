TRW Profit Fell 10% In Second Quarter; Operating Net Rose

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/18/90

CLEVELAND -- TRW Inc. reported a 10% decline in second-quarter profit because of increased interest costs and a higher tax rate. The automotive-parts, aerospace and electronic-services concern said that operating profit rose 4%, and that its major businesses "continue to perform well in a persistently difficult environment."

Net income dropped to $63 million, or $1.01 a share fully diluted, from $70 million, or $1.12 a share, in the 1989 second quarter. The company traditionally reports share earnings on a fully diluted basis.

Sales rose 14% to $2.1 billion from $1.84 billion.

Interest costs rose to $46 million from $38 million in the year-earlier quarter, reflecting higher borrowings, mainly to finance acquisitions.

Sales of auto and truck parts jumped 20% to $1.04 billion, mainly because of higher sales of air bags and other occupant-restraint systems. The group's operating profit rose 8% to $84 million. Higher engineering, product-development and start-up expenses in the restraints business, weaker car and truck sales in North America, and the unsettled Brazilian economy limited the gain in operating profit, TRW said.

TRW's space and defense segment reported a 9% rise in sales to $862 million, and a "slight" increase in operating profit to $61 million. Reduced government funding on certain programs and a stretchout of others contributed to the group's lower profit margins, as did higher investment to develop future business, the company said.

Sales of information systems and services rose 5% to $190 million, but operating profit declined about the same percentage to $18 million. The company blamed a sales decline at units performing maintenance service on electronic equipment produced by other companies, and costs of downsizing those businesses.

For the first half, TRW reported net of $120 million, or $1.93 a share, down 14% from $139 million, or $2.24 a share, a year earlier. Operating profit rose 5%, TRW said. Sales rose 13% to $4.09 billion from $3.63 billion.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, TRW fell 25 cents to close at $46.875.
