Who's News: Manitowoc Co.

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/18/90

MANITOWOC Co. (Manitowoc, Wis.) -- Fred M. Butler, former senior vice president and chief operating officer, was named president and chief executive officer. Mr. Butler, 55 years old, succeeds P. Ralph Helm, who will continue as chairman. Manitowoc is a crane and ice machine manufacturer and ship repairer. Mr. Helm, 64, is relinquishing his other positions to focus on developing a new line of cranes before his retirement, the company said. Manitowoc said G. Michael Decker, senior vice president and chief financial officer, and Jeffrey R. Cooke, senior vice president of administration, have resigned "because of philosophical differences as to the long-range strategy of the company." The company said that Messrs. Decker and Cooke resigned in response to the company's decision to develop the new line of cranes. "They felt that there shouldn't be any new line, but the board agreed with myself and the chairman that there should," Mr. Butler said. The two executives who resigned weren't available for comment.
