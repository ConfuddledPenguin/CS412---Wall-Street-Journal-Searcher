Financial Concerns Report Higher Profit For Second Quarter

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 07/18/90

Great Western Financial Corp. and Golden West Financial Corp. reported higher second-quarter earnings, citing growth in home loans despite predictions of a decline in the California real estate market. Great Western said net income was $78.2 million, or 60 cents a share, up 51% from $51.9 million, or 40 cents a share, a year earlier. The Beverly Hills, Calif., bank holding company said return on assets equaled 0.82% of assets, up from 0.58% a year earlier. Total new loans in the quarter were $3.6 billion, compared with $3.4 billion.

Separately, Oakland, Calif.-based Golden West said net in the same period was up 22% to a record $49 million, or 78 cents a share, from $40 million, or 64 cents a share. Its return equaled 0.95% of assets, up from 0.86%.
