Baptist Press's Chiefs Axed By News Service's Owners

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/18/90

The top officers of Baptist Press, a prominent religious news service used by nearly 500 newspapers and magazines nationwide, were fired by the executive committee of the Southern Baptist Convention, which owns the service. The dismissals of Al Shackleford, 58 years old, director of the Nashville, Tenn.-based news service, and Dan Martin, 51, its editor, were made effective immediately after the committee voted in closed session yesterday in Nashville. The committee named no replacements for the two men, leaving the 43-year-old news service's future unclear.

In June, the committee told the two men to resign by Sept. 30 or they would "be dealt with harshly." They refused. Last week, the Associated Church Press -- representing 195 Protestant, Catholic and Eastern Orthodox publications -- wrote to Baptist officials urging them to retain Messrs. Shackleford and Martin.

Although the committee offered no explanation for its action, its chairman, Sam Pace of Lawton, Okla., earlier issued a statement accusing Mr. Shackleford of bias against fundamentalists, who recently wrested control of the Southern Baptist Convention from a more moderate faction. Fundamentalists believe the Bible is literally true in matters of religion, science and history.
