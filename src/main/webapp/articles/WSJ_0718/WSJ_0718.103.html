Technology: Shuttle Flights to Resume This Summer, NASA Says

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/18/90

WASHINGTON -- The National Aeronautics and Space Administration said it would resume flying the space shuttle in mid-August or early September. NASA grounded the shuttle fleet last month after finding fuel leaks in two shuttles. About the same time, it discovered that its Hubble Space Telescope was crippled. The two events have led to a re-examination of NASA's future by Congress and the White House.

NASA said it discovered the fuel leaks weren't as difficult to fix as it had feared. Although the leaks looked similar on the launch pad, the space agency said, they had different origins and were relatively easy to repair.

The space agency said it would try to fix the leak on the shuttle Atlantis on the launch pad, by tightening or replacing dozens of bolts, and fly the craft around Aug. 10. Atlantis is carrying a reconnaissance satellite for the Pentagon.

If Atlantis is launched on time, the agency said it would launch Columbia around Sept. 10. Columbia will carry a set of space telescopes. But if NASA can't fix the leak on Atlantis on the launch pad, the agency said it would bring it back to the assemby building for further work and move Columbia's launch to about Sept. 1.

Launching both by mid-September "requires us to be lucky twice," said William Lenoir, NASA's associate administrator for space flight.
