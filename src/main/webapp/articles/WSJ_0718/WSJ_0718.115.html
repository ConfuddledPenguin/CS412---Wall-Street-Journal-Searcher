Business Brief -- Southern Co.: Unit Loses Legal Round In Effort to Recover Costs

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/18/90

A Southern Co. unit lost a round in its elated to Georgia Power Co.'s Vogtle plant. Georgia Power filed the appeal following a lower court's decision to uphold the disallowance, which the commission said was justified in light of certain "imprudent and excessive" costs. A Georgia Power spokesman said his company was reviewing the latest court decision and hadn't decided whether it would appeal. He said the company can ask the appeals court to reconsider its decision, and if Georgia Power loses there, it can still appeal to the state supreme court.

If Georgia Power ultimately loses, Atlanta-based Southern has said it would have to take an after tax write-off of about $200 million. That would be in addition to a $229 million write-off taken in 1987, after the total budget for the plant exceeded a self-imposed cap.
