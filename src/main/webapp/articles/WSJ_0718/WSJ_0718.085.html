CD Yields Offered By Big Institutions Move Mostly Lower

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/18/90

NEW YORK -- Yields on certificates of deposit at major banks and brokerage firms were mostly lower in the week ended Tuesday. Average yields on small-denomination CDs sold directly by banks were flat to down slightly. The average yield on six-month consumer CDs slipped to 7.62% from 7.64%, while the average yield on two-year CDs remained at 7.88%, according to a nationwide, 18-bank survey by Banxquote Money Markets, a New York information service.

Average yields on small-denomination CDs sold by leading broker dealers were down slightly. The average yield on one-year CDs eased to 8.10% from 8.19%, while the average for two-year CDs fell to 8.13% from 8.32%, according to Banxquote.

Meanwhile, average yields for large-denomination CDs, which typically require deposits of $90,000 or more, were also down slightly. The average yield on one-year jumbo CDs went to 7.81% from 7.93%, while the average for five-year maturities went to 8.19% from 8.23%.

The decrease in yields on broker-sold and large-denomination CDs was in response to the lowering of the federal funds rate (the daily interest rate that banks charge each other) by the Federal Reserve, says Richard B. Hoey, chief economist, U.S., of Barclays de Zoete Wedd.

"The expectation previously was that the Fed would cut the rate in the third quarter," he said, and that "banks would have moved their rates in sync." What happened instead, he says, is that the drop took place sooner than expected.

The outlook for the next few months? "There probably isn't an awful lot of action ahead," Mr. Hoey said.
