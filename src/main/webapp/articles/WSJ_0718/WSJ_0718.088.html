Marketing Brief -- Field Corp.: Field Publications Operations Is Being Put Up for Sale

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/18/90

Field Corp., Chicago, said it put up for sale its Field Publications educational unit, which publishes the Weekly Reader and Funk & Wagnalls encyclopedia. The closely held concern said owner Marshall Field 5th has decided to evaluate the possible sale of the business "relative to the other things in the family portfolio and relative to tax and estate planning issues."

The company said it has retained the investment banking firm Morgan Stanley & Co. to seek bids for the unit.

Field Corp. purchased the unit from Xerox Corp. in August 1985, and subsequently acquired the Funk & Wagnalls operation in 1988.

A Field Corp. spokeswoman said she couldn't confirm published reports that the Field Publications unit had 1989 revenues of about $300 million.

Field Publications, based in Middletown, Conn., has about 950 employees, the company said.
