Industry Focus: Chrysler Brings Back No-Interest Financing On 1989, 1990 Models

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/18/90

HIGHLAND PARK, Mich. -- Chrysler Corp. is once again rolling out its 0% financing gambit in an effort to spur sales of certain 1989 and 1990 model cars and trucks. Chrysler began offering the 0% rate on 24-month loans yesterday, replacing a 2.9% rate for two-year contracts it had offered since June 1. Chrysler first offered 0% financing on short term contracts in April 1989.

But the offer, which runs indefinitely, probably won't help most shoppers. Even without finance charges, many consumers can't afford to pay off a car within 24 months, and Chrysler isn't offering similar discounts on longer-term loans. Finance charges stay at 7.9% for 25-month to 48-month loans and 10.9% for 49-month to 60-month loans.

Among the vehicles covered by the 0% financing offer are 1989 and 1990 Jeep Cherokee and Wagoneer sport-utility vehicles and Comanche pickup trucks. Also affected are the 1989 and 1990 Plymouth Sundance, Dodge Shadow, Plymouth Acclaim and Dodge Spirit compact cars.

Meanwhile, Chrysler said that beginning Monday it will increase daily production by 17% at its Sterling Heights, Mich., plant that builds the Shadow and Sundance.

So far this year, Shadow sales have dropped 13% and Sundance 25%. Inventory levels are relatively high for both cars.

But Chrysler said it has received an increase of dealer orders for the 1991 models due out this fall because the cars are gradually replacing the Dodge Omni and Plymouth Horizon models -- which were discontinued in February -- as Chrysler's low-priced, entry-level compact vehicles. Dealers are anticipating higher sales of Shadow and Sundance models once they get rid of the leftover Omnis and Horizons.
