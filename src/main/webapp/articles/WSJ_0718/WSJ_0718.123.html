Business Brief -- Dime Savings Bank: New York Bank Says Net Doubled in Second Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/18/90

Dime Savings Bank of New York reported second-quarter net income doubled despite a higher loan-loss provision and more assets that aren't earning interest. Net reached $10.1 million, or 44 cents a share, from $5 million, or 22 cents a share, in the year-earlier period. The earnings include four cents and 12 cents a share, respectively, from use of tax-loss carry-forwards.

The bank said it had added to its reserves for possible losses on loans made in commercial real estate and multifamily housing, but stressed that it is in compliance with federal capital standards. Dime expects nonperforming assets will continue to rise at least through 1990.

The bank provided $9 million for possible losses on loans in the quarter, compared with $4.2 million a year earlier. At June 30, the allowance for loan losses equaled 1.01% of total loans vs. 0.27% a year ago. Nonperforming assets, those not earning interest, equaled $754.8 million, or 6.5% of total assets, compared with $550.6 million, or 4.7%, at Dec. 31, 1989, and $407.4 million, or 3.3%, at June 30, 1989.
