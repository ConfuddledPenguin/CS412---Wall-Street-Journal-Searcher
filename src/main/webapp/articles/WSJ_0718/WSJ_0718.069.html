Tosco's Planned Sale Of Chemicals Unit Has Been Canceled

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/18/90

STAMFORD, Conn. -- Tosco Corp. said a tentative agreement to sell its 50% stake in the Fort Meade Chemical Products unit of its Seminole Fertilizer Corp. subsidiary had been abandoned. The company, which also reported increased earnings, had signed a letter of intent last April to sell its stake in the phosphate acid production venture to a unit of Sinochem American Holdings Inc., which owned the remaining 50% and operated the unit. Sinochem is owned by the Chinese government. Terms weren't disclosed, but the stake was worth about $50 million, analysts said.

The breakdown in the talks was unexpected. "It came as a surprise to us," said Chief Financial Officer Jefferson F. Allen. "The fundamentals of the business have improved substantially."

Tosco, an oil refiner, acquired Seminole last year for $133 million, plus about $200 million in assumed debt from a limited partnership whose partners are substantially the same as a partnership that holds a 25% stake in Tosco. Tosco recently abandoned efforts to sell its refining operation, but hasn't yet completely abandoned efforts to sell Seminole, Mr. Allen said.

Tosco, which last May moved its corporate headquarters from Santa Monica, Calif., to Stamford, Conn., also reported increased earnings, primarily due to strong margins at its Avon oil refinery in California, caused by the unusual circumstance of lower crude oil prices and higher gasoline prices.

Second-quarter net income was $48.2 million or $1.52 a share, up 72% from $28 million or 88 cents a share in the year-ago quarter. Revenues, which now include Seminole, climbed 73% from $334.2 million to $577.5 million. The results include Seminole, which wasn't profitable. They also include a $10 million expense for moving refinery offices from Santa Monica and Bakersfield, Calif., to Concord, Calif., and higher interest expense for the Seminole acquisition.
