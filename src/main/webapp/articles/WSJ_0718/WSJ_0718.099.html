Sun Microsystems Inc. Pact

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/18/90

MOUNTAIN VIEW, Calif. -- Sun Microsystems Inc., gaining another ally in its battle to win the hearts of desktop-computer users in businesses, said a Xerox Corp. unit agreed to provide its popular desktop-publishing software for Sun's machines. The unit, Ventura Software Inc., agreed to produce its Ventura Publisher for Sun's Sparcstation computers, Sun said. Sun also announced a new software-distribution program called Catalyst CDware in which Sun will provide users with a compact disk filled with application software.

The Ventura agreement is a boon for Sun, which is trying to broaden the use of its machines to business offices from the more traditional engineering uses where Sun's machines are extremely popular. To do that, Sun machines need a critical mass of the kind of software businesses use: word processor, spreadsheet, data base and desktop publishing programs, for example.

Sun has already won some big-time allies in Lotus Development Corp., Wordperfect Corp. and Ashton-Tate Co., which have agreed to tailor their programs to Sun machines.
