Business Brief -- Polaroid Corp.: Company Posts 23% Drop In 2nd-Period Net Income

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/18/90

Polaroid Corp., suffering from weak sales in the U.S., posted a 23% decline in net income on a 2.3% drop in sales in the second quarter. The Cambridge, Mass.-based photography company, whose sales have been sluggish recently, said domestic sales declined 10% in the quarter from the year-ago period, while international sales rose 8%.

Polaroid's net income dropped to $32.9 million, or 45 cents a share, from $42.7 million, or 62 cents a share, in the year-ago period, as sales declined to $475.6 million, from $486.6 million. Earnings dropped even though the company's effective tax rate dclined to 31%, from 41% in the 1989 second quarter.

Shipments of instant film dropped, as retailers lowered inventory, the company said. Camera sales were flat. "Higher levels of investment in marketing and in new product development" also cut into profit, the company said.

For the first six months, Polaroid's net income fell 5.7%, to $64.7 million, or 89 cents a share, from $68.6 million, or 87 cents a share, on a lower number of shares outstanding. Sales dropped 1.8%, to $911.8 million, from $928.9 million.
