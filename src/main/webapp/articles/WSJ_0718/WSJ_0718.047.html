New Securities Issues

  • Author: null

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATE

General Electric Capital Corp. -- $250 million of notes, due July 24, 2008, is being offered with an 8 1/2% coupon rate at prices based on prevailing market conditions at the time of sale to investors. Investors can put their notes in five years at par. The non-callable notes were priced initially at par to yield 8.5%, a spread of 0.22 percentage point above the Treasury's five-year notes. Rated triple-A by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets. GECC is the financial arm of General Electric Co.

Kaiser Permanente -- $200 million of 9.55% debentures, due July 15, 2005, was priced at 99.656 to yield 9.593%. The non-callable debentures were priced at a spread of 1.15 percentage points above the Treasury's 10-year note. Rated double-A-2 by Moody's and double-A by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Goldman, Sachs & Co. Kaiser Permanente is a large prepaid health care delivery system.

Public Service Electric & Gas Co. -- $125 million of first mortgage and refunding bonds, due July 1, 2020, was priced at par to yield 9.75%. The bonds, which are nonrefundable at a lower interest cost for five years, were won in competitive bidding by Morgan Stanley & Co., who bid 99.626 for a net cost to the company of 9.787%. Rated single-A-1 by Moody's and single-A by S&P, the bonds were priced at a spread of 1.30 percentage points above the Treasury's 30-year note. The bonds were issued by a subsidiary of the Newark, N.J.based Public Service Enterprise Group Inc.

Anheuser-Busch Cos. -- $100 million of 8 3/4% notes, due July 15, 1995, through Goldman, Sachs & Co. The company said the notes will be offered to the public at 99.92% of the face amount to yield 8.769%. Proceeds will be used for general corporate purposes, the company said, including repayment of short-term debt incurred to fund working capital requirements and the Sea World acquisition that was completed in late 1989. The notes are being issued under a previous shelf registration that Anheuser-Busch filed with the Securities and Exchange Commission, the company said.

Northern Illinois Gas Co. -- $50 million of first mortgage notes, due July 15, 1995, was priced with a coupon of 8.70% at 99.896 to yield 8.725%. The issue was won in competitive bidding by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets, which offered a price of 99.546 for a net interest cost to the company of 8.814%. The notes, which are non-callable for four years, were priced at a spread of 0.42 percentage point above the Treasury's five-year note. The issue was rated double-A-1 by Moody's and double-A by S&P.

ASSET-BACKED

Fleetwood Credit Corp. -- $122 million of securities backed by recreational vehicle loans are being offered with a coupon rate of 9% at 99 18/32 to yield 9.204% through First Boston Corp. The offering, FCC 1990 -- a Grantor Trust, was priced at a yield spread of 0.94 percentage point above the interpolated three-year and four-year Treasury notes. Underlying loans are being serviced by Fleetwood Credit, a unit of Fleetwood Enterprises. Legal final maturity is July 15, 1997. The issue has an expected average life of 3.4 years assuming monthly prepayments at 1% of the original loan balance. The offering is expected to be rated triple-A by Moody's and S&P, based on the quality of the underlying loans and a surety bond from Capital Markets Assurance Corp., a unit of Citicorp.
