Fiat Unit Joins GE In Project to Develop Jumbo-Jet Engine

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/18/90

EVENDALE, Ohio -- General Electric Co.'s jet engine division said the aviation subsidiary of Italy's Fiat Group has signed up as a 10% partner on GE's new jumbo-jet engine program, the GE-90. The Fiat unit, Fiat-Avio S.p.A. of Turin, Italy, will share in GE-90 development, production and revenue, GE Aircraft Engines said. GE has estimated development costs at between $1.2 billion and $2 billion. The engine will compete with products by United Technologies Corp.'s Pratt & Whitney division and Britain's Rolls-Royce PLC in a market valued at between $50 billion and $100 billion over 20 years, beginning when the next generation of wide-body jets are launched in the mid-1990s.

GE announced this month that France's Snecma committed to a 25% stake in the new engine. GE has said it wants to limit its role in the GE-90 program to a 60% interest, while using foreign partners to share the development costs and risk and to help in marketing overseas.

For the GE-90, Fiat-Avio will develop the engine gearbox and drive system and participate in the aerodynamic, mechanical and structural design and development of the low-pressure turbine, GE said.

Fiat also agreed to an 11% participation in a new GE gas-turbine engine, the LM-6000, for marine and industrial use, and to increase its role as a supplier of low-pressure turbine parts for GE's existing CF6-80C2 jet engine, which powers current wide-body aircraft.

GE, headquartered in Fairfield, Conn., has interests in aerospace, financial services, broadcasting and industrial products.

Separately, GE said it will lay off 670 workers, or 14% of the work force at its Pittsfield, Mass., plant, which makes products for the electric utility industry and the military. About 80% of the cutback involves GE's Power Delivery & Controls division. The remaining cuts will affect GE's aerospace division at the plant.
