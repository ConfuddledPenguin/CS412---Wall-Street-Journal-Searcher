GULF STATES UTILITIES CO. -- Moody's Investors Service Inc. said it raised the ratings on about $2.4 billion of senior debt of this Beaumont, Texas-based utility. In making the upgrade, the ratings concern cited the recent settlement of a four year-old legal dispute related to power purchases from Southern Co., Georgia. Moody's raised the ratings on GSU's first-mortgage bonds and secured pollution control revenue bonds to Ba-2 from Ba-3, and raised the unsecured pollution control revenue bonds, Eurodebentures and convertible debentures to Ba-3 from single-B-1. Ratings on the utility's preferred and preference stock were confirmed at Caa. ---

THREE NEW JERSEY BANKS saw their credit ratings cut by Standard & Poor's Corp., which cited loan portfolios weakened by the slump in the real estate market. The rating on the preferred stock of UJB Financial Corp., Princeton, fell to triple-B-minus from triple-B; the subordinated debt of Central Jersey Bancorp, Freehold, dropped to double-B-plus from triple-B; and the senior debt of Summit Bancorp, Summit, dropped to triple- B-plus from single-A. Overall, the downgrades by the New York credit-rating company affect securities valued at $30 million at UJB, $22 million at Central Jersey, and $157 million at Summit.
