International Brief -- Perkins Foods PLC: Company Agrees to Acquire Two Dutch Food Concerns

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/18/90

Perkins Foods PLC, London, said it has agreed to buy two Dutch food manufacturers, Bakker Lekkerkerk Holland B.V. and K.B. de Souffle Specialist B.V., for an initial 71.6 million guilders ($38.4 million). Perkins, which is attempting to build an integrated food manufacturing and supply network across northern Europe, said it will pay for the purchases through the issue of 30 million convertible preference shares, expected to raise #28.9 million ($52.1 million).

The company said Bakker will allow it to move into the Dutch market for daily delivery of chilled, fresh food products to supermarkets. De Souffle is a maker of frozen filled pancakes with distribution in the Netherlands and West Germany.

A further 3.9 million guilders may be paid to the Dutch companies depending on future profitability, Perkins said.

Perkins added that it intends to seek a listing on the Amsterdam stock market.
