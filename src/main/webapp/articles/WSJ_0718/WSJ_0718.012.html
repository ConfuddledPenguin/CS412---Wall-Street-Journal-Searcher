Coca-Cola Net Grew by 19% In 2nd Quarter

  • Author: Peter Waldman
  • Date: 07/18/90

ATLANTA -- Coca-Cola Co. said second-quarter net income rose 19% on strong growth overseas, despite weak sales at home. The soft-drink giant, which earns roughly 80% of its income outside the U.S., reported earnings in the period of $409.9 million, or 61 cents a share, compared with $345.5 million, or 49 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue rose 18% to $2.74 billion from $2.33 billion.

The results were in line with analysts' expectations.

"Overseas is really what did it," said Emanuel Goldman, a beverage-industry analyst with PaineWebber Inc. in San Francisco, "especially margin expansions in Europe and the Pacific."

In Western Europe, where Coke generally earns about one-third of its profit, operating profit jumped 25%, Mr. Goldman said. The increase was paced by a 42% gain in concentrate and syrup sales in Spain, the company reported, and a 20% jump in such sales in Britain.

Coke sells concentrates and syrups to local bottlers, who mix the formulas with carbonated water to produce soft drinks.

In the Benelux region, Coke said, concentrate and syrup sales increased 17%, but they were flat in West Germany as bottlers reduced bloated inventories.

In the U.S., where profit margins on soft-drink sales are smaller than abroad because of stiffer competition, Coke said its concentrate and syrup sales grew just 1% in the period. Specifically, the company cited "slower restaurant-industry traffic" as the cause of its domestic softness, a trend confirmed by some restaurant operators. Coke derives about onethird of its U.S. volume from so-called fountain sales to restaurants.

"Around here, the restaurant business is down about 20%," said Steven Abdow, president of the specialty restaurant division of Springfield, Mass.-based Abdow Corp., which operates a chain of Big Boy restaurants in western New England. "Our own business is down between 3% and 5%" from a year earlier, he said.

Coke said its fountain sales are picking up this quarter, thanks to "new distribution" coming on line. In May, Coke wrested away the fountain business of Grand Metropolitan PLC's Burger King restaurants from Pepsi, an account that Mr. Goldman estimated will add about 7% to Coke's fountain sales.

For the six months, Coke said net grew 15% to $693.4 million, or $1.02 a share, from $605.3 million, or 85 cents a share. Revenue also jumped 15%, to $4.89 billion from $4.24 billion.
