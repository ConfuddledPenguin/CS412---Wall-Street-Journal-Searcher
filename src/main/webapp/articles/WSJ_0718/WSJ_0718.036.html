Southmark Cedes Partnerships' Control To 2 Ex-Managers

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/18/90

DALLAS -- Southmark Corp said it ceded control of 33 limited partnerships to two former executives and restructured mortgages that Southmark held on some assets owned by those partnerships. Southmark also hired the former executives, John W. Galston and Eugene H. Rosen, to provide investor-relations services on 53 additional partnerships for which Southmark remains general partner. The two had been engaged in proxy fights to replace Southmark as general partner of its privately held partnerships. Mssrs. Galston and Rosen resigned two years ago as top managers at the company's Southmark/Envicon syndication arm.

The men, now principals of Limited Partner Investor Monitoring Committee, Inc. (LPIMC), began their proxy contests last year after Southmark filed for bankruptcy law protection. The real-estate and financial services conglomerate lost 33 of the contests.

Under terms of the settlement, LPIMC will replace Southmark as general partner of those partnerships. In addition, Southmark will reduce interest rates and subordinate principal on the partnership-related mortgages the company holds so that the properties can handle their debt service again. The parties agreed to drop their lawsuits, and LPIMC agreed not to challenge Southmark's control of the other 53 private partnerships.

The restructured mortgages are held by Southmark's wholly owned San Jacinto Savings Association, or are to be contributed to the Houston thrift as part of the company's plan of reorganization, confirmed by a bankruptcy court judge Monday. A Southmark spokesperson said the settlement won't have a material effect on the plan.
