Coca-Cola Enterprises Inc.

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/18/90

ATLANTA -- Coca-Cola Enterprises Inc. reported a 43% jump in second-quarter net income, after a big gain from a property sale, on a 3.3% revenue increase. Net rose to $55.3 million, or 42 cents a share, from $38.8 million, or 26 cents a share, a year earlier. Second-quarter revenue was $1.12 billion, compared with $1.09 billion.

Coca-Cola Enterprises, of which Coca-Cola Co. owns 49%, is the largest bottler of the soft drink.

Stripping away a mishmash of unusual items, the bottler's second-quarter earnings from operations rose about 3.9%, execeeding analysts' estimates. The company's earnings have been disappointing for several quarters because soft-drink price increases haven't covered rising costs for raw materials and marketing.

But compared with price increases of 2% to 3.5% in recent quarters, Coca-Cola Enterprises said it raised its soft-drink prices to retailers by 4% in the quarter.

In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange yesterday, Coca-Cola Enterprises common rose 25 cents a share to close at $16.25.
