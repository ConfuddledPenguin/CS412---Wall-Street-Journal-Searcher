Massachusetts High Court Confirms 5% Services Tax

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/18/90

BOSTON -- Massachusetts's highest court gave the go-ahead for a new 5% sales tax on business services, including fees of lawyers, accountants, engineers and architects. The Supreme Judicial Court, by a narrow 4-to-3 margin, issued an advisory opinion saying that the legislature has authority to levy the services tax. Gov. Michael S. Dukakis had sought the opinion before signing the bill, approved recently by the state House and Senate.

Despite the advisory opinion, critics of the new tax are expected to file suit challenging it.

Opponents contend, among other things, that the state constitution doesn't specifically permit a tax on services. Aside from its constitutionality, opponents have said that the tax would be bad for the state's economy.

But the court said that services can be considered commodities, which are taxable under state law, because the word commodities "is not limited to tangible personal property." Dissenting justices argued, "Nothing but the most strained interpretation of `commodities' can include the host of services."

In a sign that it will consider challenges to the tax on other grounds, the court acknowledged that "there are other constitutional questions that have been or might be raised" concerning the tax on particular services.

The legislature passed the services tax, along with increased income taxes, to help offset a looming $1 billion budget deficit.
