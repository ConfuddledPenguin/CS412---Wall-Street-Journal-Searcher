Business Brief -- Adolph Coors Co.: Company's 2nd-Quarter Net Rose 49%; Shipments, 14%

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/18/90

Adolph Coors Co. said its net income rose 49% while beer shipments rose 14% in the second quarter. The beer, ceramics and aluminum company reported net of $25.1 million, or 68 cents a share, compared with $16.9 million, or 46 cents a share. Net sales rose 12% to $475.4 million from $425.5 million. Shipments of beer rose to 4,831,000 barrels from 4,252,000 barrels.

For the six months, the Denver-based company reported that net more than doubled to $32.8 million, or 89 cents a share, from $12.1 million, or 33 cents a share. Net sales rose about 16% to $869.7 million from $749.6 million, while beer shipments rose to 8,811,000 barrels from 7,315,000 barrels.

The company said the largest increases in earnings came from its Golden Aluminum Co., Graphic Packaging Corp. and brewing units. Analysts attributed the growth in beer shipments to Keystone, which was introduced last August, and to double-digit growth in Coors Light sales.
