REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: Ethical Priorities

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/27/90

Congress laid to rest two ethics cases this week. The Senate voted 96-0 to denounce David Durenberger for financial misconduct. The House voted 408-18 to reprimand Rep. Barney Frank for his actions on behalf of a male prostitute. Ethics experts say the votes finally show a willingness by Congress to discipline Members, though we worry that the real effect will be closer to a new ritual of confession and penance. If ethics were the first priority in the congressional club, Senator Durenberger and Rep. Frank would resign. In any event, we're less worried about personal finances or private behavior than about what Congresspersons do during working hours, especially with the S&L crisis costing every American family $3,000 or more. Congress will not have begun to tackle its ethics problem, or its tattered image, until it starts to draw some lines around "constituent service."
