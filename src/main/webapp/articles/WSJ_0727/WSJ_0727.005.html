Whirlpool Reports Profit Decline of 26% In Second Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/27/90

BENTON HARBOR, Mich. -- Whirlpool Corp., hammered by a loss from its Brazilian affiliates, said second-quarter earnings fell 26% to $37 million, or 54 cents a share. Like other manufacturers with operations in Brazil, the appliance maker has suffered under that country's economic reform plan introduced last March. In the year-earlier period, Whirlpool earned $50 million, or 72 cents a share.

The current results were slightly better than some analysts expected. In New York Stock Exchange composite trading, Whirlpool closed yesterday at $28, up 37.5 cents.

The company said the outlook in Brazil for the rest of the year remains uncertain, but added that improvements which started in the second quarter have continued into the current quarter.

Whirlpool said that, if its Brazilian affiliates' results were excluded in both the latest and the year-earlier second quarters, earnings would have risen 18%. It said North American appliance operations were strong, despite a slumping market for new homes.

Revenue for the quarter rose 4% to $1.71 billion from $1.64 billion. For the six months, earnings fell 27% to $66 million, or 96 cents a share, from $91 million, or $1.31 a share a year earlier. Revenue rose 4% to $3.34 billion from $3.22 billion.
