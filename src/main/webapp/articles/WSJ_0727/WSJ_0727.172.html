Law: Big Board's David Marcus Is to Join Shearson Sept. 4

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/27/90

NEW YORK -- David Marcus, an executive vice president at the New York Stock Exchange, soon will oversee Shearson Lehman Hutton Inc.'s legal and compliance departments. He also will be a senior executive vice president and a member of the firm's executive committee, reporting to Howard L. Clark Jr., chairman and chief executive officer.

Mr. Marcus succeeds David Hershberg, who resigned after six years as general counsel. Mr. Hershberg's political base within the firm decayed when Peter Cohen, Shearson's former chairman, was ousted in January. Mr. Hershberg will remain until Mr. Marcus joins the firm Sept. 4.

At the Big Board, Mr. Marcus handled relations with institutional investors and international regulatory organizations, among others. He joined the exchange in 1982 from Drexel Burnham Lambert Inc., where he was counsel, secretary and senior vice president for 13 years. Previously, Mr. Marcus was branch chief and enforcement attorney at the Securities and Exchange Commission.
