International Brief -- International Business Machines: Company to Reduce Staff At European Headquarters

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/27/90

International Business Machines Corp. said it plans to cut staff at its European headquarters office, in a reorganization that decentralizes management of its big European operations. A spokesman for the Armonk, N.Y.-based computer giant said the company's European headquarters in Paris is transferring direct control of key business segments to selected IBM managers in West Germany, France, Britain and Italy. Previously, the Paris headquarters supervised these business segments, which are Europewide divisions for particular types of products or customers.

In an internal memo that IBM released yesterday, following a Reuters news report from New York, IBM Europe President David E. McKinney wrote that the reorganization will "lead to a progressive reduction of the size of the European headquarters." About 170 Paris positions are currently involved in managing the reorganized business segments; some of these positions will be transferred around Europe, but others could be eliminated, the spokesman said. There won't be any "major relocation of jobs" this year, however, Mr. McKinney's memo said.
