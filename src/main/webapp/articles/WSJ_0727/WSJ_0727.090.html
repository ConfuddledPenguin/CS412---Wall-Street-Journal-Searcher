Disney Reports 3rd-Quarter Net Increased 23%

  • Author: Earl C. Gottschalk Jr.
  • Date: 07/27/90

BURBANK, Calif. -- Walt Disney Co. posted a 23% increase in third-quarter net income on a 32% increase in revenue spurred by a solid performance in its theme parks and surging movie profits. The entertainment company earned $238.5 million, or $1.75 a share, in the period, compared with $193.3 million, or $1.40 a share, a year earlier. Revenue climbed to $1.53 billion from $1.16 billion a year ago.

The third-quarter earnings were about what analysts had been projecting. "No big surprises in this quarter," said Harold Vogel, entertainment analyst for Merrill Lynch & Co. "Maybe a slight touch lighter than some people had projected-but only a penny or two a share."

Growth companies such as Disney have been under intense scrutiny by Wall Street lately; any faltering in the earnings growth rate has led to a sell-off of the stock. Disney released its earnings yesterday after the market closed. In New York Stock Exchange composite trading, Disney shares closed at $121, up $2.125.

"The 23% gain certainly exceeds Disney's targeted goal of a 20% earnings increase for every quarter," said Jeffrey Logsdon, an analyst at Seidler Amdec Securities Inc., Los Angeles. "They keep cranking them out."

Disney's biggest business segment, its theme parks and resort operations, showed a 14% increase in operating income to $288.5 million for the quarter, up from $252.3 million a year ago. Revenue for the theme park segment rose 18%.

Erwin Okun, a Disney spokesman, said that through June 30, "things have been going well" at the company's theme parks. He declined to comment on how theme park attendance has been doing in July.

Mr. Vogel said attendance at Disney theme parks in the third quarter had an "easy" comparison with last year's third quarter. The company had its Disney-MGM studio tour operating all three months this quarter compared with only part of the quarter a year ago, he said. In addition, the Easter vacation fell into April this year, compared with an earlier Easter a year ago, which diffused the Easter vacation attendance surge into last year's second quarter, he said.

Some analysts have been concerned that economic uncertainty has been affecting theme parks around the U.S. Mr. Vogel said he thinks that attendance at Disneyland and Walt Disney World has recently been "flattening -- but there is no reason to be alarmed." Disney doesn't disclose attendance numbers for its Disneyland and Walt Disney World theme parks.

Led by "Pretty Woman," the industry's highest grossing movie this year, and home video sales of "The Little Mermaid," an animated feature, the operating income for Disney's filmed entertainment segment rose 35% in the quarter to $73.3 million from $54.5 million a year ago. "Pretty Woman's box office success is significant because it wasn't an expensive movie to make," Mr. Okun said.

"Dick Tracy," released in June, had "very strong box office results," the company said. Through last weekend, "Dick Tracy" had a box office gross of $95 million, Disney said.

Operating income for consumer products, including "Dick Tracy" merchandise, increased 18% for the quarter to $46 million from $39 million a year ago.

For the nine months ended June 30, Disney's profit rose 21% to to $591.3 million, or $4.29 a share, from $490.6 million, or $3.56 a share, a year ago. Revenue climbed 27% to $4.13 billion from $3.24 billion a year earlier.
