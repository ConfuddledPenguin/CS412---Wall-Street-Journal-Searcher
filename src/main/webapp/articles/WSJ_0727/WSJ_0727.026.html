International: France Intends to Repay Almost All Its Foreign Debt

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 07/27/90

PARIS -- Economics Minister Pierre Beregovoy said yesterday that France will repay virtually all of its outstanding external debt by the end of this week through prepayments equivalent to about $400 million. Mr. Beregovoy told a meeting of journalists that the payments, to be made July 27, will cover the last $350 million of a $4 billion loan contracted by France in 1982 to shore up France's official reserves of foreign currency. France will also pay off 40 million European Currency Units ($50.9 million) of a 4 billion-ECU loan taken out in 1983 for the same purpose.

The minister said the two payments would leave 30 million ECUs of external debt outstanding. This amount is part of a fixed-maturity borrowing that comes due in 1993. Terms of this borrowing won't allow France to make prepayments until July 1, 1991, and this will be done, Mr. Beregovoy said.

Mr. Beregovoy said the move was made possible by the strength of the franc, the high level of French official reserves and the strong performance of its economy.

The debt reduction effort will make France one of Europe's least indebted states.
