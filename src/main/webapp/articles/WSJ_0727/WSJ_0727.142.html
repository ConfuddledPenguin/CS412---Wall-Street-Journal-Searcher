Business Brief -- Campeau Corp.: U.S. Bankruptcy Judge Approves Program for Allied

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/27/90

A federal bankruptcy judge in Cincinnati approved an asset-backed commercial-paper program for Allied Stores Corp., a retailing unit of Campeau Corp. Allied's three-year agreement with Chemical Bank, a subsidiary of Chemical Banking Corp., initially calls for $650 million in financing but can be increased to $721 million. The financing will be securitized by Allied receivables. A spokeswoman for Chemical said this will be the first time a company in Chapter 11 proceedings has been able to access the commercial-paper market.

Mason Funding Corp., a special-purpose corporation, will issue the commercial paper. The spokeswoman for Chemical, which structured the deal, said the first issuance is expected early next week.

The receivables financing will replace a $700 million Citibank financing that expires next week.

Allied and Federated Department Stores Inc., another Campeau unit, filed for bankruptcy-court protection in January.
