New England Slump Hurts 2 Banks' Results

  • Author: David Stipp
  • Date: 07/27/90

BOSTON -- Bank of Boston Corp., grappling with New England's continuing ecomonic woes, reported sharply lower second-quarter profit of $3.5 million, or less than one cent a share, after a $125 million provision for possible loan losses. Separately, BayBanks Inc. posted a second-quarter net loss of $11.5 million, or 72 cents a share, after a loan-loss provision of $63.5 million. BayBanks also cut its dividend by a third.

Bank of Boston's second-quarter profit compared with year-earlier profit of $97.8 million, or $1.34 a share. The year-earlier profit included a $36 million provision for loan losses.

The bank-holding company said that at the end of the second quarter, its reserves for possible loan losses totaled $892 million, or 3.91% of outstanding loans and leases, up from $645 million, or 2.41% a year earlier. The reserves as of June 30, however, were down from reserves of $967 million, or 4% of loans and leases, as of March 31.

Bank of Boston noted that "in spite of further weakening in the real estate market and a declining regional economy," its nonperforming assets as of June 30 fell to $1.96 billion from $1.98 billion as of March 31, 1990. The reduction in nonperforming assets was due partly to charge-offs of $200 million of bad loans during the quarter, said Peter Manning, chief financial officer. But a larger portion of the reduction stemmed from sales of problem real-estate assets and the reclassification of problem loans as "healthy" ones after restructuring, he added. As of June 30, 1989, nonperforming assets totaled $1.47 billion.

The company said that as of June 30, 45% of its troubled real-estate assets were in New England, 17% in Florida, 5% each in Georgia, Tennessee and Texas, and 23% in the rest of the country.

Net interest revenue for the quarter was $246 million, down from $304 million a year earlier. The net interest margin fell to 2.92% from 3.78%, reflecting a higher level of domestic nonperforming assets, lower interest spreads and lower margins from Latin American operations, the company said.

Noninterest income for the quarter rose to $205 million from $195 million a year earlier.

For the six months, Bank of Boston earned $47.2 million, or 56 cents a share, down from profit of $186.9 million, or $2.57 a share, a year earlier. Provisions for credit losses totaled $250 million in the first half of 1990, compared with $72 million during the year-earlier period.

In New York, Moody's Investors Service, citing "disappointing second-quarter earnings," said it lowered it ratings on Bank of Boston's senior debt to Ba-2 from Ba-1 and on its subordinated debt to B-1 from Ba-3. Moody's lowered its ratings on other Bank of Boston issues, affecting a total of $2.2 billion of long-term debt. Moody's noted that despite higher chargeoffs by the company during the second quarter, its "aggregate volume of nonperformers remained stable, suggesting continuing asset quality pressures."

Commenting on the Moody's downgrade, Mr. Manning, Bank of Boston's chief financial officer, said the bank-holding company is "obviously disappointed" with Moody's action but pleased "that Moody's saw fit to maintain certain of our ratings."

BayBanks second-quarter loss compared with year-earlier profit of $18.9 million, or $1.18 a share.

For the six months, BayBanks reported a net loss of $25.3 million, or $1.59 a share, compared with year-earlier profit of $39.7 million, or $2.48 a share, including a nonrecurring gain of $1.2 million.

BayBanks said its board cut the quarterly dividend to 30 cents a share from 45 cents a share "to conserve capital during an unsettled period in the region's economy." The company added that its nonperforming loans rose to $265 million as of June 30, compared with $237 million at the end of the prior quarter and $87.4 million a year earlier.

Loan loss reserves as of June 30 were $145 million, or 55% of nonperforming loans, compared with $57.3 million, or 65.6%, of nonperforming loans a year earlier, the company said.
