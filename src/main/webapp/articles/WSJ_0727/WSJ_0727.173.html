Business Brief -- International Banknote Co.: Merger With U.S. Banknote Approved by Shareholders

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/27/90

International Banknote Co. shareholders approved a merger with U.S. Banknote Co. L.P. Under the terms of the agreement between the two New York companies, each International Banknote common share has been converted into one-fourth preferred share with a liquidation value of $18 a share and one-half common share of the combined company.

Shares of the new company, U.S. Banknote Corp., will trade on a "when issued" basis on the American Stock Exchange under the symbol UBK; yesterday the shares were quoted at an indicated range of $2.50 to $3 a share. The company will have eight million common and 1.5 million preferred shares outstanding.

U.S. Banknote Co., a printer of securities and currency and producer of three-dimensional holograms used on credit cards, will have estimated combined sales of about $135 million this year, said Morris Weissman, former chairman and chief executive officer, who retains those titles at the new company. Stanley Kreitman, former president, was named president and chief operating officer of the new company.
