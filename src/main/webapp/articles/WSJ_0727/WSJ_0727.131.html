International Brief -- National Power PLC: British Electricity Company Reports $1.09 Billion Loss

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/27/90

National Power PLC reported a #605 million ($1.09 billion) loss for the year ended March 31. The company is the larger of the two British electricity generating companies getting ready for privatization. The results were given as if National Power had been an autonomous business throughout the year. The company was created by the British government March 31 as part of a plan for restructuring and selling the electricity supply industry.

National Power shares will be offered to the public in February.

In the year, National Power had operating profit of #456 million on sales of #4 billion. Year-earlier figures weren't given.

National Power said the loss for exceptional and extraordinary items was #914 million, mainly in connection with costs associated with a restructuring ahead of the sale and the revaluation of the company's coal stocks.

National Power also said 5,000 jobs will be cut over the next five years.
