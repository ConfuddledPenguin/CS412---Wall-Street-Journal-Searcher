Alleged Violations Of Big Board Rules Result in Sanctions

  • Author: Robert Daniels
  • Date: 07/27/90

NEW YORK -- A former broker with four firms agreed to be barred from associating with New York Stock Exchange members for alleged violations of Big Board rules. Without admitting or denying wrongdoing, Adnan Ahmed Durrani of New York agreed to the sanction.

The action against Mr. Durrani -- formerly employed with Shearson Lehman Hutton Inc.; E.F. Hutton & Co., a Shearson predecessor; Oppenheimer & Co., and Moseley Hallgarten, Estabrook & Weeden Inc. -- was among several disciplinary moves the exchange made.

The exchange charged that Mr. Durrani, among other things, misappropriated a security owned by a customer, effected unauthorized trades, and caused an account statement with false and misleading data to be issued to a customer.

Other disciplinary actions and related suspensions from membership and fines include:

Michael John Boorse, Horsham, Pa., 18 months and $5,000; Susan B. Weston, Waukegan, Ill., one year; Ronald Alan Rubenstein, Randolph, N.J., nine months; Howard George Einhorn Jr., Huntington, N.Y., four months.

The exchange barred the following individuals until they comply with its requests for information or testimony: Derek J. Baxter, Jersey City, N.J.; Edgar Bellenger Cooper, Nashville; Steven C. Rickun, Glendale, Wis., and Arthur Gladre Stevenson III, Grover, Mo.

The following settled charges without admitting or denying wrongdoing:

Jeffrey E. Bonham, Lafayette, N.Y., barred from membership; David Michael Swope, Ontario, Calif., three years; James Michael Owen, Richmond, Va., one year; Mark A. Willman, Dunkirk, Md., two months; Raymond Joseph LeVan, Whitmore Lake, Mich., one month; Spear, Leeds & Kellogg, New York, $20,000; the People's Securities Inc. subsidiary of People's Bank in Bridgeport, Conn., and Gerard Frost, Rockville Centre, N.Y., $5,000 apiece; Kenneth K. Ho, Queens, N.Y., Joel David Kaminer, Coral Gables, Fla., and Wallace Rogers Smith, Charlotte, Vt., censure only.

Except as noted below, the individuals and firms involved couldn't immediately be reached for comment.

A spokesman for People's Bank said the bank settled to avoid the cost of litigation, while Mr. Frost declined to comment.

Spear Leeds declined to comment.

Mr. Willman declined to comment.
