International: ICI Pretax Profit Fell 21% During First Half

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/27/90

LONDON -- Imperial Chemical Industries PLC said pretax profit fell 21% in the first half of 1990, hit by a sharp decline in the company's industrial-products division. The British chemicals giant also announced a major reorganization of its fertilizer division. ICI said it has agreed in principle to sell its U.K. fertilizer business, the biggest of its global fertilizer operations, to Kemira Oy of Finland. Terms weren't disclosed.

Shares in ICI, Britain's biggest manufacturer, plunged nearly 6% on the worse-than-expected earnings for the 1990 first half. ICI shares traded late Thursday at #10.40 ($18.81) on London's Stock Exchange, down 65 pence from Wednesday.

The results from ICI, the world's fourth-biggest chemical concern, reinforced perceptions of rough times for bulk chemical makers. Sluggish economic growth is depressing demand and prices.

ICI posted pretax profit of #733 million ($1.33 billion) in the 1990 first half, down from #925 million a year earlier. Sales rose 2.7% to #6.82 billion from #6.64 billion. But the company attributed most of that increase to more favorable foreign-exchange rates.

Net profit attributable to shareholders fell 34% to #372 million from #562 million, after a #100 million charge stemming from the reorganization of fertilizer operations.
