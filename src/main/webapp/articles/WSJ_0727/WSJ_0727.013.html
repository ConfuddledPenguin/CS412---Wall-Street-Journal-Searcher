Who's News: U.S. Shoe Corp. Gives Hudson Greater Role In Footwear Business

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/27/90

CINCINNATI -- U.S. Shoe Corp. reshuffled top executives in its footwear operations, giving Bannus B. Hudson, president and chief executive officer, a more hands-on role in that group. Martin Schulman, group president of domestic footwear, was promoted to president of footwear manufacturing and wholesaling, effective Sept. 1. He will report to Mr. Hudson.

Mr. Schulman, 51 years old, will take over a major part of the job of Howard Platt, who will retire as U.S. Shoe executive vice president and president of both footwear wholesaling and retailing. Mr. Platt, 63, will continue as a consultant to the company, managing projects for the footwear group.

Mr. Schulman will have responsibility for both U.S. footwear manufacturing operations and for wholesaling imported shoes. However, Charles Goorman will continue as president of footwear retailing, reporting to Mr. Hudson. Previously, he reported to Mr. Platt.

Mr. Hudson said the new arrangement will permit him to be more directly involved in retailing, an area where he had extensive experience developing U.S. Shoe's successful Lenscrafters optical division.

U.S. Shoe was a footwear concern that diversified into specialty retailing, which is now its largest business. For the fiscal year ended Feb. 3, the company earned $49.2 million, or $1.10 a share, on sales of $2.56 billion.

Earlier this year, the company reported accounting irregularities at Marx & Newman, its shoe importing unit. However, the management changes aren't a direct result of that problem, Mr. Hudson said.

Michael Geller was promoted to chief executive officer of the Marx & Newman division, filling the position vacated by the May 1 retirement of Howard Glick. Mr. Geller joined U.S. Shoe last January, after 27 years in the footwear industry, including manufacturing experience in Italy.
