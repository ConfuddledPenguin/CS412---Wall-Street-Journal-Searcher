Business Brief -- BIC Corp.: Company Posts Small Drop In Second-Quarter Earnings

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/27/90

BIC Corp., Milford, Conn., said second-quarter earnings dropped slightly as substantial sales and earnings decreases in its lighter-manufacturing segment offset increases in writing and shaving instruments. Earnings for the second-quarter were $7.6 million, or 62 cents a share, down from $7.7 million, or 63 cents a share, in the year-earlier period. Sales fell 4% to $91.6 million from $95.5 million. Net income includes an extraordinary gain of $498,000, or four cents a share, attributable to a Mexican tax-loss carry-forward.

Bruno Bich, president of BIC, attributed the earnings decreases in the lighter segment to a reduction in the number of smokers in the U.S. and to an increase in the number of inexpensive lighters from China, Thailand and Korea. "Sales and earnings gains from writing instruments and shavers, plus reduced losses in perfume, were not sufficient to offset" these losses, he said.

Earnings for the first half fell 3.9% to $13.6 million, or $1.12 a share, from $14.17 million, or $1.17 a share, for the year-earlier period. Sales fell 3% to $170.9 million from $175.9 million.
