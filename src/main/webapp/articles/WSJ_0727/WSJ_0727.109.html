Seven Are Charged With Rigging Bids For HUD Property

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/27/90

WASHINGTON -- Seven real estate investors were charged in federal court here with rigging bids at public auctions, some of which involved property owned by the troubled Department of Housing and Urban Development, prosecutors said. The case is the first ever in which the federal government has alleged violations of antitrust laws in connection with bid-rigging at real estate auctions, according to the Justice Department.

The seven defendants allegedly agreed not to bid against each other, enabling them to buy HUD-owned property and other real estate at artificially low prices, the Justice Department said. After one defendant bought a property at a low price, he made payoffs to the others, the government charged. This activity allegedly began in 1977 and continued until last year.

Some of the property involved was auctioned by the Superior Court here on behalf of people under the court's protection, such as children and people deemed not legally competent, prosecutors said. "These victims are among the most vulnerable citizens of our city," said James Rill, the Justice Department's antitrust chief.

The seven men charged were: Eric Baer, George Basiliko, Frank Gittleson, Douglas Goldsten, Irvin Greenbaum, Robert Greenbaum and Sidney Spiegel. The defendants couldn't be reached for comment. The crimes charged carry possible prison terms and fines of hundreds of thousands of dollars.
