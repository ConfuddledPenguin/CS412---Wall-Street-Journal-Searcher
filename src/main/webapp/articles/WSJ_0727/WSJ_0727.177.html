BSN Investor Group Boosts Stake, Seeks List of Shareholders

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/27/90

DALLAS -- A Houston investor group increased its stake in BSN Corp. and demanded a list of shareholders. In a filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission, Pelham Investment Associates Limited Partnership said it acquired 46,700 shares of the sporting-goods maker between May 11 and July 16 for $5.28 to $5.77 a share. That increased its stake in BSN to 7.3%.

The investment group said it is seeking the shareholder list to solicit consents or proxies. The group said previously that it had discussions with Michael Blumenfeld, the company's president, about buying a substantial block of BSN stock and gaining board representation. Mr. Blumenfeld owns or controls about 15% of BSN stock.

"We're not going to give them anything," Mr. Blumenfeld said.

Separately, BSN reported net income of $522,000 for the second quarter, a 13% increase from the prior year's $461,507. Per-share net rose 40% to 14 cents from 10 cents on nearly one million fewer shares outstanding. Revenue rose 11%, to $25.2 million from $22.7 million.

For the first half, net income was $347,076, or 15 cents a share, up from $424,995, or nine cents a share. The most recent period included an extraordinary credit of $198,924, or five cents a share, from the early extinguishment of debt. Revenue rose 11% to $47 million from $42.2 million.

Mr. Blumenfeld attributed the gains to the cumulative effect of a restructuring that began in 1988 and included plant consolidations and management changes, among other things. Having reached its projected goal of $3 million in cash flow for the second quarter, and with a $9 million order backlog, the company said it expects the third quarter to produce "favorable operating comparisons" to year-earlier results.
