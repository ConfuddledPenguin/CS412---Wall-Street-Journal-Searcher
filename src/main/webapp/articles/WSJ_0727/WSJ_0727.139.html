Business Brief -- Bank of East Asia Ltd.: Hong Kong Bank Says Net Gained 15% in First Half

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/27/90

Bank of East Asia Ltd. said consolidated profit rose 15% in the year's first six months. The Hong Kong bank said consolidated profit, after undisclosed transfers to inner reserves, rose to 104 million Hong Kong dollars (US$13.4 million), or 38 cents a share, from HK$90 million, or 33 cents a share, a year ago.

The board proposed raising the per-share interim dividend to 25 cents from 21 cents.

Keith Wu, research manager at Citicorp Scrimgeour Vickers Hong Kong Ltd., said the performance was surprisingly strong, considering the weakness of the local economy in the period. But he cautioned that the figures are difficult to interpret because of local banks' ability to move cash to and from secret reserves.

Mr. Wu called the dividend "very generous," adding that he expects the full-year payout to total around 75 cents a share. He said he is still forecasting the bank's full-year profit at HK$388 million.
