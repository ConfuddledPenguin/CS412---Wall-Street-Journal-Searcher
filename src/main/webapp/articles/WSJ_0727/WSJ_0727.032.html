General Motors Set To Idle 11 U.S. Plants For Model Changes

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/27/90

DETROIT -- General Motors Corp. said it will idle 11 U.S. assembly plants next week and in August to retool for 1991 models. The GM plant that builds the Buick Reatta will be shut five weeks, the longest of the shutdowns. Most of the other shutdowns will last one to three weeks.

Chrysler Corp. said it will idle the St. Louis assembly plant that builds its long wheelbase minivans for four weeks beginning Monday to retool for a major model change. Chrysler also has added one week to the shutdown at its Jeep factory in Toledo, Ohio, because of a "parts shortage." The plant was originally scheduled to reopen July 30.

Ford Motor Co. said it is planning daily overtime at its car assembly plants in Wixom and Wayne, Mich. The No. 2 auto maker has scheduled Saturday overtime at truck plants in Twin Cities, Minn., Louisville, Ky., and St. Louis.

The 10 major domestic auto makers planned to build 86,516 cars this week, up 2.6% from the 84,312 built a year earlier.
