RMED International Says Net to Be $90,000 For the Third Period

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/27/90

SEDONA, Ariz. -- RMED International Inc. expects to report profit of $90,000, or two cents a share, for the third quarter ended June 30. Barry Plaga, chief financial officer, said the result is primarily attributable to the sale of a 50% interest in a flushable-diaper patent to a Canadian investment firm in June for $225,000. A year ago, the company had a loss of $415,562, or 12 cents a share.

However, RMED conceded it is having problems bringing the flushable diaper to market. In March, the company said the diaper would be in production in May, but Edward Reiss, chairman and president, said RMED couldn't find anyone to make the diaper machine. The company is now building one from "parts of old parts," a process that will take a few more months, Mr. Reiss said.

Messrs. Plaga and Reiss said they expect revenue to drop sharply this year because three new competitors have entered the biodegradable-diaper market. RMED's TenderCare diapers, which the company claims decompose in landfills, had been its core product, but "we lost the momentum," Mr. Reiss said.

In the year ending Sept. 30, Mr. Plaga expects a loss of $510,000, or 14 cents a share, on revenue of $1.9 million to $2 million. Last year, RMED had a loss of $1.6 million, or 48 cents a share, on revenue of $3.3 million.

Mr. Reiss said consumer skepticism about products called "biodegradable" also hurt sales. In March, for instance, environmentalists specifically denounced TenderCare diapers, charging that they don't truly break down in landfills.

In response to such criticism, Mr. Reiss said RMED is shifting advertising emphasis to stress that TenderCare diapers have fewer chemicals than competitors'.
