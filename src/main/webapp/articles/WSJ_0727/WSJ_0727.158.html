Retrial Ordered in Cases Of Three Futures Traders

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/27/90

CHICAGO -- A federal judge ordered three Swiss-franc futures traders to be retried March 5 on fraud and racketeering charges on which a jury earlier this month couldn't reach a verdict. In the first courtroom test of the federal investigation of alleged futures trading fraud, two of the traders, Robert Mosky and Danny Scheck, were found guilty of eight counts of violating the Commodity Exchange Act out of more than 100 counts against them. The third trader, David Zatz, wasn't found guilty on any of the charges against him. The jury was deadlocked on 86 counts against the three traders.

The jury's unwillingness to convict any of the traders on fraud charges, or to find Mr. Mosky, a floor broker, guilty of racketeering conspiracy, was widely seen as a blow to the government's multimillion-dollar trading probe.

The government said it would modify the indictments against the traders, and defense attorneys said they would move to have the charges dismissed.

Two more trials of indicted futures traders are scheduled to begin in September.
