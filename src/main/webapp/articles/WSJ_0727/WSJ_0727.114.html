Panel Seeks Immunity for 2

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/27/90

WASHINGTON -- The House Energy and Commerce Committee voted to seek immunity for Michael Milken and Ivan Boesky in return for testimony on junk bonds. The two are expected to testify as part of a probe by the subcommittee on oversight and investigations into questions surrounding junk bonds and securities underwritten by Drexel Burnham Lambert Inc. In particular, the subcommittee has focused on about 500 partnerships that included Drexel employees and bought into Drexel-sponsored deals.

Mr. Milken, who in the spring pleaded guilty to six felony counts of securities-law violations, is to be sentenced around Oct. 1, after which he will be immunized and cooperate with prosecutors and the Securities and Exchange Commission.

Mr. Boesky, a former takeover-stock speculator, pleaded guilty to insider trading in 1986 and in April was paroled from federal prison after serving about 18 months. Mr. Boesky also has agreed to do extensive interviews with the subcommittee staff before his testimony, a panel staff member said. Mr. Milken has refused to do staff interviews, the staff member said.

A spokesman for Mr. Milken said his client will testify when the immunity grant comes through. A lawyer for Mr. Boesky wouldn't confirm whether his client would cooperate with the subcommittee.
