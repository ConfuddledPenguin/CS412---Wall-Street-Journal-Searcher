New Securities Issues

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/27/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATES

CSX Corp. -- $250 million of 9 1/2% notes, due Aug. 1, 2000, was priced at 99.62 to yield 9.56%. The noncallable notes were priced at a spread of 1.08 percentage points above the Treasury's 10-year note. Rated single-A-3 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and triple-B-plus by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by First Boston Corp.

Occidental Petroleum Corp. -- $150 million of senior notes, due Aug. 1, 2010, was priced late Wednesday at par to yield 11.125%. The notes, which were issued off the company's medium-term note shelf registration, were priced at a spread of 2.55 percentage points above the Treasury's 10-year note. Rated Baa-2 by Moody's and triple-B-minus by S&P, the issue will be sold through Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette Securities Corp.

BankAmerica Corp. -- $125 million of 10-year subordinated notes due Aug. 1, 2000, was priced with a coupon of 9.70% at 99.502 to yield 9.779%. The noncallable notes were priced at a spread of 1.30 percentage points above the Treasury's 10-year note. Rated single-A-3 by Moody's and single-A-minus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets.

MUNICIPAL

New York State Dormitory Authority -- $61 million of special act school districts revenue bonds, due July 1, 1993-2003, 2013 and 2015, with capital appreciation bonds, due 2004-2009, were priced by a Lazard Freres & Co. group. The bonds are priced to yield from 6.10% in 1993 to 7.17% in 2013. Current interest serial bonds are priced to yield from 6.10% in 1993 to 6.95% in 2003. Term bonds due in 2013 are priced with a coupon of 7% at 98 1/8 to yield 7.17%. Term bonds due in 2015, are priced with a coupon of 6% at 87 1/4 to yield 7.10%. Capital appreciation bonds are priced to yield to maturity from 7.00% in 2004 to 7.05% in 2006-2009. The bonds are insured and rated triple-A by Moody's and S&P.

ASSET-BACKED

FCC National Bank -- $700 million of securities backed by credit-card receivables with a coupon rate of 8 7/8% and a yield of 8.907% through Salomon Brothers Inc. The offering, through a special trust called First Chicago Master Trust II, was priced at a spread of 0.695 percentage point over three-year Treasury notes. The amount was increased from the expected $500 million. Expected final maturity is Feb. 15, 1994. Stated final maturity is June 15, 1995. The securities are expected to be rated triple-A by S&P and Moody's based on the quality of the underlying credit-card loans and a letter of credit from Industrial Bank of Japan covering 12% of the offering. FCC National Bank, a unit of First Chicago Corp. is the seller and servicer of the underlying credit-card loans.

EUROBONDS

Toyo Sash Co. (Japan) -- $150 million of bonds with equity-purchase warrants due Aug. 16, 1994, priced with a 4 3/4% coupon at par. Each $10,000 bond carries two warrants, exercisable from Aug. 30, 1990, through Aug. 2, 1994, to buy company shares at an expected premium of 2 1/2% to the closing share price when terms are fixed Thursday. Also $150 million of bonds with equity-purchase warrants due Aug. 19, 1997, priced with a 5 1/2% coupon at par. Each $10,000 bond carries two warrants, exercisable from Aug. 30, 1990, through Aug. 5, 1997, to buy company shares at an expected premium of 2 1/2% to the closing share price when terms are fixed Thursday.

Nationwide Anglia Building Society (United Kingdom) -- 300 million marks ($184.7 million) of floating-rate notes at six-month London interbank offered rate plus 1/32, priced at 100.20 and due Aug. 28, 1995, via Westdeutsche Landesbank Girozentrale. First Libor fixing Aug. 23.

IBM International Finance NV (U.S. parent) -- One billion French francs ($183.8 million) of 10% bonds due Aug. 29, 1997, priced at 101 7/8 to yield 10% via BNP Capital Markets Ltd. Support agreement from IBM World Trade Corp.

Credito Italiano, Cayman Islands (Italian parent) -- $150 million of variable-rate depository receipts due in August 2000, through the Law Debenture Trust Corp. via Merrill Lynch International Ltd. Initial coupon 3/16 point above the three-month Libor. Alternatively, coupon will float 1/2 point above Libor for the first five years and at a margin of 5/8 point thereafter. Sinking fund beginning in the fifth year that will retire 20% of the issue each year, giving it an average life of eight years.

Osterreichische Kontrollbank AG (OKB) -- 150 million Canadian dollars (US$130.1 million) of 11 3/4% bonds due Aug. 21, 1995, priced at 101 1/8 to yield 11.96% less fees via Deutsche Bank Capital Markets Ltd. Guaranteed by the Republic of Austria. Fees 1 7/8.

Daimler Benz International Finance B.V. (West German parent) -- 200 million marks of bonds due Aug. 29, 2000, priced at 100.80, via Deutsche Bank. Coupon for first three years is 9 1/2%. The coupon for final seven years is 15 1/8% less the six-month Libor. Guaranteed by Daimler Benz AG in Stuttgart.

Kubota Ltd. (Japan) -- 150 million Swiss francs ($108.8 million) of 7 1/2% privately placed notes due Aug. 9, 1995, priced at 99 5/8 via Swiss Bank Corp.

Toyota Motor Credit Corp. (Japanese parent) -- 125 million Canadian dollars of 11 7/8% bonds due Aug. 24, 1995, at an issue price of 101 1/4 and fixed reoffering price of 99 5/8 via Wood Gundy Inc.

Landesbank Rheinland-Pfalz (West Germany) -- 100 million Swiss francs of 7% privately placed notes due Aug. 21, 1995, priced at 101 1/4 via Union Bank of Switzerland.

Yuken Kogyo Co. (Japan) -- $60 million of bonds due Aug. 9, 1994, with equity-purchase warrants, with a 5 1/8% coupon at par via Daiwa Europe Ltd. Guaranteed by International Bank of Japan. Each $5,000 bond carries one warrant, exercisable from Aug. 23, 1990, through July 26, 1994, to buy company shares at an expected premium of 2 1/2% to the closing share price when terms are fixed Tuesday.

Uniden 21 Corp. (Japan) -- 55 million Swiss francs of privately placed convertible notes due Sept. 30, 1995, with a fixed 5% coupon at par via Union Bank of Switzerland. Callable after three years at 104 declining by one point semiannually. Each 50,000 Swiss franc note convertible from Sept. 5, 1990, to Sept. 13, 1995. Conversion terms fixed Thursday.
