Business Brief -- Cigna Corp.: Firm No Longer Plans Sale Of Financial Services Unit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/27/90

Cigna Corp., Philadelphia, said it no longer plans to sell its individual financial services division, which had been on the block for the past 18 months. Cigna said that while there had been several interested buyers for the division -- including a group that would have involved some management members -- none matched the price that the company was seeking. Also, financing, which might have included high-yield bonds, became harder to arrange during this time.

The division markets individual life and disability insurance. It also markets investment products and financial planning to small business owners, high net-worth individuals and corporations. In the last few years, Cigna has been working to position itself as a seller of commercial property/casualty and group health insurance to major corporations.

Wilson H. Taylor, Cigna's chairman and chief executive officer, said, "The outlook for the life and disability business has improved," due to market changes, as well as steps taken internally to improve the division's operations. He added that the division's 1989 results were strong, posting a more than 15% return on equity, and its 1990 performance suggests continued good results.
