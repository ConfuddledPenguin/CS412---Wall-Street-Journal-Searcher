Data General Loss Exceeds Forecast For 3rd Quarter

  • Author: Suzanne Alexander
  • Date: 07/27/90

WESTBORO, Mass. -- Data General Corp. reported a loss of $21.4 million, or 71 cents a share, in its third quarter ended June 30. The loss was far bigger than analysts had expected, though it shrank slightly from the year-ago deficit. Revenue declined 1.2% to $302.4 million from $306.1 million a year earlier.

A year earlier, the computer maker reported a loss of $23.1 million, or 79 cents a share, after a $6.3 million gain from the sale of a facility in Sunnyvale, Calif.

Data General reversed an earlier prediction that it might break even or turn in a profit in the fourth quarter. "Given the softer demand we are seeing in the continued sluggish market for proprietary computer systems within the computer industry, breaking even is not likely in the fourth quarter," Data General said. The company declined to give an estimate for the fourth quarter.

In the year-ago fourth quarter, Data General had a loss of $84.1 million, or $2.86 a share, after an $80 million restructuring charge. Revenue was $357.1 million.

Data General cited the sluggish proprietary computer market and competitive pricing pressures industrywide as reasons for the disappointing third-quarter results.

"While we remain cautious with respect to the near term, we are optimistic that demand will improve during subsequent quarters," said Ronald L. Skates, Data General's president and chief executive.

Analysts said the results were in line with "just about every other computer company." However, earnings were still "significantly lower than expected," said John Adams, an analyst at Adams, Harkness & Hill Inc. in Boston.

Recently, the company met with analysts to discuss its outlook. However, "no indication at that time" was given of the extent of the company's expected loss, said Jay P. Stevens, an analyst with Dean Witter Reynolds Inc. "I had a 25 cent (a share) loss estimate," said Mr. Stevens.

Analysts blamed the company's woes on slower-than-expected sales overseas and intense product discounting in the U.S.

In the first nine months, Data General had a loss of $50.5 million, or $1.69 a share, on revenue of $907.9 million. In the year-ago period, the company had a loss of $35.7 million, or $1.22 a share, on revenue of $957.3 million.
