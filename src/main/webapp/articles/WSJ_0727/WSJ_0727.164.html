Abbott Labs, Schoellhorn Reach Lawsuit Settlement

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/27/90

NORTH CHICAGO, Ill. -- Abbott Laboratories and its ousted chairman, Robert A. Schoellhorn, settled name-calling litigation in an agreement that would seem to allow him to keep contested stock worth about $4.4 million. The bizarre dispute between Mr. Schoellhorn and Abbott's board burst into the open earlier this year when he sued the company and some directors in Cook County, Ill., court, trying to stop them from firing him. He accused some directors of being liars.

The company retaliated by calling Mr. Schoellhorn a thief, saying he misappropriated Abbott property, and tried to deny him 51,600 restricted shares of Abbott common stock.

The stock has since split two for one. In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Abbott stock closed at $43, down 50 cents. At that price, the post-split 103,200 shares are valued at about $4.4 million.

Abbott is a pharmaceutical and hospital products concern.

In a single-paragraph statement issued late yesterday, Abbott said the settlement called for dismissal of Mr. Schoellhorn's lawsuit and that he would "retire from Abbott Laboratories effective August 31, 1990."

Neither Abbott nor Mr. Schoellhorn's lawyer, John H. Mathias Jr., would elaborate on the statement. But the wording implies the executive would be entitled to benefits he would have received had he retired normally, including the stock.
