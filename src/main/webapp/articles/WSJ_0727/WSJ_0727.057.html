Stock-Index Arbitrage Tops 50% of Program Trading

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/27/90

NEW YORK -- Stock-index arbitrage accounted for more than half of total program trading volume during the week of July 9 through 13, the New York Stock Exchange said yesterday. Stock-index arbitrage, the most controversial form of program trading, in which traders jump between stock-index futures and options and underlying stocks to lock in profits, accounted for 52.9% of all program trades during July 9 through 13. The previous week, stock-index arbitrage accounted for 45% of all program trading.

The Big Board said that of the top five program-trading firms for the week, about 79% of all index arbitrage was conducted by General Electric Co.'s Kidder, Peabody & Co. unit, Morgan Stanley Group Inc.'s Morgan Stanley & Co. unit, Susquehanna Investment Group and CS First Boston Inc.'s First Boston Corp. unit.

Aside from stock-index arbitrage, program trading also describes strategies that involve the purchase or sale of baskets of 15 or more stocks.

Overall, program trading amounted to 7.9% of average daily volume of 171.3 million shares on the Big Board during the week, down from 10.9% of average daily volume of 125.1 million shares the previous week.
