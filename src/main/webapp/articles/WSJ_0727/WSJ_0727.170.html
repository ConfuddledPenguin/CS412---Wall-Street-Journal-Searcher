UPS and Teamsters Agree To Extend Ballot Deadline

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/27/90

GREENWICH, Conn. -- United Parcel Service of America Inc. said it agreed with the International Brotherhood of Teamsters to extend by 10 days the deadline for the return of ballots on a three-year UPS contract. The deadline, which was July 30, will be extended to Aug. 10. The agreement removes the possibility that teamsters would strike the country's largest deliverer of packages when the current three-year UPS contract expires at midnight Tuesday.

UPS said the union sought to extend the deadline because many teamster members had failed to receive their ballots in the mail. The extension would allow more time for UPS teamster workers to receive those ballots.

About 140,000 UPS workers are eligible to vote on the contract. The union has advised its members to reject the company's "final" wage and benefit offer.

UPS said it agreed that the contract, if approved, would be retroactive to Wednesday.

"We think it's a reasonable request under the circumstances," said Kent C. Nelson, chairman and chief executive of UPS. "We're glad it will enable more of our people to cast votes on this important offer," he said.
