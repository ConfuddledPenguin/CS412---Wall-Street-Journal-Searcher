U.S. Trade Officers Warn Of `Fight' Over Farm Aid

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/27/90

WASHINGTON -- U.S. trade officials warned that a "big fight" with the European Community could ensue if the EC continues to hold up a global agreement to phase out billions of dollars in agricultural subsidies. The officials were reacting to the failure of a key negotiating session in Geneva to produce a "framework agreement" on reducing these subsidies as agreed upon at the international economic summit earlier this month in Houston.

This week's meeting, held under the auspices of the General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade, also failed to resolve differences between the U.S. and other countries on how to craft other agreements, including those on investment flows and on the textile and services trades.

EC officials said, however, that there weren't any grounds for the U.S. to be "unduly pessimistic," claiming that there has been progress even in the agriculture sector. The U.S. has argued that the EC's unwillingness to scrap farm subsidies as fast as the U.S. wants has been a major obstacle to the success of the four-year round of talks which ends in December.
