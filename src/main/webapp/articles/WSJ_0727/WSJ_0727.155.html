What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/27/90

GM AND FORD POSTED sharply lower second-quarter earnings, reflecting hard times in the U.S. market and economic unrest in Brazil. Ford's profit tumbled 45% while GM reported a 38% decline. The remainder of the year could be difficult, too, amid weak demand in the U.S. and competition from Japan. ---

A former Drexel official, Peter Ackerman, has become the subject of a new government probe into possible price manipulation, according to people familiar with the investigation. Mr. Ackerman was once top deputy to ex-junk bond king Michael Milken.

---

A plan to propose higher taxes on alcoholic beverages and limits on deductions for state and local taxes was tentatively agreed to by the administration and congressional Republicans, according to people close to the plan. But support was shaky.

---

OPEC ministers neared agreement on a new production ceiling intended to reduce the current world oil glut and push up crude oil prices.

---

Growing real estate problems in California are hurting lenders. Coast Savings Financial reported a $28.7 million second-quarter loss, following losses by CalFed and HomeFed.

---

GE was ordered to pay a $10 million criminal fine and agreed to pay $20 million in civil penalties in connection with fraudulent defense contract billings to the U.S. Army.

---

Sallie Mae has proposed taking over the portfolio of a troubled student-loan guarantor, a move that would greatly expand its influence in the education financing field.

---

House and Senate conferees agreed on comprehensive oil-spill legislation that would end a 16-year congressional stalemate on the issue.

---

Cigarette package warnings don't protect tobacco makers from product liability lawsuits, New Jersey's Supreme Court ruled. The decision opens the door for a review of the issue by the U.S. Supreme Court.

---

A House panel voted to reimpose rate regulation on basic cable-TV service. The measure also would benefit fledgling competitors of cable.

---

UAL's profit rose slightly in the second quarter, aided by the sale of aircraft. But Midway Airlines recorded a hefty loss for the period.

---

Westinghouse agreed to acquire Knoll International, doubling the size of its office-furniture business.

---

Eli Lilly was named in a second lawsuit charging that its popular new antidepressant drug Prozac can cause violent and destructive behavior. Lilly's stock fell $4.375 to $79.625.

---

The Senate embraced a proposal to revamp the federal crop-insurance program and cover its underwriting losses, putting senators at odds with the House's intention to scuttle the long-troubled program.

---

Microsoft is planning a costly campaign to win a bigger piece of the market for software that ties together personal computers. The effort will cost the company up to $50 million.

---

Continental Bank moved closer to full private ownership as the FDIC approved plans to sell a 26% stake in the Chicago banking concern.
