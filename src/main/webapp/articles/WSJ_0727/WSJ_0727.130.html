International Brief -- Istituto Finanziario Industriale: Net Rose 52% in Fiscal 1990 At Financial Holding Firm

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/27/90

Istituto Finanziario Industriale, the key financial holding company of the Agnelli family, announced it posted net income of 166.9 billion lire ($140.3 million) in the fiscal year ended March 31, up 52% from 110.1 billion lire a year earlier. Milan-based IFI, which directly or indirectly owns about 45% of Fiat S.p.A., Italy's largest private-sector industrial group, said fiscal 1990 earnings included a one-time gain of 51.8 billion lire from the sale of part of its holding in Gruppo Editoriale Fabbri S.p.A. to Gruppo Rizzoli.

Without the one-time gain, earnings were up 4.5% from fiscal 1989.

Revenue was 150.6 billion lire, up 17% from the previous year; financial charges fell to 8.3 billion lire in fiscal 1990 from 17.7 billion lire in 1989.
