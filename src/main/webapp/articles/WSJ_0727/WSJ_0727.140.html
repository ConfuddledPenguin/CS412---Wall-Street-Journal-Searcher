Two Ferruzzi Units Set Board Sessions To Convene Today

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/27/90

MILAN -- Montedison S.p.A. and Ferruzzi Agricola Finanziaria S.p.A., two companies controlled by Italian agricultural-industrial group Ferruzzi Finanziaria S.p.A., both scheduled separate board meetings for today. Although there was no indication given about what might be discussed at the meetings, and a Ferruzzi spokesman declined to discuss the agenda, it has been rumored in recent months that Ferruzzi Agricola, which owns 45% of Montedison, was considering a merger with the chemical group.

Italian securities regulators yesterday morning suspended trading in all key issues of the Ferruzzi group, including Montedison, on the Milan stock market pending an important announcement. Trading in Montedison was suspended on the New York Stock Exchange as well.

The Ferruzzi spokesman said the company had requested that the Italian securities regulatory agency "take measures to protect the interests of the market" after Ferruzzi had learned that "rumors" about any possible decisions by the boards of Montedison and Ferruzzi Agricola "had reached some news agencies." The spokesman declined to elaborate.

Analysts speculated that Ferruzzi Agricola might announce a public tender offer for the 51.3% of Montedison it doesn't already own. Ferruzzi Agricola could finance this bid in cash with the proceeds from the sale, earlier this year, of La Fondiaria Assicurazioni S.p.A. to Gaic S.p.A., one analyst with a securities house in London speculated.

A tender offer to be paid in Ferruzzi Agricola stock isn't likely, he said, because that would dilute Ferruzzi Finanziaria shareholding to less than 50%.

Such a restructuring would turn Montedison into the key industrial holding company of the Ferruzzi group. It would then own majority stakes in Eridania, which heads the group's agro-industrial sector, and its chemical interests.
