American Savings Had Second-Quarter Loss Totaling $40 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/27/90

WHITE PLAINS, N.Y. -- American Savings Bank reported a $40 million second-quarter net loss, reflecting an increase of $41 million in reserves against possible losses on bad loans. American Savings and many other Northeast banks and thrifts have been hurt by the slump in the region's real estate markets.

The New York state chartered bank made loan-loss provisions of $54.3 million in 1990's first six months. American Savings said it will proceed with a "back to basics" strategy, consolidating its Riverhead Savings operations and pursuing the sale of other subsidiaries to cut costs and pare assets.

American's second-quarter net loss equals $6.89 a share, compared with net income of $7.4 million, or $1.01 a share, in the year-earlier period. The year-earlier period included a one-time gain of $3.5 million before taxes from an insurance settlement for a subsidiary.

The latest quarter's net interest income before provision for loan losses fell 18% to $19.5 million from $23.7 million in the yearearlier period.

For the first six months of 1990, American Savings reported a loss of $49.4 million, or $8.67 a share, compared with earnings of $13.9 million, or $1.88 a share. The year-ago period reflects the one-time gain. Net interest income excluding provisions for loan losses declined 21% to $39 million from $49.6 million.

At June 30, American's assets totaled $4.14 billion, down 6.5% from Dec. 31 and 9.5% from June 30, 1989.
