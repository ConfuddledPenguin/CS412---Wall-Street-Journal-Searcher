International Brief -- Asahi Glass Co.: Pretax Profit Gained 2.3% To a Record in First Half

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/27/90

Asahi Glass Co. said pretax profit in the first half rose 2.3% to a record 40.14 billion yen ($267.6 million). The Japanese chemical and glass maker had profit of 39.23 billion yen in the year-earlier period.

Sales rose 12% to a record 487.68 billion yen from 437.04 billion yen. Net income also grew to a record, at 21.84 billion yen, or 18.74 yen a share, up 3.9% from 21.02 billion yen, or 18.08 yen a share. The company plans to pay an unchanged dividend of 4.5 yen a share.

Sales of glass and construction materials, accounting for 51.6% of total sales, gained 13% to 251.81 billion yen. Sales of fabricated glass had strong gains, reflecting domestic automobile sales, which more than compensated for a decline in auto exports, officials said.

Sales of chemicals, accounting for 36.2% of overall sales, gained 6.8% to 176.4 billion yen.

Earnings were damped by higher labor and transportation costs, a spokesman said. Higher raw materials costs, largely reflecting the yen's weakness in the first quarter, also hurt profit, he said.
