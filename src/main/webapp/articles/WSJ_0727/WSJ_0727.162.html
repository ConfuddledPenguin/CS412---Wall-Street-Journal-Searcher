Business Brief -- Martin Marietta Corp.: Firm Is Chosen to Develop, Build Jet Thrust Reversers

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/27/90

Martin Marietta Corp., Bethesda, Md., said it got a contract valued at about $300 million to design and develop a new jet engine thrust reverser for Pratt & Whitney and to produce 200 units. The thrust reversers, which redirect the flow of jet engine exhaust and help brake aircraft after landing, will be designed and produced by Martin Marietta Aero & Naval Systems in Baltimore.

Martin Marietta said production will begin in 1993. The contract includes provisions for follow-up production of as many as 300 additional thrust reversers, potentially doubling the value of the contract.

Pratt & Whitney, a unit of United Technologies Corp., is based in East Hartford, Conn. The company designs and produces commercial and military aircraft engines.
