Go-Video Wins Round Against Japan Firms In Lawsuit on VCRs

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/27/90

SCOTTSDALE, Ariz. -- Go-Video Inc. said it won a round in federal court against five Japanese electronics companies it has sued for allegedly blocking its efforts to market a dual-deck videocassette recorder in the U.S. A federal court in Phoenix, Ariz., denied the motions of the defendants to have Go-Video's antitrust suit dismissed, Go-Video said. The ruling means that the case will go to trial before a federal jury in Phoenix within 120 days, to determine if the Japanese VCR manufacturers conspired in violation of federal antitrust laws to block the marketing of dual-deck VCRs in the U.S., Go-Video said.

The court also rejected the defendants' challenge to Go-Video's basic dual-deck VCR patent, the company said. However, the court refused to reconsider a previous decision dismissing Go-Video's claims against Sharp Corp. Go-Video said it intends to appeal the court's dismissal of Sharp. The defendants remaining in the case include Matsushita Electric Industrial Co., Victor Co. of Japan, Sony Corp., Sanyo Electric Co., and NEC Corp.

In addition, Go-Video said the court lifted a stay on a second lawsuit Go-Video brought against these same companies, including Sharp, alleging a world-wide conspiracy to monopolize consumer electronics products.

R. Terren Dunlap, chairman and chief executive officer of Go-Video, said the company "won the first half of the battle" by bringing its dual-deck VCR to market on June 30.
