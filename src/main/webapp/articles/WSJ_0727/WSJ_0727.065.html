Big Board Bars A Broker, Citing Bounced Checks

  • Author: Robert Daniels
  • Date: 07/27/90

NEW YORK -- The New York Stock Exchange barred for five years a broker who, it charged, bounced checks on two brokerage firms and misstated information on securities-industry forms required for employment. The exchange also charged that William F. Falbo Sr. of Palatine, Ill., violated rules regarding margin, or credit. In addition to the five-year bar from membership, Mr. Falbo was fined $10,000. Mr. Falbo, who denied the charges in a letter to the exchange, couldn't be reached for comment. He no longer is employed in the securities industry, the Big Board said.

The case points up the difficulty brokerage firms and regulators have in checking the backgrounds of potential employees.

The exchange said that in 1985 and 1986, Mr. Falbo, while employed at Merrill Lynch & Co., wrote five personal checks, totaling nearly $30,000, and deposited them in an account at Merrill. The checks were returned for insufficient funds.

The Big Board also said Mr. Falbo bought stock-index options while he had insufficient funds in his securities account to pay for them. Merrill notified the exchange that the broker was discharged from the firm May 20, 1986; Merrill said he had permitted overdrafts and abused check-writing privileges.

On June 2, 1986, Mr. Falbo joined A.G. Edwards & Sons Inc., allegedly failing to report his discharge by Merrill Lynch. In June and July 1986, he bought stock-index options with insufficient funds in his Edwards account to pay for them, the exchange said.

On July 8, the exchange added, he deposited a $5,000 check into his Edwards account; the check was returned to Edwards on July 15 for lack of sufficient funds. Edwards redeposited the check on Mr. Falbo's request; the broker stopped payment on the check July 22.

Edwards, which sustained a $7,431 loss on the options transactions, discharged Mr. Falbo July 9 for failing to disclose a reportable item on his application and for violating margin rules, the exchange said.

Mr. Falbo joined First of Michigan Corp. in September 1987, allegedly failing to tell the firm that the previous February he had pleaded guilty in state court in Illinois to a misdemeanor involving issuance of a check with insufficient funds. He also failed to disclose that two judgments A.G. Edwards won against him were outstanding. He voluntarily resigned from First of Michigan in January 1988, the exchange said.

The exchange also said Mr. Falbo has declined to cooperate with its inquiries about his termination from Merrill Lynch. But in a January 1987 letter to the Big Board, Mr. Falbo denied Merrill Lynch's assertions and said those assertions led A.G. Edwards to dismiss him.
