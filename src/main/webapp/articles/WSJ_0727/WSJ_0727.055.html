Wholesale Club Share Earnings

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/27/90

INDIANAPOLIS -- Wholesale Club Inc. said it expects second-quarter earnings to fall to between seven cents and nine cents a share compared with 15 cents a year earlier. The discount-store said for several years it has gradually lowered its prices and margins. In the second quarter, this process was accelerated, bringing margins down to those of other large companies in the industry. Wholesale Club said the move is designed to increase its share of market.

The company said it expects six-month earnings to increase to between 17 cents to 19 cents a share, compared with last year's three cents.
