Marketing & Media: GE's Cable Channel CNBC Picks Barber for President

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/27/90

NEW YORK -- National Broadcasting Co.'s consumer and business cable channel, CNBC, selected Albert F. Barber, a financial executive without a background in programming or journalism, as its new president. He succeeds Michael Eskridge, who resigned earlier this week. Mr. Barber, 44 years old, has been executive vice-president and chief financial officer at NBC, a unit of General Electric Co., since 1987.

"My job first and foremost is to manage the business and motivate people," Mr. Barber said in an interview. "I have a financial background and that's what CNBC's programming is all about. I talk the businessman's language."

CNBC, a 24-hour channel based in Fort Lee, N.J., started in April 1989 with 13 million subscribers; it now has about 16.8 million subscribers. But growth has been slow, and the channel is still far short of its 30-million-subscriber breakeven point.

Mr. Barber said he "raised his hand" when the job became available, and NBC president Robert C. Wright was "pleasantly surprised. . . ."

A 20-year veteran of GE, Mr. Barber said he didn't anticipate immediate changes in CNBC personnel or programming.

CNBC is jointly owned by NBC Cable and Cablevision Systems Corp., Woodbury, N.Y. Mr. Barber, no stranger to CNBC, is one of two NBC executives to serve on its board of directors.
