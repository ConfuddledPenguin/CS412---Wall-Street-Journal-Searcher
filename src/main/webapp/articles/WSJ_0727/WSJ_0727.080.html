Procter & Gamble Purchase

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/27/90

CINCINNATI -- Procter & Gamble Co. said it completed the purchase of the toiletry and fragrance businesses of American Cyanamid Co.'s Shulton Group, excluding the Pierre Cardin fragrance line, for between $300 million and $370 million. As reported, P&G is acquiring the Old Spice and Santa Fe men's toiletries and fragrances. The final purchase price hasn't yet been determined, P&G said. It will depend on the price American Cyanamid, based in Wayne, N.J., obtains for the Breck hair care and Lady's Choice personal-care product lines, which also are part of Shulton.

P&G produces a wide range of household, food, drug and personal-care products. American Cyanamid is a major chemical company.
