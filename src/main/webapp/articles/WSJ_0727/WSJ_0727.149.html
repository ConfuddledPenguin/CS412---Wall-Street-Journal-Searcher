Business Brief -- Cie. Generale d'Electricite: GEC-Alsthom Wins Accord For Moroccan Power Plant

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/27/90

GEC-Alsthom N.V. said a consortium it leads won a turnkey contract valued at 3.6 billion French francs ($660.8 million) for a power station in Morocco. The company, a joint unit of France's Cie. Generale d'Electricite and General Electric Co. of the U.K., said the thermal power station will use coal, oil, and natural gas as fuels. The plant will be situated at Jorf Lasfar, south of Casablanca.

The power station will comprise two generating units, each with an output capacity of 330 megawatts, and will be the first of a series of six identical power generating stations.

GEC-Alsthom said its partners in the contract are Babcock & Wilcox Espanola (Instituto Nacional de Industria), a Spanish unit of Babcock & Wilcox of the U.S., which is a unit of McDermott Inc., as well as Dragados & Construccionnes S.A., a Spanish construction group.

GEC-Alsthom said the project will be financed jointly by France and Spain within the framework of bilateral financial protocols among Morocco and the French and Spanish governments.
