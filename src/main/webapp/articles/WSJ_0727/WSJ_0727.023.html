Asian Study Sees Outlook Improving in 4 Economies

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/27/90

HONG KONG -- Prospects for earnings growth improved in Hong Kong, Malaysia, South Korea and Thailand during June, according to a monthly survey of analysts by Institutional Brokers Estimate System. The earnings outlook was unchanged in Japan, Australia and Singapore, while prospects dimmed slightly in New Zealand. IBES, a unit of the New York-based securities firm Lynch, Jones & Ryan, calculates "consensus" earnings estimates based on thousands of analysts' company and market reports. It projects 12 months ahead by combining pershare earnings estimates for each company's current fiscal year with forecasts for its coming year.

The system also measures the level of disagreement among analysts, which is reflected in a so-called uncertainty estimate. In addition, the statistics include a figure for earnings revisions during the month. The figure is expressed as a percentage showing whether upward or downward revisions dominated.

Because of insufficient data, IBES doesn't provide estimates for Taiwan or Indonesia. This month, the system begins offering projections on the Philippines.
