Bolar Pharmaceutical Recalls Four Drugs After Audit Process

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/27/90

COPIAGUE, N.Y. -- Bolar Pharmaceutical Co. said it is recalling four drug products that accounted for about 5% of its sales and about 16% of income before taxes and extraordinary items in 1989. The generic drug company, which in February stopped selling all but about four or five products while it continues to conduct an audit of its products, also said it elected to stop manufacturing 14 additional drug products for economic reasons.

Bolar said the products it was recalling aren't currently being sold. Last year, before the company began recalling and reviewing its products, Bolar had a line of more than 70 products.

The recalls Bolar announced resulted from the audit process. Bolar also said it was starting to submit applications to the Food and Drug Administration for reapproval of some drugs, which it didn't name, following the audit.

The FDA in March withdrew approval of Bolar's generic version of Dyazide, a blood-pressure medicine. Bolar recalled that drug in February as evidence mounted Bolar had passed off brand-name drugs as its own to the FDA.

Bolar said three of the products it is recalling are pharmaceutical; their recall results from the company's determination that the drugs weren't manufactured in accordance with FDA approvals, or that Bolar's records don't accurately reflect the composition or processes by which the drugs or batches of them were manufactured.

The fourth recall comes as a result of Bolar's determination that test results on the drug, which were necessary for FDA approval, can't be considered reliable. Bolar said it had information indicating that the Bolar-imprinted capsules may have contained material manufactured by the brand name company to which the Bolar version was compared.
