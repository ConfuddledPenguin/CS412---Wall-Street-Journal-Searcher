Canada Union Warns Of Strike If Chrysler Rejects Pattern Pact

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/27/90

TORONTO -- Canadian Auto Workers President Robert White warned Chrysler Corp.'s Canadian subsidiary that the union would strike if the company refused to accept any contract agreement worked out with the other Canadian units of the Big Three auto makers. Speaking at the opening of contract negotiations, Kenneth Francese, Chrysler Canada's vice president, human resources, said the company hadn't yet decided whether it would follow the traditional system of pattern bargaining, in which the Big Three all fall in with the contract terms worked out between the union and one of the companies targeted for talks. But, he said, "If something is done by one of the other companies that we believe would negatively impact Chrysler in its competitiveness, then we'll have to take a look at it."

The parent company, which began contract negotiations in the U.S. last week, has told the United Auto Workers that it wants to be the target company, because it believes it can't afford to be as generous as General Motors Corp. or Ford Motor Co. If it isn't the target, Chrysler would like a different contract. The UAW wants to maintain pattern bargaining, but hasn't said what it would do if Chrysler opts out.

Contracts at the Big Three in both Canada and the U.S. expire Sept. 14.

Chrysler also has offered its U.S. workers stock in the company and bonus payments in return for greater flexibility to operate its plants. Mr. Francese said Chrysler Canada hasn't offered such incentives, but said the company considers more flexible work rules its top bargaining goal. Chrysler Canada's success in this area "will determine the size of any economic package we may negotiate," he said.

Mr. White said the union presented Chrysler Canada with nearly identical demands to those made to the Canadian units of GM and Ford earlier this week. The union is seeking higher wages, better protection against inflation and a limited degree of job security.
