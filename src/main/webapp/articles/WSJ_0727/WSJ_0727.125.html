HAWAIIAN ELECTRIC INDUSTRIES Inc. (Honolulu) -- Moody's Investors Service Inc. said it downgraded its ratings on this utility holding company's medium-term notes to single-A-3 from single-A-1, and on commercial paper to prime-2 from prime-1. The rating concern said about $115 million of securities are affected. Moody's said it believes that the debt quality of Hawaiian Electric's non-utility subsidiaries "is sufficiently below that of the utility to justify assignment of the single-A-3 rating." The utility's senior secured debt remains rated single-A-1. Hawaiian Electric Industries is the parent of Hawaiian Electric Co., American Savings Bank, Hawaiian Insurance & Guaranty Co., and Hawaiian Tug & Barge Corp. Moody's downgraded its ratings on securities of the utility subsidiary in December. ---

ORION CAPITAL Corp. (New York) -- Standard & Poor's Corp. said it raised its ratings on the company's subordinated debt to double-B-minus from single-B-plus and preferred stock to single-B-plus from single-B. The change affects about $175 million of debt and $88 million of preferred stock, S&P said. Orion Capital is a property and casualty insurance holding company.
