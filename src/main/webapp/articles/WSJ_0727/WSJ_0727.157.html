Business Brief -- Alcan Aluminium Ltd.: Union Approves 3-Year Pact And Ends Strike at Smelter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/27/90

Unionized workers at Alcan Aluminium Ltd.'s smelter in Kitimat, British Columbia, approved a revised three-year contract proposal, ending a strike that began at midnight PDT Monday. The revised accord, which follows a proposal that was rejected Wednesday, calls for annual wage increases of 6%, an immediate signing bonus of 1,750 Canadian dollars (US$1,517) per worker, and inflation-indexed payments in the second and third years of the pact, Alcan's spokesman said.

The Montreal-based aluminum maker's original offer also provided for 6% annual wage increases, though the signing bonus was C$250 less for each worker, and inflation-indexed payments were to occur only in the third year of the pact.

The Kitimat smelter, which produces about 260,000 metric tons of aluminum ingots annually, accounts for about 18% of Alcan's total primary aluminum capacity. It is expected to lose only minimal output as a result of the short strike, Alcan's spokesman said.

Management workers have kept most of the smelter running normally since the strike by 1,600 members of the Canadian Association of Smelter and Allied Workers began.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Alcan closed at $23.75 a share, up 12.5 cents.
