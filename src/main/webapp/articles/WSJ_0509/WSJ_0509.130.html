Business Brief -- Stryker Corp.: Health-Goods Firm Expects 1990 Net of $23.4 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/09/90

Stryker Corp expects net income in 1990 to meet analysts' predictions of $23.4 million, or $1 a share, Chairman and President John W. Brown said. Mr. Brown predicted the company's earnings and sales both will grow by at least 20% this year, consistent with the annual increases posted by the health-products company since it went public in 1979.

In 1989, Kalamazoo, Mich.-based Stryker earned $19.2 million, or 82 cents a share, on sales of $225.9 million. For the first quarter of 1990, it had a net income of $6.3 million, up nearly 25% from the year-earlier period.

Mr. Brown said his company will face a challenge sustaining its 20% growth rate throughout the 1990s. The tremendous increase in U.S. health-care costs will bring a "backlash" if it continues, he said.

Stryker has achieved its growth rate by bringing out a series of well-received products, such as hip implants and surgical tools. Mr. Brown said the company will introduce a new "stream of products" -- mainly variations of existing products -- during the next two to three years.

Stryker doesn't pay a dividend and doesn't intend to start paying one, Mr. Brown said. "Our argument is if you let us reinvest the money, we'll give you a better return," he explained.
