Marketing & Media: Venture to Offer Music Programs For Cable TV

  • Author: Dennis Kneale
  • Date: 05/09/90

NEW YORK -- Two fledgling cable-radio services said they will combine in a joint venture that will offer free music programming to about 10 million cable-TV subscribers by late summer. International Cablecasting Technologies Inc., which now delivers its Tempo Sound music service to fewer than one million homes and had expected to be serving five million households soon, will form the joint venture with a subsidiary of Jones International Ltd., a cable operator.

Jones International's service, Galactic Radio Partners Inc., now delivers radio programming to about 3.5 million households. Combining the two services, which use cable-TV receivers to deliver static-free music by wires plugged into the stereos of cable subscribers, will enable the two partners to reach a "critical mass" needed to attract advertising, said Tom Oliver, president and chief executive officer of International Cablecasting.

"Starting with a base of 10 million is the opening play for a cable network," in television or radio, Mr. Oliver said. The new venture will run about half the load of commercials commonly found on regular over-the-air radio stations, which typically run 10 to 12 minutes of advertising each hour, he said.

The joint service, owned 50-50 by the two partners, will offer nine channels of 24-hour-a-day FM stereo music via cable systems, which will be charged a small fee of three cents a month for each cable household.

While the cable-radio signal will be more clear than regular FM reception, it won't offer the digital-audio quality of a second service which International Cablecasting aims to start next fall. That service, known as CD-30 and commercial-free, will charge cable television subscribers about $8 a month to receive 30 channels of all-digital music, recorded on compact discs and delivered to homes via a special tuner that will be made by Scientific-Atlanta Inc.

International Cablecasting, whose shares are traded on the Vancouver stock exchange, is based in Berverly Hills, Calif. Jones International is based in Englewood, Colo.
