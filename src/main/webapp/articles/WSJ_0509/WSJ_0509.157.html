Major Stake in DHL Unit Is Studied by Three Firms

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/09/90

TOKYO -- Nissho Iwai Corp., a leading Japanese trading firm, is holding talks with Japan Airlines and Lufthansa German Airlines to acquire a joint stake in DHL Worldwide Express, the world's largest express-delivery service. A spokesman for the Japanese carrier confirmed that Japan's largest airline is "studying a proposal to invest in DHL" but refused to elaborate about the negotiations. DHL Corp., based in Brussels, couldn't be reached for comment.

According to Japanese news reports, JAL, Lufthansa and Nissho Iwai would seek to increase any DHL holdings gradually, acquiring a majority of DHL's stock by 1992. Nihon Keizai Shimbun, Japan's leading business daily, reported that the three firms are considering paying $500 million for a stake in DHL. A final agreement on the plan among the three companies is expected later this month.

DHL would give the Japanese air-cargo carrier access to the expanding freight business in Europe, analysts say. The deal would mark Nissho Iwai's entry into the document-delivery business.

Analysts add that the Japanese-German link is an attempt to counter the competitive threat of Federal Express Corp., which is aggressively expanding in Europe and in Asia.
