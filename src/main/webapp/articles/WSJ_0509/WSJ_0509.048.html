International: Continental Reports 17% Rise in Net, Sets New Tire Acquisitions

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 05/09/90

FRANKFURT -- West German tire maker Continental AG reported higher earnings and, in a move that underlines the growing concentration of the world tire industry, announced the acquisition of a Fiat S.p.A. tire subsidiary and a 49% stake in a Swedish tire company. Continental said its earnings rose 17% to 227.8 million marks ($136.6 million) in 1989 from 194.8 million marks a year earlier, while sales rose 6% to 8.38 billion marks from 7.91 billion marks in 1988.

Continental also announced it has reached principle agreement to acquire the industrial rubber activities of Gilardini S.p.A., a subsidiary of Italian auto maker Fiat, for an undisclosed amount.

The Fiat unit, called AGES, makes rubber parts for the automobile industry. The AGES activities will be integrated into ContiTech, Continental's fast-growing industrial-products division, which posted a strong rise in earnings last year.

In the past two years, ContiTech has acquired companies and plants in France, Spain, and Turkey. ContiTech and AGES had been cooperating since 1986 through a licensing agreement.

Continental has also agreed to acquire 49% of Nivis Tyre AB, a wholly owned subsidiary of Kooperativa Foerbundet Industri AB of Sweden. Financial terms weren't disclosed.

Within KFI, Nivis acts as a holding company for several Scandinavian tire activities. The alliance with Nivis will make Continental the leader in the Scandinavian tire markets and will permit the Swedish group to survive the growing competitive pressures in the global tire market, Continental said.

In February, Continental bought National Tyre Service PLC, a British tire retailing company, from Generale des Etablissements Michelin for 400 million marks, following the purchase of a 13.1% stake in Kwik-Fit Holding PLC, another British tire retailer. Last summer, the company took over the tire operations of Marbor S.A. of Portugal, and in 1987, Continental bought U.S. tire maker General Tire & Rubber Co. from GenCorp Inc. for $650 million.
