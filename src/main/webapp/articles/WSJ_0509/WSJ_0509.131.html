Marketing Brief -- New York Times Co.: Launch of Weekly Magazine Is Delayed by Media Firm

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/09/90

New York Times Co. delayed plans to launch a weekly magazine -- working-titled Blocks -- for young New Yorkers. A company spokeswoman said the magazine, envisioned as a supplement to the company's flagship newspaper, the New York Times, "has been put on hold for the foreseeable future due to the business environment in the New York area." That environment has already led to advertising linage slumps at New York area magazines as well as the New York Times.

Described as a life style and feature magazine, Blocks was seen as a way to draw a younger audience and new advertisers to the newspaper. The spokeswoman said the decision to postpone the magazine "was no reflection on the concept of the product."

Industry rumors had circulated that the Times would purchase 7 Days, a weekly magazine for New York that was folded recently by its owner, Leonard Stern. The Times spokeswoman wouldn't comment on the acquisition rumor.
