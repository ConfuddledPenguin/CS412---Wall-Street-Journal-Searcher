Southam Swaps Stake in Firm

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/09/90

VANCOUVER, British Columbia -- Southam Inc. said it swapped its 49% stake in North Shore Free Press Ltd., and unspecified cash, for 63% of Lower Mainland Publishing Ltd., whose Vancouver-area papers had gross revenue last year of about 50 million Canadian dollars (US$43 million). Specific terms weren't disclosed, and officials with Toronto-based Southam, Canada's largest-circulation publisher of daily newspapers, weren't immediately available.

North Shore Free Press published the North Shore News, one of 14 community papers that will now be operated by newly formed Lower Mainland Publishing; Madison Venture Corp. owns the remaining 37% of Lower Mainland Publishing. All but Southam are based in Vancouver.
