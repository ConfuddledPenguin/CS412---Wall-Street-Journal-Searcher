Business Brief -- Gibson Greetings Inc.: Lower Second-Quarter Net Expected; Ames Is Cited

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/09/90

Gibson Greetings Inc. said the recent bankruptcy-law reorganization filing of Ames Department Stores would lower its second-quarter earnings due to an unspecified charge for bad-debt provisions. Cincinnati-based Gibson said its Cleo Inc. greeting-card subsidiary, which shipped merchandise to Ames last fall, was a creditor in the Ames bankruptcy-law proceedings. The amount of the charge has not yet been determined, Gibson said, due to insufficient information concerning the financial status of Ames.

Gibson noted, though, that the additional bad-debt provision is expected to have a relatively minor impact on its full-year results.

In the 1989 second quarter, Gibson had net income of $2.6 million, or 16 cents a share, on revenue of $66.1 million. For all of 1989, earnings were $42.4 million, or $2.68 a share, on revenue of $465.3 million.
