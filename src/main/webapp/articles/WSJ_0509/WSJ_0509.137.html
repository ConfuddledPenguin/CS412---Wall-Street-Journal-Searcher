International Brief -- Deutsche Bank AG: Banking Sector Share of 5% In East Germany Is Goal

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/09/90

Deutsche Bank AG is aiming for market share of 5% or more in East Germany after starting commercial banking operations there July 2, the day monetary union between East and West Germany is set to begin. Hilmar Kopper, chairman of West Germany's largest bank, said he is confident that Deutsche Bank can begin providing a wide range of banking services in about 100 branches in East Germany on that day, using buildings and employees provided by its joint venture with Deutsche Kreditbank AG, the commercial banking arm of the East German central bank.

Deutsche Bank will take over 5,000 to 6,000 employees from Kreditbank and will send 1,000 bankers from West Germany as trainers and supervisors to the East German operations, Mr. Kopper told a group of Frankfurt business journalists.
