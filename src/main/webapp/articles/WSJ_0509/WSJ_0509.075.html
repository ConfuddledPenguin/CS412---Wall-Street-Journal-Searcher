Unicorp American Enters Accord on Sale Of Real-Estate Assets

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/09/90

NEW YORK -- Unicorp American Corp., a unit of Unicorp Canada Corp., said it entered a preliminary agreement with Coscan Development Corp. regarding the sale of Unicorp American's real-estate assets. Under the agreement, certain Unicorp American real-estate assets would be transferred to a partnership of Unicorp American and affiliates of Coscan. Other assets would be financed with participating mortgages. Net proceeds to Unicorp American from the transactions, which are expected to close June 15, would total about $41.6 million and would be used to repay senior secured debt.

Payment of $32.2 million to Unicorp American, including the assumption of $8 million of mortgages, would be made in the form of a secured-loan facility to the partnership, and $17.4 million of participating mortgage would be provided for other real-estate assets. A Coscan affiliate would be the managing partner, while Unicorp American would be a limited partner.

The agreement requires, among other things, consent of Unicorp American's lenders. Unicorp Canada plans to distribute in the third quarter all its common shares of Unicorp American to holders of Unicorp Canada Class A and B common.

Coscan is indirectly controlled by Hees International Bancorp, a merchant bank controlled by Toronto financiers Peter and Edward Bronfman. Unicorp Canada is a Toronto holding company with interest in energy and financial-services concerns. Hees has a 24.6% non-voting equity interest in Unicorp Canada.
