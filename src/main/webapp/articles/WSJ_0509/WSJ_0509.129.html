Business Brief -- General Motors Corp.: Delco Unit to Export Struts To Toyota Plant in Japan

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/09/90

General Motors Corp. said its Delco Products division will export suspension struts from its Kettering, Ohio, factory to a Toyota Motor Corp. car assembly plant in Japan. The agreement, which expands an existing relationship between the two auto makers, comes at a time when Japanese auto makers are under increasing pressure to buy more auto parts from American companies. The value of the contract to GM wasn't disclosed. Shipments are to begin later this year.

Under the agreement, Toyota will buy up to 500,000 strut assemblies a year from Delco Products and install them on subcompact Corolla models built in Japan. The GM division already supplies suspension parts and other components to New United Motor Manufacturing, Inc., the GM-Toyota joint venture car plant in Fremont, Calif., and to Toyota's Georgetown, Ky., and Canadian car assembly plants. Delco Products also exports windshield washer systems to Toyota in Japan.
