Regulators Take Over Mississippi Savings, Charter New Thrift

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/09/90

WASHINGTON -- Federal regulators took over Mississippi Savings Bank, Batesville, Miss., and chartered a federal mutual institution to take its place. The new institution, which has the same name, will assume certain assets and liabilities of the former thrift and will operate under conservatorship under oversight of the Resolution Trust Corp. It remains open for business as usual.

The Office of Thrift Supervision said it took over the thrift, which had assets of $173.4 million, because it was operating in an unsafe and unsound manner and had liquidity problems. The OTS also said the institution wasn't complying with federal capital requirements.
