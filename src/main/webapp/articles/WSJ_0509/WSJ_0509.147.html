Business Brief -- Stuart Hall Co.: Chairman Says Share Net Outpaces Earlier Estimate

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/09/90

Stuart Hall Co. is "running slightly ahead" of an earlier projection of a 52% boost in 1990 earnings per share from 1989, Charles G. Hanson, chairman, said. Mr. Hanson, who also is chief executive officer of the Kansas City, Mo., office and school-products concern, said Stuart Hall's earnings per share are "a couple of cents ahead" of an estimate he made at the company's annual meeting Monday.

At the meeting, he said he expects earnings from continuing operations of 86 cents per share for the fiscal year, which ends in November. Yesterday, in over-the-counter national trading, the company's stock closed at $10.50 a share, up from $8.875 a share on Monday.

He said the company's gross sales for 1990 are forecast at $127.5 million, up 28% from the year earlier's $99 million. The company sells a range of office supply products such as paper and envelopes, as well as school products, to drugstores and supermarkets.
