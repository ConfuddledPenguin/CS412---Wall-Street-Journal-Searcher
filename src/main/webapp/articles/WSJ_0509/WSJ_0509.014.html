Business Brief -- Hewlett-Packard Co.: Entry-Level Minicomputer Added to Unix-Based Line

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/09/90

Hewlett-Packard Co., Palo Alto, Calif., expanded its HP 9000 family of Unix-based minicomputers with the introduction of a new entry-level machine, the Model 822S. The new computer will be priced at $27,900 and is scheduled to become available in June.

The Model 822S, like others in the 9000 family, uses reduced instruction set computing, or RISC, technology, and is designed to serve the client-server and laboratory environment.

A server is a computer within a network that offers services to other so-called clients, including personal computers and workstations, to free them to perform other tasks.

The introduction of the new model, which brings the 9000 family to 13 minicomputers, is designed to offer "minicomputer functionality at the price of a multi-user PC," said Bernard Guyidon, general manager of Hewlett-Packard's general systems division.
