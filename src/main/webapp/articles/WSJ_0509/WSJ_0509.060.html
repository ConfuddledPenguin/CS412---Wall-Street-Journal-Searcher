Push Into Europe Planned by Power Corp. of Canada

  • Author: G. Pierre Goad
  • Date: 05/09/90

MONTREAL -- Power Corp. of Canada is poised for a major push into Europe through its new alliance with Belgian financier Albert Frere, according to Power Corp. officials. With slightly more than one billion Canadian dollars (US$859.8 million) in ready cash and the ability to borrow much more, Power Corp.'s investment plans have been a favorite topic of discussion in Canadian business circles for more than a year. While Power Corp. doesn't plan to go on an immediate spending spree, company officials said they are ready to forge ahead in Europe.

In February, Paul Desmarais, chairman and controlling shareholder of Power Corp., and Mr. Frere, chairman of Frere Bourgeois S.A. of Brussels, took control of Pargesa Holding S.A., a Geneva holding company. Pargesa in turn holds big stakes in Groupe Bruxelles Lambert S.A., Belgium's second-largest holding company, and Petrofina S.A., the big Belgian oil company. Mr. Frere was named chairman of Petrofina last week.

Late last month, Pargesa and Cie. Financiere de Paribas, a French banking concern, announced a complex transaction that bolstered Pargesa'a control of Groupe Bruxelles. At the same time, Mr. Frere and Mr. Desmarais announced they control nearly 10% of Paribas.

In their first public comments on Power Corp.'s recent moves in Europe, company officials stressed the importance of the new alliance with Mr. Frere.

Control with Mr. Frere "gives us now a vehicle in Europe" for Power Corp.'s investments there, said Paul Desmarais Jr., son of Mr. Desmarais and chairman of a Power Corp. unit. Aside from a 50.1% stake in a British investment-management firm, in Europe "everything {Power Corp. does} will be through Pargesa," the younger Mr. Desmarais said.

But he wouldn't signal where Power Corp.'s European investments will flow. He did say Pargesa won't increase three major holdings for the time being.

Because the Frere-Desmarais tandem "for all intents holds control" of Groupe Bruxelles, the partners don't have immediate plans to raise their stake, he said. The same goes for Petrofina, where Pargesa holds about 20% and a Frere family company has 5%.

The younger Mr. Desmarais said the partners don't intend to buy more Paribas shares. "We consider ourselves a friendly and stable" shareholder in Paribas, he said. But he said the Frere-Desmarais team's 9% to 10% stake in Paribas "puts us in a position of pre-eminance" among the bank's shareholders, and Mr. Frere and the elder Mr. Desmarais are being treated accordingly. He didn't elaborate.
