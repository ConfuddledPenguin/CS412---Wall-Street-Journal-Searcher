Ford Motor Predicts Its Share of '90 Market Will Top Level in '89

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/09/90

STERLING HEIGHTS, Mich. -- Ford Motor Co. expects its combined 1990 share of the U.S. car and light-truck market to exceed the 24.5% share it recorded last year. Officials with the No. 2 auto maker are making the prediction even though Ford's sales and car-market share fell through the first four months of this year. The company's January to April sales of 1,147,748 cars and light trucks are running about 6.6% below year-ago sales; its U.S. car market share was 22% at the end of April compared with 23% a year earlier.

Still, Ford Executive Vice President Alexander Trotman said the auto maker's "production schedules are up and our share aspirations are still strong." He said higher production of the recently introduced 1991 Ford Escort and its soon-to-come twin Mercury Tracer will help Ford pick up the pace. Ford is also banking on stronger truck sales because of the new Ford Explorer sport-utility vehicle.

Mr. Trotman made his remarks at a quality-award presentation to the company's Van Dyke components plant in this Detroit suburb. He also announced that Ford plans to convert the plant so it can make a new generation of electronically controlled automatic transmissions for cars and light trucks.

Mr. Trotman declined to specify when the changeover would come, saying the project needed board approval. He said Ford's directors will likely reach a decision on the project by the third quarter of this year. Ford officials said it takes at least three years to complete the changes needed to make transmissions in a plant.
