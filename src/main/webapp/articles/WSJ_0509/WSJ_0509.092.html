Letters to the Editor: . . . But Don't Call Him a Greenmailer

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/09/90

Takao Matsuura and his New York public-relations men did a beautiful job of portraying me as a greenmailer ("Boone-San, Either Put Up or Shut Up," editorial page, April 18): However, in his rather unusual editorial opinion, he forgot to mention one thing -- I've never asked Koito, or any other company, for greenmail, and I've never taken it. You may recall Mr. Matsuura is an employee at Tokyo-based Koito Manufacturing Co., where I am the largest stockholder with 26% ownership. For more than a year now, I've pressed for ownership rights, asking for board representation and access to financial information. I've been shut out at every turn.

I have even attempted to once and for all put an end to Mr. Matsuura's greenmail paranoia. In March I submitted an anti-greenmail resolution to Koito, proposing that the directors adopt it into the corporate charter. Mr. Matsuura never acknowledged my proposal.

We've come a long way with shareholder rights in corporate America. Can you imagine an American CEO telling the largest shareholder to "put up or shut up?" Mr. Matsuura can get away with such a statement because his company is not accountable to the real owners -- the stockholders. Koito is a member of Toyota's "keiretsu", or cartel, so stockholder rights are not taken seriously.

In our battle with Koito and Toyota, we are exposing a rigged Japanese system, and we are revealing how it unfairly competes with corporate America. We are clearly on the right side of the issue. I'll be on my way to Japan again in June for Koito's annual stockholders meeting.

T. Boone Pickens

Boone Co.

Dallas

---

What puzzles me is why Mr. Matsuura is afraid of allowing Mr. Pickens a seat on his board. A "raider" is a shareholder. Yet Mr. Matsuura feels Mr. Pickens will bring harm to the very company he now owns. Why would Mr. Pickens take actions to jeopardize his substantial investment in Mr. Matsuura's company (by Mr. Matsuura's estimate, $840 million)? Besides, what is good for Mr. Pickens is good for the other shareholders as well.

Reflect back on Unocal. Fred Hartley, who was in the same position as Mr. Matsuura, claimed he was looking out for his shareholders' best interest when he launched a stock-buy-back program by borrowing heavily. What he did was turn Unocal's balance sheet upside down and put the Employee Stock Ownership Plan at risk in the process. Why? Because Mr. Pickens became a shareholder.

How did Mr. Hartley's decision enhance shareholder value? Mr. Matsuura is hiding behind the same rhetoric as other CEOs who think their companies are under siege.

If Mr. Matsuura is maximizing the value of Koito why would he be concerned about who owns the stock? But if he is not maximizing the value, I can understand his vigorous campaign to paint such an inaccurate picture of Mr. Pickens as greenmailer and corporate destroyer.

Doug Ramsey

Diamond Bar, Calif.
