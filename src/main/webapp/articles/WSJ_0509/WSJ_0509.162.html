House Passes Bill Directing SEC to Improve Settlements

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/09/90

WASHINGTON -- The House passed a bill directing the Securities and Exchange Commission to improve coordination of the clearance and settlement of stocks and related financial instruments. The measure, approved on a voice vote, is a reaction to the October 1987 stock-market crash. Studies show that trading during the crash strained the back-office payment systems for stocks, options and futures.

The bill doesn't specify how the clearing and settlement functions are to be linked, but directs the SEC to report back to Congress within two years.

The SEC backs the legislation, and a similar provision is included in the Senate's pending market-revision bill.
