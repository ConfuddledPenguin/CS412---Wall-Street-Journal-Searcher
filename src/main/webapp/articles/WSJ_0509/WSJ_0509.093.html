Letters to the Editor: Lack of Education, Poverty Breed Crime

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/09/90

In your April 3 page-one article "Price of Progress" you implied that the problems of Columbus Junction, Iowa, are due to Mexicans, Hispanic-Americans and inner-city blacks from Michigan. You easily could have omitted the reference to national origin and race. The misconception most Americans have is that rising crime rates are inherently related to race -- but only those races of people with darker skin color than white. In fact, the fundamental reason for rising crime is poverty and a lack of education. In Columbus Junction's situation, when you combine this with a growing population, residents resistant to change, differing cultures (moving from one part of the U.S. to another can be a culture shock), the social equation can be horrendous. Unless properly planned for in advance, yes, the end result is crime, high-school dropouts, etc.

The sooner responsible media recognize that we all too often misuse race and national origin, the better off we will be as a nation.

Wendy Foster-Simeon

Woodbridge, Va.
