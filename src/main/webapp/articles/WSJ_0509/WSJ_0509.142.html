Business Brief -- ISS AS: U.S. Unit's Stock Soars 35% On Parent's Takeover Offer

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/09/90

The shares of ISS International Service System Inc. leaped 35% on the news that its Copenhagen-based parent, ISS AS, proposed to acquire the 34% stake that it doesn't own for $10 a share, or $13 million. Both parent and unit provide building-maintenance services.

New York-based ISS International's shares rose past the proposed offer price, indicating that investors expect a higher price for their shares. In American Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, ISS International shares added $2.75 to close at $10.50.

A panel of outside directors at the subsidiary will assess the bid, ISS International said. For advice, the panel has retained the law firm Rogers & Wells and expects to hire an investment banker.

Separately, the subsidiary reported that first-quarter net income rose 6.2% to $830,000, or 12 cents a share, from $781,000, or 11 cents a share, in the year-earlier period. Revenue edged up 2.4% to $122.2 million from $119.4 million.
