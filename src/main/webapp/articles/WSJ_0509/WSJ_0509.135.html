International Brief -- Bayer AG: Pretax Profit Drops 3.1% On Foreign-Exchange Factor

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/09/90

Bayer AG, citing the weakness of the dollar and the yen against the mark, said group pretax profit slipped 3.1% to 930 million marks ($561.6 million) in the first quarter from 960 million marks ($579.7 million) a year earlier. The West German chemicals company, whose first-quarter earnings drop reflects a general trend in chemical industry results, said sales, which were also hurt by foreign-exchange factors, slipped 2.7% to 11.01 billion marks from 11.31 billion a year earlier. It said the strengthening of the mark cut first-quarter sales by 550 million marks. Volume sales rose 3% during the period.

Bayer said the weaker first-quarter results should be seen "against the background of the excellent first quarter of 1989." Chairman Hermann J. Strenger, speaking to reporters at a news briefing, termed the earnings "a good start of the 1990s at a high level."
