Japan's Imported Vehicles Sales

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/09/90

TOKYO -- Sales of imported cars, trucks and buses in Japan totaled 20,142 units in April, up 26% from a year earlier, the Japan Automobile Importers' Association said. The rise reflects continuing strength in consumer demand for luxury goods and the narrowing price gap between domestic and imported vehicles. The upward trend of imported vehicle sales amid Japan's continuing economic expansion was further boosted in April, when a heavy commodity tax on imports was replaced by a lower consumption tax, lowering sticker prices of foreign cars.

Sales of passenger cars rose 26% to 19,894 units. Among these, medium-size cars totaled 10,074, up 20%, while small-size cars totaled 9,820, up 32%. A total of 242 imported trucks, most of them U.S.-made, were sold, up 105%.

Volkswagen-Audi Group had the largest market share among all imported vehicles, selling 4,574 units, up 22%. The second-best seller was Mercedes-Benz, up 9.3% to 3,501.

Sales of U.S.-made vehicles grew 59% to 3,188 units. The strong overall increase was supported by that of small-size cars made by Honda USA, the Japanese maker's U.S. subsidiary. It sold 1,057 units, up 128%.
