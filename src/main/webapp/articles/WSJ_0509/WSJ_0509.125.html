Hitachi Says Judge In Texas Dismissed Claim by Motorola

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/09/90

BRISBANE, Calif. -- Hitachi Ltd. said a federal judge in Texas has dismissed one of a spate of patent-infringement lawsuits pitting Motorola Inc. against the Japanese electronics company. Hitachi said that on April 26, the U.S. District Court for the Western District of Texas granted Hitachi's motion for dismissal of a case claiming that Hitachi's H16 microcontroller infringed on a Motorola patent. At the time, the court also ruled that the Motorola claim was barred by a March decision declaring the Motorola patent in question to be invalid.

The case is one of several patent disputes involving Motorola and Hitachi. Another case, involving Hitachi's patent-infringement claim against Motorola's 88000 RISC microprocessor, is scheduled for trial in September, Hitachi said.

In Schaumburg, Ill., a Motorola spokesman had no specific comment on the court decision, but noted that litigation surrounding the Hitachi H16 continues in challenges to other patents.

A Hitachi spokesman said in a news release that the two companies are engaged in negotiations to try to settle their outstanding disputes.
