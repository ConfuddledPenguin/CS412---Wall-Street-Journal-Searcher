Tuesday's Markets: Stock Prices Rise On Successful Sale Of 3-Year Notes

  • Author: Douglas R. Sease
  • Date: 05/09/90

The successful sale of $10.5 billion in three-year Treasury notes touched off a last-minute rally that helped stock prices rack up a seventh consecutive gain. The Dow Jones Industrial Average rose 11.94 to 2733.56 in light trading. The gains came in a last-minute flurry of activity after news that the Treasury auction went better than some analysts had expected. The dollar was mixed.

Nervousness about the auction kept stock prices confined to a narrow range in sluggish trading through much of the day. But investors had been encouraged by one sign that interest rates may have peaked: steadily declining estimates over the past week of the yield that would attract investors to the Treasury's $30.5 billion of notes and bonds during the three-day auction. The average yield on the $10.5 billion of three-year notes sold yesterday was 8.74%, at the low end of most estimates.

Despite the relative success of yesterday's auction, stock investors remain wary about the outcome of the remaining sales. The Treasury will auction $10 billion in 10-year notes today and $10 billion of 30-year bonds tomorrow. The bond sale has the most potential to disrupt financial markets as it is regarded as the best barometer of inflation expectations.
