Business Brief -- Atlantic Richfield Co.: Britain Clears Firm's Plans To Develop North Sea Field

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/09/90

Atlantic Richfield Co. said the British government approved the Los Angeles-based company's plans to develop the Pickerill Field in the U.K. North Sea, clearing the way for the sale of the natural-gas field's entire output to PowerGen. PowerGen is one of two generating companies formed by Britain to succeed the state-owned Central Electricity Generating Board, which Britain is taking public. Arco declined to disclose the cost of the project, but the British government said it is about #200 million ($334 million).

Pickerill Field, located about 40 miles off the Lincolnshire coast in the North Sea's southern basin, has estimated gas reserves of 750 billion cubic feet. Production is set to begin in 1992, at a maximum rate of 210 million cubic feet a day.
