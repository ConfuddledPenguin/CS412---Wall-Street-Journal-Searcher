Business Brief -- Southwest Gas Corp.: Net in Quarter Declined 5% As Gas, Thrift Profits Fell

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/09/90

Southwest Gas Corp.'s first-quarter net income fell 5% to $21.3 million, or $1.08 a share, due to lower earnings from gas operations and from the company's thrift unit. In the year-ago quarter, Las Vegas, Nev.-based Southwest Gas had net income of $22.5 million, or $1.17 a share. The company had about 19.4 million average shares outstanding in the latest quarter, compared with about 18.8 million a year ago.

Revenue rose 6% to $273.6 million from $258.3 million.

Southwest said its gas operations contributed $20 million to net income in the latest quarter, compared with $20.7 million a year earlier. The company blamed the dip on costs related to pipeline replacement programs and facilities expansion to meet increased natural gas demand.

The thrift unit, PriMerit Bank, contributed $1.3 million to consolidated net income, compared with $1.8 million a year ago. The 36% decline was mainly due to lower real-estate development income and increased expenses related to expansion of the thrift's branch networks in Nevada and Arizona, the company said.
