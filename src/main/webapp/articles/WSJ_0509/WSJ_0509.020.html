Japanese Plant Investment

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/09/90

TOKYO -- Investment in plant and equipment by Japanese corporations will increase 6.7% in fiscal 1990, which began in April, from the previous fiscal year because of healthy economic growth led by domestic demand, according to a survey by the Economic Planning Agency. The survey of 4,207 companies conducted March 1 also showed that Japan's corporate executives are becoming increasingly cautious about the future of their business.

On equipment investment, the survey said manufacturers plan to spend 10.7% more than they did in the previous fiscal year, while non-manufacturers show 4.1% growth.
