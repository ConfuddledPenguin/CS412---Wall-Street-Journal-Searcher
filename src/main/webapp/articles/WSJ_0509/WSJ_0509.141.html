Business Brief -- Fibreboard Corp.: Holders Narrowly Approve Doubling Shares in ESOP

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/09/90

Shareholders of Fibreboard Corp. narrowly passed a measure doubling the shares authorized for the company's employee stock option plan. After what was decribed as acrimonious debate at the company's annual meeting in Roxboro, N.C., shareholders voted 1,080,791 to 1,035,540 to approve a plan doubling the number of shares in the option plan to 700,000 from 350,000. Fibreboard is a Concord, Calif., lumber and construction products company.

Donald Miller, a shareholder from New York City who holds about 52,000 shares of Fibreboard, said he voted against the plan because it was proposed by the board "without the benefit of an independent study of the plan's dilutive effect."

In a related measure, shareholders voted to make options available to non-executive managers of the company at 85% of fair market value. Options for directors and executive managers are granted only at fair market value, the company said.
