`The Importance of Cultural Pluralism'

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/09/90

Following is a press release from the University of Northern Colorado, announcing it was withdrawing its invitation to Linda Chavez to speak at commencement. A related editorial appears today {see: "REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial): The Ivory Censor" -- WSJ May 9, 1990}.

After a day of discussion with students, faculty and staff, University of Northern Colorado President Robert C. Dickeson announced Thursday afternoon that the institution will rescind its invitation to Linda Chavez to speak at its undergraduate commencement ceremonies May 12.

The selection of Chavez, a former Reagan administration official viewed by many as an official English advocate, has drawn criticism from Hispanic groups, UNC's Faculty Senate, and the UNC student government. All three groups have asked Dickeson to rescind the invitation.

Dickeson spent more than seven hours at UNC's University Center Thursday to discuss the matter with members of the university community. In all, 95 people shared opinions with Dickeson.

In issuing the decision to rescind the invitation, Dickeson made five statements:

1) The intent of the University in inviting Linda Chavez to be the commencement speaker was to be sensitive to cultural diversity and the committee making the decision intended to communicate the importance of cultural pluralism. It is clear that the decision was both uninformed and gave the appearance of being grossly insensitive.

2) The decision to invite Linda Chavez to be the commencement speaker is rescinded.

3) Because the university strongly believes in free exchange of ideas, Linda Chavez is invited to an open forum on the UNC campus for the same fee, which can be scheduled during the 1990-91 academic year. Such a forum will allow for an interactive discussion of issues rather than a unilateral presentation which a commencement speech implies.

4) In order for the University to make an even stronger statement of its commitment to diversity, the vice chairman of the UNC Board of Trustees, Edmundo Gonzalez of Denver, will deliver a charge to the graduates at the May 12 ceremony in place of a commencement speaker.

5) The university needs to improve its procedures for the selection of commencement speakers. The commencement committee needs to be augmented to assure student and minority representation. The Faculty Senate and the Student Representative Council will be asked to recommend specific ways in which this can be accomplished for the future.
