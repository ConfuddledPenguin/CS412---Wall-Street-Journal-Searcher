Bass Family Gives Yale $20 Million for Studies

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/09/90

NEW HAVEN, Conn. -- Yale University received a $20 million gift from the Bass family of Fort Worth, Texas, to establish an Institute of Biospheric Studies, dedicated to research and teaching on the global environment. The gift, the largest to the school in more than 20 years, was presented by the third Bass brother, Edward P. Bass. Mr. Bass graduated from Yale with a Bachelor of Science degree in 1968 and did graduate work in architecture there from 1968 to 1970. Over the years, the Bass family has given a number of gifts to the school.

Mr. Bass, who is chairman and chief executive officer of the investment firm Fine Line Inc., is involved with a number of environmental organizations and causes. He holds positions as a director of the World Wildlife Fund, a member of the board of managers of the New York Botanical Garden, and a director of the Council of African Wildlife Foundation, among others.

Yale's faculty in biology, geology and geophysics, forestry and environmental studies will provide the institute's core academic resources, the school said.

The gift will allow Yale to renovate academic facilities, endow senior and junior academic positions and provide a $2 million endowment for the directorship of the Institute of Yale's Peabody Museum of Natural History.
