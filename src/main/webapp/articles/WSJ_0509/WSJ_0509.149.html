Marketing & Media: Jury Orders Frito-Lay Inc. To Pay Singer $2.5 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/09/90

LOS ANGELES -- A federal jury ordered Frito-Lay Inc. and ad agency Tracy-Locke Inc. to pay singer Tom Waits nearly $2.5 million for imitating his gravelly voice in a radio ad for Doritos' Salsa Rio tortilla chips. The jury's award includes punitive damages of $2 million, of which $1.5 million are against Tracy-Locke, a unit of Omnicom Group Inc., New York.

The singer contended that Frito-Lay, a PepsiCo Inc. unit, purposely imitated his "deep and growly" voice and "bluesy" style for the 1988 spot. Frito-Lay countered that the ad "was just a singer doing a jingle."

Mr. Waits is one of several celebrities recently to be embroiled in litigation with ad agencies over the misappropriation of their image or voice. Last year, a federal jury in Los Angeles issued a $400,000 judgment against Young & Rubicam for creating an automobile ad using a Bette Midler sound-alike.
