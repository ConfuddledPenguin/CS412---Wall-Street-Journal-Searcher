Marketing & Media: Dallas Morning News Is Cleared by Jury In Competition Suit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/09/90

HOUSTON -- A state court jury in Houston found that the Dallas Morning News wasn't guilty of unfair competition when it lured the rights to 26 Universal Press Syndicate features away from its rival, the Dallas Times Herald. The decision is the latest bit of bad news for the struggling Times Herald, which has been waging a circulation and legal war with the larger Morning News, a unit of A.H. Belo Corp., for years. Recently, the Federal Communications Commission dismissed a related complaint in which the Times Herald asked that Belo's Dallas television license be revoked because of its agreement with Universal Press.

The Times Herald, owned by closely held DTH Media Inc., sued its larger rival last year after Belo and Universal Press, based in Kansas City, Mo., announced a joint venture intended to explore electronic media possibilities for the syndicate's features.

But the agreement also gave Belo exclusive rights to Universal Press features for the Morning News. As a result, Universal notified the Times Herald that it couldn't run 26 features any longer, including popular comic strips such as "Doonesbury" and "The Far Side" and columns such as "Dear Abby" and Erma Bombeck. During the four-week trial, the Times Herald claimed that its circulation declined after it lost the features. The newspaper asked for $33 million in damages.

"Since the beginning of this lengthy legal process, we have said that the Times Herald's claims were totally without merit and were undertaken solely for competitive reasons," Robert W. Decherd, Belo's chairman and chief executive officer, said in a news release. "We are delighted that the jury has heard the facts and vindicated our position."

A spokesperson for DTH Media said the company wouldn't comment on the verdict.
