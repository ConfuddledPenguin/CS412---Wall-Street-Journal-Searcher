Ex-Grumman Aide Enters Guilty Plea In Pentagon Fraud

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/09/90

WASHINGTON -- A former midlevel Grumman Corp. official pleaded guilty to conspiring to defraud the government and to another charge stemming from the nationwide investigation of Pentagon procurement fraud. In federal court in Brooklyn, N.Y., William Capaldo admitted that the scheme involved passing on to the federal government sham bills for $300,000 of packaging materials. The government ultimately covered the amounts in the phony bills, which were submitted to Grumman by a Long Island, N.Y., paper company between 1982 and 1987, according to the government.

Court documents indicate that the Grumman supplier, headed by James Kane, who has since died, paid Mr. Capaldo $75,000 for certifying that the bills were legitimate. Mr. Capaldo also admitted that he failed to report the money in his federal income tax returns. Grumman has said it isn't a target of a continuing grand jury investigation in Brooklyn.

The guilty plea is an offshoot of earlier grand jury investigations of Grumman's business dealings with Mr. Kane and a Long Island-based political action committee he headed.

A 1988 search warrant for another of Mr. Kane's companies indicated that prosecutors were investigating whether he gave stock or other "things of value" to public officials. In addition, prosecutors have delved into Mr. Kane's contacts with former consultants and officials of Unisys Corp. and other defense contractors implicated in the procurement scandal.

Andrew Maloney, the U.S. attorney in Brooklyn, said there would be "some future activity coming out of this office" following yesterday's guilty plea. He declined to comment on whether this activity would involve Grumman employees or the company itself.
