International Brief -- Fiat S.p.A: Profit for 1989 Increased 9.3% on Sales Rise of 17%

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/09/90

Italian car group Fiat S.p.A reported a 9.3% rise in 1989 consolidated profit before minority interests on a 17% sales increase. The concern described the performance as "the best in the group's history." The results were achieved amid strong demand both in the Italian and European car markets last year, which, however, is now showing signs of weakening.

Fiat's consolidated profit before minority interests rose to 3.31 trillion lire ($2.73 billion) in 1989 from 3.03 trillion lire ($2.5 billion) the previous year. Total consolidated net profit climbed 13% to 3.66 trillion lire from 3.24 trillion lire, while consolidated net sales soared to 52.02 trillion lire from 44.31 trillion lire.

The Milan-based company announced a 50 lire increase in the per-share dividend to 370 lire for common and preferred shares and 400 lire for savings shares, a payment analysts described as high.
