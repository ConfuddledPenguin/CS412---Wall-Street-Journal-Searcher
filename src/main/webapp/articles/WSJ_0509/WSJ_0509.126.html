Business Brief -- General Electric Co.: Judge Temporarily Blocks Daimler Jet-Engine Projects

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/09/90

General Electric Co. received an order from a federal judge temporarily blocking a Daimler-Benz AG subsidiary from entering a series of commercial jet-engine programs with United Technologies Corp. Last month, GE filed a lawsuit asking $1.15 billion of damages, charging the West German industrial group with breaking an agreement it has with GE to develop high-thrust jet engines. GE charged that proprietary data that it shares with Daimler will find its way to GE's competitor, United Technologies, which owns the jet-engine maker Pratt & Whitney.

In Manhattan, U.S. District Judge Robert P. Patterson Jr. scheduled a hearing for May 22. GE has requested an order blocking Daimler and its Motoren-und Turbinen-Union unit from entering into agreements with GE competitors on the engines or from disclosing any GE data on the engines until the case can be tried.

GE, Fairfield, Conn., also has interests in financial services and broadcasting. United Technologies, Hartford, has diverse industrial and defense interests.

Lewis A. Kaplan, an attorney in New York for the Daimler subsidiary, said the unit "believes that the GE claim is without merit and expects to prevail."
