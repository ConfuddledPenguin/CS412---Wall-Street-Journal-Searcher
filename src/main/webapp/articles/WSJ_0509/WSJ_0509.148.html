Business Brief -- Graphic Scanning Corp.: Company Pays Off Note Given to Former Chairman

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/09/90

Graphic Scanning Corp. said it paid off an $11 million note related to a settlement of litigation between the Englewood, N.J., company and Barry Yampol, founder and former chairman. In January, Mr. Yampol agreed to accept the 12% promissory note, in addition to a payment of $16 million and a warrant to buy one million Graphic shares, to settle his suits against the company. Mr. Yampol had charged after being ousted in a proxy fight that he wasn't compensated for certain businesses started during his tenure.

Separately, the telecommunications-services concern said stockholders elected a board that included the addition of James Greenwald and Robert Price, bringing the total number of members to 10.

In March, Gabelli Group Inc., a New York investment firm that controls the equivalent of 38.8% of Graphic shares, said it couldn't support an earlier group of directors proposed by the company and suggested that Graphic nominate "knowledgeable industry professionals." In response, the company added Mr. Greenwald, president and chief executive officer of Katz Communications Inc., and Mr. Price, president of Price Communications Corp. Katz and Price are both New York-based broadcasting concerns.
