U.S. Plans to Give Bolivia $60 Million in Food Aid

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/09/90

WASHINGTON -- The U.S. plans to sign an agreement providing about $60 million in food aid to Bolivia over the next three years, the White House said. The food measure, to be signed today, was one of several trade and economic-assistance measures scheduled for adoption in the course of Bolivian President Jaime Paz Zamora's official visit to Washington this week. President Bush, who met with President Paz Zamora in February as part of the Latin American summit against illegal drugs, praised the Bolivian leader after a White House meeting yesterday. President Bush cited Mr. Paz Zamora's efforts to combat illegal drugs and restructure Bolivia's economy.

Mr. Bush particularly noted the Bolivian leader's strides since the drug summit in curbing Bolivian production of coca leaves used to make cocaine.

The Bush administration has already signed an agreement with Bolivia to set up a joint Commission on Trade and Investment to explore ways of increasing legitimate commerce between the two countries. In addition to the food aid, the U.S. and Bolivia are slated to sign accords for drug-awareness programs and to aid Bolivia in combating a prevalent, frequently fatal affliction called "Chagas disease."

The White House said the administration is also preparing agreements governing extradition, an umbrella agreement on narcotics, and a supplemental package of military aid to combat narcotics traffickers.
