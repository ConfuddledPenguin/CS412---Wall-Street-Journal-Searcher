SEC Files Charges Against B.F. Saul III, Former Roomate

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/09/90

WASHINGTON -- The Securities and Exchange Commission filed insider-trading charges against B. Francis Saul III, the son of the chairman of B.F. Saul Real Estate Investment Trust, in connection with the trust's 1988 acquisition by Westminster Investing Corp. In a complaint filed in federal court in Chicago, the SEC also named Peter D. Garvy, a former roommate of Mr. Saul's, as a defendant. Mr. Garvy was accused of trading on inside information concerning Westminster's plans to make a tender offer for B.F. Saul common stock.

The 28-year-old Mr. Saul of Chicago was charged with obtaining the confidential information from his father, B. Francis Saul II, while the two companies were in negotiations, and then passing it onto Mr. Garvy, a 28-year-old Chicagoan.

Between June 3 and June 21, 1988, Mr. Garvy bought 5,500 B.F. Saul common shares for $16.75 to $21.50 a share, the agency said. On June 22, Westminster announced its offer to acquire the B.F. Saul trust at $25 a share. Mr. Garvy later sold the 5,500 shares for a profit of about $38,438, the SEC said.

Mr Saul's father is chairman of Westminster, based in Chevy Chase, Md., as well as B.F. Saul.

The agency asked the court to enjoin the defendants from violating the securities laws. It also sought forfeiture of Mr. Garvy's alleged profits as well as civil penalties.

Mr. Saul, his father and Mr. Garvy couldn't be reached for comment.
