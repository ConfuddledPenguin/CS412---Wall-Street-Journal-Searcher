High Yields Lure Investors To Denver Airport Bonds

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 05/09/90

NEW YORK -- Very steep bond yields brought investors flocking to the first of several bond issues for Denver's disputed new airport. The city and county of Denver sold $700 million of airport revenue bonds yesterday at yields ranging from 7.60% in 1995 to a steep 8.67% in 2023. The long-term yield is close to that on long-term Treasury debt, and is nearly one percentage point higher than yields on similar transportation bond issues in the municipal market. Underwriters led by Goldman, Sachs & Co. increased the size of the deal to $700 million late in the afternoon from $600 million.

The issue has low investment-grade ratings of Baa-1 from Moody's Investors Service Inc. and triple-B from Standard & Poor's Corp. The Moody's rating is conditional on the airport being built and generating revenue to cover debt service.

To build the airport, Denver needs to raise an estimated $2 billion through bond sales and receive some $500 million in federal aid. There isn't any guarantee from Denver on the bonds. Investors can only look to future revenue from the airport, which is to replace Stapleton airport. Opponents say it isn't needed and will be too big and too costly.

In rating the bonds, Moody's and S&P cited a need for an increase in passengers through Denver to meet the city's financial projections.
