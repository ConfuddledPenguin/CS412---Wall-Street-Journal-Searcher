International: Merchandise-Trade Data Showed German Surplus

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 05/09/90

FRANKFURT, West Germany -- Buoyed by record-high exports, the West German merchandise-trade surplus in March surged to 13.40 billion marks ($8.09 billion) from 10.63 billion marks in February, according to the Federal Statistics Office. The March surplus exceeded economists' expectations of 11.5 billion marks for the month and surpassed the 12.8 billion mark surplus recorded by the Statistics Office for March 1989.

West Germany's current-account surplus also widened in March to a preliminary 10.1 billion marks from a revised 8.1 billion marks the previous month, the Statistics Office said. The February total was adjusted from the originally reported 8.3 billion marks.

The current-account balance includes trade in goods, as well as in services, plus certain transfers.

West German merchandise exports totaled 60.95 billion marks in March, up 17% from 52.02 billion marks in February. The March figure surpassed the record of 60.8 billion marks posted for June 1989.

But March imports also rose, gaining 15% to 47.545 billion marks from February's 41.39 billion marks.

Meantime, in the first quarter of 1990, the West German merchandise-trade surplus grew 2.5% to the equivalent of 36.9 billion marks from 36 billion marks in the year-ago quarter, while the current-account surplus shrank 9.1% to 28.5 billion marks from 31.1 billion marks in the first quarter of 1989.
