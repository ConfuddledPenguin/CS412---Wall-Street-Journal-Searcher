Royal Bank of Canada Agrees to Purchase International Trust

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/13/90

MONTREAL -- Royal Bank of Canada said it agreed to acquire International Trust Co., even though Canadian legislation doesn't permit banks to buy trust concerns, which are similar to U.S. thrifts. The purchase price wasn't disclosed. International Trust is wholly owned by closely held International Trustco Inc., based in Toronto. It has about 80 employees, and at the end of 1989 it was administering on behalf of clients assets of about 3 billion Canadian dollars (US$2.56 billion).

Jim MacKay, a Royal Bank vice president, said the purchase would be completed as soon as proposed changes to Canada's Bank Act permit such a transaction. But the new rules probably won't be introduced in the federal legislature until this fall, he said. In the meantime, Royal Bank will be able to refer its customers seeking trust services to International Trust. Trust services include custodial fees for pension funds, trustee, administration and record-keeping services for group savings plans, securities lending and pooled investment funds.

Canada's banks have been awaiting further deregulation of the financial-services sector since they were permitted in 1987 to own securities dealers. One of their goals is to expand into businesses that generate fee income, such as the trust business and insurance. Although current regulations prohibit banks from owning insurance companies, several of them have established links with insurance companies by referring their bank clients to them.

Mr. MacKay said that by agreeing to buy International Trust as soon as permitted by law, rather than building the business from the ground up, Royal Bank will save three years in the development of its trust operations.
