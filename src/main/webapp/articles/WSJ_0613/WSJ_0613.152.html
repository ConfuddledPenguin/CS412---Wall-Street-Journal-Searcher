NRC Proposes Fine Of $160,000 for 3M For Safety Violations

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/13/90

WASHINGTON -- The Nuclear Regulatory Commission proposed fining Minnesota Mining & Manufacturing Co. $160,000 for the company's alleged "willful violations" of NRC safety and reporting requirements. The company has 30 days to respond to the proposed penalty.

The regulatory agency's findings relate to an air-ionizer 3M manufactured in the mid-1980s. By early 1988, the NRC ordered a recall of some models of the ionizers, which contained microscopic metal spheres that held particles of a radioactive source, polonium 210.

The recall was ordered after agency investigators found that the ionizers, or static-removing devices, leaked radioactivity at some customers' industrial plants. The leakage was at levels so low that it didn't represent a significant health hazard, and consumer products made at the affected plants showed no contamination.

The company later expanded the recall to include all ionizer models containing the radioactive material, and halted those models' production.

The company said it is "surprised by the size of the proposed penalty," given the company's cooperation and compliance with NRC's requests. It is "studying the specifics of the allegations and will respond."

The NRC said it found seven violations. Among them, it said 3M failed to make sure customers would use the devices in "acceptable environments." It also said the company didn't check returned ionizers for surface contamination, a process that would identify any leaking devices that should be classified unacceptable.

The agency said 3M also failed to tell customers how the leaking ionizers should be handled, and to provide the NRC with complete reports.
