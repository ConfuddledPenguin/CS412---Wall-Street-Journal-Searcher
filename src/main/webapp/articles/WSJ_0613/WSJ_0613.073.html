CD Yields at Banks, Brokerage Firms End Week Little Changed

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/13/90

NEW YORK -- Yields on certificates of deposit at major banks and brokerage firms were little changed in the week ended yesterday. Average yields on small-denomination CDs sold directly by banks were down slightly. The average yield on a one-year consumer CD went to 7.87% from 7.93%, while five-year CDs fell to 8.11% from 8.15%, according to an 18-bank survey by Banxquote Money Markets, an information service.

"There was no significant move in interest rates, so rates remained in a holding pattern," said Norberto Mehl, Banxquote chairman.

While none of the banks in the survey offered a yield as high as 9%, investors can still find consumer CDs at some banks yielding that much or more.

Average yields on most small-denomination CDs sold by leading broker-dealers were up slightly. Two-year maturities were the exception, with the average yield down to 8.30% from 8.35%. Average yields on six-month CDs rose to 8.27% from 8.22%, while average yields on five-year CDs went to 8.51% from 8.43%, according to Banxquote.

Meanwhile, average yields for large-denomination CDs, which typically require deposits of $90,000 or more, were largely unchanged. Average yields on one-year jumbo CDs edged up to 7.98% from 7.97%, while five-year maturities eased to 8.25% from 8.28%.
