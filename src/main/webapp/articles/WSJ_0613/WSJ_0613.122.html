Professor Named to Probe Case of Revco Drug's LBO

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/13/90

NEW YORK -- U.S. Bankruptcy Court Judge Harold F. White approved the appointment of Barry L. Zaretsky, a Brooklyn Law School professor and counsel to the international law firm of Kelley Drye & Warren, as examiner in the Revco Drugstores bankruptcy case, according to the law firm. Appointment of an examiner in the case was requested by U.S. Trustee Conrad J. Morgenstern.

The law firm said that Prof. Zaretsky, with Kelley Drye as his counsel, will investigate the failed $1.25 billion management-led leveraged buy-out of the 2,000-drugstore chain. It was the first major junk-bond-financed LBO to enter Chapter 11 of the Bankruptcy Code under which a company is given protection from creditors while it tries to work out a plan for paying its debts.

Prof. Zaretsky will seek to determine whether the Revco LBO involved fraud and is to submit a preliminary report July 16.

The law firm said that if Prof. Zaretsky's investigation finds that the LBO was fraudulent and if the courts ultimately uphold such findings, it would further jeopardize junk-bond-financed LBOs as a method of corporate acquisition. The law firm added that if fraud is found in the Revco case, it could retroactively call into question billions of dollars of LBO debt outstanding.
