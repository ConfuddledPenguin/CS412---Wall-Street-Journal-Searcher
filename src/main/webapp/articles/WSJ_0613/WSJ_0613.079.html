Credit Ratings: Moody's Lifts Rating On American Brands

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/13/90

OLD GREENWICH, Conn. -- The rating on about $1.7 billion of American Brands Inc.'s debt was boosted to single-A-3 from Baa-2 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. The rating concern also upgraded a shelf registration of senior debt of the tobacco, consumer products and insurance company to preliminary single-A-3 from preliminary Baa-3. And Moody's confirmed its prime-2 rating on the company's commercial paper.

A "more-focused" American Brands "is generating increasing free cash flow from non-tobacco businesses, while continuing to benefit from {tobacco} volume and price gains in the important United Kingdom and U.S. markets," Moody's said.

Potential impediments to greater cash generation from the company's tobacco and distilled spirits businesses include continued declines in per-capita consumption of cigarettes, Moody's said. A material increase in excise taxes or an adverse tobacco product-liability judgment are major external risk factors, Moody's added.
