Who's News: Fundamentalist Picked By Southern Baptists

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/13/90

NEW ORLEANS -- For the 12th consecutive year, a fundamentalist was elected president of the Southern Baptist Convention. At the denomination's annual meeting, the Rev. Morris Chapman of Wichita Falls, Texas, received 21,471 votes, or 57.7% of the total, to defeat the Rev. Daniel Vestal of Dunwoody, Ga., who received 15,753 votes, or 42.3%. Mr. Vestal was the candidate of the convention's moderate faction, which has accused the fundamentalists of launching a purge of Southern Baptist seminaries and other organizations, targeting persons who don't accept a literal interpretation of the Bible.

A recent Page One story in The Wall Street Journal described how the struggle between the two factions had led to a decline in active membership, seminary enrollment and appointment of missionaries in the denomination, which claims to be the largest Protestant body in the U.S.

A total of 38,241 clergy and lay people registered at the New Orleans convention, making the gathering the third largest in Southern Baptist history, after the 1985 convention in Dallas and the 1986 convention in Atlanta.
