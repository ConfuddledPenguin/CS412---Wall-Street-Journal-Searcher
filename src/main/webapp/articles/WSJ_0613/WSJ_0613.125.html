Magma Power Expects Lower 2nd-Period Net Due to Storm Damage

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/13/90

SAN DIEGO -- Magma Power Co. said it expects second-quarter earnings to be adversely affected by damage to a transmission line during a storm last weekend in California's Imperial Valley. Magma officials declined to estimate the impact on earnings. In last year's second quarter, Magma had net income of $4 million, or 18 cents a share, on revenue of $14.1 million.

In national over-the-counter trading yesterday, Magma shares closed at $30.50, down $2.25.

The storm, the remnant of Hurricane Boris, toppled several towers of a transmission line owned and operated by the Imperial Irrigation District. The 400-megawatt line, completed in 1988, was built with money provided by a consortium of independent power producers, including Magma. It transmits electrical power to SCEcorp's Southern California Edison Co. Mirage Substation in Riverside County. Magma said damage to the line is covered by insurance.

Magma said the damage has caused its four geothermal plants in the Imperial Valley to curtail electrical generation, and that the outage could have an adverse impact on the second-quarter results of the four partnerships that own the affected plants. Magma and Mission Energy Co., a unit of Rosemead, Calif.-based SCEcorp, each own 50% of those partnerships. A temporary bypass of the damaged section and alternate transmission paths are being considered, but a bypass could take two weeks to one month to complete, Magma said.

Magma said the four geothermal plants, operated by its Red Hill Geothermal subsidiary, weren't damaged by the storm.
