Business Brief -- Foxboro Co.: Search for Strategic Partner Produces Takeover Offers

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/13/90

Foxboro Co., Foxboro, Mass., said it received "several" takeover proposals in connection with its previously announced search for a strategic partner to make an equity investment. The maker of industrial-process control systems said that its investment adviser, Salomon Brothers Inc., and two law firms are helping it evaluate the proposals and will assist in negotiations. It added that it hasn't decided whether to sell out.

Foxboro has advanced technology but has reported losses in recent quarters and omitted its dividend earlier this year. For all of 1989, it reported a net loss of $4.6 million, or 37 cents a share, on sales of $597.8 million.

Analysts speculate that descendants of the founding Bristol family, who hold nearly one-third of the stock, may be restive due to the dividend cuts. In addition, in April, the company added to its board two representatives of a stockholder group that had accumulated a 4.8% stake. The group agreed to hold its purchases below 10% this year and said its directors wouldn't comment publicly on Foxboro matters.

Foxboro declined to disclose details about the takeover offers. In New York Stock Exchange trading, its stock closed up $2.375 at $32.375.
