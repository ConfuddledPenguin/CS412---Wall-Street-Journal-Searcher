Kaman Sets Venture With French Company For Defense Products

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/13/90

BLOOMFIELD, Conn. -- Kaman Corp. signed a joint-venture agreement with Societe Nationale des Poudres Explosifs of Paris to develop defense and industrial products using so-called insensitive explosive-materials technology. The venture, called Advanced Energetic Materials Corp. of America, will be based in the U.S. Each company will hold a 50% stake in the endeavor.

Kaman, a defense contractor, said the joint venture intends to develop cost-effective products to improve defense capabilities of armored vehicles against munitions attacks. Kaman said the products will use explosive materials developed by the French company that are highly stable during handling and operating conditions. Societe Nationale is a specialty chemicals and advanced materials concern.

The two companies have worked together on related research since 1986, Kaman said. Developing products based on the research "is the next logical step," said Richard E.W. Smith, vice president of Kaman. Mr. Smith will be president of the joint venture.

Kaman said it is too early to estimate annual revenues from the venture.
