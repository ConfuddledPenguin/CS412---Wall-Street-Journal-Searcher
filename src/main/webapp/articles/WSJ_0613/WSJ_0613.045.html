Who's News: MIT Panel Nominates Vest Of Michigan for Presidency

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/13/90

CAMBRIDGE, Mass. -- The Massachusetts Institute of Technology's executive committee recommended that Charles M. Vest, provost of the University of Michigan, be named president of MIT. If his nomination is approved, Mr. Vest, a 48-year-old specialist in optics and former dean of Michigan's engineering school, would be MIT's 15th president. He would succeed Paul E. Gray, who is scheduled to resign in July to become chairman of MIT's governing board.

Mr. Vest is MIT's second choice for the prestigious post. Earlier this year, the committee nominated Philip A. Sharp, a prominent MIT biologist, to be president, but after a few days, he withdrew his name from consideration, choosing to continue his research and teaching.

The nomination of Mr. Vest is subject to a vote by the 65-member MIT Corp., the school's governing body.
