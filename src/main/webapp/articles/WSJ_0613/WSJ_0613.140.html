Dissident Director, Partner Seek Seats On Goldcorp's Board

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/13/90

TORONTO -- A dissident director of Goldcorp Investments Ltd. and a former director of the company's subsidiaries have launched a proxy battle against management to gain seats on the Goldcorp board. James McCartney, a partner at the Toronto law firm of McCarthy Tetrault, said he and Robert Simon, senior vice president and treasurer of Brascan Ltd., plan to stand for election at the Goldcorp annual meeting, scheduled for June 25. Goldcorp is an investment concern based here.

Mr. McCartney said Mr. Simon currently serves on the Goldcorp board but hasn't been renominated by management. Last April, Mr. McCartney was replaced by management nominees on the boards of Dickenson Gold Mines Ltd. and Wharf Resources Ltd., both Goldcorp subsidiaries.

In a proxy solicitation document, Mr. McCartney and Mr. Simon question, among other things, the fees paid by Goldcorp to CSA Management Ltd., which controls virtually all the votes in Goldcorp through its holding of common shares.

Goldcorp's Class A shareholders, with limited voting powers, are entitled to elect two directors, which are the positions that Mr. McCartney and Mr. Simon are hoping to fill.

CSA Management is 51% controlled by the family of Robert McEwen, who serves as president of Goldcorp, and as chairman and chief executive of Dickenson and Wharf.

Mr. McEwen said allegations made in the proxy solicitation circular are "without merit." He said the Goldcorp board will meet tomorrow to come up with a response.
