European Takeovers More Than Doubled In Quarter, Study Says

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/13/90

NEW YORK -- The number and value of continental European companies targeted for mergers and acquisitions more than doubled in the first quarter from a year ago, a financial data-base company said. IDD Information Services, a unit of United Newspapers PLC of Britain, said the number of continental European companies selected for acquisition in the first quarter rose to 715 from 319 a year ago. The value of the transactions increased to $18.4 billion from $7.3 billion.

The reason for the increase is largely due to continental European companies' shift in focus from the U.S. to their own markets, in preparation for the unification of the European market in 1992, IDD said.

The information service also found that the value of transactions in Britain increased, although the number of transactions decreased. In the 1990 quarter, 513 U.K. concerns were targeted for acquisitions valued at $17.8 billion. That compares with 560 U.K. concerns valued at $11.8 billion.

IDD said the number of targeted companies in Europe and Britain for the first time exceeded the number in the U.S., which was 1,054 companies. The value of the U.S. transactions was $62 billion.

The number of continental European companies targeted by U.S. companies increased to 34, from 26. But their total value declined to $77.5 million from $210.5 million, IDD said.
