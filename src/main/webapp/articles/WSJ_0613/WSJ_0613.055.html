Marketing & Media: Chevron Unveils Gasoline Sensitive to Environment

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/13/90

LOS ANGELES -- Chevron Corp. jumped on the environmental bandwagon, announcing a reformulated blend of unleaded premium gasoline. Chevron's step follows recent product announcements by other oil companies, including Shell Oil Co.'s April introduction of a similar unleaded premium gasoline sold at 2,000 stations in nine cities. The trend started with Atlantic Richfield Co.'s unveiling last August of a cleaner fuel for older cars; that product is sold mostly in the Los Angeles market.

Chevron's Supreme Unleaded is to be offered at 550 stations in Los Angeles and San Diego. Sales began at some stations yesterday, replacing an existing premium brand. The San Francisco-based oil company also plans to offer the new fuel in Houston and Baltimore later this summer. The four cities are among those the Environmental Protection Agency said have the most severe air-quality problems.

Willis J. Price, vice president of Chevron and president of its Chevron USA unit, said the new gasoline will cost about two cents more a gallon to produce. He declined to say if that cost would be passed to consumers.

Chevron officials said the new gasoline could reduce hydrocarbon emissions 9% and carbon monoxide emissions 7% -- compared with gasolines currently on the market.

Chevron also announced a cleaner diesel fuel that will be available at about 100 outlets in Southern California. The new diesel product, which will replace the existing formulation, will cost the company about one cent a gallon more to produce.
