Japan's Machinery Orders Rise

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/13/90

TOKYO -- Japanese machinery makers received orders valued at 1.871 trillion yen ($12.1 million) in April, up 16.2% from a year earlier, supported by strong domestic and overseas demand, the Economic Planning Agency said. On a seasonally adjusted basis, April's machinery orders rose 18% from March to 2.069 trillion yen. An agency official said orders remained in an upward trend during April, and will apparently stay that way for the foreseeable future.

Orders from the domestic private sector in April increased 13.3% to 1.19 trillion yen, compared with a year earlier. With seasonal factors taken into account, orders rose an adjusted 15.6% from the previous month to 1.197 trillion yen.
