Who's News: Kinark Corp. Elects 2 Altair Aides to Board

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/13/90

TULSA, Okla. -- Kinark Corp. said three directors have been elected to its eight-member board, including two candidates who opposed Kinark's nominees. Elected were Garry Brainin, chairman and president of Altair Corp., and George B. Collins, Altair's assistant secretary. But a third Altair candidate was defeated by Kinark nominee Ivan Himmel, chairman of Chicago-based Maritime Partners Limited Partnership. Maritime is Kinark's second-largest stockholder, with 7.8% of the common shares, while Altair is third, with 6.7%.

Paul Chastain, Kinark's deputy chief executive officer, said the Altair slate was pressing for more aggressive growth by the company, which owns galvanizing plants and chemical-storage facilities. The previous board rejected last month an unsolicited $24.8 million offer from Steel Partners Limited Partnership, but said it would consider additional proposals. Mr. Chastain wouldn't comment on how the new board would respond to such offers.

Messrs. Brainin and Collins couldn't be reached for comment.
