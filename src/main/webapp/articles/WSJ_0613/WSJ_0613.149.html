International Brief -- Skandinaviska Enskilda Banken: Operating Profit Increased 10% in First Four Months

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/13/90

Skandinaviska Enskilda Banken of Sweden said group operating profit for the first four months of this year jumped 10% to 1.64 billion kronor ($267.3 million) from 1.49 billion kronor a year earlier, and predicted a 10% increase for the full year. Sweden's biggest bank, known as SE Banken, cited a 40% surge in foreign currency-denominated lending. But as foreign currency credits have lower profit margins than lending in kronor, SE Banken's net interest margin fell to 2.23% from 2.41%.

SE Banken also outlined plans for a 600 million-kronor offering of new shares to its three allies in Scandinavian Banking Partners. The offering, representing about 3% of SE Banken's share capital outstanding, will be subscribed by Union Bank of Finland, Den norske Bank of Norway and Unibank Denmark. The four banks formed the group five years ago to circumvent regulations that then prevented cross-border banking in Scandinavia. They agreed to swap stakes, but SE Banken was barred from this by laws banning foreign ownership in Swedish banks, a curb Sweden's parliament is expected to scrap soon.
