Claims of Allegheny International Bought By Japonica Partners

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/13/90

PITTSBURGH -- Japonica Partners said it acquired $25.1 million of Allegheny International Inc. senior unsecured claims from a group of insurance company holders. Japonica said that with the latest purchase its debt holdings would translate into more than 50% ownership under Allegheny's proposed plan of reorganization, and that the New York investor group has begun negotiating a "change of control" agreement with management.

But an Allegheny spokesman asserted that "there are no negotiations regarding a change of control." Moreover, he added, "Allegheny questions Japonica's calculations" about how much stock Japonica's debt holdings are actually worth. Allegheny is operating under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code.

Japonica and Allegheny have proposed rival reorganization plans for the company, whose major businesses are Sunbeam and Oster small appliances. The Allegheny management plan to exchange stock for creditor claims was voted down by creditors. However, the company filed suit claiming that Japonica acted in bad faith in buying up creditor claims with the intent to vote them against management's reorganization plan. Japonica countered with a lawsuit of its own. Those lawsuits are still pending.

Meanwhile, creditors voted late last week on a plan from Japonica to pay creditors about $655 million in cash, much of it borrowed from banks. Results of that vote will be reported to the bankruptcy court later this month, but several people involved in the dispute said the Japonica plan was rejected.

Wilbur Ross, a senior manager for Rothschild Inc., New York, who represents Allegheny's unsecured creditors, said that "if our math is right, Japonica would have an absolute majority of the stock {under Allegheny's reorganization plan}. Ultimately, control follows ownership." He added, however, that the exact time and form of the transfer of control are difficult to predict because the bankruptcy proceedings have been complicated.
