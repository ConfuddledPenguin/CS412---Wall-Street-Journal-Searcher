International Brief -- National Westminster Bank PLC: Third World Debt Staff Set To Be Trimmed in July

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/13/90

National Westminster Bank PLC, London, is cutting its Third World debt-management staff sharply next month, a move signaling that Britain's second-largest commercial bank has substantially trimmed its exposure to troubled sovereign loans. NatWest's staff of 36 professionals within its Sovereign Risk Unit will be cut in early July to a custodial operation of fewer than 10 employees, possibly with just two professional staffers assigned to oversee day-to-day management of the bank's debt positions.

A NatWest spokesman declined to confirm the figures, although he said, "The staffing levels would move in line with our aggressive asset-management policy."

NatWest, which had #1.8 billion ($3.04 billion) of exposure to 24 lesser developed countries at the end of 1989, trimmed #723 million from its portfolio last year through sales into the secondary-debt market. It also exited from exposure to five countries outright as a result of those sales.
