What's News -- World-Wide

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/13/90

GORBACHEV PROPOSED a looser federation of sovereign Soviet states. In an attempt to hold together a splintering Soviet Union, the Kremlin leader told the presidents of all 15 republics to nominate representatives and prepare drafts for a union "that would have elements of a federation, a confederation or some such pluralistic relationship." Gorbachev also met separately with the leaders of Lithuania, Estonia and Latvia, the first such talks since he termed their independence bids illegal, and progress was reported toward ending the impasse. Meanwhile, Russia approved a resolution proclaiming its sovereignty. Gorbachev also indicated flexibility on a united Germany's membership in the Warsaw Pact and NATO.

The Soviet parliament passed a bill to guarantee press freedom and eliminate censorship, with officials facing charges if they hamper journalists.

---

Firefighters conquered a blaze aboard the Mega Borg oil tanker in the Gulf of Mexico. The Coast Guard said it was encouraged by the progress and that chances are now slim that the disabled ship will sink. However, crude oil continues to pour out of the vessel and authorities said a major spill is still a possibility.

---

The Senate Ethics Committee opened hearings into the financial dealings of Sen. Dave Durenberger. A special counsel to the committee recommended that the Minnesota Republican be denounced by the Senate for "knowingly engaging in unethical conduct which has brought dishonor and disrepute to this institution."

---

Bush said he hadn't decided whether to break off talks with the Palestine Liberation Organization, but he demanded that the Arab group condemn a May 30 terrorist raid on Israel by one of the PLO's factions. Secretary of State Baker earlier stated that the Arab organization's statements so far "have fallen short of the mark."

---

The Senate failed by a margin of two votes to override Bush's veto of an Amtrak-authorization bill in a hard-fought battle over a regulatory provision that provoked lobbying from unions and the railroad industry. The vote followed an intense administration campaign headed by Transportation Secretary Skinner.

---

Medical researchers reported that a comprehensive education program for premature, underweight babies that begins just after birth can significantly improve the infants' chances for productive lives. More than 250,000 babies are born prematurely each year in the U.S.

---

Algerians cast ballots in the country's first contested elections since French rule ended in 1962. The interior minister said Moslem fundamentalists were set to score an upset victory against the ruling National Liberation Front, based on initial results. Eleven parties vied in municipal and provincial races in the North African nation.

---

The House defied a White House veto threat, sending Bush a bill that would free federal workers for considerably more off-the-job political activity. The House voted to adopt a version of the legislation the Senate approved last month for liberalizing restrictions set by the 51-year-old Hatch Act.

---

Liberian rebels began talks in Sierra Leone with officials of the Monrovia government. Members of the Liberian Council of Churches are mediating the negotiations to end the West African nation's tribal war.

---

The World Health Organization said the rapid spread of the AIDS virus in developing nations means the disease will be more widespread in the next century than previously thought. WHO said its current projection of up to 20 million people infected with AIDS by the year 2000 is a minimum that the agency expects to revise upward.

---

Soviet and U.S. negotiators resumed formal talks in Geneva on proposed deep reductions in long-range nuclear weapons. The chief Soviet negotiator said he expected to reach an agreement this year on a Strategic Arms Reduction Talks pact.

---

A cease-fire accord was reached in Sri Lanka between the government and Tamil rebels. Declaration of the truce, to take effect today, followed two days of renewed fighting in the northeastern part of the Indian Ocean island. The minority Tamils are seeking political autonomy, claiming discrimination by the majority Sinhalese.

---

Mozambique delegations arrived in neighboring Malawi for peace talks. Representatives of Mozambique's left-wing government and rebel leaders were to begin their first direct discussions aimed at ending a civil war that has claimed hundreds of thousands of lives during the past 13 years in the southeast African nation.
