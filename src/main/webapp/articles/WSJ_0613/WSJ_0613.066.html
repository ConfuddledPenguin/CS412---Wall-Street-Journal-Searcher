Budget Summit May Face Acceleration Next Week

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/13/90

WASHINGTON -- Key players in the budget summit agreed to try to jumpstart the stalled talks next week in the hopes of making significant headway before the end of the month. House Majority Leader Richard Gephardt (D., Mo.) told White House Budget Director Richard Darman and Treasury Secretary Nicholas Brady that he will convene negotiators "night and day or weekends" beginning next Wednesday, according to Senate Budget Chairman James Sasser (D., Tenn.), who was present when they met. The administration officials and other important congressional negotiators concurred.

Sen. Sasser and House Budget Chairman Leon Panetta (D., Calif.), said they expect the negotiators finally will begin discussing deficit-reduction proposals next week. But aides to Republican negotiators were far less sanguine, saying there was little new reason to be optimistic about the talks.

Administration and congressional negotiators have met twice a week for a month, but so far they have accomplished little besides studying the federal budget and various economic scenarios. "We're delighted that there's a mutual interest in speeding up the process," an administration official said yesterday.

Mr. Gephardt told Mr. Darman and Mr. Brady over lunch at the Capitol that he still is aiming for some sort of deficit-reduction accord before July 4. Administration officials are skeptical that can happen. But Sen. Sasser, who has been more pessimistic than his colleagues about the budget summit, said yesterday that the odds of an agreement by that date are "better than 50-50."
