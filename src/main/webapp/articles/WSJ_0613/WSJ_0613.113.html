International Brief -- Hoechst AG: German Chemical Firm Says Profit May Fall 10% for '90

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/13/90

Hoechst AG said it may record a 10% decline in profit for 1990, compared with last year's moderate profit growth. Hoechst Chairman Wolfgang Hilger said at the annual meeting that the strength of the mark against key foreign currencies as well as downward pressure on product prices make it unlikely the company will be able to recover from its 8.5% drop in pretax profit in the first quarter.

In the first quarter, the West German chemical group's pretax profit fell to 920 million marks ($543.5 million) from 1.01 billion marks a year ago. Sales were unchanged at 11 billion marks.

Despite the expectations of softer earnings, Mr. Hilger said that Hoechst believes "a satisfactory dividend proposal {for 1990} is possible." He didn't elaborate. The company earlier this year boosted its annual cash dividend for 1989 to 13 marks a share from 12 marks.

For the first five months of 1990, Hoechst reported world-wide group sales of 18.5 billion marks, down 2%.

For 1989, Hoechst posted pretax profit of 4.15 billion marks, on sales of 45.9 billion marks.
