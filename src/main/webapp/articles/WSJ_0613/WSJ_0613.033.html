New Securities Issues

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/13/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATE

General Electric Capital Corp. -- $250 million of 18-year 8 5/8% notes via Kidder, Peabody & Co. Investors have the option of redeeming the notes at par in six years. The maturity date is June 15, 2008, and the put date is June 15, 1996. The price paid by investors will be adjusted to result in a yield 20 basis points above the average yield on the Treasury's five-year and seven-year notes. The issue is rated triple-A by both Moody's Investors Service Inc. and Standard & Poor's Corp.

Colorado Interstate Gas Co. -- $180 million of 10% senior debentures due June 15, 2005, priced at 99.385 to yield 10.08%. The non-callable debentures were priced at a spread of 160 basis points above the Treasury's 10-year note. Rated Baa-2 by Moody's and triple-B-minus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Shearson Lehman Hutton Inc.

Westvaco Corp. -- $100 million of 9 3/4% debentures due June 15, 2020, priced at par. The non-callable debentures were priced at 130 basis points above the Treasury's 30-year bond. Rated single-A-1 by Moody's and single-A by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Goldman Sachs & Co.

MUNICIPAL

Los Angeles -- $190 million of wastewater system revenue bonds due June 1, 1991-2007, 2010 and 2020, apparently won by a Prudential-Bache Capital Funding group. The bonds, rated single-A-1/single-A, were priced for reoffering to yield from 5.90% in 1991 to 7.25% in 2020. Current interest serial bonds are priced to yield from 5.90% in 1991 to 7.10% in 2007. There are $20.3 million of 7% bonds priced at 99 1/4 to yield 7.07% in 2010 and $108.8 million of 7.15% term bonds priced at 98.78 to yield 7.25% in 2020.

Kentucky Housing Corp. -- $70.4 million of housing bonds, Series A, due Jan. 1, 2010 and 2016, and Series B, due Jan. 1, 1992-2001, 2005, 2007 and 2021, tentatively priced by a Bear Stearns & Co. group to yield from 6.20% in 1992 to 7.80% in 2021. Series A consists of $9.5 million of 7.40% term bonds priced at par in 2010. There also is $13.7 million of 7.55% term bonds priced at par due in 2016. Series B current interest serial bonds are priced at par to yield from 6.20% in 1992 to 7.10% in 2001. There are $6.1 million of 7.30% term bonds priced at par due in 2005, $4.1 million of 7.65% term bonds priced at par due in 2007 and $27.4 million of 7.80% term bonds priced at par due in 2021. Interest on the Series B bonds will be treated as a preference item in calculating the federal alternative minimum tax that may be imposed on certain investors. Series A bonds are not subject to the alternative minimum tax. The bonds are rated double-A.

Reedy Creek Improvement District, Fla. -- $70.4 million of utilities revenue bonds, Series 1990-1, due Oct. 1, 1993-2003, 2010 and 2015, tentatively priced by a Merrill Lynch Capital Markets group to yield from 6.30% in 1993 to 7.38% in 2015. Current interest serial bonds are priced to yield from 6.30% in 1993 to 7.15% in 2003. There are $23.9 million of 7 1/4% term bonds priced at 99 1/4 to yield 7.32% in 2010 and $25.9 million of 7.30% term bonds priced at 99 1/8 to yield 7.38 in 2015. The bonds are insured and triple-A-rated.

San Diego Area Local Governments -- $69.6 million of 12-month pooled tax and revenue anticipation notes due June 28, 1991, apparently won by a Shearson Lehman Hutton Inc. group. The 6.14% notes, backed by a letter of credit from Fuji Bank Ltd. and rated MIG-1 by Moody's, were priced to yield 5.90%.

Indiana Transportation Finance Authority -- $60.7 million of highway revenue bonds, Series 1990A, due June 1, 1992-2000, and 2015, priced by a Morgan Stanley & Co. group to yield from 6.20% in 1992 to 7.25% in 2015. Current interest serial bonds are priced at par to yield from 6.20% in 1992 to 6.90% in 2000. There is $36 million of 7.25% term bonds priced at par and due in 2015. The bonds are rated conditional single-A-1 by Moody's and single-A-plus by S&P.

West Virginia Housing Development Fund -- $50 million of housing finance bonds, 1990 Series A, due May 1 and Nov. 1, 1992-2002, 2005 and 2017, priced by a Goldman Sachs & Co. group at par to yield from 6.40% in 1992 to 7.95% in 2017. Current interest serial bonds are priced to yield from 6.40% in 1992 to 7.50% in 2002. There are $4.5 million of 7.88% term bonds priced at par due in 2005 and $28.9 million of 7.95% term bonds priced at par due in 2017. The issue is rated single-A-1/single-A-plus. Interest on the bonds will be treated as a preference item in calculating the federal alternative minimum tax.

EUROBONDS

Eurofima (agency) -- 10 billion Spanish pesetas of 13 3/4% Eurobonds due June 28, 1995, priced at 100 7/8 to yield 13.96% less fees via Banco Espanol de Credito (Banesto). Fees 1 5/8.

Du Pont Co. (U.S.) -- 125 billion Italian lire of 12 3/8% Eurobonds due July 12, 1994, priced at 101.60 to yield 12.38% less fees via Credito Italiano. Fees 1 5/8.

Metropolitan Estate Property International N.V. (Japanese parent) -- 10 billion yen of floating-rate notes due July 5, 1995, priced at 100.10. Coupon 1/4 point above London interbank offered rate. Callable after third year. Guarantee by Metropolitan Estate Property Co. Fees 0.20.

Skandinaviska Enskilda Banken (Sweden) -- 100 million Swiss francs of 7 3/8% public bonds due June 29, 1997, priced at 101 3/4, via Credit Suisse. Fees 2.63.

American Health Properties Inc. (U.S.) -- 50 million Swiss francs of 8 1/2% public bonds due July 19, 2000. Each bond priced at 5,000 francs via S.G. Warburg Soditic. Bonds convertible from Oct. 19 until July 9, 2000, into fixed number of American Health Property shares determined by dividing bond issue price by a fixed Swiss franc-U.S. dollar rate to be set June 25. Conversion price determined by American Health Properties market price five business days before pricing, plus a premium of about 10%. Each bond unconverted to be redeemed at about $5,000. Fees 1/2% on coupons, 1/4% on conversions.

Province of Manitoba (Canada) -- 200 million Swiss francs of 7 1/4% public bonds due July 17, 2000, priced at 102, via Union Bank of Switzerland. Fees 2.88.

Meisei Industrial Co. (Japan) -- 50 million Swiss francs of 7 3/4% privately placed notes due June 27, 1995, priced at 100 1/2, via Handelsbank NatWest. Guaranteed by Mitsui Taiyo Kobe Bank. Fees 1.50.

Bank of Greece (Greece) -- 300 million marks of floating-rate notes priced at par, due July 10, 1998, via Dresdner Bank. Coupon 0.35 point above six-month Libor. Callable after five, six and seven years at par. Fees 2.88.
