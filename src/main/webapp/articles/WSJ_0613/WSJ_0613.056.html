Treasury Plans to Sell $17.2 Billion in Bills

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/13/90

WASHINGTON -- The Treasury plans to pay down about $4.95 billion on the federal debt with the sale Monday of about $17.2 billion in short-term bills. Maturing bills outstanding, including 20-day cash management bills, total $22.14 billion. The offering will be divided evenly between 13-week and 26-week bills, which will be dated June 21 and will mature Sept. 20 and Dec. 20, 1990, respectively.

Tenders for the bills, available in minimum $10,000 denominations, must be received by 1 p.m. EDT Monday at the Treasury or at Federal Reserve banks or branches.
