What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/13/90

AN APPEALS COURT struck down the SEC's "one share, one vote" rule, a move that could reshape the way some companies protect themselves from takeovers. The federal panel said the SEC had overstepped its authority and had entered corporate governance, an area traditionally determined by state law. ---

Kohlberg Kravis has raised about $1.7 billion from investors to inject into RJR Nabisco to bolster RJR's sagging bonds, according to people close to the proposed transaction.

---

Stocks surged with the Dow Jones industrials climbing 40.85 points to 2933.42, less than two points below the record high set June 4. U.S. bonds slipped a bit. The dollar rose against the mark but fell against the yen.

---

The balance of payments narrowed to $22.9 billion in the first quarter, its lowest level in six years. Analysts said strong growth overseas boosted exports, while sluggish growth in the U.S. slowed demand for imports.

Japan's trade surplus contracted in May, but economists said it may expand again in a few months.

---

The FDIC isn't likely to permit banks to underwrite insurance despite a new Delaware law that permits such activity, Chairman Seidman said.

Bank earnings fell 14% in the first quarter from a year earlier. Seidman warned of growing problems in Midwest and Northwest real-estate loans.

---

Milken's planned appearance before a House subcommittee this summer may be blocked, if prosecutors and regulators have their way. They don't want the ex-Drexel official to testify before he is sentenced Oct. 1.

---

The Big Board's proposal for trading halts drew many complaints from traders, investors and executives. The plan was recommended after a study of ways to curb market volatility.

---

NBC will slash the audience-size guarantees it makes to advertisers, joining rival Capital Cities/ABC. But both say A.C. Nielsen figures showing that many viewers deserted TV in the first quarter are inaccurate.

---

Chrysler and Renault agreed to terminate their venture to build a new mini-sport utility vehicle.

Chrysler may be seeking Dollar Rent-a-Car, one of the few big independent car rental firms left.

---

ITT plans to sell part of its 37% stake in Alcatel N.V. to its partner in a European telecommunications joint venture for $640 million. Proceeds will be used primarily to buy back stock.

---

American Express hopes to raise $825 million through a public offering of 27 million new shares. Nippon Life will buy about 10 million of them.

---

A former Chicago Merc member said orders from the trading firm of exchange head Leo Melamed got special handling, at times forcing local traders to take losses on trades.

---

Bank of New York named Alan R. Griffith president of its flagship bank. Mr. Griffith, a senior executive vice president, had been a dark horse.
