South Korea Petroleum Needs

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/13/90

SINGAPORE -- South Korea's petroleum demand is expected to rise 17% to 920,000 barrels a day this year from 788,000 barrels last year, the country's petroleum association president, Hongsun Chang, said at an oil conference here. Mr. Chang, who also is the head of Kukdong Oil Co., said demand from the country's industrial, transportation, residential and commercial sectors will gain this year. He said industrial demand for petroleum product naphtha, which is a feedstock for petrochemical plants, is expected to grow 18% to 345,000 barrels a day, owing to the increase in South Korea's ethylene production capacity.

Gasoline demand from the country's transportation sector, meanwhile, is expected to grow 25% to 60,000 barrels a day. Mr. Chang said the country's residential and commercial sectors also are likely to see an increase of about 15%, because of the switch from coal to kerosene and diesel oil.

Mr. Chang predicted South Korea's petroleum demand will reach 1.35 million barrels a day by 1994, making the country the sixth-largest importer of oil in the world.
