House Bill Introduced To Give SEC Power Over Index Futures

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/13/90

WASHINGTON -- Two House members introduced legislation that would switch oversight of stock-index futures to the Securities and Exchange Commission from the Commodity Futures Trading Commission. The bill was introduced at the administration's request by Reps. John Dingell (D., Mich.) and Norman Lent (R., N.Y.), the ranking members of the Energy and Commerce Committee, which oversees the SEC.

The bill would give the SEC the power to regulate stock-index futures, which allow investors to bet on the movement of a basket of stocks without buying the actual securities. The bill also would create federal regulation of futures margins, the good-faith money needed to trade. Currently, margins are set by the commodities exchanges and can be changed by the CFTC only in an emergency.

The legislators haven't taken a formal position on the proposal, a committee spokesman said. The legislation is supported by SEC Chairman Richard Breeden and Treasury Secretary Nicholas Brady, but it is strongly opposed by CFTC Chairman Wendy Gramm, the futures industry, and members of the House and Senate agriculture committees.

In a statement, Mr. Dingell said that the complex legislation warranted "careful, thorough and detailed hearings" and that his committee intends to begin the process as soon as possible.
