Business Brief -- Pansophic Systems Inc.: Firm Plans to Shed Business In Graphics and Incur Loss

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/13/90

Pansophic Systems Inc., Lisle, Ill., said it plans to shed its graphics business, taking an estimated $27.1 million loss on its disposal. The software concern said the divestiture -- together with net one-time charges of $7.2 million associated with the closing of its Brazilian subsidiary and the reorganization of its continuing operations -- resulted in a net loss of $28.5 million, or $1.53 a share, for the fourth quarter ended April 30, on revenue of $66.4 million. For the fiscal year, the net loss was $12.9 million, or 69 cents a share, on revenue of $218.4 million.

In April, the company announced that planned special charges would erase its earnings for the year. At the time, Pansophic said it would write down certain assets of its graphics operations, and incur other one-time costs as part of a plan to "refocus" the operation.

The company said it has now decided to get out of the graphics business altogether, because the line is "no longer complementary to its long-term strategic direction" and would require financial resources better invested in Pansophic's systems and applications software businesses.
