British Ship Is Seized Over Heavy Oil-Spill In New York Harbor

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/13/90

NEW YORK -- New York Attorney General Robert Abrams said that New York, New Jersey and New York City have obtained a federal warrant to seize a British oil tanker that spilled 250,000 gallons of heavy oil into New York Harbor when it ran aground last Thursday. He said the warrant gives New York and New Jersey possession of the tanker, at least until a large bond to cover cleanup costs is posted by the tanker's owner.

The attorney general's office said the tanker, the BT Nautilus, is owned by Nautilus Motor Tanker Co. of London, and is operated by Bulk Tanker Management Ltd., also of London. The warrant was obtained after the states and city filed a lawsuit in federal court in Newark, N.J.

In a statement, Mr. Abrams said that only by seizing the BT Nautilus can the states ensure that the company will provide the money needed to cover the full cost of the cleanup and damages to the environment. His office was unable to provide an estimate of the cleanup costs.
