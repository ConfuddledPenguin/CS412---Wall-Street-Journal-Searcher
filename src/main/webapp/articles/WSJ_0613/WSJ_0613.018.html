Hong Kong's Trade Surplus

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/13/90

HONG KONG -- Hong Kong's April trade surplus with the U.S. widened 16% to 7.17 billion Hong Kong dollars (US$921.3 million) from HK$6.17 billion (US$793.2 million) a year earlier and was 43% wider than March's surplus of HK$5.01 billion. Total exports to the U.S. grew 9% to HK$11.18 billion in April from HK$10.26 billion a year earlier and 6% from HK$10.53 billion a month earlier.

Imports from the U.S. in April fell 2% to HK$4.01 billion from HK$4.09 billion the previous year and 27% from HK$5.52 billion in March. The U.S. is Hong Kong's largest export market and is second to China in re-exports purchased from the British colony. Re-exports are goods that are imported and then shipped abroad again without material change.
