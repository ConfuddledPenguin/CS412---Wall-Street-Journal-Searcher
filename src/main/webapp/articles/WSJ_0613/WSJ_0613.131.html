Who's News: Alpharel Is Dismissing Chief Financial Officer

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/13/90

CAMARILLO, Calif. -- Troubled Alpharel Inc. said it's dismissing its secretary and chief financial officer, William H. Lane, because it simply can't afford to keep him anymore. "It wasn't a good-guy or bad-guy thing," Robert T. Bruce, chairman of the maker of computerized document management systems, said in an interview. "We just have to cut back on the number of non-revenue generating people" at the company. Mr. Lane, according to proxy material, earned $91,246 last year.

Mr. Lane, a former Honeywell Inc. executive and private business consultant who came to Alpharel a little more than a year ago, will be succeeded this week by John W. Low, who has been corporate controller of the company since August 1987.

In January, Alpharel said it would trim costs by reducing its work force to 19 from 40. In the first quarter, the company had a net loss of $313,000, or three cents a share, on revenue of $1.1 million.

Prior to Alpharel, Mr. Low was with Price Waterhouse for seven years, most recently as manager in their Entrepreneurial Services Group.

Mr. Lane couldn't immediately be reached for comment.

Separately, Alpharel said Roger H. Erickson has been appointed vice president, engineering. The five-year company veteran, who has served as, among other things, director of technical projects, succeeds Don T. Miller. Mr. Miller will leave Alpharel in August, the company said.
