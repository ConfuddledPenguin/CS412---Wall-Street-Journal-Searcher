Who's News: Semhon Leaves Posts At Parlux; Kaufman Is Named President

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/13/90

NEW YORK -- Parlux Fragrances Inc. said Gerard Semhon resigned as president, chief executive officer and a director to pursue other interests. Robert Kaufman, a new director, succeeds him as president. A spokesman for Parlux, a fragrance company that recently resolved a dispute with its largest shareholder by effectively giving this holder's group control of the board, said the management changes weren't related to that agreement.

The spokesman said the chief executive's post will be filled, but not in the "immediate future." Mr. Semhon, 53 years old, who has an 8% stake in Parlux, said through the spokesman that he will remain a consultant to the company.

Parlux also is no longer exploring the sale of any of its divisions, the spokesman said, but is "looking into the acquisition" of other companies, which he couldn't specify. Parlux, which has annual revenue of more than $20 million, said earlier this year that it intended to sell all or part of the company. Parlux has world-wide marketing rights to fragrances such as Spectacular by Joan Collins, and Anne Klein.

Mr. Semhon's departure from the board reduces the number of directors to eight. It means that five directors are appointees of a group headed by Ilia Lekach, a director and Parlux's largest holder, with a stake of about 30%. After the board shift was announced several weeks ago, Mr. Lekach agreed to drop his request for a special meeting of holders to consider the removal of three Parlux directors.

Parlux said at the time of the agreement that Mr. Kaufman, 36, one of Mr. Lekach's associates, would join the company as executive vice president. Parlux also said then that Mr. Lekach wouldn't seek further board or management changes.
