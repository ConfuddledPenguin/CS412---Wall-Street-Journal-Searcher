UAL Officer Plans to Quit If Buy-Out Is Successful

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/13/90

CHICAGO -- UAL Corp.'s Chief Financial Officer John C. Pope, as expected, said he would resign if the proposed employee buy-out of United Airlines succeeds. United's three unions have made no secret that they want to oust Mr. Pope, who is second in rank only to Chairman Stephen M. Wolf.

The labor group already picked Chrysler's former vice chairman Gerald Greenwald to succeed Mr. Wolf, and now is seeking an airline executive to take over many of Mr. Pope's duties. Mr. Pope also served as UAL's executive vice president for marketing.

Relations between Mr. Pope and UAL's pilot union have been strained ever since an earlier union-management bid collapsed last fall. Mr. Pope handled the financing effort for that bid. When it failed, banks cited Mr. Pope's negotiating tactics, in part, for their refusal to finance the offer.

Mr. Pope is the second top executive to announce his resignation. On Monday, Barry A. Kotar resigned as chief executive officer of the Covia computerized reservations unit to take a post with NWA Inc., parent of Northwest Airlines. The UAL employee group intends to spin off Covia if the $4.38 billion buy-out succeeds.

Mr. Greenwald, the employees' chairman-designate, visited UAL headquarters yesterday in an effort to convince most members of senior management to remain. Mr. Greenwald must show banks that UAL will have a strong management team under employee ownership in order to obtain financing for the offer.
