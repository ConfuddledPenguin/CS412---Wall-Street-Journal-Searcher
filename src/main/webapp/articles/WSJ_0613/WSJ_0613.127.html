Business Brief -- Western Union Corp.: Proposed Recapitalization Is Revised for Bondholders

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/13/90

Western Union Corp., New York, debt-laden and struggling to avoid bankruptcy court, again revised its proposed recapitalization plan to satisfy its bondholders. The communications and financial services company now wants to offer a different package of debt securities and common shares to holders of its 19.25% senior secured reset notes due Dec. 15, 1992, and to holders of its 16% notes due June 15, 1991. The revision affects $530 million of debt. Western Union is also seeking to reclassify its preferred stock into new common shares.

In the latest revision, the company seeks to change the principal amounts of two new notes and plans to offer a different mix of common, Class C and Class D shares, in exchange for each $1,000 face value of the 19.25% and 16% notes. In addition, Western Union is seeking a change in interest payment terms on one new note, including the rate and form of payment.
