Technology: Sun Microsystems Reaches Pact With Mentor Graphics

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/13/90

MOUNTAIN VIEW, Calif. -- Sun Microsystems said that Mentor Graphics Inc. agreed to purchase $300 million of Sun computers over the next three years, the first time Mentor has broken its practice of buying only Hewlett-Packard Co. machines. Mentor, a maker of software used in designing semiconductors and other electronic parts, announced in January that it would stop dealing exclusively with Hewlett-Packard and begin making its software available to Sun. Mentor buys computers, loads them with Mentor software, and then resells them under the Mentor nameplate.

The union between Sun and Mentor could prove a boon to both. Mentor's software will now be available on products of the two largest workstation makers. For Sun, a link with Mentor's widely used software could spur acceptance of Sun's so-called SPARC computer architecture, which is different from Hewlett-Packard's workstation architecture. Sun and Hewlett-Packard have been battling to establish their respective architectures as the standard for the workstation market.

In over-the-counter trading yesterday, Sun closed at $33, up $1.375. Mentor closed at $24.25, down 25 cents.
