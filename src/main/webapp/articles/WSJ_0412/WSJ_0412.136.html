Business Brief -- HomeFed Corp.: Thrift Reports Profit Rose To Record in First Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/12/90

HomeFed Corp. said first-quarter net income rose 13%, to a record $34.5 million, or $1.48 a fully diluted share. The San Diego, Calif.-based thrift cited increased net interest income and tight expense controls. HomeFed also benefited from an improving net-interest margin, the difference between what it pays for funds and what it earns on its assets. That at least partly reflects one positive aspect of the much-maligned thrift bailout bill. The bill has led to a reduction in cost of funds by putting some desperate thrifts, which have to pay more to attract money and bid up interest rates, out of business.

In the year-earlier period, HomeFed earned $30.5 million, or $1.31 a share.

HomeFed said net interest income totaled $116 million, 14% above the year-earlier quarter's $101.7 million. Its net-interest margin was 2.87, compared with 2.70 a year earlier.
