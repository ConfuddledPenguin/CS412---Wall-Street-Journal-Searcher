Dow Jones, Others Take Stakes in Parent Of French Publisher

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/12/90

PARIS -- Groupe Expansion S.A. said the capital base of its controlling holding company, Ponex S.A., will be strengthened when Dow Jones & Co. and two European publishing groups acquire minority interests in the French business publications company. Ponex is to remain 50.2%-controlled by Expansion Chairman Jean-Louis Servan-Schreiber. Dow Jones, the publisher of The Wall Street Journal and other publications and information services, will take a 16.3% interest in Ponex. Handelsblatt of West Germany will have a 13% stake and the Prisa Group of Spain will have a 5% shareholding. The remaining 15.5% is owned by a group of Expansion managers and institutional investors.

Expansion said Dow Jones and Handelsblatt also will become shareholders of Eurexpansion, a network of 30 European financial and economic publications in 11 countries that was set up by Expansion last autumn. Dow Jones will have a 15% share in Eurexpansion, which remains 58% controlled by the French group. Handelsblatt is taking a 20% interest.

Including its new stakes, Dow Jones's cumulative investment in Ponex, Groupe Expansion and Eurexpansion will be about $20 million. Dow Jones had held a 14.5% direct stake in Groupe Expansion. But part of that is being shifted to Ponex, reducing Dow Jones's direct holding in Groupe Expansion to about 7.5%.

Mr. Servan-Schreiber confirmed that Groupe Expansion probably will seek a listing on the Paris bourse some time in 1991. A decision will be made early next year, he said.
