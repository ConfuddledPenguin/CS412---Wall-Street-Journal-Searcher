International Brief -- Societe Generale: Profit Rose 17% in 1989; Dividend Increase Is Slated

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/12/90

Societe Generale reported in Paris that its consolidated net profit rose 17% in 1989 and unveiled plans to increase its net annual dividend to 15 francs (2.7 cents) a share from 13 francs (2.3 cents). Marc Vienot, chairman of the private-sector banking group, told a news conference that the 1989 performance was "more than acceptable," with consolidated net profit after payments to minority interests climbing to 3.56 billion francs from 3.04 billion in 1988 despite an inversion of the domestic interest-rate curve that substantially cut profits from bond market operations.

Mr. Vienot asserted that the weakness of bond market operations was offset, however, by an "exceptional" rise in profits from its asset management activity. He also noted that Societe Generale's traditional commercial bank operations continued to grow satisfactorily, with total loans to customers rising 15% to 475 billion francs while client deposits climbed 10% to 362 billion.
