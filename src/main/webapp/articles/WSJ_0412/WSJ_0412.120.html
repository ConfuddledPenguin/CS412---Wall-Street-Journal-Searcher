International Brief -- Ciba-Geigy AG: Company Buys Some Assets Of Bristol-Myers Squibb Co.

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/12/90

Ciba-Geigy AG, a large Swiss chemicals and pharmaceuticals group, said it acquired the animal health products businesses of Bristol-Myers Squibb Co. The terms and value of the transaction weren't disclosed, but a Ciba-Geigy spokesman said the agreement was completed this week.

Ciba-Geigy in 1984 acquired the Western European rights to the U.S. company's animal health products businesses. The current agreement covers Bristol-Myers Squibb's operations in Asia, Australia, Canada and Latin America.

The acquisition "comprises veterinary products for the treatment of domestic and farm animals," according to a statement released by Ciba-Geigy.

The company also said the 170 employees of the Bristol-Myers Squibb operations will be offered jobs on "comparable terms" under Ciba-Geigy's ownership.

In 1989, the Bristol-Myers Squibb animal health products businesses had sales of $40 million.
