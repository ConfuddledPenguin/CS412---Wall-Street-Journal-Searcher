Continental Air Pays Fine in Safety Case

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/12/90

WASHINGTON -- The Federal Aviation Administration said Continental Airlines, a unit of Texas Air Corp., paid $215,000 to settle charges of safety violations. The FAA uncovered the alleged problems during inspections in a five-week period in October and November of 1988. The series of inspections was one of the most intensive ever conducted by the agency.

The agency said the violations included operating a Boeing Co. 727 in passenger service with an inoperative escape slide, not repairing a cargo-hold heater on a DC10 within the required period of time and failing to update the airline's operations manual. The airline also improperly weighed an aircraft, the FAA said.

Continental, in paying the fine, didn't admit that the violations occurred, the FAA said. The agency said the airline responded quickly to remedy the problems.

In Houston, a Continental spokesman said, "While we have some technical differences on some of the FAA's findings, we've agreed to the settlement in the interest of moving ahead." He noted that the findings didn't involve any safety-of-flight violations and called the settlement one of the lowest in recent aviation inspections.
