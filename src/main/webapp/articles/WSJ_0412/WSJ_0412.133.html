First Union Corp. Posts Slight Decline In 1st Quarter Profit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/12/90

CHARLOTTE, N.C. -- First Union Corp. reported that first-quarter earnings fell to $71 million, or 67 cents a share, from $72 million, or 68 cents a share, a year ago. However, the regional banking company said results improved in all of its state banking operations over the fourth quarter, including Georgia where it had run into costly delays consolidating operations.

The results were better than most analysts had expected. Analysts had been wary of the impact of a heavy load of problem assets stemming from First Union's recent acquisition of troubled Florida National Banks of Florida Inc.

Some analysts also remained skeptical following First Union's earlier announcement that a recent federal examination of its Florida commercial real estate portfolio had turned up no significant problems. Further examinations of First Union's real estate operations in Georgia, South Carolina, North Carolina and Tennessee are scheduled for some time this quarter.

The company said Florida National accounted for $234 million of the $272 million increase over last year in First Union's nonperforming assets, including problem loans, to $502 million. The company's provision for loan losses also grew in the period to $24 million from $14 million a year earlier.

First Union said, however, it expects to squeeze $40 million worth of expense savings out of the Florida National merger both this year and next. "Our strongest focus in this regard is on gaining the merger efficiencies in Florida," said Chairman Edward Crutchfield Jr.
