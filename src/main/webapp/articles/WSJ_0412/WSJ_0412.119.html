Business Brief -- Dun & Bradstreet Corp.: Three Units Will Be Sold To Sharpen Market Focus

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/12/90

Dun & Bradstreet Corp. said it plans to sell three units as part of its strategy to sharpen its focus on certain information markets. The New York-based information company said its Neodata Services, Petroleum Information and Zytron units had combined revenue of about $200 million last year. Each is profitable, D&B said, without elaborating. Nor did D&B estimate how much it expected to get for the units.

Neodata provides subscription-fulfillment services to more than 270 publications. Petroleum Information supplies drilling, production and gas-transportation data; and Zytron provides services and products to larger computer users.

D&B reiterated its expectation of posting 1990 per-share earnings of about $2.80. In 1989, net income was $586.4 million, or $3.14 a share, on revenue of $4.32 billion.
