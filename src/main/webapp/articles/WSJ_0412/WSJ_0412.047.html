New Securities Issues

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/12/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATES

Penn Central Corp. -- $150 million of subordinated debentures, due April 15, 2000, were priced as 10 5/8% securities at 99.60 to yield 10.691%. The noncallable debentures were priced at a spread of 2.10 percentage points above the Treasury's 10-year note. Rated Baa-1 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and triple-B-minus by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Shearson Lehman Hutton Inc.

MUNICIPALS

Baltimore County (Maryland) -- $119 million of consolidated public improvement bonds, 1990 Series, and metropolitan district bonds, 61st issue, due April 1, 1991-2010, apparently were won by a Shearson Lehman Hutton Inc. group with a bid setting the true interest cost at 6.8272%. The bonds, rated triple-A by Moody's and double-A-plus by S&P, were priced for reoffering to yield from 5.80% in 1991 to 7.00% in 2005-2010.

Dallas -- $68 million of limited tax bonds, Improvement Series 1990, due Feb. 15, 1991-2010, apparently were won by a Prudential-Bache Capital Funding group with a bid setting the net interest cost at 7.0485%. The bonds, rated triple-A by both Moody's and S&P, were priced for reoffering to yield from 6.00% in 1991 to 7.20%% in 2009 and 2010.

MORTGAGE-BACKED

Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $500 million of real estate mortgage investment conduit securities were offered in six classes by Salomon Brothers Inc. The offering, Series 164, backed by Freddie Mac 10% securities, included a large group of securities targeted at foreign investors.

EUROBONDS

British Telecommunications PLC (U.K.) -- #100 million ($164.5 million) of 13 3/4% Eurobonds due Feb. 14, 1993, at 99.45 via Barclays de Zoete Wedd Ltd. Fungible with existing #300 million issue. Fees 1 3/8.

World Bank (agency) -- 100 million Swiss francs ($67.5 million) of 7 1/2% public bonds due May 23, 2000, priced at 102 to yield 7.21% via Union Bank of Switzerland. Fees undisclosed.

European Investment Bank (agency) -- $53.75 million of floating-rate notes due March 2008, priced at par and paying interest at six-month London interbank offered rate via Morgan Stanley International. Average life of 16.6 years. Fees 0.45.
