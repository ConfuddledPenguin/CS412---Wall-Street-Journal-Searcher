Canada Considers Appeal of Ruling On Antitrust Arm

  • Author: Rose Tamburri
  • Date: 04/12/90

OTTAWA -- The Canadian government is considering an appeal against a Quebec Superior Court decision, which ruled that the operations of the Canadian Competition Tribunal were unconstitutional. The Quebec court said the tribunal has been given the powers of a court but doesn't have the "essential attributes" of judicial independence.

The four-year-old tribunal is made up of appointed judicial and business experts. It reviews cases referred to it by the government's antitrust agency, the Bureau of Competition Policy.

Howard Wetston, the bureau's director of investigation and research, said the ruling was "an important decision that raises a number of questions related to the enforcement of the competition act." Mr. Wetston said he is carefully studying the decision with the Canadian Attorney General and is "considering the question of an appeal."

The Quebec court's ruling concerned a case involving Alex Couture Inc., a Quebec City-based processor of animal waste products. The tribunal approved a government order in 1987 blocking the merger between Alex Couture and a Montreal animal rendering company. Alex Couture won a stay of proceedings and then launched the court challenge against the tribunal.

The Quebec court said that because the tribunal lacks the judicial independence of a court it violated Alex Couture's "right of association" and its right to be heard by an independent and impartial tribunal.

The ruling prompted speculation about the possible impact of the court's decision on major cases including its recent approval of a consent order covering the merger of Imperial Oil Ltd. and Texaco Canada Inc.

An Imperial Oil spokesman said the ruling doesn't have any implications for its merger with Texaco Canada because the consent order was reached between the company and the competition bureau. The $4.15 billion Imperial-Texaco Canada merger, was the biggest case to come before the tribunal since the agency was established in 1986.

Imperial Oil, which is 70%-owned by Exxon Corp., purchased 78% of Texaco Canada last year from Texaco Inc. The Canadian Bureau of Competition Policy reached an agreement for a consent order with Imperial requiring Imperial to sell various assets including 600 gas retail outlets, a refinery and storage and distribution terminals.

The tribunal approved the consent order after some adjustments were made. Imperial is proceeding with the divestitures.
