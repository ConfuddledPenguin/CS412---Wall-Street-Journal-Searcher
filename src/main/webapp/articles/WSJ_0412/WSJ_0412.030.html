Fannie Mae Posts 67% Rise in Net For First Period

  • Author: Mary Lu Carnevale
  • Date: 04/12/90

WASHINGTON -- The Federal National Mortgage Association said first-quarter net income rose 67% to a record $277 million, or $1.06 a share. In the year-earlier period, Fannie Mae reported earnings of $165 million, or 66 cents a share. The results reflected increases in net interest income on Fannie Mae's portfolio, income from guaranty fees on mortgage-backed securities, and other income. At the end of the quarter, Fannie Mae's portfolio totaled $123.64 billion and it had $246.39 billion in mortgage-backed securities outstanding.

The federally chartered, publicly traded corporation operates a secondary market in home loans, buying mortgages from lenders, packaging some into securities for sale to investors and holding the rest in its portfolio. The company said the latest results were its ninth consecutive quarter of record profits.

The results were "based on solid fundamentals," said Chairman David Maxwell. "Our net interest margin was the widest in history; we issued the largest volume of mortgage-backed securities in any first quarter; and properties acquired through foreclosures dropped to their lowest level since peaking in 1987."

Fannie Mae said that net interest income rose to $378.4 million from $253.1 million the year before, reflecting increases in the average investment balance and the net interest margin. Guaranty fees rose to $120.5 million from $94.5 million.

The provision for losses was $75 million, down from $80 million the year before. Charge-offs totaled $59 million in the first period, up slightly from $58 million a year ago. During the latest quarter, Fannie Mae acquired 2,455 properties through foreclosure, down from 2,975 in the 1989 quarter and 44% below the 1987 third-quarter peak.

Administrative expenses rose to $66.3 million from $56.1 million in 1989. Federal income taxes nearly doubled to $110.1 million from $56.8 million.

Fannie Mae bought $5.7 billion of mortgages in the latest quarter and sold $1 billion. That compares with purchases of $3.4 billion the year before and sales of $1.8 billion. It also said it issued $24.1 billion of mortgage-backed securities in the latest period, compared with $14.4 billion the year before.
