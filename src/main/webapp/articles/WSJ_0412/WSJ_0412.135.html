Business Brief -- Siliconix Inc.: Chapter 11 Reorganization Filed by California Concern

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/12/90

Siliconix Inc., Santa Clara, Calif., said it filed for reorganization under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code, partly because of an unfavorable verdict in a patent infringement suit. A federal judge ruled earlier this week that Siliconix, a maker of semiconductor products, had infringed on patents relating to certain advanced power transistor technology held by International Rectifier Corp. Damages and penalties haven't been determined yet. The lawsuit has been pending for four years.

Siliconix also said it is currently in technical default of certain lending agreements with its banks, which include First Interstate Bancorp. In 1989, Siliconix had a net loss of $30 million on sales of $122.5 million.

The company didn't disclose its assets or debts, and couldn't immediately be reached for comment.

Richard E. Lee, the company's chairman, said he expects Siliconix to "continue business as usual."

In over-the-counter trading yesterday, Siliconix closed at 62.5 cents, down $1.25.
