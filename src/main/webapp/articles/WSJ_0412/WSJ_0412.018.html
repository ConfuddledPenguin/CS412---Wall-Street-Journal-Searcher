Who's News: Toronto Maple Leafs' Owner, Ballard, Dies

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/12/90

TORONTO -- Harold Ballard, the 86-year-old cantankerous majority-owner of Maple Leaf Gardens Ltd., died yesterday afternoon, renewing speculation about who will inherit control of the company and its profitable National Hockey League franchise. Mr. Ballard's 80% stake in Maple Leaf Gardens, which owns the Toronto Maple Leafs, is currently valued at about 130 million Canadian dollars (US$111.2 million). The company said a summary of Mr. Ballard's will and testament will be released after disclosure to his family and others, but it didn't indicate exactly when that will happen.

Canada's two biggest beer companies, Molson Cos. and John Labatt Ltd., have an interest in the provisions of Mr. Ballard's will. Molson, which already owns the Montreal Canadiens N.H.L. franchise, has an option to buy 20% of Mr. Ballard's stake in Maple Leaf Gardens. Labatt is a partner in an entertainment promotion concern owned in part by William Ballard, Mr. Ballard's oldest son. William Ballard also indirectly holds stock in Maple Leaf Gardens.

Control of Maple Leaf Gardens could be complicated further by any claim on Mr. Ballard's estate made by Yolanda Ballard, 56, his longtime companion. Mr. Ballard never married Ms. Ballard, but she legally adopted his last name and claimed to be his common-law wife. If an Ontario court rules that Ms. Ballard was Harold Ballard's common-law wife, she could be entitled to a portion of his estate.

After several years of acrimony in his family, Mr. Ballard recently reconciled with his two sons and his daughter. He became seriously ill last January and had been in the hospital since then for treatment of acute diabetes and heart ailments. In February, a court in the province of Ontario declared him incompetent to manage his affairs and he subsequently was replaced as president of Maple Leaf Gardens.
