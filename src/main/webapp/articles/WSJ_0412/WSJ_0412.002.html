Wednesday's Markets: Stocks Ease, Bond Prices Post Gains --- Markets Stay Neutral During Short Week; Oil Grabs Spotlight

  • Author: Douglas R. Sease
  • Date: 04/12/90

Stock prices slipped a little while investors nudged bond prices higher as financial markets remained in neutral during the shortened holiday week. The Dow Jones Industrial Average fell 1.35 points to close at 2729.73 in sluggish trading. Long-term bond prices rebounded modestly from their sell-off Tuesday. The dollar was mixed.

Oil prices provided what little excitement there was in financial markets yesterday. Oil prices tumbled in late New York trading Tuesday and continued to fall yesterday after the American Petroleum Institute released figures that showed burgeoning inventories of crude oil. But speculation that the Organization of Petroleum Exporting Countries is on the verge of calling an emergency meeting helped prices rebound late yesterday.

Although neither the stock nor the bond market reacted sharply to the fluctuations in oil prices, tumbling crude prices touched off a minor bond-market rally while prompting investors to sell oil stocks and buy airlines issues.

The gains in bond prices eased later as oil prices rebounded, but the oil stocks ended down while airline issues held on to most of their gains. Overall, though, stock investors remain worried about the outlook for first-quarter earnings.

In major market action:

Stock prices slipped slightly in slack trading. Volume on the New York Stock Exchange totaled 141.1 million shares. Declining issues on the Big Board were ahead of gainers 762 to 720.

Bond prices edged higher. The Treasury's benchmark issue ended nearly 1/4 point higher, a gain of about $2.50 for a bond with a $1,000 face amount. The yield on the issue dropped to 8.57%.

The dollar was mixed. In late afternoon New York trading the currency was at 1.6715 marks and 158 yen, compared with 1.6755 marks and 157.60 yen Tuesday.
