Canada Orders Price Waterhouse To Have Review

  • Author: Robert Melnbardis
  • Date: 04/12/90

TORONTO -- The Canadian partnership of the accounting firm Price Waterhouse was ordered by the Ontario Securities Commission to submit to an independent review of its policies on auditing public companies. The order was the result of a hearing in which commission staff argued that Price Waterhouse failed to comply with generally accepted auditing standards in its audit of the fiscal 1987 financial statements of National Business Systems Inc.

As previously reported, the president of National Business resigned in January 1988 after irregularities were found in the company's 1987 financial statements. He and four other former company executives subsequently were charged with theft and fraud.

In a negotiated settlement, Price Waterhouse agreed to comply with the order, but it didn't admit or deny the accuracy of commission staff's allegations and conclusions.

Under the order, Price Waterhouse must retain a firm of chartered accountants to review certain of its auditing policies and procedures. Price Waterhouse must implement the independent accountant's recommendations within three months of receiving its report, and must pay 70,000 Canadian dollars (US$59,900) toward the cost of the commission hearing.

The order is the first of its kind from the commission, said Brenda Eprile, associate chief accountant. It indicates the commission's willingness to investigate allegations that generally accepted auditing standards haven't been followed during the audit of a public company, she said.

"We felt this was a major case," Ms. Eprile said.

In a statement, Price Waterhouse said the "great majority" of changes made to the original 1987 financial statements of National Business "reflected a massive management fraud involving certain of the former senior {National Business} executives" and others. It said the items that resulted in changes to the financial statements occurred in the company's Canadian operations for which Greenwood Cook & Co., an accounting firm based in Mississauga, had primary responsibility.

Commission staff said the relationship between Price Waterhouse and Greenwood Cook during the 1987 audit of National Business was "unclear." But Price Waterhouse was the auditor of record and signed the financial statements, Ms. Eprile said.

In a letter sent to the commission last month, Price Waterhouse said it believed that Robert Greenwood, a partner at Greenwood Cook, received certain payments made privately by the former president of National Business while Greenwood Cook was auditing National Business' statements.

Mr. Greenwood declined to comment. In a letter sent to the commission, Greenwood Cook said it objected to publication of the settlement agreement between the commission and Price Waterhouse because certain allegations concerning Greenwood Cook are "inaccurate."

National Business Systems, based in Mississauga, Ontario, supplies plastic transaction card machines and related equipment. Hees International Bancorp Inc., a Toronto merchant bank and management and investment services concern controlled by brothers Peter and Edward Bronfman, assumed management of National Business in 1988.
