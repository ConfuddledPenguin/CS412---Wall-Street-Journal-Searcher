Mead Earnings Fell 25% in 1st Quarter; Sales Edged Up 1.1%

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/12/90

DAYTON, Ohio -- Mead Corp. reported a 25% decline in first-quarter earnings because of lower prices for some types of paper and heavy spending for new products and facilities. The forest products and electronic publishing concern earned $39.5 million, or 62 cents a share, down from $52.6 million, or 81 cents a share, a year earlier. Sales crept up 1.1% to $1.14 billion from $1.13 billion.

Net income was 10% to 20% higher than many analysts had been expecting. Mark S. Rogers, paper analyst with Prudential-Bache securities, said net was well above the 50 cents a share he had forecast, but, like other analysts contacted, he hadn't had a chance to obtain detailed numbers that would show where profits exceeded his expectations.

Lower prices for uncoated papers and corrugating medium were a major factor in the earnings decline, officials said. Uncoated papers are used in copiers, computer printers and other office applications. Corrugating medium is used to make containers.

The $560 million expansion program under way at the company's coated paperboard plant at Mahrt, Ala., disrupted production of existing facilities at the site, Mead said. Also, the company is incurring wage and training costs for the people who will operate the new facilities beginning late in the 1990 third quarter. A planned shutdown to modernize a paper machine at Escanaba, Mich., also cut into earnings. The company also is spending heavily to develop its new color copying business, which Mead calls Cycolor.

Mead Data Central Inc., the company's electronic publishing subsidiary, and Zellerbach, a paper products distribution unit, had slightly higher first-quarter profit, Mead said.
