Marketing & Media: Perrier Water's Relaunch Is Behind Schedule in U.S.

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/12/90

PARIS -- Source Perrier S.A. officials say the U.S. relaunch of their bubbly water now is a week or two behind schedule, because of delays in rebuilding inventory and in obtaining a U.S. Food and Drug Administration review. One month ago they announced that their green bottles would be back in stores on the East Coast by the first week of April, and on the West Coast by the third week of April. Now they say it will be around April 23 before the East Coast relaunch, and early May for the West Coast. Inland, it could be as late as the end of May before stores have Perrier again.

In Britain, however, Perrier was relaunched on time, on April 4, accompanied by a new advertising campaign.

Perrier announced a world-wide recall of its mineral water in February, after trace levels of benzene were found in it by U.S. scientists. Benzene is a suspected cause of cancer, although U.S. and French authorities say the amount in Perrier was too low to present a real health hazard.

Perrier says benzene always has been present in the natural carbon dioxide gas it uses to increase its water's carbonization. It says it failed to change filters frequently enough on the pipes that supply carbon dioxide gas to its bottling plant in the south of France.

Perrier officials say one purpose of the U.S. delay is to avoid shortages.
