Teledyne Inc. Says Profit Declined 17% In the First Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/12/90

LOS ANGELES -- Teledyne Inc. posted a 17% drop in first-quarter net income to $58.3 million, or $1.05 a share, from $70.5 million, or $1.26 a share, a year ago. Per-share amounts reflected a 5-for-1 common stock split distributed March 1. Sales fell 3.2% to $836.9 million from $864.3 million a year earlier. The manufacturing and insurance concern said sales dropped in all segments except its aviation and electronics businesses.

The company also for the first time broke out results for its insurance and finance operations that are being spun off into a new company called Unitrin Inc. Total revenue of the Unitrin companies increased to $298.6 million from $272.1 million a year ago. Profit from those businesses, classified as discontinued operations, fell to $25.6 million, or 46 cents a share, from $35.3 million, or 63 cents a share, a year earlier. Per-share amounts were calculated using 55.4 million average Unitrin shares. Teledyne said it expects to mail the Unitrin stock certificates to its shareholders this month.

Teledyne said the casualty insurance companies experienced a drop in underwriting results in the latest quarter, primarily because of "adverse experience from storms and in personal lines."

Profit from continuing operations fell to $32.7 million, or 59 cents a share, from $35.2 million, or 63 cents a share, a year ago. Included in the results for the latest quarter was a reversal of a $7.7 million provision for income taxes recorded in prior years on undistributed earnings of the insurance and finance subsidiaries.

Earnings were released after the close of stock market trading yesterday. In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange, Teledyne shares closed at $71.125, off 25 cents.
