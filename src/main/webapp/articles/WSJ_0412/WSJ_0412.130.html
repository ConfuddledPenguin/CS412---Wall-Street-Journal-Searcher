UAL's United Air PLans Some May-June Fare Cuts

  • Author: Asra Q. Nomani
  • Date: 04/12/90

CHICAGO -- United Airlines said it is decreasing its cheapest, most-restricted fares between some Northeast cities and California by an average of 15% for travel from May 1 through June 11. The UAL Corp. unit said the move is an attempt to spark transcontinental traffic during a typically slow travel period between the Easter and Passover holidays and summer.

It's unclear whether the move will spread to other markets but it appears to be a limited pricing move. "It sounds pretty isolated," said Robert Decker, an airlines analyst at Duff & Phelps in Chicago. Other carriers are expected to match the action.

United said the new round-trip fare levels will be $358 for off-peak travel between noon Monday and noon Thursday, and $398 for peak travel during the rest of the week. The move represents a cut of $60 to $70 from current fares.

Ticket restrictions are tight, requiring a Saturday night stay and ticket purchase 14 days before travel and 24 hours after making a reservation. Also, the fares are nonrefundable.
