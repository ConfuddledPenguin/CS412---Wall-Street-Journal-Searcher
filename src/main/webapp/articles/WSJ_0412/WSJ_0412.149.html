Business Brief -- ENSR Corp.: Environmental Consultant Agrees to $125 Million Bid

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/12/90

ENSR Corp. said it agreed to a $125 million acquisition offer from a unit of a German company. ENSR, Houston, said its directors approved an offer of $8.25 a share for all 9.6 million ENSR shares from American NuKEM Corp., a U.S. subsidiary of NuKEM G.m.b.H., a German holding company. The proposal also calls for NuKEM to assume some ENSR debt, and is subject to NuKEM's acquiring 66.67% of the shares outstanding. ENSR said a tender offer will begin within five business days.

In American Stock Exchange trading yesterday, ENSR surged $2.125 to $8.

ENSR provides environmental consulting services and hazardous waste remediation. The company had 1989 revenue of $158 million. American NuKEM, based in New Jersey, specializes in incineration and waste treatment. It had 1989 revenue of $49.6 million. The majority owner of its parent, NuKEM G.m.b.H., is Rheinisch Westfaelisches Elektrizitaetswerk AG, the largest utility in Germany. The other two holders of NuKEM G.m.b.H. are Degussa AG and Metallgesellschaft AG.

Bert Wollen, ENSR interim chief executive officer, said the two companies have almost no duplication of services, and complement each other geographically.
