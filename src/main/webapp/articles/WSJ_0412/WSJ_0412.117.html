Business Brief -- First Chicago Corp.: Shareholder Sues Company, Charging It Misled Investors

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/12/90

First Chicago Corp. shareholder sued the company, claiming it violated securities law by misleading investors about its financial condition. The complaint, filed in U.S. District Court in Chicago, alleges that in 1989 and early 1990, the bank holding company failed to disclose "adverse material information" and issued "overly optimistic statements" about its prospects.

On March 29, First Chicago disclosed that its first-quarter earnings will be down about 50% because of increased problems in its real-estate loan portfolio. First Chicago, parent of First National Bank of Chicago, said it expects to set aside between $150 million and $160 million for loan losses in the quarter. The company had provided similar information to analysts more than a week before the public announcement.

The lawsuit, filed by Edward J. Kunze, seeks class action status. The complaint, which also names certain company officials as defendants, seeks undetermined damages. A First Chicago spokeswoman declined to comment, citing a company policy against discussing litigation.
