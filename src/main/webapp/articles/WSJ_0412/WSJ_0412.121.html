Who's News: Dickenson Mines Ltd. Chief Kachmar Quits As Proxy Fight Lost

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/12/90

TORONTO -- John Kachmar resigned as president and chief executive officer of Dickenson Mines Ltd. after management lost a proxy battle with the gold mining company's major shareholder, which succeeded in unseating two of Dickenson's directors. Robert McEwen, chairman of Dickenson and president of Toronto-based Goldcorp Investments Ltd., which owns 70% of Dickenson's shares, said the company's direction won't change because of the resignation of Mr. Kachmar, 53 years old.

The Dickenson board later elected Mr. McEwen to the additional post of chief executive. David Libby, chief operating officer, was named to the additional position of president.

Mr. Kachmar, who has led Dickenson for the past nine years, said at the annual meeting that his resignation is "part of an orderly and normal process." He said he will remain a Dickenson director, but he wouldn't elaborate on the reasons for his resignation from the executive posts.

In the proxy battle, two of Dickenson's directors were succeeded by Goldcorp nominees. Mr. McEwen said the rival nominations were made to place more mining engineers on the Dickenson board.

James McCartney, a long-term Dickenson director who lost his seat, said Mr. Kachmar agreed to resign under pressure from Mr. McEwen. Mr. McCartney, a lawyer, said the change in top management breaches a covenant of Dickenson's 38 million Canadian dollar (US$32.8 million) gold loan and will probably lead to Dickenson's having to refinance the loan on less favorable terms.

Mr. McEwen said his views "differ" from Mr. McCartney's, but he wouldn't elaborate.
