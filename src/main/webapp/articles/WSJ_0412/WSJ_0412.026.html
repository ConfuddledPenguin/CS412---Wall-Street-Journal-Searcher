Raytheon Reports Net Increased 8.4% For the First Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/12/90

LEXINGTON, Mass. -- Raytheon Co. reported an 8.4% increase in net income for the first quarter on a 7.3% revenue gain. The electronics, aircraft and missile manufacturer said net income was $130.9 million, or $2 a share, compared with $120.8 million, or $1.83 a share, for the 1989 first quarter. Revenue rose to $2.23 billion from $2.07 billion.

In a statement, Raytheon said its total order backlog grew to $9.49 billion, including funded U.S. government orders of $6.84 billion. Backlog at the close of last year's first quarter was $8.88 billion, including a government-funded backlog of $6.78 billion.

Thomas L. Phillips, chairman and chief executive, said that in the first quarter, the electronics segment, Raytheon's largest, performed well, while the aircraft and energy-services segments continued rapid profit growth and all five business units remained profitable. "Raytheon continues to grow on the strength of its diversified government and commercial business base," Mr. Phillips said.

The company also said that its Beech Aircraft unit enjoyed higher sales and improved profit margins. During the quarter, Raytheon's dividend was raised to 60 cents a share from 55 cents.
