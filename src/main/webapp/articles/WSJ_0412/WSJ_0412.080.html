Who's News: Limited Inc. Says Hopkins, 57, to Fill Vice Chairman's Post

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/12/90

NEW YORK -- Thomas G. Hopkins was named vice chairman of Limited Inc., a post that has been vacant since Robert Morosky resigned as vice chairman and chief financial officer three years ago. Despite his new title, the 57-year-old Mr. Hopkins doesn't appear to be the heir apparent to Leslie Wexner, Limited's 53-year-old chairman, president and chief executive.

According to those familiar with the situation, the new designation is formal acknowledgement of the additional operating responsibilities Mr. Hopkins has assumed in recent years. Since 1982, Mr. Hopkins has served as executive vice president, organizational development.

"I don't think heir apparent is an operative term in Columbus, Ohio," said William N. Smith, an analyst at Smith Barney, Harris Upham Inc. "Mr. Wexner has done an excellent job of developing a management team, but I don't see him stepping down in the near future."

At the same time, Limited named Mr. Hopkins and Kenneth B. Gilman, executive vice president and chief financial officer, to the company's board. Limited will now have 13 board members.

Apparently determined to avoid naming a clear No. 2 executive, Mr. Wexner told one associate yesterday that "Ken and Tom are my right hand and my left hand, but I'm ambidextrous."

When Mr. Morosky served as vice chairman, he was considered Mr. Wexner's most likely successor at the retailing giant. However, after Mr. Morosky resigned, many of his responsibilities were delegated to others. Company presidents now supervise their own information systems and distribution centers, and Mr. Morosky's role as the Limited's top financial executive has been assumed by Mr. Gilman.
