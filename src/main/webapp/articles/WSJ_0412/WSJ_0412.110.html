Richards Faces Williams For Texas Governorship

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/12/90

AUSTIN -- Texas State Treasurer Ann Richards won a decisive victory over Attorney General Jim Mattox in a Democratic gubernatorial primary that sets up a Texas-sized brawl with Republican Clayton Williams in the fall. Mrs. Richards, a recovered alcoholic, rode out a storm of criticism from the pugnacious Mr. Mattox, who repeatedly called on her to say if she had ever used illegal drugs. She tried to deflect the question by charging that Mr. Mattox had profited from his years in public service, and she called on him to release his federal income-tax returns. It was, by any standard, one of the nastiest campaigns in Texas political history.

Mrs. Richards won more than 600,000 votes, or 57%, while Mr. Mattox picked up more than 460,000 votes, or 43%.

Mr. Mattox said Mrs. Richards "has proven that she's a very strong candidate" and pledged to help her in an uphill campaign against the GOP's Mr. Williams, a banker, oil man and rancher who spent $8 million, $6 million of it from his own deep pockets, to win his party's nomination.
