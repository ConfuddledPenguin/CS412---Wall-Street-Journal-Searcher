Partnership Holds Pic 'N' Save Stake; Takeover Planned

  • Author: Rhonda L. Rundle
  • Date: 04/12/90

A partnership controlled by David H. Batchelder, a former strategist for T. Boone Pickens, disclosed that it holds a 6.6% stake in Pic 'N' Save Corp. and plans to make a bid to acquire the company. The bid has an indicated value of about $447 million, based on Pic 'N' Save's roughly 35.5 million shares outstanding. The company, based in Dominguez, Calif., is a retail chain that buys manufacturers' close-out merchandise and sells it at deep discounts to customers.

Girard Partners Limited Partnership said in a filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission that it owns 2,365,100 Pic 'N' Save common shares, including shares bought from Feb. 12 to April 5 for $9.87 to $12.01 a share.

In national over-the-counter trading, Pic 'N' Save shares closed at $13.50, up 50 cents. The Girard filing surfaced after trading ended.

William M. Jacobs Jr., the retailer's senior vice president and chief financial officer, answered questions about the company and its business but declined to comment on the Girard filing. The company's shares have been active in recent sessions in the wake of reports that someone was accumulating the stock.

In the filing, Girard said it plans to solicit consents from shareholders to approve a resolution calling for a five-member board consisting of a majority of Girard nominees. Pic 'N' Save's board now has eight members. Officers and directors own less than 5% of the company's stock.

The written-consents process is an increasingly popular mechanism for acquiring management control, similar to a proxy solicitation, which doesn't require a shareholder meeting. Girard said its nominees, if elected, would seek acquisition proposals for a negotiated sale of the company to the partnership or to another bidder. Girard said it is initiating discussions with potential lending sources regarding financing an acquisition.

Mr. Batchelder left Mr. Pickens's Mesa Limited Partnership two years ago and formed his own investment firm in La Jolla, Calif. Last year, Mr. Batchelder advised Los Angeles investor Marvin Davis in his failed attempts to buy NWA Inc., parent of Northwest Airlines, and UAL Corp., parent of United Airlines. The Pic 'N' Save bid apparently is Mr. Batchelder's first major takeover attempt on his own steam. Mr. Batchelder didn't return calls from a reporter.

Pic 'N' Save was founded more than four decades ago in Southern California, went public in 1971, and in recent years has expanded into New York, New Jersey, Texas and other states. Last month, the company reported "disappointing" 1989 results. Net income fell 34% to $30.9 million, or 87 cents a share, on sales of $475.2 million. The company has 189 stores.
