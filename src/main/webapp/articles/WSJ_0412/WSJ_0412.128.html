Business Brief -- Pacific Enterprises: Gas Subsidiary Must Credit Its Customers $13.5 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/12/90

California regulators said they will require that Pacific Enterprises' Southern California Gas Co. unit credit customers $13.5 million of a $24.2 million gain on the 1987 sale of the company's downtown Los Angeles headquarters. The company said it hasn't decided whether to appeal the ruling. The gas company had asked for permission to keep the entire gain; regulators with the California Public Utilities Commission had challenged the need for the sale and argued that the capital gain should be used to offset more costly headquarters the gas company will occupy.

Frederick John, senior vice president of Pacific Enterprises, said that if the ruling isn't appealed or modified, the amount would be used to offset future increases for Southern California Gas's 4.5 million customers.
