Philadelphia Electric Sees Profit Cut if Bid On Rates Is Lowered

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/12/90

PHILADELPHIA -- Philadelphia Electric Co. said its annual earnings will be reduced nearly $1 a share if state regulators adopt a proposed $300 million reduction in the company's pending rate request. In 1989, the company earned $590 million, or $2.36 a share. Joseph F. Paquette Jr., chairman and chief executive officer, said the expected rate reduction would also require a one-time write-off of $1.20 a share during 1990 and a cut in the dividend rate. Directors are expected to review the dividend rate, currently $2.20 annually, April 23.

In composite New York Stock Exchange trading yesterday, Philadelphia Electric closed at $17, down 12.5 cents.

The Pennsylvania Public Utility Commission indicated during preliminary polling last month that it was proposing a rate increase of about $250 million, or less than half of Philadelphia Electric's $549 million request. Two administrative law judges earlier recommended a rate increase of only $117 million. A final decision is expected next Thursday.

Analyst Edward J. Tirello of Shearson Lehman Hutton said he estimates 1990 earnings of around $1.80 a share, based on a rate increase of $250 million. He said the estimate takes into account cost-cutting measures that Mr. Paquette is expected to announce soon. Mr. Tirello said he expects the annual dividend rate to be cut to about $1.45, which would reduce the yield to between 8% and 8.5%. The company declined to comment on the estimates.
