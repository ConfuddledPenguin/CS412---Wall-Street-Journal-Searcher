Letters to the Editor: Not-So-Quick Bucks

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/12/90

In your March 9 page-one article "Raider Referendum," you characterize USX Corp. stockholders who support Carl Ichan's efforts to split USX into separate companies as "quick-buck" investors. A review of the facts demonstrates that USX stock sells for about the same price today that it did nine years ago. To add insult to injury, earnings are 73% lower, book value is 72% lower, the dividend is 30% lower and long-term debt is twice as high. Just how long should stockholders wait before expecting reasonable returns on their investments? Donald S. Miller

Wilmington, Del.
