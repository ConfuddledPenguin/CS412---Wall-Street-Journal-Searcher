OTC Focus: Market Rises as Technology Stocks Gain; Apple Computer, Microsoft Are Higher

  • Author: Anne Newman and Craig S. Smith
  • Date: 04/12/90

Technology stocks led a slight gain in the over-the-counter market as investors selectively dipped their toes into quiet, pre-holiday trading. "The tech stocks are still hanging in there" after Tuesday's strength, said Bob McCready, an institutional trader with St. Louis-based A.G. Edwards & Sons.

The Nasdaq Composite Index closed up 1.63 at 433.53 for a 0.37% gain. Thus, it outstripped the Dow Jones Industrial Average, which slipped 1.35 to close at 2729.73, and the American Stock Exchange's market value index, down 1.98 to close at 357.54. Advancing OTC stocks beat decliners, 956 to 906, as volume rose to 133,820,400 shares from 129,504,900.

Leading the technology stocks, Apple Computer added 1 1/4 to 42 1/2. Apple's chairman and chief executive officer John Sculley gave a keynote address yesterday at a trade show in San Francisco that focused on the company's Macintosh personal computer hardware and software products. The company is expected to report third-quarter earnings next week.

Microsoft, which yesterday introduced a software product to help programmers boost productivity, rose 3 to 121. Intel, which is expected to report first-quarter results today, gained 1/4 to close at 42 3/4.

Investors tended to pick their shares carefully, said William R. Rothe, managing director for OTC trading for Alex. Brown & Sons in Baltimore. Most investors flush with cash are content to "just sit back and rifle shoot and take opportunities when they see them," investing in bargains and selling on bad news, Mr. Rothe said.

Jerry Mullins, head of OTC trading at Dean Witter Reynolds, saw retail buying outpace institutional buying at that brokerage as investors favored the buy side. But by the end of the day, E.E. "Buzzy" Geduld, president of Herzog Heine Geduld Inc., said he saw institutional buying "leaning toward the sell side."

Mr. McCready of A.G. Edwards predicts today's market will drift until the last hour of trading, when he suggests the market may bounce up as traders who sold shares short rush to cover their positions in advance of the three-day Easter weekend. Short sellers borrow shares and sell them, betting on a decline that will allow them to return the borrowed shares later at a profit.

MCI Communications closed off 3/8 at 36 1/8 after it was reported that chairman and chief executive William McGowan underwent exploratory chest surgery.

Disappointing earnings pressured shares of Vivigen Inc., off 2 at 19, and Home Savings & Loan, which fell 1 to close at 15 1/2.

Vivigen, a genetic testing laboratory based in Santa Fe, N.M., reported first quarter net income of 11 cents a share versus 15 cents in the year-ago quarter.

Comshare fell 2 1/4 to 36 1/4. The company said a lowered earnings forecast by a Chicago Corp. analyst was largely responsible for the drop. The analyst slashed a fiscal third-quarter earnings estimate for the company to about 40 cents a share from a previous estimate of 60 cents, according to Dow Jones Professional Investor Report.

Amid positive earnings stories, Caere added 2 to 24 1/4 after reporting first-quarter net income of 25 cents a share, up from 10 cents a year earlier.

Among bank stocks rising yesterday, University National Bank gained 4 to 32, Midlantic rose 5/8 to 16 1/2, BayBanks added 3/4 to close at 16 3/4, Northeast Bancorp added 1/2 to 32 1/2, and Society for Savings gained 5/8 to 12 1/4.

Laserscope slipped 7/8 to 15 3/8. Alex. Brown & Sons said this activity was related to the partial expiration of a lock-up agreement between the company and some major investors that restricted those investors from selling shares following Laserscope's November 1989 initial public offering.
