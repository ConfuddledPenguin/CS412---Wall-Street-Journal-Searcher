Letters to the Editor: Settling Old Scores Creates New Enmities

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/12/90

I was born and raised in Waldenburg, a city about 30 miles south of Lignitz, the town featured in your March 9 page-one article. When the war was over, the Russians and Poles came, killing many of the Germans living there and deporting the rest of us in cattle cars. During the fall of 1989, I had the opportunity to go back to my hometown. The decay you so eloquently describe was everywhere. My parents' house was only a shadow of its appearance in 1945. A friendly, elderly Pole showed me around, and he seemed to remember what the area had looked like when the new masters took over. Clearly, Germany's loss has not been Poland's gain. I now live in this country, and I do not wish to tell the Germans or the Poles how to settle their differences. However, not all the injustices in the world were perpetrated by the Germans, and settling scores by taking land from the vanquished and expelling the inhabitants of those lands does not appear to be a solid foundation for peace. I hope a lasting settlement of these thorny disputes can be reached within a peaceful Europe.

H.P. Pohlmann

Winfield, Ill.
