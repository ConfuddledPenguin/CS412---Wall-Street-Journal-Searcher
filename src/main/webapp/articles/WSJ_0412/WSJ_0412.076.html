Money Fund Yields Edged Up Slightly In the Latest Week --- By Georgette Jasen Staff Reporter of The Wall Street Journal

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/12/90

Yields on money-market mutual funds edged up in the latest week, remaining in the narrow range of the past three months. The average seven-day compound yield rose to 7.99% from 7.97%, according to IBC/Donoghue's Money Fund Report, a Holliston, Mass.-based newsletter. The yield has hovered near 8% since early this year. (Compound yields assume reinvestment of dividends.)

Assets of the 427 taxable funds tracked by Donoghue's slipped by $44.7 million to $383.6 billion.

Average maturity of the funds' investments, such things as Treasury bills, bank certificates of deposit and commercial paper (short-term corporate IOUs), remained at 45 days for the third consecutive week.

Maturities are considered a sign of which way portfolio managers think interest rates are headed, with longer maturities thought to signal declining rates and shorter maturities considered a sign of rising rates. Average maturities have been as short as 29 days and as long as 53 days in recent years.

"The {interest} rate outlook has stayed very flat," said Brenda Malizia Negus, editor of Donoghue's. "Federal Reserve policy doesn't seem to be changing in either direction." Yields on CDs and T-bills also were little changed in the latest week.

The average seven-day simple yield of the taxable funds rose to 7.69% from 7.67% in the week ended Tuesday, while the average 30-day simple yield moved to 7.68% from 7.67%. The average 30-day compound yield went to 7.98% from 7.97%.

Yields on tax-exempt money funds remained flat. The average seven-day compound yield was unchanged at 5.57%. That's equal to a taxable yield of 7.74% for investors in the 28% federal income tax bracket, and 8.31% for those in the 33% bracket.

Assets of the 220 tax-free funds tracked by Donoghue's rose by $1.5 billion, to $78.8 billion. Ms. Negus attributed the rise to tax concerns as the April 16 filing deadline approaches.

Average maturity of the tax-exempt funds remained at 46 days.
