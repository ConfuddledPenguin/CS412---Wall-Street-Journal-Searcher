Thrift Agency Is Cleared To Borrow $45.3 Billion

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/12/90

WASHINGTON -- The Resolution Trust Corp., anticipating a big speed-up in sales of sick savings and loans, received authorization to borrow up to $45.3 billion in working capital from the Treasury this quarter. The savings-and-loan bailout agency has said it plans to sell or liquidate 141 thrifts in the second quarter. It requires the cash to manage bad investments that buyers of sick thrifts don't take over, and also to pay off depositors at liquidated thrifts.

The working capital is to be repaid with revenue from selling the bad investments of insolvent savings and loans. The borrowing is expected to add to the total cost of the bailout only the amount of interest the agency must pay on the Treasury bonds, but that could total $28 billion over 30 years, according to government estimates.

In the first quarter, the agency received authority from its oversight board to spend $11 billion of the temporary capital. Most of that cash wasn't spent, as sales of problem thrifts slowed.
