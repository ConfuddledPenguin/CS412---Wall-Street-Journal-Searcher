Advisers to UAL Unions Reduce Their Fee Schedule

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 04/12/90

The UAL Corp. board cut the fees and expenses United Airlines union advisers would receive in connection with the unions' proposed $4.38 billion purchase of UAL, according to documents released yesterday. The unions' advisers had been criticized for sharing in fees totaling $58.7 million for their work on an earlier labor-management buy-out that collapsed for lack of financing.

Under a new, $4 billion offer March 19 negotiated with Coniston Partners, UAL's largest shareholder, the union advisers would have received $65 million if they were outbid, and $25 million even if they couldn't obtain financing a second time.

In negotiating a new merger agreement after the unions sweetened their bid last week, the board got the unions to reduce those amounts to $40 million for being outbid and $20 million if they can't obtain financing.
