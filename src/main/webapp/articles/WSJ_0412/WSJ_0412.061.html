East German Growth Rates

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/12/90

FRANKFURT, West Germany -- East Germany needs annual growth rates of 8.5% if the production gap between the two German states is to be closed by the year 2000, the East Berlin Institute for International Politics and Economics said. The economic research institute said in a report that the 8.5% growth in the gross national product also would be necessary to keep unemployment rates in East Germany from rising too sharply.

If the East German economy grows at a rate of only 5%, the institute said, then this will mean a loss of about 1.5 million jobs by 1995 and a loss of 2.6 million jobs by 2000, the institute said. The growth rates will depend to a large degree on the strength of capital investments.
