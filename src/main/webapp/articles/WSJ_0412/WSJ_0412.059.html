Who's News: Live Entertainment Names Patterson, Ex-Head of Retailer, to Two Top Posts

  • Author: David J. Jefferson
  • Date: 04/12/90

VAN NUYS, Calif. -- Live Entertainment Inc. named Wayne H. Patterson as chairman and chief executive officer, filling the posts formerly held by Jose Menendez, slain last August along with his wife, Kitty. Mr. Patterson, 43 years old, served more than three years at the helm of Pace Membership Warehouse Inc., and during his tenure sales and profits of the discount retailer more than doubled. Last November, Pace was sold for $323 million to K mart Corp., and since then, Mr. Patterson has served as K mart's executive vice president, specialty retailing. Prior to his work at Pace, Mr. Patterson spent more than 15 years with the accounting firm of Touche, Ross & Co.

Mr. Patterson's appointment brings a captain to a company that has drifted without a permanent chairman and chief executive since Mr. Menendez's death. "My sense is that a large part of my job will be leadership, being visible both internally and externally, and letting people know there's a full-time chairman," said Mr. Patterson, who was visiting Live's Lieberman Enterprises Inc. unit in Minneapolis yesterday.

Live distributes videocassettes and sells entertainment software.

Since the homicides, Mr. Menendez's former posts have been held on an interim basis by Peter M. Hoffman, president and chief executive of Carolco Pictures Inc., the entertainment concern that holds a 49% stake in Live.

The appointment of Mr. Patterson also comes at a time when the cloud of controversy surrounding Live following the homicides appears to have lifted.

Last month, police arrested the Menendez couple's two sons, Lyle, 22, and Erik, 19, and charged them with murdering their parents to gain access to the couple's $14 million estate. Both sons have pleaded innocent. The arrests quieted speculation that the brutal shotgun slayings might be linked to organized crime. Live had purchased two companies from individuals who have been identified by law-enforcement authorities as involved in organized crime.

In the week following the arrests, Live released the findings of an internal investigation, which found "no credible information" that would link the company's operations to the slayings. The report by the law firm Kaye, Scholer, Fierman, Hays & Handler also found "no evidence of criminal activity" at Live.

Mr. Patterson said he read the 220-page report and made inquiries to people familiar with Live as part of his "due diligence" review, and added, "I am convinced that the company has quality directors and quality people." As for the speculation of organized crime connections, he said "there was no basis for concern."

Since the arrest of the Menendez sons, Live's stock price has rebounded. In the months following the killings, the price sank to $13.25, but it has since risen to around $20 a share. Yesterday, Live shares rose 50 cents to close at $21.25 in national over-the-counter trading.

Mr. Patterson declined to lay out his specific plans for Live, saying he first wants "to really go through the business and understand it in a definitive way." But he did say of the company's existing operations that "it's likely we're going to stay in those businesses and let them grow."

He added that Live has "progressed admirably in Jose's absence," something he attributes to the "can-do attitude" of employees.
