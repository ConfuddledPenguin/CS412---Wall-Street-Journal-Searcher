Letters to the Editor: Go Pick on Someone Your Own Size

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/21/90

I am a fan of New Kids on the Block, and I think that your July 23 page-one article about them was unfair. You did not mention the older fans, like me and my girlfriends. I'm 13, and I still have to have my mommy take me to a concert. Maybe you only have to be six or seven years old to know this, but aren't articles supposed to be written unbiased? I don't know about you, but I don't think calling their bubblegum music (according to you) "horrid fluff" and saying, "At least some older children reject the New Kids as kid's stuff" sounds like it's written from a neutral point of view.

Alexis Rich

Simi Valley, Calif.

---

You stated that New Kids concerts "look more like day camp than a rock concert." I am 15 years old and a big fan of the New Kids. I am disturbed that New Kids fans are stereotyped as little children that have to bring their parents to the concerts. The New Kids are so down to earth, easy to relate to and great performers that "kids" any age should be represented as New Kids fans, including me.

Pam Sanderson

Cary, N.C.

---

As a mother of two pre-teen fans of the New Kids on the Block, I have no sympathy for Mrs. Flores, who is unable to afford a "decent dress" for herself after spending a small fortune on New Kid paraphernalia for her daughter. Poor little Ivory, hasn't she ever heard the word "no"?

Susan L. Santos

Duncannon, Pa.

---

I feel the article is totally discriminating against New Kids on the Block. Though you did quote some music critics, you only quoted the ones who feel negatively toward them. Never did you mention that the New Kids were labeled the "Top Pop Group of 1989" by Billboard or that their second album went platinum seven times. Instead, you wrote that some feel they are the "beltless slacks of the entertainment field" and "a hormonal response rather than music per se."

Donnie, Danny, Jordan, Jon and Joe encourage kids to work hard for what they believe in and always be themselves. They attend "Say No to Drugs" benefits and give a portion of the proceeds from their telephone hotline to the United Cerebral Palsy Foundation. Never did you mention any of this.

Allison Beasley

Baxley, Ga.

---

You made it sound as if they didn't have any fans over the age of 10, which just isn't true. I am 17 years old, and my friends and I enjoy listening to them very much. We attended their concert, on July 1, at the Alpine Valley Music Theatre, and saw people of many ages.

I will agree with you that the majority of their fans are pre-teens, which I think is good because they are influencing these kids at a early age to stand up for themselves and say "No" to drugs. Since drugs are reaching kids at a younger age, this is a very important lesson to learn.

Trista Wieczorek

Reedsburg, Wis.
