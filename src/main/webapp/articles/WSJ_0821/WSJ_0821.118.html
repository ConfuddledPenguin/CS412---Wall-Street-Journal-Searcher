Trump Reaches Accord With Taj Subcontractors

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/21/90

ATLANTIC CITY, N.J. -- Donald J. Trump reached a compromise with about 60 subcontractors who are owed about $70 million for their work on the Taj Mahal casino here. A pact was signed by Mr. Trump and the subcontractors just in time for the New Jersey Casino Control Commission to rule on Mr. Trump's $65 million bank bailout today without the threat of an immediate default judgment against Mr. Trump hanging over the proceedings. The contractors, many of whom said they had employed extra personnel to help Mr. Trump open the Taj by April 2, 1990, had threatened to force Mr. Trump to seek bankruptcy-law protection if they weren't paid. Mr. Trump hasn't paid them because the casino hasn't generated as much cash as expected.

Terms of the deal are believed to include a deferral of almost all the claims against Mr. Trump for at least a week. After that, the contractors are scheduled to get about 20 cents on the dollar, or about $14 million, with future payments deferred, said one person familiar with the terms of the agreement. It isn't clear where Mr. Trump will get the cash to make the payments or whether he can use any of the expected $65 million bank loan for that purpose.
