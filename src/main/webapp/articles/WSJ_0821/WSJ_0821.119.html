What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/21/90

BUDGET GAP PROJECTIONS for next year are widening by tens of billions of dollars because of higher defense spending and an economic slowdown resulting from the Mideast crisis, U.S. officials say. A record deficit of $250 billion to $300 billion is likely, which could force severe Gramm-Rudman spending cuts. Bush, meanwhile, said he will "oppose the defense budget slashers." ---

OPEC rejected a call by Saudi Arabia and Venezuela for an emergency meeting, leaving them free to pump more oil on their own. The group may still meet informally. Crude prices climbed further yesterday, and gasoline futures surged.

---

Interco agreed tentatively to give bondholders 95% of the troubled furniture and shoe company's common stock in a debt swap, plus the right to approve a new slate of directors.

---

Data General plans to lay off at least 2,000, or 17%, of its employees, company insiders said. Steep losses at the computer maker are prompting the latest round of cutbacks.

---

First Interstate Bancorp suspended its dividend reinvestment plan, which was intended to help rebuild the company's capital base.

---

Boeing received $650 million in firm aircraft orders from All Nippon Airways. The Japanese carrier also took out new options to buy $3 billion more of the Seattle firm's jets.

---

RJR Nabisco's recent $2.25 billion loan is getting a cool reception from the regional and foreign banks asked to take on some of the debt. The smaller banks want more of the sizable fees paid to the original lenders.

---

Blue-chip stock prices rose in thin trading, but smaller issues didn't fare as well. The Dow Jones industrials rose 11.64 points. Bond prices were flat, while the dollar ended mixed.

---

Some individual investors are optimistic about the outlook for stocks, showing a confidence not common among the market's professionals.

---

Northrop's accord in February to plead guilty to faking test data called for the U.S. to drop its criminal investigation of at least 11 other areas.

---

Soybean oil futures traders are reporting rumors that one firm is trying to corner the market, a year after authorities acted to block a suspected bid to dominate the soybean market.

---

Volvo unveiled a new top model, as Swedish car makers prepare for Japanese competition in Europe.

---

The Cirrus and Plus networks of automated tellers agreed to give each other's customers access to their machines. But individual banks may opt not to participate in the plan.

---

Denison Mines posted an $84.9 million second-quarter loss, after the Toronto company wrote down half of its oil and gas assets and provided for the cost of reducing its work force.
