Data-Design Labs Had Wider Loss in Quarter Despite Rise in Sales

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/21/90

RANCHO CUCAMONGA, Calif. -- Data-Design Laboratories Inc., whose net loss widened in the latest quarter, said it might have to redeem some debentures at a premium unless its tangible net-worth position improves. In the fourth quarter ended June 30, Data-Design's net loss nearly doubled, to $2.5 million, or 37 cents a share, from $1.7 million, or 26 cents a share, a year earlier. Sales rose 2.5%, to $23.5 million, compared with $22.9 million in 1989's fourth quarter.

Data-Design said it is likely that its tangible net worth on Sept. 30 will be below $28 million, the level specified in its agreement with holders of its 8.5% debentures. If the same thing happens again Dec. 31, the company said, it will have to redeem 10% of the debentures then outstanding at a possible premium.

Alan Steel, Data-Design's chief financial officer, said the company is hopeful about meeting the $28 million target by year end. Still, "we're talking to our major bondholders about loosening the requirement to give us a little more leeway," he said.

For the full year, the company's net loss narrowed, to $2.9 million, or 44 cents a share, compared with $12.2 million, or $1.82 a share, last year. Sales increased 1.6%, to $91.2 million from $89.8 million.

Data-Design makes printed circuit boards for the computer, communications and instrumentation industries.
