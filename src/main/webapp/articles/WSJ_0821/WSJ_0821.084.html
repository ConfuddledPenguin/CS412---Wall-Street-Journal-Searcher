U.S. Rejects Union Plan For Eastern, Continental

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 08/21/90

An attempt to have Eastern Airlines and its former sister airline, Continental, declared a single carrier for labor bargaining fizzled last week, more than two years after it began. The parent of the two airlines, Continental Airlines Holdings Inc., ultimately won in its attempt to have the move dropped, but only because it lost control of Eastern four months ago. In April, a bankruptcy judge ousted Continental Holdings -- previously known as Texas Air Corp. -- and appointed a trustee to run Eastern. Continental Holdings retains control of Continental Airlines.

The National Mediation Board, the federal agency that oversees airline labor relations, cited the trustee appointment as the reason for the ruling last Friday.

The mediation board said, however, it would let the unions refile their requests if the bankruptcy court approves a reorganization plan for Eastern that involves any form of control by Continental Holdings.

The case began in 1988, when Eastern's three unions asked the board to have Eastern and Continental declared one carrier for labor purposes. It was the kickoff of a bruising battle between Eastern and its unions.
