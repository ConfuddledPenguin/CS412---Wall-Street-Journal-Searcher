Business Brief -- OMI Corp.: Public Securities Considered For Foreign Joint Ventures

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/21/90

OMI Corp., an international bulk shipping company, said it is considering whether to issue publicly traded securities for its international joint venture activities. The company said a decision will not be reached before the end of the year, because of the complexity of such a transaction. Company officials couldn't be reached to elaborate.

Analysts think New York-based OMI considers its stock undervalued and hopes to get a greater value for its foreign operations. "There are times when the parts are worth more than the whole but it is not recognized by investors," says Luella White-Price of Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette Securities.

In 1989, OMI had revenue of $32.9 million from foreign operations compared with $160 million for its domestic activities. However, foreign operating profit was $21.3 million compared with $13.7 million for domestic shipping operations. OMI's net income nearly doubled to $27.5 million, or $1.01 a share, from $14.1 million, or 45 cents a share. The year-earlier per-share figure included a 25-cent reduction because of an accounting change on dry-dock expenses.

OMI maintains approximately a 50% interest in a number of operations, including Rubicon Tankers Ltd. and Mosaic Alliance Corp.

OMI shares closed yesterday at $7, down 75 cents, in American Stock Exchange composite trading.
