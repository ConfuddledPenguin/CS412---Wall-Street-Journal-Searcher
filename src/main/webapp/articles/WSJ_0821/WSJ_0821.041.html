International: Lloyd's of London Reduces Its Rates On Gulf Shipping

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 08/21/90

LONDON -- Lloyd's of London Underwriters Association reduced the recommended marine hull insurance rates for the Persian Gulf. A spokesman for the insurance firm said it cut recommended rates because there hadn't yet been any serious incidents in the Gulf. "But I'm sure if there are" any incidents in the region, the rates will increase again, he added.

The rate for "hull and machinery" insurance for vessels making calls at ports on the Persian Gulf coast of Saudi Arabia, excluding the neutral zone between Kuwait and Saudi Arabia, are now quoted at 0.75% of the insured value for every seven days' cover, Lloyd's said. That rate is a reduction from the previous level of 1%.

Similarly, a 0.75% rate now applies to vessels making calls to Iranian ports in the northern Persian Gulf, reduced from 1% previously.

Rates for ports on the coast of Iraq, Kuwait and the neutral zone are still "held covered," which means they must be negotiated on a voyage-by-voyage basis.

Rates for vessels calling at other Persian Gulf ports and at Saudi Arabian Red Sea coast ports are 0.20% of the insured value, compared with the previous rate of 0.25%.

The cost of insurance to the Gulf of Aqaba is unchanged at 0.25% of the insured value. All rates quoted are for seven days' cover, Lloyd's said.
