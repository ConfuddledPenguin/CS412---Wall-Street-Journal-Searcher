Credit Ratings

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/21/90

NEVADA POWER Co. (Las Vegas, Nev.) -- Moody's Investors Service Inc. said it lowered its ratings on about $260 million of this utility's debt. The rating concern said Nevada Power probably will have to borrow heavily over the next five years to fund a major expansion program, and it cited the utility's recent rate decreases. Moody's downgraded its ratings on Nevada Power's first-mortgage bonds and secured pollution-control bonds to Baa-1 from single-A-2, unsecured pollution-control bonds to Baa-2 from single-A-3, and commercial paper to prime-2 from prime-1. Richard Hyte, Nevada Power's chief financial officer, said the utility's explosive customer growth is straining its cash flow. "Funding for heavy construction requirements and increasing customer service costs will need to come, in part, from future rate increases," he said. Nevada Power said its capital outlays increased to $120 million last year from $50 million three years ago. It said its capital budget probably will increase 38% this year, and will average $200 million for each of the next three years.
