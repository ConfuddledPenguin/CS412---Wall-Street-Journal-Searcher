United Telecom Acts To Revamp Sprint's Sales and Service Line

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/21/90

WESTWOOD, Kan. -- United Telecommunications Inc. moved to reorganize the sales and service operation of its troubled long-distance telephone unit, US Sprint Communications Co. The revamping, which United Telecom said will cut 500 administrative jobs while adding "hundreds" of sales positions, comes on the heels of a dismal second-quarter showing by Sprint, the nation's third-largest long-distance provider.

Sprint posted a $42 million operating loss for the period, leading to a 55% profit drop for United Telecom and providing a nasty shock for Wall Street analysts, who had expected a much smaller decline. United Telecom stock plunged 23% on the news to $29.50 a share and recently bottomed out in the $23 to $25 range.

As part of a previously announced plan to cut 1,000 jobs, Sprint said it will refocus its National Markets operation, cutting three of its five marketing and service groups. The customers served by the three discontinued groups, which handled accounts generating monthly billings between $2,000 and $10,000, will be split between the two remaining groups. Those two groups, based in Atlanta and Kansas City, Mo., previously handled Fortune 1000 accounts and residential customers.

Separately, William Esrey, United Telecom's chairman and chief executive officer, said in an interview that despite the recent decline in share price, the company isn't considering buying back any of its stock.

Without naming any specific targets, Mr. Esrey said United Telecom wants to retain its ability to use the stock in place of cash in making acquisitions.

A company spokesman said no one, either from within the company or from the investment community, has been lobbying for a buy-back.
