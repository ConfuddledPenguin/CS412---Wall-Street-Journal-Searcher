Con Edison Says Power Fully Restored in New York

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/21/90

NEW YORK -- Consolidated Edison Co. of New York said that it restored power to all of Manhattan's financial district, a week after a fire knocked out service Aug. 13. Power is being drawn from three substations that serve areas adjacent to the Brooklyn Bridge substation damaged in the fire.

"It's a smaller pool (of power)," acknowledged a company spokeman, "but there's still an adequate reserve." He said that the 50-block area was "nowhere near" drawing off all available power.

Con Edison said that only 10 customers remained without power because the utility's crews have been unable to gain access to their buildings to reconnect them. Many firms in the area had been using diesel generators during the emergency.
