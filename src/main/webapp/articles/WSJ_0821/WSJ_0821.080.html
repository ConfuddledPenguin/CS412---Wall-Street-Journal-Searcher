Operation PUSH Keeps Up Pressure of Boycott on Nike

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/21/90

Operation PUSH, the Chicago-based civil-rights group, declined to end its national boycott of Nike Inc. products despite the company's announcement Friday of new affirmative-action goals. The Beaverton, Ore., athletic apparel company's new goals include naming a minority to the Nike board, increasing the number of non-white department heads by 10% within 12 months and naming a minority vice president within 24 months.

Rev. Tyrone Crider, director of PUSH, called Nike's goals "inadequate." He repeated PUSH's demand that Nike use minority-owned banks, advertising agencies and other service companies.

"The boycott and blackout campaign against Nike has not ended. In fact, our efforts have intensified," he said.

Operation PUSH has called for the boycott because it says that Nike gets about 30% of its $2.4 billion of annual revenue from black customers, but invests little in the black community and has no high-level black employees.

Nike says it gets closer to 15% of its revenue from blacks. It has defended its policies and has alleged that PUSH has financial connections with rival athletic wear company Reebok International Ltd. PUSH has denied the allegation.

In over-the-counter trading yesterday, Nike shares closed at $73.25, down $2.25.
