The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report. MORTGAGES

American Housing Trust VII -- $293 million of mortgage-backed certificates, to be issued by the Department of Veterans Affairs via First Boston Corp. and backed by vendee loans. The deal, called a proportional coupon allocation structure, is designed with two separate security structures -- one single-class and one with multiple classes -- with a range of average maturities. As investor demand increases or decreases during the marketing period for the single-class certificates, the proportion of par amount in each of the two structures will be adjusted up or down acccordingly. Details of the planned offering weren't immediately available. This is the seventh in a series of securitized offerings by the Veterans Affairs Department since 1988. Vendee loans are loans issued to finance the sale of VA-owned properties acquired as a result of VA loan defaults. The VA originates approximately $50 million to $70 million of such loans each month.

EUROBONDS

Bank of Montreal (Canada) -- Seven billion yen of 9% Eurobonds due Sept. 13, 1991, priced at 100 7/8 via Nippon Credit International Ltd. Redemption linked to the Japanese Nikkei Stock Index. Fees 7/8.
