Seven Distributors Sued for Selling Toys U.S. Considers Unsafe

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/21/90

WASHINGTON -- Seven major toy distributors were sued by the Justice Department and the Consumer Product Safety Commission for selling imported toys the government considered hazardous. The suits are the result of a yearlong investigation conducted by the commission and the U.S. Customs Service that found the companies sold imported toys that failed to meet federal health and safety standards. The items included rattles and other toys with small parts that could pose a choking risk to small children, as well as toys coated with lead-based paint.

Although most of the product labels recommended the toys for children over three years of age, commission officials said the design of the toys proved they were actually intended for younger children. The commission said there were no known injuries or deaths resulting from the toys, which were imported mostly from Taiwan.

The chief executives of the companies also were named in the suits.

The suits seek injunctions against the companies to stop the sale of the items. A commission spokesman said the action also is designed to encourage U.S. companies to require the same quality-control standards of foreign products as they do of U.S.-produced goods.

The companies named in the suits are Toys "R" Us, Paramus, N.J.; Child World Inc., Avon, Mass.; Lionel Leisure Inc., Philadelphia; Value Merchants Inc., Milwaukee; Illco Toy Co. U.S.A., New York; Beachcombers International Inc., Fort Myers, Fla.; Division Sales Inc. and an affiliated partnership, Justen Products, both of Itasca, Ill.

Michael Goldstein, vice chairman of Toys "R" Us, said the suit "is without merit. Our safety record is excellent."

Mr. Goldstein said he did not know which specific toys were mentioned in the suit and he declined to speculate as to why the suit was being brought at this time. He called the suit a "waste of taxpayers' money," but added that he was confident the company would prevail.

The other companies couldn't immediately be reached for comment.

Over the past year, several companies have agreed to voluntarily recall certain products.
