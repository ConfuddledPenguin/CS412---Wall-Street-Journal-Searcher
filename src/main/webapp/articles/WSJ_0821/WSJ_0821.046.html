Letters to the Editor: A Different Tune

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/21/90

My, aren't the Ukrainians brave, now that the modern world has forgotten their role in World War II (page one, July 17). Armed with bouquets of flowers, they greeted the invading Nazis. They remind me of the obnoxious, badly behaved child who petulantly craves constant attention. I refer you to the book "Mila 18," wherein they were called the "nightingales" because they sang and betrayed anyone to the powers that be in concentration camps. Sonia A. Boyar

Dearborn, Mich.
