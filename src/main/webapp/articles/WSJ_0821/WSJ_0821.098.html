Business Brief -- Computer Associates: Buy-Back of as Much as 8% Of Common Stock Planned

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/21/90

Computer Associates International Inc. said it will buy back as many as 15 million shares, or about 8% of its common stock, on the open market. A disappointing revenue and earnings forecast July 12 knocked nearly 40% off the giant software company's indicated value. Yesterday, Computer Associates stock rose 75 cents in composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange, to close at $7.125 -- still 56.8% below its July 11 closing price of $16.50.

The Garden City, N.Y., company's president, Anthony Wang, said he considered the company's shares a good value. "We've done many software acquisitions in years past, and very seldom have we had the chance to buy a company at less than one times revenue," he said.

At its closing price Friday, Computer Associates had an indicated market value slightly below its revenue of $1.3 billion for the fiscal year ended March 31.

Computer Associates, once a high-flier in the computer industry, hit the wall a year ago because of trouble handling a large acquisition. In recent months, the company had reported that those problems were behind it and restored some of its credibility.
