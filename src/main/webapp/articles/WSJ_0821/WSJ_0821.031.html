Seabrook Nuclear Plant Is Running at Full Power

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/21/90

The controversial Seabrook, N.H., nuclear power plant, long at the center of the battle between opponents and supporters of nuclear power, is up and running at full power. The $6.6 billion plant was completed four years ago, 14 years after construction began, but it sat idle while various groups challenged the adequacy of evacuation plans in the event of an accident at the plant. It received a full-power license from the Nuclear Regulatory Commission earlier this year.

The plant, owned by 11 New England utilities, had been tested at the 100% power level in recent weeks. It started full-power operations on Sunday and will produce 1,150 megawatts of electricity -- enough to supply one million homes, Seabrook officials said.

The prolonged battle over Seabrook caused substantial write-offs for several of its owners and eventually led to the bankruptcy-protection filing by its lead owner, Public Service Co. of New Hampshire. The utility couldn't service the debt it took on to finance its Seabrook stake.

Several legal challenges over the adequacy of evacuation plans remain, and the Nuclear Regulatory Commission is conducting an inquiry into its own procedures in licensing the plant. But analysts believe it is highly unlikely that the plant's license will be revoked.
