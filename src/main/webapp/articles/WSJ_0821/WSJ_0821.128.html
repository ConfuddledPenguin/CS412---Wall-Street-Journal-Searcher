Union Members Urge Triton Shareholders To Reject Merger Plan

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/21/90

SAN DIEGO -- A small group of union employees involved in labor contract negotiations with one of the subsidiaries of Triton Group Ltd. are urging Triton shareholders to reject a proposed merger with Intermark Inc. The merger is unfair to Triton shareholders and would hurt union members by weakening Triton and its subsidiaries, said Richard Zell, secretary-treasurer of United Food & Commercial Workers Local 1119 in the San Francisco area.

Under the merger agreement, Triton holders would receive 1.4 shares of Intermark common stock for each share of Triton common. Intermark, a San Diego-based holding company that already owns a 51.6% stake in Triton, would be the surviving entity.

Roughly 300 United Food union members are employed by Liquor Barn, a retail chain with 65 stores in California. Only 11 are members of Local 1119, Mr. Zell said. Triton, also based in San Diego, acquired a two-thirds interest in Liquor Barn in October.

In recent weeks, contract talks between Liquor Barn and its food union employees have stalled. Mr. Zell said that Liquor Barn management has proposed "a long list of takeaways" and "is trying to deprive members of union representation." The workers' contract expired last month.

Mitchell Woodbury, vice president and corporate counsel for Triton and Intermark, said that Triton "has been bargaining in good faith." The merger proposal is "fair, just and equitable" to shareholders of both companies, he said.

Mr. Woodbury said Triton has rejected requests by the union for the company's shareholder lists "as not being made in compliance with applicable law." Local 1119 purchased advertising space in this newspaper to urge shareholders to vote against the merger. Shareholders will vote on the proposal at Triton's annual meeting Aug. 30.
