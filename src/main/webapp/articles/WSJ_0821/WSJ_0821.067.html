Letters to the Editor: A Royal Huntationer Recalls the Glory Days

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/21/90

I read with interest your July 19 page-one article on TV fan clubs. Fan clubs centering on a particular series are not a recent phenomenon, nor are those which are bizarre or narrowly focused. As an undergraduate at Princeton in the early '70s, I was a charter member of just such a fan club. The Royal Huntation Society of Princeton University (its full name) comprised a small group of hard-core devotees. The members were devoted enough, in fact, to secure official recognition from the university as an extracurricular activity.

What did we do? We watched old episodes of "Sea Hunt" on late night TV and counted the number of times Lloyd Bridges would say "Huh." (Such as, "So you think those air tanks were deliberately poisoned, huh?") We counted at least 500 huh's in one semester.

Great, Huh?

Steven B. Krodman

Trumbull, Conn.
