Business Brief -- Jacor Communications Inc.: Debt Restructuring Is Set; Extended Deadline Sought

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/21/90

Jacor Communications Inc. said it reached a debt restructuring accord with its bank lenders but still faces an Oct. 15 deadline to raise $15 million in fresh equity. Under terms of the new accord, Jacor has met all previously extended interest payments, the company said. In addition, Jacor, which owns 13 radio stations in eight cities as well as other media properties, said it reduced the principal of its bank credit outstanding by $3.5 million.

As part of the new lending accord, Jacor noted that its banks extended to Oct. 15 or later their waiver of a provision requiring the company to raise $15 million in fresh equity. Jacor does not expect to meet the Oct. 15 deadline and said it is seeking a further extension. It noted, however, that "there can be no assurance that a further extension will be granted."

Moreover, the Cincinnati-based company noted that as a condition of the agreement, Jacor agreed to continue to defer indefinitely certain payments on subordinated indebtedness and warrants and cash dividends on its capital stock. Jacor said it has not yet obtained from the holders of these obligations a consent to such deferral. Failure to obtain consent will result in a default.
