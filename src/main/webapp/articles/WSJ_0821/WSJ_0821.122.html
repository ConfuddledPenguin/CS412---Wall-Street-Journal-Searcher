Feldman Is Sentenced To Prison, Ordered To Pay BankAmerica

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/21/90

LOS ANGELES -- A federal court judge sentenced David Arnold Feldman to 15 years in prison and ordered him to pay restitution of $70.7 million to BankAmerica Corp., the biggest loser in a $144 million mortgage investment scheme. Mr. Feldman, 50 years old, was convicted June 9 of three counts of mail fraud and was acquitted of two other counts. Five counts were dismissed during the trial for failure of proof. Mr. Feldman was taken into custody yesterday after the sentencing.

"A notice of appeal of the conviction and the sentence will be filed" today, said Rodney Perlman, a Los Angeles attorney representing Mr. Feldman. Judge Dickran Tevrizian "exceeded his authority" in making the restitution order, the attorney asserted.

Two other principal figures in the mortgage fraud pleaded guilty earlier this year to reduced charges of mail fraud. They are Kent Borden Rogers, 50, of Huntington Beach, Calif., and George Charles Ash Jr., 45, of Alta Loma, Calif. The three men were indicted last year for scheming to sell what proved to be practically worthless mortgage loans to 20 banks and savings and loan associations.

The men promised high interest rates and assured investors that their investments were protected by independent underwriting policies, among other safeguards. Most of the loans later went into default, and Bank of America, as the trustee for most of the mortgage securities pools, repurchased them and took $95 million in charges against the 1984 earnings of its parent, BankAmerica Corp.
