Baroid Corp.'s Board Backs Restructuring Through a Spinoff

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/21/90

HOUSTON -- Baroid Corp., controlled by Dallas financier Harold Simmons, said its board approved a previously announced plan to separate its petroleum services businesses and metals and mining businesses into two publicly traded companies in a tax-free spinoff. Under the restructuring, the company said it will distribute to current shareholders all shares of common stock in its subsidiary, New Baroid Corp., which includes its petroleum services businesses. Shareholders will receive one share of New Baroid Corp. common for each share of the company's common stock held on a record date to be established. The company will then adopt a new name and New Baroid Corp. will change its name to Baroid Corp., the company said.

Baroid also said that after the restructuring its operations will consist primarily of Titanium Metals Corp., its titanium metals business, and its bentonite mining business.

Robert J. Leidich, Baroid's vice president and general counsel, said the company is undertaking the spinoff to "separate two distinct lines of businesses."

The company said the plan is subject to several conditions, including New Baroid's ability to complete negotiations with a group of commercial banks for a $200 million credit facility. New Baroid plans to distribute $100 million of the proceeds of the credit facility to the company prior to the stock distribution and will use about $50 million of the remaining proceeds to repay debts.

The plan, which isn't subject to approval by shareholders, is expected to be completed by early October, the company said.

Baroid itself was created in 1988 when it was spun off from NL Industries, also controlled by Mr. Simmons through his Valhi Inc. holding company.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading, Baroid closed at $14.25, down 37.5 cents.
