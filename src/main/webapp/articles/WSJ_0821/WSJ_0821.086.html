The President's Vacation Is Raising Some Eyebrows

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/21/90

Although an overwhelming 72% of Americans back President Bush's actions in the deserts of the Middle East, they're less sure about his conduct in Kennebunkport, Maine. Only 53% approved of his taking time to vacation at his seacoast home just now, with 39% disapproving, in a poll taken over the weekend before the hostage drama escalated. Television news has juxtaposed Mr. Bush playing golf with poignant interviews with relatives of Americans presumably being held hostage.

"I was saddened to see . . . Mr. Bush playing golf and going fishing after having sent U.S. troops overseas," said Lawrence Naumoff, 44, a Chapel Hill, N.C., novelist. "It was a vision of the old-fashioned ugly American who doesn't reflect on what his actions have caused."

However, Edith Seibert, a housewife from Hermitage, Pa., said, "His mind is much clearer up there {in Maine} without everybody in Washington beating on him to make a military strike."

Mr. Bush broke off his vacation for the second time yesterday to meet with advisers in Washington, then returned to Maine. The White House says that this is a working vacation and that, thanks to sophisticated communications, Mr. Bush can manage the nation's affairs as well in Maine as in Washington.
