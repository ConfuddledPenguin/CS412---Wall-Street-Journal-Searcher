Letters to the Editor: Our Town Is Ours, Not the One Portrayed

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/21/90

Thornton Wilder's "Our Town" is a delightful piece of fiction . . . and so was your recent July 30 page-one article about Peterborough, N.H. Your selection of sources resulted in a distinctly one-sided portrayal of the town's relationship with Minebea Co., owner of the New Hampshire Ball Bearing plant here.

The tone of the piece, in fact, reflected a preconceived bias against small towns and their adjustments to changes in management of local companies. Further, it was written in the unfortunate tradition of Japan-bashing articles that have become all too popular in recent years.

The article implied NHBB is strictly bottom-line-oriented, having little interest in the community, but this is not borne out by the facts. The company has been actively involved in community and regional affairs for years.

Certainly there are adjustments to be made under the new ownership, but change is inevitable with the passage of time and evolving business conditions.

Let's keep Thornton Wilder's work as great entertainment and let the Peterboroughs of the nation find their way of dealing with new management in their vital industries.

Dan Malachuk

Peterborough Chamber of Commerce

Public Relations Director

Peterborough, N.H.

---

Peterborough's business community is much more than just Minebea's NH Ball Bearings subsidiary. There is certainly a small-town flavor that natives and more recent arrivals cherish. I've enjoyed this community since moving here in 1975 as founding editor and co-owner of my first major magazine, BYTE. Numerous businesses large and small find this kind of small town quite acceptable.

Our town's publishing industry alone employs hundreds of people. Our economy is much more balanced than a single manufacturing company recently acquired by new owners. Peterborough, N.H., co-exists with and trades quite well with a global world of commerce.

Carl Helmers

President

Helmers Publishing Inc.

Peterborough, N.H.
