Canada Railroad Reopens As Another Nears Opening

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/21/90

MONTREAL -- One of Canada's two main East-West railroad lines reopened and the second was expected to reopen soon. Government-owned Canadian National Railways said freight began moving Sunday night on a segment of its transcontinental route in northern Ontario. The track had been blocked for a week by native Indian protesters who were seeking action on land claims and other issues.

Meanwhile, Canadian Pacific Ltd. said its CP Rail unit obtained a court injunction ordering a separate group of Indians to end a blockade on a segment of its main transcontinental route. A CP Rail spokesman said he couldn't say when the track would reopen but added that the railroad is confident the Indians will obey the injunction. The CP Rail blockade began last Thursday.
