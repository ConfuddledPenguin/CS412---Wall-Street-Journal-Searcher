Who's News: Campbell Soup Co.'s Argabright to Retire

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/21/90

CAMDEN, N.J. -- John W. Argabright, senior vice president of Campbell Soup Co. and president of its international division, decided to retire early. Mr. Argabright, who will turn 55 years old in October, joined Campbell in 1986 as a vice president and took charge of the international division. The group of businesses hasn't been a bright spot for the food products company in recent years.

In late May, Campbell put its least profitable foreign operations on the block, conceding that these businesses can't deliver adequate returns to shareholders. The move also is part of Campbell's companywide cost-cutting measures.

Among the businesses for sale are Campbell's British frozen vegetable and seafood lines of its Freshbake Foods unit and its Italian cookie company, D. Lazzaroni & Co., which has been plagued with production and distribution problems.

Mr. Argabright said his retirement will take effect when a successor is named. While he plans to spend more time with his family, he said, he also wants to return to his "first love -- consumer marketing and advertising."
