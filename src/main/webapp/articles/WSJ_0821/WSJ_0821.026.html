Who's News: BKLA Picks Buchman To Succeed Weisstein In Two Top Positions

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/21/90

LOS ANGELES -- Mark E. Buchman was named president and chief executive officer of BKLA Bancorp and of its Bank of Los Angeles subsidiary, succeeding Gerald S. Weisstein, who has held those posts since 1985. Mr. Weisstein, 51 years old, said he was leaving because "my contract is at an end," and "it's my desire to get into a larger bank where the challenges might be a bit more expansive." During his tenure, the bank's assets grew to about $173 million from about $120 million, he said.

Mr. Buchman, 53, was president of the Government National Mortgage Association during the last year of the Reagan administration. After 20 years at Manufacturers Hanover Trust Co., he held the post of executive vice president of Union Bank in Los Angeles from 1982 to 1987.
