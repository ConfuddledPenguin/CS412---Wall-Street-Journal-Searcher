Arco Gasoline Price Freeze

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/21/90

LOS ANGELES -- Atlantic Richfield Co. said it will maintain its freeze on gasoline prices, but it is raising the price of diesel and jet fuel. The decision to continue Arco's gasoline price freeze, begun 13 days ago in the wake of President Bush's request that oil companies act responsibly in pricing, is being reviewed on a day-to-day basis, said George H. Babikian, president of Arco Products Co., the company's marketing and refining division.

A spokesman for Arco said the price of diesel fuel will climb, on average, to 76 cents a gallon from 70 cents. Jet fuel will increase to about 75 cents or 80 cents a gallon from 70 cents, depending on specific contract arrangements with customers.

"Arco is doing all it can to hold the line, but we cannot continue the freeze on diesel and jet fuel prices in the face of sharp differences in marketplace prices between Arco and its competitors," Mr. Babikian said in a statement. "In addition, we have been forced to augment our jet fuel supplies by buying on the spot market at high prices."

Meanwhile, at an average of about $1.16 a gallon in Los Angeles, Arco's regular unleaded gasoline is priced between five and 13 cents below the competition, the spokesman said. Accordingly, more than 100 Arco stations a day are running out of regular unleaded gasoline at some of their pumps.

Arco said it will continue "to encourage its dealers to show restraint in setting prices." The company added that it also is urging motorists to reduce gasoline consumption during "the current emergency."
