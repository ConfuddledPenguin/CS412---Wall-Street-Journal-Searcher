OTC Focus: Market Declines 4.90 to 388.59 Despite Gains of Listed Shares; Intel Falls 1

  • Author: Howard Hoffman Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 08/21/90

Over-the-counter stocks showed no strength, sliding lower all day as their listed counterparts crept higher. Traders reported frustration with the OTC market's weakness and their inability to explain its retrograde motion.

The Nasdaq Composite Index closed with a 4.90, or 1.25%, decline to 388.59. The New York Stock Exchange Composite Index added 0.23, or 0.13%. The Dow Jones Industrial Average climbed 11.64, or 0.44%.

"There's nothing nice to say about the OTC," says Steve Bliss, head of OTC trading for Mabon Nugent & Co.

Unlisted shares opened with a brief surge only to retreat for the rest of the day. It was the opposite story on the Big Board where major averages opened lower, rallied and finished higher on the day.

"The OTC just acted horrible right across the board," says Robert Antolini, head of OTC trading at Donaldson Lufkin & Jenrette Securities Corp.

Traders say buyers were scarce and selective. Volume fell to 115.5 million shares from 146.9 million shares Friday. Declining issues led advancers 1,231 to 693.

The OTC's unwillingness to follow the Big Board higher mystified traders.

Mabon Nugent's Mr. Bliss suggests investors were reluctant to place their money in the less liquid OTC market. He says those willing to buy despite the Middle East uncertainty probably felt safer in a more liquid market.

The day's most active issue was Intel, which lost 1 to 33 1/4 on 2.3 million shares.

Some of the OTC's biggest issues were battered in the all-day slide. Microsoft fell 4 3/4 to 55 1/2. Sun Microsystems dropped 1 1/4 to 26. Nike was 2 1/4 lower to 73 1/4. Reuters's American depositary receipts declined 2 5/8 to 46 3/4.

But some others managed to defy momentum and finish the day higher. Apple Computer added 1/4 to 36 3/4. MCI Communications rose 1/2 to 34 3/4.

Florida Employers Insurance shares were 4 3/8, or 58% higher, to 11 7/8 after the company said it signed a letter of intent to sell either its stock or its assets to American Employers Group Inc. for about $16 a share.

Spiegel's Class A shares were down 4, or 16%, to 16. The Dow Jones Professional Investor Report said Spiegel told analysts last week that sales were slow and customers gave its fall catalogue a cool reception. Spiegel Chief Financial Officer Alton M. Withers projected third quarter earnings would be flat at 21 cents a share.

Mack Trucks lost 1/4 to 6 1/2. The truck maker said late Friday that a revised forecast for the year projects losses of about $180 million compared to a loss of about $185 million for 1989. The company previously projected its 1990 loss at $130 million.

Millfeld Trading gained 7/8 to 13 1/2 after reported net income for its second quarter doubled compared with the period a year ago. The company also extended a warrant offer.

Radiation Systems also benefited from a positive earnings report. Fourth quarter net income jumped to 39 cents a share from 11 cents a year ago. Radiation Systems shares rose 3/8 to 10 3/8.
