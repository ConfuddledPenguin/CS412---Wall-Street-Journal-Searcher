International Brief -- Suedzucker AG: West German Sugar Firm's Fiscal '90 Profit Rose 48%

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/12/90

Suedzucker AG of West Germany, a sugar company, said profit for the year ended Feb. 28 rose 48% to 86.2 million marks ($52.4 million), or 40 marks a share, from 58.2 million marks, or 28.20 marks a share, a year earlier. Sales rose 33% to 3.6 billion marks from 2.7 billion marks. Suedzucker said its 1.35 billion-mark acquisition of the Belgian sugar company Raffinerie Tirlemontois S.A. last year, which made Suedzucker Europe's largest sugar producer, boosted sales in the last five months of fiscal 1990.

The company plans to raise its dividend 27% to 14 marks a share from 11 marks.

Analysts had criticized the high price paid for Tirlemontois, saying Suedzucker was primarily interested in acquiring the company's sugar production quotas. Each European Community country has a production quota, and the EC support price is well above the market level. Last November, Suedzucker officials conceded the price was high, citing the quotas. The company will eventually lower its holding in wholly owned Tirlemontois to about 60%.
