What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/12/90

THE U.S. WON assurances from six nations and the EC that they will agree to cut farm subsidies. But some Europeans at the economic summit claimed victory because the understanding doesn't call for the elimination of export subsidies, and U.S. aides said tough talks lie ahead. Meanwhile, the summit leaders agreed to follow different paths on the environment and aid to the Soviets. ---

U.S. banks were ordered to write off 20% of their $11.1 billion in loans to Brazil and an additional 20% of their $2.9 billion in Argentine portfolios. The action could force some banks, especially Citicorp, to boost reserves.

---

Stock prices rose sharply on positive second-quarter earnings and on bullish news for oil companies. The Dow Jones Industrial Average jumped 41.83 points to 2932.67. The dollar gained and bonds were flat.

---

Wall Street bond dealers deluged the Treasury with what amounted to bogus bids for seven-year notes to retaliate for a change in auction rules.

---

Five oil ministers from the Persian Gulf urged OPEC to freeze its output ceiling to try to raise its average crude price to at least $18 a barrel. Oil prices gained on the news, as did the shares of U.S. oil companies.

---

A grand jury indicted Edwin T. McBirney III, former owner of defunct Sunbelt Savings, in the second big indictment by U.S. thrift-fraud prosecutors in the past month.

---

Five senators violated ethics rules in the S&L scandal, a citizens group argued. Meanwhile, Bush voiced confidence in the integrity of his son, who faces a thrift-related probe.

---

PS New Mexico was approached by a small venture-capital firm about acquiring the utility. The move may flush out other interested buyers. PS New Mexico's stock rose $2 to $11.75.

---

The first factory for making computer chips with superconducting materials is to be unveiled today by Conductus, a tiny Silicon Valley firm.

---

Banks would be required to adhere to securities regulations if they market investment trusts to compete with mutual funds, the SEC announced.

---

First Executive is seeking to keep secret the full contents of a consulting report that the insurer said proved its financial strength and viability.

---

CBS Inc. posted a 16% increase in second-quarter profit. But the company said the prospect for continued growth this year is "uncertain."

---

The Chicago Merc will allow two traders convicted Monday on federal felony charges to keep trading for their own accounts pending a hearing.

---

Most U.S. voters believe Bush is breaking his no-tax pledge, but they don't much blame him for it and are resigned to a tax increase, according to a Wall Street Journal/NBC poll.

---

Hartmarx halved its dividend and took a $51 million after-tax charge. A sales slump has hurt the clothing firm.

---
