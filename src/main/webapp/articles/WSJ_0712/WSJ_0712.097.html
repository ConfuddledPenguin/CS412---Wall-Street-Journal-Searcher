Business Brief -- Inmac Corp.: Company to Reduce Staff At San Jose, Calif., Plant

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/12/90

Inmac Corp., Santa Clara, Calif., in an effort to stem operating costs, said it will reduce its work force at the company's San Jose, Calif., plant by 73 workers, about 60% of its work force, effective Aug. 31. Inmac, which makes a variety of products for use with computers,, has more than 1,300 employees in nine countries.

The San Jose plant, the company's only U.S.-based manufacturing site, will be kept open and used as the company's product testing center, with jobs for about 50 of its 120 employees, said a company spokesman. Three vice president positions have also been eliminated and their responsibilities combined into one position.

The reorganization will cost the company about $500,000, or three cents a share, charged to its operating costs for its fiscal fourth-quarter, ending July 28.

The company said it made the move because increased manufacturing capabilities at facilities in East Kilbridle, Scotland, and Tijuana, Mexico, along with outside vendors can adequately supply the company production needs.
