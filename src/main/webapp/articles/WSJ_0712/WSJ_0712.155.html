Magna International To Sell Motor Group To Siemens AG Unit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/12/90

TORONTO -- Magna International Inc. said it is in the final stages of negotiations to sell its automotive electric-motors division to a unit of Siemens AG of West Germany. The sale would represent Magna's first significant disposal of an operating unit since March, when the company announced plans to sell various assets as part of a program to restructure 1.09 billion Canadian dollars (U.S.$941.4 million) in debt.

Magna, an auto-parts manufacturer plagued by overcapacity, didn't disclose an asking price for the Maci Group division, a unit of Magna's Atoma International Inc. subsidiary. Some analysts estimate the unit could sell for about C$70 million.

aul Klapproth, a spokesman for Siemens Automotive LP, said "a number of points have to be resolved" before the deal is finalized. The transaction also would require Canadian regulatory approval.

Toronto-based Magna said the Maci Group, with five plants in Ontario and a distribution center in Michigan, generates annual sales of about C$120 million and has 1,200 employees.

Siemens Automotive, based in Troy, Mich., manufactures electronic components for automobiles out of facilities in Ontario and Virgina. Mr. Klapproth said the proposed acquisition would fit with Siemens Automotive's strategy of expanding in North America.
