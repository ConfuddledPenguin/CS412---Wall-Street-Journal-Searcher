Marketing & Media: Publisher Lang to Launch TV Network Airing Reruns

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/12/90

NEW YORK (AP) -- Magazine publisher Dale Lang said he plans to launch a television network this fall that will feature direct-response advertising programs and reruns of classic television shows. Mr. Lang, chairman of the newly created Star Television Network Inc., said 23 independent UHF stations -- reaching more than 13.7 million of the nation's 92 million TV-owning households -- have agreed to carry the service starting in September.

Stations that sign affiliation agreements will be given eight hours of television programs a day, consisting of movies, game shows and series like "Life of Riley," "The Invaders" and "Richard Diamond," Mr. Lang said.

In exchange for the programs, the stations must run at least four hours a day of direct-response programs, also called "infomercials." These are program-length commercials in which a marketer typically demonstrates a product and invites viewers to call in to order.

Mr. Lang said Star Television and the producers of the infomercials would share revenue from sales of the products or services with affiliate stations.

A spokeswoman for Mr. Lang couldn't say how much was being invested in the new network. The network will be transmitted to member stations via satellite.

Mr. Lang also heads Lang Communications, a New York-based media company that publishes Working Woman, Working Mother, Sassy and Success magazines.
