International: Venezuela Application For GATT Approved

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 07/12/90

GENEVA -- The ruling body of the General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade (GATT) gave the go-ahead to Venezuela's membership application yesterday. A trade official said that as a condition of joining the 96-nation trade forum, which sponsors agreements to liberalize world trade, Venezuela had agreed to limit import tariff levels to 50%, and to reduce them to 40% within two years.

Venezuela is the first member of the Organization of Petroleum Exporting Countries (OPEC) to seek GATT membership. The country derives 90% of its export income from oil, which is covered by OPEC's production regulations.

The trade official said GATT's regular council meeting also heard that agreement had been finalized on the opening of South Korea's markets to imported beef.

Under the agreement, Seoul has to import at least 58,000 tons of beef this year.
