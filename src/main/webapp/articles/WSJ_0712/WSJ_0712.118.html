Business Brief -- Kane-Miller Corp.: Firm Drops Plan to Seek Seats on Amrep's Board

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/12/90

Kane-Miller Corp. said it dropped a threatened proxy fight for representation on the board of Amrep Corp., a New York real-estate developer. In a filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission, Kane-Miller cited California developer Peter Bedford's recent decision to increase his stake in Amrep to 16.1% in explaining its decision not to seek seats on Amrep's board.

Kane-Miller, a Tarrytown, N.Y.-based food processing concern, owns 8.1% of Amrep.

An Amrep spokesman declined to comment on the Kane-Miller filing.

In its latest filing, Kane-Miller reiterated that it acquired its stake in Amrep for investment purposes, but that it would consider seeking control of the company.

In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange yesterday, Amrep closed unchanged at $8 a share.
