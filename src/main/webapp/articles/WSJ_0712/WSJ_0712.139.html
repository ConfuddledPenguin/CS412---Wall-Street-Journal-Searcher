Who's News: Three Directors Quit At Polifly Financial

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/12/90

NEW MILFORD, N.J. -- At Polifly Financial Corp., the torch may be passing to a new generation. Earlier this month, Polifly announced the appointment of 42-year-old Joseph S. Paparatto as its new president and chief executive officer. As president, he succeeds Gregg B. Van Dam, who had left the firm in March in connection with a consent agreement with federal regulators.

And yesterday, three directors resigned, including 72-year-old Chairman Frederick T. Muller, who had been acting chief executive officer prior to Mr. Paparatto's appointment. Mr. Muller said, "It is an opportune time for us to resign and enable Mr. Paparatto to nominate some individuals less senior in years."

Resigning with Mr. Muller were John R. Speich Jr., 99 years old next month, and Herbert Hoexter, 90.

Polifly, the parent of Polifly Savings & Loan Association, has reported heavy losses recently, the result of "high levels of non-performing assets caused by a deterioration in the New Jersey real estate market," according to the company. Among other problems, the company has suspended dividends and is the target of a civil class-action suit alleging securities-law violations.

Recently, Polifly Financial & Polifly Savings entered into the consent agreement restricting their activities. Any default on the agreement could result in legal action against the troubled thrift and its holding company.
