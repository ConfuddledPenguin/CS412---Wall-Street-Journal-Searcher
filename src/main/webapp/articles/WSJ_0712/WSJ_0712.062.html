Technology: Software Designer at Apple To Form Spinoff Company

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/12/90

CUPERTINO, Calif. -- An Apple Computer Inc. software whiz will quit to start a secretive spinoff company with two former Apple employees. Bill Atkinson, who designed Apple's Hypercard software, will leave Apple to form General Magic Inc. with former Apple employees Andy Hertzfeld and Marc Porat, Apple said. General Magic's founders and Apple won't say what the new company plans to make, except that it will develop a product that bears the trademark Personal Intelligent Communicator.

"They have an idea that's under development," said an Apple spokesman. "We can't tell you anything about it."

Whatever the product is, the ideas come from concepts created by an Apple development project code-named Paradigm, Apple said. Apple said it spun out the technologies because they don't fit the company's mainstream business. Apple will retain ties with the technology by becoming a minority investor and the largest corporate shareholder in General Magic. Apple Chairman John Sculley will serve on General Magic's board, and Apple has a license to make and market the spinoff's technologies, Apple said.

Mr. Hertzfeld was a chief developer of Apple's Macintosh computer, but left the company about five years ago. Mr. Porat, who left Apple to help start General Magic, was a high-level engineering manager overseeing the Paradigm project. Mr. Atkinson will maintain ties with Apple by becoming an Apple Fellow Emeritus.
