Business Brief -- Federal National Mortgage: Agency's 2nd-Quarter Net Grew 50% to $289.6 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/12/90

The Federal National Mortgage Association's net income increased 50% in the second quarter, to $289.6 million, or $1.10 per share, from $193 million, or 75 cents per share, a year ago. In the first six months, net was $566.2 million, or $2.15 a share, compared with $358.2 million, or $1.39 a share, a year earlier.

Roger Birk, Fannie Mae president and chief operating officer, attributed the second quarter gain to a $2.1 billion increase in the average investment balance, an increase of four basis points in the net interest margin and growth of $8.3 million in mortgage-backed security guaranty fees.

Mr. Birk also said the government-sponsored agency acquired 2,315 properties through foreclosure in the second quarter, down from 2,455 a year ago. At the same time, charge-offs to Fannie Mae's allowance for loan losses were $59.4 million, virtually unchanged from $59.1 million in the first quarter of 1990 and down from $71.4 million a year ago.
