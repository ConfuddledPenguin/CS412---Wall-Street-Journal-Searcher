Financing Business: Potash Sells Warrants Totaling $43.2 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/12/90

SASKATOON, Saskatchewan -- Potash Corp. of Saskatchewan said it sold about 50 million Canadian dollars (US$43.2 million) of warrants to a group of Toronto-based securities dealers. Potash said proceeds will be used to pay for its C$50 million acquisition of Saskterra Fertilizers Ltd. from Calgary, Alberta-based Husky Oil Ltd. Saskterra's main asset is a 40% stake in Saskatchewan's Allan potash mine, of which Potash owns 60%.

Under the issue, Potash said it sold about 3,670,000 special warrants at C$13.625 each to an underwriting group that includes Wood Gundy Inc., RBC Dominion Securities Inc. and Nesbitt Thomson Deacon Ltd. Each warrant entitles the holder to acquire one Potash common share, subject to certain terms and conditions. Potash has about 35 million common shares outstanding.

Saskterra and Husky Oil are affiliates of Calgary-based Nova Corp. of Alberta, a big pipeline, oil and gas, and petrochemical concern.

In Toronto Stock Exchange trading yesterday, Potash shares closed at C$14, down 50 Canadian cents.
