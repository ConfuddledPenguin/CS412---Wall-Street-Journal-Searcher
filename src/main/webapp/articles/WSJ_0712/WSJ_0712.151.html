Business Brief -- National Intergroup Inc.: Centaur Partners Files Suit Against Company's Directors

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/12/90

Centaur Partners Group, New York, said it has filed a lawsuit against National Intergroup Inc.'s directors for adopting an amendment to the company's shareholder rights plan. Centaur Partners, which owns 16.5% of National Intergroup, is waging a proxy fight against the Pittsburgh oil transportation and aluminum and steel fabrication concern.

The lawsuit, filed in New York state court, seeks compensatory and punitive damages from the directors. The amendment provides that National Intergroup's "poison pill" would take effect if any single shareholder acquired more than 10% of its common stock.

The poison pill previously took effect only after a shareholder acquired 20% or more of the company.

Last week, the Delaware Chancery Court ruled that the amendment wasn't valid because it was adopted without shareholder approval. National Intergroup has said it would appeal the court's ruling.

National Intergroup called the lawsuit without basis and said it would "seek sanctions" against Centaur Partners because of the "frivolous nature of the action."

The company said the amendment was necessary to protect shareholders from Centaur's attempt to acquire control of the company without paying a premium for control.
