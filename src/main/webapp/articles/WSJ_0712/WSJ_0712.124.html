MacMillan Bloedel To Idle a Pulp Mill

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/12/90

VANCOUVER, British Columbia -- MacMillan Bloedel Ltd., citing soft market conditions, said it will temporarily close its biggest pulp mill for for the third time since March. MacMillan Bloedel, British Columbia's largest forest products concern, said the planned two-week shutdown of its Harmac pulp mill, near Nanaimo, British Columbia, will begin Aug. 27 and will reduce its inventories of bleached pulp by about 16,000 metric tons. A metric ton is equal to about 2,204.62 pounds.

Last year, the mill produced 370,000 metric tons of market pulp, or about 75% of MacMillan Bloedel's total market pulp output. Market pulp is wood pulp that is sold on the open market to producers of paper and other products.

The shutdown will affect about 600 of Harmac's 1,300 employees, the company said, and it is encouraging workers to take their annual paid vacation during the closure.

MacMillan Bloedel is 49.9%-owned by Toronto-based Noranda Forest Inc., itself a unit of the Toronto resource giant Noranda Inc.
