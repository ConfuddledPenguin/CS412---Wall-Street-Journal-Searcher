Voters in Hoboken, N.J., Reject Real-Estate Project

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/12/90

HOBOKEN, N.J. -- Voters dealt a blow to development plans on this Hudson River waterfront city, when they rejected a $2 billion real-estate complex by the narrow margin of 4,418 to 4,406. A coalition of anti-development groups forced the referendum, following a Dec. 29 agreement between the Port Authority of New York and New Jersey and Hoboken officials to develop the waterfront. Opponents said the plan would cause congestion and destroy the city's small-town atmosphere.

As Hoboken's once-thriving port declined over the last quarter century, city officials had hoped to replace abandoned piers with a combination of offices and expensive condominiums.

Even as neighboring New Jersey waterfront towns grew, Hoboken's ambitious plans had a number of fits and starts, primarily because the city has gone through several administrations.

Among the issues still to be settled is the disposition of a $7 million payment the Port Authority made to the city of Hoboken early this year.

The referendum bars development for at least the next three years on the waterfront site.
