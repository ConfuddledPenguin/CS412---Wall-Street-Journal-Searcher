Technology: Semiconductor Industry's Leading Indicator Declines

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/12/90

SAN JOSE, Calif. -- The semiconductor industry's leading indicator dropped in June, reflecting seasonal factors and a surge of orders earlier in the year that skewed June's index. The Semiconductor Industry Association's so-called book-to-bill ratio for June fell to 1.06-to-1 from 1.14-to-1 in May. Both figures are preliminary. A book-to-bill rate of 1.06 means that for every $100 in products shipped, makers received $106 in new orders.

The lower index shows that "we're headed for a normal reasonable summer, not any kind of recession," said analyst Erik Jansen of Robertson Stephens & Co. Orders typically fall in mid-June, he said. And a rash of memory-chip orders in March translated into deliveries in June that drove the index down because of lower bookings in the month, he said.

The industry association late last year forecast a year of no growth or slightly negative growth and June's index is "right on target," said an association official.

June billings were $1.30 billion, 5% lower than the $1.36 billion posted in June 1989. Average monthly bookings for the three-month period ending in June were $1.27 billion, 1.3% higher than the year-earlier average bookings of $1.25 billion.
