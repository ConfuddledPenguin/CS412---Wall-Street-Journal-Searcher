OECD Consumer Prices

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/12/90

PARIS -- The rise in consumer prices in the Western industrialized countries slowed in May to the lowest monthly rate this year, the Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development said, reporting that the average increase for its 24 member countries was 0.5%, down from 0.7% in both March and April. Consumer price growth in the European Community was 0.4% in May, down from 0.9% in April, partly reflecting Britain's recent, so-called poll tax. Seasonal factors caused Japanese price growth to remain at the high 0.8% level recorded in April.

For the 12 months through May, the OECD inflation rate was unchanged from April at 6%. Excluding Turkey, where consumer prices rose 3.1% in May, the OECD year-to-year average was 4.6%.
