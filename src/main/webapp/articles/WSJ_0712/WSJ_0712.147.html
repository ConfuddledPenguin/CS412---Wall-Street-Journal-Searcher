International Brief -- Schering AG: German Concern Expects Higher Per-Share Earnings

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/12/90

Schering AG said it expects to boost its per-share earnings to 40 deutsche marks ($24.39) or 41 marks this year from 39 marks in 1989. The West Berlin-based pharmaceutical and chemical company added that the rise could be achieved despite the diluting impact of a 5% stock dividend and the exercise of stock options.

A spokeswoman said, however, Schering hasn't altered its previous forecast that net income for the year will about match 1989's 225 million marks. Schering had 5.85 billion marks in sales last year.

The discrepancy between the outlook for higher per-share earnings and flat profit can be explained by the fact that per-share earnings reported by West German companies aren't equivalent to profit.

Instead, they are calculated according to guidelines set by the German Association of Securities Analysts, which eliminate extraordinary items and allocations to reserves from per-share earnings. Profit data reported by West German concerns can be misleading because the companies usually make overly generous allocations to nontaxable reserves.
