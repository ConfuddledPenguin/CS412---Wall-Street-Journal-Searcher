International: A Third Bid to Privatize Mexico's Biggest Mine Fails

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/12/90

MEXICO CITY -- A bid to privatize Mexico's largest mine, Cia. Minera de Cananea S.A., has fallen apart for the third time in the past three years. A Superior Court judge rejected a $468 million bid by the Mexican construction concern Grupo ICA S.A. and its Canadian and West German partners. The court and Grupo ICA differed on whether the bidders would have to pay the Mexican sales tax on the transaction, which would have added about $70 million to the price.

The government will open bidding again next month for the copper mine, which is near the Arizona border.

One previous effort to sell the mine failed when a purchasing group's financing package collapsed, and another failed when bids were rejected by the government as too low.

The mine has been the center of political and labor strife.
