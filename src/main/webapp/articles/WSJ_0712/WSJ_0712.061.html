New Securities Issues

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/12/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATE

Sumitomo Bank International Finance N.V. -- $500 million of 9.55% guaranteed notes due July 15, 2000, priced at 99.972 to yield 9.554%. The noncallable notes were priced at a spread of 1.02 percentage points above the Treasury's 10-year note. Rated double-A-1 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and double-A by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Goldman, Sachs & Co.

Williams Cos. -- $150 million of 10 1/4% debentures due July 15, 2020, priced at par. The noncallable debentures were priced at 1.72 percentage points above the Treasury's 30-year bond. Rated Baa-3 by Moody's and triple-B by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by First Boston Corp.

MUNICIPAL

Phoenix, Ariz. -- the bulk of a $117.6 million issue of bonds, including $72.6 million of general obligation bonds, due July 1, 1991-2006, 2010 and 2014 and $45 million of street and highway user revenue bonds, Series 1990, due July 1, 1991-2006, 2009 and 2010, were seen won by a J.P. Morgan Securities Inc. group. The firm apparently won the $72.6 million of general obligation bonds with a bid setting the true interest cost at 6.95%. The bonds, rated double-A by Moody's and double-A-plus by S&P, were priced for reoffering to yield from 5.90% in 1991 to 7.05% in 2014. Current interest serial bonds are priced for reoffering to yield from 5.90% in 1991 to 7.00% in 2006. There are $12.3 million of term bonds due in 2010 that weren't formally reoffered. There are $8.3 million of 6% term bonds priced at 87.94 to yield 7.05% in 2014. The remainder of the deal, $45 million of street and highway user revenue bonds, were apparently won by a Clayton Brown & Associates Inc. group, with a bid setting the true interest cost at 7.08%. The bonds, rated single-A-1 by Moody's and double-A by S&P, were priced for reoffering to yield from 6.00% in 1991 to 7.20% in 2010. Current interest serial bonds are priced to yield from 6.00% in 1991 to 7.05% in 2006. There are $12.8 million of 6.50% term bonds priced at 93.80 to yield 7.10% in 2009. There are $4.7 million of 7 1/8% term bonds priced at 99.21 to yield 7.20% in 2010.
