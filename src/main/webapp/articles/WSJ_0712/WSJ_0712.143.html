Business Brief -- Safeway Inc.: Supermarket Firm Says Net Rose Threefold in Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/12/90

Safeway Inc., citing improved gross and operating profit, said net income rose threefold in the second quarter ended June 16. Net rose to $23.6 million, or 25 cents a share, from $7.7 million, or 10 cents a year ago. Sales increased 5% to $3.44 billion from $3.27 billion.

The Oakland, Calif.-based company, which owns a chain of supermarkets, said its operating profit margin was 3.85% of sales, compared with 3.24% a year earlier, while interest expenses declined. Gains in operating profit came from improved buying, inventory control and mix of products, said Safeway Vice President David Domeier. "Our ability to buy better has a big impact on the bottom line," he said, although he noted the net is a small percentage of sales.

Safeway's stock closed at $16.625, up 50 cents, in New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday.
