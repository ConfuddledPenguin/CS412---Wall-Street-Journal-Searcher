International Brief -- Granada Group PLC: Firm's Pretax Profit Fell 12% in the Fiscal First Half

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/12/90

Granada Group PLC reported a 12% slide in pretax profit for the six months to April 14 and said the slack demand that slowed the television and leisure group in the first half is unlikely to abate before the end of the year. Pretax profit totaled #63.3 million ($115.4 million), down from #71.6 million in the first half of the previous financial year and at the low end of analysts' expectations.

Profit after taxes and minority interests eased 7.7% to #41.7 million from #45.2 million.

With no extraordinary gains or losses in the period, net profit attributable to shareholders also stood at #41.7 million, less than half the year-earlier total of #86.1 million. Last year Granada reported a #40.9 million extraordinary gain, largely on the sale of its majority stake in Barranquilla Investments PLC.

The group also reported a 13% decline in sales to #763 million from #874 million. The slump had been forecasted by the company at the time of its annual meeting in March.
