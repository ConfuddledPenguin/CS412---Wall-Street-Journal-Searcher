Who's News: De Benedetti Wins Control Of Italy's Biggest Publisher

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 07/12/90

MILAN, Italy -- The financier Carlo De Benedetti regained operating control of Arnoldo Mondadori Editore S.p.A., when several of his aides and allies were appointed to pivotal management positions at the company, Italy's largest publisher. At the end of a five-hour board meeting, Mr. De Benedetti's chief aide, Corrado Passera, was appointed general manager of Mondadori. Mr. Passera said he will step down as general manager of Mr. De Benedetti's Cie. Industriale Riunite S.p.A.

Carlo Caracciolo, former president of Mondadori before he was unseated temporarily by the television entrepeneur Silvio Berlusconi, was appointed managing director.

The new appointments sealed Mr. De Benedetti's recovery, after a fierce eight-month battle with Mr. Berlusconi that traversed courtroom and boardroom. Mr. Berlusconi was unseated as Mondadori's chairman on June 29, and shareholders appointed Giacinto Spizzico, a representative of the Milan district court, to the post.

Yet there are still no signs of a negotiated settlement between Messrs. De Benedetti and Berlusconi, which most analysts maintain is essential for a lasting resolution of their conflict. Mr. Berlusconi still controls significant stakes in both Mondadori and its parent company, AME Finanziaria S.p.A. Without an accord, management of Mondadori could still be troubled, analysts said.
