U.S. Agency Finds Mexican Companies Are Dumping Cement

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/12/90

WASHINGTON -- The Commerce Department ruled that Mexican shipments of gray Portland cement and clinker are being dumped, or sold in the U.S. at less than their production costs or home-market prices. The department's final determination will result in stiff anti-dumping duties on imports from Cemex S.A., Mexico's largest cement producer, and other Mexican suppliers if the International Trade Commission affirms that such shipments are injuring U.S. producers. The ITC has to rule on the injury question within 45 days.

The Commerce Department found dumping margins of 57.96% on Cemex shipments, 53.26% on those from Apasco S.A. de C.V., 3.69% on Cementos Hidalgos S.C.L., and 57.66% on those from all other Mexican suppliers. These findings are likely to nudge overall cement prices higher in the Southwestern states, Florida and California, where the Mexican products have been in increasing demand.

The department acted on a petition from a group of cement producers in Arizona, New Mexico, Texas and Florida. The petitioners claim that rising Mexican shipments have depressed prices in the Southwest and displaced U.S. supplies. Gray Portland cement is widely used for public works and business construction. Clinker is an intermediate material that is ground into cement.

"Those are extremely large margins for a fungible commodity that's sold solely on the basis of price," said Joseph Dorn, a lawyer for the U.S. producers.

The Commerce Department estimated the value of last year's Mexican shipments at $111 million.
