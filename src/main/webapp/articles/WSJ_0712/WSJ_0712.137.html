Chemical Moving to Singapore

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/12/90

HONG KONG -- Chemical Bank's local branch announced it is winding up its foreign exchange operations and joining the growing ranks of financial institutions moving to Singapore. In a statement, the U.S. bank said the steps are intended "to improve the efficiency and profitability of its offices in Asia."

Douglas Short, general manager of the branch, said many of the bank's 20 foreign exchange staffers have been offered jobs in the Singapore branch.

Chemical, which has its chief Asian foreign exchange operations based in Tokyo, said the move is aimed at consolidating its non-Japanese regional treasury and trading operations.

It also announced plans to close its Taiwan branch in three to four months.
