Wages Rise 10% in Malaysia

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/12/90

KUALA LAMPUR, Malaysia -- Wages rose 10% in the second quarter from a year earlier as Malaysia's economic expansion continued to strain the labor market, according to the Malaysian Institute of Economic Research. In its quarterly survey on business conditions, the institute said 44% of roughly 500 businesses polled said they had raised their wages in the second quarter, compared with 35% a year earlier. With companies' production demands growing faster than the skilled labor pool, "more firms are now willing to provide training for workers. Other firms have resorted to personnel pinching by increasing wages," the institute said.

The institute, financed by the government but independently organized, said the competition for workers is stiff.

Official government estimates put Malaysia's unemployment rate at between 7.5% and 7.9% in 1989 and predict moderate improvement to about 7% this year. But analysts note the rate for skilled workers is much lower, and labor shortages have been reported in certain regions.
