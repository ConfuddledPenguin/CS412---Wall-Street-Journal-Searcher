REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: Living Outside the Law

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/12/90

Last year Iowa Senator Chuck Grassley shamed his colleagues into amending the Americans With Disabilities Act so they would be covered by the same anti-discrimination rules binding business. On Tuesday, the Senators retreated into hypocrisy as usual when they voted 63 to 26 to amend the ADA conference report to once again exempt the Senate. The same vote also exempted them from full coverage of the Civil Rights Bill. GOP Senator Warren Rudman claimed there was already a brief in-house rule that simply asks Members not to discriminate. Senator Grassley noted this doesn't give Senate employees recourse to the courts. "If a 150-word rule is good enough for us," he asked, "why don't we repeal the hundreds of pages of civil-rights law and make do with a simple rule?" No one replied, but by their action the Senators have shown they can't be bothered to live by the same rules they force the rest of us to follow.
