United Airlines Regains No. 1 Traffic Slot in June

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/12/90

CHICAGO -- UAL Corp. said its United Airlines unit regained the top position among airlines in June for revenue passenger miles. UAL had lost the No. 1 position to AMR Corp.'s American Airlines two years ago. Most of United's growth is coming from its Pacific routes, where traffic rose 35.6% in June from a year ago.

United's June revenue passenger miles increased 8.4%, to 6.91 billion, from 6.37 billion a year earlier. A revenue passenger mile is the revenue from one passenger flown one mile.

American still isn't far behind United. American said its June revenue passenger miles were 6.86 billion, compared with 6.88 billion last year, down 0.3%. An American spokesman attributed the decline to a drop in traffic on American's routes in the Caribbean and Mexico.

American recently started serving several countries in Latin America, which should increase its passenger traffic in the coming months.
