International: Banking Bill Sent to Senate

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/12/90

MEXICO CITY -- Mexico's lower house of Congress approved 286-28 and sent to the Senate a bill that would allow foreigners to own interests of as much as 30% in 18 commercial banks that are to be privatized. Only Mexican individual investors and financial holding companies could hold Series A stock, making up at least 51% of each bank's shares outstanding. Mexican corporations, institutional investment funds, individuals and financial holding companies could own Series B shares, limited to 49%. Foreigners would be eligible to hold Series C stock, with a 30% limit.

The bill also limits individual investors' holdings to 5% of each bank in most cases; stakes of as much as 10% would be allowed with special government consent.
