Big Board Specialists' Return on Capital Drops to Rate of 4.4%

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/12/90

NEW YORK -- Returns on capital for the 46 specialist firms at the New York Stock Exchange in the first quarter dropped to a 4.4% annual rate after taxes, the Big Board said. Specialists are firms that use their own capital to ensure an orderly market in certain assigned stocks for which they are responsible. Their job is to facilitate speedy trading, stepping in to buy shares when too many sell orders pile up and selling some when investors rush to buy.

First-quarter returns were down from an 8.6% annualized rate in the first quarter of 1989, but better than the negative 1.7% return posted for the fourth quarter.

The exchange said specialists' profits amounted to $9 million after taxes in the first quarter, down 57% from $21 million in the year-earlier period.

Meanwhile, the Big Board confirmed industry projections that its 342 member brokerage firms that deal with the public had a $486 million pretax loss in the first quarter, compared with a $200 million pretax profit a year earlier. The loss reflects $459 million in one-time retrenchment expenses at certain member broker-dealers, including Shearson Lehman Hutton Inc.

The exchange said about 59% of the 342 firms reporting for the quarter were profitable.

Member brokerage firms' expenses for the quarter rose 2.5% to $14 billion, their fourth-highest level ever. Revenue fell 2.5% to $13.52 billion, their seventh-highest level.
