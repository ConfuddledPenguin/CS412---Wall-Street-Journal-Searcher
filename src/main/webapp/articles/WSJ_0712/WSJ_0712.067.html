Money-Fund Assets Rose $4.7 Billion In Latest Period

  • Author: Georgette Jasen
  • Date: 07/12/90

Assets of money-market mutual funds rose by about $4.7 billion in the eight days ended Tuesday, reversing the outflows of the previous period. Assets of 430 taxable funds now stand at $379.2 billion, up from $374.6 billion on July 2, according to IBC/Donoghue's Money Fund Report. The Holliston, Mass.-based newsletter did its regular weekly survey a day early last week because of the July Fourth holiday, reporting a $4.3 billion drop in assets for the previous six-day period.

Yields on taxable funds dipped slightly in the latest period. The seven-day compound yield of the funds, which assumes reinvestment of dividends, slid to 7.95% from 7.97%, Donoghue's reported. Average yields have been at or just below 8% since early this year.

"People may just be parking their cash," said Martha Wittbrodt, editor of the newsletter. "The stock and bond markets have been rather choppy" and yields on certificates of deposit have softened in recent weeks, she noted.

Average maturity of money-fund investments, such things as commercial paper (short-term corporate IOUs), Treasury bills and bank CDs, shortened by two days to 43 days. Shorter maturities are often thought to indicate that portfolio managers expect interest rates to rise, because they permit funds to grab higher yields sooner. But the current average is well within the range of the last few months. Average maturity has been as short as 29 days and as long as 53 days in recent years.

The average seven-day simple yield of the taxable funds slipped to 7.65% from 7.68%. The average 30-day simple yield remained at 7.67%, while the average 30-day compound yield stayed at 7.97%.

Assets of tax-exempt funds rose by about $3.2 billion in the last eight days, Donoghue's said, while yields fell. Assets increased to $78.9 billion; the average seven-day compound yield of the 224 tax-free funds dropped to 5.30% from 5.46%. The latest yield is equivalent to 7.36% for an investor in the 28% tax bracket and 7.91% for someone paying 33%.

Average maturity for the tax-free funds lengthened to 49 days from 48.
