Business Brief -- Communications Transmission: Long-Distance Phone Firm Says It Restructured Debt

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/12/90

Communications Transmission Inc., an Austin, Texas-based provider of digital communications transmissions and network services, said it significantly restructured its debt. Prudential Insurance Co. of America, CTI's subordinated lender, was issued warrants to purchase convertible preferred stock that could increase its common equity to 68% from 23%, a CTI spokesman said. The preferred stock can be converted to about 15.8 million shares of common, in addition to the 11 million shares that had been outstanding.

The debt restructuring, the spokesman said, cured CTI's defaults in interest payments on subordinated debentures as well as similar defaults by its majority-owned subsidiary, ALC Communications Corp. The spokesman said CTI will be required to make "substantial" payments after 12 and 18 months but declined to specify the amount.

The restructuring also gives CTI, in exchange for certain contract concessions and bridge note modifications, preferred stock in ALC which, if converted, would increase CTI's ownership of ALC from about 55% to 64%.
