Union Exploration To Be Converted To Delaware Firm

  • Author: Rick Wartzman
  • Date: 06/12/90

LOS ANGELES -- Unocal Corp. said Union Exploration Partners Ltd., a publicly traded entity that it created five years ago to help fend off T. Boone Pickens Jr.'s hostile advances, will be converted from a Texas master limited partnership to a Delaware corporation, known as Unocal Exploration Corp. Unocal said it was making the switch, in part, because of recent tax law revisions. Unocal Chairman Richard Stegemeier also cited "the improving climate for investments in domestic oil and gas exploration and development." Unocal and its Union Oil Co. of California subsidiary hold about 95% of the outstanding units of Union Exploration Partners.

"A corporation is now generally a more efficient vehicle for capital accumulation and reinvestment than a publicly traded partnership," Mr. Stegemeier said. Earlier this year, Unocal announced a 22% boost in its annual budgeted spending for exploration and capital projects.

Union Oil, which acts as managing general partner of Union Exploration Partners, projected in a Securities and Exchange Commission filing that the change would be effective Aug. 1. It is expected that each outstanding Union Exploration Partners unit will be converted into a new common share of Unocal Exploration, which will be traded on the New York and Pacific stock exchanges.

Before that, on July 25, a special cash distribution will be made to Union Exploration Partners unitholders of record July 11. The pay-out will be equal to about 100% of the partnership's estimated distributable cash for the four months ended July 31. A Unocal spokesman said the company couldn't speculate on the amount.

Unocal said Unocal Exploration's board intends to declare quarterly common share cash dividends. It said the first dividend is likely to be 10 cents a share, payable Nov. 15 to shareholders of record Nov. 1.

In addition, Union Exploration Partners unit-holders will be entitled to an extra cash distribution of up to $3 per unit if, during pre-defined time periods, the average closing price of Unocal Explorations's common stock falls below $18.20 a share.

Eugene Nowak, oil analyst for Dean Witter Reynolds in New York, said the move should give Unocal "more flexibility." He said Unocal Exploration could become an acquisition vehicle for Unocal, which has shed much of its debt in recent years.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading, Union Exploration Partners units closed at $16, down $2.25, as investors faced the prospect of immediate reduced pay-outs. They have been pocketing from the partnership more than 43 cents a unit quarterly for the past year.

Unocal closed on the Big Board yesterday at $28.75, down 25 cents.

As of the end of last year, Union Exploration Partners controlled about 29% of Unocal's total U.S. proved reserves of crude oil and about 59% of its U.S. proved reserves of natural gas.
