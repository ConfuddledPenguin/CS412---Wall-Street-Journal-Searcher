Abreast of the Market: Industrial Average Climbs 30.19 As Blue Chips, Growth Issues Surge

  • Author: Craig Torres and David Wilson
  • Date: 06/12/90

NEW YORK -- Blue-chip and growth stocks advanced sharply yesterday as investors began their ritual of sprucing up portfolios before the quarter's end. The Dow Jones Industrial Average recovered nearly all of its 38.59-point net loss of last week, advancing 30.19 yesterday to 2892.57.

Stock-index arbitrage buy programs, especially in the Major Market Index, which mimics the industrial average, helped push up many blue-chip issues sharply. "There was an explosion in buy programs limited to 20 stocks," said Thomas Gallagher, head of Oppenheimer's block trading department.

Broader market measures lagged behind the industrial average. Standard & Poor's 500-stock index rose 2.92 to 361.63; the gain was equivalent to a 21-point rise in the Dow industrials. The New York Stock Exchange composite index went up 1.37 to 197.42 and the Dow Jones Equity Market index rose 2.51 to 336.89. Advancing issues outpaced decliners on the New York Stock Exchange, 918 to 563. Big Board volume totaled only 119,550,000 shares.

Although investors loaded up on blue chips such as Boeing and Chevron, and growth stocks such as Home Depot, they also sold some financial stocks they don't want to show on the books at quarter's end.

Boeing soared 3 1/2 to 58 1/2 and led the list of most-active Big Board issues, as 2.3 million shares changed hands. As expected, Korean Airlines made commitments for 23 of the company's 747-400 jumbo jets; the pact, valued at $4.8 billion, represents the third large order Boeing has received from Asia in recent weeks.

Among growth issues: Walt Disney jumped 3 1/4 to 126 5/8, Wal-Mart Stores rose 1 to 60 3/8, Gap Inc. gained 1 7/8 to 58 and Home Depot rallied 2 to 58 3/4.

Energy stocks, which were battered last week as oil prices fell sharply, rebounded on bargain hunting. Exxon gained 7/8 to 47 7/8, Chevron rose 1 1/8 to 70 5/8, Texaco went up 3/8 to 57 7/8, Amoco climbed 7/8 to 51 1/2 and Mobil gained 5/8 to 62. In the oil field-services group, Schlumberger rose 3/8 to 56 5/8, Halliburton climbed 1/2 to 45 7/8 and Baker Hughes added 7/8 to 27 7/8.

One exception was Unocal, which dropped 1/4 to 28 3/4. Union Exploration Partners, in which the company has a stake of about 95%, skidded 2 1/4 to 16 after disclosing plans to convert from a partnership form of ownership into a corporation.

Financial shares, which attracted strong buying interest in recent weeks, were sold by many investors who are forecasting a slower decline in interest rates than previously expected, according to one trader. Citicorp fell 1/2 to 23 1/2 on 1.1 million shares; Chase Manhattan declined 1/2 to 26 1/2. American Express, a stock in the Dow Jones Industrial Average, fell 1/8 to 30 5/8.

Dividend-related trading swelled volume in Occidental Petroleum, which rose 3/8 to 27 and ranked second on the most-active list at 1.6 million shares. The stock has a dividend yield of 9.3%; shareholders of record yesterday will get the company's next quarterly payout.

Environmental Systems fell 2 to 12 3/4. Arizona's governor asked state regulators to extend the period for public comments on the company's request for a permit to operate a hazardous-waste treatment plant.

HomeFed gained 1 1/4 to 33 5/8. Justin Mamis, a technical analyst at Gordon Capital, recommended the issue; last week, analysts at Montgomery Securities and Dean Witter Reynolds reiterated their buy recommendations.

Foxboro climbed 1 1/2 to 30. The company said it is exploring the possibility of a strategic alliance, which may involve the sale of an equity stake or the entire company.

H.H. Robertson added 5/8 to 11 7/8. USA Today reported that a group headed by New York investor Douglas Kass is expected to offer about $15 a share, or $100 million, for the company this week.

Manville slid 3/8 to 7 1/8. The chairman of the trust established by the company for the settlement of asbestos-related claims said the trust is exploring the possible sale of Manville stock as one of several alternatives for dealing with a cash shortfall.

Floating Point Systems remained unchanged at 1 3/4. The company signed a letter of intent to sell $14.5 million of a new series of convertible preferred stock to an institutional investor, which wasn't identified.

The American Stock Exchange Market Value Index rose 1.15 to 362.32. Volume totaled 9,360,000 shares.

Sierra Health Services went down 3/4 to 6 5/8. A jury ruled against the company in a class-action suit over alleged securities law violations and ordered that Sierra pay a $6.5 million settlement.
