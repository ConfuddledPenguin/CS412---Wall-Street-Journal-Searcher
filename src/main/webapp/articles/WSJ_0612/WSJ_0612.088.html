Monday's Markets: Stocks Gain, Bond Prices Slip Slightly --- Trading Is Desultory Ahead of Price Data; Dollar Ends Mixed

  • Author: Christopher Winans
  • Date: 06/12/90

NEW YORK -- After four days of declines from record highs, stock prices rebounded, but only on meager volume, as some investors ventured into blue chips to put idle cash back to work. The Dow Jones Industrial Average rose 30.19 to close at 2892.57.

Bond prices wound up only slightly lower than Friday's level in light trading, despite an early morning decline led by foreign selling. The dollar continued to hold its own, largely because of weakness in other currencies.

Activity in the stock and bond markets was muted in anticipation of crucial inflation indicators to be released this week. The producer price index is due Thursday and the consumer price index Friday. Investors will be looking for confirmation that inflation isn't nearly as bad as the numbers earlier this year indicated.

Lower inflation is good news for stock prices because subdued inflation can help company profits. Bond prices also benefit.

Meanwhile, both small and large investors seem to have taken last week's decline as a chance to move more cash into the stock market. But the market remains troublesome for some investors.

"We had a pullback last week, and now it's off to the races again," said Frank A. Cappiello Jr., president of San Francisco-based money manager McCullough, Andrews & Cappiello. Noting that trading was narrow, he said, "What worries us is the activity seems to be mainly in blue-chip and story stocks," or companies in unique and attractive situations.

Another bothersome element, Mr. Cappiello said, is the failure of the Dow Jones Utility Average to keep pace with the rest of the market. Yesterday, the industrial average rose a little more than 1%, while the utilities index gained 0.76 to close at 213.22, a gain of a less than 0.4%.

The utility average "has to start moving rapidly to a new high," he said. "Otherwise, the rationale for this market rise doesn't exist." Rising prices for utility stocks -- which like bonds are helped by the prospect of lower interest rates -- tend to indicate that investors believe interest rates are going down.

In major market action:

Stock prices rose in slow trading, with 119.6 million shares changing hands on the New York Stock Exchange, down from 142.6 million Friday. Advancing issues outnumbered decliners, 918-563.

Bond prices eased slightly. The yield on the Treasury's benchmark 30-year bond inched up to 8.44%.

The dollar ended mixed. In late New York trading, the currency was quoted at 1.6930 marks and 154.77 yen, compared with 1.6975 marks and 153.49 yen late Friday.
