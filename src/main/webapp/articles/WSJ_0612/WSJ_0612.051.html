Credit Ratings: Chase Manhattan Senior Debt Rating Is Cut by Moody's

  • Author: Douglas R. Sease
  • Date: 06/12/90

NEW YORK -- Moody's Investors Service Inc. said it reduced Chase Manhattan Corp.'s senior debt rating to Baa-2 from Baa-1 as a result of deterioration in the bank holding company's large commercial real estate portfolio. The rating agency said that although Chase's commercial real estate portfolio is diversified, it remains vulnerable to further deterioration in national markets. Moody's noted, however, that Chase has offset to some extent the deterioration of the real estate portfolio by "considerable capital building and business rationalization efforts."

The company's commercial paper rating of Prime-2 wasn't changed. But some debt issues of the bank subsidiary were also lowered, including the rating of long-term deposits, which was reduced to A-3 from A-2.

Moody's downgrading of Chase is the latest in a series of ratings reviews that have resulted in downgrades of several bank holding companies, including Citicorp. The reviews come on the heels of intensified regulatory scrutiny of real estate loans, especially in the Northeast, where the deteriorating real estate market has forced banks to add substantially to provisions for loan losses.

A Chase spokesman said "we are disappointed by the Moody's announcement, particularly since Moody's acknowledged our capital-building activities, which, over the last two years have added $1.3 billion to our common equity base, our diversified portfolios and revenue streams as well as significant undervalued assets and increased core funding."

Earlier, Standard & Poor's Corp. affirmed its A rating of Chase Manhattan's senior debt and its A plus rating for deposits.

Moody's announcement came after financial markets closed yesterday. Chase shares closed yesterday at $26.50, off 50 cents, in New York Stock Exchange composite trading.
