Letters to the Editor -- Dolphin-Safe Tuna: No Real Net Gain

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/12/90

Contrary to the claims made by Christopher Croft of Defenders of Wildlife (letter to the editor, May 17), the decision by Starkist, Bumblebee and Chicken of the Sea to purchase only "dolphin safe" tuna will indeed hurt America's tuna fishermen. Ironically, it will also result in more dolphins being killed -- a point that Mr. Croft chooses to ignore. Mr. Croft's letter also fails to acknowledge that U.S. fishermen have pioneered dolphin-safety techniques and technology. America's tuna fleet has a 99.4% dolphin-safety record -- three to four times better than foreign fishermen. Every day, American tuna fishermen jump into the water to rescue dolphins. Indeed, the U.S. fleet's annual dolphin kill represents only one-half of what is permitted under federal law. Federal observers are on board for every trip taken by an American tuna boat. Few foreign boats are equipped with the special safety netting that American fishermen invented; fewer still have observers on board.

Mr. Croft uses a very misleading statistic: dolphin killed per U.S. vessel. The number has gone up on a per-vessel basis, but only because there are fewer vessels with significantly higher capacity. The key is that the number of dolphin lost per ton of tuna has dropped dramatically, to just 0.186 per ton from 0.400 a decade ago.

The canners' decision will put the U.S. tuna fleet at a competitive disadvantage. By forcing us out of the tuna-rich Eastern Tropical Pacific (where tuna traditionally swim with dolphins), the "dolphin safe" labeling movement invites irresponsible foreign fishermen in -- a scenario that, according to the congressional testimony of the National Marine Fisheries Service, will result in greater dolphin mortality. The environmental movement has set its sights on the wrong target. American tuna fishermen are part of the solution to dolphin safety -- not part of the problem.

We invite all environmental organizations to join with the American Tunaboat Association in asking our government to negotiate new fishing treaties that require all fleets in the Eastern Tropical Pacific to operate under the same exacting standards as the U.S. fleet.

August Felando

President

American Tunaboat Association

San Diego
