British Air May Traffic Rises

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/12/90

NEW YORK -- British Airways said it flew 3.49 billion revenue passenger miles in May, a 13.4% increase from 3.08 billion in May 1989. A revenue passenger mile is one paying passenger flown one mile.

The carrier said its passenger load factor, or percentage of available seats filled, was 70.1%, compared with 68.5%.

In the two months ended May 31, the airline flew 6.87 billion revenue passenger miles, up 12.7% from 6.09 billion a year earlier. Load factor for the two months rose to 70.2% from 69%.
