Hong Kong Exports Decline

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/12/90

HONG KONG -- The value of Hong Kong's exports of locally made goods fell 3% in the first quarter and 1% in March from a year earlier, government figures show. Hong Kong import values rose 2% in the quarter and 6% in March.

Among re-exported items, the British colony's shipments of raw materials and semi-manufactured goods were down 5% in the quarter and 2% in the month, while re-exports of foodstuffs dropped 11% in the quarter and 13% in March. Re-exports of fuels, however, soared 175% during the month and 89% in the quarter, while those of consumer goods jumped 28% in the quarter and 36% in March.
