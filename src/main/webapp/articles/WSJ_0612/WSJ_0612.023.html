Who's News: Bidwell, Chairman of Chrysler Unit, Confirms He'll Depart by End of Year

  • Author: Melinda Grenier Guiles
  • Date: 06/12/90

HIGHLAND PARK, Mich. -- In an effort to cool rampant speculation about his future with Chrysler Corp., Bennett E. Bidwell, chairman of the Chrysler Motors unit, confirmed he plans to leave the No. 3 auto maker effective Jan. 1, 1991. Mr. Bidwell, who turns 63 next week, has hinted broadly since January that he would leave before age 65. He had indicated his departure could come early next year, but a statement issued yesterday after a story about his plans appeared in Automotive News was the first official confirmation of his intentions.

Those intentions could change, however; Mr. Bidwell said that if he's "really needed" at Chrysler, he's "prepared to alter" his plan.

Mr. Bidwell has already had to alter at least one plan regarding his role at Chrysler, where he is currently responsible for consumer affairs, service and parts, the Chrysler Canada unit and sales and marketing. Largely because of health reasons, Mr. Bidwell last year negotiated a roughly 40% reduction in his corporate responsibilities in exchange for an equal cut in pay. The new arrangement was supposed to take effect Jan. 1, 1990, but soon after it was negotiated Mr. Bidwell was given additional duties. The reduced workload never materialized, even though the pay cut remains in effect. In 1989, Mr. Bidwell received $579,279 in salary.

Mr. Bidwell is the fourth top Chrysler executive in as many weeks to announce a departure, but Wall Street didn't appear overly concerned with the news. In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Chrysler closed at $16.25, up 50 cents.

Gerald Greenwald, Chrysler's vice chairman and Mr. Iacocca's heir apparent, resigned abruptly late last month to lead a proposed employee buy-out of UAL Corp.'s United Airlines. Chrysler's treasurer has also said he will leave and its vice president of international operations resigned in mid-May to take a top job with Black & Decker Corp.

Last week, Lee A. Iacocca, Chrysler chairman and chief executive officer, said he will remain with Chrysler beyond the December 1991 expiration date of his current contract. He didn't specify further, but said one of the tasks he wants to complete before he leaves is to create a clear line of management succession.

Mr. Bidwell joined Chrysler in 1983 as executive vice president in charge of sales, marketing and public relations. He had been president and chief operating officer of Hertz Corp., a rental car concern, since 1981. Prior to that, he worked at Ford Motor Co. with Mr. Iacocca, ultimately rising to head of all sales operations for North America.
