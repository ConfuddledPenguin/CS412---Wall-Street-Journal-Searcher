International Brief -- Ing. C. Olivetti & Co.: Payment Systems Venture Set Up With French Firm

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/12/90

Ingenico S.A., a French electrical engineering company, said it formed a joint venture with a unit of Ing. C. Olivetti & Co. of Italy to develop and market electronic payment systems. The venture, Eurosix S.p.A., in which Ingenico and Olivetti's Sixcom will each have 50% stakes, intends to develop a new standard for the electronic payment markets that can be used for bank transactions, commerce and distribution networks. It will focus on systems using electronic teller or credit cards, and is expected to start operations in 1991, focusing on the banking market in Europe initially. Ingenico and Olivetti expect the joint unit's annual revenue to reach at least 110 billion lire ($88.1 million) between 1991 and 1994.

The partnership also may lead to cooperation in telecommunications and security. Ingenico said it wants to tap Olivetti's marketing and distribution acumen in foreign markets. The venture will be able to take advantage of the sales infrastructure established by Olivetti Networks & Systems in the countries where Olivetti is active, Ingenico said.
