LEISURE & ARTS: Time Off: Diversions and Excursions June 12-25

ART

National Gallery of Art, Washington, D.C.: "Old Master Drawings From the National Gallery of Scotland" -- Almost 90 drawings by Italian, Dutch and French artists such as Raphael, Piranesi, Van Dyck, Rembrandt, Poussin and Fragonard. Fourth Street at Constitution Avenue NW, June 24-Sept. 23. The show then travels to the Kimbell Art Museum in Fort Worth, Texas, Nov. 3, 1990-Jan. 13, 1991.

Museum of Science, Boston: "Soviet Space" -- More than 50 artifacts document the history of the Soviet space program, its current status and its future direction. Highlights include a full-scale model of Sputnik I, the first artificial satellite to orbit the Earth; Lunakhod 2, a lunar roving vehicle; components of Luna 24, the last unmanned probe to visit the moon; and a model of the Mir space station. The show was produced in cooperation with Glavkosmos, the Soviet civilian space agency. Science Park. Through Sept. 23.

DANCE

Mark Morris: The provocative expatriate choreographer and his Monnaie Dance Group give the home folks a chance to see what they've been up to in Brussels on this summer mini-tour. There'll be a different program at each stop. The Boston engagement, at the Emerson Majestic Theater (219 Tremont St., 617-492-7578) tonight-Sunday, offers his critically acclaimed "Love Song Waltzes"; the dates at New York's Brooklyn Academy of Music (30 Lafayette Ave., 718-636-4100), June 20-23, feature Morris's interpretation of Purcell's opera "Dido and Aeneas," with the choreographer dancing both the ill-fated Carthaginian queen and her mortal enemy, the Sorceress. The final stop, at the Jacob's Pillow Dance Festival (Route 20, Becket, Mass., 413-243-0745) June 26-30, marks the U.S. premiere of "Going Away Party."

FESTIVAL

JVC Jazz Festival: The annual Big Apple music marathon opens June 22 with a blast -- concerts by trumpeters Miles Davis and Dizzy Gillespie (Davis plays Avery Fisher Hall, while Gillespie sounds off at Carnegie Hall). For the next week, there'll be sounds to suit almost every jazz fan, at sites throughout the metropolitan area. Highlights include the dream double bill of Ray Charles and Roberta Flack (Avery Fisher Hall, June 24); the reunion of Brazilian vocalist Milton Nascimento and saxophonist Wayne Shorter (AFH, June 26); grande dame Ella Fitzgerald (AFH, June 29); a rare collaboration among guitarist Pat Metheny, Herbie Hancock, Jack DeJohnette and Dave Holland (Beacon Theatre, two shows, June 30); and a "Knitting Factory Goes Uptown" series spotlighting exciting young performers (Equitable Center, 787 Seventh Ave., 5 p.m. June 22-29). (212) 787-2020.

MUSIC

Opera Theatre of Saint Louis offers a rarely performed 1899 Dvorak piece, "The Devil and Kate," about a Bohemian princess who sets the peasants of her realm free. Richard Buckley conducts and the cast includes Joseph Evans as Jirka the shepard, Phyllis Pancella as the village girl Kate, and Alexandra Coku as the princess. June 14, 16, 20, 22 and 24. Also on the card for the season, which ends June 24, are a Colin Graham staging of Britten's "Peter Grimes" (June 16 and 23), Donizetti's "The Daughter of the Regiment" (June 13, 15, 19 and 21) and Mozart's "The Marriage of Figaro" (June 12, 17 and 23). Loretto-Hilton Center, 130 Edgar Road (at Big Bend) on the campus of Webster University. (314) 961-0644.

Johnny Clegg & Savuka: For the past decade this "white Zulu" and his multiracial ensembles have beaten the drum for freedom in South Africa. Currently, he's on tour as the opening act for Tracy Chapman, providing frenetic Zulu dancing and songs from his new album "Cruel, Crazy, Beautiful World" (Atlantic). Dates include: Clarkston, Mich. (Pine Knob Theater), June 15; Cuyahoga Falls, Ohio (Blossom Music Theater), June 16; Atlanta (Lakewood Amphitheater), June 19; Pittsburgh (Star Lake Amphitheater), June 22; Holmdel, N.J. (Garden State Arts Center), June 23; Philadelphia (Mann Music Center), June 24; New York (Beacon Theatre), June 26; Wantagh, N.Y. (Jones Beach Theater), June 28; Manchester, N.H. (Veteran's Memorial Park), June 30; Lenox, Mass. (Tanglewood), July 2.
