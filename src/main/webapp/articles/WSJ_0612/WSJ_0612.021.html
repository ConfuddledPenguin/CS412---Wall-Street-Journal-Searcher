Economy: U.S. Manufacturers' Profits Decline to 4.2% of Sales

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/12/90

WASHINGTON -- U.S. manufacturers' after-tax profits fell to an average 4.2% of sales in the first quarter from 4.4% in the fourth quarter, the Commerce Department reported. The slip marks further deterioration in profits, which a year earlier were at 5.7% of sales, according to the department.

Despite the softness, profits picked up in the durable goods industries, which have shown widespread weakness in recent quarters. Profits on durable goods -- products such as machinery, metals and home appliances expected to last more than three years -- totaled $11.67 billion in the first quarter. That was higher than the $8.82 billion in the fourth quarter but still significantly below the $15.94 billion posted for the first quarter of 1989.

Profits on nondurable goods fell to $16.24 billion in the first quarter from $19.82 billion in the fourth quarter and $21.91 billion a year earlier. Nondurable goods are items such as petroleum products, chemicals and food that have a usefulness of less than three years.

The annual rate of return on shareholders' equity fell to 10.9% in the first quarter from 11.3% the preceding quarter and 15.3% a year earlier.
