New Securities Issues

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/12/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATES

Consolidated Rail Corp. -- $550 million of 9 3/4% debentures due June 15, 2020, priced at 99 to yield 9.854%. The non-callable debentures were priced at a spread of 140 basis points above the Treasury's 30-year bond. Rated single-A-3 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and single-A by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Morgan Stanley & Co.

Pacific Gas & Electric Co. -- $150 million of 9.30% first and refunding mortgage bonds due July 1, 2023, via First Boston Corp., priced at 95.75 to yield 9.73%. The issue was priced at 127 basis points above the Treasury's 30-year bond. Rated single-A-1 by Moody's and single-A by S&P, the issue is noncallable for five years.

Circus Circus Enterprises Inc. -- $100 million of 10 5/8% senior subordinated notes due June 15, 1997, priced at 99 7/8 to yield 10.65%. The non-callable notes were priced at 212.5 basis points above the Treasury's seven-year note. Rated Ba-2 by Moody's and triple-B by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets.

MUNICIPAL

San Bernardino County, Calif. -- $85 million of 6 1/4% tax and revenue anticipation notes, apparently won by a Merrill Lynch Capital Markets group. The notes, rated MIG-1/SP-1-plus, were priced for reoffering to yield 5.9779%. The notes are due Aug. 1, 1991.

Fresno County, Calif. -- $50 million of 12-month 6 1/4% tax and revenue anticipation notes due July 1, 1991, apparently won by a Prudential-Bache Capital Funding group. The notes, rated MIG-1 by Moody's, were priced for reoffering to yield 5.80%.

MORTGAGES

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $300 million of Remic mortgage securities offered in 13 classes by Kidder, Peabody & Co. The offering, Series 1990-79, is backed by Fannie Mae 9 1/2% securities. Price and yield data weren't immediately available.

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $300 million of Remic mortgage securities offered in 20 classes by PaineWebber Inc. The offering, Series 1990-78, is backed by Fannie Mae 9% securities with a weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29.58 years. Among classes for which details were available, planned yield spreads ranged from 50 basis points above the rate on two-year Treasury securities on the issue's Class A to 155 basis points above 20-year Treasurys for its Class Z securities.

ASSET-BACKED

Marine Midland Bank -- $600 million of 8.95% securities backed by credit-card loans priced at 99 30/32 to yield 9.143% via First Boston Corp. The issue, through Marine Midland Credit Card Trust 1990-A, was priced at 75 basis points above the Treasury's three-year note. The offering has an expected average life of three years. The issue is expected to be rated triple-A by both S&P and Moody's because of the quality of the underlying Visa and MasterCard loans and a letter of credit from Union Bank of Switzerland backing 11% of the issue. A First Boston official said the deal was sold out.

EUROBONDS

Volvo Group Finance Europe B.V. (Sweden) -- $100 million of 8.96% Eurobonds due initially June 18, 1991, extendible until June 18, 2000, priced at par, via Merrill Lynch International Ltd. Fungible with $150 million offering launched in 1985 under the name Volvo Capital B.V. due initially June 18, 1990, and extendible until June 18, 2000. Put and call option. No fees.

Minebea Co. (Japan) -- 23 billion yen of floating-rate notes due June 21, 1995, priced at 100.10, via Nomura International Ltd. Coupon 0.40 point below long-term prime rate. Fees 0.40.

Electric Power Development Corp. (Japan) -- 100 million European currency units of 10 1/2% Eurobonds due July 18, 1995, priced at 101 5/8 to yield 10.56% less fees, via IBJ International Ltd. Guarantee by government of Japan. Fees 1 7/8.

World Bank (agency) -- 200 million Swiss francs of 7 1/8% public bonds due July 5, 2000, priced at 102 via Credit Suisse. Fees undisclosed.

Johnson & Johnson (U.S.) -- 150 million Swiss francs of 7% privately placed notes due July 11, 1994, priced at 101 5/8, via Union Bank of Switzerland. Fees 1.38.

Electrolux AB (Sweden) -- 70 million Swiss francs of 8% promissory notes (Shuldscheindarlehen) due July 2, 1992, priced at 101, via Credit Suisse. Fees undisclosed.

Credit Local de France (France) -- 50 million ECUs of Eurobonds in two tranches due July 13, 1995, via Societe Generale. Redemption linked to West German DAX stock index. Amount of each tranche disclosed on payment date, July 13. Coupon: zero on Tranche A, 4 3/4% on Tranche B.
