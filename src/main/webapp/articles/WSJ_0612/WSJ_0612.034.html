International: Nationalization Is Sought For French Nuclear Firm

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/12/90

PARIS -- French President Francois Mitterrand has approved a plan under which French government-owned concerns will try to take majority control of Framatome S.A., effectively nationalizing France's nuclear power-plant builder, presidential aides said. The decision reflects the continued willingness of France's Socialist-led government to get involved in industrial disputes when it believes the national interest is at stake. In this case, France is more than 70%-dependent on nuclear power for its electricity, the highest dependence rate in the world. The government wants to ensure it controls the main decisions at Framatome.

If the nationalization plan works, it would foil a plan by France's large private-sector maker of electrical machinery, telephone equipment and trains, Cie. Generale d'Electricite, which holds 40% of Framatome, to gain majority control. CGE said in March it intended to buy the 12% held by the construction company Dumez S.A., on which CGE has first refusal rights. The president seeks to block that. Until now, the government considered Framatome's holdings balanced. The state holds 45%, personnel the remaining 3%. Officials fear that CGE's purchase plan would disrupt that balance.

The president's plan calls for government-owned concerns, possibly including the bank Credit Lyonnais and the nuclear fuel-processing concern Cogema S.A., to wind up with about 51%.
