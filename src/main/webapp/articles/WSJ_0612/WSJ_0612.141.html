Texas American's Plan for Liquidation Is Approved by Court

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/12/90

FORT WORTH, Texas -- A federal bankruptcy court approved the liquidation plan proposed by Texas American Bancshares Inc., to become effective July 16 barring any creditors' appeals. Texas American, whose 24 bank subsidiaries were seized by federal regulators last summer, is the first major Texas bank holding company to have a bankruptcy plan confirmed by the courts.

The confirmation order fixes June 12 as the date of ownership of Texas American's notes and debentures for the purpose of making distributions. Although creditors have until June 17 to appeal the plan, a bank spokesman said no appeals are anticipated. Bank stockholders will not receive a distribution under the plan.

A Texas American spokesman said the company has about $46 million in cash and between $105 million and $108 million in outstanding claims, but that the initial distribution will be limited to about $17 million, pending resolution of disputed claims.

All of Texas American's remaining assets, consisting primarily of real estate, gas and oil leases, and employee loans, will be held in liquidating trusts once the plan becomes effective. Matthew Metcalfe of Mobile, Ala., will continue as liquidating trustee.
