Triton Group Posts Loss of $12.8 Million For Its First Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/12/90

LA JOLLA, Calif. -- Triton Group Ltd. posted a fiscal first-quarter loss of $12.8 million, or $1.01 a share, and said that it expects a net loss for the full year ending Jan. 31, 1991. In the year-ago quarter, Triton had a loss of $730,000, or seven cents a share. Revenue in the quarter ended April 30 rose 70% to $72.3 million from $42.6 million, mainly reflecting the acquisition of a majority interest in liquor retailer Liquor Barn Inc. in October.

Triton said its widened loss reflects higher interest expense and amortization of acquisition costs for Liquor Barn and restaurant chain Western Sizzlin Inc., in which Triton acquired a majority stake in 1988, as well as a markdown of certain security investments. Triton said its $140 million investment in Fuqua Industries Inc., a consumer-products and services concern, returned less than 5% in the quarter on an annualized basis.

"Except for the nonrecurring investment write-downs, our operating results . . . were close to plan," said Charles R. Scott, chairman and chief executive officer. "We knew when (the) Western Sizzlin and Liquor Barn (stakes) were acquired that interest costs and amortization would result in losses during the building phase."

Triton, a diversified holding company, said that its other two majority-owned businesses are operating profitably, although "the effects of the softening economy are beginning to appear." The first-quarter operating performance of Fuqua, in which the company holds a 24% stake, improved.

"We believe that most of Triton's bad news is over, but because of the new acquisitions, we do not expect the company to be profitable at the bottom line for fiscal 1991," Mr. Scott said. Intermark Inc., also based in La Jolla, owns 52% of Triton's stock and the two firms recently agreed to merge, pending various approvals.
