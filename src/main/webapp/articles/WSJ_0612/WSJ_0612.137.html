Business Brief -- General Motors Corp.: Hughes Air Unit Suspension Is Removed by Air Force

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/12/90

Hughes Aircraft Co. said the Air Force removed its suspension that had temporarily barred certain operations of Hughes missile-systems group from receiving new government contracts. The Air Force suspended the company last month in the wake of Hughes's guilty plea to charges of improperly using two classified Pentagon budget-planning documents in 1984. Hughes, a unit of General Motors Corp., agreed to pay $3.6 million in criminal and civil penalties as part of the plea agreement.

The temporary suspension, which covered engineering, research and certain other operations at the Canoga Park, Calif., facility of the company's missile-making group, was in effect while the Air Force reviewed Hughes plan to re-establish itself as a "responsible contractor."

The Air Force's decision came despite assertions by prosecutors that Hughes officials went to greater lengths than those at almost any other company to carry out the document-swapping scheme.
