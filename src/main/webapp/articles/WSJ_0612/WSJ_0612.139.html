Meret to Go Private Through Stock Split

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/12/90

COLUMBUS, Ohio -- Meret Inc. said it plans a reverse stock split that will transform the retail consulting and design concern into a privately held company. Under the plan, each 2,500 existing shares would be converted to one new share. Holders owning fewer than 2,500 shares, or a number not divisible by 2,500, would receive 30 cents a share in cash for each current share that otherwise would become a fractional share.

The company has 10.2 million shares outstanding, owned by about 1,600 shareholders. Officers and directors control 46% of the stock.

In over-the-counter trading yesterday, Meret shares closed at 25 cents bid, unchanged.

The goal of the plan is to reduce the number of shareholders to less than 300, which would allow Meret to terminate its registration with the Securities and Exchange Commission and no longer be a public company, Meret said. For the company, with annual revenue of slightly more than $10 million, the expense and management time required to meet SEC regulations for public companies is disproportionate to benefits obtained, Meret said.

Meret retained Harmon Group, a Columbus investment banking firm, to give an opinion on the fairness of the plan. Shareholders will be asked to approve the plan at a special meeting later this summer, the company said.
