Ford Motor Credit To Offer Rates Based On Credit History

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/12/90

DETROIT -- Ford Motor Credit Co., the financing arm of Ford Motor Co., says it will go nationwide this fall with a program that bases the interest rate charged for a car loan on a consumer's credit history. General Motors Corp.'s financing arm, General Motors Acceptance Corp., made a similar program available to its dealers in March 1989. Chrysler Corp.'s auto finance arm, meanwhile, is developing a similar financing system that could be offered to dealers within the next 12 months.

Ford Credit's program ranks customers on a scale from level one, the best credit risk, to level four, the worst risk. New-car buyers who are assigned a level-one rank can get financing at interest rates 1.25 percentage points below Ford Credit's base rate. That's the rate charged to consumers with "average" credit and assigned a level-two rank.

Conversely, customers deemed poor credit risks could pay a rate of interest three to four percentage points higher than the base rate. Ford Credit said its base interest rates for car loans vary from state to state, but currently range between 11.5% and 12.25%.

Ford Credit said it will assign the rankings, but dealers will continue to negotiate the actual interest charges with customers on a deal-by-deal basis.

Ford Credit officials say their aim is to attract buyers whose solid credit ratings allow them to get lower finance rates at banks. Also, by raising rates for high-risk customers, the finance unit can partly offset the costs of higher loan losses.

"It's our hope and long-term plan that {this} will be our standard program" for financing auto loans, says Leo Larkin, Ford Credit's vice president of marketing. Ford Credit started assigning credit rankings to customers in 1988 as a test program in a few dealerships. The financing rating system currently is offered through about 35% of Ford and Lincoln-Mercury dealers.
