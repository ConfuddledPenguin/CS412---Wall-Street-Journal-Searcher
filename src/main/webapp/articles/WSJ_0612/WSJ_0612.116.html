Business Brief -- Birmingham Steel Corp.: Company Has Land Option For New Mill in Arizona

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/12/90

Birmingham Steel Corp., Birmingham, Ala., said it has an option to buy 1,000 acres of land just west of Phoenix, Ariz., as a likely site for a new steel minimill. The new mill, which would produce 500,000 tons of steel annually, could begin production before the end of 1992, the company said.

A new minimill, which would use an electric furnace to produce steel from scrap, would cost more than $100 million, Birmingham said. The new mill would employ about 250 people.

The facility would replace a mill at Emeryville, Calif., which the company must close before the end of 1997, under an agreement with local officials, Birmingham said. High transportation, electric power and labor costs at Emeryville led the company to start seeking a new site well before the deadline.

Birmingham produces reinforcing bars, steel roof-support systems for underground coal mines and other steel products.

The company said it will continue to evaluate several other possible locations for the mill, but that it has begun seeking environmental and operating approvals for the Arizona site.
