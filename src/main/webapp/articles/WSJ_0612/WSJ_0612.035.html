U.S., Canada Unions Set Telecommunications Pact

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/12/90

TORONTO -- The two unions representing Canadian and U.S. telecommunications industry workers said they concluded an alliance that will allow them to support each other's organizing, collective bargaining and research activities. Morton Bahr, president of the Communications Workers of America, and Fred Pomeroy, president of the Communications and Electrical Workers of Canada, said the agreement -- the first of its kind -- is essential in negotiating with the multinational corporations that dominate the industry.

"We've got to deliver the message that a company cannot practice good labor relations in one place and be anti-union in another," Mr. Bahr said.

He said the new alliance would allow the 700,000-member CWA and the CWC, which represents 40,000 employees, to work together in organizing employees of one company in both countries.

The union leaders said they expect the alliance to strengthen their bargaining clout with multinational corporations.

A spokesman for Mississauga, Ontario-based Northern Telecom Ltd., one of the large telecommunications companies with operations in both the U.S. and Canada, said the formation of the alliance "isn't surprising" given the degree of globalization of the industry. But he declined further comment until the company has had an opportunity to discuss the move with union representatives.
