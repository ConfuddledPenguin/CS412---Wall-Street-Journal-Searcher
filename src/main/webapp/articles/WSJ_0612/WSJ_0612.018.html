Dutch Trade Volume

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/12/90

AMSTERDAM -- The volume of Dutch trade set records in the first quarter, when exports rose to 60 billion guilders ($31.4 million) from 56.6 billion guilders a year earlier, and imports increased to 57.8 billion guilders from 53.6 billion guilders, the Dutch Central Bureau of Statistics said. The Dutch trade surplus fell in the 1990 first quarter to 2.2 billion from 3.1 billion guilders a year earlier.

March exports reached a monthly record, with volume growing to 21.8 billion guilders and imports standing at 20.3 billion guilders, leaving a positive balance of 1.5 billion guilders. These figures compare with year-earlier levels of 20.8 billion guilders in exports and 19.4 billion in imports, resulting in a surplus of 1.4 billion guilders.
