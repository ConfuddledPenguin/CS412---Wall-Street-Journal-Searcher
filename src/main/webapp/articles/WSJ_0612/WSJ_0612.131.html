Canadian Dispute Will Go To the June 23 Deadline

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 06/12/90

Canada's constitutional fight will go right to the deadline. Newfoundland Premier Clyde Wells said he will submit the Meech Lake constitutional accord to a free vote in the provincial legislature just before or on June 22, one day before the legal deadline for ratifying the agreement.

The pact would give Quebec special status and end a constitutional impasse that has revived talk of independence in the French-speaking province.

Speaking in the Newfoundland House of Assembly, Mr. Wells urged legislators to consult with their constituents before the vote. In a free vote, legislators aren't bound by party discipline. Mr. Wells's Liberal government holds 31 seats in the House of Assembly. The Progressive Conservatives hold the remaining 21 seats. Opposition Leader Tom Rideout said his members will also be able to vote freely.

Saturday night, Canadian Prime Minister Brian Mulroney and the 10 provincial premiers signed a tentative agreement that attempts to deal with the concerns of three provinces that had refused to ratify the Meech Lake accord as drafted.

But Mr. Wells opposed Saturday's agreement and said he signed only so he could submit the Meech Lake accord and the add-on agreement to his legislature for approval. The premiers of the two other holdout provinces, New Brunswick and Manitoba, agreed Saturday to pass the accord. But they must still submit it to their legislatures for approval. The accord could run into trouble in Manitoba, where Premier Gary Filmon heads a minority government.
