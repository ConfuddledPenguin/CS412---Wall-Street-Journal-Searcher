Business Brief -- Eastman Kodak Co.: Company Introduces 4 New Beginners' Compact Cameras

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/12/90

Seeking to boost its share in the inexpensive 35mm-camera market, Eastman Kodak Co., Rochester, N.Y., introduced four new compact cameras designed for beginners. The Star 35 cameras, which have suggested retail prices from $44.95 to $79.95, will be smaller than slightly more expensive existing Kodak models. All are designed for point-and-shoot photography popular with beginners.

Industry analysts said the line extension isn't a significant investment for Kodak, but the company hopes the move will increase sales of film and paper. Each new camera comes with a roll of Kodacolor Gold 200 color-print film and two AA-size Kodak Photolife alkaline batteries.

Kodak had dropped out of the 35mm market in the 1970s. Since the company re-entered the market in the mid-1980s, it has concentrated on budget-minded starting photographers and left the higher-margin market primarily to Japanese companies.
