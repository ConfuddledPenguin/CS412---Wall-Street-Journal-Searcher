Parsons Corp. Says Judge Dismissed Suit Challenging Buy-Out

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/12/90

PASADENA, Calif. -- Parsons Corp. said a federal judge in Los Angeles dismissed a lawsuit brought by 15 employees who had challenged the leveraged buy-out of the engineering and construction company by its employee stock ownership plan in 1985. The case has been closely watched by pension experts and financial planners.

The suit had charged that the ESOP's acquisition of Parsons's shares was a breach of fiduciary responsibility, misuse of corporate assets and a termination of predecessor plans, all of which injured the participants in the transaction.

Specifically, the employees had charged that the company executives who designed and implemented the buy-out pocketed millions of dollars, while the employees' future retirement funds became saddled with hundreds of millions of dollars of debt.

But, according to Parsons, U.S. Distrct Judge Robert Kelleher found after a five-day trial that the ESOP retirement committee had acted "both independently and prudently in deciding that the buy-out was in the best interests of the ESOP participants."

An attorney for the plaintiffs said they would appeal. "I respectfully believe the decision will be reversed," said Frederick Furth, senior partner of Furth, Fahrner & Mason in San Francisco.
