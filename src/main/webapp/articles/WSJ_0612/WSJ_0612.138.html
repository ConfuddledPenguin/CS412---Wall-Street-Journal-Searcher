Furniture Industry Figures Are Sentenced On Fraud Charges

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/12/90

NEW YORK -- A federal judge sentenced three retail furniture businessmen on fraud and conspiracy charges in connection with a scheme that defrauded investors of $52 million. Louis Bigman, 65 years old, of Douglaston, N.Y., was sentenced to 46 months in prison. Ben Wernikoff, 64, of Jericho, N.Y., and Henry Switsky, 61, of Marlboro, N.J., were each sentenced to 37 months. The three men pleaded guilty in federal court in Manhattan in January.

The defendants were charged with defrauding investors through the sale of retail installment-loan agreements from Alcor Trading Corp. and Marba Furniture Inc., two furniture companies owned by Messrs. Bigman, Wernikoff and Switsky. The investors were told that their money would be used to buy the loan agreements at a sharp discount and that they would make a profit of 15% to 22% when the defendants collected the full amounts due under the installment loans.

Instead, according to the U.S. attorney's office in Manhattan, the money was used to pay off earlier investors in the scheme or to make unsecured loans -- which weren't repaid -- to other companies owned by the defendants. The scheme fell apart in March 1989 when Alcor and another company owned by the defendants were placed in bankruptcy-law proceedings, partly because investors sought the return of their money.

Although the defendants have agreed to try to make restitution, the defrauded investors are expected to be repaid less than 1% of the total owed to them, prosecutors said.

Another defendant, Ira Goldstein, pleaded guilty to conspiracy and will be sentenced Friday.
