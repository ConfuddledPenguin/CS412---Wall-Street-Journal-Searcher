Nestle's Unit in U.S. Combines Businesses Of Some Subsidiaries

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/12/90

NEW YORK -- Nestle Holdings Inc. consolidated several of its U.S. businesses, forming new coffee and ice cream companies and a new group to hold its Stouffer restaurant and hotel operations. Nestle Holdings is the holding company for the U.S. subsidiaries of Nestle S.A. of Switzerland.

Under the plan, Nestle combined Hills Bros. Coffee Co. and the coffee units of Nestle Foods Co. to form Nestle/Hills Coffee Co. The new company will have annual sales of about $1 billion and 3,000 employees and will have its headquarters in San Francisco.

In addition, Nestle joined the ice cream and dairy facilities of Carnation Co. with those of Nestle Foods Co. The new entity, Nestle Ice Cream Co., will report to Nestle Foods Co., Purchase, N.Y., and will market Carnation and Nestle ice cream.

At Stouffers, Nestle merged Stouffer Restaurant Co. with Stouffer Hotel Co., forming Stouffer Hospitality Group. It will continue to have its headquarters in Cleveland.

James M. Biggar, chairman of Nestle Holdings, said the moves were "aimed at increasing and improving our effectiveness in the marketplace and to take advantage of synergies" in Nestle's businesses. The changes should be completed during the second half of 1990, he added.

A Nestle spokeswoman said revenue figures for the combined Ice Cream and Stouffers businesses weren't available.

Asked about potential layoffs, the spokeswoman said its "too early to make any predictions" on force consolidations. She said the only executive change was the promotion of Jeffrey Caso, former group vice president of Nestle Foods Coffee & Tea division, to executive vice president of Nestle/Hills Coffee Co.
