Microsoft, Everex File Suit Against Alleged Product Counterfeiters

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/12/90

REDMOND, Wash. -- Microsoft Corp. said it and Everex Systems Inc. together filed suit against 16 individuals and companies, accusing them of producing and distributing counterfeit copies of a Microsoft computer product. Microsoft, a software company, and Everex, a Microsoft licensee, alleged that the group sold some 30,000 fake MS-DOS operating systems under Everex's ParCorp. label. An operating system controls the basic functions of a computer. Everex has a licensing agreement with Microsoft under which it produces and markets the MS-DOS operating system under the ParCorp. trade name.

Microsoft sells more than 10 million MS-DOS products a year.

Microsoft said the 16 defendants include J&S International Co. of San Jose, Calif.; Benny S. Lee, Norman Chan and Susan Chan, all of Fremont, Calif.; and Micro-Generation, a division of Continental Technolgoy Inc., located in Edison, N.J. None could be reached for comment.
