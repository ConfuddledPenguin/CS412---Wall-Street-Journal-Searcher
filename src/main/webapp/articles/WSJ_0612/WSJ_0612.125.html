Du Pont Co. to Get Out Of Acrylic Fiber Business

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/12/90

WILMINGTON, Del. -- Du Pont Co. said it will get out of the acrylic fiber business after 40 years of supplying the fiber to makers of sweaters, socks and other products. Du Pont, which sold the fiber under the Orlon trade name, said it had been losing money on the business in recent years because of imports and the loss of customers to polyester and cotton. "Each market, for its own set of reasons, has been under attack," said Paul G. Gillease, director of Du Pont's textile division.

He said annual sales of Orlon were about $200 million. In 1989, total fiber sales by Du Pont, including those of nylon, were $5.97 billion.

Mr. Gillease said Du Pont will withdraw from the acrylic fiber business as soon as possible to concentrate on its other apparel fabrics, Dacron polyester and Lycra spandex. He said the company will probably be able to offer other jobs to a majority of the 775 people currently employed in the Orlon business. Du Pont makes Orlon along with other fabrics at plants in Camden, S.C., and Waynesboro, Va.

The exit by Du Pont should give a boost to other acrylic fiber makers, who have been operating amid excess production capacity. Monsanto Co., which counts itself the largest U.S. producer, said there is more than enough domestic capacity to absorb the closing of Du Pont's production lines.
