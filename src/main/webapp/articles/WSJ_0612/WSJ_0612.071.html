Thomson McKinnon Files for Chapter 11, In Liquidation Plan

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/12/90

NEW YORK -- Thomson McKinnon Inc. said it filed for protection from creditors under Chapter 11 of the U.S. Bankruptcy Code, part of the liquidation of the company that began nearly a year ago. The company's principal asset is its general partnership interest in Thomson McKinnon Asset Management Limited Partnership. It listed its liabilities at $281 million and its assets at $35 million, a spokesman said. The largest creditor is the company's landlord, Assay Partners, which is owed about $62 million, the spokesman said.

Thomson McKinnon recently agreed in principle to sell its general partnership interest in Thomson McKinnon Asset Management to a group including executives. This plan calls for the group to purchase 200,000 general partnership units from Thomson McKinnon for $800,000, and to pay $5.4 million for an option to buy the balance of Thomson's total of about 6.2 million units. The proposed sale shouldn't be impeded by the filing, Thomson said. The spokesman said the transaction could be completed in six or seven weeks.

The company also is the parent of Thomson McKinnon Securities Inc., which sold its retail brokerage network last summer to Prudential-Bache Securities Inc., a unit of Prudential Insurance Co. of America. The operations that remained of the broker-dealer unit filed for Chapter 11 protection in March.
