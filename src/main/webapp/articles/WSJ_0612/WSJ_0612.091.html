HEWLETT-PACKARD Co. says use of a non-Hewlett toner in its laser printers does not, by itself, affect the overall warranty on the printer. "However," the company says, "if an HP LaserJet failure or damage is found to be directly attributable to the use of a non-Hewlett toner cartridge, Hewlett will not repair the printer free-of-charge." Wednesday's Form + Function column said Hewlett "warns that anyone who uses refilled toner may void the warranty." (See: "FORM + FUNCTION" -- WSJ June 6, 1990)

---

TIME WARNER Inc.'s loan agreement with Pathe Communications Corp. stipulates that additional financing procured by Pathe be in equity, not debt. The terms were misstated in Friday's edition.

(See: "Marketing & Media: Time Warner to Amend Loan to Pathe, Which Is Seeking to Acquire MGM/UA" -- WSJ June 8, 1990)

---

HENLEY GROUP Inc.'s Pneumo Abex Corp. subsidiary will continue to own its brakes division after Henley distributes 35% of Pneumo Abex to shareholders. Henley, which dropped an earlier plan to spin off 100% of Pneumo Abex to shareholders, would have purchased the brakes division from Pneumo Abex under the earlier plan, Henley said, clarifying a story in yesterday's edition.

(See: "Henley Modifies Plan To Spin Off 2 Units" -- WSJ June 11, 1990)

---

MEDTRONIC Inc.'s bid for Bio-Medicus Inc. calls for Bio-Medicus holders to exchange 3.1 shares for one Medtronic share, subject to certain stock-price provisions. In yesterday's edition, the ratio was misstated.

(See: "Business Brief -- Bio-Medicus Inc.: Medical-Gear Maker Accepts Medtronic's Sweetened Bid" -- WSJ June 11, 1990)
