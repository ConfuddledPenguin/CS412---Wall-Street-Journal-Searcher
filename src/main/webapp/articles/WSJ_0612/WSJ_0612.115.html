International Brief -- Ferranti International PLC: Chairman Seeks Financing To Alter Remaining Lines

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/12/90

Ferranti International PLC's chairman, Eugene Anderson, is seeking new financing arrangements to help bankroll his planned restructuring of the troubled British electronics concern's remaining businesses. Mr. Anderson said he has asked Ferranti's bankers "to refinance us to give us the working capital we need." He said he wants "to complete those {financing} negotiations this month." A U.S.-born corporate troubleshooter, he took the Ferranti post in February after the company had a crisis last fall, owing to an alleged $363 million fraud at a big U.S. unit, which led Ferranti's 29 banks to craft an emergency financing accord. Mr. Anderson said he wants to replace that pact with "a more normal banking relationship," in which the number of banks may drop to as few as 10 or 15. The new arrangement would enable Ferranti to pursue new business, he said.

Under the plan, Ferranti's five divisions will shrink to three: aerospace systems, strategic management systems, and commercial and industrial systems; some manufacturing facilities will be closed, and an unspecified number of the 12,000-member work force world-wide will be cut.
