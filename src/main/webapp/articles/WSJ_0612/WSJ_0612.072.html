Malaysia's Rate of Inflation

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/12/90

KUALA LUMPUR, Malaysia -- Despite credit constraints, Malaysia's consumer price index in April rose 0.7% from March, the steepest increase this year, the government statistics department said. The index, in which the base of 100 is calculated with 1980 prices, rose to 136.6 from 135.7. The index, which reflects retail prices of goods and services, was up 0.8% from the end of December.

Averaging the rise in prices in the first four months of this year, the index rose 2.3% from a year earlier, registering inflation below official government projections of about a 4% rate for this year. But many private analysts have forecast a rate of at least 5%, as price pressures are fueled by strong domestic demand in Malaysia's rapidly expanding economy and accommodated by the relatively fast growth of the money supply. The weighting of the index's various parts doesn't fairly represent new consumption patterns in the developing nation, the economists say, and the index thus underestimates true inflation.
