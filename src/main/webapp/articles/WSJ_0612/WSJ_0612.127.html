Technology: Semiconductor Indicator Rose in May From April

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/12/90

CUPERTINO, Calif. -- The semiconductor industry's leading indicator rose in May, as the industry's modest rebound from depressed sales in the last half of 1989 continued. The Semiconductor Industry Association said the so-called book-to-bill ratio for May was 1.14:1, up from 1.09:1 in April. Both figures are preliminary. A 1.14 rate means that for every $100 of products shipped, or billed, chip makers received $114 in new orders, or bookings.

The rising indicator reflects a stable market that has so far escaped the slump that many in the industry expected in 1990, though some in the industry were reluctant to predict any incipient boom. "It's certainly a positive sign, but the long-term impact remains to be seen," said Tom Beerman, a spokesman for the semiconductor association.

Semiconductor billings in May were $1.18 billion, 5.7% above April's $1.11 billion and 4.5% lower than a year earlier, when billings totaled $1.23 billion.

Average monthly orders for the three months ended in May totaled $1.38 billion, 7.8% above April's figure of $1.28 billion about 8% above the year-earlier figure of $1.28 billion.
