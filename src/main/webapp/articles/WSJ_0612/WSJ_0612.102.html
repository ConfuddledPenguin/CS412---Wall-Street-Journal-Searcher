Business Brief -- Genlyte Group Inc.: Lighting Fixture Maker Sees Drop in 2nd-Quarter Profit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/12/90

Genlyte Group Inc. said second-quarter earnings may plunge as much as 50% because of construction strikes in Canada and a slowdown in commercial and residential construction in the Northeastern U.S. The Secaucus, N.J., company, which makes lighting fixtures and controls under such names as Lightolier, KLP and Hadco, said it has increased spending to promote sales and maintain market share, but a spokesman said there could be some cutbacks in operations.

"When things are off this much you always have to look at {cutbacks} even if they're temporary," he said, adding: "We're watching all our expenses now, obviously." Genlyte employs 4,000 people and has 21 plants in the U.S., Canada and Mexico.

For last year's second quarter, it posted a 14% increase in net income to $3.4 million, or 26 cents a share; revenue rose 3% to $128.5 million. For all of 1989, net increased 8% to $12.3 million, or 95 cents a share; revenue rose 4% to $511 million.
