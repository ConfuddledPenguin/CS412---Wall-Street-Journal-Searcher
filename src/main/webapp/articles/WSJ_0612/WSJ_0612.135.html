Who's News: Citizens Utilities Co.'s Jacobson to Retire; Ferguson to Step Up

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/12/90

STAMFORD, Conn. -- Ishier Jacobson said he will retire as president, chief executive officer and a director of Citizens Utilities Co., effective June 30. Mr. Jacobson, who will be 68 years old this month, will continue to work closely with the company as a consultant to the board.

Daryl A. Ferguson, 51, will become president and chief operating officer July 1. Mr. Ferguson currently is senior vice president, operations and engineering.

A successor to Mr. Jacobson as chief executive hasn't been named, nor has one been named to Mr. Ferguson's post. The chairmanship has been vacant since last year, and it isn't clear whether or when the chairmanship and post of chief executive will be filled. As of July 1, Mr. Ferguson would hold the highest positions in the company, a spokeswoman said.

Citizens is a public utility that provides telecommunications services and electric, water and gas services in 12 states. Citizens earned $88 million, or $2.01 a share, in 1989, on operating revenue of $339 million, the spokeswoman said.

Mr. Ferguson has broad telecommunications experience in both regulated and nonregulated businesses. Before joining Citizens last year, he was a senior operations executive with Centel Corp.

Mr. Jacobson, who has been a director since 1968 and president since 1970, led the expansion of Citizens' utility operations into Pennsylvania, Ohio, Indiana, Illinois and Hawaii, among other places. He was instrumental in the company's recent agreement to acquire Louisiana General Services Inc., a Harvey, La., natural-gas utility, which would position the company in the Middle South for the first time.
