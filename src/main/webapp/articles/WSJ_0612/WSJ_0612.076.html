Boeing, as Expected, Gets Big Korean Airlines Order

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/12/90

SEATTLE -- Boeing Co., as expected, received an order from Korean Airlines for 23 747-400s. The aerospace giant valued the order at $4.8 billion, including spare parts and training, in delivery-year dollars. It is one of the largest orders ever placed with Boeing, the world's leading commercial jet maker.

The news sent Boeing's stock soaring. The company's shares closed in New York Stock Exchange composite trading at $58.50, up $3.50. (A 3-for-2 stock split, announced in April, kicked in yesterday.)

Korean Airlines's 747-400s are configured to carry as many as 392 passengers in a three-class seating arrangement. The planes are capable of operating at nonstop ranges of more than 8,000 miles, and will be used to connect Korea to destinations in Europe and North America.

The booking brings total orders of the 747-400 to 307 aircraft, of which 71 have now been delivered. For the year, carriers have ordered from Boeing 243 aircraft valued at $19.3 billion. That compares with 596 planes valued at $32.5 billion in the year-ago period.

The decrease partly reflects the fact that the two largest orders in Boeing history, from UAL Corp.'s United Airlines unit and GPA Group Ltd., both came in April 1989.
