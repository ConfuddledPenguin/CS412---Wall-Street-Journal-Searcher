Drexel Committee Postpones Filing Plan For Reorganization

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/12/90

NEW YORK -- The creditors' committee of Drexel Burnham Lambert Group Inc. postponed filing its own proposed reorganization plan for the securities concern. Last week, the committee had said it would include the proposal in its motion opposing an extension of Drexel's "exclusivity period," the time period in which only Drexel can officially propose a reorganization plan under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code.

Instead, according to people close to the case, an agreement has been reached to extend the period until mid-November. That would give Drexel time to further develop its preliminary reorganization proposal, which was panned by the creditors' committee. The creditors had criticized the proposal for being too vague and for leaving current management in charge of the company.

Attorneys for Drexel and the creditors didn't return phone calls seeking comment. A spokesman for the company said no comment would be made until a hearing today before U.S. Bankruptcy Court Judge Howard Buschman III.
