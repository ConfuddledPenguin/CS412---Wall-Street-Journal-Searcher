Credit Ratings: General Dynamics, McDonnell Douglas Debt Under Review

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/12/90

NEW YORK -- Debt ratings of General Dynamics Corp. and McDonnell Douglas Corp. were placed under review by Standard & Poor's Corp. and Moody's Investors Service Inc., after the St. Louis-based aerospace companies disclosed they may face second-quarter write-offs because of delays in developing the A-12 Navy fighter plane. Placed on CreditWatch with negative implications by Standard & Poor's were McDonnell Douglas's single-A-minus rated senior debt; triple-B-plus subordinated debt; and preliminary single-A-minus rule 415 shelf-registered senior debt; plus the McDonnell Douglas Finance Corp. unit's single-A-plus senior debt and single-A subordinated debt ratings. The single-A-2 rating on the parent company's commercial paper and single-A-1 rating on the finance unit's commercial paper were affirmed. Total debt is about $4.9 billion.

S&P also placed General Dynamics' single-A-plus senior debt, single-A subordinated debt and preliminary single-A-plus rule 415 shelf-registered senior debt ratings on CreditWatch with negative implications. About $710 million of long-term debt is affected.

Moody's said it will closely monitor developments on the A-12 program "related to the potential cost overruns, their magnitude, and the impact on the companies' cash flow as well as their overall operating performance. Cost problems, if significant, would have negative implications for the companies' credit quality."

A McDonnell Douglas spokeswoman said of the ratings action: "We are a company with great long-term potential that's experiencing some short-term difficulties, and we believe that the steps we are taking now are the right ones for the long term."

A General Dynamics spokesman said the rating agencies' actions were "not unexpected given the announcement regarding the A-12."
