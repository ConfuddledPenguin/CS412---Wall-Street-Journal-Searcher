Aetna Loses Plea On Its Auto Rates In Pennsylvania

  • Author: Beatrice E. Garcia
  • Date: 06/12/90

Pennsylvania insurance officials denied Aetna Life & Casualty Co.'s request for relief from the auto insurance rate rollbacks required by the state's new auto insurance law. Aetna, the state's fifth-largest auto insurer, had agreed to halt its withdrawal from the auto insurance market in the state until officials ruled on its rate filing. An Aetna spokesman said the company couldn't comment on the Pennsylvania insurance department's ruling on its rate filing until it had a chance to review the department's entire decision carefully.

The insurance reform law, enacted in February, requires that auto insurers reduce rates 10% for all drivers and as much as 22% for motorists who waive their right to sue in all but the most serious accidents. However, it does allow insurers to apply for relief from these rollbacks under an "extraordinary circumstance provision."

For instance, one unit of the Hartford-based Aetna which operates in Pennsylvania had asked for a 2.3% decrease for drivers who chose the limited tort option and an 8.6% increase for those who kept all their rights to sue.

The state rejected that request, saying this Aetna unit should decrease rates by 20.4% for drivers who elected the limit tort option and drop rates by 8.2% for drivers who didn't take this option. The insurance department said its decision was based on each company's ability to earn a 12% rate of return on its surplus capital.

But the department said it would approve revised filings, granting the company some partial relief by allowing it to drop rates to the levels calculated by the insurance department rather than the full 10% and 22% mandated by the new laws.

Officials also rejected four other applications for relief from the rollbacks yesterday. To date, 12 of 22 relief applications have been denied.
