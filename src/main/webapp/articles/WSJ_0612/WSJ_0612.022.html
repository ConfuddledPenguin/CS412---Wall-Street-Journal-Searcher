Letters to the Editor: We Aren't Marketing Cigarettes to Minors

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/12/90

Your May 3 article "Tobacco Critics See a Subtle Sell to Kids" recounted the anti-smoking-movement charges that we are marketing our cigarettes to minors. We are not. Philip Morris USA is the largest cigarette company in the U.S. Last year we sold 220 billion cigarettes. A small number of these cigarettes are unfortunately obtained by minors. We at Philip Morris do not want minors to smoke. Smoking is an adult custom. Maturity of judgment is necessary to making a responsible, informed decision about smoking. As a matter of policy, we do not market cigarettes to minors. We employ only adult models in our advertising who not only are but look over 25. We don't have celebrities or athletes endorse cigarettes in our advertising.

Every serious study of why minors smoke points toward family and friends, not cigarette-company marketing practices. Indeed, there are no significant differences in smoking incidence among minors between otherwise comparable countries that allow tobacco advertising and those that prohibit it. According to the Federal Trade Commission, tobacco-marketing expenditures in the U.S. have tripled since 1977. During that same period, the University of Michigan reports smoking among high-school seniors has dropped by more than one third -- from 29% to 18%. This decline is rooted not in marketing restrictions but in education including a program, "Helping Youth Decide," sponsored by the tobacco industry.

As your article notes, Philip Morris has taken legal action against companies that use our cigarette trademarks in products that appeal to youth, and we caused a manufacturer to remove the Marlboro logo from its computer video games.

The most effective ways to stop minors from smoking are to educate youth and make it clear to retailers that it is illegal for them to sell cigarettes to minors. This approach has reduced the number of minors who smoke by more than one-third over the past 15 years and we at Philip Morris hope and believe it will continue to do so in the future.

John R. Nelson

Vice President

Corporate Affairs

Philip Morris USA

New York
