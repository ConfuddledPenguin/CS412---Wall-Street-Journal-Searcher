Marketing & Media: `Tracy' T-Shirts to Skew Disney Purse, Warner Says

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/12/90

HOLLYWOOD -- With one of the summer's most competitive box-office weekends looming, Time Warner Inc.'s Warner Brothers film unit is complaining that Walt Disney Co. isn't playing fair. Disney is retorting, "Keep your shirt on." Disney's marketers came up with a new promotion for "Dick Tracy," in which customers may buy T-shirts from theaters for prices ranging from $12 to $20. The shirts serve as admission tickets for special midnight showings Thursday night at 1,500 theaters across the country. The film then officially opens on Friday.

Warner, which is premiering "Gremlins 2" the same weekend, is complaining because the box-office take from those special midnight shows -- at prices well above that of a normal ticket -- will be included in the three-day grosses reported by Disney on Monday.

Warner officials declined to comment, but in yesterday's edition of the trade paper Variety, distribution chief Barry Reardon said of the Disney plan, "I object to it vehemently. How can a T-shirt be part of the theatrical gross? I don't think it's fair."

A Disney spokeswoman said, "Every dollar taken in will be reported because we're not selling T-shirts, we're selling premium-priced tickets." She added that because the showings technically begin at 12:01 a.m. Friday, the midnight shows can be included as part of Friday's results.
