Business Brief -- Accor S.A.: Concern Says Motel 6's Net In '90 Will Be $19.6 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/20/90

Accor S.A., a French hospitality company, said in a filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission that Motel 6 L.P. has projected its net income for the 1990 fiscal year ending Dec. 31 will be $19.6 million on revenue of $477.4 million. The projection was made in connection with a tender offer made last week by Accor S.A. to acquire Motel 6 of Dallas for $22.50 a unit, or nearly $1.3 billion. Accor said the figure was used in merger talks.

In a related filing, Motel 6 said that three class-action suits have been filed to block the acquisition. Motel 6 executives weren't available for comment.

Although the merger isn't conditioned on financing, Accor said it estimates it will need $1.3 billion to cover costs of the deal, including buying the shares and paying fees and expenses. Accor said it would borrow the money from Banque Indosuez and Societe Generale under a special credit arrangement and from its existing working capital and credit lines.

The French company also said it will explore ways to refinance Motel 6's existing debt after the deal is completed.

Accor also said it has agreed to pay Morgan Stanley & Co. a fee of $1 million for acting as a dealer-manager in the offer. Motel 6, in its filing, said it has agreed to pay Salomon Brothers Inc. $5.5 million for acting as its financial adviser.
