Schlumberger's Net Rose 14% in Quarter On Higher Demand

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/20/90

NEW YORK -- Citing increased demand for oil-field services world-wide, Schlumberger Ltd. reported a 14% gain in net income for the second quarter. The oil-field-service company earned $144.8 million, or 61 cents a share, compared with $126.8 million, or 53 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue rose 7.8% to $1.29 billion from $1.20 billion.

The company noted that second-quarter net rose 56% excluding $35 million of extraordinary and unusual gains from a year earlier.

Evan Baird, chairman, said that despite weakening oil prices, "world-wide oil-field activity continued to increase as the oil industry remained focused on developing reserves for the future." He said revenue from oil-field activity rose 21% while the drilling rig count world-wide rose 11%.

First-half net rose 30% to $273.3 million, or $1.15 a share, from $209.2 million, or 88 cents a share; revenue rose to $2.51 billion from $2.38 billion.
