Apache Corp. to Post Profit Increase of 67% For Second Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/20/90

DENVER -- Apache Corp. expects to report second-quarter net income rose 67% to about $7.5 million, or 17 cents a share, from $4.5 million, or 14 cents a share, a year earlier, said Raymond Plank, chairman and chief executive officer. Attributing the record second-quarter results to increased natural gas production and reduced costs, Mr. Plank said six-month net income increased 92% to about $19 million, or 43 cents a share, from $9.9 million, or 30 cents a share, a year earlier. Shares outstanding as of June 30 averaged about 44.6 million, reflecting a 35% increase from 33 million a year ago. The company is expected to report its earnings next week.

Mr. Plank said revenue for the second quarter is expected to match the year-earlier's $59 million. Six-month revenue rose about 5% to $127 million from $120.6 million.

Apache's natural gas production during the quarter increased about 15% to 260 million cubic feet a day from 227 million cubic feet.

With oil prices off slightly from a year ago, Mr. Plank said second-quarter oil production was "close to flat" compared with 1989 when Apache produced about 8,600 barrels a day.

Mr. Plank said Apache had recorded about a 25% reduction in lease operating, administrative and financing costs during the first six months, much of it because of the conversion of two debenture issues, which resulted in a savings in interest costs of about $1 million a month.

Because of Apache's performance during the first six months, Mr. Plank said, "we do not have basis to disagree" with analysts' estimates that per-share earnings for 1990 will be 75 cents to 80 cents.
