U.S. Shoe's Stock Falls on Problems At Lenscrafters

  • Author: James S. Hirsch
  • Date: 07/20/90

U.S. Shoe Corp. said falling sales in its once-invincible Lenscrafters unit will torpedo second-quarter earnings. The announcement rattled investors who viewed the specialty optical chain as the one strong horse in the company's otherwise erratic stable of businesses. In composite trading yesterday on the New York Stock Exchange, U.S. Shoe stock tumbled 19%, or $4.25 a share, closing at $18. The stock plunged 9.2% Wednesday on rumors of an earnings dropoff.

Analysts blamed the sales drop on increased competition among optical retailers. While no other national chain offers the type of one-hour turnaround service that Lenscrafters does, more local optical centers are opening up, with aggressive marketing and discounting strategies.

Bannus B. Hudson, U.S. Shoe's chairman and chief executive officer, said Lenscrafters' problems reflect slack demand industrywide. Despite a strong first quarter, sales for eyeglasses are down between 14% to 16% for the quarter, he said. Mr. Hudson had no explanation for the sudden decline.

"There's nothing going on to explain this," he said. "But we don't view it as a long-term change in the market."

Lenscrafters is also in the midst of an expensive expansion -- it plans to add 100 stores this year -- but Mr. Hudson said there are no plans to change that plan.

Whatever the cause, the falloff was a blow to U.S. Shoe, which has relied on Lenscrafters' double-digit growth over the past several years to compensate for inconsistent performance in the company's specialty apparel and foot divisions. With $520 million in sales last year, Lenscrafters accounted for 21% of sales and 37% of earnings.

What's more, Lenscrafters has been the hook upon which many on Wall Street hung their investments. "Lenscrafters is the one rock on which the valuation of the company is based," said Barry Bryant, a retail analyst at Prudential-Bache Securities. "People who like the stock think Lenscrafters is worth what the whole company is selling for."

U.S. Shoe said its second-quarter earnings would be below the year-earlier net of $2.6 million, or six cents a share. Those are restated to reflect accounting irregularities discovered this year in a shoe import unit. Prior to restatement, the company earned $5.9 million, or 13 cents a share.

Besides Lenscrafters, U.S. Shoe is struggling to revitalize its specialty retail unit, which hasn't kept pace with such giants as Limited Inc. and Gap Inc. The company shoe business saw a sharp drop in operating earnings late last year but rebounded in the first quarter.
