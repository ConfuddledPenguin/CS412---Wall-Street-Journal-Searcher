Business Brief -- Textron Inc.: Firm Reports a 22% Increase In Second-Quarter Earnings

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/20/90

Textron Inc., citing continued strength in its financial services segment and lower costs on fixed-price contracts, said second-quarter profit rose 22% to $72.1 million, or 80 cents a share. In the 1989 second quarter, when the Providence, R.I., company was hurt by a $9.5 million extraordinary loss, net income was $59.3 million, or 67 cents a share.

Revenue for the period rose 11% to $2 billion from $1.8 billion.

B.F. Dolan, chairman and chief executive, said that while some of Textron's markets remain soft, the company's diversity enabled it to achieve a "good" first half. Mr. Dolan predicted that 1990 "should be another good year for Textron."

For the six months, Textron reported net of $138.3 million, or $1.53 a share, on revenue of $3.9 billion. In the year-ago period, the company had net of $122.6 million, or $1.40 a share, on revenue of $3.7 billion.

Textron is a financial services, aerospace and commercial products concern.
