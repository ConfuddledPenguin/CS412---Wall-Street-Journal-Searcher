Japan's Household Spending

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/20/90

TOKYO -- Japan's household spending rose an inflation-adjusted 3.4% in May, the statistics bureau of the prime minister's office said, reflecting the low level of year-earlier spending after a 3% consumption tax was introduced in April 1989. But even excluding the tax factor, the May spending showed continued strong growth in consumer demand, one of the pillars supporting Japan's 44-month economic expansion. Spending on luxury items continued to increase, especially for high-quality electric appliances and personal computers.

May spending by households throughout Japan averaged 298,501 yen ($2,201). Wage-earning families spent 310,980 yen, up 5.2% from a year earlier, despite a 3.6% drop in real income to 408,312 yen in the month. Families' disposable income declined 6.1% to 323,224 yen, because of a change in the quarterly distribution of government social insurance payments. Spending by independent business owners and their families inched up 0.5% to 276,828 yen.
