BankAmerica Says Its Quality of Assets Improved in Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/20/90

SAN FRANCISCO -- BankAmerica Corp., continuing its rebound, posted relatively good earnings and strong improvements in its asset quality for its second quarter. Net income for the quarter was $267 million, or $1.17 a share, compared with year-ago earnings of $304 million, or $1.50 a share. However, analysts consider the current results to be a slight improvement over year-ago figures, which were lifted by $44 million from cash interest payments made by Brazilian borrowers. BankAmerica received no such payments from Brazil this quarter.

While the results were in line with expectations, analysts were pleased with what were described as "significant" progress in reducing the amount of nonperforming assets.

"They had across-the-board, significant improvements in that area," said J. Richard Fredericks, an analyst at Montgomery Securities Inc.

The bank holding company said that during the quarter, it reduced its nonaccrual assets by 21%, to $3.1 billion. Nonaccrual assets related to restructuring countries fell even more -- to $2.2 billion, from $3 billion.

BankAmerica also reported a 21.5% reduction in the amount of domestic real-estate loans on nonaccrual status. "You have all these nervous nellies back East talking about the California real-estate market slumping, and here (BankAmerica) is reporting improvements," said Michael Abrahams, an analyst at Bateman Eichler Hill Richards in Los Angeles.
