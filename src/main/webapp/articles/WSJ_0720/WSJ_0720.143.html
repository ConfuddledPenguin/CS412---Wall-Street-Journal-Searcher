Canada's Bombardier, Family Agree to Issue 7.5 Million Shares

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/20/90

MONTREAL -- Bombardier Inc. said it and members of the Bombardier family agreed to issue 7.5 million common shares to the public for a total of about 146.3 million Canadian dollars (US$126.3 million). The company said it will offer five million Class B subordinated voting shares, and the Bombardier family will sell 2.5 million Class B shares, all at C$19.50 a share.

Bombardier said proceeds of about C$93 million from the company's sale of the five million newly issued shares will be used to reduce debt and to increase working capital.

At last report, Bombardier had total debt of about C$222 million and C$504 million in shareholders' equity. Some analysts said they had not expected the company to issue more equity.

The transportation equipment manufacturer has made several major acquisitions in recent months, including the $75 million purchase of Learjet Corp. from Integrated Resources Inc. announced in April.

The Bombardier family's voting control of the company doesn't appear likely to be affected by their sale of the shares. At last report, the family held 78.7% of the Class A shares, representing a voting stake of 67%.

A preliminary prospectus is expected to be filed with regulators Monday. The share issue is subject to board and regulatory approvals. Toronto-based securities dealer Scotia McLeod Inc. will be the issue's lead underwriter.

In trading on the Toronto Stock Exchange yesterday, Bombardier's Class B shares closed at C$19.75, down 62.5 Canadian cents. Its Class A shares closed at C$19.875, down 50 Canadian cents.
