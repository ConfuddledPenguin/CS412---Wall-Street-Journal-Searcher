United Technologies Earnings Rose 5.1% In Second Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/20/90

HARTFORD, Conn. -- United Technologies Corp. said second-quarter profit rose 5.1%, which was in line with a company statement this week. Profit was $207.5 million, or $1.64 a share, up from $197.3 million, or $1.50 a share, in the year-earlier quarter. The profit also reflected an $18 million after-tax charge that was disclosed in the company's announcement Monday. The charge was due to a plant closing at the Carrier Corp. air-conditioning subsidiary.

Revenue climbed 7.9% to $5.69 billion, from $5.27 billion.

Earnings benefited from continued strength in the results of the Pratt & Whitney jet engine division and the Otis elevator division, the company said, as well as profit increases for the automotive and Sikorsky helicopter businesses.

Besides the special charge, profit at Carrier fell sharply because of a weak residential air-conditioner market in the U.S., the company said.

"We continue to benefit from our strong international businesses," said Robert F. Daniell, chairman and chief executive officer.

The company said its order backlog on June 30 stood at $21.1 billion, up from $17.5 billion a year earlier.

For the first half of 1990, earnings rose 6.5% to $342 million, or $2.69 a share, from $321 million, or $2.45 a share. Revenue increased 7.8% to $10.49 billion, from $9.73 billion.
