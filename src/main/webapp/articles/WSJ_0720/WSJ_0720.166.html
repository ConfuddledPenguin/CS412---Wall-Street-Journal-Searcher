Letters to the Editor: Homosexuality on the Curriculum

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/20/90

In your June 12 page-one article "Uncharted Course" on the controversy over teaching about homosexuality and AIDS in high schools, curriculum director Elaine Lohr was quoted as saying, "We are teaching about homosexuality, not teaching students to become homosexuals." What irony. As a matter of plain, ordinary common sense, I ask what is going to be the final result for a nation that insists on teaching about homosexuality to its young people, while at the same time forbids teaching in its schools, for fear of proselytizing, very much of anything about 2,000 years of Christianity.

Marie C. Verheggen

Worton, Md.

---

Your article leaves me certain that once again the facts about how AIDS is transmitted will be whitewashed to suit the demands of various pressure groups.

The classic example of this so far has been the alliance of convenience between Christian fundamentalists (hoping to scare everyone away from nonmarital sex) and gay activists (trying to prevent public understanding of how gay male behavior has spread AIDS) that has buffaloed the mass media into blaming AIDS on vague euphemisms such as "unsafe sex." A more courageous press would have clearly pointed out what the two main (and interrelated) causes of AIDS among non-drug users are: 1. Heavy promiscuity among gay males. 2. A high proportion of their promiscuous couplings consists of anal sex.

While the gay press has long told its readers the plain truth about the danger of anal sex, the mass media's timidity has undoubtedly led to women dying of AIDS because they never had a chance to learn this fact. (Feminist groups deserve censure as well for not making into a women's issue the threat posed by anal sex, probably out of ideological solidarity with gay groups.)

Sure, the media have trumpeted condoms as an all-purpose talisman against AIDS, but we all know condoms' limitations. AIDS is too deadly for AIDS education to emphasize only condoms just to avoid offending organized lobbies.

Steve Sailer

Chicago

---

I am constantly amazed at how those involved with the AIDS issue regularly find someone or something else to blame for the crisis. In your article, University of Minnesota pediatrics professor Gary Remafedi says, "AIDS is a disease that is all about racism, homophobia and discrimination."

Racism, homophobia and discrimination do exist, but they did not cause, nor do they perpetuate, AIDS. AIDS is primarily about unsafe sex being practiced by homosexuals and bisexuals, and the sharing of unclean needles among drug users.

Taking personal responsibility for one's own actions is not popular in any segment of American society today, and especially not in the homosexual community and among AIDS activists. All of us must be caring and compassionate toward those with AIDS, but with all the suffering that is occurring, we ought not add truth to the list of casualties.

Richard A. Webster

Falls Church, Va.

---

Why is there a crisis today in American public education? One need look no further than your article describing San Francisco's ninth-grade classtime devoted to talking about what homosexuals do in bed. With such a display of moral and intellectual bankruptcy being further endorsed by the National Education Association, one may safely conclude that public education now suffers from a terminal disease.

Lucielle W. Robbins

Hudson, N.H.

---

Project 10, mentioned in your article, is the nation's first mainstream program that addresses the needs of adolescent homosexuals. Its message is that unchallenged discrimination will no longer be tolerated. That Project 10 has survived the attacks of professional homophobes and is being replicated throughout the country speaks for the desperate need for similar efforts.

Virginia Uribe

Founder, Project 10

Los Angeles
