Marketing & Media: UAW Begins Ad Campaign On First Day of Ford Talks

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/20/90

DETROIT -- A new television ad campaign by the United Auto Workers portrays American workers, but uses professional actors instead of actual workers on film. The campaign started yesterday, the same day the union began bargaining with Ford Motor Co. on a new three-year labor agreement. The union will spend about $3 million over the next 10 days to run the three 30-second spots in 27 markets around the country.

Although the union has periodically run "image" advertising promoting American workers while bashing corporate America, this is the first time it has done so in conjunction with the start of negotiations. "It's position advertising," a union spokesman said.

Union officials say they didn't have enough time to search their ranks for real workers to star in the commercials. Another problem, the UAW said, was negotiating with the Screen Actors Guild to get approval for non-members to have parts. The Actors Guild, like the UAW, likes to reserve work for its members instead of outsiders.

The UAW's commercials feature only cars built in the U.S. by the Big Three -- a Chevrolet Beretta, built by General Motors Corp., a Ford Taurus, and a Jeep Cherokee, built by Chrysler Corp.

In one of the UAW's ads, a gray-haired worker wiping off a wrench says, "We've worked hard to turn things around because we want the U.S. auto industry to win. But we want to share in that success with secure jobs for the future."
