Marketing Brief -- Raben Publishing Co.: Boston Publishing Company Sells Its Magazine, Walking

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/20/90

Raben Publishing Co., a special interest magazine publisher, said it sold Walking magazine to its publisher and a group of investors. Boston-based Raben sold the four-year-old magazine to Jan Thaw Bruce, the current publisher of Walking, and a group of three venture capitalists, Ms. Bruce said. The investors currently have put money into magazines such as In Health and Health Journal. Terms of the acquisition were not disclosed.

Raben Publishing, founded by Norman Raben, started Walking magazine in 1986 at the start of what appeared to be a new trend drawing active adults.

Norman Raben, president of Raben Publishing, said he will retain about a 15% interest in the magazine.

Mr. Raben, who also publishes Bicycle Guide magazine, still faces large payments to creditors. In April, Raben Publishing asked creditors, including printers, bankers, paper suppliers, and consultants, to agree to an extended schedule of debt payments.

Sources and creditors close to Raben Publishing said Mr. Raben has sunk at least $20 million into three magazines, including another, UltraSport, which later folded. Mr. Raben is estimated to still owe as much as $10 million to creditors.
