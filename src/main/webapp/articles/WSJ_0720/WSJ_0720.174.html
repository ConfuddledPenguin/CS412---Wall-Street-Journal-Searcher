American General Says 2nd-Period Net Rose to $198 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/20/90

HOUSTON -- American General Corp., which earlier this year defeated a hostile takeover bid by Torchmark Corp., reported higher second-quarter earnings; these earnings, the company says, indicate that it was correct in arguing that Torchmark's $50-a-share bid was too low. For the second quarter, the insurance company reported net income rose to $198 million, or $1.65 a share, compared with profit from continuing operations of $103.4 million, or 82 cents a share. Net income in the year-ago quarter was $190 million, or $1.47 a share. After-tax operating earnings, excluding realized investment gains, rose about 19%, to $117.5 million, or 98 cents a share, from $98.4 million, or 78 cents a share. Revenue rose to $1.2 billion, from $1.05 billion.

"Clearly our earnings substantiate what we have been saying about the underlying earning power of the company, which we feel exceeds the value" of Torchmark's offer, said James D'Agostino Jr., American General vice president and treasurer.

For the six months, the company reported net income of $305.1 million, or $2.55 a share, compared with income from continuing operations of $200.6 million, or $1.59 a share. For the 1989 six months, the company reported net income of $287.2 million, or $2.24 a share. Revenue increased to $2.24 billion, from $2.06 billion.

The company, which also said it has been approached by a number of domestic and international companies interested in buying American General, has started providing due diligence information to at least one interested party. Although American General beat back an effort by Torchmark to place its own nominees on American's board of directors in May, the company announced at its annual meeting that it was placing itself on the block anyway.
