Navistar Weighs Making an Offer For Mack Trucks

  • Author: Richard Koenig and James P. Miller
  • Date: 07/20/90

Navistar International Corp. is considering a bid for Mack Trucks Inc. that would compete with one already under way from Renault Vehicules Industriels S.A. Renault Vehicules already owns a 45% Mack stake, and it recently launched a $6-a-share, or $99 million, tender offer for the 55% of the Allentown, Pa., truck maker it doesn't currently control.

Navistar, among the U.S.'s biggest truck manufacturers, approached a committee of Mack's directors to express interest in a possible bid that would exceed Renault's offer.

However, Navistar said it couldn't proceed with an offer unless Renault decided to dispose of its interest in Mack, an action Renault said in a news release that it "has no interest" in taking.

Moreover, the truck-making unit of Regie Nationale des Usines Renault could increase its stake in Mack to 61% from 45% by exercising a warrant and converting certain debentures it owns.

In Allentown, a Mack spokesman declined to comment beyond the joint statement with Renault. Officials at Renault, based in Paris, couldn't be reached for comment.

Navistar confirmed that it had made the overture and said it will need to make a comprehensive due-diligence examination of Mack "to determine whether to make a firm offer and a specific per-share price."

The Chicago-based truck and diesel-engine maker declined to elaborate on its possible interest in financially strapped Mack.

Mack's board, four of whose nine members are Renault officials, hasn't responded to Renault's offer. Renault has said it wants to take full ownership of the company because of Mack's deteriorating condition and the need to refinance its debt.

Yesterday, Mack, which still faces a sagging market for its heavy-duty trucks, reported that it had a loss of $63.3 million, or $2.13 a share, in the second quarter on sales of $400.9 million. In national over-the-counter trading, Mack common closed unchanged at $7.125. The Mack statement came out after the market closed.

Like Mack, Navistar has seen sales and earnings suffer from a deep industry slump. In its first quarter, ended Jan. 31, it had a loss of $18 million, or 10 cents a share; second-quarter earnings were down 77% at $8 million, or one cent a share. Its six-month sales were off 11% at $1.81 billion.
