Toronto Market Suspends Work on Trading System

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/20/90

TORONTO -- The Toronto Stock Exchange said it suspended development of a comprehensive trading system because of a drop in trading volume. The new system, at an estimated cost of 7 million Canadian dollars (US$6.1 million), would have replaced four separate software systems at the exchange. About C$1.5 million has been spent on the project, which was set to be introduced in 1992.

Volume at the exchange has dropped this year to an average of 12,900 trades a day from 1989's daily average of 16,250 trades. The highest turnover in recent years was in 1987, when the exchange had an average of 22,300 trades a day.

Olaf Kraulis, vice president of information systems at the exchange, said work on the new system might resume if the number of daily trades increases to the 20,000 to 30,000 level.

Mr. Kraulis said the new system would have the advantages of higher capacity, reduced maintenance costs and greater adaptability to new trading rules. The exchange currently uses separate systems to monitor trades, depending on the size of the trade and whether it is executed on the trading floor or on an electronic screen.
