Business Brief -- Colgate-Palmolive Co.: Consumer-Products Firm Posts 11.3% Increase in Net

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/20/90

Colgate-Palmolive Co.'s second-quarter profit increased 11.3%, bolstered by a more profitable mix of products world-wide, price hikes and declining raw material costs. The New York-based consumer-products company posted net income of $81.9 million, or $1.17 a share, up from $73.6 million or $1.06 a share, a year earlier. Colgate's preferred dividend payments totaled $4.9 million during the quarter, compared with $717,000. The increase is due to the formation in June 1989 of a class of preferred that pays a high dividend. Colgate's quarterly sales rose to $1.5 billion from $1.3 billion.

The results reflected the "growing strength of our world-wide distribution system," said Reuben Mark, Colgate's chairman and chief executive officer. Colgate said its major units in most markets generated higher operating profit. Operations in Europe and Mexico were particularly strong, the company said. The company's gross profit margin during the quarter rose to 44.9% from 42.7% a year earlier.

Colgate's net for the first half of 1990 was $163.2 million, or $2.32 a share, compared with $140.8 million, or $2.04 a share. Sales increased to $2.8 billion from $2.5 billion.
