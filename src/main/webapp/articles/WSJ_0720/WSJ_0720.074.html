Primerica Reports Earnings Climbed 53% In Second Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/20/90

NEW YORK -- Primerica Corp. reported second-quarter earnings climbed 53% on 11% higher revenue, led by the consumer finance operations, where profit rose 29% on a revenue increase of 37%. The financial-services company's net income reached $96.9 million, or 84 cents a share, from $63.4 million, or 65 cents a share, in the year-earlier quarter. Revenue advanced to $1.55 billion, from $1.40 billion. Shares outstanding grew 19%, to 117.9 million, as Primerica issued stock to buy the 30.2% minority interest in its A.L. Williams insurance subsidiary.

"The range of estimates had been 75 to 82 cents" a share, so the quarter "clearly was better than expected," said Allerton Cushman Jr., analyst at Morgan Stanley & Co.

Primerica's latest results reflect gains of $9.7 million from the sale of 6.3 million shares of its Fingerhut Cos. subsidiary in a May initial public offering, and $2.6 million from the sale of part of its investment in reinsurer Transatlantic Holdings Inc., which also completed its first public offering during the quarter. The bottom line also includes $11.1 million of losses in Primerica's investment portfolios.

"The only place where earnings came in below target," Mr. Cushman said, was in specialty life and health insurance, which posted a 27% drop. The segment, which includes auto insurance, was hurt by weakness in the auto industry and by a higher tax rate; it also faced comparison with a strong year-earlier period. Primerica has been examining the cost structure and taking other restructuring measures at the segment, a spokesman said.

Traders worried, however, about an inquiry by the U.S. Attorney's Office in Jacksonville, Fla., into life-insurance sales practices at A.L. Williams. Primerica's shares fell $1.625, to close at $34.625 yesterday in New York Stock Exchange composite trading.
