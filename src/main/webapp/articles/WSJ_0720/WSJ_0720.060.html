Marketing & Media: Tales of Trump's Support Were Greatly Exaggerated

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/20/90

Last month, USA Today carried a major article reporting that a call-in poll found widespread support for troubled tycoon Donald Trump. Yesterday, the newspaper said, Oops, never mind. The article last month reported that 81% of the 6,406 50-cent calls to a 900 number agreed with the statement "Donald Trump symbolizes what makes the USA a great country." The article carried a three-column picture of Mr. Trump, with the headline "You like him] You really like him]"

Yesterday, however, Gannett Co.'s USA Today reported that "you" turned out to be one person. It seems financier Carl H. Lindner Jr. stacked the deck in Mr. Trump's favor.

Fully 72% of all the calls in the poll came from two numbers at Great American Insurance Co., a subsidiary of Mr. Lindner's American Financial Corp. Without those calls, the results would have been solidly against Mr. Trump -- who said at the time that he was "greatly honored" by the tally and looked forward to "justifying the public's confidence."

The initial USA Today article had cautioned that the calling patterns suggested someone might be stuffing the ballot box and said that such unscientific polls were "strictly for fun, anyway." Peter S. Prichard, editor of USA Today, said in an interview that the skewed results would make the paper more careful about how it handles call-ins in the future, especially if anything unusual appears in the results. He added, though, that the paper, which has done quite a few call-ins, still feels that they "are a good way to involve readers and see what their sentiments are on some issues."

Yesterday's article quoted a spokeswoman for Great American Insurance as explaining the calls by saying, "Trump is one example of the entrepreneurial spirit. . . . We disapprove of an attitude that we think is harmful of that entrepreneurial spirit and disapprove when the media and other parties continue to discredit unnecessarily the people that are visible leaders of this country."
