Business Brief -- E.W. Scripps Co.: Scripps Howard to Purchase WMAR-TV of Baltimore

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/20/90

Scripps Howard Broadcasting Co., an 80%-owned subsidiary of the E.W. Scripps Co., said it has agreed to purchase WMAR-TV in Baltimore from the Gillett Group Inc. for $154.7 million. The station, an affiliate of National Broadcasting Co., which is a unit of General Electric Co., recently signed a new three-year contract to continue as the flagship station for the Baltimore Orioles baseball team.

The Gillett Group is controlled by George N. Gillett Jr., who for several years has been trying to sell his TV stations to pay off junk-bond debt. Mr. Gillett, a Denver businessman, also controls SCI Television Inc., which has had to restructure $1.3 billion in debt.

Lawrence A. Leser, president and chief executive officer of E.W. Scripps, said the company has not made a major acquisition since 1986, "preferring to wait until an attractive opportunity presented itself."

Cincinnati-based Scripps Howard Broadcasting currently operates nine television stations.
