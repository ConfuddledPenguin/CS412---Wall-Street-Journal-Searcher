Alyeska Takes Steps to Cut Flow in Its Alaska Pipeline

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/20/90

NEW YORK -- The Alyeska Pipeline Service Co. said steps were being taken to reduce oil flow in the Trans-Alaska Pipeline to 1.2 million barrels a day from 1.6 million barrels a day. The action came as the company continued to negotiate a settlement yesterday with the Alaska Department of Environmental Conservation and the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency in compliance with air quality issues. The EPA had set a deadline of 12:01 a.m. Alaska time today to resolve these issues or face the risk of noncompliance, Alyeska said.

James Hermiller, president of Alyeska, said the reduced flow would allow for an orderly shutdown of the entire pipeline, should that become necessary. The EPA said Alyeska has "no reason . . . to consider shutting down." Regulators and oil-industry officials consider a shutdown unlikely.

Mr. Hermiller said, "We fully expect to reach resolution of this matter. Conceptually, all concerns by the agencies have been addressed. What remains is formal approval of these agreements by midnight tonight."

The critical step, according to Alyeska, is confirmation from the Alaska department, which will amend Alyeska's air quality permits. This would put Alyeska in compliance with the EPA's notice of violation. Alyeska said it has agreed to more stringent amendments to its existing permits to bring the company into compliance.
