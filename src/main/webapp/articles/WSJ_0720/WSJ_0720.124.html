Treasury Lifts Its Estimate For 3rd-Period Borrowing

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/20/90

WASHINGTON -- Calculating for loans to the thrift-bailout agency, the Treasury revised the estimate of its borrowing needs for the current quarter to a range of $62 billion to $66 billion. At its quarterly refunding May 2, the Treasury had estimated its borrowing needs in the range of $30 billion to $35 billion for the July-September quarter. Both figures assume a cash balance of $30 billion on Sept. 30, but the earlier figures didn't count any lending by the Treasury's Federal Financing Bank to the Resolution Trust Corp. The RTC is the agency that is running the savings and loan bailout.

Yesterday, the RTC Oversight Board said a new operating plan authorizes it to borrow as much as $34 billion in new working capital in the July-September quarter. The plan also authorizes the RTC to roll over $26.6 billion in working capital borrowed in the prior quarter.

The Treasury's third-quarter net market borrowing will be a record if it exceeds $63.3 billion, which was tapped in the fourth quarter of 1985.

The RTC uses the working capital to carry the assets of insolvent thrifts until they can be sold.

Peter Monroe, president of the Oversight Board, told reporters that Congress will have to approve new financing for the thrift bailout operation before the end of the current session if the thrift rescue is to continue to proceed at an orderly pace.
