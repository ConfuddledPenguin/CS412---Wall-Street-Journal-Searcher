SEC Files Motion For Civil Contempt Against Reynolds, Firm

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/20/90

LOS ANGELES -- The Securities and Exchange Commission filed a motion for civil contempt against Richard G. Reynolds for failure to comply with a court order to pay $5.7 million related to his alleged sale of unregistered securities. In December, a federal judge here found that Mr. Reynolds, an investment advisor, and his firm, raised some $3.8 million from the sale of unregistered securities. Mr. Reynolds, who has denied the allegations, is appealing the court's ruling to the 9th Circuit Court of Appeals.

Pending the outcome of the appeal, Mr. Reynolds and the SEC stipulated in February that he and his firm, R.G. Reynolds Enterprises, would disgorge funds raised from the securities sales, plus interest. The funds were to be invested in an interest-bearing account until the appeal is resolved.

"The defendants have failed to pay any part of the disgorgement and have not provided the commission with any evidence demonstrating their ability to pay," the SEC said in its filing in federal court Wednesday.

The motion, set to be heard on Sept. 24, asks the court to find the defendants in contempt and to incarcerate Mr. Reynolds and/or assess a daily fine against him and his firm until they pay the $5.7 million. Neither Mr. Reynolds, nor his attorney, could be reached.
