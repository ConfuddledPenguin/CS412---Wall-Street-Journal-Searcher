Integrated Wins Order Blocking CRI's Purchase Bid

  • Author: Robin Goldwyn Blumenthal
  • Date: 07/20/90

NEW YORK -- A federal judge overseeing Integrated Resources Inc.'s bankruptcy-law proceeding temporarily blocked efforts by CRI Inc. to buy four real estate investment partnerships managed by Integrated. At a hearing in U.S. Bankruptcy Court, Judge Howard C. Buschman III granted Integrated's request for a preliminary injunction delaying two lawsuits CRI filed in Delaware and California state courts against Integrated -- which manages more than 600 partnerships -- in connection with CRI's proposed bid for the partnership interests. CRI is seeking to gain control of the interests by approaching the investors in the limited partnerships directly, outside a court-approved auction process to sell the interests.

The order effectively gives Integrated, which has an agreement pending to sell its interests in the partnerships directly to Krupp Cos., until Aug. 1 to "straighten out the deal with Krupp," Judge Buschman said. The judge indicated a condition that was added to the agreement with Krupp after CRI announced its bid would have to be addressed.

Although Judge Buschman said "it would be a detriment to other bidders {in the auction} to renegotiate the terms after the bids are in," he said it would be "appropriate" to address the limited issue of renegotiating that condition.

As part of the court-approved auction, Integrated agreed June 7 to sell its general partner interests in its American Insured Mortgage Investors series of limited partnerships to Krupp for $45 million. But after CRI's bid was announced, a condition was added to Integrated's agreement with Krupp that CRI's proposal be ended and its lawsuits dropped. Integrated announced that accord June 15.

However, on June 12, CRI, a real estate syndicator based in Rockville, Md., filed a proposal with the Securities and Exchange Commission to offer to swap interests in two of its real estate ventures for the AIM partnership interests.

Allan M. Pepper, an attorney for Integrated, argued that CRI's actions would undermine Integrated's efforts at reorganization and the auction process. He said the company now will move to develop "a strategy toward a closing with Krupp."

Stephen D. Weinroth, Integrated chairman, said the company plans to seek a permanent injunction to block CRI's proposed exchange offer. Mr. Weinroth said Integrated was encouraged by the judge's statements "that the facts presented raised the inference that CRI may have intentionally interfered" with the court-approved auction.

Larry Gerber, president of Krupp, said it plans to "go forward" and that Krupp has had discussions with Integrated about an amendment to the purchase accord.

Attorneys for CRI wouldn't comment.
