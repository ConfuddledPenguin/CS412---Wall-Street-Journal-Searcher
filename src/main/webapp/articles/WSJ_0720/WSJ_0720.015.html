First City Bancorp Reports Its Earnings In 2nd Period Fell 30%

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/20/90

HOUSTON -- First City Bancorp. of Texas said its earnings fell 30% in the second quarter, marking the first such decline since the bank was rescued in April 1988 by an investment group led by A. Robert Abboud. The drop in net income, to $19.8 million, or 76 cents per share, from $28.1 million, or $1.19 a share last year, closely tracked a forecast by First City late last month. That early warning took away most of the sting. First City shares closed at $21.75 in composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange yesterday, up 12.5 cents.

Analysts don't expect the bad news to be the last at First City. Like most banks, the Houston bank is wrestling with slow demand for credit and rising problem loans. First City's nonperforming loans grew by $11 million, to $199 million, or 2.5% of its loan portfolio, the bank said. The rise reflects continued problems in First City's $349 million in loans to leveraged buy-outs. Such highly leveraged transactions are the focus of national scrutiny by banking regulators.

The sale of First City's credit card business also contributed to the earnings decline. Mr. Abboud said the bank was unable to replace earnings on that high-yield business. "However, during the quarter the trend was favorable," he said. The net yield rose in May and June and averaged 3.82% for the quarter as a whole, down from 4.15% the first quarter. "The decline in net margin is the primary difference in the earnings shortfall," said Frank Anderson, an analyst with Stephens Inc., a Little Rock, Ark., investment banking firm.

Mr. Abboud said that cost cutting is First City's first priority. He attributed part of the earnings decline to a consolidation of operations already underway.

Separately, Citibank said First City had sold its credit card processing business to Citicorp Acceptance Services. Under the terms, the Citicorp subsidiary will take over processing of transactions made at First City's 14,000 merchant locations. First City will continue to sell Citicorp processing services on a commission basis.
