Business Brief -- Westinghouse Electric Corp.: Investment in Firm to Buy Nuclear Power Plant Is Set

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/20/90

Westinghouse Electric Corp., Pittsburgh, said it will invest $20 million in a company that will purchase a nuclear power plant in South Haven, Mich. While Westinghouse is a major supplier of nuclear-plant technology, the move marks the company's first ownership interest in a commercial nuclear plant. The diversified company has broadcasting, financial services and manufacturing lines.

Westinghouse will have a 22% equity interest in Palisades Generating Co., which was formed two years ago to buy the 768-megawatt nuclear generating plant. A megawatt equals 1,000 kilowatts; a kilowatt-hour is the amount of electrical energy consumed when 1,000 watts are used for one hour. The purchase is expected to completed next year.

The other principal investors in Palisades Generating are Bechtel Group Inc., a San Francisco-based engineering and construction firm, with a 33% stake, and the Consumers Power Co. unit of CMS Energy Corp., with a 44% equity interest. Consumers Power, Jackson, Mich., owns and operates the plant, which began commercial operations in 1971.
