Bell Firms Post Mixed Results For 2nd Quarter

  • Author: Pamela J. Podger
  • Date: 07/20/90

Nynex Corp., BellSouth Corp. and Ameritech posted solid gains in second-quarter profit, spurred by cellular and access lines growth. But Southwestern Bell Co. reported flat earnings, and Pacific Telesis Group posted an 8.9% drop. Results were generally what analysts anticipated. "Growth is very much in line with expectations," said Frank Governali, an analyst with First Boston Corp. "Ameritech was the only one with any surprise at all."

Nynex, which took a $39 million onetime charge for an early retirement program, reported a 7.7% increase in net income due to gains in its telephone operations, mobile communications and software subsidiaries. Revenue rose to $3.44 billion from $3.3 billion. About 4,000 managers of New York-based Nynex signed up for the special early-retirement program. "It looks like Nynex had slightly more and earlier cost reductions than expected," said Dan Reingold, an analyst at Morgan Stanley & Co.

Profit of Chicago-based Ameritech surprised some analysts, surging 11.3% with strong volume growth in access lines, toll calls and minutes of use. A tight rein on costs helped to boost net income to $352.3 million. During the quarter, Ameritech and Bell Atlantic Corp., along with two New Zealand companies, were chosen by the New Zealand government to buy the state-owned telephone company for about $2.4 billion.

BellSouth's net income rose 6.5%, but revenue growth, up 5.7%, was damped by rate reductions in several Southeast states. The Atlanta-based company's cellular unit added 62,355 customers in the quarter and reported income of $13.1 million compared with a $2.6 million loss the year earlier.

Southwestern Bell's net income edged up 0.4%, despite a 1.9% drop in phone company income due to rate cutbacks and a one-time $18.1 million pretax charge for inventory. Results were helped by 71,000 new cellular customers added during the period, more than twice the number of new customers that signed up the year before. Without the write-down for inventory, St. Louis-based Southwestern's earnings would be 90 cents a share, compared with 83 cents the year before.

Pacific Telesis blamed a new rate structure in California, investment in network improvements, labor contracts and depreciation of equipment. Earnings for the quarter were $287 million, or 72 cents a share, compared with $325 million, or 79 cents a share, the year before. Pacific Telesis, based in San Francisco, reported revenue of $2.43 billion, vs. $2.4 billion.

In composite trading yesterday on the New York Stock Exchange, Nynex closed at $77.75, down 37.5 cents; BellSouth closed at $52.25, down 25 cents; Southwestern Bell closed at $53.875, unchanged; Ameritech closed at $60.625, up 50 cents, and Pacific Telesis closed at $43, down 12.5 cents.
