Abreast of the Market: Stock Prices Close Mixed as Rally In Oil Issues Offsets Further Selling

  • Author: David Wilson
  • Date: 07/20/90

NEW YORK -- Stock prices closed widely mixed as a rally in oil stocks and waves of futures-related buy programs offset further selling inspired by disappointing earnings. The technology and retailing sectors were hit especially hard as Tandem Computers and U.S. Shoe, respectively, joined the list of stocks to be battered by earnings-related sell-offs.

But the Dow Jones Industrial Average, which showed a loss of more than 23 points at midday, bounced back to finish with a gain of 12.13 at 2993.81. Five of the 30 component stocks in the average are players in the oil industry; without the gains in those issues, the industrial average would have been little changed.

Broader indexes also managed to close on the plus side, as Standard & Poor's 500-Stock Index tacked on 1.10 to 365.32, the Dow Jones Equity Market Index gained 0.86 to 339.05 and the New York Stock Exchange Composite Index added 0.55 to 199.38.

Declining stocks outpaced advancers by 858 to 621 on the New York Stock Exchange, reflecting the broad-based selling that hit the market through the first half of the session. A midday flurry of sell programs tied to stock-index arbitrage helped send prices to their lows.

"A lot of the volume started to dry up when we were at the bottom," said Dale Tills, manager of institutional equities trading at Charles Schwab & Co., San Francisco. "There weren't a lot of people hitting the exits."

Once the arbitrage selling subsided, oil stocks began to show strength and the market's rebound got under way. Among the oil issues in the Dow industrials, Exxon advanced 1 1/2 to 50, Texaco gained 1 3/4 to 61 1/2 and Chevron added 1 1/2 to 76 3/4. Du Pont, the parent of Conoco, went up 1 to 40 3/4; USX, the owner of Marathon Oil, gained 3/4 to 34 3/8.

Market indexes then turned higher in the final hour amid index-arbitrage buying, which involved the purchase of baskets of stocks and the sale of index-futures contracts to lock in profits from short-lived differences in price between the two.

Traders said the buy programs focused on the Chicago Board of Trade's Major Market Index futures, a target of program trading earlier in the week as well. The MMI, when active, has a major impact on the Dow industrials because it includes 17 of the average's 30 stocks.

July contracts on the MMI expire at the close of trading today. As of Tuesday's close, there were still 9,606 of the contracts outstanding; the so-called open interest in the futures has been rising in recent weeks, rather than falling in advance of the expiration, as is usually the case.

Against that backdrop, the late wave of arbitrage buying represented "just the lead-in to the expiration," said Christopher Pedersen, head of trading at Twenty-First Securities. He added that some institutions "are lining up to be sellers" of the MMI futures late today, based on the assumption that index-arbitrage buying will allow them to sell easily.

Tandem skidded 1 7/8 to 19 5/8 and ranked as the most active Big Board issue; composite trading totaled more than 4.8 million shares. Although its net income for the June quarter rose 5.5% from a year earlier, earnings per share dropped slightly, rather than rising as analysts had expected.

International Business Machines, boosted by the market's late recovery, rose 1/8 to 120. But elsewhere in the technology sector, Compaq Computer fell 7/8 to 63 1/2 on 1.4 million shares, Digital Equipment lost 5/8 to 82 7/8, Motorola slid 1 1/8 to 84 3/4 and Hewlett-Packard eased 1/4 to 47 1/4.

U.S. Shoe plummeted 4 1/4 to 18 on two million shares -- nearly 10 times the stock's average daily volume. Citing weaker sales at its LensCrafters subsidiary, the company said second-quarter earnings may fall short of the year-earlier level.

Wal-Mart Stores fell 1/4 to 33 1/2 on 3.3 million shares. Among other retailing issues, Limited Inc. dropped 5/8 to 23 as 1.1 million shares changed hands, TJX eased 1/2 to 14 3/8 and Circuit City Stores skidded 1 7/8 to 26.

Environmental stocks, especially popular lately with investors looking for growth areas, ran into selling despite earnings reports that were in line with analysts' projections. Waste Management eased 1/8 to 42 5/8 and Browning-Ferris Industries dropped 1 7/8 to 43 5/8.

BankAmerica, whose second-quarter earnings fell 12% from the year-ago level, lost 7/8 to 26 3/4 on 2.8 million shares. HomeFed plunged 4 3/4 to 14 1/4 after posting a loss of $108.2 million for the second quarter, reflecting a surprisingly large provision for loan losses.

Another California-based financial institution, First Interstate Bancorp, fell 2 7/8 to 37. The company disclosed to analysts that the state's real-estate market is beginning to show signs of weakness and that nonperforming assets at its California operations "are likely to increase" if the pattern continues. In the wake of that assessment, Wells Fargo fell 1 7/8 to 76 7/8, and Security Pacific lost 1 1/8 to 34 3/4.

American Express, whose second-quarter earnings were on the high end of expectations, added 1/4 to 31 1/4. The company reported that net income rose 1.1%, with its Shearson Lehman brokerage unit posting a $51 million profit for the period.

But Primerica, whose net jumped 53% in the quarter, declined 1 5/8 to 34 5/8. The U.S. attorney in Jacksonville, Fla., is investigating life-insurance sales practices at the company's ALW Marketing unit.

The oil-stock rally came amid news that the amount of oil carried through the Trans-Alaska Pipeline is being cut by 25% to 1.2 million barrels a day. The prospect of reduced supply pushed August crude-oil futures up 44 cents a barrel, to $18.99, in New York trading.

Mobil climbed 2 1/4 to 65 3/4 and Amoco added 1 5/8 to 54. Schlumberger, which reported a 58% increase in second-quarter operating earnings, added 1 1/8 to 64 1/4; among other oil-services issues, Baker Hughes gained 1 to 29 1/2, Halliburton went up 1 to 51 3/8 and and Parker Drilling rose 1/4 to 9 1/2.

The American Stock Exchange Market Value Index fell 0.22 to 361.84. Volume totaled 12,110,000 shares.
