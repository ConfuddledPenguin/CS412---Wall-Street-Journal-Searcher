Business Brief -- Martin Marietta Corp.: Postal Service Awards Job For 614 Sorting Machines

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/20/90

Martin Marietta Corp., Bethesda, Md., received a $138 million contract from the U.S. Postal Service to make 614 automated mail-sorting machines, the company's second such job, as part of the agency's multiphase initiative to automate sorting fully by 1995 at a cost of $2.5 billion. The company said the accord signals its efforts to find civilian applications for its defense and aerospace technologies to hedge against defense spending cuts.

Martin Marietta's contract is for a new, high-speed sorting machine that uses barcode technology to process first-class and third-class mail by ZIP code. The Postal Service uses bar-code machines to process 51% of its mail; the remainder is handled manually or with mechanized sorters. Martin Marietta's Information Systems Group will deliver the machines by October 1992, with the first delivery expected in late summer of 1991. It has options to build an unspecified number of additional machines; they were developed under a joint licensing pact between Martin Marietta and closely held National Presort Inc., a Dallas-based technology concern.
