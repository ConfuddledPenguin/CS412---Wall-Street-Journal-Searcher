Lancer Receives New Credit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/20/90

NEW YORK -- Lancer Industries Inc. said it received $135 million in new credit, mostly from banks, to refinance a bridge loan made in its acquisition of Fairfield Manufacturing Co. The new financing consists of $105 million from a group of banks led by Bank of America, and $30 million in subordinated debt issued to institutional investors, Lancer said. The bridge loan is with Merrill Lynch Capital Markets.

Lancer, formerly known as McLean Industries Inc., said it also completed a $25 million financing with Westinghouse Credit Corp. by issuing subordinated notes.

Lancer Chairman Paul S. Levy said the new financing lowers the company's debt service levels and provides "long-term financing for future operations and our acquisition program." The company's businesses include specialty packaging and textile printing.
