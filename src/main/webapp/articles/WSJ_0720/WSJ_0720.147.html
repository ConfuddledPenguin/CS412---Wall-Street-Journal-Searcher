Program Trading Is 10.9% Of NYSE Volume in Week

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/20/90

NEW YORK -- Program trading accounted for 10.9% of New York Stock Exchange volume, or 13.6 million shares a day, in the week ended July 6. That was down from 13.7% the previous week, the Big Board said in its weekly report on the computer-aided trading strategies.

The biggest program traders during the week, accounting for 66% of all program trading on the exchange, were General Electric Co.'s Kidder, Peabody Group Inc. (10.2 million shares), Morgan Stanley Group Inc. (8.2 million), Merrill Lynch & Co. (6.1 million), Salomon Inc.'s Salomon Brothers (5.9 million) and Susquehanna Investment Group (5.6 million).

Program trading is the purchase or sale of a group of at least 15 and often hundreds of different stocks. Stock-index arbitrage, which accounted for 45% of all program trading in the week ended July 6, involves stock purchases and sales with offsetting trades in futures and options.

Kidder, Morgan, Salomon and Susquehanna accounted for about 76% of all stock-index arbitrage for the week.
