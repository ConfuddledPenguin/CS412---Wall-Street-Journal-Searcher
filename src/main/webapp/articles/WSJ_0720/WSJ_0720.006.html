Unisys Earnings Plummeted 78% In 2nd Quarter

  • Author: Laurence Hooper
  • Date: 07/20/90

Unisys Corp. said second-quarter earnings fell 78% on lower revenue, but the computer company reiterated its hope that a strong fourth quarter would put it in the black for the year. The results came in slightly below expectations, however, and securities analysts said Unisys has a tough row to hoe -- especially in light of the company's acknowledgement that growth in its international business slowed while domestic business remained depressed.

"You kind of feel for these guys," said Dan Benton of Goldman, Sachs & Co. "They're smart guys and they know what they have to do, but they really have their work cut out for them."

Mr. Benton's comments reflected a new, almost sympathetic attitude on Wall Street toward Unisys' woes, which have been battering the Blue Bell, Pa., company's stock for more than a year. Still, many analysts again cut their estimate of 1990 earnings, to deliver a new consensus of 50 cents to $1 a share, down from 75 cents to $1.50.

In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange, Unisys shares closed at $12.75, up 12.5 cents.

Second-quarter results showed progress in cutting expenses, which dropped 5.2% from a year earlier. Operating profit rose 36%, to $102 million, and Unisys' heavy debt was trimmed by $265 million, with much of that reduction financed by a $150 million sale of preferred stock to Japan's Mitsui & Co.

But gross profit margins reversed the progress they made in the first quarter, dropping to 37% from 39%, "not the most encouraging sign," said Steve Milunovich of First Boston Corp. And revenue was hurt by a tough comparison with 1989, when a new line of mainframes rolled out to pent-up demand. That momentum has faded, replaced by growth in more strategic -- but currently unprofitable -- low-end products based on the Unix operating system.

Earnings in the quarter were $11.8 million, which translated to a loss of nine cents a share after preferred dividend payments. That compared with earnings of $53.6 million, or 17 cents a share, in the period a year ago. Revenue fell 4% to $2.47 billion from $2.57 billion.

In the first half, the company posted earnings of $8.6 million, which translated to a loss of 28 cents a share, compared with a loss of $25.1 million, or 50 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue was virtually unchanged at $4.78 billion.

"We are continuing to pursue our priority goal of profitability for the full year, recognizing that based on a seasonally weak third quarter this requires a strong fourth quarter," James Unruh, Unisys' president and chief executive, said in a statement.

Because of the lackluster quarterly showing, Standard & Poor's Corp. said it will consider lowering its ratings on debt and preferred stock of Unisys and its finance subsidiary. About $4 billion in debt is affected.

S&P said the review covers Unisys' triple-B senior debt, triple-B-minus subordinated debt and double-B-plus preferred stock, and Unisys Finance Corp.'s triple-B senior debt.
