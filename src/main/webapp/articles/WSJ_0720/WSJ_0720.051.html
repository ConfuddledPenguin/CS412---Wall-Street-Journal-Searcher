New Securities Issues

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/20/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: MORTGAGE

Salomon Brothers Mortgage Securities VII Inc. -- $93.8 million of real estate mortgage investment conduit securities are being offered in nine classes by Salomon Brothers Inc. The offering, Senior Mortgage Pass-Through Certificates Series 1990-91, is backed by 15-year and 30-year conventional jumbo whole loans serviced by PHH U.S. Mortgage Corp. with a weighted average coupon rate of 9.25% and weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29.2 years. Details on prices and yields weren't immediately available.

EUROBONDS

Ford Capital BV (U.S. parent) -- $250 million of 9 1/2% bonds due Aug. 9, 2000, at an issue price of 100.96, and a fixed reoffering price of 99.285 via Goldman Sachs International Ltd. Guaranteed by Ford Motor Co.

Ladbroke Group Finance (Jersey) Ltd. (United Kingdom parent) -- #83 million ($150.7 million) of convertible bonds, due June 30, 2005, with coupon fixed at 9% (semiannual) and at a par issue price, via Credit Suisse First Boston Ltd. Guaranteed (subordinated) by Ladbroke Group PLC. Convertible into the preference shares of the issuer that are exchangeable into the ordinary shares of Ladbroke Group. Conversion price 379 pence ($6.88), a 15% premium to the midmarket share price of 330 pence. Callable after the fifth year at par. Exercise period from Aug. 22, 1990, to June 24, 2005.

Kyushu Leasing Service (Japan) -- $75 million of floating-rate notes due August 1997, priced at par via Nikko Securities Ltd. Coupon will float 0.20 percentage point above six-month London interbank offered rate. Guaranteed by Sumitomo Trust & Banking.

Cie. Bancaire (France) -- 10 billion yen ($67.7 million) of 7.4% bonds due Aug. 23, 1993, priced at 101 3/8 via Mitsubishi Finance International Ltd.

Chugoku Marine Paints (Japan) -- 80 million Swiss francs ($56.5 million), privately placed convertible notes due Sept. 30, 1995, at indicated 3 1/2% coupon at par via Citicorp Investment Bank Switzerland. Put option Sept. 30, 1993, at indicated 108 5/8% to yield 6.03%. Conversion price fixed Wednesday at estimated 2.5% premium. Notes reset Aug. 15, 1992, if average of the closing prices for six trading days up to and including Aug. 15, 1992, is at least one yen less than the conversion price in effect. Conversion price then will be the average closing price.

Societe Nationale des Chemins de Fer Francais (France) -- 125 million European currency units of fungible bonds due June 1, 1999, priced at 97.025 via BNP Capital Markets Ltd. Guaranteed by the Republic of France. Fungible with 175 million ECU issue.
