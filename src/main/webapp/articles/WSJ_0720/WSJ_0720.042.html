Air Inter Adjusts for Stoppage

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/20/90

PARIS -- Air Inter, France's main domestic airline, said it canceled 25% of its flights today and tomorrow, because of a scheduled 48-hour strike by pilots and navigators who are seeking more pay. The airline said its small number of flights outside France and its service to Corsica wouldn't be affected. No French city served by the airline will be completely cut off, Air Inter said.

The airline's ground personnel also plan to strike today and tomorrow, but Air Inter didn't cancel any flights until the pilots union announced Wednesday that it would join the work stoppage.
