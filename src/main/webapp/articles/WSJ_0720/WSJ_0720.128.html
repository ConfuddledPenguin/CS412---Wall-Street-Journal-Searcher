U.S. Is Conducting Inquiry Into Practices At Unit of Primerica

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/20/90

NEW YORK -- The U.S. Attorney in Jacksonville, Fla., is conducting an inquiry into the practices of "a small number" of sales representatives at a unit of insurer A.L. Williams, said Primerica Corp., the insurer's parent. The practices relate to a dispute that Primerica, a financial-services company based here, has been litigating for some time with Amerishare Investors Inc., a Jacksonville insurance and mutual-fund company.

Primerica said the U.S. Attorney apparently is looking into whether the sales forces at A.L. Williams and Amerishare made improper efforts to obtain data about or interfere with each other's business.

Arthur L. Williams Jr., the subsidiary's founder and owner until Primerica acquired it last November, has requested a leave of absence from his post as a sales consultant to help resolve the matter, Primerica said.

Consumers and A.L. Williams policyholders haven't been affected by the practices the U.S. Attorney is reviewing, Primerica said, adding that its subsidiary won't likely be exposed to material financial liability.

The U.S. Attorney's Office in Jacksonville wouldn't comment. An attorney for Amerishare said, "I am not aware of any investigation into conduct by Amerishare."
