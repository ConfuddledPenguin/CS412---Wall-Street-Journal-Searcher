Credit Rating: Moody's Lowers Its Ratings on Debt Of Chrysler Corp.

  • Author: Gregory A. Patterson
  • Date: 07/02/90

HIGHLAND PARK, Mich. -- Moody's Investors Service Inc. lowered its rating of Chrysler Corp. debt to Moody's lowest investment grade level. The move affects $22.28 billion of debt. The move follows a downgrading of Chrysler debt ratings by Standard & Poor's Corp. two weeks ago. "This isn't the first shot out of the barrel," a Chrysler spokesman said.

Moody's said its move reflects an assessment that Chrysler will have less "financial flexibility" in the future. Moody's said the auto maker is likely to have weaker earnings and cash flow while incurring large capital and product-development expenditures. Chrysler's first-quarter earnings fell 80% to $71 million, or 32 cents a share, from a year earlier.

Moody's made its announcement after the stock market closed Friday. In New York Stock Exchange composite trading, Chrysler closed unchanged at $15.75.

In a statement, Chrysler said that it was "deeply disappointed" with the downgrading and that the Moody's decision was "not warranted by the facts." The company said it was ironic that Moody's would criticize its aggressive product development plans because U.S. corporations for years have been criticized for taking the short-term view.

The action pushed Chrysler's senior debt rating down one notch above speculative grade. Moody's downgraded ratings on Chrysler senior debt and its shelf registration to Baa-3 from Baa-2. Moody's handed out an identical downgrading to the ratings on senior debt of Chrysler Financial Corp., the company's finance unit and currently its chief profit center.

Chrysler criticized the downgrading of its financial unit's commercial paper. Chrysler Financial has a "continuing record of profitability, and the highest level of cash and marketable securities in the corporation's history," the company said.

The finance unit, which is responsible for $18 billion of the $22.28 billion of debt affected by the ratings move, has posted record earnings for six consecutive years. Chrysler officials are predicting record second-quarter earnings for the unit.
