West Germany Led World In Exports for 1st Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/02/90

WASHINGTON (AP) -- West Germany was the world's biggest exporter during the first three months of 1990, recapturing the lead from the U.S., the International Monetary Fund reported. West Germany increased its exports to $99.8 billion, up more than 18% from the year earlier. The U.S. increase of almost 10% brought its total to $97.2 billion.

In 1989, the U.S. regained first place with $364 billion of exports compared with West Germany's $341 billion. West Germany first forged ahead in 1986.

Experts say the economic unification of West Germany and East Germany, which officially began yesterday, could put the united Germany ahead of the U.S. as the world's biggest exporter for years to come. The IMF didn't give any figures for East Germany, which isn't among its 151 member countries.

Japan trailed in a distant third place with $274 billion of exports for 1989 and $68 billion for this year's first quarter.

The U.S. remained by far the world's biggest importer, bringing in $125 billion in foreign-made goods in the first quarter, up from $118 billion a year earlier. West Germany's imports also increased, to $78 billion from $65 billion.

Japan, striving to reduce complaints about its big export surplus, was the only one of the seven major industrial countries to report a drop in exports for the quarter. Japan still had a $12 billion surplus for the period, second only to West Germany's.
