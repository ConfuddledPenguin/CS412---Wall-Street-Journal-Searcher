Moody's Downgrades Ratings on Junk Bonds Of Charter Medical

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/02/90

NEW YORK -- Moody's Investors Service Inc. lowered ratings on about $1 billion of Charter Medical Corp.'s high-yield, high-risk junk bonds. The ratings firm cited volatility in demand for pyschiatric services and Charter's possible difficulties in refinancing debt. Moody's reduced Charter's senior debt ratings to single-B-2 from single-B-1, and its subordinated and junior subordinated debentures to Caa from single-B-3. In addition, Moody's lowered Charter's preferred stock rating to Ca from single-B-3, and affirmed Charter's senior subordinated debt at single-B-3. Last March, Moody's had placed all of Charter's credit ratings under review for possible downgrade.

Charter, based in Macon, Ga., is one of the largest operators of psychiatric hospitals in the U.S. and also operates 13 general hospitals. A Charter spokesman declined to comment on the dowgrades, except to say, "We're very positive" about the company's prospects.

Moody's said Charter's lower credit quality relates to its "limited prospects for a rebound in profitability, to the newly demonstrated potential for volatility in demand for psychiatric services, and to the increased risk facing highly leveraged companies with refinancing needs."

The ratings firm concluded, "Plans to reduce construction of new hospitals will increase discretionary cash flow through 1992. However, meeting future years' bank debt amortization obligations is likely to require external sourcing of funds which Moody's believes represents higher risk for the company in the present financial environment."
