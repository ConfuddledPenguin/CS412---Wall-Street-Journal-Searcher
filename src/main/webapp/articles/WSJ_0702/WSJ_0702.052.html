A June 22 letter from Czech Presi- dent Vaclav Havel to Cuban leader Fidel Castro:

I was sorry to hear that the court in Havana gave long custodial sentences yesterday to seven representatives of the opposition. Their only crime was that they demanded that Cuba be launched on the path toward pluralism, democracy, and a market economy: that is, on the path which is followed today by all former totalitarian countries of Central and Eastern Europe.

Mr. President, I firmly protest against the decision of Cuban justice, which I see as another step of your country toward the darkest past, as a gross violation of basic human rights, and as an insult to the civilized world. I, therefore, request that you ask for an immediate release of all unjustly condemned.
