Bell Atlantic, Telefonica Win Argentine Phone Bids

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 07/02/90

BUENOS AIRES -- Bell Atlantic Corp. of Philadelphia and Telefonica S.A. of Spain were awarded a combined 60% share in Argentina's state telephone company, Empresa Nacional de Telecommunicaciones, or Entel, under a privatization plan. President Carlos Menem signed the decree, saying the two companies' bids were the highest when bids were opened last week.

A formal takeover is scheduled for Oct. 8. Before then, 30% of the new company will be offered to investors and the remaining 10% given to employees.

Bell Atlantic, a regional telecommunications company, and Telefonica, the Spanish state monopoly, paid a combined $214 million in cash and $5 billion in debt papers for Entel, which has 3.2 million phone lines and 45,000 employees.

The two companies pledged to pay an additional $379 million more within six years, and to invest several hundred million dollars in the new enterprise.

"This is a moment of triumph," said President Menem, who has made privatizations the centerpiece of his efforts to introduce free-market revisions to Argentina's state-dominated economy.
