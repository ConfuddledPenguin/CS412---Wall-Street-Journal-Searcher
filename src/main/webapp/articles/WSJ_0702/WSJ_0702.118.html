Business Brief -- Cool Light Co.: GTE Unit Ordered to Pay Damages of $16 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/02/90

A federal court jury in Boston awarded Cool Light Co. of Hollywood, Calif., $16 million in punitive and compensatory damages to be paid by GTE Products Corp., a unit of GTE Corp., Stamford, Conn., for fraud, breach of contract and failure to deal "fairly and in good faith." Cool Light, a maker of television studio lights that generate 80% less heat than traditional studio lights, alleged that GTE Products, a Danvers, Mass., lighting products maker, breached a contract to make parts of Cool Light's product and subsequently produced its own version of the light. Cool Light brought in a host of celebrities, including the actors Michael Landon, Ed Asner and Angie Dickinson, to testify in the trial on behalf of the light's inventor, George Panagiotou.

GTE Products officials denied any wrongdoing and said they are considering an appeal. "We're disappointed at the jury's verdict and a little puzzled, because we continue to believe that we did nothing wrong," Vincent D'Alessandro, a GTE Products vice president, said.
