International Brief -- Matsushita Electric Industrial Co.: Acquisition of 25.1% Stake In West German Firm Set

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/02/90

Matsushita Electric Industrial Co. of Japan said it agreed to acquire a 25.1% equity stake in Loewe Opta G.m.b.H., a West German maker of consumer electronic products. Terms weren't disclosed. "By becoming a partner with Loewe, Matsushita would like to form an industrial partnership with the German company involving a joint development of digital TV and other areas of color TV production," said Tadakazu Yamamoto, a Matsushita managing director in charge of the company's color television sector.

Matsushita officials said acquiring the Loewe stake is part of Matsushita's program to increase its overseas production to 50% of its entire business outside Japan by 1993. Matsushita already operates 18 overseas plants in 15 countries for TV production; it makes TV sets in Britain.

Loewe, established in 1923 and capitalized at 45 million marks ($26.9 million), makes color TV sets, car electronic equipment and telecommunications products. It has about 1,500 employees and had 1989 sales of 300 million marks, Matsushita officials said.
