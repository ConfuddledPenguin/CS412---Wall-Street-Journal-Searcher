Kimberly-Clark, New York Times Sell Their Newsprint Firm

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/02/90

DALLAS -- Kimberly-Clark Corp. and New York Times Co. said they reached an agreement in principle to sell their jointly owned Canadian newsprint manufacturer to privately held Kruger Inc., a Montreal-based pulp and paper mill operator. Neither company would disclose the sale price for Spruce Falls Power & Paper Co., but Dallas-based Kimberly-Clark said the sale of its 50.5% stake will result in an after-tax charge of about $44 million, or 55 cents a share, in the second quarter. New York Times said it doesn't expect to take a charge because it established a $27 million reserve in last year's fourth quarter to cover possible losses on the sale of its forest products group.

Losses from North American newsprint operations had been hurting both U.S. companies. Kimberly-Clark blamed losses from its North American paper interests for declines in revenue and income for its paper-products lines last year. And the reserve taken by New York Times last year was intended to cover expected losses in newsprint operations beyond Spruce Falls, a spokeswoman said. New York Times has minority interests in two other newsprint mills in Canada and in a paper mill in Maine.

The companies had held their joint interest in the paper company, based in Kapuskasing, Ontario, since 1926.

George Adler, a stock analyst who follows Kimberly-Clark for Smith Barney, Harris Upham & Co., said the sale of the Canadian mill will have "a positive impact on the bottom line," regardless of the expected second-quarter charge.

In composite trading Friday, Kimberly-Clark closed at $71.625, up 75 cents on the New York Stock Exchange and New York Times closed at $24.125, up 25 cents on the American Stock Exchange.
