U.S. Studies Charges That Silverado S&L's Closing Was Delayed

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/02/90

WASHINGTON -- The Treasury Department said it is examining charges by a former thrift regulator that the government delayed closing Silverado Bank, Savings & Loan, Denver, until after the 1988 presidential election because Neil Bush, the president's son, had been a board member. Kermit Mowbray, a former federal regulator in Colorado, said that in the fall of 1988, his office received a phone call ordering the regional regulators to delay closing Silverado until after the November election. Mr. Mowbray, in testimony before a congressional committee two weeks ago, said he couldn't remember who made the call or who actually received it.

Washington S&L regulators have claimed that the huge number of insolvent S&Ls caused a delay in closing dozens of thrifts during that time, including Silverado. Losses at Silverado are expected to total roughly $1 billion.

The Office of Thrift Supervision, which was made an arm of the Treasury under the S&L cleanup law, last week requested the Treasury's inspector general to investigate the charges.

Thrift regulators are currently pursuing an administrative action against Neil Bush, charging that he didn't disclose his business relationship with several large Silverado borrowers while he was a director from 1985 to August 1988. At a news conference last week, President Bush said he has "full confidence in the integrity and honor of my son."
