Honduras Pays Loan Arrearage

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/02/90

WASHINGTON -- Honduras paid the World Bank $152.6 million and the International Monetary Fund about $36 million to clear up loan arrearage at the two international agencies. By making the payments, Honduras regains eligibility for new loans from the two agencies. The World Bank announced the payment Friday, and the IMF plans to make an announcement today.

Seven countries, with about $2.9 billion in overdue loan payments, are still on the World Bank's list of ineligible borrowers, according to a bank official, who identified the countries as: Nicaragua, Liberia, Panama, Peru, Sierra Leone, Syria and Zambia.

The IMF has a list of nine countries with overdue loan payments totaling about $4.4 billion. That list consists of Cambodia, Liberia, Panama, Peru, Sierra Leone, Somalia, the Sudan, Vietnam and Zambia.
