Bank of New England Is Planning to Sell Or Close 95 Branches

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/02/90

BOSTON -- Bank of New England, continuing its retrenchment, said it will sell or close 95 branches, more than half of them in Massachusetts, as a cost-cutting measure. The closings are part of a previously announced plan by the bank to trim overhead by 35% in the next five months in an effort to return to profitability. "By concentrating our focus on locations with the most potential, we will improve customer service while reducing overhead," said Chairman Lawrence K. Fish.

Bank of New England, one of the most aggressive lenders in the region in the 1980s, has been hurt by the deepening real estate slump. It reported a first-quarter loss of $46.6 million, or 68 cents a share, despite one-time gains from the sale of $173 million in assets. It posted a loss of $1.11 billion last year.

In all, 52 branches in Massachusetts and 25 in Connecticut are slated for sale or consolidation, said bank spokesman Thomas L. Lavelle. Closing or sale of another 18 branches, some in rural areas of western Massachusetts, is planned, said Mr. Lavelle. The bank is also planning to sell its subsidiaries in Maine and Rhode Island by year's end. Once all such plans are carried through, the bank's total will have dropped from its current level of 444 branches to 281.

Analysts agree it may be difficult for the bank to sell very many of its branches during the consolidation. "If you want to get a branch cheap, you just pick one up from the (Resolution Trust Corportation)," said Tracey Stangle, an analyst and senior vice president with First Albany Corp. "They'll sell them for nothing, which is about all most New England banks can afford right now."
