Business Brief -- Eliot Savings Bank: Institution Closed, Deposits Go to Boston Bancorp Unit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/02/90

Eliot Savings Bank, Boston, was closed by banking regulators and its deposits were assumed by the South Boston Savings Bank unit of Boston Bancorp. South Boston Savings acquired approximately $400 million in deposits of Eliot Savings from the Federal Deposit Insurance Corp. at a purchase premium of $250,000. None of Eliot Savings' assets, except cash, cash equivalents and marketable securities, were acquired by South Boston. Eliot Savings' three offices were to open for business today as branches of South Boston Savings.

Eliot Savings, with assets of $450 million, was closed by the Massachusetts commissioner of banks and the FDIC as liquidating agent. Boston Bancorp had assets of $1.42 billion as of April 30.

Eliot Savings, which had a $50.5 million loss last year, was hurt by the slump in the real estate market, as were many financial institutions in New England. Analysts have said they expect more bank failures in the region in months to come.
