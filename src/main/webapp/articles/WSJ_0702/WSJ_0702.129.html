Business Brief -- Dense-Pac Microsystems Inc.: Firm Lowers Sales Forecast, Cites Slower Defense Orders

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/02/90

Dense-Pac Microsystems Inc. lowered by as much as 30% its sales forecast for its current fiscal year ending Feb. 1991 to between $7 million and $8 million. The company cited a slowdown in orders in its defense-related and commercial businesses. "Many defense contractors are playing conservative and waiting for (government) funding until they release orders," said Bill Stowell, Dense-Pac's chief financial officer. Defense contractors account for roughly 75% of sales for Garden Grove, Calif.-based Dense-Pac, which designs and makes memory components used in advanced-technology systems such as aircraft, missiles and communications equipment.

Though June is usually a strong month for the company, new orders were weak last month, the company said.

For the fiscal year ended Feb. 28, 1990, Dense-Pac posted a net loss of $183,599, equal to five cents a share, on sales of $7.1 million. The company didn't make a bottom-line forecast for the current fiscal year.
