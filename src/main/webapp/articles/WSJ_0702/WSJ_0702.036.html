Bairnco Corp. Sees Second-Quarter Loss, Sharply Cuts Payout

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/02/90

ORLANDO, Fla. -- Bairnco Corp. said it expects to report a "significant" second-quarter net loss, and the board, as expected, slashed the quarterly dividend. Bairnco, which recently moved here from New York, manufactures specialty laminates and adhesives, shielding chambers to test electronic equipment for emissions, and cutting products, including saws and choppers for butchers and meatpackers.

The expected net loss and dividend cut stem from losses in the shielding unit and the general economic outlook, Bairnco said. In the second quarter a year earlier, Bairnco earned $3.2 million, or 30 cents a share, on $54.2 million of sales.

Bairnco also moved back the target date for spinning off its Keene Corp. unit to shareholders. It now expects to spin off the unit in July; it had hoped to complete the transaction by June 30.

The dividend the company announced -- five cents, down from 25 cents -- doesn't reflect the Keene unit, Bairnco said. Keene's board will take dividend action after the spinoff. The Bairnco dividend is payable July 20 to shareholders of record July 13.

Bairnco faces litigation regarding Keene. Shareholders have filed suit, charging Bairnco misled investors about Keene's potential asbestos liability. For five years ending in 1974, Keene had a unit that made some products containing asbestos; in Bairnco's annual report this year, Bairnco disclosed that the insurance that has covered Keene for asbestos litigation may be in doubt.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading Friday, Bairnco shares fell 50 cents to $6.50.
