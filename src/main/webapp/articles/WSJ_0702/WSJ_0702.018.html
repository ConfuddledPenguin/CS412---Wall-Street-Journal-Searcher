New Securities Issues

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/02/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATE

Home Depot Inc. -- $200 million of 6% convertible subordinated notes due June 15, 1997, at par, via underwriters led by First Boston Corp. The notes are convertible into common at $48.17 a share, subject to adjustment and certain conditions, and will be redeemable on or after June 30, 1992, at the option of Home Depot at prices starting at 104 and declining to par June 15, 1996.

Postal Square L.P. -- $193 million of amortizing notes due June 15, 2022, priced at par to yield 8.95%. The non-callable notes were priced at a spread of 54 basis points above the Treasury's 30-year bond through underwriters led by Goldman, Sachs & Co.

MORTGAGES

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $300 million of Remic mortgage securities offered in 16 classes by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets. The offering, Series 1990-91, is backed by Fannie Mae 9% securities. Among classes for which details were available, yields ranged from 9.25%, or 87 basis points above the rate on five-year Treasury securities, to 10.35%, or 190 basis points over 30-year Treasurys.

EUROBONDS

Kansallis-Osake-Pankki (Finland) -- 300 million Finnish markka of 13 3/8% bonds due Aug. 8, 1995, priced at 101.125 via lead manager Kansallis-Osake-Pankki. Fees 1 7/8.
