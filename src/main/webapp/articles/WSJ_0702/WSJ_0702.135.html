Japan's Wholesale Prices

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/02/90

TOKYO -- Japan's wholesale prices in the second 10 days of June inched up 0.1% from the previous 10 days and rose 0.9% from a year earlier, because of the yen's decline, the Bank of Japan, the central bank, said. The wholesale price index, in which the year 1985 is equal to 100, stood at 90.5 in the latest period. Though domestic prices remained flat from the preceding 10 days, central bank officials said, export and import prices rose, as a result of the Japanese currency's depreciation, and pushed up the overall price level.
