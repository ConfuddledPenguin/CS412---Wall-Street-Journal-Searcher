On the Record: Iraq Weighs Threat of War

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/02/90

The following are excerpts from an interview with Saddam Hussein, president of Iraq, by Karen Elliott House of the Journal. They enlarge on issues discussed in Ms. House's article on page one of this newspaper Thursday. A related editorial appears nearby.

How has the end to the Cold War affected the Mideast?

The positive side is much greater than the negative impact.

The positive side is that this will make the Arab nation one single share and not two to be divided between two superpowers. . . . America can no longer say Arabs are tools of the Soviet Union.

The negative side is that it is now clear the U.S. can exert influence on the Soviets and make them abandon any position contrary to the U.S. So the U.S. thinks it can cast things any way it wants in the region and in alliance with Israel can suppress any voice in support of Arab rights. So, the U.S. has tilted more towards Israel. This is a very grave change.

Can Egypt sit out a war?

Peace is the prevailing theme in the Arab world for all Arabs. Any Arab who wants to defend his rights should not be interpreted as not desiring peace. Egypt is part of the Arab world.

If war were to break out as a result of the expansionist policy of Israel it is only natural that this will be fought by all Arabs. . . . (I)f fighting continues for a long time no Arab individual shall remain capable of remaining outside the circle of contribution to confrontation with Israel.

Israel is accustomed in other wars to snatching quickly from Arabs valuable objectives and this has helped Israel in maintaining its expansionist policies.

So if Israel were to conduct an aggression, then the con- frontation must be long. This is the view of Iraq and Arabs have no different view. Once the fighting continues for a long time the outcome will be different. It will not be what Israel desires.

How can there be a long war when Israel possesses nuclear weapons?

Arabs do have new weapons which may be sufficient for confronting this small population (Israel) if it were to commit aggression against the larger population.

Do you mean chemicals can deter nuclear weapons?

Yes, I believe chemicals constitute a deterrent. Why should the U.S. fail to voice concern when they and others helped Israel possess nuclear weapons? When I said if Israel attacks Iraq with nuclear weapons then Iraq would respond with chemicals, all hell was raised and still is being raised.

This has been treated as a threat. It is as if the objective was that Arabs should accept that Israel has nuclear weapons and can dictate the situation and Arabs should abandon all their rights.

Are you seeking a nuclear capability?

It has neither been in our program nor is it now in our program to use these for military purposes.

Iraq is in possession of binary chemical weapons. Our scientists and technicians and military men calculate that this is sufficient to deter an Israeli nuclear weapon. If the U.S. were to lend us nuclear weapons with the objective of striking a balance with the nuclear weapons given Israel, we shall not decline them.

Do you expect more terrorism?

The U.S. went through a war to create an independent union and civilians were killed. So let us address human issues on a single standard. We cannot talk about those deprived of basic rights as terrorists. The U.S. attacked the house of (Libyan leader Moammar) Gadhafi with women and children inside. The raid killed civilians. Is this terrorism? If Palestinians conduct an attack in the occupied territories, if such Palestinians are regarded as terrorists, we have to regard the president of the United States as the greatest terrorist.

Is war inevitable?

The U.S. is not prepared to sacrifice anything for peace in the area. Does the U.S. feel it is doing us a favor just talking to the PLO? It is the PLO that is doing the U.S. a favor. It is opening a dialogue with a country whose bias is clear for Israel.

It is the duty of the U.S. to make peace comprehensive. The U.S. should give up the evil ideas of the Zionist lobby including the false view that the U.S. cannot guarantee oil supplies without Israel in the region to insure that.

What is your oil strategy?

Oil is $14 a barrel now, one half the real level of 1971. This price does not respond to the true value. Who is responsible for this injustice? Western countries. Isn't this a conspiracy against the economy of the region? We do not want to use oil as a political weapon. . . . But nobody can eliminate the significance of economics in political relations.

What are prospects of a final peace with Iran?

We are conducting direct dialogue with the Iranians and each party has told the other that we are placing great hopes on this dialogue achieving the desired objectives. So my hope is great that peace will come about between Iran and Iraq.

Why do you call on U.S. ships to leave the Persian Gulf?

We were not consulted by the U.S. when the ships were brought in, but we didn't fight their presence because some of our Arab brothers under the existence of Iranian aggression saw it proper that U.S. ships should be allowed. But now that East-West relations have achieved such a positive level and now that the Iraq-Iran War is over, we cannot see how the presence of U.S. ships can be interpreted for positive reasons. Ask the U.S. what are the reasons for their presence other than conducting intelligence on Arabs for Israel or creating a naval military situation between Iraq and Iran.
