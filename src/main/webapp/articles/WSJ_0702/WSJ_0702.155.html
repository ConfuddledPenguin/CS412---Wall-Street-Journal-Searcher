Mellon Foundation to Give Land to U.S. Government

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/02/90

PITTSBURGH -- A foundation here plans to donate 100,000 acres of historic and ecologically sensitive land to the federal government in what appears to be the largest such gift ever. The land, which was acquired over the past two years for $21 million, includes a 93,000-acre wildlife refuge in North Carolina and the so-called "Cornfield" at Antietam National Battlefield in Maryland, site of one of the bloodiest Civil War battles.

The gift by the Richard King Mellon Foundation is a rare show of environmental philanthropy. Only 3.6% of all foundation grant money goes to the environment, according to the Foundation Center in New York, which tracks foundation grants.

The Mellon Foundation, a leader in environmental philanthropy, purchased the properties through The Conservation Fund, an Arlington, Va.-based non-profit group. The fund selected the land from a government list of ecological and historical properties, many of which were threatened by commercial development. The conservation organization benefited from a sluggish real estate market, but often found itself competing with land developers.

The fund, for example, paid $8.8 million for the 93,000-acre wildlife refuge in Alligator River, N.C., outbidding a group of Japanese investors who wanted to mine the land.
