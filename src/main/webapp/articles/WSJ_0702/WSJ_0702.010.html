Dylex Holders Clear Authorizing Preferred Usable to Pay Debt

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/02/90

TORONTO -- Holders of Dylex Ltd. approved the authorization of a new class of convertible preferred shares that could be used instead of cash to pay off 150 million Canadian dollars (US$128.4 million) of debentures outstanding. Holders of the 9.25% debentures can retract them at 110% of their face value in 1992. The retailing company can ill afford such a cash outlay. Dylex had losses of C$10.5 million for the quarter ended May 5 and C$60.7 million for the fiscal year ended Feb. 3. But the company has the option of substituting the new securities for cash.

Wilfred Posluns, chairman and chief executive officer, said Dylex nevertheless would like to avoid issuing the newly authorized shares, because they could dilute the company's widely held Class A nonvoting shares, into which the preferred would be convertible.

He said Dylex might consider raising cash through the sale of a division or real estate, share issues by a division, or borrowing against real estate. But he said no specific plans had been made for any of these measures.

In trading Friday on the Toronto Stock Exchange, Dylex Class A shares closed unchanged at C$3.95 (US$3.38).
