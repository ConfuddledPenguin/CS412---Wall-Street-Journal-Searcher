Peru, Finance Groups Meet In Effort to Mend Relations

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/02/90

WASHINGTON -- Peru is trying to mend its relations with the international financial community. President-elect Alberto Fujimori met Friday in New York with International Monetary Fund Managing Director Michel Camdessus, World Bank President Barber Conable and the president of the Inter-America Development Bank, Enrique Iglesias, to discuss normalizing relations with these agencies.

Mr. Fujimori, at the meetings, outlined an economic austerity plan that he intends to implement when he takes office at the end of this month. It includes tax increases and other revenue-boosting measures.

The meetings were arranged by Javier Perez de Cuellar, secretary general of the United Nations.

Peru is more than $2 billion in arrears to international agencies and has several billion dollars of unresolved arrears with commercial banks. This includes about $850 million owed to the IMF, $720 million owed to the World Bank and about $450 million owed to the IADB.

Several months ago, Peru started normalizing its relations with the IMF by resuming payments on its debt to the agency but leaving its existing arrears unresolved. It has yet to make such agreements with the World Bank and IADB and, since 1987, it generally has been at loggerheads with all of its creditors.
