What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/02/90

CHRYSLER AND GM raised prices on most cars and some trucks, following Ford's lead. The unusual midyear action reflects Detroit's desire to increase revenues amid weak sales. The Big Three also hope an increase now will soften the appearance of further price rises when 1991 models come out this fall. ---

General Instrument has held recent talks with Forstmann Little, which sources say is interested in a buy-out that could top $1.1 billion. Barring some last-minute snag, an announcement of some transaction could occur this week.

---

Kay Jewelers is expected to receive a friendly offer from Ratners Group, an aggressively expanding British jewelry chain.

---

Some 155 failed S&Ls were sold in the second quarter, exceeding the Resolution Trust Corp.'s target. The rapid sales of the thrifts boosted morale at the bailout agency, but blew a hole in the federal budget.

---

Time Warner sued Pathe Communications for $100 million, charging "willful, wanton and malicious" breach of contract in the collapse of its funding pact for Pathe's proposed $1.3 billion merger with MGM/UA.

---

Securities firms' underwriting profits in the first half plunged to their lowest level in five years, despite a rising volume of new issues.

---

Farm prices fell 1.3% in June from the previous month as prices of hogs, wheat and soybeans slipped. Despite the decline, hog prices remain far higher than a year ago.

---

Campbell Soup will sell several businesses, close three plants and take a charge of $247 million as it extends a 1989 restructuring program.

---

Sales of new homes edged up 0.4% in May after a five-month decline, but failed to spark any optimism about the strength of the housing industry.

---

Junk bonds posted a total return of 4.31% in the second quarter, indicating the high-risk bonds could be climbing out of their deep hole.

---

Robert Campeau and National Bank of Canada were mum about whether he met the payment deadline to retrieve his Campeau Corp. securities seized by the bank.

---

Arco Chemical's purchase of certain Union Carbide assets has run into an obstacle with the FTC.

---

The U.S. can prohibit Capital Cities/ABC from broadcasting the 1991 Pan American Games in Cuba, a federal judge ruled.
