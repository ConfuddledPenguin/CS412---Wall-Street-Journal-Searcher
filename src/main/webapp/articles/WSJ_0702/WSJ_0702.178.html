Inquiry on Canada's Limits On Beer Imports Is Ordered

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/02/90

WASHINGTON -- U.S. Trade Representative Carla Hills ordered an investigation of Canada's beer import restrictions. Mrs. Hills acted under the Section 301 provisions of U.S. trade laws after G. Heileman Brewing Co. Inc., a Wisconsin-based company, charged that Canadian provinces are keeping U.S. beer out of the Canadian market.

"American beer companies should be able to compete on a fair basis in the Canadian market, just as Canadian brewers do here," said Mrs. Hills, who added that Canadian brewers are exporting five times as much beer to the U.S. as U.S. brewers are able to export to Canada.

Mrs. Hills said the U.S. intends to use the disputes-settlement procedures of the General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade (GATT) "to enforce our rights" to export beer to Canada. Her decision, which was expected, pulls the plug on a U.S. government inquiry that might lead to retaliatory trade action if the beer dispute isn't resolved through bilateral negotiations.

Mrs. Hills previously discussed the Heileman complaint with Canadian Trade Minister John Crosbie, who conceded that the beer marketing restrictions of Canadian provinces have made Canada vulnerable to charges of violating its international trade obligations.

In 1988, a GATT panel in Geneva, acting on a complaint from the European Community, ruled that Canada has discriminated unfairly against imports of alcoholic beverages.
