International Brief -- Japan Air Lines: Firm Reports 15% Increase In Fiscal-Year Pretax Profit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/02/90

Japan Air Lines Co. said its consolidated pretax profits rose 15% to 59.01 billion yen ($385.7 million) in the fiscal year ended March 31 from 51.35 billion yen the year earlier. Net income, however, fell 12% to 17.68 billion yen from 20.18 billion yen. Per-share net fell to 107.50 yen from 129.05 yen.

Revenues climbed 15% to 1.255 trillion yen from 1.092 trillion yen. JAL said revenues were boosted by the continuing strength of Japan's economy and the increasing trend among Japanese to travel abroad.

The company cited a fall in extraordinary profits from sales of fixed assets and an increase in corporate tax reserves as reasons for the fall in profits.

For the current fiscal year, JAL sees pretax profits edging up to 60 billion yen while net income is expected to recover to 23.5 billion yen. Revenues are expected to rise to 1.305 trillion yen.
