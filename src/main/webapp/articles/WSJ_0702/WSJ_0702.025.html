Ford Idles Assembly Plant

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/02/90

DEARBORN, Mich. -- Ford Motor Co. said it will temporarily idle its Dearborn assembly plant for one week beginning July 16, because of a shortage of dealer orders for the Mustangs built there. The move will affect about 2,100 hourly employees on the plant's two shifts, and it will cut Mustang production by about 3,700 units. According to industry trade publication Ward's Automotive Reports, Ford's dealers held an 81-day supply of Mustangs at the end of May, significantly higher than the 60- to 65-day supply that is optimum for the industry.
