Tesoro to Pay Trinidad $2.8 Million to Settle Suit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/02/90

SAN ANTONIO, Texas -- Tesoro Petroleum Corp. said it will pay the government of Trinidad and Tobago $2.8 million to settle litigation related to a now-defunct oil exploration and production venture. The Trinidad and Tobago government, in a lawsuit filed last year, sought unspecified damages and the return of $350 million in profits it said were generated by the joint venture. Trinidad-Tobago accused Tesoro of wire and mail fraud, racketeering, bribery and violations of prostitution laws.

"Tesoro has always been confident it would prevail in a trial . . . and still maintains that conviction," the refining and marketing concern said. "Nonetheless, the company has elected to settle on what it regards as very favorable terms compared to the cost of extended litigation."

Officials of Trinidad-Tobago hailed the settlement as a victory. Prime Minister A.N.R. Robinson said, "The case and its settlement show that corruption is wrong and the people of Trinidad and Tobago will tolerate it no longer."
