Bronfmans Move Closer to Acquiring Unicorp Canada

  • Author: Suzanne McGee
  • Date: 07/02/90

TORONTO -- Toronto's Bronfman family took a step closer to acquiring control of Unicorp Canada Corp. after agreeing to backstop part of a 100 million Canadian dollar (US$85.8 million) rights offering. Under the proposed offering, Hees International Bancorp Inc. and BrasPower Ltd., both indirectly controlled by financiers Peter and Edward Bronfman, could together take up rights giving them as much as 69% of the voting shares of Unicorp. Hees already has a 24% non-voting stake in the company, acquired through a previous rights issue in January.

BrasPower, jointly owned by Hees and Brascan Ltd., the Bronfmans' resource-investment arm, has agreed to underwrite up to C$50 million of the rights offering. James Leech, Unicorp's president, said the Bronfman interests could acquire between 17% and 69% of the voting shares of Unicorp, depending on how many other shareholders take up their rights.

The rights will be granted all holders of both the Class A non-voting and Class B voting Unicorp shares.

The rights issue units will consist of a total of 10 million preferred shares and 10 million warrants to purchase Unicorp's Class B voting shares. The units won't be priced until all regulatory approvals have been received, a process Unicorp said will take about 120 days.

The Bronfman interests will also move to take operating control of Unicorp almost immediately. George Mann, founder and controlling shareholder of Unicorp, said he is stepping down as chairman and passing responsibility for the company's day-to-day operations to Robert Dunford, executive vice-president of Brascan and president of BrasPower. Mr. Mann couldn't be reached for comment as to whether he plans to take up his rights.

Brascan, which has a two-year right to buy Hees' non-voting stake in Unicorp, is interested in expanding its utility operations as a "fourth leg" to complement its other resource investments. These include a 42% stake in Noranda Inc., the giant Toronto-based mining, forest products and energy concern.

Unicorp is in the throes of a major restructuring which will leave it as a company with assets in the energy sector. Through 60%-owned Union Energy Inc., Unicorp controls Canada's second-largest natural gas distribution company, Union Gas Ltd., Calgary oil and gas producer Mark Resources Ltd., as well as natural gas marketing and transportation concerns.

As part of that restructuring, the company plans to pay out as dividends shares of the troubled Unicorp American Corp. to common shareholders of Unicorp Canada. The American unit's major remaining asset is Lincoln Savings Bank, which is being held for disposition.

Unicorp said BrasPower will provide a C$50 million credit facility to assist in the restructuring of Unicorp American and support the operations of Union Energy.

Brascan officials couldn't be reached for comment. But Trevor Eyton, its president, said after the annual meeting in May that he expects Brascan to acquire Hees's stake in Unicorp before the end of the year. He also noted that Brascan usually wants a stake of between 40% and 50% in its major corporate investments.
