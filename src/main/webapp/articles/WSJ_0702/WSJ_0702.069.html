Who's News: Texaco Inc. Names Peter Bijur President Of Europe Operation

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/02/90

WHITE PLAINS, N.Y. -- Texaco Inc. named Peter I. Bijur president of its European operations. The appointment, effective in November, is a major step up for the 48-year-old executive, who for the past year has been running the company's U.K. operations. Before that, Mr. Bijur was chief executive officer of Texaco Canada Inc. until it was sold as part of Texaco's restructuring. In 1984, he drew the difficult task of selling assets related to Texaco's acquisition of Getty Oil Co.

In his new post, Mr. Bijur will succeed Paul B. Hicks Jr., 64, who is retiring after 37 years with Texaco. Mr. Hicks has served as a senior vice president of Texaco Inc. in addition to being president of Texaco Europe.

Mr. Bijur, a Texaco employee for 24 years, will take charge of Texaco Europe at a crucial time. Right now, international oil companies are vying for business in Eastern Europe and the Soviet Union. "These areas are the new frontier because they've been closed for so long," Mr. Bijur said.

Although Western Europe will remain the primary focus for Texaco Europe, Mr. Bijur said his operation will aggressively seek opportunities in Eastern bloc countries as they shed government structures that have kept the countries from striking up business relations with Western companies.

Texaco has already established a task force to look for opportunities as those countries open up. The company recently reached an agreement with the Soviet government to use computers for geological research, and is pursuing possible marketing arrangements there.
