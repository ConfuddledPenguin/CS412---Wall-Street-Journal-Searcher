Letters to the Editor: We Were Listening To What You Heard

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/02/90

If people ever questioned the power of the free press and that of The Wall Street Journal in particular, they should be aware of the Fibreboard story reported in your paper by Roger Lowenstein (Heard on the Street, May 3). He detailed certain proposals that were counter to the shareholders' interests, which were to be acted on at the annual meeting being held on May 7. The reaction to the article's disclosures was to ignite shareholders either to cast their vote or change the one previously made. The inspectors of the election got only three hours of sleep on the night previous to the meeting because of the need to recount the changed vote.

As a direct result of the article, the contested proposal did not receive a majority of the votes cast. It subsequently has been rescinded by the company.

On behalf of the thousands of shareholders of Fibreboard, I would like to express our appreciation for the outstanding coverage. It is a wonderful example of the power of the press to inquire and disclose so that the reader can make an informed judgment as to the merits of a matter.

Donald K. Miller

Managing Partner

Greylock Financial Partnership

New York
