FMC Corp. Receives $112.7 Million Order For Army Vehicles

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/02/90

WASHINGTON -- FMC Corp. received a $112.7 million Army contract for Bradley Fighting Vehicles.

Motorola Inc. was given a $75.5 million Army contract for bomb fuzes.

Westinghouse Electric Corp. got a $76.2 million Air Force contract for aircraft radar.

American Telephone & Telegraph Co. received a $48.8 million Navy contract for communications equipment.

Martin Marietta Corp. won a $42.1 million Army contract for air defense systems and night-vision sensors.

Tampa Shipyards Inc. got a $37.1 million Navy contract for fleet oiler construction.

Litton Systems Inc., a unit of Litton Industries Inc., received a $33.4 million contract for Navy submarine navigation systems and Army helicopter equipment.

Boeing Co. was given a $29.8 million contract for Air Force missiles and Army laser equipment.

EG&G Management Systems Inc., a unit of EG&G Inc., received a $28.9 million Air Force contract for radar target facilities.

Magnavox Electronic Systems Co., a unit of North American Philips Corp., won a $27.3 million Air Force contract for radio equipment.

General Electric Co. got a $27.2 million contract for Army helicopter engines and Air Force jet fighter engines.

Ford Aerosapce Services Inc., a unit of Ford Motor Co., received a $23.7 million Air Force contract for weapons center range maintenance.

Aerojet General Corp. was given an $18.5 million Army contract for 25mm cartridges.

United Technologies Corp. received a $17.9 million Army contract for helicopter modifications.

Chamberlain Manufacturing Corp. got a $15.6 million Army contract for artillery projectiles.

Dynaweld Inc. received a $15.4 million Army contract for semitrailers.
