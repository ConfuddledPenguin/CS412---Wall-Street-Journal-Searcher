Marketing & Media: News Corp. Sale to G.P. Group

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/02/90

NEW YORK -- News Corp. said it completed the sale of Star magazine to G.P. Group Inc., publisher of the National Enquirer and Weekly World News, for $400 million. The publishing and broadcasting concern said it will receive $200 million in cash and $200 million in convertible preferred stock in G.P. Group, based in Lantana, Fla. G.P. Group is owned by principals of New York-based Macfadden Holdings Inc. and Boston Ventures Limited Partnership.

The National Enquirer is the nation's largest supermarket tabloid, with circulation of about four million; the Star's circulation is about 3.5 million.
