Foothill Group Says It Will Report a Loss For Second Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/02/90

LOS ANGELES -- Foothill Group said it plans to write down certain junk bonds in its portfolio, which will lead to an estimated second-quarter net loss of between $3.8 million and $4.3 million. The commercial lending and equipment leasing concern said the pretax writedowns are $6 million at Foothill Thrift & Loan and $3 million at the parent Foothill Group. After tax, the write-downs will total about $5.4 million.

A year ago, Foothill reported second-quarter net income of $3.2 million, or 32 cents a share.

Henry Jordan, chief financial officer, said Foothill's book value after the adjustments would be about $6.50 at June 30, and the company's junk-bond securities portfolio would be less than 5% of consolidated assets. Mr. Jordan said the portfolio writedowns are unrelated to Foothill's management of about $130 million in distressed securities in two private limited partnerships.

In response to the announcement, Fitch Investors Service affirmed Foothill Capital Corp.'s F-2 commercial paper, noting that the financing unit has no high-yield bond portfolio and is unaffected by the writedowns.
