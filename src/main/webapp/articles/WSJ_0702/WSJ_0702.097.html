Bruce Nobles, Head Of Trump Shuttle Steps Down From Post

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/02/90

The head of the Trump Shuttle has stepped down, a move reflecting the continuing turmoil at Donald Trump's one-year-old airline. Bruce Nobles, president of the Shuttle, was replaced Friday by Richard Cozzi, who has been the airline's vice president of operations since its inception. Mr. Cozzi will also be the chief operating officer.

The Shuttle has lost almost $100 million since Mr. Trump acquired it from Eastern Airlines for $365 million in June last year. Although Trump officials and Mr. Nobles weren't available for comment, Mr. Trump has previously expressed disappointment that the Shuttle hasn't performed better. The airline, which operates Boston-New York-Washington shuttle flights, has increased its share of that market to 50%, but Trump officials had hoped for 60% by now.

Mr. Nobles's departure follows a series of other changes at the Trump Shuttle. It recently canceled four daily round trips in both the Boston-New York and Washington-New York markets on Saturday, as well as two other flights in each market Sunday. Also, it has laid off about 70 employees and cut back a few frills, such as its concierge desk.

Mr. Nobles was president of the Pan Am Shuttle before joining the Trump airline. At Pan Am, Mr. Nobles helped to end Eastern Airlines' dominance in the lucrative Boston-New York-Washington shuttle flights. By the end of 1988, after two years of operations, Pan Am had garnered 45% of the travel on those routes, largely because of Mr. Nobles's start-up efforts and Eastern's much publicized difficulties.
