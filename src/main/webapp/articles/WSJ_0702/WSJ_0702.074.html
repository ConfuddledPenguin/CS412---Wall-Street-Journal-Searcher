Brunswick Mining Employees Reject Pact, Begin Strike

  • Author: Larry M. Greenberg
  • Date: 07/02/90

TORONTO -- More than 1,100 mine workers at Brunswick Mining & Smelting Corp.'s zinc and lead mine began striking after voting to reject what the company has called its "final" contract offer. Meantime, contract talks covering 3,500 workers at Vancouver, British Columbia-based Cominco Ltd. continued through the weekend after workers last week voted down the company's latest offer and authorized the union bargaining committee to call a strike. A union spokesman said the union plans to continue bargaining at least until tomorrow.

Workers at both companies are represented by local affiliates of the United Steelworkers of America. Contracts at both companies expired Saturday.

Cominco and Brunswick Mining, 64%-owned by Toronto's Noranda Inc., are Canada's largest and second largest producers of zinc and lead. Prices of these metals on world markets were bolstered last week in anticipation of a possible strike, and analysts expect a cutoff of zinc and lead supplies from either company could boost prices further.

Picket lines went up at the Brunswick mine near Bathurst, New Brunswick, yesterday, said Larry Urbanoski, a superintendent at the mine. "We're going through a process of arranging an orderly shutdown of the plant," he said. As of midday yesterday, there weren't any new talks scheduled between the union and the company, he said.

Blair Doucet, president of Local 5385 of the United Steelworkers, said late last week that the two sides are "still quite a ways away" from reaching agreement. He said workers rejected Brunswick Mining's offer, which includes a 20% wage boost over three years and increased monthly pension payments, because they are determined to make up ground after the company "got away cheap" in the last round of talks.

Brunswick Mining officials couldn't be reached for comment.

Brunswick Mining estimates its production of zinc and lead accounts for roughly 5% and 1.5%, respectively, of Western world output.

Cominco produces about 5.8% of the Western world's refined zinc, and 2.6% of the West's lead, at its big Trail, British Columbia, smelter complex. Cominco also owns British Columbia's Sullivan lead-zinc mine, where output was suspended earlier this year due to operating losses.
