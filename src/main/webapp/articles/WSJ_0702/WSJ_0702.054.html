Kolokouris Settles Fraud Charges Tied To Firm's Stock Sale

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/02/90

ROCHESTER, N.Y. -- The former president of North Atlantic Fisheries Inc. settled a Securities and Exchange Commission lawsuit accusing him of stealing company funds, falsifying books and records, and filing false statements with regulators. The case stemmed from allegations by the SEC that Peter Kolokouris, as president of North Atlantic, committed fraud in connection with the company's 1985 initial public offering. In the civil suit filed in federal court in Rochester, the SEC alleged that Mr. Kolokouris had a secret ownership interest in the company's underwriter, from which he received kickbacks.

Mr. Kolokouris settled the case without admitting or denying the allegations. North Atlantic, which processes and distributes fish, is operating under a temporary receiver appointed by the federal Judge Michael Telesca in Rochester.

John R. Parrinello, an attorney for Mr. Kolokouris, said, "It was clear that a settlement was in the best interest of everybody."

As part of the settlement, Mr. Kolokouris turned over $2.1 million in stock in North Atlantic, Flower City Chemicals Inc., Zorba's Palace Inc. and Rochester Venture Associates Inc. This amounts to all the stock Mr. Kolokouris owned or controlled in those companies. He will also resign as an officer of those companies.

The settlement prohibits Mr. Kolokouris from being an officer or owning 5% or more of any public company for five years.
