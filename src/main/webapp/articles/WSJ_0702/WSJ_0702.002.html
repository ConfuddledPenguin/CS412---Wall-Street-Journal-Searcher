DOW JONES INDUSTRY GROUPS were calculated with an incorrect price for BankAmerica Corp. stock Thursday. Using the stock's correct closing composite price of $30 a share, the money center banks industry group index for Thursday should be 186.45; the financial sector index should be 284.48, and the Dow Jones Equity Market Index should be 332.56. ---

DIGITAL SYSTEMS INTERNATIONAL'S National Market System short position in the month to mid-June was 34,415 shares, not the 1,914,090 shares listed in Wednesday's edition. The incorrect number was due to a reporting error by a Nasdaq member firm, and reduced total NMS short interest for the period to about 256.5 million shares.

---

OLIN Corp. expects changes in its mix of businesses and certain other factors to contribute to strong second-half earnings that should result in full-year profit at the lower end of the company's per-share target range of $6 to $6.40. Friday's edition reported Olin's expectation that second-quarter earnings would decline 15% to 20% from the 1989 period's record level, but didn't mention the outlook for the second half and full year.

(See: "Abreast of the Market: Industrials Rise 16.58 as Investors Buy Firms With Proven Earnings" -- WSJ June 29, 1990)

---

VSB BANCORP Inc. expects to report a third-quarter pretax loss of $2.5 million, which will result in "several hundred thousand dollars" of savings on an after-tax basis, according to a spokesman. Friday's edition failed to note the $2.5 million loss was before taxes.

(See: "Business Brief -- VSB Bancorp Inc.: Savings Bank Parent Sees $2.5 Million 3rd-Period Loss" -- WSJ June 29, 1990)

---

GRANT STREET NATIONAL BANK is an independent, publicly held company. In Friday's edition, Grant Street National was incorrectly identified as a unit of Mellon Bank Corp.

(See: "Mellon's Grant Street Unit" -- WSJ June 29, 1990)
