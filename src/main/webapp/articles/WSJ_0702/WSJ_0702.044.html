Mitsubishi, Chrysler Recall Cars That Failed EPA Exhaust Tests

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/02/90

WASHINGTON -- Mitsubishi Motors Corp. and Chrysler Corp. are recalling about 43,000 cars that failed federal exhaust tests, the Environmental Protection Agency said. The cars are 1986 Mitsubishi Cordias, Tredias and Space Wagons; 1986 Dodge and Plymouth Colt Vistas; and 1987 Mitsubishi Space Wagons and Dodge and Plymouth Colt Vistas. All of the cars have two-liter engines and nonelectric chokes.

The manufacturers will modify the cars' air-injection systems, clean and modify the carburetors and clean the exhaust gas recirculation systems. The companies agreed to the recall, which will be free to owners, after reviewing EPA test results that showed excessive levels of hydrocarbons, carbon monoxide and nitrogen oxide in the cars' exhaust.

Mitsubishi began notifying owners by mail Friday. Chrysler, which will repair the Dodge and Plymouth models, expects to begin notifying owners in mid-August, the EPA said.

Chrysler confirmed the recall but said it had no comment. Mitsubishi wasn't available for comment.
