Massachusetts Passes Bill On Interstate Bank Rules

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/02/90

BOSTON -- The Massachusetts Legislature approved an interstate banking bill that will allow banks outside of New England to open or buy banks in the state. Until now, only banks based in other New England states have been able to enter the Massachusetts market.

The bill will permit interstate banking 60 days after its expected signing by the governor. However, a bank can choose to opt out of the interstate banking provisions until July 1, 1992, by a two-thirds vote of its directors.

Analysts don't expect the legislation to result in a flurry of bank takeovers, largely because many of the state's banks are struggling under the real estate and general economic slump and thus aren't considered attractive targets for outsiders.
