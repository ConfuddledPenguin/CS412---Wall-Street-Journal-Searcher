Marketing Brief -- Lewis Galoob Toys Inc.: Judge Enjoins Distribution Of Toy Firm's Video Genie

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/02/90

Lewis Galoob Toys Inc., San Franisco, said a federal judge enjoined it from distributing its new video Game Genie, acting in a copyright infringement suit brought by Nintendo of America Inc., based in Redmond, Washington. Galoob said the decision by Judge Robert H. Schnacke of the San Francisco U.S. District Court affects its planned distribution of the product this month, and will be appealed immediately.

Nintendo said Judge Schnacke ruled that Galoob's Game Genie operated on "derivative" audio-visual works that infringed on Nintendo's copyrights.

Galoob, which markets and distributes a variety of toy products world-wide, bills the game as the world's only "video game enchancer." The video product allows players to modify games on Nintendo's popular home-entertainment system.

Nintendo has filed more than 50 suits against individuals and companies in recent years over copyright issues.
