Demand for One-Megabit Chips Will Grow in Japan

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/02/90

TOKYO -- Japan's demand for one-megabit dynamic random access memory (DRAM) chips will grow 28.7% in the second half of this year, from the previous six months, to about 277.50 million units, the Ministry of International Trade and Industry (MITI) predicted Friday. MITI officials noted that demand is shifting increasingly to one-megabit computer chips, the most sophisticated now on the market, from 256-kilobit chips.

Demand for a variety of semiconductors, which languished early this year, will rebound in the second half as the result of a recovery in demand for computers and other office automation equipment, according to officials.

But as the shift in demand progresses, they said, demand for 256-kilobit chips will decline 9.1% between July and December, from the prior six months, to 139.50 million units.
