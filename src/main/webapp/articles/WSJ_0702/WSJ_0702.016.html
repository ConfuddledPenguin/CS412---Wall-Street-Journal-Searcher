Fairfield-Noble Closes Operations; Lenders Foreclosed on Loans

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/02/90

NEW YORK -- Fairfield-Noble Corp. said that it discontinued operations and that its lenders have foreclosed on its loans. The maker and seller of sportswear for women and girls blamed a tumultous retail environment and the company's failure to obtain financing for its decision last Friday to close. A spokesman wouldn't elaborate.

Fairfield-Noble, which went public in 1969, consisted of its Lloyd Williams and Cathy Hardwick divisions. The company closed the Cathy Hardwick unit during the fiscal year that ended June 30, 1989, for which it had a net loss of $5.9 million, on $24.5 million in sales.

According to Securities and Exchange Commission filings, the company in fiscal 1989 violated its loan agreement with Chase Manhattan Bank, but it didn't default; if it had, Fairfield-Noble said in SEC filings, it would have been unable to continue operations.

According to SEC filings, Fairfield-Noble at Sept. 30, 1989, had assets of $5.9 million and a net worth, or assets minus liabilities, of $11,000. It had 2.3 million shares outstanding. Two principals, Hy Rabin and Clarence Scaduto, controlled 45.2% of the company's stock. As of June 30, 1989, Fairfield-Noble had a total of 2,400 shareholders.
