Chicago Merc Levies Fines Of $165,000 for Violations

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/02/90

CHICAGO -- The Chicago Mercantile Exchange said it issued fines totaling $165,000 for "major" violations of exchange trading rules. The Merc fined Ronald Wertz, a former commodity representative, $125,000 for making unauthorized sales in its Standard & Poor's 500-stock index futures pit and allocating them to customer accounts in April 1987. The exchange also said Mr. Wertz made unauthorized transfers of funds out of customer accounts in 1986 and 1987.

Mr. Wertz, who failed to appear at the hearing where the exchange found him culpable and who couldn't be reached for comment Friday, also was stripped of his rights to act as a commodity representative.

In addition, the exchange fined James G. Sledz $40,000, and expelled him from membership in the Merc. Mr. Sledz, the exchange said, "prearranged" yen futures trades in August and October of 1987. Mr. Sledz "participated in an ongoing pattern of illegal transactions in which brokers deliberately converted customer funds and opportunities to their own use and the use of others," according to the exchange.

Mr. Sledz is cooperating with federal officials in their broad investigation into trading abuses at the Chicago futures pits, and the information about his alleged violations was included in his plea agreement, a Merc spokesman said.
