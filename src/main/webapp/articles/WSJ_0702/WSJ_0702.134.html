International Brief -- Anglo American Corp.: Holding Company Launched For European Interests

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/02/90

Mining house Anglo American Corp. of South Africa Ltd. said it has established a wholly owned offshore company to hold its European interests. The establishment of the new company, A.E.H. Ltd., is disclosed in the corporation's annual report. A spokesman said the move was part of Anglo American's efforts to position itself in Europe before the establishment of a single European market in 1992.

"Significant" investments made during the review year in two European companies are held by A.E.H., the report said. They are an effective 22.2% interest in Deutsche Steinindustrie AG of West Germany, an international stone operation, and an effective 19.7% of health-care company the Medgenix Group of Belgium.

The spokesman declined to comment on A.E.H.'s long-term strategies.

The investments in Europe form part of a total 1.123-billion-rand ($423 million) net expenditure by Anglo American on investments during the past financial year, the biggest of which was an increase to 25% in its stake in mining house Gold Fields of South Africa Ltd.
