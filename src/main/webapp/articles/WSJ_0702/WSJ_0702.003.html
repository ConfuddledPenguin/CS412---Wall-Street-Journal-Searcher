Marketing & Media: Ziff's PC/Computing Buys Subscriber List Of a VNU Magazine

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/02/90

CAMBRIDGE, Mass. -- PC/Computing, a computer magazine owned by the Ziff-Davis Publishing Co. unit of Ziff Communications Co., New York, said it acquired the subscriber list of Personal Computing, a computer magazine that the Netherlands-based VNU N.V. is discontinuing. Terms weren't disclosed.

Personal Computing, which has a paid circulation of more than 400,000 and which was recently named "best computer magazine" by the Computer Press Association, said it will stop publishing after its August issue.

VNU (United Dutch Publishers) has been systematically selling off its U.S. magazines in the past three years and expanding its acquisitions of computer data-based services for the marketing, legal and financial professions. At one point, VNU published seven computer and electronic magazines under VNU Business Publications Inc., based in Hasbrouck Heights, N.J.

Personal Computing, founded in 1976, is the last of the magazines that VNU owned in the U.S. The magazine, which has changed owners three times since its inception, was acquired by VNU in 1980.

With the folding of Personal Computing and the sale of its subscriber list to Ziff-Davis, Robert J. Lydon, president of VNU Business Publications and Personal Computing, said he will leave the company. "Ziff came to us with a very attractive offer," he said, adding, "In today's increasingly complex market, we felt our readers and advertisers would be better served by Ziff's multi-title resources in the computer field."

Ziff-Davis also owns PC Magazine, a biweekly computer magazine, and PC Week, a weekly newspaper. The three publications combined have 1.2 million readers, the company said.

PC/Computing has a paid circulation of more than 500,000. With the acquisition, PC/Computing will add about 100,000 subscribers to its list. Most of Personal Computing's circulation will be lost to PC/ Computing, owing to duplications, lost newsstand sales and the termination of a bulk subscription from the Pan Am Shuttle, a Northeast corridor air carrier. PC/ Computing said its product is already sold in airport newsstands.
