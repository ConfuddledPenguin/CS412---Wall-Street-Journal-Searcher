TransTechnology Posts a Deficit, Suspends Payout

  • Author: Rhonda L. Rundle
  • Date: 07/02/90

LOS ANGELES -- TransTechnology Corp. posted a fiscal fourth-quarter loss of $4.6 million, or 90 cents a share, and said it suspended its dividend as part of a revised credit agreement with its bank lenders. In the year-ago quarter, the maker of aerospace and industrial products earned $5.3 million, or $1.02 a share. Revenue fell 13% to $62.4 million from $71.6 million.

TransTechnology had a loss from continuing operations of $3 million, or 59 cents a share, compared with earnings from continuing operations of $5.3 million, or $1.23 a share, a year earlier. Two unprofitable units were recently sold.

Lee M. Parker, vice president, said the fourth-quarter loss included "between $10 million and $12 million" in write-downs, reflecting mainly inventory adjustments and an increase in reserves for environmental cleanup of a facility. TransTechnology's computer graphics business also was hurt by the loss of a major customer.

Last month, TransTechnology cautioned that it would report a loss for the quarter and year. In January, Chairman Arch Scurlock predicted a strong fourth quarter that would "put the company solidly in the black" for fiscal 1990.

Mr. Parker said Friday that "once it became apparent we would be running a loss for the whole year, it became advantageous to recognize things we might have debated with auditors on and maximize the losses that will allow us to go back and file for income tax refunds and generate income tax-loss carry-forwards."

For the year, the company had a loss of $8.4 million, or $1.64 a share, compared with year-earlier net income of $9.2 million, or $1.80 a share. Revenue fell to $198.5 million from $219 million. The full-year loss from continuing operations was $3.3 million, or 64 cents a share, compared with year-ago earnings from continuing operations of $12.3 million, or $2.39 a share.

TransTechnology said that last week it reached agreement with its bank lenders led by Bank of America in Los Angeles for a $37 million loan to replace a $40 million revolving credit facility. Mr. Parker said TransTechnology has borrowed about $34 million of the $37 million available.

TransTechnology paid a dividend of 24 cents a share on common stock in June. TransTechnology said that prior to this year, it had increased its dividend every year for the past decade.
