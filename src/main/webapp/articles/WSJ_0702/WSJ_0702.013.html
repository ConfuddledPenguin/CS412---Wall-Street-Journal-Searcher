Ferro Stock Soars As U.S. Approves Stock Purchase

  • Author: James S. Hirsch
  • Date: 07/02/90

Salt Lake City businessman Jon M. Huntsman received federal approval to acquire up to 15% of troubled Ferro Corp., a much-rumored takeover candidate last year, sending Ferro stock surging $4.375 to $25.50 in heavy New York Stock Exchange composite trading Friday. Brent Stevenson, president of Huntsman Holdings Inc., said Mr. Huntsman didn't intend in the short term, either alone or with someone else, to exercise control of Cleveland-based Ferro.

Mr. Stevenson said Mr. Huntsman already has an undisclosed equity position in Ferro, which has 19.4 million common shares outstanding.

Ferro, which makes specialty chemicals, specialty ceramics and engineered plastics, declined to comment. Earlier this year, Mr. Huntsman failed to acquire another plastics-related company, Aristech Chemical Corp., Pittsburgh.

Separately, Ferro said it expects second-quarter earnings to be "substantially lower" than the $13.1 million, or 87 cents a share, it earned a year earlier. The company's first-quarter earnings plunged to $6 million, or 26 cents a share, from $12.3 million, or 60 cents a share, a year earlier.

Ferro said it expects second-quarter results to be better than they were in the first quarter. It attributed its problems to economic conditions in Latin America, particularly Brazil, start-up costs for a new chemicals operation in France, and sluggish demand in the automotive market.
