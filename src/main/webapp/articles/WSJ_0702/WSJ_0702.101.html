Business Brief -- Bohemia Inc. Firm to Close Wood Plant Because of Timber Shortage

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/02/90

Bohemia Inc. said it will permanently close its Culp Creek plywood facility within two months, citing a timber shortage expected to be compounded by the federal government's decision to list the northern spotted owl as a threatened species. The move will affect the jobs of 248 employees of the Eugene, Ore.-based concern. Rising material costs -- resulting from loss of access to old growth forests -- and sagging plywood prices were factors in the decision to close the facility. The company said the situation is "expected to worsen" because of the Bush administration's decision last month on the spotted owl.

The plant, also in Oregon, generated $20 million of the company's $350 million revenues in fiscal 1990. Richard D. Tinney, Bohemia's president and chief executive officer, said the company will try to place as many employees as possible in its other facilities.
