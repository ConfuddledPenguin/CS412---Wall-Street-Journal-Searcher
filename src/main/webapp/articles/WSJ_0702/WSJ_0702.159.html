Who's News: Source Perrier Searching For Stronger Leadership

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/02/90

PARIS -- By replacing aging Chairman Gustave Leven, Source Perrier S.A. begins a search for more vigorous leadership amid lingering sales problems caused by this year's benzene contamination incident. Mr. Leven, 76 years old, built his once-sleepy water company into an international marketing force. But the idiosyncratic chairman failed to groom a strong successor.

With sales recovering only slowly after Perrier recalled its green bottles in February from stores and restaurants world-wide, the company's main shareholders decided to take action.

The new chairman is Jacques Vincent, who heads Perrier's main shareholder, investment group Exor S.A., and who until now was Perrier's vice chairman. But Mr. Vincent, 66 years old, is expected to be an interim chairman. His job is to find a longer-term professional manager, something Mr. Leven apparently hadn't done to Exor's satisfaction.

Mr. Vincent's accession quieted rumors that Perrier might be put on the block or become a takeover target, although it didn't put the talk entirely to rest. Exor controls around 35% of Perrier shares and the sometimes divided Leven family controls about 18% more.
