Business Brief -- General Signal Corp.: Firm Revokes Plan to Sell Telecommunications Unit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/02/90

General Signal Corp. said it decided not to sell its telecommunications equipment group, saying offers for the business had been inadequate. The Stamford, Conn., maker of semiconductors and electrical equipment said the group's results exceeded expectations for the first five months of 1990, and that it expects sales of approximately $150 million for the full year.

General Signal had announced in March its intent to sell the group, consisting of three units that supply telecommunications test equipment, transmission and network products. Instead, a spokesman said, "earnings are worth more to us than the proceeds we would receive to pay down debt." General Signal said at the time it intended to use proceeds of the telecommunications sale to pay down debt.

In a separate announcement, General Signal said it will shed two unprofitable units in its technology group, namely semiconductor systems and thin film processing.

General Signal reported earnings for 1989 of $78.5 million, on sales of $1.92 billion.
