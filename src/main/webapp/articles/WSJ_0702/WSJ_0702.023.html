WNS Is Refinancing Bank Debt, Proposes Stock, Notes Exchange

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/02/90

HOUSTON -- WNS Inc. said it refinanced its bank debt and proposed an exchange of subordinated notes for preferred stock. Separately, the specialty retailer said its president and chief operating officer, John Jones, resigned Friday. A corporation formed by Mr. Jones and certain WNS management members on Friday purchased the company's Deck the Walls division for $9 million. No successor for Mr. Jones was announced.

WNS refinanced its bank debt with First Interstate Bank of Texas N.A., extending the maturity date to Jan. 31, 1992. WNS Chairman Harold Otto couldn't be reached for details of the refinancing.

WNS said it proposes to exchange preferred stock for $4 million in subordinated notes outstanding held by Textron Collective Investment Trust Fund B. The company said its proxy solicitation of shareholders would begin immediately.

WNS operates primarily through franchisees with 254 Wicks 'N' Sticks stores selling candles and room fragrancing and 124 Wallpapers To Go stores selling wallcoverings.
