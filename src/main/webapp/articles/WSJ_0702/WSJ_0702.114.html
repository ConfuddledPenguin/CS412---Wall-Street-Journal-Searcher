Business Brief -- Comprehensive Care Corp.: Firm Will Pay Cash, Issue Stock to Settle a Lawsuit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/02/90

Comprehensive Care Corp. said it agreed to settle a class action lawsuit by paying $150,000 in cash, and issuing 687,500 shares of common stock. The company, which denied any wrongdoing, said the litigation arose of an unsuccessful attempt by closely held First Hospital Corp. of Norfolk, Va. to acquire it last year.

Comprehensive Care, which runs chemical dependency treatment programs, said the settlement is subject to approval by the court, among other things.

Comprehensive Care shares closed Friday on the New York Stock Exchange at $2.625, unchanged. That price gives an indicated value of $1.8 million to the shares that will be issued. The company currently has about 10.2 million common shares outstanding.
