McDonald's Is Dropping Its Mini-Billboard Ads

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/02/90

CHICAGO -- McDonald's Corp. will announce today that it's ending its mini-billboard campaign for its new cheddar cheese hamburger. The ill-fated effort to promote the Cheddar Melt on newsracks for the Chicago Observer, a newspaper sponsored by outdoor advertising concerns, drew the ire of many Chicago residents and civic groups. Hundreds of three-foot by four-foot signs appeared on sidewalks here last week. The signs prompted Mayor Richard M. Daley to introduce an ordinance in the City Council restricting the size of newsracks and banning advertising on them.

Rick Johnson, marketing supervisor for McDonald's Chicago region, said McDonald's had asked the Chicago Observer to pull the signs from its boxes. The action was taken after a meeting of the board of Chicago-area franchise owner-operators with McDonald's officials, according to Mr. Johnson. "Our concern for the local environment was more important than our concern for this particular (advertising) communication," Mr. Johnson said.
