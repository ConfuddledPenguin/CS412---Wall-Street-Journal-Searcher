International Brief -- N.V. Philips: Firm Plans Further Cuts In Computer-Related Jobs

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/02/90

N.V. Philips is preparing to announce the elimination of at least several hundred more jobs in its computer division, sources close to the company said. The Dutch electronics company, which in June announced plans to cut 210 computer-related jobs in the Netherlands, plans further reductions at plants in France, Sweden and Canada, the sources said.

In addition, Jan Timmer, Philips's new chairman, is likely to sketch plans for broader cost-saving measures when he assumes his new post this week.

In the computer division, which has had losses estimated at more than 100 million guilders ($53 million) a quarter in the past year, Philips apparently aims to improve performance enough to attract potential partners or buyers for parts of the business.

An extraordinary meeting today is expected to draw about 1,000 shareholders, a Philips spokesman said.
