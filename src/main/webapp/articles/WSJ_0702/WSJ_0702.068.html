Who's News: Emulex Founder Cox Plans to Step Aside In Firm's Operations

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 07/02/90

COSTA MESA, Calif. -- Fred B. Cox, founder, chairman and chief executive officer of Emulex Corp., said he plans to relinquish daily operating responsibilities as the firm seeks a new chief executive officer. David Hanna, a board member and consultant, has been named interim president and will assume day-to-day operating responsibility for the company. Mr. Cox said in a statement that he plans to remain chairman and a board member, and will retain contact with the company's key accounts. Mr. Cox founded Emulex in 1979, and has spent 30 years in the electronics and computer industry. The release didn't explain why Mr. Cox is stepping aside, and company officials couldn't be reached for comment.

Mr. Hanna spent 14 years with IBM in sales, marketing and general management, and has served as chief executive officer for several computer firms, including Altos Computer Systems of San Jose, Calif.

Emulex makes disk, tape and communications controllers for peripheral computer equipment. For the 39 weeks ended April 1, Emulex reported a net loss of $2 million, or 19 cents a share, on sales of $88 million.
