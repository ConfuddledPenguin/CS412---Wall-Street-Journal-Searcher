Technology: Compaq Sets Up Dealers In East European Nations

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/02/90

LONDON -- Compaq Computer Corp. is setting up a network of personal-computer dealers in East Germany and Yugoslavia, in a campaign to establish operations in Eastern Europe. In an interview, Eckhard Pfeiffer, head of the Houston-based personal-computer maker's international division, said the company has appointed 13 private computer-dealers in East Germany and four in Yugoslavia to sell Compaq personal computers there. He added that he expects to expand the East German dealer-network to 30 outlets by year-end and also plans soon to select dealers in Hungary and Czechoslovakia.

The expansion adds Compaq, the world's second-biggest maker of personal computers, to the growing list of Western computer companies establishing sales networks in Eastern Europe. International Business Machines Corp., Digital Equipment Corp. and West Germany's Siemens AG are also expanding their dealer networks in the region. Spurring the computer companies is a decision last month by Western nations to liberalize technology trade with former East Bloc nations.

Mr. Pfeiffer said he can't predict Compaq sales in the region because of the shortage of hard currency in most of the countries. East Germany, he said, offers the best immediate sales prospects because of the country's currency union yesterday with West Germany.

He added that Compaq is naming Yugoslav-born Zelimir Ilic, its 45-year-old manager for the Middle East, Africa and some West European countries, as sales and marketing director in Eastern Europe.
