Insurers Pay $344.5 Million On Alaskan Oil Spill Claims

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/02/90

LONDON (AP) -- The London insurance market paid $344.5 million in settlement of the pollution cleanup from the Exxon Valdez oil spill in Alaska in March 1989. The Institute of London Underwriters, which represents the company marine insurance market, said it was the largest single marine claim ever paid by the London insurance market.

"Pollution cleanup resulting from the Exxon Valdez disaster and agreement of the claim was finalized only recently," said the institute's general manager, Peter Worsfold.

The money was paid to Thos. R. Miller & Son (Reinsurance Brokers) Ltd., representing the liability insurers who incurred the loss for the cleanup, the institute said.

Members of the institute paid $73.4 million, over 21% of the total. Institute spokesman Denzil Stuart said the rest was paid by Lloyd's of London underwriters and some European insurers.

The payment was made by electronic transfer through a new computer system for claims management which connects all the institute's companies and brokers.

"Without the new computer technology, settlement of a claim of this size and complexity, with the many interests involved, would have taken much longer to process," Mr. Worsfold said.
