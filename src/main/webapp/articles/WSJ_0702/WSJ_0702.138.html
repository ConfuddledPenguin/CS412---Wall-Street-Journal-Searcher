Go-Video Gets Shipments Of Its Dual-Deck VCRs

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/02/90

SCOTTSDALE, Ariz. -- Go-Video Inc. said it received the first shipments of its long-awaited dual-deck videocassette recorders from the manufacturer, Samsung Electronics Co. of South Korea. Go-Video has received purchase orders for 30,000 of the VCRs from its distributor, D&H Distributing Co., Harrisburg, Pa. Go-Video expects to fill those orders before the end of the year, said R. Terren Dunlap, chairman.

The dual-deck VCRs, which allow viewers to duplicate tapes or play one while recording another, will retail at stores in the Northwest, mid-Atlantic and Arizona, including Sharper Image and Hammacher Schlemmer. At Hammacher Schlemmer, the VCR will retail for $1,295 and have a five-year warranty.

Go-Video acquired the technology for its VCR by taking a group of Asian electronics companies to court two years ago, charging that they refused to sell it the technology to make the decks. Samsung was dismissed as a defendant in that case after it agreed to make Go-Video's patented dual-deck recorder. Litigation in that case is continuing against several other manufacturers, and Go-Video filed a second suit against the companies in January, alleging that they have monopolized the entire consumer-electronics business. The defendants include Matsushita Electric Industrial Co. and its Panasonic unit, Victor Co. and its U.S. JVC Corp. unit, Sony Corp., NEC Corp., Sharp Corp. and Sanyo Electric Co.
