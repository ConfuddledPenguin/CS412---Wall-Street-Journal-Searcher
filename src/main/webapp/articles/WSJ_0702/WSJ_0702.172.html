Business Brief -- Sundstrand Corp.: Milton Roy Stake of 15% Held, Takeover Is Planned

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/02/90

Sundstrand Corp., Rockford, Ill., disclosed that it has accumulated an almost 15% stake in Milton Roy Co. and will seek to acquire the St. Petersburg, Fla., maker of instrumentation and control equipment. In a filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission, Sundstrand said it holds 796,000 Milton Roy shares, or 14.99%. In a release, Sundstrand said it acquired the stock "with the intention of holding it as an investment in the near term," but "with the ultimate goal of initiating negotiations that will lead to an acquisition."

Sundstrand, which makes aerospace and industrial equipment, said it considers Milton Roy an "attractive strategic fit." In St. Petersburg, John McNamara, chief financial officer of Milton Roy, said: "We have a stated desire to remain independent." He added, however, that the board recognizes that it has "a fiduciary responsibility to assess the fairness and adequacy of any offer presented."

Sundstrand's filing wasn't disclosed until after the market closed Friday. In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange, Milton Roy stock closed at $22.875, off 12.5 cents; the company has about 5.3 million shares outstanding.
