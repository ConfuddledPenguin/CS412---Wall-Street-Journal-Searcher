Lionel Corp. Holder Group Is Frustrated And Says It May Act

  • Author: null
  • Date: 07/02/90

FORT LAUDERDALE, Fla. -- A dissident shareholder group in Lionel Corp., expressing frustration with the toy retailer's management, said it had begun to consider steps it might take in an effort to boost the value of the company. The group, led by Trendex Capital Management, previously has sought to press Lionel to find a buyer. The Trendex group, which holds 5% of Lionel, said it amended its filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission to reflect its wish to get greater accountability from Lionel executives and an increase in the company's share value. It cited repeated efforts by "Trendex. . . .other shareholders and the news media" to get answers about the company's low market value, "business practices and poor results," all to no avail.

Lionel officials couldn't be reached for comment on Friday.

Earlier this year, Lionel rejected investor Robert I. Toussie's offer of $8 a share, or $95.4 million. Since then, the stock has gradually declined; it closed Friday at $2.75, up 37.5 cents, in trading on the American Stock Exchange.

Recently, the company reported a wider fiscal first-quarter loss of $6 million, or 44 cents a share, although sales were up 7.5% to $65.9 million. The company cited reduced sales at existing stores and the expenses involved in opening new stores.
