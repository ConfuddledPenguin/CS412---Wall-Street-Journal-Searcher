Sumitomo Bank to Issue Debt in U.S. and Europe

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 06/29/90

In a highly unusual debt offering, Japan's giant Sumitomo Bank Ltd. said it plans to issue $1 billion of subordinated debt in the U.S. and Europe. Through underwriters led by Goldman Sachs & Co., which is 12.5%-owned by Sumitomo, the Japanese banking concern will issue $500 million in so-called Yankee bonds, which are dollar-denominated bonds issued by foreign companies.

"This is the first time anybody has ever brought a Japanese bank to the subordinated debt market," said Jon Winkelried, vice president in charge of new issues at Goldman Sachs.

At the same time, Sumitomo will offer $500 million of floating-rate notes in European markets. Sumitomo Finance International is expected to be the lead manager for the European issue.

Sumitomo Bank will be permitted to use the $1 billion to boost its capital base. Under capital standards agreed to by the Bank for International Settlements, which is owned by the central banks of 11 major industrial nations, banks involved in international business must achieve a capital-to-assets ratio of 8% by 1993.

Bankers said there is concern in the market that U.S. banks, which could also need to boost their capital bases with subordinated debt, might be crowded out by the appetites of Japan's huge institutions.
