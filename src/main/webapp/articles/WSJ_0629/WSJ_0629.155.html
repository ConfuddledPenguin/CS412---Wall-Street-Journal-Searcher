Circle Express Founder Gets Three-Year Term On Conspiracy Charge

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/29/90

INDIANAPOLIS -- The founder and former chief executive of Circle Express Inc. was sentenced to three years in prison and fined $250,000 after pleading guilty to conspiring to provide false statements to the Securities and Exchange Commission. Arthur L. Belford, who left the trucking concern in 1989, also agreed to surrender the Circle Express shares held by him and his family and to make some restitution to bondholders and shareholders.

Mr. Belford was the subject of a probe by the Federal Bureau of Investigation. He was accused of using a company condominium and airplane for personal purposes and of agreeing to misrepresent the company's financial condition in annual and quarterly reports.

The trucking concern is under new management.
