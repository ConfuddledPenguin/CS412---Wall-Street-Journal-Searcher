Business Brief -- Winnebago Industries Inc.: Industrywide Slump Fueled $4 Million Quarterly Loss

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/29/90

Winnebago Industries Inc., battered by an industrywide slump in motor home sales, reported a loss for its third quarter on a 21% decline in revenue. For the quarter, ended May 26, the maker of motor homes had a loss of $4 million, or 17 cents a share, on revenue of $89.7 million. A year earlier, Winnebago earned $301,000, or two cents a share, on revenue of $112.9 million.

The company, based in Forest City, Iowa, said results were hurt by lower demand for motor homes, a shift to lower-priced motor homes, start-up costs for its commercial vehicle division, and a loss by its Cycle-Sat subsidiary.

For the nine months, Winnebago had a loss of $14 million, or 57 cents a share, compared with net income of $6.6 million, or 27 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue fell 28% to $249.2 million from $348 million.
