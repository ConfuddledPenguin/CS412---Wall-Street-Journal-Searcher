USX Corp. Registers For Sale of Debentures Totaling $250 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/29/90

PITTSBURGH -- USX Corp., in an effort to raise cash and reduce debt, said it filed a registration statement with the Securities and Exchange Commission for the issuance of zero coupon convertible senior debentures due 2005. The offering is expected to result in proceeds of about $250 million, which will be used to reduce debt and for general corporate purposes. The energy and steel giant has about $5.9 billion in long-term debt outstanding. First Boston Corp. is the sole manager of the offering.

Each debenture will be convertible at the holder's option any time on or before the maturity date into -- at the company's option -- USX common stock or cash. The yield and conversion rate haven't been determined.

Holders have the right to sell debentures back to USX five and 10 years after issuance for cash, shares of common stock, or repayment notes, at USX's option. In addition, holders have the right to sell debentures back to USX for cash in the event of a change in the company's control.
