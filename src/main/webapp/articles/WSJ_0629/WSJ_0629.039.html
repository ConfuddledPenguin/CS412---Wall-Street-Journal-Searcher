General Mills' Net More Than Doubled In Fiscal 4th Period

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/29/90

MINNEAPOLIS -- General Mills Inc. reported that net income more than doubled in its fiscal fourth quarter ended May 27. Earnings in the quarter totaled $75.3 million, or 93 cents a share, compared with $35.6 million, or 45 cents a share, a year earlier, when discontinued operations reduced net by $22.1 million, or 27 cents a share. Sales rose 18.6% to $1.7 billion from $1.4 billion.

For the year, net income was lower, totaling $381.4 million, or $4.64 a share, compared with $414.3 million, or $5.06 a share, previously. However, last year's net included a $169 million gain from the sale of two retailing businesses.

For the year, earnings from continuing operations set a record, totaling $373.7 million, or $4.55 a share, compared with $315.3 million, or $3.85 a share, previously.

Sales for fiscal 1990 rose 14.7% to a record $6.4 billion from $5.6 billion.

Both consumer foods and restaurants sectors achieved double-digit sales and earnings gains for the year. Pacing the food side was ready-to-eat cereals, which despite capacity constraints increased its No. 2 share to 27% from 24.5% in fiscal 1989, the company said. Kellogg Co., which lost share over the last 12 months, has about 39% of the $7 billion domestic ready-to-eat cereal business.

General Mills stock rose on the news, closing at $90.125, up $3.75, in composite New York Stock Exchange trading.
