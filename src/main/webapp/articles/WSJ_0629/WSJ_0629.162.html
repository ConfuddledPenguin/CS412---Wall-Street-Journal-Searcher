Business Brief -- Pantera's Corp.: Firm Says Judge Approves Statement on Reorganization

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/29/90

Pantera's Corp. said a U.S. bankruptcy court judge approved a disclosure statement for a plan of reorganization that would merge the company into its Pizza Inn Inc. subsidiary and reconstitute it primarily as a franchise service firm. Pantera's, a Dallas-based owner and franchiser of Pizza Inn restaurants, would assume the name Pizza Inn Inc. under the plan and sell most of its 100 company-owned restaurants to existing or new franchisees. It would then concentrate on improved marketing and operations support for franchise restaurants, which currently number over 300, the company said.

Pantera's said the plan would give current shareholders a 5% stake in the reorganized company, while giving unsecured creditors a 62.5% stake. Management would receive 32.5% over several years. Secured creditors would be repaid over a period of five years, the company said.

Pantera's said the plan has the support of secured and unsecured creditors and of franchisees, and will be put to a vote by creditors and holders prior to July 18.

Jeff Rogers, president of Pizza Inn, said the company has been experiencing "new record average unit sales" recently, adding that "interest in international locations is increasing dramatically."
