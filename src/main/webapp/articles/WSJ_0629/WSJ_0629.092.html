REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: Children 2, Unions 0

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/29/90

The 1,000 low-income Milwaukee children who hope to use a new voucher plan to attend a nonsectarian, private school of their choice won two victories this week. The teachers' unions that had tried to block the plan lost their bid to have the case heard first by the Wisconsin Supreme Court. And the anti-voucher State Superintendent of Public Instruction, Herbert Grover, agreed to let schools join the program even if they hadn't filled out the maze of forms he had devised to frustrate them. Mr. Grover gave in one day before a hearing on a request for an injunction against him by pro-voucher forces. Applications for the program are pouring in by the hundreds and there's talk of expanding it if the results are good. That's why Mr. Grover and his union allies will still insist on enveloping the schools in the same stifling bureaucracy that has ruined Milwaukee's public schools. As the opposition to the choice plan grows ever more stubborn, we suspect that increasing numbers of parents of schoolchildren in the inner cities will challenge the bureaucratic status quo with teaching alternatives.
