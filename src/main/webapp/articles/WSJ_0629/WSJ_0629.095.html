Marketing & Media: Reliance Capital Units Add to Investments In Spanish TV Group

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/29/90

NEW YORK -- Reliance Capital Group L.P. continues to pump money into Telemundo Group Inc., the unprofitable Spanish language television network it controls. Reliance, Telemundo's principal shareholder, and Reliance Insurance Co. agreed to invest $37.5 million in the broadcaster. Both investors are affiliates of Saul P. Steinberg's Reliance Group Holdings Inc.

Reliance Capital will purchase two million shares from Telemundo for $12.5 million; Reliance Insurance will purchase four million shares for $25 million. The purchases will boost ownership to about 43% for Reliance Capital and 35% for Reliance Insurance of Telemundo's 36.9 million shares then outstanding. The two companies own about 74% of the company.

Telemundo will use the proceeds for working capital and debt repayment.

In the first quarter, Telemundo had a net loss of $14.7 million on $24.8 million of revenue, compared with a year-earlier loss of $17.2 million on $22.5 million of revenue. In 1989, the company had a net loss of $38.2 million on revenue of $116.4 million.

The two Reliance Group companies in December injected about $42.5 million into the company through purchase of about 8.1 million new shares.

Despite the losses at Telemundo, a Reliance spokeswoman said the network is "meeting management's expectations" with revenue continuing to increase.

In over-the-counter trading, Telemundo closed at $6.50 a share, unchanged.
