Technology Brief -- Altos Computer Systems Inc.: Talks on Possible Purchase By Taiwan Firm Disclosed

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/29/90

Altos Computer Systems Inc., a San Jose, Calif., maker of multiuser computer networks, said it is engaged in talks with the Acer Group regarding possible acquisition of Altos at a proposed cash purchase price of $8.35 a share, or about $90 million. Altos said material terms remain to be negotiated with Acer Group, a Taiwan-based maker of clones of personal computers made by International Business Machine Corp. The price now under discussion would represent a 60% premium over Altos's stock price, which recently had languished at about $5.3758 following several quarters of troubled performance.

Although the takeover prospects weren't formally announced until yesterday, Altos's stock took an anticipatory jump on Wednesday to close at $7.375 in national over-the-counter trading. Yesterday, Altos closed at $7.625, up 25 cents.

Altos has suffered recent losses and stock-price erosion due to inventory problems and new product start-up costs. In its fiscal third quarter ended March 14, Altos posted a loss of $1.7 million or 16 cents a share, on sales of $32.6 million.

Altos President Ron Conway said he hopes negotiations will be concluded "imminently." However, Altos cautioned in a press release that there can be no assurance that the two companies will reach a definitive agreement.
