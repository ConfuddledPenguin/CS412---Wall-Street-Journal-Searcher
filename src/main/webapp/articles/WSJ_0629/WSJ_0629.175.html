What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/29/90

JAPAN AND THE U.S. completed a broad accord designed to reduce their trade imbalance. Japan promised to spend more on public works, toughen antitrust policies and streamline its distribution system. The U.S. pledged to balance its budget, improve worker training and boost research spending. The pact got a cool reaction from some legislators, economists and business people. ---

Polaroid is letting Minolta sell a Polaroid instant camera to U.S. customers under a Minolta brand name. The highly unusual marketing move reflects Polaroid's sluggish sales.

---

Crude oil inventories are at an eight-year high, raising the prospect of tumbling petroleum prices if OPEC doesn't rein in production soon. Persian Gulf producers have been cutting their third-quarter contract prices.

---

Budget negotiators are talking increasingly about raising taxes on Social Security benefits or reducing the program's cost-of-living raises.

---

The earnings outlook for the second quarter is largely positive, despite some recent negative surprises, analysts and money managers say. This is good news for investors with broadly diversified stock portfolios.

---

Lotus's copyright on the 1-2-3 program was infringed by Paperback Software International in its VP Planner, a federal judge ruled. The decision could foreshadow similar verdicts in other software disputes.

---

Apple Computer has dropped plans for an initial public offering by its Claris software unit and intends to fold the business back into the parent firm, industry executives say.

---

A Mobil affiliate won clearance from the Nigerian government to proceed with the $1 billion development of a rich offshore petroleum field.

---

Bond prices rose as investors reacted to brisk demand for the latest Treasury note offering. The stock market also gained, boosting the Dow Jones Industrial Average 16.58 points to 2878.71. The dollar was mixed.

---

Venezuela reached an agreement with its major bank creditors that includes provisions for new loans and for cutting back old credits and the interest payments due on them.

---

Pathe's $1.31 billion bid to buy MGM/UA appeared in jeopardy after Pathe said it reached an impasse with Time Warner over financing. Pathe said it will seek other sources of funding, and continue talking to Time.

---

Manufacturers Hanover plans to take a substantial second-quarter charge linked to its cost-cutting plan. The banking concern also is placing more than $150 million of loans to Trump on nonperforming status.

---

AT&T will cut long-distance prices an average of 2.2%, or more than $300 million a year, starting Sunday.

---

Soybean planting covered 58 million acres this year, the government estimated, cutting its previous figure by 1.4 million acres. Some analysts expect soybean prices to climb today.

---

A group of insurers is developing a computer network to improve communications with independent agents. Critics are skeptical about the system's benefit to consumers.
