Abreast of the Market: Industrials Rise 16.58 as Investors Buy Firms With Proven Earnings

  • Author: Craig Torres and David Wilson
  • Date: 06/29/90

NEW YORK -- Investors' confidence in earnings was boosted a bit yesterday as some companies reported improved quarterly results, while a flight to shares of companies with proven earnings records continued to buoy the broad market. The Dow Jones Industrial Average closed 16.58 points higher at 2878.71. Many traders expect a rally today as big institutions continue to load up on the year's topperforming stocks on the last trading day of the second quarter. The industrial average would have to add more than 57 points to finish the quarter with a new record.

Though several stocks moved up sharply yesterday, New York Stock Exchange volume was light at 136,120,000 shares. Advancing issues outpaced decliners, 911 to 559.

Food stocks attracted buyers as quarterly earnings reports from General Mills and ConAgra were well received, while the drug and consumer products groups also were standouts.

American Telephone & Telegraph topped the Big Board's list of most-active issues for the second consecutive session. The stock fell 3/8 to 38 3/4 on volume of 3,753,100; the company Wednesday said it expects to post lower second-quarter earnings compared with a year earlier.

But among the Dow industrials, the setback in AT&T was offset by rallies among the safe-haven consumer stocks. Merck gained 1 7/8 to 86 1/8, Procter & Gamble climbed 2 to 85 3/4 and McDonald's rose 1 1/4 to 35 7/8.

"Investors are hiding in about 30 stocks" because they are afraid of negative second-quarter earnings surprises, said Thomas Gallagher, head of equity trading at Oppenheimer & Co.

Although International Business Machines eased 3/8 to 117 5/8, other technology issues helped lead the market's latest advance. Digital Equipment rose 3 1/8 to 85 1/4, Compaq Computer added 3 3/8 to 122 3/4 and Hewlett-Packard rose 1 1/8 to 47 3/8.

Broader averages also advanced. Standard & Poor's 500-stock index gained 2.49 to 357.63; the New York Stock Exchange Composite index rose 1.31 to 195.18, and the Dow Jones Equity Market Index rose 2.34 to 332.63.

General Mills, which reported a 31% increase in earnings from continuing operations for the fiscal quarter ended May 27, rose 3 7/8 to 90 1/4, a 52-week high. The latest results were stronger than most analysts had expected; after the report, Morgan Stanley analyst Kurt Feuerman increased his earnings estimates for fiscal 1991 and 1992 by five cents a share to $5.30 and $6.20, respectively, according to Dow Jones Professional Investor Report.

ConAgra added 1 3/4 to 35. Its earnings for the same period went up 13% from the year-earlier level and were on target with analysts' estimates.

Other food stocks to draw buyers included Gerber Products, which went up 2 1/8 to 54 7/8; CPC International, up 1 3/4 to 80; Kellogg, up 1 7/8 to 66 1/2, and Ralston Purina, up 1 5/8 to 93.

Conversely, Olin's forecast that earnings for the second quarter would be down 15% to 20% from a year earlier sent the stock down 1 7/8 to 49 3/4 before a trading halt late in the session.

Another victim of earnings-related selling was Sotheby's Holdings, which dropped 1 1/8 to 15 5/8. Salomon Brothers downgraded the stock's investment rating and cut its 1990 earnings estimate to a range of $2 to $2.10 a share from $2.25.

Pennzoil, which is canceling plans to register 3,690,000 common shares issued earlier this year in an acquisition, climbed 3 1/2 to 76 3/8. The decision means that the holders of those shares won't be able to sell them publicly.

Avon Products rose 7/8 to 36 1/4 after Smith Barney, Harris Upham added the stock to the firm's list of recommended issues.

Polaroid added 7/8 to 40 5/8. The company agreed to produce instant cameras for Japan's Minolta to market in the U.S. under the Minolta brand name.

Manufacturers Hanover lost 1/4 to 34 1/2 after disclosing that its second-quarter results would include an as-yet-undetermined charge for restructuring.

SunTrust Banks gained 1/2 to 20 7/8 after outlining plans for a pretax addition of about $65 million to loan-loss reserves in the second quarter. The company said it doesn't expect an examination by the comptroller of the currency, which resulted in the charge, to have a "significant adverse effect" on the period's earnings.

American Savings Bank, which suspended dividends on a preferred-stock issue, fell 3/8 to 2 3/8. The company said it expects a slowing economy and weakness in the commercial real estate market in the Northeast to depress its second-quarter results.

Bally Manufacturing was unchanged at 9 1/2. The company extended until July 11 an offer to exchange common stock for a $135 million issue of junk bonds; holders had tendered only about $20 million of the issue as of Wednesday, the original deadline.

The American Stock Exchange Market Value Index rose 1.52 to 358.57. Volume totaled 12,990,000 shares.

Energy Service dropped 1/4 to 4 1/4 in trading highlighted by a block of 1.5 million shares, which changed hands at 4 1/8 during the afternoon. The trade accounted for most of the 1.7 million shares to change hands in composite dealings.

Corrections & Amplifications

OLIN Corp. expects changes in its mix of businesses and certain other factors to contribute to strong second-half earnings that should result in full-year profit at the lower end of the company's per-share target range of $6 to $6.40. Friday's edition reported Olin's expectation that second-quarter earnings would decline 15% to 20% from the 1989 period's record level, but didn't mention the outlook for the second half and full year.
