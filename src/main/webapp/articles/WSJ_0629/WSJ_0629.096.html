Former Broker Settles Charges of Defrauding Pro Football Players

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/29/90

WASHINGTON -- The Securities and Exchange Commission said a former Robinson-Humphrey Co. broker settled charges that he violated securities laws by defrauding at least 18 professional football players. Without admitting or denying guilt, Oscar Ayala, now a salesman at the Shearson Lehman Hutton Inc. unit in Marietta, Ga., consented to the entry of a permanent injunction agreeing not to violate securities laws in the future.

Three weeks ago, the SEC filed a complaint against Mr. Ayala, alleging that he defrauded football players who held accounts at Robinson-Humphrey starting in 1988. The SEC, which didn't identify the players, alleged that Mr. Ayala misappropriated at least $1 million for personal use instead of purchasing securities for his clients.

Neither Mr. Ayala nor his attorney could be reached for comment.
