House Banking Panel Clears Bill to Boost S&L Enforcement

  • Author: Paul M. Barrett
  • Date: 06/29/90

WASHINGTON -- Amid fear of voter anger over the expanding savings-and-loan debacle, a House Banking subcommittee approved legislation to authorize an additional $154 million over the next two years for investigating and prosecuting crimes involving failed thrifts. The bill, approved unanimously yesterday, also would make it easier for government investigators to discover and punish S&L fraud. One provision would establish new authority for civil regulators to use tax records in thrift investigations. President Bush has expressed support for many of the expanded enforcement powers in the bill.

The new authorization would allow S&L enforcement spending by the Justice and Treasury departments on top of the $75 million already appropriated by the full House for fiscal 1991, which begins Oct. 1.

The legislation approved yesterday, which is thought to have a strong chance of being enacted, would make concealing assets from thrift regulators a federal crime. It would also establish a new statute barring fraudulent conveyances of assets by thrift executives and allowing enforcers to undo such transactions even if they have been completed.

The bill would increase wiretap authority for financial fraud investigators and would expand the government's ability to freeze assets before the conclusion of legal proceedings over alleged S&L-related fraud. It would also prevent S&L defendants from using state bankruptcy laws to shield their personal assets.
