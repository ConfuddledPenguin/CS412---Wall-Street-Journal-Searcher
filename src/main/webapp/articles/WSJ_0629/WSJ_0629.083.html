Initial State Jobless Claims Increased in June 16 Week

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/29/90

WASHINGTON -- Initial claims for regular state unemployment benefits rose to a seasonally adjusted 359,000 in the week ended June 16 from 356,000 in the previous week, the Labor Department said. The number of people receiving regular state benefits in the week ended June 9 was a seasonally adjusted 2,472,000, or 2.4% of those covered by unemployment insurance, up from 2,390,000 in the previous week, when the insured unemployment rate was 2.3%.

Counting all state and federal benefit programs, the number of people receiving unemployment benefits in the week ended June 9 fell to 2,253,200 from 2,269,900 a week earlier. These figures aren't seasonally adjusted.
