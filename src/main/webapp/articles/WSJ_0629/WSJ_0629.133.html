Bristol-Myers Buys Company

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/29/90

NEW YORK -- Bristol-Myers Squibb Co. said it agreed to acquire S+G Implants G.m.b.H from Beiersdorf AG. As part of the agreement, Bristol-Myers will sell its Jobst Institute Inc. unit to Beiersdorf. Terms weren't disclosed.

A Bristol-Myers spokeswoman said S+G Implants, a Lubeck, West Germany, maker of hip and knee implants with annual sales equivalent to $12 million, will complement the implant operations of Bristol-Myers's Zimmer division.

She said Jobst Institute, a Toledo, Ohio, maker of specialty medical products with $50 million in annual sales, no longer fit into the company's long-term growth plans. Jobst has been a Bristol-Myers subsidiary since 1983.
