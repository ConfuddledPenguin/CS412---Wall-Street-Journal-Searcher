Business Brief -- Union Texas Petroleum Holdings: Offshore Platform Planned For Oil and Gas Production

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/29/90

Union Texas Petroleum Holdings Inc. said it will construct a $74 million platform with processing facilities to produce oil and natural gas discovered on two properties it holds in the Gulf of Mexico. The Houston-based company said production from the platform, to be located about 150 miles south of Lafayette, La., is scheduled to begin in 1992, at a rate of 60 million cubic feet of gas and 3,000 barrels of oil a day.

Union Texas is the operator of Vermilion blocks 412 and 404, with a 50% interest in each. AGIP Petroleum Co., a unit of AGIP S.p.A. ENI Group, holds the remaining 50% interests in both properties.

Union Texas, 40%-owned by Kohlberg Kravis Roberts & Co. and 40%-owned by Allied-Signal Inc., recently said it was putting itself up for sale and hired an investment banker to assist it in the potential sale.
