Dividend News: American Savings Bank

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/29/90

AMERICAN SAVINGS BANK said its board voted to suspend payment of the quarterly cash dividend on its $1.8125 cumulative convertible preferred stock, normally declared in June for payment on Aug. 1. The thrift, which posted a first-quarter loss of $9.4 million, cited its poor operating results and uncertainties in the real estate market. The preferred dividend would have been $1.3 million. A spokeswoman could not say under what conditions American Savings might restore the dividend. In April, citing similar factors, American Savings suspended payment of its quarterly cash dividend on common stock. The unpaid preferred dividend will accumulate as an obligation of the thrift without interest. Under its charter and New York law, the preferred dividends in arrears must be paid before any common stock dividends.
