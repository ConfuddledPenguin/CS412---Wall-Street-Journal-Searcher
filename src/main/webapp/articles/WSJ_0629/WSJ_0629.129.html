Ampal-American Gets Offer by Main Holder To Buy Israeli Units

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/29/90

NEW YORK -- Ampal-American Israel Corp. said its principal shareholder, Bank Hapoalim B.M., has offered to acquire the industrial loan portfolios of Ampal's three banking subsidiaries in Israel. Under terms of the offer, Bank Hapoalim, Israel's largest commercial bank, would acquire the loan portfolios for $875 million, their book value at Dec. 31. The bank would pay $53 million in cash, all of which would be deposited at Bank Hapoalim and used over the next 15 years to pay off Ampal-American debentures.

The bank also would assume $822 million in liabilities. About $547 million of those liabilities are Bank Hapoalim funds deposited at the Ampal-American units.

The banking units of Ampal-American, which also has interests in Israeli industrial and agricultural projects, real estate, hotels and tourism, stopped making new loans in 1989. It said it could not compete with the rates of domestic Israeli lenders, which have benefited from changes in Israeli banking and currency regulations.

The sale of the units would "eliminate a lot of uncertainty for us," says Lawrence Lefkowitz, Ampal-American's secretary.

The offer, which expires Aug. 15, must be approved by committees made up of Ampal directors unaffiliated with Bank Hapoalim. Three of Bank Hapoalim's officers sit on Ampal-American's board. Ampal-American's president and chief executive, Michael Arnon, until April 1990 was a co-chairman of a Bank Hapoalim subsidiary. The bank owns 34% of Ampal-American's Class A stock and 87% of its common stock. The company's common is not publicly traded.
