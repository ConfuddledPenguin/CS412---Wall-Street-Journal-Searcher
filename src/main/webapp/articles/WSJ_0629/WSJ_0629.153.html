Los Angeles County Agrees To Settle Pollution Claim

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/29/90

WASHINGTON -- The Commerce Department said Los Angeles County sanitation districts agreed to pay about $12 million to settle a pollution claim and help clean up Los Angeles Harbor. Last month, the department's National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration sued eight corporations for allegedly polluting the coastal waters off Los Angeles and Long Beach, Calif. NOAA previously informed the county sanitation districts that it might also sue them for operating the sewers that carry pollutants, primarily the pesticide DDT and hazardous chemicals known as polychlorinated biphenyls, or PCBs.

In announcing the settlement, NOAA said the sanitation districts agreed to pay $1 million to help prepare cases against the eight corporations, $2 million to help determine the cause and extent of environmental damage in Los Angeles Harbor, $2 million to analyze what kind of restoration should take place, $2 million for a pilot restoration project and $5 million for other restoration projects.

NOAA said any money it collects from the eight companies will be used to restore waters and habitats. Such efforts could include treating underwater sediments, restocking fish and installing artificial reefs.
