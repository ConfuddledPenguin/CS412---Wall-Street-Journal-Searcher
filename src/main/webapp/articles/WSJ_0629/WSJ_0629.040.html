Nike Profit Forecast For Quarter and Year Sends Stock Soaring

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/29/90

BEAVERTON, Ore. -- Nike Inc. forecast better-than-expected fourth-quarter and year-end profits, sending its stock soaring. The stock closed at $77.50, up $4.25 in national over-the-counter trading yesterday, with about 1.3 million shares changing hands. Nike credited a combination of favorable accounting and tax changes for the boost in profits, which will exceed Wall Street's expectations.

Philip Knight, chairman of the athletic shoe company, said profit in the fourth quarter ended May 31 will be in the range of $64 million to $66 million, or $1.70 to $1.75 a share, exceeding analysts' estimates of $1.50 to $1.60 a share. In last year's fiscal fourth quarter ended May 31, 1989, the company posted profit of $36.5 million, or 97 cents a share.

For the year ended May 31, the company expects to report profit of $240 million to $242 million, or $6.35 to $6.40 a share, exceeding Wall Street estimates of about $6.15 to $6.25 a share. In fiscal 1989, profit was $167 million, or $4.45 a share.

The unexpectedly strong profits stem from a favorable revision in the company's last-in, first-out inventory reserves, and a lower-than-anticipated tax rate.

Nike said it plans to announce complete fourth quarter and year-end results July 9 at an analysts' meeting in New York.
