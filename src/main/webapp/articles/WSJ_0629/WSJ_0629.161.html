International Brief -- TSB Group PLC: British Bank's Pretax Profit For Fiscal Half Rose 6.7%

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/29/90

TSB Group PLC, one of Britain's biggest banks, reported pretax profit for the six months ended April 30 rose 6.7% to #175 million ($305.2 million) from #164 million a year earlier, as firm returns from retail banking and financial services offset depressed returns in corporate banking. Per-share earnings fell 2.7% to 7.2 pence from 7.4 pence.

The fiscal first-half pretax results were weaker than most market forecasts but a modest improvement from a year earlier, when TSB's pretax profit slid 22%. TSB, which also moved late last year to curb operating costs, doesn't disclose results by unit or specify loan-loss provisions. But it said pretax profit was held back by increased mortgage-exposure provisions of its retail banking division and advances by its corporate banking division to the property, construction and retailing sectors.

Retail banking, the largest contributor to TSB profit, rose to #117 million from #89.1 million, though TSB said the year-earlier figure isn't directly comparable, citing the continuing restructuring.
