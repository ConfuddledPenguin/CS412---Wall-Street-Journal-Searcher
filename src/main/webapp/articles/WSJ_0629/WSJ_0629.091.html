Kay Jewelers Arranges Revolving-Credit Pact Granting $80 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/29/90

ALEXANDRIA, Va. -- Kay Jewelers Inc. said it arranged an $80 million revolving credit agreement designed to meet working-capital needs through next January, replacing an $86 million line of credit. Kay also said that three directors invested $5 million in the company and have pledged to invest another $5 million later in the year.

Kay, which spent much of the spring trying to ease its credit crunch, said it also renewed a $90 million letter-of-credit commitment that will support its credit-card receivables program with NCNB National Bank.

The new agreements leave Kay with about $15 million in free credit and cash infusions to tide it over until the fourth quarter. Jewelry retailers such as Kay typically have negative cash flow for the first nine months of the year. As of March 31, Kay had only $114,000 in cash.

The $15 million "should be more than sufficient for our working capital requirements" through Jan. 31, said Michael Zarraga, Kay's treasurer. In addition to operating expenses, Kay has a $1 million payment on its senior debt due Aug. 1.

In connection with the latest transactions, Kay said three directors of the company who also are major shareholders -- Jorg Bucherer, Oliver Stahel and Chairman Anthonie van Ekris -- invested $5 million in Kay in exchange for subordinated convertible debentures.

Between mid-March and mid-May, four of Kay's bank lenders pulled about $16.5 million in credit lines. Three of them sought to withdraw $32 million more but ended up joining the new revolving-credit accord, Mr. Zarraga said. The accord is secured by all of Kay's inventory.
