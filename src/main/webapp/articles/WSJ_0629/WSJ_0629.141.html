Enterprise: SBA Puts 90-Day Freeze To Investing-Firm Licenses

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/29/90

WASHINGTON -- The Small Business Administration put a 90-day moratorium on approving new licenses for Small Business Investment Companies, beginning today. SBICs are privately organized venture capital firms that invest in small businesses. The SBA licenses and regulates these firms, and then makes loans to them. The companies then use those funds to invest in other concerns.

Sen. Dale Bumpers, (D., Ark), chairman of the Senate Small Business Committee, recently called for a license moratorium. The committee has been investigating SBICs since last spring when a Virginia-based SBIC went bankrupt, owing the government about $32 million. At a Senate hearing last week, SBA Inspector General Charles Gillum said his most recent review of the program shows government losses could exceed $800 million, up from a previously estimated $563 million.

SBA Administrator Susan Engeleiter said the moratorium includes SBIC license applications already in hand at the agency.
