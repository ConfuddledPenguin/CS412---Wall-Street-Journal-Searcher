Business Brief -- VSB Bancorp Inc.: Savings Bank Parent Sees $2.5 Million 3rd-Period Loss

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/29/90

VSB Bancorp Inc. said it expects to report a loss of $2.5 million for its third quarter as a result of the falling value of its real estate investments in New Jersey. VSB, a Closter, N.J., holding company for Valley Savings Bank, said it plans to add $2.8 million to its loan-loss reserves for the quarter ending tomorrow. VSB said $1.7 million of the addition will fully cover equity investments in a townhouse development in central New Jersey and a single-family home project in Morris County, N.J. The projects represent the bank's largest real estate investment.

The bank said it will reduce the value of its real estate held for investment to $9.4 million from $12.9 million.

---

Corrections & Amplifications

VSB BANCORP Inc. expects to report a third-quarter pretax loss of $2.5 million, which will result in "several hundred thousand dollars" of savings on an after-tax basis, according to a spokesman. Friday's edition failed to note the $2.5 million loss was before taxes.

(WSJ July 2, 1990)
