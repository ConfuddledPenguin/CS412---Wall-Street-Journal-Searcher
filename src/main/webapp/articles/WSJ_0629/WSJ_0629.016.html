Birmingham Steel Expansion

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/29/90

BIRMINGHAM, Ala. -- Birmingham Steel Corp. said it plans to spend $35 million to $45 million to double the melting capacity and upgrade the rolling operation at its Jackson, Miss., mini-mill. The mini-mill operator said it has begun engineering and environmental-impact studies for the proposed project, which includes replacing the melt shop furnace and continuous caster. The company said the expansion project, which should be completed in the summer of 1992, will enable the company to take advantage of the growing Southeast steel market.
