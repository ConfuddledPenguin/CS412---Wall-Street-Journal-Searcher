Toyota Plans to buy U.S.-Supplied Chips Over Next 3 Years

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/29/90

WASHINGTON -- The Toyota group announced plans to purchase as much as $94 million in U.S.-supplied semiconductors for use in its world-wide car production over the next three years. Hironobu Ono, a Toyota Motor Corp. electronics engineering manager, announced these purchasing plans after assuring U.S. trade officials that Toyota and its family of suppliers are doing their part in increasing U.S. access to the Japanese semiconductor market. Mr. Ono said that U.S. suppliers likely will increase their share of the group's semiconductor business to 10.9% in 1992 from 2.1% in 1989.

Toyota announced that, in addition to its purchases of U.S. standard chips, it has chosen five U.S. semiconductor makers to deliver as many as 23 types of custom-made devices for controlling transmission, suspension, engine, audio and other automotive functions. The U.S. vendors are Motorola Inc., Intel Corp., National Semiconductor Corp., International Rectifier Corp. and Texas Instruments Inc.

Mr. Ono said these U.S. devices could cost a little more than the Japanese ones that they are intended to replace, but are comparable in quality.

A Motorola spokesman said plans by Toyota and other Japanese carmakers to expand their purchases of U.S. semiconductors could push the U.S. closer to its goal of capturing 20% of the Japanese semiconductor market. The U.S. share currently is 11%.
