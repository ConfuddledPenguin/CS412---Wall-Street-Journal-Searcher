Business Brief -- Meridian Bancorp: Unit Reaches Pact to Buy Part of Travelers Operation

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/29/90

Meridian Bancorp, Reading, Pa., said its Meridian Mortgage Corp. unit agreed in principle to acquire the retail production operations of Travelers Corp.'s mortgage services unit. Terms weren't disclosed. But Meridian said the deal will create a combined retail mortgage-origination business with an annual loan volume of about $3 billion.

Included in the transaction are 16 branch offices in Connecticut, New York, New Jersey, Pennsylvania, Delaware, Maryland and Virginia, plus a customer service office and some administrative support operations. Meridian Mortgage won't acquire the Travelers operations in the New England region, however.

Meridian Mortgage said the proposed acquisition is consistent with the company's strategy to develop significant production outside its banks' geographic markets.

The pact follows the sale of the other part of Travelers mortgage-services business, including relocation services and mortgage-wholesale operations, to GE Capital Corp. Travelers had planned to sell the entire mortgage services unit to a management and investor group nearly a year ago, but the sale fell through.
