U.S. Vision Completes Acquisition of Royal International Optical

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/29/90

DALLAS -- Royal International Optical Corp., an optical-stores retailer and frame maker, said U.S. Vision Inc. completed its acquisition of the company and named the surviving entity Royal International Optical Inc. U.S. Vision, an optical retailer based in Glendora, N.J., paid $12.50 a share for 80% of Royal's 7.1 million shares outstanding and gave holders of the remainder a 25% stake in the new company.

William A. Schwartz, chairman and chief executive officer of U.S. Vision, assumed identical posts at the new company. Donald Gross, executive vice president of the former Royal, was named president, the company said.

Royal said it will operate 865 retail optical stores under the names Royal and Wall & Ochs and as a licensee of several major department store chains.

Mr. Schwartz said the new company, which will be traded on the over-the-counter market under the symbol RIOC, will divide its operations between New Jersey and Dallas. He said the company, which he described as the U.S.'s largest publicly held optical retailer, hasn't yet decided which city will be its headquarters.
