Business Brief -- Cyprus Minerals Co.: Concern Plans Write-Down For Coal-Mining Operation

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/29/90

Cyprus Minerals Co., a Denver-based mining company, said it will write down about $27.5 million of its investment in the Kanawha Coal Mine in West Virginia in the second quarter, and may spend about $7 million in development costs at the mine during the second half of the year. In the 1989 second quarter, Cyprus reported net income of $61.7 million, or $1.48 a share, on revenue of $453.5 million. Net income included a net charge of $4.7 million and a net special, one-time gain of $21.4 million. Analysts had projected Cyprus's second-quarter 1990 earnings at about 85 cents to 90 cents a share. That was in the ballpark before the write-down, a company spokesman said, but now that estimate is too high.

Cyprus, which acquired the property for $20 million in March of 1989, attributed problems at the mine to lower-than-anticipated reserves from the mountain-top mining area, higher-than-expected reclamation costs and poor performance of its highwall mining machines.
