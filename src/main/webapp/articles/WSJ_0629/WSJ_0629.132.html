Psychiatrist Pleads Guilty In Insider-Trading Case

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/29/90

NEW YORK -- A psychiatrist pleaded guilty to trading stock on inside information he obtained during sessions with the wife of financier Sanford I. Weill. Robert Howard Willis, accused of buying BankAmerica Corp. stock based on information he received from a patient during therapy, pleaded guilty to two counts of securities fraud in federal court in New York.

Dr. Willis admitted that he purchased BankAmerica stock when he learned that Mr. Weill, former chief executive officer of Shearson Loeb Rhodes, was seeking to become chief executive of BankAmerica, according to a statement released by the U.S. attorney's office in New York. In an opinion last month, the judge in the case revealed that the patient who allegedly disclosed the information to Dr. Willis was Mr. Weill's wife. Mr. Weill is chairman of Primerica Corp.

The case is believed to be the first to charge a doctor with trading on confidential information received from a patient.

The two counts to which Dr. Willis pleaded guilty involved the purchase of 400 shares of BankAmerica stock, according to his lawyer, Edward Brodsky.
