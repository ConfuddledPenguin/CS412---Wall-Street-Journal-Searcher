Airport Departure Taxes Approved by House Panel

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/29/90

WASHINGTON -- The House Public Works and Transportation Committee approved legislation that would permit airports to impose departure taxes to fund airport-expansion projects. The taxes, officially called passenger facility charges, currently are banned by federal law. Under legislation authored by Rep. James Oberstar (D., Minn.), chairman of the House Aviation Subcommittee, the 71 biggest airports in the U.S. would be permitted to impose fees of as much as $3 per flight. For a one-way trip, a traveler would have to pay no more than $6, regardless of how many airports were involved.

The legislation, which also authorizes $17.7 billion for airport construction and air traffic-control modernization in the next two years, now goes to the House floor. Transportation Secretary Samuel Skinner said yesterday's vote in repealing the departure-tax ban was an important step in dealing "with our serious congestion problem." The legislation was approved 36-10.

The outlook is uncertain in the Senate, where Democrat Wendell Ford of Kentucky, chairman of the Senate Aviation Subcommittee, opposes the tax. He believes that the $7 billion surplus in the federal government's aviation trust fund should be spent before passengers are compelled to pay additional fees, an aide said.

In a related development, the House panel approved legislation requiring special inspections for aging aircraft.
