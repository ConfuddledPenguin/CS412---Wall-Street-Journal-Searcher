July Economic Summit To Consider Soviet Aid

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/29/90

WASHINGTON -- President Bush and other Western leaders will discuss at their economic summit in Houston July 9-11 whether to provide aid and loans to the Soviet Union, according to the Treasury's undersecretary for international affairs, David Mulford. U.S. policy on the issue is still "under review," Mr. Mulford said. France and West Germany recently have pushed for more aid for the Soviet Union, but it's widely expected that discussions at Houston merely will lead to an agreement to study the issue further.

The U.S., Britain, Canada and Japan want to see more market-oriented reform in the Soviet Union before committing large sums of money.

Mr. Mulford said the leaders of the seven summit countries -- the U.S., Japan, West Germany, France, Britain, Italy and Canada -- also will review the state of the international economy. The Treasury expects the economies of the seven countries to grow 3% a year in 1990 and 1991.

The current Uruguay round of trade negotiations under the General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade also will be a major topic at Houston, said Mr. Mulford, who added that he expects there will be discussion of an impasse over how to address agricultural subsidies. International debt strategy and initiatives on the environment, drug trafficking and money laundering also will be summit items, Mr. Mulford said. He added that relations with China will be discussed as well.
