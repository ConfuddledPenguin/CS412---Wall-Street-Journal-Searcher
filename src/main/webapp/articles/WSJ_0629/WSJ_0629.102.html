AT&T Cuts Prices An Average 2.2%, Beginning Sunday

  • Author: John J. Keller
  • Date: 06/29/90

NEW YORK -- American Telephone & Telegraph Co. said it will cut prices an average of 2.2%, or more than $300 million annually, on interstate and international long-distance services, starting Sunday. The action comes only a month after AT&T, the nation's No. 1 long-distance carrier, said it would cut just its evening rates an average of 2.5%, or $160 million annually. Under the new plan, evening rates will drop 5%.

But AT&T last week said it would increase the size of its price cut, after the Federal Communications Commission ordered local phone companies to reduce the fees they charge AT&T and other long-distance phone companies for originating and terminating interstate phone calls.

That FCC-ordered reduction amounts to $1.1 billion over the next 12 months -- about $696 million more than the local phone companies had proposed in April.

AT&T's reduction applies to basic long-distance service, operator-handled calls, calls from the U.S. to Canada, and the company's optional calling plan named Reach Out America.

For example, effective Sunday, price reductions on direct-dialed, out-of-state calls will average 5% during evening calling periods from Sundays through Fridays, and one-half percent during the day on weekdays. The Reach Out America evening discount plan will drop to $7.80 a month from $8.15. And per-minute charges for operator-assisted calls will be cut an average of 3%.

The decrease is AT&T's 11th since the comany's breakup in 1984. Since then, longdistance prices have been reduced almost 43%, AT&T said.

AT&T's primary rivals, MCI Communications Corp. of Washington, D.C., and the US Sprint Communications Co. unit of United Telecommunications Inc., Kansas City, Mo., said they will take whatever pricing action is necessary to stay competitive with AT&T's rates.
