International: East Germany to Allow Free Flow of EC Goods

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 06/29/90

East Germany said that it will allow a free flow of goods from nations in the European Community beginning July 1. The Economics Ministry in East Berlin said the country also will adopt EC trading and customs rules following this Sunday's economic and monetary union with West Germany.

There had been some question whether East Germany would apply special restrictive measures such as high tariffs against goods from abroad to protect its fragile economy.

But that apparently won't be the case, now that the East German cabinet has decided the country's tariff structure should be aligned to that of the EC as a whole.

The ministry said that East Germany will honor all existing trade agreements with East Bloc countries and that trade with them will remain free of tariffs until the end of 1990.

East and West Germany are expected to merge fully into one country by the end of this year or in 1991. The ministry didn't mention how it would treat the import of goods from other non-EC countries.
