Business Brief -- Telecredit Inc.: Los Angeles Firm Reports 11% Rise in Quarter Profit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/29/90

Telecredit Inc., which agreed last week to be acquired by Equifax Inc., posted an 11% rise in fiscal fourth-quarter net income to $6.6 million, or 59 cents a share, on a small revenue increase. In the same quarter a year ago, Telecredit posted net of $6 million, or 54 cents a share. Revenue in the quarter ended April 30 inched up to $38.4 million from $38.2 million.

Telecredit, based in Los Angeles, guarantees personal checks for retailers and other merchants and processes credit cards for credit unions and small and medium-sized financial institutions.

Lee A. Ault III, chairman, said the company is "looking forward to revenue and net earnings gains in the second half of fiscal 1991 following a relatively flat first half" due to the loss of a major credit-union customer in October.

Atlanta-based Equifax, a major provider of credit reports and similar information for retailers, banks and insurers, has agreed in principle to acquire Telecredit in a stock swap valued at about $580 million. Telecredit said it expects a definitive agreement to be completed by July 10.
