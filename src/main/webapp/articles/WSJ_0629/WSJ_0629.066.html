Italy's Foreign Trade Account

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/29/90

ROME -- Italy's foreign trade account deficit widened to a provisional, unadjusted 2.495 trillion lire ($2.05 billion) in May from 2.139 trillion lire a year earlier and from 877 billion lire in April, the state statistical office Istat said. Istat also said the trade balance in this year's first five months showed a deficit of 12.506 trillion lire, slightly narrower than 13.501 trillion lire a year earlier.

The lira value of imports in May rose 5% to 19.805 trillion lire from a year earlier, and exports rose 3.5% to 17.31 trillion lire. In the first five months, imports rose 5.6% to 93.651 trillion lire, and exports grew 7.9% to 81.145 trillion lire.

Import value is calculated on a cost, insurance and freight basis, and exports are accounted for on a free-on-board basis.
