Business Brief -- Avon Products Inc.: Amendment to Poison Pill Held Invalid by U.S. Court

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/29/90

Avon Products Inc., New York, said a U.S. appeals court upheld a judge's ruling that invalidated an amendment to its "poison pill" shareholder rights plan. The Second Circuit Court of Appeals affirmed federal Judge Pierre Leval's ruling in April that Avon couldn't lower the so-called trigger level for the poison pill. Currently, if any buyer acquires more than 20% of the cosmetics company's shares outstanding, other holders would receive the right to buy another share at half-price for each share held.

Avon had sought to amend the rights plan by reducing the trigger level to 12.5%, in an effort to prevent Chartwell Associates L.P., the biggest Avon holder, from acquiring more stock at market prices without bidding for the entire company at an expected premium. Chartwell, which challenged the amendment, held 12.4% of Avon's shares at the time.

Avon said in a statement that all other aspects of its shareholder rights plan remain in effect. Avon shares rose $1 yesterday to close at $36.25 in New York Stock Exchange composite trading.
