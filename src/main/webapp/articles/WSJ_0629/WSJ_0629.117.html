Arkansas Regulator Seeks to Block Sale Of Thrift to Worthen

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/29/90

LITTLE ROCK, Ark. -- Worthen Banking Corp. said the Arkansas state banking commissioner filed a motion in federal court seeking to block the Resolution Trust Corp. from selling a thrift to Worthen, arguing that the move would violate state bank branching law. The move by Commissioner Bill Ford is the latest round in a battle being waged between several state banking commissions and the RTC, which argues that it has the power to override state laws to facilitate the sale of failed thrifts to banks. A federal judge in Santa Fe, N.M., ruled in the RTC's favor in a similar dispute earlier this month, and a federal judge in Colorado has also been asked to rule on the policy's legality.

Worthen said the Arkansas controversy arose after the RTC agreed to sell the holding company's Little Rock bank certain assets and liabilities of Independence Federal Bank FSB, Batesville, Ark. Under Arkansas law, banks are prohibited from acquiring and operating branches located outside the bank's home county.

Neither the RTC nor the Arkansas banking commission could immediately be reached for comment.
