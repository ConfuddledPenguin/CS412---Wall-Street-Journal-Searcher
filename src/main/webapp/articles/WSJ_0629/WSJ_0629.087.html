Who's News: NCNB Picks Lewis To Head Consumer, Commercial Banking

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/29/90

CHARLOTTE, N.C. -- NCNB Corp. said it created a new layer of management to oversee its commercial and consumer banking operations. The company named Kenneth Lewis, president of NCNB Texas, as president of the new group, called NCNB's General Bank. Mr. Lewis, 43 years old, thus becomes NCNB's top executive in charge of consumer and commercial banking, and a possible successor to the bank-holding company's 54-year-old chairman and chief executive officer, Hugh McColl.

"This certainly puts him in a leading spot -- if not the leading spot -- provided he doesn't commit hari-kari," said John Mason, a banking analyst with Interstate/Johnson Lane in Atlanta. "But that's well down the road -- at least several years."

Mr. Lewis, who will remain president of NCNB Texas until a successor is named, will move from Dallas to Charlotte, and will report directly to Mr. McColl.

In a statement, Mr. McColl said, "Ken Lewis's broad background, especially his consumer and commercial banking experience in Texas and Florida, prepares him well to head NCNB's General Bank."

NCNB has branch-banking operations in Texas, Florida, North Carolina, South Carolina, Georgia, Maryland and Virginia. Before becoming president of NCNB Texas in August 1988, Mr. Lewis served as president of NCNB National Bank of Florida. He joined the company in 1969.
