FCC Gives Lawyer Root One Week to End Practice

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/29/90

WASHINGTON -- The Federal Communications Commission disbarred Thomas Root, the lawyer who gained national attention a year ago when his small plane mysteriously crashed into the ocean off the Bahamas and he was found with a gunshot wound. The FCC gave Mr. Root until July 6 to end his FCC practice. Mr. Root, who didn't challenge his disbarment, had asked the FCC to allow him to continue practicing until Aug. 6, the day he is scheduled to be sentenced in federal court on five felony counts.

Mr. Root pleaded guilty June 6 to the five counts, including defrauding clients and submitting counterfeit documents to federal agencies. He faces up to 35 years in prison and $1.25 million in fines.
