Letters to the Editor: Where They Lie Down At Night Is Their Home

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/29/90

David Frum missed the point on his journey of bewilderment into Penn Station ("Why Would Anyone Live in Penn Station?" editorial page, May 30). Why, indeed, would anyone sleep in Penn Station, if he could go home to a nice warm bed, with a down comforter and a four-poster all around? These people are home. Operating in the same economic system as Mr. Frum, they wound up at the bottom. At that level, few boots have large enough straps for self-elevation.

I doubt if many of them would choose to remain there, if given the opportunity to live in a condo, or house, and wake up to a good-paying, interesting job each day. But I get the impression from his article that Mr. Frum believes they do have better accommodations elsewhere, with good jobs begging them anon. And, simply to vex him and his fellow travelers, they choose not to work, and then have the effrontery to spend their nights at Penn Station, sleeping off the results of their drunken celebrations at having beaten off the cursed evils of that demon called work for yet another day.

Capitalism and free enterprise have created great rewards and wealth for the majority of Americans, and will continue to do so. But they are competitive systems, and while winners do very well, the also-rans sometimes do not. Mr. Frum seems to believe that only the winners count, and appears to ask, somewhat bewildered, why everyone isn't a winner like himself.

The homeless and the jobless are the other face of the modern economic coin. Although somewhere along the way they lost their place in line for a share of the American dream, they, like Mr. Frum, still need to sleep somewhere, if only to dream.

Stephen Katzman

Boston
