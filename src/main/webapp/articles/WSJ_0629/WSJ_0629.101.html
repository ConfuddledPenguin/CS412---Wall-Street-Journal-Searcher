Consumer Confidence Falls, Survey Reports, As Job Outlook Sours

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/29/90

NEW YORK -- Consumer confidence declined significantly as the index compiled by the Conference Board fell almost five points this month to 102.5 from May's revised 107.3. The monthly survey, conducted for the board by National Family Opinion Inc. of Toledo, Ohio, found consumers less optimistic about the immediate future, particularly on the issue of jobs. The Conference Board is a nonprofit research center.

Less than 24% of all survey participants felt that jobs are "plentiful," a three point drop for the month. The survey uses the year 1985 as an index base of 100 and covers 5,000 households throughout the U.S.

Opinion is slightly less favorable on current business conditions, and on expectations for the next six months. Those who fear there will be fewer jobs now outnumber those who expect there will be more.

Slightly more respondents expect worse business conditions and a personal income decrease. Plans to spend money over the next six months on automobiles, homes, major appliances and vacations declined slightly.

Fabian Linden, executive director of the Conference Board's consumer research center, said, "While the measure of consumer confidence is now lower than it has been in more than two and a half years, there are no indications of impending recession."
