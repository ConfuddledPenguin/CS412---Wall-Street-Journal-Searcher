Business Brief -- Stone Container Corp.: Paper Mill in Connecticut Expands Recycling Capacity

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/29/90

Stone Container Corp., Chicago, said it opened an expanded operation costing $24 million at the paper and packaging company's paper mill in Uncasville, Conn. It said the operation will recycle 144,000 tons annually of used corrugated boxes to be collected from the New York City and other metropolitan areas in five Northeastern states.

In the expansion, part of a $200 million program to increase Stone's recycling capacity, the plant will make recycled paper for new boxes. Stone said 16 of its 24 mills world-wide purchase and recycle about two million tons of used corrugated boxes and newspapers annually, and an official said that "by 1992, we'll be consuming three million tons" a year.
