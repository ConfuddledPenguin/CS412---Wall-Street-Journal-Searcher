International Brief -- Broken Hill Proprietary Co.: Firm Sells 30% Shareholding In Australian Oil Company

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/29/90

Broken Hill Proprietary Co., Australia's biggest company, said it has sold a 30% stake in Woodside Petroleum Ltd. to Barclays de Zoete Wedd Australia Ltd. for 2.62 Australian dollars ($2.06) a share, or a total of A$525 million ($413.6 million). BHP said in January it was considering selling its stake in Woodside, an Australian concern that is one of six equal partners in a huge liquefied natural gas project offshore Western Australia state.

BHP said Barclays de Zoete Wedd, 51%-owned by Barclays Bank Australia Ltd., will sell the Woodside shares to institutional investors in Australia and overseas. BHP will retain a 10% stake in Woodside.

The North-West Shelf liquefied natural gas project started shipping gas to Japan in July. Peak LNG production of up to six million metric tons a year is scheduled to be reached in 1993. A metric ton equals 2,204.6 pounds.

Shell Australia Ltd., a unit of Royal Dutch/Shell Group, holds 34.3% of Woodside directly and 5.8% indirectly. In a separate statement, Shell Australia said it "has no intention of increasing or reducing" its shareholding in Woodside.
