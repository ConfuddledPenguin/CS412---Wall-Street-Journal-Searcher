Business Brief -- Manatron Inc.: Higher Profit to Be Posted For Fiscal '90, 4th Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/29/90

Manatron Inc., Kalamazoo, Mich., said it will report operating earnings of $225,000 to $275,000, or 11 cents to 13 cents a share, before the positive impact of its pension-fund adjustment, for the year ended April 30. With the pension-fund adjustment of approximately $250,000 to be recorded in the fourth quarter, Manatron will report net income of $475,000 to $525,000, or 23 cents to 25 cents a share, the company said. In its previous year, Manatron had net of $101,713, or five cents a share.

For the fourth quarter, Manatron expects to post operating earnings of between $75,000 and $125,000, or between four cents and six cents a share. In the year-earlier quarter, Manatron had net of $39,119, or two cents a share.

Allen F. Peat, chairman, said the software and computer-services company will report earnings in mid-July. For the nine months ended Jan. 31, Manatron posted net of $150,465, or seven cents a share, compared with $62,594, or three cents a share, the year before.

In a recent interview, Mr. Peat said he expected year-end revenue of $8.5 million to $8.7 million, compared with $7.7 million in fiscal 1989.

The higher revenue and earnings result from increased software sales, Mr. Peat said. Manatron reaped higher software sales from its movement to expand its personal computer-based based software and through the service contracts to maintain the computer systems.

"As we add new customers, the layer of service revenue continues to grow," Mr. Peat said, noting that nine of 10 new customers elect to purchase service contracts as well.

Manatron's complement of 30 major computer systems that it markets to governments is mostly complete, Mr. Peat said, and the company isn't planning to "expend a dramatic effort on increasing that." he added. Instead, the company will increase its product offerings on a selective basis, Mr. Peat said.

Manatron has customers nationwide, but its core of customers is in Michigan, Indiana, Illinois and Missouri. The company's basic customer is a midsized county. Manatron's computer systems automate functions such as bill collecting and property deed record keeping.

Industrywide sales of software to local governments reaches over $1 billion annually, and there is no identifiable industry leader, Mr. Peat said. Manatron's approach has been to develop software for a wide range of government processing and record keeping functions.

Manatron hopes to boost sales and earnings 20% annually in the future, Mr. Peat said.
