Mellon's Grant Street Unit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/29/90

PITTSBURGH -- Mellon Bank Corp. said its Grant Street National Bank unit, which is in liquidation, has deposited $65.7 million with Continental Bank N.A., trustee for holders of Grant Street's Class A-2 Extendible Pay-Through Notes. The deposit is sufficient to retire the remaining $61.2 million outstanding A-2 Notes on Nov. 1, 1990, their first call date, the bank said.

Mellon also said Grant Street has made an initial payment of $33 million to retire 325,000 shares of senior preferred stock, plus accrued dividends. Additional payments will be made as cash becomes available, the company said.

---

Corrections & Amplifications

GRANT STREET NATIONAL BANK is an independent, publicly held company. In Friday's edition, Grant Street National was incorrectly identified as a unit of Mellon Bank Corp.

(WSJ July 2, 1990)
