Moore Asks to Withdraw Guilty Plea on 5 Felonies

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/29/90

CHARLESTON, W.Va. -- Former West Virginia Gov. Arch A. Moore Jr. is seeking permission to withdraw his guilty plea on five felonies relating to a scheme to extort money from a coal operator and to buy votes. In a federal court filing, attorneys for the three-term Republican governor maintain that his plea was "the result of a hasty decision forced by a deadline imposed by the government." The filing seeks permission to plead not guilty.

Mr. Moore pled guilty to charges of extortion, fraud, tax evasion and obstruction of justice. Those charges carried a maximum 36-year prison sentence and $1.2 million in fines.

The filing says Mr. Moore was given one day to decide to plead guilty or face an indictment on 28 counts, including a federal racketeering charge.

Moreover, the filing said Mr. Moore was strongly advised by his counsel, William G. Hundley, to plead guilty after the government presented Mr. Hundley with recorded tapes relating to the obstruction charge. The filing said the government played the tapes and then required a signed plea agreement within one day to avoid the 28-count indictment.

Michael Carey, a U.S. attorney, said he would file a memorandum refuting Mr. Moore's claim and request the court to proceed with sentencing July 10.
