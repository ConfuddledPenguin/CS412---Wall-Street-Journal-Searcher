Ecogen Sets Pact to Let Investor Boost Stake To About 36% From 9%

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/29/90

LANGHORNE, Pa. -- Ecogen Inc. said it signed a letter of intent with New York investor David Blech for Mr. Blech to increase his stake in the company from about 9% to about 36%. Under the proposed agreement, Mr. Blech will buy 3.25 million newly issued shares for $2 a share, or $6.5 million. Because Mr. Blech is injecting new capital, the biotechnology firm said it is no longer seeking a buyer for the company. In February, Ecogen retained Piper, Jaffray & Hopwood Inc. to assist it in reviewing its options, including the sale of all or part of the company.

Under terms of the proposed purchase, Mr. Blech will also receive a seven-year warrant to purchase as much as four million additional shares at $3 a share. In addition, he will have the right to name a majority of nominees to Ecogen's board.

The company currently has about 7.5 million shares outstanding.

Under a March agreement with Mr. Blech, the company can require him to buy 666,666 shares at $2.25 a share. As part of that pact, Mr. Blech also received a warrant to purchase 500,000 shares at $4.50 a share as part of that pact. Ecogen said that both the company's put option and Mr. Blech's warrant will expire upon the closing of the current transaction, which is expected to occur in July.
