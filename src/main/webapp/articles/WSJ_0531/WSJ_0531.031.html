Dividend News: First Connecticut Small Business Omits Payout

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/31/90

BRIDGEPORT, Conn. -- First Connecticut Small Business Investment Co., which lends to and invests in small-business concerns, said it will omit its dividend indefinitely, citing poor results and falling values in the New England real estate that secures many of its loans. The quarterly cash dividend of 25 cents a share was last paid Jan. 26. The company had a loss of $460,667, or 39 cents a share, for its fourth quarter ended March 31, according to David Engelson, president. For the fiscal year, First Connecticut's earnings declined 42% to $1.2 million, or $1.05 a share, from a year earlier.

Mr. Engelson said the dividend omission will help the company conserve cash, but he emphasized that First Connecticut remains "basically solid. We just want to see our earnings before declaring a dividend." Dividend payments amounted to about $1.1 million in fiscal 1990, and about $2.3 million in fiscal 1989, when an extra dividend of $1 was paid, he said.

First Connecticut shares plunged $1.875, to close at $9.50, in American Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday.
