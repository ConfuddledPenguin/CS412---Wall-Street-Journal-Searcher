Meyer Emerges As Chief Contender For Harvard Post

  • Author: James A. White
  • Date: 05/31/90

NEW YORK -- Jack R. Meyer, treasurer of the Rockefeller Foundation, emerged as the leading candidate to head Harvard University's $5 billion endowment, people familiar with the university said. The search to find a new president for the Harvard Management Co., Harvard's investment arm, neared a conclusion this week as the management company's board met on the issue. People familiar with the situation said a formal announcement of the choice is expected soon.

Mr. Meyer, a 1969 graduate of the Harvard Business School, declined to comment.

The presidency of Harvard Management is considered one of the premier jobs in the money-management business. In addition to handling the nation's largest private university endowment, Harvard Management has gained prestige in the investment world as an early and large investor in such areas as venture capital, oil and gas properties and leveraged buy-outs.

While Mr. Meyer already holds a top investment job at the Rockefeller Foundation, a move to Harvard would be a major step up. Rockefeller Foundation has about $2 billion in assets and relies heavily on outside money managers to handle its investments. Harvard Management, on the other hand, is a much larger organization, with a staff of about 115 people, and manages many of its investments directly.

Harvard has been looking for a new endowment head since January, when the university announced that Walter Cabot, the only president of Harvard Management since it was set up in 1974, was stepping down, following a 1989 heart attack.

Finding a replacement for Mr. Cabot is believed to have been a priority for Harvard University President Derek Bok, who announced Tuesday that he planned to leave his post at the end of the next academic year. A Harvard spokesman said yesterday he wasn't aware of any announcement plans regarding Mr. Cabot's replacement.

The selection of Mr. Meyer would be "a superb choice," said David Swensen, director of investments for the Yale University endowment. "He's one of the most impressive institutional fund managers I've ever met," Mr. Swensen said.
