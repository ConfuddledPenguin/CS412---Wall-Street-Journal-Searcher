Business Brief -- Harnischfeger Industries Inc.: Firm Plans to Buy Stake, Possibly 20%, of Measurex

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/31/90

Harnischfeger Industries Inc., Milwaukee, said it plans to buy shares in Measurex Corp., in a move it says will "strengthen the existing relationship" between its 80%-owned Beloit unit and Measurex. Harnischfeger isn't obliged under the agreement to purchase any shares, but said it had agreed not to exceed a 20% Measurex stake through its purchases. Harnischfeger produces construction equipment and industrial machinery. Beloit makes equipment for the paper industry.

Measurex, a Cupertino, Calif., maker of industrial process-control systems, has about 17.8 million common shares outstanding.

In composite New York Stock Exchange trading yesterday, Measurex shares climbed $2.25 to close at $21.875.

Additionally, Harnischfeger said, Beloit and Measurex entered into a joint marketing, sales and development agreement which expands an existing 1987 agreement to cover additional products.

As part of the link between the companies, Harnischfeger's chairman and chief executive officer William W. Goessel, will join Measurex's board, increasing membership to eight.
