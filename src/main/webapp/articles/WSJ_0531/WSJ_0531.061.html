International Brief -- S.G. Warburg Group PLC: Fiscal 1990 Pretax Earnings Rose 68% to $318.4 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/31/90

S.G. Warburg Group PLC, a British merchant banking and stockbroking concern, said pretax profit for the year ended March 31 rose 68% to #188 million ($318.4 million) from #112 million a year earlier, but warned it is unlikely to match this performance in fiscal 1991. The pretax profit was below some market forecasts. Fiscal first-half pretax profit more than doubled to #102.6 million, largely from corporate finance activity; its pace slowed in the second half.

Profit after taxes and minority interests jumped 67% to #112 million from #66.9 million, and fully diluted per-share earnings rose 64% to 49.9 pence from 30.4 pence. Net profit attributable to shareholders, after preference-share dividend payments, increased 73% to #108 million from #62.5 million. Warburg said it will recommend a final dividend of 10.5 pence a share, raising the total payout for the year to 15 pence from 12.5 pence.

In its cautious fiscal 1991 forecast, Warburg cited tight monetary policies and high real interest rates as reducing corporate takeover activity, and low securities trading volume.
