Business Brief -- Phoenix Technologies Ltd.: Phoenix Technologies Says 14 Firms Licensed Software

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/31/90

Phoenix Technologies Ltd. said 14 personal computer makers will license its EISA-standard system software, suggesting new strength for the EISA computer communications standard over International Business Machines Corp.'s MCA design. EISA, which stands for extended industry-standard architecture, was developed last year by a group of PC makers led by Compaq Computer Corp. to counter IBM's effort to push a new standard that wasn't compatible with existing PCs. IBM says its MCA -- or micro-channel architecture -- design allows faster data exchange within and among its computers.

In a statement, Ron Fisher, president and chief executive officer of the Norwood, Mass., company, said, "We continue to see a groundswell of development activity on the EISA" design. Phoenix, which sells system software that allows PC makers to build IBM-compatible computers, said its EISA products are made for PCs using Intel Corp. 80386 and 80486 chips.

Companies licensing the EISA compatibility products from Phoenix include Intel, NEC Corp., Tandy Corp. and Altos Computer Systems.
