New Securities Issues

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/31/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: MUNICIPALS

Port Authority of New York and New Jersey -- $201 million special project bonds, Series 2, Continental Airlines Inc. and Eastern Airlines Inc. LaGuardia Airport Passenger Terminal Project, due Dec. 1, 2005, 2010 and 2015, repriced by a Smith Barney Harris Upham & Co. group to lower yields by 12 to 13 basis points. The bonds are now priced to yield from 9.38% in 2005 to 9.63% in 2015, changed from priced to yield from 9.50% in 2005 to 9.75% in 2015. There are now $51 million of 9% bonds priced at 96.96 to yield 9.38% in 2005, changed from $50 million of 9% bonds priced at 95.98 to yield 9.50% in 2005. There are $30 million of 9% bonds priced at 95.52 to yield 9.50% in 2010, changed from $30 million of 9.13% bonds priced at 95.56 to yield 9.63% in 2010. There are now $120 million of 9.13% bonds priced at 95.27 to yield 9.63% in 2015, changed from $130 million of 9 1/4% bonds priced at 95.32 to yield 9.75% in 2015. Interest on the bonds will be treated as a preference item in calculating the federal alternative minimum tax that may be imposed on certain investors. The bonds are not rated by Moody's Investors Service Inc. or Standard & Poor's Corp.

Illinois Regional Transportation Authority -- $100 million general obligation bonds, due Nov. 1, 1991-2005, 2010 and 2020, tentatively priced by a Bear Stearns & Co. Inc. group to yield from 6.10% in 1991 to 7.30% in 2010. Current interest serial bonds are priced at par to yield from 6.10% in 1991 to 7.20% in 2005. There are $16.64 million of 7.30% term bonds priced at par in 2010 and $57.07 million of 7.20% term bonds priced at 99 3/4 to yield 7.22% in 2020. The bonds due in 2020 are noncallable. The bonds are AMBAC-insured and rated Triple-A by Moody's and S&P.

Arkansas Development Finance Authority -- $93.4 million various single-family mortgage revenue bonds, 1990 Series A, due Sept. 1, 2023 and current refunding bonds, Series B, due Sept. 1, 2016, tentatively priced by a PaineWebber Inc. group to yield 6.15% for Series B bonds due in 2016 and 6.35% for Series A bonds due in 2023. The bonds have a mandatory put date of March 1, 1991. Interest on the Series A bonds will be treated as a preference item in caluculating the federal Alternative Minimum Tax that may be imposed on certain investors. The Series B bonds are not subject to the Federal alternative minimum tax. A rating of SP1-plus is expected from S&P, according to an official with the lead underwriter. A rating wasn't applied for from Moody's.

Los Angeles Municipal Improvement Corp., Calif. -- $85 million leasehold revenue bonds, City of Los Angeles Central Library Project, Series 1990 A, due June 1, 1994-2011, 2015 and 2020, apparently won by a Bank of America NT&SA group. The bonds, rated conditional-double-A by Moody's and provisional-single-A-plus by S&P, were priced to yield from 6.25% in 1994 to 7.25% in 2020. Current interest serial bonds are priced to yield from 6.25% in 1994 to 7.15% in 2010 and 2011. There are $16.6 million of 7.10% term bonds priced at 98.85 to yield 7.20% in 2015. There are $28.67 million of 7.20% term bonds priced at 99.39 to yield 7.25% in 2020.

Howard County, Md. -- Bulk of $55.6 million consolidated public improvement bonds, 1990 Series A, due May 15, 1991-2010, and metropolitan district bonds, 1990 Series A, due May 15, 1991-2020, were won by a Chase Securities Inc. group. The $36.5 million consolidated public improvement bonds, 1990 Series A, were priced to yield from 5.90% in 1991 to 7.00% in 2008-2010. The balance of the issue, $20 million metropolitan district bonds, won by a Shearson Lehman Hutton Inc. group. The bonds were priced to yield from 5.90% in 1991 to 7.10% in 2011 and 2012. Bonds due in 2013-2020 aren't formallly reoffered. The bonds are rated doubleA-1 by Moody's and double-A-plus by S&P.

New York State Dormitory Authority -- $51.6 million revenue bonds, Fordham University issue, due July 1, 1994-2000, 2005 and 2015, were tentatively priced by a First Boston Corp. group to yield from 6.40% in 1994 to 7.34% in 2015. Current interest serial bonds are priced at par to yield from 6.40% in 1994 to 6.90% in 2000. There are $9.6 million of 7 1/4% term bonds priced at par in 2005 and $32.98 million of 7 1/4% term bonds priced at 99 to yield 7.34% in 2015. The bonds are AMBAC-insured and rated triple-A by Moody's and S&P.

Birmingham Airport Authority, Ala. -- $50.1 million airport revenue bonds, Series 1990 A and B, due July 1, 1994-2005, 2010 and 2020, tentatively priced by a Smith Barney Harris Upham & Co. Inc. group to yield from 6.30% for non-AMT bonds due in 1994 to 7.52% for AMT bonds due in 2020. Series A current interest serial bonds are priced at par to yield from 6.50% in 1994 to 7.40% in 2005. There are $5.17 million of 7.40% term bonds priced at 99.25 to yield 7.46% in 2010. There are $17.19 million of 7.25% term bonds priced at 96.75 to yield 7.52% in 2020. Series B current interest serial bonds are priced at par to yield from 6.30% in 1994 to 7.20% in 2005. There are $3.52 million of 7.20% term bonds priced at 99.25 to yield 7.27% in 2010. There are $11.98 million term of 7.13% bonds priced at 97.75 to yield 7.31% in 2020. Interest on Series A bonds will be treated as a preference item in calculating the federal alternative minimum tax that may be imposed on certain investors. Series B bonds aren't subject to the federal alternative minimum tax. The bonds are AMBAC-insured and rated triple-A by both Moody's and S&P.

University of California Regent's -- $50 million revenue bonds, 1990 Seismic Safety Projects Issue, due Sept. 1, 19912005, 2010 and 2020, apparently won by a Dean Witter Reynolds Inc. group. The bonds, rated single-A-plus by S&P, priced to yield from 6% in 1991 to 7.30% in 2020. Current interest serial bonds are priced to yield from 6% in 1991 to 7.10% in 2004 and 2005. There are $8.39 million of 7 1/4% term bonds priced at par in 2010 and $28.85 million of 7.30% term bonds priced at par in 2020.
