Technology: Japanese Ministry Clears Standard for DAT Players

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/31/90

TOKYO -- The Ministry of International Trade and Industry officially approved a standard for digital audio tape players Wednesday, clearing the way for Japanese makers to produce machines for the mass market. A year ago, the major hardware makers from Japan and Europe agreed on a standard for the equipment, but they had to seek clearance from their governments. DAT machines reproduce sound more clearly and accurately than conventional tapes.

Spokesmen from Sony Corp. and Matsushita Electric Industrial Co., which makes Panasonic equipment, said the companies will probably start selling machines in the U.S. later this year.

It still isn't clear, however, whether record companies will produce the necessary software for the machines. Many record companies have expressed concern that the DAT machines will encourage greater private copying in violation of copyright rules. The standard is supposed to discourage such behavior by limiting the ability of the tapes to copy from compact disks.

Some Japanese companies displayed DAT machines at the Las Vegas, Nev., consumer electronics show earlier this year, but those were only prototypes, a ministry official said. More companies will probably display such machines at the electronics show in Chicago, which opens Saturday.
