International Brief -- SmithKline Beecham PLC: European Cosmetics Lines To Be Bought by Benckiser

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/31/90

In another move to reduce its debt, the Anglo-American pharmaceutical company SmithKline Beecham PLC said it agreed to sell its European cosmetics businesses to closely held Joh. A. Benckiser G.m.b.H. of West Germany, a household chemical company, for #210 million ($355.6 million). The sale, which requires West German government approval, involves the Margaret Astor and Lancaster businesses, which had 1989 sales of #264 million; the rights to several designer brands, and manufacturing facilities in West Germany, France and Spain. Benckiser's payment will be made in at least two installments, the first for about #176 million on completion of the sale, expected in about a month. SmithKline said the deal brings to #701 million the sum it has raised in its continuing program of selling assets.

Benckiser said the acquisition will boost its 1990 sales more than 50% to 3.4 billion marks ($2.03 billion). The purchase, Benckiser's first in the cosmetics industry, also marks its second deal with SmithKline; in December, it bought the U.S.-based Household Products operations for #66 million.
