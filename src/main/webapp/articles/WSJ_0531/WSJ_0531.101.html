OTC Focus: Market Is Higher as Technology Stocks Are Mixed; Adobe Systems Falls 2 1/2

  • Author: Anne Newman
  • Date: 05/31/90

Over-the-counter stocks gained slightly as ardor for technology shares cooled a bit. The Nasdaq Composite Index rose 1.26 to 458.77, a 0.28%, gain, matching the rise in the Dow Jones Industrial Average. Advancing stocks continued to lead over declining shares, 1,041 to 923, as volume rose to 169.4 million from 134 million Tuesday.

"I saw them take everything a little too fast and too soon right at the opening," said James Tarantino, head of OTC trading at Hambrecht & Quist in San Francisco. The Nasdaq Composite Index gained more than 2 points by midmorning before fading. "That left {stocks} vulnerable later in the day," Mr. Tarantino said, adding that he saw some "nervousness" in late-afternoon trading.

Technology shares are beginning to falter with the approach of summer, typically a slow season for the computer industry, according to two managers of technology funds. "Summer generally is not a good time to own tech stocks," said William Keithler, manager of the $75 million Financial Dynamics Fund of Denver-based Financial Programs.

Larry Bowman, manager of the Fidelity Select Computer Fund and Fidelity Select Technology Fund, Boston, explains that summer demand for computer wares are particularly weak in Europe, where August is a big vacation month. Typically there's a sell-off in technology shares by the end of the summer, when computer companies are reporting weak revenue, Mr. Keithler added.

Mr. Keithler and Mr. Bowman said they've already begun paring back their technology holdings, taking profits in stocks they think have become too expensive and holding on to shares of companies with strong earnings-growth momentum. Both men said they are selling shares of Apple Computer.

Mr. Bowman, who noted that the last quarter of the year is typically a strong one for technology stocks, said he's holding on to issues of Pyramid Technology, Quantum, Applied Materials, MCI, Maxtor and Intelligent Electronics. Mr. Keithler said he likes LSI Logic, Seagate Technology and Intel.

Shares of Adobe Systems continued to trade heavily, falling 2 1/2 to 35 3/8 on 3.7 million shares. Technology analyst Peter D. Schleider of Wessels, Arnold & Henderson, Minneapolis, said in an interview he downgraded his investment opinion on the Mountain View, Calif., software maker to "hold" from "buy."

Mr. Schleider said that given Adobe's surprise revenue outlook last week, along with his earnings estimate for the company, he thinks the stock is fairly valued at $30 a share, but no higher. The analyst pointed out that Adobe has been losing some market share for inexpensive software for printers to Hewlett-Packard and isn't expected to come out with a competitive product until the fourth quarter. He estimates that Adobe will earn $1.65 a share in fiscal 1990, ending in November, and $2.45 in 1991. But, he added, the company must do well in the next two quarters to reach those levels. In the long run, however, he said that he believes Adobe "is a very solid company."

Elmwood Federal Savings rose 1/4 to 13 1/4. Susquehanna Bancshares of Lititz, Pa., said it will now buy the Media, Pa., bank in a stock swap valued at about $15.85 a share, down from an indicated value of about $19 a share agreed to in November. The revised terms call for an exchange of shares at a rate of 0.88 Susquehanna share for each for each Elmwood share outstanding. Susquehanna said the price was reduced "in light of recent changes in the market for bank stocks."

Psicor gained 1/4 to 8 1/2 after the San Diego, Calif., medical service company said that Diatek Inc. will develop for Psicor a data base management system used in surgery.

Immucor rose 1/4 to 14 3/4 after the Norcross, Ga., maker of blood-bank reagent systems and related products said late Tuesday it plans to acquire its German distributor, Immucor G.m.b.H, which markets blood collection bags and Immucor's products.

First American (Tennessee) advanced 1 to 14 3/4 after the Nashville, Tenn., bank named vice chairman James F. Smith Jr. to replace chairman, president and chief executive officer Kenneth L. Roberts on an interim basis.

Goulds Pumps added 3/4 to 21 3/4. Goldman Sachs analyst Thomas G. Burns Jr. spoke positively about the stock, Dow Jones Professional Investor Report said. The company's chief financial officer, John Morphy, told DJPIR there weren't any corporate developments to account for the rise, but said he was comfortable with Mr. Burns's estimates that Goulds will earn $1.50 a share in 1990 and $1.70 a share in 1991, up from $1.41 in 1989.

Office Depot tumbled 2 1/4 to 18 3/4 on nearly eight times average volume. The Boca Raton, Fla., operator of office supply stores said that sales for the second quarter ending in June will be lower than originally expected. The company said it plans to open nine new stores in the quarter, but several will open later than anticipated. Office Depot also said that gross margins are being hurt by pricing competitiveness, but it hopes to offset that weakness in other ways.

Survival Technology slid 1/2 to 9 3/4 after the Bethesda, Md., medical technology concern said two-thirds of its common stock outstanding was left to the Stanley J. Sarnoff Endowment for Cardiovascular Science Inc. by Dr. Sarnoff, the company's founder, who died May 23. The company's board elected James H. Miller, president, to succeed Dr. Sarnoff as chief executive.

Rax Restaurants dipped 1/4 to 2 1/8. The Columbus, Ohio, chain of fast food restaurants reported that the loss in the fiscal third quarter ended April 30 narrowed to 10 cents a share from a loss of 27 cents a year earlier.
