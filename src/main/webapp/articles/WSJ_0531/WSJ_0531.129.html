Who's News: Pinola Ends 12-Year Stint At Helm of First Interstate

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/31/90

Joseph J. Pinola today ends his ill-starred 12-year tenure as chairman and chief executive officer of Los Angeles-based First Interstate Bancorp, the nation's eighth-largest bank. Mr. Pinola yields his posts to Edward M. Carson, 60 years old, who moves from president to chief executive and chairman as of today. William Siart, 43, who has been running First Interstate's profitable California unit, becomes president of the parent.

This is a bitter moment for the proud, headstrong Mr. Pinola. Analysts and industry executives give him credit for assembling a far-flung and potentially lucrative retail franchise in the western U.S. But Mr. Pinola wasn't able to capitalize on that network.

He had visions of First Interstate dominating banking in the West, yet at the end of his reign, he finds his reputation as banker is tainted and his beloved company staggered by disastrous decisions he and his subordinates made, most notably an acquisition in Texas that has cost First Interstate about $500 million since 1987.

Mr. Carson has effectively been running the company since Mr. Pinola announced his retirement in January. He has said that he intends to rebuild the company from within, concentrating on streamlining First Interstate's bloated cost structure, one of the worst in banking.
