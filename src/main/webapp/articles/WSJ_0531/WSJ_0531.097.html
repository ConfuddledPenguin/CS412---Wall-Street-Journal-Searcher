First American Corp. Dismisses Top Officer Amid Earnings Woes

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/31/90

NASHVILLE, Tenn. -- First American Corp., struggling with problem loans, dismissed its chairman, president and chief executive officer, Kenneth L. Roberts. The banking company said James F. Smith Jr., 60 years old, currently vice chairman, will fill Mr. Roberts' positions until a successor can be found.

Problems at First American, which has seen its earnings fall drastically in recent quarters, reached a head three weeks ago when the company posted a loss of $44.8 million, or $1.94 a share, for the first quarter, compared with net income of $15.4 million, or 67 cents a share, in the year-earlier period. The loss, the company said, stemmed from a $94.4 million provision for possible loan losses after net charge-offs of $60.8 million of loans, primarily related to real estate.

The results prompted First American to eliminate its dividend for the second quarter of this year.

First American said Mr. Roberts was dismissed by "mutual agreement" between himself and the board. Neil Cunningham, executive vice president, said the departure "obviously" was connected to First American's recent performance, but he declined to comment further.

Mr. Roberts, 57, couldn't be reached for comment. He joined First American in 1976, and was named chairman and chief executive in 1979.

He is the second top executive to leave First American during the past year. In August, the company's president and chief operating officer, Andrew Higgins, resigned. While no reason was given for his departure, it coincided with a major revision downward in second-quarter earnings. At the time, Mr. Roberts assumed the position of president.

Mr. Roberts' dismissal "wasn't surprising, given recent developments at First American," said Henry J. Coffey Jr., an analyst at J.C. Bradford & Co. in Nashville. Added Jon Burke at Robinson Humphrey Co. in Atlanta: "It was a hard decision, but it appears to be the right one."
