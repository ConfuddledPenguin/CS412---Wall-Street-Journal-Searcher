Letters to the Editor: Why You'll Never See Haitians Row to Cuba

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/31/90

This is in regard to Jose de Cordoba's April 19 article recalling his trip to Cuba. It may be true that you do not see children begging in Havana or "misery belts" surrounding the city. Other things that you do not see in Havana are freedom and prosperity. You do not see the small business. Or the corner newsstand where people drink a cup of coffee and enjoy a political discussion. Or the middle-class neighborhoods where families live and enjoy watching their uncensored TV programs.

By Mr. de Cordoba's standard, most cities in the world wouldn't compare favorably with Havana. Yet we do not see Haitians rowing their rafts to Cuba. But we do see Cubans rowing to Miami.

One thing you do see in Havana, and not in other Latin American capitals, are hotels and restaurants for the exclusive use of tourists. There is no other country that excludes its citizens, and its currency, from tourists' resorts. Newlyweds cannot spend their honeymoon in the better Cuban resorts. A Cuban cannot take his family out to dinner at one of these exclusive restaurants. This is the way life is in the "socialist paradise" of Cuba.

Something puzzles me. Journalists who visit Cuba always speak of the free health care enjoyed by Cubans. On the other hand, our relatives in Cuba are always requesting medicine in their letters. The answer is that Fidel Castro has been better at building hospitals than providing medicine. Furthermore, he has done all of this by turning Cuba into one of the world's indebted nations. His legacy will be political prisons and a massive foreign debt.

When Mr. Castro moves from the political scene, and Cuba's doors are finally opened to the world, we will learn the truth of Cuba. As with Romania and the other East European countries, we will learn about the corruption, the faulty health care, the ecology crisis and the people's discontent.

Communism has been far better at impressing U.S. journalists and academicians than at gaining the acceptance of local citizens. We saw this in Eastern Europe. And we will soon see what Cubans have known for 30 years: The venerated Mr. Castro and his socialist paradise have been a big fraud.

Silvio Canto Jr.

Carrollton, Texas
