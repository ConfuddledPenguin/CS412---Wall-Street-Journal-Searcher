Bally Offers Exchange Of Over 20% Stake To Retire Junk Bonds

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/31/90

CHICAGO -- Bally Manufacturing Corp. offered to exchange a more than 20% stake in the gambling and health-club concern to retire a four-year-old, $135 million issue of junk bonds. The move is what Bally called a "first step" toward shoring up its heavily leveraged balance sheet and improving its stock price. Bally said it also is considering a variety of asset sales, refinancings, joint ventures and other measures.

The bonds, sold through Drexel Burnham Lambert Inc. in January 1986, have been trading at about 53 cents to 55 cents on the dollar, Paul Johnson, Bally's chief financial officer, said. Bally offered 57 shares of its common stock for each $1,000 bond, or a total of about 7.7 million newly issued shares if all the bonds are tendered. The company currently has about 28 million shares outstanding.

Bally's stock tumbled $1.50 yesterday on the news to close at $10.625 in Big Board trading.

Despite greatly increasingly the number of shares outstanding, Bally said the equity-for-debt swap would increase per-share profit by reducing the company's debt load. The bonds, due January 2001, pay 13 5/8% interest, and are the company's costliest debt.

Bally had first-quarter profit of $7.6 million, or 21 cents a share, compared with $11.8 million, or 37 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue was $551.3 million compared with $516.7 million. The company wouldn't project future 1990 results or say how much per-share net would be increased by the exchange.

In all, Bally has about $1.77 billion of long-term debt, Mr. Johnson said, and its 1989 interest expense was $206 million. "The market perception is that we had too much debt," he said.

Bally joins some other junk-bond issuers in trying to buy back its debt at a deep discount to the original price. Mr. Johnson said, however, that since the company is under no financial pressure to retire the bonds, it wouldn't sweeten the offer. "It's terribly unlikely we would do anything else," he said.
