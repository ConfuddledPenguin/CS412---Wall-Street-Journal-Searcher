South Korea to Limit Stocks

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/31/90

SEOUL -- The South Korean government will limit the supply of new stocks to 300 billion won ($427.4 million) a month in a bid to revive the stock market, the Ministry of Finance said. Ministry officials said the government will limit the issuing of shares by listed companies to 250 billion won a month and by previously unlisted companies to 50 billion won. The limits will remain in effect until the stock market recovers from its current bearishness, they said. The market's key index has fallen about 14% this year, despite government measures.

Financial institutions, which have provided a large amount of new stocks to the market recently, will be banned from issuing new shares until market sentiment improves, under the ruling. Small and midsized companies, which face more difficulties in raising capital than large corporations, will be given priority in getting government approval for issuing new shares.

Under the ruling, stocks issued this year could total only about 3.4 trillion won, including 1.3 trillion won to date, compared with 14.1 trillion won in 1989.
