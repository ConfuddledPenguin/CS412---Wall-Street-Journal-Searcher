Commerce Agency Sets Role for Participation In Automation Talks

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/31/90

WASHINGTON -- The Commerce Department said it will assume responsibility for negotiating U.S. participation in Japan's plans to pool international know-how in automated manufacturing. The measure effectively halts Tokyo's earlier attempts to recruit individual U.S. companies and trade associations for its Intelligent Manufacturing System, or IMS, program, and it gives the department the right to coordinate any U.S. role. "Our aim is that everyone's interest be served in a balanced manner," Commerce Secretary Robert Mosbacher said in a department statement.

The statement said the action is based on an agreement reached earlier this month in Brussels by the U.S., the European Community and Japan. At that meeting, the U.S. and the EC agreed to submit proposals for taking part in the IMS program, while Japan agreed to refer all private U.S. inquiries to the Commerce Department.

A Commerce Department spokesman said yesterday that before any U.S. company can participate in the IMS program, there must be an agreement detailing the methods of cooperation, the ownership of patent rights, the nature of the funding, and the specific areas of research. The original Japanese plan was to create an international consortium to design the automated factories of the future, using the most advanced computer and artificial-intelligence techniques. Some U.S. and European officials had charged that the scheme was a Japanese "Trojan Horse" intended to obtain Western technology without a reciprocal pooling of knowledge.

A Japanese Embassy spokesman here declined to say how Japan will implement the agreement.
