Business Brief -- Webcott Holdings: Resolution on Del Webb's Takeover Plan Is Approved

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/31/90

Webcott Holdings, holder of a 9.7% stake in Del Webb Corp., said its nonbinding shareholder resolution to remove Del Webb's anti-takeover provisions was approved by more than 78% of the holders who voted on the proposal, according to an official tally. The votes in favor of the proposal represented a majority of the company's shares outstanding.

Del Webb, a Phoenix, Ariz., real estate development concern, had said last week that a preliminary report of inspectors of election for its May 16 annual meeting indicated that the resolution had passed.

The resolution by Los Angeles-based Webcott, controlled by Del Webb director James L. Cotter, requested that the board redeem shareholder rights issued under a so-called "poison pill" plan adopted last year. In April, Del Webb agreed to several of Mr. Cotter's demands to avert a proxy fight at the annual meeting. A poison pill plan is aimed at making hostile takeovers prohibitively expensive.

A special committee of the company's board will consider options and make a report to the board regarding the nonbinding resolution.
