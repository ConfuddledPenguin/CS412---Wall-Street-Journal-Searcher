Business Brief -- Agnico-Eagle Mines Ltd.: Shareholder to Ask Court To Block Planned Offering

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/31/90

Agnico-Eagle Mines Ltd., Toronto, said it was notified that one of its shareholders will seek a temporary court injunction to block the gold mining company's planned offering of 40 million Canadian dollars (US$33.9 million) in common shares and share-purchase warrants. Application for the injunction will be made tomorrow in Quebec Superior Court by Jacques Forget, who owns about 3% of Agnico's shares, the company said. Agnico said Mr. Forget also will seek a permanent injunction, on grounds concerning the company's management, operations and policies. Mr. Forget, who has said he plans to establish a group of holders to pursue a takeover bid for Agnico, has accused the company of "errors in management, which undermined shareholders' equity and pushed down the share price."

Agnico said it will contest the injunction applications, which it said are "totally without merit." The company said it will proceed with its planned offering of shares and warrants.
