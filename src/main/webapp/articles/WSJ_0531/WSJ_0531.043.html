Technology Brief -- Lotus Development Corp.: Lotus Development Buys Alpha's Software Package

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/31/90

Lotus Development Corp. said it acquired the AlphaWorks integrated software package from Alpha Software Corp. and will market the package as LotusWorks. Terms weren't disclosed. The software, which provides word processing, data management, financial analysis and communications functions in a single package, competes with Microsoft Corp.'s popular Microsoft Works product.

The purchase strengthens the Cambridge, Mass., company in the entry-level segment of the market for integrated software. Its Symphony product is aimed at high-end customers, but that market isn't growing as fast. Symphony sells for $695, while LotusWorks will sell for $149.

Alpha Software says it has shipped more than 300,000 copies of AlphaWorks since its first version was introduced in 1983. The program has been well-received by reviewers and software experts.

Lotus also said it has struck agreements with several personal computer manufacturers, including Olivetti Office USA, Packard Bell and Poquet Corp., which will sell the product bundled with some PCs.
