Politics & Policy: Nelson Wins GOP's Slot For Arkansas Governor

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/31/90

LITTLE ROCK, Ark. -- Former Arkla Inc. executive Sheffield Nelson won the Republican nomination for governor of Arkansas, besting Tommy Robinson in a bitterly personal campaign. The defeat of Mr. Robinson, a flamboyant U.S. congressman who described himself as a "junkyard dog," was also a setback for the powerful Little Rock investment-banking firm of Stephens Inc. Chairman Jackson T. Stephens heavily backed Mr. Robinson, causing Mr. Nelson to charge that the chairman was out to destroy him politically.

Democratic Gov. Bill Clinton, who's widely thought to harbor presidential ambitions, won renomination for a fifth term. But the governor's percentage of the vote, about 54% in a six-candidate race, was lower than expected.

Mr. Nelson is the former chief executive officer of Shreveport-based Arkla, owner of Arkansas-Louisiana Gas Co. He's a Democrat-turned-Republican, as is Rep. Robinson, whose political conversion was announced by President Bush last year.
