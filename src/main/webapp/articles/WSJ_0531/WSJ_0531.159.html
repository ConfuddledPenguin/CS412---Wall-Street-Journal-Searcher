Marketing & Media -- Advertising: Xerox in Talks With Agencies About a Domestic Ad Project

  • Author: Joanne Lipman
  • Date: 05/31/90

Xerox Corp., a major blue-chip client of Backer Spielvogel Bates, is talking to other agencies about its advertising, industry executives said. The executives said Xerox has asked the agencies on its roster to compete for a domestic advertising project. Backer, a unit of Saatchi & Saatchi, typically handles most of Xerox's domestic account, which billed close to $20 million last year. The other agencies on the big electronics company's roster include Ogilvy & Mather, Young & Rubicam and Lowe Marschalk.

Rumors have been swirling for months that Xerox was about to put its account at Backer up for review. The loss would be a painful one, especially since Backer lost the $60 million Prudential Insurance account earlier this year.

But one of the executives involved in the pitch for the ad project stressed that the full account isn't up for review. "I'm sure that they aren't even thinking review," he said. "They have a little problem; if (an agency) is on a roster of a client, it helps out on things that aren't its account. This happens quite often. It's a way a client can get help without putting their account up for review."

Xerox executives couldn't be reached, and executives at the agencies involved declined comment. However, all three agencies aside from Backer have been hoping to pry away the domestic business from Backer, whose predecessor agency Ted Bates snared the account in 1986. "We've been chasing the business for years," conceded an executive at one of the competing agencies, who asked not to be identified. Clearly, the competing agencies are hoping the project work will turn into a bigger chunk of the domestic business.

All three agencies have powerful reasons for coveting more Xerox work. Ogilvy, a unit of WPP Group, has been working desperately to turn around its troubled New York office. Earlier this month, it picked up the $45 million Eastern Airlines account, its first major win in more than two years. Another prestigious win would help wipe out the poor morale and problems stemming from account losses and management turnover that have plagued the agency's New York office for several years. Ogilvy currently handles some direct response work for Xerox.

Lowe Marschalk, which also handles some Xerox direct response work, has been trying to build its roster of blue-chip clients. It recently won the Prudential account away from Backer. Young & Rubicam, meanwhile, is among the most competitive of all agencies. After some setbacks last year, including lawsuits -- later settled -- involving its backing of a start-up agency and its work on the Jamaica account, it also is looking for a big win.

The agencies have been unsuccessful so far, though, in wooing the Xerox account away from Backer, despite persistent rumors to the contrary. Backer's hallmark is its service, and its hands-on attention from its chairman, Carl Spielvogel. Mr. Spielvogel was traveling and couldn't be reached for comment.

Ad Notes. . . .

TENNIS: Bud Collins, tennis commentator for NBC Sports, was named editor in chief of World Tennis magazine, where he has been a contributing editor for 20 years. He retains his post at NBC. Family Media, which publishes World Tennis, said Mr. Collins will work with editor Steve Flink in "maintaining the general direction of the magazine in the 1990s."

WHO'S NEWS: Jane Gundell was named president at Weiss, Whitte, a small New York agency. She was a management supervisor at Chiat/Day/Mojo.

ACCOUNT: Herald Center, New York, awarded its ad account to AC&R Advertising, a Saatchi & Saatchi Co. unit. Billings weren't disclosed.
