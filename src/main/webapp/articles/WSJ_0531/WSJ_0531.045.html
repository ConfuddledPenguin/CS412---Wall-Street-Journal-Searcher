Business Brief -- Quantum Corp.: Western Digital Will Pay $4 Million to Settle Lawsuit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/31/90

Quantum Corp., a Milpitas, Calif., maker of hard-disk drives for personal computers, said it has settled a patent infringement suit that it filed against Western Digital Corp. in 1988. As part of the agreement, Western Digital, Irvine, Calif., said it will pay Quantum $4 million. The suit charged that certain disk drive products that Western Digital acquired from Tandon Corp. in 1988 infringed on patents covering Quantum's wedge-servo architecture and the popular Hardcard disk drives made by Quantum's subsidiary, Plus Development Corp.

Western Digital said it stopped making the contested drives that use wedge-servo architecture at the end of last year. It said it agreed, as part of the settlement, to sharply limit production of its version of Quantum's Hardcard, which Western Digital calls FileCard, by Jan. 1, 1991.

A company with a reputation for zealously guarding its patents, Quantum has successfully waged patent wars against several rivals. In recent years, it has won $6 million from Computer Memories Inc. and $3 million from Japan's NEC Corp. Last year, it escalated a legal feud with Sony Corp., filing a federal lawsuit that accused the Japanese firm of patent violations and unfair business practices.
