Who's News: Deere & Co. Names Chief Hans Becherer To Additional Post

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/31/90

MOLINE, Ill. -- Deere & Co. named Chief Executive Officer Hans W. Becherer to the additional post of chairman. Mr. Becherer, 55 years old, succeeds Robert A. Hanson, 65, who is retiring. Mr. Becherer succeeded Mr. Hanson in the chief executive post last August.

Mr. Becherer, a 28-year veteran at the maker of farm and industrial equipment, previously also held the title of president; Deere yesterday named as president and chief operating officer David H. Stowe Jr. The post of chief operating officer has been vacant since last August.

Mr. Stowe, 53, formerly served as the company's executive vice president, worldwide agricultural equipment and consumer products. He was succeeded in that position by 56-year-old Bill C. Harpole, formerly senior vice president, world-wide parts and logistics.

Bernard L. Hardiek, 50, formerly Deere's vice president for business development, planning and taxation, assumed Mr. Harpole's former posts.

Mr. Harpole was named a Deere director, as was Crandall C. Bowles, president of Springs Co., expanding board membership to 15. Messrs. Stowe and Becherer were already board members, and Mr. Hanson will remain on the board.
