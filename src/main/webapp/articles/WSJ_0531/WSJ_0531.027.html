International: France Wants 5 To Be Charged In Insider Case

  • Author: E.S. Browning
  • Date: 05/31/90

PARIS -- French prosecutors asked that insider trading charges be brought against five people, including the French finance minister's onetime chief of staff, in connection with a failed 1988 raid on banking company Societe Generale. The former chief of staff is Jean-Charles Naouri, who had left the government and become a banker by the time of the alleged insider trading.

Mr. Naouri declined to comment, saying he would wait for the judicial process to be completed. He earlier acknowledged buying Societe Generale shares in July 1988, before the raid was launched. He said he bought 10 million francs ($1.8 million) of the shares for the Euris investment fund that he manages and 1.5 million francs of the shares for himself. He said he sold the shares before the raid on the company, and he has denied wrongdoing.

Mr. Naouri did confirm that before he bought the shares he was contacted by businessman Georges Pebereau, who later orchestrated the raid. He said he turned down Mr. Pebereau's offer of jointly investing in Societe Generale but later decided to make the investment himself.

The others against whom charges were sought are three investors and the estranged wife of one of the raid's backers.

The Societe Generale raid became politically controversial in France, where some viewed it as an effort backed by Mr. Naouri's former boss, Socialist Finance Minister Pierre Beregovoy, to manipulate the political composition of Societe Generale shareholders. The insider trading allegations shook the government and weakened Mr. Beregovoy politically.
