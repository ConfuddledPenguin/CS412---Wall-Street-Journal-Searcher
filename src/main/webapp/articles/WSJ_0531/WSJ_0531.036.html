Business Brief -- Chrysler Corp.: Customer Cash Incentives On Car Lines to Be Cut

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/31/90

Chrysler Corp. will reduce the cash it offers consumers on at least three of its car lines, when it renews its buyer incentive program this week. A spokesman confirmed that the Highland Park, Mich., auto maker will reduce to $750 from $1,000 the rebate to buyers of its Dodge Shadow and Plymouth Sundance compact cars; its Chrysler LeBaron line; and its Dodge Spirit and Plymouth Acclaim midsized cars.

Chrysler officials attributed the reduced incentives to stronger sales and lower stocks of the cars. Sales of the Spirit and Acclaim vehicles this year through May 20 rose 34% from a year earlier, Chrysler said. Inventories of the cars represent about a 50-day supply, relatively low for the industry. Chrysler said its aggressive sales campaign, which compares the cars directly to those built by Japan-based auto makers, has helped boost sales.

Chrysler isn't the only auto maker to cut incentives recently. Ford Motor Co. this month reduced its cash incentives on some of its more popular models.
