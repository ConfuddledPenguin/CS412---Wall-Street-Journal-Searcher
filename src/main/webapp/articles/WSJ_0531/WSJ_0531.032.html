Wendy's Chief Says Company to Perform `Much Better' in 1990

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/31/90

NEW YORK -- Wendy's International Inc. said its restaurants are running better, its revamped menu is selling better and its outlook is more optimistic than many analysts think. Wendy's President and Chief Executive Officer James W. Near said that analysts' second-quarter operating earnings estimates of eight to 10 cents a share are "low" and that the company will do "much better" in all of 1990 than last year.

April sales were very strong, Mr. Near said; the trend seemed to continue into May.

Rumors have circulated that Wendy's may be a candidate for takeover-rumors that the company won't address.

Today, Shearson Lehman Hutton raised its earnings estimate to 35 cents a share for 1990, rating Wendy's as likely to outperform the market by 10 to 20 percentage points.

The fast-food industry is emerging from a period of discounting that swallowed profits as restaurants offered coupons and other inducements to diners. Wendy's problems were compounded by financial difficulties at some franchises.

Mr. Near says Wendy's expanded too rapidly through the early and mid-1980s, selling franchises to owners who wanted the investment and not the work. The company has been atoning for those errors by buying back ailing franchises.

While the company was able to show improved net income for 1989, thanks to a five cent-a-share gain from an accounting change and a two cent gain from early extinguishment of debt, operating results eroded from 1988. Wendy's net from operations last year was $23.7 million, or 25 cents a share, down from $28.5 million, or 30 cents a share, in 1988. Revenue increased slightly to $1.07 billion, from $1.06 billion in 1988.

Wendy's revenue fell in the first quarter of 1990 to $241.3 million, from $259.5 million a year ago. Operating profit increased to $3 million, from $814,000 in the 1989 first quarter. The 1990 quarter included a $499,000, or one-cent-a-share, gain on extinguishment of debt for net income of $3.5 million, or four cents a share. The 1989 quarter included $339,000 from extinguishment of debt and $5.2 million, or six cents a share, from an accounting adjustment for net income of $6.3 million, or seven cents a share.
