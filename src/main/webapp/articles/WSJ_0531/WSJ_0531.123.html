Smith Barney Is Sued By Former Employee Over Sale of Shares

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/31/90

LOS ANGELES -- A former employee has sued Smith Barney, Harris Upham & Co. for allegedly forcing employees to resell stock to the securities firm just before a merger with Primerica Inc. in 1987. According to a complaint filed in Los Angeles Superior Court by John L. Heath, a former first vice president in Smith Barney's San Francisco office, the firm falsely claimed it had the right to force repurchase of shares owned by employees in Sbhu Holdings Inc. The suit, which seeks class-action status, is requesting unspecified damages.

Mr. Heath said he bought blocks of stock annually in the holding company, under a company stock-purchase plan, accumulating shares with a book value of more than $300,000 between 1979 and 1985.

In his complaint, the executive said he left the company in 1985 and was subsequently told three times by Smith Barney that he must sell portions of the shares back at book value. The last repurchase of about $200,000 of shares came just two months before the Primerica acquisition, Mr. Heath alleges in his suit.

It was common practice among Wall Street brokerage partnerships to require partners who resigned to gradually sell back their stock in the partnerships. Smith Barney was a partnership before being acquired by Primerica, and had such a stock buy-back plan.

The complaint says that Smith Barney breached its fiduciary duty to employees because it didn't disclose the pending acquisition by Primerica in which shares were acquired at more than double book value.

Richard Callahan Jr., Mr. Heath's attorney, said, "The company had the right to buy back the stock under certain terms of the employee agreements, but those terms weren't met. It's our position, and we intend to prove, that there must have been awareness that at least negotiations {to merge with Primerica} were going on, and employees should have been given knowledge of that." A Primerica official said the company and its Smith Barney unit hadn't seen the complaint, and couldn't comment.
