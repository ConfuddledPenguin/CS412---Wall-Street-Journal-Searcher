Foreign Exchange: Mark Weakens on Trader Nervousness About the Summit, Soviet Developments

  • Author: John Bader Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 05/31/90

NEW YORK -- Populist Boris Yeltsin's election as president of the Soviet Union's Russian Republic and expected wrangling at the Bush-Gorbachev summit over the military status of a unified Germany highlighted underlying nervousness about German unification yesterday, driving the mark broadly lower. The mark dropped almost a pfennig against the dollar and slipped about a third of a yen after rallying against both currencies on Tuesday. The pound, bolstered by British Chancellor of the Exchequer John Major's remarks supporting continued high interest rates, rose about 1 1/2 pfennigs against the mark.

In late New York trading yesterday, the dollar was quoted at 1.6830 marks, up from 1.6740 marks late Tuesday, and at 151.45 yen, up from 151.25 yen late Tuesday. Sterling was quoted at $1.6950, up from $1.6940 late Tuesday.

In Tokyo Thursday, the dollar was trading soon after the opening at 151.62 yen, up from Wednesday's Tokyo close of 150.75 yen. Meanwhile, the U.S. currency was trading at about 1.6830 marks.

The mark's decline came despite a steady stream of positive-sounding statements about unification by West German monetary authorities and news that the West German cost-of-living index rose a preliminary 0.2% in May.

Anticipation of a moderate cost-of-living figure had helped the West German currency post gains Tuesday, and past remarks by West German authorities suggesting that the process of unification is well in hand have helped boost the mark.

But both the inflation news and the remarks on unification were overshadowed yesterday by a burst of pessimism about the chances for a smooth transition to one Germany, participants said.

The closer unification gets, the more "people are wary," said Jack Griffin, a vice president and corporate trader at Banque Indosuez in New York.

The election of the maverick Yeltsin to the presidency of the huge Russian Republic added to traders' nervousness because he is seen as a rival to Soviet President Mikhail Gorbachev and possibly a further destabilizing influence within the already tumultuous Soviet political scene. Mr. Gorbachev recently has been widely perceived as having political trouble at home despite successes overseas.

The issue of a united Germany's membership in the North Atlantic Treaty Organization is likely to be discussed at the U.S.-Soviet summit meeting that begins today in Washington. Market participants will watch the summit carefully because the two sides may find it difficult to compromise on that issue, traders said.

Chief among the slew of statements by West German officials yesterday was Bundesbank President Karl Otto Poehl's remark that the central bank is "very satisfied" with the state treaty between East and West Germany on economic and social unification. Rumors of a split between the Bundesbank and government over the treaty had been weighing on the West German currency recently, and the mark enjoyed a slight rebound against the dollar after news of the comment at midafternoon New York time, but soon lost its gain on continued selling.

Earlier, Bundesbank directorate member Hans Tietmeyer said there is no extreme threat of inflation in West Germany due to unification, a comment supported from the government side by Economics Minister Helmut Haussmann, who said that the central bank will take appropriate action to keep West German inflation below a 3% annual rate after July 2, when the treaty on unification takes effect.

Mr. Griffin of Banque Indosuez noted that the potential impact of the statements by West German officials was diluted by the perception that the remarks were politically motivated as different forces within West Germany jockey for position before unification and coming national elections.

On the Commodity Exchange in New York, gold for delivery next month settled at $364.10 an ounce, down $3.30. Estimated volume was a heavy six million ounces.

In Hong Kong Thursday, gold was quoted at $364.45 an ounce.
