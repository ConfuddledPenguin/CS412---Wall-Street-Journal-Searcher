Boesky Ex-Aide Testifies He Conducted Illegal Trades With John Mulheren

  • Author: Laurie P. Cohen
  • Date: 05/31/90

NEW YORK -- A key aide to convicted former stock speculator Ivan F. Boesky said that he conducted numerous illegal securities trades with defendant John A. Mulheren Jr. and that Mr. Mulheren proposed a bogus scheme to compensate Mr. Boesky for profits on those trades. Michael Davidoff's testimony provided the most damaging evidence yet in the government's case against Mr. Mulheren, former chief of Jamie Securities Co. Mr. Davidoff appeared to help the government much more than did his former boss, Mr. Boesky, who testified last week.

Mr. Mulheren is charged with 41 counts of conspiracy, securities fraud and keeping false records in connection with his dealings with Mr. Boesky.

Appearing nervous and speaking with a slight stammer, Mr. Davidoff recalled a series of trades with Mr. Mulheren that Mr. Davidoff said were designed to generate tax losses for Mr. Boesky's trading company and to help evade net capital requirements.

Mr. Davidoff, 46 years old, said the trades with Mr. Mulheren, which Mr. Davidoff said he believed to be illegal at the time he did them, began in July 1985 and continued until March of the following year. Mr. Davidoff said he conducted the trades at Mr. Boesky's direction. Mr. Davidoff, once Mr. Boesky's chief trader, pleaded guilty in 1987 to one felony count related to securities fraud and is awaiting sentencing.

In every case, Mr. Davidoff said, Mr. Boesky's company agreed to sell securities to Mr. Mulheren's firm with the understanding that those securities would later be repurchased. Mr. Davidoff said he repeatedly assured Mr. Mulheren that all profits and losses from the trades "would be borne by" Mr. Boesky's company.

Mr. Davidoff said the first of these trades with Mr. Mulheren involved stock of Unocal Corp. and was arranged by Mr. Boesky himself. Mr. Davidoff said he did the trade with Mr. Boesky after talking with him. Shown records of the trade by assistant U.S. attorney Alfred Pavlis, Mr. Davidoff acknowledged it was his handwriting on the documents.

Mr. Davidoff said that Mr. Mulheren later agreed to buy securities in five other companies with the understanding Jamie Securities would sell these securities back to Mr. Boesky at a later date and be compensated for any losses. By December 1985, Mr. Davidoff said, Jamie Securities owed Mr. Boesky's firm "over $1 million" from profits that Jamie made on the trades.

According to Mr. Davidoff's testimony, Mr. Mulheren proposed a scheme where Jamie would inflate brokerage bills it paid to Mr. Boesky's trading company in late 1985. But in March 1986, when Mr. Boesky wanted to speed up the payments, Mr. Davidoff said that he agreed with Mr. Mulheren that Jamie would sell Mr. Boesky securities at below-market prices, resulting in a profit to Mr. Boesky.

Mr. Davidoff is expected to continue his testimony today and will likely be cross-examined by Thomas Puccio, an attorney for Mr. Mulheren.
