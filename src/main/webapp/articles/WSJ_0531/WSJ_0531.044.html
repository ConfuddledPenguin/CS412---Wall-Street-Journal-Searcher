Dividend News: Home Depot Sets 3-for-2 Stock Split, 50% Payout Boost

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/31/90

ATLANTA -- Home Depot Inc., which has seen its shares' market value soar 20% since early May, declared a 3-for-2 stock split and effectively boosted its dividend rate 50% by maintaining the payout at three cents a share on a post-split basis. The highflying building-materials and home-improvement company said its board made the decisions at a session after the annual meeting yesterday.

"We had a long discussion about it and decided it was the right thing to do," Ronald M. Brill, senior vice president and chief financial officer, said, pointing to strong earnings and a recent surge in the company's share price. "We want the shares to be more affordable on a per-share basis to our customers and employees -- to the retail (stock) purchasers."

Home Depot's first-quarter net income rose 49% to $38.6 million, or 48 cents a share, from $25.9 million, or 33 cents a share, a year earlier. Sales jumped 37% to $880.9 million from $641.5 million. The company this year has opened seven new stores, increasing its total to 125.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Home Depot shares closed at $59.875, up 37.5 cents.

Home Depot said it would accomplish the stock split by issuing one-half common share for each share held, payable July 6 to stock of record June 14. It said that after the split, its quarterly dividend of three cents a share will be paid Aug. 3 to stock of record July 20.

The company has about 77 million shares outstanding. At the meeting, shareholders approved a measure increasing the number of authorized shares to 250 million from 100 million.
