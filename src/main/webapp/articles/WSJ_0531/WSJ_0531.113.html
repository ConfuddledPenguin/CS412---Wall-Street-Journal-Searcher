South Korea Current Account

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/31/90

SEOUL, South Korea -- The current account deficit for South Korea narrowed to $171 million in April, the smallest in four months. A year earlier there was a surplus of $516 million, according to the Bank of Korea. The April deficit -- the fourth-consecutive monthly shortfall -- was attributed to sluggish merchandise exports and expanding imports.

The central bank listed a cumulative current account deficit for the first four months at $1.17 billion, compared with the previous year's surplus of $1.86 billion.

Citing a continued slowing in exports, bank officials said the current account could register a deficit of $400 million in May. Current account includes balances on payments for trade in merchandise and services plus certain transfer payments.
