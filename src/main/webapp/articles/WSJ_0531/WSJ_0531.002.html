Report Says Assets Of Taxable Funds Fell $1.1 Billion

  • Author: Georgette Jasen
  • Date: 05/31/90

Investors resumed pulling their money out of money-market mutual funds as yields stayed where they've been since January. Assets of 427 taxable funds fell by $1.1 billion to $375.6 billion in the week ended Tuesday, according to IBC/Donoghue's Money Fund Report. The Holliston, Mass.-based newsletter has reported only one increase in assets in these funds in the past nine weeks -- the week ended May 15.

Some of the cash "may be going into the stock market," said Brenda Malizia Negus, editor of Money Fund Report. "People may be tempted by the latest rally." Individual investors accounted for most of the latest outflow -- $830 million.

The average seven-day compound yield of the taxable funds remained at 7.98% for the second consecutive week. It has been at or just below 8% for 19 weeks. (Compound yields assume reinvestment of dividends.)

The average maturity of the funds' investments -- such things as commercial paper (short-term corporate IOUs), Treasury bills and certificates of deposit -- shortened by a day to 43 days. It was the first change in seven weeks, but shifts of one day usually aren't considered significant.

Shorter maturities generally are considered a sign that portfolio managers think interest rates are rising, because they allow a fund to latch onto higher rates sooner. Conversely, longer maturities are thought to indicate declining rates. Average maturities have been as short as 29 days and as long as 53 days in recent years.

The average seven-day simple yield remained at 7.68%. The average 30-day simple yield also remained at 7.68% and the average 30-day compound yield again was 7.98%.

Yields on tax-exempt money funds fell. The average seven-day compound yield slid to 5.54% from 5.59%. That's equivalent to 8.27% for an investor paying 33% in taxes, but just 7.69% for someone in the 28% bracket.

Assets of the 222 tax-free funds tracked by Donoghue's declined by $289.2 million to $75.4 billion. Average maturity shortened by a day to 39 days.
