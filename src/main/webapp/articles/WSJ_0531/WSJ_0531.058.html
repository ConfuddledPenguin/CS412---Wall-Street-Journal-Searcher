Japan's Imports Seen Growing

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/31/90

TOKYO -- Trade statistics issued by the Japan Trade Council, a group of major trading houses, show that Japan's imports will continue to climb sharply because of strong domestic demand. Import contracts signed in April by 18 leading trading companies surged a record 65% from a year earlier to 1.359 trillion yen ($9 billion), the council said. This followed a 54% jump in import contracts concluded in the previous month. The statistics indicate that imports will continue to register sharp gains in the coming months, helping reduce Japan's trade surplus.

The trade gap has been dwindling steadily as the pace of import growth has exceeded export gains, because of vigorous domestic demand such as corporate investment in plant and equipment that has been fueling sustained economic growth in Japan. Export contracts signed in April totaled 849.7 billion yen, a 20% increase from a year earlier.

The council's report, based on contract data provided by the trading companies, is considered to be a short-term indicator of overall trade trends. The 18 trading houses handle about half of Japan's overall exports and two-thirds of imports.
