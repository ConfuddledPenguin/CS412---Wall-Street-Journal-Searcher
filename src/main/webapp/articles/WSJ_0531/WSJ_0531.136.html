Business Brief -- Avery International Corp.: Over 10% Drop Estimated For Fiscal 2nd-Period Net

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/31/90

Avery International Corp., which last week agreed to merge with Dennison Manufacturing Co., said it expects fiscal second-quarter net income to be "in excess of 10% below" the year-ago level on a projected 5% sales increase. For the year-earlier quarter, Avery posted net of $24.1 million, or 55 cents a share, on sales of $446 million. Avery's fiscal second quarter ends today.

Charles D. Miller, chairman and chief executive officer of the Pasadena, Calif.-based company, told securities analysts in New York that the expected lower earnings reflect "significantly slower" demand by car makers and owners for Avery's decorative films and labels.

"Sales bookings turned up" in May, Mr. Miller said, "encouraging us to believe the last half of the year could be better than the first. Overall, we continue to believe that for the full year we can exceed last year's results."

Yesterday, Avery shares fell $1.50 to $24.75 on volume of more than 1.5 million shares in New York Stock Exchange composite trading.
