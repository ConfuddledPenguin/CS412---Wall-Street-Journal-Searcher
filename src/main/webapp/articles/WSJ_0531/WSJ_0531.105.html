Thomas James Told To Make Repayments In Stock-Offerings Case

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/31/90

WASHINGTON -- The Securities and Exchange Commission said a federal judge ordered Thomas James Associates Inc., its former president and a director to repay $1.5 million in connection with four initial public offerings underwritten by the firm in 1989. The order stems from an SEC complaint in March charging the Rochester, N.Y., concern with undisclosed domination of the market for four companies' stocks. The suit also charged that the securities firm used "fraudulent and high-pressure sales techniques" to inflate demand for the stocks.

Of the $1.5 million payment ordered by the federal district court in Rochester, $300,000 is to be paid by Thomas James Associates; $800,000 by Brian Thomas, former president of the firm; and $400,000 by James Villa, a 50% owner of the firm and a member of its board. Mr. Thomas also agreed to accept a lifetime ban from the securities industry, the SEC said.

The SEC alleged a scheme involving the shares of Sunrise Technologies Inc., Phoenix Laser Systems Inc., Megamation Inc. and International Superconductor Corp. The agency said Thomas James Associates generated about $4.5 million in undisclosed excessive mark-ups by purchasing securities from customers at arbitrary prices and reselling them to other customers at arbitrary prices.

Thomas James Associates and Mr. Villa didn't return phone calls seeking comment. Mr. Thomas couldn't be reached for comment.
