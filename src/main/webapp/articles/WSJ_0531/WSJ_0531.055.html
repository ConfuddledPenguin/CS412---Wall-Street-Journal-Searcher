Spain's Current Account Gap

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/31/90

MADRID -- Spain's current international payments account deficit widened sharply in April to 155.3 billion pesetas ($1.49 billion) from 81.1 billion pesetas a year earlier, the Bank of Spain reported. The central bank said a sharp drop in foreign capital investment, which fell to 84 billion pesetas from 193 billion pesetas in April 1989, was the major reason for the marked deterioration in the current account deficit. The current account includes trade in goods and services, plus some unilateral transfers.

The central bank said Spain's overall trade deficit widened by 8% to 252 billion pesetas in April from 233.1 billion pesetas a year earlier. Imports registered a slight 0.9% increase, while exports slipped 3%.
