Xerox Seeks to Sell Part of Financing, Leasing Operation

  • Author: Laurence Hooper
  • Date: 05/31/90

STAMFORD, Conn. -- Xerox Corp. said it put up for sale much of its third-party financing and leasing operation, further backing away from its financial-services business and counterbalancing a large write-off that is said to be in the offing. The businesses on the block represent about $2.2 billion, or about 40%, of the financial assets of Xerox Credit Corp., Xerox's financing subsidiary. They also include $140 million in assets in international third-party financing but don't affect the financing of Xerox equipment or any other Xerox finance-oriented businesses.

Alex Henderson, analyst at Prudential-Bache Securities Inc., valued the businesses for sale at between $150 million and $300 million. Xerox couldn't be reached for comment on its target price.

Yesterday's announcement comes amid Xerox's recent disillusionment with its financial-services business, which was responsible for a $375 million first-quarter write-off related to bad real-estate investments.

Wall Street analysts have tied that change in corporate attitude to the appointment of Paul Allaire, head of the company's business-equipment arm, as successor to David Kearns as chief executive.

Mr. Allaire is known as a cost-cutter, and analysts say he has sent out word that unprofitable businesses, especially in the financial-services sector, must shape up or be sold.

But yesterday's announcement also coincided with speculation about another impending write-off, related to Xerox's Crum & Forster insurance unit. That speculation, featured in an article in yesterday's Wall Street Journal, sent Xerox shares diving $1.75 a share to close at $46.50 in composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange.

"The timing obviously isn't an accident," said Mr. Henderson. "The specter of some fairly significant write-offs scared a lot of people, but this should compensate and put the stock back on track."

The announcement was made after the market closed yesterday. Mr. Henderson said he expects Xerox shares to rebound to previous levels on the news today. "The Street will laud any move to sell anything in financial services," he said. "Anything that takes debt and liability off the balance sheet will be viewed positively."
