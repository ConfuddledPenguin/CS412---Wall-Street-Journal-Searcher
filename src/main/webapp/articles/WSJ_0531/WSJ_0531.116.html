International: Gorbachev, in a Significant Gesture, Plans to Meet South Korea's Roh in U.S.

  • Author: Damon Darlin
  • Date: 05/31/90

SEOUL, South Korea -- The U.S. arranged a last-minute meeting between South Korean President Roh Tae Woo and Soviet leader Mikhail Gorbachev. The meeting would be the most significant gesture by the Soviets toward South Korea in more than four decades. The meeting will take place in San Francisco Sunday, U.S. officials confirmed, after Mr. Gorbachev finishes the official phase of his summit meeting with President Bush in Washington.

Seoul and Moscow don't have formal diplomatic ties and Moscow maintains strong links with North Korea. But trade and other business links between the South Koreans and the Soviets have been strengthening. Topics would most likely include South Korea asking Soviet help in diminishing the antagonism between the South and the Communist North and Soviet diplomatic recognition of Seoul.

The U.S. involvement in setting up the meeting indicates Washington's eagerness to defuse one of the world's last trouble spots where U.S. troops face a Soviet-supported army. About 42,000 U.S. troops are based in South Korea, many of them at the Demilitarized Zone that divides the Korean peninsula, eye-to-eye with North Korean troops.

In Washington, a State Department spokeswoman said the U.S. "would welcome a meeting" between the Soviet and South Korean leaders, but she declined to answer questions about such a meeting.

The San Francisco meeting would be the first between a Soviet leader and a high-level South Korean government official, let alone a South Korean president. In addition to seeking diplomatic recognition from the Soviets, Seoul wants Moscow to let it join the United Nations. North Korea, which also isn't a U.N. member, doesn't want South Korea to join and has asked its allies -- China and the U.S.S.R. -- to veto any admission attempt. Moscow, on the other hand, needs South Korean trade and investment to help its struggling economy.

Seoul has tried for many months to convince North Korea's allies to recognize South Korea and to rein in North Korea, whose excesses in past years have included bombing and killing South Korean leaders in Rangoon and Burma, and blowing up a Korean Air Lines commercial jet on the eve of the 1988 Summer Olympics. Even before communism began faltering in Eastern Europe last year, South Korea made progress with many of those nations and was isolating North Korea. Seoul established diplomatic ties with Poland, Hungary and Czechoslovakia; in December, it and Moscow began limited consular services in their trade offices in each other's capitals.

But Moscow, North Korea's main supporter, has held back on full diplomatic ties. The Soviets very much want South Korea's trade and economic assistance. China, which has become more hard-line following the Tiananmen massacre last summer, also shows little eagerness to openly support South Korea over North Korea, though its trade relations with South Korea have been growing rapidly, too.

North Korea has always successfully played China off against the Soviets and it has never been clear how much real influence Moscow and Beijing have on North Korean leader Kim Il Sung. North Korea has shown no sign of retreating from its rigid Stalinist policies.

The Gorbachev-Roh meeting could provide a major boost for Mr. Roh, whose popularity is slipping as South Koreans blame him for their economy's fits and starts. The Korean stock market reversed its plummet Wednesday when rumors about the trip circulated. It closed at 789.89, up 6.02 points.

The trip will also undoubtedly feed criticism of Mr. Roh and the U.S. by Korea's radicals, who are likely to contend that the U.S. hand in setting up the Roh-Gorbachev meeting proves their allegation that Mr. Roh is a puppet of the U.S.

Mr. Roh originally planned to be in the U.S. during the Gorbachev visit, but canceled his trip to Mexico, Canada and the U.S. because he didn't want to risk being criticized for leaving Korea when the country faced economic troubles.
