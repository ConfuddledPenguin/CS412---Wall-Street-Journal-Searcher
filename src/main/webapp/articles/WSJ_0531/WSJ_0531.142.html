Business Brief -- Intel Corp.: Chip Maker to Stop Using CFCs at Its Plants by 1992

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/31/90

Intel Corp. said it resolved to stop using ozone-damaging chlorofluorocarbons at its manufacturing plants by 1992. The Santa Clara, Calif., company, which makes microcomputer parts and systems, said it is starting "an aggressive world-wide program" to find alternative manufacturing techniques that don't rely on CFCs. Intel's measures are in response to a federal goal to eliminate industry's use of CFCs by the year 2000. The move is in line with a tentative industrywide effort by chip makers to rid the ozone-depleting emissions from their plants.

Intel began this process three years ago, when it started tracking CFC levels at its plants. Since then, Intel says it has lowered its CFC use by about 30%, by experimenting with new conservation and control methods.

A company spokesman said the new equipment that Intel plans to install to replace CFCs will cost several hundred thousand dollars and "in the long run, it will probably be cheaper." The entire program won't run more than several million dollars, the spokesman said.
