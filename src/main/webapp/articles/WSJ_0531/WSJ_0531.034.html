International Brief -- Suzuki Motor Co.: Annual Pretax Profit Rose 76%; Cost Controls Cited

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/31/90

Suzuki Motor Co. of Japan reported that consolidated pretax profit for the year ended March 31 rose 76% to 25.48 billion yen ($168.7 million) from 14.48 billion yen a year earlier, after the company limited growth in manufacturing and administration costs. But net income fell 14% to 5.61 billion yen, or 13.75 yen a share, from 6.54 billion yen, or 17.38 yen a share, because of higher tax payments.

Sales of Suzuki vehicles and parts rose 2.3% to 982.57 billion yen from 960.49 billion yen. Costs increased only 0.4% for sales and 1.9% for administration, allowing operating profit to surge from a year earlier. A Suzuki official attributed the slower growth of costs to exchange-rate factors, as well as changes in Japan's tax system.

Receipts from auto sales slipped 0.7% to 617.06 billion yen from 621.66 billion yen. Motorcycle sales inched up 0.6% to 148.266 billion yen from 147.384 billion yen. Parts sales rose 13% to 217.247 billion yen from 191.45 billion yen. Vehicle volume sagged in every area but sales of motorcycles overseas; unit sales fell 2.2% for motorcycles and 6.6% for autos.
