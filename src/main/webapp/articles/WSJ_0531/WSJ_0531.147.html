Jordache and Guess Families Settle Feud, Divide $106 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/31/90

LOS ANGELES (AP) -- A feud between families owning two blue jeans companies was settled out of court yesterday when Jordache agreed to give back its half-interest in Guess. The settlement came after a four-month trial and during the jury's third day of deliberations.

The terms for division of about $106 million held in an escrow account weren't disclosed. Attorneys said all parties had agreed not to disclose disposition of funds.

The family feud involved four Frenchborn Marciano brothers -- Maurice, Armand, Paul and Georges -- who founded Guess in 1981, and three Israeli-born Nakash brothers -- Joe, Ralph and Avi -- who own Jordache and who purchased a half-interest in Guess in 1983.

The partnership disintegrated into allegations of fraud and double-dealing that led to two separate trials to resolve the claims.

"The parties agree and the court orders that the sale of Guess stock by the Marcianos to the Nakashes is hereby rescinded," said the settlement agreement filed in Los Angeles Superior Court.

Under terms of the accord, the Nakashes returned to the Marcianos a half-interest in Guess that they bought for just under $5 million. The Marcianos won an earlier court fight in which a jury determined that the Nakashes had defrauded the Marcianos when they purchased the interest in the then-fledgling company.

"It seemed like a senseless thing to ever have it happen," said Dennis Townsend, who was a juror in the lengthy trial. "These people are supposedly such good friends, and it turned into a war."
