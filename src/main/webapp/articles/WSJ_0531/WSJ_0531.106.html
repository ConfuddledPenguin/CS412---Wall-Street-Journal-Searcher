Credit Ratings: Fitch Investors Lowers Ratings on Citicorp's Debt, Preferred Stock

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/31/90

NEW YORK -- Fitch Investors Service Inc. pared its ratings on Citicorp's senior and subordinated debt and its preferred stock, while affirming the banking giant's commercial-paper rating. Citicorp called the move somewhat disappointing, noting that on Tuesday, another rating concern, Duff & Phelps, affirmed its Duff-1-plus rating on Citicorp's commercial paper, "along with ratings for our senior debt, subordinated debt and preferred stock that are basically in line with Standard & Poor's {Corp.} and now with Fitch."

"The weakening economic environment" is hurting the commercial real-estate sector and "highly leveraged borrowers," Fitch said in announcing the downgrade. Fitch added that Citicorp's $8.4 billion refinancing portfolio of loans to less-developed countries "continues to be under-reserved relative to other international lenders."

Fitch ratings fell on Citicorp's senior debt to double-A-minus from double-A; on subordinated debt to single-A-plus from double-A-minus; and on preferred stock to single-A from single-A-plus. Fitch affirmed the commercial-paper rating at F-1-Plus.

The Fitch downgrades affect about $15 billion of senior debt, $3 billion of subordinated debt and $1.8 billion of preferred stock. It affirmed ratings on about $5 billion in commercial paper.

"While disappointing in some aspects," Citicorp said, "Fitch's comments are extremely positive in citing Citicorp's diversified income stream and consistently strong global consumer-business profits."
