Who's News: Blackstone Group Hires Richard Ravitch As a General Partner

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/31/90

NEW YORK -- Blackstone Group said Richard Ravitch, the former public official and turnaround specialist who ran unsuccessfully for mayor of New York in 1989, will join the investment boutique as a general partner. Mr. Ravitch, who will start work tomorrow, said he will focus mainly on restructuring over-leveraged companies -- "of which there will be many in the 1990s" -- and on recapitalizing troubled thrifts and other financial institutions. He also said he will scout out prospects for Blackstone's $800 million buy-out and investment fund.

"Everything he does will be tied in some way to his past experience and background," said Jerome Traum, a Blackstone general partner.

Mr. Ravitch, 56 years old, is known mainly for his chairmanship during the mid-1970s of the New York State Urban Development Corporation, which he is credited with saving from bankruptcy; and for heading the New York State Metropolitan Transit Authority, which he saw through a bitter transit strike in 1980 and spent until 1984 rebuilding with bond issues and innovative financing.

His bailout plan for the UDC became the model for the Municipal Assistance Corporation, created in 1975 to help New York City out of its fiscal crisis.

Mr. Ravitch has also served as chairman of HRH Construction, a developer of several major New York office buildings and residential projects. In 1985 he led a group of investors who bought, ran and resold in 1988 -- at a big profit -- New York's Bowery Savings Bank. "That's one of the most successful turnarounds that has ever taken place," said Mr. Traum.

Since 1989, Mr. Ravitch has concentrated on writing, teaching at New York University and seeking new opportunities in business.
