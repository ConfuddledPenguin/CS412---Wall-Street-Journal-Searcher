Merrill Is Expected To Buy Main Lines Of Elders IXL Unit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/31/90

NEW YORK -- Merrill Lynch & Co. has agreed to buy the main businesses of Elders Futures Inc., a futures and options arm of Australian brewer Elders IXL that has been on the block for several months, people close to the transaction said. The move is part of a series of asset sales by merchant bank Elders Finance Group Ltd., an Elders IXL unit that owns Elders Futures.

Merrill is making the acquisition to beef up its Merrill Lynch Futures metals and energy futures operations. Merrill will pay less than $20 million for Elders Futures, people close to the transaction said.

A Merrill spokesman declined to comment. However, an announcement of a definitive agreement to acquire the main businesses of Elders Futures is expected as soon as today.

The one part of Elders Futures that Merrill won't buy is the financial-futures operations. Securities-industry executives said those operations would be sold to Chicago-based Cargill Investor Services Inc., a subsidiary of Cargill Inc., Minneapolis. A Cargill spokesman confirmed the purchase yesterday, but declined to disclose the terms.

Gene D. Donney, president of Elders Futures, didn't return phone calls.

The Elders IXL parent company operates agribusiness, trading, brewing and finance companies in nearly 30 countries, and is perhaps known best for its Fosters Lager. Elders Futures provides brokerage, research and other services in New York, Chicago, London and Geneva, with a staff of about 175.
