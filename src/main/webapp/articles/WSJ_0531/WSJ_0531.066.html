Japanese Vehicle Exports Fall

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/31/90

TOKYO -- Japan's exports of cars, trucks and buses fell 18% to 430,216 units in April from a year earlier, the Japan Automobile Manufacturers' Association said. The year-to-year decline was the sharpest since December 1986, when vehicle exports fell 17.8% year-to-year, according to the association.

A sharp drop in exports to the U.S., the largest importer of Japanese vehicles, and a decline in exports by Toyota Motor Corp., the nation's top car exporter, contributed to the fall in the overall exports, an official at the association said.

The slowdown of exports to the U.S. should be seen as a trend due to declining demand and continuing expansion of production in the U.S. by Japanese car makers, the official said.
