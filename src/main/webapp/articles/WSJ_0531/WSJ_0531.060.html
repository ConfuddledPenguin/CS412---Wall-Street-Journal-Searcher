Britain's Economy Indicator

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/31/90

LONDON -- Britain's longer leading indicator of economic activity fell in April from March, continuing a nearly two-year slide, the Central Statistical Office said. The index, which indicates economic turning points about a year in advance, dropped to 92.9 from 93.6. The index has declined or stood even each month since May 1988.

The agency said the index's fall so far in 1990 is partly because of the influence of declining share prices.

The coincident index, which generally moves in line with current economic activity, also declined in April to 97.4 from 98.4 in March. This index has declined or gone unchanged since August 1988.

The shorter leading and the lagging indexes aren't available for April.
