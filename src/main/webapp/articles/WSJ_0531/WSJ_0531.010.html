Letters to the Editor: Let Him Have His Say, Even From the Grave

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/31/90

Knowing Leslie Combs II's "fondness for younger women was legendary" ("Dead Horse Breeder Raises a Ruckus With a Beneficiary," page one, April 25), Linda Mauk should have taken special cautions to see that volunteers (not paid staff) connected with the McDowell Cancer Foundation cultivated the late Mr. Combs and involved themselves in all his vicissitudes. As foundation director, her lack of professional judgment with a major potential contributor resulted in this unfortunate situation. She already has let the issue fester by publicly involving the board and the community. This can be ameliorated simply by Ms. Mauk doing the honorable thing and resigning. The good work of the McDowell Cancer Foundation and the needs of those yet to be served by its eleemosynary efforts far outweigh the personalities and idiosyncrasies involved. Let Mr. Combs speak from the grave -- that's the idea of a legal and directed gift.

David B. Chamberlain

Fund-Raising Consultant

Grand Rapids, Mich.
