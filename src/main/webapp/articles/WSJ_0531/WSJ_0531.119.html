Business Brief -- Centel Corp.: Investor Agrees to Acquire Voice, Data Systems Unit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/31/90

Centel Corp., Chicago, said it agreed in principle to sell its Centel Communications Systems operation to a group headed by investor Edward H. Lavin Jr. Terms weren't disclosed. Centel Communications Systems, which sells voice and data systems, had 1989 revenue of $218 million.

Centel Corp., a local-exchange telephone and cellular communications concern, had revenue last year of $1.19 billion, excluding the communications systems operation. Centel Corp. noted that it had said in 1988 that it planned to sell its Business Systems Group, which included Centel Communications Systems, Centel Federal Systems and Centel Information Systems.

Centel Corp. sold its Federal Systems unit, which sells data systems to federal agencies, to a management group earlier this year. Yesterday, the company said it is "still seeking bids" for the Information Systems unit, which makes data-communications products for private industry customers.

Mr. Lavin is a onetime president and chief executive officer of TIE Systems Inc., a maker of telecommunications equipment.
