Freddie Mac, Local Firms Back Fund in California

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/31/90

LOS ANGELES -- The Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. and several local companies have committed $39 million toward a targeted $125 million investment fund for building 4,000 low-income housing units in California. The five-year fund raising effort is spearheaded by the Local Initiatives Support Corp., a nonprofit community development organization formed by the Ford Foundation in 1979. The organization operates three investment funds -- National Equity Fund, California Equity Fund and New York Equity Fund -- which allow companies to take advantage of federal and state tax credits for investing in low-income housing. Since their inception in 1987 and 1988, the funds have raised $141 million from 59 companies, and invested in more than 5,200 rental apartments and houses in 23 cities.

Freddie Mac has pledged up to $10 million this year to the California fund, agreeing to invest one dollar for every two raised by a California company. The quasi-private agency has previously invested $11 million in the National and New York Equity Funds.

Great Western Financial Corp. and Security Pacific Corp.'s Security Pacific National Bank each invested $10 million for the five-year California effort. City National Corp.'s City National Bank committed $5 million for five years. Atlantic Richfield Co., Avery International Corp., H.F. Ahmanson & Co.'s Home Savings of America and Kaufman & Broad Home Corp. invested $1 million each this year.
