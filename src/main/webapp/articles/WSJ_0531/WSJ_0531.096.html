Tonka Corp. Expects A Significant Loss In Second Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/31/90

MINNETONKA, Minn. -- Tonka Corp. said it expects to post a "significant" second-quarter loss because of lower sales and that it might post a loss for the full year as well. The toy maker said sales of some fad toy lines have been lower than expected, failing to take up the slack from the company's popular Real Ghostbusters line, which is petering out after three strong years of sales.

Sales from 1989 also included a video game line that has been discontinued.

Tonka said it now is doubtful that its revenue will exceed last year's $870.5 million, and that the company is in talks with its banks to relax some debt covenants that the company could be in violation of. "We are clearly disappointed by the current outlook," Stephen G. Shank, chairman and chief executive officer, said, "and are examining every option to improve performance for the balance of the year."

A spokeswoman attributed the sales decline to a series of relatively small disappointments in the fad toy line, which promotes toys tied to movies and other phenomena through Tonka's Kenner Products unit. Tonka wouldn't identify the items not selling well, she said, for fear that sales would be further hurt by identifying the lines as slow sellers.

In the year-earlier second quarter, Tonka had profit of $100,000, or two cents a share, on sales of $189.4 million. Tonka wouldn't project results for the current quarter.

For all of 1989, Tonka had profit of $5.7 million, or 67 cents a share, after a $2 million charge.

Tonka expects a profitable second half for 1990, but now isn't sure whether that will be enough to offset the first-half loss. For this year's first quarter, Tonka had a $10.3 million loss, or 71 cents a share, on revenue of $135.9 million.

International sales and sales of Parker Brothers games are above last year's levels, Tonka said. In addition to the fad lines, sales in the Tonka Products division, which makes toy trucks, Play-doh and other general items, are off.
