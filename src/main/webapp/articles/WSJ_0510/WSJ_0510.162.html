Eastern Airlines Sets Ticket Redemption

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/10/90

MIAMI -- Eastern Airlines said it will redeem about 47,000 tickets outstanding since its Chapter 11 filing more than a year ago. Under the plan, which has received bankruptcy-court approval, ticket holders have several options. They can exchange tickets for travel vouchers worth 135% of the original unused fare. Or they also can receive full face value of the ticket with either a first class upgrade or a companion ticket for half the regular fare.

The program applies to travelers who bought tickets before March 9, 1989, when Eastern filed for Chapter 11 protection, and who didn't turn in their tickets to a redemption program last year. Eastern said ticket holders will receive letters about the program over the next several weeks.

Eastern, a unit of Houston-based Texas Air Corp., has been placed under the control of a trustee by the bankruptcy court.
