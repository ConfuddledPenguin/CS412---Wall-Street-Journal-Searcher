Letters to the Editor: Amtrak Derails Working Hoosiers

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/10/90

Your April 5 page-one article about W. Graham Claytor's "discipline" in revitalizing Amtrak has this frustrated former passenger in a state of apoplectic rage. When my husband and I purchased our home in Valparaiso, Ind., 48 miles southeast of Chicago, one of the selling points was its proximity to the Amtrak station, where we could catch the train to our jobs in Chicago's Loop. We have since learned that this service, with a 65% on-time record, nearly daily equipment failures and only one remaining scheduled departure to the Loop (6:10 a.m.), is to be discontinued in September. This despite the fact that 400 people ride or drive from Valparaiso alone to work in the Loop every day, and there are as many as 85,000 rail commuters from Northwest Indiana per year -- an average of 326 per workday.

Mr. Claytor extends rail service to gamblers in Atlantic City, and cuts off hundreds of working Hoosiers.

Deanne Gloppen

Valparaiso, Ind.
