DNA Plant Venture Begins Regional Sales Of Special Vegetables

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/10/90

OAKLAND, Calif. -- DNA Plant Technology Corp. said its joint venture with Du Pont Co. has begun to sell the venture's VegiSnax line of carrots and celery through 400 supermarkets in the Pacific Northwest. At its annual meeting here, DNA Plant Technology also said the venture, called FreshWorld Inc., is selling the carrots and celery through about 40 McDonald's restaurants in what the company called an "operational test" that isn't as broad as a regular market test. In the restaurants, the vegetables, produced by advanced growing techniques meant to impart better taste and longer shelf life, carry the McDonald's brand name.

DNA Plant, based in Cinnaminson, N.J., said it also has started a joint venture with European investors to test naturally occurring bacteria that may prevent frost damage when applied to crops. The company said the bacteria, a strain of Pseudomonas syringe, don't produce a protein that serves as a site for ice crystal formation, as other natural bacteria strains do.
