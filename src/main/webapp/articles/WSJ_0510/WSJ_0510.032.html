Marketing & Media: Record Industry Unveils Label for `Explicit Lyrics'

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/10/90

WASHINGTON -- The Record Industry Association of America unveiled a uniform voluntary label -- which will appear on certain records, cassettes and compact disks -- warning customers of explicit lyrics. Beginning in July, the black-and-white sticker will appear, at the discretion of record companies, on the lower right-hand corner of the product, inside the cellophane. It will read: "Parental Advisory -- Explicit Lyrics."

The plan is endorsed by the six record distributors that dominate the business: Time Warner Inc.'s Warner Records, MCA Inc.'s MCA Records, Sony Corp.'s CBS Records, Bertelsmann AG's BMG unit, N.V. Philips's PolyGram Records and Thorn EMI PLC's Capitol-EMI Records. It is also endorsed by the National Association of Recording Merchandisers, which represents retailers.

This uniform label replaces a 1985 plan under which individual record companies devised their own labels. The new system is designed to blunt criticism of the record industry by citizen's groups and state legislators.
