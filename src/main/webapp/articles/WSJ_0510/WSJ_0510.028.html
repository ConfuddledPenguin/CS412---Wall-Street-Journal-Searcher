Finance Official Cites Fall In Japan's Outflows to U.S.

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/10/90

WASHINGTON -- Capital flows from Japan to the U.S. have slowed recently, Makoto Utsumi, Japan's vice minister for international affairs at Japan's finance ministry, said. But Mr. Utsumi, in an interview, refused to speculate on whether this week's record Treasury refunding of more than $30 billion would be affected. Japan has been an important investor in Treasury securities in recent years.

Speaking at the end of three days of international meetings here, Mr. Utsumi expressed satisfaction with Sunday's communique from the Group of Seven industrialized nations. He stressed that the statement, released Sunday, is more explicit about the need for a stronger yen than a previous G-7 communique released in Paris a month ago.

"The current level of the yen is to a certain extent out of line with fundamentals," said Mr. Utsumi, who would like to see his country's currency strengthen.

The G-7 statement said "the present level {of the yen} may have undesirable consequences for the global adjustment process." There wasn't any mention of the present level in the earlier communique released in Paris, Mr. Utsumi pointed out. (The G-7 includes the finance ministers and central bank presidents of the U.S., Japan, West Germany, France, Britain, Italy and Canada.)
