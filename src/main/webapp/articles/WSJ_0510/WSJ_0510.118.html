Credit Ratings: Moody's Downgrades Pennzoil Debt Issues Totaling $1.7 Billion

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/10/90

NEW YORK -- Moody's Investors Service Inc. said it lowered the ratings on about $1.7 billion of debt of Pennzoil Co. because of uncertainty surrounding the company's investment in Chevron Corp. That $2.1 billion investment represents about 45% of Pennzoil's total assets. Moody's said concern remains over how long Houston-based Pennzoil will retain its holdings in San Francisco-based Chevron or whether it will attempt to increase its stake, at the risk of further burdening an already leveraged capital structure.

Additionally, Pennzoil is experiencing weak operating performance and may soon be hit with a tax bill that could total close to $800 million. The tax issue stems from the settlement of litigation with Texaco Inc. Downgraded by Moody's were Pennzoil's debentures, notes, Euronotes and preliminarily rated shelf registration, to single A-3, from single A-2. Its commercial paper also was downgraded to Prime-2, from Prime-1.
