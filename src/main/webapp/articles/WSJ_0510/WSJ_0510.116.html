Integra-A Hotel Says Its Biggest Shareholder Is Selling 13% Stake

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/10/90

DALLAS -- Integra-A Hotel & Restaurant Co. said it and several other companies signed agreements that would raise new capital and allow Hallwood Group Inc. and affiliates to sell their 13% stake in Integra. Integra, which owns and operates hotels, said it expects the agreements to lead to a shake-up of its board and to substantial changes in the company's management. They would also lead Hallwood, a New York merchant bank and Integra's biggest shareholder, to substantially end its relationship with the company, Integra said.

Integra said Burrus Investment Group Inc., New Orleans, agreed to make a $10 million, five-year secured loan with an interest rate of four percentage points above the prime rate. The agreement also calls for Integra to give Burrus six-year warrants to buy as many as 1.8 million shares of Integra common for 46.25 cents each.

An affiliate of Burrus, meanwhile, agreed to purchase 578,763 Integra shares, or 6.5% of total common shares outstanding, from Hallwood, according to Integra.

Separately, Integra said a unit of Westbrooke Hospitality Corp., a Dallas hotel operator, agreed to provide centralized hotel operating services to most hotels currently operated by Integra. Integra would receive an option to acquire the subsidiary between 1993 and 1995.

Also, 867 Jennings Investment Co., a private company owned by a relative of a Westbrooke principal, agreed to purchase a 6.5% Integra stake from Hallwood, Integra said.

In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange, Integra closed at 46.87 cents a share, up 3.12 cents.
