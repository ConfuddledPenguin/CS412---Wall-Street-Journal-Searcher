Technology Brief -- Pioneer Electronic Corp.: Satellite Navigation System Is Developed for Vehicles

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/10/90

A new navigation system made by Pioneer Electronic Corp. of Japan uses satellites to calculate a vehicle's location and then displays it to the driver on maps on a small color screen. The system, which goes on sale in Japan next month, connects to a vehicle's audio system and uses special compact disks to display maps of any part of Japan, Pioneer spokesman Toru Yokomizo said.

He said it is the first satellite navigation system for cars available as an accessory on the retail market.

The device, named AVIC-1, can be used by cars, trucks or helicopters while in motion or standing still, Mr. Yokomizo said.

The complete system, including navigation device, display, antenna and audio system, will cost about 500,000 yen ($3,200), he said.
