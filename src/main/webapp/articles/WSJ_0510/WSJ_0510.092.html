International Brief -- Deutsche Bank AG: Insurance Venture Is Slated To Serve Corporate Clients

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/10/90

Deutsche Bank AG said it will set up a joint venture with the Gerling insurance group to provide life-insurance products to corporate clients. In response to a question, a spokesman for West Germany's largest bank said Deutsche Bank will hold 70% of the new company, while Gerling, a closely held West German insurance group, will take 30%. The new company will specifically serve small and midsize companies that want to offer life policies to employees as part of their compensation package.

Gerling is the only major German insurer cooperating with Deutsche Bank's insurance ventures. Most other groups, especially market leader Allianz AG, opposed Deutsche Bank's entry into life insurance from the start.
