What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/10/90

THE DOLLAR SKIDDED on speculation that Washington's effort to cut the budget deficit may push U.S. interest rates lower. The currency fell to a 28-month low against the mark. Bond prices also dropped despite a good response to the Treasury's $10 billion auction of 10-year notes. Stock prices closed mixed. ---

Budget talks will begin Tuesday between Bush and top congressional leaders. But the administration insisted the president hasn't given up his opposition to tax increases.

---

Competition is heating up again in international express delivery. JAL, Lufthansa and Nissho Iwai are seeking equity ties with DHL Worldwide Express, putting more pressure on Federal Express and UPS, which are pushing hard to expand overseas.

---

Ford's patent-infringement case involving windshield wipers was deadlocked over damages. A new jury will decide how much the auto maker should pay inventor Robert Kearns.

---

Mattel announced its first quarterly dividend in seven years and said it plans to repay $75 million in senior notes, reflecting improved earnings. The toy maker also announced a licensing agreement involving the hit TV cartoon series "The Simpsons."

---

Interco said it won't pay interest due June 15 on its bonds, proposing instead that bondholders accept a $900 million debt restructuring. Analysts view the plan as a first step in what may be a long series of negotiations.

---

Prices for summer travel aren't going up despite increased airline traffic and hotel occupancy rates. Many travel companies don't want to risk boosting prices because the market is still relatively soft.

---

Inflation may be worse than recent statistics indicate, economists say. They cite hard-to-measure, extra-inflationary forces such as higher property and local taxes, soaring health costs and increasing tuition fees.

---

Major TV networks are hoping to scrap the long-standing practice of guaranteeing advertisers a certain number of viewers. But the effort, reflecting a shrinking TV audience, is being resisted by the ad industry.

---

Bank failures have slowed considerably this year, according to FDIC Chairman William Seidman. As of Tuesday, 52 banks had failed in 1990, compared with 73 that failed or got FDIC aid at this time last year.

---

Investor choices in arbitrating disputes with brokers were narrowed by a federal appeals court. The court said investors nationwide can be required to arbitrate in New York rather than their hometown.

---

Money market-fund assets fell for the sixth week in a row as investors found other places to put their cash.

---

Kodak expects improved sales and earnings this year after a bleak 1989. The photography giant also plans an aggressive expansion into Eastern Europe and the Soviet Union.
