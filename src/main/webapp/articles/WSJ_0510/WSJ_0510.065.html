Salomon and Soviet Union Hold Talks on Investments

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 05/10/90

The Soviet Union has talked with Salomon Inc. and possibly other big U.S. securities firms about setting up a $1 billion to $2 billion fund for investing in that country. Andrew Jonas, head of East European investment banking at Salomon Brothers International Ltd. in London, confirmed that Salomon has had discussions with Soviet banking officials that began at the Soviets' initiative. However, he said the discussions are "at somewhat of a dead end because of political problems there."

Mr. Jonas's remarks were made in response to a question at a conference on Eastern European investment opportunities sponsored by the Corporate Finance Institute in New York.

He said a possible fund might center on financing for three or four large projects. "I mean big projects, because the Soviets only think in terms of big projects," Mr. Jonas said, but he gave no specifics.

The fund, if realized, the Salomon executive said, would start out much more modestly than the Soviets might have had in mind, probably at $20 million.

Even if the Soviets overcome their political problems, there is no guarantee that Wall Street will work with them. Other investment banks aside from Salomon are said to have been approached by the Soviets. But, said Mr. Jonas, Salomon is worried about how marketable a fund would be.
