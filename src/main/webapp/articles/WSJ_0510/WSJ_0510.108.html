International Brief -- Brent Walker Group PLC: Pretax Profit Leaped in '89 With Gains in All Divisions

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/10/90

Brent Walker Group PLC, a British leisure concern, said 1989 pretax profit leaped 97%, helped by large gains in earnings from all divisions. Pretax profit for the year was #82.2 million ($137.3 million), up from #41.7 million a year ago. Sales more than quadrupled to #526 million. Earnings per share more than doubled to 88.7 pence from 41.1 pence a year ago. The surge in earnings partly reflects the company's purchase in October of William Hill Organization and Mecca Bookmakers Ltd. from Grand Metropolitan PLC for #685 million.

However, the company reported an increase in interest payments to #40 million from #5.4 million in the year-earlier period as a result of its buying spree. Nonetheless, Brent Walker Chairman George Walker said the company's broad base of activities gave investors protection during an economic downturn.

The company said a "substantial proportion" of its business is in pubs, brewing and betting activities, and these areas have shown "a strong resistance to reductions in consumer demand."
