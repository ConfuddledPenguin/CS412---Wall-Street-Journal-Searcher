Marketing & Media: Harcourt Terminates Talks About Possible Assets Sales

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/10/90

ORLANDO, Fla. -- Harcourt Brace Jovanovich Inc. said it terminated discussions about possible sales of some of its assets and public debt. The publishing and insurance concern, struggling under debt from a highly leveraged takeover escape in 1987, confirmed April 25 that it was holding the talks with another company, but declined to elaborate. A Harcourt director, however, former Sen. Eugene McCarthy, said the talks involved Toronto-based Thomson Corp.

Thomson officials couldn't be reached for comment on the termination of talks. Harcourt made the announcement after the markets closed.

"I didn't give a high probability to anything happening with Thomson anyway," said Andrew Herenstein, an analyst with Delaware Bay Co., a New York-based investment research firm. Mr. Herenstein, who anticipates a slight drop in the value of Harcourt Brace Jovanovich stock today in wake of the announcement, said the company must still complete some kind of restructuring.

Roger Gordon, an analyst with Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette Securities Corp. in New York, said Harcourt isn't under any pressure to complete a restructuring in the immediate future.

"They can move with deliberate speed," he said, "because they're not facing an immediate cash crunch and won't be confronting large cash demands for another three years" when cash payments on subordinated debt issues are set to begin.
