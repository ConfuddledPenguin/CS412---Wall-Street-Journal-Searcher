South African Vehicle Sales

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/10/90

JOHANNESBURG, South Africa -- South African new-vehicle sales dropped 20% to 23,096 units in April from 28,951 units a year earlier, the National Association of Automobile Manufacturers (Naamsa) said yesterday. April's sales were 24% lower than the 30,576 units sold in March, the association said.

Naamsa's director, Nico Vermeulen, said the decline in sales in April were larger than expected. He attributed it to a combination of unfavorable factors including uncertainty over political changes in the country, work stoppages at various assembly plants during the month and the impact of stringent monetary measures.
