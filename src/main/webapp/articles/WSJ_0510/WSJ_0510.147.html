Business Brief -- Primerica Corp.: Proposal Made to Buy Rest Of Financial-Advisory Firm

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/10/90

Primerica Corp. proposed buying the remainder of the publicly held stock in American Capital Management & Research Inc. Primerica already owns 82.6% of American's shares. Primerica, a New York financial-services concern, said it would exchange 0.3 share of its common stock for each American share it doesn't own. American has a total of 24.4 million shares outstanding.

The company said it would issue 1,270,000 million new shares to complete the transaction. Based on Primerica's recent stock price, the transaction's value would be $38 million.

American Capital Management, Houston, provides investment advice to mutual funds and closed-end investment companies, with a total of $12.8 billion in assets under management as of March 31. Its business has been hurt recently by an oversupply of mutual funds and slumping stock and bond prices.

The transaction is subject to the approval of both companies' boards.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading, Primerica was unchanged at $29.625 a share; American Capital closed at $9.125 a share, up 75 cents.
