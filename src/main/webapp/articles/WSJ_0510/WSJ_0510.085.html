New Securities Issues

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/10/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: MUNICIPALS

Stanislaus County Waste to Energy Finance Agency, Calif. -- $92.3 million of waste-energy revenue bonds, due Jan. 1, 1992-1999, 2005 and 2010, were tentatively priced by a Prudential-Bache Capital Funding group to yield from 6.60% in 1992 to 7.81% in 2010. Current interest serial bonds are priced to yield from 6.60% in 1992 to 7.50% in 1999. There are $10 million of 7 5/8% term bonds priced at 99.25 to yield 7.71% in 2005 and $64.9 million of 7 3/4% term bonds priced at 99.39 to yield 7.81% in 2010. The bonds are rated triple-B by Standard & Poor's Corp.

Delaware -- $65 million of general obligation bonds due May 1, 1991-2010, apparently were won by a Manufacturers Hanover Securities Corp. group with a bid setting the net interest cost at 6.912911%. The bonds, rated double-A by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and double-A-plus by S&P, were priced for reoffering to yield from 6.00% in 1991 to 7.20% in 2009 and 2010. There are no term bonds.

MORTGAGES

Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $500 million of real estate mortgage investment conduit securities is being offered in nine classes by Salomon Brothers Inc. The offering, Series 174, is backed by Freddie Mac 10% securities. Details on yields weren't immediately available.

UBS Securities Inc. -- $300.3 million of UBS Trust 5 Remic securities is being offered in 13 classes. The offering is backed by Government National Mortgage Association 9 1/2% securities. For classes with details available, yields range from 9.29% to 10.15%. The issue is rated triple-A by S&P and Moody's.

ASSET-BACKED

Rochester Community Savings Bank -- $125 million of certificates backed by automobile and light-truck loans with a coupon rate of 9 1/4% at 99 33/64 to yield 9.51% through First Boston Corp. The issue, through RCSB 1990-A Grantor Trust, was priced at a yield spread of 0.79 percentage point above the Treasury two-year note. The issue has an estimated average life of 1.88 years, assuming monthly prepayments at 1.3% of the original loan balance. The legal final maturity date is Nov. 11, 1995. The issue is rated triple-A by Moody's, based on the quality of the underlying auto and light-truck loans and a letter of credit covering 12% of the deal from Credit Suisse. There is no guarantee from Rochester Community Savings Bank on the issue. A First Boston official said the issue was all sold.

SHV Holdings N.V. (Dutch) -- 200 million Dutch guilders ($107.4 million) of 9 3/8% bonds due June 15, 2000, priced at 101 1/2 to yield 9.14%, via Bank Mees & Hope N.V.

Abbey National Treasury Services -- 150 million Finnish markka ($38.3 million) of 14% bonds due Dec. 29, 1992, priced at 101.65 via Bankers Trust International (books) and Postipankki.

Toyota Motor Credit Corp. -- 150 million European currency units of 10 5/8% bonds due June 6, 1994, priced at 101 5/8 to yield 10 5/8% via Banque Paribas Capital Markets Ltd.
