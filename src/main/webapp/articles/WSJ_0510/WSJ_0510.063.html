Law: U.S. Judge Finds Adviser In Contempt of SEC Accord

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/10/90

NEW YORK -- A federal judge found a New Jersey investment adviser in civil contempt, saying he "has not made a good faith effort" to comply with a consent agreement he made with the Securities and Exchange Commission. In March, the SEC charged Frank R. Breitweiser, doing business in Newton, N.J., under the name of Orange County Financial Planning Services, with making "material misrepresentations."

The charges stemmed from the adviser's offering for sale approximately $1.1 million of securities for two investment firms under whose names Mr. Breitweiser also does business, Orange County Financial Income Fund and Orange County Financial Stock Management. Mr. Breitweiser was accused of misrepresenting "the nature, value, liquidity, rate of return, use of proceeds and risk" of the securities.

In New York, U.S. District Judge Peter Leisure ordered Mr. Breitweiser to pay a fine of $250 a day, beginning May 14, until he complies with the agreement. Under that accord, Mr. Breitweiser didn't admit or deny any wrongdoing, but he agreed to repay investors more than $151,000. He agreed not to commit any such violations in the future.

Mr. Breitweiser declined to comment. According to the judge's order, Mr. Breitweiser "does not contest the fact that he has not satisfied the conditions" of the agreement. "Rather, he claims that he is unable to satisfy" the terms of the accord "due to his current financial position."
