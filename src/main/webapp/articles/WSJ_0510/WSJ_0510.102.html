Credit Ratings: New York City Gets SP-1 Rating From S&P On $900 Million Notes

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/10/90

NEW YORK -- Standard & Poor's Corp. said it assigned its SP-1 rating to New York City's $900 million of general obligation revenue anticipation notes -- or RANs -- fiscal 1990, Series C and D. S&P also affirmed its SP-1 rating on $1.25 billion of outstanding RANs due June 29. S&P said the city is being forced to do additional fiscal 1990 cash flow borrowing because of New York state's delay in adopting its 1991 budget. S&P also said the city's long-range credit outlook remains negative because of a looming $1 billion budget gap. "There are enough risks and uncertainties . . . that balanced financial results are by no means assured," S&P said.

Also, Moody's Investors Service Inc. assigned its MIG-2 rating to today's RAN issue. Moody's also lowered to MIG-2 from MIG-1 the ratings on the fiscal 1990 series A and B notes also due June 29.

Moody's also cited uncertainties surrounding the resolution of the state budget process or the city's statutory ability to renew its notes if the state monies aren't received on time.

Last night New York City Mayor David N. Dinkins blasted the Moody's decision, calling it "premature and unnecessary." Speaking at a dinner of the Municipal Forum of New York, Mr. Dinkins said he understands the rationale for the decision but strongly disagrees with it and hopes to persuade Moody's to change its decision.
