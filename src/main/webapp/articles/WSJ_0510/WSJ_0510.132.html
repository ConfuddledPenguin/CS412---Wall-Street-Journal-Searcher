French Economic Forecast

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/10/90

PARIS -- France's total output of goods and services is likely to continue expanding at an annual rate of between 2% and 3% in the first half of 1990 on a market basis, little changed from the pace recorded in the second half of 1989, the National Statistics Institute said. The French gross national product grew 4.1% on a market basis and 3.7% overall in 1989. The former figure only takes into account market activity, thereby excluding certain government transactions.

French government forecasters in the Economics Ministry said last month that GNP in 1990 would rise 3.2%.

Inflation, meanwhile, is expected to ease to an annual rate of 3.1% by June, down from 3.4% in March, helped by lower petroleum and food prices, as well as the stronger franc, which lowered the cash cost of imports in the short term.
