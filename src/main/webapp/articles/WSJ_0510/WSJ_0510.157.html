Marketing Brief -- Time Warner Inc.: Entertainment Weekly Buys 7 Days' Subscription File

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/10/90

Entertainment Weekly, the new magazine put out by New York-based Time Warner Inc.'s Time Inc. Magazine Co., said it has bought the name and subscription file of 7 Days, which was folded last month. Terms weren't disclosed. Michael Klingensmith, publisher of Entertainment Weekly, said that 7 Days' 20,000 paid subscribers would be offered issues of Entertainment Weekly to fulfill the rest of their subscriptions. Mr. Klingensmith said that those readers would roughly double Entertainment Weekly's circulation in New York City, and that the 7 Days audience "has extremely attractive demographics to advertisers."

The 7 Days name may be used for a special regional edition or supplement to Entertainment Weekly, which would provide specific entertainment listings for New York, said Mr. Klingensmith. "It's all speculative at this point," he said, "But we feel the name has very good equity, particularly in this market." Just 10 days after 7 Days' owner Leonard Stern suspended publication, the magazine won a National Magazine Award for General Excellence among small circulation magazines.
