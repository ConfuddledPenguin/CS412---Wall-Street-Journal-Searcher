Marketing & Media: Tisch Is Optimistic About CBS Ratings At Annual Meeting

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/10/90

NEW YORK -- CBS Inc. President Laurence A. Tisch chose to look on the bright side of the ratings at the struggling TV network during CBS's typically contentious annual shareholders meeting. Responding to a stockholder's criticism that almost every portion of the network's schedule -- including daytime, news, prime-time and late night -- was in disarray, Mr. Tisch took exception. "We're a total disaster only in prime time," the chief executive officer said. "We will correct that."

In the 1989-90 season, CBS finished in third place for the third consecutive year. Last week, a CBS show tied for first place, but it was a 40-year-old never-before-seen pilot of the 1950's hit "I Love Lucy."

The only other CBS show that consistently places in the Top 10 is "60 Minutes," a 21-year-old broadcast produced by the company's news division. In the just-completed TV season, the highest-ranked CBS entertainment show was "Murder, She Wrote," which placed No. 13 and has been on the air for six years.

"We all realize what's going on," Mr. Tisch said, "and you're going to see progress starting in September. We're making progress in prime time. Slower than we had hoped, but we're getting there."

A shareholder asked about reports that Walt Disney Co. would purchase CBS. "I don't know of anybody who is going to buy CBS," he said. "I think CBS is going to remain an independent company."

He also said that CBS wouldn't purchase other TV stations "unless they make economic sense." CBS owns five stations covering 20.8% of the country. Under regulations by the Federal Communications Commission, a network can own stations covering 25% of the country.
