Jury Convicts Art Gallery And Officers in Dali Fraud

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/10/90

HONOLULU -- A federal jury convicted Center Art Galleries and two of its officers on mail and wire fraud charges in connection with the sale of fake lithographs by surrealist artist Salvador Dali. The U.S. District Court in Hawaii ruled that the gallery and its officers sold prints that they had reproduced at a cost of $50 to customers for prices ranging from $1,000 to more than $20,000. The major selling tool of the gallery, according to the U.S. attorney's office in Honolulu, was to tell customers that the prints were a great investment opportunity.

In some cases, according to the attorney's office, even the original works from which the reproductions were made hadn't been executed by Dali.

The two convicted officers of the gallery are William D. Mett, its president, and Marvin L. Wiseman, its vice president.
