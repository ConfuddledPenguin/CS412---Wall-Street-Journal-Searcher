Spain's Tourism Revenue

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/10/90

MADRID -- Spain's tourism revenue dropped 3.2% in the first three months of the year to 363.9 billion pesetas ($3.51 billion) from 375.9 billion pesetas ($3.62 billion) a year earlier, according to the Ministry of Transportation, Tourism and Communication. Tourism, which accounts for nearly 10% of Spain's gross domestic product, has sagged in recent months after booming in 1987 and 1988 and stabilizing in 1989.

Last year, revenue from tourism reached $16.1 billion.
