Federal Judge Rules Against a Landlord Of Bloomingdale's

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/10/90

NEW YORK -- A federal bankruptcy judge in Cincinnati refused a request by B. Brothers Realty Limited Partnership, landlord of the Manhattan Bloomingdale's store, to permit it to pursue an action to terminate that store's lease. Judge J. Vincent Aug Jr. threw out that request, ruling that, for procedural reasons, B. Brothers' actions violated the Bankruptcy Code. The judge called B. Brothers' actions "vexatious," according to two lawyers at the hearing, and assessed sanctions against the landlord, which was represented by the New York law firm of Pryor, Cashman, Sherman & Flynn.

At the hearing, Judge Aug also extended the period during which Federated Department Stores Inc. and Allied Stores Corp. have the exclusive right to file a reorganization plan for the two Campeau Corp. retail units. Judge Aug gave Federated and Allied until Sept. 12 to file their plan. The company's creditors hadn't objected to the extension.

In bankruptcy cases, the debtor has a right to file its own reorganization plan before creditors have a chance to suggest an alternative. But the debtor must file within a designated period or lose that right.

In court documents, B. Brothers charged that Federated had breached its lease by improperly transferring its interest in the store lease to a unit, Bloomingdale's Inc. B. Brothers also argued that Federated had violated its rights by pledging the lease as security for new financing after Federated filed for bankruptcy-court protection on Jan. 15.

David Heiman, counsel to Federated, said B. Brothers' arguments are "based on a distinction without a difference." No rent payments have been missed.

In assessing sanctions, the judge said he would require B. Brothers to pay the legal fees incurred by Federated in this matter.

After the hearing, Frederick Danziger, a lawyer representing B. Brothers, said, "We find what happened extraordinary. We obviously just want to review the transcript before commenting further. {We} view the judge's action as very surprising."
