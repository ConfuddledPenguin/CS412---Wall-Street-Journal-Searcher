U.S. Implicates Others at Trial Of Futures Trader

  • Author: Scott McMurray
  • Date: 05/10/90

CHICAGO -- A federal prosecutor implicated four unindicted Swiss franc futures traders -- in addition to the three indicted traders on trial here -- as allegedly taking part in an illegal scheme to defraud customers. The allegations against the four traders in the Swiss franc pit at the Chicago Mercantile Exchange are a sign the federal futures trading probe will focus on alleged wrongdoing by more than the 48 individuals who have so far been indicted on fraud charges at the Merc and the Chicago Board of Trade. The allegations came during the government's opening argument in the first of three scheduled trials of futures traders and a clerk charged last year with stealing from customers.

Exchange officials have pointed to the limited number of indictments as evidence that wrongdoing in their markets is a relatively rare event. But the government's opening salvo in the Swiss franc case indicates that many more exchange members may have the propriety of their trades questioned by prosecutors.

Sixteen Japanese yen futures traders on the Merc and 12 soybean futures traders and a clerk on the Board of Trade will stand trial in September on fraud charges in two separate trials. In documents previously filed relating to the soybean futures case, the government alleged that its undercover agent in that pit made illegal trades with "scores of unindicted co-conspirators."

The government said the Swiss franc traders on trial, Robert Mosky, Danny Scheck and David Zatz, were aided in their alleged scheme to steal from customers by four other traders: Gary Kost, Michael Joyce, Michael Ansani and Scott Schumer.

Prosecutors gave no indication as to whether they had enough evidence to seek grand jury indictments against the four traders.

Mr. Kost is an employee of Shearson Lehman Hutton Inc. and is a broker in the Swiss franc pit buying and selling futures contacts for his employer and its customers. Mr. Joyce is an unaffiliated broker who handles a significant number of Shearson orders. A Shearson official said he was unaware of the government allegations and couldn't comment.

Mr. Kost couldn't be reached for comment. A woman answering the phone at Mr. Joyce's home referred a call to his lawyer, Terry Grimm, who couldn't be reached for comment.

Mr. Ansani, one of the most active independent traders in the Swiss franc futures pit, couldn't be reached for comment. Mr. Schumer also trades for his own account, and declined to comment on the government allegations.
