Turkish Firm to Pay $29 Million for 75% Of FNB Rochester

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/10/90

ROCHESTER, N.Y. -- FNB Rochester Corp. said Cukurova Holding AS Group, a diversified Turkish concern, agreed to buy 75% of FNB's common stock outstanding at $20 a share, for a total of $29 million. FNB is the holding company for First National Bank of Rochester and the small Atlanta (N.Y.) National Bank. FNB has assets of nearly $300 million.

In national over-the-counter trading yesterday, FNB's stock closed at $13.50, up $2.25 a share. The stock had been trading between $7 and $7.50 a share until FNB announced May 1 that it had received an unsolicited inquiry about a purchase of the bank's stock. The book value is $8.60 a share.

The agreement calls for redemption of FNB's $7.25 million of 10% convertible capital notes at 110% of the principal amount, and provides for an infusion of $10 million in new capital.

Cukurova has interests in banking, insurance, steel, cement, construction and international trade, FNB said. The Turkish company is seeking a New York bank to handle international transactions, partly because of plans for a unified European market after 1992, said Wilbur F. Beh, FNB president. He said a business broker employed by Cukurova happened to know one of FNB's directors.

First National Bank of Rochester, which has offices in Rochester, Syracuse and Elmira, may open an office in New York City following completion of the transaction, Mr. Beh said. He said other European concerns also may seek banking outlets in the U.S. in preparation for the 1992 changes, which probably will increase trade between Europe and the U.S. as well as among European countries.

The agreement is subject to negotiation of a definitive agreement within 30 days and to approval by FNB shareholders and bank regulators, the company said. Cukurova would purchase the 75% interest through a tender offer, Mr. Beh said.

Under the preliminary agreement, present management and directors would continue, FNB said. The additional capital would allow the banking concern to continue its growth, Mr. Beh said. He said the bank hasn't had nonperforming loan problems comparable to some other banks, partly because it hasn't reached outside its service area for business.
