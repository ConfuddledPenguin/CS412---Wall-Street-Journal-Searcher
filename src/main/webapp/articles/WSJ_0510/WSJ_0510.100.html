Business Brief -- Dun & Bradstreet Corp.: Direct-Marketing Company Ordered to Pay Damages

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/10/90

Dun & Bradstreet Corp. said a federal court ordered a direct-marketing company and its principals to pay it $876,225 in damages and attorney fees for copyright infringement. Dun & Bradstreet, Parsippany, N.J., said Chief Judge Barefoot Sanders, of the U.S. District Court in Dallas, ruled that Southwest Data Processing Inc., Bedford, Texas, infringed the copyright of Dun's Marketing Services, a unit that licenses a data base containing information on nearly nine million companies.

Dun's Marketing Services alleged in November that Southwest Data and principals Walter H. Cushman and Rodney L. Holloman were selling unauthorized copies of the data base.

Mr. Cushman denies any wrongdoing but said he can't afford to appeal the judge's order. As a result of the legal fight, Mr. Cushman said, Southwest Data "is in the process of filing for bankruptcy."
