Business Brief -- Ultimate Corp.: Computer-Products Concern May Get New Takeover Bid

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/10/90

Investor Norman Elowitz said he may make a new offer to acquire Ultimate Corp., an East Hanover, N.J., computer-products concern. In a filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission, Mr. Elowitz, chairman of Honey Fashions Ltd., also said he raised his stake in Ultimate to 7.2%, or 686,500 shares, from 6.2%. Mr. Elowitz said he increased his stake to strengthen his position at the company.

In March, Ultimate rejected Mr. Elowitz's $10-a-share offer, valued at $95 million. It said it doubted Mr. Elowitz's ability to finance a takeover.

Michael O'Donnell, Ultimate's chairman, said: "Nothing has changed from the company's perspective. But I think the board must fulfill its fiduciary responsibility, and if that means looking at a new offer, it will."

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Ultimate closed at $6 a share, down 12.5 cents.
