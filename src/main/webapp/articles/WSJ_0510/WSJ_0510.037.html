For Sixth Week In a Row, Assets Of Funds Decline

  • Author: Georgette Jasen
  • Date: 05/10/90

Money-market mutual fund assets dropped for the sixth week in a row, as investors found other places to put their cash. Assets of 427 taxable funds fell by $1.2 billion in the week ended Tuesday, to $377 billion, according to IBC/Donoghue's Money Fund Report, a Holliston, Mass.-based newsletter.

"I take my cue from the auction" of three-year Treasury notes, said Walter Frank, chief economist for Donoghue's. "Money funds are getting some competition."

Individual investors bought record amounts of three-year notes at the auction Tuesday, getting a yield of 8.74%. Demand from individuals also was high at yesterday's auction of 10-year notes, when the yield was 8.88%. Six-month T-bills yielded 8.28% at Monday's auction.

Average money fund yields, meanwhile, remained just below 8%, where they've been for the last three months. Donoghue's reported that the average seven-day compound yield, which assumes reinvestment of dividends, was 7.96% for the second consecutive week.

It's still possible to get yields approaching 9% in a money fund, although it requires some looking. The top performing fund in the latest week, Dreyfus Worldwide Dollar, had a seven-day compound yield of 8.91% and a few other funds offered more than 8.5%.

Yields aren't likely to change much in the next few weeks. Average maturity of the funds' investments remained at a fairly long 44 days, about what it has been for most of the year. Money funds invest in such things as commercial paper (short-term corporate IOUs), bank certificates of deposit and short-term Treasury securities.

Longer maturities usually are thought to indicate that portfolio managers expect rates to decline because they make it possible to retain comparatively high yields. Mr. Frank of Donoghue's said he expects rates, and hence money fund yields, to remain stable for the next couple of months because of uncertainty about the strength of the economy.

The average seven-day simple yield of the taxable funds slipped to 7.66% from 7.67%. The average 30-day simple yield remained at 7.68% and the average 30-day compound yield stayed at 7.98%.

Yields on tax-free funds declined. The average seven-day compound yield, for instance, eased to 5.74% from 5.79%. That is equal to 7.97% for an investor in the 28% tax bracket and 8.57% for someone paying 33%.

Assets of the 220 tax-exempt funds tracked by Donoghue's rose by $180.6 million to $75.6 billion. Average maturity lengthened by a day to 42 days.
