DDB Offers Pledge To Benefit Clients, But Its Rivals Shrug --- Omnicom Unit Calls Policy Of Rebate, Bonus Novel, Says 2 Firms Interested

  • Author: Thomas R. King
  • Date: 05/10/90

NEW YORK -- DDB Needham Worldwide Inc. announced a new compensation plan in which it promises to "guarantee" its clients' results -- but industry executives expressed skepticism. DDB Needham, a unit of Omnicom Group, heralded its new plan, which it calls "Total Creativity. Guaranteed Results," as a groundbreaking step in agency compensation. Clients will be given a rebate of as much as 30% off a negotiated base rate if they agree to a long list of conditions and if specific, agreed-upon results aren't reached. If goals are met, the client would pay DDB Needham a bonus.

But ad-agency executives, while lauding DDB Needham's attempts to be more accountable to its clients, said they had no plans to follow suit. They noted that agencies have been negotiating compensation plans with clients for years. The once-standard 15% commission has given way to negotiated incentive and bonus systems.

"I'm very skeptical," said Marvin Sloves, chairman of ad agency Scali, McCabe, Sloves. "Is this a thoughtful, intelligent way of being recompensed, or a publicity gimmick?"

DDB Needham insists that its plan is different from other incentive plans. For one thing, DDB Needham is the first agency to announce a compensation package as policy. For another, the plan relies on DDB Needham and the client identifying specific goals, such as specific sales increases.

Industry executives also wondered how DDB Needham can guarantee results, when so many factors besides advertising come into play in a product's sales. "Anytime you get paid through an incentive, it's a great idea," said Mr. Sloves. "But I just don't understand how to implement" such a system intelligently.

DDB Needham's clients will be trying to sort that out now. Keith Reinhard, the agency's chairman, said that two, which he declined to identify, are interested in trying it. And Peter Falcone, president of DDB Needham's New York office, said H.J. Heinz Co.'s Weight Watchers is interested. But Mr. Reinhard refused to speculate on how many of DDB Needham's 55 U.S. and Canadian clients, all of whom are being offered the plan, would ultimately take the agency up on its idea. DDB Needham insiders quietly speculated that the figure would be relatively low as clients take a wait-and-see attitude.

Clients may be put off, moreover, by the onerous conditions DDB Needham demands. Among other things, DDB Needham insists that it be given total, unquestioned control over a client's entire marketing program -- including work by outside public-relations, direct-mail and other unrelated marketing firms. "Insane," scoffed Arthur A. Anderson, an advertising consultant at Morgan, Anderson & Co., New York. "This may be a classic marketing ploy for the ad agency to reassert control over these other budgets."
