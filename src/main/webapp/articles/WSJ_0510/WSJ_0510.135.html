Business Brief -- Niagara Exchange Corp.: Lawsuit Seeks to Block Sale Of Preferred in Proxy Fight

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/10/90

Niagara Exchange Corp. said Riedman Corp. is seeking to set aside its recent sale of convertible preferred to W.R. Berkley Corp. for about $8.2 million, or to permanently enjoin the voting rights of that preferred stock. Riedman, a Rochester, N.Y., insurance agency that currently owns about 14% of Niagara's 1.8 million common shares outstanding, launched a proxy vote to seek two of the company's seven board seats. W.R. Berkley's 970,000 shares of Niagara's convertible preferred represent a 35% stake. Niagara, Buffalo, N.Y., and W.R. Berkley, Greenwich, Conn., are property-casualty insurance holding companies.

Riedman, in a lawsuit filed in Delaware Court of Chancery, is claiming that Niagara's sale of the preferred stock, which is still subject to regulatory approval, has blocked its proxy fight.

Niagara said the allegations in the complaint are without merit.
