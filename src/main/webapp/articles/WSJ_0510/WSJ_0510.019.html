From a Oct. 13, 1988 letter by George Bush to Povilas Janciauskas, an officer of the Lithuanian American Community:

{F}reedom is not a privilege -- it is a human right. The United States has always condemned the violation of your national sovereignty, and in the years ahead it will never condone the forced occupation of Lithuania by the Soviet Union. To do so would be to turn our backs on our own values and heritage. As a nation born out of a struggle against tyranny, Americans should never apologize for helping others win their freedom and I never will. And so, as we enter the final stretch of the campaign, I would respectfully request that you support my candidacy.
