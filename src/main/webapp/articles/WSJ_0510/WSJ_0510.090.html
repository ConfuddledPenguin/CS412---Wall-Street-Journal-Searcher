Wednesday's Markets: Dollar Posts Steep Drop, Bonds Fall --- Stocks, in Response, End Mixed as Streak Of Gains Is Halted

  • Author: Douglas R. Sease
  • Date: 05/10/90

Slumping bond prices and a plunging dollar kept stock prices from posting an eighth consecutive gain. The Dow Jones Industrial Average fell a scant 0.68 point to 2732.88 in moderately active trading. Long-term bond prices fell despite a good response to the Treasury's sale of $10 billion in 10-year notes. The dollar plunged to its lowest level in two years against the West German mark.

Bond prices and the dollar set the tone for the day. The dollar's steep drop against the mark reflected the opinion of many currency traders that U.S. interest rates may be headed lower. That opinion is rooted in evidence the U.S. economy is more sluggish than had been thought and in the success of the first leg of the Treasury's three-part, $30.5 billion quarterly refunding auction Tuesday.

The dollar tumbled to a 28-month low against the West German mark as news spread that the Bush administration might be willing to raise some taxes to reduce the U.S. budget deficit.

"It was a bad day for the dollar," said Robert Hatcher, a vice president at Barclays Bank PLC in New York. "The market feels that if Bush is inclined to hike taxes and reduce the budget deficit, then interest rates in the U.S. can go lower. Some of the hot money started to leave." Lower interest rates generally tend to reduce a currency's allure to international investors and speculators. Over the past two weeks, the dollar has fallen 3.5% against the mark.

Bond prices fell because of uncertainty about the outcome of the remainder of the Treasury's refunding auction. Although the first sale went well, traders were more concerned about the sale of 10-year notes yesterday and $10 billion in 30-year bonds today. News that the 10-year notes sold at an average yield of 8.88% wasn't enough to convince traders today's 30-year auction would go as well.

The weakness in the dollar and bond prices spread to stocks early in the day. The Dow industrials were off about 13 points with less than 30 minutes of trading left when the results of the note sale were announced. Some computer-assisted buy programs were triggered, recouping nearly all the lost ground by the close. Standard & Poor's 500-stock index eked out a small gain.

In major market action:

Stock prices were mixed. Volume totaled 152.2 million shares on the New York Stock Exchange, where gaining issues outnumbered decliners 760 to 733.

The dollar plunged against the mark. In late New York trading the dollar was quoted at 1.6360 marks, down from 1.6573 Tuesday, and at 156.60 yen down from 157.62 yen Tuesday.

Bond prices fell. The Treasury's benchmark 30-year bond lost 1/2 point, or $5 for each $1,000 of face amount. The yield on the issue climbed to 8.86%.
