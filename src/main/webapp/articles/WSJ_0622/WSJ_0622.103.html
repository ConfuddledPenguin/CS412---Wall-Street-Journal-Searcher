Merrill Lynch Payment Settles CBOT Allegations

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/22/90

CHICAGO -- Merrill Lynch, Pierce, Fenner & Smith Inc. paid $30,000 to settle Chicago Board of Trade allegations that it violated exchange trading rules. Under the settlement, the brokerage unit of Merrill Lynch & Co., New York, didn't admit or deny the violations. The CBOT said it had "reason to believe" Merrill Lynch floor clerks disclosed "the possibility of a customer order" to a trader for the company's Government Securities unit before the order was initiated and entered in the trading pit.

Prior knowledge of a big pending order can enable traders to profit from the market effect of the order, and is known as "front running."

Merrill Lynch said that it settled the action to avoid the expenses of a protracted legal proceeding, and that it is "confident that the trade at issue was executed competitively and in a manner consistent with the best interests of our customer."

In December, Merrill Lynch paid a $250,000 CBOT fine, without admitting or denying the allegations, for executing non-member trades as trades for the firm, a practice that reduced the fees it paid the exchange. In that case, Merrill Lynch also agreed to pay the CBOT "certain fees and expenses," which one source said totaled $2.9 million.
