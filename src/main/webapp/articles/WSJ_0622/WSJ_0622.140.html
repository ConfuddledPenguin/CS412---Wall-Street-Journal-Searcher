Business Brief -- Ames Department Stores Inc.: Bankruptcy Court Approves Nearly All Proposed Closings

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/22/90

Ames Department Stores Inc., Rocky Hill, Conn., said a U.S. bankruptcy court judge approved 219 of 221 store closings Ames had proposed. The troubled retailer, operating under a Chapter 11 bankruptcy reorganization, also received clearance to resume construction on a distribution center in Leesport, Pa., and to make vacation payments to former employees who left the company before April 25, the date of the bankruptcy filing. The case is being heard by Judge Howard C. Buschman III in federal bankruptcy court in New York.

Judge Buschman reserved judgment on the closing of two Ames stores in Cincinnati and Bloomsburg, Pa., pending further testimony from affected employees and Ames management at a hearing July 11.

The 219 stores will be shut in August and September after inventories and clearance sales, an Ames spokesman said. The company has said it needs to close the stores and let go their 18,000 employees to stem losses and regain profitability.

"While we recognize the impact this will have, we had to take this action to preserve the jobs of the majority of the Ames employees," said Stephen L. Pistner, Ames chief executive officer.
