Marketing & Media: Forbes Ends Ties to Writer Over Credentials Dispute

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/22/90

NEW YORK -- Forbes magazine ended its association with a free-lance reporter whose activities regarding a recent article about Merv Griffin were questioned by a federal judge. A spokesman for Forbes said that John Connolly was "a free-lancer who is no longer with Forbes." He declined to comment further. Mr. Connolly, who Forbes said is a former policeman and stockbroker, shared credit for an article about Mr. Griffin's purchase of Resorts International, the hotel and casino concern.

The Griffin article, in the June 11 issue, said that Michael Nigris Jr., the former president of Mr. Griffin's Griffin Co., had ties to organized crime. Mr. Nigris sued Mr. Griffin last November for breach of contract after he was fired.

Federal Judge Dickinson Debevoise in Newark, N.J., asked the U.S. attorney last month to investigate whether Mr. Connolly had misrepresented himself as a police officer to obtain sealed affidavits in Mr. Nigris's lawsuit. The judge, in letters to parties in the suit, said he had granted Mr. Connolly's request for access to the affidavits but subsequently learned Mr. Connolly's police identification was outdated.

Mr. Connolly, who said he was a New York City policeman from 1965 to 1976, last month denied he showed a police badge in getting the affidavits. He said he never spoke to the judge and that he had access to the documents before they were sealed March 9.
