Business Brief -- Abitibi-Price Inc.: De-Inking Plant to Be Built At Alabama Newsprint Mill

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/22/90

Abitibi-Price Inc., a Toronto-based forest products concern, said it plans to build a US$56 million de-inking plant at its 50%-owned newsprint mill in Claiborne, Ala. Abitibi-Price, 82%-owned by the Reichmann family's closely held Olympia & York Developments Ltd., said the plant will process 125,000 metric tons of wastepaper annually. The newsprint mill has an annual capacity of 220,000 metric tons and is scheduled to begin operations next month. A metric ton is equal to about 2,204.62 pounds.

The Claiborne newsprint mill, Alabama River Newsprint Co., is jointly owned by Abitibi-Price and Parsons & Whittemore Inc. of New York, a woodpulp maker and builder of pulp and paper mills. By the end of next year, newsprint made at the mill will contain as much as 40% recycled paper, Abitibi-Price said.

Construction of the de-inking plant, which could begin next month, is subject to completion of financing agreements with Alabama River Newsprint's creditors. The Alabama State Teachers' Retirement Fund agreed in principle to lend US$15 million to the project, Abitibi-Price said.
