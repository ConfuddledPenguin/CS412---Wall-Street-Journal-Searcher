Judge Dismisses 11 Counts Against John Mulheren

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/22/90

NEW YORK -- Citing a lack of evidence presented by prosecutors, a federal judge dismissed 11 of the 41 counts against John A. Mulheren Jr. Mr. Mulheren, the former chief of Jamie Securities Co., has been on trial since early last month. He has been charged with conspiracy, securities and mail fraud and keeping false records. The government has alleged that Mr. Mulheren conducted numerous illegal securities trades with Ivan F. Boesky, the convicted former stock speculator.

Judge Miriam Goldman Cedarbaum dismissed many of the counts that charged Mr. Mulheren with mail fraud. The judge said there was no showing that Mr. Mulheren had defrauded his limited partners by many of the actions he is accused of in those counts.

Thomas Puccio, attorney for Mr. Mulheren, said he is pleased the judge dismissed the charges. Assistant U.S. Attorney Alfred Pavlis wouldn't comment on the matter. A federal jury is likely to begin deliberations in the case late next week.
