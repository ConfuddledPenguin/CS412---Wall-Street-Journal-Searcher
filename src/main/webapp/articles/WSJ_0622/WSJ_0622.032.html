Letters to the Editor: But Those Fellows Just Had to Gloat

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/22/90

Your June 1 editorial criticizing my role in Mikhail Gorbachev's cancellation of his scheduled visit to the Hoover Institution gives Hoover too little credit. If the Hoover Fellows had been able to contain themselves, and not publicly gloat over the prospect of Mr. Gorbachev coming hat in hand to the tower of anti-communism for advice on how to save his failed country, I would not have written my letter, and Hoover would not have ended up looking silly. No self-respecting head of state of a proud nation would knowingly walk into such a trap. All I did was help point it out to him.

John F. Manley

Professor

Department of Political Science

Stanford University

Stanford, Calif.
