Business Brief -- Hanson Industries: Bank Financing Is Arranged For Completing Acquisition

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/22/90

Hanson Industries, the U.S. unit of Hanson PLC of the U.K., said it completed arrangements for $2.6 billion in new bank financing with a banking group led by Chemical Bank. The financing will be used to complete the acquisition of Peabody Holding Co., a large U.S. coal producer, and to refinance Hanson's bank debt.

In April, Hanson acquired 45% of Peabody for $504 million and later bid $715 million to purchase the remainder of the company from Newmont Mining Corp. A move by Amax Inc. to top the bid was quickly countered by Hanson with a new price of $725.6 million. The remaining Peabody shares will be closed to new bidders after June 25, and Newmont holders are to vote on the sale June 27; the acquisition is expected to be completed within five days of the vote.

Hanson has interests in tobacco, specialty chemicals, building products and mining, including a 49% stake in Denver-based Newmont.
