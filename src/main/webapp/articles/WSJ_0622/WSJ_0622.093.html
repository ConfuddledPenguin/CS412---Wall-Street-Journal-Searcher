World Bank Approves $592 Million in Loans

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/22/90

WASHINGTON -- The World Bank approved $592 million in loans to Hungary and Yugoslavia. Hungary will get $200 million to support economic reforms and $100 million to finance projects that will boost agricultural exports. Yugoslavia will receive $292 million for highway projects. The World Bank said Yugoslavia plans to spend about $2.3 billion over the next two years to modernize its highway network. The bank called the endeavor an "important" part of the country's drive toward economic reform and growth.

In another action, the International Development Association, a World Bank affiliate, cleared a $35 million credit to Ethiopia for urban development and housing projects in Addis Ababa.
