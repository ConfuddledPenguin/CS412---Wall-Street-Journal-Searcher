Thursday's Markets: Bond Prices, Stocks Edge Up; Dollar Is Mixed

  • Author: Christopher Winans
  • Date: 06/22/90

NEW YORK -- Bond prices, reacting to mixed signals on the economy, seesawed through the day, and stocks followed as both markets ended with small gains. The Dow Jones Industrial Average rose 6.43 points to 2901.73. Long-term U.S. Treasury bond prices rose about 3/8 of a point, or $3.75 for each $1,000 of face amount. The dollar fell against the mark and rose against the yen.

After opening higher, bond prices headed lower on news that first-quarter economic growth was stronger than previously estimated. On the surface, the gain would seem to be bearish for bonds because there was less chance of a recession and therefore less likelihood of the Federal Reserve Board's easing interest rates.

But later, Fed Chairman Alan Greenspan said he found the recent slowing of the economy "puzzling," cheering traders looking for any nuance suggesting Mr. Greenspan might begin leaning toward easing interest rates.

The budget-deficit report for May also helped bonds turn higher. Some investors, expecting the deficit to be wider than most estimates, sold bonds in advance of the news. But when the deficit turned out to be close to expectations, those investors bought back bonds to even up positions.

Stocks tagged along, with the added influence at the end of the session of futures-related buy programs and some institutional buying related to end-of-the-quarter efforts to dress up portfolios.

In currency trading, the dollar firmed against the yen on news suggesting the Japanese economy is overheating, said Linda McLaughlin, currency trader at Chemical Bank.

In major market action:

Stock prices rose on Big Board volume of 138.6 million shares.

Bond prices rose. The yield on the Treasury's benchmark 30-year bond eased to 8.49%.

The dollar was mixed. In late New York trading, the currency was quoted at 1.6778 marks and 154.80 yen, compared with 1.6785 marks and 153.93 yen Wednesday.
