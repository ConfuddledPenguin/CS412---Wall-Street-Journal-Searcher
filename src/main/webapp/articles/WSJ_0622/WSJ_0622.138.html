Business Brief -- Manufacturers Hanover Corp.: London Offices Will Move To Tower at Canary Wharf

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/22/90

Manufacturers Hanover Corp., New York, said it agreed to lease space for its London offices from Olympia & York in the Canary Wharf 50-story office tower. The banking concern said it plans to move about 1,000 employees in commercial banking, corporate finance and investment banking, to seven floors, or about 200,000 square feet, in the tower in 1992 or 1993. Manufacturers Hanover said the move would allow it to put its London operations in one building. The bank currently occupies six offices in various areas of London.

Canary Wharf is a 71-acre, $6.03 billion development in London's Docklands being developed by Olympia & York Developments Ltd. A spokesman for Olympia & York, a big developer in Toronto, said Manufacturers Hanover brings to six the number of tenants scheduled to lease space in Canary Wharf.
