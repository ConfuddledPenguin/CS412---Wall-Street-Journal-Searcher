Business Brief -- Ashland Oil Co.: Profit Expected to Exceed Third-Quarter Record in '86

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/22/90

Ashland Oil Inc., bouyed by substantially higher refinery margins, said it expects fiscal third-quarter profit to "exceed by a comfortable margin" 1986 third-quarter record profit of $82 million, or $1.21 a share. In the 1989 third quarter, ended June 30, the Ashland, Ky., refiner had net income of $44 million, or 78 cents a share, on sales of $2.09 billion.

Ashland said it expects to report that operating income at its Ashland Petroleum Co. unit would exceed the 1986 third-quarter record of $110 million. In the same quarter a year earlier, Ashland Petroleum, the company's refining and marketing division, had operating income of $53 million.

The company declined to reveal its refinery margins-the margins between prices on crude oil and refined products. In the same quarter a year earlier, Ashland reported that its refinery margins were between $3.25 and $3.50, down from between $5 and $6 in the 1988 third quarter.
