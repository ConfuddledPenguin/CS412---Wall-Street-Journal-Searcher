Duriron Sees Profit Decrease

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/22/90

DAYTON, Ohio -- Duriron Co., a maker of pumps, valves and filtration systems, said second-quarter earnings probably won't meet year-earlier record profit of $5.1 million, or 43 cents a share, citing product development programs and manufacturing upgrades. But it said net income should equal about 39 cents a share, the average of the past five quarters. For the full year, Duriron said it still expects to exceed its 1989 record earnings of $19.7 million, or $1.58 a share. Sales last year were $281 million. The company said incoming business remains strong, as a result of capital spending by customers involved in chemical processing.
