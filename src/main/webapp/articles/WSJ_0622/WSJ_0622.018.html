Taiwan's Export Orders Grow

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/22/90

TAIPEI, Taiwan -- Export orders for Taiwanese products in May rose 2.6% to US$6 billion from the previous month and represent growth of 4.5% from $5.74 billion in exports a year earlier, the Economics Ministry said. Officials attributed the month-to-month rise to an increase in orders for machinery and transportation equipment. Orders for machinery totaled $580 million, up 27% from the previous month. About 48% of the orders came from Southeast Asian clients, followed by 21% from the U.S. Transportation orders increased to $397 million in May from $323 million in April.

Taiwan export orders from January through May amounted to $27.73 billion, down 0.64% from a year earlier.

The U.S. remained the leading destination for Taiwanese exports, accounting for orders valued at $2.37 billion in May, up 10% from April but down 0.63% from a year earlier.
