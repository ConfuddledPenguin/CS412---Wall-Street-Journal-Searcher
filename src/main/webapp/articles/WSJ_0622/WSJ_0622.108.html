Dividend News: Syntech to Omit Preferred Dividend Sixth Time in a Row

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/22/90

RENO, Nev. -- Syntech International Inc. said it will miss a sixth consecutive dividend payment on its preferred stock, and may have to seek bankruptcy-court protection if it fails to win restructuring of two existing lottery contracts. Loss-plagued Syntech is one of a handful of makers of transaction-processing equipment used in state-supported lotteries. The company said omitting of the preferred dividend triggers provisions in its certificate of incorporation that allows preferred holders as a class to select two members on the company's four-member board.

Syntech couldn't be reached for comment on whether those holders had yet demanded those seats.

Syntech said it omitted the latest payout, due in September, because of its deteriorating financial condition. The company said its "ability to continue as a going concern and remain in existence" hinges upon restructuring of the two lottery contracts. It provided no details about the contracts.

"If such new arrangements cannot be worked out promptly the company will have no alternative but to consider seeking relief" under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code, Thomas A. McChristy, Syntech's chief executive, said in a news release. Under Chapter 11, a company seeks protection from its creditors while working out a plan to pay its debt.

Syntech posted a loss in fiscal 1989 ended Sept. 30 of $15.1 million, or $2.78 a share, on revenue of $11.3 million. In the first quarter ended Dec. 31, the company had a loss of $2.1 million, or 40 cents a share, on revenue of $6.6 million.
