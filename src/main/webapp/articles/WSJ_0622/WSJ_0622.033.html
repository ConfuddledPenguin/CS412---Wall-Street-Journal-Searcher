Ferrofluidics to Take A Charge in Quarter Of Up to $15 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/22/90

NASHUA, N.H. -- Ferrofluidics Corp., a maker of seals and dampers for computers, said its expects to take a charge of as much as $15 million in its fourth quarter and year ending June 30, related to writeoffs and a corporate restructuring. In fiscal 1989, the company earned $52,000, negligible in per-share terms, on sales of $20.2 million. Fiscal 1989 fourth-quarter results weren't broken out separately. In the fiscal 1990 first nine months, Ferrofluidics had a net loss of $561,000, or 19 cents a share, on sales of $21.6 million. The company declined to forecast results for the current fiscal year.

Ferrofluidics said the planned charge will result partly from a loss from the sale of its AF Technologies Ltd. unit in Britain, and write-downs of other assets, inventories and patents, including some related to its ferrofluid film bearing program. The company also said it will eliminate a deferred-tax asset on its books, because of adoption of new Financial Accounting Standards Board standard.

Ronald Moskowitz, chief executive officer, said the restructuring "is in the long-term best interests of the company and its shareholders, and will result in a more profitable company."
