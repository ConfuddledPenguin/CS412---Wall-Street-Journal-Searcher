Who's News: Kemper Names Sher Chairman and Chief Of Its Bateman Unit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/22/90

LONG GROVE, Ill. -- Kemper Corp. said it named Allan L. Sher chairman and chief executive officer of its Bateman Eichler, Hill Richards Inc. brokerage unit. Mr. Sher, 58 years old, has served as vice chairman of Drexel Burnham Lambert Inc. since January. The brokerage firm Mr. Sher is leaving is in Chapter 11 bankruptcy-law proceedings, and virtually all its operations have been liquidated.

Kemper said Richard Capalbo, 43, chairman and chief executive of Bateman since February 1988, resigned "to pursue other interests." Mr. Capalbo couldn't be reached.

James R. Boris, chairman of Kemper Securities Group Holdings, said: "Rich Capalbo and I just have different management styles. We both came to realize that." Mr. Boris said he expects Mr. Sher to "help us improve profitability" at Bateman, which Mr. Boris said had operating losses last year and so far this year.

Bateman, based in Los Angeles, is one of five regional securities brokerage firms owned by Kemper, an insurance and financial services firm. Late last month, Kemper said it would take a $126.5 million charge to pare its brokerage operations.

A Kemper spokesman said the chairman of its Blunt Ellis & Loewi Inc. brokerage unit in Milwaukee resigned May 24, and a successor hasn't been named.
