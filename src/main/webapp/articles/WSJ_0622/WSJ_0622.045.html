GM Will Recall Camaros, Firebirds To Fix Seat Belts

  • Author: Joseph B. White
  • Date: 06/22/90

DETROIT -- General Motors Corp. said it is recalling about 1.6 million 1984-90 model Chevrolet Camaros and Pontiac Firebirds to fix defective seat belt buckles linked to at least one injury. The company said plastic push buttons that operate the buckle latches in the cars could be weakened by prolonged exposure to sunlight and heat and could break, causing the buckles to fail.

Two other GM cars have the same buckles, the subcompact Pontiac LeMans and the ultra-luxury Cadillac Allante, a GM spokesman said. But the company hasn't heard of any problems with the buckles on those models, he added.

GM said that because it doesn't have enough replacement parts on hand to fix all the cars immediately, it is asking Camaro and Firebird owners not to head for dealerships unless their belt buckles appear broken. Owners who decide not to get the problem fixed immediately will get a second letter this fall to schedule replacement of buckles "as replacement parts become available," the auto maker said.

This is the largest recall GM has announced since July 1989, when it recalled 1.7 million full-size and luxury cars to fix defective cruise control systems suspected of causing throttles to stick open.

GM said Camaro and Firebird owners will know they have a problem if the red plastic push buttons on the safety belt latches fail to hold the belt buckle in place, or won't release properly.

GM officials learned about the problem from reports gathered by its field representatives, the spokesman said. The National Highway Traffic Safety Administration was also investigating the problem. GM decided to conduct the recall voluntarily, however, the spokesman said. GM didn't say how many incidents it had discovered of the buckles failing, other than the one that resulted in an injury to a passenger.

GM said it will eventually replace all of the buckles in the affected models at no charge to owners.
