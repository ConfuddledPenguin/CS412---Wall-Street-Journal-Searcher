Who's News: Celgene Names Ufheil To Succeed Fernandez As President and Chief

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/22/90

WARREN, N.J. -- John L. Ufheil was named president and chief executive officer of Celgene Corp., succeeding Louis Fernandez in those posts. Mr. Fernandez was named chairman of the biotechnology concern but expects to retire next year. Mr. Ufheil, 56 years old, is the former chief operating officer of Mallinckrodt Inc., a medical products and specialty chemicals unit of International Minerals and Chemical Corp., Northbrook, Ill. A company spokesman said the change in leadership is part of a transition planned by Mr. Fernandez, 66, when he became president of Celgene at the company's inception in 1986. Celgene was spun off from the former Celanese Corp. before it merged with the U.S. unit of Hoechst AG of West Germany. Mr. Fernandez, the spokesman said, plans to retire from Celgene next year.

Frank T. Cary, who has been chairman, was named chairman of a new executive committee of the board. Mr. Cary is a former chairman of International Business Machines Corp.

Celgene develops specialty chemicals and processes hazardous waste. It reported revenue of about $3 million last year, derived from research contracts and interest earned.
