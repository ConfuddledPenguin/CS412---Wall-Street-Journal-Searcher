Seidman Has Hip Surgery Following Riding Accident

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/22/90

WASHINGTON -- William Seidman, chairman of the Federal Deposit Insurance Corp., was in satisfactory condition in Santa Fe, N.M., yesterday following surgery for a separated hip. Mr. Seidman, who also heads the government's savings-and-loan cleanup efforts, was injured in a horse-riding accident at his ranch in Santa Fe Wednesday, the agency said. He was in New Mexico delivering a speech to banking executives. Mr. Seidman yesterday had to bow out of a scheduled appearance before the Senate Banking Committee to discuss bank-lending standards.

It wasn't clear how long he would be in the hospital, but an FDIC spokesman said it would probably be at least several days. "I knew he was OK when I heard he ordered a fax machine and a private telephone line in his room," the spokesman said. Mr. Seidman is widely expected to leave his posts in autumn.
