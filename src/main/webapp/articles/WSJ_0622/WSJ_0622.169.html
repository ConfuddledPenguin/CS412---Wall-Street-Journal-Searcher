Credit Ratings: Moody's Lowers Debt Ratings Of Centel Corp.

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/22/90

NEW YORK -- Moody's Investors Service, citing Centel Corp.'s "aggressive expansion of its cellular operations," lowered its ratings on about $1.2 billion in debt issued by the telecommunications concern and two subsidiaries. The rating concern said the "capital expenditure requirements, external financing burden, and earnings pressure" accompanying Centel's cellular-telephone expansion strategy will result in "increased business risk and debt protection measures."

Moody's added, however, that it thinks the cellular telephone business "can provide Centel with considerable cash flow and profit potential over the longer term."

Centel's first mortgage bonds, debentures and notes were marked down to Baa-3 from Baa-1; its commercial paper rating was reduced to Prime-3 from Prime-2.

The company's Centel Capital unit had ratings on its debentures and notes reduced to Baa-3 from Baa-1, and its commercial paper was lowered to Prime-3 from Prime-2.

Ratings on the company's Central Telephone Co.'s first mortgage bonds were lowered to single-A-1 from double-A-2, and its debentures reduced to single-A-2 from double-A-3. Moody's said Central Telephone is entering a series of regulatory hearings that may result in soft earnings performance in the "intermediate term."

A Centel spokesman said the company is "disappointed" with Moody's move. "We remain very optimistic about cellular and its long-term potential," and will continue the expansion effort, he said. The spokesman specifically disagreed with Moody's markdown of the company's commercial paper, saying that Centel's "short-term liquidity situation is strong," particularly after it recently boosted a revolving credit agreement with lenders.
