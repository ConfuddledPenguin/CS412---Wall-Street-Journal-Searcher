International Brief -- Koor Industries: Israeli Firm's 1989 Deficit Was Record $291 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/22/90

Israel's struggling Koor Industries, the country's biggest conglomerate, said it had a record loss of 595 million shekels ($291 million) last year. Koor's annual report also said the holding company had agreed "in principle" to restructure domestic and foreign debts.

"The finalization of a comprehensive financial arrangement would enable Koor to derive the full benefit of its operating progress over the past two years," the report said. Koor said nearly half of the 1989 loss was sustained by Tadiran, its consumer and defense electronics subsidiary. Tadiran and another subsidiary, Soltam, which makes artillery and other weapons, were hurt by cutbacks in Defense Ministry purchases.

The 1989 loss compared with red ink of 430 million shekels in 1988.

Koor, which is owned by Israel's Histadrut labor federation, employs more than 23,500 workers. Sales in 1989 totaled 4.6 billion shekels.
