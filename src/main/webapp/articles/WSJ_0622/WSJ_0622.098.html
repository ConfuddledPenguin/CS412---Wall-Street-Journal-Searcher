Who's News: Caspen Oil Appoints Chief as Parent Firm Arranges Stock Swap

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/22/90

DALLAS -- Caspen Oil Inc. named a new chairman and chief executive in an organizational shuffle that also involved a stock swap between its parent, Caspen PLC, and Churchill Technology Inc., based in Lakewood, Colo. U.K.-based Caspen PLC exchanged half of its 70% stake in Caspen Oil for half of Churchill's 30% stake in a fourth oil exploration company, PRC Technologies Inc. The swap gives Churchill 5.1 million shares of Caspen Oil, which has 14.9 million shares of common outstanding.

Anthony J. Carroll, former president of Churchill, was named chief executive and chairman of Caspen Oil but continues as chairman of Churchill. He has been replaced as president by Wendy A. Cribari, the former vice president and a continuing board member.

Stepping down as chairman of Caspen Oil was Delo H. Caspary, 68 years old, who continues as chairman of Caspen PLC. Mr. Caspary will serve as an advisor to Caspen Oil, said Mr. Carroll, and intends to pursue offshore expansion capital.

Mr. Carroll said he specializes in reorganizing companies and in four years at Churchill increased shareholder equity fourfold, to $2.1 million. In its third quarter ended April 30, Caspen Oil reported a loss of $95,458, or 2 cents a share, compared with a loss of $41,148, or 1 cent a share, in the same period last year.
