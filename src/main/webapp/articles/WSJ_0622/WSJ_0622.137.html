Business Brief -- Public Service Co. of New Mexico: Utility Settles All Litigation With Southern Union Co.

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/22/90

Public Service Co. of New Mexico said it agreed to settle all litigation with Southern Union Co., including a $100 million take-or-pay dispute. PS New Mexico wouldn't say how much it paid to settle the natural-gas contract disputes, citing similar litigation with other companies, but it did say that it will recover most of its settlement costs from customers and has already established adequate reserves for the rest.

Some of the other lawsuits arose out of PS New Mexico's 1985 purchase of Gas Co. of New Mexico from Southern Union, a natural gas distribution company based in Dallas. Southern Union sold the unit as part of its own settlement of a price-fixing suit filed against it in 1979 by consumer groups who were later joined by PS New Mexico and the state of New Mexico.
