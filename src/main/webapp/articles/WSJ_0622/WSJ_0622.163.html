U.S. Auto Makers Expect 3.1% Boost In Output This Week

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/22/90

DETROIT -- The 10 major U.S. auto makers planned to build 145,014 cars this week, a 3.1% increase from 140,592 cars built a year ago. Domestic auto makers said all plants are scheduled to operate next week, with several working overtime.

General Motors Corp. said it plans overtime at its assembly plants in Janesville, Wis., and Lansing, Mich. GM said its Arlington, Texas, assembly plant reopens June 27. It was idled April 27 to re-tool for the 1991 Chevrolet Caprice and Cadillac Brougham.

Ford Motor Co. scheduled daily and weekend overtime at production facilities in Wayne, Mich., Wixom, Mich., and Louisville, Ky. Ford said all of its truck plants are to work overtime next week.

Chrysler Corp. said it plans daily and weekend overtime at its assembly plants in Sterling Heights, Mich., St. Louis, Mo., Belvidere, Ill., and Newark, Del.
