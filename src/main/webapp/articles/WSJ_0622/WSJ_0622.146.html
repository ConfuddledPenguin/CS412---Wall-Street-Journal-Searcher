Credit Ratings: HongKong & Shanghai Debt Ratings Lowered

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/22/90

LONDON -- The credit-rating concern IBCA Ltd., London, said it lowered its ratings on debt of Hongkong & Shanghai Banking Corp., citing general financial performance and uncertainties associated with the takeover of Hong Kong by China in 1997. IBCA, which publishes ratings on U.S. and foreign banks, lowered its individual rating of the Hong Kong-based banking company to B from AB and its long-term rating to AA-minus from AA. The short-term rating was unchanged.

The changeover in control of the British colony "is going to be difficult, and especially so for Hongkong Bank, the largest in the territory, which is seen by many as British," IBCA said.

The bank owns Marine Midland Bank in the U.S. and United Kingdom stock brokerage firm James Capel & Co., but "despite this diversification, the bank remains heavily dependent on its domestic base for the bulk of its earnings," the report said.
