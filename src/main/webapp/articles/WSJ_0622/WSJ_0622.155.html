What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/22/90

CONISTON PARTNERS PLANS to disband. One of the best-known corporate raiders of the 1980s, the firm will keep its holdings in TW Services and UAL and return the rest of its cash to investors next month. The partners cited a dearth of undervalued companies and a lack of financing for the kinds of highly leveraged transactions Coniston favored. ---

The economy grew at a 1.9% annual rate in the first quarter, the government said, boosting its estimate from the 1.3% pace initially reported. The revision reflects export growth that was stronger than first thought.

Greenspan discounted anecdotal evidence of a credit crunch due to tighter bank lending practices. The Fed chairman, saying enough credit appears to be available, predicted "continued modest economic growth."

---

The FCC ordered local phone companies to cut by $1.1 billion the annual access rates charged to long-distance concerns. Previous such reductions have been passed on to customers.

---

Jacobs Suchard suspended trading in its stock amid reports that Philip Morris intends to acquire all or part of the giant Swiss chocolate and coffee company. The whole firm would cost about $4.5 billion, analysts say.

---

Four defense firms face a review by Moody's for a possible downgrading of their debt. The four are Litton, Lockheed, McDonnell and Raytheon.

---

Pathe Communications reached a new, $1.31 billion accord to acquire MGM/UA, but shareholders won't get most of their money until October.

---

The federal budget deficit widened to $42.55 billion in May from $25.43 billion a year earlier. The latest figure reflects sizable thrift-bailout spending and declining tax revenue.

Democrats continued to grouse about the Bush deficit-reduction plan, but didn't walk away from budget talks. Some Republicans also signaled uneasiness with the proposal.

---

Apple Computer's top sales executive is moving to part-time status as part of a continuing shake-up of the firm's domestic sales and marketing.

---

Equifax agreed in principle to acquire Telecredit for $580 million in stock. The purchase would bolster Equifax's already strong position in the information-services industry.

---

Roy Disney's Shamrock has formed a $450 million partnership that will seek to acquire or invest in companies that are burdened by debt.

---

Securities firms are objecting to an SEC request to let investors who have disputes with brokers go to an independent arbitration forum instead of an industry-subsidized panel.

---

Short interest reached record levels for the month ended June 15, rising 2.6% on the Big Board and 0.3% on the American Stock Exchange.

---

Goldman Sachs and Morgan Stanley asked the government to curtail bartering used by pension and mutual funds to obtain research and other services from brokerage firms.
