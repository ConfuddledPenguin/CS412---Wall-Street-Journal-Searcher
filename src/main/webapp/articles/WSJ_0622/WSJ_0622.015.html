Guyana Repays Loans And Regains Eligibility For Credit From IMF

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/22/90

WASHINGTON -- The International Monetary Fund said Guyana has regained eligibility for IMF credit after paying off about $139 million in overdue loans. At the same time, a World Bank official said the South American country also is settling its loan arrears to that agency and will be removed from its list of ineligible borrowers.

IMF officials said Guyana repaid its overdue loans with the help of a "support group" of other IMF member countries, headed by Canada and including France, West Germany, Italy, Japan, Sweden, Britain, the U.S., Venezuela and Trinidad and Tobago.

The IMF's list of ineligible borrowers still includes Peru, Panama, Honduras, Liberia, Somalia, the Sudan, Vietnam, Cambodia, Sierra Leone and Zambia. Most of these countries also aren't eligible to borrow from the World Bank.
