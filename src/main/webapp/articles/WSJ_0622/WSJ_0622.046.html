South Korea Mulls Setting Up Market For Stock Futures

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 06/22/90

SEOUL -- South Korea is considering the establishment of a stock futures market in the near future. The Korea Stock Exchange has recently formed a task force to study the possibility of setting up a stock futures market to stabilize the stock market, according to stock exchange officials.

Shin Poong-Ho, a manager at the stock exchange's task force, said they have long studied the possibility to prepare for the forthcoming liberalization of the local capital market.

Beginning in 1992, foreign investors will be allowed to invest directly in South Korean stocks. Foreigners now invest in the local stock market through indirect channels, such as country funds or equity-related bonds.

Mr. Shin cautioned, however, that it will take a lot of preparation to set up a stock futures market in South Korea since the country now lacks preconditions for such a system. South Korea doesn't even have a commodity futures market yet, he commented.

Sohn Chon-Kyun, a professor at the Korea Banking Institute, said the local stock market isn't mature enough to allow stock futures trading. "The investment pattern is still too volatile and the market is too distorted to allow such trading," Mr. Sohn said.

He added, however, that quantitatively the local stock market and its trading volume are big enough to have such a system.

In his recent report on the possibility of a financial futures market in South Korea, Mr. Sohn said stocks would be the most probable choice for South Korea at the moment if it decides to introduce a financial futures market. Bonds and foreign exchange could follow stocks, he said.

Mr. Sohn proposed that any Korean stock futures market be modeled after the Japanese one. The Japanese style is more suitable for South Korea in that it is more conservative than some other models, he said. South Korea wouldn't have to set up a separate stock futures market, but could use the current stock market for stock futures trading, he said.
