International Brief -- Nationale-Nederlanden N.V.: Health Insurance Alliance With Aegon Being Studied

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/22/90

Nationale-Nederlanden N.V. and Aegon N.V., the Netherlands' two largest insurers, said they are studying the possibility of cooperating in health insurance in the Dutch market. Spokesmen for both insurers said they aren't looking at any broader cooperation and rejected speculation that the talks could lead to a merger. Analysts said some investors hope Nationale eventually might bid for its smaller rival, and suggested that the Dutch insurance business might be ripe for further consolidation.

Aegon and Nationale, both of which derive a large share of their profits outside the Netherlands, are relatively small players in the Dutch health insurance market. They estimated their combined premium income at 550 million guilders ($292.4 million) a year, or 10% of the total for private-sector health insurers. Government entities provide more than two-thirds of Dutch health insurance. Nationale and Aegon said cooperation could help them cut costs, especially in buying automation equipment, and they will study whether other health insurers want to join.
