International Brief -- RWE AG: Energy Concern to Acquire Degussa's 35% Nukem Stake

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/22/90

Degussa AG, a West German metal and chemical company, said RWE AG, West Germany's largest utility and energy concern, agreed to boost its interest in Nukem G.m.b.H. to 90% from 55% by acquiring a 35% stake from Degussa. Financial details weren't disclosed. The deal would complete the reorganization of the West German nuclear fuel-processing company, which ran into trouble in 1987, following charges of bribery of government officials and breaking rules in shipping and storing nuclear materials. In early 1988, Degussa took management control of Nukem, but RWE subsequently became the parent of Nukem when RWE raised its stake in the company to 55%.

Degussa said the reorientation of Nukem away from the nuclear industry and toward environmental, solar energy and process-engineering technology has been successful, but has strengthened the company's affinity to the activities of RWE. For that reason, Degusa said, both companies agreed that "RWE should take more shares in Nukem."
