Business Brief -- Henley Properties Inc.: Real Estate Holding Firm Sees Rise in Quarterly Net

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/22/90

Henley Properties Inc., Hampton, N.H., said it expects second-quarter net income of about $80 million, or $3.83 a share, compared with a pro forma $4.8 million, or 23 cents a share, a year earlier. The real estate holding company also said it expects to report shareholders' net equity of $230 million at the end of the quarter, up from $145 million on a pro forma basis a year earlier. Henley Properties was spun off from Henley Group Inc., also based in Hampton, last December.

Both the earnings and equity gains result mainly from accounting adjustments involving two trash-to-energy plants owned by Henley Properties and operated by Wheelabrator Technologies Inc. under a sale and lease-back arrangement. Henley Properties said it expects to sell the plants for $10 million to Wheelabrator this month. Thus, about $100 million of deferred income treated as a liability on Henley Properties' balance sheet will be eliminated, boosting net equity. Wheelabrator said the deal isn't expected materially to affect its second-quarter results or balance sheet.
