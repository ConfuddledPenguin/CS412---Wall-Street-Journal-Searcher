Letters to the Editor: `Motor Voter' Act: Drive 'Em to the Polls

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/22/90

Your May 18 editorial "Blaming The Voters First" ignores the most frequently cited reason for nonvoting: not being registered to vote. Nonvoters, when surveyed after the 1988 presidential election, said registration requirements were the most significant reason why they did not vote. Eighty-five percent to 90% of those who are registered do vote in presidential elections. Registration requirements differ in each state -- in some instances, from township to neighboring township -- and procedures are often cumbersome, sometimes discriminatory. What is needed is a fair, simple, uniform system of voter registration nationwide.

"The National Voter Registration Act," which passed the House with strong bipartisan support in February and is now before the Senate, would create a more accessible registration system and encourage more Americans to vote. The legislation, dubbed "motor voter" because of its inclusion of automatic applications to register to vote with driver's license applications or renewals, provides opportunities to register at other public agencies as well as at motor-vehicle licensing bureaus.

The costs to set up and administer "motor voter" and the bill's other provisions have been minimal in the more than half-dozen states that already have automatic applications with driver's licensing. Those states report voter registration is dramatically up with a manageable work flow of applications year-round.

The League of Women Voters believes the act is a sensible first step to reverse declining voter turnout. Arcane and obscure registration procedures are a barrier to voter participation that we can do something about. Government should be in the position of helping citizens to vote, not putting up roadblocks that keep people away from the polls on Election Day.

Nancy M. Neuman

Immediate Past President

League of Women Voters

Washington
