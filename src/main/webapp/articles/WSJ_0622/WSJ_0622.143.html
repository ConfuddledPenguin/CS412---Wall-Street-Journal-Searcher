Business Brief -- Drexel Burnham Lambert Group: Government Securities Unit Also Files Under Chapter 11

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/22/90

A Drexel Burnham Lambert Group Inc. unit that specializes in trading government securities joined the parent company in bankruptcy-law proceedings. Drexel filed for protection under Chapter 11 of the U.S. Bankruptcy Code Feb. 13; several of its subsidiaries subsequently filed as well.

In its filing in federal bankruptcy court in Manhattan, Drexel Burnham Lambert Government Securities Inc. said that as of March 30, its total assets were $262.2 million and total liabilities were $155.2 million. The parent company began liquidating its subsidiaries' assets after it filed its own Chapter 11 petition.

The bankruptcy proceedings for the parent company and the subsidiaries are being combined under Judge Howard Buschman III in U.S. Bankruptcy Court in Manhattan. Drexel has said a single reorganization plan will be developed for all the entities.

In its filing, the government securities unit said its largest unsecured creditors include Florida Federal Savings & Loan of St. Petersburg, Fla., which is owed $204,718; Nomura Securities Co., $103,442; Key Bank of Albany, $47,467; and Colorado National Bank of Denver, $20,260.
