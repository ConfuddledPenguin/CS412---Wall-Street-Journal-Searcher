AT&T Says Package Of Discount Services Was Put Into Effect

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/22/90

WASHINGTON -- American Telephone & Telegraph Co. said a discount package of long-distance services went into effect for Resort Condominiums International after the Federal Communications Commission allowed a suspension period to lapse. The FCC had suspended the special discounts for the vacation time-share company in January while the commission investigated the legality of cut-rate custom-tailored packages. At the time, the agency said it was also investigating an equivalent offer from MCI Communications Corp.

The FCC could still declare the AT&T offer, known as Tariff 15, illegal, but the decision to let the suspension expire signaled that the agency may allow AT&T to offer special discounts to meet competition.

"We find it very exciting because we fought so hard for it," said Joseph Nacchio, AT&T vice president-marketing for business communications services. Nevertheless, he acknowledged that he would have preferred an affirmative decision from the agency. The tariff for Resort Condominiums was originally filed nine months ago.

The company has made about six other filings under Tariff 15, including discount services for CBS Inc., PepsiCo Inc., and La Quinta Motor Inns Inc.
