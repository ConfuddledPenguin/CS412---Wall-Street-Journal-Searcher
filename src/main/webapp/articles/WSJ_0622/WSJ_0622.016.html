Outlook for Japan's Economy

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/22/90

TOKYO -- Considering the continuing strength of Japanese domestic demand amid continued worries about the so-called triple demerits of weaker currency, bond and stock markets, Japan's economic growth will slow to a 4.6% pace in the year ending March 31, Cosmo Securities Co.'s research unit predicted. Recently released Japanese gross national product data showed 5% growth in fiscal 1990, with little evidence of damage from the triple demerits, the research group said, but as these factors continue into the current fiscal year, economic growth is likely to cool.

More diversified demand, with a greater desire for luxury goods, will remain an underlying support for personal consumption, seen growing at a 4.4% pace in fiscal 1991, the unit said. Also, housing being constructed will probably decrease slightly, but higher costs from increased value will offset the slowdown.
