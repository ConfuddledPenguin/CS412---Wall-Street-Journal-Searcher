Reynolds Metal Sees 1991 Pretax Earnings Rising $150 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/22/90

RICHMOND, Va. -- Reynolds Metals Co. said 1991 pretax earnings will increase about $150 million as a result of capital projects completed this year. In 1989 the aluminum producer had pretax earnings of $758.3 million. Last year's net was $532.7 million, or $9.20 a share, on revenue of $6.21 billion.

In addition, the aluminum producer said orders and prices began to turn up at the end of the first quarter. "We should see improvement in our second quarter report," the company said. It wasn't more specific.

In the 1989 second quarter the company had net income of $150.7 million, or $2.55 a share, on revenue of $1.68 billion.

The company said 1992 pretax earnings would rise about $200 million as a result of capital expenditures.

"We are investing more in this company now than at any time in our history," the company said. In the current year Reynolds Metals will spend about $900 million on capital projects, up from the annual average of about $700 million spent over the past three years, the company said.
