Business Brief -- First Bank System Inc.: Pact to Buy Banks of Iowa Ended by Minneapolis Firm

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/22/90

First Bank System Inc., Minneapolis, said it ended a nearly nine-year old agreement to acquire Banks of Iowa Inc. and plans to sell back to the Des Moines banking concern 550,197 Class B nonvoting shares for about $38 million, or $69 a share. The 1981 agreement called for First Bank to acquire Banks of Iowa for at least 1.5 times its shareholders equity, when permitted by Iowa banking law, which at the time didn't allow interstate acquisitions.

Both companies have gone through some big changes since then, however. First, the depressed farm economy battered nearly all Iowa banks. And more recently, First Bank itself was hurt by some big bond and loan losses.

Interstate banking powers come to Iowa Jan. 1, and Banks of Iowa said it plans to seek another buyer. With about $2.6 billion in assets and shareholders equity of $214 million at March 31, it is the biggest bankholding company based in Iowa.

Banks of Iowa common stock closed at $69.50, down 50 cents, in over-the-counter trading.
