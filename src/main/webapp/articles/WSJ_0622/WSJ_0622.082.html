Japonica's Planned Revamp of Allegheny International Blocked

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/22/90

PITTSBURGH -- Japonica Partners failed to get enough votes to confirm its plan to reorganize Allegheny International Inc., giving the current edge to a company-sponsored plan. U.S. Bankruptcy Court Judge Joseph L. Cosetti said that in light of the thwarted Japonica effort, the court would "address its energy to the confirmation of the debtor plan." Japonica, based in New York, proposed paying creditors about $655 million in cash, much of it borrowed from banks. Allegheny's plan would repay creditors with stock.

Still, Japonica, which has purchased enough bankruptcy claims to give it more than 50% ownership of the reorganized company, won't likely give up. If it can woo the company's banks -- which rejected the plan by 62% -- with a sweetened offer, Japonica could ultimately prevail.

Indeed, there is growing concern about Allegheny's ability to finance its own reorganization plan. Allegheny's lenders are concerned that the management team tapped to run the reorganized company won't want to stay, given rival Japonica's controlling stake. They are also concerned that Japonica will appeal confirmation of the company plan. At the same time, Japonica's lenders aren't finished conducting due diligence and won't be ready to finance the group's plan for another six to eight weeks.

Allegheny's senior unsecured creditors and common shareholders also voted against Japonica's plan. However, after the votes were cast, Japonica purchased $25.1 million of the senior unsecured claims from a group of insurance company holders.

Allegheny filed for protection under Chapter 11 of the federal bankruptcy code in February 1988. The company has presented 10 reorganization plans, the most recent one approved earlier this year by all creditors except Japonica. The court has indicated that it would invalidate Japonica's blocking vote.
