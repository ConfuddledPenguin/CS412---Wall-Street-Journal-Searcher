Maxco Inc. Expects Profit Improvement In This Fiscal Year

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/22/90

LANSING, Mich. -- Maxco Inc. expects to report profit from continuing operations for the year ended March 31 of about five to six cents a share, or between $300,000 and $400,000. But earnings "absolutely" will improve in fiscal 1991, Max Coon, chairman, president and chief executive, said. In the fourth quarter, Mr. Coon said seasonal losses in the company's construction materials business may have more than cut in half the $737,000, or 13 cents a share, profit from continuing operations accumulated in the first nine months. In fiscal 1989, profit from continuing operations was $425,000, or six cents a share.

A one-time gain of $650,000, or 13 cents a share, from an accounting change will put net income for fiscal 1990 at about 18 cents to 19 cents a share, or about $1 million, compared with a year-earlier net loss, including losses from discontinued operations, of $1.4 million, Mr. Coon said.

For fiscal 1991, Mr. Coon said Maxco expects improved profits to come primarily from an anticipated sale gain of 20% or better in its auto refinishing paint distribution business, improved margins in its construction materials operations, and profits from a 40%-owned factory automation subsidiary, Medar Inc. Maxco had losses related to its Medar stake fiscal 1990, Mr. Coon said.

"We don't have the negatives we've been through for a period of years," Mr. Coon said.

Mr. Coon expects profit from continuing operations in the first quarter ending June 30 to show "an improvement" from the year-earlier $305,000, or six cents a share.

Maxco recently expanded its auto paint supply distribution operations into Chicago, and expects revenues from that business to grow to $33 million in fiscal 1991 from $27 million in fiscal 1990.

The auto refinishing business "is our main area of expansion," Mr. Coon said.
