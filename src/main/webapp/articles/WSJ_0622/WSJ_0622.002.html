Initial State Jobless Claims Increased in June 9 Weeks

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/22/90

WASHINGTON -- Initial claims for regular state unemployment benefits rose to a seasonally adjusted 355,000 in the week ended June 9 from 351,000 in the previous week, the Labor Department said. The number of people receiving regular state benefits in the week ended June 2 fell to a seasonally adjusted 2,388,000, or 2.3% of those covered by unemployment insurance, from 2,400,000 in the previous week, when the insured unemployment rate also was 2.3%.

Counting all state and federal benefit programs, the number of people receiving unemployment benefits in the week ended June 2 rose to 2,267,300 from 2,194,800 a week earlier. These figures aren't seasonally adjusted.
