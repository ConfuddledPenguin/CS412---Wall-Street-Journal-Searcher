Earth Technology Reports Strong Rise In Quarterly Profit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/22/90

LONG BEACH, Calif. -- Earth Technology Corp. reported sharply higher results for the fiscal third quarter and nine months, but cautioned that a slowdown in part of its business may "slightly" affect fourth-quarter results. Jack J. Schoustra, chairman and chief executive, said he is "comfortable" with earnings projections of about 50 cents a share for the current fiscal year, which ends Aug. 25. Earth Technologies is involved in waste management consulting and specialized analysis of potential industrial sites.

In the third quarter ended May 25, net income totaled $190,000, or six cents a share, more than five times the $33,000, or one cent a share, posted in the year-earlier quarter. Revenue jumped 62% to $14.5 million from $9 million on the strength of both acquisitions and growth of existing business.

Mr. Schoustra said the sharp earnings increase was due mainly to depressed results in the year-earlier quarter, stemming from an office closure and relocation. Moreover, he said the latest period included "well in excess of $100,000" of preparation and proposal costs for two, multiyear Air Force contracts awarded during the period totaling $97 million.

The executive said recent softening in backlog for asbestos consulting and laboratory services might lower fourth-quarter profit by "a cent or two." He said the problem was a "hole" in an otherwise busy asbestos-removal work schedule.

For the fiscal nine months, Earth Technology had net income of $1 million, or 32 cents a share, more than double the $456,000, or 16 cents a share, in the year-earlier period on fewer shares. Revenue rose 49% to $40.3 million from $26.9 million. Included in the latest period was about $100,000 of "non-operating" costs related to a restated loss by a subsidiary and a valuation change in deferred compensation related to an acquisition.

Mr. Schoustra said the company "never said it was comfortable with one analyst's forecast that net income will total about 55 cents a share this fiscal year" and that it was "more comfortable" with an estimate of about 50 cents. The executive said possible softening in the fourth quarter might mean a drop of one or two cents from that 50-cent figure.

In the fiscal year ended Aug. 25, 1989, Earth Technology had net income of $518,000, or 18 cents a share, on revenue of $37.8 million.
