International Brief -- Peugeot S.A.: Revenue Increase in 1990 Is Expected to Top 7.9%

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/28/90

French auto maker Peugeot S.A. said it expects consolidated revenue for 1990 to exceed the 7.9% gain it reported for the first quarter, when sales totaled 44.14 billion francs ($7.90 billion). Jacques Calvet, chairman of the French auto group, didn't specify the scope of the expected improvement, but he said it would reflect catching up on production lost to a flood in one of the group's main plants in February, as well as the success of new models launched in the luxury end of the car market during 1989.

Mr. Calvet didn't forecast full-year earnings or cash flow, although he said he wouldn't be disappointed if consolidated 1990 cash flow came in close to the 1989 level of 18.553 billion francs.

Calvet also said the net treasury position of the group showed a surplus of between 1.5 billion and 2 billion francs. As reported, Peugeot was a net debtor until the end of 1989.

For all of 1989, Peugeot's consolidated net profit after payments to minority interests was 10.30 billion francs on revenue of 152.96 billion francs.
