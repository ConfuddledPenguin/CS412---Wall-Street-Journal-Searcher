USX Is Consolidating Texas Oil Operations With Marathon Unit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/28/90

PITTSBURGH -- USX Corp., in a move to cut costs and create efficiencies, said it is consolidating its Texas Oil & Gas Corp. operations with its larger and more profitable Marathon Oil Co. The move was widely anticipated, after USX unsuccessfully tried to sell all of the assets of Texas Oil & Gas. But instead of selling all six geographic packages of exploration and production assets, it now expects to sell only three.

The unsold properties, including the subsidiaries Delhi Gas Pipeline Corp. and FWA Drilling Co., will be combined with Marathon's domestic exploration and production operations. The two subsidiaries will continue to be managed from Dallas and Wichita Falls, Texas, respectively.

The company said it didn't know how many of Texas Oil & Gas's 2,000 employees would be affected by the consolidation. But the greatest effect is expected in the unit's Dallas headquarters staff and in distribution and sales-related offices in California, Texas, Oklahoma and the Gulf Coast area. Marathon had about 23,000 employees at year's end.

USX also said it didn't know what, if any, charges could be taken as a result of the move, or when the the consolidation will be complete. A spokesman said the company hopes savings from the consolidation will offset costs related to the merged operations.

USX Chairman Charles A. Corry said the reorganization will enable the company to "take advantage of numerous synergies to reduce costs and optimize our capital budget and exploration programs in the U.S."

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, USX shares closed at $32.75, up 50 cents.
