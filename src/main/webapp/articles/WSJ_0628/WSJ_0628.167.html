U.S. Can Extradite Banker To Greece, Judge Rules

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/28/90

BOSTON -- A federal judge here ruled that George V. Koskotas, a Greek banker accused by the Greek government of a scheme to embezzle $200 million, can be extradited to Greece. Judge Douglas Woodlock, upholding a magistrate's order filed last year, said Mr. Koskotas, 35 years old, can be extradited by the State Department to face charges that he "misappropriated and embezzled" funds from the Bank of Crete between 1986 and 1988, while he was serving as bank's chairman, managing director and majority owner. Mr. Koskotas has two months to appeal the decision.

The Koskotas affair, as it is called in Greece, began enveloping the government of former Greek Premier Andreas Papandreou in the fall of 1988. Although Mr. Koskotas fled to the U.S. where he was arrested by Federal Bureau of Investigation agents, the banker's claims that he embezzled the money with Mr. Papandreou's approval and deposited the money in accounts of Mr. Papandreou's Panhellenic Socialist Party were a factor in the fall of the government last year.

For the past 19 months, Mr. Koskotas has been in a Salem, Mass., jail while his attorneys have filed motions to avoid extradition. Among the claims overruled by Judge Woodlock are that both the terrorist group called 17 November and the Greek secret police are under orders to kill Mr. Koskotas.
