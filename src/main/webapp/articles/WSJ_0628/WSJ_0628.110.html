Sysco Is One Target In Bid-Rigging Probe In Southeast Texas

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/28/90

HOUSTON -- Sysco Corp. said the Justice Department launched a probe of possible bid-rigging involving several companies, including Sysco, which all provide food services to southeast Texas schools. The big food-service concern said the investigation involves its Sysco Food Services Inc. subsidiary, which distributes food and related products to schools and other organizations in the Houston area. The company said the unit received a subpoena from a federal grand jury here about five weeks ago requesting certain records.

Sysco said it complied fully and opened an internal investigation as well. It said it also offered to compensate customers for any overcharges should the allegations prove true.

"Sysco has always prided itself on being a corporation whose entire family of companies is committed to ethical and lawful business practices," said John F. Woodhouse, chairman and chief executive officer.

Analysts and company officials said the amount of money involved is likely to be minimal. Sysco said that the unit accounts for just 3% of total company revenue and that the unit's public-schools business accounts for 7% of its sales volume.

The stock market appeared to be unconcerned about the probe. In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange, Sysco shares closed at $35, up $1.375.

It couldn't be learned which other food-service companies have been targeted by the probe.

An official with the U.S. attorney's office here confirmed the investigation but wouldn't elaborate.
