Metro Airlines to Post Fourth-Quarter Loss But Sees Profit Ahead

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/28/90

DALLAS -- Metro Airlines Inc. will post a fourth-quarter loss of $4 million to $4.5 million, or 70 cents a share, but is projecting a profitable first and second quarters of the current fiscal year, J.L. Seaborn, president and chief operating officer, said. The loss in the three months ended April 30 was less than half Metro's year-earlier fourth-quarter deficit of $9.2 million, or $1.54 a share, which was caused primarily by a strike and bankruptcy filing at Eastern Airlines. Metro operates feeder carriers affiliated with several airlines, including Eastern in Atlanta.

Metro expects to report fourth-quarter revenue of about $55 million, up from $34.4 million a year earlier, Mr. Seaborn said in an interview.

For the full fiscal year, revenue was about $195 million, up from $147.5 million. Full-year net losses were about $19 million, or $3.20 a share, he said, compared with a year-earlier loss of $10.7 million, or $1.83 a share, before an accounting change.

In the current quarter, operations are showing sharp improvement, Mr. Seaborn said. "Revenues are at an all-time high," he said, reflecting a turnaround in Atlanta and an improvement in Metro's Northeast operations. He didn't offer specific projections for revenue or net income for this quarter or next.

Mr. Seaborn said Metro showed a profit for May and June, the first two months of its current fiscal year, now that Eastern is operating 288 flights a day in the Atlanta market. This month, he said, Metro will carry about 80,000 passengers in and out of Atlanta, topping its previous one-month high there of 66,000 passengers, when Eastern was at full strength.

Although Atlanta operations represent only a quarter of Metro's flights, about 80% of those connect with Eastern. Mr. Seaborn said Eastern's troubles were a primary cause of Metro's heavy losses over the last fiscal year.

Traffic also began to pick up in the fourth quarter on Metro Air Northeast, and the division posted a profit in May, said Brian Miller, Metro's chief financial officer.

Metro Northeast feeds passengers into the Trans World Airlines Inc. network. The division, formerly Brockway Air Inc., was purchased by Metro in 1989. Last July, Metro changed the unit's name and switched its affiliation to TWA from Piedmont Airlines. The changes caused traffic to drop temporarily and contributed to Metro's losses during the just-concluded year.

In addition to its operations with Eastern in Atlanta, Metro operates as a feeder carrier for American Airlines in Dallas/ Fort Worth. Mr. Seaborn said Metro's there have been stable and profitable.
