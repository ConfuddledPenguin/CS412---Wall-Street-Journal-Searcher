Florida Federal Fined $2.2 Million by Court In Student-Loan Case

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/28/90

ST. PETERSBURG, Fla. -- Florida Federal Savings Bank said that a U.S. District Court judge in Tampa, Fla., ordered Florida Federal to pay $17 million of restitution and fined the bank $2.2 million for its conviction of student-loan fraud. In addition, the bank said that the Office of Thrift Supervision has asked it to enter a consent agreement.

Florida Federal was found guilty last month of falsifying student-loan documents between November 1986 and May 1987.

Judge Ben Krentzman also sentenced Robert O. Harmas, a former Florida Federal officer, to two years in prison in connection with the student-loan scandal. In addition, Judge Krentzman placed James J. LaMantia, another former officer, on probation for three years and ordered him to do 300 hours of community service.

"The court's recent decision defines a significant component of the potential student-loan loss and, thereby, helps clear the way for Florida Federal to be recapitalized," said the bank's president and chief executive officer, John W. Sapankski.

He added that "there are several investment groups currently performing `due diligence' on Florida Federal, and once the student loan uncertainty is quantified and settled, we can look for investor commitments to be made."

Mr. Sapanski said Florida Federal is also negotiating settlements of the student-loan matter with the civil division of the U.S. Justice Department and with the U.S. Education Department.

Mr. Sapanski said the Office of Thrift Supervision, which has overseen Florida Federal under a supervisory agreement for a year, has asked the bank to enter a more stringent consent agreement, because its regulatory capital has fallen below $15 million.
