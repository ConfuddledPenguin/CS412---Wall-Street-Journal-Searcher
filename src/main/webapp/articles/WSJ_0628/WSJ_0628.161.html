Rostenkowski Now Favors Bill for Enterprise Zones

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/28/90

WASHINGTON -- In a reversal of his position, House Ways and Means Committee Chairman Dan Rostenkowski plans to introduce a bill creating tax-free enterprise zones in impoverished areas. The Chicago Democrat plans to hold a press conference on the subject today, a senior aide to the chairman said. His change of heart greatly increases the chances that some sort of enterprise-zone bill will be part of any tax legislation enacted this year.

The Bush administration, particularly Housing Secretary Jack Kemp, has been pushing a proposal to establish as many as 50 enterprise zones in distressed urban and rural communities. Investments made in the zones would be given tax-favored treatment, costing the government an estimated $300 million annually by fiscal 1993. But the administration's proposal has been rebuffed in the past, partly because of a lack of support from Mr. Rostenkowski.

Mr. Rostenkowski's proposal would allow for fewer zones than the administration proposal, but tax staffers said it is designed to cost the same amount.

The aide to Mr. Rostenkowski said it was a coincidence that the Ways and Means chairman's change of heart came in the same week that President Bush reversed his position on tax increases. "The chairman has been looking at this for quite a while," the aide said.
