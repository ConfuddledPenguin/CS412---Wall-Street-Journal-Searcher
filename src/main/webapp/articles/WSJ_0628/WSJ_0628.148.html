Business Brief -- United Companies Financial Corp.: Concern Agrees to Acquire Most of Mortgage Servicer

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/28/90

United Companies Financial Corp. said it has signed a letter of intent with the Resolution Trust Corp. to acquire most of the assets of Foster Mortgage Corp., a mortgage-servicing company based in Fort Worth, Texas. United Companies, based in Baton Rouge, La., is a financial-services holding company. Foster is a unit of Benjamin Franklin Federal Savings Association, a Houston-based thrift that is under the conservatorship of the RTC, the federal agency that is selling off assets seized in the thrift-industry bailout.

Foster currently services about $8 billion in mortgage loans, and United Companies said the purchase price will be based on the mortgage-loan balances at closing. An RTC spokeswoman said the agency hopes to close the sale within 90 days.
