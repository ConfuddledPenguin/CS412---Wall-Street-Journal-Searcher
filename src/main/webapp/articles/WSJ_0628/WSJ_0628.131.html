Business Brief -- E-Systems Inc.: Electronics Firm Receives Contract for System Test

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/28/90

E-Systems, Inc., Dallas, said it has received a "multimillion-dollar" contract from a Mobil Corp. unit to test its computer-based mass storage system over a three-year period. The contract represents the first adaptation of the data storage system for a nongovernment group, said Philip J. Rasch, manager of business development for the project. E-Systems, an electronic systems and products company, hopes the contract with Mobil will give it a leg up on an estimated $2 billion market in the 1990s.

The mass storage system will store the oil company's exploration data on tape cartridges similar to those used by video cassette recorders. The recording system was adapted from one produced by Ampex Corp., Redwood City, Calif., for broadcast television. The cassettes are stored on shelves and retrieved by robot for playback on a recorder; the system is operated by a minisupercomputer produced by Convex Computer Corp., Richardson, Texas.

Ampex has the rights to market its portion of the system, primarily the recording device, for non-automated uses.
