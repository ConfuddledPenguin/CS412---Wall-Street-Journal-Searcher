GM, Chrysler Venture Signs Pact With Firm

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/28/90

DETROIT -- The manufacturing joint venture between General Motors Corp. and Chrysler Corp. signed a four-year contract to supply Rover Group PLC of Britain with transmissions. Officials of New Venture Gear, the joint venture that builds transmissions and parts, said the contract represents the first work the venture received from outside the two auto makers. The companies announced their intention to form a venture last October.

New Venture Gear officials declined to specify the amount of transmissions or the value of the contract.

The contract calls for New Venture Gear to supply five-speed, front-wheel drive transmissions for use on the Rover 825 Turbo Diesel vehicles being introduced next month in Europe. The transmission is being manufactured at New Venture Gear's facility in East Syracuse, N.Y.
