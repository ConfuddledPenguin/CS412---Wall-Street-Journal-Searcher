Menard Enterprises, A Big Auto Dealer, Files Chapter 11 Plea

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 06/28/90

Menard Enterprises Inc., Rhode Island's second-largest auto dealership has filed for protection under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code, underscoring the tough times for new car dealers caught in a sales slump. In reaction to the slump, some auto makers, concerned about the potential loss of sales outlets, are stepping up efforts to keep struggling dealers in business.

Menard, which has dealerships in three Rhode Island cities, is the latest in a string of big car dealer chains to petition a federal bankruptcy court for protection from creditors and for time to develop a debt-repayment plan. Menard's seven auto franchises listed an unspecified amount of assets and liabilities.

"It's a tough business right now," said Aldo Sammartino, president of a Rhode Island dealer group. Last year, about 25% of new car dealers lost money and about 300 of the 24,900 dealers in the U.S. went out of business, he said. The National Automobile Dealers Association expects just as many failures this year.

General Motors Corp., meanwhile, says 35%-40% of its dealers currently aren't profitable. Normally, only about 15%-20% of GM dealers are unprofitable at this time of year.

In an effort to shore up its dealer network, GM says it's recently increased the assistance it gives to dealers to finance the vehicles they stock. GM also announced it will offer to repurchase the vehicles it sells to car-rental companies, a move that will keep its dealers from having to compete with cheaper used cars. Ford Motor Co. has followed with a similar promise to its dealers.

Dealerships like Menard, which sell many inexpensive models, are among the hardest hit, experts say. These economy-car dealers are pinched by the twin problems of finding customers qualified for loans, and reaping thinner profits on the vehicles they sell.
