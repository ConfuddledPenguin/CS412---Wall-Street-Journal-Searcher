Who's News: Charter Medical Names Developer To Hospital Post

  • Author: Martha Brannigan
  • Date: 06/28/90

Charter Medical Corp., the troubled psychiatric and general-hospital chain, named Michael Z. Kay, a hotel developer, to head its hospital operations. Separately, Charter conceded that it had overcharged Medicare by $900,000. The company said earlier this week that federal regulators were investigating Charter concerning possible fraud in its Medicare and Medicaid billings.

The hiring of Mr. Kay, 50 years old, underscores Charter's strategy of marketing its way to higher occupancy rates. Mr. Kay fills a vacancy created in March when James D. Daher was fired by Charter's chairman and president, William A. Fickling, in the midst of a special audit of widespread accounting irregularities throughout the hospital chain. Mr. Daher maintained he was unfairly blamed for the problems, although no reason was given for his dismissal.

Mr. Kay is currently president and chief operating officer of the Portman Hotel Co. in Atlanta, a unit of closely held Portman Cos.

As executive vice president of hospital operations, Mr. Kay will be one of a triumvirate of key officers working under Mr. Fickling, Charter's founder who led a $1.6 billion leveraged buyout of the company in 1988.

Mr. Kay hasn't any background in health care. But both he and Charter pointed to common ground between the hotel and hospital businesses, particularly in the area of marketing.

"We're really excited because of his experience in the operation of hotels and his tremendous marketing skills," said J. Rodney Laughlin, executive vice president of human resources and development.

Charter and Mr. Kay said the executive will draw on his skills of selling the company's services to groups as the industry shifts from obtaining most of its patients from individuals and doctor referral to obtaining them through contracts with employers and other groups.

"Increasingly, hospitals get their business from group sources such as insurance companies, managed-care companies and from the government and military," said Mr. Kay, who assumes his post July 1.

Mr. Kay was well known to Mr. Fickling because of advisory services that Portman Hotel Co. has provided to hotels Mr. Fickling's family controls, Mr. Kay said. Mr. Kay has also served in the past few months as a marketing consultant to Charter.

Mr. Laughlin said Charter is banking on Mr. Kay's skills in implementing strategic plans "to help us respond to the changing industry over the next three or four years."

Charter, which until recently enjoyed almost unbridled growth, faces serious problems in servicing its debt as the economic environment for psychiatric hospitals tightens and insurers and employers push for shorter hospital stays and other ways to reduce costs. For instance, while hospital admissions rose 20%, patient days rose only 8% and expenses soared 26% in the quarter ended March 31.

In addressing the Medicare probe, Charter said it discovered certain hospital cost reports filed with Medicare were incorrect and resulted in a request for "about $900,000" in excessive reimbursement. It said that it reported the matter to its Medicare agents and corrected its filings accordingly.

The inspector general for investigations for the Health Care Financing Administration in Atlanta said this week that the agency is looking into allegations of possible Medicare and Medicaid fraud related to reimbursements to Charter.

Charter also acknowledged that the Labor Department is "continuing its review" of Charter's Employee Stock Ownership Plan. The investigation was disclosed in an SEC filing earlier this year but gained little publicity. Charter said it believes the Labor Department investigation probe is "not unusual" for ESOPs involved in leveraged buyouts and said it doesn't believe that any improprieties exist in the ESOP.
