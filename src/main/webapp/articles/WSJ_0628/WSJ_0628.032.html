Hunt Manufacturing Reports 48% Decline In 2nd-Quarter Profit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/28/90

PHILADELPHIA -- Hunt Manufacturing Co. said second-quarter net income plunged 48% from a year ago, reflecting a general softness in sales and special problems with a recent acquisition. Second-quarter net fell to $2.5 million, or 15 cents a share, from $4.8 million, or 30 cents, a year earlier. Sales in the latest quarter rose 5.6% to about $50.7 million from $48 million a year earlier.

Hunt, a maker and distributor of office products and art and craft products, blamed most of the decline on a decrease of about 10% in sales of several major product lines and on major losses incurred by the Data Products business, acquired last June.

In addition, a company official cited some start-up problems in Hunt's new operation in Mexico that have hampered performance.

Trading of Hunt shares was delayed yesterday morning while the company disseminated the earnings news. Hunt shares closed at $13.375 in New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, down $2.

For the latest six months, Hunt reported that net income dropped 26% to $6.4 million, or 40 cents a share, from $8.7 million, or 54 cents, despite a 5.5% increase in revenue to $100.1 million.

Hunt's chairman and chief executive officer, Ronald J. Naples, said the magnitude of the earnings drop has "all but dashed our previous expectation of another record earnings year," unless the company can manage to make "a remarkable recovery."

However, Mr. Naples expressed confidence that sales would rebound in the second half as more customers replenish inventories.
