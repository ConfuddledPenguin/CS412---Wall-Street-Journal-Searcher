Hovnanian Expects Earnings to Decline For This Fiscal Year

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/28/90

RED BANK, N.J. -- Hovnanian Enterprises Inc., a developer of moderately priced suburban homes, said it expects sharply lower earnings for its year ending Feb. 28, 1991, as the sluggish regional economy continues to hurt sales. The company said it expects earnings of $5 million to $7 million, or 25 cents to 35 cents a share, on revenue of $290 million to $325 million. In fiscal 1990, net was $21.8 million, or $1.05 a share, on revenue of $410 million.

The fiscal 1990 earnings were 24% below fiscal 1989's. While projecting a second year of decline, the company stressed its belief "that fundamental demand for competitively priced homes in the best locations remains strong." In the past, the company has had up to 75% of its volume in townhouses and single-family homes priced between $70,000 and $130,000, which sold quickly, but such homes have become harder to develop and harder to sell.

"In the past years we had that success. People were camping out, waiting on long lines -- we had to hold lotteries," said John J. Schimpf, executive vice president. But with layoffs and employment uncertainties in the New York metropolitan area and a great supply of unsold houses in its important central New Jersey market, buyers have become very picky.

"There's been healthy skepticism about housing prices in the metropolitan area. A lot of the pent-up demand for housing has been run out of the marketplace," Mr. Schimpf said. Instead of cutting prices, however, Hovnanian is offering buyers appliance packages and paying closing costs to aid sales. Such incentive costs, along with declining sales volumes, are depressing earnings, Mr. Schimpf said.
