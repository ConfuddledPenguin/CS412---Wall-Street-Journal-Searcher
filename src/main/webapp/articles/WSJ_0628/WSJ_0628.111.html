International: Allianz AG Buys East Germany's Dominant Insurer

  • Author: Terence Roth
  • Date: 06/28/90

WEST BERLIN -- Allianz AG of West Germany, outflanking a last-minute rival bid by domestic competitors, said it signed final contracts giving it a 51% stake in East Germany's state insurance monopoly for 270.7 million marks ($163.1 million) in cash. Announcing the pact yesterday, the day after it was signed, Allianz said the transaction "provides the conditions for the venture to begin business operations" in East Germany on Sunday -- the day the two Germanys are to link their monetary and social institutions to form one German economy.

East Berlin's public trust for East German industry, or Treuhandanstalt, will retain a 49% interest in the new venture, called Deutsche Versicherungs-AG. But Allianz said its controlling interest gives it the right of first refusal should Treuhandanstalt ever decide to sell.

The controversial transaction gives Europe's largest insurance group a dominating position in the East German market. In disclosing its intention of taking even a lesser 49% stake in Deutsche Versicherungs in March, Allianz triggered howls of protest from other West German insurance groups, angered over the prospect of Allianz achieving a quick monopoly in East Germany before they could build their own East German operations.

West German antitrust authorities said the case affected only the East German market, and thus was outside their jurisdiction.

On Tuesday, the day Allianz signed contracts with the East German government, a group of five other West German insurers led by Colonia Versicherungs in Cologne announced a counteroffer for the East German insurance network. Promising to pay the same price as Allianz, the group vowed to break up the former monopoly into regional insurance groups.

But, said an Allianz official yesterday: "Their offer has been overtaken by events."

Allianz's successful sweep into East Germany leaves other West German insurers infuriated at having to start from scratch. Colonia spokesman Norbert Kampf said that even some East German officials had warned the Allianz takeover gives the Munich-based group a dominating share in a market of 16 million inhabitants.

"Allianz's position will be reduced in time, as other insurance companies set up their own operations. But even a 25% or 30% market share is very high in the insurance business," Mr. Kampf contended.

Allianz sought to defuse criticism by citing the approval of East German authorities, who also promised to throw open the market to other insurance companies after Germany's economic merger on Sunday.
