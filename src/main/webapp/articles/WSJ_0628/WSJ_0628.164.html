Union Camp to Form Employee Stock Plan With Preferred Issue

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/28/90

WAYNE, N.J. -- Union Camp Corp. said it will form an employee stock ownership plan with $160 million in new convertible preferred stock. The manufacturer of paper, packaging and wood-based chemicals, said the plan will cover salaried employees and be part of their existing savings and investment plan. After the issuance of the preferred stock, Union Camp plans to buy back as many as 4.5 million shares of its common stock in periodic open-market transactions. Union Camp has 68.9 million common shares outstanding.

The ESOP and terms of the convertible preferred stock will be completed within a month, the company said.

Some companies have issued convertible preferred stock to ESOPs in order to put stakes in friendly hands, a maneuver that helps thwart takeover attempts. But analysts said Union Camp's explanation for the move -- simply to "further align the interests of employees with those of our shareholders" -- was the more likely motive.

With the overall decline in takeover activity, the use of ESOPs as anti-takeover devices "is pretty much dead," says Lawrence Ross, an analyst at PaineWebber Inc.

Separately, Union Camp said it has developed a new paper-bleaching process that greatly reduces the amount of chlorinated organic waste, including dioxin and chloroform.

The company said it plans to install the new technology as part of a $158 million modernization of its Franklin, Va., pulp and fine-paper plant. The process, for which Union Camp said it is seeking a patent, will help it comply with new Virginia water-quality standards, the company said. Installation is expected to be completed in the second half of 1992.
