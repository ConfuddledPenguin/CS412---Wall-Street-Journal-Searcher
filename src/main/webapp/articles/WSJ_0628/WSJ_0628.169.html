Canada Shelves Legislation For Hibernia Development

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/28/90

OTTAWA - The Canadian government temporarily shelved its Hibernia oil project spending bill because of growing anti-Newfoundland sentiment in the wake of Canada's failed constitutional talks. Canadian Trade Minister John Crosbie said the Hibernia legislation has been postponed until the fall because a vote now could have killed the bill.

The proposed bill would authorize the government to contribute 2.7 billion Canadian dollars (US$2.3 billion) in grants and loan guarantees to develop the Hibernia oil field offshore Newfoundland.

The government had planned to hold a vote on the spending bill this week before it recesses for the summer holidays. But it postponed the bill after several members of the Quebec Parliament threatened to reject it in retaliation for Newfoundland's failure last week to ratify the Meech Lake accord.

The Meech Lake Accord, which was designed to meet Quebec's minimum constitutional demands and diffuse separatist sentiment in the largely French-speaking province, died when the Newfoundland and Manitoba legislatures adjourned without ratifying it.
