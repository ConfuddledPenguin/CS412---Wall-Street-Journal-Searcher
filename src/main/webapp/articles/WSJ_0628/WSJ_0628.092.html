Horsburgh, Pioneer of Country Funds, To Help Barclays Unit on U.S. Start-up

  • Author: Michael Siconolfi
  • Date: 06/28/90

NEW YORK -- A pioneer of the country-fund craze in the U.S. has found a new home. Michael Horsburgh, who co-headed Bear Stearns Cos.' international equity department for nearly 10 years before leaving last year, joined Barclays de Zoete Wedd Investment Management to launch and head its U.S. investment-fund business.

As a senior managing director at Bear Stearns, Mr. Horsburgh, 45 years old, was principally known for the development of several funds that invested in securities of a single country. His work helped open the field of country funds to U.S. investors, leading to a frenzy among Wall Street securities firms to launch investments in such exotic markets as Malaysia and Turkey.

Unlike early country funds that had focused on markets in countries that had been closed to U.S. investors, Mr. Horsburgh developed funds -- such as First Australia Fund and Scandinavia Fund -- that invested in nations already open for investors. He also helped launch First Hungary Fund.

Mr. Horsburgh resigned from Bear Stearns last fall after 13 years at the big securities firm. But Bear Stearns insiders say he was forced out after disagreements with top Bear Stearns executives over the firm's international investment strategy.

For his part, Mr. Horsburgh said he left Bear Stearns "completely friendly," adding that he felt it was "time for a change."

Two Bear Stearns executives with global stock experience, James Sheehan and Dennis Baum, succeeded Mr. Horsburgh in heading the firm's international equity area.

At Barclays de Zoete Wedd Investment Management, a unit of the investment banking and securities arm of British bank Barclays PLC, Mr. Horsburgh will serve as managing director and concentrate on launching an investment-fund business in the U.S., largely for institutions. The unit currently manages $35 billion for institutional investors in Europe, Japan, Hong Kong, Australia and Canada.

In an interview, Mr. Horsburgh conceded that the competition will be stiff, but said there's room for growth. "The use of international investments has certainly grown, but it's still significantly lower for the average (U.S.) institution than the typical institution in Europe," he said.

The move by Barclays is part of a broad strategy at increasing its U.S. presence. Earlier this year, the firm hired several former Drexel senior stock analysts and support staff to beef up its international securities business in the U.S. The hires included economist Richard Hoey, investment strategist Abby Joseph Cohen and financial-services analyst John Keefe.

Before joining Bear Stearns, Mr. Horsburgh was a partner at Hoare, Govett Ltd., a London securities firm. He joined Bear Stearns in 1976 as an institutional equity salesman and was appointed co-head of the international equity department in 1980.
