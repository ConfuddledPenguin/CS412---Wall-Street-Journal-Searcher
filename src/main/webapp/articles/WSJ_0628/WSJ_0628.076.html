New Securities Issues

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/28/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATE

Republic New York Corp. -- $100 million of 9 1/2% subordinated notes due July 1, 2000, priced at 99.68 to yield 9.55%. The 10-year notes were priced at a spread of 105 basis points above the Treasury's 10-year note. Rated single-A-1 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and double-A-minus by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Goldman, Sachs & Co.

MUNICIPAL

Maryland -- $95 million of general obligation bonds due July 15, 1993-2005, via a Marine Midland Capital Markets group. The bonds, rated triple-A, were priced for reoffering to yield from 6.10% in 1993 to 6.90% in 2005. There are no term bonds.

Houston -- $83 million of 6.50% 12-month tax and revenue anticipation notes due June 27, 1991, via a Lehman Brothers Inc. group. The notes, rated MIG-1/SP1-plus, were priced for reoffering to yield 5.95%.

Ohio -- $75 million of highway general obligation bonds due Nov. 15, 1991-1997, via a First Chicago Capital Markets group. The bonds, rated double-A/triple-A, were priced for reoffering to yield from 6% in 1992 to 6.40% in 1997. Bonds due in 1991 are not formally reoffered. There are no term bonds.

MORTGAGES

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $200 million of Remic mortgage securities offered in 14 classes by Kidder, Peabody & Co. The offering, Series 1990-90, is backed by Fannie Mae 8 1/2% securities. Price and yield information wasn't immediately available.

EUROBONDS

Cardiff Automobile Receivables Securities U.K. PLC (special purpose vehicle) -- #328 million of five-year auto loan-backed bonds via Goldman Sachs International Ltd. Mature on last business day in June 1995. Par issue price, fixed reoffering price of 99.93. Coupon will float at 1/4

European Investment Bank (agency) -- one billion French francs of 9 7/8% fungible Eurobonds due Feb. 9, 2000, priced at 100.20 via Credit Lyonnais. Fungible with the existing one billion franc issue. Fees 1 7/8.

Asahi Breweries Ltd. -- 30 billion yen of floating-rate Eurobonds due July 18, 1996, priced at 100.20, via Nomura International Ltd. Coupon will float at 0.45 point below Japan's long-term prime rate. Two short first coupons, on Oct. 18 and Jan 18. The bonds are puttable at par on July 18, 1995. Fees 0.40.

Government Insurance Office of New South Wales -- 50 million Australian dollars of 15 1/2% Eurobonds due Aug. 2, 1993, priced at 101.95 to yield 15.3% less fees via Westpac Banking Corp. Fees 1 1/2.
