Ex-Mayor of Providence To Run for Office Again

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/28/90

PROVIDENCE, R.I. -- Buddy is running again. In the middle of his popular afternoon radio talk show, former mayor Vincent A. (Buddy) Cianci Jr. announced that he will run for mayor as an independent.

Mr. Cianci was the subject of a front-page article in The Wall Street Journal last week. He served three terms as mayor of Providence (two as a Republican and one as an independent) from 1975 to 1984 before being forced to resign after pleading no contest to two assault charges for beating up the lover of his estranged wife.

Mr. Cianci, in an emotional speech on his show, told listeners, "Experience is a great teacher, but often a painful one."

Because of his candidacy, Mr. Cianci is leaving his jobs as a radio talk-show host and a TV political commentator. Indeed, he went off the air yesterday after making his announcement about 4 p.m., two hours before the radio program was scheduled to end.

He decided to run even though one poll conducted recently showed that 44% of the city's voters would be embarrassed by his election. Mr. Cianci is still believed to be a strong candidate, however, because he has a core of staunch supporters and the incumbent isn't running for re-election.
