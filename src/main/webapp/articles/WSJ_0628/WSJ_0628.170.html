NRC to Change Guidelines On Unregulated Materials

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/28/90

WASHINGTON -- The Nuclear Regulatory Commission announced guidelines that will increase the amount of mildly radioactive waste and other material that is exempt from NRC regulation. This "below regulatory concern" approach is designed to give the commissioners a uniform framework for granting blanket exemptions covering such activities as the decontamination of nuclear power plants, and such materials as consumer products and low-level radioactive waste.

In the past, the commission has granted exemptions on a case-by-case basis to a few products. The NRC contends that allowing for blanket exemptions will enable it to focus its resources on more-serious risks.

Consumer and environmental groups attacked the change, saying it will result in excessive radiation exposure to individuals and increase cancer deaths.

Under the new policy, a material or practice can be considered for exemption if it will result in an average radiation dose to an individual of less than 10 millirems a year. By comparison, a single round-trip cross-country airline trip exposes an individual to about five millirems of radiation, the NRC said.

For consumer products or recycled materials, the dose couldn't be above one millirem a year.
