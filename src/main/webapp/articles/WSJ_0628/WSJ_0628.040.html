Technology: Global Session Sets Most Details for Fund For Protecting Ozone

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/28/90

LONDON -- Delegates to an international government conference agreed on most details for creation of a $160 million global fund to protect the world's ozone layer. The fund, to be managed by the World Bank and two United Nations agencies, will help developing nations start converting their industries to ozone-friendly technologies. As the first large fund targeted at a specific global environmental ill, the fund may become a model for how the world will finance such other problems as the threat of global warming and the loss of species.

"We are establishing a new political mechanism to deal with the environmental crisis," said Mostafa Tolba, executive director of the United Nations Environment Program.

The fund will pay the first three years of developing countries' efforts to phase out use of chlorofluorocarbons, or CFCs, halons and carbon tetrachloride -- all man-made chemicals, used in refrigerators, air conditioners, spray cans and factories -- which eat away the atmosphere's protective layer of ozone gas. The developed nations, which represent 85% of world consumption of these chemicals, are expected to agree here tomorrow to ban usage by either 1997 or 2000. The world's developing nations will have an extra decade to ban the substances, but say they will still need financial help from the fund to adopt ozone-friendly substitutes.
