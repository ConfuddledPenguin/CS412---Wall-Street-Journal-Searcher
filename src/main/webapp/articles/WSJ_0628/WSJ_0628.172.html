BCE Inc. to Sell Most Of 48.9% Encor Stake To Dealers for Resale

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/28/90

MONTREAL -- BCE Inc., a telecommunications, manufacturing, financial services and resources concern, said it agreed to sell most of its 48.9% stake in Encor Inc., a Calgary, Alberta, oil and gas company, to a group of investment dealers for subsequent resale. After the stock market closed yesterday, BCE said it will sell 14,850,600 units for seven Canadian dollars (US$5.97) each to the dealers. Each unit consists of three Encor common shares and two warrants. Each warrant entitles the holder to purchase from BCE one Encor common share for C$2.65 until Jan. 21, 1992.

BCE said it holds 74,253,930 Encor common shares. Upon the sale of the units, its holding in Encor would be reduced to 29,702,130 shares, or about 19.6%, prior to the exercise of the warrants. If all the warrants were exercised, BCE said, its stake would be reduced to less than 1%.

BCE said it continues to hold 29,850,000 Encor redeemable convertible preferred shares. These shares are convertible into 74,618,433 Encor common shares, or about 33% of the total on a fully diluted basis.

BCE said the sale of the units is expected to close on or about July 20. It said proceeds will be used principally to reduce short-term debt. BCE said the transaction won't have an immediate effect on earnings.

Richardson Greenshields of Canada Ltd. and RBC Dominion Securities Inc., both based in Toronto, are co-managers of the dealer group, BCE said.
