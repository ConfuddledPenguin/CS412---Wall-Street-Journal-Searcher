Business Brief -- Rogers Corp.: Loss Seen for 2nd Quarter, Reflecting $7 Million Charge

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/28/90

Rogers Corp. said it will take a second-quarter pretax charge of about $7 million and will post a loss for the quarter and the full year. The Rogers, Conn.-based maker of electronic parts said it is taking several steps to reduce costs and improve profit margins, including selected divestitures and a "significant" reduction in salaried payroll through terminations and early-retirement offers.

In last year's second quarter, Rogers had net income of $386,000, or 13 cents a share, on sales of $43 million. For the year, the company had net of $1.6 million, or 53 cents a share, on sales of $175 million.

Norman L. Greenman, president of Rogers, said that "savings from the actions taken are projected to recover the entire amount of the one-time charge within two years."
