Japanese Retail Stores' Sales

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/28/90

TOKYO -- Japan's major retail stores reported sales of 1.63 trillion yen ($10.54 billion) in May, up 10.4% from a weak year-earlier level, but supported by continued strong consumer demand for a broad range of items, the Ministry of International Trade and Industry said. The high-paced year-to-year growth in May represents a recovery from low levels a year earlier that owed to the damping effect on sales of a 3% consumption tax introduced in April 1989, an MITI official said, estimating that about 4% of May's sales increase was accounted for by the depressed year-ago level.
