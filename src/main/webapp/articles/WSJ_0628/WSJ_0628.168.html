International Brief -- Lloyd's of London: Insurer Posts 22% Decline In Pretax Profit for 1987

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/28/90

Lloyd's of London posted a 22% decline in pretax profit for 1987, and executives said the giant insurer faces leaner times ahead. The decline, to pretax earnings of #509.2 million ($887 million) from a record #650 million in 1986, reflected tighter underwriting conditions, higher expenses and huge catastrophe losses in 1987. In that year, a series of storms -- including a hurricane that swept Britain and Western Europe in October -- led to higher claims.

As a result, underwriting profit for the year fell 45%, to #411.7 million, from #744.5 million. In particular, Lloyd's said underwriting profit in its nonmarine lines was "disappointing" at #8.7 million, a drop from #218.3 million a year earlier.

Lloyd's reports its results three years late to account for the filing of claims and litigation. Profit from underwriting and investment operations represents the aggregate results of 401 syndicates back by 28,770 investors.

Murray Lawrence, chairman of the 302-year-old insurer, said Lloyd's continuing exposure to U.S. asbestos, pollution and other so-called "long-tailed" liability policies makes the 1988 and 1989 accounts look even "less satisfactory" than 1987. Profit in 1988 may be lower than 1987, in part due to such disasters as the explosion aboard the Occidental Petroleum North Sea oil platform.
