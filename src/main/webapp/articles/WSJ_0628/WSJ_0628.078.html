Who's News: Laidlaw Founder To Quit Top Posts By End of Year

  • Author: Suzanne McGee
  • Date: 06/28/90

TORONTO -- Michael DeGroote, founder and chairman of Laidlaw Inc., said he plans to step down as chief executive officer Aug. 1 and to resign as chairman at year end to devote more time to personal matters. The announcement surprised analysts and investors. More than six million shares changed hands in trading on the Toronto Stock Exchange and in over-the-counter trading in New York. Laidlaw's Class B shares closed at $21.75, down 37.5 cents in hectic over-the-counter trading.

"This is the first change in succession we've seen at Laidlaw," said Steve Garmaise, vice-president of Toronto-based First Marathon Securities Ltd.

Mr. DeGroote sold his 47.2% voting stake and effective control of Laidlaw to Montreal-based Canadian Pacific Ltd. in 1988. That move led many industry analysts to assume he would step down, at least as chief executive, within the next five years.

"But it wasn't expected that Mr. DeGroote would get out this soon or this completely," Mr. Garmaise said. "People thought he would still play an active role in determining strategy and ferreting out acquisitions. So the market is kind of spooked."

Mr. Garmaise and some other analysts said they had expected Laidlaw's stock to rise by as much as $1 yesterday following an announcement that the company's net income for the nine months ended May 31 rose nearly 40% to $194.8 million, or 81 cents a share, from $139.6 million, or 68 cents a share, a year earlier.

"So while the market reaction may seem mild, this news has not only kept the stock from rising, but has actually pushed it down," Mr. Garmaise said.

Laidlaw didn't say who would succeed Mr. DeGroote as chairman and chief executive, but the company said Donald Jackson, recently appointed president of the transportation and waste management concern, will take over responsibility for day-to-day operations.

Ivan Cairns, vice president and general counsel, said the company's board was advised of Mr. DeGroote's resignation late Tuesday.

"Mr. Jackson will be running the company, whether he is eventually appointed chief executive or not," Mr. Cairns said, adding that the company "has no intent of looking outside" for someone to fill the chief executive position.

Mr. Cairns said he expects directors will "take their time" before announcing a chairman.

Mr. Jackson joined Laidlaw in August 1989 as executive vice president. He was appointed president in November.

"This move clearly indicates that Laidlaw is moving to further de-emphasize transport and stress the waste management side of the business," said First Marathon's Mr. Garmaise.

Mr. Cairns said Mr. Jackson worked for Calgary-based Trimac Ltd. for several years and served as chairman of Trimac's chemical waste disposal unit, Tricil Ltd., which Laidlaw acquired in November.

Neither Mr. DeGroote nor Mr. Jackson could be reached for comment.
