Rodman & Renshaw Plans Europe Venture With Viennese Bank

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/28/90

CHICAGO -- Rodman & Renshaw Inc. said it signed a letter of intent to form a joint venture with Creditanstalt-Bankverein, the Viennese commercial and investment bank, to provide futures brokerage services to central and Eastern European investors. The Chicago futures and brokerage firm, a subsidiary of Rodman & Renshaw Capital Group Inc., said the joint venture, CA Global Futures AG, would be 70%-owned by the bank and 30%-owned by Rodman. In a related transaction, the bank will invest $5 million in the brokerage firm in return for 4.99% of Rodman & Renshaw Capital Group's stock and some subordinated debentures.

Both transactions are subject to the signing of a definitive agreement and regulatory approval. The transactions are expected to be completed by Sept. 30.

Kurt Karmin, Rodman & Renshaw Inc.'s chairman and chief executive officer, said the $5 million, which isn't required to fund the firm's existing or planned operations, will be used to retire existing debt.

Jerome de Bontin, a managing director of Rodman & Renshaw Inc.'s financial futures department, will be co-managing director of the joint venture, based in Vienna. The bank will choose the other co-managing director.
