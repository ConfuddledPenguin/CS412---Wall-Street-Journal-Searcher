International: EC Tells British Aerospace To Repay Rover Subsidies

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/28/90

BRUSSELS -- The European Community Commission ordered British Aerospace PLC to repay #44.4 million ($77.2 million) in illegal state subsidies granted when the company bought the state-owned car maker Rover Group PLC in 1988. The decision, announced by Sir Leon Brittan, EC competition commissioner, follows the disclosure last year by national auditors that the British government offered British Aerospace "sweeteners," or hidden subsidies at the time of the sale. These were to ensure British Aerospace went ahead with its purchase of Rover after EC competition authorities slashed a government aid package by #331 million to #469 million.

The ruling is expected to fuel the political controversy in Britain over the Rover affair, which has proved an embarrassment to the government of Margaret Thatcher. Critics accuse the British government of misleading Parliament as well as EC authorities in offering the hidden subsidies to British Aerospace.

In London, the British government didn't immediately comment on the commission's announcement, which is to be the subject of a statement today in the House of Commons by Trade and Industry Secretary Nicholas Ridley.

British Aerospace also said it wouldn't comment on the commission's decision before the government's statement to Parliament.
