CONSOLIDATED FREIGHTWAYS Inc. (Menlo Park, Calif.) -- Moody's Investors Service Inc. said it lowered its rating on senior debt of this transportation company. The rating concern said its action affects about $275 million in long-term debt. Separately, Standard & Poor's Corp. said it put on its CreditWatch list with negative implications $425 million of Consolidated Freightways' debt. Moody's lowered its ratings on the company's senior debt to Baa-3 from single-A-3 and on commercial paper to prime-3 from prime-2, citing reduced financial flexibility, a difficult operating environment, and diminished bondholder protection in light of a proposed senior bank credit agreement. Continuing losses at Consolidated Freightways' Emery Worldwide unit also were a factor in the rating change, Moody's said. S&P placed on Creditwatch the company's senior debt and industrial revenue bonds, rated triple-B, and commercial paper, rated single-A-2. S&P also cited Emery's losses and the new bank credit facility, as well as continuing poor prospects for a profit turnaround. The diversified company has operations in trucking and air freight. ---

COOPER COS. (New York) -- Standard & Poor's Corp. lowered its rating on $79 million of subordinated debt to triple-C-plus from single-B and removed Cooper from its CreditWatch list. S&P said the downgrade reflects the "uncertain nature" of planned acquisitions. It said the company has reduced debt and created a $180 million pool of liquid investments by divesting itself of most operating assets. The ratings agency said the remaining businesses, a small contact lens unit and a cryosurgical equipment unit, could provide a base for re-entry into the eye-care market.

NEW HAVEN, Conn. -- Moody's Investors Service Inc. said it downgraded its rating on this city's $76.9 million of general obligation bonds to Baa-1 from single-A. The rating concern attributed its action to the "rapid weakening of the city's financial position since fiscal year 1988," which caused "a significant projected fiscal year-end 1990 general fund deficit." Moody's also cited "the slowing regional economy, large capital needs and below-average socioeconomic base."
