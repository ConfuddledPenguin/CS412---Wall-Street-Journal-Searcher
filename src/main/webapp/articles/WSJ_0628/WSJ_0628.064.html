Dividend News: Topps Co.

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/28/90

TOPPS Co. declared a 3-for-2 stock split, payable Aug. 1 to stock of record July 9. The board also boosted the quarterly; it will pay five cents a post-split share on the same pay and record dates. The Brooklyn, N.Y., maker of baseball and other collector cards as well as Bazooka bubble gum has been paying six cents a share. Topps will have about 47.3 million shares outstanding after the split. John Perillo, vice president, controller and chief financial officer, said that "keeping the stock price low gives a lot more people an opportunity to buy the shares." He noted that the company recently reported strong first-quarter earnings.In national over-the-counter trading yesterday, Topps shares rose 25 cents to $23.25.
