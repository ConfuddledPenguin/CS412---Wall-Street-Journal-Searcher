Business Brief -- Salant Corp.: Company and Denton Unit Seek Bankruptcy Protection

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/28/90

Salant Corp., New York, and its Denton Mills Inc. subsidiary said they filed for protection from creditors under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code. Ray Williams, chairman and chief executive of the apparel company, said continued softness in the retail apparel market "has made it impossible for us to satisfy our obligations to our lenders on a timely basis."

Salant said it has arranged with CIT Group/Factoring for as much as $100 million of secured financing for working capital, subject to court approval.

Mr. Williams said the company "strongly believes" it will be able to operate effectively while negotiating with creditors. He said "production will not be restricted and shipments to our customers will not be delayed."

This is the second time in recent years that Salant has been in bankruptcy court proceedings. The company earlier this month defaulted on a $10.8 million payment of bank debt and warned that it might have to file a Chapter 11 petition.

Salant has $75 million of subordinated notes outstanding as well as other debt.
