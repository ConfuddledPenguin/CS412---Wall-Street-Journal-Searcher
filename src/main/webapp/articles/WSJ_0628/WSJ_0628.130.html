L.A. Gear Profit Declined by 36% In Its 2nd Period

  • Author: David J. Jefferson
  • Date: 06/28/90

MARINA DEL REY, Calif. -- L.A. Gear Inc. posted a 36% drop in net income for the second quarter ended May 31 to $10.2 million, or 51 cents a share, in line with estimates it made earlier this month. The drop from $16.1 million, or 91 cents a share, a year earlier, represents the first decline in earnings for the high-flying sneaker maker in three years. Sales, however, were a record $225.4 million, up 33% from $169.6 million a year earlier. Year-earlier per-share earnings were restated for a 2-for-1 stock split in September.

L.A. Gear shares climbed $1.125 to close at $27.25 in New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, after a skittish Wall Street sent the stock price tumbling $1.375 a share Tuesday on anticipation of the earnings release.

The company blamed the earnings drop on lower gross profit margins, which were around 35%. L.A. Gear has been selling more of its lower-priced, less-profitable shoes in an attempt to gain market share. Earnings were also affected by increased sales of L.A. Gear apparel and international footwear sales, both of which have lower profit margins than domestic footwear sales.

Company executives told shareholders at the annual meeting here that profit margins should be better in the next two quarters, as the company launches new and potentially more profitable lines. The executives declined to be more specific about earnings, however, saying the company doesn't make projections.

The new footwear will include a highly touted line promoted by singer Michael Jackson available for the back-to-school season, and an expanded line of hiker-style shoes. Robert Greenberg, chairman and chief executive officer, also told shareholders that the company will introduce an "inflatable Regulator" sneaker in the third quarter to compete with Reebok International Ltd.'s successful Pump basketball shoes, and a bouncy-soled "catapult" shoe to compete with Nike Inc.'s Air shoes.

Incoming orders for the second quarter were about $400 million, up more than 50% from a year earlier, L.A. Gear said. That, coupled with the "expected strong retail demand for our new higher margin styles . . . should contribute to renewed earnings increases in subsequent periods," Mr. Greenberg said.

The company said earlier this month that with the drop in second-quarter earnings, net income for the full year should be "approximately equal to the low end" of analysts' estimates, which at the time ranged from $4 to $4.50 a share, compared with $3.01 a share for fiscal 1989.

In the first six months, net rose 13% to $24.1 million, or $1.20 a share, from $21.4 million, or $1.23 a share adjusted for the stock split, while sales jumped 75% to $412.7 million from $235.7 million.

L.A. Gear also said it will increase its annual advertising and promotions budget about 8%. The company had planned to spend more than $50 million this year, up from $36.3 million the prior year.
