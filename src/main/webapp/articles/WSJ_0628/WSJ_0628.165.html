Boston Fed Chief Criticizes State Sales Tax Proposal

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/28/90

BOSTON -- The president of the Federal Reserve Bank of Boston called a proposal before the Massachusetts Legislature for a 5% sales tax on services "a potential economic disaster." Richard Syron, in an unusually strong comment by a Federal Reserve president, said the tax would hurt the state's competitive position in services and could drive business away from Massachusetts -- and even result in lost tax revenue.

The state Senate recently approved a proposed sales tax on fees charged by mutual funds and other financial services, accountants, lawyers and public relations firms, among others, to help eliminate a budget deficit now estimated at about $1 billion for the fiscal year ending Saturday; the Senate bill also includes a small increase in the state income tax. The state House, however, has passed a bill that relies mostly on a larger increase in the state income tax.

The two legislative bodies haven't been able to resolve their differences. Business groups are lobbying strongly against expanding the sales tax, which would be among the broadest on services in the nation, while labor groups are lobbying for it and against increasing the income tax.

The state's budget deficit, and the legislature's deadlock on a solution, have resulted in Massachusetts' having the lowest-rated general obligation bonds in the nation, with a Baa rating from Moody's Investors Service Inc.
