Money Fund Yields Were Unchanged In Latest Week

  • Author: Georgette Jasen
  • Date: 06/28/90

Investors took some of their cash out of money market mutual funds in the latest week, as yields didn't budge. The average seven-day compound yield, which assumes reinvestment of dividends, remained at 7.96%, according to IBC/Donoghue's Money Fund Report. Assets of the 431 taxable funds tracked by the Holliston, Mass.-based newsletter slipped by $611 million to $378.9 billion in the week ended Tuesday.

Most of the withdrawals were from funds open only to institutions, said Martha Wittbrodt, editor of Donoghue's. She said some of that cash may have been invested directly in short-term Treasury securities. Yields on Treasury bills sold at Monday's auction were about a tenth of a percentage point higher than on the average money fund.

Money funds invest in such things as Treasury bills, commercial paper (short-term corporate IOUs) and bank certificates of deposit. Average maturity of the funds' investments remained at 44 days, indicating no substantial change in yields any time soon.

Average maturity has been 43 or 44 days for nearly three months. The average seven-day compound yield has been at or slightly below 8% since late January.

The average seven-day simple yield of the taxable funds remained at 7.67%. The average 30-day simple yield again was 7.67% and the average 30-day compound yield stayed at 7.96% for the second consecutive week.

Yields on tax-exempt funds rose slightly, although assets fell. The average seven-day compound yield of the tax-free funds, for instance, increased to 5.45% from 5.40%. That's equivalent to 7.57% for someone in the 28% tax bracket and to 8.13% for those paying 33% in taxes.

Assets of 222 tax-exempt funds declined to $75.9 billion from $76.1 billion. Average maturity lengthened to 43 days from 40.
