REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: Drilling Oil Politics

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/28/90

As our Gerald Seib detailed yesterday, President Bush's offshore-drilling edict was the product of extended political calculation. The oil industry rightly objects to his restrictions on their merits, but we suspect he got the politics of the situation pretty near right. Given the psuedo-environmentalist ethic now in force, little new coastal extraction was to be expected soon in any case. Of course, White House leadership on difficult issues is welcome, but the greater responsibility in this case lies with Congressmen and state officials, including conservative Republicans, who formed a near-solid wall of opposition to drilling; with judges, who've demonstrated hair-trigger sensitivity to anti-drilling lawsuits; and with that segment of the public that wants cheap gasoline but is intolerant of the hazards of either tankers or less risky drilling platforms off the coast. In time, as oil grows dearer, the matter may become riper for rational choices.
