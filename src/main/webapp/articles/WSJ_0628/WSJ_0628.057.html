Letters to the Editor -- Voodoo Economics: Coke's MagiCans

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/28/90

Your "MagiCans" article on June 5 missed the same point that the Coca-Cola management missed. The real problem with MagiCans was that they did not contain any Coca-Cola] Purchasers expect a can of Coca-Cola to be a can of Coca-Cola. They buy it because they are thirsty or need the sugar or the fizz or the caffeine or whatever they bought it for. When they open it (12 miles down a dusty road on the way to an important sales call or halfway up the side of a mountain) they don't expect a can full of tricks.

To include promotions is interesting; to substitute them for the product is dishonest, bad business and stupid. The real thing may be worth more to the customer than a can with $10 in it.

Tom Boyd

Salesman

Bolton, Conn.
