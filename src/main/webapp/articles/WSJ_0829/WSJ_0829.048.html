Belgian Firm's Unit Buys Mine

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/29/90

BRUSSELS -- Acec-Union-Miniere, a Belgian nonferrous metals and mining unit of Societe Generale de Belgique, said it and a Mexican partner bought the Cananea copper mine from the Mexican government for $475 million. A spokesman said the purchase is Acec's first venture in South America. Acec holds slightly more than 21% of the mine, which was bought through a joint venture, Mexicana de Cananea S.A., formed with a mining affiliate of Grupo Mexico. Acec has spent $85 million on the mine; Grupo Mexico, which holds the remaining 79%, has spent $390 million.

The venture also is investing $30 million in expanding Cananea output, now 150,000 tons of copper a year, which the spokesman said the company hopes to increase to 250,000 tons.
