Japan's Leading Indicator Slips

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/29/90

TOKYO -- The leading indicator for June slipped to 54.5 from a revised 58.3 in May but remained above the so-called boom-or-bust line of 50 for the fourth consecutive month, the Economic Planning Agency said. The indicator shows the Japanese economy remains strong after 45 months of continued expansion and should sustain growth in the future, the agency said. A reading exceeding 50 signals economic expansion and a reading below that level indicates possible contraction ahead.

Rising construction activity is fueling continued strong personal consumption and capital investment expenditures, keeping the economy humming, an agency official said. Meanwhile, prices remain in a stable range.
