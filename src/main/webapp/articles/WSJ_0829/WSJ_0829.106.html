Marketing & Media: CBS Seen Naming Rodgers To Head TV Stations Unit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/29/90

NEW YORK -- CBS Inc. is expected to announce today the appointment of Johnathan Rodgers as president of the division that runs the television stations the broadcasting company owns. Mr. Rodgers is general manager of WBBM, the CBS-owned station in Chicago. He would succeed Eric Ober, who was named president of CBS News last week, succeeding David W. Burke, who was fired.

Prior to joining WBBM in 1986, Mr. Rodgers was executive producer of the "CBS Morning News" and of the weekend edition of the "CBS Evening News."

Speculation here late yesterday was that Paul Sagan, news director of WCBS in New York, would succeed Mr. Rodgers. CBS declined to comment on either of the executives.

CBS owns five television stations, which have total annual revenue exceeding $400 million.
