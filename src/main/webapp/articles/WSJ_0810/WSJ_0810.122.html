Bechtel Continues Running Projects in Saudi Arabia

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/10/90

SAN FRANCISCO -- Bechtel Group Inc. said it is continuing operations and keeping business offices open, while providing charter flights from Saudi Arabia to London for any employees or family members who wish to leave. In Saudi Arabia, Bechtel, a privately held engineering-construction concern, has an $80 billion petrochemical complex under construction in the industrial city of Jubail, 150 miles from the Kuwaiti border, and is working on the King Fahd International Airport construction project. Some 1,200 employees are employed on the two projects.

A company spokesman said that one chartered airliner with about 100 people left Saudi Arabia late Wednesday night and arrived in London early yesterday morning.

Two planeloads of Bechtel employees or their family members -- about 200 people -- have left since the company began providing transport. Others have left on commercial flights booked by the company.

The company, which has worked on projects in the Middle East for nearly 50 years, said it is monitoring the situation on a 24-hour basis and receiving information and advice from the U.S. State Department and the British Foreign Service.
