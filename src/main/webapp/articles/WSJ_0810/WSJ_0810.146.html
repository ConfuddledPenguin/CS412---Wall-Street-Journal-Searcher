Business Brief -- Avnet Inc.: Fourth-Period Net Fell 62% After Restructuring Charge

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/10/90

Avnet Inc., after taking a restructuring charge, reported fiscal fourth-quarter net income fell 62%. The maker of industrial and consumer products said net income was $7.5 million, or 22 cents a share, after the $9.8 million charge. Year-earlier net was $19.8 million, or 55 cents a share.

Sales for the quarter ended June 30 were $447.9 million, down from the year-earlier $456.9 million.

For the fiscal year, the New York firm said net income rose to $56.5 million, or $1.57 a share, from $54 million, or $1.51 a share. Sales were $1.75 billion, down from $1.92 billion. Year-ago results included a gain of $1.3 million on the sale of four operations.

The company said the restructuring charge includes costs related to the disposition of its Taiwanese operation and restructuring of its Avnet Computer Technologies and Harper S.I.D operations.

Avnet Chairman Leon Machiz said that, despite the 9% decline in fiscal-year sales, earnings from operations, excluding the charge, rose 26%. Net operating margins increased to 3.8% from 2.7%.
