Group of Reinsurers Fails in Appeal Effort On Mission Insurance

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/10/90

SAN FRANCISCO -- The California department of insurance said the state's Supreme Court denied an appeal that could have blocked the department from seeking recovery of $1.5 billion in damages from reinsurers of Mission Insurance Co. Los Angeles-based Mission, which, with its units, has been in liquidation by California regulators since February 1987, is claiming the money as a principal asset in the vast insolvency.

The California insurance department will pursue its claim for the $1.5 billion at a trial scheduled to begin Nov. 1.

Underwriters Reinsurance Co., one of Mission's reinsurers, has led a challenge to a lower court ruling that regulators are entitled in litigation to seek not only actual current claims but also an amount equalling projected future claims. Reinsurers objected to regulators' demands for the far larger future claims on the ground that their calculation is too uncertain. Underwriters, Woodland Hills, Calif., is a unit of Underwriters ReCorp.

The apparent exhaustion of appeals for reinsurers may accelerate settlements between the companies -- which insure insurance companies -- and state regulators in the Mission case. To date, regulators have recovered more than $300 million through settlements with Mission's reinsurers.
