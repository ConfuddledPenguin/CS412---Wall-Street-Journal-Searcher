Business Brief -- Nordstrom Inc.: Retailer's Costs Rose, Profit Fell 7% in Second Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/10/90

Nordstrom Inc., still grappling with rising costs, reported profit fell 7% in the second quarter. Earnings for the second period, ended July 31, were off 7% to $35.9 million, or 44 cents a share, from $38.5 million, or 47 cents in the comparable period last year. Costs and expenses totaled $745.6 million, up nearly 12%, while sales rose 10% to $801.8 million.

John A. Goesling, the Seattle retailer's senior vice president, said the company isn't "yet satisfied" with its results. He added, however, that trends in sales and expenses are "moving in the right direction." Sales increased at a higher rate and expenses at a lower rate during the second quarter than the first, ended April 30.

Mr. Goesling said the company brought its inventories in line with sales in the second quarter. It also took steps to cut expenses across the board.

Profits for the six months, ended July 31, declined 20% to $49.1 million, or 60 cents a share, from $61.5 million, or 75 cents a share.
