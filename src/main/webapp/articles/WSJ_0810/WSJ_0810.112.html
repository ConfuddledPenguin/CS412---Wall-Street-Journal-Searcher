Business Brief -- Del Webb Corp.: Real Estate Company Ends Its Shareholder Rights Plan

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/10/90

Del Webb Corp., a Phoenix, Ariz.-based real estate development concern, said that it terminated its shareholder rights plan and that all rights outstanding expired yesterday. A Del Webb spokesman said the move came in response to shareholder approval at the annual meeting in May of a nonbinding resolution to drop the anti-takeover plan. The resolution was proposed by Webcott Holdings, a California partnership, controlled by James J. Cotter, that holds a large stake in Del Webb.

In April, Del Webb agreed to several of Mr. Cotter's demands to avert a proxy fight at the annual meeting. Del Webb agreed to expand the board by two members and renominate four of 10 directors, including Mr. Cotter, whose terms were set to expire. Mr. Cotter is also chairman of Craig Corp., a Los Angeles supermarket chain and photographic accessories concern.
