Sundstrand Insurers Will Pay $15 Million To Settle Litigation

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/10/90

ROCKFORD, Ill. -- Sundstrand Corp. said insurance companies agreed to pay $15 million to settle shareholder litigation against some current and former officers and directors of the defense contractor, arising out of its 1988 plea of guilty to defense fraud charges. The company couldn't immediately say how much of the $15 million it would receive; plaintiffs' lawyers are seeking a portion, plus expenses, for bringing the so-called shareholder derivative actions in federal court in Chicago and state court in Delaware.

Sundstrand paid a then record $115 million to settle the government's procurement fraud charges. Shareholders in turn sued officers and directors for the damage to the company.

"Sundstrand is extremely pleased that this litigation has been resolved," Harry C. Stonecipher, president and chief executive officer, said in a news release. "This development puts the company's past difficulties squarely behind it."

The settlement, which the officers and directors had to agree to, didn't call for them to admit any liability, and they continue to deny having any liability, Sundstrand said.
