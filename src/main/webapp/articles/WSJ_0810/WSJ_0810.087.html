Marketing & Media: Lever Brothers Will Bottle Liquids in Recycled Plastic

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/10/90

NEW YORK -- Lever Brothers Co. said it has agreed to use 10 million pounds of recycled plastic annually in its bottles for such liquid household products as Wisk laundry detergent and Snuggle fabric softener. Lever Brothers has agreed to purchase 50% of the output of a new plastics recycling facility owned by Sonoco Graham Co., one of the nation's largest producers of the type of high-density plastics used in the bottles. The Sonoco Graham recycling facility, in York, Pa., is scheduled to start producing the resins in September.

Lever, a unit of Unilever Group, an Anglo-Dutch consumer products company, in June began filling bottles that contain some recycled resins. The new agreement with Sonoco Graham will accelerate those efforts. By mid-1991, the company predicts 50% of all its bottles will contain between 25% and 35% recycled plastic.

Environmentalists have applauded these efforts. Said Sam Hartwell, a research associate at the Natural Resources Defense Council, "Creating demand for recovered materials is an important step in encouraging consumers and municipalities to recycle."

But with consumers' interest in environmental protection soaring, competitive pressures also play a big role. Procter & Gamble Co. already has announced plans to package liquid laundry detergents in bottles made partly from recycled plastic. Colgate-Palmolive Co. also plans to use recycled plastics in bottles of dishwashing liquids.
