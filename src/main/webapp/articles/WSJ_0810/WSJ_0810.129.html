Business Brief -- Sierra Pacific Resources: Nevada Utility Cancels Plan For Thousand Springs Plant

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/10/90

Sierra Pacific Resources said market uncertainty has caused it to cancel the development of the Thousand Springs electric generation project resulting in a $35 million charge on the utility's 1990 earnings. The Reno, Nev., utility holding company posted earnings of $44.9 million, or $2.05 a share, in 1989. Although several purchase proposals were submitted by potential energy buyers, the company said it was unable to obtain long-term purchase agreements necessary to sustain the project, planned for construction in Elko, Nev.

Sierra Pacific was the project manager and largest participant in this independent power project. In the past few weeks, four companies, Bonneville Pacific Corp.; Foster Wheeler Energy Corp.; The Pittsburgh & Midway Coal Mining Co.; and Westinghouse Electric Corp. have withdrawn from the project, company officials said.
