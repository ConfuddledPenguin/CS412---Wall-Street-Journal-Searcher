Honda Creates Unit To Sell Cars in Italy

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/10/90

TOKYO -- Honda Motor Co. established a wholly owned automobile importing and sales subsidiary in Verona, Italy, scheduled to begin operations in October. To be called Honda Automobili Italia S.p.A., the unit was capitalized at 43 billion lire ($36.9 million). The new subsidiary will handle and reinforce automobile operations previously managed by Honda Italia Industriale S.p.A., which also manages motorcycle manufacturing and sales, as well as power-product importing and sales.

Honda said that Italy's automobile market, with 2.362 million passenger vehicles sold in 1989, is the second-largest in Europe next to West Germany. Not including off-road, four-wheel drive vehicles, Japanese passenger vehicles account for 8,579 units of this total, Honda said.

Italy's market has grown at a 45% pace over the past five years, the Japanese auto maker said.

Honda Automobili Italia is expected to sell 4,500 units in 1990, including projected sales of 2,900 units through September by the former sales office, the parent company said. Sales are seen at 7,500 units in 1991.

There is an import restriction for Japanese vehicles in Italy, so Honda has been selling its automobiles through licensed import. Italian sales of its Concerto model began in April. The Concerto is produced by U.K. auto maker Rover Group, a unit of British Aerospace PLC.
