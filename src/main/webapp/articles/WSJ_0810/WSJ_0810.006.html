Bond Corp. Holders Approve One Part Of Restructuring

  • Author: Barbara Toman
  • Date: 08/10/90

LONDON -- Investors in Bond Corp. Holdings Ltd. approved one aspect of the ailing company's proposed restructuring, but once again postponed decisions on three others. The partial approval leaves the future of Australian entrepreneur Alan Bond in doubt. Mr. Bond last month said he would quit as chairman of his flagship company in exchange for investors' support for actions crucial to Bond Corp.'s survival. The resignation was to come within 30 days of the investors' approval.

It wasn't clear yesterday whether that 30-day clock had started ticking. Mr. Bond was sailing in Australia and unavailable for comment, according to Peter Lucas, Bond Corp.'s director of public relations.

"I can't speak for Alan Bond," Mr. Lucas said at a news conference. But Mr. Lucas added that "clearly there is a desire" among investors to see Mr. Bond step down. And if Mr. Bond needs to resign to obtain approval of the company restructuring, "that is what he'll do," Mr. Lucas said.

The bondholders have been asked to clear the proposed sale of Bond Corp.'s breweries to its 58%-controlled Bell Resources Ltd. unit, and to decide whether to waive about $25 million Australian (US$19.8 million) in interest due on securities over the next year. Decisions on the matters had already been postponed twice.

Yesterday, holders of sterling-denominated convertible bonds approved the breweries sale. But among holders of U.S. dollar-denominated bonds at yesterday's meeting, approval for the breweries sale fell short of the required 75% majority. To avoid defeat, bondholders voted to adjourn the meeting without a vote on the matter, and to resubmit it at a new meeting tentatively scheduled for Sept. 10.

Support for the interest moratorium fell short of the required 75% majority among both sterling and U.S.-dollar denominated bondholders. Both groups postponed decisions on that matter until Nov. 30.

The investors also intended yesterday to elect a bondholders' committee to advise the company. But that effort was postponed until Sept. 4 because yesterday's meeting lacked the necessary quorum. Meanwhile, Mr. Lucas said, investors attending the meeting formed "unofficial committees" which will try to settle certain procedural issues before formal discussions begin with Bond Corp. directors.

Mr. Lucas blamed summer vacations for thwarting Bond Corp.'s effort to round up holders of its bearer bonds. But "there is a very clear indication that the majority of bondholders want the brewery transaction to proceed," he said.
