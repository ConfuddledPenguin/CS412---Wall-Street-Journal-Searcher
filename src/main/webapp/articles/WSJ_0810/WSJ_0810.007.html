`Playmates' Earnings Skyrocket on Success Of the `Ninja Turtles'

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 08/10/90

HONG KONG -- Playmates International Holdings Ltd. announced a 69-fold surge in first-half profit, as the luminous performance of its "Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles" line drove earnings far above even the most sanguine expectations. The Hong Kong toy group said final profit skyrocketed to 350 million Hong Kong dollars (US$45 million) in the first half from HK$5 million a year earlier. Earnings per share soared to HK$2 (25 U.S. cents) from 3 Hong Kong cents, while turnover climbed to HK$1.38 billion from HK$254 million.

"No one thought their results could look like this," said an analyst of industrial issues at an international brokerage firm. The results were about 50% higher than most full-year profit expectations of HK$200 million to HK$250 million.

The group's directors proposed an interim dividend of 10 Hong Kong cents a share; no payout was made in the 1989 first half. They also recommended a 3-for-1 bonus issue to help improve the shares' liquidity.
