Metropolitan Circuits Reports GE Lawsuit Seeks to Recover Debts

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/10/90

SANTA ANA, Calif. -- Metropolitan Ciruits Inc. said that General Electric Co. filed a $1.7 million lawsuit against the company to collect certain debts. A General Electric spokesman said the debt for its sale to Metropolitan Circuits of copper-clad laminate was due more than a year ago.

Metropolitan Circuits, a circuit-board maker, said last month that it would sell options to buy as much as a 37.5% interest in the company to two consultants, if they prove successful in raising new capital for the company. "For Metropolitan to continue doing business, the financing is absolutely essential," said Richard J. Sandberg, chairman, president and chief executive officer. Kenneth C. Cleveland and Paul G. McNamara also would become the company's top executives if they raise between $2 million and $3.5 million within a month, Mr. Sandberg said July 17.

If the consultants are successful, the financing would be used for, among other purposes, retiring the General Electric debt, Metropolitan said.
