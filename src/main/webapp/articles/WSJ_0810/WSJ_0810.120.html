Argentina and Pakistan Get World Bank Loans

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/10/90

WASHINGTON -- World Bank executive directors cleared the way for Argentina to receive the second $150 million installment of a $300 million loan approved in late 1988 to support trade-policy adjustments in the South American country. In other actions, the World Bank cleared a $130 million loan to Pakistan to help expand natural-gas distribution facilities and reduce the country's dependence on imported oil.

Also, the International Development Association, a World Bank affiliate, approved a $64 million credit to China for agricultural development projects along the Yangtze River.

The World Bank decided this year to delay the Argentine loan because the government had failed to carry out broader economic policy actions required under the original loan agreement. It was reported last week that the Argentine government expected to get the money later this month. The loans would help finance the country's efforts to ease its import and export restrictions.

The loan to Pakistan will help finance the construction of a gas-purification plant at Sui, in the southwestern part of the country. As part of the same project, Sui Northern Gas Pipelines Ltd. will increase its gas-distribution network serving the Northern and Central parts of the country by 300 million cubic feet a day.
