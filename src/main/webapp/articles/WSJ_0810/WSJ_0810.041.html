Enterprise Brief -- Safer Inc.: Dispute Over Safety Claims Is Settled With the EPA

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/10/90

Safer Inc., Newton, Mass., said it agreed to settle a dispute with the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency concerning promotional safety claims the closely held concern has made about its biodegradable pesticides. In July 1989 the EPA filed an administrative complaint against the company, charging that its literature violated federal laws prohibiting safety claims in pesticide advertising. Safer argues that the laws were drafted before relatively benign pesticides such as its "insecticidal soaps" were available, and so are "outdated" and shouldn't apply to its products, which it has advertised as nontoxic. Furthermore, Safer noted, the EPA hasn't questioned the safety of its products -- only the language used to promote them.

The company said that under its consent decree with the EPA, it will pay a $10,000 civil penalty and undertake a program of "environmentally beneficial expenditures promoting the safe use of pesticides." Safer added that it agreed to the settlement to avoid "costly and protracted" litigation.
