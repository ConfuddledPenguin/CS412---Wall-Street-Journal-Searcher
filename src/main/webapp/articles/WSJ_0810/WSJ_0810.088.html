California Hospital Group Sues State Health Agency

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 08/10/90

California's largest hospital association sued the state's health department director, alleging that "woefully inadequate" state Medi-Cal outpatient reimbursements violate federal laws and cripple health care to the poor. Medi-Cal funds are matched by federal Medicaid dollars to provide health care to 3.4 million eligible Californians.

The California Association of Hospitals and Health Systems and the Orthopaedic Hospital in Los Angeles filed a lawsuit against Ken Kizer, director of the State Department of Health Service, in U.S. District Court in Los Angeles. The association says that outpatient service payments average 55 cents for every dollar hospitals spend to provide the care. "Hospitals are getting clobbered with these rates," said C. Duane Dauner, president of the association.

A spokesman for the state health department declined to immediately comment on the lawsuit.

Mr. Dauner said California hospitals lose more than $575,000 a day providing outpatient services to Medi-Cal patients. He also said that since 1982, the state has increased payment rates to hospitals for Medi-Cal outpatients by 2%, while hospital costs have risen 50%. Similar lawsuits in four states have resulted in higher payment rates to hospitals, he said.
