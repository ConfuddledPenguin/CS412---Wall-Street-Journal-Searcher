New Securities Issues

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/10/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report. CORPORATE

Chemical Waste Management Inc. -- $500 million redemption amount of liquid yield option notes due Aug. 16, 2010, priced at $306.56 for each $1,000 note, for a yield to maturity of 6%. The LYONs, as zero-coupon convertible subordinated notes, are typically priced at a deep discount. The notes are convertible into common at $26.255 a share, representing an 18% premium over yesterday's closing price. Rated single-A-2 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and double-A-minus by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets.

Merrill Lynch & Co. -- $100 million of 9.25% notes due Nov. 15, 1994, priced at par. The non-callable notes were priced at a spread of 93 basis points above the Treasury's four-year note. Rated single-A-2 by Moody's and single-A by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets.

Navistar Financial Corp. -- $100 million of 9 3/4% notes due Aug. 15, 1995, priced at 99.80 to yield 9.801%. The non-callable notes were priced at 145 basis points above the Treasury's five-year note. Rated Baa-3 by Moody's and triple-B-minus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Lehman Brothers.

MUNICIPAL

Westchester County, N.Y. -- $75 million of improvement unlimited tax bonds, Series 1990A, due Feb. 1, 1992-2010, via a Chase Securities Inc. group. The bonds, rated triple-A, were priced for reoffering to yield from 5.90% in 1992 to 6.95% in 2008-2010. There are no term bonds.

Monmouth County, N.J. -- $68.7 million of general improvement bonds Series 1990, due Aug. 1, 1991-2002, and utility bonds Series 1990, due Aug. 1, 1991-2010, via a Merrill Lynch Capital Markets group. The $38.2 million general improvement bonds are priced to yield from 5.80% in 1991 to 6.75% in 2002. The $30.5 million utility bonds are priced to yield from 5.80% in 1991 to 7.05% in 2009 and 2010. The bonds are rated double-A-1/double-A. There are no term bonds.

MORTGAGES

Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $500 million of Remic mortgage securities offered in 11 classes by Morgan Stanley & Co. The offering, FHLMC 193, is backed by Freddie Mac 9% securities. Price and yield data weren't immediately available.

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $300 million of Remic mortgage securities, offered in 12 classes by Lehman Brothers. The offering, Series 1990-111, is backed by Fannie Mae securities with a weighted average coupon rate of 10.70% and weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29.83 years.

Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $300 million of Remic mortgage securities via Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette. The offering, Series FHLMC 192, is backed by Freddie Mac 9 1/2% 30-year securities.

ASSET-BACKED

Chrysler Financial Corp. -- A two-part issue-CFC 11-A Grantor Trust and CFC 11-B Grantor Trust-offered through Chemical Securities Inc., a unit of Chemical Banking Corp., with a coupon rate of 8.90% at 99 41/64 to yield 9.072%. The CFC 11-A Grantor Trust, which totals about $139 million, is a marine receivables-backed certificate issue. The securities are expected to be rated triple-A by Moody's and S&P based on the quality of the underlying loans and a 15% Capmac surety bond. Capmac, or Capital Markets Assurance Corp., is a unit of Citicorp. The originator and servicer of the underlying marine loans is Chrysler First Inc. The securities have an average life of 3.19 years and a stated final maturity of Aug. 15, 1997. The CFC 11-B Grantor Trust, which totals about $75 million, is a recreational vehicles-backed certificate issue. A triple-A rating from Moody's and S&P is expected based on the quality of the underlying loans and a 13.5% Capmac surety bond. The originator and servicer of the underlying RV loans is Chrysler First Inc. The average life is 3.28 years assuming monthly prepayments at 1% of the original loan balance. Stated final maturity is Aug. 15, 1997. Both issues were priced at 90 basis points over three-year Treasury notes.

OTHER

Student Loan Marketing Association -- $250 million of floating-rate notes due Aug. 22, 1994, priced initially at par to float weekly at 50 basis points above the Treasury's three-month bill. The non-callable notes will be reoffered at various prices and will pay interest quarterly.

EUROBONDS

Japan Leasing Corp. (Japan) -- $100 million of Eurobonds due in August 2000, via Goldman Sachs International Ltd. Price 100.30, bears interest at 9 1/4% for the first four years and at the six-month London interbank offered rate plus 0.20 point thereafter. Guaranteed by Long Term Credit Bank of Japan, callable at par in 1994. Fees 0.30.

Sumitomo Cement Co. (Japan) -- $150 million of Eurobonds due Aug. 30, 1994, with equity-purchase warrants, with a fixed 5% coupon at par, via Nomura International Ltd. Each $5,000 bond carries one stock warrant exercisable from Sept. 13, 1990, through Aug. 16, 1994. Terms to be set next Friday. Fees 2 1/4.
