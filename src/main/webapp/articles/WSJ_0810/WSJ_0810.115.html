Eagle Food Centers To Report Flat Sales For Its Second Period

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/10/90

MILAN, Ill. -- Eagle Food Centers Inc. will report virtually flat sales for its second quarter ended Aug. 4. Pasquale V. Petitti, the grocery-store company's chairman, blamed both competition and delays in the concern's plans to construct new stores. He declined to estimate whether earnings were off, saying the company hasn't yet totaled up expenses. Sales rose less than 1% to $282.5 million. In comparison, first-quarter profit rose about 5% to $2.7 million, or 24 cents a share, on sales growth of 3% to $281.6 million.

For the year ending Feb. 2, 1991, Mr. Petitti said he hopes the company will post earnings that "equal or exceed" last year's $10.5 million, or 92 cents a share, on sales of $1.1 billion.

Eagle's stock price has dropped 50% since the company went public about a year ago at an initial offering price of $20. The company's shares are trading over-the-counter. Mr. Petitti said the price may have fallen because of delays in construction of Eagle's new stores.

At the time the company went public, it expected that two stores in Roselle and Normal, Ill., would be operating by last spring, he said. In addition, it once hoped to have another four stores open this year. However, unexpected troubles arose over zoning and construction issues in Roselle and Normal. The company didn't break ground for those stores until spring and they won't be open until this fall. The other four were also delayed, though one should be open next spring.

Meanwhile, the company, which runs 106 stores in the Midwest, has faced competition from new stores put up by its rivals. Last year, about 20 competing stores opened in its marketplace. This year another 18 will go up, the company said.

Still, the company intends to fight back by getting on schedule with its store expansion plans, Mr. Petitti said. It plans to open at least four stores next year, relocate six, and remodel another six.

The company's efforts to boost sales also include plans to distribute coupon books in 20 of its individual markets. The coupons will be specially targeted for the demographics of each store. For instance, in professional, suburban neighborhoods, frozen items and flavored waters will be promoted. In rural areas, the basics, such as baking ingredients and peanut butter, will be emphasized.
