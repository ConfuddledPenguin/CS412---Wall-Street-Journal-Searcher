Credit Ratings: Moody's Reviews Mortgage Security Issued by 19 Thrifts

  • Author: Robin Goldwyn Blumenthal
  • Date: 08/10/90

NEW YORK -- Moody's Investors Service Inc. said it is reviewing for possible downgrade about $4.5 billion of triple-A rated mortgage-backed securities issued by 19 thrifts. The review affects only those securities sold at a slight market discount from par and issued by thrifts that aren't rated investment grade by Moody's. A total of 33 interest-bearing mortgage-backed bonds and medium-term notes are affected. Blanton Neill, an assistant vice president in Moody's structured finance group, said the securities are mostly held by institutional investors.

Some of the issuing thrifts are insolvent, Ms. Neill said. She said the ratings service believes there is a possibility that others may become insolvent. Among the insolvent issuers are Columbia Savings & Loan Association, CenTrust Federal Savings Bank, Imperial Savings Association and Anchor Savings Bank FSB, Ms. Neill said.

Moody's said it is concerned that the federal Resolution Trust Corp. might change the terms of the securities. In June, the RTC repudiated the indentures and took early redemption of $3.8 billion of interest-bearing mortgage-backed bonds issued by Franklin Savings Association in Kansas. In that case, Moody's said, investors received the original discounted price, plus accrued interest through the payment date, plus a calculated amortization of the discount from par. Moody's said it's concerned the same thing may happen to the 33 issues under review.

Thus the actual payments to Franklin investors were slightly less than if the RTC, the government's thrift-bailout agency, had paid the full principal at par, plus accrued interest, as provided for in the indentures if the bonds were redeemed early or payments accelerated. Ms. Neill said the RTC has the authority to repudiate the indentures, the agreements between issuer and buyer.

"The question we're focusing on is if the RTC redeems a bond early, does it pay the same redemption price that the indenture indicated would be paid in the event of an aceleration." She added that "nothing in the documents on which we based the rating indicated the redemptions would be less than par." Although she said the RTC policy is evolving, the agency hasn't made an official statement on how it plans to redeem the securities. But she said its action on the Franklin Savings securities and other indications "leads us to believe" that if the RTC redeems other such securities they will so at a discount to par.
