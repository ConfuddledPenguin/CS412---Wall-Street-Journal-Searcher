Sun Microsystems Reports Profit For Its 4th Quarter

  • Author: Chip Johnson
  • Date: 08/10/90

MOUNTAIN VIEW, Calif. -- Sun Microsystems Inc., continuing to pound its competitors in the computer workstation business, posted fiscal fourth-quarter net of $49.1 million, or 51 cents a share. It had a loss of $20.3 million, or 25 cents a share in the year-ago quarter. The company's earnings for the quarter ended June 30 exceeded expectations of even the most optimistic analysts and provided fresh evidence that Sun's grip on the workstation market is tightening. In recent months, Sun has outpaced competitors, most notably Hewlett-Packard Co. and Digital Equipment Corp., with new models that are faster and less expensive than prior products.

Revenue for the fourth quarter was $700.1 million, a 62% rise from the $431.2 million reported a year ago. "Their star is rising," said Robert Herwick, an analyst with Hambrecht & Quist.

For the year, Sun's revenue totaled $2.47 billion, a 40% increase over its 1989 fiscal year. Net income rose 83% to $111.2 million, or $1.21 a share, compared with $60.8 million, or 76 cents a share, in the prior year.

Bill Raduchel, Sun's chief financial officer, attributed the company's rapid sales growth to new distribution arrangements that give Sun an entre into commercial and office markets. Traditionally, the company has sold its machines to technicians and scientists.

"We see a broad diversity of firms coming to us and major industries are buying our systems, it's just not engineers and scientists anymore," he said.

Mr. Raduchel cited a recent order by the New Zealand Inland Revenue Department, which wants Sun machines for an office automation project.

Sun's quarterly results amid a slow growth period for computer sales presents a bright financial picture for company. It ended the year with $394 million in cash, compared with $54 million a year ago.

The fourth-quarter loss posted by Sun a year ago was the only such loss for the company since it went public in March 1986.
