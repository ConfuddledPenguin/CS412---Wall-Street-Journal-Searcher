Dissident Shareholder In Agnico-Eagle Mines Sells Final 2% Stake

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/10/90

TORONTO -- A dissident shareholder of Agnico-Eagle Mines Ltd., who had planned to seek a buyer for the company, said he has sold his remaining 2% interest in the gold-mining concern. Montreal businessman Jacques Forget said he sold his Agnico shares because he doesn't expect the company will reach its targeted level of production this year.

But Agnico spokesman Sean Boyd said the company expects to produce in 1990 between 5,000 and 10,000 ounces more than the 160,000 ounces the company had targeted. Last year, Agnico's production amounted to 157,000 ounces.

Mr. Forget couldn't be reached for further comment. In a statement, he didn't disclose the terms of the sale of the Agnico shares nor did he identify the buyer.

As previously reported, Mr. Forget said earlier that he intended to seek a change of control at Agnico because of what he called "numerous errors in management." He gave no indication in his statement of whether he still plans to pursue a change in management and control.

On the Toronto Stock Exchange, Agnico shares closed yesterday at 8.13 Canadian dollars (US$7.09), up 25 Canadian cents. At last report, Agnico had 27.4 million shares outstanding.
