Marketing & Media: Denver Media Firm Plans to Complete Restructuring by '91

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/08/90

DENVER -- Tele-Communications Inc. said it hopes to complete a restructuring by the first quarter of 1991 that would put most of its cable television and programming interests in a new company, Liberty Media Corp. As reported, Tele-Communications earlier this year said it planned a major restructuring, but uncertainty in the regulatory environment and difficulties in getting needed consents have delayed the plan's implementation.

In March, the company said it was considering a rights offering to holders, which would entitle them to exchange a portion of their shares in Tele-Communications Class A and Class B common stock for shares of a new company, Liberty Media Corp.

Yesterday at the company's annual meeting, Tele-Communications President John Malone said that directors hadn't yet determined the definitive structure of Liberty because, among other things, a number of tax and valuation issues have to be resolved.

Mr. Malone said that, under the plan, the company's investment interest, generally 50% or less, in 18 affiliated entities would be transferred to Liberty. The program investments include stakes in American Movie Classics, The Discovery Channel, QVC Networks and The Family Channel, but exclude Turner Broadcasting System Inc.

Tele-Communications is eager to keep its 22% stake in Turner under control of the original company.
