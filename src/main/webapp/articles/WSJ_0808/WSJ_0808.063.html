Capital Spending in Japan

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/08/90

TOKYO -- Capital spending by Japanese corporations this year will grow 16% from a year earlier, according to responses by 4,296 companies in a survey by Japan's Economic Planning Agency. The forecast represents a slowing of growth, following year-to-year gains of 27% in 1989 and 22% in 1988, but it would still mark the third consecutive year of double-digit increases in capital spending outlays. The agency said it is certain that growth in capital outlays for the year ending in March will exceed 10%.

The agency acknowledged that recent market instability, following Iraq's invasion of Kuwait, could hurt market sentiment. But it said corporate investment for energy conservation and new technologies is expected to remain strong.
