Business Brief -- Harley-Davidson Inc.: Firm Adopts Revised Plan To Guard Against Takeovers

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/08/90

Harley-Davidson Inc. said it adopted a revised shareholder rights plan to thwart any hostile takeover attempts. However, the Milwaukee motorcycle maker said it isn't aware of any efforts currently to gain control of the company.

Under the plan, shareholders of record Aug. 20 will receive, for every common share they hold, a right to buy one-hundredth of a newly issued share of preferred stock at an exercise price of $100. The rights would be exercisable if a bidder acquires 15% or more of the company's common stock -- or announces a tender offer for at least 25%.

If Harley-Davidson is taken over, each right will entitle the holder to buy shares in the acquiring company's common stock with a market value of twice that of the right's exercise price.

In addition, if a bidder purchases a 15% stake in the motorcycle concern, each right holder (other than the acquirer) can receive preferred shares in Harley-Davidson with a market value equal to twice that of the exercise price. The company's previous rights plan required that a bidder acquire at least 25% of its shares before the rights were triggered.
