Business Brief -- Hicks, Muse & Co.: Investment Firm to Buy, Combine Neodata and TMI

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/08/90

Hicks, Muse & Co., a Dallas investment partnership, said it agreed to buy Neodata Services Group of Louisville, Colo., from Dun & Bradstreet Corp. for $85 million. The investment firm also said it will buy TMI Corp., a Dallas data management concern, for about $14 million. After working capital and expenses, the total deal is estimated at $104 million. It is expected to be completed within 30 days.

Hicks Muse said it will combine TMI and Neodata, a magazine subscription service concern, to form a single media service company, Neodata Holdings Inc., in Boulder, Colo. Electronic Data Systems Inc., a unit of General Motors Corp., is investing $6 million in preferred stock in the purchase of the two companies. EDS is also an investor in the Hicks, Muse Equity Fund, which is providing equity for the deal. EDS will provide data processing services and equipment to the new holding company.

Senior management of Neodata and TMI will remain at the new company and will participate in the investment. Thomas Hicks, chairman of Hicks Muse, will be chairman and chief executive officer of Neodata Holdings Inc.
