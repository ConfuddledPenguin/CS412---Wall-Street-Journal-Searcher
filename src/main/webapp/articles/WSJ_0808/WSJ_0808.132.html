Marketing Brief -- General Electric Co.: NBC to Implement Contract Despite Rejection by Union

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/08/90

National Broadcasting Co. said it would implement a new four-year contract Aug. 15 for a union representing producers, news writers, engineers and technicians despite the union's overwhelming rejection of the pact. The decision was made following a meeting yesterday between NBC, a unit of General Electric Co., and the union, the National Association of Broadcast Employees and Technicians.

A spokesman for NABET said the union, which has about 2,500 members, will meet tomorrow to discuss what action could be taken. "We tried to encourage further negotiations, but NBC said it would implement the final offer even though we rejected it twice," the spokesman said.

The previous contract expired March 31. No strike vote has been taken, and the NABET spokesman said it was "highly unlikely" that a strike would occur. The union went on a 17-week strike against New York-based NBC in 1987. The main issue in this contract dispute involves jurisdiction over people hired by the day rather than on a full-time basis.
