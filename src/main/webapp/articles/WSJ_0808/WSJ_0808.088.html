Rockwell NASA Job Extended for 4 Years

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/08/90

WASHINGTON -- The National Aeronautics and Space Administration extended for four years a contract with Rockwell International Corp. to conduct tests to improve the safety and performance of the space shuttle's main engines. Under an $819.8 million contract extension, the company's Rocketdyne Division will conduct an extensive test program designed to improve the engines' high-pressure turbopumps and other features.

Each shuttle carries three reusable engines, which burn a mixture of liquid hydrogen and liquid oxygen propellants. They are fired for about eight minutes as the shuttle ascends into orbit. In addition, the shuttle's two solid-fuel booster rockets burn during the first two minutes of flight.
