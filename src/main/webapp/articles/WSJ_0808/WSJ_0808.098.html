New Securities Issues

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/08/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report. CORPORATE

AMR Corp. -- $250 million of 8 5/8% notes due Aug. 15, 1992, priced at 99.91 to yield 8.675%. The noncallable notes were priced at a spread of 65 basis points above the Treasury's two-year note. Rated single-A-3 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and single-A by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Salomon Brothers Inc.

Merrill Lynch & Co. -- $100 million of 9% notes due Aug. 15, 1993, priced at par. The noncallable notes were priced at a spread of 90 basis points above the Treasury's current three-year note. Rated single-A-2 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and single-A by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch.

MUNICIPAL

New York State Dormitory Authority -- $208 million of City University of New York consolidated second general resolution refunding revenue bonds, Series 1990D and 1990E, priced by a Morgan Stanley & Co. group. The $142.7 million Series 1990D comprises current interest serial bonds priced to yield from 6.75% in 1998 to 7.40% in 2003 and $95.8 million of 7% term bonds priced at 94.98 to yield 7.50% in 2009. The $65.3 million Series 1990E comprises current interest serial bonds priced to yield from 6.75% in 1998 to 7.35% in 2002 and $11 million of 7.75% term bonds priced at par due in 2017. The bonds are rated single-A/triple-B-plus, except for Series 1990D bonds due in 1998-2000, 2002 and 2003 and Series 1990E bonds due in 1998-2000 and 2002, which are insured and triple-A-rated.

Los Angeles -- $201.3 million of certificates of participation, Series 1990, priced by a PaineWebber Inc. group. The cerfificates, for the city's Convention and Exhibition Center, were priced to yield from 6.20% in 1994 to 7.23% in 2021. Zero-coupon capital appreciation bonds are due in 2001-2005 and yield from 6.80% to 7.00%. There are $33.1 million of 7% term bonds priced at 98.25 to yield 7.17% in 2010 and $52.7 million of 7% term bonds priced at 97.25 to yield 7.23% in 2021. The issue is insured and triple-A-rated.

Philadelphia School District -- $120 million of 6 1/4% school district tax and revenue anticipation notes, Series 1990-91, due June 28, 1991, tentatively priced by a Merrill Lynch Capital Markets group to yield 6% in 1991. The notes are rated MIG-1/SP-1.

Vermont -- $100 million of 10 1/2-month, 6 1/4% general obligation temporary notes due June 28, 1991, priced mostly by a Merrill Lynch Capital Markets group to yield 5.80%. The notes are rated MIG-1/SP1-Plus.

MORTGAGES

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $300 million of Remic mortgage securities offered in seven classes by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets. The offering, Series 1990-107, is backed by Fannie Mae 10% securities with a weighted average coupon rate of 10.75%. Among classes for which details were available, yields ranged from 8.33%, or 45 basis points over the yield on two-year Treasury securities, to 10.02%, or 155 basis points over 30-year Treasurys.

EUROBONDS

There were no major European debt issues priced yesterday.
