Campeau Creditor Extends Agreement On $480 Million Loan

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/08/90

TORONTO -- Campeau Corp. said a major secured creditor, Edward J. DeBartolo Corp., agreed to extend to Aug. 15 an arrangement under which it won't call in its $480 million loan to the financially troubled real-estate and retailing concern without first giving nine days' notice. The previous agreement to give Campeau the nine-day warning before acting to seize DeBartolo's collateral against the loan expired Monday. A similar agreement with two units of the Reichmann family's Olympia & York Developments, which remains in effect, doesn't have an expiration date.

Campeau defaulted on the DeBartolo loan as well as $225 million remaining on its Olympia & York loan after its U.S. retailing units, Federated Department Stores Inc. and Allied Stores Corp., filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection. In March, Campeau refused to make further interest payments on the loans.

The DeBartolo loan is secured mostly against 83.8% of the stock of Ralphs Grocery Co., Campeau's Southern California grocery chain. Ralphs isn't involved in the bankruptcy court proceedings. The Olympia & York loan is secured principally by some of Campeau's Canadian real-estate assets, which are slated to be sold.

The DeBartolo loan helped Campeau finance its $6.6 billion takeover of Federated in May 1988. DeBartolo is a closely held real-estate developer in Youngstown, Ohio.
