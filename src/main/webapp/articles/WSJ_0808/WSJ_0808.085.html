Who's News: Two Senior Officers Quit at Puerto Rico's First Federal Savings

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/08/90

SAN JUAN, Puerto Rico -- First Federal Savings Bank, Puerto Rico, said two senior officers -- Arturo J. Somohano and Fernando Ochoa -- resigned as a result of flaws noted in a recent audit by the Office of Thrift Supervision. The thrift said Angel Alvarez, executive vice president and general counsel, will assume Mr. Somohano's duties as senior executive vice president and chief operating officer. Jorge R. Rodriguez, vice president and investment manager, will succeed Mr. Ochoa on an interim basis as senior vice president for investments.

After the audit, First Federal made a special provision for loan losses of $16.7 million for the second quarter.

"In neither case was it a conflict of interest or malfeasance," Annie Astor de Carbonell, executive vice president and chief financial officer, said of the resignations. "It was mostly a result of lack of proper administration and management on their part."

Messrs. Somohano and Ochoa couldn't be reached for comment.
