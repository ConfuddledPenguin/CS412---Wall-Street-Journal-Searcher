Marketing & Media: Japan Firm Buys 50% of Company Owned by Lipstein

  • Author: Patrick M. Reilly
  • Date: 08/08/90

NEW YORK -- In a move expected to salvage his magazine company, Owen Lipstein said a Japanese media company has purchased a 50% interest in his New American Magazine Co. Terms of the acquisition were not announced, but industry executives close to Mr. Lipstein said Japanese Independent Communication Co. spent less than $15 million for its half interest in the publisher of Smart, Mother Earth News and Psychology Today.

The acquisition by Tokyo-based JICC is the first investment of any size by a Japanese media company in a U.S. magazine company. JICC publishes 10 consumer magazines in Japan as well as non-fiction books. It also produces records, compact disks and computer software; and it owns a travel agency.

Industry experts, however, said the acquisition will probably not signal a wave of Japanese buying in U.S. magazines. "This may stimulate more interest by the Japanese, but they are still trying to figure out the American market," said John Suhler, president of Veronis, Suhler & Associates Inc., the New York-based investment banker who linked Mr. Lipstein with JICC.

Mr. Lipstein said he will continue as chairman and president of New American. He will also remain president of Smart, a men's magazine he co-owns with its founding editor, Terry McDonell.

Seiichi Hasumi, chairman of JICC, said the company intends to "establish and develop a leading-edge publishing and media organization in the U.S." Mr. Lipstein said that JICC's acquisition was a "homerun, by any standards." He said the publishing partnership will represent a pattern other Japanese companies may follow in entering publishing in the U.S. "I have sold half the company, but I feel great freedom and no sense of loss."

For almost a year, Mr. Lipstein's magazines teetered near disaster. Last year, he was forced to sell American Health magazine to Reader's Digest Association Inc. for $29.1 million. The sale was necessary to pay off debt incurred by Mr. Lipstein's purchases of Mother Earth News in 1985 and Psychology Today in 1988.

As he wrestled with debt payments, Mr. Lipstein was forced to delay the mailing of certain issues of his magazines to subscribers, a development which angered advertisers. And as talks with the Japanese dragged on, Mr. Lipstein was forced to dismiss 30 staffers in early June, while a number of top executives and editors left the company.

Publication of Psychology Today was suspended in December 1989. Mr. Lipstein said the magazine will reappear in "late fall," with a redesign.
