Marketing & Media: U.S. Files Suit to Block Procter & Gamble Plan

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/08/90

WASHINGTON (AP) -- The Justice Department, as expected, filed suit to block a plan by Procter & Gamble Co., the maker of Pepto-Bismol, to acquire the exclusive sales rights for Maalox, another stomach remedy. The Justice Department sought a court injunction under the Clayton AntiTrust Act to prevent Procter & Gamble from acquiring the exclusive rights to market and distribute Maalox.

Court papers filed in federal court in Philadelphia charged that the proposed agreement between Cincinnati-based Procter & Gamble and Rhone-Poulenc Rorer Inc., which makes Maalox, would lessen competition in the market of over-the-counter stomach remedies.

Procter & Gamble, based in Cincinnati, sold $125 million of Pepto-Bismol last year, or 14% of the domestic retail market. Domestic sales of Maalox totaled $111 million last year, or 12% of the market.

Under the proposal, Procter & Gamble would acquire the exclusive rights to market and sell Maalox and an option to purchase the manufacturing assets from Rhone-Poulenc Rorer, which is based in Fort Washington, Pa. The deal would make Procter & Gamble the largest seller of stomach remedies in the U.S. market, according to the complaint.
