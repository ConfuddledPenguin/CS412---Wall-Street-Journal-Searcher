Who's News: Whitman's Copper Quits Pet Unit Posts

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/08/90

CHICAGO -- Whitman Corp., which has been trying to improve results at its big Pet Inc. food subsidiary, said the unit's president and chief executive officer, J. Robert Copper, agreed to resign. A Whitman spokesman said Mr. Copper, 50 years old, stepped aside after Whitman's president, Miles L. Marsh, made it clear he felt improving Pet's performance required a management change. Mr. Copper, reached at his home in St. Louis, didn't dispute that, but said he quit because it was apparent that his path to the top of Whitman was blocked.

"There can only be one CEO in a company," Mr. Copper said. He joined what was then IC Industries in 1980 as vice president in charge of strategic planning, and handled acquisitions and divestitures for the conglomerate. In 1988, he was named Pet president, and had hopes of some day running the renamed Whitman, he said, but his "upward track" appeared blocked beginning about six months ago. IC Industries was renamed to Whitman Corp. in 1988.

"It's better for my career," Mr. Copper said. "After discussing the future of the whole corporation, my aspirations were not consistent with what the corporation saw for me." After some time off, Mr. Copper said, he plans to look at other opportunities.

Whitman has sold a lot of businesses to focus on consumer products and services. Mr. Marsh will oversee Pet's day-to-day operations while the company looks for a successor to Mr. Copper, Whitman said.

Pet accounts for about 50% of Whitman's sales, which were $1.96 billion for the first half of 1990, with profit of $58.6 million, or 38 cents a share.
