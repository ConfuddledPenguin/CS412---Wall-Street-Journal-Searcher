Who's News: Centaur Partners Slate Unseats the Chairman Of National Intergroup

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/08/90

NEW YORK -- Centaur Partners Group won three seats on the board of National Intergroup Inc., unseating Chairman Howard M. Love. Elected to the board were Melvyn J. Estrin, a principal of Centaur; Robert J. Slater, former vice chairman of Crane Co.; and William G. Tull, former president of American Security Bank. Centaur said its slate received two-thirds of the shares cast at National Intergroup's meeting July 25.

A spokeswoman for National Intergroup said that the results of the preliminary count, reported by Centaur, corresponds with their tally. The company had no further comment.

Centaur also said that three of its governance resolutions were approved by between 65% and 70% of shareholders. Those resolutions provide for confidential voting by stockholders, prevent the board from lowering the trigger of the company's shareholder-rights plan below 20% and require a majority of stockholders' approval of any golden-parachute plans for management.
