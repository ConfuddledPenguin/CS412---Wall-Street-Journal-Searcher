Western Union Strike Averted

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/08/90

NEW YORK -- Western Union Corp.'s unionized workers remained on the job as the company and the Communications Workers of America worked toward a new three-year contract to replace one that expired midnight Monday. The United Telegraph Workers/CWA had threatened to strike if it didn't get a new contract. But a company spokesman said Western Union's 2,500 unionized employees were working and that company and union negotiators were still meeting at the Cavalier Hotel in Virginia Beach, Va., trying to reach a new agreement.
