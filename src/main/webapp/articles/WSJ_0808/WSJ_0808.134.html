Business Brief -- Berlitz International Inc.: Second-Period Net Rose 35% Despite Difficulties Overseas

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/08/90

Berlitz International Inc. said second-quarter net rose 35%, despite difficulties in its Brazilian and German operations. The Princeton, N.J., language-instruction company said net income was $5.8 million, compared with $4.3 million in the year-earlier quarter. Sales rose 11% to $61.9 million from $55.9 million.

Per-share net declined, however, to 15 cents, from 23 cents a year earlier, after providing for a preferred stock dividend payment of $3 million. There were no preferred dividends in 1989.

Berlitz reported a slight decline in overall lesson volume, as setbacks in Brazil and Germany offset volume increases in almost all other countries. It cited the Brazilian government's harsh new economic program and a teachers strike in some of its German schools.

For the six months, Berlitz reported a rise in net income to $11.6 million from $7.6 million in the year-earlier period. After providing for a preferred stock dividends of $5.9 million, per-share net dropped to 30 cents, from 40 cents. Sales rose 17%, and lesson volume rose 5.6%.

Berlitz was acquired in 1988 by Britain's Maxwell Communication Corp., as part of Maxwell's acquisition of Macmillan Inc., and a public offering was made last December. Its shares closed yesterday at $17.125, unchanged, in New York Stock Exchange composite trading.
