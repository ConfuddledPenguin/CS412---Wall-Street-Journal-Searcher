Corporate Giving to Rise 5% in 1990, Survey Says

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/08/90

NEW YORK -- Corporate giving to education and charitable causes rose a meager 3% in 1989, and is likely to grow only 5% this year, according to a survey by the Council for Aid to Education. After more than a decade of annual increases averaging 15%, corporate contributions began to drop off in 1988, the council said. A spokeswoman attributed the slowing rate to changing tax laws, mixed corporate profits and uncertainty over the economy.

Increases in donations to education also have slowed in recent years, the survey found. Contributions to education rose 4% in 1989 and 6% in 1988, after 12 years of double-digit jumps.

In addition to curbing their largess, the study found companies are redirecting their gifts. The share of dollars devoted specifically to pre-college education jumped 10.5% in 1989, after years of much smaller increases.

Total corporate giving to all causes in 1989 rose to an estimated $6.5 billion, or about 2.1% of pretax corporate profits. Giving to education rose to $2.8 billion, or 0.9% of corporate profits, the council estimated. The growth in corporate giving in 1988 was only 2%, the council now estimates, down from an earlier estimate of 3.2%.

Data for the survey were provided by 333 companies, which account for 25% of corporate giving.
