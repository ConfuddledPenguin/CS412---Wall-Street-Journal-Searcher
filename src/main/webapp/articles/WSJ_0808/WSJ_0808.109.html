REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: A Choice Victory

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/08/90

It looks as if Herbert Grover, Wisconsin's School Superintendent, can no longer stand in the schoolhouse door and block 1,000 low-income children from attending the school of their choice. On Monday, a Wisconsin judge ruled that black Rep. Polly Williams's plan to allow students to use vouchers to attend private schools is constitutional. Judge Susan Steingass ruled invalid Mr. Grover's contention that the voucher plan didn't serve a valid public purpose and was discriminatory in being limited to Milwaukee inner-city schools. She wrote that if choice works, the benefit would flow "not only to a few students in Milwaukee but to our educational system as a whole, both by the lessons learned and the education improved." The judge also ruled that Mr. Grover exceeded his authority in demanding the private schools fill out complex forms and meet other bureaucratic "diktats" not specified in the legislation.

The voucher plan has divided black parents from many liberals who claim to represent them. When a white attorney for the teachers' unions fighting the plan told Judge Steingass the plan wouldn't help minorities, he earned a swift rebuttal from Anne Sulton, the Madison, Wis., NAACP's attorney. "I am offended at that suggestion," she told the court. "I don't see any African-Americans here opposing this plan. I do see a courtroom full of African-American parents supporting increased educational opportunity for their children."
