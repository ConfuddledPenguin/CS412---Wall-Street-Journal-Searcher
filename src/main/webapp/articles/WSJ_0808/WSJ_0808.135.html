U-Haul Heir Wife Slain; Inquiry Focuses on Feud

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/08/90

Eva Berg Shoen, wife of U-Haul heir Dr. Sam Shoen, was murdered in what authorities say resembled "a professional hit," and they are looking for a connection to the family feud over the U-Haul empire. "The controversy surrounding that family {and} U-Haul is certainly an angle we're investigating," says Bill Masters, sheriff of San Miguel County, Colo.

Mrs. Shoen, 43 years old, was shot early Monday in the family home outside Telluride. The murder apparently occurred while her three children slept undisturbed in the house, and Sheriff Masters said it did not appear to be a case of either sexual assault or burglary. "There's something else" going on here, he said. "This doesn't appear to be a local issue."

At the time of the murder, the victim's husband was in Phoenix, Ariz., base of the embattled U-Haul empire. As reported in a recent front-page Wall Street Journal story, Dr. Shoen is battling with certain of his 11 siblings over control of the empire founded by their father, L.S. Shoen.

At present, the company -- a subsidiary of Amerco -- is being run by siblings unfriendly to Dr. Shoen's cause. But a company spokesman denied that the family dispute could have had anything to do with Mrs. Shoen's death. "The whole family is devastated," said the spokesman.

Dr. Shoen is offering a $250,000 reward for information leading to the arrest of his wife's murderer.
