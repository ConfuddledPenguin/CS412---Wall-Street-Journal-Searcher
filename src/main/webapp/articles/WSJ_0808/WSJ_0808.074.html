Yields on CDs Slipped During Latest Week

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/08/90

NEW YORK -- Yields on certificates of deposit at major banks and brokerage firms fell slightly in the week ended Tuesday. The average yield on six-month small-denomination CDs sold directly by banks slipped to 7.42% from 7.53%, according to an 18-bank national survey by Banxquote Money Markets, a New York information service. The average yield for five-year consumer CDs fell to 7.87% from 8.01%.

Average yields on small-denomination CDs sold by leading broker dealers were also down. The exception was the average yield on five-year broker-sold CDs, which edged up to 8.28% from 8.27%. The average yield on a one-year CD fell to 7.72% from 7.80%, while the average for two-year CDs eased to 8.04% from 8.10%, according to Banxquote.

Average yields for "jumbo" CDs, which typically require deposits of $90,000 or more, declined. The average yield on one-year jumbo CDs eased to 7.60% from 7.68%, while the average for five-year maturities slipped to 8.12% from 8.16%.
