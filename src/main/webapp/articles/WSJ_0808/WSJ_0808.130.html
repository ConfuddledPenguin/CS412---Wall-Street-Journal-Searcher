Business Brief -- PWA Corp.: Pact to Sell American Air 2 Boeing Planes Is Canceled

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/08/90

Canadian Airlines International, a unit of PWA Corp., said an agreement to sell two aircraft to American Airlines for 385 million Canadian dollars (US$334.9 million) was canceled because of a U.S. regulatory action. PWA, based in Calgary, Alberta, previously had said it expected a pretax gain of about C$100 million on the sale of the two Boeing 747-400 aircraft. The company reported a C$16.1 million loss in this year's first half.

PWA ordered the two aircraft, still in production, for delivery next February and May, then sold the delivery positions to American on condition that American be awarded the right to serve a route between Chicago and Tokyo. A U.S. administrative law judge recommended last week that the route go to United Airlines instead. American is a unit of AMR Corp., Fort Worth, Texas. United is a unit of Chicago-based UAL Corp.

PWA said it plans to take delivery of the aircraft scheduled for February and seek a buyer for the second plane.
