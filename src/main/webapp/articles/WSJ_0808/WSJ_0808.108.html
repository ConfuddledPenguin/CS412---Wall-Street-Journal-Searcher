Bank of New England To Merge Bank Units For Massachusetts

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/08/90

BOSTON -- Bank of New England Corp. said its small affiliate bank in western Massachusetts will be merged into the bank's main Boston-based affiliate to form one large bank for the state. The merger is the latest step in a series of consolidations the troubled bank has undertaken this year in order to trim staff, streamline management and reorganize assets. In February, it consolidated three eastern Massachusetts affiliates into the bank's large Boston affiliate, called Bank of New England N.A., which currently has $13 billion in assets.

Bank of New England-West, based in Springfield, Mass., has $1 billion in assets, 700 full-time employees and 40 branches offices. The merger will be effective pending approval by the Office of the Comptroller of the Currency. Bank of New England-West's longtime president, C.D. "Buzz" Harrington, had previously announced he will be retiring this summer.

Bank officials said the western bank will eventually be run by a regional president, who will report to Bruce Wheeler, president of Bank of New England N.A.

Since its $1.1 billion loss last year, largely due to bad real estate loans made during its rapid expansion in the 1980s, the regional bank has trimmed 4,000 employees through asset sales, layoffs and consolidations.

"Certainly, we will examine the management situation out there to see if there is any duplication," said bank spokesman James Dorsey, who added that the layoffs from the merger "should be modest."
