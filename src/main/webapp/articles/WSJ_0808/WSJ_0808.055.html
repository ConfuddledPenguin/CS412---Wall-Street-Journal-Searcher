Bank of New York Resigns Position As Primary Dealer

  • Author: Constance Mitchell
  • Date: 08/08/90

NEW YORK -- Another big financial institution resigned as a primary dealer of U.S. securities, citing declining profits. Bank of New York said yesterday that it closed its BNY Securities Inc. unit, and laid off nearly half of the unit's 40 employees. Including Bank of New York, four securities firms have given up or lost their primary-dealer status so far this year, reducing the number of dealers to 41.

Primary dealers are an elite group of big commercial banks and brokerage firms that do business directly with the New York Federal Reserve Bank in buying and selling U.S. government securities that are used to influence national monetary policy. Once one of the most coveted franchises in international finance, the business of primary dealers has become brutally competitive in recent years. As the number of dealers has swelled, profit margins have been trimmed to the bare bones. The Federal Reserve estimates that half of the primary dealers were unprofitable or barely broke even last year.

Some of the resignations of primary dealers have been attributed to the inability of the firms to earn enough from the Treasury market to justify the capitalization of at least $50 million required by the Fed.

Officials at Bank of New York said their operation made money. "It was a profitable business for us, but not profitable enough," said a company spokesman. "The market conditions are such that we are not getting the returns from the business that we require."

The resignation of Bank of New York comes less than two years after it became a primary dealer through the acquisition of Irving Bank. People in the bond market said that Irving had tried to sell the dealership operations of Irving Securities Inc. prior to the merger with Bank of New York, but couldn't find a buyer.

Several other primary dealerships are rumored to be on the block or face closing, several traders said.

Despite the closing of its primary dealer operation, Bank of New York said it will continue trading U.S. government bonds for its own account, as well as other securities.
