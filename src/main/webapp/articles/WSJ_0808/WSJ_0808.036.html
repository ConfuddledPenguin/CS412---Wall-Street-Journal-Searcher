Harris Corp. Expects `Flat' Net in 1st Half Of This Fiscal Year

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/08/90

MELBOURNE, Fla. -- Harris Corp. said that it expects earnings for the first half of the current fiscal year to be "relatively flat" compared with last year's first half. John T. Hartley, chairman and chief executive of the information and communications concern, said, "We're operating in a difficult economic environment that shows no signs of early improvement." In addition, he said earnings in the semiconductor sector are expected to be lower in the first half of the year due to manufacturing consolidations and the discontinuance of nonstrategic product lines.

However, Mr. Hartley added, "Assuming no further deterioriation in the economic environment in which we operate, we expect good earnings growth in the second half of the year, resulting in a solid increase for the year as a whole."

Harris reported net income for the fourth quarter ended June 30 rose 14%, to $39.5 million, or 99 cents a share, compared with profit from continuing operations of $34.8 million, or 90 cents a share. For the year-ago fourth quarter, an $86.8 million loss from discontinued operations resulted in a net loss of $52 million, or $1.34 a share. The company said revenue rose 5%, to $819.4 million in the latest fourth quarter, compared with revenue from continuing operations of $778 million a year earlier.

Harris also said its board approved the repurchase of as many as 1.5 million of its common shares in the open market. It said the shares will be used for general corporate purposes. Harris has 39.6 million common shares outstanding.
