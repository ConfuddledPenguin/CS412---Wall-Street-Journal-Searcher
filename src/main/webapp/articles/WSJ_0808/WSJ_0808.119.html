International Brief -- Saga Petroleum AS: Oil Concern's Pretax Profit More Than Doubled in Half

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/08/90

Saga Petroleum AS said pretax profit more than doubled in the first half and predicted pretax profit for all of 1990 would rise 29%. But the Norwegian oil concern's forecast assumes a conservative 120 Norwegian kroner a barrel average price for crude oil, roughly $18.50 a barrel at exchange rates prevailing for most of the year, according to a Saga spokesman. Industry analysts predicted that Iraq's invasion of Kuwait would push Saga's average crude price higher, probably to between $22 and $25 a barrel.

Under that scenario, analysts added, Saga's 1990 pretax profit would nearly triple, making the Oslo-based company one of Europe's biggest winners from skyrocketing oil prices.

First-half pretax profit soared to 389 million kroner ($63.6 million) from 151 million kroner a year earlier. Operating profit jumped to 494 million kroner from 394 million kroner. Expenses were halved, falling to 105 million kroner from 243 million kroner a year earlier. Revenue rose 12.7% to 1.28 billion kroner.

Saga directors said they expect fullyear operating profit to reach between 800 million kroner and 900 million kroner, compared with 760 million kroner last year.
