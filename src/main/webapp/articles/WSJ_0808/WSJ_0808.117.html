International Brief -- Kirin Brewery Co.: Unconsolidated Pretax Profit For First Half Rose 30%

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/08/90

Kirin Brewery Co., Japan's largest brewer, said first-half unconsolidated pretax profit climbed 30% to 34.77 billion yen ($231.2 million) from 26.73 billion yen a year earlier. Net income jumped 49% to 15.86 billion yen, or 15.81 yen a share, from 10.62 billion yen, or 11.16 yen a share. Net grew faster than pretax earnings, as extraordinary losses narrowed to 1.83 billion yen from 3.27 billion yen a year earlier.

Sales rose 8.7% to 586.94 billion yen from 539.81 billion yen.

Officials said demand for beer was strong in the latest period, pushing sales volume up to post an average of 8% year-to-year growth among Japanese breweries, despite a rise in beer prices that went into effect last March. Kirin's first-half beer sales rose 6.9% to 524.48 billion yen from 490.56 billion yen.
