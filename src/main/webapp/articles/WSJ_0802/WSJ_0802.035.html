Foley Condemns the IRA For Killing of Politician

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/02/90

WASHINGTON -- Speaker Thomas Foley, in a strongly worded statement to the full House, condemned the "terror tactics" of the Irish Republican Army and "cowardly murder" of Ian Gow, a British conservative killed this week by a bomb planted in his car. "The clear and despicable purpose of this callous act was intimidation through terror," said the Washington State Democrat, who broke with custom by speaking from the well of the House immediately after the chamber convened yesterday. Surprised by the speaker's appearance, members sat in unusual silence but then rose in applause at the conclusion of his remarks.

"The IRA's guns and bombs have not brought independence for Northern Ireland, nor will they," said Mr. Foley, one of the most prominent Irish-American officeholders in the U.S. "What they have brought -- in the years since British troops were sent to Northern Ireland in 1972 -- is the deaths of many more civilian bystanders than of soldiers or policemen, the deaths of many more Catholics than Protestants, in fact, many more deaths than can be attributed to the British."

A supporter of the 1985 Anglo-Irish accord giving the Republic of Ireland a consultative role in the governing of Northern Ireland, Mr. Foley has disagreed with Mr. Gow on a number of Irish issues. But he was described as deeply affected by the killing and the threat it posed to Parliament.
