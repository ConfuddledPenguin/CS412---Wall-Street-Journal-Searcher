Southern Pacific Ends Bid for Soo Rail Line, Gets Trackage Rights

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 08/02/90

Southern Pacific Transportation Co. said it terminated an agreement with Soo Line Railroad Co. to purchase Soo's rail line connecting Kansas City and Chicago; however, the company said it reached a trackage rights agreement with Burlington Northern Inc. that would give it a Chicago route. Financially troubled Southern Pacific, a San Francisco unit of Denver-based Rio Grande Industries, had sought to acquire its own direct rail route to Chicago to better compete with Western rail rivals.

But the company said it failed to gain the consent of Chicago & North Western Transportation Co., which has joint ownership of a small portion of the line. Southern Pacific had planned to spend $86 million to purchase the 532-mile route from Soo and another $50 million to renovate the track over three years.

Having called off the agreement to purchase the line, Southern Pacific said it reached a trackage rights agreement with Burlington Northern. Under the agreement, Southern Pacific will operate trains with its own locomotives and crews on Burlington Northern's 466-mile route between Kansas City and Chicago. Southern Pacific declined to discuss terms.

Soo Line, a wholly owned subsidiary of Canadian Pacific Ltd., said the proposed sale of its line to Southern Pacific had received unanimous approval from the Interstate Commerce Commission. But Soo said it faced "excessive economic demands" from Chicago & North Western for consent to Soo's transfer of ownership of a 37-mile portion of the line. CNW Corp., Chicago & North Western's parent, declined to comment.
