Casino Revenue In Atlantic City Was Flat in July

  • Author: Mitchell Pacelle
  • Date: 08/02/90

Despite the addition of $40.5 million in July gambling revenue from the new Trump Taj Mahal, the Atlantic City casino industry saw a growth of only 0.7% last month compared with a year earlier. The preliminary July results, released by the Casino Association of New Jersey, wiped out hopes that the Taj would permanently increase the size of the gambling market. "The market is dead flat and the overall industry in New Jersey is struggling," acknowledged Thomas Carver, president of the association.

Revenue at the Trump Castle plunged 28% from a year ago to $21.4 million, the steepest decline among the city's 12 casinos; the Trump Plaza's revenue dropped almost 24% to $23.4 million. These sharp drops suggest that the Taj continues to cannibalize Trump's other gambling properties.

Elsewhere in city, revenue at Bally's Grand dropped 20% to $18.5 million; Harrah's plunged 17% to $24.2 million; Tropworld fell 16% to $26.5 million; Resorts was down 12% to $21.2 million; Bally's Park Place dropped 11% to $25.7 million; the Showboat's revenue fell 7.8% to $23.6 million; and Caesars slipped 6.8% to $30.5 million.

The only casinos to register increases were the Claridge, where revenue jumped 13% to $13.3 million, and the Sands, where revenue edged up 2% to $21.6 million.

"We are very discouraged by the less than 1% growth in casino gross revenues," said Mr. Carver, adding: "The decrease in gross revenues for most casinos coupled with increased operating expenses spells double trouble."

Pointing out that the association's July figures were based on projections from actual revenue through July 28, Al Glasgow, a gambling industry consultant, predicted that when the final July figures are tabulated, the industry will show either flat revenue or a slight decline.
