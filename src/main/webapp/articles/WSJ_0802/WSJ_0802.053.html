Italian Firms Get Contract

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/02/90

ROME -- A group of Italian state-owned companies has been awarded a contract to build a sewage-treatment plant near Cairo, Egypt, it was announced. The contract was valued at 320 billion lire ($276 million), the companies said.

Engineering company Ansaldo S.p.A. and public works company Condotte S.p.A., both owned by Istituto per la Ricostruzione Industriale (IRI), a state holding company, said in a statement the sewage-treatment plant will have a capacity of 1.1 million cubic meters of water a day.
