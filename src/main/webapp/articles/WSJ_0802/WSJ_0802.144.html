What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/02/90

THE ECONOMY REMAINS stagnant, three monthly reports suggest. The leading indicators index and construction spending were unchanged in June, and the purchasers' index of manufacturing health fell in July. The data fueled speculation by some about a possible recession.

Bond prices rose on the gloomy economic reports, as did utility stocks and gold. Stocks in general slumped, while the dollar reversed its slide.

---

Kodak's profit jumped in the second quarter, as cost cutting yielded savings sooner than analysts expected. The company's stock surged $4.25 to close at $42.375 a share.

---

IBM will set up its office-products business as a separate unit, preparing for its sale to Clayton & Dubilier for a sum estimated at nearly $2.3 billion. The deal will enable IBM to cut its staff by nearly 6,000 people.

---

Salomon plans further cutbacks at its Philipp Brothers commodity firm and may consider selling the unprofitable unit, Salomon executives say.

---

Chicago Board of Trade Chairman Karsten Mahlmann resigned after weeks of criticism of his role as head of financially crippled Stotler Group. Vice Chairman William O'Connor was elected to succeed Mahlmann.

---

The thrift-bailout agency intends to accelerate its sale of seized S&L assets, targeting $50 billion by year end.

---

AT&T, Nynex and Ameritech plan to develop a radio technology that could significantly increase the capacity of cellular radio systems.

---

Pathe Cinema will remain French, as Giancarlo Parretti and Banque Rivaud agreed to sell it to Chargeurs for $207.3 million, ending Parretti's bid to buy all of the film company.

---

A $54 billion farm bill cleared the House. The five-year measure would end a decline in income supports, but tighten limits on aid to individuals.

---

Japan agreed to give U.S. firms more opportunities to sell telecommunications gear and data-transmission services to Japanese customers.

---

Oil prices soared on reports of renewed tension between Iraq and Kuwait. The price of crude for delivery next month jumped 85 cents a barrel.

---

Bush is threatening to step up attacks on Democrats about the budget, which could hurt chances of a deficit-cutting compromise this fall.

---

Washington Bancorp. of D.C. sought protection from creditors. The firm expects that holders "will be unable to realize value" for their stock.

---

Purchase prices for small firms fell further in the first half of this year, as regulators and recession fears led banks to tighten financing.
