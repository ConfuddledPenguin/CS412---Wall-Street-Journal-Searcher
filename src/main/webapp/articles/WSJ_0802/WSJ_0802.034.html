Twentieth Century Fox Is Sued by `Aliens' Group

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/02/90

LOS ANGELES -- The star, director and producers of the hit science-fiction film "Aliens" filed suit against Twentieth Century Fox Film Corp., charging that the movie company didn't pay them their fair share of the profit. The suit was filed in Los Angeles Superior Court by actress Sigourney Weaver, director James Cameron, producer Gail Anne Hurd and executive producers Gordon Carroll, Walter Hill and David Giler. They alleged that Fox failed to include all the film's revenue in account statements and made improper expense deductions.

Fox, a unit of Australia-based News Corp., also allegedly set aside improper and excess reserves and provided inaccurate accountings, the suit charged.

The complaint seeks unspecified damages, a judicial determination that the film is now in net profit and an accounting. Ms. Weaver claims she was to receive 10% of the 1986 film's net profit. "Aliens" was the sequel to the 1979 science fiction hit, "Alien," about monsters in deep space.

A spokesman for Fox said the company had no comment on the suit.
