International: Italy's Lower House Clears Bill on Media Ownership

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/02/90

MILAN, Italy -- The Chamber of Deputies approved a bill that restricts private ownership of Italy's press and television and establishes strict guidelines for media advertising. The bill now returns to the Senate, Italy's upper house, where it's expected to have an easy passage. The measure strikes at the heart of issues -- media financing and ownership -- that are intricately bound up with both the wielding of political influence in Italy and the financing of its political parties. It limits cross-ownership of television stations and newspapers, particularly affecting the media empire of Silvio Berlusconi, who controls three television stations and the Milan-based daily Il Giornale.

Newspaper owners controlling more than 16% of the national market will be banned from owning national television stations, while those controlling between 8% and 16% of the market will be restricted to one such station. Those with less than 8% will be permitted to control two stations. Groups with no papers will be allowed up to three stations. Officials of Mr. Berlusconi's company, Fininvest S.p.A., say the bill means he has to give up Il Giornale, and lose $86 million a year in advertising.

The measure also limits the advertising breaks during drama programs, restricts the showing of sexually explicit material and requires private TV and radio owners to broadcast news items.
