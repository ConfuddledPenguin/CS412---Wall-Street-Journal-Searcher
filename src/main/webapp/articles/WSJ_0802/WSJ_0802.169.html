Business Brief -- Aifs Inc.: Majority of Debt Holders Approve Buy-Out Proposal

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/02/90

Aifs Inc., San Francisco, said a majority of its debenture holders approved a previously proposed buy-out of the company's shareholders and that it had settled class-action lawsuits challenging the transaction. As previously reported, a group led by Chief Executive Officer Roger O. Walther is proposing to acquire the company for $6.50 a share. Aifs is an operator of cultural and educational exchange programs for students. The group hopes to complete the transaction in September.

Aifs said a majority of holders of its $10 million outstanding in debentures had backed the plan after the company agreed to boost the interest rate on the debentures to 13.5% from 11.25% and to offer to repurchase one-quarter of the debentures at 90% of par value plus interest for a period of 90 days following the buy-out's completion.

The company also said the Delaware Court of Chancery approved a previously reported settlement of shareholder lawsuits challenging the buy-out, which was originally proposed at $5.25 a share. Mr. Walther said the court granted $125,000 in attorney fees for the plaintiffs.

In composite trading yesterday on the American Stock Exchange, Aifs stock closed at $6.375, up 25 cents.
