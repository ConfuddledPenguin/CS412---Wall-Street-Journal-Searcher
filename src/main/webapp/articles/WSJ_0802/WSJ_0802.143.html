Who's News: Kidder Peabody Picks Managing Director To Head Sales Force

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/02/90

NEW YORK -- Kidder, Peabody & Co. named a new head of its national sales force to jumpstart its strategy of focusing on wealthy individuals, small companies and small institutions. The General Electric Co. unit named managing director Michael A.M. Keehner to succeed Charles V. Sheehan in overseeing Kidder's 45 branches that cater to individual investors.

In an interview, Mr. Keehner said he hopes to be "getting our people better focused" and help the sales force better target wealthy markets with the proper resources and support.

The 47-year-old Mr. Keehner, currently chairman of Kidder, Peabody International Ltd., has never been a broker. But he has held a variety of management positions at the firm. Among other things, he has headed Kidder's asset finance group, and was president and chief executive officer of the firm's exploration and production subsidiary from 1982 to 1988. He also was chairman of Kidder Peabody Realty.

Kidder, which has 1,400 brokers, has never been a major force in the retail-investor market. But it recently has been trying to carve out a niche in focusing on the higher end of the individual-investor market, to separate itself from big retail firms such as Merrill Lynch & Co. and Shearson Lehman Brothers Inc.

In a statement, Michael A. Carpenter, Kidder's chairman and chief executive, cited the "depth of his management experience across many areas of the firm and his strategic capabilities."

Mr. Keehner will assume his new duties at the end of the year, when Mr. Sheehan retires.
