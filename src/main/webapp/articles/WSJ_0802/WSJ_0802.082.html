Cigna Profit Rose In Quarter by 7%; Travelers Net Fell

  • Author: Beatrice E. Garcia
  • Date: 08/02/90

Cigna Corp.'s second-quarter net income rose 7% to $158 million, or $2.05 a share, while Travelers Corp.'s net dropped nearly 11% to $102 million, or 98 cents a share. While both companies saw earnings increase for their employee benefits and group-health business, the continued pricing war in the property/casualty area hurt their results.

"The lack of improvement in property and casualty industry pricing conditions will constrain both 1990 and 1991 results, said Wilson H. Taylor, Cigna's chairman and chief executive officer. Cigna, based in Philadelphia, said property/casualty operating profit fell to $17 million from $27 million a year ago.

As a way of improving results in this market segment, Cigna plans to accelerate the previously announced consolidation of its domestic property/casualty field offices. Later this year, the company expects to take a charge of no more than $65 million, on an after-tax basis, to reflect these consolidation costs.

Consolidated revenue at Cigna rose 26% to $4.8 billion in the second quarter. For the six months, net fell 13% to $200 million on revenue of $8.7 billion, which rose 16% from the 1989 first half.

Travelers Chairman Edward H. Budd said that the company has taken on a number of programs to reduce operating costs and improve the quality of its book of business in the property/casualty area. During the second quarter, the company restructured its agent commission schedule to provide agents with incentives for bringing in higher-quality automobile insurance business. Travelers, based in Hartford, Conn., also announced plans to withdraw from the personal lines market from nine states.

While net from Travelers' commercial property/casualty business rose 23% to $65 million, partly due to realized capital gains of $20 million, earnings from its personal property/casualty lines plunged to $300,000 from $6 million a year earlier.

Travelers also said its results were adversely affected by continued underperforming real estate assets. While real estate conditions had improved in the Southwest, the company said markets continued to soften in other areas. The company is continuing its program for restructuring loans, property sales and the management of foreclosed properties to maximize value longer term.

Travelers' second-quarter revenue fell to $3 billion from $3.2 billion a year ago. For the six months, earnings fell to $193 million, or $1.85 a share, from $212 million, or $2.06 a share.

In composite New York Stock Exchange trading yesterday, Cigna closed at $47.25, up 25 cents, while Travelers closed unchanged at $27.375. The news concerning Cigna was released after Big Board trading had ended for the day.
