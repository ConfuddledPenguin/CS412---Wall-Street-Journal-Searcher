Technology Brief -- Compaq Computer Corp.: Prices on Laptop PCs Cut To Maintain Market Share

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/02/90

Compaq Computer Corp. said it cut prices on its laptop personal computers in a move to hold onto its commanding share of the battery-powered lightweight models. Compaq dropped its suggested retail price by $500 and $800 on its LTE/286 models, and by $300 and $400 on its LTE models. The Compaq PCs now list for prices ranging from $1,999 to $3,999, compared with the previous range of $2,399 to $4,799. The Compaq models account for 65% of retail sales of laptop computers, according to surveys by Storeboard/Computer Intelligence, a market research firm.

Competitors are now offering laptops as powerful and as light as the Compaq models, said Eugene Glazer, an analyst with Dean Witter Reynolds.

Compaq also cut prices on Advanced Graphics System components.

Meanwhile, Houston-based Compaq said it opened its 18th sales subsidiary in Hong Kong to serve dealers in Hong Kong, South Korea, mainland China and Taiwan. In the first half of this year, Compaq sales abroad were larger than its sales in North America.
