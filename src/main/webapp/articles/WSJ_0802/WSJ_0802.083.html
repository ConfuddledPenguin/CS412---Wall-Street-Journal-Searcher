Grand Jury Indicts Two Former Managers Of Hughes Aircraft

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/02/90

PHOENIX, Ariz. -- Two former managers of Hughes Aircraft Co.'s Missile Systems Group who worked at a plant in Tucson were indicted on charges of concealing information about disposal of hazardous wastes. They are Robert E. Morrison of Longmont, Colo., former manager of environment and energy programs of the Hughes manufacturing facility at Air Force Plant No. 44, and Jay L. Barnett of Tucson, former industrial wastewater treatment plant supervisor at the plant. Hughes Aircraft is a unit of General Motors Corp.

The two men were charged by the U.S. Justice Department and the Arizona attorney general in a five-count indictment that alleges conspiracy to defraud the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency and omitting material information or making false representations about hazardous waste incidents at the plant to government regulators and inspectors.

According to the indictment, hazardous wastes were released from plant units supervised by the two men. If found guilty on all counts, both defendants could be sentenced to 16 years imprisonment and fined as much as $1.2 million.

The two defendants couldn't be reached for comment. A spokesman for Hughes Aircraft said the company participated in the investigation and cooperated with the government and wasn't named in the indictment. The spokesman said the company had no comment on the indictment.
