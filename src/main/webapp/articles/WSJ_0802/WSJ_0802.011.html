Letters to the Editor: Grass-Roots Origins Of Sexist Bigotry

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/02/90

I was outraged that you published and consequently endorsed Dave Shiflett's review "Bookshorts: Help for the Suburban Yard Slave" (Leisure & Arts, July 17). In the guise of a book review on how-to manuals for lawn-mower repairs, the author has found a rather unimaginative way to divulge his prehistoric sexist thinking. I am disappointed that the editors of The Wall Street Journal did not recognize this rhetoric as offensive bigotry. The article, based on the premise that man is inherently "oppressed" by woman, is blatantly insolent in its attempt to make a potentially dull subject humorous. It is not difficult to imagine Mr. Shiflett to be a morally inept, social outcast in any slightly progressive society. His article became quickly ineffectual in reviewing the manuals because of the emotional response it provoked by attacking women as being a race of low-IQ slave drivers who sit indoors packing themselves with carbohydrates.

What a pity it must be to live in Mr. Shiflett's world of fat, screaming wives who demand a manicured lawn before they can feel at peace with themselves and their marriages. I find little in common with the people who must live on Mr. Shiflett's little suburban block and I am sure that most of your well-educated and enterprising readers would agree.

Susan P. Woods

Curatorial Assistant

Whitney Museum of American Art

New York
