The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report. CORPORATE

United Illuminating Co. -- $211.8 million of Seabrook I nuclear power plant secured lease-obligation bonds through underwriters Morgan Stanley & Co. Although the bonds are technically issued by Meridian Bank, the so-called owner-trustee in the transaction, they are considered obligations of United Illuminating. The first part, $82.8 million of secured lease-obligation bonds due Jan. 2, 2006, was priced at par to yield 9.76%. The bonds, which are non-callable for five years, were priced at a spread of 1.42 percentage points above the Treasury's 10-year note. The issue has a sinking fund beginning Jan. 2, 1991, giving it an average life of 10.4 years. The second part, $129 million of secured-lease obligation bonds due Jan. 2, 2020, was priced at par to yield 10.24%. The bonds, which are non-callable for five years, were priced at 1.83 percentage points above the Treasury's 30-year bond. The issue has a sinking fund beginning Jan. 2, 2006, giving it an average life of 24.6 years. The issue is rated Baa-3 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and triple-Bminus by Standard & Poor's Corp.

MUNICIPAL

Maryland Department of Transportation -- $100 million of consolidated transportation revenue bonds, Series 1990, apparently won by a Goldman, Sachs & Co. group. The issue, expected to receive a rating of double-A from Moody's and S&P, was priced for reoffering to yield from 6.00% in 1993 to 6.80% in 2005.

EUROBONDS

GMAC Canada Ltd. (U.S. parent) -- 125 million Canadian dollars (US$108.5 million) of 11 7/8% bonds due Sept. 7, 1995, priced at 101 1/4 via Scotia McLeod.

Eksportfinans AS (Norway) -- 100 million Australian dollars (US$79.5 million) of 14 1/2% bonds due Sept. 7, 1993, priced at 101.95 to yield 14.30% less fees via Westpac Banking Corp. Fees 1 1/2.

Sharp International Finance (U.K.) (Japanese parent) -- 10 billion yen ($68.6 million) of 7 5/8% bonds due Aug. 16, 1994, priced at 101 1/2 via Daiwa Europe Ltd. Guaranteed by Sharp Corp.
