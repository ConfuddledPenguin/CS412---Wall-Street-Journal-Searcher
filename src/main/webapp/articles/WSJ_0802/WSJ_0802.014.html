Repsol Posts Rise in Earnings

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/02/90

MADRID -- Spain's state-controlled oil concern Repsol S.A. said first-half profit rose 1.4% to 39.68 billion pesetas ($406.6 million) from 39.14 billion pesetas a year earlier, but called the results "positive," in light of the "extraordinarily strong" 1989 first-half results and lower crude oil prices. Revenue fell 11% to 58.455 billion pesetas from 65.413 billion pesetas. But Repsol said that without an 11 billion-peseta depreciation on the value of its crude-oil and oil-products inventories, revenue would have totaled more than 69 billion pesetas.

Repsol predicted that higher world oil prices will boost second-half revenue, and said a move by the Spanish government partly to deregulate gasoline prices will help lift earnings.
