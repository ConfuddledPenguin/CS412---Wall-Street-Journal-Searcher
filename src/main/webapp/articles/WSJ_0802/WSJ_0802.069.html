U.S. Will Need $63.5 Billion Cash In Third Quarter

  • Author: Alan Murray
  • Date: 08/02/90

WASHINGTON -- The federal government's borrowing needs continue to explode as a result of sluggish economic growth and the cash demands of the savings-and-loan bailout. The Treasury Department reported that the government will need to borrow a record $63.5 billion in the third quarter to finance the general needs of the government as well as the S&L bailout.

The government also said it will need to borrow $65 billion to $70 billion in the fourth quarter, excluding the money needed to provide working capital for the thrift bailout. The amount of working capital that will be needed in the fourth quarter for the S&L bailout isn't yet known, but it could push total government borrowing over the $100 billion mark.

To help fill this massive financing gap in the current quarter, the Treasury said it plans to sell $32.5 billion of notes and bonds this month to refund $20 billion of notes maturing on Aug. 15 and to raise $12.25 billion in cash.

The new securities will consist of:

-- $11.5 billion of three-year notes, to be auctioned next Tuesday;

-- $10.5 billion of 10-year notes, to be auctioned next Wednesday;

-- $10.25 billion of 30-year bonds, to be auctioned next Thursday.

The three-year notes will mature on Aug. 15, 1993, the 10-year notes will mature Aug. 15, 2000, and the 30-year bonds will mature Aug. 15, 2020.

The Treasury noted that it may have to change its auction schedule if Congress has not raised the limit on federal debt by Aug. 6.

The Treasury said it also will sell $10 billion of 36-day cash management bills next Thursday.

Including next week's refunding, the Treasury will have raised $40.1 billion of its $63.5 billion borrowing needs. The remaining $23.4 billion will be raised through the sale of regular 13-week, 26-week, and 52-week bills, as well as a two-year note in August and a five-year note in early September. The department said it may be necessary to sell additional cash management bills in September.

The Treasury said the next auction of Refcorp bonds to finance the S&L bailout will be auctioned Oct. 9 and settled Oct. 16.
