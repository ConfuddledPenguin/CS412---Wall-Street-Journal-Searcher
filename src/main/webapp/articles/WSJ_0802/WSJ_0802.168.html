Business Brief -- Daisy Systems Corp.: Firm Files for Protection Under Bankruptcy Statutes

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/02/90

Daisy Systems Corp., responding to growing creditor pressure, said it filed for protection under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code. The filing, in San Jose, Calif., federal bankruptcy court, responded to an involuntary bankruptcy petition filed in a Denver court against the company by certain bondholders last May.

Daisy, once a leading maker of computer-aided engineering systems, has been dogged for about a year by disappointing sales as well as a crushing debt load associated with its $200 million acquisition of Cadnetix Corp. last year. It said it has about $40 million in secured debt and $73 million of debentures outstanding.

Daisy, based in Sunnyvale, Calif., said it hopes to implement a restructuring that includes a "substantial" reduction in its U.S. operations and work force.

It added that it hired McGettigan, Wick & Co., a San Francisco investment banking firm, to explore an eventual sale of the company or its remaining assets.
