Law: ABA Head Reports Theft Of $55,000 Held for Client

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/02/90

CHICAGO -- The annual meeting of the American Bar Association in Chicago is off to an unusual start. Tuesday night, on the eve of the convention, ABA President Stanley Chauvin discovered that $55,000 he had placed in a hotel lockbox was missing.

Mr. Chauvin, a 55-year-old Louisville, Ky., attorney, received the money from a client Sunday night "in an escrow transaction" in Louisville, a spokesman for the ABA said. Lawyers in civil practice are frequently involved in escrow transactions, in which money is held on behalf of clients during certain proceedings. Such transactions, however, are not usually conducted in cash. Because banks weren't open Sunday and he was leaving for Chicago early Monday morning, Mr. Chauvin took $55,000 in $100 bills with him on the plane to Chicago.

Upon his arrival at the Hyatt Regency Hotel, he rented a deposit box and put a cloth bag containing the bills into it. Tuesday night, Mr. Chauvin checked on the money and found the box empty, the ABA spokesman said. He wouldn't identify the client involved, citing attorney-client privilege.

Mr. Chauvin wasn't available for comment yesterday. No one at his Louisville firm, Alagia, Day, Marshall, Mintmire & Chauvin, would comment.

A hotel spokesman said that it takes two keys to open the deposit box -- the key of the renter and another key held by hotel employees.

The box didn't appear to have been broken into, said Sgt. Booker Moore, a policeman called to the scene to investigate the reported theft.
