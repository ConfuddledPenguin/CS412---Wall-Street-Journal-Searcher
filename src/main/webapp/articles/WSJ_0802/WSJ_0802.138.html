Business Brief -- McDonnell Douglas Corp.: Shelf Registration Is Filed For $1.15 Billion in Debt

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/02/90

McDonnell Douglas Corp. said it filed a shelf registration with the Securities and Exchange Commission for $1.15 billion in senior and/or subordinated debt securities. Including the unissued portion of an earlier shelf registration, the St. Louis-based defense contractor said it will be able to issue up to a total of $1.5 billion in senior and/or subordinated debt securities.

The aerospace concern said it intends to use the net proceeds from the sale of securities to finance inventories, provide other working capital, repay debt and for general corporate purposes.

Last week, Standard & Poor's Corp. and Moody's Investors Service Inc. each lowered their ratings on debt of McDonnell Douglas, citing its weakened financial condition and the troubles it is having with military programs, such as cost overruns on the Navy's A-12 attack plane. According to S&P, total debt of McDonnell Douglas and its finance unit is $5.2 billion.
