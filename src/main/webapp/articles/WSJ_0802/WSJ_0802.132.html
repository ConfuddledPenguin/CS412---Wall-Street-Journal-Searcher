International Brief -- Oakbridge Ltd.: Takeover Fight for Concern Intensifies With New Move

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/02/90

The contest to take over Australian coal miner Oakbridge Ltd. intensified as National Mutual Life Association of Australasia Ltd. withdrew its acceptance of Elders Resources NZFP Ltd.'s offer. Independent directors of Oakbridge told Oakbridge holders who had already accepted Elders Resources' conditional offer they can withdraw their acceptance if Elders Resources doesn't achieve compulsory acquisition of all Oakbridge shares.

Elders Resources is attempting to fend off a rival bid from a Japanese-Australian consortium led by TNT Ltd.'s coal mining associate McIlwraith McEacharn Ltd. McIlwraith leads a consortium with Nippon Oil Ltd. and Toyo Menka Kaisha Ltd. that wants to take over Elders Resources' controlling stake in Oakbridge.

McIlwraith raised its bid for Oakbridge this week to 91 Australian cents (72 U.S. cents) a share from A88 cents a share. Elders Resources then lifted its conditional bid to A91 cents a share.

Elders Resources is offering A88 cents a share, as well as a conditional A91 cents a share if it reaches the 90% acceptance level required for compulsory acquisition of the shares outstanding.

National Mutual withdrew its acceptance of Elders Resources' offer for National Mutual's 3.5% stake in Oakbridge.
