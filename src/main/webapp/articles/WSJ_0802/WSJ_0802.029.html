International: EC Commission Negotiates On Cut in Airbus Subsidies

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 08/02/90

BRUSSELS -- European Community Commission sources said the EC may substantially cut production subsidies to the European consortium Airbus Industrie to try to resolve a long dispute with the U.S. The sources said commission negotiators, acting on behalf of the four countries involved in the consortium -- Britain, France, Spain and West Germany -- have offered to cut all production subsidies on aircraft with more than 100 seats. This category would include the Airbus 330 and 340 aircraft that are at the heart of the dispute.

Figures weren't immediately available on the amount of the subsidies covered by the EC offer.

The U.S. trade delegation in Washington has indicated that if the commission made such a move, it wouldn't take the issue to the Geneva-based General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade.

The U.S.'s original deadline for resolving the dispute was Tuesday, but now negotiations between the two sides are set to continue.

Sources say the commission is also offering to cut subsidies for development costs by as much as 50%. The U.S. wants no more than 35% of the costs subsidized.
