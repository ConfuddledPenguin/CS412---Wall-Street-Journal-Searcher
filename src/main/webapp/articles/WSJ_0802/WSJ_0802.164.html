Business Brief -- Reliance Group Holdings Inc.: Gain From Unit's Sale Lifts Firm's Net to $164.8 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/02/90

Reliance Group Holdings Inc. reported second-quarter net income of $164.8 million, or $2.20 a share, largely because of a one-time gain from the sale of the insurance company's General Casualty subsidiary. In the year-earlier period, Reliance had a loss of $6 million, or 8 cents a share. Revenue in the recent quarter rose 26%, to $1.15 billion from $911 million.

The sale of General Casualty, completed in April, resulted in an after-tax gain of $245.5 million. Another extraordinary gain of $4.6 million resulted from early debt retirement, but the gains were offset somewhat by losses totaling $80.7 million from the write-down and sale of various portfolio holdings.

New York-based Reliance blamed soft pricing and losses from recent fires around Santa Barbara, Calif., for a drop in profitability in its property and casuality insurance operations, its largest lines of business.

For the six months, Reliance reported net income of $174.4 million, or $2.33 a share, compared with $21.1 million, or 28 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue rose 17.5% to $2.08 billion.
