Canadian Market Pulp Output in June Fell 15% From Year Earlier

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/02/90

MONTREAL -- Canadian production of market pulp in June dropped 15% from a year earlier, as the industry operated at 80% of capacity. The Canadian Pulp and Paper Association, an industry group, said Canadian mills produced 500,000 metric tons of market pulp in June, compared with 590,000 tons a year earlier. A metric ton is equal to about 2,204.62 pounds.

The June operating rate of 80% was down from 85% in May and from 96% a year earlier.

For the six months through June, Canadian mills produced 3,251,000 metric tons of market pulp, a 9.1% decline from 3,578,000 tons a year earlier. The industry operated at 86% of capacity in the first half, down from 97% a year earlier.

Market pulp is wood pulp sold on the open market to producers of paper, sanitary and other products. The statistics exclude pulp consumed at the producing mill or shipped to another mill that is affiliated with the producing mill. Canada is the world's largest producer of market pulp.

Total market shipments in June dropped 19% to 510,000 metric tons from 632,000 tons a year earlier. Shipments to all markets were down.

Market shipments in the first half dropped 14% to 3,038,000 metric tons from 3,549,000 tons a year earlier.

Canadian producer inventories at the end of June totaled 573,000 metric tons, a 35-day supply, compared with 583,000 tons, a 35-day supply, at the end of May, and 268,000 tons, a 13-day supply, at the end of June 1989.
