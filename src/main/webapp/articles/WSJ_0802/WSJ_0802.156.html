Investor Group Will Buy Nautilus Sports/Medical

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/02/90

ROANOKE, Va. -- A group of private investors said they agreed to buy Nautilus Sports/Medical Industries Inc. from Travis Ward International Inc. for a price in excess of $14 million, according to a Nautilus spokesman. The investors include Dan Baldwin, former president and chief executive officer of Nautilus; Charles Ho, owner, TKH Holdings Ltd. in Sarasota, Fla,; Jim Patterson, Louisville, Ky., founder of Rally's, Long John Silver's and Chi Chi's restaurants; and Les Aberson, a Louisville attorney.

Nautilus, a maker of exercise machinery, is based in Dallas. The company's headquarters will be moved to Independence, Va., the site of its manufacturing facility, said John Urmston, chief operating officer.

Founded in 1970 by Arthur Jones, the company gained a reputation as the premier producer of fitness equipment. In the mid-1980s, however, sales began to slide, and Mr. Jones sold the company to Travis Ward, a Dallas investor. Mr. Urmston said current revenue and earnings figures for Nautilus weren't available.
