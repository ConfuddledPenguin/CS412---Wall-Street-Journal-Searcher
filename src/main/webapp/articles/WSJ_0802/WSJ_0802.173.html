Two Big Shareholders Of Bruxelles Lambert, Pargesa to Pool Stakes

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/02/90

MONTREAL -- Paul Desmarais Sr., a Canadian financier, and Albert Frere of Belgium completed an agreement to pool their combined controlling stakes in two big European holding companies, Pargesa Holding S.A. and Groupe Bruxelles Lambert S.A. Messrs. Desmarais and Frere, through companies they control, together hold about 50% of Geneva-based Pargesa. Mr. Frere holds about 8% of Brussels-based GBL, and Pargesa holds 37%.

Under the agreement, Messrs. Desmarais and Frere will put their Pargesa stakes into a new holding company and Mr. Frere's GBL stake into a second new holding company. Each new company will be equally owned by the two partners.

An official at Power Financial Corp., a subsidiary of Desmarais-controlled Power Corp. of Canada, said the Desmarais and Frere groups will act together at Pargesa and GBL, adopting common positions on matters such as board and management representation, financing activities and purchases and sales of assets.

"It's essentially a joint venture," the Power Financial official said. "We will achieve decisions by consensus."

The agreement runs for 11 years and can be renewed for five-year terms by mutual agreement. Each partner has a right of first refusal to buy the other's interests in Pargesa or GBL for five years after the termination of the agreement, the Power Financial official said.

Messrs. Desmarais and Frere said in February that they held 50% of Pargesa and intended to work together closely.

The Power Financial official said yesterday that the partners "will be concentrating on what we see as our core assets" at Pargesa and GBL. He wouldn't elaborate.
