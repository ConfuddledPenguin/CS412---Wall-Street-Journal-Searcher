Deloitte to Fight Lawsuit Concerning Failed Thrift

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/02/90

The accounting firm of Deloitte & Touche will "vigorously" defend itself against a lawsuit filed in connection with its work as auditor of the failed FirstSouth Savings & Loan Association of Pine Bluff, Ark., a spokeswoman for the firm said. The suit, filed by the Federal Deposit Insurance Corp. last week in federal court in Little Rock, Ark., seeks a minimum of $400 million in damages from the accounting firm for allegedly faulty audits performed by one of its predecessors, Deloitte Haskins & Sells.

Deloitte Haskins, which merged last December with Touche Ross & Co., had performed audits of FirstSouth's books in 1983, 1984 and 1985.

The suit specifically accuses Deloitte of ignoring "grossly deficient underwriting" of loans and letters of credit at the thrift; of failing to recognize that the thrift was delaying the recording of losses on nonperforming loans by "parking" the loans with insiders; and of failing to detect violations of federal limits on loans to one borrower.

The new lawsuit against Deloitte is the most recent of the government's aggressive efforts to recoup some of its losses from the failure of FirstSouth. Last year, the government obtained $27.6 million through separate settlements with FirstSouth's former law firm, the thrift's officers and directors and a now-disbanded Dallas mortgage brokerage firm that had placed several loans with the thrift.
