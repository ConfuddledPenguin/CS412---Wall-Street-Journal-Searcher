Federal Grand Jury Indicts 4 Employees Of E-Systems Inc.

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/02/90

TAMPA, Fla. -- A federal grand jury returned indictments against four employees of E-Systems Inc., alleging that the men repeatedly falsified test results and other records concerning the manufacture of U.S. Army field radios. "We are aware of the indictments," said a spokesman for the Dallas-based defense contractor. "These employees are on administrative leave. We can only extend to these employees their constitutional guarantee of a presumption of innocence." An investigation of the company continues.

E-Systems closed its Memcor plant in Bushnell, Fla., in 1986 after the Army declared that the company was in default on two contracts valued at $19 million and terminated the contracts. E-Systems appealed, claiming $13 million in damages. The criminal investigation arose from that dispute.

The indictment alleges that three managers and a supervisor at the Memcor plant ignored requirements to report test failures and repairs on the Army field radios and covertly created false documents on untested radios.

The indictments charged the men with one count of conspiracy to defraud the government and five counts of making false claims. In a related case in January, Justice Department attorneys won a conviction of Memcor production supervisor Sadonna S. Hobbs for perjury. She was sentenced to 16 months in a federal correctional facility.

E-Systems expects a speedy resolution of investigations, a spokesman said.

"The company is negotiating a complete resolution of all matters pending," said Barbara Kittay, an attorney in the Justice Department's fraud section.

E-Systems announced a second-quarter after-tax charge of $8.6 million to cover Memcor claims; the company expects no further charges, the spokesman said.

Charged in the indictment are Howard DeWight Rader, former plant manager; Harley W. Hopson, former manager of quality assurance; Houston Stanley Burke, former manager of test production; and former quality assurance supervisor Paul R. Preston Jr.
