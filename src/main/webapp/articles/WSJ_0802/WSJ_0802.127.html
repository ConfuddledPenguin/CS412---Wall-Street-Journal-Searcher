Who's News: Entergy Corp. Realigns Management as Part Of Cost-Cutting Plan

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/02/90

NEW ORLEANS -- Entergy Corp. said it realigned its management, expanding the duties of four senior executives, as part of a cost-cutting plan. The utility holding company said Jack L. King, its 50-year-old senior vice president, operations, becomes executive vice president, operations, of the company's Entergy Services Inc. unit. Donald Hunter, 56, president and chief operating officer of the company's Louisiana Power & Light Co. and New Orleans Public Service Inc. units, will become a senior vice president of the Entergy Services unit.

Mr. Hunter will be succeeded by Donald E. Meiners, 54, president and chief executive of Entergy Services. Jerry D. Jackson, 45, a former senior vice president at both Entergy and Entergy Services, was named executive vice president, finance and external affairs, of the parent and the unit.

Edwin A. Lupberger remains chairman and chief executive officer of Entergy.
