Girard to Continue Battle for Control Of Pic `N' Save

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/02/90

LA JOLLA, Calif. -- Despite a restructuring plan and management shake-up announced by Pic 'N' Save Corp., Girard Partners L.P., an investor group controlled by David H. Batchelder, said it will continue its battle for control. Girard, which holds 6.7% of the discount retailer's common stock, said it will resume its solicitation of proxies to elect its nominees to a majority of seats on the company's board at the Aug. 29 annual meeting.

In a filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission, Girard also said that it intends to purchase additional shares of Pic 'N' Save common in the open market and privately negotiated transactions.

Some analysts think Mr. Batchelder's chances to win the proxy fight aren't good. "Unless he comes up with a program that is more attractive than management's, his chances are slim," said Barry Rothberg, an analyst at Mabon, Nugent & Co., a New York investment firm. "Management's restructuring plan was reasonably well-received by investors."

Pic 'N' Save stock closed at $10.625 a share, up 62.5 cents, in national over-the-counter trading yesterday.

Pic 'N' Save, which specializes in close-out merchandise, said Tuesday that it had been in discussions with Girard to try to find a way to avoid resumption of a proxy fight but that Girard's demands were unacceptable to the board. These demands included a proposal for a "reconstituted board," according to the Girard SEC filing. Mr. Batchelder didn't return a telephone call asking him what he thought of Pic 'N' Save's new plan.

That plan would cut costs and resume a $60 million stock buy-back program in an effort to placate shareholders. The Dominguez, Calif., company also announced the resignation of its chairman, president and chief executive officer, Lewis Merrifield III, who had been under fire for increasing expenses and overexpansion. The moves appeared to meet most of the demands of the Batchelder group.

Mr. Batchelder, a former protege of T. Boone Pickens, the Texas-based takeover specialist, still hasn't made a profit on his investment in Pic 'N' Save. "His cost is $10.80 a share and that doesn't include his legal, administrative and proxy solicitation costs," Mr. Rothberg said.
