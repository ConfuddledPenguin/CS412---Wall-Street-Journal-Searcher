Loews Net Fell 41% In Quarter Amid Skid In Insurance Business

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/02/90

NEW YORK -- Loews Corp.'s second-quarter net income fell 41%, reflecting a 58% profit drop at its insurance business. Analysts said the fall in earnings in the insurance business was expected. They were surprised, however, by the 15% drop in Loews's tobacco profit.

Second-period net dropped to $192.9 million, or $2.60 a share, from $329 million, or $4.38 a share, a year earlier. The results also reflect a loss from realized investments of $16.8 million, or 23 cents a share, compared with gains of $61.5 million, or 82 cents a share, a year earlier.

Revenue at Loews, which has interests in hotels, watches, ships and insurance, among other things, grew 3.4% to $3.09 billion from $2.99 billion.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading, Loews shares dropped $4.50 to $99.75 on heavy volume of 643,000 shares.

Earnings from the CNA Financial insurance business dropped to $70.4 million from $169.5 million. The tobacco business, which generates about 50% of the parent's profit, saw earnings fall 15% to $104.5 million from the year-earlier $122.8 million.

"I wasn't expecting a shortfall in the tobacco business," said Diana K. Temple, analyst at Salomon Brothers Inc. "The earnings were about 40 cents less than I thought." The company attributed the lower earnings at the Lorillard tobacco operation to a drop in demand after prices were raised in May.

Loews also said it had purchased in the open market about 952,000 of its common shares during the past six months and said it intends to buy additional shares periodically. At June 30, Loews had an average 74.1 million shares outstanding, down 1.3% from a year earlier.

For the six months, Loews said net fell 22% to $416.6 million, or $5.60 a share, from $533.5 million, or $7.08 a share, for the like 1989 period. Revenue rose 8.3% to $6.07 billion from $5.61 billion.

The latest six-month results reflect a gain of $70.2 million, or 94 cents a share, on the sale of three oil tankers.
