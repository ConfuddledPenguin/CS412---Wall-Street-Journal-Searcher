Business Brief -- FHP International Corp.: Firm Expects Lower Profit As Result of Lost Lawsuit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/02/90

FHP International Corp., a health maintenance organization based in Fountain Valley, Calif., said earnings for its fourth quarter, ended June 30, will be hurt by a recent $10 million jury verdict against it. In a medical malpractice suit, the jury awarded the plaintiffs $10 million plus interest and costs, FHP said. As a result, FHP will increase its malpractice reserves for claims relating to the period 1975-83.

FHP said its medical treatment was entirely appropriate in this case and that it intends to request a new trial and to seek a reduction of damages. If the requested relief isn't granted, it will appeal.

The company said it hasn't determined the suit's exact impact on earnings for the fourth quarter and the year. In the year-earlier fourth quarter, FHP posted a $7.7 million profit, or 65 cents a share, on revenue of $199.3 million. For fiscal 1989, FHP reported net income of $22 million, or $2.05, on revenue of $699.1 million.

The company declined to provide further details on the malpractice case.
