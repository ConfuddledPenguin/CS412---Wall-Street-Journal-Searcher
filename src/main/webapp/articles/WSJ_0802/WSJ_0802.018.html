Japan's July Vehicle Sales Rose

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/02/90

TOKYO -- Japan's sales of cars, trucks and buses in July increased 5.9% from a year earlier to 591,230 units, a record for the month, and rose 10.2% from the previous month, the Japan Automobile Dealers' Association said. The July data marked the 17th consecutive month in which vehicle sales in Japan posted a record for the month, supported by strong demand by domestic industries and individual consumers, as the nation's economy continues to expand.

Passenger-car sales rose 4.8% to 434,652 units. Among them, sales of medium-sized cars with an engine capacity of more than 2,000cc increased 61.2% to 41,703 units, compared with a year earlier. Small cars posted the first year-to-year gain in four months, edging up 1% to 392,949 units. Truck sales totaled 154,296 units, up 9.1% from a year earlier. Sales of medium-sized trucks increased 14.9% to 15,928 units, and of small trucks rose 8.5% to 138,368 units.

A total of 2,282 buses were sold in July, up 3.1%.
