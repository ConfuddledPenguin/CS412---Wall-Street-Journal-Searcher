Business Brief -- Datron Systems Inc.: Communications Company Begins Buying Back Stock

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/02/90

Datron Systems Inc. said it has begun its previously reported plan to buy back as many as 150,000, or about 5%, of its common shares in the open market. Datron, a Simi Valley, Calif., maker of satellite-communications systems and radio products, said the shares would be held as treasury stock for use in stock option and other incentive compensation plans.

"At the current levels, we believe our stock is undervalued," said David Derby, president and chief executive officer. "While our first-quarter performance was below expectations, we are highly confident about the company's long-term growth prospects."

Last week, Datron reported an 80% decline in fiscal first-quarter net income to $170,000, or six cents a share, from $866,000, or 28 cents a share. Sales in the period ended June 30 fell 21% to $8.3 million from $10.6 million.

In national over-the-counter trading, Datron closed yesterday at $7, up 62.5 cents.
