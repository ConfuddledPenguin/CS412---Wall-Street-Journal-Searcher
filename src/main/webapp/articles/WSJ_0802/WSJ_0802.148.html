Steel Operations Halt As 16,000 Start Strike At 2 Canadian Plants

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/02/90

TORONTO -- Operations were halted yesterday at two of Canada's largest steel producers as about 16,000 union members began striking following a breakdown of contract talks. The shutdowns at Stelco Inc. and Algoma Steel Corp., a subsidiary of Dofasco Inc., come as Canada's steel industry is under pressure from a slowing domestic economy and increased penetration of the Canadian market by U.S. steelmakers.

Leo Gerard, director of District Six of the United Steelworkers of America, said the union, which represents about 10,000 workers at Toronto-based Stelco, rejected what the company has called its final contract offer, proposing wage increases of about 2.5% in each year of a three-year pact. The union is seeking a higher wage settlement and better protection against inflation for workers and pensioners.

At Algoma, where the union represents about 6,000 workers, monetary issues haven't been broached, Mr. Gerard said. Talks with the Sault Ste. Marie, Ontario-based company broke down over language concerning the contracting-out of work.

Officials at both companies said an orderly shutdown of operations had been underway for several days in anticipation of a strike. Fred Telmer, president of Stelco's steel division, said inventory stockpiles have been transferred to customers. Mr. Telmer added, "We're in a closed mode."
