Aur Resources Gets Settlement Concerning Quebec Metals Deposit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/02/90

TORONTO -- Aur Resources Inc. said it reached a settlement in a legal dispute over the ownership of a large copper and zinc deposit it discovered in northern Quebec. The agreement, reached between Aur, its partner Louvem Inc. and Louvem's parent, St. Genevieve Resources Inc., gives Aur a 60% stake in the deposit, called the Louvicourt property, hailed as one of the largest base metals discoveries in Canada in recent years. Louvem will own the remaining 40% of the property.

Louvem agreed to transfer a 10% working interest in the Louvicourt property to Aur in exchange for five million Canadian dollars (US$4.3 million) and a 2.5% interest in the deposit's net profits. Aur agreed to pay a further C$7 million to Louvem, half when commercial production at the property reaches a level of 10,000 tons a day and the remainder on the first anniversary of that date.

The companies said the settlement is conditional on approval required by Louvem and St. Genevieve from a Noranda Inc. unit, or on Noranda and St. Genevieve holding a combined interest of less than 50% of Louvem. Noranda holds a 22% stake in Louvem and 19% of St. Genevieve, which in turns owns 56% of Louvem.

St. Genevieve said it advised Noranda of its plan to sell up to 4.1 million Louvem shares at C$8 each. If Noranda doesn't exercise its right of first refusal to buy those shares, and enough are sold to reduce the combined holdings of the two companies in Louvem below the 50% threshold, the agreement will take effect.
