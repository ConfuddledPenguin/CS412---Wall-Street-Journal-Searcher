Prime Motor Inns' Stock Falls as Sales Of Assets Are Sought

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/02/90

NEW YORK -- Prime Motor Inns Inc. shares have taken a battering in the last two days, as the hotel company continues to seek to reduce debt by selling assets. Prime Motor shares have fallen 23% since Monday, closing down 50 cents at $5.125 a share in composite trading yesterday on the New York Stock Exchange.

The shares had closed at $6.625 a share on Monday.

The Fairfield, N.J., company issued a statement at the Big Board's request, saying there were no corporate developments to account for the trading activity.

A year ago, the company's shares were trading around $38 a share. But it found itself facing a heavy debt load from acquisitions as well as the inabililty of FCD Hospitality Inc. to repay about $200 million of borrowings from Prime.

Prime Motor yesterday said a bank credit agreement had become effective, folding $60 million of unsecured loans into a collateralized bank lending facility that matures next March. The company said it has reduced bank debt to $110 million and its balance sheet restructuring is on schedule.

Analysts noted there is a hefty short position in the stock by investors who expect the price to go lower. Prime is a $5 stock "partly because of concern about the quality of earnings," as well as weak hotel space demand and a soft real estate market, said Margo L. Vignola, an analyst with Salomon Brothers Inc.
