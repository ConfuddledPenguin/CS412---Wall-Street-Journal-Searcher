Use of the Secret Service To Probe S&Ls Approved

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/02/90

WASHINGTON -- The Senate Appropriations Committee approved a $20.7 billion bill funding the Treasury and Postal Service through fiscal 1991 and incorporating provisions to deploy Secret Service agents in federal investigations of savings-and-loan fraud. The final language represents a compromise between the Secret Service and the rival Federal Bureau of Investigation, which strongly resisted an initial House-passed proposal giving the Secret Service new authority to initiate its own S&L inquiries.

The Senate bill is phrased to give authority to the Justice Department to "accept" assistance from the Secret Service. But within these parameters, the Secret Service would have the same authority as the Justice Department to conduct civil and criminal inquiries.

"I believe it is critical that the Secret Service be brought into the picture as soon as possible," said Attorney General Richard Thornburgh, in a letter this week to the appropriations leadership. Mr. Thornburgh's hand is strengthened by the compromise. But the FBI came to the negotiations reluctantly and, according to sources, was apparently prodded by suggestions from the committee that it could otherwise lose $98.4 million elsewhere in the bill for its Washington field office.
