International Brief -- Berliner Bank AG: Concern Plans to Buy Unit Of East German Staatsbank

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/02/90

Berliner Bank AG of West Germany said it signed a letter of intent to acquire in a two-step process the corporate-banking subsidiary of East Germany's former central bank, Staatsbank. Berliner Bank said it first will help Berliner Stadtbank AG transform itself from a bank serving state-run businesses into a universal banking group offering a full array of financial services for commercial and private customers.

Berliner Stadtbank was spun off as an independent banking group as part of the April 1 banking reform in East Germany. Its shareholders are the Staatsbank Berlin, successor to the former central bank, as well as some other East German enterprises, a Berliner Bank spokesman said.

Stadtbank at the beginning of July opened 13 branch offices in East Berlin in an effort to start up a retail-banking business.

Meantime, Berliner Bank said it will "support with all its power" the new East German partner's aim to become a competitive universal bank.

Once the transformation of Berliner Stadtbank is complete, the two companies will merge into one legal entity under Berliner Bank's name and management control, the official said.
