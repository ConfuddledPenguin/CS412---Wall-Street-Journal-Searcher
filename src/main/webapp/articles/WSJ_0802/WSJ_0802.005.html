South Korea Trade Surplus

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/02/90

SEOUL, South Korea -- Reduced imports helped South Korea register a trade surplus for the second straight month in July, but the nation's current account balance is still in a shaky position, officials said. The Trade-Industry Ministry said Korea's trade showed a surplus of $6 million in July on a customs clearance basis. This compared with a $230 million June surplus.

Korean exports grew 5.1% in July from a year ago, to $5.502 billion, while imports expanded 6.7%, to $5.496 billion, producing a surplus of $6 million, the officials said.

The July surplus reduced Korea's seven-month trade deficit to $2.76 billion, which compared with a $1.6 billion surplus a year ago, they said.
