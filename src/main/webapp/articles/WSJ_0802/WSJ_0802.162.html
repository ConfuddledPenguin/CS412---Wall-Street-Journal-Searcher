Business Brief -- Briggs & Stratton Corp.: Profit Reached $7.7 Million In Fiscal Fourth Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/02/90

Briggs & Stratton Corp.'s net income reached $7.7 million in the fiscal fourth quarter, or 54 cents a share, after a year-earlier loss of $25.1 million, equal to $1.74 a share. The Milwaukee maker of light engines said sales in the quarter ended June 30 increased 34% to $255.7 million from $190.5 million a year earlier. The company said the spring lawn-and-garden selling season "was a pretty good one."

For the fiscal year, profit hit a seven-year high of $35.4 million, or $2.45 a share, a substantial turnaround from the previous year's $20 million loss, equal to $1.39 a share. Sales rose 14% to $1 billion from $876.4 million, aided by a 19% rise in unit shipments of engines.

Discussing fiscal 1991, officials said they believe engine-unit shipments and sales "will be a bit lower." But they added that the company "will be working to achieve modestly higher earnings."
