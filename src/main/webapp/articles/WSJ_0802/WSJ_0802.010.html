U.S. Jury Acquits Pelullo on Charges Of Defrauding Thrift

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/02/90

CINCINNATI -- A federal jury acquitted Miami Beach, Fla., hotelier Leonard A. Pelullo on all charges that he defrauded a Gahanna, Ohio, thrift. Mr. Pelullo and Houston businessman David A. Friedmann had been charged in U.S. district court in Cincinnati with four counts of wire fraud and one count of conspiracy involving the now defunct Savings One Association, which Mr. Friedmann once owned.

The Cincinnati jury's decision removes a cloud hanging over Mr. Pelullo in a lengthy legal battle with DWG Corp., the centerpiece of Miami financier Victor Posner's battered financial empire. Mr. Pelullo, a former consultant to Mr. Posner, is seeking a Cleveland federal court's permission to sue DWG for $8 million in fees he claims he is owed.

Granada Investments Inc., an investment company with which Mr. Pelullo once was associated, has filed a lawsuit in the same Cleveland court alleging that Mr. Posner and DWG's directors shirked their fiduciary duty and manipulated DWG's stock in rejecting Granada's $22-a-share, or $572 million, bid for DWG last year.

DWG has countersued, charging that Granada's bid was a sham intended to boost the value of its DWG holdings. The company also alleged that the investor group obtained its original DWG stake based on "material, non-public, inside information" obtained by Mr. Pelullo during his time as a consultant. The two sides, which each deny any wrongdoing, are involved in settlement talks.

The thrift indictment handed up by a federal grand jury here last year alleged that Messrs. Pelullo and Friedmann conspired to obtain a $1 million loan that was used for purposes other than stated in the loan applications.

The indictment also alleged that Mr. Pelullo paid Mr. Friedmann some $145,000 in kickbacks for help in obtaining loans.

But Mr. Pelullo argued in court that the alleged kickback was, in fact, a deposit he had placed in escrow to be used as prepaid interest on a loan extension he was expecting to receive, and that Mr. Friedmann had stolen the money.

Mr. Friedmann, who has pleaded not guilty, is scheduled to go to trial Aug. 13.
