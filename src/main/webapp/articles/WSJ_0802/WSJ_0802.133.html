Business Brief -- Logicon Inc.: Firm Says 1st-Quarter Net Fell Due to Navy Contract

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/02/90

Logicon Inc. said its net income dropped 32% in its first quarter ended June 30 because of problems with a $30 million contract to provide air traffic controller training systems for the U.S. Navy. Logicon, a Los Angeles-based supplier of electronic systems and high-tech services, said net income was $1.8 million, or 43 cents a share, compared with $2.6 million, or 58 cents a share, in the same period last year. Revenue rose 7% to $63.5 million from $59.1 million.

Logicon said its earnings drop stemmed from a $1.1 million pretax loss provision on the Navy contract. A company spokesman said that if it weren't for the contract loss, Logicon's net income would have risen slightly in the quarter.

The spokesman said Logicon has filed for an adjustment in the contract, saying the Navy imposed changes in system performance criteria that weren't included in the contract. These changes increased costs to the company and caused delivery delays.
