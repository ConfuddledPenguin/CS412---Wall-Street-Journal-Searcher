GAO Faults System Used To Fight Bank, S&L Crime

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/02/90

WASHINGTON -- The General Accounting Office said the Justice Department doesn't have the kind of information system its new financial-institutions fraud chief needs "to effectively oversee and coordinate" the government's attack on thrift and bank crimes. The GAO told the Senate Banking Committee it sees "strong indications" that the department is "gearing up and deploying resources for increased effort against financial-institution fraud." But the GAO, an investigative arm of Congress, said the department doesn't have a system to track bank and thrift fraud cases, "how well the efforts are proceeding, what more needs to be done, where it needs to be done, and what further resources are actually needed.

In fact, James Richmond, the department's special counsel for financial-institution fraud, has expressed frustration over his inability to provide precise statistics on the status and number of major cases being handled by regional U.S. attorneys' offices. Such information is crucial in making decisions about where to send additional resources. In general, the Justice Department has moved slowly toward creating case-management systems, which use computers to track the status of cases.
