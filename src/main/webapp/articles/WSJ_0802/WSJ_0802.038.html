ITI Says Shareholder Files Suit in U.S. Court Against Firm, Directors

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/02/90

DALLAS -- International Telecharge Inc. said a shareholder filed a purported class-action suit in federal court here against the company and its directors. The suit alleges that ITI made false and misleading statements about its expectations for the second quarter ended June 30. On July 19, ITI said it anticipated a substantial net loss for the quarter, including a special charge of $1.3 million for fraudulent billings.

ITI said it had not seen the suit and declined to comment on it. The company said it would defend its interests vigorously.

The suit is the latest in a series of legal blows to the provider of operator services and pay telephones. American Telephone & Telegraph Co. sued ITI last March, alleging that ITI engaged in deceptive trade practices to win pay-phone customers away from AT&T. Last year, shareholders filed two purported class-action lawsuits alleging securities violations. Those three suits are still pending, ITI said.
