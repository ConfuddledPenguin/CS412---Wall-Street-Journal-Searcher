Key Witness Gets Probation In Southern Co. Inquiry

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/30/90

ATLANTA -- Ray Howell, a key witness in a federal investigation of political contributions by a Southern Co. unit, was sentenced to two years' probation on contempt and tax charges. Mr. Howell, who did graphics work for Southern's Pensacola, Fla., Gulf Power Co. unit, disappeared prior to his scheduled appearance before a federal grand jury in Atlanta and hid for a year before surrendering last December.

As part of his guilty plea in April, Mr. Howell agreed to cooperate in a continuing investigation into illegal political contributions. The U.S. attorney's office recently filed papers under seal that gave details of Mr. Howell's cooperation.

Individuals familiar with the investigation say Mr. Howell can shed light on the roles of individuals who were involved in the alleged political contributions scheme.

Last October, Gulf Power pleaded guilty to two felony charges of conspiracy to make illegal political contributions and tax evasion and paid a $500,000 fine, ending the case against it. However, an investigation of the individuals involved continues.

Papers filed in federal court in Atlanta in the Gulf Power case alleged that Gulf Power used Mr. Howell's Design Associates Inc. to funnel money to the 1988 campaign of Florida State Sen. W.D. Childers. Federal law bars utility holding companies from making political contributions.
