Business Brief -- American Express Co.: Travel Unit Agrees to Buy AIT Travel, Phoenix, Ariz.

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/30/90

American Express Co.'s travel unit, getting acquisitive after nearly five years on the sidelines, has agreed to buy AIT Travel Management Cos. of Phoenix, Ariz. American Express officials declined to disclose the price of the acquisition, which the company expects to be completed soon, pending regulatory approval. The purchase by American Express Travel Related Services is the first acquisition of a business travel firm since mid-1985.

Indeed, the decision to buy AIT Travel, which specializes in business travel in the Southwest region, reflects American Express's bullish outlook on the business-travel market. Despite a slowdown in the U.S. economy, the company estimates that the overall market for business travel and entertainment in the U.S. will reach $115 billion in 1990, up from $95 billion two years ago. The acquisition also fits with American Express's strategy to expand in the Southwest.

AIT Travel Management operates 26 full-service ticket locations in eight Southwestern states, with sales volume for 1990 expected to top $90 million.
