Business Brief -- General Development Corp.: Real-Estate Firm Introduces Plan to Stabilize Operations

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/30/90

General Development Corp., the beleaguered real-estate company, introduced a plan in U.S. Bankruptcy Court in Miami to reassure its customers and to stabilize its own operations. The Miami-based company, which filed for bankruptcy-law protection in April, said the plan includes, among other measures, financial incentives for buyers of the company's home sites to continue making payments, and a lot-transfer program, under which customers with contracts for home sites that are currently undeveloped would be transferred to a comparable developed one.

General Development said company surveys have shown that the top priority among its home-site buyers is assurance that they will receive a deed to a developed home site. About 21% of the company's customers made their July home-site payment. But if customers received assurances of obtaining a deed and financial incentives, the survey indicated, then about 63% of customers would continue making payments.

General Development's president, Thomas J. Hutchison, characterized the program as the "first major step in establishing a reorganization plan which will allow General Development to emerge from bankruptcy."
