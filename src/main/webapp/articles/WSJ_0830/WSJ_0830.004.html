Money Fund Yields Resumed Decline In the Latest Week

  • Author: Georgette Jasen
  • Date: 08/30/90

Yields on taxable money-market mutual funds resumed their slide in the week ended Tuesday, and investment inflows slowed markedly. The average seven-day compound yield of 433 taxable funds slipped to 7.76% from 7.80%, according to IBC/Donoghue's Money Fund Report. The annual yield had increased fractionally in the previous week after declining since early July. Compound yields assume reinvestment of dividends.

After pouring several billion dollars a week into money funds for three weeks, investors added just $475.4 million to the taxable funds in the latest week, the Holliston, Mass., newsletter reported. The total now stands at $400.9 billion, by Donoghue's count.

"Recent optimism about the Mideast crisis has reduced the turmoil in the stock and bond markets," said Martha Wittbrodt, editor of Donoghue's, explaining the small increase. She noted that institutional investors took about $528 million out of taxable funds, meaning that individuals added a total of about $1 billion.

Institutional investors typically take cash out of money funds when yields on competing investments look better. Stock prices, as measured by the Dow Jones Industrial Average, have risen somewhat in the past few days, and yields on Treasury bills sold at auction have moved slightly higher in the past two weeks.

Yields on taxable money funds are unlikely to change much any time soon. The average maturity of the funds' investments -- such things as commercial paper (short-term corporate IOUs), bank certificates of deposit and Treasury bills -- remained at a fairly long 45 days for the fourth consecutive week.

Yields on tax-free funds rose in the week ended Tuesday. The average seven-day compound yield increased to 5.34% from 5.26%. The latest yield is equivalent to 7.42% for an investor in the 28% tax bracket and 7.97% for someone paying 33% in taxes.

Assets of the 229 tax-exempt funds tracked by Donoghue's declined $392.6 million, to $80.7 billion. Average maturity lengthened two days, to 55 days.
