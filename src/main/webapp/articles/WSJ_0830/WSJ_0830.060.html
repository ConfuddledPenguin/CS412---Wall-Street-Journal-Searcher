Letters to the Editor: The Merits Of Protectionism

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/30/90

In "Socialism Is Dead; Leviathan Lives" (editorial page, July 18), James Buchanan correctly argues that centralized management of economies has not worked and cannot work. He is much less convincing in his argument that laissez-faire economics has worked and can work. A free-market economy can indeed produce more goods, but suppose it produces more than can be sold? According to Presidents Herbert Hoover and Franklin Roosevelt, "destructive," "excessive" and "cutthroat" competition was the primary cause of the Great Depression, and both took steps to restrict competition. Bernard Baruch, the leading financier of his time, proposed in 1930 the creation of a worldwide "Supreme Court of Business" to restrict "overproduction."

Maybe they were wrong, but why do economists never consider the possibility? In cutthroat competition, "protectionism" is only an attempt to ensure job security, and fully understandable. I assume Mr. Buchanan is familiar with the benefits of protectionism -- unless he and his colleagues are actively opposed to professorial tenure.

Frederick C. Thayer

Professor Emeritus

University of Pittsburgh

Graduate School of Public and International Affairs

Los Angeles
