GATX's Jet Leasing Joint Venture to Get $400 Million Capital

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/30/90

CHICAGO -- GATX Corp. said its aircraft-leasing joint venture with France's Credit Lyonnais agreed to obtain $400 million in additional capital from new Japanese and U.S. investors. The move will trim the two original partners' stake to 30% each, GATX said.

The new investors -- Mitsubishi Bank Ltd., Nichimen Corp., and Sanwa Bank Ltd. of Japan; and the Household Commercial Financial Services unit of Household International Inc. -- each received a 10% interest in the venture, and committed to invest $100 million apiece.

GATX and Credit Lyonnais began the aircraft-leasing venture, known as GATX/CL Air, in 1988, with total funding of $1 billion. The funds were used to acquire aircraft that are leased to certain South American airlines, and for 20 Airbus A320-200 jets to be delivered to other airlines over the next several years.

GATX said it anticipates the new financing will be used to acquire additional aircraft.

GATX has interests in railcar and aircraft leasing, financial services, and bulk liquid terminal storage.
