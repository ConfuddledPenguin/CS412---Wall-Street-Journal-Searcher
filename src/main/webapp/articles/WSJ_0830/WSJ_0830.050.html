Italy Plans Oil-Price Freeze

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/30/90

MILAN -- Italian financial ministers agreed to freeze prices of petroleum products in Italy for three months, to fight the inflationary impact of the oil crisis. Treasury Minister Guido Carli, Finance Minister Rino Fomica and Budget Minister Paolo Cirino Pomicino will ask the cabinet tomorrow to approve a price freeze that would go into effect between September and November.

Because Italian gasoline prices include a hefty tax, the decree to be passed by the government tomorrow will entail reducing that tax in order to accommodate the higher market price but keep the price at the pump the same.

The move will cost the government about 800 billion lire ($697.5 million) to 900 billion lire this year, according to government estimates.
