Business Brief -- Northeast Bancorp Inc.: Holding Company's Board Adopts Poison-Pill Measure

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/30/90

The board of Northeast Bancorp Inc., holding company of Union Trust Co., adopted a poison-pill measure designed to make a hostile takeover prohibitively expensive. The move comes two weeks after the Federal Reserve Board turned down a proposed acquisition of Northeast by Bank of New York Co. The Fed said the acquisition would have hurt the New York bank's ability to maintain a strong capital position.

On Sept. 11, Northeast will distribute rights to purchase common stock to holders of record Sept. 10. Under certain circumstances, the rights would entitle the holders to acquire shares of Northeast Bancorp or its acquirer at half price.

Northeast Bancorp, which had about 7.3 million shares outstanding at June 30, said the plan isn't a response to any effort to take control of the Stamford, Conn., banking company.

In national over-the-counter trading yesterday, Northeast shares fell $1.125 to $12.125.
