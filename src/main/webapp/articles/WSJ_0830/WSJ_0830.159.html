Business Brief -- James River Corp.: Loss of $84.8 Million Posted For the Fiscal First Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/30/90

James River Corp. posted a fiscal first-quarter loss of $84.8 million, or $1.11 a share, after taking a charge of about $143.6 million, or $1.76 a share, to restructure its operations. In the year-ago quarter, the paper-products maker had profit of $57 million, or 64 cents a share, including one-time items that resulted in a net gain of $800,000, or one cent a share.

James River announced the reorganization plan earlier this month, and the loss for the first quarter, which ended July 29, was generally in line with analysts' expectations. As part of the plan, the company intends to sell or shut down operations with sales of $1.3 billion.

The Richmond, Va., company said revenue for the quarter was $1.42 billion, compared with $1.48 billion in the year-ago period.

James River said earnings dropped in its communications papers division due to continued low prices for uncoated freesheet papers, but its towel and tissue division posted a "significant" increase in earnings.

The company's specialty papers division also posted an earnings gain. James River has agreed to sell 80% of the division to a new company controlled by AEA Investors Inc. and a management group.
