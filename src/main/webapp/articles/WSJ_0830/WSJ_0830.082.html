REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: Walking Voters

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/30/90

Preliminary census figures are beginning to confirm the belief that the centers of political power have tipped. Altogether, California, Texas and Florida may pick up 14 congressional seats, while New York, New Jersey and Massachusetts lose ground. We pick the latter three because each is in the process of significantly raising taxes to cover public outlays that they won't reduce. It is an interesting spectacle to watch the political culture of these prominent states operate in apparent obliviousness to potential losses both of fleeing residents and of political importance.
