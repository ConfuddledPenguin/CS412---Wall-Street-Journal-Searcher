Kansas City Power Is Seeking Talks With Kansas Gas & Electric

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/30/90

KANSAS CITY, Mo. -- Kansas City Power & Light Co. urged Kansas Gas & Electric Co. to meet with its officials before entering talks with any other potential buyers. Kansas City P&L last month launched an unsolicited $27-a-share, or $857 million, tender offer for the Wichita utility. But Kansas G&E rebuffed the offer as inadequate and overly conditional, saying also that the bid "raises substantial and serious legal questions and numerous uncertainties under state and federal statutes."

Earlier this week, Kansas G&E disclosed in a Securities and Exchange filing that it might consider a merger or recapitalization plan as a means of foiling its unwanted suitor.

In a letter made public yesterday, Kansas City P&L President and Chief Executive Officer Drue Jennings told Kansas G&E Chairman and President Wilson K. Cadman: "We are concerned that KG&E's statement indicates that you may enter discussions with third parties without also engaging in discussions with us."

Mr. Jennings urged: "Let's meet before you take any steps, such as agreeing to substantial commitment and other fees or agreeing to merge with, or sell significant assets to, a third party."

Kansas City P&L's offer has been extended until Sept. 14; at the time the original expired Aug. 20, about 43% of Kansas G&E's common shares had been tendered.
