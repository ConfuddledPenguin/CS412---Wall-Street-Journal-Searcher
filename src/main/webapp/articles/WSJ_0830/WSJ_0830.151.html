SEC Files Complaint Against 3 Involving Prime Computer Bids

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/30/90

WASHINGTON -- The Securities and Exchange Commission charged three people with insider trading in connection with a 1988 bid by MAI Basic Four Inc. to acquire Prime Computer Inc. In a complaint filed in federal court in Los Angeles, the SEC charged that Dale Jabour, Ronald Lee Thurman and Winston Cheong bought Prime Computer call options after learning that MAI was considering an attempt to acquire Prime. All three were employees at MAI at the time of the discussions, the SEC said.

The suit charged that Mr. Jabour, who was director of accounting at MAI in 1988, overheard an MAI executive officer tell another executive that the company was engaged in discussions concerning Prime Computer. Mr. Jabour later learned that any "significant corporate event" with respect to MAI and Prime Computer would take place after the Nov. 8, 1988, presidential election, the SEC said.

The complaint alleged that Mr. Jabour passed on the information to Messrs. Thurman and Cheong. All three subsequently bought call options in Prime Computer in violation of their duty to MAI, the SEC said.

Without admitting or denying the charges, the three men agreed to final judgments under which they would be enjoined from further violations of securities laws and Mr. Thurman would be required to disgorge $1,875 and Mr. Cheong to disgorge $3,830 in alleged profits from their trades. Mr. Jabour previously returned profit of $21,800 to the sellers of the options.

The judgments, which are pending before the court, also call for the three to pay to the Treasury civil fines equal to their alleged profits.

Messrs. Jabour, Thurman and Cheong couldn't immediately be reached for comment.

In August 1989, Prime Computer was acquired by J.H. Whitney & Co.
