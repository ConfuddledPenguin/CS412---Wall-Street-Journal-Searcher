Bank of Montreal Lowers Prime Lending Rate to 14%

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/30/90

TORONTO -- Bank of Montreal, one of Canada's major commercial banks, cut its prime lending rate to 14% from 14.25% effective today. Canada's other commercial banks didn't immediately follow Bank of Montreal's move.

Lloyd Atkinson, Bank of Montreal chief economist, said a lower prime rate was appropriate because of "the stability of the Canadian dollar combined with evidence of weak economic activity."

Bank of Montreal last adjusted its prime rate Aug. 14 when it lowered the rate one-half percentage point to 14.25%. Canada's other commercial banks lowered their prime rates later in the week.
