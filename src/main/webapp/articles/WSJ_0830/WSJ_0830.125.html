Business Brief -- Interphase Corp.: Company to Take a Charge Of $2.8 Million for Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/30/90

Interphase Corp. said it will take a charge of $2.8 million, or 85 cents a share, against third-quarter earnings after writing off its investment in Omni Solutions Inc. The Dallas maker of microcomputer products said profit posted in its core business during the period was not enough to offset the charge, and the company expects to post a loss for the quarter ended July 31.

A year after acquiring its majority interest in Omni, a computer components maker, Interphase said it decided to write off the investment because of high start-up costs and "significant delays" in Omni's product development. Effective Sept. 1, Omni will cease to exist as an independent company, and Interphase will provide customer support for Omni's products in the marketplace, the company said.

"Since we will no longer be incurring losses associated with Omni operations, we expect to report a profit for the fourth quarter," said Michael E. Cope, Interphase president.

For the six months ended April 30, Interphase posted a loss of $1.2 million, or 37 cents a share, on revenue of $12.4 million.
