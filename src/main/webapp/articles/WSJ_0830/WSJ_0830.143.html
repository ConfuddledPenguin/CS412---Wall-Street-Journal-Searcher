Pic `N' Save to Post Loss for Quarter, Half Due to Restructuring

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/30/90

DOMINGUEZ, Calif. -- Pic `N' Save Corp. said it will report a loss for its fiscal second quarter and six months due mainly to charges and costs related to its recent proxy contest and restructuring. Pic `N' Save recently forestalled a threatened proxy fight with Girard Partners L.P. and investor David Batchelder by agreeing to put Mr. Batchelder and two associates on the board, widening a planned stock buy-back and lifting certain anti-takeover measures.

Pic 'N' Save, which operates a chain of retail stores specializing in close-out merchandise, didn't specify the amount of the costs, charges or losses, nor did it comment on operating results.

The company said the charges include a $1.6 million after-tax severance package for Lewis B. Merrifield III, president and chief executive officer, who has resigned effective Jan. 1, and $1.7 million that Pic 'N' Save agreed to pay Girard Partners as reimbursement for proxy-fight expenses.

In the quarter ended July 2, 1989, Pic 'N' Save had net income of $6.4 million, or 18 cents a share, on sales of $101.8 million. In last year's first half, the company had net income of $11.8 million, or 33 cents a share, on sales of $195.9 million.

Pic 'N' Save said it believes that after the 1990 second quarter it will have recognized all expenses and extraordinary items from the proxy fight and restructuring except for interest and other expenses related to additional stock repurchases.

"We are confident that with the distraction and disruption of the proxy contest behind us and the programs we have recently put in place, we should be profitable for the remainder of the year," Mr. Merrifield told the annual meeting yesterday. The company also expects to be profitable for the full year, a spokesman said.
