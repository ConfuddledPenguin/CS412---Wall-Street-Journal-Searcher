Letters to the Editor: Real-Estate Figures Do Not Add Up

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/30/90

James M. Weichert claims (letters, Aug. 17) that a house that rose in value from $107,000 to $895,000 over 14 years had an average annual appreciation of 52.6%. Average annual return refers to an annual compounded rate; an investment of $107,000 would grow to $39,732,624 in 14 years at an average annual rate of 52.6%. The correct rate of return is 16.4% -- comparable with an investment in a Standard & Poor's 500 index fund over the same period. Noreen Murray

Boston
