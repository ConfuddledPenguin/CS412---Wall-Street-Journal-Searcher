Plains Petroleum's Price Pact

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/30/90

LAKEWOOD, Colo. -- Plains Petroleum Co. said it reached an agreement with KN Energy Inc., the purchaser of about 85% of its natural gas production, for wellhead prices to be charged under base contracts for 1991. With this new agreement, Plains estimates that 1991 gas volumes sold will increase by about 17.5% and that revenue received from its principal purchaser will increase 12.5% from its current 1990 forecast of about $33 million.

Plains said the annually renegotiated price will vary for 1991 from a high of $1.95 per million British thermal units for sales made in the high-demand season in the winter and late fall to $1.65 per million BTUs in the low-demand season. Assuming normal weather, the expected average price for 1991 for the base contracts is $1.8 million BTUs, or about 2% less than the current-year price of $1.84 million BTUs.

Plains Petroleum is an oil and gas company. KN Energy, also based in Lakewood, Colo., is a diversified energy company.
