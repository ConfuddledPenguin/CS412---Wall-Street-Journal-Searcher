BANCORP HAWAII Inc. (Honolulu) -- Standard & Poors Corp. said it lowered its rating on Bancorp Hawaii Inc.'s senior debt to single-A from single-A-plus. The debt-rating company reaffirmed its rating of Bancorp Hawaii's commercial paper at A-1. ---

NATIONAL GYPSUM Co. (Dallas) -- Moody's Investors Service Inc. said it lowered the debt ratings of this construction conern predicting that falling gypsum wallboard prices would "erode the company's already very thin" reserves for making interest payments. Moody's said it lowered to Caa from single-B-3 the ratings on priority senior subordinated debentures and subordinated debentures. The gypsum wallboard industry continues to suffer from overcapacity and depressed price levels, Moody's said. The poor outlook for housing starts combined with what Moody's called "the unlikelihood of a near-term turnaround in the company's performance" raises doubts about National Gypsum's ability "to generate enough cash to service its debt through the downcycle," Moody's said. Moody's noted that National Gypsum's debt is nonrecourse to Lafarge Coppee, a French construction company that owns 54% of National Gypsum. National Gypsum declined to comment.

CALFED Inc. (Los Angeles) -- Moody's Investors Service Inc. said it lowered its rating on this financial services holding company's subordinated debt to single-B-2 from Ba-3. The rating concern also downgraded the California Federal Bank unit's long-term deposits and senior debt to Ba-2 from Baa-3, and short-term deposits and commercial paper to not prime from prime-3. Moody's said about $875 million of debt is affected by the changes. Moody's said its action reflects California Federal's "somewhat weak" capital, and increased uncertainty about the future of California commercial real estate. A Calfed spokesman said the Moody's move reflects the "increasingly pessimistic outlook for California real estate values."
