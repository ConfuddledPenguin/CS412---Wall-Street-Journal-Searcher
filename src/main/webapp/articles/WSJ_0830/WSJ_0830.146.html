Who's News: Citizens & Southern, Sovran Fill Positions At Merged Company

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/30/90

ATLANTA -- Citizens & Southern Corp. and Sovran Financial Corp. named top executives to manage the two banking companies when they merge. The merger, announced last year, is expected to be completed Saturday. It will create the nation's 13th-largest bank holding company, based on its more than 1,000 offices in seven states and about $50.3 billion in assets. The newly created company will be named C&S/Sovran Corp., but local banks will continue to operate under their current names. The new company will have dual headquarters in Atlanta and Norfolk, Va.

As expected, Bennett A. Brown, 61 years old, currently chairman and chief executive officer of Citizens & Southern, will serve as chairman and chief executive officer of C&S/Sovran. Dennis C. Bottorff, 45, currently president and chief operating officer of Sovran, will be president and chief operating officer and was also designated as Mr. Brown's eventual successor as chief executive officer.

Albert B. Gornto Jr., 61, currently chairman and chief executive officer of Sovran, will be chairman of the executive committee and will be responsible for consumer and commercial services.

Hugh M. Chapman, 57, currently president and chief operating officer of Citizens & Southern, will be vice chairman responsible for corporate banking and trust services.

Named as senior executive vice presidents in the following positions and areas of responsibility are: Willard A. Alexander, 58, chief credit officer; Page D. Cranford, 54, general counsel and secretary; James D. Dixon, 46, chief financial officer; Eileen M. Friars, 40, corporate development; and Doyle E. Hull, 57, real estate and consumer non-bank activities.
