Refco to Pay Fine Of $220,000 to Settle Charges by CFTC

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/30/90

WASHINGTON -- Refco Inc., a Chicago futures firm, agreed to pay a $220,000 fine to settle Commodity Futures Trading Commission charges that it failed to maintain certain records, and that it violated regulatory orders relating to its record keeping. The charges stemmed from alleged actions by Ray Friedman of Palm Beach, Fla., a founder of Refco and a longtime trader. The CFTC alleged that Mr. Friedman, trading for an unnamed customer, violated speculative position limits for pork belly futures on the Chicago Mercantile Exchange for about a year, beginning in June 1988.

The commission charged Mr. Friedman with aiding and abetting Refco in not keeping written records of the orders. The CFTC said that Mr. Friedman hasn't settled the charges.

Refco was charged with failing to maintain the records, failing to retain customer risk-disclosure statements, failing to supervise, and violating two cease-and-desist orders entered by the CFTC in 1983 and 1988 relating to record keeping and supervision charges.

The company, which didn't admit or deny any wrongdoing, consented to a CFTC finding that it violated the regulations cited by the commission. In addition to the fine, Refco agreed to refrain from further violations and to review its supervising procedures.

In a prepared statement, Refco said: "The alleged record-keeping violations did not affect the profitability of customer accounts. It is Refco's intent to comply with all applicable laws, rules and regulations. Refco has settled this matter in order to maintain its positive working relationship with the CFTC and to avoid the costs and distraction of protracted litigation."

In an unrelated action, the CFTC charged Craig LaCrosse of Oak Brook, Ill., with aiding and abetting another floor broker in entering false order records and engaging in improper and false sales. The CFTC also alleged that Mr. LaCrosse engaged in noncompetitive trading.

Mr. LaCrosse, a registered floor broker and member of the Chicago Board of Trade, was one of 45 traders indicted last year by a grand jury for alleged trading abuses.

Mr. LaCrosse last year pleaded guilty to aiding and abetting the creation of a false record in connection with a customer order and to making a prearranged accommodation trade.
