West Coast Bancorp Write-Off

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/30/90

ORANGE, Calif. -- West Coast Bancorp said its third-quarter results will include a $1.4 million loan write-off that is expected to reduce net income by $850,000, or nine cents a share. West Coast Bancorp said the increase in provision for possible credit losses stems from its inactive nonbanking subsidiary, West Coast Realty Finance. The loan is secured by a second deed of trust on a 60,000-square-foot office building.

John B. Joseph, chairman and chief executive, said the regional bank holding company expects to be profitable for the year despite the write-off.
