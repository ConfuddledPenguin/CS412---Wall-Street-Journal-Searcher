International Brief -- Toyota Motor Corp.: Car Maker's Pretax Profit Jumped 34% in Fiscal Year

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/30/90

Citing successful introduction of new models and solid gains for domestic sales, Toyota Motor Corp., Japan's biggest car maker, said its consolidated pretax profit in the fiscal year ended June 30 surged 34% to 837.81 billion yen ($5.83 billion) from 625.66 billion yen a year earlier. Toyota Motor's vehicle sales during the reporting period grew strongly world-wide, supported in particular by strong demand in the domestic market. Japan is the company's largest market, where sales reached record levels for the third consecutive year, the company said.

Net income rose 27% to 441.3 billion yen, or 144.15 yen a share, from 346.26 billion yen, or 121.19 yen a share.

Sales totaled 9.192 trillion yen, a 15% gain from 8.021 trillion yen last year.

Toyota said its overseas operations are likely to face much more severe conditions, as demand in the U.S. market continues to slacken and competition increases in Europe.

The company disclosed a plan to change Toyota's corporate charter as part of an effort to branch out into nonautomotive businesses. Toyota officials will ask a shareholders meeting, scheduled for Sept. 26, to approve diversification plans. The company wants to raise nonautomotive sales, currently accounting for 2% of overall unconsolidated sales, to 6% in fiscal 1994.
