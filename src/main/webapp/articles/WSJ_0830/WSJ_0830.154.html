Business Brief -- PNC Financial Corp.: Bank Unit Offers Back Pay To Settle Charges of Bias

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/30/90

Pittsburgh National Bank, a unit of PNC Financial Corp., said it paid $100,000 of back pay to 13 employees to settle charges by federal regulators that the bank discriminated against minority job applicants. Pittsburgh National said it didn't discriminate, but that it didn't maintain adequate records to prove that its hiring policies didn't violate federal regulations.

Examiners from the Office of Federal Contract Compliance Programs said Pittsburgh National hired a lower proportion of minority applicants than white applicants for clerical positions.

The bank said more of the minority applicants may have applied at times when the bank had no openings, or they may have requested full-time jobs when the bank had only part-time positions available. However, the bank didn't note on each job application why the applicant didn't obtain a job, and thus was unable to prove that it hadn't discriminated, said Wilbur E. Hines, senior vice president, personnel.

As part of the settlement, the bank hired 13 minority job applicants who previously hadn't obtained jobs and split the $100,000 of back pay among them, Mr. Hines said. Also, the bank improved its record-keeping to ensure that it can document its compliance with anti-discrimination rules, he said.
