Who's News: Interstate/Johnson's Redwine Resigns Posts

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/30/90

CHARLOTTE, N.C. -- Interstate/Johnson Lane Inc., a brokerage firm, said its chairman and chief executive officer, J. Craighill Redwine, resigned. Mr. Redwine, 49 years old, was named chief executive in 1986. In an interview, he said that he had decided "it was time to move on" from his posts, and that there wasn't any pressure to leave the firm. He said that he probably will remain in the brokerage industry, but that he doesn't have any immediate plans.

Interstate/Johnson, one of the largest brokerage firms in the Southeast, has had several quarters of declining revenue and widening losses. In the quarter ended June 30, the company had a loss of $1.4 million, or 20 cents a share, on revenue of $31.9 million. The loss included a $750,000 charge related to litigation.

Elmon L. Vernier Jr., president and chief operating officer, said Interstate/Johnson's board decided to leave Mr. Redwine's posts vacant temporarily. The company, Mr. Vernier said, is reviewing its businesses and options to develop a "strategic direction."
