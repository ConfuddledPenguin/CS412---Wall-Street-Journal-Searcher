Dennison Agrees To Settle Lawsuit Over Avery Merger

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/30/90

FRAMINGHAM, Mass. -- Dennison Manufacturing Co., seeking to placate shareholders and clear the way for its proposed merger with Avery International Corp., has agreed to settle a holder suit filed against it in connection with the merger. Under the proposed settlement, Avery Dennison Corp., the surviving entity after the merger, would pay a quarterly dividend of 20 cents a share for four consecutive quarters after the merger closes.

Avery, based in Pasadena, Calif., currently pays a quarterly dividend of 16 cents a share and has said that it will recommend a quarterly rate of 18 cents a share if the merger is completed.

The dividend rate to be paid after the four quarters covered by the proposed agreement would be at the discretion of the company's board, according to Gerald Hendrick, Dennison's general counsel.

The stock-swap merger of the two companies would create one of the nation's largest office-products concerns, with about $2.5 billion in annual sales. Dennison holders would receive 1.12 Avery shares for each Dennison share.

Dennison said the settlement is contingent upon completion of the merger, an agreement on a stipulation in the settlement and court approval. The suit is pending in Massachusetts Superior Court.
