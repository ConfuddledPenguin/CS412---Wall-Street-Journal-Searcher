Technology: Fujitsu Unveils Upgrades To Supercomputer Line

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/30/90

Fujitsu Ltd. said it has made various enhancements to its VP2000 series supercomputers, including the introduction of two new models. The new systems, the VP2400/40 and VP2200/40, provide improved high-speed program processing, the Japanese company said.

The systems will be available in September 1991, with a monthly rental fee of about 79 million yen ($550,156) for the VP2200/40 and of about 103 million yen for the VP2400/40.

The other enhancements are intended to respond to increased user demands for large-scale program processing involving simulations, Fujitsu said.

In a separate announcement, Fujitsu disclosed the release of the UXP/M operating system for the VP2000 series supercomputers and M series of mainframes, the first mainframe computer adaptation of Unix System V Release 4. The SVR4 is the latest standard for the Unix operating system.

Fujitsu said it plans to adopt the Unix SVR4 standard for all its Unix products.

Basic software for UXP/M is scheduled to be delivered next April, with a monthly rental fee beginning at 210,000 yen.
