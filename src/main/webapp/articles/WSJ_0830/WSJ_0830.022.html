E-Systems Agrees To Buy Defense Unit From Hadson Corp.

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/30/90

DALLAS -- E-Systems Inc. said it agreed to acquire Hadson Corp.'s HRB Systems Inc. for $65 million, half what Hadson paid for it only two years ago. Like many defense contractors, HRB's sales have declined in recent years because of "the breakout of peace," said Hadson President J. Michael Adcock, explaining why the unit's value dropped 50%. In 1989, the unit had sales of about $113 million, down from about $140 million only two years earlier, Mr. Adcock said.

He stressed, however, that "HRB remains a good solid company," and that its sale is part of a larger Hadson strategy to reduce debt and concentrate on producing and supplying energy products and facilities, the primary business of Oklahoma City-based Hadson.

HRB will give E-Systems, a maker of defense electronics systems, "access to new customers, emerging programs, and synergistic opportunities which we expect will result in a positive future contribution to our earnings," said E.G. Keiffer, chairman and chief executive of E-Systems. He added that HRB "will further complement and expand our position in the advanced intelligence and reconnaissance systems marketplace, an area which we believe has and will become increasingly important." HRB, based in State College, Pa., employs about 1,300.

Mr. Adcock said Hadson established reserves in the second quarter large enough to cover the loss on the sale of HRB. Of the $147 million, or $3.71-a-share, loss that Hadson reported for the second quarter, about $144 million came from discontinued operations. In the year-earlier quarter, the company had a loss of $2.7 million, or seven cents a share, after taking a $2.8 million loss on discontinued operations. Revenue in the quarter fell to $106.5 million from $130.2 million.

E-Systems reported six-month earnings of $35.9 million, or $1.15 a share, compared with $38.5 million, or $1.24 a share, a year earlier. Sales rose to $896 million from $791 million.
