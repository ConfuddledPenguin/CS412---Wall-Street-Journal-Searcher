Technology: Device Can Concentrate Light to Greater Intensity

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/30/90

CHICAGO -- University of Chicago researchers said they developed a solar device that can concentrate sunlight to an intensity 15% greater than on the surface of the sun itself. Roland Winston, head of the Department of Physics and a researcher, said scientists have been trying to heighten the intensity of collected sunlight because a highly concentrated beam of light could someday power laser communication in space or incinerate hazardous wastes, among other uses.

The group's feat, which amounted to concentrating sunlight to 84,000 times its normal intensity on Earth, surpasses the previous record achieved by the researchers in 1988 by 40%, he said.

He said the group achieved the feat by first using a conventional telescope mirror to focus the light to a square-centimeter area. A precisely machined cone of sapphire then concentrated that focused light into a square-millimeter area. Key to the advance in intensity was a sapphire that was "mathematically designed" to give the maximum possible refractive index, or ability to bend light.

In the latest experiments, the researchers measured the energy output of the solar collector by heating fluid. The solar collector generated 72 watts a square millimeter, compared with 63 watts generated at the surface of the sun. Normal sunlight that reaches Earth amounts to less than one-thousandth of a watt a square millimeter.
