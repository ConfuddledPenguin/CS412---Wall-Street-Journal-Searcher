Letters to the Editor: When a Man Is a Woman

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/30/90

Recently you noted (editorial, Aug. 2) that Meryl Streep is analyzing Hollywood's employment practices. Projecting from a study showing "men had twice as many roles as women in 1989's movies, Ms. Streep says that . . . `by the year 2000, we will have 13% of roles . . . and in 20 years, we will be eliminated from the movies.'" But will the trend stop there? Golly, this is even worse than she imagined, because when the trend is projected for an additional 10 years, men will be playing women] And yes, there is irrefutable evidence to support this: 10 years ago, one man, Dustin Hoffman, played one woman in "Tootsie." Today, two men, Eric Idle and Robbie Coltrane, play two women in "Nuns on the Run." The trend is obvious.

Apparently, Ms. Streep has seen the future and it isn't her.

George E. Ertel

Phoenix, Ariz.
