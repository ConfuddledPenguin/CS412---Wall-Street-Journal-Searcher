Volvo's Pretax Profit Drops 56% in Quarter; 5,000 Jobs to Be Cut

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/30/90

STOCKHOLM -- Smarting from a third consecutive quarter of plunging pretax profit, AB Volvo announced plans to trim heavy truck production in the U.S. for the rest of this year and begin hefty job cuts at both its car and truck divisions. The Swedish auto concern said profit before taxes skidded 56% in the second quarter, to 1 billion Swedish kronor ($174.7 million) from 2.29 billion kronor a year earlier. Pretax profit was down 54% for the first six months, to 2.06 billion kronor from 4.48 billion kronor the previous year.

"It's quite clear that market conditions this year are tougher than we expected. Downturns in some of our larger markets -- the U.S., U.K. and Scandinavia -- have hit us pretty hard," said Christer Zetterberg, Volvo's chief operating officer.

Mr. Zetterberg admitted the company "doesn't expect to get much help from markets during the rest of this year or 1991." Consequently, Volvo management has ordered tough cost-cutting steps "which are necessary to restore our Swedish competitiveness," he said.

Investors seemed to welcome the move. Volvo B-free shares, the series most traded internationally, jumped 10 kronor to close at 325 kronor in trading yesterday on the Stockholm Stock Exchange.

Volvo intends to eliminate 5,000 jobs by the end of next year, with about 80% of the layoffs affecting car plants in Sweden.

But overseas units also are beginning to feel the pinch. Volvo GM heavy Truck Corp. -- the three-year-old alliance with General Motors Corp. -- is about to lay off 350 employees, about 8.5% of its total payroll, and reduce 1990 production by 2,000 trucks or about 10%.

Sagging heavy truck demand this year has taken its toll. Volvo said unit sales of its heavy trucks in the U.S. declined 22% during the first six months. The total U.S. market fell 18% during the same period.

Volvo's sales dropped 2.4% in the second quarter to 22.92 billion kronor from 23.48 billion kronor the previous year. First half sales shrank 4.2% to 44.59 billion kronor from 46.55 billion kronor a year earlier, the company said.
