Marketing & Media: Columbia Pictures Taps Dolgen to Head Its Film Division

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/30/90

NEW YORK -- Columbia Pictures Entertainment Inc. named Fox Inc. president Jonathan Dolgen to the new post of president of the Columbia Pictures film studio. The appointment marks a return to Columbia for Mr. Dolgen, who first joined the old Columbia Pictures Industries Inc. in 1976 and rose to president of domestic operations in 1983, before quitting in 1985 to join Fox, a unit of News Corp.

Mr. Dolgen, 45 years old, will be president of the Columbia Pictures film division and will report to Frank Price, chairman of the studio. He will oversee daily operations for Columbia, which operates separately from the parent company's other production units, including Tri-Star Pictures and a television production arm.

At Fox, a replacement for Mr. Dolgen hasn't been named. Mr. Dolgen had been president of Fox Inc., which owns the Fox television network, since 1988 and a year ago took on the additional title of president of Fox's television-production unit, Twentieth Television, which makes such series as "L.A. Law"

Although Mr. Dolgen will report to Frank Price, Columbia's studio chairman, he also will have other corporate responsibilities that will have him reporting directly to Peter Guber and Jon Peters, the co-chairmen of Columbia Pictures Entertainment, which was recently acquired by Sony Corp.

"I'm going to work with Frank and my other friends at Columbia Pictures, and I'm going to work with Jon and Peter to deal with issues they think are suited to my skills," Mr. Dolgen said in an interview.
