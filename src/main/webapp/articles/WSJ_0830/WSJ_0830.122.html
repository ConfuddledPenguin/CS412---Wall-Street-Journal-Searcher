Saudis Double Their Order Of Dutch Alcohol-Free Beer

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 08/30/90

LIESHOUT, the Netherlands -- The Bavaria brewery said Saudi Arabia has asked it to double its exports of non-alcoholic beer to the Middle East nation to help slake the thirst of U.S. soldiers there. But the company already finds it hard to satisfy both normal demand from Islamic clients and from a home market where alcohol-free brews enjoy a growing reputation.

"The demand is much too large for us at the moment," said Rien Coolen, a spokesman for the Bavaria brewery, which has been exporting alcohol-free beer to Saudi Arabia for 15 years.

A strict Islamic nation, Saudi Arabia prohibits the sale of alcohol. To help meet the Saudi request, the brewery is increasing output and will ask employees to work overtime, Mr. Coolen added.

Mr. Coolen declined to disclose the size of the Saudi request or give details of his brewery's exports, citing competitive reasons.
