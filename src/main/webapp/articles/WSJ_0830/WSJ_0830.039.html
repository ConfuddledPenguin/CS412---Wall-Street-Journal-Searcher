Ogden Projects Unit To Build Florida Plant For Waste-to-Energy

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/30/90

FAIRFIELD, N.J. -- Ogden Martin Systems Inc. said it received approval from Lee County officials in Florida to build and operate a $147 million waste-to-energy facility. It will be the fourth such facility in Florida to be built by the unit of Ogden Projects Inc. The award is significant because Florida is one of the states where the solid-waste disposal crisis is acute and where incineration, much opposed by the public, is trying to get a toehold.

The facility will convert up to 657,000 tons of residential and commercial solid waste annually into more than 47 megawatts of electric power. Ogden will sell the power to Florida Power & Light Corp. The county will receive 90% of the revenue from electric sales, which will be used to pay down the cost of financing and operating the plant.

The facility will be financed by revenue bonds that the county, as owner, will issue by year end. Construction of the plant, which will take 27 months, will begin when all applicable federal, state and local environmental permits are issued.

In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange yesterday, Ogden shares closed at $19, up 12.5 cents.
