International: Laura Ashley To Sell 15% Stake To Jusco of Japan

  • Author: Barbara Toman
  • Date: 08/30/90

LONDON -- Laura Ashley Holdings PLC agreed to sell a 15% stake to Japanese chain-store operator Jusco Co. in an effort to lighten the troubled British company's debt load and expand in Asia. Under the agreement, subject to shareholder approval, Jusco would pay #29.9 million ($58.1 million) for 35.2 million new Laura Ashley shares. The per-share price of 85 pence is 73% above Laura Ashley's trading level of 49 pence late Tuesday.

In addition, Laura Ashley said it would sell more than half the equity in its wholly owned U.S. subsidiary, Revman Industries Inc. Jusco would acquire about 48% of the equity, with Revman's management taking as much as 5% more. The transaction, which values all of Revman at about $35 million, will make Revman an associated company of Laura Ashley and remove its roughly $27 million in borrowings from the parent company's balance sheet.

For Laura Ashley, the Jusco deal offers "room to maneuver, and steps up the pace in the Far East," said Nick Bubb, a London-based analyst at Morgan Stanley. But, he said, until Laura Ashley shifts from costly U.K. factories to less-costly Asian sources, "I'm reluctant to say they've turned the corner."

Jusco and Laura Ashley already have a retail joint venture, Laura Ashley Japan Co. Under yesterday's agreement, Jusco will boost its stake in the venture to 60% from 50%, although Laura Ashley is to retain control of its brand name and receive increased royalties.

Laura Ashley home furnishings, in particular, are increasingly popular in Japan, said Sir Bernard Ashley, chairman and co-founder. "We have tremendous opportunities there, and this will unlock them," he said. Sir Bernard characterized Jusco as a "friendly, passive investor taking a long-term view." Yesterday's agreement stipulates that Jusco can't raise or lower its 15% share of Laura Ashley's enlarged share capital without approval from the company's board.

The Ashley family's stake in the company would be diluted to 59.2% from 69.7% currently. That holding could fall to 54.2%, as the family has agreed to give Jusco a right of first refusal to buy an additional 5% stake at a price to be agreed on later. Sir Bernard said his family isn't necessarily committed to retaining a majority holding in the company, although he doesn't envisage relinquishing control "in the immediate future."

In a statement, Jusco's chairman and chief executive officer, Takuya Okada, said the arrangements would "enhance the global presence of both groups" and "contribute to the improvement of Laura Ashley's financial position." Jusco, which operates more than 450 retail outlets in Japan, had pretax profit of 39.4 billion yen ($274.2 million) on sales of 1.35 trillion yen in the year ended Feb. 20.
