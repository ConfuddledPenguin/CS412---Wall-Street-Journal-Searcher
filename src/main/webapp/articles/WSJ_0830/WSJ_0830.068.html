Credit Ratings: Moody's Downgrades Bank of New York Debt

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/30/90

NEW YORK -- Moody's Investors Service Inc. said it lowered its ratings on Bank of New York Co.'s long-term debt and commercial paper. The rating concern's action affects a total of $1.2 billion of securities.

Moody's downgraded Bank of New York senior debt to single-A-3 from single-A-2, subordinated debt to Baa-1 from single-A-3, preferred stock to single-A-3 from single-A2; and commercial paper to prime-2 from prime-1.

Moody's said its move "primarily reflects the company's exposure to highly leveraged transaction lending." It said that "although these portfolios have exhibited only moderate deterioration in asset quality, they nevertheless remain substantial in relation to equity and reserves."

A Bank of New York spokesman said that the company regrets Moody's decision but that it will work with the rating concern to improve the ratings.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Bank of New York shares closed at $23.50, down 25 cents.
