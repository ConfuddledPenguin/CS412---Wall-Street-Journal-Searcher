Marketing & Media: Werner Leaves ESPN to Head Prime Ventures

  • Author: Dennis Kneale
  • Date: 08/30/90

NEW YORK -- Roger L. Werner Jr. resigned as president and chief executive officer of sports cable channel ESPN to head Prime Ventures Inc., a regional sports network company newly formed by cable executive Bill Daniels. Mr. Werner, 40 years old, was considered one of the most promising young executives at Capital Cities/ABC Inc., which runs ESPN and holds an 80% stake in the cable channel. A successor will be named shortly, said Herb Granath, ESPN's chairman and the president of ABC Video Enterprises. ESPN's No. 2 executive, Steve Borenstein, may be the likely choice.

A former consultant with McKinsey & Co. who had advised ESPN in its early days, Mr. Werner joined the channel in 1980 as a vice president of finance and rose through several promotions before jumping over to a sister unit, the ABC television network, to become executive vice president there. He returned to ESPN as its new president two years ago.

Mr. Werner leaves ESPN a cable powerhouse, with 56 million subscribing households and pretax earnings that topped $100 million last year. But the sports channel faces declining profits this year because of expected losses of $20 million or more on the first year of a new ESPN baseball contract, and additional losses expected on a new football contract.

ESPN officials said, however, the sports channel will post ample profits this year, though earnings will be down from 1989 levels.

Mr. Granath said Mr. Werner's departure was his own choice. "We're very much chagrined to see him leaving, though ESPN is in good shape," Mr. Granath said.

Mr. Werner was lured to the new venture by Mr. Daniels, the chairman of cable company Daniels & Associates, with a generous offer that Capital Cities/ABC couldn't afford to match.

"Bill Daniels made a sensational offer, and I decided to take him up on it," Mr. Werner said yesterday in a telephone news conference. He wouldn't reveal whether his new job includes equity in the venture, but he said, "I do have a substantial upside opportunity in growing the business. I look forward to being an entrepreneur again."

Prime Ventures Inc. will oversee a variety of sports-oriented properties controlled or minority-owned by Mr. Daniels. They include six regional sports networks owned and operated by Daniels and Tele-Communications Inc., the nation's largest cable operator.

Mr. Werner's new post also will oversee a national sports programming service called Prime Network; Prime Ticket Netowrk, which operates in southern California and Hawaii and is the nation's largest regional sports network; minority interests in Home Sports Entertainment in Dallas and Houston and regional sports channels elsewhere; and Daniels's majority ownership of production company First Auto Sports Television, and Denver Grand Prix Auto Race Inc.
