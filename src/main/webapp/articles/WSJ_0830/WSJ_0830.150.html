Business Brief -- Reynolds Metals Co.: Aluminum Firm to Offer Non-Stick Microwave Wrap

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/30/90

Reynolds Metals Co. said it will introduce a non-stick microwave wrap. The Richmond, Va., aluminum company said the product, which will be in stores in the Midwest next week, will be the first paper food-wrap designed exclusively for microwave use.

Microwave cooking typically uses paper towels or waxed paper, which tend to rip easily, or plastic wraps, which tend to sag and stick to dishes and food in the microwave.

First Brands Inc. came out with Glad Microwave Wrap, a plastic-based wrap, but when it sagged under the oven's heat, so did its sales.

Reynolds says its product, a result of six years of research, won't sag, tear, pick up grease or stick to food and containers. "You can even cook cheese on it," says John M. Lowrie, vice president of the company's consumer products unit.

Mr. Lowrie said the product, which will look like freezer paper and have a coating of fatty acid, will be available nationwide within a year. He predicts the wrap will have sales of $20 million in its first year.
