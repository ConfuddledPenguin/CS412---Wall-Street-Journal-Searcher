Business Brief -- National Intergroup Inc.: Firm Says Terms Improved In Tender Offer for Units

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/30/90

National Intergroup Inc. improved the terms of a tender offer for the cumulative convertible preference units outstanding representing limited partnership interests of Permian Partners L.P. Pittsburgh-based National Intergroup still will pay $3.85 a preference unit. But it agreed to extend the term of accompanying contingent value rights to June 30, 1993, from June 30, 1992, and to increase the possible payout under those rights.

The rights would allow the holders to share in the proceeds in excess of the cash they had already received in the event that the Permian partnership is sold within a specified time period. Under the new terms, holders would receive 100% of the amount of the excess proceeds during the entire life of the rights, instead of a graduated scale dropping to 50% after June 1, 1992.

National Intergroup said earlier this week that only 5,535,680 preference units had been tendered as of Aug. 27, well below the 7,875,001 units, a majority of the publicly held units, that National Intergroup had specified as the minimum required to make the offer effective.

National Intergroup is primarily a drug wholesaler but it also has interests in metals, general merchandising and oil production. Directors recently agreed to consider sale of the firm's drug unit, FoxMeyer Corp., or of the entire company.
