International Brief -- BASF AG: Chemical Concern's Profit In First Half Declined 21%

  • Author: null
  • Date: 08/30/90

BASF AG said its 1990 first-half group pretax profit tumbled 21% to 1.74 billion marks ($1.11 billion) from 2.19 billion marks in the year-earlier period. The profit decline for West Germany's largest chemical concern came on a 4.5% drop in group sales, which fell to 23.37 billion marks from 24.46 billion marks a year before.

For the second quarter, group pretax profit plummeted 31% to 891 million marks from 1.29 billion marks. Group sales fell 8.1% to 11.48 billion marks from 12.49 billion marks.

BASF said it sees difficulties for the rest of the year. The company said that although there are "good" prospects for boosting the volume of products sold, it expects to attain only the "solid earnings" level of 1987, rather than the level of 1988 or the record performance of 1989.
