Business Brief -- Barry's Jewelers Inc.: Loss Grew to $12.1 Million For Fiscal Fourth Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/30/90

Barry's Jewelers Inc., Monrovia, Calif., said its fiscal-fourth-quarter loss widened to $12.1 million, or $2.42 a share, because of increased provisions for bad debts. In the year-earlier quarter, Barry's had a loss of $754,000, or 15 cents a share. Sales for the latest period, ended May 31, dipped to $31.6 million from $31.9 million.

Trading of Barry's stock in the national over-the-counter market was halted briefly yesterday pending the earnings announcement. The stock closed at $4.25, down $1.50.

Barry's, which operates 224 stores nationwide, said it hadn't expected the increase in bad-debt provisions to be as large as it was. The rise reflects "continuing deterioration in the general retail environment," Barry's said, and its own "increasingly adverse bad-debt experience," mainly in the Southwest. Barry's also cited the effect of adjusting the value of its accounts receivable for financing purposes.

For fiscal 1990, Barry's posted a $15.5 million loss, or $3.10 a share, compared with net income of $6 million, or $1.21 a share. Sales rose 5.6%, to $165.7 million from $157 million.
