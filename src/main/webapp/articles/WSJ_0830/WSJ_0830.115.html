Ponce Federal Gets Order From U.S. Thrift Agency

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 08/30/90

WASHINGTON -- Ponce Federal Bank, one of the largest thrifts in Puerto Rico, was hit with strong enforcement measures by the Office of Thrift Supervision -- including an order to change the locks on its doors. The measures, designed to "increase the bank's safety and soundness," according to the OTS, include a temporary cease-and-desist order issued against Ramiro L. Colon, former chairman, president and chief executive officer and now a stockholder, and his brother, Wendell W. Colon, former thrift counsel and also a stockholder.

According to the OTS, the Colons have been ordered to stop causing the bank to violate federal regulations against loans to affiliated parties and insider transactions, and to post a $50,000 bond to make funds available for restitution to the thrift.

The OTS order also calls for members of the thrift's board and Ramiro Colon to pay $615,000 in restitution to Ponce Federal.

Ponce Federal itself has been directed, among other measures, to comply with OTS regulations dealing with loans to affiliated parties and insider transactions; to stop any direct employment-related payments to Ramiro Colon; and to deny the Colons access to any of the institution's records or files. Federal regulators also ordered the thrift to change its locks and deny keys to the Colons.

Regulators noted that the measures don't affect the thrift's daily operations or depositors' accounts, which continue to be insured up to $100,000.

Thrift officials and Messrs. Colon weren't available for comment.
