Business Brief -- Chemical Fabrics Corp.: Jury Finds Against Concern In Suit Over Stadium Roof

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/17/90

Chemical Fabrics Corp., Merrimack, N.H., said a Minneapolis jury found it liable for $2.2 million in damages related to the construction of the roof of the Metrodome stadium in Minneapolis. Chemical Fabrics said the jury upheld a claim that certain panels in the roof weren't designed to contract specifications. It said interest on the claim could amount to an added $1.3 million.

In a statement, Duane C. Montopoli, president and chief executive officer, said "we believe strongly that neither the finding of liability nor the damages awarded are supported by the facts or the evidence presented in this case." He said the maker of coated fiberglass materials plans to appeal and is "optimistic that we will ultimately prevail in this matter."

The lawsuit, filed about five years ago by the Minneapolis Sports Facilities Commission against Chemical Fabrics and others involved in the design and construction of the roof, centered on the performance of a snow-melting system, Mr. Montopoli said.
