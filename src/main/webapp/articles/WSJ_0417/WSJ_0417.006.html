Israeli Consumer Prices Up

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/17/90

JERUSALEM -- Israel's consumer price index rose 1.2% in March, the government said. The price index measures price changes in a fixed market-basket of goods and services. The state-run Central Bureau of Statistics attributed the increase to price boosts in all the items making up the basket, apart from clothes and footwear.

An increase of 2.2% was measured in housing costs, and it followed a rise of 1.3% in February. Rents and home sale prices have been pushed up because of a housing shortage caused by an influx of Soviet Jews to Israel.
