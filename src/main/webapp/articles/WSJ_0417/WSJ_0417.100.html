Business Brief -- Universal Foods Corp.: Fiscal 2nd-Quarter Profit Rose 19% to $10 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/17/90

Universal Foods Corp., citing the benefits of a continuing push into what it calls "high growth, high return businesses," said net income for its second quarter ended March 31 climbed 19%. The Milwaukee-based food products concern reported earnings of $10 million, or 40 cents a share, up from the year-earlier $8.4 million, or an adjusted 34 cents a share. Per-share earnings figures reflect an increase in shares outstanding to 25.3 million from the year-earlier 24.9 million, which was adjusted for a 3-for-2 stock split paid last October.

Revenue rose 8.9% to $219.7 million from $201.6 million.

The company said it is "confident that our shareholders will see a strong increase in earnings during fiscal 1990."

For the fiscal first half, Universal's net increased 21% to $23.4 million, or 93 cents a share, from $19.2 million, or 78 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue rose 14% to $444.1 million from $389.3 million.
