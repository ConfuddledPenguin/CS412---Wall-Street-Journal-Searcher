What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/17/90

IBM'S PROFIT SURGED 9.2% in the first quarter, a surprisingly strong performance that included the company's best U.S. results in years. The report sent IBM's stock up $3.625, to $110.75. But some analysts cautioned that the computer giant's outlook for all of 1990 remains uncertain. ---

Stocks finished mixed after a blue-chip rally, triggered by IBM's earnings report, was choked off by profit-taking and slumping bonds. The Dow Jones industrials closed up 11.26, at 2763.06. The dollar also gained.

---

Saudi Arabia urged other OPEC members to cut excess oil production to halt the recent slide in prices. Prices rose on U.S. futures and spot markets following the announcement.

---

Four major banks posted generally weak quarterly earnings, reflecting varying factors. Chase and Manufacturers Hanover reported declines. Morgan and Banc One had gains.

---

Bank of New England will set up a new entity to take over much of its troubled real estate assets, industry officials said. The plan may resemble programs formed by other banks.

---

Eight publicly traded funds that were spawned by troubled VMS Realty Partners took big charges to cover possible bad loans, resulting in steep losses for the fourth quarter.

---

Eli Lilly's profit rose 20% in the first quarter. The drug maker plans to buy back up to $1 billion of stock and certain convertible warrants.

---

Magazine publishers are expecting an industry shakeout in coming months due to a sharp drop in ad pages at many major magazines.

Time Warner is making a major effort to attract readers to its faltering Entertainment Weekly.

---

Wheeling-Pittsburgh's shareholders group agreed to back a rival reorganization plan, marking a breakthrough in the steelmaker's five-year bankruptcy proceedings.

---

Dataproducts agreed to be acquired for $160 million by two Hitachi affiliates, apparently ending two years of turmoil at the firm.

---

American Air withdrew a plan to boost some air fares by as much as 23% after most other carriers failed to match the proposed increases.

---

Resorts International filed an amended Chapter 11 reorganization plan that includes selling its Paradise Island property in the Bahamas.

---

Primerica's profit jumped 62% in the first quarter, reflecting good performances in its three main businesses. Revenue climbed about 17%.

---

The Supreme Court agreed to decide whether workers may sue their employers under state law for allegedly firing them to avoid having to pay pension benefits.
