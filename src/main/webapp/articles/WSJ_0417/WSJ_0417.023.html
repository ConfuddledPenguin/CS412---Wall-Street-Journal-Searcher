Japanese Firms' Mergers Rise

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/17/90

TOKYO -- Japanese companies were involved in 740 mergers and acquisitions in the fiscal year ended March 31, up from 556 a year earlier, a Yamaichi Securities Co. survey found. The survey included mergers and acquisitions by Japanese corporations domestically and overseas, as well as those of Japanese concerns by foreign companies. Officially announced mergers and acquisitions activities in the latest fiscal year, 400 cases of the total, had a total value of 3.672 trillion yen ($23.2 billion).

Japanese purchases of foreign concerns increased to 448 from 334, Yamaichi said. Foreign concerns acquired or merged with only 14 Japanese companies in fiscal 1990. Mergers and acquisitions between Japanese companies totaled 14.

European companies were a focus of Japanese mergers and acquisitions, which doubled to 114. Japanese companies are pursuing entry to the region ahead of the creation of a unified market in the European Community at the end of 1992.

Of the total cases, 226 involved U.S. companies, up from 163 a year earlier.
