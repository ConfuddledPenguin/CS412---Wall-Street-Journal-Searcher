National Bancshares Gets a Court Order That Restrains Bank

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/17/90

SAN ANTONIO, Texas -- National Bancshares Corp. of Texas said a state judge here granted it a temporary restraining order, preventing another Texas bank from interfering with its recapitalization by trying to collect on a $4,950,000 loan. National Bancshares is trying to complete a recapitalization with help from Equimark Corp. and the Federal Deposit Insurance Corp. But, it said, a special asset bank managed by Team Bank on behalf of the FDIC has indicated it might try to foreclose on three of National Bancshares' most profitable banks, which were used as collateral to secure the loan.

In a news release, National Bancshares said the Federal Asset Management Co., a Team Bank unit that is liquidating assets for the FDIC, had notified National Bancshares that it was in default of the loan as of Dec. 31. National Bancshares said it had been negotiating an agreement with Federal Asset that called for National Bancshares to repay the debt shortly after its recapitalization is completed. But National Bancshares said Team Bank and Federal Asset have acted "in a manner contrary" to agreements and understandings of the parties.

A National Bancshares spokesman refused to elaborate on the dispute, saying the matter is in litigation. A Team Bank spokesman also declined to comment.

National Bancshares said the loan is secured by its NBC Bank-Laredo, NBC Bank-Eagle Pass and NBC Bank-Rio Grande Valley. At March 31, 1989, those three banks accounted for about 80% of the remaining positive shareholders' equity of all the company's subsidiary banks.

National Bancshares said the temporary restraining order expires April 27, and it intends to seek a temporary injunction on that date.

Team Bank recently acquired the former subsidiary banks of Texas American Bancshares Inc. of Fort Worth, Texas, with help from the FDIC.
