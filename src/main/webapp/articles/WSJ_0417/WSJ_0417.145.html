Business Brief -- Farmers Group Inc.: Delay Is Sought in Effort To Acquire Insurance Firm

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/17/90

Axa-Midi Assurances and Hoylake Investments Ltd. requested a delay of up to two months in regulatory deliberations now under way regarding Axa's proposed acquisition of Farmers Group Inc. Axa, a French insurance concern, said the delay would allow time for "modifications" of its $4.5-billion bid for Los Angeles-based Farmers. The move comes after insurance regulators in California last week declined to approve the hostile bid, citing the high leverage involved.

The bid is part of Sir James Goldsmith's #13 billion hostile takeover attempt of B.A.T Industries PLC, the British tobacco, retailing, paper and financial services concern that is Farmers' parent.

If Sir James's Hoylake succeeds in acquiring B.A.T, it would sell Farmers to Axa. But any change in control of Farmers needs approval of insurance regulators in all nine states where Farmers operates.

"This brief hold will allow us to adjust our transaction to answer the concerns expressed by U.S. regulators," said Axa Chairman Claude Bebear. "We are dedicated to relentlessly pursuing our bid for Farmers within the regulatory process in the U.S."

A Farmers spokesman said, "It's our belief that the problems identified by the California regulators with their proposal aren't fixable. Axa doesn't have the size and financial resources to acquire Farmers in a way that wouldn't jeopardize our policyholders."
