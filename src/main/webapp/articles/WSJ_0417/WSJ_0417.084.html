Occidental Petroleum Starts Production Test Of Oil Field Off China

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/17/90

LOS ANGELES -- Occidental Petroleum Corp. said its Occidental Eastern subsidiary began an extended production test of the Lufeng field in the South China Sea. The Lufeng 22-1-3 well, drilled late last year, began producing at about 10,000 barrels a day of 31 degree API gravity oil.

In a shortcut of the usual process, Occidental will use the exploratory well as a production well. Oil flows from the well to a semi-submersible floating platform and then into a converted 45,000-deadweight-ton tanker that acts as a floating production, storage and off-loading vessel. Occidental said that "safety and environmental concerns have been addressed in the design criteria."

The Lufeng discovery well, Lufeng 22-12, was drilled early last year and was tested for 12 days last June at 10,600 barrels of oil a day.

Occidental said the test is being conducted to confirm design parameters and reservoir performance prior to full-scale development in 1991 of the field, which is located about 120 miles off the coast of China.

Occidental Eastern is operator of the project, with a 65% stake. Ampol Exploration and AGL Petroleum of Australia each have a 10% interest. Repsol Exploracion S.A. of Spain has a 15% interest.
