REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: Ortega's `Grave Situation'

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/17/90

Danny Ortega held a news conference yesterday to sound the clear message that in Nicaragua it ain't over 'til it's over. He said that the April 25 inauguration day for President-elect Violeta Chamorro was by no means a certain date. The voters may have spoken in February, but Mr. Ortega may not have been listening. No hand-over of power from the Sandinistas will occur, he said, until the Contras are totally disarmed. "We are analyzing this situation in order to find an adequate response to this so critical, so difficult and so grave situation in Nicaragua," Mr. Ortega said.

These threats from Mr. Ortega may have only been his way of jockeying before negotiations begin this week in Managua with the Contras. It is also possible, however, that they could signify something rather more ominous. "We are very close to peace and very close to war," Mr. Ortega said. In other words, there is still very good reason that the Contras are armed.
