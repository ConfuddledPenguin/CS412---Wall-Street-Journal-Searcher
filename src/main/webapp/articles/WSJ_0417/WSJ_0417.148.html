Business Brief -- AmVestors Financial Corp.: Junk Bond Woes Result In Loss for Fourth Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/17/90

AmVestors Financial Corp. said it had a net loss of $7.9 million, or 55 cents a share, for the fourth quarter ended Dec. 31, largely because of losses related to junk bonds. In the fourth quarter of 1988, the Topeka, Kan.-based life insurance concern had net income of about $2 million, or 15 cents a share.

AmVestors said its fourth-quarter results include $16.3 million in investment losses, most of which were related to high-yield bonds issued by real-estate concerns. In the fourth quarter of 1988, it had investment losses of $930,158.

The company said it stopped purchasing high-yield bonds at year end, and is now trying to reduce its junk-bond holdings to less than 20% of assets by the end of 1990, from about 30% of assets at Dec. 31.

The fourth quarter also included a $6.1 million after-tax gain related to a co-insurance agreement in which AmVestors ceded 90% of the risk of its single-premium, whole-life policies written before 1989.

For the year, AmVestors had a net loss of $4.5 million, or 31 cents a share, after investment losses of $18.7 million. In 1988, AmVestors had net income of $6.6 million, or 58 cents a share, after investment losses of $579,428.
