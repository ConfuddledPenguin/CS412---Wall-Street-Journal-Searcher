Bank of New York Steps Up Pressure Over Merger Terms

  • Author: Robert Guenther
  • Date: 04/17/90

NEW YORK -- Bank of New York Co. escalated its war of nerves with Northeast Bancorp over the terms of their pending $400 million merger agreement. On one hand, Bank of New York asked the Federal Reserve Board for permission to buy Northeast Bancorp as part of a seven-year-old agreement, now that Connecticut has removed the last legal impediment to the transaction.

However, at the same time, Bank of New York raised questions whether the transaction will be completed on the existing terms. In the filing, the Bank of New York estimated Shelton, Conn.-based Northeast's book value at $12.31 a share, compared with Northeast's year-end estimate of $33 a share.

Furthermore, Bank of New York said in its filing that it had advised Northeast that it should "seek other bidders." Earlier this year, Bank of New York said that it would fire 700 Northeast employees if the merger as presently structured were completed.

Northeast, the parent of Connecticut's third-largest commercial bank, has said that it will seek to enforce its merger agreement.

Bank of New York has agreed to pay 250% of Northeast's book value, provided Northeast's nonperforming assets are no more than 150% of its peer group of banks. Furthermore, Bank of New York can cancel the agreement if regulators impose conditions that would be onerous to Bank of New York shareholders.

For the first quarter, Northeast posted a $6.5 million loss, compared to earnings of $8 million a year ago, as loan quality worsened. A Bank of New York spokesman said that he didn't know whether Northeast's nonperforming loans now exceeded 150% of its peer banks' levels.
