Dataproducts Agrees to Be Acquired By Hitachi Venture for $160 Million

  • Author: Rhonda L. Rundle
  • Date: 04/17/90

WOODLAND HILLS, Calif. -- Dataproducts Corp. said it signed a definitive agreement to be acquired for $160 million by a joint venture formed by two affiliates of Hitachi Ltd., the Tokyo electronic equipment maker. The affiliates, Hitachi Koki Co. and Nissei Sangyo Co., said they will launch a $10-a-share tender offer within five days for all of Dataproducts' stock outstanding. The offer will be followed by a merger in which any remaining shares will be acquired at the same price.

In American Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Dataproducts jumped $3.25 to $9.625 on volume of more than 1.5 million shares.

The agreement appears to end two years of turmoil during which time Dataproducts fended off a hostile suitor, sought "strategic" bidders to buy the company, sold its headquarters building and Southern California manufacturing operations, and used those proceeds to repurchase 20% of its stock.

The proposed merger gives Dataproducts the "strategic partner" it has been searching for. Jack C. Davis, chairman and chief executive officer, said he expects to remain in those posts and that no immediate changes in company programs are expected. Mr. Davis said he anticipates that he will be joined on the board by representatives of Hitachi Koki and Nissei Sangyo.

"There's good chemistry here; we have similar kinds of motivations for long-term investments and a belief in focusing on quality products," Mr. Davis said. "As time goes along, we'll be looking for ways to gain" from the new ownership.

Mr. Davis said that Dataproducts, a computer printer maker with 1989 sales of $353 million, is strong in the U.S. and Canada. Nissei Sangyo, Hitachi's 56.9%-owned trading arm, had world-wide sales of 482 billion ($3.05 billion) yen last year. "They have strengths in many, many countries we don't have a presence in," Mr. Davis said.

Hitachi Koki, 22.5% owned by Hitachi, had sales of 109 billion yen last year and makes power tools, printers and scientific instruments. In the printer business, "we compete with Hitachi Koki at the top of our line and bottom of their line," Mr. Davis said.

DPC Acquisition Corp., which was forced to terminate a $10-a-share tender offer for Dataproducts in January because it couldn't obtain financing, expressed disappointment in the merger agreement. The New York investment group, which holds about one million Dataproducts shares, is controlled by Crescott Investment Associates, Wilson Investment Group, Kernel Corp. and Catalyst Partners.

"Management should have been able to obtain a better price. A strategic buyer can pay more for a company's assets than a financial buyer," said Micki Hidayatallah, Crescott's chief executive officer. He said it wasn't clear whether DPC would or could take any action, in light of a standstill agreement with Dataproducts.

Dataproducts's board believes that the merger agreement "is in the best interests of shareholders, as well as our employees, customers and suppliers," Mr. Davis said. The bid is fully financed, whereas "DPC was never able to raise any financing or a `highly confident' letter," he added.
