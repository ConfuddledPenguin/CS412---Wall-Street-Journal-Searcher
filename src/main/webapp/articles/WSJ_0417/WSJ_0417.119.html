Business Brief -- Imasco Ltd.: Chief Agrees With Forecasts On Rise in Profit for Year

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/17/90

Analysts' estimates of a 13% to 18% increase in Imasco Ltd.'s net income this year are "in the ballpark," said Purdy Crawford, chairman, president and chief executive. The estimates of 1990 per-share net range from 3.25 to 3.40 Canadian dollars (US$2.79-$2.92). In 1989, Imasco reported net income of C$366.1 million, or C$2.87 a share, on net revenue of C$4.24 billion. Net revenue excludes sales and excise taxes collected.

"We expect a good year," Mr. Crawford said in an interview. "We think there will be growth at each unit." The Montreal tobacco, financial services, fast food and retailing concern is 40.4%-owned by Britain's B.A.T Industries PLC.

Earnings at Imasco's Imperial Tobacco unit will grow in each of the next five years even as cigarette consumption in Canada continues to decline, Mr. Crawford said. Imasco expects its U.S. fast-food unit, Hardee's, to post "modest market-share growth" this year. In 1989, Hardee's had a 9.9% share of customer traffic in the sandwich category of the fast-food market.
