Business Brief -- Pittston Co.: Estimate of 1st-Period Loss Shrinks; Strong March Cited

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/17/90

Pittston Co., Greenwich, Conn., reduced its first-quarter loss estimate to 27 cents a share from 35 cents, citing strong results in March. The coal, security and transportation concern had 1989 first-quarter profit of $15 million, or 40 cents a share, including a credit of $4 million, or 11 cents a share, from a tax-loss carry-forward.

The latest month's performance showed a turnaround from heavy losses in the first two months of 1990, the company said. Pittston noted an especially strong showing by its Brink's and Brink's Home Security operations, healthy American Eagle and Pyxis coal profit centers, and a $1.3 million gain on a small sale of reserves in its coal group. Pittson also said its Burlington Air Express unit is benefiting from cost-control programs.

The company said final results for the latest quarter will be released in about a week.
