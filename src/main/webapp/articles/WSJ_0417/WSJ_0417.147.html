Business Brief -- International American Homes: New Jersey Builder Files For Chapter 11 Protection

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/17/90

International American Homes Inc. said it filed for protection from creditor lawsuits under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code. The Clark, N.J.-based company builds and sells houses through its subsidiaries and related partnerships, which also have filed Chapter 11 petitions. International American Homes operates in the Washington, D.C., area as well as in parts of California and Florida.

The company said the filing was to give it time to respond to a cash shortage caused by a significant reduction in home and land sales, and the loss of anticipated financing that occurred from changes in banking regulations.

Officials couldn't be reached for comment.

In February, one of the company's affiliated partnerships defaulted on bank loans, and a unit was late in paying interest on a "substantial bank facility." At the time, International American said the events were likely to hurt its current loan arrangements and that it planned to restructure itself into a smaller operation.

International American had a loss in its third quarter ended Dec. 31 of $7.6 million, or $1.07 a share, on revenue of $43.1 million.
