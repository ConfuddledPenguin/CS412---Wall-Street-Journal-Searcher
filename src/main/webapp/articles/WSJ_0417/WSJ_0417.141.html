Business Brief -- Boise Cascade Corp.: Forest Products Firm's Net Slumped 57% in 1st Period

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/17/90

Boise Cascade Corp. said first-quarter net income plunged 57%, largely as a result of depressed prices for its paper products line. The Boise, Idaho, forest products concern posted profit of $33.6 million, or 79 cents a share, compared with $78.3 million, or $1.74 a share, in the year-earlier quarter.

Revenue declined 3% to $1.07 billion from $1.1 billion. The fall in both profit and revenue resulted from an 11% drop in sales from the company's paper and paper products division, which makes up about half of Boise Cascade's revenue. The division posted sales of $572.4 million, down from $643 million; the company blamed sluggish demand and lower prices for most paper grades, as well as increased manufacturing costs.

In the latest quarter, the company had an average of 38.1 million shares outstanding, down from 45 million. The company said the decline reflects a stock buy-back plan.
