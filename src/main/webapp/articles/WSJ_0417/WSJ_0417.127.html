Business Brief -- Borland International Inc.: Record Profit and Revenue Posted for Fourth Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/17/90

Propelled by brisk sales of its electronic spreadsheet program, Borland International Inc. said it posted record profit and revenue for its fourth quarter ended March 31. The Scotts Valley, Calif., software maker said net income more than doubled to $4.2 million, or 29 cents a share, on revenue of $34.8 million. In the year-earlier quarter, Borland had a profit of $2 million, or 17 cents a share, on revenue of $25.9 million. In the year, Borland said net grew to $11.8 million, or 90 cents a share, compared with a loss of $2.8 million, or 24 cents a share. Revenue rose 25% to $113.3 million from $90.5 million.

Borland attributed the improvement largely to heavy sales of its Quattro Pro electronic spreadsheet program, which it began shipping during its third quarter. The company also said results were aided by continued strong demand for the latest version of its Paradox database program, which it introduced a year ago.
