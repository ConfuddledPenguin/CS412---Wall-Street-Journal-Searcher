LITHUANIA CALLED for talks with Gorbachev about Moscow's economic threats. Amid reports that the Baltic republic was prepared to compromise on certain independence-related issues, Lithuania appealed in a telegram for a meeting with the Soviet leader to discuss details of the Kremlin's threatened economic sanctions. The move followed a meeting of the republic's leadership. Gorbachev, in an ultimatum issued Friday, gave Lithuania 48 hours to end its secession bid or face a cutoff of crucial products. Moscow apparently hasn't taken any action.

Vilnius Radio reported that "political decisions" about Gorbachev's ultimatum would be addressed during a meeting today of Lithuania's parliament.

NEPAL'S KING ASKED the once-banned opposition to form an interim government.

Bowing to demands for further democratic change, King Birendra asked the Nepali Congress Party to form a government to rule until the country's first free elections in nearly three decades are held. The opposition party and the seven-party United Left Front led nationwide pro-democracy protests that began in February. The move came after the monarch dissolved the nonpartisan national assembly and announced the resignation of the prime minister.

The concessions followed the king's April 8 decision to lift a 29-year-old ban on political parties in an effort to end the protests in the Himalayan nation.

The Supreme Court refused to hear the appeal of Dalton Prejean, a retarded man scheduled to be executed in Louisiana last November for the shooting death of a state trooper. Prejean alleged, among other things, that his rights had been violated because he is black but was convicted by a jury from which blacks were excluded.

---

A leaking gas cylinder exploded aboard a moving commuter train in northeastern India, killing at least 80 people and injuring 65 others, authorities said. The number of passengers wasn't known, and the casualty toll is expected to rise. Fire swept through the 16-car train as it traveled in Bihar state, about 500 miles southeast of New Delhi.

India's government banned eight militant Moslem groups in Kashmir and closed two newspapers in its latest effort to control unrest in the disputed territory. At least 270 people have been killed in a three-month-old independence drive in the region.

---

Nicaragua's Ortega raised the possibility that the April 25 inauguration of President-elect Violeta Chamorro could be postponed if the Contra rebels aren't disarmed by that date. In Washington, Bush suggested that a congressional delay on a Nicaraguan aid package would be an affront to Chamorro.

---

Election returns in Yugoslavia showed that a center-right coalition won a legislative majority in Slovenia's April 8 multiparty balloting. The final results will allow the six-party alliance in the northern republic to form the nation's first non-Communist regional government in 45 years.

---

Delegates from 17 nations gathered for a White House-backed parley today on global warming, amid criticism that Bush isn't dealing with the problem. At the two-day meeting, the U.S. is expected to urge increased international research on the greenhouse effect and its economic implications.

---

The U.S. and Soviet Union were urged in a National Academy of Sciences report to follow a coordinated but independent exploration of Mars with robots. The study's head said he envisioned a $10 billion project in which the U.S. launched three robotic "excursions" to Mars as early as the year 2000.

---

Mandela attended a rock concert at London's Wembley Stadium and, in an address to the crowd of about 72,000 people, the 71-year-old black nationalist leader urged the world to maintain anti-apartheid sanctions against South Africa. Prime Minister Thatcher already has eased such curbs.

---

Lawyers in Cincinnati entered innocent pleas for the city's Contemporary Arts Center and its director on misdemeanor charges alleging that photographs in an exhibit of the works of the late Robert Mapplethorpe are obscene. Some of the photos are of nude children and homosexual acts. A pretrial hearing was scheduled for April 30.

---

Gelindo Bordin of Italy won the 94th Boston Marathon. The Olympic champion's finishing time was 2:08:19. Rosa Mota of Portugal, also a reigning Olympic titlist, won the women's division in a time of 2:25:24.
