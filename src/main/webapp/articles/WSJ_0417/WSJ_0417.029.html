Technology & Medicine: Sony Said Ready To Build TV Sets In Pennsylvania

  • Author: Alecia Swasy
  • Date: 04/17/90

PITTSBURGH -- Sony Corp. of Japan is expected to announce today that it will assemble television sets in a New Stanton, Pa., factory formerly operated by Volkswagen AG. The expected move is a victory for Pennsylvania and its governor, Robert P. Casey, who has lobbied hard for years to find a new tenant for the mothballed factory. Gov. Casey is expected to be here today to announce details of the state's agreement with Sony. The governor's office declined to comment.

The new Sony factory initially will employ 1,000 people to make television sets in the 2.8 million-square-foot plant, which Volkswagen closed in 1988. Sony employment there is expected to climb to 2,000.

The plant eventually could produce high-definition television sets, people close to the negotiations said. Sony officials declined to comment on their plans.

Sony, which already operates five U.S. plants, makes most of its television sets for the U.S. market in facilities in California.

For the expansion, Sony considered sites in Kentucky, Ohio and other states before selecting the western Pennsylvania factory. Those states have frequently offered hefty incentive packages to other Japanese companies, which are eager to establish U.S. manufacturing sites.

In this instance, Pennsylvania appears to have agreed to foot at least part of Sony's costs. The package calls for the state to buy the Volkswagen plant from the West German auto maker and then lease it to Sony's U.S. division, officials said.

The state also offered Sony loans for infrastructure projects, such as sewer and water lines. The incentive package also includes job training funds, the people familiar with the situation said.

Sony is expected to assemble the TV sets at the Pennsylvania site using imported parts from a variety of international suppliers. Some could come from Sony's own factories in Asia, as well as other suppliers outside the U.S.
