Business Brief -- Temple-Inland Inc.: First-Quarter Net Rose 14%; Drop in Tax Rate Is Cited

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/17/90

Temple-Inland Inc. reported first-quarter earnings increased 14% because of a reduced tax rate. Pretax operating results declined by about 4.7% due to lower margins in its paper and building products divisions and in spite of improved results in its financial services and bleached paper operations. The Diboll, Texas, company reported that earnings declined in its building products group because of "softness in building activity." Earnings in the company's container and containerboard businesses also declined, the result of price declines and increased costs. The company said its building products earnings have begun improving in the second quarter, but it expects continued "margin erosion" in its paper products businesses for the rest of the year.

For the first quarter, the company reported earnings of $62.2 million, or $1.12 a share, compared with $54.5 million, or 98 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue increased about 25% to $591.2 million from $473.5 million, while manufacturing sales increased 3% to $477 million from $462.6 million.
