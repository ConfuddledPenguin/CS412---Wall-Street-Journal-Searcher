Business Brief -- Analog Devices Inc.: Purchase Accord Is Reached On Integrated-Circuits Firm

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/17/90

Analog Devices Inc. said it has agreed in principle to buy closely held Precision Monolithics Inc. for $80 million in cash plus warrants to purchase $1 million of Analog stock at $12 a share. The purchase is subject to regulatory approval and the approval of an agreement by the board and Precision's owners. Precision, Santa Clara, Calif., makes linear and mixed-signal integrated circuits and had 1989 sales of $88 million.

In a statement, Analog, a maker of electronic parts based in Norwood, Mass., said it decided to disclose discussions with Precision so that it could resume the buy-back of Analog stock on the open market pursuant to its previously authorized stock buy-back program. Analog's president, Ray Stata, said that "given the recent decline in the market price of Analog's stock, we believe it is in the best interests of Analog and its shareholders to resume the repurchase program at prices we consider to be attractive."

Analog stock rose 12.5 cents yesterday to close at $7.375 a share in New York Stock Exchange composite trading.
