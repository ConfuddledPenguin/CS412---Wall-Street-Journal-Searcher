International: Bull Agrees to Pay Zenith $15 Million to End Dispute

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/17/90

PARIS -- Cie. des Machines Bull will pay $15 million to Zenith Electronics Corp. to end a two-year dispute and complete its acquisition of Zenith's personal-computer business. In Glenview, Ill., Zenith said it will take a charge of about $11 million in the first quarter because the negotiated final payment was less than the net receivable for the sold business that it had recorded on its books at year end.

Bull agreed to the purchase for $635 million last October. The sale was subject to approval by Zenith stockholders. By the time the stockholders approved the deal in December, Bull officials asserted that the assets of Zenith's once fast-growing personal computer division had deteriorated, and they paid Zenith $496.4 million.

Bull remained dissatisfied even at that lower price and demanded a refund of $49 million after a review of Zenith's books. In March Zenith not only rejected that claim but declared it was entitled to an additional $49 million.

To resolve the matter, both companies have agreed that Bull should pay Zenith $15 million, bringing the price to $511.4 million.

With the Zenith acquisition, Bull hopes to enter the personal-computer big leagues. It more than doubles the size of the French computer maker's personal-computer business.
