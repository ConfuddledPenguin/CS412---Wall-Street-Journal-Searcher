Business Brief -- National Business Systems Inc.: Holders Clear New Name, Plan for Recapitalization

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/17/90

National Business Systems Inc. said shareholders approved a recapitalization plan that includes changing the Toronto company's name to Consolidated NBS Inc. The plan also calls for consolidating the outstanding common shares on a 1-for-10 basis and creating three new classes of preferred shares. It didn't give the value of the new preferred issues.

At last report, National Business had about 16 million shares outstanding.

The company said its noteholders will exchange 20 million Canadian dollars ($17.2 million) of senior subordinated notes for an equivalent amount of debentures convertible into Class AAA cumulative participating preferred shares.

The company also plans a rights offering which would allow shareholders to purchase an additional C$20 million of the convertible debentures.

Hees International Bancorp Inc., which was retained in 1987 to manage and turn around the troubled credit-card accessory manufacturing concern, said it will take any convertible debentures not subscribed for under the offering.
