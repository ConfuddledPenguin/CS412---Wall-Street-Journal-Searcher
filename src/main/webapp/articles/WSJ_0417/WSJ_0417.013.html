Woodward's Ltd. Plans Issue of Class A Shares

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/17/90

VANCOUVER, British Columbia Woodward's Ltd. said it plans to raise about 40 million Canadian dollars (US$34.4 million) through an issue of Class A shares, and an accompanying rights offering to current holders. Woodward's, a century-old department store chain of which the founding Woodward family relinquished control last fall to a group including Cambridge Shopping Centres Ltd. of Toronto, said funds from the offering will be used to help finance its turnaround program.

Woodward's had a previously reported net loss last year of C$59 million, or C$3.10 a share, on revenue of C$706.9 million. The results included special charges of C$17.2 million, or 90 Canadian cents a share, reflecting costs associated with the layoff of about 20% of the chain's full-time staff.

Woodward's didn't disclose terms of the planned offering. It has about 15.3 million Class A shares outstanding.

In Toronto Stock Exchange trading yesterday, Woodward's Class A shares closed at C$1.80 each, down 10 Canadian cents.

Wood Gundy Inc., a Toronto-based securities dealer, will act as agent for the offering, the company said.
