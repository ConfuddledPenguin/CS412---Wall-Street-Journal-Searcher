Baldwin Technology Is Planning to Acquire Dyson-Kissner Units

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/17/90

ROWAYTON, Conn. -- Baldwin Technology Corp. plans to expand its printing-equipment manufacturing operations by acquiring two subsidiaries of Dyson-Kissner-Moran Corp. for $44 million. The two subsidiaries, Misomex AB of Stockholm and Misomex of North America Inc., Chicago, manufacture so-called prepress equipment -- machines that, among other things, impose images onto printing plates.

The acquisition would expand Baldwin's lines of equipment for the pressroom -- including presses -- and for post-press processes, including machines for labeling, inserting, stacking and bundling, said Kenneth W. Reynolds, Baldwin's chief financial officer.

For the year ended Dec. 31, 1989, for Misomex AB and Jan. 31, 1990, for Misomex of North America, the companies had combined net income of more than $4 million on $40 million of sales. For the six months ended Dec. 31, Baldwin earned $5.3 million, or 32 cents a share, on $83.4 million of sales.

Dyson-Kissner-Moran, a closely held New York investment firm, recently sold its printing-press business to a subsidiary of an East German company, said John H. FitzSimons, DKM's secretary and general counsel. "Misomex is more valuable to a company like Baldwin, which is in related businesses," he said. DKM has a number of remaining interests, including real estate, oil and gas, and insurance.

The transaction is subject to clearance by the Swedish government and by U.S. regulators.

In American Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Baldwin's shares rose 12.5 cents to close at $8.625.

Corrections & Amplifications

BALDWIN TECHNOLOGY Co. produces accessories and controls for printing equipment. In Tuesday's edition it was incorrectly indicated that the company was considering expanding into manufacturing printing presses.
