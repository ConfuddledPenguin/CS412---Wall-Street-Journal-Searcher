NII Board Expansion Is Supported by 54% Of Company's Shares

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/17/90

PITTSBURGH -- Centaur Partners Group said holders of 54% of National Intergroup Inc.'s shares backed the group's proposal to expand the company's board to 15 from 12. However, company bylaws require 80% support to change the current board number. Centaur, which holds a 16.5% stake in National Intergroup, has asked the Delaware Supreme Court to rule that only a simple majority be required to change the bylaws. Should Centaur prevail, stockholders would elect eight directors at this year's annual meeting in July.

Centaur said the vote indicated that a majority of stockholders have issued an "overwhelming mandate regarding the future of NII." Furthermore, the investor group said it believes the company should be swayed by the majority to reduce to 45 days the advance notice of director nominees. As a result, Centaur said it would forward a written notice containing its eight nominees for election at the 1990 annual meeting.

However, National Intergroup, a steel, drug-distribution and retail concern, said Centaur failed to receive the necessary support to "pack the NII board." Moreover, the company noted that only 29% of the company's 21.8 million shares outstanding, excluding the 25% owned by the Centaur Group and another dissident group, Walker Street Associates, backed the move.

"These numbers indicate that the dissidents received the support of substantially less than a majority of the independent shareholders," the company noted. The current board structure was approved by National Intergroup shareholders in 1984.

The company last week filed suit asking that Centaur and Walker Street Associates be forced to divest themselves of their holdings. The lawsuit alleges that the two groups have made materially false and misleading statements in their solicitation of proxies and are acting together as a group. Centaur denied the allegations and Walker didn't comment on them.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, National Intergroup closed at $16, up 12.5 cents.
