Business Brief -- Financial Corp. of Santa Barbara: Thrift Holding Firm's Loss Grew Sharply in 4th Period

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/17/90

Struggling Financial Corp. of Santa Barbara posted a wider loss for the fourth quarter of $37.9 million, or $6.28 a share, after taking write-downs and increasing reserves associated with new federal thrift capital requirements. In the year earlier, the company had a loss of $9.5 million, or $1.57 a share. The Santa Barbara, Calif., thrift holding concern also disclosed that it had fired its auditor, Deloitte & Touche, over a wide range of disagreements including an auditor's report questioning the "adequacy of the company's system of internal controls." The auditing concern couldn't be reached for comment.

For the year, Financial Corp. had a loss of $60.3 million, or $10.01 a share, compared with a loss of $7.1 million, or $1.24 a share, for 1988.

Financial Corp., which is under regulatory orders prohibiting it from making new loans, said the 1989 loss reflected a write-down of about $12.1 million on the value of preferred stock of a subsidiary and a write-down of $10.1 million of goodwill. The thrift said it also took reserves of $12.3 million to cover write-downs associated with its corporate bond portfolio.
