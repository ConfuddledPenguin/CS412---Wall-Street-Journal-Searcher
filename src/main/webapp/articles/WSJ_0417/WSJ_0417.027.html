Nintendo Launches Campaign to Thwart Video-Game Pirates

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/17/90

REDMOND, Wash. -- Nintendo of America Inc. said it launched a campaign against video-game pirates by filing several federal copyright infringement lawsuits against some retailers and by cooperating with federal customs agents in a recent "sting" of alleged Nintendo game cartridge counterfeiters. The U.S. unit of Nintendo Co. of Japan said the actions were necessary to try to halt the "outrageous theft of Nintendo's valuable intellectual property rights."

In its lawsuits, filed in three states and Canada, Nintendo said it seeks to stop retailers and video-game rental outlets from selling or renting pirated versions of its proprietary video-game cartridges. These versions are so-called multiple game devices that contain as many as 40 counterfeit Nintendo licensed video games, the company said. Nintendo itself doesn't make multiple game cartridges.

Thus far, according to Nintendo, the counterfeit cartridges, most of which come from Taiwan, have been found only in video-game rental outlets or in retail stores that aren't part of Nintendo's authorized dealer network.

Nintendo said 700 such cartridges were seized in the recent arrest of a Taiwanese couple and two U.S. citizens in Wilmington, N.C. The company said the four people offered to sell the cartridges to undercover U.S. Customs agents for $60,000.
