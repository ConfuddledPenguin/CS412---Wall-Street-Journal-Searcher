Business Brief -- Tyler Corp.: Special Meeting Is Slated For Vote on Sale of Unit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/17/90

Tyler Corp. said it set a special meeting May 14 for shareholders to vote on the proposed sale of its Atlas Powder unit to Imperial Chemical Industries PLC, London. The company said terms call for all of its assets and liabilities except Atlas Powder and bank debt estimated at $63 million to be transferred to a new Tyler Corp. Current Tyler shareholders will receive a dividend of one share of the new Tyler and will swap their old Tyler shares for about $120 million of stock in Imperial.

Previously, Tyler said Imperial would offer about $128 million of new stock for the old Tyler shares and would assume $55 million in bank debt. Tyler, a holding company that will be left with a pipe and fittings company when the transaction is completed, said a conversion rate of Imperial stock for old Tyler shares hasn't been set yet because the bank debt to be assumed is subject to change.

However, based on the current estimates and Tyler's 21.3 million shares outstanding, holders of old Tyler stock would receive Imperial stock equal to about $5.63 a share. In addition, $9.9 million in Imperial stock could be paid later to Tyler holders, subject to certain conditions.
