Marketing & Media: The National Geographic's Editor Abruptly Replaced

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/17/90

WASHINGTON (AP) -- The editor of National Geographic magazine was replaced suddenly yesterday, and neither he nor the society that publishes the magazine was saying whether he was fired. "I just don't know the circumstances well enough to deal with it," said Wilbur E. Garrett, 59 years old, who had been editor since 1980.

He added that there had been some "personality conflicts" and some differences of opinion at the magazine, but he wouldn't elaborate on the circumstances of his abrupt departure.

Mr. Garrett was succeeded by William T.E. Graves, 63, who has been a senior member of the magazine's editorial staff for 12 years.

Mr. Graves becomes the eighth editor in the 102-year history of the publication that has explored the world and the creatures that inhabit it. The magazine is sent to the 10 million members of the National Geographic Society.

Robert Sims, a spokesman for the society, said Mr. Garrett's departure and Mr. Graves's promotion take effect immediately.
