Business Brief -- Battery One-Stop Inc.: Group Led by DeBartolo Acquires 13.2% of Company

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/17/90

Edward J. DeBartolo said he and an investment group have acquired 13.2% of Battery One-Stop Inc. The Canadian company issued the DeBartolo group 375,000 units, each unit consisting of one share of common stock and a half warrant to purchase one share per full warrant. The warrants are exercisable for two years, at a price of $5 a share.

As a result, the DeBartolo group holds 1.3 million common shares of Battery One-Stop and warrants for an additional 187,500 shares, as well as additional rights. The DeBartolo holdings represent 19.2% of Battery One-Stop's common stock on a fully diluted basis and 13.2% percent on an undiluted basis. The group plans to purchase another 375,000 common shares of Battery One-Stop from its officers.

The DeBartolo group said the purchase was made for long-term investment. Battery One-Stop, a specialty kiosk retailer of batteries and battery-powered electronic products, said funds raised by the sale will be used to continue expansion in the U.S.
