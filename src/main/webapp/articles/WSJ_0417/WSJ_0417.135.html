Business Brief -- Environmental Power Corp.: Loss Is Reported for 1989; Auditors Raise Questions

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/17/90

Environmental Power Corp., Boston, reported its second consecutive year of big losses and said its auditors raised questions about its ability to "continue as a going concern." The developer of plants that turn waste coal and trash into electrical power reported a net loss of $4.2 million, or 55 cents a share, compared with a loss of $4.7 million, or 61 cents a share, for 1989. Revenue rose to $583,026 from $514,341.

The company said the loss was partly due to $2.5 million in reserves related to a former unit that is developing a waste-to-energy plant in Galveston, Texas.

Environmental Power's auditors, Ernst & Young, said in their opinion that the company's ability to continue may depend on completing financing of its current projects on schedule or else finding alternative financing.

Environmental Power said it believes its "arrangements will be sufficient," but added that there isn't any assurance the financing will be completed on time or on favorable terms.
