Primerica Net Advanced 62% In First Period

  • Author: Johnnie L. Roberts
  • Date: 04/17/90

NEW YORK -- Primerica Corp., reflecting good performance in its three principal business segments, posted a 62% jump in first-quarter profit as revenue rose about 17%. Primerica said its Smith Barney, Harris Upham & Co. unit, despite the tough business climate on Wall Street, performed well, particularly in retail brokerage, asset management and investment banking. Smith Barney's results also positively reflected the acquisition of 16 former offices of Drexel Burnham Lambert Inc., the former Wall Street powerhouse that has sought bankruptcy protection.

In the quarter, Primerica's net soared to $83.2 million, or 73 cents a share, from $51.4 million, or 53 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue at the financial services concern increased to $1.44 billion from a restated $1.24 billion. The year-earlier figure was restated to reflect revenue of Fingerhut's direct-marketing unit, which was classified as "assets held for sale" a year ago, and thus that revenue wasn't included in consolidated figures.

The company also noted that the average number of shares outstanding has been steadily increasing as holders convert shares they owned before Primerica's acquisition by Commercial Credit Inc. to new shares following the transaction. The number of shares averaged 116.9 million in the latest period, compared with 99.4 million a year ago.

In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange yesterday, Primerica closed at $30.25, up 75 cents.

The performance of the consumer services, insurance services and investment services segments showed "continuing improvement in our marketing and product development as well as cost management," said Sanford I. Weill, chairman and chief executive officer.

Profit and revenue of the consumer finance operations both rose 21% to $33 million and $199.3 million, respectively. Primerica cited the integration of the consumer lending business acquired from Barclays American/Financial on March 1 and higher profit from the Fingerhut direct-marketing unit. In addition, the consumer operations continue to post growth in quarter-to-quarter receivables and to keep a tight lid on expenses, Primerica said.

Profit for insurance services rose 37% to $63.2 million on a 12% increase in revenue to $570.8 million. The results reflected Primerica's 100% ownership of the A.L. Williams insurance company unit and the A.L. Williams general agency, which consisted of its sales force. In November, Primerica acquired the 30.2% stake of the insurance company unit it didn't already own and all of the closely held agency.

Investment services, which include Smith Barney, showed a 34% gain to $18.1 million. The results include Primerica's share of earnings from a minority investment and $3.3 million in good-will costs and other adjustments related to the acquisition of Smith Barney. Revenue rose 29% to $441.7 million.

Smith Barney itself earned $13.1 million, up about 15%. Revenue spurted 32% to $384.4 million.
