World Bank Approves Loan of $400 Million To Aid Yugoslavia

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/17/90

WASHINGTON -- The World Bank approved a $400 million loan to Yugoslavia to help the country adopt market-related reforms and expand imports, bank officials said. The bank said the Yugoslavian government will use the credit to promote private-sector initiatives, seek to correct price distortions in its economy, and "improve the allocation and use of resources."

Referring to the country's reform efforts, it added, "Interest rates will be set at positive levels, a market for foreign exchange will be established and foreign-trade and domestic pricing regimes will be liberalized."

The bank also is expected to approve a $200 million credit to Hungary by midyear. The International Monetary Fund previously approved a $598 million loan to Yugoslavia and a $206 million loan to Hungary.

The World Bank's loan to Yugoslavia, to be repaid in 15 years, will carry a variable interest rate, currently 7.75%.
