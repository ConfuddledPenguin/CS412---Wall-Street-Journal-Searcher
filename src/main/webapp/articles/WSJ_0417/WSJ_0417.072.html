Landmark Land to Sell Real Estate to Developer

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/17/90

CARMEL, Calif. -- Landmark Land Co. said it would sell a major portion of its real estate holdings, including its prestigious roster of golf courses, to California developer Barry G. Hon for $967 million. Landmark owns the real estate through Oak Tree Savings, its savings and loan subsidiary based in New Orleans. The sale was triggered by new federal regulations which raised the amount of capital needed to stay in the real estate business.

Mr. Hon, who owns Hon Development Co. of Laguna Hills, Calif., said he wasn't concerned about the weakened real estate market nationally. "Landmark is in the higher end of the business so we're not as concerned as we might be," he said.

In American Stock Exchange composite trading, Landmark closed at $17.375, down 12.5 cents, on relatively heavy volume of 342,500 shares.
