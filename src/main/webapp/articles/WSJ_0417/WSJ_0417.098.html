Business Brief -- Nikko Securities Co.: U.S. Commodity Futures Investment Fund Planned

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/17/90

Nikko Securities Co. of Japan plans to introduce a new type of investment trust fund that includes investment in U.S. commodity futures markets, a company official said. Nikko expects to collect $100 million to $200 million for the new fund at the end of June through private offerings to institutions, the official said.

Operation of a commodity fund isn't allowed under Japan's Ministry of Finance regulations, which limit investment trust funds' portfolios to securities such as stocks and bonds. The Nikko official said the firm has received unofficial ministry approval for the new fund and expects official approval in May.

About 50% of the total amount of the fund will be invested in zero-coupon bonds to guarantee its principal. The rest will be invested in the U.S. futures markets for agricultural products, energy and precious metals.
