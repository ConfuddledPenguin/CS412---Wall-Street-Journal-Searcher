Letters to the Editor: `Shorting Options' Isn't Good for S&Ls

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/17/90

In his March 20 Business World column on the seizure of Franklin Savings Association, Tim W. Ferguson reports that the Office of Thrift Supervision district chief ". . . maintains that Franklin's dealings aren't as arcane as they've been made out to be." While they may not be arcane, they are analytically sophisticated, and I doubt that many federal regulators have the refinement to understand them or to differentiate between risk-reducing and risk-increasing strategies using mortgage derivatives. So I agree with Mr. Ferguson that this kind of "experimentation" does not belong in institutions that receive taxpayer guarantees and require regulatory oversight. By the same token, the traditional practice of making 30-year loans while issuing short-term deposits -- or as Ernest Fleischer of Franklin aptly labels it, "shorting an option" -- doesn't belong there either.

DAVID H. PYLE

Booth Professor of Banking and Finance

Haas School of Business

University of California, Berkeley

Berkeley, Calif.
