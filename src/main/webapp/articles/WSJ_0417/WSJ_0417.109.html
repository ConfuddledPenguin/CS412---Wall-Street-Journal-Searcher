Business Brief -- Brown Brothers Harriman & Co.: Firm Picked by Swiss Bank As U.S. Securities Custodian

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/17/90

Swiss Bank Corp., the Basel-based banking giant, selected Brown Brothers Harriman & Co., a private banking firm based in New York, as its U.S. securities custodian. When foreign banks invest funds in the U.S., "It's impractical to ship the securities back and forth across the Atlantic," said Laurence F. Whittemore, a general partner at Brown Brothers. Foreign banks thus hire U.S. banks to keep custody of the securities. Swiss Bank said last month that it was ending its own U.S. securities-custody business.

Brown Brothers also said it was selected by the State of Alaska Permanent Fund as the global custodian for the pension fund's international securities portfolio. In this case, Mr. Whittemore said, the law requires U.S. pension funds to retain U.S. custodians. Brown Brothers will be custodian for the pension fund's international portfolio, though it will act through a network of subcustodians overseas, he said.
