Vestron Posts Loss For Fourth Quarter On 29% Revenue Fall

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/17/90

STAMFORD, Conn. -- Vestron Inc., struggling in a crowded home-video market, posted a large fourth-quarter loss, compared with a year-earlier profit, with revenue plunging 29%. Vestron also said it is continuing negotiations for its sale to Live Entertainment Inc. of Los Angeles. In February, Live Entertainment said it was discussing a possible acquisition of Vestron in exchange for new Live equity securities.

Vestron's fourth-quarter net loss totaled $47.2 million, or $1.26 a share, compared with year-earlier net income of $1.7 million, or five cents a share. Revenue fell to $66.7 million from $94.4 million.

Vestron blamed the loss on problems related to the move by Security Pacific Bank Corp. in 1988 to back out of a $100 million loan agreement. Vestron said the company was faced with a "constricted cash situation" resulting from the termination of the loan. Vestron, betting on the loan, poured money into an unprofitable video-store chain. When the bank withdrew, the company defaulted on debt agreements.

Live, with estimated 1989 revenue of $450 million, produces and distributes videocassettes and distributes music and personal software.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Vestron stock closed at 75 cents a share, down 6.25 cents.

For 1989, Vestron reported a loss of $135.1 million, or $3.60 a share, compared with net income of $20.6 million, or 55 cents a share, in 1988. Revenue fell 26% to $236.2 million from $321 million.
