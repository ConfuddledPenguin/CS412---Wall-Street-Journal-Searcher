New Securities Issues

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/17/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: MUNICIPALS

King County, Wash. -- the bulk of $130 million of unlimited tax general obligation bonds, 1990 Series A, and limited tax general obligation bonds, 1990 Series B, due Dec. 1, 1992-2009, apparently was won by a First Boston Corp. group with a bid setting the true interest cost at 7.05%. The First Boston group apparently won the $117.6 million of unlimited tax general obligation bonds, 1990 Series A. The Series A bonds, rated double-A-1 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and double-A-plus by Standard & Poor's Corp., were priced for reoffering to yield from 6.10% in 1992 to 7.15% in 2008 and 2009. There are no term bonds. Details on the winner of the remainder of the issue, the Series B limited tax general obligation bonds, weren't readily available.

Connecticut -- $75 million of general obligation capital appreciation bonds, College Savings Plan, 1990 Series A, due May 15, 1995-2010, were tentatively priced by a Smith Barney, Harris Upham & Co. group to yield to maturity from 6.40% in 1995 to 7.15% in 2009 and 2010. There are no term bonds. The bonds are rated double-A by Moody's and S&P and will be sold to retail investors only.

MORTGAGES

Perpetual Savings Bank -- about $200 million of mortgage pass-through certificates was underwritten by Shearson Lehman Hutton Inc. and Salomon Brothers Inc. The certificates represent undivided interests in a pool of one-year, Treasury-indexed, adjustable-rate residential mortgages originated or purchased by Perpetual. The issue was rated double-A-2 by Moody's, Perpetual said. Credit enhancement consists primarily of a pool insurance policy issued by General Electric Mortgage Insurance Co. The pool of mortgage loans has an initial aggregate principal balance of about $200 million and a remaining term to maturity of about 345 months. Interest will be passed through to the certificate holders monthly at a rate equal to the weighted average mortgage note rate less servicing and administrative fees. The initial pass-through rate is about 8.46%. Perpetual will be master servicer.

OTHER

Federal Farm Credit Banks -- $250 million of notes, due May 1, 1992, were priced at par for variable price reoffer. The non-callable notes were priced at a spread of 0.34 percentage point above the Treasury's three-month bill through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets.

Student Loan Marketing Association -- $250 million of notes, due April 24, 1991, was priced at par for variable price reoffer. The non-callable notes were priced at a spread of 0.225 percentage point above the Treasury's three-month bill.
