Marketing & Media: Disney's `Dick Tracy' Nabs Solid Turnout at Opening

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/18/90

"Dick Tracy," Walt Disney Co.'s much ballyhooed summer movie release, opened last weekend on about 2,300 screens around the country with a solid, albeit not mind-numbing, showing. It grossed between $22 million and $23 million, Disney reported. Industry sources independently confirmed these preliminary results. "It augurs very well for the film," said Martin Grove, box office analyst for Baseline, an entertainment information service. He noted that the three-day gross was a Disney record. Last summer, "Batman" broke box-office records with a $40.5 million opening weekend.

Three films vied for second place. Early indications were that "Another 48 Hours," released by Paramount Communications Inc.'s film unit, grossed about $10.4 million. And the premiere of "Gremlins 2," from Time Warner Inc.'s Warner Brothers unit, took in about $10.1 million. Carolco Pictures's "Total Recall" apparently finished fourth at $9.8 million. Official tallies are due out today.

Although Warner officials had been upset that Disney would include in its figures the sales of $12 to $20 "Dick Tracy" T-shirts that counted as admission tickets for special Friday morning showings of the film, the marketing ploy apparently didn't have much of an impact on the box-office race. Industry sources attributed only about $1.25 million of Disney's gross to the T-shirt sales.

The key for the new films' staying power will be their second weekend: As each new mega-hyped release opens, it has knocked back its predecessor: "Back to the Future III" dropped 43% in its second weekend; "Total Recall" dropped 41%. "Another 48 Hours" is expected to have dropped 47%.
