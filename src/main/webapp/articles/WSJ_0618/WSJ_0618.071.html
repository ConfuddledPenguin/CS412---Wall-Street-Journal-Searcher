Honda Will Help Chrysler Sell Some Models in Japan

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/18/90

TOKYO -- Chrysler Corp. will soon get help selling its vehicles from an unlikely ally -- Honda Motor Co. Honda, the target of a recent Chrysler advertising campaign, said it agreed to help the No. 3 U.S. auto maker sell its Jeep Cherokee and Wrangler models in Japan beginning this fall.

Honda said it plans to sell 2,000 Jeep models in 1991. Chrysler sold only 1,500 cars and Jeeps in Japan last year.

Honda and Chrysler officials previously disclosed they were negotiating a distribution agreement, but details weren't announced until Friday.

A Chrysler spokeswoman said there is "potential for discussion about Honda distribution of other Chrysler vehicles" but declined to elaborate.

Honda, which doesn't build off-road vehicles or minivans, said it entered into the agreement to help broaden its appeal in the Japanese market. A Honda spokesman said the Chrysler ad campaign that claims Chrysler models are superior to Honda's Civic and Accord never deterred the Japanese company from going ahead with the marketing alliance.

"This agreement is not a matter of helping anybody; it's a matter of Honda dealers expanding sales," the Honda spokesman said.

Honda said it will sell and service Chrysler vehicles through its 1,375-dealer Primo chain.
