Technology Brief -- Micron Technology Inc.: Chip Maker Says Net Fell By 93% in Fiscal 3rd Period

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/18/90

Micron Technology Inc., continuing to suffer from cutthroat competition in the semiconductor industry, reported net income for its fiscal third quarter plunged 93% to $1.8 million, or five cents a share. Micron's third consecutive quarterly decline in profit came as revenue slipped 29% to $84.1 million from $119.2 million. In the year-earlier quarter, Micron earned $28.8 million, or 75 cents a share.

For the nine months ended May 31, profit plummeted 98% to $1.9 million, or five cents a share, from $90.1 million, or $2.37 a share, a year earlier. Revenue tumbled 34% to $228 million from $343.4 million.

Micron said the earnings declines reflect the effects of "downward price pressure on the company's principal products," as well as a slow and costly updating of its product line to include the so-called one megabit dynamic random access memory chips, or DRAMs.
