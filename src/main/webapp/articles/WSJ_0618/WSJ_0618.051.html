Wahlco Cancels Plan For Debenture Issue As Stock Price Drops

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/18/90

SAN DIEGO -- Wahlco Environmental Systems Inc. canceled plans for a $50 million offering of convertible subordinated debentures and said delays in orders for its core product could reduce 1990 earnings by about 10 cents a share from previous expectations. The maker of air-pollution control systems for utilities said it withdrew the offering because of its eroding stock price, which has fallen from a record $16.625 late last month. In New York Stock Exchange composite trading Friday, Wahlco dropped $2.75 a share to $10.375.

The company didn't disclose what its previous earnings expectations were, but it said it is comfortable with analysts' current expectations of 70 to 75 cents a share for the year. Last year, Wahlco had net income of $10.4 million on revenue of $40.6 million. The company, which has about 17.6 million shares outstanding, didn't report per-share results for last year because it didn't go public until April.

Wahlco blamed the drop in anticipated earnings on delays in several orders for its flue gas conditioning systems. Of particular significance, analysts said, was the delay of a $6 million order by a U.K. concern. Wahlco said it hasn't received contract cancellations from its customers.

Wahlco said it believes clients are delaying their orders until the Clean Air Act is completed by Congress, probably later this year, and that the earnings lost this year "should be realized" in 1991.
