Steel Mill Products Imports Increased 17% In April From March

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/18/90

WASHINGTON -- U.S. imports of steel mill products in April rose 17% from March but remained slightly below the year-earlier month, according to an American Iron and Steel Institute compilation of Commerce Department figures. Imports in April totaled 1,313,000 tons, compared with 1,120,000 tons in March and 1,341,000 tons in April 1989, the trade group said. April imports were 16.4% of total steel consumption, the same percentage as a year earlier but up from 13.5% in March.

For the first four months of 1990, imports totaled 4,975,000 tons, equal to 15.7% of consumption, compared with 5,677,000 tons, or 17% of consumption, a year earlier. The 1990 import share of the market was well below the maximum of 19.1% established under a revised "voluntary" import restraint system that took effect last Oct. 1.

Steel exports in April were 335,000 tons, down 8.2% from March but practically flat with 334,000 tons a year earlier, the trade group said. For the first four months, exports of 1,388,000 tons were down 3.3% from 1,436,000 tons a year earlier.
