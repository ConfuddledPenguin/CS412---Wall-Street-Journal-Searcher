Business Brief -- First Commerce Corp.: Bank's Nonperforming Assets Show $14 Million Increase

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/18/90

First Commerce Corp. said its nonperforming assets have increased $14 million since March 31 and are $6 million higher than it reported at the end of the second quarter of 1989. The primary reason, it said, is the placement on nonaccrual status of several credits at the New Orleans bank. Nonperforming assets now total about $79 million, or 3.4% of all loans.

The company's provision for loan losses is expected to be about $12 million for the second quarter, compared with $8 million in the first quarter and $6 million in the year-earlier period. Because of weaknesses in the loan portfolio, bank officials said, second-quarter earnings are expected to fall in a range of 35 to 40 cents a share, compared with 62 cents a share in the first quarter and 58 cents a share a year earlier.
