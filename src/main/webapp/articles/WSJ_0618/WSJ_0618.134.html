International Brief -- Saab-Scania AB: Group's Pretax Profit Rose 27% in First Four Months

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/18/90

Saab-Scania AB said Friday its pretax profit in the first four months surged 27% to 817 million kronor ($133.9 million) from 642 million kronor in the corresponding period a year ago. The Swedish aerospace and automotive group said consolidated sales rose by 11% to 10.2 billion kronor from 9.2 billion. Consolidated income before appropriations and taxes was 2.52 billion kronor, compared with 642 million in the first four months last year. The increase reflects 1.7 billion kronor from the sale of 50% of Saab's profit-losing car operations to General Motors Corp.

Saab's pretax profit in the first four months was about 130 million kronor above the 690-million-krona average predicted by analysts.

Among business areas, Saab Aircraft reported a 74% surge in sales to 1.65 billion kronor from 951 million, propelled by the success of its turboprop commuter planes, Saab 340 and Saab 2000.

The car division, Saab Automobile, owned jointly by Saab and GM, which started operations Jan. 1, reported a 995-million-krona loss in the first four months.
