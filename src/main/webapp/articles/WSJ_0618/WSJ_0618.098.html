Japan Pegs Spending For Public Works

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 06/18/90

TOKYO -- Japan said it will commit 420 trillion to 450 trillion yen ($2.72 trillion to $2.92 trillion) to public works spending over the next 10 years in response to U.S. calls that it spend more on roads, parks, and other social infrastructure programs. Japan, however, will reject a U.S. request to specify the size of public works spending as a percentage of gross national product, government sources said. The figure of 450 trillion yen could be roughly 8% of Japan's GNP, they said. The plan doesn't include works of the JR Group of railway companies and Nippon Telegraph and Telephone Corp.

Japan's 10-year public works plan will put emphasis on programs aimed at raising living standards and protecting the environment, the sources said. Japan will reveal the plan during the fifth round of bilateral structural impediments initiative talks set for June 25-26 in Tokyo.
