Marketing & Media: Kodak, IBM Agree To Jointly Develop Publishing System

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/18/90

ROCHESTER, N.Y. -- Eastman Kodak Co. and International Business Machines Corp. said they will jointly develop an improved computerized publishing system that permits publishers to add business and other functions to the electronic system. Kodak, of Rochester, N.Y., has been selling Atex publishing systems for several years. The systems control the editorial, advertising and production activities involved in publishing a newspaper or magazine. Kodak said it will contribute that expertise, focusing on imaging and publishing systems software.

IBM, White Plains, N.Y., the world's largest computer company, is a major supplier of computerized business systems to the publishing industry. IBM will provide technical, marketing and development assistance and some financing for the project. The big computer producer and Kodak both declined to disclose how much financing IBM will provide.

Kodak, a photographic products, chemicals and drug company, has a sizable office-equipment business.

Automation suppliers in the past few years have been developing systems that include sales, purchasing, administration and other functions in the same computerized system that controls production. The Kodak-IBM venture intends to supply such a system for publishers. In addition, they want to establish a system that can readily accept and communicate with other equipment that third parties currently manufacture, Kodak said.
