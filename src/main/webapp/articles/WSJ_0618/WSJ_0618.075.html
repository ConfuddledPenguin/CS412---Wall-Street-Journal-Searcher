Madrid Exchange Gets Set To Launch Options Market

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/18/90

MADRID -- The Madrid stock exchange plans to spend 1.1 billion pesetas ($9.6 million) to launch Spain's first share options market, expected to come into operation sometime in 1991, officials said. As a first step, a 340 million peseta agreement is expected to be signed in Frankfurt this week covering computer software, technical assistance and personnel training.

The system to be acquired is the DTB system in use on the Frankfurt stock exchange. That system is based on the Zurich stock exchange's Soffrex system, Madrid officials said.

The total cost of the project, including the computer equipment, is expected to be about 1.1 billion pesetas. The Madrid exchange hopes part of this cost will be borne by Spain's three regional stock exchanges, making the options market a joint project.

Separately, market sources said Volkswagen AG of West Germany is expected to become the first foreign company quoted on the Madrid stock exchange. They said Volkswagen shares are expected to be quoted in the next few weeks in the form of depositary receipts.
