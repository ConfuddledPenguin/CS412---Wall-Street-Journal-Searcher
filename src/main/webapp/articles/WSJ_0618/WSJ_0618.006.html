Marketing & Media: Pathe Extends Tender Offer For MGM/UA

  • Author: Richard Turner
  • Date: 06/18/90

LOS ANGELES -- Pathe Communications Corp. said it extended the expiration date of its $1.2 billion tender offer for MGM/UA Communications Co. by another week. The offer now expires Friday. Pathe Communications's $20-a-share offer, launched last March, has already been extended several times, and this latest delay was expected. The originally agreed-upon "drop-dead" date is June 23. However, "if all parties agree, it's possible" there could be another extension, a Pathe Communications spokesman said. An MGM/UA representative said, "additional discussions would have to be scheduled" if Pathe Communications asked for more time.

Time Warner Inc. has agreed to lend Pathe Communications $650 million toward the transaction in exchange for certain distribution rights to its films and a security interest in the valuable United Artists film library. Pathe Communications has indicated it won't meet all the terms of its agreement with Time Warner, but Time Warner has already made one concession and discussions are continuing about more. Despite the complexity of Pathe Communications's financing and the fact that Pathe Communications principal Giancarlo Parretti is appealing a prison sentence in Italy for fraudulent bankruptcy, Time Warner is believed to be anxious to see the transaction completed.

Pathe Communications last week revealed that it is raising about $368 million from a variety of European asset sales and bank loans. The company has already deposited $200 million in cash as a down payment.

MGM/UA shares closed Friday at 18.625, down 37.5 cents, in New York Stock Exchange composite trading.

Separately, Mr. Parretti suffered another blow as France's Finance Ministry, citing a threat to "public order," blocked his latest effort to take over Pathe Cinema.

Mr. Parretti's Paris representatives said they would fight the French move.

The government decision doesn't have any direct impact on Mr. Parretti's main holdings, which are grouped in Pathe Communications. Despite the similar names, Pathe Communications and Pathe Cinema aren't legally related.
