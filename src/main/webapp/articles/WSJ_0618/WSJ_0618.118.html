New Securities Issues

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/18/90

The following were among Friday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATE

Chrysler Financial Corp. -- $300 million of 10.30% notes, due June 15, 1992, priced at 99.939 to yield 10.33%. The notes were priced at a spread of 200 basis points above the Treasury's two-year note. Rated Baa-2 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and triple-B-minus by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through First Boston Corp. The Chrysler Financial issue offers bondholder protection in the form of event-risk language. Investors can put the notes back to the company at the offering price if the company's debt ratings falls below investment-grade while Moody's has Chrysler's debt on its watch list. Earlier this week, Moody's said it is reviewing the ratings of $22.28 billion of Chrysler debt for possible downgrade. The sale of debt Friday comes after Standard & Poor's Corp. late Thursday downgraded certain debt ratings of parent Chrysler Corp. and Chrysler Financial. Corp.

Illinois Tool Works -- $275 million of zero-coupon convertible subordinated debentures, due June 22, priced at $404.56 per $1,000 bond with a yield to maturity of 6.125%. The size of the issue was increased from an originally planned $250 million. The debentures, which are noncallable for two years, may be put back to the company in the fifth and 10th years. Rated single-A-3 by Moody's and single-A-plus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by First Boston Corp.

AMR Corp. -- $100 million of debentures, due June 15, 2020, priced at par to yield 9.88%. The noncallable debentures were priced at a spread of 145 basis points above the Treasury's 30-year bond. Rated single-A-3 by Moody's and singleA by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Morgan Stanley & Co.

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $300 million of Remic mortgage securities offered in nine classes by J.P. Morgan Securities. The offering, Series FNMA 1990-81, is backed by Fannie Mae 10% securities in stripped form. J.P. Morgan took all of the principal-only strips from the new strips trust SMBS Trust 80, and $270 million of the interest-only strips from the trust and used them as collateral for the Remic. The remaining interest-only strips not pledged to the Remic were sold separately.

Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $300.1 million of Remic mortgage securities offered in 15 classes by PaineWebber Inc. The offering, Series FHLMC 184, is backed by Freddie Mac 9% securities.

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $200.1 million issue of Remic mortgage securities offered in three classes by Goldman Sachs & Co.The offering, Series 1990-80, is backed by 8 1/2% Fannie Mae securities.

EUROBONDS

Eurofima (agency) -- 200 million marks of 9% bonds due July 26, 2000, priced at 100 5/8, to yield 8.90%, via J.P. Morgan. Callable from 1992 at par and thereafter every interest payment at par until maturity.

Kyushu Leasing (Japan) -- 50 million marks of private-placement floating-rate notes at six-month Libor, priced at par, due Sept. 16, 1994, via West LB. Co-led and guaranteed by Mitsui-Taiyokobe Bank. Non-callable.
