Energy Agency to Begin Talks With TRW Unit On $1 Billion Contract

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/18/90

WASHINGTON -- The Energy Department announced it will comply with a federal court order and begin negotiations with a TRW Inc. subsidiary on a possible award of a 10-year, $1 billion contract. TRW Environmental Safety Systems Inc. lost out in initial bidding for the contract to a consortium led by Bechtel Systems Management Inc., a subsidiary of Bechtel Group. But TRW went to court and won a federal claims court order last August barring award of the contract, as it was originally solicited, to anyone but TRW.

The Energy Department had been planning to appeal the ruling. However, it announced Friday it is changing course to try to prevent further delays in work on its high-level nuclear-waste disposal program. The department said it will drop its appeal "if a mutually satisfactory contractual agreement can be achieved" with TRW.

Although TRW wasn't the agency's first choice, it called the company "a qualified contractor capable of successfully performing the work." The contract is for systems engineering, development and management services for the government's waste program. The department currently is evaluating a Yucca Mountain, Nev., site for possible use as the first permanent dump for highly radioactive waste from nuclear power plants.

The department said the Bechtel-led consortium was barred from receiving the contract "through no fault of its own." TRW had alleged in claims court that the selection process was illegal and that the department used different criteria than it had said it would when it selected Bechtel.
