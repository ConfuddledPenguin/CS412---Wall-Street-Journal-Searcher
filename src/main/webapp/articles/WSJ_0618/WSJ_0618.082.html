International: Anglo American Says Units To Trim Mining Operations

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/18/90

JOHANNESBURG -- Anglo American Corp. of South Africa said it will curtail mining operations at two of its subsidiaries because of the plummeting price of gold. Anglo American said the moves at its Free State Consolidated Gold Mines Ltd. and Vaal Reefs Exploration & Mining Co. subsidiaries would cut at least 7,800 jobs.

The price of gold reached a four-year low of $346.25 an ounce in New York and London last Thursday before recovering amid central bank buying. Gold is South Africa's major industry, earning more than half the country's foreign currency.

Lionel Hewitt, managing director of Anglo American's gold and uranium division, said the curtailment of operations will take place over the next nine months. He said the cutbacks at Vaal Reefs won't necessarily eliminate jobs, because workers may be assigned to other areas of the mine.

Operating reductions at Free State will reduce its gold output by less than 1%, while the proposed action at Vaal Reefs probably will improve production, Mr. Hewitt said.
