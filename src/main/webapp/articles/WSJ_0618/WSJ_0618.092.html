Farley Is Sued by Printer Claiming Unpaid Services

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/18/90

CHICAGO -- William Farley's troubles with red ink may be spilling over into his dealings with printer's ink. R.R. Donnelley & Sons Co. has sued the investor for $2.5 million in unpaid bills arising from his troubled takeover of West Point-Pepperell Inc. The Chicago-based printing concern says Mr. Farley hasn't paid for the massive amount of financial printing he ran up during his protracted $1.56 billion tender offer for the West Point, Ga.-based textile company.

Mr. Farley has been unable to complete the purchase of the 5% of West Point-Pepperell shares he doesn't own and is said to be considering offers for his majority stake from other investors in order to raise cash.

Mr. Farley's difficulties have mounted in the wake of the collapse of his investment banker, Drexel Burnham Lambert Inc., earlier this year. His closely held Farley Inc. had a pretax loss of about $40 million in 1989, analysts estimated.

Farley spokesmen couldn't be reached for comment.
