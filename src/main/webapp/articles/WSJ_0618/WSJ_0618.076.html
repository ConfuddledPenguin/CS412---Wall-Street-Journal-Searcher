Marketing & Media: Saatchi & Saatchi Hopes to Secure Bank Guarantee

  • Author: Joann S. Lublin
  • Date: 06/18/90

LONDON -- Saatchi & Saatchi Co. hopes to win a #45 million (US$76.9 million) bank guarantee this week so that its advertising agencies can keep booking commercials on British television stations. But some of the troubled ad giant's 20 banks apparently are balking. The bankers may demand a stiff price: toughened terms for Saatchi's recently renewed $450 million line of credit. "That is something Saatchi may have to consider," said one knowledgeable individual. The recalcitrant banks "would rather not do it {the guarantee}" because "it's a risk on top of a fairly fully drawn {lending} facility," this person added.

A Saatchi spokesman said the company has nearly completed several weeks of negotiations over the bank guarantee. "Whenever it is required, we'll have it," he said, but declined to elaborate.

Officials of Saatchi's lead banks, including Barclays Bank PLC, Chase Manhattan Bank and Citibank, also declined comment Friday about the guarantee talks. The consortium of 20 banks renewed the broader $450 million credit line earlier this year to cover all of Saatchi's financial obligations over the next three years.

"Saatchi is a very borrowed company," observed Mark Shepperd, an analyst at UBS Phillips & Drew, a British brokerage firm. "Most banks don't want to expand their exposure for something they aren't very happy with."

The ITV Association, which represents British commercial TV companies, asked Saatchi for the guarantee last month, the knowledgeable person said. The company is supposed to arrange the backing before an association committee meets this week. Without support from all 20 banks, Saatchi may have to ask the association for more time or to "reduce its demand," this individual suggested.

The association requests such bank guarantees when it becomes concerned about ad agencies' financial condition. Saatchi never had to provide such a guarantee before, the Saatchi spokesman said, although other big advertising groups have done so. Saatchi, saddled with heavy debt from a flawed diversification strategy, recently posted a #7 million net loss for the fiscal first half and omitted its dividend for the first time.

The bank guarantee covers the highest amount that agencies expect to spend on TV commercials during two consecutive months over the next year, according to Susan de Cesare, the association's agency executive. The association may withdraw credit from agencies which fail to arrange a bank guarantee. These agencies then must "pay for it {air time} up front or they can't book it," she added.
