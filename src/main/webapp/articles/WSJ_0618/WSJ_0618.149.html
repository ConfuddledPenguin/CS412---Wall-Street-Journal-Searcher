Amoco Will Post 2nd-Quarter Gain On Iran Payment

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/18/90

CHICAGO -- Amoco Corp. said the settlement for about $600 million of all claims and counterclaims between it and Iran will result in an undetermined second-quarter gain. The company said it expects to receive by the end of June a net payment of about $594 million but that the gain will be offset by income taxes and a charge that will also be booked in the second quarter. The amount of the charge is undetermined, in line with the completion of a study under which the company has targeted specific worksites for environmental cleanup.

In the year-earlier quarter, the energy concern had net income of $497 million, or 96 cents a share, on revenue of $6.76 billion.

The settlement ends Amoco's claims for compensation for the seizure by Iran of its two major Iranian operations in 1978 and 1979. In Washington, U.S. officials said the settlement wouldn't have any direct bearing on the chances of gaining release of the six U.S. hostages still held by pro-Iranian groups in Lebanon. "It's really not related," said a senior State Department official, pointing out that Iran will be paying money, not receiving it.

Nevertheless, the Bush administration privately hopes that such progress at The Hague, and the promise of more, will help induce economically strapped Iran to prod the Lebanese kidnappers into fresh releases. President Bush has adopted a policy of making whatever gestures appear reasonable in their own right -- such as working to clear up claims -- and hoping Tehran interprets them as favorable gestures.

However, claims settlements so far seem to have had little impact on the hostage problem. After a series of claims settlements last month, no hostages were released, and Iran's line even seemed to harden. Tehran and its allies are demanding the release of Shiite prisoners from Israel and Kuwait before any more hostages are released, and anti-U.S. feelings have been rising in the Middle East lately as the war of words against Israel has heated up.
