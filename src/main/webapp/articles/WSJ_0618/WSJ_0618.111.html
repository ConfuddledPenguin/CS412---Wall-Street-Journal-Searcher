Business Brief -- Wendy's International Inc.: Suit Stemming From Sale Of Restaurants Is Dropped

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/18/90

Wendy's International Inc., Dublin, Ohio, agreed to drop a year-old lawsuit against Carl Karcher Enterprises Inc., Anaheim, Calif., that related to conversion of 10 Wendy's fast-food restaurants in Arizona to Carl's Jr. restaurants. Karcher bought the restaurants from Ronald Brown, owner of Wendy's of Tucson Inc., a Wendy's franchisee, over the objections of Wendy's International. Wendy's filed suit against Karcher, Mr. Brown and his company, charging that the transaction violated Wendy's franchise relationship.

Under the settlement, Wendy's agreed to approve sale of the restaurants and to drop its lawsuit, which sought damages exceeding $100 million from Karcher and Mr. Brown. Karcher, also a fast-food restaurant chain, agreed not to buy any additional Wendy's restaurants without the consent of Wendy's International.
