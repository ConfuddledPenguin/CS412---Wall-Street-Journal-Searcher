Edward Sargent, on how Washington Mayor Marion Barry was once his role model, in the April Regardie's:

. . . when black leaders blame racism as a major factor in Marion's saga, they shame him, themselves, and blacks in general. Such charges make us seem like wimps, for in effect we're saying that the white man captured our king with the easiest trick in the book: a willing woman, an intoxicating substance, and a lair in swank surroundings.
