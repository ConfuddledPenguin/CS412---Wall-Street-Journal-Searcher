Israel's Prices Rise

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/18/90

JERUSALEM -- Pushed by rapidly rising housing prices, Israel's consumer price index rose 1.6% in May, the government announced. The rise in the index, which measures price changes in a fixed market basket of goods and services, compares with 2.5% in April and 0.9% in May 1989.

The government-run Central Bureau of Statistics projected that inflation for the year would probably be about 18%, or little changed from the three preceding years.

The bureau attributed May's increase to price rises for fruit and vegetables, clothing, footware and housing. The housing component climbed 2.2% during the month, the bureau said.

Housing costs have been pushed up by a shortage caused by an influx of Soviet Jews to Israel, expected to reach up to 150,000 this year.
