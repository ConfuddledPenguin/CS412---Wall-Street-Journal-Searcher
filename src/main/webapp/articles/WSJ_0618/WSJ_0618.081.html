Ex-Im Bank to Back Loans For Uruguay Power Plant

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/18/90

WASHINGTON -- The U.S. Export Import Bank approved a $19.3 million grant and a $35.9 million loan guarantee for an electric power plant project in Uruguay. The Ex-Im Bank said it dipped into its tied-aid "war chest" for the grant after the French government offered a similar subsidy in an effort to win the equipment contracts for French suppliers.

General Electric Co. will supply gas turbines and other equipment for the power station from its factories in Schenactady, N.Y., and Greenville, S.C.

The guarantee will be on a loan provided by the Private Export Funding Corp., New York, which is backed by a group of U.S. commercial banks and other private financial institutions.
