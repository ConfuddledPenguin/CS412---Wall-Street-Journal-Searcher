Sutton to Seek Seats On DeSoto Board, Offers to Buy Lines

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/18/90

CHICAGO -- Sutton Holding Corp., which has been trying to acquire DeSoto Inc. since last year, said it will seek three seats on DeSoto's board. Sutton also said it offered to buy certain of DeSoto's businesses as an alternative to its $50-a-share, or $200 million, tender offer for the Des Plaines, Ill.-based maker of paints, chemical coatings and detergents.

DeSoto stock fell $3.25 Friday, closing at $40.25 in New York Stock Exchange composite trading.

Two Sutton executives, Chairman William Spier and Senior Vice President Michael Zurawin, will seek board seats at Desoto's annual meeting July 16, along with Giorgio Arra, a Sutton director.

Sutton and a DeSoto spokesman said the nominations were made under an agreement with Sutton, a New York investor group. The election of the three would expand DeSoto's board to 13 members.

Saying that DeSoto had failed to enhance shareholder value, Sutton said it would offer $45 million for DeSoto's detergent business if the company sold all of its other businesses. Sutton said it would then take steps to acquire all of DeSoto's outstanding shares.

Failing that, Sutton said, it would offer about $90 million for DeSoto's detergent and surfactant businesses.

In a statement, Sutton said the offers were made because DeSoto "was having trouble selling certain businesses in a timely manner."

Sutton also said it extended its tender offer until midnight EDT June 25. The offer had been set to expire last Friday.

The DeSoto spokesman had no comment on Sutton's unsolicited offers for DeSoto or its businesses.
