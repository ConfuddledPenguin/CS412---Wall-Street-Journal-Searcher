HOMEFED Corp. (San Diego) -- Standard & Poor's Corp. said it lowered its ratings on $110 million of debt of HomeFed Corp.'s HomeFed Bank unit, citing concerns about the thrift's exposure to deteriorating real estate markets. The ratings concern said it lowered its rating on HomeFed Bank's subordinated debt to triple-B-minus from triple-B-plus. It lowered its rating on the thrift's long-term certificates of deposit to triple-B from single-A-minus. It affirmed at single-A-2 its ratings on the bank's commercial paper and short-term certificates of deposit. HomeFed officials couldn't be reached for comment. ---

PHILADELPHIA -- Citing a chronic fiscal imbalance, Standard & Poor's Corp. lowered its rating on $1.3 billion of Philadelphia general obligation bonds to Triple-B-minus from Triple-B-plus. The rating agency cited the city's inability to erase its deficit, which is expected to reach $60 million for the year ending June 30. It would be the city's third consecutive deficit and next fiscal year's budget also has a likelihood of an imbalance, S&P said. Other issues downgraded to Triple-B-minus from Triple-B-plus include $9 million issued by the Philadelphia Redevelopment Authority, $6 million issued by the Philadelphia Parking Authority, and $7.6 million issued by the Philadelphia Parking Authority. A final rating is still pending on the March 1990 offering of $148 million of variable rate general obligations, backed by a letter of credit by Fuji Bank. S&P said Philadelphia's financial outlook for fiscal 1991 is not expected to improve. A recent court stipulation will require the city to provide an additional $32 million for child welfare. The city also may be required by court order to refund an additional $25 million in real estate transfer tax collections that were declared illegal a year ago. S&P also anticipates the city having to pay $20 million in labor arbitration settlements and an indeterminate amount in court costs.

RELIANCE GROUP HOLDINGS Inc. (New York) -- Standard & Poor's Corp. said it raised its ratings on this insurance and financial holding company's senior debt to double-B from single-B-plus and on subordinated debt to single-B-plus from single-B-minus. The rating concern affirmed its ratings on debt of related entities, including Reliance Group Inc., Reliance Financial Services Corp., and Reliance Insurance Co. About $1.2 billion in debt was affected. S&P noted that Reliance Group Holdings continues to show improved financial leverage, augmented by a large capital gain from the sale of an insurance unit in April, and that it expects the trend of improved financial leverage to continue. But S&P lowered to triple-B-plus from single-A its rating on the claims-paying ability of United Pacific Life Insurance Co., which S&P said has a "significant tolerance for single-issue concentrations in its large high-yield portfolio."
