Dividend News: American Steel Sets $3.25 Special Dividend

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/18/90

CUYAHOGA HEIGHTS, Ohio -- American Steel & Wire Corp. declared a special dividend of $3.25 a share after completion of a $95 million borrowing. The dividend, the steel producer's first since it went public in 1988, is payable June 29 to stock of record June 24.

The company said the dividend will consume about $27 million of the borrowed funds. Slightly more than that will be used to refinance debt, the company said, with the remainder available for capital expenditures, working capital and other corporate purposes.

In late April, American Steel said it might pay a dividend of as much as $4.25 a share when the financing was completed. However, sales have been picking up, making it advisable to retain more of the borrowed funds for improving facilities and working capital, said C.V. Meserole III, executive vice president and chief financial officer.

Directors decided to use borrowed funds to pay a special dividend to shareholders, who haven't fared as well in the stock market as they might have expected when they bought the stock at $9 a share in the 1988 initial offering, Mr. Meserole said. The stock closed Friday at $6.25, up 25 cents, in over-the-counter trading.

The lending institutions include Security Pacific Business Credit Inc., Sanwa Business Credit Corp., State Bank of South Australia and Cigna Investments Inc., American Steel said.
