New Stock Listings

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/18/90

NEW YORK -- The New York Stock Exchange announced three new listings, the American Stock Exchange two, and the National Market System of the over-the-counter market four.

On the Big Board, Home Shopping Network Inc., a Clearwater, Fla., marketer on television, will begin trading Wednesday under the symbol HSN. The company's shares had traded on the American exchange.

Molecular Biosystems Inc., a San Diego developer of diagnostic probes and imaging agents used to genetically identify viruses and bacteria, joins the New York exchange from the OTC listings tomorrow under MB.

Transatlantic Holdings Inc., a New York casualty and property reinsurer, began trading as an initial public offering under TRH.

On the Amex, Summa Medical Corp., an Albuquerque, N.M., company with interests in high-definition television, computer systems analysis and pharmaceutical products, began trading under SU.

Xytronyx Inc., a San Diego biotechnology research and development company, began trading under XYX. Both Xytronyx and Summa Medical previously traded OTC.

On the OTC market, Digital Systems International Inc., a Redmond, Wash., maker of phone-call processing systems, began trading under DGTL.

Ellwood Federal Savings Bank, Ellwood City, Pa., began trading under ECCB.

NSC Corp., a Methuen, Mass., asbestos-abatement services company, began trading under NSCC.

Xilinx Inc., a San Jose, Calif., maker of software for electronic systems design, began trading under XLNX.

The Chicago Board Options Exchange began trading options on the following three companies: Borland International Inc., a Scotts Valley, Calif., maker of personal computer software; MIPS Computer Systems Inc., a Sunnyvale, Calif., maker of products based on reduced-instruction set computer technology, and Shaw Industries Inc., a Dalton, Ga., carpet maker. Options on MIPS Computer Systems also began trading on the Amex.

The Pacific Stock Exchange began trading options on Software Publishing Corp., a Mountain View, Calif., maker of personal computer software.
