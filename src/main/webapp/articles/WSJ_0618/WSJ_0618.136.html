Business Brief -- Manpower PLC: Pretax Profit Declined 14% For Six Months to April 30

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/18/90

Manpower PLC said that pretax profit for the six months to April 30 declined 14%, principally because of lower profit in its non-Manpower brand operations, which were sold June 4. The London-based company, an employment and financial services group, said it isn't declaring an interim dividend because of insufficient distributable reserves. Last year, it declared an interim dividend of 0.6 penny a share.

Pretax profit for the six-month period was #21.3 million ($36.4 million), down 14% from year-earlier profit of #24.8 million. Profit after tax was #12.7 million vs. # 15.6 million a year earlier. Per-share earnings eased 18% to 1.8 pennies a share from 2.2 pennies.

Analysts said the results were slightly worse than expected, with the average forecast for around #23.5 million pretax profit.

Manpower's share price rose 1 penny to 88 pennies on the results, before easing to 87 1/2 around midday Friday on the London Stock Exchange.
