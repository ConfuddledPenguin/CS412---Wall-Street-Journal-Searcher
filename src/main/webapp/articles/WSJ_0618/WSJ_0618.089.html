Sweden's Trade Surplus Falls

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/18/90

STOCKHOLM -- Sweden's foreign trade surplus narrowed to 1.4 billion kronor ($229 million) in May from 2.4 billion kronor in April but was wider than the May 1989 surplus of 700 million kronor, Statistics Sweden said. The national statistics agency said preliminary May figures showed that exports totaled 29.8 billion kronor, up 9% from May 1989, while imports amounted to 28.4 billion kronor, up 7% from the corresponding month last year.

The agency said Sweden's trade surplus in the first five months was 10.2 billion kronor, unchanged from the January-May period last year.

Exports during the five months rose 4% to 146.2 billion kronor, while imports also increased 4%, to 136 billion kronor.
