Action Auto Stores Files to Reorganize Under Chapter 11

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/18/90

FLINT, Mich. -- Action Auto Stores Inc. said it has voluntarily filed a petition for reorganization under Chapter 11 of the U.S. Bankruptcy Code "in order to continue the company's restructuring and consolidation program." C. James Sabo, chairman and chief executive officer of the auto-parts retailer, said: "We regret having to take this action, but we believe that it was absolutely necessary for the overall well-being of the company."

Chapter 11 protects a company from creditor lawsuits while it works out a plan to repay debt. A spokesman said the company filed under Chapter 11 after taking an "assessment of our financial situation." He declined to be more specific.

In reaction to recent quarterly losses, Action Auto in March said it started a cost-cutting program, obtained a $9 million secured line of credit from its bank and modified its agreement with another lender.

Mr. Sabo said that the Chapter 11 filing shouldn't affect the efforts under way by the investment banking firm of PaineWebber Inc. to restructure the company.
