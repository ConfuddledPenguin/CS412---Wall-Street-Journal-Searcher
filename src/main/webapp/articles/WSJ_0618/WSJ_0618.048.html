Letters to the Editor: Billions for Schools, But Failure Persists

  • Author: null
  • Date: 06/18/90

Your April 24 page-one article "Report Cards: Connecticut Grades Its Schools and Holds Officials Responsible" is a tribute to a state that made a commitment to its children and to the state's own destiny. I have found that government can no longer throw money at a problem and expect it to go away. Illinois spends about $8 billion a year on education. Yet we have a 25% dropout rate statewide, with 50% in Chicago alone. There are average test scores, rampant drug abuse and a swollen bureaucracy.

So, where's our education policy? What are the goals we're trying to achieve? How will we know if test scores will climb and drug use drop? How will we know tax dollars are going into the classroom and not into the bureaucracy? Why should we keep throwing money at this problem if we don't know whether conditions are improving?

State government must be held accountable for these failures, especially when children can no longer wait for the long-overdue federal standards to guide them.

Neil F. Hartigan

Attorney General

State of Illinois

Chicago
