International: Mexican Telephone Group Approves Recapitalization

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/18/90

MEXICO CITY -- Shareholders of Telefonos de Mexico approved a recapitalization plan that should pave the way for the state-controlled Mexican telephone monopoly's privatization. The recapitalization, which includes a stock dividend of new, limited-voting shares, is designed to reduce the size of the investment a buyer will need to obtain operating control of the company, Mexican officials said. Under the plan, full-voting shares will be reduced to just 40% of the total shares oustanding; the buyer will need to obtain the majority of only the full-voting shares to gain operating control. The government currently holds an interest of slightly more than 50% in Telmex.

The new capital structure was approved by 87% of shareholders at a special meeting Friday afternoon. In New York, amid investor nervousness over the plan, Telmex's American depositary receipts were the most actively traded stock on the Nasdaq, closing at $1.53 bid, down 18 cents. Under the plan, Telmex will use $230 million in accumulated earnings to issue a dividend of 1.5 shares of new Class L stock for each share held. The Class L shares will have limited voting rights.

Of the 40% of shares outstanding with full voting rights, slightly more than half will be held by the government. A group seeking operating control of Telmex would have to pay roughly $1.5 billion for the government's full voting shares, based on recent market prices.
