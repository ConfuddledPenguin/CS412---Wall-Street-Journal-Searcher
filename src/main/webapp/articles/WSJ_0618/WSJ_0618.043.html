Letters to the Editor: Playboy Clarifies Stock Voting Policy

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/18/90

I want to correct a misquote in the May 23 "Heard on the Street" column regarding Playboy's new recapitalization plan. For the record, the recapitalization plan does not dilute the voting power of any shareholder nor change control. It does provide the company with additional flexibility to use equity in future acquisitions, financings and employee-benefit plans, which will benefit all shareholders. In my comments regarding the plan and voting rights of stockholders, the reporter quoted me as saying, "I'm sort of a believer in one share, one vote," which would be strange and illogical on the eve of creating a nonvoting class of stock. What I said was, "I'm a believer in one voting share, one vote." Anyone who holds voting stock has a right and responsibility to exercise his vote. This right has been preserved under Playboy's recapitalization plan, and that is why we did not disenfranchise the majority shareholder, as most companies have, in his right to vote on this plan.

Christie Hefner

Chairman and CEO

Playboy Enterprises Inc.

Chicago
