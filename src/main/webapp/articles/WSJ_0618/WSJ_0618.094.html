American Republic Bancorp

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/18/90

WASHINGTON -- Chicago investor Leon Greenblatt, who holds a 9.9% stake in American Republic Bancorp, said in a filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission that he plans to ask the company to form a committee to consider alternatives to enhance shareholder values. Bill Oberholzer, executive vice president of the Torrance, Calif., bank holding company, said this is the first time that Mr. Greenblatt, who has been a holder for about two years, has formally made a proposal to the board.

"We view him as someone with a good stake in the bank who feels that the stock price is undervalued," Mr. Oberholzer said. The stock has been trading at a small discount to its book value. In national over-the-counter trading Friday, it closed at $7.75, up 25 cents.
