Repsol and La Caixa Negotiate to Create Spanish Gas Utility

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 06/18/90

MADRID -- Repsol S.A., the Spanish state-controlled oil company, and La Caixa, Spain's largest savings bank, are understood to be at an advanced stage in negotiations for the creation of a giant Spanish gas utility. The principal remaining question, however, is understood to be who will be Repsol's partner in the utility: Repsol wants La Caixa itself as a partner, while the savings bank would prefer to participate indirectly through water utility Aguas de Barcelona S.A., of which it is a shareholder.

At stake are Spanish government efforts to reorganize Spain's fragmented gas industry and keep it under Spanish control. Repsol officials say their aim is to create a single large gas utility along the lines of British Gas, Gaz de France or Belgium's Distrigas.

Repsol's parent, the state oil and gas agency Instituto Nacional de Hidrocarburos, is investing heavily through Repsol's sister company, Enagas, in a project to bring natural gas by pipeline to Spain from Norway.

In parallel, Repsol began talks last year aimed at regrouping its gas activities with those of Spain's biggest gas utility, Barcelona-based Catalana de Gas S.A.

But alarm bells rang a few months ago when the U.K.'s British Gas disclosed it had bought around 8% of Catalana de Gas.

The prospect of foreign companies getting an inexpensive back door into Spain's gas market added impetus to Repsol's efforts to forge a unified national gas utility.

To strengthen Repsol's hand, INH launched a bid last month for control of Gas Madrid, Spain's second largest gas utility. As a result of this bid, INH now controls 87% of Enagas, directly and through Repsol.

The plans under discussion between Repsol and La Caixa call for the creation of a joint holding company owning Repsol's holdings in Gas Madrid and a number of regional gas utilities. The regional interests would include a sizable stake in Catalana de Gas, which in turn has stakes in gas firms in Andalusia and Valencia. Repsol owns 11.8% of Catalana de Gas, and La Caixa owns 24%. Another Barcelona-based savings bank, Caja de Barcelona, which is planning to merge with La Caixa, owns 10% of Catalana de Gas.

But a spokeswoman for La Caixa said the talks, though at an advanced stage, appear for the moment to have reached a dead end.

The reason, she said, was the government's insistence that La Caixa be Repsol's direct partner in the gas venture.
