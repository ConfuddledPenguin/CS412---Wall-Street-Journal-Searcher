Credit Ratings: Teledyne Debt Rating Is Lowered by S&P

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/14/90

Standard & Poor's Corp. said it lowered its ratings on $640 million of Teledyne Inc. debt in the wake of the Los Angeles diversified manufacturer's recent spinoff of its insurance operations. S&P said it lowered ratings on Teledyne's senior debt to triple-B-plus from single-A, and to triple-B from single-A-minus on the company's subordinated debt.

Teledyne's financial flexibility and asset protection are reduced since the Unitrin Inc. spinoff, the rating agency said. The insurance operations were "conservatively capitalized" and held valuable investments that benefited Teledyne debt holders, the agency added.

S&P said that its revised ratings also reflect the risk of further corporate restructuring and possible deterioration in the company's cash-flow protection.

Teledyne's "relatively stable earnings stream," together with moderate capital needs, currently provide "satisfactory cash-flow protection," but that "could erode reflecting mediocre prospects for {the company's} defense operations and weakness in a number of its end markets," S&P said.
