Marketing & Media: Two Dutch Brewers Play a Tug of War By Rotating Agencies

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/14/90

AMSTERDAM -- Heineken Nederland B.V. and Grolsch Bierbrouwerij N.V., two Dutch beer brewers, have embarked on a process of advertising agency musical chairs in an effort to separate themselves from each other. The ad agency war began earlier in the year when Heineken switched from using a number of local agencies to represent its European accounts to consolidating all of them with the London office of J. Walter Thompson.

However, Grolsch had been using the Amsterdam-based office of J. Walter Thompson, known as PPTH-JWT, to represent it in the Netherlands. In response, the company has announced that it has dropped PPTH-JWT in favor of FHV/ BBDO, the Netherlands subsidiary of BBDO.

"Heineken chose J. Walter Thompson for the whole of Europe, so we had to seek another agency," said a spokesman for Grolsch.

Unfortunately, FHV-BBDO also represented Heineken's Amstel brand of beer. Heineken retaliated by pulling its Amstel account from FHV-BBDO and placing the account with the Dutch subsidiary of Young & Rubicam, known as PMSWY&R.

"The announced cooperation between FHV-BBDO and Grolsch came as a surprise to Heineken Nederland," Heineken said in a statement.

Heineken said that it regretted FHV-BBDO's decision, but noted, "PMS-WY&R could be appointed without any hesitation."
