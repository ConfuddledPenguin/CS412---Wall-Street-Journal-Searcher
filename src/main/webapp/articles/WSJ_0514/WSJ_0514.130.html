International Brief -- Unilever N.V.: Pretax Profit Rose by 5.2% On Sales Gain in 1st Period

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/14/90

Unilever N.V., the Anglo-Dutch food and home-products group, said pretax profit for the first quarter advanced 5.2% as growth in Europe countered declining profitability in North America. Pretax profit hardened to #366 million ($614.7 million) on sales of #5.12 billion ($8.6 billion) against pretax profit of #348 million and sales of #4.55 billion in the comparable period. The figures were in line with analysts' expectations.

Interest charges, which more than doubled to #82 million as borrowings increased to finance acquisitions, ate into Unilever's profit. Profit after tax and minority interests firmed 13% to #221 million from #195 million in the first quarter of 1989. Earnings per share jumped to 12.3 pence a share from 10.4 pence, a gain of 18%.

On the Amsterdam stock exchange, investors bid up Unilever to a high of 144.50 guilders a share Friday before it closed at 143.90, up 2.60 guilders from the previous finish. In New York Stock Exchange composite trading Friday, Unilever closed at $79.75 a share, up $3.
