Business Brief -- Artel Communications Corp.: Revenue in Second Quarter To Be Below Expectations

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/14/90

Artel Communications Corp. said it expects to report second-quarter revenue that is "significantly" lower than "might have been expected." Artel added that it doesn't expect to report a profit for the quarter.

The Hudson, Mass., maker of fiber optic communications systems attributed the lower revenue to delays in U.S. and European government programs, lower sales to Pacific Rim customers and a longer-than-expected purchasing approval process among some new customers.

In the second quarter ended July 1, 1989, the company earned $281,000, or seven cents a share, on revenue of $2.8 million.

Artel said it has "implemented cost-containment actions while maintaining the ongoing product development programs." The company added that it expects to make "significant" new product announcements by year end.
