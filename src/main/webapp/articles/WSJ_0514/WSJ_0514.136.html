Unions' Plan to Buy UAL Clears Hurdle on Contracts

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/14/90

CHICAGO -- Unions representing United Airlines' pilots, machinists and flight attendants ratified new contracts that will be key to financing the unions' $4.38 billion buy-out proposal for UAL Corp., United's parent. The agreements will provide the company with an estimated $2 billion in savings over five years through wage concessions, tougher work rules and benefit reductions. They also include a five-year no-strike clause.

UAL's board approved the offer on April 6 and gave the labor groups four months to complete bank financing for the transaction. The union offer doesn't have an outside equity investor. The labor groups are trying to persuade banks that the cost savings from their concessions represent "sweat equity."

Ratification of the agreements is the clearest indication to date that the buy-out proposal has the support of the unions' rank and file. The contracts will go into effect only when the buy-out is completed.
