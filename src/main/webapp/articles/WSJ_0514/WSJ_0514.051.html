Marketing & Media: Turner Broadcasting Reports Turnaround For the First Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/14/90

ATLANTA -- Turner Broadcasting System Inc. reported first-quarter net income, compared with a loss the previous year, and a 25% revenue surge, reflecting its continued investment in building its networks. The cable programmer said it had quarterly net of $4.3 million, including a $3.9 million tax gain, compared with a loss of $421,000 a year earlier. But on a per-share basis, Turner reported a first-quarter loss of 15 cents a share, reflecting accretion of preferred stock dividends, compared with a loss of 25 cents a share a year earlier, which also reflected the preferred stock dividends.

Turner's revenue jumped to $290.8 million in the period from $232 million a year earlier. Its operating profit was flat at $52 million, reflecting the company's increased investments in news and entertainment programming, including the founding of an investigative-reporting unit for Cable News Network and original dramatic programming for Turner Network Television.

Turner said it took a pretax charge in the period of $3 million from anticipated losses on this summer's Goodwill Games in Seattle, bringing its total charges from the Games to $13 million. It added that it recorded a one-time, $3 million gain in the quarter from its Atlanta Hawks basketball team's share of expansion fees from the National Basketball Association.

In American Stock Exchange composite trading Friday, Turner's Class A shares closed at $44.375 each, up 37.5 cents.
