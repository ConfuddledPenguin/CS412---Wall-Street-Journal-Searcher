Bouygues, Affiliate And Kinnevik Reach TV-Cooperation Pact

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/14/90

PARIS -- The French building group Bouygues S.A., its television affiliate TF1 and the Swedish media concern Kinnevik AB announced Friday that they have signed an accord to cooperate in the field of television. The three concerns said that they signed the preliminary cooperation accord May 9. Their first step, they said, will be to consider a bid for the entertainment arm of Esselte AB, which includes the pay television channel Filmnet. Esselte put the unit up for sale about two months ago; as many as 10 different media groups from Europe and North America are thought to be interested.

Analysts have estimated that the unit may fetch a price as high as $400 million.

Beyond the Filmnet purchase, Bouygues, TF1 and Kinnevik are expected to use the cooperation accord to generate economies of scale, especially regarding the purchase of programs.

TF1 has the largest television market share in France. Bouygues is its largest shareholder with an interest of about 25%.

Kinnevik controls the Scandinavian channel TV3 as well as the recently launched pay channel TV 1000.
