SUNWEST FINANCIAL SERVICES Inc. said it recorded additions to loan-loss reserves and other charges totaling $7 million due to a continuing regulatory exam, leaving the company with net income of $2.1 million for the four-month period ended April 30. The total for the charges was misstated in Friday's edition. (See: "OTC Focus: Market Ends on Highs, Index Up 1.86; Sun Microsystems Leads Most-Active List" -- WSJ May 11, 1990)

---

NWNL Cos. reported net income, after securities losses, of $1.11 a share, compared with an average estimate by analysts over the previous 30 days of $1 a share, resulting in a positive surprise of 11%. In Friday's Quarterly Earnings Surprises table, the estimate was incorrect because of a data base error at Zacks Investment Research. As a result, the company's earnings were mistakenly listed as a negative surprise.

(See: "Quarterly Earnings Surprises" -- WSJ May 11, 1990)

---

LIFETIME Corp. filed a registration statement with the Securities and Exchange Commission for an offering that includes 960,688 shares owned by company management. In Friday's edition, the ownership of the shares was listed incorrectly.

(See: "Recent SEC Filings" -- WSJ May 11, 1990)
