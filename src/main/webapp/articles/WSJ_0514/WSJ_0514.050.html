Credit Rating: S&P Takes Sotheby's Off CreditWatch List

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/14/90

NEW YORK -- Standard & Poor's Corp. said it affirmed Sotheby's Inc.'s A-1 commercial paper rating and removed it from CreditWatch, where it was placed April 26 with negative implications. The review was prompted by the company's planned $142.8 million acquisition of the extensive art inventory of the Pierre Matisse Gallery.

S&P had said the move exposed the Sotheby's Holdings Inc. unit to a "somewhat higher business risk because it more directly exposes the company to future art price volatility."

But S&P now has concluded that "the impact of this transaction is not significant enough to warrant a downgrade. Given Sotheby's strong earnings and cash flow, S&P believes that lower financial leverage will be quickly restored."

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading Friday, Sotheby's Holdings closed at $17.50 a share, up 50 cents.
