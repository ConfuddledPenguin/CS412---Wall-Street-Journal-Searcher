Who's News: David Payne to Leave Bank of New England

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/14/90

BOSTON -- Bank of New England Corp. said its chief financial officer, David B. Payne, will take early retirement in July. Mr. Payne, 54 years old, is also an executive vice president of the bank holding company, which hasn't yet named a successor to him. Mr. Payne, who was named chief financial officer last year, was formerly president of the concern's Connecticut Bank & Trust unit.

Mr. Payne said he decided to take early retirement because "my family is still in Hartford, and I feel it's time to go back and join them. I always considered (the role of chief financial officer) a short-term appointment."

Bank of New England, hard hit by bad real estate loans, has undergone a series of top management changes during the past few months as federal bank regulators have worked to rectify its problems.
