Trump Is Joining Ranks of Celebrities Plastering Their Names on Products

  • Author: Gilbert Fuchsberg
  • Date: 05/14/90

If you haven't seen enough of Donald Trump's name lately, just wait: Coming soon are Trump desk blotters, Trump briefcases, Trump pens and Trump wallets. And one day, perhaps, Trump tuxedos. The developer, whose name already adorns an airline, three Atlantic City, N.J., casinos and other properties, is joining the ranks of celebrity athletes and fashion designers who license their names to manufacturers in exchange for royalties of between 5% and 10% of sales.

"Donald Trump represents the entrepreneurial power in all of us," says Robert A. Stone, vice president for licensing at International Management Group, a New York marketing concern that approached Mr. Trump with the idea six months ago. "Why not come out with a line of products that represents that?"

After turning down earlier requests and fighting some product makers, who he claimed had misappropriated his name, Mr. Trump says he finally agreed to market his moniker because "it won't be time consuming" and because "what we've put our name on has been successful." He donated proceeds from both his best-selling book and a board game that bears his name to charity, and says he intends to do the same with income from the licensing effort.

"It's a lot of money that's going to go to charity," he says. "In that sense, I felt an obligation to do it."

To avoid diluting the value of Mr. Trump's name, Mr. Stone says he is negotiating only with makers of "upscale quality merchandise," limited for now to leather goods and desk accessories -- including pencil cups, picture frames and blotters -- "that represent business and the Wall Street tycoon." After the first products debut this Christmas, Mr. Stone says, licenses may be granted to makers of tuxedos and other apparel.

One company anticipating a bonanza is Diplomat Pen Co. of Stuttgard, West Germany, which plans to launch three Trump pen brands next January. "Our early projections for year one are in excess of $5 million," says Frank Salacuse, who heads Diplomat's U.S. operations. Topping the line: the Trump "Signature Collection." Only 200 of the solid gold pens will be produced, Mr. Salacuse says: 100 fountain pen versions and 100 ball points. The price: $10,000.

Not content to do anything in a small way, Mr. Trump also is starting a program to license the name of his Plaza Hotel to makers of china, crystal, wall coverings, sheets, towels, pillow cases, furniture and "very expensive throw rugs," Mr. Stone says.

But there are limits, Mr. Stone insists. "We're not going to do mugs," he says. "That's not what Donald's about."
