Who's News: Horsham Chief Given Two Additional Posts At Clark Oil Unit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/14/90

TORONTO -- Horsham Corp. said its president and chief operating officer, Jeremy Garbutt, has been named to the additional positions of president and chief operating officer of 60%-owned Clark Oil & Refining Corp., St. Louis. Horsham, a Toronto investment concern, said the appointment followed the resignation from Clark Oil of its chairman and chief executive officer, Ralph Cunningham, who will leave Aug. 31 to become president of Texaco Chemical Co., a division of Texaco Inc.

Mr. Garbutt said Clark Oil will seek a replacement for Mr. Cunningham, but the exact title of the new position hasn't been decided. In the meantime, Mr. Garbutt said he plans to spend at least the next six months in St. Louis getting to know the Clark Oil business and providing for a smooth management changeover.

Mr. Garbutt moved to Horsham last month from mining company affiliate, American Barrick Resources Corp., where he was chief financial officer. Since then, Horsham has said that it intends to take a more active role in running Clark Oil and that it will exercise an option to increase its stake in the subsidiary to 75% later this year.

Mr. Garbutt said he would have preferred Mr. Cunningham to have remained at Clark Oil. "He got head-hunted away," a Horsham spokeswoman said.
