T.H.E. Says Complaint By Ohio Agency Seeks Control of Energy Unit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/14/90

CONCORD, Mass. -- T.H.E. Group Inc. said the Ohio Department of Insurance filed a complaint in an Ohio state court seeking to assume control of the company's Oil & Gas Insurance Co. unit. The company said that the Ohio department alleges that the unit doesn't meet minimum surplus requirements under Ohio law. T.H.E. Group added that the unit "believes that it has adequately complied with those conditions and is now contesting" the department's complaint.

Separately, T.H.E. Group said it has received inquiries from the Securities and Exchange Commission and the National Association of Securities Dealers concerning its failure to file its 1989 10-K annual report, which was due several weeks ago. The company said it hasn't filed the report because it hasn't completed certain audit procedures, some of which are related to its Ohio unit and its dispute with the Ohio department. The company added that it is uncertain when it will file its 10-K.

T.H.E. Group said "third parties" have indicated "in principle their willingness" to buy its Ohio unit and the other insurance units of the holding company. The sales, if completed, "are expected to be at not less than {the units'} carrying value" on its books, the company said. After completion of the sales, the company would retain interest in a Texas-based insurance company and certain other assets, including a retail sporting goods store, a spokeswoman said.

The company said it has taken steps to cut its expenses, including the sale of its Forum Re Finance unit to an affiliate for the redemption of a $5 million secured debenture. The company said it expects to recognize a gain of more than $2 million from the sale. Additionally, T.H.E. Group said it purchased and retired $5.1 million of its Series B preferred shares from an affiliate.
