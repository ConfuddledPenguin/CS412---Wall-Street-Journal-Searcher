International: Brazil Lifts Curbs on Flow Of Capital to Foreign Firms

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/14/90

BRASILIA -- Brazil lifted a ban that prevented foreign multinational companies from remitting profits, dividends or capital abroad. But remittances that are already frozen will remain so for 16 months, the central bank said. "In the future, those operations won't be subject to compulsory deposit in the central bank, and companies here can freely remit resources to their parent firms," central bank spokesman Pedro Kleiberg said.

The ban on capital remittances was announced July 3, 1989, to protect the country's dwindling hard-currency reserves.

Mr. Kleiberg said the measure applies only to future operations. The money now on deposit at the central bank -- estimated at about $1 billion -- will be released in 12 equal monthly installments starting Sept. 16, 1991, he said.

Separately, Economy Minister Zelia Cardoso de Mello said inflation in April was close to 3% a month, and not zero as the government had predicted.

Mrs. Cardoso de Mello told senators on Thursday that President Fernando Collor de Mello's anti-inflation program called for a drop in inflation to around 10% a month in 100 days.

"We managed to knock inflation down to 3% in 40 days," she said. The official inflation rate is to be announced tomorrow.
