International: Italy Halts Shipments Linked to Iraq Gun

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 05/14/90

ROME -- Italian authorites said they seized more than 90 tons of components believed to be parts for the alleged Iraqi super gun. At a Saturday news conference, police said they believed the parts, made in Italy and destined for Iraq, were for the posterior section of a giant artillery piece. Officials said the huge gun would be capable of firing rounds hundreds of miles, including ones with nuclear or chemical warheads.

Italian law prohibits the sale or shipment of arms to Iraq. Documents seized along with the components indicate that the parts were for oil-field equipment ordered by Iraq's Ministry of Industry.

Carabinieri, Italy's paramilitary police, confiscated the parts and documents Friday during raids in Terni, Naples, Rome and Brescia, officials said.

One of the companies involved is Societa delle Fucine S.p.A., a steel company based in Terni in central Italy and owned by the state-owned steel company Ilva S.p.A.. Ilva is part of the state industrial conglomerate Istituto per la Ricostruzione Industriale.

A spokesman for Societa delle Fucine said, "The indication from the Iraqi ministry was that they were for a petrochemical plant. It did not seem possible that they could be used for any suspicious machinery."

Police said they haven't yet made any arrests.
