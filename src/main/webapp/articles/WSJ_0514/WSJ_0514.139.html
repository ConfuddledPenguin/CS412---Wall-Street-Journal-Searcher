International Brief -- Cie. Generale d' Electricite: French Concern's Revenue Was Up 1% in First Period

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/14/90

Cie. Generale d'Electricite (CGE) said its consolidated revenue edged up 1% in the first quarter to 32.70 billion francs ($5.92 billion) from 32.42 billion francs ($5.87 billion) a year earlier. The French electronics, telecommunications and engineering group said that the relatively flat sales performance was strongly influenced by changes in group structure, the most notable of which was its decision in 1989 to create GEC Alsthom N.V., a joint unit with General Electric Co. of Britain in the field of power distribution and engineering. CGE said that first-quarter revenue would have been up 7% from a year earlier after adjustment for this and other changes in group structure.

New orders taken during the first quarter totaled 38.5 billion francs, CGE said, up 16% from a year earlier.

CGE didn't provide comparable first-quarter earnings figures. For all of 1989, its consolidated net profit after payments to minority interests was 4.94 billion francs on revenue of 143.9 billion francs.
