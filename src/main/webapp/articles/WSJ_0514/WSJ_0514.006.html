Banner Unit Pleads Guilty to Charges Of Falsifying Tests

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/14/90

TORRANCE, Calif. -- A unit of Banner Industries Inc.'s Fairchild Industries Inc. subsidiary pleaded guilty to federal charges of conspiring to falsify test reports on special nuts, bolts and other fasteners used on most U.S. commercial and military aircraft. Fairchild's VSI Corp. on Friday agreed to pay a fine of $2.5 million and reimburse the government for about $1 million for the costs of the investigation. In addition, VSI agreed to pay $14.5 million to the government, part of which will be distributed to former employees who brought allegation of wrongdoing to federal investigators.

Last week, Assistant U.S. Attorney Bruce D. Carter brought charges against Fairchild's Voi-Shan testing laboratory in Chatsworth, Calif., a unit of VSI, and two of its managers in a complaint filed in federal court in Seattle.

Documents cite numerous instances in which Voi-Shan allegedly failed to conduct tests of strength, durability and brittleness. The unit allegedly produced reports approved by a fictitious inspector indicating that such tests had been properly performed in an attempt to hold down costs and boost profits, according to court documents.

"Any breach of government inspection procedures is a very serious matter," said Ben W. Prescott, newly appointed president of the Fairchild Fasteners Group. He said the company has "taken steps to prevent such conduct from ever taking place again."

Customers include such major aircraft and engine manufacturers as Boeing Co., McDonnell Douglas Corp., Lockheed Corp., General Electric Co. and United Technologies Corp.'s Pratt & Whitney Co. subsidiary.

The complaint also names as defendants James E. Ryan, a former Voi-Shan quality-assurance manager, and Aram Marderian, a former metallurgical lab supervisor for Voi-Shan. If convicted, each faces a maximum sentence of five years in prison and a $250,000 fine. Neither could be reached for comment.
