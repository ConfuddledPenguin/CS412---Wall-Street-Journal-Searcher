Air Canada Says Loss For 1st Quarter Was Flat at $11.1 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/14/90

MONTREAL -- Air Canada reported a first-quarter loss of 13 million Canadian dollars (US$11.1 million), or 18 Canadian cents (15 cents U.S.) a share, unchanged from a year earlier. The loss, which was expected, came despite a 12% jump in revenue to C$930 million from C$829 million.

Excluding one-time gains, the company's operating loss widened to C$40 million from C$28 million a year earlier.

Pierre Jeanniot, president and chief executive, said "the traditionally weak first quarter was made worse by a market decline and the increase in fuel prices." He said he expected 1990 would be a "difficult year."

Air Canada's yield per revenue passenger mile -- the airline's revenue for each mile flown by a paying passenger -- was 15.9 Canadian cents in the latest period, unchanged from a year earlier. While yield stayed flat, fuel costs shot up 24% to C$143 million and labor costs rose 10% to C$307 million.

The cost increases were partly due to an increase in capacity. Canada's largest airline had 5.58 billion available seat miles in the quarter compared with 5.36 billion available seat miles a year earlier.

Air Canada posted extraordinary gains totaling C$6 million from the sale of a DC-8 cargo aircraft and the sale of 10,000 shares of GPA Group PLC, the Irish aircraft leasing concern.

Corrections & Amplifications

AIR CANADA'S first-quarter results included 6 million Canadian dollars (US$5.1 million) net proceeds from the sale of shares in an investment and an undisclosed amount from the sale of an aircraft. Yesterday's edition incorrectly described these sales as extraordinary gains and misstated the total amount.

(WSJ May 15, 1990)
