REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: Frank Hypocrisy

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/14/90

Ah, Washington. The part of it most people can see is presumably consumed with the possibility of a grand deal on the deficit. Out of view, more important matters are being dealt with. For the first time, last year, the House placed limits on the free mailing privileges incumbents use to bombard voters with news of their good works. But the House isn't required by law to abide by them; it can just pass a supplemental appropriation months later to cover any overspending. And so the Members will exhaust this year's $44 million franking allowance by the end of this month. The Postal Service thinks the House eventually will spend $80 million this year sending out 535 million pieces of free mail explaining why voters should send them back to Washington.
