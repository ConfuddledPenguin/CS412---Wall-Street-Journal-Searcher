Business Brief -- Builders Transport Inc.: Two Holders May Make Offer to Acquire Concern

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/14/90

Two shareholders, David C. Walentas and Stanford M. Dinstein, said they may make an offer to acquire Builders Transport Inc. whether or not their plans for a possible proxy fight to oust the board is successful. In a filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission, Messrs. Walentas and Dinstein, who hold together a 7.8% stake, said that if they don't make an acquisition proposal before Builders Transport's annual meeting, they will seek the sale of the company to the highest bidder if their nominees are elected.

The two said they plan to ask Builders Transport's board for confidential data and intend to review the information before deciding whether and at what price to make an offer. According to the filing, Messrs. Walentas and Dinstein formed a committee on May 2 in connection with their possible proxy contest. Each of the members has agreed to be a nominee on the dissidents' slate.

In Camden, S.C., Robert Lee Garner, Builders' general counsel and corporate secretary, said the company hasn't received a copy of the filing, and had no comment.
