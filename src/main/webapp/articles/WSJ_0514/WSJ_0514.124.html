Business Brief -- Matra S.A.: Company, Two Other Firms Plan To Develop Missile

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/14/90

French defense and electronics company Matra S.A. said it would join French government-owned Aerospatiale S.A. and West Germany's Messerschmitt-Boelkow-Blohm G.m.b.H in a project to develop an improved version of the Roland short-range surface-to-air missile. A linkup among the three companies had been expected since Thomson S.A. of France and British Aerospace PLC announced plans late last year for a joint venture to develop missiles. Aerospatiale Chairman Henri Matre indicated then that he would welcome an alliance with Matra and MBB.

The new missile to be produced by the three companies, the Roland Mach 5, will cost about one billion francs ($181 million) to develop over five years, Matra said. Each company will contribute an equal share to the venture, while overall technical coordination will be in the hands of Aerospatiale.
