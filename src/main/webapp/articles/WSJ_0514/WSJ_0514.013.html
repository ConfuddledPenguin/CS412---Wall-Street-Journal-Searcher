Advantage Life Profit Expected to Fall Below Analysts' Forecasts

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/14/90

LAGUNA HILLS, Calif. -- Advantage Life Products Inc. said it expects fiscal fourth-quarter revenue and profit to be comparable to its third-quarter results and below analysts' expectations because of a major customer's financial problems. The customer, Ames Department Stores Inc., recently sought bankruptcy-law protection while it reorganizes to pay its debts. Advantage Life, a distributor of smoking cessation products, said that over the past 12 months, Ames has accounted for about 15% of its revenue.

Advantage Life reported net income of $350,000, or seven cents a share, on revenue of $3.1 million in its third quarter ended Jan. 31. In the fourth quarter ended April 30, 1989, the company had income of $323,000, or seven cents a share, on revenue of $2.1 million. The company didn't pay any taxes in the year-ago quarter.

Advantage Life said Ames owes about $360,000 from the purchase of anti-smoking kits. Ames also has ordered between $250,000 and $300,000 of smoking-deterrent gum that Advantage Life said it expected to deliver last month; now the company expects to deliver in the current quarter.

"The long-term impact on Advantage sales will depend on (Ames's) reorganization plan," said William W.F. Cowie, Advantage Life chief financial officer. "The filing will have no short-term impact on the company's viability," he said. Advantage Life said it expects its Ames business to continue, but at lower levels.
