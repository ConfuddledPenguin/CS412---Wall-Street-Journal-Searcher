International Brief -- Source Perrier S.A.: Consolidated Net Fell 74% In 1989 for Water Marketer

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/14/90

Source Perrier S.A. said consolidated net income fell 74% to 266 million francs ($48.2 million) in 1989 from the year earlier's unusually high level of 1.03 billion francs. The French mineral-water group's widely anticipated earnings plunge largely reflected its much-publicized problems with benzene content in its Perrier mineral water and a subsequent world-wide recall of the product. At the same time, it recorded a marked decline in non-recurring gains from asset sales that had significantly boosted its 1988 results.

The cost of the recall, which was outlined in the group's 1989 accounts even though it was incurred in February 1990, was 435 million francs. Perrier has expected to cover most of these costs with non-recurring gains from the sale of non-strategic assets, but these totaled only 254 million francs for 1989.

Excluding the non-recurring factors, Perrier said that its operating profit for 1989 was 1.41 billion francs ($255.8 million), up 21% from a year earlier. Revenue climbed 11% to 17.19 billion francs during the same period.
