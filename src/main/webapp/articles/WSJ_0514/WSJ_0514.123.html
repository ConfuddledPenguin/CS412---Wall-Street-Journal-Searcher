Bush Sets 30-Year Target To Put American on Mars

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/14/90

KINGSVILLE, Texas -- President Bush set a goal of putting an American on Mars in 30 years. Mr. Bush had previously endorsed a manned mission to Mars, but hadn't embraced the goal of accomplishing the job in 30 years. Officials of the National Aeronautics and Space Administration already had been discussing a timetable of roughly 30 years, however.

Mr. Bush, who is busy trying to begin negotiations with Congress to cut the federal budget deficit, didn't say how much he thinks it will cost to reach Mars in that time.

The president declared his goal in a commencement address here at Texas A&M University. Noting that 20 years have passed since the first Apollo mission to the moon, Mr. Bush declared: "Thirty years from now I believe man will stand on another planet. . . . I believe that before Apollo celebrates the 50th anniversary of its landing on the moon, the American flag should be planted on Mars."
