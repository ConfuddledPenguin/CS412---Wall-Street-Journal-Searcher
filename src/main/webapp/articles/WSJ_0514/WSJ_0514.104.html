Business Brief -- BFS Bancorp: Net Fell 55% in 1st Period As Loss Provision Tripled

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/14/90

BFS Bancorp reported first-quarter net income fell 55% to $770,000, or 54 cents a share, partly from a tripling of its provision for loan losses. The Bristol, Conn., holding company for Bankers Federal Savings noted that first-quarter net also included a smaller gain from the sale of investment securities, compared with a year earlier. Year-ago first-quarter profit was $1.7 million, or $1.18 a share.

The company's provision for loan losses increased to $310,000 from $101,000 a year earlier. The company said nonperforming loans are above historical levels but below the level of a year earlier. A spokeswoman said the bank didn't expect any further increases in its loan-loss reserve.

Interest income before provisions for loan losses increased 4% during the first quarter to $3 million from $2.9 million a year earlier.

In April, BFS settled litigation with a dissident shareholder, First Capital Group, a Redondo Beach, Calif., investment firm. As part of the settlement, First Capital gave up a seat on the company's board that it had won in a proxy fight.

In national over-the-counter trading Friday, BFS closed at $8.75 a share, down 75 cents.
