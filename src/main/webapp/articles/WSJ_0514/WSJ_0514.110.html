New Securities Issues

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/14/90

The following were among Friday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATES

Associates Corp. of North America -- $250 million of notes, due July 1, 1993, priced at par to yield 9.20%. The noncallable notes were priced at a spread of 61 basis points above the comparable Treasury. Rated double-A-3 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and double-A-minus by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through Kidder Peabody & Co.

MORTGAGE-BACKED

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $500 million of Remic mortgage securities, offered in 12 classes by Bear Stearns & Co. The offering, Series 1990-65, is backed by Fannie Mae 9 1/2% securities. The "A" class is eligible for thrift liquidity purposes. The collateral has an underlying weighted average coupon rate of 10.125% and weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29.4 years.

OTHERS

Sears Roebuck & Co. -- $500 million of securities backed by loans generated from its Discover credit cards, with a coupon rate of 9.20% at a price of 99.687 to yield 9.36%, via a syndicate headed by Dean Witter Reynolds Inc. The issue, through Discover Card Trust 1990-B, was priced at a yield spread of 75 basis points above the Treasury's new three-year note. The issue has an estimated average life of just over three years. The final stated maturity date is Nov. 16, 1995. The issue is rated triple-A by Moody's and by S&P, based on the quality of the underlying loans and a subordinated class totaling $50 million, or 10% of the senior securities. There is no guarantee from Sears or any Sears unit on the issue.

EUROBONDS

Swiss Bank Corp. Australia (Swiss parent) -- 100 million Australian dollars of 14 3/4 Eurobonds due June 6, 1994, priced at 102 to yield 14.66%, via Swiss Bank Corporation. Guaranteed by Swiss Bank Corp.
