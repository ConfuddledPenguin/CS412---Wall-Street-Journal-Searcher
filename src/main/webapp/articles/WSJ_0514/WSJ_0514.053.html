Consolidated Oil's Plan To Leave Chapter 11 Is Approved by Court

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/14/90

DENVER -- Consolidated Oil & Gas Inc. said a U.S. bankruptcy court approved its plan to emerge from Chapter 11 bankruptcy proceedings. The plan calls for all of the company's oil and gas properties to be sold for $24.2 million to a new entity owned by institutional investment partnerships under management of First Reserve Corp. of Greenwich, Conn.

Proceeds from that sale will be used to repay a $27 million loan from Mellon Bank Corp., Pittsburgh, Consolidated said.

Meanwhile, assets valued by Consolidated at about $8 million -- including $1.5 million in cash and 880,306 shares of common stock of Columbus Energy Corp., Denver -- will be liquidated and distributed to satisfy subordinated debt totaling about $28 million and to shareholders. The company said shareholders will receive 10% of any cash recovered in excess of $7 million.

First Reserve said it intends to retain George Simmons, president and chief operating officer of Consolidated, to head the new company, which will keep the old name and remain based in Denver.

Consolidated filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy-court protection in March of last year, giving it protection from creditors while it worked out a plan to pay its debts.
