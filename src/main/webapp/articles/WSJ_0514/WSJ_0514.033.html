Letters to the Editor: Hubble Is a Muddle Of Space Bureaucracy

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/14/90

Your April 5 article on the Hubble space telescope memorialized yet another icon of inefficient resource allocation by "governmental science." Governments embrace science and exploration for nationalistic reasons, not for science and not for politics as implied. America cannot afford the maintenance of antiquated ideas -- like the space shuttle. For all its advanced engineering it easily can be replaced by something more efficient.

So, too, America cannot afford the muddle of bureaucracy in future science projects such as Hubble. The pictures Hubble will send back to Earth are sure to be spectacular and likely will rekindle voter support for space exploration, no matter how dear the cost and how fruitless the effort. In the long run, Hubble may turn out to be more nationalistic than scientific.

The European Space Agency currently maintains an orbiting non-optical telescope that is much cheaper, more practical and purely scientific. However, this telescope does not send back pretty pictures, just data -- the stuff of which science is made.

Martin F. Medeiros

Washington
