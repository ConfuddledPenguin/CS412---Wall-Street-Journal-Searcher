Campeau Says Creditor Agrees to Give Notice Before Calling Loan

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/14/90

TORONTO -- Campeau Corp. said a major secured creditor, Edward J. DeBartolo Corp., agreed that, until June 30, it won't call its $480 million loan to the financially strained real estate and retailing concern without first giving nine days' notice. The previous agreement to give Campeau a nine-day warning before acting on DeBartolo's security against the loan expired May 10. A similar agreement with the Reichmann family's Olympia & York Developments Ltd. doesn't have an expiry date.

In January, Campeau defaulted on the DeBartolo loan and $225 million remaining in a loan from Olympia & York when its U.S. retailing units, Federated Department Stores Inc. and Allied Stores Corp., sought Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection. In March, Campeau refused to pay interest due on the loans.

DeBartolo's loan is secured mainly against 83.8% of the stock in Ralphs Grocery Co., Campeau's southern California supermarket chain, which isn't in Chapter 11 proceedings. DeBartolo, a closely held real estate developer based in Youngstown, Ohio, lent Campeau $480 million as part of the equity contribution toward Campeau's $6.6 billion takeover of Federated in May 1988. The Olympia & York loan, made last September to help Federated and Allied meet their fall working capital needs, is secured mostly by some of Campeau's Canadian real estate assets. Campeau plans to sell much of its real estate under a financial restructuring of its Canadian operations.
