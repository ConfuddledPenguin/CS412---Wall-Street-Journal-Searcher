Business Brief -- Esselte Business Systems Inc.: Quarterly Earnings Fell 28% Despite 8.7% Rise in Sales

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/14/90

Esselte Business Systems Inc., Garden City, N.Y., reported first-quarter net income fell 28%, partly because of weak demand for the company's retail supplies, equipment and office supplies. Net dropped to $12.4 million, or 60 cents a share, from $17.4 million, or 84 cents a share, a year earlier. Part of the decline was caused by an increase in interest expense from debt related to acquisitions. Sales rose 8.7% to $398.9 million from $367 million. Esselte attributed about one-third of the sales growth to improving foreign-exchange rates.

Esselte is the U.S. holding company for the office supplies, graphic arts supplies, retail supplies and equipment operations of Esselte AB of Sweden, which owns 78% of the U.S. company and wants to acquire the remaining shares.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading Friday, Esselte was quoted at $47.625 a share, up 12.5 cents.

Esselte AB is offering $47.64 for each of the 4.9 million shares it doesn't own.
