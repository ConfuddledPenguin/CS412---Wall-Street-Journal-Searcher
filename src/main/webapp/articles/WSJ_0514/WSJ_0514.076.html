Boston Five Suspends Dividend, Posts a Loss For Its Second Period

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/14/90

BOSTON -- Boston Five Bancorp said it suspended its dividend and expects to report a loss for the second quarter ended April 30. The company last paid a quarterly dividend of six cents a share on May 4. The holding company for Boston Five Cents Savings Bank said it would suspend its dividend "until earnings get back on track."

Boston Five said it expects to make a provision for possible loan losses of $3.5 million to $4 million for the second quarter due to an "assessment of the New England real estate market." Results for the quarter will be released the week of May 21, the company said.

In the quarter ended April 30, 1989, the company earned $2.9 million, or 41 cents a share.

Separately, Somerset Bankshares Inc., based in Somerville, Mass., said it suspended its quarterly dividend of seven cents a share because of its increase in nonperforming loans and uncertainty about the Massachusetts economy.
