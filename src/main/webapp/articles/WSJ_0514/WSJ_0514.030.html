Letters to the Editor: Aquino Story Ignores Political Realities

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/14/90

The April 2 page-one article on Eduardo "Danding" Cojuangco Jr. ("All in the Family: Closest Marcos Crony Is Favorite to Succeed Aquino in Philippines") ignores three realities in today's Philippine politics: A presidential candidate needs a nationwide party machine to win (Mr. Cojuangco has none), the majority of the Filipino electorate no longer can be bought and will reject anyone tainted with the Marcos legacy, and Defense Secretary Fidel "Eddie" Ramos is now the most popular person in the country and is running for the presidency in 1992. Mr. Cojuangco will have an outside chance of winning only if he can get himself to head a major-party ticket.

The Philippines has a very high literacy rate and even in the remotest "barrios" the people know why the country's economy is below the level of 25 years ago: Ferdinand Marcos and his cronies raped the country for more than two decades and caused the peso to lose 90% of its buying power.

Filipinos are no different from the East Europeans or Nicaraguans who just booted out their totalitarian masters. They will not vote for another regime headed by the very people who stole the country's wealth and who have remained unpunished.

It is in the interest of the U.S. to nurture the growth of real democracy in the Philippines instead of supporting people like Eduardo Cojuangco who would promise continued use of the U.S. military bases in the islands in exchange for such support. After all, that was Ferdinand Marcos's ploy, and look what happened.

Eugene V. Corazo

Beverly Hills, Calif.

---

You anoint Mr. Cojuangco "the leading candidate" for Philippine president, apparently excluding even President Corazon Aquino, who many believe could quite easily be drafted by popular demand to run again and win. In a call to the Philippine Embassy, I learned that Mr. Cojuangco hardly shows in the most recent polls. You fail to cite a single poll or mention one of many Aquino allies with strong followings, starting with Defense Secretary Ramos, Foreign Affairs Secretary Manglapus, Speaker of the House Mitra or Senate President Salonga.

You realize chief opposition leader Sen. Juan Ponce Enrile and Vice President Salvador "Doy" Laurel (in spite of his office) lack significant electoral stature, which polls and popular sentiment confirm. But your theme that Mr. Cojuangco has stature flies in the face of credulity and seems based solely on Mr. Cojuangco's Marcos crony capitalist patronage, which your research confirms is well known, and a one-sided exaggeration of Philippine political culture.

L. John Swallow Jr.

Washington
