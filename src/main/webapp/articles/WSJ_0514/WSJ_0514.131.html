Who's News: William Laidig Resigns From Georgia-Pacific Unit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/14/90

ATLANTA -- Georgia-Pacific Corp. said William R. Laidig, 63 years old, resigned as chairman of Great Northern Nekoosa Corp., which Georgia-Pacific acquired earlier this year after a protracted takeover battle. The forest-products concern said Mr. Laidig will receive more than $2.4 million for his resignation, as a result of the severance clause in his employment contract. That agreement guaranteed Great Northern's senior executives that they would get three times their salaries if Great Northern was taken over. Mr. Laidig's cash compensation in 1989 was $801,000. The agreement also assured Mr. Laidig that, upon his resignation, he would receive three times the amount of his highest annual noncash bonus, but a Georgia-Pacific spokeswoman could not ascertain that amount on Friday.

Georgia-Pacific said Ronald P. Hogan, 49, Great Northern's vice chairman and Georgia-Pacific's president and chief operating officer, will succeed Mr. Laidig. Georgia-Pacific said A.D. "Pete" Correll, 49, Georgia-Pacific's executive vice president, pulp and paper, will continue as Great Northern's president and chief executive officer.
