Bombardier Lines Up Partners for Rail Plan, Executive Discloses

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/14/90

MONTREAL -- Bombardier Inc. has "identified most if not all" of the members of a consortium that would build a proposed high-speed rail link between Quebec City and Toronto using French TGV (very fast train) technology, said Pierre MacDonald, the vice president in charge of the project. "The consortium would be a broad group" that would include Canadian, U.S. and international "industrial and financial leaders," Mr. MacDonald said. He declined to identify the members of the group but said Bombardier has "concluded agreements with some members and is negotiating with the others." Bombardier is a Montreal-based transportation equipment and aerospace concern. It holds the North American rights to the French TGV technology.

In an interview after a presentation here to a government task force studying high-speed rail, Mr. MacDonald wouldn't say if the CP Rail unit of Canadian Pacific Ltd. and government-owned Canadian National, Canada's two major railroads, have agreed to join the Bombardier consortium, but added "we've said all along that the project can't fly without CP and CN."

A Canadian Pacific spokesman said "we've had discussions with many groups {regarding high-speed rail} but we haven't made any commitments yet." A person familiar with the Bombardier project said Canadian Pacific staff visited France to study the TGV train and are enthusiastic about the potential of a Canadian TGV line.

As reported, Bombardier proposes building a TGV line connecting Quebec City, Montreal, Ottawa and Toronto. The line would cost about 5.3 billion Canadian dollars (US$4.5 billion), of which the Canadian, Quebec and Ontario governments would be asked to contribute about C$1.6 billion.
