Christiana Unit Told To Pay $8.1 Million By Jury in California

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/14/90

EL CAJON, Calif. -- A state court jury awarded $8.1 million to homeowners in a verdict against a unit of Christiana Cos. for faulty construction of a San Diego condominium project. Owners of the 50 luxury units charged Christiana with negligence and implied breach of warranty that resulted in leaky roofs and balconies in addition to other problems. After a seven-week trial, the jury agreed with the homeowners' allegations.

Pat Prindle, a San Diego attorney representing Christiana, said that the verdict probably will be appealed, although a decision hasn't been reached.

A Christiana spokesman said that the real-estate investment and management concern has insurance coverage that will pay part of any award arising from the litigation, which involved the company's Christiana Community Builders subsidiary.
