What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/17/90

THE U.S. IS EXPORTING more gasoline, even as debate continues over the adequacy of the nation's future petroleum supplies. Higher foreign gas prices are spurring the trend, which could result in continued high costs for U.S. motorists and tight wholesale supplies in the coming months. Those factors in turn could create new policy problems for the federal government. ---

GM and the UAW agreed to continue contract talks, working past Friday's strike deadline. The union's move marked only the second time ever that it put off a strike after the expiration of a Big Three pact. In Canada, almost 13,000 workers at the Ford unit began a strike Saturday.

---

GOP budget negotiators proposed a limit on all tax deductions for taxpayers making more than $200,000 annually, said top aides. The move was a response to a Democratic bid to pair the capital-gains tax cut with higher income taxes on the rich.

---

Airlines are attempting to lift fares for the second time since the Mideast conflict boosted fuel prices and created the prospect of record industry losses. Several big carriers said they plan to raise fares by 4.2%.

---

A UAL union group is likely to abandon its most recent $4.38 billion offer to acquire the airline company and is considering making a lower offer, according to people familiar with the group. The union group has ended its formal alliance with New York investment firm Coniston Partners.

---

The U.S. economy is clearly being hurt by rising oil prices, according to the latest government economic reports. In August, producer prices soared 1.3%, retail sales fell 0.6% and industrial output slipped 0.2%.

---

Foreign investment in the U.S. is down sharply this year, while American investment abroad is rising. Analysts warn that these trends will bring the U.S. a weaker dollar, higher interest rates and a slower economy.

---

Stock prices fell on signs of growing Mideast tensions and weakness in the U.S. economy. The Dow Jones industrials dropped 18.56 to 2564.11.

---

A First Gibraltar Bank affiliate acquired Oklahoma's largest thrift, Sooner Federal S&L of Tulsa.

---

The Resolution Trust Corp. wants to alter roughly 200 controversial thrift sales that bestowed about $4.2 billion in tax benefits on wealthy buyers in 1988, but it's unlikely the cash-strapped S&L cleanup agency can take action any time soon.

---

U.S. grain export subsidies have reached record levels, as the Agriculture Department seeks to counter the competitive challenge of the EC.
