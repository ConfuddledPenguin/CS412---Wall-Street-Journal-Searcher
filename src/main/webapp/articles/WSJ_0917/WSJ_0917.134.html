Hot Diggity Dogs Are Back At Wal-Mart Stores Unit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/17/90

BENTONVILLE, Ark. -- In what amounts to a major triumph for a Texas hot dog vendor and her elderly, handicapped work force, a Wal-Mart Stores Inc. unit that had come under fire for ordering the vendor off its properties said it has reversed its position and will allow the vendor to stay. After Wal-Mart's Sam's Wholesale Clubs unit ordered Scarlett Rabalais's Hot Diggity Dogs company to remove its stands from in front of 21 Sam's stores in Texas, the local press covered the eviction's implications for Ms. Rabalais's largely elderly and handicapped work force. Eager to stop the bad publicity, Sam's officials invited Ms. Rabalais to Bentonville last week to discuss the problem.

Following that meeting, Sam's said that it agreed to let Hot Diggity Dogs not only stay at its existing sites, but also expand to three others.
