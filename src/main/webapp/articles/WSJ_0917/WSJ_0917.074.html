Letters to the Editor -- Lesson for Israel: In Strength Lies Hope

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/17/90

Barry Rubin, in "Arafat's Iraq Debacle," editorial page, Aug. 24, failed to mention the debacle for those organizations such as the Peace Now movement that have pursued the idea of a negotiated "peace process" with the PLO. In its wildest dreams Israel could never hope to meet the positions held by Kuwait with respect to Iraq and the PLO. These include internationally recognized boundaries, full trade relations, no refugee problems, recognition by the U.N., a practically demilitarized posture (armed forces 1/50th the size of Iraq's) and the same cultural and religious background. Yet the response to these positions by Iraq, supported by the PLO, was swift and complete obliteration of a nation at peace.

Since Iraq is within missile, chemical and nuclear range of Israel, the lesson for Israel is clear: No amount of peace-loving posturing will bring any security. Rather, Israel should remain as strong as possible and continue its policy of offering to negotiate with any Arab organization or nation that does not have a charter calling for Israel's destruction.

Seymour Yusem

New York
