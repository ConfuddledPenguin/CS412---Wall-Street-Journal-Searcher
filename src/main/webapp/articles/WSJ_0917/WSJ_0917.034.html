WestAir Holding Inc. Realigns Some Routes Of United Express Unit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/17/90

FRESNO, Calif. -- WestAir Holding Inc. said it realigned some routes flown by its United Express airline unit. That move includes the addition of new service and the suspension of other service that will entail furloughing pilots and other personnel. Among the changes, WestAir said it plans to begin service Oct. 15 between Dulles airport outside Washington, D.C., and Charlotte, N.C. On Oct. 31, WestAir said it will expand service between Los Angeles and San Diego, as well as resume flights between Long Beach, Calif., and San Jose, Calif.

WestAir also announced cutbacks in service that will result in the termination of 99 of its about 2,780 employees. Included are 25 customer-service personnel and 24 pilots. In addition, 50 pilots are set to be furloughed as the result of schedule adjustments in California, WestAir said.

The company added that it plans to retire a dozen aircraft by year end, replacing them with more fuel-efficient models. Currently, the company has a fleet of about 100 aircraft.
