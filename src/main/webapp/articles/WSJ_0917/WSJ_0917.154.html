International: Talks Continue in Tokyo On Allied-Signal Dispute

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/17/90

TOKYO -- U.S. and Japanese trade negotiators continued talks over the weekend in an effort to settle a dispute over whether Allied-Signal Inc. is being denied fair access to the Japanese market for its amorphous metals material. Although the deadline for settling the issue without formal action by the Office of the U.S. Trade Representative was technically Saturday, Allied won't be able to activate its petition until Monday morning in Washington.

A U.S. official familiar with the talks indicated that, while there hadn't been any fundamental breakthroughs as of Sunday morning, there had been enough movement to continue talking. He rated the chances for an eleventh-hour agreement as "an even call."

Allied, based in Morristown, N.J., filed a complaint last March charging that Japanese electric utilities were delaying commercial introduction of amorphous-metal electrical transformers until Japanese steelmakers could find a way around Allied's Japanese patent protection for the material. At the request of U.S. trade officials, Allied later suspended the complaint until Sept. 15 to allow time for the issue to be settled without formal action.

Under U.S. trade law, reactivation of the petition could lead to retaliatory action against Japanese imports to the U.S. next year.
