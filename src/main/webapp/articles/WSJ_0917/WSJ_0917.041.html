Premark International Will Pay $11.9 Million To Settle Lawsuits

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/17/90

DEERFIELD, Ill. -- Premark International Inc. said it will pay $11.9 million to settle disputes with Weldotron Corp. concerning food-wrapping machine patents. The consumer and restaurant products concern said that in return for the payment, Weldotron, based in Piscataway, N.J., has dropped its lawsuits and has agreed not to attempt to enforce the patents against Hobart, a Premark subsidiary that produces a broad line of food equipment including food-wrapping machines.

Premark said Weldotron sought total damages of more than $200 million in two lawsuits filed in 1986 in the U.S. District Court of New Jersey alleging that the unit infringed on its patents. Hobart countersued, claiming the patents were invalid and unenforceable.

Premark said it decided to settle the suits to avoid the uncertainty and additional costs of further litigation. A spokesman for Premark said that the settlement will have a financial impact on the company, but that it hasn't determined if a charge will be necessary.
