International Brief -- Northern Star Holdings Ltd.: Two Receivers Are Named To Manage TV Operator

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/17/90

Two Ernst & Young partners were appointed as receivers to manage Northern Star Holdings Ltd., an Australian television network operator. Ernst & Young said the partners, James Millar and Robert Dunn, were appointed at the request of Northern Star and its three major banks, Westpac Banking Corp., Commonwealth Banking Corp. and Citicorp.

The banks are owed more than 400 million Australian dollars (US$330.8 million), mostly to Westpac. Steve Cosser, Northern Star chief executive officer, said the receivership arrangement was made in cooperation with the three banks.

Westpac said that subject to the receivers' investigations, the banks propose to provide continued financial support so the Channel Ten network operated by Northern Star can recover.

Rupert Murdoch's News Corp. owns about 5% of Northern Star. Britain's Daily Mail & General Trust Group PLC has 14.5% and Thames Television PLC holds 10%.

Two of Australia's three commercial TV networks now are in receivership.
