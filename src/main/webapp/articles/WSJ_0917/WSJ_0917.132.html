Business Brief -- Recognition Equipment Inc.: Settlement Reached in Suit By Ex-Postmaster General

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/17/90

Recognition Equipment Inc., Dallas, said it reached an out-of-court settlement in the civil lawsuit filed by Paul Carlin, former U.S. postmaster general. Recognition agreed to pay an undisclosed amount in return for the release of all claims against the company and its former officers. Carlin filed the suit in May 1989 against Recognition and two former company officers. The suit was based on allegations relating to a 1988 indictment that accused the company and two of its top officers with attempting to manipulate the award of $400 million in U.S. Postal Service contracts. In November 1989, a federal judge acquitted all defendants in the case against Recognition.

Recognition, a maker of image and data capture computer products, has settled most of the major litigation stemming from the indictment, according to a spokeswoman.
