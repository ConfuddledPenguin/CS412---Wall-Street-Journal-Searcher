Washington Power System Sets $500 Million Bond Sale

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/17/90

RICHLAND, Wash. -- Washington Public Power Supply System said it plans to sell $500 million of refunding revenue bonds. The utility said $250 million of the bonds will be sold on Sept. 25 and another $250 million will be sold within 45 days.

WPPSS defaulted in 1983 on $2.25 billion of bonds, the largest default in municipal bond history. This will be its third bond sale this year and will complete its high-coupon refunding program involving $3.5 billion of bonds. The new securities, whose maturities range from two years to 27 years, involve WPPSS units 1 and 2, which didn't default, and are guaranteed by the Bonneville Power Administration, a regional power agency of the Energy Department. Goldman, Sachs & Co. is senior manager.
