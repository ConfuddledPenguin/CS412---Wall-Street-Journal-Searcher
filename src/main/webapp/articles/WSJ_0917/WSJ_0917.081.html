Letters to the Editor: Police and Prisons Can't Solve Addiction

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/17/90

For a publication that seems to understand so well the dangers of big government (e.g., "The Imperial Congress"), you seem remarkably satisfied with the "war on drugs" ("Cracking the Drug Problem," Review & Outlook, Aug. 13). As research scientists studying addiction, we are concerned that this heavily funded and promoted "war on drugs" is ignoring the most dangerous drugs, tobacco and alcohol. As Americans weary of government draining our resources with failed promises, we are sorry to see you supporting another set of money-hungry, power-hungry politicians and bureaucrats. Addiction is a scientific/medical problem that cannot be solved by policemen and prisons.

J.E. Martins

President, CEO

Francis Gale

Vice President, Research

BioResearch Inc.

Denver
