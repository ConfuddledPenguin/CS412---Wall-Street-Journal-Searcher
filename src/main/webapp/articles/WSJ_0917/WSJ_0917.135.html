International: Soviets Are Said to Import Gasoline From the West

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 09/17/90

LONDON -- The Soviet Union is importing gasoline for the first time in at least eight years, European oil-industry sources said. The move coincides with other efforts by Moscow to import goods in short supply, including billions of cigarettes from the U.S. One oil-industry official said that it was difficult to tell how much gasoline the Soviet Union was acquiring, but that he had heard estimates it bought as much as 500,000 tons so far this month from suppliers throughout Western Europe.

The industry sources said the purchases might result from problems in the Soviet refining industry, or from cutbacks in imported crude oil. One official said Soviet imports of Libyan oil may have fallen sharply lately, depriving Soviet refineries of a key feedstock. It wasn't clear if Moscow would continue buying gasoline.
