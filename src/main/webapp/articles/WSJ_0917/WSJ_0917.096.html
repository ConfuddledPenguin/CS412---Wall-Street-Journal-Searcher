World Markets: Tokyo Stocks Slipped 177.62 Points on Friday; London Shares Ended Near Day's Lowest Level

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 09/17/90

Tokyo stocks finished lower Friday as heavy futures and options related selling because of the close of the September futures contract met some resistance from investment trust fund buying by the Big Four securities houses. Traders said a weak day in the Japanese government bond market aggravated fears of interest rate increases, damping sentiment.

London shares finished close to the day's lowest level as the market was hurt by a poor session on Wall Street and a discounted futures market.

Tokyo's Nikkei Index of 225 issues was down 177.62 points, or 0.71%, at 24897.46. Volume on the first section was estimated at 530 million shares, down from 565.9 million Thursday. Decliners outnumbered advancers 691 to 294, while 144 issues were unchanged.

In early trading Monday, the Nikkei index fell another 207.15 points to close the morning session at 24690.31.

In Friday's trading, the Tokyo Stock Price Index of all issues listed in the first section fell 17 points, or 0.89%, to 1895.68.

The second section index fell 11.18 points, or 0.31%, to 3586.82. Volume in the section was estimated at seven million shares, down from 7.5 million shares Thursday.

Traders said there was a huge amount of sell orders at the start of trading Friday as the market moved to settle arbitrage positions.

"There were 14.5 million sell orders at the opening. I thought the market would fall 500 points," said one trader.

However, the trader said new investment trust funds set up by the Big Four securities firms "bought cash like crazy to support the market."

Although the volume of trading was relatively heavy compared with recent levels, traders said dealings were mostly lethargic following the early flurry of activity, with institutional investors on the sidelines.

Traders said the seven-basis point rise on the benchmark Japanese government bond to 8.415% at the close of stock trading depressed sentiment, aggravating fears of further interest rate increases. A basis point is 1/100th of a percentage point.

Traders said most sectors suffered overall declines Friday.

But despite the drop, many traders said they see the market poised to move higher in the near future.

Amy Yip, sales manager with Baring Securities Ltd., said the market could push higher to around 28000 points.

"The market has been worrying about oil prices, the yen and interest rates," Ms. Yip said. "The yen has turned positive and the impact of higher oil on the Japanese economy looks less negative than originally feared so you can say that 1 1/2 of the factors have turned positive," Ms. Yip said.

Stephen Good, a sales representative with McIntosh Securities, said the recent uptick in trading volume suggests that a number of individual investors are returning to the market.

Mr. Good said the potential for one more discount rate increase could weigh on the market for the near term but once that is out of the way, the market should be set to recover on the strength of Japanese economic fundamentals.

Traders said expected buying at around 24000 points plus the potential addition of more than 200 billion yen ($1.47 billion) in new investment trust funds could begin to send the market higher.

Traders said they expect growing interest in Japanese investments will start with the bond market and spill over into the stock market.

However, some participants remain bearish about the market's outlook.

"There weren't a lot of institutional investors out there buying" Friday, said one trader. "There were just four or five big players supporting the market and they had to eat an awful lot of stocks to keep it at this level. And there were a lot of people eager to sell," the trader added.

In London, traders said that with the escalation of hostilities in the Middle East and Friday's disclosure of an unexpectedly high 10.6% U.K. August inflation rate, caution would prevail in the market for the near future.

"It was an unhappy end to a lackluster week," one trader said. Wall Street's decline during the course of London trading generated selling pressure in the Financial Times-Stock Exchange 100 futures market, prompting falls in the main market, traders said.

Market makers retained control of price movements in a "trendless market," one trader said, as only selective buying occured.

The FT-SE 100 Share Index closed down 33.3 points at 2093.8, falling steadily after a midmorning recovery pushed the market to an intraday high of 2122.1.

The Financial Times 30 Share Index fell 30.3 points to 1609.6; volume was 418 million shares, up from Thursday's 335.4 million shares.

In the futures markets, the September FT-SE 100 index contract traded at 2085, down from Thursday's close of 2137.

Among the main movers in the market, chemicals conglomerate ICI slipped 36 pence to 829 pence (68 cents to $15.60) following a statement in Toronto on Thursday by the chairman that was taken by some analysts to indicate a slowdown in the company's earnings in the year ahead. The company, however, said his remarks pertained to a general, global economic slowdown.

Thorn EMI gained 4 pence to 616 pence, helped by a buy recommendation from securities house Kleinwort Benson.

Brewing concern Bass fell 32 pence to 958 pence as Barclays de Zoete Wedd cut the company's profit forecast. Other brewing stocks fared poorly. Allied-Lyons fell 10 pence to 465 pence and Grand Metropolitan dropped 7 pence to 564 pence.

Rank Organisation fell 32 pence to 558 pence. One trader said the fall followed news of a downgrading by a London brokerage firm, while another trader said he detected selling pressure from New York.

Water stocks held firm. Thames Water added a penny to 227 pence. North West Water gained 3 pence to 223 pence.

Enterprise Oil, down 15 pence to 650 pence, was hit by continuing rumors that Elf Aquitaine might sell its stake in the company. Other oil stocks, were mixed.

Meanwhile, shares closed lower on all major European and Asian markets. South African gold stocks closed narrowly mixed.
