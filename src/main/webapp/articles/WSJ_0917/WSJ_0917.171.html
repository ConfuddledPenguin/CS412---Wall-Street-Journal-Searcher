U.S. Court Rejects Restraining Order Sought By Ferruzzi

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/17/90

CHICAGO -- A federal court turned down Ferruzzi Finanziaria S.p.A.'s request for a restraining order to prevent the Chicago Board of Trade from undertaking disciplinary proceedings against the Italian concern. The CBOT said its Business Conduct Committee will decide next week, as scheduled, on charges that the Milan grain merchant attempted to manipulate the soybean market in 1989.

Ferruzzi said it was "disappointed" by the ruling but noted that the petition was only "the first step" in seeking judicial redress of what it called the CBOT's "unfair treatment."

Ferruzzi had filed two suits. One alleged that the CBOT violated antitrust and commodities law when it issued a 1989 order that forced soybean traders to close out their positions. That order forced Ferruzzi to liquidate a potentially lucrative long, or buying, market position in soybean futures, whose value had climbed because of a drought.

Ferruzzi claimed the exchange issued the order not in response to a market emergency, but to protect companies affiliated with CBOT board members from suffering big losses because they were holding short, or selling, positions.

The second suit alleged that the CBOT, because of a conflict of interest, shouldn't be allowed to render judgment on Ferruzzi involving the 1989 soybean-market activities. It was the latter suit that asked the court to block the disciplinary hearing which was ruled on; the litigation alleging the CBOT acted wrongfully remains pending.

The CBOT has denied any wrongdoing.

In a prepared statement Friday, the CBOT said it is "pleased that the federal court has reaffirmed the exchange's responsibility and right to conduct investigations of violations of its rules and to take disciplinary action in defense of the integrity of our markets."
