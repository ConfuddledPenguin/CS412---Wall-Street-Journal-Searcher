Dutch Concern Starts International Environmental Fund

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/17/90

AMSTERDAM -- Pierson, Heldring & Pierson N.V. launched an open-ended mutual fund to invest internationally in companies involved in cleaning up the environment or preventing pollution. The fund, Environment Growth Fund N.V., is likely to attract initial investments totaling 50 million guilders (US$28 million) to 100 million guilders, said Frans J. Ballendux, head of capital management at Pierson, a merchant banking arm of ABN Amro Holding N.V. Mr. Ballendux said he believed the fund would have attracted far more investors had it not been launched amid stock market turmoil.

The shares, to be listed on the Amsterdam Stock Exchange, will be sold at 50 guilders each, and the offering period ends Sept. 26. New shares will continue to be available after that date, but investors who buy during the offering period avoid the 1% stock market commission.

The fund initially will focus on companies involved in purification and waste management, Pierson said. As opportunities arise, however, the fund aims to invest more in companies providing systems and products that are "friendly" to the environment.

Mr. Ballendux said the U.S. offers the most opportunities for buying environmental stocks at the moment but that Europe offers bigger long-term potential, particularly given the huge task of cleaning up pollution in Central Europe.
