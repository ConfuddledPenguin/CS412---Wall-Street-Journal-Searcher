International Brief -- Kloeckner-Humboldt-Deutz AG: German Concern Posts Net, Reversing Year-Ago Loss

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/17/90

Kloeckner-Humboldt-Deutz AG, a West German engineering and machinery concern, posted first-half net income of five million marks ($3.2 million), reversing a year-earlier loss of 30 million marks. World-wide group sales advanced 10% to 1.88 billion marks from 1.7 billion marks. Year-earlier figures were adjusted downward to account for shedding operations.

Amid a far-reaching corporate restructuring, Kloeckner's return to profitability was apparently aided by the disposal of some unprofitable businesses in the past 12 months. On the basis of continuing operations, the company said its five million-mark profit compared with a year-earlier loss of 18 million marks.

Kloeckner's order backlog at June 30 increased 22% to 2.08 billion marks.

For 1990, Kloeckner said it expects "better operating earnings" than the year-earlier 44 million marks, before taxes and extraordinary charges, and expressed confidence about posting net. In 1989, after charges of 194.1 million marks, the company had a net loss of 170 million marks on 4.06 billion marks of world-wide sales.
