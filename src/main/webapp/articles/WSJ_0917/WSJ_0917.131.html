Technology: MIT Appeal on Magnet Lab Is Dismissed by Foundation

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/17/90

CAMBRIDGE, Mass. -- Directors of the National Science Foundation dismissed Massachusetts Institute of Technology's appeal of the decision to establish the new national magnet center at Florida State University. The NSF's selection of the Florida school last month shocked MIT, which has been home to the world's leading magnet laboratory, and chagrined many scientists who worry that access to top notch laboratory facilities in the U.S. will be lost for several years while Florida State builds up its lab.

MIT said that its Francis Bitter National Magnet Laboratory, the national laboratory since 1960, will have to be closed. NSF is providing $6 million in annual funding through next year. But with the laboratory going to Florida, researchers are likely to drift away from MIT and may postpone long-term projects until the new lab is established.

MIT has orchestrated a major letterwriting campaign by scientists who use the laboratory and has been sharply critical of the NSF for choosing Florida State, which will be building a laboratory from scratch. However, the state of Florida has committed $58 million to building the center and NSF officials questioned the commitment of MIT to the project in picking Florida.

Friday, the NSF board of directors reaffirmed its earlier decision saying it will "best maintain the long-term health and vitality," of magnet research.
