Olympia & York Negotiates Loans For Canary Wharf

  • Author: Michael T. Malloy
  • Date: 09/17/90

TORONTO -- Olympia & York Developments Ltd. said it is negotiating long-term project financing for its Canary Wharf real estate development in London, which eventually is expected to cost about #4 billion ($7.53 billion). An Olympia & York spokesman said two separate project financings were under negotiation. He declined to describe their size or possible terms. He said they wouldn't be used to reduce the company's #500 million equity stake in the project.

Canary Wharf is an office project meant to be the centerpiece of the world's biggest property development, the commercial-residential Docklands redevelopment.

Olympia & York so far has sunk more than #1 billion into Canary Wharf, which still isn't finished. The spokesman said the loans under negotiation would be the first project financings for the development.

Developers normally fund the early stages of a development with short-term construction loans, then refinance that debt with longer-term loans that are secured by the project itself.

Such project loans may be further guaranteed by the developer, or may be nonrecourse loans without such guarantees. The spokesman declined to describe the degree of exposure that Olympia & York might have under the proposed financings.

Canary Wharf has had trouble attracting tenants, which could pose financial uncertainty for Olympia & York, which is owned by Toronto's Reichmann family. A first phase of 4.3 million square feet is to open next year, but only about half the space has been rented.

The Olympia & York spokesman said the company remains firmly committed to the project. He said its #500 million equity investment "will remain in place at least until the project is entirely completed."
