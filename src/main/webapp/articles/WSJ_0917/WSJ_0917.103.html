Indonesia Detains Ex-Bank President In Inquiry Into Losses

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/17/90

JAKARTA, Indonesia -- The former deputy president of P.T. Bank Duta has been detained by Indonesian police in connection with investigations of major, unexplained losses at the bank. The Indonesian government, in a bid to deal with the scandal, appears to be pursuing two separate probes into dealings at Bank Duta. The Bank is the fifth-largest in the country. The investigations -- one by the central bank and one by Attorney General Singgih -- are unlikely to be completed quickly, observers say.

Police, acting on orders of Mr. Singgih, detained Dicky Iskandar Di Nata on Thursday, who was Bank Duta's deputy president until he, other managers and the whole board of supervisors were fired Sept. 4. Although no formal charge has been placed against him, the 40-year-old Mr. Dicky can remain in detention for some time under Indonesian law.

Indonesian newspapers reported that authorities have seized Mr. Dicky's assets and industry sources close to the bank say that losses of about $300 million were accumulated.

Bank Duta's own position has been secured, as the majority shareholders -- three charity foundations chaired by President Suharto -- have injected new capital to make good the losses. But many questions remain about how the losses were incurred and were kept from supervisors at the central bank, Bank Indonesia, and minority shareholders of Bank Duta, which sold shares on the Jakarta Stock Exchange last May.
