Arizona PS Unit Of Pinnacle West To Cut More Jobs

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/17/90

TUCSON, Ariz. -- Arizona Public Service Co., the main unit of Pinnacle West Capital Corp., said it will eliminate about 675 more jobs this year, or 14% of its 4,908-member non-nuclear work force. The belt-tightening move is part of a process begun in 1988 and accelerated in June, when the company undertook an intensive review designed to make it leaner and more competitive with other energy suppliers.

The cutbacks affect 60 managers and supervisors, 315 clerical and professional workers, and 300 union employees, the company said. About 400 positions have already been eliminated this year, mainly through an early-retirement program.

The utility said none of the 2,840 employees at its Palo Verde nuclear power plant will be affected by the latest reductions.

Two vice presidents, Russ Hulse, 62 years old, and Chuck Jarman, 54, are retiring, the utility said. Another vice president, Dave Ellis, 51, has resigned. The departures reduce the number of company officers to 13 from 16.

Mark De Michele, president and chief executive officer, said a major objective is "to reduce our pending rate-increase request before the Arizona Corporation Commission," which regulates utilities. Details of the revised filing won't be available until later this year, he said.

"All of this is driven by increased competition and the flattening in the Arizona economy," said a spokesman for the utility. "It has nothing to do with Pinnacle West and PacifiCorp."

The two-year-old restructuring program began before PacifiCorp, a utility holding company based in Portland, Ore., launched its effort last year to acquire Arizona Public Service or Pinnacle West. Earlier this month, PacifiCorp dropped its pursuit in exchange for seasonal power swaps with Arizona Public Service.
