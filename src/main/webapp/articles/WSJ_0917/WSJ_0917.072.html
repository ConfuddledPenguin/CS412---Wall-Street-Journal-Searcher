Letters to the Editor: Oh, Let's Call The Whole Thing Off

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/17/90

As a European working in the U.S. materials industry, I have often conducted spirited coffee-break debates on the appropriate spelling of the 13th element. Imagine my delight at finding the logical European spelling, aluminium, in your report on FMC Corp. (Aug. 16, Money & Investing). Picture, then, my confusion at your reporter's reversion to the unfounded American spelling, aluminum, in the next clause of the same sentence. I am concerned that your august and influential publication cannot determine a spelling policy for such an important material. Please, in the American vernacular, get with the program . . . er, programme.

Andrew McTiernan

Evansville, Ind.
