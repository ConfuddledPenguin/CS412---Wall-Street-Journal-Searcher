Politics & Policy: GAO Urged Congress to Act On Government Securities

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/17/90

WASHINGTON -- Congress should enact legislation to assure that the U.S. government securities market has adequate investor protection and that bond-price data are publicly available on a timely basis to those willing to pay for it, the General Accounting Office said. The congressional watchdog agency urged Congress to give the Treasury authority to adopt rules to upgrade the sales practices of government securities brokers and dealers and to require interdealer brokers in the market to make transaction information available to market participants on a real-time basis.

The Public Securities Association, a bond trade group whose members include all 41 primary dealers in U.S. government securities, said it is working on an initiative that "will obviate the need for Congress to mandate price dissemination through federal regulation." The joint venture, which involves primary dealers and most interdealer brokers, "will begin disseminating prices in early 1991 on a 24-hour global real-time basis," the association said.

The GAO recommended that Congress extend the Treasury's rule-making authority over the government market. This authority, granted in the 1986 Government Securities Act, is scheduled to expire next year. GAO also urged Congress to extend Securities Investor Protection Corp. coverage to customer accounts in specialized government securities dealers.

Acting Assistant Treasury Secretary Michael Basham, in a letter attached to the GAO report, supported the recommendation that Congress order public access to broker screen information. He didn't address the sales-practices question.
