National Medical Enterprises Inc.

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/17/90

SANTA MONICA, Calif. -- National Medical Enterprises Inc., a health care concern, said it will redeem on Oct. 15 all extendable notes, due 1999, that haven't been tendered for repayment on or before Oct. 1. The company has $98 million of the notes outstanding. It issued the notes in October 1984. National Medical said it will redeem the notes at 100% of the principal amount. Interest accrued through Oct. 14 will be paid to notes of record Sept. 30. The current interest rate is 9.4%, and the current interest-rate period expires Oct. 14.

Note holders' option of tendering for repayment arises when a new interest period and rate for the extendable notes is established. Under terms of the notes, the company must establish a new interest period and rate for a five-year period beginning Oct. 15. National Medical has set an interest rate of 8.65% for the coming five-year period.
