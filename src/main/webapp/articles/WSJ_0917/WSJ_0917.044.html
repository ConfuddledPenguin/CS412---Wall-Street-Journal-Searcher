Newmark & Lewis Profit Falls

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/17/90

HICKSVILLE, N.Y. -- Citing weak sales of weather-related air conditioning products, Newmark & Lewis Inc. reported a 60% decline in net income for the fiscal second quarter ended July 31 to $552,000, or nine cents a share, from $1.4 million, or 22 cents a share, a year earlier. Sales rose 31% to $90.3 million from $69.1 million. The second quarter includes results for the former Brick Church Appliance Inc., a New Jersey-based chain of appliance and electronics stores acquired by Newmark & Lewis in May.

For the six months, the discount appliance and electronics retailer had a net loss of $594,000, or 10 cents a share, compared with net income of $853,000, or 14 cents a share, a year ago. Sales rose 20% to $142.5 million from $118.7 million.

Liquidation of Brick Church's inventories boosted sales but also lowered profit margins, contributing to the decline in net income, a company spokesman said.
