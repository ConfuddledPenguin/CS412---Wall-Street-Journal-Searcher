N.V. Philips's PolyGram

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/17/90

NEW YORK -- PolyGram NV said it will have satisfactory increases in net sales and a "successful" 1990 year. Speaking to the New York Society of Security Analysts, PolyGram officials would not disclose their expectations for 1990 earnings.

David Fine, president and chief executive, told analysts that he expects "very satisfactory increases in net sales" for the year.

Chief Financial Officer Jan Cook said "we look forward to a successful full year for PolyGram."

The Dutch music firm saw its earnings decline in the first half of 1990 as it absorbed the costs of assimilating and integrating Island International Ltd. and A & M Records Inc. Both record companies were acquired by PolyGram in 1989. PolyGram is 80%-owned by N.V. Philips

Fine said the acquisitions will help boost U.S. sales -- an area the company has targeted for much of its future growth. He added the company's dominance in Europe will give it an advantage in exploiting a growing East European music market.
