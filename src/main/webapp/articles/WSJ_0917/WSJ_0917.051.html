Union Camp to Buy Bag Maker

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/17/90

WAYNE, N.J. -- Union Camp Corp., a paper, packaging and chemical concern, said it will acquire all the stock of Chase Packaging Corp., an Old Greenwich, Conn., maker of bags for agricultural and chemical uses and laminated specialty bags, for about $86 million. Under the agreement, Union Camp will pay $330 a common share and $107 a preferred share of Chase Packaging. Holders of Chase Packaging common may choose Union Camp common stock or cash in exchange for their shares. Trading on the pink sheets, Chase's common was quoted Friday at $200 to $225. Francis H. Luddigton, sole trustee of family trusts that together own 55% of Chase Packaging, said the trusts support the transaction and will elect to take Union Camp stock.

In 1989, Chase Packaging earned $6.9 million on sales of $183 million. Union Camp had 1989 profit of $299 million on sales of $2.8 billion.
