REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: Voters Can Add

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/17/90

The smell of a bubbling cauldron is wafting out of the glass cocoon at Andrews Air Force Base. Deprived of any sensory contact with reality, Republican negotiators are ready to lop in the final portion of toadstools and a dose of nightshade. The scents suggest dick Darman will come back with a cut in the capital-gains tax, but Democrats will get to decide how much in revenue this will "cost" (gain). Then Democrats will get a "surcharge" on tax rates for the rich," but also get to say how much revenue this will "raise" (lose). To add insult to injury, Republicans will tell us they didn't "raise rates," but only adopted a "surcharge." Voters will have to figure out for themselves that a 20% surcharge is the same as a boost in the marginal rate, perhaps a substantial one depending on details.

Director Dick and Congressman Newt, please tell us it isn't true.
