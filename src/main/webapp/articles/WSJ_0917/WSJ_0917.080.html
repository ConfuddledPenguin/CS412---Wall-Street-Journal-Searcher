State Health Officials Oppose Clean Harbors' Planned Incinerator

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/17/90

BRAINTREE, Mass. -- Massachusetts health officials recommended against a planned hazardous waste incinerator in a blow to Clean Harbors Inc.'s plans to build a $42 million facility. In a nonbinding opinion, the Department of Public Health said that the plan to build the facility in an industrial area of heavily populated Braintree was unsuitable because of concerns about respiratory problems for residents. The state Secretary of Environmental Affairs is expected to rule on the plant this week.

William Geary, vice president of public affairs for Clean Harbors, said company studies indicated "there wouldn't be any adverse health impact." He said the state health department hasn't set limits establishing an acceptable risk level. He added the company might be able to alter its plan to meet health department worries by limiting either the volume or the types of waste incinerated.

Clean Harbors is a hazardous waste hauler and cleans up oil and toxic waste spills. It operates an incinerator for medical wastes at the Braintree site. The hazardous waste incinerator would be Clean Harbors' first such venture and would be the first hazardous waste facility in Massachusetts, one of the biggest exporters of hazardous wastes.
