International Brief -- Manpower PLC: Employment Group Selling Part of British Operations

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/17/90

Manpower PLC, the Anglo-American employment services group formerly named Blue Arrow, said it is selling a large part of its British operations to an investor group for #106 million ($199.6 million). The assets include the Blue Arrow Personnel Services and Brook Street Bureau employment services units that formed part of the original Blue Arrow group which bought Milwaukee-based Manpower Inc. in 1987.

The investor group behind the purchase includes executive directors of Brook Street as well as Chase Manhattan Bank, Charterhouse Bank Ltd. and Prudential Venture Managers.

Manpower will take a 15% stake in the company formed to make the purchase and will be allowed to put a representative on its board, the company said.

Manpower will receive the payment in cash, though it has promised to reinvest #2 million in variable-rate non-voting preference shares. The exact consideration is subject to an audit of the sold properties.

Mitchell Fromstein, who headed Manpower at the time of Blue Arrow's hostile bid and who last year gained control of the merged companies in a board-room coup, has set about restructuring the debt-burdened company. Along with the name change, he has proposed reincorporating the company in the U.S.

Manpower said the sale reflects its desire to focus its operations in the U.K. temporary help market under a single brand name and eliminate competition among wholly owned units of the company.
