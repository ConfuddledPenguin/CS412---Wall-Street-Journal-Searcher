French Tobacco Prices

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/17/90

PARIS -- France's Finance Ministry, searching for a way to hold down inflation figures, decided not to raise tobacco prices, which were scheduled to increase 3%. The increase was part of a broad anti-smoking campaign under which prices were set to go up another 15% sometime next year.

The tobacco monopoly still will get a wholesale-price boost, but not till Jan. 1. But that day the government also will cut the value-added tax on cigarettes, neatly offsetting the wholesale boost. So consumer prices won't change.
