International: Britain's Inflation Rate Increases

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/17/90

LONDON -- Britain's retail price index in August rose 1% from July and 10.6% from a year earlier, the Central Statistical Office said, citing seasonal food prices, the highest since February 1986. The year-to-year increase, which exceeded market expectations for a 10.3% rise, was the highest since the 11% advance in February 1982, and outstripped the 10.4% gain in March 1982.

The underlying level of inflation, which excludes mortgage payments and community tax charges, was 7.9% in August, compared with 7% in July. Certain mortgage costs and rising fuel costs in Britain also contributed to the August advance in inflation, the agency said. Fuel costs contributed 0.2 percentage point to the month-to-month rise. The end of summer retail sales also contributed to the advance, as retailers returned to higher-priced items.
