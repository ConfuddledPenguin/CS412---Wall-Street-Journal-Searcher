Major Arms Sale to Saudis Recommended by Pentagon

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/17/90

WASHINGTON -- The Pentagon is recommending arms sales to Saudi Arabia that could eventually total as much as $20 billion, a Bush administration official said. The official stressed, however, these military sales essentially are in two distinct categories: those that answer Saudi Arabia's immediate security needs and those that would help safeguard its long-term interests.

The official rejected the notion that this was a single transaction. "It isn't one big package," and he stressed that "the size of some sales will depend on how the situation (in the Persian Gulf) unfolds."

Since Iraq's Aug. 2 seizure of Kuwait, the U.S. has so far agreed to sell Saudi Arabia 24 F-15 jet fighters at a cost of about $2.3 billion. These are expected to come out of U.S. inventory and should be delivered quite quickly, the administration official said.

Future sales to Saudi Arabia are expected to include such items as Apache helicopters, M-1 tanks, Bradley Fighting Vehicles, Stinger anti-aircraft missiles and Patriot air defense systems.

In Jerusalem, Israeli officials reacted with concern to the prospects of a large U.S. arms sale to the Saudis. "We object to this kind of arms deal and find it a threat," said one official. The potential volume, number and sophistication of the weapons under consideration could "affect the qualitative edge" the Israeli forces rely on to deter an Arab attack.
