Letters to the Editor: Pentagon's Zombies Come Back to Life

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/17/90

On your Oct. 18, 1989, editorial page there was a satirical article by former Secretary of the Navy John Lehman, entitled "In the Pentagon, the Undead Walk." In it, Mr. Lehman criticized President Reagan and Secretary of Defense Weinberger for actions they took to better prepare us to respond to Middle East contingencies such as establishing the Rapid Deployment Force and placing stocks of ammunition and equipment on pre-positioned ships in the Indian Ocean. These preparations have been invaluable to our military during the current Iraqi crisis. We could not have responded as rapidly as we did without them. Mr. Lehman made the same mistake as many military leaders in the 1980s. They were so focused on the Soviet Central Europe threat that they failed to perceive the other potential threats to our security. As a result, much of the defense buildup in the past decade is oriented solely to a Soviet threat and not of great value to us in the current situation in the Middle East, such as submarines and nuclear missiles.

Frank H. Johnson

Annapolis, Md.
