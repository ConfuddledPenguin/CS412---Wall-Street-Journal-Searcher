Business Brief -- CSX Corp.: Purchase of Rail Operations Of RF&P Corp. Is Closer

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/17/90

CSX Corp. moved closer to acquiring the rail operations of RF&P Corp. CSX, a Richmond, Va., freight transportation company, agreed to a proposal under which it would acquire RF&P's rail operations in exchange for CSX's 62.7% voting position in RF&P.

Virginia Retirement System, which initiated the transaction, would acquire CSX's remaining shares in RF&P for $35 each. Virginia Retirement System, the second-largest independent RF&P holder, owns about 28% of the combined voting and nonvoting shares.

CSX said the proposed transaction would result in RF&P's retaining all of its real estate while transferring to CSX only its railroad operations in a tax-free transaction. RF&P owns the 113-mile Richmond, Fredericksburg & Potomac Railroad, which links the northern and southern parts of CSX's rail system.

The transaction is subject to approval by RF&P's board, the board of CSX, and Virginia Retirement System trustees.

This past spring, CSX offered to pay one share of its common or $34.50 cash -- a total of $385 million -- for each RF&P share it didn't already own. But CSX withdrew the offer amid opposition from RF&P holders who deemed the bid too low.

---

Corrections & Amplifications

CSX Corp. said the Virginia Retirement System proposed an exchange of a portion of CSX's RF&P Corp. dividend obligations for the stock of an RF&P subsidiary that holds the RF&P railroad. The Virginia Retirement System also proposed that it purchase CSX's remaining RF&P dividend obligations and its RF&P voting shares at $35 a share. In yesterday's edition, terms of the transaction were misstated.

(WSJ Sept. 18, 1990)
