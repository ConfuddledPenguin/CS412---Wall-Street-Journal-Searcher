Marketing & Media: Hearst, Burda to Scrap New Astrology Magazine

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/17/90

NEW YORK -- It was not in the stars. Hearst Corp. and Burda Publications of West Germany said they would fold Jupiter, the "upscale" astrology magazine they jointly published.

Jupiter first appeared on newsstands last December but sales after two issues were disappointing. As is typical of Hearst, most new magazines are launched first on newsstands to see if circulation gradually builds before increasing publishing frequency.

Jack Heistand, vice president of marketing at Hearst Magazines, said Hearst and Burda had spent a "modest amount" on the first two issues, but that Jupiter's "newsstand sales didn't pass the hurdle rate we set for it." He said the astrology magazine would probably not be attempted again.

Burda currently publishes a monthly version of Jupiter in West Germany. Astrology magazines and booklets are among the best-selling publications at supermarket checkout counters.

Hearst continues to test two other magazines on newsstands, American Home and Countryside. However, another new magazine, Auto Times Weekly, has been suspended for one year, because of what Mr. Heistand called "the current soft auto market." Auto Times Weekly was a mass-market, weekly tabloid published jointly by Hearst and Axel Springer Verlag.
