Who's News: Zecher Is Expected To Return Full Time To Post at Chase Unit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/17/90

NEW YORK -- J. Richard Zecher is returning full time to his post as chairman of Chase Investor Management Corp., the money management unit of Chase Manhattan Corp., the bank is expected to announce today. Since late last year, the 50-year-old Mr. Zecher also had been in charge of global risk management activities for the bank.

Mr. Zecher, who holds a doctorate in economics, said he agreed at that time to take on the task of pulling together Chase's various risk management activities in such areas as foreign exchange, commodities and sophisticated hedging instruments for corporate clients, as long as the post wasn't permanent. Risk management involves both trading for the bank's own account as well as devising hedging programs for customers seeking protection from volatility in financial and commodity markets.

"We had those in several different administrative parts of the bank and we wanted them pulled together," said Mr. Zecher. "But I didn't want to do that permanently so I retained my chairmanship at CIMC and I'm returning there full time now. It's a business I helped create and it's doing very well." Chase Investor Management manages approximately $33.5 billion for pension funds and other institutional clients.

Mr. Zecher said Mark B. Grier, 38 years old, currently head of financial management for Chase's retail operations, will succeed him as the bank's global risk management executive. Mr. Grier previously was chief financial officer at Lincoln First Bank in Rochester when Chase acquired that bank.
