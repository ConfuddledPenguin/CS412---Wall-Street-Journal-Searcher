Tax-Rule Change Planned By FASB on Future Credit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/27/90

NEW YORK -- The Financial Accounting Standards Board took steps toward replacing a controversial rule on accounting for income taxes. The proposal would allow companies to book a deferred or future tax credit on current income statements by anticipating future income. Because a company must have earnings to realize a tax benefit in future years, it must expect that it will be profitable enough to recognize the tax benefit on its financial statement in the current year. Therefore, the new rule would largely apply to profitable companies; loss-producing companies would continue to use tax-loss carry-forwards.

Under the existing rule, such current recognition of a future tax benefit can't be recognized, because income anticipation isn't permitted. "Now they are saying you can anticipate future incomeincome that is expected or more likely than not to occur in future years, which would allow you to recognize" a later tax benefit currently, said Ray Simpson, manager of the tax project for the FASB.

The board, based in Norwalk, Conn., directed its staff to develop an "exposure draft," or a new proposal that could eventually lead to a new rule. The staff could issue a new proposal in two months. But it isn't clear how soon after that a new rule might be issued, because a public-comment period is required, after which the board will reconsider the proposal.

Although he said the existing rule doesn't officially take effect until fiscal years beginning after Dec. 15, 1991, Mr. Simpson said some companies are already following it.
