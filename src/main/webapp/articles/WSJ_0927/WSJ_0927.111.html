Business Brief -- Federal Paper Board Co.: Third-Period Profit Fell By 55% to $21.9 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/27/90

Federal Paper Board Co. reported substantially lower fiscal third-quarter earnings, citing problems in a paper mill expansion project. The Montvale, N.J., pulp and paper maker said net income for the 12-week quarter ended Sept. 8 was $21.9 million, or 48 cents a share, down 55% from $49.1 million, or $1.06 a share, in the year-earlier period. Sales were $294.2 million compared with $295.3 million a year before.

For the 36 weeks ended Sept. 8, the company reported net of $95.3 million, or $2.07 a share, down 35% from $147.6 million, or $3.22 a share. Sales rose 4.4% to $928.2 million.

Federal Paper Board said operating earnings were hurt by a series of mechanical difficulties mostly related to the expansion project at its mills. It also noted a 12-cent-a-share non-cash charge for the retirement of assets associated with the expansion project and a decline in market prices for paper pulp.
