Business Brief -- Du Pont Co.: Titanium Dioxide Pigment Plant Being Built in Brazil

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/27/90

Du Pont Co., which has interests in chemicals, fibers and energy, said it is building a plant in Brazil for the grinding and packaging of titanium dioxide pigment, an essential part of paint, plastics, paper and other products. The Wilmington, Del., company declined to disclose the size of the investment. The plant in Uberaba, about 200 miles north of Sao Paolo, is expected to begin operating in late 1991 and to serve the Brazilian market. Du Pont said the plant is being built on a company-owned, 235-acre site with ample space for constructing additional processing units later. Plans call for developing the site into a full-scale manufacturing plant by the mid-1990s.

Du Pont is the world's largest producer of titanium dioxide. Its titanium dioxide plants in the U.S. and Mexico have a combined annual capacity of more than 700,000 metric tons. A metric ton is equal to about 2,204.62 pounds.
