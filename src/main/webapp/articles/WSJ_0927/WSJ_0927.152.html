Business Brief -- MCA Inc.: Firm's Shares Surge $7.25 On News of Takeover Talks

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/27/90

Shares of MCA Inc. surged $7.25 a share to close at $61.25 in New York Stock Exchange composite trading, based on a series of rumors suggesting that MCA's talks to be acquired by Matsushita Electric Industrial Co. were progressing rapidly. However, MCA issued a statement after the close of trading that merely repeated its confirmation on Tuesday that it is talking about a friendly buy-out with a "major international company" which it didn't identify. MCA, based in Universal City, Calif., said it issued the statement after the Big Board asked if there were any corporate developments that would explain the jump in MCA shares.

People familiar with the discussions have said that Tokyo-based Matsushita could pay between $80 and $90 a share for MCA, or a total of $7.4 billion to $8.3 billion.

Several takeover stock traders attributed the surge in MCA shares just before the close to a panic among professional traders who want to own big positions in MCA if a deal is announced soon. "People are trying to make a whole year's profit on this one deal. They're afraid that the train is leaving the station," said one takeover-stock trader.
