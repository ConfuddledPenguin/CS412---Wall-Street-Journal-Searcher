AT&T Offers Access To Private Networks For Midsized Firms

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/27/90

American Telephone & Telegraph Co. is offering midsized companies access to private international voice and data communications networks previously available only to the largest firms. Separately, the US Sprint Communications Co. unit of United Telecommunications Inc., Westwood, Kan., plans to market a service by which customers can link local area networks at many locations for data transmission through a single access facility.

Currently, a company must use a number of leased private lines and a number of access facilities to gain the same capability for LANs, which are groups of PCs that can share files and communicate with each other.

Both telecommunications companies made the announcements at the Tele-Communications Association trade show in San Diego.

AT&T, New York, and five foreign telecommunications companies are linking their capabilities to provide the expanded private-network service, a spokesman said.

A private network allows a company to design its own dialing plan. On such a network, phone calls and data transmissions connect faster because they bypass the local phone-company switches that they normally follow.

Currently, only the biggest companies can afford totally private networks, in which AT&T provides the companies with private lines for unlimited transmissions at all times.

A spokesman explained that AT&T now is tailoring plans for smaller companies to use such private lines only when they need them, so-called virtual private networks. Pricing on these private lines is lower than simply placing a long-distance call through AT&T's regular lines, the spokesman said.
