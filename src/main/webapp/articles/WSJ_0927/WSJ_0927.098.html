Who's News: Taylor Will Retire From BankAmerica After Aiding Reversal

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/27/90

SAN FRANCISCO -- Glenhall Taylor Jr., credited as playing a key role in turning around BankAmerica Corp., said he will retire at the end of the year from his positions as board vice chairman and head of credit policy at the bank. Mr. Taylor, 65 years old, leaves behind more than four decades of banking. He was best known for coming out of retirement in 1985 to turn around BankAmerica's troubled loan portfolio through tough new credit procedures and policies.

Mr. Taylor's plan, which called for identification of problem loans, rigorous review of new credits and successful recoveries of loans previously charged off, brought improvements in the bank's credit portfolio.

Mr. Taylor began his banking career in 1948 with American Trust Co., eventually becoming chairman of the credit policy committee at Wells Fargo & Co., which acquired American. He left Wells Fargo in 1983 to become chief credit officer of BankAmerica's financially troubled Seafirst Bank unit.

Mr. Taylor will be succeeded as head of credit policy by executive vice president Michael E. Rossi, 45, now head of Bank of America's commercial banking division. Following his retirement, Mr. Taylor will continue as a director of both Seafirst Corp. and Seafirst Bank, and as a credit-policy consultant to BankAmerica.
