What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/27/90

THE U.S. WILL SELL five million barrels of oil from the Strategic Petroleum Reserve to rein in surging crude prices. Bush, who until recently had opposed tapping the supply, called the draw-down a "test." The administration intends to consult with Germany and Japan about a coordinated, long-term use of reserves. ---

Bush is prepared to drop his insistence that the capital gains tax rate be cut, according to top GOP leaders, who say he is willing to consider other ways to encourage economic growth.

---

Banks and depositors should bear more of the risk now assumed by the taxpayers, a Treasury official said in outlining proposed changes to the federal deposit insurance system.

---

The SEC wants to force companies to account for assets using more current rather than historical values. The banking industry, the agency's first target, is fighting the change.

---

General Electric hid serious doubts about its design of a nuclear containment vessel, a judge ruled, calling GE's conduct "unconscionable." The decision raises the firm's exposure to potential damages.

---

Orders for durable goods fell 0.8% in August despite a big jump in orders for defense equipment. Personal income and spending, meanwhile, failed to keep pace with inflation.

---

Milken should receive a prison term longer than Boesky's three-year sentence, prosecutors urged.

---

Tokyo stocks plunged Wednesday, in part on rumors of selling by Kuwait's exiled government. The Nikkei fell 1108.70 points, or 4.75%. It lost a further 222.75 Thursday morning.

---

U.S. stock prices tumbled as oil markets rallied on rising expectations of supply disruptions. The Dow Jones industrials fell 25.99 points.

---

Conflict-of-interest rules allegedly violated by Neil Bush as an S&L official don't explicitly bar the ties he had with borrowers, regulators said.

---

Stock offerings plunged in the third quarter to $3.2 billion thus far in the period, 48% less than a year ago.

---

Fidelity Investments is seeking to change the objective of its junk-bond fund to focus on appreciation, not just high yields. Other mutual fund groups are likely to copy the move.

---

BankAmerica apparently won the government's auction of MeraBank, once Arizona's biggest S&L, with a bid thought to exceed $81 million.

---

Arbitragers holding Corroon stock are using an obscure Delaware law to try to derail the company's stock-swap merger with Willis Faber.

---

USAir suspended its dividend and deferred for several years the delivery of 16 new Boeing aircraft.

---

Alan Bond resigned as chairman of Bond Corp. Holdings, apparently to pave the way for a restructuring of the foundering Australian company.

---

Bush moved to begin negotiating a free-trade agreement with Mexico under "fast-track" procedures.
