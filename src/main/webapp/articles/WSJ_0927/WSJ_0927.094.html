Canada-U.S. Fisheries Bilateral Pact Reached

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/27/90

OTTAWA -- Canada and the U.S. have reached an agreement aimed at reducing tensions between U.S. fishermen and Canadian patrol boats. The clashes have occurred mainly in Georges Bank, a big fishing ground in the Gulf of Maine, where some U.S. fishermen who have been caught fishing in Canadian waters have tried to outrun Canadian patrol boats. Disputes also have occurred on the Pacific Coast, where Canadian fishermen have been caught in U.S. waters.

The agreement requires each country to prohibit its fishermen from violating the fisheries laws of the other country.

"Fishermen who violate fisheries laws in the other country will no longer be able to escape enforcement action by crossing back across the border into their own waters," Canadian External Affairs Minister Joe Clark said. The accord also will reduce tensions along boundary areas, he said.
