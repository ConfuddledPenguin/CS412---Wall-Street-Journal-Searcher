Anheuser to Proceed With Project in Spain But With Partners

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/27/90

ST. LOUIS -- Anheuser-Busch Cos. said it decided to proceed with previously announced plans to build a $300 million theme park and resort in Spain, but "only with the equity involvement of international developers or partners." The brewing concern said last year it planned to build the park, on Spain's Costa Brava, on its own. But earlier this year, it said the plans had been placed "on hold," after problems arose with municipal officials in the region. Anheuser said yesterday that "progress has been made toward an agreement" between the company and the two municipalities.

In Madrid, Javier de la Rosa, best known in Spain for his links with the Kuwait Investment Office, said he has offered to lead a group of investors taking an 80% stake in a revised project valued at 40 billion pesetas ($406.1 million). He said through a spokesman that final details haven't been settled, but that the plan is for Anheuser to remain in the project with a 20% stake and act as a technical adviser. An adviser to the Kuwait Investment Office and partner in its Spanish holding company, Grupo Torras S.A., Mr. de la Rosa controls 55% of the Barcelona amusement park known as Tibidabo.

An Anheuser spokesman said: "We haven't signed a definitive partnership agreement with anyone. We have held preliminary discussions with interested parties, and, as a matter of corporate policy, we can't comment on any ongoing discussions with any party."

Anheuser said its total investment to date in the project is $90 million, of which $50 million is in land. To complete the site acquisition, the company said, could require an additional $30 million.

Under the initial plan, Anheuser envisioned spending an additional $275 million in a possible second phase that would begin after the turn of the century.
