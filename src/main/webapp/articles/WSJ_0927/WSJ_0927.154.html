Aid to Afghan Rebels Wins Approval of a House Panel

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/27/90

WASHINGTON -- A House Appropriations subcommittee approved continued covert aid to anti-government forces in Afghanistan but withheld half of the estimated $300 million sought by the Central Intelligence Agency until a vote next spring. The action follows an appeal by CIA Director William Webster at a closed-door meeting Tuesday after the subcommittee's bipartisan leadership recommended that no new money be approved for the prolonged war.

The outcome is a victory for supporters of the guerrillas, but the restrictions reflect new skepticism in the panel, which has been one of the strongest advocates of aid in the past.

The subcommittee's decision came as part of deliberations on an underlying defense and intelligence appropriations bill for the fiscal year beginning Monday. Among major weapons systems, the measure would provide an estimated $350 million in advanced procurement funding for the Navy's 18th Trident submarine, made by General Dynamics Corp.'s Electric Boat division. Still, the funding represents a deep cut from the $1.2 billion authorized by the House.
