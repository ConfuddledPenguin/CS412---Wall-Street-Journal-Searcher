Letters to the Editor: Peck's Golden Voice Defamed Judge Bork

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/27/90

Gregory Peck (Letters, Sept. 12) tries to defend himself against Paul A. Gigot's charge that People for the American Way "paid Gregory Peck to smear Judge Bork." Not so, cries the offended Mr. Peck. "It is not true that I was paid." Well, half a defense is better than none, I suppose, and I for one am quite willing to believe him. Indeed, Mr. Peck has been a tireless supporter of left-wing causes for, by his own account, "more than 40 years." He has been among the most reliable of Hollywood's corps of trained seals, ever ready to spout whatever, from time to time, the left needs to have spouted. And so he was in Judge Bork's case: His charges of racial intolerance, solemnly intoned in that golden voice, were defamatory and false -- a smear by any definition.

Worse, his explanation of why he did it -- what flaws in Judge Bork's makeup justified his assault? -- bears a disquieting resemblance to the intemperate mouthings of Teddy Kennedy, kicking off the smear-Bork campaign.

Moreover, the moral sensibilities that Mr. Peck found lacking in Judge Bork are precisely the kind of thing that can have no proper part in judging, as David Souter recently informed Sen. Kennedy and his colleagues on the Judiciary Committee.

So Mr. Peck adds ignorance to arrogance, which should (but, regrettably, probably won't) detract from his credibility the next time Norman Lear, or some other puppeteer, calls upon him.

Frank O'Connell

Aptos, Calif.
