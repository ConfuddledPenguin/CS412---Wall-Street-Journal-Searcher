Rodamco Share Price Sinks On Policy Change at Fund

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/27/90

BRUSSELS -- Shares of Rodamco N.V., a $4.5 billion international property investment fund, sank 18% to 60 guilders ($33.86) on the Amsterdam Stock Exchange. A plunge in the stock price had been expected, in light of a surprise announcement from the Rotterdam-based company Monday that it was suspending its practice of purchasing its own shares at book value. Trading in Rodamco was suspended Monday and Tuesday, to give investors time to digest the news. On Friday, Rodamco shares closed at 73.30 guilders.

The policy change at Rodamco, one of the major funds in the Robeco Group, means that it is no longer an open-end fund -- one that issues new shares or redeems old ones in line with market demand, keeping the price near the underlying asset value. Shareholders who want to get out of the fund now must accept the prevailing stock price.

Rodamco said an outflow of funds this year, amounting to more than 20% of the total, had forced it to suspend repurchases in the interests of shareholders. Otherwise, it would have had to borrow heavily or sell properties to pay for redemptions.

Robeco Group continues to repurchase shares in its Robeco and Rolinco equity funds, as well as in its Rorento bond fund. A spokesman said it was "very unlikely" that the company would switch the policy for those funds.
