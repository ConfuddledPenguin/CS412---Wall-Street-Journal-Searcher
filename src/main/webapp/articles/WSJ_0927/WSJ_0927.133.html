Trump Shuttle May Go On Sale Again, Lenders Say

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/27/90

NEW YORK -- The Trump Shuttle, which has been on and off the market once this year, may soon be up for sale again. Some of Donald Trump's bankers, holding $245 million in senior Shuttle debt, suggested that Mr. Trump sell the airline rather than lose money operating it, according to people who attended a meeting of Shuttle lenders yesterday.

While the banks do not expect the Shuttle to sell for anything close to what Mr. Trump paid for it, some of them -- including lead lender Citibank -- said in the meeting that it would be better to cut the banks' losses and sell the airline anyway.

Mr. Trump borrowed $380 million to pay for what was the Eastern Shuttle last year. Under last month's $65 million bank bailout, interest on $135 million of Mr. Trump's Shuttle debt was suspended. Last week, Mr. Trump told his bankers that soaring fuel costs and a declining northeast economy would cause him to miss interest payments on the senior $245 million loan, as well.
