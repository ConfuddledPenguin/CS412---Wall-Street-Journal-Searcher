T/SF Expects to Post A Slight Loss for 1990, Contrary to Forecasts

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/27/90

TULSA, Okla. -- T/SF Communications Corp. said it expects to report a slight loss for 1990, based on losses in the first six months and expectations that net income for the second half will be the same as or only slightly better than a year earlier. The communications company reported losses of $436,000 on revenue of $22.6 million for the first six months of this year. For all of 1989, the company reported net income of $325,000 on revenue of $74.3 million.

Separately, the company said its board authorized the repurchase of up to 500,000 shares of its common stock in a "Dutch auction." Under the plan, shareholders could offer to sell their stock back to the company at a price specified by the tendering shareholder, subject to the company's agreement to the offered price.

The company said its projections contradict those of some analysts who had forecast better earnings for the second half of this year.

In a statement, Chairman G. Douglas Fox attributed the expected results to continuing problems in the commercial printing division of the company's shopper-newspaper operation in New Jersey.

"Continued softening of the economy in the Northeast is contributing to our current problems in New Jersey and New York," Mr. Fox said. "Our daily newspaper, information services and trade journal divisions are doing well and will likely complete the year with substantial improvement from 1989."
