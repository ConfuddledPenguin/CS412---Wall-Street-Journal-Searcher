International Brief -- MAN AG: Pact Set to Sell KM Stake Of 76.91% to SMI of Italy

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/27/90

MAN AG of West Germany, an engineering, machinery and vehicles concern, said it agreed to sell its 76.91% controlling stake in KM -- Kabelmetal AG, a major producer of copper products, to Societa Mettallurgica Italia S.p.A., or SMI, of Italy for 551.7 million marks ($350.9 million). SMI, a holding company controlled by the Italian industrialist Luigi Orlando, is Europe's largest copper producer with 20% of the market. MAN said it will receive 265.70 marks for each of its KM shares.

The remaining 23.09% of KM is held by private investors. KM shares are listed on the Frankfurt bourse and closed Wednesday at 211 marks. In the year ended June 30, KM earned eight marks a share on sales of 2.6 billion marks.

An SMI spokesman said KM would remain a separate entity and operate next to SMI's Europa Metalli-LMI S.p.A. nonferrous metals unit. SMI doesn't plan to buy out KM's minority shareholders, he said. "It will remain a listed company."

The sale is to be completed Nov. 2.
