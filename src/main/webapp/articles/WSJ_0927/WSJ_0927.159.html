Business Brief -- Avery Inc.: Merger Accord Completed With Holders, Top Officers

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/27/90

Avery Inc. said it completed a merger agreement with its two controlling shareholders and top officers under which they will acquire the shares they don't already own for about $4 million. The two are Nelson Peltz and Peter May, who own 83% of Avery. Avery said the two will acquire the remaining 9.5 million shares at 43 cents a share.

Avery said its directors approved the merger agreement following the recommendation of a special committee appointed to evaluate the proposal.

In over-the-counter trading, Avery shares were quoted yesterday at a bid price of 31 cents a share.

Avery started out as a coal company in 1984. In 1986, it bought Uniroyal Chemical Holding Co. from Uniroyal Inc. for $710 million and financed the acquisition through $910 million of high-yield, high-risk junk bonds. It then sold Uniroyal Chemical to a management-led group last year in a transaction valued at $800 million.

Avery, which continues to operate a coal company, said last November that after fees and repayment of debt the company was left with about $24 million in cash and securities from the Uniroyal sale.

When the company completed the sale of Uniroyal Chemical last year it also completed a recapitalization in which Messrs. Peltz and May surrendered warrants and preferred stock in exchange for a larger stake in Avery's common shares.
