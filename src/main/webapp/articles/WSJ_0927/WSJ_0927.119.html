Study Says Shoreham Plant Could Be Gas-Fired Facility

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/27/90

NEW YORK -- The abandoned Shoreham nuclear power plant on Long Island could profitably be converted to a natural-gas facility, according to a study for the state agency that will take over the facility. The plant, which was built by Long Island Lighting Co. but mothballed when state officials refused to give necessary clearances, is scheduled to be sold by Lilco for $1 to the Long Island Power Authority. The study concludes that rather than let the plant sit and crumble, it could be converted to a gas-fired generating facility.

Richard M. Kessel, chairman of the power authority, said a final decision on converting Shoreham could be reached by next year. According to the study, Shoreham could be converted to a nonnuclear unit for less than it would cost to build a new plant at a new site.

Depending on a number of factors, the study said it would cost about $715 million to convert Shoreham to a 1,351-megawatt natural gas-fired facility. The power authority would have various financing options, ranging from doing the conversion itself to farming it out.

After costing some $5.2 billion to build, the Shoreham plant was forced to close because local opposition prevented Lilco from meeting emergency standards for evacuation procedures. Such procedures for the area were necessary in the event of a nuclear disaster.
