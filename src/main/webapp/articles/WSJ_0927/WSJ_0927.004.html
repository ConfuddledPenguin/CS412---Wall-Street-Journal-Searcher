Letters to the Editor: Your `Cuisinart' Hashed My Facts

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/27/90

Your Sept. 20 editorial "A City Flounders" about my mass-transit proposal does to facts what Cuisinart does to onions, starting with the notion that I still chair the House Budget Committee. I don't -- not since 1988. You think my provision is designed just to "support Philadelphia's transit system." Actually, Pennsylvania has 38 transit authorities. All would benefit, which is why the idea has drawn bipartisan support all around the state.

You imply the provision is a response to Philadelphia's fiscal woes. Wrong. For five years -- long before Philadelphia's current crisis -- I've been urging Pennsylvania and other states to establish a dedicated funding source for mass transit. Every other state with a major mass-transit system already has done so. That's why Secretary of Transportation Samuel Skinner has called dedicated state revenue an essential part of transit funding -- and blasted Pennsylvania for lagging behind.

Would my provision be a "pre-emption of state tax policy by Congress?" Absolutely not. It purposely leaves the state to decide how to come up with dedicated revenue. It doesn't mandate a tax increase. Pennsylvania could make a multiyear commitment from general revenue -- as New York has done -- or use an existing fee such as vehicle registration.

Actually, with dedicated revenue, transit authorities would be much better off even if Pennsylvania didn't give transit another nickel. They could then issue bonds against what would be a guaranteed income stream and leverage their funding resources up to 10 times. That's a move your readers should understand -- even if it's too complicated for your editorial writers.

Finally, let's look at the wildest misstatement of all: that this provision constitutes "blackmail . . . liberalism's ultimate urban policy."

What hypocrisy. Did you cry blackmail when Republicans threatened to withhold federal funds to influence the District of Columbia's abortion policy? What about the provision in this same transportation spending bill that withholds highway funds unless states suspend the licenses of convicted drug offenders?

I'm aware that editorials are opinion pieces -- but I wish we could get back to the days when opinions were based on fact.

Rep. William H. Gray III (D., Pa.)

Majority Whip

Washington
