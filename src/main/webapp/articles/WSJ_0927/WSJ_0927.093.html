Business Brief -- Tecumseh Products Co.: Third-Period Loss Expected Of as Much as $16 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/27/90

Tecumseh Products Co. said it will have a third-quarter loss of about $14 million to $16 million, after taking a $19.2 million charge for an environmental cleanup near a plant. The Tecumseh, Mich., maker of compressors, engines and pumps said the per-share loss will be about $2.50 to $3.

The charge, which comes to $3.51 a share, goes to clean up PCB contamination in the Sheboygan River near a Tecumseh die-casting plant. The plant used PCB, a suspected carcinogen, as a fire retardant in the 1950s and 1960s.

Tecumseh had net income of $27 million, or $4.94 a share, in the first half of 1990.
