Business Brief -- Dana Corp.: Firm Takes Charge to Raise Loan-Loss Reserves of Unit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/27/90

Dana Corp. of Toledo, Ohio, said it was taking a $10 million charge to increase the loan-loss reserves of its Diamond Savings & Loan subsidiary. The action will decrease Dana's earnings for the first nine months by about 12%, the company said. That indicates that the company expected to earn roughly $83 million for the first nine months before the charge and now expects to earn about $73 million. For the first three quarters of 1989, Dana earned $111.4 million, or $2.73 a share.

Despite the charge, Dana Chairman Southwood J. Morcott said he was "pleased with our operating performance given these turbulent times."

In addition, Dana said it might have to inject up to $33 million in additional capital over the next year into Diamond to cause it to exceed all of the increased regulatory capital requirements. This action would have no effect on Dana's profits, said Robert Richter, Dana's controller. Mr. Richter noted that Diamond could take several other steps to meet the capital requirements, and that the injection of capital was a last resort.
