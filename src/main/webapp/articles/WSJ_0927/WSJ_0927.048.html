London `Fox' Exchange, Baltic Futures Plan Merger

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/27/90

LONDON -- The tiny Baltic Futures Exchange plans to merge with the London Futures and Options Exchange to regenerate slack business. Volume on the Baltic Futures Exchange, which trades grain, potatoes, freight futures and other commodities, is less than a tenth of the four million to five million contracts traded on what is informally called the London "Fox."

London's commodity markets are the largest in Europe, but are small when compared with those in New York and Chicago.

"By merging with London Fox we will be able to develop our existing contracts and expand into new areas," said Pat Elmer, chairman of the Baltic Futures Exchange. London Fox actively trades coffee, cocoa and sugar and has launched several contracts on an automated trading system.

Saxon Tate, chairman of London Fox, said the Baltic contracts have "great potential." If members of both exchanges approve the merger, the markets will have greater resources for future development, he said.

London Fox plans to launch an Arabica coffee futures contract to trade alongside futures on the lower-quality robusta coffee. It is also examining the possibility of contracts in property and electricity futures, and is considering links with Japanese futures markets, the exchange said.
