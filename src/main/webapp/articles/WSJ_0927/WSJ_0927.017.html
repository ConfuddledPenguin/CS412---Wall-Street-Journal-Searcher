Dividend News: D&N Financial to Cut 3rd-Quarter Dividend To Strengthen Capital

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/27/90

HANCOCK, Mich. -- D&N Financial Corp. said it is reducing its third-quarter dividend to 7.5 cents a share from 15 cents to strenthen its capital position. The dividend will be paid Oct. 25 to stock of record Oct. 9.

"The reduction in the dividend was made to strengthen our capital position," said D&N's chairman, Kenneth D. Seaton. "Our board of directors believes it is prudent and good banking practice to conserve capital at this time in recognition of the uncertain commercial real estate market and associated risks that we, as well as other financial institutions, are facing."

In the first half, D&N earned $2.8 million, or 76 cents a share, up from $1.2 million, or 33 cents a share, a year earlier.

In other action at D&N Financial's regular board meeting, the corporation announced changes to the boards of the corporation and its subsidiary, D&N Savings Bank FSB. R. Daniel Musser's recent resignation from the savings bank board "due to other commitments" was accepted, and George Schwartz and Richard Nelson were appointed to the D&N Financial board. The staggered terms of the D&N Financial board were adjusted and the term of director Joseph Bromley will now expire at the 1991 annual meeting.

D&N Financial has financial services offices throughout Michigan, as well as mortgage banking operations in Arizona, Illinois, Michigan, North Carolina and Texas.
