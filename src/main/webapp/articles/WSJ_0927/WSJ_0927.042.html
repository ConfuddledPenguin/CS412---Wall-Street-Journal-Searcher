Canadian Auto Workers Set Strike Deadline for GM

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/27/90

TORONTO -- The Canadian Auto Workers union set a strike deadline of midnight EDT on Oct. 10 to reach a new contract covering about 36,000 employees of the Canadian subsidiary of General Motors Corp. Contract negotiations got under way yesterday, three days after union members ratified a new three-year pact with Ford Motor Co.'s Canadian unit, ending a nine-day strike.

CAW president Robert White said he expects GM to accept about the same economic terms as those agreed upon with Ford. That pact gives workers wage gains of 3% in the first year and 2% in the second and third years, plus protection against inflation. The Ford contract also includes more time off the job, a vacation bonus and an income security package that would take effect in the event of layoffs.

Frederick Curd, GM Canada's chief negotiator, called the CAW's demands "expensive." He said, however, that GM could accept the union's terms if the contract also provided a "framework for improvements in {the company's} operational efficiencies."

Mr. Curd said he hasn't yet tabled specific proposals to improve GM's "cost competitiveness" in Canada. But he identified certain areas where the company would like to make progress. These include changing the union's system of job classifications to create a more flexible work force. He also said he would ask the union to help address attendance problems at GM's Canadian plants, where absenteeism runs about 4% higher than at U.S. plants.

The CAW's Mr. White said that, besides monetary issues, the CAW would press for resolution of matters affecting individual local union affiliates.
