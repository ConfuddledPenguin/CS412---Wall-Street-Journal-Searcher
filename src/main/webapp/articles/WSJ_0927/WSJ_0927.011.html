Consumer Confidence Was 7-Year Low in September

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/27/90

NEW YORK -- Consumer confidence, which fell sharply in August after the Iraqi invasion of Kuwait, remained at a seven-year low in September. The Conference Board said its consumer confidence index remained at 84.7 in September, the same level as the revised August figure. The index had registered 101.7 in July. It was in the 115-117 range in the first quarter.

"With little or no progress in the Kuwait crisis, the consumer's continued uncertainty is perhaps to be expected," said Fabian Linden, executive director of the board's consumer research center. Mr. Linden added that consumers are "increasingly concerned that inflation and interest rates will be rising. Concerned consumers are cautious spenders."

In the latest monthly survey conducted for the board by National Family Opinion Inc. of Toledo, Ohio, consumers expressed slightly less confidence about current business conditions and job availability. On the other hand, expectations for six months ahead showed consumers expect slightly better business conditions and employment prospects. The survey uses 1985 as a base of 100 and covers 5,000 households throughout the U.S.

The survey found 7.3% expect to buy an automobile within six months, down from 7.5% in August; and 2.9% expect to buy a home in the next six months, down from 3% last month.
