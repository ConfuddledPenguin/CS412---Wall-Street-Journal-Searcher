Business Brief -- Warner-Lambert Co.: Discount Accord Is Reached With California on 4 Drugs

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/27/90

Warner-Lambert Co. said it signed a three-year contract with the state of California to provide four additional prescription drugs at discounts to Medi-Cal patients. Warner-Lambert's action follows similar moves by Merck & Co. and other drug makers in response to state and federal pressure to cut health-care costs.

Pharmaceutical companies previously had been offering discounts on brand-name drugs only to big-volume purchasers, such as hospitals. But budget deficits and rising prescription-drug prices have led legislators to look for ways to control costs of state and federal health-care programs. That has put pressure on drug manufacturers to cut prices, in order to keep their drugs on the state and federally approved lists.

Warner-Lambert, based in Morris Plains, N.J., said the agreement with California covers four drugs made by its Parke-Davis unit. They are lipid-regulating Lopid, oral contraceptives Loestrin and Norlestrin and anti-anginal Nitrostat. The company declined to disclose terms of the agreement.
