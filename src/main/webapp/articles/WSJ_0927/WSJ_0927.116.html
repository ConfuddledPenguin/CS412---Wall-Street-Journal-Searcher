International Brief -- Thomson-CSF S.A.: Firm's Consolidated Profit Dropped 29% in First Half

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/27/90

The French arms and electronics group Thomson CSF reported that its consolidated profit after payments to minority interests fell 29% in the first half of 1990 to 1.07 billion francs ($203 million) from 1.5 billion a year earlier. A finance official of the state-controlled group said that one of the main elements behind the drop was a lower contribution from minority-held units, essentially in the finance sector. She said these operations generated only 525 million francs of profit in the first half, down from their unusually strong year-earlier contribution of 825 million francs.

On an operating level, the group's main majority-held units generated a 19% rise in consolidated revenue to 17.36 billion francs from 14.54 billion.
