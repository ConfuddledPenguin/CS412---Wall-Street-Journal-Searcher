REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: Full of Beans

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/27/90

Left-wing gunslingers in El Salvador have found a new ally to help wreck the country's already precarious economy. On the agenda awaiting Procter & Gamble shareholders in Cincinnati on Oct. 9 is a proposal to stop the company from buying Salvadoran coffee beans for its Folger's unit. James Gamble, an heir to the P&G fortune, blames Salvadoran landowners for the continuing strife, and in alliance with a few family members and another shareholder, has asked that the company slam the producers with a boycott. The company itself has let it be known that it does not wish to depart from U.S. government policies. While Mr. Gamble can no doubt afford to savor a pricier brew as he ponders his activist agenda, we doubt he will be toasted by small Salvadoran farmers and workers who would be hurt by the boycott.
