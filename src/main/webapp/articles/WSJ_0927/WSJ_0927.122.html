Business Brief -- ASK Computer Systems Inc.: Lennane, Florida Investor, To Seek Control of Board

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/27/90

James P. Lennane, a Naples, Fla., investor, said in a Sept. 26 letter sent to ASK Computer Systems Inc.'s chairman that he intends to seek control of ASK's board. In a filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission, Mr. Lennane said he has filed preliminary proxy materials with the SEC to be in a position to seek control through a solicitation of proxies.

An ASK spokeswoman said the Mountain View, Calif., company was examining the materials and had no comment.

In the letter, Mr. Lennane also criticized the board for failure to submit for holder approval the issuance of shares representing almost 42% of ASK's common stock outstanding to finance, in part, ASK's acquisition of Ingres Corp.

According to the filing, Mr. Lennane has also begun legal proceedings in the Chancery Court of Delaware seeking to enjoin the completion of the issuance of shares and the acquisition, pending its submission to, and approval by, a vote of the ASK shareholders. Mr. Lennane now holds 9.67% of ASK's common outstanding.
