Business Brief -- UnionFed Financial Corp.: Company to Restate Results For 1990 and Report Loss

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/27/90

UnionFed Financial Corp. said it will restate its fiscal year 1990 results and report "a significant loss" compared with previously reported profit of $11.1 million, or $1.60 a share, in the year ended June 30. The restatement is due mainly to "substantial additional provisions for possible losses on loans and real estate" made "in connection with current examinations" of Union Federal Savings Bank by the Office of Thrift Supervision and the Federal Deposit Insurance Corp., the company said. Union Federal Savings, the principal subsidiary of Brea, Calif.-based UnionFed, has 27 branches in Southern California.

Additional loss reserves are needed because of a "further deterioration in real estate markets and a significantly more conservative approach to valuations of real estate and real estate secured loans . . . based in part on more conservative federal regulatory valuation estimates and assumptions," UnionFed said in a brief statement issued Tuesday night.

Company executives were in meetings yesterday and weren't available to discuss the expected size of the loss or to answer any other questions.

UnionFed shares lost almost half their market value yesterday, skidding $2.25 to $2.50 in New York Stock Exchange composite trading. The shares have plummeted from a high of $19.50 this year.
