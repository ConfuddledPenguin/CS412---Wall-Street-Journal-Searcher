Holders of Thrifts' Debt Get Good News From RTC

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 09/27/90

The Resolution Trust Corp. has good news for holders of certain securities secured by now-bankrupt thrifts: Interest on bonds backed by letters of credit redeemed early by the RTC will be paid up to the time the securities are redeemed. The RTC has the power to repudiate bonds issued or secured by now-insolvent thrifts, and redeem or "call" them early. When this happens, the RTC says it will prepay "an amount equal to the outstanding principal amount or accreted value of the secured obligations."

Last April the RTC adopted a policy of paying interest up to the time the bonds were repudiated, and not just up to the date that the thrift was taken over. But it left unclear whether it would do the same for bonds secured by "collateralized letters of credit" issued by the busted thrifts. Because of this, holders of those securities worried about the possibility of losing months' worth of interest if the bonds were called.

About $840 million of collaterized letters of credit were issued by thrifts currently in conservatorship, estimates Standard & Poor's Corp. In most cases, the letters of credit were issued by thrifts to secure tax-exempt bonds issued by local housing agencies and sold to individual investors, says S&P's Francis Parisi.
