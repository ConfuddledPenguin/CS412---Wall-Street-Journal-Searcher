Business Brief -- Justin Industries Inc.: Acquisition of Tony Lama For $18 Million Completed

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/27/90

Justin Industries Inc., a Fort Worth, Texas, maker of Western boots, bricks and building materials, said it completed its acquisition of Tony Lama Co. for $9 a share, or $18 million, with 97% of the El Paso, Texas, boot maker's stock tendered. Holders of the remaining Lama stock will receive $9 a share. In national over-the-counter trading yesterday, Lama shares closed at $8.50, down 25 cents.

The deal, in which Justin also assumed $34 million of Lama debt, is a setback for dissident holders seeking to acquire Justin. The dissident group had sued, seeking, among other things, to block the Lama deal, but a U.S. judge in Fort Worth denied the request Monday. The group Tuesday launched an $18-a-share tender offer for 6.2 million Justin shares, to boost its stake to 78% from about 12%. Justin shares ended unchanged at $15 in national OTC trading. The group said its offer would have been $20 a share but for the Lama acquisition, which they contend lowers Justin's value. Lama had a 1989 loss of $1.8 million, or 95 cents a share, on sales of $75.6 million.
