Business Brief -- Hexcel Corp.: Third-Period Net Will Fall Below Estimates, Firm Says

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/27/90

Hexcel Corp., citing cutbacks in orders from the military, said it expects to report lower-than-anticipated third-quarter net income. The Dublin, Calif., maker of composite materials said it expects to be only "marginally profitable" in the quarter ending Sunday, with net income far lower than analysts' minimum estimates of about $1 million, or 15 cents a share. Net in the year-ago period was $1.6 million, or 24 cents a share, on sales of $97 million.

Hexcel blamed the revised outlook on military cutbacks, poor profit margins and difficulties in starting its manufacture of aspartame, the artificial sweetener. European business was also slugish, Hexcel said.

The company's shares closed at $8.25 a share yesterday, down 62.5 cents, on the New York Stock Exchange.
