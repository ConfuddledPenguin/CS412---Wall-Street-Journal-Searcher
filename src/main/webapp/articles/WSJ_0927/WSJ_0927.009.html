NCNB Will Post Much Lower Net; Loan Woes Cited

  • Author: Michael J. McCarthy
  • Date: 09/27/90

NCNB Corp., struggling with weaker commercial real estate markets in the Southeast, said it will increase its allowance for loan losses and report sharply lower third-quarter earnings. The Charlotte, N.C., bank holding company said it expects to boost its allowance for loan losses by $65 million, to about $590 million. With net loan charge-offs of about $75 million to $85 million, the provision for loan losses in the third quarter should be about $145 million. That compares with an $84 million provision in the second quarter.

Including the weightier provision, third-quarter earnings will plummet to $55 million to $65 million, or 50 cents to 60 cents a share, from the year-earlier $143.6 million, or $1.45 a share.

Analysts were surprised by the size of the provision. "People have been expecting some kind of softness, but the magnitude has thrown them," said Henry Coffey, a bank analyst at J.C. Bradford. Although NCNB will report earnings at about half of what he projected, Mr. Coffey said that, amid a difficult real estate environment, the company is "still profitable, the dividend is safe, and the capital structure is in good shape."

The third-quarter earnings would be even worse, conceded an NCNB spokesman, if not for the strong health of the company's Texas operations.

NCNB disclosed the news after the market closed. In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange yesterday, NCNB closed at $23.25, down $2.125.

Several southeastern banks will be reporting lower earnings in the third quarter, said Richard Stillinger, an analyst at Keefe Bruyette. "The Southeast, by virtue of having one of the stronger growth rates, is more vulnerable to a pullback than, say, the Midwest."

Hugh McColl, NCNB chairman, said the increased loan-loss provision "reflects our awareness of weakening economic conditions that have affected not only the real estate markets, but lending activities in general."

NCNB said its allowance for loan losses at Sept. 30 will total $585 million to $600 million, or 1.6% to 1.65% of total loans. That compares with an allowance of $525 million, or 1.46% of loans, at the end of the second quarter.
