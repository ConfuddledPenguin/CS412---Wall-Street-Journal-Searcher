Business Brief -- Thomson McKinnon Inc.: New Bid for Firm's Interest In Partnership Is Weighed

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/27/90

Thomson McKinnon Inc., which is operating under bankruptcy-law protection, said it is considering another bid for its interest in an asset-managing partnership. New York-based Thomson, parent of Thomson McKinnon Securities Inc., said it was granted an adjournment by a U.S. Bankruptcy Court to consider a new bid of $33 million by Manufacturers Life Insurance Co., Toronto, for Thomson Advisory Group L.P., formerly Thomson McKinnon Asset Management L.P., and its affiliated distributor. The partnership manages nine mutual funds and is an investment adviser to private accounts; it had more than $7 billion of assets under management as of June 30. The court hearing had been called to consider an earlier bid for the interest by Thomson Advisory Group Inc., formed by a Thomson management group.

Thomson McKinnon Securities sold its branch office system last year, and has been liquidating since July 1989. It and the parent are both protected from creditors' lawsuits under Chapter 11 of the U.S. Bankruptcy Code.
