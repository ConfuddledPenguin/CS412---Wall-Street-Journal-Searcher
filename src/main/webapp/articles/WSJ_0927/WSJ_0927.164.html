Marketing & Media: Times Mirror Joins Newsweek to Offer Media Ad Package

  • Author: Patrick M. Reilly
  • Date: 09/27/90

NEW YORK -- In one of the largest partnerships between two separate media companies, Newsweek magazine and Times Mirror Magazines have joined to create a multimagazine, multimedia advertising package. By offering the package, to be known as the Ad-Vantage program, Newsweek, owned by Washington Post Co., and Times Mirror Magazines, a unit of Times Mirror Co., are taking direct aim at competition from packages offered by such multimedia companies as Time Warner Inc. While national advertisers have largely slashed their ad budgets, such packages try to lure more dollars from advertisers with discounts offering access to video, print and promotions.

Ad-Vantage received generally good reactions from advertising agency media directors, who said it offers an attractive, heavily male audience, and the ability to buy time on network TV affiliates and cable systems owned by both Washington Post Co. and Times Mirror. Advertisers would be able to create special ad supplements in the magazines and arrange special events -- such as in-store promotions or sponsorship of sporting events.

Times Mirror publishes a number of magazines with heavy male readership, such as Field & Stream, Outdoor Life, Golf Magazine, Popular Science and The Sporting News.

Newsweek is expected to benefit the most from the plan. For years it has had to compete with Time magazine, whose parent, Time Warner, can offer attractive multi-magazine ad packages. "This makes Newsweek stronger," says Peter Gardiner, senior vice president at DDB Needham Worldwide. "Their weakness was being a sole publication that couldn't offer tie-ins to other titles."

But Michael Drexler, national media director of Bozell agency, questioned the success of a package targeted so heavily to male readers. "It's a brilliant scheme, but I don't think anyone will jump at it right away," he said.

The timing of the Newsweek-Times Mirror announcement was crucial: In the coming weeks, ad agencies are planning how and where they will spend millions in clients' ad dollars in 1991.

The partnership underscores the cutthroat nature of magazines' advertising sales as the industry works its way through an advertising slump. Partnerships are rare among consumer magazines, since they compete so closely for advertisers' dollars and readers' time.
