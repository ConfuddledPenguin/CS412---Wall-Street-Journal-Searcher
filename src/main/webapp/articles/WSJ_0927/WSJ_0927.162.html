BankAmerica Appears to Win Bid for MeraBank

  • Author: Richard B. Schmitt
  • Date: 09/27/90

SAN FRANCISCO -- BankAmerica Corp., continuing to dance on the grave of the troubled thrift industry, has apparently won the bidding for MeraBank, once Arizona's largest savings and loan association. The acquisition from the federal Resolution Trust Corp., BankAmerica's second of a major, insolvent Arizona S&L this year, would catapult the bank holding company into one of the state's largest financial institutions within less than four months.

Terms of the transaction weren't known, but analysts believe BankAmerica paid slightly more than the $81 million it paid in May for Western Savings & Loan Association, a Phoenix-based thrift that is somewhat smaller than cross-town rival MeraBank.

Analysts figured in both instances that BankAmerica paid an amount roughly equal to 3% to 4% of so-called core retail deposits. By contrast, BankAmerica earlier this month paid a hefty premium of 6.85% of deposits to acquire Benjamin Franklin Savings & Loan Association, based in Portland, Ore.

Spokesmen for BankAmerica and the Resolution Trust Corp. declined comment on the transaction. The RTC took control of MeraBank in January and recently vowed to sell it by the end of the third quarter. A BankAmerica official said he understood that the company had been informed that it was the successful bidder for the thrift. And Security Pacific Corp., considered BankAmerica's main rival in the sale, is understood to have been told by regulators that its bid had been rejected.

"There is a strong feeling that there is a window of opportunity" to acquire market share on the cheap through purchasing failed thrifts, said the BankAmerica official. "The appetite for further acquisitions is very strong."

MeraBank has 66 branches and deposits totaling roughly $4.7 billion. Western Savings had 61 branches and deposits of about $3.5 billion. Only Valley National Corp. and First Interstate Bancorp would have larger Arizona operations than BankAmerica, state banking officials estimated.

A subsidiary of Phoenix-based Pinnacle West Capital Corp. until its January seizure, MeraBank rolled up huge losses in commercial real-estate. MeraBank's collapse, including losses from an expansion into Texas, operations of which aren't believed to be part of the sale to BankAmerica, is expected to cost the government at least $1.5 billion.

Indeed, the sale to BankAmerica, and other recent sales of thrifts in the state to banks, marks the end of an era. "For all practical purposes, the S&L industry in Arizona has been eliminated," said Hank Rivoir, the Arizona banking superintendent. "I personally am happy to see it."
