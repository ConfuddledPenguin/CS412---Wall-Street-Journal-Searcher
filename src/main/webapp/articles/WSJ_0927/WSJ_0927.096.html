Rockwell to Work On U.S. Space Project With Westinghouse

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/27/90

WASHINGTON -- Rockwell International Corp. and Westinghouse Electric Corp. said they agreed to work together on the development of a nuclear thermal propulsion system for use in the government's Space Exploration Initiative. The SEI is a plan for manned and unmanned lunar and Mars missions. A nuclear thermal propulsion system would use the heat from a nuclear fission reactor to raise the temperature of a gas propellant, increasing the speed of a rocket.

The two companies said they plan to define and pursue technologies that will lead to the evaluation and application of a nuclear thermal propulsion system.

Rockwell applies advanced technology for products in the electronics, aerospace and automotive and graphics businesses. Westinghouse, based in Pittsburgh, is a diversified technology company with interests in the nuclear power industry.
