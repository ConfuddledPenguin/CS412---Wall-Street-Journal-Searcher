Marketing & Media: Murdoch Says News Corp. Must Reduce Its Debt Load

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 09/27/90

SYDNEY, Australia -- News Corp.'s short-term borrowings soared 34% in the year ended June 30 and Rupert Murdoch, chief executive officer, has conceded that his international media concern must trim the debt load. The annual report shows that interest-bearing debt jumped to 10.53 billion Australian dollars (US$8.77 billion) in fiscal 1990 from A$7.87 billion a year earlier. Short-term debt (due within one year) soared to A$2.95 billion from A$508.7 million. Longer-term borrowings rose to A$7.58 billion from A$7.36 billion. News Corp. also has A$1.41 billion in preference shares issued through units; including these securities, debt totals A$11.94 billion.

In a speech to investors in New York on Monday, Mr. Murdoch, who controls 45% of News Corp.'s shares, said the company has "too much short-term debt" and is examining ways to refinance the borrowings. Mainly, though, "We plan to get our debt down with earnings from our assets" in the next few years, he said.

The company has been selling some assets in an effort to damp the rise in debt. But it also is investing billions of dollars in color printing presses for newspapers in Britain and Australia, the Sky Television satellite-TV operation in Britain, and the Fox Broadcasting Co. TV-programming service in the U.S.
