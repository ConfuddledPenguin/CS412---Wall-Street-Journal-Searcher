MCorp Pension Plan

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/27/90

DALLAS -- MCorp said it intends to terminate its pension plan, which covers about 7,000 current and former employees. The bank holding company, reorganizing under Chapter 11 of the U.S. Bankruptcy Code, said the pension plan has assets of about $80 million, or about $10 million more than current obligations.

Termination of the pension plan is subject to approval of the Pension Benefit Guaranty Corp. and the Internal Revenue Service. If the termination is approved, MCorp expects to begin making distributions in the second half of 1991, a company spokesman said.
