Judge Won't Force Greyhound to Give Data to Union Group

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/27/90

McALLEN, Texas -- A federal bankruptcy judge refused to force Greyhound Lines Inc. to turn over financial data to a potential buy-out group that includes the striking Amalgamated Transit Union. Judge Richard Schmidt also extended to Nov. 19 from Oct. 2 the time during which Greyhound has the exclusive right to present a reorganization plan to creditors.

The union was seeking to force the bus company to open its books to Blackstone Group, a New York investment banking firm that has joined the union in an effort to buy the company, as a condition of the extension. Blackstone Group had told the union that it needed the financial data to proceed with its plans. Shelby Jordan, who represents the union, acknowledged in court that the judge's decision could "really freeze" Blackstone's efforts.

But Judge Schmidt said, "I don't think that that's a proper way to do that." He pointed out that Blackstone or any other potential suitor could obtain financial information when Greyhound files its disclosure statements and reorganization plan in November.

In another setback for the union, major bondholders in the case supported Greyhound's stand against making the disclosures to Blackstone. Both the union and the company have been engaged in aggressive efforts to obtain bondholders' support. Moreover, the bondholders succeeded in getting three seats on the unsecured creditors' committee, which had previously consisted of union representatives and trade creditors. Lawyers involved in the case said that could dilute union influence on the committee.

Mr. Jordan, the ATU lawyer, said the union intended to file additional motions asking the judge to release the financial information to Blackstone.
