Marketing & Media: U.S. Newsprint Use Fell 0.3% in March

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/26/90

WASHINGTON -- U.S. consumption of newsprint in March decreased 0.3% from a year earlier, according to the American Newspaper Publishers Association. The industry group said preliminary estimates indicate consumption by all users totaled 1,044,161 metric tons last month, compared with 1,047,011 tons in March 1989. A metric ton is equal to about 2,204.62 pounds.

U.S. daily newspapers used 782,554 metric tons last month, down 0.7% from 787,735 tons a year earlier.

For the first three months of this year, estimated U.S. newsprint consumption totaled 2,945,517 metric tons, up 0.4% from 2,932,421 tons a year earlier.

Newsprint inventories at newspapers in the association's statistical sample averaged a 37-day supply last month, compared with a 41-day supply in March 1989. Newsprint stocks in North American mills last month decreased about 5.6% from a year earlier.

The association said Canadian newsprint mills operated at 95% of capacity in March and at 94% in the first quarter; U.S. mills operated at 94.6% in the month and 97.2% in the quarter.
