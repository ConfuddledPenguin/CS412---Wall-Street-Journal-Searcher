Marketing Brief -- McGraw-Hill Inc.: Firm Weighs Sale of Stake In Venture With Macmillan

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/26/90

McGraw-Hill Inc. said it is considering the sale of a 15%-to-30% stake in Macmillan/McGraw-Hill School Publishing Co., its school publishing joint venture with Macmillan Inc. Joseph L. Dionne, chairman and chief executive officer of the New York-based publishing and information services company, declined to elaborate on the timing and the exact size of the offering. The joint-venture partner, Macmillan, is the New York-based publishing unit of Maxwell Communication Corp., of London.

McGraw-Hill said that the joint venture, formed last year, posted a first-quarter loss. But McGraw-Hill's share of the loss -- $12.8 million -- was "actually less than expected."

The joint venture company's revenue for the six months ended Dec. 31 was $286.4 million. McGraw-Hill's share of the operating profit was $17.8 million.
