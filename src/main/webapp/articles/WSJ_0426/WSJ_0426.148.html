Bristol-Myers Squibb Sees Per-Share Profit Surpassing $3.25 in '90

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/26/90

NEW YORK -- Bristol-Myers Squibb Co. said it agrees with analysts' estimates that per-share 1990 profit will be between $3.25 and $3.30, adding that earnings dilution from its merger last year should end by late 1991 or 1992. In 1989, the company earned $747 million, or $1.43 a share -- including a $693 million charge related to the merger last October of Bristol-Myers and Squibb -- on sales of $9.2 billion. Last week, the giant pharmaceutical and health care-products company posted a 19% rise in first-quarter earnings, to $409.1 million, or 78 cents a share.

Richard L. Gelb, chairman and chief executive officer, said that although there are many areas where the two companies have been able to combine operations, "Bristol-Myers and Squibb didn't merge in order to save money. Nevertheless, by the end of next year, we expect to have achieved savings sufficient to make up in full for the dilution. From that point on, former Bristol-Myers shares will have the same earning power as before the merger, with the incremental earning power of Squibb added."

The company's statement was apparently issued because of investment community interest about how the merger is proceeding.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading, Bristol-Myers closed at $52.875 a share, up 25 cents.
