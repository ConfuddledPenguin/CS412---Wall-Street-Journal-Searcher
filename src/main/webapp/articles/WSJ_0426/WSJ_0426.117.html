Business Brief -- Pegasus Broadcasting Inc.: Closely Held Illinois Firm To Be Bought by GE Unit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/26/90

GE Capital Corp., a financial services unit of General Electric Co., Fairfield, Conn., said it agreed to acquire Pegasus Broadcasting Inc., a closely held communications concern based in Northbrook, Ill. Terms weren't disclosed. GE Capital said it has owned preferred stock and warrants in Pegasus since 1986. Neither GE Capital nor Pegasus would comment on why GE Capital is taking an equity position in Pegasus.

Pegasus, which said it has annual sales of about $65 million, consists of three independent television stations in San Juan, Puerto Rico; Augusta, Ga.; and Sacramento, Calif.; and two radio stations in Helena, Mont. Under the agreement, GE Capital will acquire the TV stations and Pegasus shareholders will retain ownership of the two radio stations.

The transaction is subject to regulatory approvals, and is expected to close by July. At the closing, a GE Capital spokeswoman said, the company plans to provide more details about its interest in Pegasus.
