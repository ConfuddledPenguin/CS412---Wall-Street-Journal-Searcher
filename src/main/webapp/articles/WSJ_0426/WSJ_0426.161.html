`Distressed' Market Value Estimated at $200 Billion

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/26/90

LOS ANGELES -- A new study estimates the total market value of bankrupt and distressed securities was about $200 billion at the end of January. Of that amount, about $13 billion was publicly traded, according to a survey of 56 mostly private vulture funds by Edward I. Altman, finance professor at New York University's Stern School of Business.

The results of the survey, sponsored by Foothill Group, a Los Angeles financial-services firm, were released at an investment conference here. Foothill manages about $130 million of distressed securities in two private limited partnerships.

All together, the investment firms included in the survey manage about $5 billion of distressed securities. They target about a 30% total return, Prof. Altman said in an interview.

Whereas junk-bond buyers primarily look for high yields, "the distressed-securities investor is primarily interested in the upside price potential," he said.

Although the distressed securities in the survey had a market value of $200 billion, Prof. Altman put their par value at about $300 billion.
