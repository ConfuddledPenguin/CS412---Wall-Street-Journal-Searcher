Viking Freight's Ex-Chairman Settles Lawsuit With SEC

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/26/90

NEW YORK -- The Securities and Exchange Commission said that Richard Bangham, former chairman of Viking Freight Inc., settled a civil suit involving alleged insider trading. In the settlement, Mr. Bangham agreed to pay a penalty of $117,625. In agreeing to the settlement, he neither admitted nor denied the allegations.

The SEC suit, filed in November, alleged that Mr. Bangham, as Viking chairman, learned on July 1, 1988, that Roadway Services Inc. was interested in acquiring the company. The suit alleged that Mr. Bangham passed that information to Kenneth Liroff, a friend and business associate. The SEC charged Mr. Liroff, of Livingston, N.J., with using that information to purchase Viking common shares.

Mr. Liroff has denied the allegations. The SEC's civil charges against him are pending in federal court in Manhattan.

Viking and Roadway announced Roadway's tender offer for the company on July 18, 1988. After the announcement, Viking's stock price climbed 60% above the previous day's closing price. The penalty to be paid by Mr. Bangham, of the British West Indies, equals the profit that the SEC alleges Mr. Liroff made on the transactions.
