Exxon Corp. Will Begin To Sell `Cleaner' Gasoline

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/26/90

HOUSTON -- Exxon Corp. said it will begin selling on the East and Gulf coasts reformulated gasoline designed to improve air quality. The company said the fuel should be available in more than 40 major markets by summer. Exxon expects the new gasoline to account for more than 50% of the its sales in those markets.

Exxon is following major rival Shell Oil Co., which earlier this month introduced a reformulated gasoline, and several smaller refiners.

While Exxon said it will sell its new gasoline in both premium and midgrade unleaded, it shunned the "cleaner" formulation for the most popular and least costly grade, regular unleaded. Shell's new fuel is available only in its most expensive and most profitable octane grade, its premium unleaded.

The new gasolines are lower in volatility and specifically targeted to reduce hydrocarbon emissions in the summer when ozone problems are most severe.
