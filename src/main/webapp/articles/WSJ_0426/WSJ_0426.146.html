Spiegel's Profit Rose 23% in First Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/26/90

OAK BROOK, Ill. -- Spiegel Inc. said first-quarter profit rose 23% on sales that increased 20%. The catalog company, which is outpacing some of its rivals in a difficult retailing environment, said net income for the quarter was $7.8 million, or 15 cents a share, compared with $6.3 million, or 12 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue rose to $389.9 million from $325.2 million.

John J. Shea, Spiegel's president and chief executive officer, said the company is gaining market share by adding new customers in all of its units. "We believe the remainder of 1990 holds great potential," he said.

Spiegel, whose majority shareholder is the Otto family of West Germany, sells apparel and household furnishings through its Spiegel catalogs. It also operates Eddie Bauer and Honeybee stores and catalogs.
