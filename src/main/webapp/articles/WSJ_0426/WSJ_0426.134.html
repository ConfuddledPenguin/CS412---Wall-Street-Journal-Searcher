Business Brief -- Bass PLC: Holiday Inns Unit to Cut 600 Posts in Streamlining

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/26/90

Holiday Inns Inc. said it will dismiss about 600 employees, as part of an effort to streamline the lodging company, based in Memphis, Tenn. Holiday, a unit of Bass PLC of Britain, said it will drop about 260 people from its hotel-services division; 210 from its Holiday Clubs operation, which will include the disbanding of the sales force; 80 from its international operations; and 50 from its headquarters staff. The company said the moves were prompted by findings from an organizational review conducted since February. Holiday has 40,000 employees.

Holiday said it intends to focus solely on its hotel operations. As part of its restructuring, the hotel business in the U.S., previously run as one operation, will be split between company-managed hotels and a franchise system.

The company also announced the resignation of Kenneth B. Hamlet as president and chief executive officer of the Holiday Inn Hotel division. He will remain a consultant to the company.
