New Securities Issues

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/26/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATES

Morgan Stanley Group Inc. -- $200 million of 9 7/8% notes, due May 1, 1995, was priced at 99 7/8 to yield 9.907%. The size of the issue was increased from $150 million. The noncallable notes were priced at a spread of 0.95 percentage point above the Treasury's five-year note. Rated single-A-1 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and single-A-Plus by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Morgan Stanley & Co.

MUNICIPALS

New Jersey Health Care Facilities Finance Authority -- $55.2 million of hospital revenue bonds, Kimball Medical Center, due July 1, 1998, and 2013, were priced by a Bear, Stearns & Co. group. The bonds were priced to yield from 8.13% in 1998 to 8.47% in 2013. There are $12.1 million of 8% term bonds priced at 99.25 to yield 8.13% in 1998. There are $38.8 million of 8% term bonds priced at 95 1/4 to yield 8.47% in 2013. There are no current interest serial bonds. The bonds are rated Baa by Moody's and triple-B-minus by S&P.

MORTGAGES

Federal National Mortgage Association -- $300 million of real estate mortgage investment conduit securities is being offered in 11 classes by Smith Barney, Harris Upham & Co. The offering, Series 1990-57, is backed by Fannie Mae 9% securities with an underlying weighted average coupon rate of 9.70% and weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29.5 years. Details on yields weren't immediately available. Also $250 million of Remic securities is being offered in 16 classes by Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette Securities Corp. The issue, Fannie Mae's Series 1990-56, is backed by Fannie Mae 9% securities. Details of the pricing weren't immediately available.

ASSET-BACKED

Citicorp -- $250 million of certificates backed by credit-card loans with a coupon rate of 9.15% at 99.676 through an underwriting group led by Shearson Lehman Hutton Inc. The yield on the securities, calculated on a monthly basis, is 9.231%. But the bond-equivalent yield on the securities is 9.41%. The issue pays interest monthly. The Citicorp credit-card securities, sold through a special trust, Standard Credit-Card Trust 1990-4, were priced at a yield spread of 0.45 percentage point above the five-year Treasury note, based on the bond-equivalent yield. The issue has been structured so investors get all their principal returned on May 10, 1995. The securities are being targeted to retail investors, not institutional buyers, who are the standard purchasers of credit-card securities. There is no guarantee from Citicorp on the offering. The issue is rated triple-A by Moody's and S&P based on the quality of the underlying Visa and MasterCard credit-card loans and a subordinated class of securities that totals $31 million. The size of the subordinated securities represents 12.4% of the senior securities. The subordinated securities, which absorb losses on the senior issue, were placed privately with a rate of 10.10% at 99.971 to yield 10.11%.

OTHER

Exxon Corp. Employee Stock Ownership Plan -- $220 million of Exxon-guaranteed debt securities previously issued by Exxon's ESOP was remarketed by J.P. Morgan Securities Inc. The notes were remarketed for a seven-year, fixed-rate period with a coupon set at 7.589%, which resulted in a taxable equivalent cost of about 0.98 percentage point over the seven-year Treasury note yield on the day the rates were set. J.P. Morgan Securities, adviser to the ESOP, used a proprietary structure developed with NCNB Corp. in arranging the remarketing of the securities to an investor. The remarketed notes, which are part of a $1 billion series of notes originally issued by the ESOP in 1989, have been held until now by investors on a short-term floating-rate basis.

Province of New Brunswick -- $200 million of 9 3/4% debentures, due May 15, 2020, was priced at 98.85 to yield 9.87%. The noncallable debentures were priced at a spread of 0.90 percentage point above the Treasury's bellwether 30-year bond. Rated single-A-1 by Moody's and single-A-plus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Salomon Brothers Inc.

EUROBONDS

World Bank (agency) -- 750 million marks ($441.5 million) of 8 3/4% bonds due April 19, 2000, priced at 99 7/8, via Deutsche Bank. Fungible beginning May 11 with a 500 million mark issue launched March 23.

Societe Nationale des Chemins de Fer Francais (France) -- 1.25 billion French francs ($219.5 million) of 9 1/4% bonds due April 12, 1997, priced at 98.30 to yield 9.98% less fees, via Credit Commercial de France. Fungible with an outstanding 750 million franc bond. Guaranteed by the Republic of France. Fees 1 7/8.

Asian Development Bank (agency) -- 20 billion yen ($125.8 million) of 7 1/4% securities due May 17, 2000, priced at 101 3/4 to yield 7.25% less full fees via IBJ International Ltd. Fees 1 3/4.

General Electric Capital Corp. (U.S.) -- 20 billion yen of 7 5/8% bonds due May 22, 1993, priced at 101 3/8, to yield 7 5/8% less full fees via DKB International Ltd. Fees 1 3/8.

Canadian Imperial Bank of Commerce, (London branch) (Canadian parent) -- 100 million Canadian dollars (US$86.2 million) of 14% bonds due June 7, 1993, priced at 101.95 to yield 13.75% less fees, via Wood Gundy. Fees 1 3/8.
