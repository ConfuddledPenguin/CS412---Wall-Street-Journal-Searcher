Investment in East Germany

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/26/90

HAMBURG, West Germany -- East Germany plans to permit 100% foreign ownership of businesses, the Hamburg-based newspaper Bild quoted Gerhard Pohl, the economics minister of the formerly Communist country, as saying. Mr. Pohl said East Germany will repeal recent laws that restrict foreign ownership in joint ventures to 49%, Bild reported. Many foreign companies have shied away from investments in East Germany because of restrictions limiting investors to a minority share.

Mr. Pohl said new laws will be passed "very soon," Bild reported, but he added that East Germans will receive preferential treatment in investments.
