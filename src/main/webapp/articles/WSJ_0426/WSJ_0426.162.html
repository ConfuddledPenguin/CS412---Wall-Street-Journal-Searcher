Franklin Savings Unit Halts Municipal Trades

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 04/26/90

NEW YORK -- Less than a year after resuming operations in the municipal bond business, L.F. Rothschild & Co. has halted trading in the market and may consider leaving the tax-exempt sector altogether. Joel Miller, the firm's chief executive officer, said Rothschild has reduced its trading positions "to the bare bone" and will decide within two weeks whether to exit the business permanently.

"The markets have been sloppy. It has not been profitable," said Mr. Miller. He said the firm's decision to leave the municipal bond market is unrelated to the financial woes of holding company L.F. Rothschild Holdings or of its owner, Franklin Savings Association.

Last November, the broker-dealer re-established its presence in the securities industry, resuming municipal operations and dealings in investment banking, mergers and acquisition and retail brokerage.

The firm had halted those operations in late 1987 after Rothschild Holdings fell victim to huge losses as a result of the October 1987 stock market crash.

Last July, Rothschild Holdings filed for bankruptcy-court protection. Since then, Franklin Savings has been seized by the federal government. The unit L.F. Rothschild & Co. wasn't included in the holding company's Chapter 11 filing.
