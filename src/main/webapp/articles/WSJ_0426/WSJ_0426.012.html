International: Swedish Financier to Sell Saab Stake to Wallenberg

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 04/26/90

STOCKHOLM -- Financier Sven-Olof Johansson will sell his stake in Saab-Scania AB to the Wallenberg group for 4.4 billion kronor ($717 million), according to news reports, thus ending a power struggle that began last spring. The sale will result in Wallenberg controlling Saab, with 58% of the voting shares.

Mr. Johansson, who started to comb the market for Saab shares last spring and early autumn, became its biggest owner, currently holding 19% of the group's shares and 22% of its votes.

The deal was completed a few days before Saab's annual meeting, to be held tomorrow, the business newspaper Dagens Industri said yesterday.

It said the Wallenberg group will have an option to buy Johansson's 12.8 million shares for 350 kronor (57 cents) a share. Mr. Johansson, who declined to comment, has previously said he paid an average of 250 kronor a share. His gain on the deal is estimated at 1.2 billion kronor.

Saab-Scania's departing chairman, Sten Gustafsson, confirmed the Johansson sale, according to the Direkt news agency.

Mr. Johansson criticized Saab last autumn when its shares tumbled 34 kronor over two weeks after rumors of heavy losses in its car division, for not informing its shareholders of the seriousness of the crisis.

Since General Motors Corp. acquired half of Saab's car division last December, the Swedish auto maker's shares have regained ground, however, and closed at 296 kronor Tuesday.
