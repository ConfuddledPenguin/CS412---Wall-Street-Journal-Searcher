Business Brief -- West Point-Pepperell Inc.: Auction of Fabrics Division Dropped Due to Low Bids

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/26/90

West Point-Pepperell Inc., West Point, Ga., said it has pulled its apparel fabrics division off the auction block because of low preliminary bids. In its annual report filed with the Securities and Exchange Commission, the textile concern said it had solicited offers for the division -- its most profitable behind household fabrics -- but stopped pending an improvement in the financial markets and its parent company's financial position.

West Point Acquisition Corp., the company set up by William F. Farley to purchase West Point-Pepperell last year for about $1.56 billion, is in dire straits. Earlier this month, West Point Acquisition defaulted on $796 million in bank debt and on interest payments on $705 million in subordinated debt.

The company reiterated in the filing that it is negotiating with its bankers, led by Wells Fargo & Co. and Bankers Trust Co., and has made an exchange offer to its bondholders in an effort to restructure debt incurred in the acquisition.

Last month, the company got only $410 million in cash and notes for its Cluett, Peabody & Co. unit, after projecting it would fetch more than $600 million.
