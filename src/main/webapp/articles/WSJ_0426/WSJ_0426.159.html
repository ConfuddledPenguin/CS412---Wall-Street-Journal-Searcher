Technology: FDA Approves Kit to Test For Rare Strain of AIDS

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/26/90

WASHINGTON -- The Food and Drug Administration approved the first test kit for HIV-2, a comparatively rare strain of the acquired immune deficiency syndrome virus. The test kits will be used by blood banks. Because only 17 cases of HIV-2 infection have been reported in the U.S. until now, the FDA won't require blood banks to test for HIV-2, as they are required to do for the more common AIDS strain, HIV-1.

But the American plasma industry will probably begin screening for HIV-2 to meet import requirements in Europe, according to Steve Bryant, a spokesman for Genetics Systems Corp. of Seattle, which makes the test.

Genetics Systems is a unit of Sanofi of France. Sanofi purchased the company from Bristol-Myers Squibb Co. a week ago.
