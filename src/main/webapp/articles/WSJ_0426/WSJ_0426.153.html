GE Chairman Expects `Another Good Year'

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/26/90

ERIE, Pa. -- General Electric Co. expects "another good year" in 1990, Chairman John F. Welch Jr. told the annual meeting. But he added that "the first year of the decade will be the toughest one in the past eight, as several of our businesses contend with a softening U.S. economy." He said results this year will be aided by "especially powerful performances from aircraft engines, power generation, medical systems and financial services."

Although most of the meeting was dominated by discussion of a resolution asking the company to justify to shareholders its continued production of nuclear materials for the defense industry, the resolution was opposed by almost 94% of votes cast. GE operates facilities for the Energy Department that make nuclear material for weapons systems.

Mr. Welch said the nuclear material business meets the nation's defense requirements. A strong defense policy has brought about the recent changes, and business opportunities, in Eastern Europe, he said.

GE also announced major contracts.

Arrendadora International of Mexico, a leasing company, ordered 100 GE locomotives, which will be leased to Mexico's national railroad. Mr. Welch called the $168 million order GE's largest export locomotive order in recent years. Fifty units will be delivered late this year and the balance in early 1991.

GE's environmental systems business received a $60 million contract to supply emission-control systems for Texas Utilities Electric Co. The units are scheduled for commercial operation in 1995 and 1996.
