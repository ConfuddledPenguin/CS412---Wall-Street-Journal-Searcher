Dayton Hudson Plans Shift to Minneapolis Of Field's Operations

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/26/90

CHICAGO -- Dayton Hudson Corp. told employees of Marshall Field's that it plans to consolidate "the majority" of the chain's central office operations into its own department-store operation in Minneapolis. Dayton Hudson, which last week agreed to buy Field's from B.A.T Industries PLC for $1.04 billion, had been expected to combine some operations once the acquisition is completed.

In meetings with Field's employees yesterday and in letters distributed to the staff, Dayton Hudson executives said they would maintain a regional office in Chicago to focus on "customer service and community involvement." The company said other central office functions, such as merchandise buying, would be handled through its department store unit in Minneapolis.

"We know the merchandising functions will get consolidated right away -- after closing," a spokeswoman said. "The rest of it will take place over a period going into fall, probably October."

The spokeswoman said she didn't know how many of Field's 1,700 central office staffers will lose their jobs, although some will be offered positions in Minneapolis. Field's employees at its warehouses and its 24 stores aren't affected by the consolidation.

Still uncertain is the fate of Philip B. Miller, the chief executive officer of Field's who led an unsuccessful bid for the chain. Mr. Miller, who is widely credited with turning around the chain, isn't expected to stay, because he would likely lose his autonomy. Spokeswomen for Dayton Hudson and Field's would say only that discussions are continuing between Mr. Miller and the new owner.
