AMR Unit Focuses Upon Amenities In Expansion Push --- Shrimp Cocktail, Lobster, Sheepskin Seats Beckon International Customers

  • Author: Bridget O'Brian
  • Date: 04/26/90

FORT WORTH, Texas -- American Airlines, looking beyond U.S. borders for growth, unveiled its latest weapons for doing battle in the international skies: service, seats and food. With where to fly, the ordering of aircraft and the planning of expanded airport space taken care of long ago, the AMR Corp. unit is focusing on hardware and amenities as it continues an ambitious $11 billion international expansion effort inaugurated in 1983.

In presentations here and in Chicago, its two main international hubs, American trotted out for reporters and travel agents some of the plush features reserved for overseas customers -- many already in use.

Included are wider first class and business class seats swathed in sheepskin and capable of reclining nearly all the way to horizontal for sleeping. The seats use foam rubber softer than that in domestic coach seats with an especially soft headrest that acts like a pillow. The airline also ordered up larger blankets and plumper standard pillows. By summer, first class seats will also get individual video cassette recorders and electrically driven foot rests.

The new seats are already installed in all but three of the carrier's 36 international aircraft -- the rest will be finished within two months.

American also served up samples of its luxury food service started earlier this year: shrimp cocktail followed by lobster fajita (for first class) or Chateaubriand (for business class). Dessert was a chocolate Marquis mousse topped with shaved chocolate. The airline has hired a wine consultant to plan menus and has bought a year's worth of some wines.

By moving into international markets, American is taking on Swissair, British Airways and Cathay Pacific, carriers known for their luxurious service.

"Our ambition is to become known as the U.S. flag carrier. We intend to compete with the best international carriers in the world," said George Mueller, vice president of customer service.

As part of its growth plan, American has ordered $7.7 billion in international jets and committed to spend as much as $655 million to expand some of its terminals. It is spending an additional $3.4 billion developing special international services, hiring multilingual flight attendants and extra airport staffs.

The airline last year began flights to Belgium, Switzerland, Spain and France and stepped up its expansion this year, buying Eastern Airlines' Latin American route system, Continental Airlines' Miami-London route and Trans World Airlines Inc.'s Chicago-London routes. Federal approval of those sales are pending.
