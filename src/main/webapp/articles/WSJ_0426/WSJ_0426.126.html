Business Brief -- Newmont Mining Corp.: Company and Gold Unit Post Sharply Higher Profits

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/26/90

Newmont Mining Corp. and its 90.1%-owned Newmont Gold Corp. reported sharply higher first-quarter earnings, largely due to increased gold prices and lower production costs. Newmont Mining, based in Denver, reported net income rose sixfold to $57.4 million, or 85 cents a share, from $9.4 million, or 14 cents a share, a year earlier. Sales rose to $165.5 million from $111.3 million.

Profit from continuing operations was $42.7 million, or 63 cents a share, compared with a loss of $2.7 million, or four cents a share, a year earlier. In the latest quarter, continuing operations included a pretax gain of $16.4 million from a sale of shares in Newmont Australia Ltd.

Profit from discontinued operations was $14.7 million, or 22 cents a share, compared with $12 million, or 18 cents a share, a year earlier. Newmont restated its earnings to reflect its 55% interest in Peabody Holding Co. as discontinued operations. Newmont has agreed to sell its Peabody investment to Britain's Hanson PLC.

The company said it realized an average of $414 an ounce for its gold sales, including a gain from a gold-loan payment, up from $405 an ounce a year earlier.

Newmont Gold reported net of $42.5 million, or 41 cents a share, compared with $16.5 million, or 16 cents a share, a year earlier. Sales rose to $160.4 million from $108.2 million.
