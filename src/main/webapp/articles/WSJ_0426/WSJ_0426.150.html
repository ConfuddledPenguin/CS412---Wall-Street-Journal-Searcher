First Federal Savings' Holders Request Talks Be Held to Sell Firm

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/26/90

EAST HARTFORD, Conn. -- First Federal Savings & Loan Association said its shareholders voted to request that directors initiate discussions with parties interested in acquiring the company. The proposal was brought before the annual meeting by the Baupost Group of Cambridge, Mass. First Federal has $420 million in assets and nine offices.

James D. Shelton, president and chief executive officer of First Federal, said, "The board will undertake efforts to initiate the shareholders' petition and to continue to investigate other ways of maximizing shareholder value."

"Our earnings, asset quality and capital position are well above Connecticut regional and national averages which, we believe, make the institution a very valuable investment whether it remains independent or is sold," Mr. Shelton said.
