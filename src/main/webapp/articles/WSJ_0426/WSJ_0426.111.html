International Brief -- Laura Ashley Holdings PLC: Retailer Has Pretax Loss Of $7.5 Million for Year

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/26/90

Laura Ashley Holdings PLC, a British retailing company, posted a pretax loss of #4.6 million ($7.5 million) for the year ended Jan. 27, citing declining fabric and wallpaper sales in Britain and disappointing garment sales in the U.S. The deficit, compared with year-earlier profit of #20.3 million, came despite an 18% rise in sales to #296.6 million from #252.4 million.

After taxes but before extraordinary items, the company reported a loss of #6.7 million, compared with year-earlier profit of #13.1 million. Laura Ashley had a loss of 3.38 pence a share, compared with earnings of 6.57 pence a share in fiscal 1988.

With the disappointing results and #100 million in debt, directors decided not to recommend a final dividend and said they are "unlikely to declare an interim dividend during the current year." Last year, Laura Ashley issued a final dividend of 1.50 pence. The company said it is starting a cost-cutting and streamlining program that "might include the disposal of certain businesses or assets, the reduction of stock and the restriction of new shop openings."
