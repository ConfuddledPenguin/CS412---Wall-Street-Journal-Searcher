Swiss Capital Exports Surge

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/26/90

ZURICH, Switzerland -- Swiss capital exports in the first quarter surged 68% to 15.4 billion Swiss francs ($10.34 billion) from a year earlier, the Swiss National Bank, the central bank, said in a preliminary report. The bulk of the growth in capital exports was accounted for by a big increase in the volume of straight bonds, which more than doubled, the central bank said. Overall, exports of capital through bonds grew 79%, while loans increased 24%.

The central bank attributed the increase in straight bonds -- as opposed to convertible bonds or those with warrants issued in Switzerland, mostly for Japanese borrowers -- to the sharp drop on the Tokyo Stock Exchange. Straight bond issues as a percentage of total bond issues increased to 53% from 29%.

Capital exports accounted for by Japanese borrowers slipped to 40% from 50%, the central bank said.
