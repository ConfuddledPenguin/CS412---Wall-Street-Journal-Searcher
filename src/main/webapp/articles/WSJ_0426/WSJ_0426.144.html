American Steel Gets Backing for Financing

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/26/90

CUYAHOGA HEIGHTS, Ohio -- American Steel & Wire Corp. said it received letters of commitment for $95 million of financing. The steel rod and wire maker said the money will be used to pay for capital spending, working capital, refinancing debt and for paying a possible special cash dividend of as much as $4.25 a share. The company said its board will weigh the special payout, once it completes financing documentation.

Financing will come from Security Pacific Business Credit Inc., Sanwa Business Credit Corp., State Bank of South Australia and Cigna Investments Inc.

American Steel & Wire is composed of three former USX Corp. plants acquired in 1986 through a leveraged buy-out. The company went public two years later and now is 50.7%-owned by Chicago West Pullman Corp., a Cincinnati-based transportation and manufacturing concern controlled by Roger W. Ach II, a businessman.
