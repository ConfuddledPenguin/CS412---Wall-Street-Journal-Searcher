Measurex Says Profit In Its Second Quarter Will Drop About 60%

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/26/90

CUPERTINO, Calif. -- Measurex Corp., citing a decline in sales, said fiscal second-quarter earnings will be about 60% lower than a year ago; the company said it is trimming expenses to bolster earnings. The maker of computer-integrated manufacturing systems had net income of $10.2 million, or 54 cents a share, on revenue of $72 million, in the 1989 second quarter. The current second quarter ends June 3.

In trading yesterday on the New York Stock Exchange, Measurex closed at $20.50, down $1.625.

Measurex said last month that it expected results to be down in the quarter, but in a statement yesterday, the company indicated that the results would be "lower than anticipated."

The anticipated drop in revenue and earnings is due to lower system sales and reduced gross margin percentages related to lower volume and one-time costs for consolidating certain operations, the company said. Even though first-quarter European orders were at a record level, the company said its delivery lead times were longer than expected for many of its large, integrated measurement and control systems.
