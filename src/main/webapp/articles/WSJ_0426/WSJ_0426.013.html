International: Tuntex to Put $10 Billion In California Development

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/26/90

SAN FRANCISCO -- Tuntex Group, a Taiwan conglomerate, said it planned to invest $10 billion to develop a new community on a 1,000-acre site it purchased south of here. A spokeswoman for Tuntex Properties Inc. USA, based in San Jose, Calif., said the company is interviewing architects and planning consultants to determine the best use of the Brisbane, Calif., property, which was bought in January from Southern Pacific Railroad for $108 million. She said discussions have centered on a mixed-use development that would include residential, commercial, retail and recreational sites.

She also said that the company is conducting an environmental impact study on the property, of which only 500 acres is on dry land. The dry acreage is landfill, the spokeswoman said, and California law mandates that before landfill can be developed it must undergo rigorous tests for pollutants, among other things.
