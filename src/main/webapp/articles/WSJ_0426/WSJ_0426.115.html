Business Brief -- Tucson Electric Power Co.: Accord Is Reached to Settle Lawsuit by Southwest Gas

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/26/90

Tucson Electric Power Co. said it agreed in principle to pay $25 million to Southwest Gas Corp. to settle a 1983 lawsuit. In a filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission, Tucson Electric, a troubled utility, said the settlement calls for the company to pay the money to Southwest Gas in four installments.

The first payment of $10 million is due before July 1. Tucson Electric said it agreed to make three other payments of $5 million each, plus 10% simple annual interest, on July 1 for the next three years.

A Tucson Electric spokesman said the company set aside $25 million in reserves late last year in anticipation of the settlement. He added that it may seek to recover the money from its insurance carriers.

The settlement stems from a 1983 suit in which Southwest Gas, based in Las Vegas, Nev., claimed that Tucson Electric breached certain representations or warranties in connection with gas-distribution facilities it sold Southwest Gas in 1979 for $39 million.
