Business Brief -- Consolidated Freightways Inc.: Emery Unit Deficit Blamed For Firm's 1st-Period Loss

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/26/90

Consolidated Freightways Inc., Menlo Park, Calif., continuing to suffer deficits from its Emery Worldwide unit, said it had a loss of $37.1 million, or $1.06 a share, in the first quarter. A year-earlier it earned $32.5 million, or 86 cents a share. The transportation concern said revenue climbed 37% to $997.7 million from $726.1 million. The 1989 quarter was restated, the company said, because of an accounting change that added $11.3 million.

The company said its Emery unit had an operating loss of $69.5 million on revenue of $317.8 million.

The firm acquired the unit last year for $230 million and the assumption of $240 million in debt. Emery consistently has posted losses because of either bad weather, high fuel costs or both. The subsidiary also has been hurt by the increasing competitiveness of the air freight industry.
