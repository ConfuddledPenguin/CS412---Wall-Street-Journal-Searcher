Japan's Imports in Sector Rise

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/26/90

TOKYO -- Japan imported agricultural, forestry and fishery products valued at $51 billion in 1989, up 7.9% from 1988 and exceeding $50 billion for the first time, the government said, citing rising grain prices and the yen's strength for most of last year that made imports easier. Imports from the U.S., which has been asking Japan to open up its market for more agricultural products, rose 9.1% to $16.3 billion.

Overall imports of grain and other agricultural goods last year increased 8.3% to $29.6 billion. Those of wheat from the U.S., where the 1988 drought pushed up prices, rose 15%, while corn showed an 8.8% rise. Imports of U.S. beef climbed 28%. The Japanese government plans to lift restrictions on beef imports in 1991.

Imports of forestry products, a subject of bilateral trade talks with the U.S., jumped 21% from 1988 to $10.8 billion, topping $10 billion for the first time.

Despite an increase in volume, though, fishery product imports, at $10.5 billion, registered a 3.5% drop because of falling prices of some fish.
