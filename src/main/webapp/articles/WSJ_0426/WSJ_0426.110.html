Marketing & Media: Times Mirror, Knight-Ridder to Combine National Ad Sales Arms Into New Firm

  • Author: Rhonda L. Rundle
  • Date: 04/26/90

Two of the nation's biggest newspaper chains plan to combine their national advertising sales arms into a single jointly owned company in the wake of slumping ad revenues. Times Mirror Co. and Knight-Ridder Inc. said their new company, to be called Newspapers First, will enable national advertisers to buy space in about 40 newspapers from a single source. The newspapers reach about 20% of all adult newspaper readers in the U.S., the companies said.

The merger is also a way to make the newspaper companies' advertising-sales functions more efficient in response to the decline in recent years in the number of national accounts. "There's a smaller pool of customers because so many companies have merged, or been bought out," said a Knight-Ridder spokeswoman.

Some analysts predicted that the merger will result in the loss of jobs of some sales people in the companies' existing organizations. "Instead of having two salesmen call on every national account they'll have one," predicted John Morton, a Washington-based media analyst with Lynch, Jones & Ryan.

Mr. Morton said that many national advertisers "are attracted to deals where they can cover a lot of markets with one buy and one bill." While it wasn't clear how the combination would affect advertising rates, Mr. Morton said he expected that "costs per thousand" readers would be lower.

Company officials, who were attending an industry conference yesterday, couldn't be reached for comment. Spokeswomen for both companies said they weren't aware of any layoff plans.

In a joint statement, the companies stressed the advantages of the merger to advertisers. "We have an opportunity and a determination to package and position newspapers in a context that makes sense to advertisers," said G. Kingsley Anthony, president and chief executive officer of Newpapers First, which will be formally launched on June 4.

Mr. Anthony currently holds those titles at Times Mirror's national sales affiliate, Million Market Newspapers/Times Mirror National Marketing. John Kosanke, president and chief executive officer of Knight-Ridder Newspaper Sales Inc., will become executive vice president of the joint venture, which will have headquarters in New York.

For its part, Times Mirror said the joint venture extends an arrangement begun in July 1986 when its Times Mirror National Marketing unit merged with Million Market Newspapers Inc., a group of independent newspapers including the Boston Globe, the Denver Post, the Indianapolis Star and News, the Milwaukee Journal and Sentinel, the Arizona Republic and Phoenix Gazette, the Seattle Times and the Seattle Post Intelligencer.

Miami-based Knight-Ridder publishes 29 newspapers in 26 cities, including the Miami Herald, the Philadelphia Inquirer and Philadelphia Daily News, the San Jose Mercury News, the Detroit Free Press and the Charlotte Observer.

Los Angeles-based Times Mirror publishes the Los Angeles Times, Newsday and New York Newsday, the Baltimore Sun newspapers, the Hartford Courant, the Morning Call, the (Stamford) Advocate and Greenwich Time. Times Mirror and Knight-Ridder also have interests in cable television and other businesses.
