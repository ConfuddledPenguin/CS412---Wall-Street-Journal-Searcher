Business Brief -- Cyprus Minerals Co.: Net Fell 63% in 1st Period; Lower Copper Prices Cited

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/26/90

Cyprus Minerals Co. reported net income fell 63%, on a 1.4% decline in revenue, due to lower copper and molybdenum prices and higher costs at some of its mines. For the first quarter, net income declined to $30.1 million, or 65 cents a share, compared with $80 million, or $1.96 a share, in the year-earlier period. Year-ago earnings included a $5 million gain from the sale of clay operations. Revenue declined to $429.5 million, from $435.8 million.

Denver-based Cyprus said it is seeing improvement in copper production costs, but the mining company doesn't expect 1990 earnings to be higher than 1989 earnings. For all of 1989, Cyprus earned $250.1 million on revenue of $1.79 billion.

The large earnings drop indicates how vulnerable the company's earnings still are to fluctuations in copper prices, despite efforts to diversify into other kinds of mining ventures over the past five years. Each one cent increase in copper production costs, or one cent decrease in the price of copper, results in a 10-cent a share decline in annual earnings for Cyprus, said R. Wayne Atwell, analyst at Goldman Sachs & Co.
