Big Japanese Retailers' Sales

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/26/90

TOKYO -- Sales at Japan's major retail stores in March declined 2% to 1.762 trillion yen ($11.09 billion), reflecting an unusually high level of sales a year earlier, the Ministry of International Trade and Industry said. The year-to-year drop was the first since February 1985, when sales fell 0.6%. The extended expansion of Japan's economy, led by domestic demand, has pushed up retail sales in every month since.

Overall retail sales remained at a high level in March and the basic upward trend in sales is unchanged, an official said.
