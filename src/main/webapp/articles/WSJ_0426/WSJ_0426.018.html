Icahn Says He'll Buy More Shares in USX If Spinoff Plan Wins

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/26/90

PITTSBURGH -- Carl C. Icahn said he will purchase an additional $800 million of USX Corp. stock if his proposed spinoff of USX's steel business is approved by shareholders. He said he would pay up to $48 a share. The New York-based investor has proposed that USX holders approve a plan that would spin off as much as 80% of USX's steel business as a separate company. Mr. Icahn contends that the separation, which is to be voted on by USX holders at the company's annual meeting May 7, would yield a combined value of $48 a share in the two companies.

USX shares closed up $1.25 yesterday to $34 in New York Stock Exchange composite trading.

Charles A. Corry, chairman and chief executive officer of USX, said in a statement that Mr. Icahn's announcement "sounded like a ploy." Mr. Corry said, "It seems to me that Icahn is simply trying to buy votes and distract stockholders from the serious questions that we and a number of prominent analysts have raised about his proposal to break up USX." He said he remains opposed to the spinoff proposal.

USX has said Mr. Icahn's proposal would cost the company an additional $110 million to $120 million in interest and insurance expenses, among other things. USX also has disparaged Mr. Icahn's $48-a-share valuation as the result of a "lightweight analytical job."

Mr. Icahn, however, insists the company is undervalued. He said yesterday that even if shareholders approve the spinoff and USX does not implement it, he believes USX "would have to take some bold actions to enhance shareholder value. Consequently, the purchase of additional USX stock immediately following a favorable vote on the spinoff would be, in my opinion, an excellent investment."

To increase his stake, Mr. Icahn said he would direct his various affiliates to deposit $400 million with Shearson Lehman Hutton Inc. so that Shearson could begin a buying program for $800 million.

Mr. Icahn currently owns 13.3% of USX's shares outstanding. A further $800 million investment at current market prices would bring the value of his USX holdings to nearly $2 billion.

He said the purchases would be made in the open market, by tender offer or other means.
