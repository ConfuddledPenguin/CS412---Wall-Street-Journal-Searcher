International Brief -- Bond Corp. Holdings Ltd.: Tompson Pacific and Others May Acquire Stake in Unit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/26/90

Bond Corp. Holdings is in talks to sell its 66% stake in Bond Corp. International to Tompson Pacific Ltd. and a group of unrelated parties, according to the Hong Kong Stock Exchange. The announcement about the Australian company, the flagship of Alan Bond's troubled corporate empire, comes after speculation over the fate of the investment holding subsidiary. BCIL has sold off nearly all of its assets in the past 18 months, keeping only its 50% stake in a Rome property development site and a brewery in China.

The statement didn't disclose the price being discussed, indicating only that Tompson Pacific, a property investment and investment holding company, is in talks to buy 34.5% of BCIL. The report also said Bond Corp. is in discussions to place the remaining 31.5% with unrelated parties.

Trading in shares of BCIL and Tompson Pacific was stopped before the start of yesterday's session and will remain halted until further details are disclosed, the companies said.
