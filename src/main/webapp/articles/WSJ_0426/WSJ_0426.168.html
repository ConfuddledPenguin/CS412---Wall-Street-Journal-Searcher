Royal Dutch Unit In Canada Posts Fall In First-Period Profit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/26/90

TORONTO -- The Canadian subsidiary of Royal Dutch/Shell Group reported a 46% drop in net income for the first quarter from a year earlier. Shell Canada Ltd. reported net of 63 million Canadian dollars (US$54.3 million) or 56 Canadian cents a share on revenue of C$1.24 billion. In the first quarter of 1989, the oil, gas and chemicals concern reported net of C$117 million or C$1.04 a share, on revenue of C$1.22 billion.

The bulk of the decline came from substantially reduced earnings in Calgary-based Shell's chemical and downstream, or refined, oil retailing divisions, the company said. Although the company reported slightly increased earnings from its oil and gas production division, it wasn't enough to offset the declines in other sectors.

John MacLeod, president and chief executive of Shell Canada, said the company expects lower earnings throughout 1990 compared with last year. But he said, "we don't expect to see the same degree of decline as we saw in the first quarter of 1990."

He declined to give a specific earnings estimate for the year.

Mr. MacLeod said Shell is taking steps to improve earnings at the downstream oil products and retailing division. Among the options the company is considering as part of a restructuring are sales of some of the company's downstream assets, including retail gas stations and outlets and assets.

Donald Taylor, executive vice-president of Shell, declined to elaborate on the planned restructuring, saying that a company review of the assets is scheduled to be completed next month.

Mr. Taylor said that the downstream assets are generating a return of only 4%, which he hopes a restructuring would raise to about 8%.

In trading on the Toronto Stock Exchange, Shell's Class A shares closed at C$38 a share, unchanged.
