REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: Nicaragua's New President

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/26/90

As of yesterday, Nicaragua has a president deserving of the title. A president should be freely determined by a nation's voters, and that is precisely how Violetta Chamorro came to be inaugurated yesterday as president of Nicaragua. Now, after years of the Somoza and Sandinista dictatorships, Nicaraguans have a chance to shape their own affairs. But it won't be easy. President Chamorro takes office under circumstances that are politically bizarre. She has agreed to let Humberto Ortega run the army, while she holds the title of Defense Minister. Clearly, she is not yet fully in control of the country's substantial arsenal of military power. Sandinista "unions" already are hammering with strikes what little is left of the economy. At her inauguration yesterday, Sandinistas threw water balloons at Mrs. Chamorro and rocks at Vice President Dan Quayle. Against this backdrop, the U.S. Congress, controlled by Democrats who bellowed for years about how much they cared for Nicaragua, is dragging its feet on voting $300 million in much-needed aid for the Nicaraguans. The Democrats' silence is obvious and deafening at the moment that the new Nicaraguan democracy most needs their support.
