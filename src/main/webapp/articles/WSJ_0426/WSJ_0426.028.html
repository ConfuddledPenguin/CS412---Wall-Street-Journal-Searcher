International: Daimler-Benz AG's 1989 Earnings Soared On Accounting Shift

  • Author: Terence Roth
  • Date: 04/26/90

FRANKFURT, West Germany -- Daimler-Benz AG said a change in the way it calculates pension liabilities and inventories added a one-time contribution of 5.1 billion marks ($3 billion) to 1989 group net income, which totaled 6.8 billion marks with the extraordinary gain. Analysts said the balance-sheet adjustment was aimed at making Daimler-Benz look more attractive to international investors, who don't always account for conservative West German accounting practices when assessing West German balance sheets.

When excluding the accounting adjustment, the automotive and technology conglomerate said that the group's net income would have been unchanged at the 1988 net income level of 1.7 billion marks, reflecting pressured earnings from automotive operations last year. The extraordinary gain will be allocated to equity reserves, the company said.

The gain came on a write-back of part of Daimler-Benz's overfunded pension provisions and undervalued inventories accumulated over previous years. Daimler-Benz said it altered the conservative basis on which it calculates the two positions to better reflect international standards in time for planned stock listings on the London and Tokyo stock exchanges later this year.

Stephen Reitman, an auto analyst at UBS-Phillips & Drew in London estimated that the change will boost the company's equity as a percentage of total assets to around 30% from 22%, bringing its per-share net asset value to 400 marks from 267 marks. "Basically, it will make the company look more solid than under its old system of overprovisioning for pensions," Mr. Reitman said. After cumulative gain for 1989 results, the analyst judged that the change to pension valuations in the future will reduce the company's annual labor costs by an annual 500 million marks.
