Who's News: Teledyne's Board Appoints Rutledge President, Director

  • Author: Pauline Yoshihashi
  • Date: 04/26/90

LOS ANGELES -- Teledyne Inc.'s board elected William P. Rutledge president and a director of the newly streamlined aerospace and manufacturing company. He succeeds George A. Roberts, 71 years old, who was elected vice chairman and remains chief executive officer. Mr. Rutledge, 48, had served as executive vice president and his appointment to Teledyne's board expands it to seven members. Mr. Rutledge and Hudson B. Drake, 55, senior vice president overseeing Teledyne's aviation, electronics and international operations, had been considered prime contenders for the president's post.

Also, Douglas Grant, controller, was named treasurer and chief financial officer. Mr. Grant, 39, succeeds Gordon Bean, who was named vice president in charge of the company's metals operations. Gary Riley, a group executive, 53, was named vice president in charge of manufacturing.

Henry Singleton, Teledyne's chairman, announced at last year's annual meeting his decision to step down from day-to-day management of the multi-industry company he founded three decades ago.

Teledyne recently completed the spinoff of its insurance and finance subsidiaries into a new publicly traded company, Unitrin Inc. Teledyne also declared a 5-for-1 stock split, boosting the number of common shares outstanding to 55.4 million.

Those moves have prompted speculation by analysts and investors that Mr. Singleton, 73, may have plans to sell his 13.1% stake in the company, sell major portions of the company or take other major steps. In an interview, Mr. Singleton and Mr. Roberts said they don't have any plans to sell major assets, or cash in on Teledyne's substantial real estate holdings.

Nor do they plan a big stock buy-back, another scenario envisioned by analysts. Mr. Singleton said the company wants to let the stock adjust to the spinoff and stock split before evaluating any such move. Teledyne's stock closed yesterday at $28 a share, down 62.5 cents, in New York Stock Exchange composite trading.

Mr. Singleton and Mr. Roberts don't usually discuss plans, but the company was careful to introduce several senior executives at the annual meeting. Lawrence M. Harris, an aerospace analyst with Bateman Eichler, Hill Richards, said it was meant to show Teledyne has managers capable of running the company when the chairman and vice chairman step aside.

The board also declared a regular quarterly dividend of 20 cents a share, payable May 22 to stock of record May 7. The dividend reflects the 5-for-1 stock split.
