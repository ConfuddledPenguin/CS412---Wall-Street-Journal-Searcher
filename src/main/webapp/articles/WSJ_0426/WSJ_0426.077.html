Disney Profit Was Up 20% In 2nd Quarter

  • Author: Roy J. Harris Jr.
  • Date: 04/26/90

Walt Disney Co.'s motion picture business surged in the second quarter, ended March 31, pacing a 20% rise in net income on a 26% gain in revenue. The entertainment company earned $178.5 million, or $1.29 a share, in the period, compared with $149 million, or $1.08 a share, a year earlier. Revenue climbed to $1.3 billion from $1.04 billion.

Disney said in Burbank, Calif., that its second-quarter results continued its string of more than 20 consecutive record periods.

Led by the strong home video showing of "Honey, I Shrunk the Kids," "Dead Poets Society" and "Turner and Hooch," and the performance in theaters of "The Little Mermaid" and "Pretty Woman," Disney's filmed entertainment segment posted a 40% rise in operating earnings to $57.8 million on a 30% revenue rise to $469 million.

But its huge theme park operation and consumer products also scored major gains, Disney said. Consumer-products operating earnings rose 24% to $59.2 million on a 29% rise in revenue to $124.5 million. And theme parks and resorts recorded operating earnings of $194.5 million, up 14% from last year, on a 22% increase in revenue to $710.3 million.

Disney, which for competitive reasons doesn't divulge the number of visitors to its California Disneyland, Florida Disney World and other theme parks, said record attendance, higher per-capita guest spending and resort expansions all contributed to higher operating results for that segment.

Further, the latest quarter lacked the benefit of the strong Easter holiday season, which occurred in the fiscal 1989 second quarter but will bolster third-quarter figures this year. Disney also cited the opening last May of the Disney-MGM Studios Theme Park, near Disney World in Orlando as a "major contributor" to this year's rise.

The Orlando business will begin getting its first major test in a few weeks, however, as MCA Inc.'s Universal Studio Tour attraction opens a few miles away. "Until now, Disney has had a lot of studio tour (in Orlando) vs. nothing," said Harold Vogel, who follows the entertainment industry for New York-based Merrill Lynch Research.

Mr. Vogel, however, expects Disney to continue doing well in the third quarter, as "The Little Mermaid" animated hit is released for home video and the longawaited Warren Beatty-Madonna film "Dick Tracy" is released in theaters. "That's their big hope," Mr. Vogel said, adding that because it is an expensive film, "it has to do well."

Earnings were announced after the stock market's close yesterday. In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange Disney shares closed at $111.25, up $1.

For the six months, net income rose 19% to $352.9 million, or $2.55 a share, from $297.3 million, or $2.16 a share. Revenue spurted 25% to $2.59 billion from $2.08 billion.
