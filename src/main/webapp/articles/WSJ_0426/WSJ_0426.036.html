ITT Net Rose 27% In First Quarter On a Special Gain

  • Author: James C. Hyatt
  • Date: 04/26/90

NEW YORK -- ITT Corp. said net income rose 27% in the first quarter, results it called "better than expected." Net income rose to $253 million, or $1.88 a share, from $200 million, or $1.40 a share. The latest quarter included the previously reported after-tax gain of $47 million, or 33 cents a share, on the sale of the company's investment in Compagnie Generale d'Electricite. Without the gain, net income would have risen 3%.

Rand V. Araskog, chairman and chief executive officer, said operating results, or income before taxes and interest, for all businesses except electronic parts were equal to or better than year-earlier results.

Sales rose 2% to $4.93 billion from $4.83 billion. The year-earlier quarter included $139 million in sales from businesses that have since been divested. "Despite the lower results in some of our businesses, 1990 is off to a good start," Mr. Araskog said.

ITT said defense business operating income improved because of increased contract margins and product shipments. Automotive gains in Europe were offset by softness in North American markets. Fluid-technology results were flat, while pulp and timber income improved, reflecting favorable profit margins from log sales and higher pulp volume. Income from electronic components declined substantially, reflecting "continuing competitive pressures within the connector and semiconductor businesses resulting in lower sales and margins."

ITT said its equity from Alcatel N.V. "rose dramatically;" Alcatel is a European telecommunications-equipment joint venture with Compagnie Generale d'Electricite.

Earnings from service businesses declined because of "the difficult business climate in the U.S. during the quarter." Operating results at the Hartford insurance business rose due to higher investment income and improved life-insurance operations, offset by "continued deterioration" in casualty underwriting.

ITT Financial had "a substantial decline" because of increased provisions for consumer-loan losses from "portfolio control actions and an increase in personal bankruptcies." Results declined at the ITT Sheraton hotel operations.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, ITT closed at $52, up 62.5 cents.
