Anheuser Profit Rose 9.2% in First Quarter; Belt-Tightening Set

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/26/90

ST. LOUIS -- Anheuser-Busch Cos. reported record sales and earnings for the first quarter but said price discounting by competitors was resulting in company belt-tightening. The nation's largest brewer said net income totaled $182.5 million, or 64 cents a share, a 9.2% increase from last year's $167.1 million, or 58 cents a share. Sales rose 8.1%, to $2.59 billion, from $2.39 billion.

While beer sales to wholesalers hit a record 20.1 billion barrels, a 2.9% increase, and rose 4% at retail during the quarter, the company said discount pricing that began last summer requires heavy marketing expenditures.

To offset those costs, Chairman and President August A. Busch III said Anheuser has implemented a hiring freeze for the year, reduced health insurance costs and cut bonuses for management.

"We believe these steps will allow us to reach reasonable earnings growth in 1990 despite our higher level of price promotion activity," he told shareholders at the company's annual meeting yesterday. He termed the increased promotional activity a short-term strategy, but added that while Anheuser has no intention of starting a price war, it would meet competitive pricing levels in most markets.

To capture sales, Miller Brewing Co., a unit of Philip Morris Cos., Coors Brewing Co. and other competitors last summer cut prices on premium beers. Eventually, Anheuser was forced to meet that competition, as what Mr. Busch termed "aggressive price promotion tactics and emphasis on low-price beer began to hamper our ability to get retailer ad and display space."

Despite those pressures, Anheuser implemented a price increase this past February, which the competition followed.

Company officials also confirmed they intend to spend about $30 million on a media campaign to stimulate sales of its Budweiser premium beers.
