International Brief -- Total-Cie. Francaise des Petroles: Energy Company Reports 49.2% Rise in Group Profit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/26/90

Total-Cie. Francaise des Petroles, Paris, said its 1989 net consolidated profit jumped 49.2% to 2.2 billion francs ($386 million) from 1.48 billion francs in 1988. Consolidated revenue rose 15.6% to 107.9 billion francs ($18.9 billion) from 93.3 billion francs.

The French state-controlled oil group noted that the steep rise in profit occurred despite 1.14 billion francs in extraordinary items. In 1988, the company had extraordinary gains of 242 million francs.

About one-half of the 1989 extraordinary charges was attributable to various provisions linked to restructuring within the group. The other half was because of the acceleration of depreciation and provisions affecting the group's oil and gas assets, mainly in North America.

Total-CFP said the sharp increase in revenue reflected strong sales growth both in terms of value and volume. The group sold 62.1 million tons of crude oil in 1989, up 14.2% from a year earlier.
