International: U.K. Sets Antitrust Probes For 2 Foreign Transactions

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/21/90

LONDON -- The British government ordered antitrust probes of two separate transactions planned by France's Thomson CSF S.A. and Finland's Kemira Oy. The action holds up, at least temporarily, plans by France-owned Thomson and British Aerospace PLC to merge their guided-missile businesses. The merger, for which planning began last year, would create one of the world's largest missile makers, with annual sales of about #1.4 billion ($2.63 billion).

The government also ordered an investigation into plans for Kemira, a Finland-owned chemical company, to buy the British nitrogenous fertilizer business of Imperial Chemical Industries PLC. The transaction, announced in July and valued at less than #100 million, would make Kemira the largest fertilizer supplier in Britain.

The decision highlights how serious the ruling Conservative Party is taking its recent vow to scrutinize mergers involving foreign state-controlled companies.
