U.S. Antitrust Review Slows Close of Merger Of Avery and Dennison

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/21/90

PASADENA, Calif. -- Avery International Corp. said its merger with Dennison Manufacturing Co. has been delayed until mid-October because the Justice Department is taking slightly longer than expected to complete its antitrust review. "No problem is anticipated," an Avery spokeswoman said. The office-products concern had expected the review to be done by Sept. 28. Completion is now expected Oct. 12. The merger with Dennison, an office-products manufacturer based in Framingham, Mass., will close shortly thereafter, Avery said.

Avery also reported that net income fell 30% to $13.3 million in the third quarter ended Aug. 31. The results include $3.7 million in merger-related expenses, which reduced per-share net by eight cents to 30 cents a share. In the year-ago quarter, the company had net of $18.9 million, or 43 cents a share. Sales in the latest quarter rose 6% to $448.6 million from $422.7 million.

Avery said "a weak world-wide industrial economy" hurt the company. The company was also hurt by the bankruptcies of three large European customers. Avery said strong performances of its U.S. office products businesses didn't offset weakness in overseas operations.
