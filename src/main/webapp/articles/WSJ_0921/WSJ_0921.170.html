International Brief -- Air France: Airline's Board Intensifies Concern's Cost-Cutting Plan

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/21/90

Air France's board decided to intensify the French-owned airline's cost-cutting program in response to an estimated loss of 170 million French francs ($32.3 million) in the first half and oil-related difficulties anticipated in the second half. The board also delayed until 1991 any non-aircraft investments planned for the rest of this year, while nonessential work on buildings or information systems has effectively been canceled.

Air France also adopted a hiring freeze, with the exception of navigation personnel, while the airline's program of chartering planes from other companies will be phased out following the charter in January of a Boeing 747.

The measures follow Air France Chairman Bernard Attali's announcement in June that the company is cutting back flights on some less-traveled routes in the face of sluggish traffic growth.

Air France had consolidated net profit after payments to minority interests of 841 million francs in 1989 on revenue of 39.6 billion francs.
