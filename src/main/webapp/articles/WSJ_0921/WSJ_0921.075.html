Ford and GM Say Sluggish Sales Cause Four Plant Closings

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/21/90

DETROIT -- Ford Motor Co. and General Motors Corp. said sluggish sales will force them to temporarily close a total of four U.S. car plants, idling 11,400 workers. GM said it will idle 4,300 workers at the Willow Run, Mich., assembly plant that builds the large Chevrolet Caprice model for two weeks beginning Monday. When the plant resumes production Oct. 8, it will operate only one shift for at least four weeks, also as a result of slow consumer demand for the cars.

The Caprice is one of the biggest cars GM sells, and was relaunched this spring with a radically new, aerodynamic exterior. However, sales have slumped in recent weeks and now GM dealers have a 96-day supply of the cars. A 60-to-65 days supply is considered ideal. A Chevrolet spokesman said it's not clear whether concerns about gas prices are affecting sales of the car.

Ford, meanwhile, said it will temporarily idle three of its U.S. assembly plants next week, laying off about 7,100 workers. The plants build the Ford Taurus and Mercury Sable, the Ford Thunderbird and Mercury Cougar, and medium and heavy-duty trucks.

Additionally, Ford said the Lorain, Ohio, truck assembly plant will operate only one shift next week, putting 1,500 workers on temporary layoff. The plant builds the Econoline and Club Wagon vans.

Ford said the strike by Canadian Auto Workers at its Canadian operations hasn't forced any plant shutdowns in the U.S. However, if the six-day walkout continues into next week, parts shortages could force down time at certain Ford's operations in the U.S. The facilities most likely to be idled are the Wixom, Mich., luxury car factory, and two plants that build the Taurus and Sable midsized models.

Meanwhile, the 10 domestic auto makers planned to build 148,711 cars this week, down 2.3% from 152,135 made a year ago.
