Technology: Texas Instruments Sues Dell, Alleging Violation of Patent

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/21/90

DALLAS -- Adding to an already long list of companies it is suing for patent infringement, computer-chip giant Texas Instruments Inc. filed suit last Thursday against mail-order computer firm Dell Computer Corp. TI asked the U.S. District Court for the Northern District of Texas to assess a dollar amount for damages, claiming that Dell, of Austin, Texas, violated several key patents in building its personal computers. "People who make PC clones generally violate our patents," a TI spokesman said.

TI and Dell had been discussing patent issues since April. "We were very surprised when they filed," said a Dell spokesman.

Dell says the suit is without merit -- merely another example of TI's overactive legal department. Within the past year, TI has sued its own customers and competing U.S. chip firms on patent-related matters. Among those companies are Zenith Data Systems Corp., Analog Devices Inc. and LSI Logic Corp.

Dell is the second Texas computer maker this month to spar in court over patents with TI. Tandy Corp. on Sept. 6 alleged that TI broke federal antitrust laws by falsely claiming ownership of certain microprocessor technologies. Tandy also alleged that TI demanded unreasonably high royalty payments.

Japanese chip firms have also accused TI of demanding exorbitant royalty rates. A Japanese press report said TI demanded 10% of the wholesale value of products using chips based on a TI patent. TI would not confirm the report.
