Marketing & Media -- Advertising: Interpublic Plans to Buy the 64.6% Of Lowe Group It Doesn't Own

  • Author: Thomas R. King
  • Date: 09/21/90

Interpublic Group of Cos., which currently holds 35.4% of Lowe Group PLC, said yesterday it plans to acquire the remaining 64.6% of the British agency group in a transaction valued at approximately $160 million. The transaction would give Interpublic full ownership of a third international ad network. Lowe, a fast-growing firm with offices in more than 10 countries including the Lowe & Partners agency in New York, would join Interpublic's two major advertising networks, McCann-Erickson and Lintas.

As part of the deal, Lowe shareholders will be offered a choice of cash or Interpublic stock. New York-based Interpublic said it will offer #4.25 ($8) in cash for each Lowe share or, alternatively, seven Interpublic shares for every 25 Lowe shares. That ratio works out to 0.28 Interpublic share for each Lowe share. There are 19.5 million Lowe shares affected by the offer.

The stock offer values the Lowe shares slightly higher than the cash offer, and Interpublic officials are clearly hoping that the majority of holders will opt for the stock swap. Interpublic shares fell 50 cents yesterday to close at $30.125 a share in composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange.

Analysts said Interpublic is snagging Lowe at a bargain price. Lowe's stock, amid a dismal U.K. advertising environment, has taken a beating on the London stock exchange recently. Industry executives said Interpublic wouldn't have been able to afford the company just six weeks ago, when Lowe stock was trading at around 430 pence a share. Yesterday, shares of Lowe soared 55% to trade at 410 pence, up 145 pence from Wednesday's late price of 265 pence.

Interpublic said it decided to buy the rest of Lowe Group to further strengthen its position in Europe and to offer a third independent ad network to prospective clients. While the McCann and Lintas agencies are known for solid, if unexciting, ads for clients like Coca-Cola and Unilever, Lowe Group, which is headed by Frank Lowe, has gained notice for its offbeat, spunky, creative work.

"Each of the Interpublic agencies have different philosophical approaches to the business," said Philip H. Geier Jr., chairman and chief executive officer of Interpublic. "Looking at the competitive situation in the marketplace, this provides prospects with three different options." In turn, Interpublic will be able to increase its market share, he said.

Analysts cheered the move, saying Interpublic is picking up a profitable agency that will help boost its own prospects. "I think it's a very good deal," said Jim Dougherty, an analyst at County NatWest Securities USA. "Lowe's net income is growing a lot faster than Interpublic's is. This is certainly going to be a plus to Interpublic's growth rate."

Said Karen Ficker, an analyst at Labe Simpson in New York, "It adds some flair to the agency network. They've picked up an additional piece of the agency puzzle with no conflicts at a relatively low price."

The analysts also noted that the transaction would give a handsome boost to billings at Interpublic, which hasn't been immune to the downturn in the U.S. ad business. Advertisers, wary of the shaky economy, have in many cases drastically cut their advertising budgets, and the ad-spending outlook is uncertain. Major ad concerns like Interpublic have reported decreasing revenue from U.S. operations. Lowe's billings at the end of 1989 were #858 million.

Interpublic has for several years had various financial relationships with Lowe agencies. Last year, it exchanged its stake in the Lowe Marschalk agency, now known as Lowe & Partners, for a larger stake in Lowe Group.

Interpublic said it believes around 30% to 40% of Lowe's shareholders will opt for Interpublic stock, with the remaining shareholders taking cash. It said Lowe employees, officers and directors -- who together control about 10% of the stock outstanding -- all plan to take the Interpublic stock option. The acquisition, which is subject to certain regulatory conditions, is expected to be completed by year end.

Mr. Lowe, a charismatic British adman, will be invited to join the Interpublic board, the company said.

Eugene P. Beard, executive vice president of finance and operations at Interpublic, said, "We're paying 10 times projected earnings on estimated 1990 {Lowe Group} earnings. For an agency like Lowe and the talent of Frank and his people, I think it's a very fair deal for everybody."

Saatchi Wins Holly Farms

Tyson Foods pulled its $15 million Holly Farms chicken account from Long, Haymes & Carr, Winston-Salem, N.C., and awarded it to Saatchi & Saatchi Advertising, New York.

Tyson acquired Holly Farms last year following a long takeover fight. Saatchi, a unit of Saatchi & Saatchi Co., creates ads for Tyson's other fresh and processed chicken lines, an account that also bills about $15 million. The agency created Tyson's "Feeding You Like Family" campaign.

Jack Dunn, vice president of retail marketing at Tyson, said the company moved the business to consolidate its chicken advertising at one agency.

"Basically, it boils down to getting as few cooks in the kitchen as we possibly can," Mr. Dunn said. "Saatchi had the inside track and was doing good work for us." He said the account was moved without a review.

Ad Notes. . . .

GRIFFIN BACAL: Ralston Purina, St. Louis, named Griffin Bacal, New York, to handle future ad assignments for new children's food products. It also awarded the shop agency-of-record status for those products, which may include breakfast cereals and snacks. Billings haven't been determined. Griffin Bacal currently handles some ad assignments for Continental Baking, a Ralston unit.

WHO'S NEWS: At Lintas:USA, four senior vice presidents were promoted to executive vice president in the shop's media department. Marc Goldstein was elected executive vice president, director of national broadcast media and programming; Carolyn Libby was named executive vice president, executive media director of Lintas:New York; and Paul Hunt and Bob Gervason will assume similar positions at Lintas:Campbell-Ewald in Detroit.

HIP ACCOUNT: Health Insurance Plan (HIP) of Greater New York, a large East Coast health maintenance organization, awarded its ad account to TBWA Advertising, New York. Billings weren't disclosed.
