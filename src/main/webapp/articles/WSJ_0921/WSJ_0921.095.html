Fabrique Nationale Wins Munitions Order Totaling $74.3 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/21/90

LIEGE, Belgium -- Fabrique Nationale Herstal S.A. obtained a munitions order from a Middle East country totaling 2.4 billion Belgian francs ($74.3 million), an industry executive said. The order, whose size could be doubled later, might improve the survival chances of FN, which has been teetering on the edge of bankruptcy proceedings for months. FN, whose products include various small arms and Browning sport rifles, is 76%-owned by Societe Generale de Belgique S.A., a Brussels holding company.

FN wouldn't comment on the order, but the industry executive confirmed a report in a Brussels newspaper that the sale had been concluded. The executive, who declined to be identified, wouldn't disclose which Middle East country awarded the contract.

The executive also confirmed earlier reports that Giat Industries, a French state-owned arms maker, is in talks that could lead to an offer to buy all or part of FN. A Giat spokesman couldn't be reached.

But the industry executive said Giat isn't the only company interested in acquiring FN. He declined to name others.

FN, whose losses over the past four years have totaled about 12 billion Belgian francs, has scheduled an extraordinary shareholders meeting for a week from today. Holders are expected to decide whether the company should continue operations. FN's management might report on prospects for selling the company at the meeting, the executive said.
