Federal Express Posts 42% Rise In 1st-Period Net

  • Author: Michael J. McCarthy
  • Date: 09/21/90

Federal Express Corp. reported a 42% gain in net income for its first quarter, ended Aug. 31, on a 16% gain in revenue. The Memphis, Tenn., express-delivery company said net was $43.1 million, or 81 cents a share, compared with $30.4 million, or 58 cents a share. Revenue rose to $1.92 billion from $1.65 billion.

The per-share earnings exceeded analysts' estimates, but there were several one-time items that contributed to the results.

"It was seemingly a good quarter," said Kevin Murphy, an analyst with Morgan Stanley & Co. "But there were some materially significant items in there as well."

For one thing, Federal Express gained some charter business from the U.S. military's Operation Desert Shield buildup of troops and equipment in the Middle East. It also picked up some shipments that would have gone to rival United Parcel Service of America Inc., as some customers shifted their business amid fears of a strike at UPS during the quarter. Earnings also benefited from the sale of noise-reduction kits for aircraft engines.

On an operating basis, Federal attributed the earnings increase to cost-control efforts and productivity improvements. It also said there was a "modest rebound" in Asian air freight volume from the depressed levels of April and May.

A company spokesman said Federal Express had another loss on its international business, which has been going through a costly expansion process, but he wouldn't elaborate.

Analysts said the U.S. operations' results were more promising. "The domestic side of the house looks very strong, even with weak volume, because of the much higher margins they're getting" as a result of price increases this year, said Doug Arthur, an analyst at Kidder, Peabody & Co.

To cover rising fuel costs caused by the Mideast showdown with Iraq, Federal said it will raise some prices on most overseas routes before the end of the year. It said the increases will vary by route but will be within the company's usual 3% to 7% range. The spokesman said he expects some international competitors to follow suit.

In the U.S., Federal Express said it won't raise prices further in its small package express business. The company has increased those prices twice this year, by 4% to 5% in April and 1% to 2% in July.

Federal Express said its fuel costs were in line with expectations during the latest quarter but will be hurt by higher prices as current fuel contracts expire.
