Massachusetts Forms Fund for Tax Receipts Pledged to Pay Bonds

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/21/90

BOSTON --- Massachusetts, hoping to get a good reception from nervous investors for an approaching bond issue, will set up a special fund for tax receipts that will be specifically pledged to pay off the bonds. Massachusetts' finances have investors worried in part because of a tax rollback petition on the November ballot that would reduce taxes by more than $1 billion in the current fiscal year; the state's budget is around $13 billion. The anti-tax measure, if approved, would eliminate a tax-increase package adopted recently by the legislature to narrow a huge budget deficit.

Establishing a dedicated trust fund is uncommon, although it has been used in the past by other borrowers having financial problems as a way to bolster investor interest.

The state is setting up an "irrevocable trust fund" for the new Series A bonds into which sufficient personal income tax receipts will be paid monthly, according to Moody's Investors Services Inc. Massachusetts is expected to issue about $385 million of the Series A dedicated income tax bonds next week.

Moody's gave the new issue a rating of Baa-1, slightly better than the worst-in-the-nation rating of Baa that it has given Massachusetts' general obligation bonds. "We feel there is some degree of enhancement provided" by the trust fund, said George Leung, managing director for state ratings at Moody's. Massachusetts also plans to issue another $225 million other bonds that are expected to be secured by a letter of credit.
