World Beer Output in 1989 Rose 2%, Kirin Reports

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/21/90

TOKYO -- World beer production rose 2% in 1989 to a record 110.7 million kiloliters, Kirin Brewery Co., Japan's largest brewery, said. A kiloliter equals 264.18 gallons.

The previous record was 108.5 million kiloliters in 1988, Kirin officials said.

In 1989, Japan surpassed Britain to become the world's fourth-largest beer brewer, Kirin said.

The largest producer by far was the U.S., with an estimated production of 23.4 million kiloliters, up 0.6% from 1988. U.S. brewers accounted for 21.1% of total global production, the officials said.

They said West Germany was second with 9.3 million kiloliters, up 0.4%, followed by China with 6.2 million kiloliters, down 3%.

Japan was next at 6.1 million kiloliters, up 5.1%, followed by Britain with six million kiloliters, down 0.4%.

The figures are based on data obtained from beer associations around the world, Kirin said.
