Corroon Stands Firm On Planned Merger, Refuses Aon's Offer

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/21/90

Corroon & Black Corp. refused Aon Corp.'s request for a meeting to discuss its $40-a-share, or $840 million, bid. In a letter to Patrick G. Ryan, Aon's chairman and chief executive officer, Corroon's Chairman and Chief Executive Richard Miller said "the board has unanimously rejected Aon's demands for a meeting to discuss its proposal and for an opportunity to conduct a due diligence examination of New York-based insurer Corroon & Black.

After Aon's offer was rebuffed by Corroon's board late Tuesday, the Chicago insurer said it would leave its all-cash bid on the table. But Wednesday, Aon's Mr. Ryan reiterated that he wouldn't pursue the offer if Corroon's board continues to oppose it.

Mr. Miller also reiterated that Corroon's board believes its previously announced merger with international insurance broker Willis Faber PLC of London "is in the best interests of Corroon & Black shareholders . . ."

The Willis Faber merger is currently valued at $31.38-a-share, or $643.3 million, based on yesterday's closing price. In London trading yesterday, Willis Faber shares closed at 214 pence, up 4 pence.

When Willis Faber made its merger proposal to Corroon on June 4, the offer was valued at $34.72 a share, or $712.3 million.

Corroon shareholders are set to vote on the Willis Faber merger next Friday.

Corroon shares fell $1.125 to $30.875 in New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday.

In Chicago, Aon officials weren't immediately available for comment late yesterday afternoon.
