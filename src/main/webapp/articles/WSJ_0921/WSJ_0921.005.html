Business Brief -- TCBY Enterprises Inc.: Fiscal Third-Quarter Profit Declined 42% to $6.7 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/21/90

TCBY Enterprises Inc. said fiscal third-quarter net income fell 42%, adding that it doesn't expect to post "positive" same-store sales for the year. In the nine months ended Aug. 31, TCBY said same-store sales fell 12.7%.

The Little Rock, Ark., chain of frozen-yogurt stores said third-quarter profit was $6.7 million, or 25 cents a share, down from $11.5 million, or 43 cents a share, a year ago. In late August, the company said it expected such a drop, largely because of increased competition and bad weather.

Sales and franchising revenue fell 11% to $43.6 million from a restated $49.2 million. TCBY said it opened 99 stores in the quarter, bringing the total to 1,894.

TCBY said it is continuing to implement programs for sales outside stores. But it added that "additional sales from these programs are not expected to reach significant levels in 1990." The company said it is also testing in-store menu items such as salads, Slim-Fast shakes and Slim-Fast frozen products. TCBY franchisees have complained recently about a paucity of new products.
