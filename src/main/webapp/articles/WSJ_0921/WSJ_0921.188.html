First Commercial's Stock Plan

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/21/90

LITTLE ROCK, Ark. -- First Commercial Corp. said its board adopted a shareholder rights' plan designed to deter hostile takeovers. The multibank holding company said it doesn't know of any such efforts to acquire the company, but adopted the plan to prevent an acquirer from gaining control of the corporation without offering a fair price to all holders.

The company said it would issue one preferred stock purchase right for each common share outstanding to stock of record as of a week from today. The rights entitle holders to buy common shares at half price if a hostile suitor acquires 20% of the company's common stock or launches a tender or exchange offer to do so.
