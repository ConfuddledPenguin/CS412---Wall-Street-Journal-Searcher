Four Canadian Banks Cut Their Prime Lending Rate

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/21/90

TORONTO -- Four Canadian banks cut their prime, or minimum lending, rate yesterday following a reduction in the Bank of Canada's bank rate. The Royal Bank of Canada, Bank of Nova Scotia, Toronto Dominion Bank and Canadian Imperial Bank of Commerce all cut their prime lending rates to 13.75% from 14.25%.

The Bank of Montreal, cut its prime rate to 13.75% last week.

The bank rate, the levy charged by the Bank of Canada on loans to financial institutions, is set weekly at 25 basis points above the average yield of three-month treasury bills. Following yesterday's treasury bill sale, the bank rate dropped to 12.61% from 12.70%.
