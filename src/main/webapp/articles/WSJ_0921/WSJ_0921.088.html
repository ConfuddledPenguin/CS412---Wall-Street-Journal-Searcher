National Fuel Gas's Project

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/21/90

BUFFALO, N.Y. -- National Fuel Gas Supply Corp., the pipeline subsidiary of National Fuel Gas Co., said it will invest $30.1 million to complete a project to import gas from Canada. To finish the project, National Fuel will construct an 8,600-horsepower compressor in Concord, N.Y., while its partner, Tennessee Gas Transmission Corp., will extend its Niagara Spur pipeline by almost 50 miles. Total cost of the jointly owned facilities is estimated at $65.4 million.

The Federal Energy Regulatory Commission has approved the final phase of the project, which will allow National Fuel to transport an additional 161.5 million cubic feet of gas from Canada per day. Construction begins this fall and is scheduled for completion next summer.
