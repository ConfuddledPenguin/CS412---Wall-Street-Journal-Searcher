REC, Others Settle SEC Fraud Charges On Stock Purchases

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/21/90

WASHINGTON -- The Securities and Exchange Commission accused REC Investors Inc. of buying about $150 million of securities without having enough money to pay for them. The complaint also named the firm's sole shareholder, Robert E. Connors, and National State Bank of Summit, N.J. The bank was accused of lending the firm money to buy the stock with insufficient collateral.

According to the commission, REC, based in Far Hills, N.J., purchased about $150 million of securities without sufficient funds to pay for the trades in 1987 and 1988. It earned $2.6 million in profit on the trades.

At the same time the suit was filed, the defendants settled, without admitting any violations. Under the consent agreement, REC will disgorge $47,803, representing the costs that the firm would have incurred, plus interest, had it borrowed the funds in compliance with margin rules, which allow investors to buy stock without putting up the full cost.

Mr. Connors and the bank agreed to refrain from the alleged violations in the future.

In a separate settlement of administrative proceedings against REC and Mr. Connors, REC agreed to a censure from the agency and Mr. Connors agreed to a 15-day suspension of trading.

Mr. Connors couldn't be reached for comment yesterday.
