Clean Harbors to Take Charge for Rejection Of Its Incinerator Plan

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/21/90

BRAINTREE, Mass. -- Clean Harbors Inc., denied state permission to build a proposed hazardous waste incinerator, said it will take a pretax charge of about $16 million that will result in a loss for its third quarter, ending Nov. 30. The waste cleanup and hauling company said the one-time charge will be related to a write-down of its investment in the incinerator. The state Department of Environmental Protection, in a decision that followed the recommendation of the state Public Health Department, said the company's site in Braintree isn't suitable for the project. It said the site is in a heavily populated area and lacks an adequate buffer zone between it and the area's residents.

A Clean Harbors spokesman said the state's decision "clearly stated that the factors which caused them to reach the decision that the site is not suitable aren't mitigable." Clean Harbors has been planning to build the incinerator for several years, but opposition among local residents has been strong.

In the year-earlier third quarter, Clean Harbors had net income of $1.6 million, or 18 cents a share, on revenue of $33.5 million.

The company also said it had fiscal second-quarter net of $1.4 million, or 16 cents a share, up 7.5% from $1.3 million, or 15 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue rose 16% to $40.6 million in the period ended Aug. 31 from $35.1 million. For the fiscal first half, net rose 17% to $2 million, or 23 cents a share, on revenue of $75.1 million, from $1.7 million, or 19 cents a share, on revenue of $68.2 million.
