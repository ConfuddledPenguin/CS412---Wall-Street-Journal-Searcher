Dayton Hudson Approves Planned Parenthood Grant

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/21/90

MINNEAPOLIS -- A Dayton Hudson Corp. foundation, reversing an earlier decision blasted by abortion-rights advocates, approved an $18,000 grant to Planned Parenthood. Still, Dayton Hudson is being careful not to anger anti-abortion groups. The big retailer said its money is going into a "restricted public-education fund" that Planned Parenthood's Minnesota chapter will add as a "separate line item subcategory" in its audit.

Dayton Hudson said, "Our contributions have never been nor will they be used to finance abortion services. We are satisfied that we can make this grant without taking a position on this issue."

Last month, the Dayton Hudson Foundation decided to sever longtime financial ties to Planned Parenthood in an attempt, it said, to distance itself from the battle over abortion. For 22 years, the foundation had given the group money for education programs designed to prevent teen-age pregnancy.

It isn't known if the reversal will appease women's groups that announced plans to boycott Dayton Hudson stores during the Christmas season if the foundation didn't restore funding.
