IRS Is Suspending Audits For Persian Gulf Hostages

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/21/90

WASHINGTON -- As long as Saddam Hussein holds Americans hostage in the Middle East they're safe from the IRS. The Internal Revenue Service said it will suspend audits of any Americans detained in Iraq and Kuwait as well as any efforts to collect unpaid taxes.

To obtain such relief, the agency asks families of hostages to call 1-800-829-4351 during business hours and give the names and Social Security numbers of those detained -- and impart any information "concerning ongoing actions with the IRS."

The agency also indicated it will consider postponing examinations or tax bills of U.S. troops or military reservists assigned to Operation Desert Shield in the Persian Gulf. They should call the regular federal tax-assistance number, 1-800-424-1040, "in response to correspondence received regarding an examination or past due taxes so that alternatives may be considered, such as postponing an examination or a payment," the IRS said.
