Initial State Jobless Claims Were Unchanged in Week

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/21/90

WASHINGTON -- Initial claims for regular state unemployment benefits totaled a seasonally adjusted 382,000 in the week ended Sept. 8, unchanged from the previous week's level, the Labor Department said. The number of people receiving regular state benefits in the week ended Sept. 1 was a seasonally adjusted 2,471,000, or 2.4% of those covered by unemployment insurance, down from 2,546,000 the previous week, when the insured unemployment rate was also 2.4%.

Counting all state and federal benefit programs, the number of people receiving unemployment benefits in the week ended Sept. 1 fell to 2,138,500 from 2,319,800 a week earlier. These figures aren't seasonally adjusted.

Most state and federal government offices were closed Sept. 3 for the Labor Day holiday, which may have affected the level of initial claims during the week ended Sept. 8.
