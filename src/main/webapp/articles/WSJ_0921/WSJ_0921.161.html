U.S. Reserve Assets Increased in August To $78.91 Billion

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/21/90

WASHINGTON -- U.S. official reserve assets rose $1 billion in August to $78.91 billion, the Treasury Department said. Last month's gain compared with a $608 million rise in reserve assets in July to $77.91 billion, the department said.

U.S. reserve assets consist of foreign currencies, gold, special drawing rights at the International Monetary Fund and the U.S. reserve position -- its ability to draw foreign currencies -- at the IMF.

The nation's holdings of foreign currencies increased $717 million in August to $48.17 billion, while its gold reserves rose $1 million to $11.07 billion.

U.S. holdings of IMF special drawing rights last month rose $81 million to $10.78 billion, and its reserve position at the IMF increased $204 million to $8.89 billion.
