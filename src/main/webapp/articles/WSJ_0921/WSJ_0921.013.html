Dividend News: Home Savings Bank To Suspend Payment Of Quarterly Dividend

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/21/90

NEW YORK -- The Home Savings Bank, whose shareholders just approved the bank's acquisition by H.F. Ahmanson & Co., said it will suspend payment of the third-quarter dividend. Robert Pondt, Home Savings' senior executive vice president, said the board of directors voted to suspend the 18.75-cent-a-share payout in the hope of saving about $2 million by the end of the year. The quarterly dividend was last paid on July 19.

Mr. Pondt said the suspension of the payout wasn't at Ahmanson's request, but he added that it could enhance Home Savings' value to Ahmanson. He also said payment of a fourth-quarter dividend depends on earnings reported for the current quarter and completion of the acquisition, which is expected by year end. However, he said the bank expects flat third-quarter earnings.

The acquisition, valued at about $300 million, was approved Wednesday by Home Savings' shareholders, and is subject to regulatory approval.

Home Savings, with assets of $1.5 billion, operates 13 full-service banking offices in the New York metropolitan area.

H. F. Ahmanson of Irwindale, Calif., with more than $47.6 billion in assets, is the parent company of Home Savings of America and The Bowery Savings Bank.
