Business Brief -- Concord Camera Corp.: Loan Is in Default, Owing To Losses, Unit Problems

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/21/90

Concord Camera Corp., Avenel, N.J., said it defaulted on a bank loan, citing poor results from retailing softness and problems at its Hong Kong unit. The loan agreement allows the bank to declare a default and seek remedies, but Concord said it expects a waiver. For its fourth quarter ended June 30, Concord had a loss of $2.4 million, or 57 cents a share, on revenue of $6.8 million. The annual loss was $3.2 million, or 77 cents a share, on revenue of $28.4 million.

Concord sells 35mm and 110 cartridge cameras that it makes in China under its label and private labels. Concord said that softness in the retail industry in the fiscal third and fourth quarters hurt camera sales, and that "wrongful actions by certain former officers and directors" of the Dialbright Co. unit in Hong Kong resulted in manufacturing disruptions and revaluation of certain inventories. Concord said that the unit has been reorganized, and that legal proceedings have begun in Hong Kong against the former employees.
