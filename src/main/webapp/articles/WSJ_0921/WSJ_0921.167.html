Business Brief -- NEC Corp.: Japanese Firm to Set Up Related Ventures in China

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/21/90

NEC Corp., a leading Japanese high-technology concern, said it has agreed to set up related joint ventures in China to produce integrated circuits and digital telephone switching systems. The deal, the first between a Japanese electronics firm and China, was initiated in 1988 but suspended after the Chinese government's crackdown on demonstrators in June 1989. The project was reopened after Japan's government lifted its freeze on loans to China in July.

The agreement, signed by NEC and the Chinese Ministry of Machinery and Electronics Industry, will become effective when formal approval is obtained from the Chinese and Japanese governments.

Under the deal, a joint venture in Beijing would produce large-scale integrated circuits jointly with Shoudu Iron and Steel Co. of China, NEC said. It will be capitalized at the equivalent of $62 million and 60% of it will be owned by Shoudu and the rest by NEC, the officials said.

NEC also will set up a joint venture to produce telephone switching systems in Tianjin with Tianjin Zhonghuan Computer Co., Tianjin Post and Telecommunications Administration, both of China, and Sumitomo Corp., a Japanese trading firm.

They said the projected venture would be worth $29 million, with the Chinese computer company owning 45%, the postal authority 15%, NEC 35% and Sumitomo 5%.
