Nonfinancial Debt Grew At 6% Rate in 2nd Period

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/21/90

WASHINGTON -- Borrowing by households, nonfinancial businesses and federal, state and local governments grew at an annual rate of 6% in the second quarter, trailing the 7.6% rate of the first period, the Federal Reserve Board said. The Fed said borrowing by the federal government grew at an annual rate of 9.2% in the second quarter, while private, state and local nonfinancial borrowing grew at a 5% rate. The figures are adjusted for seasonal changes.

Second-quarter nonfinancial borrowing totaled $598.1 billion, of which $216.7 billion was by the federal government.

Total credit market debt owed by the nonfinancial sector at the end of the second quarter was $10.127 trillion, up from $9.973 trillion at the end of the first quarter and $9.439 trillion a year earlier.

Borrowing by nonfinancial businesses increased at an annual rate of 4.9%, compared with 5.9% the previous quarter. Household borrowing increased at a 5.5% rate, compared with 8.4% in the previous quarter.

State and local government borrowing increased at an annual rate of 2.3% in the second quarter, compared with 1.4% in the previous quarter.
