New Jersey Will Reward Pollution Whistle-Blowers

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/21/90

New Jersey began a bounty program to reward state residents who blow the whistle on polluters. Under the program, started Wednesday, anyone with a tip that results in a civil or criminal fine for illegally dumped waste, including garbage, medical waste, toxic chemicals and low-level radioactive waste, will receive either 10% or $250 of the fine collected, whichever is greater. For information resulting in the arrest and conviction of anyone illegally storing or dumping hazardous or low-level radioactive waste, the reward is 50% of the penalty collected.

"We want to enlist every citizen in New Jersey in the fight to protect our clean air and water," Gov. Jim Florio said in a statement. "We need seven million pairs of eyes looking out for polluters."
