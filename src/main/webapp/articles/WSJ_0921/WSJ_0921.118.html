Federal Investigators Seize Firm Suspected Of Dealing With Iraq

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 09/21/90

An Ohio machine tool concern was seized Monday by federal agents who are investigating its suspected ties to the Iraqi government and its possible supply of machinery for Iraqi armaments. The company, Matrix-Churchill Corp., is a unit of Matrix-Churchill Ltd., of Britain. In turn, the British concern is owned by Technology & Development Group Ltd., a subsidiary of Al-Arabj Trading, an Iraqi holding company, according to Stephen Hartkop, assistant agent in charge of the U.S. Customs office in Cleveland that conducted the raid.

The 12-employee company, based in Solon, Ohio, near Cleveland, was padlocked and its assets seized under the presidential order that Iraqi firms' assets be frozen, said Mr. Hartkop. The firm had about $2 million in its bank accounts, he said.

Mr. Hartkop said the Ohio arm of Matrix-Churchill for years had been a machine tool concern and that several machines were on display in its offices. However, he said Matrix-Churchill also had been purchasing other types of manufacturing companies and apparently was shipping manufacturing equipment to Iraq.

The Customs agent said the investigation would focus on whether the Solon company illegally exported munitions or controlled commodities or violated laws concerning trading with Iraq. He declined to say whether assets of other Ohio companies also had been seized.

Yesterday, the Cleveland Plain Dealer reported that a secretary at Matrix-Churchill denied involvement with the Iraqi government. A lawyer at a Cleveland firm that has represented Matrix-Churchill couldn't immediately be reached.
