Business Brief -- Cockerill Sambre S.A.: First-Half Profit Rose 22% Due to Some Special Gains

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/21/90

Cockerill Sambre S.A. said unconsolidated profit for the first six months of fiscal 1990 rose 22%, to 8.9 billion Belgian francs ($275.6 million) from 7.3 billion Belgian francs in the year-earlier period. Sales declined 6% to 50.1 billion Belgian francs from 53.3 billion Belgian francs.

The Belgian steel foundry said the increase in profit was due to extraordinary gains. Without the gains, profit would have been about half the level of year-ago earnings, the company said.

Cockerill predicted noticeably weaker second-half results because of lower prices and volume for steel products. Moreover, it said it didn't expect to realize the same level of one-time gains as during the first half.

"The profit for 1990 should nonetheless remain satisfactory, without attaining the exceptional level seen in 1989,' the company said.
