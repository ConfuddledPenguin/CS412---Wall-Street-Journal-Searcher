Letters to the Editor: Old Ideologies Rabidly Defended

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/21/90

Susan Lee is entitled to her opinion that anyone who questions the performance of an unbridled free market is "weird" and "benighted." But her review of my book "Prosperity Lost" (Leisure & Arts, Aug. 27) went off the deep end in labeling me an advocate of a "police state." Redistribution of income and limits on the accumulation of wealth have been the official policy of the U.S. ever since the creation of the income tax, inheritance taxes and the New Deal social programs. The problem is that these measures have traditionally been applied only half-heartedly, and during the Reagan years they were decimated.

I thought conservatives had outgrown the practice of mindlessly condemning critics of laissez faire as totalitarian. Far from embracing Stalinism, my book was inspired by the struggles of East Europeans against repressive government power.

Yet, as many Poles, for example, are beginning to realize, allowing the market completely free rein is not the most effective way of restoring prosperity for all. Faced with deregulatory disasters such as the savings-and-loan mess, many Americans are coming to the same conclusion. The solution to the economic problems of East and West will come from innovative thinking, not rabid defense of old ideologies.

Philip Mattera

Brooklyn, N.Y.
