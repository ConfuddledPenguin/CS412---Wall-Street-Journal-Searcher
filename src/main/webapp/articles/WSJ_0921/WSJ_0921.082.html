Judge Won't Hear Group's Case to Halt Hallwood Proposal

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/21/90

DALLAS -- Hallwood Group Inc. said Judge Thelton E. Henderson of the U.S. District Court of Northern California has informed the company that he has no jurisdiction to hear a motion by a third party that filed suit to block a proposed limited partnership consolidation plan offered by a Hallwood affiliate. The motion involves a proposal by Hallwood Realty Partners to consolidate 11 limited partnerships sponsored by Equitec Financial Group into a single partnership. The proposal calls for an exchange of the interests in the 11 partnerships for newly issued interests in a single partnership. The motion was sought as part of a class action suit filed by Robert Greathouse, who alleged fraud and various securities laws violations, the company said.

As previously announced, Hallwood Realty Partners has received consents representing about 82% of the total exchange values of the 11 Equitec partnerships, thus meeting minimum participation conditions for the proposed consolidation. Equitec and its affiliate, Equitex Properties Co., have filed a joint motion in the U.S. Bankruptcy Court for the Northern District of California seeking authorization to complete the consolidation. A hearing is scheduled for Oct. 19.
