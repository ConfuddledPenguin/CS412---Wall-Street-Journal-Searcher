Business Brief -- Ogden Corp.: Concern Will Retain 85% In Waste-to-Energy Unit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/21/90

Ogden Corp. ended speculation that it might split into two public companies, and said it intends to retain its 85% holding in Ogden Projects Inc., its waste-to-energy unit. Odgen, based in New York, also announced a major reshuffling of the Ogden Projects board, replacing five of its eight members and adding three others.

Earlier this year, Ogden had been discussing the possible creation of Ogden Projects as a separate entity.

Ogden Projects, which went public in 1989, has been growing rapidly as it brings on line large waste-to-energy plants operating long-term contracts; at the end of 1989, it had 13 plants in operation, 7 under construction, and 6 awaiting license.

Ogden's other major activity is a broad array of service business which generated $1 billion of revenue in 1989, ranging from asbestos abatement, managing arenas and convention centers, and operating commercial aviation facilities to managing industrial and building locations and providing building maintenance services.

For the six months, Ogden Projects had net income of $15.9 million, or 45 cents a share, on revenue of $189 million.
