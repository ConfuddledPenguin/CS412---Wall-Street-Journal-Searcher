Business Brief -- Southern National Corp.: Banking Concern to Merge With NBSC in Stock Swap

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/21/90

Southern National Corp. and NBSC Corp. said they agreed to merge through an exchange of common stock valued at $53 million. The two banking companies said a formal merger agreement will be negotiated in the next several weeks. The companies said the number of shares of Southern National stock to be exchanged will vary from 4.8 million to 5.3 million, depending on the price of Southern National stock at the time the exchange ratio is set.

The merger is subject to negotiation of a definitive agreement and approval by federal regulators and shareholders of both corporations.

The principal subsidiary of NBSC is the National Bank of South Carolina. That bank will become part of Southern National Bank of South Carolina, a subsidiary of Southern National.

The transaction, which is expected to be completed in the second quarter of 1991, will make Southern National of Lumberton, N.C., the fifth-largest bank-holding company in North Carolina with $4 billion in assets, while doubling its market share in South Carolina.

NBSC is based in Sumter, S.C.
