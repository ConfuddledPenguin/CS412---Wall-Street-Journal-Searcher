New Securities Issues

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/21/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report. CORPORATE

Premark International Inc. -- $100 million of 10 1/2% notes due Sept. 15, 2000, priced at 99.979 to yield 10.502%. The noncallable notes were priced at a spread of 160 basis points above the Treasury's 10-year note. Rated Baa-2 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and triple-B by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Goldman Sachs & Co.

MUNICIPAL

Metropolitan Transportation Authority, N.Y. -- $165.9 million of transit facilities 1987 service contract bonds and commuter facilities 1987 service contract bonds, Series 4, due July 1, 1992-2003, 2007, 2016 and 2019, tentatively priced by a Goldman Sachs & Co. group to yield from 6.40% in 1992 to 8.13% in 2016. There are $108.4 million of transit facilities 1987 service contract bonds and $57.5 million of commuter facilities 1987 contract bonds. Current interest serial bonds are priced to yield from 6.40% in 1992 to 7.90% in 2003. In the transit facilities service contract issue, there are $12.9 million of 8% term bonds priced at 99.63 to yield 8.03% in 2007, $48.4 million of 7.88% term bonds priced at 97.3 to yield 8.13% in 2016 and $25 million of 7.50% term bonds priced at 93.33 to yield 8.10% in 2019. In the commuter facilities contract issue, there are $6.9 million of 8% term bonds priced at 99.63 to yield 8.03% in 2007, $25.6 million of 7.88% term bonds priced at 97.3 to yield 8.13% in 2016 and $13.2 million of 7.50% term bonds priced at 93.33 to yield 8.10% in 2019. Ratings of single-A/triple-B-Plus are expected.

Student Loan Marketing Association -- $250 million of 8.15% fixed-rate notes due Sept. 18, 1992, priced at par. When-issued trading will begin today. Settlement will be Thursday. Interest payments will be made semiannually beginning March 18, 1991. The notes are sold through a selling group managed by Sallie Mae and are available in book entry form.

Farm Credit System Financial Assistance Corp. -- $89 million of 15-year 9.20% fixed-rate optional principal redemption bonds were priced at par. The bonds may be redeemed in whole or in part on any interest payment date beginning 10 years from the date of issue. These Series B-2005 bonds, the fifth Financial Assistance Corp. issue, mature Sept. 27, 2005. Interest will be paid semiannually on March 27 and Sept. 27. The issue will be dated and delivered Thursday and will be available in book entry form only through the Federal Reserve System. The offering will be made by the Financial Assistance Corp. through a limited selling group of recognized securities dealers and dealer banks. Members of the Federal Farm Credit Banks consolidated systemwide bonds selling group are also eligible to participate through a reallowance.

EUROBONDS

Province of Saskatchewan (Canada) -- 400 million marks of floating-rate notes at the six-month London interbank offered rate plus 0.20 point, priced at par, due October 1997, via CSFB Effektenbank. First Libor fixing Oct. 18. Fees 0.35.

Landwirtschaftliche Rentenbank (West Germany) -- 200 million marks of domestic bonds due Oct. 10, 2000, priced at 100.30, via Deutsche Genossenschaftsbank. Coupon 10 1/4% for first three years and 15 3/4% less six-month Libor for final seven years. Fees 1.70.

British Airports Finance B.V. (U.K. parent) -- 15 billion yen of floating-rate notes due Jan. 25, 1996, priced at par via Daiwa Europe Ltd. Coupon initially at spread of 0.10 point above three-month Libor, then 0.10 point above six-month Libor. Guaranteed by British Airport Authority PLC. Once-only call at par on Jan. 25, 1994. Fees 0.25.

Mutoh Industries Co. (Japan) -- $100 million of Eurobonds due Oct. 4, 1994, with equity-purchase warrants indicating a 5 1/8% coupon at par, via New Japan Securities Ltd. Each $5,000 bond carries one warrant, exercisable from Oct. 25, 1990, through Sept. 15, 1994, to buy shares at an expected premium of 2 1/2% to the closing price when terms are fixed Wednesday. Fees 2 1/4.

Interfinance Credit Nationale (France) -- 500 million French francs of 10 1/4% Eurobonds due May 21, 1993, at issue price of 100 1/4 (plus 151 days' accrued interest) via Credit Commercial de France, to trade interchangeably with 1.5 billion franc issue outstanding. Guaranteed by Credit National. Fees 1 3/8.
