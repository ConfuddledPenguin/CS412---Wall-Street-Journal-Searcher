Credit Ratings: Norwest Corp. Rating On $2.17 Billion Debt Is Lowered by S&P

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/21/90

NEW YORK -- Standard & Poor's Corp. lowered its ratings on about $2.17 billion of debt issued by Norwest Corp. and a unit, citing "the risks associated with Norwest's current strategy of acquiring weaker financial institutions" to expand its banking franchise. S&P said the Minneapolis bank holding concern's pending stock-swap acquisition of lower-rated United Banks of Colorado "will present exceptional challenges to Norwest's management given the sluggish economy {in Colorado} and United Banks' high-risk profile." United Banks' $6.3 billion in assets will represent nearly 20% of Norwest's total after the acquisition is concluded.

The "majority of Norwest units continue to perform well," S&P said, adding that the company's exposure to such high-risk assets as loans for commercial real estate, high-leverage transactions, and loans to lesser developed countries are "modest relative to {its} peers."

S&P lowered its ratings on senior debt of Norwest Corp. and its Norwest Financial Inc. unit to single-A-plus from double-A-minus; reduced subordinated debt of the parent and unit to single-A from single-A-plus; trimmed Norwest Corp.'s preferred stock to single-A-minus from single-A; and lowered commercial paper for Norwest and Norwest Financial to A-1 from A-1-plus.

Norwest chief financial officer John T. Thornton called the downgrading unjustified. "Frankly, we were really disappointed" with the action, which the company found "a little bit surprising," he said. Standard & Poor's "has oriented itself toward the acquisition risk," he said, even though Norwest had stressed to the rating concern the "improvement in our capital ratios" over the past couple of years, and "the extensive due diligence" it carries out prior to its bank acquisitions.
