Pension Fund Gets Women, Minority Money Managers

  • Author: James A. White
  • Date: 09/21/90

The California Public Employees Retirement System, the nation's largest public pension fund, completed a problem-prone search to hire its first outside money-management firms owned by minorities and women. Trustees of the $56 billion California system, known as Calpers, selected 12 "small and emerging" stock investment firms, including five Hispanic-owned firms, two each owned by women, blacks and Asians, and one owned by a manager with multiple sclerosis.

Some of the firms named had owners in two categories. Two managers were chosen on grounds that they were small, even though they weren't owned by minorities or women. Candidate firms were limited to having assets under management of between $20 million and $500 million, compared with Calpers' normal minimum of $500 million in assets for its outside managers.

How much money Calpers, based in Sacramento, Calif., will allot to each winning firm to manage will be decided next month. But the amount is expected to total several hundred million dollars, making the search the largest ever to hire women and minority money managers. Last July, Calpers' sister fund, the California State Teachers Retirement System, awarded $150 million to three minority-owned money managers.

The Calpers search sparked an uproar in June as the original 29 finalists for the business didn't include any black-owned firms. Because of a scoring system later scrapped, all four of the black-owned money-management firms in the field of 85 candidates had been eliminated as finalists by the Calpers staff and two minority and women-owned consulting firms helping in the search.

Critics argued that the absence of black finalists defeated a major reason for the search. In addition, several black-owned firms have been hired by big pension funds, indicating that there were black candidates qualified to reach the Calpers finals.

Rather than scrap the search, Calpers trustees took the highly unusual step of interviewing all 85 candidates during the summer. Of the 12 winning managers, five weren't among the original 29 finalists, meaning they won the Calpers business after the new round of interviews with Calpers trustees.

Firms selected by the Calpers trustees are:

Amerindo U.S. Advisors, Larkspur, Calif.; Brown Capital Management, Baltimore; CIC Asset Management Inc., Beverly Hills, Calif.; First Capital Advisors, New York; Oak Associates, Akron, Ohio; Spare, Tengler, Kaplan & Bischel Inc., San Francisco; Systematic Financial Management, Fort Lee, N.J.; Taplin, Canida & Habacht Inc., Tiffany Capital Advisors, San Ramon, Calif.; Valenzuela Capital Management, New York; Denis Wong & Associates, Honolulu; and Valuequest Ltd., Marblehead, Mass.
