Morgan Stanley Launches Third World Debt Unit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/21/90

NEW YORK -- Morgan Stanley Group Inc., facing a slump in its core business, is seeking profit from a source farther afield: the debt of developing nations. The big securities firm said it launched a unit for developing-country debt and hired several veteran Wall Street executives to run it. It said the unit will focus on trading and securitization of the debt as well as public and private financing in developing countries.

The market for developing-country debt, while volatile and risky, also has profit potential. Lehman Brothers' Developing Country Debt Index, which tracks the value of the commercial bank debt outstanding of 11 nations with a face amount of nearly $250 billion, rose 7.8% in this year's first half.

To help run the new unit, Morgan Stanley brought in a group of executives led by Jay H. Newman who established a similar unit at Dillon, Read & Co., in 1989. Mr. Newman and Keith Fogarty will join Morgan Stanley as managing directors; others in the group include Pierre Biraben, John Foley, Peter Grossman and Gordon Wood. The unit will become part of Morgan Stanley's fixed-income division.

Morgan Stanley is active in privatization of state-owned companies in Europe, Latin America and Asia, and its asset management division runs several developing-country stock funds. In July, the company posted a 48% drop in second-quarter earnings, reflecting significant drops in its core investment-banking and merchant-banking operations.
