CalFed Set to Sell Unit to Conseco Inc. For $140.2 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/21/90

LOS ANGELES -- CalFed Inc. said it will sell its annuity and life insurance subsidiary to Conseco Inc., a Carmel, Ind., insurance firm, for about $140.2 million. As a result of the sale, Calfed said it has established a provision for a loss of about $40 million in the third quarter.

Under the agreement, CalFed will sell Beneficial Standard Life Incurance Company to Conseco in exchange for cash. CalFed will purchase about $25 million of real estate and other assets owned by Beneficial Standard. In addition, in the three months after the sale, CalFed may choose to buy an additional $35 million of other real estate assets that its insurance unit acquired through foreclosure, as well as some nonperforming and other mortgage loans.

About $12 million of the $40 million loss expected involved tax liabilities incurred in the sale, a CalFed spokesman said. The rest of the loss is the result of the company's repurchase of some of Beneficial Standard's assets.

"There will definitely be an impact on earnings," the spokesman said. CalFed will report its third-quarter earnings in mid-October.

The sale is part of CalFed's two-year effort to restructure its financial service business, selling marginally profitable assets and jettisoning side ventures. Since the summer of 1989, its principal subsidiary, California Federal Bank, has sold its $125 million credit card portfolio, and CalFed has sold off several insurance units, a retirement-plan business and its interests in a string of real estate properties.

Last year, Beneficial Standard had a loss of $5.3 million on revenue of $150.4 million. In the first six months of 1990, it had earnings of $3.5 million. Beneficial Standard had $1.8 billion in assets as of June 30, 1990.
