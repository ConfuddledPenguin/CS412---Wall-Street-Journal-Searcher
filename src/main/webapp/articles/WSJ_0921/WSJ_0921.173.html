U.S. Comptroller Expects Further Real Estate Slump

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/21/90

WASHINGTON -- Comptroller of the Currency Robert Clarke said he expected New England real-estate markets to continue to deteriorate and that it "will affect the banks." Mr. Clarke, speaking to reporters, didn't say how much further he expected the New England real-estate markets to erode or how much it would cost the banks. But he said it was "clear these markets are not going to bounce back fast and high."

In general, the comptroller said, "1990 will not be a good year for bank profitability and asset quality" because of real-estate loans and other problem loans related to the slowing economy. He added that the outlook for next year remained unclear.

Mr. Clarke said his agency remained concerned about real-estate loan practices. Though he has been urging bankers to be cautious in their lending, Mr. Clarke said that federal regulators in special audits have continued to find faulty appraisals, inadequate management supervision of loans, and loans with little or no borrower equity.

Separately, Mr. Clarke said the Federal Deposit Insurance Corp. is looking over data on how the proposed increase of insurance premiums paid by banks would affect the banking industry. He said there is at least a possibility that premiums could be increased above the proposed 19.5 cents per $100 of deposits per year if the FDIC is given the authority by legislation.

The House has passed such legislation, and the Senate is expected to take up the matter soon.
