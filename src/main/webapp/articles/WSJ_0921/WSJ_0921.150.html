Arbitrage Activity in Week Is Dominated by 2 Firms

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/21/90

NEW YORK -- Bear Stearns Cos. and Morgan Stanley Group Inc. accounted for about half of all stock-index arbitrage activity on the New York Stock Exchange for the week ended Sept. 7. In its regular weekly report on computer-aided program trading, the Big Board said program trading accounted for 9.8% of average daily volume, or 11.4 million shares a day. That was down from 12.7% the previous week.

Index arbitrage -- rapid-fire purchases and sales of baskets of stocks with offsetting trades in futures and options -- accounted for 53.9% of total program trading by all firms. Bear Stearns used arbitrage in nearly all its program trading; Morgan Stanley used it in slightly more than half.

Overall, the busiest program traders were Morgan Stanley (10 million shares), Bear Stearns (6.8 million), Merrill Lynch & Co. (5.7 million), General Electric Co.'s Kidder, Peabody & Co. (4.3 million) and CS First Boston Inc.'s First Boston Corp. (4.2 million). Bear Stearns and Kidder did most of their trading for their own accounts; the other firms for customers' accounts.

"Buy" programs accounted for an average 3.8 million shares and "sell" programs for 7.6 million, during a week in which the Dow Jones Industrial Average rose 0.2%.

Program trading is the purchase or sale of a basket of at least 15, and often hundreds, of stocks with a value of at least $1 million.
