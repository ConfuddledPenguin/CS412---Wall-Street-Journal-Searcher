Sabena Asks Belgium For a Cash Injection To Finance Expansion

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 09/21/90

BRUSSELS -- Sabena is seeking an injection of six billion Belgian francs ($185.8 million) from the government to finance an ambitious expansion plan, a spokesman for the Belgian carrier said. Separately, the spokesman said Sabena's operating loss for the year will be wider than 1989's loss of 1.26 billion Belgian francs. He declined to elaborate.

According to an article in Le Soir, a Belgian daily, the funds Sabena is seeking would be part of a six-year, 97 billion Belgian franc expansion program that will boost the airline's fleet to 91 aircraft in 1996 from the current 41.

The expansion is related to the formation of Sabena World Airways, an operating subsidiary Sabena has formed with British Airways and KLM Royal Dutch Airlines to convert Brussels into a major European passenger air service hub. British Air and KLM each have a 20% stake in Sabena World Airways, with Sabena holding the remaining 60%.

Sabena is 53%-owned by the Belgian government.

Andre Pahaut, the carrier's chief executive officer, said Sabena would boost fares 5% to 8% starting in October.
