International: Two German Parliaments Ratify Unification Treaty

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/21/90

FRANKFURT, West Germany -- The two German parliaments independently ratified the treaty that will officially unify their nations Oct. 3. But the long day of debate in the West German Bundestag was marked by fierce skirmishes over the costs of unification, particularly on whether new taxes are needed. The question of whether German troops should be allowed to deploy outside the boundaries of the North Atlantic Treaty Organization also generated heated words, as politicians looked beyond Oct. 3 and began to stake out campaign stands for the first all-German elections, on Dec. 2.

The 1,000-page unification treaty details how all functions of the East German state will be united with those of West Germany under one government and parliament. For East Germany's parliament, the Volkskammer, the vote was its last important business before its dissolution in two weeks.

The Bundestag approved the treaty by 442-47; the Volkskammer, by 299-80.
