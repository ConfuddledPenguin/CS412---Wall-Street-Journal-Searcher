Technology: Wang Laboratories Sold Its Intecom Unit For Just $26 Million

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/21/90

LOWELL, Mass. -- Wang Laboratories Inc. disclosed in its annual report that it sold its Intecom subsidiary to the Matra Communications unit of Matra S.A. of Paris for just $26 million. Wang had previously announced the sale, but hadn't divulged the price. It had acquired Intecom, a Richardson, Texas, maker of telephone switchboards, for some $89 million in cash and Wang stock then valued at $140 million. The acquisition, which took place in stages beginning in 1984, was completed in 1986. One former executive who asked not to be identified said that Wang had spent a total of $240 million buying the unit and attempting to make it profitable.

As previously reported, Wang had a net loss of $715.9 million, or $4.35 a share, in fiscal 1990, widened from a net loss of $424.3 million, or $2.59 a share, in fiscal 1989. The loss for fiscal 1990 included a loss on discontinued operations, including Intecom, of $87.3 million. The 1990 loss was over and above a $100.l million loss on discontinued operations in 1989 that included an earlier projection for losses on the sale of Intecom.

Wang sold off Intecom, two financing subsidiaries and some smaller units over the past 12 months to raise cash and pay off bank debt. Richard Miller, who was hired as president in August 1989 to turn around the struggling computer maker said that bank debt now has been cut to $12 million from $575 million in August 1989.

Wang said that total current liabilities have shrunk 10% to $872.1 million and long-term debt was reduced 11% as of June 30. It said that shareholders equity fell 63% to $414.9 million from $1.13 billion.

Wang has been slashing costs and said that operating costs have been cut by about $500 million a year. Employment has been reduced to about 19,500 today from 31,500 in December 1988.

Intecom's buyer, Matra Communications, is 70%-owned by the parent, 20% by Cie. Financiere De Paribas, and 10% publicly held.

Although Wang didn't break out Intecom separately, in the annual report it disclosed that Intecom and Wang Financial Information Services Inc., a much smaller operation that was sold for $16 million, had a combined loss from operations of $18.6 million on revenue of $103.5 million in fiscal 1990.
