Apple Bancorp Ends Its Attempt to Block Stahl Takeover Bid

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/21/90

NEW YORK -- Apple Bancorp, ending a long, contentious battle with Stanley Stahl, said it terminated two trusts it created as its last attempt to block the real estate investor from gaining control of the thrift holding company's board. The move effectively clears Mr. Stahl to complete his tender offer of $38 for each Apple share, or an indicated $179.4 million, and install a majority on Apple's board. Mr. Stahl currently has a 30.3% stake in the company.

Investors, some of whom described the latest news as a "done deal," reacted positively, boosting the price of Apple stock $5.50 to $36.375 a share in composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange yesterday.

But one analyst cautioned that Mr. Stahl could still back out of the offer. "For economic reasons he might not want to proceed," said Reid Nagle, an analyst with SNL Securities.

Separately, Mr. Stahl said in a statement he expects to complete the offer, which expires Oct. 1. As of Sept. 4, about 2.8 million, or about 46% of the shares outstanding had been tendered, giving Mr. Stahl control of about 76% of the company.

A spokesman said Mr. Stahl should have no problem with financing the completion of the offer, which would be in cash that he holds in certificates of deposit.

The announcement followed Mr. Stahl's assertion earlier this week that the trusts violated conditions of his tender offer. Last week, Apple established the trusts to pay for costs that its officers and directors might incur in litigation between the bank and Mr. Stahl, and to compensate employees who might incur costs in enforcing the pacts.

The trusts were the last of several barriers Apple raised in the past year to prevent Mr. Stahl from seeking control of the company without completing the tender offer. Tactics included rescheduling its annual meeting to come after the completion of the tender offer and adopting anti-takeover measures.

Yesterday, Mr. Stahl said he and Apple had begun negotiating adequate assurances that Apple will, simultaneously with the completion of the offer, elect his nominees to form a majority on the board.
