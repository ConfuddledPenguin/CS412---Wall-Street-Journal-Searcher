Business Expectations Hit 7-Year Low in Poll By Dun & Bradstreet

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/21/90

NEW YORK -- Business expectations for sales and profits have hit a seven-year low, the latest quarterly survey by Dun & Bradstreet Corp. indicates. However, Joseph W. Duncan, economist and chief statistician at the business information marketing concern, cautioned that the results "may have been exaggerated by the high levels of anxiety" immediately after the Aug. 2 invasion of Kuwait by Iraq. The survey was conducted from Aug. 12 to Aug. 30. "Until further data become available, the outlook for a recession remains uncertain," he said.

The survey found manufacturers to be the most pessimistic group, with the sales optimism index for the fourth quarter falling to 47 from 66 in the third quarter. Overall, the index fell to 50 from 60, the lowest level since the 1983 first quarter.

The profits optimism index for all companies fell 10 points to 43, with manufacturers' expectations falling 14 points to 44 from 58. The survey found expectations for higher selling prices rising three points to 33.

Dun & Bradstreet's survey interviewed 1,500 executives in manufacturing, retailing and wholesaling. The quarterly survey asks whether executives expect an increase, decrease or no change in measures such as sales, profits, prices and employment. The index reported in each category represents the difference between those expecting an increase and those expecting a decrease.

The employment optimism index fell six points to 11, its lowest level in nearly eight years, while the new orders index among manufacturers fell 16 points to 46.
