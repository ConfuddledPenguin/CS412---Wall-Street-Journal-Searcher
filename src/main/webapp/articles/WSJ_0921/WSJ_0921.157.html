Business Brief -- American Telephone & Telegraph: Telmex Fiber-Optic Contract For $130 Million Is Won

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/21/90

American Telephone & Telegraph Co. made its first major entry into the Mexican market by winning a $130 million contract from Telefonos de Mexico S.A. de C.V., Mexico's national telephone company. AT&T, based in New York, will build a major part of a fiber optic network that will link 54 locations throughout the country, significantly enhancing Mexico's international long-distance infrastructure.

The contract is a coup for AT&T, whose other major contract with Telmex was a $13 million project involving digital access and cross connecting equipment.

AT&T outbid two of Mexico's largest traditional suppliers, L.M. Ericsson of Sweden and Alcatel of France, to win the right to build 60% of the network. Alcatel has been awarded a contract to build 25% of the network and Ericsson 15%.

The Mexican project begins this month and will be completed by 1993. It involves 8,000 miles of fiber-optic cable and related equipment. AT&T and its Spanish subsidiary will provide the cable, components, and transmission equipment. AT&T will also be working with a Mexican engineering and construction firm to provide Telmex with a turnkey system.

The project will create one of the most modern telephone networks in the world, offering high-quality transmission, faster connection speeds and a larger call handling capacity.
