Mastercard Moves To Decentralize, Opens An Office in Singapore

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 09/21/90

SINGAPORE -- MasterCard International took the first step in decentralizing its management, marketing and member-support services when it opened its first regional headquarters in Singapore. The Asia-Pacific office will oversee Hong Kong, Sydney and Singapore -- and two representative offices -- Beijing and New Delhi.

James A. Cassin, president of MasterCard's Asia/Pacific operations, said the office will be the prototype by which subsequent regional headquarters will model themselves.

Between four to six more regional offices are expected to be opened by the end of 1992. These will be for the U.S., Latin America, Europe, the Middle East and Africa, and Canada.

The New York office will then become an umbrella for the overall coordination of each region, being responsible for the broader policy decisions of the company.
