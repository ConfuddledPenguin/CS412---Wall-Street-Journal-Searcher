Letters to the Editor: Work Takes the Wind Out of a Weekend Sailor

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/21/90

Regarding your Aug. 31 Enterprise story "Fiberglass's Long Life Is Sinking Sailboat Producers," I don't agree with the contention that the sailboat industry is suffering from "design constipation" and that all it needs is an infusion of innovative design to free it from diminishing sales figures. My experience from retailing more than 4,000 fiberglass boats and having represented more than 50 national manufacturers the past 20 years that have gone belly up, leads me to somewhat other conclusions. One concerns the significant lifestyle change of the 25-to-40-year-olds. With a majority of the families having both partners working full time, there is not the time to sail on weekends. The careful planning necessary to juggle a weekend family junket aboard a sailboat, such as food preparation, fueling, trip planning, marina reservations, weather, balancing children's lessons and other family commitments, requires hours of time not available anymore to the nonexistent "stay-at-home" member of the family. Making boats go faster or simpler to operate will not bring back the market. Only a return to a former familial life style will, and this is not likely in the near future.

Arthur Rieders

Havencraft of New England Inc.

Sudbury, Mass.
