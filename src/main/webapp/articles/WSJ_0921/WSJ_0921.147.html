Bush Plans to Name Terzic to Energy Panel

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/21/90

WASHINGTON -- President Bush said he will nominate Branko Terzic, vice president of a Milwaukee consulting firm, to fill a vacant seat on the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission. Mr. Terzic, if confirmed by the Senate, would serve until Oct. 20, 1991, filling out the remainder of the term of former chairwoman Martha Hesse, who left late last year.

In addition to being group vice president of AUS Consultants in Milwaukee, Mr. Terzic, a Republican, is chairman of the Wisconsin Racing Board. He also has served a six-year term on the Wisconsin Public Service Commission.

An aide to the Senate Energy Committee said lawmakers hope to act on the nomination before Congress adjourns next month. In that case, Mr. Terzic could be on board for the commission's vote, scheduled for Nov. 28, on the controversial Iroquois natural gas pipeline project. The proposed 369 mile, $582.6 million pipeline is designed to bring Canadian gas to the Northeastern U.S.
