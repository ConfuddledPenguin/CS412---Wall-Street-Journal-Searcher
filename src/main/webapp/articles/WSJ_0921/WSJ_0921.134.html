Who's News: Perkin-Elmer's Chief, McDonnell, Will Retire; Kelley Named to Post

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/21/90

NORWALK, Conn. -- Perkin-Elmer Corp. said its chairman and chief executive officer, Horace G. McDonell, will retire in early November at age 62. Mr. McDonell, who led the company through its most recent restructuring, will be succeeded by Gaynor N. Kelley, 59, president and chief operating officer.

The move comes near the end of the company's most extensive restructuring program, which saw the divestiture of businesses that generated more than $500 million in revenue, most prominently its semiconductor business. That left the maker of analytical instruments more narrowly focused and slightly more profitable.

Mr. Kelley, in an interview, said he doesn't expect to deviate from the course set by Mr. McDonell. "I'm not interested in a diversification strategy," Mr. Kelley said. He noted that his challenge would be to increase sales and profit from the analytical instruments business, which currently generates more than $800 million in annual sales.

But he said he has thought about an "aggressive position on acquisitions" in which he would seek similar businesses.

Some analysts said Mr. Kelley will take a more hands-on approach to management than Mr. McDonell. He will also face tremendous pressure to increase profit for the company, which has had several years of sluggish growth.

Perkin-Elmer said Mr. McDonell, who was elected chairman in 1985, will continue to serve as director after retirement. The company also said it was eliminating the position of chief operating officer, as Mr. Kelley will serve as chairman, president and chief executive.
