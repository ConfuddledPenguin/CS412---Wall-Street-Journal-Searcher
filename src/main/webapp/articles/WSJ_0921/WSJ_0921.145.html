Business Brief -- Asarco Inc.: Mine Expansion Suspended Pending Waste-Site Talks

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/21/90

Asarco Inc., a New York-based nonferrous metals, specialty chemicals and environmental services company, said it temporarily suspended mill construction at its Ray copper mine expansion project in Arizona, pending discussions with state and federal environmental agencies. The company said the issue involves the optimum location of a mine waste-disposal site, part of a $194 million expansion at the 4,500-acre Ray mine in Hayden, Ariz.

Asarco said the mill construction is ahead of schedule. The expansion, which is to be completed in early 1992, will increase production capacity to 182,000 tons of copper a year from 115,000 tons.
