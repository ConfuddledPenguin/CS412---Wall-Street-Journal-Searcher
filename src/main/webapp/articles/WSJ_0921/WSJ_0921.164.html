International Brief -- Guinness PLC: British Brewer's Pretax Net Jumped 31% in First Half

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/21/90

Guinness PLC, the British brewing and drinks group, said pretax profit jumped 31% in the first half of the year, beating both analysts' and the company's expectations for the period. Guinness's rise in pretax profit to #322 million ($606.8 million) from #246 million a year earlier outperformed the market's expectations of between #306 million and #310 million.

Profit after taxes, minority interests and preference dividends rose 31% to #201 million from #154 million a year earlier. Earnings per share rose 30% to 21.9 pence from 16.9 pence.

Net profit attributable to shareholders nearly trebled to #201 million from #69 million. There were no extraordinary items in the latest period, but a year-earlier extraordinary loss of #85 million helped flatter the comparison. The loss related to a payment to shareholders stemming from the illegal share-support mechanism used during the company's bid for United Distillers in 1986.

Guinness increased the interim dividend by 23% to 5.4 pence from 4.4 pence a year earlier.

Sales rose 20% to #1.52 billion from #1.26 billion the previous year.
