Beneficial Unit Charged With Deceiving Borrowers

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/24/90

NEW YORK -- State officials filed suit against a Beneficial Corp. consumer finance subsidiary for allegedly using a "predatory and deceptive" scheme to get borrowers. The unit, Beneficial New York Inc., denied the charges. "We're confident we're within New York state regulations," said Robert Wade, a company spokesman. "I can't say anything beyond that because I haven't seen the complaint," he added.

The suit, filed in state supreme court in Rochester, charges that Beneficial mailed unsolicited checks to consumers around the state for up to $1,500, urging they be cashed immediately.

According to Attorney General Robert Abrams and Banking Superintendent Jill Considine, the documents Beneficial mailed to consumers stated that by cashing the check they agreed to repay the loan. State officials charge that Beneficial deceived customers by calling the interest payments on the loan "low," when in practice the rates charged would be 25%. Under New York state law the maximum rate allowed is 25%, or about double most conventional bank loans.

Mr. Abrams said he is asking the court to suspend repayment of the loans and to bar Beneficial from continuing its marketing campaign.

The Beneficial spokesman said the company would wait to see the complaint before it decided on a course of action.
