U.S. Budget Deficit Grew to $48.05 Billion During November

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/24/90

WASHINGTON -- The federal government's budget deficit totaled $48.05 billion in November, compared with $29.73 billion a year earlier, the Treasury Department said. Last month's budget gap compared with a deficit of $31.46 billion in October. In its monthly statement, the Treasury said receipts totaled $72.82 billion last month, compared with $71.21 billion a year earlier and $78.71 billion in October. Outlays totaled $120.87 billion last month, compared with $100.95 billion a year earlier and $110.17 billion in October.

The slim increase in revenues and the big increase in outlays reflected the slowing economy and increasing costs because of the large U.S. military presence in the Persian Gulf.

For the first two months of fiscal 1991, which began Oct. 1, government receipts totaled $151.53 billion, compared with $139.64 billion during the same period last year. Outlays through November totaled $231.04 billion, compared with $195.45 billion a year earlier. That produced a total deficit of $79.51 billion for the first two months of fiscal 1991, compared with $55.82 billion during the same period in the previous fiscal year. The deficit for all of fiscal 1990 was $220.39 billion.

The government paid $21.88 billion in interest last month on the federal debt.

Revenue from the defense cooperation account, which consists of contributions from U.S. allies to help cover the cost of the U.S. military operation in Saudi Arabia, totaled $2.82 billion in November, compared with $1.63 billion last month, the Treasury said.

Military outlays rose in November to $29.02 billion, compared with $24.09 billion in October. So far in fiscal 1991, military spending has totaled $53.11 billion, up from $43.73 billion for the same period in 1990.
