Allwaste Inc. to Post Charge on Divestiture Of Troubled Unit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/24/90

HOUSTON -- Allwaste Inc. said it plans to divest itself of its troubled asbestos-removal division and report a one-time charge of 13 cents a share for the first quarter ended Nov. 30 as a result of the decision. Part of the charge reflects the anticipated costs of closing two offices in New York and Massachusetts "as soon as practical." Remaining operations will be based in Houston.

The environmental-services concern also said R.L. Nelson, chairman and chief executive, was named to the additional position of president, succeeding Clayton K. Trier, who resigned as president, co-chief executive and a director, to start a consulting practice. Mr. Trier will continue to work with Allwaste as a consultant.

Allwaste said that in addition to the first-quarter charge, it expects to report a loss from discontinued operations of about $2.4 million, or seven cents a share, reflecting after-tax operating losses of the division. The one-time charge and loss will be partially offset by a nine-cents-a-share gain on the previously reported sale of a manufacturing operation.

The company didn't give an earnings estimate for the quarter. In the year-earlier quarter, Allwaste had net income of about $3 million, or 10 cents a share, on revenue of $44.3 million.
