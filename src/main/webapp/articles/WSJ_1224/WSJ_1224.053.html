Kroger Co. to Extend Offert to Swap Bonds

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/24/90

CINCINNATI -- Kroger Co. said it will extend until 5 p.m. Jan. 9 its offer to bondholders to exchange their 15 1/2% junior subordinated discount debentures for a new, cash-interest-paying bond. The offer, announced on Nov. 5, was initially set to expire on Dec. 5, but was extended to last Thursday. This latest move is being made because holders have tendered less than the required $150 million principal amount. Terms call for the supermarket chain to give bondholders between $575 and $660 principal amount of a new 13 1/2% junior subordinated debenture, due 2003, for each $1,000 face value of the 15 1/2% junior subordinated discount debentures distributed as part of the company's 1988 restructuring.

Kroger said it won't extend the time for odd-lot holders, who will receive a cash payment equal to the higher of a $590 minimum, or the price obtained for the debentures when they are sold. Kroger said it expects to begin mailing payments to the odd-lot holders on Jan. 2.
