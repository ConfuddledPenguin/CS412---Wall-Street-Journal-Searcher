Business Brief -- CMS Energy Corp.: Utility Boosts Credit Line Held by Unregulated Units

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/24/90

CMS Energy Corp. said it has boosted the line of credit held by its unregulated energy businesses to meet their higher capital needs next year and to post a bond in a legal proceeding over $1.5 billion of frozen assets. The Dearborn, Mich., electric and gas utility said Friday that it has arranged for a $500 million line of credit with a bank group led by Union Bank and the Citibank unit of Citicorp. The new loan will be used to consolidate credit arrangements with several different banks that had extended it a total of $280 million in credit.

CMS Energy officials said they will spend $125 million of the credit to post a surety bond in their ongoing court case over $1.2 billion in notes and $341 million in equity the company received for its contribution to the Midland Cogeneration Venture. CMS officials said they plan to use the remainder of the funds in their unregulated energy businesses.
