Business Brief -- J.M. Peters Co.: Revenue Declined by 45% In Fiscal Third Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/24/90

J.M. Peters Co. said its fiscal third-quarter revenue fell 45% to $40 million, resulting in a net loss of $7.1 million, or 51 cents a share. The loss included a $3.1 million write-down on undeveloped land the company has agreed to sell. The property is in escrow and the sale is set to close early next year, the company said.

In the third quarter ended Nov. 30, 1989, the Newport Beach, Calif., home building concern had net income of $5.7 million, or 41 cents a share, on revenue of $72.4 million.

The company said its latest results "continue to mirror the downturn in sales for single family move-up housing throughout most of Southern California." Increased finance and marketing costs on the company's unsold homes also contributed to the loss. Gross profit percentages on sales was 8.8% compared with 21.3% a year earlier.

In the first nine months, the company had a net loss of $6 million, or 43 cents a share, compared with year-earlier net income of $14.2 million, or $1.02 a share. Revenue fell 14% to $161.9 million from $188.2 million.
