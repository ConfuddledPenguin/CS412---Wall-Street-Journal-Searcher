Who's News: General Dynamics Taps Webb to Head Its A-12 Program

  • Author: David J. Jefferson
  • Date: 12/24/90

General Dynamics Corp. named the former director of its F-16 fighter program to head its ailing A-12 program, which is in danger of cancellation by the Pentagon as early as Jan. 2. Ted S. Webb, 60 years old, who retired in August 1989 after heading the F-16 program for nine years, will return as program director for the A-12 and as vice president of the Fort Worth, Texas, division.

He succeeds Charles A. Anderson, 58, former general manager of the Fort Worth division who was put in charge of the A-12 just last July. General Dynamics said Mr. Anderson "is being removed and reassigned" as division vice president for special programs and other business, which includes the company's work on the national aerospace plane and indigenous fighters for Japan and Taiwan.

The shake-up, part of an overall shuffling of management duties at the Fort Worth division, comes as General Dynamics and A-12 team partner McDonnell Douglas Corp. are negotiating with the Navy and aides to Defense Secretary Dick Cheney to try to salvage the program, which has been plagued by engineering problems and cost overruns. The Pentagon has threatened to cancel the program unless the two St. Louis defense contractors come up with a plan by Jan. 2 to solve the problems. General Dynamics and McDonnell Douglas each notified 4,000 employees last week that they could be laid off as early as Jan. 2 if the A-12 is canceled.

General Dynamics downplayed the A-12 problems as a cause for the management reorganization at Fort Worth. The change is designed "to concentrate authority and resources in the hands of executives in charge of the individual business groups and then hold them directly responsible for the results," said Herbert F. Rogers, General Dynamics' president and chief operating officer. Mr. Rogers in July moved from corporate headquarters to become general manager of the Fort Worth division in an effort to shore up the A-12, shortly after the company took a $450 million write-off on the program.

As part of the latest changes, General Dynamics has split the Fort Worth division into five business groups, according to its major military programs there. In addition to the appointments of Messrs. Webb and Anderson, Dain M. Hancock, 49, program director for the F-16, was named to head the new F-16 group; D. Randall Kent, 64, program manager for the Advanced Tactical Fighter, will head the new ATF group; and John E. Vick, 57, formerly division vice president for electronic products, will head the newly created military electronics business group.
