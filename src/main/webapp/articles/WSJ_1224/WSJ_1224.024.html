Letters to the Editor: A Christmas Carol to the President

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/24/90

The Ghost of Christmas Past

During last Christmas season, President Bush ordered U.S. forces to invade Panama. More than 700 U.S. and Panamanian soldiers and civilians were killed while Congress enjoyed the holidays. It was not called war.

The Ghost of Christmas Present

The joy is gone from this Christmas. My daughter and son-in-law are in the infantry and are joining 400,000 other troops in the Persian Gulf. They joined the Army together hoping to finish college, and now that choice may cost them their lives in yet another undeclared war.

Mothers must be braver than fathers. I see them on the evening news fighting back tears while saying they support the president. But I am having trouble. Suddenly, issues like control of oil, stable monarchies and shaky coalitions boil down to the love between a father and daughter. My sorrow for her is equaled only by my despair. I may never see her again.

War is more than a military maneuver or a United Nations resolution. For perhaps tens of thousands, it will be the way their lives end. As a former U.S. Army soldier who served through the Berlin Airlift and the Cuban missile crisis, I know the fear of death our troops are feeling. But this is different.

Let there be no doubt about it, if the fuse is lit, it will be war. The president insisted that the U.N. approve the use of force, and he changed our goals from protecting the Saudi king to invading Kuwait and Iraq to wrench Saddam Hussein from power. As a result, a half-million troops on each side are pointing the most destructive weapons ever known at each other across Mr. Bush's line in the Saudi sand.

My greed is at a more personal level than the price of oil or saving Western life styles. I do not want my daughter's name etched in stone like those who died in Vietnam. I believe her destiny holds a higher purpose than restoring Kuwaiti royalty to the throne.

Therefore, I have written her and her husband and urged them not to show up at the battlefield unless Congress declares war. If her blood is to spill, it should taint the hands of Congress as well. Too many presidents have asked us to overlook their abuse of the same Constitution they were elected to uphold. Congress has a legal and moral obligation to the American people to debate the issues before sending our troops to death.

The Ghost of Christmas Yet to Come

If Congress declares war, what comfort will loved ones take next Christmas? Will the youthful souls of the dead return with their mangled bodies? Or will they float like black angels of death over the Jewish Wailing Wall or a Moslem temple or the tomb of Christ, haunting the nations that sent them to die in the desert?

It is to be hoped that, like Ebenezer Scrooge, Congress will awaken from its holiday sleep filled with a new spirit of Christmas, take charge of the Gulf crisis, open negotiations and send the world a message of peace, by voting no to war.

Don Trepagnier

Austin, Texas
