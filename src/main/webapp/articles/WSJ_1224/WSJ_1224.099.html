Carolco Shares Owned by Kassar Frozen by Court

  • Author: Kathleen A. Hughes
  • Date: 12/24/90

LOS ANGELES -- A state court judge here froze 2.2 million Carolco Pictures Inc. shares owned by the company's chairman, Mario Kassar, and limited Mr. Kassar's ability to draw funds from the independent movie production company. Judge John Zerbrowski took the action at a Thursday hearing of a shareholder's suit that charged Mr. Kassar with self-dealing in a $45 million stock transaction with Carolco last September.

In the transaction, Carolco agreed to buy back about 3.4 million shares from Mr. Kassar at $13 a share. At the time, the shares were trading at about $7.25 on the New York Stock Exchange. The company didn't offer an explanation for the much higher price. The buy-back was completed last month.

On Friday, Carolco closed at $8.375 a share, down 25 cents, in New York Stock Exchange composite trading.

Carolco, one of the few remaining independent movie companies, has a reputation for paying some of the highest fees in Hollywood to writers, directors and actors. The company is known for producing a string of action movies. It released the high-budget "Total Recall," starring Arnold Schwarzenegger, last summer.

In his order Thursday, Judge Zerbrowski said the evidence suggested "self-dealing" and a breach of fiduciary duties. In addition to freezing 2.2 million shares, the judge froze one million warrants held by Mr. Kassar and ordered Carolco not to approve any loans or other financial transactions with Mr. Kassar without court review.

Mr. Kassar founded Carolco with Andrew Vajna about 14 years ago, and the two ran the company as co-chairmen. Mr. Vajna left the company in November 1989, and Mr. Kassar purchased his 36% stake, or 11.2 million shares, for about $108 million, or $9.60 a share. Based on that price, Mr. Kassar made a profit of about $11.5 million when he sold the smaller number of shares to the company. The Carolco buy-back left Mr. Kassar with 15.1 million shares, or a 57% stake in the company.

After buying the shares from Mr. Vajna, Mr. Kassar also sold about 3.8 million shares to four institutional investors at prices ranging from $10.50 to $11.50 a share.

Carolco officials declined to comment on the Thursday action. In a statement, the company said the preliminary action "has no effect on the company and its operations." Carolco said the transaction was approved by its independent committee and its board and that the company received support from institutional and other large holders representing a majority of the shares not owned by Mr. Kassar.

As a result, Carolco said it believes that its "view of this case is correct" and that "the courts will agree once they have had the opportunity to review all the facts."
