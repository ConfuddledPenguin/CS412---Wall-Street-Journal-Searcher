Politics & Policy: Election Complaint Against Pro-Israel Group Dismissed

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/24/90

WASHINGTON -- The Federal Election Commission dismissed a complaint that a pro-Israel lobbying group illegally coordinated political contributions. The American Israel Public Affairs Committee, one of the most powerful lobbying groups here, had been accused of violating federal campaign-spending limits by secretly coordinating activities of at least 27 pro-Israel political action committees nationwide. A group of retired U.S. diplomats led by former Undersecretary of State George Ball, along with former Illinois Rep. Paul Findley, had filed a complaint with the FEC in January 1989, charging the groups were "affiliated" with AIPAC in a network that should be regarded as a single political action committee.

But the FEC, in a statement Friday, found unanimously "there is insufficient evidence to indicate that AIPAC and the respondent PACS are affiliated."

The FEC said that the 27 PACS received data regarding congressional races from AIPAC, and that their contributions followed a similar pattern. But FEC concluded there was no collusion between them and AIPAC. "It appears that each respondent PAC makes independent decisions regarding which candidates to support," the FEC said.

In its filings to FEC, AIPAC said, "No one directs American Jews to become active . . . in political campaigns, or to give to candidates, or to form political organizations of their own."
