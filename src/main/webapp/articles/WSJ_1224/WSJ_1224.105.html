International: Philips Injects Funds Into Video Company, Boosts Stake to 21%

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/24/90

BRUSSELS -- The Belgian unit of N.V. Philips said it agreed to pump at least four billion Belgian francs ($128 million) into Super Club N.V., a Belgian operator of video and music stores. Philips said the injection would be made in the form of equity and convertible debt. Philips's stake in Super Club would rise to about 21% from 12%. "This will strengthen Super Club's financial position by January 1991," Philips said.

Both sides denied, however, that the move was a rescue operation. Charles Cool, a Super Club director, said it was too early to say how the money would be used.

The investment surprised analysts because Philips, an electronics giant based in the Netherlands, is suffering from severe financial problems and heavy debt. The company is eliminating nearly 20% of its work force world-wide and has warned that its loss for 1990 will total about four billion Dutch guilders ($2.4 billion).

"I don't think it's the opportune time" to make such an investment, said Luc van Lent, an analyst at Swiss Bank Corp. in London.

But the Dutch company said Super Club's activities "fit in perfectly with Philips's strategy." A Philips spokesman said his company, which owns 80% of PolyGram N.V., a recorded-music company, wants to expand further in software to go with the consumer-electronics hardware made by Philips.

Super Club had 1989 net income of about $38 million on sales of $349 million.
