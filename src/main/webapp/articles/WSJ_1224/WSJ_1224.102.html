Harsco Pact With Army

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/24/90

HARRISBURG, Pa. -- Harsco Corp. said it has reached an agreement with the U.S. Army to modify a contract that allows the company to produce fewer five-ton trucks in exchange for dismissing certain claims against the government. The industrial concern said cost problems related to its five-ton truck contract had required it to build loss reserves totaling $60 million. Harsco said it doesn't anticipate having to add to its reserves as a result of the new agreement.

The new agreement calls for the company to provide the government with 17,092 vehicles, as opposed to the original contract calling for 21,000 trucks. Harsco said it has already built and delivered 10,000 of those trucks.

In return, Harso has agreed to drop certain claims, representing about $55 million, against the government. However, the company said it still continues to pursue claims in excess of $55 million.
