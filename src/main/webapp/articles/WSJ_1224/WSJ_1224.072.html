Reservation Rules for Airlines

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/24/90

WASHINGTON -- The Transportation Department extended the rules governing airline computer reservation systems to Nov. 30, 1991, to allow for further study. The rules, which were to expire next Monday, are intended to foster competition. They include requirements that participation in the systems be open to all carriers on a nondiscriminatory basis and that schedule information be organized in an unbiased manner.

Critics of the rules -- including the Justice Department, travel agents and some airlines -- contend that anti-competitive practices still flourish.
