Business Brief -- Ashland Oil Co.: Refiner to Report a Deficit For the Fiscal First Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/24/90

Ashland Oil Inc., citing depressed petroleum refining margins, said it expects to post a loss for its first quarter ending next Monday. In the year-earlier fiscal first period, the Ashland, Ky., oil refiner earned $22 million, or 40 cents a share, on revenue of $2.04 billion.

"Refining margins have been depressed and declined throughout the quarter, as petroleum markets have tried to adjust to the volatility brought on by the Persian Gulf crisis," Ashland said. Also, it said significant maintenance turarounds were performed at two of the company's refineries during the quarter. Ashland said demand for gasoline and heating oil dropped considerably in October and November, which hurt refinery margins. Though crude oil prices have declined in recent weeks, they haven't fallen as far or as quickly as product prices, Ashland said.

Ashland said it "remains cautiously optimistic" about the outlook for fiscal 1991 ending Sept. 30, but it wasn't more specific.
