Pentagon Will Investigate Allegations Concerning Jet

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/24/90

WASHINGTON -- Defense Department officials "will look into" allegations that the Air Force may have deliberately misled Congress about a 1986 safety inspection of some Lockheed Corp. C-5B cargo jets, a Pentagon spokesman said. Rep. John D. Dingell (D., Mich.) asked the Pentagon to investigate the actions of Lockheed, the Air Force, and a former Air Force procurement chief, after evidence surfaced suggesting that only a cursory inspection was performed.

The evidence was a written statement by Lawrence B. Welborn, a retired lieutenant colonel, which apparently was obtained by plaintiff attorneys in connection with related litigation against Lockheed. Mr. Welborn said the inspection team spent about one hour examining only one aircraft.

An Air Force spokesman said he couldn't comment on allegations that might become the subject of an investigation.

In a statement, Calabasas, Calif.-based Lockheed said that "in five years of very demanding service with the Air Force, there have been no incidents suggesting any deficiency in C-5B mainframes." Lockheed also said it has "absolutely no reason to doubt" the Air Force testimony about its 1986 safety inspection.
