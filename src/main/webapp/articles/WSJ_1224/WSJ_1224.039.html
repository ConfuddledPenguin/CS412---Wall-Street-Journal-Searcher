U.S. Firms Allowed To Import Iranian Oil On Case-by-Case Basis

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/24/90

WASHINGTON -- U.S. companies are being allowed to import Iranian oil on a case-by-case basis, but payments for the oil must go to an escrow fund for U.S. claims against Iran. A State Department spokesman said that under a one-month-old ruling, U.S. companies may apply for a license to import the oil, as permitted under a 1987 Treasury Department regulation allowing certain transactions with Iran. Previously, a trade embargo prohibited U.S. companies from buying Iranian oil.

The escrow fund was established in 1981 as part of the Iran-U.S. Claims Tribunal, which arbitrates commercial disputes between the two nations. The security account is used to pay awards determined by the tribunal to the U.S. government for claims, the department spokesman said.

The Iranian government is obligated to keep the security account funded at $500 million or higher, the department said. Since the fund was established, $2 billion in claims have been paid from the fund to U.S. claimants.

"If such transactions occur, this will help insure there are adequate funds available to pay tribunal awards to U.S. claimants," the spokesman said.

The decision was made last month, and approved by President Bush. The State Department spokesman didn't know if any U.S. company had taken advantage of the ruling.
