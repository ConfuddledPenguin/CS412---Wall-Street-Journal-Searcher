International: Japanese Panel Proposes Raising Fines for Cartels

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/24/90

TOKYO -- An advisory panel to Japan's Fair Trade Commission recommended that fines against large companies engaged in illegal cartels be raised to 6% of relevant sales from the current level of about 1.5%. The recommendation follows Japan's pledge during the Structural Impediments Initiative trade talks with the U.S. earlier this year to strengthen the country's weak anti-monopoly regulations.

The proposed 6% level would still leave Japan's anti-monopoly surcharges below those typically charged in the U.S. and the European Community. The U.S. also wants Japan to strengthen criminal enforcement against illegal cartels and to make it easier for private parties to bring civil suits against companies engaged in cartels.
