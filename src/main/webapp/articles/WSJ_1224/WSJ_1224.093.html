France's Foreign Trade Deficit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/24/90

PARIS -- France's foreign trade deficit narrowed significantly to a preliminary, seasonally adjusted 601 million francs ($117.1 million) in November from a revised 6.69 billion franc gap in October, which had been estimated earlier at 5.87 billion francs, the government said. The latest result came in well below the previous forecasts of Paris market economists for a November deficit of about 7.6 billion francs.

Unadjusted November data showed a deficit of 1.7 billion francs, after a revised October deficit of 6.77 billion francs.

For the first 11 months, France's adjusted trade deficit fell to 44 billion francs from the year-earlier deficit of 44.51 billion francs. The cumulative deficit was 39.84 billion francs on an unadjusted basis, compared with 41.65 billion francs a year earlier.
