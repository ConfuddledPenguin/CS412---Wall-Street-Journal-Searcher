Iraq's Loans Are Placed On a Nonaccrual Status

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/24/90

WASHINGTON -- The World Bank placed all its $51.4 million of loans to Iraq on nonaccrual status, signifying that nation is more than six months overdue on interest payments. Nonaccrual means that the World Bank will now only count Iraqi interest payments it receives and won't automatically accrue payments that are due.

As the international agency noted, Iraq's assets have been frozen since August, when it invaded Kuwait. It has therefore been unable to make payments to the World Bank.

Altogether, Iraq now has about $10.2 million of overdue payments, the World Bank said, consisting of $7.1 million of principal and $3.1 million of interest payments and other charges.
