Business Brief -- United Telecommunications Inc.: Sprint Offers New Service For International Callers

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/24/90

US Sprint, a unit of United Telecommunications Inc., said it began an international telephone calling service that would provide significant savings over its regular rates. Called Sprint WORLD, the plan offers simplified low flat rates for customers making calls to 36 international regions from anywhere in the U.S., the concern said. A spokeswoman said the regions include most major cities.

A caller in the U.S. who subscribes to the services would be able to talk to a person in Paris for about 59 cents a minute during Sprint's discount and economy calling periods: The rates don't apply during peak business hours. That compares to the normal rate of $1.38 for the first minute and 79 cents for each additional minute if the same person was calling during the discount calling period.

The Kansas City, Mo., company said subscribers would be billed a monthly fee of $3 for the service, although the fee would be waived for the first three months of service for those who sign up before Feb. 28.

"International calling is the fastest growing segment of the industry," said Steve Akin, president of Sprint's consumer services group. "It has been the most widely requested new service by our customers."
