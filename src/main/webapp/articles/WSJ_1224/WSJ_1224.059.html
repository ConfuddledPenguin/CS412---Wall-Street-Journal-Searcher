Reichmans Reach Pact To Buy Rest of Shares Of GW Utilities Ltd.

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/24/90

TORONTO -- GW Utilities Ltd. said its board agreed to the terms of a 125.7 million Canadian dollar (U.S.$108.5 million) proposal by Toronto's Reichmann family to buy out the company's minority shareholders. The offer was made by the Reichmann's closely held holding company, Olympia & York Developments Ltd., which already owns 89.3% of GW Utilities. Olympia & York has offered C$30 a share. GW Utilities shareholders also will receive a special dividend of C$10 a share following the sale of the company's 82% stake in Consumers' Gas Ltd. to British Gas PLC.

Separately, British Gas said it has completed the acquisition of 99% of the shares of Consumers' Gas, a natural gas distributor based in Toronto. The British Gas bid closed Thursday.

The buy-out of GW Utilities is subject to the approval of the minority shareholders at a meeting scheduled for next March.

The buy-out also is conditioned on regulatory approval and the completion of a proposed reorganization of Olympia & York's energy holdings. The company has proposed to spin off Interprovincial Pipe Line Ltd. and Home Oil Co. as separate public companies by the first quarter of 1991.

Interprovincial and Home Oil are units of Calgary, Alberta-based Interhome Energy Inc., which is, in turn, owned 40.7% by GW Utilities, 22.8% by Gulf Canada Resources Ltd., and 0.5% by Olympia & York. Olympia & York controls Gulf Canada.
