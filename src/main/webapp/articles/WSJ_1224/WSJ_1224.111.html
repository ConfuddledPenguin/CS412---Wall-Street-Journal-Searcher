Business Brief -- Carnival Cruise Lines Inc.: Company Estimates Profit Increased for Fiscal 1990

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/24/90

Carnival Cruise Lines Inc. expects to report per-share earnings for the fiscal year ended Nov. 30 of about $1.50 to $1.55, up from $1.44 in the prior year, Micky Arison, chairman and chief executive officer, said. For fiscal 1989, the Miami company had net income of $193.6 million on revenue of $1.15 billion.

Despite greater capacity -- this year the company has added one new ship and a greater number of "berths," or beds, on another -- Carnival's bookings are currently at about the same percentage of occupancy as they were this time last year, he said. He wouldn't be specific, however.

The trouble spot was the company's resort and casino business in Nassau, the Bahamas, which "performed below our expectations," Mr. Arison said.

Despite the weakening economy in the fourth quarter, Carnival's occupancy rates, an important measure of a cruise company's performance, were "approximately the same, maybe a little better" than the year-ago quarter, Mr. Arison said.
