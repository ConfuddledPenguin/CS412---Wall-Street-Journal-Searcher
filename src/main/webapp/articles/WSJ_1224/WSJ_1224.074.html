Kemet Electronics Buy-Out

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/24/90

DANBURY, Conn. -- Union Carbide Corp. said Kemet Electronics Corp., in which it holds a 50% stake, will be sold for $235 million to an investor group that includes Kemet management. Union Carbide had been trying since September to divest itself of its stake in Kemet, an electronic capacitor maker, as part of its plan to stick to its core businesses in chemicals and industrial gases. It expects a fourth-quarter earnings gain of about $29 million, or 21 cents a share, from the sale.

The remaining 50% of Kemet, based in Greenville, S.C., is held by General Electric Co.'s GE Capital Corp. and Kemet management.

In the third quarter, Union Carbide had net income of $94 million, or 63 cents a share.
