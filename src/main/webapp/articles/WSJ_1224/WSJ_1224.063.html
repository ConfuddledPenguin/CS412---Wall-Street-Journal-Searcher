Credit Ratings: Allied-Signal Debt Put On Review by S&P

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/24/90

NEW YORK -- Standard & Poor's Corp. said it is reviewing the debt ratings of Allied-Signal Inc. with the possibility of a downgrade. The review affects the ratings of Allied-Signal's single-A senior debt, preliminary single-A Rule 415 shelf registration senior debt and A-1 commercial paper. About $1.9 billion in securities are involved, S&P said.

The credit-rating agency said it was taking the action following a decision by Allied-Signal's 40%-owned Union Texas Petroleum Holdings Inc. to discontinue efforts to sell the entire company and instead place individual parts on the auction block.

S&P said the move "creates uncertainty over timing and value of potential proceeds." Allied-Signal was planning to use the proceeds from the sale of Union Texas to reduce debt incurred during its stock repurchase program.
