House Panel Reports Finding No CIA Link With Thrift Failures

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/24/90

WASHINGTON -- A congressional committee said it found no evidence to support claims that the Central Intelligence Agency was involved with savings-and-loan failures. The House Intelligence Committee said Friday that it was closing a nine-month preliminary inquiry into allegations that the CIA had fraudulently used S&L funds, among other claims. Those charges, the committee concluded, were "based primarily on second-hand information."

The conclusions were disclosed in a letter to Rep. Frank Annunzio (D., Ill.), chairman of a House banking subcommittee. He sought the investigation after a series of articles in the Houston Post that alleged the CIA or organized crime was involved in the failures of 26 thrifts.

The Intelligence Committee said it investigated three specific charges: that CIA operatives used connections with S&Ls to fraudulently obtain loans, that the agency used S&L funds to support covert operations and that the CIA intervened with federal prosecutors to deter them from pursuing particular cases.

The committee said it interviewed several individuals who claimed to know others who had information about CIA involvement in S&L failures. But either those parties "refused to identify the source, or the source refused to be interviewed," said the letter, signed by chairman Anthony Beilenson (D., Calif.).

Federal prosecutors denied that the CIA had attempted to quash any S&L prosecutions. "The federal prosecutors and law enforcement personnel interviewed were quite clear on that point," Mr. Beilenson said.

The congressional investigators said the CIA did use routine financial services at four of the S&Ls named by the Houston Post. "Those were consistent with routine agency practices," Mr. Beilenson said.
