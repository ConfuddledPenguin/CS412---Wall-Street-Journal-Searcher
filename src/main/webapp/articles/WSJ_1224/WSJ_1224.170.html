Southland Must Hold New Creditor Vote On Reorganization

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/24/90

DALLAS -- A federal bankruptcy judge ordered Southland Corp. to solicit a new vote of its creditors on the company's reorganization plan, which had been approved by most creditors before Southland sought bankruptcy-court protection in October. Attorneys for the company and a group of dissident bondholders who had objected to the earlier vote said they don't expect the new vote to change the plans of Southland's Japanese partners to buy a 70% stake in the company. The sale to the Japanese is the key provision of Southland's reorganization plan.

"We don't believe the Japanese will be scared off by this," said Paul Silverstein, an attorney for the dissident bondholders who sought the new vote. He said the creditors he represents hope to persuade other creditors that the cash and notes being offered by the Japanese "should be enhanced."

The dissident bondholders objected to the original vote on Southland's reorganization plan because Southland "gave minimal time" for creditors to consider the plan, said Mr. Silverstein, of the New York law firm of Milgrim Thomajan & Lee.

Michael Sutherland, an attorney for Southland, said the company doesn't view the new vote as a "negative or a setback." The new vote was ordered by Judge Harold Abramson of Dallas.

"This will give us a means to show {the judge} the broad base of creditor support for the plan," said Mr. Sutherland, a lawyer with the Houston-based firm of Vinson & Elkins. "This will help us to remove whatever doubt remains in his mind."

The company hasn't yet decided how the new vote will be solicited. A hearing on the voting procedure is scheduled for Thursday before Judge Abramson, according to Mr. Sutherland.
