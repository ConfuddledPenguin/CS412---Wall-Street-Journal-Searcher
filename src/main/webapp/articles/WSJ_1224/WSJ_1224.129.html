Business Brief -- Triangle Corp.: Tools Unit Will Be Sold In Effort to Reduce Debt

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/24/90

Triangle Corp. said it is selling its special tools business and issued a tender offer for debentures as part of a restructuring aimed at reducing debt load. The Stamford, Conn., company said it expects to report a net loss for the fourth quarter and year because of failure to close a sale as scheduled.

Sale of the special tools business to SPX Corp., Muskegon, Mich., for $12 million is expected to close early next year. Triangle will use $7 million to reduce bank debt.

Triangle is also seeking to arrange a new long-term senior credit facility -- a move that is currently prohibited by covenants in its senior debentures. To modify that provision, Triangle proposed to purchase 51% of the 13.75% debentures, or at least $12.8 million principal amount, at $600 plus accrued interest for each $1,000 principal amount.

The company didn't complete the sale of a fuel injector cleaning equipment product line to Parker Automotive Corp. because of a breach of the purchase and sale agreement.

In the 1989 fourth quarter, Triangle had a loss of $120,000, or four cents a share, on sales of $21.3 million. In 1989, it had a loss of $931,000 or 36 cents a share, on $70.2 million in sales.
