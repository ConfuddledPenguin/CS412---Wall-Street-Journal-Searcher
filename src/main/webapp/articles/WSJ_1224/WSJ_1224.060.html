CrossLand Proposal To Convert to Equity Cleared by Regulators

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/24/90

NEW YORK -- CrossLand Savings said it reached a long-sought agreement with federal regulators that allows it to convert debt and preferred stock to equity in order to raise its capital levels. The agreement follows an earlier rejection by regulators, who had wanted CrossLand to do more than convert the cumulative preferred to raise capital. It also comes at a time when the viability of several major banks and thrifts in the Northeast, including CrossLand, has been questioned as they continue to post substantial losses and carry high amounts of bad real estate loans.

CrossLand, one of the nation's largest thrifts with assets exceeding $11 billion, said the Office of Thrift Supervision has asked it to submit a plan within 45 days for raising its core capital level to 3% of assets by the end of 1991 and 5% by the end of 1994.

Peter Cunningham, senior vice president of CrossLand, said, "We've worked for some time" with the OTS on the plan, and the thrift expects to have the plan submitted in about two weeks. The 3% level is standard for thrifts, he said, but CrossLand will increase to 5% because of the higher level of risk associated with its loan portfolio.
