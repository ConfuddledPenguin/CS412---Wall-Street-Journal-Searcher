International Brief -- Bridgestone Corp.: Japanese Tire Maker Lowers 1990 Profit, Sales Estimates

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/24/90

Bridgestone Corp. of Japan, a tire maker, lowered its forecasts for 1990 net income and consolidated sales. Bridgestone said 1990 profit will amount to 10 billion yen ($74.1 million), which is off 50% from the company's initial prediction in March of 20 billion yen. Sales are expected to reach 1.78 trillion yen ($13.19 billion), or 1.1% less than the initial forecast of 1.8 trillion yen.

In 1989, Bridgestone earned 9.6 billion yen on sales of 1.69 trillion yen.

For its latest revision, Bridgestone cited soft market conditions in North America and Europe, with weak customer demand in the replacement-tire market and decreased new-auto production. It said the lower forecasts also were prompted by the suspension of natural rubber operations in Liberia, as a result of that country's civil war, and increased production costs owing to a rise in raw materials prices.
