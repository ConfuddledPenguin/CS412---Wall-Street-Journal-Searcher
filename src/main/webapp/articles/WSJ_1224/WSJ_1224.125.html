Business Brief -- Curtiss-Wright Corp.: U.S. Files $22 Million Suit Against Unit Over Contracts

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/24/90

Curtiss-Wright Corp.'s Target Rock Corp. unit said it was advised that the U.S. government filed a complaint against it seeking $22 million and other penalties in connection with military contracts. Target, based in East Farmingdale, N.Y., said the complaint alleges that former employees embezzled funds from the unit and as a result "damaged" the U.S. government.

The government alleges that the employees made false travel and expense reports, inflated invoices from suppliers and mischarged labor hours to government subcontractors, the company said.

Target, which makes engineered valves for use in U.S. Navy nuclear propulsion systems, said the "information currently available to the company would not, in its opinion, substantiate a claim for an amount even approaching the sum claimed." Target said it intends to fight the suit "vigorously."

A spokesman said the company believes the government charges result from an investigation conducted by Curtiss-Wright, Lyndhurst, N.J., in March 1987. The probe resulted in the indictment and guilty pleas of the former employees and vendors. The company wasn't charged.
