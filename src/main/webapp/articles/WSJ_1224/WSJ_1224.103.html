Merrimac Holders Clear Change

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/24/90

WEST CALDWELL, N.J. -- Merrimac Industries Inc. said shareholders approved a change in its charter that eliminated cumulative voting in the election of its directors. The change, approved at a special shareholders meeting, means that each shareholder will be entitled to one vote per common share. It represents a setback for Matec Corp., Merrimac's largest shareholder with a 16.2% stake, which had sought to enhance its voting power.

Matec, based in Hopkinton, Mass., makes instruments for industrial research and military operations. Merrimac makes signal processing equipment.
