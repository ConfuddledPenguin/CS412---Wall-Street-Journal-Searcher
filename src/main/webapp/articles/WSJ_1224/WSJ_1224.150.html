Business Brief -- Northeast Bancorp: Company Expects to Report A `Sizable' 4th-Quarter Loss

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/24/90

Northeast Bancorp, Stamford, Conn., hurt by further deterioration in its loan portfolio in October and November, said it expects to report a "sizable loss" for the fourth quarter. It said the loss would "approximate" the loss of $25.8 million, or $3.27 a share, recorded in the year-earlier quarter.

Northeast Bancorp said the loss will result "principally" from a boost in loan-loss reserves to cover increases in nonperforming assets and charges against bad loans.

"The increase in nonperforming assets since the third quarter reflects the severity of the economic decline in Connecticut, which is affecting all business sectors as well as consumers," said Frank J. Kugler Jr., chairman and chief executive officer.

He said that based on year-end projections, "our capital ratios will be well above regulatory minimums."
