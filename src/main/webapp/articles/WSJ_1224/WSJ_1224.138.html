Mulroney Sees Big Changes From Canadians' Demands

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/24/90

OTTAWA -- Prime Minister Brian Mulroney said Canada's federal system must undergo "substantial change" to meet the demands of Quebecers and other Canadians. He said at a news conference that "the alternative to a fundamentally reformed Canada is probably no Canada."

Mr. Mulroney didn't indicate what specific changes might be made in the federal system. But he said he is confident that "we will be able to devise a more modern, flexible brand of federalism that will accommodate the desires and aspirations of all Canadians."

The largely French-speaking province of Quebec is preparing demands for more autonomy, backed by polls showing record support for Quebec's gaining independence. In western Canada, protest movements are demanding constitutional changes to give western provinces greater influence in Canada's Parliament.

Mr. Mulroney said Quebec separatists would like a vote on Quebec independence immediately. They want "to strike while the iron appears to be hot," he said, but added, "It isn't going to work that way."

He said the federal government will deal in an orderly way with the constitutional demands. He attributed the current constitutional grievances to the failure of the provinces to ratify the Meech Lake constitutional accord earlier this year. The proposed accord would have granted new powers to the provinces and recognized Quebec as a "distinct society" within Canada.
