Crying Fowl: Chicago Merc To Trade Chicken Futures

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/24/90

CHICAGO -- The Chicago Mercantile Exchange said that it received regulatory approval to begin trading broiler-chicken futures contracts. The exchange said it expects to begin trading the contracts, each of which represent 40,000 pounds of broilers, on Feb. 7, 1991.

A previous version faded into dormancy in 1982 because traders were frustrated with its cumbersome delivery provisions. To fix that, the Merc devised a contract that doesn't require physical delivery of the underlying broilers.

Instead, the new contract will use an unusual cash settlement system based on the Agriculture Department's 12-city average price report on Grade A broilers.

Contracts will be traded for delivery in February, April, May, June, July, August, October and December.

As approved by the Commodity Futures Trading Commission, the speculative limit is 2,000 broiler contracts in all contract months combined. Speculators are limited to 500 contracts in any specific contract month.

The exchange slashed its original proposal for a speculative limit of an enormous 10,000 contracts in all trading months combined after the CFTC raised questions.

The CFTC is still considering a Merc proposal to trade options on the broiler futures contracts.
