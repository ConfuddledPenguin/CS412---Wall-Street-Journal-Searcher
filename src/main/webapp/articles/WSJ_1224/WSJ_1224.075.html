Horn & Hardart Co. Says It Plans to Exit Restaurant Business

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/24/90

WEEHAWKEN, N.J. -- Horn & Hardart Co., best-known for creating the American automat cafeteria, is getting out of the restaurant business. The company said it will sell its last restaurant business, International King's Table, to focus on its direct-mail marketing business.

Last Friday, the company said it will be taking a significant fourth-quarter charge resulting from its divestiture of the restaurant business, which it plans to complete by the end of March. International King, which operates 83 buffet-style restaurants in the West coast, had a $5.2 million net loss in the first nine months of this year.

The divestiture marks Horn & Hardart's exit from its once core restaurant business in favor of Hanover Direct Inc., its direct-marketing concern. In the past year, the company has sold or is trying to sell nearly all of its East Coast restaurant holdings, including Bojangles' Fried Chicken 'N Biscuits, Tony Roma's Italian restaurants in New York and Arby's in southern Florida.

According to Jack E. Rosenfeld, president and chief executive of Horn & Hardart, the company decision to change its line of business was motivated by continuing losses in its diverse restaurant holdings. "With 10 years of trying many facets of the restaurant business, the company wasn't able to make any kind of return on it," he said.

For the third-quarter, Horn & Hardart reported a net loss of $3.2 million, or 23 cents a share, compared with a net loss of $1.6 million, or 12 cents a share, the previous year.

Hanover Direct is expected to report a 44% increase in revenue this year to $550 million from $382 million in 1989.
