Who's News: American Savings Bank

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/24/90

NEW YORK -- American Savings Bank said William G. Lillis, president and chief executive officer, will retire at the end of October 1991. A committee was named to begin a search for a successor. Mr. Lillis, who has been president for 10 years, is stepping down as a "back-to-basics" strategy that American Savings recently initiated goes into effect. Following a second-quarter $40 million loss posted last July, reflecting an increase in reserves against possible loan losses, the bank said it would consolidate its Riverhead Savings operations and pursue the sale of other subsidiaries to cut costs and pare assets.
