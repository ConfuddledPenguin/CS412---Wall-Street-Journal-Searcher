Business Expectations On Sales, Profits, Jobs Plunge, Survey Finds

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/24/90

NEW YORK -- Business expectations for sales, profits and employment for the first quarter of 1991 are at their lowest levels in eight years, according to Dun & Bradstreet Corp. In its latest quarterly survey of 1,500 business executives nationwide, D&B found that business optimism, already rattled during the fourth quarter by the Middle East situation, has fallen off even more for early 1991.

Business expectations were down across the U.S, with the retail sector reporting the sharpest drop in optimism. For all firms, the sales optimism index fell 14 points to 36, the lowest level since 1982. Retailers' expectations plummeted 30 points to 22, compared with 52 in the fourth quarter of 1990. On a regional basis, the West North Central region reported the sharpest decline in sales optimism, down 26 points to 36.

Major industries across the U.S. expect first-quarter profits to be down, the survey revealed. Dun & Bradstreet's profits optimism Index fell nine points to 34. By industry, retailers are again the gloomiest, with their expectations down 17 points to 23 from 40 in the fourth quarter of 1990. By region, New England reported the sharpest decline in profits optimism, down 21 points to 18.

On the employment front, industries agree the prospects aren't bright. The employment optimism index for the first quarter of 1991 fell three points to eight, the lowest level in eight years. Only the services sector's optimism remains unchanged from the previous quarter's survey. On a regional basis, first-quarter employment expectations were mixed, with only the East South Central states reporting much hope.

Dun & Bradstreet, a marketer of business information and related services, has been conducting the Survey of Business Expectations since 1947. The latest survey, conducted last month, asked 1,500 executives in manufacturing, retailing and wholesaling if they expected an increase, decrease or no change in first-quarter sales, profits, prices, inventories, employment, exports and new orders.
