Business Brief -- Sears, Roebuck & Co.: Firm's Canada Unit to Take A Charge in 4th Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/24/90

Sears Canada Inc., the 62%-owned unit of Sears, Roebuck & Co. of the U.S., will report a fourth-quarter charge related to its recently completed voluntary staff-reduction program, said Joseph Reddington, president and chief executive officer of the Toronto retailer. "More people than were anticipated actually did accept the voluntary severance package," Mr. Reddington said. He declined to estimate the size of the charge.

Sears Canada offered the voluntary severance to all full-time employees in Eastern Canada in an effort to pare about 1,000 jobs.

Mr. Reddington said earnings will be well below last year's 10.6 million Canadian dollars (U.S.$9.2 million), or C$1.23 a share. Revenue for 1990 will be little changed from last year's C$4.56 billion, he said.
