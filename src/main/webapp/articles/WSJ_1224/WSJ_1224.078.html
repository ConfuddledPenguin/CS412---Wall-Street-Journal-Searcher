MiniScribe Corp. Sues The Former Officers And Directors of Firm

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/24/90

DENVER -- MiniScribe Corp., the defunct disk-drive maker, sued its former officers and directors, accusing them of manipulating the company's financial statements, among other wrongdoing. Any damages collected as a result of the suit will become part of MiniScribe's estate in bankruptcy court and will be used to pay creditors, said Denver attorney Greg Ruegsegger, who filed the suit in state court in Boulder.

MiniScribe filed for protection from its creditors under Chapter 11 of the U.S. Bankruptcy Code almost a year ago, and most of its assets were sold in April to Maxtor Corp., a California-based maker of data-storage products.

MiniScribe staged a comeback in the last half of the 1980s under the chairmanship of Q.T. Wiles, onetime chairman of the venture capital firm of Hambrecht & Quist. After Mr. Wiles's resignation in February 1989, company investigators found that much of the company's recovery was the product of creative bookkeeping.

Mr. Wiles is among the defendants in the company's suit and in suits previously filed by shareholders and creditors. Mr. Wiles's Denver attorney, Ken Starr, said his client is "denying any wrongdoing" in his stewardship of the company.
