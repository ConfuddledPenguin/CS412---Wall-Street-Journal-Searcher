Marketing Brief -- Affiliated Publications Inc.: Boston Globe to Cut Staff; Advertising Decline Is Cited

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/24/90

The Boston Globe, citing advertising declines, said it will reduce its work force by 100 full-time positions through attrition over the next five years. The newspaper's advertising revenue for the first 11 months of 1990 is running 10.7% behind the like period for 1989, said a Boston Globe official.

The Globe is a unit of Affiliated Publications Inc.

"We find this is the least painful way to become cost effective in a declining economy," said a Globe spokesman. "And, frankly, we don't expect 1991 to be any better than 1990 in terms of ad revenues."

The spokesman said the Globe will naturally lose 100 positions, about 5% of its fulltime staff, by not filling positions that become vacant, except in special cases. The attrition program is another step in the newspaper's overall cost-cutting effort started in spring 1988, which has meant reductions in department expenditures and in hours of part-time staff.

William O. Taylor, publisher of the Globe and chairman of Affiliated Publications, said the company plans to raise revenue by increasing the price of the Globe to 50 cents, from 35 cents, in areas 30 miles or more from Boston.
