Marketing & Media: Bank Says Loan To Infotechnology Is in Default

  • Author: Kevin Goldman
  • Date: 12/24/90

NEW YORK -- Security Pacific Corp. said late Friday that Infotechnology Inc., which owns 46% of struggling Financial News Network Inc., was in default on a $20.7 million loan and the bank "moved to protect its interests." A spokeswoman for the Los Angeles bank declined to elaborate on the move. The loan originally was due July 1991. Infotechnology said its banks "accelerated the due date" of the $20.7 million loan. Security Pacific said it was acting on behalf of itself and Midlantic National Bank, a unit of Midlantic Corp., Edison, N.J.

The loan is collateralized by Infotechnology's interest in FNN. In a statement, Infotechnology said the bank "has not scheduled a foreclosure sale of Infotech's FNN stock, and the company believes that no such sale is imminent."

One person close to Infotechnology said Security Pacific could be taking the action in part to protect its legal interests. Midlantic couldn't be reached for comment.

In November, new management at FNN and Infotechnology recommended selling operations, including FNN. Candidates to purchase FNN are said to include Dow Jones & Co., which publishes The Wall Street Journal, Paramount Communications Inc., Capital Cities/ABC Inc. and General Electric Co.'s NBC unit. Friday, Infotechnology said the "company has no intention at the present time of altering its previously announced plans to sell its interest" in FNN.

Separately, FNN said that, as of Dec. 21, its stock was removed from quotation in the National Market System of the National Association of Securities Dealers Inc., but was granted a continuing exception to the NASDAQ system listing requirements; FNN fails to meet net worth requirements.
