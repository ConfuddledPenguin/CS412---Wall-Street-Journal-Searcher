Business Brief -- Greater New York Savings Bank: Rise in Bad Loans to Lead To Losses for Quarter, Year

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/24/90

Greater New York Savings Bank said it expects to post losses for the fourth quarter and full year because of an increase in nonperforming assets. The New York bank, citing a deterioration of the commercial real estate market, said it expects to report a loss of between $20 million and $25 million for the current quarter and $15 million to $20 million for the full year. It also plans to omit its dividend on common stock.

In the 1989 fourth quarter, the bank posted net income of $1.4 million, or seven cents a share, and declared a 12 cent dividend. It reported a profit of 19.3 million, or $1.37 a share, for the whole of 1989.

Greater New York Savings said its nonperforming assets are expected to more than triple to $260 million from $83.8 million in the 1989 quarter. Nonperforming assets stood at $145 million Sept. 30. The bank expects to add $25 million to $30 million to its loan-loss reserves. Following chargeoffs during the quarter, the balance in the reserves will total about $40 million to $45 million. Loan-loss provisions were $3.7 million in the 1989 quarter and $3.5 million in the third quarter.
