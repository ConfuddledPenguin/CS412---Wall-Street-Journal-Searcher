Arco Signs Initial Pacts To Develop Soviet Oil, Gas

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/24/90

LOS ANGELES -- Atlantic Richfield Co. said it signed two preliminary agreements to explore, develop and produce oil and gas in parts of eastern Siberia in the Union of Soviet Socialist Republics. The oil giant said the agreements were with the regional councils of the Chukotka Autonomous Area and of Madagan, on the east coast of the Sea of Okhotsk. Arco said the agreements are the first step toward securing "exclusive rights" for onshore and offshore oil exploration and development in the region.

Arco said the agreements mark its first business foray into the Soviet Union.

"This is a very preliminary agreement," an Arco spokeswoman said. There's no timetable for completing a final agreement or to begin operations there, she said. The regional councils will help Arco obtain required legal approvals of the Russian Federation and the U.S.S.R., Arco said.

It wasn't clear why Arco believes it needs approval from the Russian Federation, which is in a different part of the U.S.S.R. The spokeswoman said company officials who might be able to answer the question weren't available.
