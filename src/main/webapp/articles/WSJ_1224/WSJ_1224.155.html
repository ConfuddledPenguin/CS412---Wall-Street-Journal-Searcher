International Brief -- Fokus Bank AS: Company Gets Equity Boost To Shore Up Loan Losses

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/24/90

Fokus Bank AS, Norway's No. 3 bank, received an equity injection of 1.5 billion Norwegian kroner (US$253 million) from a national bank guarantee fund to shore up a capital base eroded by soaring loan losses. The financial bail-out of Fokus Bank came only a day after Norway's second biggest bank, Christiania Bank & Kreditkasse, disclosed that it now expects loan losses this year to more than double and to plunge the bank to an operating loss of nearly one billion kroner. Banking analysts said the loan losses underlined continued weakness in the Norwegian economy.

Fokus Bank blamed depressed real estate markets and a collapse of the regional fish farming industry for a sharp jump in nonperforming credits in its loan portfolio. The bank expects to increase provisions against possible bad loans in this year's accounts to 980 million kroner, more than wiping out a 360 million kroner operating profit and leaving it with a full-year operating loss of 600 million kroner.

The guarantee fund's injection of guaranteed but not paid-in equity capital will qualify as core capital and raise the bank's capital ratio to 8.5%.
