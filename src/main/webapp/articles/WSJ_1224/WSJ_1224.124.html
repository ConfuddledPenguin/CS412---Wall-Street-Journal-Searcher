Business Brief -- Star States Corp.: Loss of Up to $28 Million Expected in Fourth Period

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/24/90

Star States Corp. said it expects a loss of $22 million to $28 million in the fourth quarter, reflecting a provision for loan losses and a write-down. The Wilmington, Del., financial and leasing services concern said that $12 million to $18 million would reflect potential loan losses and that it would write down about $8.5 million for the carrying value of certain intangible assets.

The company said it expects a loss of $70 million to $76 million for the year. In the year-ago fourth quarter, Star States earned $1.6 million, or 34 cents a share. For 1989, it reported a loss of $5.8 million, or $1.23 a share.

"While we believe this loss provision will result in an accurate reflection of the value of the company's loan portfolio and financial condition today, we realize that the real estate market is volatile and that further deterioration in the economy could result in additional loan losses in the future," said Marvin N. Schoenhals, president and chief executive officer.
