Stephens Is Awarded Damages in Fraud Suit Against Geldermann

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 12/24/90

LITTLE ROCK, Ark. -- A federal district court jury awarded Stephens Inc. $1,380,000 in compensatory damages and $1 million in punitive damages in a fraud and conspiracy suit that the Little Rock-based investment banking concern had brought against Geldermann Inc., a Chicago commodity-futures trading firm. The civil suit involved an alleged commodities trading scheme operated from 1982 until 1987 by the late John Markle, a former Stephens vice president who controlled a futures-trading account at Geldermann for Stephens. Mr. Markle was allegedly allowed to wait until the end of the trading day to designate the fate of trades made through Geldermann, with losses being assigned to Stephens and gains to an account Mr. Markle had established for his mother, Academy Award-winning actress Mercedes McCambridge, of San Diego, Calif.

Ms. McCambridge joined Stephen's suit against Geldermann and sought $500,000 in compensatory and $1 million in punitive damages. The jury awarded her nothing.

Stephens had sought $6.2 million in compensatory damages and an equal amount in punitive damages.

During the three-week trial, Geldermann's attorneys argued that Stephens contributed to its own losses by poorly supervising Mr. Markle. Stephens countered that it didn't learn of the scheme until Oct. 6, 1987. Stephens fired Mr. Markle on Nov. 13, 1987. Three days later, the Little Rock executive killed his wife, two daughters and himself, authorities say.
