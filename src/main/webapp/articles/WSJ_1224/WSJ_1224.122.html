Business Brief -- General Electric Co.: American Mortgage Banking To Buy Offices From Unit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/24/90

A unit of GE Capital Corp. said it agreed to sell its 13 retail mortgage origination offices to American Mortgage Banking Ltd., Westbury, N.Y. It didn't disclose the amount. The unit, GE Capital Mortgage Services Inc., Cherry Hill, N.J., acquired the retail origination branch network when it bought Travelers Mortgage Services Inc. That transaction was completed in October.

When the Travelers Mortgage purchase was announced, GE Capital Mortgage said it didn't want to be in the business of making mortgage loans because it doesn't want to compete with its customers, which are thrifts, banks and other mortgage finance companies. Travelers Mortgage had tried to find other buyers for the retail network but failed.

American Mortgage Banking said it plans to change its name to Arbor National Mortgage Inc. after the purchase is completed. The 13 offices are in Connecticut, Maryland, New Jersey, New York, Pennsylvania and Virginia. The transaction is expected to be completed next week. GE Capital Corp., Stamford, Conn., is a financial services unit of General Electric Co., Fairfield, Conn.
