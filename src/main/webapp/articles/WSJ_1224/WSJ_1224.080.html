Who's News: Sweden Continues Nordbanken Changes By Firing Chairman

  • Author: null
  • Date: 12/24/90

STOCKHOLM -- Sweden's Social Democratic government ousted Nordbanken Chairman Tony Hagstroem and heralded a sweeping board reshuffle in an apparent political purge of the bank, which is 71% state-owned. Management upheaval at Nordbanken, Sweden's second largest bank, began last Thursday with the unexplained resignation of its chief executive officer, Rune Barneus. After a stormy meeting Friday, Swedish Finance Minister Allan Larsson effectively discharged Mr. Hagstroem as the bank's chairman.

The official version of Mr. Barneus's departure relates to a confidential report by Swedish banking regulators that sharply criticized Nordbanken management. Mr. Hagstroem said Friday that the report prepared by Sweden's Bank Inspection Board blamed lax credit controls and other management gaffes for hefty loan losses that have surfaced at Nordbanken in recent months.

Finance Ministry officials reportedly had grown disenchanted with Nordbanken's management in recent weeks as the threat of soaring loan losses led to a series of hefty provisions, slashing expected profit for this year.

A special shareholders' meeting will be held Jan. 18 to elect a new slate of directors. In a separate statement, the Finance Ministry said it will name its nominee for the post of Nordbanken's new chairman this week.
