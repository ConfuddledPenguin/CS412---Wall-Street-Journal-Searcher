IMF Threatens to Expel Liberia Over Late Payments

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/02/90

WASHINGTON -- The International Monetary Fund warned Liberia that it is facing expulsion from the 152-nation organization because of its $395.3 million in loan arrears. The IMF executive board on Friday approved a "declaration of non-cooperation" that could lead to the removal of Liberia from the IMF by Aug. 30, unless it "resumes active cooperation" with the IMF in resolving its overdue obligations.

IMF officials said this was the first time the IMF has adopted such a policy declaration involving a member country with overdue loan payments. The IMF soon may issue a similar warning to the Sudan, which is about $1.2 billion in arrears on its IMF loans.

The IMF said Liberia has "persisted in its failure to fulfill it obligations" to the IMF, although it has made payments to other creditors.
