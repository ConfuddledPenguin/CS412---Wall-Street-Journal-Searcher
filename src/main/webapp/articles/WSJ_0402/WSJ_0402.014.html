American Century Says Creditors Clear Merger With Tescorp

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/02/90

SAN ANTONIO, Texas -- American Century Corp. said its creditors committee approved in principle a modified reorganization plan that would allow the company to emerge from federal bankruptcy court proceedings by merging with Houston-based Tescorp Inc. American Century said the board of Tescorp, which makes and distributes oilfield products, also approved the merger in principle. The reorganization plan would give Tescorp shareholders almost 93% of American Century, which owned Commerce Savings Association, a thrift that was placed in federal conservatorship last year.

Recently modified terms call for American Century debenture holders to receive 7.25% of the stock of the reorganized company, changed from 5%. The plan also provides for 700,000 shares of a new class of $5 par value convertible preferred to be issued to debenture holders. Those holders also would receive warrants to purchase a total of two million common shares over three years at an initial exercise price of $3.50 a share.

Current American Century shareholders, meanwhile, would get no shares; previously, they were allotted a 2.25% stake. However, these holders would be given warrants to purchase a total of 857,000 shares in the new company over a three-year period for an initial exercise price of $3.50 a share, American Century said.

The merger and the reorganization plan remain subject to approval by the bankruptcy court, American Century's debenture holders and Tescorp shareholders.
