Panel Urges Labeling AZT With Childen's Dosages

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/02/90

WASHINGTON -- A Food and Drug Administration advisory committee recommended that labels for the anti-AIDS drug AZT carry information about proper dosage for children under 13. AZT has been approved for adults since 1987. Last October, the FDA extended the drug's availability to children through an experimental distribution program.

Under the program, the drug is provided free of charge by the manufacturer, Burroughs-Wellcome Co., the U.S. subsidiary of Wellcome PLC of London. The company distributes a strawberry-flavored syrup version of the drug, instead of the capsule form it markets to adults.

Dr. Ellen Cooper, director of the FDA's division of anti-viral products, called the advisory committee's labeling recommendation "an official statement from the government" that AZT is "safe and effective" for children with AIDS. Some insurance companies have been reluctant to reimburse physicians who prescribe the drug to children because of uncertainties about dosage.
