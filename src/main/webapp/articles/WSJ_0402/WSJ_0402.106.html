UAL Confers With Unions On Acquisition Proposal

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 04/02/90

Advisers to United Airlines labor unions and directors conferred during the weekend in an effort to reach agreement on an employee acquisition of UAL Corp. before a board meeting today. Both sides were seeking an accord before the outbreak of hostilities in a proxy fight by UAL's largest shareholder, Coniston Partners, which supports the employee bid and is seeking to oust UAL directors at the annual meeting April 26.

The biggest outstanding issue between the unions and directors is price. The labor group has offered roughly $185 a share, or about $4 billion, for a 75% stake, and the board is seeking about $220 a share, or $4.8 billion. Other open questions include financing and the composition of management.

Today's board meeting is scheduled to be conducted by telephone. If no agreement is reached but the two sides are making progress, some people familiar with UAL said the talks might continue. But both Coniston and the board are ready to unleash attacks on each other that could make a peaceful agreement more difficult to reach.
