Marketing & Media: Parretti Sentenced To Prison for Role In Newspaper Failure

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/02/90

MILAN -- Italian financier Giancarlo Parretti, who has launched a $1.26 billion bid to buy MGM/UA Communications Co, received a prison sentence for fraudulent bankruptcy from a Naples court in connection with the 1981 collapse of a newspaper in which he was a part owner. The sentence relates to the failure of Il Diario, a daily newspaper published in the Naples region between 1979 and 1981 and of which Mr. Parretti was briefly a director. Mr. Parretti was sentenced to three years and 10 months in jail and banned him from holding public office for five years.

Mr. Parretti said he had already lodged an appeal and that the court's decision would have no effect on his bid for MGM/UA, a film and television studio. "Why should it have any effect?" he said. "Everyone knew about this case. We made full disclosure in the documents."

In Los Angeles, officials at MGM/UA couldn't be reached for comment.

There is no prospect of Mr. Parretti being jailed in the near future. Manlio Pennino, a lawyer representing the Diario journalists who brought the suit against Mr. Parretti, said an appeal hearing in Naples would be unlikely to start until at least next year. Even if Mr. Parretti lost that case, he would be able to request a further hearing before the Supreme Court of Appeal in Rome.

The court judgment said Italafin S.p.A., the company founded by Mr. Parretti that controlled Il Diario, defaulted on capital payments and that accounts were falsified. Six other people have been under investigation in relation to the newspaper's collapse; four of them have since died.

The court decision comes at a critical time in Mr. Parretti's bid for MGM/UA. Mr. Parretti's company, Pathe Communications, has pledged to pay a $200 million security deposit on the transaction in installments by June, and is negotiating with Time Warner Inc. to sell it distribution rights to the United Artists film library.

Mr. Parretti declined to give details of the financing package he is putting together for the $20-a-share MGM/UA bid, except to confirm that banks he has contacted include Italian ones.

Pathe has extensive cinema and television interests in Europe, including controlling Italian commercial television station Odeon. Mr. Parretti also owns a Spanish real-estate company, Renta Group.
