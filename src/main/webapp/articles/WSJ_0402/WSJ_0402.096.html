Japan's Current Account Data

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/02/90

TOKYO -- Japan posted a $4.91 billion current account surplus in February, narrowing 32% from a $7.26 billion surplus a year earlier,the Finance Ministry said. But the results also marked a return to overall surplus in the current account, after a $636 million deficit in January, Japan's first such gap in six years.

Japan's trade surplus in February was $5.49 billion, a 36% shrinkage from the $8.52 billion surplus posted a year earlier, but up strongly from January's $1.08 billion surplus, which shrank because of seasonal and special factors.

Exports declined 2.2% to $21.5 billion from $22 billion a year earlier. Imports climbed 19% to $16.1 billion from $13.5 billion.
