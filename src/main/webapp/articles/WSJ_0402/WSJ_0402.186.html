Chemical Banking Will Take a Charge For the First Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/02/90

NEW YORK -- Chemical Banking Corp. said first-quarter results will be reduced by a $52 million pretax charge for expenses stemming from the previously announced move to cut 1,100 jobs and other cost-saving steps. The after-tax effect of the charge is expected to be about $40 million, according to securities analysts. The company didn't specify the charge after taxes.

Analysts had been expecting Chemical to post first-quarter earnings of about $144 million, or $1.75 a share, including the gain from the sale of its stake in Florida National Banks of Florida Inc. to First Union Corp. Analysts expect the sale will produce an after-tax gain of about $80 million for Chemical.

James J. McDermott, research director of Keefe, Bruyette & Woods Inc., said, "With the gain from Florida National, this was a good time for Chemical to take the charge."

Chemical's slimming down was prompted by two special additions to its loan-loss reserves in last year's third quarter: a $600 million provision for Latin American debt and a $300 million for provision to readjust values of real estate held by Chemical's Texas Commerce Bancshares Inc. subsidiary.

As a result, Chemical had a loss of $482.2 million, or $8.29 a share, last year.

The elimination of 1,100 jobs is a somewhat sharper cut than the estimate the company gave in February, when it said it planned to cut 900 jobs in the first quarter. No explanation was given for the higher number. Chemical now has about 29,000 employees world-wide.

The staff reductions are expected to save Chemical about $60 million on an annualized basis, though the bank wouldn't estimate the net savings for this year.

In addition to pruning its work force, Chemical said it consolidated certain branches and by midyear will relocate headquarters and staff of its Chemical Bank New Jersey subisidiary from several locations in Morristown, N.J., to a single site in East Brunswick, N.J.

Keefe's Mr. McDermott said that "Chemical has actually done a pretty good job of expense control. Their expenses totaled $2.6 billion last year. Expenses have been about flat since 1987, and since then they've acquired a New Jersey bank."

In composite trading Friday on the New York Stock Exchange, Chemical dropped 37.5 cents to $23.375.
