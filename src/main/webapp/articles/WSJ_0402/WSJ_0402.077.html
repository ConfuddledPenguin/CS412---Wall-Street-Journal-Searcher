Southmark, MGF Oil Complete Stock Deal

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/02/90

DALLAS -- Southmark Corp. and MGF Oil Corp., a Midland, Texas, energy firm, said they completed their agreements to sell all MGF common stock held by Southmark and MGF's minority shareholders to closely held Kaiser-Francis Oil Co., a Tulsa, Okla., energy concern. Southmark had owned 94.1% of MGF's common shares outstanding, and the remaining 5.9% had been publicly held.

Under the agreement, MGF sold its assets to Kaiser-Francis for $26 million. Following the asset sale, Southmark acquired the 5.9% of MGF's 353.3 million shares outstanding for 7.4 cents a share, then sold its stock to a new company related to Kaiser-Francis for about 7.4 cents a share.

Southmark, a real estate and financial services concern that is operating under Chapter 11 bankruptcy-law protection from creditors, said its proceeds from the sale were approximately $23.5 million in cash and a long-term nonrecourse note with a balance of about $1.2 million.
