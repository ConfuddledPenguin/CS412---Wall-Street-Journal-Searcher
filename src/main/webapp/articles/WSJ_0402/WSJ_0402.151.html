MCA's Indicated Revenue From Park Was $85 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/02/90

SAN FRANCISCO -- The National Park Service here released figures indicating MCA Inc. posted revenue of about $85 million in Yosemite National Park last year. The Park Service said MCA paid the government a franchise fee of $635,772 to do business in Yosemite in 1989. Under terms of its contract, MCA pays the government three-fourths of 1% of annual revenue from Yosemite.

In 1988, MCA paid a franchise fee of about $590,000, indicating revenue of about $78 million. Neither the government nor MCA will disclose how much the company earns from Yosemite.

MCA, a diversified entertainment concern that owns Universal Studios, is the concessionaire for Yosemite, providing lodging, meals, souvenirs, tours and recreational activities. As reported, monopolistic concessionaire contracts in national parks have been criticized by those who believe the agreements are too favorable to private companies.

After visiting Yosemite, Secretary of the Interior Manuel Lujan called for a review of concessions policy nationwide. Results of that review are expected in about a month. Renewal of all concession contracts is on hold until the department determines whether the policy should be changed.

MCA pays one of the lowest franchise fees among the more than 500 concessionaires at national parks around the nation. Though MCA's contract calls for the fee to be renegotiated every five years, the amount hasn't been raised since the contract was written in 1963. MCA purchased the concession rights in 1973.
