Business Brief -- HAL Inc.: Net Loss for 1989 Widened; Decrease in Traffic Is Cited

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/02/90

HAL Inc., citing a significant decrease in passenger traffic at its Hawaiian Airlines unit, reported a wider net loss for 1989 on reduced revenue. The company reported a 1989 net loss of $46.9 million, or $24 a share, compared with a net loss of $8.8 million, or $4.48 a share, in 1988. HAL's 1989 revenue fell to $348.9 million from $354 million in 1988.

Extended periods of aircraft downtime during the fourth quarter racked up higher losses than in the first three quarters of 1989, HAL said. Inter-island passenger traffic fell significantly during the fourth quarter because of reduced capacity, the company added.

Operating expenses for the year rose to $392.5 million in 1989 from $358.1 million in 1988, the company said.
