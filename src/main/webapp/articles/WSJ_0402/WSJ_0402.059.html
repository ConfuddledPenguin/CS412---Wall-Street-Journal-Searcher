Economy: Jump in Orders For Cars Boosts Factory Totals --- Bookings in February Rose 1.8% After 5.5% Plunge In Revised January Data

  • Author: Katherine Walsh Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 04/02/90

WASHINGTON -- Increased bookings for cars helped produce a modest rebound in factory orders in February, the Commerce Department said. New orders increased 1.8%, the department said, after a revised 5.5% plunge in January. That was the largest decline since 1974 and stemmed from a record 10.5% drop in orders for durable goods, or products that are expected to last at least three years.

In February, durable-goods orders climbed 2.6%, compared with the 3.3% figure the department gave in a preliminary report.

Behind the growth in durable goods was principally a 10.4% jump in transportation equipment -- mostly cars -- which came despite a 23.1% plunge in orders for aircraft and parts.

The February rebound in the auto industry wasn't a surprise, because production was low in January.

And analysts weren't concerned about the decline in aircraft. Although orders and production tend to be synonymous in the auto industry, aircraft orders don't reflect current sales. "We know the aircraft industry is one of the healthiest in the economy," said Gregory Fager, chief economist at First City Bancorp. of Texas, Houston.

Many economists agreed that the report reflects continued sluggishness in the manufacturing sector, however. "The rate of decline seems to have eased, but we're not in the midst of recovery," Mr. Fager said. He said the manufacturing sector "is more or less holding its own."

Joan Schnieder, money market economist at Continental Bank of Chicago, said the report provided confirmation of "the sense of stagnation we've had about the manufacturing sector over the past year."

Orders for nondurable goods rose 0.9% in February after rising a revised 0.6% in January.

Manufacturers' inventories fell 0.1% in February after rising 0.9% in January. Durable-goods inventories dropped 0.3%, while stocks of nondurable goods were up 0.2%.

February shipments increased 3.3% after a decline of 1.8% in January. Shipments of durable goods jumped 5.5% after slipping 4.3% in January. Shipments of nondurables rose 0.9%, the same as they did in January.
