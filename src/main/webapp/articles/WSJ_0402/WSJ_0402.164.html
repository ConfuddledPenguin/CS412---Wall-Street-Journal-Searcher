Business Brief -- Uniforce Temporary Personnel: Firm No Longer Considers Sale or Merger of Company

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/02/90

Uniforce Temporary Personnel Inc., New Hyde Park, N.Y., said it is no longer considering a sale or merger of the company. In May, Uniforce said it had retained Merrill Lynch Capital Markets to evaluate strategies to maximize the company's value to shareholders, including a possible sale or restructuring.

Uniforce said it decided instead to raise the quarterly dividend rate and to increase its borrowing facility from $5 million, to $15 million, "in connection with its intention to expand operations through the acquisition and conversion of existing temporary services."

The dividend will be three cents a share, up from 2.5 cents, and payable May 4 to stock of record April 20.

Through its Uniforce Temporary Services, the company operates a national network of offices providing temporary office and light-industry workers. Uniforce's 1989 net income grew to $3.9 million, or 84 cents a share, from 1988 net of $3.1 million, or 67 cents a share. Sales rose to $110.5 million from $101 million.
