Business Brief -- Information International Inc.: Litigation With Publisher Over Software Is Settled

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/02/90

Information International Inc. said it settled certain litigation with Morris Communications Corp. over computer software used for newspaper publishing and commercial printing. Under the settlements, which dealt with suits filed last October in federal court in Georgia, Morris Communications withdrew demands for restitution of the $3 million purchase price of a software system bought from Information International. For its part, Information International paid royalty payments owed to Morris on separate software acquired from Morris.

Information International, based in Culver City, Calif., makes computer software for the publishing business, among others. Closely held Morris Communications, based in Augusta, Ga., is a newspaper publishing concern.
