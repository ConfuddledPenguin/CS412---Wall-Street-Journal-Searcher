G-7 Nations to Discuss IMF Capital Proposal At Meeting in Paris

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/02/90

MONTREAL -- The Group of Seven industrialized countries will discuss the International Monetary Fund's proposed capital increase at a meeting in Paris on Saturday, according to Canada's Finance Minister Michael Wilson. Mr. Wilson adding that he hopes for agreement on the issue at the meeting.

The IMF has sought to double its capital to $240 billion from $120 billion. But the U.S. and several other powerful members have proposed lesser amounts, and it is likely the agency will receive a capital increase of about 50% or $60 billion.

Mr. Wilson, who also heads the IMF's powerful policy-setting interim committee, said he didn't think the coordination of economic policies -- carried out through the G-7 -- needs "revitalizing." But he said "coordination hasn't produced results" recently. That may not be possible, he said, because "we don't sit there with seven sets of levers and make things happen."

The G-7 groups the U.S., Japan, West Germany, France, Britain, Italy and Canada. Mr. Wilson will fly to Washington today for a pre-G-7 talk with U.S. Treasury Secretary Nicholas Brady.

In recent weeks, foreign-exchange intervention by Japan, the U.S. and big European countries has failed to stem the weakening of the yen and the strengthening of the dollar. Mr. Wilson declined to comment on the strained yen-dollar relationship. But several other bankers and financial leaders at this year's annual meeting of the Inter-American Development Bank, held here during the weekend, said Japan will soon have to raise interest rates to shore up the yen.

Commenting on recent talks between the U.S. and Mexico concerning a bilateral free-trade agreement, Mr. Wilson said a separate bilateral accord between Canada and Mexico wouldn't be necessary.
