Who's News: Reuter Shareholder Set to Solicit Proxies For Board Candidates

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 04/02/90

An investor has decided to solicit proxies to nominate and elect James H. McGuire, Charles J. Mitchell Jr. and Edward E. Strickland to Reuter Inc.'s board. Richard P. Davisson, a New York broker and holder of 12.5% of Reuter common stock, asked Messrs. McGuire, Mitchell and Strickland to be a part of a slate that he hopes is elected at the annual meeting. Mr. Strickland is a director but his seat is up for re-election this year.

In a Securities and Exchange Commission filing, Mr. Davisson said he contacted Reuter of Hopkins, Minn., earlier in the month to ask that the board include a majority of independent directors. He said the maker of computer parts and plastic-refuse containers hasn't responded.

In October, Mr. Davisson agreed that until March 15, he wouldn't solicit proxies in opposition to Reuter's board. That agreement resulted in an expansion of Reuter's board to nine members and the appointment of James W. Taylor, whose election Mr. Davisson had sought.

Mr. McGuire is chairman of Intercim Corp., a Minneapolis factory-automation concern. Mr. Mitchell is president of Hayden/Wegman Inc., a New York environmental-engineering firm. Mr. Strickland is a Wyzetta, Minn., financial consultant.

Officials for Reuter weren't available for comment.
