Thrift Agency Agrees To Postpone Naming Receiver for Franklin

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 04/02/90

The Office of Thrift Supervision agreed to postpone the appointment of a receiver for Franklin Savings Association of Ottawa, Kan., pending the trial of a lawsuit challenging the thrift office's dealings with Franklin. Franklin, in turn, agreed to withdraw its request for an injunction that would have prohibited the OTS from appointing a receiver and liquidating any of the thrift's $11.4 billion in assets.

The agreements effectively forestalled a court ruling on the constitutionality of M. Danny Wall's appointment as director of the OTS.

"We just didn't touch that issue and didn't have to," said Judge Dale E. Saffels, who is presiding over Franklin's lawsuit in federal court in Topeka, Kan.

Franklin and a handful of other thrifts are seeking to block certain OTS actions because, they argue, Mr. Wall should have been appointed by President Bush rather than Congress. The OTS is currently appealing a decision favoring that argument in a lawsuit brought by Olympic Federal Savings & Loan Association of Berwyn, Ill. In a similar lawsuit brought by Benjamin Franklin Federal Savings & Loan Association of Portland, Ore., the OTS won the first round when Judge Helen J. Frye of Portland refused to block the appointment of a receiver for the thrift. Benjamin Franklin was placed in conservatorship on Feb. 21.

Franklin Savings was placed in conservatorship Feb. 16 after the OTS forced the institution to immediately recognize about $119 million in losses on futures positions it held as a hedge against losses in its $10.26 billion portfolio of mortgage-backed securities.

Franklin filed suit against the OTS on March 12 and won an order from Judge Saffels, temporarily barring the OTS from appointing a receiver. The suit also challenges Mr. Wall's appointment and the OTS's use of new thrift accounting rules in determining whether the institution was in need of a conservator. Judge Saffels scheduled the trial for June 25 in Topeka.
