Greyhound Lines Refuses To Bargain After Violence

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/02/90

DALLAS -- After another outbreak of strike violence, GLI Holding Co.'s Greyhound Lines Inc. said it would not take part in a renewed round of bargaining with the Amalgamated Transit Union, which represents 9,300 Greyhound employees who have been on strike since March 2. The two sides had been set to resume negotiations in Tucson, Ariz., today, but within hours after the resumption of bargaining was announced last Thursday, two more Greyhound buses had been hit by sniper fire and a bomb threat was phoned into the company's Chicago terminal.

"We cannot resume negotiations while the violence continues," Greyhound chief labor negotiator P. Anthony Lannie said in a letter sent Friday to Edward Strait, president of the Amalgamated Council of Greyhound Local Unions, the ATU's bargaining unit.

The company said that if there is no further violence, it would be willing to meet with the union on April 9.

Mr. Strait responded angrily to the company's action, calling it a "smokescreen."

"This union has repeatedly and publicly condemned violence," Mr. Strait said. "But these isolated incidents have no bearing on Greyhound management's legal and moral obligation to negotiate in good faith, a fair and equitable contract for Greyhound employees."
