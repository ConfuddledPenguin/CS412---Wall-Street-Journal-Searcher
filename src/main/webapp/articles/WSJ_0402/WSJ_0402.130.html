U.S. Charges McHaffie With Falsifying Test Data

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/02/90

LOS ANGELES -- McHaffie Inc., a Sylmar, Calif., defense contractor, was charged by the U.S. Justice Department with falsification of test data on bolts the company made for use in various military jet aircraft engines. In a criminal information filed in federal court in Los Angeles, the government alleged that McHaffie failed to test the bolts and submitted fraudulent certifications to the government over an eight-year period beginning in 1979. The certifications indicated that the bolts had been tested and met all manufacturing standards and other specifications, the government charged.

The information named as defendants the company; its 56-year-old owner and president, Norman McHaffie, and two employees, James Hicks, 45, and William Whitham, 37.

Attorneys for Mr. McHaffie and Mr. Hicks said they hadn't seen the information and couldn't comment. Neither Mr. Whitham nor his attorney could be reached.

The company faces a maximum fine of $1.5 million, the government said. Mr. McHaffie faces a maximum sentence of 15 years in prison and a $750,000 fine. Messrs. Hicks and Whitham, who cooperated with the government in its investigation, each face a maximum sentence of 10 years in prison and a $500,000 fine.
