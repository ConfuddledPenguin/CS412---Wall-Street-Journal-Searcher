Hallmark Sweetens Offer for Junk Bonds Issued by Univision

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/02/90

KANSAS CITY, Mo. -- Hallmark Cards Inc. sweetened its bid to buy back at a discount $270 million of junk bonds issued by its majority-owned Univision Holdings subsidiary. The closely held greeting-card concern also turned up the heat on reluctant bondholders, who tendered only a small fraction of the bonds under the earlier proposal despite repeated extensions, by announcing that Univision will file for Chapter 11 bankruptcy-law protection from creditors if the modified bid isn't accepted.

Hallmark launched the tender offer in February, as part of an attempt to rescue its investment in Univision. Hallmark holds a 63.5% stake in the financially troubled operator of a Spanish-language broadcast network.

Hallmark's new bid calls for it to pay holders of Univision's $165 million in senior subordinated discount notes, due 1998, 55.3 cents on each dollar of face amount -- an increase from the earlier 45 cents. Holders of Univision's $105 million in subordinated debentures, due 1999, are being offered 31.5 cents on each dollar of face amount, up from the previous 30 cents. The offers are scheduled to expire at 12:01 a.m. April 13; holders of the senior notes are being offered accrued interest from Feb. 1, but the offer for the subordinated notes doesn't include any such accrual.

In New York, Wilbur Ross, senior managing director of Rothschild Inc., who represents holders of a majority of Univision's two debt issues, said the group views the latest proposal "as a constructive change, made in good faith."

He added, however, that the bondholders still have questions about the allocation of the funds between the two issues, and the total amount to be offered for the debt.
