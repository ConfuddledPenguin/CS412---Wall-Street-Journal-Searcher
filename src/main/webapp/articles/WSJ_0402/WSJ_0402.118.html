Rep. Bill Frenzel Says He Won't Seek Re-Election

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/02/90

WASHINGTON -- Minnesota Rep. Bill Frenzel, the ranking GOP member of the House Budget Committee, said he won't run for re-election. "Every member of Congress knows when the time has come not to run again," said Mr. Frenzel, who is 61 years old, at a St. Paul, Minn., news conference. "For me, it is now."

Rep. Frenzel, known for his quick wit and curmudgeonly demeanor, has long fought for lower federal spending and resisted raising taxes to reduce the budget deficit. A senior member of the House Ways and Means Committee, Mr. Frenzel also is a leading GOP voice on trade issues. He is currently serving his 10th House term.

The ranking GOP member on the budget committee is selected by a vote of all House Republicans. Ohio Rep. Willis Gradison is a leading contender to replace Mr. Frenzel.
