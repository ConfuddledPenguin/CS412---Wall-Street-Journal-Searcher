McCaw Cellular Files With SEC for Sale Of 2.4 Million Shares

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/02/90

KIRKLAND, Wash. -- McCaw Cellular Communications Inc. filed with the Securities and Exchange Commission for the sale of 2.4 million Class A shares. The shares are held by a trust controlled by William Taylor, chairman of Affiliated Publications Inc., and two of Affiliated's directors. The proposed offering is contingent on an offering by the trust of 2.8 million Affiliated Publications Class A common shares. The trust owns 12.8% of McCaw's Cellular's Class A common, or 7.8% of the company's total common stock outstanding.

In the filing, McCaw said investment in the shares involves a high degree of risk because the company expects its net losses to increase significantly for the foreseeable future and "may exceed $1 billion in some years." However, a spokesman said a substantial part of the loss would be in non-cash categories related to depreciation and amortization.

McCaw officials weren't available for comment. A spokesman said he didn't know for many years the company expected to post a loss.

In 1989, McCaw had a net loss of $288.5 million on revenue of $504 million. The company has never posted a profit, as its heavy marketing costs, debt and other expenses have been a drag on earnings.

However, in the fledgling cellular industry, cash flow and subscriber growth are closely watched as the best indicators of a company's progress. McCaw showed rapid growth in number of subscribers and its cash flow increased in its most recent quarter.

In the filing, McCaw also said it has a large amount of debt related to its recent acquisition of a controlling interest in LIN Broadcasting Corp. McCaw said it has long-term debt of about $3.1 billion, including about $1.4 billion under a credit agreement with a group of banks.

McCaw said it doesn't expect its operations to generate sufficient cash flow to service all of its debt for the next several years. However, the company said it believes it will be able to make the required interest payment primarily from cash generated by operations and borrowings from its bank credit facility.

McCaw said it believes it and LIN have sufficient internally generated funds to repay its debt at maturity. Alternatively, the company said it believes it will be able to make necessary repayments and repurchases through the refinancing of the debt obligations, sales of equity or sales of assets.
