Simpler Tax Rules Sought For Large Partnerships

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/02/90

WASHINGTON -- The Treasury recommended drastically simplifying the system for calculating the income tax of widely held partnerships. In a report to the tax-writing Senate Finance and House Ways and Means committees, the Treasury and the Internal Revenue Service concluded that the requirements for reporting income of large partnerships "are overly complex and inefficient."

A simplified reporting system would ease the burden and cost of compliance on partnerships and make it easier for the IRS to administer that area of tax law, the two agencies said.

In general, the agencies are recommending radically reducing the number of items that must be reported separately to partners. Instead "all income and expense, including capital gains and losses, would be netted at the partnership level," the report said.

"The goal of these proposed changes is not to increase or decrease the overall tax due partnerships," the report said. But it added, "We recognize that the total income tax attributable to a partnership subject to the simplified reporting system would almost certainly vary to some degree from the total income tax under the current rules."
