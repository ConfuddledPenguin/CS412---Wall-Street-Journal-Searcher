Who's News: Torchmark Offers Its Slate

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/02/90

HOUSTON -- American General Corp. said it was notified by a Torchmark Corp. unit of its intent to nominate a slate for American General's board. The notice, contained in a letter from Torchmark's Liberty National Life Insurance Co., appears to confirm rumors that the Birmingham, Ala., insurer intends to press its hostile bid for American General in a proxy fight, American General said.

But American General said it plans to inform Liberty National that it missed the deadline to nominate a slate. American General last week filed a lawsuit in federal court here asking a judge to uphold a company bylaw that requires shareholders to notify the company at least 60 days before a shareholders meeting if they plan to nominate directors.

An American General spokesman said he didn't know if the letter, which was received Saturday evening, contained the names of specific Torchmark nominees. But he said the letter indicated Torchmark intends to nominate five people for the six seats that come up for election at the annual meeting May 2.

Last week, Torchmark made a surprise offer to acquire American General for $50 a share in cash and stock, or more than $6 billion. American General said its board plans to consider the offer at its regular meeting after the shareholders meeting.

Torchmark couldn't immediately be reached for comment.
