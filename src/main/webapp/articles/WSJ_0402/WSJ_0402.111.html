International: EC Finance Ministers Agree on Monetary Plan

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/02/90

GALWAY, Ireland -- European Community finance ministers agreed on the "overall design" of a plan for economic and monetary union. In their first detailed discussion of the plan, 11 of the 12 ministers said they reached a consensus on the broad lines of a system that would include an independent, federal central banking system. British Chancellor of the Exchequer John Major, who doesn't support a central bank, refused to endorse the joint conclusions.

Most ministers said the meeting of minds would give impetus to technical work that needs to be done before treaty negotiations on the plan begin at the end of the year. "All delegations indicated a large measure of agreement on the ultimate objective," said Irish Finance Minister Albert Reynolds.

The 11 ministers agreed they would create an "independent and federally structured central banking institution" similar to the "EuroFed" proposed late last month by the EC Commission. But they failed to back the European Currency Unit as a future single EC currency.
