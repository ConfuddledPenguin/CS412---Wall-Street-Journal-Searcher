American Therapeutics Plea

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/02/90

BOHEMIA, N.Y. -- American Therapeutics Inc. said it pleaded guilty in connection with the delivery of a gratuity to a Food and Drug Administration official. "We deeply regret any impropriety taken under American Therapeutics' name," John D. Meloy, a company director, said in a statement.

Last month, American Therapeutics said it would plead guilty to one count of use of interstate facilities in aid of racketeering. It also pledged to cooperate in the continuing investigation by the U.S. attorney's office in Baltimore.

Sentencing is set for July 23. The company faces a maximum fine of $500,000.

Also last month, a former consultant to the generic drug maker, Mohammed Azeem, pleaded guilty to two criminal counts related to providing illegal gratuities to FDA employees.
