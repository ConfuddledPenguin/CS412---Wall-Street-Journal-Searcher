Business Brief -- Occidental Petroleum Corp.: Unit Discovers Oil and Gas In North Sea, Off Scotland

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/02/90

Occidental Petroleum (Caledonia) Ltd., a unit of Los Angeles-based Occidental Petroleum Corp., said it discovered oil and natural gas in the North Sea about 120 miles northeast of Aberdeen, Scotland. The Occidental unit in London said an exploration well about nine miles southeast of the North Sea's Piper field tested at a maximum daily flow rate of 6,529 barrels of oil and 12.5 million cubic feet of natural gas through a 48/64-inch opening. The well was drilled by a semi-submersible rig on the Westray prospect in Block 15/17 to a total depth of 15,670 feet.

Analysts said they couldn't determine the significance of the discovery, partly because of its proximity to the Piper field and its satellite Saltire field. "This at least confirms that they have a decent-sized discovery at Saltire," said Bryan Jacoboski at PaineWebber Inc.

Occidental is the well operator and holds a 36.5% interest in it.
