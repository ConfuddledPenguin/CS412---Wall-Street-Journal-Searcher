New Securities Issues

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/02/90

The following were among Friday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: CORPORATE

Associates Corp. of North America -- $250 million of notes due April 1, 2000, priced at par for an initial yield of 9 1/8%. The notes, which will be reoffered at various prices, may be put back to the company on April 1, 1994. Rated double-A-3 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and double-A-minus by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Salomon Brothers Inc.

MORTGAGES

Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $300 million of Remic mortgage securities offered in 11 classes by Salomon Brothers Inc. The offering, Series 163, is backed by Freddie Mac 10 1/2% securities with a weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29.3 years. Price information wasn't immediately available. Planned yield spreads on classes for which details were known ranged from 60 basis points above two-year Treasurys for Class A, with an expected average life of 1.2 years, to 160 basis points above 10-year Treasurys for Class I, with an expected average life of 11.2 years.

OTHER

Student Loan Marketing Association -- $300 million of 9.05% notes due April 13, 1994, priced at par for variableprice reoffering. The notes, which are non-callable for two years, will be sold through underwriters led by Goldman Sachs & Co. The notes were priced at a spread of 37 basis points above the Treasury's when-issued four-year note.

EUROBONDS

Republic of Italy (sovereign) -- one billion European currency units of 10 3/4% Eurobonds due April 18, 2000, at a fixed reoffer price of 100.15 to yield 10.72%, via Paribas Capital Markets. Fees 0.325.

Amro Australia Ltd. (Dutch parent) -- 60 million Australian dollars of 14 3/4% Eurobonds due May 15, 1995, priced at 101.85 to yield 14.79% annually less full fees, via Amro Bank. Guaranteed by Amro Bank. Fees 2.

Rautaruukki Oy (Finland) -- 50 million marks of 9 1/4% private placement bonds due April 26, 1995, priced at par, via J.P. Morgan G.m.b.H. Fees undisclosed.
