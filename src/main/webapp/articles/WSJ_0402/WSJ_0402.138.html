Conrail Sets Consolidation Of Headquarters Operation

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/02/90

PHILADELPHIA -- Consolidated Rail Corp. said it plans to consolidate its headquarters here. The railroad, which operates in the Northeast and Midwest, said it will lease 750,000 square feet of office space in a tower to be built at Commerce Square, 20th and Market streets. Conrail won't have an ownership interest in the 41-story, 950,000-square-foot structure, which it said it expects to begin occupying in early 1992.

Conrail said the move will allow the company to consolidate its four central Philadelphia offices, with more than 3,000 total employees, in one location, "increasing our efficiency and productivity." The company didn't disclose rental costs or say whether the new space will result in net savings.

The building is being developed by a joint venture of Maguire Thomas Partners, Los Angeles, and International Business Machines Corp., Armonk, N.Y.
