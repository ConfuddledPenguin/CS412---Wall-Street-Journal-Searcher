Canadian Production Of Market Pulp Fell 7.3% in February

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/02/90

MONTREAL -- Canadian production of market pulp in February dropped 7.3% from a year earlier, as the industry operated at 91% of capacity. The Canadian Pulp and Paper Association, an industry group, said Canadian mills produced 534,000 metric tons of market pulp in the latest month, compared with 576,000 tons in February 1989. A metric ton is equal to about 2,204.62 pounds.

The 91% operating rate in February was down from 99% in January and 101% a year earlier. Operating rates can exceed 100% because some mills may operate for a limited period at higher capacity than that for which they are rated.

For the first two months of this year, Canadian mills produced 1,180,000 metric tons of market pulp, a 4.5% decline from 1,236,000 tons a year earlier. The industry operated at 95% of capacity in the first two months, down from 103% a year earlier.

Market pulp is wood pulp sold on the open market to producers of paper, sanitary and other products. The statistics exclude pulp consumed at the producing mill or shipped to another mill that is affiliated with the producing mill. Canada is the world's largest producer of market pulp.

Total market shipments in January dropped 16% to 505,000 metric tons from 599,000 tons a year earlier. Shipments to all markets were down.

Market shipments for the first two months dropped 13% to 998,000 metric tons from 1,146,000 tons a year earlier.

Canadian producer inventories at the end of February were 541,000 metric tons, a 30-day supply, compared with 512,000 tons, a 28-day supply, at the end of January, and 327,000 tons, a 15-day supply, at the end of February 1989.
