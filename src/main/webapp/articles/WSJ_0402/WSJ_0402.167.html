Business Brief -- Graphic Scanning Corp.: Telecommunications Firm Tries to Answer Objections

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/02/90

Graphic Scanning Corp. moved to answer Gabelli Group Inc.'s objections to the Englewood, N.J., telecommunications company's proposed slate of directors to be voted at the annual meeting. Gabelli, which controls the equivalent of 38.8% of Graphic Scanning shares, had said it couldn't support the slate and suggested the company nominate "knowledgeable industry professionals" to the board.

Graphic responded by adding two more candidates to the slate -- James L. Greenwald, president and chief executive officer of Katz Communications Inc., and Robert Price, president of Price Communications Corp. If both men are elected, the board would increase to 10 members. Graphic also postponed the meeting until April 19. Katz and Price are based in New York.

Mario J. Gabelli, who heads the investor group, said he couldn't say whether the move would satisfy the group's concerns, until he sees the new proxy material. He did say, however, that he believed reducing the size of the board to six would have been better than expanding it to 10.
