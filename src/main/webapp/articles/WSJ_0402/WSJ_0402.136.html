Senate Rejects Buffer Plan In Clean-Air Legislation

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/02/90

WASHINGTON -- The Senate voted to eliminate a clean-air provision that would have allowed factories whose fumes pose cancer risks to buy out adjoining neighborhoods to create a buffer zone. On a voice vote Friday, the Senate adopted an amendment by Sen. Albert Gore (D., Tenn.) rejecting the creation of what environmentalists had branded "dead zones" around polluting plants. The provision had been written into a clean-air agreement between the White House and Senate leaders to help plants avoid mandatory shutdowns if they can't eliminate most of the cancer risks posed by their emissions.

Environmentalists had loudly criticized the provision for proposing to permit factories to move people out of their homes rather than reduce toxic-air emissions to unthreatening levels. While industry still is worried that the Senate's toxic-air requirements could shut some plants down, industry spokesmen said the buffer-zone provision wasn't crucial for them.
