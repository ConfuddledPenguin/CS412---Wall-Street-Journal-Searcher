Ivaco Plans to Buy Remainder of Canron, A Toronto Steel Firm

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/02/90

MONTREAL -- Ivaco Inc. and Canron Inc. said Ivaco agreed to acquire the 21% of Canron it doesn't already own under a proposed arrangement approved by the boards of both companies. Ivaco and Canron said the arrangement is subject to approval by regulatory authorities and Canron holders at a meeting in May. Ivaco, a Montreal-based steel and steel-products concern, said that under the arrangement it will make an offer valued at about 42.6 million Canadian dollars (US$36.3 million) for the publicly held shares of Canron, a Toronto steel and pipe concern. Under the arrangement, Ivaco said Canron holders would receive C$20.50 for each Class A or Class B share and C$35 for each Series B preferred share.

On the Toronto Stock Exchange Canron Class A shares last traded Friday before a trading halt at C$11.50, unchanged. In Toronto, Ivaco Class A shares closed at C$10.25, down 25 Canadian cents.

Paul Ivanier, president and chief executive officer of Ivaco, said funding for the acquisition would come from the combined resources of Ivaco and Canron.

Both Ivaco and Canron have been struggling lately. Each posted a loss for 1989 after extraordinary items and losses from discontinued operations.
