NFL's Raiders to Return To Oakland in New Deal

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/02/90

OAKLAND, Calif. -- The Los Angeles Raiders agreed to move back to Oakland with a less lucrative revenue guarantee than in an earlier accord. Last month, Raiders' owner Al Davis tentatively agreed to the team's return to its previous home in exchange for the city's guaranteeing $602 million in ticket revenue over 15 years.

The deal brought a public outcry because it essentially obligated the city to ensure that every game be sold out. In the new deal, the amount of guaranteed revenue was reduced to $428 million.
