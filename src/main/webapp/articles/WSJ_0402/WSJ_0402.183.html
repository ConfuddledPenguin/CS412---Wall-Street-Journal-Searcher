The increase in savings deposit insurance to $100,000 from $40,000 became law in 1980. Friday's Potomac Watch column wrongly attributed the increase to the 1982 Garn-St Germain act. Sen. Jake Garn was the ranking Republican on the Senate Banking Committee in 1980, not its chairman. (See: "Potomac Watch -- Washington Rule: It's Not a Scandal If a Senator Did It" -- WSJ March 30, 1990)
