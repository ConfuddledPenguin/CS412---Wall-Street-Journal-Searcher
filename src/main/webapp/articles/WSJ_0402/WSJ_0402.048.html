Bridgestone Corp., Sumitomo Each Post Profit Gains for '89

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 04/02/90

TOKYO -- Bridgestone Corp. and Sumitomo Rubber Industries Ltd., two of Japan's major makers of tire and other rubber products, reported strong gains in consolidated pretax earnings for 1989. Bridgestone, the No. 1 tire producer, said its pretax profit climbed 15% to 110.33 billion yen ($705.5 million) from 95.56 billion yen in 1988. But net income plunged 76% to 9.644 billion yen, or 12.60 yen a share, from 39.96 billion yen, or 55.73 yen a share.

A company official attributed the sharp fall to foreign exchange losses amounting to 20.67 billion yen. While pretax profits are measured at an exchange rate prevailing when each overseas sale is made, the official explained, net is gauged with the same currency rate at year end, thus often leading to a sharp difference in pretax and after-tax earnings.

Also dragging down net were large losses stemming from rising corporate and resident taxes, and a sluggish performance by Bridgestone/Firestone Inc., a U.S. subsidiary that the official said was saddled with heavy interest burdens and increasing equipment investment.

In the U.S., Bridgestone's sales fell conspicuously in the fourth quarter, as the U.S. economy slowed down, the official said. Unstable economic conditions in Latin America and fierce sales competition in Europe hurt Bridgestone/Firestone's international tire operations.

But Bridgestone's overseas sales, accounting for 59% of overall sales, surged 82% to 999.9 billion yen from 549.7 billion yen in 1988. In the domestic market, sales rose 7% to 689.1 billion yen from 641.6 billion yen, owing to the robust domestic demand-led economic expansion.

Sumitomo Rubber, Japan's third-largest tire maker, reported a 15% rise in pretax profit to 12.69 billion yen from 11.04 billion yen in 1988. Net rose 30% to 6.69 billion yen, or 46.83 yen a share, from 5.16 billion yen, or 37.91 yen a share.

Sales rose 8.1% to 478.61 billion yen from 442.8 billion yen.

A Sumitomo official said the favorable performance resulted from robust domestic demand.

Sales of tires totaled 376.24 billion yen, up 8.2% from 347.73 billion yen. Sporting goods sales rose 3.3% to 69.75 billion yen from 67.49 billion yen.

Sumitomo's overseas performance, including Dunlop Tire Corp. of the U.S., Dunlop France S.A., SP Tyres UK Ltd. and SP Reifenwerke G.m.b.H. of West Germany, was relatively sluggish, compared with that in Japan, where demand for new and replacement tires was strong.
