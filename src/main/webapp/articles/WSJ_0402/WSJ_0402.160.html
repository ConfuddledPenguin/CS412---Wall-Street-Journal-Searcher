Business Brief -- Howard Savings Bank: Lifting Loan-Loss Reserves To Cause Quarterly Deficit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/02/90

Howard Savings Bank, Livingston, N.J., said it plans to add about $30 million to its loan-loss reserves for the first quarter, causing a loss of about $17 million, or $1.16 a share. In the 1989 first quarter, Howard earned $8.5 million, or 59 cents a share. Howard also said its board suspended payment of the quarterly dividend.

Leo J. Rogers Jr., president, said the additional reserves became necessary "because of both the continuing weakness in the region's real estate markets and a further deterioration in loans previously identified by the bank as problem loans."

After the addition to reserves, the bank's allowance for loan losses will be about $112 million, compared with $84.9 million at the end of 1989. The bank anticipates that its nonperforming loans will increase to about $410 million at the end of the first quarter from $314.8 million at year end. Howard said it remains in compliance with all applicable capital standards.

In national over-the-counter trading Friday, Howard shares closed at $5.125, down 12.5 cents.
