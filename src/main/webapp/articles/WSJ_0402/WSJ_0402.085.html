Bank of New York Purchase

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/02/90

NEW YORK -- Bank of New York Co. purchased a 19.9% interest in Inter-Maritime Bank Geneva for a sum believed to be less than $15 million. Inter-Maritime's principal owner is Bruce Rappoport, a Swiss financier who is also one of Bank of New York's largest shareholders.

A Bank of New York spokesman said the purchase reflects the bank's interest in international trade finance.

In 1988, Bank of New York reported that Mr. Rappoport held about two million of the company's shares. The bank's latest filings, which reflect the additional shares issued to acquire Irving Bank Corp. at the end of 1988, don't mention Mr. Rappoport's stake, suggesting that it has fallen below 5%.
