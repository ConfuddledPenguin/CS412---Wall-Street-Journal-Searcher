International Brief -- Pearson PLC: British Publishing Concern's '89 Pretax Profit Rose 26%

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/02/90

Pearson PLC, a British publishing conglomerate that owns the Financial Times, reported 1989 pretax profit rose 26% to #250.5 million ($409.7 million) from #198.9 million a year earlier, helped by gains in all divisions except entertainment. Profit after taxes and minority interests increased 35% to #171.8 million from #126.9 million. Per-share earnings gained 20% to 67 pence from 55.7 pence. Sales jumped 22% to #1.45 billion from #1.19 billion.

The company recommended a final dividend of 12.5 pence a share, which would boost the total payout for the year 19% to 21.5 pence from 18 pence.

Pearson said the results represented "the best-ever profits for the group," with record earnings for its publications, the Financial Times, the Economist and Les Echos, the publishers Addison-Wesley and Longman, financial services interests and Royal Doulton china unit.

Profit attributable to shareholders was boosted by an extraordinary profit of #104.4 million, mostly from shedding assets. Net income rose 57% to #276.2 million from the year-earlier #175.7 million, which included an extraordinary gain of #48.8 million.
