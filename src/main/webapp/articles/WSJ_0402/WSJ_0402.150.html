Eastern Pilot, Fired For Buying Crack, Had Failed Drug Test

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 04/02/90

An Eastern Airlines pilot fired in March after he allegedly bought crack cocaine from an undercover police officer had tested positive for cocaine use seven months earlier. Dain Schneemilch, a 36-year-old flight engineer at Eastern, was arrested Feb. 23 and charged with buying one $20 rock of crack from a police officer in Key Largo, Fla. According to the police records, the transaction was recorded and videotaped.

In the letter notifying Mr. Schneemilch of his dismissal, Eastern said the pilot tested positive for cocaine use July 19 during a routine back-to-work physical examination. Mr. Schneemilch struck the carrier in March 1989 along with most members of the Air Line Pilots Association, but crossed the picket line to return to work.

Although he failed the drug test, Mr. Schneemilch flew as a second officer, or flight engineer, on Eastern's Boeing 727 jets. He was promoted to first officer in October, and was in training when he was arrested. Mr. Schneemilch couldn't be reached for comment.

A spokesman for Eastern said Mr. Schneemilch's positive drug test went unnoticed because of "an oversight." The airline last summer hired 1,000 pilots to replace pilots still striking the carrier, "and somehow his results went into the file with all the negative results," the spokesman said. Mr. Schneemilch "never touched the controls. There was not a thing in his performance to indicate he had a problem."

Eastern has since examined all the drug tests of all pilots hired or re-hired since last year to be certain no other results were similarly misfiled. "The pilot records are clean," the spokesman said.

Separately, Eastern said the possibility of a liquidation at the financially ailing carrier was "exceedingly remote" and not in the interest of unsecured creditors.

The company was responding to reports last week that its unsecured creditors threatened to look into liquidating the carrier if Eastern's parent, Texas Air Corp. of Houston, doesn't agree to play a bigger role in the reorganization. The creditors took that position after Eastern told them it must withdraw from an agreement to pay them 50 cents for every dollar owed.

In a statement issued Friday, Eastern said it was confident that when negotiations with its creditors committee continue that "reason will prevail and the outcome will be one that allows Eastern to successfully emerge from bankruptcy."
