New Stock Listings

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/02/90

NEW YORK -- The New York Stock Exchange added four new stock listings, the American Stock Exchange three and the National Market System of the over-the-counter market seven.

Alliance New Europe Fund Inc. joined the Big Board with the symbol ANE as an initial public offering. The New York closed-end investment company invests primarily in equity securities of European firms.

Emerging Germany Fund Inc. began trading as an initial public offering with the symbol FRG. The New York closed-end investment company invests mainly in securities of small and mid-sized West German companies.

Geneva Steel, a Vineyard, Utah steel products company, also began trading as an initial public offering, under the symbol GNV.

Irish Investment Fund Inc. was listed as an initial public offering with the symbol IRL. The Chicago closed-end investment company invests primarily in equity securities of Irish companies. On the American Stock Exchange, Epitope Inc., a Beaverton, Ore., biotechnology, research and development company began trading under EPT. The company previously traded on the over-the-counter market.

Santa Monica Bank, Santa Monica, Calif., will begin trading capital shares Wednesday under the symbol SMO and voting trust certificates under the symbol SMV. The company previously traded over the counter and operates banks in the metropolitan Los Angeles area.

NFC PLC a Bedford, England, international transport, logistics, property and services company will begin trading ADR's Friday under the symbol NFC. The company, whose U.S. interests include Allied Van Lines Inc., also trades on the London International Stock Exchange. On the over-the-counter market, America's All Season Fund Inc., a Winter Park, Fla., closed-end investment company will begin trading tomorrow under the symbol FUND.

Elm Financial Services Inc., an Elmhurst, Ill., bank holding company for Elmhurst Federal Savings Bank, began trading under the symbol ELMF.

Hornbeck Offshore Services Inc., a Galveston, Texas, operator of offshore oil and gas vessels, will start trading tomorrow under the symbol HOSS.

Syntellect Inc. started trading under the symbol SYNL. The Phoenix, Ariz., company makes interactive voice response systems that allow individuals to access computer information through a touch-tone phone.

Tokos Medical Corp., began trading under the symbol TKOS. The Santa Ana, Calif., company provides specialized home health care for pregnant women.

Unilab Corp., a New York medical services and products holding company that provides laboratory testing services, will begin trading tomorrow under the symbol ULAB.

VeriFone Inc. will start trading under VFIC. The Redwood City, Calif., company makes point-of-sale terminals that automate credit-card payment transactions.
