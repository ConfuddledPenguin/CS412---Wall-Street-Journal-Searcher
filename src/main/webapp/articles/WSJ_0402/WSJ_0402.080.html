Texas Meridian Agrees To Buy 65% Interest In American National

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/02/90

HOUSTON -- Texas Meridian Resources Ltd. said that it agreed to acquire a 65% interest in American National Petroleum Co. for $4.50 a share, or about $18.4 million, and that it intends to merge the two companies' businesses. Texas Meridian, an oil and gas master limited partnership, will buy its largest block, nearly 3.5 million shares, from San Francisco-based Ironstone Group Inc. for $15.6 million. Ironstone owns a total of about 4.2 million shares, or about 63% of American National. Ironstone said it expects to realize a pretax gain of about $6.3 million on the sale.

The balance of Texas Meridian's purchase of nearly 4.1 million shares will be from certain shareholders contractually related to Ironstone. As part of the transaction, American National will also sell to Ironstone some energy-related investments not part of its primary oil and gas exploration and production business.

Texas Meridian's purchases depend on the signing of a definitive agreement for the combination of the two companies' businesses into a Texas Meridian subsidiary, plus certain other conditions.

Details of that business combination haven't been completed, said Michael J. Mayell, Texas Meridian president. But he said holders of the two companies would share in the resulting operation according to the value of each firm's the oil and gas properties. A combination would need approval by American National and Texas Meridian directors and stockholders or unitholders.
