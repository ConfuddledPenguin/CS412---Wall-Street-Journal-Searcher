Great Pacific Capital Increases to 11.13% Its Stake in Ipco Corp.

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/02/90

WHITE PLAINS, N.Y. -- Great Pacific Capital Ltd., a New York investment concern owned by a Canadian financier, Jim Pattison, said it raised to 11.13% its stake in Ipco Corp.'s common shares outstanding from 9.37% last November. In a Securities and Exchange Commission filing, Great Pacific said it holds 563,600 Ipco shares, including 77,800 acquired between Jan. 30 and Tuesday, for $7.125 to $8.125 each. In New York Stock Exchange composite trading Friday, Ipco shares closed at $7.75, down 25 cents.

Ipco operates a chain of about 250 Sterling Optical and Ipco Optical stores in 20 U.S. states and Canada, which generated earnings last year of about $453,000 on sales of $123.9 million.

Ipco officials weren't available to comment on the filing, and a Great Pacific executive wouldn't elaborate. Great Pacific said previously that it may buy additional Ipco shares or "make a proposal with respect to a business combination," among other things, depending on market conditions and other factors.

Mr. Pattison, based in Vancouver, British Columbia, has various closely held companies that generate aggregate annual sales of more than two billion Canadian dollars (US$1.71 billion). He formed Great Pacific in 1987 to search for U.S. acquisitions.

In a recent interview, Mr. Pattison said that he wants to acquire U.S. assets to help diversify his holdings, and that he is mainly interested in retail or consumer product companies. "We're comfortable serving the public," he said. Mr. Pattison's holdings include car dealerships, grocery stores, and advertising and packaging companies.
