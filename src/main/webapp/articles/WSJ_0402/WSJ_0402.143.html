Hoax Is Suspected in Fire Reported at Libyan Plant

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/02/90

WASHINGTON -- The U.S. now suspects that an apparent fire March 14 at a Libyan plant allegedly used for making chemical weapons may not have damaged the facility extensively and may have been a hoax. Satellite photos taken in the days after the announcement of a fire appeared to show the building at Rabta blackened, though intact. Other information from unspecified sources led the U.S. to conclude that the equipment in the building had been badly damaged and the plant wasn't able to operate.

The Libyan government blamed the West, especially West Germany, for the blaze, and reported casualties and arrests in the incident, while maintaining its posture that the Rabta facility makes pharmaceuticals, not chemical weapons.

But as intelligence officials analyzed more evidence, the circumstances of the incident have become vaguer instead of clearer, Bush administration officials said, raising suspicions that the plant may not have suffered much or any damage.

Asked whether the fire might have been a Libyan hoax to trip up a U.S. campaign to get the plant dismantled, State Department spokesman Richard Boucher said, "I can't rule that out." One U.S. official said the administration was actively considering the possibility that the whole event was staged. He said intelligence analysts are studying a claim made by a Libyan exile group that the fire was set using old tires and other debris as fuel.
