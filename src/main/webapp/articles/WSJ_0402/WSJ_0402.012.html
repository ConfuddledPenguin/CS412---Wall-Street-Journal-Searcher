Grand Met Expects 15% Profit Rise In Fiscal 1990

  • Author: Richard Gibson
  • Date: 04/02/90

MIAMI -- Grand Metropolitan PLC expects a 15% increase in earnings per share in fiscal 1990 and believes that its U.S. businesses will generate half its profits, Chairman Allen Sheppard predicted. The forecast assumes a 5% inflation rate and 10% real earnings growth, Mr. Sheppard said. In the year ended last Sept. 30, the London-based food, retailing and drinks concern posted per-share earnings of 55.6 pence, an 18.5% gain from fiscal 1988.

Mr. Sheppard made his prediction while addressing analysts, primarily from Britain, who this week toured some of Grand Met's U.S. properties, including Heublein distillers, Pearle health services, Pillsbury food and the Burger King fast-food chain.

Contending that "we don't have any bad businesses," Mr. Sheppard said Grand Met will concentrate near-term on expanding Burger King and Pearle as "international brandings," while developing "critical mass" for its food businesses in Central Europe.

"We're looking for a stable of international brands -- that's our strategy," Mr. Sheppard said, adding that Grand Met also is searching for "another Pearle that everybody else has missed in service retailing."

Mr. Sheppard and Group Finance Director David Nash also said they hope to have Grand Met American Depositary Receipts listed on the New York Stock Exchange by September. ADRs are receipts for shares of a foreign-based corporation, which trade much like stock in the U.S.

"If we list I'd like to be able to issue some stock," Mr. Nash said, indicating a total offering of perhaps $100 million. He and other Grand Met executives told analysts they would expect considerable demand for such an offering in the U.S.

Grand Met has received the first round of bids for its U.S.-based Alpo Petfoods Inc. unit and is "in the middle of conversations with a number of serious purchasers," the company said.

Grand Met recently put Alpo up for sale because its goal of building it into a billion-dollar business seemed unobtainable, said Ian Martin, chief executive of Grand Met's food sector.

Mr. Martin termed Alpo's recent entry into the cat-food business "very successful." Although its market share is only around 1%, he said would-be buyers have been "surprised at the success" of the new business.

Reviewing Grand Met's extensive retailing (licensed restaurants) group in Europe, Mr. Sheppard acknowledged that Grand Met had "misread" the eating-out market in the United Kingdom but is "back on track now."

As for overseas expansion of Pillsbury brands, Mr. Martin said he foresees "significant" opportunities for Green Giant vegetables and Haagen-Dazs premium ice cream. He said the company is seriously considering building a Haagen-Dazs plant somewhere in Europe.
