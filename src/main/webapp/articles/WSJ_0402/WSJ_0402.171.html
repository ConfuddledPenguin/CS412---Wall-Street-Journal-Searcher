Business Brief -- Philips Industries Inc.: Pretax Charge for the Year To Total Up to $50 Million

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/02/90

Philips Industries Inc. said it will take a pretax charge of $45 million to $50 million for the year ended Saturday. The Dayton, Ohio, maker of motorhome and housing parts said it will take an additional pretax charge of as much as $12 million if a merger agreement with a group formed by Merrill Lynch Capital Partners Inc., a unit of Merrill Lynch & Co., is terminated.

The group has been unable to obtain financing for the proposed offer of $700 million, or $24 a share.

Philips also said it is providing certain information to another party that has indicated possible interest in a transaction with Philips. The company said neither Merrill Lynch nor any other party has made any new proposals to Philips.

The company also said it will sell the following subsidiaries: Shelby Advanced Automotive Technology Inc., Mid-West Conveyor Co., Dearborn Fabricating & Engineering Co. Shelby makes aluminum wheels for autos, while the other two make heavy conveyors for the auto industry.
