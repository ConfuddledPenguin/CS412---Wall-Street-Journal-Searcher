Former Thrift Holder Agrees to Guilty Plea On Criminal Charges

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/02/90

DALLAS -- A former shareholder of the defunct Southwest Savings & Loan Association of Abilene, Texas, agreed to plead guilty to artificially inflating the thrift's net worth to raise its lending limits. Raymond Foy Phillips, 54 years old, was a prime beneficiary of the increase in Southwest's lending limits, according to documents filed Friday in connection with his plea agreement in Dallas federal court.

The defunct Abilene thrift, which is unrelated to Southwest Savings Association of Dallas, had $162.5 million in assets and negative regulatory capital of $202.7 million in August 1988. Two months later, Southwest was sold with 10 other thrifts to the Adam Group, an owner of cable television stations based in Bryan, Texas. The thrifts are now part of Olney Savings Association.

Mr. Phillips, who acquired a significant block of Southwest stock in 1983, used several different company names to borrow funds from the thrift for the purchase of 292 acres of land in Houston, according to court documents.

When Southwest reached its legal lending limits, based on its net worth, Mr. Phillips agreed to purchase certain assets of the thrift at inflated prices, allowing it to book false profits and boost its net worth. In one instance, Southwest lent Mr. Phillips $2.4 million to enable him to purchase its own assets, according to court documents.

Mr. Phillips, who is charged with conspiring to pump up Southwest's net worth, is the first person to face criminal charges in connection with the thrift's failure. He is cooperating in further investigations by U.S. Justice Department's Dallas Bank Fraud Task Force.
