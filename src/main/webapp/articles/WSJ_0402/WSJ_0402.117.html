International: Mexico Reopens Program To Swap Foreign Debt

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/02/90

MEXICO CITY -- Mexico reopened its debt-for-equity swap program, which had been suspended since November 1987. Under the revamped program, $3.5 billion dollars in Mexican foreign debt is to be swapped during the next 3 1/2 years for ownership interests in infrastructure projects and state-owned companies undergoing public offerings. The minimum discount the government said it would accept is 35 cents on the dollar of debt being swapped.

The new swap program is more limited in scope than the original, in which banks traded debt outstanding for local currency that was then used to buy into local companies. By restricting the program to swaps of debt for infrastructure or state-owned enterprises the government won't have to print new money or issue more domestic debt.
