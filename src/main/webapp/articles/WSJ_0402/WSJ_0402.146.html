Business Brief -- Colorado-Ute Electric Association: Electric Power Firm Files For Chapter 11 Protection

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/02/90

Colorado-Ute Electric Association said it filed a voluntary petition for protection under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code to resolve its financial difficulties. The Montrose, Colo., wholesale electric-power producer said the filing follows the suspension of merger negotiations with TriState Generation and Transmission Association. The negotiations were suspended because of a tentative decision by the Colorado Public Utilities Commission on March 8 to reduce Colorado-Ute's wholesale rates to its 14-member distribution systems.

Colorado-Ute said the bankruptcy-law filing won't affect its normal operations. About 200,000 customers receive electricity from its systems.

The utility said that Tri-State has withheld advances of funds under the revolving loan agreement and that Colorado-Ute "can no longer operate without protection under the federal Bankruptcy Code."

Colorado-Ute said its situation has been further "confused by conflicting orders" from regulators concerning the wholesale rate it should charge its member-owners. It said its directors will discuss the matter at the company's next regularly scheduled board meeting in April.
