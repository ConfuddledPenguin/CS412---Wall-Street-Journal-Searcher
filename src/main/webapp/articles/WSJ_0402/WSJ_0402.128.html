International Brief -- Robert Horne Group PLC: Buehrmann-Tetterode Offers $251.9 Million for Company

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/02/90

The Dutch paper and packaging manufacturing group Buehrmann-Tetterode N.V. offered a bid of #154 million ($251.9 million) for the U.K. paper distributor Robert Horne Group PLC. The takeover bid has been widely anticipated since Buehrmann-Tetterode announced in mid-January that it was conducting talks with Robert Horne.

Buehrmann-Tetterode said that it was prepared to offer for each ordinary voting share 490 pence ($8.01) while it would pay 452 pence ($7.39) for each ordinary nonvoting share.

Shareholders of Robert Horne would have the option of receiving payment in cash, or as much as 30% of the value of the offer in Buehrmann-Tetterode bearer depositary receipts. Also, shareholders will be offered loan notes from Buehrmann-Tetterode or any combination of cash, depositary receipts or loan notes.

Although the takeover is friendly, with both sides agreeing to the terms, it is also conditional on Buehrmann-Tetterode's acquiring 90% of the ordinary voting and nonvoting shares outstanding.
