Marketing & Media: MCA Executive Gets $7.8 Million In Stock Options

  • Author: Richard Turner
  • Date: 04/02/90

UNIVERSAL CITY, Calif. -- Taking note of dizzying increases in compensation for movie executives, MCA Inc. did the right thing by its film chief Thomas Pollock, breaking with tradition and giving Mr. Pollock stock options that had a value of $7.8 million at the time MCA directors approved them. According to proxy materials mailed to shareholders Friday, MCA will ask holders to approve the company's first stock-option plan in a generation, a plan that calls for the issue of five million new shares. The only beneficiary of the plan so far is Mr. Pollock, who would receive options to buy 600,000 shares at an exercise price of $55 a share, which were valued at $68 a share at the time they were awarded last August.

But the shares closed at $51 a share Friday, so they wouldn't be worth anything today. Mr. Pollock may exercise the options in 20% yearly increments beginning in July.

In addition, Mr. Pollock receives a salary increase to $850,000 a year, from $636,692, as part of a new five-year contract signed last year. He also receives 95,000 shares of stock worth $5.7 million, and will receive 150,000 more shares during the next two years. Company Chairman Lew Wasserman receives a salary increase to $900,000 a year, from $559,000, while President Sidney Sheinberg, goes to $900,000 a year from $729,000.

Mr. Sheinberg also received stock valued at $7.3 million.

MCA, with Hollywood's most long-serving management team by far, has always been relatively conservative about executive compensation. A stock option plan was adopted by shareholders in 1968, but never exercised, and though an incentive stock plan for executives has existed since 1975, the new "stock awards plan" breaks new ground.

Proxy materials argue that the plan is for "attracting and retaining executive personnel, motivating executive personnel by means of growth-related incentives {and} providing incentive compensation opportunities that are competitive with those of other major corporations."

These other corporations include Walt Disney Co., whose chairman Michael Eisner's salary, bonus and options totaled about $40 million in fiscal 1988. Disney President Frank Wells realized $72.4 million from the sale of stock bought through options in 1989, and former Chief Financial Officer Gary L. Wilson exercised options valued at $47.5 million.
