Minneapolis Fed to Search For Larger Headquarters

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/02/90

MINNEAPOLIS -- For sale: The Federal Reserve Bank of Minneapolis building. After months of study, bank officials decided the structure not only is too small but also that removing hazardous asbestos insulation would be too costly.

Built in 1972, the structure is an award-winning example of suspension-bridge architecture. Its 10-story reflective-glass office tower seems to hang between two pillars on a block-sized downtown plaza. The bank's vault is underground.

Initially, the bank's staff recommended temporarily relocating while asbestos was removed and other renovations and repairs were made. But officials abandoned that plan after determining that the 550,000-square-foot structure couldn't be easily expanded to meet growing space requirements.

Bank officials now are seeking to build, buy or lease a larger facility. A spokesman said the current building could be sold, leased or perhaps traded. "We're open to all options," he said.
