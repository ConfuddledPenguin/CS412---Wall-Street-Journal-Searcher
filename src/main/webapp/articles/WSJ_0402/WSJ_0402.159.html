International: Taiwan Ministry Plans Offering of Stocks In Three State Banks

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 04/02/90

TAIPEI, Taiwan -- The Finance Ministry of Taiwan said it will sell 46.3 million shares valued at 25.76 billion New Taiwan dollars (US$975.7 million) of three state-owned banks on the Taiwan Stock Exchange to quicken the pace of its public-offering plan. The ministry said it will sell 23 million shares of First Commercial Bank, 2.2 million shares of Hua Nan Commercial Bank and 21.1 million shares of Chang Hwa Commercial Bank. The Bank of Communications will be underwriter. The stocks will be sold from April 14 to 19.

The underwriting price for First Commercial Bank will be at NT$561.96 a share, NT$587.35 a share for Hua Nan Commercial Bank and NT$545.88 a share for Chang Hwa Commercial Bank.

The Taiwan provincial government is also planning to sell more than 100 million of its shares later. The government holds stakes of 74.4% in First Commercial, 58.1% in Hua Nan and 56.2% in Chang Hwa. The sales will leave the government with 51% of the shares of the three banks.

Brokers expected the news to boost the stock market.

Taiwan launched its public-offering drive in March 1989 by selling about 150 million shares of state-owned China Steel Corp. The proposal to slash the government holdings in the three banks was approved by Taiwan's cabinet in May 1989. The government has also agreed to a public offer or partial public offer of 15 other state-owned companies such as China Shipbuilding Corp. and Taiwan Machinery Manufacturing Corp.
