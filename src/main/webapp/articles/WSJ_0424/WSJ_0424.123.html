Business Brief -- New World Development Co.: Sale of 17 Ramada Hotels For $183 Million Planned

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/24/90

New World Hotels Ltd., which completed the acquisition of Ramada Inc. four months ago, said it agreed to sell 17 of the unit's U.S. hotels for $183 million. The Hong Kong hotel group, which is itself the focus of a privatization bid by its parent, New World Development Co., is to sell 11 hotels to Wyndham Hotel Co., a Texas limited partnership, for $116 million, in a transaction expected to be completed May 21.

Hospitality Unlimited Investments Co., of California, and an unidentified individual have also agreed to buy six Ramada Inns for $67 million. It may also purchase a further hotel in Rosemont, Ill., for $35 million. The purchases are expected to be completed by July 16.

New World has long hinted at the sales as a way for it to reduce debt associated with the $540 million Ramada purchase.
