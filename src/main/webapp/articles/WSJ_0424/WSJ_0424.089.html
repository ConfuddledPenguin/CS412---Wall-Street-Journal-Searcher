Dividend News: Norwich Financial Corp.

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/24/90

NORWICH FINANCIAL Corp., citing a quarterly loss because of problem loans, reduced its quarterly dividend 67% to five cents a share from 15 cents. The Norwich, Conn., bank holding company said the dividend is payable May 18 to stock of record May 4. Norwich yesterday posted a first-quarter loss of $14.3 million, or $2.75 a share, including a provision for loan losses during the quarter of $16.5 million, compared with year-earlier profit of $1.4 million, or 25 cents a share. Norwich said its loan-loss provision reflected "concerns over the continuing deterioration in the commercial real estate market in Connecticut" and other factors. "In view of the current loss, under state statute, the company is unable to maintain a 15-cent {a share} quarterly dividend throughout 1990 without regulatory approval," Norwich said. The company said it expects to continue paying a quarterly dividend of five cents a share throughout this year.
