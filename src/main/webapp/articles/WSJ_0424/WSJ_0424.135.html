Northeast Utilities Purchase Plan Cleared On PS New Hampshire

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/24/90

MANCHESTER, N.H. -- Bankruptcy Court Judge James Yacos confirmed a plan by Northeast Utilities to acquire Public Service Co. of New Hampshire for about $2.3 billion. The judge's endorsement of the reorganization plan for PS New Hampshire follows its approval last month by the company's shareholders and creditors.

The bankruptcy settlement is still subject to approval by New Hampshire state regulators of Northeast's plan for 5.5% annual rate increases over seven years. State regulators recently began hearings on the request; the "target date" for their decision is the end of June, according to a spokesman for Hartford, Conn.-based Northeast.

The acquisition of PS New Hampshire by Northeast also will be subject to review by federal regulators. Northeast will, however, begin supervising the New Hampshire utility's operations as of April 30, a spokesman for PS New Hampshire said.
