P-I-E Nationwide Gets Authority to Control Transcon Lines Inc.

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/24/90

JACKSONVILLE, Fla. -- In a move toward further consolidation in the trucking industry, P-I-E Nationwide Inc. said it was authorized by federal regulators to take control of Transcon Lines Inc. Both trucking companies are part of the $14 billion "less-than-truckload" industry. Such companies carry several small shipments in a single trailer. The industry has been battered of late by pricing wars that have cut into profits.

Transcon, formerly a unit of Transcon Inc. in Beverly Hills, Calif, was sold earlier this month to closely held Growth Finance Corp. in Miami. The price wasn't disclosed. In 1989, Transcon Lines had revenue of $226.5 million and operating losses of $31.6 million.

P-I-E said Transcon had requested that P-I-E take control of all freight in its system and deliver it to customers. Last Friday, the Interstate Commerce Commission in Washington cleared the transaction.

Terms of the deal weren't disclosed.

P-I-E said the move wasn't a merger but rather an "absorption" of one company's business into another. P-I-E said that the transition has gone smoothly and that all Transcon freight will be delivered this week.

P-I-E is also closely held. Olympia Holding Corp., based in Miami, acquired a majority interest in P-I-E earlier this month. Terms weren't disclosed. In 1989, P-I-E had revenue of about $500 million.

Officials at Transcon couldn't be reached for comment.
