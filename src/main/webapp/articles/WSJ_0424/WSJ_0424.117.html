Business Brief -- Avon Products Inc.: Profit More Than Doubled In 1st Period; Revenue Fell

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/24/90

Avon Products Inc. reported that first-quarter profit more than doubled to $15.2 million from a year earlier, aided by cost-cutting and lower interest expenses. The cosmetics concern's net income rose to $15.2 million, or 11 cents a share, from $6.2 million a year earlier, which resulted in a net loss of five cents a share after the preferred dividend requirement.

First-quarter sales fell slightly to $703.8 million from $704.9 million a year earlier. Avon said its retail fragrance sales fell 44% reflecting the sale of Parfums Stern, the fragrance division that Avon sold last January. Excluding Parfums Stern, Avon's sales for the quarter rose 4% from a year earlier.

In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange, Avon shares closed yesterday at $35.375, down 50 cents.

Avon said that its interest expense fell 13% in the first quarter from a year earlier. The company added that it is progressing on reducing its debt, which at the end of the first quarter was $695.9 million, compared with $1.13 billion a year earlier.
