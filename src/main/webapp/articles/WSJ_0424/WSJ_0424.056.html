AT&T Debt Gets Upgrade From Moody's

  • Author: John J. Keller
  • Date: 04/24/90

NEW YORK -- Moody's Investors Service Inc. said it upgraded its rating on American Telephone & Telegraph Co.'s $9.2 billion in senior debt, the first such boost for the company since the 1984 breakup of the Bell System. The rating concern said it based the upgrade, to double-A-3 from single-A-1, on AT&T's recent reorganization into a "much more efficient and competitive business entity . . . {and its} ongoing cost reduction programs." Moody's said the steps would improve profit and cash flow, noting that AT&T has put most of its large restructuring costs behind it.

An AT&T spokesman said of the action by Moody's, "They reflect the same optimism we have."

The new rating isn't the highest ever at AT&T. In 1982, when the company still owned and ran the Bell System, its senior debt was rated triple-A-3.

Moody's said on March 12 that it was considering the upgrade, which includes AT&T Euronotes and Eurobonds and senior debts of AT&T Credit Corp. and AT&T Technologies Inc., which are guaranteed by the parent.

Moody's on Feb. 1 confirmed its single-A-1 rating for the telecommunications company.

The latest announcement said AT&T's reorganization, together with cost-cutting, will result in "enhanced profitability and more predictable cash flow protection for debt." Cash flow is net income plus depreciation. Moody's said it assumes that new pricing flexibility being allowed by the Federal Communications Commission for long-distance services will continue.

Moody's said it expects that AT&T's debt will remain relatively high during the intermediate term, as the company continues to spend heavily for global competition through internal growth and acquisitions in both its manufacturing businesses and financial services.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, AT&T shares closed at $40.375, down 50 cents.
