International Brief -- Internationale Wagons-Lits: Group Net Fell 46% in '89; Problems of Europcar Cited

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/24/90

Cie. Internationale des Wagons-Lits & du Tourisme reported a 46% plunge in group profit in 1989 in the absence of extraordinary earnings and amid problems for its Europcar car-hire venture. Group profit fell to 1.46 billion Belgian francs ($41.9 million) from 2.68 billion francs in 1988, the Belgium-based travel and tourism group said. Group revenue gained 21% to 96.44 billion francs from 80.02 billion francs in 1988.

Operating profit edged up 6.8% to 3.096 billion francs.

Wagons-Lits said that the group's results in 1989 followed an exceptional year in 1988, when the group had earned a windfall profit on the sale of part of an office building in Paris.

But Wagons-Lits' 1989 results also were influenced by the poor performance of the company's Europcar unit, which was hit by the rise in interest rates and a slump in the second-hand automobile market in Britain.
