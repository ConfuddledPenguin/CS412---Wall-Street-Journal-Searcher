REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: Public Non-Financing

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/24/90

The closest thing this country has to a national referendum is a box on everyone's 1040 IRS form. Since 1973 filers have been asked if they wanted $1 of their taxes to go to a presidential-election fund. Though they are explicitly told that checking "yes" won't raise their taxes, the number of people who want to subsidize White House aspirants is obviously diminishing. It fell from 29% in 1980 to 20% last year. Despite this overwhelming rejection, Washington is overflowing with proposals at the moment to extend public financing to Congress. Not surprisingly, no one is even suggesting that the money be raised by giving taxpayers a "vote" on the matter with a checkoff system for congressional elections. The results would likely be embarrassing.
