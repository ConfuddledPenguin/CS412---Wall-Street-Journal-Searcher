Meredith Net Rose 2.1% to $4.8 Million In Fiscal 3rd Quarter

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/24/90

DES MOINES, Iowa -- Meredith Corp. said net income rose 2.1% in the third quarter ended March 31 to $4.8 million, or 26 cents a share, from $4.7 million, or 25 cents a share, a year earlier. Excluding earnings from the Meredith/Burda commercial printing operations, which the company has agreed to sell to Donnelly & Sons Co., profit fell 33% to $2 million, or 11 cents a share, from $2.9 million, or 16 cents a share.

The company cited lower profit margins in its book-publishing business and soft revenue at its television stations for the decline. Meredith said it expects the sale of Meredith/Burda to be completed in the fourth quarter.

Revenue rose 1.2% to $199.1 million from $196.7 million.

Profits from Meredith's magazines, which include Ladies' Home Journal, Better Homes and Gardens and other publications, were "significantly higher" than a year ago, the company said.

Book-publishing operations, however, were hurt by increased manufacturing costs and other expenses.

For the first nine months, net fell 9% to $23.5 million, or $1.27 a share, from $25.7 million, or $1.35 a share, a year earlier. Revenue rose 4% to $606.7 million from $583.1 million.
