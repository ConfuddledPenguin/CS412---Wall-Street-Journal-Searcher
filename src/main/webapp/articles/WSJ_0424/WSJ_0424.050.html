New Securities Issues

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/24/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: MUNICIPALS

State Environmental Improvement and Energy Resources Authority, Missouri -- $60 million of environmental improvement revenue bonds, Union Electric Co. project, Series 1990 A, due May 1, 2020, were priced by a Goldman, Sachs & Co. group to yield 7.57% in 2020. The single tranche of 7.40% bonds is priced at 98 and rated single-A-2 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and single-A by Standard & Poor's Corp.

OTHER

Federal Farm Credit Banks -- $250 million of notes, due May 1, 1994, was priced at par for an initial yield of 9.25%. The notes will be reoffered at various prices through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets. The notes, which are non-callable for two years, were priced at a spread of 0.35 percentage point above the Treasury's four-year note.

EUROBONDS

Japan Finance Corp. for Municipal Enterprises (Japan) -- $150 million of 9 3/4% bonds due May 18, 2000, priced at 101 1/4 to yield 9.87% less fees, via Bank of Tokyo Capital Markets. Guaranteed by the government of Japan. Fees 2.

Interfinance Credit National N.V. (French parent) -- 500 million French francs ($87.7 million) of 10 1/4% bonds due May 21, 1993, priced at 101 to yield 10.61% less full fees via Paribas Capital Markets Ltd. Guaranteed by Credit National. Fees 1 3/8.

Daiwa Overseas Finance Ltd. (Japanese parent) -- $67 million of 10% bonds due May 17, 2000, priced at 102 to yield 10%, via Morgan Stanley International. Guaranteed by Daiwa Bank. Once-only call option at 100 in 1994.

Heller Financial Inc. -- 10 billion yen ($63.5 million) of floating-rate notes due June 1995 priced at 100.15 via Goldman Sachs International Ltd. Interest will be paid at the six-month London interbank offered rate flat. Callable and puttable at par on the second, third and fourth anniversaries of the issue date.

Kingdom of Spain (sovereign) -- 500 million European currency units of 10 3/4% bonds due May 22, 1995, priced at 100.20 to yield 11.21% via Banco Bilbao Vizcaya and Banco Espanol de Credito (Banesto).
