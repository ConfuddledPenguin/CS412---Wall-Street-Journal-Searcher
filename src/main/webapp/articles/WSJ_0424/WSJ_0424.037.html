Letters to the Editor: Shooting Down Bison Is Anything but a `Hunt'

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/24/90

In regard to Ruth Rudner's April 3 Leisure & Arts article "Where the Buffalo Don't Roam": Permitting the shooting of bison that wander outside the boundaries of Yellowstone Park makes no more sense than declaring open season on animals that escape from their cages at the zoo. Park officials' claim of "keeping animals wild by allowing them natural movement through their natural habitat" is ludicrous. Are bears that live off garbage and wander up to tourists' cars begging handouts natural? Yes, "population control has to happen," but surely it can be accomplished more humanely. I saw scenes of the "hunt" on TV -- animal-rights activists pleading to have a bleeding, struggling animal put out of its misery while "hunters" stood about laughing, prolonging the creature's terrible suffering because they got a kick out of the effect it had on the activists. Even the park rangers, condoning this behavior and chasing away those who attempt to keep the animals within park boundaries, don't behave like real rangers in Yellowstone.

Christine Dale

Nanuet, N.Y.

---

While Ms. Rudner's article did a fairly accurate job of portraying a complex situation, attention should be brought to a coloring of an important fact. Ms. Rudner states, "Because {bison} can carry brucellosis, which causes cattle to abort if they come into contact with the fresh afterbirth of an aborted bison fetus, ranchers are understandably concerned if bison cross the boundary."

That sounds like reasonable justification for the hunt -- but the Humane Society says there isn't a case on record of brucellosis being passed from wild bison to domestic cows. The ranchers' stated reason for protection is actually nothing more than a smoke screen to masquerade their true motives -- to eliminate competition from increasingly scarce grazing lands.

It's time the public took notice of this travesty, and let Congress know that it won't put up with the senseless slaughter of an American treasure.

Steven E. Rovner

Jersey City, N.J.
