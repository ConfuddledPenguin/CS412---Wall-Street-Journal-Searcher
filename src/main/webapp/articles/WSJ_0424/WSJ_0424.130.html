What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/24/90

SIR JAMES GOLDSMITH dropped his pursuit of B.A.T Industries, thwarted by growing opposition to highly leveraged hostile takeovers. The financier, whose initial $21.13 billion bid for B.A.T was the second-largest in history, faced obstacles in several U.S. states where the British giant had operations. The final blow apparently was California's recent decision to bar a change in control of B.A.T's Farmers Insurance unit. ---

General Motors expects to shut a Michigan assembly plant in 1993, the latest in a series of closings due to slowing sales. The UAW union has vowed to make job security a top issue in coming contract talks with GM.

The Supreme Court refused to hear an appeal by GM over a warranty-related suit that may become a class action for nearly 600,000 customers.

---

AT&T senior debt totaling $9.2 billion was upgraded by Moody's, the first ratings boost since the 1984 breakup of the Bell system.

---

The IRS may be reluctant to loosen a tax regulation that some investors say thwarts free trade in debt securities. The decision, expected this week, involves reviving bearer bonds.

---

Exxon's profit fell 29% in the first quarter due to one-time items, while Amoco's net rose 1.7%. Higher oil prices boosted exploration and production results at both firms but hurt chemical and refining margins.

---

Stock prices sank amid continued worries about higher interest rates, while bond prices fell modestly. The Dow Jones industrials closed off 29.28 points, at 2666.67. The dollar rose.

Oil prices continued to rebound on speculation that OPEC may agree soon to curb its overproduction.

---

Coca-Cola is moving into East Germany with a $140 million investment. The soft-drink giant hopes to match its success in West Germany.

---

A key Gulf Power witness pleaded guilty to contempt and tax charges. Ray Howell also agreed to cooperate with the federal probe of political contributions by the Southern unit.

---

Three Asian nations were accused by the U.S. of exporting more than $1 billion of synthetic-fiber sweaters to America at unfairly low prices.

---

Time Warner posted a $51 million loss for the first quarter, reflecting the continued cost of its merger last year. The company said earnings hit a record if those costs are excluded.

---

A proxy fight will be reviewed by the Supreme Court to determine if shareholders may sue under federal law for misleading proxy statements.

---

A group of disabled people has joined forces with home builders to oppose proposed guidelines to make apartment buildings more accessible.
