International: Powerless Mondays Arrive in Philippines; Many Businesses Close

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/24/90

MANILA, the Philippines -- Powerless Mondays arrived in Manila, causing thousands of companies to begin a series of once-a-week closings. The daylong power cut, which didn't affect factories, marked the most severe step yet to ease the country's serious power shortage. Together with water shortages, traffic jams and continuing concerns about political instability, the cut added to the frayed nerves in the capital that have accompanied the heat.

"It's really ridiculous," said an American chemicals manufacturer who spent much of Monday operating from his apartment terrace dressed in shorts and a T-shirt. "Something should have been done a long time ago."

Foreign business executives have said for months that the power crisis will further hinder the efforts by the Philippines to attract new investment. Those efforts already have been hurt by December's coup attempt and perceptions of continued political instability.

The program of Monday power cuts, which was announced last month and affects all of Luzon island, is aimed at minimizing the impact of the electricity shortage during the rest of the week. Under the plan, offices agree to institute four-day workweeks in return for government assurances that power cuts on other days will be minimal. During the past month, businesses, factories and residences have endured four to six hours a day without electricity.

Although factories aren't participating in the plan, the government has asked them to voluntarily shut down or use emergency generators for as long as 12 hours a day once a week.

The government has said the Monday cuts will continue at least through May.
