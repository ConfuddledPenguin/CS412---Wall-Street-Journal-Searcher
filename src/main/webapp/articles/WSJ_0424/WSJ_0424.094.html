Ryder System Posts 72% Decline in Profit For the First Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/24/90

MIAMI -- Ryder System Inc. reported that first-quarter net income fell 72% to $7.3 million, or seven cents a share, in another dismal quarter for the struggling transportation-services company. M. Anthony Burns, chairman and chief executive, said the company, continued to be hurt by among other things, sluggishness in commercial truck rentals. A year earlier Ryder earned $26.2 million, or 31 cents a share. Revenue rose slightly to $1.25 billion from $1.22 billion a year earlier.

The latest results were in line with analysts' forecasts for the company. As expected, the automotive carrier division, crippled by the worst U.S. automobile industry slump in a decade, reported a loss of $2.3 million, compared with net income of $9.4 million a year earlier.

On the aviation services side, gains in Ryder's aircraft engine overhaul and new-parts distribution businesses were offset by continued softness in leasing of used aircraft, and sales of reusable aircraft parts.

There were some bright spots, however. The company said revenue increased in its huge Vehicle Leasing and Services division. In addition, the company said that consumer truck rental prices are finally stabilizing after last year's lengthy price war with competitor U-Haul International Inc.

This led Mr. Burns to express confidence in Ryder's performance over the remainder of 1990. "When you look behind the numbers, it is clear that, while demand is still soft, the work we have been doing is bending the trends in the right direction," he said. "We believe the March results indicate that our comparisons will be more favorable in the second and third quarters."
