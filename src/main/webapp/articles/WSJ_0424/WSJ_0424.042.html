Japan to Require Report On Big Holder Changes

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 04/24/90

TOKYO -- The Finance Ministry has decided to require domestic securities companies to file a report whenever there is a change in major shareholders. Ministry officials said the securities houses, numbering some 220, will be notified of the decision this week.

The step will be taken, one official said, in an effort to "ensure the independence of securities companies," indicating that the ministry is anxious to prevent unfriendly takeovers just when the financial conditions of securities houses, especially smaller ones, have been deteriorating as a result of recent sharp declines in the Tokyo stock market.

Specifically, officials said, the securities companies are to report when someone acquires more than 5% of the outstanding shares or when a shareholder who already owns more than 5% buys or sells more than 1% of his shares.

A report is to be filed when talks start in the case of friendly share acquisitions or when a securities company finds out about major shareholder changes in the case of hostile purchases, according to officials.
