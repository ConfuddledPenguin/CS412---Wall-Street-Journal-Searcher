Financial Corp. Of Santa Barbara's Net Jumps Sevenfold

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/24/90

SANTA BARBARA, Calif. -- Financial Corp. of Santa Barbara, aided by a debt swap, said first-quarter net income surged sevenfold to $37.7 million, or $1.71 a share, from a restated $5.3 million, or 86 cents a share, a year earlier. Financial Corp. said the increase was the result of restructuring efforts that included an equity-for-debt swap. The swap lifted Santa Barbara Savings & Loan Association, the principle subsidiary of Financial Corp., out of insolvency, although the S&L remains significantly undercapitalized with tangible capital of 0.6%.

Under last summer's thrift-rescue bill, thrifts are required to maintain a tangible capital ratio of 1.5% currently and of 3% by the end of 1994. Tangible capital is the excess of real assets, excluding intangible assets such as good will, over liabilities.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Financial Corp. stock closed at 15.625 cents, unchanged.

In a statement, Philip R. Brinkerhoff, Financial Corp.'s chairman, president and chief executive officer, said the company will continue to "aggressively pursue opportunities to increase capital, including capital infusion or sale."

The company's restructuring efforts included a debt swap in January that resulted in an extraordinary gain of $36.5 million and a $50 million addition to tangible capital. The exchange offer provided for the company to issue 17 million new common shares for preferred stock, subordinated debentures and notes.

The year-earlier quarter was restated to recognize a one-time $3.2 million gain from a change in accounting methods, Financial Corp. said. The financial-services concern has assets of $4.3 billion.
