Business Brief -- Beckman Instruments Inc.: Firm Posts Earnings Drop Of 25% for First Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/24/90

Beckman Instruments Inc. reported first-quarter net income fell 25% to $8 million, or 28 cents a share, from $10.7 million, or 38 cents a share, a year earlier. Sales dropped 7.1% to $185.5 million from $199 million. The company attributed much of the decline to tight market conditions among firms serving publicly-funded biological research. Beckman, based in Fullerton, Calif., makes and sells diagnostic and life-sciences laboratory instruments and related systems.

Louis Rosso, chairman and chief executive, noted that operating income before research and development costs declined 2% to $35.5 million, while research and development costs jumped 22% to $19.2 million. Mr. Rosso said the company is trimming operating expenses and continuing product development.

In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Beckman's stock closed at $15.375 a share, down 62.5 cents.
