Browning-Ferris Had Loss of $226.3 Million In Fiscal 2nd Period

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/24/90

HOUSTON -- Browning-Ferris Industries Inc. reported a loss of $226.3 million, or $1.48 a share, for its second quarter ended March 31, reflecting a previously announced charge to exit the hazardous-waste business. Earlier this month, the waste management concern disclosed the $295 million charge. The firm has been buffeted by low margins and regulatory problems in its hazardous-waste business and plans to focus on solid waste, recycling and medical-waste disposal.

Revenue was $708.5 million. In the year-ago second quarter, Browning had net income of $58.8 million, or 39 cents a share, on restated revenue of $582.3 million.

Results for the year-ago period were restated to reflect the discontinued hazardous-waste operation.

In the six months, Browning-Ferris had a loss of $159.8 million, or $1.05 a share, compared with net of $118.4 million, or 79 cents a share. Revenue increased to $1.41 billion from a restated $1.15 billion.
