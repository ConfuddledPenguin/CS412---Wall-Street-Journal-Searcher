Business Brief -- Lancaster Colony Corp.: Rights Program to Deter Unwanted Bids Is Adopted

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/24/90

Lancaster Colony Corp., a consumer goods concern based in Columbus, Ohio, said it adopted a shareholder rights plan, in which holders will receive, as a dividend, one right for each share of stock held at the close of business tomorrow. Under the plan, the rights may be exercised if an investor acquires, without board approval, at least 15% of the corporation's common stock or announces a tender offer that would result in the investor's owning at least 30% of the common. If the rights are exercised, holders will be able to buy the company's common stock having a market value of $140 for $70. Such plans are designed to make unwanted takeover bids prohibitively expensive.
