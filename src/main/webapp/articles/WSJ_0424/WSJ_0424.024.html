United Technologies' Daniell Expects Net To Continue Growing

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/24/90

HARTFORD, Conn. -- In the wake of the company's report that first-quarter profit rose nearly 9%, United Technologies Corp. Chairman Robert F. Daniell said he's confident the earnings growth will continue. Addressing the company's annual meeting, Mr. Daniell attributed the growth to corporatewide, cost-cutting efforts that "will bear fruit for years to come." He cited the Pratt & Whitney jet engine division's plan to trim costs as much as 30% by 1992 as "emblematic of these efforts."

Earnings climbed to $134.5 million, or $1.05 a share, from $123.7 million, or 95 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue increased to $4.8 billion from $4.46 billion.

Mr. Daniell said a three-year working capital reduction program, which is in place, is expected to save $1 billion by 1991. "In 1989, the first year of the program, we reduced the absolute level of working capital tied up in our businesses by about $350 million, even as revenue rose 7%," he said. "New opportunities for us on the commercial side in Eastern Europe and the Soviet Union can more than make up for any slack caused by reductions in the defense budget."

He said the company will continue to invest in research and development. It has done a better job of transferring and applying aerospace and defense technology to its commercial and industrial businesses, Mr. Daniell said. The company's businesses include building systems, automotive products and aerospace.
