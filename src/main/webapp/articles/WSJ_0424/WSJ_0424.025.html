Marketing & Media: Journal Reporters Win Farm-Journalism Prizes

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/24/90

NEW YORK -- Two reporters for The Wall Street Journal were named winners in the 1989 writing contest of the National Association of Agricultural Journalists. Scott Kilman, who covers commodities markets and agriculture in the Journal's Chicago bureau, received first prize in the special-projects category for a series of stories on the spread of aflatoxin, a cancer-causing agent, in the nation's corn crop.

Mr. Kilman, 30 years old, joined The Wall Street Journal in 1984 and transferred to Chicago from Atlanta in 1986.

Bruce Ingersoll, who covers agriculture in the Journal's Washington bureau, was named third-place winner in the special-projects category for a series of stories on food safety, including meat inspection deficiencies, illegal trafficking in unsafe shellfish, and tainted milk.

Mr. Ingersoll, 48, joined the Journal's Washington bureau in 1984.
