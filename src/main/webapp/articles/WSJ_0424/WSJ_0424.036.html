Merger Activity Fell 13% In 1st Quarter From 1989

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/24/90

SCHAUMBURG, Ill. -- Cautious buyers and a troubled junk-bond market continued to depress merger activity in the first quarter. Mergers and acquisitions announced in the first quarter were down 13% to 597 from 685 a year earlier, according to W.T. Grimm & Co., a unit of New York-based Merrill Lynch & Co.'s Merrill Lynch Business Brokerage & Valuation subsidiary. The total value of transactions plummeted 38% to $34.3 billion from $55 billion.

The absence of megadeals greatly contributed to the sharp drop in value, said Beth Rosencrans, research director of the Merrill Lynch unit. The total value of the 10 largest deals announced in the latest first quarter was $15.7 billion -- less than the $16.1 billion acquisition, announced last April, of SmithKline Beckman Corp. by Beecham Group PLC. The largest transaction announced in the 1990 first quarter was the $4.4 billion bid for UAL Corp.

The drop continued a downward trend that began late last year, Ms. Rosencrans added. In the 1989 fourth quarter, the number of deals fell 13% while the total value fell 11% from a year earlier.

The number of foreign concerns bidding for U.S. companies rose 31% to 93 from 71 last year. Simultaneously, the dollar value of those transactions climbed 65% to $12.9 billion from $7.8 billion.
