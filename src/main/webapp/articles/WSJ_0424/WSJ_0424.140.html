Business Brief -- CalFed Inc.: First-Period Profit Rose 8%; Net Interest Income Spurted

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/24/90

CalFed Inc. said improved core earnings pushed first-quarter profit up 8% to $18.9 million, or 75 cents a share, from $17.5 million, or 70 cents a share, a year earlier. CalFed, whose principal unit is California Federal Bank, attributed the improvement in the quarter to a 28% spurt in net interest income in its financial services segment to $128.5 million from $100.7 million.

The Los Angeles company also said that the quarter benefited modestly from a $1.3 million after-tax gain on the sale of Anglo-American Insurance Co., CalFed's London-based property-casualty insurance subsidiary. The Feb. 6 sale marked the company's exit from the property-casualty business.

During the quarter, CalFed said its net interest margin -- the spread between the yields on loans and the cost of funds -- improved to 2.24% from 1.47% a year ago.

CalFed's California Federal unit, with $25.3 billion in assets, currently exceeds all three capital requirements under the thrift-rescue bill, CalFed said.
