Who's News: Owens-Corning Elects Weber Top Executive

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/24/90

TOLEDO, Ohio -- Owens-Corning Fiberglas Corp. said its board elected Max O. Weber chief executive officer to succeed William W. Boeschenstein, 64 years old, who will retire in September. Mr. Boeschenstein will remain a director. Mr. Weber, 60, joined Owens-Corning in 1955. He was named president and chief operating officer in 1988 and will continue to serve in that capacity after succeeding Mr. Boeschenstein Oct. 1.

Mr. Weber takes over a company that went through a major recapitalization four years ago. The maker of home insulation and construction products reported net income of $32 million, or 76 cents a share, for the first quarter, 5.9% lower than a year earlier. Last year's net income dropped 9%, which the company attributed to an economic slowdown in the U.S. that affected its construction, automotive and pleasure-boat markets.
