Digital Microwave Stock Price Tumbles On Earnings News

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/24/90

SAN JOSE, Calif. -- Digital Microwave Corp. stock plunged almost 50% yesterday on news that fiscal fourth-quarter earnings fell below estimates because of company errors in manufacturing and selling a new product. The company wasn't more specific about the impact on earnings, but it did say that it believes that earnings for the quarter ended March 31 were "substantially lower" than the 28 cents a share reported in fiscal 1989's fourth quarter. In the year-ago quarter, Digital had net income of $3.4 million on revenue of $20.4 million.

In over-the-counter trading yesterday, Digital shares were battered, closing at $14.125, off $13.875 from Friday's close of $28. Volume topped 2.4 million shares.

The communications product manufacturer said it suffered a host of problems related to the introduction of its state-of-the-art microwave radio used by telephone concerns and other communications companies. The company said the price of the radio varies depending on its configuration but that it typically sells for $30,000.

William E. Gibson, Digital's president and chief executive officer, said problems included accelerated costs when the company moved from pilot manufacturing to mass manufacturing of the radios. In addition, he said that the radio was underpriced and added that the company is now studying how much of an increase it should tack on to current prices.

Prior to the company's announcement, Wall Street had expected that Digital would earn between 40 cents and 44 cents a share for the fourth quarter. But yesterday, Steven B. Levy, an analyst at Hambrecht & Quist, told the Dow Jones News Service that he expects Digital's earnings were only 16 cents to 17 cents a share.
