Marketing & Media: Pacific West, Affiliate Sue Turner in Dispute Over Programming

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/24/90

SACRAMENTO, Calif. -- Pacific West Cable Co. and an affiliate filed a lawsuit in state superior court here against Turner Broadcasting System Inc. in a dispute over Turner's cable programming. At issue, according to Pacific West, is a new cable technology that allows companies to beam cable programming into homes without the use of cables. But Pacific West alleges that Turner Broadcasting wants to do business only with operators that use cable because wiring a neighborhood for cable television limits availability and thus enhances the value of Turner's programming.

In the suit, which seeks $25 million in punitive damages, Pacific West said Turner maintains that use of the wireless technology violates the programming agreement between Turner and Pacific West, although Pacific West said there is no such ban in its agreement. In Atlanta, Turner said it doesn't comment on pending litigation.

In its suit, Pacific West said it has attempted to resolve its differences with Turner but said its overtures have been rebuffed.
