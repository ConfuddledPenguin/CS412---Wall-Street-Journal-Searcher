Who's News: CS First Boston's Top Japanese Official In Tokyo Office Quits

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/24/90

TOKYO -- The Tokyo office of CS First Boston Inc. was hit by its second high-level resignation in a month, as the brokerage firm's top Japanese executive resigned to join a rival Swiss firm. Allen D. Wheat, the new head of CS First Boston in Asia, said in a statement to employees that Keisuke Inaba, president and branch manager of CS First Boston (Japan) Ltd., resigned because he said he wanted to work in a smaller office and had "a belief that he's contributed all he can toward expanding our effort in Japan."

He said Mr. Inaba will join UBS Phillips & Drew International Ltd., the securities subsidiary of Union Bank of Switzerland. The controlling shareholder of CS First Boston is Credit Suisse, another major Swiss bank.

Mr. Inaba, a former executive at Merrill Lynch & Co. and Kidder Peabody International Corp., was out of the country and couldn't be reached for comment.

His resignation came just three weeks after James M. Walsh, chairman of CS First Boston in Japan, quit. Mr. Walsh said he resigned after the firm asked him to move to London.

But employees at CS First Boston have speculated that the arrival in February of Mr. Wheat, former chairman of Bankers Trust Co.'s international arm, created friction at the firm that prompted Messrs. Walsh and Inaba to leave.

Mr. Wheat said CS First Boston will continue its expansion in Asia, despite the departures. "Moving forward in a changing environment is never easy," he said in the annoucement to employees.

With a staff of more than 400, memberships on the Tokyo Stock Exchange and the Tokyo International Financial Futures Exchange, and a representative office in Osaka, CS First Boston is among the largest foreign brokerage houses in Japan.
