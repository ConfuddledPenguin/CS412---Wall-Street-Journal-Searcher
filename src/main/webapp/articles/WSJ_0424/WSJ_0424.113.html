Who's News: Columbia S&L Says Sachs Resigns His Post As Its Finance Chief

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/24/90

BEVERLY HILLS, Calif. -- Troubled Columbia Savings & Loan Association said its chief financial officer, David A. Sachs, resigned effective May 11 to pursue "other business opportunities." Neither Mr. Sachs nor the company could be reached for comment yesterday evening. But in a statement, Columbia said that Edward G. Harshfield, who was named president and chief executive of the insolvent thrift last month, would serve as acting chief financial officer until a successor to Mr. Sachs could be found.

The thrift, once one of the nation's most profitable because of its massive junk-bond holdings, was sent reeling because of its junk-bond portfolio. Earlier this month, the thrift said that declines of 50% or more in its junk-bond holdings contributed to its insolvency.

For the fourth quarter, Columbia had a loss of $379.1 million, or $19.17 a share. The worse-than-expected results have left Columbia teetering on the edge of federal receivership.
