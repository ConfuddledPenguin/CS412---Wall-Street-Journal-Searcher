Ex-Broker Is Found Guilty on 12 Counts

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/24/90

LOS ANGELES -- Retired Merrill Lynch & Co. stockbroker Willard R. Walls Jr. was convicted by a jury on 12 counts of securities fraud, mail fraud and interstate transportation of stolen property, Assistant U.S. Attorney Ronni B. MacLaren said. The government had alleged that while at Merrill Lynch, Pierce, Fenner & Smith Inc. in Long Beach, Calif., Mr. Walls, 65 years old, illegally diverted $400,000 from a client's account. The client, Everett Reiten, now deceased, had $13 million in the account.

The trial, in federal court here, ended yesterday. Mr. Walls faces as long as 75 years in prison and between $3.5 million and $3,750,000 in fines and restitution to Mr. Reiten's estate. Sentencing is set for June 7. Neither Mr. Walls nor his attorney could immediately be reached for comment.
