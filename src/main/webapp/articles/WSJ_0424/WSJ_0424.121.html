Business Brief -- Digital Equipment Corp.: Company Slates Buy-Back Of Up to 5 Million Shares

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/24/90

Digital Equipment Corp., continuing its stock buy-back program, said its board authorized the purchase of as many as five million, or 4.1%, of its common shares on the open market. Digital's stock, which rose last week after it reported mildly positive third-quarter results, closed yesterday at $83.25, up 25 cents, in New York Stock Exchange composite trading.

The Maynard, Mass., computer maker has purchased 18 million shares of its common for $2.1 billion over the past several years. In announcing the latest buy-back authorization, Digital asserted that the current market price of its stock "does not reflect the underlying strengths of the company."

Marc Schulman, an analyst with UBS Securities Inc., said Digital's buy-back announcement didn't affect its stock much partly because investors didn't interpret it as "a signal that Digital's business is starting to get stronger." He added that the indicated $415 million share repurchase "isn't a very large buy-back, given that Digital has $2.2 billion of cash."
