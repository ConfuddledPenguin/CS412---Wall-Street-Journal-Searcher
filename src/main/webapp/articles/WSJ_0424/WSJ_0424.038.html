Letters to the Editor: Sotheby's Views On Art Market

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/24/90

Your Friday, April 20, article presents an inaccurate picture of Sotheby's views of the art market, and does not reflect the substance of the conversation I had with the reporter. The discussion of reserves with consignors is a perfectly normal aspect of the art-auction business that has been completely misrepresented to indicate a concern about the market that simply does not exist. The confidence that we do feel going forward into May was omitted from her report. For the record, the quality of paintings in our May sales is unparalleled, with excellent works that have not been on the market for a long time. We are seeing world-wide interest in these paintings, and have every expectation that the May sales will be successful and that the art market will continue its strong performance.

Michael L. Ainslie

President and CEO

Sotheby's Holdings Inc.

New York

---

Editor's Note: The Wall Street Journal stands by its story.
