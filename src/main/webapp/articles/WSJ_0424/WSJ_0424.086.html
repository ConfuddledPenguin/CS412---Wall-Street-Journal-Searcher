Pansophic Is Planning Charges Eliminating Fiscal-Year Earnings

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/24/90

LISLE, Ill. -- Pansophic Systems Inc. disclosed it plans special charges that it expects will effectively erase its earnings for the year ending Monday. The news sent the software concern's stock price plunging 33%. While Pansophic didn't break out the size of the charges, its statement that it now expects break-even results for the full year indicates the charges will about wipe out the $15.7 million, or 84 cents a share, in earnings in the first nine months.

Pansophic said it will write down certain assets of its graphics operation and incur other one-time costs as part of a plan to "refocus" the operation on its core presentation-graphics business.

In addition, the company said it is closing its Brazilian subsidiary, because recent economic austerity measures announced by that nation have created an "untenable environment" for direct sales there. Pansophic will continue to sell in Brazil through a distributor.

In composite New York Stock Exchange trading yesterday, Pansophic shares plunged $5.125, closing at $10.50.

A.G. Edwards & Sons Inc. analyst David Soetebier said he thinks the "sell-off was overdone," adding that the "core of the company is still quite strong." The disclosure of the Brazilian unit's shutdown came as a surprise, he noted. "Part of the problem with Pansophic in the last couple of years," he said, is that management hasn't moved to resolve problems "as quickly as they should have."

Pansophic also announced that its chairman and chief executive officer, David J. Eskra, had assumed the additional title of president, succeeding in that role William G. Nelson, who resigned in January.
