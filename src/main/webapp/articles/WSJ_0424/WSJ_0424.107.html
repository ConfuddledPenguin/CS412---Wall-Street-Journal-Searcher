Business Brief -- MCI Communications Corp.: CBS, Comp-U-Check Jobs Won by Phone Company

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/24/90

MCI Communications Corp. has won two contracts: an $18 million, three-year contract from CBS Inc. in New York and a five-year contract, valued at $2.5 million, from Detroit-based Comp-U-Check. For CBS, MCI will provide its Vnet, or virtual private network service, to all of the broadcasting company's facilities in the U.S.

Washington-based MCI will also be providing CBS with 800 service. MCI's Vnet will be used to link CBS's 33 U.S. locations, including its five television stations, its corporate headquarters and broadcast center in New York, television studios in Los Angeles, as well as news bureaus and sales and administrative offices. MCI's Vnet will also be used for remote broadcasts, such as sporting events. MCI will become CBS's primary telecommunications provider.

For Comp-U-Check, MCI will provide its PRISM I and 800 services. Comp-U-Check's business offices were relocated to a building adjacent to MCI's Southfield, Mich., operations facilities, in a move that will save Comp-U-Check about $20,000 a month in service costs.
