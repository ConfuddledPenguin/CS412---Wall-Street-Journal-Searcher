British Reports on Trade Due

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/24/90

LONDON -- Britain's merchandise trade and current account deficits are both expected to be #1.3 billion ($2.13 billion) in March, according to the average of expectations in a survey of 12 economists. The data are due to be disclosed tomorrow. In February, both the trade and current account deficits totaled #1.4 billion. For all of 1989, the current account deficit was #20.9 billion and the government has forecast a 1990 deficit of #15 billion.

The range of expectations for the trade data was quite narrow -- from #1.1 billion to #1.4 billion.
