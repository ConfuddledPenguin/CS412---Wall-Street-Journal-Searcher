South Korea's Trade Gap

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/24/90

SEOUL -- South Korea posted a trade deficit of $1.9 billion for the quarter ended March 31, compared with a surplus of $58 million a year earlier. The Office of Customs Administration said exports fell 1.1% to $13.93 billion from $14.06 billion, while imports surged 13% to $15.84 billion from $14.09 billion.

Analysts blame the drop in exports on the rising value of the won against the Japanese yen. The yen's value against the won has fallen by about 20% since the beginning of 1989.
