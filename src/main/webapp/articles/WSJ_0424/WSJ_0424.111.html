Business Brief -- Howard Savings Bank: First-Period Loss Confirmed; Layoffs, Other Steps Slated

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/24/90

Driven to losses by the Northeast's real estate slump, Howard Savings Bank, announced a plan to return to profitability by cutting staff, raising service fees and reducing nonperforming loans. At the Newark, N.J., bank's annual meeting, Leo J. Rogers Jr., president, confirmed his projection last month of a $17.5 million loss for the first quarter. The loss resulted from the addition of $29.5 million to the bank's allowance for possible loan losses during the quarter. In the year-earlier quarter, Howard Savings Bank earned $8.5 million, or 59 cents a share.

Mr. Rogers said that about 10% of the current work force of about 1,700 would be let go, with about 100 employees offered early retirement at full pensions and other reductions coming "through attrition." He said the bank's first priority is to control and reduce nonperforming loans, which will be supervised by the recently hired chief credit officer, a new post.

Mr. Rogers said Howard Savings will concentrate on residential mortgages, home equity and other consumer loans for the rest of the year.
