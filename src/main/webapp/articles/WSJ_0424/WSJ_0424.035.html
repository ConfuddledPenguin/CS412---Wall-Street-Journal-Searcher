International: Lufthansa, 3 Other Carriers Plan Cargo-Data Network

  • Author: null
  • Date: 04/24/90

FRANKFURT -- Lufthansa German Airlines said that it and three other major international airline companies have signed a letter of intent to form a world-wide computer information network for cargo handling and logistics support services. A new joint venture company is to be established in Frankfurt to develop the information system and provide the data, which initially will be limited to the cargo and logistics support services offered by the individual airlines involved in the venture, Lufthansa said.

"It will start by offering freight forwarders and trade and industry direct access to in-house airline cargo computer systems and will provide schedule information, cargo reservation, tracking and {other} value-added services," the airline said.

A spokesman for the 51% state-owned West German airline stressed that the new venture won't engage in cargo haulage or logistics management-services itself, but rather serve as a clearing house for data concerning the freight-handling activities and services of the airlines involved.

Initial Lufthansa partners will be Air France, Hong Kong's Cathay Pacific Airways and Japan Air Lines. Financial details of the accord weren't disclosed, but Lufthansa said other air carriers will be invited to join the venture.

The agreement falls short of rumors last October of a far-reaching linkup of air-freight operations by the companies. At that time Japanese newspapers suggested Lufthansa, Air France and JAL planned to combine not only their cargo computers, but also their ground and air-cargo systems.
