Lomas & Nettleton Posts Quarterly Loss; Reserves Up Sharply

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 04/24/90

DALLAS -- Lomas & Nettleton Mortgage Investors, citing a rocky real estate climate, said it nearly tripled its loan-loss reserves to $50.6 million, leading to a net loss of $35 million, or $2.99 a share, in the fiscal third quarter. The real estate investment trust also said that while it plans to make a third-quarter dividend payment of 21 cents a share, it will forgo a payment in August and "defer consideration of further dividend payments until October 1990."

"The deterioration of the country's real estate markets has accelerated in recent months, and liquidity available to the real estate industry appears to be at its lowest point since the end of World War II," said Jess Hay, Lomas & Nettleton's chairman and chief executive.

The third-quarter loss provisions included a special $37.5 million addition to aggregate reserves and a regular $500,000 quarterly provision for possible losses, the trust said. Reserves currently stand at 6.5% of total investments, the highest level in the trust's 20-year history.

Revenue for the third quarter ended March 31 fell to $21.9 million from $34.1 million in the year-earlier quarter, when Lomas & Nettleton reported net income of $6.3 million, or 54 cents a share.

For the first nine months of fiscal 1990, the company reported a net loss of $26.8 million, or $2.29 a share, on revenue of $76.8 million. In the comparable period a year ago, the company reported net income of $20.5 million, or $1.76 a share, on revenue of $97.1 million.

Separately, the trust said it has reduced the aggregate principal amount of its notes due April 30, 1990 to $177.3 million from $246 million at Sept. 30, 1989. It said it has accumulated more than enough cash to make full payment on April 30, and won't be forced to request an extension from creditors. By that date its senior debt-to-equity ratio will have been reduced to 176% from 276% at June 30, 1989, it added.

Lomas & Nettleton, which is managed by a unit of Lomas Financial Corp., Dallas, engages in short-term real estate construction and development lending. In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange, Lomas & Nettleton Mortgage shares closed at $5.625, up 25 cents.
