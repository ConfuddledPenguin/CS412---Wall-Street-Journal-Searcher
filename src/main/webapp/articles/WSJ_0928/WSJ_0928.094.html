Mulroney Adds 8 Allies To Senate to Push Tax Bill

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/28/90

OTTAWA -- Canadian Prime Minister Brian Mulroney named eight additional government supporters to the Senate to try to push through the government's proposed value-added tax. The move increased the size of the Senate to 112 seats from 104 seats. It was the first time in Canadian history that a prime minister has taken such action.

The highly unpopular tax bill has encountered stormy resistance from the Liberal Party opposition in the appointed chamber.

The appointments give the ruling Progressive Conservatives 54 seats in the Senate. The Liberals have 52 seats. The Conservatives will need the support of independent senators to get the tax bill passed. The 7% tax is to take effect Jan. 1 if it is approved by the Senate.

The appointments required the approval of Britain's monarch who is Canada's titular head of state. Mr. Mulroney said the queen authorized the appointments.

Mr. Mulroney's action doesn't assure the passage of the proposed value-added tax. The Liberal leadership in the Senate said it will use various parliamentary tactics to delay a vote on the tax.
