What's News -- Business and Finance

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/28/90

BUDGET NEGOTIATORS neared a compromise that would limit tax deductions for the rich and lower the levy on capital gains less than Bush had sought. The package would also tax more of the Social Security benefits paid to middle-income and wealthy recipients, and raise the gas oline tax by eight cents a gallon. ---

T. Boone Pickens agreed to disgorge $2.3 million of profit from his 1988 sale of Homestake Mining stock, as part of a settlement of SEC charges that he misled investors.

---

MCA and Matsushita plan to meet next week to begin negotiating the Japanese electronics giant's possible acquisition of the entertainment firm.

---

The FDIC predicted its bank insurance fund will lose $3 billion this year, up from the $2 billion projected by the agency earlier this month.

---

Bank of Boston cut its dividend and said it will add about $175 million to its loan-loss reserves and trim its staff by 1,500, or 8%, of its employees.

Banking stocks sank for the fifth session in a row amid fears that the industry's exposure to problem loans is worse than bankers believe.

---

Oil prices rose, briefly surpassing $40 a barrel, despite an initial drop in reaction to Bush's decision to tap reserves. The International Energy Agency is expected to urge its 21 member countries to be ready to dip into their stockpiles, but it won't yet call for a coordinated release.

---

Stocks fell and bonds rose as recession fears intensified. The Dow Jones Industrial Average closed 32.17 points lower after fluctuating wildly in heavy trading. The 30-year Treasury bond jumped one point.

---

Neil Bush conceded he stood to benefit from a line of credit he sought from Silverado S&L for a partner.

---

Milken's sentencing was delayed as a judge ordered an unusual hearing on additional crimes that the prosecution contend he committed.

---

Union Carbide plans to buy as much as 14% of its stock, cut its debt and reap about $300 million from as set sales. The moves are aimed at bolstering the firm's shares, which rose 62.5 cents to $14.875 on the news.

---

GM and Ford are idling five plants that make light trucks in a sign of weakness in that market segment.

---

Unisys decided to omit its common-stock dividend. The company's already battered stock sank 23%.

---

Mexico is worried that Canadian participation in U.S.-Mexican talks on a free-trade agreement could delay or jeopardize the accord.

---

Campbell Soup's only director to have pushed publicly for a sale of the company quit the board abruptly, dimming prospects for a takeover.

---

Tokyo's property-related stocks have plummeted 55% so far this year, indicating to some analysts that Japan's land prices are about to plunge.
