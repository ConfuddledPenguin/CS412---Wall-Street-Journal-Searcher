Federal Court Awards Brooktree $26 Million In a Patent Lawsuit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/28/90

SAN DIEGO -- A federal court jury awarded Brooktree Corp. $26 million in damages for infringements Advanced Micro Devices made on the tiny chip maker's patents and copyrights. The award comes two weeks after the U.S. District Court ruled that Advanced Micro, a Sunnyvale, Calif., chip maker, illegally used three Brooktree patents in computer chips called "color palettes," which control the display of colors on computer screens. The court also ruled that Advanced Micro infringed on Brooktree "mask work" rights, the rights to circuit layouts on chips.

The award is a big one for Brooktree, a private company that expects revenue of just over $60 million this year. Brooktree charged that offending Advanced Micro chips cut deeply into the smaller company's revenue. Advanced Micro's color palette chips comprised less than 1% of its $1.1 billion revenue in 1989.

An Advanced Micro spokesman said the company will appeal the damage award as well as the ruling. "This a shocking and unsupportable amount of damages," the spokesman said. He added Advanced Micro didn't copy Brooktree's chips and that patent violation, if there was any, was inadvertent because Advanced Micro's product was on the market before the patents were issued.

The federal judge in the case issued an injunction calling for Advanced Micro to stop making, using or selling any infringing products. The court stayed the injunction for 10 days, but Advanced Micro says it will comply by immediately ceasing sales of the product in question.

Advanced Micro has a patent-infringement case pending against Brooktree, and says its patents are broader than Brooktree's.
