International Brief -- Montedison S.p.A.: Capital Increase Is Linked To a Possible Acquisition

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/28/90

Chemical group Montedison S.p.A., Milan, Italy, said it plans a capital increase of up to 2.5 trillion lire ($2.13 billion), in the event it decides to buy the 40% stake in its Enimont S.p.A. joint venture currently held by Italian state energy group Ente Nazionale Idrocarburi, or ENI. Montedison also reported an 18% drop in consolidated profit for the first half of this year, reflecting worsening business conditions in the chemical sector.

The announcements came at a critical time for the group, as it faces the decision whether to pay out the huge sums required to achieve the control it has long sought over Enimont, or whether to cut its losses, sell its stake to ENI and effectively pull out of the Italian chemical business.

Enimont was formed last year when ENI and Montedison merged their base chemical businesses. But the joint venture, in which each holds 40%, has been plagued by fundamental disagreements over long-term strategy. Now the Italian government has proposed that the venture should effectively be auctioned between the partners, with ENI having the right to name a price and Montedison the right of first refusal.
