BCE, Nynex Join In Bid for Mexico's Phone Company

  • Author: G. Pierre Goad
  • Date: 09/28/90

MONTREAL -- BCE Inc. and Nynex Corp. are joining with a Mexican financial concern to bid for control of Telefonos de Mexico. The joint venture is the first group to announce it will make an offer for a 20.4% TelMex equity stake held by the Mexican government. The 20.4% block represents just over half of TelMex's full-voting shares. Three other groups are expected to join the bidding.

Henri Roy, a BCE senior vice president, said that based on the current stock price TelMex is worth about $7 billion, giving the 20.4% stake an indicated value of about $1.4 billion.

Mr. Roy wouldn't say if the joint venture expects to offer a premium for the control block. The Mexican government currently holds about 56% of TelMex while the remainder is publicly held.

BCE and Nynex will each own about 25% of the venture and Casa de Bolsa Inverlat Associates, a Mexican financial concern, will own at least 50.1%. Mr. Roy said the partners are still working on the details of their bid, which must include a detailed operating plan for TelMex.

Under rules established by the Mexican government, groups making bids must be at least 50.1%-owned by Mexican investors and must include at least one foreign telephone company. The bidding process is only open to concerns approved by the government. Four Mexican groups (a fifth dropped out) and 12 foreign telephone concerns have been accepted as possible bidders by the government, Mr. Roy said.

The bidding deadline is Nov. 15 and the government expects to make a decision by the end of the year, Mr. Roy said.

The winning bidder faces a massive job modernizing TelMex, Mr. Roy said. But for BCE and Nynex the Mexican telephone monopoly also represents a "tremendous opportunity." He said there is the potential for "double-digit annual growth" at TelMex. He said the telephone monopoly only has about five million telephone lines in operation now and the Mexican economy could support twice that number.

Mr. Roy said the Mexican government has guaranteed TelMex a monoply on basic phone service until 1996. He said the telephone monopoly is profitable and has annual revenue of about $5 billion.

BCE, parent of Bell Canada, is a Montreal-based holding company. Nynex is the New York-based Baby Bell. Inverlat is based in Mexico City. The joint venture's announcement was made after the market closed yesterday.
