International: Japan's Gets a Green Light To Sell More Autos In EC

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/28/90

BRUSSELS -- The European Community Commission plans to let Japanese auto makers have as much as 11% of the French and Italian markets by the end of 1998, more than triple their current share, a senior EC official said. The figure is part of a so-called voluntary agreement for Japanese auto makers to gradually increase their potential share in the protected EC market after the planned unification of the 12 EC nations' markets in 1992. The tentative accord was worked out in private talks in recent months between the EC Commission and Japan's Ministry of International Trade and Industry.

In Spain, the commission intends to allow Japanese makers as much as 19% of the auto market.

The French and Italian governments have objected to the allotment as too generous. But the EC official said the 11% figure represents the upper limit that Japanese car makers would be likely to reach eight years from now. If European car makers become more competitive, the official said, the Japanese share could be lower.

Japanese auto makers currently are restricted to less than 3% of the market in France, 1.5% in Italy and 1.2% in Spain. In EC nations that haven't imposed such curbs, Japanese car makers already have significant market shares. For example, Japanese manufacturers have 15% of the auto market in West Germany, 20% in Belgium and 40% in Ireland.
