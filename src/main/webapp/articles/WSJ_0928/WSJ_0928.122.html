Who's News: Figgie Chief's Son Is Named Vice Chairman

  • Author: Ralph E. Winter
  • Date: 09/28/90

Harry E. Figgie Jr., chairman and chief executive officer of Figgie International Inc., brought his son, Harry E. Figgie III, into the company as vice chairman, with the assignment of speeding the adoption of new technology. The Figgies said during a joint interview that there isn't any understanding that the move places Dr. Figgie, who is 37 years old, in position to succeed his 66-year-old father as chief of the diversified company the senior Mr. Figgie founded 26 years ago.

There has been speculation since Dr. Figgie joined the Figgie International board in 1985 that the senior Mr. Figgie would keep the top job in the family. However, when asked whether he plans to retire at any given date, Mr. Figgie responded, "Hell, no. This is an exciting time. Things I've waited my whole career to see are happening now."

The senior Mr. Figgie said the Willoughby, Ohio, company has "a five-year window of opportunity to integrate new technology" into its divisions. "There's been a technological revolution," in machining, welding, assembly, product design and other functions, he said, and in some industries the company that successfully employs the new methods first "will own the business."

Figgie, which expects 1990 sales of nearly $1.4 billion, supplies fire-protection and security systems and services; produces sporting goods, electronics systems and industrial products, and is involved in real estate development and other activities.

Dr. Figgie, who is an orthopedic surgeon as well as a manufacturing executive, will phase out of his medical practice over the next 18 to 36 months to head technology and strategic planning at Figgie. He will be a member of a new three-member office of the chairman, which will include the senior Mr. Figgie and Vincent A. Chiarucci, 60, the company's president and chief operating officer.

The senior Mr. Figgie said those divisions serving the construction industry have experienced a rapid drop in orders during the current quarter. The company is cutting employment and speeding up the previously planned consolidation of certain units to adjust to the lower business volume, he said. He declined to make an earnings forecast.

Dr. Figgie has headed Clark-Reliance Co., a family-owned manufacturer of valves, controls and instrumentation since 1975, when he was entering Case Western University's School of Medicine. In addition to his medical degree, Dr. Figgie has undergraduate degrees in bio-medical engineering and accounting and a master's degree in factory automation. He currently is an assistant clinical professor of medicine at Case and has a surgical practice specializing in artificial joint replacement.

Dr. Figgie said he had "reached a fork in the road" where he had to concentrate exclusively on business or medicine. He said he chose business because he finds the application of new manufacturing technologies exciting and challenging.
