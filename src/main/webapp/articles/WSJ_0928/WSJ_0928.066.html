Technology: Merck to Change Drug's Name

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/28/90

WEST POINT, Pa. -- Merck Sharp & Dohme, the pharmaceutical arm of Merck & Co., said it is changing the trade name of Losec, the medication used for peptic disorders, to Prilosec. The company said the change was being made to prevent further confusion between the drug and a medication made by Hoechst AG's Hoechst-Roussel unit called Lasix, used to treat high blood pressure. It said it received necessary federal approvals.

Merck said it received reports that some patients who had been prescribed Losec were "erroneously" given Lasix, which is also used to prevent bleeding in horses. Merck is based in Rahway, N.J.
