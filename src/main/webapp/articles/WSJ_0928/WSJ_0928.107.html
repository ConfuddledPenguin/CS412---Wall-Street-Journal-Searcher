Several Finance Companies In Sweden Facing Crisis

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/28/90

STOCKHOLM -- Big banks and market regulators are scrambling to head off a crisis that has crippled Sweden's high-flying commercial paper market and called into question the survival of several big finance companies. The trouble started Monday, when sour property loans forced Nyckeln Holding AB, one of Sweden's biggest finance companies, to slash its full-year profit forecast. The news sent shares of Nyckeln and its parent Beijer Capital AB into a free fall on the Stockholm Stock Exchange.

Even worse, as investors shunned its commercial paper in Stockholm markets Nyckeln sank into a liquidity squeeze. Problems swiftly spread to other finance companies, which like Nyckeln had been big dispensers of real estate credits.

By yesterday morning, when the Gamlestaden AB unit of Nobel Industries AB announced it no longer could meet payment obligations because of acute liquidity woes, the exchange had halted trading in six companies' shares.

Trading reached a virtual standstill in commercial paper issued by about 30 Swedish finance companies. The companies rely heavily on those corporate IOU's with maturities of as much as six months for the bulk of their normal financing needs. Alarmed regulatory authorities huddled with big Stockholm banks, trying to arrange emergency financing and avert bankruptcies.
