Berisford Concludes Refinancing Package For 2.2 Billion Debt

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 09/28/90

LONDON -- Berisford International PLC, a troubled U.K. food, financial services, property, commodities and industrial group, said it concluded a #1.2 billion ($2.2 billion) refinancing package with about 70 banks. The package covers all of Berisford's bank debt, said the company, except for its coffee business, which is subject to separate negotiations scheduled for completion in the "near future."

The group said the new arrangements will help it carry out an orderly disposal program and restructuring of the business. It said the sale of its major division, British Sugar, is "well under way."

In July, Berisford Chairman John Sclater said the company was willing to sell any part of the business to reduce its debt. In its latest six-month results to March 31, Berisford reported net borrowings of #1.02 billion.
