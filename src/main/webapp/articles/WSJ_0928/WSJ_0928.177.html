Labatt Plans to Join Flour Mill Operations With Canada Packers'

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/28/90

TORONTO -- John Labatt Ltd., a brewing and consumer products concern, and Canada Packers Inc., a packaging and food products concern, said they plan to combine their flour milling operations and related businesses. The new partnership would have assets of about 310 million Canadian dollars (US$268.3 million), the two companies said. The Canada Packers operations had 1989 sales of C$430.7 million, and the Labatt businesses had sales of C$425.7 million in the year ended April 30.

The plan calls for Labatt to put its Ogilvie Mills Ltd. unit, which includes flour and oat milling, and starch, bakery and pizza operations into the partnership. The Canada Packers business units to be contributed include flour milling operations, bakeries and retail franchise lines.

Canada Packers would hold 60% and Labatt 40% of the partnership, to be called Maple Leaf-Ogilvie, in which they will invest about C$40 million to upgrade the capacity of Montreal-area flour mills.

The transaction, which the companies said they hope to complete by year end, is subject to regulatory approval by Canada's competition watchdog and foreign investment regulators. Hillsdown PLC of Britain holds a 56% stake in Canada Packers.

The move is Labatt's latest step to decrease its exposure to food and other consumer products. Last year, the company sold its Catelli Inc. pasta and foods unit, fueling speculation that the next asset for sale would be the Ogilvie milling operations. A Labatt official said a partnership was the best option to pursue, "due to Canada's loss of export markets for flour and resulting inefficiencies for Canadian millers." Canada's share of the world export market for flour fell to 7% in 1989 from more than 30% in the years following World War II.
