Who's News: Cooperman, Thoman Named to Head Unit Of American Express

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/28/90

NEW YORK -- American Express Co. named two executives to head its largest and most profitable unit, American Express Travel Related Services Co. Edwin M. Cooperman and G. Richard Thoman were named chairmen and co-chief executive officers of the charge card, traveler's check, and travel business, effective Jan. 1. They will succeed Aldo Papone. Mr. Papone, 58 years old, began cutting back on his activities for medical reasons in April 1989, when he was named chairman. He will become senior adviser to American Express, reporting to Chairman James D. Robinson III, and will continue as a director of the parent.

Mr. Cooperman, 46, has been president TRS North America since 1989, responsible for businesses in the U.S. and Canada. Mr. Thoman, also 46, was named president of American Express International in 1989, responsible for American Express activities outside North America.

In 1989, the travel-related services unit had profit of $830 million on revenue of $8.4 billion.
