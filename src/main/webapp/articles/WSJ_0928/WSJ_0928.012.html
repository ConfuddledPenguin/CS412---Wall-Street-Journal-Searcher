Letters to the Editor: Protection, Not Policy Helped Harley Cycles

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/28/90

Harley-Davidson is often cited as an example of "industrial policy that worked." Over the longer term, however, the company is more likely to make it into textbooks as a classic case study on the follies of protectionism. Your Aug. 31 story on Harley notes that the market for smaller motorcycles has shrunk to a fraction of its former self. But your writer ignores the significance of this fact. Buyers of smaller bikes traditionally have honed their riding skills on the less powerful machines, then "stepped up" to ride Harleys. The motorcycle tariffs of the 1980s destroyed this junior market. As a result, there is no younger generation of bikers being weaned on Hondas. When trend-conscious baby boomers eventually tire of Harleys, the company may be hard pressed to find replacement customers.

Gordon T. Anderson

Senior Editor

Individual Investor magazine

New York
