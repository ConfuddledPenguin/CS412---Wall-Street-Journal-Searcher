International: West Germany Sets Added Borrowing Requirement

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/28/90

FRANKFURT, West Germany -- The Finance Ministry set a 25.8 billion mark ($16.48 billion) additional borrowing requirement for 1990, as part of West Germany's third, and largest, supplementary budget for the year. Ministry officials said the new borrowing will bring the all-German federal spending deficit to 66.8 billion marks for this year, a 63% jump from the combined 41 billion-mark deficit previously planned for both German states, which will reunite next Wednesday.

Finance Minister Theo Waigel today will present a new budget outline, requiring the additional borrowing to cover 20.1 billion marks in new federal spending and about five billion marks in new credits pledged to East German municipalities last July. Officials said the supplementary budget, which is to become the first budget of a united Germany, was crafted after an effort to siphon funds from Bonn programs.

The new strains on West German capital markets don't end there. Including off-budget financing for the agency trying to salvage East Germany's inefficient industries, and deficit spending of West German states and municipalities, the total public-sector deficit of a united Germany will total about 100 billion marks this year, even after a 20 billion mark surplus in West Germany's social security system. In 1989, West Germany had a public-sector deficit of 21.6 billion marks.

The newly budgeted all-German federal spending of 20.1 billion marks is added to the previously budgeted outlays of 311.8 billion marks for the West German federal government, and 64.2 billion marks for the central government of East Germany.
