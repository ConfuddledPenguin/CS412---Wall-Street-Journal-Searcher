Hewlett-Packard in China

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/28/90

PALO ALTO, Calif. -- Hewlett-Packard Co. said it agreed with a Chinese government agency to form a joint venture in Shanghai to make computers and software for sale in China. Hewlett-Packard said it signed an agreement with China's Ministry of Machinery and Electronics Industry to form the venture, to be named Huapu Information Technology Co. The new company, Hewlett-Packard said, will assemble and market the U.S. company's new line of HP Apollo Series 400 workstations, and will develop engineering and manufacturing software.

Hewlett-Packard, which wouldn't disclose terms of the agreement, formed another joint venture with China in 1984 to make minicomputers and other electronics products.
