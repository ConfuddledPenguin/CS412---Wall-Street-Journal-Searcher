Salmon Imports Are Sold At Unfair Prices, U.S. Says

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/28/90

WASHINGTON -- The Commerce Department said in a preliminary ruling that imports of semiprocessed Atlantic salmon from Norway are being sold in the U.S. at less than their home-market prices or production costs. The department recommended anti-dumping duties of 1.66% to 4.90% to be imposed on the imports to offset their alleged price advantage over salmon harvested by U.S. fleets.

The duties are to be collected if the finding is verified by a subsequent investigation and the U.S. fishing industry is found to be injured by these imports.
