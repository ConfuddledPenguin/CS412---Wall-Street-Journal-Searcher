Big Board Reports An Increase of 10.1% In Program Trading

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/28/90

NEW YORK -- Program trading rose to 10.1% of New York Stock Exchange volume, or 12.5 million shares a day, for the week ended Sept. 14. In the previous week, program trading amounted to 9.8% of daily volume, the Big Board said in its regular report on the computer-aided trading strategies.

Bear Stearns Cos. was the most active program-trading firm for the latest week, trading 14.6 million shares in program strategies; it also did more than one-third of all stock-index arbitrage volume, mostly for its own account.

Index arbitrage -- in which baskets of stocks are bought and sold with offsetting trades in futures and options -- accounted for an unusually high 64.7% of program trading volume. Arbitrage generally accounts for only about half of overall program volume.

Next to Bear Stearns, the next most-active firms were Morgan Stanley Group Inc. (11.5 million shares), General Electric Co.'s Kidder, Peabody & Co. unit (10.4 million), Merrill Lynch & Co. (5.4 million) and PaineWebber Group Inc. (5 million).

"Buy" programs accounted for an average 2.6 million shares daily and "sell" programs for 9.9 million, during a week in which the Dow Jones Industrial Average fell 55.44 points, or 2.1%.

Program trading is the purchase or sale of a basket of at least 15, and often hundreds of stocks worth at least $1 million.
