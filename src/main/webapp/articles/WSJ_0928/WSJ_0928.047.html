Paribas Profit Rose 30% in 1990's 1st Half; Capital Gains Cited

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 09/28/90

PARIS -- Cie. Financiere de Paribas said consolidated profit jumped 30% in the first half of 1990, largely on the strength of capital gains on its portfolio of securities and real estate. The French bank and financial holding company said consolidated profit after payments to minority interests rose to 2.49 billion francs ($474.7 million), or 39.10 francs a share, from 1.91 billion francs ($364.5 million), or 35.50 francs a share, in the first half of 1989.

Paribas said the rise was led by capital gains and dividends from minority-held industrial affiliates. A company official said much of the increase came from the sale of an office building in Paris for a pretax capital gain of 1.3 billion francs.

On the other hand, income from traditional banking operations and dividends from financial units fell to 168 million francs from 1.04 billion francs a year earlier. Paribas said one reason was heavy spending on new portfolio investment, profit from which was recorded as securities income. It didn't give details of the new investment, although an official suggested that much of it came in a 2.7 billion franc outlay for 54% of the French food group Guyomarch S.A. in February.

The group's main bank units, Banque Paribas, Credit du Nord and Cie. Bancaire, generated profit of 1.06 billion francs, up 15%. The rise included the 1.3 billion franc pretax capital gain on the Paris real estate sale, which was made by Banque Paribas. The company said Credit du Nord and Cie. Bancaire posted small profit gains but didn't disclose details.

Otherwise, it said profitability in the banking sector was held back by rising interest rates and efforts to boost provisions for loan risks. Paribas said new provisions for the first half totaled 2.3 billion francs, compared with 1.8 billion in the first half of 1989. It didn't make any new provisions for its sovereign, or government, debt exposure, which is covered by insurance it took out last year.

Profit from Paribas's non-banking units, mostly in the portfolio sector, rose 43% in the first half to 1.43 billion francs.
