Canadian Panel Backs Electricity Exports By Quebec Utility

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/28/90

OTTAWA -- Canada's National Energy Board conditionally approved electricity exports valued at 24 billion Canadian dollars (US$20.8 billion) by Hydro-Quebec to Vermont and New York over the next 30 years. The board said the exports will be subject to an environmental review by the federal government of two big hydroelectric dams, planned by Hydro-Quebec partly to meet the export requirements.

The dams are to be built in the James Bay and Hudson Bay areas of northern Quebec.

Hydro-Quebec proposed to export 450,000 kilowatts of electricity to Vermont Joint Owners, a group of nine utilities in Vermont, over a 30-year period starting Nov. 1. The provincial government-owned utility also proposed to export to New York Power Authority one million kilowatts of electricity from May 1, 1995, to April 30, 2016.

Jacques Guevremont, a Hydro-Quebec vice president, said the board's conditions are acceptable and shouldn't be an obstacle to the exports.

Several native and environmental groups have opposed the construction of the dams, because they claim the facilities will pollute the air and water and destroy the hunting grounds of the Cree Indians living in the area.

Hydro-Quebec's electricity exports totaled about C$190 million last year.
