European Car Sales Fell 3.4% in August; '90 Outlook Still Soft

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 09/28/90

LONDON -- Car sales in Europe fell 3.4% to about 1,110,000 in August from 1,149,000 million a year earlier, largely because of sluggish British demand, but rose 16% from 959,722 in July. The industry statistics are based on new-car registrations in 12 countries that normally account for 94% of sales from Western Europe. Analysts were reluctant to draw many conclusions from August and its implications for the year because of seasonal distortions in Britain. But they generally stuck with earlier predictions that 1990 car sales will range from unchanged to down 2% from 12.6 million units in 1989.

In Britain, August sales surged more than eightfold to 432,867 from July, and though the total represented the third-highest for the country, it was still down 13% from the year-earlier record 500,112. Britain usually has a big seasonal upswing in auto sales in August, as consumers there try to buy cars at the start of the auto registration year.

Had Britain's car sales been excluded from the year-to-year total, European sales in August would have risen 4.3%.

Also, August sales in Italy and France usually decline from the previous month, as consumer interest fades at the height of the summer holidays.

"Of any one month of the year, August is the one you have to be most careful about," as part of the measurement for the year's trend, said Gavin Launder, a European car analyst at Kleinwort Benson Securities Ltd.

Analysts said the August data weren't influenced by the rise in crude oil prices springing from the Persian Gulf crisis. This effect might not be felt in the car market until next year, Mr. Launder said, though production plans might be reduced in this year's fourth quarter to prepare for an economic downturn, owing to the oil factor.

The West German market, where August sales climbed 16% to 224,088 cars from a year earlier, continues to be "exceptionally strong," said Philip Ayton, European car analyst at Barclays de Zoete Wedd Ltd. Recently released East German savings continue to be sunk into the West German car market, analysts said.

Analysts also said France is among the main markets bucking the trend and showing strength in the face of what they expect will be a dismal year overall in Europe. French sales continue to be helped by a reduction in taxes on new cars. In the past three years, France has reduced taxes on consumer goods to 22% from 28%; the latest cut took effect this month. The tax cuts brighten the outlook for sales for the rest of this year, analysts said. France's August sales rose 3.6% to 178,500 from a year earlier but fell 22% from July.
