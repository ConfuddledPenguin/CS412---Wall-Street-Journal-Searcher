REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial): Necessary Losses

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/28/90

If the budget summit is settling on indexation of the base for capital gains, it's also time to look seriously at deductibility of capital losses. For starters, here are some reasons: Equity. While the system adds gains to ordinary income and taxes it all, it allows the deduction from ordinary income of only $3,000 of a loss.

Economic vitality. Indexing of its nature affects filed-away investments. Loss deductibility of its nature would occur quickly most of the time, freeing capital to move on to better uses. It would help high-risk, high-reward investments. Don't ask "the rich" about that; ask an electronics or biotech entrepreneur.

Whether George Mitchell likes it or not, investments have something to do with America's health. Some go up, some down. A budget summit that both indexed gains and allowed deductibility of losses would reassure the country that Washington still wants it to grow.
