Ex-Im Bank Clears Financing for Brazil's Purchase of Satellites

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/28/90

WASHINGTON -- The U.S. Export-Import Bank said it gave final approval to a $178.6 million direct loan and a guarantee covering $8.5 million in other financing for Brazil's purchase of two telecommunications satellites from Hughes Aircraft Co., the Los Angeles-based unit of General Motors Corp. The Ex-Im Bank said the equipment will cost about $210 million and that the two satellites, to be launched in 1994, will replace satellites launched in 1985 and 1986.

The Ex-Im Bank loan and credit guarantee, to cover certain "local costs" in Brazil, will be provided to Embratel, a Brazilian government-owned telecommunications company. Embratel is a subsidiary of Telebras, which is responsible for all of Brazil's domestic, long-distance and international telecommunications facilities.
