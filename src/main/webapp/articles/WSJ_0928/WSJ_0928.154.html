International Brief -- Cerus S.A.: Paris Firm Will Sell Stake In Real Companhia Velha

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/28/90

Italian financier Carlo de Benedetti has withdrawn from a clash with one of Portugal's businessmen, Manuel da Silva Reis. A spokeswoman for Cerus S.A., the Paris-based firm that runs Mr. De Benedetti's European investments, confirmed it plans to sell its 40% stake in Real Companhia Velha, a leading port wine producer controlled by Mr. Silva Reis. Real Companhia Velha is one of Portugal's oldest and largest port wine producers. Founded in 1756 as a state-backed monopoly to oversee the port industry, it accounted for nearly 12% of world-wide port sales volume last year.

Cerus's Portuguese holding company, Cofipsa, which groups Mr. De Benedetti and other European partners with Portuguese partners, agreed to take the 40% stake last year, when Mr. Silva Reis was looking for outside help to overcome financial difficulties. But differences soon arose between new management brought in by the De Benedetti group and Mr. Silva Reis.

Mr. Silva Reis and his family are to remain as the company's principal shareholders.
