Business Brief -- Rowan Cos.: Houston Concern May Post First Yearly Profit Since '84

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/28/90

Rowan Cos. said third-quarter net of about 10 cents a share, combined with an expected fourth-quarter profit could result in its first profitable year since 1984. In the year-earlier third quarter, the energy service concern had a net loss of about $211,000, or five cents a share, on revenue of about $31.4 million. A spokesman said the latest third-quarter net will be about $7.2 million, but would say only that revenue "will definitely be better" than last quarter -- referring to second-quarter revenue of about $36 million.

Rowan said it expects drilling revenue to offset seasonal reductions in aviation activity, resulting in an overall profitable fourth quarter. The company operates a fleet of charter helicopters and other aircraft used in the energy industry.

Rowan said it expects a "borderline or break-even year." In 1984, its last profitable year, Rowan had restated net of $3.2 million, or six cents a share, on revenue of about $198.4 million.
