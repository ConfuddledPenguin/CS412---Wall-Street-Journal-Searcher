Business Brief -- Teradata Corp.: Computer Concern Expects Fiscal First-Quarter Loss

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/28/90

Teradata Corp. said it sees a fiscal first-quarter loss of between 40 cents a share and 50 cents a share on lower-than-expected revenue of about $50 million. In the year-ago quarter, the Los Angeles computer systems concern earned $2.4 million, or 17 cents a share, on revenue of $43 million.

Teradata said it is "taking a number of actions to significantly reduce operating expense levels in anticipation of lower-than-expected revenue growth" for the fiscal year ending June 30. Some of these actions will result in a "modest" first-quarter charge, the company said.

"We intend to reduce our operating expenses . . . sufficiently to provide year-over-year earnings growth" in fiscal year 1991, Teradata said. This year, the company had net income of $16.3 million, or $1.15 a share.

Teradata attributed the revenue shortfall to slow sales in Germany and Europe, including postponement of purchases by a large European customer. The company said orders had been disappointing from bank customers, particularly in the Northeast.
