Big Board Bars 12 Individuals From Industry

  • Author: Robert Daniels
  • Date: 09/28/90

NEW YORK -- The New York Stock Exchange barred 12 people from the securities industry for alleged violations of exchange rules and federal securities laws. The NYSE charged that John Joseph Leadem of Normandy Beach, N.J., a former broker at Merrill Lynch & Co., stole $250,000 from a customer. Also barred was Joseph Patrick Michael Desposito of Brooklyn, N.Y. The exchange alleged that he stole funds from Mabon, Nugent & Co., where he was an assistant controller.

Eight individuals consented to a bar from the securities industry, but didn't admit or deny wrongdoing: Michael R. Freglette, Marietta, Ga.; Ralph Edgar Howard, Daly City, Calif.; Amen S. Kahwajy, Richmond, Va.; Laurel Darlyne Lopez, Waco, Texas; Michael John Pavkovich, Flint, Mich.; Michaele P. Quinn, Winnetka, Ill.; Dorothy Kay Smith, Olympia, Wash.; and James F. Wallace, Akron, Ohio.

Two others, Harvey M. Bassin of Miami and Mark Muenster of Clifton, N.J., were barred until they provide information the exchange has requested.

Four firms settled Big Board charges without admitting or denying wrongdoing: Ferris, Baker Watts Inc., Washington; Financial Clearing & Services Corp. and Lepercq, De Neuflize Securities Inc., both New York, and Rose & Co. Investment Brokers, Chicago. Financial Clearing was fined $45,000, while the three others were assessed $20,000 each.

The exchange took disciplinary action against a number of individuals. Suspensions and fines are noted:

Richard Baker Lohrer Jr., New York, 10 years; Thomas Lewis Desmond Jr., Louisville, Ky., and Richard A. Wilburn, New York, 30 months each; Robert M. Fanning, Newport Beach, Calif., 18 months; and Lynn T. Shaw, Memphis, Tenn., one month.

The following neither admitted nor denied wrongdoing:

Michael S. Penfield, Billings, Mont., six years; Timothy Harrington, Grenada Hills, Calif., three years; Ronald Harry, Salt Lake City, one year; Robert Leslie Fell, Mineola, N.Y., six months; John P. Aquino, Clifton Park, N.Y., five months; Rodney Dee Aftuck, Atlanta, three months and must complete a course.

And Howard R. Knapp, Sanibel, Fla., five weeks; Steven J. Aquino, Latham, N.Y., and Jeffrey Eric Susskind, Santa Monica, Calif., each one month and $10,000; Bruce E. Piccirillo, Robstown, Texas, two weeks and $5,000; Salvatore Guerrieri Jr., North Chili, N.Y., one week; Andrew Racz, New York, $25,000; and Thomas M. McDonald, Hudson, Ohio, and Jeffrey M. Tauber, Darien, Conn., censure only.

Except as noted below, the firms and individuals involved in New York Stock Exchange disciplinary proceedings couldn't be reached for comment.

At Ferris Baker Watts, General Counsel Theodore Urban referred to the exchange's remarks in the matter, which say in part that the firm's failure to fully comply with an NYSE rule "reflected a systems-related problem at the firm's clearing broker," and that the firm's "procedures in place are adequate to handle the normal stream of {account} transfers within the specified time frame." He said that at any firm handling a large volume of account transfers, "there are going to be slip-ups," and that the exchange hasn't spelled out a standard of how many such errors might be acceptable.

Mr. Susskind said, "It was something that took place years ago. It's unfortunate that it took so long to get it resolved. I'm happy that it's been resolved."

Messrs. Penfield and Harry and Ms. Quinn declined comment.
