Business Brief -- Marriott Corp.: Fall Is Reported in Earnings From Continuing Operations

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/28/90

Marriott Corp. said net income for the third quarter was $27 million, or 27 cents a share, down 57% from $63 million, or 56 cents a share, in the year-earlier period. Year-ago results included airline catering and discontinued restaurant operations. The lodging and food services company, based in Bethesda, Md., reported sales of $1.66 billion, down 2.2% from $1.7 billion in the 1989 third quarter.

Marriott said per-share earnings from continuing operations fell sharply to 27 cents from 46 cents a year ago.

It attributed much of the decline to events in the Middle East, which have damped business travel. Marriott said pershare earnings from continuing operations actually rose 9%, excluding the impact of several non-comparable items.

J.W. Marriott Jr., chairman and president, said sales for ongoing lodging and contract-services operations were above 1989 levels.
