Business Brief -- Chase Manhattan Corp.: Bank Fires London Workers As Part of Broad Cutback

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/28/90

Chase Manhattan Corp. said it was firing 98 workers in its foreign-exchange and money-market operations in London in the first wave of a plan to trim its non-U.S. operations by 1,600 employees. The bank holding company said it also had trimmed five workers from its Frankfurt foreign-exchange operations and closed its Zurich dealing room, eliminating 20 jobs.

The cuts are part of a decision, which Chase announced a week ago, to trim costs by shedding 5,000 employees world-wide. Chase has about 2,600 remaining U.K. employees and 4,500 throughout Europe. A spokesman declined to say how many more of the planned non-U.S. reductions would come from its London and European pool of workers. Chase intends to complete all of its the staff reductions by year end, the spokesman added.

The cutbacks stem from the company's deteriorating commercial real estate loan portfolio.
