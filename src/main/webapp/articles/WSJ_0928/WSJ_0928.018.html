Senate Committee Approves Souter Nomination by 13-1

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/28/90

WASHINGTON -- The Senate Judiciary Committee approved the Supreme Court nomination of Judge David Souter by a 13-1 vote, and approval by the full Senate is expected next week. At least five of the eight Democrats on the committee expressed reservations about the views of the 51-year-old New Hampshire judge, but only Sen. Edward Kennedy of Massachusetts voted against him.

Sen. Joseph Biden (D., Del.), the committee chairman, said his support for the nominee "is a very close call." He said he has questions about Judge Souter's commitment to separation of church and state, eliminating race and sex discrimination, and protecting the right to privacy and abortion. Sen. Howard Metzenbaum (D., Ohio) said he was unsure but was voting his instincts. "My sense is that David Souter is a fair and open-minded jurist," he said.

Including Sen. Kennedy, six senators, all Democrats, have announced their opposition to Judge Souter thus far. And Senate aides say there aren't likely to be more than a dozen votes against the nomination. The opponents are Sens. Bill Bradley and Frank Lautenberg of New Jersey, Alan Cranston of California, Barbara Mikulski of Maryland and Brock Adams of Washington.

Yesterday, Sen. Kennedy warned that Judge Souter might "solidify a 5-4 anti-civil rights, anti-privacy majority inclined to turn back the clock. . ."
