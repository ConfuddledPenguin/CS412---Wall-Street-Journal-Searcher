International Brief -- Bond Corp. Holdings Ltd.: European Bondholders Clear Sale of Breweries to Unit

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/28/90

Bondholders of Australia-based Bond Corp. Holdings Ltd. approved the sale of its brewery assets to its 58%-owned Bell Resources Ltd. unit. The holders of US$200 million of 10-year 5 3/4% subordinated convertible bonds carried the resolution in London to sell the division. Bell plans to sell a 50% stake in the breweries to Lion Nathan Ltd., a New Zealand-based brewer, and operate them as a joint venture.

The clearance followed by a day Alan Bond's resignation as chairman and a director of the company, to pave the way for the financially troubled concern to be restructured. Mr. Bond foreshadowed his departure in July, saying he would step down if European holders of some Bond Corp.-related securities approved the sale of the company's Australian brewing interests. Even though European investors cleared the brewery sale yesterday, the deal could still collapse unless a group of investors who hold US$510 million of debentures issued by Bond Brewing Holdings Ltd. in the U.S. agree to sell their securities back to the company at a discount.
