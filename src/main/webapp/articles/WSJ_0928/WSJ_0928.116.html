Dermocrats Are Crying Foul Over GOP Campaign Unit

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/28/90

WASHINGTON -- The Democratic Senatorial Campaign Committee filed a complaint with the Federal Election Commission charging that its GOP rival is violating federal contribution limits in Senate races through a fund-raising program called the Inner Circle. Set up earlier this year as a separate fund-raising committee, the National Republican Senatorial Committee is using the Inner Circle to funnel $11 million to its Senate candidates.

Federal law places limits on the contributions that party committees can make to candidates. Each can contribute $17,500 directly to candidates and make certain additional expenditures on their behalf. Because the Inner Circle is set up independently from the Republican committee, the Republicans contend that those contributions to candidates don't fall under these limits. In its complaint, the Democratic committee says they should. Robert Bauer, general counsel to the Democratic committee, says the Republican committee is "trying to buy Senate seats."

In a news release, the GOP defended the Inner Circle program as legal and ethical.

Earlier this year, a federal judge here ruled that the Republican committee had illegally circumvented spending limits by routing $2.7 million to its Senate candidates in 1986.
