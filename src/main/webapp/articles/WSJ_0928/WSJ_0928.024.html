Who's News: UST's Gierer Named President in Shuffle Of Top Management

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/28/90

GREENWICH, Conn. -- UST Inc., a leading U.S. marketer of smokeless tobacco, said it made several changes in its top management, as part of its succession plan. Vincent A. Gierer Jr., formerly executive vice president and chief financial officer, was named president and chief operating officer. As president, the 42-year-old Mr. Gierer succeeds Louis F. Bantle, chairman and chief executive officer, who had assumed the additional post after Nicolas A. Buoniconti resigned as president and chief operating officer last December. Mr. Bantle, 61, had left the operating chief's post vacant.

Jack Africk, 61, formerly executive vice president and president of UST's U.S. Tobacco Co. unit, was elected a vice chairman of the parent. He will oversee legal and government relations and a new corporate development department.

Succeeding Mr. Africk as U.S. Tobacco president is Joseph R. Taddeo, who was senior vice president of the unit. Mr. Taddeo also was elected a UST director, increasing board membership to nine.

UST said much of Mr. Gierer's former responsibilities as chief financial officer will be assumed by John J. Bucchignano, who was named principal financial officer and senior vice president, and will remain principal accounting officer and controller.

UST produces smokeless tobacco sold under brand names such as Copenhagen and Skoal. It also markets wines and makes tobacco-related products, such as pipes and pipe tobacco.
