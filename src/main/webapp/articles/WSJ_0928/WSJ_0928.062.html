CML Group to Take A Charge, Post Losses For Quarter and Year

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/28/90

ACTON, Mass. -- CML Group Inc. said it expects to take a pretax charge of $24 million to $25 million in the fourth quarter, resulting in losses for the quarter and fiscal 1990. The charge stems from write-downs of the Carroll Reed division, which was sold to a Hong Kong concern last month. As a result, the company expects to report a net loss of $13 million to $14 million for the fourth quarter ended July 31, Charles M. Leighton, chairman and chief executive officer said. A year earlier, CML posted a loss of $43 million, or $6.70 a share, after write-down charges.

It is also anticipated that the direct marketer of consumer goods will report sales of about $61 million, up 30% from $46.8 million.

For the full year, CML said its net loss narrowed to $12.9 million from $31.9 million, or $4.92 a share, which includes a loss of $26.3 million, or $4.07 a share, from discontinued businesses. The company said it will report financial results Monday.

Excluding the Carroll Reed write-down, the company expects to post a profit from continuing operations for the year of about $13 million, or $1.95 a share, compared with a $5.5 million, or 85 cents a share, loss from continuing operations in fiscal 1989.

The company also expects to report that full-year sales increased 19% to $278.8 million from $234.6 million.

Leighton attributed CML's sales growth to brisk business at its Nature Co. and NordicTrack divisions. Fiscal 1990 sales at Nature Co., a retailer that sells telescopes, maps and outdoor instruments, increased 39% to more than $70 million; sales at NordickTrack, a maker of home exercise equipment, rose 16% to about $85 million.

Gains at Britches, the company's clothing retail-chain division, were more modest. Hampered by a soft apparel market, sales at its Britches stores division grew 11% to $117 million from $105.7 million.

Leighton said the company whittled its long-term bank debt $17 million in fiscal 1990 to $68 million. Part of the money came from proceeds from the sale of its Syber Vision and Gokeys units, the rest from company cash. In fiscal 1991, CML plans to further reduce that debt $20 million, using money it received from the sale of Carroll Reed, Leighton said.

The Carroll Reed sale was the last of CML's efforts to rid itself of four businesses over the past fiscal year. "We've sold off everything we want to sell, and from now on we think our growth potential is outstanding," Leighton said.

Its streamlined operations, reduced inventories and debt will help the company gear up for a lackluster economy expected in fiscal 1991, Leighton noted.
