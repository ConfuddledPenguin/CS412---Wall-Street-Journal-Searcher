Marketing & Media: Chiat/Day/Mojo Loses McElligott In Latest Setback

  • Author: Thomas R. King
  • Date: 09/28/90

NEW YORK -- In another blow to Chiat/Day/Mojo Inc., Thomas McElligott quit yesterday as executive vice president and executive creative director to open his own advertising agency in Minneapolis. Mr. McElligott, a highly regarded but eccentric creative executive who cofounded Minneapolis's Fallon McElligott agency, was brought in to Chiat just nine months ago to oversee creative work in the New York office, as well as its offices in Toronto and London. Chiat's headquarters are in Venice, Calif.

His departure comes at a difficult time for the agency, which is defending the $30 million to $35 million Royal Caribbean Cruise Line Inc. account. That account is under review, and Chiat has pulled out all the stops to save the business. In an unusual move the agency offered up Mr. McElligott to personally act as the top creative executive on the account.

Mr. McElligott couldn't be reached yesterday. In a statement, he didn't discuss his reasons for leaving, but said he felt it was a good time to start his own agency. And in an interview yesterday, Jay Chiat, chairman and chief executive officer, said Mr. McElligott told him that he wanted to live in Minneapolis once again and run his own shop.

"He had pretty much made up his mind to start an agency and he was getting somewhat concerned about getting more and more deeply involved here," Mr. Chiat said.

Mr. McElligott did help to bring in a couple of clients to Chiat's Toronto operations, but he failed in his bid to win the $13 million Haagen-Dazs account for the New York office, which earlier this week was awarded to BBDO, the only other competitor. Meanwhile, the shop lost many accounts and fired numerous staffers.

Mr. McElligott, 47 years old, will now be chairman and creative director of a new agency, called McElligott Wright Morrison White. Two partners, Tony Wright, 30, and Rob White, 34, are currently executives at Chiat. John Morrison, 38, currently works at Carmichael Lynch, a Minneapolis agency.

"We simply are going to make very smart ads . . . and we hope to make them for very smart advertisers," said Mr. Morrison, who said the new shop will open Nov. 1. He said he and his partners want the agency, although based in Minneapolis, to become regarded as a national agency. "We will go where the work is," he said.

Mr. Morrison said the agency doesn't yet have any clients.

Mr. Chiat said Mr. McElligott won't be replaced, but that Lee Clow, the shop's president who shared the executive creative director title with Mr. McElligott, will hold that position alone.

Mr. Chiat said he will now devote even more time to the New York operations, having recently been spending most of his time building a European network. "I plan to be much more active in the agency's operations," he said. "We've just got to get some new business in here."
