Unisys Corp. Obtains $180.6 Million Job For Navy Upgrades

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/28/90

WASHINGTON -- Unisys Corp. said it received a $180.6 million Navy contract for targeting system improvements.

OMI Bulk Transport Inc. got a $144.7 million Navy contract for transport charters.

General Dynamics Corp. was given contracts of $110.3 million for Army M-1 tanks and Navy aircraft.

Honeywell Inc. was issued a $104.7 million job for Army munitions.

Rockwell International Corp. got an $83.3 million Air Force contract for B-1B aircraft repair.

Southern Air Transport Inc. was awarded a $54.1 million Air Force contract for air transport services.

Grumman Corp. won a $48.2 million Air Force contract for F-111 aircraft components and Navy aircraft training equipment.

Motorola Inc. received a $36.9 million Navy contract for Standard missile components.

Avco Corp., a unit of Textron Inc., got a $35.4 million Air Force contract for MX missile components.

A joint venture of Textron Inc. and Boeing Co. received a $31.5 million Navy contract for development of the V-22 aircraft.

Boeing also received a $21.3 million Air Force contract for engineering services. And the company's Boeing Computer Services received a $15.8 million Army contract for automation systems.

Hughes Aircraft Co., a unit of General Motors Corp., was awarded a $23.3 million Navy contract for a targeting system. General Motors also got a $17.5 million Navy contract for aircraft engines.

Zantop International Airlines received a $23.2 million Air Force contract for air transportation services.

Martin-Baker Aircraft Co. was given a $19 million Navy contract for aircraft ejection seats.

ITT Corp. received an $18 million Army contract for a radar jammer.

Computer Sciences Corp. won a $15.3 million Army contract for automation systems.

Republic Corp. was issued a $14.7 million Air Force contract for C-130 aircraft overhaul.

GTE Government Systems Corp., a unit of GTE Corp., won a $12 million Army contract for communications equipment.
