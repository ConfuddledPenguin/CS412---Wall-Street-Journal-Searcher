Letters to the Editor: Other Voices Heard On Israeli Left Wing

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/28/90

In reprinting an article by Yossi Sarid of the Israeli Ratz-CRM Party (editorial page, Sept. 10), you editorialized that the article "touched off an outpouring of similar statements from the Israeli left doubting whether compromise with Palestinians is possible." Your readers should know that Shulamith Aloni, head of CRM, and CRM Knesset member Dedi Zucker both criticized Mr. Sarid. Mr. Zucker said Mr. Sarid's statement that the Palestinians will now have to "look for me" when they want to talk is in fact a classic right-wing argument. "Anyone who conditions negotiations on the Palestinians changing their leadership is speaking with the Likud's mouth, because the Likud, too, is unwilling to talk to them unless they change their leadership," Mr. Zucker said. Said Mr. Aloni: "Never has the enemy dictated our moral criteria or determined what was good and right for the State of Israel."

Merle Thorpe Jr.

President

Foundation for Middle East Peace

Washington
