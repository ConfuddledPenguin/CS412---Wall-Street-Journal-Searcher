Business Brief -- Information International Inc.: Investment Group Asserts That Firm Should Be Sold

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/28/90

Mr. Dorsey R. Gardner, president of Kelso Management Co., sent a letter on behalf of an investment group to Information International Inc.'s chairman saying he felt the company should be sold. The group holds a 33.9% stake in the company. In a filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission, Mr. Gardner stated, among other things, that the company has suffered operating losses over the past two years. According to the filing, Mr. Gardner feels the company should retain an investment banker and proceed with an orderly auction of the business.

Alfred Fenaughty, president of Information International, said, "We don't believe this is the right time to even consider such a plan."

Mr. Fenaughty conceded that the company has had operating losses in the past two years but he said the company did report net income in those years due to settlement from a lawsuit and interest income. He also said he expects the company to have net income again for the fiscal year ending April 30, 1991.
