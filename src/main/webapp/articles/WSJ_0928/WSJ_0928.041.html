Marketing & Media: U.S. Newsprint Usage Dropped 1% in August

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/28/90

WASHINGTON -- The American Newspaper Publishers Association estimated newsprint consumption by all U.S. users at 992,824 metric tons in August, a decrease of less than 1% from a year earlier. For the first eight months of the year, the industry group estimated that consumption rose 1% to 8,034,531 metric tons. A metric ton is equal to about 2,204.62 pounds.

The association said U.S. daily newspapers used 735,722 metric tons of newsprint in August, slightly lower than 742,642 metric tons used a year earlier. Daily newspapers' consumption in the first eight months was little changed at 5,980,211 metric tons.

Newsprint inventories of newspapers in the association's statistical sample averaged a 41-day supply in August, compared with a 40-day supply a year earlier. The association said stocks of North American mills were down 17%.

The association said Canadian newsprint mills operated at 90% of rated capacity in August and at 94% in the first eight months. U.S. mills operated at 98.7% in August and at 97.1% in the year-to-date.
