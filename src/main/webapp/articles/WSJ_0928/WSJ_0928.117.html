Experts Check Break-In Of U.S. General's Home

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/28/90

WASHINGTON -- U.S. counterterrorism and intelligence experts are combing the details of a break-in at the Brussels home of an American general to see if it was a terrorist attack connected in any way with Iraq. The incident, believed to have been an attempt at murder or kidnapping, was aimed at Gen. John Douglass, who works on the military committee at North Atlantic Treaty Organization headquarters. Gen. Douglass wasn't home, so the attack failed. No group has publicly claimed responsibility for the raid Tuesday.

Recently, U.S. intelligence has spotted agents of several Arab terror groups with links to Iraq conducting surveillance in Europe at American military installations. No attacks have resulted from the surveillance, but the Bush administration has warned Baghdad it will hold Iraq responsible for terrorist attacks that occur during the Gulf crisis.

In the Brussels incident, several men broke into Gen. Douglass's house and knocked out his aide, while confederates waited outside. The dazed aide, who said he was also drugged by the attackers, has been unable so far to provide a full, detailed account of the incident.
