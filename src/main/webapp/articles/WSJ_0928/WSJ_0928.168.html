Goldman Begins New Fund To Invest in Sick Firms

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/28/90

NEW YORK -- In the latest sign of Wall Street's rush to profit from companies in financial distress, Goldman Sachs & Co. said it finished raising a $750 million fund that will invest in troubled corporations. Goldman's fund is the second biggest of more than 20 "restructuring" or "turnaround" investment pools being raised this year, according to pension consultants. The Goldman fund, known as Water Street Recovery Fund I, is exceeded in size only by a $1 billion pool raised earlier this year by Chicago investors Samuel Zell and David Schulte.

Goldman figures there is plenty of money to be made from turnaround investing. In the four years prior to setting up the Water Street fund, the firm says it made 22 investments of its own in distressed companies' bonds, bank debt or equity, usually at cut-rate prices. Eleven of those investments produced annual returns of 35% or better, says Goldman partner Mikael Salovaara. Mr. Salovaara is managing the new fund.

In a memo to investors, the Water Street fund states that it is seeking annual returns of 25% to 35%. If that goal is achieved, Goldman will benefit richly. Goldman has $100 million of its own money in the fund; it also is entitled to as much as 20% of the profits earned by its other investors, which include corporate pension funds, university endowments and foreign banks, mostly Japanese.
