Business Brief -- Au Printemps S.A.: French Retail Firm Returns To Profit in the First Half

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/28/90

Au Printemps S.A. said it had consolidated net profit of 57 million French francs ($10.9 million) for the first half of 1990, compared with a year-earlier loss of 50 million francs. The French retailing group said that part of the improvement reflected its decision to no longer include in its results its 25% stake in the distribution group Euromarche S.A. During the first half of 1989, Euromarche cut about 30 million francs off Printemps' earnings.

Printemps said earnings were also helped by the use of proceeds from 1989 asset sales -- totaling about 800 million francs -- to pay down its debt and reduce costs.

Revenue climbed 2% to 13.96 billion francs from 13.66 billion. Pretax profit from current operations surged to 192 million francs from 92 million.

Printemps wouldn't forecast full-year earnings because of the unknown impact of the Persian Gulf crisis on demand during the second half. However, it said it expects a significant improvement in its current operating profit for the year.

For 1989, Printemps had consolidated net profit of 524 million francs on revenue of 28.5 billion francs.
