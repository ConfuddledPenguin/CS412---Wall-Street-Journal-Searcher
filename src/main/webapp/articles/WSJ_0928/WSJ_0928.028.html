Nuclear Energy Proposes Fine for Plant in Arizona

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/28/90

WINTERSBURG, Ariz. -- The U.S. Nuclear Regulatory Commission proposed a $75,000 civil penalty against the Palo Verde Nuclear Generating Station for two apparent violations of NRC rules on medical examinations for operators. The station is owned by a consortium of seven utilities, including the Arizona Public Service Co. unit of Pinnacle West Capital Corp., Phoenix.

Licensed operators of nuclear power plants are required to have biannual medical exams. The commission said the station apparently failed to notify the NRC about four licensed operators who had developed potentially incapacitating medical conditions. It also cited apparent inaccuracies in certification to the NRC that candidates for operator licenses had received complete medical exams.

The apparent violations occurred between May 1987 and last August. Palo Verde said the safety of the plant wasn't affected. Palo Verde has 30 days to respond to the notice.
