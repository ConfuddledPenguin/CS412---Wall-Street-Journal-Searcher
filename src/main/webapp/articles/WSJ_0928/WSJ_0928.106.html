Tenneco Group Slates Purchase of Assets From Georgia Pacific

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/28/90

HOUSTON -- Tenneco Inc. said it and a number of investors concluded definitive agreements with Georgia Pacific Corp. for the purchase of various Georgia-Pacific assets for about $740 million. Earlier this month the companies disclosed preliminary negotiations. The transaction is expected to close by year end, Tenneco said.

Under the agreements, Tenneco's Packaging Corp. of America unit will purchase 19 cardboard container assembly plants located primarily in the south and southeast, and cutting rights on timberland previously leased to Georgia Pacific.

In addition, GE Capital Corp. agreed to buy two cardboard container materials plants and lease them to Packaging Corp. Tenneco has options to purchase the facilities at the end of an undisclosed lease term. GE Capital is the finance arm of General Electric Corp., Fairfield, Conn.

Tenneco said John Hancock Mutual Life Insurance Co. and Metropolitan Life Insurance Co. agreed to purchase from Georgia Pacific and lease to Packaging Corp. 540,000 acres of timberland in four states.

Tenneco also said it will make a $50 million working capital infusion into Packaging Corp., which makes paperboard and containerboard products and plastic and aluminum packaging.
