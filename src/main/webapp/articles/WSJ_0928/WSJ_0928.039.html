Bloomingdale's Wins Approval for Outlet In Minnesota Mall

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/28/90

NEW YORK -- The Bloomingdale's department store chain received court permission to proceed with a new store at the Mall of America in Bloomington, Minn. Judge J. Vincent Aug Jr. made his ruling in U.S. bankruptcy court in Ohio. His approval was necessary because Bloomingdale's, together with the Federated and Allied retail units of Campeau Corp., Toronto, filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy-law protection Jan. 15.

"We're very excited. You can't deny the magic of their name," said Herbert Simon, president of Melvin Simon & Associates Inc., Indianapolis. He is in charge of daily operations at the mall, expected to open in September 1992. The $625 million project is a joint venture between affiliates of Melvin Simon & Associates and Triple Five Corp. of Edmonton, Alberta.

Separately, Judge Aug ruled that attorneys for the Allied Stores Corp. bondholders committee could conduct a fact-finding interview, or deposition, with former Campeau Chairman Robert Campeau. The issue will be heard by a court in Ontario.

Attorneys for bondholders want to question Mr. Campeau in connection with a suit for fraudulent conveyance they hope to file against Federated Stores Inc., Campeau Corp. and other parties involved in Allied's sale of Ann Taylor and Brooks Brothers. The bondholders want to recover between $700 million and $1 billion they say belongs to Allied and its creditors.

Judge Aug hasn't ruled on whether he will allow the bondholders to sue.

An attorney for Mr. Campeau said he didn't know how his client would respond to the ruling.
