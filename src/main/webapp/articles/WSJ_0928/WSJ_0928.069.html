Carnival Cruise Lines Expects Rise in Profit For Third Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/28/90

MIAMI -- Carnival Cruise Lines Inc. said it expects to report fiscal third-quarter per-share earnings in line with analysts' estimates of 79 cents to 81 cents, up from the year-earlier 63 cents, said Micky Arison, president and chief executive officer. Net income will be between $105 million and $110 million, Mr. Arison estimated, compared with the year-earlier quarter's $84.4 million. Revenue for the quarter, ended Aug. 31, will approximate $500 million compared with $405.2 million a year earlier.

Mr. Arison attributed the improved results to new business generated from the Fantasy cruise ship, added to the fleet in March. Extra capacity also came from the addition of 490 berths to the Westerdam this year. Carnival is the world's largest cruise line based on passengers carried.

In recent weeks, amid investor concerns about a recession and rising fuel prices, Carnival's stock has had the wind blown out of its sails. Recent analyst reports have reflected some of the caution. Since early August, Carnival's stock price has fallen about 34%. "Our stock has taken a pretty good hammering," said Mr. Arinson.

But the president said the concerns are overblown. On fuel, he said, there was "very little impact" on third-quarter earnings. And fuel costs are proportionally much lower for the cruise industry, he said, than for others in the travel business, such as airlines. When Iraq invaded Kuwait, said Mr. Arison, fuel represented less than 3% of Carnival's cost of sales -- a figure that has now only climbed to about 4%. While conceding some small negative impact on earnings in the future, he said, "We don't believe that these fuel rates are sustainable." He added that "some percentage of our going-forward fuel needs have been hedged."

As to the possibility of a recession, Mr. Arison says investors need only look at history. In the 1982 recession, he said, Carnival's bookings didn't decline. "We offer a good value, all-inclusive vacation," that people who already plan to go on vacation flock to in penny-pinching times, he said.

In the third quarter, occupancy levels were slightly higher than the year-earlier quarter's 112.2%. (In the cruise business, two passengers in a room count as 100% occupancy; if kids or others stay, too, it rises above 100%.)

For the nine months, net will approximate $184 million to $189 million, or $1.38 to $1.40 a share, compared with the year-earlier period's $172.3 million, or $1.28 a share. Revenue will be about $1 billion, up from the $921.6 million in the year-earlier period.
