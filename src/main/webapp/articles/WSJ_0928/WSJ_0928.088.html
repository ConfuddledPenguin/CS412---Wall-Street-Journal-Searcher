Business Brief -- Colonial Realty Corp.: Bankruptcy-Court Petition Is Converted to Chapter 11

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/28/90

Colonial Realty Corp., the embattled West Hartford, Conn., real estate syndicator, got a reprieve yesterday when a petition for involuntary bankruptcy-court liquidation of the company was converted into a Chapter 11 reorganization. U.S. Bankruptcy Court Judge Robert Krechevsky approved a request by Colonial's lawyers to convert the Chapter 7 petition, which was filed on Sept. 14 by six banks. The banks, including a unit of Bank of New England Corp. and People's Bank of Bridgeport, did not oppose the conversion.

Colonial, which is closely held, has marketed limited partnerships on real estate once valued at more than $1 billion.

"Obviously, this is in the best interests of the investors and of the company," said Colonial spokesman Tom Drohan.

John Nolan, a Hartford lawyer who represents one of the banks, said that a creditors commitee had been formed, which would seek court approval to hire Coopers & Lybrand to examine the company's books.
