A LIST of the world's 25 largest securities firms compiled by Wright Investors Service should have given Nomura Securities' capital as $12.8 billion, not $10.9 billion. The list represents figures as of each company's 1989 fiscal year's end. In addition, Principal Mutual Life is based in the U.S., not the U.K. as indicated in the list of the world's 50 largest insurers. Both lists appeared in The Wall Street Journal Report on world business. {See: "World Business (A Special Report): Rankings --- The World's 25 Largest Securities Firms" -- WSJ Sept. 21, 1990}

---

RCS EMERGING GROWTH FUND had a total return of -9.18% in the four weeks ended Aug. 30, a 0.96% return for this year through that date and a return of 14.68% over the preceding 12 months. These returns were misstated in a recent Mutual Fund Scorecard due to a data base error at Lipper Analytical Services Inc.

---

FIELDSTONE Co., a consulting firm in New York specializing in personal computers and sales and marketing training for executives, is not related to Fieldstone Private Capital Group L.P., an investment bank specializing in lease financings and private placements of securities. A report of Fieldstone Private Capital's formation appeared in Wednesday's editions.

(See: "Who's News: An Investment Bank, Fieldstone, Is Formed" -- WSJ Sept. 26, 1990)

---

UAL Corp. and Pan Am Corp. don't pay quarterly dividends. An earlier edition incorrectly cited quarterly dividend figures that currently aren't being paid by the companies.

(See: "USAir Suspends Quarterly Payout, Defers Deliveries" -- WSJ Sept. 27, 1990)
