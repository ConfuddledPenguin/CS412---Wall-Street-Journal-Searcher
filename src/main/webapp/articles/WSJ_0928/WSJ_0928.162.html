Florida Judge Says CPAs Can Solicit Clients Directly

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/28/90

A federal judge in Florida struck down as unconstitutional a rule by the state's board of accountancy that restricted certified public accountants from directly soliciting new business. In his ruling, Judge Maurice M. Paul found the state's administrative code "places an unconstitutional ban on protected commercial speech in violation of the first and fourteenth amendments."

The case was brought by Scott Fane, a CPA licensed to practice in Florida, against the state board, which the Legislature has empowered to promulgate rules for the profession. An attorney in the Florida attorney general's office, which defended the case, said a decision hasn't been made whether to appeal.

The state argued that its prohibition of accountants soliciting business in person, or by telephone, was similar to restrictions on attorney solicitation recently upheld by the U.S. Supreme Court. But the judge held that concerns surrounding solicitation by attorneys were unique to that profession, and couldn't be extended to accountants.

David Vladeck, an attorney with the Public Citizen litigation group in Washington who represented Mr. Fane, said he doesn't know whether his client has begun exercising his right to drum up business. Mr. Fane, a tax specialist, "believes it will be much more efficient for him" to be able to call potential clients and offer his services, Mr. Vladeck said.
