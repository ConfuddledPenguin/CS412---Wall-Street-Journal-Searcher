International Brief -- Ing. C. Olivetti & Co.: Consolidated Pretax Profit For the First Half Fell 41%

  • Author: null
  • Date: 09/28/90

Ing. C. Olivetti & Co. of Italy, an office automation equipment maker, reported that first-half consolidated pretax profit fell 41% to 60.9 billion lire ($51.9 million) from 102.6 billion lire a year earlier, continuing a 3 1/2-year earnings slide. Operating profit fell 35% to 102.1 billion lire from 156.4 billion lire.

Revenue rose 5.8% to 4.167 trillion lire from 3.938 trillion lire.

Carlo De Benedetti, chairman and the controlling shareholder, blamed the earnings drop on tough market conditions, a continuing squeeze in profit margins and an unfavorable exchange rate for the lira. But the company claimed it fared much better than most of its European and U.S. competitors, and predicted it will continue to outpace other computer makers for the rest of the year, though it didn't give a specific earnings forecast.
