Construction Startups Slipped 3% in August, Continuing the Trend

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 09/28/90

NEW YORK -- Construction contracting dropped 3% in August, but that reflects virtually no change in the recent weak rate of building activity, the latest F.W. Dodge report said. F.W. Dodge, the forecasting unit of McGraw-Hill Inc., said total construction startup rates slipped to $230.14 billion in August, from $236.31 billion in July. The seasonally adjusted annual rate brought the Dodge index of contract values down to 146 from 150 the previous month, as was expected based on fluctuations reported since last winter's steep decline. The index uses 1982 as a base of 100.

Regarding the impact of the Middle East conflict on the value of new construction, George A. Christie, vice president and chief economist for F.W. Dodge said, "The risk, of course, is whether higher-cost oil will tip the already weakened economy into recession by next winter. Chances are it will, and that implies a secondary decline for construction, particularly for commercial building and housing."

Values for both nonresidential and residential building eased 4% in August. Office building remained the weakest segment, with an August rate of $77.52 billion, down from $80.34 billion in July, when the level plummeted 23% to its lowest level in a decade. Nonbuilding construction, including public works and utilities, edged up 1% due to a surge of sewer and water projects.

Through the eight months of 1990, total contracting values fell 8% from the year ago period. The Northeast region remained most depressed with a 24% shortfall for the eight months, followed by the Southeast, which reported a 14% drop. The only gain this year was in the North Central region, which rose 3% in August.
