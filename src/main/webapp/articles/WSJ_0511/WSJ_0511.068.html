Greenspan Offers Options Concerning Thrift Rules

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/11/90

CHICAGO -- Federal Reserve Board Chairman Alan Greenspan suggested several options for toughening regulation of banks and thrifts if the federal "safety net" is expanded to cover broader powers that may be given to those institutions. Mr. Greenspan, in a speech to a bankers convention here, said the safety net has "provided measurable benefits to the U.S. economy" including "virtually {eliminating} financial panics." But he said the recent savings-and-loan industry bailout shows the government must find ways to limit the costs from failures of financial institutions.

The Fed chairman described the safety net as including not only federal deposit insurance, but the Fed's lending to banks for liquidity purposes, as well as the interbank payments system backed by the Fed.

A key issue facing policy makers, Mr. Greenspan said, is whether to expand or restrict the safety net to cover the new powers likely to be given to banks in the next few years, such as selling insurance and participating in broader securities-underwriting and real estate activities.

Mr. Greenspan said that if the safety net is effectively expanded to back such new activities, "we should probably develop" new deposit-insurance policies such as pricing premiums according to the riskiness of a bank's assets, and more frequent examinations of banks.
