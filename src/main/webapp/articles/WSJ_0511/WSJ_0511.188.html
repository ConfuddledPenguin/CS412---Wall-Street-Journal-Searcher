CAE Profit Plunged 94% in Fiscal Year; U.S. Results Cited

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/11/90

TORONTO -- CAE Industries Ltd. said lower earnings from the company's U.S. operations and accounting changes resulted in a 94% drop in net income for the year ended March 31. CAE, a dominant player in the military and commercial simulation markets, reported net income of 2.7 million Canadian dollars (US$2.3 million) or three cents (Cdn) a share, in the 1990 fiscal year, down from C$42.8 million, or 55 cents (Cdn) a share, the previous year. Revenue rose 33% to C$1.12 billion from C$838.8 million.

CAE attributed the decline to changes in accounting practices, which increased the company's income taxes and the amount of a write-down on two unprofitable contracts at its U.S. operations, CAELink. Income taxes payable in fiscal 1990 rose to C$19.1 million from C$11.7 million in 1989. The write-down, originally estimated at C$10.5 million, now amounts to C$17.5 million.

Interest expenses also rose by C$15 million to C$42.9 million.

CAE said that without the accounting changes, the company would have reported net of about 30 cents (Cdn) a share.

CAE acquired Singer Co.'s Link Flight Simulation Division in 1988 for $550 million. The company has embarked on a restructuring program which CAE's senior vice president of finance, John Caldwell, said will improve the unit's profitability in a few years' time.

David Race, the company's president and chief executive officer, said he expects to see "modest growth" in revenue and profitability in its fiscal 1991 year. He declined to give a specific estimate.

"Based on the . . . progress we expect to make this year, I am optimistic that fiscal 1992 should show a return to improved growth in revenue and earnings," Mr. Race said.
