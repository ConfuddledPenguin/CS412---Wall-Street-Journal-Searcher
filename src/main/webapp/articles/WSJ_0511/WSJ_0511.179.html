ABB Asea Is Said To Set Unit's Sale For $525 Million

  • Author: Randall Smith
  • Date: 05/11/90

ABB Asea Brown Boveri Ltd took a step toward digesting its recent acquisition of Combustion Engineering Inc. by agreeing to sell Georgia Kaolin Co. for about $525 million, according to sources familiar with the transaction. The buyer is ECC Group PLC, formerly English China Clays PLC, based in Cornwall, England, which plans to add Georgia Kaolin's line of paper pigments to its industrial minerals business. Kaolin, also known as China clay, is used to give magazine pages a glossy sheen.

The agreement, which is subject to U.S. antitrust clearance and other regulatory approvals, is expected to be announced in London this morning.

ABB, the world's largest electrical engineering company with more than $25 billion in estimated sales, acquired Combustion Engineering late last year for about $1.6 billion to bolster its U.S. presence in its main business of making equipment used to generate and transmit electrical power.

The Zurich, Switzerland, concern is selling Georgia Kaolin, which has three plants in Georgia, because the industrial pigments business doesn't fit with its main power-plant business. ABB is co-owned by BBC Brown Boveri AG of Switzerland and Asea AB of Sweden.

ECC, which has annual sales of about $1.6 billion, already has some operations in Georgia. Georgia Kaolin had pretax profit of about $41 million in 1989 on sales of roughly $170 million.

ABB is being advised on the sale by First Boston Corp., a unit of CS First Boston Group Inc. ECC is advised by J. Henry Schroder Wagg & Co. in London.

ECC plans to finance the purchase with a three-year borrowing of about $600 million, which it may refinance with longer-term securities later. It doesn't expect to use an equity rights offering as financing.

ECC also plans a $200 million preferred stock issue and intends to sell certain businesses to raise roughly #100 million ($167.9 million) more by the end of 1991. ECC doesn't expect the acquisition to dilute its earnings in the first full year after completion, and it should boost operating profit in 1991.
