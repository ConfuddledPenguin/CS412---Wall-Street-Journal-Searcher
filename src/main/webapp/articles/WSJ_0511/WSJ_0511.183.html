X-Rite Sees '90 Net Exceeding $4 Million Despite Weak Period

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/11/90

GRANDVILLE, Mich. -- Despite a drop in first-quarter earnings, X-Rite Corp. said it should meet an analyst's prediction that 1990 net income will rise to more than $4 million, or about 80 cents a share, from $3.6 million, or 70 cents a share, the previous year. Ted Thompson, X-Rite chairman, president and chief executive officer, says he is upbeat despite a lackluster first quarter in which earnings fell 6.6% to $764,000, or 15 cents a share, from $818,000, or 16 cents a share, a year earlier. Quarter revenue rose 10% to $5.5 million from $5 million.

X-Rite has diversified from its original business of developing tapes to mark X-rays, and currently derives 77% of its $21.2 million in annual revenue from sales of quality-control instruments, including color-measurement instruments used in the graphic-arts industry.

In December, X-Rite began rolling out a new line of machines designed for broader industrial uses such as measuring the colors of paints, fabrics, ceramics, floor coverings. Mr. Thompson said the new instruments have been well received, but it is too early to predict how they will affect revenue and profit for the year.

X-rite is nearly half owned by a group of insiders, including Mr. Thompson. The company has no debt and about $9.3 million in cash and short-term investments.

X-rite recently raised its quarterly dividend to three cents a share from two cents. Mr. Thompson doesn't want to raise the dividend again, however, despite the substantial cash position for a company its size. Instead, Mr. Thompson wants to use the cash to finance a factory expansion as well as inventory, marketing, and other costs associated with the new line of instruments.
