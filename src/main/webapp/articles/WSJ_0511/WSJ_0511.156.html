Business Brief -- Washington Post Co.: Board Clears the Buy-Back Of Up to 1 Million Shares

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/11/90

Washington Post Co.'s board cleared the company to buy on the open market as many as one million shares of its Class B common stock, currently trading at $241.50 each. The board didn't set a price ceiling or a time limit for the purchases. In New York Stock Exchange Composite trading yesterday, the stock closed up $8.50 a share. At current levels the transaction would cost the company as much as $241.5 million.

A spokesman said that since 1987 the company has purchased about 700,000 of its shares on the open market.

Peter Keefe, an analyst with Johnston, Lemon & Co., noted the buy-back strategy is common among companies in which Warren E. Buffett is a major investor. Mr. Buffett, chairman of Berkshire Hathaway Inc., controls 16.5% of Washington Post's Class B stock, which are nonvoting shares. Class A shares are 100% controlled by the family of Katharine Graham, Washington Post chairman.

The media company publishes the Washington Post newspaper and Newsweek magazine.
