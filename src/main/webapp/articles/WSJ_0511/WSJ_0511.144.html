Bond Media's Deal For U.S. Cash Boost Appears Less Likely

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/11/90

NEW YORK -- Reports that Bond Media Ltd., the owner of an Australian television network, is about to receive a large cash infusion from three U.S. companies may be greatly exaggerated. Dispatches out of Sydney, Australia, quoted Bond Media officials as saying they were close to completing a deal to receive $125 million from an unusual trio of U.S. companies: Paramount Communications Inc., CBS Inc. and General Electric Co.'s NBC unit.

But a Paramount spokesman would say only that the entertainment company is considering making an investment and wouldn't comment on the involvement of other parties. An NBC spokesman said that company isn't involved at all: "We were approached by Bond. We told them what it would take to do a deal from our point of view. It is our understanding they are pursuing other options."

And a CBS spokeswoman said CBS is in only "preliminary" discussions regarding such an investment. CBS President Laurence A. Tisch has been reluctant in the past to expand CBS's scope of operations beyond getting the third-place network back on track.

Bond has been under pressure to refinance a large amount of debt taken on several years ago to buy the Channel Nine network.
