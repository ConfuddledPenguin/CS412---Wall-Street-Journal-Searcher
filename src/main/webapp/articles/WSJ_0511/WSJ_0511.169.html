Business Brief -- Hershey Foods Corp.: Company to Sell 17% Stake In Sweden's AB Marabou

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/11/90

Hershey Foods Corp. signed a definitive agreement to sell its 17% equity stake in AB Marabou, a Swedish confectionary company, to Orkla Borregaard A.S. for more than $75 million. Orkla is a Norwegian concern with interests in consumer packaged goods, chemicals and communications. The Hershey, Pa., company said it expects the sale to result in a one-time aftertax gain of more than $35 million, or 39 cents a share. The gain from the sale, which requires approval from the Swedish and Norwegian governments, will most likely be applied to second-quarter results, said a company spokesman.

Freia AS, a major Norwegian shareholder of Marabou, is in the midst of a tender offer for those shares of Marabou it doesn't own. The spokesman said Hershey decided not to tender its shares because Orkla offered about $12 million more for the food company's stake, which carries 20% of the voting rights. "It's called maximizing shareholder value," he said.
