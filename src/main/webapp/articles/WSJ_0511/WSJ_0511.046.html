New Securities Issues

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/11/90

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report: MUNICIPALS

New York City -- the bulk of $900 million of general obligation revenue anticipation notes, Fiscal 1990 Series C and D, due June 29, 1990, apparently was won by a Merrill Lynch Capital Markets group. Merrill Lynch purchased $400 million in notes at yields ranging from 6.247% to 6.419%. The notes were priced as follows: $100 million of notes priced to yield 6.247%, $50 million of notes priced to yield 6.288%, $100 million of notes priced to yield 6.337%, $100 million of notes priced to yield 6.386% and $50 million of notes priced to yield 6.419%. The notes, rated MIG-2 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and SP-1 by Standard & Poor's Corp., were priced at the initial reoffering to yield 6.40%, according to an official with the lead underwriter. The notes are being sold to help the city meet its financial obligations until it receives funds from the state. The state funds are unavailable until a state budget is passed. All together, seven firms purchased the $900 million in notes, according to city officials.

MORTGAGES

Shearson Lehman Hutton Inc. -- $153 million of Shearson Lehman Hutton Mortgage Trust real estate mortgage investment conduit securities is being offered in eight classes. Full pricing details weren't immediately available. The offering, Trust 1, is backed by 15-year and 30-year mortgages with a weighted average coupon rate of 10.97%. The mortgages are "non-conforming," which means they aren't eligible for purchase by the Federal National Mortgage Association or Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. Most of the mortgages are too large to be purchased by either agency. The issue is rated triple-A by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and Standard & Poor's Corp. based on the quality of the underlying loans, which are mostly from California, and an insurance policy covering 10% of the deal from General Electric Mortgage Insurance Co.

EUROBONDS

World Bank (agency) -- 200 million marks ($122.3 million) of 8 7/8% bonds due June 1, 2000, priced at 101 5/8 to yield 8.62%, via Bayerische Landesbank Girozentrale. Also 100 million Australian dollars (US$75.6 million) of 14 1/2% bonds due June 7, 1996, priced at 102 to yield 14.47% less fees, via Hambros Bank. Fees 1 7/8.

Autobahnen & Schnellstrassen-Finanzierungs- Aktiengesellschaft (Austria) -- 150 million Swiss francs ($107.1 million) of 7 1/4% public bonds due June 25, 2000, priced at 7 1/4% via Swiss Bank Corp. Guaranteed by the Republic of Austria.

Okobank (Finland) -- 10 billion yen ($64 million) of 7.4% bonds due May 22, 1995, priced at 101 7/8, via Nomura International. Call option at par on May 22, 1992.

Cellular Inc. (U.S.) -- $40 million of 8% convertible subordinated debentures due 2000, priced at par, via Kidder, Peabody International Ltd. The debentures are convertible at any time prior to maturity into shares of the company's common stock on or after the date on which definitive debentures are issued in exchange for the temporary global debenture. The conversion price will be $14.95 per share, subject to adjustment under certain circumstances. The company also said the debentures are redeemable at the option of the company at any time after the expiration of 30 days following the date on which the temporary global debenture is exchanged for definitive debentures, at prices declining from 106%, if called prior to May 18, 1991, to 100% on or after May 18, 1996. The debentures aren't redeemable prior to May 18, 1993, unless the closing price of the common stock for a specified period is at least 200% of the conversion price then in effect. Proceeds will be used to finance acquisitions of cellular telephone properties.

Canton of Bern (Switzerland) -- 51 million Swiss francs of bonds due June 1, 2000, with equity-purchase warrants, fixed 5 3/4% coupon at par, via Citicorp Investment Bank Switzerland. Exercise period of warrants from June 1, 1990, to Dec. 1, 1991. Each 6,800 franc bond issued with one warrant allowing the right to acquire one basket of shares of Swiss chemical companies consisting of one Sandoz Ltd. registered share, four Ciba-Geigy Ltd. registered shares and six La Roche Holding participation certificates at a price of 45,945 francs, an 18.6% premium to Wednesday's closing prices.
