Ten Big Groups Pledge To Reduce Real Estate Held in South Korea

  • Author: Special to The Wall Street Journal
  • Date: 05/11/90

SEOUL -- South Korea's 10 largest business groups pledged Thursday to cooperate with a strict government order requiring them to sell excessive real estate holdings within six months. The businesses, which include Hyundai, Lucky-Goldstar, Daewoo, Lotte, Ssangyong and Sunkyong, also agreed to donate 1% of pretax business profits to create an employee welfare fund and to use their resources to build apartments for their workers.

Lee Kun-hee, chairman of the Samsung Group, made the announcement on behalf of the 10 business groups at a news conference. The heads of the other groups also attended.

He said the proposed sales would involve 5,140 hectares scattered throughout the country, roughly 15% of their total real estate holdings. Under the agreement, all land not sold by the six-month deadline will be transferred to a state-owned development agency at prices set by the government.

Among the 10 groups, Samsung stands to sell the most holdings, roughly 18.9 million square meters, followed by Sunkyong's 10.3 million, Lucky-Goldstar's 4.6 million, Ssangyong's 3.6 million and Hyundai's 3.3 million.

The group owners also pledged to do their best to overcome what they termed the critical condition of South Korea's economy.

Thursday's announcement came two days after the government ordered the nation's 49 leading business groups to sell by year's end millions of dollars in real estate they are holding for speculation or don't urgently need for their business operations. The government said businesses that fail to abide by the order will face stiff taxes and a cutoff in fresh bank loans.
