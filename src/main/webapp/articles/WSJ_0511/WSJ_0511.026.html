Sharon Steel to Pay Penalty

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/11/90

PITTSBURGH -- Sharon Steel Corp. agreed to upgrade its waste-water treatment facilities in western Pennsylvania and to pay a $500,000 penalty for violating federal and state clean-water laws. Under the terms of the settlement, announced by the Pennsylvania Department of Environmental Resources, Sharon Steel will complete construction of new waste-water treatment facilities to lower its discharge of solid wastes into the Shenango River.

The federal consent decree also requires Sharon to pay civil penalties of $300,000 to the Department of Environmental Protection and $200,000 to the Pennsylvania state agency, for deposit in the state Clean Water Fund.
