Mexico's Consumer Prices Rise

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/11/90

MEXICO CITY -- Consumer prices rose 1.5% in April, bringing the rise for the year to 10.8%, the central bank said. The rate was the same as was registered in March, a Bank of Mexico communique said. Prices on housing, milk, restaurants, automobiles, airline tickets, hotels, newspapers, beer and some foods were the biggest contributors to the overall increase, the bank said. Declines in prices of some vegetables and in electricity prices in cities that apply summer rates helped compensate for some of the increases, it said.

The prices that producers pay rose 0.8% to a total of 7.2% for the year.

The government held inflation to 19.7% in 1989 with a wage-price control program introduced in December 1987, when the annual rate reached 159.2%.
