Technology: Chip Industry Indicator Was Unchanged in April

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/11/90

CUPERTINO, Calif. -- The semiconductor industry's leading indicator remained unchanged in April, as semiconductor orders continued a steady increase. The Semiconductor Industry Association said the so-called book-to-bill ratio for April was 1.09:1, the same as in March. Both figures are preliminary. A 1.09 rate means that for every $100 of products shipped, or billed, chip makers received $109 in new orders, or bookings.

The rising indicator shows stability in the chip market instead of the slump many were expecting for 1990, said Doug Andrey, the chip association's statistics director. "The great fear was that of a recession, but the figures show a relatively flat" chip market instead, he said. Bookings tend to peak in April and May during a flat year, he said, and fall off in the seasonally slower months.

Semiconductor billings in April were $1.114 billion, 14.2% lower than March's $1.298 billion and 10.5% lower than the year-ago period, when billings were $1.245 billion. Average monthly orders for the three months ended April 30 were $1.279 billion, 1% above March's figure of $1.267 billion and 2.7% lower than orders during the year-ago period, which were $1.315 billion.

Until April, the book-to-bill ratio rose every month after skidding to 0.88 in September, the lowest level since 1985.
