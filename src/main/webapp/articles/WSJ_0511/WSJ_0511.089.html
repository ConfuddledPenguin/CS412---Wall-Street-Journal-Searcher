Bell Atlantic Suspension Lifted

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/11/90

WASHINGTON -- The Treasury said it has lifted its suspension of Bell Atlantic Corp. from receiving future business from the federal government after the company agreed to verify the information it provided to the government. On April 3, the Treasury sent a letter to a Bell Atlantic subsidiary, Chesapeake & Potomac Telephone Co., alleging that C&P had made "material misrepresentations" in a bid for a $100 million contract to modernize the Treasury's telephone system in the Washington area. American Telephone & Telegraph Co. also is bidding on the work.

Bell Atlantic said it was reinstated after it agreed to tell its employees to check the information provided to the government, including information supplied by subcontractors. The phone company said it also agreed not to pursue further litigation on the contract.
