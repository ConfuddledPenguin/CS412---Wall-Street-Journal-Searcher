Business Brief -- Nordstrom Inc.: Earnings Plunged by 43% In the Fiscal First Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/11/90

Nordstrom Inc., hurt by slower-than-anticipated sales and higher expenses, said profit in the fiscal first quarter plunged almost 43%. The Seattle retailer, which recently reported its first annual profit decline since going public in 1971, said net income in the period ended April 30 fell to $13.2 million, or 16 cents a share, from $23 million, or 28 cents a share, a year earlier. Sales rose 8.4% to $555 million from $512.2 million. But analysts said sales at stores open at least a year inched up only 1.5% in the period -- and actually fell in April.

The results, which were worse than Wall Street expected, were released after the stock market closed.

Nordstrom attributed its sluggish sales partly to difficulties it encountered in maintaining appropriate inventory levels. Earnings fell, the company said, because it reduced its merchandise markup in an attempt to make its prices more competitive. Expenses also rose for other reasons, some of which Nordstrom said were related to its contract dispute with a union representing clerks and other employees at Nordstrom's Seattle-area stores.
