Merger of Conwest, Mineral Resources, And Barons Approved

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/11/90

TORONTO -- Conwest Exploration Co., Mineral Resources International Ltd. and Barons Oil Ltd. said their shareholders approved the merger of the three companies. Under terms of the merger, Conwest, a Toronto-based oil, gas and mining concern, will issue new Class B subordinate voting shares to minority holders of both subsidiaries. Conwest owns 51% of Mineral Resources, a mining concern. Toronto-based Mineral Resources owns 85% of Barons, an oil and gas production company based in Calgary, Alberta.

The new company will retain the name Conwest Exploration Co. The merger is expected to take effect June 1.

Separately, John Lamacraft, Conwest's president and chief executive officer, said he expects operating income this year of 65.5 million Canadian dollars ($56.2 million) on revenue of C$122.1 million, up from last year's operating income of C$56.6 million and revenue of C$103.2 million. Operating income is figured before taxes, depreciation, interest costs and minority interest payments.

Mr. Lamacraft, speaking at the company's annual meeting, said the gains will come largely from increased production of oil and natural gas, following several recent acquisitions.

In yesterday's trading on the Toronto Stock Exchange, Conwest Class B shares closed at 12.75 Canadian dollars ($10.94), up 37.5 cents. Mineral Resources shares rose 37.5 cents, to C$5.50; and Barons shares gained 15 cents, to close at C$2.90.
