International Brief -- BASF AG: Group Pretax Net Declined By 6.6% in First Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/11/90

BASF AG said group pretax profit slipped 6.6% in the first quarter, as a stronger mark and lower product prices depressed the group's profit margins. Earnings at West Germany's largest chemical concern slipped to 845 million marks ($516.8 million) from 905 million marks a year earlier. Group sales were little changed, easing to 11.89 billion marks from 11.97 billion marks.

Chairman Hans Albers said the rise of the mark against the dollar, yen and pound depressed revenue after conversion into marks. If currency rates had remained steady, first-quarter profit would have been up from a year earlier, he said at a news conference.

Another negative factor was product prices, which fell significantly from a year-earlier level because of a buildup of global production capacity and stronger competition, he said.

Despite slower growth, "we don't see a profound period of weakness for chemicals approaching," Mr. Albers said. He added that he hopes BASF will be able to match its 1989 record results this year, but he declined to make a specific forecast.
