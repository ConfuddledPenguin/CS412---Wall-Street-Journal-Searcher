Dividend Brief -- Hilton Hotels Corp.: Quarterly Common Payout Is Raised 20% to 30 Cents

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/11/90

The board of Hilton Hotels Corp., regrouping from its failed efforts to auction the Beverly Hills, Calif.-based hotel and casino company's assets, boosted the company's quarterly common stock dividend 20% to 30 cents a share from 25 cents. The dividend is payable June 15 to shareholders of record June 1. Barron Hilton, chairman and chief executive, said in an interview before the annual shareholders' meeting that the company is assessing the casino market in Atlantic City, N.J. Recent Wall Street rumors have suggested that Hilton is interested in buying Trump's Castle from New York investor Donald Trump. Mr. Hilton said the company isn't in negotiations with anyone, but said the company would consider the Castle and other properties as well.

Hilton built the Castle in 1984, but the company suffered a stunning reversal when its licensing application was rejected by state casino officials. Hilton sold the Castle to Mr. Trump in 1985, who opened the property that June. Hilton and Mr. Trump filed suit against one another over the closing of the sale, but the claims later were dismissed.

Hilton's stock closed at $48.125, down 62.5 cents in composite New York Stock Exchange trading.
