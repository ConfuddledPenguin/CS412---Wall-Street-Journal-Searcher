International Brief -- Peugeot S.A.: Auto Firm's Revenue Grew By 8% During First Quarter

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/11/90

The automobile group Peugeot S.A. said in Paris that its consolidated revenue rose to 44.14 billion francs ($8.02 billion) in the first quarter from 40.92 billion ($7.44 billion) in the comparable 1989 period. Peugeot said the 8% overall advance was led by its Automobiles Citroen unit, which recorded a 13% sales gain to 16.84 billion francs. Sales by the group's other main car unit, Automobiles Peugeot, climbed only 4%, to 25.42 billion francs.

The group said the slower sales growth for Automobiles Peugeot reflected flooding problems at its plant in Sochaux in February. It added that the floods trimmed production by about 12,000 units and said that delay is currently being made up.

Peugeot didn't report comparable earnings figures for the first quarter. For all of 1989, the group reported consolidated net profit of 10.30 billion francs after payments to minority interests. Revenue was 152.96 billion francs.
