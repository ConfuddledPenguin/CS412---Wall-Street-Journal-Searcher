Business Brief -- McDonnell Douglas Corp.: Dismissal of 4,000 Is Slated At California Plane Factory

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/11/90

McDonnell Douglas Corp., St. Louis, still stinging from an $84 million first-quarter operating loss at its transport aircraft factory in Long Beach, Calif., said it will lay off 4,000 people in Long Beach, 1,000 more than it had announced late last month. The overall cost-cutting effort will largely affect white-collar workers. Lawrence Harris, aerospace analyst at Bateman Eichler, Hill Richards Inc., noted that the company would have made a profit on its MD-80 commercial jetliner program in the first quarter if it weren't for all its general and administrative expenses.

There have been a rash of layoffs announced across the aerospace industry of late, mostly attributable to the declining U.S. military budget.

General Electric Co.'s aerospace arm announced 4,200 job cuts a couple of weeks ago. And Lockheed Corp., which revealed earlier this week that it was pulling its aircraft operations out of Burbank, Calif., plans to let go at least 2,750 workers by June. In addition, Boeing Co., General Dynamics Corp., Northrop Corp. and General Motors Corp.'s Hughes Aircraft unit all have talked during the past year about slashing their employment rolls.
