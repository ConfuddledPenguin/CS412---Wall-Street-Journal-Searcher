Ex-Aide of McDade Is Indicted in Case Tied to Defense Firm

  • Author: A Wall Street Journal News Roundup
  • Date: 05/11/90

WASHINGTON -- Federal prosecutors investigating Rep. Joseph McDade (R., Pa.) obtained an indictment charging the lawmaker's former top aide with racketeering, conspiracy and tax violations. The eight felony charges against Raymond Wittig, Rep. McDade's primary link to a minority defense contractor, indicate that prosecutors have stepped up their inquiry into whether the veteran GOP lawmaker accepted illegal campaign contributions and gratuities from officials of the company.

In the Philadelphia federal court indictment, Mr. Wittig was charged, among other things, with conspiring to funnel bribes and gratuities to public officials who helped the now-defunct United Chem-Con Corp. secure and maintain federal contracts over the years. The indictment doesn't identify any of those officials, but some of the charges parallel allegations against the congressman first raised in a December 1988 front-page story in The Wall Street Journal.

Rep. McDade, Mr. Wittig and their lawyers couldn't be reached for comment. In the past, Rep. McDade has maintained that he hasn't violated any laws and said his efforts on behalf of United Chem-Con were intended to keep jobs in his district. Mr. Wittig has acknowledged soliciting campaign gifts and other favors for Rep. McDade from company officials, but has denied any wrongdoing.

Assistant U.S. Attorney Nicholas Harbist, who has headed the two-year Philadelphia grand jury investigation, said only that the inquiry is continuing.

If convicted on all counts, Mr. Wittig faces a maximum of 41 years in jail and a $1.4 million fine.
