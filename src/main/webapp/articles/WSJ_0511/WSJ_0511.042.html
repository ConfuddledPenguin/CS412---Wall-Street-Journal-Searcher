Marketing & Media: McCaw's Sale of Unit Results in Huge Gain, First-Period Earnings

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/11/90

KIRKLAND, Wash. -- A huge gain on the sale of its southeast cellular systems boosted McCaw Cellular Communications Inc.'s first-quarter net income to $851.9 million, or $4.97 a share. That compares with a year-ago net loss of $97.7 million, or 74 cents a share. Revenue increased 76% to $180 million from $102 million in last year's first quarter.

McCaw sold its southeast cellular systems to Contel Cellular Inc. for a gain of about $1.16 billion. Results of the most recent quarter also reflect completion of its tender offer for LIN Broadcasting Corp., as well as an extraordinary tax benefit of $157.8 million.

Prior to the one-time gain on the asset sale, McCaw had a loss for the period, including interest expense, of $96.6 million. A year ago, the comparable operating loss, plus interest expenditures, totaled $102.5 million.

McCaw also reported strong first-quarter rises in both cash flow and customers. Cash flow rose sharply in the first quarter to $31.4 million, compared with $4.3 million in last year's first quarter. The number of cellular subscribers in McCaw's markets increased 64% to 853,000 from 520,000 a year ago. According to analysts, McCaw will need sustained sharp growth over the next five years to pay off a huge debt burden assumed in the acquisition of a controlling interest in LIN Broadcasting.
