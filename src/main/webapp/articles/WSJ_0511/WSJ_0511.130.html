Marketing & Media: P&G Pulls Ads at Station Due to Boycott Commercial

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/11/90

BOSTON -- A television station here said Procter & Gamble Co. canceled its ads at the station after it broadcast an advertisement urging consumers to boycott P&G's Folgers coffee. The ad was produced by Neighbor to Neighbor, a national organization based in San Francisco that advocates ending U.S. investments in El Salvador. The group also is urging consumers to boycott coffee made of beans grown in El Salvador.

The ad ends with an upturned coffee cup spilling what appears to be blood and urges viewers to boycott Folgers. A local branch of Neighbor to Neighbor paid Boston's WHDH-TV, a CBS affiliate, $1,000 to run the ad, said Sy Yanoff, president of the station.

"We looked at the ad, and it met our standards for commercial advertisements," Mr. Yanoff said. He added that a P&G official called the station Wednesday to discuss the Neighbor to Neighbor ad but didn't say the company planned to pull its ads.

Then yesterday, "our representative in New York called and said P&G just canceled all its current ads at our station," Mr. Yanoff said. He added that P&G spends about $1 million annually on ads at the station.

A spokesman for the Cincinnati-based consumer products firm, confirming that P&G had canceled its ads, said the Neighbor to Neighbor commercial is "highly offensive." It attacks P&G and Folgers, "and that's not the type of ad environment we want for our ads."
