Business Brief -- Fried. Krupp G.m.b.H.: Solid Earnings Are Expected After Restructuring Program

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/11/90

Fried. Krupp G.m.b.H. says it has completed its restructuring program and is poised to post a significant group profit for 1990. "Our large structural problems have been solved," said Gerhard Cromme, chairman of the Essen, West Germany-based steel and engineering concern, in an interview. "We are herewith returning to normalcy."

Krupp posted solid operating earnings last year after a deficit in 1988, and earnings are set to climb further this year, Mr. Cromme said.

"Everything is running well at the moment," and even the slowdown in the global steel market won't create any major problems for Krupp, the chairman said. "In the steel sector, we are hurt just like everybody else. But one can't talk about a significant weakening. . . . It's just a return to a normal level."

Despite that positive picture, the group is likely to post only a breakeven result for 1989 because of charges related to previous losses and measures to strengthen the balance sheet, Mr. Cromme said.
