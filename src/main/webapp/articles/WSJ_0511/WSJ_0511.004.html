Bank of England Forecast

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/11/90

LONDON -- The Bank of England forecasts that faster growth, higher inflation and sustained high interest rates can be expected in the world's major economies over the next year. In its quarterly bulletin, the bank warns that although "moderation" has so far prevailed in labor market pressures in North America, Japan and Europe, "the risk nevertheless remains that unduly buoyant demand could, in due course, overstretch supply and push up labor and goods prices everywhere."

The bank said that following the swift move toward German reunification, along with signs of further acceleration in other economies, it has increased its forecast to 3 1/2% average growth in the major economies this year. That's half a percentage point higher than the forecast given its previous bulletin in February.
