REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial) -- Asides: RICO's Injudicious Use

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/11/90

Chief Justice Rehnquist complains that the federal courts don't have the time and resources to play much of a role locking up drug criminals, and one recent case shows why. The federal courts must now spend time hearing a retrial of a massive case because prosecutors confused jurors the first time by throwing in an abusive RICO count. Earlier this year, federal Judge Robert Aguilar of California was the first federal judge ever tried as a racketeer, charged with interceding with other judges to go easy on people he owed money to. He was also charged with obstructing justice and conspiracy. A jury couldn't reach a verdict. Jurors might have figured that what Judge Aguilar allegedly did doesn't sound very nice, but also doesn't sound much like the Mafia or "racketeering." This week federal prosecutors dropped the RICO counts against Judge Aguilar and will bring a case on the less confusing charges. This sounds to us like progress.
