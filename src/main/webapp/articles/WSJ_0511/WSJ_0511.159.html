International Brief -- Volkswagen AG: Audi Unit's 1989 Net Rose By 51% on Sales Increase

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/11/90

Audi AG's 1989 profit jumped 51% to 228 million marks ($139.4 million) from 151 million marks in 1988 on sales of 12.2 billion marks, the company said. The luxury car unit of auto maker Volkswagen AG, which Wednesday said its group profit climbed 33% in 1989, said the pretax profit margin climbed to 5.2% from 3.6% a year earlier.

Audi said that in the first quarter world-wide sales rose 0.7% to 105,327 vehicles. Domestic unit sales, however, declined 0.7% to 39,351. Audi said its domestic market share fell to 5.3% from 5.7%.

In the U.S., unit sales climbed 7% to 4,949 in the first quarter. But Wednesday, Volkswagen Chairman Carl H. Hahn said that in the first four months, Audi unit sales had risen 14%, suggesting sales jumped sharply in April.

Sales in the U.S. had been hurt in recent years by since-discredited allegations that Audi cars had spontaneous acceleration problems. Signs that Audi's U.S. sales had turned the corner emerged in 1989.
