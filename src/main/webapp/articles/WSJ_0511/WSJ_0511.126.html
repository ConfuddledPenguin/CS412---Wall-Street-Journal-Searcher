Business Brief -- Toronto Dominion Bank: Fed Approves Underwriting Of Corporate Debt in U.S.

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/11/90

Toronto Dominion Bank said it received approval from the Federal Reserve to underwrite corporate debt in the U.S. The permission given Toronto Dominion to expand its securities-related activities in the U.S. is similar to measures approved last January for two other big Canadian banks, Royal Bank of Canada and Canadian Imperial Bank of Commerce, and London-based Barclays Bank PLC.

In January 1989, the Fed permitted several U.S. banks to underwrite corporate debt. As with the U.S. concerns, the foreign banks' securities subsidiaries can generate only 10% of their revenues from the underwriting of corporate debt, commercial paper, securities backed by mortgages, consumer debt and certain municipal bonds.

L. Arthur English, senior vice president for Toronto Dominion's U.S. division, said the bank's securities unit, Toronto Dominion Securities (USA) Inc., will be ready to use its new powers by Monday.
