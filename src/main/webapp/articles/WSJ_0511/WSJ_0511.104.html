Two House Members Ask SEC for Solution To an `Insider Ruling'

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/11/90

WASHINGTON -- Two House members asked the Securities and Exchange Commission to help create "any necessary legislative solution" to an appeals court ruling last week that is likely to hurt the agency's enforcement efforts aimed at insider trading. Reps. John Dingell (D., Mich.) and Edward Markey (D., Mass.) also asked the agency to brief them on the impact of the ruling by an appeals court panel, which overturned the insider-trading conviction of Robert Chestman, a former Gruntal & Co. broker.

Mr. Chestman had been convicted in federal district court of insider trading in Waldbaum Inc. stock before the company was acquired by Great Atlantic & Pacific Tea Co. The appeals court ruling threatened to throw into turmoil the SEC's use of its Rule 14e-3 in insider-trading cases. The rule makes it a violation to trade on misappropriated tender-offer information even if the defendant isn't violating a fiduciary duty by making the trade.

The government hasn't decided whether it will appeal the latest ruling to the full appeals court or the Supreme Court.

Legislation to restore any authority the SEC may have lost as a result of the court action could include devising a statutory definition of insider-trading, something Rep. Dingell has opposed in the past.
