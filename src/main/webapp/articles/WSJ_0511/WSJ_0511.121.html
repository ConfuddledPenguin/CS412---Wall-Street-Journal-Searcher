Initial Jobless Claims Fell To 350,000 in Latest Week

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/11/90

WASHINGTON -- Initial claims for regular state unemployment benefits fell to a seasonally adjusted 350,000 during the week ended April 28 from 372,000 the previous week, the Labor Department said. The number of people receiving regular state benefits in the week ending April 21 increased to a seasonally adjusted 2,402,000, or 2.3% of those covered by unemployment insurance, from a revised 2,386,000 the previous week, when the insured unemployment rate was a revised 2.3%.

Counting all state and federal benefit programs, the number of people receiving unemployment benefits in the week ended April 21 fell to 2,497,900 from a revised 2,569,600 a week earlier. These figures aren't seasonally adjusted.
