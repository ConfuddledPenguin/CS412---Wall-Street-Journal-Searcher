Business Brief -- Grove Bank for Savings: Two Members of Dissident Group Will Serve on Board

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/11/90

Grove Bank for Savings, Boston, said it reached an agreement with a dissident shareholder group, under which the bank will name two members of the group to its board. James B. Rosenwald III, chief executive officer of Redondo Beach, Calif.-based Rosenwald Partners L.P., and Christopher P. Sullivan, a lawyer, will be named directors at the June 7 annual meeting. Those new members will increase the number of directors to 17.

The Rosenwald group, which last month said it intended to propose a resolution calling for the sale of the bank, agreed to vote its shares in favor of management proposals and for management nominees.

The group also agreed that for three years it will not acquire any additional shares of Grove stock, with the exception that one member of the group, Mr. Sullivan, may acquire up to an additional 5,000 shares.

The agreement also provides that the company will consider increasing its dividend and repurchasing its shares in the public market in an effort to enhance shareholder value.
