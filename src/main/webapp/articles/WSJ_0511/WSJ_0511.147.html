Business Brief -- Apple Computer Inc.: Court Dismisses Last Count In Copyright Suit by Xerox

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/11/90

A federal judge in San Francisco dismissed the last count of a lawsuit brought by Xerox Corp. challenging the copyright covering the command system of Apple Computer Inc.'s Macintosh. The judge, Vaughn Walker, had dismissed in March the first five counts of the lawsuit, which sought to invalidate the copyright covering the Macintosh's easy-to-use graphics command system, charging it copied the Xerox Star program.

Xerox, Stamford, Conn., agreed to dismissal of the sixth count "because it wouldn't provide the relief we originally sought," cancellation of the Apple copyright, said Xerox attorney Henry Bunsow. He said Xerox is weighing an appeal of the overall decision.

Apple, Cupertino, Calif., has pending before the same judge lawsuits against Microsoft Corp. of Bellevue, Wash., and Hewlett-Packard Co. of Palo Alto, Calif., charging their products infringe upon the Macintosh copyrights.
