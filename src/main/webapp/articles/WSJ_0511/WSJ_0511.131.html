Credit Rating Brief -- Domtar Inc.: Debt Ratings Are Lowered, Weak Earnings Are Cited

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/11/90

Two bond-rating services downgraded Domtar Inc.'s debt, citing weak earnings at the Montreal paper and building products concern. Standard & Poor's Corp. said it downgraded Domtar's senior secured debt rating to triple-B-minus from triple-B, affecting about U.S. $250 million of debt. Standard & Poor's said the "downgrade reflects Domtar's weaker-than-expected earnings performance in 1989, which has continued into 1990."

Canadian Bond Rating Service said it cut its ratings on Domtar's commercial paper to single-A-2 from single-A-1, on Domtar's debentures to B plus plus (Low) from B plus plus, and on Domtar's preferred shares to P-3 (Low) from P-3.

The service said the strength of the Canadian dollar against the U.S. dollar "exacerbated the overall financial condition of the company" as prices for paper products weakened. As well, the rating service said declining construction activity in North America has hurt Domtar's building products operations.
