Reuters Holdings Unit Introduces 70 Stocks To Electronic Network

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/11/90

NEW YORK -- Reuters Holdings PLC said its Instinet Corp. subsidiary introduced 40 French and 30 German "blue chip" stocks to its electronic stock-trading network. All transactions will occur in the local currency. Quotes on the stocks will come from SEAQ International, but that will cover only 37 of the French issues and 17 of the German shares.

Instinet is a global electronic trading network where traders anonymously enter buy and sell orders in an electronic book that trades 14 hours a day. The Instinet network is used to trade nearly 10,000 U.S. and British securities. In 1989, the volume of shares traded on Instinet totaled 2.56 billion.

Instinet called the addition of the French and German securities a "critical step" in developing its global trading capabilities. Instinet said it would add other market data from France and Germany later this year.

Reuters, based in London, is an international news and financial information provider.
