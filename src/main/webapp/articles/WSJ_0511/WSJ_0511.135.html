UBS President Assails Swiss Government's Economic Policies

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/11/90

GENEVA -- Union Bank of Switzerland President Robert Studer criticized the Swiss government for pursuing conflicting economic policies, and warned that if reforms aren't implemented to make the Swiss financial center more competitive, the country's largest banks may be forced to take more of their business abroad. "A lot will depend on the government to create the environment for the banks to do what they have to do in order to stay internationally competitive, Mr. Studer said, in a speech to the Swiss-American Chamber of Commerce. "If the Swiss authorities continue to hinder Switzerland from maintaining its present position . . ., the major Swiss banks will certainly follow their customers."

In particular, he attacked the government's 35% withholding tax on interest and dividend income, and the stamp tax on securities transactions, both of which he said make Switzerland less competitive. The stamp tax alone, he added, "is largely responsible for the noteworthy absence of a liquid and developed money market in Switzerland, and for driving the Eurobond market abroad."

Mr. Studer also said various branches of the Swiss government appear to be pursuing conflicting policies, further muddying the image of the country's financial center.

"Whereas the Swiss National Bank is following a tight monetary policy to fight inflation, the government tells the banks to restrict themselves in increasing lending rates," he said.
