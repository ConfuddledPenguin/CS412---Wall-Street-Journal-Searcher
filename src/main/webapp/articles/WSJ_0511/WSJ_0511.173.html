Money-Fund Assets Fell by $2.22 Billion

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/11/90

NEW YORK -- Assets of the nation's 508 money-market funds fell $2.22 billion, to $383.50 billion, in the week ended Wednesday, according to the Investment Company Institute, a trade association. Assets of 246 general-purpose funds fell $531.6 million, to $132.17 billion, while assets of 102 broker-dealer funds fell $854.3 million, to $159.84 billion. Assets of 160 institutional funds fell $831.2 million, to $91.48 billion. Mutual-fund assets revised for the week ending May 2 fell $2.15 billion, to $385.71 billion.

"Yields on money-market funds changed very little during the week," said Jacob Dreyer, vice president and chief economist at the Washington-based institute. But yields on direct money-market instruments were "quite volatile," he said. "These changing relationships may have prompted money managers, as well as individuals to engage in asset shuffling."
