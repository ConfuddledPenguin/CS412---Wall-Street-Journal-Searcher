Letters to the Editor: There's No Mystery To Failure Statistics

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/11/90

Your March 19 OTC focus on Newbridge Networks provided some bogus information. On failure rates, for example, I would like to point out that failure statistics are absolute numbers. If I purchase 100 widgets and 10 fail, that yields an absolute failure rate of 10%. One can then say that provided I buy similar equipment, I can project failures of approximately 10% plus or minus about 3%. In a projection, the failures haven't happened yet, but in the historical analysis, the figures are absolute, and no amount of wishful thinking can change them.

It is therefore impossible to have a failure rate of between 20% and 50%, as quoted in your article by a former Newbridge customer (who happens to be affiliated with a Newbridge competitor). Let me tell you that Newbridge's historical failure rates are below 2%. To be exact, 1.97%.

There is no mystery here. My company, Novatel, buys more Newbridge 3624 Channel Banks than any other company in the U.S. We don't hide them away in a closet, we install them. We install the majority on MCI spans. We also sell and install Telco Systems and Rockwell Channel Banks. I'm happy to say we have very satisfied customers. The facts are therefore absolute, can't be adjusted, can't be ignored. Newbridge makes very high-quality, feature-packed, cost-effective Channel Banks and TI Multiplexers.

Daniel D. Pearlson

President

Novatel Computer Systems

San Fernando, Calif.
