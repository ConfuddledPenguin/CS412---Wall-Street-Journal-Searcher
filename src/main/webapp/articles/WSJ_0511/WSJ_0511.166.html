International Brief -- British Telecommunications PLC: Reorganization to Reduce Work Force by Up to 5,000

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/11/90

British Telecommunications PLC, London, said it plans to eliminate 4,000 to 5,000 middle-management jobs as part of a previously announced corporate reorganization to cut costs. The job cuts in a workforce of about 244,000 are the first in what Britain's main telephone company says will be a steady shedding of jobs over the next few years as fewer workers are needed because of technological improvements.

Also as part of the reorganization, the company named Mike Bett, 55-year-old managing director of its main U.K. subsidiary, to the newly created post of vice chairman effective Oct. 1.

Deep job cuts and public boardroom shuffles are relatively new experiences for British Telecom, a former state telephone monopoly privatized in 1984. In the past few years, pressure for job cuts has been growing inside the company despite stiff union opposition.
