Business Brief -- British Petroleum PLC: Earnings Are Down by 43%; Inventory Losses Are Factor

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/11/90

British Petroleum PLC registered a 43% plunge in first-quarter profit, with depressed chemical earnings and inventory losses countering strong growth in exploration and production. First-quarter sales advanced 36%.

Britain's biggest oil company saw net in the quarter drop to #344 million ($578 million) from #608 million ($1.02 billion) the year earlier. These figures are on a historic-cost basis, which values inventories at the cost of acquisition. They include a #19 million inventory loss, compared with a #234 million gain a year earlier.

Per-share earnings dropped 38% to 6.4 pence (10.7 cents) from 10.3 pence.

On a replacement-cost basis, which excludes the effect of inventory gains or losses, net fell a more modest 5.2% to #361 million from #381 million.

Sales jumped to #8.86 billion from #6.53 billion.

Despite the earnings slide, the company said it would increase its first-quarter dividend to 3.95 pence from 3.65 pence.
