Canada's Lavalin May Get Contract for Thai Skytrain

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/11/90

BANGKOK, Thailand -- A group led by Canada's Lavalin Inc. appears likely to win the 40 billion baht ($1.54 billion) contract for Skytrain, Bangkok's long-delayed and much-contested mass-transit system, if it can get financial guarantees within two weeks. On Wednesday, a committee appointed by Interior Minister Banharn Silpa-Archa decided Lavalin International Group should build the elevated mass-transit system and operate it for 30 years before handing it over to the government.

But Lavalin must secure commercial-bank guarantees for 800 million Canadian dollars (US$686.4 million) in financing. Government-owned Export Development Corp. of Canada offered the group that amount at concessional terms if Lavalin can secure guarantees for it. The Thai Finance Ministry said guarantees must be obtained by May 24. But some observers of the project pointed out that mass-transit systems generally lose money, and said banks may be reluctant to provide the guarantees.

Burin Borriboon, deputy managing director of Sino-Thai Engineering & Construction Co., one of Lavalin's Thai partners, conceded that "it will still be a scramble" to secure the guarantees in two weeks because they probably will require approval from the boards of participating international banks. But he said he is confident that Lavalin will be able to meet the May 24 deadline.
