Business Brief -- Citicorp: Rise of $500 Million Seen For Problem Loans in U.S.

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/11/90

Citicorp's domestic problem loans could rise another $500 million to $600 million, or about 18%, by the end of the year. Thomas E. Jones, executive vice president for financial control, tax and auditing, said in an interview that deteriorating conditions in commercial real estate markets largely are responsible for the rise.

He added that the $500 million to $600 million was based largely on the New York-based bank's "feel" for where the market is headed and isn't an estimate.

Such an increase would be in line with Citicorp's first-quarter results when nonperforming commercial real estate loans rose to $1.3 billion from $1.1 billion at the end of 1989.

As of March 31, Citicorp had $7.5 billion in nonperforming loans out of a total portfolio of $160 billion. Of that, about $3.3 million are domestic loans, including $1.3 billion of problem loans on commercial real estate projects.

In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange, Citicorp rose 37.5 cents a share to close at $22.75.
