Ex-Thrift Director, Lawyer Are Charged In Loan Investigation

  • Author: null
  • Date: 05/11/90

DALLAS -- A former director of the defunct Metroplex Federal Savings Association and his defense lawyer were indicted on charges of obstructing a federal criminal investigation into the thrift's lending activities. Bob R. Franks, a former director of Metroplex, already was under indictment on charges that he illegally benefited from a loan made by NorthPark Savings Association while he was a director at that institution.

Mr. Franks and his lawyer in the NorthPark case, Earl Rutledge, of Hurst, Texas, were indicted yesterday by a federal grand jury in Dallas on charges related to two loans made by Metroplex Savings.

The loans, totaling $500,000, were made to a company that was purchasing an interest in the joint venture that owned the Metroplex Savings building, according to the indictment. Messrs. Franks and Rutledge, who were partners in the joint venture, are charged with concealing Mr. Rutledge's receipt of funds from the loan from other directors at Metroplex Savings.

The indictment also charges Mr. Franks and his lawyer with filing affidavits that falsely stated that the Metroplex directors were aware that Mr. Franks benefited from the loan. The affidavits were filed with three separate legal motions in federal court in Dallas, including a motion to have the affidavits presented to the federal grand jury considering the Metroplex case, according to the indictment.

Neither Mr. Franks, 55 years old, nor Mr. Rutledge, 56, could be reached for comment yesterday.

Metroplex was declared insolvent in late 1988 with $65.3 million in assets. NorthPark was declared insolvent at the same time with $124.1 million in assets. The two thrifts were merged with 12 others in December 1988 to become Consolidated Federal Bank FSB.
