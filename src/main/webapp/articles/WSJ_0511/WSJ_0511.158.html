Big Board Reports Lowest Net Income Of Past Five Years

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/11/90

NEW YORK -- The New York Stock Exchange reported its lowest earnings of the past five years. The Big Board said its net income in 1989 dropped 39% to $6.3 million from $10.4 million in 1988. Revenue at the exchange increased 7.9% to $349.3 million. But expenses surged 12% to $339.2 million, partly because of an upgrading of technology for trading and surveillance.

To rein in expenses, the Big Board recently imposed a hiring freeze and has tried to reduce costs and increase certain fees. A spokeswoman declined to comment on hiring or budgetary matters.

The Big Board could face another profit squeeze this year. The exchange had predicted that daily average volume would amount to about 166 million shares this year. Through the end of April, daily average volume on the Big Board amounted to 154.7 million. Lower trading volume generally cuts into the exchange's fee income.

Nonetheless, there are plenty of signs that the exchange remains vital. Some 41.6 billion shares traded at the Big Board last year, the second highest volume in the exchange's history. The number of listed companies rose to 1,721, an all-time high.

However, return on members' equity fell to 2.7%, the second year of low, single-digit returns. Working capital at the exchange fell to $108.9 million from $114 million in 1988.

At the American Stock Exchange, net income rose 64% to $4.2 million in 1989 from $2.6 million the previous year. Revenue at the Amex rose 6.7% to $109.6 million from $102.7 million in 1988, partly because of gains in transaction and clearance charges. Expenses grew 3.6% to $102.8 million last year from $99.2 million in 1988.

The Amex traded 3.1 billion shares last year, the second highest share volume ever, compared with 2.5 billion in 1988. A center of options trading, the Amex traded 49.8 million stock options contracts, up from 45 million in 1988.

"The outlook for 1990 is good," said James Jones, Amex chairman. He said first-quarter net income was $1.2 million, while trading volume in the period was up 31% from a year earlier.
