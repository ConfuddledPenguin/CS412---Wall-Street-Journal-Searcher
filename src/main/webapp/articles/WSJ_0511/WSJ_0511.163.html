International Brief -- Sears PLC: British Firm's Pretax Profit Fell 15% for Jan. 31 Year

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/11/90

Sears PLC, a U.K. retailing, property and investment concern that owns the London department store Selfridges, said pretax profit fell 15% for the year ended Jan. 31, with earnings declines in all divisions. Sears, which isn't related to Sears, Roebuck & Co., said pretax profit dropped to #231.4 million ($388.6 million) from #272.8 million ($458.2 million). The decline wasn't as deep as market predictions, which ranged between #195 million and #205 million.

Profit after tax, less minority interests, was #167 million, down 9.8% from #185.1 million a year earlier. Per-share earnings declined 9.7% to 11.1 pence (19 cents) from 12.3 pence (21 cents).

The company said that the first quarter of 1990 is "showing an encouraging trend in sales" but "the economic climate is not likely to improve significantly in the current year."
