Business Brief -- Sears, Roebuck & Co.: Brennan Tells Holders Sales To Increase in Second Half

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/11/90

Sears, Roebuck & Co. Chairman Edward A. Brennan said at the annual meeting that sales would strengthen in the second half. Facing shareholders who expressed displeasure over Sears's stagnant earnings and stock price, Mr. Brennan also reiterated his goal of achieving a return on equity of more than 15% "over time." He wouldn't specify when the goal might be met. The retailing and financial services company's return on average equity in 1989 was 10.9%.

G. Leslie Fabian, a large shareholder from New York, drew applause when he noted that per-share earnings and dividends haven't grown as much as other companies in the Dow Jones stock averages. "My question is one word: When?" he asked.

"We agree with you," Mr. Brennan responded. "We're not happy with our performance. . . . You are impatient. I don't blame you, so am I."

In 1989, Wilmington, Del.-based Sears had a 3.8% rise in net income to $1.51 billion, or $4.30 a share, from $1.45 billion, or $3.84 a share, in 1988.
