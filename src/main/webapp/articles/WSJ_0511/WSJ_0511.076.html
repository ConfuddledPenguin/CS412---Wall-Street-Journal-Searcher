Who's News: Dow Jones Appoints Carl Valenti President Of Telerate Subsidiary

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/11/90

NEW YORK -- Dow Jones & Co. named Carl M. Valenti, 51 years old, to the additional post of president of the Telerate Inc. financial information-service subsidiary. Mr. Valenti retains his position as senior vice president of the parent. At the same time, Dow Jones said, John D. Jessop, 47-year-old senior executive vice president of Telerate, resigned.

"We regret" the resignation, a Dow Jones spokesman said, "but it's not uncommon after a merger, and with greater parent-company involvement in operations, for a dedicated and responsible executive to leave." Dow Jones closed the acquisition of Telerate in January.

The resignation "doesn't reflect any difference in fundamental priorities, plans and business objectives at Telerate and Dow Jones," the spokesman said. He added, however, that the resignation was unexpected.

Mr. Jessop, who was named to his current post effective March 1, declined to comment.

Mr. Valenti has had overall responsibility for the direction of both Telerate and Dow Jones's Information Services group, of which he remains president and publisher. Dow Jones said he will press ahead with projects in progress to further strengthen the position of the parent and subsidiary in the electronic-information field.

Dow Jones also publishes The Wall Street Journal, Barron's magazine, and community newspapers.
