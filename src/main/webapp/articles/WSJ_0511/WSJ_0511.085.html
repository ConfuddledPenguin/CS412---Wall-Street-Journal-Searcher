U.S. Reduces Duties On Colombian Flowers

  • Author: Author Unknown
  • Date: 05/11/90

WASHINGTON -- The Commerce Department reduced anti-dumping duties for cut flowers imported from Colombia. Officials said the department had overestimated the extent to which the imports are "dumped" -- that is sold at prices below those in the home market or the cost of production -- when it first imposed 4.4% duties in 1987. The duties will drop to 4.16% on some flowers and to 2.24% on others. The imports -- mostly carnations, chrysanthemums and pompons -- are valued at about $180 million a year.

Colombia has protested that the duties undermine anti-drug efforts by injuring legitimate farmers. A lawyer for the Colombian growers said the reduction in the duties "is an important victory for us."
